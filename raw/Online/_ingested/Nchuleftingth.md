Apart from various [[ON:Dwarven Constructs|Dwemer constructs]], the dungeon has lately been occupied by treasure hunter [[Online:Artisan Lenarmen|Lenarmen]] and his hired [[ON:Bronzefist|Bronzefist]] mercenaries, searching for a [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|powerful artifact]]. [[ON:Butterfly|Butterflies]], [[ON:Centipede|Centipedes]], [[ON:Dragonfly|Dragonflies]] and [[ON:Skavenger|Skavengers]] are found in numbers within.

The seven [[Online:Fragments#Dwarven_Theodolite|fragments]] for the [[Online:Dwarven Theodolite|Dwarven Theodolite]] pet and [[Online:Dwarven_Theodolite_(achievement)|achievement]] are randomly dropped from enemies in this zone.

==Layout==
===Nchuleftingth Approach===
This is the first part of the public dungeon. The whole public dungeon is of dwarven architecture and is fully overgrown with Morrowind's vegetation. It splits into three directions: Forward is a small square room, to the west and east both go into the larger part of '''Nchuleftingth Approach'''. This larger part is a cavern with a mix of Morrowind's Fauna and dwarven parts. Various animals and animunculi are present here, dwarven traps as well. The cavern has a lower and a higher level. A sort of hidden part in the higher level houses one of the public dungeon's bosses. Two rivers join towards the center. You can go to '''Nchuleftingth Bailey''' by using a door at the east.

===Nchuleftingth Bailey===
You arrive at this part from '''Nchuleftingth Approach'''. To the left is a small room with two bosses and to the right is the door to '''Nchuleftingth Ruins''', though both are on the same map.

===Nchuleftingth Ruins===
You arrive at this part from '''Nchuleftingth Bailey'''. It takes shape of a ring with a giant hole into lava at the center. You will finally encounter Bronzefist enemies there with dwarven animunculi. It is littered with dwarven traps. You can access '''Nchuleftingth Core''' from the door at the southeast, '''Ametric Interval Chamber''' from the door west of it and '''Nchuleftingth Lavaworks''' from the door to the north. The far east part is only accessed by either first going through '''Nchuleftingth Core''' or '''Nchuleftingth Lavaworks''', or by a dangerous ledge full of traps near the north door. From here there are three doors, a door to '''Nchuleftingth Core''' at the southeast and a door to '''Nchuleftingth Lavaworks''' at the northeast, as mentioned, but also the door to '''Nchuleftingth Cathedra''' at the east by a staircase.

===Ametric Interval Chamber===
You arrive at this part from the door in '''Nchuleftingth Ruins'''. It is only accessible if you have the [[ON:Adversarial Adventures|Adversarial Adventures]] quest active. It is purely of dwarven architecture with a medium sized area where you arrive. A staircase to your left leads to a door to the outside. The exterior door to the '''Nchuleftingth Ametric Chamber''' cannot be opened from the outside, as it is a one-way exit.

===Nchuleftingth Core===
You arrive at this part from either doors of '''Nchuleftingth Ruins'''. Their path joins and you will encounter the public dungeon's group boss, Nchulaeon the Eternal, which stands at the crossroad of three extremely similar paths. Upon combat, it will roll to the end of a path where a moving platform will raise it higher and renders it untargetable while waves of dwarven animunculi will fight you. This section is the most filled with dwarven traps.

===Nchuleftingth Lavaworks===
You arrive at this part from either doors of '''Nchuleftingth Ruins'''. If you use the north door you will arrive from the west and if you use the northeast door you will arrive from the south, where the skyshard is located. As indicated in the name, this section features lava everywhere. Almost none of Morrowind's Vegetation is found here, it is mostly dwarven architecture in a cavern. You will have to use high bridges without railings to navigate, dwarven animunculi and Bronzefist forces will fight you here, as well as many dwarven traps. The door to '''Nchuleftingth Control Room''' is found to the east.

===Nchuleftingth Control Room===
You arrive here from the door in '''Nchuleftingth Lavaworks'''. It is a very small rectangular room and is only used for [[ON:The Heart's Desire|The Heart's Desire]] quest, though you can access it anytime. It also houses the unique [[ON:Traps#Laser|Laser]] trap which are four lines of electricity lined vertically that activate on a pattern, dealing extremely fast ticks of damage.

===Nchuleftingth Cathedra===
Accessed from the central door at the east of '''Nchuleftingth Ruins''', a long staircase leads to a room where [[ON:Artisan Lenarmen|Artisan Lenarmen]] can be found at the center. During combat, he will summon various automata to aid him. It is a large square-shaped room with large pillars placed in the corners of a smaller square pattern. The door to '''Nchuleftingth Sanctuary''' is found to the north.

===Nchuleftingth Sanctuary===
A room connected to the Cathedra, the Bthark Prism is found here inside a chest. A Dwemer Astrolobe, Centurion Head, and empty chest can be searched here during the related quest. The book ''[[ON:The Heart of Lorkhan, My Final Prize|The Heart of Lorkhan, My Final Prize]]'' is found on a table next to the empty chest and a door to the surface is found on the left side. A door to the outside is found to the west.

==Notes==
*Nchuleftingth also [[Morrowind:Nchuleftingth|appears]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|TES III: Morrowind]]''.
*The loading screen for [[ON:Nchuleft|Nchuleft]] mentions these locations as being confused for one another, due to their similar names.
*The metal chair found above the prism in the sanctuary is identical to the seat used by the [[ON:Harmonic Auditor|Harmonic Auditor]].
*Several traps can be found throughout the dungeon:
**Steam vent traps that deal continuous moderate flame damage when standing on.
**Piston traps that inflict moderate shock damage and knock you away.
**Shock beam traps that inflict continuous low shock damage and snare you if you pass through, while overcharging and buffing dwarven automatons that go through.
