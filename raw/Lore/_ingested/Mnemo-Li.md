[[Lore:Mnemo-Li|Mnemo-Li]] (or '''Mnemoli the Blue Star'''){{Ref|name=EOMN}} also known as the '''Reclusive Princess''',{{Ref|name=9C}} is a [[Lore:Stars|star]], the most well-known [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] and a daughter of [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]. She is associated with "un-time" events, and it is said that she was visible even in the daytime sky during the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]].{{Ref|name=EOMN|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}}{{Ref|name=COTMX3}} Similarly to other Magna Ge she is now synonymous with the star, which she created.{{Ref|name=EOMN}}{{ref|name=WOT|{{Cite Book|Wayshrines of Tamriel}}}} She was one of the Star-Orphans of the Nine Coruscations, who followed the parabolas that led away from Magnus. The remaining eight, her sisters were named [[Lore:Merid-Nunda|Merid-Nunda]], [[Lore:Sheza-Rana|Sheza-Rana]], [[Lore:Iana-Lor|Iana-Lor]], [[Lore:Xero-Lyg|Xero-Lyg]], the eldest [[Lore:Ithelia|Prime Archon]], [[Lore:Londa-Vera|Londa-Vera]], [[Lore:Unala-Se|Unala-Se]], and [[Lore:Valia-Sha|Valia-Sha]].{{Ref|name=EOMN}}{{Ref|name=9C}}

Mnemoli's origins go beyond her relation to Magnus. It is believed that Mnemoli was retroactively constructed by [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] who personally gave birth to his cosmic daughter, naming her '''Memory'''.{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}}{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}} Memory served as the legacy of Sotha Sil and survived him following his death at the hands of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]].{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}}<noinclude>
She was a subject of study by various scholars. Arch-Prelate of the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] [[Lore:Fervidius Tharn|Fervidius Tharn]] researched Mnemo-Li at some point during the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] and mentioned her in ''On the Detachment of the Sheath''. Through the intervention of Mnemoli and a particular manipulation, called the "Hurling Disk", of the identity between the circle of the Aurbis and [[Lore:The Towers#The Metaphysical Tower|the Tower]], the Arch-Prelate apparently believed that it would be possible to detach a sheath from the Aurbis' integument and "twist" it to provoke the Dragon Break of the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]].{{Ref|name=OTD|{{Cite Book|On the Detachment of the Sheath}}}} 

Sarshaar, a [[Lore:Senche-raht|Senche-raht]] [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] [[Lore:Whispering Claw|Whispering Claw]] monk of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] and loyalist of Queen [[Lore:Khamira|Khamira]] of [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] was another of the known scholars devoted to the research of Mnemoli.{{ref|name=Q|[[ON:Queen's Claw Senche-raht|Queen's Claw Senche-raht]]'s description and default name in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

[[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] mentioned Mnemoli in his ''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes'', where she is said to "run blue, through noise, and shine only when the earth trembles with the eruption of the newly-mantled." He claimed that in order to ward against them one should invoke: "Go! GHARTOK AL MNEM! God is come! NUMI MORA! NUM DALAE MNEM!" {{Ref|name=COTMX3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} According to him, Mnemoli together with other Magna Ge as a whole were responsible for creation of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] in "the bowels of [[Lore:Lyg|Lyg]]".{{Ref|name=MDC4|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 4}}}}

She is linked to endless possibility, rewritten narratives, including those of the Elder Scrolls, and the constant emergence of the [[Lore:Hero|Prisoner Unbound]] as is the will of the [[Lore:Ithelia|Prime Archon]].{{Ref|name=9C}} Mnemoli are occasionally referred to as plural.{{Ref|name=COTMX3}}{{Ref|name=mkirc|group=UOL|{{TIL|Michael Kirkbride - IRC Q&A Sessions|michael-kirkbride-irc-quotes}}}} Some texts accredit them with collecting "all the [[Lore:Music|songs]]" made by mortals and keeping of the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]].{{Ref|name=mkirc|group=UOL}}{{NewLeft}}

==Notes==
*The Elder Scroll of Mnem was one of the known Elder Scrolls.{{ref|name=PA|[[ON:Protector Arfire|Protector Arfire]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=STOM|[[ON:Scroll Temple of Mnem|Scroll Temple of Mnem]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Mnem is mentioned in Mankar Camoran's ''[[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes]]''.{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}
*A large blue star sometimes seen in the skies of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] is also associated with [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]].{{Ref|name=KE8|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VIII}}}}{{ref|name=ContrabandZ|[[ON:Contraband Z#Zenithar's Measure|Zenithar's Measure]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Its connection to Mnemo-Li is unknown.
*''[[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37|Sermon Thirty-Seven]]'' mentions a female "signal blueshift". According to this source she told [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] that "The sign of royalty is not this, There is no right lesson learned alone."{{ref|name=S37|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}} [[Lore:The Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]] associates this phrase with Mnemo-Li.{{ref|name=9C}}
*According to certain texts, Mnemoli is a wayward child of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]], one of a pantheon of forgotten deities known as the 'Star Orphans', a tribe of gods and goddesses that apparently felt abandoned when they withdrew from creation. Like many of her siblings, Mnemoli is both confused and delighted with the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], and explores its five quarters as best she can without the help and regulation of worship, during breakings of the sideways wheel. She is believed to run through the [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] part of the Aurbis every untime. [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijiics]] hold her in much reverence, and many of them make pilgrimages to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] when it appears because the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] there catches fire at its passing. This mountain is reputed to be one of the last refuges of the Dwemer before they departed from this world.{{ref|group=UOL|name=VBOH|[[General:Vehk's Book of Hours|Vehk's Book of Hours]]}}
*According to some sources the adjective 'mnemolic' refers to the Star Orphans.{{Ref|name=MUW|group=UOL|[[General:Made Up Word Round Up|Made Up Word Round Up]]}}{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride's Posts|Michael Kirkbride's Posts]]}}
*Mnemoli is sometimes referred to as "the blessed Magna-Ge". According to some texts those born under the Signs of the Y once knew her harmony, but today their heart-strings are as conflicted as their Patterns.{{ref|name=MGP|group=UOL|[[General:Magne-Ge Pantheon|Magne-Ge Pantheon]]}}
