[[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]] (meaning "Old Sotha" in Dark Elvish) was an ancient, ruined settlement located northeast of [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]], in the [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. In the time of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]], prior to the formation of the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]], Ald Sotha was originally a town with many towers that was home to [[Lore:House Sotha|House Sotha]]. It was later destroyed by [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], with [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] saving [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] from Dagon's armies, leaving him as its only survivor.{{ref|name=Saryoni}}{{Ref|name=Arox|[[Online:Arox the Mutilator|Arox the Mutilator]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} By the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Ald Sotha had become a temple to Mehrunes Dagon, though it was visited by members of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] during the Pilgrimages of the Four Corners.<noinclude>

[[Lore:Roland's Tear|Roland's Tear]], a rare variation of the common [[Lore:Gold Kanet|gold kanet]] flower, is known to grow in the ruins. It was sacred to the Tribunal,{{Ref|name=DS|[[Online:Disciple Sildras|Disciple Sildras]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} and is prized by alchemists for its wondrous powers when used in potion making.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Aurane Frernis|Aurane Frernis]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

==History==
[[File:ON-place-Ald Sotha.jpg|thumb|right|Ald Sotha circa {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Originally, Ald Sotha was the seat of [[Lore:House Sotha|House Sotha]], a minor house of the settled [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. It was the birthplace of [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], who would later achieve godhood through the power of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] and become a member of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]], as well as his sister [[Lore:Sotha Nall|Sotha Nall]]. The town of was recounted as a tranquil place, with a fish pond, flower garden with many birds, artisanal kitchens, and many sunny days with activities involving pottery wheels, catching torchbugs in jars, and eating scrib jelly on rainy days.{{ref|name=ESOAmbient|[[Online:Sotha Nall (person)|Factotum]] [[Online:Factotum Dialogue|ambient dialogue]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} The town was destroyed by the armies of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] early in the First Era; Sotha Sil was the sole survivor, having been saved by [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]].{{ref|name=Saryoni|[[Morrowind:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=ABHoAS|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of Ald Sotha}}}} The attack occurred at some point after he and his sister snuck out of their home on a rainy morning. His family woke up to find their homes and tapestries already burning, and screamed in agony in their final moments.{{ref|name=ESOAmbient}} [[Lore:Arox|Arox the Mutilator]] was present at the attack and recounted his talons rending flesh, the screams of the damned, and the towers of Ald Sotha tumbling down.{{Ref|name=Arox}}

Prior to its destruction, sometime during the reign of [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], a representative from Ald Sotha traveled to the vast temple of [[Lore:Assurnabitashpi|Assurnabitashpi]] in light of the death of [[Lore:Mordrin Hanin|Mordrin Hanin]]. On the ninth day of mourning, everyone drank from the cup of passage, and a great many of them were killed by the deadly concoction, supplying Hanin with companions to the next world. It is unknown if the Ald Sotha diplomat was among these perished souls.{{Ref|name=HW|{{Cite Book|Hanin's Wake}}}}

A shrine to Mehrunes Dagon was later built at the location, likely in remembrance of the Prince's destruction of the original city. The shrine fell into ruin, although it continued to be populated by [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. The [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] occasionally made pilgrimages to Dagon's shrine deep within the ruins,{{ref|name=Saryoni}} and the [[Lore:Ordinators|Ordinators]] were charged with keeping away Daedra worshippers.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Sinnammu Mirpal|Sinnammu Mirpal]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Mehrunes Dagon's sentiments towards Sotha Sil's threats for not upholding the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] were of indifference, with Ald Sotha used a reference for how easily he can destroy things that he pleases.{{Ref|name=ONTWMD|{{Cite Book|Transcribed Words of Mehrunes Dagon}}}} After Dagon violated the pact by attacking [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], the Clockwork God was finally able to enact revenge for Ald Sotha, and banished the oathbreaker with the aid of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] to the Deadlands.{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}}{{ref|name=2920v11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}

By {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] had taken over the ruins, and it became the headquarters of the Brotherhood in Vvardenfell. This attempt at expansion was viciously repelled by the native [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], the ancient enemies of the Brotherhood. The Tong infiltrated the ruins and executed the local [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]], [[Lore:Severa Magia|Severa Magia]], which helped to put an end to the Dark Brotherhood's activities on Vvardenfell.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Execute Severa Magia|Execute Severa Magia]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Tong also liberated several [[Lore:Threads of the Webspinner|Threads of the Webspinner]] from the ruins, artifacts created by [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] which rightfully belonged to their patron, [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]].{{ref|[[Morrowind:Threads of the Webspinner|Threads of the Webspinner]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Tribunal Temple also made a pilgrimage to Dagon's shrine that year, and recovered the [[Lore:Shoes of St. Rilms|shoes]] of [[Lore:Rilms|St. Rilms]] from the ruins.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Shoes of St. Rilms|Shoes of St. Rilms]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The ruins were likely destroyed in {{Year|4E 5}}, when [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]] crashed into Vivec City. This impact devastated the surrounding area, creating [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]], a large crater filled with boiling water from the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=BK|The Infernal City}}}}
