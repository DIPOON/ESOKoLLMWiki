# Lore:Skeleton Key

The [[Lore:Skeleton Key|Skeleton Key]] (also called the '''Skeleton's Key''') is a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]].{{ref|[[OB:Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue during [[OB:Blood of the Daedra|Blood of the Daedra]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

In appearance, the Skeleton Key doesn't always take the form of a key, and sometimes manifests as a lockpick instead. In its key form, it can be used to unlock any lock. As a lockpick, it is nigh unbreakable and can get past even the toughest locks. The two limitations placed on the Key by wizards who sought to protect their storehouses were that the Key could only be used once a day and it would never be the property of one thief for too long, eventually disappearing.{{ref|name=AR/DF|''[[Arena:Skeleton's Key|Arena]]/[[Daggerfall:Skeleton's Key|Daggerfall]]'' in-game description}}

The artifact functions as a tool for "unlocking" all things, including portals, hidden potential, and other unknown possibilities.{{ref|name=Karliah|[[Skyrim:Karliah|Karliah]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Its ultimate function, however, is to unlock and hold open the Ebonmere, a portal to Nocturnal's realm of [[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]], located in the [[Lore:Twilight Sepulcher|Twilight Sepulcher]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. The [[Lore:Nightingale Trinity|Nightingales]] are tasked with guarding the Sepulcher and retrieving the Key should it be stolen. Unfortunately, the Prince is said to allow the Skeleton Key to be stolen or lost periodically, whether by purpose or apathy.{{ref|name=TN1|{{Cite Book|The Nightingales Vol. 1}}}}<noinclude>

==Mythology==
According to pre-ri'Datta Khajiiti myth, sometime after the death of [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], Noctra (Nocturnal) was born of his [[Lore:Ebony|black blood]] at the steps of the Void Gate. [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]] battled her "until she knew she was not [[Lore:Namiira|Namiira]]", and when the fighting subsided, Noctra was brought before Azurah to be judged. Azurah allowed Noctra to live so long as she served Azurah and the ja-Kha'jay. Instead, when the opportunity was right, Noctra stole the Skeleton Key from Azurah and fled into the void.{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}} 

According to one pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany Khajiit account, Noctra addressed Boethra as mother and master, as she'd trained under her blade. During a battle to defend the [[Lore:Moons|Lunar Lattice]] from [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molagh]] and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Merrunz]], Noctra, after learning from Azurah's vanquishing of [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaarmina]] and steeling herself to leave all she had behind, used the Skeleton Key on herself in order to stop Merid-Nunda from accessing the [[Lore:Sun|Aether Prism]]. This caused her form to become as "shadow and cloth, a cloak of darkness" and "grow mighty, taller than the rest" of the deities present, until it went on to blend with the night itself, shrouding the Aether Prism and foiling Merid-Nunda's attempt as the burning light that was covering everyone present receded. It is said that in this act, with the Key, Noctra could say that she was not Namiira at last.{{Ref|name=Blade|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume III}}}}

==History==

The first supposed wielder of the Key was the thief Arrovan. After disappearing, the Key always chose inaccessible places to hide, and for the benefit of historians Arrovan made a list of the various resting places.{{Ref|name=ARTIL|{{TIL|Artifact Dialogue — The Imperial Library|skeletons-key}}}}

In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the key was in the possession of [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], who kept it in his [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]]. Nocturnal tried to take control of the Clockwork City in {{Year|2E 582}}. She summoned Sotha Sil's Shadow, who replaced him as ruler of the Clockwork City. Meanwhile, a [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostle]], Chancellor {{Lore Link|Gascone Dusant}}, sought to deliver the Skeleton Key, which had been accidentally found by a [[Lore:Factotum|Factotum]] beneath the Brass Fortress, to [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], only for the Key to be stolen by the [[Lore:Blackfeather Court|Blackfeather Court]]. [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]], assisted by [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] and the Clockwork Apostles, unveiled the plot, but were unable to reclaim the Skeleton Key before it was taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow. 

Nocturnal and the Shadow then attempted to seize control of the Clockwork City by using the key to try unseat the real Sotha Sil from his control center, the Throne Aligned, and have the shadow take over it. The Vestige and [[Lore:Luciana Pullo|Proctor Luciana Pullo]] intervened and defeated the shadow in combat, but Nocturnal had her shadows envelop the area as a countermeasure, in an attempt to consume the heroes. Luciana refused to yield, and used her shadow-banishing light to keep Nocturnal's shadows at bay. Divayth Fyr arrived at the last second and kept Sotha Sil alive and constrained the shadow. They bought the Vestige enough time to remove the Skeleton Key from the Throne Aligned and awaken Sotha Sil, who then reunited with his shadow and banished Nocturnal from his realm. After thwarting Nocturnal's plot, Sotha Sil declined the Vestige's request to have the Key as a reward, and instead entrusted it to Divayth Fyr to keep it away from Nocturnal's clutches for as long as possible.{{ref|name=ESOCWC|Events of [[Online:DLC#Clockwork City|ESO: Clockwork City]]}} A replica of the Skeleton Key was created for the Vestige, however, as thanks for their aid.{{ref|{{Item Link|quality=4|Skeleton Key Replica|id=134288}}, obtainable from [[Online:Razoufa|Razoufa]] after completing the [[Online:Clockwork City#Story Quests|Clockwork City Story Quests]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

During the Imperial Simulacrum, the Skeleton Key was uncovered a number of times. The [[Lore:Hero|Eternal Champion]] discovered the artifact in an unknown province while following rumors of the Key having appeared in one of the known dungeons.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}} The key was then recovered in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] by the Spire Thieves of Broken Wing sometime during  [[Lore:Wars#The War of Bend'r-mahk|The War of Bend'r-mahk]]. In 3E 397, the [[Lore:Soul of Conflict|Soul of Conflict]] invaded Broken Wing and exterminated the Spire Thieves. Within the dungeon, they located the Key within their treasure troves.{{Ref|name=Shadowkey|Events of [[Shadowkey:Broken_Wing_II|Broken Wing]] in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}}

In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:Hero|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] was tasked by Nocturnal to kill a mage in their stronghold in return for the Skeleton Key. However, the mage had already had a contingency plan in case the Daedric Prince had sent someone to slay them. They created a fake artifact ([[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]], [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]], or the [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|Warlock's Ring]]) that functioned identically to the true artifact to tempt the agent with, though it would degrade after several days; enough time to flee when the illusion was broken. Accounts conflict if the agent took the mage up on the offer and was bamboozled, or if they slayed the mage and received the Key in return.{{ref|name=DFNocturnal|[[Daggerfall:Nocturnal's Quest|Nocturnal's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

The Key later came into the possession of [[Lore:Gentleman Jim Stacey|Gentleman Jim Stacey]] while he lived in Hammerfell. He later became the leader of the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] in [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. By {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] allied themselves with the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] Master in Vvardenfell, Sjoring Hard-Hart. The alliance used the Fighters Guild as an extension of the Camonna Tong's influence, hunting down and eliminating the struggling Thieves Guild to prevent them gaining a foothold in Vvardenfell.{{ref|name=FG|Events of the [[Morrowind:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] questline in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} It is rumored that after Hard-Heart and his lieutenant's corruption was discovered, they were brought to justice - though it is unknown if this was the work of the Thieves Guild or an internal cleansing by the Fighters Guild.{{ref|name=Hard-Heart|[[Morrowind:Kill Hard-Heart (Fighters Guild)|Fighters Guild]] and [[Morrowind:Kill Hard-Heart (Thieves Guild)|Thieves Guild]] quests both having the same objective in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} Scholarly documents contradict the victory against the Camonna Tong, instead stating that the corrupt Fighter's Guild and the Tong wiped their opposition out.{{ref|name=MOM|{{Cite Book|Myth or Menace?}}}} Regardless, the Skeleton Key was either taken from Gentleman Jim Stacey's corpse after his loss,{{ref|name=MWKMT|[[Morrowind:Kill the Master Thief|Kill the Master Thief]] quest in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} or he passed on the Key to the next [[Lore:Nerevarine|Thieves Guild Master]] after their victory.{{ref|name=Hard-Heart}}

Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, an artifact known as the Eye of Nocturnal was stolen from Nocturnal's shrine in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]] region. Nocturnal tasked the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] with recovering it. The thieves, an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] couple in [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], hid it in a flooded cave in [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]]. The Hero of Kvatch returned it to Nocturnal and was rewarded with the Skeleton Key.{{ref|name=OBNocturnal|[[Oblivion:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

Some time before {{Year|4E 201}}, the Key was stolen from the Twilight Sepulcher by [[Lore:Mercer Frey|Mercer Frey]], a corrupt Nightingale. Its long-term loss resulted in a decline of the Skyrim branch of the Thieves Guild due to bad luck without Nocturnal's influence. The guild's influence declined until their only presence was in the Ratways beneath [[Lore:Riften|Riften]]. Mercer used the Key to unlock his potential, giving him a number of magical abilities, but he was eventually confronted by the Nightingales beneath the Great Statue of [[Lore:Irkngthand|Irkngthand]]. Mercer was killed, and the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] brought the Key back to the Sepulcher, reopening the Ebonmere.{{ref|name=Karliah}}{{Ref|name=SRTG|[[Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)#Quests|Thieves Guild questline]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
