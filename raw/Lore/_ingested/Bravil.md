[[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] is a [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] city located on the banks of the [[Lore:Niben Bay|Niben Bay]], south of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. It is found in the [[Lore:Nibenay Valley|Nibenay Valley]], which was historically said to be a tropical region enclosed by an equatorial rain forest and broken up by rivers.{{ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} The [[Lore:Larsius River|Larsius River]] passes through and wraps around the town, as the [[Lore:Green Road|Green Road]] curves around it. A remarkably large [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] settlement is found underneath the city.{{ref|name=ACOV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} The symbol of the city is the wild stag.{{ref|name=GF|[[Books:Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel|Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of the Imperial Champion]]; Hall Steward Longinus Attius}}

Under [[Lore:Alessian Order|Marukhati]] rule, cities of the Nibenay Valley were forced to become mercantile magocracies, allowing them to be wealthiest city-states in the region throughout the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=PGE1}} In the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Bravil was built in a unique Nibenese architectural style.{{Ref|name=ESO|[[ON:Bravil|Appearance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} By the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Bravil was a squalid town described as one of the poorest in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]{{Ref|name=GTB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Bravil}}}} with houses built like shacks, being wooden and stacked upon each other. The Larsius River that runs through the town serves as Bravil's sewers. Because of its location on the bay's shore and the marshes and swamps that surround it, the climate is damp.

==History==
[[File:MW-item-Light Cuirasses (cropped).jpg|thumb|left|Imperial [[Lore:Newt|River-Newt]] Scale armor, used by the Nibenay River Guard]]

It is known that [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] once lived in the region before Bravil was established. The ''[[Lore:Daughter of the Niben|Daughter of the Niben]]'' claims what the Ayleids called the region around Bravil translates to "home", which is ''"Math"'' (or sometimes ''"Hame"'') in [[Lore:Ayleidoon|Ayleidoon]].{{Ref|name=DOTN}} The Niben provided food and transportation, and their settlement flourished as a result of increased trade after the establishment of the great [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], being even more populous than Bravil is today.{{ref|name=ACOV}}{{Ref|name=DOTN}} The Alessian Army, led by the [[Lore:Alessia|Empress Alessia]]'s centurion [[Lore:Teo Bravillius Tasus|Teo Bravillius Tasus]], attacked the Ayleids in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{Ref|name=DOTN}} After discovering the Ayleid's ability to use [[Lore:Alteration|Alteration]] type magic to defend themselves, Tasus managed to defeat them. Because of his success, the town owes its name to Tasus.{{Ref|name=DOTN}} It is unclear when exactly the modern city itself was founded. At some point after {{Year|1E 700}},{{ref|name=PGE1H|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}{{ref|name=TBoRM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} the mortal [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] precursor to the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]] lived in a predecessor town at the future site of the city, but it is unknown if it was called Bravil at this time.{{ref|name=TNMT|{{Cite Book|The Night Mother's Truth}}}} The main city itself has been in existence since at least {{Year|1E 1152}}.{{Ref|name=IG|{{Cite Book|Imbel Genealogy}}}}

Restrictions imposed by the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] against certain kinds of meat-eating and animal cults soon made agriculture and husbandry nearly impossible, and as a result, many of the Eastern Imperials were forced to become merchants, soon allowing the Nibenay Valley to become the wealthiest city-state in the region. Despite this, the rich and powerful merchant class were still forced to pay tithes to the Alessian priesthood, reminding them of their status of tenanted citizens. The [[Lore:War of Righteousness|War of Righteousness]] ended Alessian rule in {{Year|1E 2321}}, and control of the Nibenay Valley reverted to a mercantile-magocracy, which the western Colovians still thought far too arcane to consider a reunification of Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=PGE1}}

In {{Year|2E 581}}, [[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]] dreamed that an army of Daedra and a legion of the undead under [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] would raze Bravil and destroy all of Cyrodiil. In this dream, Bravil would be defended by the [[Lore:Imperial_Legion#Known_Legions|Fourth Legion]]. It is unknown if these events actually occurred.{{ref|name=EGTCyrodiil|{{Cite Book|EGT|Dreams of Cyrodiil}}}} When the [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] fell in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], Captain {{Lore Link|Jena Apinia}} had her legion flee the city and go to Bravil, asking the mayor for help. He refused, and the citizens rioted against Captain Apinia, resulting in her slaughtering many of them and looting the Castle, earning her the title the Butcher of Bravil.{{ref|name=TBoB|{{Cite Book|The Butcher of Bravil}}}}{{Ref|name=RD|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Debts of War|Debts of War]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

County Bravil became independent from the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] of {{Year|3E 433}}. Bravil was often at odds with [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], which had also gained independence, until the two counties were subdued by [[Lore:Titus Mede|Emperor Titus Mede]].{{Ref|name=TIC|1={{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}} At the onset of the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Dominion]] invasion forces coming out of Elsweyr flanked Imperial defenses along the Valenwood border, allowing them to soon take Leyawiin and lay siege to Bravil. The city fell the next year. At the climax of the war, Dominion forces in Bravil sought to counterattack the largely Nordic forces under General Jonna, but these efforts failed, leading to the encirclement of the Imperial City and the destruction of the Dominion's main army in Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=GWB|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}    

[[File:LG-quest-Brotherhood's Justice.jpg|thumb|left|Overlooking the city of Bravil]]
By the 27th of Hearthfire, {{Year|4E 188}}, the city had erupted into violence due to a war of control being waged by Cyrodiil's two largest [[Lore:Skooma|skooma]] traffickers. The Listener of the Dark Brotherhood, [[Lore:Alisanne Dupre|Alisanne Dupre]], was forced to employ sellswords to protect her private residence in the city. By the 1st of Sun's Dusk, things in Bravil had come to a head: the statue of the Lucky Old Lady had been destroyed, and Alisanne Dupre had abandoned her residence to defend the Night Mother's crypt. Rasha, the apparent leader of the [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]] sanctuary, sent Garnag and Andronica to Bravil to aid her. After Garnag and Andronica left and the Cheydinhal Sanctuary didn't receive any communication from the city for nineteen days, the remaining members feared the worst. Those fears were realized on the morning of the 21st, when Garnag returned alone with the stone coffin of the Night Mother. Garnag told that the crypt of the Night Mother was raided, Andronica cut to pieces, and Alisanne Dupre was burned alive in a storm of mage fire. Garnag managed to fend off the attackers and transport the coffin safely out of the city, though he got gravely injured and was likely to lose his right eye.{{Ref|name=CJ2|{{Cite Book|Cicero's Journal - Volume 2}}}}

Around the 18th of Morning Star, {{Year|4E 201}}, Daynas Valen became wanted in the city when he killed a collector in possession of the Ivory Claw. He fled for three days of nonstop riding to escape his pursuers, taking a ship in [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] to [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]].{{ref|name=DVJ|{{Cite Book|Daynas Valen's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}

==The Lucky Old Lady==
[[File:OB-quest-Whispers of Death.jpg|thumb|left|The Lucky Old Lady]]
[[File:LG-quest-Chaos in Bravil.jpg|thumb|right|The Destruction of the Lucky Old Lady]]The Lucky Old Lady was a statue which once stood in a square east of Bravil's Great Chapel of Mara. 

No one knows when the statue to her was erected, or who the artist was, but it has stood there for thousands of years, since the first era. A story printed in the book ''[[Lore:Daughter of the Niben|Daughter of the Niben]]'' relayed the story of a woman born into poverty, who became famous for her kindness and charity. When she was a child, a priest soothed her woes of not knowing the identity of her father by telling her she was the daughter of [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], "the God of Mercy, Charity and Well-Earned Luck". After this encounter she took to referring to herself as a "child of Luck" when asked about her parentage. For showing kindness to an incognito prince who was stripped of his senses after being kidnapped and cursed by witches, she was rewarded with countless riches after he recovered from his ordeal. She shared her great wealth with the rest of Bravil, and lived to an old age. Before the statue was destroyed in {{Year|4E 188}}{{Ref|name=CJ2}}, people would visit the statue to ask the Lucky Old Lady for her blessing of luck.{{Ref|name=DOTN|{{Cite Book|Daughter of the Niben}}}}

In actuality, the statue's true purpose was to act as a conduit between the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]], whose crypt laid beneath the statue, and the Listener of the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]]. {{Ref|name=COTN|The [[Oblivion:Crypt of the Night Mother|Crypt of the Night Mother]]}}{{Ref|name=Arquen|[[Oblivion:Arquen|Arquen]]'s dialogue during [[Oblivion:Honor Thy Mother|Honor Thy Mother]]}} After the destruction of the statue, the Night Mother's remains were removed and ultimately sent to a Dark Brotherhood chapter in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{Ref|name=FOTDB|The conclusion of [[Legends:The Fall of the Dark Brotherhood|The Fall of the Dark Brotherhood]]}}{{Ref|name=NMS|The [[Skyrim:Night Mother|Night Mother]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
