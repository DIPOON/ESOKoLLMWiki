The [[Lore:West Gash|West Gash]] is one of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]'s largest contiguous areas, stretching from the city of [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]] and the [[Lore:Odai River|Odai River]], to the northwest coastline around historically [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]]-owned settlements (i.e., [[Lore:Gnisis|Gnisis]], [[Lore:Ald Velothi|Ald Velothi]] and [[Lore:Khuul|Khuul]]) The region is mostly rocky shrubland throughout the interior, marked by natural rocky bridges and arches, as well as the sparse highland along the north coast.{{Ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}} The land between the [[Lore:Salothran Ancestral Tomb|Salothran Ancestral Tomb]] to the north, to [[Lore:Caldera|Caldera]] and later Balmora to the south is a long stretch of land that separates the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] from the [[Lore:Ashlands|Ashlands]] like a gash in the earth.{{Ref|name=MWGash|[[Morrowind:West Gash|West Gash]] location in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Early History & Tribunal Myth===
[[File:MW-concept-15.jpg|thumb|right|Vivec and the Ruddy Man]]
[[File:ON-place-Arkngthunch-Sturdumz.jpg|thumb|left|Arkngthunch-Sturdumz]]
According to [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] mythology, the West Gash was the result of an epic battle between the Warrior-Poet, [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] and the [[Lore:Ruddy Man|Ruddy Man]], the fifth monster that escaped the warrior's spear, [[Lore:Muatra|Muatra]].{{Ref|name=36LOVS18|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 18}}}} As the story dictates, a Velothi boy from Gnisis found the monster's carapace and became possessed by it. Vivec found the boy near his home village and their resulting duel created an upheaval of the land that created the West Gash. Pilgrims that travel down the land site will hear sounds from the battle.{{Ref|name=36LOVS28|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 28}}}} The temple built the Shrine of Valor where the battle took place, in the old sea cave, {{Lore Link|Koal Cave}}. Pilgrims are advised to bring [[Lore:Dreugh Wax|dreugh's wax]] to the shrine as a token to Vivec's victory.{{Ref|name=TPP|{{Cite Book|The Pilgrim's Path}}}} In another story in Vivec's sermons, the Warrior-Poet had to fight the Flute-and-Pipe [[Lore:Ogre|Ogre]]s of the West Gash, another of the many factions stirred up by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] while they prepared for the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]].{{Ref|name=36LOVS31|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 31}}}}

Both the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] and Dwemer had lived in the region since antiquity, long before the Tribunal Temple was ever established. In the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], {{Lore Link|High Velothi Culture}} had come to a rapid decline and the Chimer decided to settle next to the abandoned Velothi towers throughout the region. The Chimer that would later create Gnisis settled south of the old tower, Arvs-Drelen, next to the [[Lore:River Samsi|River Samsi]]'s estuary. The Chimer that settled in these settlements would become the House Dunmer while those that cling to their nomadic roots became the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]].{{Ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The [[Lore:Urshilaku Tribe|Urshilaku Tribe]] have lived in the northern West Gash for years.{{Ref|name=ATAC|{{Cite Book|Ashlander Tribes and Customs}}}} The earliest examples of Dwemer colonies also date here{{Ref|name=BTAOM}} but unlike the southeast, the Dwemer left barely a footnote in the West Gash, however, there are a couple of examples. The ruins of {{Lore Link|Arkngthunch-Sturdumz}} sits on the northwest point of the island, next to the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]]'s western entrance. Its name translates to the "Graveyard of the Ghost Invaders" in [[Lore:Dwemeris|Dwemeris]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadSturdumz|[[Online:Arkngthunch-Sturdumz|Arkngthunch-Sturdumz]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Other than that, the only other Dwemer ruin in the region is {{Lore Link|Bethamez}}, located deep in the Gnisis [[Lore:Kwama|Kwama]] Mine. It was discovered in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{Ref|name=MWBeth|[[Morrowind:Bethamez (quest)|Bethamez]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The West Gash has a wide variety of ancient Velothi landmarks such as ancestral tombs, and Daedric shrines. Since at least King [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]]'s reign over [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the temples, [[Lore:Ashalmawia|Ashalmawia]] and [[Lore:Maelkashishi|Maelkashishi]] were active settlements. Ambassadors from these temples traveled to the compound, [[Lore:Assurnabitashpi|Assurnabitashpi]] for the wake of [[Lore:Mordrin Hanin|Mordrin Hanin]]. Many of the guests were killed during the ninth day of mourning in Hanin's attempt to bring them with him to the afterlife.{{Ref|name=HW|{{Cite Book|Hanin's Wake}}}} The ancestral tombs in the West Gash are noteworthy for their status and ancient history. Seven of the thirty revered families of Vvardenfell ({{Lore Link|Ginith}}, {{Lore Link|Rethandus}}, {{Lore Link|Salothran}}, {{Lore Link|Seran}}, {{Lore Link|Telvayn}}, {{Lore Link|Tharys}}, and {{Lore Link|Uveran}}) are located throughout the region.{{Ref|name=ATOTRF|{{Cite Book|Ancestral Tombs of the Thirty Revered Families}}}} The {{Lore Link|Salothan}} family was a warrior clan that fought in an important and monumental battle but barely left a footnote in history.{{Ref|name=ONIvulen|[[Online:Ivulen Andromo|Ivulen Andromo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Elves of {{Lore Link|House Hleran}} are an ancient family that traces their origin to the [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]] folk-hero, {{Lore Link|Dranoth Hleran}}, who slew the [[Lore:Emperor Crab|Emperor Crab]], [[Lore:Skar|Skar]] and united the four major tribes.{{Ref|name=ONDranoth|[[Online:Dranoth Hleran|Dranoth Hleran]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The [[Lore:Veloth (family)|Veloth Clan]] have a long and prominent history with Vvardenfell and also claim to descend from the prophet, [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] the Pilgrim.{{Ref|name=ONLoadVeloth|[[Online:Veloth Ancestral Tomb|Veloth Ancestral Tomb]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===The West Gash in the Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Balmora.jpg|thumb|left|Balmora]]
[[File:ON-place-Kudanat Mine.jpg|thumb|right|The Kudanat Ebony Mine]]
Vvardenfell by the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] is lightly populated, except for the four ashlander tribes. The northwest portion of the island was taken over by [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]] at the behest of the Great House Dunmer in [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]]. They were sent there to exploit the land of its resource{{Ref|name=ESOLive15|''[[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]] [https://www.imperial-library.info/content/eso-live-episode-15-lore-time-lawrence-schick Episode 15: Lore-time with Lawrence Schick — The Imperial Library'']}} but eventually by {{Year|2E 582}}, House Redoran had expanded their control of Vvardenfell from the Gnisis region, all the way south toward Balmora and [[Lore:Suran|Suran]].{{Ref|name=HCS|{{Cite Book|Hlaalu Construction Syndic}}}} 

Begrudgingly, however, the Redoran were forced to employ their rivals [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] to build these settlements and even trade. So even though their power extended to roughly half of the island, Balmora and Suran are noticeably built in the Hlaalu style than the Redoran shell design, and the Hlaalu now had a larger foothold than previously in [[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]].{{Ref|name=HCS}} In that time also, the [[Lore:Circle of Champions|Circle of Champions]] built a camp near the West Gash's [[Lore:Wayshrines|wayshrine]] to the ruins, [[Lore:Ularra|Ularra]], where they engaged in arena combat.{{Ref|name=ONUlarra|[[Online:Ularra|Ularra]] location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

House Redoran acquired complete control of the West Gash, from Balmora to the coast. On the road north of the city, they built a large compound, and slowly but surely, they expanded their territory north into the environs of the West Gash. At some point in the mid-Second Era, House Redoran took control of the land north of the Caldera Crater{{Ref|name=ONEris|[[Online:Councilor Eris#Of Faith and Family|Councilor Eris]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and opened with great fanfare, the {{Lore Link|Kudanat Mine}} for its promising [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] veins. It later became evident that the ore in Kudanat was shallow and the mine's hype was soon played out.{{Ref|name=ONLoadKudanat|[[Online:Kudanat Mine|Kudanat Mine]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} House Redoran was forced to leave the area when an ash storm blew over and in their absence, the [[Lore:Zainab Tribe|Zainab Tribe]] took it over.{{Ref|name=ONEris}} Not long after, the Redoran nobleman, {{Lore Link|Ulran Releth}} sought refuge with them after he was exiled by his house in Balmora.{{Ref|name=ONKhamishi|[[Online:Wise Woman Khamishi|Wise Woman Khamishi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

[[File:ON-place-Redoran Garrison.jpg|thumb|left|The Redoran Garrison]]
[[File:ON-place-Khartag Point.jpg|thumb|right|Khartag Point]]
By orders of Ulran's father, Councilor {{Lore Link|Eris Releth}} ordered his captain, {{Lore Link|Brivan Malrom}} to displace the Ashlanders around Kudanat Mine,{{Ref|name=HRO|{{Cite Book|House Redoran Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} unaware that his son was hiding there. Captain Brivan led his mercenaries, {{Lore Link|Ferhara's Warclaws}} to the mine and blindly slaughtered them.{{Ref|name=HRO}} Ulran tried to talk them out of it but he was killed amid the chaos.{{Ref|name=ONUlran|[[Online:Ulran Releth|Ulran Releth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} All the while, his sister, {{Lore Link|Veya Releth}} went missing, trying to find her brother. She eventually found her body and [[Lore:Memory Stone|memory stone]] in the hollow Kudanat Mine and set forth her plans for revenge against the captain and her father.{{Ref|name=ONFaith|[[Online:Of Faith and Family|Of Faith and Family]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

As the Redoran Council assembled at the Redoran Garrison north of Balmora, Veya attacked the garrison and slaughtered her way to the meeting. In her wake, she killed her father and the captain until she was put down by her mentor, [[Lore:Naryu Virian|Naryu Virian]] and her [[Lore:The Vestige|associate]]. Because she killed without a writ, the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] would put Veya to death but with the help of the Redoran High Councilor, the whole situation was swept under the rug and Veya escaped to the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]].{{Ref|name=ONFamily|[[Online:Family Reunion|Family Reunion]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

By the mid-Second Era, a group of [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]ish abolitionists called the {{Lore Link|Ropefish}} started making the rounds across the entire island while they set their base on a sea cave in the West Gash. They would liberate slaves from places such as the [[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] region and put them on a boat to the mainland. Eventually, they caught the attention of the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] syndicate and a meeting was arranged with the Ropefish leader, [[Lore:Khartag|Khartag]]. That meeting went awry and the Camonna Tong, led by {{Lore Link|Avrusa Duleri}} attacked the cave. Despite the resistance, the Ropefish were unable to withstand the assault and Khartag was at his wit's ends. An adventurer traveled through the battlefield and rescued anyone they came across. Ultimately, they defeated the Camonna Tong commander but were unable to save Khartag's life. Defeated, but undeterred, the Ropefish continued their operations{{Ref|name=ONLast|[[Online:A Smuggler's Last Stand|A Smuggler's Last Stand]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} while Khartag became a local hero to the Orcish people. The cave and rocky promontory that he called home were now called [[Lore:Khartag Point|Khartag Point]] and became a famous pilgrimage spot for Orcs.{{Ref|name=MWKhartag|[[Morrowind:Khartag Point|Khartag Point]] location in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Vvardenfell's Expansion and the Caldera Land Grab===
[[File:MW-place-Caldera.jpg|thumb|left|Caldera]]
[[File:MW-item-Raw Ebony.jpg|thumb|right|The Raw Ebony deposits of Caldera]]
[[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s invasion over Morrowind was interrupted when the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] solidified the province's integration into the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]]. One of the treaty's terms was to mark Vvardenfell as a temple preserve protected by Imperial authority. It was previously undeveloped and unpopulated, except for the ashlander tribes and Great House settlements sanctioned by the Temple. However, several years later in {{Year|3E 414}}, King [[Lore:Athyn Llethan|Athyn Llethan]] revoked the Temple's ban, causing a vast flood of [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] colonists and House Dunmer to come and expand on the territory.{{Ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} 

The land grab for the West Gash between the Hlaalu and Redoran ended with both parties acquiring familiar territory. House Hlaalu managed to take over Balmora, as well as Vvardenfell's southern tier (i.e., Seyda Neen and Suran),{{Ref|name=MWHlaalu|[[Morrowind:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] faction in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} while House Redoran regained most of the West Gash from Gnisis to the coast, and even more with [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]] and [[Lore:Maar Gan|Maar Gan]] in the Ashlands.{{Ref|name=MWRedoran|[[Morrowind:House Redoran|House Redoran]] faction in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Redoran Council had split the West Gash politically three-ways,{{Ref|name=RBO3E|{{Cite Book|Red Book of 3E 426}}}} from the West Gash around Gnisis, which was ruled by Councilor {{Lore Link|Hlaren Ramoran}}.{{Ref|name=MWGenHetman|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue H|Hetman Abelmawia]] generic description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The '''North Gash''', which was governed by Councilor {{Lore Link|Miner Arobar}} and the '''South Gash''', which was governed by Councilor {{Lore Link|Athyn Sarethi}}.{{Ref|name=RBO3E}}

Despite the spread of their newfound territory, the Great Houses squabbled for more land, especially land that intersected between them. The biggest example of this was the land grab for Caldera and the crater in the mountains. In {{Year|3E 427}}, the land was acquired by the {{Lore Link|Caldera Mining Company}}, a company chartered by the Empire that was granted a monopoly to extract the [[Lore:Raw Ebony|raw ebony]] while working with House Hlaalu.{{Ref|name=MWGenCaldera|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue C|Caldera]] generic description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} House Redoran believed that Caldera sat in territory that was rightfully theirs and because the company was hired by the Empire, they could not start a House War for it. Meanwhile, they were suspicious that the Hlaalu Governor, {{Lore Link|Odral Helvi}}, who oversaw the town was corrupted.{{Ref|name=MWRumCaldera|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue L|Caldera]] latest rumors in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Odral Helvi was extracting ebony that was not reported to the Empire for his project called the {{Lore Link|Ashlands Management Fund}}.{{Ref|name=SCL|{{Cite Book|Secret Caldera Ledger|ns_base=MW}}}} It is unknown if he was caught, but House Redoran sent one of their agents to find evidence of their corruption.{{Ref|name=MWEvidence|[[Morrowind:Evidence of Corruption|Evidence of Corruption]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

In that same year, the Redoran noble, {{Lore Link|Arethan Mandas}} went crazy when his daughter was kidnapped by the Telvanni lord, [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]. Amid this crisis, Mandas fled to the cave, {{Lore Link|Milk}} and proclaimed himself, the "Lord of West Gash". He managed to gain a following of brigands and demanded tribute from travelers on the road toward [[Lore:Maar Gan|Maar Gan]]. Lord {{Lore Link|Athyn Sarethi}} sent a lawman of House Redoran to deal with the Mad Lord of Milk. Arethan's reign was put to an end, but it is unknown if he was cured of his [[Lore:Madness (disease)|madness]] or if he was killed in action.{{Ref|name=MWMilk|[[Morrowind:The Mad Lord of Milk|The Mad Lord of Milk]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===The West Gash in the Fourth Era===
When [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] erupted in {{Year|4E 5}}, the entire island of Vvardenfell was destroyed by the waves of lava coming from the fire cone. The West Gash was obliterated in the wake of the destruction. The town, Gnisis was plowed over by the lava and its people were burned alive, even unaware of what was happening. One of the only things left from the town was the resident, {{Lore Link|Neria Relethyl}}, who passed out on the Samsi's riverbed and managed to avoid the fire. She recovered two days after in the Temple of Azura in Blacklight and essentially found Gnisis had been wiped off the face of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{Ref|name=TRY1|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. I}}}} The widespread destruction was reported all over the province and many mourned the loss of loved ones in cities such as Balmora. Balmora was barely recognizable but a relief effort led by House Redoran from [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] was sent to repair the city and other settlements hit the hardest. It took roughly two months or so for the town to recover.{{Ref|name=TRY2|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}}

==Notable Locales==
[[File:ON-place-West Gash Wayshrine.jpg|thumb|right|West Gash's wayshrine]]
;[[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]]: The second largest city on Vvardenfell and the seat of power for the Great House Hlaalu in the district.
;[[Lore:Gnisis|Gnisis]]: A medium-sized town owned by the Great House Redoran and occupied by the Velothi, Ashlanders that settled to life as a House Dunmer.
;[[Lore:Caldera|Caldera]]: An Imperial charter town that was founded in the late Third Era by the Caldera Mining Company. It is built in the Western Imperial style and is comprised of colonials.
;{{Lore Link|Caldera Crater}}: A large crater in the mountains of southwest Vvardenfell. It has a large vein of raw ebony that is prized by the Great Houses.
;[[Lore:Khartag Point|Khartag Point]]: A rock formation that is famous to the local [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s. It was named after an [[Lore:Khartag|Orcish]] champion and it later became the site of a statue dedicated to [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]].
;{{Lore Link|Koal Cave}}: A holy site to the Tribunal Temple, where Vivec fought the Ruddy Man and confiscated his carapace.
;[[Lore:Veloth Ancestral Tomb|Veloth Ancestral Tomb]]: The tomb of an ancient family with prominent ties to the province's history.
