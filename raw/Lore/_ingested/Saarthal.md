[[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]] was the first city built in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] after the arrival of [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]] sometime in the middle of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} Saarthal was also the first capital of Nord civilization in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], as well as the largest of the ancient Nordic cities.{{Ref|name=TolfDial|[[Skyrim:Tolfdir|Tolfdir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=NoT|{{Cite Book|Night of Tears (book)}}}} The city was destroyed late in the Merethic Era by the Snow Elves, and then rebuilt by [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] after he retook the city.{{Ref|name=NoT}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Imperial Report on Saarthal}}}} It was destroyed and finally abandoned sometime in the late Merethic or early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{Ref|name=DVN|{{Cite Book|Daynas Valen's Notes}}}} In the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the ruins of the city were located by archaeologists near the modern city of [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} After [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] and his followers were corrupted into [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] and the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s, they fled to the northern wastes near Saarthal.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}}<noinclude>

[[File:SR-interior-Saarthal.jpg|The Saarthal excavation, circa {{Year|4E 201}}|thumb|right]]
During the city's construction, the Nords unearthed the [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]], which they attempted to keep secret by burying it under the city. The [[Lore:Falmer|Snow Elves]] learned of the powerful artifact, however, and wanted it for themselves. They attacked the city in order to secure this power, an event known as the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]].{{Ref|name=NoT}} One of the participants was [[Lore:Serenarth (Snow Elf)|Serenarth]], a Snow Elf wizard who made a bargain with malevolent forces after being shot by Ysgramor, and has been encased in ice ever since with the threat of thawing out and exacting vengeance.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}} Everyone except for Ysgramor and his two sons, [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]] and [[Lore:Ylgar|Ylgar]], were killed and the city was burned to the ground.{{Ref|name=SotRv2|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 2}}}} Ysgramor fled back to Atmora to gather an army with which to fight the Elves.{{Ref|name=SotRv5|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 5}}}} Before the Snow Elves could find their prize, Ysgramor and his Five Hundred Companions returned, strengthened by powerful enchanted weapons and armor created by [[Lore:Ahzidal|Ahzidal]],{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ahzidal's Descent}}}} drove the Elves back from Saarthal, and began a campaign to drive them from Skyrim entirely.{{Ref|name=BtAoM}} Reports from Dominion scouts suggest the ruins were still visible during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|name=ONVole|[[ON:Vole|Vole]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Although the city was lost, the Eye remained buried under Saarthal until the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] unearthed it in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Under Saarthal|Under Saarthal]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The local Nords, however, did not approve of the college mages digging among the dead of the ruined city.{{Ref|Rumors in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

{{Lore Link|Jyrik Gauldurson}}, one of the three sons of [[Lore:Gauldur|Archmage Gauldur]], fled to Saarthal after his treachery in seizing the Archmage's powerful amulet. He fought and killed ten of [[Lore:Geirmund|Archmage Geirmund]]'s battlemages before finally succumbing, and was sealed within the ruins.{{Ref|name=DVN}}

One of Ysgramor's Five Hundred Companions, the legendary warrior Reynir, claimed to have survived the Night of Tears and sailed back to Atmora with Ylgar.{{Ref|name=RtD|{{Cite Book|Reynir the Destroyer (book)|ns_base=Online}}}}
