[[Lore:Yngol Barrow|Yngol Barrow]] (also called the '''Barrow of Yngol'''){{Ref|name=Return|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 19}}}} is an ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic ruin found in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], in the region of [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]]. It is known as the burial ground of [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]], the eldest son of the Harbinger, [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]. In some sources, Yngol Barrow is found below the cliffs of [[Lore:Hsaarik Head|Hsaarik Head]],{{Ref|name=Yngol|{{Cite Book|Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts}}}} though an entrance can be found along the [[Lore:White River|White River]]. It is constructed in the [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]n tradition.<noinclude>

==History==

During a trip across the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]], Ysgramor and the [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] were caught in a terrible storm which caused the loss of both Yngol and the crew of the {{Lore Link|Harakh}}.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 5}}}}{{Ref|name=Notes|{{Cite Book|Notes on Yngol Barrow}}}} When Ysgramor claimed his son's body he had it buried in the Atmoran barrow-hill tradition near where the White River met the Sea of Ghosts.{{Ref|name=Yngol}} After the disbanding of the Five Hundred Companions, Ysgramor would reencounter Yngol Barrow and decreed that a [[Lore:Windhelm|great city]] should be built near by in order that he have a view of the barrow from his seat of power.{{Ref|name=Return}} 

During the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], Yngol Barrow was largely untouched and had become [[Lore:Draugr|Draugr]]-infested. After acquiring the Coral Dragon Claw in [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]], an [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|adventurer]] would delve Yngol Barrow. There they encountered Yngol's Shade and claimed the Helm of Yngol upon his defeat.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Yngol's Barrow|Yngol's Barrow]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
