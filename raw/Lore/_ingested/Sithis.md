[[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]], also known as '''[[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]]''', '''Akel''', '''Is-Not''', '''El''',{{ref|name=tM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} '''Ixtaxh-thtithil-meht''' (meaning '''Exact Egg-Cracker'''),{{Ref|name=ONFamia|[[Online:Famia Mercius#Ixtaxh Xanmeer|Famia Mercius]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}}{{ref|name=Mono2|[[General:The Monomyth (French translation)|French translation]] of ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}} the '''Dread Father''',{{ref|name=OBTeinaava|[[Oblivion:Teinaava|Teinaava]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=AKSW|{{Cite Book|A Kiss, Sweet Mother}}}} '''Night Lord''',{{ref|name=BN|{{Cite Book|Bloodstained Note|ns_base=OB}}}} '''Raj-Sithis''',{{Ref|name=Waku-Mat|[[ON:Waku-Mat|Waku-Mat]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Unsuitable Suitors|Unsuitable Suitors]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} '''Serpent of Chaos''', '''Unmaker''', '''First Creator''',{{Ref|name=Waku-Mat}} '''Final Destroyer''',{{Ref|name=Waku-Mat}} and '''Bringer of Ends''',{{ref|name=ONFS|[[Online:Fluvius Salva|Fluvius Salva]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} is a representation of the one primordial state of chaos. Sithis is most often associated with the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]], an assassin organization dedicated to his worship. He is often associated with serpent-like imagery, and small aspects of Sithis have even been known to reveal themselves in the form of ghostly serpents.{{ref|name=PtLF|[[ON:Aspect of Sithis|Aspect of Sithis]] in the [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online]] questline, [[Online:Pull the Last Fang|Pull the Last Fang]]}} 

Despite being revered as a god, Sithis is neither [[lore:Aedra|Aedric]] nor [[lore:Daedra|Daedric]],{{ref|[[Merchandise:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Tarot Deck and Guidebook|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Tarot Deck and Guidebook]] Death Card, pg 38}} but the embodiment and personification of chaos and change. He is also sometimes equated to the unending [[Lore:Void|Void]], a realm of nothingness beyond Oblivion and Aetherius.{{ref|name=Gwendis|[[ON:Gwendis|Gwendis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}{{ref|name=tM}}{{ref|name=NMT}} The Dark Brotherhood believes that the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of those killed in the name of Sithis will be sent to the Void upon death as well.{{ref|name=NMT}} One of their faithful servants, [[Lore:Lucien Lachance|Lucien Lachance]], was known to have gone to the Void after death. His [[Lore:Ghost|spirit]] claimed to feel disturbances in the Void when the Dread Father was displeased.{{ref|name=SA|[[SR:Spectral Assassin|Spectral Assassin]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

It is said that those who enact the will of Sithis with perfect clarity will one day see the Night Lord himself in the Void, though what that sight may do to the viewer is never discussed.{{ref|name=Eyes|[[ON:Eyes of Sithis|Eyes of Sithis]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Mythology==
[[File:MER-art-Death.jpg|left|thumb|200px|Depiction of Sithis (Tarot Card)]][[File:OB-interior-Night Mother's Crypt 02.jpg|The skeletal remains of the Night Mother, and the children she bore with Sithis|right|thumb]]

It is said that the name "'''Sithis'''" is a corruption of the elder term for Padomay, "'''Psijii'''", which itself was a corruption on Padomay's ancient [[Lore:Ehlnofex|Ehlnofex]] (or Aldmeris) name, "'''PSJJJJ'''". Many of the Daedric characteristics of Sithis are also said to have originated from the teachings of Mephala, gifted to a Nihilist sect of the Chimeri Exodus, whom would go on to found the  [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]].{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Source of Chaos|Source of Chaos]]}}

Variations on the story of Sithis exist in many Tamriellic Pantheons. In most of these, Sithis is related in some way to a version of [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]], the personification and embodiment of order and stasis, and ends with him creating the god [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] so that he can in turn create the Mundus.{{ref|name=tM}} According to a supposed account of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], Sithis is the soul of Padomay,{{ref|name=TTGtC|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}}  mirroring Anuiel's nature as the soul of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]].{{ref|name=tM}} According to Vivec, Stasis and Change, Anu and Padomay, are infinite forces and realms residing in the infinite Void, the latter infinity paradoxically enclosing the others in a manner akin to an encircling sphere. At the intersection of the two forces, where they touch, lies a "perfect circle of pattern and possibility", the Wheel, and inside that Wheel lies the Aurbis which is its foundation. Vivec claims that outside the Wheel exists the Void, which cannot truly be named and is bereft of anything. The Void is said to have more aspects than just Stasis and Change, but they cannot be named as they are outside of true language. Vivec claims Anu and Padomay "awakened" during the process of sub-creation caused by their intersection as "to see your antithesis is to finally awaken". It is said that in reaction to this "each gave birth to their souls", [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] and Sithis. Each of these souls regarded the Aurbis in their own part, and from that came the [[Lore:Gods|et'Ada]], the "original patterns.{{ref|name=TTGtC|group=UOL}} Some sources claim that, rather than being a single wheel, the Aurbis, the intersection of Anu and Padomay, is instead more akin to "a telescope that stretches all the way back to the eye of Anui-El, with Padomaics innumerable along its infinite walls".{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

In the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmeri]] mythos, Sithis was created as 'the sum of all limits' by Anuiel, the soul of Anu, so that Anuiel could create borders and define himself. As Sithis isn't an active being in this story, Anuiel instead has the role of creating Lorkhan, who is created when Anu creates the soul of his soul, Auriel, and time begins. Lorkhan, again, tricks the gods into becoming trapped in the mortal world, full of limitations like himself, which was then known as 'the house of Sithis'.{{ref|name=tM}}

The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] believe that Sithis is the true start of creation, of Aurbis. It is said that before Sithis emerged there was only nothing. This nothing was the stasis of Anu, which is said to be foolish to name or revere, since "stasis asks only for itself, which is nothing", as also mentioned in the Sermons of Vivec. It was Sithis who disrupted the stillness and fashioned the myriad of possibilities that make up existence from it, creating new ideas that arose, ebbed and flowed, and faded away in an endless cycle. Anuiel, the demon, came later and created the Aedra in order to counter him by building 'everlasting, imperfect worlds'. To counter this, Sithis begat Lorkhan, who created the mortal world and allowed things to once again decay by trapping the other gods within.{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}} The Dark Brotherhood, having split off from the Dunmeri organization known as the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], seems to share this belief, as this story can be found in many of their hideaways.{{ref|name=Sb}} The [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] of [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] claim that Padomay and the padomaic Daedra are illusions who only exist and have influence due to the flawed design of Nirn, the result of the Void taking root within the cracks of the Aedra's work.

The creation myth of the [[Lore:Adzi-Kostleel|Adzi-Kostleel]] tribe equates nothingness with the serpent [[Lore:Padomay|Kota]], who fought the Great Root [[Lore:Anu|Atak]] at the beginning of time until the two became one and shed their Shadow. The myth ends with the Shadow of Atakota devouring creation and settling over it as a shroud of darkness to protect it from destruction.{{ref|name=CotR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}}

One pre-ri'Datta [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] account regarding the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Middle-Dawn]] depicts the ongoing interplay of Anu and Padomay as two warring serpents surrounded by twelve flaming spinning [[Lore:Aurbis|wheels]]. One a serpent of [[Lore:Anu|flaming feathers and crystal scales with a head like a hunting bird]], and another a [[Lore:Padomay|crimson eyed serpent of blackest scales and a white mane]] followed by all the Void. The flame-feathered serpent is said to emanate rejection of all "[[Lore:Man|Mannish]] impurity in all the known worlds". The dark serpent, though surrounded by chaos, is said to emanate gentleness and love for the spirits of the worlds. The interaction of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]] with these two warring serpents is said to have ended the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]], an event known as the Division of Heaven.{{Ref|name=TBoBVV|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume V}}}}

==Worship==
===Guilds===
[[File:SR-interior-Dark Brotherhood Sanctuary 10.jpg|Dark Brotherhood stained glass depiction of Sithis|right|thumb]]
Despite being recognized in most Tamrielic cultures, Sithis is rarely publicly worshiped. Instead of priesthoods, many of the organizations dedicated to Sithis act as assassin guilds first and foremost. 

The Dark Brotherhood is one such organization, and has ties all throughout Tamriel. Their worship of Sithis involves multiple intermediates. The lower ranking members of each branch answer to a Listener, who in turn communes with the supposed bride of Sithis, known as the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]].{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of the [[OB:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] questline in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} To get in contact with a member of the Dark Brotherhood, one only needs to construct an effigy of the intended victim and pray to the Night Mother, and a representative of the Dark Brotherhood will soon contact you.{{ref|name=AKSW}} Though brutally effective assassins, their method of worship is extremely macabre. Their effigies to Sithis often incorporate bones or organs of some kind, and even their telltale symbol is a bloody handprint.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}{{ref|name=Skyrim}} The [[Lore:Wrath of Sithis|Wrath of Sithis]] is a [[Lore:Spirit|spirit]] of vengeance which comes to claim the souls of traitors who have been exiled from the Dark Brotherhood for breaking one of ''[[Lore:The Five Tenets|The Five Tenets]]'' and drags them to the Void as punishment.{{ref|name=TFT|{{Cite Book|The Five Tenets}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Green-Venom-Tongue|Green-Venom-Tongue]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OBExile|[[Oblivion:A Dark Exile|A Dark Exile]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOWrath|[[Online:The Wrath of Sithis|The Wrath of Sithis]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dark Brotherhood (DLC)|Dark Brotherhood]]''}}{{ref|name=Rites|[[ON:Gold Coast Antiquities|Book of Dark Rite]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} There are Brotherhood tomes bound in black leather that detail a series of grisly rituals, including the "Rite of Penance" which is said to serve to summon forth something more dangerous than even the Brotherhood's assassins. Members of the  Brotherhood believe that, when Sithis wishes someone killed, their soul comes to the Void after death.{{ref|name=Ocheeva|[[Oblivion:Ocheeva|Ocheeva]]'s dialogue during [[Oblivion:Permanent Retirement|Permanent Retirement]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The Morag Tong is yet another assassin organization that's dedicated to Sithis, though unlike the Dark Brotherhood their organization is both legal and an accepted part of Dunmeri society. Instead of relying on gory rituals, they take writs of execution from the various Houses, committing the political assassinations out in the open. Within the Morag Tong, the act of murder is considered an eternal re-enactment of the murder of Nir originally committed by Padomay.{{ref|group=UOL|name=RFM}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} Because of this, the act of assassination is considered the purest celebration of joy or living.{{ref|group=UOL|name=RFM|[[General:Redguard Forum Madness|Redguard Forum Mandess]]}}  Unlike the Dark Brotherhood, they're no more obsessed with gore and death than the rest of Morrowind, and instead have a hall similar to any other guild you may come across.{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} They lack a Night Mother entirely and instead worship the Daedric Prince, [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], one of the three [[Lore:Tribunal Temple#The New Temple|Reclamations]] in Dunmeri society. Indeed, some have come to the conclusion that 'the Night Mother' herself is some version of Mephala because of this missing connection, and the fact the Dark Brotherhood initially splintered off from the Morag Tong without a Night Mother.{{ref|name=FaD|{{Cite Book|Fire and Darkness}}}}

===In Black Marsh===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Nest of Shadows.png|The Nest of Shadows, an Argonian representation symbolizing the interplay of Sithis, the serpent, and the primordial origin|thumb|left]][[File:ON-quest-She Who Eats the Light 02.jpg|right|thumb|Ancient Argonian sacrificial altar to Sithis]]{{Quote Box|''We worship the change which it wills to be! Once, we thought to shield ourselves from this blessing by becoming stone. We built monuments to Sithis, not out of reverence, but out of fear. "Do not change us!" we cried. "Do not let us die!"''|[[Online:Nisswo Uaxal|Nisswo Uaxal]]}}
[[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] are one of the only cultures in Tamriel to openly revere Sithis, which they have done since at least the Merethic Era. In Black Marsh, priests of Sithis are referred to as "[[Lore:Nisswo|Nisswo]]".{{ref|name=Xode|[[ON:Nisswo Xode|Nisswo Xode]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

Prior to Duskfall, the Nisswos were the ruling class of Black Marsh. These Nisswos are referred to in modern times as the "Nisswo-Kings".{{ref|name=Xukas|[[ON:Xukas|Xukas]]' dialogue during [[Online:Death and Dreaming|Death and Dreaming]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The old Nisswo believed that Sithis was a malevolent destructive force, and placed great emphasis on appeasing it to avoid destruction. They developed a number of rituals to appease Sithis, including live blood sacrifices.{{ref|name=Xode}} This order of Nisswos declined during Duskfall, clinging to the old ways and refusing to change.{{ref|name=Xode}}

After dusk fell, the Nisswo-Kings were eventually succeeded by a loose coalition of Sithis preachers known collectively as the Clutch of Nisswo. The Clutch now revered Sithis as a force of change rather than a purely destructive force, just as the Hist revealed before Duskfall.{{ref|name=Xode}} Modern Argonians are more conscientious of this cycle of change that Sithis embodies, and seek to overcome what is known as "shunatei"&mdash;feelings of regret and loss caused by that which has come to pass.{{ref|name=Talen|[[Online:Talen-Jush|Talen-Jush]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} An example of a change brought about by this shift in perception of Sithis is how Argonians tend to build homes with mud or reeds, which are easily replaceable. The Argonians recognize that their structures will be destroyed in time, and so instead of dwelling on the loss of a hut destroyed by a storm, they rebuild it.{{ref|name=Xukas}}{{ref|name=Talen}}

Argonian society also honors [[Lore:Shadowscale|Shadowscale]], an order of assassins dedicated to serving Black Marsh. Traditionally, Argonians born under the sign of [[Lore:The Shadow|The Shadow]] would be given to the Dark Brotherhood to be trained as one.{{ref|name=OBTeinaava}} Shadowscales are honored as ku-vastei, those who bring about the needed change. They are respected as instruments of Sithis's will. The Nisswos view the Shadowscales' training by the Dark Brotherhood as a necessary evil, but urge the Shadowscales to turn away from the Brotherhood's view of Sithis when their training is complete.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Scales of Shadow}}}} Indeed, many do leave the Dark Brotherhood in order to serve as arbiters and peacekeepers within Black Marsh.{{ref|[[Online:Sulahkeesh|Sulahkeesh]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Shadowscales declined from prominence along with the Dark Brotherhood itself. Following the closure of the training ground in Archon and the death of the {{Lore Link|Veezara|last known living Shadowscale}} in {{Year|4E 201}}, the fate of this organization has been left unknown.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:Dark Brotherhood#Quests|Events]] of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The ancient [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]] and Argonians were theorized to have worked together on a religious depiction symbolizing Sithis and his interplay with the primordial origin in the form of an enchanted statue. The statue depicted a dark serpent poised to strike at a polished azure gemstone, the primordial origin which was metaphorically represented as an egg.{{ref|name=Nest|[[ON:Nest of Shadows|Nest of Shadows]]' description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

According to the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]], the Void is the place where "all roots lead" and they claim to "rejoin" it upon death. It is also said that if the memories of the Hist that rejoins the Void are still held by someone else, they might be used to grow a new Hist one day. The Hist claim they can invoke the "Songs of Sithis", to "bring the Void" when driven to take vengeance, making those who transgressed against them "taste fear, then nothing".{{ref|name=VoiceHist|[[ON:Voice of the Hist|Voice of the Hist]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=HistBrother|[[ON:Hist-Brother|Hist-Brother]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===In Cyrodiil===
[[File:OBR-interior-Deepscorn Hollow 08.jpg|Shrine of Sithis in {{Lore Link|Deepscorn Hollow}}|thumb|right]]
The ancient ruins within [[Lore:Deepscorn Hollow|Deepscorn Hollow]] are believed to have been a [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] temple devoted to Sithis. Its subterranean halls eventually fell into ruin and sank into the boggy southern coast of [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]], although various vile cults were known to have taken shelter within Deepscorn over the years.{{ref|name=ESOGreywyn|[[ON:Greywyn Blenwyth|Greywyn Blenwyth]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Deepscorn Hollow|Deepscorn Hollow]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} One such cult was the [[Lore:Unmakers|Unmakers]], a [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] group led by the self-styled "Daughter of Sithis", an [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] woman named Lalais.{{ref|[[ON:Thadarond|Thadarond]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Originally a noblewoman from [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], Lalais gave herself eagerly to the darkness she found within Deepscorn Hollow, and used its power to bewitch others into joining her.{{ref|name=ESOGreywyn}} The cult carried out ritual sacrifices within the ruins until Lalais was confronted and killed circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|[[ON:Competition and Contracts|Competition and Contracts]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Greywyn Blenwyth|Greywyn Blenwyth]] later converted Deepscorn Hollow into a [[Lore:Vampirism|vampiric]] lair. Following the [[Lore:Purification|Purification]] of the [[Lore:Crimson Scars|Crimson Scars]], Greywyn retreated to Deepscorn and constructed an altar to Sithis to seek his forgiveness for Greywyn's betrayal of the [[Lore:Black Hand|Black Hand]].{{ref|name=GJ|{{Cite Book|Greywyn's Journal}}}} Sithis eventually spoke to him and demanded that Greywyn cure his vampirism, but he was ultimately tracked down by Dark Brotherhood assassins and killed while praying at the shrine before he could cure himself.{{ref|name=OBdownload|[[Oblivion:Vile Lair#Background|Vile Lair's original ''obliviondownloads.com'' description]]}}

===On Solstice===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Solstice Giant Crocodile Skull.jpg|The giant skull of a Solstice temple guardian|thumb|right]]
Argonian tribes have been present on [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]] since before the Duskfall period,{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/2466390916 Twitch Stream of ''Solstice Walkthrough w/ Devs''], time stamp 49:19-50:50}} and worship of Sithis existed within their societies. Solstice Argonians viewed [[Lore:Crocodile|crocodiles]] as guardian beasts of the Unmaker, Sithis. Colossal crocodile skeletons, said to have belonged to beasts once used as both temple guardians and mounts for Argonian Assassins during their ancient conflicts with the Daedra of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] and the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya]] High Elves, have been found around ceremony sites of the [[Lore:Death-dancer|Death-dancer]] sect, a Void-dwelling tradition dating back centuries, and tales exist of groups of [[Lore:Stone-Nest Tribe|Stone-Nest]] Assassins riding such beasts to battle. These giant crocodiles are described as having served as guardians for the entire duration of their long lives, and to have kept growing in size for as long as they lived.{{ref|name=SolsticeSkull|[[Online:Solstice Giant Crocodile Skull|Solstice Giant Crocodile Skull]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SolsticeTail|[[Online:Solstice Giant Crocodile Tail|Solstice Giant Crocodile Tail]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SolsticeRibs|[[Online:Solstice Giant Crocodile Ribs|Solstice Giant Crocodile Ribs]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The [[Lore:Ghost|spirit]] of a Nisswo of the ancient [[Lore:Zaman-Tia|Zaman-Tia]] tribe described Sithis as the embodiment of chaos and change. He claimed to be able to hear the call of the Void beckoning him through sounds of chaos and destruction, and believed that, once freed from the living world, his spirit would be pulled into the Void to face the judgment of Sithis for his deeds in life.{{ref|name=Akech|[[Online:Akech-Chun|Akech-Chun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Invocation and Manifestations==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Ha! To try to put a name on me is to try and hold chaos in your hand, mortal!<br>If it helps you stay sane, think of me as a miniscule piece of Sithis ... a jot of intellect and will, contained within this shadowed form.''|Aspect of Sithis}}
In 2E 582, [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] encountered an aspect of Sithis which took the form of a spectral red serpent. This being recognized the "void" of the absence of a soul within the Vestige, deeming it intriguing. It described trying to name or understand it as futile, like trying to hold chaos in one's hand.{{ref|name=Aspect|[[ON:Aspect of Sithis|Aspect of Sithis]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

In 2E 582, [[Lore:Shuxaltsei|Xulneihavu Shuxaltsei]], a vampire priestess of Sithis during the [[Lore:Duskfall|Duskfall]], attempted to carry out a ritual meant to make the great serpent, Sithis, stir and return all creation to the Void. Shuxaltsei's ritual was interrupted by the  Vestige and the priestess herself was slain.{{ref|name=ESOXode|[[ON:Nisswo Xode|Nisswo Xode]]'s dialogue during [[ON:She Who Eats the Light|She Who Eats the Light]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Artifacts==
===Blade of Woe===
[[File:SR-item-Blade of Woe.jpg|left|150px|thumb|Blade of Woe]]
The [[Lore:Blade of Woe|Blade of Woe]] also known as the '''Night Mother's Kiss''' is a ritualistic dagger associated with Sithis and the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]]. Anyone who is slain with the dagger supposedly has their soul sent straight to the Void. Its name is directly associated with the dagger the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]] used to slay her own children.{{Ref|name=TGiTN|{{Cite Book|The Glint in the Night}}}} It takes the form of an [[Lore:Ebony|Ebony]] dagger and can, at its most lethal, instantly kill its target and can shroud the wielder from view. It is also known to damage or drain health, deplete magicka and willpower, and demoralize anyone struck by it. The blood spilled by the blade may become tainted, and it has also been known to completely incinerate targets marked for ritual execution.{{ref|name=Online|[[Online:Dark Brotherhood|Events]] of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Fangs of Sithis===
The [[Lore:Fangs of Sithis|Fangs of Sithis]] are a set of [[Lore:Duskfall|pre-Duskfall]] weapons, taking the form of either two large eggs,{{ref|name=FOS|[[ON:The Fangs of Sithis|The Fangs of Sithis]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or a set of dual daggers.{{ref|name=Online2|Quest reward from [[ON:Pull the Last Fang|Pull The Last Fang]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Fangs were gifted to the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]] by Sithis, the Dark Husband, who in turn gifted them to the [[Lore:Shadowscales|Shadowscales]].{{ref|name=ONShavara|[[Online:Sharava|Sharava]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Dark Husband}}}} It is said that a single Shadowscale wielding the Fangs could lay waste to entire cities not just via mass killings, but also by causing crop-failures and famine. However, most are unable to safely wield the Fangs, as they feed upon the wielder's mind and soul, causing the weak-minded either to go mad, or become mindless agents of Sithis who are devoid of free will.{{ref|name=ONLooks|[[Online:Looks-Under-Rocks|Looks-Under-Rocks]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
* The word for "relating to Sithis" is "Sithian".{{ref|[[Oblivion:Magical Stones#Sithian Stone|Sithian Stone]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
*The "Night Child" was a spirit that was at one point intended to appear in the Night Mother's crypt in ''[[OB:Oblivion|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion]]''. The spirit was possibly intended to be one of the children of Sithis and the Night Mother mentioned in ''[[Lore:The Night Mother's Truth|The Night Mother's Truth]]'',{{ref|name=NMT|{{Cite Book|The Night Mother's Truth}}}} however he was ultimately not included in the game.{{ref|name=Night|[[Oblivion:Night Child|Night Child]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}
