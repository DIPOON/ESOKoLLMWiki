[[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] (also known as '''Daedric Regents''',{{ref|name=DFDPQ|[[Daggerfall:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah's Quest]] and [[Daggerfall:Nocturnal's Quest|Nocturnal's Quest]] dialogue in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} '''[[Lore:Daedra Lords|Daedra Lords]]<noinclude>{{intnote|qc}}</noinclude>''',{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}}{{ref|name=TTNOO|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Aymar Douar|Aymar Douar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} '''Demon Lords of Misrule''',{{Ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} '''Lords and Ladies of Oblivion''',{{Ref|name=NRM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} '''Dark Princes''', '''gods of torment''',{{ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}} or '''The Greater Princes'''{{Ref|name=LFNHATRAYQ2}}) are the most powerful of the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], and thus most commonly worshipped as [[Lore:Gods|gods]].{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} Each has a particular sphere, which it is said to govern. <noinclude>In fact, [[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]] stated that "a broad and well-defined sphere of influence that enables clarity of purpose and focused expression of will" is what made a Prince a Prince and claimed that because of this, though Demiprinces such as himself far outstrip any mortal in power and consequence, they are still "but trivial compared to the Greater Princes".{{Ref|name=LFNHATRAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}} In various cultures, the Princes are viewed as siblings,{{ref|name=VOF}} with they themselves having been known to refer to each other as such, regardless of their actual origins.{{Ref|name=16Accords12|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness, v. XII}}}}{{ref|[[Redguard:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|name=Sheo|[[Online:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in ''[[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Daedric Princes may assume a typically masculine or a typically feminine form, sometimes both.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} They are usually referred to as Princes regardless of what gender they appear most frequently as. 

The various Daedric Princes and their spheres are listed below.</noinclude> In all, there are seventeen powerful Princes known to mortals: [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]], [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], [[Lore:Namira|Namira]], [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]], [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]], [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]], [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], and [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]].{{ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Book of Daedra}}}} Another Prince, [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]] has been forgotten by all but Hermaeus Mora and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]].{{ref|name=ItheliaIMO|[[Online:In Memory Of|In Memory Of]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road (Chapter)|Gold Road]]''}}<noinclude>{{TOCleft}} 

The title [[Lore:Ur-dra|Ur-dra]] is used to denote the "eldest and most powerful" of the Daedric Princes.{{ref|name=Lilatha|[[ON:Lilatha|Lilatha]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}. [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] and the [[Lore:Imperial Geographical Society|Imperial Geocraphical Society]] attribute the title to the Daedric Prince Nocturnal,{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}}{{Ref|group=UOL|name=Census|{{Cite Book|Imperial Census of Daedra Lords|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=Lilatha}} and the Society claims nearly all the royalty of Oblivion recognize the claim. However, the ancient [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] used the title to describe Namiira instead.{{ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}{{ref|name=TFDOF|{{Cite Book|The Favored Daughter of Fadomai}}}} According to Azura, the Princes who attended the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]]: Boethiah, Hermaeus Mora, Hircine, Malacath, Mehrunes Dagon, Molag Bal, Sheogorath, and Azura herself, are among the most powerful of the Princes, who "stand above their lesser kith".{{Ref|name=ONAzura|[[Online:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in [[Online:The Missing Prophecy|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]] claimed that "cheap mortal dualisms" of "Friend" and "Foe" cannot capture the complexity of the relationship between Daedric Princes and that the Princes ultimately keep their own counsel on matters of this scale, though they do sometimes pursue parallel interests that make them act in concert.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}} According to [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], it is not possible to truly defeat a Daedric Prince through force of arms.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}}</noinclude>

Each Daedric Prince, with the exception of Jyggalag, is said to have their own [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]],{{ref|name=OO|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} formed out of [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|Chaotic Creatia]],{{ref|name=Fox|[[Online:The Fox|The Fox]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LSP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Lawrence Schick's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=LMA FNH|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} over which they have control, with the realms themselves being extensions of their masters.{{Ref|name=EGRN|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ns_base=General}}}}{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} It is said that Oblivion realms can be infinite in size and shape, and that all proper Oblivion realms, such as the main realms of each Prince, are infinite in scale.{{ref|name=LeramilWise|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road (Chapter)|Gold Road]]''}}{{Ref|name=TESONRWRL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Talking Elder Scrolls Online's Necrom Reveal With Rich Lambert|ns_base=General}}}} The realm of each Prince is said to be fundamentally shaped and curated by their will and change to accommodate their slightest whim, to the extent that it has been said by some that the Prince is the realm and the realm is the Prince.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}{{Ref|name=MalkhestArchive|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}}{{Ref|name=EGRN|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ns_base=General}}}} Though for most Daedric beings creating a realm is an enormous undertaking as it requires enormous will and power, the Daedric Princes are said to possess such might that in ages past they formed vast and practically limitless domains around the initial seed of their realm. As such some of them are known to also have sway over Adjunct Realms or border realms, such as the [[Lore:Infinite Archive|Infinite Archive]] of Apocrypha, which lie within their plane's "greater fabric".{{Ref|name=MalkhestArchive|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}}Some of these border realms have been described as having extensive or even infinite scale, and not lying within the same time and space as their Prince's main plane.{{ref|name=Panopticon|{{Cite Book|On the Infinite Panopticon}}}}{{ref|name=Working|{{Cite Book|Working in the Infinite Panopticon}}}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ESO:Leramil the Wise|Leramil the Wise]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}. Indeed, according to Sotha Sil, were a Prince to simultaneously invade the realms of the other Princes to seize the power within through the use of something like the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], they'd gain infinite power through the infinite connections made.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The limits on their planes, powers, and influence are not readily understood, as there are over 37,000 other planes (such as the [[Lore:Soul Cairn|Soul Cairn]] and the [[Lore:Chimera of Desolation|Chimera of Desolation]], as well as chaos realms and pocket realms) over which they are not known to exert control,{{ref|name=OO}}{{ref|name=TDOB|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=DDCFA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Dossier: Cold-Flame Atronach}}}} and indeed there exists far more than that, with accounts from the Daedra describing the minor realms in Oblivion as overall being infinite in both number and complexity.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}}. The planes of Oblivion are said to exhibit infinite variation, much like the Daedra that inhabit them, thus it is said they share little in common, being "the very definition of change and variation, manifesting all possibilities, and validating all understanding and misunderstanding".{{ref|name=Jackdaw|[[ON:Jackdaw Daedrat|Jackdaw Daedrat]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LA:LtF|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}} Indeed, it is said that what the mortal senses perceive while in an Oblivion realm is crafted from illusion and metaphor, the result of their mind attempting to make sense of the surrounding chaos. Because of this phenomenon things like the "grass" one might perceive while in an Oblivion realm are actually just the memory of that thing as interpreted by the mortal mind.{{ref|name=Telbaril|[[ON:Telbaril Oran|Telbaril Oran]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The smaller planes are said to often "twist into hyperogonal paradoxes" in an attempt to resist any sort of order being imposed on them, or wink in and out of existence, making it difficult for Daedra to gain a foothold there.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}} The term "pocket realm" is said to refer to any minor plane of Oblivion, whether it is autonomous from the rule of a Daedric Prince, semi-autonomously under the rule of a Prince-appointed "functionary", or fully incorporated by a Prince.{{Ref|name=LFNHATRAYQ2}} The realms comprising Oblivion are described as being part of a different universe or a different dimension in relation to Mundus.{{ref|name=Atronite|[[Blades:Atronite|Atronite]]'s description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

Daedric Princes are bound by the [[Lore:Pact Primordial|Pact Primordial]], an ancient treaty which prevents interdimensional incursions from one Prince into the realm of another. Certain rites such as those that exist within the Tormenting Eye [[Lore:Black Books|Black Book]] can open special [[Lore:Magical Transportation|portals]] that allow for the Pact to be circumvented so a Prince can enter another's realm uninvited, when combined with methods such as possessing a dead mortal body.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Secret of the Tormenting Eye|ns_base=ON}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=OTPP|{{Cite Book|On the Pact Primordial}}}}{{ref|name=Conclave|Events of [[Online:Conclave of Fate|Conclave of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The ten Princes that are signatories of the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] with Sotha Sil have also had restrictions placed upon their ability to directly interact with Nirn, being able to manifest directly only when called by certain intermediaries such as [[Lore:Witch|witches]] or sorcerers. As the Compact binds both ways, with the other party being all mortals, this has also placed restrictions on mortal travel to the realms of those Princes.{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}}{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}}{{ref|name=CWCSothaDialogue|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{Ref|name=SOiA|{{Cite Book|Secrets Overheard in Apocrypha}}}}

The Daedric Princes seem to view Men and Mer as little more than minor amusements, occasionally applauding the actions of mortals when they exceed their expectations. They do not know the mortal sense of "good" and "evil", and usually have extremist tendencies, which is why Men and Mer fear them greatly. However, several princes do seem to take genuine pleasure in tremendous acts of devastation, in particular Boethiah, Molag Bal, Vaermina, Mehrunes Dagon, and Peryite.

Although the beings are considered evil by most, they are widely worshipped in the realms of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Elaborate shrines are created to honor the Daedra as gods. They often take a keen interest in their worshippers, and it is speculated that this is either because of the obvious ego-gratification of being somebody's god, or because the Daedra like to keep an eye on potential future subjects (assuming people of demonic disposition enter Oblivion after death, that is; there are as many afterlife theories as there are religions in the world). Mainstream religious authorities discourage Daedra worship, and often mount witch-hunting expeditions to drive out Daedra worshippers from the local area. During these encounters, they are often surprised at the marginal sanity that comes of worshipping the Daedric Princes. For the most part, however, dealing with the Daedra, one gets the distinct impression of being mused over as a person peering under an upturned rock may momentarily wonder at the lives of the bugs living ignorantly there. <includeonly>For more information, see [[Lore:Daedric Princes|main lore article]].</includeonly><noinclude>

The Daedric Princes can be summoned, and there is a popular notion that the summoned lords will then give the summoner a quest or task to fulfill and that the quest, once completed, will make the questor richly rewarded. Mostly these tasks are merely for the entertainment of the Daedra. However, this is probably not true of most Daedra summons, and the summoner would do well to consider the consequences of his actions in advance. A consecrated shrine is the most reliable conduit for contacting a Daedric Prince, though there are other ways to make contact with the powers of Oblivion.{{ref|[[Online:Earl Leythen#Cathedral of Webs|Earl Leythen]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Necessary Alliance|A Necessary Alliance]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

While Aedra and Daedra are precisely defined, there are still some beings who challenge this dichotomy in some respects. Malacath, who legend holds was once the Aedra [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], is particularly hostile to other Daedra; he even created [[Lore:Scourge|Scourge]], Bane of Daedra.{{ref|name=TBOD}}{{ref|name=TTNOO}} According to [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} However, this is uncorroborated, and little is known of the mysterious Trickster deity; while some stories suggest Lorkhan was impervious to death, similar to a Daedra, he is also credited with helping to bring about creation (in fact, per many sources, it was allegedly his idea).{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{ref|name=SONGOM|{{Cite Book|Spirit of Nirn, God of Mortals}}}} Obscure stories also question the origins of Meridia{{ref|name=Merid|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}} and Mehrunes Dagon.{{ref|name=7fights|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga}}}}

Just like with other beings, each reality along the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]] contains its own version of each entity, be they mortal or Daedra, even deities such as the Daedric Princes have their own alternate versions within the realities. These alternate versions are said to be "reflections" of one another: different in some ways, like images in a cracked mirror, but originating from the same seed. However, there is at least one reality where concepts such as [[Lore:Magic|magic]] and Daedra do not exist at all.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

For information on other Daedric creatures, and the characteristics of Daedric beings more broadly, see the entry on [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].
