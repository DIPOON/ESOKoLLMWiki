The [[Lore:Abanabi Caves|Abanabi Caves]]{{ref|name=MWLords|[[Morrowind:Lord's Mail (quest)|Lord's Mail]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} runs beneath [[Lore:Zafirbel Bay|Zafirbel Bay]] in the [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] region of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. A surface entrance to one section of the cave can be found southeast of the [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] city of [[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] and northeast of [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]].{{ref|name=Morrowind|Appearance in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Another section of the cave could be accessed directly from Tel Fyr.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Along with {{Lore Link|Abaelun Mine}} and {{Lore Link|Dun-Ahhe}} it is one of the few places in Vvardenfell where [[Lore:Diamond|diamonds]] occur.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}

==History==
In the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the partly-flooded cave was home to a forest of extraordinarily rare, bizarre, bioluminescent, and possibly sentient{{ref|[[ON:Loading Screens|ESO loading screen]] for [[ON:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]]}} [[Lore:Land-Coral|land-coral]] which thrived in the cavern, likely due to a nearby aquifer. The caverns teemed with life and had a palpable divine warmth, which was compared to the energy one felt in close proximity to the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Telvanni Journal|ns_base=ON}}}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the powerful [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] mage [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] was made an honorary member of [[Lore:Great House Telvanni|Great House Telvanni]] and was gifted land in Zafirbel Bay to create his tower of Tel Fyr.{{ref|[[ON:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Divayth planned to extend his tower into the cavern{{ref|{{Cite Book|Tel Fyr, Additional Specifications|ns_base=ON}}}} until a portal to the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] inexplicably opened up inside the cave, spilling out Refabricants and [[Lore:Fabricants|Fabricants]] from the {{Lore Link|Halls of Fabrication}}. These clockwork monstrosities decimated the local wildlife such as the [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]] colony and [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh]] which inhabited the cave.{{ref|name=ESO}} The Fabricant threat was neutralized when a large group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] accompanied Divayth into the cave and entered Clockwork City to disable their production, allowing Divayth to study the portal.{{ref|[[ON:Forging the Future|Forging the Future]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

By the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], most of the cavern had been converted into the Corprusarium,{{ref|name=twitch|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/150663592 Bethesda Twitch Stream 16:14-17:06]}}
which would house victims of [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]], the "divine disease". One such resident was [[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}} An unconnected section of Abanabi remained accessible from the surface. It is unknown if this section of the cave which shares its name was once connected to area Divayth Fyr would convert to the Corprusarium. It would become occupied by a pair of hostile sorceresses. One of these sorceresses, Draramu Hloran, came into possession of [[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]], although it was recovered from her by the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] in {{Year|3E 427}} at the request of the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]].{{ref|name=MWLords}}
