[[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]], also known as the '''Hammer of Might''', is an ancient [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] created by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwarven]] [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]] clan. It is known to manifest as a warhammer of [[Lore:Dwarven Metal|Dwarven Metal]].{{ref|name=AR/DF|''[[Arena:Volendrung|Arena]]/[[Daggerfall:Volendrung|Daggerfall]]'' in-game descriptions}} For unknown reasons, Volendrung became a Daedric artifact of [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]].{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} Therefore, it is culturally linked to both the Dwemer and the Daedra.{{Ref|name=WIV|{{Cite Book|What is Volendrung?}}}} It earns its name with its capability to demolish even the walls of enemy keeps with ease.{{ref|name=ESOVolendrung|[[Online:Volendrung|Volendrung]]'s abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} It is enchanted with the ability to paralyze foes comparable to that of a [[Lore:Medusa|Medusa]]'s gaze.{{Ref|name=ARTIL|{{TIL|Artifact Dialogue — The Imperial Library|volendrung}}}} It is also known for draining those it strikes of their strength.{{ref|name=AR/DF}} The hammer is prone to disappearing like its Dwarven creators, sometimes resurfacing in days, sometimes in eons.{{ref|name=AR/DF}}

==History==
[[File:ON-interior-Volenfell 03.jpg|The Hall of Volendrung in Volenfell, the site where the hammer landed|right|thumb]]
===Origins===
The hammer originally belonged to the [[Lore:Rourken (person)|chieftain]] of the Rourken clan.{{ref|name=PGE1Hammerfell}} It was wielded in battle not only as a weapon but also as a symbol of their family's honor.{{Ref|name=WIV}} When his clan refused to join the other [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] in the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]], the chieftain threw his hammer across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], promising to settle where ever it landed. The hammer landed in western Tamriel, and the Rourken called the land [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]], literally "City of the Hammer". This area later came to be called [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. The Rourken's journey across Tamriel is depicted in many of the ruins of the region, Volendrung appearing as a shining star showing the way.{{ref|name=PGE1Hammerfell|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}{{Ref|name=WIV}}

Sometime before the Dwemer disappeared in {{Year|1E 700}}, the Rourken were driven out of Hammerfell by a race of [[Lore:Goblin|giant Goblins]] who worshipped and were led by [[Lore:Malooc|Malooc]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part I}}}}{{ref|name=RGH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}}{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=Early|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview/REDGUARD.TXT|REDGUARD.TXT]] - [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview|Daggerfall Preview]]}} Sometime after their disappearance, Volendrung ironically came to embody the power of Malacath, one of the Dwemer's most bitter foes.{{ref|name=OBMartin}} Some sources suggest that Malacath's Volendrung existed as a separate hammer, with records of its existence dating back nearly as far as the Dwemer's. Speculations arose as to why the God of Curses would align himself with an object crafted by their adversaries. Some believed that it was to ridicule Dwemer craftsmanship. Others claimed that Malacath aimed to wield something cherished by the Dwemer as a weapon against them. It was believed that such actions appeared unusually subtle and indirect, departing from Malacath's typically straightforward approach.{{Ref|name=WIV}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-render-Volendrung 02.jpg|Volendrung destroying a keep's wall in war-torn Cyrodiil (ESO)|right|thumb]]
Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, pieces of Volendrung's handle were found in circulation in various shady markets and sold for a hefty sum.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband P#Piece of Volendrung's Handle|Piece of Volendrung's Handle]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Regardless, Volendrung later saw usage in the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] when the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] stole it from Malacath and then sent it onto Cyrodiil, hoping the resulting chaos would bring him entertainment. Sheogorath kept it as part of his collection within Cheesemonger's Hollow, a secluded daedric ruin located within the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]. He would occasionally invite the insane to the hollow to test out its power. For fun he cursed the artifact, with its negative effects becoming active while the artifact was in Cyrodiil. If it didn't consume souls in a certain timespan, it would instead consume the soul of the user.{{ref|name=CCTCWAA|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/56335 Chaos Comes to Cyrodiil with Artifact Weapons ] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}{{ref|name=ESOChaosMagic|[[Online:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in the quest [[Online:Chaos Magic|Chaos Magic]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The hammer would also eventually return to the Shivering Isles, only to reappear in Cyrodiil once again on Sheogorath's whim. Throughout the Three Banners War, the hammer was used by all three alliances against each other, as it changed hands often. Among one of the many wielders was the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], but reports conflict on which alliance they wielded the hammer for.{{ref|name=ESOVolendrung}} The alliances commissioned elaborate replicas of Volendrung for those who had shed blood with it, and those who had managed to slay its wielder.{{ref|{{Item Link|quality=legendary|Volendrung Replica|id=153751}} description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] (circa {{Year|3E 389}}-{{Year|3E 399}}), rumors began circulating around Tamriel that at long last, Volendrung had reappeared somewhere in Tamriel, having by this point become a popular object of myth. One of these rumors eventually came to the attention of the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]], who tracked the source of the rumor to an odd man in a tavern who claimed to have discovered the location of a map leading to Volendrung's current whereabouts. At purportedly great cost, the man had learned this map was located in a particularly dangerous dungeon in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. Advising the Eternal Champion that the powerful artifact would attract the attention of equally powerful beings, the man sold them the location of the map. After traveling to the dungeon in Hammerfell and retrieving the map leading to Volendrung, the Eternal Champion followed the map to another dungeon located outside of Hammerfell where they successfully recovered Volendrung.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}} 

In the events leading up the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] summoned Malacath seeking power, and a worshipper of the Prince gave the agent Volendrung in return for slaying a [[Lore:Mazken|Daedra Seducer]] who had betrayed him.{{ref|name=DFMalacath|[[Daggerfall:Malacath's Quest|Malacath's Quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

Volendrung later came into the possession of [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] of [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]], [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. He kept it in a locked chest in his [[Lore:Corprusarium|Corprusarium]], near [[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s living area. A hobby of his was to invite thieves to steal from his chests to amuse him, as they would often fail. 

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] summoned Malacath and was tasked with freeing the [[Lore:Ogre|ogre]] slaves of Lord Drad from his mines. In return, Malacath gifted the Hero with Volendrung.{{ref|name=OBMalacath|[[Oblivion:Malacath|Malacath]] quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
Sometime after the end of the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], circa {{Year|4E 180}}, the [[Lore:Hero|Warrior]] came across Volendrung in their journey. The weapon is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.{{ref|[[Blades:Artifacts|Artifact]] acquisition methods in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] settlement of Largashbur began to be openly attacked by the [[Lore:Giant|giants]] in the area. The giants had desecrated a shrine to Malacath and taken possession of Shagrol's Warhammer, a mundane hammer. Malacath cursed the tribe for its weakness, and the Orcs asked how they could obtain forgiveness. Chief Yamarz was tasked with clearing the giants from Malacath's shrine by the Prince. He was accompanied by the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]], but he was a weak leader and was slain when he turned on the Dragonborn. Malacath praised the Dragonborn for killing Yamarz. He then ordered the Dragonborn to return Shagrol's Hammer to Largashbur. Malacath transformed the hammer into Volendrung and gave it to the Dragonborn, while naming Gularzob as the new chief of the tribe.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:The Cursed Tribe|The Cursed Tribe]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}<noinclude>
