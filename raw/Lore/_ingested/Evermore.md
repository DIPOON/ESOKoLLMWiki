[[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], sometimes spelled '''Evermor'''{{Ref|[[:File:LO-map-High Rock (Oblivion Codex).jpg|Map of High Rock — Pocket Guide to the Empire (3rd Edition)]]}}, is a city on the banks of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]] in eastern [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], located near the border with [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. 

Its impressive palace was assembled from slabs of [[Lore:Granite|granite]] quarried from nearby [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]].{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}} Local traditions include the worship of [[Lore:Saint Pelin|Saint Pelin]], a hero of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] who once served as a clergyman at [[Lore:Bangkorai Garrison|Bangkorai Garrison]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Martyrdom of Saint Pelin}}}}

Historically, the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]]s of Evermore ruled over the region of [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]]. These lands were subject to frequent attacks from the [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] of the mountains, who asserted their birthright to the city and aimed to reclaim their ancestral homeland. Southern Bangkorai included the [[Lore:Fallen Wastes|Fallen Wastes]] region of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], leading Evermore's rulership to often be contested by the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s of [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]].{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Evermore's position along trade routes spanning the Tamriel allowed it to become an important trading hub. Locally, it linked eastern High Rock, the southern and central regions of Hammerfell, [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], and western [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], while more distant trade routes to the southeast connected it with [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}}<noinclude>

==History==
According to some Reachmen, the lands of northern Bangkorai, encompassing the place where the city of Evermore was built and the areas extending northward, were once a part of the Reachfolk [[Lore:Western Reach|homeland]].{{Ref|name=MZ|[[ON:Medya Zurric|Medya Zurric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Brinarch|[[ON:Brinarch|Brinarch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The city itself was founded in {{Year|1E 983}} by the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River Tribes|Bjoulsae River Tribes]] after the fall of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] opened the Bjoulsae for trade and transport. According to the {{Lore Link|Decretal of Fiefdoms}} by Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]], the city's earliest leader was Barron Guy Marde of ye Rivver Banke. Their descendants would go on to form {{Lore Link|House Guimard}}, the ruling house of Evermore during the mid-Second Era.{{ref|name=TRHoKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}}

In {{Year|2E 542}} Evermore was sacked by a horde of Reachmen led by [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] the Black Drake after only a three day siege. It was retaken by Duke {{Lore Link|Blaise Guimard}}, who was later elected to the throne of Evermore.{{ref|name=TRHoKE}} Evermore along with Daggerfall, [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]], [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], and [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] signed the first [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] following the defeat of the Reachmen.{{Ref|name=TaToaM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} 

In {{Year|2E 582}} Evermore and its lands were once again attacked by Reachmen, this time led by {{Lore Link|Uela}}. The [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire]] attempted to infiltrate the city in preparation for the arrival of the [[Lore:Seventh Legion|Seventh Legion]]; however, Covenant agents were able to prevent the fall of the city with the help of Uela, who instead sought to take Evermore for herself, though she was ultimately killed before ever reaching the city.{{ref|name=ESO}}

By the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] the lands of Evermore had become fragmented and the Fallen Wastes region was possessed by Hammerfell. During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] the city-state of Evermore was ruled by King Tristynak. It had a rivalry with [[Lore:North Point|North Point]] and the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Evermore|Evermore]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Evermore was neighbored by several settlements, including {{Lore Link|Black Wastes}} to the west and {{Lore Link|Dunkarn Haven}} to the east.{{Ref|name=MapArena|[[:File:AR-map-High Rock towns.png|Map of High Rock – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} Before the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] it was one of eight kingdoms in High Rock{{ref|{{Cite Book|Etiquette With Rulers}}}}, though the aftermath of the Warp would see Evermore as one of only five remaining Kingdoms in High Rock.{{ref|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}}
