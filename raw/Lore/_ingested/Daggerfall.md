==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-place-Daggerfall Castle.jpg|thumb|left|Castle Daggerfall]]
[[File:ON-place-Daggerfall Docks.jpg|thumb|right|Daggerfall's harbor district]]
Daggerfall is a large walled city on the coast of High Rock, specifically on the north side of a bay at the southernmost point of Glenumbra, around where the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] meets the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]].{{Ref|name=ONMap|[[:File:ON-map-Glenumbra.jpg|Map of Glenumbra – The Elder Scrolls Online]]}}{{Ref|name=DFCity|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall (city)|Daggerfall]] (city) in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} As the [[Lore:Book of Life|Book of Life]] puts it, Daggerfall is located north of the highest bluffs, south of the moors, west of the hills, and east of the sea.{{Ref|name=AHOD}} 

More specifically, Daggerfall is built at the top of a tall, steep hill that towers over the coast but gradually inclines down into the forests to the northeast. The city is cradled in a river valley of a mountain that anchors the western peninsula of High Rock and is generally well-defended.{{Ref|name=ONDaggerfall|[[Online:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Several islands exist just outside of Daggerfall, namely the [[Lore:Glenumbra Banks|Glenumbra Banks]]{{Ref|name=BTGB|{{Cite Book|Beware the Glenumbra Banks}}}} and [[Lore:Sword's Rest Isle|Sword's Rest Isle]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadSword|[[Online:Sword's Rest Isle|Sword's Rest Isle]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The mountain's caves extend endlessly under the earth and have been used by people for years. The caves underneath Daggerfall are described as measureless, and legend has it that the [[Lore:Horn of Summoning|Horn of Summoning]] was buried somewhere beneath it.{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} On a more tangible and visible surface, the Themond family of Bretons built a large mine behind the Daggerfall Cathedral, and after its abandonment, deceased members were buried in front of it.{{Ref|name=ONTsiniuc|[[Online:Tsiniuc|Tsiniuc]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A refuge for the city's outlaws which inconspicously lies underneath Castle Town has two entrances, one on the eastern wall near the city gates and another hidden in the Harbor District. It was created from a part of the city's sewer system{{Ref|name=ONOutlaws|[[Online:Daggerfall Outlaws Refuge|Daggerfall Outlaws Refuge]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and is even used by people from outside like the Red Rook bandits of [[Lore:King's Guard|King's Guard]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadOutlaws|[[Online:Daggerfall Outlaws Refuge|Daggerfall Outlaws Refuge]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Daggerfall is neighbored by several settlements, one of which includes [[Lore:Anticlere (city)|Reich Gradkeep]] to the east.{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-High Rock towns.png|Map of High Rock – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

Daggerfall is divided into several districts but the most important are Castle Town, which is the northern central district that contains the main city gate and its guildhalls. Also, the Harbor District, which is the city's main port-of-call on the Eltheric Ocean, and the Trade District, the southern district across the [[Lore:Lorais River|Lorais River]] from Castle Town that contains the local marketplace and the Cathedral. There are many landmarks between them, such as the lake behind the cathedral and the local wayshrine in northernmost Castle Town. Towering over the marketplace basin and the Harbor District thoroughfare is [[Lore:Castle Daggerfall|Castle Daggerfall]], the king's seat of power. The path up to the castle is a large set of stairs on a street that connects Castle Town with the Cathedral's lake. The castle itself is at the base of the great mountain, which is also the water source of Daggerfall's Lorais River.{{Ref|name=BAM|[[ON:Shiftless Gaven|Shiftless Gaven]] and [[ON:Panhandler Thomas|Panhandler Thomas]]' dialogue about the river during [[ON:Back-Alley Murders|Back-Alley Murders]]}}{{Ref|name=CC|group=UOL|[[ON:Cut Content|ESO Cut Content]] - [[Online: Cut_Content/Glenumbra#Dialogue|Daggerfall]]-related dialogue}} It flows down into the cathedral lake from the west and further down into the marketplace basin from the north until it splits Castle Town and the Trade District, and leaves the city eastward.{{Ref|name=ONDaggerfall}}

==History==
:''For the history of the kingdom and region, see the: '''[[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)#History|History of Daggerfall]]'''''
===Early History===
[[File:ON-place-Daggerfall Overlook (manor).jpg|thumb|right|Daggerfall Overlook, the city's original seat of power]]
Historians have surmised that a [[Lore:Proto-Breton|human]] settlement of some kind existed where Daggerfall is located since long before recorded history, but the first time it was used to refer to a general area was around {{Year|1E 246}}, during the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]]{{Ref|name=AHOD}} of the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|Nordic Empire]] under High King [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} According to the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]'s census, the [[Lore:Book of Life|Book of Life]], Daggerfall's population totaled 216 people, and these people typically thrived on the land as coastal fisherfolk or raised livestock on the fertile land. The settlement thrived under the Nords, and even though High King [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]]' death marked the decline of the Nordic Empire, Daggerfall continued to prosper as it expanded militarily.{{Ref|name=AHOD}} 

Cities such as Daggerfall and nearby [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]] lost touch with their Nordic roots and became thoroughly Bretonic over many years of assimilation and commerce.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/High Rock}}}} At some point in time,{{intnote|Edward|[Note 1]}} the kingdom was ruled by King [[Lore:Corcyr I|Corcyr I]], who banned [[Lore:Dark Elves|Dark Elves]] from entering the city.{{Ref|name=KEIV|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part IV}}}}{{intnote|Chimer|[Note 2]}} Years later his successor, King [[Lore:Edward|Edward]], drove out the Nords from High Rock. The weapon he used to achieve this, the Horn of Summoning, was reportedly buried in Daggerfall's extensive cave system.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} The [[Lore:Direnni Hegemony|Direnni Hegemony]], which rose to prominence around {{Year|1E 355}},{{ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}} used Daggerfall as a minor base of operations when they went to war with the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]]. One of Castle Daggerfall's first residents was [[Lore:Raven Direnni|Raven Direnni]], the master [[Lore:Enchanting|enchantress]] who helped secure victory in the [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]] in {{Year|1E 482}}.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=TLKOTA|{{Cite book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}}

The power of the Direnni Hegemony faded over the next several centuries, while Daggerfall's grew. King Thagore of Daggerfall's victory over Glenpoint in {{Year|1E 609}} cemented his kingdom's position as the preeminent economic, cultural, and military force in southern High Rock. In {{Year|1E 950}}, King [[Lore:Joile|Joile]] declared war on the kingdom of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] and led Daggerfall's armies into the [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]]. Many Breton warriors plundered ivory from the [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]],{{Ref|name=AHOD}} Daggerfall's ivory trade played a big part in the region's economic expansion.{{Ref|name=ONAntiTusks|[[Online:Wrothgar Antiquities#Tusks of the Orc-Father|Tusks of the Orc-Father]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} However, their gains from the Siege of Orsinium were trivial compared to those of Wayrest, as Orsinium's downfall opened trade throughout the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]]. The trade boom which resulted ended Daggerfall's effective monopoly over the Iliac Bay, and within several decades, Wayrest would surpass Daggerfall as the predominant trade power of the region. In {{Year|1E 1100}}, the [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Kingdom of Wayrest]] would be formed, one of Daggerfall's greatest rivals.{{Ref|name=AHOD}} 

After Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] incorporated High Rock into the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] in {{Year|1E 1029}},{{Ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} the city flourished.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} In {{Year|1E 2200}}, however, the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] had spread across Tamriel, starting from the continent's western harbors. In High Rock and the Iliac Bay, Daggerfall was the first port to contract the disease, and in turn, the Iliac Bay's population drastically decreased.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=1TTP|{{Cite Book|1. The Thrassian Plague}}}}

===Daggerfall in the Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Daggerfall.jpg|thumb|right|The Gates of Daggerfall, where Emperor Durcorach was defeated]]
[[File:ON-trailer-Deadlands and Damnation-Durcorach.jpg|thumb|left|Emperor Durcorach the Black Drake]]
In {{Year|2E 455}}, during Queen Donella's reign,{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}} the city was terrorized by a serial killer known as the Daggerfall Devil, who claimed dozens of innocent lives. In early [[Lore:Evening Star|Evening Star]], the killer was caught and due for execution. However, he was able to evade [[Lore:Death|death]] by making a deal with the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] of Fate and Hidden Knowledge, [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]. On the 7th of Evening Star, the Daggerfall Devil escaped to [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]].{{Ref|name=POTDD|{{Cite Book|Pact of the Daggerfall Devil}}}}

In the sixth century of the Second Era, following the [[Lore:Battle of Granden Tor|Battle of Granden Tor]], the kingdoms of High Rock experienced a dozen years of peace. Ships from Daggerfall traded in every port across Tamriel. That peace was threatened, however, when Emperor [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]] invaded High Rock in {{Year|2E 541}}. After a lengthy siege on Wayrest that yielded no results,{{Ref|name=TOAM3|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch, Ch. 3}}}} the Emperor diverged his forces and set his sights on Daggerfall.{{Ref|name=SHOTLE|{{Cite Book|Secret History of the Longhouse Emperors}}}} As the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] were about to mount their attack on the city's gates, they were flanked by [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric of Cumberland]] and his army of Wayrest's finest warriors. The Emperor was defeated by Emeric and the King of Daggerfall, [[Lore:Bergamot (person)|Bergamot]], was able to finish off the rest with his group of knights.{{Ref|name=TOAM3}}

Only a fortnight later, the kings of High Rock banded together to create the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]],{{Ref|name=TOAM3}} with Wayrest as its capital, much to the chagrin of Daggerfall's citizens. Daggerfall was positioned to play a central role in the Covenant, but Wayrest's growth outpaced their own thanks to the [[Lore:Cumberland|Cumberland]] family, who judiciously exploited newly-discovered ore deposits. Bergamot's successor, [[Lore:Folbert the Wide|Folbert the Wide]], aided Wayrest during [[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]] and participated in the wider Daggerfall Covenant of {{Year|1E 567}}. He evidently believed that, while Wayrest had been lucky in recent years, their luck was sure to run out, and when it did, Daggerfall would rise to the top again.{{Ref|name=EGT}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, a conspiracy was uncovered involving the burgeoning [[Lore:Bloodthorn Cult|Bloodthorn Cult]] and their assassination attempt on King Casimir. With help from Captain Aresin of the city guard, an [[Lore:The Vestige|adventurer]] was able to defeat the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] assassins and save the King. For their service, King Casimir gave the adventurer the task of investigating the Bloodthorn Cult and their leader, [[Lore:Angof|Angof]] as they wreak havoc across Glenumbra.{{Ref|name=ONCrescent|[[Online:Blood and the Crescent Moon|Blood and the Crescent Moon]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONBloodthorn|[[Online:Bloodthorn Assassins|Bloodthorn Assassins]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Daggerfall in the Third Empire===
[[File:DF-misc-Lysandus 02.png|thumb|right|The ghost of King Lysandus]]
By the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Daggerfall was recognized by the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] as the de-facto capital of High Rock, by its antiquity, prominence, and prosperity.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} In a map of other capitals in the Empire, Daggerfall is listed among them.{{Ref|name=MWMapPOT|[[:File:MW-book-Provinces of Tamriel.jpeg|Map of Tamriel – Provinces of Tamriel]]}} The city was in relative peace during these early years, although not long after the Battle of the Bjoulsae, Admiral [[Lore:Vasi Hadrach|Vasi Hadrach]] was assassinated by rebel Reachmen in a Daggerfall brothel.{{Ref|name=RGBio|[[General:Redguard.com Archive/Characters|Amiel Richton' biography – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}} Years later under [[Lore:Senecus Goddkey|Senecus Goddkey]]'s reign over [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], nobles from Daggerfall vacationed to the [[Lore:Sentinel|capital city]] across the bay.{{Ref|name=PGE1H|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}} 

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Daggerfall was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Tristyctor and had developed rivalries with Camlorn and the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} A census was held in {{Year|3E 401}} which revealed the city's population to be over 110,000, well beyond that of other rivals like Sentinel or Wayrest.{{Ref|name=AHOD}} Not long after this census, the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] broke out between the Kingdoms of Daggerfall and Sentinel, and although the former emerged victorious, the events of the war have left a significant impact on Daggerfall and its people.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|ns_base=BK}}}} 

In {{Year|3E 404}},{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} on the 13th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] (the [[Lore:Day of the Dead|Day of the Dead]]){{Ref|name=DFDead|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of the Dead|Day of the Dead]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]]' [[Lore:Ghost|ghost]] returned and began haunting the city's streets at night with a horde of spirits. As he roamed around screaming vengeance, ghosts and [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] terrorized the local populace. The city guard could barely fight back, and as more of them died fighting, more spirits joined Lysandus.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} In {{Year|3E 405}}, an [[Lore:Agent|Agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] traveled to the Iliac Bay and over time, avenged the King by taking down the person who killed him, Lord [[Lore:Woodborne|Woodborne]]. Whether that was actually killing him in his [[Lore:Woodborne Hall|home]] in Wayrest or revealing the truth is unknown, but with his downfall, the curse on Daggerfall city was lifted.{{Ref|name=DFRevenge|[[Daggerfall:Lysandus' Revenge|Lysandus' Revenge]] story quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:DF-banner-Daggerfall Crest.png|thumb|left|The Crest of Daggerfall]]
[[File:ON-misc-Kynareth.png|thumb|right|A Bretonic statue of Kynareth]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Breton#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Breton#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Breton page.''
Daggerfall and the surrounding Daenia region are the home of [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] society's quest obsession. Young people from the lower and serf classes partake in heroic tasks and adventures to rise to the ranks of nobility.{{Ref|name=GP}} The people are also obsessed with their past,{{Ref|name=WJOTB}} despite Bretons being unsentimental about history.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The city recognizes a couple of holidays much like the rest of the kingdom, namely [[Lore:Dancing Day|Dancing Day]] on the 23rd of [[Lore:Midyear|Midyear]]{{Ref|name=DFDancing|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Dancing Day|Dancing Day]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and the Day of the Dead on the 13th of Rain's Hand.{{Ref|name=DFDead}}

The kingdom's local temple is the [[Lore:Temple of Kynareth|Temple of Kynareth]], whose patron deity, [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]] is considered the kingdom's goddess-protector.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} Two of her temples are located in the city but Daggerfall's main place of worship is the Daggerfall Cathedral in the Trade District, which worships the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] as a whole.{{Ref|name=ONCathedral|[[Online:Daggerfall Cathedral|Daggerfall Cathedral]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Knights of the Dragon|Knights of the Dragon]] is the kingdom's [[Lore:Knightly Orders|knightly order]] and has two garrisons in the capital city.{{Ref|name=DFCity}} Although sometimes the Kingship of Daggerfall is known as the Dragon,{{Ref|name=KOTD|{{Cite Book|Knights of the Dragon (book)}}}} the dragon on the kingdom's banner represents [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]].{{Ref|name=ONPitof|[[Online:Pitof|Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Many noble Breton houses live in Daggerfall and have varying influence in the kingdom. One of these houses is House Silvelle, a noble trading house that took in the former heir of another local house, Bastian Hallix as a troubleshooter.{{Ref|name=MTCBastion|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Bastian Hallix}}}} Others include, the [[Lore:Baelborne|Baelbornes]],{{Ref|name=ONAthel|[[Online:Athel Baelborne|Athel Baelborne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the Bouchends,{{Ref|name=ONHallix|[[Online:Bastian Hallix#Things Lost, Things Found|Bastian Hallix]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} [[Lore:House Diel|House Diel]],{{Ref|name=CWHD|{{Cite Book|Contract with House Diel|ns_base=ON}}}} the Laurents,{{Ref|name=ONStibbons|[[Online:Stibbons#The Jeweled Crown of Anton|Stibbons]]' dialogue during the quest [[Online:The Jeweled Crown of Anton|The Jeweled Crown of Anton]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONClarisse|[[Online:Lady Clarisse Laurent#The Jeweled Crown of Anton|Lady Clarisse Laurent]]'s dialogue during the quest [[Online:The Jeweled Crown of Anton|The Jeweled Crown of Anton]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the Themonds.{{Ref|name=ONTsiniuc|[[Online:Tsiniuc|Tsiniuc]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Daggerfall is a central trading hub in western Tamriel and the Iliac Bay,{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=TOAM3}} although, its importance has lessened since the rise of Wayrest.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Despite this, Daggerfall sees imports from all over the continent. Smugglers from [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] to the east sell [[Lore:Scuttler|scuttlers]] and [[Lore:Bantam Guar|bantam guars]] as pets{{Ref|name=MF1|{{Cite Book|Morrowind Fauna, Part One}}}} while some illicitly smuggle [[Lore:Skooma|skooma]].{{Ref|name=CDJ1|{{Cite Book|Cap'n Dugal's Journal, Part 1}}}} The [[Lore:Larimar|Larimar]] stones from [[Lore:The Systres|the Systres]] are known as "sea-stones" by the merchants in Daggerfall because of their color.{{Ref|name=ONLarimarAnti|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Larimar Gems|Larimar Gems]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Locally, the city's metalworks creates [[Lore:Steel|steel]] that the [[Lore:Knights of the Silver Rose|Knights of the Silver Rose]] use for their axes.{{Ref|name=CM106|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 106: Silver Rose Style}}}} Pranks & Pleasures was a popular card game in Daggerfall during the mid-Second Era.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresCards|[[Online:Treasures P#Pranks & Pleasures Card Game|Pranks & Pleasures Card Game]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Merchants Guild|Merchants Guild]] has a chapter in Daggerfall and members in good standing are awarded a small, golden anvil emblematic of [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]].{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresAnvil|[[Online:Treasures G#Golden Anvil of Zenithar|Golden Anvil of Zenithar]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Architects Guild|Architects Guild]] were also present in Daggerfall, with one of their most well-known projects being [[Lore:Castle Thorn|Castle Thorn]] in the mountains of Skyrim.{{Ref|name=ASSR|{{Cite Book|Architectural Survey Summary Report|ns_base=ON}}}}

Historically, there is a summer shipping route between Daggerfall and [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], and since then, [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]]{{Ref|name=NAA|{{Cite Book|Northpoint: An Assessment}}}} and [[Lore:Morkul Stronghold|Morkul Stronghold]] were added as stopovers on the route.{{Ref|name=NJW|{{Cite Book|Naryu's Journal/Wrothgar}}}} An alternate route used by the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]] in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] branches Daggerfall with [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] and [[Lore:Farrun|Farrun]].{{Ref|name=SRMapEEC|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} People will use the phrase, "from Daggerfall to [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]]" to emphasize the size of something since these are the furthest anchored cities on Tamriel.{{Ref|name=OBTsrava|[[Oblivion:Tsrava|Tsrava]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=SJ|{{Cite Book|Sondivel's Journal}}}}

Many people sail into Daggerfall's harbors but from the north, people are advised to look out for the [[Lore:Glenumbra Banks|Glenumbra Banks]], shifting sand bars that are constantly changing throughout the seasons. Local pilots in the city are especially wary of the banks between Mid Year and [[Lore:Sun's Height|Sun's Height]] and are left wondering how they can change so drastically overnight. Some people believe that mythical leviathan, [[Lore:Ithguleoir|Ithguleoir]], fills the old bank channels and dredges new ones.{{Ref|name=BTGB}}
