[[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]] (formerly known as '''Reich Gradkeep'''){{Ref|name=TDCN|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|ns_base=BK}}}} is a historical country in southwestern [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], right on the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region.{{Ref|name=DFAnticlere|[[Daggerfall:Anticlere|Anticlere]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Beyond the three original kingdoms in the region, Anticlere is one of the primary powers in the Iliac Bay.{{Ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}} The region, as well as southern High Rock is known for its rolling, verdant hills.{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} The Goddess of Love and Fertility, [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] is considered the goddess-protector of Anticlere and by the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the [[Lore:Knights of the Flame|Knights of the Flame]] served as the region's [[Lore:Breton Knightly Orders|knightly order]] and protector of the royal family.{{Ref|name=DFAnticlere}}<noinclude>

===Early History===
[[File:OB-place-Leyawiin (Torval the Pilot - Cropped).jpg|thumb|right|Topal the Pilot]]
When Anticlere, the political region was founded is unknown, but the region may have been first discovered by [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] explorers from the middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{Ref|name=FOTN}} In [[Lore:Florin Jaliil|Florin Jaliil]]'s translation and dissertation of the ''[[Lore:Udhendra Nibenu|Udhendra Nibenu]]'', [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]]'s written records of his voyage, he believed that the Aldmeri explorer sailed northeast from [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]] and found the Iliac Bay after roughly two months of travel. As Topal wrote, he landed on a "sun-kissed meadow of gentle dells" but as his men rested, ferocious [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] from the glen attacked them. This note would contradict the ancient tradition of how [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]]'s defeat gave rise to the Orsimer after the elves had already inhabited mainland Tamriel.{{Ref|name=FOTN}} Suffice it to say, no Orcish tribes or strongholds existed in Anticlere in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{Ref|name=DFDungeons|[[Daggerfall:Anticlere/Dungeons|Anticlere]] dungeons in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The settlement of Reich Gradkeep was founded in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] of the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|Nordic Empire]], who built fortified coastal towns amid their conquest of High Rock. These settlements became assimilated by the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] over time and turned into modern-day cities.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/High Rock}}}} Other races and subcultures historically live in the Anticlere region. [[Lore:Barbarians|Barbarians]] live in strongholds throughout the region, and minor races such as [[Lore:Giant|giants]] and [[Lore:Harpy|harpies]] live in dungeons.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}} The [[Lore:Haarvenu|Haarvenu]] bloodline is the most dominant one in the region.{{Ref|name=DFAnticlere}}

Citizens of Anticlere observe the 21st of [[Lore:Last Seed|Last Seed]] as [[Lore:Appreciation Day|Appreciation Day]], an ancient holiday held in honor of the goddess-protector, Mara. It is a holy and contemplative day with a thanksgiving celebration dedicated to the bountiful harvest.{{Ref|name=DFHolidays|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Appreciation Day|Appreciation Day]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} By the fifteenth century of the First Era, Reich Gradkeep was ruled by a Duke,{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/High Rock}}}} whom by tradition governs a duchy of the same name.{{Ref|name=NRAT|{{Cite Book|Noble Ranks and Titles}}}} By the time the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]] traveled north toward the Iliac Bay, the Lord of Reich Gradkeep was deathly ill and could not build a defensive effort against the invader. They passed away at the end of {{Year|3E 266}} and did not live to see the Usurper's defeat the year later.{{Ref|name=TFOTU}}{{NewLine}}

===Reich Gradkeep and the War of Betony===
[[File:DF-npc-Lord Auberon Flyte (face).png|thumb|left|Lord Auberon Flyte]]
[[File:DF-npc-Lady Doryanna Flyte (face).png|thumb|right|Lady Doryanna Flyte]]
By the late Third Era, Reich Gradkeep was a fiefdom ruled by the Graddock family. When the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] occurred between the kingdoms of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], Reich Gradkeep took a neutral stance,{{Ref|name=TWOB|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Newgate)}}}} and as King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] began to advocate for a peace treaty, Lord [[Lore:Graddock|Graddock]] stepped forward to serve as the facilitator with other notaries. At the time, Lord Graddock was married to Lady Graddock and had two children, the heir apparent Mara Graddock, and a sickly infant.{{Ref|name=TDCN}}

The summit for the [[Lore:Treaty of Reich Gradkeep|treaty]]'s signing was held in {{Year|3E 403}} in the capital city's palace. Despite its promising start, it ended in complete disaster as violence broke out in the palace and into the city streets. The bloody massacre within Reich Gradkeep's palace took the Graddock family's lives, except for the infant while the kingdoms' armies met in battle one last time in [[Lore:Cryngaine Field|Cryngaine Field]]. With no one else in the royal family to ably rule the region, Lord Graddock's cousin, [[Lore:Auberon Flyte|Auberon Flyte]] was appointed as a regency until the child was old enough.{{Ref|name=TDCN}} 

Lord Flyte's early reign was described as "strong, almost dictatorial" but was able to restore order admirably, for both the town{{Ref|name=TDCN}} and even the Knights of the Flame, who swore fealty to the [[Lore:Flyte|Flyte]] family for centuries.{{Ref|name=DFGenFlame|[[Daggerfall:Generic Dialogue|Knights of the Flame]] generic descriptions in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} When the infant passed not long after, Auberon Flyte was able to properly succeed as ruler of Reich Gradkeep and in honor of his ancestral house, renamed both the city and region to Anticlere. His wife, Lady [[Lore:Doryanna Flyte|Doryanna Flyte]] was just as instrumental in Anticlere's success as her ability to coerce counselors and merchants is how Lord Flyte's imperious demands were executed.{{Ref|name=TDCN}} With his transition into power, he appointed the Knights of the Flame as the region's knightly order.{{Ref|name=DFGenFlame}}

===Anticlere in the late Third Era and onward===
Lord Flyte had an apparent disdain for everyone in his company, including his wife but all of that changed when he contracted the [[Lore:Guedoilic Plague|Guedoilic Plague]] from an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] ambassador. He became sick and feeble and retired to his bedchamber while he gave Lady Doryanna significant power to effectively rule in his stead. She was quick to replace Anticlere's previous council of older, more experienced members with newer, younger members that she could easily manipulate.{{Ref|name=TDCN}} The Knights of the Flame, consisting of loyal citizens of Anticlere were well-received in the region.{{Ref|name=DFGenFlame}} 

There were rumors at the time that claimed Lady Doryanna was consorting with the local guildmaster of the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] and that she participated in midnight raids, under a disguise on Anticlere's highways.{{Ref|name=TDCN}} At the same time, the Knights of the Flame could not patrol the fiefdom's roads and were only regulated to the cities, much to their confusion. Some of these knights also served as Lady Doryanna as her personal guard.{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|100}}

By {{Year|3E 405}}, Lady Doryanna Flyte continued to hold power over Anticlere. Since Mara is the region's goddess-protector, the [[Lore:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]] was Anticlere's main temple and found across its many cities. These cities included Aldingborne Commons, Aldshire, Aldwall Rock, Charborne Wood, Chestermarket, Cromward, Crosswych Wood, Eastcastle, Gallovalloe, Grayborne, Holleigh, Ipscart Rock, Ipstale, Kirkhope, Lamtown, Newwell, Oxfort, Stoklech, and Tamham.{{Ref|name=DFAnticlere}} Anticlere was neighbored by several nations, including the [[Lore:Daenia (duchy)|Daenia]] to the north, [[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]] to the east, [[Lore:Shalgora|Shalgora]] to the west, and [[Lore:Urvaius|Urvaius]] to the northeast.{{Ref|name=DFMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of the Iliac Bay – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}} As [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]]'s docks were expensive, it was not uncommon for travellers travelling through the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]] to stop at the small fishing villages along its coast instead.{{Ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}

In the wake of the [[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]] in {{Year|3E 417}}, the kingdoms of the Iliac Bay annexed the many minor nations of the region and expanded their borders. Anticlere was caught by Daggerfall and Wayrest, and split between them{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} as a buffer. These separate halves of the region were referred to respectively as '''Daggerfallian Anticlere''' and '''Wayrestian Anticlere'''.{{Ref|name=LRPTESP|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/The Evening Star Parade|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 7/The Evening Star Parade – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} The land between kingdoms were blackened, desolate wastes that no mortal army could create.{{Ref|name=TWITW}} While violence between the neighboring kingdoms had waned over the years, localized skirmishes on the border continued into the twilight years of the Third Era.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

===Known Rulers===
[[File:DF-place-Anticlere Palace.png|thumb|right|The Palace in Anticlere]]
While the region is considered a fiefdom, as a state ruled by lords and ladies, Anticlere is known as a lorddom.{{Ref|name=DFGenFlame}} In the mid First Era, Reich Gradkeep was formerly a duchy.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Families descended from the house of Anticlere had presided over the region since before it was renamed. In the late Third Era, the Graddock family was the ruling house and their cousins were the [[Lore:Flyte|Flyte]] family. The ruler of Anticlere is a separate entity from the mayor of the capital city.{{Ref|name=TDCN}}

==Notes==
*Even though the region was renamed to Anticlere by Lord Auberon Flyte in the late Third Era, the ''[[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition]]'' uses the name despite being written around {{Year|2E 864}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} However, early versions of ''[[Lore:Father of the Niben|Father of the Niben]]'' (specifically versions that were circulating in {{Year|2E 582}}) did not mention the name Anticlere, which appeared in later versions in the Third Era.
*There is an unnamed island off the coast of the fiefdom, south of the capital city. There were some towns and ruins on the island by the late Third Era.{{Ref|name=DFAnticlere}} It could possibly be [[Lore:Dourstone Isle|Dourstone Isle]], given its location.
