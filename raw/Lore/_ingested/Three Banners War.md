The [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], also called the '''Alliance War''' and the '''Great War''',{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Fasaran's Diary|ns_base=ON}}}} or the '''Usurper War''' by Imperials, was a major conflict involving numerous [[Lore:races|races]] and [[Lore:factions|factions]] that took place across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].

The years leading up to the war saw a divided Tamriel witness a series of conflicts and crises that inspired the different nations of the land to band together into three distinct alliances: the greater [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] in {{Year|2E 566}},{{ref|name=GTTDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}} the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] in {{Year|2E 572}},{{ref|name=GTTEP|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Ebonheart Pact}} — anonymous ethnology (~2E 580)}} and the first [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] in {{Year|2E 580}}.{{ref|name=TROQA|{{Cite Book|The Rise of Queen Ayrenn}}}}{{ref|name=ATUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn – The Unforeseen Queen}}}}

The competing ideologies of these alliances set them on a course for war, which began in earnest sometime in {{Year|2E 580}}. Tensions between the alliances were further exacerbated by the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] and subsequent military aggression of the Empire of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] under [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s puppet,{{Ref|name=ONAbnurTharn|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CotFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}{{ref|name=AL|{{Cite Book|Anchorite's Log}}}} Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]].{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}} Each alliance saw the opportunity to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime by capturing the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and installing their own emperor on the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]], but also had to deal with the other two alliances.{{ref|name=Carlier|{{Cite Book|Lady Carlier on the topic of the Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=barracks|{{Cite Book|Posted outside barracks at Windhelm}}}} The three central leaders of these three competing alliances were [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] of the Aldmeri Dominion, [[Lore:Emeric|High King Emeric]] of the Daggerfall Covenant, and [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] of the Ebonheart Pact.

The armies of the Covenant, Pact, and Dominion each invaded Cyrodiil, which served as the main theater of war, however they also launched attacks against each other's lands. The war caused enormous damage to Cyrodiil's towns and cities, and countless civilians were displaced and resettled throughout Tamriel.{{ref|name=ONWrits|[[Online:Imperial Charity Writs|Imperial Charity Writs]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The war was ongoing as of {{Year|2E 582}},{{ref|[[Online:Alliance War|Alliance War]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} despite numerous attempts at [[#The Three Banners Truce|peace talks]]. The war's ultimate outcome is unknown, but all three alliances, as well as the Empire of Cyrodiil, had ceased to exist by the ninth century of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].

The conflict of the Three Banners War is often symbolically represented by a three-headed [[Lore:Ouroboros|ouroboros]], with each head symbolizing one of the alliances: the eagle for the Aldmeri Dominion, the lion for the Daggerfall Covenant, and the dragon for the Ebonheart Pact.{{Ref|name=Legends|[[Legends:Card Backs#Alliance War|Alliance War]] card back in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=AUA|group=UOL|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 15|Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 15]]}}<noinclude>{{NewLine}}

==War in Cyrodiil==
{{TOCright|limit=2}}
{{Quote Box|''The Imperial province of Cyrodiil dominates central Tamriel. With the Empire's collapse, armies of the Dominion, Covenant, and Pact have all invaded Cyrodiil, vying for the Imperial throne and control of the greatest prize in the land.''|[[Online:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:LG-cardart-Emeric, Covenant King.png|left|thumb|250px|High King Emeric of the Daggerfall Covenant]]
All three alliances launched an invasion into [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] via adjacent territories under their control and established bases of operations across the border. Grand Warlord Dortene, leading the Daggerfall Covenant forces, invaded Cyrodiil from [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] via the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] and the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian Highlands]] in the northwest.{{ref|name=ONCDortene|[[Online:Cyrodiil Gate Garrison|Cyrodiil Gate Garrison]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Grand Warlord Zimmeron, leading the Ebonheart Pact forces, invaded from [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] via the [[Lore:Valus Mountains|Valus Mountains]] in the northeast.{{ref|name=ONCZimmeron|[[Online:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] map in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Grand Warlord Sorcalin, leading the Aldmeri Dominion forces, invaded from [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]] via the [[Lore:Nibenay Valley|Nibenay Valley]] in the south.{{ref|name=ONCSorcalin|[[Online:Border Crossings#Reaper's_March_Eastern_Gate|Reaper's March Border Crossings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The three alliances established individual [[Lore:Transitus Network|Transitus Network]]s in Cyrodiil, a form of teleportation magic. This temporary teleportation network allowed troops to instantaneously travel between keeps and outposts, although the networks seemingly had limited range and required constant maintenance by groups of mages when pushing deeper into enemy territory.{{ref|name=Herendas|[[ON:Vother Herendas|Vother Herendas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Canis|[[ON:Felycie Canis|Felycie Canis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Viriniel|[[ON:Viriniel (Eastern Elsweyr Gate)|Viriniel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

During the war soldiers from each alliance discovered the whereabouts of several [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]] that were kept hidden by the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Cult of the Ancestor Moth]]. The alliances stole the scrolls and built vast holy temples across Cyrodiil to house the scrolls they had stolen.{{ref|name=ONAxius|[[Online:Sandana Axius|Sandana Axius]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The temples were built close enough to the battlefields to bestow the scrolls' blessing onto the troops.{{ref|name=ONPA|[[Online:Protector Arfire|Protector Arfire]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Some soldiers also sought the Blessing of the Whitestrake - a boon that was said to imbue warriors with a "holy fervor for righteous carnage" and improve their contributions to the war. Priests of St. [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] were found in each alliance garrison during [[Lore:Pelinal's Midyear Massacre|Pelinal's Midyear Massacre]] offering to bestow blessings on anyone who asked for them.{{ref|name=ONMaera|[[Online:Predicant Maera|Predicant Maera]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONPredicant|[[Online:Pelinal Predicant|Pelinal Predicant]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Unsatisfied with the carnage taking place in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] involved himself indirectly with the war when he sent [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]] to the battlefields hoping the resulting chaos would bring him entertainment. The hammer was used by all three alliances against each other throughout the war, as it changed hands often.{{ref|name=CCTCWAA|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/56335 Chaos Comes to Cyrodiil with Artifact Weapons ] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}{{ref|name=ESOChaosMagic|[[Online:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in the quest [[Online:Chaos Magic|Chaos Magic]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The alliances issued "[[Lore:Alliance Points|alliance points]]" as a form of scrip to those who participated in the Three Banners War. This was treated as currency along the front. Soldiers from the war were also encouraged to participate in the bloodsports held by the [[Lore:Circle of Champions|Circle of Champions]] by being rewarded by these alliance points.{{ref|[[Online:Alliance Points|Alliance Points]] Daily Rewards description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} While participating in these bloodsports, they did not fight for Queen Ayrenn, High King Emeric, or Jorunn the Skald-King, but for the Pit Daemons, Fire Drakes, or Storm Lords teams.{{Ref|[[General:ESO: Morrowind – Battlegrounds Basics|ESO: Morrowind – Battlegrounds Basics]] from the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}

===Siege Warfare===
[[File:ON-concept-Keep walls.png|right|300px]]
Battles between the alliances in Cyrodiil were characterized primarily through siege warfare. Great keeps and forts were fought over by the alliances and used to further their invasion deeper into Cyrodiil, with the ultimate goal of capturing the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] at the center of the province. Each alliance fleetingly gained control of the Imperial City at some point at during the war, and they would crown a figurehead Emperor from among their warriors, however their reigns were extremely short-lived.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions 2}}}}

One notable conflict was the Battle of Chalman Keep - a three-way battle between Pact, Dominion, and Covenant forces at the contested and strategically important [[Lore:Chalman Keep|Chalman Keep]]. The Covenant defenders held out against two enemy armies, but ultimately the keep fell to a Dominion invasion force, after which the Dominion crowned a [[Lore:Battlefield Emperors|figurehead]] [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|Emperor]] from among their warriors.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Those Who Stood at Chalman Keep}}}}{{ref|[[Legends:Battle of Chalman Keep|Battle of Chalman Keep]] card in ''[[LG:Legends|Legends]]''}}

===Cities Affected by War===
While large parts of central Cyrodiil faced the horrors and destruction of open warfare, some areas around the periphery of the war zone remained relatively unscathed. The counties of [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]] and [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] were protected from the ongoing battles by [[Lore:Varen's Wall|Varen's Wall]], a large edifice created to split the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]] from the rest of the [[Lore:Colovian Estates|Colovian Estates]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Varen's Wall}}}} Despite this protection from the war, Anvil was seized by the Red Sails pirates led by "Pirate Queen" [[Lore:Fortunata ap Dugal|Fortunata ap Dugal]] who overthrew Count [[Lore:Ephrem Benirus|Ephrem Benirus]] and declared independence.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Pirate Queen of the Gold Coast}}}} This led to a state of hostility between Anvil and Kvatch and open engagements such as the Gottshaw Massacre.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Wolf and the Pirate Queen}}}} Count [[Lore:Calantius of Skingrad|Calantius]] of [[Lore:Skingrad|Skingrad]] was said to be a canny opportunist who managed to keep his city just out of the chaos of war.{{ref|name=SZLMIwLS|{{Cite Book|Special ZOS Lore Master Interview with Lawrence Schick|ns_base=GEN}}}} Countess [[Lore:Nevenia Caro|Nevenia Caro]] of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] ceded control and protection of the city to the Chamber of Legates and the [[Lore:Ivory Brigade|Ivory Brigade]] prior to the war. Since the fall of the Empire, the Ivory Brigade doubled its efforts to replace the Imperial Legion and maintained active defenses to keep the ongoing war at bay.{{ref|name=ONLiore|[[Online:Captain Rian Liore|Captain Rian Liore]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] became neutral ground during war, but was attacked by [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] when a [[Lore:Dark Anchor|Dark Anchor]] opened above the city in an attempt to draw it into [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]]. Several citizens barricaded themselves within key buildings, and the survivors sent pleas to both the Daggerfall Covenant and the Ebonheart Pact for aid.{{ref|name=Brumaaid|''[[Online:Bruma Pleads for Aid|Bruma Pleads for Aid]]'' &mdash; Grigerda}}

Other towns did not fare as well. The Imperial garrison amassed control over [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]] and imposed themselves as tyrannical rulers.{{Ref|name=ONVyctoria|[[Online:Vyctoria Girien|Vyctoria Girien]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]] was devastated by large fissure that tore through the town years prior to the war. The town became plagued by famine and banditry and changed hands many times over the course of the war.{{ref|name=TCC}}{{ref|[[ON:Chorrol|Chorrol]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] was subject to a mass slaughter and looting when Captain Jean Apinia and her legion took refuge in the city. Captain Apinia asked the mayor to give up their food supplies to aid her legion. When the Mayor refused Captain Apinia ordered her troops begin slaughtering the cityfolk, resulting in over half the population being killed and the town looted of valuables.{{ref|name=TBoB|{{Cite Book|The Butcher of Bravil}}}}{{Ref|name=RD|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Debts of War|Debts of War]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} These events however pale in comparison to the destruction that was wrought upon the Imperial City.

==Conquest of the Imperial City==
[[File:ON-concept-22.jpg|ON-concept-22.jpg|400px|The three alliances fight for control of the Imperial City|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|''At the center of Cyrodiil stands the Imperial City. There, the Three Alliances vie with Daedric overlords and each other for control of the White-Gold Tower.''|[[Online:Imperial City|Imperial City]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

While the war raged across the countryside of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], all three alliances were locked in a struggle for control of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] in an effort to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime and install their own emperor on the Ruby Throne.

Sappers in each alliance established forward bases in the city's sewers to launch attacks on the districts above in an attempt to thin the enemy's numbers before marching on the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]].{{ref|name=ONRythe|[[Online:Captain Rythe|Captain Rythe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONNedras|[[Online:General Nedras|General Nedras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

During the fighting, a new danger unexpectedly appeared when chains fell from the sky. The [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] began and the forces of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] swarmed the city districts and sewers and took the city. Many [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] soldiers died defending the city while others fled.{{ref|name=TBoB}} Fighting alongside [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s Daedra horde were a group known as [[Lore:Legion Zero|Legion Zero]]. These were members of the Imperial Legion that deserted when the Planemeld began and swore fealty to Molag Bal and the [[Lore:Worm Cult|Worm Cult]]{{ref|{{Cite Book|Naryu's Journal/Imperial City}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Drake of Blades Answers Your Questions}}}} While the entire city was tearing itself apart, a small group of Imperial Legion loyalists known as the [[Lore:Immortal Eight|Immortal Eight]] fought back against the alliances and Legion Zero and played a part in stopping Bal's plans to snuff out the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]].{{ref|name=TSB|[[Online:The Sublime Brazier|The Sublime Brazier]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MTCCC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Captain Caudex}}}} As a result of the war, the city itself was devastated. Few city folk remained and the buildings in each district suffered enormous damage, some completely demolished.

==Military Campaigns==
Although the main theater for the war took place in the province of Cyrodiil, numerous invasions, battles and skirmishes took place within the lands of the respective powers. The Daggerfall Covenant was arguably the most aggressive in their attacks on other alliances, making multiple forays primarily into Ebonheart Pact and Dominion territory. Similarly, the Dominion also plotted mainly against the Pact through their schemes in [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]]. As such, the Pact spent much of the war defending their lands, with their only notable invasions being into Cyrodiil. The [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire]] did not sit idle as the armies of the alliances forged deep into its territory. During the war it launched its own invasions into both Covenant and Dominion territory.{{ref|name=ESOBangkorai|Bangkorai questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOMarch|Reaper's March questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Daggerfall Covenant Campaigns===
====Attack on Bleakrock Isle====
[[File:ON-place-Bleakrock Village.jpg|right|thumb|Bleakrock Village]]
:''"We sighted a ship offshore. I've sent my troops to investigate. If it's the vanguard of an invasion ... well, we'll need to evacuate."'' &mdash;Captain Rana
The Daggerfall Covenant targeted [[Lore:Bleakrock Isle|Bleakrock Isle]] as the first step to a large-scale invasion of the Ebonheart Pact's territory. In an attempt to cause a distraction for their initial naval attack, the Covenant hired the Frostedge bandits to steal relics of power in Hozzin's Folly and cause havoc on the isle.{{ref|name=ONHF|[[Online:Hozzin's Folly (quest)|Hozzin's Folly]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ContractScroll|{{Cite Book|Contract Scroll|ns_base=ON}}}} They also sent a necromancer to Skyshroud Barrow to raise the dead.{{ref|name=ONWWB|[[Online:What Waits Beneath|What Waits Beneath]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ScrollofBanishment|{{Cite Book|Scroll of Banishment|ns_base=ON}}}}

Although the Ebonheart Pact had established a small garrison on the island and thwarted these plots, it was too late, and they were unable to request backup in time. Led by General Vette, the Covenant began their attack and slaughtered many of the inhabitants of [[Lore:Bleakrock Village|Bleakrock Village]]. The village was razed by pyromancers but a small number of survivors were evacuated by ship to [[Lore:Dhalmora|Dhalmora]] in mainland [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|name=ONEFB|[[Online:Escape from Bleakrock|Escape from Bleakrock]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONRana|[[Online:Captain Rana|Captain Rana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Attack on Bal Foyen====
:''"Tell the soldiers there to light the signal fires along the coast. The fires will warn Davon's Watch that war is upon them."'' &mdash;Captain Rana
In an effort to warn the mainland of the impending Covenant naval invasion, Pact forces lit several signal fire watchtowers across [[Lore:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]].{{ref|name=ONIBS|[[Online:If By Sea|If By Sea]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In response, the Covenant launched a two-pronged attack on the region led by General Emax and General Vette. Large battles took place at both Fort Zeren and Bal Foyen's docks between Pact and Covenant forces.{{ref|name=ONBTT|[[Online:Breaking the Tide|Breaking the Tide]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONZIP|[[Online:Zeren in Peril|Zeren in Peril]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} While Bal Foyen suffered heavy losses, the Pact succeeded in driving off the invaders and warning the city of [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] to the west.{{ref|name=ONHolgunn|[[Online:Holgunn One-Eye|Holgunn One-Eye]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

====Siege of Davon's Watch====
[[File:ON-quest-City Under Siege 02.jpg|Balreth attacking a group of Daggerfall Covenant soldiers|thumb|right]]
:''"The Covenant is attacking Davon's Watch. The devils bombarded the northern district. Now they're ashore, preparing to attack. This port's the key to Stonefalls. That's why these Covenant bastards are attacking. They're keen on the city, but we aren't going to let them. The Nords are here to defend the Pact."'' &mdash;Holgunn One-Eye

Following the attack on Bal Foyen, the city of [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] was put under siege by the invading Daggerfall Covenant. Capturing Davon's port was the Covenant's key to pushing deeper into [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]]. After making landfall on the western beach, the Covenant bombarded the Davon's Watch for several days.{{ref|name=ONHolgunn}}

Although Pact forces managed to sabotage the Covenant siege equipment and delay their attacks, Covenant forces infiltrated Davon's Watch by scaling the cliffs, threatening take the city from within.{{ref|name=ONDTD|[[Online:Delaying the Daggers|Delaying the Daggers]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In a last ditch effort to save the city, Grandmaster Tanval Indoril performed a Daedric ritual to release [[Lore:Balreth|Balreth]], an ancient [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. Balreth completely destroyed the Covenant's invasion force, freeing Davon's Watch.{{ref|name=ONCUS|[[Online:City Under Siege (Stonefalls)|City Under Siege]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Failed Attack on Kragenmoor====
[[File:ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg|Kragenmoor|thumb|right]]
:''"The dogs of the Pact do not understand a contract well made. They see no profit in peace. The only thing they understand is force. Overwhelming force. If we're going to take Ebonheart, we need breaches, footholds. I've received word from a scout. She found an abandoned mine near Kragenmoor that might do nicely. Occupy it. Hold it. Use it. And do not fail me."'' &mdash;General Alexandra Conele
Despite their defeat at Davon's Watch, the Daggerfall Covenant remained fast in their plan to take [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]]. As they pushed into Stonefalls General Alexandra Conele sought to establish footholds to launch their attacks. One such place was in Softloam Cavern, an abandoned mine near Kragenmoor.{{ref|name=GeneralOrders|{{Cite Book|General Conele's Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} 

Within the cavern the [[Lore:Ring of Daggers|Ring of Daggers]] created a long-range portal that led straight back to Covenant lands. From here they began to amass their numbers in order to launch an attack on [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]] and the rest of Stonefalls. Pact soldiers discovered their plot and were successful in destroying the portal and preventing an attack on Kragenmoor.{{ref|name=ONDathlyn|[[Online:Dathlyn|Dathlyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONSTT|[[Online:Stem the Tide|Stem the Tide]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Battle at Vivec's Antlers====
[[File:ON-place-Vivec's Antlers Land-Coral.jpg|The glowing coral at Vivec's Antlers|thumb|right]]
:"''I don't care how you do it. I don't care if you have to invade that mud-hut pigsty next door and enslave every Argonian there, get it done! That heart is the key, and I will not fail in my mission. And neither will you."'' &mdash;General Serien
Although the Covenant's attack on Kragenmoor failed, they launched another major offensive on the shores to the west of Ebonheart. The Covenant sought to retrieve an artifact known as the [[Lore:Coral Heart|Coral Heart]] and use it to summon an ancient Daedra named [[Lore:Sadal|Sadal]], who would lay waste to Stonefalls. Led by General Alexandra Conele, the Covenant landed on the shores of [[Lore:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]] and clashed with Ebonheart Pact forces - outnumbering them five to one. In response, the Coral Heart summoned the local [[Lore:dreugh|dreugh]] to defend itself, halting the Covenant's invasion.{{ref|name=ONNoris|[[Online:Captain Noris|Captain Noris]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GetitDoneConele|{{Cite Book|Get it Done, Conele|ns_base=ON}}}}

Alexandra Conele eventually managed to steal the Coral Heart from its subterranean chamber, however a detachment of Ebonheart Pact soldiers fought and killed Conele and recovered the heart.{{ref|name=ONTCH|[[Online:The Coral Heart|The Coral Heart]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Battle at Fort Virak====
[[File:ON-place-Fort Virak.jpg|Fort Virak|thumb|right]]
:"''A scout from the northwest border made it to the city, bloody and bruised. He said a force was marching on Fort Virak. If they take Virak, they're on the doorstep of Riften, up in Skyrim."'' &mdash;Holgunn One-Eye
After their grave loss at Vivec's Antlers, the Daggerfall Covenant moved west toward Fort Virak and laid waste to the countryside. The fort was a vital link between Stonefalls and the Rift, as it controlled the supply lines between Pact forces.{{ref|name=ONHolgunn}} The Covenant managed to take the keep and quickly reinforced its walls, bracing for a massive siege from the Ebonheart Pact under the command of Garyn Indoril.{{ref|name=ONGjorring|[[Online:Sergeant Gjorring|Sergeant Gjorring]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Pact's siege had little effect on the keep's walls. During the war, Count Calantius of [[Lore:Skingrad|Skingrad]] took advantage of the power vacuum and re-designated the Arenthia Vale region of Valenwood as the South Weald of the Colovian Estates. This edict was supposedly ratified by the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] and gave the Legion of the West Weald the authority to annex its newly acquired lands and secure its borders. Previous ownership of all lands in Arenthia Vale were declared null and void and its previous inhabitants were deported or taken as slaves.

Javad Tharn was one of the leading figures in the campaign to capture the region. Although the pretext of the invasion was meant for its annexation, Javad Tharn had an ulterior motive that related to the downfall of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] and the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=ONGavo|[[Online:Centurion Gavo|Centurion Gavo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} General Lavinia Axius took command of the actual occupation and urged [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]ns to join the Legion of the West Weald, granting numerous incentives such as farms for soldiers and estates for officers.{{Ref|name=YFTT}}{{ref|name=SZLMIwLS}}
General Lavinia orchestrated a sneak attack on [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] with a large force. Although the city guard fought back, most died in the fighting and the city was subdued. During the occupation General Lavinia herself delivered propaganda speeches via magical projections in the town square to further cow the city folk.{{ref|name=ONTCO|[[Online:The Colovian Occupation|The Colovian Occupation]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONBurri|[[Online:Centurion Burri|Centurion Burri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONKazirra|[[Online:Kazirra|Kazirra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] of Arenthia Vale fought back and, with aid from the Aldmeri Dominion, formed a resistance against the invading Colovians. They learned that the Colovians themselves were being deceived by General Lavinia and Javad Tharn, who were both working with the Stonefire Daedric cult. The resistance believed that if the Colovians were to discover their general was consorting with a Daedric cult, their resolve would break and the legion would desert.{{ref|name=ONKazirra}}

The resistance kidnapped a Colovian centurion and forced a confession from him detailing General Lavinia's relationship with the Stonefire cult and their plans to construct a Dark Anchor in the Temple District of Arenthia. Using the same method General Lavinia used to deliver her propaganda speeches, the resistance broadcasted the confession to the Legion of the West Weald. As expected, the Legion abandoned the city and in the chaos the resistance stormed General Lavinia's base and killed her, thus ending the occupation.{{ref|name=ONTCO}}{{ref|name=ONGavo}}
{{NewLine}}

====Battle at Senalana====
[[File:ON-place-Senalana.jpg|thumb|right|Senalana]]
:''"We came for the circlet. General Lavinia has great plans. We've ... we've started with Arenthia. With the circlet, we'll take the Dominion apart piece ... by ... piece."'' &mdash;Gratidia Arius
While the main Colovian force was based in Arenthia, General Lavinia sent detachments to various locations in [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]], including Fort Grimwatch, Vinedusk and the Ayleid ruins of [[Lore:Senalana|Senalana]] in Reaper's March. Dominion scouts spread throughout the Northern Wood spotted the Colovians and responded immedietely.{{ref|name=ONBurri}}

At Senalana the Colovians sought a powerful artifact known as the Grand Circlet of Elven Authority. According to legend, the circlet's wearer would have complete domination over Elves. Every descendant of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] blood would bend the knee to its wearer. General Lavinia coveted the circlet and with it would have the power to defeat the Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=ONArius|[[Online:Gratidia Arius|Gratidia Arius]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

[[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], leader of Queen Ayrenn's personal intelligence network, led Dominion troops to battle with the Colovians to ensure the circlet didn't fall into their hands. After battling though Colovians and the numerous Ayleid traps within the ruin, the circlet was recovered by Dominion forces. It remains unknown if the circlet was destroyed or removed from the ruins and hidden elsewhere, but regardless the Colovians retreated from battle.{{ref|name=ONGOF|[[Online:Gates of Fire|Gates of Fire]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

====Attack on Vinedusk====
[[File:ON-place-Vinedusk Village.jpg|thumb|right|Headquarters of the Vinedusk Rangers]]
:''"The Rangers are spread thin across the Dominion and into Cyrodiil. Damned Colovians wouldn't have been able to take us at all if we were at full strength."'' &mdash;Captain Odreth
Furthering General Lavinia's plans in the invasion of [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]], she sent an elite division of the Colovian army, known as the [[Lore:Vlastarus|Vlastarus]] Cohort, to the military settlement of [[Lore:Vinedusk|Vinedusk]] and took it by surprise. The Vlastarus Cohort splintered off from the main attacking force at [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] with the mission to specifically take out the [[Lore:Vinedusk Rangers|Vinedusk Rangers]]. The rangers were able to hold back the invaders, despite many of their numbers spread across the Dominion territory and Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=ONMengaer|[[Online:Scout Mengaer|Scout Mengaer]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The commander of the rangers planned a two-pronged counterattack. First to burn the Colovians out of the village, then take them by surprise in their camp. With the aid of a Dominion agent the first stage of the rangers' plan was a success, but both sides suffered heavy losses.{{ref|name=ONOOI|[[Online:Oath of Excision|Oath of Excision]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONOdreth|[[Online:Captain Odreth|Captain Odreth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

With the Colovians removed from the village, the Vinedusk Rangers planned a counterattack. They used a secret tunnel to gain access to the Colovian camp that was built atop a nearby ruin. Once they were behind their front lines, the rangers destroyed the Colovian siege weapons, tents, and cargo before going after the commander of the Colovian army. The rangers were successful in killing the commander and ending the Colovian attack on Vinedusk.{{ref|name=ONDASH|[[Online:Down the Skeever Hole|Down the Skeever Hole]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

====Siege of Dune====
[[File:ON-quest-The Fires of Dune.jpg|thumb|right|Javad Tharn opens a Dark Anchor above Dune]]
:''"You cannot stop the inevitable. The city is ours. Soon I will hold your "Mane" in my hand, and my Dark Mane will walk the Path. The soul of the Khajiit will be lost to darkness!"'' &mdash;Javad Tharn
After the defeat at [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]], Javad Tharn grew tired of waiting and launched an attack on the city of [[Lore:Dune|Dune]]. Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] and the Lunar Champion were on their way to the Temple of Two Moons Path to anoint the new [[Lore:Mane|Mane]] when their advance team reported smoke over Dune and Stonefire cultists in the streets. Javad Tharn opened portals across Dune, allowing Daedra into the streets. His plan was to enslave the Lunar Champion and anoint his own Dark Mane, thus corrupting the spiritual soul of the Khajiit.{{ref|name=ONAyrenn|[[Online:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONJavad|[[Online:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

With the aid of the Moon Hallowed, the Lunar Champion was able to seal the portals and cleanse the desecrated shrines but this did not stop Javad Tharn opening a [[Lore:Dark Anchor|Dark Anchor]] above the city. The Moon Hallowed stormed the Shrine of Lorkhaj and attempted to stop Tharn, but he escaped into the demi-plane of [[Lore:Jode|Jode]]. Regardless, the siege was lifted and Dune was saved from the Daedric threat.{{ref|name=ONFOD|[[Online:The Fires of Dune|The Fires of Dune]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

==The Three Banners Truce==
[[File:ON-quest-The Weight of Three Crowns 02.jpg|thumb|right|Three alliance leaders meet on Stirk]]
While the Three Banners War raged, the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] was also taking place across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] sought to bring the three alliances together and mount an assault on [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] in order to end the threat of the Planemeld. The war in Cyrodiil had been long and bloody, and all three alliances were spread thinly, unable to commit resources to lead an assault without making themselves vulnerable.{{ref|name=ONVanus|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

In order to broker a peace treaty, Vanus and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] first contacted the spy networks of each alliance and arranged meetings with each leader. The Vestige acted as the perfect diplomat given their unique position and reputation, and convinced each of the leaders to meet at a neutral, undisputed location to discuss a ceasefire.{{ref|name=ONMOT|[[Online:Messages Across Tamriel|Messages Across Tamriel]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONVanus}}

The alliance leaders met on the island of [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]] and were about to begin negotiations when [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] interupted the conclave. He sent his hordes of Daedra to kill the three alliance leaders in one fell swoop, but the leaders fought back and defeated the Daedra. The alliance leaders realized the true threat was the Planemeld and committed a number of their troops to the assault on Coldharbour. The assault was a success and the Vestige helped to end the Planemeld, but as a result the ceasefire had served its purpose and hostilities between the alliances resumed.{{ref|name=ONWOTC|[[Online:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Donolon|Chef Donolon]] was a legendary Breton confectioner who believed that delicious desserts, cakes in particular, have a way of bringing people together, and that was perhaps the key to ending the Three Banners War. To that end, he traveled the world by sea and converted a barge into a bakery. His most coveted creation was the Jubilee Cake, which helped propelled him to stardom.{{Ref|name=ONCD|[[ON:Chef Donolon|Chef Donolon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

There were other attempts at peace between the alliances after the Planemeld. [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]] also sought to end the war by acquiring a mythical "demon weapon" and using its existence to force all sides to cease their aggressive behavior. His plan failed when it turned out the weapon was in fact an uncontrollable rage of [[Lore:dragons|dragons]].{{ref|name=ONAbnurTharn}} 

The [[Lore:Society of the Steadfast|Society of the Steadfast]] was a philanthropic organization that provided aid to those displaced or wounded by the war in Cyrodiil, among other charitable works.{{ref|name=ONJakarn|[[Online:Jakarn|Jakarn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During the war they sponsored peace talks with the alliance leaders on [[Lore:High Isle|High Isle]], but they were disrupted by the [[Lore:Ascendant Order|Ascendant Order]]. Led by the [[Lore:Ascendant Lord|Ascendant Lord]], this terrorist organization became pan-Tamrielic thanks to its mass appeal to common folk, who were recruited through its promise that it would bring down Tamriel's tyrants who grow richer and powerful at the expense of misery and bloodshed.{{ref|name=MTCALM|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character(s) - The Ascendant Lord and Magus}}}}{{ref|name=ONTAP|{{Cite Book|The Ascendant Proclamation}}}} Ultimately, the Ascendant Order threat was neutralized and the peace talks went ahead, but their outcome remains unknown.{{ref|name=ONDavaux|[[Online:Lady Arabelle Davaux|Lady Arabelle Davaux]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONPeace|[[Online:And Now, Perhaps, Peace|And Now, Perhaps, Peace]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
