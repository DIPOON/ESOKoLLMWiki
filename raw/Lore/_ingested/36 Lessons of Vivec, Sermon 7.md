As the caravan of [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] now made for the capital of [[Lore:Resdayn|Veloth]], anon [[Lore:Almalexia (city)|Almalexia]], there came great rumblings from the [[Lore:Oblivion|oblivion]]. A duke among {{Lore Link|scamp}}s wandered into the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]], pausing before each scripture door to pay his respects, until finally he was met by the major domo of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]].

The Duke of Scamps said, {{nst|ON="|'}}I was summoned by Lord Dagon, master of the foul waters and fire, and I have brought the pennants of my seven legions.{{nst|ON="|'}}

The major domo, whose head was a bubble of foul water and fire, bowed low, so that the head of the Duke of Scamps became enclosed in his own.

He saw the first pennant, which commanded a legion of {{Lore Link |36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9|grim warriors}} who could die at least twice.

He saw the second pennant, which commanded a legion of [[Lore:Morihaus|winged bulls]] and the [[Lore:Empire|emperor]] of color that rode upon each.

He saw the third pennant, which commanded a legion of inverted [[Lore:Gorgon|gorgons]], [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|great snakes]] whose scales were the faces of men.

He saw the fourth pennant, which commanded a legion of double-crossed lovers.

He saw the fifth pennant, which commanded a legion of jumping wounds looking to hop onto a victim.

He saw the sixth pennant, which commanded a legion of abridged [[Lore:Aedra|planets]].

He saw the seventh pennant, which commanded a legion of armored winning moves.

To which the major domo said, {{nst|ON="|'}}[[Lore:Kh-Utta|Duke Kh-Utta]], your legions while mighty are not enough to destroy Nerevar or the Triune way. Look upon the Hortator and see the wisdom he takes to wife.{{nst|ON="|'}}

And they looked into the middle world and saw:

: Evaporating in a throng of thunder
: Of red war and chitin men, 
: Where destines
: Take him further from our ways
: The heat that we have wanted
: And pray they still remember,
: Where destines
: Clothe the distance, 
: Glad in the golden east that we saw it now,
: Instead of the war and repair
: Of the oblivious fracture
: A curse on the Hortator
: And two more on his hands

And the Duke of Scamps saw the palms of the Hortator, upon which the egg had written these words of power: GHARTOK PADHOME GHARTOK PADHOME.

The ending of the words is [[Lore:Almsivi|ALMSIVI]].
