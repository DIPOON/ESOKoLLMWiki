[[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] is a major city in the center of the [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]] region of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], at the mouth of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]]. It serves as the capital of a [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|kingdom]] that forms one of High Rock's provinces.

The city is split into seven districts. The Residential District lies in the northern part of the city. The Merchant District and Market Square lie west of the river and south of the Residential District. The Temple and Palace Districts are off to the west, while the Banking District can be found in the east. To the south, on the banks of Iliac Bay, sit Wayrest Docks.<noinclude>

==History==
===First Era===
Wayrest has always been seen as a rival to [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], which was already well established when it first was founded as a sleepy fishing village at the mouth of the Bjoulsae River in {{Year|1E 800}}. Wayrest prospered greatly after the Fall of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] in {{Year|1E 980}} when the commerce of all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] began to pass through its gates. Wayrest was granted the right to call itself a kingdom in {{Year|1E 1100}}. Today the city can boast the largest and richest population in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. During its early years, the kingdom was ruled by the Gardner Dynasty.{{ref|name=WJotB}}

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 541}}, the city was besieged by a horde of [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], led by [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach the Black Drake]], for fifty-seven days. After the Reachmen were defeated, Wayrest, along with Daggerfall, [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]], [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] and [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], signed the first [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]].{{Ref|name=TaToaM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} During the [[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]] in {{Year|2E 566}} - {{Year|2E 567}}, Wayrest was besieged again, this time by the forces of King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]] of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]]. His army stood firm, but after the other kingdoms of the Covenant and the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] sailors from [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] came to Wayrest's aid, Ranser and his troops were forced to retreat back to Shornhelm.{{ref|name=TFoKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}}

Thanks to the efforts of merchants from Wayrest's trade hub capitalizing on the holiday through consumer goods, [[Lore:Saturalia|Saturalia]] influenced [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]] traditions. Widespread adoption of Saturalia-themed trees, decorations, and more across Tamriel for usage in the New Life Festival make for a booming market during this time of year, touching even Tamrielans that grew up with their own New Life traditions.{{Ref|name=LATH|[[Lore:Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays|Loremaster's Archive—Tamriel's Holidays]]}} This convergence of these Breton-influenced aesthetics and practices were started during the time of the Daggerfall Covenant,{{Ref|name=LATH}} and has carried onto the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|name=SASL|[[Blades:Grand Saturalia Tree|Grand Saturalia Tree Decoration]]  in ''[[BL:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|[[Blades:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]] event having Saturalia decorations in ''[[BL:Blades|Blades]]''}}

===Third Era===
[[File:DF-place-Wayrest.png|thumb|right|Wayrest circa {{Year|3E 405}}]]
Emperor [[Lore:Cassynder|Cassynder]] and his half-brother [[Lore:Uriel IV|Uriel IV]] were kings of Wayrest before they took the throne of the Empire.{{ref|name=BHotE|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire}}}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Wayrest was an active settlement, being ruled by King Tristore. It had a rivalry with [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]].{{Ref|name=Arena|[[Arena:Wayrest|Wayrest]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} It was neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Kings Guard|Kings Guard]] to the far northwest, {{Lore Link|Vermeir Wastes}} to the northeast, and Hammerfell to the south, towards [[Lore:Thorstad Place|Thorstad Place]].{{Ref|name=MapArena|[[:File:AR-map-High Rock towns.png|Map of High Rock – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}{{Ref|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

During the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], it was ruled by King [[Lore:Eadwyre|Eadwyre]].{{ref|name=DF|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} After Eadwyre died, his daughter [[Lore:Elysana|Elysana]] and stepson [[Lore:Helseth|Helseth]] briefly fought over the throne, but eventually Elysana won and banished Helseth and his mother, [[Lore:Barenziah|Barenziah]]. {{ref|name=AGAD|{{Cite Book|A Game at Dinner}}}}

===Fourth Era===
In {{Year|4E 188}} the city of Wayrest was sieged by corsairs. A [[Lore:Forgotten Hero|spy]] who was infiltrating the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] was given the task of assassinating King Barynia. Fearing for his life and knowing about the conspiracy against him, he made a deal with the pirates and would betray his own kingdom by leaving the city gates open for them, so they could come in and destroy the conspirators who plotted to assassinate him. The Forgotten Hero would aid the city defenders in driving off the corsairs from the city gates. It's unknown if the Forgotten Hero spared the life of King Barynia and allowed him to sail away with the pirates, or if the hero went through with the contract and ended the king's life.{{ref|name=CG|Events of [[ Legends:City Gates (quest)|City Gates (quest)]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=CWTR|Events of [[Legends:Castle Wayrest, Throne Room|Castle Wayrest, Throne Room]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

==Notes==
*''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Wayrest's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Highlanders".{{Ref|group=UOL|[http://www.imperial-library.info/content/go-blades Original TES: Arena texts at the Imperial Library]}} This was finally used in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]'', where the Wayrest Highlanders arena team competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.{{Ref|[[Castles:Gladiatorial Glory|Gladiatorial Glory]] event in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}
