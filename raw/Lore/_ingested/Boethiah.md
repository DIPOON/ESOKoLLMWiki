Boethiah's destructiveness comes from inspiring the arms of mortal warriors.{{Ref|name=OO|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} The Dark Warrior has been described as "cold" and "impersonal",{{ref|name=IOA}} "cruel", and one of the more "demonic" of the Daedra lords.{{Ref|name=OO}} Worshippers are known to hold bloody competitions in Boethiah's honor, battling—even killing—each other, for Boethiah is said to care only for their blood.{{ref|name=IOA}} In Morrowind, the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] known as the [[Lore:Hunger|Hunger]], foul creatures that are eternally bone thin, are associated with the Prince.{{Ref|name=DD}} 

Boethiah sees battle as a blessing,{{Ref|name=BG|{{Cite Book|Boethiah's Glory}}}} and is frequently depicted as a great caped warrior, often in a stoic pose.{{ref|name=Statues|Appearance of Boethiah's statues in [[Morrowind:Khartag Point|''Morrowind'']], [[Oblivion:Boethia|''Oblivion'']], [[Skyrim:Sacellum of Boethiah|''Skyrim'']], and [[Online:Knife Ear Grotto|ESO]]}} While sometimes described as male,{{ref|name=MWAppearance|[[Morrowind:Boethiah|Boethiah]]'s appearance in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}}{{ref|name=OBAppearance|[[Oblivion:Boethia|Boethia]]'s appearance in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} Boethiah is also commonly referred to as female,{{Ref|name=IOA}}{{ref|name=DFAppearance|[[Daggerfall:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah]]'s appearance in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}{{ref|name=SRAppearance|[[Skyrim:Boethiah's Calling|Boethiah]]'s appearance in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} sometimes within the same text.{{Ref|name=VOFDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=GU|{{Cite Book|Glorious Upheaval}}}} Notably, Boethiah is usually depicted as male in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],{{ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=MWShrine|Boethiah's voice and the appearance of their shrine during [[MW:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah's Quest]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} but is also known for taking the form of a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] "warrior-queen".{{Ref|name=BP}}{{ref|name=DFAppearance}}

She rules from the realm of [[Lore:Attribution's Share|Attribution's Share]].{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]] Dawnguard Rulebook}} Boethiah often manifests using tricky, unpredictable avatars, which can appear as a male or female of any race. These avatars become involved with important or momentous events, working behind the scenes to achieve an outcome that serves Boethiah's will. They will often challenge mortals, seeking to test the mettle of heroes and commoners alike. After a trial ends, the avatar is likely to vanish, leading to strange and mysterious stories and legends among the participants.{{ref|name=BAHA|{{Cite Book|Boethiah and Her Avatars}}}}

Despite the dark undertones of Boethiah's sphere and influence on Tamriel, the Prince was considered in Morrowind to be one of the three "[[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]]" and the Anticipation of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] by the old [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]],{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}} and one of the Reclamations by the New Temple.{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} The Prince of Plots has a scion, the [[Lore:Demiprince|demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]].{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} Despite this, the information broker Madam Whim believes that the demiprince did not actually originate from Boethiah, while also stating that "Boethiah supports their claim of scionship". In the same interview, Whim would describe how Daedric Princes have been known to adopt scions in the past.{{Ref|name=MAM|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} In the ''[[Lore:Five Songs of King Wulfharth|Five Songs of King Wulfharth]]'', [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] states that [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] is the son of Boethiah, one of the strongest Padomaics,{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} but it is unclear how literal the statement was. ==Worship==
===Chimer and Dunmer===
[[File:MW-item-Daedric Face of Inspiration.jpg|left|thumb|200px|"The Face of the Inspiring Fountain" - Dunmer mask aspect of Boethiah]][[File:MW-place-Shrine to Boethiah.jpg|thumb|right|A Third Era statue of Boethiah in Morrowind]]
Known as the '''God-King of the Chimer''',{{ref|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-Daedric Symbols.jpg|Morrowind Concept art]]}} Boethiah was championed by the Prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]], and is considered by the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dark Elves]] to be their original god-ancestor.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=VOFDE}}  It was Boethiah (along with [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]]) who told them [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|the truth of Lorkhan's test]], and who defeated [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]]'s champion, [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]].{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TCO}} These and other "illuminations" convinced the eventual [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], or Changed Folk, to renounce their ties to the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] and found the nation of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]] based on Daedric principles.{{Ref|name=TCO}}{{Ref|name=VOFDE}} Numerous Dark Elven cultural "advances" are attributed to Boethiah, including philosophy, magic, and [[Lore:Responsible Architecture|"responsible" architecture]]. Ancient Velothi tales, allegories of Chimeri struggles, tell of Boethiah triumphing over enemies of every type.{{Ref|name=VOFDE}}

During the time of the [[Lore:Almsivi|Almsivi]], Boethiah was recognized by the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] as the Anticipation of Almalexia, but male to her female.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=MWAppearance}} With the advent of the New Temple following the disappearance of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]], worship of Boethiah was once again elevated to its old status among the Dunmer. The Prince is now revered, alongside [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], as one of the Reclamations.{{ref|name=TR}}

Boethiah was a god worshipped by the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]] and, after the New Temple was established after the devastation of the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], by the Dunmer people in general.{{ref|name=TR}}{{Ref|name=EO|[[Skyrim:Elder Othreloth|Elder Othreloth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===Khajiit===
[[File:ON-statue-Shrine of Boethra.jpg|thumb|right|Shrine of Boethra (ESO)]]
'''Boethra''', the '''Warrior of the East and West''',{{ref|name=TWS}} is a sharp-tongued{{ref|name=ESOMazza-Mirri|[[Online:Mazza-Mirri|Mazza-Mirri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] deity, an ancestor spirit and a teacher of the old ways,{{ref|name=ESOShando-ri|[[Online:Shando-ri|Shando-ri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} and the patron of warriors and rebellious exiles.{{ref|name=ESOSB|[[Online:Shrine of Boethra|Shrine of Boethra]] furnishing from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} It is unknown if she remained in the Khajiiti Pantheon after the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar]] cult scrubbed and reformed many aspects of Khajiiti religion, but she is not mentioned in the ''[[Lore:Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi]]''. Much of what is known about her comes from myths that predate the Riddle'Thar Epiphany. Ancient Khajiit did not see it necessary to pray to Boethra, honoring her instead by "walking the Path, and only hiding in order to pounce". Additionally, they would not speak her name on nights of the [[Lore:Ghost Moon|Ghost Moon]], nights in which it is said Boethra wears the death-shroud of [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]] and "wages war beyond the [[Lore:Lunar Lattice|Lattice]]". She is the mate of [[Lore:Mafala|Mafala]].{{ref|name=TWS}}

[[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]] once exiled Boethra due to her rebellious nature. During this time, Boethra walked the Many Paths, eventually returning. It is said Mafala did not forget her love for Boethra during her time away.{{ref|name=TWS}} The ancient songs tell that the demon [[Lore:Orkha|Orkha]] followed her back from the Many Paths, before being banished by the combined might of Boethra, Lorkhaj, and [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]].{{ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}} For this reason, she is known as '''Orkha-Bane'''.{{ref|name=ESOSBO|[[Online:ESO Plus Exclusive Free Items#Statuette: Boethra, Orkha-Bane|Statuette: Boethra, Orkha-Bane]] furnishing from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Boethra fought the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Demon King]] [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molagh]] to a draw when he attacked the Lattice along with [[Lore:Merrunz|Dagon]] and [[Lore:Merid-Nunda|Merid-Nunda]]. Boethra's actions allowed [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] to shackle Molagh.{{ref|name=TAS}} She also fought [[Lore:Noctra|Noctra]] until "it knew it was not [[Lore:Namiira|Namiira]]".{{ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}} Fighting alongside Lorkhaj, it was Boethra who pried out the first of [[Lore:Magrus|Magrus]]' eyes.{{ref|name=TWS}}{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}

The songs also sing of an unnamed spirit of vengeance with no will of its own, born of Azurah's grief after the deaths of [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]] and Lorkhaj. This spirit may appear in songs as a black panther, a warrior in ebony armor, or as a hidden sword. It is said only Azurah, Boethra, and Mafala can summon this spirit, as only they know its true name.{{ref|name=TDS}}

Early shrines to Boethra depict her wielding a Khajiiti styled katana alongside an unknown figure resembling a lynx. She appears distinct from any known [[Lore:Furstock|furstock]], though displays some similarity to an [[Lore:Ohmes-raht|Ohmes-raht]] Khajiit.{{ref|name=ESOSB}}

''[[Lore:The Bladesongs of Boethra|The Bladesongs of Boethra]]'', a pre-Ri'Datta text of the [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]] cult among the Khajiit, prominently features Boethra. This account claims that Boethra fought alongside Azurah in defending the Lattice. Myth suggests that the battle happened many times.{{ref|name=BOB|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}} This text says that Akha exiled her to the Many Paths. However, new writings after [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]]'s reforms do not mention Akha, along with Alkosh, Alkhan, and all the Children of Akha in that context. Amidst this confusion, Boethra questioned her identity as the Daughter of Blades, wondering if it had all been a long dream of someone she never knew.{{Ref|name=TBOB|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}}

===Orc===
[[File:ON-npc-Acolyte Dugikh.jpg|thumb|right|Dugikh, an Orc faithful of Boethiah, whose body acted as Prince's vessel]]

According to many accounts, the Orcish race was created when their patron god [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] was transformed by Boethiah. He became [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] and his faithful became the cursed folk, or 'Orsimer', and were henceforth known as Orcs.{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} 

The Orcs say that Trinimac was enraged by [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] and his cult trespassing on Tamriel during their exodus, and challenged his patron Boethiah to a duel. Just as Trinimac was about to succeed, a second Prince, [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], interfered and stabbed Trinimac in the back. Boethiah used the opportunity to curse Trinimac, transforming both him and his followers as well as casting him into a [[Lore:Ashpit|place of choking air and ash]]. There, Trinimac was reborn in his blood and rage and became Malacath, the God of Curses.{{ref|name=MOF|{{Cite Book|Mauloch, Orc-Father}}}} The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] account of events begins much the same, but Boethiah instead triumphs over Trinimac alone and devours him whole. He proceeds to take Trinimac's form and speak with his voice, and together with Mephala showed the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] the rules of the [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]]. Once he finished, he relieved himself of Trinimac there on the spot. That excrement became Malacath, and his followers became the Orcs.{{ref|name=tCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}}{{ref|name=VoF:TO|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Orcs}}}} A disguised Malacath, when told one of these accounts, derided the story as being too "literal-minded".{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}

According to the myth recounted in ''[[Lore:From Exile to Exodus|From Exile to Exodus]]'' by Tarvyn Aram, during the Velothi exodus, the Velothi encountered the followers of Trinimac, who outnumbered them three to one. While Trinimac remained silent, his followers called the dissidents rebels, traitors, and filth. Boethiah then intervened, revealing the truth about the followers' identity as Orsimer. Boethiah then gestured with her hands, forming a triangular sign, revealing the path to achieve an Exodus. As the attendees witnessed this, the veil was lifted from their eyes. They no longer saw Trinimac but Malak, the King of Curses. Similarly, Boethiah appeared as Trinimac, the Warrior of East and West, bearing the burden of separating divinity from her beloved. Enraged, Malak cursed and attacked Boethiah, causing her to collapse in agony, seemingly lifeless. However, Mephala and Azura intervened, aiding Boethiah in casting out Malacath and his curse. The followers were divided among the Daedric Princes, with Boethiah ultimately transporting the remaining followers to the [[Lore:Ashpit|Ashpit]] in the Oblivion. There, Boethiah confronted Malacath once more, defeating him and reshaping him into Mauloch, guiding the Orcs to find strength through adversity. With a single precise strike, Malacath was cleaved into the ashen wastes, his bones forming a new foundation. His blood mingled with the ash and soon the realm was his. Boethiah then spent time with the surviving followers, their skins changing under her teachings and the influence of the realm. When Malacath's spirit reappeared, Boethiah addressed him, urging him to seek glory through struggle and to understand the Will Against Rule. She then formed a powerful sign with her hands, encapsulating Malacath and his followers into a singular sphere, casting it back onto the mountains of Nirn. Together, Boethiah and Mauloch tested their chosen peoples, leading them toward an Exodus.{{Ref|name=FETE|{{Cite Book|From Exile to Exodus}}}}

In the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]],{{ref|name=Curate|[[ON:Curate Gadayn|Curate Gadayn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Boethiah was known to have Orcish followers. Among them, Dugikh emerged as a vessel for the Prince when Boethiah, alongside Azura, Mephala, and [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], sought to banish [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]].{{ref|name=Acolyte|[[ON:Acolyte Dugikh|Acolyte Dugikh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=POF|Events of [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
===Merethic Era===
[[File:GEN-misc-Boethiah memospore.jpg|Art of Boethiah (middle)|right|thumb]]
In the Late-Middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]],{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} Boethiah began appearing in the dreams and visions of a young prophet, Veloth. These visions guided Veloth in the creation of a new sect of Aldmeri dissidents, with a core belief being that mortals could ascend to godhood.{{ref|name=VTP|{{Cite Book|Veloth the Pilgrim}}}}{{Ref|name=TFOT|{{Cite Book|The Fall of Trinimac}}}} However, the followers of [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], the most powerful of the [[Lore:et'Ada|et'Ada]], stridently opposed this new cult.{{Ref|name=TCO}}{{Ref|name=TFOT}} His priests condemned the dissidents for blasphemy, threatening them with exile should they remain devoted to the teachings of Veloth.{{Ref|name=TFOT}} In response, it is said that Trinimac was tricked into going into Boethiah's mouth—or more literally, that Boethiah ate Trinimac.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TCO}}{{Ref|name=TFOT}}

As the priests prepared to pass judgment on the Veloth and his cult, Boethiah appeared before them in the guise and with the voice of Trinimac.{{Ref|name=TFOT}} He (Boethiah) gathered a crowd and began to tell them of the "lies" of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], and in particular of Trinimac; he spoke of the "Tri-Angled Truth", how to create the future Chimer culture, and with [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], the rules of [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]].{{Ref|name=TCO}} When he was sure the priests were shamed and broken, and as proof of the truth of his words, Boethiah relieved himself of Trinimac in front of the crowd.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TCO}}{{Ref|name=TFOT}} The followers of both the Aedra and the Daedra then "rubbed the soil of Trinimac upon themselves and changed their skins".{{Ref|name=TA}} This reportedly made it easy for Boethiah's new people to become the "[[Lore:Chimer|Changed Ones]]".{{Ref|name=TCO}}

While the exact details of Boethiah's victory over Trinimac are unclear, it is said that after being swallowed, she (Boethiah) "tortured his spirit in her belly". Eventually, Boethiah grew bored and released Trinimac, later exiling him to a [[Lore:Ashpit|plane of ash]]. Trinimac became enraged and twisted after this torture and dishonor; he faded away and was reborn as [[Lore:Mauloch|Mauloch]]. The former Aedra's most devoted followers changed with him, becoming the [[Lore:Orc|Orsimer]].{{Ref|name=TFOT}}{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} However, according to Orcish accounts of the battle, Trinimac was about to deal a terrific blow, when Mephala appeared and stabbed him in the back. As Trinimac was reeling, Boethiah cast a "terrible ritual" to scar and twist his appearance before banishing Trinimac to "a place of choking air and ash", where accounts agree that he became [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]].{{ref|name=MOF|{{Cite Book|Mauloch, Orc-Father}}}} Other sources also claim that ''all'' of Trinimac's followers were changed into the Orsimer.{{ref|name=VOFTO|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Orcs}}}}

Towards the end of Veloth's exodus, they were approached by an aspect of Boethiah posing as a woman at [[Lore:Dunmeth Pass|Dunmeth Pass]] who proclaimed herself to be "Chimer-Friend", and had him swear an oath that would make him and his followers into better Mer. Veloth proclaimed on what would later become known as [[Lore:Veloth's Judgement|Veloth's Judgement]], that he would never again use it nor any other tool to slay a foe, and that he would give his heart to his people and dedicate his life and soul to them. The Chimer-Friend then melted the ice blocking the pass and accompanied them into Morrowind.{{Ref|name=ESOEJ|{{Cite Book|End of the Journey}}}}{{intnote|nb2|[nb 2]}}

===First Era===
Around {{Year|1E 1000}}, a tract by King [[Lore:Hale the Pious|Hale the Pious]] of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] compared the schemes of his political enemies to those of several Daedric Princes, including Boethiah: ''"the wickedness of the demons of Oblivion... their depravity equals that of [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] itself, they are cruel as Boethiah, calculating as [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], and mad as [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]."'' It is the earliest known written record of Boethiah (as well as the other three Princes).{{Ref|name=OO}} One of her artifacts, [[Lore:Fearstruck|Fearstruck]], was supposedly destroyed by a [[Lore:Dragon|Wyrm]]'s fire in the First Era.{{Ref|name=TSoL|{{Cite Book|The Story of Lyrisius}}}}

In response to the destruction of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] at the hands of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] sought out eight of the most influential Daedric Princes. Boethiah was among them, and between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in {{Year|1E 2920}}, a deal was made which would be known as the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]].{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} Little is known about the Compact, but it is theorized that it prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. It is also speculated that, in order to secure the agreement of the Princes, Sotha Sil either made a credible threat to them or presented a significant offer in exchange.{{ref|name=TCC}}{{ref|name=CWCSothaDialogue|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-The Champion's Arena.jpg|thumb|righ|Boethiah's statue in [[Lore:Dragonstar Arena|Dragonstar Arena]]]][[File:ON-interior-Knife Ear Grotto 02.png|Imperial statue of Boethiah|left|thumb]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, Boethiah manifested into an animus geode within [[Lore:Abamath|Abamath]] to help keep the Exile of Mauloch sealed away.{{Ref|name=Arithiel|Events of [[Online:Arithiel (quest)|Arithiel]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Boethiah had also had a hand in the formation of the Maulborn, manipulating Urili Vox into stealing the [[Lore:Veloth#Veloth's Judgment|Judgement of Veloth]] to avenge her son. A mysterious Chimeri spirit by the name of Aspera also appeared around this time period, seeking to help [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]] in acquiring the power of Saint Veloth and stopping Magistrate Vox. Aspera would show up once again in Eastmarch in the form of Aspera Giant-Friend, a mysterious Dunmer who could commune with giants. She was instrumental in the Ebonheart Pact's defeat of Filgor Orcthane's army of Orsimer by helping forge an alliance with said giants to overtake [[Lore:Cradlecrush|Cradlecrush]], the ritual arena of Malacath. When asked about who her master was, she refused to answer, simply stating that "some grudges are ancient and some answers are beyond your understanding."{{ref|name=ONAspera|Events of [[Online:Strange Allies|Strange Allies]] and [[Online:The Siege of Cradlecrush|The Siege of Cradlecrush]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Later that same year, Boethiah held a tournament at the [[Lore:Dragonstar Arena|Dragonstar Arena]] in the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]]. Participants from across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] came to try and defeat her champion, Hiath the Battlemaster. Here, Aspera appeared once again as an observer of the event, but this time as a living Dunmer named Aspera Arena-Friend. She references her time as a Chimeri spirit and as a friend to giants, and heavily implies that she has been an aspect of Boethiah. This would mean that the Prince was responsible both for the rise of Magistrate Vox and Vox's defeat by the Tribunal.{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Motive for Heresy|Motive for Heresy]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
[[File:OB-place-Boethia's Realm Statue.jpg|thumb|right|Giant statue of Boethia overlooking the Tournament of Ten Bloods]]
Around {{Year|3E 405}}, Boethiah offered "power" to an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]] in exchange for slaying a spellsword in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region. In the Prince's own words, the target of her wrath was a "perfectly charming and charitable ass" who had been "annoying the hell out [her] for years." Once the deed was done, Boethia rewarded the agent with the [[Lore:Ebony Mail|Ebony Mail]].{{ref|name=DFBoethiah|[[Daggerfall:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

In {{Year|3E 427}}, Boethiah commanded the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] to help restore his ruined shrine off the coast of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. After the Nerevarine found a sculptor to restore his statue to its former glory, Boethiah granted the legendary sword [[Lore:Goldbrand|Goldbrand]] to the hero.{{ref|name=MWBoethiah|[[Morrowind:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah's quest]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}}

Boethiah once again rewarded a hero with Goldbrand in {{Year|3E 433}}. This time, the recipient was the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]], who traveled to one of the Prince's [[Lore:Realm of Boethia|realms]] in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] to participate in the Tournament of Ten Bloods. After the other nine challengers were slain, Boethiah declared the Hero to be his "Chosen One".{{ref|name=OBBoethia|[[Oblivion:Boethia|Boethia's quest]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:LG-quest-Hopes Dashed 02.jpg|thumb|right|Cultists of Boethiah (Legends)]][[File:SR-interior-Temple 05.jpg|Dunmer shrine to Boethiah|left|thumb]]
[[Lore:Naarifin|Naarifin]] was said to have been a Boethiah worshipper who intended to bring about a prophecy known as 'The Culling', the implementation of which involved mass Daedric summoning. A large Boethiah cult operating under his orders was active during the Great War, with the Dremora blademaster {{Lore Link|Reive}} having been granted Goldbrand by Boethiah and working with the cult. Naarifin was defeated in battle and hanged from the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] for thirty-three days, with one source claiming he was carried away by a winged [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] on the thirty-fourth.{{ref|name=ESL|[[Legends:Story|Story]] of ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Boethiah called upon the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] to become her new champion. True to form, this involved slaying the ''old'' champion. Once the deed was done, Boethiah bestowed the Ebony Mail upon the Dragonborn.{{ref|name=SRBoethiah|[[Skyrim:Boethiah's Calling|Boethiah's Calling]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
