[[Lore:Azura's Star|Azura's Star]] (also called the '''Twilight Star'''){{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Survival Guide to Tamriel|ns_base=Books}}}} is a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] created by the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], and is prominently used as her symbol.{{ref|{{Item Link|quality=5|Banner of Azura|id=126118}} in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} In appearance it looks like a large, intricately designed throwing star or gemstone, with eight star-like prongs. In the right hands, Azura's Star acts as a reusable [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gem]] of almost unlimited capacity.{{ref|name=CKL|{{Cite Book|Charwich-Koniinge Letters}}}} It is considered to possess legendary magical powers.{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} This makes it highly sought by mages and assassins. It can only capture white [[Lore:Souls|souls]], but was once corrupted by mortals to trap black souls instead, becoming the '''Black Star'''.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:The Black Star|The Black Star]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Using Azura's Star empties it, freeing the trapped soul to make its journey to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=DAG|[[Daggerfall:Azura's Star|Azura's Star]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Some believe Azura's Star can turn living creatures into statues.{{ref|name=Daggerfall|[[Daggerfall:Azura's Quest|Azura's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}<noinclude>

Wielders of Azura's Star are known to her faithful as Star-Bearer.{{ref|name=Oblivion|[[Oblivion:Azura|Azura]]'s quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==History==
[[File:SR-interior-Azura's Star Realm.jpg|Inside Azura's Star|thumb|right]]
An [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] received the Star from the previous owner, a worshipper of Azura, in return for killing a priest in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] area who had insulted the Prince by condemning her sphere of egotism. {{ref|name=Daggerfall}} Between {{Year|3E 411}} and {{Year|3E 412}}, two friends named Charwich and Koniinge searched for the Star.{{ref|name=CKL}} It was discovered that a man named Hadwaf Neithwyr had summoned Azura in the town of [[Lore:Tel Aruhn|Tel Aruhn]] in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] and had accepted the Star. He then returned to [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], where he and his sister Peryra attempted to kill the [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropic]] caretaker of their family graveyard to power the Star. They failed, and the caretaker hid their remains in one of the crypts. Charwich found the Star, and tricked Koniinge into thinking he was dead by sending misleading letters.{{ref|name=CKL}} Charwich and his fiancée Lady Elysbetta Moorling ran away with the Star, using it to become wealthy and powerful. Eventually the Star vanished, and Koniinge caught up with both of them and killed them. The letters between the two have been published in the ''[[Lore:Charwich-Koniinge Letters|Charwich-Koniinge Letters]]'' series of books.{{ref|name=CKL}}

The Star was returned to the mortal realm when the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] did a service for Azura in {{Year|3E 427}}. She and [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] had a wager that Azura's priestess, Rayna Drolan, could live in silence for one hundred years. The time was almost up, so Sheogorath sent his [[Lore:Golden Saint|Golden Saint]] champion [[Lore:Staada|Staada]] to lead his servants to disturb the priestess on her secluded island, located in the [[Lore:Sheogorad|Sheogorad]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. The Nerevarine banished the Mad God's Daedric servants, and was given the Star in return.{{ref|name=Morrowind|[[Morrowind:Azura's Quest|Azura's Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

In {{Year|3E 433}} the star was again given as a reward, to an adventurer who visited Azura's shrine in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Azura sent the adventurer to cleanse a nest of vampires who had once been her worshippers. Their eternal suffering pained her, so she unsealed their prison in order for the adventurer to put them to rest.{{ref|name=Oblivion}} In the same year, the Star was needed by [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] to open a portal to the realm of [[Lore:Gaiar Alata|Gaiar Alata]], [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran's]] Paradise. The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] was sent to recover the Star from the cave where it was held, and took it from the shrine's pedestal found within.{{ref|name=OBMobile|Events of ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}} The Star was consumed in Martin's ritual, and was not seen again for many years.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}  Some say another artifact was used for this purpose instead.

Some time in the second century of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], one Malyn Varen, a teacher at the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] with a scholarly interest in soul gems, came into possession of Azura's Star and began to study it along with a few colleagues and students. Unknown to the others, Varen was dying and was trying to find a way to trap his own soul in the Star, becoming immortal. This research drove him insane, partly due to the visions sent to torment him by Azura, until one day he killed a student and used her soul in his experimentation. He was banished from the College, after which he and a few loyal disciples moved to [[Lore:Ilinalta's Deep|Ilinalta's Deep]], an abandoned Imperial fort which sunk into {{Lore Link|Lake Ilinalta}}. This band of rogue necromancers continued their experimentation, and Varen eventually succeeded in corrupting Azura's artifact to accept black souls, renaming it as the Black Star. Varen eventually died, but his soul remained, trapped in the Star.{{ref|name=Skyrim}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] was summoned to the Shrine of Azura south of [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]], built by the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] refugees who fled from [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. {{Lore Link|Aranea Ienith}}, Azura's priestess, tasked the Dragonborn with finding her lost artifact. Azura's message led the Dragonborn to Nelacar, a mage living in Winterhold who was once a member of the college there. He knew of Varen and directed the Dragonborn to Ilinalta's Deep. There, the Dragonborn found the broken Black Star and was captured by it. The interior of the Star consisted of crystalline paths suspended in a void and was home to Varen and his [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] minions. The Dragonborn destroyed Varen's soul and had to decide whether to give the Star to Ienith, who would have Azura restore the artifact, or bring it to Nelacar, who offered to complete Varen's work and permanently corrupt the artifact, leaving it as the Black Star.{{ref|name=Skyrim}} It is unknown which option the Dragonborn chose.
