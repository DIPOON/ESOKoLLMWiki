[[Lore:Wolf|Wolves]] are aggressive canines found throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. They are pack hunters and can often be found in packs of two or three, using their highly developed sense of smell to track their prey. When threatened, they may howl and thus draw in other members of their pack. When attacking, they will often attempt to circle around behind their prey to bite.

Wolves are related to domesticated [[Lore:Dog|dog]]s, and the two can be bred to produce [[Lore:Wolfdog|wolfdogs]].{{ref|[[OB:Hjalti|Hjalti]] in [[OB:Fighter's Stronghold|Fighter's Stronghold]] download for ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] tradition, the Goddess of Love, [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] is associated with the wolf. Often times she is also known as the "Mother Wolf" or the "She-Wolf".{{Ref|name=TSOG|{{Cite Book|The Song of Gods}}}}{{Ref|name=DATN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}} Similar connection can be found among the [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]].{{Ref|name=MaraWolfMarkarth|[[ON:Mara (wolf)|Mara]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} A wolf is one of the many animals carved into puzzles on the dungeons of Skyrim.{{ref|name=SRPuzzleDoor|[[Skyrim:Puzzle Door|Puzzle doors]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] see wolves as one of the most blessed of the creations of the [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]], for their speed, agility and skills as hunter.{{ref|name=BMSkaal|[[Bloodmoon:Skaal|Skaal]] dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} One of [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]]'s Sacred Trials is to slay the {{Lore Link|Wolf Guardian Spirit}}, one of the seven {{Lore Link|Guardian Beasts}} of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|name=KST|[[Skyrim:Kyne's Sacred Trials|Kyne's Sacred Trials]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRFroki|[[Skyrim:Froki Whetted-Blade|Froki Whetted-Blade]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]]'s aspect of speed takes the form of a wolf.

Wolves are known to carry infectious diseases and in some places, like [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], they are called [[Lore:Plague Wolf|plague wolves]]. The wolves from Cyrodiil may carry helljoint, the ones in Skyrim sometimes carry rockjoint and the wolves from Solstheim can carry yellow tick.  

Wolves are hunted for their pelts, which can be tanned into leather. They are also occasionally domesticated and kept as companions and mounts.{{ref|name=ESOPet|ESO [[Online:Non-Combat Pets|pet]] descriptions/Crown Store blurbs}}{{ref|name=ESOMount|ESO [[Online:Mounts/Canines|mount]] descriptions/Crown Store blurbs}} [[Lore:Dire Wolf|Dire Wolves]] are a related but separate species of canine.{{ref|name=GDW|{{Cite Book|Guide to Taming Dire Wolves}}}}

Many people and factions have adopted the wolf as their coat of arms or symbol. The cities of [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]]{{Ref|name=ONKvatchBanner|[[:File:ON-banner-Kvatch.png|Banner of Kvatch]] – The Elder Scrolls Online: Dark Brotherhood}} and [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] use a wolf's head as their heraldry. For the latter, it represents the spirit of its people.{{Ref|name=OTTF|{{Cite Book|Ode to the Founding}}}} For the former, it was coming from Count [[Lore:Carolus Aquilarios|Carolus Aquilarios]], who was known as the Wolf of Kvatch for his bravery and inherited the wolf's head as his symbol in the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|name=TWATD|{{Cite Book|The Wolf and the Dragon}}}} Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede I|Titus Mede I]]'s personal symbol was the wolf's head and anyone branded with it was part of his innermost circle.{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|22}} Arguably most famously, the Queen of [[Lore:Solitude (kingdom)|Solitude]], [[Lore:Potema|Potema]] was known as the "Wolf-Queen" because in her youth, she invoked a resemblance to the wolf, and not because of her looks.{{Ref|name=TWQ2|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v2}}}}{{Ref|name=BOTWQ|{{Cite Book|Biography of the Wolf Queen}}}} Her banners depict a wolf design.{{Ref|name=SRPotemaBanner|[[:File:SR-banner-Potema.png|Potema's banner]] – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} 

==Breeds==
===Doom Wolf===
Doom Wolves are a breed of giant [[Lore:Wolf|wolf]]. They have black fur and glowing red eyes, and their howl is unnaturally distorted. Doom Wolves are used as mounts and were bred for size, savagery, and stealth from the fierce [[Lore:Wolf#Timber Wolf|timber wolves]] of the southern [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]]. They were adopted by the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] for use as stealthy mounts, with the breed's glowing red eyes weighing heavily in the decision.{{ref|name=ESOMount}}

Not to be confused with [[Lore:Death Hound|Death Hound]]s and [[Lore:Hell Hound|Hell Hound]]s.

===Gorne Striped Wolf===
'''Gorne Striped Wolves''' (or '''Gorne Wolves''') are wolves found on the Isle of [[Lore:Gorne|Gorne]], off the east coast of Morrowind, where they terrorized the island's settlers until they were suppressed in the {{Lore Link|Troubled House Hunt}}.{{ref|name=ESOMount}} When Gorne was invaded by legions of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] from the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}, many of the wolves were magically tamed by the Daedra for use as war beasts. In particular, a male [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]] named Solemn tamed a significant number.{{ref|[[Online:Gorne Wolf|Gorne Wolf]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Solemn|Solemn]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
*Some [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwarven]] [[Lore:Vamidium|vamidiums]] were constructed in the shape of wolves.
*Conjurers are able to compel [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame]] and [[Lore:Storm Atronach|Storm Atronachs]] to adopt the form of wolves.
*A mushroom known as the [[Lore:Flora_C#Canis_Cup_Mushroom|Canis Cup]] is known to prevent wolves from attacking those who eat it for a short time. Upon consumption, it disguises its user scent by replacing it with one described by [[Online:Thane Oda Wolf-Sister|Thane Oda Wolf-Sister]] as "[...]that wonderful musky scent of fur and dirt and grass and old meat."
