# Lore:All-Maker

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Gods Trail}}[[File:BM-place-Sun Stone.jpg|thumb|right|A Stone of the All-Maker]][[File:ON-npc-Gellhidil.jpg|thumb|right|A [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] priest of All-Maker]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]], also known as the '''All-Father''',{{ref|name=TSOASS|{{Cite Book|The Story of Aevar Stone-Singer}}}} is a mysterious deity revered as the wellspring of creation. The All-Maker could be a deity from the Atmorans or branched from the animal deities of the Atmorans. Either way they share similarities with each other and derive from the ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s, reverence for the All-Maker is now nearly extinct. However, the isolated [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] preserved the ancient beliefs throughout the ages, and modern scholars have learned of the All-Maker through their eyes.{{ref|name=COTA|{{Cite Book|Children of the All-Maker}}}} At least some of the Skaal were known to spread the faith of All-Maker outside of Solstheim, attempting to convert their mainland kin.{{ref|name=Gel|[[ON:Gellhidil|Gellhidil]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

All life flows from the All-Maker like a great river, and, in time, this river flows back to its source.{{ref|name=DBStorn|[[Skyrim:Storn Crag-Strider|Storn Crag-Strider]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} It is believed that the All-Maker dwells in the world of the spirit.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Tharsten Heart-Fang|Tharsten Heart-Fang]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} The spirits of dead creatures return to the All-Maker, who shapes new life and returns it to [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]]. Death, then, is simply the beginning of the next stage of this endless journey.{{ref|name=COTA}} However, each life is a gift given in the great wisdom of the All-Maker, thus each one is very precious and sacred. Even the snow is viewed as a blessing for the shelter it can offer and for allowing hunters to track prey.{{ref|name=BMSkaal|Dialogue of Skaal villagers in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} The All-Maker favors those who hone their skills, and disfavors the greedy and lazy.{{ref|name=DBWulf|[[Skyrim:Wulf Wild-Blood|Wulf Wild-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}{{ref|name=TSOASS}} The six [[Skyrim:All-Maker Stones|All-Maker Stones]] of Solstheim are viewed as representations of the six aspects of nature, the six great gifts of the All-Maker&mdash;flora, fauna, the sun, the earth, water, and wind. The stones are believed to be conduits by which the All-Maker's power flows into the world.{{ref|name=DBStorn}}{{ref|name=BMKorst|[[BM:Korst Wind-Eye|Korst Wind-Eye]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=DBFrea|[[Skyrim:Frea|Frea]]'s dialogue [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]}}<noinclude>

"Oneness" with the land is considered crucial to pleasing the All-Maker.{{ref|name=COTA}}{{ref|name=BMKorst}} The Skaal practice this concept by seeking harmony with their surroundings. For example, firewood is collected from dead trees. Hunting is for subsistence, not sport. The sanctity of life demands that violence only be used as a last resort.{{ref|name=COTA}} The Skaal draw power from the natural order (the harmony, or balance, of the land); when they are in harmony with the land, they are joined with it through the grace of the All-Maker, and when this harmony is upset, so is the All-Maker, and their power dwindles.{{ref|name=BMKorst}}{{ref|name=TSOASS}}{{ref|name=DBYrsa|[[Skyrim:Yrsa|Yrsa]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

It's believed the All-Maker cursed ancient warriors with undeath for committing acts of cannibalism, thus creating the [[Lore:Draugr|draugr]]. Another legend says that when the All-Maker breathed life into the creatures of the land, that "Breath" blew through the trees as well. Some of these trees kept a part of this life, creating the [[Lore:Spriggan|spriggan]]s.{{ref|name=BMSkaal}} In times of great need, the Skaal perform a ritualistic hunt of blessing and cleansing that they call the Ristaag in order to demonstrate their gratitude to, and hopefully please, the All-Maker. They also practice the Ritual of the Gifts, a pilgrimage to perform certain rituals at each of the All-Maker Stones to restore Oneness with the land.{{ref|name=BMKorst}} Skaal often accredit the All-Maker with greater wisdom and power than deities which are part of polytheistic traditions.{{ref|name=DBNikulas|[[Skyrim:Nikulas (Dragonborn)|Nikulas]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

In opposition to the All-Maker is [[Lore:The Adversary|the Adversary]], an equally enigmatic opposing force which works to corrupt the dominion of the All-Maker.{{ref|name=BMSkaal}}{{ref|name=TSOASS}} The often-malevolent [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] are treated much the same in the Skaal tradition as they are in [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]]-worshipping cultures,{{ref|name=DBStorn}}{{ref|name=DBWulf}}{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} though they are presumably considered to be merely some of the many evil aspects of the Adversary.{{ref|name=TSOASS}}

The All-Maker's mystique often leads to speculation on the connections the deity may bear with other known deities, such as [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]].  However, [[Lore:Thartaag|Thartaag]], an aspect of the Adversary, bears clear connections to Alduin, serving as a World-Devourer who will consume the world at the 'End of Seasons'.{{ref|name=TGATT|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}} In the context of ''[[Lore:The Story of Aevar Stone-Singer|The Story of Aevar Stone-Singer]]'', the All-Maker and the Adversary bear some resemblance to [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], who are in a similar antithetical conflict.{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} Certain texts state that Sithis is considered to be the All-Maker.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}}
