# Lore:Magna Ge

[[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] (or '''Magne-Ge'''), are a family of divine beings that fled [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] soon after its creation, following [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], the architect.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mysteries of the Mundus Stones}}}}{{ref|name=TOBD}}{{Ref|name=EOMN|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}} These [[Lore:Anu|Anuic]] spirits left tears in the veil of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] as they retreated to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], allowing its light to reach [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]]. The Magna Ge are now synonymous with the [[Lore:stars|stars]], which they created.{{ref|name=EOMN}}{{ref|group=UOL|''[[General:Cosmology|Cosmology]]''}} Those who separated from Magnus as he withdrew from creation are sometimes called '''Star-Orphans''', and were recognized by the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]]s as the Nine Coruscations.{{ref|name=TOBD|{{Cite Book|Testimonials on Baar Dau}}}}{{ref|name=EOMN}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|On the Detachment of the Sheath}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=VBOH|[[General:Vehk's Book of Hours|Vehk's Book of Hours]]}}{{ref|name=MUW|group=UOL|[[General:Made Up Word Round Up|Made Up Word Round Up]]}} Magnus created his offspring from aether, with his daughters emerging from forges. These offspring comprised the Magna Ge, which included the Nine Coruscations.{{Ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}}{{ref|name=9C}}

[[Lore:Mnemoli the Blue Star|Mnemoli the Blue Star]], or Mnemo-Li, is the most well-known Magna Ge. She is associated with "un-time" events, and it is said that she was visible even in the daytime sky during the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]]. Merid-Nunda was once one of the Magna Ge before she consorted with the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] and became the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]. [[Lore:Xero-Lyg|Xero-Lyg]] is a third identified Magna Ge. Mnemoli, Merid-Nunda and Xero-Lyg were referred to as sisters, and together with six other Magna Ge: [[Lore:Iana-Lor|Iana-Lor]], [[Lore:Londa-Vera|Londa-Vera]], [[Lore:Sheza-Rana|Sheza-Rana]], [[Lore:Unala-Se|Unala-Se]], [[Lore:Valia-Sha|Valia-Sha]], and the eldest of them all, the Prime Archon, they formed the group of the Nine Coruscations.{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{Ref|name=EOMN}} A [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] folktale also claims the moonlet [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]] originated as a wayward Magna Ge named Una who came to rest above Nirn to console it after the other Magna Ge departed, but this story is uncorroborated.{{ref|name=TOBD}}

[[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] credits the Magna Ge with creating [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] in "the bowels of [[Lore:Lyg|Lyg]]".{{Ref|name=MDC4|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 4}}}} The Order of the Waking Flame embraced Sister [[Lore:Celdina|Celdina]]'s beliefs that Mehrunes Dagon was created by the Magna Ge to unmake the flawed creation of Mundus in accordance to Magnus's plans.{{Ref|name=DCT|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}} To them, Mundus is a place of pain and suffering, thus they looked to Dagon to move past their mortal perils, hoping that he would purge the impurities of existence so that it may emerge anew.{{ref|name=EoMDV1|{{Cite Book|Epics of Mehrunes Dagon, Volume 1}}}}{{ref|name=ONCeldina|[[ON:Sister Celdina|Sister Celdina]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} This contrasted the ideas of Lyranth, who believes that when the Magna Ge fled, they spat on the conviction of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] that remained to finish creating Mundus, and Dagon's emergence and sphere may be reflective of the destruction caused by their abandonment.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}

Before it was pillaged by the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] in {{Year|2E 541}},{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sister Celdina}}}} the {{Lore Link|Priory of the Golden Staff}}, a monastery in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] dedicated to Magnus, held forbidden texts that described the Magna Ges' search for a tool that could unmake Nirn's flaws and thus restore it to Magnus's design, and this tool was said to be Mehrunes Dagon.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}}
