# Lore:Planemeld

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore History Trail}}[[File:ON-concept-Dark Anchor.jpg|thumb|left|Dark Anchors surrounding the Imperial City]]{{TOCright}}</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] was a monumental [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] invasion of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] that took place sometime between {{Year|2E 578}} and {{Year|2E 582}},{{intnote|Planemeld|[nb 1]}} during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. The [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] used [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]], huge Daedric interplanar machines of his own design, in an attempt to drag [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], which had been "set adrift" and become "unmoored from the fabric of the [[Lore:Aurbis|multiverse]]" as a result of the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]],{{ref|name=ILTESO}}{{ref|name=TCA}} out of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] and into his realm of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]].{{ref|name=PDA|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}} Rifts of darkness that lead from Tamriel directly to Coldharbour appeared across the continent. Each death in Tamriel weakened the barriers between Nirn and the [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]] beyond, and the concurrent [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] only accelerated the Planemeld.{{ref|name=ILTESO|[[Online:Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online]]}} The scale of the Planemeld is comparable to that of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]]. The Planemeld was ultimately stopped by [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] with [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]'s assistance.{{ref|name=ESOMeridiaTFS|[[Online:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOTFS|[[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Background==
===[[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|Varen's Rebellion]]===
In {{Year|2E 576}},{{ref|name=PQOTGC|{{Cite Book|Pirate Queen of the Gold Coast}}}} Emperor [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] of the short-lived [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Dynasty]] legalized Daedra worship throughout the crumbling [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]]. After hearing of this, [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Duke Varen Aquilarios]] of [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]] mustered the forces of the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian Highlands]] to depose Leovic.{{ref|name=EFEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}} Thus began a bloody year-long revolt,{{ref|name=PQOTGC}} which Duke Varen waged with the necromancer lord [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s counsel.{{ref|name=COTFC1}} Varen based his rebellion out of [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]], where he coordinated his troops as they liberated [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s other cities to further weaken Leovic's hold over the Empire. The rebellion claimed victory when Varen's forces stormed the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], and Varen personally dispatched Leovic at the foot of the Ruby Throne before proclaiming himself emperor.{{ref|name=COTFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}

===[[Lore:Five Companions|The Five Companions]]===
After Varen claimed the White-Gold Tower, he wished to legitimize his claim to the Ruby Throne by lighting the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]], as all true Emperors must. This could only be done by a [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]], one with the blood and soul of the [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]]&mdash;which Varen was not, just like Leovic before him. Mannimarco, now the chief advisor of Varen's court, convinced the emperor that a modified version of the coronation ritual would not only light the Dragonfires, but convince [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] to grant him the Dragon's blood. To do this, they needed the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], which was lost in the years following the fall of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]].{{ref|name=COTFC1|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}}

Thus, the Five Companions were formed in 2E 577: Varen Aquilarios himself, the sorcerer Mannimarco, the half-giant warrior [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]], the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] blademaster [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]], and Grand Chancellor [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]. They set out on a two-year quest to find the Amulet of Kings.{{ref|name=COTFC1}}

===The Soulburst===
[[File:LG-cardart-Soulburst 02.jpg|The Soulburst|thumb|left]][[File:ON-quest-The Harborage 04.jpg|thumb|right|A vision of the Soulburst]]
The Five Companions were able to retrieve the Amulet of Kings, and begin the ritual in {{Year|2E 579}}.{{ref|name=COTFC1}} The Five gathered at the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]] in the Imperial City, and began the ritual. At that moment, Mannimarco revealed his true intentions: he was an agent of Molag Bal. The ritual would not reignite the Dragonfires, but in fact did the exact opposite. Unbeknownst to the rest of the Five Companions, Mannimarco had corrupted the ritual, having spent weeks inscribing glyphs and incantantions that would weaken the ancient barriers protecting Nirn and tear the veil between worlds , setting the mortal realm adrift in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]{{ref|name=ILTESO}} in an event known as the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]].{{ref|name=ProphetHarborage|[[ON:The Prophet#The Harborage|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Harborage (quest)|The Harborage]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONHarborage|Events of [[Online:The Harborage (quest)|The Harborage]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}

This event allowed Molag Bal to initiate the Planemeld. As the Soulburst occurred, magical aftershocks swept across every corner of Nirn. Mages died or went mad, [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] trembled, earthquakes shook [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], heavy storms devastated the coastlines of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] and [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], and Nirn came "unmoored from the fabric of the [[Lore:Aurbis|multiverse"]]. In [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]], a great chasm split the city in two, and [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame Atronachs]] emerged from its depths, as daily quakes continued to take place.{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}} The constellation of the [[Lore:the Serpent|Serpent]] grew so large that it seemed to threaten every star sign at once.{{ref|name=ILTESO}} Through the Soulburst Molag Bal was given the opportunity to disconnect the [[Lore:Souls|Souls]] of Nirn's inhabitants from their living hosts.{{ref|name=ProphetHarborage}} In the wake of the Soulburst, [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]], [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] and other revenants started manifesting with greater frequency and became visible and audible even to average mortals due to the fraying of the veil between Nirn and the [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherial]] realms of the afterlife. The weakening of the veil also made it easier for necromancers to raise corpses on an unprecedented scale.{{ref|name=GBAYQ}}

Consumed by the fires of the Soulburst, Varen Aquilarios vanished. He was officially declared dead{{ref|name=EFEV}}&mdash;a fact which Mannimarco used to implicate Lyris and Sai in the emperor's supposed regicide. Lyris was captured by Mannimarco, while Abnur pledged his allegiance to Mannimarco in a bid to preserve the Empire. Sai Sahan fled with the Amulet of Kings, securing the artifact within the depths of [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]].{{ref|name=COTFC1}}

Afterwards, the Tharn family, who were now the new rulers of Cyrodiil through Varen's widow, Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]], made a secret pact with Mannimarco. The King of Worms agreed to strengthen the Imperial armies by resurrecting their fallen soldiers.{{ref|name=GIESO2012|group=UOL|[[General:The Elder Scrolls Online: From Skyrim to Morrowind, and everything in between|''The Elder Scrolls Online: From Skyrim to Morrowind, and everything in between'']]}} Abnur, Clivia's father, grew a deep hatred for Mannimarco after the Soulburst. He was soon brought to the [[Lore:Castle of the Worm|Castle of the Worm]] in Coldharbour and imprisoned there.{{ref|name=ONCastle|Events of [[Online:Castle of the Worm (quest)|Castle of the Worm]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

After the fall of the Longhouse Dynasty but before the Soulburst, Mannimarco approached Ard [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]] of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] with a proposition. The Reachfolk were embittered by Varen's rise to power. Durcorach's conquest of Cyrodiil saw the Reachmen's land returned to them, and the rise of the Longhouse Dynasty was a point of pride for the Reach. Mannimarco pledged justice for Caddach's people in exchange for allowing the Worm Cult shelter in Markarth. Eventually, Mannimarco delivered: because of the Soulburst, Varen lost his throne, his lands, and (as far as the Ard was concerned) his life.{{ref|name=ESOArd|[[ON:Ard Caddach#The End of Eternity|Ard Caddach]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The End of Eternity|The End of Eternity]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} 

Following the Soulburst, Mannimarco spent a good deal of time in Markarth. He and his Worm Cult were granted access to the ruins of [[Lore:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]] by Ard Caddach, who was grateful to Mannimarco for bringing about the downfall of Varen Aquilarios.{{ref|name=LyrisTEOE|[[Online:Lyris Titanborn#The End of Eternity|Lyris Titanborn]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The End of Eternity|The End of Eternity]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}{{ref|name=EGT|{{EGT||The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel}}}} Caddach gave him a sizeable portion of land in the hills near Markarth for experimentation.<!--I gave that parcel to Mannimarco years ago. It was his to offer to Molag Bal as he pleased.--> Mannimarco performed a ritual to meld this land with Coldharbour, but his attempt failed; the land was sundered from [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]],{{ref|name=LyrisTEOE}} but was not sent to Coldharbour, instead becoming lost in Oblivion. However, Mannimarco was able to use the knowledge he gained from this failure to initiate the Planemeld.{{ref|name=ESOArd}} This sundered land would become the lost realm of [[Lore:Grayhaven|Grayhaven]].{{ref|name=ESOTEoE|[[ON:Count Verandis Ravenwatch#The Library|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The End of Eternity|The End of Eternity]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

==The Planemeld==
===The [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] Fall===
The Planemeld began sometime between {{Year|2E 578}} and {{Year|2E 582}}.{{intnote|Planemeld|[nb 1]}} Transliminal [[Lore:Portals|portals]] called Dark Anchors were summoned throughout Tamriel, hooking into the ground, slowly ripping parts of Nirn away to float loosely in Oblivion and gradually drawing the realms closer together with each weakening the barrier between worlds further.{{ref|name=ILTESO}}{{ref|name=PDA}} Through the Anchors Daedra also crossed over into Nirn.{{ref|name=ESODarkAnchor|[[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} These portals were all metaphysically moored to a colossal construction in Coldharbour called [[Lore:The Great Shackle|the Great Shackle]], a device which existed in multiple locations at once and was thus capable of binding disparate realms together,{{ref|name=GreatShackle|[[Online:The Great Shackle |The Great Shackle]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} which was meant to cast metaphysical chains that'd grip Nirn across the planar chaos and pull it into Coldharbour, completing the merging of worlds.{{ref|name=Galerion|[[Online:Vanus Galerion | Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Planemeld was centered on [[Lore:The Endless Stair|the Endless Stair]], a fortified impossibility constructed by the [[Lore:Mad Architect|Mad Architect]].{{ref|name=EndlessStair|[[ON:The Endless Stair|The Endless Stair]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Once an area was sundered from Tamriel, the pieces would reform as a pocket realm of Coldharbour before joining the [[Lore:Planar Vortex|Planar Vortex]], an inter-planar realm where Tamriel and Coldharbour converged.{{ref|name=ESOMeridiaTFS}}{{ref|name=ESOTFS}} The Vortex could only be accessed from within the Endless Stair and, like the Stair itself, was designed for Molag Bal by the Mad Architect.{{ref|name=EndlessStair}} The largest Dark Anchor opened above the skies of the Imperial City, intent on pulling the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], a metaphysical focus so important it had the potential to pull all of Tamriel in with it once taken,{{ref|name=Tower|[[Online:White-Gold Tower |White-Gold Tower]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} into Coldharbour.{{ref|Appearance of the [[ON:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Under the influence of the Dark Anchor, part of the White-Gold Tower formed into a distorted space known as a Shattered Void.{{ref|name=Terran|[[ON:Sister Terran Arminus|Sister Terran Arminus]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ShatteredVoid|[[ON:White-Gold Tower|Shattered Void]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Concurrently, eruptions of darkness which allowed whoever passed through to enter directly into Coldharbour started spreading across Nirn.{{ref|name=ILTESO}} Around each Dark Anchor the [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|Chaotic Creatia]] of Coldharbour started seeping into Nirn and, responding to the will of Molag Bal, gradually transformed the infested land to mirror Coldharbour itself.{{ref|name=GBAYQ|{{Cite Book|Gabrielle Benele Answers Your Questions}}}} Each death further weakened the barrier between worlds and furthered Molag Bal's plan.{{ref|name=ILTESO}}. Some of the places taken from Nirn by the Planemeld were brought together to form border realms of Coldharbour, as was the case with the [[Lore:Village of the Lost|Village of the Lost]], a border realm formed out of two villages and an [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] stronghold, which were taken from Tamriel through rifts that formed when inhabitants of those places made deals with one of Molag Bal's [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] servants. The border realm rose out of a sea of [[Lore:Azure Plasm|Azure Plasm]].{{ref|name=World|Events of [[Online:Holes in the World|Holes in the World]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Survivors|Events of [[Online:Soul Survivors|Soul Survivors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Lost|[[Online:Village of the Lost|Village of the Lost]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONMezha|[[Online:Mezha-dro|Mezha-dro]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-interior-White-Gold Tower 02.jpg|The Shattered Void, the distorted space which formed within the White-Gold Tower due to the influence of the Dark Anchor|thumb|left]][[File:ON-crown store-Red Diamond Guardian.jpg|Red Diamond Guardians, strange beings that started appearing after the Soulburst|thumb|right]]
The Anchors fell upon virtually every region in Tamriel. Dolmen construction was interrupted in the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]], as the [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] of the newly-built [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] used the Black Worm Clan's ancient power stones to construct the city. The stones were stolen before the anchors even began to fall, forcing the cultists to start anew.{{ref|name=OCL|{{Cite Book|Our Continued Labor}}}} The [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] protected [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] from Dark Anchors, forcing the Worm Cult to find other means of domination such as using an ancient shrine in [[Lore:Ashalmawia|Ashalmawia]] to summon Bal's minions.{{Ref|name=ONSavile|[[Online:Savile Alam|Savile Alam]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

With Cyrodiil in ruins and the provinces divided by the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], few were left to oppose the invading Daedra. Meridia had her servant, [[Lore:Sees-All-Colors|Sees-All-Colors]], assassinate the leader of the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] in order to assume the role of guildmaster. Under Daedric command and funded by the wealth of Meridia's worshippers, the guild began refusing all other contracts and focused its efforts on destroying Dark Anchors and opposing Molag Bal's schemes. Meanwhile, the Five Companions were reformed by the arrival of the prophesied [[Lore:The Vestige|Soulless One]],{{ref|[[ON:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]]' dialogue during [[ON:Council of the Five Companions|Council of the Five Companions]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} who freed the Companions from Bal's clutches and reclaimed the Amulet of Kings, defeating [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] in the process.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point after the Soulburst, mysterious slate-skinned figures, known as Red Diamond Guardians, periodically appeared in the Imperial City to fight the Daedra. Each one had the Red Diamond encrusted upon their torso. No one knew where they came from or where they went after each fray.{{ref|[[ON:Red Diamond Guardian|Red Diamond Guardian]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Tamrielic Invasion of Coldharbour===
[[File:ON-interior-The Planar Vortex.jpg|thumb|right|The Planar Vortex]][[File:ON-npc-Guild Skirmish World Event 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Guilds teaming up to fight the Daedra]]
As the Three Banners War dragged on, Molag Bal's power over Tamriel continued to grow. Together with the Fighters Guild, the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] under [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] and the Soulless One finally arranged for a meeting of the three alliance leaders on the island of [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]] to discuss a potential counter-invasion of Coldharbour. Before an agreement could be reached, Molag Bal sent Daedra to the island to kill the alliance leaders. Although the attackers were repelled, the leaders quickly consented to a guild-led invasion, having seen Bal's influence firsthand.{{ref|Events of [[Online:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Though initially scattered across Coldharbour by the realm's defenses, the invasion force was gradually reassembled within the [[Lore:Hollow City|Hollow City]], a sanctuary of Meridia within Coldharbour which, encased by the power of the [[Lore:Light of Meridia|Light of Meridia]] and pushed into Molag Bal's realm long ago by its Prince, served as a piece of Meridia's realm within the domain of the God of Schemes, and so could not be touched.{{ref|name=Groundskeeper|[[ON:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The last [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] king, [[Lore:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]], who had been brought to Coldharbour alongside the Hollow City and been kept alive for centuries to be tortured by the Daedra, was also rescued from his prison in the Lightless Oubliette through the use of one of the Lights of Meridia, and went on to assume leadership of the force assembled in the Hollow City.{{ref|name=Dynar|[[ON:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LaloriaranDynar}}

During the invasion circumstances that threatened to accelerate the completion of the Planemeld emerged. Vanus Galerion, who had been teleported outside the Black Forge of Coldharbour alone by the realm's defenses,{{ref|name=Groundskeeper}} attempted to disable it to help stop the Planemeld. He was soon captured by the Daedra, who intended to use him as a power source, believing that powerful mortals and especially magic users were the most efficient power source for the Planemeld{{ref|name=PDA|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}} and that, as the "quintessential mortal mage", the power raging inside Galerion would allow them to complete the forging of the Great Shackle and power it with Galerion's magic, thus allowing them to complete the Planemeld in mere hours instead of months. To prevent this outcome Galerion separated his being into its constituent [[Lore:Souls|essences]] of magicka, health and stamina, which the Daedra could not use to complete the Planemeld individually. Despite this, the overseers of the Black Forge imprisoned the separated essences and used them to power the Black Forge itself, using them to craft Dark Anchors and components of the Great Shackle, to power their [[Lore:Soul Shriven|Soul Shriven]] slaves so they'd keep working tirelessly, and to feed their [[Lore:Vampire|vampiric]] servants. The Vestige freed him, restored and reunited his essence, and journeyed with him, when he personally destroyed the Great Shackle that was intended to bind Coldharbour and Nirn together by sending metaphysical chains across the planar chaos to latch onto the world. With this task accomplished, Galerion also joined the force assembled in the Hollow City.{{ref|name=TSoP|{{Cite Book|The Source of Power}}}}{{ref|name=GalerionMagicka|[[Online:Galerion's Magicka|Galerion's Magicka]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GalerionStamina|[[Online:Galerion's Stamina|Galerion's Stamina]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GalerionHealth|[[Online:Galerion's Health|Galerion's Health]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Galerion|[[Online:Vanus Galerion | Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-interior-The Mooring.jpg|The Great Shackle|thumb|left]][[File:ON-quest-The Final Assault 02.jpg|Molag Bal battles Meridia in the Planar Vortex|thumb|right]]
Though the Great Shackle had been destroyed, it had generated a Planar Vortex prior to its deactivation. Likened to an "Oblivion straw" that would pull Nirn and "its very essence" into Coldharbour by itself if not dispersed.{{ref|name=TSoP}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}{{ref|name=Cadwell|[[Online:Cadwell |Cadwell]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LaloriaranDynar|[[ON:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Army of Meridia|The Army of Meridia]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The guild forces, the "Army of Meridia", crossed {{Lore Link|the Chasm}} of Coldharbour but were met by the vampires of {{Lore Link|the Orchard}} which were controlled by one of Molag Bal's Harvest Hearts. Vanus proposed enacting a ritual that would destroy the Heart and target the [[Lore:Blood Magic|blood]] of all vampires connected to it, rendering them mindless and allowing the guilds to pass through. The vampire leader Virgar proposed using a method that would free the vampires from the Heart instead, allowing them to become allied to the guild forces.{{ref|name=Galerion}} It is unknown what the Vestige ultimately chose, but the vampires were severed from Molag Bal's control, allowing the guilds to enter the {{Lore Link|Reaver Citadel}}, which they captured to gain access to {{Lore Link|the Endless Stair}}, to which they were granted access by the restored [[Lore:Foolkillers Clan|Foolkillers Clan]].{{ref|[[Online:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue at the end of [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]]}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}{{ref|name=Harvest|Events of [[Online:The Harvest Heart|The Harvest Heart]]  in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Citadel|Events of [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]]  in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Thus a small group entered the Planar Vortex with Meridia. Their goal was to unleash the Light of Meridia at the focal point of the Vortex thus dispersing it. However, before the Light of Meridia could be unleashed, the group needed to destroy the Dark Ancors that Molag Bal was using to bind the Vortex together as, otherwise, the resulting backlash would "destroy Nirn as surely as the Planemeld would". Meridia, whose power was greater in the in-between realm than it had been in Coldharbour, aided in this task by distracting Molag Bal and defending the group from him. After the destruction of the Anchors, the Light, magically prepared by Vanus Galerion and [[Lore:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]], destroyed the vortex entirely, setting back all progress Bal had made on the Planemeld. The victory, however, would cost the life of Laloriaran Dynar, who had stayed outside the protective barrier Meridia had created when the Light was unleashed in order to deal with the portals Molag Bal's forces were using, pushed towards the shield at the last moment, he was teleported away with the rest, but it was too late for him to be saved and he was fatally scorched as a result.{{ref|name=ESOMeridiaTFS}}{{ref|name=ESOTFS}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}{{ref|name=LaloriaranDynarFinal|[[ON:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Thinking themselves victorious, the invasion force returned to Tamriel. However, Molag Bal had simply retired to his palace at [[Lore:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]], and planned to attempt a second Planemeld when he eventually recovered his strength.{{ref|[[ON:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Planemeld Obverse===
[[File:ON-npc-Molag Kena.jpg|Molag Kena atop the distorted Tower|thumb|right]][[File:ON-place-White-Gold Tower.jpg|thumb|left|White-Gold Tower]]
In a final attempt to restart the Planemeld,{{ref|group=UOL|[[Online:Temple District|Temple District]] background in the [[General:Future of The Elder Scrolls Online Panel from QuakeCon 2014#Temple District|Future of The Elder Scrolls Online Panel from QuakeCon 2014]]}} Bal sent a [[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] called [[Lore:Molag Kena|Molag Kena]] to pose as the seemingly-deceased Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]] and "retake" the White-Gold Tower. In truth, Kena sought an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] within the Tower and planned to use its power to initiate the "Planemeld Obverse" and begin simultaneously pulling Coldharbour into Nirn. However, a [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|moth priestess]] called [[Lore:Terran Arminus|Terran Arminus]] had read the scroll before the Daedric occupation of the Imperial City, and had learned that she would need aid in order to save the scroll. This aid arrived in the form of a group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], and the prophesied Soulless One, who helped Clivia's entourage ascend the shattered reality of the Tower's upper levels before being betrayed by the imposter. These heroes defeated Molag Kena atop the Tower before she could fully complete the ritual, and in the process destroyed the Anchor above the city.{{ref|[[ON:Planemeld Obverse|Planemeld Obverse]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Terran|[[ON:Sister Terran Arminus|Sister Terran Arminus]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Coinciding with the events at White-Gold Tower, Bal's forces began to search the city for a relic called the [[Lore:Sublime Brazier|Sublime Brazier]]. Housed in the hidden [[Lore:Dragonfire Cathedral|Dragonfire Cathedral]] in the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruins beneath the city, this brazier was key to relighting the Dragonfires.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sublime Brazier}}}} If it was destroyed, Akatosh's Covenant would be broken and Mundus would be eternally vulnerable to Daedric invasion. This plot was uncovered by the [[Lore:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]], a mysterious [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] agent still operating within the city. With the help of the Vestige and other allies, the Drake entered Dragonfire Cathedral and performed a ritual to become the brazier's immortal guardian. The Daedric siege of the cathedral was lifted in the process, and the leader of Bal's forces, Xivkyn General [[Lore:Malivus|Malivus]], was banished.{{ref|name=IC|Events of [[Online:Imperial City|Imperial City]] storyline in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Drake would serve as guardian for all time, effectively foiling Molag Bal's plot.

===Banishment of Molag Bal===
[[File:ON-quest-God of Schemes 09.jpg|The Vestige, imbued with the power of the Divines, fights Molag Bal|thumb|right]]
Though the immediate danger had been quelled, the power Molag Bal had gained over Nirn was still prodigious. In order to stop any future attempts at another Planemeld and restore protection to Nirn, the Five Companions journeyed to Heart's Grief and confronted Bal using the Amulet of Kings. Mantling the power of Akatosh via the Amulet, made possible through the sacrifice of one of the Companions and a ritual performed by Abnur Tharn, the Vestige faced the Prince in single combat and banished him to the [[Lore:Void|Void]], freeing the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] and restoring the land he had captured, and ensuring the erasure of what remained of Molag Bal's hold over Nirn.{{ref|name=ESOGodofSchemes|[[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOAbnur|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn's]] dialogue during [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The event gravely wounded Molag Bal, whose power would take some time to recover, and expended the energies of the Amulet of Kings for a generation or more.{{ref|name=ESOMeridia|[[ON:Meridia|Meridia's]] dialogue during [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
