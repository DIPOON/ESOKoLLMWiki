# Lore:Mer

<noinclude>{{Trail|Races}} [[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[File:OB-misc-TopalStatue.png|250px|right|link=Lore:Topal the Pilot]]{{TOCright}}</noinclude>
'''Mer''' ({{IPA|m|E|r}}, meaning ''Folk''),{{intnote|Mer|[nb 1]}} or '''Elves''',{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue A|Aldmeris]] dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} are the "Elder Races" of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], such as: the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]], [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]], and [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]—all of which are descendants of the primordial [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] race.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} The [[Lore:Orc|Orsimer]] are also commonly believed to share this ancestry.{{Ref|name=TTNOO|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}} The term Mer is also commonly used for non-elven peoples, like [[Lore:Betmer|Betmer]] which refers to Beastfolk, and Nemer which seemingly refers to anyone who is not an elf.{{ref|name=MWNTH|{{Cite Book|Note to Hlevala|ns_base=Morrowind}}}}{{Ref|name=MWDialogueBetmeri|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue B|Betmeri]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=Tendil|[[Online:Captain Tendil|Captain Tendil]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Lifting the Veil|Lifting the Veil]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The terms "elven" and "merish" are adjectives meaning "of or relating to the Mer".{{ref|{{Cite book|4th Manifesto of Kinlord Rilis XII}}}}{{ref|[[Online:The Rift Antiquities|Ebony Fox Totem Antiquities Codex]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Ring of the Pale Order|Direnni Elegy Loop Antiquities Codex]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} Elves are said to consider themselves the only "truly human race", as they are descended directly from the gods. Some elves regard Mannish and Beast races as nothing more than intelligent animals.{{ref|name=GerH|Generic dialogue regarding [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue H#Humanoid Races|Humanoid Races]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

The [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]], often called Manmer, are the result of interbreeding between Aldmer and [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]];<noinclude>{{Ref|name=PGE1High|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}}</noinclude> they are most commonly considered men, and not mer.{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} A couple races of mer have disappeared from the world or fallen from prominence, including the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] and the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]].{{Ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} The Maormer either have a different origin entirely or diverged from their brethren before the Aldmer inhabited [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]].{{Ref|name=PGE3Other|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}

What sets the mer apart from the other races of Tamriel is their [[Lore:Lifespan|long lifespans]]; aside from the Orsimer, Aldmeri lifespans typically range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors like disease or violence.{{ref|name=TRB2}}{{Ref|name=IWADE|{{Cite Book|Interview with a Dark Elf|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{ref|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4|''Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4'']]}} Elven longevity has been described as a kind of “godhood” that was “granted by the gods”.{{Ref|name=KEXII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part XII}}}}{{ref|name=TRB2|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 2}}}} Lifespans in the thousands are typically seen among powerful mages or nobles. It is said Elven nobles live “a lot longer” than common folk due to “selective breeding” and a “luxurious lifestyle” (among other reasons).{{ref|name=TPP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Ted Peterson's Posts}}}} Some of the most ancient and powerful Elven mages have extended their lives far beyond the natural span, in some cases enduring for millennia through the use of potent magical practices.{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}}{{ref|name=PMB|[[Online:Psijic Mascot Bear Cub|Psijic Mascot Bear Cub]] pet description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{Ref|[[MW:Alfe Fyr|Alfe Fyr]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

According to one source the average elven woman can conceive at least twice, but no more than four times—and typically only around the time she turns sixteen.{{Ref|name=TRB3|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 3}}}} However, the same sources describe a [[Lore:Barenziah|particular Dark Elven woman]] giving birth up to four times within a two decade period when she was in her 300s.{{Ref|name=TRB3}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}}{{Ref|name=TDD|{{Cite Book|The Nightingales|ns_base=SR}}}} There are also several known Dunmer families with over six children.{{ref|name=ONPKI|[[Online:Pentarch Kovan Indoril|Pentarch Kovan Indoril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONME|[[Online:Mirri Elendis|Mirri Elendis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Regardless, the rule does not apply to Bosmer, who are known for their numerous offspring.{{ref|name=ONEVE|[[Online:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Other Ethnonyms===
Before the "-mer" suffix rose to prominence, the archaic "-che" suffix was used in its place to refer to the Elder Races (around the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]){{ref|name=TSOP}}{{ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=Nantharion}}, in terms such as: the [[Lore:Bosmer|Boiche]],{{ref|name=TWEDF|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves|ns_base=DF}}}}{{ref|name=AU4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}}{{ref|name=ACfR|{{Cite Book|A Call for Recollection}}}} [[Lore:Dunmer|Moriche]]{{ref|name=TWEDF}}{{Ref|name=PoM}} (or [[Lore:Chimer|Maliache]]),{{ref|name=RPTrialOfVehk|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 1: The Trial of Vivec|''The Trial of Vivec'']]}} Noiché (or Noiché'el),{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} and [[Lore:Altmer|Salache]]{{ref|name=TWEDF}} (or [[Lore:Ayleids|Saliache]]).{{ref|name=TSOP|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal}}}}{{ref|name=TOotO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}} The ruling government of the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] was referred to as the [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Imperatum Saliache]]{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}}—and though the term "Ayleid" seems to be the accepted modern name for the people used in common parlance,{{ref|name=TWEDF}}{{ref|name=ONLaloriaran|[[Online:King Laloriaran Dynar|King Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the term "Saliache" is considered the older endonymic name.{{ref|name=Nantharion|[[Online:King Nantharion|King Nantharion]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

The "-iil" particle is an ancient elven suffix describing the "folk" of a region,{{ref|name=CyrusComic}}{{ref|name=LandWarPoetry}} seen in terms like [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], [[Lore:Delodiil|Delodiil]], [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayniil]].{{ref|name=CyrusComic|''[[Books:The Origin of Cyrus!/Transcription#Chain Mail: Letters to The Elder Scrolls Adventures|The Origin of Cyrus!]]''}}{{ref|name=TWOD|{{Cite Book|The Whithering of Delodiil}}}}{{ref|name=LandWarPoetry|[[Online:Music#A Land of War and Poetry lyrics|''A Land of War and Poetry'' lyrics]]}} In the case of Cyrodiil, that term stood for the capital city, land, the people, and the figurehead of that region—which is derived from an ancient the-king-is-the-land tradition dating back to when the Ayleids ruled there.{{ref|name=CyrusComic}}

Additionally the older version of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] seems to be [[Lore:Alcharyai|Alcharyai]],{{Ref|name=PoM}} and the name of the [[Lore:Left-Handed Elves|Left-Handed Elves]] of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] is [[Lore:Left-Handed Elves|Kanuryai]],{{ref|name=CFEM|{{Cite Book|Cries from Empty Mouths}}}} indicating that "-yai" is an archaic or older suffix used by Aldmeri cultures.

===Elvendom===
"Elvendom" is a term that refers broadly to the Elven world or collective body—the selves and souls—of all the Elven [[Lore:Races|races]], and their continued existence in the world. A band of great heroes from the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], [[Lore:Moraelyn's Companions|Moraelyn's Companions]], hid the legendary [[Lore:Horn of Summoning|Horn of Summoning]] artifact away in a secret location, so that it may be used "at [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'s last need" and if "all of Elvendom" faced dire peril.{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} 

Amongst some Elves, there was an belief that all the Elven races must one day unify. During the rise of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]], [[Lore:¥R|¥R]]—a minor member of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]]—expressed this belief, claiming that "the Mer must unite ''at last'' or be consumed one by one".{{Ref|name=PGE1PR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Preface}}}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|Mer|^|Mer means folk. Examples include Aldmer meaning First Folk,{{Ref|name=CotB|{{Cite Book|Corruption of the Blood}}}} or Chimer meaning Changed Folk.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}}}
* Of the races of elves, there are at least two with native names that have no known "-mer" suffix: the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]]s and [[Lore:Kanuryai|Kanuryai]]. An archaic naming system also provides alternate names with a "-che" suffix, such as Boiche (for Wood Elves), and Moriche (for Dark Elves).{{ref|name=TWEDF|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves|ns_base=DF}}}} Similarly, while the Orcs are also known as Orsimer, they call themselves "Ornim" in their [[Lore:Old Orcish|native tongue]].{{ref|name=TATSCT|{{Cite Book|Torug at the Summit, Complete Translation}}}}
* For a list of notable elves, see [[:Category:Lore-People-Mer|here]].
*In his conceptualization notes for the PGE2 project, Micheal Kirkrbide briefly suggested including a sidebar in the Tatterdemalion section about a race or races that live upon the moons of Nirn, suggesting "Moonmer" as one of the concepts.{{ref|name=PGE2Concept|group=UOL|''[[General:Pocket Guide to the Empire, Second Edition/Conceptualization|Pocket Guide to the Empire, Second Edition/Conceptualization]]'' - [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]}}
