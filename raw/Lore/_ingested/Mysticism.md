# Lore:Mysticism

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

{{Trail|Magic}}[[File:LG-cardart-Telekinesis.png|thumb|right|Telekinesis]]
'''Mysticism''' is one of the six colleges of [[Lore:Magic|magic]], and the one least understood by the magical community.{{Ref|name=M|{{Cite Book|Mysticism (book)}}}} The art has been described as allowing a mage "to alter the nature of magic itself"{{Ref|name=PSOM|{{Cite Book|Proposal: Schools of Magic}}}} and as involving "the manipulation of magical forces and boundaries to bypass the structures and limitations of the physical world."{{ref|name=MGM|Morrowind Game Manual}} The spells in the school have their origins with the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], who call their study the "Old Way",{{Ref|name=M}} which, according to some sources, they were taught by the original inhabitants of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]].{{ref|group=UOL|name=PetersonInterview|[[General:Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Religion|Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Religion]]}} Thus the school has occasionally been referred to as the Way of the Psijic.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dawn (v2)}}}}  The practice of Mysticism seems to require dealing with "conundrums and paradoxes"{{Ref|name=M}} and abandoning logic to "embrace a temporary sort of insanity" which may lead the student to long-term harm if practiced without proper instruction.{{Ref|name=TBAOT|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}} It is said that, while most look at an object and fit it into a place in their way of thinking, those skilled in Mysticism can look at an object and identify it by its proper role instead. Practicioners can than achieve "true understanding" by identifying the object by its role and its truth and interpreting that meaning.{{ref|name=2920L|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}} Its usage was common among the mystic [[Lore:Reachman|Reachman]] [[Lore:Reachfolk Shamans|shamans]] during the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=ONRee|[[Online:Ree-Zish|Ree-Zish]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Later, when Hannibal Traven, Arch-Mage of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] in the closing years of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], launched his investigation that led to [[Lore:Necromancy|Necromancy]] being banned in the Guild, an attempt was made by necromancers to involve Mysticism in the debate to protect their favored school.{{Ref|name=TBAOT}}

The spells in the school can be divided into five main sections: transport spells, detection spells, absorption spells, reflection spells and "other". The Other spells work directly upon magical forces of the unseen world, with effects such as dispel, soul trap, and telekinesis. Absorption magic is often considered part of the college of [[Lore:Restoration|Restoration]] rather than Mysticism.

* Transportation spells are used mainly in locations where a poorly-developed road network makes fast travel by horse impossible. They allow the caster to return to a previously-marked location, or to appear instantly at a location associated with power, such as a shrine. When teleporting, there may be a risk of being caught between dimensions if the distance to be traveled is too great.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Charwich-Koniinge, Volume 3}}}}

* A wide variety of detection spells are also available. These may allow the caster to divine the locations of lifeforms, objects or any other item for which a specific spell has been developed.

* Absorption spells enable the caster to transfer energy directly from the target to him or herself. The spell may target attributes, skills, health, magicka, or any other aspect of the target's form.

* Reflection spells enable the caster to turn a certain proportion of incoming magical or physical attacks back onto the target.

* Dispel simply allows the caster to remove a previously-existing magical effect from the target.

* Telekinesis confers the ability to move items without physically touching them.

* The last effect, Soul Trap, is the most controversial. It enables the caster to store the soul of a creature in a gem, and to use it to create or recharge a magical artifact. Souls are divided into "white" - the souls of most creatures, and "black" - souls from humans, mer and other advanced beings.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Souls, Black and White}}}}

In {{Year|3E 431}}, the Council of Mages decided that Mysticism magic in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] was to become the responsibility of the guildhall in the town of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}} By {{Year|4E 201}}, Mysticism was no longer regarded as a separate school of magic by the College of Winterhold. There was no Mysticism specialist there, and the traditional spells had all been moved to other schools or were no longer in use.
