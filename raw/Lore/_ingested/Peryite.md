Such will-imbued creatia can form 'potentia vortices' which, if unattended, can form destructive 'realm-rips', phenomena which Peryite prevents from forming across Oblivion.{{ref|name=LFNHTRPAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}} When asked, [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s tutor Riparius says that "trying to keep ahead of it all keeps Peryite mighty busy, but nobody really feels sorry for him." Such was the case with the realm corroding plague Peryite unleashed against Apocrypha during the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], which afflicted both Apocrypha's inhabitants and Prince with debilitating pain, gradually severed Mora's connection to his domain, and threatened to dissolve the realm itself to nothingness.{{ref|name=Note}}{{ref|name=MoraCalamity}}{{ref|name=MoraUnhealthy}}{{ref|name=Calamity}}{{ref|name=Unhealthy}} It is also theorized that Peryite may have been involved in the creation of the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] developed by the Sload.{{ref|name=TAAYQ}}

==History==
[[File:ON-npc-Glyphic of Lost Memories.jpg|A memory of the meeting between Peryite, Vaermina, and Hermaeus Mora|thumb|right]]
A legend regarding the viridescent [[Lore:Dragon Frog|dragon frog]] states that the little amphibians were once a breed of [[Lore:Death Hopper|giant green frogs]] that grew as large as [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]]. These frogs envied the Dovah's ability to fly, so they sought out Peryite and made a pact with him. The Daedric Prince granted their wish, but it came with a price. The frogs got the wings they wanted, but the size of their bodies was greatly reduced.{{ref|name=ONVDF|[[Online:Viridescent Dragon Frog|Viridescent Dragon Frog]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] sought out the aid of the other Princes in imprisoning the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], whose manipulations of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]] and planned use of the [[Lore:Loom of the Untraveled Road|Loom of the Untraveled Road]] threatened to unravel all of [[Lore:Aurbis|reality]].{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LeramilWise|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Eventually an alliance was formed between Hermaeus Mora and [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], who proceeded to defeat Ithelia through their combined efforts and design a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.{{ref|name=Mora|[[ON:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dandera|[[ON:Priest Dandera Helas|Priest Dandera Helas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Prisoner|Events of [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Two of the Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought were [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]] and Peryite. However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sancrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.{{ref|name=Hidden|Events of [[ON:A Hidden Fate|A Hidden Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Vaermina|[[ON:Vaermina|Vaermina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Second Era===
====Before the Interregnum====
[[File:LO-book-Knahaten Flu.jpg|Plague doctors carting away corpses and boarding up condemned houses during the Knahaten Flu|thumb|right]]
The text ''[[Lore:Deal with a Daedric Prince|Deal with a Daedric Prince]]'' contains an account by healer Rindral Thirano, of a deal he made with Peryite in {{Year|2E 573}}. A series of disasters had befallen the city of [[Lore:Tear|Tear]], starting with a series of chill nights in the summer, which was followed by a flood and then a miasma that engulfed the city and afflicted both people and animals with a number of terrible plagues, including the grippe, ague, consumptive coughs, Fiddler's quinsy, and flying heart ticks. After the number of afflicted became too great for the city's healers to treat with their [[Lore:Spells|spells]] and [[Lore:Alchemy|potions]], and surrounding communities refused to lend aid for fear of the diseases spreading to them, one healer named Rindral Thirano decided the only solution was to bargain directly with the source of the disasters and plagues itself, Peryite. Summoned forth through a spell cast with specific alchemical ingredients, Peryite manifested in the form of a ghostly Skeever. Rindral furiously demanded that Peryite put an end to the plagues he'd inflicted on Tear, only for the amused Prince to ask how the healer planned to force a Daedric Prince to do his bidding. Rindral threatened that he'd amassed knowledge of disease during his life and would amass more, he'd spread this knowledge as far as he could so that people could use it to cut disease off at the root. In this manner Peryite's sphere would be reduced and his own influence would lessen. Peryite conceded to make a deal, promising that, for a year and a day, Tear would be free of illness. The following day people began to recover and the diseases subsided. Rindral himself, however, paid a heavy price. His limbs began to weaken, his skin grew scaly and lizardlike on one arm while boils grew on the other, food lost all taste and sleep and breathing became difficult. Soon, Rindral's sight started to fail and he finished penning his last account, secluded, as he felt something moving in his chest and cursed ever having made a deal with a Daedric Prince. As Rindral lay in agony, Tear's citizens celebrated their good health outside. Rindral's rotted corpse was discovered in his offices and burned to prevent whatever had afflicted him from spreading. Some witnesses, however, claimed the body was still moving as it was consigned to the plague fire.{{ref|name=Deal|{{Cite Book|Deal with a Daedric Prince|ns_base=Online}}}}

Some believe Peryite is responsible for the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]].{{ref|name=LTP|{{Cite Book|Letter to Peryite|ns_base=Online}}}} No one knows what brought the flu to [[Lore:Orcrest|Orcrest]], but some natives blamed the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s, while others theorized that Peryite was responsible.{{ref|name=FSOTF|{{Cite Book|First Signs of the Flu}}}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, a pair of Peryite worshipers sought to enter the city and gather evidence, which they could bend to claim that Peryite was responsible for the flu.{{ref|name=UP|Events of [[ON:Unhealthy Preoccupation|Unhealthy Preoccupation]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Nothing substantial came of the investigation, but they found a few scraps parroting the aforementioned theories.{{ref|name=UP}}

====Invasion of Apocrypha====
[[File:ON-place-Bal Sunnar (future) 05.jpg|The temple to Peryite in Bal Sunnar's future|thumb|left]][[File:LO-misc-Peryite Statue.jpg|right|thumb|Statue to Peryite in Bal Sunnar]]
During that same time, the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]], former servant of Ithelia, approached Peryite and Vaermina, seeking their aid in uncovering the truth behind a recurring dream he had. The dream was a suppressed memory of the meeting between Hermaeus Mora, Vaermina, and Peryite, that was preserved due to the last act of Ithelia, who'd saved Torvesard at the last moment. Torvesard was told that Vaermina and Peryite had experienced the same dream, though in their case it was not quite as vivid. The two Princes, furious with Hermaeus Mora for altering their memories, formed an alliance with Torvesard and with each other, determined to enter [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] and uncover the forgotten truth.{{ref|name=Torvesard|[[ON:Torvesard|Torvesard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TorvesardJournal|{{Cite Book|Torvesard's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}

In the course of this effort, Peryite became involved in the events surrounding the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Time Knot]] that  enveloped the [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] township of Bal Sunnar, trapping it in a time loop from sometime after the Battle of Red Mountain to 2E 582, when it was undone by the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Monk]] Saresea and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]]. The Knot was created by a Telvanni splinter group who sought to escape the rising rule of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] through the use of a Glyphic, an artifact of Hermaeus Mora. The Telvanni, recognized the Vestige and Saresea from previous loops and tried to stop them, tapping into the magic of the Knot to summon creatures from the time of ancient [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] or to conjure temporal duplicates of themselves to aid them in combat. The Knot itself resisted Saresea, exploding outward and transporting her and the Vestige both to the distant past and the distant future. When they were transported to the future, Saresea and the Vestige found themselves in a version of the city filled with Peryite's plague and creatures, the result of a desperate deal the Telvanni Matriarch had made with Peryite in order to stop them. Ultimately Saresea and the Vestige succeeded in defeating the Telvanni Matriarch and dispelling the Knot but at a cost: Saresea was fragmented and scattered across time. Though Hermaeus Mora, who had been observing, reached out and pulled her back together, she remained "multichronal", a being that manifests at multiple points in space and time simultaneously, both in the past and in the future, ranging from her own birth to her own death and beyond.{{Ref|name=UFT|Events of [[ON:Unstuck From Time|Unstuck From Time]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Scribes of Fate|Scribes of Fate]]''}}{{Ref|name=Saresea|[[ON:Saresea|Saresea]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Scribes of Fate|Scribes of Fate]]''}}{{Ref|name=Nerile|{{Cite Book|Journal of Nerile Belvayn}}}}{{Ref|name=Kovan|{{Cite Book|Journal of Kovan Giryon}}}}

In order to uncover the truth Mora had hidden, Vaermina and Peryite had their followers form an alliance and form a new cult devoted to both Princes called the Hidden Kindred, which the high priest of Peryite, Blightcrown, assumed leadership of.{{ref|name=NewCult|{{Cite Book|A New Cult Arises|ns_base=Online}}}} The Hidden Kindred also formed an alliance with Telvanni Master [[Lore:Shelreni Baro|Shelreni Baro]].{{ref|name=Shelreni|[[ON:Master Shelreni|Master Shelreni]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Shelreni|[[ON:Master Shelreni|Master Shelreni]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Spirit|Events of [[ON:Spirit of Fate|Spirit of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In order to proceed with their plans, the Hidden Kindred sought to retrieve a powerful [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] called the [[Lore:Fulcrum Obscura|Fulcrum Obscura]]. The relic, crafted long ago by Hermaeus Mora for [[Lore:Durdryn|Prior Durdryn]], had the power to uncover any hidden secret and was kept within the necropolis of [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]].{{ref|name=TPF|{{Cite Book|The Prior's Fulcrum}}}}{{ref|name=OTFO|{{Cite Book|On the Fulcrum Obscura}}}} Disguised as a prelate, Blightcrown infiltrated the city and had his Hidden Kindred place [[Lore:Censer of Corruption|Censers of Corruption]], plague spreading relics which contained "Peryite's blessed breath" throughout the chapel, hoping the afflicted [[Lore:Keepers of the Dead|Keepers of the Dead]] would reveal the relic's location. Investigating the spread of the illness that had gripped the Keepers, the Vestige found evidence of the Hidden Kindred's activities and plans on the ship they'd used to arrive, and tracked them to the catacombs with the aid of Curate Gadayn. In the catacombs, the Vestige encountered Blightcrown and Torvesard, who fled with one half of the Fulcrum Obscura after the artifact cracked, as it contained the power they needed even when broken.{{ref|name=KOTF|Events of [[Online:Keeper of the Fate|Keeper of the Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BO|{{Cite Book|Blightcrown's Orders|ns_base=ON}}}}

===Third Era===
[[File:OBR-quest-Peryite.jpg|The shrine of Peryite in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], east of [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] (Oblivion Remastered)|thumb|right]]
In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] contacted Peryite in search of power. The agent reminded the Prince of a mortal from the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], and Peryite took a shine to the agent. He had the agent slay a [[Lore:Vampirism|Vampire Ancient]] who had displeased him, and in return he had one of his worshippers give Spellbreaker to the agent.{{ref|name=DFPeryite|Events of [[Daggerfall:Peryite's Quest|Peryite's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, five worshippers of Peryite performed a ritual at the Prince's hidden shrine, located along the banks of the [[Lore:Silverfish River|Silverfish River]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The spell was intended to summon Peryite, but instead the worshippers became trapped between worlds, their bodies on Nirn and their [[Lore:Souls|souls]] in the Pits. The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] stumbled upon the motionless bodies of the worshippers by the shrine and was tasked by Peryite to travel to a plane in the Pits and recover their souls. The plane consisted of a volcanic island inhabited by many [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], with a Blood Well tower and a series of volcanic caves called the Sightless Grotto. The Hero rescued the souls of the worshippers and returned them to Tamriel to earn the Prince's favor and receive his artifact, Spellbreaker.{{ref|name=OBPeryite|Events of [[Oblivion:Peryite|Peryite]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Peryite tasked the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] with slaying {{Lore Link|Orchendor}}, a former priest of Peryite who had abandoned the Prince. Peryite had tasked Orchendor to shepherd those the Prince had afflicted with a wasting disease, initially spread among a number of [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] villages, to the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemeri]] ruin [[Lore:Bthardamz|Bthardamz]]. ==Worship==
[[File:ON-costume-Peryite Skeevemaster (male).jpg|thumb|left|Heavy and concealing clothes worn by Skeevemasters, who often collaborate with rats (ESO)]][[File:SR-npc-Afflicted Refugee 02.jpg|thumb|right|An Afflicted uses Vile Vapor]]
Peryite's followers preach the wonders of their Prince and the mysteries of the divine skeever.{{Ref|name=Filbert}} Peryite's worshippers are not well known or documented, and take a variety of methods in how they worship him. The [[Lore:Natural Order|Natural Order]] is a cult that consists of venerable nobles and aristocrats across Tamriel who secretly worship the Taskmaster.{{ref|name=DTTA|{{Cite Book|Dagonists Through the Ages}}}} Some worshipers liken Peryite more to the pus in the wound rather than disease itself; although the pus is unpleasant, it expels foul [[wikipedia:Humorism|humors]] and restores the blood, thereby cleaning it and restoring health.{{ref|name=SRKesh}} Peryite is viewed as the perfect truth of order by his followers, and they worship him because "sometimes the world can only be cleansed by disease", and "cleansing" the world would bring true natural order.{{ref|name=SRKesh}}{{ref|name=RONMV}} 

Cults such as the Diseased and Order of the Sacred Skeever are organizations of self-described social outcasts that take more extreme and controversial methods.{{ref|name=DTTA}} They would be glad to contract a disease and die in their own filth "as Peryite intended".{{Ref|name=ONMarthine}} They see every malady as a blessing from Peryite and revel in suffering and the spreading of plagues: fungal infections, scabies, rashes, mucus-filled lungs, itchiness and irritation in all forms, and tooth decay are some of the many afflictions adored by his cultists.{{Ref|name=Filbert}}{{ref|name=RONMV|{{Cite Book|Research of Nathien Mortieu}}}}{{ref|name=Gadayn}} These cultists seek to spread whatever illnesses they have to everyone they meet.{{Ref|name=Filbert}} Their recruitment attempts are often unsuccessful, as many see them as imbeciles.{{ref|name=ONRazumdar|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Scalecaller Cult viewed Nirn as a nasty imperfect world with no order or respect, and they saw other Princes as chaotic lies. They concocted an all-consuming plague to bury the world in corpses so that it would belong to Peryite, who would then bring about a new age of an ordered world without chaos.{{ref|name=RONMV}} This event is prophesied among various branches, who say only the most faithful followers of the Prince of Pestilence will survive.{{Ref|name=Filbert}} An interview with [[Lore:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus of Elinhir]] and {{Lore Link|Lady Cinnabar}} of [[Lore:Taneth|Taneth]] also implies that Peryite might have had a minor presence and influence among the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] of the Nords due to their veneration of dragon-kind.{{Ref|name=AMOVAB|{{Cite Book|A Matter of Voice and Brass: Dragon Bones DLC Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

There was also a particular group known as the Afflicted that consisted of [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] located within [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] in {{Year|4E 201}}. Peryite's "blessing" for them was infection with a plague, although he also granted them some resistance to its deadly effects.{{ref|name=SRAfflicted|[[Skyrim:Afflicted Refugee|Afflicted Refugee]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They had unusually bright red skin and the ability to spit poisonous vomit at their enemies, but they were eventually killed by Peryite's champion in his quest to kill their overseer, the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] known as Orchendor. Originally from the town of {{Lore Link|Cul Aloue}} in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], they were "shepherded" to the Dwemer ruin of [[Lore:Bthardamz|Bthardamz]].{{ref|name=UAL|{{Cite Book|Unsent Afflicted Letter|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Orchendor and the Afflicted were meant to stand ready, awaiting Peryite's command to cover the world with his blessing.{{ref|name=SRKesh}} They also worshiped strange draconic totems and brewed tinctures in their worship of Peryite.{{ref|name=SRTotem|Appearance of the Dragon-tipped totems deep within Bthardamz in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Afflicted drank the solutions they brewed, which other mortals would call poisons, to "heal" themselves.{{ref|name=UAL}}
[[File:ON-npc-Plague Concocter Mortieu.jpg|Nathien Mortieu, a plague concoctor of Peryite|thumb|left]][[File:ON-npc-The Noxious Herald.jpg|The Noxious Herald, a follower of Peryite, spreading disease|thumb|right]]
Peryite, who is known as the '''Master of Tasks''' and the '''Lord of Order''', is a revered spirit in the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] pantheon that shares many characteristics with [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], in pivotal ways such as being attributed to "time, rigid natural order, (and) draconic imagery". This has led to scholars concluding that there was a cultural diffusion between the early ancestors of [[Lore:Men|Men]] and [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] in the Northwestern parts of Tamriel. To the Reachfolk Peryite serves as a foil to the primacy of conflict. Everything that exists will pass. The fort that rises too high will fall. The clan that starves will one day grow strong. This eternal balance is the work of Peryite. To describe Peryite as a necessary evil would thus be a misnomer, for while the Reachfolk see him as necessary, they see the good in him maintaining a balance as a force of nature. While Reachmen may be claimed by blight or plague, they will come out of these natural tragedies healthier. This keeps the dangers of overpopulation in check and allows a new generation of Reachfolk to build immunity to what Peryite throws at them.{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 4}}}}

Peryite is invoked in [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]] rites{{Ref|name=ROA}} and is referred to as "Figuring father Peryite" in relation to "[[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] mother" in an obscure text which also names one of his attributes as his "Golden Order".{{Ref|name=SermonZero|group=UOL}} The stewards of Bal Sunnar built a temple to Peryite as their Matriarch believed he offered the most protection from the Scourge. Sacrifices would later begin that brought about unrecognizable creatures such as "Peryite's Glory", voriplasm-like blobs capable of sapping strength.{{ref|name=JAF|{{Cite Book|Journal of Adosi Fevur|ns_base=ON}}}}

Some of the Prince's followers work as plague concoctors, experimenting on the creation and spread of new diseases which they engineer to have specific traits, such as both being nigh incurable and exhibiting symptoms with some delay, in order to help them spread further.{{ref|name=ResearchMortieu|{{Cite Book|Research of Nathien Mortieu}}}} Plague concoctors sometimes give Peryite samples of disease as an offering. These offerings need to be of similar strains, such as [[Lore:Crimson Plague|Crimson Plague]] and [[Lore:Blood Rot|Blood Rot]], as the Taskmaster is said to abhor heterogony.{{ref|name=PlagueInstructions|[[ON:Plague Concoctor's Instructions|Plague Concoctor's Instructions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
