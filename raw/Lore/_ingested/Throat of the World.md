The [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]] (also called the '''Monahven''' (lit. Mother Wind) by the [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]]{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|[[Skyrim:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=HOJG|{{Cite Book|Holdings of Jarl Gjalund}}}} as well as in common parlance,{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}}{{rp|311, 321-328}} '''Mount Hrothgar''',{{Ref|name=FOTG|{{Cite Book|Fable of the Gryphon}}}} '''High Hrothgar''',{{Ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}{{Ref|name=LRPFTA|group=UOL}} or '''Throat Mountain'''){{Ref|name=TSWATD|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Snow Whale and the Dirtbird|ns_base=GEN}}}} is the highest mountain in all of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and one of the tallest peaks in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], second only to [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] in the east. Ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] tradition dictates that [[Lore:Man|man]] was created when the sky breathed on the land through the mountain, hence its name.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} It is because of this, one of Skyrim's nicknames is also the Throat of the World.{{Ref|name=COTS|{{Cite Book|Children of the Sky}}}} The mountain is believed to be one of the mythical [[Lore:The Towers|Towers]] and is known through this legend as the '''Snow Throat'''{{Ref|name=ONTowers|[[Online:The Towers' Remains|The Towers' Remains]] faction quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} or the '''Snow Tower'''.{{Ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}} The Snow-Throat's founding stone is described as "the cave".{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{intnote|Cave|[nb 1]}}<noinclude>

==General Information==
===Cultural Interpretations===
[[File:SR-place-High Hrothgar.jpg|thumb|right|High Hrothgar]]
[[File:SR-place-Throat of the World2.jpg|thumb|left|The Throat of the World's summit]]
The only people known to live on the Throat of the World are the [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]], reclusive Nordic monks that worship [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], from their ancient monastery of [[Lore:High Hrothgar|High Hrothgar]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The mountain itself is sacred to Kyne, as well as [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]], her [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] aspect among the main [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] pantheon. Both are spirits of the winds; their great temple is the sky and the natural world. They were also the ones to gift the ancient Nords speech in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|dragon language]] and the use of their [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]], or more commonly known as Voice.{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{Ref|name=SRArngeir|[[Skyrim:Arngeir|Arngeir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The Nordic myth is that the sky breathed down the mountain, creating man and establishing Skyrim as their original homeland, as opposed to [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]] in the far north. This idea also gives a second meaning to the ancient Nordic stories, the ''[[Lore:Songs of the Return|Songs of the Return]]''.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} It is considered the most sacred mountain in Skyrim, where the ancient [[Lore:Tongues|Tongues]] mastered the voice and defeated the World-Eater, [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]].{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} 

A tradition among [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] is to climb the Throat of the World via the '''Seven Thousand Steps''' to High Hrothgar, a long ascending road that spirals around the mountain, starting from its southeastern base in [[Lore:Ivarstead|Ivarstead]]. Many pilgrims come from all over Skyrim to walk the steps and meditate in absolute silence, reflecting on their life, honoring the Greybeards, or attuning themselves to the sky above.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=SRBarknar|[[Skyrim:Barknar|Barknar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, pilgrims run the risk of encountering beasts and monsters between prayer posts.{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}} Along this path are ten stone etched tablets that detail Skyrim's history with the voice and the Greybeards' history from Jurgen Windcaller to Tiber Septim.{{Ref|name=SRTablets|[[Skyrim:High Hrothgar#Etched Tablets|Etched Tablets]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Its summit was the roost of [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]], Alduin's former lieutenant and later master of the Greybeards.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}{{Ref|name=SRArngeir}} To the broader world, people know him through the rumors of a dragon monk who dwells with the Greybeards on the Throat of the World,{{Ref|name=LADITSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Dragons in the Second Era}}}} but the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] and their modern iteration, the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] had been familiar with him since at least the early Second Era. An established policy was to avoid confrontation with the Greybeards while waiting for the moment to enact justice on him.{{Ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}} The path to the summit is perilous, covered in a neverending blizzard that only wielders of the voice can climb.{{Ref|name=SRArngeir}}

[[Lore:Calcelmo's Stone|Calcelmo's Stone]], a large stone tablet with inscriptions in both [[Lore:Dwemeris|Dwemeris]] and the [[Lore:Falmer Language|Falmer language]] uses the words ''"kemelmzulchond"'' and ''"spantelepe-laelia"'' to translate "snow-throated".{{Ref|name=TOCS|{{Cite Book|Translation of Calcelmo's Stone|ns_base=GEN}}}} As the Snow Tower, the Throat of the World is a vital part in the ancient [[Lore:Prophecy of the Dragonborn|Prophecy of the Dragonborn]], which is sometimes attributed to the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]] or the ancient [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]]. The line, "''when the Snow Tower lies sundered, kingless, bleeding..."'' has been construed to refer to the [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|Skyrim Civil War]], which broke out just when Alduin returned in time and the Last Dragonborn appeared. The next line elaborates further by stating, ''"The World-Eater wakes, and the Wheel turns upon the Last Dragonborn."''{{Ref|name=TBOTD}} [[Lore:Alduin's Wall|Alduin's Wall]], which displays the prophecy in full, features the Throat of the World split or sundered in half.{{Ref|name=SRWall|[[:File:SR-misc-Alduin's Wall.jpg|Alduin's Wall]] – The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim}} A piece of the [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]], created by the former [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] king, [[Lore:Anumaril|Anumaril]], is based on the Snow-Throat.{{Ref|name=SOT|{{Cite Book|Staff of Towers (book)}}}} As with all Towers, the Throat of the World is said to exist beyond the physical and in some sense apart, or detached, from ordinary space and time.{{ref|name=ONCelarus|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

===Geography and Ecology===
[[File:SR-place-South Skybound Watch.jpg|thumb|left|South Skybound Watch]][[File:LO-map-Skyrim (PGE1).jpg|thumb|right|Map of Skyrim, with Hrothgar in its center]]
Though the Greybeards are neutral in Skyrim's politics, High Hrothgar and the entire Throat of the World fall under [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun Hold]]'s borders, even when the only entrance to the Seven Thousand Steps is in [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]]. However, both High Hrothgar and [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] city are visible from each other on a clear day.{{Ref|name=SRThroat|[[Skyrim:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} More settlements are found along its base, such as [[Lore:Riverwood|Riverwood]] on the eastern valley of Whiterun Hold. There are other notable landmarks like the [[Lore:Standing Stones#Standing Stones|Ritual Stone]] on the [[Lore:White River|White River]], Orphan Rock, a secluded grove to the south, Skybound Watch Pass, a small Nordic ruin overlooking Helgen and Riverwood,{{Ref|name=SRSkybound|[[Skyrim:Skybound Watch Pass|Skybound Watch Pass]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and Hillgrund's Tomb, a Nordic family crypt in [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]].{{Ref|name=SRThroat}}

The Throat of the World is surrounded by several mountains, but mostly separated by valleys, like the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] to the south, split by the [[Lore:Lion's Den|Lion's Den]] valley, the [[Lore:Brittleshin Hills|Brittleshin Hills]] and [[Lore:Shearpoint|Shearpoint]] to the west and north respectively, divided by the White River valley, and [[Lore:Ysgramor's Teeth|Ysgramor's Teeth]] by the waterfalls flowing from [[Lore:Lake Geir|Lake Geir]] to the [[Lore:Darkwater River|Darkwater River]].{{Ref|name=SRThroat}} Somewhere around the mountain is the long never-before-seen valley of waterfalls called Vakkervanir, initially named by [[Lore:Waughin Jarth|Waughin Jarth]]. Mount Einar is another neighboring mountain.{{Ref|name=LRPFTA|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/From the Ashes|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 14/From the Ashes – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}}

Underneath the mountain are several cave systems, such as the White River Watch, a common bandit holdout near [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]], Honeystrand Cave near the Rift's southwest crossroad,{{Ref|name=SRHoneystrand|[[Skyrim:Honeystrand Cave|Honeystrand Cave]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and [[Lore:Pinepeak Cavern|Pinepeak Cavern]], a safe place for locals in Ivarstead and the tomb of [[Lore:Hakra|Hakra]], one of the [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] and a protector-spirit of the Rift.{{Ref|name=ONPinepeak|[[Online:Pinepeak Cavern|Pinepeak Cavern]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONNetapatuu|[[Online:Netapatuu|Netapatuu]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The [[Lore:Bat#Snow Throat Fruit Bat|Snow Throat Fruit Bat]] is a pure-white native species that blends easily in the winter weather.{{Ref|name=ONBat|[[Online:Snow Throat Fruit Bat|Snow Throat Fruit Bat]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} All kinds of magical and rare phenomena occur on the mountain. On the mountain's peak is a [[Lore:Time Wound|Time Wound]], an anomaly where [[Lore:Time|time]] is broken. It was there, the ancient Nordic tongues defeated Alduin and sent him forward in time.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} The snow on the mountain is magically infused and was used to create the legendary bottle, the [[Lore:White Phial|White Phial]].{{Ref|name=SRNurelion|[[Skyrim:Nurelion|Nurelion]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Frostheart Blossom|Frostheart Blossoms]] are rare, blue flowers that only grow in Skyrim's snow-blown country and mountains like the Throat of the World.{{Ref|name=ONMizrali|[[Online:Mizrali|Mizrali]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The path up the mountain is home to dangerous creatures such as [[Lore:Ice Wolf|ice wolves]] and [[Lore:Frost Troll|frost trolls]].{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} [[Lore:Ice Wraith|Ice Wraiths]], a [[Lore:Nature Spirit|nature spirit]] made out of pure ice, live on the mountain,{{Ref|name=ONWraith|[[Online:High Hrothgar Wraith|High Hrothgar Wraith]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} especially higher towards the peak.{{Ref|name=SRWraith|[[Skyrim:Ice Wraith|Ice Wraith]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==History==
===Ancient History===
{{Quote Box|''"Hold, Alduin on the Wing! Sister Hawk, grant us your sacred breath to make this contract heard! Begone, World-Eater! By words with older bones than your own we break your perch on this age and send you out! You are banished! Alduin, we shout you out from all our endings unto the last!"''|Felldir's chant to banish Alduin}}
[[File:SR-npc-Jurgen Windcaller.jpg|thumb|left|Jurgen Windcaller, also called Jurgen the Calm]]
Snow-Throat's origin lies in the myths surrounding the Towers. From the speculative thoughts of Lady [[Lore:Cinnabar of Taneth|Cinnabar]] and other sources, when the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] created the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], the physical flesh of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] hung on a skeleton of joints, with each joint radiating a palpable reality. The first point became the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] and its founding stone was the Zero Stone. With its creation, the [[Lore:Aldmeri|Aldmeri]] of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] saw to emulate it and create their own towers.{{Ref|name=SCAS|{{Cite Book|Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation}}}}{{Ref|name=NMI4|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Mantia Intercept, Letter Four|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{Ref|name=NMI8|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Mantia Intercept, Letter Eight|ns_base=GEN}}}}

However, the creation of multiple towers suggests that the Aldmer did not cooperate together.{{Ref|name=SCAS}}{{Ref|name=NMI4|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=NMI8|group=UOL}} Some towers are artifical constructs, but others like Snow Throat are natural.{{Ref|name=SRThroat}} Using these towers, the elves shaped the land and conformed it to their desires.{{Ref|name=SCAS}} Each of the towers are associated with one of the modern elven races, like the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] and the [[Lore:Crystal-Like-Law|Crystal-Like-Law]], or the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] and the Red-Heart,{{Ref|name=AE4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} but Snow Throat has no confirmed association. The ancient [[Lore:Snow Elves|Falmer]], however, lived in Skyrim before Ysgramor arrived in Tamriel.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=SRGelebor|[[Skyrim:Knight-Paladin Gelebor|Knight-Paladin Gelebor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}

Before the Greybeards lived on the Throat of the World, its summit had been visited since the days of the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]]. A [[Lore:Word Walls|word wall]] buried in snow is the only structure at the top of the mountain.{{Ref|name=SRFire|[[Skyrim:Fire Breath|Fire Breath]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Walls like this were created by the ancient Nords in the Dragon language.{{Ref|name=DLMNM|{{Cite Book|Dragon Language: Myth no More}}}} The [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] of the late Merethic Era, when the ancient Nords rebelled against their dragon overlords, ended with Alduin's defeat on the Throat of the World.{{Ref|name=TOTDC|{{Cite Book|Temples of the Dragon Cult}}}} The tongue warrior, [[Lore:Felldir the Old|Felldir the Old]] devised a plan to bring Alduin to the mountain by issuing a challenge and sending him forward in time with an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]].{{Ref|name=SRBane|[[Skyrim:Alduin's Bane|Alduin's Bane]] story quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} With his defeat, a time-wound was left in its place. Alduin's brother and lieutenant, Paarthurnax, decided to roost on the battlefield, waiting for the day of his return.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}

After the [[Lore:Conquest of Morrowind|Conquest of Morrowind]] in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]],{{intnote|End|[nb 2]}} one of the most powerful [[Lore:Tongues|tongues]] in the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|Nordic Empire]], [[Lore:Jurgen Windcaller|Jurgen Windcaller]] began contemplating on the thu'um and how it failed them in [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]]. Jurgen began his seven-year meditation and sat silently, eventually developing his new philosophy, the [[Lore:Way of the Voice|Way of the Voice]]. Part of this creed was denouncing the thu'um's use in war. Seventeen tongues, who disputed his way of thinking, challenged him and failed. With Jurgen as their leader, they built High Hrothgar high on the mountain and sat in silence for many years.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=SRTablets}} Paarthurnax also began to study the Way of the Voice and used his mastery to overcome his innate dominate nature and teach future generations of Greybeards.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}{{Ref|name=SRArngeir}}

The seven thousand steps were built from the base next to Ivarstead to High Hrothgar. A local in town will deliver food to High Hrothgar and regularly walk the steps.{{Ref|name=SRKlimmek|[[Skyrim:Klimmek|Klimmek]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} One of High King [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]]'s many deeds to the Old Gods was repairing the 418th step toward High Hrothgar after it was damaged by a dragon.{{Ref|name=FSOKW}}

===The Interregnum and beyond===
[[File:SR-concept-Shrine Of Talos.jpg|thumb|left|A Shrine of Talos]]
[[File:LG-cardart-Duel Atop the World 02.jpg|thumb|right|Alduin and Paarthurnax's duel atop the world]]
As the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] occurred in {{Year|2E 572}}, the newly-named King of [[Lore:Eastern Skyrim|Eastern Skyrim]], [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] stealthily left [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] traveled up the Seven Thousand Steps to speak with the Greybeards in High Hrothgar.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} Scholars such as [[Lore:Yngmaer Raven-Quill|Yngmaer Raven-Quill]] of [[Lore:Western Skyrim|Western Skyrim]] were suspicious of these claims and sought funding from High King [[Lore:Svargrim|Svargrim]] to investigate.{{Ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, an avalanche blocked the steps and prevented anyone from moving between High Hrothgar and Ivarstead.{{Ref|name=ONMizrali}}

As it goes in legend, before he was named [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|Emperor]] and created the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], Tiber Septim climbed the steps and entered High Hrothgar. He came answering the Greybeards' summons, the first time they spoke in years since Jurgen the Calm's time.{{Ref|name=SRTablets}} According to ''The Song of Tiber Septim'', this was after the [[Lore:Battle of Old Hroldan|Battle of Old Hroldan]], the event that revealed his ability to use the thu'um, and shortly before he left for [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] with King [[Lore:Cuhlecain|Cuhlecain]].{{Ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the Greybeards summoned the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] with their voice after they absorbed their first [[Lore:Dragon Souls|dragon soul]]. The warrior traveled to Ivarstead and climbed the steps to reach High Hrothgar.{{Ref|name=SRWay|[[Skyrim:The Way of the Voice|The Way of the Voice]] story quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In time, the hero learned about the Dragonrend shout and further scaled the mountain to its pinnacle where they met Paarthurnax.{{Ref|name=SRWorld|[[Skyrim:The Throat of the World|The Throat of the World]] story quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

Paarthurnax assisted the Dragonborn on their quest to fulfill the [[Lore:Prophecy of the Dragonborn|prophecy]] and with his guidance, the hero used an Elder Scroll on the Time-Wound to see into the past and learn the Dragonrend shout. Only moments after learning the shout, Alduin confronted the Dragonborn and Paarthurnax on the summit. Alduin, on the verge of his defeat, fled to [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]] to swallow more [[Lore:Souls|souls]] and regain his power.{{Ref|name=SRBane}}

The cave of Monahven was used as a sanctuary by [[Lore:Oviron Salith|Oviron Salith]] and the Chosen of Boethiah, where they opened a portal to [[Lore:Attribution's Share|Attribution's Share]] -- the realm of Prince [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]].{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|''Skyrim — The Adventure Game'']]}} The cave had numerous entrances throughout the immediate region surrounding Monahven.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}}
