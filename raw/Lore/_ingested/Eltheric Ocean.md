The [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] is a vast body of water encompassing the seas west of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. It meets the [[Lore:Padomaic Ocean|Padomaic Ocean]] at two points within the Padomaic: the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] in the north{{Ref|name=ESOContraband|[[Online:Treasures F#Fine Ceramic Leviathan Pitcher|Fine Ceramic Leviathan Pitcher]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=BtGB|{{Cite Book|Beware the Glenumbra Banks}}}} and the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]] in the south.{{intnote|BlueDivide|[nb 1]}} Its waters touch at least four continents: [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] to the east, [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] to the west,{{ref|name=Map|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel]]}} [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]],{{ref|name=Pearls|[[ON:Sea-Serpent's Coil#Black Eltheric Pearls|Black Eltheric Pearls]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} and, allegedly, [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]] to the south.{{ref|name=Seat|[[ON:Seat of the Snow Prince|Seat of the Snow Prince]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}

==History==
===Origins===
According to myth, during the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], shortly after the creation of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] had interspersed seas, but no oceans. After the war between the Wandering and Old [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]], the land was reshaped, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans, forming the Eltheric.{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}

===Exploration===
In the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Early Merethic Era|Early Merethic Era]],{{ref|name=AE12|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored, Chapter XII}}}} Aldmeris suffered a catastrophe, leaving the continent doomed.{{ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The native [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] fled across the Eltheric,{{ref|name=Seat}} then inhabited extensively by the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]],{{ref|name=TLoF|{{Cite Book|The Lay of Firsthold}}}} before making land on northern [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]] at Nine Prows Landing.{{ref|name=AE12}} Later, in the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Middle Merethic Era|Middle Merethic Era]], [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]], along with Illio and an unknown third captain, sailed out from Firsthold in search of Aldmeris. Using navigational devices known as [[Lore:Waystone|waystones]], the three set sail in various directions, each heading out into the Eltheric Ocean. Illio's sailed southward toward Pyandonea, whereas the unknown ship sailed northwest, toward Thras and Yokuda.{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}}

Topal sailed northeast, coincidentally taking the longest path possible through the Abecean Sea without striking land. Eventually, after entering the Iliac Bay, Topal made land - near what would later be known as [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]] - after a two-month voyage. From there, Topal charted and mapped the coast of Tamriel, continuing in the Eltheric along [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] before entering the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]]. Topal would later return to Firsthold with treasures, the only of the three Aldmeris-bound vessels to return, sparking wonder in the faraway land{{ref|name=FOTN}} and prompting other Aldmeri sailors to follow in his footsteps.{{ref|name=BtAoM}} Among these subsequent Aldmer explorers was [[Lore:Direnni Cygnus|Direnni Cygnus]], who discovered the Isle of Balfiera and Adamantine Tower.{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}}{{ref|name=BtAoM}}

Around {{Year|1E 330}}, the [[Lore:Druids of Galen|Druids of Galen]], after being deemed a target by virtually every faction in northwestern Tamriel, fled their native province of High Rock. According to some legends, the druids may have been following a song that the elder druids heard on the wind, while others attest that the Druids navigated by way of an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]]. When the druids made land, they found themselves on the island of [[Lore:Y'ffelon|Y'ffelon]] before spreading to the nearby islands.{{ref|name=SH|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}}{{ref|name=SoA|{{Cite Book|Secrets of Amenos}}}}

Many dissidents of the Yokudan Empire during the [[Lore:Sword-singers|Singer]] Period sought refuge on the countless rocky islands east of the continent. Squatters that had already inhabited the islands, some being remnants of the Lefthand Empire, were forced to vacate to evade the incoming refugees. Some sailed south toward Summerset and some sailed toward the Iliac Bay, but those who found the most success were those that arrived at the Systres, aiming to - and eventually succeeding in - conquering the archipelago. However, the Lefthanded reign of the islands was relatively short-lived.{{ref|name=SH}} The first [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] warrior-wave from Yokuda, leaving their homeland following the [[Lore:War of the Singers|War of the Singers]],{{ref|name=RTHaTH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}} arrived on the Systres in {{Year|1E 785}} and annihilated the remaining Lefthander forces before continuing east across the ocean to [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=SH}}

During the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], several expeditions into the Eltheric Ocean were conducted. One such journey was led by Captain [[Lore:Wereshark|Wereshark]], under the employ of Duke Gignac of [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]]. The crew set out to plunder the isles of Pyandonea, eventually arriving at the archipelago and discovering [[Lore:Sea Serpent|sea serpents]], [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] settlements, and previously unknown races of [[Lore:Lizard Bull|lizard bulls]].{{ref|name=TSoCW|{{Cite Book|The Saga of Captain Wereshark}}}} The famous [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] merchant-explorer Captain Margaux sailed into the Sea of Pearls on her ship, the ''Golden [[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggan]]''. By {{Year|2E 582}}, she had been declared missing and her house was up for sale.{{ref|[[Online:Captain Margaux's Place|Captain Margaux's Place]] loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} In the same year, the infamous Graven Deep was explored by Dhulef, a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] sailor, who discovered an uncharted island within the region.{{ref|name=Dhulef}}

==Composition==
The Eltheric Ocean contains a variety of currents of both wind and sea, such as the maritime Herne Current, which runs offshore from High Rock, and the western-originating Yokudan Zephyr, which steadily but slowly passes over the Glenumbra Banks in the summer.{{ref|name=BtGB|{{Cite Book|Beware the Glenumbra Banks}}}} The waters of the Eltheric are described as warm, particularly when contrasted with the frigid waters of the Sea of Ghosts.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Passenger's Log: Disaster at Sea|ns_base=ON}}}} While the Abecean Sea is calm, the neighboring Cursed Sea is prone to rolling waves capable of afflicting seasoned sailors with sea sickness.{{ref|name=JoT2|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei, Part Two}}}} The Thrassian Archipelago within the Cursed Sea surrounds a brackish lagoon.{{ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}}

In the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], within Cursed Sea - the waters that surround Thras - a magical whirlpool larger than [[Lore:Alten Corimont|Alten Corimont]] tore ships into a portal to [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]]. It was capable of suddenly spinning up before ships could react.{{ref|name=1I|[[ON:Captain One-Eye|Captain One-Eye]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Lost Depths|Lost Depths]]''}} A very similar whirlpool, known as the Maelstrom of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Bal]], was said to exist within the Abecean Sea,{{ref|name=TDoO|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}} where some sources place Thras.{{ref|name=TMotSS|{{Cite Book|The Myth of the Sea Sloads}}}} Maormer have referred to a mysterious Mother Sea,{{ref|[[ON:Castire|Castire]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} but it is unknown if the term refers to a body of water.

==Fauna==
[[File:ON-crown store-Aquatic Splendor Furnishing Pack.jpg|Aquatic life found in the Eltheric Ocean (ESO)|thumb|right]]
The Eltheric Ocean contains many varieties of [[Lore:Fish|fish]], including the [[Lore:Ray|ray]],{{ref|name=CM64|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 64: Pyandonean Style}}}} [[Lore:Abecean Halibut|Abecean halibut]], [[Lore:Grouper|Eltheric and dusky groupers]], [[Lore:Flounder|Azurain and Stormhaven flounders]], [[Lore:Sole|finless sole]], [[Lore:Tuna|tuna]], [[Lore:Pike|Alcaire pike]], [[Lore:Dab|dab]], [[Lore:Cod|Northpoint cod]], [[Lore:Snakehead|snakehead]], [[Lore:Hake|hake]], [[Lore:Bonefish|bonefish]], [[Lore:Alewife|alewife]], [[Lore:Driftfish|driftfish]], [[Lore:Sablefish|sablefish]], [[Lore:Rattail|scorpionfish rattail]], [[Lore:Sparking Anglermouth|sparking anglermouth]], [[Lore:Crestfish|crestfish]], [[Lore:Bluespotted Cornetfish|bluespotted cornetfish]], [[Lore:Daggerfish|daggerfish]], [[Lore:Gonfalon Rockfish|Gonfalon rockfish]], [[Lore:Lingweloce|lingweloce]], [[Lore:Radiant Dory|radiant dory]], [[Lore:Slaughterfish|slaughterfish]], and [[Lore:Quicksilver Lingwe|quicksilver lingwe]].{{ref|name=ESOFish|[[ON:Fishing|Fish]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Shells known as [[Lore:Ammonite|ammonites]], derived from an unknown, extinct species, were found throughout the Eltheric in the Second Era.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mysteries of the Eltheric Ammonite}}}} In the center of the Thrassian Archipelago, known as the [[Lore:Coral|Coral]] Kingdoms,{{ref|name=PGE1TWR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}} a network of intricate coral reefs can be found.{{ref|name=PGE3OL}} Abnormally large [[Lore:Nautilus|nautiluses]] were located in the waters surrounding Thras in the Second Era. It was theorized that the abundant resources of Agonio, the largest island within the archipelago, allowed them to grow to larger sizes.{{ref|name=SA|[[Online:Thrassian Stranglers#Nautilus Shell Guards|Nautilus Shell Guards]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Among the larger aquatic animals that prowl the Eltheric include [[Lore:Shark|sharks]] - scrappy, resilient hunters{{ref|[[Online:Gaze of Sithis#Pad-Sa Shark Teeth|Pad-Sa Shark Teeth]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} who use infamous circling tactics against their prey.{{ref|name=ASGTSE|{{Cite Book|A Sailor's Guide to Sea Elves}}}}{{ref|name=ESOFish}} [[Lore:Whale|Whales]] can be found in the Abecean Sea and are hunted for their oils.{{ref|name=WBG|
[[Online:Treasures W#Whalebone Gaff|Whalebone Gaff]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Treasures A#Abecean Whale Buckle Wax|Abecean Whale Buckle Wax]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Dolphin|Dolphins]] can also be found in the Abecean.{{ref|Appearance of [[RG:Dolphin|Dolphins]] in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} In ancient folk-lore, killer whales capable of pulling and steering ships through the ocean were tamed by the [[Lore:Seamount Clan|Seamount Clan]] of [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] and trained as war mounts.{{Ref|name=ONSiege|[[Online:Betnikh Antiquities#Orcish Siege Gear|Orcish Siege Gear]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Sea serpents, native to Pyandonea, can be found as far north as the Abecean Sea and are capable of standing 64 feet above the water.{{ref|name=RGArchive|[[General:Redguard.com Archive/Inside the Game|Game Insider]] on [[RG:Redguard|''Redguard'']]'s old site}} [[Lore:Porpoise|Porpoises]] can be found in the waters of Pyandonea, hunted by the Maormer out of sheer hatred for the blissful creatures' exuberance and joy.{{ref|name=CM64}}

Some creatures prowl both the Eltheric and land. Among these are the [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]], who inhabit the Abecean and Iliac, losing their intelligence when they migrate to land and regaining it upon returning to the ocean.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Revolting Life Cycle of the Dreugh}}}} Though unrelated to the Dreugh, the similarly crustacean [[Lore:Hadolid|Hadolids]] migrate between sea and land in decade-long rotations.{{ref|name=HRJ}} [[Lore:Sload|Sea Sload]] come from the underwater kingdom of [[Lore:Ul'vor Kus|Ul'vor Kus]], located in the Sea of Pearls, and occasionally invade Summerset.{{ref|name=TMotSS|{{Cite Book|The Myth of the Sea Sloads}}}} [[Lore:Yaghra|Yaghra]], who take various forms, serve the Sea Sload{{ref|name=ESODS|[[Online:Dominion Soldier#Summerset|Dominion Soldiers]]' dialogue during [[ON:Encounters#Summerset|Random Encounters]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} and have assisted them in their land assaults.{{ref|[[ON:Yaghra|Yaghra]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} [[Lore:Ornaug|Ornaugs]] take to the seas not for food, but only to reach the littoral regions of new reefs or islands.{{ref|name=EotO|{{Cite Book|Ecology of the Ornaug}}}}

===Creatures of Legend===
Though thought to be a myth by some, veteran sailors have attested that [[Lore:Ithguleoir|Ithguleoir]], an immortal leviathan, haunts the depths of the Eltheric Ocean, dining on its sailors one by one. He is described as having an oily back and blowing sea-mist like a whale, releasing a foul stench akin to those from Oblivion. It is reportedly intelligent, setting traps in the [[Lore:Glenumbra Banks|Glenumbra Banks]] designed to run ships ashore.{{ref|name=BtGB|{{Cite Book|Beware the Glenumbra Banks}}}} Pyandonea is said to provide the perfect environment for southern water spirits.{{ref|name=PGE1TWR}}

During the Thrassian War,{{ref|name=HRJ|{{Cite Book|Hadolid Researcher's Journal}}}} the Sload of Thras released huge sea-monsters to attack the Systres Archipelago. The mightiest of these beasts was the [[Lore:Molmor|Molmor]], variously described as a massive whale or an octopus with grasping tentacles, with an array of long, sharp spines and dripping, slimy skin. It was said to turn the water it swam in black and boiled, capable of heaving itself ashore and crushing everything in its path. It ravaged the coast of the island of [[Lore:Galen|Galen]] for seven years before it was killed. Its remains were overtaken by [[Lore:The Green|the Green]] and became [[Lore:Molmor Islet|Molmor Islet]].{{ref|name=BatM|{{Cite Book|Belaigh and the Molmor}}}} During the Interregnum, old sailors would swap stories about the mysterious Graven Deep. Among the explanations for the disappearance of ships within the region was a giant whale.{{ref|name=Dhulef|[[ON:Dhulef|Dhulef]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Lost Depths|Lost Depths]]''}}
