# Lore:Dragon Priest

'''Dragon Priests''' were originally members of the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] of ancient [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. Many of them have achieved a state of [[Lore:Lich|lichdom]], and retained much of their power through the eternal worship of their [[Lore:Draugr|draugr]] servants.{{ref|name=ATD|{{Cite Book|Amongst the Draugr}}}}{{Ref|name=SRValdar|[[Skyrim:Valdar|Valdar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} When they were living, they ruled over countless armies of men in their [[Lore:Dragon|gods]]' names. They were powerful mages that could use the [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]],{{Ref|name=TGATT|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}} and ruled with power equal to that of kings.{{Ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} They are now found residing in coffins throughout areas of prominent [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]] worship; some have been sealed away and imprisoned using sacred fires maintained by the priests of [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]].{{Ref|name=TDoM|{{Cite Book|The Danger of Morokei}}}}

If their sleep is disturbed, Dragon Priests were known to burst out of their coffins with considerable force and prepare to destroy whomsoever intruded upon their resting place. In undeath, they guard the secrets of the [[Lore:Word Walls|Word Wall]] jealously.{{Ref|name=MorbiusTwitter|[[General:Twitter_Archive#@Modiphius|Modiphius: Call to Arms - ''Domain of the Dragon Priest'' Bundle]]}} The highest-ranking priests of the Dragon Cult were granted magical [[Lore:Dragon Priest Masks|masks]] that defy the laws of time and possess powerful enchantments.{{Ref|name=SRLoad|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Skyrim's History|Skyrim's History]] loading screen text in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The individual masks are made of varying materials, and each bears the same name as the Dragon Priest that possessed it.{{ref|name=MP|{{Cite Book|Morokei's Power|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=DPMFIS|[[Skyrim:Dragon Priest Mask|Dragon Priest Masks]] with distinctive materials and enchantments in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Similar to their brothers in Skyrim, the Dragons of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] recruited faithful mortals of the [[Lore:New Moon Cult|New Moon Cult]] to serve as Dragon Priests.

==Known Dragon Priests==
===Dragon Cult===
*[[Lore:Ahzidal|Ahzidal]], the first great [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] enchanter.
*Akiirdal, buried in the Howling Sepulchers of [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]].
*[[Lore:Arthosiis|Arthosiis]], who was banished from [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and led his followers to [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]].
*Dukaan, who was entombed in White Ridge Barrow on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]].
*Haldriin, who was entombed in Skyshroud Barrow on [[Lore:Bleakrock Isle|Bleakrock Isle]].
*Hevnoraak, who planned his resurrection obsessively before dying, and was interred in [[Lore:Valthume|Valthume]]. His plans were prevented by a Nord named [[Lore:Valdar|Valdar]]; even in death, his spirit ensured that Hevnoraak remained sealed within the tomb.
*Korthor, who was entombed in Vuldngrav in Skyrim, possessed the [[Lore:Fork of Horripilation|Fork of Horripilation]] in {{Year|2E 582}}.
*[[Lore:Krosis|Krosis]], who was entombed atop [[Lore:Shearpoint|Shearpoint]] mountain.
*Magrathi, a high priest who betrayed [[Lore:Sivdur|Sivdur]] and slew his brother over a property dispute, causing Sivdur to curse his skull out of revenge. He was interred at Shroud Hearth Barrow.{{ref|name=SRERN|{{Cite Book|Eranya's Research Notes|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}
*[[Lore:Miraak|Miraak]], the first [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]].
*[[Lore:Morokei|Morokei]], who was entombed in [[Lore:Labyrinthian|Labyrinthian]]. Morokei obtained the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]] sometime in the Fourth Era, which brought him into contact with a group of mages from the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]]. The future Arch-Mage, [[Lore:Savos Aren|Savos Aren]], was the only member of the group to survive the venture into Labyrinthian. Aren sacrificed two of his companions to contain Morokei, and sealed the ruins. In {{Year|4E 201}}, following the activation of the [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]], the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was sent to Labyrinthian by the College to retrieve the Staff, and Morokei was destroyed.
*Nahkriin, who was tasked with presiding over [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin's]] portal to [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]] at [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]].{{ref|[[Skyrim:Nahkriin|Nahkriin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
*[[Lore:Otar the Mad|Otar]], who was interred in [[Lore:Ragnvald|Ragnvald]], his tomb was sealed with two keys guarded by draugr.
*[[Lore:Rahgot|Rahgot]], who was entombed in [[Lore:Forelhost|Forelhost]]
*Tulnir, who was entombed in [[Lore:Labyrinthian|Labyrinthian]].
*Vahlok the Jailor, buried within his tomb on Solstheim. He was once the ruler of Solstheim, tasked with watching over the traitor, Miraak, after discovering Miraak's plot against their masters and defeating him in an epic battle.
*Vokun, entombed in [[Lore:High Gate Ruins|High Gate Ruins]].
*Volsung, entombed in [[Lore:Volskygge|Volskygge]].
*Vosis, entombed in Forelhost, in {{Year|2E 582}}, Vosis was in possession of a shard of [[Lore:Wuuthrad|Wuuthrad]].
*Zaan, who followed the dragon [[Lore:Thurvokun|Thurvokun]]. Killed by her followers, she was resurrected by [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] and gifted with [[Lore:Spellbreaker|Spellbreaker]], becoming a renegade dragon priest.
*Zahkriisos, entombed in Bloodskal Barrow on Solstheim, he was a member of the Bloodskal Clan.

===New Moon Cult===
*[[Online:Ra'khajin|Ra'khajin]], the Khajiiti Dragon Priest of Laatvulon.
