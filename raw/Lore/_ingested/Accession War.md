The [[Lore:Accession War|Accession War]], more commonly known as the [[Lore:Accession War|Argonian Invasion]]{{ref|name=DBDreyla|[[Skyrim:Dreyla Alor|Dreyla Alor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} was a war waged by the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] [[Lore:An-Xileel|An-Xileel]] against the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] [[Lore:Great Houses|Houses]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] in the immediate aftermath of the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] of {{Year|4E 5}}.{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}} The invasion devastated much of southern{{ref|name=DBDreyla}} and eastern{{ref|name=LTJ|{{Cite Book|Lymdrenn Tenvanni's Journal}}}} Morrowind, including the capital city of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], and almost led to the complete destruction of [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]]{{ref|name=LTJ}} and temporary occupation of much of Morrowind.{{ref|name=DBAdril}} The war would compound the ongoing alteration of the political dynamics of Morrowind and result in the solidification of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]] as the dominant power in Morrowind.</onlyinclude>

==Background==
For millennia, the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] and their later Dunmer descendants had enslaved the Argonians of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] for use in the slave trade and as cheap labor,{{ref|name=MurkmireQnA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive: Murkmire Q&A}}}} frequently going on slave raids deep into the province with the assistance of collaborationist [[Lore:Archein|Archeins]]. This was a traditional part of Dunmer society dating back to the time of the Chimer{{ref|name=Utha|[[Online:Ix-Utha|Ix-Utha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and was later guaranteed to them by the [[Lore:Armistice|Treaty of the Armistice]]. [[Lore: House Dres|House Dres]] in particular cemented themselves as the linchpin of the Argonian slave trade, though House Telvanni, [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] and [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] also heavily utilized [[Lore:Slavery|slavery]].{{ref|name=Brallion|[[Morrowind:Brallion|Brallion]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Telvanni, in particular, had refused to join the Second Era [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] in protest of the contemporary abolition of slavery.{{ref|name=PP:C|{{Cite Book|Pact Pamphlet: Congratulations!}}}} As a result of this, Argonians and Dunmer have a long-standing and bitter hatred of one another{{ref|name=provinces|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}}.

During the [[Lore:Third Era Wars#The Arnesian War|Arnesian War]] of {{Year|3E 396}},{{ref|name=Timeline|{{Cite Book|The Third Era Timeline}}}} Morrowind invaded Black Marsh. The war began when an Argonian slave uprising in the south against House Dres resulted in the murder of [[Lore:Roris|Saint Roris]] at the hands of Argonian rebels, which led to his posthumous canonization.{{Ref|name=Saints|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Saints}}}} The war was very successful for Morrowind, which was able to expand its territory south into [[Lore:Arnesia|Arnesia]].{{ref|name=PGE3MO|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}} However, following the Arnesian War, the Argonians began to slowly retake the lands lost to them during the war.{{Ref|name=PGE3AR|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Argonia}}}} This was not the first time Dunmer rule had extended into northern Black Marsh, the region of Arnesia was originally settled by the [[Lore:Cantemiric Velothi|Cantemiric Velothi]] before they were wiped out by the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]],{{Ref|name=PGE3AR}} and in the mid-Second Era the cities of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] and [[Lore:Thorn|Thorn]] had been under Dunmer rule.{{ref|name=SCOS|{{Cite Book|Stormhold, City of Shadowfen}}}}{{ref|name=SFLS|[[Online:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Keshu|{{Cite Book|Keshu}}}}{{ref|name=FAtS|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}}

In the decades following the Arnesian War, successive catastrophes struck Morrowind. In {{Year|3E 427}} the [[Lore:The Blight|Blight]] worsened and spread across [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]],{{ref|name=morro|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] collapsed in the wake of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]]'s deaths and disappearances.{{ref|name=morro}}{{ref|name=Galsa|[[Tribunal:Galsa Andrano|Galsa Andrano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}{{ref|name=OBRumors|[[Oblivion:Radiant Conversations|Rumors]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 1: The Trial of Vivec|The Trial of Vivec]]}} Civil War broke out between the Great Houses in response to [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|King Helseth]]'s abolition of slavery in {{Year|3E 430}},{{ref|name=PGE3MO}}{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 12: Playing Houses|Playing Houses]]}} and in {{Year|3E 433}} the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] hit Morrowind particularly hard.{{ref|name=DBAdril}} Among the losses was the city of [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]], the Redoran seat of power in Vvardenfell district, which was destroyed by the invading [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].{{ref|name=RavenRock|{{Cite Book|History of Raven Rock}}}} Simultaneously, armies of [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] with [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] mercenaries invaded [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|from the northwest]] into the territory of House Redoran.{{ref|name=OBRumors}} Finally, in {{Year|4E 5}} the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] occurred when [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]] destroyed [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] and triggered the eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], causing widespread destruction across the whole of the province. During these disasters and in the wake of the Empire's departure from Morrowind, House Redoran restored their previously disbanded military and mobilized a [[Lore:Redoran Guard|standing army]] with which to defend themselves.{{ref|name=DBAdril}}.

Meanwhile, the Argonians had gained strength. The [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] had accurately predicted the incoming Oblivion Crisis and summoned many Argonians back to Black Marsh,{{ref|name=OBRumors}} altering their physiology to make them more powerful warriors,{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}} capable of defending Black Marsh from the armies of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]]. The Hist's plans were wildly successful, forcing the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] to close the gates they opened in Black Marsh to end the onslaught.{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook}} Despite the success of the Hist's defense, the fanatical An-Xileel party took credit for the organization of the defense of Black Marsh. With most Argonians believing the An-Xileel were responsible for their salvation, the An-Xileel successfully united most of the Argonian people under their rule. The An-Xileel then turned their wrath on the Empire, declaring Black Marsh independent and sacking Imperial colonies in the province.{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook}} While the abolition of slavery in Morrowind did improve relations somewhat, tensions still ran hot.{{Ref|name=PGE3AR}}.

==The Invasion==
[[File:LO-map-Morrowind (Anthology).jpg|thumb|left|Map of Morrowind]]
The precise reasons for the Argonian Invasion are unknown. Speculation ranges from revenge for millennia of slavery and oppression by the Dunmer,{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}}{{ref|name=DBAdril}} to a premeditated plan by the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]]{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=books}}}} to expansionist opportunism against the weakened Dunmer by the Argonians of the An-Xileel, who were at the time in a position of strength,{{ref|name=DBAdril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} to even incitement by the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] in an effort to weaken the Empire and its relations with Morrowind,{{ref|name=risingthreat|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} or a combination of these factors.

The Argonians first launched their invasion sometime in the months following the Red Year. The city of Mournhold was sacked during the course of the war,{{ref|name=DBAdril}} and the Argonian advance reached as far as the southern coast of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] and the [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]] where Vivec City once stood, there they performed rituals of unknown purpose.{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook}} By the middle of {{Year|4E 6}} the Argonians had pushed deep into the territory of House Telvanni in the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|northeast of the province]] and threatened the very existence of the House.{{ref|name=LTJ}} It was only through the efforts of the newly re-established army of House Redoran that the complete occupation of Morrowind was prevented and the invasion halted.{{ref|name=DBAdril}} At some point between {{Year|4E 6}} and {{Year|4E 28}}<ref group="note">The Argonian advance is described as ending decades before {{Year|4E 48}} which means at minimum twenty years.</ref> the Argonians stopped advancing. Since then the Argonians have shown no interest in conquering any more land from the Dunmer.{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}} The invasion cost thousands of Dunmer their lives, and many became refugees that fled west to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] over land{{ref|name=refugeesrest|[[Skyrim:Refugees' Rest|Refugees' Rest]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and sea.{{ref|name=brandshei|[[Skyrim:Brand-Shei|Brand-Shei]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Aftermath==
[[File:SR-place-Wreck of The Pride of Tel Vos.jpg|thumb|right|The wreck of ''The Pride of Tel Vos'', a refugee ship]]
In the immediate aftermath of the invasion, the An-Xileel continued to occupy southern Morrowind; with some tribes settling the land.{{ref|name=DBDreyla}} By {{Year|4E 48}} the Argonians still occupied territory as far as southern Vvardenfell and the Scathing Bay{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}} and prevented both Morrowind and the Empire from reclaiming it.{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=books}}}} The invasion compounded the refugee crisis that had started during the Red Year, and as a consequence the High King of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] ceded control of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] to Morrowind for the refugees to settle{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}}.

The war completed the shift of power in Morrowind that had begun during the Oblivion Crisis; because of it and the simultaneous [[Lore:Elder Council#The Stormcrown Interregnum|Stormcrown Interregnum]] Morrowind would become effectively independent from the Empire,{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook}} though some Imperials would continue to refer to it as a province.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}} The Dunmer themselves consider modern Morrowind an independent nation.{{ref|name=DBAdril}} As a result of Morrowind's independence and the Argonian Invasion - as well as their leading role during the decades of crisis - House Redoran became the most powerful of the Great Houses and used their newfound influence to relocate the capital to [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]] and expel House Hlaalu from the Grand Council,{{ref|name=DBAdril}} replacing them with [[Lore:House Sadras|House Sadras]].{{ref|name=DBMorvayn|[[Skyrim:Lleril Morvayn|Lleril Morvayn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

Black Marsh was devastated in {{Year|4E 48}} by the floating island of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]] and its [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|undead hordes]], with entire cities such as [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]], [[Lore:Gideon|Gideon]], and Stormhold stripped of life as the island traveled up the core of Black Marsh and southern Morrowind, killing all below. This disaster appears to have broken the hold the An-Xileel held over the Argonian people, and the mysterious party has not been mentioned in the following centuries.{{ref|name=InfernalCityBook}}{{ref|name=LordSoulsBook}}

After the end of the initial invasion, the Dunmer began to slowly to reclaim their lost territory. Mournhold was reclaimed by the Dunmer at some point prior to {{Year|4E 151}}<ref group="note">Nilara says she hasn't been back to Mournhold in 'almost fifty years'.</ref> and it would be rebuilt and restored following its sacking, with work still ongoing as of {{Year|4E 201}}, the city is considered secure enough that the bulk of the Redoran Guard garrison once stationed in the city has been reassigned elsewhere.{{ref|name=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}} By {{Year|4E 201}}, only a few Argonian tribes remained within the province's [[Lore:Thornmarsh|southernmost extremities]] with occasional patrols along the frontiers,{{ref|name=Delvin|[[Skyrim:Delvin Mallory|Delvin Mallory]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and the Dunmer considered the Argonian Invasion to have ended "long ago."{{ref|name=DBDreyla}} These Argonian remnants would continue to harass the Dunmer sporadically, attacking small villages near the Black Marsh border,{{ref|name=DBDreyla}} and in one case unsuccessfully attempting to raid Solstheim in {{Year|4E 150}} before being thwarted by the Redoran Guard of the island.{{ref|name=RavenRock}}
