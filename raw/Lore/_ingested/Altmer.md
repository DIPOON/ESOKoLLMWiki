# Lore:Altmer

The [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], also known as '''High Elves''' by the denizens of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]],{{ref|name=PGE1P|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Prologue}}}} by the archaic term {{Hover|Salache is one letter away from Saliache, which is a term exclusively for the Ayleids|'''Salache'''}},{{ref|name=TWEDF|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves|ns_base=DF}}}} or as the self-titled "'''Cultured People'''", are a tall, golden-skinned [[Lore:Mer|elven]] race hailing from the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]]. They regard themselves as the direct descendants of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]]{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{Ref|name=AD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}} and as the purest, most unaltered heirs of the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]],{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} the ancient elven race who first arrived on Tamriel from the mythical lost continent of [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]].{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} 

The Altmer are renowned as one of the most intelligent races, and their exceptional innate aptitude for magic makes them the most gifted in the [[Lore:Magic|arcane arts]].{{ref|name=LGDesc|[[Legends:Altmer|Race Description of Altmer in ''Legends'']]}}{{Ref|name=SkAltmerDescription|[[Skyrim:High Elf|High Elf]] in-game description in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]'' and ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} This magical affinity is further enhanced by their naturally long [[Lore:Lifespan|lifespans]], which range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.{{ref|name=VarietyPack4|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4|Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4]]}}{{ref|name=TRB2|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 2}}}} Some of the most ancient and powerful Altmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for millennia through the application of highly advanced magical disciplines.{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}}{{ref|name=PMB|[[Online:Psijic Mascot Bear Cub|Psijic Mascot Bear Cub]] pet description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} In addition to their magical prowess, Altmer possess natural resistances to [[Lore:Disease|disease]] and [[Lore:Paralysis|paralysis]], though they are noted to be somewhat vulnerable to elemental forces.{{Ref|name=DFAltmerStats|[[DF:High Elf|High Elf]] racial abilities in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=MWAltmerStats|[[MW:High Elf|High Elf]] racial abilities in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

[[Lore:Altmer#Social_Structure|Altmeri society]] has been shaped by millennia of scholarly refinement and is deeply hierarchical and tradition-bound. The Altmer have long pursued an ideal of cultural perfection, venerating their lineage and striving to preserve the traditions, knowledge, and practices of their ancient ancestors.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=ESOOlnewil|[[ON:Olnewil|Olnewil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} This ideal continues to shape all aspects of Altmeri life, from education and governance to art and daily conduct.{{ref|name=TPoP|{{Cite Book|The Price of Praxis}}}}{{ref|name=ONFamilyShrine|{{Cite Book|Lirendel's Family Shrine|ns_base=Online}}}} The [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]], a prestigious council of scholars and sages, are entrusted with preserving knowledge, overseeing education, and maintaining cultural continuity.{{ref|name=OtCoS}}{{ref|name=SotCT|{{Cite Book|Sages of the Crystal Tower}}}} 

High Elves are often perceived by other races as proud, aloof, or even arrogant.{{ref|name=NOEA|{{Cite Book|Notes on Elven Architecture}}}} These perceptions are reinforced by centuries of cultural insularity and the perceived superiority of their customs, ancestry, and intellectual traditions. The Altmer, for their part, consider themselves Tamriel's most civilized people: the continent's common tongue is derived from [[Lore:High Elvish|Altmeri]] speech and writing, and much of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]]'s art, law, architecture, and scholarship can trace its roots back to Altmeri tradition.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}}{{ref|name=AODL|{{Cite Book|Antecedants of Dwemer Law}}}}

==Appearance==
[[File:LG-arena-Highelf 4.png|left|170px|High Elf male]]
The Altmer are typically relatively tall, often standing a full head taller than the average [[Lore:men|men]] or [[Lore:mer|mer]].{{Ref|name=DFAltmerDescription|[[DF:High Elf|High Elf]] in-game description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=PGE1AD|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} They possess slender, graceful builds with long limbs and refined features,{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} and their skin tones are typically various shades of gold; a trait that commonly sets them apart from most other elven races.{{ref|name=PGE1AD}} Their hair is usually fair-blond, ginger, white, or silver, though darker shades such as brown or black are also fairly common.{{ref|name=ESOCharCreate|[[ON:Altmer|High Elf]] [[ON:Character Creation|character creation]] options in [[Online:ESO|ESO]]}} Eye colors range from blue, green, and brown to gold and amber, often luminous or piercing.{{Ref|name=ESOCharCreate}} Their facial features are angular, with high cheekbones, narrow chins, and long, sharply pointed ears.{{Ref|name=ESOCharCreate}} In both posture and expression, Altmer often project an air of superiority or aloofness, reinforced by their cultural upbringing.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}

Altmer fashion reflects their fixation with beauty, status, and arcane mastery. Jewelry and personal grooming are highly valued, and signs of very advanced aging are rarely visible—thanks to both natural longevity and magical upkeep.{{ref|name=VOFHE}}{{ref|name=TRB2}} The common practice of Altmer to dress in a manner resembling that of the ancient Aldmer, or that of other cultures that resemble the Aldmeri style, such as the ancient Ayleids and [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], elves of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], which is often viewed by the Mer as their golden age, is called "draping Ehlnofic". Clothes in this style often exhibit stylistic motifs in which circles, arcs, and tendrils abound, symbolizing the elven ancestors, the Aedra, being constrained by the creation of Mundus.{{ref|name=AncientElf|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 11: Ancient Elf Style}}}}

==History==
===Origin===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Echoes of Aldmeris 03.jpg|right|thumb|Artistic representation of Aldmeris]]
The Altmer trace their ancestry and spiritual identity to the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], a mythic and nonlinear age when the first spirits—the [[Lore:Et'Ada|Et'Ada]]—emerged from the primordial interplay between [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]], the soul of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and the force of stasis and ordered reflection, and [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]], the shadowed echo of Anuiel, embodying change and primordial chaos. From the Altmeri perspective, these spirits are not merely gods but literal ancestors—an idea that deeply influences their worldview and social hierarchy.{{ref|name=TM}}

A central tenet of Altmeri theology, laid out in works such as ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]'', is the belief that mortality is not a divine gift, but a curse. The mortal plane of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] was created through deception by [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], the so-called Missing God, who tricked many spirits into sacrificing their essence.{{ref|name=TM}} While humans—who, with the exception of the [[Lore:Redguards|Redguards]], see themselves as creations of the gods—interpret this act as a gift of life through divine sacrifice, the Altmer regard it as a catastrophic betrayal that severed them from the immortality of their ancestors.{{ref|name=TAX}}{{ref|name=TM}} According to the creation myth, when [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], the architect of the plans for the mortal world, decided to terminate the project, the gods decided to convene at the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] to decide on what to do. Most of the spirits departed with Magnus, becoming the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]]. But some Aedric spirits chose to stay on Nirn after Magnus and the Magna Ge departed in order to keep working on the new world so it'd remain viable. Among those spirits, some, typically referred to from that point on as [[Lore:Ehlnofey|"Earthbones"]], are thought to have followed the example of [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law as the "bones of the earth", eternal laws of nature. While others, typically referred to from that point on as [[Lore:Ehlnofey|"Ehlnofey"]], are thought to have chosen not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, thus becoming the progenitors to mortal life, which arose from their lines and took on its current form due to a phenomenon of gradual diminishment, of each consecutive generation becoming weaker and more removed than its progenitors in stature and might. It was due to this phenomenon that the first [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]], forebears of modern Altmer, are thought to have eventually emerged.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=Mystery|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}}{{ref|name=Anuad}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}}{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}

An alternate creation myth that explains Altmeri origin is put forward in the ''[[Lore:Anuad|Anuad]]'', attributed to the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]].{{ref|name=MR|[[ON:Monomyth Reforged|Monomyth Reforged]] antiquity codex entries in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Mono2|group=TRL|[[General:The Monomyth (French translation)|French translation]] of ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}} It describes the cosmic struggle between Anu and Padomay, the death of the world-mother Nir, and the creation of Nirn from the remnants of twelve shattered worlds. The [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]]—ancestors of elves and men—survived, but a war between their factions reshaped the world, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans and destroying most of the realm of the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] who were also survivors of the original Twelve Worlds. As this account tells it, the Elves, including the Altmer, are descended from the 'Old Ehlnofey,' a group who remained in their ancestral homeland and preserved much of their original power and knowledge while remaining fewer in number. 
{{ref|name=Anuad|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}

====Early Schisms====
The Altmer are thought to be the most direct, unaltered descendants of the original Aldmer elves,{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} and the transition from one to the other is not entirely clear. When the Aldmer left their homeland of [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]]—a place regarded by some sources as allegorical rather than literal—they settled primarily in the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], arriving first on [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]] and later spreading westward.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}}

[[File:LO-misc-Velothi.png|left|thumb|Depiction of [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] leading the Chimer to Morrowind]]
Following the [[Lore:Convention|Convention]] at the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]], where the gods set the laws of reality and cast out [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Lorkhan's Heart]] before departing from the affairs of Nirn,{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{Ref|name=Enigma|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} the Aldmer organized into powerful arcane societies on the Summerset Isles. Much of their early history is lost or obscured, but it is known that many ruins and structures—most notably the [[Lore:Ceporah Tower|Ceporah Tower]]—predate Aldmeri settlement by centuries.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} During this period, the Aldmer likely warred with the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] and other unknown beings for dominance over Summerset.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} Eventually, they unified and constructed the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] as a monument to their collective spirit and a pinnacle of magical achievement, unsurpassed until the rise of the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]], which served as the headquarters of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] founded by [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]].{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=tCotT|{{Cite Book|The Crystal of the Tower}}}} The Crystal Tower was considered their greatest architectural and metaphysical achievement, a monument to unity, arcane mastery, and the metaphysical Tower principle of Crystal-Like-Law.{{ref|name=tCotT}}{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=tCotT|{{Cite Book|The Crystal of the Tower}}}} It served as a beacon of Aldmeri civilization and the foundation of Altmeri identity.

Yet ideological schisms fractured Aldmeri unity. The most prominent of these was the [[Lore:Velothi|Velothi dissident movement]], which rejected Altmeri orthodoxy and led the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] eastward to what would become [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} Other groups, such as the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]], [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], and [[Lore:Orsimer|Orsimer]], emerged independently in response to distinct cultural, spiritual, or metaphysical divergences—each embracing different interpretations of the divine.{{ref|name=TCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} Many traditions attribute the divergence of elven subraces to their differing relationships with the gods,{{ref|name=TTNOO|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}}{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}}{{ref|name=VAM|{{Cite Book|Vivec and Mephala}}}}{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}}{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} or to the realization that different Towers could tell different "stories" and thus shape different domains, leading to elven unity breaking due to different groups aligning themselves with alternative Towers and destinies.{{Ref|name=Enigma}}{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}} The [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]] were once considered a splinter group from Summerset, but tapestries in the Crystal Tower suggest their separation occurred before the Aldmer left Aldmeris. This view was accepted by both Altmer and Maormer sources by the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=TMoP|{{Cite Book|The Maormer of Pyandonea}}}}{{ref|name=TCPoA|{{Cite Book|The Chosen People of Aldmeris}}}}{{ref|name=TFRotM|{{Cite Book|The False Revanchism of the Maormer}}}} The Altmer who remained in Summerset came to define themselves as the truest heirs of the Aldmer. Their society emphasizes purity of form, ancestral memory, and strict continuity of tradition. Songs are still composed in the [[Lore:Aldmeris (language)|Aldmeri language]].{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_H#High Elf Songbook|High Elf Songbook]] contraband description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Their arcane, political, and religious institutions all reflect a reverence for the past and a desire to emulate the perfection of their divine ancestors in hopes to one day rejoin them in the stars.{{ref|name=ESOVairabrian}}{{ref|name=ESOOlnewil}}

===First Era===
Two prominent Altmeri institutions emerged during the First Era, shaping not only the Isles but also the wider development of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielic]] civilization. The [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]], based on the isle of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], became the foremost authority on mystical philosophy and arcane theory. Claiming descent from the oldest traditions of Aldmeri magic, the Order played a formative role in early conceptions of the metaphysical structure of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] and the practice of the Elder Way.{{ref|name=PSIJIC|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}}{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} In contrast, the Direnni Clan embraced a more worldly approach. Originally one of the noble lineages of Summerset, the Direnni relocated to [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]] in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] and gradually expanded their influence across [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. During the early to mid-First Era, they established a quasi-imperial dominion over large swaths of western Tamriel, including human populations.{{ref|name=DRD|{{Cite Book|De Rerum Dirennis}}}} Their rule culminated in the enforcement of the [[Lore:Direnni Hegemony|Direnni Hegemony]], a legal and arcane regime that laid early groundwork for structured governance across the region.{{ref|name=BeforeTheAges|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}

====The Psijic Order and the Old Ways====
[[File:ON-banner-Psijic Order.png|100px|right|Banner of the Order]]
The [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] is the oldest known magical and philosophical organizations in Tamriel, predating even the establishment of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] by many centuries. Its first known mention in recorded history dates to {{Year|1E 20}}, when the Breton sage [[Lore:Voernet|Voernet]] chronicled his journey to the isle of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] to confer with the Psijic Ritemaster, [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]].{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} However, the Order's true origins likely reach back to the earliest days of Aldmeri colonization of the Summerset Isles during the late Merethic Era.{{ref|name=PGE3SI|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}}

According to tradition, the Psijic Order was formed in response to a theological and social transformation among the early Aldmer. As Aldmeri society began to shift from the general veneration of all ancestors to the more hierarchical worship of ancestor-heroes—those claimed as divine progenitors by the ruling elites—the foundations of the Altmeri pantheon began to coalesce around the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]]. In opposition, a faction of conservative Aldmeri mystics rejected this selective reverence, favoring the original, more universal worship of their ancestors.{{ref|name=PGE3SI|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}} These dissenters withdrew from mainstream society and settled on Artaeum, where they formalized their esoteric beliefs into what became known as the Elder Way.{{Ref|name=M|{{Cite Book|Mysticism (book)}}}}{{Ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} The Elder Way, central to Psijic philosophy, emphasized introspection, meditation, and mastery of the self through magical discipline—especially through the school of [[Lore:Mysticism|Mysticism]], which they viewed as a bridge between mortal perception and the divine architecture of the Aurbis. The Psijics taught that through disciplined spiritual and magical study, one could come to perceive and influence the hidden currents of reality.{{ref|name=PSIJIC|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}}

Though the Psijic Order generally discouraged direct intervention in worldly politics, it did not wholly isolate itself. The doctrine of seliffrnsae—“grave and faithful counsel”—governed their rare engagements with secular leaders.{{ref|name=PSIJIC|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}} The Psijics offered guidance only to those rulers they deemed wise or receptive to their teachings, often through cryptic visions, subtle manipulation of fate, or timely prophecy. The Ritemaster and the [[Lore:Council of Artaeum|Council of Artaeum]] collectively oversaw such decisions, and their influence could be felt from the courts of the Altmeri kings to the battlemages of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=PSIJIC|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}}

At the center of Psijic mysticism stood the legendary Ceporah Tower, which housed the [[Lore:Dreaming Cave|Dreaming Cave]]—a site of immense arcane significance. This ancient structure allowed initiates to peer beyond the veil of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], accessing the different planes of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] or beyond.{{ref|name=ESOIachesis|[[Online:Ritemaster Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=2920TLYFR|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}

Although Artaeum and the Order itself would mysteriously vanish from the world for centuries at a time—most notably in the late Second Era—the Psijics' legacy endured in the institutions and magical philosophies they inspired, including the later formation of the Mages Guild by the Altmer [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]], a former Psijic himself.{{ref|name=MagesGuild|{{Cite Book|Origin of the Mages Guild}}}}

====The Direnni Hegemony====
The [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] are an aristocratic merchant clan of Altmer whose members laid the foundation for modern [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]], [[Lore:Conjuration|conjuration]], and [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanting]].{{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ald|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}}{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}}
In stark contrast to the Psijic Order’s inward-looking mysticism, the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni Clan]] pursued a deliberate campaign of expansion and domination beyond the shores of Summerset. Descended from noble Altmeri lineages, the Direnni departed [[Lore:Alinor|Alinor]] during the Middle Merethic Era and established their power base on the [[Lore:Isle of Balfiera|Isle of Balfiera]], where they seized and repurposed the ancient [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]]. Revered as the oldest structure in Tamriel and the mythic site of the Convention, control of the Tower imbued the Direnni with both symbolic legitimacy and metaphysical authority, reinforcing their claim as rightful stewards of Merethic heritage.{{ref|name=DRD|{{Cite Book|De Rerum Dirennis}}}}

[[File:DF-banner-Direnni Crest.png|thumb|left|The [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] Banner of the Third Era]]
Over the following centuries, the Direnni hegemony stretched from all of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], into large parts of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] constructing a formidable aristocracy upheld by magical dominance, bureaucratic centralization, and carefully orchestrated intermarriage.{{Ref|name=ALOSAS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}}{{ref|name=TDCP11|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}}{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} The Direnni are known more for their legendary warriors and monarchs, and less for their spellcasters, but magic is still an important tradition to them.{{Ref|name=DRD|{{Cite Book|De Rerum Dirennis}}}}

The Direnni played a pivotal role in the ethnogenesis of the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]]. During their dominion over High Rock, they engaged in widespread breeding with the subjugated [[Lore:Nedes|Nedic]] population, who were often used as concubines or sexual slaves. This Aldmeri and Nedic interbreeding{{ref|name=PGE3All}} occurred for centuries as far back as the Merethic Era.{{Ref|name=MDBMM|{{Cite Book|Modern Day Bretons: Man or Mer?}}}}{{ref|name=LATD|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}} The resulting mixed-race offspring, known as [[Lore:Manmer|Manmer]], formed a distinct racial group that gradually evolved into the Bretons over successive generations.{{ref|name=Bretons|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1HR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} These individuals possessed partial Elven ancestry and a natural aptitude for magic. Although initially marginalized, the Bretons gradually rose in prominence within the Direnni administration and came to occupy more formalized roles, serving as retainers, scribes, battlemages, and even minor nobles within the Direnni courts. Over time, they developed a distinct cultural identity that synthesized Elven and human traditions.{{ref|name=Bretons|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1HR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}}

The apogee of Direnni power came in {{Year|1E 482}}, when the battle-mage [[Lore:Aiden Direnni|Aiden Direnni]] achieve an important victory over the Alessian Order at the [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]]. This decisive triumph checked Alessian expansionism and secured Direnni dominance in High Rock. However, the momentum proved short-lived. By {{Year|1E 498}}, they were weakened after the war, and a combination of factors—including overextension, waning central authority, and the growing autonomy of Breton nobility—forced the Direnni to withdraw from the mainland, retreating to Balfiera.{{ref|name=TLKotA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} One account claims that the Direnni had already completed their withdrawal to Balfiera by the time the Redguards arrived in Tamriel.{{Ref|name=WebsiteLore|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/elder-scrolls-online-lore-answers Elder Scrolls Online website - Lore Answers]}} Another contradictory source places the final stages of the Direnni’s flight from High Rock sometime around or after the ninth century of the First Era.{{ref|name=ROTPO|[[Online:Ring of the Pale Order|Ring of the Pale Order]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} Yet another source claims that the Direnni remained dominant in High Rock until the middle of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3High|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}}

While no other Altmeri family would ever rival the Direnni in scope or longevity, smaller and unrelated efforts to establish mainland strongholds did occur. Among these, the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya Clan]] is the most notable. In the sixth century of the First Era, the Corelanya settled the partially abandoned Ayleid city of [[Lore:Salas En|Salas En]] in southern Hammerfell. There, they revitalized its ancient port and established maritime trade routes with [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] and the Topal Bay archipelagos. Unlike their orthodox cousins in Summerset, the Corelanya were known practitioners of Daedra-worship and were rumored to maintain covert ties with the seafaring [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]], further alienating them from mainstream Altmeri society.{{ref|name=TSEE|{{Cite Book|The Salas En Expedition}}}} This southern enclave was ultimately destroyed in {{Year|1E 853}} by Ra Gada forces under King [[Lore:Xakhwan|Xakhwan]], who launched a campaign to purge foreign settlements from the Hammerfell coast.{{ref|name=ESOHaqmir|[[ON:Prince Haqmir|Prince Haqmir]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Corelanyan Elves located outside the Ra Gada's reach regrouped at Summerset and attempted to convince High King Naurnindon to help them reclaim their territory. Frustrated from years of failed diplomacy, they attempted a coup to install Kinlady Arlinwe of Firsthold as High Queen. In {{Year|1E 909}}, Naurnindon gave the Corelanya a choice: surrender their nobility and assets or face exile. They chose to exile themselves to the island of [[Lore:Solstice|Solstice]] for its ample space for growth, and distance from any territory under the High King of Summerset’s rule.{{ref|name=HoCC3|{{Cite book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 3}}}}{{ref|name=HoCC4|{{Cite book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 4}}}}

Despite their retreat, the Direnni retained control of the Isle of Balfiera and demonstrated their continued martial strength by repelling a [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudan]] naval assault during the Ra Gada invasion in {{Year|1E 907}}.{{ref|name=Bretons}} Nevertheless, by the Second Era, the clan had faded into a largely ceremonial role. What endured was their legacy: preserved in ancient texts, in the arcane traditions of their descendants, and in the enduring presence of the Adamantine Tower—silent and immutable, as the world changed around it.{{ref|name=ESOLaurielsLament|[[ON:Lauriel's Lament|Lauriel's Lament]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Second Era===
The Second Era was a tumultuous period for Tamriel, yet the Altmer of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] remained relatively insulated from much of the early chaos that followed the fall of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]] in {{Year|2E 1}} and the ensuing rise of the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]]. While the continent fractured into local powers during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] (beginning in {{Year|2E 430}}), the Altmer remained committed to their insular traditions and isolationist policies.{{Fact}} The Altmer continually fended off incursions from the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]], including a disastrous naval expedition near [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]] in {{Year|2E 486}} in which the majority of the Altmeri fleet was lost.{{ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Maormer of Pyandonea}}}}

====The Aldmeri Dominions and Imperial Rule====
[[File:ON-icon-Aldmeri Dominion.png|right|thumb|The sigil of the First Aldmeri Dominion]]
While mainland Tamriel experienced many problems and sagas during the [[Lore:First Era|First]] and early [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the sea insulated the Altmer of Summerset from their problems, though they had their own limited internal conflicts as well as repeated, large-scale invasions from the Sload and Maormer to deal with.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} In {{Year|2E 580}},{{ref|name=TUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn: The Unforeseen Queen}}}} however, amid the chaos of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] that followed the fall of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] of Summerset allied with the Bosmer of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] in the Elden Accord to form the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=ESOAyrenn|[[ON:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Dominion's propaganda decried the carelessness of Men and promoted the establishment of Elven rule across Tamriel, viewing the Aldmeri races as the only peoples with the "wisdom and restraint" to be its proper stewards. To that end, the Dominion fought the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] against the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] and [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] for control of mainland Tamriel.{{ref|name=Welcome|{{Cite Book|Welcome to New Aldmeri Irregulars}}}} The war's ultimate outcome is unknown, but all three alliances, as well as the Empire of Cyrodiil, had ceased to exist by the ninth century of the Second Era.
====First Dominion====
{{Quote Box|''I have no hatred for the races of Man, but they are young. Like all children, they are driven by emotion. They lack the wisdom that comes with age.  I would sooner place an Altmer infant on the Ruby Throne than surrender Tamriel to their capricious whims.<br>The Altmer, the Bosmer and the Khajiit share the common traits of intelligence, patience and reason.  We do not seek riches or plunder. Domination is not our goal, nor is the acclamation of power for its own sake.<br>Today we make our stand. Today we take back the Ruby Throne, which is ours by ancient right and the blessings of the Divines.<br>Stand with us.''|Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri}}
A major turning point came with the birth and later return of [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri]], who vanished in {{Year|2E 573}} and was presumed dead. Upon her dramatic reappearance in {{Year|2E 580}}, Ayrenn claimed the throne of Alinor just as her brother, Prince Naemon, prepared to ascend. Her coronation marked the beginning of a new, outward-facing era for the Altmer. Ayrenn's experiences traveling Tamriel shaped her progressive vision, which challenged traditionalist values and prioritized strategic cooperation with neighboring provinces.{{Ref|name=AUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn – The Unforeseen Queen}}}} Under Ayrenn’s rule, the [[Lore:Elden Accord|Elden Accord]] was signed, leading to the formation of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]] between the Altmer, [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]], and [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. Though publicly framed as a partnership of mutual aid, the Dominion's ideology promoted Altmeri supremacy, emphasizing the Elves’ perceived divine mandate to steward Tamriel.{{Ref|name=WTNAI|{{Cite Book|Welcome to New Aldmeri Irregulars}}}} The Dominion quickly mobilized its military might, incorporating Altmeri battlemages, naval forces, and elite agents like the [[Lore:Eyes of the Queen|Eyes of the Queen]] into its war efforts. It joined the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] (circa {{Year|2E 582}}), contesting control of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] against the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]]. The Dominion's invasion route through [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]] into [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenay]] was marked by strategic offensives and magical superiority, though the war itself devolved into protracted stalemate.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Although Ayrenn’s progressive vision won admiration among younger Altmer and allied races, it sparked fierce opposition from conservative factions within Summerset, such as the Kinlady dynasties of [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]] and the increasingly powerful Sapiarchs of Alinor. Dissent intensified as the war dragged on and losses mounted. Prince Naemon, having reluctantly accepted a secondary role under Ayrenn, was later discovered consorting with the [[Lore:Veiled Heritance|Veiled Heritance]]{{ref|name=LitES|{{Cite Book|Life in the Eagle's Shadow}}}}{{ref|name=TofER|[[Online:The Orrery of Elden Root|The Orrery of Elden Root]] quest [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

====Second Dominion====
A Second Dominion was established in {{Year|2E 830}} following Altmer intervention in a dynastic dispute in Valenwood. The Second Dominion once again allied the Altmer with the Bosmer and Khajiit, but also with the Maormer — their hereditary enemies, who claim that the Altmer are a mongrel race and that the Sea Elves (Maormer) are the true descendants of the “purest strain” of Aldmeri ancestors, though this claim is highly dubious.{{ref|name=TCPoA}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}} This expanded alliance could not stand against [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], who crushed the Dominion with the help of the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] and finally brought the Summerset Isle into the Imperial fold in {{Year|2E 896}}.{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}} The Altmer remembered the conquest with shame and horror, and likely contributed to social unrest among their youth later in the Third Era.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}

===Third Era===
A notable moment of collaboration between the wider Empire and Summerset was [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]] in {{Year|3E 110}}, during the reign of [[Lore:Antiochus|Emperor Antiochus]]. The [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] of Pyandonea, led by [[Lore:Orgnum|King Orgnum]], launched a massive invasion of the Summerset Isles.{{ref|name=BHOTE1}}{{ref|name=TDC|Tamriel's Timeline, ''[[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles|The Daggerfall Chronicles]]'' — Ronald Wartow}} In spite of the longstanding rivalries among the Altmeri kingdoms, the threat prompted unprecedented unity. Despite this combined strength, the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial Navy]] and the royal navies of Summerset could not withstand Orgnum’s fleet.{{ref|name=WotI|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v2}}}} Victory seemed certain for the Maormer when, off the coast of Artaeum, a freak storm arose and utterly annihilated their ships. Legends attribute the storm to the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], and this has since been widely accepted.{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=TWQ4|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v4}}}}

Not long after the war, the Psijic Order vanished along with the Isle of Artaeum, withdrawing from worldly affairs for nearly 500 years.{{Ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}}

====The Oblivion Crisis====
[[File:LG-cardart-Oblivion Gate.png|thumb|right|An open Oblivion Gate]]

The [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] of {{Year|3E 433}} proved an even more traumatic moment for the Altmer. As Daedra overran Tamriel, the unnatural turmoil of the seas killed many of the Altmer fleeing the mainland to Summerset, and wrecked the ports that would have received them. The Crisis devastated Tamriel and inflicted an grievous wound on the Altmer. The Crystal Tower briefly served as a stronghold for the surviving refugees in Summerset until it fell to powerful Daedric forces.{{ref|name=RTI|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. I}}}}{{ref|name=RT|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. II}}}} Its fall during the Daedric incursion not only caused widespread chaos but dealt a psychological blow to the very foundation of Altmeri identity.{{ref|name=TD|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}} Although Martin’s sacrifice finished the crisis, the Empire, already weakened by the Crisis and the death of Emperor [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]], was unable to effectively respond on time.{{ref|name=RT}}

In the immediate aftermath of the Oblivion Crisis, power in the Isles shifted dramatically. The [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]], originally an obscure bureau for the preservation of Altmeri heritage, had played an important role in the governments of the First and Second Dominions.{{ref|name=Aicantar}}{{ref|name=PGE1AD}} Following the chaos of the Crisis, an extremist cabal of Altmer supremacists now assumed this name and claimed credit for saving the Altmer from extinction using "deep and subtle magicks".{{ref|name=RTII|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. II}}}} Many Altmer, disillusioned by Imperial failure and desperate for leadership, embraced Thalmor rhetoric. The faction swiftly consolidated influence through both propaganda and pragmatic crisis management, portraying themselves as the true inheritors of Altmeri destiny.{{citation needed}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:LG-quest-Fugitives 02.jpg|thumb|left|Soldiers of the Third Aldmeri Dominion during the Great War]]
In {{Year|4E 22}}, the re-formed Thalmor seized power in Summerset outright and deposed the Altmer monarch, renaming the province Alinor.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}{{ref|name=RTIV}} Soon afterwards, in {{Year|4E 29}}, a faction friendly to the Thalmor took over Valenwood and formed a new union with the Altmer, establishing the Third Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=TGW}}

The Third Dominion proceeded to break off all contact with the Empire, and the Altmer lands remained silent to the outside world for some seventy years as the Thalmor consolidated their power.{{ref|name=TGW}} A grim demonstration of the ruthlessness of the Thalmor in this pursuit – and the continuing resistance to their rule among some Altmer – was the [[Lore:Night of Green Fire|Night of Green Fire]] in {{Year|4E 42}}, in which a group of Altmer dissidents were massacred by Thalmor mages at [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]].{{ref|name=SFasendil}} At any rate, the Thalmor's sway continued to expand. After Nirn's moons vanished in the [[Lore:Void Nights|Void Nights]] of {{Year|4E 98}}–{{Year|4E 100|100}}, they claimed credit for their restoration and persuaded the Khajiit of Elsweyr to ally once more with the Aldmeri Dominion in {{Year|4E 115}}. Finally, the Thalmor turned their sights on the Empire itself. From {{Year|4E 171}} to {{Year|4E 175|175}} the Dominion fought the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] against the Empire, inflicting a series of punishing defeats that led to the signing of the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], which ceded large parts of southern [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] to the Dominion, disbanded the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], and banned the worship of the man-god [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] across the Empire.{{ref|name=TGW}} 

With the entire province of Hammerfell having gained independence from the Empire, and the Empire itself in turmoil from a civil war in Skyrim – abetted by the Thalmor and the possible assassination of Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]]{{ref|name=Dossier|''[[Skyrim:Thalmor Dossier: Ulfric Stormcloak|Thalmor Dossier: Ulfric Stormcloak]]}}{{ref|name=SRKillEmpire|Events of [[Skyrim:To Kill an Empire|To Kill an Empire]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} – the Altmer had attained a position of substantial power in Tamriel by {{Year|4E 201}}, though much remained unclear about the situation in Alinor itself.

The apprentice of [[Lore:Balagog gro-Nolob|Balagog gro-Nolob]], Urzhag gro-Larak, visited the Summerset Isles several times around the end of the Second Century of the Fourth Era during his travels across Tamriel which saw him learn from a variety of races to improve his cooking skills. During one of these visits, he got permission with two other foreign chefs to research under some limitations in the College of Sapiarchs. Though the Isles were opened up, many of the native inhabitants were still hesitant to outsiders.{{ref|name=Cookbook2|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook Vol. 2}}}}

==Culture==
Among the Altmer, the arts serve not merely as entertainment or expression, but as mechanisms for cultural continuity, philosophical discipline, and the formal demonstration of refinement. The ideal Altmeri artist is not an innovator but a refiner—one who improves upon ancestral works in accordance with established praxic standards.{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLiveSummerset|[[General:ESO Live Summerset Story and Lore|ESO Live Summerset Story and Lore]]}}

===Language===
The Altmer language [[Lore:High Elvish|High Elvish]] (alternatively Altmeri,{{Ref|name=BeastGalen|{{Cite Book|The Beast of Galen}}}} High Elven,{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|6}} or Elvish),{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOSummerfall|[[General:ESO Summerfall Event Special Livestream|ESO Summerfall Event Special Livestream]]}} is a direct descendant of [[Lore:Aldmeris (language)|Aldmeris language]], preserved through rigorous scholarship and formal oratory.{{ref|name=PGE1AD}} Though few beyond the borders of [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset]] speak modern Altmeri with fluency, the continent’s common tongue, the official language of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] and the most widely spoken across modern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], known as [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]], is derived from Altmeri speech and script. Many formal, magical, and legal terms in Tamrielic are either direct borrowings or derived calques.{{ref|name=TM}}

Public address, legal declarations, and even private correspondence are expected to conform to syntactic traditions. The [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] are regarded as the ultimate custodians of both spoken and written purity.{{ref|name=OtCoS}}

===Literary Arts===
Altmeri literature is dominated by philosophical treatises, genealogical chronicles, ceremonial scripts, and didactic histories. The Scrolls of Praxis, maintained by the College of Sapiarchs, serve as both a moral code and a textual anchor for all ceremonial behavior.{{ref|name=SotCT|{{Cite Book|Sages of the Crystal Tower}}}}{{ref|name=OtCoS|{{Cite Book|On the College of Sapiarchs}}}} Altmeri prose tends toward elevated registers and strict structural conventions. Even political or ideological writings such as [[Lore:An End to Isolation|''An End to Isolation'']] or [[Lore:A Rejection of Open Borders|''A Rejection of Open Borders'']] follow the same rhetorical frameworks as religious or historical texts.{{ref|name=AEtI|{{Cite Book|An End to Isolation}}}}{{ref|name=ARoOB|{{Cite Book|A Rejection of Open Borders}}}} Subversive literature—particularly works authored by apraxics—has historically been censored or banned.{{ref|name=ESOTanorian}}{{ref|name=ESOLanarie}}

[[File:ON-prerelease-Rellenthil.jpg|thumb|left|Amphitheatre of Rellenthil]]

The city of [[Lore:Rellenthil|Rellenthil]], located in central Summerset northeast of Alinor, is widely regarded as the cultural heart of Altmeri theatrical arts.{{ref|name=Rellenthil|[[ON:Rellenthil|Rellenthil]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} It is best known as the home of the [[Lore:House of Reveries|House of Reveries]], a prestigious and enigmatic troupe of performers whose influence spans the entire isle.{{ref|name=HoR|[[ON:Manor of Masques|Manor of Masques]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Founded on the Altmeri ideal that artistry must transcend ego and identity, members of the House of Reveries are required to adopt masks and stage names, never revealing their birth names or personal affiliations to the public—even to each other.{{ref|name=HoR}} The House performs elaborate stage plays, which combine dance, illusion magic, lyrical narration, and thematic symbolism. These productions often center on abstract Altmeri concerns such as self-discipline, transcendence, and the tension between duty and desire.{{ref|name=HoR}} 

The troupe's annual performances are considered major events in Altmer cultural life, drawing nobles, scholars, and artisans from across [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset]].{{ref|name=Rellenthil}} The House also provides occasional commentary on political or philosophical trends through symbolic characters and veiled dialogue—one of the few forms of sanctioned cultural critique in an otherwise rigidly formal society.{{ref|name=HoR}}{{ref|name=ESOLanarie}} Despite their use of [[Lore:Illusion|illusion]] and anonymity, Reveries are not considered subversive by Altmeri authorities. On the contrary, their adherence to tradition and their use of symbolic performance make them an exemplary model of controlled artistic expression.{{ref|name=HoR}}

Altmeri poetry is ritualized, metrically complex, and almost exclusively public in function. It is composed and recited to mark rites of passage, funerals, ascensions, and diplomatic events. Deviation from traditional meters is discouraged and often viewed as a violation of ceremonial form.{{ref|name=TPoP|{{Cite Book|The Price of Praxis}}}} Court bards and orators are not entertainers but custodians of cultural memory. They recite ancestral genealogies, mythic sequences, and didactic parables drawn from canon. Improvisation is rare and discouraged in favor of perfect recitation.{{ref|name=TPoP}}

===Architecture===
Altmeri cities and towns reflects their pursuit of beauty, order, symmetry perfection, aligning with the [[#Path to Alaxon|Path to Alaxon]]. Structures are typically constructed from pale stone such as alabaster or coral limestone, and are characterized by elegant spires, high arches, domed ceilings, and gilded ornamentation.{{fact}}<!--{{ref|Summerset Chapter 3 loading screen in ''[[ON:Online|ESO]]''}}--> Buildings often feature open-air courtyards, garden terraces, and magical lighting that adjusts with the time of day.{{fact}}<!--{{ref|name=SummersetLS2|Summerset Chapter 2 loading screen in ''[[ON:Online|ESO]]''}}--> Imperial critics are divided on their opinions of Altmeri architecture. Some would call it "soaring and graceful" while others would say it is "repetitive and static". They have curved gables and strong, pointed steeples that emphasize height, and ceilings far higher than most buildings. The historian, Cantaber Congonius makes note of discernable similarities with Ayleid architecture, though subtle changes are less obvious.{{Ref|name=NOEA}}

Architectural design is a form of praxis in itself, with each generation of Altmeri builders refining the work of their ancestors through a process of idealization and inherited aesthetic standards.{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLiveSummerset}} They are resistant to large-scale changes and innovation, and are content to tinker on what is established. The end result is highly sophisticated precision, harmony, and the repeated use of orthodox compositions.{{Ref|name=NOEA}} The finest structures are said to echo the divine geometries of Aetherius, and many cities are arranged according to numerological principles derived from ancestral records.{{ref|name=SotCT|{{Cite Book|Sages of the Crystal Tower}}}} Notable examples of Altmer architecture include the towers of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]], the College of Sapiarchs, and the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]].{{ref|name=RTIV|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}}

===Military===
[[File:LG-cardart-Battlereeve of Dusk 03.png|thumb|left|A Battlereeve of [[Lore:Dusk|Dusk]] commanding Altmeri forces.]]
Altmeri [[Lore:Weapons|weapons]] and armor exemplify refined craftsmanship and aesthetic philosophy, favoring graceful, organic designs inspired by birds, flowers, and seashells. Their craftsmanship is elegant and visually appealing, but still powerful.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor|{{Cite Book|Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel|ns_base=Books}}}} Armor is often embossed with motifs resembling feathers or scales, while helms and cuirasses may be stylized with winged or beaked features.{{fact}}

A particularly illustrative account of Altmeri martial culture is recorded during a mainland fighters' gathering, where the aloof, golden-skinned representative of the Isles was persuaded—through the diplomatic efforts of the Khajiiti champion, Zadabal-ra—to attend. He arrived not alone but with a caravan of gleaming shields, winged helms, and spears consecrated to [[Lore:Phynaster|Phynaster the Guardian]]. While the Altmer offered extensive commentary on the martial deficiencies of the mainlanders—provoking the ire of a Nord champion—tempers were ultimately eased by mead and rigorous sparring. It was in this combat that the soaring, swan-necked elegance of Altmeri weaponry was put to the test.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}}

Forged from rare minerals and exotic ores sourced from the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], their weapons reflect the same intricacy found in Altmeri speech. Even the most humble Altmer disdains the use of iron or steel, viewing such materials as crude and provincial. Instead, they favor complex forging processes involving refined crystalline elements.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}}

Among these, volcanic glass, formed from resins and mineral deposits, is especially prized. Weapons made from this glass are well-balanced, milky green in hue, and unexpectedly durable. Despite their apparent fragility, such blades are effective in battle, though they require frequent and careful sharpening to maintain their edge. The Altmer further enhance their arms through embellishments and enchantments. One might encounter a long blade inlaid with shimmering glass, crowned with a golden pommel, and etched with eagle wings or avian heads. Their bows, noted for their elegant winged curves, have even been likened in shape to a maiden's thigh.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}} The long bow, in particular, is historically associated with Altmer aristocrats.{{Ref|name=MWGarothmuk|[[Morrowind:Garothmuk gro-Muzgub|Garothmuk gro-Muzgub]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Rather than use leather from wild beasts, the Altmer breed [[Lore:Guar|guar]] specifically for their pliable hides, ensuring each component of their weaponry adheres to the standards of controlled refinement.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}} Altmeri weapons often reflect the physical and cultural characteristics of their wielders—elongated, barbed, and richly gilded. The result is a striking combination of delicate beauty and deadly function.{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}}

In addition to their traditional forces, the Altmer have at times employed unconventional auxiliaries. During certain periods, goblins were trained under Altmeri direction to support their armies.{{Ref|name=Fedris|[[Tribunal:Fedris Hler|Fedris Hler]]'s dialogue during [[Tribunal:The Goblin Army|The Goblin Army]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

Naval warfare has long been a domain of Altmeri excellence.{{Ref|name=TMoP}} Faced with repeated [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] incursions during the First and Second Eras, the Altmer constructed a formidable fleet. Although their maritime dominance had waned by the time of the [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]], necessitating support from the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijics]] and the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], their naval tradition remained deeply respected. Altmeri ships, like their blades, are often described as slender and swan-necked—embodying the same principles of elegance, precision, and lethality.{{Ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{Ref|name=ArmsAndArmor}} The southern city of [[Lore:Sunhold|Sunhold]] holds the largest fleet in the province and has a reputation of being a capable fortress-city.{{Ref|name=ONHelenaere|[[Online:Kinlady Helenaere|Kinlady Helenaere]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{Ref|name=OBIAG|{{Cite Book|Our Blessed Isles: A Guide}}}} King [[Lore:Orgnum|Orgnum]] of Pyandonea, who had long sought after the Summerset Isles, had his eyes particularly on the city.{{Ref|name=TKOVC17|{{Cite Book|The King of Vipers, Canto 17}}}} Under the First Aldmeri Dominion, Sunhold was their navy's home port.{{Ref|name=ONLoadSunhold|[[Online:Sunhold|Sunhold]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

===Dueling===
[[File:OB-concept-04.jpg|thumb|right|Elven weaponry]]
Altmer who find themselves in disagreement typically prefer to resolve such matters through dialogue rather than violence. Extended conversation, contemplation, and the sharing of wine and fellowship are culturally favored methods of dispute resolution. However, if discussion fails to yield a mutually satisfactory conclusion, the matter may be settled through a formal duel.{{ref|name=GuideAltmerCult}}

====Rules of Dueling====
The traditional rules of Altmeri dueling are as follows:

*Combatants may only arm themselves with a traditional Altmeri dueling foil. The use of [[Lore:Weapons#Blades|longswords]], [[Lore:Weapons#Axes|axes]], [[Lore:Weapons#Blunt Weapons|flails]], or similar weapons is strictly prohibited. The foil must be washed in mineral water, thoroughly dried, and polished to a fine sheen.

*Before the duel begins, each participant must recite the full name of their opponent, optionally referencing relevant past misdeeds. If a combatant mispronounces or stumbles over the name, they are required to pause and repeat it in full two more times.

*A neutral witness must be present to observe the duel. This individual is honor-bound to intervene should either participant commit a violation of dueling etiquette, such as fleeing the field, using dishonorable tactics (e.g., throwing dirt), or harassing the opponent beyond the bounds of the engagement.{{ref|name=GuideAltmerCult|{{Cite Book|Guide to Altmeri Culture (On Dueling)}}}}

The victor of the duel is expected to show humility and courtesy toward the defeated, providing medical aid, comfort, and any necessary support for recovery. In return, the defeated must formally acknowledge their error and request forgiveness. Through this mutual exchange, both parties preserve their honor—a concept regarded by the Altmer as the highest ideal on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=GuideAltmerCult}}

===Symbolism===
Altmeri visual art emphasizes symmetry, divine geometry, and cosmological motifs. Civic and sacred buildings are adorned with murals and mosaics depicting scenes from Altmeri myth, particularly the ascension of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], the construction of the Crystal Tower, and key moments in Aldmeri history. Art is not an individual endeavor: many major commissions—especially in temples or public spaces—must be approved by guilds or Sapiarchic oversight. Common symbols include latticework patterns representing the Aurbis or cosmic order; starlight and rays indicating alignment with Aetherius; mirrored forms expressing balance and ancestral symmetry.{{ref|name=OtCoS}}

The Altmer use the eagle both in association with Auri-El himself,{{ref|name=ContDriftwood|[[ON:Contraband D|Driftwood Idol contraband]] in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ContWingedAuriel|[[ON:Contraband W|Winged Auri-El contraband]] in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}} and as a symbol representing the Aedra, their ancestors, who descended from [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] and became trapped in physical form by the creation of Nirn.{{Ref|name=ESOKB|{{Cite Book|Elder Scrolls Online - Knowledge Base|ns_base=GEN}}}}. Altmeri prayers reference Auri-El as the Fixed Center of existence who always envelops all people with his "scales and fangs and flame", and invoke him to grant his followers stability.{{ref|name=COTF|{{Cite Book|Coils of the Father}}}}

These symbols are also echoed in [[#Architecture|architecture]] and religious iconography, reinforcing the unity of Altmeri aesthetics across disciplines.

===Food===
:→''For more detailed information, see: [[Lore:Altmer Cuisine|Altmer Cuisine]]''
Being isolated in the Summerset Isles has allowed the Altmer to perfect their own cuisine without much interference from the outside races. When they do open their shores to the other races, they will embrace their culinary contributions. Among themselves, Altmer culinary tastes may differ depending on where they are from as those from one area may not agree with those from another on who has the best take on their cuisine.{{ref|name=CCV1|{{Cite Book|Corelanyan Cuisine, Vol. 1}}}} 

The Altmer have strict rules when it comes to table manners and deportment{{ref|name=TESOC|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook]]}} with several details and rules about how to behave at the dinner table. Some of these rules include refilling the glass of the diner to your left and never refilling your own, never resting utensils on the edge of any vessel, not eating more than the most prestigious attendee at any function, always allowing the head of your table to lead the discussion, never watching another while they chew, and never using the wrong utensil as it is considered barbaric. They believe it is better to watch and follow the example of the other guests than to make such a mistake.{{ref|name=PPS|{{Cite Book|Protocol and Propriety in Summerset}}}} Rules around food and drink in Summerset can be similarly strict as well. Altmeri beverages must all be brewed according to the "rules of conformance"{{ref|[[Online:Riharai|Riharai]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and being caught with any drinks that don't follow the laws can cause one to run afoul of the [[Lore:Divine Prosecution|Divine Prosecution]] and lead to possible imprisonment.{{ref|[[Online:Finnegil|Finnegil]]'s dialogue in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}} 

Seafood is commonplace on Summerset Isle, which is surrounded by sparkling seas. There is no aversion to raw meat and several Altmeri dishes contain raw [[Lore:Fish|fish]], however Altmer will never consume anything that is alive and there are certain animals that are not to be eaten such as rodents.{{ref|name=CCV1}}

==Society==
===Social Structure and Hierarchy===
[[File:ON-statue-Alinor Kinlord Statue 02.png|right|180px]][[File:OB-concept-04.jpg|thumb|300px|right|Elven weaponry]]
At the time the Crystal Tower was built in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the Elves of Summerset had a relatively egalitarian society, though social stratification slowly developed into a rigid hierarchy of classes over the millennia.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} The Wise (teachers and priests) became the highest echelon, followed by Artists, Princes, Warriors, Landowners, Merchants, Workers, and enslaved peoples such as [[Lore:Goblin|Goblin]]s.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} Early literature by man painted the High Elves in poor light, though the impartiality of these accounts is questionable. It was suggested that the Altmer practiced eugenic infanticide, putting to death nine in ten of their newborns due to impurities, that [[Lore:Altmer Names|Altmeri names]] are simply combinations of numbers in their own language, and that Altmer culture revolves around displaying social status.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} Some of this has basis in fact; Altmer born with deformities or impurities are often – though not always{{ref|group=UOL|name=TuttleAmalien|[[General:Leamon Tuttle's Posts#2020|Leamon Tuttle's Posts - 12/29/20]]}} – abandoned by their parents as "hulkynd" (sometimes to be raised by priests of [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]]),{{ref|name=ONManacar|[[Online:Manacar|Manacar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}} and the full name of an Altmer is relatively extensive, detailing their patrilineal and matrilineal histories in addition to the history of their clan.{{ref|name=ONFamilyShrine|{{Cite Book|Lirendel's Family Shrine|ns_base=Online}}}}

=====Praxis=====
At an Altmer's coming of age, they are granted a {{Anchor|calian|Calian}}, a sphere of [[Lore:Aetherquartz|aetherquartz]] crystal representing their honor and social status.{{Ref|name=TPoP|{{Cite Book|The Price of Praxis}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Auridon Antiquities|Aquiline Calian Sphere]] codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The aetherquartz in calian spheres is polished using ground pumice and ocean sand.{{Ref|[[Online:Pearls of Ehlnofey|Aetherquartz Prayer Beads]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Calians are considered to be "praxic talismans".{{ref|name=ESOMoR|Dialogue of the [[ON:Mother of Rats|Mother of Rats]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Mer who have committed some particularly grievous crime against Altmer law are termed "ousters" and "apraxics". While ousters are simply banished from their homes,{{ref|[[ON:Azarari|Azarari]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} apraxics are treated more severely: their calians are ceremonially shattered by a curate and it is wholly forbidden for Altmer in good standing to speak to them.{{ref|name=ESOLanarie|[[ON:Lanarie|Lanarie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOMoR}} Some apraxics labor for years to restore their status, as part of which they must painstakingly repair their own calian,{{ref|name=TPoP}}{{ref|name=ESODecanter|[[Online:Summerset Antiquities|Apraxic Decanter]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} while others choose to remain in exile. Nonetheless, in the middle of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] subversive literature written by aprax mer gained wide currency among young Altmer on Summerset Isle,{{ref|name=ESOLanarie}} and provoked some of them to engage in revolutionary activity.{{ref|name=ESOMoR}} This questioning of Altmer customs coincided with reforms introduced by Queen Ayrenn after the founding of the First Aldmeri Dominion, including the end of Summerset's longstanding isolation and the opening of its borders to other races and cultures, which provoked heated debate and political maneuvering.{{ref|name=AEtI}}{{ref|name=ACfOB|{{Cite Book|A Case for Open Borders}}}}{{ref|name=ARoOB|{{Cite Book|A Rejection of Open Borders}}}}

=====Alaxon=====
One of the dominant philosophies of the Altmer of Summerset is the "Path to Alaxon". Alaxon is the state of perfection that every High Elf strives for. The Path to Alaxon represents the approach Altmer take to achieve that state. Practice of the Path requires intense dedication and concentrated effort.{{ref|name=ESOOlnewil|[[ON:Olnewil|Olnewil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Those who are judged to have attained a degree of perfection are granted an alaxon sphere, which, like calians, may be destroyed if they are judged to have fallen from it.{{ref|name=CotDP|{{Cite Book|Cases of the Divine Prosecution}}}} The Path to Alaxon is reflected in Summerset's characteristic architecture, which builds on the aesthetic practice of past architects in a sequential process of refinement.{{ref|name=twitch1|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live Summerset Story and Lore|ESO Live Summerset Story and Lore]]}}

===Law and Government===
[[File:ON-place-College of Sapiarchs.jpg|thumb|left|The College of Sapiarchs]]
Altmer society was highly stratified by the middle of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. The monarch of Summerset ruled as sovereign with the advice of the [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]], an elite and highly selective scholarly institute,{{ref|name=OtCoS|{{Cite Book|On the College of Sapiarchs}}}} and was sometimes represented by a viceroy titled the Proxy Queen or King.{{ref|name=ESOValsirenn|[[ON:Valsirenn|Valsirenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The monarch descended from an almost continuous line of royalty extending back to the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{ref|name=ESOPalace|[[ON:Alinor Royal Palace|Alinor Royal Palace]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Their behavior was regulated by a set of written precedents known as the Scrolls of Praxis, whose chief interpreters were the Sapiarchs: these formed part of a broader system of inherited norms termed the Ceremoniarchy.{{ref|name=SotCT|{{Cite Book|Sages of the Crystal Tower}}}}{{ref|name=TUQ}} The heir to the throne was required to study the Ceremoniarchy for 3,555 days in the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth before they could take the crown,{{ref|name=OtCoS}} and royal decrees were issued in its name.{{ref|name=AEtI|{{Cite Book|An End to Isolation}}}} Hereditary nobles termed kinlords and kinladies held many of the Altmer lands in fief. Altmer royalty were assisted by lieutenants titled vicereeves,{{ref|name=ESOPelidil|[[ON:Vicereeve Pelidil|Vicereeve Pelidil]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} while cities that served as county seats were administered by canonreeves, civil officials equivalent to mayors in other cultures.{{ref|name=ESOElquisa|[[ON:Canonreeve Elquisa|Canonreeve Elquisa]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

The [[Lore:Divine Prosecution|Divine Prosecution]] administers secular and religious justice,{{ref|name=TDP|{{Cite Book|The Divine Prosecution}}}} and is made up of jurisreeves overseeing investigations and rank-and-file justiciars. Jurisreeves travel and deliberate in groups of three, each with distinct responsibilities: a revelator-naganwe to conduct magical inquiries, an admanen to serve as an inspector, and a thalmilan who would weigh the evidence and render a verdict.{{ref|name=ESOLorne|[[ON:Jurisreeve Lorne|Jurisreeve Lorne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The jurisreeves may have originated as a religious order.{{ref|name=ESOEye|[[ON:Summerset Antiquities|Jurisreeve's Eye]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Under [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]], executive responsibilities were increasingly assumed by the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]], originally a bureau for the protection of Altmeri heritage.{{ref|name=Aicantar|{{Cite Book|Aicantar of Shimmerene Answers Your Questions}}}} The Thalmor incorporated the Divine Prosecution as a division at that time,{{ref|name=ESOCarawen|[[ON:Chief Justiciar Carawen|Chief Justiciar Carawen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} but the Prosecution existed long before the Thalmor's rise to power.{{ref|name=ESODaiyanni|{{Cite Book|Please Respond, Your Beloved Aunt Daiyanni}}}}

The Altmer stringently regulate traffic into Summerset, and they have banned the import of such foreign goods as books, foods and perfumes in the past.{{ref|name=ESORenzir|[[ON:Renzir|Renzir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} At the time of the First Aldmeri Dominion, the roster of forbidden books included mundane foreign writings judged to be "coarse", but the Divine Prosecution devoted particular attention to the suppression of literature promoting Daedra worship.{{ref|name=ESOTanorian|[[ON:Justiciar Tanorian|Justiciar Tanorian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

Altmer politics was transformed in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] by the return of the Thalmor as a militant Elven supremacist group following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]]. In the wake of the chaos caused by the Crisis and the fall of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], the Thalmor overthrew the Summerset monarchy in a coup d'état.{{ref|name=RTIV|{{cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} Since then, officers of the Thalmor have taken direct charge of government functions such as diplomacy and espionage.{{ref|name=SkyrimDI|Events of [[Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity|Diplomatic Immunity]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Dissidence and Reform===
[[File:LG-cardart-Thalmor Justiciar 02.jpg|thumb|left|A Fourth Era Thalmor justiciar]]
As mentioned, whenever a substantial minority had some sort of socio-political or theological disagreement with the majority in Summerset in the Dawn Era, the dissidents would leave and usually go on to become other races of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]]. However, some time in the Merethic Era, one early group of dissidents who opposed the evolution to the traditional Altmer pantheon left the mainland of Summerset but remained quintessentially Altmer. The [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijics]] decided to follow the mystical Old Ways of Aldmeris and broke away to settle [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], the third-largest island of the Isle.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}{{ref|name=BHOTE1}}{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} Though they have often refused requests for dialogue from Alinor during their periods of seclusion from Tamriel,{{ref|name=TMoA|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}} the Psijics are not hostile to the other Altmer, and have offered their magic to protect Summerset in the past.{{ref|name=ESOTS|Events of [[ON:The Tower Sentinels|The Tower Sentinels]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} A leader of the Psijics, [[Lore:Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]], led them through the entire First Era and most of the Second, a span of some 3,500 years, giving some indication of the potential longevity of the Altmer.{{ref|name=FOA}}{{ref|name=2920L|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}{{ref|name=ESOLiT|Events of [[ON:Lost in Translation|Lost in Translation]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

A subsequent bout of dissent occurred late in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], as younger Altmer again began pushing for social reform. These episodes were unusual in a society used to dissidents simply leaving.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} But there were few if any places left to go at that point. Some change had taken place by the end of the Third Era: the Altmer once more allowed foreigners to come to their shores; some non-Altmer were even allowed to rise to the ranks of nobility in Summerset.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} Many of these young rebellious Altmer turned to the Psijics for guidance, somewhat ironically consulting their distant past for a path to their future, while some militant radicals turned to terrorism.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}}

These tentative reforms were substantially reversed at the turn of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] by the rise of the Thalmor, who instituted a policy of persecution and even open slaughter of dissidents and non-Elven races.{{ref|name=RTIV}} Numerous surviving Altmer dissidents were forced to flee to other parts of Tamriel, where they were frequently targeted by Thalmor assassins.{{ref|name=SFasendil|[[Skyrim:Legate Fasendil|Legate Fasendil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=RTIV}} While many Altmer continue to disagree with the Thalmor in private, few venture to oppose them publicly.{{ref|name=BTelarendil|[[Blades:Telarendil|Telarendil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{NewLine}}

==Religion and Cosmology==
The religious worldview of the Altmer is deeply rooted in their belief that they are the direct descendants of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], or "ancestor spirits"—divine beings who gave their essence to shape the mortal world.{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{Ref|name=AD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}} According to Altmeri myth, the cosmos began before the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], when the primordial force [[Lore:Anu|Anu]], representing stasis and order, brought forth [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]], the soul of all things. Anuiel then created [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]], the personification of change and limitation, so that it might know itself.{{ref|name=S|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}} The interplay of Anuiel and Sithis gave rise to the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], the chaotic void in which the Original Spirits-that would come to be known as Aedra and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]—emerged.{{ref|name=TM}}
{{Quote Box|''[E]ven Auriel, when told he would become the king of the new world, agreed to help Lorkhan.''|[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Heart of the World]]}}
The Altmer interpret the creation of the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] as a cosmic catastrophe initiated by [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], the "Trickster God," who persuaded some spirits to participate in creating a flawed, mortal plane. This act is seen as a spiritual fall from the divine to the temporal, severing the [[Lore:Aldmer|Elves]] from their ancestral eternity.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} The emergence of the elves is described in the Altmeri creation myth, according to which some of the Aedra that stayed to work on Nirn after [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]] departed, known also as the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]],{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=Mystery|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}} chose to have offspring. The ensuing offspring grew progressively weaker than their parents with each generation, a phenomenon of generational diminishment that soon led to the emergence of the first Aldmer.{{ref|name=TM}} [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], the chief deity of the Altmeri pantheon and the soul of Anuiel, from whom most modern Altmer claim direct descent, is said to have opposed Lorkhan, led the faithful Aedra against him, and eventually ascended to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], becoming a model of apotheosis for his followers.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=ESOVairabrian|[[ON:Vairabrian|Vairabrian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-map-Summerset (physical).jpg|thumb|left|An Altmeri depiction of Summerset watched over (from left to right) by Xarxes, Auri-El, Mara, and Y'ffre/Jephre, capped by the Crystal Tower]]
Over time, social stratification influenced religious practice. The lower classes increasingly adopted the ancestor spirits claimed by their social superiors over their own, contributing to the consolidation of a more centralized pantheon, comprised of figures such as Auri-El, Trinimac, Syrabane, and Phynaster, who were among those ancestor spirits that came to be considered gods. A shift in religion opposed by some of the Aldmeri elders, who broke off to form the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] in protest.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer}} Despite this convergence, debates persist over which beings are truly worthy of veneration, and dissenters—especially those sympathetic to [[Lore:Men|Men]] or heterodox figures like [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]—are often marginalized or persecuted.{{ref|name=TGW}} Altmer view themselves as "true children of the Et'Ada", descendants of the Aedric Divines in an unbroken line dating back to Nirn's creation, a divine lineage which is thought to allow them to achieve [[Lore:Lifespan|Apotheosis]] in certain circumstances.{{ref|name=OATO}}{{ref|name=Ayrenn|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn: The Unforeseen Queen}}}}{{ref|name=TAX}} The ultimate spiritual aspiration of the Altmer is thus to transcend mortality and return to the divine state of their ancestors, following the example of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] and the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]]. In this, their religion serves not only as a cultural anchor but as a metaphysical roadmap back to the [[Lore:Stars|stars]].{{ref|name=ESOVairabrian}} The principal Altmeri pantheon consists of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y’ffre]] (also called [[Lore:Jephre|Jephre]]), [[Lore:Xarxes|Xarxes]], [[Lore:Mara|Mara]], [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], and [[Lore:Syrabane|Syrabane]].{{ref|name=VOFHE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The High Elves}}}} Additional cults also revere deities such as [[Lore:Phynaster|Phynaster]], [[Lore:Oghma|Oghma]] and, controversially, Lorkhan.{{ref|name=VOFHE}} However, limitations to eight or nine deities are considered an [[Lore:Empire|Imperial]] imposition and are generally rejected by traditionalist Altmer.{{ref|name=PGE3Summer|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}}  The Corelanya Clan has its own unique pantheon as well.{{ref|name=VoFS}}

His escape from creation, at the very last moment, cost him and what remained of him on Nirn is wielded by mortals as magic.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=TGoMag|[[Lore:The Gifts of Magnus|The Gifts of Magnus]]}} Venerations to Magnus invoke his [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye]] and the [[Lore:Aetherius|Empyrean Light]] as a request for his wisdom and patience.{{ref|name=TGoMag}} His Eye is similarly called upon to watch over spells High Elves cast.{{ref|name=MoAuriel}} Altmer invoke him in sign-offs.{{ref|name=LettersMG|[[ON:First Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|First Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy]], [[ON:Second Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|Second Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy]] and [[ON:Third Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|Third Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy]] texts in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

====[[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]]====

====[[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]]==== 
The God of the Forest, '''Y'ffre''' is recognized by the Altmer as the one who became the first Ehlnofey, creating the laws of nature and allowing mortals safety through understanding of the world.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=BLtoAld|[[ON:Brief Letter to an Aldarch|Brief Letter to an Aldarch]]}} Prayers to Y'ffre praise the god as the one who showed the Altmer people the way, and whose Bones give them "food, shelter, warmth, and companionship"{{ref|name=PB}}{{ref|name=MoAuriel}} Altmer see [[Lore:Indrik|Indriks]] as divine beings woven by the god{{ref|name=YSparkle|[[Lore:Y'frre's Sparkling Steeds|Y'frre's Sparkling Steeds]]}}, and have formed a group of [[Lore:Jephrine Paladins|paladins]] modeled after the creatures.{{ref|name=ECEBR|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/57851 Eat Cake & Earn Bonus Rewards During The ESO Anniversary Jubilee] showcasing [[Online:Jephrine Paladin Style|Jephrine Paladin Style]] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} Knowledge of the god extends to cultural splits of the Altmer, such as Clan Corelanya, who show interest in the archipelago's ways.{{ref|name=YffreCB|[[ON:Contraband A#Annotated Jephre Effigy|Annotated Jephre Effigy]] item descriptions in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some [[Lore:House of Reveries|House of Reveries]] members wear masks inspired by Y'ffre, symbolizing his tales-  be it nature and growth, or his shaping of creation.{{Ref|name=FVMask|[[ON:Reveries Flowervine Mask|Reveries Flowervine Mask]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=BYMask|[[ON:Breath of Y'ffre Mask|Breath of Y'ffre Mask]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====[[Lore:Xarxes|Xarxes]]==== 
'''Xarxes''' is the God of Ancestry and Secret Knowledge. He was Auri-El's scribe and keeps track of all Aldmeri feats since the start of time. This extends to not just individual stories, but all connections and lineage that bind the Altmer people together. It is this way that Xarxes has a steadfast role in defining Summerset society.{{ref|name=TAX|[[Lore:Tu'whacca, Arkay, Xarxes|Tu'whacca, Arkay, Xarxes]]}}{{ref|name=VOF}} It is said the Mouth of Xarxes whispers in the ears of Altmer, encouraging them to always seek, learn and discover.{{ref|name=MoAuriel}} Prayer songs to him praise him as the "scriver" and "archivist of glory" for transcribing Altmeri story.{{ref|name=PB}} He is said to have created his wife [[Lore:Oghma|Oghma]] from his favorite moments in history.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=XandO|[[Lore:Xarxes and Oghma|Xarxes and Oghma]]}} Worshippers show their devotion through idols such as statuettes.{{ref|name=XarxesStatuette|[[ON:Statuette: Auri-El and Xarxes|Statuette: Auri-El and Xarxes]] furnishing in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He is also credited with authoring the Aldmeriad, the Elven origin epic, and instructing the Altmer to create [[Lore:Oghma|Oghmas]]—everscriven scrolls that memorialize their lives as sacred duty in honor of their inherited divine spark.{{ref|name=OATO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}}

===Clan Corelanya===
The Corelanyas have a unique religious tradition, being descended from Altmer who embraced the dissident teachings of the prophet Veloth. While most of the Elves who followed Veloth eventually came to venerate the living gods of the Tribunal, the Corelanyas (like the Ashlanders of Morrowind) continue to revere Daedric powers. While the Corelanyas of old had dealings with a number of Daedric powers, during their long exile on Solstice their faith coalesced around three particular Daedra: [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], and [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]. The Elves of Solstice refer to them as the Queen of Light, the Queen of Dusk, and the Queen of Darkness. These Three Queens reign over all aspects of life, both the good and the bad.{{ref|name=VoFS|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: Solstice}}}} They have a cyclical faith, believing that the nature of their clan is the circle of history itself. Dawn, Dusk and rinse and repeat- it's a cycle they see as a beautiful truth in a difficult world. They believe their Three Queens are allied in support of their clan, though their Daedric allegiences between one another is a subject of debate in the religion.{{ref|name=SolsticeLA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Stirk Fellowship & Solstice}}}}  The Corelanyas have various worship trinkets and idols for the Three Queens, such as ivory carved icons, prayer songs and ornate washbasins.{{ref|name=CBThreeQueen|[[ON:Contraband I#Ivory Three Queens Icon|Ivory Three Queens Icon]], [[ON:Contraband T#Three Queens Washbasin|Three Queens Washbasin]], and [[ON:Contraband T#Three Queens Hymnal|Three Queens Hymnal]] item descriptions in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Besides the gods listed below, many others are worshipped- The open nature of Clan Corelanya shows itself in freedom of religion, as there are little restrictions or prohibitions on what one can worship enforced by the Regents.{{ref|name=ESOLucillaGrantho|[[ON:Lucilla Grantho|Lucilla Grantho]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]:''[[Online:Seasons of the Worm Cult|Solstice]]''}} Some of these other gods include [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]] who was introduced by Imperial traders, and found popularity among the merchants of [[Lore:Sunport|Sunport]], whilst [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] and [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] found reverence among Nord settlers- although the worship of Shor is considered somewhat uncomfortable by the Corelanyas.{{ref|name=VoFS}} The Corelanyas, particularly the [[Lore:Collegium Praxis|Collegium Praxis]], also find interest in studying the native [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] religions of [[Lore:The Stone|The Stone]] and [[Lore:The Tide|The Tide]]{{ref|name=VoFS}}, as well as Summerset religious practices.{{ref|name=YffreCB}}

===Religious Culture & History===
====The Divine Body====
The Altmer view their gods as parts of a "divine body", often articulated in religious teachings as follows: the Hand of Auri-El, Eye of Magnus, Arm of Trinimac, Bones of Y’ffre, Mouth of Xarxes, and Heart of Mara. Altmer theology holds that through devotion, they remain spiritually united with these divine aspects.{{ref|name=ESOUurkar|[[ON:Uurkar of Auri-El|Uurkar of Auri-El]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}

====The Sacred Numbers====
[[File:ON-place-Garden of the Sacred Numbers.jpg|thumb|right|The Garden of the Sacred Numbers(ESO)]]
Sacred Numbers in Altmeri numerology include [[Lore:Numerology|three]], five, and eight, each reflecting metaphysical ideals of harmony and balance. Three represents the "Prime Celestials", the [[Lore:Sun|sun]] and the [[Lore:Moons|moons]]. Five the number of the [[Lore:Elements|Elements]]: Earth, Air, Water, Fire and Aether. Eight for the eight [[Lore:Planets|Planets]] as well as the sum of three and five. These "Good Numbers" all summed together are [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Sixteen]], which is considered a very powerful number. Finally, Two is the "Bad Number" because it represents duality, which Altmer consider impossible. Due to this, the Altmer rarely do anything in twos aside from romantic couplings. It is also seen as a metaphysical force of Man, due to the common Mannish worldview of opposing forces, leading further to the belief of Two being a Bad and inauspicious number. Contrarily, Three is seen as a fulcrum, an essential and central force, which makes it an auspicious number. Because of this, certain Altmeri government roles are only done in groups of three.{{ref|name=ESOLorne}}{{ref|name=TotSN|{{Cite Book|Thoughts on the Sacred Numbers}}}} In Summerset, there's an elven garden known as the [[Lore:Garden of Sacred Numbers|Garden of Sacred Numbers]]. In [[Lore:Many Paths|another reality]], the power of the Sacred Numbers was called upon to fight a threat from beyond [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], casting endless nets of pure magicka- though even they failed in the end.{{ref|name=GSNumbersAU|{{Cite Book|Garden of Sacred Numbers Temporal Tome}}}}

====Religion in Society====
[[File:ON-load-Monastery of Serene Harmony.jpg|thumb|left|An Altmeri monastery. (ESO)]] 
Altmeri religious authority is governed by a priestly hierarchy. Aldarchs lead monastic communities dedicated to peace and meditation,{{ref|name=ESOFirinore|[[ON:Monastic Firinore|Monastic Firinore]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}} while temple priests instruct acolytes and officiate public rites.{{ref|name=ESOUurkar}} Ascendant Curates manage the conferral, refinement, and ceremonial destruction of [[Lore:Calian|calians]], which are talismanic tokens bestowed upon young Altmer at the age of majority, signifying their entrance into adult society.{{ref|name=TPoP}}{{ref|name=ESOPrayerBeads|[[Online:Pearls of Ehlnofey|Aetherquartz Prayer Beads]] codex entry in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship is officially outlawed and culturally taboo, though some Altmer, particularly those drawn to power and arcane mastery form clandestine cults to the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]].{{ref|name=ESOSeekers|Dialogue from [[ON:Seeker Duperil]] and [[ON:Seeker Veraye]] in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOBanished|[[ON:The Banished Cells I|The Banished Cells]] loading screen in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}} Civil authorities prosecute these practices harshly, and the living gods of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] are viewed as seditious heresies.{{ref|name=ESOSumSotha|[[ON:Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}} Some Altmer are venerated posthumously as saints, such as [[Lore:Ohmandil|Saint Ohmandil]] and [[Lore:Terilde|Saint Terilde]]. Their relics, including the Circlet of Saint Ohmandil and Saint Terilde's Fingerbone, are preserved and honored in temples and monasteries.{{ref|name=COSO|[[ON:Auridon Quest Items#Circlet of Saint Ohmandil|Circlet of Saint Ohmandil]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=STF|[[ON:Auridon Quest Items#Saint Terilde's Fingerbone|Saint Terilde's Fingerbone]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MN}}

With the coming of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], many Altmer resented the apotheosis of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Talos]], bitterly recalling Tiber Septim’s brutal conquest of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]].{{ref|name=RTIV|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} However, large-scale religious controversy did not erupt until the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] seized power in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. The Thalmor declared that no human like Tiber Septim, could achieve godhood,{{ref|name=SKOndolemar|[[Skyrim:Ondolemar|Ondolemar]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}} and outlawed the worship of Talos within their territories.{{ref|name=RTIV}} In the lead-up to the [[Lore:The Great War|Great War]], the ban on Talos worship was formalized as one of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]]’s demands to the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]]—demands largely seen as a pretext for an invasion that was already planned.{{ref|Events of [[Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Emperor Titus Mede II|Emperor Titus Mede II]] conceded to the ban in the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], a peace treaty that ended the conflict.{{ref|name=SkyrimWGC|Dialogue and documents in [[Skyrim:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Concordat]]}} Afterward, incidents such as the [[Lore:Markarth Incident|Markarth Incident]] saw the Thalmor demand the right to enforce the ban on Talos worship within Imperial territory, claiming the authority to uphold the treaty—despite using it to justify further espionage and suppression in preparation for renewed conflict.{{ref|name=SkyrimMarkarth|Events of [[Skyrim:Markarth|Markarth Incident]]}}{{ref|name=SkyrimThalmor|Dialogue and Thalmor dossiers in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

====[[Lore:Heart of Transparent Law|Heart of Transparent Law]]====
[[File:ON-furnishing-Replica of The Heart of Transparent Law.jpg|thumb|right|Heart of Transparent Law]]
The Heart of Transparent Law is a part of the Stone of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], Transparent Law. The Heart was taken from the Tower shortly after the founding of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] in 2E 230 by [[Lore:Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]] of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]], who was under the control of [[Online:K'Tora|K'Tora]] of the Abyssal Cabal through the use of mind magic. In a desperate attempt to keep the [[Lore:Sea Sload|Sea Sloads]] from using the Heart to sink Summerset, Iachesis enlisted the help of [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] to hide the Heart inside his own body. During the Interregnum, Nocturnal tried to take control of the Crystal Tower. Using a technique she stole from Sotha Sil utilizing life energy as a power source, she planned to use the life energy of Nirn, and then the energy of the other Princes afterwards, to amplify the Crystal Tower to make her power limitless. Nocturnal's plan was foiled by the Psijic Order and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], with the assistance of Mephala and Clavicus Vile, who had been betrayed by Nocturnal, and the Golden Knight, who was the vessel of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]. Using the divine power of Meridia's [[Lore:Dawnbreaker|Dawnbreaker]], the Vestige defeated Nocturnal and restored Transparent Law using the Heart. The Heart of Transparent Law was said to awaken a dark urge to seize it for themselves in those who looked upon it.

==Notes==
* The Altmer are a version of the modern fantasy "[[Wikipedia:Noldor|High Elf]]" archetype, shaped by genre conventions that became especially prominent after [[wikipedia:J. R. R. Tolkien|J. R. R. Tolkien]]'s [[wikipedia:Tolkien's legendarium|legendarium]]. 
** The similarities are mostly broad and thematic, such as the portrayal of an ancient, long-lived, culturally conservative people, and the social prestige attached to lineage and language.
** The noted resemblance between [[Lore:Alinor|Alinor]] (used for Summerset Isles and its capital in different periods) and [[wikipedia:Valinor|Valinor]] (Tolkien's far-western, blissful realm across the sea, where many Elves live) can also be understood in terms of their similar written form, and in the way both places evoke idyllic, luminous western homelands associated with beauty, cultural memory, and a sense of elven antiquity.
