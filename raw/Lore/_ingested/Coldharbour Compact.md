# Lore:Coldharbour Compact

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Trail|Appendices}}{{Quote Box|''Disasters such as befell Gilverdale should be averted. Only through certain intermediaries such as witches or sorcerers will they answer the call of man and mer.''|[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|Sotha Sil]]}}</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] was an agreement between the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and eight powerful [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]]: [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethia|Boethia]], [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], and [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]. The Compact was created in response to the destruction of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] by Molag Bal.{{ref|name=ONAzura|[[Online:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The summit took place in Coldharbour between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand, {{Year|1E 2920}}{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} after Sotha Sil entered [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] from [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]]'s Dreaming Cave. Little is known about the specifics of the compact, but Sotha Sil managed to persuade the Princes to cease meddling in mortal affairs directly through a private bargain. Possibly a credible threat or a significant offer was presented in exchange. It is theorized that the compact prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]],{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}}{{ref|name=CWCSothaDialogue|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} but did not stop them from exerting their will through intermediaries.{{ref|name=ONAzura}}
==Treaty Signing==
{{Quote Box|''The Coldharbour Compact was an accord reached at a specific moment in time, to solve for a specific set of variables. Like all negotiations, it is only as binding as signatories wish it to be. And its stipulations neither impacted nor excepted individuals not present at the time. You wish to see order where there is chaos and seek uniformity where there exists only exceptions. Such is the truth of Nirn. Such will it always be.''|[[Lore:Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City|Sotha Sil]]}}
According to Hermaeus Mora's [[Lore:Seeker|Seeker]]s, the prince made a pact with ''every'' mortal on Nirn to cease all direct interposition in [[Lore:Mundus|Mundial]] affairs, and for his agreement it seemed he was paid a great price, something he dearly desired. The compact appeared to bind in both directions, resulting in mortals no longer coming to [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]].{{Ref|name=SOiA|{{Cite Book|Secrets Overheard in Apocrypha}}}} 

Mehrunes Dagon himself leads credence to the idea that Sotha Sil spoke of a consequence to violating the pact.{{Ref|name=ONTWMD|{{Cite Book|Transcribed Words of Mehrunes Dagon}}}} Despite agreeing to it, Mehrunes Dagon arrived in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] and destroyed the city of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] just a few months later in Sun's Dusk of {{Year|1E 2920}}, before being banished by [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and Sotha Sil.{{ref|name=LY:V11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}{{ref|name=TTiS:V8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} For breaking the truce, Dagon himself was broken on the soil of Mournhold.{{ref|name=SSLW|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Sotha Sil's Last Words|ns_base=General}}}} After banishing him alongside Almalexia, Sotha Sil also closed the planar doors between Nirn and the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]], blocking off all access for Dagon as further punishment for breaking the Compact, and thus showing at least one punishment that could be imposed on the Princes for not following the terms of the compact.{{ref|name=ONLyranth|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Violations==
The Compact did not bind Daedric Princes that were not present during the Compact, as the [[Lore:Daedric Triad|Daedric Triad]] composed of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], and [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] attempted to remake Nirn in their image during the Daedric War of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. Mephala and Clavicus Vile would join a Compact with Sotha Sil circa {{Year|2E 582}} after Nocturnal took control of the triad's plot, and aided the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] in stopping her from achieving infinite power. Much like the Coldharbour Compact itself, the specifics of the agreement are unknown. Furthermore, it is not even certain if they are considered to be the same Compact.{{ref|name=ESOSummerset|[[Online:Summerset#Story Quests|Main story]] of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

It is in the nature of Daedric Princes to be chaotic and uncontrollable, and thus the terms of the agreement has been violated at times.{{ref|name=LACW|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City‎}}}} The Compact did not prevent Molag Bal's attempted [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] in the Second Era,{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} nor did it stop Mehrunes Dagon from manifesting directly on Nirn in his true form during the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] in {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} It is possible that Sotha Sil's demise left nobody to enforce the Compact. It's also possible that the Compact was circumvented in these cases due to them being brought about by mortals (namely [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] and [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]).<noinclude>
