The [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]] (sometimes '''Magnus' Eye'''){{ref|name=Augur}} is an ancient [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] said to have a similar origin to the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]]{{ref|name=DFCN}}{{rp|160}} and also features [[Lore:Elder Alphabet|Elder script]].{{ref|name=SREye|[[:File:SR-npc-Tolfdir 02.jpg|Eye of Magnus]]'s visual appearance in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=ElderScroll|[[Lore:Elder Alphabet|Script]] on an ''[[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]]''}} It is described as a massive ten-foot orb,{{ref|name=Prima|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Legendary Edition Prima Official Game Guide'']]}}{{rp|256}} and is said to be a "distant and mysterious object" that may have crashed into [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] like a meteor in the eons before the {{Year|Fourth Era}}.{{ref|name=DFCN|[[Books:The Art of Skyrim|''The Art of Skyrim'']]}}{{rp|160}} The Eye possesses a great amount of magical power and can be manipulated with the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]], which once belonged to the [[Lore:Magnus (god)|God of Magic]] himself.{{Ref|name=NoT}}{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} When unstable, the Eye has the potential to unmake or destroy the entire world.{{ref|name=Prima}}{{rp|267}}{{ref|[[SR:Ancano#The Eye of Magnus|Ancano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Dawn Era===
Ancient [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] mythology writes that when Magrus fled from [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]] and [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], he could only see out of one eye and fell into the [[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]]. There Azurah judged him as too full of fear to rule a sphere, and she tore out his other eye. Magrus left to the [[Lore:Aetherius|heavens]] blinded, but Azurah made of his eye a stone to reflect the {{Lore Link|Varliance Gate}}. This is the "[[Lore:Sun|Aether Prism]]", which opens at Dawn and closes at Dusk. Some sorcerers hold that Magrus left the eye willingly as an offering to Azurah and her children, and these magi still utter prayers to his name.{{Ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} However, it is not known if the Eye of Magnus has any connection to this myth.

===Merethic Era===
The Eye of Magnus was discovered by the ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] when they were building the city of [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]]. The Nords attempted to keep it buried, but the [[Lore:Snow Elves|Snow Elves]] learned of its existence and coveted it for themselves. During the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]] the elves assaulted Saarthal to secure this powerful artifact for themselves. [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] rallied together his people to keep the elves from seizing it, and the Nords were successful in preventing the elves from obtaining the artifact. The Eye was buried deep below the earth and sealed away.{{Ref|name=NoT|{{Cite Book|Night of Tears (book)}}}}{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}}

===First Era===
The evil Nord Jyrik, a depraved warlock and the son of the great mage [[Lore:Gauldur|Gauldur]], was locked away by the wizards of [[Lore:Harald|High King Harald]] in the Orb Chamber of Saarthal, where the Eye of Magnus had been placed, in order to avoid Jyrik ever being reanimated to life. And Jyrik's power had become bound up in the magic of the Eye of Magnus.{{Ref|name=DVN|{{Cite Book|Daynas Valen's Notes}}}}{{ref|name=Prima}}{{rp|256}}{{rp|787}}{{Ref|name=QuestUnderSarth}}

===Fourth Era===
In {{Year|4E 201}}, during an expedition in Saarthal, the Eye of Magnus was uncovered by members of the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] and transported to the college for examination after a confrontation with the undead corpse of Jyrik Gauldurson.{{Ref|name=QuestUnderSarth|[[Skyrim:Under Saarthal|Under Saarthal]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Ancano|Ancano]], a [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] agent, learned about the Eye's properties from the [[Lore:Augur of Dunlain|Augur of Dunlain]]{{ref|name=Augur|[[Skyrim:Augur of Dunlain|Augur of Dunlain]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Prima}}{{rp|261}} and eventually turned on the College, then attempting to activate the power of the Eye, but was prevented and killed by members of the college.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Revealing the Unseen|Revealing the Unseen]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=QuestEye|[[Skyrim:The Eye of Magnus|The Eye of Magnus]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Monks from the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]], who had been observing the situation, arrived to take the artifact, claiming that it was too unstable to keep in [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{Ref|name=QuestEye}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Quaranir|Quaranir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Ancano's meddling with the eye had long-term effects, causing Ruptures to appear across [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], through which dangerous {{Lore Link|Magic Anomaly|Magic Anomalies}} emerged. The college tried its best to close these Ruptures using the Staff of Magnus.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Containment|Containment]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Aftershock|Aftershock]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notes==
*In the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmeri]] lesson of the divine body, the phrase ''"The Eye of Magnus is always upon us, in the spells and enchantments that devout Mages conjure."'' is recited by religious preachers.{{Ref|name=ESOMirnor|Dialogue of the [[ON:Mirnor of Auri-El|Mirnor of Auri-El]]}}{{Ref|name=ESOUurkar|Dialogue of the [[ON:Uurkar of Auri-El|Uurkar of Auri-El]]}} This appears to refer to the concept of the [[Lore:Aurbic Eye|Aurbic Eye]], also sometimes called the Eye of Magnus, rather than the artifact itself.
*The orb of Vanus Galerion's Staff directly resembles the Eye of Magnus.{{ref|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion's Staff]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Seasons of the Worm Cult|Seasons of the Worm Cult]]''}}
*The central orb component of the [[Lore:The Towers#Green-Sap|Orrery of Elden Root]] was modelled to directly resemble the Eye of Magnus, but its plates rotate counter to each other when activated and featured [[Lore:Elven Alphabets|Elven script]] rather than Elder script.{{Ref|name=EldRootOrreryArt|[[:File:ON-concept-Ayleid Puzzle.jpg|Elden Root Orrery concept art]]}} Identical orbs (albeit smaller) to the Orrery of Elden Root's orb can be found all throughout the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]]'s chambers in their home [[Lore:Ceporah Tower|Ceporah Tower]].{{ref|[[Online:Ceporah Tower|Ceporah Tower]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Obscuros|The Obscuros]] artifact on [[Lore:Dranil Kir|Dranil Kir]] visually matches the orbs of Ceporah Tower.{{ref|name=Knives|[[ON:Of Knives and Long Shadows|Of Knives and Long Shadows]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}
*[[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] landed on the White River close to Winterhold looking for something or someone called the "[[Lore:Ordained Receptacle|Ordained Receptacle]]", which may or may not have been related to the Eye of Magnus.{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}{{Ref|name=WritingAUA|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO Writing Team AUA|ns_base=General}}}}
*[[:File:SR-concept-Eye of Magnus Diadem.png|Unused concept art for ''Skyrim'']] shows a wearable diadem artifact labelled "Eye of Magnus", which was meant to be embody a [[Lore:Third Eye|Third Eye]].{{ref|group=UOL|1=[https://youtu.be/D8UmVUXpfEE?si=4l6L70EA2WeBpn5l&t=225 GameInformer: ''Tour Skyrim's Bethesda Game Studios With Todd Howard'']}}
