[[Lore:Veloth|Saint Veloth]], also known as '''Veloth the Pilgrim''' or '''Veloth the Prophet''', was a [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] (or [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]){{Intnote|race}} mystic hailing from the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], either during the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] or Late Middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]. He is best known as the father of the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] race, when he and his followers split from their Aldmeri roots and migrated en masse to their new homeland called Resdayn, now known as [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{Ref|name=VTP|{{Cite Book|Veloth the Pilgrim}}}}{{ref|name=EGTMorrowind|{{Cite Book|EGT|Morrowind}}}} After his passing, Veloth became arguably one of the most revered figures in Dunmer history, and became known as the [[Lore:Temple Saints|patron saint]] of outcasts and seekers of spiritual knowledge.{{Ref|name=RoSV|{{Cite Book|Relics of Saint Veloth}}}}<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}
==Background==
[[File:LO-map-Summerset Isle (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumbnail|left|''The Summerset Isle'']]
[[File:ON-place-Mehrunes' Spite.jpg|thumbnail|right|A secret shrine for illicit worship of the Daedric Princes in Auridon]]
Despite coming from a noble background, Veloth viewed his homeland with disdain as he believed Aldmeri society was founded on ambition, greed, and decadence. The young mystic called out to his downtrodden countrymen and found support from those who hoped for a society that preserved traditions, praised honesty, and rewarded the just.{{Ref|name=VTP}}{{Ref|name=EFS|{{Cite Book|Exodus from Summerset}}}}

Seeking a more ascetic, stoic and pure way of life for his followers, Veloth inscribed the Velothi Prophecies which abandoned the commonly accepted worship of [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], in favor of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], collectively known as the "Good Daedra".{{Ref|name=DW|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Chimer}}}} He also instructed his followers on how to carefully negotiate with the "Bad Daedra", also known as [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]]. This ability to distinguish the good from the bad was a hallmark of the living saint, as was his penchant for healing and healing items.{{Ref|name=JoSV|{{Cite Book|The Judgment of Saint Veloth}}}} Some sources claim that it was Boethiah herself who influenced Veloth's decision to reject the Aedra through dreams and visions.{{Ref|name=TFoT|{{Cite Book|The Fall of Trinimac}}}} Other accounts relate that Boethia took the form of the Aedra [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] and taught Veloth and his followers the rules of [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]] - a method where supposedly mortals could ascend to become [[Lore:Gods|gods]].{{Ref|name=TCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}}

Whatever the origins of Veloth teachings, more and more Aldmeri clans accepted Veloth as their prophet and guide, and the Velothi dissident movement was born. In response, Veloth and his followers were scorned and cast out by the establishment. The [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] prohibited the schism, and Trinimac's priests condemned the movement for blasphemy and threatened exile, should Veloth's followers not abandon Veloth.{{Ref|name=DW}}{{Ref|name=TNO|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}}

==The Exodus==
[[File:LO-misc-Velothi.png|left|thumb|Depiction of Veloth leading the Chimer to Morrowind]][[File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg|thumbnail|right|A fresco of Veloth leading his followers to Resdayn]]Eventually, Veloth and his followers embarked on a mass pilgrimage from Summerset to the northeast of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], with the promise of a new land and a better future. According to the contemporary texts, he "spared not a boat, ration, or strong-armed soul among his people in this exodus and toiled to reach the land of Resdayn."{{Ref|name=VTP}}

Known as the time of Great Despair, Veloth and his followers traveled for untold weeks, crossed the sea and climbed a mighty range of mountains.{{Ref|name=EotJ|{{Cite Book|End of the Journey}}}} Many among the Chimer considered this path to be folly, but they were driven by Veloth's leadership and unyielding certainty and commitment. Veloth drove them onward, chasing a vision that had come to him in a dream. He claimed to have seen a great [[Lore:Hawk|hawk]] in the sky and vowed that the hawk would lead the Chimer to a new home.{{Ref|name=EotJ}} According to legend, they finally reached a great pass cutting through a mountain range covered in snow, but a large wall of ice blocked their path. A young woman appeared before Veloth and demanded that he swear an oath before she would lead them to their new home. Veloth cast aside his mighty Daedric warhammer and vowed to dedicate his life and soul to his people. The young woman melted the wall of ice and revealed a land of fungus and ash: Resdayn.{{Ref|name=EotJ}} Legend holds that when Veloth first laid eyes upon Resdayn, a single tear slid down his cheek and froze into crystal.{{ref|name=ONSen|[[Online:Healer Senar|Healer Senar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It later became a holy relic imbued with healing and restorative powers.{{Ref|name=RoSV}}

Not all of Veloth's followers chose to abandon their lives in Summerset. Some, bound by duty, family, or a reluctance to forsake their ancestral homes, remained behind, continuing to observe elements of the Velothi faith in secret. Among these were the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanyas]],{{Ref|name=VOFS|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: Solstice}}}}{{Ref|name=HOCC}} descendants of Kinlady [[Lore:Valarmil Corela|Valarmil Corela]]. While they did not follow Veloth to Morrowind, their faith remained strong. After High King [[Lore:Tenalarion|Tenalarion]] imposed harsher restrictions on Velothi beliefs following the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Rebellion]], the Corelanyas chose exile over submission. They first settled in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and later established their new homeland on the island of [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]]. Though they maintained their veneration of Azura, over time their Daedric worship evolved to include Nocturnal, Molag Bal and Meridia, eventually turning away from Molag Bal in favor of the latter.{{Ref|name=HOCC|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya}}}}
{{NewLine}}

==The Golden Age==
Veloth's mass pilgrimage to the new land was a success. After renouncing all ties to the Aldmer, Veloth's followers became known as the Chimer and founded a new nation based on Daedric principles. The Chimer settled the area rapidly and flourished, enjoying a period of high culture known to many as the Golden Age.{{Ref|name=VTP}}{{Ref|name=DW}}{{Ref|name=VoFDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=EGTMorrowind}} All manner of cultural advances are attributed to Boethiah, from philosophy to magic to 'responsible' architecture and spirituality.{{Ref|name=VoFDE}}{{ref|name=EGTMorrowind}}

One of the first Chimeri settlements in Resdayn was named in honor of Veloth: Narsis-Where-All-Fervently-Praise-the-Prophet-Veloth, which was later shortened to [[Lore:Narsis|Narsis]].{{ref|name=ONNarsis|[[Online:Narsis Ruins|Narsis Ruins]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Although the Chimer built several Daedric shrines while in Summerset, they were done in secret.{{ref|name=ONMehrSpite|[[Online:Mehrunes' Spite|Mehrunes' Spite]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In their new homeland there was no need for secrecy, which allowed them to build mighty temples to Boethiah, Azura, and Mephala, and established the traditions of worship in Morrowind that were later co-opted by the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]].{{Ref|name=DW}} Veloth also planted various seeds from Summerset which grew into trees across Morrowind, although only one, the [[Lore:Tree of Summerset|Tree of Summerset]] survived in the following centuries.{{ref|name=ONForlorn|[[Online:Forlorn One|Forlorn One]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Veloth was credited with first recognizing the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]] and informing Boethiah of it. His detection of the anomaly resulted in several gods, even the Four Corners of the House of Troubles, rising up to protect Morrowind from its effects.{{ref|name=WWYWTDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}} It is unknown whether Veloth was alive for these events or assisted in a spiritual state. If he was indeed alive, he would have been a Dunmer following the race's transformation.

==Legacy==
[[File:ON-concept-Shrine of Veloth.jpg|left|thumb|Shrine of Veloth (concept art)]]
[[File:ON-place-Eidolon's Hollow.jpg|thumbnail|right|Eidolon's Hollow. One of the holiest sites of Chimer worship of the Good Daedra.]]
Veloth became one of the most revered and venerated figures in Dunmer history.{{Ref|name=RoSV}} Veloth is thought of mainly as a peaceful and scholarly soul, to which the Dunmer's healing enchantments that bear his name attest.{{Ref|name=VTP}} Ancient Velothi allegories are uniformly heroic successes of Boethiah over enemies of every type, foundation stories of Chimeri struggle. The spiritual beliefs and metaphysical ideals of Lord [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]] spring directly from the teachings of Veloth, whose movement he regarded as an element of barbarism necessary to break from Aldmeri culture.{{Ref|name=VoFDE}}

Over time, some of Veloth's teaching surrounding the worship of the Good Daedra fell out of favor among the Dunmer, an act that the spirit of Veloth, curiously, agreed with, stating that his time had passed.{{ref|name=ONVeloth|[[Online:Saint Veloth|Saint Veloth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] prevailed as the dominant faith circa 1E 700, and Veloth was canonized as a greater saint, patron of outcasts and seekers of spiritual knowledge.{{Ref|name=LotS|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Saints}}}} Conversely, the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlander]] cult rejects the Tribunal completely and hold true to the teachings and traditions of the Prophet Veloth. They believe that embracing the comforts and decadency of civilization is exactly what corrupted the Aldmer back in Summerset.{{ref|name=NCN|{{Cite Book|Nerevarine cult notes}}}}{{Ref|name=ZN|{{Cite Book|Zainsubani's Notes}}}}{{Ref|name=PoT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}}

Several houses of worship exist in [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] dedicated to Veloth's memory and to the veneration of relics left by him and his original followers.{{Ref|name=RoSV}} Throughout [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] are numerous shrines dedicated to Veloth, which grant blessings bolstering Magicka.{{ref|name=Shrine|[[Morrowind:Shrine of St. Veloth#St. Veloth|Shrine of St. Veloth]] blessing in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Visitors to Saint Veloth's Shrine are customarily given small books of prayers in exchange for voluntary contributions.{{ref|name=ContrabandS1|[[ON:Contraband S#Saint Veloth Prayer Book|Saint Veloth Prayer Book]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Not only is he the patron saint of [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]],{{Ref|name=MotDGH|{{Cite Book|Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses}}}} but also settlements like [[Lore:Ald Velothi|Ald Velothi]].{{Ref|name=MWTheldyn|[[Morrowind:Theldyn Virith|Theldyn Virith]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Veloth's people honored him so much, his influence is apparent generations later. The [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] on the border of Morrowind and [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] are named after him, as are the Velothi towers of Morrowind.{{ref|name=EGTMorrowind}}{{ref|name=MW|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Certain common items are crafted by his followers to bear his likeness.{{ref|name=ContrabandS2|[[ON:Contraband S#Saint Veloth Nutcracker|Saint Veloth Nutcracker]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The golden age of Chimeri history is known as the period of High Velothi Culture.{{Ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The influence of antiquity is keenly felt, as "Velothi" is still an accurate descriptor of the Dunmer people, and is used in connection with references to their oldest cultural traditions.{{Ref|name=VTP}}{{ref|name=EGTMorrowind}}{{ref|name=TS|{{Cite Book|The Stranger}}}}{{ref|name=TSV|{{Cite Book|The Seven Visions}}}} Veloth's lineage supposedly lives on through the [[Lore:Veloth (family)|Veloth]] family, who have a long and prominent history in Vvardenfell.{{Ref|[[Online:Veloth Ancestral Tomb|Veloth Ancestral Tomb]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

There were elements of syncretism in the personal beliefs of some Dunmer who chose to follow the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]]. Some of them viewed the sacred toil, fortitude, and triumph in the face of hardship spoken of by the prophet Veloth as virtues best embodied by [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]].{{Ref|name=UV|[[ON:Uvoo Vadryon|Uvoo Vadryon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

==Veloth's Relics==
[[File:ON-item-Veloth's-Judgement.png|thumbnail|right|Veloth's Judgment]]A number of powerful healing relics are associated with Saint Veloth. Many of these holy artifacts were protected in the vaults of [[Lore:Mournhold Temple|Mournhold Temple]] in the centuries following his death.{{Ref|name=JoSV}}

===[[Lore:Veloth's Judgment|Veloth's Judgment]]===
The magical Daedric warhammer Saint Veloth wielded during his exodus from Summerset.{{Ref|name=RoSV}} This symbol of power stood for everything Veloth embodied. Veloth's Judgment was said to have rang with authority throughout the land. Its enchantments could cleanse corruption from the souls of those it struck down.{{Ref|name=JoSV}} Veloth's precision with the hammer was such that he could remove the corruption from a soul and leaving the remainder alive and healthy. The warhammer stored the corruption for a time, turning it into energy that could be used by the wielder to enhance the power of the [[Lore:Weapons|weapon]]. Veloth saw fit to set aside this weapon when he vowed to turn his attention from war to the task of building a new home for the Chimer in Resdayn.{{Ref|name=RoSV}} Since Veloth's ascension to sainthood, the hammer was rumored to have gained legendary powers, to the point where it can siphon a living creature's entire soul.{{Ref|name=JoSV}} This rumor turned out to be true when the hammer was [[#The Maulborn Threat|stolen from the temple]] in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. It was eventually returned to the Temple and in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] it was in possession of a priest in [[Lore:Molag Mar|Molag Mar]]. It subsequently made its way into the hands of the [[Lore:Hero|Nerevarine]], who later sold it to the [[Lore:Museum of Artifacts|Museum of Artifacts]] in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=MW}}

===Shield of Saint Veloth===
Shield of Saint Veloth was a blessed shield he bestowed to the Vestige after they restored his Shrine,{{ref|name=Shield|{{Item Link|Saint Veloth's Shield|level=23|quality=2|id=15809}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} alongside with the power of his holy favor that ensured that his hammer could not kill them.{{ref|name=ONVeloth}}
===The Tear of Saint Veloth===
This radiant crystal is believed to be a tear shed by Veloth when he first laid eyes upon the land of Resdayn, the new homeland he had seen in his visions. According to the legend, the tear froze as it slid down Veloth's cheek. Kept by the monks of the monastery of [[Lore:Muth Gnarr|Muth Gnarr]], this crystal has great healing and [[Lore:Restoration|restorative]] powers - provided the monks maintain a constant vigil and pattern of prayer.{{Ref|name=RoSV}}

===The Holy Vessel of Veloth===
This blessed decanter was used by Veloth to carry water to quench the thirst of his sick followers during the exodus, and then later to irrigate the young crops of the new homeland.
In the Second Era, the Holy Vessel was housed in [[Lore:Selfora|Selfora]] Temple, Deshaan.{{Ref|name=RoSV}} It is said to be the most important relic in the temple, and the reason why the temple exists at all.{{ref|name=THV|{{Cite Book|The Holy Vessel|ns_base=ON}}}}

===The Reliquary of Saint Veloth===
In the [[Lore:Shrine of Saint Veloth|Shrine of Saint Veloth]] in Deshaan stands the Reliquary of Saint Veloth. Not a relic, per se, but instead contains perhaps the most powerful and holy relics associated with Veloth - his skull, known as the Sacred Cranium, and bones.{{ref|name=ONHlen|[[Online:Acolyte Hlen|Acolyte Hlen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The bones of the saint are said to have many magical properties, and pilgrims travel from across Morrowind to receive the saint's blessing. Legends claim that the most faithful sometimes receive visions of the saint while meditating and praying before the Reliquary.{{Ref|name=RoSV}}

===The Book of Veloth===
The Book of Veloth is an ancient book of laws that judges use for rulings. When [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|King Helseth]] was destroying temples and outlawing slavery, he had consulted the Book of Veloth for appropriate punishment.{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 12: Playing Houses|''Playing Houses'']]}}

==The Maulborn Threat==
In {{Year|2E 582}}, many of Veloth's relics were involved in [[Lore:Magistrix Vox|Magistrix Vox]]'s scheme to de-throne the Tribunal. Magistrix Vox bore a personal vendetta against [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and sought to return Morrowind to Daedric worship.{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

To achieve her goal, she first unleashed the [[Lore:Llodos Plague|Llodos Plague]] on Deshaan in order to raise an army of plague husks. In the Muth Gnarr monastery, the Tear of Saint Veloth was used by temple monks to slow the effects of the plague. However, a violent tribe of Ashlanders called the [[Lore:Vereansu Tribe|Vereansu]] believed this act had defiled the Tear, and they attempted to destroy it until their plan was thwarted by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], working with the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]].{{ref|name=ONHB|[[Online:Honor Bound (Deshaan)|Honor Bound]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONSen}}{{ref|name=ONRav|[[Online:Healer Ravel|Healer Ravel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Vox also stole Veloth's Judgment from Mournhold Temple. After the town of Selfora refused to ally with her, she used the hammer to create a dimensional tear, releasing hostile spirits into the settlement.{{ref|name=ONArth|[[Online:Tidyn Arthalen|Tidyn Arthalen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RtV|{{Cite Book|Response to Vox, First Draft|ns_base=ON}}}} The Vestige, with the aid of a temple priest used the Holy Vessel to seal the tear and capture the echo spirit of Vox.{{ref|name=ONArth}}{{ref|name=ONRR|[[Online:Rescue and Revenge|Rescue and Revenge]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In an effort to remove the spirit of Saint Veloth from the battle, Vox traveled to the Shrine of Saint Veloth and called forth the spirit of Veloth. She unleashed a wave of destruction and defiled the Shrine by shattering his reliquary and scattering his bones.{{ref|name=ONGami|[[Online:Acolyte Gami|Acolyte Gami]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This act weakened the spirit of Veloth, and his connection to the mortal plane began to fade.{{ref|name=ONVeloth}} The Vestige recovered Veloth's bones and restored the Reliquary. In return, the spirit of Veloth bestowed a blessing onto the Vestige to protect them in their coming battle against Vox. The Vestige defeated Vox and Veloth's Judgment was returned to Mournhold Temple.{{ref|name=ONASA|[[Online:A Saint Asunder|A Saint Asunder]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
