# Lore:Tonal Architecture

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Trail|Magic}}[[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[File:CT-Events-Dwemer 4 (Large).png|Corrupting force created by Tonal Architecture|thumb|left]][[File:ON-place-Dwarven Tonal Barrier.jpg|thumb|right|A Dwarven Centurion generating a tonal barrier]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Tonal Architecture|Tonal architecture]] is the manipulation of sound to alter reality. The most notable users of tonal architecture were the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] tonal architects who could shape tones through their own vocalizations, amplified by magical torcs;{{ref|name=ONTorc|[[Online:Torc of Tonal Constancy|Torc of Tonal Constancy]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} they would wear protective headgear known as Dwemeri tonal attenuators, which would protect their ears and thought-organs from the harmonic feedback fluctuations of their work.{{ref|name=ONDwemerTonalAttenuator|[[Online:Dwemeri Tonal Attenuator|Dwemeri Tonal Attenuator]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Dwemer exhibited near total mastery of tonal forces and would use it in mining, medicine, architecture, and even psychology. Ancient [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]i scholarship states that the Dwemer could employ tonal forces to bend weaker minds to their will—a form of complex aural hypnosis. The Dwemer were able to create massive devices called tonal resonators, complex architectural wonders that stood taller than the most imposing giants, and filled cavernous chambers with pipes, dials, and pistons. When enabled, the resonators released a series of powerful tones that could alter the brainwaves of lesser mer and men, with the ability to induce deep calm, profound pleasure, or paranoia and terror; it is said these devices had virtually limitless uses.{{Ref|name=GTDMS|{{Cite Book|A Guide to Dwemer Mega-Structures}}}}<noinclude>

In the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], following the discovery of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] by the Dwemer, [[Lore:Kagrenac|Lord Kagrenac]] and his tonal architects created [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Kagrenac's Tools]] to manipulate the Heart via tonal architecture and use it to create their own god, the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]].{{ref|name=MWYagrum|[[MW:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[MW:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}} Instead, this act ultimately led to the disappearance of the Dwemer in {{Year|1E 700}}, and the tools were subsequently used by the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] and [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] to achieve divinity.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}}

A Dwarven Resonator was discovered in the [[Lore:Kwama|Kwama]] mines of [[Lore:Gnisis|Gnisis]], when miners uncovered a Dwemer ruin within. It caused madness to the kwama inside, making the kwama hostile and making them turn on their own queen and on the miners. The miners inside also became mad, becoming fixated upon the song, humming it over and over and becoming fixated on making the melody right. People afflicted by it described the experience as being able to see the melody, its shimmer, putting a tone and a color to describe it. The resonator itself was disabled by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] and the miners were able to return to normal. Theories existed of it being a mind control device.{{ref|name=AMM|[[ON:A Melodic Mistake|A Melodic Mistake]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

[[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] drew inspiration from the Dwemer for his creations, and their influence in his work is rumored to be traceable as far back as before their disappearance.{{Ref|name=TSLT|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Among these influences was his research on Dwemer tonal architecture, which he uses to perform his divine workings. He refined it further and created items based on their research, such as improving Dwemer tonal forks{{ref|name=ONDwarvenTonalFork|[[Online:Dwarven Tonal Forks|Dwarven Tonal Forks]] memento description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} that could function as a divining rod,{{ref|name=ContFork|{{Item Link|Divaricated Tone Fork|quality=5|id=125355}} item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the [[Lore:Resonant Sphere|Resonant Sphere]], one of Sotha Sil's minor marvels. The sphere produces an aural response upon applying magic to it, and chimes with a sound similar to the ones that ring in the [[Lore:Brass Fortress|Brass Fortress]].{{ref|name=RVDCWC|[[Online:Raynor Vanos|Raynor Vanos]]' dialogue in [[Online:DLC#Clockwork City|ESO: Clockwork City]]}}

==Notes==
*The first reference to tonal architecture may have come from ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''. After the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] retrieved the [[Lore:Hammer of Gharen|Hammer of Gharen]], King Casik of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] struck it on the [[Lore:Anvil of Mithas|Anvil of Mithas]]. A piercing tone rang from the anvil and it revealed the location of the mines of [[Lore:Dagoth-Ur (place)|Dagoth-Ur]]. Both the artifacts are of Dwemer origin.{{Ref|name=ARDagoth|[[Arena:Dagoth-Ur|Dagoth-Ur]] story quest in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}
*The [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]] is a form of magic that also involves altering reality by manipulation sound, drawing some comparisons to Tonal Architecture.{{ref|name=LTUpdate22|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Elsweyr & Update 22 AUA|ns_base=GEN}}}}
