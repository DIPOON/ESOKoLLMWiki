# Lore:Mechanical Heart

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}[[File:LG-cardart-The Mechanical Heart.png|thumb|The Mechanical Heart as depicted in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]'']]
:''"The new Heart of Lorkhan will be completed. And it will power my city for all eternity.'"'' —[[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]

</noinclude>The [[Lore:Mechanical Heart|Mechanical Heart]], also referred to as the New Heart of Lorkhan, is a mechanical replication of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] created by Sotha Sil. It first began development shortly after the events that occurred in {{Year|2E 882}}, where [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] awakened and then ambushed and nearly killed the Tribunal during their annual pilgrimage to [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] to renew their divine powers. The event resulted in the Tribunal being cut off from access to the Heart of Lorkhan, and the Mechanical Heart project would be conceived by Sotha Sil as a backup to ameliorate the loss of the original Heart of Lorkhan.{{ref|name=TRMTCC|Events that occur in the mission [[Legends:The Red Mountain|The Red Mountain]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}<noinclude>

The Mechanical Heart was stored and would be developed in heavy security deep within the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City's]] [[Lore:Cogitum Centralis|Cogitum Centralis]] in the Chamber of Lorkhan. The Cogitum Centralis contained an automated defense system controlled by a master [[Lore:factotum|factotum]].{{ref|name=Aios|[[Online:Aios|Aios]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

In its early stages of development, the Heart proved to be dangerously unstable and Sotha Sil devised a plan to contain it as it threatened to destroy the entirety of his Clockwork City. With the creation of his own variant of [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Kagrenac's Tools]], he was able to control the Heart, and even shape or dismantle it if necessary.{{ref|name=SSDTTRTCC|[[Legends:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The Tools|The Tools]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} His plans would be rushed, as in {{Year|3E 427}}, the goddess [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], having been driven mad by her loss of immortality, manipulated the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] to retrieve [[Lore:Barilzar's Mazed Band|Barilzar's Mazed Band]], which she used to travel to the Clockwork City to murder Sotha Sil in cold blood. Knowing that one day his friend Almalexia may come to kill him,{{ref|name=Aios|[[Online:Aios|Aios]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}, he attempted in his final moments to ensure that his City would live on after his death. He was successful in sealing the Heart within the Chamber of Lorkhan, where his machines would finish the construction of the Heart.{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS|[[Legends:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The Last Stand|The Last Stand]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}

The Mechanical Heart was completed in {{Year|4E 201}}, approximately 649 years after it was first conceived and 207 years after Sotha Sil's death. Sotha Sil's creations previously required his presence, with the city's functions going into a slumbering mode in his absence.{{ref|name=LucianaPullo|[[Online:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Luciana Pullo]]' dialogue in the [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} His presence was no longer needed as the heart continued to power the city and sustain its inhabitants even after his demise.{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS|[[Legends:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The Last Stand|The Last Stand]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} The Heart also allowed the realm to find new life, as the city had been overrun with vibrant flora, with vines covering the clockwork architecture and abundant plant life even piercing out of various buildings in the city's landscape,{{ref|name=LRTCCTCF|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The City Falls|The City Falls]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} possibly making the Everwound Wellspring obsolete.

It is unknown what happened to the Mechanical Heart, but Sotha Sil had made a "[[Lore:Hero#Forgotten Hero|rounding error]]" in his calculations.{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS}} Stories tell of this error coming to play with the legend of the Forgotten Hero, who ventured into the Clockwork City and defeated the former Clockwork Apostle, [[Lore:Mecinar|Mecinar]], but the stories differ from there. Some claim that the Forgotten Hero destroyed the Heart in order to cut Mecinar's divine power to fight on equal ground, and the city ceased all functions, its inhabitants forced to abandon their home.{{ref|name=LRTCCTCF|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The City Falls|The City Falls]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} Others claim that the hero used the Heart to harness its power and fought Mecinar on equal ground, ensuring the Heart would live on, along with the city. The Heart was too much for the hero to handle and they were disintegrated, with the hero's consciousness living on within the city.{{ref|name=LRTCCKJE|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue in the mission [[Legends:Journey's End|Journey's End]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}{{ref|name=RCWC|Events of [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}{{ref|name=RTCC|[[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]] story in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}
