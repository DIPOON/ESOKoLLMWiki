[[Lore:Adjacent Place|Adjacent Places]] are realms within the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] that are [[Lore:Magical Transportation|accessible by magic]].{{ref|name=Sermon26}}{{ref|name=FAW}} Some sources say they exist as parallel reflections of the realms they are 'adjacent' of.{{ref|name=MKAMA|group=UOL|[[GEN:Michael Kirkbride AMA|Michael Kirkbride r/teslore AMA]]}} Severe [[Lore:Magic|magical]] anomalies that disrupt the normal non-cardinal points can open breaches to Adjacent Places.{{ref|name=Sermon26|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 26}}}} Arch-[[Lore:Conjuration|conjurer]] [[Lore:Corvus Direnni|Corvus Direnni]] wrote that he would temporarily lodge his [[Lore:Flame Atronach#Flame Atronach Wolf|Flame Atronach Wolf]] in a pocket realm Adjacent Place.{{ref|name=FAW|[[ON:Flame Atronach Wolf|Flame Atronach Wolf]] mount description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Though some sources claim that there are multiple Adjacent Places,{{ref|name=FAW}}{{ref|name=MKAMA|group=UOL}} others point to one, unique '''Adjacent Place'''. The [[Lore:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]] claims to have an alternate form in the Adjacent Place.{{ref|[[ON:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]]'s [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|teachings]] tell of the Adjacent Place, describing it as a forbidden realm of apology for absolution and the illusion of the vocal.{{ref|name=Sermon27|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 27}}}} The teachings tell of a people known as [[Lore:Grabber|Grabbers]] who inhabited the Adjacent Place, having never built a city of their own. The Grabbers tried to conquer [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] at some point by transforming the concept of Ha-Note into a monster named [[Lore:City-Face|City-Face]]. The monster was stopped by the [[Lore:Gods|god]] [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]], who showed the Grabbers the error of their false hope.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 30}}}}<noinclude>
