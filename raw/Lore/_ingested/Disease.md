# Lore:Disease

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

[[Lore:Disease|Disease]] is widespread throughout [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] and beyond. [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] has its fair share of diseases, many of them fatal. Diseases can be transmitted through contact with the afflicted or through fighting animals and undead. They can also be transmitted magically or through dart traps. Most common diseases can be cured by magic, blessings or potions. The diseases brought about by the [[Lore:The Blight|Blight]] usually require special spells or potions to cure, but few survived the defeat of [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]]. Diseases can be resisted as well as cured; the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] are famous for their natural immunity to almost all disease. Certain diseases such as [[#Corprus|Corprus]] and [[#Porphyric Hemophilia|Porphyric Hemophilia]] cannot be resisted. [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] is the Daedric Prince of Pestilence and therefore most diseases are under his domain.
====Ash Woe Blight====
Ash Woe Blight is an acute blight disease affecting the victim's will and thought processes. It may be contracted from corprus beasts or other blighted creatures.

====Ash-Chancre====
Ash-Chancre is an acute blight disease affecting the victim's behavior. It may be contracted from corprus beasts or other blight monsters.

====Astral Vapors====
A magical disease which circumvents resistance. Symptoms include stunted and damaged magicka reserves. It may be caught from powerful [[Lore:Dread Zombie|Dread Zombies]].

====Ataxia====
Ataxia is a mild common disease affecting the victim's strength and dexterity. Symptoms include generalized pain, muscle stiffness and paleness. This can affect the victim's muscle coordination with complex tasks. It may be contracted from [[Lore:Slaughterfish|slaughterfish]], [[Lore:Bear|bear]]s, [[Lore:Zombie|zombie]]s, [[Lore:Skeever|skeever]]s and [[Lore:Alit|alit]]. Ataxia can weaken a victim's bones, eventually leading to death if not cured.{{ref|name=NftToK|{{Cite Book|ns_base=SR|Note from the Temple of Kynareth}}}} The disease was supposedly quite problematic in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] circa {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|name=SRAd|[[SR:Arcadia|Arcadia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

====Azure Blight====
{{Lore:Azure Blight}}

====Black-Heart Blight====
Black-Heart Blight is an acute blight disease affecting the victim's strength and endurance. It may be contracted from corprus beasts or other blight monsters. The disease has also spread to zombies, allowing it to live on despite the ending of the Blight in {{Year|3E 427}}.

====Blood Lung====
Contracted from rats, victims of Blood Lung suffer from loss of endurance.

====Blood Rot====
Sometimes fatal, Blood Rot adversely affects the victim's health, personality, and willpower.

====Bone Break Fever====
Contracted from rats and bears, victims of Bone Break Fever suffer from loss of strength and stamina.

====Brain Fever====
Brain Fever results in a slow death, as the victim's willpower, health and even personality trickle away, day by day.

====Brain Rot====
Brain Rot is contracted from zombies and hagravens. Symptoms include loss of strength and magicka.

====Brown Rot====
Brown Rot is a mild common disease affecting the victim's strength and behavior. Symptoms include necrosis and sleeplessness. It may be contracted from the undead.

====Caliron's Curse====
Some people have recovered from Caliron's Curse by themselves in a week or two, but their strength, speed, and agility remain stunted. It is best to cure the disease quickly. It is named after the eponymous [[Lore:Caliron|Caliron]].

====Cannibal's Prion====
Cannibal's Prion affects the victim's agility and intelligence. It can be contracted from partaking in cannibalism.

====Chanthrax Blight====
Chanthrax is an acute blight disease affecting the victim's dexterity and mobility. It may be contracted from corprus beasts or other blight monsters. The disease has also spread to boars and zombies, allowing it to live on despite the ending of the Blight in {{Year|3E 427}}.

====Chills====
Chills is an extremely dangerous common disease affecting the victim's mind and coordination. Symptoms include clumsiness and mental confusion. It may be contracted from the undead.

====Cholera====
Cholera is one of the deadliest diseases in Tamriel, and is always fatal unless a cure is provided very quickly.

====Chrondiasis====
Chrondiasis is a magical disease which consumes the victim's intelligence and innate magicka day by day. Unless cured, this will eventually result in death.

====Collywobbles====
Collywobbles is a serious common disease affecting a victim's strength, endurance, and mobility. Symptoms include uncontrollable shaking and chronic weariness. It may be contracted from shalk and zombies.

====Consumption====
The willpower, agility, and strength of a victim of Consumption will slowly leave them, until they are cured or they die.

====Corprus====
{{Lore:Corprus}}

====Dampworm====
Dampworm is common disease with serious effects. Symptoms include uncontrollable muscle spasms and twitching. It may be contracted from deer and nix-hounds.

====Dementia====
Dementia is a hideous disease which slowly robs the victim of their intelligence, willpower, and even personality.

====Droops====
Droops is a serious common disease that affects a victim's strength and ability to use a weapon. Symptoms include weak and flaccid muscle tissues. It may be contracted from all forms and stages of [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]], as well as [[Lore:Sheep|sheep]] and [[Lore:Ash Hopper|ash hoppers]].

====Feeble Limb====
Contracted from rats, victims of Feeble Limb suffer from loss of strength.

====Flay Blight====
Flay Blight ravaged the western shore of Vvardenfell throughout the long and scorching summer of {{Year|2E 565}}. Hundreds of Dark Elves contracted rashes that irritated the flesh and blotched the skin. After an extensive investigation by the [[Lore:Council of Healers|Council of Healers]], it was categorically concluded that the Flay Blight was the direct result of a change to the alchemical formula of Mystic's Majestic Face Cream.{{ref|name=SBbaoN|{{Cite Book|Skin Blights By Any Other Name}}}}

====Frost Limbs====
The disease can be contracted in the wilds around [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]].

====Gangrene====
A repulsive disease associated with [[Lore:Namira|Namira]].

====Gray Fever====
Gray Fever is a disease with similar symptoms to Lunar Chills.{{ref|name=The Healing}}

====Greenspore====
Greenspore is a serious common disease affecting a victim's behavior. Symptoms include irritability and violent outbursts, and may include mild dementia. It may be contracted from [[Lore:Slaughterfish|slaughterfish]] or [[Lore:Zombie|zombie]]s.{{ref|name=MWSlaughterfishQuest|[[Morrowind:The Man Who Spoke to Slaughterfish|The Man Who Spoke to Slaughterfish]] Quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=MWMehraDrora|[[Morrowind:Mehra Drora|Mehra Drora]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

====Grievous Harm====
This disease can be contracted in the wilds around [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]] and in the dungeons of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]].

====Guedoilic Plague====
Lord [[Lore:Auberon Flyte|Auberon Flyte]] of [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]], contracted this plague after an encounter with an Argonian ambassador, which caused the once strong and healthy lord to become sickly and weak, in state of chronic infirmity.{{ref|name=DFC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}

====Gutworm====
Gutworm is a disease that can be contracted from [[Lore:Troll|troll]]s in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. Symptoms include reduced stamina and obtaining less nutrition from consumed food.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Diseases|Diseases]] from [[SR:Survival Mode|Survival Mode]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

====Helljoint====
Helljoint is a mild common disease affecting a victim's mobility and dexterity. Symptoms include persistent irritation and inflammation of joints. It may be contracted from wolves, cliff racers and zombies.

====Leprosy====
A repulsive disease associated with [[Lore:Namira|Namira]]. Victims of Leprosy slowly waste away, unless a cure is provided.

====Lunar Chills====
Lunar Chills is an unusual disease that is often mistaken for Gray Fever. It can be lethal, but [[Lore:Hoar Frost Root|Hoar Frost Root]] can be used to cure it.{{ref|name=The Healing|Events of [[Daggerfall:The Healing|The Healing]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

====Madness====
[[File:LG-cardart-Drive Mad.png|thumb|right|Madness is prevalent in the Shivering Isles]]
'''Madness''' (also called '''Insanity'''){{Ref|name=MWMilo|[[Morrowind:Mehra Milo|Mehra Milo]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} is any illness of the mind that limits rational thought. It can be brought about by any number of factors. The [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of madmen are the property of [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Madness. 

To the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],  it is called '''soul sickness'''. When [[Lore:Dagoth Uthol|Dagoth Uthol]] took over the ruins of [[Lore:Kogoruhn|Kogoruhn]] in {{Year|3E 400}}, soul sickness began to spread to regions closer to [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]].{{Ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} Years later in {{Year|3E 427}}, it became more prevalent as [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] continued to vie for power against the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]]. Those weak in their faith were targeted by Dagoth Ur and stricken with strange dreams and uncontrollable impulses that caused them to commit bad deeds. Those that were strong in their faith to the Tribunal were able to resist these attacks but those that were susceptible were advised to seek counsel from the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]].{{Ref|name=MWMilo}}

====Noxiphilic Sanguivoria====
Noxiphilic Sanguivoria is a strain of vampirism that was common during the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. Its origin can be traced back to the first vampire, [[Lore:Lamae Beolfag|Lamae Beolfag]]. Unlike other vampiric diseases such as [[#Porphyric Hemophilia|Porphyric Hemophilia]] and [[#Sanguinare Vampiris|Sanguinare Vampiris]], vampires of this strain are not weakened by daylight, but are instead strengthened during the night.{{ref|name=NS|{{Cite Book|Noxiphilic Sanguivoria (book)}}}} They also do not grow stronger as they starve; instead, they grow more powerful as they feed. Vampires afflicted by noxiphilic sanguivoria can shroud themselves in mist.{{ref|name=ESOVamp|[[ON:Vampirism|Vampirism]] mechanics and associated disease in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Full-blown vampirism can be avoided by receiving disease-curing services from a priest of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]], and not seeking to complete the blood ritual required to become a vampire.{{ref|name=NS}} The only known way for a [[Lore:Soul Shriven|soul shriven]] to become a vampire is to be exsanguinated and have their blood replaced by that of Lamae Beolfag herself.

====Ostraekeratic Fever====
A rare virus that begins to show symptoms when dark and rigid splotches appear on the victim's arms and chest, eventually hardening the skin like stone or oyster shell. It also causes blindness and eventually drives the victim to insanity. It is speculated to have been created by the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] after a merchant fleet was shipwrecked on a Sload island during the mid-Second Era and the entire crew became infected.{{ref|name=ONNora|[[Online:Norasea|Norasea]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Any attempts to fragment the developing stones will not stop the disease's progression; it will instead spread faster. The disease spreads in the blood, converting elements of the blood into rigid grains that accumulate in the veins just beneath the skin, then clumping together into larger growths. The only known cure for the disease is giving those inflicted Porphyric Hemophilia and turning the infected into a vampire, provided that the virus has not progressed far enough.{{ref|name=ONNora}}{{ref|name=HaladanNotes|[[Lore:Haladan's Research Journal|Haladan's Research Journel]]}}

====Plague====
The Plague is a very serious, often fatal disease. Very quickly it will spread through the victim's system. Few who contract it live for more than a few days unless they are cured quickly. The Plague has been known to wipe out entire cities.

====Porphyric Hemophilia====
Known as the "vampire's disease", those infected with Porphyric Hemophilia will succumb to [[Lore:Vampire|vampirism]] after a three day incubation period. By itself, the disease affects the victim's stamina, and on the third night of infection the victim will become a vampire. The disease can easily be cured within the three-day period, but vampirism is notoriously hard to cure. The disease is similar to the less-common [[Lore:Sanguinare Vampiris|Sanguinare Vampiris]] and [[Lore:Noxiphilic Sanguivoria (disease)|Noxiphilic Sanguivoria]].

====Rabies====
Rabies is a serious disease which leads to madness and eventual death. It can be contracted from rabid [[Lore:Wolf|wolves]].

====Rattles====
Rattles is a mild common disease affecting a victim's willpower and dexterity. Symptoms include muscle spasms, listlessness and poor stamina. It may be contracted from nix-hounds, zombies and chaurus.

====Red Death====
Red Death is a very serious disease which can quickly decimate a victim's endurance and even destroy personality until a cure is given or the victim dies.

====Red Fever====
An infectious disease. Vexis Velruan once experimented on victims of the Red Fever during his time as a temple healer, attempting to use magic to turn the disease on itself. He was expelled from the temple for trying and failing to find a cure.

====Red Plague====
A common disease.

====Red Rage====
Contracted from rats, victims of Red Rage suffer from loss of willpower and strength.

====Rockjoint====
Rockjoint is an acute common disease affecting a victim's manual dexterity and ability to use melee weapons. Symptoms include painful swelling and immobility of all joints. It may be contracted from wolves, alit, zombies and domesticated guar.

====Rotbone====
Contracted from [[Lore:Durzog|Durzogs]], Rotbone causes fatigue.

====Rust Chancre====
Rust chancre is a mild common disease affecting a victim's mobility and behavior. Symptoms include swelling and rash, and painful muscle spasms. It may be contracted from waste rats and zombies.

====Sai's Affliction====
Sai's Affliction is a disease caused by excessive worship of [[Lore:Sai|Sai]], the God of Luck. Sufferers of the disease have been abandoned by Sai, and long for the god's presence. They are driven to incessant gambling, seeking proof of the god's favor. This usually leaves the victim in poverty or debt.

====Sanguinare Vampiris====
Similar in nature to [[#Porphyric Hemophilia|Porphyric Hemophilia]] and [[#Noxiphilic Sanguivoria|Noxiphilic Sanguivoria]], victims of the less-common Sanguinare Vampiris will become [[Lore:Vampirism|vampires]] after a three day incubation period. By itself, the disease negatively affects the victim's health: the victim will experience weakness during the day and acquire a peculiar thirst. On the third night of infection, the victim will turn into a vampire.{{ref|name=SkyrimVamp|[[Skyrim:Vampirism|Vampirism]] disease mechanics in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The disease can easily be cured within the three-day period, but vampirism is notoriously hard to cure. Sanguinare Vampiris is found in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].

====Sanies Lupinus====
Sanies Lupinus, also called Canis Hysteria,{{ref|name=SSAYQ|{{Cite Book|Sage Svari Answers Your Questions}}}} is contracted from [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropes]], and causes drowsiness. However, after a three day incubation period the victim will become a lycanthrope (the type of which depends on the creature that caused the infection). The disease can easily be cured within the three days, but lycanthropy can only be cured by ritual.

====Scalon Blight====
Contracted from [[Lore:Scalon|Scalon]], creatures native to the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]. Symptoms include loss of endurance and a weakness to frost and fire.

====Scalon Fever====
Contracted from Scalon, creatures native to the Shivering Isles. Symptoms include loss of endurance and a weakness to frost.

====Scalon Sunburn====
Contracted from Scalon, creatures native to the Shivering Isles. Symptoms include loss of endurance and a weakness to magical attacks.

====Serpiginous Dementia====
Serpiginous dementia is a serious common disease affecting the victim's mind and behavior. Symptoms include hallucinations and an itchy and unsightly scaly skin condition resembling snake scales. It may be contracted from netch and zombies.

====Shakes====
Contracted from rats, victims of Shakes suffer from loss of agility.

====Sloughskin====
A disease contracted from [[Lore:Fleshfly|fleshflies]]. Commonly afflicts mounts unsuited to travel through the swamps of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]. [[Lore:Lizard-Steed|Lizard-Steed]]s are notably immune.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Scaly Steeds of Black Marsh}}}}

====Stomach Rot====
Stomach Rot is a permanent condition, unless cured, characterized by a slight daily drop in health.

====Stone Blood====
Stone Blood is a disease found in the dungeons of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]].

====Swamp Fever====
Swamp fever is a mild common disease affecting the victim's strength and behavior. It may be contracted from mudcrabs, baliwogs and zombies. The symptoms include high body temperature and delirium, but there are no easily visible signs. The [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] is infamous for the abundance of Swamp Fever cases.

====Swamp Rot====
Victims of Swamp Rot will have their willpower, agility, and strength fall daily, until they waste away or are cured.

This disease is not to be confused with the [[Lore:Voriplasm|Voriplasms]] found in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]], also known as Swamp Rot.{{ref|[[ON:Swamp Rot|Swamp Rot]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

====Thrassian Plague====
{{Lore:Thrassian Plague}}

====Ticklebritch====
Symptoms of Ticklebritch include a loss of endurance and attractiveness. Contracted from handling dirty equipment.

====Troll Thirst====
Victims of Troll Thirst are blinded by rage. They must continuously kill to stay alive. The ailment is mercifully short-lived, and the victim will return to normal if they manage to survive. The disease can be contracted in the wilds around [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]].

====Tunnel Cough====
Tunnel Cough is prevalent among miners. An inconvenience to those who want to remain undetected, as it negatively affects their ability to sneak.

====Typhoid Fever====
Typhoid Fever is a deadly disease affecting a victim's intelligence, endurance, and health. The condition is permanent, barring a cure or death.

====Winter Worn====
The disease can be contracted in the wilds around [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]].

====Witbane====
Witbane is an acute common disease affecting a victim's memory, thought processes and ability to regenerate magicka. Symptoms include loss of memory and disorientation. It may be contracted from dogs, sabre cats and rats.

====Witches' Pox====
Witches' Pox will slowly deteriorate the victim's strength, endurance, and health until they are cured or dead.

====Witchwither====
Witchwither is a common disease which paralyzes its victims. It can only be contracted from the curse of a witch.

====Wither====
Wither is a mild common disease that affects the victim's strength and endurance. Symptoms include loss of energy and shortness of breath. It may be contracted from dreugh, mountain lions and zombies. The disease is prevalent among [[Lore:Sixth House|Sixth House]] cultists.

====Witless Pox====
Contracted from rats, victims of Witless Pox suffer from loss of intelligence.

====Wizard Fever====
Wizard Fever is a sometimes merely irritating, sometimes devastating magical disease. Some have suffered this and barely noticed it before the fever broke on its own, and some have been left feebleminded. The victim's innate magicka and intelligence will trickle away, day by day, for as long as three weeks.

====Wound Rot====
Wound Rot very slowly erodes the victim's strength, endurance, and health until cured.

====Yellow Fever====
The endurance, willpower, and health of a victim of Yellow Fever will decline each day, until they find a cure or die.

====Yellow Tick====
Yellow tick is a mild common disease affecting a victim's strength and mobility. Symptoms include dark, bruise-like swelling, sensitive to touch. It may be contracted from wolves, bears, kagouti and zombies.
