[[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], the '''City of Kings''', also known as "Ysgramor's city",{{Ref|name=SotR|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return}}}}{{ref|name=SRSerana|[[SR:Serana|Serana]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]:'' ''[[SR:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}  is a city in northeastern [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], close to the border with [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], and is the only substantial city in the [[Lore:Hold|Hold]] of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]].<noinclude>

It is not only the oldest extant city in Skyrim{{ref|name=SkyrimLoading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Windhelm|Loading screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} (the oldest city being [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]]),{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} but it does hold the title of being the oldest continuously inhabited human settlement in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=ESOLoading|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Windhelm was once the capital of the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]] and the old palace of the [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] dynasty, the [[Lore:Palace of the Kings|Palace of the Kings]], still stands in the center of the city.{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}

[[Lore:Fort Morvunskar|Morvunskar]] is located on the strategic high ground southwest of Windhelm, overlooking the main roads leading to the city. Like most Nord cities, Windhelm has a [[Lore:Hall of the Dead|Hall of the Dead]] where bodies are interred, overseen by a Priest of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] who ensures that corpses are properly consecrated and cared for.{{ref|name=ESOLoading}}{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The city's arena is known as the Hall of Trials.{{ref|name=ESOLoading}}

==History==
===Merethic Era===
Windhelm was founded by the legendary [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] and the crew of his ship, the Ylgermet, following the [[Lore:Battle of the Moesring|Battle of the Moesring]] in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]. According to Nordic tradition, after the [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] disbanded, the Ylgermet's crew stumbled upon the barrow of [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]], Ysgramor's son. When Ysgramor looked south and saw where the [[Lore:White River|White River]] flowed into the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]], he decreed that a great city should be built there to serve as a monument to the glories of mankind, a place from where he could gaze upon the hill of [[Lore:Yngol Barrow|Yngol's barrow]], and a gateway to guard Skyrim's interior against incursion via the river. It was built by [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elf]] slaves of the early Nords.{{Ref|name=SotR}}

Captive elves were forced to build the city in the architectural style of their captors, and it is alleged that as many elves died during Windhelm's construction as were killed by the Ylgermet's crew before they had arrived at the site. The city's great bridge was designed to allow a vigilant watch on the White River for elven attacks, and Ysgramor's palace, the Palace of the Kings, towered over the city to show man's dominion over the very winds that had caused Yngol's death on the voyage from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]. An elaborate tomb was built in the catacombs beneath Windhelm to serve as Ysgramor's final resting place upon his death, but was never used because he chose to be buried [[Skyrim:Ysgramor's Tomb|by the sea]].{{Ref|name=SotR}}
===First Era===
Ysgramor's descendants would rule the burgeoning territory of the Nords from Windhelm. When [[Lore:Harald|Harald]] assumed the mantle of High King in {{Year|1E 143}}, he relinquished his holdings in Atmora and established Skyrim as an independent kingdom with Windhelm as its capital.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S}} During the time of [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage the Gifted]], the city became the capital of not just Skyrim, but the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]] that also encompassed [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], and parts of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{Ref|name=PGE1S}} When the First Empire of the Nords later collapsed amidst the [[Lore:War of Succession|War of Succession]] that raged from {{Year|1E 369}} to {{Year|1E 420}}, the city was sacked.{{Ref|name=PGE1S}}
===Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Windhelm.jpg|thumb|left|Windhelm circa 2E 582]]
In {{Year|2E 431}}, after the onset of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], High King [[Lore:Logrolf|Logrolf]] was assassinated, precipitating a succession dispute between his daughter, [[Lore:Freydis|Freydis]], and Jarl [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]] of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]. While Freydis was accepted as High Queen by the Crown of Verity in Windhelm, a partial [[Lore:Moot|Moot]] convened in Solitude and declared Svartr to be High King. From then on, Skyrim was divided into two kingdoms, with Windhelm serving as the capital of [[Lore:Eastern Skyrim|Eastern Skyrim]] and the seat of Freydis and her line.{{ref|name=TCOF|{{Cite Book|The Crown of Freydis}}}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

Windhelm was sacked a second time in {{Year|2E 572}} by the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] army of [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]], and then-High Queen [[Lore:Mabjaarn Flame-Hair|Mabjaarn Flame-Hair]] was slain in battle along with her daughter, [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]], who briefly succeeded her.{{Ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} The Palace of the Kings was one of the few of the city's original structures that survived these events, however it was badly damaged. The palace court was temporarily displaced as High King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]], who succeeded Nurnhilde, elected to repair the rest of city first, and work on the palace was not completed until {{Year|2E 582}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=Jorunn|[[Online:Jorunn the Skald-King|High King Joruun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

At some point circa 2E 582, Windhelm was raided by a group of [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]], including the terrifying [[Lore:Pale Fingers|Pale Fingers]],{{ref|name=SRGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Eastern & Western Skyrim Gazetteer''}}{{rp|34}} an agent of the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] mage [[Lore:Deslandra|Deslandra]].{{ref|name=BOTSEAB|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Limited Edition Art and Lore Book''}}{{rp|60}} The raid was one of many in Eastern Skyrim, as Pale Fingers was wreaking havoc on the surface to draw attention away from Deslandra's plans underground in [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]].{{ref|name=BOTSEAB}}{{rp|60}} The [[Lore:Outer Watch|Outer Watch]] learned of this and provided relief to the city.{{ref|name=SRGazetteer}}{{rp|34}} 

At some point during the Second Era, the city also suffered under the disastrous reign of Jarl [[Lore:Elgryr the Unminded|Elgryr the Unminded]], who taxed his citizens to the point of destitution and eventually drove them to revolt.{{Ref|name=TWL|{{Cite Book|The Windhelm Letters}}}} In the ninth century of the Second Era, during the formation of the [[Lore:Empire|Third Empire of Tamriel]], Windhelm served as a base for Imperial troops guarding the [[Lore:Dunmeth Pass|Dunmeth Pass]] into Morrowind prior to that region's incorporation into the Empire.{{Ref|name=PGE1S}}

===Third Era===
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Windhelm was an active settlement, being ruled by King Asgan. It had a rivalry with [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] and [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]].{{Ref|name=Arena|[[Arena:Windhelm|Windhelm]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} It was neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Amol|Amol]] to the west, [[Lore:Dragon Wood|Dragon Wood]] to the south, {{Lore Link|Laintar Dale}} to the southwest, and Morrowind to the east, towards [[Lore:Cormar View|Cormar View]].{{Ref|name=MapArena|[[:File:AR-map-Skyrim towns.png|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}{{Ref|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} When the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] broke out in {{Year|3E 433}}, Skyrim was under siege and faced severe casualties. It was said that everything between [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]] and Windhelm was attacked by the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric hordes]].{{Ref|name=OBGate|[[Oblivion:Generic Dialogue#Tamriel Gate Responses|Tamriel Gate Response]] rumors in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
By {{Year|4E 201}}, Windhelm was the center of the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloak]] [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Rebellion]] against the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]]. Jarl [[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]], leader of the rebellion, sought to liberate Skyrim from Imperial control to protect the traditional Nord way of life and restore the right to worship [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], which had been banned Empire-wide by the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]]. In open defiance of the ban, Windhelm's Temple of Talos was kept open throughout the conflict with the Empire. The city had also come to house a substantial population of [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] refugees who had fled Morrowind in the aftermath of the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]]. The elves were relegated to the city's run-down Snow Quarter, later named the Gray Quarter after the Dunmer refugees, and faced constant suspicion from the xenophobic local Nords.{{ref|name=Skyrim}}
