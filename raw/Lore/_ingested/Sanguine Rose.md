# Lore:Sanguine Rose

The [[Lore:Sanguine Rose|Sanguine Rose]] (sometimes called '''Sanguine's Rose''') is a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] created by the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]]. It can take on many forms, including that of an actual [[Lore:rose|rose]], a wooden stave carved like a rose or a staff-sized rose. Although powerful, the Rose is not an artifact most would care to possess. It can be used to summon a lesser [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], who will attack all except the holder. The Daedra is uncontrollable, unlike those summoned by [[Lore:Conjuration|Conjuration]], but as with conjured creatures it is only bound to the mortal plane temporarily. Each time the Rose is used it wilts a little, and when all the petals fall off, it loses its power. When this happens, a new rose blooms somewhere in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] and is plucked by Sanguine to be given to another champion.{{Ref|Daggerfall description}}<noinclude>

Petals that fall from Sanguine's roses are not known to wilt, and are always slightly damp with blood. They can be used in certain summoning rituals.{{ref|[[Online:Roseblood Bat|Roseblood Bat]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

==History==
Just before the events of the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] was sent by Sanguine to kill a monk who had slain his worshippers. Sanguine promised the Rose as a reward, despite the fact that it had been set aside for his favored disciples. The agent accomplished the task, and was given the Rose by a worshipper of the Prince.{{ref|name=DFSanguine|[[Daggerfall:Sanguine's Quest|Sanguine's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

Sometime after these events, a group of apprentices from the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] who had turned to Daedric magic came into the possession of the Rose. The young [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] was a part of this group and possessed the Rose for a time. The group's dabblings in Daedric magic soon turned dangerous, and many of them died, although the circumstances are not understood. This caused Martin to turn away from Sanguine and become a priest, losing the Rose in the process.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Sanguine offered the Rose to the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] in return for disrupting a formal dinner hosted by {{Lore Link|Alessia Caro}} of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]]. The Hero infiltrated the party and cast a spell supplied by the Prince, which stripped all attending of their clothing, including the Hero. Sanguine was pleased and rewarded the Hero of Kvatch with the Rose.{{ref|name=OBSaguine|Events of [[OB:Sanguine|Sanguine]]'s quest in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} An article on the prank was published by the Black Horse Courier in a special edition paper titled ''Pranks Spoils Society Gathering!''.{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=OB|Pranks Spoils Society Gathering!}}}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Sanguine manifested himself as a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] man named Sam Guevenne. For his own amusement, he challenged the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] to a drinking competition in a tavern in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], after which the two drunkenly explored the province on a quest to repair the Rose. The Dragonborn couldn't remember the events the next day, and traced their steps across Skyrim, collecting the items necessary to repair this promised staff. The Dragonborn eventually caught up with Sam, who revealed his true identity and told the Dragonborn that the items weren't needed and it was more for Sanguine's fun than anything else. The Prince then gave the Dragonborn his Rose as a prize for amusing him.{{ref|name=SRSanguine|Events of [[Skyrim:A Night To Remember|A Night To Remember]] in  [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
