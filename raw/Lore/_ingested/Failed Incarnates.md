# Lore:Failed Incarnates

<noinclude>{{Trail|People}}__NOTOC__[[File:ON-interior-Cavern of the Incarnate.jpg|thumb|right|The Cavern of the Incarnate]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Failed Incarnates|Failed Incarnates]] (also called '''"False Incarnates"''' by the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]]) were individuals who sought to fulfill the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|Nerevarine Prophecy]], but failed. The [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]] once believed them possessed by the spirit of [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]], but each had a critical flaw that made them destined to fail to fulfill the prophecy. Their spirits reside within the {{Lore Link|Cavern of the Incarnate}} on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]].{{ref|name=ESODovrosi|[[ON:Wise Woman Dovrosi|Wise Woman Dovrosi]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Divine Delusions|Divine Delusions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude> Some of the failed Nerevarines were buried with death masks resembling the visage of Indoril Nerevar.{{ref|name=TI|[[ON:Treasures I#Indoril Death Mask|Indoril Death Mask]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

==Aduri==
[[File:ON-npc-Incarnate Aduri.jpg|thumb|left|Aduri]][[File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry of a Failed Incarnate, The Warseeker.jpg|thumb|right|''"To seek only war is to find only death."'']]
To fulfill the Nerevarine prophecy, Aduri followed a path of blood and war. Her love of senseless war proved to be her undoing; the path she followed led nowhere, and she was cast down as the hope of her people.{{ref|name=ESOAduri|[[ON:Incarnate Aduri|Incarnate Aduri]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Divine Delusions|Divine Delusions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} She helped the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] deny Conoon Chodala's claim to the title of Nerevarine in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=ESODD|Events of [[ON:Divine Delusions|Divine Delusions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
{{NewLine}}

==Ane Teria==
[[File:MW-npc-Ane Teria.jpg|thumb|left|Ane Teria]]
Ane Teria was a holy crusader of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Temple]] in the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]]'s golden era. She contributed substantially to the writings that were suppressed by the temple and considered apographa. She followed the Tribunal unquestioningly, and didn't believe in the Nerevarine prophecies until it was too late. She offered her mace to the Nerevarine in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=MWAne|[[MW:Ane Teria|Ane Teria]]'s dialogue during [[MW:The Path of the Incarnate|The Path of the Incarnate]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

==Danaat==
[[File:ON-npc-Incarnate Danaat.jpg|thumb|left|Danaat]][[File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry of a Failed Incarnate, The Fool.jpg|thumb|right|''"The fool hears only his own poor advice."'']]

==Elvil Vidron==
[[File:MW-npc-Elvil_Vidron.jpg|thumb|left|Elvil Vidron]]
Elvil Vidron received waking dreams from Dagoth Ur. However, unlike other Dreamers who fell to Dagoth Ur's control, he instead believed these dreams to be prophetic and that he was the Nerevarine. He began walking around Suran, proclaiming himself Nerevarine, attracting the attention of the Tribunal Temple.
{{NewLine}}

==Erur-Dan==
[[File:MW-npc-Erur-Dan.jpg|thumb|left|Erur-Dan]]
Erur-Dan the Wise was a wizard and counselor of Ashkhan Assur-Abanba of the Erabenimsun Tribe.{{Ref|name=Manirai|[[MW:Manirai|Manirai]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Erur-Dan saw [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] fall to the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]]. He lived through Morrowind's surrender, and swore vengeance upon the Imperials and the Tribunal for their betrayal. In his later years, he left for [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], where he grew old and died fighting monsters and the [[Lore:The Blight|blight]]. He offered the Nerevarine his spear and cuirass in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=MWErur-Dan|[[MW:Erur-Dan|Erur-Dan]]'s dialogue during [[MW:The Path of the Incarnate|The Path of the Incarnate]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
{{NewLine}}

==Hort Ledd==
[[File:MW-npc-Hort Ledd.jpg|thumb|left|Hort Ledd]]
Hort Ledd died four hundred years prior to the fulfilment of the Nerevarine Prophecy, in the final days of turmoil after the Empire came to Morrowind. He was a thinker, not a doer, and though he was chosen, he was not a hero. He offered the Nerevarine his robe in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=MWHort|[[MW:Hort Ledd|Hort Ledd]]'s dialogue during [[MW:The Path of the Incarnate|The Path of the Incarnate]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
{{NewLine}}

==Idrenie Nerothan==
[[File:MW-npc-Idrenie Nerothan.jpg|thumb|left|Idrenie Nerothan]]
Idrenie Nerothan lived in the late years of the Tribunate, and helped demoralize and repel Akaviri invaders behind the scenes. She knew nothing of the Nerevarine prophecies before she took refuge with the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]]. She died attempting to loot the ruins of [[Lore:Kogoruhn|Kogoruhn]]. She offered the Nerevarine tools with which to pass locks in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=MWIdrenie|[[MW:Idrenie Nerothan|Idrenie Nerothan]]'s dialogue during [[MW:The Path of the Incarnate|The Path of the Incarnate]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
