# Lore:Hircine

<noinclude>{{Lore Gods Trail}}{{Featured Article}}{{#local:showrefs|1}}[[File:BL-misc-Spirit of the Hunt Promo.png|Hircine, the Lord of the Hunt|thumb|left]]{{TOCright|limit=2}}</noinclude>
[[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], known as '''the Huntsman''', '''Lord of the Hunt''',{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}<noinclude>{{rp|293}}</noinclude> '''Master of Beasts'''{{ref|name=ONLoading|[[Online:The Hunting Grounds (Hircine's Gift)|The Hunting Grounds]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}, '''Wolves' Father'''{{ref|name=MDP1|[[Lore:Oblivion|Mythic Dawn Pamphlet 1]]}} and  the '''Master of the Chase'''{{ref|name=IOH|{{Cite Book|Invocation of Hircine}}}}, is a [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] whose sphere is the Hunt, the Sport of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]],{{ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Book of Daedra}}}} the Greatest Game, and the Chase and Sacrifice of Mortals.{{ref|name=TRBOD|{{Cite Book|The Requisite Book of Daedra}}}} His realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] is the [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]],{{ref|name=MTCH|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Hanu}}}} an endless forest where the Prince and his Huntsmen hunt great beasts, people, and even other Daedra.{{ref|name=ONLoading}} Hircine is typically portrayed with a great spear and either the head or skull of an [[Lore:Elk|elk]],{{ref|name=DFAppearance|Hircine's appearances across ''[[General:The Elder Scrolls|The Elder Scrolls]]'' franchise}} the latter of which Khajiiti myths say belonged to [[Lore:Y'ffer|Y'ffer]]'s champion, the [[Lore:Graht-Elk|Graht-Elk]].{{ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Worldly Spirits}}}}

Hircine is one of the more widely worshipped Daedric Princes in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], despite never actively seeking worship or reverence.{{ref|name=THP|{{Cite Book|The Huntsman Prince}}}} His appeal lies in his sphere, which encompasses the thrill of the hunt,{{ref|name=TGH|{{Cite Book|The Glory of the Hunt|ns_base=Online}}}} though this can be taken to extremes.{{ref|name=ONTDP|[[Online:The Deadliest Prey|The Deadliest Prey]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONSOU|[[Online:Shade of Ulthorn|Shade of Ulthorn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GAYQ|{{Cite Book|Gwaering Answers Your Questions}}}} His advocacy for sportsmanship is another lure, as the Law of Fair Hunt which he holds to prohibits cheating prey (referred to as "the Hare") out of a genuine chance at escape.{{ref|name=IOH}}{{ref|name=VOFTWE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Wood Elves}}}} He admires the Hare that overcomes whatever is thrown against them, no matter the odds. For example, one who turns the game on its head by hunting the hunters may earn the Prince's respect.{{Ref|name=SRMoonlight|Events of [[Skyrim:Ill Met By Moonlight|Ill Met By Moonlight]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Finally, he is straightforward in his desires; he often seeks people out, tests them, and rewards those who prove themselves worthy with a boon.{{ref|name=THP}}{{ref|name=ONHanu}}

As the progenitor of [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropy]], Hircine is recognized as '''the Father of Manbeasts'''.{{ref|name=ESOT}}{{ref|name=THP}} He serves as the guardian of were-creatures, which are often referred to as his "children".{{ref|name=AAOW|{{Cite Book|An Accounting of Werewolves}}}}{{ref|name=AOLH|{{Cite Book|Aspects of Lord Hircine}}}}{{ref|name=LOTI|{{Cite Book|Legend of Thane Icehammer}}}} The condition is called Hircine's Gift by those that view it as a blessing,{{ref|name=ESOT}} and Hircine's Curse to those that consider it an abomination.{{ref|name=ONWNLF|{{Cite Book|Wanted: Nimriian the Longfang|ns_base=Online}}}} Hircine is quite prideful of lycanthropy, and resents those that received it willingly but have come to regret it; he's known to have ingrates slain to rid them of their misery.{{ref|name=DFHircine|Events of [[Daggerfall:Hircine's Quest|Hircine's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} Regardless of whether they worshipped him or not, Hircine will typically claim the souls of skinshifters, and they will spend eternity in the Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=ONHanu|[[Online:Hanu|Hanu]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Wolfhunter|Wolfhunter]]''}}{{ref|name=THP}}

The [[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] [[Lore:Dearola|Dearola]] is Hircine's daughter.{{ref|name=TTWT|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, III}}}} There is a belief that Hircine is allied with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]],<noinclude>{{intnote|Noonan|[nb 1]}}</noinclude> though [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]] claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}} Hircine has [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lords]] that manage some of his sub-spheres and report to him, such as the Lord of Fishing.{{ref|name=ESOLive1|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live Funny Lore Bits with Lawrence Schick and Zach Bush#Is Hircine the Daedric Prince of Fishing?|Is Hircine the Daedric Prince of Fishing?]] on [[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}} He is also associated with the Daedra Lord [[Lore:Hollowjack|Hollowjack]],{{ref|[[Legends:Card Backs#Hollowjack|Hollowjack]] card back description in ''[[LG:Legends|Legends]]''}} whose realm of [[Lore:Detritus|Detritus]] is a "carving" from the shell of the [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]].{{ref|name=LATH|{{Cite book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays}}}} [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]] is an enemy of Hircine.{{ref|name=DFFactions|[[Daggerfall:Oblivion|Factions]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} Hircine's [[Lore:Hircine's Summoning Day|summoning day]] is the 5<sup>th</sup> of Mid Year.{{ref|name=DFHolidays|[[Daggerfall:Holidays|Holidays]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall]]''}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}

==Aspects==
[[File:ON-concept-Prince-Hircine-emblem.png|thumb|left|An emblem representing Hircine]][[File:ON-crown store-Great Dark Ram 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Dark Ram (ESO)]]
The [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] believe that there are five aspects of Hircine, each worthy of reverence.{{ref|name=AOLH}}{{ref|name=ONHSG|[[Online:High Shaman Glynroch|High Shaman Glynroch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some believe there are more than five, however, as the Glenmoril Wyrd worships an additional [[Lore:Owl|owl]] aspect.{{ref|name=ESOLive2|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/281631351 Developer comments on [[Online:Wyress Strigidae|Wyress Strigidae]] (32:30)] on [[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}} An aspect in the form of a [[Lore:Sheep|ram]] dubbed the Great Dark Ram also appears during [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s [[Lore:Great Forest|Great Forest]].{{ref|name=ONGreatDarkRam|[[Online:Great Dark Ram|Great Dark Ram]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Hircine's worshippers are known to craft their [[Lore:Weapons|weapons]] and armor in a style that honors these figures.{{ref|name=CMSC|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 31: Skinchanger Style}}}}{{ref|name=CMH|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 65: Huntsman Style}}}}

'''{{Anchor|Alrabeg}}''' (''the Hunter / Aspect of Guile'')<br>
Alrabeg is the hunter, and he bears the [[Lore:Spear of Bitter Mercy|Spear of Bitter Mercy]] when he manifests.{{ref|name=AOLH}} On occasion, he wields the [[Lore:Spear of the Hunter|Spear of the Hunter]].{{ref|name=BMGuile|[[Bloodmoon:Hircine's Aspect of Guile|Hircine's Aspect of Guile]]'s appearance, abilities and drops in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} He comes to [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] to hunt new prey, or to bring prey native to the Hunting Grounds to the mortal plane. If he arrives without prey, then he has come to select a Hare in his next hunt.{{ref|name=AOLH}} Worshippers invoke Alrabeg's name when praying for a bountiful hunt.{{ref|name=IOH}}

'''{{Anchor|Storihbeg}}''' (''the Manbeast / Skinchanger / Skinshifter / King of Wolves / Wolf Aspect of Hircine / Wolf That Craved the Sky / Wolf with the Daedric Howl / Aspect of Speed'')<br>
Storihbeg is the [[Lore:Wolf|wolf]], and wears the Wolf Skull Totem. Storihbeg introduced the gift of skinshifting to mortals to remind them that they can be predators, not prey.{{ref|name=ONAO|[[Online:Apprentice Orla|Apprentice Orla]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} When he comes to Nirn, he hunts with these skinshifters, or seeks to "adopt new children and turn them pelt-side-out"; that is to say, he turns mortals into werewolves. His howl is capable of freezing his prey's innards,{{ref|name=AOLH}} which when combined with his speed, makes it difficult for prey to flee from him.{{ref|name=BMSpeed|[[Bloodmoon:Hircine's Aspect of Speed|Hircine's Aspect of Speed]]'s appearance and abilities in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} An account from the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]] tells of Storihbeg appearing as a wraithlike wolf composed of extreme storm and fog, and his arrival was proceeded by inclement weather. The account tells of him stealing the unwary during the night before vanishing. His terrifying Daedric howl was unforgettable; described as mournful, brazen, and unrelenting.{{ref|name=ESOSDB1|{{Cite Book|Scary Tales of the Druadach, Book 1}}}}
[[File:ON-crown store-Great Dark Stag 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Dark Stag (ESO)]]
'''{{Anchor|Uricanbeg}}''' (''the Great Dark Stag / Great Stag'')<br>
Uricanbeg is the [[Lore:Deer|stag]]. He comes to mate with hinds, and may transform a mortal woman into one for the same purpose. He may also arrive to cull the herd of the weak. His hooves drum the Blood Summons that lure prey into his herd, which are then led into the Hunting Grounds where they meet their end.{{ref|name=AOLH}} Some of his offspring have granted mortals the honor to ride them.{{ref|name=ONGreatDarkStag|[[Online:Great Dark Stag|Great Dark Stag]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

'''{{Anchor|Gulibeg}}''' (''the Quick Fox'')<br>
Gulibeg is the [[Lore:Fox|fox]]. He shares characteristics with some cultural interpretations of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]. Like Lorkhan, Gulibeg is a trickster; at his worst, Gulibeg will deceive mortals into meeting their own demise, or will use his swift bite to weaken his prey.{{ref|name=ONGulibeg|[[Online:The Symbol of Gulibeg|The Symbol of Gulibeg]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He shows some parallels with [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] interpretation of Lorkhan,{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} whose totem is the fox.{{ref|name=ESOEFT|[[Online:Ebony Fox Totem|Ebony Fox Totem]] furnishing from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOFAHM|{{Cite Book|Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus}}}} Gulibeg's symbol is the Wand of Bone, which was fashioned from a shard from [[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]]'s ribs, and has the ability to confound any mortal.{{ref|name=ONHSG}} In one tale, a medallion associated with Gulibeg makes less conventional forms of communication understandable.{{ref|name=TSW|{{Cite Book|The Silver Werewolf}}}} Gulibeg is said to reward those he finds clever by teaching them tricks and secrets,{{ref|name=AOLH}} which the Reachmen claim helped them survive the fall of Lorkh.{{ref|name=ONHSG}} 

'''{{Anchor|Hrokkibeg}}''' (''the Mighty Bear / Aspect of Strength'')<br>
Hrokkibeg is the [[Lore:Bear|bear]]. He embodies strength, and has ties to the [[Lore:Totem of Claw and Fang|Totem of Claw and Fang]]. He comes to Nirn seeking solitude, peace from labors, and renewal of the Burning Spirit Within. He may bless those that make offerings of mead with the power of the Bear-Heart. However, he will attack those who disturb his peace. Though he is slow, he makes up for it with his strength,{{ref|name=BMStrength|[[Bloodmoon:Hircine's Aspect of Strength|Hircine's Aspect of Strength]] appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} and tears his prey apart if he catches up to them.{{ref|name=AOLH}}

==Worship and Culture==
[[File:ON-wallpaper-Wolfhunter 02-2880x1620.jpg|thumb|350px|Hircine overseeing a Great Hunt within the Hunting Grounds]]

===Afterlife===
Hircine's devoted are promised an afterlife within the [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]], a savage land that provides to its dwellers. Here, one can indulge in spectacular hunts. Those within the realm experience an endless cycle of violence, as well as death and rebirth. Its inhabitants reside in the many hunting lodges dotting the landscape. The forests of the Hunting Grounds are teeming with powerful beasts, which allow Hircine's followers to engage in the most spectacular hunts. To the faithful, the plane is a paradise.{{ref|name=THP}}

Hircine is the '''Master of the [[Lore:Great Hunt|Great Hunt]]''',{{ref|name=ONHGBT|[[Online:Hunting Grounds Body Tattoo|Hunting Grounds Body Tattoo]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} a grandiose contest that typically pits participants of the Prince's choosing against each other for his amusement. This event is a common occurrence in the Hunting Grounds, to which mortals are sometimes invited.{{ref|name=ONHanu}} Outside of his plane, they are rare, rumored to happen only once in an era.{{ref|name=BMKorst|[[Bloodmoon:Korst Wind-Eye|Korst Wind-Eye]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Hircine may call upon a Great Hunt on Nirn for his own merriment,{{ref|name=BMHunt}} or to punish those that defy him.{{ref|name=THP}}{{ref|name=SRLoading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Daedric Princes|Hircine Loading Screen]] description in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=SRMoonlight}} 

When the Great Hunt is hosted on Nirn, [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]] turns red, becoming the Bloodmoon, and the hunt proceeds until the Bloodmoon subsides.{{ref|name=BMKorst}} The Bloodmoon is said to symbolize the blood of the Hunter's Prey, and those wishing to join the Great Hunt craft rounded bone pendants that are stained with blood to represent the event.{{ref|name=BMKorst}}{{ref|name=ONPendant|[[Online:Contraband B#Bloodmoon Pendant|Bloodmoon Pendant]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Should one emerge victorious from the Great Hunt, they are permitted to ask Hircine for a grand boon.{{ref|name=ONHanu}}

===Altmer===
[[File:ON-prerelease-Divine Prosecution 02.jpg|thumb|right|Hircine's Altmeri faithful may face the wrath of the Divine Prosecution]]
The [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] detest anything related to the Daedra. They believe Daedra are not to be bargained with, and Summerset laws supposedly decrees that summoning them warrants a death sentence.{{ref|name=ONMerormo|[[Online:Merormo|Merormo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} To wield Daedric magic is to insult one's lineage.{{ref|name=ONCirion|[[Online:Keeper Cirion|Keeper Cirion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Altmer are very zealous in their beliefs, that it warrants the existence of the [[Lore:Divine Prosecution|Divine Prosecution]], which is tasked with enforcing religious laws, and that includes stomping out any Daedra worship.{{ref|name=DtOyEiS|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/26371 Discover the Organizations You'll Encounter in Summerset] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} There is some leeway however as evident with the author of the book ''[[Lore:Liminal Bridges|Liminal Bridges]]'', and the organization known as the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] utilizing daedric summons in combat.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Thalmor|Thalmor]] summons in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|Events of [[Legends:The Forgotten Hero|The Forgotten Hero]] story in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

In the context of Hircine, thanks to the isolationist policies of the Altmer, they are vulnerable to the spread of lycanthropy. This became evident when [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] opened up the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]] to immigrants of other races.{{ref|name=ESOAEI|{{Cite Book|An End to Isolation}}}} Lycanthropes are a rarity on the Isle, and thus the Altmer were not familiar with how to best handle them. When the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmeri]] Thornfang Pack migrated to Summerset, they found it comparable to a safe haven, and were able to spread their "gift" with relative ease.{{ref|name=TPAG|{{Cite Book|The Pack of Archon's Grove|ns_base=Online}}}} Summerset werewolves often collect and embellish the skulls of their most impressive kills. [[Lore:Indrik|Indrik]] skulls are especially prized trophies.{{ref|name=ContrabandI|[[ON:Contraband I#Indrik Skull Trophy|Indrik Skull Trophy]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Altmer seeking to worship the Lord of the Hunt often look elsewhere to avoid persecution. One such individual was Lady [[Lore:Ghisiliane|Ghisiliane]], an Altmer noble who held grand hunts away from Summerset, where mortals and exotic creatures were brought in as quarry. She ran a kidnapping ring to populate her hunts with fellow mortals. She invited the local elites of society, including other Altmer, to participate in her games.{{ref|name=ONTDP}}{{ref|[[Online:Jun-Jassa|Jun-Jassa]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Deadliest Prey|The Deadliest Prey]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

By the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Daedra worship had become increasingly prevalent in the Summerset Isle, though specific details regarding the worship of Hircine were unclear.{{Ref|name=OblivionRadiant|[[OB:Radiant Conversations#Summerset Isles|Radiant Conversations about Summerset Isles]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Bosmer===
[[File:ON-misc-Statue of Hircine.jpg|thumb|left|Shrine to Hircine in Valenwood]]
Hircine is a Daedric power of some significance among the Bosmer,{{ref|name=VOFTWE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Wood Elves}}}} though their perception of the '''Hunter''' varies. While the Hunter's name may be invoked for luck in a hunt, going further can lead to cruel or violent extremes.{{ref|name=GAYQ}}

In the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], before [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]]'s Naming stabilized the forms of most creatures,{{ref|name=ESOWHC|[[Online:Wild Hunt Crate|Wild Hunt Crown Crate]] season description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} life was stuck in a chaotic, ever changing state called the [[Lore:Ooze|Ooze]]. From this Ooze, Y'ffre gave shape to the Bosmer, but forbade them from ever changing that shape through the terms of the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]]. The souls of those who violate the Green Pact would be condemned to return to the Ooze.{{ref|name=TOAF}} The only shapeshifting allowed by the Wood Elves' culture is during the collective ritual known as the [[Lore:Wild Hunt|Wild Hunt]], which is only invoked in an act of vengeance or desperation.{{ref|name=TOAF|{{Cite Book|The Ooze: A Fable}}}} The Bosmer believe that Hircine wishes to return to this chaotic state. As in other cultures, his cultists see shapeshifting as a gift, and view being culturally mandated to a single form as "tyranny".{{ref|name=ESOTBOH|group=UOL|[[Online:The Blessings of Hircine|The Blessings of Hircine]] removed book in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Bosmer can transform into a creature of the Wild Hunt outside the need for the collective ritual.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} Doing so this way is not condoned by Y'ffre, and is punishable for breaking the Green Pact. Hircine is known as a source for tapping into the transformation in this illicit manner.{{ref|name=ONBOF|Events of [[Online:Beasts of Falinesti|Beasts of Falinesti]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Hircine is said to have meddled with the nature of wolves, making them susceptible to shapeshifting. This modification is the origin for both werewolves, and the Wild Hunt Wolf, who as their name suggests, are wolves that have become unraveled because they have forgotten their Y'ffre-taught form,{{ref|name=ONWildHuntWolf|[[Online:Wild Hunt Wolf| Wild Hunt Wolf]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} much like creatures that get caught up in the Bosmer's Wild Hunt ritual.{{ref|name=ESOWHC}}

Outside influences are a factor to consider in the Hunter's popularity within [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{ref|name=ONEDNPC|[[Online:Ervalir| Ervalir]]'s dialogue with various other NPCs in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The formation of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]] led to the rise of Bosmer extremists turning to Hircine, as they saw the union as potential for their subjugation under High Elven rule.{{ref|name=ESOOBRD|{{Cite Book|One Bosmer's Response to the Dominion|ns_base=Online}}}} The Dominion attempted to suppress his worship, as Hircine's cultists started kidnapping unwary travelers.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Notice on Marbruk gates}}}} Some went as far as to ask for Hircine's blessing to purge the Dominion's presence from Valenwood.{{ref|name=ESOWCN|{{Cite Book|What Comes Next|ns_base=Online}}}}

===Bretons===
[[File:ON-hat-Nighthunter's Cowl.jpg|thumb|right|Nighthunter's Cowl]]
Within two [[Lore:Breton|Bretonic]] subcultures,{{ref|name=ONWD|{{Cite Book|Wyrd and Druid}}}} reverence towards Hircine can be found. The [[Lore:Druid|Druids]] worship Hircine as the '''Elk of Arrows'''. Speculation exists on his significance, with one theory that he represents the importance in nature of life and death between hunter and prey. Cowls with prayers to the Elk of Arrows were found in the [[Lore:Systres|Systres]].{{ref|name=ONNighthunterCowl|[[Online:Nighthunter's Cowl|Nighthunter's Cowl]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Within the [[Lore:Wyrd Covens|Wyrd Covens]], those that fall under the [[Lore:Glenmoril Wyrd|Glenmoril Wyrd]] are known for worshipping Hircine in a variety of different ways. Some, such as the witches of the Hagfeather Coven, the Rimerock Wyrd, and the Markarth Sisters, worship his more feral aspects, while the [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan]] and Viridian Wyrd worship his less feral side, and are known to provide services to cure lycanthropy.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Glenmoril Wyrd}}}} In the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region, the Glenmoril Coven of the Ilessan Hills of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] are the only group for whom Hircine will answer a summons.{{ref|name=DFSummoning|[[Daggerfall:Daedra Summoning|Daedra summoning]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

Religious idols comprised of tree roots shaped by wyrd magic have been unearthed by scholars. They depict female humanoid-animal hybrids, such as a woman with stag horns and cloven feet, a scaled woman, and a six breasted wolf woman. One researcher initially theorized they were representations of Hircine, but later concluded they may represent totemic Divines, like those of the [[Lore:Atmora|Atmorans]].{{Ref|name=ONClodAnti|[[Online:Bangkorai Antiquities#Wyrd Root Figure|Wyrd Root Figure]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

The Glenmoril Wyrd are known for their ability to shapeshift into different creatures; this beast-form magic is attributed to their knowledge of the mysteries of Hircine's [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|domain]].{{ref|name=ESOLSP|group=UOL|[[General:Lawrence Schick's Posts|Lawrence Schick's Posts]] discussing Glenmoril Wyrd shapeshifting}} Druids can also shapeshift, but their magic is attributed to Y'ffre.{{ref|name=LADCG|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Druid Circles of Galen}}}}

===Imperial===
[[File:OB-quest-Hircine.jpg|thumb|right|Shrine to Hircine in Cyrodiil]]
The Bosmeri name for Hircine - '''Half the Conscience of Man'''{{ref|name=ONSOU}} - is also used by the [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]], as the title was adopted and its connotations invocated as one of the 16 Acceptable Blasphemies for what was [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Invocation}}}}

[[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] views towards Hircine can be understood by their general negative views of Daedra, with a notable difference between the behavior of Colovians and the Nibenese during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. The more religious Colovians would typically refuse to associate themselves with them{{ref|name=ONKazirra|[[Online:Kazirra|Kazirra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}, whereas the Nibenese were more inclined to turn to Daedra worship.{{ref|name=FTLCH|{{Cite Book|From a discussion with Tatianus Lepidus, Colovian Historian}}}}

Daedra worship in Cyrodiil is technically allowed through the Imperial Charter, which permits the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] to summon Daedra. Due to the negative views of religious institutions and the general public, Daedra worshipping is not openly practiced. Shrines are known to be built in the wilderness of Cyrodiil{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} - one of such examples is a shrine to Hircine found in Great Forest.{{ref|name=OBHS|[[Oblivion:Hircine's Shrine|Hircine's Shrine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Khajiit===
[[File:ON-quest-The Hungry Cat's Favor.jpg|thumb|left|Khajiiti altar to Hircine]]
Hircine, the '''Hungry Cat''',{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} '''Beast Prince''',{{ref|name=ContrabandE|[[ON:Contraband/Elsweyr#Antler of the Beast Prince|Antler of the Beast Prince]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} '''Father of the Hunt''', and '''Spirit of Pursuit and Purposeful Change''',{{ref|name=TWS}} is one of the many prominent spirits worshipped by cultists in [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] culture.{{ref|name=VOFTK|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Khajiit}}}} It is said every Khajiiti hunter respects him.{{ref|name=MBHAYQ}} His sphere is hunting and skinchanging, and he is renowned for his cunning and ferocity.{{ref|name=VOFTK}} He was born of [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]] and [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]]'s second litter, from which many spirits known in other cultures as the Daedric Princes sprang.{{ref|name=WOCMA}}</div>

Ancient texts predating the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar Epiphany|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]] state that Hircine was once in love with [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]], but she instead chose [[Lore:Y'ffer|Y'ffer]] to be her mate after he created the first flower for her. A heartbroken Hircine slew  Y'ffer's champion, the Graht-Elk, and took to wearing its head as a trophy. Y'ffer and Nirni made many children together, but Y'ffer eventually became corrupted by [[Lore:Namiira|Namiira]]. He struck and killed Nirni in a frenzy. Hircine, along with [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] and [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]], slew Y'ffer and made a [[Lore:Earthbones|cairn out of his bones]] for their fallen sister.{{ref|name=TWS}}

The Hungry Cat is said to be fond of mortals, who are Nirni's children. As such, he frequently walks among them. Some Khajiit believe Hircine to be the father of Nirni's first litter, who were "as changeable as the Moons".{{ref|name=TWS}} This litter would later have their shape stabilized by [[Lore:Azura|Azurah]], who bound their forms to the [[Lore:Ja-Kha'jay|Ja-Kha'jay]].{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} Khajiit who have strayed from the Path pray to Hircine, believing he will guide them back upon it.{{ref|name=TWS}} However, lycanthropy is frowned upon, and seen as a "parody" of Khajiiti faith.{{ref|name=MBHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Moon Bishop Hunal Answers Your Questions}}}}

Predator Mesa in [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] hosts a great temple to Hircine. The Mesa is said to draw out the bloodlust in every hunter, causing the tribe that built the shrine to hunt each other to death. With the temple untended, it fell into disrepair, and nature reclaimed the area. Thus, savage beasts began to roam freely within.{{ref|name=ONSarazi|[[Online:Sarazi|Sarazi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The area still serves hunters seeking to test their strength and cunning.{{ref|name=ESOHTH|Events of the [[Online:Hunting the Hunters|Hunting the Hunters]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} On Predator Mesa, anything with a pulse, even people, are fair game.{{ref|name=ONSarazi}} Those who sate their lust for blood on the Mesa can offer their game to the temple's decrepit altars; some believe it may earn them the Hungry Cat's favor.{{ref|name=ONNisuzi|[[Online:Nisuzi|Nisuzi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Nedes===
[[File:ON-place-Bloodroot Forge 03.jpg|thumb|right|The Bloodroot Forge]]
The [[Lore:Keptu|Keptu]], a [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] tribe from the [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] region of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], were known to worship Hircine.{{ref|name=ESOHOTR|[[General:Horns of the Reach and Update 15 Preview|Horns of the Reach and Update 15 Preview]]}} The Huntsman Prince aided them in the creation of the [[Lore:Bloodroot Forge|Bloodroot Forge]], which was built for the purpose of harvesting [[Lore:Nirncrux|Nirncrux]]. The Forge was said to rival the ingenuity of the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] and the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{ref|name=NNF|{{Cite Book|Nicolard's Notes on the Forge}}}} The forge was later abandoned, and would be shunned by the neighboring Nords as a place of evil; one where the Nedes worshipped dark and forgotten gods.{{ref|name=ONBFL|[[Online:Bloodroot Forge|Bloodroot Forge]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The forge called to all who resided near its boundaries, causing those within its vicinity to experience a feral bloodlust.{{ref|name=ONF|[[Online:Filand|Filand]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONRH|[[Online:Rena Hammerhands|Rena Hammerhands]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Nedic architecture present in Craglorn features a variety of carved symbols. A symbol of a stag is prevalent, and is speculated by the famed archaeologist, Lady Clarisse Laurent, as possibly being attributed to Hircine.{{Ref|name=LadyClarisse|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}} The imagery is also present in the armor of the Keptu, [[Lore:Duraki|Duraki]], and [[Lore:Perena|Perena]] tribes.{{Ref|name=NedicArmorPack|Stag symbol present in the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Costumes#Nedic Warrior Pack|Nedic Warrior Pack]]}}

Bear companions of the Keptu are ornamented with gradient body markings to signify (or in hopes to gain) Hircine's favor. During the developmental stages of a cub, they receive gradient markings once each season as part of a marking ritual to form a bond with their human hunting companion, and afterwards their companion will then too mark themselves.{{ref|name=ONKeptuBearCub|[[Online:Keptu Bear Cub|Keptu Bear Cub]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} An adult bear may receive them if they and their human companion emerge victorious in the annual Ket Keptu Hunt.{{ref|name=ONKeptuBear|[[Online:Keptu Bear|Keptu Bear]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Nords===
To the Nords, Hircine is seen as a potential obstacle to reaching [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]], as contracting lycanthropy will result in the infected's soul being prevented from entering the realm.{{ref|name=AAOW}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGGL|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide#Legendary Edition|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|243}} A great wolf will forcibly drag the souls of the unwilling into the Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=KJ|{{Cite Book|Kodlak's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Jurgald thanes and their families ruled the area around the lower [[Lore:Yorgrim River|Yorgrim River]] for centuries, and openly embraced Hircine's curse.{{ref|name=ESOWHB|[[Online:Eastmarch Antiquities#Wolf-head Brooch|Wolf-head Brooch]] codex from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The area around the Yogrim River was where one of the earliest sightings of Hircine and his hounds occurred.{{ref|name=LOTI}}

One Nord tale claims that a forlorn ghostly [[Lore:Giant|giant]] haunts the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]]. In the story, he made a bargain with Hircine to be resurrected in order to find his wife, to no avail. He hunts down any unfortunate enough to cross his path, beating them to a pulp with his frosty club, and sates his hunger on the flesh of his victims.{{Ref|name=ESOEGTS|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}}

The [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] are an offshoot of Nords who view Hircine negatively. To them, he is a demon god whose coming is predicted by the [[Lore:Bloodmoon Prophecy|Bloodmoon Prophecy]], which foretells when he will visit Nirn with his hounds to collect mortal quarry for the [[Lore:Hunter's Game|Hunter's Game]]. The selected are either taken to the Hunting Grounds,{{ref|name=BMKorst}} or preyed upon on Nirn.{{ref|name=BMHunt|Events of [[Bloodmoon:Hircine's Hunt|Hircine's Hunt]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Lycanthropes are viewed as twisted mockeries of the [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]]'s noble creatures.{{ref|name=SRWWB|[[Skyrim:Wulf Wild-Blood|Wulf Wild-Blood]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} They believe that Man was not meant to live a dual life as both rational being and animal.{{ref|name=BMKorst}}

===Reachmen===
[[File:ON-item-Reachfolk Hircine.png|thumb|right|Hircine's Reachfolk Symbol]]
Hircine is the most widely worshipped deity within the Reachfolks' pantheon, and is known by many names. Among them are the '''Spirit of the Hunt''',{{ref|name=ONSpirit|[[Online:Spirit of the Hunt|Spirit of the Hunt]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONFialyn|[[Online:Apprentice Fialyn|Apprentice Fialyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Great Beast''',{{ref|name=ESORWC|{{Cite Book|Reach Witch Chant}}}} the '''Hunt-Father''',{{ref|name=ONHSG}} the '''Hunt-King''', the '''Beast Father''', '''Old Elk-Eye''', the '''Skinshaper''', the '''Spear with Five Points''',{{ref|name=GSR2}} the '''Horned Lord''',{{ref|name=MTCD|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Domihaus the Bloody-Horned}}}} the '''Horned Huntsman''',{{ref|name=RMCS|[[ON:Reach-Mage Ceremonial Skullcap|Reach-Mage Ceremonial Skullcap]] description}} the '''Wolf-Charmer''',{{ref|name=ONA|[[Online:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Stag Prince''',{{ref|name=LADCG}} the '''Master of the Chase''',{{ref|name=OTCOTR|{{Cite Book|On the Clans of the Reach}}}} and the '''Huntsman of the Princes'''.{{ref|name=ONGherig|[[Online:Gherig Bullblood|Gherig Bullblood]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Reachmen creation story speaks of Hircine's rise to prominence after Lorkh made a deal with Namira, who ruled the realm of spirit. Lorkh convinced Namira to grant him a place in the infinite void to create a realm for wayward spirits, but it was [[Lore:Dark Heart|not without a cost]]. Lorkh sacrificed himself to create a harsh realm, one that is unforgiving and intended to teach through suffering.{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} Hircine took the mantle of Lorkh's creation, becoming the sovereign of the world of flesh.{{ref|name=GSR3|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 3}}}} For this reason, the Reachmen refer to him as the '''Lord of the Arena'''. The Reachfolk believe that Hircine will fight alongside them at the "[[Lore:Kalpa|end of all days]]".{{ref|name=ONHSG}}{{Ref|name=ESOLAG|{{Cite Book|Letter to Apprentice Gwerina|ns_base=Online}}}}
[[File:SR-place-The Reach.jpg|thumb|left|The Reach, a gift from Hircine]][[File:ON-misc-Briarheart Ritual 02.png|thumb|right|Depiction of the Briarheart Ritual]]
The Reachfolk believe that Hircine gifted them their homeland, [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]],{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} and their geographical proximity to enemies on all sides drives them to follow the Prince's principle of living in the "now". Hircine engenders a sense of urgency; the unease needed to keep the people of the Reach safe, as there may be another threat looming over the horizon. Thus, Hircine has reason to be cruel in his lessons. He also taught the Reachfolk how to hunt, fight, and survive,{{ref|name=ONArdanir|[[Online:Ardanir|Ardanir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} all in an effort to make them swifter, stronger, and more cunning.{{ref|name=GSR2}} The briarheart ritual is reflective of this need to improve, and involves communing with Hircine to ritualistically replace the heart of a skilled Reachfolk warrior with [[Lore:Briar Heart|poisoned briars]].{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB}} This ritual represents Lorkh's immortal sacrifice,{{ref|name=GSR5}} which is reflected by ending ones' own life, with the end result being that the warrior is resurrected{{ref|name=ONFaorin|[[Online:Faorin|Faorin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} as a living weapon with great strength and endurance. These warriors are called "[[Lore:Briarheart|Briarhearts]]".{{ref|name=ONMD|[[Online:Medone|Medone]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They suffer constant pain,{{ref|name=ONK|[[Online:Kyrtos|Kyrtos]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} a price they pay to benefit from the transformation and protect their clan.{{ref|name=ONMD}} The Bloodroot Forge was used by the [[Lore:Dreadhorn Clan|Dreadhorn Clan]] to create bloodthirsty beings known as the [[Lore:Blood-Forged|Blood-Forged]], who share some similarities with Briarhearts. However, the Blood-Forged's creation involves a nirncrux [[Lore:Heartstone|heartstone]] being used in place of a briar heart,{{ref|name=ONF|[[Online:Filand|Filand]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} resulting in a creature that is an amalgamation of blood, nirncrux, and iron.{{ref|name=ONBloodForged|[[Online:Blood-Forged|Blood-Forged]] skin description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Due to their reverence for Hircine, it is not uncommon to find lycanthropes among the Reachmen.{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB}} In a similar vein to Briarhearts, lycanthropy is viewed by most Reachfolk as self-sacrifice. It is seen as more of a useful condition rather than a gift; one which is suffered so the individual can better serve their clan.{{ref|name=GSR2}} Some Reach Clans have proven zealous about the gift, as conflicts between them and the Viridian Wyrd have been reported.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=ONHeart|[[Online:The Heart of the Beast|The Heart of the Beast]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Reachmen are often referred to as the Witchmen of High Rock, a name given to them due to their use of dark "Reach-magic". It is believed that they learned much of this magic through trading with the [[Lore:Orsimer|Orsimer]], their neighbors who also occupy the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{Ref|name=PGE1EHR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/High Rock}}}} Another source for their magic comes from [[Lore:Hagraven|hagravens]], which involves "ecstatic ceremonial heres[ies]", such as sacrifices, to acquire dark nature magic.{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB}} Hircine is believed to be the source of where Reach-magic is drawn from,{{ref|[[Online:Vernim Woods|Vernim Woods]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} though the magic crosses into [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s domain when the cruel magic of the Gravesingers (Reachmen [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancers]]) is involved.{{ref|name=ONZK|[[Online:Zhagush gro-Korlag|Zhagush gro-Korlag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB}}{{ref|name=ESOAngof|[[On:Angof the Undying|Angof the Undying]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Reach-magic has been outlawed within the Mages Guild, though that has not stopped the art from being widely studied.{{Ref|name=PGE1EHR}}
[[File:SR-creature-White Stag.jpg|thumb|left|A White Stag]]
Hircine is involved in the legend of Chieftain [[Lore:Fiasof|Fiasof]], a tragedy told by Reachfolk [[Lore:Vateshrans|storytellers]] throughout generations. The story teaches Reachfolk to challenge themselves, and touts humility as an essential trait. The legend of Chieftain Fiasof tells of a hunter whose great skill meant that he never struggled in his hunts. His mastery of the chase initially brought Hircine joy. However, Fiasof became prideful, and grew content with these trivial hunts. This angered Hircine, who proposed a new game to punish the chief. Hircine cursed the chieftain's entire tribe, turning them to white deer, and instructed the chieftain to hunt them. Fiasof complied and rained a hail of arrows upon them, easily turning their white hides to red. It was when Fiasof went to dress the game that he realized what he had done. He broke his bow over his knee and asked for forgiveness, which the prince then provided.{{ref|name=ONVC|[[Online:Vateshran Calbokh|Vateshran Calbokh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} White deer are interpreted as omens sent by Hircine,{{ref|name=TTWT}} and his association with them can be seen in other cultures.{{Ref|name=SRMoonlight}}

Hircine oversees pacts between Reach clans, approving of the deal if the union is worth committing to. The ritual calling upon Hircine to bless a pact involves tossing a briar-heart seed, the eye of a hagraven, and a handful of [[Lore:Shale|bright river-shale]] into a ritual fire. If he approves, the Huntsman may send a sign, which can vary in nature. After this, the parties are bound by blood and spirit. Should a participant dare to break a pact, they will find no peace in the wilds, as nature will turn against them.{{ref|name=ONA}}

The Song of [[Lore:Gwyna|Gwyna]] tells of Hircine's daughter, [[Lore:Dearola|Dearola]], intermingling with Reachfolk. One of her descendants was Chief [[Lore:Rowolan|Rowolan]], the enemy of the [[Lore:Horn-Stride Clan|Horn-stride clan]], whose mythical prowess could be attributed to his lineage. However, there are several different versions of the song, all of which all lead to Rowolan's bloodline being killed.{{ref|name=TTWT}}

==History==
===Merethic Era===
====Earliest Interactions====
[[File:DF-quest-The Vanguard 03.jpg|thumb|right|Two of Hircine's children, a werewolf and a wereboar]]
Due to the lack of written accounts from the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], there is a lacuna of information on Hircine.{{intnote|Here-Seen|[nb 2]}} However, Hircine is rumored to have unleashed the curse of lycanthropy upon Nirn early in history, with the first accounts being in the early Merethic Era.{{ref|name=SSAYQ|{{Cite Book|Sage Svari Answers Your Questions}}}} Indeed, the Father of Manbeasts bestowed his gift upon the first hunters that worshipped him, by melding their cunning "to the strength and form of beasts".{{ref|name=ESOT}} These early few that were directly blessed by the Huntsman are referred to as the first turned, and are comparable to Molag Bal's [[Lore:Daughters of Coldharbour|Daughters of Coldharbour]]. These individuals would pass down their gift, and their chosen would follow suit, leading the condition to spread throughout Tamriel.{{ref|name=ESOLive3|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/281631351 Twitch Stream of ''The Elder Scrolls Online: Wolfhunter First Look''], time stamp 16:34-17:02}}

===First Era===
Hircine was honored alongside the likes of [[Lore:Lamae Bal|Lamae Bal]] as a patron of shapeshifting around the time of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]]. This is evident by the engravings on the Shapeshifter's Chain, an artifact composed of multiple metal chains that are each enchanted to be beneficial to shapeshifters of all walks of life.{{ref|name=ESOSC|[[Online:Shapeshifter's Chain|Shapeshifter's Chain]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

One of the earliest known encounters with Hircine is told in the tale of a Nord known as [[Lore:Thane Icehammer|Thane Icehammer]], who sometime in the mid-late fifth century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], unknowingly killed several lycanthropes during a hunting trip in the Yorgrim River basin. Angered by the death of his kin, Hircine thrust the Spear of Bitter Mercy into Icehammer's side, whereupon the tip broke off and remained lodged inside him. Thane returned wounded to his home of [[Lore:Cragwallow|Cragwallow]], but the magic of Hircine's Spear slowly turned him mad, ultimately causing him to murder an acolyte of Kyne in a fit of rage. Matron Icehammer was appalled by her husband's actions, and ordered the guards to subdue him, but he fled into an ancient Nordic burial ruin in the mountains northeast of Cragwallow, whereupon he was sealed alive within as punishment. The ruins would become known as Icehammer's Vault, and would become a source of infamy, as Thane Icehammer continued to stalk its halls as a [[Lore:Draugr|Draugr]]. His state of undeath and rage were fueled by the Spear fragment that remained inside him,{{ref|name=LOTI}} until he was finally slain in {{Year|2E 582}} by an adventurer.{{ref|name=ESO|[[Online:Thane Icehammer|Thane Icehammer]]'s death needed for the [[Online:Icehammer's Vault Explorer|Icehammer's Vault Explorer]] achievement in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Hircine's vengeance reached further, as hardships for the Icehammer clan soon followed, which led to their name dying out by the end of the First Era.{{ref|[[Online:Icehammer's Vault|Icehammer's Vault]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In response to the destruction of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] at the hands of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], the [[Lore:Tribunal|living god]] [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] sought out eight of the most influential Daedric Princes. Hircine was among them, and between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in {{Year|1E 2920}}, a deal was made which would be known as the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]].{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} Little is known about the Compact, but it is theorized that it prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}}

===Second Era===
During the Second Era, a strain of vampirism known as [[Lore:Disease#Noxiphilic Sanguivoria|Noxiphilic Sanguivoria]] became commonplace throughout Tamriel. [[Lore:Vampire|Vampires]] of this breed were not weakened by the sunlight, and were strengthened by the moonlight, leading to the scholar Cinna Scholasticus to believe that it originated as a result of a bargain between Hircine and Molag Bal.{{ref|name=NS|{{Cite Book|Noxiphilic Sanguivoria (book)}}}} However, this theory is unlikely, as it was first popularized by the piece of fiction ''Seventeen Tastes of Infamy'', and Cinna himself is infamous as a sensationalist within scholarly circles.{{ref|name=SSAYQ}}

====Hircine and the Hound====
[[File:ON-npc-The Hound.jpg|thumb|right|Ulthorn the Hound]]
Hircine sought the [[Lore:Green Lady|Green Lady]] as a trophy for generations, for reasons that remain ambiguous. One theory as to why was that she represents the strongest of the Bosmer, who are said to be among the greatest hunters of Nirn.{{ref|name=ONSOU}} After the Bosmer Gwaering was chosen as the Green Lady, she was obligated to marry the new [[Lore:The Silvenar|Silvenar]], Indaenir, for the good of the Bosmeri people. Her former lover [[Lore:Ulthorn|Ulthorn]] became bitter, and Hircine took advantage of his passion, forging him into a faithful servant. Ulthorn became a husk of his former self known as the Hound, and sought to usurp the Silvenar's role so he could be bonded to the Green Lady through the handfast. This ritual represents the joining of the civilized (the Silvenar) and the wild side (the Green Lady) of the Bosmer. If Ulthorn were to sever the bond, the Bosmer would revert into their primordial nature, as the Green Lady by nature would influence them to go feral,{{ref|name=ONMalabal|[[Online:Gwaering|Gwaering]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Restore the Silvenar|Restore the Silvenar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} turning them into a bestial horde with an unquenchable thirst and hunger.{{ref|name=SIDiag|[[Online:Spinner Indraseth|Spinner Indraseth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

After becoming the Hound, Ulthorn returned to his home town of Wilding Run, and wiped the local populace as he hunted them for sport.{{ref|name=ESOOWN|{{Cite Book|One Wilding Night}}}} The town was subsequently overseen by Gamekeeper Ozzai, a [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]] entrusted with utilizing the mortal trophies of Hircine's conquests, by hollowing them out into shells that will roam Nirn hunting indiscriminately. Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, after reports that researchers of the Mages Guild started vanishing reached the Fists of Thalmor, they investigated the site and were almost wiped out by Ozzai's creations during an ambush, prompting them to resort to entrusting an adventurer with the investigation. After discovering a shade of what was left Ulthorn's humanity, it guided them into the Hunting Grounds, where the Harvester was located and slain in her lair, eliminating a potential army from aiding Ulthorn's forces.{{ref|name=ESOTTB|Events of [[Online:The Ties that Bind|The Ties that Bind]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

That same year, Ulthorn and his hounds took the city of [[Lore:Silvenar|Silvenar]] hostage, forcing the city's three great spinners to enact a barrier to keep the Silvenar from the Green Lady, as well as compelling the Guardian of Silvenar to prevent his advance. The [[Lore:Hero#The Vestige|Vestige]] aided Indaenir in defeating the guardian and freeing the hostages, but the Green Lady was enthralled by Hircine's magic, and was utilized by the Hound to prevent Indaenir's further progression. To overcome this, the Vestige exploited her innate warrior tendencies, and challenged her to a battle, which was when Ulthorn joined in the fray. No longer a barrier to withhold him, the Silvenar was able to get in a close distance of his betrothed, allowing him to free her from Hircine's grasp. With Ulthorn's plan thwarted,{{ref|name=ESORS|Events of the [[Online:Restore the Silvenar|Restore the Silvenar]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the events serve as a reminder to the Bosmer of the dangers of taking Hircine's worship to extremes.{{ref|name=GAYQ}}
====Trouble in Bangkorai====
[[File:ON-place-Fallen Grotto.jpg|thumb|left|The Fallen Grotto]]
Long ago, a man, his wife and their seven sons and daughters made their home in [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]], and gave their thanks to Hircine by erecting a shrine to him within a grotto as thanks for the plentiful game in the area. Upon summoning him, Hircine asked them to prove their devotion, and ordered the father to give up his children, to which the father refused. Hircine punished them by collapsing the grotto, transforming the family into [[Lore:Troll|trolls]], and slaughtering them. The site is now known as the Fallen Grotto,{{ref|name=ESOLTFG|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Fallen Grotto}}}} its shrine has since been attended to by the [[Lore:Glenmoril Wyrd|Glenmoril Wyrd]].

Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the Reachfolk clan, the Dark Witnesses, sought to reclaim [[Lore:Western Reach|their historic territory]], and attacked the Glenmoril Wyrd, taking over the Grotto. With a sacred shrine of Hircine now theirs, their lieutenant, [[Lore:Brinarch|Brinarch]], used the opportunity to directly commune with the Huntsman, seeking the approval for a briar heart, to which the Prince approved. Since the Wyrd fell prey, they lost Hircine's favor, which was instead granted to the invaders. Hircine refused to grant an audience to the disgraced coven, forcing them to resort to having an adventurer contact him in their stead. He responded once the heart of one of the Wyrd sisters was presented to him. Intrigued by the act of self sacrifice by the coven, Hircine offered the hunter's test as a chance to earn back the Grotto; the adventurer was to track down and defeat his Aspects of Strength, Speed, and Guile. The Prince brought a mere shade of the Hunting Grounds to the Fallen Grotto, where therein the challenge began. After slaying the aspects, the final challenge was between two hunters. The adventurer prevailed, and Brinarch's briar heart was torn out and offered to Hircine as tribute, winning the Huntsman's favor back for the coven. Brinarch's spirit was to wander the Hunting Grounds with a gaping hole where his heart once was, a reminder of his failure.{{ref|name=ESOTHB|Events of [[Online:The Heart of the Beast|The Heart of the Beast]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Around this time, some individuals who were infected with lycanthropy and sought to become one of Hircine's creatures were found by those kin to Hircine's own Packmaster [[Lore:Songamdir|Songamdir]]. These lucky few were invited into the Hunting Grounds to participate in an ancient ritual the Packmaster. They presented a tribute to Hircine and his pack, and after a successful hunt, they were inducted into Hircine's own pack of werewolves, and were given greater mastery over their form than the average lycanthrope.{{ref|name=ESOHG|[[Online:Thoreki|Thoreki]], [[Online:Songamdir|Songamdir]], and [[Online:Hircine|Hircine]]'s dialogue during the quest [[Online:Hircine's Gift|Hircine's Gift]]}}

====Vykosa the Ascendant====
[[File:ON-quest-The Great Hunt 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Moon Hunter Pack among the Great Hunt's participants]]
Later that year, Hircine hosted the Great Hunt in his plane, inviting mortals from Tamriel to participate. During this time, one of the first turned, the werewolf lord [[Lore:Vykosa|Vykosa]], amassed the [[Lore:Moon Hunter Pack|largest werewolf pack]] that Tamriel had ever seen.{{ref|name=RJ|{{Cite Book|Rolea's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} She plotted to use her pack to take over [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and exact revenge on Hircine, for she and other werewolves felt they were tricked into playing the part of prey instead of the role of a predator.{{ref|name=ESOTHCC|{{Cite Book|The Hungry Cat's Curse|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=LtM|{{Cite Book|Letter to Mylenne|ns_base=Online}}}} Vykosa sent her lieutenant Balorgh along with a reconnaissance squad to infiltrate the Hunting Grounds, though he and his pack would instead opt to participate in the Hunt and seek Hircine's Boon.{{ref|name=LTB|{{Cite Book|Letter to Balorgh|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=BPESO|{{Cite Book|Balorgh's Plan|ns_base=Online}}}} Unfortunately for Balorgh, Hircine would ultimately grant his boon to the true winners of the Great Hunt, the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]].{{ref|name=TGHESO|[[Online:The Great Hunt|The Great Hunt]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Meanwhile, the Undaunted invaded {{Lore Link|Moon Hunter Keep}}, a fortress of the [[Lore:Silver Dawn|Silver Dawn]] that had been taken over by Vykosa and her pack. The adventurers defeated the werewolf lord, cutting her reign short.{{ref|name=ESOMA|Events of [[Online:Moonlight Ascent|Moonlight Ascent]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
Hircine is believed to have hosted the Great Hunt within the [[Lore:Chimera of Desolation|Chimera of Desolation]] circa {{Year|3E 398}},{{intnote|Noonan|[nb 1]}} when Mehrunes Dagon's forces attacked and seized control of the [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]]. With the portal back to Tamriel blocked, an [[Lore:Hero#The Apprentice|apprentice]] trapped within the Battlespire made it to the Chimera and became the Hare in a Great Hunt instigated by Dagon's [[Lore:Herne (creature)|Herne]] minions. However, the apprentice was then aided by [[Lore:Chimere Graegyn|Chimere Graegyn]], an inhabitant of the Chimera of Desolation and someone who had previously defied Dagon.{{ref|name=Battlespire}} The apprentice retrieved the Spear of Bitter Mercy, wore the Saviour's Hide, and was able to slay their hunter and escape the realm.{{ref|name=Battlespire|Events of [[Battlespire:Level 5|Level 5]] in [[Battlespire:Battlespire|''Battlespire'']]}}

====An Ungrateful Child====
Around {{Year|3E 405}}, an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]] paid the [[Lore:Glenmoril Coven|Glenmoril Coven]] of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] to summon Hircine. The Huntsman told the agent of a contrite wereboar in the area, and requested he be "taught a lesson". After impressing the Prince by putting the creature out of its "obvious misery", the agent was rewarded with Hircine's Ring.{{ref|name=DFHircine}}

====The Hunter's Game====
[[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] legend speaks of the Bloodmoon Prophecy, which foretells the coming of the Great Hunt to the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]. According to the Prophecy, a demon god will appear on Solstheim with his Hounds and initiate the Hunter's Game. The Hunter's arrival would be preceded by three signs: Fire From the Eye of Glass, the Tide of Woe, and the Bloodmoon.{{ref|name=BMKorst}} When the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] visited the isle in {{Year|3E 427}}, the Prophecy came to pass.{{ref|name=BMHunt}}

[[File:BM-quest-Hircine's Hunt 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Bloodmoon, the Final Sign]]
While investigating a werewolf attack on the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] and the disappearance of Captain [[Lore:Falx Carius|Falx Carius]] from [[Lore:Fort Frostmoth|Fort Frostmoth]], the Nerevarine had to earn the trust of the Skaal.{{ref|name=BMDisappearance|Events of [[Bloodmoon:The Disappearance of Captain Carius|The Disappearance of Captain Carius]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Before long, the signs appeared; a pillar of fire erupted from [[Lore:Lake Fjalding|Lake Fjalding]],{{ref|name=BMTS|Events of [[Bloodmoon:The Skaal Test of Strength|The Skaal Test of Strength]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} the shores became littered with dead [[Lore:Horker|horkers]],{{ref|name=BMCastle|Events of [[Bloodmoon:The Castle Karstaag|The Castle Karstaag]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} and finally, the Bloodmoon appeared.{{ref|name=BMHunt}} During this time, a pack of werewolves attacked the [[Lore:Skaal Village|Skaal Village]], and the Nerevarine was infected with [[Lore:Sanies Lupinus|Sanies Lupinus]]—the disease which leads to lycanthropy. However, it is unclear if the Nerevarine cured the disease in time or became a werewolf.{{ref|name=BMSiege|Events of [[Bloodmoon:The Siege of the Skaal Village|The Siege of the Skaal Village]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}

Ultimately, the Nerevarine was taken by Hircine to the [[Lore:Mortrag Glacier|Mortrag Glacier]], where the Hunter's Game was getting underway.{{ref|name=BMHunt}} The Prince had chosen four mortals—the missing Captain Carius, Skaal leader Tharsten Heart-Fang, [[Lore:Karstaag|Karstaag]] the [[Lore:Frost Giant|frost giant]], and the Nerevarine—for the "honor" of being in his Hunt, as he deemed them worthy hunters.{{ref|name=BMHircine|[[Bloodmoon:Hircine|Hircine's dialogue]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} The four mortals were required to navigate a maze within the glacier, fighting off werewolves and each other, with the survivor earning the right to challenge Hircine.{{ref|name=BMHunt}}{{ref|name=BMHircine}}

The Nerevarine entered the maze and encountered Captain Carius; the two teamed up against the werewolves, and when it came time to venture deeper into the glacier, Carius volunteered to stay behind. Tharsten Heart-Fang was in the next section, and revealed himself to be a werewolf before being slain by the Nerevarine. The Nerevarine slew Karstaag at the heart of the glacier, and was met by Hircine.{{ref|name=BMHunt}} The Huntsman then bestowed the "greatest honor" he could give a mortal—the Nerevarine would be his prey. The Huntsman allowed the Nerevarine to choose one of his aspects to battle, to give his prey a sporting chance.{{ref|name=BMHircine}}
{{Quote Box|''The Bloodmoon will rise again, and my hounds will walk the lands. Let this world enjoy its brief respite, for Hircine will hunt again!''|Hircine{{ref|name=BMHircine}}}}
With the Bloodmoon setting, the battle began, and the Nerevarine stood victorious by the time it disappeared.{{ref|name=BMHunt}} Hircine was surprised, but vowed to one day return to Solstheim and hunt once again under the Bloodmoon.{{ref|name=BMHircine}} The Nerevarine escaped through a portal as Mortrag Glacier collapsed, ending the Hunter's Game until the next era.{{ref|name=BMHunt}}

Contemparary with the release of the [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition|3rd Edition of the Pocket Guide to the Empire]], around {{Year|3E 432}}, the gateway to Hircine's Hunting Grounds was closed on consensus of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] until further notice.{{ref|name=Census|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Imperial Census of Daedra Lords|ns_base=General}}}}

====The Last Unicorn====
[[File:OB-creature-Unicorn.jpg|thumb|right|The last unicorn]]
In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] summoned Hircine at a shrine in Cyrodiil's Great Forest. The Prince tasked the Hero with slaying a [[Lore:Unicorn|unicorn]] in Harcane Grove that was protected by fearsome minotaurs. After offering the unicorn's horn to the Prince's shrine, the Hero was rewarded with the [[Lore:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]].{{ref|name=OBHircine|Events of [[Oblivion:Hircine|Hircine's quest]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} Although unicorns are native to the Hunting Grounds, this hunt is believed to have resulted in the beasts' extinction on Nirn. Despite this, they would remain a favorite subject for poets and artists.{{ref|name=BLIceUnicorn|[[Blades:Ice Unicorn|Ice Unicorn]] description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} It wasn't until {{Year|4E 201}} that a Unicorn would be spotted on Nirn again, with one theory being that the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] had transported one of the very last unicorns into the future to prevent their utter extinction.{{ref|name=SJ|{{Cite Book|Soran's Journal}}}}

===Fourth Era===
====Umbriel Crisis====
During the [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|Umbriel Crisis]], Hircine impeded the journey of [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus Mede]] and his companions as they were passing through his Hunting Grounds to reach their destination. As eternal hunts are the norm within the realm, Mede and his companions became unwilling prey. They were slowly being bottled in by drivers until nighttime, where Hircine would arrive with his pack of werewolves. Nighttime came, two horns blared, and a deep and primal voice was heard upstream that was capable of leading one to a mindless terror. At a glance, they saw an enormous silhouette of a man with the horns of a stag and they knew Hircine had arrived. To ward off Hircine and his pack, the members of the Khajiiti [[Lore:Khajiit#Khajiiti Clans|F'aashe]] clan that accompanied Mede stayed behind to stall them, allowing Mede and his companions to escape the Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=TIC}}

====The Companions====
[[File:SR-quest-Glory of the Dead 04.jpg|thumb|right|Freed from Hircine's grasp]]
Following Harbinger [[Lore:Kyrnil Long-Nose|Kyrnil Long-Nose]]'s founding of [[Lore:The Circle|the Circle]] in the late Second Era,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} the upper ranks of the [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] became infected with lycanthropy after Harbinger [[Lore:Terrfyg|Terrfyg]] made a bargain with the witches of the Glenmoril Coven.{{ref|name=KJ}} The terms were that if the Companions were to hunt in the name of Hircine, they would be granted great power, but the Circle was deceived into believing it would not be a permanent affliction. Around 4E 201, some members of the Circle, led by Harbinger [[Lore:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]], would eventually conclude it was not a gift, but a curse with serious spiritual implications that would threaten their souls' opportunity to reach Sovngarde, and worked to eliminate it from the Companions.{{ref|name=SRKodlakBH|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue during [[SR:Blood's Honor|Blood's Honor]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The heads of the Glenmoril witches who cursed the Companions' Inner Circle were required to cure them. Before he could be cured, Harbinger Kodlak Whitemane was killed by a group of werewolf hunters called the [[Lore:Silver Hand|Silver Hand]].{{ref|name=SRBH|Events of [[SR:Blood's Honor|Blood's Honor]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Companions destroyed the Silver Hand, then used the severed heads of the Glenmoril witches to cleanse their dead Harbinger and themselves of lycanthropy. Kodlak Whitemane's Wolf Spirit was killed by the Companions, allowing his soul to find rest in Sovngarde.{{ref|name=GOTD|Events of [[SR:Glory of the Dead|Glory of the Dead]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Kodlak Whitemane was grateful for the chance to join his ancestors in Sovngarde.{{ref|name=GOTDKodlak|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue after killing his [[SR:Kodlak's Wolf Spirit|Wolf Spirit]] during [[SR:Glory of the Dead|Glory of the Dead]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, he hoped that someday, he and other honored warriors could invade the Hunting Grounds and rescue the souls of the Companions who did not have the chance to free themselves from Hircine's grasp before death. He called this imagined battle "The Harrowing of the Hunting Grounds",{{ref|name=GOTDKodlak}} but whether or not it is possible to posthumously wrench a werewolf's soul free from the Hunting Grounds through battle is unclear.

====Spirit of the Hunt====
[[File:SR-quest-Ill Met By Moonlight.jpg|thumb|left|The Bloodmoon reappears]]
That same year, a murder occurred in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], the victim being a young girl from [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]].{{ref|name=SRMoonlight}} The [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] visited the imprisoned culprit, Sinding, who revealed he was a werewolf.{{ref|name=SRMoonlight}} Sinding had taken the Ring of Hircine to help control his condition—this evidently upset the Prince, who cursed the ring to cause the wearer to transform at random. The girl was merely an innocent bystander.{{ref|name=SRSinding|[[Skyrim:Sinding|Sinding]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

Sinding had come to Falkreath to hunt the majestic White Stag, as Hircine would commune with anyone who slayed it, and wished to beg the Prince's forgiveness.{{ref|name=SRSinding}} The Dragonborn offered to take the ring to Hircine in Sinding's place, and the werewolf gratefully accepted before escaping his cell. The Dragonborn tracked down and slew the White Stag, and an Aspect of Hircine appeared. The Huntsman agreed to "consider" lifting the ring's curse, but initiated the Great Hunt as he demanded an offering—Sinding's hide. The werewolf was holed up within Bloated Man's Grotto; the Bloodmoon had appeared, and by the time the Dragonborn arrived, one group of hunters had already been slain. Sinding made one last plea, asking the Dragonborn to defy Hircine and fight alongside him.{{ref|name=SRSinding}}

Reports conflict on what occurred during the Great Hunt. Some sources claim that the Dragonborn joined the pursuit and was rewarded with the Savior's Hide in exchange for Sinding's pelt. Other accounts state that the Dragonborn instead sided with Sinding and hunted the remaining hunters, resulting in the Prince purifying the Ring for the Dragonborn's personal use out of respect for turning the hunt on itself. Regardless of the direction the chase took, Hircine was pleased, and Sinding never appeared in civilized areas again.{{ref|name=SRMoonlight}}

===Servants and Creatures===
Among some of Hircine's most revered servants are the [[Lore:Huntsman|Huntsman]], mysterious humanoid servants from the Hunting Grounds that have [[Lore:Daedra Heart|Daedric hearts]]. Indeed, it is not uncommon for worshippers of Hircine to emulate the clothing of a Huntsman as a proxy of worship to Hircine.{{ref|name=CMH}} The typical lycanthrope is also likely to serve Hircine, who is the progenitor of their divine disease.

Hircine is the creator of the legendary Unicorn, which he occasionally brings from his Hunting Grounds and into Nirn to hunt.{{ref|name=AOLH}} He has also been associated with some legendary pale and white creatures, such as the Pale Senche and the White Stag. The hunting of such creatures has resulted in him granting his favor upon their deaths.{{ref|name=ONSenche|[[Online:The Senche|The Senche]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SRMoonlight}}

==Notes==
* The term [[Wiktionary:Hircine|hircine]] is an adjective which means "like a [[Lore:Goat|goat]]", which is usually used to describe odors similar to the smell of a goat. It was conceived by [[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]], the writer who came up with all the Daedra names in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'', who noted that he may have intended for the name to be [[Wiktionary:hirsute|Hirsute]] and got the two words confused.{{ref|name=TeddyPP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ted Peterson's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}}
