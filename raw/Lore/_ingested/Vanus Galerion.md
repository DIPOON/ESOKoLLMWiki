[[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] (sometimes '''Galerion Vanus'''){{ref|name=ResearchNotesQuest|[[Daggerfall:Research Notes|Research Notes]] quest in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} (born '''Trechtus'''), called the '''Great Mage''',  was the founder and first Archmagister of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] and one of the most famous users of [[Lore:Magic|magic]] in the history of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=GTM|{{Cite Book|Galerion the Mystic}}}} By some accounts Galerion was the "quintessential mortal mage", his magical ability acknowledged even by the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], who believed his power could be used to complete the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] in a matter of mere hours.{{ref|name=TSoP|{{Cite Book|The Source of Power}}}}{{ref|name=GalerionHealth}} He himself claimed to have been the greatest mage of his time.{{ref|name=GalerionJustice|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Justice for the Fallen|Justice for the Fallen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] regarded him as a gifted magical talent who, in his view, squandered that potential by devoting himself to the pursuits of the Mages Guild.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} Vanus trained with the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] along with fellow student [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]. He was a staunch opponent of Mannimarco's practice of [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]], and ultimately led an army of mages against Mannimarco's legions of undead. Though he succeeded in halting Mannimarco's advance, he was slain in the battle.{{ref|name=MKOW|{{Cite Book|Mannimarco, King of Worms}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Early Life===
Trechtus was born in the early years of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] (roughly 600 years before the Alliance War){{Ref|name=Emmandic|[[Online:Emmandic Oltz|Emmandic Oltz]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} in the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]]s to serfs on the estate of a minor nobleman, Lord Gyrnasse of [[Lore:Sollicich-on-Ker|Sollicich-on-Ker]]. His father had secretly, against the law of Lord Gyrnasse, taught himself and then Trechtus to read. They had to rely on a black market to get around the restriction Gyrnasse put in place, but still managed to get a steady stream of books and scrolls. When Trechtus was eight, the smugglers were found and imprisoned. Some said that Trechtus's mother, an ignorant and religious woman fearful of her husband, was the betrayer of the smugglers, but there were other rumors as well. The trial of the smugglers was nonexistent, and the punishment swift. The body of Trechtus' father was kept hanging for weeks during the hottest summer Sollicich-on-Ker had seen in centuries.{{ref|name=GTM}}

Three months later, Trechtus ran away, making it as far as [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]], half-way across Summerset Isle. A band of troubadours found him nearly dead, curled up in a ditch by the side of the road. They nursed him to health, and employed him as an errand boy in return for food and shelter. One of the troubadours, a soothsayer named Heliand, who had trained as a mystic at [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], recognized the boy's potential. When the troupe was performing in the village of {{Lore Link|Potansa}} on the far eastern end of Summerset, Heliand took Trechtus, then eleven, to Artaeum, where the Magister of the Isle, [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]], also recognized this potential and took Trechtus on as pupil, giving him the name of Vanus Galerion.{{ref|name=GTM}}

===At Artaeum===
[[File:ON-quest-Half-Formed Understandings.jpg|thumb|right|A vision of Mannimarco assaulting a young Vanus in the Traitor's Vault on Artaeum after Vanus informed the Psijic elders of his experiments]]
It was on Artaeum where Vanus Galerion met Mannimarco. The two quickly developed a relationship defined by both friendship and rivalry, born of mutual respect for each other’s genius. Vanus was spontaneous and intuitive, wielding magic as naturally as breathing, while Mannimarco possessed a talent for dissecting theories and exploring possibilities beyond the grasp of most mages. Their brilliance attracted followers who elevated them into positions of influence within the Order. The pair frequently debated various topics, captivating any Psijics who stopped to listen, and their debates on the secrets of magic helped establish principles that are still studied today.{{ref|name=FDESO|{{Cite Book|The First Dialogue of Mannimarco and Galerion}}}}{{ref|name=SDESO|{{Cite Book|The Second Dialogue of Mannimarco and Galerion}}}}

One such topic began as a debate about whether magicka truly exists but soon shifted into a discussion about the morality of magical alteration. Mannimarco argued that magicka does not exist as an external force and is simply the manifestation of a mage’s will. To challenge this view, Vanus transmogrified his robe to a bright green. Mannimarco remained unconvinced and explained that magicka is neither moral nor immoral, requires no consent, and is limited only by the will of the caster. Another debate focused on whether Arkay’s laws apply to [[Lore:Soul Magic|Soul Magic]], which naturally brought the discussion to the nature of Necromancy. Mannimarco repeated his belief that power itself has no intrinsic morality and that it is the mage who chooses to use magic for good or harmful purposes. He argued that Necromancy is not inherently immoral, noting that other forms of magic, such as electromancy or cryomancy, can also be used for murder. Vanus disagreed and stated that Necromancy is objectionable because it does not seek the consent of the deceased before forcing them into servitude. He added that necromancers often use deceptive methods to obtain consent or simply ignore the issue when circumstances allow.{{ref|name=FDESO}}{{ref|name=SDESO}}

While Galerion used his talents wisely and honorably, being described as having a "light and warm" heart, Mannimarco turned to the dark arts and was slowly corrupted by them, being noted as having a "dark and cold" heart. Galerion became aware of his contemporary's transformation, and confronted Mannimarco, enraged at the discovery their fellow students were being used as test subjects, however Mannimarco scoffed at Galerion's threats, and continued to study the dark arts. Eventually, Galerion confronted Mannimarco at [[Lore:Ceporah Tower|Ceporah Tower]], demanding that Mannimarco end his foul ways, but to no avail. Galerion informed the Psijic elders of his discovery and was nearly killed by the retaliation of Mannimarco, who remained unconcerned believing he'd simply be banished in the end. When the Psijic order banished Mannimarco to mainland Tamriel, Galerion realized that the necromancer would use the opportunity to amass a following there. The masters ignored Galerion's warnings about the terror they were releasing on the rest of Tamriel, a mistake that Galerion would not soon forget.{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|EGT}}}}{{ref|name=GalerionHalf|[[Online:Vanus Galerion | Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue during [[Online:Half-Formed Understandings|Half-Formed Understandings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  

After having many disagreements with the Psijics (though they were united in their hatred of necromancy), Galerion left Artaeum to pursue his dream: bringing the wondrous benefits of magic to the general public.{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=TBAOT|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}}

===Birth of the Guild===
[[File:ON-place-Mages Guild (Firsthold).jpg|thumb|right|The Mages Guildhall in Firsthold, the very first headquarters of the Guild]]
When he began collecting magic-users from around Summerset Isle, Galerion attracted the animosity of all. He was operating out of the urban center of [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]], and there was a common (and not entirely unfounded) attitude that magical experiments should be conducted only in unpopulated areas. Even more provocatively, Galerion proposed to make magical items, potions, and even spells available to any member of the general public who could afford to pay. No longer was magic to be limited either to the aristocracy or intelligentsia.{{ref|name=OOTMG|{{Cite Book|Origin of the Mages Guild}}}} Some labeled Galerion's efforts "Galerion's Folly."{{ref|name=KRatMG|{{Cite Book|Kinlord Rilis and the Mages Guild}}}}

In {{Year|2E 230}},{{ref|name=MGC}}{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}} less than a year after leaving Psijics, Galerion was brought before Iachesis, the King of Firsthold, [[Lore:Rilis XII|Rilis XII]],{{ref|name=MGC|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}}{{ref|name=MKOW}} and numerous nobles and members of the Psijic Order in the "Charter Conclave" to state the intentions of the fraternity he was forming.{{ref|name=KRatMG}} Galerion's speech to Rilis and Iachesis was not recorded for posterity, but his charter, at any rate, was approved.{{ref|name=OOTMG}} It proved to be extraordinarily successful, and Galerion himself would become very well-known for his wisdom. He's still cited as the authority on many magical concepts, such as trafficking with [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]],{{ref|name=LB|{{Cite Book|Liminal Bridges}}}} and the modern schools of magic were developed by him.{{ref|name=TBAOT}} He soon after formed the [[Lore:Order of the Lamp|Order of the Lamp]] as protectors of the Guild.{{ref|name=OOTMG}} Galerion's policy on the practice of necromancy was that it should be condemned, except for special cases, and only under controlled conditions.{{Ref|name=GMoST}}

Before Mages Guild chapter houses had spread to the mainland of Tamriel,Vanus Galerion invented hyper powerful automated tools of alchemy and enchanting, so that anyone, no matter their own skill in the craft, could concoct whatever they wanted to. This was balanced by the fact that the cost in gold to create devices too dangerous were not affordable for the common man. Some few incidents occurred where some "unschooled yokels" invented things beyond their ken, such as the enchanting of the Daedric artifact [[Lore:Feyfolken|Feyfolken]]. As an inevitability, Galerion later tore apart his tools, and created less powerful versions used within the guild for most of that institution's longevity.{{ref|name=Feyfolken|{{Cite Book|Feyfolken}}}}

====Mannimarco Gains Influence====
Seeking to normalize necromancy and gain a following to his discipline, Mannimarco created his [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]] to rival the ascendant Mages Guild.{{ref|name=TOTTBW|{{Cite Book|The Order of the Black Worm}}}}{{ref|name=WS|{{Cite Book|Worm Saga}}}} Vanus believed Mannimarco was responsible for making the [[Lore:Souls|soul]]-trap spell common knowledge among mages, leading to him passing strictures classifying the art of soul trapping into two categories: the legal, "White" souls consisting of lesser essences such as beasts and animals, and illegal "Black" souls derived from sentient beings.{{ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} In time, it became impossible for standard Soul Trap spells to capture a Black Soul within a regular soul gem, instead requiring the use of the more dangerous and elusive [[Lore:Soul Gem|Black Soul Gems]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Souls, Black and White}}}}

When [[Lore:Vastarie|Vastarie]], a necromancer who had been a disciple in the Psijic Order alongside Galerion and had worked with Mannimarco to retrieve the first Black Soul Gems from Coldharbour, attempted to present the Sigil Geodes she'd invented to the fledgling guild, Galerion attended the demonstration.The geodes allowed for a soul to be captured upon the moment of death and than be interrogated for their knowledge at the mage's leisure, before being released or moved between crystals at will without the need of [[Lore:Enchantment|enchantment]]. Vastarie's invention is said to have left Galerion white faced with horror, and he departed after giving a furious and vindictive speech condemning the practice, with some among the assembled students following him.{{ref|name=TLoV|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Vastarie}}}}

===Disillusionment===
{{Quote Box|style=float:right; max-width:4; margin-left:0|''"The Guild has become nothing more than an intricate morass of political infighting."''|''Vanus Galerion''}}
Over time, Galerion grew bitterly disillusioned with the contrived hierarchies and sinister political environment that the Mages Guild had become. He grew regretful for establishing the guild in the first place, as it had become monster of its own, and was too late for him to fix. After denouncing the guild, Galerion elected to leave Tamriel entirely to travel to other lands. For many years Galerion wandered around [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. Eventually, after long his abandonment of the Mages Guild, Galerion claimed that he had found the virtue of magic in his solitary travels.{{ref|name=OOTMG}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=MasserAndSecunda|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Masser and Secunda|''Masser and Secunda'']]}}{{Ref|name=ESOEGTS|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}} Despite this, Galerion still held the venerable title of Archmagister Emeritus, and influenced many of the Guild's affairs.{{Ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} He also served as Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]]'s tutor during her youth, earning her deep trust, a bond she once compared to that of a family member.{{Ref|name=Ayrenn|[[ON:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] also established a long friendship with Vanus. While publicly neutral about it, Emeric would be secretly supportive of the Mages Guild's foothold on [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]]. King Emeric and King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] would come to owe Vanus a few favors.{{ref|name=ESOD|[[ON:High King Emeric|High King Emeric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Emmandic Oltz|Emmandic Oltz]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Vanus Galerion's hiatus would last for over a century, but he would eventually return to his duties as the Archmagister due to the crisis that would follow the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] in {{Year|2E 578}}.{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

===Invasion of Coldharbour===
[[File:ON-load-The Mooring.jpg|thumb|left|Galerion stands before the Great Shackle]][[File:ON-quest-The Army of Meridia 02.jpg|thumb|right|Vanus (center left) meets with the Guilds coalition in the Hollow City's Chapel of Light]]
Vanus Galerion became increasingly concerned about the activities of the Worm Cult and the looming threat of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s Planemeld. In {{Year|2E 582}}, he and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] attempted to negotiate a cease-fire in the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]], so that the three alliances could commit their full resources to an assault on Coldharbour. This negotiation took place on the island of [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]] and was ultimately unsuccessful, but, after an attack by Molag Bal's forces, the alliances did endorse a smaller action, in which Mages Guild and [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] forces invaded Coldharbour to stop the Planemeld. Vanus personally opened the [[Lore:Portals|portal]] used to enter Coldharbour. {{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Online}}{{ref|name=Galerion|[[Online:Vanus Galerion | Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Though initially scattered across Coldharbour by the realm's defenses, the invasion force was gradually reassembled within the [[Lore:Hollow City|Hollow City]], a sanctuary of Meridia within Coldharbour which, encased by the power of the [[Lore:Light of Meridia|Light of Meridia]] and pushed into Molag Bal's realm long ago by its Prince, served as a piece of Meridia's realm within the domain of the God of Schemes, and so could not be touched.{{ref|name=Groundskeeper|[[ON:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The last [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] king, [[Lore:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]], who had been brought to Coldharbour alongside the Hollow City and been kept alive for centuries to be tortured by the Daedra, was also rescued from his prison in the Lightless Oubliette through the use of one of the Lights of Meridia, and went on to assume leadership of the force assembled in the Hollow City.{{ref|name=Dynar|[[ON:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LaloriaranDynar}}

During this invasion, Galerion was teleported outside the Black Forge of Coldharbour alone by the realm's defenses,{{ref|name=Groundskeeper}} and attempted to disable it to help stop the Planemeld. He was soon captured by the Daedra, who intended to use him as a power source, believing that powerful mortals and especially magic users were the most efficient power source for the Planemeld{{ref|name=PDA|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}} and that, as the "quintessential mortal mage", the power raging inside Galerion would allow them to complete the forging of the [[Lore:Dark Anchor|Great Shackle]] and power it with Galerion's magic, thus allowing them to complete the Planemeld in mere hours instead of months. To prevent this outcome Galerion separated his being into its constituent [[Lore:Souls|essences]] of magicka, health and stamina, which the Daedra could not use to complete the Planemeld individually. Despite this, the overseers of the Black Forge imprisoned the separated essences and used them to power the Black Forge itself, using them to craft [[Lore:Dark Anchor|Dark Anchors]] and components of the Great Shackle, to power their [[Lore:Soul Shriven|Soul Shriven]] slaves so they'd keep working tirelessly, and to feed their [[Lore:Vampire|vampiric]] servants. The Vestige freed him, restored and reunited his essence, and journeyed with him, when he personally destroyed the Great Shackle that was intended to bind Coldharbour and Nirn together by sending metaphysical chains across the planar chaos to latch onto the world. With this task accomplished, Galerion also joined the force assembled in the Hollow City.{{ref|name=TSoP}}{{ref|name=GalerionMagicka|[[Online:Galerion's Magicka|Galerion's Magicka]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GalerionStamina|[[Online:Galerion's Stamina|Galerion's Stamina]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GalerionHealth|[[Online:Galerion's Health|Galerion's Health]] dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Online}}{{ref|name=Galerion|[[Online:Vanus Galerion | Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Though the Great Shackle had been destroyed, it had generated a Planar Vortex prior to its deactivation. Likened to an "Oblivion straw" that would pull Nirn and "its very essence" into Coldharbour by itself if not dispersed.{{ref|name=TSoP}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}{{ref|name=Cadwell|[[Online:Cadwell |Cadwell]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LaloriaranDynar|[[ON:Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Army of Meridia|The Army of Meridia]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-npc-Vanus Galerion 07.jpg|thumb|right|Vanus readies the Light of Meridia with Gabrielle.]]
The guild forces, the "Army of Meridia", crossed {{Lore Link|the Chasm}} of Coldharbour but were met by the vampires of {{Lore Link|the Orchard}} which were controlled by one of Molag Bal's Harvest Hearts. Vanus proposed enacting a ritual that would destroy the Heart and target the [[Lore:Blood Magic|blood]] of all vampires connected to it, rendering them mindless and allowing the guilds to pass through. The vampire leader Virgar proposed using a method that would free the vampires from the Heart instead, allowing them to become allied to the guild forces.{{ref|name=Galerion}} It is unknown what the Vestige ultimately chose, but the vampires were severed from Molag Bal's control, allowing the guilds to enter the {{Lore Link|Reaver Citadel}}, which they captured to gain access to {{Lore Link|the Endless Stair}}, to which they were granted access by the restored [[Lore:Foolkillers Clan|Foolkillers Clan]].{{ref|[[Online:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue at the end of [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]]}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}{{ref|name=Harvest|Events of [[Online:The Harvest Heart|The Harvest Heart]]  in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Citadel|Events of [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]]  in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Thus a small group entered the Planar Vortex with Meridia. Their goal was to unleash the Light of Meridia at the focal point of the Vortex thus dispersing it. However, before the Light of Meridia could be unleashed, the group needed to destroy the Dark Ancors that Molag Bal was using to bind the Vortex together as, otherwise, the resulting backlash would "destroy Nirn as surely as the Planemeld would". Meridia, whose power was greater in the in-between realm than it had been in Coldharbour, aided in this task by distracting Molag Bal and defending the group from him. After the destruction of the Anchors, the Light, magically prepared by Vanus Galerion and [[Lore:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]], destroyed the vortex entirely, setting back all progress Bal had made on the Planemeld.{{ref|name=ESOMeridiaTFS|[[ON:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOTFS|Events of [[Online:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Galerion}}

===Rediscovery of Scribing and Guild Affairs===
[[File:ON-render-Hero's Return.jpg|Within the Aetherian Archive|thumb|left]]
During this time, [[Lore:Scribing|Scribing]], a "metamagical theoryset"{{ref|name=Largesse}} created by [[Lore:Ulfsild the Evergreen|Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen]] and the [[Lore:Luminary|Luminaries of Magic]],{{ref|name=Crow|[[Online:The Crow|The Crow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=How|[[Online:How to Scribe|How to Scribe]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TI|[[Online:The Indrik|The Indrik]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} which was a lost art up until the time of the Three Banners War, was rediscovered by the Mages Guild's Order of the Lamp. Vanus Galerion was particularly enthusiastic of the possibilities it would open up, with early ideas being altars being installed in every guild hall, or that Scribing will perhaps one day be taught in the wilds.{{ref|name=LAS|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Scribing}}}} The legacy of Ulfsild's lived on, with Scribing eventually evolving into the modern [[Lore:Spellcrafting|Spellcrafting]] systems.{{Ref|name=ON2024|group=UOL|[[General:ESO 2024 Global Reveal|ESO 2024 Global Reveal]]}}

At this time, the Arch-Mage would also confront the warlock Carindon over his imprisonment of the [[Lore:Changeling|Changeling]] [[Lore:Selene|Selene]], an act which violated the accord that existed between Selene and the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=Allanwen|[[Online:Allanwen|Allanwen]]'s dialogue [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Around {{Year|2E 582}}, he enlisted the Vestige to help free Oracle Marieve from [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] during the Daedric War. Galerion contacted the Vestige through the use of a Mages Guild Message Stone which conjured his projection. He [[Lore:enchantment|enchanted]] a boat to take the Vestige to their destination and provided a summoning medallion which the Vestige could use to summon him to their location so that he could provide guidance during the investigation, but departed upon discovering that the Psijic Order was involved due to his bitter history with them. The guild also provided care for Marieve after she was rescued.{{ref|name=GalerionVeil|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion's ]] dialogue during [[Online:Through a Veil Darkly|Through a Veil Darkly]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Sometime in the First Era,{{ref|name=ESOArn|[[ON:Firstmage Arnure|Firstmage Arnure]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the [[Lore:Firstmages|Firstmages]] assumed guardianship of the [[Lore:Aetherian Archive|Aetherian Archive]], a “once-Great Source of knowledge and power”. In the Second Era, the Mages Guild reinforced the Archive’s defenses by placing powerful wards around it. In 2E 582, Brulo Rlane, a wizard trained by Galerion himself, went rogue and broke through the wards and entered the tower, seeking to claim the Great Source for his own. As Brulo's actions had the potential to threaten all of Tamriel, Arnure of the Firstmages reached out to Galerion for aid. In an attempt to delay his former master, Brulo opened planar breaches that unleashed [[Lore:Shadow|shadowy]] [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] beings to slow Galerion’s progress. While Galerion began sealing the rifts, he summoned the Vestige to the Archive to battle the Daedra. As the Vestige fought through the summoned forces, including a powerful [[Lore:Gloam Knight|Gloam Knight]], Galerion confronted and ultimately defeated Brulo in a magical duel. With the danger passed, Galerion remarked that Nirn would sleep peacefully, unaware of how close it had come to disaster. The two quickly departed from the tower, as the power of the Great Source was too much for either the Vestige or Galerion to linger around long.{{ref|name=GalerionRogue|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Rogue Mage|The Rogue Mage]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Rogue|Events of [[Online:The Rogue Mage|The Rogue Mage]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Stirk Fellowship===
[[File:LO-book-The Stirk Fellowship & Solstice 02.jpg|thumb|right|Prominent figures of the Stirk Fellowship]]
Sometime after the Planemeld, the Mages and Fighters Guilds were targeted by the resurrected Worm Cult. With the guild hall underneath [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] devastated, and the Fighters Guild leader [[Lore:Merric at-Aswala|Merric at-Aswala]] killed by a strange new device known as a [[Lore:Soul Reaper|Soul Reaper]], Vanus Galerion arrived at the city to accompany his compatriots to the Cult's hideout near the lighthouse west of [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]]. There, they learned the Worm Cult planned to hide the Soul Reapers across Tamriel and then activate them to siphon souls at a massive scale, making them a top priority target to disable.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Resurrection and Revelation}}}} However, Vanus Galerion was abducted by the [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]] Mezamma disguised as trusted ally Skordo the Knife, who teleported him to the remote island of [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]]. Vanus's staff was left behind, which Gabrielle Benele retrieved to put it under safekeeping. With the two organizations now without their guildmasters, the guilds held a summit on Stirk to negotiate with the ambassadors of the Three Alliances for allies. They proposed the creation of Stirk Fellowship for the purpose of combating this new threat when Vanus projected himself out of his staff on Stirk and informed them of the danger the Soul Reapers posed.{{ref|[[ON:A Guild in Crisis|A Guild in Crisis]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Justice for the Fallen|Justice for the Fallen]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

With Gabrielle Benele and [[Lore:Azah|Prince Azah]] assuming the roles of acting guildmasters for the Mages Guild and Fighters Guild respectively, they sought to fully convince the Three Alliances to join their fight against the Worm Cult, which was a successful endeavor.{{Ref|name=MTCA|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Prince Azah of Sentinel}}}}{{Ref|name=MTCWB|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Wormblood}}}}{{Ref|name=MTCWA|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Walks-In-Ash}}}} Sometime after the Vestige discovered that the remains of Mannimarco had been transported to Solstice, Vanus Galerion's projection manifested to advise Gabrielle to travel to Li-Xal Pass where the [[Lore:Writhing Wall|Writhing Wall]] was thin so he can clearly reveal the Worm Cults plans where he would not interrupted by magical audio jamming.{{ref|name=ESOTPB|[[ON:The Passages Beneath|The Passages Beneath]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} There, Vanus revealed that the Worm Cult was gathering an army of necromancers and daedra on the eastern side of Solstice, and attempting to do a reverse Planemeld. Vanus also informed the two of the dangers posed by an artifact known as the Gift of Death, and urged them to swipe it from Vosgah Shrine before the Worm Cult could use it for some dark purpose. While the Vestige claimed the Gift of Death with Razum-dar, Gabrielle went to Sunport to warn the Stirk Fellowship of recent developments.{{ref|name=ESOTWW|[[Online:The Writhing Wall|The Writhing Wall]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

At Vosgah Shrine, the Vestige and Razum-dar secured the Gift of Death, but they also learned that their attempts to slay Wormblood would be futile, as he would just revive himself every time he was killed. The heroes soon discovered that the artifact had the ability to resurrect someone in exchange for a willing sacrifice. With its importance as leverage, Gabrielle concocted a plan to have the Vestige lure Wormblood to Broken Light Temple with a trade offer of the Gift of Life in exchange for Vanus Galerion. Gabrielle would then use the Meridian relics to trap Wormblood in the Colored Rooms, but this would also cause everyone to be teleported within. When Wormblood agreed to the trade, the Stirk Fellowship ambushed the Worm Cultists and Gabrielle's plan was enacted. Everyone was scattered in the Daedric Realm, but the traitor Farinor took advantage of the confusion and disguised herself as Vanus Galerion. She called out to Gabrielle and stabbed her with a poisoned dagger as she had come to rescue Vanus. Realizing she was about to die, Gabrielle chose to use the Gift of Death to sacrifice herself so Darien Gautier could be revived and finish the fight.{{ref|name=ESOTGD|[[Online:The Gift of Death|The Gift of Death]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Gift of Death was now purified into a Light of Meridia and was utilized to make quick work of the Worm Cult's devices, while Darien honored Gabrielle's wishes and charged into the Worm Cultists. Farinor tried to stop the Stirk Fellowship's advances but was slain. Ultimately, Mannimarco realized his Cult's plans on resurrecting him had fallen apart and so fell back on a contingency plan. He possessed Wormblood's body, stealing it, and escaped from the Colored Rooms and back into Nirn. Gabrielle's sacrifice was appreciated, but with Mannimarco back and the bulk of the Stirk Fellowship's forces arriving by ships soon, their next focus was pushing past the Writhing Wall and into eastern Solstice where the Worm Cult were gathering their army for a final confrontation.{{ref|name=ESOTGD}}

===Fate===

''And their power to me, and you shall live as befits the dead."''<br>
''The hollow laugh of Mannimarco answered: "You die first."''<br>
''The mage army then clashed with the obscene and unholy force.''<br>—[[Lore:Mannimarco, King of Worms|''Mannimarco, King of Worms'']] by [[Lore:Books by Author#Horicles|Horicles]]}}

Sometime after the known adventures of the Vestige, Mannimarco would escape from his imprisonment and return to Tamriel and was active as early as {{Year|2E 864}}.{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Lawrence Schick's Posts|Lawrence Schick's Posts]] discussing why Vanus and Mannimarco never interact during [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Gasta|[[RG:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]]'s dialogue in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} At some point during the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]],{{intnote|Battle|[nb 3]}} Galerion headed north with an army of Mages and Lamp Knights to a cursed mountain path to settle his business with Mannimarco. Galerion and his army were met by an equally powerful army of necromancers on the mountain, and both sides were ready to do battle.{{ref|name=MKOW}} As the two armies clashed, fire, frost and lightning tore through the battlefield. Necromancers summoned waves of undead, only to have them shattered by the battlemages’ searing streams of light. It was said that with a single touch, Galerion "shook the mountain to its base", and the undead horde crumbled. From the dying cries of his minions, the King of Worms, Mannimarco, emerged. The Worm King exhaled a miasma of darkness that chilled all who stood upon the mountain, yet even this power could not last. His artifacts were torn from him, and the battle ended. Though Galerion fell, Mannimarco endured, ensuring necromancy would survive. Mannimarco's followers either scattered or were slain, and the triumphant army secured both Galerion’s legacy and that of the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=MKOW}} Following the battle, Galerion may have been raised from the dead to serve as one of Mannimarco's Worm Thralls for some time.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Galerion's visage was later captured in a morbid one hundred foot tall statue inside [[Lore:Scourg Barrow|Scourg Barrow]], constructed of decayed bodies and bones.{{ref|name=TPWaroftheWormgod|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 13: War of the Wormgod|''War of the Wormgod'']]}}

==Contributions to Magical Research==
Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, itemmakers, and spellmakers were originally codified by Vanus Galerion. Galerion is said to have restructured the schools of magic so they'd be understandable by the masses and to have invented the tools of alchemy and enchanting so everyone could concoct whatever they wanted without fears of magical backfire. It is said that the original tools of enchanting and alchemy that Galerion crafted were more automated than modern ones, robotic master enchanters and alchemists that could create anything the customer asked for as long as they paid for it and could be used for that purpose by any layman, even without any understanding of magic. Even an enchanted sword that could "cleave the world in twain" is said to have been theoretically possible to make through these original tools, obstructed only by the enormous cost in magicka and gold. This level of accessibility is said to have resulted in a number of incidents where laymen created dangerous arcane objects beyond their ken. Galerion is said to have eventually realized the danger his original enchanting and alchemy tools posed, and to have had them destroyed and replaced by less automated versions which required users to have some understanding of what they were doing to use them. Because of these contributions to making the study of magic more accessible and free, Galerion is described in some sources as the originator of the practice of magic in its modern form.{{ref|name=Feyfolken|{{Cite Book|Feyfolken}}}} 

By some accounts Galerion was responsible for codifying the division between "Black", or illegal to use, and "White", or legal to use, souls during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. In an effort to combat the spread of [[Lore:Soul Trap|Soul Trapping]] and [[Lore:Soul Gem|Soul Gems]], which had become ubiquitous across every market and bazaar, Galerion co-opted the practice by restricting the Soul Trapping spells that would be taught only to those that'd imprison souls the guild approved of, thus codifying the division between black and white souls.{{Ref|name=GMoST}} Some sources also credit Galerion with codifying the schools of magic,{{ref|name=TBAOT}} though others credit [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]], or the [[Lore:Shad Astula|Shad Astula]] school of magic, with their creation instead.{{ref|name=AMM|{{Cite Book|A Minor Maze}}}}{{ref|name=PSoM|{{Cite Book|Proposal: Schools of Magic}}}}

Galerion is also known to have written works on the fundaments of the discipline of traffic and commerce with [[Lore:Daedra Lords|Daedra Lords]], with his texts being cited by other scholars as an important resource for students interested in learning more about the esoteric but well established practice.{{ref|name=LB}} 

By his own account Galerion had a preference for shock magic over other elemental applications of the school of Destruction, and considered shock the element which a true scholar would give pride of place to in his grimoire, owing to its greater ease of use and more varied applications. He wrote a text listing some of the additional uses of shock magic, such as shrouding the caster in a lightning cloak, placing explosive runes upon surfaces, or "riding the lightning" to appear instantly where the bolt landed, in an attempt to promote the use of the element.{{ref|name=OTUoSM|{{Cite Book|On the Utility of Shock Magic}}}}

The first nine Scribing Scripts circulated among the Mages Guild are said to have been hand-copied and examined by Galerion, resulting in reference materials that were, in some cases, even better than the originals. This claim would be contested by Lamp Knight and Mages Guild Chronicler Firandil, however, who claimed it was he who gathered the text analyzed and created the copies, and intended to lodge a complaint over the misattribution of the achievement to Galerion.{{ref|name=Largesse|[[Online:The Largesse of the Archmagister|The Largesse of the Archmagister]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
