# Lore:An-Xileel

The [[Lore:An-Xileel|An-Xileel]] are a political party in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] formed sometime during the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], consisting primarily, if not entirely, of [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]]. They supported Black Marsh's independence from the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] and were said to spread anti-[[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] propaganda, capturing prisoners of war. Many Argonians firmly held the belief that the An-Xileel were the sole reason that [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] failed to conquer Black Marsh during the Oblivion Crisis.{{ref|name=TIC|Keyes, Greg. ''[[Books:The Infernal City|The Infernal City]]''. Del Rey Books, 2009.}}{{rp|19}}

According to the Imperial perspective, the An-Xileel "were entirely nativistic in their views, interested only in purging the former colonial influences" and returning Black Marsh to the way it was prior to [[Lore:Duskfall|Duskfall]], or at least how they imagined it had been in before it was ruled by foreign powers.{{Ref|name=LoS|Keyes, Greg. ''[[Books:Lord of Souls|Lord of Souls]]''. Del Rey Books, 2011.}} They refer to Argonians who have been living under Imperial ways as "Lukiul", or "Assimilated".{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|34}} The few non-Argonians that work for them are poorly paid advisors.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|18}} Many of those who have dealt with the An-Xileel view them as being uniformly rude and arrogant.{{Ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|65}}

The exact structure of the An-Xileel is unknown, but they were known to have had an Archwarden by the name of {{Lore Link|Qajalil}} in {{Year|4E 48}},{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|30}} and were led by '''The Organism''', the ruling council based in Lilmoth.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|37}}<noinclude>

==History==
When the gates from the Deadlands opened in Black Marsh during the Oblivion Crisis in {{Year|3E 433}}, the Argonians supposedly poured into the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] "with such fury and might, Dagon's Lieutenants had to close them."{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|19}} Invaders from the An-Xileel participated in the [[Lore:Accession War|Accession War]] around {{Year|4E 6}},{{ref|name=AXI|[[Legends:An-Xileel Invader|An-Xileel Invader]] card in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} capturing small parts of southern [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] that were once the "rightful" lands of Argonia. The An-Xileel stopped their advance in Morrowind after being pushed back by [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]],{{ref|name=DBArano|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} and haven't shown the slightest interest in doing anything since then. They have, however, prevented the Empire from reclaiming that territory.{{Ref|name=LoS}}

Cities within Black Marsh have been known to be under the control of the party, such as [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]] in [[Lore:Murkmire|Murkmire]], defeating the Imperial forces and looting the Old Imperial Quarter, including the Hoïnart estate.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|14}} They forbade all but licensed foreigners in the city.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|36}} Only the An-Xileel and wild Argonians have complete access to the Lilmoth city-tree,{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|45}} and the An-Xileel talk only to Lilmoth's city tree.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|31}} The An-Xileel have been observed to sing to the tree in a long, awful cacophonous chant.{{Ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|65}} Political enemies of the An-Xileel hid out in the {{Lore Link|Pussbottom}} district of Lilmoth.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|14}} Only the topmost chamber of [[Lore:Ixtaxh Xanmeer|Ixtaxh]]-thtithil-meht [[Lore:Xanmeer|Xanmeer]] jutted above the silt west of Lilmoth, as the An-Xileel had excavated it, room by room, pumping it out and laying magicks to keep the water from returning.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|35}}

In {{Year|4E 47}}, Prime Minister {{Lore Link|Hierem}} made a secret trip to Black Marsh with Delia Huerc, ostensibly to negotiate with the An-Xileel leaders. Huerc was under the impression that [[Lore:Titus Mede|the Emperor]] was aware of her and the Minister's trip to Black Marsh, and that the "secrecy and misdirection were to avoid any of the Emperor's enemies learning what he was about." Though she did not witness the meeting with the An-Xileel, she worked out that some agreement had been reached, supposedly that Hierem was there to propose an alliance against the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]], but he was vague about what the negotiations actually entailed. In order to seal the agreement, Hierem had to perform some sort of ritual at the city tree; it involved magic and the word "[[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]]".{{Ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|65}}

In {{Year|4E 48}}, the An-Xileel were able to summon the floating city of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]] from [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] after the pocket realm had detached itself from the [[Lore:Fields of Regret|Fields of Regret]]. Using the rogue Hist tree of Lilmoth to contact its "cousins" aboard the city, the An-Xileel plotted to exterminate all foreign taint and "assimilated" Argonians in Black Marsh. The An-Xileel and the Wild Ones planned to hide themselves away, waiting out the carnage, only to return once Black Marsh was "clean".{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|62}} The city did this for them, but soon began to pursue its own agenda after the An-Xileel lost control over it. Umbriel started on a northwestern path towards [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] in the Imperial City, moving through Black Marsh and Morrowind in the process; those slain by the floating city were resurrected as an invincible horde of undead "[[Lore:Tasker|taskers]]" to aid the city's dark goal. It was through the efforts of the Breton girl Annaïg Hoïnart, an Argonian named Mere-Glim, Prince [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus Mede]], and the Dunmer mage Sul (along with a few others) that Umbriel was finally destroyed and its threat to Tamriel eliminated.{{ref|name=LoS}}

The status of the An-Xileel following the Umbriel Crisis is unknown. In {{Year|4E 150}}, a small force of Argonian raiders landed on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] to stir up trouble but were repelled by the [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]] of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]].{{ref|name=HoRRV1|{{Cite Book|History of Raven Rock}}}}  A [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] member also complained about Argonian patrols in southern Morrowind, implying the Argonians have indeed begun some form of governance of those conquered areas.{{ref|name=Delvin|[[Skyrim:Delvin Mallory|Delvin Mallory]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Conversely, other areas that were sacked, such as city of Mournhold, have since been returned to Dunmeri control by 4E 201.{{ref|name=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}}
