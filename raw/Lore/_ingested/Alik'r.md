The [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]] (best known as the '''Alik'r Desert''') is a large region that takes a significant portion of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell Province]]. It is a vast sea of sand and dunes, stretching across the inner province, surrounded by the mountains in eastern Hammerfell, and the grassland along the coastline.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}} Borders between Hammerfell's nations in the Alik'r Desert are hazy. Travelers in the desert can never tell where they start or end.{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Alik'r}}}} When Alik'r Desert was an independent state of Hammerfell Province, its capital city was [[Lore:Alik'ra|Alik'ra]].{{Ref|name=DFAlik'r}}<noinclude>

The Alik'r Desert encompasses much of west-central Hammerfell and reaches as far north as [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]],{{Ref|name=STJOA|{{Cite Book|Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r}}}} as far south as the [[Lore:Gilane (kingdom)|Gilane]] region,{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Hammerfell}}}} [[Lore:Sunkeep|Sunkeep]] and the [[Lore:Irk River|River Irk]],{{Ref|name=RGMap|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel – The Elder Scrolls Adventurers: Redguard]]}}{{Ref|name=LOMap|[[:File:LO-map-Hammerfell (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Hammerfell – Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}} and as far east as [[Lore:Skaven|Skaven]]'s outskirts.{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}} [[Lore:Lainlyn|Lainlyn]] and [[Lore:Satakalaam|Satakalaam]] were considered part of the Alik'r during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]],{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r|[[Online:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}{{intnote|Location|[Note 1]}} but are reported as being part of a jungle biome as of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{Ref|name=DFClimateMap}} Geographically, it is bordered by [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]] and the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]] to the east,{{Ref|name=DFMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of the Iliac Bay (labeled) – The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall]]}}{{Ref|name=ONMap|[[:File:ON-map-Tamriel 02.jpg|Map of Tamriel – The Elder Scrolls Online]]}} [[Lore:Cape Shira|Cape Shira]] to the west,{{Ref|name=STJOA}} [[Lore:Abibon-Gora|Abibon-Gora]] and the forests of [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] to the southwest,{{Ref|name=DFMap}}{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} [[Lore:Dak'fron|Dak'fron]] to the southeast,{{Ref|name=DFMap}} and [[Lore:Khefrem|Khefrem]] to the south. The northern Alik'r Desert converges with the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region found between Hammerfell and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{Ref|name=DFMap}}{{TOCleft}}

==Society and Geography==
===Demographics===
[[File:SR-npc-Alik'r Warrior 05.jpg|thumb|right|Alik'r Warriors]]
[[File:DF-place-Sentinel.png|thumb|left|Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r]]
The Alik'r Desert is populated by the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] who call Hammerfell their home. Across the province, society is split between those who live in the cosmopolitan, coastal cities like Sentinel and Lainlyn and those who live as nomads and mercenaries across the desert,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} generally known as the Alik'r.{{Ref|name=SRSaadia}} The settled Redguards on the coast adopted traditions and customs from the nearby [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] and [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]], but with their own style inspired by Old [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

Three factions make up this population. The [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]], who live mostly in northern Hammerfell, have stuck to their Yokudan roots and ancient Na-Totambu noble traditions.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=TNTOY|{{Cite Book|The Na-Totambu of Yokuda}}}} The cities of [[Lore:Bergama|Bergama]] and Lainlyn are some of their strongholds.{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}}{{Ref|name=AUADC1|{{Cite Book|Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)|ns_base=GEN}}}} The [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]] are descendants of the Ra Gada and are more assimilated into Tamrielic culture.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The Kingdom of [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]] and its [[Lore:Sentinel|capital]] are the Forebears' largest city and their ancient hold. A third new faction, the [[Lore:Lhotunics|Lhotunics]] was created in the late Third Era by the King of Sentinel, [[Lore:Lhotun|Lhotun]]. Though it strives to bridge the two main factions together, it brought nothing but contempt.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

The Alik'r nomads are divided into numerous tribes, and their influences can be either traced to the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]] or the Yokudans.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The most well-known tribe is the [[Lore:Ash'abah|Ash'abah]], who are largely rejected for their treatment of the dead.{{Ref|name=SAMITA|{{Cite Book|Sacrilege and Mayhem in the Alik'r}}}} They are an ancient, off-shoot tribe of Yokudans from [[Lore:Yath|Yath]].{{Ref|name=ONInitiation|[[Online:The Initiation|The Initiation]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Exiled Sun-Eaters|Exiled Sun-Eaters]] of Numaneh are an obscure tribe whose tale is forbidden.{{Ref|name=TUAAYQ|{{Cite Book|The Unveiled Azadiyeh Answers Your Questions}}}} Although not inherently nomadic, another subculture in the region are the [[Lore:Barbarians|barbarians]],{{Ref|name=DFDungeons|[[Daggerfall:Alik'r/Dungeons|Alik'r dungeons]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} people who live in the wilderness and are dependent on wildlife.{{Ref|name=MWGenBarbarian|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue M#My trade|Barbarian]] description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The disparate races of [[Lore:Mer|mer]] have lived in the Alik'r Desert. Perhaps the first group to appear was the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], who built the city of [[Lore:Salas En|Salas En]] sometime in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] using their Nedic slaves.{{Ref|name=TSEE|{{Cite Book|The Salas En Expedition}}}}{{Ref|name=ONClarisse|[[Online:Lady Clarisse Laurent|Lady Clarisse Laurent]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Chronologically, the next group to come was the Rourken Clan of [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], who left their holdings in the east in {{Year|1E 420}}. The many [[Lore:Dwemer Ruins|Dwemer ruins]] that dot the region were originally their settlements. Their capital was [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]], where the [[Lore:Rourken (person)|king]]'s mighty hammer fell from the sky and landed in Alik'r.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Some of their other holdings included [[Lore:Santaki|Santaki]] and Rkulftzel.{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r}} Many of these sites are largely untouched{{Ref|name=ONLoadSantaki|[[Online:Santaki|Santaki]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} while others have been plundered by [[Lore:Pirates|pirates]].{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=EGT}} 

The [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] occupied mainly the Alik'r Desert's coast for an unspecified time. An Altmer [[Lore:Kinhold|kinhold]] based in Salas En was founded by the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya Clan]] in {{Year|1E 522}}, who also owned several hidden ports along Cape Shira and Tamriel's western seaboard. They were eventually driven out by the Ra Gada in the ninth century of the First Era.{{Ref|name=HOCC1|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 1}}}}{{Ref|name=HOCC2|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 2}}}} The [[Lore:Direnni Hegemony|Direnni Hegemony]] dominated the Iliac Bay in the early First Era and claimed portions of the Alik'r Desert. Before it was known as Sentinel, it was an elven port and home to several elves and their [[Lore:Proto-Breton|proto-Breton]] subjects.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=STJOA}}{{Ref|name=PGE3HR|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}} According to the ''[[Lore:Epode of the Ansei Wards|Epode of the Ansei Wards]]'', elven necromancers lived in towers in the desert interior.

Other early races to inhabit the region were several [[Lore:Goblin-ken|Goblin-ken]], such as [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] and [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]] in the interior wastes.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} The [[Lore:Desert Orcs|Desert Orcs]] were a subculture made of many tribes and clans. The Ra Gada drove them out of Hammerfell and those that survived helped establish [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] in the early First Era.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|Thulgeg's March}}}} By the late Third Era, Orcish strongholds were active across the coastal regions and unconquered interior.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}} The Goblins were one of the Ra Gada's greatest enemies. In the Second Era, the Sandgrubber Tribe claimed territory in the Alik'r,{{Ref|name=ONSandgrubber|[[Online:Sandgrubber Tribe|Sandgrubber Tribe]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} but in the ancient past, the region was dominated by the [[Lore:Flint-Tooth Tribe|Flint-Tooth Tribe]] of [[Lore:Giant Goblin|giant goblins]],{{Ref|name=ONFarvad|[[Online:Throne Keeper Farvad|Throne Keeper Farvad]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} who originally came from [[Lore:Goblin Gate|parts unknown]].{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview/REDGUARD.TXT#The Song of Divad|''The Song of Divad'']] — [[Lore:Destri Melarg|Destri Melarg]]}}

Other minor races include the [[Lore:Giant|Giants]], who live in strongholds,{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}} and the [[Lore:Lamia|Lamia]], who mostly live on the coast. Alik'ri viziers in the Interregnum eventually discovered an untamed desert subspecies that garnered a worse reputation than their coastal brethren.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=KCE|{{Cite Book|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|ns_base=BK}}}} [[Lore:Ogre|Ogres]] sometime live with the goblin tribes{{Ref|name=ONSandgrubber}} and lend their name to the Ogre's Bluff oasis.{{Ref|name=ONBluff|[[Online:Ogre's Bluff|Ogre's Bluff]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} [[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]] live in the hills known as Shrikes' Aerie, near Kozanset.{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r}} They are even associated with Lainlyn's ancient tradition, Riglametha.{{Ref|name=Ghraewaj|{{Cite Book|Ghraewaj}}}}

===Culture and Industry===
====Society in the Settled World====
[[File:ON-place-Tu'whacca's Throne.jpg|thumb|right|Tu'whacca's Throne]]
[[File:ON-npc-Merric at-Aswala.jpg|thumb|left|Merric at-Aswala, a Redguard gallant]]
Though nations and their borders are depicted on maps, the shifting sands make it hard to discern where one region starts and where the Alik'r ends. Because of this, people believe they are one with the desert. They share this philosophy with the desert nomads of the interior.{{Ref|name=TA}} The province's cities lie on the edge of the desert,{{Ref|name=SRLoadRedguard|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Races|Races]] loading screen text in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} while outlying territories and contested Alik'r border states exist far from the coast.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=TOOC}}{{rp|25}}

Many holidays and festivals in Hammerfell have existed since long before the main Ra Gada force arrived in {{Year|1E 808}}, with many being direct translations from older celebrations back on Yokuda.{{Ref|name=HOTIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}} Many of these holidays are observed across the region but some are only celebrated in certain regions and kingdoms. These include [[Lore:Koomu Alezer'i|Koomu Alezer'i Yaghoub]] (Sentinel),{{Ref|name=STJOA}}{{Ref|name=HOTIB}}{{Ref|name=DFKoomu|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Koomu Alezer'i|Koomu Alezer'i]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Riglametha|Riglametha]] ([[Lore:Lainlyn (barony)|Lainlyn]]),{{Ref|name=EGTHR|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}{{Ref|name=Ghraewaj|{{Cite Book|Ghraewaj}}}} and [[Lore:Serpent's Dance|Serpent's Dance]] ([[Lore:Satakalaam (county)|Satakalaam]]).{{Ref|name=HOTIB}}{{Ref|name=DFSerpent|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Serpent's Dance|Serpent's Dance]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}  

In the Alik'r Desert at large, the Redguards observe several holidays. Two of these holidays venerate [[Lore:Frandar Hunding|Frandar]] and [[Lore:Divad Hunding|Divad Hunding]], history's most accomplished sword-singers and folk heroes to the Redguards.{{Ref|name=RTHATH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, Their History and Their Heroes}}}} During [[Lore:Dirij Tereur|Dirij Tereur]] on the 5th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]], people gather in temples to read through the [[Lore:Book of Circles|Book of Circles]].{{Ref|name=DFDirij|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Dirij Tereur|Dirij Tereur]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] on the 26th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]] celebrates the Hundings' victory against [[Lore:Malooc|Malooc]]'s Horde of giant goblins. This holiday is very popular in the region, despite many scholars considering the story a myth.{{Ref|name=DFBlades|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=T26OFSIU|{{Cite Book|The 26th of First Seed is Upon Us!|ns_base=ON}}}}{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}}  

[[Lore:Baranth Do|Baranth Do]], on the 18th of [[Lore:Evening Star|Evening Star]] is the people's farewell to the past year. Pageant-goers wear demonic representations of the year and revelers honor the new one everywhere.{{Ref|name=DFBaranth|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Baranth Do|Baranth Do]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The 29th of [[Lore:Sun's Height|Sun's Height]] is [[Lore:Fiery Night|Fiery Night]], celebrating the hottest day of the year.{{Ref|name=DFFiery|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Fiery Night|Fiery Night]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Ovank'a|Ovank'a]], on the 12th of [[Lore:Morning Star|Morning Star]] is a holy day dedicated to the [[Lore:Divines|Divine]], [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], the God of Justice and Mercy. People pray for a mild and merciful year.{{Ref|name=DFOvank'a|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Ovank'a|Ovank'a]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Although Stendarr is only recognized by the Forebears,{{Ref|name=VOFTF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}} when the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] took over Hammerfell, various regions established temples in their settlements to a patron Divine. In the Alik'r Desert, it was Stendarr who had many [[Lore:Temple of Stendarr|temples]] dedicated to him.{{Ref|name=DFStendarr|[[Daggerfall:Temple of Stendarr|Temple of Stendarr]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Divine is also the favored god of gallants,{{Ref|name=VOFTF}} who are prevalent in the Alik'r Desert.{{Ref|name=LOTYC|{{Cite Book|Legend of the Yokudan Chargers}}}}  

[[File:ON-place-Motalion Necropolis.jpg|thumb|right|The Motalion Necropolis]]
The [[Lore:Gallants of Hammerfell|Gallants of Hammerfell]], also called the Knights of the Desert, are expert sword-wielders and [[Lore:Horse|horse]] riders. Their ideal steed of choice is the [[Lore:Yokudan Charger|Yokudan Charger]], who are specially bred and raised in [[Lore:Aswala Stables|Aswala Stables]],{{Ref|name=LOTYC}} a settlement outside of Lainlyn that is run by the eponymous family of Crowns.{{Ref|name=LHALAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Honnorah af-Lahreq Answers Your Questions}}}} The Gallants are an aspect of Redguard tradition that harkens back to Yokuda, where it predisposed the people to the culture surrounding knightly orders.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} [[Lore:Knights|Knights]] are present in some form across all Redguardic mythos, like the [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]] who fought with Sentinel and [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] in the [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]],{{Ref|name=PGE1TWR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Wild Region}}}} and the Black Knight known as [[Lore:Reymon Ebonarm|Reymon Ebonarm]].{{Ref|name=TEA|{{Cite Book|The Ebon Arm}}}}  

In northern Hammerfell, particularly the Alik'r Desert, the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] are allies with Ebonarm's temple, the [[Lore:Citadel of Ebonarm|Citadel of Ebonarm]]. The guild's chapters are also considered temples to the God of War, to which they are alternatively known as Fighter Trainers. The Citadel of Ebonarm are enemies to the Temple of Stendarr.{{Ref|name=DFTrainers|[[Daggerfall:Fighters Guild#Fighters Trainers Guildhalls|Fighters Trainers]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFEbonarm|[[Daggerfall:Citadel of Ebonarm|Citadel of Ebonarm]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The necropolises of the Alik'r Desert are large, sacred sites that resonate with spiritual energy. [[Lore:Tu'whacca's Throne|Tu'whacca's Throne]], which sits at the top of a plateau over northwest Alik'r, is one such temple. Not only is the eponymous [[Lore:Tu'whacca|deity]]'s presence felt here, but the temple also serves a monument to the many Yokudans that perished in the destruction of their home.{{Ref|name=RFTD|{{Cite Book|Reverence for the Dead}}}} The Motalion Necropolis near Satakalaam is the largest in the region but is also considered a dark place.{{Ref|name=ONThoda|[[Online:General Thoda|General Thoda]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

[[Lore:Leki|Leki]], also known as the Saint of the Spirit Sword, is the goddess of aberrant swordsmanship. She is at the center of a legend with a prolific warrior who boasted his powers named Rada al-Saran. As the story goes, Leki drew him to an oasis in the Hollow Wastes to challenge and humble him. He found her as a warrior of sand, wielding a blade made out of the hardest rock. Their battle lasted for three days and nights until, in one final strike, Rada broke Leki's blade, creating a great sandstorm that split the horizon. When it cleared, Leki built a shrine from the sand and it became the town of Leki's Blade.{{Ref|name=ONRahama|[[Online:Rahama|Rahama]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Another tradition, celebrated during the [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]], involves a [[Lore:Storihbeg|great wolf]] who shaped Tamriel with his ceaseless hunger. One example was that he drank the Iliac Bay dry enough to create the Alik'r Desert.{{Ref|name=FOLTNL|{{Cite Book|From Old Life To New}}}}

Many dishes come from the Alik'r Desert. [[Lore:Pomegranate|Pomegranates]] are a distinct part of the cuisine. Not only are they served during the Koomu Alezer'i Yaghoub,{{Ref|name=STJOA}} but they are also made into the region's most popular wine,{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresP|[[Online:Treasures P#Pomegranate Distiller|Pomegranate Distiller]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} or used as a secondary ingredient, like in seared venomsteaks.{{Ref|name=RFDD|{{Cite Book|Recipe from Desert Delicacies}}}} The process of creating [[Lore:Cheese#Shrieking Cheese|shrieking cheese]] is a tightly kept secret by the Redguards, but it is a dish that locals love to serve foreigners. At a certain melting point, the cheese starts to shriek. It is sometimes added to common spicy meat dishes while cooking.{{Ref|name=ONShrieking|[[Online:Alik'r Shrieking Cheese|Alik'r Shrieking Cheese]] emote description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}{{Ref|name=COT|{{Cite Book|Cheeses of Tamriel}}}} Another popular native dish is Beets with Goat Cheese.{{Ref|name=ONBeets|[[Online:Alik'r Beets with Goat Cheese|Alik'r Beets with Goat Cheese]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

The Alik'r people in coastal cities like Lainlyn and Sentinel developed a maritime industry. The famous shipyards at Lainlyn produced critical ships, like the small Alik'r caravel, for clients like the royal navy of the King of Sentinel.{{Ref|name=SAF|{{Cite Book|Strakes and Futtocks}}}}{{Ref|name=ONMeriq|[[Online:Meriq|Meriq]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The [[Lore:Impervious Vault|Impervious Vault]] in Sentinel, built in the First Era by [[Lore:Yaghoub|Grandee Yaghoub]], contained the Alik'r Desert's greatest treasures,{{Ref|name=ONLoadVault|[[Online:Impervious Vault|Impervious Vault]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} including the Ansei Wards.{{Ref|name=ONVault|[[Online:The Impervious Vault|The Impervious Vault]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

====Society in the Desert====
[[File:ON-concept-Desert water catcher.png|thumb|left|Redguard Rain-Catchers]]
[[File:ON-furnishing-Coil of Satakal.jpg|thumb|right|The Coil of Satakal]]
As stated above, there are several nomadic tribes in the Alik'r.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} While people more accustomed to the cities avoid the wastelands, those that survive in the desert develop hardiness and resourcefulness to live with the bare minimum,{{Ref|name=WAS|{{Cite Book|Wind and Sand}}}} developing better ways to thrive in the harsh environment over the years. Travelers commonly wear special clothing to protect their mouths from the sand and wear typical Redguard [[Lore:Medium Armor|leather armor]], which offers lightweight protection and room to breathe.{{Ref|name=ASFO|{{Cite Book|Alik'r Survival for Outsiders}}}}

The writer, [[Lore:Enric Milres|Enric Milres]] contemplated the Redguard's nature and their strong character to describe their relationship with the desert. As he puts it in his book, ''[[Lore:The Alik'r|The Alik'r]]'', ''"They are warriors by nature. As a group, there are none better. Nothing for them has worth unless they have struggled for it. No one fought them for the desert, but the Alik'r is a great foe. The battle goes on. It is a war without rancor, a holy war in the sense the phrase should always imply."''{{Ref|name=TA}}

Many ancient ruins can be found buried in the sand. A single gust of wind could easily uncover and bury it again at a moment's notice. Some of these ruins, like the Alik'r Shade-Temples, provide coverage from the [[Lore:Sun|sun]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=TA}} These ruins were built by Redguard ancestors for their descendants and leave inscriptions to inspire travelers.{{Ref|name=IFIROAASFT|{{Cite Book|Inscription found in ruins of an ancient shelter for travelers}}}} The sands, and even the winds themselves bear [[Lore:Magic|magicka]] and help Alik'r wizards develop a unique magical style. It is believed that the ever-shifting sands, endlessly intermingling in infinite combinations, contain remnants of [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]' gift to the mortal realm, and that this exposure and combinatorial explosion creates a spectrum of diverse magical energy that cannot be found anywhere else.{{Ref|name=WAS}} The [[Lore:Anka-Ra|Anka-Ra]] of Craglorn draw from the distant Alik'ri sands to create their warrior mounts.{{Ref|name=ONAnka|[[Online:Anka-Ra Sandcamel|Anka-Ra Sandcamel]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

The wind is viewed as the mechanism that lets the vast array of magic in the sands carry itself out and guide it into novelty and expression. The writer Afa-Saryat in his treatise, ''[[Lore:Wind and Sand|Wind and Sand]]'', compared this line of thinking to the Nordic tradition of [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], whose winds are seen as a "celestial mourning" for the death of her lover, [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], and a gift for mortals to benefit from. He goes on to assert that "reading the knowledge of the sands" is in itself a difficult task, one which would call for an army of clerks to achieve.{{Ref|name=WAS}} Locals use a sleeping [[Lore:Boar#Goldspine Boar|goldspine boar]]'s twitches to predict incoming sandstorms.{{Ref|name=ONGoldspine|[[Online:Goldspine Boar|Goldspine Boar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} By the morning, a vast, mysterious red mist envelops the desert. The poet [[Lore:Weltan|Weltan]] described this as a "sacred flame" that "rises above the fire".{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=OTIOD|{{Cite Book|On the Immortality of Dust}}}} The Yokudan [[Lore:Tanbur|tanbur]] called Long Fire is named for the quiet nights in the deep desert when a bard sings to his travel companions under the night sky.{{Ref|name=LF|{{Cite Book|Long Fire}}}}

The ancient Yokudan god, [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]] is a popular deity among the Alik'ri,{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and particularly venerated by Crowns.{{Ref|name=VOFCR|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}} Worshippers among them are known as the [[Lore:Satakal (faction)|Satakals]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} It is a commonly held belief that everyone dwells upon the serpent, and maps of the land would depict a giant serpent as a symbolic representation.{{Ref|name=ONMapAnti|[[Online:Alik'r Desert Antiquities#Antique Map of Alik'r Desert|Antique Map of Alik'r Desert]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The Satakals are respected by the people, who in turn, provide for them through charity. They are said to wander the desert in a sidewinder fashion for hundreds of miles and band together like the ancient Ra Gada to terrorize the countryside. When the Third Empire established its presence in Hammerfell, they fought with the Satakals and had to ban them from their cities.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The modern aversion to striking down the undead stems from the Alik'r Desert, rather than an ancient Yokudan tradition. The slow decay of corpses in the desert fostered a belief that an ancestor's corpse is sacred.{{Ref|name=ONFahara'jad}}

[[File:ON-mount-Tattooed Shorn Camel.jpg|thumb|right|A Tattooed Shorn Camel]]
Wayward spirits roam the Deep Desert and can lead camels astray as they cross the region. A tradition of Shorn Camels was developed in the Alik'r region. Floral designs of blooming flowers are popular and well-beloved.{{Ref|name=ONFloralShorn|[[Online:Floral Tattoo Shorn Camel|Floral Tattoo Shorn Camel]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The [[Lore:Dervishes|Dervishes]] of [[Lore:Rihad|Rihad]] shave their camels and tattoo mystical symbols to placate them.{{Ref|name=ONTattooed|[[Online:Tattooed Shorn Camel|Tattooed Shorn Camel]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Even in the harsh and dry environment, the Redguards have found ways to cultivate and sustain themselves with fresh water. The people use inverted tent-like structures called Rain Catchers{{Ref|name=EGT}} to take moisture from the air and funnel it into rainwater cisterns called johads.{{Ref|name=ONJahi|[[Online:Overseer Jahi|Overseer Jahi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}{{Ref|name=MSITM|{{Cite Book|Moon-Sugar in the March}}}} The water is then carried to the cities in large urns to be sold.{{Ref|name=ONJahi}} When the elves lived predominantly in the region, they used glass divining rods to search for water and traverse the desert.{{Ref|name=ONRodAnti|[[Online:Alik'r Desert Antiquities#Hegathe Divining Rod|Hegathe Divining Rod]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

[[Lore:Amethyst|Amethyst]] geodes can be found across the central Alik'r Desert, with many jewelers and gem enthusiasts claiming the region produces some of the best examples. Some of the finest amethysts come from [[Lore:Kulati Mines|Kulati Mines]], between Tu'whacca's Throne and Leki's Blade, where ancient lava had been spared the layers of sand and rock blanketing the rest of the region.{{Ref|name=AMITA|{{Cite Book|Amethyst Mining in the Alik'r}}}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadKulati|[[Online:Kulati Mines|Kulati Mines]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The Coldrock Diggings mine near Lainlyn is important to the local shipwrights since it provides them with [[Lore:Grit-rock|grit-rock]] and [[Lore:Holystone|holystone]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadColdrock|[[Online:Coldrock Diggings|Coldrock Diggings]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The Sandblown Mine near [[Lore:Kozanset|Kozanset]] produced [[Lore:Copper|copper]] before running dry.{{Ref|name=ONLoadSandblown|[[Online:Sandblown Mine|Sandblown Mine]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}
 
The most dominant [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] bloodline in the Alik'r Desert are the [[Lore:Anthotis|Anthotis]], who possess a great intellect and occupy neighboring regions like Dak'fron and [[Lore:Antiphyllos|Antiphyllos]].{{Ref|name=DFAnthotis|[[Daggerfall:Anthotis|Anthotis]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Witches of the Marsh are a [[Lore:Witches|coven]] in the region.{{Ref|name=DFMarsh|[[Daggerfall:Witch Covens#The Witches of the Marsh|The Witches of the Marsh]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Although [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]] were largely nonexistent from the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]{{Ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} until {{Year|4E 201}},{{Ref|name=SRBalgruuf|[[Skyrim:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} denizens of the Alik'r Desert have long insisted the region is home to many dragons.{{Ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}} In the neighboring Dragontail Mountains, the Redguards observe a holiday called the [[Lore:Day of Waiting|Day of Waiting]], where they stay inside to hide from an supposed dragon from the desert which will come and devour the wicked.{{Ref|name=DFWaiting|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Waiting|Day of Waiting]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

===Ecology===
[[File:ON-concept-Alikr Load screen 01.png|thumb|left|A grand vista of the Alik'r Desert, as illustrated by [[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]]{{Ref|name=EGT}}]]
[[File:ON-furnishing-Alik'r Cactus.jpg|thumb|right|The Alik'r Cactus]]
The Alik'r interior, notorious for its inhospitality, is commonly referred to as '''the Deep Desert'''.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=ABOOH|{{Cite Book|A Betrayal of Our Heritage}}}} The Knight-Gallant Titianus Iulus describes it as a "nightmare land" devoid of nourishment, prowling with horrors, and treacherous. The ancient lava beds underneath the earth have been buried by rock layers and weathered by the sands. Salt pits also dry the land and poison the water,{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=AMITA}} but despite everything, life has managed to find a way here.{{Ref|name=EGT}} The city of Bergama, for example, is a large city and the seat of a barony in the Deep Desert.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=ABOOH}} 

The interior is rich with mineral resources, something that attracted the Rourken Clan to settle in the desert.{{Ref|name=ONLoadSantaki}} Plant life can exist in the Deep Desert, but blooming flowers are rare and prized among the people.{{Ref|name=ONFloralShorn}} [[Lore:Satakal's Blossom|Satakal's Blossom]], an extremely hallucinogenic flower used by the Ash'abah for rituals, is one such example.{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r}} More commonplace in the wilderness are [[Lore:Cactus|cacti]], from the signature Alik'r Cactus to the [[Lore:Torturer's Pillow|Torturer's Pillow]]. [[Lore:Palm Tree|Palm trees]] are equally present in the desert, with a variant like the [[Lore:Cave Palm|Calm Palm]] exclusive to the region. [[Lore:Juniper|Juniper]] trees, and even [[Lore:Nirnroot|nirnroots]] on the water, appear in the Alik'r.{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r}}

Most of the populace lives on the coast, a tropical wet-dry climate{{Ref|name=DFClimateMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay Climate.png|Climate Map of the Iliac Bay – The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall]]}} sometimes described as grasslands hugging the coastline.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The Sentinel region at times consists of fertile fields.{{Ref|name=ONLoadAlik'r|[[Online:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} At other times, it is described as a barren plain with poor soil.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The locals harvest [[Lore:Fig|figs]], [[Lore:Olive|olives]], and pomegranates, staple foods in the Koomu Alezer'i celebration and thanksgiving in honor of the gods.{{Ref|name=STJOA}}{{Ref|name=DFKoomu}} Rainfall can be expected around places like the city of [[Lore:Myrkwasa (city)|Myrkwasa]].{{Ref|name=DFClimateMap}}{{Ref|name=DFMyrkwasa|[[:File:DF-place-Myrkwasa (city).png|Myrkwasa (city)]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The Hammerfell [[Lore:Camel|Camel]] is the common yet reliable beast of burden of the province often seen in the desert.{{Ref|name=ONCamel|[[Online:Hammerfell Camel|Hammerfell Camel]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though they are native to Tamriel, the Ra Gada brought many camels with them, and it was these same Yokudan Camels that the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] modeled for their [[Lore:Psijic Beasts|camel exemplars]].{{Ref|name=ONPsijic|[[Online:Psijic Camel Exemplar|Psijic Camel Exemplar]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Similarly, Alik'r night riders on a moonless [[Lore:Witches Festival|Witches Festival]] may find themselves headless, riding through the desert on shadowy camels and holding [[Lore:Hollowjack|hollowjacks]].{{Ref|name=ONWitches|[[Online:Hollowjack Rider Camel|Hollowjack Rider Camel]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Unlike most parts of Tamriel, horses are not well-suited for the desert landscape. However, Yokudan horses have adapted and found use in the region. The Ra Gada from Akos Kasaz brought [[Lore:Yokudan Warhorse|Yokudan Warhorses]] with them and bred them into the modern [[Lore:Yokudan Charger|Yokudan Charger]], who traverse the desert's terrain with ease.{{Ref|name=LOTYC}}

The most common canine of the desert is the [[Lore:Jackal|jackal]], widely known across Tamriel as the "thieves' dog" for being a clever and resourceful scavenger, but a loyal companion once tamed.{{Ref|name=ONJackalMob|[[Online:Jackal|Jackal]] mob in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONJackalPet|[[Online:Jackal (pet)|Jackal]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Fennec Fox|Fennec Fox]], like the ones near Bergama, is considered sacred to the Ash'abah, who believe they lead lost children back to the tribe.{{Ref|name=ONFennec|[[Online:Bergama Fennec Fox|Bergame Fennec Fox]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The larger [[Lore:Wolf#Speckled Wolf|speckled wolf]] has a gray and white pattern, and rose in popularity as a companion mount after being featured in an [[Lore:Investigator Vale|Investigator Vale]] story.{{Ref|name=ONSpeckled|[[Online:Speckled Wolf|Speckled Wolf]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Skink|Copper-Tongued Skinks]] are small lizards that can eat anything, even poisonous plants and venom sacs.{{Ref|name=ONJee|[[Online:Jee-Lar|Jee-Lar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONArlowe|[[Online:Arlowe Viducia|Arlowe Viducia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:DF-creature-Scorpion.gif|thumb|left|The Giant Scorpion]]
[[File:ON-concept-Duneripper.png|thumb|right|The Duneripper]]
The desert hosts large and intimidating creatures. [[Lore:Giant Snake|Giant Snakes]], specifically Sand Serpents, live in and around the oases of the Alik'r.{{Ref|name=ONSerpent|[[Online:Sand Serpent|Sand Serpent]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  [[Lore:Assassin Beetle|Assassin Beetles]] are large dark bugs that can blend in the shadows and stalk their prey. A legend persists that they were once known as Samara Scarabs and were small pets in Yokuda until the Hunger of [[Lore:Sep|Sep]] corrupted them, turning them into the giant beetles of today.{{Ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Hunger of Sep}}}} [[Lore:Gargoyle|Gargoyles]] are a kind of [[Lore:Stone Golem|stone golem]] that can be found deep in the Alik'r wastes and guarding necropolises, among other places.{{Ref|name=KCE}}{{Ref|name=TMOGS|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Gargoyles—Solved!}}}}{{Ref|name=ONNorianwe|[[Online:Norianwe|Norianwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONGargoyle|[[Online:Gargoyle|Gargoyles]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Duneripper|Duneripper]], a predator which thrives in the western wastes, is an immense creature reaching up to three horses in length, with a tail covered in chitinous scales. When it comes to hunting, they are extremely patient and can lie in wait for days for their target. The first sign of a duneripper is its dorsal crest striking through the sand, and the trailing sand shifting into whirlpools.{{Ref|name=EGT}} Alik'r nomad chieftains claim that dunerippers were much larger when the Ra Gada landed on Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=ONMapAnti}} Another dominant predator is the [[Lore:Giant Scorpion|Giant Scorpion]], whose girth is compared to a large [[Lore:Bull|bull]], and its enormous tail is described as the most feared natural weapon in Hammerfell. Legends have circled around these creatures since before the Redguards arrived, and native Ash'abah shamans value their giant stingers.{{Ref|name=EGT}} Scorpion nests are some of the most abundant types of dungeons found in the northern Alik'r.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}}

Old mausoleums brimming with the dead are found across the Alik'r, particularly around Bergama.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}} This amply supply of corpses attracts those seeking to resurrect them with [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]]. Grave robbers have witnessed hooded and tattered [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]], returned spirits without memory, which were either raised from the dead or summoned by magic.{{Ref|name=EGT}} Undead like [[Lore:Skeleton|skeletons]] and [[Lore:Mummy|mummies]] are also common.{{Ref|name=KCE}} Mummification is a sacred and time-consuming practice in Redguard society,{{Ref|name=EGT}} and improperly interacting with the body post-death is considered sacrilegious. The Yoku term for the undead is [[Lore:Ra-Netu|Ra-Netu]] (roughly translated as ''"Turned Warrior"'' in [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]]), but Ra-Netu specifically refers to resurrected Redguard ancestors, either from the forgotten graveyards of the deep desert or from the sea floor.{{Ref|name=SAMITA}}{{Ref|name=ONRisen}}

Isolated bodies of water, known as oases, can be found near settlements and important landmarks, like the Goat's Head Oasis near Tu'whacca's Throne{{Ref|name=ONGoat|[[Online:Goat's Head Oasis|Goat's Head Oasis]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} and Ogre's Bluff near Bergama and Aldunz.{{Ref|name=ONBluff}} The oasis in Leki's Blade is said to hold the purest water in all the region, providing the foundation for that settlement.{{Ref|name=ONRahama}} The Goat's Head Oasis hosts all sorts of freshwater fish that can only be found in this section of the Alik'r Desert. These include the [[Lore:Banded Killifish|Banded Killifish]], the [[Lore:Lungfish|Lungfish]], the [[Lore:Saw Belly|Saw Belly]], and the rare [[Lore:Desert Pupfish|Desert Pupfish]]. Foul water across the land contains the [[Lore:Cutthroat Eel|Cutthroat Eel]], the [[Lore:Sand Moray|Sand Moray]], and the illusive [[Lore:Midget Salmon|Midget Salmon]] and [[Lore:Sand Eel|Sand Eel]].{{Ref|name=ONFishing|[[Online:Fishing#Daggerfall Covenant|Alik'r Desert]] fish catches in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The Alik'r's access to the Iliac Bay provides it with a variety of sea-based creatures. Local fishmongers can find fish like the [[Lore:Alewife|Alewife]], the [[Lore:Driftfish|Driftfish]], the [[Lore:Sablefish|Sablefish]] and the rare [[Lore:Bonefish|Bonefish]].{{Ref|name=ONFishing}}

==History==
===Before the Ra Gada===
[[File:ON-interior-Volenfell 03.jpg|thumb|left|Where Volendrung first landed in Hammerfell]]
[[File:ON-place-Salas En.jpg|thumb|right|Salas En]]
Before the Redguards arrived, Hammerfell was said to be a barren wasteland, with sparse settlements populated by varying races. The oldest known elven settlement in Alik'r is Salas En, originally settled by an unknown clan of Ayleids and built by their Nedic slaves in the Merethic Era.{{Ref|name=TSEE}}{{Ref|name=ONClarisse}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadSalas|[[Online:Salas En|Salas En]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

When the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]] was created in {{Year|1E 420}}, the [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken Clan]] of Dwemer self-exiled themselves from the [[Lore:Morrowind|east]] and followed [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]]'s path as it flew across the sky. The hammer landed somewhere in the Alik'r Desert, where they built their capital of [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}}  The Rourken Clan had a sparse population spread across several cities and mines.{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} Though they found a land with untapped and rich resources, and they were quick to establish an easy trade relationship with their neighbors,{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadSantaki}} their cities were reportedly few in riches.{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}}

At some point in time, a [[Lore:Goblin Gate|rent in space time]] opened over Hammerfell and a previously unknown breed of goblins, the legendary giant goblins invaded and pillaged Dwemer settlements. The Dwemer eventually fled by sea through a lone surviving port city.{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} With the Dwemer gone, the giant goblins established themselves on Hammerfell and built strongholds in the deep desert.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}} Salas En was eventually abandoned by the Ayleids, though the circumstances are unknown.{{Ref|name=TSEE}} In {{Year|1E 522}}, the Corelanya Clan of Altmer left the distant [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] after a harsh ban on [[Lore:Daedra|daedric]] worship. [[Lore:Sundavar Corelanya|Sundavar Corelanya]] laid claim to Salas En and made it the seat of a new kingdom, with him as its first kinlord.{{Ref|name=HOCC1}} 

Other groups that lived in the Alik'r included clans of [[Lore:Desert Orcs|desert Orcs]]. [[Lore:Mer|Elves]] and later [[Lore:Breton|Manmeri]] established an outpost at what is now the city of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] in order to protect fisherfolk and seafaring merchants from inland Orc marauders, Goblin raiders, and other hazards of the desert.{{ref|name=PGE3}}{{ref|name=EGH|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Hammerfell}}}}{{Ref|name=STJOA}}

The Corelanya kingdom was a thriving mercantile realm with a friendly relationship with the Direnni to the north and the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] to the far south. Because of the hostile desert tribes, the Corelanya shifted their focus to the sea and sailed for lands as far away as the [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]] and beyond. Some of their typical threats were the giant goblins on the mainland and the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]]. Their relationship with the Direnni Hegemony was strained after [[Lore:Iniel Corelanya|Iniel Corelanya]] rose to power sometime around {{Year|1E 726}}. Her high-handed ways alienated them the two factions.{{Ref|name=HOCC2}} Whether the Dwemer ever retook their lost lands is unknown, but they ultimately vanished from Tamriel in {{Year|1E 700}}.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

====The First Warrior Wave====
[[File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei's Victory 02.png|thumb|left|190px|Frandar Hunding and the Giant Goblins]]
{{Quote Box|''The Song of Divad tells us that he and his band of Sword Singers worked their sacred forges and consulted eldritch tomes for many long years to create five great swords. After the [[Lore:War of the Singers|War of the Singers]] in {{Year|1E 780}},{{Ref|name=RTHATH}}{{Ref|name=DTS|{{Cite Book|Divad the Singer}}}} Frandar Hunding, his son Divad and their fellow warriors, the [[Lore:Army of the Circle|Army of the Circle]] embarked from [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] and landed in Tamriel, becoming the first Redguards to settle Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=RTHATH}} Traveling across the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]], they landed in the abandoned port city the Dwemer escaped from{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} and found the land inhabited by the vicious giant goblins.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}} 

Their subsequent conquest of the Alik'r Desert is a staple in Hammerfell legend.{{Ref|name=LABHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}} Seeking to rid them of their new land, Frandar rallied the Army of the Circle to once again fight,{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}} now against the army known as Malooc's Horde.{{Ref|name=T26OFSIU}} But in one of their bloodiest battles, Frandar was killed and Divad took up his crusade.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}} At his side were several companions, such as [[Lore:Razul|Razul]]{{Ref|name=ONRamati|[[Online:Ramati at-Gar|Ramati at-Gar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} and [[Lore:Sabasad the Shield|Sabasad the Shield]].{{Ref|name=ONArmin|[[Online:Armin|Armin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Despite their initial successes, the goblins kept returning at an endless rate. Divad devised a plan in which the singer swordsmiths created five magical swords, each imbued with his spirit, a mix of Shehai and smithing, and used by his sword-singers to close the goblin gate.{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}}

It took many years to forge the swords and consult eldritch tomes.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}} By the end, Divad was incredibly weak, but the swords were immensely powerful and given to five [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]]. The five singers closed the goblin gate, ending the Goblin invasion.{{Ref|name=STDH|{{Cite Book|Shrine to Divad Hunding}}}}{{Ref|name=NFRH}}{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} Divad's actions allegedly cursed the Goblins, leaving them diminished to smaller forms.{{Ref|name=TTTTOH2}}{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL} The story of their victory is recited in the ''[[Lore:Song of Divad|Song of Divad]]'', his death poem written twelve days after the five swords were sealed.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}}{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} By the time [[Lore:Destri Melarg|Destri Melarg]] began his research for the book, ''[[Lore:Redguards, History and Heroes|Redguards, their History and their Heroes]]'' in the early Third Era,{{Ref|name=RTHATH}}{{Ref|name=NFRH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}} there was considered to be no scholarly evidence for both the magical swords and giant goblins.{{Ref|name=RTXT|group=UOL}} Some modern scholars consider the war with the giant Goblins a myth, the victory of the Redguards is still celebrated during the annual [[Lore:Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]]{{Ref|name=DFBlades} Though both the giant Goblins and the magic swords were real according to first-hand accounts.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}}{{ref|name=DerikESO|[[ON:Derik Hallin|Derik Hallin]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

===Early History===
[[File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg|thumb|right|Sentinel in the mid-Second Era]]
[[File:ON-item-Ansei-Ward 02.jpg|thumb|left|One of the Ansei Wards]]
Several years later, having lost their homeland, the rest of the Yokudans arrived in Hammerfell in {{Year|1E 808}}.{{Ref|name=HOTIB}} This second wave of Ra Gada first landed on [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]]'s shores and set themselves out across the coast to find new ports. The third wave lef by Grandee Yaghoub sailed north around Cape Shira, becoming the first of his people to discover the Iliac Bay. As he followed [[Lore:The Steed|the Steed]] on the 17th of Second Seed, his watchmen spotted an occupied port of elves and humans. Yaghoub named it "Sentinel" after his watchmen and conquered its people despite their attempts at peace. Yaghoub made them [[Lore:Slave|slaves]] and used their labor to build present-day Sentinel and [[Lore:Samaruik|Samaruik]], its seat of power.{{Ref|name=STJOA}}

Not only did the Ra Gada settle Hammerfell's coast but embarked into the desert. In the wastes, they built the Hall of Judgment in Bergama{{Ref|name=ONSulma|[[Online:Magistrate Sulma|Magistrate Sulma]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} and traveled as far east as [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] to build landmarks like [[Lore:Hel Ra Citadel|Hel Ra Citadel]].{{Ref|name=CAC3|{{Cite Book|Castles and Coffers Volume III: Hel Ra Citadel}}}} By this time, the town of [[Lore:Tava's Blessing|Tava's Blessing]] was established east of Sentinel.{{Ref|name=ONFahara'jad}}{{Ref|name=AS|{{Cite Book|Ansei Shrine}}}}{{intnote|Founding|[Note 2]}} Yaghoub's warrior wave, including his famous band of sword-singers called [[Lore:Yaghoub's Thirteen|Yaghoub's Thirteen]], set up camp on the [[Lore:Ancestor's Landing|beachfront]] west of Sentinel. The actions of one member, [[Lore:Navid the Singer|Navid the Singer]],{{Ref|name=TBONTS|{{Cite Book|The Ballad of Navid the Singer}}}} cursed the site and his story became a local legend.{{Ref|name=ONOwynok|[[Online:Azei at-Owynok|Azei at-Owynok]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

Their biggest opposition at the time was the elves of Hammerfell. From the beginning, the Yokudans of Sentinel and the Altmer of Salas En were at odds.{{Ref|name=HOCC2}} Having a bad history with the [[Lore:Lefthanded Elves|Lefthanded Elves]] of Yokuda{{Ref|name=ONLoadSalas}} and taking offense to their daedric worship, the Yokudans were adamant in eradicating them from Hammerfell. Iniel Corelanya, on the other hand, had no intention on sharing the land with them, and so for years, they engaged in combat. The Corelanya Clan defended Salas En with magic and stealth, using sandstorms, mirages, and summoned daedra to their advantage. Sentinel grew to resent the ''"accursed city"''of Salas En.{{Ref|name=HOCC2}} 

In {{Year|1E 853}}, the former King of [[Lore:Yath|Yath]], the Crown [[Lore:Xakhwan|Xakhwan]] decided it was time to end this conflict and launched a proper invasion of Salas En.{{Ref|name=HOCC2}}{{Ref|name=ONHaqmir|[[Online:Prince Haqmir|Prince Haqmir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Princes [[Lore:Haqmir|Haqmir]] and [[Lore:Sameer|Sameer]] led the defense in Ash'abah Pass and defeated [[Lore:Iniel Corelanya|Iniel Corelanya]], who in their last breath, used the darkest, most dreadful magic of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] to resurrect dead soldiers. King Xakhawn died in battle and Iniel's most powerful [[Lore:Necromancers|necromancer]], [[Lore:Venerien|Venerien]] resurrected him. Given the difficult task of disposing of their father's reanimated remains, one prince destroyed the corpse and was subsequently banished for his treatment of the honored dead, while the other prince succeeded his father as King.{{Ref|name=ONInitiation}}

Though the Corelanya Clan was defeated and returned to the Summerset Isles, some elves fled into the interior. Any remaining Corelanya havens in Hammerfell were found and its people slaughtered. In the wastes were powerful necromancers who lurked in desert towers. With the introduction of necromancy, the war between the Ra Gada and elves turned into a holy war.{{Ref|name=HOCC2}}{{Ref|name=EOTAW|{{Cite Book|Epode of the Ansei Wards}}}} Perhaps the greatest threat in the Alik'r's history was when these elven necromancers raised an army of the dead in the Deep Desert.{{Ref|name=ONFahara'jad|[[Online:King Fahara'jad|King Fahara'jad]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

The Ra Gada were now presented with the same dilemma as in Ash'abah Pass. So that they may not striking against the honored dead, three Ansei warriors — Halelah, Maja, and Radan — sacrificed themselves to create the [[Lore:Ansei Wards|Ansei Wards]], swords made from their [[Lore:Shehai|shehai]] and [[Lore:Souls|souls]] that allowed people to strike the dead and consecrate them in Tu'whacca's name while upholding their faith.{{Ref|name=EOTAW}} After their victory, the Ansei Wards were placed in Sentinel's Impervious Vault, where they sat for years.{{Ref|name=ONFahara'jad}}

===Post-Ra Gada History===
[[File:ON-place-Leki's Blade.jpg|thumb|right|Leki's Blade]]
[[File:ON-npc-Derik Hallin.jpg|thumb|left|Derik Hallin]]
Once the Ra Gada invasion was over, the Na-Totambu made landfall and established themselves as Hammerfell's ruling faction. While Hegathe was made their capital,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} other nobles and kings moved to the northern Alik'r Desert. The Na-Totambu wanted places to preserve and reproduce Yokudan culture. The now-lost city of the [[Lore:Na-Totambu (settlement)|Na-Totambu]] near modern-day [[Lore:Kozanset|Kozanset]] was supposed to serve this purpose before it was buried in a great sandstorm.{{Ref|name=ONLoadLost|[[Online:Lost City of the Na-Totambu|Lost City of the Na-Totambu]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

As the Na-Totambu's influence slowly superseded the Ra Gada's, Divad Hunding's former battle companion, Razul, declared himself Yokeda and cemented his legacy as the first Forebear. He was later put to rest in Tu'whacca's Throne.{{Ref|name=ONRamati}} Though the Forebears mainly held southern Hammerfell, Sentinel and its kingdom became their main foothold in the Alik'r Desert.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Legends of Hammerfell's early years soon sprung up, with oral traditions persisting to this day about the Alik'r Yokudans and their battle with the Firehide Clan.{{Ref|name=ONFingerAnti|[[Online:Alik'r Desert Antiquities#Na-Totambu Two-Finger Gauntlet|Na-Totambu Two-Finger Gauntlet]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

At roughly the same time,{{Ref|name=ONSaran|[[Online:Rada al-Saran#The Despot of Markarth|Rada al-Saran]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} the Ansei [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]] wandered the desert and found the Saint [[Lore:Leki|Leki]] at an oasis.{{Ref|name=ONRahama}} The two warriors dueled for three days and three nights. It ended in a draw when Rada struck Leki's rocky blade at its weakest point. The blade broke in two, creating a great sandstorm that split the horizon. Leki built a shrine at the oasis when it passed, and a school called [[Lore:Leki's Blade|Leki's Blade]] was built around it.{{Ref|name=ONRahama}}

Despite the crusades against the goblin-ken, a few goblin tribes remained, as did the Desert Orcish tribes of the Hollow Wastes. Despite sometimes holding their own against the coastal Redguard kingdoms, such as during warrior-chief [[Lore:Thulgeg|Thulgeg]]'s robust defense against the Redguards of Hegathe, they gradually lost favor and came to feel trapped on multiple fronts. Even with their combined effort, the newly named Warlord Thulgeg saw defeat in their future and made the march east through Tigonus and the [[Lore:Fallen Wastes|Fallen Wastes]] to escape through the [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]]. The Redguards believe they destroyed the goblin-ken in {{Year|1E 874}}, but in reality, remnants of the desert clans helped establish the foundations of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]].{{Ref|name=TM}}{{Ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} This conflict would continue with Sentinel's involvement in the [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]], from {{Year|1E 950}} to {{Year|1E 980}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1TWR}}

Sometime in the First Era, by the time the art of sword-singing was nearly lost, the goblin warlord [[Lore:Mahgzoor Rockhand|Mahgzoor Rockhand]] descended on the city of [[Lore:Ojwambu|Ojwambu]] and forced their people to retreat to the Alik'r Desert. Queen Ojwa led her citizens through a secret mountain path while [[Lore:Derik Hallin|Derik Hallin]], the last Ansei, stood alone to defend the city. While they evacuated, Hallin's students drilled the Book of Circles' teachings to the people. After Hallin managed to defeat the warlord at the cost of his life, Queen Ojwa led her new army of sword-singer warriors to defeat the [[Lore:Endwise Army|Endwise Army]].{{Ref|name=TTTTOH2|{{Cite Book|The True-Told Tale of Hallin, Pt 2}}}}
{{NewLine}}

===The Alik'r in the Late First and Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-King Fahara'jad.jpg|thumb|right|King Fahara'jad of Antiphyllos]]
[[File:DF-place-Sentinel Palace 01.png|thumb|left|The Palace of Sentinel, where the Crowns moved from Hegathe]]
In the late [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] entered Hammerfell via the old capital, Hegathe{{Ref|name=1TTP|{{Cite Book|1. The Thrassian Plague}}}} and traveled up north into the Alik'r Desert. The Crowns of Salas En were effectively wiped out by the middle twenty-third century,{{Ref|name=TSEE}} while the population in the Iliac Bay at large radically decreased.{{Ref|name=1TTP}} Only nobles in Sentinel and other kingdoms escaped by making safe passage to the [[Lore:Isle of Balfiera|Isle of Balfiera]].{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|6}} 

At roughly around the same time, a woman named Afsar became a Queen of Alik'r when she quelled the civil unrest that came after her predecessor died without an heir. She killed any opposition to the throne, and despite the bloody circumstances, her reign was peaceful.{{Ref|name=ONRamati}} Upon the arrival of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], the Redguards formed two parties to administrate the newly-named Imperial Province of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} 

The Crowns settled across the Alik'r Desert via Bergama, Lainlyn, and other states in the north, while the Forebears based themselves in Sentinel.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Concurrent with the annexation, the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] built one of their temples, [[Lore:Wind Scour Temple|Wind Scour Temple]], underneath HoonDing's Watch.{{Ref|name=TRAFOTB|{{Cite Book|The Rise and Fall of the Blades}}}}{{Ref|nname=ONSai|[[Online:Sai Sahan#The Dragonguard's Legacy|Sai Sahan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The [[Lore:Phyllocid Dynasty|Phyllocid Dynasty]] ruled the province from Hegathe during this time,{{Ref|name=TWAHD|{{Cite Book|The Worthy Ar-Azal, His Deeds}}}} but at some point in the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], the capital was moved to Sentinel by the High King.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

By the mid-sixth century of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Sentinel was ruled by High King [[Lore:Ramzi the Distrait|Ramzi the Distrait]], but as the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] took hold in {{Year|2E 560}}, the land devolved into chaos and the king sealed himself away in Samaruik. In the wake of it all, Prince [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]] of Antiphyllos arrived from Bergama and named himself King of Hammerfell. The southern rulers resisted his claim and rumors spread of a Forebear plot, but with support from the north and other Forebears, Fahara'jad restored Sentinel to its previous glory.{{Ref|name=EGT}} The new King of Sentinel assembled a circle of advisors, some of whom were Forebears that openly opposed Ramzi's reign. One of these viziers was [[Lore:Suturah|Suturah]], who orchestrated an assassination plot by killing all of the King's other advisors and resurrecting them to attack. Though Fahara'jad escaped, so did Suturah, who fled into the Deep Desert and created an army of Ra-Netu to sack Sentinel.{{Ref|name=SAMITA}} Fahara'jad personally requested help from the Ash'abah, led by Headman Marimah. Though the terms of this alliance are unknown, Marimah defeated the undead army and killed Suturah. To the public, the King attributed their defeat to divine intervention, while keeping his pact with the Ash'abah a secret.{{Ref|name=SAMITA}}

In {{Year|2E 566}}, Fahara'jad's daughter Princess {{Lore Link|Maraya}} married High King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of High Rock, bringing Fahara'jad's kingdom into the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]]. The Covenant had also brought in the [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] of [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]], despite protests from the Crowns of the Alik'r region, among others.{{Ref|name=GTTDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}} Much of northern Hammerfell, including [[Lore:Hallin's Stand|Hallin's Stand]] in Bangkorai, stood with the Covenant, while outer territories remained distrustful.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=GTTDC}} Although the Redguards were least represented in the alliance, the Covenant benefitted greatly by using the shipyards of Tava's Blessing to supply the navy.{{Ref|name=SAF}}{{Ref|name=ONMeriq}}
{{NewLine}}

===The Withered Hand and the Ansei Wards===
[[File:ON-npc-Uwafa.jpg|thumb|right|Uwafa]]
While Suturah remained in the Motalion Necropolis, his plans to conquer the Alik'r with the undead lived on through his children; [[Lore:Uwafa|Uwafa]], [[Lore:Alasan|Alasan]], and Shiri. The brothers, Uwafa and Alasan created a necromantic cult called the [[Lore:Withered Hand|Withered Hand]] and conducted the first phase of their plan, the attack on Sentinel. It began with the resurrection of many Ra-Netu raising from the water to storm the city harbor.{{Ref|name=ONRisen|[[Online:Risen From the Depths|Risen From the Depths]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Meanwhile, the brothers dug through to the Impervious Vault, bypassing the impenetrable doors and taking the Ansei Wards. By the time Sentinel's forces had opened the vault themselves, the swords were gone.{{Ref|name=ONVault}} 

Much like the previous event, Fahara'jad sought help from the Ash'abah but through an [[Lore:The Vestige|Agent]] of the Covenant. By becoming an honorary member of the tribe, they gained their support and trust.{{Ref|name=ONInitiation}} Uwafa had been tracked to Tu'whacca's Throne with the ward of Ansei Radan, where he took over the temple and resurrected its dead. With help from the Throne Keeper, the Agent cleansed the temple and defeated the necromancer, thus securing the first ward.{{Ref|name=ONUwafa|[[Online:A Reckoning with Uwafa|A Reckoning with Uwafa]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} Meanwhile, Prince Azah and Shiri, under the guise of his new assistant, find Alasan in Tava's Blessing with the ward of Ansei Maja. The town at that point was lost while Alasan performed his ritual in the lighthouse across town. The Agent destroyed his focuses across town and killed the necromancer. However, they inadvertently broke the ward and Alasan's spirit remained, albeit invulnerable.{{Ref|name=ONAlasan|[[Online:Alasan's Plot|Alasan's Plot]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

With help from Ansei Maja's spirit, her Ansei Ward was restored and Alasan's spirit was sealed inside it. Before her spirit was put to rest, however, Maja revealed the presence of a third sibling.{{Ref|name=ONAlasan}} Shiri, in an attempt to lead the Agent astray, told them to find the last ward; the Ward of Ansei Halelah in HoonDing's Watch.{{Ref|name=ONShiri|[[Online:Shiri's Research|Shiri's Research]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} While the Agent was distracted, however, Shiri went to Satakalaam to uncover her father's text and proceed further with her brothers' plan.{{Ref|name=NJ|{{Cite Book|Nahirah's Journal|ns_base=ON}}}} By the time the Agent and Sentinel learned the truth, Shiri took Ansei Radan and Maja's re-corrupted wards to resurrect her father in the Motalion Necropolis while Satakalaam was taken over by the [[Lore:Seventh Legion|Seventh Legion]], who were in league with the Withered Hand.{{Ref|name=ONFarvad}}{{Ref|name=ONThoda}}

In the final confrontation with the necromancers, Shiri resurrected the evil dead of the Motalian Necropolis and brought her father back to life as a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] with the Ansei Wards. The Agent defeated Suturah and Shiri in the crypt, while a companion held the doors open with Ansei Radan's ward.{{Ref|name=ONAmputating|[[Online:Amputating the Hand|Amputating the Hand]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With the threat dealt with, the wards were once again purified and kept in the Impervious Vault under stronger seals.{{Ref|name=ONRestoring|[[Online:Restoring the Ansei Wards|Restoring the Ansei Wards]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At around the same time, [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]] was under siege by [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] and the Seventh Legion after the [[Lore:Eamond|King]] of [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] was killed. King Fahara'jad sent the Agent there to investigate.{{Ref|name=ONMourning|[[Online:Kingdom in Mourning|Kingdom in Mourning]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Alik'r and the Third Empire===
[[File:DF-map-Sentinel.png|thumb|left|The Kingdom of Sentinel ca. 3E 405]]
[[File:DF-map-Alik'r Desert.png|thumb|right|Partial map of the unconquered Alik'r Desert ca. 3E 405]]
Well after Fahara'jad's reign, the Crowns regained their High Kingship in Sentinel, with the last of their line being [[Lore:Thassad II|Thassad II]]. In an attempt to strike peace, [[Lore:Volag|Volag]], the Forebear Baron of Sentinel's outlying territories, struck an accord with him that lasted until his death in {{Year|2E 862}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=TOOC|{{Cite Book|The Origin of Cyrus!|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|25}} The Purge of the North, as it is remembered today, was Baron Volag's attempt to claim the throne with an attack on Sentinel that forced the Crowns to flee. While Prince [[Lore:A'tor|A'tor]] led the Crowns to [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] to plan a counterattack, Volag assumed power over Sentinel. In the ensuing war, the Forebears were on the back end until they enlisted help from the emerging [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]].{{Ref|name=RGTobias|[[Redguard:Tobias|Tobias]]' dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{Ref|name=RGKrisandra|[[Redguard:Krisandra|Krisandra]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

After the [[Lore:Battle of Hunding Bay|Battle of Hunding Bay]] ended the war, Baron Volag disappeared into the hills and Provisional Governor [[Lore:Senecus Goddkey|Senecus Goddkey]] was left to govern Sentinel. As a result, a new treaty was signed that allowed the Imperial Legion to settle in the province's cities.{{Ref|name=RGTobias}} The Satakals soon attacked these garrisons, and were, in turn, run out of the towns by the Provisional Governors.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} After the [[Lore:Stros M'Kai Uprising|Stros M'Kai Uprising]] ended its [[Lore:Amiel Richton|governor]]'s reign, Baron Volag used this moment of weakness to retake Sentinel.{{Ref|name=RGIszara|[[Redguard:Raze the Palace|Raze the Palace]] story quest in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} 

With the Crowns Regent-Queen, [[Lore:Iszara|Iszara]],{{Ref|name=DTES|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Decrypting the Elder Scrolls|ns_base=GEN}}}} the Baron and Tiber Septim went to Stros M'Kai and formally incorporated Hammerfell into the Empire by signing the [[Lore:First Treaty of Stros M'Kai|First Treaty of Stros M'Kai]].{{Ref|name=RGIszara}} Though Sentinel was a Forebear kingdom, northern Hammerfell was mostly Crown territory, and its people stuck to traditional Yokudan traditions.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} 

The Iliac Bay region was ill-prepared for the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper's Invasion|Camoran Usurper's Invasion]]. As refugees fled north, the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Usurper]]'s armies followed suit in an unstoppable northward march. The King of Sentinel at the time was unable to unite its people to fight back, but after many years, the invasion was halted in {{Year|3E 267}} by unlikely forces from High Rock.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}}{{Ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper|The Fall of the Usurper}}}}

By the fifth century of the Third Era, the northern Alik'r was split between several regions, each with a patron deity from the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] and their associated temple. Most of them laden the coastline, from [[Lore:Abibon-Gora|Abibon-Gora]] to Sentinel and [[Lore:Tigonus (fiefdom)|Tigonus]], but some landlocks nations like Antiphyllos, [[Lore:Bergama (barony)|Bergama]], and [[Lore:Santaki (county)|Santaki]] also existed.{{Ref|name=DFMap}} Most of the Alik'r Desert was considered unconquered territory,{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} with the city of [[Lore:Alik'ra|Alik'ra]] as the provincial capital of the northern wastes, though no ruler claimed the region. Despite its large size, the region's settlements were spread sparsely across the land but among them were several other cities; namely, Agogurana, Anteliiga, Berbaaqnia, Ceranda-Kom, Ceriburea, Isfunnusa, Kaloguja, Mettula, Mogandaton, Ramoaweyn, Rhogila, and Tsetissiara.{{Ref|name=DFAlik'r|[[Daggerfall:Alik'r|Alik'r]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The political landscape in the Iliac Bay had drastically changed in {{Year|3E 417}} with the [[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]]. This magical intervention transformed the many nations, including unconquered territories like the Alik'r, into four vassal kingdoms of the Empire. It occurred between the 9th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]] and the 11th of Frostfall, all within a second. A [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] agent in the Alik'r Desert and en route to Bergama reported that a ginormous sandstorm blew across the land and by the end of it, Sentinel, under Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]], controlled not just the barony, but the entire southern Iliac Bay. While former independent countries in northern Hammerfell became a part of Sentinel,{{Ref|name=TWITW}} it is unknown how much of north Alik'r was absorbed by the kingdom. By the end of the Third Era, it sat between the Alik'r and the Iliac Bay regions.{{Ref|name=LRPWormgod|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/War of the Wormgod|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 13/War of the Wormgod – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} 

===Post-Warp in the West and Onward===
[[File:ON-interior-Wind Scour Temple.jpg|thumb|right|Wind Scour Temple, the Blades hideout in the Alik'r]]
Queen Akorithi and her heir apparent, [[Lore:Greklith|Greklith]], passed away from a plague,{{Ref|name=NFOS|{{Cite Book|Night Falls on Sentinel}}}} leaving the Kingdom of Sentinel with the youngest sibling, Lhotun. Although he now reigned as one of the strongest kings in the Empire,{{Ref|name=TWITW}} much of these lands did not align with the Forebears. The king went to great lengths to keep them under his wing and his main solution to this was creating the Lhotunic party that took aspects of both the Forebears and the Crowns. Many figures from both factions took issue with this, namely the High Prophet of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]], [[Lore:Ayaan-si|Ayaan-si]], who called upon True Crowns to war with Sentinel and even had forays built on Bergama and Dak'fron's borders. What became of this war is unknown, or how it was affected by the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

On the 30th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]], {{Year|4E 171}}, the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] erupted throughout Cyrodiil and Hammerfell. The Alik'r Desert was a central location in the war, starting with the March of Thirst. The province's southern coast was swarmed by the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] and the outnumbered [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] retreated across the desert, hence the name. Before the war, a [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] ambassador approached Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]] with an ultimatum, to which the Emperor rejected.{{Ref|name=TGW}}

One of the stipulations of the ultimatum was the Blades' disbandment, but despite the Emperor's unwillingness to dissolve them, the Thalmor were quick to hunt them during the Great War. Many of them died defending their temples, such as Wind Scour Temple in the Alik'r Desert.{{Ref|name=TRAFOTB}} While the Crowns and Forebears reconciled and secured Hegathe, Lady [[Lore:Arannelya|Arannelya]]'s main army crossed the Alik'r Desert and barely took Skaven from the Legion led by General Decianus. When the General was called back to the Imperial City in late {{Year|4E 174}}, he left behind a contingent of "invalid" soldiers that formed the core of the army that retook Skaven and forced Arannelya back across the desert. En route, they faced heavy losses and constant harassment from Alik'r warriors.{{Ref|name=TGW}}
