'''Regent''' [[Lore:Karinwe Corelanya|Karinwe Corelanya]] was an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] noblewoman who served as the leader of [[Lore:Sunport|Sunport]], as well as the head of [[Lore:Clan Corelanya|Clan Corelanya]] during the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]'s [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|name=RK}} In addition to her political role, she was also a historian and author.{{Ref|name=HOCC1}}<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}
==History==
===Reign===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''And so we come to an end of my account of ancient times. Quite a few things have, of course, transpired on Solstice since 1E 2633: chief among them the arrival of settlers other than High Elves, including Nords, pirates, shipwrecked merchants, and more. Yet these events seem to fall within the normal ebb and flow of history's tides, as our Argonian neighbors might say. Someday I hope to add another part to this history. But if that is not the future the Three Queens have in mind for me, then perhaps another Corelanya will finish my work. And perhaps even look back in wonder at the times I lived through, as well. 
'' —[[Lore:History of Clan Corelanya|History of Clan Corelanya]]'' by Karinwe Corelanya{{Ref|name=HOCC5}}}}

Little is known about Karinwe's life prior to her ascension to the throne. She was the daughter of the previous Regent, [[Lore:Estunirion Corelanya|Estunirion]],{{Ref|name=RK|[[ON:Regent Karinwe|Regent Karinwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and succeeded him in {{Year|2E 439}}.{{Ref|name=TSEA|{{Cite Book|The Sea Elf Ambassador}}}} She received magical training as part of her education, though she rarely got opportunity to put her skills into practice.{{Ref|name=RK}}

In {{Year|2E 570}}, during the 131st year of her regency, Lady Karinwe Corelanya received an ambassador from King [[Lore:Orgnum|Orgnum]] of the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]. Sealord [[Lore:Loruuyamo|Loruuyamo]] arrived in Sunport proposing an alliance against [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset]], citing the rise of the untested Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] and offering support to reclaim lands once denied to Clan Corelanya. Regent Karinwe, however, rejected the offer, stating that King [[Lore:Hidellith|Hidellith]], Ayrenn's father, had offered no threat to her house, and she considered it only right to return that courtesy. Her firm yet diplomatic response reaffirmed Solstice's commitment to neutrality, though she continued to permit Maormer ships and traders to visit her realm.{{ref|name=TSEA}}

[[File:ON-place-Vinutilmo Palace.jpg|thumb|left|Vinutilmo Palace]]

During her reign, Regent Karinwe authored a comprehensive five-volume chronicle of her clan's history, preserving the legacy of House Corelanya for future generations. The work begins with [[Lore:Valarmil Corela|Valarmil Corela]], an [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] ancestor drawn to the teachings of [[Lore:Temple Saints|Saint]] [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]], and traces the lineage through [[Lore:Sundavar Corelanya|Sundavar Corelanya]], who led the exodus from Summerset to [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and founded a kingdom at [[Lore:Salas En|Salas En]].{{Ref|name=HOCC1|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 1}}}} It recounts the deeds of his descendants, [[Lore:Roliondil Corelanya|Roliondil]] and his daughter [[Lore:Iniel Corelanya|Iniel]], who discovered the island of Solstice but perished during the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] invasion.{{Ref|name=HOCC2|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 2}}}} The tome continues with [[Lore:Earlenque Corelanya|Earlenque Corelanya]], founder of the new realm in Solstice,{{Ref|name=HOCC3|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 3}}}} and chronicles generations of rulers, including their conflicts and eventual peace with the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]], culminating in the Peace of [[Lore:Xor-Hist|Xor-Hist]], partially brokered by [[Lore:Torinwe Corelanya|Torinwe Corelanya]].{{Ref|name=HOCC4|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 4}}}} The final volume details the fall of the tyrant [[Lore:Istamil Corelanya|Istamil]] and the rise of the regency under [[Lore:Vinutilmo Corelanya|Vinutilmo]] in {{Year|1E 2633}}, marking a new era for the Corelanya legacy.{{Ref|name=HOCC5|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 5}}}}

It is alleged that Regent Karinwe once personally purchased goods from a merchant affiliated with the [[Lore:Circle of Champions|Circle of Champions]]. The merchant later used this transaction in their advertising, claiming it as a mark of quality and distinction.{{Ref|name=Nerya|[[ON:Nerya Aalvul|Nerya Aalvul]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Regency issued a formal warning urging caution to any who might venture into the southwestern reaches of the isle, where a sect of the cult of [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] had gathered, hosting a hedonistic fair marked by excess and debauchery. This was due to the cult's recent behavior having turned darker than usual.{{ref|name=VGTWS}}

As regent, Karinwe was responsible for actively sponsoring and sanctioning the pursuits of the various colleges of the Collegium Praxis.{{Ref|name=Korasephina|[[ON:Korasephina|Korasephina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Tazurr|[[ON:Tazurr|Tazurr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TCP|{{Cite Book|The Collegium Praxis}}}}

===Worm Cult Crisis===
[[File:ON-npc-Prince Azah 05.jpg|thumb|left|Azah of the Fighters Guild]][[File:ON-npc-Gabrielle Benele 05.jpg|thumb|right|Gabrielle Benele of the Mages Guild]]

During the Worm Cult crisis, a decree issued by the Regency of Sunport called upon skilled adventurers to aid the Collegium in matters concerning the safety and stability of the isle.{{Ref|name=SSA|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Sunport Seeks Adventurers!}}}} Regent Karinwe entrusted the colleges with addressing the threats to her people amid the Worm Cult crisis. She made sure that Collegium is funded well enough to outsource some of their tasks to mercenaries.{{Ref|name=Korasephina|[[ON:Korasephina|Korasephina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of the tasks involved confronting Gaulm, a [[Lore:Vahath Behemoth|Vahath Behemoth]] who had made his lair in the training grounds of the College of Tomes in [[Lore:Tuniria|Tuniria]].{{Ref|name=Morlia|[[ON:Morlia|Morlia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Karinwe was aware of the squatters living in Deetra Grotto and made it clear that the Regency of Sunport would tolerate their presence, as long as the pirates, smugglers, and freetraders kept the peace and caused no trouble to her people.{{ref|name=DG|[[ON:Deetra Grotto|Deetra Grotto]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, amid the Worm Cult crisis, Regent Karinwe was approached by the acting guildmasters of the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters]] and [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guilds]], [[Lore:Azah|Azah]] and [[Lore:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]]. Initially cautious, she informed Azah that she would consult her advisors before offering a formal response. Regent Karinwe was reluctant to see her people drawn into another's war, maintaining a deep desire to preserve peace for Solstice. Yet she understood that the threat posed by the Worm Cult could not be ignored. Azah regarded Karinwe as a capable leader who genuinely cared for the well-being of Sunport and all of Solstice, and who did not act with haste or recklessness.{{Ref|name=PA|[[ON:Prince Azah|Prince Azah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Recognizing the strategic value of cooperation, she granted the guilds permission to establish headquarters within the city.{{Ref|name=VGTWS|{{Cite Book|Visitor's Guide to Western Solstice}}}} However, this decision stirred discontent among Sunport's more destitute citizens, who viewed it as favoring outsiders over their own needs.{{Ref|name=Golb|[[ON:Golb|Golb]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This move allowed Azah to begin mustering forces on Solstice soil. However, the question of formally joining the wider conflict remained under deliberation, as persuading the regent's court was expected to be a slow and complex process.{{Ref|name=PA}} The long silence was surprising to some members of the Fellowship, as Karinwe had previously been willing to cooperate.{{Ref|name=GB|[[ON:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Before she could reconvene with Azah following her meeting with advisors, Regent Karinwe was abducted by the forces of the Worm Cult from the palace.{{Ref|name=MM|[[ON:Midconjuror Mirmal|Midconjuror Mirmal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TROS|Events of [[ON:The Regent of Sunport|The Regent of Sunport]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A small group of people posing as merchants arrived, carrying crates and bundles as was customary. They quickly dispatched a few guards and took Regent Karinwe, spiriting her away to Swencoast, an old manor perched atop a hill.{{Ref|name=Viniama|[[ON:Viniama (Vinutilmo Palace)|Viniama]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=CNS|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Crumpled Note (Solstice)}}}} The forces of the Fellowship investigated the incident and then split up.{{Ref|name=TROS}} Some chose to secure the palace, while others set out for Swencoast to rescue Karinwe.{{Ref|name=Skordo|[[ON:Skordo the Knife|Skordo the Knife]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Walks|[[ON:Walks-in-Ash|Walks-in-Ash]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of the Fellowship members, along with a representative of the Dominion, [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], rescued the Regent from the estate and escorted her back to Sunport.{{Ref|name=TROS}}{{Ref|name=Razum|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Karinwe overheard her captors discussing their plans. They intended to use powerful weapons on the Palace District. Her kidnapping was only the beginning. The Worm Cult had secretly hidden soldiers throughout the palace, who later emerged and took the defenders by surprise, allowing them to seize control of the Palace District.{{Ref|name=RK}}{{Ref|name=PA}} During the attack, Regent Karinwe formally declared an alliance with the Fellowship, offering Sunport's support. While their defensive forces were limited, she pledged everything they had to aid the Stirk Fellowship. Although she detested bloodshed, she joined the fight to protect her people, using old secret passages beneath the city to help the defenders reach key positions. Ultimately, the city was saved and the forces of the Worm Cult were driven out, though their presence on the island was far from over.{{Ref|name=RK}}{{Ref|name=TROS}} While some citizens welcomed the Fellowship's assistance and placed their trust in Karinwe's leadership,{{Ref|name=Ancajja|[[ON:Ancajja|Ancajja]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} others feared that the current situation could spark unrest or even lead to open revolt.{{Ref|name=Golb}}

Following the death of Gabrielle Benele in the war against the Worm Cult, Lady Karinwe was contacted by Prince Azah and invited to participate in the series of interviews once hosted by Gabrielle. The series had since been taken over by [[Lore:Amalien|Amalien]], Gabrielle's colleague and fellow member of the [[Lore:Antiquarian Circle|Antiquarian Circle]]. Intrigued by the opportunity and eager to contribute, Karinwe responded enthusiastically. The regent intended to send a series of letters to Amalien, addressing various topics, including the Antiquarian Circle, [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]], and broader cultural matters. During her participation in the interview series, Lady Karinwe shared valuable insights into Corelanyan history and traditions. She spoke at length about the spiritual symbolism of the [[Lore:Three Queens|Three Queens]] and their connection to the movements of the [[Lore:Sun|sun]]. She also touched upon the deep religious debates throughout her House's history, acknowledging that those who see the hand of an [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] in the acts of kindness of the Three Queens would not be alone. Karinwe further described how the worship of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] was eventually replaced by that of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]] following the fall of [[Lore:Iniel Corelanya|Iniel]], reflecting the Corelanyan belief in the cyclical nature of history. She explored the complex and layered relationships among the Three Queens, as interpreted by Corelanyan clergy, and did not shy away from discussing the complex and sometimes tense history between the Corelanyans and the two Solstice [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] tribes: the [[Lore:Tide-Born Tribe|Tide-Born]] and the [[Lore:Stone-Nest Tribe|Stone-Nest]].{{Ref|name=LA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Stirk Fellowship & Solstice}}}}
