[[Lore:Namira|Namira]] is a [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] whose sphere is the ancient [[Lore:Void|Darkness]]. She is also known as the '''Daedric Prince of Hunger''',{{ref|name=DFNamira|NPC [[DF:Namira's_Quest|dialogue]] about Namira's worshippers in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} the '''Mistress of Decay''' or '''Lady of Decay''',{{ref|name=ESONMOD}}{{ref|name=ESONexith|[[ON:Tree-Minder Nexith|Tree-Minder Nexith]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Deep Disturbance|Deep Disturbance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|293}} the '''Spirit Daedra''',{{ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Book of Daedra}}}} and the '''Goddess of the Dark'''.{{ref|name=ALBAB|{{Cite Book|A Life Barbaric and Brutal}}}} Namira is the ruler of sundry dark and shadowy spirits, and is often associated with [[Lore:Slug|slugs]], [[Lore:Spider|spiders]], and other things that inspire mortals with instinctive revulsion.{{ref|name=TBOD}} The symbol of a [[Lore:Bat|bat]] is featured on her emblem. The Khajiit believe that all creatures who feed on rotten flesh are her spies.{{ref|name=TDAS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}} She is the Daedric Prince of spirits and shadows, as well as the patron of vermin and squalor.{{ref|name=ESONexith}} Namira also appears to be associated with beggars and the beggaring gifts of disease, pity, and disregard.{{ref|name=BP|{{Cite Book|Beggar Prince}}}} Namira and her shadowy endeavors are often recognized to bear some association with eternity.{{ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Invocation}}}} Somewhat in line with this, the Khajiit believe that she is a spirit of infinite realms.{{ref|name=TDAS}} Namira sees herself as the rightful ruler of all spirits.{{ref|name=AON}} In some culture's creation stories, she is responsible for corrupting [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]],{{ref|name=ESOFDF|{{Cite Book|The Favored Daughter of Fadomai}}}} or making a deal with him during his endeavor to create [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} 

Mortals who become ensnared by this spirit are tortured until they forget who they were and know only Namira. Ancient Khajiit knew her as an '''[[Lore:Ur-dra|Ur-dra]]''',{{ref|name=TDAS}}{{ref|name=ESOFDF}} a term also used to describe [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]].{{ref|name=AIOS|[[Online:Aios|Aios]]' dialogue in [[Online:DLC#Clockwork City|ESO: Clockwork City]]}} It implies that the owner of the title is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes".{{ref|name=Lilatha|[[ON:Lilatha|Lilatha]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] is similarly called the Ur-Daedra{{ref|name=SOIA|{{Cite Book|Secrets Overheard in Apocrypha}}}} who is "elder than Ada".{{ref|name=ESOFAHM|{{Cite Book|Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus}}}} Namira's followers and the priesthood of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] have clashed in the past.{{ref|name=OBNamira|Events of [[Oblivion:Namira|Namira]]'s quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}{{ref|name=SRTaste|[[Skyrim:The Taste of Death|The Taste of Death]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Namira counts [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]],{{ref|name=AC|{{Cite Book|Azurah's Crossing}}}} the [[Lore:Twilight Cantors|Twilight Cantors]],{{ref|name=TRAH|{{Cite Book|Twilight Rites and Hymns}}}} [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]]{{Ref|name=ESOCrossroads|Events of [[Online:Chiaroscuro Crossroads|Chiaroscuro Crossroads]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} and [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]] among her enemies.{{ref|name=DFFactions|[[Daggerfall:Oblivion|Oblivion Faction]] data in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} Her summoning day is [[Lore:Marukh's Day|Marukh's Day]], the 9<sup>th</sup> of Second Seed.{{ref|name=DFHolidays|[[Daggerfall:Holidays|Holidays]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

Namira is occasionally depicted in statuary as a long-haired woman wearing robes, typically flanked by a scrawny (occasionally humanoid) little beast.{{ref|name=OBNamira}} Other depictions portray Namira as a cloaked figure with a slug-like body and a skeletal face and torso, with many spiny arthropod legs.{{ref|name=ESONMOD|[[ON:Namira, Mistress of Decay|Namira, Mistress of Decay]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Religion and Culture==
[[File:ON-concept-Prince-Namira-emblem.png|thumb|right|An emblem representing Namira]]
Several [[Lore:Reachman|Reachman]] clans pay reverence to Namira.{{ref|name=TIEGTTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} Some tribes call Namira the '''Children's God''' because she is the mistress of small pests.{{ref|name=ALBAB}} To the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]], she is known as '''Namiira''',{{ref|name=ONVastaire|[[Online:Vastarie|Vastarie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Great Darkness''', '''The Eldest Spirit''', and '''The Void'''.{{ref|name=TDAS}} 

===Khajiit===
[[File:LG-cardart-Dro-m'Athra Reaper.jpg|thumb|left|A dro-m'Athra]]
In Khajiit mythology, it is said that [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]] fled from [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]] into the [[Lore:Void|Great Darkness]] to birth her youngest child, [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], and the Great Darkness filled his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart]]. The Great Darkness then knew its name was Namiira.{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} When [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]] approached Lorkhaj, whom she asked to create for her children a dwelling; he did so, and yet the Great Darkness in his heart influenced him to deceive his siblings so that they were trapped in the new place with Nirni. Some managed to escape death and become the stars, and those who remained punished Lorkhaj by tearing out his heart and hiding it deep within Nirni, so that he would be with her whom he had done the most harm.{{ref|name=WOCMA}} 

Pre-[[Lore:Riddle'Thar Epiphany|ri'Datta]] texts state Lorkhaj survived and fled to Azurah with a wound in his chest, where she saw Namiira dwelling within as a corruption resembling a heart.  Azurah cleansed Lorkhaj and flung his [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]] into the Void, and Lorkhaj perished within Azurah's embrace.{{ref|name=ESOFDF}} From this false heart came the twisted shade of Lorkhaj known as the Moon Beast, first of the [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|dro-m'Athra]]. And so Lorkhaj represents the duality of Khajiiti souls and their susceptibility toward the spiritual corruption of the Bent Dance.{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}{{Ref|name=TDAS}} Y'ffer was corrupted by the Great Darkness sometime after Lorkhaj's death, and murdered Nirni. [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], Azurah and Khenarthi slew him in retaliation for the deed, then built a [[Lore:Earthbones|cairn]] for Nirni using his bones.{{Ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Worldly Spirits}}}} 

Namiira is considered an enemy of the living, and is placated rather than worshiped.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel}}}} She serves as the antithesis to [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]]'s role for Khajiiti spirits, preventing them from reaching [[Lore:Llesw'er|Llesw'er]] by corrupting them into dro-m'Athra via the Bent Dance, so that they may be dragged down into the Dark Behind the World. These spirits occasionally slip through the cracks in Nirni, and tempt Khajiit so their souls can be sent to the Dark to serve Namiira.{{ref|name=ESOLMD|{{Cite Book|Litter-Mates of Darkness}}}} Giant spiders and hoarvors also tend to spill out from the Dark Behind the World in places where the Great Darkness' presence is pervasive.{{ref|name=ESOHurz|[[ON:Hurzarr|Hurzarr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Dragonhold|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=|The presence of hoarvors and spiders on the path to the Wall of Life prior to completion of [[ON:Chiaroscuro Crossroads|Chiaroscuro Crossroads]] in [[Online:Dragonhold|ESO]]}} An unusual increase in the presence of [[Lore:Weevil|weevils]], [[Lore:Locust|locusts]], slugs and other bugs harmful to crops is a sign that a source of Namiira's corruption is nearby.{{ref|name=ESOFa|[[ON:Fa'zin|Fa'zin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}

===Reachmen===
[[File:ON-concept-Reachfolk Namira.png|thumb|left|Namira's Reachfolk Symbol|class=backdrop]][[File:ON-creature-Voidmother.jpg|thumb|right|A Voidmother]]
Namira, (also spelled '''Nimeria''' in the ancient [[Lore:Reach-tongue|Reachfolk language]]){{ref|name=FTR|{{Cite Book|Fynboar the Resurrected}}}} who is known as the '''Spirit Queen''', the '''Goddess of Death''',{{ref|name=GSR3|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 3}}}} and the '''Black Fly''',{{ref|name=ESORWC|{{Cite Book|Reach Witch Chant}}}} is a revered spirit in the Reachman pantheon that represents darkness, endings, and rebirth.{{ref|name=ESOND|{{Cite Book|Namira's Dance}}}} She is seen as the sovereign of the infinite world of spirit, one of the two worlds of existence.{{ref|name=GSR3}} The Reachfolk creation story speaks of [[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]] having an epiphany when he visited the darkness; that which is perceived as nothingness is ripe for possibility.{{ref|[[Online:The Dark Descent|The Dark Descent]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} And so, Lorkh approached Namira and convinced her to grant him a place in the infinite void to create a realm for wayward spirits, but it was not without a cost. Lorkh sacrificed himself to create a harsh realm, one that is unforgiving and intended to teach through suffering.{{ref|name=GSR5}} Hircine took the mantle of Lorkh's creation, becoming the sovereign the second world: the realm of flesh.{{ref|name=GSR3}} 

Hircine and Namira's roles complement each other. While Reachfolk dwell in the world of the living, Hircine is a guide that helps them navigate Nirn's hardships.{{ref|name=ONArdanir|[[Online:Ardanir|Ardanir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Upon death, Namira acts as a psychopomp for Reachman souls that gives and takes lives, until their spirits are enlightened. Thus, Namira is seen as the avatar of primal dualisms, such as beginnings and ends, which stem from her world of spirit.{{ref|name=GSR3}} At the "[[Lore:Kalpa|end of all days]]", Hircine will fight alongside the Reachmen,{{ref|name=ONHSG|[[Online:High Shaman Glynroch|High Shaman Glynroch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and Lorkh's Dark Heart will beat again, reawakened by feeding on the deaths of mortals, and its darkness will spread from its depths to consume everything, sparing only Namira's faithful.{{ref|name=ONTAD|[[Online:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Awakening Darkness|The Awakening Darkness]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} 

Celebrations and rituals to Namira vary per clan. The entire tribe of the [[Lore:Boldclaw Clan|Boldclaw clan]] participate in Namira's Dance around a large bonfire, which honors life and death. Those that dance naked represent how they are born into the world, while those who wear dark colors and paint represent death. Blood is smeared across various tribesmen during the dance.{{ref|name=ESOND}} Some clans may look to darker avenues to appease Namira, such as human sacrifices. Those that undergo such practices may seek to twist and abuse Namira's gifts to gain power, which when wielded, can cause great calamities.{{ref|name=ONGwenyfe|[[Online:Gwenyfe|Gwenyfe]]'s dialogue on [[Online:Voidmother Elgroalif|Voidmother Elgroalif]] related quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This brings out dark corruptions known as "voidstuff", which can empower a clan's witch, turning them into Voidmothers (also known as Void [[Lore:Lich|Liches]]), which can consume the souls of their victims.{{ref|name=ONGRitesMatron|[[Online:Rites Matron|Rites Matron]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They are considered a type of [[Lore:Shade|Shade]]: dark, hateful, and hungry spirits that have been twisted by contact with Namira's energy.{{ref|name=ONGVerandis|[[Online:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Worship===
{{Quote Box|''[Khenarthi has] forsaken you. But I haven't. There's a place in the Dark for all you cast off, forgotten souls.''|Namiira{{ref|name=CCSON}}}}
Namira can be worshiped in a variety of different ways depending on the cult's motive. Simply living in darkness and squalor is enough in some cases.{{ref|name=OBNQ|Namira's dialogue during [[OB:Namira|Namira's quest]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Others practice ritual murder and cannibalism to honor Namira.{{ref|name=SRTaste}} Namira does not consider the practice of mortals eating [[Lore:Ogre|ogres]] to be cannibalism, and would be offended if one of her followers did such a thing while touting it as such.{{ref|name=ESOOrzorga|[[ON:Orzorga|Orzorga]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Thicker Than Water|Thicker Than Water]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Namira's worshipers are rarely open about their beliefs, as they would be viewed as dangerous and perverse by society. They often live normal lives, working standard jobs and socializing with fellow civilians as anyone else would, and organizing into secret covens to hide their unconventional appetites.{{ref|name=SRTaste}} 

Namira's followers keep to themselves, some preferring to live peacefully in dark and squalid conditions. Those who devote their full time to Namira can be found congregating around her shrines or forming colonies, such as the Forgotten Ones, deep within abandoned ruins.{{ref|name=OBNamira}} The Forgotten Ones were so accustomed to the dark, they would recoil from the light. They also reacted violently at any attempts to "save" them from their lifestyle.{{ref|name=OBForgotten|The [[OB:Forgotten One|Forgotten One]]s' dialogue and behavior toward torchlight in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}{{ref|name=NamiraOblivion}} Some of Namira's cults (such as Namira's Forgotten) require prospective members to consume human flesh in order to join.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Picnic at Pelin (A Horror Story)}}}}

[[File:LG-cardart-Namira's Shrine 02.jpg|Namira's Shrine in ''Legends''|thumb|right]]
Namira's followers tend to congregate in dark or filthy places, reveling in their misery and the darkness.{{ref|name=NamiraOblivion|Events of [[Oblivion:Namira|Namira's quest]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBNQ}} Those who are ostracized by others may be drawn to worship Namira.{{ref|name=SRHogniConvo|[[SR:Hogni Red-Arm|Hogni Red-Arm]]'s [[SR:Markarth|Markarth]] market conversations in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Her worshipers are more inclined to have unsanitary habits, such as a tendency to not wash their hands before handling meat.{{ref|name=SRHogni|[[SR:Hogni Red-Arm|Hogni Red-Arm]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Cannibals also tend to worship Namira. A life in Namira's service is said to bring bleak and horrifying side-effects.{{ref|name=ESONamira's Bile|[[Online:Namira's Bile|Namira's Bile]] collectible description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Crow-Wife Clan|Crow-Wife Clan]] of Reachmen worships Namira. At every [[Lore:Dark Moon|two-moons'-dark]], the clan draws lots at random to select a child from the clan, be it kin or slave, to be sacrificed. The sacrifice is set upon the Ever-Oozing Altar, where its heart is cut out by the clan's hagraven matriarch and offered to Namira.{{ref|name=ALBAB}} In addition to these regular sacrifices, children the matriarch deems too weak are also sacrificed to Namira.{{ref|name=OTNOR|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Reachmen}}}} The Crow-Wife Clan also practices live burnings, bathing in blood and engaging in raucous ritual dismemberment to please Namira.{{ref|name=OTNOR}} When the tribe calls to Namira, writhing masses of [[Lore:Centipede|centipedes]], [[Lore:Roach|roaches]] and other squirming creatures form a thick carpet on the floor of the clan's ritual hut. Members of the clan sometimes pluck these creatures off the ground and eat them during worship.{{ref|name=OTNOR}} The {{Lore Link|Hagfeather Coven}} of western [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]], a subset of the [[Lore:Glenmoril Wyrd|Glenmoril Wyrd]], also reveres Namira.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Glenmoril Wyrd}}}}

The witches of the [[Lore:Ghostsong Clan|Ghostsong Clan]] witches have historically spoken directly and often with Namira. Namira personally chose the clan's foremothers to keep dark, powerful secrets, such as the Prophecy of the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]]. She led the first Ghostsong matron to a bottomless pit deep beneath [[Lore:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]] called the Dark Descent. In ancient times, the Reachfolk honored Namira with blood sacrifice, believing death pleased her; death is the greatest, most permanent gift a mortal can give. Their beliefs held that Namira granted them power in exchange for the souls they sent to her realm.{{ref|name=ONTAD}} 

Namira is the spirit of death and decay. As such, using her energy and gifts to avoid dying a natural death is an affront to the Daedric Prince.{{ref|[[Online:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue at the end of [[Online:A Feast of Souls|A Feast of Souls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

The [[Lore:Night Knives|Night Knives]] were a minor Namira cult situated in Western Skyrim.{{ref|name=TMA|[[ON:Trifold Mirror of Alternatives|Trifold Mirror of Alternatives]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

===Appeal===
{{Quote Box|style=float:left; max-width:330px; margin-left:2em; margin-right:2em|''Poach the blind, the sickly, and the lame. They belong with us.<br>Let the beat fill their ears and our venom fill their hearts.<br>We are outcast no longer!''|Quotes from the dro-m'Athra, servants of Namiira{{ref|name=DAD|[[ON:Dro-m'Athra|Dro-m'Athra]] [[ON:Dro-m'Athra Dialogue|Ambient Dialogue]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}}}
Namira appeals to those who are shunned by society. Those who feel out of place, are ugly, wretched, or have been ostracized for one reason or another often flock to Namira. Some of her worshipers revile beautiful people.{{ref|name=OBHjolfrodi|[[OB:Hjolfrodi the Harrier|Hjolfrodi the Harrier]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} Grief, hopelessness, and loss of faith are traits which Namira can easily take advantage of; someone who feels abandoned by the gods may be approached by Namira in their darkest hour. She seduces lonely individuals with the promise of power or a sense of belonging, drawing them into her grasp when they are most vulnerable.{{ref|name=CCSON|The [[ON:Shadow of Namiira|Shadow of Namiira]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Chiaroscuro Crossroads|Chiaroscuro Crossroads]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} 

In Elsweyr, Namiira is revered by the jealous, angered, and maligned.{{ref|name=TIEGTTE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}} Those who are prideful, power-hungry or hateful may easily find themselves swayed toward the Great Darkness.{{ref|name=ESOMazza-Mirri|[[ON:Mazza-Mirri|Mazza-Mirri]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Path of the Hidden Moon|Path of the Hidden Moon]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Those who find joy in violence or are prone to it are also susceptible to Namiira's influence.{{ref|name=ESOShando-ri|[[ON:Shando-ri|Shando-ri]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Path of the Hidden Moon|Path of the Hidden Moon]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} This fact is very apparent in the dro-m'Athra, who tend to be cynical,{{ref|name=ESOKulan-dro|[[ON:Kulan-dro|Kulan-dro]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Into the Maw|Into the Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} hateful and miserable creatures.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai2|[[ON:Adara'hai|Adara'hai]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Chiaroscuro Crossroads|Chiaroscuro Crossroads]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}

==History==
===First Era===
In the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], two [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]i mages that worshipped Namira created a {{Lore Link|spellplague}}, which was to be used as a weapon against the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]. It can be likened to a disease that weakens and saps one's life force. The spellplague acts quickly, and its effects are fatal. Those afflicted by the curse do not simply die; their souls return as dire apparitions.{{ref|name=ESOBeron|[[ON:Beron Telvanni|Beron Telvanni]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Curse of Heimlyn Keep|The Curse of Heimlyn Keep]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} When it became apparent that the spellplague was too powerful to be controlled, the Chimeri Lords sealed both the plague and its creators into a warded vault, which [[Lore:Heimlyn Keep|Heimlyn Keep]] would be built around. [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] was responsible for watching over the reliquary which the weapon was bound in.{{ref|name=ESOBeron}} The spellplague and its creators were sealed for ages, falling into obscurity.{{Ref|name=ONVildras|[[Online:Keeper Vildras|Keeper Vildras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

It is unknown if the story of the [[Lore:Beggar Prince|Beggar Prince]] is fact or fiction, but it takes place sometime in the First Era. It speaks of a person by the name of Wheedle who was the distant 13th child of a king in Valenwood, and thus was not entitled to any riches. Wheedle sought to carve their own fortune and glory and set out to distant lands. After a few days, they encountered a group of three men about to slay a diseased beggar, and Wheedle confronted the men and "saved" her. The beggar revealed herself to be Namira, and Wheedle, knowing her name saw an opportunity. For 33 days and nights, the adventurer pleaded with the Prince for an apprenticeship until they physically could no longer do so. Unbeknownst to Wheedle, Namira was testing them, and they passed. The Daedric Prince bestowed upon Wheedle the gift of pity. To benefit from it, Wheedle was required to always have at least one physically visible disease that could be changed at will. And so Wheedle became known as the Prince of Beggars, irresistible for charity, and often disregarded by people who would talk about important secrets within hearing distance. Upon the story's conclusion, the author insists that beggars are said to know all the secrets and the daily routines of their cities' citizens.{{ref|name=BP}}

===Second Era===
The curse of the spellplague resurfaced in {{Year|2E 582}}. The [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], thinking Heimlyn Keep a military base, opened the reliquary and set the spellplague free. Its mystical corruption emanated from the Telvanni reliquary, killing soldier, mage and civilian alike, and flooding the keep's grounds with angry spirits.{{ref|name=ESOBeron}}{{Ref|name=ONTCOHK|Events of [[ON:The Curse of Heimlyn Keep|The Curse of Heimlyn Keep]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The curse was broken before it could spread past Heimlyn Keep.{{Ref|name=ONUndone|Events of [[ON:What Was Done Must Be Undone|What Was Done Must Be Undone]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-concept-Maw of Lorkhaj.png|thumb|left|The seals in the Temple of Seven Riddles' High Lunarium]]
That same year, Namira attempted to extend her influence over the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] village of {{Lore Link|Xal-Ithix}}, a community of scavengers.{{ref|name=ESONexith}} Xal Ithix lies downstream from other communities. Refuse, garbage and scraps often float downstream. The village's general philosophy was to repurpose anything they could find a use for, including the armor found on corpses that float downstream.{{ref|name=WFD|{{Cite Book|What Flows Downstream|ns_base=Online}}}} In this fetid yet peaceful corner of the swamp, Namira's servants placed a relic called the Hand of Namira at the base of Xal-Ithix's [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] tree. The relic poisoned the swamp, aggravating the [[Lore:Hoarvor|hoarvors]] and [[Lore:Strangler|stranglers]] which usually left the Argonians well alone. Namira's goal was to attack the Hist tree, the heart, soul and memories of the local Argonian community.{{ref|name=ESONexith}} Namira was determined to show the locals that the spirits of their Hist are "pale shadows" compared to her, believing that she is the rightful ruler of their spirits.{{ref|name=AON|The [[ON:Aspect of Namira|Aspect of Namira]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Deep Disturbance|Deep Disturbance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} As the Hand weakened the Hist tree, Namira's servants performed a ritual to corrupt Xal-Ithix and claim it for Namira. [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]] aided the village's tree-minder by using the Hand to stop the ritual and end Namira's plot.{{ref|name=DD|Events of [[ON:Deep Disturbance|Deep Disturbance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Namiira has a history with the {{Lore Link|Temple of Seven Riddles}} in [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]]. The temple was purportedly built too tall&mdash;as the Clan Mothers say, "Khajiit aren't meant to stand so close to the Ja-Kha'jay before the dead-climb".{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai}} The dro-m'Athra have emerged at the Temple of Seven Riddles in part because of this factor,{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai}} but there is also a story told among the {{Lore Link|Doubting Monks}} which recounts the first instance of dro-m'Athra invading the Temple.

{{Quote Box|''Excuse my informality, but I feel like I know you, I recognize your craving as if it were my own. You want to control, and the thought makes you slaver like a starving wolf. I can fill your belly, but first, I need to know. Will you kill in order to fill this void?''|Namira{{ref|name=DFNQ|Namira's dialogue during [[DF:Namira's Quest|Namira's Quest]] in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}}}
Long ago, a Moon-Bishop referred to as the Stone-Tapper resided within the Temple of Seven Riddles. He carried a magic stick that told him secrets when he tapped it on the floor.{{ref|name=VazJou|{{Cite Book|Vazshara's Journal}}}} Day and night, the Bishop tapped the floor, learning all of Nirni's secrets. Eventually, the magic stick ran out of Nirni secrets, so it started telling him darker secrets: Namiira's secrets. The Tapper listened, growing old and crazy. The temple's monks went mad as well because the tapping never stopped. It was a loud and steady sound, like a heartbeat. Eventually, the monks plotted to kill the Stone-Tapper. They lured him to the High Lunarium and stabbed him. All the blood and secrets drained out of the Tapper's body and broke the Temple asunder, revealing the [[Lore:Maw of Lorkhaj|Maw of Lorkhaj]].{{ref|name=VazJou}}

The next part of the story varies depending on who's telling the tale; in some versions, the Tapper awakens when his blood is drained and kills the monks with lightning. In another variation of the tale, a great winged beast bursts out of Lorkhaj's throat and eviscerates the monks.{{ref|name=VazJou}} Either way, the dro-m'Athra were unleashed upon the Temple. However the Stone Tapper's murderers were killed, the hole between Nirni and the Dark Behind the World was eventually closed. Seals were erected within the temple to keep the dro-m'Athra at bay, depicting the Moon-Bishop flanked by monks, summoning bent spirits.{{ref|name=VazJou}} These seals did not hold forever.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai}} 

[[File:ON-quest-Chiaroscuro Crossroads.jpg|thumb|left|The corrupted Wall of Life]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, Namiira's gaze was fixed upon the temple. The Temple's abbot, Kulan-dro, heard the beating of Lorkhaj's Heart. He was driven to defile the protective seals, allowing Namiira's brood to assault the Temple and the monks within to be corrupted by the Bent Dance.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai}} With the aid of the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], the [[Lore:Twilight Cantors|Twilight Cantors]] drove back the dro-m'Athra and confronted the temple's abbot.{{ref|name=ItM|Events of [[ON:Into the Maw|Into the Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Reminiscent of one of the endings to the Stone-Tapper's story, a large winged dro-m'Athra named {{Lore Link|Rakkhat}} tore free from the Maw of Lorkhaj and attacked those who sought to close the breach. Rakkhat was put down by the Undaunted, and the Twilight Cantors resealed the Temple.{{ref|name=ItM}}

Namiira struck again later that year in [[Lore:Black Heights|Black Heights]]. The Wall of Life, an ancient mural that is part of the village's death rite, was tainted by the dark magic of Namiira. Years prior, the [[Lore:Dagi|Dagi]] painter, {{Lore Link|Dak'radhi}}, became vulnerable to Namiira's advances when the death of his wife shook his faith in Khenarthi. Namiira convinced the painter that Khenarthi had abandoned Dak'radhi, his wife, and their terminally ill son, whom Dak'radhi was left alone to care for. Namiira commanded him to mix a special paint using ritual components of her choosing, which he would use to leave his mark on the Wall of Life when his time came to pass from Nirni. Upon his death, Dak'radhi was transformed into a dro-m'Athra. Many years later, his influence began to seep into the Wall of Life, allowing the Great Darkness to rise from the paint he laid.{{Ref|name=ESOCrossroads}} From then on, villagers who came to leave their mark upon the wall of life were taken by Namiira instead of Khenarthi.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai2}} A Twilight Cantor visited Black Heights, purging Dak'radhi's influence from the Wall of Life with [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]]'s aid.{{Ref|name=ESOCrossroads}}

===Third Era===
In {{Year|3E 405}}, Namira tasked an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]] with slaying a [[Lore:Vampire|vampire ancient]] that had fallen out of favor with the Prince.{{ref|name=NamiraDaggerfall|Events of [[Daggerfall:Namira's Quest|Namira's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] was tasked by Namira to kill a group of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] priests who were attempting to convert a group of her followers which resided in the ancient [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruin of [[Lore:Anga|Anga]].{{ref|name=NamiraOblivion}} In both of these instances, the Prince awarded her champion with the Ring of Namira.

===Fourth Era===
In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was tasked by Namira's cult with bringing a priest of Arkay to Namira's shrine in [[Lore:Reachcliff Cave|Reachcliff Cave]], then killing and eating him.{{ref|name=NamiraSkyrim|Events of [[Skyrim:The Taste of Death|The Taste of Death]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Last Dragonborn would be rewarded with the Ring of Namira for completing this task.

===Hand of Namira===
The Hand of Namira is a rotting hand that corrupts everything in its general vicinity. The Dremora who serve Namira are terrified of the relic, as its energies can be turned against them to destroy their physical forms.{{ref|name=ESONexith}} Under the Hand's influence, creatures act irrationally, and may go out of their way to attack people.{{ref|name=ESOSharp-Eye|[[ON:Sharp-Eye (Argonian)|Sharp-Eye]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Deep Disturbance|Deep Disturbance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Servants===
[[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] have been seen serving Namira.{{ref|name=DD}} Among Namira's servants, the dro-m'Athra are the most unique. {{Lore:Dro-m'Athra}}

The dro-m'Athra speak of anguish and despair. They seek to cause pain and drag others into the Great Darkness so they may share in the dro-m'Athra's suffering.{{ref|name=ESOSuthayTwins|[[ON:Vashai|Vashai]] and [[ON:S'kinrai|S'kinrai]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Into the Maw|Into the Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESORakkhat|[[ON:Rakkhat|Rakkhat]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The darkness within the dro-m'Athra drives them to harm others; to unleash their hatred and pain upon True Cats, and force them to hear Namiira's will.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai2}} 

Some of Namiira's most dangerous servants are liars: those who sow the seeds of doubt and attempt to turn mortals against each other.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai|[[ON:Adara'hai|Adara'hai]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Into the Maw|Into the Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Even more dangerous are those who do not know that they are Bent. These are the souls whom Namiira tricks into joining her in the Darkness by playing on their fears, grief and doubts. If Namiira promises sanctuary to one who is hurt, and that individual is convinced that dragging their loved one to the Great Darkness would end their suffering, nothing would stop them from trying to bring that person into Namiira's grasp.{{ref|name=ESOAdara'hai2}}{{ref|name=ESODak'radhi|[[ON:Dak'radhi|Dak'radhi]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Chiaroscuro Crossroads|Chiaroscuro Crossroads]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}
