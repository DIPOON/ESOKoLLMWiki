[[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] (translated as ''"Above the [[Lore:Karth River|Karth]]"'' in the Reach language, also known as '''Mar-Karth'''{{Ref|name=HOMASIS|{{Cite Book|History of Markarth: A Story in Stone}}}} and also called '''Markarth Side'''){{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Markarth Side|Markarth Side]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} is one of the nine major cities in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]],{{Ref|name=OBMap|[[:File:LO-map-Skyrim (Oblivion Codex).jpg|Map of Skyrim — Pocket Guide to the Empire (3rd Edition)]]}}{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:MER-art-Skyrim Anthology Map.jpg|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} found in the river valley of [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]]. Known colloquially as the ''"City of Stone,"'' it is a former [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwarven]] city built through a large stone crag, intricately designed around the cliffs and waterfalls from the environment. Since the region is poor farmland, Markarth is mainly a mining-town that has a vast influx of [[Lore:Silver|silver]] veins in the mountain.{{Ref|name=TCOS|{{Cite Book|The City of Stone}}}} The city is the focal point of power throughout the region. It is often disputed over between the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] and the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]].{{Ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} Markarth was founded on the abandoned remains of the radiant city, '''[[Lore:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]]'''.{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}}<noinclude>

==Layout & Geography==
[[File:SR-place-Cidhna Mine.jpg|thumb|right|The Cidhna Mines]]
[[File:ON-place-Markarth 02.jpg|thumb|left|Markarth under Reachman rule]]
Markarth is one of the larger cities in Skyrim, nestled in the river valley of the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]]. It is unique in that the city's layout is intricately built into the mountain of [[Lore:Karthmad|Karthmad]],{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}} dividing the city into two districts. The northern end, Dryside has streets that ascend above each other with well-refined homes, below the main square and marketplace where the populace tend to congregate. The city's southern end is the Riverside, the basin populated by the lower-class and the bustling silver mines. These districts meet each other at either end of the city, at the main gates on the east side, while on the west side, they ascend high towards the top of the crag and at [[Lore:Understone Keep|Understone Keep]], which contains the Mournful Throne, the seat of power in the Reach.{{Ref|name=TCOS}}

The precipices between the keep and the Riverside contain the city's smithy and other important places, like the local crypt and the Markarth Ruins.{{Ref|name=TCOS}} There is an ornate temple on the highest peak of the crag that has been used for different deities. It has been known as [[Lore:Shrine of the Hunt-Father|Shrine of the Hunt Father]] and used by the cult of [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] during the Reach's independence in the Second Era, whereas, under the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], it contained the local chapter of the [[Lore:House of Dibella|House of Dibella]]. Underneath the crag are the slums of Markarth, known as the Warrens, which is a collapsed hallway with campfires bearing minimal light.{{Ref|name=TCOS}} During Reachfolk rule of the city Warens were not in ruin and known as the Underhall and functioned as hub of commerce and craftsmanship.{{ref|name=Underhall|[[ON:Underhall|Underhall]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Markarth Side is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Dragon Bridge|Dragon Bridge]] to the north and [[Lore:Snowhawk|Snowhawk]] to the south.{{Ref|name=MapArena|[[:File:AR-map-Skyrim towns.png|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:SR-interior-Nchuand-Zel.jpg|thumb|right|Nchuand-Zel]]
:''For information on the Dwemer settlement, go to: '''[[Lore:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]]'''''
Markarth's earliest history dates back to the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] when it was known as Nchuand-Zel, a prosperous [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] stronghold that was built into the mountain [[Lore:Karthmad|Karthmad]]. It was well known for its formidable defenses and great storage halls.{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}} Like many other Dwemer cities in Skyrim, it was sacked during the campaigns of High King [[Lore:Gellir|Gellir]],{{Ref|name=TAW|{{Cite Book|The Aetherium Wars}}}} and it was also conquered by [[Lore:Ryain Direnni|Ryain Direnni]] and his legions sometime during the {{Lore Link|Alessian Reforms}},{{Ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} but it was not completely abandoned until {{Year|1E 700}} when the entire Dwemer race disappeared from existence. A few years after their disappearance, the local tribes of Reachmen took refuge in the confines of the stronghold during times of seasonal hardship. By {{Year|1E 930}}, the community developed into a fully-fledged settlement that was active all year round. They called it "Mar-Karth," and the strongest chieftain that lived in the town was known as the "Ard," known in the Reach tongue as the "King of the Fort." At that point, Markarth was an occupied ruin with huts made out of animal hide and decorated with rough furs.{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}}

One of the goods exported by Markarth early on was granite mined from its quarries. Evermore Palace in the [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] city of [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], which was founded by the [[Lore:River Horse Bretons|River Horse Bretons]] in {{Year|1E 983}},{{ref|name=TRHoKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}} was built out of Markarth granite.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}}

===Markarth & the Empires of Cyrodiil===
[[File:SR-creature-Red Eagle.jpg|thumb|left|Faolan the Red Eagle]]
[[File:SR-place-Sky Haven Temple Exterior.jpg|thumb|right|Sky Haven Temple]]
Markarth had their first encounters with the Empires of Cyrodiil when Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] attempted to conquer the Reach in {{Year|1E 1033}}. Their first target was Markarth, the largest settlement in the region and so they advanced forward with the iron might of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]]. The Ard of Markarth fiercely defended the stronghold, but the Empire's well-trained tacticians proved to overcome the disorderly and unorganized Reach clans. The city had fallen to the Empire and some of the Ard's greatest warriors chose death than to admit defeat. During the year-long campaign, Imperial-Markarth was sacked relentlessly by the rebellious Reachfolk of the Red Eagle, [[Lore:Faolan|Faolan]], but his forces were never able to reclaim the city, and it was because of that, Markarth was able to flourish as a city for the rest of the Empire's lifespan. The Empire had fallen in {{Year|1E 2331}} after the [[Lore:War of Righteousness|War of Righteousness]] had ended.{{Ref|name=PGE1EC|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} And by that point, Markarth's storerooms were converted into grand halls, homes, and workshops, and the city has taken on the appearance it is known for today. However, no one was ever able to improve on the ancient Dwemeri architecture.{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}}

By the time of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], Markarth had been under the control of the Nords of Skyrim. They were faced with aggression from Emperor [[Lore:Kastav|Kastav]] in {{Year|1E 2801}}, who ordered the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] to seize hostages from Markarth and [[Lore:Hroldan|Hroldan]], so that their Jarls would meet their conscription quotas. Situations like this had hurt the public perception of the Dragonguard and the Empire,{{Ref|name=AOTD|{{Cite Book|Annals of the Dragonguard}}}} however, it was mended with [[Lore:Reman II|Reman II]]'s reign.{{Ref|name=RIITLOA|{{Cite Book|Reman II: The Limits of Ambition}}}} After the years of the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]] and the start of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], Markarth had fallen into obscurity and it became a dangerous place for travel and the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Legion garrison]] that was present. [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] Barons and Nord Jarls continuously sieged Markarth, but the city had always come out intact. Eventually, the Reach had claimed independence and took Markarth as their capital. Tales of the Reach's rule over Markarth have scarcely made out to the rest of the world, as the Reachmen went back to their hostile ways of trade and travel.{{Ref|name=HOMASIS}}

===The Black Drakes of the Reach & the Ard of Markarth===
[[File:ON-npc-Ard Caddach.jpg|thumb|right|Ard Caddach of Markarth]]
[[File:ON-npc-Emperor Leovic.jpg|thumb|left|Emperor Leovic of Clan Blackdrake]]
In {{Year|2E 533}}, the warlord, [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] the Black Drake gathered a massive horde of Reach warriors and descended onto [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], where he claimed the Ruby Throne and proclaimed himself the [[Lore:Tamrielic Emperors|Emperor]]. It was through this, the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Dynasty]] had ruled over the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and Markarth return as a sort of Imperial authority. The Reach warlords brought many plundered treasures and captives from the battlefront back home and inspired many more Reachfolk to leave home for glory. The trade route between Markarth became intensely active and it brought the city into a new age of prosperity. {{Ref|name=HOMASIS}} When Emperor [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]] felt that he needed to apply authority in the Reach, he entrusted a cousin of the family, [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]] of the [[Lore:Blackdrake Clan|Blackdrake Clan]] as the Governor of Markarth. Despite this, Moricar limited Caddach's reach of power to just the Markarth Region, leaving the Reach Clans to their own throughout the rest of the Reach. When Emperor [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] was overthrown, however, Caddach went ahead and claimed absolute supremacy over the Reach and took upon the ancient title of Ard for himself. Next, he purged or exiled the rest of his clan so that no one would challenge his authority.{{Ref|name=ROTDOM|{{Cite Book|Report on the Despot of Markarth}}}}

Ard Caddach claimed that the Reach was his tribe, and as long as he can rely on the tribes for assistance and arms, he did not govern them nor interfere in their politics. In his five year tenure, the clans have served his court, sometimes begrudgingly, and not a single one of them had challenged his authority. To his enemies and outsiders, he was known as the ''"Despot of Markarth,"'' an epithet that is not entirely accurate, but he was by no means a tyrant.{{Ref|name=ONBelain|[[Online:Lady Belain|Lady Belain]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} Caddach uses the knowledge from his time studying in the Imperial City to turn Markarth into a modern-day functioning state that the Reachmen had never seen.{{Ref|name=ROTDOM}}

The law in Markarth was enforced and factions such as [[Lore:Stonehands|Stonehands]] and [[Lore:Markarth Watch|Markarth Watch]] were responsible for peacekeeping and crime investigation.{{Ref|name=ROTDOM}}{{ref|[[ON:Black Mask Bear-Dog|Black Mask Bear-Dog]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESO Gwenyfe|[[Online:Gwenyfe|Gwenyfe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

Under Reachfolk control, the city of Markarth was protected by magical totems and wards set up by Reach Witches.{{ref|{{Item Link|name=Reachwitch Protective Totem|id=171390|summary=y|quality=3|Reachwitch Protective Totem}} item  description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Markarth and the Dark Heart Prophecy===
[[File:ON-npc-Arana.jpg|thumb|right|Arana of the Wayward Guardians]]
[[File:ON-npc-Lady Belain.jpg|thumb|left|Lady Belain of the Nighthollow Clan]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, Ard Caddach was approached by the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]], who had sundered through the kingdoms of Skyrim at that point. They proposed that if the Ard left them to search the Reach for an ancient relic and to keep his people in the city walls, that Markarth would be left unscathed in their impending conquest. While Caddach staunchly agreed, this deal proved undesirable to some of the Reach, and so a rebel faction called the [[Lore:Wayward Guardians|Wayward Guardians]] was formed. Their leader, the former [[Lore:Ghostsong Clan|Ghostsong]] witch, [[Lore:Arana|Arana]] advised the people of Markarth to leave the city and join them at their foothold in [[Lore:Karthspire|Karthspire]], the camp called {{Lore Link|Rebel's Retreat}}. Much to the dismay of the Ard, the guardians became public enemy number one to the Court of Markarth.{{Ref|name=ONCaddach|[[Online:Ard Caddach|Ard Caddach]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

Sometime after, the Ard was approached by Count [[Lore:Verandis Ravenwatch|Verandis Ravenwatch]] and his [[Lore:The Vestige|associate]] for matters regarding the Gray Host. Caddach was not willing to listen to the Count nor the associate, and so his right-hand advisor, Lady {{Lore Link|Belain}} agreed to hear them out if they were to track down the Wayward Guardians and their leader. While the associate was out in their investigation, they learned about Belain's allegiance to the Gray Host and not only her infiltration into the Court of Markarth, but her influence over the Ard. They planned to fill the city with as much people they can. The associate returned to Markarth and informed Caddach about their findings, leaving him disgruntled. He finally agrees to speak with the Count of [[Lore:House Ravenwatch|House Ravenwatch]]{{Ref|name=ONDespot|[[Online:The Despot of Markarth|The Despot of Markarth]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} while the associate left to learn more about their plans at the ruins of [[Lore:Bthar-Zel|Bthar-Zel]].{{Ref|name=ONBlood|[[Online:Blood of the Reach|Blood of the Reach]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

In their investigation, not only did they learn more about their plans, they uncovered a greater plot involving a dwemer keystone, energy from the [[Lore:Void|Void]], the ancient [[Lore:Nighthollow Clan|Nighthollow Clan]] of vampires, and the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]]. Lady Belain convinced the matriarch of the Ghostsong, [[Lore:Nathari|Nathari]] to sacrifice much of her clan in the vaults of the [[Lore:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]],{{Ref|name=ONAwakening|[[Online:The Awakening Darkness|The Awakening Darkness]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} which in turn provided enough [[Lore:Soul|soul]]s to the Dark Heart kept in the lost Library of {{Lore Link|Arkthzand}}. With that energy they planned to feed the heart even more with the dense population of the Reach that was kept in the confines of Markarth.{{Ref|name=ONHeart|[[Online:The Dark Heart|The Dark Heart]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} With the heart activated, the Gray Host had descended onto the city streets and slaughtered as many people as they could, sustaining the heart with countless souls. At some point earlier, Arana and her soldiers approached Ard Caddach and offered their arms to protect the city. Lady Belain even tried to take out the Ard herself, but was stopped by Arana. She escaped to Arkthzand to harness the heart's power but was followed by Arana and Verandis' associate. In an epic clash, Belain was destroyed but Arana died in the process. While the Gray Host had fled with [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]], the city of Markarth had been saved from further destruction.{{Ref|name=ONFeast|[[Online:A Feast of Souls|A Feast of Souls]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

In the same year, a circle of [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] scholars of [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] was interested in forming relations with Reachfolk in exchange for knowledge. They opened an embassy in the city and worked with various [[Lore:Reachman Clans|clan]] chiefs.{{ref|name=Bralthahawn|[[ON:Bralthahawn|Bralthahawn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} In turn, a diplomatic delegation from the Reach, headed by Ambassador [[Lore:Goronwy|Goronwy]], was sent to the Bosmeri city of Vashabar.{{Ref|name=AG|[[ON:Ambassador Goronwy|Ambassador Goronwy]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Miracle at Markarth and Peace Treaty===
Following the defense of Markarth, the Vestige and Count Verandis Ravenwatch followed him to the [[Lore:Grayhaven|Grayhaven]] where Rada al-Saran was defeated. Once Verandis absorbed the powers of the Heart he released all the souls of the fallen during the Gray Host invasion of Markarth and brought them back to life.{{ref|name=KOA|Events of [[ON:Kingdom of Ash|Kingdom of Ash]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} This event was remembered as "Miracle at Markarth".{{ref|name=FYN|[[Online:Fynvagh|Fynvagh]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Leader of the Markarth announced the moot during which Jarl of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] {{Lore Link|Svana}}, Skald-King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] of Eastern Skyrim and Ard Caddach of the Reach signed the peace treaty between the Reach and the Eastern and Western Skyrim. [[Lore:Arana|Arana]] was appointed a role of keep with at Caddach's court.{{ref|name=SC|Events of [[ON:Second Chances|Second Chances]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Markarth in the Third Empire===
After [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s victory over the [[Lore:Battle of Old Hroldan|Battle of Old Hroldan]], the Reach and by extension, Markarth had been taken by the Nords of Skyrim. A major result of the battle was the public perception of the Reachfolk. The scholars of that day labeled the people as savages and madmen, ones that are prone to irrational violence and worship old and heretical gods. Markarth became the capital of the Reach Hold of Skyrim.{{Ref|name=TMOTR|{{Cite Book|The "Madmen" of the Reach}}}} Sometime in {{Year|2E 864}}, Tiber Septim ordered the construction of the Imperial College of the Voice, an institute dedicated to bring back the ancient art of [[Lore:The Voice|the voice]]. Some of the Empire's loyal nordic families sent some of their own children to the academy.{{Ref|name=PGE1E}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the village of Markarth Side was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lady Hellne and it had a rivalry with {{Lore Link|Amber Guard}} and [[Lore:Dunstad Grove|Dunstad Grove]].{{Ref|name=AR}}

===The Forsworn Uprising & the Great War Aftermath===
[[File:SR-npc-Madanach.jpg|thumb|left|Madanach, the King in Rags]]
When the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] ravaged across [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and the Dominion sacked the Imperial City in {{Year|4E 174}}, the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] had left their position in the Reach for the capital city, leaving Markarth undefended. Taking advantage of this, the Reachmen formed an army known as the [[Lore:Forsworn|Forsworn]] that claimed Markarth as an independent city-state in an event called the [[Lore:Forsworn Uprising|Forsworn Uprising]]. The rebel faction had usurped the previous Jarl of Markarth, [[Lore:Hrolfdir|Hrolfdir]] in favor for their leader, King [[Lore:Madanach|Madanach]].{{Ref|name=SRNepos|[[Skyrim:Nepos the Nose|Nepos the Nose]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In that time, the city-state sought to establish the Reach as a legitimate kingdom of the Empire. However, public opinion from the Nordic-people was against the Reachmen; while Reachmen believed in a legendary, historical, and peaceful city-state, Nords lacked this cultural reference as these legends were historically ignored by the skalds of Skyrim.{{Ref|name=SRIgmund|[[Skyrim:Igmund|Igmund]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Thus, Nords of all classes viewed the natives of the Reach only as savages and brutes who knew only how to kill and serve their Daedric Prince. In {{Year|4E 176}}, before any arrangement was made between Markarth and the Empire, Jarl Hrolfdir formed a militia of Nords that recruited Jarl [[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]], besieging the gates of Markarth. Ulfric's slogan, ''"you are with us, or you are against Skyrim"'' rang true across the Reach, any citizen that did not join his cause was exposed to the jarl's wrath. King Madanach was sentenced to the Cidhna Mine. The Forsworn retreated into the hills.{{Ref|name=TBoM|{{Cite Book|The Bear of Markarth}}}}{{Ref|name=CL|[[Legends:Card Lore#Markarth Bannermen|Markarth Bannermen]]'s card lore in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

[[File:SR-npc-Igmund.jpg|thumb|right|Jarl Igmund of Markarth]]
The victory was to the Nords and Ulfric Stormcloak, who used his unique position to his advantage. Before the Imperial Legion could re-established order in Markarth, Ulfric demanded that worship of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] was freely practiced in Markarth, which was in violation of the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]]. As chaos ensued across the city street, the Empire was forced to comply with Ulfric's demand.{{Ref|name=TBoM}} These events were later dubbed the {{Lore Link|Markarth Incident}} and it was seen as one of several reasons for the formation of the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]] and later the [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|Skyrim Civil War]]. With his father dead, [[Lore:Igmund|Igmund]] was named the Jarl of Markarth and continued the fight against the Forsworn, who became brigands across the Reach. But to fulfill the concordat, Jarl Igmund was forced to arrest Ulfric and his militia, believing it was in Skyrim's best interest.{{Ref|name=SRIgmund}} When the civil war came into full-force, Jarl Igmund and his court favored the Empire, who were based in the northern city, [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]. The Legion's militia in the Reach was represented by Legate {{Lore Link|Emmanuel Admand}}.

===The Forsworn Conspiracy===
In {{Year|4E 201}}, murder strings occurred across Markarth that revealed a large-scale conspiracy involving the Forsworn and the {{Lore Link|Silver-Blood Clan}}. An [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|interloper]] had delved into the vast rabbit hole after they witnessed an incident in the city's marketplace. Their investigation took them across Markarth, including the warrens in the riverside district, and the Treasury House that was ran by the Silver-Blood Clan. Eventually, they were arrested by the corrupt city-guard in the town's temple of Talos and taken into Cidhna Mine.{{Ref|name=SRConspiracy|[[Skyrim:The Forsworn Conspiracy|The Forsworn Conspiracy]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Their time in the prison led them brought the attention of the Forsworn kept in the mines and the King of Rags, Madanach. The interloper was offered to join in their escape plan through the Markarth Ruins, but it is unknown whether it was successful and they had cut a bloody-swathe through town, or if they died in the attempt.{{Ref|name=SREscape|[[Skyrim:No One Escapes Cidhna Mine|No One Escapes Cidhna Mine]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
