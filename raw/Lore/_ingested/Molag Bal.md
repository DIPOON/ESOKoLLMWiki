[[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] (Stone-Fire,{{ref|name=Meridia|[[Online:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or literally the "Fire Stone"){{ref|{{Cite Book|Dream Journal of Firilanya}}}}{{intnote|StoneFire|[nb 1]}} is the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] whose sphere is the domination and enslavement of mortals.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Book of Daedra}}}} His ultimate desire is to harvest the souls of all mortals, bringing them within his sway by spreading seeds of strife and discord in the mortal realms.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Requisite Book of Daedra}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Spawn of Molag Bal}}}} To this end, he obsessively collects soul gems,{{ref|Appearance of [[Online:The Wailing Maw|The Wailing Maw]], the [[Online:Castle of the Worm (place)|Castle of the Worm]], [[Online:The Foundry of Woe|the Foundry of Woe]] and several other locations where soul gems are piled high in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OX|{{Cite Book|On the Xivkyn}}}} and has even dragged pieces of Nirn into his realm to satisfy his insatiable desire for conquest.{{ref|Appearance of [[Online:Haj Uxith|Haj Uxith]], [[Online:The Moonless Walk|The Moonless Walk]] and [[Online:The Reaver Citadel|the Reaver Citadel]] within [[Online:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The more souls he collects, the more he wants.{{ref|name=EGTColdharbour}}

Molag Bal values patience and cunning. He often deceives those he deals with, and has no trouble waiting exceedingly long periods of time for his plans to come to fruition.{{ref|[[Online:Wisdom of the Ages|Haj Uxith]]'s appearance in Coldharbour in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Faraniel|Faraniel]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Shadow's Embrace|The Shadow's Embrace]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MoViv|Events of the [[Online:Fighters Guild #Quests|Fighters Guild]] questline in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He also takes great pleasure in the suffering of mortals, and often has them tortured for his amusement.{{ref|name=TSPC|{{Cite Book|The Slave Pits of Coldharbour}}}} Those who fall into his clutches are forced to labor ceaselessly for his benefit.{{ref|name=ESOHOTLoad|[[ON:The Halls of Torment|The Halls of Torment]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screen]] text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Prince has a penchant for necromancy,{{ref|name=ValJou|{{Cite Book|Valasha's Journal}}}}{{ref|name=UL|{{Cite Book|Unhallowed Legions}}}} often employing the use of the risen dead and forcing his followers to serve beyond the grave.{{ref|name=ESOPalolel|[[ON:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Prismatic Core|The Prismatic Core]]}} He is capable of preserving live mortals far beyond their normal lifespan to prolong their suffering.{{ref|name=LFTD|[[ON:King Laloriaran Dynar#The Lightless Oubliette|King Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Light from the Darkness|Light from the Darkness]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Molag Bal is acknowledged as the father of several children, though it is unclear what those relationships mean to him.{{ref|name=GGLST|[[ON:Grunda's Gatehouse|Grunda's Gatehouse]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He has a son, [[Lore:Ozozzachar|Ozozzachar]],{{ref|name=MBalGeneral}} who is a [[Lore:Daedric Titan|Daedric Titan]].{{ref|name=Ozzozachar|[[Online:Ozzozachar|Ozzozachar]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He also has a daughter named [[Lore:Molag Grunda|Molag Grunda]], a [[Lore:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilight]] who took the lowly [[Lore:Frost Atronach|Frost Atronach]] [[Lore:Nomeg Gwai|Nomeg Gwai]] as her consort. Molag Bal did not approve of the relationship and had them both banished to Oblivion so he could punish them for eternity.{{ref|name=MWVamp|Events of [[Morrowind:A Cure for Vampirism|A Cure for Vampirism]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} A persistent legend about [[Lore:Haymon Camoran|Haymon Camoran]] claims he is the progeny of Molag Bal and a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] woman.{{ref|name=DCSG|[[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles|The Daggerfall Chronicles, pg.8]]}}

Molag Bal's enemies are [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]],{{ref|name=DFFactions|[[Daggerfall:Oblivion|Oblivion Faction]] data in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]],{{ref|name=THOH|Events of [[Skyrim:The House of Horrors|The House of Horrors]] and Molag Bal's dialogue during the quest in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]],{{ref|name=TLO|{{Cite Book|The Lightless Oubliette}}}} [[Lore:Lamae Bal|Lamae Bal]]{{ref|name=ESOLamae}} and [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]]. He was at one point allied with [[Lore:Azura|Azura]].{{ref|name=DFFactions}} His summoning day is during [[Lore:Chil'a|Chil'a]], on the 20th of Evening Star.{{ref|name=DFHolidays|[[Daggerfall:Holidays|Holidays]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} He appears before his cultists in the guise of a mortal when invoked on other dates.{{ref|name=InvocAz}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

It is rumored among the [[Lore:Dremora|kyn]] who serve him that his second-favorite type of [[Lore:Weapons|weapon]] after the mace is the axe, as it is the tool of the headsman. However, nobody is brave enough to ask him about it.{{ref|name=CM63D|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 63: Dremora Style}}}} He purportedly has terrible hygiene and smells of rotting flesh.{{ref|name=Irrai|[[Online:Irrai#Quest-Related Events|Irrai]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Eyes of Azura|Eyes of Azura]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Titles==
[[File:ON-statue-Coldharbour Sculpture.jpg|thumb|left|A sculpture of slaves being tortured in Coldharbour]]
Mortals have often used descriptors and alternate names to refer to the Daedric Prince in conversation. Molag Bal is unique in that he is known by an exceedingly large amount of nicknames, some used by specific groups for different reasons. He is most commonly referred to as the '''God of Schemes''',{{ref|name=COT5C1|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}} the '''Harvester of Souls''',{{ref|name=OBYushi|[[OB:Yushi|Yushi]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=CTOC|{{Cite Book|Closing the Octal Cage}}}}{{ref|name=JOC|{{Cite Book|Journal of Culanwe}}}} the '''Lord of Domination''',{{ref|name=GoSMB}} the '''Prince of Domination'''{{ref|name=ESOMaceStyle|[[ON:Mace of Molag Bal Style|Mace of Molag Bal]] [[Online:Artifact Styles#Mace of Molag Bal|Artifact Style]] flavor text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and the '''Lord of Brutality'''.{{Ref|name=ONAfneer|[[ON:Afneer Cold-Moon|Afneer Cold-Moon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=StoneRitTom|{{Cite Book|Stonefire Ritual Tome}}}} These terms are comparatively neutral, and can be used by anyone to describe him, from normal citizens to his greatest enemies and his most devout worshippers. These titles are descriptive, and utilize aspects of his sphere of influence to identify him as the subject of conversation. The '''Dark Lord''' is another neutral name, used by fearful slaves in hushed whispers, and uttered by the Prince's enemies in defiance.{{ref|name=ESOJurisa|[[ON:Jurisa Denter|Jurisa Denter]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Vanus Unleashed|Vanus Unleashed]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TzikTFA|[[Online:Tzik'nith#The Final Assault|Tzik'nith]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Molag Bal's minions use the name in reverence.{{ref|name=OCL|{{Cite Book|Our Continued Labor}}}}{{ref|name=ESOAmbient|[[ON:Dremora Dialogue|Ambient Dialogue]] from [[ON:Dremora|Dremora]] in the [[ON:Imperial City|Imperial City]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Several other nicknames which the mortals of Tamriel have identified Molag Bal with include the '''Harvester''',{{ref|name=ESOAbnab|[[ON:Abnab|Abnab]]'s 29th, 30th and 31st hireling correspondence mails from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Lord of Corruption''',{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|293}} the '''Corrupter''', the '''Tormentor of Men''', the '''Corrupter of Souls''',{{ref|name=OBAmir|[[OB:Amir|Amir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the '''Lord of Troubles''',{{ref|name=OJOGR|{{Cite Book|Ongoing Journal of Galur Rithari}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Galur Rithari|Galur Rithari]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Prince of Schemes''',{{ref|{{Cite Book|Alvada's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=DanysTWD|[[Online:High Ordinator Danys|High Ordinator Danys]]' dialogue during [[Online:The Wakening Dark|The Wakening Dark]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the '''God of Brutality''',{{ref|[[Online:Nardis|Nardis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Sower of Strife'''{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ritual of Resonance}}}} and the '''Scheming Lord of Coldharbour'''.{{ref|{{Cite Book|To All Who Pass Through}}}} These nicknames share a common theme in that they describe Molag Bal's sphere of influence. These titles also draw from his most easily recognizable behaviors. Molag Bal is known to harvest souls, torment mortals for fun and corrupt the minds of good people with promises of power.{{ref|name=HLBJ|{{Cite Book|Hadmal Lastblood's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} He hatches elaborate schemes to spread his influence across Tamriel. The '''Prince of Murder''' is a more vague title that has been affixed to the Prince by the servants of his enemy, Meridia.{{ref|name=TG|[[ON:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The title does not frame murder as an aspect of his sphere in the way that it falls under Mephala's divine portfolio; rather, it illustrates the idea that Molag Bal is an individual of an unpleasant nature. In {{Year|2E 582}}, an ally of the Army of Meridia referred to Molag Bal as the '''Dark One'''.{{ref|name=TzikTFA}}

The '''King of Rape'''{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}}{{ref|name=LOV12|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 12}}}} is a title that is used in the Living God [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s sermons. It draws upon Molag Bal's brutal nature and, at least in ''[[Lore:The House of Troubles|The House of Troubles]]'', refers to literal rape and his desire to "ruin the Dunmer gene pool".{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} The '''Elder Spirit of Domination and Supreme Law''' is a moniker derived from the epistles of [[Lore:Amun-dro|Amun-dro]], an ancient Khajiit priest who committed the names of the old Khajiit deities to paper.{{ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}} [[Lore:Carlovac Townway|Carlovac Townway]] referred to Molag Bal as the '''Prince of Rage''' in his popular historical fiction novel, ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]''.{{Ref|name=2920v4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} [[Lore:Sigillah Parate|Sigillah Parate]] identified Molag Bal as the '''Schemer Princess''' in her work, ''[[Lore:Invocation of Azura|Invocation of Azura]]''.{{ref|name=InvocAz|{{Cite Book|Invocation of Azura}}}}

'''Molag the Slave-Lord'''{{ref|name=OtNoC|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Coldharbour}}}} is a one-off nickname used by [[Lore:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus of Elinhir]] in his lecture on the nature of Molag Bal's plane of Oblivion, Coldharbour. The name is in reference to Molag Bal's voracious desire to amass unfathomable amounts of souls, and his tendency to enslave those whose souls he comes into possession of. The '''Schemer''',{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}} the '''Prince of Pain''' and the '''Captain of Corruption''' are monikers written in popular bardic performances from the Interregnum. ''The Plane Meld'' was a song that described the impending doom from the then-ongoing Planemeld. It likens Molag Bal to some sort of demented farmer, tilling the land of Tamriel and sowing corruption as far as he can reach.{{ref|name=ESOBards|[[Online:Bardic Performances #The Plane Meld|Bardic Performances]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-place-Bal Ur.jpg|thumb|right|Bal Ur, a temple of great importance to Molag Bal's followers on Vvardenfell]]
The '''Dark Father''',{{ref|name=ESORahaja|[[ON:Rahaja|Rahaja]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Scion of the Blood Matron|Scion of the Blood Matron]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Lafara|Lafara]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Father of Vampires''',{{ref|name=ESONoryon|[[ON:Noryon|Noryon]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOFreed|The [[ON:Freed Soul|Freed Soul]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} '''Lord of the Undead''',{{ref|name=CM60|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 60: Worm Cult Style}}}} and the '''Father of Undeath'''{{ref|name=EGTColdharbour|{{Cite Book|EGT|Dreams of Cyrodiil}}}} are nicknames derived from Molag Bal's association with [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]]s and the [[Lore:Undead|undead]]. Regardless of whether or not they revere him, vampires who recognize Molag Bal as the progenitor of the first vampire may refer to him as the Dark Father. Scholars and laymen who are familiar with Molag Bal's association with vampires or his propensity for raising the dead may refer to him as either of the latter.{{ref|name=ESORahaja}} The '''Defiler'''{{ref|name=ESOLamae|[[ON:Lamae Bal|Lamae Bal]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Scion of the Blood Matron|Scion of the Blood Matron]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} is a title of note among the scions of [[Lore:Lamae Bal|Lamae Bal]], the first vampire. The vampires of Lamae's coven are encouraged to despise Molag Bal, and they acknowledge him as the one responsible for their matron's state of undeath.{{ref|name=ESORS}} The scions of Lamae have also referred to Molag Bal as the '''Father of Torment''' and the '''King of Corruption'''{{ref|name=ESORS|{{Cite Book|Rite of the Scion}}}}{{ref|name=SRLogrolf|[[SR:Logrolf the Willful|Logrolf the Willful]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:The House of Horrors|The House of Horrors]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} in light of the notion that Molag Bal takes great delight in the suffering of mortals, and actively seeks to corrupt anything he believes will further his goals or spread strife throughout Nirn. 

The '''Lord of Lies''',{{ref|name=NMV|{{Cite Book|Notes on the Mortuum Vivicus}}}} the '''Master of Domination'''{{ref|Dialogue uttered by [[Online:Worm Cult Bonelord|Worm Cult Bonelords]] during [[Online:Soul Reaper|Soul Reaper]] encounters in [[Online:ESO|ESO]]}} and the '''Lord of Darkness'''{{ref|name=LTDVolcat|{{Cite Book|Letter to Diabolist Volcatia|ns_base=Online}}}} are names that have been uttered by the Prince's adherents. The '''Dark Master'''{{ref|name=NMV}}{{ref|name=ICXiv|[[Online:Xivkyn Dialogue|Overheard dialogue]] from [[Online:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}} and the '''Dread Lord'''{{ref|name=CMX|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 17: Xivkyn Style}}}} are titles which have been explicitly used by Molag Bal's cultists and Daedric minions in the context of their service. The Daedra who serve Molag Bal refer to him as "the Dread Lord", "the Dark Lord" and their "Brutal Lord"{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crumpled Arena Flyer}}}} in their missives. Servants both mortal and otherwise have identified their god with many variations on the words "lord" and "master", denoting the Prince's position of power above the denizens of his Plane.{{ref|name=NMV}}{{ref|name=DDTT}} An example of such a name is the '''Lord of Schemes'''.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}}{{ref|name=TEWObserver|[[Online:The Observer|The Observer]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Endless War|The Endless War]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The '''Lord of Coldharbour''' is a simple title that refers to his dominion over Coldharbour.{{ref|name=ESOSkull|The [[ON:Cursed Skull|Cursed Skull]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Curse of Skulls|Curse of Skulls]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Religion and Culture==
===Altmer===
Alongside Azura and [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]], the Daedra worshiping [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya Clan]] revered Molag Bal as one of their primary Daedric patrons. The Corelanyan [[Lore:Altmer|High Elves]] prayed to him for strength when the need to defend their livelihood arose.{{ref|name=HoCC2|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 2}}}} Molag Bal fell out of favor around {{Year|1E 2633}}, after the [[Lore:Three Queens|Three Queens]] rose as the Corelanyan's guardian triumvirate.{{ref|name=HoCC5|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 5}}}}

===Ayleid===
[[File:OB-interior-Garlas Agea.jpg|right|thumb|Ayleid statuette of Mola Gbal]]
Molag Bal was known as '''Mola Gbal''' to the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]],{{ref|name=ESOHoSLoadTxt|[[Online:Halls of Submission|Halls of Submission]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and was the patron deity of [[Lore:Abagarlas|Abagarlas]].{{ref|name=TWoD|{{Cite Book|The Whithering of Delodiil}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Unholy Temple}}}} 

===Breton===
The Bretons of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] share stories about the "Terrible [[Lore:Torchbug|Torchbug]] of Molag Bal" the night before [[Lore:Waking Day|Waking Day]]. The stories vary, but the one consistent part of every variation of the tale involves a fearsome, savage entity being followed by blue torchbugs.{{ref|name=BlueTorchbug|[[Online:Blue Torchbug|Blue Torchbug]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Dunmer===
Molag Bal is a Daedric power of much importance in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], where he is always the archenemy of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], the Prince of Plots. He is seen as a primary obstacle to the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] people (and their [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] predecessors). In the legends, Molag Bal always tries to upset the bloodlines of Houses or otherwise ruin Dunmeri "purity".{{ref|name=VOFIT|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel}}}} He is said to be the parent (along with [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]) of a population of [[Lore:Monster Children|supermonsters]] that terrorized the [[Lore:Molag Amur|Molag Amur]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]].{{ref|name=LOV14|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 14}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Tusenend|Tusenend]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

He is known to the Dunmer as the third corner of the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]], and one of the Bad Daedra.{{ref|name=MWThirdCorner|Events of [[MW:Molag Bal of the House of Troubles|Molag Bal of the House of Troubles]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'', and the spells rewarded during the quest: [[MW:Conjuration Spells#Command of the Third Corner|Command of the Third Corner]] and [[MW:Restoration Spells#Blessings of the Third Corner|Blessings of the Third Corner]]}}{{ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}} Dunmeri mythos cites [[Lore:Bal Ur|Bal Ur]] as the "birthplace of Molag Bal",{{ref|{{Cite Book|Vampires of Vvardenfell}}}} where the [[Lore:Lorkhan|Doom Drum]] tricked him into becoming mortal.{{ref|name=TSDM|[[Morrowind:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue during [[MW:Molag Bal of the House of Troubles|Molag Bal of the House of Troubles]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Bal Ur is a place of great importance for Vvardenfell's vampire clans, particularly the [[Lore:Aundae Clan|Aundae]], who believe the ruin is important to their vampire heritage.{{ref|name=OJOGR}} In the days of old Resdayn, Molag Bal was not worshipped so much as he was placated.{{ref|name=ESOBalUr|[[ON:Bal Ur|Bal Ur]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Molag Bal plays a pivotal role in the narrative of Vivec's famous sermons. The Prince and the Warrior-Poet parleyed in the wilds of Molag Amur,{{ref|name=LOV12}} an event which served as the catalyst for the creation of a variety of monsters which the tribune would gradually slay throughout the rest of the sermons. During their time together, Molag Bal taught the God-Poet the secret syllable of royalty, CHIM, which, in his lessons, Vivec described as essential to becoming a Ruling King of the world. Their meeting ended in treachery as great monsters arose from the earth in the wake of their union, and Vivec vanquished the Daedric Prince in kind.{{ref|name=LOV14}} The Shrine of Inevitable Secrets, a temple devoted to [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] in Morrowind, was dragged beneath the waves by dreugh. Some legends attribute the shrine's disappearance to Vivec and Molag Bal's fury upon learning that the Lord of Secrets had spied on them, removing his shrine from the world and disallowing more to be built as punishment.{{ref|[[Online:Monk Methas Irethi|Monk Methas Irethi]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

===Dreugh===
Molag Bal purportedly has connections to the [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh]]. According to legend, the dreugh used to be far more intelligent and civilized than they are today. The dreugh were said to worship a creature known as the [[Lore:Ruddy Man|Ruddy Man]]. Not much is known about this entity, but it is believed to be a manifestation of Molag Bal.{{ref|name=CETD|[[General:Creating ESO: The Dreugh|Creating ESO: The Dreugh]] from the official ESO website}} Dunmeri folklore alleges that Molag Bal was the chieftain of the dreugh in a time when they ruled the world. During that time, Molag Bal took on a form that was spiny, armored, and built for life beneath the waves. The Ruddy Man from the teachings of the Tribunal Temple is said to resemble this iteration of Molag Bal, and was supposedly a product of Vivec and Molag Bal's union.{{ref|name=Vivec28|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 28}}}}

===Reachmen===
{{Quote Box|''"Hail Molag Bal, the Prince of Pain<br>''
''Brutal master, lord of troubles<br>''
''Giver of strength to slay and strive<br>''
''For men are meant to struggle"''|''From [[Lore:Hail to the Ancient Spirits|Hail to the Ancient Spirits]], an old Reach song''}}
[[File:ON-concept-Reachfolk Molag Bal.png|thumb|left|Reachfolk emblem for Molag Bal|class=backdrop]]
Molag Bal is venerated by some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachman]] clans. The people of [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] recognize the Lord of Lies as a schemer who enjoys his tricks. Known as the '''Father of Torment''', the '''Defiler''' and '''He Who Schemes in the Shadows''', Molag Bal is a spirit that represents hardship.{{ref|name=ESOAydolan|[[Online:Aydolan|Aydolan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: [[Online:DLC#Markarth|Markarth]]}} He's somewhat of a testing god, acknowledged as a spirit who brings trouble, and in doing so, he teaches the people of the Reach to endure.{{ref|name=Fialyn|[[Online:Apprentice Fialyn|Apprentice Fialyn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:After the Storm|After the Storm]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: [[Online:DLC#Markarth|Markarth]]}} 

The Reachfolk understand that hardship is inevitable, but instead of bemoaning their sorrows, they embrace the pain as wisdom.{{ref|name=ESOAydolan}} They look up to those who work hard,{{ref|name=ESOHereline|[[Online:Hereline|Hereline]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: [[ON:DLC#Markarth|Markarth]]}} and believe those that are plagued by troubled times are strengthened by their experiences. Molag Bal grants nothing but unexpected pain. Those of the Reach who venerate Molag Bal appreciate the lessons he teaches, growing resilient in his shadow.{{ref|name=ESOAydolan}} 

The [[Lore:Cinder-Heart Clan|Cinder-Heart Clan]] reveres the Defiler,{{ref|name=ONCHCC|[[Online: Cinder-Heart Clan Camp|Cinder-Heart Clan Camp]]'s shrine to Molag Bal in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and their practices reflect the extreme horrors of Molag Bal's sphere. They are known for their cruel treatment of captives, whom they burn alive. Their briarheart ritual is augmented with the intense pain of opening a subject's chest cavity and filling it with hot coals.{{ref|name=OTCOTR|{{Cite Book|On the Clans of the Reach}}}}

Reachmen are sometimes referred to as the Witchmen of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], a name that stems from their use of dark "Reach-magic".{{Ref|name=PGE1EHR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/High Rock}}}} Although [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] is believed to be the source of the dark nature magic used by Reach hedge wizards,{{ref|[[Online:Vernim Woods|Vernim Woods]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Molag Bal can grant similar power. The name "gravesinger" is worn by some Reachman necromancers who broker deals with Bal{{ref|name=ONZK|[[Online:Zhagush gro-Korlag|Zhagush gro-Korlag]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Swine Thief|Swine Thief]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} in exchange for the power to command the loathsome dead.{{ref|name=TIEGTTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} These gravesingers can command corpses by planting the seeds of foul [[Lore:Flora B#Bloodthorn Vines|vines]] in their victims' bodies. Molag Bal's "gifts" may leave a gravesinger's body in a state of decay.{{ref|name=ESOAngof|[[Online:Angof the Undying|Angof the Undying]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Khajiit===
Some ancient Khajiit tribes knew him as '''Molagh''', one of the twelve Demon Kings and the Elder Spirit of Domination and Supreme Law. In some forms of Khajiiti mythos, Molagh has a wife who freed Merrunz to weaponize his destructive nature. Molagh was the first to attempt an assault on the Lunar Lattice with intent alongside Merrunz and Merid-Nunda. Boethra fought him to a standstill before the Lattice, and Azurah brought him down.{{ref|name=TAS}} A pre-ri'Datta text known as ''The Bladesongs of Boethra'' describes the battle at the Lattice in greater detail, alleging that Molagh taunted Boethra with the severed head of [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]] to lure her away from guarding the Lunar Lattice, allowing Dagon and Merid-Nunda to break through the moonlight barrier.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume II}}}} Amun-dro's writings say that he is to be faced along the Path and overcome by the Will Against Rule, a concept associated with Boethra. Molag Bal is among those the Epistles of Amun-dro refer to as the Adversarial Spirits.{{ref|name=TAS}}

===Philosophy===
The Lord of Lies is the center of a longstanding philosophical debate known as the Molag Bal Paradox. Revolving around the notion that Molag Bal consistently defies the will of others (often suppressing their will by imposing his own upon them), the paradox poses the following question: "If everyone would express the belief that [Molag Bal] exists, would he therefore cease to do so because his nature demands he oppose the wishes of others?" Assuming that Molag Bal is subject to the metaphysical rules of the Aurbis, philosophers have argued whether or not the Prince "truly" embodies the concept of domination.{{ref|name=Isayas|[[Online:Isayas|Isayas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}  The Molag Bal Paradox is considered a classic puzzle, and scholars have engaged with it since at least the Second Era.{{ref|name=Agmund|[[Online:Agmund the Insightful|Agmund the Insightful]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

==History==
===First Era===
[[File:ON-place-The Lost Fleet.jpg|thumb|left|The remains of the All Flags Navy]]
The ancient Daedric temple-city of [[Lore:Mor Naril|Mor Naril]], built sometime in early history on the island of [[Lore:Solstice|Solstice]], was dedicated to Molag Bal. His servants built it to harvest souls departing the mortal plane.{{ref|name=ESOArvoril|[[Online:Arvoril|Arvoril]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Gift of Death|The Gift of Death]] in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Season of the Worm Cult|Season of the Worm Cult]]''}} The temple predates the Argonians' arrival to the island, who were called over across the sea to settle on Solstice before the event known as [[Lore:Duskfall|Duskfall]].{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/2466390916 Twitch Stream of ''Solstice Walkthrough w/ Devs''], time stamp 49:19-50:50}}{{Ref|name=TAECS|{{Cite Book|The Amber Egg and Cerulean Sea}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Mor Naril|Mor Naril]]'s loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} These Argonians considered the Daedra on the island their enemies, and constructed stone guardians known as [[Lore:Voskrona Guardian|Voskrona]] to wage war on them.{{Ref|name=TCWTM|{{Cite Book|The Collected Writings of Tzinik-Muz}}}}

Kinlady [[Lore:Iniel Corelanya|Iniel Corelanya]] learned necromantic magics from Mor Naril during her ill-fated campaign to conquer Solstice in {{Year|1E 723}}. She later called upon Molag Bal during the Corelanyans' war against the [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudans]] in {{Year|1E 853}}.{{ref|name=HoCC2}} Her necromantic exploits caused a schism in her clan betweeen those who rejected the Daedra and those unafraid to petition them for aid. The Corelanyans eventually came to reside on Solstice, coexisting peacefully with the native Argonians after several centuries of conflict. The [[Lore:Peace of Xor-Hist|Peace of Xor-Hist]] ushered in an era of calm and prosperity that lasted until {{Year|1E 2631}}, when the tyrant Kinlord [[Lore:Istamil Corelanya|Istamil Corelanya]] rose to power. He smothered dissent with impunity, and was largely unpopular, inciting a civil war. Desperate to maintain power, Istamil sought Molag Bal's assistance in silencing the opposition at the temple of Mor Naril. The Lord of Brutality bestowed legions of Daedra and great necromantic power unto Kinlord Istamil, who used these gifts to slaughter his enemies wholesale. Molag Bal's power ran unchecked until Istamil's cousin [[Lore:Vinutilmo Corelanya|Vinutilmo]] rallied beneath the Three Queens, Meridia, Azura, and Nocturnal, to push back against the kinlord and his Daedric patron. Istamil was slain, and Mor Naril was ultimately sealed.{{ref|name=HoCC5}}

Sometime in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Molag Bal made a pact with [[Lore:Styriche|King Styriche]] of [[Lore:Verkarth (Hammerfell)|Verkarth]], a powerful vampire and the leader of the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]].{{ref|name=MBSTE|{{Cite Book|My Beloved Siblings, the Exarchs}}}} The terms of their agreement eventually brought thirteen of the Gray Host's exarchs into Coldharbour, where they were tortured for eternity by the Daedric Prince.{{ref|name=MBSTE}} [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]], the last known surviving leader of the Gray Host, sought to free his kin from Coldharbour in {{Year|2E 582}} with the help of the [[Lore:Icereach Coven|Icereach Coven]].{{ref|name=GHOESO|{{Cite Book|Gray Host Orders|ns_base=Online}}}} At some point, a Molag Bal-worshipping Ayleid clan that lived in [[Lore:Greenshade|Greenshade]] ceased making offerings to the Prince at [[Lore:Moonhenge|Moonhenge]]. Their city was consumed in blue fire and transported to Coldharbour.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Swallowed City}}}}

In {{Year|1E 2260}}, the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]] sailed to [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] and defeated the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]]. The Sloads' Coral Tower was destroyed during the siege, causing it to plunge into the sea.{{ref|name=JOTEi|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}} The [[Lore:Maelstrom of Bal|Maelstrom of Bal]]{{ref|name=TDO|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}} opened up in the wake of the tower's fall, and a large chunk of the fleet was pulled beneath the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] and into Coldharbour. Molag Bal appeared before the wreckage and struck a deal with the leader of the fallen fleet, giving the admiral complete control over his sailors with a relic known as the Crown of Bones.{{ref|name=JOTEi}}{{ref|name=BBABone|Events of [[ON:Between Blood and Bone|Between Blood and Bone]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CCESO|{{Cite Book|Coldharbour's Conquests}}}}

Molag Bal was responsible for the destruction of the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmeri]] settlement of [[Lore:Gil-Var-Delle|Gil-Var-Delle]] at the end of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], when he was summoned by King [[Lore:Dro'Zel|Dro'Zel]] of [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]].{{ref|name=HoSAO|{{Cite Book|History of Senchal: An Overview}}}} Molag Bal consumed the souls of all mortals present that day, leaving nothing more than the charred remains of the town's once mighty [[Lore:Graht-oak|graht-oak]].{{ref|name=ESOGil|Appearance of [[ON:Gil-Var-Delle|Gil-Var-Delle]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} His corrupting influence lingered into the second era.{{ref|name=DanysTWD}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|From the notes of Culalanwe, Sapiarch of Oblivion Studies}}}} The disaster at Gil-Var-Delle was the catalyst that sparked the conception of the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]]. Molag Bal was present at the meeting where [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] sealed his famed bargain with the powers of Oblivion, and agreed to the terms the Living God set.{{ref|name=TCC}}

Some time after Molag Bal's defeat, the Worm Cult began sacrificing souls to the Lord of Brutality in earnest with the help of magical engines known as soul reapers, seeking to curry the Daedric Prince's favor.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Resurrection and Revelation}}}} To this end, the cult slew Guildmaster [[Lore:Merric at-Aswala|Merric at-Aswala]] of the Fighters Guild and kidnapped [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]].{{ref|Events of [[Online:A Guild in Crisis|A Guild in Crisis]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|Events of [[Online:Justice for the Fallen|Justice for the Fallen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The cult gathered on the eastern half of the island of Solstice, erecting a [[Lore:Writhing Wall|barrier of soulstuff]] to bisect the island so they could work in secret. On their half of the wall, the cult prepared a "reverse Planemeld": while the original Planemeld sought to drag Nirn into Coldharbour, this new ritual sought to bring Coldharbour to the mortal realm.{{ref|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Writhing Wall|The Writhing Wall]] in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Season of the Worm Cult|Season of the Worm Cult]]''}}

==Appearance==
[[File:ON-creature-Molag Bal 02.jpg|The face that inspires Molag Bal's religious iconography|thumb|right]]
Molag Bal has been depicted several different ways by the sculptors who construct shrines dedicated to him. The only truly consistent trait across all portrayals is the presence of a tail, horns and/or frills, and a muscular frame with bestial legs. Some artists interpret him as a bipedal creature with the head of a reptile, long claws, three digits on each hand, and digitigrade legs on paws shaped like that of a lion{{ref|name=OBApp|Appearance of Molag Bal's shrine in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} or a large reptile.{{ref|name=MWApp|Appearance of Molag Bal's shrine in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} These depictions possess aquatic frills, and occasionally horns.{{ref|name=OBApp}} Molag Bal once manifested before an agent of the Blades as a green-skinned humanoid with red eyes, elven ears, the legs of a goat, a long, thin tail, a goat-like face, and curved black horns.{{ref|Molag Bal's [[:File:DF-prince-Molag Bal.gif|appearance]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Acclaimed author Carlovac Townway described the destruction of Gilverdale in ''2920, The Last Year of the First Era'': by his account, bright red mist poured over the town in a wave, engulfing building after building in its wake, with Molag Bal arriving as a grinning, red-skinned giant.{{Ref|name=2920v2|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dawn (v2)}}}}

[[File:ON-concept-Prince-Molag Bal-emblem.png|thumb|left|An emblem representing Molag Bal]]
His most iconic appearance is depicted stylistically on his eponymous Mace: a face with ridged horns, aquatic frills, and sharp fangs. During the Planemeld, Molag Bal appeared in the form that inspired the Mace's rendering of his face. Molag Bal possesses a broad, sinewy, muscular frame carried by digitigrade legs with hips crowned by jagged protrusions. A row of spikes ran from the top of his head down the length of his back, and a bony tail extended from the base of his spine, ending in a tri-tipped axe-shaped structure. Ridges jutted up from his sternum, lining the space where the base of his neck and his shoulders met to frame a horned head with frills on each side.{{ref|name=ESOApp|[[Online:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

His face was reminiscent of a bare skull: a sunken nose, dark circles around light blue eyes that were overseen by pronounced eyebrows, angled cheeks, and rows of lengthy pointed teeth. Small spikes lined his chin. His flesh was blue-grey in coloration, and his horns were black. His extremities grew darker in color as they extended further away from his torso: while his shoulders were blue-grey, his hands were almost dark enough to match his black claws.{{ref|name=ESOApp}}

==Behavior==
{{Quote Box|''Molag Bal is a master of all forms of domination over mortals, including the arts of illusion. As a matter of course the Lord of Brutality prefers lies to the truth—except when the truth is more cruel.''{{ref|[[ON:The Manor of Revelry|The Manor of Revelry]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screen]] text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}}}
Molag Bal is arrogant, calculating, and cares little for the welfare of mortalkind.{{ref|name=GoSMB|[[ON:Molag Bal #God of Schemes|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue during [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OnOb|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} He views pacifism as a form of weakness.{{ref|name=OBMolagBalQuest|[[Oblivion:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s quest in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} He is also fond of ostentatious and macabre displays of power, occasionally going out of his way to hold elaborate ceremonies. An example of such an event was the "first Daedric Titan unveiling and Soul Shriven impalement celebration".{{ref|name=Cad|[[Online:Cadwell#The Heir of Anequina|Cadwell]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Heir of Anequina|The Heir of Anequina]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}<!--Molag Grunda has allegedly hosted dinner parties.{{ref|name=Cad2|[[Online:Cadwell#Jode's Core|Cadwell]]'s dialogue at the [[Online:Moon Gate of Anequina|Moon Gate of Anequina]] during [[Online:Jode's Core (quest)|Jode's Core]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}--> 

The God of Brutality has little patience for failure. He scorns the Daedra beneath him for falling in battle, considering their "weakness" a blemish on their reputation and deserving of recourse.{{ref|[[Online:Molag Bal#Dark Anchors|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue at [[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''"My subjects failed me. They deserve to be destroyed."'' … ''"Kill my minions and you only remove the weaklings from my service."''}} Independent thought is among that which Molag Bal's servants consider "weak": they believe absolute obedience to the Prince is a virtue, and indeed a point of strength.{{ref|[[Online:Dremora Dialogue|Overheard dialogue]] from [[Online:Dremora|Dremora]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]'' (''"Thought is weakness. Obedience is the true power."'')}} Weakness is, of course, punished by pain.{{ref|name=ICXiv}}

[[File:ON-npc-Inmate.jpg|thumb|left|A Mind-Shriven thrall]]
Molag Bal is quite keen on depriving mortals of free will. To this end, Molag Bal wields a form of [[Lore:Mind Magic|mind magic]]: he intrudes upon the minds of mortals as a form of torture. It is an invasive, agonizing experience.{{ref|name=ESOTPDOG|[[ON:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Daughter of Giants|Daughter of Giants]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} By forcing his way into a mortal's mind, he can show them horrific visions.{{ref|name=VarenCouncil|[[ON:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]]' dialogue during [[ON:Council of the Five Companions|Council of the Five Companions]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He also does this to Daedra, particularly magically-gifted Xivkyn. These augurs share a mental link with Molag Bal, acting as vessels to carry out his dark whims.{{ref|name=ESOXivAug|[[Online:Xivkyn Augur|Xivkyn Augur]] [[ON:Polymorphs|Polymorph]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} This practice has unintended risks, as mortals who have had a mental connection to the Prince forced upon them can see through his eyes if they concentrate hard enough. Molag Bal can feel the presence of one who accomplishes such a feat, and he doesn't appreciate the trespass. During the Planemeld, [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] did just that to distract the Daedric Prince, allowing the Vestige to traverse Coldharbour unmolested. Molag Bal was enraged by Varen's presence, leaving him blind to the hero's deeds in his realm while he dealt with the former Emperor.{{ref|name=ESOTPDOG}}

This mode of mental binding is not limited to the torment of living mortals: Molag Bal's minions have bound unfortunate souls to mundane objects with the intent to place them near his enemies on Tamriel, enabling the Prince to spy on those who oppose him from the mortal realm. Being forced to serve as a looking glass for Molag Bal is unbearably painful for the bound spirit.{{ref|name=ESOSkull}}

[[File:ON-skill-Soul Burst 06.jpg|Molag Bal wields souls as ammunition|thumb|right]]
Some mortals have had their force of will completely sapped as a result of Molag Bal tampering with their mind.{{ref|name=ESOCBL|{{Cite Book|Captain Blackheart's Log|ns_base=Online}}}} Such was the case with the infamous pirate, [[Lore:Captain Blackheart|Captain Blackheart]], whose entire crew was transformed into undead slaves.{{ref|name=ESOJumpShip|Events of [[ON:Jumping Ship|Jumping Ship]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Deckhand's Log|ns_base=Online}}}} He was cursed when a relic the crew had plundered spoke to him, claiming to be Molag Bal. The Prince uttered a spell, transforming the crew and making them unable to disobey their Captain's orders:{{ref|name=ESOShiftyTom|[[ON:Shifty Tom|Shifty Tom]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Jumping Ship|Jumping Ship]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the Captain also fell utterly under Molag Bal's control.{{ref|name=ESOMarthaJou|{{Cite Book|Martha's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} Captain Blackheart never killed the inhabitants of the ships he plundered&mdash;following the crew's enslavement,{{ref|name=ESOMarthaJou}} Molag Bal proceeded to slaughter hundreds of innocents with Captain Blackheart's hands, tormenting him with fleeting moments of independent thought.{{ref|name=ESOCBL}}<!-- Captain Blackheart was eventually slain by the Undaunted in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|Events of [[ON:Jumping Ship|Jumping Ship]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}-->

The practice of stealing the minds of mortals is not restricted to spellcraft, as Molag Bal also accomplishes this through [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]]. The [[Lore:Mind-Shriven|Mind-Shriven]] are created when mortals are forced to imbibe a poison called the Blood of Coldharbour. These individuals have their force of will stolen by the Daedric Prince,{{ref|[[Online:Mind-Shriven|Mind-Shriven]] skin description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and become unnaturally aggressive as a result of their transformation.{{ref|name=ESORegilus|[[Online:Captain Regilus|Captain Regilus]]' dialogue during [[Online:The Lock and the Legion|The Lock and the Legion]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}}

As is expected of a Daedric Prince, Molag Bal can summon his Daedric minions at will. Unfortunately for those who oppose him, this means he can summon a horde of Daedric Titans.{{ref|name=ESOGOS|Events of [[On:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Molag Bal can [[Lore:Soul Magic|weaponize souls]] and cold fire to assault those who wrong him.{{ref|name=TFAFire|[[ON:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s actions and dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOBalCombat|[[ON:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s abilities during [[On:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Those who offend Molag Bal may find themselves suddenly missing important features, such as their skin.{{ref|name=GOSBS|[[On:Aelif|Aelif]]'s transformation into a [[ON:Bloody Skeleton|Bloody Skeleton]] during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Renowned Daedrologist Endemel Githendas posits that it is plausible to assume that Molag Bal may be capable of altering a victim's bone structure in pursuit of his eternal desire to inflict agony upon others.{{ref|name=AOCS|{{Cite Book|Analysis of Ossein Cage Sketches|ns_base=Online}}}} He is not above turning people into ravenous undead monsters,{{ref|name=GOSBS}} and [[Lore:Shadow Magic|manipulates shadows]] to imprison powerful servants of Meridia.{{ref|name=LFTD}}

==Relations with Other Entities==
The politics of Oblivion are complex. Alliances come and go, and what each Daedric Prince thinks of one another is largely unknown. However, Molag Bal has a few clearly defined relationships with other Princes, particularly Meridia. The two aren't very fond of each other. Molag Bal revels in undeath and destruction,{{ref|name=ValJou}} while Meridia believes that the sanctity of life energies is not to be perverted.{{ref|name=SRLoading|Meridia-related [[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] text in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Though they both share a strong distaste for free will,{{ref|[[Online:Urgarlag Chief-bane#Depths of Malatar|Urgarlag Chief-bane]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Pledge: Depths of Malatar|Pledge: Depths of Malatar]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} these fundamental beliefs are the foundation for their bitter rivalry.

[[File:ON-quest-The Final Assault 02.jpg|A confrontation between gods|thumb|left]]
The two have actively antagonized each other throughout the course of Tamrielic history: Meridia's role in halting the Planemeld is a prominent example of this. Meridia sent a city under her protection{{ref|name=TWoD}} into Coldharbour to host an invasion force of her own making,{{ref|Events of [[ON:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:The War Council|The War Council]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} wrested control of the Fighters Guild to turn its attention toward interfering with the Planemeld,{{ref|name=MoViv}} and personally appeared in the heart of Molag Bal's stronghold to destroy the Planar Vortex. Meridia plotted to oppose the Planemeld for years in advance.{{ref|name=ESOTFA|[[ON:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue and actions during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

During the same time period, Meridia foiled Molag Bal's efforts to use the Mortuum Vivicus, a relic that siphons mortal souls and is capable of unleashing a storm of undeath.{{ref|name=MoViv}} This would be the second time Meridia has thwarted Bal's attempts to release the Vivicus upon Tamriel, the first occurring in the First Era during the time of [[Lore:Abagarlas|Abagarlas]] and [[Lore:Delodiil|Delodiil]]. Molag Bal's worshippers plotted to harvest the souls of Meridia's faithful and unleash the Mortuum Vivicus' necromantic potential,{{ref|name=BTMM|{{Cite Book|By the Master's Mace}}}} and the Lady of Light destroyed the Abagarlan's city in response.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Curano's Journal}}}} 

[[File:ON-quest-God of Schemes 17.jpg|thumb|right|Meridia exchanges words with Molag Bal]]
Molag Bal became so familiar with Meridia's methods during their years of conflict that he had a facility constructed specifically to imprison her servants.{{ref|name=TLO}} Some ancient Khajiiti tales suggest that Meridia and Molag Bal once fought for a common goal during Bal's assault on the Lunar Lattice,{{ref|name=TAS}} but for the most part, their history is rife with conflict.

Boethiah is another Prince with whom Molag Bal isn't necessarily "on good terms". This is another case of a difference in fundamental beliefs: Molag Bal expects those who worship him to obey without question.{{ref|name=DA}} On the other hand, Boethiah expects her followers to actively question and oppose authority, and despises mortals who attempt to appeal to her with bent knees and words of adoration.{{ref|name=BoePro|{{Cite Book|Boethiah's Proving}}}} Molag Bal seeks strength for himself, while Boethiah looks for strength in others.{{ref|name=TDBAYQ|{{Cite Book|Thendaramur Death-Blossom Answers Your Questions}}}} Their differences aren't the only thing setting them apart; the two Princes have some record of antagonizing one another. In the past, Boethiah has sent her followers to desecrate Molag Bal's holy sites, and Molag Bal has retaliated in kind.{{ref|name=THOH}}

[[File:ON-quest-Eyes of Azura.jpg|thumb|left|Molag Bal exacts vengeance upon a priestess of Azura]]
Molag Bal is apparently bitter rivals with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], and the two will thwart each other at every given opportunity.{{ref|name=FAAYQ}} The [[Lore:Order of the Waking Flame|Order of the Waking Flame]] alleges that Mehrunes Dagon once personally freed a group of his faithful from imprisonment in Coldharbour by sending a projection of himself to do battle against Molag Bal while the faithful were transported to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]].{{ref|name=EoMD2|{{Cite Book|Epics of Mehrunes Dagon, Volume 2}}}} [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] views Molag Bal's desire for adulation as unnecessary, interpreting it as a weakness.{{ref|name=ESOMalacath|[[ON:Malacath|Malacath]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Flames of Forge and Fallen|Flames of Forge and Fallen]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The servants of Molag Bal and Azura have also thwarted each other in the past. The most noteworthy of such instances revolved around a priestess of Azura who brokered peace between two groups in Valenwood whom Molag Bal's minions were sent to divide, foiling his plan.{{ref|name=ESOEOA|Events of [[ON:Eyes of Azura|Eyes of Azura]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Eyes of Azura|Eyes of Azura]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=JOC}}

Molag Bal doesn't just have conflict with other Princes, but the Divines as well. Molag Bal has a particular distaste for Arkay, who is invoked when the souls and bodies of deceased mortals are consecrated for burial. Molag Bal delights in the art of necromancy, and Arkay's opposition to the undead contradicts Molag Bal's belief that corpses are tools for him to use to further his goals.{{ref|name=VOFIT}} In the legend of the first vampire's creation, Molag Bal invents a new form of undeath to spite Arkay's sway over the cycle of life and death.{{ref|name=ESORahaja}}{{ref|name=OLBTM}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Manifesto Cyrodiil Vampyrum}}}} While Arkay and Molag Bal never engage each other personally, their servants are likely to find themselves at odds with one another. The [[Lore:Order of Arkay|Order of Arkay]] is an organization that seeks to protect the autonomy of mortal souls and prevent their misuse by necromancers and Daedra. They oppose the necromantic energies wielded by Bal's cultists and the very existence of vampires.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Consecrations of Arkay}}}} Additionally, Arkay's blessing is capable of preventing mortal souls from being perverted by Molag Bal's dark influence.{{ref|name=ESOWillBroken|Events of [[ON:Will of the Broken|Will of the Broken]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOMallius|[[ON:Mallius Falto|Mallius Falto]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Will of the Broken|Will of the Broken]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The followers of [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]] have also opposed the God of Schemes. [[Lore:Cardinal Belforte|Cardinal Belforte]] of the [[Lore:Order of Stendarr|Order of Stendarr]] devoted his life to ridding Tamriel of Daedric corruption, and he was especially thorough in his persecution of Molag Bal's faithful. He acquired numerous tracts from the cults his order extinguished, which furthered scholars' understanding of the Daedric Prince's motivations.{{ref|name=OtNoC}}

===Harvest Hearts===
Harvest Hearts are artifacts that Molag Bal uses to enslave large communities and empower specific parts of his divine portfolio.{{ref|name=VanusHarvestHeart|[[Online:Vanus Galerion#The Harvest Heart|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Harvest Heart|The Harvest Heart]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

During the Planemeld, the Vestige destroyed a Heart within a portion of Coldharbour known as the Orchard, a large graveyard full of vampires. That particular heart greatly increased the rate at which vampirism spreads, and turned a few of the Coldharbour invasion force's own into bloodfiends. While that Heart was intact, the vampires in the Orchard were completely under Molag Bal's control, unable to disobey their orders to protect the area.{{ref|name=Virgar|[[Online:Virgar the Red|Virgar the Red]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Harvest Heart|The Harvest Heart]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Stones of Cold Fire===
The seven Stones of Cold Fire are artifacts that were gifted directly from Molag Bal and distributed throughout Daedric ruins on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] that were dedicated to the Lord of Lies. These stones hold the power to summon and compel Daedra. They were used during the Interregnum by [[Lore:Mistress Dratha|Mistress Dratha]] to make a deal with a Dremora named [[Lore:Xykenaz|Xykenaz]] to prolong her life. The stones were located at [[Lore:Ashalmawia|Ashalmawia]], [[Lore:Bal Ur|Bal Ur]], [[Lore:Esutanamus|Esutanamus]], [[Lore:Kushtashpi|Kushtashpi]], [[Lore:Ramimilk|Ramimilk]], [[Lore:Yansirramus|Yansirramus]], and [[Lore:Tusenend|Tusenend]].{{ref|name=ESOAAC|Events of [[ON:At Any Cost|At Any Cost]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Mistress Dratha#At Any Cost|Mistress Dratha]]'s dialogue during [[ON:At Any Cost|At Any Cost]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

The stones have a polished black surface and are cold to the touch. Blue flames swirl within them.{{ref|{{Item Link|Stone of Ramimilk|quality=1|itemid=94215}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

[[File:ON-item-Vampiric Shard.jpg|A vampiric shard|thumb|right|150px]]
===Vampiric Shards===
Vampiric Shards are foul relics that corrupt whatever they come in contact with. When the Saxhleel village of Haj Uxith was brought to Coldharbour, Molag Bal gave the Argonians one of these crystals to keep their [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] alive; it was the only thing sustaining the Hist in Coldharbour's inhospitable soil. Over time, the shard poisoned the Hist. The Hist was dying, but the shard kept it from passing, forcing it to endure constant torment.{{ref|[[ON:Treeminder Xohaneel|Treeminder Xohaneel]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Wisdom of the Ages|Wisdom of the Ages]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Coldharbour==
[[File:ON-place-Heart's Grief 02.jpg|thumb|left|Heart's Grief's vestibule, where the most wretched of failures are tormented]]
{{Lore:Coldharbour}}

Coldharbour is full of innumerable facilities, all built to further Molag Bal's dark schemes. Among these facilities is the [[Lore:Library of Dusk|Library of Dusk]], a library filled with propaganda that serves as a trap for scholars.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] text for the [[Online:Library of Dusk|Library of Dusk]] in [[Online:Loading Screens|ESO]]}} It is available for use by Molag Bal's minions.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}} There is another library in Coldharbour that is slightly less accessible. It is Molag Bal's personal collection, located in his stronghold. The shelves in Molag Bal's library span entire cliffs and run deep into a chasm. Unsurprisingly, plenty of the books on his shelves are very disturbing.{{ref|[[Online:Kireth Vanos|Kireth Vanos]]' dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-place-The Endless Stair.jpg|thumb|right|Molag Bal's citadel]]
The realm possesses several qualities that function as security measures. Things that teleport into Coldharbour will find themselves scattered, ending up in locations they did not intend to land.{{ref|name=ESOMalkur|[[ON:Malkur Valos|Malkur Valos]]' dialogue during [[ON:Hall of Judgment (quest)|Hall of Judgment]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Army of Meridia|The Army of Meridia]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Magical constructs known as [[Lore:Coldharbour Sentinel|Coldharbour Sentinels]] are used by Molag Bal to guide his vision in Coldharbour. With their aid, he can see into whatever prison they're anchored in. These Sentinels are magically connected, so destroying one will blind the other Sentinels it was bound to.{{ref|[[ON:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Soul Shriven in Coldharbour|Soul Shriven in Coldharbour]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Coldharbour mostly consists of prisons, but other facilities include those where research is conducted. He is fascinated by the metamagical technology of Soul Gems, and often has a mystical research project or two in the works to develop new and more efficient methods of stealing and imprisoning the souls of mortals.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens#Location Messages|Loading Screen]] text for [[Online:The Wailing Maw|the Wailing Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Not every part of Coldharbour serves a practical use; there are many graveyards in Molag Bal's plane of Oblivion for no other reason than because he likes them.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens#Location Messages|Loading Screen]] text for [[Online:Mal Sorra's Tomb|Mal Sorra's Tomb]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The giant skeletal structures in the Ossein Cage region are intended to instill a sense of horror and dread in those who walk its fractured valleys.{{ref|name=AOCS}}

===Servants===
Molag Bal maintains a strong military within his realm. He expects complete loyalty from his servants, and does not tolerate weakness among his ranks.{{ref|The [[Online:Duchess of Anguish|Duchess of Anguish]]'s appearance at [[Online:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|Events of [[ON:The Endless War|The Endless War]] and subsequent quests at the [[Online:Cliffs of Failure|Cliffs of Failure]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DA}} Daedra most commonly seen in his service include [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], [[Lore:Clannfear|Clannfear]], [[Lore:Daedroth|Daedroth]],{{ref|name=DD}} [[Lore:Grievous Twilight|Grievous Twilights]], [[Lore:Daedric Titan|Daedric Titans]], [[Lore:Cold-Flame Atronach|Cold-Flame Atronachs]], [[Lore:Banekin|Banekin]] and [[Lore:Harvester|Harvesters]]. [[Lore:Ogrim|Ogrim]], [[Lore:Scamp|Scamps]], [[Lore:Watcher|Watchers]], [[Lore:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilights]], [[Lore:Xivilai|Xivilai]], [[Lore:Spider Daedra|Spider Daedra]] and [[Lore:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducers]] are also found in his service. The [[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] are a Daedric race that is exclusively loyal to Molag Bal, and serve as his [[Lore:Coldharbour Elite Guard|personal guard]].{{ref|name=NarJou}} They behave much like their master; they constantly scheme against one another and they obsessively collect soul gems.{{ref|name=OX}}{{ref|name=ESOCEG|Appearance of the [[ON:Coldharbour Elite Guard|Coldharbour Elite Guard]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The hooved [[Lore:Maldrith|Maldrith]] are also counted among his ranks.{{Ref|name=MaldrithESO|Appearance of [[Online:Maldrith|Maldrith]]s at the [[Online:Ossein Cage|Ossein Cage]] in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Season of the Worm Cult|Season of the Worm Cult]]''}}{{Ref|name=AS|group=UOL}} They serve Molag Bal as roaming inspectors and inquisitors, and are feared by other Daedra.{{ref|name=MCoC|{{Cite Book|Maldriths: Correctors of Coldharbour}}}}

Molag Bal forbade ordinary [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame Atronachs]] from being summoned in his realm after a subordinate conjured one in his palace and damaged his favorite ice sculpture. Their presence within Coldharbour was eventually replaced by [[Lore:Cold-Flame Atronach|Cold-Flame Atronachs]].{{ref|name=DDCFA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Dossier: Cold-Flame Atronach}}}}

[[File:ON-npc-Duchess of Anguish 04.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Xivkyn]]<!--alt: [[File:ON-npc-Zeffij, Incinerator of Souls 02.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Xivkyn]]-->
The Dremora within his service are divided into several clans, the most prominent and powerful being the [[Lore:Deathbringer Clan|Deathbringers]] and the [[Lore:Foolkillers Clan|Foolkillers]], who struggle against each other for prominence within Molag Bal's ranks.{{ref|name=OOADC|{{Cite Book|Oath of a Dishonored Clan}}}} During the Interregnum, the Deathbringers were at the forefront of this struggle.{{ref|name=ESOTCMF|Events of [[ON:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Their Valkynaz was a trusted servant of Molag Bal who was tasked with guarding the key to the Prince's inner sanctum and had access to the Daedric Prince himself.{{ref|name=ESORCVanus|[[ON:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Three other Dremora are known to have been part of Molag Bal's inner council.{{ref|name=FAESO|[[ON:Clorsatari|Clorsatari]], [[ON:Kehothar|Kehothar]] and [[ON:Verkynvayl|Verkynvayl]]'s appearance and role during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Aside from playing a part in Coldharbour's politics, they fill the ranks as soldiers, couriers, guards, and torturers, among other roles.{{ref|name=IWSBAM|{{Cite Book|I was Summoned by a Mortal}}}}{{ref|name=ESOWailing|The [[ON:Dremora|Dremora]]'s role in the [[ON:The Wailing Prison|Wailing Prison]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Darkbinders|Darkbinders]] are a low-ranking Dremora clan that serve as jailers within Coldharbour. Darkbinders are occasionally assigned to administer punishment to soul shriven who are of particular interest to Molag Bal.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Our Darkbinder Allies}}}}

It is a fairly simple task to discern a minion of Molag Bal from those in service to another Prince: the easiest way to tell is by looking at their eyes. The eyes of Daedra within Molag Bal's service tend to be icy blue, just like their masters', and many of them have blue or blue-grey skin.{{ref|name=KCAHC|[[Books:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]]; ''Ogrim and Scamp'', ''Winged Twilight''}}{{ref|name=ESODaedra|Appearance of Daedra found in [[ON:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] (such as blue [[ON:Ogrim|Ogrim]] and black [[ON:Spider Daedra|Spider Daedra]]) and those that are sent to Mundus from Coldharbour (such as grey [[ON:Clannfear|Clannfear]])}} It is believed to be because Molag Bal likes to twist things into his own image.{{Ref|name=AS|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Andrew Siañez-De La O's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}} Stonefire Scamps are particularly easy to discern as servants of Molag Bal, as their flesh and fur are a rich blue-grey, their eyes are the same tone as their master's and they have deep black horns.{{ref|name=StonefireScamp|Appearance of [[Online:Stonefire Scamp|Stonefire]] [[ON:Scamp|Scamps]] during Molag Bal's occupation of the [[ON:Imperial City|Imperial City]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Daedrat|Daedrat]]s and [[Lore:Fiendroth|Fiendroth]]s can be seen scuttling around in Coldharbour and in places on Mundus where Molag Bal's influence is pervasive.{{ref|Appearance of [[Online:Daedrat|Daedrats]] and [[Online:Fiendroth|Fiendroths]] in [[Online:Ashalmawia|Ashalmawia]] and the Daedra-infested [[Online:Imperial City Sewers|Imperial Sewers]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Slate-Skinned Daedrats are a breed whose distinct markings and blue eyes stem from their diet of corpses that have been touched by the Lord of Brutality. While not brazen minions of Molag Bal, they bear characteristics common in his servants, and are often studied by those interested in researching or thwarting the Prince.{{ref|name=SlateDaedrat|[[Online:Slate-Skinned Daedrat|Slate-Skinned Daedrat]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-creature-Dread Watcher.jpg|thumb|left|A Watcher that serves Molag Bal]]
The rules that govern Molag Bal's minions are outlined in two known doctrines: the ''Seven-Hundred-and-One Edicts'' is the primary set of rules, and the ''Mandatory Codicils'' cover special cases. Breaking one of these rules lands the offending party a sentence to a facility known as the Scathe-Rings, where those imprisoned can be sentenced to many excruciating punishments.{{ref|name=ESOLCLoad|Loading screen text for the Lightless Cell in [[Online:Loading Screens|ESO]]}} One such punishment is "Second-Degree Gradual Discorporation".{{ref|name=TLO}} Strict uniform codes are among the guidelines that govern Bal's servants.{{ref|name=CM63D|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 63: Dremora Style}}}}{{ref|name=CMX}} Those whose service is exceptional (i.e.: exceeding quotas in certain realms of employment) may earn themselves time in the Bliss-Cells.{{ref|name=TSPC}} 

Daedra are not the only ones rewarded in Coldharbour's work culture. Soul-Shriven slaves who inform on their compatriots can be rewarded with shifts of lighter labor or a visit to the Grotto of Depravity,{{ref|[[Online:The Grotto of Depravity|The Grotto of Depravity]] [[Online:Loading Screens/Coldharbour|loading screen]] text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} a cave that serves as the site of an eternal party. This doesn't mean the shriven get to have fun: there's a high probability that the Daedra who are actually enjoying themselves will torment the soulless husks in some manner, and shriven suffering is often mandated.{{ref|name=ParThe|{{Cite Book|Party Theme|ns_base=Online}}}} Despite this, the shriven assigned to the grotto are permitted brief reprieve, even fishing in the plasm pools under constant Daedric supervision.{{ref|[[Online:Shriven Traveler|Shriven Travelers]] fishing at [[Online:The Grotto of Depravity|the Grotto of Depravity]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

If one of Molag Bal's servants is "put on trial" for their transgressions, they are tried in a facility known as the [[Lore:Court of Contempt|Court of Contempt]], where they are entitled to a counsel of [[Lore:Scamp|Scamps]] outfitted in puffy wigs. Scamps are not allowed to speak in the Court of Contempt, and the Court has a one-hundred-percent conviction rate of defendants, otherwise known as "the Guilty". These trials are mostly for show and never end fairly.{{ref|name=PCC|{{Cite Book|Protocols of the Court of Contempt}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|On the Matter of the Prisoners|ns_base=Online}}}} One notable instance of a minion incurring the Daedric Prince's wrath is an incident known as "The Seat of Tyranny Rude Cushion Incident", wherein a Dremora named Xykenaz was imprisoned in a nanoplane for seven epochs for putting a whoopie-cushion on Molag Bal's throne.{{ref|[[Online:Prison of Xykenaz|Prison of Xykenaz]] [[Online:Loading Screens/Morrowind|loading screen]] text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

[[File:ON-skill-Daedroth's Heat.jpg|thumb|right|A Daedroth general in service to Bal]]
Molag Bal has appointed quite a few powerful servants to serve as generals or other high-ranking officers. Among these servants are spies,{{ref|[[Online:Yggmanei|Yggmanei]]'s appearance at [[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Menta Na|agents of chaos]], [[Lore:Lord Warden Dusk|brutal prison wardens]], and [[Lore:Molag Kena|gifted mages]].{{ref|name=DA|[[Online:Molag Bal#Dark Anchors|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue at [[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MBalGeneral|[[Online:Molag Bal #Summoning Generals|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue while summoning a general at [[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Molag Bal values cunning as well as strength, and seems to grant power to those he finds interesting or strong... even if their claim to fame is upsetting the balance of power among the other Daedra within his service. Though he would prefer his military hierarchy remains stable, he has rewarded servants of his for displays of guile in the past.{{ref|name=DA}}{{ref|name=ESOLordWarden}}{{ref|The fact that [[Online:Lyranth|Lyranth]] is still breathing after the events of [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]] and [[Online:Summary Execution|Summary Execution]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Molag Bal has also employed a scribe named [[Lore:Denogorath|Denogorath]], whose full title is "Dread Archivist and Promulgator of Mandatory Doctrine".{{ref|name=ESODenogorath|[[Lore:Books by Author#Denogorath the Dread Archivist|Denogorath]]'s name, title and purpose as entailed by his three available publications, which can be found in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Mortals also serve the Daedric Prince in his realm. Apart from the cultists that worship him on Nirn, Molag Bal has the [[Lore:Soul-Shriven|Soul-Shriven]] and Mind-Shriven as slaves, with the Dremora as their common overseers.{{ref|Appearance of the [[ON:Soul-Shriven|Soul-Shriven]] and [[ON:Mind-Shriven|Mind-Shriven]], and their [[ON:Dremora|Dremora]] overseers in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Vampires may also be found serving the Daedric Prince.{{ref|Appearance of the vampire guards in [[ON:The Orchard|The Orchard]] and the working vampires in [[ON:The Black Forge|The Black Forge]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LanJour|{{Cite Book|Lanath's Journal}}}}

The extent that the ''Seven-Hundred-and-One Edicts'' applies to mortals is not known, but there are consequences for mortals who displease Molag Bal nonetheless. Agents who disappoint the Daedric Prince harshly enough are pitted against each other in an endless war at the [[Lore:Cliffs of Failure|Cliffs of Failure]]. The basic premise of the "game" at the Cliffs is for the participants to fight to win back the Dark Lord's favor.{{ref|name=TEWObserver}} However, there is no end to the game, and these individuals are doomed to fight for Molag Bal's amusement forever.{{ref|name=ESOAngof}} Mortals and Daedra who anger Molag Bal severely are tormented in the vestibule of Heart's Grief.{{ref|[[Online:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue as you enter Heart's Grief Vestibule during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} These individuals have usually failed the Daedric Prince{{ref|name=GOSAelif|[[On:Aelif|Aelif]]'s presence in [[ON:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]] during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} or committed some other egregious infraction.{{ref|name=GOSMannimarco|[[Online:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s presence in [[ON:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]] during [[On:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
