The [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]] (also called '''Castle Solitude'''){{Ref|name=BotWQ|{{Cite Book|Biography of the Wolf Queen}}}} is a large castle found on the eastern end of the city of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]. It is the seat of power in [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar Hold]], where the Jarls of Solitude and even the High Kings of Skyrim occupy its grandiose and stately halls. The northeast wing contains the Jarl and their court's living quarters, while the southwest wing, known as the Pelagius Wing, is in a mass state of disrepair and avoided since the death of the eponymous [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Mad King]] of Solitude.{{Ref|name=WtW|{{Cite Book|Walking the World, Vol XI}}}} The Imperial Gardens in the palace courtyard contains a formal and motley assortment of various regional flora like [[Lore:Thistle Branch|thistle branches]] and [[Lore:Douad Shrub|Douad shrubs]].{{Ref|name=TWQ3|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v3}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
===First – Second Eras===
The Blue Palace was built sometime before {{Year|1E 143}}. As the city expanded across the [[Lore:Great Arch|Great Arch]], the Avenues District was one of the last places built in the city, and it all culminated into the Blue Palace, which is right on the tip of the arch, directly above the [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River]]. It later became the seat of power after the consolidation of Skyrim in the midst of High King [[Lore:Harald|Harald]]'s ascension to the throne.{{Ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} Before then, the Jarls of Haafingar ruled from the [[Lore:Emperor's Tower|Emperor's Tower]], the citadel of Castle Dour atop of the hill in Solitude.{{Ref|name=WtW}}

In the late [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the people of Solitude laid siege against the Blue Palace, at the time occupied by High Queen "Head-Rolling" [[Lore:Roleke|Roleke]], who killed over twenty rebels in less than five minutes. In that siege, Roleke was murdered in battle by her several children, who in turn died from their injuries. With no one else to rule, [[Lore:Irthvyd|Irthvyd]] of a long-forgotten hold was named the new King of Solitude, with blessings from [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] herself. Years later, [[Lore:Alldimar|Alldimar]] became the leader of Solitude and during his reign, suspicious accidents took place all over the city, and servants in the Blue Palace would hear strange chanting in the high king's quarters. After his death, the truth had been revealed when a secret compartment in his room had every skull of every victim in the past year. To this day, no one knows what Alldimar's intentions were for the collection but ever since then, he earned the moniker, "Ghostmaker".{{Ref|name=SOSR|{{Cite Book|Scandals of Solitude's Royalty}}}}

When High King [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]] ruled over Solitude and the Kingdom of [[Lore:Western Skyrim|Western Skyrim]], a royal delegation sent by the Warrior-Poet [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] traveled to the Blue Palace and presented the King with a table string now known as [[Lore:Jarlsbane|Jarlsbane]]. But because the court musicians denounced its sound, it sat in the Blue Palace for many years as a curiosity until it was discovered by the Master Bard of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]], [[Lore:Endroni Selvilo|Endroni Selvilo]] and later donated to the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]] by High King [[Lore:Svargrim|Svargrim]].{{Ref|name=JB|{{Cite Book|Jarlsbane}}}}

By the mid-sixth century of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the Blue Palace was occupied by High King Svargrim and High Queen [[Lore:Gerhyld|Gerhyld]], until the latter was assassinated by the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]] in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{Ref|name=ONClouds|[[Online:Dark Clouds Over Solitude|Dark Clouds Over Solitude]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The tragedy was the start of a series of events that threatened Western Skyrim as a whole. Princess [[Lore:Svana|Svana]], along with [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]], [[Lore:Fennorian|Fennorian]] of [[Lore:House Ravenwatch|House Ravenwatch]], and an [[Lore:The Vestige|adventurer]] would meet in the Blue Palace to discuss their plans against the Gray Host until the attack on Solitude.{{Ref|name=ONHost|[[Online:The Gray Host|The Gray Host]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The ceremony after their victory was held in the Blue Palace's courtyard and it had High King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] in attendance.{{Ref|name=ONDaughter|[[Online:Daughter of the Wolf|Daughter of the Wolf]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

===Third – Fourth Eras===
In {{Year|3E 81}}, the elderly King [[Lore:Mantiarco|Mantiarco]] marries [[Lore:Potema|Potema Septim]] of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], who took residence in the Blue Palace. On several occasions, her mother, Dowager-Empress [[Lore:Quintilla|Quintilla]] visited the Blue Palace and was known to have frequently strolled through the Imperial Gardens.{{Ref|name=TWQ6|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v6}}}} Eventually, the King passes away and their son, [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel III]] becomes the King of Solitude and rules jointly with his mother. Throughout the years, Wolf-Queen Potema invited various diplomats and political figures across the Empire's northern tier, from other kingdoms in Skyrim and nations in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. As time went on, the years of negotiation and intermingling culminated into the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]] in {{Year|3E 121}}. The conflict ended in favor of the Third Empire in {{Year|3E 137}}, after a month-long siege on the Blue Palace. The Wolf-Queen died in the palace and it was burned to the ground. Even when it was rebuilt, according to local myth, her spirit is infused within the castle and drives its ruler into madness.{{Ref|name=BotWQ}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|421}}

It is one of the several theories that explain the madness of King [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]]. King Pelagius ruled after his aunt's death, by the order of his father, then King [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus Septim]].{{Ref|name=BotWQ}} He was infamous for his eccentric behavior and strange outbursts. He moved from the throne of Solitude to become the Emperor of Tamriel in {{Year|3E 145}}. His sister, [[Lore:Jolethe|Jolethe]] took his place as the Queen of Solitude.{{Ref|name=TMoP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} Ever since his death in {{Year|3E 153}}, another myth is that his spirit haunts the southwest wing and ever since then, the Pelagius Wing has been abandoned, as far as {{Year|4E 201}}.{{Ref|name=WtW}} During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late Third Era, the Solitude Palace was occupied by Queen Hania, who would often give out quests to any adventurer that approached her court.{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Palaces|Palaces]] sub-location in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

By the third century of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the Blue Palace was occupied by the Regent Jarl of Solitude, [[Lore:Elisif the Fair|Elisif the Fair]].{{Ref|name=SRBlue|[[Skyrim:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]] sub-location in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} At the time, she was in possession of a [[Lore:Stone of Barenziah|Stone of Barenziah]], which was later stolen by the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]], who in turn re-assembled the crown it belonged to.{{Ref|name=SRStone|[[Skyrim:No Stone Unturned|No Stone Unturned]] faction quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} An [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|adventurer]] entered the Pelagius Wing after they were tipped off by a [[Lore:Dervenin|madman]] looking for his [[Lore:Sheogorath|master]]. Unbeknownst to the rest of the palace, the adventurer emerged from the abandoned wing with the [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]].{{Ref|name=SRMadness|[[Skyrim:The Mind of Madness|The Mind of Madness]] daedric quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notes==
*In the context of ''[[Lore:The Wolf Queen|The Wolf Queen]]'' books series originally released for ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind]]'' (which coined the Blue Palace), the Blue Palace was rather explicitly a location in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. The designers of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim]]'' seemingly misunderstood the context and assumed it was located in the city-state of Solitude where most of the ''The Wolf Queen'' takes place, and thus the Blue Palace appears in ''Skyrim'' as the city's castle.{{Ref|name=TWQ|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen}}}} The discrepancy has not been explained in-world.
*The Blue Palace has a hereditary office called the "Royal Cheesemonger". They go under a strict inspection of the milk imported into the city, determining which barrel is deemed worthy to make Solitude Eidar, the city's signature cheese. They are extremely rare and are preserved in the vaults underneath the city. This office was instated by Jarl [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]], who entered office in {{Year|2E 431}}.{{Ref|name=COSHH|{{Cite Book|Cheeses of Skyrim: Hjaalmarch, Haafingar}}}}
