The '''Dragonfires''' were mystical fires that powered the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|liminal barrier]] between the [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|Planes of Oblivion]] and [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], created from the blood of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and given to [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]]. This barrier protected against invasion by the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].{{ref|name=LtD|[[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=DoB|Dialogue from the [[Online:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} The fires were relit whenever an heir ascended to the Ruby Throne. They were kept burning throughout his or her lifetime and represented the Covenant between the Cyrodilic Empire and Akatosh.

The pedestal that served as a pyre may be found in the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]] in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], though there seems to have been no apparent connection between the ritual and the Alessian teachings. A [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] Emperor had to use the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] to light the Dragonfires in the Sublime Brazier, a sacred cauldron located in the Dragonfire Cathedral deep beneath the {{Lore Link|Imperial Sewers}}, said to stretch to the very heart of Nirn, and described as Akatosh's "first light". Once the Sublime Brazier was kindled, it would light the Dragonfires in the Temple of the One.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sublime Brazier}}}} The Dragonfires could also be lit directly from the Temple of the One.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The darkening of the Dragonfires played an essential role in two [[Lore:Daedric|Daedric]] invasions of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The first occurred in {{Year|2E 579}}, when Emperor [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] of the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] misused the Amulet of Kings in a ritual that Varen's advisor [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] had convinced him would curry Akatosh's favor and make Varen a Dragonborn. Instead, the ritual violated the Covenant with Akatosh and caused the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]], which set Nirn adrift in Mundus and made it come "unmoored from the fabric of the [[Lore:Aurbis|multiverse"]].{{Ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}{{ref|name=ILTESO|[[Online:Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online]]}} [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] took the opportunity to begin rending the souls of Nirn's mortals from their hosts to use as fuel for the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], the process of merging Nirn with [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] using devices called Dark Anchors. Fortunately, a [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] who had been a victim of Mannimarco and Molag Bal's schemes arose to unite the warring nations of Tamriel against Molag Bal, invading Coldharbour and halting the Planemeld in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Molag Bal sought to destroy the Sublime Brazier to prevent the Dragonfires from ever being relit, sending a Xivkyn General known as Malivus to spearhead an attack on the temple. This attack was repelled by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], the [[Lore:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]] and Captain [[Lore:Anatolius Caudex|Anatolius Caudex]] using siege weapons. While the attack was repelled, Father Egnatius, a priest of Akatosh, performed a ritual on the Brazier. Once this ritual was complete the Drake of Blades became the immortal guardian of the Brazier and the Dragonfire Cathedral, blessed with the power of Akatosh himself and forever bound to the temple.{{ref|name=TSubBraz|[[Online:The Sublime Brazier|The Sublime Brazier]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Vestige also later stopped an alternate form of the Planemeld ritual called the Planemeld Obverse from being carried out by the [[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] Molag Kena at [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]].{{ref|name=ESO}}

With the ascension of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] as Emperor of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] at the end of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the Dragonfire ritual was resumed while the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]] reigned, but in {{Year|3E 433}} that dynasty was broken and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] threatened Tamriel with an [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|invasion]] of his own.{{Ref|name=TAoK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} With the assassination of Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] and all of his legitimate heirs by the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] cult,{{Ref|name=BHCA|1={{Cite Book|Assassination!}}}} no known Dragonborn heir remained to relight the Dragonfires. The barriers between Oblivion and Mundus were broken, allowing stable portals to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] to be created. With each Gate that opened, the barriers between Nirn and Oblivion weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [[Lore:Astronomy|Firmament]], would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.{{ref|name=OBMartin}}{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} When [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]], the illegitimate son of the late Emperor, smashed the Amulet of Kings to summon an avatar of Akatosh, the barriers were resealed permanently, thus ending the long but now obsolete tradition.{{ref|name=OB}} The new barrier created by Martin's sacrifice was noted to be too powerful for even a [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]], an object in the presence of which it was said even "a thousand [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gems]] would likely dissolve", to breach for more than fleeting moments.{{ref|name=LtD}}{{ref|name=Excavation|[[Skyrim:Excavation Leader's Journal|Excavation Leader's Journal]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}}

==Notes==
*Some sources also speak of [[Lore:Aurora|the aurora]] or "Kyne's lights", and the [[Lore:Moons|Lunar Lattice]], also serving as boundaries that protect [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] from harmful influence from beyond.{{Ref|name=Celestial|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 41: Celestial Style}}}}{{Ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Some sources describe the Lunar Lattice as burning spirits that touch it with moonlight fire, causing even [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] to be set aflame and fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said by those sources that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Merrunz]], released to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe.{{ref|name=Boethra|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}} This might suggest the influence of the Dragonfires was potentially not the sole protection of Mundus.
