# Lore:Wisp

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Bestiary Trail}}__NOTOC__[[File:SR-creature-Wisp.jpg|left|thumb|Wisp]][[File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Wispmother.jpg|Wispmother and wisps|right|thumb]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Wisp|Wisps]], also known as '''Witchlights''',{{ref|[[Online:Witchlight|Witchlight]]s in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} are enigmatic, floating entities often found in the vicinity of [[#Wispmother|Wispmother]]s, although they can also exist independently.{{ref|name=SR|[[Skyrim:Wisp|Wisp]] and [[SR:Wispmother|Wispmother]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Current consensus on wisps is divided, with it being suggested they are scavengers that lure prey to the Wispmother and share in the psychoetherial energy released by her kills. Another theory states that they are merely emanations or conjurations of the Wispmother and not free-living creatures, which is supported by the fact that killing a Wispmother also kills the wisps surrounding her, leaving only [[Lore:Glow Dust|glow dust]] behind.{{Ref|name=TW|{{Cite Book|The Wispmother}}}}<noinclude>

[[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]] is known to send wisps to herald the storms of Rain's Hand. To see one is to see the promise of new growth, new life, and a new chapter in nature's cycle.{{ref|name=Galen Wisp|[[Online:Galen Wisp|Galen Wisp]] collectible description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The followers of the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]] consider Wisps to be gifts from Y'ffre, signaling periods of change, and believe they carry fortunes and omens of what is yet to come. Some Bosmer were known to commune with the Wisps to conduct fortune readings.{{Ref|name=Phythia|[[ON:Phythia|Phythia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

==Wispmothers==
[[File:SR-misc-MothersDay Art22.jpg|Wispmother|right|thumb]]
'''Wispmothers''' (sometimes '''wispfolk''' or '''wispfathers'''){{ref|[[Castles:Wispfolk|Wispfolk]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} are magical-energy beings thought to be either [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]], [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] or [[Lore:Nature Spirits|nature spirits]],{{ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Black Marsh}}}}{{ref|name=TW}}{{ref|name=ASpell}} and they can be found in the wilds of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Wispmothers are capable of speech and are known to refer to wisps as their children.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Sentient Beasts of the Gallery}}}} Killing a wispmother causes all of its attendant wisps and shades to die. Legends in Black Marsh state that wispmothers cannot be truly destroyed, and that their essence must be sealed, or else they will rise again eventually.{{ref|[[Online:Xijai-Teel|Xijai-Teel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] they are known as the '''Amronal''',{{ref|[[Online:The Amronal of Valenwood|The Amronal of Valenwood]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ASpell|{{Cite Book|The Amronal's Spell}}}} though they are much rarer than the wispmothers of Skyrim.{{ref|name=TW|{{Cite Book|The Wispmother}}}}

Wispmothers can use illusionary magic to alter their appearance to appear like mer.{{ref|[[Online:Alanya|Alanya]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They are able to teleport and restore their health. Wispmothers can also summon multiple shades, which are weak clones of themselves. More powerful wispmothers are also capable of some form of necromancy, where they send out wisps to possess and reanimate nearby corpses.{{ref|name=SR}}

Many legends and theories exist which seek to explain the true nature of wispmothers. Nords variously believe them to be [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]] of the ancient [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] (as supported by their tapered ears) or natives of [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]].{{ref|name=TW}}{{ref|name=ASpell}} Master Sadren Sarethi proposed that they are a forgotten type of [[Lore:Lich|lich]] from the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. Lydette Viliane of the [[Lore:Synod|Synod]] proposed that they are elemental personifications of snow or mist.{{ref|name=TW}}

The spirits of some dead mortals can take on the form of Wispmothers.{{ref|name=ONLost|[[Online:Lost Maiden|Lost Maiden]], [[Online:Saradin the Lost Love|Saradin the Lost Love]] and [[Online:Mercedene the Cursed|Mercedene the Cursed]]  in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At least one wind spirit also manifested in a similar form.{{Ref|name=TFOT|Events of [[ON:Tower Full of Trouble|Tower Full of Trouble]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

Wisp wrappings and glow dust, both used in alchemy, can be gathered from vanquished wispmothers.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Wisp Wrappings|Wisp Wrappings]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Additionally they are capable of leaving behind physical remains, such as their heads.{{ref|[[Online:Elaldor|Elaldor]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
