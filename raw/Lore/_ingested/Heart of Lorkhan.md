# Lore:Heart of Lorkhan

{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''But when [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] and [[Lore:Auriel|Auriel]] tried to destroy the Heart of Lorkhan it laughed at them. It said, "This Heart is the heart of the world, for one was made to satisfy the other." So Auriel fastened the thing to an arrow and let it fly long into the sea, where no aspect of the new world may ever find it.''|''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}}</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] is the "divine spark" of the deity most commonly known by his [[Lore:Altmer|Aldmeri]] name, [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]].{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} It appears in other cultures as concepts such as Heart of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]],{{ref|name=FSKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} the "hunger" of [[Lore:Sep|Sep]],{{ref|name=TM}} and others. The "star-wounded East"{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}} gained its name when the Heart fell from the sky like a [[Lore:Meteors|meteor]],{{ref|name=TLL|{{Cite Book|The Lunar Lorkhan}}}} crashing into the [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] region in ancient times, where it formed [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]].{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} 

In the mythology of [[Lore:The Towers|the Towers]], the Heart of Lorkhan was known as the First Stone, the Stone of Red Tower (Red Mountain), a focal point for the Tower's reality altering power, upon which the rules according to which the Tower shaped its domain, were inscribed. The First Stone and its Tower are said to have allowed [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] to exist without the full presence of the divine, and granted it a special kind of divinity, which is called "NIRN", the "consequence of variable [[Lore:Fate|fate]]".{{ref|group=UOL|''[[General:Nu-Mantia Intercept|Nu-Mantia Intercept]]''}}{{ref|name=RMM|{{Cite Book|Red Mountain's Might}}}}{{Ref|name=Enigma|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Some sources claim that [[Lore:Hero|Heroes]] are created by the Heart of Lorkhan as a defense mechanism for the mortal world, and that the Shade of [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] shrouds their doom.{{ref|name=OBDoomstones|Messages given by [[Oblivion:Doomstones|Doomstones]] in {{g|oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=CCAP|{{Cite Book|Chaotic Creatia: The Azure Plasm}}}}

The Heart of Lorkhan was discovered in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], who fashioned [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|tools]] to harness its power.{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}} The Dwemer wanted to use this power to create a [[Lore:Anumidium|new god of brass]], but [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|disappeared]] before it could be activated.{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[Morrowind:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} The Heart was later used by the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] and [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] to become gods themselves.{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=Morrowind}}

Thereafter, the Heart of Lorkhan was visited annually by the Tribunal in order to renew their powers, until {{Year|2E 882}}, when [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] awoke and ambushed the Tribunal. The event resulted in the Tribunal being cut off from access to the Heart of Lorkhan.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] responded by beginning development on a new Heart of Lorkhan, called the [[Lore:Mechanical Heart|Mechanical Heart]], as a backup to the loss of the original.{{ref|name=TRMTCC|Events that occur in the mission [[Legends:The Red Mountain|The Red Mountain]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} Dagoth Ur would later attempt to create his own brass god, [[Lore:Akulakhan|Akulakhan]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{Ref|name=DUP}} This plan was thwarted in {{Year|3E 427}} by the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]], and as a result, the Heart of Lorkhan seemingly vanished from [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}<noinclude>

According to some sources, the red gemstone that is the centerpiece of the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], originated as a drop of blood that fell from the Heart of Lorkhan as it flew across Tamriel. The blood fell into an [[Lore:Ayleid Wells|Ayleid Well]] and was fused into a strong red crystal by the [[Lore:Magic|magicka]] within. The [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] found this crystal, and cut and polished it down to Chim-el Adabal, the Amulet of Kings.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad}}}}{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}

==History==
{{Quote Box|''Beneath Red Mountain, Dwemer miners discovered a great magical stone. By diverse methods, Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]], High Priest and Magecrafter of the ancient [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], determined that this magical stone was the heart of the god [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], cast here in the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] as a punishment for his mischief in creating the mortal world.''|''[[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools (book)|Kagrenac's Tools]]''}}
===Dawn Era===
Every [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]ic mythic tradition has an archetypal Missing God in its pantheon. This god is credited with convincing or tricking the Original Spirits  into creating the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], the Mortal Plane. He then died or was killed for doing so, was separated from his "divine center", and left to wander creation in this state.{{ref|name=TM}} Examples include:

====Lorkhan====
[[File:ON-place-Balfiera.png|thumb|right|Adamantine Tower, site of Lorkhan's death]]
[[File:ON-place-Red Mountain.jpg|thumb|right|Red Mountain, where the Heart of Lorkhan came to rest]]
After the Mundus was created during [[Lore:Dawn Era#Convention|Convention]], Lorkhan was slain by [[Lore:Auriel|Auriel]] and [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] atop [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]];{{ref|name=TOA|{{Cite Book|Tower of Adamant}}}} it is said Trinimac reached into Lorkhan and tore out his Heart with "more than hands".{{ref|name=TM}} However, the Heart laughed at them and refused to die, for it was one with the new world. Instead, Auriel fastened the Heart to an arrow and shot it across the world into the sea.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=TOA}}

====Sep, the Second Serpent====
[[Lore:Ruptga|Ruptga]], the Tall Papa of the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n pantheon, created [[Lore:Sep|Sep]] out of past worldskins shed by [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]], the First Serpent. Though Sep shared some of the hunger of Satakal, he helped Tall Papa guide the other spirits to the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]] where they could avoid being eaten by First Serpent. Eventually, Sep grew tired of this, gathered all the old worldskins into a ball (the Mundus), and convinced many spirits to live there. However, they eventually began to die, and found it was no longer possible to reach the Far Shores. Ruptga refused to help them, telling them they had to find new paths or live on through their children. He also punished Sep, beating him to death with a stick, whereupon his hunger (the Heart of Lorkhan) fell out. The Second Serpent was left to "slink around in a dead skin".{{ref|name=TM}}

====Lorkhaj, the Moon Beast====
In the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]i tradition, [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]] was the last of [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]]'s many children, and his Heart was filled with a Great Darkness which was given the name [[Lore:Namira|Namiira]]. Before her death, Fadomai told another child, [[Lore:Nirn|Nirni]], to bear many children. But Nirni had no place for them, so she sought Lorkhaj for advice; Lorkhaj created [[Lore:Mundus|a place for Nirni]] and put her there to give birth. But due to the Great Darkness in his Heart, Lorkhaj was influenced to trick his other siblings into going there as well, and they became trapped. [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Some of Fadomai's children]] died to stabilize the new world, and the survivors punished Lorkhaj for his trickery by tearing out his Heart, hiding it deep within the new world, and cursing him to "walk Nirni for many phases".{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} Some ancient songs attribute [[Lore:Merid-Nunda|Merid-Nunda]] as being the one that convinced the siblings to enact revenge.{{Ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}} Ancient Khajiiti scriptures predating the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar Epiphany|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]] state that Lorkhaj survived this event, and fled to Azurah with the hole in his chest. Azurah saw that Namiira still dwelled within his wound, keeping him alive and corrupting him by imitating his heart. Azurah cleansed Lorkhaj of the corruption and flung his [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]] into the Void, and Lorkhaj perished within Azurah's embrace.{{ref|name=ESOFDF|{{Cite Book|The Favored Daughter of Fadomai}}}} From the Dark Heart came the twisted shade of Lorkhaj known as the Moon Beast, the first [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|dro-m'Athra]]. And so Lorkhaj represents the duality of Khajiiti souls and their susceptibility towards the Bent Dance.{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}

====Lorkh, the Sower of Flesh====
The Reachmen creation story speaks of Lorkh having an epiphany when he visited the darkness; that what is perceived as nothingness is ripe for possibility.{{ref|[[Online:The Dark Descent|The Dark Descent]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} And so, Lorkh approached Namira, who ruled the world of spirit. Lorkh convinced her to grant him a place in the infinite void to create a realm for wayward spirits. To make this a possibility, Lorkh sacrificed himself to create a harsh realm, one that is unforgiving and intended to teach through suffering.{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} Although his endeavor led to his sundering, Lorkh "still believed in the light of [[Lore:Men|Man]]", whose ancestors fled into the mountains from the darkness emitted from Lorkh's heart.{{ref|name=ONNZ|[[Online:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Thus, to the Reachmen, Lorkh serves as an example to fight against the darkness in their hearts.{{ref|name=ONArdanir|[[Online:Ardanir|Ardanir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

====Lunar Lorkhan====
[[File:SR-photo-Astronomy.jpg|thumb|right|Masser and Secunda]]
One amongst the many "Heart Stories" claims that the moons, [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]], were and are the two halves of Lorkhan's "flesh-divinity'" (his literal body). Where the other [[Lore:Divines|Eight Divines]] (here represented as plane(t)s) each gave a part of themselves to create the Mundus, Lorkhan's plane(t) was split in half; his divine spark fell to Nirn as a literal shooting star, in order to "impregnate it with the measure of its existence and a reasonable amount of selfishness."{{ref|name=TLL}} 

===First Era===
At some point by the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] miners discovered a mysterious, magical stone beneath [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]]. The Dwemer Tonal Architect Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]] determined that this stone was the Heart of Lorkhan, and constructed a set of [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|tools]] to harness its powers. His goal was to create a new god for the Dwemer—[[Lore:Anumidium|Anumidium]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=KT}}{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} This god took the form of a colossal brass golem called [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]],{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}} and was the key to the Dwemeri bid for ascension, be it godhood, immortality, or something greater.{{ref|name=NARM}}{{ref|name=MWYagrum|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}}

By some accounts, Kagrenac constructed Anumidium without the knowledge of the Dwemer King [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac]]. The [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] learned of it, however, and Dumac's claims of innocence when confronted by his friend Lord [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] sparked the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]].{{ref|name=NARM}} The war culminated around {{Year|1E 700}} with the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]]; while again accounts vary widely, most agree that Nerevar, [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]], and others made their way into the Heart Chamber beneath the Dwarven citadel.{{ref|name=FSKW}}{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=NARM}}

In the ensuing battle, Kagrenac (or Dagoth Ur, by [[Lore:Azura|Azura]]'s instruction) used the Tools on the Heart of Lorkhan, which caused the Dwemer to immediately vanish from Nirn.{{ref|name=KT}}{{ref|name=NARM}} Nerevar died (or was murdered){{ref|name=NARM}} soon after, the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] took possession of Kagrenac's tools, and used them to tap the Heart to fuel their apotheoses,{{ref|name=KT}}{{ref|name=NARM}} and thereafter made an annual pilgrimage to the Heart Chamber to recharge their powers.{{ref|name=DUP}}

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 882}}, Dagoth Ur, who was believed to have died beneath Red Mountain during the battle, reawakened, along with numerous members of the [[Lore:Sixth House|Sixth House]], also thought wiped out during the battle. They seized control of the Heart Chamber, and Dagoth Ur bound them all to the Heart of Lorkhan, himself included, through unknown means. The Tribunal were driven off when they made their annual pilgrimage, and henceforth could no longer renew their powers. In an apparent adoption of the views and motivations of Kagrenac, Dagoth began construction of a Second Numidium, [[Lore:Akulakhan|Akulakhan]], to be powered by the Heart of Lorkhan.{{ref|name=DUP}}

===Third Era===
In {{Year|3E 417}}, two of Kagrenac's tools, [[Lore:Keening|Keening]] and [[Lore:Sunder|Sunder]], were stolen by the forces of the Sixth House.{{ref|name=DUP}} Ten years later, an outlander sent by Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] arrived in [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], and was believed by many to be the reincarnation of Nerevar—the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]]. After many trials, the Nerevarine recovered Kagrenac's stolen tools and used them to disrupt the enchantments on the Heart of Lorkhan,{{ref|name=PTDDU|{{Cite Book|Plan to Defeat Dagoth Ur}}}} destroying Dagoth Ur and Akulakhan in the process.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} Azura claims the Heart was freed from its prison{{ref|[[Morrowind:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in the ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} of the mortal plane,{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on [https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2010/12/26/decrypting-the-elder-scrolls.aspx Decrypting The Elder Scrolls] [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}} and the people of Tamriel commonly believe that the Heart of Lorkhan was destroyed.{{ref|name=LTHR|Dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The Heart, Revealed|The Heart, Revealed]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}{{ref|[[SR:Septimus Signus|Septimus Signus]]' dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} 

The Heart of Lorkhan would serve as an important facet in the teachings of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] and the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] in {{Year|3E 433}}, citing the lasting presence of the Heart after the god's death as evidence that Lorkhan is [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|<i>Oblivion</i>]]}}

===Fourth Era===
Many years prior to {{Year|4E 201}}, the Heart was rumored to have been found by an unknown entity and sealed within a Dwemer lockbox buried deep in the ice in the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]].{{ref|[[SR:Septimus Signus|Septimus Signus]]' dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} The renowned scholar [[Lore:Septimus Signus|Septimus Signus]] uncovered the lockbox and spent many years attempting to open it.{{ref|[[SR:Urag gro-Shub|Urag gro-Shub]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} He succeeded in 4E 201 with the help of the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]], but was turned to ash upon discovering his folly: the lockbox did not contain the Heart of Lorkhan, but rather the [[Lore:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]], an artifact of [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], who had manipulated Signus for all those years.{{ref|[[SR:Discerning the Transmundane|Discerning the Transmundane]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

The replica Mechanical Heart was completed circa 4E 201 in the depths of the ruined [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]].{{ref|name=RTCC|[[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]] story in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} For more information, see the '''[[Lore:Mechanical Heart|Mechanical Heart article]]'''.

==Notes==

** A [[Skyrim:Per Your Requests|note from the College of Winterhold]] to [[Skyrim:Wylandriah|Wylandriah]], the Court Wizard of [[Lore:Riften|Riften]], indicates she requested a sample of the Heart for study (the College had no such item available).
** The quest [[Skyrim:Arniel's Endeavor|Arniel's Endeavor]], involves a mage trying to recreate Dwemer experiments on the Heart with a specially prepared soul gem and [[Lore:Keening|Keening]].
** The quest [[Skyrim:Discerning the Transmundane|Discerning the Transmundane]] involves an eccentric old man trying to open a Dwemer lockbox which he believes holds "the heart of a god", undoubtedly referring to the Heart of Lorkhan.
