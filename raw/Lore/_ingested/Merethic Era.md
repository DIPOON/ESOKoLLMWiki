'''Note:''' The [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], also known as the '''Merethic Age''',{{ref|name=OBLecture|[[Oblivion:University Lectures|University lectures]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} '''Mythic Age''',{{Ref|name=OBMankar|[[OB:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} '''Mythic Era''',{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} '''Mythic Times''',{{ref|name=OBGenDialogue|[[Oblivion:Generic Dialogue#Upper Class|Generic dialogue]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the '''Era of Myths''',{{Ref|name=ONMalana|[[Online:Malana|Malana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the '''days of myth'''{{Ref|name=TGTL|{{Cite Book|Traveler's Guide to Leyawiin}}}} is a period with few known, exact dates, and some of the events below may be misplaced in the general narrative. The era is dated backwards from Year Zero of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] (the traditional [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic Merethic dating), which is marked by the founding of the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]].{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] had a method of Merethic dating, abbreviated P.D., but how it relates to the Nordic method is unknown.{{ref|name=CoN|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of Nchuleft}}}}{{ref|name=AoDL|{{Cite Book|Antecedents of Dwemer Law}}}} The [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] recognized a Merethic dating system in the Old Calendar.{{ref|name=AotD|{{Cite Book|Annals of the Dragonguard}}}} [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] also had an old way of reckoning which included the Merethic Era.{{ref|name=DtS|{{Cite Book|Divad the Singer}}}}{{ref|name=RHaH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}}

==Late Middle Merethic Era==
{{History Entry
|The Dwemer expand||The Dwemer, a free-thinking, reclusive Elven clan devoted to the secrets of science, engineering, and alchemy, establish underground cities and communities in the mountain range separating modern [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and modern-day [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] (later known as the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]]).{{ref|name=BTAOM}}}}{{History Entry
|The Transformation of Trinimac||According to [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]]i tradition, a great battle occurs between [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] and [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] as a result of the [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]]i dissident movement. Trinimac is consumed, and returns later as [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]]. His followers become the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s. The followers of Veloth and Boethiah become the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], who migrate to their promised land of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]].{{ref|name=FOTN}}{{ref|name=TCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}} Some traditions trace these events back to the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]].{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}}{{ref|name=TTNOO|{{Cite Book|The True Nature of Orcs}}}}}}{{History Entry
|High [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]]i Culture||High Velothi Culture thrives in [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]]. The [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] become dynamic, ambitious, long-lived Elven clans devoted to fundamentalist ancestor worship. Despising the secular culture and profane practices of the Dwemer, the Chimer also covet their lands and resources, and for centuries provoke them with minor raids and territorial disputes.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}}}

*{{LNote|nb1|^|In ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|TES V: Skyrim]]'', [[Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]] says the Companions are "nearly 5000 years old", which would indicate that the Return occurred sometime in the last six centuries of the Merethic Era.
