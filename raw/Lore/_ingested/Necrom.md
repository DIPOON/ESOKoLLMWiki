[[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]] is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} and is perched on the [[Lore:Padomaic Ocean|Padomaic Ocean]], right on the eastern edge of the [[Lore:Padomaic Crest|Padomaic Crest]]. The city is the center of [[Lore:Ancestor Worship|ancestor worship]] in Morrowind and has thus earned the moniker, the '''City of the Dead'''.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Morrowind}}}} But despite the nature of this name, Necrom can be a teeming and bustling metropolis for commerce, pilgrims, and the mournful all day and night.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=AFATD1|{{Cite Book|A Feast Among the Dead, Chapter I}}}} While modern [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] left their ancient customs behind, Necrom helps them re-connect with that lost part of their history and their ancestry.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD|{{Cite Book|History of Necrom: The City of the Dead}}}}

Throughout its history Necrom has been traditionally ruled by the [[Lore:Keepers of the Dead|Keepers of the Dead]], a monastic order that serve as caretakers of the city and its necropolis.{{Ref|name=OOTK}}{{Ref|name=LAHT}} The Keepers answer to the authority of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Temple]].{{Ref|name=ONGadayn}} The eastern coast is presently ruled over by [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]],{{Ref|name=GHOM}} which as of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], is considered one and the same with the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]].{{Ref|name=TR}}<noinclude>{{TOCleft|limit=3}}

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-misc-Great Arm.png|thumb|right|GULGA MOR JIL's "Great Arm" and Necrom to the right]]
[[File:ON-place-Necrom 03.jpg|thumb|left|Necrom's northwest square]]
Necrom is a dense cityscape built on a steep, mountainous island, surrounded by perilous cliffs,{{Ref|name=ONNecrom|[[Online:Necrom|Necrom]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} and at the mouth of the river that divides the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|eastern peninsula]]'s southern region. Despite its inaccessibility from the outside, its people have taken advantage of the terrain to accommodate pilgrims coming in and out of the city. In its heyday, wooden ramps were built over the great ravine for ease of access and as an application of Boethiah's values. One can see echoes of those values in the marble causeways that have since been built in place of those ramps. {{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} Necrom's architectural style is noted for its lofty walls and white towers. {{Ref|name=PGE1}}

The Necrom outskirts comprise of steep hills and winding roads strewn up and across the small river valley around it. Travelers on foot can enter the city either through the outer gate, a fortified outpost just across the valley that connects via a large marble bridge, or the underpasses at the bottom that scale up toward the city walls. Off this beaten path is the [[Lore:Silt Strider|Silt Strider]] tower.{{Ref|name=ONNecrom}} Just off the coast, north of Necrom is the island of [[Lore:Gorne|Gorne]],{{Ref|name=LOMap|[[:File:LO-map-Morrowind (Anthology).jpg|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls Anthology'']]}}{{Ref|name=ONGorne|[[Online:Gorne|Gorne]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} which is historically owned by [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]].{{Ref|name=ONGaralo|[[Online:Garalo Givyn|Garalo Givyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{Ref|name=PSI|{{Cite Book|Poison Song I}}}} Necrom is neighbored by several settlements, one of which includes [[Lore:Sailen Vulgate|Sailen Vulgate]] to the west.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

Necrom's main entrance is the southern inner gate and it is where all these paths come together. The city's layout is like an intertwined web that meets at several points. From the main entrance, travelers are greeted by the large, open-aired trading plaza that is anchored from the other side by the local wayshrine. The long street that intersects there branches the northwestern square with the large, eastern interior district. Perhaps the city's most striking feature is the large rib-like rock formations that encapsulate it from above. As it is perpetuated in [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal]] mythology, these are the bones of [[Lore:GULGA MOR JIL|GULGA MOR JIL]], the strongest monster child of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] and [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], and the foundation of modern-day Necrom.{{Ref|name=36LOVS34|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 34}}}}{{Ref|name=LRPMM|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/Morrowind Moonlight|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 10/Morrowind Moonlight – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} Over the years, locals have carved [[Lore:Rune-Language|runic symbols]] on the rocks,{{Ref|name=ESOReveal|{{Cite Book|ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ns_base=GEN}}}} which have gone brittle and fell into the city on several occasions.{{Ref|name=POSV|{{Cite Book|Parables of Saint Vorys}}}} The city has a palace,{{Ref|name=AR}}{{Ref|name=PVA|[[Online:Palace View Apartments|Palace View Apartments]] name and location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} composed of many dark, twisted hallways.{{Ref|name=LRPMM|group=UOL}}

Parts of the city are built into this rib-like formation. The eastern and western sections of the city are constructed underneath the stone; like a large, encased hallway. The western area, aptly named the Guild District,{{Ref|name=ONSeler|[[Online:Seler Ralvas|Seler Ralvas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} is the smallest. It is one, thin thoroughfare that connects the trading plaza with the northwestern square near the Necropolis. The eastern area is a much larger place and takes a significant portion of the city's flank. This area is also split between the main street on the ground floor and the upper level where the guards monitor the premises. The streets are wide and filled with merchant stalls and shops. At many points, it splinters off west to the center of the city or the docks to the north. The northwest square splinters east to a large street contain the Necropolis and paths to the docks and eastern district, and north to separate back area with a graveyard and an entrance to the Underways.{{Ref|name=ONNecrom}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:ON-misc-Necrom Vivec Statue.png|thumb|right|The statue depicting Vivec's victory over GULGA MOR JIL]]
[[File:LO-misc-Velothi.png|thumb|left|Veloth leading the Chimer to Morrowind]]
Necrom's ancient history begins way back in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], before [[Lore:Man|man]] migrated to Tamriel.{{Ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} While the city itself was founded by the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], no one knows who truly built the settlement.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} These facts would place the settlement's discovering sometime in the late Merethic Era when the Chimer began to build permanent settlements, sometimes around old Velothi towers.{{Ref|name=BTAOM}}

The [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] historian, [[Lore:Florin Jaliil|Florin Jaliil]] theorized that [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]], on his journey charting Tamriel's coast passed Necrom's cliff coastline and the neighboring Gorne, where he encountered "'[[Lore:Bat Lizard|bat-lizards]]", a kind of creature that no longer exists in the region.{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] explorers like Topal have mapped nearby [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]'s coast and built wizard towers since the middle Merethic Era.{{Ref|name=BTAOM}} The access to fresh water and its defensible spot made the site perfect for a settlement. They built shrines to the Good Daedra and put their lessons into practice, like when they built great wooden ramps to cross the chasms; taking in [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]]'s teachings.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} The modern-day catacombs were immediately built into the bedrock and the settlement was created atop that.{{Ref|name=ONLoadNecropolis|[[Online:Necrom Necropolis|Necrom Necropolis]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} 

[[Lore:The Impresario|The Impresario]] claims to have witnessed the celebration of [[Lore:Bone Anew|Born Anew]] during Necrom's early days, which she described as a mass-summoning of the spirits of every person that had arrived to the region, saying that there were "Glimmering spirits as far as the eye could see, processing in a grand parade that filled the streets."{{Ref|name=LATH|[[Lore:Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays|Loremaster's Archive—Tamriel's Holidays]]}} Though Necrom's origins are rooted in ancient Chimer history{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} and even predate the Tribunal Temple by years,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} the [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|thirty-six lessons]] of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], the Warrior-Poet and Lord of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] provides the people the legend of how the city came to be. Before Necrom became the large metropolis it is today, it was a village, near where [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] found Vivec.{{Ref|name=36LOVS34}}

After the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] were expelled from Morrowind, Vivec left to train his giant form in the [[Lore:Molag Amur|Molag Amur]] badlands. But after a fateful encounter with Molag Bal, the two created a race of monster children, the strongest of which was known as GULGA MOR JIL.{{Ref|name=36LOVS12|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 12}}}}{{Ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} With his new spear, [[Lore:Muatra|Muatra]], Vivec began to hunt and kill each of his monster children across ancient Morrowind.{{Ref|name=36LOVS14|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 14}}}} The God-Poet managed to kill seven of the eight most powerful monsters until GULGA MOR JIL was the only one left. Together with [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]], the two looked into the middle world and found the beast at the village.{{Ref|name=36LOVS34}}

GULGA MOR JIL did not act like a monster but instead, sat on the edge, with his legs to the ocean and a solemn look on his face. When he saw Vivec, he asked him why he had to die and return to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]. The Warrior-Poet said that to do otherwise was to betray his nature, but when this did not satisfy him, Vivec recited a poem, ''"The fire is mine: let it consume thee, And make a secret door, At the altar of Padhome, In the House of Boet-hi-Ah, Where we become safe, And looked after."'' GULGA MOR JIL then accepted his fate, and with peace of mind his bones became Necrom's foundation.{{Ref|name=36LOVS34}}

===Necrom in the First and Second Eras===
[[File:ON-misc-Necrom Vorys Shrine.png|thumb|right|Saint Vorys the Immolant]]
[[File:ON-interior-Necrom Necropolis.jpg|thumb|left|Prior Durdryn's tomb in the Necropolis]]
Many necropolises dot Morrowind's landscape but Necrom's has proved to be one of the oldest, perhaps the oldest.{{Ref|name=ONGadayn|[[Online:Curate Gadayn|Curate Gadayn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} The Necropolis was founded by a mer named Prior [[Lore:Durdryn|Durdryn]], a devotee to the Tribunal Temple that was obsessed with Necrom's history and possessed a vast array of knowledge on everything around him. His faith in the Tribunal gave him the means to study the daedra and [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] like no other mortal and in his quest for knowledge, he earned the acquaintance of the Tribunal themselves, especially Vivec. The Warrior-Poet awarded them with a tusk of GULGA MOR JIL and gave him the mission to turn it into an artifact of great power.{{Ref|name=TPF|{{Cite Book|The Prior's Fulcrum}}}}

Durdryn prayed to the Daedric Prince of Knowledge and Fate, [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], who granted him the runes needed to uncover secrets. He carved these runes into the tusk to create a powerful relic, the [[Lore:Fulcrum Obscura|Fulcrum Obscura]].{{Ref|name=OTFO|{{Cite Book|On the Fulcrum Obscura}}}} Though no known records exist to corroborate this deal with the Prince, the artifact was presented to Vivec and later returned to Prior Durdryn. Many rumors started to circulate about the Prior and his uses with the Fulcrum. He used it to settle matters with the dead such as solving murders and finding lost treasures. But it was also rumored he used the artifact to pilfer secrets from the dead. As the Prior became more and more obsessed with the artifact, he spent the last years of his life secluded in his chambers. Since no other keeper wanted to take up the Fulcrum Obscura after his death, it was buried with him in his tomb in the Necropolis.{{Ref|name=TPF}}

In some point in time, Durdryn also founded the Keepers of the Dead as the order to facilitate the Necropolis and set up the boundaries and rules to perform their sacred duties.{{Ref|name=OJTKOTD|{{Cite Book|On Joining the Keepers of the Dead}}}} By the time the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]] had passed, GULGA MOR JIL's bones dried up and became brittle, making it prone to collapse and killing passersby on streets below. At the time, Saint [[Lore:Vorys|Vorys]] oversaw the Necropolis' expansion. Taking these sudden deaths into account, Vorys left Necrom and traveled southwest into [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], where he hoped to die honorable in war.{{Ref|name=POSV}}

===The Fulcrum Obscura and Daedric Conspiracy===
[[File:ON-quest-Keeper of the Fate.jpg|thumb|right|The Necropolis closed by the Abbot]]
[[File:ON-npc-Curate Gadayn.jpg|thumb|left|Curate Gadayn of the Dead Keepers]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, a Prelate from the Tribunal Temple named Faram traveled to Necrom from [[Lore:Firewatch|Firewatch]] and brought with him aides. But after they arrived, Keepers of the Dead began to develop a fever and eventually, Abbot Ilvel closed the Necropolis from the public. Suspicious of the current events, Curate Gadayn and an adventurer chosen by Hermaeus Mora known as [[Lore:The Vestige|Fate's Chosen]] began to investigate. What they found was that these "Temple" priests were members of a daedric cult of [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] known as the [[Lore:Hidden Kindred|Hidden Kindred]] and their leader, Blightcrown posed as the Prelate.{{Ref|name=ONKeeper|[[Online:Keeper of the Fate|Keeper of the Fate]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

They had closed down the Necropolis and used Peryite's magic to torture the Dead Keepers in order to find the Fulcrum Obscura and pilfer it from Prior Durdryn's tomb. Blightcrown and his daedra associate, [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]] escaped with one-half of the artifact while Fate's Chosen recovered the other half. Abbot Ilvel entrusted the piece with Fate's Chosen and Curate Gadayn was recruited by them to help fight this threat.{{Ref|name=ONKeeper}} After Blightcrown and one of their patrons, [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]] were defeated, Curate Gadayn and Fate's Chosen were able to return the Fulcrum Obscura to the Necrom Necropolis and Abbot Ilvel announced the catacombs re-opening.{{Ref|name=ONChronicle|[[Online:Chronicle of Fate|Chronicle of Fath]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

===Necrom in the late Second Era and onward===
[[File:MW-place-Ghostfence.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Ghostfence]]
Sometime after the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] was signed in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Great Ghostfence]] that surrounded [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] was erected on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. It was built to keep out [[Lore:The Blight|the Blight]],{{Ref|name=AATD|{{Cite Book|Ancestors and the Dunmer}}}} an ancient disease released by [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] after centuries of slumber in {{Year|2E 882}}.{{Ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} The Great Ghostfence used a large quantity of bones from several sources. This ranged from heroes of House Indoril and [[Lore:House Redoran|Redoran]], remains from Morrowind's many battlefields, and bones from Necrom's catacombs. The fence had thus forcibly changed many practices in ancestor worship as any remains of the deceased thereafter were put toward the Ghostfence. This would mean fewer remains were interred into Necrom.{{Ref|name=AATD}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Necrom was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Hekothah and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{Ref|name=AR}} From the late Third Era to the early Fourth Era, there was a great religious change in Morrowind, for better or for worse.{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} The Blight was ultimately annihilated along with Dagoth Ur in {{Year|3E 427}}, when the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] defeated him in the depths of Red Mountain.{{Ref|name=MWSixth|[[Morrowind:The Citadels of the Sixth House|The Citadels of the Sixth House]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Ghostfence was then deemed unnecessary by the province at large and its shield was deactivated.{{Ref|name=MWGen|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue G#Ghostfence|Ghostfence]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} This would have affected Necrom and the original tradition regarding ancestral remains, but to what effect is unknown.

Conversely, upon the [[Lore:Red Year|destruction]] in {{Year|4E 5}}, the Tribunal Temple and any support toward the Almsivi crumbled. The [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] swiftly took action and replaced the "false" Tribunal with the Reclamations, the original Good Daedra. The [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]] established themselves at the top of the Temple's hierarchy and denounced the "false" Tribunal for their misguided teachings,{{Ref|name=TR}} among which would be Vivec's thirty-six lessons. How this affected Necrom and its strong ties to the thirty-fourth sermon is currently unknown.

===Known Rulers===
[[File:ON-npc-Abbot Ilvel.jpg|thumb|right|Abbot Ilvel of the Necrom Necropolis]]
The [[Lore:Keepers of the Dead|Keepers of the Dead]] administrate the city and the necropolis,{{Ref|name=LAHT}}{{Ref|name=ABHOHT|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of House Telvanni}}}} and they themselves are headed by an Abbot. As a religious administration, the Keepers also answered to the Tribunal Temple which was based in Mournhold.{{Ref|name=ONGadayn}}

Necrom is a city that has changed hands between the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] of Morrowind over centuries. In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the vast majority of the peninsula, from the southern borders to the northern tip fell under [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]].{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} Using Necrom's status as an active metropolis gave the Telvanni the means to keep contact with the other houses.{{Ref|name=ABHOHT}} For a time, however, Necrom saw nominal independence after the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was formed in {{Year|2E 572}}, when the Telvanni chose not to join with the rest of their kin. It was agreed upon between the Pact and the House's barristers that Necrom's cultural significance made it an exception and allowed Pact-affiliated Dunmer to visit.{{Ref|name=LAHT}} 

At some point in time, Necrom and the southeast Morrowind coast fell under [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] administration{{Ref|name=MWConceptMap|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-map-Morrowind Concept.jpg|Concept Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind'']]}} and by the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], House Indoril held the Morrowind heartland, from the southern [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] coast to the general eastern mainland coast.{{Ref|name=GHOM|{{Cite Book|Great Houses of Morrowind}}}} How much of that territory extended toward Necrom is unknown, but their northernmost known territories were [[Lore:Draloris|Draloris]] northwest of Necrom{{Ref|name=MWConceptMap|group=UOL}} and the island of Gorne just across the city.{{Ref|name=ONGaralo}}{{Ref|name=PSI}} With Indoril's absorption of the Temple in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], and by extension the Dead Keepers, the rulership of Necrom has now passed into their hands.{{Ref|name=TR}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-concept-Keeper of the Dead.jpg|thumb|left|The Keepers of the Dead]]
{{Quote Box|''Throughout Necrom's dark halls, the priests' footsteps are heard<br>They welcome visitors to their ancestors' home<br>To wander catacombs, don't fear—you're not alone<br>Your ancestors and priests will remind you ….''<br><br>
''Life's end sets your spirit free with nary an exception<br>So honor your forebears with every breath<br>You should ask no boons unless ill-pressed, lest Mortal Chill should come<br>And seek Necrom to commune with death''|''[[Lore:The Waiting Door (Necrom)|The Waiting Door]]''}}
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Dunmer page.''
Necrom's status as the center of ancestor worship comes from this ancient, inherent connection with the afterlife that radiates from the area. Chimeri writings support this notion. They speak of how easy it was to reach spirits from beyond the grave and how they viewed Necrom as a waystation between the living and the dead.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} Along with the City of the Dead, Necrom has other epithets, such as the '''City of Tombs'''.{{Ref|name=ONBoryn|[[Online:High Ordinator Boryn|High Ordinator Boryn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

Before the Great Ghostfence in Vvardenfell was built, the Dunmer people carried their ancestor's remains to Necrom, where they would be properly interred. From there, the ancestor spirit draws comfort from others and moves on into the afterlife. However, if they were acknowledged for their great honor and sacrifice, they could have some of their remains placed in their ancestral tomb to provide energy to their clan's personal ghost fence.{{Ref|name=AATD|{{Cite Book|Ancestors and the Dunmer}}}}

[[Lore:Death/Dunmer|Death]] is a large recurring theme in the city that gives its people a unique outlook on life. Sometimes Necrom holds large, grandiose festivals in honor of the dead and people come in such large masses that it sometimes clogs roads from Necrom to [[Lore:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]] out west. Mourners crowd the streets, great plumes of incense envelop the air, and the dead are aligned across the street in great numbers.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}} [[Lore:Buoyant Armigers|Buoyant Armigers]] that are stationed in Necrom often meditated and reflected on what they hoped to achieve. Once they achieved those goals, a Dead Keeper body artist imprints Necrom symbology all over their body.{{Ref|name=ONArmigerBody|[[Online:Necrom Armiger Body Markings|Necrom Armiger]] body markings description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though it is widely known as the City of the Dead,{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=AR}} there are multiple cities of the dead in Morrowind and these necropolises are used to inter the remains of deceased Dunmer. One of these sites include [[Lore:Othrenis|Othrenis]] in eastern Stonefalls.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}}{{Ref|name=ESOUnused|group=UOL|{{TIL|ESO Unused Strings|game-data}}}} However, Necrom being the center of ancestor worship in Morrowind and with its unique connection with the dead, it is seen as the main destination for the Dunmer and their ancestors.{{Ref|name=HONTCOTD}}

The [[Lore:Keepers of the Dead|Keepers of the Dead]] are the religious caretakers of the necropolis and the city at large.{{Ref|name=OOTK|{{Cite Book|Oath of the Keepers}}}}{{Ref|name=LAHT}} They are described as a complex hierarchy of priests and attendants{{Ref|name=PGE1}} who use a myriad of mandates, rituals, and statutes to conduct nonstop business.{{Ref|name=OOTK}} Members of the Dead Keepers have described the job as difficult and many aspiring attendants leave after months of hard work.{{Ref|name=OJTKOTD}} There is an accord called the Necrom Agreement. The thirty-sixth statute involves the relationship between House Telvanni and the Dead Keepers.{{Ref|name=ONMeln|[[Online:Meln the Mouthless|Meln the Mouthless]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

Necrom contains many artifacts of interest. Breathtaker is an ancient [[Lore:Malachite|glass]] claymore from Necrom that can siphon the opponent's spirit and vigor for a killing strike.{{Ref|name=BLBreathtaker|[[Blades:Breathtaker|Breathtaker]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} The [[Lore:Indigo Tomes|Indigo Tomes]] are a collection of scrolls kept in Necrom's vaults. Before she was the Queen of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] disguised herself as a Dunmer to read them, according to propaganda written by Headmaster Tanion, from the [[Lore:College of Aldmeri Propriety|College of Aldmeri Propriety]].{{Ref|name=ATUQ}} The tomes' reference in the book caused some amount of stress with the Keepers of the Dead.{{Ref|name=LAHT}}

People will use the phrase, "from [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] to Necrom" to emphasize the size of something since these are the furthest anchored cities on Tamriel.{{Ref|name=OBTsrava|[[Oblivion:Tsrava|Tsrava]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=SJ|{{Cite Book|Sondivel's Journal}}}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadVaermina|[[Online:Shrine of Vaermina|Shrine of Vaermina]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} There are many [[Lore:Dunmer Cuisine|dishes]] originally from the city, such as Beetle-Cheese Poutine,{{Ref|name=ONPoutine|[[Online:Necrom Beetle-Cheese Poutine|Necrom Beetle-Cheese Poutine]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Rat|Rat]] Necrom with [[Lore:Bonemeal|Bonemeal]] Gravy.{{Ref|name=OBWeebam|[[Oblivion:Weebam-Na|Weebam-Na]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} There is also a wide variety of beverages such as [[Lore:Flin|Flin Fizz]],{{Ref|name=BFTB|{{Cite Book|Beverages for the Bereaved}}}} [[Lore:Mazte|Nights Mazte]],{{Ref|name=ONMazte|[[Online:Necrom Nights Mazte|Necrom Nights Mazte]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Sujamma|Spiced Sujamma Posset]], and Swamp Muck.{{Ref|name=BFTB}} Necrom became a primary stop in the [[Lore:East Empire Trading Company|East Empire Trading Company]]'s shipping lines and connected to other cities like [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]], [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] and [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]].{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}}
