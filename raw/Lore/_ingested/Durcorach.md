[[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]], known as the "'''Black Drake'''", was a [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachman]] warlord and later Emperor of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. After gaining control of [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]], he and his army conquered the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] in {{Year|2E 533}}, where he was crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil, becoming the first emperor of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]] dynasty.{{ref|name=SHotLE|{{Cite Book|Secret History of the Longhouse Emperors}}}} In {{Year|2E 541}} or {{Year|2E 542}}, he led an invasion of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and captured and sacked many of the province's cities, but he was killed by [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of Cumberland while attempting to besiege [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=TOAM3|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch, Ch. 3}}}} He was succeeded by his son, [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Early Life and Rise to Power===
[[File:ON-trailer-Deadlands and Damnation Tagh Droiloch.jpg|thumb|left|Tagh Droiloch]]
According to a hagiography commissioned by Durcorach, on the night of his birth around {{Year|2E 485}},{{ref|name=SHotLE}} a great comet blazed across the sky, and "seven sevens of silver swans" flew over his mother's shelter in the snowy mountains of the Reach. All who beheld the infant were said to be "struck by the strong beauty of his young face, with eyes so dark and fierce that great warriors could not bear to meet his gaze". He was raised in the challenging wilderness of the Reach, which taught him "the value of courage, honor, endurance, and wisdom". The hagiography continues to describe his early life: at the age of three, Durcorach allegedly saved his mother's life by seizing his father's hunting spear and slaying a [[Lore:Bear#Black Bear|black bear]] that had already devoured twenty men. At the age of five, he saved his village from starving in a harsh winter by hunting a herd of a hundred elk from [[Lore:Karthwasten|Karthwasten]] to the high peaks of the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]]. On his seventh birthday, a tribe of [[Lore:Giant|giants]] invaded the peaceful pastures where his people kept their flocks, and he somehow managed to beat them back.{{Ref|name=TCD|{{Cite Book|The Crowned Dragon}}}}

Durcorach was known for his imposing stature, savage temper, and prowess with both blades and [[Lore:Magic|magic]].{{ref|name=Maz|[[Online:Mazrahil the Scarab|Mazrahil the Sly Scarab]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=HCPTTD|{{Cite Book|High Chancellor's Papers: The Tagh Droiloch}}}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} By the time he was in his late twenties, he had become the chieftain of his clan.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} In {{Year|2E 518}}, his son, Moricar, was born.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Durcorach soon became an exceptionally strong and clever chieftain, and in {{Year|2E 527}}, he began building a following in the Reach.{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}} His growing power caught the attention of the [[Lore:Tagh Droiloch|Tagh Droiloch]], a coven of witch-men, and he allied with them.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}}{{ref|name=TBDFW|[[ON:The Black Drake's Face Warpaint|The Black Drake's Face Warpaint]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With their help, he grew and solidified his power and subdued all rival clans in the Reach through means involving Daedric bargains.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}} By {{Year|2E 528}}, he was a warlord in command of over two thousand soldiers, which rose to ten thousand by {{Year|2E 529}}.{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}

===Conquest of Cyrodiil and Emperor of Cyrodiil===
[[File:ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg|[[Lore:Statues|Bas-relief]] depicting Durcorach's conquest of Cyrodiil|left|thumb]]
In 2E 529, Durcorach and his army, having subdued the rival [[Lore:Reachman Clans|Reachfolk clans]] and taken control of the Reach, began to march south toward Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}} On the march south, Durcorach claimed victories and territory beyond the Reach.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Around this time, Durcorach, with the assistance of secret Daedra worshippers and noble supporters within the Empire, struck a deal with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] to have Durcorach crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil and secure his rule for generations.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=ADiS|{{Cite Book|A Deal is Struck}}}}{{Ref|name=SoMI|{{Cite Book|Secrets of Moricar the Inheritor}}}}{{Ref|name=XBoC|{{Cite Book|Xynaa's Book of Contracts}}}} The terms of the deal were that Mehrunes Dagon would assist Durcorach in being crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil; in exchange, the Black Drake would oversee the rituals and preparations needed to create the Daedric weapons known as the [[Lore:Four Ambitions|Four Ambitions]], which were to be created under the specific [[Lore:Constellations|signs]] and circumstances described in the [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|Mysterium Xarxes]].{{ref|name=ADiS}}{{Ref|name=XBoC}} The bargain was to last fifty years, and upon its completion, Mehrunes Dagon would claim his due.{{ref|name=ADiS}}{{Ref|name=SoMI}}

In {{Year|2E 533}}, Durcorach and his horde of Reachmen conquered Cyrodiil, and he was crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=HMSS|{{Cite Book|History of Markarth: A Story in Stone}}}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}} The Tagh Droiloch disappeared soon after the conquest, but there were suspicions that Durcorach's sorcerers, [[Lore:Baarselg|Baarselg]], [[Lore:Faoshor|Faoshor]], and [[Lore:Wunagh|Old Wunagh]], were members of the coven.{{Ref|name=HCPTTD}} After the conquest, the Emperor's kinsmen and allies in the Reach founded the [[Lore:Blackdrake Clan|Blackdrake Clan]].{{ref|name=OTCOTR|{{Cite Book|On the Clans of the Reach}}}}

On 1 Sun's Dawn, {{Year|2E 534}}, Durcorach reconstituted the membership of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=IDREC|{{Cite Book|Imperial Decree Regarding the Elder Council}}}} The Emperor reconfirmed Grand Chancellor [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]], Councilor [[Lore:Itinia|Itinia]], and Councilor Velan Sophus; appointed [[Lore:Tarnian Lovidicus|Tarnian Lovidicus]], [[Lore:Ertus Vandacia|Ertus Vandacia]], Pheomas Lucasta, and [[Lore:Kelgan|Kelgan]] the Taker of Teeth; and dismissed Councilor Orlandus Plavi, Councilor Hestraia Silan, Councilor Martiax Granciola, and Councilor Sergan Euraphus. Lovidicus was also named chief councilor upon his appointment to the Elder Council.{{ref|name=IDREC}}

In 2E 534, Durcorach married [[Lore:Veraxia Tharn|Veraxia Tharn]], of the prominent [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] [[Lore:House Tharn|Tharn]] family, to reinforce his commitment to the Empire and to legitimize his claim to the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=COTFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}} He attempted to embrace [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] culture during his reign and to merge Reach sensibilities with Imperial culture in an effort to win more support in Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Durcorach demanded to be taught the proper procedures and protocols of the Imperial Court, and he tried to hold his court in a way similar to that of prior [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|Cyrodilic emperors]], as he wanted to carry himself like an Imperial.{{ref|name=AwtLE|{{Cite Book|Audiences with the Longhouse Emperors}}}} Although he tried hard to carry himself like an Imperial, it was said to have brought him unease and frustration, and he was never able to truly understand Imperial culture.{{ref|name=AwtLE}} He appointed Imperial tutors to educate Moricar, which enabled him to understand Imperial culture in a way Durcorach never could.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=AwtLE}}

At some point during his reign, Durcorach ordered the construction of an estate called Black Drake Villa on the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]].{{Ref|name=BDVBD|[[General:Flames of Ambition Preview—Black Drake Villa|''Flames of Ambition Preview—Black Drake Villa'']]}}{{ref|name=ESAd|[[Online:Eveli Sharp-Arrow#Flames of Ambition|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Flames of Ambition|Flames of Ambition]]''}} He stored many of the items he acquired during his conquests at Black Drake Villa.{{ref|name=ESAd}}

===Invasion of High Rock===
Durcorach did not wish to rest on his past victories and began planning another campaign to capture more territory.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} In {{Year|2E 541}}{{ref|name=TOAM3}} or {{Year|2E 542}},{{ref|name=IEGTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} the Emperor led a horde of Reachmen through [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] into the mountains east of the region of [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]], bypassing the [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Garrison]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=GttDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=BSHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} After traversing through the mountains, they descended from the mountains along the south shore of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]].{{ref|name=GttDC}}{{ref|name=BSHR}}

The Reachmen laid siege to [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], which fell after only three days of besiegement; they then sacked the city.{{ref|name=TRHKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}}{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=IEGTB}} A breed of wolves known as Black Drake Howlers were among the first creatures to storm Evermore when the Reachmen's attack broke through the city's walls.{{ref|name=BDH|[[ON:Black Drake Howler|Black Drake Howler]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} To the north of Evermore, Durcorach's forces attacked and burned the village of [[Lore:Murcien's Hamlet|Murcien's Hamlet]].{{ref|name=VR2E5412E542|{{Cite Book|Vital Records, 2E 541—2E 542|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=RFd|[[Online:Rolbert Foucher|Rolbert Foucher]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After sacking Evermore, the Reachman marched south and began assaults on the walls of the Bangkorai Garrison.{{ref|name=BSHR}}{{ref|name=GHAH1|{{Cite Book|The Gray Host: A History Part 1}}}} Durcorach sent wave after wave of Reach warriors against the walls of the garrison.{{ref|name=GHAH1}} However, after five months of daily slaughter at the gates, the Reachmen and the [[Lore:Order of Saint Pelin|Knights of Saint Pelin]] were at a stalemate, so he and his forces decided to retreat from the Bangkorai Garrison.{{ref|name=GHAH1}}{{ref|name=BSHR}} Undeterred by the failure to capture the garrison, Durcorach and his forces bypassed the Bangkorai Garrison, marched south, and besieged [[Lore:Hallin's Stand|Hallin's Stand]].{{ref|name=TOAM3}} Although Hallin's Stand held out longer than Evermore, eventually the Reachmen breached the walls and sacked the city.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=Maz}}

Durcorach and his forces crossed the Bjoulsae within days of sacking Hallin's Stand, quickly overran much of the [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]] region, and began besieging the well-fortified settlements of [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] and the [[Lore:Alcaire (city)|Alcaire Castle]].{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=SoSB|{{Cite Book|Statue of Sir Byric}}}} The Siege of Wayrest lasted for fifty-seven days, during which the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] of Stormhaven manned the walls and repulsed the assaults by the Reachmen.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}} The Black Drake's forces were unable to breach the city walls due to a lack of siege engines, and they could not reduce the city to starvation due to a lack of ships to blockade the harbor.{{ref|name=TOAM3}} Eventually, Durcorach grew impatient with the siege, so he left enough troops in the revetments around Wayrest's walls to keep the defenders confined inside the city.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} He then marched west to the region of [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]].{{ref|name=TOAM3}}

In Glenumbra, the Reachfolk horde took [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]] by surprise, and after capturing the city, they sacked it.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=GttDC}} Following the sacking of Camlorn, the Emperor and his forces set their sights on [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], located to the south.{{ref|name=TOAM3}} When his forces arrived at Daggerfall, they massed before the city gates and then began investing the city.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR}} While his forces were investing Daggerfall, [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of Cumberland, in command of the [[Lore:Menevia|Menevia]] Heavy Dragoons, executed an attack on the rear of Durcorach's ranks, taking them by surprise.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR}} In the ensuing battle, Emeric sought out and struck down Durcorach himself.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR}} After Durcorach's death, the [[Lore:Knights of the Dragon|Knights of the Dragon]], led by King [[Lore:Bergamot (person)|Bergamot]], launched a sortie against the Reachmen, helping Emeric and the Menevia Heavy Dragoons completely rout them.{{ref|name=TOAM3}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR}}

===Aftermath and Afterlife in the Deadlands===
[[File:ON-npc-Emperor Durcorach.jpg|Durcorach's soul in the Deadlands|left|thumb]]
Moricar, already a vital advisor to the throne, declared himself emperor as soon as news of his father's death reached him in the Imperial City.{{ref|name=SHotLE}} In one of his first acts as emperor, Moricar negotiated peace with [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], as he considered the invasion his father's quagmire and did not want it to become his own.{{ref|name=AwtLE}} He also recognized the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], an alliance of High Rock's kingdoms formed in response to Durcorach's invasion.{{ref|name=AwtLE}}{{ref|name=GttDC}}{{ref|name=IEGTHR}}

Upon his death, Durcorach's [[Lore:Soul|soul]] was sent to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]].{{ref|name=TLA|Events of [[ON:The Last Ambition|The Last Ambition]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} Moricar inherited responsibility for the Four Ambitions from Durcorach, which Moricar passed on to his son [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] upon his death in {{Year|2E 564}}.{{Ref|name=SoMI}}{{Ref|name=XBoC}}{{Ref|name=ESOMd|[[ON:Emperor Moricar|Emperor Moricar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} Leovic was killed in the [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]] in {{Year|2E 577}}, and he failed to pass responsibility for the Four Ambitions to an heir, leading to the declaration that the Longhouse Emperors had defaulted on their bargains with Mehrunes Dagon.{{Ref|name=XBoC}} Eventually, the souls of Durcorach, Moricar, and Leovic were imprisoned in the Brandfire Reformatory in the Deadlands, where Daedra tortured them by fusing the Longhouse Emperors' souls with a chunk of [[Lore:Iron|iron]] slag.{{ref|name=TLA}}{{Ref|name=ESOTAd|[[ON:The Anchorite|The Anchorite]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}

After Leovic's death, Black Drake Villa was abandoned.{{ref|name=ESAd}}{{Ref|name=ESOBDVls|[[Online:Black Drake Villa|Black Drake Villa]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Flames of Ambition|Flames of Ambition]]''}} Following the estate's abandonment, most of the items Durcorach acquired during his campaigns were looted and later appeared on the black market.{{Ref|name=ESOBDVls}} 

In {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] and [[Lore:Mairead|Mairead]], one of the Four Ambitions, released the Longhouse Emperors' souls from a chunk of iron slag in the Brandfire Reformatory Forge. The Vestige then questioned the spirits of the Longhouse Emperors about the Four Ambitions. Sombren and Calia, two of the Four Ambitions, along with [[Lore:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]] and [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]], interrupted when they entered the Reformatory Forge through a portal. Sombren then destroyed the Longhouse Emperors' spirits with frost magic, although he claimed their spirits would reform in the Deadlands eventually.{{ref|name=TLA}}{{Ref|name=ESOSom|[[ON:Sombren|Sombren]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}

==Notes==
* Durcorach had a warhound named Elf-Biter,{{ref|name=EBC|[[Online:Contraband E#Elf-Biter's Collar|Elf-Biter's Collar]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and a female Black Drake Howler, a specimen of the breed of wolves named after him.{{ref|name=BDH}}

* When Durorach allied with the Tagh Droiloch coven, the witch-men added asp oil to his soot warpaint. It was said that he fell into a battle frenzy whenever he applied the warpaint.{{ref|name=TBDFW}}
