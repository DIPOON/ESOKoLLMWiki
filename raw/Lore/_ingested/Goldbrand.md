The [[Lore:Goldbrand|Goldbrand]] is an ancient [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] created by the [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]] of the [[Lore:Skyrim|North]] which takes the form of a golden katana.{{ref|name=TL}} According to legends, they first bestowed the blade upon the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] warrior [[Lore:Sivdur|Sivdur]], (ancestor to the modern [[Lore:Battle-Born|Battle-Born]] clan){{ref|name=SREJ|{{Cite Book|Eranya's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=SRERN}} who was sworn to protect them.{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} It embodies the power of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]],{{ref|name=SREJ}} and burns those that it strikes.<noinclude>

==History==
[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended the sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing Goldbrand among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Goldbrand and the other artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}

In {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] discovered the sunken shrine to Boethiah beneath the waves, west of the small village of [[Lore:Hla Oad|Hla Oad]] in the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. The Daedra was upset that his shrine had been allowed to remain in ruins, and ordered the Nerevarine to erect a new shrine in return for the Goldbrand. The Nerevarine funded an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] sculptor to create the shrine at [[Lore:Khartag Point|Khartag Point]], a landmark northwest of [[Lore:Gnaar Mok|Gnaar Mok]]. When the statue was complete, Boethiah rewarded the Nerevarine with the Goldbrand.{{ref|name=MWB|[[MW:Boethiah's Quest|Boethiah's Quest]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The blade is said to have been able to be empowered under mysterious circumstances and dubbed '''Eltonbrand'''.{{ref|name=MWSK|[[MW:Shashev's Key|Shashev's Key]] possible reward in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} It is unclear if the Nerevarine pursued this transformation, or if it was sold to [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]] and put on display in the [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] Museum of Artifacts later that same year.{{ref|name=Tribunal|Events of [[Tribunal:The Museum|The Museum]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] visited the [[Lore:Realm of Boethia|Realm of Boethia]] and partook in the Tournament of Ten Bloods. After defeating all nine of Boethiah's Chosen in mortal combat, Boethiah declared the Hero as his Chosen One, gifting the Goldbrand as a boon.{{ref|name=OBB|[[OB:Boethia|Boethia]] quest in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 175}}, Goldbrand was wielded by a [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] named Reive, who was in the service of Thalmor general [[Lore:Naarifin|Lord Naarifin]]. Reive was slain by the [[Lore:Forgotten Hero|Forgotten Hero]], who may have wielded the Goldbrand at the Battle of the Red Ring while impersonating Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]]. This led to a pervasive rumor that Titus II wielded it at the battle, although this has never been officially confirmed by the Imperial government.{{ref|name=ESL|[[Legends:Story|Story]] of ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

Sometime after the Battle of the Red Ring, Goldbrand was returned to the tomb of Sivdur located somewhere in the mountains near [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], though only speculation remained as to who did so.{{ref|name=SREJ}} The blade was to be a reward to who could bring the head of Sivdur's betrayer, the [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priest]] Magrathi, who had slain Sivdur's brother over a property dispute.{{ref|name=SRERN|{{Cite Book|Eranya's Research Notes|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=ENoST|{{Cite Book|Eranya's Notes on Sivdur's Tomb|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, discovering the notes of a slain researcher at the Sacellum of Boethiah, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] took on the task of retrieving the blade. With Magrathi's [[Lore:Shroud Hearth Barrow|tomb]] defiled, and his severed head at hand, Sivdur's tomb allowed entry, and Goldbrand was claimed by the Last Dragonborn.{{ref|[[Skyrim:A Matter of Pride|A Matter of Pride]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
