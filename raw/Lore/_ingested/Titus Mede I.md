[[Lore:Titus Mede I|Titus Mede I]] was an [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|emperor]] of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] and the first emperor of the [[Lore:Mede Dynasty|Mede Dynasty]] during the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. Circa {{Year|4E 22}}, he captured the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and deposed the [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] witch-warrior named [[Lore:Thules the Gibbering|Thules the Gibbering]], ending the [[Lore:Stormcrown Interregnum|Stormcrown Interregnum]].{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|109}}{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|96}}{{ref|name=RTV4|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} After becoming emperor, he worked to stabilize and rebuild the Empire, although he was never able to restore imperial authority to all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|18, 109, 282}} His reign witnessed the formation of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#Third Aldmeri Dominion|Third Aldmeri Dominion]] following the overthrow of the imperial government in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] by the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] and their [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] collaborators.{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|154}} Titus I continued to reign during the [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|Umbriel Crisis]] in {{Year|4E 48}}, and he contributed to the defeat of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]].{{ref|name=TIC}}{{ref|name=LOS}}<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Background and Rise to Power===
There is limited information available about Titus Mede's life prior to his accession to the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]]. He was born somewhere in [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]].{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|247}} Titus had a lean face, strong chin, green eyes, and curly auburn (later half silver) hair.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|108}} He had a brother whom the [[Lore:Penitus Oculatus|Penitus Oculatus]] would later surveil to ensure that he made no attempt to usurp the throne.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|155}} He also possibly had another brother.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|121}}

Prior to {{Year|4E 22}}, Titus had been a soldier in an outlaw army, a warlord in Colovia, and a king in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|108}}{{ref|name=RTV4}} Circa 4E 22, Titus captured the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] with a force of less than a thousand men and deposed [[Lore:Thules the Gibbering|Thules the Gibbering]], the Nibenese witch-warrior who ruled what remained of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|109}}{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96}}{{ref|name=RTV4}} Following the capture of the Imperial City, Titus routed the army of [[Lore:Eddar Olin|Eddar Olin]] with a force of around two thousand men.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|109}}

===Emperor of Tamriel===
Titus I's accession marked the end of the [[Lore:Stormcrown Interregnum|Stormcrown Interregnum]].{{ref|name=RTV4}} Thules the Gibbering was not a well-liked ruler, but since he was Nibenese, many on the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] favored him over a Colovian usurper.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96}} [[Lore:Hierem|Hierem]], who was from an old and well-connected Nibenese family and a minister to Thules the Gibbering, helped smooth over the conquest by convincing the Elder Council to accept Titus I as a liberator rather than a conqueror.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96}} Hierem later became his prime minister.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|63}}

By the time Titus I became emperor, the Empire had virtually collapsed, as [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] and the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] had seceded from the Empire, and [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] was devastated by the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] and [[Lore:Accession War|Accession War]].{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|206, 261}} [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], as well as the Cyrodilic counties of [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] and [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], had become independent.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|143, 180, 223}} As a result, following his accession, Titus I worked to stabilize and reunite the Empire.{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|18, 109, 282}} He soon proved himself to be a shrewd and capable leader, and the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] endorsed him as emperor.{{ref|name=RTV4}} Ultimately, during his reign, the Empire regained control of the provinces of Hammerfell, [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], Skyrim, and [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], as well as Counties Bravil and Leyawiin.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|223}}

At some point during his reign, a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] named [[Lore:Takar|Takar]], who was originally from [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], fought against the Empire until Titus I won him over, supposedly through personal combat.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|250}} Takar later became one of Titus I's most trusted generals.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|250}} One of Titus I's most valued men was another Redguard, Tralan the Two-Blade, who hailed from [[Lore:Cespar|Cespar]].{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|98}}

With his wife, Titus I fathered a son, [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus]], in {{Year|4E 26}}.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|105}} At some point during his son's childhood, Hierem suggested to Titus I that the people needed a hero, and that Attrebus should be that hero.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|245, 272}} Titus I agreed to Hierem's plan, either to provide a sense of direction for Attrebus or because he believed it would placate Hierem.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|245, 272}} To make Attrebus a hero, the Imperial Court staged every battle or duel he fought to ensure his victory, and if he went on an unstaged adventure, one of his guards would discreetly report it to the Emperor or Hierem, who would end it.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|146-147, 159-160}}{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|66-67}} Attrebus was unaware that all of his adventures were staged.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|146-147, 159-160}}{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|66}} The court also paid bards and authors to sing and write about Attrebus.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|66}}

In {{Year|4E 29}}, the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] and their [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] collaborators staged a coup, overthrowing the imperial government in Valenwood and capturing the province's imperial strongholds in a brief campaign.{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|154}} After the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] and Bosmer defeated the Empire in Valenwood, they proclaimed the formation of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#Third Aldmeri Dominion|Third Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=RTV4}}{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|154}}

Titus I did not trust Hierem.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|246}} The Emperor had no choice but to keep Hierem in a position of power, since it was believed that removing him would trigger a civil war due to his many connections and extreme influence with the [[Lore:Synod|Synod]].{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96, 99-100, 246}} In {{Year|4E 38}}, Titus I placed a member of his inner circle, Letine Arese, in Hierem's ministry to spy on him.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|22-23, 72, 98-99}} When he placed Arese in the ministry, the Emperor had no particular concerns about Hierem; only the sort of general paranoia a successful monarch must have.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|23}}

===Umbriel Crisis===
[[File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord of Souls.jpg|left|thumb|Umbriel at the Imperial City]]
In {{Year|4E 48}}, a woman named Annaïg Hoïnart, who was on the flying city known as [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]], contacted Attrebus, prompting him to visit the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|Imperial Palace]] to discuss Umbriel with his father.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|107-108}} At the Imperial Palace, Titus I and Hierem informed Attrebus that the Synod, [[Lore:College of Whispers|College of Whispers]], and other specialists were investigating the flying city and that they believed there was no need to send an expeditionary force since Umbriel was slowly moving north toward Morrowind and was yet to threaten the Empire's borders.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|108-109}} The Emperor also forbade the Crown Prince from attempting to stop Umbriel.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|109}} 

Attrebus disobeyed his father and left the Imperial City with his guards, but before they departed, the Crown Prince's right-hand man, Gulan, followed protocol and reported the plans to the prime minister's office.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|111, 160, 272}}{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|67}} Attrebus and his guards went to the village of [[Lore:Ione|Ione]] and then to the springs near the [[Lore:Ayleid Ruins|Ayleid ruin]] of [[Lore:Sardavar Leed|Sardavar Leed]], where eight assassins ambushed and killed Attrebus' guards.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|111, 119-124, 140, 142}}

Colin Vineben, a Penitus Oculatus inspector, was ordered to track Attrebus and later investigated the ambush site, where he discovered a headless body thought to be the prince's.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|137-141}} After Vineben's return to the Imperial City, Titus I, with Administrator Remar Vel and Intendant Marall, questioned him about his investigation.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|152-155}} Vineben told the Emperor that he believed Attrebus was alive and that the headless body that was assumed to be Attrebus' was an attempt to draw attention away from the true perpetrators and to the Natives insurgents in County [[Lore:Skingrad|Skingrad]] and Thalmor.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|153-155}} Titus I agreed with Vineben, believing it was too convenient that the only burned portion of the body was where Attrebus had a birthmark, so he ordered the Penitus Oculatus to find his son.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|155}} Later, a courier delivered news from [[Lore:Water's Edge|Water's Edge]] to Titus I that Attrebus was alive, so he sent troops to investigate.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|273}}

Umbriel eventually stopped moving north and instead crossed the [[Lore:Valus Mountains|Valus Mountains]] to invade Cyrodiil and begin moving west toward the Imperial City.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|46}} One of Umbriel's reanimated soldiers, known as [[Lore:Wormy|wormies]], delivered a letter from the lord of Umbriel, Vuhon, to one of Titus I's generals, who then informed the Emperor.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|99-100}} The letter claimed that the citizens of the Imperial City were free to leave until Umbriel's arrival, but the Emperor refused to evacuate the city.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|99-100, 254}} Umbriel's wormies eventually completely encircled the Imperial City and then began besieging it.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|199, 237, 245}}

Vineben, who had covertly continued investigating Attrebus' disappearance, used a codeword to arrange a meeting with Titus I in the Imperial Palace, where he presented the journal of a woman named Delia Huerc to the Emperor.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96-100}} According to the journal, Hierem made a trip to [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]] in Black Marsh in {{Year|4E 47}}, where he and the [[Lore:An-Xileel|An-Xileel]] seemingly performed a ritual to summon Umbriel to [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|64-66}} Vineben told Titus I that he and Letine Arese believed that Hierem ordered the assassination attempt on the Crown Prince and was allied with Umbriel, but the Emperor found neither the journal nor Arese's statements incriminating enough to use against Hierem.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|98-100}} Titus I told the Inspector that he needed real proof to present to the Elder Council to remove the Prime Minister from his position or else risk a civil war.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|99-100}} He then gave Vineben a key to Hierem's ministry and rooms and told him to find undeniable proof of Hierem's involvement.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|99-100}} Vineben and Arese later killed Hierem, which was speculated to be the Emperor's true motivation for giving the Inspector the key.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|165-166, 278-281}}

The [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|Umbriel Crisis]] ended when Attrebus and a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] named Sul defeated Vuhon, and an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] named Mere-Glim used his connection with Umbriel and the city's [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] tree to lead Umbriel to the [[Lore:Realm of the Hist|Realm of the Hist]] in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|298-304}} After Umbriel's banishment, Titus I ordered the opening of the food and wine storehouses.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|311}} Later, Titus I met with Attrebus and apologized for overprotecting him.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|311-312}} The Emperor believed that the people should know the truth—that their prince had saved the Imperial City, and perhaps all of Tamriel.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|312}} However, Attrebus believed that the Synod and the College of Whispers should receive credit for stopping Umbriel, not him, because after Hierem's death, Titus I needed their support to secure the Elder Council's support.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|313}} Titus I agreed with Attrebus' suggestion and told him that he returned not only as a man but more than that, as a prince.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|313}}

It is unknown when Titus I died, but Attrebus presumably succeeded him as emperor.{{intnote|Succession|[nb 1]}}
