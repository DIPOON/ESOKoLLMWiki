[[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]] (also called '''Haafingar Hold'''{{Ref|name=LFTSKA|{{Cite Book|Letter from the Skald-King's Agent|ns_base=ON}}}} and the '''Kingdom of Solitude'''){{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} is a relatively small, mountainous, coastal [[Lore:Hold|hold]] in northwestern {{nbsl|Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim}}.<noinclude>{{Ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}}</noinclude> Its capital is the famed city of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], and the two are sometimes treated as one and the same.<noinclude>{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}</noinclude> Solitude is perched atop a large rock outcropping known as the [[Lore:Great Arch|Great Arch]], towering high above the Solitude Inlet, which is found at the end of the [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River]] and the massive Karth delta. However, Haafingar is far more than just Solitude. The Karth River allows for a great deal of commerce in Solitude and the town of [[Lore:Dragon Bridge|Dragon Bridge]] in the [[Lore:Karth River Forest|Karth River Forest]] (mostly in timber and fish), and the long [[Lore:Giant's Coast|Giant's Coastline]] with the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] is littered with frozen shipwrecks and treasures.<noinclude>{{Ref|name=PGE1S}}{{Ref|name=MOS|[[:File:SR-map-Skyrim.jpg|''Map of Skyrim'']]}}</noinclude> [[Lore:Mount Kilkreath|Mount Kilkreath]] and its associated mountains take a large portion of the hold and it is filled with caves and ruins where bandits and creatures make their homes.

The hold shares borders with two other holds, [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] to the south and [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]] to the southeast, and the province of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] borders it to the west, the [[Lore:Forgotten Vale|Forgotten Vale]] found between the hold and [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]]. The [[Lore:Haafingar Mountains|mountains of Haafingar]] are only the tapered end of the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]], an enormous range of jagged mountain peaks to the south which rise up among the clouds of the Reach.<noinclude>

==History==
Given Solitude's obvious importance as a major city, many forts and castles were established around the hold to deter and defend from attackers. Most have fallen into disrepair or have been completely demolished.

Around the time of the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]], in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], it is insinuated that the [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]] Heirs reigned over Haafingar, and employed a militia of marines referred to as the {{Lore Link|Longboat Legions}}. [[Lore:Tobias Hold-None|Tobias Hold-None]] served the Heirs for a time as a Longboat marine, before he later deserted and turned smuggler.{{Ref|name=RGTobias|[[General:Redguard.com Archive/Characters|Tobias' Biography – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}}

In {{Year|3E 127}}, after the death of [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel Septim III]], his mother, Potema, turned Haafingar for the next 10 years to the land of death. Stories of the ancient Wolf Queen being waited on by rotting skeletal chambermaids and holding war plans with vampiric generals terrified her subjects.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Biography of the Wolf Queen}}}}

In {{Year|3E 145}}, the Jarl of Solitude, [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]] was declared Emperor. Unlike most Emperors he ruled mainly from Solitude and not the Imperial City. Almost from the start of the reign of Pelagius III, the eccentricities of his behavior were noted. He embarrassed dignitaries, offended his vassal kings, and, on one occasion, marked the end of a grand ball by attempting to hang himself. His madness is speculated to have been caused by Potema the Wolf Queen, whose own insanity was said to have been infused in Castle Solitude, infecting the next king to rule there.

In the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], following the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] established an embassy in these mountains from which to root out the worship of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] in Skyrim.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} At the time, around 80% of the hold's population was in Solitude.{{ref|name=PRIMA|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide#Legendary Edition|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Legendary Prima Official Game Guide]]}}{{rp|692}}
