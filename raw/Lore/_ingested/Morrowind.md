==Geography==
Morrowind is the northeasternmost nation of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], bounded on the north and east by the Sea of Ghosts, on the northwest by Skyrim, on the southwest by [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], and on the south by [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]].{{Ref|name=PoT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}}{{Ref|name=GtV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}}{{Ref|name=Dialogue|Morrowind Generic Dialogue topics: [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue G|Geography of Morrowind]], [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue M|Morrowind]], [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue V|Vvardenfell]]}} There are numerous crossings on Morrowind's borders with the other provinces, such as [[Lore:Chinzinch Pass|Chinzinch Pass]],{{ref|name=CON|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of Nchuleft}}}} [[Lore:Shadowgate Pass|Shadowgate Pass]],{{ref|name=PGE1M}} [[Lore:Kavas Rim Pass|Kavas Rim Pass]] along Cyrodiil's border,{{ref|name=2920v5|{{Cite Book|2920, Second Seed (v5)}}}} as well as [[Lore:Dunmeth Pass|Dunmeth Pass]] and potentially Frimvorn Pass near Skyrim's border.{{Ref|name=PGE1S}}{{Ref|name=ATOTD|{{Cite Book|Ancient Tales of the Dwemer}}}}

Morrowind is the seat of intense volcanic activity that shapes its landscapes. The [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] separate Morrowind from Skyrim, and the [[Lore:Valus Mountains|Valus Mountains]] separate it from Cyrodiil; all of these ranges and borders intersect at the peak of [[Lore:Trolhetta|Trolhetta]].{{Ref|name=ONTrolhetta|[[ON:Trolhetta|Trolhetta]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} As a result of this isolation, Morrowind has developed a unique ecosystem. The area south of the Inner Sea is composed of the tormented volcanic coasts of [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] and [[Lore:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]]. They lead further south to the fertile [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan Plain]] and the marshes that surrounds [[Lore:Tear|Tear]]. Most of the modern population of Morrowind is gathered in the high hilly uplands and rich fertile river valleys of central Morrowind, especially around the Inner Sea.{{Ref|name=PoT}}

Cloud movement shows that the province is predominantly exposed to upper-level southerly winds, resulting in a mild, though often hot, climate. Morrowind is mostly free from snow and ice (excluding Solstheim), unlike other parts of Tamriel at the same latitude. Depending on the Era, the island of Solstheim to the northwest is part of either Morrowind or Skyrim. The [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] created by [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], one of the three living gods of Morrowind, exists outside of the reality of Nirn.

Morrowind is composed of several districts:

{{ListFix|
* The [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] District on the eponymous great island, dominated by the huge Red Mountain and separated from the continent to the west, south and east by the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]]. It was formerly the domain of House Dagoth and the Dwemer.
* The Dres District in the south and southeast, amid the plains near the border with Black Marsh.
* The Indoril District in the central heartlands and eastern coast, amid the fertile river valleys.
* The Telvanni District in the extreme northeast, made up of their remaining island settlements.
* The Redoran District in northwest, found in the lands along the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] with Skyrim's borders and the Inner Sea.
* The Sadras District of unknown coverage. It is presumably in the same area as its predecessor, the Hlaalu District, within the central/southwest mainland near Cyrodiil's borders.}}

===Vvardenfell===
{{Lore:Vvardenfell}}

In ancient times, the majority of the population in Morrowind (then named Resdayn) was concentrated in Vvardenfell. After the [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]] eruption in {{Year|1E 668}} made the island largely inhospitable, there was a permanent population shift to the mainlaind.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} Most of the remaining population of Vvardenfell was confined to the relatively hospitable west and southwest coasts.{{Ref|name=Dialogue}}

Before the eruption of Red Mountain in {{Year|4E 5}}, the landscape of Vvardenfell was diverse, with the grasslands of the [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] and [[Lore:Grazelands|Grazelands]], the dry plains of the [[Lore:West Gash|West Gash]], the volcanic crags of the [[Lore:Ashlands|Ashlands]], [[Lore:Molag Amur|Molag Amur]] and Red Mountain, and the coastal areas of [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]], the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]], [[Lore:Zafirbel Bay|Zafirbel Bay]] and the [[Lore:Sheogorad|Sheogorad]] region.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}

===Stonefalls===
[[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] is a region situated in the western mainland along the southwestern shore of the Inner Sea.{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}  It borders Bal Foyen to the east, Deshaan to the south, Cyrodiil to the west, [[Lore:The Rift|The Rift]] in Skyrim to the northwest, and the Morrowind part of the Velothi Mountains to the north. The region contains the cities of [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]], [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]], and [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]]. Other notable places include the necropolis of [[Lore:Othrenis|Othrenis]] and two great volcanoes, [[Lore:Ash Mountain|Ash Mountain]] and [[Lore:Tormented Spire|Tormented Spire]].{{ref|name=ON}}

The region's landscape fluctuates from volcanic crags to fertile fields and fungal forests.{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Southern Morrowind===
[[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] rules the southern sweep of Morrowind, where the fertile Deshaan Plain merges with the swamps of Black Marsh.{{Ref|name=Dialogue}}{{Ref|name=MWElone|[[Morrowind:Elone|Elone]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The southeast is a warm coastal plain with broad cultivated areas and marshes and swamps similar to those found in Black Marsh. Southeast Morrowind is flat, wet, and warm.{{Ref|name=Dialogue}}{{Ref|name=MWElone}} The area around [[Lore:Tear|Tear]] is much hotter than the [[Lore:Grazelands|Grazelands]] and is "practically Argonia".{{Ref|name=MWDrulene|[[Morrowind:Drulene Falen|Drulene Falen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Settlements in this area include [[Lore:Silnim Dale|Silnim Dale]] and [[Lore:Rathendis Falls|Rathendis Falls]], with some also theorizing the entrance to [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] was hidden here.{{Ref|name=PGE1M}}{{Ref|name=MWConceptMap|group=UOL}}

===Northwest Morrowind===
The west region is found east of the Velothi Mountains bordering Skyrim and has settlements such as [[Lore:Cormaris View|Cormaris View]], [[Lore:Silgrad Tower|Silgrad Tower]],{{ref|name=OM}} [[Lore:Selethis|Selethis]], as well as a region called the Great Valley.{{Ref|name=MWConceptMap|group=UOL}} The city of [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]], a Redoran stronghold, is located at its northern extremity, near the Dunmeth Pass. ==History==
"Dwemereth" had been inhabited by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] since time immemorial, but the history of Morrowind under their rule is a mystery. When the exiled [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] led by the prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] left [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] and came to what they considered their promised land,{{ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} they found the Dwemer were already well-established in their underground colonies. The two races would not live in peace, as disputes over land and religion regularly led to conflict.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} They would, however, unite against a common enemy following almost two hundred years of oppression by the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s of Skyrim, who invaded the area circa {{Year|1E 240}}.{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} Due to the personal friendship between [[Lore:Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]] of the Chimer and the Dwarf-King [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac]] of the Dwemer, an alliance was formed between them, and the Nords were pushed out by {{Year|1E 416}}. This alliance born out of need formed the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]].{{ref|name=PGE1M}}
[[File:TR-creature-Almalexia.jpg|thumb|left|Almalexia (Morrowind)]]
===Resdayn===
[[File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg|thumb|right|A fresco depicting Veloth leading the Chimeri exodus]]
Nerevar and Dumac ruled jointly over Resdayn. The peace between the Dwemer and Chimer brought prosperity, but the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]] broke out when relations went sour. It ended with the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], which saw the death of both leaders. [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], and [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] tapped the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] to attain god-like powers (though Dagoth Ur would disappear until {{Year|2E 882}}).{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} As a result, the Chimer changed into the Dunmer.{{ref|name=TBORM}} Red Mountain erupted (during the battle, apparently),{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} laying waste to half of Morrowind and beyond.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} {{Year|1E 668}} is known as "Sun's Death" and the "Year of Winter in Summer".{{ref|name=PGE1S}} It's believed that this eruption created the Inner Sea and destroyed entire cities,{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=RoKZ|{{Cite Book|Ruins of Kemel-Ze}}}}{{ref|name=TNM|{{Cite Book|The Nirnroot Missive}}}}{{ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} and led to a population shift to the south. It was sometime after this eruption that the land became known as Morrowind.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=PGE3M}} Some sources seem to suggest this name wasn't popularized until the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=ASHOM}}

===Tribunal Morrowind===
Morrowind under the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] marks the beginning of [[Lore:Dunmer#History|Dunmeri history]]. The Tribunal consolidated their power and succeeded in incorporating themselves into the Chimeri belief system of ancestor and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship, at the expense of the worship of the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]], whom the Dunmer know as the "Bad Daedra".{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} Massive shrines to those four [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] still dot Morrowind, though at the close of the Third Era, they were abandoned ruins typically inhabited by monsters and criminals.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} The Tribunal replaced the First Council with the Grand Council of Morrowind,{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}} and performed many amazing deeds as the Dunmer's immortal protectors and leaders. Cyrodiil sought to incorporate Morrowind into the [[Lore:Second Empire|Empire]] of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]], perpetrating the bloody [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]], which ultimately ended in a treaty at the close of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=PGE3M}} In {{Year|2E 373}}, the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] reported the [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] Ahbiilok to be lairing somewhere in Morrowind.{{ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}}

In {{Year|2E 572}}, the Akaviri army of [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] invaded northern Tamriel. They were defeated at Stonefalls by the combined forces of the Nords (along with [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth Ash-King]]), Dunmer legions of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] Shellbacks.{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}{{ref|name=JtSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} This event led to the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], an uneasy alliance between powers from all three races. The Pact later participated in the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]].{{ref|name=AWON|Events of the [[Online:Alliance War|Alliance War]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Much to the surprise of almost everyone, no such conflict occurred at the rise of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], and [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s [[Lore:Tiber Wars|ambitions of conquest]] were fulfilled through diplomacy. Vivec and Tiber Septim signed the [[Lore:Armistice|Treaty of the Armistice]] in the early years of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{intnote|Armistice|[nb 1]}} The Dunmer became Imperial citizens, accepting Imperial garrisoning forces and the incursion of Cyrodilic institutions and business interests. However, they retained self-rule, house traditions, and religious practices. Vivec secretly supplied the Emperor's agents with the golem [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] as part of this deal.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=OM}}{{ref|name=MWArtisa|[[Morrowind:Artisa Arelas|Artisa Arelas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The Dunmer, and the Chimer before them, divided Morrowind into political districts based on their [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]]. An unknown number of Great Houses rose and fell over the years, but in the late Third Era, [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]] controlled the western mainland bordering Cyrodiil and Skyrim. [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] controlled the central western part of the mainland. [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] controlled the central eastern part of the mainland. [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] controlled the area forming the border with Black Marsh. [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] controlled the eastern region of the nation.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=GHOM|{{Cite Book|Great Houses of Morrowind}}}} For ages, Vvardenfell was a [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Temple]] preserve predominantly inhabited by a few Great House settlements and [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]], but it was opened to greater settlement in {{Year|3E 414}}.{{ref|name=ASHOM}} House Telvanni snatched up the east, House Hlaalu took some of the center and the marshy southwest, and House Redoran held dominion over the remaining areas, though it largely refused to expand settlement.{{ref|name=ASHOM}}{{ref|name=GHOM}} 

In the early-mid [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Prince [[Lore:Uriel Mantiarco|Uriel Mantiarco]] of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] garnered support from many disgruntled kings of Morrowind,{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}} including [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]]{{Ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} and other northern provinces, and coordinated three attacks across the Empire. The second attack was in eastern Morrowind and targeted Imperial garrisons across the coastal islands. [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]]'s consort, Kontin Arynx died while defending these forts. While the [[Lore:Imperial Army|Imperial Army]] was split between eastern Morrowind and western [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], Prince Uriel used that time to invade the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and proclaim himself Emperor Uriel Septim III.{{Ref|name=BHOTE1}}

The [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]] henceforth was underway. Emperor Uriel III and his mother [[Lore:Potema|Potema]] had support across all of Skyrim and northern Morrowind, while High Rock was contentious when the Emperor's uncle, King [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus]] of [[Lore:Gilane|Gilane]] re-conquered western High Rock.{{Ref|name=BHOTE2|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 2}}}}{{Ref|name=TWQ7|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v7}}}} King [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus]] of [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]] led his Argonian army in the battle for Morrowind. In the summer months, Magnus' warriors fought well but had to retreat during the winter. The second century sage [[Lore:Inzolicus|Inzolicus]] believed that this setback made the war last two more years than needed.{{Ref|name=TWQ7}} Meanwhile, the Empire's historical ally [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], led by King [[Lore:Symmachus|Symmachus]], won many battles.{{Ref|name=TMOP}}

The war ended when Uriel III was defeated by Cephorus and killed in a riot in {{Year|3E 127}}. Potema died during the [[Lore:Siege of Solitude|Siege of Solitude]] in {{Year|3E 137}}. When Cephorus became Emperor of Tamriel as Cephorus Septim I, the kings across Tamriel offered their complete support in exchange for new levels of autonomy and independence.{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} When the Emperor passed away only three years after the Siege of Solitude ended, his brother Magnus replaced him and spent the rest of his reign mending political damage from the war, either by forming new relations or punishing traitorous kings.{{Ref|name=BHOTE2}} To form a clean slate with Ebonheart, Emperor Magnus I arranged the marriage of his son, King [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius]] of Solitude, to [[Lore:Katariah|Katariah]],  the heiress of the [[Lore:Ra'athim|Ra'athim]] clan.{{Ref|name=TMOP}}

[[File:MW-place-Sadrith Mora Slave Market.jpg|thumb|right|Telvanni slave pens (Morrowind)]]
Morrowind under the Tribunal was distinct from most of Tamriel in many ways. For one, their legalization of assassination set them apart.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} Also, the Dunmer banned [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]] in their land long before the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] saw fit to do so.{{ref|name=CP|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation}}}}{{ref|name=TBAOT|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}} However, necromancers could continue to operate secretly thanks to another peculiar Morrowind institution: [[Lore:Slavery|slavery]].{{ref|name=CP}} For ages, the Dunmer had enslaved humans and elves, but mainly concentrated on [[Lore:Beastfolk|Beastfolk]], namely [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]], Argonians, and [[Lore:Goblin|Goblin]]s. The right to retain this tradition was guaranteed by the Armistice, and it continued for virtually all of the Third Era, mainly due to the lobbying of Houses Dres and Telvanni, and despite the general abhorrence of the institution by the rest of Tamriel.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=TEP|{{Cite Book|The Eastern Provinces}}}} In the final years of the era, the new King [[Lore:Helseth|Helseth]], looking for a way to exert his authority, banned the controversial practice, eliciting an initially bloody reaction.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} As the Dunmer would find out, though, their transgressions were not forgotten.

Dagoth Ur awoke beneath Red Mountain in 2E 882, heralding enormous changes in Morrowind. The Tribunal led campaigns against him and his monsters for over four hundred years.{{ref|name=DUP}} Around {{Year|3E 417}}, the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Great Ghostfence]] was erected to contain the threat,{{ref|name=TRMehra|[[TR:Mehra Helas|Mehra Helas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} though some sources suggest the Ghostfence existed for centuries before this.{{ref|name=ASHOM}}{{ref|name=MWGDG|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue G|Generic Dialogue G]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The enormous shimmering-blue barrier encircled the entire Red Mountain region of Vvardenfell, but still, Dagoth Ur's monsters and [[Lore:The Blight|The Blight]] managed to seep through. With the advent of the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] in {{Year|3E 427}}, Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Dagoth Ur were soon dead, and Vivec went missing. The Dunmer were still struggling to come to terms with this at the close of the Third Era.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=Tribunal|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

===The Fourth Era===
After the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the Nords and [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s launched attacks.{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}} In Vivec's absence, the [[Lore:Ministry of Truth|Ministry of Truth]] eventually fell, destroying Vivec City and causing Red Mountain to erupt, covering the rest of Vvardenfell in ash and lava. Since this [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] of {{Year|4E 5}}, Red Mountain has been spewing ash even more regularly than before, and Vvardenfell remains largely uninhabitable. When Solstheim came under Dunmer rule, the Redoran took over its administration, though the Telvanni also established the settlement of [[Lore:Tel Mithryn|Tel Mithryn]].{{ref|name=Dragonborn|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

The Argonians of Black Marsh, stronger than ever, soon invaded Morrowind, seizing large tracts of land that had long ago belonged to them.{{ref|name=TIC}} They also sacked many cities, including Mournhold, in southern Morrowind. Though a Redoran army halted their advance, countless Dunmer were killed or displaced,{{ref|name=DBAdril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} and they were forced to establish dozens of colonies elsewhere, including the island of Solstheim.{{ref|name=HRR|{{Cite Book|House Redoran's Reply|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Apart from organized colonies, many Dunmer refugees have settled in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], especially in the so-called Gray Quarter of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]]. Though Mournhold was rebuilt,{{ref|name=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}} the capital of Morrowind was moved to the [[Lore:Rootspire|Rootspire]] in the Redoran city of Blacklight, as the leadership House Redoran had exhibited had greatly increased their power and influence.{{ref|name=DBCindiri|[[Skyrim:Cindiri Arano|Cindiri Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

The Empire's ineffectual protection and seeming indifference to Morrowind's plight made them reviled by the Dunmer. After the Empire "released its grasp" on Morrowind,{{ref|name=DBAdril}} House Hlaalu's Imperial ties resulted in it being replaced by [[Lore:House Sadras|House Sadras]] on the Council of Great Houses.{{ref|name=HORR3|{{Cite Book|History of Raven Rock, Vol. III}}}}{{ref|name=DBLleril|[[Skyrim:Lleril Morvayn|Lleril Morvayn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

==Flora==
[[File:SR-flora-Emperor Parasol Moss.jpg|thumb|left|Emperor Parasols (Dragonborn)]]
[[Lore:Tibrol|Tibrol]] is a species of tree that grows in Morrowind, known for its longevity and for serving as nesting spots for birds.{{Ref|name=36L1|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 1}}}} The largest plantlife in Morrowind is the mighty emperor parasol mushroom, a fungus the size of a tree. They dot the landscape of Morrowind, particularly in Telvanni regions. Other types of fungi include [[Lore:Bloatspore|bloatspore]], [[Lore:Bungler's Bane|bungler's bane]], [[Lore:Hypha Facia|hypha facia]], [[Lore:Luminous Russula|luminous russula]], and [[Lore:Violet Coprinus|violet coprinus]]. Much of the flora of Morrowind must be adapted to its harsh environment, like the [[Lore:Black Lichen|black lichen]], [[Lore:Fire Ferns|fire fern]], and [[Lore:Chokeweed|Chokeweed]], which are found in many rocky, infertile areas. [[Lore:Kreshweed|Kreshweed]] and [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]] appear to thrive in salt water, and can be found along Morrowind's extensive coastline. Some plants haven't adapted and thus are rare, like [[Lore:Red Lichen|red]] and [[Lore:Green Lichen|green lichen]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=Tribunal}} Other tree species found in the province include ash laurels, cedars, and poplars.{{Ref|name=HomeGoods|[[ON:Uzipa|Uzipa]]'s wares in [[ON:Vivec City|Vivec City]] in [[ON:Morrowind|ESO]]}}
[[File:SR-flora-Scathecraw.jpg|thumb|right|Scathecraw (Dragonborn)]]
The Dunmer have managed to make do with what they've been given. They have domesticated many plants, such as [[Lore:Scrib Cabbage|scrib cabbage plants]], which are commonly grown within indoor planters. [[Lore:Wickwheat|Wickwheat]] is an edible grain that grows in the wild. [[Lore:Saltrice|Saltrice]] is both an important food crop and the main ingredient in the popular alcoholic beverage [[Lore:Mazte|mazte]]. [[Lore:Muck|Muck]] is prepared from the common [[Lore:Muckspunge|muckspunge]] plant. The roots of [[Lore:Trama|trama]] shrubs are used to make tea. The berries of the [[Lore:Comberry Plant|comberry plant]] are edible, though they are known for their bitterness. Something tastier, like the [[Lore:Hackle-Lo|hackle-lo]] leaf or [[Lore:Marshmerrow|marshmerrow]] pulp, are more preferred. [[Lore:Corkbulb|Corkbulb]] is grown because its tough roots are used for construction in lieu of large amounts of wood. Dunmer have long made [[Lore:Ash Yam|ash yams]] a staple of their diet, as the hardy vegetable can thrive when planted in ash (hence its name).

Some of their vegetation is sought for the alchemical ingredients they offer, such as [[Lore:Draggle Tail|draggle tail]], the [[Lore:Emperor Parasol Moss|moss of an emperor parasol]], [[Lore:Meadow Rye|meadow rye]], [[Lore:Nirthfly|nirthfly]], [[Lore:Roobrush|roobrush]], [[Lore:Scathecraw|scathecraw]], [[Lore:Slough Fern|slough ferns]], [[Lore:Spiky Grass|spike grass]], [[Lore:Spiny Lloramor|spiny lloramor]], and [[Lore:Sweetbarrel|sweetbarrel]]. Flowering [[Lore:Bittergreen|bittergreen]] vines can be found in wet and dry regions alike. Various other flowers include the [[Lore:Black Anther Plant|black anther]], [[Lore:Gold Kanet|gold kanet]], [[Lore:Golden Sedge|golden sedge]], [[Lore:Heather|heather]], [[Lore:Horn Lily|horn lily]], [[Lore:Noble Sedge|noble sedge]], [[Lore:Stoneflower|stoneflower]], [[Lore:Timsa-Come-By|timsa-come-by]], and the [[Lore:Willow Flower|willow flower]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=Tribunal}}

==Fauna==
[[File:SR-quest-Fetch the Netch.jpg|thumb|right|Netches (Dragonborn)]] Much of the fauna of Morrowind is uniquely adapted to, and dependent on, the ashfall from Red Mountain.{{Ref|name=PoM}} Due to their domestication, [[Lore:Silt Strider|Silt Strider]]s, [[Lore:Netch|netch]]es, and [[Lore:Guar|guar]] are some of the most iconic beasts of Morrowind. But not to be forgotten are the [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]], enormous cave-dwelling insects in Vvardenfell whose eggs the Dunmer have made an industry of "mining" for food. The [[Lore:Sand Kwoom|sand kwoom]] and long-legged [[Lore:Velk|velks]] are species not often seen outside of the province due to their reliance on kresh-weed.{{Ref|name=PoM}} [[Lore:Slaughterfish|Slaughterfish]] and [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] fill the waters of Morrowind, and [[Lore:Mudcrab|mudcrab]]s line the shores. Bothersome [[Lore:Cliff Racer|cliff racers]] dominate the sky in numbers so great they are said to have driven off the dragons (though the racers themselves were purportedly driven from Vvardenfell by [[Lore:Jiub|Saint Jiub]]). Some claim instead that the strange floating gas bags known as [[Lore:Skylamp|skylamps]] ate the Dragons.{{Ref|name=PoM}}

The large, black [[Lore:Shalk|shalk]] bugs in the wilderness can attack with magical fire if threatened. Various Daedra, such as the [[Lore:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilight]], roam the landscape along with various ash creatures and more natural beasts like the [[Lore:Alit|alit]], [[Lore:Kagouti|kagouti]], and packs of [[Lore:Nix-Hound|nix-hounds]]. [[Lore:Goblin|Goblin]]s have been known to inhabit the depths of Morrowind, where they kept [[Lore:Durzog|durzog]]s, ferocious dog-like reptiles, as patrols and war animals. [[Lore:Ash-Fowl|Ash-Fowl]] are a type of creature eaten in the province.{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=MW|Tradehouse notice}}}} And, like virtually every other place in Tamriel, [[Lore:Rat|rat]]s are common.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=Tribunal}}

The island of Vvardenfell used to hold more unique animals such as the [[Lore:Cliff Strider|cliff strider]], the [[Lore:Nix-Ox|nix-ox]], and [[Lore:Vvardvark|vvardvark]], but by the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Ash Blight had wiped them out on the island (though all three species were also known to live on mainland Morrowind). [[Lore:Verminous|Verminous]] were bipedal therapods once common across Velothi lands that went extinct long ago.{{ref|name=LACW|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City‎}}}} Another exotic thing even among the alien wildlife in Morrowind is the bizarre, bioluminescent, and possibly sentient [[Lore:Land-Coral|land-coral]] species that grows on the land around the Inner Sea of mainland Morrowind and Vvardenfell.

==Architecture==
[[File:MW-place-Tel Naga.jpg|thumb|right|Tel Naga, a typical Telvanni tower (Morrowind)]]
Several architectural styles can be seen in Morrowind:

*'''Dunmeri styles:'''
**'''Redoran style''', with organic buildings shaped like (and sometimes made from) crustaceans and mollusks. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second{{ref|name=ESOMW|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and Third Eras.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} The most impressive Redoran structure was [[Morrowind:Under-Skar|Under-Skar]], the mostly subterranean but palatial manor district of [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]], built inside, below, and around the giant shell of an [[Lore:Emperor Crab|Emperor Crab]].
**'''Hlaalu style''', with simple, stuccoed stone, and a multi-level design often involving stairways and ramps. The style&mdash;squarish with rounded edges, and often buttressed&mdash;was present in Vvardenfell in the Second{{ref|name=ESOMW}} and Third Eras.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}
**'''Telvanni style''', with bizarre, twisted mushroom towers and dwellings, which are magically grown rather than built. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second{{ref|name=ONFiri|[[Online:Madam Firilanya|Madam Firilanya]]'s correspondence mentions the Telvanni towers.}}{{ref|name=ESOMW}} and Third Eras,{{ref|name=Morrowind}} as well as in eastern Solstheim in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|name=Dragonborn}}
**'''Velothi style''', which is seen in ancestral tombs, ancient towers, and Temple buildings.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=ON}} Of the later styles, it most clearly influenced Hlaalu.
**'''Responsible Architecture''', a style that is attributed to the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]].{{ref|name=VOF}} This style was similar to that seen in various Daedric ruins in Morrowind, and it was used by the famed architect Guylaine Marilie to build a prison to contain Daedra at the site where Fort Arand was later constructed.{{ref|name=FALT|[[Online:Fort Arand|Fort Arand]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
**An unnamed style, featuring ornate stone buildings with peaked, shingled roofs, was common in mainland Morrowind in the Second Era and even extended into Cyrodiil. The city of Mournhold retained the style in the Third Era.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=ON}}

*'''Other styles:'''
**The Imperial settlements founded in Vvardenfell after {{Year|3E 414}} were built in the traditional Imperial style.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}
**Daedric and Dwemer ruins dated to the early First Era are a common sight in Morrowind, both in Vvardenfell and on the mainland.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=ON}} The heavy curved vaults of the Daedric shrines are considered comfortable by the Dunmer due to their sturdiness, but outsiders regard them as brutal and oppressive.{{ref|name=LOTSS|[[Online:Lair of the Skin Stealer|Lair of the Skin Stealer]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
**[[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] architecture in the form of bulbous, gourd-like mud structures flourished for a time in mainland Morrowind after the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] and the freeing of Argonian slaves in the Second Era.{{ref|name=ON}}
