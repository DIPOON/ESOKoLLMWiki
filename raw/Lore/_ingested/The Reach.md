[[Lore:The Reach|The Reach]] (also known as '''Karth'''){{Ref|name=ONLoadLost|[[Online:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}{{ref|name=RLL|{{Cite Book|Reach Loyalist's Letter|ns_base=Online}}}} is a geographic region surrounded by [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], {{nbsl|Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim}} and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. Its territory once extended into High Rock, but that portion was split off to create the [[Lore:Western Reach|Western Reach]] during the late [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] and has remained separate ever since.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}} The region was historically inhabited by the native, [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-worshipping [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]].{{ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE}}}}{{ref|name=TMOFTR|{{Cite Book|The "Madmen" of the Reach}}}}{{ref|name=AOTD}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Holdings of Jarl Gjalund}}}} Control of the Reach has changed hands between the Reachmen, [[Lore:Nord|Nords]], and even the [[Lore:Imperial|Cyrodilics]] numerous times over the centuries in various conflicts, and the Reachmen became well-known for resisting foreign rule by using ancient magic and an intimate knowledge of the landscape to their advantage in guerrilla warfare and espionage tactics.{{ref|name=TMOFTR}}

In the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the Reach is the southwestern [[Lore:Hold|hold]] of {{nbsl|Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim}}.{{ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} Near the southwestern corner, the capital [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]], formerly a [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] stronghold, emerges from the living rock of the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]], which transition into the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] near the hold's southeast corner.{{ref|name=TCOS|{{Cite Book|The City of Stone}}}} The [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River]] begins in the southern region and drains the mountains, running like a deep gouge through the middle of the hold called the [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River Canyon]].{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|138}} Settlements such as [[Lore:Karthwasten|Karthwasten]] and [[Lore:Old Hroldan|Old Hroldan]] can be found along the Karth, with the {{Lore Link|Sundered Hills}} bordering [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun]]'s [[Lore:Tundra Plateau|Tundra Plateau]] to the east.{{ref|name=TLORE}} The peaks of the Druadach range lie to the west of it, and high bluffs typically rise on the east leading to grassland and tundra. The Reachmen constitute the demographic majority of the Reach and its various settlements, and an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] stronghold can be found in the steppes of the mountains in the south of the hold.<noinclude>

==History==
{{Quote Box|''Anyone can be a king in the Reach. No one can be a king of the Reach.''|[[Online:Lady Belain|Reachman proverb]]}}
Long before the conquests of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] and [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] in the Karth region, [[Lore:Nede|human tribes]] dwelled within the caves in the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]].{{ref|name=ONLVR|[[Online:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At an unknown point in the history between [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] and {{Year|1E 221}}, in the ancient past, the Reach was controlled by the [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] of the [[Lore:Nighthollow Clan|Nighthollow Clan]], known to the Reachfolk as the Night Lords. Eventually, the influence of the vampires waned and accounts of their terror were reduced to mere bedtime stories.{{ref|name=RBS|{{Cite Book|Reach Bedtime Stories}}}}{{ref|name=VSG|[[ON:Vampiric Stained Glass|Vampiric Stained Glass]] antiquity codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The first to invade it were the earliest [[Lore:Atmora|Atmoran]] tribes to settle in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} High King [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|Olaf One-Eye]] conquered the Reach for Skyrim at some point during his reign between {{Year|1E 420}} and {{Year|1E 452}}.{{ref|name=Plaque|The [[:File:SR-misc-Olaf Plaque.png|plaque commemorating Olaf]] outside the [[Skyrim:Palace of the Kings|Palace of the Kings]] in Windhelm}} The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] of [[Lore:Kragen|Clan Kragen]] arrived in the region sometime after 1E 420 and established the city-state of [[Lore:Arkngthamz|Arkngthamz]]. Clan Kragen's success at fending off the local Nords inspired other Dwemer clans to expand into Skyrim as well.{{ref|name=AP|{{Cite Book|Arkngthamz-Phng (book)}}}} It is presumably during this period, prior to the Dwemer's disappearance around {{Year|1E 700}}, that the city of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] was constructed.

Around {{Year|1E 1030}}, the Reach was ruled by ten warring Reachman kings. Most of them submitted to the invading [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Imperial]] forces of Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]], though many ultimately joined the rebellion led by [[Lore:Red Eagle|Red Eagle]], the legendary hero of the Reachmen. They could not win this conflict, but in a campaign lasting two or more years, Red Eagle and his followers made the Empire pay dearly for invading their land.{{ref|name=TLORE|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Red Eagle}}}} Although the Empire was ultimately able to exert control over the Reach, widespread resistance continued long afterward.

After thwarting the [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]i invasion in {{Year|1E 2704}}, Emperor [[Lore:Reman|Reman]] of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] turned his attention to the "madmen of the Reach". He divided the geographical Reach between the Imperial provinces of High Rock and Skyrim to reduce the power or incomes of any individual ruler, and the [[Lore:Western Reach|Western Reach]] has existed as a separate entity ever since. Despite this, the Reach failed to be tamed, and not a decade passed without troops from either [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] or [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] marching into the Reach in another vain attempt to bring the Reachmen to heel.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Emperor Reman's [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] also built [[Lore:Sky Haven Temple|Sky Haven Temple]] in the mountains of the Reach to support their crusade against [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]].{{ref|name=AOTD|{{Cite Book|Annals of the Dragonguard}}}}{{ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}} The Temple was sealed off by Emperor [[Lore:Reman II|Reman II]] after the completion of [[Lore:Alduin's Wall|Alduin's Wall]] in {{Year|1E 2818}}.{{ref|name=AOTD}}
{{NewLine}}{{Quote Box|''Aye. Free blood. That's what the Reach is. Freedom.''|[[Lore:Arana|Arana]], Reach witch}}
Following the Second Empire's collapse in {{Year|2E 430}}, the Reach was left to its own devices. It eventually became an independent kingdom ruled by the Reachmen themselves under the infamous [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach the Black Drake]], who conquered the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] and installed himself as the first of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]].{{ref|name=COTFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}} Durcorach met his demise during a failed invasion of High Rock in {{Year|2E 542}}.{{ref|name=TATOAM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} By {{Year|2E 578}}, even as Durcorach's grandson [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] reigned over the Empire, control of the Reach had passed to Ard [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]], the so-called Despot of Markarth.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Amidst the chaos of both the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] and the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], numerous Reachman tribes attempted to extend their influence into High Rock and Skyrim, but met fierce resistance.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Shortly before {{Year|2E 582}}, Western Skyrim carved a new hold named [[Lore:Karthald|Karthald]] from the northern territory of the Reach to use as a bulwark against Reachman incursions into [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]] and [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]].{{ref|name=GtWSK|{{Cite Book|Guide to Western Skyrim: Karthald}}}}

The Nords reclaimed the Reach as the ninth hold of Skyrim in {{Year|2E 852}}, when the future Emperor [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] besieged and defeated Reachmen forces at [[Lore:Old Hroldan|Old Hroldan]] with an army of [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]n troops and Nord berserkers.{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} Markarth and the Reach were subsequently occupied by the Nords,{{ref|name=SRKibell|[[Skyrim:Kibell|Kibell]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} while Imperial propaganda branded the Reachmen as rebellious and lawless mongrels.{{ref|name=PGE1}} Historians circa {{Year|2E 864}} described the Reach as the most cosmopolitan of Skyrim's Holds. Others stated "the Reach could be mistaken for one of the petty kingdoms of High Rock; it is full of Bretons, Redguards, Cyrodiils, elves of all stripes, and even a few misplaced Khajiit."{{ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|498}}

Nationalistic Reachmen passively resisted Nord rule for centuries until finding the perfect opportunity to strike in {{Year|4E 174}}, while the forces of the Empire were committed to fighting the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] in the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]]. The Reachmen rose up, seizing Markarth and most of the hold, ruling the Reach as an independent kingdom for two years. The government of Skyrim, growing desperate, enlisted a militia led by [[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]] which retook the Reach in {{Year|4E 176}}. The survivors of the revolt were scattered into the wilderness, becoming the [[Lore:Forsworn|Forsworn]].{{ref|name=TBOM}}
