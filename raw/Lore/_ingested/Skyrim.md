[[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], the northernmost province of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], is a cold and mountainous region also known as the '''Old Kingdom''', '''Mereth''', or the '''Fatherland''',{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}} or '''Keizaal''' in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|dragon tongue]].{{ref|[[Skyrim:Odahviing|Odahviing]]'s dialogue}} Many past battles have given it a ravaged appearance and many ruins.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} Though currently inhabited primarily by [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s, the [[Lore:Snow Elf|Elves]] who they replaced had resided there since time immemorial.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} The sovereign, the High King of Skyrim, is chosen by the [[Lore:Moot|Moot]], a convention of [[Skyrim:Jarl|jarls]].{{ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Skyrim's Rule}}}} A jarl is a regional ruler chosen through heredity and, rarely, through right of arms.{{ref|name=SR}}{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The High King typically rules until death, though acts of dishonor, particularly the appearance of cowardice, can lead to the recall and reconsideration of the Moot.{{ref|name=SR}}{{ref|name=Sybille|[[Skyrim:Sybille Stentor|Sybille Stentor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Since the [[Lore:Pact of Chieftains|Pact of Chieftains]] was signed in {{Year|1E 420}}, the Moot does not give serious consideration to anyone but the High King's direct heir unless one is not available.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}<includeonly>

==Geography==
[[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] lies to the east over the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]], [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] is south beyond the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]], [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] (and the latest incarnation of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]) is to the south and west, [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] is past the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]] to the west, and the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] runs along the province's long northern coastline. Skyrim holds four of the five highest mountains in Tamriel{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} (the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] being the only one outside Skyrim). Much of the northern half of Skyrim is cold and covered in snow. However, the southern regions of the province are relatively mild.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Holds===
The Nords long ago divided the province into nine geopolitical regions known as "holds". Each hold is a large area of land roughly equivalent to a county in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], each individually governed by a jarl who maintains court in the hold's capital city. The nine traditional holds of Skyrim are: [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]], [[Lore:The Pale|the Pale]], [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]], [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]], [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun]], [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath]], and [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]].{{ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} During the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], when the Reach became an independent [[Lore:Reachman|Reachman]] kingdom and lost its status as a Nordic hold, a tenth hold named [[Lore:Karthald|Karthald]] was created from the Reach's northern fringe.{{ref|name=GtWSK|{{Cite Book|Guide to Western Skyrim: Karthald}}}}

Winterhold, Eastmarch, the Rift, and the Pale, lying to the north and east of the province, are collectively known as the "Old Holds", and remain isolated from the rest of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], both geographically and politically. In contrast, the western hold of the Reach has historically been more cosmopolitan and members of all the races of the continent have made their homes there.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}

===Solstheim===
Although the history of the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] is somewhat complicated, there was a time when it was considered a part of Skyrim.{{ref|name=DBLoading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Lore|Loading screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} The [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]], an offshoot tribe of Nords, inhabit the northeastern corner of the island.{{ref|name=BM|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=DB|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, the Nords of Skyrim launched a successful campaign to claim the whole of Solstheim.{{ref|Rumors in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} In {{Year|4E 16}}, the High King of Skyrim formally gave the island to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] as a refuge for the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] after the events of [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]].{{ref|name=DBLoading}} The northern half of the island is snowy and mountainous. The southern half was originally covered in coniferous forests, but after the Red Year it was transformed into an [[Lore:Solstheim Ashlands|ashland]] similar to those seen in Morrowind.{{ref|name=BM}}{{ref|name=DB}}

===Bleakrock Isle===
[[Lore:Bleakrock Isle|Bleakrock Isle]], also considered a part of Skyrim, is a small, snowy island in the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] between [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]] and [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]. A small populace of the Nords inhabited the [[Lore:Bleakrock Village|Bleakrock Village]] until they were driven from the island by the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] in {{Year|2E 582}}. It is unknown if the island was resettled afterwards.{{Ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Icereach===
[[Lore:Icereach|Icereach]] is a small island in the Sea of Ghosts. Once used by the Nords as an outpost to protect whalers from Orcish corsairs, the island eventually became the home of a coven of Reachfolk witches who adopted the island's name. While the Nords believe that the Icereach Coven seized the island by force, the coven maintain they acquired it through a cursed bargain with its original Nordic inhabitants. Around 2E 582, the Icereach Coven was defeated by an expeditionary force sent by High King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] to investigate strange storms around the island that had been caused by the witches' rituals.{{Ref|name=Online}}

==History==
Skyrim's history is mostly [[Lore:Nord#History|that of the Nords]]. The Nords believe the sky, [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], breathed life into them at the summit of the [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]], which is now considered the tallest mountain in Tamriel (the Red Mountain was acknowledged as the tallest before its eruption during the Red Year).{{ref|name=COTS|{{Cite Book|Children of the Sky}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=Loading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} While historians tend to portray Ysgramor as the first Nordic settler of Skyrim, ushering in concurrent waves of immigration from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]], this is not the case. While their exact origins are unknown, the most widely accepted theory is that the early [[Lore:Nedic|Nedic]] peoples (a now extinct culture) settled in Skyrim after arriving from Atmora well before the arrival of Ysgramor, and from there spread to the rest of Tamriel.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}

In ancient times, Skyrim was ruled by the malevolent [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]]. This early society revolved around the worship of dragons through intermediaries known as dragon priests, who ruled as gods above men. Sometime in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the mythical [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] saw the overthrow of the Dragon Cult when [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], Akatosh's firstborn, was defeated atop the Throat of the World. The veneration of animals as gods was eventually replaced by the Eight Divines.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}}

The many mountain ranges in and around the province resulted from unknown cataclysms in the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]].{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} The only known relic from this time is the [[Lore:Skyforge|Skyforge]], an ancient, powerful, and still-active forge built into the living rock in modern-day Whiterun. Who built it remains a mystery, but the Elves feared it, and this led the invading [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]ns to claim it for themselves.{{ref|name=SOTR7|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 7}}}}

The [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] and [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] occupied Skyrim until the late Merethic Era, when the final wave of Nordic immigration from Atmora, led by [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]], finally established Nordic supremacy in modern-day Skyrim.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=FC}} Settlers from Atmora crossed the Sea of Ghosts and made landfall here many times, often clashing with those who had already established themselves.{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=FC}} These early settlers called the land "Mereth", after the Elves that roamed the untamed wilderness. While the Nedic peoples were generally peaceful, the arrival of Ysgramor and the proto-Nords sparked a long, antagonistic chain of conquests that ultimately expelled the Elves from Skyrim.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}}{{ref|name=PGE1S}} The instigating spark of conflict was the infamous [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]], where the Elves attacked the human settlement of [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]]. According to Imperial scholars, the Elves realized that this newer race could outpace their relatively stagnant own. Others posit that the human inhabitants had discovered a powerful artifact, the [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]], that the Elves desired.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Nonetheless, Ysgramor escaped the carnage, returning to Skyrim with his now legendary band of [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]], slaughtering the Elves and establishing man as the foremost race in Skyrim.{{ref|name=PGE1S}} Ysgramor's clan expanded their territory, and it continued to expand and contract by winning and losing territories in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] during the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=TLST|{{Cite Book|The Legendary Sancre Tor}}}} Elven rule of Skyrim is thought to have ended under the reign of King [[Lore:Harald|Harald]] ({{Year|1E 143}} - {{Year|1E 221}}), though pockets of [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] hid away in the wilderness.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}

In the years after {{Year|1E 420}}, however, the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] of [[Lore:Kragen|Clan Kragen]] migrated into Skyrim from [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]], following in the footsteps of [[Lore:Rourken|Clan Rourken]]'s exodus. Clan Kragen founded the city of [[Lore:Arkngthamz|Arkngthamz]], which flourished despite constant attacks from the Nords. The success of Arkngthamz encouraged other Dwemer clans to settle in Skyrim, and these joined with Clan Kragen to form a loose alliance of four city-states that were considered unassailable. Clan Kragen's presence eventually extended further west into the [[Lore:Dragon's Teeth Mountains|Dragon's Teeth Mountains]], where they carved out an even larger bastion called [[Lore:Fang Lair|Arkngthamz-Phng]].{{ref|name=AP|{{Cite Book|Arkngthamz-Phng (book)}}}} The prosperity of Skyrim's Dwemer city-states was relatively short-lived, as the discovery of rich deposits of [[Lore:Aetherium|Aetherium]] and the creation of the Aetherium Forge immediately shattered the alliance and launched decades of internal conflict. With the Dwemer distracted and weakened, the Nords under High King [[Lore:Gellir|Gellir]] finally conquered the squabbling city-states in only three years, though the Dwemer would return a century later and reclaim their former holdings.{{ref|name=TAW|{{Cite Book|The Aetherium Wars}}}} When the entire Dwemer race disappeared around {{Year|1E 700}}, they left behind the [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]], Snow Elves who they had allowed to take refuge with them and then twisted into rebellious slave monsters.{{ref|name=TFAS|{{Cite Book|The Falmer: A Study}}}} They remained under the ground, occasionally making forays to the surface and creating legends among the Nords of their presence and malevolent intent.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=TFAS}}

[[File:LO-map-Skyrim (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumb|right|Skyrim circa {{Year|3E 432}}]]
After the assassination of High King [[Lore:Logrolf|Logrolf]] in {{Year|2E 431}}, a dispute over the succession resulted in Skyrim's division into two independent kingdoms. It began when Jarl [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]] of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] challenged the legitimacy of Logrolf's heir, [[Lore:Freydis|Freydis]], and a [[Lore:Moot|Moot]] was convened. Although Freydis was accepted by the Crown of Verity, a magical artifact crafted to test the worthiness of potential rulers of Skyrim, and named High Queen in [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], a partial Moot held in Solitude declared Svartr to be High King.{{ref|name=TCOF|{{Cite Book|The Crown of Freydis}}}} From then on, Skyrim was divided into two kingdoms: Eastern Skyrim and Western Skyrim. In the east, Freydis and her successors ruled over [[Lore:The Rift|The Rift]], [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], [[Lore:The Pale|The Pale]], and [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]],{{ref|name=TCOF}}{{Ref|name=ESSO|[[ON:East Skyrim Scout Outfit|East Skyrim Scout Outfit]]'s item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} while in the west, Svartr and his successors ruled over [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]],{{ref|name=MtC:ES|[[Lore:Meet the Character - Eerika Skjoralmor|Meet the Character - Eerika Skjoralmor]]}}{{Ref|name=WSSO|[[ON:West Skyrim Scout Outfit|West Skyrim Scout Outfit]]}} [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun Hold]],{{Ref|name=WSSO}}{{ref|name=MtC:ES}} [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]],{{Ref|name=GtWSHj|{{Cite Book|Guide to Western Skyrim: Hjaalmarch}}}} [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]],{{Ref|name=GtWSH|{{Cite Book|Guide to Western Skyrim: Haafingar}}}} and later the newly-established hold of Karthald.{{ref|name=GtWSK|{{Cite Book|Guide to Western Skyrim: Karthald}}}}{{ref|name=TCOF}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI}} This schism persisted at least until the time of the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] in {{Year|2E 582}};{{ref|name=SIR|{{Cite Book|Second Invasion: Reports}}}} Eastern Skyrim fought in the war as a member of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], while Western Skyrim remained neutral.{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} At that time, [[Lore:The Reach|The Reach]] did not belong to either kingdom and the Reachmen had banded together under Ard [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]], the Despot of Markarth, who temporarily allied himself with [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]].{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI}} The two kingdoms of Skyrim, as well as The Reach, were eventually reunified into a single realm following the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]].{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}{{ref|name=SRKibell|[[Skyrim:Kibell|Kibell]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]], the Kingdom of Skyrim waged the [[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]] against [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], temporarily annexing the territories of [[Lore:Jehanna|Jehanna]] from High Rock, and [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] and the northern half of [[Lore:Dragonstar|Dragonstar]] from Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=PGE3S}}

[[Lore:Orc|Orc]] strongholds dot the wilderness; many Orcs were escorted into Skyrim by the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] following the sacking of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] in the early Fourth Era.{{ref|name=TIF|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}} Besides the Orcs, the Elven population has also increased due to the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] of {{Year|4E 5}}, when natural disasters in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] sent many [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] fleeing into eastern Skyrim.{{ref|name=TRY|{{Cite Book|The Red Year}}}}{{ref|name=DOS|{{Cite Book|Dunmer of Skyrim}}}} Following the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] officials hunted for and persecuted [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] worshippers, thus bringing some small measure of Elven rule back to Skyrim.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=NA|{{Cite Book|Nords Arise!}}}} While these smaller creatures vied for dominance over the millennia, the [[Lore:Giant|Giant]]s have been largely ignored (and often protected by law).{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Known Rulers==
[[File:SR-icon-armor-Jagged Crown.png|thumb|right|The Jagged Crown]]
The political structure of the Elves who first inhabited Skyrim is unknown. When the Atmorans first migrated to Skyrim, before the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priest]]s of the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] held roles on par with kings, but the Atmorans recognized relatively secular chieftains as their leaders.{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Skyrim}}}}{{ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} Ysgramor is the first known human ruler of Skyrim.{{ref|name=PGE1S}} His progeny would continue to rule after his death as the [[Lore:Ysgramor Dynasty|Ysgramor Dynasty]]; King Harald, the 13th of his line, is considered the first "historical" ruler.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} Since Harald's time or even before, the High King traditionally wore the [[Lore:Jagged Crown|Jagged Crown]], though it was lost with [[Lore:Borgas|King Borgas]] in {{Year|1E 369}} until its rediscovery in {{Year|4E 201}}.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} A new symbol of rulership, the Crown of Verity, later called the Crown of Freydis, was crafted to determine the worthiness of potential rulers following the death of Olaf One-Eye, but has not been seen beyond the Second Era.{{ref|name=TCOF}} Known High Kings/Queens and their reigns are listed below:

== Capitals of Skyrim ==
[[File:ON-item-Crown of Freydis.jpg|thumb|right|The Crown of Freydis]]
In practice, the city where the High King resided also served as the capital of Skyrim. This has changed over the course of centuries, and at times of division there was more than one capital city. When the Dragon Cult dominated Skyrim until the late Merethic Era, the capital was at one point Bromjunaar, located at what would later be called [[Lore:Labyrinthian|Labyrinthian]]. When [[Lore:Harald|Harald]] assumed the mantle of High King in {{Year|1E 143}}, he relinquished his holdings in Atmora and established Skyrim as an independent kingdom with [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] as its capital.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S}} During the time of [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage the Gifted]] (r. 1E 222 – ?), Windhelm became the capital of not just Skyrim, but the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]] that also encompassed [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], and parts of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{Ref|name=PGE1S}} High King [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]] (died 1E 369) ruled from [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]], however.{{Ref|name=AHoD}} High King [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|Olaf One-Eye]] (r. 1E 420 – 1E 452) resided in [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]].{{ref|name=OATD|{{Cite Book|Olaf and the Dragon}}}} In the late First Era, [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] served as the seat of High Queens such as "Head-Rolling" Roleke and High Kings like [[Lore:Alldimar|Alldimar]] the Ghostmaker.{{Ref|name=SoSR}} During the schism of the Second Era (since 2E 431), [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]] and his descendants ruled the Kingdom of Western Skyrim from Solitude, while [[Lore:Freydis|Freydis]] and her descendants reigned the Kingdom of Eastern Skyrim from Windhelm.{{ref|name=TCOF}} Winterhold was counted among other provincial capitals circa {{Year|3E 427}}.{{Ref|name=MCMap|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Tamriel]] – [[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex|Morrowind Codex]]}} Solitude had been the capital of Skyrim for several years by {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|name=Thaer|[[SR:Thaer|Thaer]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Architecture==
[[File:SR-place-Winterhold.jpg|right|thumb|The stone and timber buildings of Winterhold]]
[[File:SR-place-Blue Palace.jpg|left|thumb|The Blue Palace of Solitude]]
===Nordic===
Nord architects are masters of creating structures that last for generations, with designs that can be found from [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] to [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] dating over 3,000 years old.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} Many ancient Nordic ruins built into the mountains dating back to the Merethic and First eras remain standing throughout Skyrim.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They are towering, foreboding structures of black granite. Some were settlements and temples from when the Nords lived under the rule of the Dragon Cult and after; others are burial tombs for the honored dead. Most modern buildings in Skyrim are built partly underground to conserve heat, and are made with stones, with wood used only for support, and roofs of straw. The Nords are known as masters of lumber construction, and these houses are essential to keep in heat in the freezing climate.{{ref|name=PGE1S}} Some Nord wells are known to have spikes attached to the bottom of buckets to crack through ice.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}}

After the reign of Ysgramor, Nord stonesmiths created a method of erecting igneous rock. Initially, these blocks were hewed from porphyry deposits, although by the Second Era, they were somewhat supplanted by hard-wearing granite and volcanic stone gathered from the eastern side of the province. Each block was slotted together without requiring seams or mortar, allowing huge walls, structures, and towers to be constructed with both ease and haste, even with irregularly-shaped stone. Many of these structures proved difficult for invaders to dismantle, such as the Old Fort, a royal bastion on Skyrim's northern frontier, which still stood in the mid-Second Era after being built in the time of the First Empire.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}}{{ref|name=PGE1S}}

===Imperial===
With inclusion in the Second and Third Empires, Imperial forts are spread across Skyrim's landscape.{{Ref|name=IF|[[Skyrim:Military Forts|Military Forts]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Solitude is seen as the epicenter of Imperial influence.{{Ref|name=SRLoadSolitude|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#City-Specific|Solitude]] loading screen in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Snow Elf===
Remaining elements of Snow Elf architecture is largely absent in most of Skyrim, the grandest examples being in the Chantry of Auriel within the Forgotten Vale, completed during the First Era, well into the Nords conquest of Skyrim.{{Ref|name=FV|[[Skyrim:Forgotten Vale|Forgotten Vale]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}} Another example lays in the underground of Fort Greenwall.{{ref|name=LLAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}} Their architecture bears some superficial similarities to the Ayleids or Altmer stonecraft, and they were capable of making wayshrines with magical teleporting gates.{{Ref|name=FV}} By the Second Era the Falmer made use of Chaurus chitin to make huts and fencing in their underground dwellings and rarely in their burgeoning surface outposts.{{ref|name=ONGG|[[ON:Ghostlight Grotto|Ghostlight Grotto]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

===Dwarven===
The Dwemer cities and outposts of clan Kragen dots Skyrim's landscapes, most of which are abandoned.{{Ref|name=SDR|[[Skyrim:Dwarven Ruins|Dwarven Ruins]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The major exception is the city of Markarth, formerly Nchuand-Zel, which acts as the capital of the Reach.{{Ref|name=Markarth|Markarth in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]'' and ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Orcish===
Several Orc Strongholds are found throughout Skyrim, typically centered around wooden long-houses, many lie directly outside mines. Strongholds are typically surrounded by wooden walls.{{ref|name=OS|[[Skyrim:Orcish Strongholds|Orcish Strongholds]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Flora==
[[File:SR-place-Falkreath Hold.jpg|left|thumb|Skyrim's Pine Forest]]
[[File:SR-place-Eldergleam.jpg|thumb|right|The [[Lore:Eldergleam|Eldergleam]]]]
Skyrim's frigid environment still supports a great wealth of flora, notably its many pine forests.{{ref|name=Loading}}{{ref|name=HGTS|{{Cite Book|Herbalist's Guide to Skyrim}}}} Many of its plants and fungi are useful for alchemy, including [[Lore:Bleeding Crown|bleeding crown]] mushrooms, the [[Lore:Deathbell|Deathbell]] flower, [[Lore:Jazbay|Jazbay]] grapes, and [[Lore:Nightshade|Nightshade]]. [[Lore:Tundra Cotton|Tundra cotton]] is not only used for making potions, but for making many of the fabrics for clothing and other purposes in Skyrim.{{ref|name=HGTS}}

[[Lore:Crimson Nirnroot|Crimson Nirnroot]] is a special variant of nirnroot that can be found exclusively in the caverns of Blackreach beneath the province.{{Ref|name=SFJ|{{Cite Book|Sinderion's Field Journal}}}}

==Fauna==
[[File:SR-book-Horkerscene.png|right|thumb|A horker attack]]
The [[Lore:Bestiary|beasts]] of Skyrim are as diverse as they are dangerous. [[Lore:Horker|Horker]]s line the northern coast, posing a greater danger to hunters and fisherman, and [[Lore:Ice Wolf|ice wolves]] prowl in pairs all over the province.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=HA|{{Cite Book|Horker Attacks}}}} [[Lore:Slaughterfish|Slaughterfish]] and other types of fish glide through the rivers and lakes.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Other common beasts roam the wilderness, and rumors abound of less common ones like [[Lore:Werewolf|werewolves]] and [[Lore:Wispmother|Wispmother]]s.{{ref|name=LL|{{Cite Book|Lost Legends}}}} Many of them, such as [[Lore:Hagraven|Hagraven]]s and [[Lore:Luna Moth|luna moths]], yield body parts used in alchemy.{{ref|name=HGTS}} [[Lore:Ice Wraith|Ice wraiths]] and other creatures are hunted in the mountains by young traditional Nords as rites of passage.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=HB|{{Cite Book|Herbane's Bestiary}}}} Undead [[Lore:Draugr|Draugr]] still guard the ruins of the province.{{ref|name=ATD|{{Cite Book|Amongst the Draugr}}}} Of course, the most legendary "creatures" are the majestic and intelligent [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]], who have left their mark on Skyrim in more ways than one. Ancient structures dedicated to the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] and "word walls" in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|language of the dragons]] still dot the landscape.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=DLMNM|{{Cite Book|Dragon Language: Myth no More}}}}

;[[Lore:Labyrinthian|Labyrinthian]]: An ancient Nordic ruin. Named for the labyrinth built sometime in the First Era by Arch-Mage [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]], although the ruins themselves are much older, having been the city of Bromjunaar, the capital of the Dragon Cult.

;[[Lore:Morthal|Morthal]]: The fairly small, humble town of little economic or strategic importance is noteworthy as the capital of Hjaalmarch.

;[[Lore:Riften|Riften]]: The capital of the Rift, long a center for crime, lies close to both Cyrodiil and Morrowind.

;[[Lore:Snowhawk|Snowhawk]]: A city located in Hjaalmarch, abandoned sometime during the Fourth Era.
;[[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]: A major port city and capital of Haafingar.
;[[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]]: The highest peak in the province of Skyrim. Though once considered second to Red Mountain, it is now known as the highest mountain in all of Tamriel.
;[[Lore:Treva River|Treva River]]: A river at the center of the Rift. It flows from Lake Honrich to Lake Geir.
;[[Lore:White River|White River]]: The longest river in Skyrim, stretching from the south of the province to the northeast corner. Its source is Lake Ilinalta, north of Falkreath. It flows northeast to join the Sea of Ghosts past Windhelm.
;[[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]]: A city in central Skyrim, and the capital of Whiterun Hold. Built at the spring of the White River.
;[[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]]: The capital of Eastmarch lies close to the border with Morrowind. It's the famed location of the Palace of the Kings.
;[[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]]: The capital of the eponymous hold was once quite prosperous, but was devastated in the Great Collapse in {{Year|4E 122}}.
