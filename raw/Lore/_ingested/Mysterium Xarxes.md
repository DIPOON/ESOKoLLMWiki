# Lore:Mysterium Xarxes

The ''[[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|Mysterium Xarxes]]'' was [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes (book)|a tome]] written by <noinclude>[[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]]</noinclude><includeonly>Mehrunes Dagon</includeonly>, who scribed it in "<noinclude>[[Lore:Deadlands|the deserts of rust and wounds]]</noinclude><includeonly>the deserts of rust and wounds</includeonly>".{{ref|name=MDC1|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 1}}}} It was an artifact of great—and evil—power. The ''Xarxes'' was said to have a vile corrupting influence; reading from the book required magical protection from its powers, even merely handling it was said to be dangerous.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}  Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [[Lore:Tracts Perilous|Tract Perilous]].{{Ref|name=OTP|{{Cite Book|On Tracts Perilous}}}}

In <noinclude>{{Year|2E 582}}</noinclude><includeonly>2E 582</includeonly>, the Xarxes was stolen from the retreat of <noinclude>[[Lore:Leovic|Emperor Leovic]]</noinclude><includeonly>Emperor Leovic</includeonly> by <noinclude>[[Lore:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]</noinclude><includeonly>Eveli Sharp-Arrow</includeonly>.{{Ref|name=ESOEveli|[[Online:Eveli Sharp-Arrow#The Emperor's Secret|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Emperor's Secret|The Emperor's Secret]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Calia, one of the <noinclude>[[Lore:Four Ambitions|Four Ambitions]]</noinclude><includeonly>Four Ambitions</includeonly> of Mehrunes Dagon, would eventually use the book to learn how to use her power to drain Daedric energies, an ability which would aid in the defeat of high priest of the Waking Flame <noinclude>[[Lore:Ertus Vandacia|Ertus Vandacia]]<noinclude><includeonly>Ertus Vandacia</includeonly>, who had imbued himself with some of the power of Dagon's <noinclude>[[Lore:Nymic|Egonymic]]</noinclude><includeonly>Egonymic</includeonly> in the process of performing the ritual to create the Ambitions.{{ref|name=ESOCalia|[[ON:Calia|Calia]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOSombren|[[ON:Sombren|Sombren]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} After Vandacia was defeated and his attempt to merge <noinclude>[[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]</noinclude><includeonly>Nirn</includeonly> and the Deadlands using the power of the Ambition Destron was foiled, the book was given to the Ambition Sombren, who intended to study it to learn more about the Four Ambitions.{{Ref|name=PoA|[[ON:Pyre of Ambition|Pyre of Ambition]] quest in {{g|on|blackwood}}}} {{ref|name=ESOSombren}} However, the power of the book took its toll. Influenced by the artifact, Sombren joined Dagon and stole the power of two of the other Ambitions, which he used to start a merging of the Deadlands and Nirn. The crisis was ended when fourth Ambition, <noinclude>[[Lore:Mairead|Mairead]]<noinclude><includeonly>Mairead</includeonly>, took the power of the other three Ambitions from Sombren and used the Egonymic to banish Mehrunes Dagon himself, ending the merging of worlds and saving Nirn. The power of the Ambitions was then stolen from Mairead by <noinclude>[[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]]<noinclude><includeonly>Lyranth the Foolkiller</includeonly>.{{ref|name=ESOCalia}}{{ref|name=ESOMairead|[[ON:The Anchorite|Mairead]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOLyranth|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Ambition's End|Ambition's End]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}

The book was given by Dagon to <noinclude>[[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]</noinclude><includeonly>Mankar Camoran</includeonly>. After studying the tome, Mankar wrote the <noinclude>''[[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes]]''</noinclude><includeonly>''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes''</includeonly> (also known as the ''Mythic Dawn Commentaries''). Inspired by the prophecies and promises within the book, Mankar founded the <noinclude>[[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]]</noinclude><includeonly>Mythic Dawn</includeonly>, a Daedric cult devoted to Mehrunes Dagon. The ''Xarxes'' acted as the cult's holy book, and was stored in the Mythic Dawn's hidden shrine in the caverns beneath [[Lore:Lake Arrius|Lake Arrius]] in <noinclude>[[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]</noinclude><includeonly>Cyrodiil</includeonly>. Using the power of the book, Mankar created <noinclude>[[Lore:Gaiar Alata|Gaiar Alata]]</noinclude><includeonly>Gaiar Alata</includeonly>, or "Paradise", an alternate realm where the souls of Mythic Dawn cultists went in death, to be resurrected as 'Ascended Immortals', who were revived continuously whenever slain.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

In <noinclude>{{Year|3E 433}}</noinclude><includeonly>3E 433</includeonly>, following the Mythic Dawn's assassination of <noinclude>[[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Emperor Uriel Septim VII]]</noinclude><includeonly>Emperor Uriel Septim VII</includeonly> and all of his legitimate heirs, the <noinclude>[[Lore:Blades|Blades]]</noinclude><includeonly>Blades</includeonly> infiltrated the cult and stole the ''Mysterium Xarxes'' from their shrine to Mehrunes Dagon. It was taken to [[Lore:Cloud Ruler Temple|Cloud Ruler Temple]], where <noinclude>[[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]</noinclude><includeonly>Martin Septim</includeonly>, the illegitimate son of Uriel and the heir to the throne, translated the ''Xarxes'' and discovered a way to create a portal to Camoran's Paradise. The ritual required a <noinclude>[[Lore:Great Welkynd Stone|Great Welkynd Stone]]</noinclude><includeonly>Great Welkynd Stone</includeonly>, a [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Great Sigil Stone]], a <noinclude>[[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]]</noinclude><includeonly>Daedric</includeonly> artifact, and an <noinclude>[[Lore:Aedra|Aedric]]</noinclude><includeonly>Aedric</includeonly> artifact. The <noinclude>[[Lore:Hero|Hero of Kvatch]]</noinclude><includeonly>Hero of Kvatch</includeonly> entered Gaiar Alata and defeated Camoran, retrieving the <noinclude>[[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]]</noinclude><includeonly>Amulet of Kings</includeonly>. The ''Mysterium Xarxes'' was destroyed in the process.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}<noinclude>

Only a single, singed page survived the events of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], and came into the possession of the Vesuius family, who were once members of the Mythic Dawn. In {{Year|4E 201}}, the page was put on display by Silus Vesuius in his "Mythic Dawn Museum" in the [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] city of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]].{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} [[Lore:Vonos|High Priest Vonos]] of the reformed Mythic Dawn had traveled to Dawnstar many times, before the museum had even opened, to study the page.{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}}

The first page can be translated to "as for the rest the weak shall cast down the might shall trem" . Though some letters on the second page are missing, it can be translated to "be winnowed the timid shall be bleed at my feet and pray for pardon".
