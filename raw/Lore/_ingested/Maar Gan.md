[[Lore:Maar Gan|Maar Gan]] (also called '''Markgran Forest'''){{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Markgran Forest|Markgran Forest]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} was a [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]]-mining village in the ash wastes of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], found within the province of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. According to [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal]] mythology, the town was the site of a confrontation between [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], where the warrior-poet taunted the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|daedric prince]] so he would throw a rock intended for the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] at them instead. Since then, a shrine was built around the '''Magic Rock of Maar Gan''' and the town became a popular pilgrimage stop.{{Ref|name=MWPilgrim|[[Morrowind:Pilgrimage to Maar Gan|Pilgrimage to Maar Gan]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
{{Quote Box|''"Here Mehrunes Dagon held this rock high above the Dunmer. Vivec taunted {{sic|Mehrines|Mehrunes}} Dagon so that Dagon threw the rock at Vivec instead of the people."''|Description found on the Magic Rock of Maar Gan{{ref|name=MS|[[Morrowind:Shrines#Magic Rock of Maar Gan|Magic Rock of Maar Gan]]'s description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}}}
When the incident took place is unknown. Even though the settlement did not exist in {{Year|2E 582}},{{Ref|name=VTAN|{{Cite Book|ESO: Morrowind - Vvardenfell Then and Now|ns_base=GEN}}}} a location of the same name was a regular stop used by [[Lore:Silt Strider|silt strider]] caravaneers.{{Ref|name=SSCL|{{Cite Book|Silt Strider Caravaner's Log|ns_base=ON}}}} As the story goes, Mehrunes Dagon threatened the Dunmer with a large rock but Vivec was able to convince him to redirect it to them. The site where it landed is where the Maar Gan Shrine is built. The [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], [[Lore:Anhaedra|Anhaedra]] was bound to the rock and pilgrims traveled to the shrine to re-enact the event by hurling insults at him.{{Ref|name=MWPilgrim}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the village of Markgran Forest was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Nisain and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Verarchen Hall|Verarchen Hall]].{{Ref|name=AR}} Markgran Forest was neighbored by several settlements, one of which included [[Lore:Stoneforest|Stoneforest]] to the south.{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

After Vvardenfell was opened to the rest of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] in {{Year|3E 417}}, [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]] began to covet the land and captured much of northwestern Vvardenfell.{{Ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} Maar Gan's main industry was eggmining and the closest mine to the town was the Hairat-Vassamsi Egg Mine found northwest of it. At the time, the town was often harassed by monsters coming over from the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Ghostfence]]. In response, monster hunters traveled to the town and volunteered to protect it.{{Ref|name=MWGeneric|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue M#Maar Gan|Maar Gan]] generic description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Maar Gan had a close call to destruction in {{Year|3E 424}} when a swarm of [[Lore:The Blight|blighted]] creatures nearly destroyed it. The [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] was called to defeat the monsters and were able to save the town. Councilor [[Lore:Athyn Sarethi|Athyn Sarethi]] even awarded one member named [[Lore:Percius Mercius|Percius Mercius]] with [[Lore:Malachite|glass armor]] for their efforts.{{Ref|name=MWPercius|[[Morrowind:Percius Mercius|Percius Mercius]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} When the Blight infected the kwama in Hairat-Vassamsi, an adventurer named Ravila Neryon attempted to remove it but died in the process and a memorial was dedicated to her in Maar Gan's outpost.{{Ref|name=RM|{{Cite Book|Ravila Memorial|ns_base=MW}}}}

Ever since the Redoran Council moved to [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]], Maar Gan was governed by Councilor [[Lore:Remas Morvayn|Remas Morvayn]] until his death at the hands of blight monsters. Since at least {{Year|3E 426}}, his position was filled by his wife, [[Lore:Brara Morvayn|Brara Morvayn]].{{Ref|name=RB427|{{Cite Book|Red Book of 3E 426}}}}{{Ref|name=MWBrara|[[Morrowind:Brara Morvayn|Brara Morvayn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The ranking Redoran official in town was Kinsman Alds Baro, who also worked as the local [[Lore:Blacksmithing|blacksmith]] in the outpost.{{Ref|name=MWAlds|[[Morrowind:Alds Baro|Alds Baro]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=MWSpecific|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue S#Someone in particular|Someone in particular]] general dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The exact fate of the town during the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] is unknown but all of Vvardenfell was caught in the destruction brought about by [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]]'s eruption. A Redoran councilor began a relief effort to support outlying settlements that were hit the hardest.{{Ref|name=TRY2|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}} How Maar Gan's holy shrine fared when the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] replaced the former one is also unknown as the New Temple denounces Tribunal teachings as misguided.{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}}
