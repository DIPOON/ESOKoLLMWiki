[[Lore:Hannibal Traven|Hannibal Traven]] was the Arch-Mage of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] at the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]. He was a staunch opponent of [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]] and was responsible for several poorly-implemented guild reforms.

Originally, Professor Traven was the head of the [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] Mages Guild, and supposedly ran a "clean hall".{{ref|[[Oblivion:Marc Gulitte|Marc Gulitte]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} He was promoted to the position of Arch-Mage circa {{Year|3E 431}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}} Among his controversial reforms were a tightening on admission to the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]] and an outright ban on the practice of necromancy by guild mages. Traven's divisive stance on necromancy had many strong supporters even outside the guild, although it caused a large degree of internal fracturing. Half the Council of Mages resigned, and Magister [[Lore:Ulliceta gra-Kogg|Ulliceta gra-Kogg]] of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] was forced to flee following her attempted arrest by the [[Lore:Knights of the Lamp|Knights of the Lamp]] for practicing necromancy. Traven would go on to publish [[Lore:The Black Arts On Trial|''The Black Arts On Trial'']], to document the debate between gra-Kogg and Magister {{Lore Link|Voth Karlyss}} of [[Lore:Corinth|Corinth]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, coinciding with Traven's policy change, [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] returned to the affairs of mortals. With necromancers now shunned from the Mages Guild, many flocked to join the [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]]. Mannimarco's followers were soon openly attacking the guild, going so far as to burn down the [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] guildhall. As Traven struggled to control the Council of Mages, he turned to the [[Lore:Hero#Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] for help in investigating and delaying Mannimarco's plans. Traven had the Hero secure both the [[Lore:Necromancer's Amulet|Necromancer's Amulet]] and the [[Lore:Bloodworm Helm|Bloodworm Helm]], as well as a unique colossal black soul gem the necromancers had created. In a final effort to save the guild, Traven sacrificed himself by trapping his [[Lore:Souls|soul]] within the colossal gem to allow the Hero to confront Mannimarco. With the filled gem, the Hero was shielded from Mannimarco's powerful magic and was able to defeat him in [[Lore:Echo Cave|Echo Cave]]. Traven's final act as Arch-Mage was to appoint the Hero as his successor.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Although Traven's sacrifice saved the Mages Guild from destruction at the hands of the Order of the Black Worm, the institute was not able to survive the strong anti-[[Lore:Magic|magic]] sentiment that arose in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]]. The [[Lore:Synod|Synod]] and the [[Lore:College of Whispers|College of Whispers]] formed following the guild's dissolution.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}} Before his defeat, Mannimarco revealed that he had planned to reanimate Traven as a [[Lore:Worm Thrall|Worm Thrall]] for study.{{ref|name=OB}}<noinclude>
