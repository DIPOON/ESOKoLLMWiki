[[Lore:Pelagiad|Pelagiad]] was a small settlement in the [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] region of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], on the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]].

In {{Year|2E 582}}, a Pelagiad Garrison was maintained by the {{Lore Link|Ebonheart Pact}},{{ref|{{Cite Book|Letter to Rana|ns_base=Online}}}} although no town or fort existed by that name in {{Lore Link|Vvardenfell}} at the time.{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} The opulent Amaya Lake Lodge was built upon the shores of Lake Amaya during an earlier era, near the future site of Pelagiad.{{Ref|name=ALL|[[ON:Amaya Lake Lodge|Amaya Lake Lodge]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]]}}{{ref|name=eso|Events of [[Online:Morrowind|Elder Scrolls Online: Morrowind]]}} After Vvardenfell was opened by the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] for settlement in {{Year|3E 414}}, Fort Pelagiad was built by the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] and [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] to guard the roads between [[Lore:Vivec (city)|Vivec]], [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]] and [[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]]. Legion veterans began to build farms around the fort, and several shops were set up as the village grew. The town was officially chartered, and noted for its Western architecture resembling that of a [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] village.{{ref|Dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} By {{Year|3E 427}}, the settlement boasted a tavern, tradehouse, blacksmith and [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]] services.{{ref|Events of Morrowind}} Following the destruction caused during the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] of {{Year|4E 5}},{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} Pelagiad's fate is uncertain.<noinclude>
