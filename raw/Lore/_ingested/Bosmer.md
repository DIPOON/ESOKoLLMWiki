The [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] (formerly the '''Boiche'''){{ref|name=AU4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}}{{ref|name=TWEDF|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves|ns_base=DF}}}} are the [[Lore:Elf|Elven]] clan-folk of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], a forested province in southwestern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}} In the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], they are often referred to as '''Wood Elves''', but they typically call themselves Bosmer{{ref|name=PGE1P|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Prologue}}}} and the '''Tree-Sap people'''.{{ref|name=MWdialogue|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue B|Bosmer]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Bosmer rejected the stiff, formal traditions of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] high culture, preferring a more romantic, simple existence in harmony with the land. They are relatively nimble and quick in body compared to their more "civilized" [[Lore:Altmer|Altmeri]] cousins (who often look down upon the Bosmer as unruly and naive). Their agility makes them well-suited as scouts and thieves.{{ref|name=VAS|{{Cite Book|Valenwood: A Study}}}} However, they are also a quick-witted folk, and many pursue successful careers in scholarly pursuits or trading.{{ref|name=ASGTN|{{Cite Book|A Scholar's Guide to Nymphs}}}}{{ref|name=ADIF1|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 1}}}}{{ref|name=TBG|{{Cite Book|The Buying Game}}}}

Bosmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.{{ref|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4|Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4]]}}{{ref|[[Lore:The Real Barenziah, v 2|The Real Barenziah, v 2]]}} Though they are considered less influential than some of their Elven brethren, the Bosmer are also relatively prolific, with 12 children in a family considered average.{{ref|name=ONEVE|[[Online:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Emperor's Secret|The Emperor's Secret]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} As a result, they outnumber all other [[Lore:Mer|mer]] on Tamriel.{{ref|name=RM3TWE|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 3: Wood Elf Style}}}} 

The best archers in all of Tamriel, the Bosmer snatch and release arrows in one continuous motion; they are even rumored to have invented the bow.{{ref|name=TGROM|{{Cite Book|The Gold Ribbon of Merit}}}}{{ref|name=TRG|{{Cite Book|The Rear Guard}}}}{{ref|name=TML|{{Cite Book|The Marksmanship Lesson}}}}{{ref|name=ADIF3|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 3}}}} They have many natural and unique abilities; notably, they can command simple-minded creatures{{ref|name=RacialsSkyrim}}{{ref|name=RacialsOblivion}}{{ref|name=MorrowindRacials}} and have a nearly chameleon-like ability to hide in forested areas,{{ref|name=PGE1Ald|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1W|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}} as well as natural resistance to poison and disease.{{ref|name=RacialsESO|[[Online:Wood Elf|Wood Elf passive abilities]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RacialsSkyrim|[[Skyrim:Wood Elf|Wood Elf]] traits in [[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}}{{ref|name=RacialsOblivion|[[Oblivion:Wood Elf|Wood Elf traits]] in [[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=MorrowindRacials|[[Morrowind:Wood Elf|Wood Elf ability and power]] in [[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]}} '''Nature's Grace''' grants Bosmer greater affinity with [[Lore:Restoration|healing magics]] and the capability to recover from harm quickly.{{ref|name=ShadowkeyRacials|[[Shadowkey:Wood Elf|Wood Elf perks]] in [[SK:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]}} Many in the forests of Valenwood follow the tenets of the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]]. These "Green Pact Bosmer" are religiously carnivorous and cannibalistic, and do not harm the vegetation of Valenwood, though they are not averse to using wooden or plant-derived products created by others.{{ref|name=ADIF1}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=GPBO|{{Cite Book|Green Pact Bosmer: Observations}}}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

==Appearance==
As elves of the forest, Bosmeri appearance is said to match the life of Valenwood.{{ref|name=BosAppArena|[[Arena:Wood Elf|Wood Elf]] racial description in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Bosmer are known for their slight stature,{{ref|name=VAS}}{{ref|name=BAppGeneral|Appearances of Bosmer in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'', ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]'', ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]'' and [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CAOV}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Memory Book}}}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} though their height appears to be sexually dimorphic, as women are often taller than men.{{ref|name=BIESO|Appearance of Bosmer in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} The eyes of Bosmer have star-like pupils{{ref|name=BIESO}} and they're known to file their teeth to sharp points.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband C#Corundum Tooth File|Corundum Tooth File]] item description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Bosmer often sport antlers, which are typically glued on, although some are grown with [[Lore:Magic|magic]].{{ref|name=ESOAoCH|[[Online:Contraband A#Assortment of Cosmetic Horns|Assortment of Cosmetic Horns]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PHatHMC|{{Cite Book|Prince Hew and the Haj Mota Chariot}}}}{{ref|name=LEoERPZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=General}}}}

==History==
===Origin===
:''The Boiche Elves were of the Earth Bones who most hearkened to Jephre and his greensongs. They did not build a Tower, they grew it, a great graht-oak whose roots sprang from a Perchance Acorn. And this was their Stone. And because the Acorn might perchance have been elsewhere, thus was Green-Sap manifold and several. And each could walk.'' — [[Lore:Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree]]
[[File:ON-place-Heart of Valenwood.jpg|thumb|right|The Heart of Valenwood]]

The earliest days of the Bosmer are lost in legend. Some speak of the [[Lore:Ooze|Ooze]], the shape-shifting formlessness from which they came before the Green Pact with Y'ffre. The forest was flourishing and primordial still, making shapeshifting a necessity.{{ref|name=IndinaelDia|[[ON:Spinner Indinael|Spinner Indinael]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TOAF|{{Cite Book|The Ooze: A Fable}}}} More obscure legends also suggest some dissented to the Green Pact, with some Bosmer wishing to keep their shape-shifting abilities. According to legend, Y'ffre turned the Ooze into a kind of purgatory state for these individuals, where they would be stricken from Y'ffre's story upon death and unable to interact with any world or afterlife. Eventually, the dissenters were overcome by the other Bosmer faithful to Y'ffre. These "Oathbreakers" were then buried in [[Lore:Ouze|Ouze]] so that they could be imprisoned and protected until the time of renewal.{{ref|name=IndinaelDia}}{{ref|name=OOO|{{Cite Book|Oathbreakers of Ouze}}}} Some evaded capture and became known as [[Lore:Changeling|Changelings]].{{ref|name=OOO}}{{ref|name=Selene|[[ON:Selene|Selene]]'s appearance in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Khajiiti myth includes the Bosmer in their origins. [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] is said to have taken some of [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]]'s favorite children, the shapeless forest people, and turned them into Khajiit. Y'ffer (the Khajiiti spelling of Y'ffre) learned how Azurah did so and told Nirni, which made her angry. Nirni separated her remaining children from those Azurah had changed, and allowed Y'ffer to change them so that they would always be mer and never beasts, and Y'ffer named them the Bosmer.{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}
[[File:LO-map-Valenwood (Oblivion Codex).jpg|left|thumb|[[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], the land of the Bosmer]]

[[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] myth holds the Bosmer descended from the first elves to arrive in Valenwood, long before the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=VAS}} The forests of Valenwood, or [[Lore:The Green|the Green]] (though this term can mean more than just Valenwood), were so dense and inhabited by beasts that they could not hope to tame it, so they had to fight and adapt to become predators instead of prey.{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}} This adaptation led to the creation of the Bosmer.{{ref|name=VAS}}{{ref|name=PGE3V}}{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} It is said that the city of [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] is named after the first King of the Bosmer.{{Ref|name=ARArenthia|[[Arena:Arenthia|Arenthia]] description in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

There's some suggestion that early Bosmer were known for intermarrying with humans.{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} Another legend is that of the Boiche Elves, made from the [[Lore:Earthbones|Earthbones]] that followed Jephre, grew the first great [[Lore:Graht-oak|Graht-oak]] out of the Perchance Acorn, which would become [[Lore:The Towers#Green-Sap|Tower of Green-Sap]]. Due to the nature of the Acorn being, perchance, elsewhere, many different Green-Saps sprung in many different forms, forming the walking Graht-Oak forests of the Valenwood. Each one told its own stories of the Green, the forest home of the "Boiche-become-Bosmer".{{ref|name=AU4TET}} 

The establishment of the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] by the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] in the Middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] brought increased trade to the Bosmer, and cities like [[Lore:Woodhearth|Woodhearth]], [[Lore:Haven|Haven]], and [[Lore:Silvenar|Silvenar]] flourished.{{ref|name=ACOV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} The ancient Ayleid site of [[Lore:Hectahame|Hectahame]] is home to the [[Lore:Heart of Valenwood|Heart of Valenwood]], a magnificent tree of immense power which stands as the physical symbol of the soul of Valenwood. It's unclear exactly when Ayleids first started migrating to Valenwood, but it was around that time when a [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancer]] twisted the power of the Heart and used it to raise an undead army, forcing the Ayleids to seal off Hectahame, along with the Heart, in order to contain the horde.{{ref|name=HOV|{{Cite Book|Heart of Valenwood (book)}}}} Ayleids and Bosmer during these early days also had to fend off numerous savage attacks by the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]. They completely destroyed both Haven and Woodhearth, and sacked Elden Root.{{ref|name=ACOV}}

===The First Era===
Eventually, the Bosmer would be united under the leadership of [[Lore:Eplear|King Eplear]], whose founding of the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] heralded the start of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3V}} The [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] circa {{Year|1E 243}} (remembered by the Altmer as the "White-Gold Catastrophe") triggered the "[[Lore:Ayleid Diaspora|Ayleid Diaspora]]", which saw many Ayleids flee to Valenwood for safety. These migrants agreed to adopt aspects of the Green Pact and to refrain from harming the forest.{{ref|name=AU4TET}}{{ref|name=ASIV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Survivals in Valenwood}}}}

An infamous dissenter to this arrangement was the Ayleid sorcerer [[Lore:Anumaril|Anumaril]], who used powerful magic to convert the Perchance Acorn of the [[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]] Graht-Oak into a Definite Acorn, which prevented Elden Root from walking and gave a Green-Sap a single, definite form. He attempted to convert this Green-Sap into a new, singular [[Lore:White Gold Tower|White-Gold]], but due to the Perchance nature of the Bosmeri Tower, he could neither succeed or fail.{{ref|name=AU4TET}} Though they found sanctuary in Valenwood, the Ayleids who migrated there seemed to fade from history. For reasons still debated by scholars, they apparently had little lasting impact on Bosmeri culture beyond imparting some stonework and masonry techniques.{{ref|name=ASIV}} The Bosmer made a formal trade treaty with the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] in {{Year|1E 340}}, though it would only last for several decades before breaking down into on-and-off border skirmishes with [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]] for most of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3V}}

The earliest known Wild Hunt occurred in {{Year|1E 369}}, which ended with the death of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] High King [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]].{{ref|name=PGE3V}}{{ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}} This assassination can be viewed as an act of self-defense, though perhaps proactive, as Borgas had been journeying to [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] to rally support for a joint war against Valenwood. Another Wild Hunt would not occur again until the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]],{{ref|name=PGE3V}} though whether one or more occurred then is not clear. After the Bosmer were devastated by the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] in {{Year|1E 2714}}, they were absorbed by the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]. The Empire, in their usual fashion, divided power in Valenwood among several districts to ensure they would not unite against Imperial rule.{{ref|name=PGE3V}}

In {{Year|1E 2920}}, the last year of the First Era, on the 19th of Sun's Dawn, [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] destroyed the town of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] in [[Lore:Grahtwood|Grahtwood]] (eastern Valenwood). This prompted [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], the living wizard-mystic god of the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], to negotiate an agreement with several Daedric Princes to forestall further attacks, though the details of this agreement are still unknown.{{ref|name=2920L|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}

===The Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 406}}, a salacious scandal rocked Valenwood: the sleepy trading post of [[Lore:Cormount|Cormount]] of Grahtwood was likely home to numerous potential heirs of the Camoran Dynasty, as Camoran rulers had evidently been journeying to the secluded town for untold years to engage in extramarital relations. Cormount's population nearly doubled in a week, and weddings became a daily occurrence for months, as many Bosmer sought to sire children with a noble lineage despite the ignoble circumstances. The news even brought many Bosmer who had never lived in Valenwood back to their spiritual home. These migrants, who largely came from Cyrodiil and thus did not follow the Green Pact, were shocking to the established Bosmer of Grahtwood, and tensions brewed for years as a result. Finally, in {{Year|2E 420}}, the migrants' village, [[Lore:New Joy|New Joy]], was burned to the ground for provocations that have been lost to history. The incident eventually inspired the formation of the Blacksap Movement in {{Year|2E 489}}, a Cormount-based cultural organization which hoped to formalize the Bosmeri approach to the Green Pact and thereby prevent any further tragic misunderstandings.{{ref|name=TROC|{{Cite Book|The Rise of Cormount}}}} With the fall of the Empire in {{Year|2E 430}}, the regional powers went about warring among themselves, and [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] pirates were able to get a foothold along the coast which they used to harass the Altmer of [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]].{{ref|name=PGE3V}}
[[File:ON-icon-Aldmeri Dominion.png|thumb|right|The symbol of the Aldmeri Dominion]]
During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the Bosmer became part of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], along with their allies the Altmer and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. The alliance was rocky, though the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] Committee on Alliance Relations encouraged tolerance of the Bosmer's cultural eccentricities to help keep the Dominion together.{{ref|name=TH|{{Cite Book|Thalmor Handbill}}}}{{ref|name=ASIV}} [[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]] was made the capital of the Dominion, and drew many new migrants as a result (this also became the impetus for plans to establish a gladiatorial arena in the city).{{ref|name=WMTM|{{Cite Book|Want More than Middens?|ns_base=Online}}}} Many Bosmer saw the Dominion as their only chance at survival.{{ref|name=LTC|{{Cite Book|Letter to Calahawn|ns_base=Online}}}} However, many others remained resentful of the alliance, believing the Dominion had "polluted" Valenwood.{{ref|name=WCN|{{Cite Book|What Comes Next|ns_base=Online}}}} Resentment may have also festered due to the "Massacre at Cormount", wherein the Grahtwood city was apparently razed by Dominion soldiers utilizing particularly offensive tactics.{{ref|name=TMAC|{{Cite Book|The Massacre at Cormount}}}}

In {{Year|2E 830}}, to secure their border from attack, the Altmer, using an old treaty and encroachment from Colovia for justification, invaded Valenwood and reestablished the Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=PGE3V}}{{ref|name=PGE3Summer|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset Isle}}}} Details on the circumstances leading up to this are disputed,{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}} but the reformed Thalmor government apparently brought peace to Valenwood until the rise of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=PGE3V}} During the [[Lore:War of Rihad|War of Rihad]] in {{Year|2E 862}}, Colovian armies employed the Bosmer as archers, though they proved to be prone to desertion.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}

===The Third Era===
The Camoran monarchy, maintained by the [[Lore:Third Empire|Septim Empire]] as a "figurehead", ruled over 250 years of peace in Valenwood. However, the Bosmer became increasingly alienated by Imperial neglect.{{ref|name=PGE3V}} In {{Year|3E 249}}, a pretender to the throne, the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]], led an army of undead and mercenaries which devastated Valenwood, and most of Tamriel's western coast, until he was finally defeated in {{Year|3E 267}}.{{ref|name=PGE3V}}{{ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}} Since then, the Bosmer have become more isolationist, traditionalist, and distrusting of central authorities, and this political weakness has led to significant losses of territory to their neighbors.{{ref|name=PGE3V}}

The Bosmer would suffer heavily during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] (3E 389 - 3E 399), losing the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]] against the Khajiit and the [[Lore:War of the Blue Divide|War of the Blue Divide]] against the Altmer. Both saw large territorial concessions and no intervention by the Empire.{{Ref|name=ADIF7|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 7}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3SB|{{Cite book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Sugar and Blood}}}} At the end of the Third Era, rumors surfaced that a prophet called the Precursor had emerged among the Bosmer promising new gifts from Y'ffre to reward their devotion.{{ref|name=PGE3V}}

===The Fourth Era===
Following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the Summerset Isles declared independence from the Empire. It's alleged that Thalmor agents, who had formed close ties to certain Bosmeri factions even before the Oblivion Crisis, conspired to overthrow Valenwood's government. In {{Year|4E 29}}, they succeeded, quickly overcoming Imperial resistance and reforming the Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite book|The Great War (book)}}}}

==Culture==
===Art and Architecture===
[[File:BL-quest-Pool of Despair 04.jpg|thumb|left|A Bosmeri hunting camp in Cyrodiil (Blades)]][[File:ON-concept-Skull decorations.png|thumb|right|Depictions of Bosmeri skull and bone decorations]]
The Green Pact has had an enormous impact on Bosmeri artwork, crafts and clothing. Their walls are often decorated with skulls and bones.{{ref|name=CWS|{{Cite Book|Conversation with Shamunur}}}} It forces their architects to take great care in their work, apparently strengthening their craftsmanship. Inside tree hollows and residences, hides are stretched out over frames of bones to form partitions.

Traditional timber clanhouses can be found at sites scattered along the coast, where imported lumber is easy to find and cheap to purchase, and through the Valenwood interior, connected only by undeveloped foot trails.{{ref|name=mwvalenwoodtopic|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue V|Valenwood]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} More of these types of buildings can be found on the forest floor of cities like Elden Root or Silvenar, typically to house foreigners like the Altmer, but Bosmer cities in the Green are more well-known for the many homes scattered throughout the canopy of the trees.{{ref|name=AA}}

Many "citified tree-folk" favor a life among the branches, and have woven them together to form limbed pathways without harming the trees. Trails of thick, living vines anchor dozens of platforms that carry goods and people among the graht-oaks. These platforms are hoisted by strong, often foreign laborers. Bone, resin, and sinew are employed by the Bosmer in a plethora of ways, including bridge design. A secondary market in such scraps allows the tree-dwellers to recycle their animal waste by simply tossing it to the ground below, where it is scavenged and reworked into a variety of items.{{ref|name=AA}} What is not scavenged is absorbed by the roots of the trees and used to keep the settlement healthy. These cities are grown by Bosmer [[Lore:Greenspeakers|Greenspeakers]], who use rituals of speech or song to grow entire villages in just a few days.{{ref|name=ONGloo|[[Online:Glooredel|Glooredel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LMAGwaering|{{Cite Book|Y'ffre's Beckoning}}}} This magic can also be used to fashion wooden items, presumably without violating the Green Pact.{{ref|name=ContW|[[ON:Contraband W#Warped Oaken Flatware|Warped Oaken Flatware]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Below ground, the trees share a common root system which delivers hot sap into each hut to keep them warm.{{ref|name=ONGloo}}

Like the Nords, the Bosmer rely heavily on stylized, often interlocking natural motifs in their architecture, crafts, and clothing. However, while the Nords emphasize animals, Bosmeri designs are mainly floral.{{ref|name=RM4TN|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 4: Nord Style}}}} Since the Bosmer believe that a species' form is a sacred gift from Y'ffre, each species is depicted by a particular, idealized motif which represents the ur-form it was given by Y'ffre. Thus, while there a great plethora of designs in Bosmeri artwork, there is very little room for variation in these designs. Seemingly inconsequential details are rigidly scrutinized, and those which deviate from the norm are looked down upon as plain "wrong".{{ref|name=RM3TWE}}

Border settlements often follow the architectural designs of other cultures.{{fact}}

===Clothing and Fashion===
The Bosmer have a sense of fashion that may seem strange to some of Tamriel's other inhabitants. For example, Wood Elves in the Second Era thought that shawls made of blue bunting feathers were the height of fashion, while people from most other cultures perceived them as tacky.{{ref|[[ON:Contraband F#Feathery Finery Shawl|Feathery Finery Shawl]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Humor also plays a role in Wood Elf fashion, as some clothing items are simply worn as a joke.{{ref|name=ZVKMNHat|[[ON:Z'en Vengeance Kiss-Me-Not Cap|Z'en Vengeance Kiss-Me-Not Cap]] hat description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Pod-Singers wear clothes made entirely out of animal leather and quills.{{ref|[[ON:Deepwoods Pod-Singer Tunic|Deepwoods Pod-Singer Tunic]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} On the whole, Wood Elves prefer clothing that allows their skin to breathe, and think covering oneself from head-to-toe doesn't make a whole lot of sense. Leather is a common component in Bosmer clothing: doeskin, chamois,{{ref|name=ESODoeskin|[[ON:Doeskin-and-Chamois Woods Wear|Doeskin-and-Chamois Woods Wear]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} crocodile skin&mdash;if it has hide, it's free game for leather.{{ref|[[ON:Ranger's Crocskin Overkilt|Ranger's Crocskin Overkilt]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Their gravitation towards leather doesn't keep them away from fabric; wool is also a component in Bosmeri stitchwork.{{ref|name=ESOSpinnerCos|[[ON:Valenwood Spinner|Valenwood Spinner]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Felt is also used in clothing, with the Silvenar's personal guard being known to wear unique felt caps.{{ref|name=ESOFeltHat|[[ON:Silvenari Felt Bycoket|Silvenari Felt Bycoket]] hat description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
Beading, turquoise, sea-beads, and even iridescent deep-forest beetle shells are common accents in Bosmer fashion, giving clothing extra flair.{{ref|[[ON:Treethane Ceremonial Dress|Treethane Ceremonial Dress]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESODoeskin}}{{ref|[[ON:Treethane's Mosaic Jerkin|Treethane's Mosaic Jerkin]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Translucent cloths, and nothing more, are traditionally worn during ritual dances.{{ref|name=TransparentShawl|[[ON:Contraband W# Wood Elf Dance Shawl|Wood Elf Dance Shawl]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Cultural mixing between Bosmer and Khajiit has had an effect on fashion as well, such as unique hairstyles.{{ref|name=LEBraid|[[ON:Long Elven Braid|Long Elven Braid]] hair style description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} A messy and dyed hairstyle popular in the Second Era was named after the Bosmeri drink, Rotmeth.{{ref|name=RHBed|[[ON:Rotmeth Hangover Bed-Head|Rotmeth Hangover Bed-Head]] hair style description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Weaponry and Armor===
Due to the proscriptions of the Green Pact, forges are rare in Valenwood, and metal weapons have never been widespread among the Bosmer. They typically utilize bone clubs, or else axes and spears with blades of stone or obsidian. Their composite horn bows are arguably the finest in Tamriel. In coastal towns, imported metal weapons are more common, and Altmeri tutelage in these areas has benefited many Bosmeri swordsmen. However, Altmer who are schooled in "Proper Conflict", as they call their system of swordsmanship, typically refuse to adapt their techniques to the needs of their shorter cousins, and Bosmer students are typically less attentive to the finer philosophical points of the lessons, thus the experience has often proven to be mutually exasperating.{{ref|name=CAOV|{{Cite Book|Common Arms of Valenwood}}}}

By the age of fourteen, Bosmer youth are typically proficient enough with the bow to accompany hunting parties.{{ref|name=CAOV}} Long-distance archers are known as [[Lore:Jaqspurs|Jaqspurs]].{{ref|name=CAOV}}{{ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}} It is their style of draw, "snatching and releasing in one continuous motion", which has made the Bosmer famous as archers. Their traditional bows are crafted from horn and sinew. Strings are also made of sinew; Khajiiti gut is said to work best, and is thus prized among Valenwood archers. Bosmeri arrows are carved from bone, and fletched with the feathers of various bird species. They believe the source of bone used for an arrow influences its characteristics: [[Lore:Mammoth|Mammoth]] bone arrows are thought to knock down a target, bird arrows fly faster and more accurately, and the [[Lore:Senche-Tiger|Senche-tiger]] brings extra damage. Much to the amusement of the Bosmer, outsiders have not been able to replicate these effects.{{ref|name=CAOV}}

Beetle-chitin is often used in armor and weapons, as it as protective as iron at a much lighter weight. Likewise, beetle-hide is incredibly slick, making it difficult for a weapon to find purchase on armor made from it. It also isn't unheard of for these materials to be mixed with iron and steel.{{ref|name=SoZB|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 77: Stags of Z'en Style}}}}
Bone and antlers are also used to accent clothing and armor.{{ref|name=ESOJaqspurCos|[[ON:Jaqspur|Jaqspur]] costume description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOSpinnerCos}}

[[Lore:Throwing Weapons|Darts]] of Judgment are very rare and expensive Daedric projectiles fashioned by the Bosmer of Valenwood.{{Ref|name=ATJ|[[Morrowind:Aengoth the Jeweler|Aengoth the Jeweler]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

==Society==
The elves of Valenwood have always stood ready to defend themselves against aggression, and have done so on many occasions.{{ref|name=MUT|{{Cite Book|Mixed Unit Tactics}}}} They often rely on their forest-coupling skills to make use of guerrilla tactics. The title of their most famous poem, the Meh Ayleidion, means "The One Thousand Benefits of Hiding".{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}} However, the Bosmer seem to be the most pacifistic of all the modern races on Tamriel, as they have never instigated war with other nations (leading some more militant cultures to criticize them as cowards),{{ref|name=FWOB|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Fav'te)}}}} though they have been accused of needlessly escalating [[Lore:Five Year War|some conflicts]]. They have never found a tried and true ally in any other Tamrielic nation. Mistrust has been engendered by the ritual cannibalism they practice, and it's often difficult for them to adjust their lifestyles to the expectations of other races and cultures. Nevertheless, the Bosmer of Valenwood are heavily dependent on good trade relations with others, particularly for timber.{{ref|name=POT}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=ADIF2|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 2}}}} Their own resources for trade include hides, river pearls, and finger-bone charms made from the magically-charged hands of their dead wizards. When they are at odds with Colovia or other trading allies, they are said to rely on piracy or banditry to get what they need.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}} 
[[File:BT-concept-Wood Elf Deadeye.png|thumb|right|Bosmer victim of necromancy]]
The Bosmer greatly value diversity. Other races are ''generally'' welcomed in most of Valenwood, though most foreigners stay in large cities.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=PGE3V}}{{ref|name=AMGTV|{{Cite Book|A Merchant's Guide to Valenwood}}}} Travelers are encouraged to take care when imbibing drinks of fermented milk which are popular among the Bosmer, as non-Bosmer may have adverse reactions to them.{{ref|name=ADIF|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire}}}}{{ref|name=FTNOACC|{{Cite Book|From the notes of Ancemion, Ceremony Coordinator}}}} Their respect for life's many facets is inherent in many of their old sayings, like "One mans miracle is another mans accident".{{ref|name=AOOGAW|{{Cite Book|An Overview of Gods And Worship}}}} Conversely, their general animosity towards necromancy is said to be extraordinary,{{ref|name=CP|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation}}}} though this animosity for necromancy may be exaggerated, as many individuals show little concern over the reanimation of the dead.{{ref|name=BOR|{{Cite Book|Bone Orchard Research}}}} They have attempted to accommodate other races in Valenwood throughout history, though many remained wary of outlander encroachments into their home.{{ref|name=OBRTTD|{{Cite Book|One Bosmer's Response to the Dominion|ns_base=Online}}}} To this day, they have little more than foot paths connecting their tiny settlements in Valenwood's sparsely populated forests.{{ref|name=POT}}{{ref|name=FTJOAHES|{{Cite Book|From the journal of Aretille, High Elven scholar}}}} 

===Life in Valenwood===
Though the Bosmer first united under the Camoran Dynasty, this form of governance has been called a "loose hegemony".{{ref|name=TWEOV|{{Cite Book|The Wood Elves of Valenwood}}}} There are in fact many leaders throughout Valenwood who wield varying degrees of authority, such as the treethanes.{{ref|name=TWEOV}}{{ref|name=DDTTH|{{Cite Book|Diplomacy during the Handfasting}}}} Their territory has sometimes been referred to as the "Valenwood Nations",{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}} perhaps because of the autonomy practiced by various leaders.{{ref|name=TWEOV}} At the clan-level, Bosmeri society is matrilineal.{{ref|name=TWEOV}} Greenspeakers, mages who dedicate their lives to serving the Green, use their spells to guide and enhance the growth of trees and plants, and in so doing, create furniture and housing for their people.{{ref|name=ESODorinlas|[[ON:Dorinlas|Dorinlas]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Bosmer have adapted to life in the Green in many ingenious ways. For instance, in the heat and humidity of Valenwood, popular Altmeri ink refuses to dry. It is still prized, though, as it is perfect for writing temporary messages on certain types of porous stone found in the Green. But to write words permanently, the Bosmer uses a type of ink that can dry even if a parchment is submerged in water. Ink which glows faintly in the dark, perfect for writing in the shadows of the forest, is also prevalent. Writing on a leaf or a flat stone is common in Valenwood, and often preferable in a place where quality parchment is rare and even paper is uncommon.{{ref|name=TC7|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Calligraphy, Chapter VII}}}} Green Pact typically does not allow the use of paper, which makes vellum made of animal hides more common in Valenwood.{{ref|name=Zuzik|[[ON:Zuzik|Zuzik]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They've also found means to both deter and eradicate [[Lore:Hoarvor|Hoarvor]] infestations.{{ref|name=HF|{{Cite Book|Healer's Fieldbook}}}} Though they refrain from smoking plants, many enjoy smoking bugs from bone pipes.{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=RM3TWE}} Finding light without resorting to the danger of fire is an issue on the perpetually shadowy forest floor, but as the moons rise, additional light is provided by luminous lichen, molds, and fungal growths at the base of the oaks. Higher up, nocturnal flowers feed from the graht-oaks, attracting [[Lore:Torchbug|Torchbugs]], whose hives light the branch platforms.{{ref|name=AA|{{Cite Book|Arboreal Architecture}}}}
[[File:LO-race-Imga.jpg|thumb|right|A sketch of an Imga, the Great Apes of Valenwood]]
The Bosmer share Valenwood with the [[Lore:Imga|Imga]] and so-called "Wood [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]]", along with the many beasts of the forest, including [[Lore:Centaur|Centaurs]].{{ref|name=PGE1Ald}}{{ref|name=CAEWO1|{{Cite Book|Civility and Etiquette: Wood Orcs I}}}} They have a long history of welcoming those who seek refuge within Valenwood.{{ref|name=VAS}} They accepted slaves fleeing the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], and then Ayleids fleeing the slaves, without showing favorites. Around the time of the Three Banners War, they also freely welcomed [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] refugees who had left their homeland in protest over the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]].{{ref|name=ARD|{{Cite Book|Argonian Refugee's Diary}}}} Many foreigners visit [[Lore:Falinesti|Falinesti]] at some point, the marvelous traveling city of the Bosmer (though at last report, the city had mysteriously rooted itself).{{ref|name=PGE3V}} Cultural loyalties have tended to divide the Bosmer, such as the rivalry between the [[Lore:Blacksap|Blacksap]] and the Camorans, and the schism between followers of the [[#The Green Pact|Green Pact]] and "salad-eaters" who have no qualms about breaking the tenets of the Pact.{{ref|name=OBRTTD}}

===Laws and Traditions===
The Rite of Theft is a cherished Bosmeri custom, though one which outsiders have trouble appreciating.{{ref|name=TGPATD|{{Cite Book|The Green Pact and the Dominion}}}} The Bosmer steal from each other, and upon return of the stolen item, they demand a boon commensurate to the item's worth.{{ref|name=HOTS|{{Cite Book|Heroes of the Sanctuary}}}} In ancient times, the Bosmer [[Lore:Vanirion the Thief|Vanirion the Thief]] was renowned for demanding over two hundred payments after invoking the Rite.{{ref|name=VOTGPB}} In more modern times, the Bosmer of Greenshade are particularly infamous for their thievery.{{ref|name=AMGTV}} Occasionally, some Bosmer have been known to observe the Rite of Theft even from beyond the grave, such was the case with the Blackroot Clan when their chief's bow was stolen. {{ref|name=ESORoT|[[Online:Right of Theft|Right of Theft]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Outsiders who understand the Rite of Theft may use it to their own advantage as well. Three Altmer, the so-called "Heroes of the Sanctuary", once used the Rite of Theft to secure a safe port of Summerset ships. They successfully stole from various treethanes while keeping their own treasure secure, earning the right to build their own settlement, known as [[Lore:Seaside Sanctuary|Seaside Sanctuary]], on the shores of Valenwood.{{ref|name=HOTS}}

Often when making contracts with the tribes of Valenwood, one will be expected to put up some sort of collateral to ensure equal treatment between unequal partners, such as when a mundane individual is dealing with a powerful mage. In traditional Bosmeri villages, this involves the more powerful party giving the weaker a loved one as a 'pact-hostage'. Should the contracted party complete their task, the pact-hostage will be returned. If they fail to uphold their end of the contract, the pact-hostage will be killed, cooked, and served up to them as Unthrappa, or Atonement. Eating the Unthrappa represents eating one's failure.{{ref|name=ESORanneth|[[Online:Treethane Ranneth|Treethane Ranneth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Nearly everywhere outside the cities of Valenwood, Bosmeri tribes in the wilds of Valenwood still practice the tradition of the "Mourning War". When a tribe member is slain, they are symbolically replaced via a hostage-taking raid on a neighboring tribe. If the deceased was an especially powerful or prestigious member of the tribe, multiple captives may be taken to replace them. The captive or captives undergo a period of physical torture, supposedly to test their worthiness, and then are joyously welcomed into the clan. Traditionally, the victim was given the deceased tribe member's position, possessions, and family, though this practice may be rarely honored in modern ages.{{ref|name=WCOTTB|{{Cite Book|War Customs of the Tribal Bosmer}}}} Mourning wars are an ancient tradition; scholarly references to them date back to the First Era,{{ref|name=VOTGPB|{{Cite Book|Visions of the Green Pact Bosmer}}}} though when they actually started is not known.

The Rite of Propagation is an ancient rite invoked by villages to prevent stagnation against the Green.{{ref|name=SBVOTV|[[ON:Spinner Belwelas|Spinner Belwelas]]' dialogue during [[ON:The Voice of the Village|The Voice of the Village]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=VOTV|Events of [[ON:The Voice of the Village|The Voice of the Village]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

'''Y'ffre''' is the '''God of Song and Forest''' and is the chief god of the Bosmer people. They believe that Y'ffre's first tale was that of the world, giving shape to the primordial [[Lore:Ooze|Ooze]]. Only after did the second tale come, that of the Bosmer people, and with it the Green Pact.{{ref|name=TOAF}}{{ref|name=VOFTWE}}
Ehlnofey, or "Earthbones," were created from Y'ffre's corporeal form to establish safety and the laws of nature. These laws took the shape of stories, and there is great debate between tribes competing to interpret Y'ffre's ways and knowledge of the times of chaos, before Y'ffre arrived on the mortal plane. This way, they became a vital part of Bosmeri religion to such an extent that the [[Lore:Spinner|Spinners]], priests of Y'ffre, are considered to hold real power in Valenwood, above treethanes and kings.{{ref|name=GTEV|{{Cite Book|EGT|Valenwood}}}}

====[[Lore:Ius|Ius]]====
'''Ius the Extremely Agitated, God of Animals''' is represented by statues throughout Valenwood and has strange stories regarding his origins. He's often portrayed holding a large set of scales, but his worshippers have no idea, nor care to know why.{{ref|name=Ius|{{Cite Book|Ius, Animal God}}}}
[[File:ON-render-Selene 02.jpg|thumb|right|Selene in Bosmer form (ESO)]]
====[[Lore:Selene|Selene]]====
'''Selene''' is a powerful [[Lore:Changeling|Changeling]] and a High Priestess of [[Lore:Shagrath|Shagrath]], another goddess associated with the Green.{{ref|name=SeleneDia|[[ON:Selene|Selene]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She's supposedly a remnant of the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] and thought to be one of the earliest beings on Nirn. {{ref|name=MtCS|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Selene}}}} She rules over the [[Lore:Selene's Web|Selene's Web]] region of Valenwood, and is known by her followers as the '''Forest Mother'''{{ref|name=LCDia|[[ON:Longclaw|Longclaw]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
====[[Lore:Brackenleaf (tree)|Brackenleaf]]====
'''Brackenleaf''' is fabled to be one of the first trees of Valenwood, planted by Y'ffre during her world-shaping tale. Legends say the tree's heart holds the same power of creation used by Y'ffre.{{ref|name=ERDia|[[ON:Eringor|Eringor]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} A [[Lore:Brackenleaf (settlement)|settlement of the same name]] sprouted around the tree and so did a [[Lore:Brackenleaf (tree)#Brackenleaf's Briars|group of warriors]] dedicated to protecting Brackenleaf, the forest and the way of the hunt.{{ref|name=ERDia}}{{ref|name=GlaraDia|[[ON:Glaras|Glaras]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
====[[Lore:The Eldest|The Eldest]]====
'''The Eldest''' is an ancient [[Lore:Strangler|Strangler]] which has been in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] since creation itself. It was the lifeblood and the very essence of [[Lore:Greenshade|Greenshade]], and the state of its being reflected upon the forest around it.{{ref|name=IndaeDia|[[ON:Indaenir|Indaenir]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Blight of the Bosmer|The Blight of the Bosmer]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
}}

===Additional Figures===
Some other gods and figures while not actively venerated or worshipped do have their place in Bosmer traditions and culture. [[Lore:Mistria|Mistria]] and [[Lore:Caliron|Caliron]] are two figures who the Bosmer exclaim by,{{Ref|name=DFExclamations|[[Lore:Wood Elf|Wood Elf]] exclamations in ''[[Daggerfall:Wood Elf|Daggerfall]]''}} and the latter of the two has an [[Lore:Caliron's Curse|ailment named after them]]. The wife of Xarxes, [[Lore:Oghma|Oghma]] is mentioned in the same manner as by the Altmer, created by the god from his favorite moments in history. [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]]'s creation of time-law is said to have been interpreted by Y'ffre to manifest day, night and everything inbetween.{{Ref|name=ONGirnalin|[[ON:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They also believe that the stars are holes to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] created by [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]] and other spirits during their escape from Nirn.{{Ref|name=ONGirnalin}}

The [[Lore:Heart of Valenwood|Heart of Valenwood]] is viewed by Bosmer with both fear and admiration; although wary of the atrocities of the past, they venerate its incredible life-creating power.{{ref|name=HOV}}

==Notes==
* [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] often prefers to hire Bosmer and [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] as mercenaries to ensure they are left in peace.{{Ref|name=DialogueRaces|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue R|Races]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
* The term "[[Wikipedia:Sundering of the Elves#Nandor|Wood Elf]]" originates in the works of [[Wikipedia:J. R. R. Tolkien|J. R. R. Tolkien]]'s [[Wikipedia:Tolkien's legendarium|legendarium]].
* The word 'Bos' is Dutch for 'woods' or 'forest'.
* Early ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' concept art depicted Bosmer women as beautiful [[Wikipedia:Swan maiden|Swanmays]] with black eyes, and the men as ugly Woodland [[Wikipedia:Imp|Imps]] and Forest [[Wikipedia:Goblin|Goblins]]. The concept art showed the men were originally going to have several subtypes, those being [[Wikipedia:Satyr|Satyrs]], [[Wikipedia:Redcap|Redcaps]], Boarfolk, Tree-[[Wikipedia:Troll|trolls]], and [[Wikipedia:Bogle|Boggles]].{{Ref|name=MWConceptArt|[[:File:MW-concept-Early Bosmer.jpg|''Morrowind'' Concept Art]]}}
*{{note|nb1|^|The in-game description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' characterized the Bosmer as tall.{{ref|name=BosAppDag|[[Daggerfall:Wood Elf|Wood Elf]] racial description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}}}
