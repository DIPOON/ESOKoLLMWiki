The [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] is a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] created by the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]. It takes the appearance of a Daedric [[Lore:Staff|staff]] that is usually engraved with angry or sad gaping faces at the top. As befits the Prince of Madness, his artifact is unpredictable in its effects. The staff has the power to transform its target into a completely random creature. This can be helpful to the wielder, turning a fearsome opponent into a docile animal; similarly, it can be detrimental, transforming a relatively weak enemy into a powerful monster.{{ref|name=DFWabbajack|[[Daggerfall:Wabbajack (artifact)|Wabbajack]] item description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The staff can produce other effects, such as damaging, healing, turning to stone, or instantly killing the target.{{ref|name=SRWabbajack|[[Skyrim:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] item description in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The staff's victims that are turned into animals, and are cooked after the fact, become the meat known as Wabbajerky. The meat has created debates on whether eating it would be considered cannibalism.{{ref|name=Wabbajerky|Appearance of {{Item Link|Wabbajerky|questid=5975}} in [[Online:Greenshade Quest Items|ESO]]}} Sheogorath finds only a few worthy of Wabbajack's "absurdly sweet power".{{ref|name=ESOChaosMagic|[[Online:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in the quest [[Online:Chaos Magic|Chaos Magic]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
Sometime in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the staff came into the possession of Prince {{Lore Link|Maleel al-Akir}}, a formidable [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudan]] warrior. In death, Maleel was entombed in the necropolis of {{Lore Link|Asakala}} in the [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]] along with his generals and army. During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], the dead of Asakala rose from their graves due to the ongoing conflict in the region. In {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:Shalidor|Arch-Mage Shalidor]] and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] recovered the Wabbajack from Maleel, who guarded it fiercely even in undeath. The Vestige was then commanded to use the staff on denizens of [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] as part of Sheogorath's trials.{{ref|name=ONSM|[[Online:Simply Misplaced|Simply Misplaced]] quest events in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Sometime either before or after the start of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], a [[Lore:Hero of the Outer Watch|member]] of the [[Lore:Outer Watch|Outer Watch]] encountered the Wabbajack wielded by a [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] cultist. They are thought to have banished the staff, or decided to flee but suffered from its transformation effects.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] Wabbajacky overland encounter}}

An anonymous author wrote about how he obtained the staff sometime shortly after he ascended the throne of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] in the book ''[[Lore:Wabbajack (book)|Wabbajack]]''. In his search for the [[Lore:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]], the author was tricked into summoning Sheogorath, believing him to be [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]. After serving the prince, Sheogorath's servant gave him the Wabbajack.{{ref|name=W|{{Cite Book|Wabbajack (book)}} (book)}}

In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] summoned Sheogorath and dispatched a battlemage who had displeased the Prince. In return, the agent was given the Wabbajack from a worshipper of Sheogorath.{{ref|name=DFSheogorath|[[Daggerfall:Sheogorath's Quest|Sheogorath's Quest]] events in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Sheogorath was aided by the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] in tormenting the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] settlement of [[Lore:Border Watch|Border Watch]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The inhabitants were very superstitious, and were driven into a state of frenzy when the Prince simulated their {{Lore Link|K'sharra Prophecy}}, said to mark the end of the world. These occurrences were reported in a special edition of the Black Horse Courier newspaper entitled ''Rain of Burning Dogs!''.{{ref|name=OBSheogorath|[[Oblivion:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] quest events in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}{{ref|name=ROBD|{{Cite Book|Rain of Burning Dogs!|ns_base=OB}}}} It is unknown whether this took place before or after the Hero assumed the position of Sheogorath; regardless, the Hero received the Wabbajack from either Sheogorath or [[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]].{{ref|name=OBSheogorath}}{{ref|name=Shivering|Differing dialogue during the quest [[Oblivion:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']] if [[Shivering:The Prince of Madness|The Prince of Madness]] quest in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] is completed}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] unknowingly entered the mind of the dead [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]], tasked with convincing Sheogorath to return to the Shivering Isles. He agreed to return, but only if the Dragonborn could escape Pelagius' mind. Sheogorath decided to assist the Dragonborn by providing the Wabbajack, along with instructions on how to use it to remedy Pelagius of his insanity. Having completed several challenges and conquering the dead emperor's inner fears, the Dragonborn was able to return to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], but not before being given the Wabbajack by Sheogorath as a parting gift.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:The Mind of Madness|The Mind of Madness]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}</noinclude><noinclude>
