# Lore:Good Daedra

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Gods Trail}}[[File:ON-furnishing-Tri-Angled Truth Altar.jpg|thumb|right|Chimeri Tri-Angled Truth Altar to the Good Daedra]]</noinclude>
The Three [[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]] is the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] (and before that, [[Lore:Velothi|Velothi]]) term for three of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]]: [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]]. These Princes are worshipped by the Dunmer as their ancestors. They are contrasted with the [[Lore:Bad Daedra|Bad Daedra]], who are believed to test the Dunmer people's resolve and faith.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} To their worshippers, they were known as the '''Three Sisters''' and the '''Triangular Gate'''.{{ref|name=ADAW|{{Cite Book|A Dance Along the Webs}}}}{{Ref|name=FEE|{{Cite Book|From Exile to Exodus}}}}

Mephala gave the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] the [[Lore:Codes of Mephala|Codes of Mephala]], taught them the ways of evasion and assassination, also founded the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]]. Boethiah and Mephala together organized the structure that would end up as the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] of the Dunmer. Boethiah taught the Dunmer philosophy, magic, and [[Lore:Responsible Architecture|"Responsible" Architecture]] of their daedric shrines.{{ref|name=VF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} According to one myth, Boethiah asked the Chimer to be the color of bruise, turning them into Dunmer,{{ref|name=LV32|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 32}}}} however Boethiah sometimes gets attributed with the more conventional teachings of Azura,{{ref|name=VF}} and in traditional retellings it is Azura who curses the Chimer with ashen skin.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Azura is credited with teaching the Chimer the mysteries needed to be different than the Altmer.{{ref|name=VF}}

The [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] replaced worship of the Good Daedra among the settled Dunmer following their apotheosis at the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], and the three Good Daedra were thereafter known to the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] as the '''Anticipations''' of the three Tribunes.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} The [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]] tribes refused to worship the Tribunal, rejecting them as 'false gods', remaining true to their worship of the Good Daedra. [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] and [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] still honored the Good Daedra.{{Ref|name=UHI|{{Cite Book|Understanding House Indoril}}}} The [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] however despised them for their fear of wisdom and order, calling them the '''False Princes''' and "the Anti-Gears that turn counter to the Nameless Will".{{ref|name=TIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}}

Following the deaths and disappearance of the Tribunes and the rise of the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]], worship of the Good Daedra was restored and they became known as the '''Reclamations'''.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} They are now also called the '''True Tribunal''' or '''House of Reclamations''', having reclaimed their place in the hearts of the Dunmer people from the "false Tribunal" that used their status to shield the True Tribunal from the public's minds. The name "Anticipations" is no longer used for them, as they have reclaimed their identity and no longer represent the patronage of the false Tribunal.{{ref|name=DBElderOthreloth|[[Skyrim:Elder Othreloth|Elder Othreloth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} Not all settled Dunmer turned away from Tribunal worship, although those few hidden sects who continued to worship the Tribunes as gods were persecuted by the New Temple for their heretical beliefs.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}}<noinclude>

The Halls of Veneration is a subterranean temple under [[Lore:Eidolon's Hollow|Eidolon's Hollow]] and is considered one of the holiest places of worship for the Good Daedra. It was abandoned when the Tribunal replaced the Good Daedra as the prime Dunmer faith.{{Ref|name=ONLoadEidolon|[[Online:Eidolon's Hollow|Eidolon's Hollow]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
