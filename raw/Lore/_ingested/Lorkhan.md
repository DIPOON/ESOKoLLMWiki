[[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], the '''Missing God''', is the '''Creator-Trickster-Tester''' deity present in every [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielic]] mythic tradition.{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} He is known as the '''Spirit of Nirn''', the god of all mortals.{{ref|name=SONGOM|{{Cite Book|Spirit of Nirn, God of Mortals}}}} His most popular name is the [[Lore:Aldmer|elven]] variant Lorkhan, or '''Doom Drum''',{{ref|name=TM}} which sometimes appears as '''LKHAN'''{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}} or '''LHKAN'''.{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}} Names for versions or aspects of Lorkhan include '''[[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]]''' (the Moon Beast) in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]],{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} and '''[[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]]''', the '''Spirit of Man''', the '''Mortal Spirit''', or the '''Sower of Flesh''' among the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]].{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} He is recognized as '''[[Lore:Sep|Sep]]''' in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], '''[[Lore:Sheor|Sheor]]''' in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], '''[[Lore:Shor|Shor]]''' in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and '''[[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]]''' in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} To the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], he is also called the '''Corpse-God'''.{{ref|name=FOM|{{Cite Book|Folly of Man}}}} He is also known as the '''Void Ghost'''.{{ref|name=LOV33}} He convinced or contrived the Original Spirits ([[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]]) to bring about the creation of the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], upsetting the status quo—much like his father [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]] or [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]] is usually credited for introducing instability, and hence possibility for creation, into the undivided universe (or the [[Lore:Beginning Place|Beginning Place]]).{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}<noinclude>

Lorkhan is sometimes associated with the number '''9''',{{Ref|name=V29|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 29}}}} and has been referred to as the '''Missing Sibling''' or '''Missing Ninth''' to the [[Lore:Gods_E#Eight_Divines|Eight Divines]].{{ref|name=SatD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=TEC|{{Cite Book|The Exclusionary Mandates}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=FPKirkbride|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=gen}}}} 

==Mythology and History==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none;|All Tamrielic religions begin the same. Man or mer, things begin with the dualism of Anu and His Other. These twin forces go by many names: Anu-Padomay, Anuiel-Sithis, Ak-El, Satak-Akel, Is-Is Not. Anuiel is the Everlasting Ineffable Light, Sithis is the Corrupting Inexpressible Action. In the middle is the Gray Maybe ('Nirn' in the Ehlnofex)...<br>

In any case, from these two beings spring the et'Ada, or Original Spirits. To humans these et'Ada are the Gods and Demons; to the Aldmer, the Aedra/Daedra, or the 'Ancestors'. All of the Tamrielic pantheons fill their rosters from these et'Ada, though divine membership often differs from culture to culture. Like Anu and Padomay, though, every one of these pantheons contains the archetypes of the Dragon God and the Missing God.|''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}}
As with most other gods, accounts of Lorkhan and his various feats date back to the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]].{{ref|name=BTAOM}} Owing to the non-linear nature of time in the Dawn, the War of Manifest Metaphors is said to have rendered stories of this era incapable of supporting anything resembling a singular "narrative",{{ref|name=TLL|{{Cite Book|The Lunar Lorkhan}}}} rendering attempts at constructing one absurd.{{ref|name=ONDOL|[[Online:Den of Lorkhaj|Den of Lorkhaj]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Nonetheless, various people and cultures have their own views on Lorkhan's actions and motivations, with varying levels of consistent motifs. Every [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielic]] mythic tradition has an archetypal Missing God in its pantheon. This god is credited with convincing, tricking, or coercing the Original Spirits  into creating the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], the Mortal Plane.

After the world had been materialized, myths hold that Lorkhan was killed, mutilated, or at least separated from his divine center, known by various terms across cultures but most commonly dubbed the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], in an event sometimes called the [[Lore:Convention|Convention]].{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}} This separation, which purportedly took place in Tamriel,{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}} is variously considered a punishment, a betrayal, or voluntary sacrifice.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}{{ref|name=PTDDU|{{Cite Book|Plan to Defeat Dagoth Ur}}}} Whatever remained of Lorkhan afterward was exiled to wander through creation, while the rest of the gods withdrew from the Mortal Realm.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=AAD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}}

The result of these events, Nirn, has been described by some as a source of mental anguish to all living things, who know deep down that their souls came from somewhere else, and that something must come next. Many wish to return to the [[Lore:Aetherius|spirit realm]], the original state of the universe; to them, Nirn is a prison keeping them sundered from the spirit realm, an illusion to escape, and Lorkhan is often the demon that hinders their way. Others think that Lorkhan created the world as the testing ground for transcendence; to them the spirit realm was already a prison, and that [[Lore:Amaranth|true escape]] is now finally possible.{{ref|name=SONGOM}}

As the impetus for creating the Mundus, Lorkhan is at the center of the great ideological schism between the [[Lore:Mer|elven]] and [[Lore:Man|human]] perspectives on the event, which is known as the Sundering to the elves or simply the Creation to other cultures, and thus opinions of him vary dramatically across the different religions of Tamriel.{{ref|name=TM}} Listed below are the more popular interpretations.

====Aldmeri Lorkhan====
[[File:SR-item-Auriel's Bow.jpg|Auriel's Bow, said to have launched Lorkhan's Heart into the sea.|left|thumb]]

The most popular name of the Missing God is his Aldmeri moniker, "Lorkhan", meaning "Doom Drum", and literally translated as "Dark Chief"{{ref|[[MW:Generic Dialogue A#Ashlander Courtesy|Generic dialogue]] on the topic of '''Ashlander Courtesy''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=ART|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Reference Text}}}}. For most Aldmeri cultures, particularly the Altmer, Lorkhan is a malevolent trickster, and the most unholy of all the higher powers.{{ref|name=VOF}} Lorkhan is believed to have forever sundered their connection to the [[Lore:Aetherius|spirit plane]].{{ref|name=SONGOM}}

The most detailed account of these beliefs is found in the Altmeri creation Myth, "Heart of the World". Within the tale, Lorkhan is characterized as "a barely formed urge" more akin to the limitations of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]] rather than a nature, unlike other spirits born from the ruminations of [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] and his soul, [[Lore:Auriel|Auri-El]]. Eventually, Lorkhan infiltrates the various aspects of Anuiel to plant a new idea, one based wholly on limitation. He outlined a plan to create a soul for Aurbis, the Mundus, so the spirits may self-reflect like [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] once did, and form their own cosmos. Many spirits joined in, including Auriel, who agreed on the assurance that he would be king of this new realm. This offer turned out to be a trick, as the new world was hardly a thing of Anu at all - Mundus was the House of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]. As the spirits on the newly formed Nirn began to die off, many of the et'Ada vanished completely. Some, following [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]'s example, fled to Aetherius and became the [[Lore:Magna Ge|stars]]. Some, such as [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], fully merged with the world and transformed into the natural laws known as [[Lore:Earthbones|Earthbones]], or Ehlnofey. Among those, a fourth group endured by marrying and making children with one another, living on through their descendants.{{ref|name=TM}} These Ehlnofey are known to the Altmer and Bosmer as [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], meaning Ancestors.{{ref|name=BTAOM}} 

Eventually, over many generations these children weakened into the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]], whom Auriel continued to lead as they founded the great kingdoms of Altmora and Aldmeris, also known as [[Lore:Old Ehlnofey|Old Ehlnofey]]. Meanwhile, the weakest of the surviving souls were gathered up by Lorkhan, who named them Men and led their armies against Auriel. In the ensuing war, Lorkhan destroyed the kingdom of Old Ehlnofey, shattering that land into many.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}} Eventually, the greatest knight of the Ehlnofey and champion of Auriel{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|Part 1, Prologue}}, [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], knocked Lorkhan down in front of his army and reached in "with more than hands" to remove his Heart. Lorkhan was undone, and his body was carried away by his vengeful followers. Despite Lorkhan's end, his Heart lived on, as it had become the Heart of the World and thus indestructible. Auriel then took the Heart and fastened it to an arrow, launching it into the sea where none may find it.{{ref|name=TM}} 

Some less mythic accounts from the Altmer describe Lorkhan's end as a trial undergone within the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] by the Aedra. There, Lorkhan was judged guilty and [[Lore:Auriel|Auriel]] slew him atop the Tower, removing his divine spark as punishment. Much like the "Heart of the World" account, the Heart proved to be indestructible, and was instead launched from the tower by an arrow from [[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]] and landed in the Sea, where a [[Lore:Red Mountain|volcano]] formed as a result. Afterward, the gods withdraw from the mortal realm, while Lorkhan remains exiled to wander Nirn forever.{{ref|name=TOA|{{Cite Book|Tower of Adamant}}}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}}

Lorkhan is believed by some Bosmer to still exist under the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], and that the rumblings of the volcano are a result of him eating food that upset his intestines.{{Ref|name=DanrithriO|[[ON:Danrithri|Danrithri]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some Aldmeri Creation myths hold that [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] is 'born' when Lorkhan's divine spark is removed. One crucial myth calls him the 'Sithis-shaped hole' of the world.{{ref|name=VOF}}

====Dunmeri Lorkhan====
[[File:MW-place-Akulakhan.jpg|thumb|Akulakhan, the second Numidium built by the Sixth House to contain Lorkhan's Heart]]
In contrast to their fellow elves, [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] culture, especially under [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] rule, always had a close relationship with the Missing God. 

The Dunmeri Creation Myth, "[[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]", characterizes Lorkhan as a cruel but ultimately benevolent god, who was created and sent by the titular primordial force to free the slaves of the false gods, [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] and the Aedra, as well as destroy their illusionary realms of [[Lore:Aetherius|everlasting imperfection]] that they used to make themselves immortal. Lorkhan does this via deceit, first creating his own dominion with what appears to be his own slaves, and then coming to Anuiel and the Aedra as a friend. Lorkhan told them that he was tired of his dominion, and wanted to create another. Magnus was the first to express his opinion on this, and eventually Nirn was created.{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}}{{ref|name=Mono2|group=TRL|[[General:The Monomyth (French translation)|French translation]] of ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}} However, according to the teachings of Boethiah, Lorkhan was betrayed by Magnus and the Aedra when he was not looking, and undone by them.{{ref|name=SermTen}}

Later Dunmeri legends claim that [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]]'s creation of the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] and the subsequent [[Lore:Veloth|Velothi Exodus]] were directly inspired by Lorkhan's ideas. Trinimac had tricked the Aldmer into believing tears were the best response to the Sundering, and took the Missing God's name in vain, calling his narratives into question. So one day, Boethiah came and defeated Trinimac, using Trinimac's own guise to expose his lies, and inspired his many new followers to head east, to Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}} These followers, led by Veloth, became known as the Chimer, and Boethiah bade them to pledge themselves to the 'Frame-Maker', one of Lorkhan's many titles, for creating this world for them to cherish and love him in.{{ref|name=SermTen|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 10}}}} 

Together, Boethiah and Mephala established the rules of the [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]], a process considered closely tied to Lorkhan, where the Chimer are charged with transcending the Aedra.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}} Dunmer acknowledge Nirn to be a cruel place full of mental anguish, however they are among those who believe Lorkhan created a realm where [[Lore:CHIM|true escape]] is now finally possible.{{ref|name=SONGOM}}{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=LOV33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}}

In later years, Lorkhan's Heart would become the source of Dagoth Ur and the Tribunal's divinity, with Lorkhan and his motivations becoming a pondered topic in the ''[[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|36 Lessons of Vivec]]''. The [[Lore:Scarab|Scarab]] and the [[Lore:The Serpent|Serpent Constellation]] are Dunmeri symbols for Lorkhan.{{ref|name=SermTen}}{{ref|name=LOV21|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 21}}}}{{ref|name=LOV33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}} The Black Scarab in particular is the symbol of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]].{{ref|name=CM52R|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 52: Redoran Style}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Black Scarab Chest|Black Scarab Chest]] body marking in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

In [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec's]] sermons, it is said that at the Altar of Padhome, there exists a [[Lore:CHIM|secret door]], one created by Lorkhan to show the mortals the secret at the Heart of the [[Lore:Aetherius|Beginning Place]].{{ref|name=LOV3|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 3}}}}{{ref|name=LOV13|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 13}}}}{{ref|name=LOV33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}}. This door is the promise of PSJJJJ, which is the [[Lore:Amaranth|flowering scheme]] of the Aurbis, and by following it one can fulfill Lorkhan's grand design for the Mundus, wherein it reaches the end of its usefulness and becomes a receptacle for something new.{{ref|name=LOV25|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 25}}}}{{ref|name=LOV37|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}} Additionally, Vivec's forbidden eighteenth sermon claims that Lorkhan has already vanished in the west, replaced by someone in the same manner that Tribunal replaced the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]] in Morrowind.{{ref|name=LOV18|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 18}}}} 

In his thirty-third sermon, Vivec's child, [[Lore:Baar Dau|Lie Rock]], met with the Void Ghost, an aspect of Lorkhan that dwells within the "Hidden Heaven", the [[Lore:The Serpent|Scaled Blanket]] made of [[Lore:Unstars|not-stars]], haggling with it for [[Lore:Walking Ways|powers]] only meant for Ruling Kings.{{ref|name=LOV33}} Later the sermon describes a meeting between Nerevar and the Void Ghost, Nerevar compares himself to Lorkhan, asking which of them are out of place, to which Lorkhan replied both. While the sermon does not say what was spoken between the two masters, Vivec's numerology explains that the thirty-third sermon teaches the nature of Anticipations.{{ref|name=LOV29|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 29}}}}{{ref|name=LOV33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}} In a similar vein, the "Sithis" creation myth compares Dagoth Ur to [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] and the Aedra, while the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] is likened to Lorkhan and advised to go unto the Sharmat Dagoth Ur as a friend, so that he may cast down the false gods as Lorkhan once did.{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}}

Later in the Third Era, it is said Vivec shared further teachings with his followers after journeying to Cyrodiil to consult the Emperor on matters of the Tower. According to Vivec, the Missing God is Padhome's Firstborn, who wandered the Aurbis and wanted no name, yet was branded with "Lorkhan" by others. As time passed, Lorkhan watched the Aurbis shape itself and grew equally delighted and tired with each new shaping. As the gods and demons of the Aurbis erupted, the child of Padhome tried to leave it all behind for he wanted all of it and none of it all at once. It was then that he came to the border of the Aurbis. There, he saw the Tower, for a circle turned sideways is an "I". This was the first word of Lorkhan and he would never, ever forget it.{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}}

====Mythic Dawn====
To the Mythic Dawn, Lorkhan is the God of the Mundus, a Daedric Prince whose realm of Oblivion is Dawn's Beauty, misnamed [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] by deluded mortals.{{ref|name=Com3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}}{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} According to the teachings of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], Tamriel is the realm of Change, and the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] are usurpers, betrayers and false gods posing as divinities for a Daedric Plane that has lost its Prince, whom they unsuccessfully attempted to erase from history.{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Camoran claims that in Convention, the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|First Tower]] Dictate separated not only Lorkhan, but all mortals, his progeny, from their divine sparks, forcing  mortals to see the Eight Aedra as the sole [[Lore:Aetherius|exit]] from the mortal prison. Further, in a secret message within his writings, he claims that the Eight Aedra woe every reader that has come to understand [[Lore:CHIM|CHIM]], the royalty Lorkhan taught mortals to seek.{{ref|name=Com3}}

====Lunar Lorkhan====
[[File:Mfull Sfull.jpg|thumb|left|Masser and Secunda, the supposed corpse of Lorkhan]]
[[Lore:The Lunar Lorkhan|The Lunar Lorkhan]] Theory is an account of the Dawn proposed by the [[Lore:Temple Zero Society|Temple Zero Society]]{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}, intended to explain the origin of the Moons.{{ref|name=Masser and Secunda|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Masser and Secunda|ns_base=General}}}} The theory claims that, where the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] contributed to the Great Construction by lending portions of their [[Lore:Planets|heavenly bodies]], Lorkhan's was instead cracked asunder so that his divine spark might "impregnate Nirn with the measure of its existence and a reasonable amount of selfishness". What was left of his "flesh-divinity" became the Moons, a personification of what [[Lore:Psijic Order|Artaeum]] calls the "Cloven Duality", the dichotomy that Lorkhan legends often rail against: ideas of good versus evil, being versus nothingness, and so on. The theory further asserts that the Moons were thus purposefully set in the night sky as Lorkhan's constant reminder to his mortal issue of their duty, and that all other '[[Lore:Convention|Heart Stories]]' are mythical degradations of the true origin of the moons.{{Ref|name=TLL|{{Cite Book|The Lunar Lorkhan}}}}

====Khajiiti Lorkhaj====
[[File:Maligned Saligned.jpg|Jone and Jode under a dark eclipse to form the Dark Moons, as seen from Nirn|thumb|right]]

Known to the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] as [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], the Missing God is a divisive subject in Khajiiti theology. He is the youngest of [[Lore:Padomay|Fadomai's]] children, born secretly in the [[Lore:Great Darkness|Great Darkness]] after she was struck by [[Lore:Anu|Ahnurr]], and thus forever haunted by the darkness that filled his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart]]. Like other cultures, the Khajiit universally attribute the creation of the Mundus to Lorkhaj in their myths, however their attitude towards that event and his role in it has changed dramatically after the First Mane [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]] restructured the pantheon in what is known as the [[Lore:Gods_R#Riddle.27Thar|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]].{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}{{ref|name=ESOESA1|{{Cite Book|Epistle on the Spirits of Amun-dro Vol 1}}}}

In pre-ri'Datta theology, Lorkhaj was known to the Khajiit as the White Lion and the Favored Son of Fadomai. He was said to have been born in the Great Darkness and carried it with him as a burden, existing in conflict with himself from the moment of his birth. Lorkhaj was the first spirit to forge his path forward with purpose, and his courage inspired all those that he encountered, earning him the title of the Moon Prince as he united his siblings to create the [[Lore:Mundus|World]].{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} Afterwards, he suffered a [[Lore:Convention|great betrayal]] at the hands of the other spirits, which some songs attribute to the machinations of [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]], and the Great Darkness overcame him, filling the hole where his heart had been. In his wisdom Lorkhaj, sought out his sister [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] who tore the darkness from his chest before it could consume him, and she named it [[Lore:Namira|Namiira]] before casting it beyond the Void Gate. From this [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]] was born the Moon Beast, the first of the [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|Dro-m'Athra]] and a shade of Lorkhaj that knows nothing but hunger, stalking the Lunar Lattice for any Khajiit that stray too far from the Path. The Moon Prince then expired in Azurah's arms, who burned his body upon a pyre and scattered the ashes across the [[Lore:Lunar Lattice|Lunar Lattice]].{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}{{ref|name=ESOFDF|{{Cite Book|The Favored Daughter of Fadomai}}}} Thus, to the ancient Khajiit Lorkhaj represented the duality of the Khajiiti soul and the hardships that all Khajiit must overcome, and they honored his sacrifice by walking the Path with purpose and resisting the call of the Dark.{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}

Later Khajiiti theologians look less favorably upon Lorkhaj, disregarding his Moon Prince aspect entirely as a dangerous trick that would mislead the Khajiit into attempting to revive the Moon Beast's true spirit only to be swallowed by his [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|eternal curse]].{{ref|name=ESOESA1|{{Cite Book|Epistle on the Spirits of Amun-dro Vol 1}}}} According to a post-ri'Datta creation myth, "Words of Clan-Mother Ahnissi", Fadomai birthed the last of her litter in the [[Lore:Great Darkness|Great Darkness]], having hidden there from her husband Ahnurr after he had beaten her. Thus, the Great Darkness filled the Heart of Lorkhaj and when he was born, the Great Darkness knew itself to be [[Lore:Namira|Namiira]]. Eventually, the spirit [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]] requested that Lorkhaj make her a place where she may birth many children, as Fadomai instructed her. Lorkhaj agreed, but because his heart was filled with darkness, he tricked his siblings so that they were forced into this new place with Nirni and trapped them within. Many of Fadomai's children escaped and became the stars, and many others died to make Nirni's path stable. The survivors stayed and punished Lorkhaj, tearing out his Heart and hiding it deep within Nirni. And though Nirni herself later forgave him, for now she could make children and had a place for them, the other spirits cursed "noisy Lorkhaj" to wander Nirni for many phases, forbidden by Azurah from hearing the First Secret while the other spirits learned how to cross the Lunar Lattice and escape.{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}

Both modern and ancient [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] religion places the two moons, Jone and Jode, as separate entities that existed prior to Lorkhan's punishment at Convention,{{ref|name=WOCMA}} and only believe that the third moon, the [[Lore:Dark Moon|Dark Moon]], is his corpse. In pre-ri'Datta tradition, they were stillborn twins, and it was their lanterns with which Azurah lit Lorkhaj's funeral pyre.{{ref|name=MBHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Moon Bishop Hunal Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=TMCD|{{Cite Book|The Moon Cats and their Dance}}}}{{ref|name=ESOShazah|[[Online:Shazah|Shazah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}

====Ayleid Lorkh====

By this lesson, the Adamantium Tower was set down, the laws of Convention enforced, to cleave a path to [[Lore:CHIM|Numancia]].{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}

====Reachmen Lorkh====
[[File:ON-item-Dark Heart.jpg|left|thumb|The Dark Heart]]
Known to the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachfolk]] as [[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]], the Missing God is considered the Spirit of Man, the Mortal Spirit, or the Sower of Flesh.

According to [[Lore:The Reach|Reach]] Myths, in darkness Lorkh came to understand, nothing does not exist. Where there is nothing there is possibility. And so he found a space in the Void where all that is could be. With this in mind he came to the Spirit Queen, Namira, to grant him a place in the infinite void to make a realm for wayward spirits, Nirn. Rather than a paradise, Lorkh created a hard and painful place—a realm that taught through suffering.{{ref|name=GSOR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} The early Reachmen fled to the mountains as with this creation Lorkh met his demise, and the world grew dark with shadows which sprung from the heart of Lorkh, who though greatly sundered still believed in the light of Man.{{ref|name=ONNZ|[[Online:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Reach Myth tells that Lorkh still wanders Nirn among mortals, appearing only in times of desperate need to aid the Reachfolk.{{ref|name=GSOR5}}

The Reachmen believe that at the "end of all days", [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] will fight alongside them,{{ref|name=ONHSG|[[Online:High Shaman Glynroch|High Shaman Glynroch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart of Lorkh]] will beat again, reawakened by feeding on the deaths of mortals, and its darkness will spread from its depths to consume everything, sparing only [[Lore:Namira|Namira]]'s faithful.{{ref|name=ONA|[[Online:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

It is thought the [[Lore:Briarheart|Briarheart]] ritual is a reflection by the immortal sacrifice of Lorkh.{{ref|name=GSOR5}}

====Nordic Shor====
[[File:SR-item-Shor's Throne.jpg|thumb|right|Shor's Throne in the Hall of Valor]]

Known to the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] as [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], the Fox Totem{{ref|[[Online:Eastmarch Antiquities#Ebony Fox Totem|Ebony Fox Totem]] Antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}, the Missing God is considered the God of the Underworld who wielded his clever [[Lore:Magic|magic]] to create their afterlife of [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]].{{ref|name=TRtS|{{Cite Book|The Road to Sovngarde}}}} The Nords revere him for having fought off Aldmeri oppression many times in Atmora.{{ref|name=VOF}}  His widow is the warrior goddess [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], the purported creator of mankind and leader of the gods after Shor's demise.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=DatN}}

Shor is said to have died at the hands of Elven Giants, who ripped out his heart at the end of the war that brought about the current world. {{ref|name=DatN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}}{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} The Nords believe this has happened before, as they consider the World itself to be a [[Lore:Kalpa|cycle]] in which the chief, Shor father of Shor, perishes every iteration of the world, spitting a helix of [[Lore:Shezarrine|ghosts]] into the world below to make way for his son, Shor son of Shor, who leads mankind into war again.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Shor Son of Shor|ns_base=GEN}}}} In more modern Nordic understanding, this is the transition from Dead Gods to Twilight Gods that occurs over each new iteration of the world, marked by the coming of the Last War in which Shor and his host descend from Sovngarde to fight again as they have at the beginning of time.{{ref|name=DatN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}}{{ref|name=SSCSS|[[Skyrim:Stormcloak Soldier (Sovngarde)|Stormcloak Soldier]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Sovngarde (quest)|Sovngarde]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}}{{ref|name=SRHS|[[Skyrim:Hero of Sovngarde|Hero of Sovngarde]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNTR|[[General:The Nords' Totemic Religion|The Nords' Totemic Religion]]}} Some myths consider [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]] the father of Shor.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga|ns_base=GEN}}}}

Shor is believed to wander in times of need as a ghost, sung into the world by the chant of Tongues. He once appeared to fight Alduin as he did in the beginning of time, and save the Nords from trickery of [[Lore:Orkey|Orkey]].{{ref|name=FSOKW}} {{ref|name=VOF}} Another mythic account describes his appearance at the Battle of Red Mountain, where he led the Nords against the armies of the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]], with [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]] and [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] serving as his generals.{{ref|name=FSOKW}} In Cyrodilic legends, he is tied to mortal incarnations such as "the [[Lore:Shezarrine|Shezarrine]]", who has also been called "Shor-Who-Lives".{{Ref|name=TFOS|{{Cite Book|The Footsteps of Shezarr}}}} [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] is one alleged Shezarrine, and Nords who saw him and his white hair proclaimed that Shor had returned, though Pelinal himself called this profanity.{{ref|name=TSOP4|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 4}}}}

====Cyrodilic Shezarr====
[[File:OB-KotN-PelinalPainting.jpg|thumb|left|Pelinal Whitestrake, sometimes believed to be the Shezarrine, fighting Umaril]]
Known to the [[Lore:Imperial|Cyrodiils]] (particularly the subculture of the Nibenese), as [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]], the Missing God is considered "the spirit behind all human undertaking" and the God of Man.{{ref|name=SatD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=VOF}} He is deeply revered as an early hero of the Nedes, and is considered to be synonymous with "Freedom" in the myths of the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Rebellion]].{{ref|name=TSOP2}}

According to the Cyrodilic Creation Myth, "Shezarr's Song", Shezarr came to the Aedra with a new idea: to become mothers and fathers, and make great sacrifices with no guarantee of success. Shezarr spoke beautifully to them, and moved them beyond mystery and tears. In some legends, [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]] is first to agree to Shezarr's proposal, and provided the space for this creation in the void.{{ref|name=VOF}} Thus the Aedra gave free birth to the world, the beasts, and the beings, making these things from parts of themselves. This free birth was very painful, and afterwards the Aedra were no longer young, and strong, and powerful, as they had been from the beginning of days.

The [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] mocked Shezarr and the other Aedra, and decided to make [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|their own worlds]] within themselves where they wouldn't have to sacrifice control, though they came to envy the Aedra's creation. Some Aedra were disappointed, bitter, and angry with Shezarr, and with all creation, for they felt Shezarr had lied and tricked them. These Aldmeri gods, led by [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], resolved to teach the elves to suffer with dignity, to chastise themselves for their folly, and to seek vengeance on Shezarr and his allies for reducing them to their enfeebled selves. But the gods of men and beast folk, led by Akatosh, were pleased with creation despite their great sacrifices, and resolved to teach the mortal races to live well, to cherish beauty and honor, and to love one another as the gods love them.{{ref|name=TM}}

Rather than use his Aldmeri counterpart as a scapegoat, some second era Cyrodilic bard songs claim Akatosh directly slew Shezarr (Lorkhan in the song), launching his laughing immortal heart across Tamriel. The Heart's Blood is said in these tales to have fallen into an Ayleid well and fused with the magicka therein, solidifying into a crystal that the Ayleids cut and polished into the Red Diamond, [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Chim-El Adabal]].{{ref|name=CEA|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad|Chim-El Adabal:A Ballad}}}}

Despite Shezarr's demise, he continued to wander Tamriel, aiding the tribes of man at various points as a warrior or a wise teacher before vanishing again to help mankind elsewhere.{{ref|name=SatD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{Ref|name=TFOS|{{Cite Book|The Footsteps of Shezarr}}}} Some believe Shezarr did not do this directly, but through mortal incarnates known as "[[Lore:Shezarrine|the Shezarrine]]", or "Shor-Who-Lives".{{ref|name=TSOP1|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 1}}}}{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}}{{Ref|name=TFOS|{{Cite Book|The Footsteps of Shezarr}}}} Pelinal Whitestrake was once said to be the alleged Shezarrine, although this was best left unsaid in Pelinal's presence as Plontinu, a soldier who fought alongside Pelinal, was said to have been smothered by moths in the night after voicing the claim.{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}} Some claim it was Pelinal or Shezarr, rather than Akatosh, that met [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] at her Deathbed, making her the first stone of the Amulet of Kings and forging the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Covenant]].{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}}{{ref|name=FET|group=UOL|{{TIL|First Era Timeline|first-era}} at The Imperial Library}}

It is said that Kynareth made the first rain at Shezarr's sundering, a phenomenon that did not exist before the removal of his Heart.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}

====Yokudan Sep====
[[File:RG-constellation-Serpent.jpg|thumb|right|The Serpent (Redguard)]]
The Missing God is known to the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] as Sep, the Second Serpent created from the detritus of the First Serpent [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]]'s dead skin.

According to the Yokudan Creation Myth, "Satakal the Worldskin", Satakal was born from the duality of Satak, the Snake who came Before, and Akel, the Hungry Stomach. Because there was nothing outside of Satak, it could only think about what it was, and so it devoured itself until its hunger caused it to bite down on its own heart and die. The hunger, though, refused to stop even in death, and so the First Serpent shed its dead skin to begin anew as Satakal. Once the other spirits realized this [[Lore:Kalpa|pattern]], the strongest of them learned to survive this cycle by striding between the worldskins at strange angles, in a process they called the Walkabout. One of the spirits, [[Lore:Ruptga|Ruptga]], was so tall he could place the stars in the sky to help weaker spirits to find their way to the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]], and sired many children across the cycles until he became known as Tall Papa.

Eventually, there were so many spirits that Tall Papa had to make himself a helper from the detritus of dead skins, and this was Sep, the Second Serpent. However, Sep carried much of the Hungry Stomach within him, multiple hungers from multiple dead skins, and would often devour the spirits he was supposed to help. When he did, Ruptga would reach in and pull them back out. After some time, Sep grew tired of helping Ruptga, and so he gathered all the dead worldskins into a ball. He tricked the other spirits, promising them this was how you reached the new world, by making one out of the old, and many joined him thinking this was an easier way of living, without jumping from place to place all the time. However, soon the spirits on the skin-ball began to die, as it was far removed from the real world of Satakal, and it was too far now for them to jump into the Far Shores, and Ruptga said they had to live on through their children and search for new ways to follow the stars. Sep, however, needed more punishment, and so Ruptga struck him with a [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|big stick]], and his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|hunger]] fell out of his mouth and was the only thing left of the Second Serpent. While the rest of the world strived back to godhood, Sep could only wander Nirn, trapped in his own dead skin, or swim about in the sky as the Serpent Constellation, a hungry void that jealously tries to eat the stars.{{ref|name=TM}}

====Argonian Shadow of Atakota====
Allegedly known to the Argonians of the [[Lore:Nisswo|Nisswo]] and Adzi-Kostleel tribes is a Time God, Atakota, and his nameless shadow.{{ref|name=COR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}}

According to the Adzi-Kostleel Creation Myth, "Children of the Root", [[Lore:Anu|Atak]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Kota]] become one, Atakota is born and says "Maybe", it sheds its skin. The skin follows it around as a Shadow. As Atakota coiled and [[Lore:Kalpa|devours worlds]], each a scale on its skin, things had time to begin and end. The Shadow wished to eat these things, but its belly was full of growing [[Lore:Hist|Roots]]. The Shadow soon spat out the Roots, and regarded them as its own, and so taught them secrets before falling into slumber. Over time, the Roots spread the Shadow's teachings, and things learn to take on new forms and names, but in doing so took the Shadow into themselves, and so learned what it was to be temporary. They came to fear the Shadow's change and call it Death. The Roots taught the Spirits how to evade death using riverways, and then went to sleep.

Eventually the Worlds of Atakota grew too big, and in desperate hunger the Spirits broke Atakota, splitting him into Atak and Kota once more. Some spirits grew claws and fangs and wings in the chaos. Others clung to what they were, such as a [[Lore:Y'ffre|Forest Spirit]], who made her [[Lore:Bosmer|own people]] like the Roots made [[Lore:Argonian|theirs]]. Soon, the Roots awoke, saw how Atak's sap had made stones and Kota's blood had made oceans, and knew what it would mean. And so the Roots awoke the Shadow, asking  it to protect its children. The Shadow devours the broken halves of Atakota before the World can end, to save its children, but in doing so devoured the roots as well. Seeking the Roots within itself, the Shadow remember it was the skin of something that came before, and so shed its [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|skin]], even though that was all it was, and kept its children safe  within its secrets.{{ref|name=COR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}} 

Tales about an explorer deep in Black Marsh describe a golden Argonian whose face changed twelve times. Soon after the explorer witnessed a vision of many wheels and their towers, that began to break, before quickly the Shadow of Atakota came to devour them.{{ref|name=LTOTFE4|{{Cite Book|Lost Tales of the Famed Explorer: Fragment VI}}}}

Some Argonians tie the Shadow of Atakota to the Serpent constellation.{{ref|name=ESOWUS|[[Online:Walks-Under-Shadow|Walks-Under-Shadow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Worship==
As a result of his nature as Missing God, Lorkhan is rarely the subject of direct worship in majority of cultures. Despite this, various cults to him have still arisen, alongside culture-agnostic examples of folklore. Some Lorkhan cults may have predicted the rise of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}}

In the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Lorkhan Cults began to see a rise across Tamriel,{{ref|name=TLL}}  and are said to have been responsible for spreading the doctrine of the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] phenomenon. Various scholars look down upon these cults and the notion of Dragon Break, regarding it as borne of scholarly inertia, and obsession with eschatology and [[Lore:Numidium|Numidiumism]].{{ref|name=TDBRE|{{Cite Book|The Dragon Break Re-Examined}}}} At some point in the late Third Era, contemporary to the publication of the [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition| third pocket guide to the empire]], a self-proclaimed Lorkhan priestess named Jsashe, the Witch-Queen of Whiterun, came into power over the state hold.{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}}

Lorkhan across varying beliefs has obscure relationships with other deities. He and [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] are the only deities who seem to be present in every tradition. As a result, [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] is sometimes considered his wife or concubine, as is Kynareth (or Kyne), and in others, Akatosh takes this role.{{ref|name=VOF}} While Lorkhan is the god of all mortals, [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] is known as the Mortals' God, and did not come into being before the creation of the mortal world. For the Redguards, this is relationship is detailed in [[Lore:Tu'whacca|Tu'whacca]] taking Sep's role as guide on the Walkabout after the creation of the mortal world.{{ref|name=VOF}}  Lorkhan is almost always associated with [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], across various cultural interpretations.{{ref|name=VOF}} It is thought that Lorkhan may still act in Mundus through mortal incarnations, known in Cyrodiil as the [[Lore:Shezarrine|Shezarrine]].{{ref|name=TSOP|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal}}}}

[[Lore:Ebony|Ebony]] is an extremely hard, durable, black glass-like substance said to be the crystallized blood of a god or gods,{{Ref|name=MWEBony|[[Morrowind:Raw Ebony|Raw Ebony dialogue]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=HBNW|{{Cite Book|Hrorvild Blackrock, Nord weaponsmith}}}} Lorkhan in particular.{{Ref|name=CM22T|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 22: Trinimac Style}}}} [[Lore:Skyshard|Skyshard]]s  and Sky Prisms are a rare type of [[Lore:Meteoric Glass|meteoric glass]]. Sometimes called "Shards of Aetherius", skyshards are shards of Aetherial magicka that carry the essence of Nirn. They are sometimes linked to Lorkhan.{{ref|name=SSIC|[[Online:Soul Shriven in Coldharbour|Soul Shriven in Coldharbour]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It is believed among the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] that [[Lore:Nirncrux|Nirncrux]] is another form of the blood of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]].{{ref|name=ONRH|[[Online:Rena Hammerhands|Rena Hammerhands]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Orc followers of Trinimac accent their armor and weapons with polished ebony in token of Lorkhan's blood when Trinimac tore out his Heart.{{Ref|name=CM22T}}

==Associated Realms and Celestial Bodies==

[[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], the mortal plane of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] itself, is Lorkhan's most renowned and controversial plane of existence. He is often considered the Spirit of Nirn,{{ref|name=SONGOM}} or the God of Mundus,{{ref|name=Com3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} and it is said his Heart has become the Heart of the World.

[[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]] is one of Lorkhan's realms, tied to his culturally Nordic aspect of Shor, it is said to be a realm of Aetherius{{ref|{{Cite Book|"Death" of Morphotypical Entities}}}} though an obscure source once claimed it to reside on the moons.{{ref|group=UOL|name=FPKirkbride|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=gen}}}} Also called the Underworld, Shor, as a trickster, wielded his clever [[Lore:Magic|magic]] to create [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]].{{ref|name=TRtS|{{Cite Book|The Road to Sovngarde}}}}

The twin [[Lore:Moons|moons]], [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]], are alleged remains of the planetary realm of Lorkhan. Per the Lunar Lorkhan theory, the original moon was cracked into two by the removal of his heart. The Cloven-Duality of the moons is acknowledged by the Psijics of Artaeum as well as Vivec.{{ref|name=TLL}}{{Ref|name=V32|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 32}}}} The [[Lore:Dark Moon|Dark Moon]], also called the Den of Lorkhaj, is the other rumored plantery realm of Lorkhan. The [[Lore:The Serpent|Serpent Constellation]], is tied to Lorkhan by numerous cultures such as the Yokudans, the Argonians, and the Dunmer.
