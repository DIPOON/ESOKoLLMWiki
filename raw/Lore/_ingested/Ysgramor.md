[[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] (sometimes '''Ysgramoor''',{{ref|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part X}}}} '''Ysgrimnoor''',{{ref|name=XL|group=UOL|[[General:Pantheon|''Pantheon'']]}} or '''Ysgrim'''),{{ref|name=Wiwi|group=TRL|1=[https://lagbt.wiwiland.net/index.php?title=Religions_de_l%27Empire&oldid=115294#:~:text=ayant%20trait%20%C3%A0-,Ysgrim,-racontent%20comment%20ce ''Religions de l'Empire''] — [[Lore:Books by Author#Mikhael Karkuxor|Mikhael Karkuxor]]}}{{ref|name=SevenFights|group=UOL}} known as '''Ysgramor the Invader''',{{Ref|name=TEGS|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}} '''Ysgramor the Returned''',{{ref|name=SevenFights|group=UOL}} the '''Harbinger of us all''',{{ref|name=SOTR|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return}}}} '''Lord''',{{ref|name=SOTR|}} '''High King''', and '''Hoary King''' was an [[Lore:Atmora|Atmoran]] who came to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] before recorded history as a refugee fleeing civil war in Atmora.{{ref|name=BAM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} He is generally regarded as the first human ruler and king of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].

Some [[Lore:Mer|Elven]] scholars insist Ysgramor was responsible for unspecified "provocations and blasphemies" that led to the genocide known as the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]], when the human settlement [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]] was attacked by the [[Lore:Snow Elves|Snow Elves]] and all humans purportedly were slain except Ysgramor and his two sons, [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]] and [[Lore:Ylgar|Ylgar]],{{ref|name=SOTR2|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 2}}}} though some scholars believe this attack was unprovoked.{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} Ysgramor fled back to Atmora, gathered the legendary [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]], then sailed back to [[Lore:Hsaarik Head|Hsaarik Head]] and drove the Elves from Skyrim and [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], cementing himself as a "culture hero" of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s.{{ref|name=BAM}}{{ref|name=FSP|{{Cite Book|Fall of the Snow Prince}}}}{{ref|name=VAB|{{Cite Book|Vernaccus and Bourlor}}}} He wielded the axe [[Lore:Wuuthrad|Wuuthrad]] in battle{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of {{g|skyrim}}}} and rode upon a [[Lore:Storm Atronach|Storm Atronach Bear]] conjured for him by his personal [[Lore:Clever_Folk|Clever Man]],{{ref|name=SAB|[[Online:Storm Atronach Bear (mount)|Storm Atronach Bear]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Alabar the Oddly-Colored.{{Ref|name=T500MC|group=UOL|[[General:The Five Hundred Mighty Companions or Thereabouts of Ysgramor the Returned|The Five Hundred Mighty Companions or Thereabouts of Ysgramor the Returned]]}}

Since he is the first known human to transcribe Nordic speech using Elven principles of writing,{{Ref|name=TOOTO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}} Ysgramor is credited with being the first human historian.{{ref|name=BAM}} Because of his exploits, he is known as "the first Harbinger, the first Man, [and] the bringer of Words", and the modern-day [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] still revere him as their only true leader.{{ref|name=GH|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} His progeny ruled Skyrim until {{Year|1E 369}}, when the death of [[Lore:Borgas|King Borgas]] brought an end to his direct line of known heirs.{{ref|name=PGE1S}} However, his bloodline survived,{{ref|[[SR:Anska|Anska]]'s dialogue in {{g|skyrim}}}} and he is still believed to be the wellspring from whom all Nordic kings are descended.{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} <includeonly>For more information, see the [[Lore:Ysgramor|lore article]].</includeonly><noinclude>

==History==
===Life in the Elder Wood===
[[File:ON-concept-Nord statue.png|A statue of Ysgramor with a canine companion|thumb|left]]
Ysgramor was born and raised in the northern continent of Atmora, the land was evidently much more temperate in his day than the frozen wasteland it would eventually come to be known as.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}} Certain legends of Ysgramor's Atmoran days exist such as him supposedly spending a day and a night wrestling a giant [[Lore:Chub Loon|Chub Loon]] down from [[Lore:Shivering Glacier|Shivering Glacier]].{{ref|[[Online:Atmoran Chub Loon|Atmoran Chub Loon]] pet description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Another yet involves his use of his legendary bow [[Lore:Long-Launcher|Long-Launcher]]. Ysgramor often used the bow to hunt in the [[Lore:Atmoran Frostwood|Atmoran Frostwood]]. The bow once needed to be re-strung, so Ysgramor collected the laments of the [[Lore:Giant|Giant]]-Wives and brought them to individuals known as [[Lore:Froa|Froa]] and [[Lore:Grosta|Grosta]] who twisted them into the Woeful Bowstring. When the bow made use of this new bowstring, it took on a form of sentience, sighing when Ysgramor held it and moaning whenever it was shot.

Ysgramor then used this restrung bow to hunt the White Stag of Forelgrim. It proved near impossible for Ysgramor to land a shot on the [[Lore:stag|stag]], as it was quiet and elusive. A [[Lore:hare|hare]] appeared before Ysgramor and told him that it knew the location of the stag because of its long ears, and that if Ysgramor also had long ears he could hear and successfully track the stag. The hare granted Ysgramor long ears upon his request, but a [[Lore:Fox|fox]] then emerged from the coppice and slew the hare, revealing himself to be [[Lore:Shor|Shor]]. Shor the Fox reverted Ysgramor's ears back to normal and then revealed to Ysgramor that the hare was in fact [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Herma Mora]] and nearly tricked him into becoming an [[Lore:Elf|elf]]. He bid him to henceforth rely only on the methods of man and not the tricks of elf lest he become one, and encouraged him to continue his pursuit of the White Stag.{{Ref|name=FAHM|{{Cite Book|Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus}}}} This story served as one of many legends that involved Ysgramor and Herma-Mora as an ever present antagonist.{{ref|name=VoF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}

[[File:ON-item-armor-Ysgramor's Ascendance Helm.jpg|Visage of an armor Ysgramor wore during his original Atmoran days|thumb|right]]
Beyond the more commonly accepted Nordic legends of Ysgramor, others yet speak to his relationship with the Atmoran [[Lore:Dragons|Dragons]] and [[Lore:Dragon Cult|their cult]]. Ysgramor appears to have had the ancient powers of [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]] according to various sources. With certain legends speaking to his breath that was weighted with power sounds,{{ref|name=SevenFights|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga|ns_base=GEN}}}} and his act of  "Shouting Some Sense" into the Nords.{{Ref|name=FSKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} The Thu'um was said to have originated in Atmora,{{ref|name=MOTLAHSF|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Minutes of the Lusty Argonian Historical Society, Evening Star, 3E 432}}}} but only for the [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priests]] and Dragons themselves.{{Ref|name=TGATD|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}} 

Some sources claim Ysgramor was himself, a literal Dragon and his name took the style of other dragons: '''YS GRA MOR''',{{ref|name=DWD|group=UOL}} or '''Ysmaalithax the [[Lore:Ysmir|Northerly Dragon]]'''.{{Ref|name=T500MC|group=UOL|}} Other sources dismiss this theory and propose it was more likely that he was a Dragon Priest given his stature during the existence of the Dragon Cult, with the rank of "dragon" being  an honor bestowed to someone of high stature in the Cult. These ties are believed to be obscured possibly due to people favoring chauvinism over historical truth.{{ref|name=DWD|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War, documented|ns_base=General}}}}

===Settlement of Mereth===
At some point in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], civil war broke out in Atmora. Ysgramor, his family, and many other Atmorans chose to flee this civil war, sailing south to the continent of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Ysgramor and his people landed in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], at the extreme northern tip of the [[Lore:Broken Cape|Broken Cape]] upon [[Lore:Hsaarik Head|Hsaarik Head]].{{ref|name=PGE1S|}}{{ref|name=BAM|}}

Apparently, some historians have misrepresented Ysgramor as being the leader of the first large group of human settlers from Atmora, when in fact he was merely following what was already a long-established tradition of migration.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}

When Ysgramor arrived to Tamriel, his people brought along their faith of a pantheon consisting of animal [[Lore:Gods|gods]]. Above all these animal totems was that of the dragon. Grand temples were erected by the Atmorans to honor these totems including the dragons themselves who served as living embodiments of the dragon totem.{{ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} The Atmorans named the land of Skyrim "Mereth" for the [[Lore:Elves|Elves]] that roamed the land. Initially there was peace between Ysgramor's people and the Elves.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}}

Eventually more settlers from Atmora arrived to Skyrim where men had begun to flourish. Ysgramor and his men erected the great city of [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]], the first and largest Nordic settlement on Tamriel.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}}

===Night of Tears===
[[File:SR-npc-Tolfdir 02.jpg|The Eye of Magnus in Sarthaal|thumb|left]]
Eventually the local elves, namely the [[Lore:Snow Elf|Falmer]] turned on their human neighbors, slaughtering them in great abundance and razing the city of Saarthal, the event would come to be known as the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]].

Scholars debate on the reason why the Night of Tears took place. With some Elven sources claiming that Ysgramor committed unknown blasphemies to kickstart the conflict.{{Ref|name=TEGS|}} Human sources on the other hand, claim innocence on the matter and cite Elven fear that the young vital race of men would eventually surpass their stagnant culture as cause for the attack.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}} Others yet claim that when the Atmorans built Saarthal, they discovered the mythical [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]] and despite their best attempts to keep this secret, the elves would learn of this artifact and take Saarthal in an attempt to secure this power for themselves.{{Ref|name=NoT|{{Cite Book|Night of Tears (book)}}}}

Regardless of the reason for the event, utter devastation was dealt to the Atmorans. The only survivors of the Night of Tears were Ysgramor and his two sons [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]] and [[Lore:Ylgar|Ylgar]].{{ref|name=PGE1S|}} Ysgramor and his sons managed to reach the last intact ship in their fleet and the trio fled Tamriel back to their homeland of Atmora. As Ysgramor stared across the waters, a storm began to brew and the Harbinger watched as Saarthal burned from afar. Ysgramor was so grief stricken that he began to cry not conventional tears, but that of pure [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]]. His son Yngol captured these tears in a stein and then consoled his grief stricken father. From their Yngol, who was the greatest Atmoran smith of his age, began to craft a weapon using the raging storm and his father's ebony tears. The next morning Yngol presented his father the great axe forged from his grief, Ysgramor cried out with emotion and named the weapon [[Lore:Wuuthrad|Wuuthrad]] meaning "Storm's Tears" in the Atmoran language.{{ref|name=SotR5|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 5}}}}{{ref|name=ONYsgramor|[[Online:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Gathering of the Five Hundred===
[[File:ON-crown store-Music Box, Hymn of Five-Hundred Axes.jpg|Miniature depiction of Ysgramor captaining Ylgermet (ESO)|thumb|left]][[File:ON-crown store-Storm Atronach Bear.jpg|A Storm Atronach Bear, a known mount of Ysgramor|thumb|right]] 
When Ysgramor returned to Atmora, he was deeply angered and wished to instantly set sail back to Tamriel to exact revenge on the elves. Instead he calmed himself and took his bow Long-Launcher into the eastern marshes where he slayed [[Lore:Faldrosta|Faldrosta the great Snow-Goose]]. He then plucked a great quill from her corpse and proceeded to use it to develop a [[Lore:Nordic#Atmoran Dictionary|runic alphabet]] based on Elvish principles, which enabled the written transcription of Atmoran speech. Ysgramor's first work in this new form of writing was a chronicle of his own life and its events, solidifying him as the first human historian.{{Ref|name=TOOTO|}}{{ref|name=BAM}}

Ysgramor sent his sons Yngol and Ylgar to gather the finest warriors of Atmora together to form a fleet of warriors. This mighty fleet consisted of various ships including the ships of Ysgramor's sons&mdash;the [[Lore:Darumzu|Darumzu]] and the [[Lore:Harakk|Harakk]], named after the [[Lore:Stars|stars]] favored by the Atmorans{{ref|name=SOTR2}}&mdash;as well as Jorrvaskr, captained by [[Lore:Jeek of the River|Jeek of the River]], Ysgramor's childhood companion, and the third Harbinger.{{ref|name=GH}}{{ref|name=SOTR7|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 7}}}} and also the Kaal Kaaz, the Krilot Lok, the Fallowfire, the Sadon Reyth, and the ship of Lord Ysgramor himself, the Ylgermet.{{ref|name=ESOValdur|[[Online:Valdur|Valdur]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SOTR49|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 49}}}}{{ref|name=SOTR19|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 19}}}} Ysgramor and his mighty Five Hundred Companions being gathered, set sail for Tamriel.

===The Return===
[[File:ON-npc-Yngol.jpg|thumb|left|Yngol, Ysgramor's son]][[File:SR-misc-Ysgramor Statue.png|A statue of Ysgramor the Returned|thumb|200px|right]]
During his voyage to Tamriel with his Five Hundred Companions, a great storm was set upon the fleet that was said to have been the works of [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] herself. Ysgramor lost his son Yngol to the "sea-ghosts" (likely an ancient, superstitious term for the dangers in the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]]),{{ref|name=SOTR19|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 19}}}} and Ysgramor braved the storm to search for him in the treacherous waters.{{ref|name=SOTR2}}{{ref|name=YATS|{{Cite Book|Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts}}}} When Yngol was found dead, Ysgramor wept with grief and killed a "dozen dozen beasts" in Yngol's honor.{{ref|name=SOTR2}}{{ref|name=YATS}} One legend of metaphysical nature claims that Ysgramor during the Return took a gigantic form and pushed Atmora closer to Tamriel, so the journey from one of the continents to the other was never as long again.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=Ysgramorshade|[[:File:GEN-misc-Ysgramoor.jpg|Ysgramor Shade]] — ''[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]''}}

When Ysgramor arrived to Tamriel, he and his men camped near the ruins of Saarthal, which the elves had fortified against him. Ysgramor was met by [[Lore:Ahzidal|Ahzidal]], a legendary Nordic [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanter]] who had a personal vendetta against the elves, and thus offered to bedeck the Atmoran's armor and armaments with powerful enchantments, to which Ysgramor happily obliged.{{Ref|name=AD|{{Cite Book|Ahzidal's Descent}}}} One powerfully enchanted armor type were the Gauntlets of the Companions, powerful Dragonplate gauntlets that Ysgramor gifted to some of his most trusted companions to ensure they had the fortitude to keep up with him in his conquests of Skyrim that were to come.{{ref|[[Blades:Gauntlets of the Companions|Gauntlets of the Companions]] description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

With their combined might, and the help of Ahzidal's enchanted gear, Ysgramor and his companions were able to recapture Saarthal, dealing the Falmer a resounding defeat with great slaughter.{{Ref|name=AD|}}{{ref|name=SOTR7|}} One particular skirmish within the retaking of Saarthal speaks of Ysgramor confronting the ancient Snow Elf wizard, [[Lore:Serenarth (Snow Elf)|Serenarth]]. As both the elves and men fought for control of Saarthal, Ysgramor drew his bow Long-Launcher and managed to strike a decisive blow to the wizard, allowing the Atmorans to move on and reclaim the city.{{Ref|name=TEGS|}}

After Saarthal was retaken, Ysgramor let out a mighty war cry that was said to have been heard even in Atmora, and when the Atmorans heard it they knew it was time for them to set sail for Tamriel. The Companions looked to Ysgramor for what to do next, and Ysgramor bade that the various crews that had sailed together split up and spread out among Skyrim, to continue to eliminate the Falmer and other enemies wherever they may be.{{ref|name=SOTR7|}} This would begin the creation of the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First human Empire]] in Tamriel's history.{{Ref|name=TEGS|}}

Ysgramor and his people rebuilt Saarthal,{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Imperial Report on Saarthal}}}} while his great friend Jeek of the River, and 21 others close friends of Ysgramor, the crew of the Jorrvaskr reached an ancient structure that would come to be known as the [[Lore:Skyforge|Skyforge]] and built the city of [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] around it.{{ref|name=SOTR7|}} Eventually Ysgramor and his crew of the Ylgermet left Saarthal behind, where they came upon [[Lore:Yngol's Barrow|Yngol's Barrow]], the burial place or Ysgramor's son who had perished during the beginning of the Return. 

Ysgramor looked south of Yngol's Barrow where the river met the sea and decided a great city would be built there. With the hands of Atmorans, and elven slaves from prior conquests, the City of Kings, [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] was created.{{ref|name=SOTR19|}}

===Further Conquests===
[[File:BM-interior-Jolgeirr Barrow.jpg|The remains of the Snow Prince on Solstheim|thumb|left]]
A final confrontation between Ysgramor and the Snow Elves took place on the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]. Having repelled the elves from the province of Skyrim in near entirety, the Nords were also set on erasing their presence from Solstheim. This conflict was concluded at the [[Lore:Battle of the Moesring|Battle of the Moesring]]; the Nords dominated the battlefield with their superior weapons, and it was said that the slopes of the Moesring "ran red with Elf blood".{{ref|name=FotSP|{{Cite Book|Fall of the Snow Prince}}}}

[[File:ON-quest-The Rise of Sage Svari.jpg|A projection of Ysgramor and Ylgar fighting Sinmur|thumb|right]]

The turning point came when the [[Lore:Snow Prince|Snow Prince]] rode into battle on a "brilliant steed of pallid white". He parted the ranks of the elves and swooped into Ysgramor's forces. He killed many with his [[Lore:Spear of the Snow Prince|spear]] and his ice magic, including [[Lore:Ulfgi Anvil-Hand|Ulfgi Anvil-Hand]], [[Lore:Strom the White|Strom the White]], [[Lore:Freida Oaken-Wand|Freida Oaken-Wand]], [[Lore:Heimdall the Frenzied|Heimdall the Frenzied]] and Jofrior. [[Lore:Finna|Finna]], the daughter of Jofrior, watched as the Snow Prince killed her mother. In her rage, she threw Jofrior's sword at the Prince, which struck him in the breast and killed him. With their savior lost the Elves fled the battle, and the Nords cut down any who remained.{{ref|name=FotSP}}

The fate of the Snow Elves was sealed by their defeat. According to one tale Ysgramor had their noses cut off so they could be distinguished from other Elves, and drove them underground.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Monsters of Northern Folklore}}}} Those Elves who were driven underground sought refuge with the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], and in doing so, doomed their entire race. Deep underground in the realm of [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]], the Dwemer agreed to provide sanctuary for the refugees. Unbeknownst to the Elves, however, their saviors would exact from them a terrible cost. Mistrustful of their charges, the Dwemer tricked and enslaved the Elves, twisting the once-majestic beings into the [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]], a race of bloodthirsty creatures made blind by the cruel hands of their masters.{{ref|name=TFaS|{{Cite Book|The Falmer: A Study}}}}{{ref|name=Dawnguard|[[SR:Gelebor|Gelebor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}

According to the skalds of the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]], Ysgramor slew so many [[Lore:Frost Giant|frost giant]]s that he was covered in their blue blood. The rest of his foes fled the field and frost giants avoided the more populated areas of the province, thereafter becoming much rarer to find.{{ref|name=WSA}} He and his [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] were the first to wear the blue war-paint based on these battles.{{ref|name=WSA|[[Online:Ysgramor's Chosen Body Marking|Ysgramor's Chosen Body Marking Antiquities Codex]] from [[ON:Greymoor|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=YCBM|[[Online:Ysgramor's Chosen Body Marking|Ysgramor's Chosen Body Marking]] description from [[ON:Greymoor|ESO]]}} A large number of Giant clans inhabited the land, and were ruled by a powerful chieftain named [[Lore:Sinmur|Sinmur]]. According to legend, Ysgramor and the Five Hundred Companions fought a large-scale war with Sinmur and his kin, and both sides suffered severe casualties. The conflict culminated in a final battle between the two leaders in Sinmur's barrow. As they dueled, Ysgramor shattered Sinmur's club and finally slew him with his legendary battleaxe, [[Lore:Wuuthrad|Wuuthrad]].{{ref|name=SotR27|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 27}}}}{{ref|name=ONSvari|[[Online:Skald Svari|Skald Svari]]'s dialogue in {{g|eso}}}} According to legend, he also defeated a powerful mage known as [[Lore:Lodorr|Lodorr]], who forged a magical crown that allowed him to enslave an army of followers.{{ref|name=ONMalana |[[Online:Malana|Malana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Ysgramor's death was met with great sorrow and mourning among his kin, and the news quickly spread across Tamriel.{{ref|name=SOTR56|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 56}}}} Although a grand tomb was built for him beneath Windhelm in preparation for his passing, Ysgramor instead chose to be buried with his most trusted generals in [[Lore:Ysgramor's Tomb|a barrow]] on the northern shores of Skyrim, facing towards Atmora.{{ref|name=SOTR19}}{{ref|name=Vilkas|[[SR:Vilkas|Vilkas]]' dialogue in {{g|skyrim}}}} His son, Ylgar, subsequently took up the mantle of leadership.{{ref|name=ONSvari}}

===Legacy===
[[File:SR-npc-Anska.jpg|Anska, Possible descendant of Ysgramor (Skyrim)|thumb|left]][[File:ON-npc-Ysgramor.jpg|The spirit of Ysgramor|thumb|right]]
In death, Ysgramor journeyed to the [[Lore:Hall of Valor|Hall of Valor]] in [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]], where he continued to watch over the Nord people and the Companions. After Ysgramor died his son, Ylgar, subsequently took up the mantle of leadership.{{ref|name=ONSvari}} This [[Lore:Ysgramor Dynasty|Ysgramor Dynasty]] would endure for centuries on. The thirteenth member of the Dynasty, [[Lore:Harald|Harald]], is remembered for being the first king to relinquish all holdings in [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]. Establishing the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s as a separate people, he was responsible for the consolidation of Skyrim as a centralized kingdom.{{ref|[[Online:Eastmarch Antiquities#Sacred_Chalice_of_Ysgramor|Sacred Chalice of Ysgramor]] antiquity codex entry in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}

Where Harald finished Ysgramor and other predecessors work of fully unifying Skyrim and driving out all manner of elves from the province, the fifteenth in the line of Ysgramor [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage the Gifted]] fulfilled the Companions vision of journeying beyond the province of Skyrim, and furthering the rule of the First Empire of the Nords.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}} The Ysgramor Dynasty ended with the death of the controversial [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]], who instituted edicts that went against the traditional Nordic faith.{{ref|name=PGE1S|}}

Despite the Ysgramor Dynasty meeting its end, his bloodline was said to have survived,{{ref|name=Ansks|[[SR:Anska|Anska]]'s dialogue in {{g|skyrim}}}} and he is still believed to be the wellspring from whom all Nordic kings are descended,{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} although the evidence for this claim is limited.{{Ref|name=TEGS|}} Ysgramor's heirs faced trouble and threats from various foes such as Ysgramor's former rival Serenarth,{{Ref|name=TEGS|}} a figure known as [[Lore:Aumriel|Aumriel]]{{Ref|name=LL|{{Cite Book|Lost Legends}}}}, and even the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Mauloch|Mauloch]].{{ref|name=VoF|}}

During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], the [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]], led by the [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancer]] {{Lore Link|Thallik Wormfather}}, sought to reanimate Sinmur and an army of his kin.{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TO|{{Cite Book|Thallik's Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} With the aid of a [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] tribe they attacked Shor's Stone, infiltrated Sinmur's tomb and awakened his spirit.{{ref|name=ONUN|[[Online:Unearthed|Unearthed]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They also stole Wuuthrad from [[Lore:Fallowstone Hall|Fallowstone Hall]], as the axe had become imbued with great power after slaying Sinmur. The Worm Cult believed this power could be used to raise Sinmur and use him in their schemes.{{ref|name=ONVigrod|[[Online:Vigrod Wraithbane|Vigrod Wraithbane]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Ysgramor guided the [[Lore:Hero|Heroic]] [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] to undo the works of the Worm Cult and put a stop to his old foe Sinmur.{{ref|name=ONYsgramor|}} Eventually the Vestige with the help of [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] was able to deal the worm cult a decisive blow by banishing Sinmur completely.{{ref|name=ONSS|[[Online:Stomping Sinmur|Stomping Sinmur]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The spirit of Ysgramor and many of his Companions appeared to congratulate the pair directly for the feat before departing back for Sovngarde.{{ref|name=ONYsgramor|}}

In {{Year|4E 201}} during the rise of the prophetic [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]], [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] loomed over the Underworld of Sovngarde, devouring souls of the dead to gain power. When the Last Dragonborn arrived to the Hall of Valor, they encountered many heroes including the spirit of Ysgramor, who informed the Last Dragonborn that Shor had bade them keep their blades sheathed, as the fight with Alduin belonged only to the Last Dragonborn, and the three ancient tongues [[Lore:Hakon One-Eye|Hakon One-Eye]], [[Lore:Felldir the Old|Felldir the Old]], and [[Lore:Gormlaith Golden-Hilt|Gormlaith Golden-Hilt]].{{ref|name=SRYsgramor|[[SR:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The four ultimately succeeded in defeating and banishing Alduin.{{ref|name=SkyrimDragonslayer|[[Skyrim:Dragonslayer|Dragonslayer]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
