[[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]] (also called the '''Chrysamere Blade''',{{Ref|name=ARTIL|[[Arena:Artifact Quests#Chrysamere|Artifact Quest Dialogue]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} or '''the Chrysamere'''){{ref|name=DFTI|[[Daggerfall:The Impostor|The Impostor]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Agning|Agning]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} is an ancient [[Lore:Adamantium|adamantium]] claymore [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] with offensive capabilities only surpassed by its defenses.{{ref|name=AR/DF|''[[Arena:Chrysamere|Arena]]/[[Daggerfall:Chrysamere|Daggerfall]]'' in-game description}}{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} It has [[Lore:Magic|magical]] properties which, unlike most weapons, are defensive in nature: it heals the wielder, grants them resistance to fire, and reflects hostile spells.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}}{{ref|name=AR/DF}}{{ref|name=TL}} It is unaligned, and is not a Daedric artifact.{{ref|name=D4C|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|78}} Chrysamere was forged in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] by the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] swordsmith [[Lore:Asterie Bedel|Asterie Bedel]], who delighted in using the techniques of both [[Lore:Men|man]] and [[Lore:Mer|mer]] in her creations. In Chrysamere's case, this most clearly manifests in the placement of her maker's mark, a "mage's knot" within the heart of the sword that is said to harken back to the earliest days of Bretonic culture. Chrysamere is also said to have been influenced by the magic of Breton society's Elven forebears, and it gained further enchantments over time.{{ref|name=LAIA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}} Chrysamere does not typically stay with one swordsman for long, for it chooses not to favor one champion.{{ref|name=AR/DF}} It has earned the title of the '''Sword of Heroes''',{{ref|name=LAIA}} but it also goes by other names such as the '''Sword of Kings'''{{ref|name=ARCS|''[[Books:Codex Scientia|Codex Scientia]]'' [[Books:Codex Scientia/Advertisement|advertisement]]}} and the '''Blade of Blades'''.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}} Chrysamere is considered a holy weapon, and in this context it is referred to as the '''Sword of the [[Lore:Paladins|Paladin]]''', or simply the '''Paladin's Blade'''.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}}{{ref|name=TL}}

==History==
===Merethic and First Era===
After Chrysamere's forging in the late Merethic Era, it would subsequently be used in the wars to secure [[Lore:Direnni|Clan Direnni]]'s rule over [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. By this time, Chrysamere had already passed hands several times, each time gaining further enchantments. Its defensive enchantments were further enhanced and retooled during the siege of the Fellthunder Clan of [[Lore:Giant|giants]] in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]], and it would later be seen at the [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]] where it played a small yet important role.{{ref|name=LAIA}}

Chrysamere was also present at the first sacking of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]],{{ref|name=LAIA}} although it is unknown if this was the sacking supposedly carried out by [[Lore:Ryain Direnni|Ryain Direnni]] with the help of his Breton legions,{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} or the sacking of the First Orsinium that took place during the tenth century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}
===Second Era===
[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended the sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing Chrysamere among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Chrysamere and the other artifacts detailed in the book.{{ref|group=TRL|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}

[[File:ON-item-weapon-Chrysamere 03.jpg|Chrysamere Replica (ESO)|right|thumb]]
Where Chrysamere was during the time of the Interregnum is unknown, but records from the [[Lore:Infinite Archive|Infinite Archive]] in [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] hint that a series of letters penned between a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] researcher from [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] and a [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] fence from the city-state of [[Lore:Alabaster|Alabaster]] in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] may have contained clues of its whereabouts during that time.{{ref|name=LAIA}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, both solid gold letter openers in Chrysamere's image{{ref|[[ON:Treasures C#Chrysamere Letter Opener|Chrysamere Letter Opener]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and a number of weapon replicas of Chrysamere were seen throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=ONCS|[[ON:Chrysamere Style|Chrysamere]] outfit style in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] (circa {{Year|3E 389}}-{{Year|3E 399}}), a scribe in Alabaster working with the city's archivists learned that Chrysamere had resurfaced somewhere in Tamriel. Further research on the part of the scribe revealed the existence of a tablet, hidden within a dungeon located in Elsweyr itself, which was inscribed with the current location of Chrysamere. Rumors of Chrysamere's return eventually came to the attention of the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]], who followed the rumors to the brother of the scribe. The scribe's brother, who had little desire for adventure, offered to relay the tablet's location in Elsweyr in exchange for gold, which would allow the scribe to complete his studies and his brother to raise a family. After journeying to Elsweyr and discovering the tablet, the Eternal Champion learned Chrysamere was located in a second dungeon outside of Elsweyr, where they were able to acquire the sword.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}}
 
[[File:DF-quest-The Lost Artifact 03.jpg|The Mages Guild would like to keep their artifact (Daggerfall)|left|thumb]]
Sometime during the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] (circa {{Year|3E 405}}–{{Year|3E 417}}), [[Lore:The Agent|the Agent]] obtained Chrysamere in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. One story claims it was bestowed to them by a [[Lore:Knights|knightly order]] after putting a stop to an [[Lore:Lich|arch-lich]] that was raising an army of undead.{{ref|name=DFTAD|[[Daggerfall:The Army of Undead|The Army of Undead]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Another account builds upon this, stating that after putting a stop to the undead menace, the Agent discovered a clue to the blade's whereabouts. This led them to retrieve Chrysamere from a [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] branch, who had stolen it from the great-grandfather of a secluded wizard. However, there are conflicting accounts that the artifact centered around these events was actually a different one altogether, namely the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]], the [[Lore:Necromancer's Amulet|Necromancer's Amulet]], or the [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|Warlock's Ring]].{{ref|name=DFTLA|[[Daggerfall:The Lost Artifact|The Lost Artifact]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Records also indicate the Agent had at least one unsuccessful attempt in obtaining Chrysamere, during which they were hired to acquire a daedric lodestone in exchange for the artifact. The lodestone's guardian, a [[Lore:Daedra Seducer|daedra seducer]], revealed that their employer was deceiving them. It is unknown who they chose to believe, but this ultimately led to a violent confrontation in which the Agent slew their employer and confirmed they never had the artifact in their possession. This resulted in the Agent gaining infamy with the imposter's associates, who turned out to be the faction belonging to the King of Orsinium, [[Lore:Gortwog gro-Nagorm|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]].{{ref|name=DFTI}} Nevertheless, the Agent is also thought to have been offered Chrysamere by Gortwog himself at one point in exchange for the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=DFWGTT|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{intnote|DF|[nb 1]}}
[[File:MW-quest-Grandmaster Duel.jpg|Varus Vantinius (Morrowind)|right|thumb]]
Chrysamere would later be in the possession of the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]]'s [[Lore:Order of Ebonheart|Order of Ebonheart]] for many years.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Varus Vantinius|Varus Vantinius]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Circa {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] was tasked by this knightly order with recovering Chrysamere and the [[Lore:Lord's Mail|Lord's Mail]] from some thieves. The Mail was in the possession of an expelled member of the Legion, while the sword had been taken by a treacherous knight. However, the Legion's spies reported that Chrysamere had changed hands to that of Draramu Hloran, a powerful sorceress found in the [[Lore:Abanabi Caves|Abanabi Caves]] near [[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]]. The sword had gained a reputation for being designed for killing spellcasters,{{ref|[[Morrowind:Maranique Jolvanne|Maranique Jolvanne]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and Draramu became feared even in Sadrith Mora.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue D#Draramu Hloran|Draramu Hloran]] dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Nerevarine dealt with the two threats and returned both artifacts to Varus Vantinius, the Knight of the Imperial Dragon on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]].{{ref|name=MWLords|[[Morrowind:Lord's Mail (quest)|Lord's Mail]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Nerevarine later challenged Vantinius for the title of Knight of the Imperial Dragon; the two dueled in the [[Lore:Vivec (city)|Vivec City]] arena, and Vantinius was slain, allowing the Nerevarine as victor to claim both artifacts from his corpse.{{ref|name=MWGD|[[Morrowind:Grandmaster Duel|Grandmaster Duel]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Later that year, the cuirass and blade were sold to [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]], who put them on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=Tribunal|[[Tribunal:The Museum|The Museum]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}
===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-quest-The Lost Paladin.jpg|The Lost Paladin (Skyrim Creation Club)|right|thumb]]
Chrysamere re-emerged around {{Year|4E 180}}, when the [[Lore:Hero|Warrior]] came across the blade during their journey. The weapon is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.{{ref|[[Blades:Artifacts|Artifact]] acquisition methods in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} Sometime afterwards, the blade was wielded by a [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] warrior known as the Lost Paladin, who died with the blade at [[Lore:Forelhost|Forelhost]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the paladin's aggressive spirit was encountered and laid to rest by the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]], who then claimed Chrysamere.{{ref|name=ccBGSChrysamere|[[Skyrim:The Lost Paladin|The Lost Paladin]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
