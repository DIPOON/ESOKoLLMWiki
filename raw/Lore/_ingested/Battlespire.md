The [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]], also called the '''Spire'''{{ref|name=anchorwarn|{{Cite Book|Anchor Warning|ns_base=BS}}}} or the '''Celestial Citadel''',{{ref|name=BSIdeal|[[BS:Ideal Master|Ideal Master]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} is a void-castle{{ref|group=UOL|name=SwordMeeting|{{Cite Book|Tiber Septim's Sword-Meeting with Cyrus the Restless|ns_base=GEN}}}} that serves as the magical war academy of the [[Lore:Imperial Battle College|Imperial Battle College]] and [[Lore:Imperial Battlemages|Imperial Battlemages]], acting as a testing facility for those seeking to join the [[Lore:Imperial Guard|Imperial Guard]]. The strongest mages in the Empire reside and train in the Battlespire. It is described as the fortress of all fortresses that encompasses the entirety of a realm, with ramparts that tower over the edges of Oblivion. Beyond them, only the [[Lore:Void|Void]].{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel|Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel]] (not to be confused with regular Imperial Battlemages) is an aristocratic position in which one serves as the advisory head of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]],{{ref|name=CATLA2|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions 2}}}}{{ref|name=ONMTCAB|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Abnur Tharn}}}} and it was customary for future advisors to be plucked from the administers of Battlespire, the Spire Council, who were themselves battlemage classmen.{{ref|name=Athenaeum|{{Cite Book|Battlespire Athenaeum|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|17}}<noinclude>

The Battlespire itself is an enormous floating castle comprised of many towers, which is built into a "stone-carved mountain" in the shape a "[[Lore:Dragon|dragon]]'s head."{{ref|name=BSIntro}}{{ref|name=XLTranslab|group=UOL}} The castle floats far above a "sea of gas" ringed by steep mountains. The skies above feature three large celestial bodies: one red, and two orange.{{ref|name=BSIntro}}{{ref|name=XLTranslab|group=UOL|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/20010129050800/http://www.xltranslab.com:80/spire/Spire.htm# XLTranslab.com: Battlespire animation sequences]}} The inhabitants measure time in "moons" rather than [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn's]] days.{{ref|name=SL|{{Cite Book|Starlover's Log}}}} It is possible to access numerous realms of Oblivion from the Battlespire through the use of teleportals and voidgates. The facility is moored in place by five great Anchors, which assure its safety and stability in the surrounding flux.{{ref|name=anchorwarn}} Access to the edifice is granted via the "[[Lore:Pillar of Light|pillar of light]]" on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], a teleportation device linked to the [[Lore:Weir Gate|Weir Gate]].{{ref|name=BSIntro|[[BS:Quests|Intro]] to ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

Some sources place the Battlespire deep in [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]],{{ref|name=Athenaeum}}{{rp|17}} while others place it within the vast chaotic emptiness of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=Codex}} Perhaps explaining the discrepancy, the Battlespire is said to reside in a location known as a [[Lore:Slipstream Realm|Slipstream Realm]], which are points within the Aurbis at which transliminal forces balance. The [[Lore:Imperial Mananauts|Imperial Mananauts]] used it a transition zone for mortals to acclimate to other realities; as the mortal mind can be driven to madness by Oblivion, the Battlespire is useful as it allows the mind to acclimate to the contradictory nature of the outer realms without the same levels of mental strain.{{ref|name=Fa-Nuit-Hen|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}}

==History==
===Second Era===
[[File:RG-texture-Battlespire Book 02.png|A Second Era book depicting the Battlespire|thumb|right]]
The first Imperial College of Battlemages was founded at some point between {{Year|1E 243}} and {{Year|1E 498}},{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}} though its connection to the Battlespire is unknown. Although the date of the construction of the Battlespire Academy remains obscure,{{ref|name=Fa-Nuit-Hen}} both the academy and the Weir Gate were known to exist as of {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=ONScute|[[ON:Contraband S#Scute of the Mock Turtle|Scute of the Mock Turtle]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The institution was accepting recruits by the time of the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] in {{Year|2E 580}}. 

During the Interregnum, the Spire's council continued to train and dispatch battlemages to preserve parts of the Empire.{{ref|name=Martus|[[ON:Martus Tullius|Martus Tullius]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Waking Flame|Waking Flame]]''}}

The institution was once again accepting recruits by the time of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] in {{Year|2E 864}}.{{Ref|name=Jaggy|[[RG:Jaganvir|Jaganvir]]'s dialogue in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

===Third Era===
====Attack by Clan Dagon====
[[File:LO-misc-Battlespire History.gif|thumb|right|The invasion of the Battlespire]]
After numerous failed attempts in the past,{{ref|name=SL}} the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] succeeded in capturing the Battlespire{{ref|name=Codex|[[GEN:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary#Battlespire|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary: Battlespire]]}} during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]].{{ref|name=LTM|{{Cite Book|Letter to Mactana|ns_base=BS}}}} Dagon had been instrumental in [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]]'s imposture and assumption of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Imperial]] throne,{{ref|name=BSAngada|[[BS:Sirran Angada|Sirran Angada]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} a proposition engineered by Dagon's son, [[Lore:Xivilai Moath|Xivilai Moath]].{{ref|name=Athenaeum}}{{rp|158}} In return for his assistance, Tharn gave the Battlespire to the Prince, on the condition that Dagon eliminate Tharn's rival battlemages within.{{ref|name=Athenaeum}}{{rp|18}} In {{Year|3E 398}}, Mehrunes Dagon collected his prize.{{ref|name=ESTimeline|[[GEN:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Timeline|Elderscrolls.com Archive Timeline]]}} The Prince's forces, led by [[Lore:Imago Storm|Imago Storm]] of [[Lore:Clan Dremora|Clan Dremora]], mustered in the [[Lore:Havoc Wellhead|Havoc Wellhead]], then rampaged through two other planes of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]—the [[Lore:Shade Perilous|Shade Perilous]] of [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]{{ref|name=LFTBH|{{Cite Book|Letters for the Battlespire Hero}}}}{{ref|name=BSFrost|[[BS:Frost Daedra|Frost Daedra]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} and the [[Lore:Soul Cairn|Soul Cairn]]—before invading the Battlespire itself.{{ref|name=BSIdeal}}

Shade Perilous was home to two Daedra clans, the lordless [[Lore:Seducer|Seducer]]s and the [[Lore:Nocturnals|Nocturnals]] who had given them refuge. The Nocturnals were imprisoned in their own demense as the Seducers were bent to Dagon's will. However, some Seducers willingly entered his service, and these converts were remade in the image of Dagon's greed and treachery, making them much more powerful—and giving them wings.{{ref|name=BSDeyanira|[[BS:Deyanira Katrece|Deyanira Katrece]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=BSDarkSeducer|[[BS:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducers]] in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} These "Dark Seducers" joined the ranks of the invasion forces, with some being given [[Lore:Daedric Crescent|Daedric Crescent]]s to wield in battle.{{ref|name=BSIntro}} One Dark Seducer named [[Lore:Si'ess|Si'ess]] became Mehrunes Dagon's bodyguard and lover, and was entrusted by the Prince with one of his artifacts, the [[Lore:Sword of the Moon Reiver|Sword of the Moon Reiver]].{{ref|name=LFTD|{{Cite Book|Letters from the Dremora|ns_base=Battlespire}}}}{{ref|name=BSBodyguard|[[BS:Si'ess|Si'ess]]' dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} 

When after several moons of battle the invading daedric armies of Mehrunes Dagon had nearly wiped out all of the Battlemages, Samar Starlover told the other survivors of his plan to mount [[Lore:Dragonne Papré|Papré]] and fly to the [[Lore:Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace]] on Nirn in order to notify Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] (although, at the time, [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]] was impersonating the Emperor and was perfectly aware of the invasion). However, the Daedra had prevented Papré from escaping by sealing the main gate, and presumably killed him. When Starlover fought his way to Papré's lair, all he found was a carcass. Unable to request a rescue force, Starlover gave up hope and took comfort in being "eternally reunited" with his companion in death.{{ref|name=SL}} Once the Battlespire was taken, the [[Lore:Weir Gate|Weir Gate]], the portal to Tamriel, was sealed with Dagon's personal warding sigil. This only prevented travel out of the Battlespire, however; it did not keep others from entering in.{{ref|name=LTM}}{{ref|name=BSImago|[[BS:Imago Storm|Imago Storm]]'s dialogue in [[BS:Battlespire|''Battlespire'']]}}{{ref|name=BSLevel1|[[BS:Level 1|The Weir Gate]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

====Recovery of the Battlespire====
[[File:BS-misc-Battlespire Cinematic Render.jpg|thumb|right|The Battlespire surrounded by celestial bodies]]
Unaware of these events, a pair of [[Lore:Apprentice|apprentice]] battlemages, [[Lore:Shadow Legion|Shadow Legion]] aspirants, were the last to enter the Battlespire. The first apprentice went ahead, following the invasion force's trail through the Battlespire and then through the Soul Cairn and Shade Perilous. They survived by pretending to be one of Dagon's Daedra, while secretly leaving behind notes and helpful items for the second apprentice, but was ultimately caught and brought to [[Lore:Dagon's Hunting Lodge|Dagon's hunting lodge]] before the Prince himself.{{ref|name=LFTBH}}{{ref|name=BSLevel7}}{{ref|name=BSInformant|[[BS:Dremora Informant|Dremora Informant]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} The other apprentice entered later, after the fighting had died down, and went in search of the first (and for a way back to Tamriel) by fighting through, bluffing past, and making deals with the various forces, Daedric and otherwise, along the way.{{ref|name=BSLevel7}}

After following the trail back to the Shade Perilous, the apprentice befriended the Nocturnals and freed their leader, [[Lore:Jaciel Morgen|Jaciel Morgen]].{{ref|name=BSLevel4|[[BS:Level 4|Shade Perilous]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} Curiously, Clan Dremora also offered its assistance, for while Mehrunes Dagon had allowed his armies to enter Shade Perilous and use it as a waystation for the assault on the Battlespire, he had not sanctioned its overthrow or occupation. Instead of negotiating for passage or showing restraint, Dagon's protégés and adoptive children, [[Lore:Faydra Shardai|Faydra Shardai]] of the [[Lore:Fire Daedra|Fire Daedra]] clan and Moath of the [[Lore:Frost Daedra|Frost Daedra]] clan, decided to crush their long-time enemies, the Nocturnals. The Dremora believed this needed to be undone in order to rectify things.{{ref|name=BSDremora|[[BS:Dremora#Level 4: Shade Perilous|Dremora Oathkin]]'s dialogue during [[BS:Level 4|Shade Perilous]] in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

[[File:BS-creature-Mehrunes Dagon.gif|left|Mehrunes Dagon (Battlespire)|left|thumb]]
However, the path from the Shade Perilous led to the [[Lore:Chimera of Desolation|Chimera of Desolation]], where the apprentice became the hare in a [[Lore:Great Hunt|Great Hunt]].{{ref|name=BSLevel5|[[BS:Level 5|Chimera of Desolation]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} While the Dremora could offer little help there,{{ref|name=LFTD}} the apprentice was able to survive and locate [[Lore:Chimere Graegyn|Chimere Graegyn]]. Chimere drew on his past experience with Mehrunes Dagon to help the apprentice escape the realm, offering up the [[Lore:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]], as well as knowledge of the [[Lore:Spear of Bitter Mercy|Spear of Bitter Mercy]] to end the hunt. The most vital information he provided was Dagon's [[Lore:Nymic|protonymic]], which can be used to banish him. However, it is likely that by this time, the Prince had added an unknown neonymic as well.{{ref|name=BSChimere|[[BS:Old Man Chimere|Old Man Chimere]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=BSLevel5}}

Once through the Chimera, the apprentice wound up in the Havoc Wellhead, and came face to face with Grand Vizier Imago Storm of the Dremora. Storm was of the opinion that with the conquest of the Shade Perilous combined with the taking of the Battlespire, Clan Dagon had "overreached its resources and {{sic|judgements|judgments}}". He felt it best if Faydra, Xivilai, and Mehrunes Dagon himself were temporarily removed from the picture so he could restore order and stability to Clan Dagon.{{ref|name=BSLevel6|[[BS:Level 6|Havok Wellhead]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} Storm then gave the neonymics of all three to the apprentice, as the mortal had little choice but to go along with this plan.{{ref|name=INAN|{{Cite Book|Imago's Notes about Neonymics}}}}{{ref|name=BSImago}}

[[File:BS-screenshot-Bs1a.jpg|thumb|right|Dagon is defeated by the [[Lore:Apprentice|Apprentice]]]]
After dealing with the lesser Daedra, the apprentice portaled into the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] near Dagon's hunting lodge, the Prince's personal pleasure palace.{{ref|name=BSDremora}} According to the Dremora, banishing Mehrunes Dagon required the Armor of the Savior's Hide, his proto- and neonymics, the "aid of absent friends", a bit of hope, and a weapon made from Dagon's own substance, the Sword of the Moon Reiver.{{ref|name=LFTD}} This last item was soon taken by force from Dagon's Dark Seducer lover, and the apprentices battled up a mountainside and entered the lodge to face Dagon himself.{{ref|name=BSLevel7|[[BS:Level 7|Dagon's Hunting Lodge]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

Mehrunes Dagon was waiting in his inner sanctum with the first aspirant, who was chained from the ceiling and paralyzed. The Lord of Destruction smugly thanked the mortal for bringing him so many valuable magic items, though he was perturbed that the Savior's Hide prevented the paralytic effect. And more so when the apprentice revealed knowledge of Dagon's nymics. But the Prince remained smug until the apprentice revealed the Moon Reiver, at which point he decided to take action. But in that moment, the apprentice summoned Jaciel to the battle; her sudden appearance momentarily distracted Dagon, giving the apprentice enough time to strike.{{ref|name=BSLevel7}} Dagon was sucked back into the [[Lore:Void|Void]], being "compressed into an ever-smaller volume" before vanishing.{{ref|name=Athenaeum}}{{rp|180}}
 
The Battlespire was partly destroyed during the invasion's aftermath. The Empire reclaimed the ruins,{{ref|name=BSIntro}} and gathered up all the Daedric Crescents to be destroyed, bar one.{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} These events led to the conclusion that investment into its reconstruction was unwise at that time, due to the installation being a great temptation for Daedra to target and other matters demanding the Empire's resources. Therefore, it was proposed to leave the gates to the Spire sealed indefinitely.{{ref|name=Athenaeum}}{{rp|180}}

[[File:ON-render-Lucilla Caprenia.jpg|Lucilla Caprenia, a Potential who was conscripted at 6 years of age|thumb|right]][[File:BS-npc-Clarentavious Valisious.png|Clarentavious Valisious|thumb|right]]
The Battlespire was administered and maintained by five powerful battlemages called the '''Spire Council''', with the five in {{Year|3E 398}}{{Ref|name=BethTime|[[GEN:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Timeline|Elderscrolls.com Timeline]]}} being:

*[[Lore:Paxti Bittor|Paxti Bittor]], Exalted Summoner and Lion-Lord Invoker Pursuivant.

Recruits of the Battlespire were known as '''Potentials''' and usually identified as such around the age of 12, though there were cases of candidates with especially high affinity being conscripted as early as 6 and starting their training in the Battlespire itself at 10. These Potentials learned magic, magical and martial warfare, and contemporary studies. Every year there was a Potentials Battle Tourney, with single combat as a known part of it.{{ref|name=MTCLC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Lucilla Caprenia}}}}{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Emperor also had a personal liaison to the Battlespire, with the title of this position being the Imperial Secretary. Lomegan Mariel held this position circa 3E 398.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Jagar Tharn's Letter}}}}

The Spire Council was known to also administer facilities outside the Battlespire itself. One such location was the Dread Cellar, a magically warded prison where the Empire's worst enemies were held and interrogated, or tortured, for information. The Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel, traditionally selected from among the administrators of the Battlespire, was also aware of the existence of the facility and held visitation rights to it. The Dread Cellar operated for centuries until its eventual abandonment during the tenure of [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]] as Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel due to the collapse of the Empire, and it is unknown if its operation ever resumed.{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Martus|[[ON:Martus Tullius|Martus Tullius]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Waking Flame|Waking Flame]]''}}{{ref|name=VL|[[ON:Vitellia Laenius|Vitellia Laenius]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Waking Flame|Waking Flame]]''}}{{ref|name=DCLS|[[ON:The Dread Cellar|Dread Cellar]] loading screen in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Athenaeum|{{Cite Book|Battlespire Athenaeum|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|17}}

==Training of Potentials==
[[File:ON-render-Exiled Redoubt.jpg|Prime Sorcerer Vandorallen, a scion of Cyrodiilic nobility who was sent to the Battlespire as a child|thumb|right]]
Becoming a full fledged Battlemage required the completion of a grueling training regimen. Potentials were scouted for the makings of a Battlemage and high magical affinity early on, and subsequently brought to the Battlespire for training. The standard time of conscription was twelve years of age, though there are known cases of candidates with especially high affinity being conscripted as early as six years old and starting their training in the Battlespire itself at ten. At the Battlespire Potentials received education in each of the schools of [[Lore:Magic|magic]], including disciplines such as [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]], as well as in magical and martial warfare and contemporary studies. Known training exercises included the [[Lore:Conjuration|summoning]], banishment, and control of [[Lore:Atronach|Atronachs]], an early stage of training known to take place while the Potentials were children, as well as magic duels between the potentials themselves, learning how to combat [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], and learning how to conserve one's energy while spellcasting. The Potentials also received training in how to scout and operate stealthily behind enemy lines, with one known case of a Potential recruited earlier than the standard twelve years having started this part of her training at ten years of age. Potentials were separated into training groups, with candidates in the same group going through daily activities such as training, dueling and eating together alongside one another for many years. The conditions were exceptionally harsh, leading to a high casualty rate among the Potentials, a number of which were known to meet their end during the course of the magic duels or other exercises. Many Potentials could not endure the training and the grief of losing their fellows and so removed themselves from the Spire. Completion of the training program took years and culminated in a series of field missions where the Potential gained practical experience outside the Battlespire itself by carrying out operations selected for them by their tutors.{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MTCLC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Lucilla Caprenia}}}}{{ref|name=GtC|{{Cite Book|Guide to Cheydinhal}}}} 

Upon completion of the field missions, the Potential underwent Initiation, being sent on a journey through several [[Lore:Planes of Existence|planes]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] and declared a full fledged Battlemage upon their return. Though Potentials dealt with Daedra more than most, the Initiation was still known to claim many lives and was a source of considerable fear among the Potentials, with it being said many would rather join a [[Lore:Mages Guild|guild]] or even fight in war rather than to face it."{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Despite the potential danger field missions posed they were considered by far the easier trial compared to the Initiation itself, and some Battlemage tutors considered missions that involved fighting Daedra the perfect practice for the journey through Oblivion itself.{{ref|name=Martus|[[ON:Martus Tullius|Martus Tullius]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Waking Flame|Waking Flame]]''}} The pool of Potentials was known to sometimes include scions of noble families, sometimes sent to the Spire, though the families retained the ability to pull the Potential from their training should they desire to.{{ref|name=Vandorallen|[[ON:Prime Sorcerer Vandorallen|Prime Sorcerer Vandorallen]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Lucilla|[[ON:Lucilla Caprenia|Lucilla Caprenia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notable Locations==
The Battlespire consists of many notable locations, with a lot of these relating to the daily function of the college, for example, the High Court Room. The Battlespire also contained a large room for dragons such as [[Lore:Dragonne Papré|Dragonne Papré]].

===The Star Galley Crib===
[[File:BS-place-Star Galley.jpg|thumb|right|The Star Galley]]
Aside from the [[Lore:Weir Gate|Weir Gate]] itself, the only access to the colleges was via the Star Galley, a magic boat which could navigate the surrounding environs.{{ref|name=BSLevel1}}{{ref|name=BSScamp|[[BS:Scamp|Scamp]]'s dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} The vessel was constructed by Battlemage and Artificer [[Lore:Clarentavious Valisious|Clarentavious Valisious]],{{ref|name=LTTGMOTIL|{{Cite Book|Letter to the Grand Marshall of the Imperial Legion}}}} and was kept in the Star Galley Crib near the Weir Gate, though access was normally restricted.{{ref|name=SGCN|{{Cite Book|Star Galley Crib Note|ns_base=Battlespire}}}}

This section of the facility is also notable for being the home of the gigantic [[Lore:Mock Turtle|Mock Turtle]].
{{NewLine}}
===The High Halls and Librarium===
[[File:BS-level2b-Point E.png|thumb|right|A college library]][[File:BS-level2b-Point M 03.jpg|Voidgate|left|thumb]]
The upper sections of the Battlespire consist of the war college itself. Here are found dormitories, mess halls, libraries, lecture halls, an infirmary. Other areas found here are the Great Hall, the Conjury Hall and the Summoning Hall.

Of particular importance was the voidgate. This device, powered by artifacts known as voidguides, allowed passage to Oblivion. The [[Lore:Soul Cairn|Soul Cairn]] was accessed in this manner during Mehrunes Dagon's invasion, but it is not known which other realms the voidgate was able to reach.{{ref|name=BSLevel2b|[[BS:Level 2b|Administration, Labs and Library, Part 2]] level in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

===Other Realms===
Several realms of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] were known to be accessible from the Battlespire or from each other. These include the [[Lore:Soul Cairn|Soul Cairn]], [[Lore:Shade Perilous|Shade Perilous]], the [[Lore:Chimera of Desolation|Chimera of Desolation]], the [[Lore:Havoc Wellhead|Havoc Wellhead]], and [[Lore:Dagon's Hunting Lodge|Dagon's Hunting Lodge]] in the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]].

==Notes==
*According to [[Lore:The Doors of Oblivion|''The Doors of Oblivion'']], years after Dagon's invasion was defeated, the mage [[Lore:Morian Zenas|Morian Zenas]] and his apprentice attempted to use the remains of the Battlespire to enter Oblivion. They found that although the Weir Gate still stood and the shattered academy remained accessible from Mundus, the link to Oblivion had been broken. However, this is contradicted by the [[Lore:Crafting Motifs|''Crafting Motifs'']] series, according to which the two lived in the Second Era. Copies of ''The Doors of Oblivion'' were also available at this time, but no mention of the Battlespire was seen within.
*In the [[Morrowind Mod:Developer Mods|Developer Mod]] ''[[Morrowind Mod:Morrowind Advanced|Morrowind Advanced]]'', there exists an Imperial Proving Ground very similar to the Battlespire, in which very similar events to the game take place.
*The {{Cite Book|Tiber Septim's Sword-Meeting with Cyrus the Restless|ns_base=GEN}} unofficial text, written by [[GEN:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]], indicates that there are multiple Battlespires within the armada of the Empire's New Void Navy. ''Battlespire'' and ''ESO'' however, treat it as if there is the only a single Battlespire within the lore, ''the'' Battlespire. Additionally, the ''Sword-Meeting'' did not follow the lore that the Imperial Battlespire is a [[Lore:Slipstream Realm|pocket universe]] unto itself.{{ref|name=SwordMeeting|group=UOL}}
