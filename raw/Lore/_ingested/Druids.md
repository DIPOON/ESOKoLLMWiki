# Lore:Druids

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

{{Trail|Magic}}{{Featured Article}}{{TOCright}}[[File:ON-misc-Druid Laurel.jpg|left|thumb|A community of Stonelore Circle Druids]]<onlyinclude>
[[Lore:Druids|Druids]] (also known as '''vinebeards'''){{ref|[[ON:Peverel Luluelle|Peverel Luluelle]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} are children of the land whose kind have their origins in ancient [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|name=ESODR|[[ON:Druid Ryvana|Druid Ryvana]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}} and {{g|on|firesong}}}}{{Ref|name=EOTD|{{Cite Book|Exodus of the Druids}}}} They are practitioners of [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]]'s True Way (also known as Druidism),{{ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sir Stefan Mornard}}}} in which one is to lead a life connecting with, valuing, and stewarding the growth of the natural world{{ref|name=LABHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}}{{ref|name=ESODA|[[ON:Druid Audrine|Druid Audrine]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} referred to as [[Lore:The Green|the Green]].{{ref|name=LADCG|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Druid Circles of Galen}}}} The eponymous god takes the forefront of their faith, with some reverence also being shown to the stars.{{ref|name=ONWD|{{Cite Book|Wyrd and Druid}}}} The True Way is a promise made to the "groves, to the seas, to the sky",{{ref|name=LADCG}} and under the Oak's Promise, druids are to "stand strong, stay true, and shelter all".{{Ref|name=ESOOFM|[[Online:Oak's Promise Face Marks|Oak's Promise Face]] and [[Online:Oak's Promise Body Marks|Body Marking]] descriptions in {{g|on}}}} Druids serve in accordance to Y'ffre's will in roles such as priests,{{ref|name=ESODR}} arbiters,{{ref|name=ONDJ|[[ON:Druid Jend|Druid Jend]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} teachers, healers,{{ref|name=ESODA}} and [[Lore:Sight|soothsayers]].{{ref|name=LADCG}} Druids have little interest in the quarrels of monarchies, often leading many to believe that they are pacifists. However, as champions of nature, they are not afraid to defend their ancient sites from the cruelty of civilization, as violence and death are natural.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=LABHI}}

Y'ffre and his [[Lore:Earthbones|Earthbones]] sacrificed themselves during the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] to create the Green, an act which bound [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] to the laws of nature.{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}{{ref|name=TOAF|{{Cite Book|The Ooze: A Fable}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Bretons}}}}{{ref|name=ESODA}} Their voices are sought by druids so they may best be their advocate, and to ensure a life guided by natural principles and the willfulness of nature.{{ref|name=ESODA}} Druidic spellcraft (which differs from the arcane) is said to be the first truly [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] creation,{{ref|name=ONLOTB|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Bretons}}}} and its practitioners utilize the wellspring of primordial energy the Earthbones left behind, which flows via lines through the ground.{{ref|name=ONSWC|[[Online:Stormweaver's Cavort|Stormweaver's Cavort]] codex entries in {{g|on|firesong}}}}{{ref|name=ONPE|[[Online:Pearls of Ehlnofey|Pearls of Ehlnofey]] codex entries in {{g|on|markarth}}}} Junctions where this resource intersects are known as "[[Lore:Bonelines|bonelines]]", places where druidic spells exhibit strange properties, and where prayers carry deeper emphasis.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Visitor's Guide to Y'ffre's Cauldron}}}} It can be harnessed by druids to overcome their physical limitations,{{ref|name=ONSWC}} shapeshift into various animals,{{ref|name=LADCG}} terraform the environment around them,{{ref|name=ONSHV1|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 1}}}} or even invoke natural disasters to consume their foes.{{ref|name=ONPOT|[[ON:People of Import|People of Import]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Druids are also known animists{{ref|name=ONHIDS|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Draoife Storystone|Draoife Storystone]] codex entries in {{g|on|highisle}}}} who can call upon [[Lore:Nature Spirits|nature spirits]] to aid them.{{ref|name=ONLOTB}}</onlyinclude>{{NewLeft}}
==Overview==
:''"The story of the druids is the story of the Bretons. And of the wyrd. At least to a point. You ask what we as druids believe, and I say that what we believe comes from where we were born. And both the wyrd and the druids—indeed, all Bretons—rocked in the same cradle."'' —''[[Lore:Wyrd and Druid|Wyrd and Druid]]''
[[File:LO-race-Breton.png|left|thumb|Illustration of a typical Breton male]]
Druids are defined by many dichotomies, and prime among these is their acknowledgement of their Breton heritage of hailing from both [[Lore:Men|Men]] and [[Lore:Mer|Mer]].{{ref|name=ONSWC}} The first vinebeards are referred to as the [[Lore:Druids of Galen|Druids of Galen]] by retroactive nomenclature, and they were the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]] of High Rock{{ref|name=LATD}} (or proto-Bretons){{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Leamon Tuttle's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=LADCG}} that intermingled with the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]{{ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}} (primarily [[Lore:Direnni|Clan Direnni]]) for centuries during the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=LATD}} From this union, the Bretons (then called "Manmer" and "Half-Elves"){{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{ref|name=PGE1High|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} were conceived as unwanted half-breeds,{{ref|name=ONLOTB|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Bretons}}}} rejected by their Direnni parents that considered them sub-Mer.{{ref|name=ONLOTB}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} The Druids of Galen led this newly emerged race,{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL|[[General:Firesong_DLC_%26_Update_36_Preview#How_Druids_tie_into_the_Breton_Legacy|How Druids tie into the Breton Legacy]] section in [[General:Firesong DLC & Update 36 Preview|Firesong DLC & Update 36 Preview]]}} and Manmer were elevated in Nedic society, where they thrived.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=TBMOP}} Although the term Manmer is archaic, modern druids embrace it.{{ref|name=ONHIDS}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} Bretons have a natural affinity for environmental manipulation,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|ns_base=Online}}}} which druids believe should be utilized to enhance or perfect nature, reshaping it just as Y'ffre once did.{{ref|name=ONHIDS}}{{ref|name=ONWD}}

Druids as a distinct identity emerged shortly after the Breton race first came into existence.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} This was further warranted when [[Lore:The Wyrd|the Wyrd]] diverged from the True Way in a religious schism, ultimately driven away by what they called the "excesses of the Elves".{{ref|name=ONWD}} In druidic belief, Bretons{{ref|name=ONWD}} (and others) were not meant to separate themselves from nature, as one can rejuvenate and enjoy Y'ffre's bounty through it.{{ref|[[ON:Druid Farel|Druid Farel]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|name=ESODR}} While ancient druids revered nature, they were not keen to isolate themselves and worship the untamed wilds.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}} Therein lies the primary difference in culture between druids and the Wyrd: the Wyrd abandoned civilization in favor of living in the wilds,{{ref|name=ONWD}} and believe that they are nature itself: the forest, the plants and beasts within it, and even the weather.{{ref|[[Online:Wyress Demara|Wyress Demara]] and [[Online:Wyress Linnae|Wyress Linnae]]'s dialogue in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} In contrast, the druids accepted what they interpreted as their "proper place" as people, and embraced the idea that civilization can coexist alongside nature.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=ESOWM|[[ON:Wyress Matilde|Wyress Matilde]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Sojourn of the Druid King|Sojourn of the Druid King]] in {{g|on|firesong}}}} The Druids of Galen and the Wyrd disapproved of each other's lifestyles: the druids found the ways of the Wyrd unsavory, and the Wyrd thought the druids as "barons in ivy robes" that were no better than city-dwellers.{{ref|name=ESOWM}} 

Druids would much later practice self-isolation to varying degrees,{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=DP|[[ON:Druid Peeska|Druid Peeska]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|name=ESOSSM|[[ON:Sir Stefan Mornard|Sir Stefan Mornard]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} as a response to the historical encroachments and heartless deprivations that would await them.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}} The Druids of Galen wanted self-governance without the Direnni's interference, as well as freedom from the religious persecution such as that from the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]],{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}} but ultimately, they were driven into exile to [[Lore:The Systres|the Systres]].{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=LATD}} The Druids of Galen were a united entity{{ref|name=ESOWM}} led by a line of ancient kings,{{ref|name=LABHI}} and they were already Bretons themselves by the time the [[Lore:Kasorayn|last Druid King]] led them on their exodus to find a new home.{{ref|name=LATD}} When they arrived on the Systres, they disbanded and reorganized themselves into fellowships referred to as druidic circles. Each circle interprets Y'ffre's will differently, while druids within the same fellowship share the same traditions and objectives.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{Ref|name=ESOLLB|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1604662466 Twitch Stream of ''ESO Live Legacy of the Bretons Autumn Event''], time stamp 59:10-59:26}} Xenophobia among the druidic circles varies in the modern day, with the Stonelore being welcoming and taking willing converts, and the Eldertide and Firesong being hostile.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=DP}} Druids in turn are treated as convenient scapegoats,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Druid Scapegoats}}}} and some devotees of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] may trivialize their religion as some woodland cult.{{ref|[[ON:Priest Listien|Priest Listien]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}}

==Disambiguation==
Druidism's practitioners are not to be confused with others that fall under the nature magic user archetype, such as [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmeri]] [[Lore:Greenspeakers|Greenspeakers]] and [[Lore:Spinners|Spinners]], [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachfolk]] [[Lore:Reachman Shamans|Shamans]], and the [[Lore:Warden|Wardens]].{{ref|name=LATD|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}}

The True Way is not the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]],{{ref|name=ESOEdaen|[[ON:Druid Edaen|Druid Edaen]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} a bargain Y'ffre struck with the Bosmer.{{ref|name=TOAF}} The two differ in that Wood Elves have many restrictions that druids do not have, such as them not being allowed to eat plant life, and not being allowed to shapeshift except through the ritual known as the [[Lore:Wild Hunt|Wild Hunt]].{{ref|name=TGPATD|{{Cite Book|The Green Pact and the Dominion}}}} In return for following the Green Pact, Bosmer can invoke the Wild Hunt in dire times, which irreversibly transforms the participants into a horde of feral and all-consuming eldritch beasts, which go on to lay waste to the enemies of the Bosmer, and then itself.{{ref|name=VAS|{{Cite Book|Valenwood: A Study}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 4}}}}

Certain aspects of the True Way share similarities with the religious practices of the Reachmen, but they differ in implementation.{{ref|name=ONNighthunterCowl}}{{ref|name=ONKEM|[[ON:Kemshelar|Kemshelar]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Reachfolk are known to build relationships with spirits big and small, but they lean more on negotiation rather than on reverence.{{ref|name=SU|[[ON:Shaman Ulvoch|Shaman Ulvoch]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|markarth}}}} Reachfolk also primarily worship the Daedric Princes as the "Great Spirits",{{ref|name=GSR2|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 2}}}} and their nature magic comes from sources such as [[Lore:Hagraven|Hagravens]],{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} [[Lore:Orcs|Orcs]],{{Ref|name=PGE1EHR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/High Rock}}}} or the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Princes]].{{ref|[[Online:Vernim Woods|Vernim Woods]] loading screen in {{g|on}}}}

Druids are speculated to be related to Wardens, although moreso in the heart rather than in the mind. Druids use the True Way to guide their magics, while Wardens feel Nirn calling to them in their gut.{{ref|name=LATD}}

Though the True Way is Breton in origin,{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} outsiders such as those of other races have become druids, in particular thanks to the Stonelore Circle, who are not hostile to people of other faiths, nor would they force their beliefs on others.{{ref|[[Online:Druid Phillic|Druid Phillic]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=DP}} One [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] became a druid of the Stonelore because they saw Y'ffre as a shepherd of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]'s will, as change adheres to natural laws.{{ref|name=DP}} Another example is a Bosmer of the Stonelore, who followed both Druidism and their race's Green Pact at the same time.{{ref|name=ESOEdaen}} The other two major druidic circles however do not see outsiders as druids.{{ref|name=ESOEdaen}}{{ref|name=DP}}{{ref|[[ON:Elder Pitof|Elder Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
====The Proto-Bretons of High Rock====
[[File:LO-map-High Rock (Anthology).jpg|left|thumb|High Rock, origin of the True Way]]
Druidic history is primarily oral history,{{ref|name=LADCG}} for many records have been lost, and druidkind has been nearly wiped out several times throughout history.{{ref|name=ESODL|[[ON:Druid Laurel|Druid Laurel]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}}{{ref|name=ONSHADG|{{Cite Book|Systres History Addendum: The Druids of Galen}}}} Naturally, the origin of Druidism is steeped in myth. Some say a Nedic figure known as Druid Queen Galen was one of the first to channel the songs of Y'ffre, and became the first Archdruid. Others believe the True Way emerged from an island named Galen in the middle of a lake, where the Breton's forebears heard Y'ffre's heart beating; yet in another, Galen was the name of a spirit that wandered into a proto-Breton settlement and taught them of the Green.{{ref|name=LADCG}} Y'ffre's name itself was not known until the "daughters and sons … [of] [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]]" taught it to the Breton's forebears.{{ref|name=ONWD}} Regardless of their origin, it is agreed that the True Way was born in High Rock.{{Ref|name=EOTD}}

Whether Man or Mer came to High Rock first is debated in scholarly circles. Some believe that Nedic civilization and their [[Lore:Druid King|Druid King]] were already established in the province when the Aldmer arrived,{{Ref|name=MDBMM|{{Cite Book|Modern Day Bretons: Man or Mer?}}}} while others claim that the Nedes arrived in High Rock afterwards, and encountered the elves gradually with a variety of reactions and outcomes.{{ref|name=FC}}{{ref|name=PGE3High}}{{Ref|name=LSP:OAC|group=UOL|{{TIL|Lawrence Schick and Phrastus on Altmer Culture|lawrence-schick-and-phrastus-altmer-culture-0}}}} There is evidence of human settlements in High Rock dating back at least a thousand years before recorded history.{{ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}} Archaeological excavations have dated the ruins of Nedic habitation to as early as {{Year|ME 1000}}, predating [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] by centuries.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}} The Aldmer that settled High Rock did so sparsely and in very specific locations, the most successful being Clan [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]],{{ref|name=PGE3High|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}} who came to the province from the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isles]] in the Middle Merethic Era.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}}

The Direnni didn't enslave the Nedes outright as the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] did; instead, they ruled them as a noble caste. The elves established a system of feudal vassalage over their new subjects and took Nedic concubines.{{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=Once}} They inevitably interbred with their human subjects, producing Half-Elven children (also known as Manmer).{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{ref|name=PGE1High}}

====Druids Arise as a Distinct Identity====
The Druids of Galen led the new race when it first emerged,{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL}} as children begat from Elf-Nede relations were considered sub-Mer and not accepted by their Direnni parents. Instead, they were given privileged positions among the Nedes, which eventually lead to a recognizable mixed-blood human caste dubbed "Bretons", from the Ehlnofex "beratu", meaning "half".{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=TBMOP}} These Bretons were only allowed to marry humans, and over time, the Breton's human features became more prominent than their elven traits.{{ref|name=TBMOP}}

[[File:ON-misc-Druid King Wyrd and Frii.png|thumb|right|Symbols representing the Druid King (top), the Beldama Wyrd (left), the Glenmoril Wyrd (right), and Frii (center)]]
Druid as a distinct identity was created shortly after the Bretons first came into existence.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} This was justified further when sisterhoods repulsed by the Direnni became the Wyrd when they retreated into isolation, and embraced the more primal aspects of nature.{{ref|name=ONWD}} The Direnni, seeking to tame nature, forged a pact with the Earthbones to protect their settlements from the encroachment of the wilds.{{ref|name=TVS|{{Cite Book|The Viridian Sentinel}}}} Ancient druids were considered "Meriphilic",{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} but eventually their tolerance for outside influence would wane, and they would find themselves fighting for the soul of High Rock.{{ref|name=ESODR}} The Druids of Galen believed civilization should live alongside nature, not tame it,{{ref|name=ESODR}} and they attempted to hold onto their power with fearsome magic, either behind the scenes or directly.{{ref|name=ESOD|[[ON:High King Emeric|High King Emeric]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}}  

===First Era===
Ultimately, the Druids of Galen became the split from Bretondom around {{Year|1E 330}}, when two major powers vied for control of High Rock. The Direnni Hegemony did not take kindly to the attempts for druidic governance of the province, and the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] persecuted them as they deviated from their religion.{{ref|name=ONWD}} Following the guidance of Y'ffre's visions{{Ref|name=EOTD}} (or a song, or an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]]),{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} [[Lore:Kasorayn|Druid King Kasorayn]] led somewhere between an estimated few thousand to a million druids on an exodus to the Systres,{{Ref|name=EOTD}}{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} and a few remained on the mainland.{{ref|name=ESODR}} It is said that for their exodus, Kasorayn received a vision from Y'ffre which taught him how to sing the plants and trees of High Rock into seaworthy vessels, and he in turn taught the druids how to do so.{{Ref|name=EOTD}}

====Post-Circle Druids====
[[File:ON-misc-Druidic Knotwork Face Tattoos.png|thumb|left|Triquetra Tattoo (around forehead)|class=backdrop]]
The surviving vessels eventually made it to the Systres,{{Ref|name=EOTD}} but sources conflict on whether the island of [[Lore:Galen|Galen]] or [[Lore:Y'ffelon|Y'ffelon]] was the site of the Druids of Galen's first landing.{{ref|name=ONSHADG}}{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} Regardless, the Draoife terraformed Y'ffelon from a barren volcanic island into a lush and verdant volcanic paradise,{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} and then the druids swiftly expanded across the rest of the archipelago,{{Ref|name=SoA|{{Cite Book|Secrets of Amenos}}}} sprouting life where it was once barren.{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} The dissolution of the Druids of Galen was brought about with the event known as the "three mornings of sail", when priest-navigators set out to sea on enchanted vessels outside the archipelago. Their outside experiences and their interactions with different races resulted in three sects developing, each with their own interpretation of Y'ffre's will; the Stonelore, Eldertide, and Firesong Circles.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} Other sources claim Kasorayn caused the druidic schism when he foresaw a coming disaster in a prophecy now known as the [[Lore:Dream of Kasorayn|Dream of Kasorayn]].{{ref|name=ESODL}} 

Before the Druids of Galen ventured into the Systres, Druid King [[Lore:Kasorayn|Kasorayn]] visited both the Beldama{{Ref|name=LDA|{{Cite Book|Logbook of Druid Anwas}}}} and Glenmoril Wyrd Covens in preparation.{{ref|name=ESODL}} Together, they nurtured the nature spirit known as Frii from the dream of the Green.{{ref|name=ESOFrii|[[ON:Frii|Frii]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}}{{ref|name=ONSDK|[[Online:Sojourn of the Druid King|Sojourn of the Druid King]] quest in {{g|on|firesong}}}} Frii was the Sower, the entity that was to create sacred seeds which were to contain the regalia of the Druid King, royal symbols to be passed onto the future druidic circles.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}} Despite the religious schism, to symbolize their unity matters more and that they share the same goals, the druidic circles came to shaping knot-work into a [[Lore:Triquetra|triquetra]],{{ref|[[Online:Druidic Knotwork Body Tattoos|Druidic Knotwork Body Tattoos]] body markings description in {{g|on}}}} a symbol that would become adopted by civilized Bretons.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 93: Ancestral Breton Style}}}} 

[[File:ON-trailer-Firesong-Kasorayn.jpg|thumb|right|Druid King Kasorayn addressing the Druidic Circles]]
The druidic monarchy ended after heretics of the Allwither Order murdered the Druid King in their attempt to seize the Ivy Throne.{{ref|name=ESODruidKing|[[ON:Druid King Kasorayn|Druid King Kasorayn]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} It is speculated that they wanted to go back to High Rock to enact revenge on their former Elven oppressors.{{ref|name=ONSHADG}} Before Kasorayn succumbed to his wounds, he reverted his regalia into seeds. The crown was given to the Eldertide, the gauntlet to the Firesong, and the staff to the Stonelore Circle for safekeeping until a new Druid King could rise. He then ordered those still loyal to him to rally against the Allwither Cult.{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}} 

The druids were left with the Dream of Kasorayn detailing two possible futures, dependent on the morality of the next Druid King. When the druidic circles are reunited, they may bring the Green Renewal (also known as the Green Reward), in which they would teach everyone to live in harmony with nature. The other possibility was the Green Scourge, in which nature would run amok among the tyranny of the druid.{{ref|name=ESODL}} After Kasorayn's death, the regal symbology of the Druid Kings disappeared almost overnight.{{ref|name=LABHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}}{{ref|name=HIASCE|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Stonelore Circle Effigy|Stonelore Circle Effigy]] codex entries in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Filling the leadership role afterwards was the Draoife, which consists of some of the most influential members of each druidic circle. They handle the leadership role for modern druids, settling disputes and handling concerns.{{ref|name=ESODL}}

Eventually, the differences in beliefs of the three major druidic circles nearly led to conflict. This however was stopped by the appearance of a common enemy, the [[Lore:Sinistral Mer|Sinistral Elves]], who arrived at High Isle to flee the latest wave of [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudan]] immigrants. Their invasion was temporarily halted when Y'ffelon's volcano of Mount Firesong erupted somewhere around {{Year|1E 668}}. Regardless, the Sinistral Elves took over the Systres, and the druidic circles were forced into hiding. With the coming of the Ra Gada in {{Year|1E 785}}, what remained of the Elves were purged, and the Yokudans quickly left the island to go eastward, seeking glory in Hammerfell. In the end, only the druids remained, a mere shell of what they once were.{{ref|name=ONSHV2|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 2}}}}

====The All Flags Navy====
Seeking vengeance for Tamriel for the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] that was unleashed by the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]], the Colovian King of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]], [[Lore:Bendu Olo|Bendu Olo]], was granted an Imperial fleet by the emperor of the Alessian Empire,{{ref|name=ONSHV3|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 3}}}} and looked to other races to bolster the size of the fleet which would become known as the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]].{{ref|name=JOTE|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}} Sometime around {{Year|1E 2243}}, the Navy arrived in the Systres Archipelago, where they established a naval base and shipyard within High Isle's Gonfalon Bay, which dwarfed others created for this conflict.{{ref|name=ONSHV3}} Hundreds of members of all three major druidic circles were recruited to join the All Flags Navy.{{ref|name=SHV}} After various skirmishes evolved into the eventual final assault on [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] in {{Year|1E 2260}}. The druids acted as weather-soothers and wayfinders on the way to Thras{{ref|name=ONSHADG}} and the entire landmass was sunk into the ocean, with half of the fleet being caught within the giant whirlpool.{{ref|name=ONSHV3}} Some credit the druids as playing a part in this devastation, though that is speculation on a few records.{{ref|name=ONSHADG}} The remaining influential figures returned to the Systres, where these representatives of each race signed the Concordat of Fraternity on All Flags Islet, in hopes to bring about peace and cooperation,{{ref|name=ONSHV3}} and druids healed the wounded.{{ref|name=ONSHADG}}

In {{Year|1E 2484}}, Mount Firesong erupted once more, devastating the island and killing many of the inhabitants. What changed was a shift of political power, for those in the lower classes were most affected by the starvation and illnesses. What followed was a renaissance of Druidism, for it was the Stonelore Circle that pulled the city folk out of the brink of death, helping them in the creation sprawling pastoral and agricultural operations. This time period was referred to as the Green Years, and druids earned a place of respect.{{ref|name=ONSHV5|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 5}}}} Circa {{Year|1E 2704}}, the Green Years were swiftly ended when the [[Lore:Reman Empire|Reman Empire]] reclaimed the archipelago, and reinstalled Breton coin-barons as the rulers.{{ref|name=ONSHV6|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 6}}}}

===Second Era===
It is unknown what happened to the few Druids of Galen that remained on the mainland instead of following Kasorayn's exodus, but in Stonelore tales, these forsaken also split into circles and roamed Tamriel's forests executing Y'ffre's will.{{ref|name=ESODR}} By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], druids had been absent in High Rock for centuries.{{ref|name=ESOWM}} While the druids of old were gone, the Systrean druids during this time period still considered themselves to be the Druids of Galen at least in name, in that the title is special and conveys "solace, home and hearth, reverence and conservation".{{ref|name=LADCG}}

The Stonelore druids who inhabited the [[Lore:Systres|Systres]] investigated and attempted to shut down volatile volcanic fissures that had begun to plague the islands of the Systres circa 2E 582 {{ref|name=VV|[[ON:Volcanic Vents|Volcanic Vents]] in {{g|on|highisle}} and {{g|on|firesong}}}} Around this time, the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] sought to research druidic magic. The volatile magic of the Eldertide was prioritized.{{ref|[[Online:Druidic Research|Druidic Research]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}}

The Ascendant Order was a pan-Tamrielic movement created to bring the rule of tyrants to an end.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character(s) - The Ascendant Lord and Magus}}}} Terrorist attacks using druidic magic were used during their operations in the Systres.{{ref|name=ONPOT}} A faction within the Eldertide Circle joined the Ascendant Order during their assault on the All Flags Islet.{{ref|[[Online:The Ascendant Storm|The Ascendant Storm]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}

The Firesong Circle wished to bring about the revival of the Druids of Galen by reuniting the druid circles{{Ref|name=ESOLLB|group=UOL}}{{ref|name=ESOYoutubeFiresongTour|group=UOL|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JhQeU-Izx1w YouTube Stream of ''Firesong DLC Guided Tour with the Developers''], time stamp 25:33-26:08}} and crowning a new Druid King. Circa 2E 582, they assassinated the Draoife when they refused to elect a new Druid King, and made moves to obtain the other circle's seeds. The leader of the Firesong was disposed of, but the puppeteer and leader of the Ascendant Order, Lord Bacaro Volorus, took matters into his own hands and claimed the Ivy Throne. Bacaro's reign was swiftly put to an end by the [[Lore:Vestige|Vestige]], and thus the Green Scourge was prevented.{{ref|[[ON:The Ivy Throne|The Ivy Throne]] quest in {{g|on|firesong}}}}

Count Stefan Mornard subsequently sought to give the druid circles a voice in governing the island of Galen, believing this would give way to a path that would combine both legacies into something new. He felt that the druids don't require a king or a prophecy to make the world a better place, and that House Mornard and the druids had sought to reclaim the glory of the past for too long.{{ref|name=ESOSSM}} Indeed, the Druids of Galen may not be resurrected, for Kasorayn's dream may simply be a metaphor. However, Druid Laurel of the Stonelore Circle believed that druidkind should live life as though the event is close at hand.{{ref|name=ESODL}} She believed that druidism may once again have a future on mainland Tamriel in years to come, stemming from an increase in travel between Galen and the mainland at that time.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Druid Circles of Galen}}}}

==Practices==
Druids are known to consort with nature spirits who are tied to the environment around them, but they are as unpredictable as nature itself.{{Ref|name=ONS|{{Cite Book|On Nature Spirits}}}} Friendships can be established with spirits, or they can also be coerced into doing one's bidding,{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Only Record of Our Plan|ns_base=Online}}}} but this is frowned upon by some druids.{{Ref|name=ONS}} Druids can also communicate with{{ref|name=ONDJ}}{{ref|[[ON:Druid Erold|Druid Erold]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} and form cordial relationships with animals, who can serve as intermediaries between druids and spirits.{{ref|[[Online:Wisdom of the Druids|Wisdom of the Druids]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Animals can serve as mounts and guardians,{{ref|[[Online:Glimmertarn Ursauk|Glimmertarn Ursauk]] mount description in {{g|on}}}} and there are some instances where spirits can take the form of animals.{{ref|[[Online:Oldefire Elk|Oldefire Elk]] mount description in {{g|on}}}} Like a song or tale can be retold, druids utilize shape magic, drawing from the Singer's power to transform into various animals. This differs from the shapeshifting of the Glenmoril Wyrd, who instead look to Hircine for this gift.{{ref|name=LADCG}}

Druids believe in a natural cycle of death and rebirth,{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}}{{Ref|name=ONDFPY|{{Cite Book|Druid Funerals: A Piece of Y'ffre}}}} for Y'ffre blesses the living with the same breath that flows across the world, and it is seen as a blessing for him to withdraw that breath. To die means that one once lived, and at the end there is always new beginnings.{{Ref|name=ONDFPY}} Indeed, druids believe in reincarnation. [[Lore:Dovah-Fly|dovah-flies]] are such an example, and they're believed to be their ancestors given new life.{{ref|[[Online:Glimmergreen Dovah-Fly|Glimmergreen Dovah-Fly]] pet description in {{g|on}}}} The corpse that is left behind also plays a part into natural cycle by returning to the living, whether by serving as food for an animal, fertilizing the forest, or having a body part dried and worn in memoriam as pendant.{{Ref|name=ONDFPY}} Some among the Druids of Galen practiced [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]] as a legitimate venue of faith, as it is focused on the end of Y'ffre's blessing of life.{{Ref|name=LDA}} [[Lore:Soulrazer Knight|Soulrazer Knights]] are animated suits of armor powered with necromantic energies with various theories on what created them. One of many is the druids created them.{{ref|name=ONTSK|{{Cite Book|Theories: Soulrazer Knights}}}} In the Iliac Bay, druids are responsible for bringing the bodies of the departed to their final resting place in the crypts.{{ref|name=DFDeath}}{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL}}

Druids are known to engage in ritualistic sacrifices. The Firesong Circle examines the entrails of sacrificial animals to interpret omens.{{ref|Meaning of Haruspex from the [[ON:Druid King#Firesong Haruspex|Firesong Haruspex]] [[ON:Tales of Tribute|Tales of Tribute]] card in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Others have a gift to predict the future through songs sung by Y'ffre which are hard for mortal minds to understand.{{ref|name=LADCG}} The Druids of Galen pulled the [[Lore:Chimera|chimeras]] from myth and gave them form through [[Lore:Blood Magic|Blood Magic]] in honor and sacrifice to Y'ffre.{{ref|name=LADCG}} The Eldertide Circle is able to create the [[Lore:Reefborn|Reefborn]], creatures of [[Lore:Coral|coral]] that mimic Nirn's creatures, a process that requires living sacrifices.{{ref|name=ESOCC|[[Online:Coral Conundrum|Coral Conundrum]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} [[Lore:Moth#Phoenix Moth|Phoenix Moths]] are another creature attributed to being created by druids.{{Ref|name=BGTGB|{{Cite Book|Bolga's Guide to Galen Beasts}}}} They are creatures native to Systres island of Galen, and are known for their regenerative powers and affinity with ash and fire.{{Ref|name=PM|[[ON:Phoenix Moth|Phoenix Moth]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DC|[[ON:Druid Calysa|Druid Calysa]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=BGTGB}} [[Lore:Forest Wraith|Forest Wraiths]] are the embodiment of death and evil nature spirits.{{ref|group=UOL|https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1604662466 Twitch Stream of ''ESO Live Legacy of the Bretons Autumn Event'', time stamp 1:04:46-1:05:10}}{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong2|group=UOL|[[General:Firesong_DLC_%26_Update_36_Preview#Forest_Wraith|Forest Wraith]] section in [[General:Firesong DLC & Update 36 Preview|Firesong DLC & specters.{{ref|name=DG|[[ON:Druid Gastoc|Druid Gastoc]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some however are forced to undergo the process unwillingly.{{ref|[[Online:A Heart Turned Black|A Heart Turned Black]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Totems and fetishes are used in druidic rituals,{{ref|name=ESODL}} tanna leaves are considered sacred,{{ref|[[Online:Valentyn Dantaine|Valentyn Dantaine]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} and Spriggan's Veils are branches that are sometimes shed by [[Lore:Spriggan|spriggans]], which are believed to have symbolic meaning in druidic practices.{{ref|name=Contraband S|[[ON:Contraband S#Spriggan's Veil|Spriggan's Veil]] item description in {{g|on}}}} One example of a druidic fertility relic had the power to encourage lovers to indulge in life's reward (the passion of sexual intimacy), and propagate like seeds.{{Ref|name=ONRLC|{{Cite Book|Ritual of Life's Commencement|ns_base=Online}}}} 

Druidic armorers, best described as plant-shapers, use their magic to improve their equipment's defensive and offensive traits. Their work is essentially vegetation carved and coaxed into intricate structures with near iron-like density.{{ref|[[Online:Y'ffre's Will Style|Y'ffre's Will Style]] motif description in [https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/63457 January 2023 Crown Store Showcase]}}{{ref|name=ONYWS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 115: Y'ffre's Will Style}}}} The wood used remains alive, aware, and unharmed. Leather is also used, but with a sense of loss and respect for the animal it was harvested from.{{ref|name=ONYWS}} The Firesong Circle don themselves with obsidian armor, but guard the secrets on how to shape this magma-born armor.{{ref|[[Online:Firesong Obsidian Mask|Firesong Obsidian Mask]] hat description in {{g|on}}}} While druids generally do not work with metals, when they do, metals like [[Lore:Bronze|bronze]] and [[Lore:Iron|iron]] are preferred. Polishing metal is out of the norm, for druids prefer their metals to tarnish naturally.{{Ref|name=MBBSCodex|[[Online:Music Box, Blessings of Stone|Music Box, Blessings of Stone]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Crow-feather fletching creates broadhead arrows, the feathers which are provided by birds that are convinced to provide their feathers by druids.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 114: Systres Guardian Style}}}} Druids bless tailoring fabrics,{{ref|[[ON:Alvaren Barthele|Alvaren Barthele]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} while some druids may braid their blessings and prayers into their leatherwork.{{ref|name=ONSWC}} Crafting volcanic jewelry is a druidic tradition, and these goods have been found exported outside the Systres.{{Ref|name=OSRCodex}}

Druids guard their secrets for their processes of cultivation and cross-pollination. Foods are imbued with flavor-enhancements and can invigorate one's vitality. Some have dabbled with creating vegetable hybrids, such as the Singer tuber, a violet-streaked cross of leek, carrot, and nirnroot, which is used in salads.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Druid Food of Vastyr}}}}

Druidic tattoos which can be quite colorful have been made out of mystic ink, and these markings can serve to respect, give in, or to placate nature.{{ref|[[ON:Touch of the Draoife Face Marks|Touch of the Draoife Face]] and [[ON:Touch of the Draoife Body Marks|Body Marks]] item descriptions in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Y'ffelon's Face Brand|Y'ffelon's Face]] and [[ON:Y'ffelon's Body Brand|Body Brand]] item descriptions in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Eldertide Face Ink|Eldertide Face]] and [[ON:Eldertide Body Ink|Body Ink]] item descriptions in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Touch of the Draoife Face Marks|Touch of the Draoife Face]] and [[ON:Touch of the Draoife Body Marks|Body Marks]] item descriptions in {{g|on}}}} Druids are known to go further by modifying their bodies in reverence of or to better understand nature. For example, in seeking to better understand the seasons, one can perform a ritual in oneself becomes rooted, taking in water and nutrients as a tree would.{{Ref|[[Online:Barkroot Blessing|Barkroot Blessing]] codex entries in {{g|on|firesong}}}} Others may infuse their very being with flame, becoming men of magma.{{Ref|[[Online:Magmaheart|Magmaheart]] skin description in {{g|on}}}} Druids seeking to dwell among aquatic wildlife may imitate their hues, taking on vibrant colors such as blues and greens.{{Ref|[[Online:Reefdweller|Reefdweller]] skin description in {{g|on}}}}

==Hierarchy==
Druids follow a hierarchy, but titles among the druids are earned and not something they are born into.{{ref|name=ONGTSC|{{Cite Book|Glimmertarn: A Stonelore Community}}}} At the top sits the Druid King (or Druid Queen){{ref|name=LADCG}} who holds the highest authority,{{ref|name=LABHI}} though the role has been vacant for thousands of years.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Dream of Kasorayn}}}} Archdruid was once used to refer to the Druid King,{{ref|name=HIASCE}}{{ref|name=LADCG}} but it took on a new meaning and became a title druids obtain through immense presence and power.{{ref|name=ESODR}} There are many archdruids per circle,{{ref|name=ESODR}} and they lead communities{{ref|name=ONGTSC}} and oversee lesser ranked druid's training in the True Way.{{ref|name=ONAA|[[ON:Archdruid Agathie|Archdruid Agathie]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Next are Elders, who serve as advisors for archdruids.{{ref|name=ONAA}} Full-fledged druids are those who have passed sacred tests, but before then they are considered initiates. The lowest rank are the aspirants, who are complete novices.{{ref|name=ONGTSC}}

The Draoife is the druidic council which consists of the most influential members of each druidic circle,{{ref|name=ESODL}} numbering three archdruids (one from each circle){{ref|name=ONDD|[[Online:Draoife Dell|Draoife Dell]] loading screen in {{g|on|firesong}}}} as well as Elders.{{ref|name=ESODL}} They handle the leadership role after the last Druid King's death by settling disputes, issuing proclamations for all circles, and declaring the beginning and ending of seasons.{{ref|name=ONDD}}{{ref|name=ESODL}} The Draoife is traditionally convened in [[Lore:Draoife Dell|Draoife Dell]].{{ref|name=ONDD}}{{ref|name=ASOT|[[ON:A Sea of Troubles|A Sea of Troubles]] quest in {{g|on|firesong}}}}

==Architecture==
Druids create settlements where manmade constructions intertwine with and honors nature,{{ref|name=ONGTSC}}{{ref|[[Online:Initiate Hinadin|Initiate Hinadin]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} echoing the druids of old's belief that civilization should coexist alongside nature and not tame it.{{ref|name=ESODR}} On the Systres Archipelago, druidic architecture is intended to invoke the feeling of a culture that has embraced nature yet is not primitive.{{ref|name=FDTZ|[[General:Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone|Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone]] on the official {{g|on}} website}} Druids are considered master stonecutters, and stone has a primacy within their creations. Druids build with multi-facetted stones which are then coated with clay from the banks of Y'ffre's Tears, an odoriferous oak resin.{{ref|name=ONHIDS}}{{Ref|name=OSRCodex}}{{Ref|name=MBBSCodex}} Their simple stacked stone huts have thatched roofs{{ref|name=FDTZ}} and are conical shaped to help regulate heat on hot days.{{Ref|name=MBBSCodex}} Druids build into the earth to maintain a deep connection to Nirn,{{Ref|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1572918818 Twitch Stream of ''Kai & Saint Crow visit the Lost Depths dungeons with the help of Lasuu & Vrayni''], time stamp 5:01:10-5:01:25}}{{ref|name=FDTZ}} and this can be seen with their stone huts flooring which are built below grade, requiring their stairs to lead down into it from the entrance of their domiciles.{{ref|Druidic architecture in [[Online:Stonelore Grove|Stonelore Grove]] in {{g|on}}}} More complex is their rock-cut architecture which is present on the Systres Archipelago's sandstone cliff faces, which they hollow out to live in.{{ref|name=FDTZ}} Windcatcher towers are present in their villages whose windowed openings can help funnel fresh air throughout them.{{Ref|name=MBBSCodex}}

The Wyrd gather around megalithic structures that are similar to Systrean druids. Additionally, both are known to dwell in stone huts.{{ref|Appearance of Wyrd architecture and Druidic architecture in {{g|on}}}} The Wyrd use different patterns of symbology from druids.{{ref|name=SCE|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Stonelore Circle Effigy|Stonelore Circle Effigy]] codex entries in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Whether the spiral patterns on druidic monoliths are purely aesthetic or hold religious significance is a matter of scholarly debate.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Druid Monoliths}}}} Patterns do however indicate things, with examples including fertility, flames, and spirits.{{ref|[[Online:Druidic Wall Stone, Fertility|Druidic Wall Stone, Fertility]], [[Online:Druidic Wall Stone, Flame|Flame]], and [[Online:Druidic Wall Stone, Spirits|Spirits]] in {{g|on}}}}

The city of Vastyr on the isle of Galen features of blend of druidic and city Breton architecture, thanks to the House Mornard's connections to Druidism.{{ref|name=SHV|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Vastyr}}}}

===Druid King's Regalia===
The Druid King's Regalia consists of a crown, a gauntlet, and a staff which were created by the god Y'ffre. These gifts were worn with wisdom and mercy by the line of Druid Kings,{{ref|name=ONEV|[[ON:Elder Verline|Elder Verline]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} and serve to legitimize one's ascension to the Ivy Throne.{{ref|name=ESOOrlaith|[[ON:Archdruid Orlaith|Archdruid Orlaith]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} After the death of Kasorayn, the last Druid King, they remained dormant within sacred seeds entrusted to the three main Druidic Circles.{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}} The regalia supposedly has no innate power of their own,{{ref|name=ESODR}} but after a pretender stole the Druid King's Regalia, they almost brought upon the Green Scourge which would have called forth the wrath of nature upon Tamriel but they were slain before being able to do so.{{ref|name=ESODL}}

===Oakensoul Ring===
The Oakensoul Ring is a ring showcasing druidic master craftsmanship with some Direnni influence. It represents the authority of the Draoife, and its power represents the might of the druids. It is estimated to have been forged at least sometime around the Twenty-Fourth Century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] for a figure of importance.

The artifact's power comes from the various components that make up the ring's anatomy. With Stonelore craftmentship, the stone shank frame was grinded into shape with Nedic-like stonework, but aesthetically it is Direnni-shaped. It is enchanted with protective energies, and its central grove is shaped to accommodate a second loop made out of petrified oak that radiates with magicka. This oaken loop is accented with enchanted igneous flakes representing the Firesong Circle, theorized to bring immovable strength. The ring's stones [[Lore:Larimar|larimar]] gems represents the Eldertide and contain draoifoglyphs--druidic runes. Lastly, Tears of Y'ffre were applied to the ring, used in druidic spellcraft.{{Ref|name=OSRCodex}}

==Religion==
[[File:ON-quest-Tournament of the Heart.jpg|thumb|right|The Ritual depicted on stone pillars]]
====[[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]]====
Y'ffre who is the Green-King, the Slumbering Father of Nirn,{{ref|name=ONWD}} the Green Man,{{Ref|name=DPS|[[ON:Druidic Provisioning Station#Druidic Kettle Spout|Druidic Kettle Spout]] codex entries in {{g|on|highisle}}}} the Singer,{{ref|name=LADCG}} the Storyteller, the God of Song and Forest, and the Spirit of the Now,{{ref|name=ESODA}} is the primary deity associated with the druids, and several druidic circles formed with different ways that they revere him. His existence was known, but his name was not known until the daughters and sons of [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] introduced his name.{{ref|name=ONWD}}

====[[Lore:Hircine|Elk of Arrows]]====
Druids worship Hircine as the Elk of Arrows. Speculation exists on his significance, with one theory that he represents the importance in nature of life and death between hunter and prey.{{ref|name=ONNighthunterCowl|[[Online:Nighthunter's Cowl|Nighthunter's Cowl]] hat description in {{g|on}}}} Druids also recognize Hircine as one of the gods of the ancient past, which they believe the Wyrd worship for his "beneficent mask".{{ref|name=LADCG}}

====[[Lore:Astronomy#The Ritual|The Ritual]]====
The druids of old praised the stars, the Ritual constellation above all.{{ref|name=ONWD}} As of {{Year|2E 582}} megaliths devoted to the Ritual,{{Ref|name=TOTH|[[Online:Tournament of the Heart|Tournament of the Heart]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} and charms devoted to the Constellation of Ritual could be found in the Systres, among the modern druidic circles. Seven green beads hung in the shape of the ritual constellations were presumably rubbed for good luck.{{ref|name=TRSC|[[ON:Contraband/Galen#The Ritual Star Charm|The Ritual Star Charm]] item description in {{g|on}}}}

====[[Lore:Una the Green Elk|Una the Green Elk]]====
Una the Green Elk is a lesser spirit worshipped by the Druids.{{ref|[[Online:Druidic Provisioning Station#Painted Elk Clay Pot|Painted Elk Clay Pot]] codex entries in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{Ref|name=Contraband|[[ON:Contraband U#Una the Green Elk Figurine|Una the Green Elk Figurine]] item description in {{g|on}}}}

[[File:ON-tribute-druid-Wispcaller Totem.png|The Wispcaller Totem|thumb|right]]
====The [[Lore:Evergrowth|Evergrowth]] and the [[Lore:Allwither|Allwither]]====
The Evergrowth and its darker aspect, the Allwither, are two spirits in the opposite side of the spectrum, a physical manifestation of the cycle of life.{{ref|name=ESODA}} Worshippers of the latter were responsible for the regicide of the last Druid King.{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}}

====[[Lore:Mara's Tear|Mara's Tear]] and [[Lore:Shandar's Sorrow|Shandar's Sorrow]]====
Mara's Tear and Shandar's Sorrow are the ancient Bretonic interpretations of the moons, and were originally mortal lovers in a forbidden relationship. After Shandar suffered a mortal wound defending his lover, the goddess Mara took the two mortals and placed them in the heavens so they could always be together, and the light of their love could shine upon the world as moonlight.{{ref|name=MT|{{Cite Book|Mara's Tear}}}} Ancient druids had Mara's Tear and Shandar's Sorrow as a witness to sanctify rituals.{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}}{{ref|[[ON:Elder Verline|Elder Verline]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}}

==Notes==
*Druids were first [[Arena:Races#Breton|mentioned]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|The Elder Scrolls: Arena]]'' when creating a Breton, with the line ''"Thy race is descended from the ancient Druids of Galen, quick witted and strong in the mystical arts. Thy folks are crafty and intelligent, a learned people who use their gifts to guide others to enlightenment..."''
**Druids were also [[Daggerfall:Breton|mentioned]] in ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'' via the Breton exclamation ''"By the ArchDruid"''. Druids were planned to be used in main installments of the series past Daggerfall, until the ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''-''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' writing team reformed their approach and did away with elements from ''[[wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons|Dungeons & Dragons]]''.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}
**Druid's last mention before they were expanded upon in ESO's [[Online:Legacy of the Bretons|Legacy of the Bretons]] story arc was in ''The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion'', with the [[Oblivion:Cowl of the Druid|Cowl of the Druid]] item.
*For druidic architecture, The Elder Scrolls Online developers were inspired by the [[wikipedia:Moai|Moai]], [[wikipedia:Olmecs|Olmecs]], and [[wikipedia:Stonehenge|Stonehenge]]. [[wikipedia:Rock-cut architecture|Rock-cut architecture]] came from ancient [[wikipedia:Turkey|Turkey]], such as the [[wikipedia:Lycia|Lycian tombs in Dalyan]] and [[wikipedia:Göreme Historical National Park|Göreme Historical National Park]].{{ref|name=FDTZ}}
*Unused dialogue with druids in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' mentions that some of their practices include conducting services in parks, preparing nature-worshipping disciples, and finding human sacrifices.{{Ref|name=AROccupations|[[Arena:Greetings#Generic NPCs Greetings|Occupations and their Descriptions]] — [https://www.imperial-library.info/content/exclamations exclamations] in {{g|ar}}}}
