# Lore:Clockwork Apostles

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Factions Trail}}[[File:ON-npc-Provost Varuni Arvel.jpg|thumb|right|Provost Varuni Arvel, a high-ranking Apostle]][[File:LG-cardart-Galyn the Shelterer.png|thumb|right|Galyn the Shelterer]]__NOTOC__</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] are devout followers of the Clockwork God, [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]. They reside in the Clockwork Basilica, located within the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]]'s Brass Fortress.{{ref|name=ItCA|{{Cite Book|Inveigling the Clockwork Apostles}}}} They differ from the faithful of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]].{{ref|name=LACW|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City‎}}}}

Those seeking to become an Apostle must possess great expertise in magic, machinery, or some other skill that they deem worthy. Apostles are known to replace their limbs with "perfect" clockwork parts designed by Sotha Sil. They consider this practice to be an expression of faith and reverence in their god as well as a means to improve upon what they consider to be flawed biological anatomy.{{ref|name=Motif|{{cite Book|Crafting Motif 56: Apostle Style}}}}{{Ref|name=ONFyr|[[ON:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{ref|name=twitch1|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live Lore of the Clockwork City|Lore of the Clockwork City]] on [[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}} An infrequently observed tradition among the Clockwork Apostles is to enshrine their amputated body parts after replacement.{{ref|name=ContHand|{{Item Link|itemid=132512|Bronzed Apostle Hand}} contraband item in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Apostles are often seen as elitist by outsiders, and they are known to delegate menial tasks to others.{{ref|name=ItCA}} They follow the [[Lore:The_Truth_in_Sequence|sermons of Deldrise Morvayn]] and devote their work towards one goal: achieving Tamriel Final, a new [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=TUP|{{Cite Book|The Unseen Potential of Clockwork}}}}{{ref|name=TIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}}<noinclude>

The highest-ranking Apostles form the Congress of Calibration, the governing body of the City.{{Ref|name=ONNeramo|[[ON:Neramo|Neramo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} The highest rank of the Apostles is the Tourbillon.{{ref|name=twitch1|group=UOL}}

The Clockwork Apostles' legal system is relatively rudimentary, mostly because it is used so rarely. Infractions, such as research sabotage or plagiarism, typically result in exile. For more serious crimes, Apostles use a method of punishment known as a cognitive prison &mdash; an induced coma where the condemned's mind is set to a recursive loop, trapping them in an inescapable mental space. By the mid-Second Era, the punishment had only been used twelve times.{{ref|name=ONPullo|[[Online:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Proctor Luciana Pullo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
Following the death of Sotha Sil, the Apostles were led by [[Lore:Galyn|Galyn the Shelterer]], who took it upon himself to keep the Clockwork City's inhabitants alive. With the [[Lore:Mechanical Heart|Mechanical Heart]] finished, the exiled Apostle [[Lore:Mecinar|Mecinar]] returned into the realm circa {{Year|4E 201}}, in an effort to claim it. This endeavor was a success, and Mecinar claimed the artifact's godly powers, and he began the process of converting the realm to his own image, turning the inhabitants into [[Lore:Abomination|Abominations]]. The tyrant was stopped when the [[Lore:Forgotten Hero|Forgotten Hero]] trailed him into the City, but stories differ from there. One rumor states that the Forgotten Hero destroyed the Heart, and the Clockwork City's inhabitants were forced to abandon it as it ceased to function.{{ref|name=LRTCCTCF|[[Legends:The City Falls|The City Falls]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}} Another account holds that the hero used the Heart to harness its power and fought Mecinar on equal ground, allowing the Heart to live on along with the city. The Heart's power overwhelmed the Hero and they disintegrated, it is claimed, but their consciousness lived on within the City. In this story, the Apostles remained in the realm.{{ref|name=LRTCCKJE|[[Legends:Journey's End|Journey's End]] in [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}{{ref|name=RCWC|Events of [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}

==The Truth In Sequence==
An interpretation of the teachings of Sotha Sil by the Clockwork Apostles existed within the text [[Lore:The Truth in Sequence|The Truth in Sequence]]. According to the text, the goal of Sotha Sil was the creation of a Second [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]—the Nirn-Ensuing—and a new Tamriel Final. This future world would be free of individual [[Lore:et'Ada|et'Ada]],{{Ref|name=TIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}} and the Clockwork City would serve as the "[[Lore:The Towers|center of the wheel]]."{{ref|name=TUP|{{Cite Book|The Unseen Potential of Clockwork}}}} The Apostles denied the existence of [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], asserting that there is only the unity of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]], rendered imperfect in Nirn Extant by its division into "multitudes." The [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], they claimed, were "of the Nothing," a mere "lie creation tells itself." The Apostles noted that, of all the Daedra, only the "Gray Prince of Order" knew his nature, and "[[Lore:Sheogorath|went mad]] in the knowing." The Clockwork City would free Anu of these multitudes by forging unity through convergence, synthesizing [[Lore:Mer|mer]] and machine, nature and engineering, and the past and the future. To the Apostles, the greatest threat to this project was the legacy of the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], whose "ghosts ... cry out, 'Multitudes!'" and, above all, their "walking horror" -- the brass god [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] -- described as an "Antipodal God-Thing" that "reigns over the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere," and the "welded knot at the center of Anu that must be untied."{{ref|name=TIS}} Indeed, in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the City's supervising Factotum, Aios, would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.{{ref|name=Aios|[[Online:Aios|Aios]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
