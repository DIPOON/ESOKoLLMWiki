Many more years passed in [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdaynia]], and the [[Lore:Kagrenac|high priests]] of the {{Lore Link|Dwemer}} were almost ready to make [[Lore:War of the First Council|war]] on the rulers of {{Lore Link|Chimer|Veloth}}. The [[Lore:Nerevar|Hortator]] had become the husband of {{Lore Link|Almalexia|Ayem}} during this time, and the first [[Lore:Tribunal Saints|saint of the Triune way]]. {{Lore Link|Vivec|MWlink=Morrowind:Vivec (god)}} had tired of fighting his sons and daughters, and so took a respite from trying to find them.

The Hortator said to his wife, {{nst|'|ON="}}Where is Vivec, my teacher? I love him still, though he grows cold. His lamentations, if I may call them that, have changed the skin of the whole {{Lore Link|Morrowind|country|MWlink=Lore:Morrowind}}. He is hardly to be found anywhere in Veloth of late. The people grow dark because of it.{{nst|'|ON="}}

And Ayem took mercy on her troubled husband and told him that the sword of the Triune had been fighting minor monsters stirred up by the Dwemer as they worked on their brass siege machines. She took the Hortator inside her and showed him where his master was.

[[Lore:Almsivi|ALMSIVI]], or at least that aspect that chose to be Vivec, sat in the Litany Hall of the {{Lore Link|Boethiah|False Thinking}} Temple after his battle with the Flute-and-Pipe {{Lore Link|Ogre}}s of the {{Lore Link|West Gash}}. He began writing, again, in his Book of Hours. He had to put on his Water Face first. That way he could separate the [[Lore:Chimer|bronze of the Old Temple]] from the {{Lore Link|Dunmer|blue of the New}} and write with happiness. Second, he had to take another feather from the [[Lore:Masser|Big Moon]], further rendering it dead. That way he could write about mortals with truth. Third, he recalled the {{Lore Link|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 12|Pomegranate Banquet}}, where he was forced to marry to {{Lore Link|Molag Bal}} with wet scriptures to cement his likeness as {{Lore Link|Mephala}} and write with black hands. He wrote:

The last time I heard his voice, showing the slightest sign of impatience, I learned to control myself and submit to the will of others. Afterwards, I dared to take on the sacred fire and realized there was no equilibrium with the [[Lore:Et'Ada|ET'ADA]]. They were liars, lost roots, and the most I can do is to be an interpreter into the rational. Even that fails the needs of the people. I sit on the mercy seat and pass judgment, the waking state, and the phase aspect of the innate urge. Only here can I doubt, in this book, written in water, broadened to include evil.

Then Vivec threw his ink on this passage to cover it up (for the lay reader) and wrote instead:

Find me in the blackened paper, unarmored, in final scenery. Truth is like my husband: instructed to smash, filled with procedure and noise, hammering, weighty, heaviness made schematic, lessons learned only by a mace. Let those that hear me then be buffeted, and let {{Lore Link|Dissident Priests|some die}} in the ash from the striking. Let those that find him find him murdered by illumination, pummeled like a {{Lore Link|House Dagoth|traitorous house}}, because, if an hour is golden, then immortal I am a secret code. I am the partaker of the [[Lore:Lorkhan|Doom Drum]], chosen of all those that dwell in the middle world to wear this crown, which reverberates with truth, and I am the mangling messiah.

The ending of the words is [[Lore:Almsivi|ALMSIVI]].
