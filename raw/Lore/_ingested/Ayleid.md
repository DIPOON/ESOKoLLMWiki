The [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]]s, also known as the '''Heartland High Elves''',{{ref|name=TAOK}} '''Wild Elves''', or '''Saliache''',{{ref|name=TOotO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}} were the first race to establish an empire in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]],{{ref|name=TWE|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves}}}} and had ruled modern-day [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] for countless years dating back to before recorded history.{{ref|name=TLKOTA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} They were thin and lean with pointed ears and angular facial features. Their complexion was lighter than that of the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], but they were darker than [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]];{{ref|name=2920L5}} their skin tone being a light tan to a dark bronze in color and their eyes ranging from white to turquoise.{{Ref|name=Models|Appearance of [[ON:Queen Palolel|Queen Palolel]], [[ON:Myndhal|Myndhal]], and various Ayleids}}

[[Lore:Ayleidoon|Ayleidoon]], their language,{{ref|name=TVC|{{Cite Book|The Vanishing Crux}}}}{{ref|name=TSOP|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal}}}} is similar to [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]], the ancient language of the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]].{{ref|name=ART}}{{ref|name=BJ2|{{Cite Book|Butcher Journal 2|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Ayleids were the original founders of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. They constructed the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], which they called the "Temple of the Ancestors", in emulation of the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]].{{ref|name=OBUmbacano|[[OB:Umbacano|Umbacano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=BOTZ|[[Online:Beacon of Tower Zero|Beacon of Tower Zero]] furnishing name, appearance and Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire]] collapsed in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] when it was overthrown by the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]].{{ref|name=TLKOTA}} While it's believed that the Ayleids remain in the wilds of every province on Tamriel, sightings have been few.{{ref|name=TWE}} There have been no known sightings in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third]] or [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]s.<noinclude>

==History==
===Merethic Era===
:''"Wear lore as your armor."''{{ref|name=ART}}[[File:LO-book-Before the Ages of Man.png|right|thumb|A depiction of the Aldmer with the White-Gold Tower in the Merethic Era]]
The Ayleids began as groups of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] who left the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] to create splinter cultures in central and southwest Tamriel.{{ref|name=DWTA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Ayleids}}}} They first left in the Middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], after the area had been charted by [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]] and others,{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3Sum|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}} In some cases, they left specifically to evade the strictures of Aldmeri regulation, which forbade (among many other things) the worship of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].{{ref|name=DWTA}} The Ayleids were in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] as early as the First Era,{{intnote|AyleidHR|[nb 1]}} and their ruins can be found throughout  the province, including the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] and as far as the island of [[Lore:Betony|Betony]].{{ref|name=Enduum|[[Online:Enduum|Enduum]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ARLIESO|[[Online:Ayleid Ruins|Ayleid ruin]] locations in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  

Though they settled predominantly in Cyrodiil, the [[Lore:Barsaebic Ayleids|Barsaebic Ayleids]] also had settlements in modern-day [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]. The Ayleid rulers of Black Marsh were renowned as necromancers and titled the Fenlords.{{ref|name=Zahra|[[Online:Zahra|Zahra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The cities [[Lore:Gideon|Gideon]] and [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] were originally Ayleid settlements whose names have been lost to history.{{ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Argonia}}}}{{ref|name=Loriasel|[[Online:Loriasel|Loriasel]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The earliest Aldmeri account of the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]] of Cyrodiil from Topal the Pilot speaks of friendly feathered [[Lore:Bird Men|Bird Men]] who resided there,{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} but these natives disappeared from history, only to be replaced by the Ayleids. It is also alleged that the Bird Men were enslaved by the Ayleids.{{Ref|name=OBDecentius|[[OB:Decentius Opsius|Decentius Opsius]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Far away from [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]], Ayleidic culture flourished. In the last millennium of the Merethic Era, Daedra worship took hold and spread among the Heartland High Elves. The cults devoted to the various [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] which had sprung up across Cyrodiil, once merely tolerated, became celebrated.{{ref|name=DWTA}} Unlike the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] who left Summerset in the Late Middle Merethic Era,{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=LOTS|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Saints}}}} the Ayleids made no distinction between "good" and "bad" Daedra. Even some of the more heinous Princes received mass veneration, especially when their worship was adopted and endorsed by Ayleid kings and aristocrats.{{ref|name=DWTA}}

The Ayleids made deals with Daedra for more power, blessings, and other advantages.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}{{ref|name=TOAC|{{Cite Book|Treatise on Ayleidic Cities}}}} They employed entire armies of Daedra to conquer, subjugate, and enforce their rule.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}}{{ref|name=COTF|{{Cite Book|Cleansing of the Fane}}}} In the earliest Cyro-Nordic stories, [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] fought against the Ayleids on mankind's behalf, then vanished, presumably to go help humans elsewhere. Without his leadership, the Cyrodiils were dominated and enslaved by the Ayleids.{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} At first this enslavement of the [[Lore:Nede|Nedic peoples]] was occasional, but eventually this became a systematic, widespread institution of Ayleid society.{{ref|name=DWTA}} They eventually controlled the entirety of modern-day Cyrodiil, and kept the Nedic peoples there enslaved for generations.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}} The Ayleids are also said to have enslaved beastfolk.{{ref|name=OBDecentius}}

[[File:ON-npc-Master Stormwarden Faranwe.jpg|Master Stormwarden of Ilayas|left|thumb]]
The fortress of [[Lore:Ilayas|Ilayas]] was built on a seaside hill of [[Lore:Malabal Tor|Malabal Tor]], in the province of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], many years into the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], and it was also active during the golden years of the Ayleid Empire, roughly between {{Year|ME 850}} and {{Year|ME 900}}. The Ayleid lords of Ilayas were part of an ancient order of lightning wizards called the [[Lore:Stormwardens|Stormwardens]], who defended the coast from foreign invasion and operated an [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] artifact called the [[Lore:Sphere of Storms|Sphere of Storms]], a potent orb that utilizes [[Lore:Weather Magic|weather magic]] to manipulate the waves of the coast and supply water for crops.{{Ref|name=ONCirnean|[[Online:Stormwarden Cirnean|Stormwarden Cirnean]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], at the height of Ayleid rule, Abagarlas was a wealthy city which prospered on account of its large population of mercenaries.{{ref|name=TWOD}} Located close by was the city of Delodiil, which was home to a glorious temple dedicated to the worship of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], the Lady of Light. Growing envious of Delodiil's temple, King [[Lore:Anumaril|Anumaril]] of Abagarlas ordered the construction of a far more massive temple dedicated to Molag Bal. Although Anumaril's temple dwarfed Delodiil's, its design was crude and aesthetically displeasing, leaving Delodiil's people, including their king, [[Lore:Cenedelin|Cenedelin]], unimpressed. Hearing of Delodiil's dismissal of his temple enraged King Anumaril, and after profaning Abagarlas' small shrine to Meridia, he ordered the destruction of Delodiil and proclaimed that he would sacrifice its populace to Molag Bal.{{ref|name=TWOD}} Anumaril, calling upon his dark master, was granted an army of undead and the knowledge to construct a weapon known as the [[Lore:Mortuum Vivicus|Mortuum Vivicus]], which would drain all life from Delodiil, and convert its citizens to undead servants of Molag Bal.{{ref|name=CJ|{{Cite Book|Curano's Journal}}}} Meridia, knowing of Molag Bal and Anumaril's twisted plans, warned her subjects in Delodiil, and in response a small group of priests were dispatched to Abagarlas to destroy the weapon. Upon reaching Abagarlas the priests tore a bloody swath through the city, putting both Anumaril's wife Queen Palolel and his son Prince Malyon to the sword. Soon, the priests successfully reached the Mortuum Vivicus, in time to stop the contraption's activation. However, Molag Bal pulled the device into [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] before it could be entirely destroyed. After the priests fled the city, undead in tow, Meridia completely destroyed the city through divine lightning.{{ref|name=LJ|{{Cite Book|Lanath's Journal}}}}

The [[Lore:Narfinsel Schism|Narfinsel Schism]] was a civil conflict that spanned the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Late Merethic Era|Late Merethic Era]] to {{Year|1E 198}}. The war was held between the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-worshipping Ayleids of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The war's conclusion, brought in by the Scouring of [[Lore:Wendelbek|Wendelbek]], marked the end of any organized resistance to Daedra-worship in Cyrodiil. The lasting effects of the Schism were theorized to have greatly contributed to the fall of the Ayleid Empire and the White-Gold Tower, nearly half a century later.{{ref|name=ASIV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Survivals in Valenwood}}}}{{ref|name=CAATAYQ|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions}}}}

===First Era===
[[File:LO-misc-Blades Mural.png|right|thumb|Celemaril's rise and fall]]
In the First Era, together with his undead forces, the immortal [[Lore:Celemaril Light-Bringer|Celemaril Light-Bringer]] waged war across Tamriel.{{ref|name=Telarendil|[[Blades:Telarendil|Telarendil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} His brother Valentis led other Ayleid kings in a rebellion against Celemaril. With [[Lore:Varla Stone|varla stones]] and an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]], Valentis donned Celemaril's crown and spoke a ritual incantation, sealing his brother within a crypt beneath a [[Lore:Marble|marble]] statue bearing his likeness.{{ref|[[:File:BL-quest-A Tale of Two Brothers.jpg|The mural]] in Valentis' tomb during [[Blades:A Tale of Two Brothers|A Tale of Two Brothers]] quest in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} As king, Valentis would then erase much of the records of Celemaril and his conquest.{{ref|name=Telarendil}} Valentis also founded the town of [[Lore:Rivercrest|Rivercrest]] around his statue which sealed Celemaril.{{ref|[[Blades:Founder's Statue|Founder's Statue]] decoration description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

[[File:ON-interior-Vakka-Bok Xanmeer Golden Path.jpg|right|thumb|Ayleid nexus gates in Eld Angavar]]
At some point prior to the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]], a clan of Barsaebic Ayleids led by an Ayleid mage known as Myndhal invaded the region of Murkmire in southern [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] in order to steal the souls of the native [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] The Ayleids launched a series of attacks on the [[Lore:Root-Whisper Tribe|Root-Whisper Tribe]]. The tribe was unable to resist the invaders, forcing the tribe's [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] tree to take a drastic measure: the creation of a "seed", an amber gem which would later be known as the [[Lore:Remnant of Argon|Remnant of Argon]], which was capable of holding the souls of the tribe, preventing the Ayleids from harvesting them. In this way, the Hist planned to preserve its tribe and become dormant, only to be awakened when the Ayleid threat to Murkmire was ended.{{ref|[[ON:Sap-Speaker Kuzei|Sap-Speaker Kuzei]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}}{{ref|name=ESODreaming|[[ON:Death and Dreaming|Death and Dreaming]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} Upon learning of the Remnant's creation, and having failed to secure it during its formation, Myndhal attempted to locate it by any means necessary in order to make use of its power. An Ayleid mage named Houtern began to construct [[Lore:Welkynd Stone|Welkynd]]-powered Nexus Gates that all connected to [[Lore:Eld Angavar|Eld Angavar]] to traverse Murkmire in search of the Remnant. Over time, this resulted in the creation of a "golden path", a sea of azure gates linking to one another within an otherwise-empty void. However, the Ayleids were ultimately unsuccessful in their search, and were forced to turn to Argonian technology to locate the Remnant.{{ref|name=ESORemnant|[[ON:The Remnant of Argon (quest)|The Remnant of Argon]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}}{{ref|name=Myndhal|[[ON:Myndhal|Myndhal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} Through the use of Argonian [[Lore:Vakka Stone|vakka stones]], Myndhal theorized that he could create a link to wherever the Hist had hidden the Remnant, and swore to find a way to it even if it would take "hundreds of years". In {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] traversed Eld Angavar and killed Myndhal, who had become undead and seemed to still be working on a way to uncover the Remnant.{{ref|name=ESORemnant}}

====Alessian Slave Rebellion====
The example of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s to the north inspired the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] of {{Year|1E 242}}.{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} Alessia's rebellion coincided with a civil war within the Ayleid Empire which led to many rebel Ayleid lords joining forces with Alessia and aiding her rebellion.{{ref|name=TLKOTA}}{{ref|name=OBHerminia|[[OB:Herminia Cinna|Herminia Cinna]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} These allies of Alessia were largely Aedra-worshippers,{{ref|name=DWTA}} indicating that the schism among the Ayleids was likely related to religious differences.

[[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], of course, lent help to their enslaved relatives under the Ayleid Empire, which also played a part in the Ayleids being overthrown.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3Sky|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} In only a year, the Slave Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] and her supporters had taken the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], founded the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], and secured the subsequent shift in power from mer to men on Tamriel.{{ref|name=FC}}{{ref|name=PGE3C}}

Sometime after the Alessian Slave Rebellion,{{Ref|name=FBJ|{{Cite Book|Frostbitten Journal}}}} various Ayleid kingdoms launched a desperate search for the fabled [[Lore:Wrathstone|Wrathstone]], a weapon that they believed would be their salvation against the encroaching forces of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]]. An expedition marched to the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] city of [[Lore:Mzulft|Mzulft]], in the hold of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where it was rumored the Wrathstone was held. The Ayleids besieged Mzulft, but failed to breach it or retrieve the artifact.{{Ref|name=IPOM|{{Cite Book|In Pursuit of Mhuvnak}}}} At the time, the Ayleids were unaware that the Dwemer only possessed the left half of the Wrathstone, and that half was stored not at Mzulft, but at nearby [[Lore:Kagrenzel|Kagrenzel]].{{Ref|name=IPOM}} After the failure of the raid on Mzulft, [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]] appeared to her faithful with a cryptic warning that the Ayleid Empire would soon fall, and entrusted the right half of the Wrathstone to Garlas Malatar and King Narilmor. To keep his piece of the Wrathstone safe,{{Ref|name=FBJ}} Narilmor denied both aid and asylum to the desperate Ayleid refugees from other kingdoms, and locked them out of his city.{{ref|name=MTCKN|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - King Narilmor}}}} The inhabitants of Garlas Malatar were not heard of again. Although Garlas Malatar was eventually besieged by [[Lore:Men|Men]], some accounts have suggested that the attackers did not even make it inside Narilmor's city before it collapsed into ruin; it was speculated that the city fell from within, brought down by internal conflict or sabotage.{{ref|name=MTCKN}}

====The Late Ayleid Period====
:''"Our exiled Elven ancestors heard the welcoming gifts of peace in the streams and beech trees and stars."''{{ref|name=ART}}
The Alessian Slave Rebellion was devastating to the Ayleids. Legend says entire settlements were slaughtered at the hands of Alessia's champion, [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]].{{ref|name=TSOP}} Though the Ayleids would continue to have a presence in Cyrodiil for several hundred years, the fall of the White-Gold Tower in 1E 243 signaled the beginning of the Late Ayleid Period and the start of the [[Lore:Ayleid Diaspora|Ayleid Diaspora]]. The Temple of the Ancestors was turned into the Imperial Palace by Alessia and her descendants, and it remains so to this day. The [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] region was one of the very last areas to be liberated by the Alessian army. It took Alessia's forces, led by Bravillius Tasus, four attempts to finally defeat them.{{ref|name=DOTN}} In these early years, the Alessian Empire forbade the worship of the Daedric Princes and focused on hunting down and exterminating Daedra-worshipping Ayleids.{{ref|name=DWTA}}

Ayleidic culture lingered in the Aedra-worshipping city-states that survived as client kingdoms of Alessian Cyrodiil, but resentment at the continued presence of Ayleid nobles within the Empire was a contributing factor to the rise of the so-called [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] founded by [[Lore:Marukh|Marukh]]. In {{Year|1E 361}}, the Alessians gained control of the Empire and enforced the Alessian Doctrines throughout its domain. The Ayleid lordships were abolished. Enforcement of this decree does not appear to have required much direct violence &mdash; it seems that by this point the balance of power was so overwhelmingly against them, and their fate so long foreshadowed, that most of the remaining Ayleids simply left Cyrodiil to join the Ayleid Diaspora, eventually being absorbed into the elven populations of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|name=TLKOTA}}{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}} Some Ayleid clans were known to have settled in the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{ref|[[ON:Thukhozod's Sanctum|Thukhozod's Sanctum]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} It's speculated that the exodus of the Ayleids from Cyrodiil may have vastly strengthened the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni Hegemony]] in High Rock - in which case, the Ayleids had their revenge, as the war with the Direnni eventually crippled the Alessian Empire and brought about its fall.{{ref|name=TLKOTA}}{{ref|name=DRD|{{Cite Book|De Rerum Dirennis}}}}

The only known Ayleids who remained in Cyrodiil served as mentors and tutors for the new human nobility.{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}} 
[[File:ON-place-Halcyon Lake.jpg|right|thumb|Ruins of Bisnensel, a city founded in High Rock by King Dynar]]

In the years {{Year|1E 372}}-{{Year|1E 374|374}}, King Dynar was given an ultimatum by the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian]] [[Lore:Ami-El|Emperor]]. He and his people were eventually forced to abandon Nenalata and flee to [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] in order to escape the anti-Ayleid pogroms.{{ref|name=ALoSaS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}} During their exodus, hunters among the Nenalata Ayleids used senche mounts daily to track and kill game to help feed the evacuees. {{Ref|name=NA|[[Online:Nenalata Ayleid Senche|Nenalata Ayleid Senche]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
In High Rock, King Dynar and his people were welcomed by the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] clan. After displacing previous Orc residents, they settled on the shores of the [[Lore:Halcyon Lake|Halcyon Lake]]. There, King Dynar founded the new city of [[Lore:Bisnensel|Bisnensel]], which was a miniature copy of Nenalata.{{ref|name=ALoSaS}}{{ref|name=BOAR|{{Cite Book|Bisnensel: Our Ancient Roots}}}} 

In the years {{Year|1E 453}}-{{Year|1E 460|460}}, the Primeval Seekers, a cult devoted to [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], was formed in Bisnensel. Their leader, High Priest [[Lore:Uluscant|Uluscant]], demanded more of a say in municipal affairs as the cult grew in power. King Dynar denied these demands. As a result, after a few months Dynar was deposed and forced to flee from Bisnensel.{{ref|name=ALoSaS}}{{ref|name=BOAR}} King Dynar and his family fled to [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]], where they were granted asylum among the Direnni. Later, Dynar became a highly regarded tactician and strategist in the Direnni army.{{ref|name=ALoSaS}}

In the late 400s, the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] invaded High Rock. The invasion culminated in the great [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]], in which King Dynar participated.{{ref|name=ALoSaS}}{{ref|name=TLKOTA}} The battle was won by the Direnni and was a fatal blow to the Alessian Order.

In the years {{Year|1E 482}}-{{Year|1E 484|484}}, King Dynar returned to the [[Lore:Heartland|Heartland]] and was lured to Nenalata, where he fell into [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s trap and was imprisoned in [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], in a [[Lore:The Lightless Oubliette|replica of the White-Gold Tower]].{{ref|name=ALoSaS}} There he remained for over three thousand years, until he was freed.{{ref|name=ESOKLD|[[ON:King Laloriaran Dynar|King Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Other sources state that King Dynar was not captured by Molag Bal in Nenalata, but instead was present in the [[Lore:Hollow City|Hollow City]] while it was still in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], was pushed into Coldharbour along with it, and captured shortly afterwards.{{ref|name=ONGroundskeeper|[[Online:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

It is possible that some tribes of Ayleids may continue to live deep within the forests of Cyrodiil even into modernity, yet the most recent reported sightings (as of the Late Third Era) are more than 1000 years old.{{ref|name=TWE}}{{ref|name=2920L5}} These Ayleids, or Wild Elves, are at best a rarity and may not have actually survived into modern times.

==Legacy==
===Second Era===
Ayleid sages were present at the turn of the Second Era. [[Lore:Tjurhane Fyrre|Tjurhane Fyrre]] founded the [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]] and studied the history of his people, publicly speaking about their religion and culture, such as when he released a book during the reign of [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]] in {{Year|2E 12}} known as the ''Nature of Ayleidic Poesy''. He passed away in {{Year|2E 227}} at the age of 357.{{ref|name=TWE}}

In the year {{Year|2E 582}}, King Dynar was finally freed by the [[Lore:Hero|Vestige]], who infiltrated Coldharbour with the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters]] and [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]]s in an effort to stop Molag Bal's terrible [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]]. The Ayleid king assumed command over the Fighters Guild forces, who had been leaderless after their captain had been slain. The invasion force, led by the Vestige and aided by [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], eventually managed to stop the Planemeld inside the {{Lore Link|Planar Vortex}}, but King Dynar was mortally wounded while trying to close a Daedric portal. He died soon afterwards in the Hollow City, in Meridia's Chapel of Light.{{ref|name=ESOTLA|Events of [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} His spirit is believed to have gone to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]].{{ref|[[ON:Old Life Observance|Old Life Observance]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Petronius Galenus|Petronius Galenus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

King Hadhuul's spirit, along with spirits of his army and some [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame Atronach]]s, reappeared at Ceyatatar during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] in {{Year|2E 582}}, but these invaders were banished by soldiers fighting in the ongoing [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] before they could harm the nearby settlement of [[Lore:Vlastarus|Vlastarus]].{{ref|name=ON|[[ON:Essence of Flame|Essence of Flame]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Society==
:''"In the caverns of lore, ugly and obscure, shines the gold of truth."''{{ref|name=ART}}
[[File:ON-furnishing-Ancient High Elf Statue 02.png|right|140px]]
Ayleidic society was an alliance of kingdoms united under one empire, which ultimately ended in 1E 243 with the death of their champion, [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]], and the fall of the White-Gold Tower.{{ref|name=TWE}}

The impact of the loss of the Ayleids' tower, what they called the "Temple of the Ancestors", should not be underestimated. They had built it in emulation of the Adamantine Tower,{{ref|name=BOTZ}} and made the [[Lore:Chim-el Adabal|Chim-el-Adabal]], which they believed to be the crystallized blood of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], into the tower's Founding-Stone.{{ref|name=AE4TET}} It is the site where the [[Lore:Ten Ancestors|Ten Ancestors]], statues sacred to the Ayleids, were traditionally held. They were spread to various other settlements during the siege of the White-Gold Tower for safekeeping, but were not reunited again until the late Third Era, long after the fall of the Ayleids.{{ref|name=OBUmbacano}}{{ref|name=Oblivion}}

Ayleids frequently adorned themselves with feathers and magic beads.{{ref|name=TSOP}} They are said to have been "obsessed" with the number eight (which may have been why the pantheon Saint Alessia created was made up of [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]]).{{ref|name=TA}}

Most humans, at least, agree that the Ayleids' fall from power, if not their extinction, is for the better, as the portrayals of them passed down by their former slaves are not kind. Their scholars label the Ayleids as bellicose and arrogant, and point to this being the ultimate reason for their downfall.{{ref|name=TOAC}}{{ref|name=PGE3All}}{{ref|name=OBHerminia}} Slaves were utilized by the Ayleids for a variety of purposes, including agricultural work, entertainment, and the building and maintenance of their empire's infrastructure. According to legend, the Ayleids literally turned cruelty into an art form, and many Daedra-worshipping Ayleids across Cyrodiil derived strange pleasures from "art-tortures".{{ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=DWTA}}

Typically, Ayleid kingdoms where the most malevolent Daedric Princes were worshipped also proved to exhibit the worst treatment of their slaves.{{ref|name=DWTA}} Various settlements became famous for their particular, elaborate method of torturing their slaves, such as "wailing wheels", "gut gardens", "flesh-sculptures", and forced ingestion of hallucinogenic [[Lore:Drugs|drugs]], as well as nighttime "tiger sport", which involved the immolation of human children.{{ref|name=TA}} 

As the kingdoms of the Ayleids dwindled, they apparently adapted to a more rural, elusive, and possibly nomadic lifestyle. Any remaining Ayleids are unknown; if they continue to exist, they are likely living in hiding. The Ayleid Sage [[Lore:Tjurhane Fyrre|Tjurhane Fyrre]] ({{Year|1E 2790}} - {{Year|2E 227}}) talked and wrote extensively of the state of the Ayleids' culture. According to Fyrre, the Ayleids of his time lived in a tribal society, with each "tribe" being substantially different from the next.{{ref|name=TWE}} The Ayleid settlements in modern-day [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] were particularly far removed from the culture of the heartlands, and were never attacked by the Alessian armies.{{ref|name=PGE3A}} It's not clear how these settlements met their fate.

{{NewLine}}
===Magic===
[[File:SR-quest-The Cause 05.jpg|thumb|right|A Great Welkynd Stone in Rielle]]
Magic was an enormous part of their society. In accordance with High Elf religion, they believed [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] was composed of four basic elements: earth, water, air, and light (as opposed to the four elements of modern natural philosophy: earth, water, air, and fire).{{ref|name=GAL}}{{ref|name=MFTS}} Some of their magic tapped into the power of the weather.{{ref|name=Oblivion}} The most sublime form was starlight, as the stars were the link between [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] and [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]]. 

They used aetherial fragments which fell from the heavens, particularly [[Lore:Meteoric Iron|meteoric iron]], to construct receptacles which are believed to harness energy from starlight. These "Ayleid Wells" are scattered all over Cyrodiil, and can still be used by mages to replenish their energy. The logic used in their placement is a mystery, and none are known to be associated with any particular Ayleid settlements. They kept specially cut pieces of [[Lore:Meteoric Glass|meteoric glass]] known as Welkynd Stones (meaning literally "sky children") as storage containers for magical energy, as well what are called Varla Stones (or "star stones").{{ref|name=MFTS}} This was still an emerging technology when the Alessian Slave Rebellion brought about the decline and disappearance of their society.{{ref|name=LRN|{{Cite Book|Lithnilian's Research Notes}}}} Some of these crystals had the capacity to store and release memories directly to the reader; overuse of these crystals can cause a 'problem of capacity' for mortals.{{ref|name=ESOFlooded|[[Online:The Flooded Grove|The Flooded Grove]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Varla gems were occasionally used to imbue the diadems by Barsaebic for that purpose.{{ref|name=ContrabandB|[[ON:Contraband B#Barsaebic Varla Diadem|Barsaebic Varla Diadem]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

They preserved many of the ancient runes and [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] magics of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] that other elves had forgotten.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=AD|{{Cite Book|Ahzidal's Descent}}}} They also developed what came to be called the school of [[Lore:Alteration|Alteration]], as they were skilled in magics such as shapeshifting and levitation.

==Architecture==
[[File:OB-place-Nornal.jpg|right|thumb|Ayleid ruins, as seen in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]]] 
Much like how the [[Lore:Dwemer Ruins|ruins]] of the lost [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] dot [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], the Ayleids have left [[Lore:Ayleid Ruins|ruins]] scattered around [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and other regions which were once under the dominion of their Empire. These underground cities can prove deadly for adventurers, hiding such dangers as traps, monsters, undead, and criminals. Their cities were amazing feats of architecture ranging from small settlements to gigantic, labyrinth-like metropolises shaped out of whitish rock.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=GJ|{{Cite Book|Gelebourne's Journal}}}} They also seem to have had a firm grasp of the [[Lore:Magic|magical arts]], as many of their ruins contain stones filled with magicka essences harvested from the sky. They also developed what came to be called the school of [[Lore:Alteration|Alteration]], as they were skilled in magics such as shapeshifting and levitation.{{ref|name=GAL|{{Cite Book|Glories and Laments}}}}{{ref|name=MFTS|{{Cite Book|Magic from the Sky}}}}{{ref|name=DOTN|{{Cite Book|Daughter of the Niben}}}}{{ref|name=2920L5|{{Cite Book|2920, Second Seed (v5)}}}} 

The prophecies of the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|moth-eyed]] foretold that Ayleidic hubris would "bear bitter fruit",{{ref|name=AE4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} a warning that many, apparently, failed to heed. It would be their undoing.

==Religion==
===Aedra===
[[File:ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg|Auri-El|right|thumb]]
Few blanket statements can be made about Ayleidic religion. They began as splinter groups, and they were never totally united in their beliefs.{{ref|name=DWTA}} But while they are most well-known to historians for being "godless" and "[[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-loving",{{ref|name=GTTIC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Imperial City}}}} their society was fundamentally dedicated to the veneration of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], not the Daedra, until the last millennium of the Merethic Era.{{ref|name=TWOD|{{Cite Book|The Whithering of Delodiil}}}} 

Even after the Daedric cults rose to power (discussed [[#History|above]]), many Ayleidic kingdoms continued to honor the Aedra.{{ref|name=DWTA}} Many slaves in Cyrodiil became Aedra-worshippers by adopting many of the beliefs of their masters,{{ref|name=TAOK}}{{ref|name=SATD}} and it's suspected that Alessia incorporated elven Aedra into her Eight Divines pantheon in order to appease her Ayleid and Ayleid-influenced vassals.{{ref|name=DWTA}} Ayleids venerated their ancestors,{{ref|name=OBUmbacano}} and Aedra is an Elven word for "ancestor".{{ref|name=AAD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}}

[[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] was known to be worshipped by the Ayleids.{{ref|name=OBUmbacano}} Ayleid statues of Auri-El throughout Cyrodiil depict an eagle lifting up a figure carrying a bow and shield from the ensnaring ground.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}{{ref|name=TM}}

====Blood-Made-Pleasure====
To the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] was known as the '''Blood-Made-Pleasure'''.{{ref|name=MotI|{{Cite Book|Masterwork of the Inducer}}}} Those from the kingdoms that worshipped the more malevolent Daedric Princes were known to indulge in the pleasures of art-torture, such as flesh-sculpting and gut-gardening.{{ref|name=DWTA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Ayleids}}}} An ancient Ayleid manuscript of Eldhaal the Inducer describes him being overseen and guided by Sanguine in these heinous arts, particularly in the creation of the "Messenger Beast Redeemed".{{ref|name=MotI}}

====Hyrma Mora====
The city of [[Lore:Bisnensel|Bisnensel]] began to worship Hyrma Mora under the lead of High Priest Uluscant, who established a congregation in Bisnensel that attracted many of the learned and most influential people in the community. As their numbers became more numerous, on their behalf Uluscant requested more of a say in municipal affairs, a request that was denied by King Dynar. Within a few months of his rebuff, Dynar was deposed.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bisnensel: Our Ancient Roots}}}} The Ayleids of Bisnensel turned the city into a shrine and stored forbidden knowledge of the natural order, which could allow one to manipulate the very fabric of reality.{{ref|[[Online:Herald Kixathi|Herald Kixathi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Mehrunes Dagon====
In ancient times, Dagon created a construct known as the Nefarivigum to challenge those who drew near to undertake a trial. One who passed the trial would be deemed worthy of wielding [[Lore:Mehrunes' Razor|Mehrunes' Razor]]. Located in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], the Nefarivigum was discovered by the ancient [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], who arrogantly believed they could turn away any who sought the Razor. To this end, they built the vast, ringed city of [[Lore:Varsa Baalim|Varsa Baalim]] during the height of Ayleid civilization. For many years, pilgrims came to Varsa Baalim seeking the Razor, but the Ayleids turned them away. One day in the early years of the First Era, a [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] infiltrated the city. Before long, the city was infested with undead and thrust into chaos. The city suddenly and mysteriously disappeared. None know what happened, but Varsa Baalim seemingly vanished into the mountains of the Eastern [[Lore:Nibenay Basin|Nibenay Basin]].{{ref|name=TOAC|{{Cite Book|Treatise on Ayleidic Cities}}}}

====Mephala====
The Ayleid city of [[Lore:Erokii|Erokii]] was ruled by princess Anurraame, whose wisdom, grace, and beauty were unsurpassed. She married a prince from another state, and their relationship was happy for a time. However, Anurraame's husband was away for extended periods of time. The princess's passion died, and she took a younger warrior as her lover. Her husband eventually caught wind of the affair, and gathered his army to storm the city. Anurraame pledged to face him in battle, and her lover promised to muster his own forces in defense of the city. When the day of the battle arrived, Anurraame saw that her lover had betrayed her for a bribe, as his legions had joined her husband's. The spurned princess called upon the Daedra to help defend her city, and Mephala answered her plea. She commanded Anurraame to pour all her tears in a basin, then imbued them with the power of Anurraame's hatred for her traitorous lover, which was only strengthened by the genuine love she had for him before he turned on her. Her tears hardened into a powerful artifact known as the [[Lore:Tears of Anurraame|Tears of Anurraame]], which the princess used to destroy both armies and her city in the blink of an eye.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Tears of Anurraame}}}}

====Merid-Nunda====
[[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]] (known as the '''Bright One''' and '''Rainbow of Light'''){{ref|name=RDP|{{Cite Book|Ripped and Discarded Page|ns_base=ON}}}} was an extremely important figure for the Ayleids, as she was considered the personification of their fourth element, light.{{ref|name=EOMN|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}} The Ayleids often depicted Meridia with wings and a cowl.{{ref|name=ESOAS|[[ON:Grahtwood Antiquities#Ayleid Statuette|Ayleid Statuette]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Merid-Nunda was the patron deity of Garlas Malatar, a city which was built upon the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]]. Its ruler, [[Lore:King Narilmor|King Narilmor]], was a devout worshiper of Merid-Nunda who dedicated his life and city to her veneration. He was considered a favorite of the Daedric Prince, second only to her champion, Umaril the Unfeathered. She gifted the city with a relic known as the {{Lore Link|Guiding Light}}, which shone above the city with her unquenchable light. It was a literal and metaphorical beacon, visible for great distances.{{ref|name=MTCKN|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - King Narilmor}}}}

She is remembered by the [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]] as a patron and mentor to the Heartland High Elves, and complicit in the bondage and oppression of their ancestors.{{ref|name=AYQ|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}}
{{NewLine}}

====Mola Gbal====
[[File:OB-interior-Garlas Agea.jpg|right|thumb|Ayleid statuette of Mola Gbal]]
Molag Bal was known as '''Mola Gbal''' to the Ayleids,{{ref|name=ESOHoSLoadTxt|[[Online:Halls of Submission|Halls of Submission]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and was the patron deity of [[Lore:Abagarlas|Abagarlas]].{{ref|name=TWOD}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Unholy Temple}}}} 

King [[Lore:Anumaril|Anumaril]] of Abagarlas was granted an army of undead and the knowledge to construct a weapon known as the [[Lore:Mortuum Vivicus|Mortuum Vivicus]], which would drain all life from Delodiil, and convert its citizens to undead servants of Molag Bal.{{ref|name=CJ|{{Cite Book|Curano's Journal}}}} Meridia, knowing of Molag Bal and Anumaril's twisted plans, warned her subjects in Delodiil, and in response a small group of priests were dispatched to Abagarlas to destroy the weapon. Upon reaching Abagarlas the priests tore a bloody swath through the city, putting both Anumaril's wife Queen Palolel and his son Prince Malyon to the sword. Soon, the priests successfully reached the Mortuum Vivicus, in time to stop the contraption's activation. However, Molag Bal pulled the device into [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] before it could be entirely destroyed. After the priests fled the city, undead in tow, Meridia completely destroyed the city through divine lightning.{{ref|name=LJ|{{Cite Book|Lanath's Journal}}}}
