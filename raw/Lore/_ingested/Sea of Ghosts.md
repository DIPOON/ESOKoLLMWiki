The [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] (also known as the '''Ghost Sea'''{{Ref|name=SRLoading|[[Skyrim:Solitude|Solitude]] [[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Solitude|Loading Screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} or the '''Northern Sea'''){{Ref|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/exclamations Exclamations in the Elder Scrolls: Arena — The Imperial Library]}} is the body of water that consists of the [[Lore:Northern Coast|northern coast]] of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]],{{Ref|name=GtV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}} the eastern coast of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],{{ref|name=GenerG}} and the southern coast of [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]].{{Ref|name=PGE3EOL|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}} It is a part of the [[Lore:Padomaic Ocean|Padomaic Ocean]], which consists of the north and east seas of Tamriel. The Sea of Ghosts is not easy to navigate. It took some time for the people of Tamriel to explore these misty waters, which are often riddled with glaciers and icebergs.{{Ref|name=PGE1ESR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE3ESR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Skyrim}}}} The Sea of Ghosts acquired its name from the ancient tale of Skyrim, known as ''[[Lore:Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts|Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts]]'' which refers to the death of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s eldest son, [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]], at the hands of the "''Sea-Ghosts"''.{{Ref|name=YatSG|{{Cite Book|Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts}}}} The Sea of Ghosts host its own variety of animals that are not commonly found in other parts of the continent, specifically [[Lore:Horker|horker]]s and [[Lore:Chub Loon|chub loon]]s.{{Ref|name=MotCL|{{Cite Book|Mystery of the Chub Loon}}}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}

==History==
[[File:SR-place-Yngol Barrow.jpg|thumb|left|Yngol Barrow (Skyrim)]]
In the mid-[[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]i explorers sailed across Tamriel, charting the continent's waterways and sea lanes. The most well-known was [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]], who explored all of Tamriel's coast, but most notably the [[Lore:Niben River|Niben River]].{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} Aldmeri explorers mapped the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], building several wizards towers along the coast, specifically in [[Lore:Ald Redaynia|Ald Redaynia]], [[Lore:Bal Fell|Bal Fell]], [[Lore:Tel Aruhn|Tel Aruhn]], and [[Lore:Tel Mora|Tel Mora]]. But these towers were eventually abandoned as the {{Lore Link|High Velothi Culture}}, and the early [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] began to establish the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] and the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlander Tribes]].{{Ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}

Migration of Men from Atmora to Skyrim occurred over the centuries, with the founding of several settlements the largest of which was [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]]. In the late Merethic Ega, Ysgramor himself was one such settler alongside his two sons fleeing Civil War in Atmora. However the [[Lore:Snow Elves|Snow Elves]] of Skyrim destroyed Saarthal in the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]],{{Ref|name=BtAoM}} leaving only Ysgramor and his sons alive, who came to Atmora and gathered the [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] to return to Skyrim to wreak vengeance against the Elves. Ysgramor the Harbinger and the Atmorans sailed across the Sea of Ghosts from the port city of [[Lore:Jylkurfyk|Jylkurfyk]] and landed on [[Lore:Hsaarik Head|Hsaarik Head]]. However, their fleet was caught in the {{Lore Link|Storm of Separation}} and the Harbinger's eldest son, Yngol, was killed in the raging storm in the Atmoran Strait.{{Ref|name=ESOFallowstone|[[Online:Fallowstone Hall|Broken Statue]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In legend, it is said that Yngol was claimed by the ''"Sea-Ghosts"''.{{Ref|name=YatSG}}{{Ref|name=SotRV2|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 2}}}} Yngol's body was recovered by Ysgramor and buried in a traditional Atmoran barrow on the [[Lore:White River|White River]] estuary, where it meets the Sea of Ghosts. Ysgramor looked to the south and decided to have a great city built near the estuary. This city of kings, [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], was made to have [[Lore:Yngol Barrow|Yngol Barrow]] viewable from the castle.{{Ref|name=SotRV19|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 19}}}} As time went on, more [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic cities were built along the coastline, such as [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]], [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], and [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]].

The cities of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and Solitude maintained a summer route, with no waypoint between each location. That was when the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] entrepreneur, [[Lore:Yric Flowdys|Yric Flowdys]] discovered shores for an ideal harbor at the base of {{Lore Link|Dore Elard}} in the 9th century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. The deep waters were able to accommodate large merchant ships, and on the northernmost point, Flowdys built the town of Northpoint. As time went on, Yric Flowdys adopted the name of the mountain as his family name, {{Lore Link|House Dorell}}. They later attained the title of Barony from Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]], and became a prominent power in the Kingdom of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]]. They have extensive ties with the Court of Solitude.{{Ref|name=NAA|{{Cite Book|Northpoint: An Assessment}}}}

An army of [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]]s and [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropes]] known as the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]] once occupied a castle on the remote island of [[Lore:Grayhome|Grayhome]] in the Sea of Ghosts. The island was left abandoned following the defeat of the Gray Host circa {{Year|1E 1029}}.{{ref|[[ON:Grayhome|Grayhome]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BSHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}}

There have been two instances where the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] of the eponymous [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]] have invaded Tamriel, both of which arrived on the continent's shores the same way. The [[Lore:First Akaviri Invasion|First Akaviri Invasion]] landed on the north coast of both Skyrim and Morrowind, cutting a bloody swathe all the way to the [[Lore:Pale Pass|Pale Pass]] in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]]. By {{Year|1E 2703}}, General [[Lore:Reman|Reman]] defeated the [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] and established the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{Ref|name=LotD|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Dragonguard}}}} In case of another threat, Reman I built strongholds across Skyrim's north coast as his bulwark.{{Ref|name=IaC|{{Cite Book|Ice and Chitin}}}} In any case, the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] rarely took place in Skyrim's coast. This invasion led by [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] traveled across the Sea of Ghosts, bypassing [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]], and [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], only to land on the shores of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]]. Here, Dir-Kamal's forces sacked the city, killing the High Queen of East Skyrim, [[Lore:Mabjaarn Flame-Hair|Mabjaarn Flame-Hair]], and her shortly-lived successor, [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]]. From there, his armies traveled southeast into the region of [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] in Morrowind. As history knows it, the Akaviri were defeated and gave rise to the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]].{{Ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}

In {{Year|3E 271}}, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim V|Uriel Septim V]] explored the Sea of Ghosts and conquered the island of [[Lore:Roscrea|Roscrea]], as of his several campaigns to expand the Empire.{{Ref|name=BHotEv3|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 3}}}} Roscrea is found off the coast of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] and it was converted into an Imperial fiefdom. The history of the territory is unknown, however, it was later claimed by the Kingdom of Solitude in the late years of the Third Era, in {{Year|3E 432}}, to further expand their power. After marrying the Queen [[Lore:Macalla|Macalla]] of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]], King [[Lore:Thian|Thian]] held a majority of control of Skyrim's northern coast. Under his reign, the island had been used to further chart the sea's mysterious waters.{{Ref|name=PGE3ESR}}

==Society==
[[File:BM-npc-Reaver Group.jpg|thumb|left|A reaver raiding party on Solstheim]][[File:ON-place-Kyne's Aegis 02.jpg|thumb|right|A Sea Giant longship]]
The people who inhabit the Sea of Ghosts are known as out-islanders. They spend most of their time at sea, journeying from the far north on their ships to raid the coasts of northern Tamriel. The Nords refer to these raiders as {{Lore Link|reaver}}s. They generally target one-hut settlements and hunting camps, dropping off small raiding parties rather than beaching their ships for fear that their vessels would be stolen or burned.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue R|Reaver dialogue]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Raiders are known to make base on remote frozen islands in the Sea of Ghosts; for example, the Frostbite Raiders once inhabited an icy island off the [[Lore:Frozen Coast|Frozen Coast]] of northeastern [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]].{{ref|[[ON:Magreta|Magreta]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

In addition to reavers and other raiders, the Sea of Ghosts is home to the elusive [[Lore:Sea Giant|Sea Giant]]s, a species of [[Lore:Giant|giant]] rarely seen on Tamriel. They allegedly only make landfall during the coldest winters and never stay for long. They slaughter and pillage, then vanish into the ice and fog.{{ref|name=ESOOgvar|[[ON:Thane Ogvar|Thane Ogvar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Fight for Kyne's Aegis|The Fight for Kyne's Aegis]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Sightings of their longships have been reported over the centuries, but generally sailors who witness the Sea Giants do not live to tell the tale.{{ref|name=LTTO|{{Cite Book|Letter to Thane Ogvar}}}} Sea Giants sometimes come into conflict with raiders and invade their territory.{{ref|[[ON:The Fate of the Frozen|The Fate of the Frozen]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} They are also known to interbreed with the people of Tamriel, producing offspring known as [[Lore:Half-Giant|Half-Giant]]s who accompany them on their raids.{{ref|[[ON:Half-Giant|Half Giant]]s in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

The Sea of Ghosts was once home to the infamous [[Lore:Vampire Lord|Vampire Lord]] [[Lore:Falgravn|Falgravn]]. Said to have once been a pirate, Falgravn traveled the Sea of Ghosts since the reign of King [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir]], disappearing for hundreds of years at a time before being sighted again.{{ref|name=ESOOgvar}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, Lord Falgravn mounted an invasion of [[Lore:Kyne's Aegis|Kyne's Aegis]] with an army of vampires and Sea Giants, but he was killed by the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] and the island liberated.{{ref|name=TFFKA|[[ON:The Fight for Kyne's Aegis|The Fight for Kyne's Aegis]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

For the peoples of northern Tamriel, diving naked into the Sea of Ghosts is a rite of passage.{{ref|[[Online:Bolgrul|Bolgrul]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} The Nords of Windhelm participate in the Snow Bear Plunge, a [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life]] tradition involving such dives.{{ref|[[Online:Snow Bear Plunge|Snow Bear Plunge]] quest in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

==High Rock==
[[File:ON-place-Northpoint 02.jpg|thumb|right|Northpoint (ESO)]]
At some point, the Sea of Ghosts meets the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] to the west, as the town of [[Lore:Eagle's Brook|Eagle's Brook]] is considered [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]]'s gateway to the Eltheric Ocean.{{Ref|name=GTaC|{{Cite Book|Glenumbra's Towns and Cities}}}} The coast of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] and [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]] are at least considered part of the Sea of Ghosts. The port-cities of [[Lore:Fharun|Fharun]], [[Lore:Jehanna|Jehanna]], and [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]] are the largest settlements on the northern coast. Although, there are some small harbors across the coast, such as the {{Lore Link|Morkul Stronghold}}, home to the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] clan of smiths of the same name. Formerly, the once great city of [[Lore:Erokii|Erokii]] was found on the shores, hidden from the rest of Rivenspire by the [[Lore:Kurallian Mountains|Kurallian Mountains]]. It is now known as {{Lore Link|The Doomcrag}}. From the east to the west, the Sea of Ghosts becomes more hospitable as glaciers are less common. Where the regions of Rivenspire and Wrothgar meet on the mainland is the [[Lore:Great Bay|Great Bay]], a water basin with several shipwrecks and stones jutting out. Further northeast into the frozen north, traversing is much more difficult. The {{Lore Link|Frozen Fleet}} is a ship graveyard held together in an icy wasteland. Wrothgar's coast is notably populated by the chub loon, mysterious flightless birds that came from Atmora.{{Ref|name=MotCL}} In the seas surrounding Rivenspire and [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]] is a notorious creature known as the {{Lore Link|Ithguleoir (creature)}}, a leviathan that preys on the people of the coast.{{Ref|name=Ithguleoir|{{Cite Book|Ithguleoir}}}} The royal {{Lore Link|House Dorell}} are the ancestral rulers of Northpoint and are known for their maritime endeavors. They founded the settlement as a waypoint on the summer route between [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and Solitude. The shores around the city are notably deep, which made the location all the more favorable for a checkpoint.{{Ref|name=NAA}}

==Morrowind==
[[File:MW-place-Khuul.jpg|thumb|left|Khuul (Morrowind)]]
Morrowind's coast is shared between the Great Houses of [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]] and [[Lore:Telvanni|Telvanni]]. The cities of [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]] and [[Lore:Firewatch|Firewatch]] are found on the coast, as well as many minor settlements. These include [[Lore:Ald Velothi|Ald Velothi]], [[Lore:Dagon Fel|Dagon Fel]], [[Lore:Gnisis|Gnisis]], and [[Lore:Khuul|Khuul]]. The [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] empties into the Sea of Ghosts, separating the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] from the rest of mainland Morrowind. It is believed that the First Era eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], known as [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]], caused the Inner Sea to form and separate the region.{{Ref|name=RoKZ|{{Cite Book|Ruins of Kemel-Ze}}}} [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]i mythology indicates that the Inner Sea was created by the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]], the Brass-God of the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{Ref|name=36LoVs36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Bypassing Firewatch leads into the rest of the Padomaic Ocean, all the way to distant [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]. The coast of Vvardenfell is ridged and volcanic, and both the {{Lore Link|Foyada Bani-Dad}} and the [[Lore:Valley of the Wind|Valley of the Wind]] empty into the Sea of Ghosts. The archipelago of [[Lore:Sheogorad|Sheogorad]] is found off the coast of the Black Isle. These scattered islands surround the large island of Sheogorad, from which the archipelago gets its name. These islands have only one known settlement, the town of [[Lore:Dagon Fel|Dagon Fel]]. The remote fishing village of [[Lore:Ald Redaynia|Ald Redaynia]] no longer exists, but it was found on the westernmost point of the Sheogorad region, on the second-largest island. There is an uninhabited island between Sheogorad and the coast, west of the ancient stronghold of {{Lore Link|Rotheran}} known as {{Lore Link|Shuran Island}}. The villages along the Morrowind coast are typically built on wooden foundations and are often controlled by House Redoran. Morrowind's eastern coast is also bounded by the Sea of Ghosts,{{ref|name=GenerG|[[MW:Generic Dialogue G|Generic dialogue]] regarding '''Geography of Morrowind''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} containing the rugged [[Lore:Northeast Archipelago|northeast archipelago]] and lush island of [[Lore:Gorne|Gorne]], with the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]] bordering it further south.

==Skyrim==
[[File:SR-place-Solitude 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Arch of Solitude]]
Skyrim's northern coast consists of the cities of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]], [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]], and [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], as well as their respective holds ([[Lore:The Pale|The Pale]], [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]], and [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]]). Each of them are prominent cities that are often utilized by the East Empire Trading Company. The latter two, Solitude and Windhelm are built where two great rivers empty into the Sea of Ghosts. The [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River]] runs across Haafingar as well as [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]], while the [[Lore:White River|White River]] starts from [[Lore:Lake Ilinalta|Lake Ilinalta]] in southern Skyrim and descends northeast into Eastmarch. However, the city of Winterhold is not a port city. It is built atop of the [[Lore:Broken Cape|Broken Cape]], where it overlooks the entire sea from the highest bluffs. A large chunk of the city fell into the sea after the Great Collapse.{{Ref|name=OtGC|{{Cite Book|On the Great Collapse}}}} Beyond the cities, there have been little to no settlements on the coast of Skyrim. The only known example is the small town of [[Lore:Dunbarrow|Dunbarrow]].{{Ref|name=CDJP1|{{Cite Book|Cap'n Dugal's Journal, Part 1}}}} It is not uncommon to find wrecked ships across the northern coast. The most infamous case of this is the [[Lore:Pilgrim's Trench|Pilgrim's Trench]], a graveyard of ships off the coast of Winterhold. Islands off the coast are sparsely populated, much less so than in Morrowind, but there are still people, nonetheless. The [[Lore:Volkihar|Volkihar Clan]] of [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]]s live in an ancient castle on an island north of Haafingar. East of Winterhold is Serpentstone Isle, which contains the eponymous Serpent Stone, one of the [[Lore:Standing Stones|Standing Stones]] of Skyrim. The Sea of Ghosts transitions into the region of [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]] through the [[Lore:Drajkmyr Marsh|Drajkmyr Marsh]], which takes a large portion of Hjaalmarch and ends toward the towns of [[Lore:Morthal|Morthal]] and [[Lore:Fort Snowhawk|Fort Snowhawk]]. Along the coast can be found various ruined forts built during the time of Emperor [[Lore:Reman|Reman I]] of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]. As a defense measure for another invasion from [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]], he built several strongholds across Skyrim's northern coast.{{Ref|name=IaC}} Further from the coast, the [[Lore:Merringar|Merringar]] is a rare fish that is hunted in schools for food. [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] are also found here and are attracted to the Merringar's blood.{{Ref|name=TRKR|{{Cite Book|The Red Kitchen Reader}}}}

==Islands==
[[File:Solstheim map Bloodmoon.jpg|thumb|right|Map of Solstheim (ca. 3E 427)]]
*[[Lore:Blackbone Isle|Blackbone Isle]]: A haunted island that houses the treasure and wealth amassed by Cyrus the Restless
*[[Lore:Bleakrock Isle|Bleakrock Isle]]: A small island between Windhelm and Solstheim far from major trading routes
*[[Lore:Castle Volkihar|Castle Volkihar]]: An island north of the border between Skyrim and High Rock, the ancient home of the Volkihar Clan
*[[Lore:The Chill|The Chill]]: A glacial island north of Winterhold, with an ice cave that serves as the city's jail
*[[Lore:Giant's Tooth|Giant's Tooth]]: An island inhabited by Giants northeast of Winterhold that contains the last remaining Ironwood trees in Skyrim
*[[Lore:Gorne|Gorne]]: A lush island off the coast of Necrom that is home to several exotic species
*[[Lore:Grayhome|Grayhome]]: An island far to the north of Northpoint, formerly the home of the Gray Host
*[[Lore:Icereach|Icereach]]: An island north of Haafingar, home to the Icereach witch coven
*[[Lore:Kyne's Aegis|Kyne's Aegis]]: A medium-sized island northwest of Solitude, where many ships pass before reaching the city
*[[Lore:Northeast Archipelago|Northeast Archipelago]]: A sparsely populated, rugged archipelago located northeast of Morrowind
*[[Lore:Olenveld|Olenveld]]: An island-city roughly a summer's day sail away from Winterhold that was abandoned in the Second Era
*[[Lore:Japhet's Folly|Rockjoint Island]]: A frozen island northeast of Winterhold, considered inhospitable for a permanent settlement
*[[Lore:Roscrea|Roscrea]]: An island fiefdom somewhere north of Solitude that was originally conquered by Uriel V
*[[Lore:Serpentstone Isle|Serpentstone Isle]]: An icy island northeast of Winterhold, named after the Serpent Stone at its peak
*[[Lore:Sheogorad|Sheogorad]]: A rocky archipelago off the north coast of Vvardenfell that is mostly uninhabited, save for Dagon Fel
*[[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]: A large island northeast of Windhelm that was formerly occupied by the Empire until it was passed to the Dark Elves
