[[Lore:Briarheart|Briarhearts]] (sometimes written as '''Briar Hearts'''), also called '''Briar Warriors'''{{ref|name=SRQN|[[SR:Quintus Navale|Quintus Navale]]'s dialogue during [[SR:Repairing the Phial|Repairing the Phial]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} are [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] men and women{{ref|name=TCAFOBC|{{Cite Book|The Care and Feeding of Briar Hearts}}}} whose hearts were ritualistically replaced with [[Lore:Briar Heart|poisoned seeds]] of [[Lore:Flora B#Briar Heart Tree|Briar Heart Trees]]. Traditionally, the ritual is performed on skilled warriors, who are further empowered by the gifted briar heart. Briarhearts are living weapons—warriors imbued with powerful magic that grants them enhanced hearing as well great endurance and strength with which they defend their kin.{{ref|name=ONMD|[[Online:Medone|Medone]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SKFB|[[Skyrim:Forsworn Briarheart|Forsworn Briarheart]] description}} They serve as the protectors of the clans of the Reach.{{ref|name=ONF}}

==Origins==
[[File:ON-statue-Red Eagle.jpg|thumb|right|Painting of Faolan (ESO)]] 
{{Quote Box|style=float:left;|''At last he traded his Eagle's heart<br>To the ravens who covet our power<br>In his breast they planted the briar seed<br>That grows from the corpse's flower.''|''[[Lore:Red Eagle's Song|Red Eagle's Song]]''}}
The earliest known account of the Briarhearts date back to c.{{Year|1E 1028}}. According to the legends of the Reachfolk, King Faolan, also known as [[Lore:Red Eagle|Red Eagle]], was prophesized by the vashterans to unite the disparate clans of Reachmen under one banner. This prophecy came to fruition when the [[Lore:First Empire|Alessians]], led by [[Lore:Hestra|Empress Hestra]], invaded the Reach. Red Eagle refused to yield to the Empire even as the other kings surrendered or were slaughtered by the southern forces. Eventually, all his allies had defected or died, and Faolan was exiled by the elders of his clan.{{ref|name=TLORE|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Red Eagle}}}}

During this exile, Faolan came across a mysterious and powerful [[Lore:Hagraven|hagraven]], also known as the Hag in early works,{{Ref|name=TTWOTIV|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, IV|ns_base=ON}}}} who offered him the power to free his people in exchange for his heart. Red Eagle had to clear his mind before he could embrace his destiny and filled his heart with the thing he loved the most - his people.{{ref|name=ORES|Events of [[Online:Red Eagle's Song (quest)|Red Eagle's Song]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He accepted the offer and became the first Briarheart,{{ref|name=Antiquity|[[Online:Red Eagle Cave Painting|Red Eagle Cave Painting]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} bringing an onslaught of violence and death to the occupying Imperials.{{ref|name=TLORE}}

After two years, Faolan had freed the Reach from the occupation of the Empire, but at a great cost to himself. He was forever changed by his deal with the Hag. His eyes had been turned pitch black and his humanity seemed lost, existing only as a remorseless spirit of vengeance.{{ref|name=TLORE}} 

Soon, in {{Year|1E 1030}} the southern armies would return and exact their vengeance on Faolan. It is said that it took over a hundred arrows to finally put Red Eagle down.{{ref|name=RES|{{Cite Book|Red Eagle's Song}}}} After his death, the Hag returned to claim his darkened briar heart. His followers attempted to stop her but she was far too powerful for even a hundred Reachmen, revealing herself as something "beyond the spear, the sword, the bow".{{Ref|name=TTWOTIV}} Still, his fellow warriors claimed that his eyes were once again clear in his final moments.{{ref|name=TLORE}}

==Ritual==
[[File:ON-misc-Briarheart Ritual 02.png|Briarheart ritual|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|''Becoming a briarheart is considered a sacred, worthy tradition to the Reachfolk—if done appropriately. It's not inherently evil.''{{ref|name=ONBR|[[Online:Bralthahawn|Bralthahawn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}}}
The briarheart ritual involves communing with [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]],{{Ref|name=ESOEGTB|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Bangkorai}}}} who may approve or decline the transplant of a briar heart into a skilled Reachfolk warrior.{{ref|name=ONHeart|Events of [[Online:The Heart of the Beast|The Heart of the Beast]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The briar heart acts as a real, beating heart thanks to hagraven magic.{{ref|name=BAR|[[Books:Agents and Reagents: The Bounty of Mundus|Agents and Reagents: The Bounty of Mundus, Ingredient Classification 17: Intelligent Plant Cuttings]]}}{{ref|name=ONCU|[[ON:Chief Urgdosh|Chief Urgdosh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During the ceremony, the subject's chest is opened, their heart removed, and a briar heart, an engorged bud from a briar thicket{{ref|name=BAR}} is set in its place.{{ref|name=TCAFOBC}} 

The ritual is reflective of the Reachfolk's need to improve themselves and overcome hardship. It also represents [[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]]'s immortal sacrifice{{ref|name=GSR5|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5}}}} in that a candidate sacrifices their own life to be resurrected as a living weapon.{{ref|name=ONF}} Briarheart warriors possess great strength and endurance;{{ref|name=ONMD}} the briar heart is a powerful gift that makes these warriors nigh-invincible foes.{{ref|name=ONWL|[[Online:Wyress Linnae|Wyress Linnae]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Heart of the Beast|The Heart of the Beast]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=IHJ|{{Cite Book|Ice-Heart's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} 

The briarheart ritual can be performed on both alive and dead warriors. The ritual holds great importance to warriors of the Reach.{{ref|name=TCAFOBC}} The rituals are performed by hagravens{{ref|name=SRLVR|Observation of the ritual in [[SR:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and are deemed a sacred, worthy tradition to the Reachfolk—if done appropriately.{{ref|name=ONBR}} 

Receiving a briar heart is considered a high honor, one that is especially grand if the ritual is performed on a live Reachman. In cases when the ritual is performed on live subjects, the chosen warrior meditates, undergoing a ritualistic period of solitude before their heart can be replaced.{{ref|name=ONV|[[Online:Vilum|Vilum]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} According to some sources, the hopeful warrior is marked and scarred by infected tattoo and ritual markings prior to accepting the knot of poisoned briars for a heart, in order to master invincibility in battle.{{ref|name=GF|[[Books:Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel|Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel, The Feral Reachman Barbarian, Cagarach]]}} 
Some candidates undergo a marking ceremony long before the ritual. Scars shape ornate feathers and claws into their skin as a sign the Reach Hagravens chose them. They often lead others into battle. Some of those markings were subject of study of artists.{{ref|name=BBS|[[ON:Briarheart Body Scarification|Briarheart Body Scarification]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BFS|[[ON:Briarheart Face Scarification|Briarheart Face Scarification]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:SR-npc-Forsworn 06.jpg||thumb|right|Briarheart Ritual performed by two Hagravens (Skyrim)]]
The ritual can be abused, however. It is considered tainted if the subject is transformed into a Briarheart against their will;{{ref|name=ONBR}}{{ref|name=ONMD}}{{ref|name=ONF}} in that case, the free will of the Briarheart is stripped by the hagravens after the ritual is completed.{{ref|name=ONF}} It is also frowned upon to use the ritual as a punishment.{{ref|name=ONAD|[[Online:Overheard Dialogue#Thornroot Clansmen|Dialogue]] overheard from [[Online:Thornroot Clan|Thornroot Clansmen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} While this ritual is traditionally performed on Reachfolk, it can also be used on other races. It was also used at least once on a Breton,{{ref|name=COTHD|{{Cite Book|Crypt of the Heart - Draft}}}} and on a few [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiits]].{{ref|name=SRGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Eastern & Western Skyrim Gazetteer''}}{{rp|49}}

A Briarheart's 'lifespark' is put in direct connection to the natural forces,{{ref|name=SNBN|[[SR:Neloth|Neloth]]'s dialogue during [[SR:Briarheart Necropsy|Briarheart Necropsy]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} making them greatly attuned to nature.{{ref|name=ONA|[[Online:Alinon the Alchemist|Alinon the Alchemist]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The connecting soul thread is knotted three times, once for the heart, once for the spirit, and once for the body. The knot gives the seed's connection to the soul significant strength and stability.{{ref|name=SNBN}} Magical practices involving [[Lore:Heart Stone|heart stones]] are similar in nature to the Briarheart ritual.{{ref|name=SNBN}}

The exact process used in the ritual may differ from clan to clan. The ritual is often accompanied by various chants performed by hagravens,{{ref|name=SRLVR}}{{ref|name=ONMG|[[Online:Matron Grygarsnit|Matron Grygarsnit]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} though they are not necessary for the ritual to be completed.{{ref|name=ONWH|[[Online:Hagraven|Winterborn Clan Hagraven]]'s Briarheart Resurrection ability in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Thornroot Clan|Thornroot Clan]] [[Lore:Reachman Shamans|shamans]] utilize the blessed tools that allow them to desecrate the ritual without angering the spirits.{{ref|name=ONBR}}
The [[Lore:Cinder-Heart Clan|Cinder-Heart Clan]] reveres [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]],{{ref|name=ONCHCC|[[Online: Cinder-Heart Clan Camp|Cinder-Heart Clan Camp]]'s shrine to Molag Bal in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and their practices reflect the extreme horrors his sphere represents. An example is their briarheart ritual, which is augmented with the intense pain of opening a subject's chest cavity and filling it with hot coals.{{Ref|name=OCR}}  

Hagravens of the [[Lore:Winterborn|Winterborn]] clan can, to a limited degree, observe the actions of Briarhearts they brought back to life and sense certain objects they touch from a large distance.{{ref|name=ONK}}  

The [[Lore:White Phial|White Phial]] was created with the usage of magic that was lost to history as of the 4th era, but the nearest modern incarnation of said lost art is the Briarheart Ritual. A briar heart seed can be used to repair the Phial and lock its magic into physical form.{{ref|name=SRQN}}

==Characteristics==
{{Quote Box|style=float:left;|''I know their beliefs, the way they attune with nature. Those briarhearts? They're as natural as you and I.''|Alinon the Alchemist{{ref|name=ONA}}}}
[[File:ON-creature-Lurcher (Briar Rock Ruins).jpg|thumb|right|A briarheart lurcher (ESO)]]
The most defining characteristic of briarhearts is their visible lack of heart, which is ritualistically removed upon transition. The empty chest cavity contains a beating seed of the Briar Heart tree instead.{{ref|name=ONK}}{{Ref|name=STK|[[ON:Skordo the Knife|Skordo the Knife]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The new heart is occasionally referred to as their 'essence'.{{ref|name=TIEGTTSI|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Summerset Isles}}}}

Their hearts are harder than steel{{ref|name=ONK}}, resistant to heat{{Ref|name=OCR|{{Cite Book|On the Clans of the Reach}}}} and considerably heavier than they appear.{{ref|name=ESOMQI|[[Online:Markarth Quest Items#Briar Heart Seed|Briar Heart Seed]] quest item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} When exposed, the heart provides a distraction to their foes, who might consider it a weak point. Briarheart warriors occasionally have to deal with the inconvenience of beetles and other critters taking shelter in their open chests.{{ref|name=ONK}}

They suffer from constant pain{{ref|name=ONK|[[Online:Kyrtos|Kyrtos]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} in exchange for immense power, which they use to protect their clan.{{ref|name=ONMD}}

There have been instances among the Winterborn clan Briarhearts transforming themselves into briarheart tree [[Lore:Lurcher|lurchers]] through hagraven rituals involving fire, vines, and blood.{{Ref|name=Corintthac|Fight and dialogue with [[ON:Corintthac the Abomination|Corintthac the Abomination]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Ushang|[[ON:Ushang the Untamed|Ushang the Untamed]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Nature's Bounty|Nature's Bounty]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Briarhearts are often high-ranking members of their respective clans, with some even being declared chief.{{ref|name=ONUIH|[[Online:Urfon Ice-Heart|Urfon Ice-Heart]]'s dialogue and appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONBRI|[[Online:Brinarch|Brinarch]]'s dialogue and appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They are meant to be treated with respect and reverence for their sacrifice to the people of the Reach, and fringe attempts to weaponize them or remove their free will are considered sacrilegious.{{ref|name=BABR|Events of [[Online:Betrayal at Briar Rock|Betrayal at Briar Rock]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The briar heart seed itself possesses traits that fortify and regenerate magicka when used in alchemical solutions.{{ref|name=LAR|[[Lore:Alchemy B|Briarheart alchemical properties]]}} It strengthens the essence or energy used when spells are cast, so they do not wane as quickly. They can be used to increase one's stamina and accelerate physical regeneration, and also provide a muffling boon.{{ref|name=BAR}}

Although Briarhearts are reanimated through ritualistic means, they are not considered undead and are not affected by spells and enchantments designed to harm or detect the undead.{{ref|name=ESOB|[[ON:Briarheart (Condition)|Briarhearts]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SKFB}} They are also not vulnerable to silver,{{ref|name=SKFB}} and can sire children.{{Ref|name=OS|{{Cite Book|Our Story}}}}{{ref|name=Bjora|[[ON:Bjora|Bjora]]'s dialogue and appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

==Afterlife==
[[File:ON-load-The Hunting Grounds.png|thumb|left|Hunting Grounds, the realm of Hircine]][[File:SR-quest-Crypt of the Heart 04.jpg|thumb|right|A Briarheart ghost passing to the afterlife (Skyrim Creation Club)]]{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''His punishment for failure is to roam the Hunting Grounds as prey. His chest, a gaping hollow, a reminder of his weakness.''{{ref|name=ONHeart}}}}

It is believed that mortals themselves decide the destinations of their souls by the choices they make during life.{{Ref|name=TAC|{{Cite Book|The Abbot Crassius Viria Answers Your Questions}}}}

It is known, however, that in certain circumstances, Hircine can punish the Briarhearts he deems unworthy and those who failed him with an eternity of being hunted within [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|his realm]]. Such was the fate of the Brinarch of the Dark Witnesses. His claim over the souls of the Briarhearts is unknown, however.{{ref|name=ONHeart}}

A Briarheart can also be prevented from moving on to the afterlife by defiling their original heart, damning their spirit to the mortal plane.{{ref|name=BWN|{{Cite Book|Beldama Witch's Note|ns_base=SR}}}}{{ref|name=COTHD}} The original heart of the deceased Briarheart must be purified at a shrine of [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] and properly buried before the spirit can rest in peace and move on to the afterlife.{{ref|name=SKCOTH|Events of [[Skyrim:Crypt of the Heart|Crypt of the Heart]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
