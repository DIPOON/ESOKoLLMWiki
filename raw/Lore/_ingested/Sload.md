The [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] (sometimes called '''Slugmen''',{{ref|name=FNS}} '''slug-folk''',{{ref|name=ALC|{{Cite Book|A Loathsome Civilization}}}} '''slug people''',{{ref|name=GuideCulast|[[Online:Guide Culast|Guide Culast]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} '''grubs''',{{Ref|group=UOL|name=Grublord|[[:File:MW-concept-Grub Druglord.jpg|Grub Druglord]] concept art for [[MW:Concept Art|Morrowind]]}} or '''worms'''){{ref|name=Ngas|[[Redguard:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} are a race of [[Lore:Slug|slug]]-like (sometimes described as [[Lore:Toad|toad]]-like){{Ref|name=AAFCS|{{Cite Book|An Argument For Common Sense}}}} [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] native to the Coral Kingdoms of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]], west of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=PGE3/OL|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}}{{ref|name=CP}} They are semi-aquatic, corpulent beings, known to grow to massive sizes. Their culture is largely based on [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]].

Not much is known of their history, as the Sload Coral Kingdoms have generally followed an isolationist policy, mostly due to often being at war with the rest of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], but they have had diplomatic relations with the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]].{{ref|name=FNS}} They are described by the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] explorer [[Lore:Ja'dasha|Ja'dasha]] as among the most horrible and dangerous creatures of the sea, along with [[Lore:Kraken|krakens]] and [[Lore:Sea Serpent|sea serpents]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Survival Guide to Tamriel|ns_base=Books}}}} They are particularly infamous for releasing the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] upon [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in {{Year|1E 2260}}, killing more than half of the population.<noinclude>

==History==
===Early History===
The Sload are in recorded history as early as the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Early Merethic Era|Early Merethic Era]], when the Aldmer migrated from [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]] to Summerset.{{ref|name=AEXII|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored, Chapter XII}}}} Though they likely existed prior, as [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] is said to have claimed that during the first of the "[[Lore:War of Manifest Metaphors|Altmeri formwars]]" his people sieged the "necromantic meatmerchants of Thras".{{Ref|name=HowBeautifulAmongUs|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|How Beautiful You Are That You Do Not Join Us|ns_base=General}}}} The Sload were said to occupy the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]].{{ref|name=TLoF|{{Cite Book|The Lay of Firsthold}}}}{{ref|name=Grotto|[[ESO:Serpent's Grotto|Serpent's Grotto]] loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Sload of Thras may have been original inhabitants of Summerset as well,{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}} as the oldest of all the ruins there are made of coral, thought to have been carried a great many miles away from the sea. The material and style of the ruins strongly suggest that the Sload may have once counted Summerset as a part of their kingdom of Thras.{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}}

The Sload have used necromantic magic and infernal machines to attack the Altmer since before recorded time, conjuring sea monsters along the coasts and capturing many Altmer, though they have never succeeded in reclaiming Summerset.{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}} An unnamed ship of Topal's fleet traveled northwest of Summerset, towards Thras and Yokuda. It is thought that this ship and Pasquiniel may have perished at the hands of the ancient Sload, as they did not return.{{ref|name=FotN|{{Cite Book|Father of the Niben}}}}

The sload visited horrors upon Summerset during the [[Lore:Sack of Skywatch|Sack of Skywatch]] in {{Year|1E 1301}}, capturing some Altmer to bring them back to Thras.{{ref|name=PGE3S}} The few Altmer that escape mention being kept in a lagoon at the center.{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}}

===Thrassian Plague and Fall of the Sload===
[[File:ON-trailer-AllFlagsNavy.jpg|right|All Flags Navy|left|thumb]]The Sload unleashed the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] in {{Year|1E 2200}} through the use of infected gastropod sea beasts sent onto the coastlines.{{ref|name=Tendil|[[On:Captain Tendil|Captain Tendil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] asserted in his "Pestilent Progeny Thesis" that the infectious slugs the Sload sent to Tamriel were mutated versions of the Sloads' own infantile larvae.{{ref|name=Ques2|{{Cite Book|Telenger the Artificer Answers Your Questions 2}}}}  The Thrassian Plague began to rapidly spread through Tamriel, leaving hundreds of thousands of misshapen corpses in its wake and afflicting the populace with symptoms such as boils, brittle bones, seeping mucous from the eyes and ears, and an unquenchable thirst that drove people to madness. The Plague proved resistant to attempts at a cure, and a number of unsung heroes laid down their lives in search of one in vain, managing only to confirm the suspicion that the disease was unnatural and had originated from the Sload of Thras.{{ref|name=Sys|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}} At its height, the disease was described as ever penetrating, seeping into every person, every creature and every drop of water,{{Ref|name=ESONemfarion|[[ON:Kinlord Nemfarion|Kinlord Nemfarion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} and soon over half the population of the continent was dead, particularly along the western coastlands closest to Thras.{{Ref|name=PGE1Cyrodiil|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3AM|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}}

In retaliation, [[Lore:Bendu Olo|Bendu Olo]], the Baron-Admiral of the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]], led the largest allied naval force in Tamrielic history against Thras. During the construction of the fleet in the [[Lore:Systres Archipelago|Systres shipyards]], the nascent All-Flags Navy had to contend with the [[Lore:Molmor|Molmor]], the greatest of the sea beasts unleashed by the Sload. Described as a cross between a massive whale and an octopus, with a "veritable forest of spines, each longer than the lance of a knight", the massive creature proved impervious to all attempts to slay it, until a young [[Lore:Druids|Druid]] of the [[Lore:Eldertide Circle|Eldertide Circle]] named [[Lore:Belaigh|Belaigh]] tricked it into consuming an enchanted clod of ground which petrified it and turned it into [[Lore:Molmor Islet|Molmor Islet]].{{ref|name=Sys|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}}{{ref|name=BATM|{{Cite Book|Belaigh and the Molmor}}}} The fleet was beset by Sload raids as it crossed the seas to reach Thras. Accounts by a Dunmer fleet captain describe great sea beasts, resembling blighted whales, rising from the water. The fleet was sprayed with clouds of acidic ichor that dissolved everything it touched, and stopped only when it was set aflame. At the same time, Sload riders used powerful spells that made the [[Lore:Mer|elves]] on board clutch their heads in pain until all the Sload were slain. Unlocking the secret to breaching the Sload sea beasts proved to be a turning point to the conflict, as prior to that the Sload could attack with impunity from beneath the waves. It is theorized that [[Lore:Syrabane|Archmage Syrabane]] might have had a hand in this, as his focus throughout the conflict appears to have been arcane naval warfare, though the details remain hazy.{{ref|name=Sys|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}} 

Eventually the All-Flags Navy prevailed, sinking the Sloads' coral kingdoms in an event known as the Fall of the Sload.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} A great [[Lore:Towers|Coral Tower]] was present Thras' largest island Agonio, and it was the destruction of this arcane edifice that created the giant whirlpool that sunk the archipelago and a section of the All-Flags fleet.{{ref|name=Journal|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}}{{ref|name=FNS}}

It is unknown when the disease fully subsided, as cases of the Thrassian Plague were still being reported near [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] over four hundred years after its initial outbreak.{{Ref|name=WJotB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}} The influence of the disease was so pervasive that certain locations such as the Corgrad Wastes were reputed to bear a taint of corruption even well into the Second Era.{{ref|name=ESOFarowel|[[ON:Justiciar Farowel|Justiciar Farowel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The effects of the Plague and the Fall were very impactful, Olo was thereafter known as the "Dealer of Swift Justice to the Foul Spot of Thras",{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} and the glory of the Cyrodilic people became known throughout the world.{{Ref|name=PGE1Cyrodiil}} Elsweyr was reduced from 16 down to 2 kingdoms,{{ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Sugar and Blood}}}} Valenwood later fell to the Empire from being weakened by the plague,{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}} and Hammerfell was on friendlier terms with its neighbors, all as a result of the Fall.{{ref|name=PGE3H|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}}

===Post-Sinking and Rise of Thras===
[[File:RG-npc-N'Gasta.jpg|left|thumb|N'Gasta, the necromancer of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]]]]
The Sload were long presumed extinct after the Fall.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} They recovered by the end of the era, and waged the [[Lore:First Era Wars#The War of the Uvichil|War of the Uvichil]] from {{Year|1E 2911}} to {{Year|1E 2917}}, regarded as one of the most terrible events in Tamriel's history.{{ref|name=PGE3S}}

By {{Year|2E 864}}, Thras was considered a "free" or "wild region" by the [[Lore:Septim Empire|Septim Empire]], and it was reported that it had risen again. Imperial authorities encouraged citizens to report the existence of its masters, the Sload, if seen in the various areas of Tamriel.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}}

In {{Year|3E 120}}, maps of lands beyond Tamriel, including Thras, could be seen in the Map Room of the [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]].{{Ref|name=TWQ5|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v5}}}} By the waning years of the Third Era, from {{Year|3E 402}} to {{Year|3E 432}}, the western seas were very quiet and [[Lore:Agonio|Agonio]] had become considerably great in size, giving hope to Tamriel that the Sload had retreated back to Thras for good.{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}}

Following the Warp in the West in {{Year|3E 417}}, it was said that the Sload priests began talking to their new God of Worms.{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} Only a few Sload necromancers operated successfully in Black Marsh around {{Year|3E 427}}, and even then they were only active near the coast.{{ref|name=CP}} During the Mages Guild debate regarding outlawing Necromancy, Ulliceta gra-Kogg said necromancers are the scourge of Tamriel, whether operating independently or in concert with the sloads or King of Worms.{{Ref|name=TBOT|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}}

==Society and Culture==
[[File:MW-concept-Sload Skyport.jpg|A Sload skyport|thumb|right]]
</noinclude>
They are highly intelligent and skilled at magic,{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} and their mastery of necromancy is renowned.{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}} They supposedly only practice Necromancy on other races, which some say explains the number of slaves they purchase in [[Lore:Tear|Tear]], and the rumors of Sload [[Lore:Air Transportation#Airships|airships]] carrying corpses from [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]].{{ref|name=CP|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation}}}} Said airships are known as Sload floaters, which are living gasbags, used for mass transit and warfare.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=Art|[[:File:MW-concept-Sload Airship.jpg|Sload Floater]] and [[:File:MW-concept-Sload Skyport.jpg|Skyport]] concept art}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nemon's Memorystone|ns_base=GEN}}}} Dunmeri records suggest they were adept at casting curses.{{Ref|name=HA|{{Cite Book|Honored Ancestors}}}}

Sload are innately cautious and careful, and in their mythic traditions their heroes spend years planning, considering and consulting with wise Sload before taking the correct action and achieving their goal. By contrast, their villains all act quickly or rashly and always fail. The [[Lore:Sload (language)|Sload language]] has no word for 'adventure', and the closest equivalent more accurately means 'tragic disaster'.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Wild Region}}}} Similarly to the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]], the Sload are known to speak in the third person.{{ref|name=Ngas}}

According to {{Lore Link|Bendu Olo}}, Sload do not seem to experience emotions as the Tamrielic races do, but are often skilled at acting and may exaggeratedly simulate them for the benefit of more emotive beings.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} They will honor laws, loyalties, and friendships only as long as they calculate it to be in their best interests to do so. They also have no compunctions against blasphemy, theft, kidnapping, murder, genocide or anything else that may help them reach their goals.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}}

Sload are apparently not religious and generally reject worship, but will willingly serve [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] if they benefit from it.{{ref|name=ALC}} Some, in particular the famed [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] necromancer [[Lore:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]], also follow the [[Lore:King of Worms|King of Worms]].{{ref|name=Ngas}}{{Ref|name=PoM}}{{ref|name=TPWormgod|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP/War of the Wormgod|''War of the Wormgod'']]}} Despite their general rejection of worship, some accounts describe the Sload engaging in elaborate sacrificial rituals which involve individuals with years of preparation re-enacting the deeds of mythical heroes and villains, before sacrificing themselves via the application of "dessication crystals" to their bodies. Such rituals have been theorized to be a form of worship directed towards the Sload ancestors, or towards unnamed deities of theirs.{{ref|name=ALC|{{Cite Book|A Loathsome Civilization}}}}<noinclude>

Texts appropriated from a defeated [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] fleet describe an audience with an "Elder Distended One" taking place in a submerged tower. The Elder is described as having an "impressively corpulent body and strangely pulsating head" as well as "three eyes that emerged upon its belly" that each opened again as a "toothless mouth" which disgorged undefined objects its attendants eagerly consumed. This account has been theorized to describe the cultural leadership of the Sload. The same text describes a visit at a "Menagerie of Sublime Infection" where all manner of afflictions such as "blood rot" and "festering myriasis" are kept. The facility is thought to represent a "grotesque fascination" with disease.{{ref|name=ALC|{{Cite Book|A Loathsome Civilization}}}} 

Due to their slippery grip, Sloads have eschewed the use of most tools. They do use armor on occasion, as their flabby bodies don't provide much natural protection. In these armors, they are known to use the shells of large [[Lore:Nautilus|nautiluses]], cephalopods native to the waters around Agonio.{{ref|name=SA|[[Online:Summerset Antiquities|Nautilus Shell Guards]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Despite their size, Sloads were known to engage in fencing, and developed an intriguing regimen known as 'Welcoming the Cut.'{{Ref|name=LFNHATRAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}}

==Biology==
[[File:LO-misc-Sload figurehead.jpg|left|thumb|Sload figurehead on a Tamrielic ship]][[File:MW-ing-Sload Soap.jpg|[[Lore:Sload Soap|Sload Soap]], a waxy substance made from the immature non-sentient forms of the Sload|right|thumb]]
It is unknown exactly how Sload [[Lore:Sex|reproduce]]. They are believed to be hermaphrodites in their youths, though the sexual organs seem to be absorbed by the time they are old enough to survive on land.{{ref|name=NRP|{{Cite Book|Notes on Racial Phylogeny}}}} Adult Sload parents care little for the fate of their young they created prior in their life, and will often kill them to make Sload Soap, which they use in necromantic rituals. Sload begin their lives as "disgusting little amorphous grubs" according to [[Lore:Bendu Olo|Bendu Olo]], leader of the punitive expedition sent by Tamrielic nations to Thras after the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]]. The young Sload mutate into aquatic quasi-cephalopods called "polwygles" before they reach adulthood.{{ref|name=FNS|{{Cite Book|Further Notes on the Sload}}}}{{ref|name=AF|{{Cite Book|The Alchemist's Formulary}}}} Sloads are thought to be incapable of having mixed offspring with [[Lore:Man|Man]], [[Lore:Elves|Elves]], and other Beastfolk, and as such they are not considered to be human.{{ref|name=GerH|Dialogue in [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue H|Morrowind]]}} 

Due to the corpulence of the adult Sload's slug-like humanoid body, they are said to spend much of their time wallowing in shallow lagoons and move very slowly.{{ref|name=FNS}} Though according to another, while slow elsewhere, the buoyant Sload move about with relative quickness and grace in the brackish lagoon at the center of the Thras island chain, through an intricate network of coral formations and ancient shipwrecks.{{ref|name=PGE3/OL}} The integument of Sload can slowly adapt to objects and surfaces, which allows them to climb surfaces like slugs.{{ref|name=PGE1/TWR}} Despite their resemblance to slugs, Sload have skulls.{{ref|name=TPWormgod|group=UOL}}
