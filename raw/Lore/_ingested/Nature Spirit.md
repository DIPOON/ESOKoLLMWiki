==Characteristics==
[[File:ON-crown store-Cinderstone Kagouti.jpg|A Cinderstone Kagouti, given form by Druids|thumb|right]]
Nature spirits are described as "dreams of the Green" and "forces of nature in the bosom of Y'ffre",{{ref|name=Laurel|[[Online:Druid Laurel|Druid Laurel]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} made real and given form and purpose either naturally or through the will of practicioners of [[Lore:Nature Magic|nature magic]] such as the [[Lore:Druid|Druids]] and the [[Lore:Wyrd|Wyrd]].{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|[[ON:Cinderstone Kagouti|Cinderstone Kagouti]] mount description in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}} Certain greater spirits are said to be a subset of [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]]'s laws shaped into spirits through [[Lore:Nature Magic|nature magic]],{{ref|name=ESODA|[[ON:Druid Audrine|Druid Audrine]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}} or to have been bound to a location with a certain task by Y'ffre himself.{{ref|name=PIT|[[Online:Elder Pitof|Elder Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They are said to reside in all things, rocks, roots, leaves, brooks, the wind, a field of flowers, or even a swarm of [[Lore:Torchbug|Torchbugs]], though their will might be difficult to perceive, even for those who know how to listen.{{ref|name=Ryvana|[[Online:Druid Ryvana|Druid Ryvana]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} According to the [[Lore:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]], waterfalls house a deluge of water spirits, and each individual stone has its own spirit with its own stories to tell.{{ref|name=Augur of the Obscure|[[ON:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Nature spirits that haven't yet emerged into the physical world are said to "slumber" within the [[Lore:The Green|Green]], druids use a concoction named the "Elixir of Y'ffre's Thoughts", said to temporarily turn the imbiber into a dream or memory also, to communicate with them and exchange knowledge while in that state.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}} When a nature
spirit "dies", it returns to its slumber within the Green, ending its current incarnation.{{ref|name=Chronicle|[[Online:The Chronicle|The Chronicle]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PIT|[[Online:Elder Pitof|Elder Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Many vanquished nature spirits are said to reform or reincarnate, eventually.{{ref|name=Laurel}}{{ref|name=Talomar|[[Online:Talomar|Talomar]]'s dialogue in {{g|Summerset}}}}

Those spirits that are invoked from the Green to fulfill a purpose can be made to take on a variety of different forms, such as that of a [[Lore:Wamasu|wamasu]], [[Lore:Gryphon|gryphon]], or a [[Lore:Faun|faun]].{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|name=orl|[[Online:Archdruid Orlaith|Archdruid Orlaith]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In some cases a spirit might choose to manifest itself in a specific form suited to tasks such as protecting local nature from a fire.{{Ref|name=Lost|{{Cite Book|Spirits of Lost Nature}}}} The purpose for which a nature spirit has been invoked might range from guarding a location to recording and passing on stories however, if a spirit is made to wait for a very long time before it can fulfill the purpose it was given, it can forget itself and grow wild and hostile.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}} Due to this, and how unpredictable they can be, invoking a spirit from the Green through magic is something done sparsely even by Druids, only the [[Lore:Druid King|Druid King]] can safely invoke and command even the greater spirits of nature.{{ref|name=PIT|[[Online:Elder Pitof|Elder Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Laurel}}

The emotions and thoughts of mortals are something nature spirits generally can't understand, preventing them from experiencing such things even through the use of magic, but exceptions exist in the form of spirits such as the Chronicle, whose purpose involves passing on the experiences of mortals.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|name=PIT|[[Online:Elder Pitof|Elder Pitof]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Nature spirits can sense and communicate with nature and other nature spirits, something the spirits themselves refer to as hearing their "song". A nature spirit can perceive those things the element of nature it's linked to touches, an earth spirit's "Stonesight" might allow one to perceive all that rests within the rock of the area it's bound to, a root spirit's to hear through the interconnected roots of all plants, and an air spirit's grant knowledge of what is touched by the air. Druids are known to use ritual altars which allow them to commune with nature spirits and share in their senses while doing so.{{ref|name=Laurel}}{{ref|name=AD|[[ON:Archdruid Devyric|Archdruid Devyric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

When compelled, it is possible for a nature spirit to merge with a mortal vessel, allowing for its power to be drawn upon, this allows for the spirit's "sight", its knowledge of what the element of nature it presides over touches, to be shared by the mortal. Should the vessel's body die, the spirit will be released, freed of prior bonds to a specific location or purpose.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|name=TSD|Events of [[ON:The Stonelore Defense|The Stonelore Defense]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=AD|[[ON:Archdruid Devyric|Archdruid Devyric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Most spirits are bound to a specific location, and to a purpose and nature that typically don't change, and won't act in way that contradicts them, a peaceful flower spirit won't fight and an unmoving spirit of stone won't help move crops. A spirit's purpose and nature can become temporarily imbalanced through the use of powerful magic or the destruction of the nature they preside over, placing great strain on the spirit and potentially making it lash out in anguish, however, even if such a change occurs, the spirit's original nature will be restored when it is reminded of it, as nothing can truly erase it. Spirits that lost the element of nature they were initially bound to can be rehoused, the spirit of a lost river might find a new river to call home. Exceptions to the general rule exist in the form of spirits who were invoked from the Green to make choices of their own, and so might not be bound by rigid purpose or location the way most spirits are. In addition, a spirit's purpose can become cloudy when their objectives are fulfilled, in which case they may need to seek a new purpose for themselves.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|name=Laurel}}{{Ref|name=ONS|{{Cite Book|On Nature Spirits}}}}{{Ref|name=Lost|{{Cite Book|Spirits of Lost Nature}}}}

Should the spirit that's bound to a specific area or part of nature depart, it is possible for another nature spirit to bind themselves to it and take the departed spirit's place. This is said to cause the newly bound spirit to transform and grow to match its new purpose. Such an event occurred when the spirit Frii became the new spirit of [[Lore:Y'ffelon|Mount Firesong]] in order to prevent the volcano from erupting and destroying the [[Lore:Systres|Systres]] archipelago.{{ref|name=ESOFrii}}{{ref|name=Laurel}}

==Cultural Significance==
Nature spirits have a great deal of influence in some of the cultures of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. One of the possible origins for the nature worship of the ancient [[Lore:Druids of Galen|proto-Bretons]] is from the spirit Galen, and they would be later dubbed the retroactive moniker "Druids of Galen". Though the Druids of Galen as a single unifying group are now defunct, modern [[Lore:Druids|druids]] are known to are known to consort with nature spirits.{{Ref|name=ONS|{{Cite Book|On Nature Spirits}}}} Friendships can be established with spirits, or they can also be coerced into doing one's bidding,{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Only Record of Our Plan|ns_base=Online}}}} but this is frowned upon by some druids.{{Ref|name=ONS}} Druids can also communicate with{{ref|name=ONDJ|[[ON:Druid Jend|Druid Jend]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Druid Erold|Druid Erold]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} and form cordial relationships with animals, who can serve as intermediaries between druids and nature spirits.{{ref|[[Online:Wisdom of the Druids|Wisdom of the Druids]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Some nature spirits can take the form of animals.{{ref|name=KST}}{{ref|[[Online:Oldefire Elk|Oldefire Elk]] mount description in {{g|on}}}} The [[Lore:Wyrd Covens|Wyrd Covens]] are also animists who hold nature spirits sacred, as historically druids and the Wyrd were once the same before a religious schism.{{ref|name=ONWD|{{Cite Book|Wyrd and Druid}}}} The 18th of [[Lore:Morning Star|Morning Star]] was known as the [[Lore:Waking Day|Waking Day]], when the people of the [[Lore:Yeorth Burrowland|Yeorth Burrowland]] woke the spirits of nature after the winter, somewhat like their more reverential ancestors, in one of the oldest holidays in High Rock before recorded history.{{ref|name=HOTIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}}

The [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] hold reverence for numerous spirits, spanning the spectrum from grand to minor. In reality, the number of faiths within [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] aligns with the multitude of [[Lore:Reachfolk Clans|clans]] that call it home. Among these clans, some may venerate nature associated spirits such as sacred [[Lore:Elk|elks]] or the spirits of a mountain springs.{{Ref|name=GSOTR|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 2}}}} It was customary to thank to nature spirits before felling trees. Subsequently, the Reachfolk often would plant new saplings in the vacant space left by the fallen tree.{{Ref|name=Malug}}

===Guardian Spirit===
[[Lore:Guardian Spirit|Guardian spirits]] are spectral nature spirits found in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] that are associated with [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]].{{ref|name=KST|Events of [[Skyrim:Kyne's Sacred Trials|Kyne's Sacred Trials]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SoS}} They are often tied to a specific natural landmark, such as a lake, a hill, or a copse of woods. A guardian spirit "haunts" the area it is connected to, and cannot abandon or wander far from the location. The tradition had fallen into obscurity by {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|name=FWB|[[Skyrim:Froki Whetted-Blade|Froki Whetted-Blade]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Marsh Giant===
[[Lore:Marsh Giant|Marsh giants]] are a [[Lore:Giant|giant]]-like race native to [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]. These manifestations of nature are a form of woodland spirit comparable to [[Lore:Spriggan|spriggans]]. They are not related to the common giants of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], but they do share their size and pastoral activities. Their exact level of intelligence is unknown due to limited interactions. However, the Hist apparently have a long and acrimonious history with the Marsh Giants, and urge the Argonians to avoid the creatures if they can.

They play an important role in [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]]'s ecosystem by knocking down dead trees, turning the soil and making room for younger vegetation to grow. This encourages the growth of mosses and suppresses the development of invasive fungi. Marsh giants also hinder loggers, contribute to [[Lore:Birds|avian]] diversity and ward off certain predators, such as feral [[Lore:Cat|cats]].{{ref|name=OMGAS|{{Cite Book|On Marsh Giants: A Study}}}}

===Nereid===
[[Lore:Nereid|Nereids]] (also called '''water-sprites''' by the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]], or '''water nymphs'''){{Ref|name=KCAHC|{{Cite Book|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|ns_base=BK}}}} are [[Lore:Mer|mer]]-like, feminine water spirits that live near bodies of water and can often be heard singing an angelic, but transfixing [[Lore:Music|melody]]. They are known practitioners of [[Lore:Water Magic|water magic]], an obscure form of magic, and can either become hostile or tolerant of people's company.{{Ref|name=KCAHC}} Generally speaking, the Nereid are an intelligent-folk that more often than not, protect the land they live on and its denizens, even claiming old ruins as their own haven.

The '''Leimenid''' is a variant of nereid that practices frost magic. They resemble the typical nereid, but appear more ethereal and in a frosty mist. They are typically found in cold climates such as the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]], or atop wintry mountains like {{Lore Link|Scalecaller Peak}}.{{Ref|name=ONPestilience|[[Online:Plans of Pestilence|Plans of Pestilence]] group quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragon Bones|Dragon Bones]]''}} They have also been used in the [[Lore:Maelstrom|Maelstrom Arena]], the pocket realm of the [[Lore:Demiprince|demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]].{{Ref|name=ONMaelstrom|[[Online:Maelstrom Arena (quest)|Maelstrom Arena]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

===Spriggan===
[[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggans]], often called '''nature's guardians''',{{Ref|name=OBRumours|[[Oblivion:Rumors#Wilderness|Wilderness rumors]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} are nature spirits{{ref|name=FGTS|{{Book Link|Field Guide to Spriggans}}}}{{ref|name=OBPrima|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|55}} that take the form of tree-like female humanoids. They possess moderate intelligence, but despite being revered they are usually hostile towards travelers. Spriggans have their own unique [[Daggerfall:Spriggan|language]], which can be learned by scholars and used to calm the creatures.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Spriggan|Daggerfall skill]]}} They are usually found protecting secluded glades and groves all over [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], often blending in with the plant life and catching trespassers unaware. Spriggans attack with their long, sharp fingers as well as with their teeth, and have the ability to poison enemies. They have incredible regenerative abilities, resulting in adventurers often having to defeat them several times before they remain dead. If threatened, they can call nearby woodland animals to aid them in battle, and some even have the ability to magically summon [[Lore:Black Bear|black bears]].

The [[Lore:Bloodthorn Cult|Bloodthorn Cult]] is known to perform perverted rituals that uses Spriggans as a base, twisting the creatures into [[Lore:Lurcher|lurchers]], which are wooden [[Lore:Golem|golems]] of multiple origins. This is said to leave them a shell of their former selves.{{Ref|name=ESOWI|[[Online:Wyress Ileana|Wyress Ileana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Costumes#Scarecrow Spectre Polymorph|Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase]]}}
In [[Lore:Greenshade|Greenshade]], the tribal bosmer see lurchers as gifts from the [[Lore:Wilderking|Wilderking]], and "embodiments of the raw energy of the forest".{{Ref|name=ESOFrighten|[[Online:Frighten the Fearsome|Frighten the Fearsome]] quest in {{g|eso}}}}

The [[Lore:Gnarl|gnarls]] of the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] are treelike creatures who seem to have some relation to spriggans,{{ref|[[Skyrim:Corrupted Spriggan|Corrupted Spriggans]] have a similar appearance to gnarls and drop Gnarl Bark}} and are sometimes classified with them.{{ref|name=LG|[[Legends:Spriggan|Legends]] card category}}{{ref|name=Utter-Leyton|group=UOL|[[General:Josh Utter-Leyton Isle of Madness Interview|Josh Utter-Leyton Isle of Madness Interview]]}} They are believed to be caretakers of the Shivering Isles' [[Lore:Root|Root System]],{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bark and Sap}}}} and have a truce of sorts with the natural creatures of the Isles.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Living Woods}}}}

===Wispmother===
[[Lore:Wispmother|Wispmothers]] (sometimes '''wispfathers'''){{ref|[[Castles:Wispfather|Wispfathers]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} are magical-energy beings thought to be either ghosts, [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] or nature spirits,{{ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Black Marsh}}}} and they can be found in the wilds of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Killing a wispmother causes all of its attendant [[Lore:Wisp|wisps]] and shades to die. Lydette Viliane of the [[Lore:Synod|Synod]] has proposed that they are elemental personifications of snow or mist.{{ref|name=TW}}

[[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]] is known to send wisps to herald the storms of Rain's Hand. To see one is to see the promise of new growth, new life, and a new chapter in nature's cycle.{{ref|name=Galen Wisp|[[Online:Galen Wisp|Galen Wisp]] collectible description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
