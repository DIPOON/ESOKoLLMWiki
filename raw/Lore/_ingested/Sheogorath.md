==Stories of Sheogorath==

Sheogorath owes his origin to his fellow Daedric Princes, for he was formerly [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]], the Daedric Prince of Order. In times beyond memory, Jyggalag had vastly grown in power, and his influence spread across the seas of Oblivion. The other Daedric princes grew jealous and fearful and cursed Jyggalag to live as Sheogorath, the incarnation of the thing he hated most. The curse did allow Jyggalag to return in his true form at the end of every Era, and reconquer his lands, an event known as the [[Lore:Greymarch|Greymarch]]. After that, however, he would be transformed back into Sheogorath, and Sheogorath would spread madness upon his lands once more.{{ref|name=SI}} 

Sheogorath is one of the first four Daedric Princes mentioned in written history,{{ref|name=OO|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} They say he is the "[[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]-shaped hole" of the world brought into being when [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]'s [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|divine spark]] was removed.{{Ref|name=VOF}} One superstition is to avoid thunderstorms, for they belong to Sheogorath.{{ref|name=IOA}} Another is that madmen are driven to that state by speaking with Sheogorath, who gives them advice and information which is beyond their capacity to know.{{ref|name=CF|{{Cite Book|Chance's Folly}}}} History is littered with stories of worshippers of Sheogorath doing great harm to themselves and others,{{ref|name=TLOA|{{Cite Book|The Liturgy of Affliction}}}} as well as mortals succumbing to madness thanks to his efforts and those of other daedra.{{ref|name=Feyfolken|{{Cite Book|Feyfolken}}}}{{ref|name=FOV|{{Cite Book|Fall of Vitharn}}}} Such people are said to be "touched by Sheogorath",{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}} or "Sheogorath-kissed".{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}}

Sheogorath is, above all, unpredictable. He is the type of deity that does things on a whim to quench his boredom, such as indulging in a sadistic pleasure and treating everyone as a plaything. A great number of myths, stories, and legends notably reflect this. Sheogorath's favorite passtime is torture, {{ref|name=SSRTCCTT|Dialogue in the mission [[Legends:Torture Fun|Tortune Fun]] in [[Legends:Skooma Cat's Playthings|Legends]]}} and Sheogorath himself claims to have spent his first three to four thousand years of existence tormenting butterflies, before eventually growing out of it.{{ref|name=ESOSC}} Butterflies are now associated with him, serving as a sign of his influence.{{ref|name=ESOElazuraandNaraku|[[Online:Elazura|Elazura]]'s and [[Online:Naraku|Naraku]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} [[Lore:New Sheoth|New Sheoth]], the divided capital of the Shivering Isles, is said to have been founded at his whim.{{ref|name=GTNS}} ''[[Lore:Myths of Sheogorath|Myths of Sheogorath]]'' is an anthology of tales that serves as another example: "Sheogorath Invents [[Lore:Music|Music]]" (from the body parts of a young woman), "Sheogorath and King Lyandir" (who was killed by his deranged son after his entire kingdom fell to madness), and "The Contest of Wills" (which Sheogorath wins without even trying). ''[[Lore:Fall of Vitharn|Fall of Vitharn]]'' tells the tale of a Count in the Shivering Isles who disrespected Sheogorath, and who Sheogorath rewarded with an overabundance of madness in his family, leading to their demise. While his fellow Daedric Princes feared Jyggalag's raw power, they must fear Sheogorath's cunning. ''[[Lore:16 Accords of Madness|16 Accords of Madness]]'' are stories of Sheogorath's encounters and inevitable triumphs over other Daedric Princes, such as besting [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] in a duel of champion beasts (by letting Hircine's beast maul itself to death), winning a wager with [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]] (by doing nothing), and claiming the soul of an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] who had murdered one of his followers (by letting [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] do the work and kill the Orc, who turned out to be Malacath's own son). The general theme is that he lets his opponents defeat themselves, remaining impervious and yet doing relatively little unless he's in a bout of (often violent) creativity.{{Ref|name=16Accords|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness}}}} Sheogorath has also sentenced a man to death for the crime of trying to grow a beard.{{ref|name=DD}} 

Sheogorath is fond of threats, in particular, ones written on the back of an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] concubine.{{ref|name=SRSheogorath}} He hates indecision, or maybe he doesn't.{{ref|[[Legends:Loading Tips|Loading Tips]] tips in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} His title of Mad God is also one which gets passed down from Sheogorath to Sheogorath every few thousand years.{{ref|[[Legends:Matchmaking Messages|Chaos Arena matchmaking messages]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} Sheogorath's favored weapons are the sword and crossbow.{{ref|name=sJtA| Sheogorath's dialogue in [[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Daggefall]]}}

==Religion and Culture==
[[File:ON-npc-Skooma Cat 02.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Sheogorath as the Skooma Cat]]
===Khajiit===
Sheogorath is one of the many prominent Daedric Princes in [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] culture.{{Ref|name=VOF}} To them, he is "Sheggorath",{{Ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} a crook-tailed Cat who shares his madness with sugar-tooths through [[Lore:Skooma|skooma]],{{ref|name=ONHaduras}} a hallucinatory narcotic{{ref|name=EGTE|{{Cite Book|EGT|Elsweyr}}}} that is known to leave its victims in a constant, alternating state of delusional euphoria and lethargy.{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Elsweyr}}}} Indeed, he is known as the Skooma Cat, "for nothing is madder than a cat on skooma".{{Ref|name=WOCMA}} 

The Khajiit believe he was born from the union of [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]] and [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]], from whose union all the God Cats came, including Sheggorath.{{ref|name=ONHaduras|[[ON:Priest Haduras|Priest Haduras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Elsweyr|ESO]]}} Pre-[[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|ri'Datta]] texts state that he is an adversarial spirit that tests Khajiit on the Path by making them doubt the truth of their own thoughts, beliefs, and actions. He must be faced along the Path and be overcome before a Khajiit can visit [[Lore:Apocrypha|Hermorah's library]]. Some ancient Khajiit tribes believed Sheggorath is dead and has been replaced by something Other.{{ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}} The Skooma Cat takes the form of an [[Lore:Alfiq|Alfiq]], and acts more like a common housecat than a Khajiit. For instance, he is easily distracted by yarn.{{ref|name=ESOSC}}

One of the earliest documented encounters with Sheggorath was once told in the now lost tale of [[Lore:Khunzar-ri|Khunzar-ri]] and the Skooma Cat.{{ref|name=ONKhunzar-ri|[[ON:Khunzar-ri|Khunzar-ri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Dragonhold|ESO]]}}

===Chimer/Dunmer===
[[File:MW-quest-Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies 03.jpg|thumb|right|Baar Dau (Morrowind)]][[File:LO-misc-Sheo Mask.jpg|left|thumb|150px|"The Comforting Face of Tendrils" - Dunmer mask aspect of Sheogorath that can only be worn by the insane]]
Sheogorath is believed to have accompanied the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] on their exodus to Morrowind.{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=THOT}} However, when the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] took power in Morrowind, Sheogorath, as well as [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], and [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], became known as one of the "[[Lore:House of Troubles|Four Corners of the House of Troubles]]" to the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] and called the '''Prince of Fools'''.{{ref|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-Daedric Symbols.jpg|Morrowind Concept art]]}} It is written that when he rebelled against the Tribunal, the worship of Sheogorath became punishable by death.{{ref|name=THOT}}{{intnote|nb 3|[nb 3]}} Sheogorath rebelled by tricking the moon [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]], inspiring it to hurl itself at the [[Lore:Vivec City|city of Vivec]], and justified his actions by claiming Vivec was built in mockery of the heavens. Vivec is then said to have frozen Baar Dau in its descent with one gesture; the moon then swore itself to the Tribunal's service forever.{{ref|name=TPP}} Nonetheless, [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] apparently made an agreement with Sheogorath to protect his people from madness, an agreement the Dunmer must occasionally renew by performing the Pilgrimages of the Four Corners. Sheogorath's shrines were abandoned, but many have remained standing into modern times.{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Legends say Daedra Lords such as Sheogorath built the great shrines themselves because mortals could not build anything grand enough to suit them.{{ref|name=MWSinnammu}} After the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] destroyed the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] and Vivec disappeared, Baar Dau eventually resumed its descent, destroying Vivec City, fulfilling its original goal, and instigating the disasters of the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]].{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}

Dunmer believe Sheogorath tests them for psychological weakness. In many legends, he is called upon by one Dunmer faction against another, though whether he betrays or helps those who call him proves unpredictable. They often associate him with the fear other races have of them, especially those who might prove useful allies.{{Ref|name=THOT}} Though nascent for thousands of years in Morrowind, Sheogorath worship began picking up again following the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] with the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], and his shrines began receiving more visitors as more and more Dunmer fell into "dark worship".{{ref|name=MWDunmer|[[Morrowind:Dark Elf|Dark Elf]] dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Followers===
[[File:LG-chaosarena-Temple.png|Daedric and mortal creatures alike paying reverence to Sheogorath|thumb|left]][[File:ON-banner-Banner, Jester's Standard 02.png|150px|right|The banner of the Jester's Festival]]
The [[Lore:Jester's Festival|Jester's Festival]] is an annual holiday that falls on the 28th of Rain's Hand.{{ref|name=WFDF|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Rain's Hand|Jester's Day]] description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The origins of the Jester's Festival are speculated, with some believing that it originated with Sheogorath once turning the whole world mad.{{ref|name=JFJQA|[[Online:Jester Queen Ayrenn|Jester Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Indeed, there appears to have some association with Sheogorath, as the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] himself once manifested under the alias of a Breton named "Samuel Gourone" during the celebration to spice it up in honor of his daedric sibling, calling him the '''Cheese Prince'''.{{ref|name=ONGBT|[[Online:Getting the Band Together|Getting the Band Together]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Daedric cultists visit the Shrine of Sheogorath during the Festival of the Mad; this tradition may be local to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{ref|name=Gwinas|[[Oblivion:Gwinas|Gwinas]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

''[[Lore:The Blessings of Sheogorath|The Blessings of Sheogorath]]'', a prayer among Sheogorath worshippers, asserts that without Sheogorath, "all thought would be linear, and all Feeling would be fleeting". They praise him because it is Sheogorath "who tricks us when we are foolish, punishes us when we are wrong, tortures us when we are unmindful, and loves us in our imperfection".

Within the Shivering Isles themselves, society functions similarly to Tamriel - but with obvious and glaring exceptions, of course. The mentally ill, bifurcated populace have held a range of beliefs about Sheogorath.{{ref|name=SI}} Some [[Lore:Zealots|Zealots]] revered him as a living god,{{ref|name=Z|{{Cite Book|Zealotry}}}} while others, labeled [[Lore:Heretics|Heretics]], thought he was nothing more than a mortal impostor.{{ref|name=HT|{{Cite Book|Heretical Thoughts}}}} Some urged armed uprising,{{ref|name=BAS|{{Cite Book|Bark and Sap}}}} and many committed various deranged acts in attempts to gain his favor.{{ref|name=SI}}

Sheogorath devised a unique spell called Manipulate Weather, and has been known to teach this spell to his most favored followers. The spell allows the manipulation of the climate within an entire region. Sheogorath himself determines the weather effects the spell produces, and the spells effects are random. For example, those trying to summon a flash flood may instead summon a torrential rain storm.{{ref|name=WW|{{Cite Book|War Weather}}}}

Some say that [[Lore:Grummite|Grummites]] worship Sheogorath, though this has never been proven.{{ref|name=SIRumor|[[SI:Rumors|Rumors]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}

==History==
===First Era===
In the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Shalidor established the island of [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]] as a retreat and sanctuary for all mages. However, in the time period roughly around the [[Lore:First Era#Twenty-Third_Century|Twenty-Third Century]] of the First Era,{{ref|name=LAS|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Scribing}}}} Shalidor wagered Eyevea to Sheogorath for the [[Lore:Folium Discognitum|Folium Discognitum]], a tome of knowledge which he had been obsessed with obtaining. Shalidor lost, and the island was duly spirited away to the Shivering Isles, where it would remain for thousands of years. Shalidor went on to regret wagering the island, feeling that he had been tricked by the Mad God.

Between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand, {{Year|1E 2920}}{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}}, Sheogorath, along with seven other powerful Daedric Princes, entered into a contract known as the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] with the Tribunal God [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]].  Little is known about the Compact but it is theorized that it prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. It is also speculated that, in order to secure the agreement of the Princes, Sotha Sil either made a credible threat to them or presented a significant offer in exchange.{{ref|name=TCC}}{{ref|name=CWCSothaDialogue|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

===Second Era===
Sometime before {{Year|2E 582}},{{ref|name=WitchwiseHeaddress|Description of the [[Online:Witchwise Headdress|Witchwise Headdress]]}} the witch [[Lore:Naenra Waerr|Naenra Waerr]] created the sword [[Lore:Umbra|Umbra]]{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} at the request of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], who wanted the sword as a tool of mischief which would send him souls from the mortal realm.{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}} Naenra created the sword, but it was unstable, requiring Vile to give the witch a piece of his power to be imbued in the sword in order to stabilize it. However, Naenra (sometimes credited with being Sheogorath in disguise) supposedly tricked him, as the piece of Vile's power imbued in the sword became a sentient being which called itself Umbra. Naenra was executed for her evil creation, but the sword remained behind, becoming the identity to those unfortunate enough to wield it.{{Ref|name=LOS}}

In 2E 582, the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] was expelled from [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and became fractured due to the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], despite claiming neutrality in the war. Although he had had nothing to do with the guild in life, Shalidor returned to the affairs of mortals in order to win back Eyevea from Sheogorath so that it could serve its original purpose and act as a sanctuary for the guild.{{ref|name=MGESO|Events of the [[Online:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Sheogorath agreed to give Shalidor the means of reclaiming the island if his champion agreed to complete four trials for the Prince's amusement. An [[Lore:Hero|adept]] of the Mages Guild agreed to undertake the {{Lore Link|Trial of Eyevea}}, and was then subjected to various inane tests. At the end of each trial, the guild was given a tome containing clues to returning Eyevea to [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]]. {{Lore Link|Valaste}}, the guild's Master of Incunabula, took on the burden of decrypting these books, as Shalidor himself was forbidden to read them. The guild was eventually successful in restoring the island, but as a final test, Sheogorath neglected to remove Eyevea's [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] guardians before returning it. As such, it was swarming with [[Lore:Golden Saint|Golden Saint]]s and [[Lore:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducer]]s, which needed to be cleared out. With that done, the adept was forced to fight [[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]] to finally clear the island of Sheogorath's influence.{{ref|name=MGESO}}

In the same year, Sheogorath turned his gaze to [[Lore:Southpoint|Southpoint]], and took advantage of a disgruntled mayor who claimed to have lost his birthright. The mayor demanded power to gain the respect he needed for his position, but Sheogorath instead cursed his tongue with madness, and let him make a mess of the town. With madness pouring into Southpoint, its citizens began killing one another in the streets and Dominion soldiers cordoned off the town. The [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] ended the madness, dealing with both the corrupt Imperial mayor and the Daedric Prince, and the city remained in the Dominion until its eventual dissolution.{{ref|name=GOMESO|[[Online:The Grip of Madness|The Grip of Madness]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Soon after, Sheogorath donned the guise of the Skooma Cat and ravaged the Two Moons at Tenmar Temple in [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]]. To receive a premonition or message from the Twin Moons, instead of using moon sugar in her ceremony, the temple's moon-bishop had used the dangerous narcotic skooma, and Sheogorath came to her. He plotted to convince the priests to blind themselves with the [[Lore:Fork of Horripilation|Fork of Horripilation]] in order to be hidden from the [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]] that plagued [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]. Sheogorath was stopped by the Vestige, who located and confiscated the Fork, and rescued the Moon-Bishop from his clutches.{{ref|name=ONTLTMQ|[[ON:The Lunacy of Two Moons|The Lunacy of Two Moons]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the Planemeld were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was Sheogorath, portrayed conversing with Valaste.{{ref|[[Online:Painting: 10-Year Anniversary|Painting: 10-Year Anniversary]] furnishing depiction in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] summoned Sheogorath and dispatched a battlemage who had displeased the Prince. In return, the agent was given the Wabbajack by a worshipper of Sheogorath.{{ref|name=DFSheogorath|[[Daggerfall:Sheogorath's Quest|Events]] of Daggerfall}} 

Sheogorath is a known visitor to the [[Lore:Blind God|Blind God]]'s realm of [[Lore:Mantellan Crux|Mantellan Crux]] within [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]].  Sheogorath, who was visiting the Blind God at the time, aided the Agent of the Blades retrieve the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]]. {{ref|name=JtA| Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Journey to Aetherius]]''}} Because of this, Sheogorath inadvertently played a key role in the events that led up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} 
[[File:SI-creature-Jyggalag 02.jpg|thumb|right|Jyggalag]]

In {{Year|3E 427}}, Sheogorath tasked the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] with retrieving the Fork of Horripilation from Big Head, a mad [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] hermit and a worshipper of Sheogorath who lived in a small shack on an island in the [[Lore:Sheogorad|Sheogorad]] region of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. Sheogorath instructed the Nerevarine with an odd task, to kill a giant [[Lore:Bull Netch|Bull Netch]] with the fork. Big Head willingly gave the fork to the Nerevarine, who then killed the Bull Netch and returned the fork to Sheogorath, and was rewarded with the [[Lore:Spear of Bitter Mercy|Spear of Bitter Mercy]].{{ref|name=MWSQ|[[MW:Sheogorath's Quest|Sheogorath's Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} How Sheogorath came into possession of the spear is unknown.

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Sheogorath was aided by the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] in tormenting the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] settlement of [[Lore:Border Watch|Border Watch]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The inhabitants were very superstitious, and were driven into a state of frenzy when the Prince simulated their {{Lore Link|K'sharra Prophecy}}, said to mark the end of the world. These occurrences were reported in a special edition of the Black Horse Courier newspaper entitled ''Rain of Burning Dogs!''.{{ref|name=OBSheogorath|[[Oblivion:Sheogorath|Events]] of Oblivion}}{{ref|name=ROBD|{{Cite Book|Rain of Burning Dogs!|ns_base=OB}}}} It is unknown whether this took place before or after the Hero assumed the position of Sheogorath; regardless, the Hero received the Wabbajack from either Sheogorath or [[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]].{{ref|name=OBSheogorath}}

{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''The Greymarch comes, and Jyggalag walks. Or runs. Never skips, sidles, or struts. Mostly, he just destroys everything around him.''|Sheogorath{{ref|name=SISheogorath}}}}
At the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], in order to deal with the coming Greymarch that threatened to destroy the Shivering Isles once again, Sheogorath manifested a portal upon the [[Lore:Niben Bay|Niben Bay]], and invited a champion into the Isles. As the portal was an invitation and posed no danger to Mundus, the Coldharbour Compact was not violated. {{ref|name=HaskillSI|[[SI:Haskill|Haskill]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} The Hero of Kvatch mantled Sheogorath, and defeated Jyggalag and the forces of Order.{{ref|name=SI}}{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on [https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2010/12/26/decrypting-the-elder-scrolls.aspx Decrypting The Elder Scrolls] [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}} In doing so, the Hero finally broke the cycle, and Jyggalag himself stated he was free from the curse.{{ref|name=SI}} Nonetheless, the "Gentleman With a Cane" continued to stroll through Tamriel, and, as always, one can never be sure what his visits will bring.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:LG-quest-A_Reckoning_01.jpg|thumb|right|The Prince of Madness driven to madness]]
Although Sheogorath is known to outplay his opponents, legends tell of a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] by the name of [[Lore:Talym Rend|Talym Rend]] who sometime in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]{{Intnote|nb 4|[nb 4]}} beat the Mad God at his own game. Talym struck a deal with Sheogorath: make him forget his involvement in the death of his son, Novos. Sheogorath agreed, then tampered with his memories so he thought he was fighting to cure his son of madness instead.{{ref|name=TFBIOM|Dialogue in the mission [[Legends:The Final Battle|The Final Battle]] in [[Legends:Isle of Madness (story)|Isle of Madness]]}} The Prince requested three souls to be driven mad in return for restoring Novos to sanity. When he had done as directed, Talym returned home only to realize that Novos had been dead the entire time. Sheogorath denied Talym another audience when he returned to the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]], and Talym wandered around the realm aimlessly, slowly driving himself mad with unanswered questions. Eventually, he regained a semblance of composure and journeyed to Knifepoint Hollow. There, Talym was informed by [[Lore:Dyus|Dyus]], the librarian of Jyggalag, that he might gain an audience with Sheogorath if he retrieved an artifact of Jyggalag. Sheogorath finally granted Talym an audience when he retrieved the [[Lore:Sword of Jyggalag|Sword of Jyggalag]], and used the [[Lore:Memory Wand|Memory Wand]] on him to reveal Talym's original intent and memories. Sheogorath mocked Talym, and in his anger, Talym turned the Memory Wand against Sheogorath and forced him to recall his memories of his previous incarnation: the Daedric Prince of Order, Jyggalag. The memories pained Sheogorath, who pleaded with Talym to stop. He fulfilled Talym's wishes and let him and his companions free from his grasp.{{ref|name=IOM|Events of [[Legends:Isle of Madness|Isle of Madness]]}}

Circa {{Year|4E 180}},{{ref|[[Blades:Junius the Elder|Junius the Elder]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} Sheogorath disguised himself as a mad Breton by the name of Theodor Gorlash (an anagram of "Lord Sheogorath"),{{intnote|nb 1|[nb 1]}} and encouraged the [[Lore:Hero#The Warrior|Warrior]] to enter a portal outside the town of [[Lore:Rivercrest|Rivercrest]]. The portal led to [[Lore:The Abyss|the Abyss]], a mysterious realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] consisting of a never-ending series of rooms and corridors, both real and unreal at the same time, which together form an endless dungeon.{{ref|name=Theodor|[[BL:Theodor Gorlash|Theodor Gorlash]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the former [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] High Priest of [[Lore:Mania|Mania]], [[Lore:Dervenin|Dervenin]], who at the time seemed like a simple beggar, convinced the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] to assist him in finding his master. This resulted in the Dragonborn unknowingly entering the mind of the dead [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]], tasked with convincing Sheogorath to return to the Shivering Isles. He agreed to return them, but only if the Dragonborn could escape Pelagius' mind. Sheogorath decided to assist the Dragonborn by providing the Wabbajack, along with instructions on how to use it to remedy Pelagius of his insanity. Having completed several challenges and conquering the dead emperor's inner fears, the Dragonborn was able to return to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], but not before being given the [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] by Sheogorath as a parting gift.{{ref|name=SRTMM|[[Skyrim:The Mind of Madness|Events]] of Skyrim}}

===Cold Finger===
'''Cold Finger''' is powerful magical staff said to have been created in the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] by [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] himself. The staff was owned by [[Lore:Dervenin|Dervenin]] for a while, but due to a cruel joke his mad master played on him, the staff ended up with a [[Lore:Skeever|Giant Skeever]]. Eventually, the Giant Skeever was defeated by warriors from [[Lore:Hero#Dynasty of Odar the Brave|Odar's Kingdom]], to which Dervenin turned for help, but the local ruler decided to keep the staff  rather than return it to Dervenin.{{ref|Event [[Castles:Skeevers of Unusual Size|Skeevers of Unusual Size]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}

===Associated Servants and Creatures===
The most iconic Daedric servants of the Mad God are the Aureal and Mazken, both rival daedra with matriarchal societies that look down on males as inferiors. They each make up half of Sheogorath's army, with the Aureal serving the denizens of Bliss, and the Mazken doing the same for the denizens of Dementia.{{ref|name=SIGSR|[[Shivering:Golden Saint|Golden Saint]]'s information in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}{{ref|name=SIDSR|[[Shivering:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducer]]'s information in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}{{ref|name=SASL|{{Cite Book|Saints and Seducers}}}} Although [[Lore:Hunger|Hungers]] are typically associated with [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], they are known to inhabit the isles as servitors and guards and are said to be the perfect representation of Sheogorath's darker side.{{ref|name=TSB|{{Cite Book|The Shivering Bestiary}}}} [[Lore:Ravenous Rodent|Ravenous Rodents]] are tiny [[Lore:Daedrat|daedrats]] that stare with forlorn eyes, considered to be Sheogorath's pests. They chitter quietly, and those who listen carefully may be able to understand what they have to say, with great calamity befalling those who listen to their lies.{{Ref|name=MIT}}{{Ref|name=TWotR|{{Cite Book|The Words of the Rodent|ns_base=Online}}}}

The Shivering Isles are home to creatures that are seemingly not Daedric in nature, such as the large mantis-like [[Lore:Elytra|Elytra]], and the [[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggan]]-like [[Lore:Gnarl|Gnarl]]. The most unique fauna are the [[Lore:Grummite|Grummites]], frog-like [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] with a complex life cycle.{{ref|name=SIBaliwog|[[Shivering:Baliwog|Baliwog]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}
