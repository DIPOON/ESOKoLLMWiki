[[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] is a great city of the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]], and the capital of the [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|kingdom of Sentinel]]. It is located in the northwestern hills of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], overlooking the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] to the north,{{ref|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel]]}} and bounded by the surrounding [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]] to the south.{{ref|name=STJOA|{{Cite Book|Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r}}}} However, some fertile fields can be found along the coast near the city,{{ref|name=ONLoading|[[Online:Loading Screens|ESO loading screen]]}} and the city is actually a major population center in Hammerfell.{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}}

The city is an exotic retreat for the nobility of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], who delight in the cooking, craftsmanship, and plays which can be found there.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}} Many great and famous people throughout history have called Sentinel their home, including the renowned poet [[Lore:Weltan|Weltan]] and the famous Redguard hero [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]].{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Alik'r}}}}{{ref|name=EOTAW|{{Cite Book|Epode of the Ansei Wards|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=Redguard|Events of ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} <includeonly>{{#ifeq:{{NAMESPACE}}|Lore||For more information, see the [[Lore:Sentinel|lore article]].}}</includeonly><noinclude>

==History==
[[File:DF-banner-Sentinel Crest.png|thumb|left|The banner of Grandee Yaghoub became Sentinel's crest]]
===The Rise of Sentinel===
Before the Redguards arrived from Yokuda, the [[Lore:Mer|elves]] and later [[Lore:Breton|Manmeri]] established an outpost at what is now Sentinel in order to protect fisherfolk and seafaring merchants from inland [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s, [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]], and other hazards in the desert.{{ref|name=PGE3Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}}{{ref|name=EGH|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Hammerfell}}}} Little did they know that the greatest threat would come by sea.

After the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] first landed in [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]], the warrior-sailors of the Grandee [[Lore:Yaghoub|Yaghoub]] sailed north until they rounded [[Lore:Cape Shira|Cape Shira]], becoming the first of the Ra Gada to discover [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. Yaghoub decided to make his home there. As they sailed "toward [[Lore:The Steed|the Steed]] at dawning of the seventeenth of Second Seed", a watchman spotted a suitable harborage, and Yaghoub declared they would build a city there, calling it Sentinel after the watchman.{{ref|name=STJOA}}

They quickly discovered and established control over the locals' port with little if any opposition. Yaghoub conscripted the locals to serve as masons and servants in the building of his new palace, [[Lore:Samaruik|Samaruik]], and the rest of the city.{{ref|name=STJOA}} Samaruik, also called Sentinel Palace, remains the oldest and largest architectural achievement in the city, and several additions have been built onto it over the years.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}} After Sentinel was attacked by Abecean pirates, Yaghoub built the {{Lore Link|Impervious Vault}} - a fortified storehouse under the city, most notable for housing the [[Lore:Ansei Wards|Ansei Wards]].{{ref|name=ONLoading}} When the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] followed the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]] into Hammerfell, they considered Sentinel a "worthy seat", and many of the [[Lore:Na-Totambu|Na-Totambu]] settled there (but the city has always been considered a Forebear stronghold, though that term wouldn't be in use until the days of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]).{{ref|name=STJOA}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}

Of course, the people of Sentinel still had their share of setbacks. They had to adapt to life as merchants, a stark contrast to their origins as warriors. The city did not exhibit the same population growth as [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] during the First Era, with the land itself keeping the population in check.{{ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}} Once, [[Lore:Peregrine Direnni|Peregrine Direnni]] drove an entire Ra Gada flotilla back to Sentinel by using her prodigious sorcery to manipulate the waves of the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} Like the rest of the Iliac Bay, they also faced the devastation brought by the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]], the [[Lore:War of Righteousness|War of Righteousness]], and an invasion from [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]].{{ref|name=AHOD}}

===[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]===
[[File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg|left|thumb|Sentinel circa {{Year|2E 582}}]][[File:ON-place-Sentinel Docks.jpg|thumb|right|Sentinel Docks]]
Sentinel and the surrounding area have long been notorious for widespread, underground [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]].{{ref|name=RGCyrus|[[Redguard:N'Gasta#Dialogue|Cyrus' dialogue with N'Gasta]] in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, under the reign of the Forebear King [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]], Sentinel came under the threat of attack from an undead army. The army was vanquished, though, in what the Forebear king asserted was a [[Lore:Eight Divines|divine]] intervention.{{ref|name=SAMITA|{{Cite Book|Sacrilege and Mayhem in the Alik'r}}}}

During the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], control of Hammerfell reverted to the hereditary monarchy of the Na-Totambu. To the chagrin of the Forebears, the new High King boldly moved his throne from the old capital Hegathe to the more prosperous Sentinel. By this time, Sentinel had mastered a third of the trade in the entire Iliac Bay. Upon the death of High King [[Lore:Thassad II|Thassad II]] in {{Year|2E 862}}, the Forebears retook their city by force. The vengeful Crown Prince [[Lore:A'tor|A'tor]] set sail to the city to avenge his father, resulting in one of the bloodiest massacres in Tamrielic history. When [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] swept in and reestablished [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodilic]] control over the province, it was seen as a humanitarian intervention.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}

===[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]===
[[File:DF-place-Sentinel Palace 01.png|Samaruik circa {{Year|3E 405}}|left|thumb]][[File:DF-map-Sentinel (city).jpg|Map of Sentinel (Daggerfall)|right|thumb]]
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Sentinel was an active settlement, being known as the ''"Guardian of [[Lore:Starfall Bay|Starfall Bay]]."'' It was ruled by King Caacte, and it had a rivalry with [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]].{{Ref|name=Arena|[[Arena:Sentinel|Sentinel]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Sentinel was neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Chasetown|Chasetown]] to the south, and [[Lore:Lainlyn|Lainlyn]] to the east.{{Ref|name=MapArena|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

Despite the defeat during the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] circa {{Year|3E 402}}, the city of Sentinel continued to flourish, and it was renowned at the time as the place to go to earn some quick gold or find a bit of adventure.{{ref|name=DFTutorial|Rumors heard during the [[Daggerfall:Tutorial|tutorial]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Hammerfell was controlled from the city's throne.{{ref|name=DFMynisera|[[Daggerfall:Mynisera|Mynisera]]'s dialogue in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} An unsuccessful assassination attempt on the life of Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]] was widely attributed to jealous Daggerfall royalty, though this was never proven.{{ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}}

===[[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]===
<center>{{Quote Box|''Above the sea Sentinel shines,<br>Her domes agleam in the Iliac dawn.<Br>Her people throng the bazaars, and find<br>Their way between man's walls of stone.<br>But even in the towered town,<br>Sandals tread tracks in Alik'r sand.''|''[[Lore:On the Immortality of Dust|On the Immortality of Dust]]''}}</center>

In {{Year|4E 42}}, Altmer dissidents who had fled to Sentinel from the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] in [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Alinor]] were attacked by Thalmor operatives in what became known as the [[Lore:Night of Green Fire|Night of Green Fire]]. Their efforts to fight back against the Thalmor were in vain and, by the time [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] soldiers arrived, the entire refugee district of the city had been destroyed. According to Legate Fasendil, an Altmer stationed in Sentinel at the time as a member of the Imperial Legion, the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] was not content with killing dissidents at home, so they went to Hammerfell to finish the job.{{ref|name=LF|[[Skyrim:Legate Fasendil|Legate Fasendil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Thalmor agents were also harassing and possibly murdering refugees in [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]] and Sentinel around {{Year|4E 48}}; the victims were all of mixed blood or had associations considered by the Dominion  to be "unclean."{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}<!--{{rp|?}}--> 

Animosity between the Crowns and Forebears was sometimes so great that the factions have been known to leave each other to their own fates during foreign invasions.{{ref|name=PGE3Ham}} But in {{Year|4E 173}}, when the Crown city Hegathe found itself besieged during the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] by the seemingly unstoppable forces of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], a Forebear army from Sentinel came to their aid and broke the siege. This led to the reconciliation of the two factions.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}
