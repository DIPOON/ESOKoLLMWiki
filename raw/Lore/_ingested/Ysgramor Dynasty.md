# Lore:Ysgramor Dynasty

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore History Trail}}[[File:SR-place-Palace of the Kings.jpg|thumb|Ysgramor's palace in Windhelm]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Ysgramor Dynasty|Ysgramor Dynasty]] (also called the '''Ysgramor Clan'''){{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} is the name given to the series of [[Lore:Empires|kings]] that ruled [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] after the time of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic]] and [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. It pre-dates and overlaps with the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]] by centuries. Initially, Ysgramor established his kingdom after his victory against the [[Lore:Snow Elves|Snow Elves]]. In the aftermath of [[Lore:The Return|The Return]], Ysgramor laid the foundations of the first human Empire by building the city of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] and the [[Lore:Palace of the Kings|Palace of the Kings]] which dominated the center of the Old City. [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]], [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], [[Lore:the Rift|the Rift]], and [[Lore:the Pale|the Pale]], collectively known as the "Old Holds", were also established.{{Ref|name=PGE3S}}{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}

After Ysgramor died, his descendants went on to rule his kingdom for centuries after. However, it was also around this time the authority of the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] took hold and [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priests]] ruled the land with an iron fist. The relationship between the cult and Ysgramor's Dynasty in respect to ruling is unknown, but some sources speculate that Ysgramor could have been a Dragon Priest himself (or at least associated with them) given his stature at the time.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Kurt Kuhlmann's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Also, weaponry and heirlooms bearing dragon-headed mofits were known to be a mark of the royal line.{{ref|[[Online:Eastmarch Antiquities#Royal_Ivory_Hilt|Royal Ivory Hilt]] antiquity codex entry in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

The Dragon Cult was eventually overthrown after the Dragon War and the line of kings from Ysgramor's Dynasty continued in the following centuries. While many of the kings that came after Ysgramor remain unknown, one prominent ruler stands out. King [[Lore:Harald|Harald]] was the thirteenth in Ysgramor's line and is remembered for being the first king to relinquish all holdings in [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]. Establishing the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s as a separate people, he was responsible for the consolidation of Skyrim as a centralized kingdom.{{ref|[[Online:Eastmarch Antiquities#Sacred_Chalice_of_Ysgramor|Sacred Chalice of Ysgramor]] antiquity codex entry in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}

The Jagged Crown dates back to around Harald's reign and served as the symbol of office for the High King. The period of its usage is remembered as a time when the High King reigned by right of arms and collective adulation.{{ref|name=SRGalmar|[[Skyrim:Galmar Stone-Fist|Galmar Stone-Fist]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Around {{Year|1E 184}}, Harald established the first [[Lore:Moot|Moot]] - a gathering of representatives from each Hold to choose the next King from qualified members of the royal family. This continued as the system of succession for centuries.{{Ref|name=ONGirdleAnti|[[Online:Western Skyrim Antiquities#King's Belt Plate|King's Belt Plate]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}{{ref|name=PGE1S}}

Ancient records indicate that Ysgramor's heirs battled a mysterious figure named [[Lore:Aumriel|Aumriel]] for decades until it was sealed away in [[Lore:Frostmere Crypt|Frostmere Crypt]].{{Ref|name=LL|{{Cite Book|Lost Legends}}}} The Nordic goal of conquering the vast new land of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] was fully realized under Harald's son, [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage]]. King Vrage initiated multiple military expansions in {{Year|1E 240}} that pushed past the borders of Ysgramor's kingdom and into [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], and heralded a greater empire known as the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]]. While it is unknown exactly how many rulers were part of the dynasty, the Ysgramor's lineage ended {{Year|1E 369}} with the death of [[Lore:Borgas|King Borgas]].{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=PGE3S}}<noinclude>
