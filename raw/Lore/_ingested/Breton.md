# Lore:Breton

<noinclude>{{Trail|Races}}[[Category:Lore-All Terms]]{{TOCright|limit=3}}{{Featured Article}}[[File:LO-race-Breton.png|thumb|left|Illustration of a typical Breton male]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] (from the [[Lore:Ehlnofex Languages|Ehlnofex]] ''beratu'' meaning "half"){{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}} are a [[Lore:Multiraciality|hybrid]]{{ref|name=CotDC|{{Cite Book|Crimes of the Daggerfall Covenant}}}} race of both [[Lore:Man|human]] and [[Lore:Mer|elven]] ancestry that primarily inhabit their ancestral homeland of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], and the [[Lore:The Systres|Systres Archipelago]].<noinclude></noinclude>{{ref|name=ONSHV|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}}{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ|[[General:High Isle Preview—The Zone|High Isle Preview—The Zone]] from the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}{{ref|name=OBBR|[[Oblivion:Races#Oblivion Remastered|Breton Race Backgrounds]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}} Bretondom originated from the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]] of High Rock and [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] interbreeding{{ref|name=PGE3A}} for centuries as far back as the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{Ref|name=MDBMM|{{Cite Book|Modern Day Bretons: Man or Mer?}}}}{{ref|name=LATD|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}} This mixed ancestry gives Bretons a unique perspective on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]'s oldest feud: the conflict between men and mer.{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL|[[General:ESO 2022 Global Reveal|ESO 2022 Global Reveal]]}} The Bretons' Nedic ancestors are referred to as the [[Lore:Druids of Galen|Druids of Galen]] (or proto-Bretons){{ref|name=LTP2022|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Leamon Tuttle's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=LADCG|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Druid Circles of Galen}}}} by retroactive nomenclature,{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=LATD}}{{ref|name=RDBA|[[Arena:Races#Breton|Breton Race Description]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ}} while [[Lore:Direnni|Clan Direnni]] are primarily credited as being their Aldmer ancestors.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=LATD|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}} Bretons are also distantly related to the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachfolk]].{{intnote|Reachmen|[nb 2]}}{{ref|name=SRAV|[[Skyrim:Anton Virane|Anton Virane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}}

The Direnni left a significant mark on the Bretons: the race's obsession with nobility, status, and lineage can be traced back to their days as vassals of Clan Direnni.{{ref|name=ONBS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 5: Breton Style}}}} Bretons possess an inborn and instinctive bond for the [[Lore:Magic|magical arts]], often attributed to their elven blood,{{ref|[[Shadowkey:Races#Breton|Breton Race Description]] in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}}{{ref|name=TCCAG}}{{ref|name=MWGDB|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue B|Breton]] dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and they are known for their intelligence and quick, perceptive grasp of [[Lore:Magic|spellcraft]], [[Lore:Enchanting|enchantment]], and [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]].{{ref|name=TS|{{Cite Book|The Sage}}}}{{ref|name=HOTIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}}{{ref|name=KE9|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part IX}}}}{{ref|name=MWGDB}} 

Bretons are also noted for having some natural resistance to destructive and magical energies, a trait associated with their race but not tied to their elven lineage.{{Ref|name=AltmerStats|High Elf racial abilities in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' and ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=MWGDB}}{{Ref|[[Online:Shifty Tom|Shifty Tom]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Captain Blackheart's Log|ns_base=Online}}}} However, their shorter [[Lore:Lifespan|human lifespans]] place them at a disadvantage compared to their elven counterparts, whose naturally long lives grant them far more time to study, refine their craft, and establish lasting reputations.{{Ref|name=IWADE|{{Cite Book|Interview with a Dark Elf|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 2}}}}{{Ref|name=LAHT}}{{ref|[[Online:Margrette Canne|Margrette Canne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Even so, their potential has not gone unrecognized. The Assistant [[Lore:Sapiarch|Sapiarch]] of Culinary Arts once remarked that Bretons were the second-best sorcerers after the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], believing they had the potential to achieve more than they have demonstrated so far if given equal opportunity.{{ref|name=Cookbook2|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook Vol. 2}}}} While it is often difficult for an individual Breton to leave a lasting mark on the greater magical community—since the widespread mystical aptitude among their people makes it harder for any one mage to stand out{{Ref|name=LAHT}}—Bretons have nonetheless produced mages considered among the greatest in Tamriel,{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLI|[https://ftw.usatoday.com/2022/01/elder-scrolls-online-lore-interview The Elder Scrolls Online lore interview – we won't "step anywhere near" the Dwemer]}} with notable figures such as [[Lore:Voernet|Voernet]], [[Lore:Kasorayn|Druid King Kasorayn]], and [[Lore:Gyron Vardengroet|Gyron Vardengroet]].{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} 

Bretons have also retained a connection to their human [[Lore:Druid|druidic]] forebears, and are believed to be the reason for their "shrewd resourcefulness".{{ref|name=BOTSEAB|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Limited Edition Art and Lore Book''}}{{rp|28}} Druidic spellcraft (which differs from the arcane) is said to be the first truly Breton creation.{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} {{Hover|Druids use the term Manmer for modern Bretons. To avoid confusion, do not change it to 'Manmer'.|Bretons}} have a natural affinity for environmental manipulation,{{ref|name=ONILES|{{Cite Book|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|ns_base=Online}}}} which druids believe should be utilized to enhance or perfect nature, reshaping it just as their god [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]] once did.{{ref|name=ONHIDS|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Draoife Storystone|Draoife Storystone]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} What it means to be a Breton is considered an age-old quandary by their people.{{Ref|name=MTCSSM|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sir Stefan Mornard}}}} To make sense of it all, some may focus on a specific aspect of their culture, or seek to blend it all together.{{ref|name=ONLOTB}}{{ref|name=ESOSSM}}{{ref|name=ONCDD|[[ON:Count Damard Dufort|Count Damard Dufort]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} Some Bretons hold their elven heritage in high esteem, which may influence them to worship [[Lore:Phynaster|Phynaster]], the patron deity and "teacher" of the Direnni.{{Ref|name=VOFB|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Bretons}}}} Others attempt to seek out the druidic roots of their Nedic ancestors and reconnect with nature.{{Ref|name=MTCSSM}}{{ref|name=ONLOTB|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Bretons}}}}  

High Rock is fractious and divided politically,{{ref|name=PGE1High|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} which is seemingly encouraged by the layout of the land itself.{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}} Warfare between [[Lore:Kingdoms of High Rock|kingdoms]] accounts for much of Breton history.{{ref|name=TCCAG|[[General:Daggerfall_Covenant#Breton|Daggerfall Covenant game guide]] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} Despite this, Bretons across the province share many facets of culture, including [[Lore:Bretic|language]], bardic traditions, and heroic tales.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=POT}} Bretons make up the peasantry, soldiery, magical elite, and ruling families of the feudal kingdoms competing for power within High Rock,{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}{{ref|name=POT}} but they are found across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], often as merchants or mercenaries.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]] [[SkyrimTAG:Races|Race]] descriptions}} [[Lore:Knights|Knighthood]] is pivotal to the Breton identity; its earliest practitioners served under Clan Direnni,{{ref|name=ONARCP|[[Online:Alana Relin|Alana Relin]] and [[Online:Commander Parmion|Commander Parmion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Step Back in Time|A Step Back in Time]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTNS|[[Online:The Nameless Soldier (person)|Knight of the Direnni]] passive during [[Online:A Step Back in Time|A Step Back in Time]] and [[Online:The Nameless Soldier (quest)|The Nameless Soldier]] quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but knights are credited with kickstarting High Rock's modern cultural history through tales of liberating the race from their elven oppressors.{{ref|name=ONTKOHR|{{Cite Book|The Knightly Orders of High Rock}}}} These knights of old set the example for those that followed by going on grand quests for adventure and glory.{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL}} In their free time, Breton youths of all walks of life imitate knights through chivalric pursuits in their thoughts or by their actions.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} Indeed, the profession encapsulates the traditional chivalric principles of Breton society,{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}}{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}}{{ref|name=ONDHQ|{{Cite Book|Dame Helenie's Quest}}}} and the "quest-obsession" that has become a national identity for High Rock.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}

==Appearance==
[[File:ON-misc-Breton Pointed Ear.png|thumb|left|A male Breton displaying a remnant of elven blood: pointed ears (ESO)]]
Whether Bretons are humans or elves is a topic of debate,{{Ref|name=MDBMM}} with most people believing that they are the former.{{ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}} The earliest Bretons were referred to as '''Manmeri''',{{ref|name=PGE1High}} '''Half-Elves''',{{ref|name=TBMOP}} and possibly '''[[Lore:Chimera|Chimera]]''' (from the Altmeri word for "change"),{{Ref|name=TBOG|{{Cite Book|The Beast of Galen}}}} and were more elven in appearance.{{ref|name=TBMOP}} These elf-like physical features included pointed ears, angled faces, slender bodies, and distinctive eyes.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}} Although the terms are archaic, some modern Bretons such as druids embrace this heritage and still refer to themselves as Manmer or Half-Elves.{{ref|name=ONWD|{{Cite Book|Wyrd and Druid}}}}{{ref|name=ONHIDS}}{{ref|name=ESOSSM|[[ON:Sir Stefan Mornard|Sir Stefan Mornard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} Due to their mixed heritage, Bretons have been described disparagingly as "degraded hybrids" or a "mongrel race".{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{ref|name=CotDC}}

When the Manmer fully transitioned to modern Bretons is unknown, though by the fourth century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the ethnicity of the Druids of Galen was already "Breton-y".{{ref|name=LATD}} Modern Bretons, by and large, are pale-skinned{{ref|name=DFRB|[[Daggerfall:Breton|Breton Race Description]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and slightly built, less muscular than [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] and [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]].{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Compared to the other human races, Bretons have more rounded and softer features.{{ref|[[Books:The Art of Skyrim|The Art of Skyrim]]}}{{rp|126}} Bretons also typically have straight or wavy hair,{{ref|name=DFRB}} and possess more defined features such as high and delicate cheekbones, sensuous lips,{{ref|name=tIC173|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}{{rp|173}} and have been depicted with pronounced brow ridges.{{Ref|name=PGDrawing|[[:File:LO-race-Breton.png|Illustration of a typical Breton male]]}} Some Bretons even display pointed ears much like elves.{{Ref|[[:File:ON-misc-Breton Pointed Ear.png|Pointed Ears]] for [[Online:Breton|Bretons]] during [[Online:Character Creation|Character Creation]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BOTSEAB}}{{rp|28}} The Bretons' elvish physical features have waned and become more human over time, but have not vanished.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}

==History==
===Origin===
[[File:LO-map-High Rock (Anthology).jpg|left|thumb|[[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], home of the Bretons]]
The province of High Rock is the ancestral home to the modern day Breton people, and by tradition the name High Rock originates from humanity.{{ref|name=TVS|{{Cite Book|The Viridian Sentinel}}}}{{ref|name=ONSHV4|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 4}}}} Before them, there's evidence that a [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] population were High Rock's original inhabitants, though they did not build permanent communities, and what interactions they had with men or mer are unknown.{{ref|name=PGE3High|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}} Considered a cursed and hostile race in the modern day, the [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh]] are thought to have once had a great civilization that flourished around the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] before the First Era.{{ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}} Legends abound of the hill giants, a race of [[Lore:Giant|giants]] from [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] that migrated to the present day region of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]], bringing along their massive mammoths. Though they are no longer around, giant remains found within a cavern system in the region gives credence to this theory.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Barrowton's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=ONBF|[[ESO:Breagha-Fin|Breagha-Fin]]'s loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{intnote|Hill Giants|[nb 3]}}

Bretons themselves originated from Nedes and Aldmer interbreeding{{ref|name=PGE3A}} for centuries as far back as the Merethic Era.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=LATD|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}} Who first arrived in High Rock is debated in scholarly circles: some believe that Nedic civilization and their [[Lore:Druid King|Druid King]] were already established in the province when the Aldmer arrived,{{Ref|name=MDBMM}} while others claim that the Nedes arrived in High Rock after, encountering the elves gradually with a variety of reactions and outcomes.{{ref|name=FC}}{{ref|name=PGE3High}}{{Ref|name=LSP:OAC|group=UOL|{{TIL|Lawrence Schick and Phrastus on Altmer Culture|lawrence-schick-and-phrastus-altmer-culture-0}}}}

The Nedic ancestors of the Bretons were a people that survived in ancient High Rock by tapping into and nurturing nature.{{ref|name=LADCG}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} They followed Y'ffre's True Way (later known as Druidism),{{Ref|name=MTCSSM}} in which the eponymous god took the forefront of their faith. Under it, one is to lead a life connecting with and valuing the natural world.{{ref|name=ESODA|[[ON:Druid Audrine|Druid Audrine]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} Some reverence was also shown to the stars, especially the [[Lore:The Ritual|Ritual constellation]].{{ref|name=ONWD}} These Nedes are known by the retroactive nomenclature, the Druids of Galen,{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=LATD}}{{ref|name=RDBA}}{{ref|name=ONHIPTZ}} (or proto-Bretons){{ref|name=LTP2022|group=UOL}}{{ref|name=LADCG}} and what would become their moniker is derived from a mythical "Galen", an entity or place which druids first came about.{{ref|name=LADCG}} Regardless of the origin, it is agreed that the True Way was born in High Rock.{{Ref|name=EOTD}}

There is evidence of human settlements in High Rock dating back at least a thousand years before recorded history.{{ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}} Archaeological excavations have dated the ruins of Nedic habitation to as early as {{Year|ME 1000}}, predating [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]] by centuries.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}} The Nedes of [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]] had strained relations with the neighboring [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] clans, which regularly clashed with other Orc clans and their Nedic neighbors.{{ref|[[Online:Stormhaven Antiquities#Nedic Hex Totem|Nedic Hex Totem]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

[[File:BT-concept-Breton Female 2.png|thumb|right|Female Breton with Elvish ears]]
The Aldmer that settled High Rock did so sparsely and in very specific locations,{{Ref|name=LSP:OAC|group=UOL}} and it is speculated that there was internal strife between the various Aldmer clans of High Rock.{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/ted-peterson-high-rock-and-summerset-legendary-edition Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Legendary Edition — The Imperial Library]}}{{ref|name=ESODruidKing|[[ON:Druid King Kasorayn|Druid King Kasorayn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} The most successful of these settlers were from Clan Direnni,{{ref|name=PGE3High}} who came from the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isles]] in the Middle Merethic Era and captured the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]].{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} Some believe the Direnni were following their destiny to rule from [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]], while others believed that they were exiled.{{ref|name=ESOBE|[[ON:Bailiff Erator|Bailiff Erator]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The Direnni didn't enslave the Nedes as the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] did; instead, they ruled them as a noble caste. The elves established a system of feudal vassalage over their new subjects and took Nedic concubines.{{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=Once}} They inevitably interbred with their human subjects, producing Half-Elven children (also known as Manmer).{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{ref|name=PGE1High}}

====The Direnni, Druids, and the Wyrd====
[[File:ON-misc-Druid King Wyrd and Frii.png|thumb|left|Symbols for the Druid King (top), the Beldama Wyrd (left), the Glenmoril Wyrd (right), and later Frii (center)]]
The Druids of Galen led the new race when it first emerged,{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL|[[General:Firesong DLC & Update 36 Preview|Firesong DLC & Update 36 Preview]]}} as children begat from Elf-Nede relations were considered sub-mer and not accepted by their Direnni parents. Instead, they were given privileged positions among the Nedes, which eventually led to a recognizable mixed-blood human caste dubbed "Bretons", from the Ehlnofex "beratu", meaning "half".{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{ref|name=TBMOP}} These Bretons were only allowed to marry humans, and over time, the Bretons' human features became more prominent than their elven traits.{{ref|name=TBMOP}} Shortly after the Breton race emerged, Druidic spellcraft became the first truly Breton creation, and "druid" as a distinct identity was fully realized.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} Though the term Manmer is archaic in the modern day, Breton druids embrace this heritage by still referring to themselves as such,{{ref|name=ONWD|{{Cite Book|Wyrd and Druid}}}}{{ref|name=ONHIDS}} and believe in Breton's ability to enhance or perfect nature.{{ref|name=ONHIDS}} Indeed, the modern Breton has a natural affinity for environmental manipulation.{{ref|name=ONILES}}

Eventually, the witches of [[Lore:The Wyrd|the Wyrd]] diverged from the True Way in a religious schism, ultimately driven away by what they called the "excesses of the Elves".{{ref|name=ONWD}} In druidic belief, mortals{{ref|name=ONWD}} were not meant to separate themselves from nature, as one can rejuvenate and enjoy Y'ffre's bounty through it.{{ref|[[ON:Druid Farel|Druid Farel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|name=ESODR}} While the druids revere nature, they were not keen to isolate themselves and worship the untamed wilds.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}} Therein lies the primary difference in culture between druids and the Wyrd: the Wyrd abandoned civilization in favor of living in the wilds.{{ref|name=ONWD}} They believe that they are nature itself: the forest, the plants and beasts within it, even the weather.{{ref|[[Online:Wyress Demara|Wyress Demara]] and [[Online:Wyress Linnae|Wyress Linnae]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONWD}} In contrast, druids accepted what they interpreted as their "proper place" as people, and embraced the idea that civilization can coexist alongside nature.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=ESOWM|[[ON:Wyress Matilde|Wyress Matilde]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Sojourn of the Druid King|Sojourn of the Druid King]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} The Druids of Galen and the Wyrd disapproved of each other's lifestyles: the druids found the ways of the Wyrd unsavory, and the Wyrd thought the druids were no better than city-dwellers.{{ref|name=ESOWM}} Druids would much later practice self-isolation to varying degrees{{ref|name=ESOSSM}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=DP|[[ON:Druid Peeska|Druid Peeska]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} as a response to historical encroachments and deprivations.{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=LABHI}}

While the Aldmer maintained control of Tamriel, Clan Direnni coexisted peacefully alongside their Nedic and Half-Elven subjects.{{ref|name=FC}} The Direnni ruled largely through their Nedic clients,{{Ref|name=LSP:OAC|group=UOL}} while the "Meriphilic"{{ref|name=ONSHV1|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 1}}}} Druids of Galen used their fearsome magic to enforce their influence over early Bretons, and the Wyrd avoided politics altogether.{{ref|name=ESOEFS|[[ON:High King Emeric|High King Emeric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} Some Manmeri devotional idols from this period depict [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] as an elven woman in a lover's embrace with a man, suggesting Aldmeri culture may have been welcomed with relatively open arms.{{ref|name=Manmer|[[Online:Glenumbra Antiquities#Manmer Coupled Idol|Manmer Coupled Idol]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} However, their comity would not last: localized groups of Bretons displeased with mer butted heads with their elven overlords long before they officially came to blows.{{ref|name=LATD}}

===First Era===
[[File:ON-crown store-Chrysamere.jpg|left|thumb|Chrysamere (ESO)]]
The earliest written account of the existence of a Breton is from the late Merethic Era, which is the blacksmith [[Lore:Asterie Bedel|Asterie Bedel]], who is credited with the creation of the legendary artifact [[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]].{{ref|name=LAIA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}} The history that followed would see the Breton peoples venturing out of High Rock and into other provinces. In {{Year|1E 20}}, the sage and author Voernet traveled to [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] to meet with [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]], the Ritemaster of the [[Lore: Psijic Order|Psijic Order]].{{ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} 

Written in {{Year|1E 200}}, the [[Lore:Tamrilean Tractates|Tamrilean Tractates]] speak of an early encounter between a Nord hunting party and the Bretons. The Bretons were scarcely recognizable as men, and the Nords attacked them thinking they were Aldmeri. The Nords ceased their assault when one of the Bretons spoke in broken Nordic, and the hunting party brought news of this race back to [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]]. The Nords believed the Manmeri from beyond [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] were descended from human slaves taken during the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]].{{ref|name=PGE1High}} The Breton [[Lore:Gauldur|Archmage Gauldur]], who was contemporary with [[Lore:Harald|High King Harald]], became cemented in Nordic oral traditions despite an age-long campaign to erase his legacy.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Lost Legends}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Daynas Valen's Notes}}}} Upon forming the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire of the Nords]], [[Lore:Vrage|High King Vrage]] made the liberation the Bretons from their elven masters his first priority.{{ref|name=PGE1High|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} Vrage's initial campaign brought him as far as the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae]], but progress was impeded by elven magic, and the elves were also aided in battle by many of their Breton vassals.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} Though the Aldmer kept hold of their lands for most of the era,{{ref|name=PGE3High}} some sources claim that all of High Rock was conquered by {{Year|1E 240}}.{{ref|name=TDCP11|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}} To support their pillaging parties, the Nords founded many fortified towns along the coasts around this time, such as [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], and [[Lore:Reich Gradkeep|Reich Gradkeep]].{{ref|name=PGE3High}}

During the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]], [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] hired many Breton and Nordic warbands as mercenaries with promises of land and trade concessions. After the Alessian Cyrodiils achieved victory, the mercenaries that participated were assimilated into the Nibenese culture.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Legendary Sancre Tor}}}}

Tired of outside influence, the Druids of Galen advocated for druidic rule of High Rock, making them a target of virtually every faction vying for control.{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} The rising [[Lore:Direnni Hegemony|Direnni Hegemony]] saw them as a threat, and the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] was intolerant of their religion.{{ref|name=ONWD}} Ultimately, the druids split from Bretondom around {{Year|1E 330}}. Following the guidance of Y'ffre's visions,{{Ref|name=EOTD}} a song, or an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]],{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} Druid King Kasorayn led somewhere between a few thousand to a million druids on an exodus to the Systres.{{Ref|name=EOTD}}{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} A few remained on the mainland.{{ref|name=ESODR|[[ON:Druid Ryvana|Druid Ryvana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

[[File:ON-misc-Druidic Knotwork Face Tattoos.png|thumb|right|Triquetra Tattoo (around forehead)|class=backdrop]]
When the Druids of Galen arrived in the Systres, the Draoife terraformed a barren volcanic island into a lush and verdant volcanic paradise and named it [[Lore:Y'ffelon|Y'ffelon]]. The druids then swiftly expanded across the archipelago into the three other islands,{{Ref|name=SoA|{{Cite Book|Secrets of Amenos}}}} sprouting life where it was once barren.{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} The dissolution of the Druids of Galen was brought about with the event known as the "three mornings of sail", where priest-navigators set out to sea on enchanted vessels outside the archipelago. Their outside experiences and their interactions with different races resulted in three sects developing, each with their own interpretation of Y'ffre's will; the Stonelore, Eldertide, and Firesong Circles.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{ref|name=ONSHV1}} Despite their separation, to symbolize their unity matters more and that they share the same goals, the druidic circles came to shaping knot-work into a [[Lore:Triquetra|triquetra]],{{ref|[[Online:Druidic Knotwork Body Tattoos|Druidic Knotwork Body Tattoos]] body markings description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} a symbol that would become adopted by civilized Bretons.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 93: Ancestral Breton Style}}}} Kasorayn himself took on the role of an observer after he helped establish the druids on the isle, and only intervened when necessary. The druidic monarchy ended after heretics from the Allwither Order murdered Kasarayn. The heretics are speculated as wanting to seize the Ivy Throne{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}} to enact revenge on their former elven oppressors back in High Rock.{{ref|name=ONSHADG|{{Cite Book|Systres History Addendum: The Druids of Galen}}}} Though the Allwither heretics were defeated,{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}} prophecy would deem that the title of Druid King remain vacant for thousands of years.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Dream of Kasorayn}}}} The Druid King's symbology would disappear almost overnight.{{ref|name=LABHI}}{{ref|[[Online:High Isle Antiquities#Stonelore Circle Effigy|Stonelore Circle Effigy]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====The Rise and Fall of the Direnni Hegemony====
[[File:ON-misc-Direnni.png|thumb|left|Symbol of Clan Direnni (ESO)|class=backdrop]]
After much infighting between the mer of High Rock over who would rule the province,{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}} circa {{Year|1E 355}}, Clan Direnni established their Hegemony after they emerged victorious through political maneuvering and devious machinations.{{ref|name=TDCP11}} However, some consider the Direnni's rule in the years leading up to its establishment as part of the Direnni Hegemony as well.{{ref|name=ONSHV1}}{{Ref|name=EOTD|{{Cite Book|Exodus of the Druids}}}}{{ref|name=ESODR}} The Nord's holdings in High Rock were threatened and ultimately lost as a result of the [[Lore:War of Succession|War of Succession]] erupting in {{Year|1E 369}},{{ref|name=AHOD}} which ended in {{Year|1E 420}}.{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} 

After gaining influence and purchasing the Isle of Balfiera in {{Year|1E 461}},{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}}{{ref|name=ALOSAS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Tower of Adamant}}}} [[Lore:Ryain Direnni|Ryain Direnni]] led Clan Direnni to conquer all of High Rock by {{Year|1E 477}}.{{ref|name=TDCP11}}{{ref|name=ALOSAS}}{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}} His Breton legions were supposedly the first to sack the Orc hold of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]],{{ref|name=ALOSAS}} though what's known as the "First Orsinium" is usually dated much later to the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}{{ref|name=BSOHR}}{{ref|name=WJOTB}} As the Direnni Hegemony expanded geographically, the elves, who were few in number, passed increasing powers of administration to the Breton caste.{{ref|name=TBMOP}} Clan Direnni was inspired to outlaw the religion of the Alessian Reform by [[Lore:Rislav Larich|Rislav Larich]]'s victory against Emperor [[Lore:Gorieus| Gorieus]], and began pushing into Imperial territory.{{ref|name=RTR}} The timeframe between {{Year|1E 480}} and {{Year|1E 482}} would see a coming conflict with the Alessian Order. Seeing the threat the Order posed, [[Lore:Aiden Direnni|Aiden Direnni]] signed the [[Lore:Rights Charter|Rights Charter]] in an effort to muster much-needed troops for the coming conflict.{{ref|name=ALOSAS}} With the aid of Breton knights, Aiden's army defeated the Alessians at the [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbria Moors|Battle of Glenumbria Moors]] on the 8th of Sun's Dawn in {{Year|1E 482}}. {{ref|name=ONARCP}}{{ref|name=TLKOTA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} The Bretons of the Glenumbra Moors celebrate the [[Lore:Day of Release|Day of Release]] to commemorate this victory.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Release|Day of Release]] information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} A popular [[Lore:The Battle of Glenumbria Moors|song]] from the Second Era memorializes the Bretons' role in turning the tide against the Alessian generals, and commemorated the Direnni clansmen who were instrumental in defending High Rock.{{ref|name=TBoGM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Glenumbria Moors}}}}

Thoroughly spent, the Direnni withdrew to Balfiera, and their hegemony fractured circa {{Year|1E 498}}.{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{ref|name=TDCP11}}{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}} With most of High Rock free from elven control by {{Year|1E 500}},{{ref|name=PGE1High}} the Breton kingdoms began establishing themselves around this time.{{ref|name=HorseFolk|{{Cite Book|The Horse-Folk of Silverhoof}}}} The Bretons' rise to prominence in High Rock initially started through villages being founded in tactical locations, such as [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], which started as a fishing hamlet possibly as early as {{Year|1E 800}}.{{ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}} As the emerging Bretons absorbed the remaining Nordic population, Nordic cities such as Camlorn were assimilated rather than conquered, and High Rock would be definitively Breton by the end of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3High}}

[[File:ON-place-Isle of Balfiera.jpg|thumb|right|Balfiera, home of Clan Direnni]]
Though the province was in Breton hands, it was by no means a cohesive nation. Clan Direnni's retreat to Balfiera left no united government, and High Rock fractured into a hundred squabbling fiefdoms.{{ref|name=PGE3High}} The elf-blooded nobility of [[Lore:Greater Bretony|Greater Bretony]] took up the mantle Clan Direnni left behind, maintaining the feudal hierarchy their elven predecessors forged and placing their noble families, the oldest of whom the elven blood flowed strongest within, in the position of aristocracy. The new noble class, consisting of highborn Bretons, justified their ascension to aristocracy and the preservation of the peasant class by disassociating themselves from their elven heritage. The Bretons vilified the Direnni, and redefined themselves around the notion that they were not elves. The Breton nobility fabricated a history of noble resistance to Direnni rule to suit the narrative that Breton cultural identity had no elven roots. In time, a merchant class sprouted and thrived, trading around Tamriel's coasts.{{ref|name=TBMOP}} In addition to the conflicts over borders and crowns, the Bretons were left to deal with the encroachment of the wilds upon their settlements: the Direnni renounced their covenant with the Earthbones after their retreat, which legends claim they did out of spite. This pact protected Direnni roads and townships from the forest and the beasts that inhabited it.{{ref|name=TVS}}

According to one source, the Bretons permitted the Direnni to retreat to Balfiera without resistance.{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}} Another account claims that the Direnni had already completed their withdrawal to Balfiera by the time the Redguards arrived in Tamriel.{{Ref|name=WebsiteLore|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/elder-scrolls-online-lore-answers Elder Scrolls Online website - Lore Answers]}} A third, contradictory source places the final stages of the Direnni’s flight from High Rock sometime around or after the ninth century of the First Era.{{intnote|Pale|[nb 7]}} During this later period, violence against the Direnni escalated under the reign of King [[Lore:Festrien Spenard I|Festrien Spenard I]]. Backed by vassals from [[Lore:House Aurmine|House Aurmine]], the despot king’s [[Lore:Knights of the Pale Order|Knights of the Pale Order]] slaughtered the Direnni wholesale during the latter years of their flight from High Rock, reportedly maintaining lists of Direnni warriors and magisters they killed.{{ref|name=ROTPO|[[Online:Ring of the Pale Order|Ring of the Pale Order]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} Yet another source claims that the Direnni remained dominant in High Rock until the middle of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3High}} In spite of the vilification of the Direnni and disassociation from their elven heritage, at least some Bretons would continue to emphasize their elven blood.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}

The earliest known [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudans]] encountered by Bretons were the [[Lore:Silverhoof Horsemen|Silverhoof Horsemen]], who came to Tamriel centuries before the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]], while the Direnni Hegemony was dissolving and the Breton kingdoms were forming. This opportune moment in history allowed the Horsemen to firmly establish themselves in Rivenspire without significant organized resistance from the locals. They would become "Bretonized" by their neighbors over the centuries, but retained significant elements of their original culture.{{ref|name=HorseFolk}}

====Bretons on the World Stage====
By the time the Druids of Galen fled the mainland circa 1E 330, the Breton peoples had already encountered the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], though such occurrences with them were minimal.{{Ref|name=LDA|{{Cite Book|Logbook of Druid Anwas}}}} Circa {{Year|1E 420}}, when the self-exiled [[Lore:Clan Rourken|Clan Rourken]] relocated to [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]] in Hammerfell, south of High Rock, it opened up new trade opportunities for the Bretons.{{ref|name=PGE3Hammerfell|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}}

[[File:ON-map-Systres Archipelago.jpg|left|thumb|The Systres Archipelago]]
Eventually, the differences in the beliefs of the three major Systrean drudic circles nearly led to conflict. This was avoided thanks to the appearance of a common enemy, the [[Lore:Sinistral Mer|Sinistral Elves]], who fled to the Systres circa {{Year|1E 660}} to escape the Yokudans. Their invasion was temporarily halted when Y'ffelon's volcano of [[Lore:Mount Firesong|Mount Firesong]] erupted sometime around {{Year|1E 668}}. Regardless, the Sinistral Elves took over the archipelago, and the druidic circles were forced into hiding. With the coming of the Ra Gada in {{Year|1E 785}}, what remained of the elves were purged, and the Yokudans quickly left to sail eastward. In the end, only the druids remained.{{ref|name=ONSHV2|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 2}}}}

This was only the first Yokudan "warrior wave".{{ref|name=RTHATH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}}{{ref|name=ONSHV2}} The next wave, known as the Tavan wave,{{ref|name=CM28|{{Cite book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} came after the time of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]'s sinking in {{Year|1E 792}}, making landfall in {{Year|1E 808}}.{{ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} The Tavans captured a harborage from the beastfolk, which they named [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] (the Aldmeri name for Hammerfell), and used it as a staging ground for conquest.{{ref|name=PGE3Hammerfell}}{{Ref|name=STJOA|{{Cite Book|Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r}}}} Long before the arrival of the Ra Gada, the elves and later Bretons established an outpost at what is now [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]] in order to protect fisherfolk and seafaring merchants from inland [[Lore:Desert Orcs|desert Orc]] marauders, [[Lore:Giant Goblin|giant Goblin]] raiders, and other dangers of the desert.{{ref|name=PGE3Hammerfell}}{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell}} The next warrior wave, led by [[Lore:Grandee Yaghoub|Grandee Yaghoub]], quickly discovered and established control over this port with little resistance. Yaghoub conscripted the locals to serve as masons and servants in the building of his new palace, [[Lore:Samaruik|Samaruik]], and the rest of the city.{{ref|name=STJOA}} The kings of the Ra Gada continued to raid Breton coastal cities and trading vessels,{{ref|name=ONYokudan|[[Online:Yokudan King|Yokudan King]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the Cyro-Nords from Colovia who plundered the Iliac Bay annually were pushed out by them.{{Ref|name=PGE1C}} The descendants of the Yokudans, the Redguards, would later become established as historical enemies to the Bretons.{{ref|name=ONILES}}

[[File:ON-interior-Honor's Rest 03.jpg|thumb|right|Baloth Bloodtusk and Gaiden Shinji, assassinated by King Joile's general during the Siege of Orsinium]]
Orsinium was built on the boundary between western [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]] and Rivenspire in the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3O}}{{ref|name=BSOHR}}{{ref|name=WJOTB}} King [[Lore:Golkarr|Golkarr]] of the Orcs amassed an army and was determined to seize control of the Bjoulsae River,{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Great Siege of Orsinium}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1WR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}} upon which the nomadic Breton [[Lore:Bjoulsae River Tribes|Bjoulsae River Tribes]] lived.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Mirror}}}} They intended to force Wayrest to pay to make use of the river.{{ref|name=PGE1WR}} In {{Year|1E 874}}, Warlord [[Lore:Thulgeg|Thulgeg]]'s army of Orcs and [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]] attempted to flee Hammerfell for Orsinium in High Rock, but were blocked at [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]] by the [[Lore:Bangkorai Garrison|Bangkorai Garrison]].{{ref|name=BSOHR}}{{Ref|name=SC|[[ON:Scholar Cogline|Scholar Cogline]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|1E 948}}, [[Lore:Joile|King Joile]] of Daggerfall sent a letter to [[Lore:Gaiden Shinji|Gaiden Shinji]] of the [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]] proposing a joint attack on Orsinium. Joile cited that the Orcs were raiding along the Bjoulsae and impeding traffic to the heart of the land as reason enough to make war against the Orcs. Agreeing to provide aid, an alliance was formed between the Order of Diagna, [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], and Sentinel. The armies amassed and began a 30-year long campaign known as the [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]].{{ref|name=WJOTB}}{{ref|name=PGE3O}} In that time, King Joile plotted to betray the Redguards and invade Hammerfell. Joile and his Reachman general [[Lore:Mercedene|Mercedene]] assassinated Gaiden Shinji and Orcish hero [[Lore:Baloth Bloodtusk|Baloth Bloodtusk]]. Daggerfall's own [[Lore:Knights of the Dragon|Knights of the Dragon]], though they failed to stop the assassination, defeated General Mercedene.{{ref|[[Online:The Ashes of Our Fathers|The Ashes of Our Fathers]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} In {{Year|1E 973}}, Joile's invasion of Hammerfell proceeded, but he was stopped by the [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] warrior [[Lore:Makela Leki|Makela Leki]] at Bangkorai Pass.{{ref|{{Cite Book|From The Memory Stone of Makela Leki}}}} The Siege of Orsinium finally ended in {{Year|1E 980}} when the allied forces breached the city's defenses, overran it, and razed it to the ground.{{ref|name=PGE3O}}

With the Bjoulsae River region opened up for trade after the siege, the Bretons experienced an economic boom. The kingdom of [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] was founded by the Bjoulsae River Tribes,{{ref|name=TRHOKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}} and Wayrest also established a presence on both banks of the river. The [[Lore:Masconian Trade Way|Masconian Trade Way]] was formed by the hard-working traders and merchants of the Bjoulsae, greatly expanding commerce and marginalizing [[Lore:Piracy|piracy]].{{ref|name=WJOTB}} Daggerfall developed a burgeoning [[Lore:Ivory|ivory]] trade, which originated with ivory plundered from Orsinium following its sacking.{{ref|name=ESOIvory|[[Online:Wrothgar Antiquities#Tusks of the Orc-Father|Tusks of the Orc-Father]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The uneasy truce between the Bretons and Redguards over Orsinium transitioned into economic relations between the two races.{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}}

====Relations with Morrowind====
[[File:ON-place-Ebonheart.jpg|thumb|right|Ebonheart in Morrowind]]
Historically, the Bretons maintained ties with the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] (and possibly the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]){{intnote|Chimer|[nb 4]}} through a trade agreement between [[Lore:Ra'athim|Clan R'aathim]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] and the Kings of High Rock. Under this pact, [[Lore:Moraelyn|King Moraelyn]] of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] 's retainers were granted rights to mine ebony in the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] and establish a colony in High Rock. In return, they supplied the [[Lore:Corcyr|Breton lords]] with ebony weapons and taught them how to smith ebony themselves.{{ref|name=KEII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part 2}}}} [[Lore:House Mora|House Mora]] of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] and its Clan R'aathim notably had human blood in its family tree, before that house fell and was adopted into the [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]].{{Ref|name=TRB5|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 5}}}} There are some sources that suggest [[Lore:Edward|King Edward]] of Daggerfall was the son of the Breton [[Lore:Aliera|Queen Aliera]] of Wayrest and [[Lore:Moraelyn|King Moraelyn]] of Ebonheart.{{ref|name=Jindra|group=UOL|[[General:Anne Jindra's Posts|''Anne Jindra's Posts'']]}} Stories tell of King Edward and King Moraelyn wielding the [[Lore:Horn of Summoning|Horn of Summoning]] to banish the Nords from High Rock.{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} But in the historical fiction ''[[Lore:King Edward|King Edward]]'', Edward was born after Wayrest was founded, which is after the Nords were expelled from High Rock.{{ref|name=KE10|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part X}}}} 

Some of the people of High Rock held negative attitudes the elves of Morrowind before the time of King Edward, many who believed them to be thieves and kidnappers, with myths claiming that they sprouted from the ground.{{ref|name=KE10}} This led to them being banned from Daggerfall for a time.{{Ref|name=KEIV|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part IV}}}} Around the second millennium (circa {{Year|1E 1000}}), a noble family of Bretons called the [[Lore:Imbel|Imbels]] inhabited the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], living near the village of [[Lore:Suran|Suran]].{{ref|name=IG|{{Cite Book|Imbel Genealogy}}}} [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] in Morrowind often prefers to hire [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] and Bretons as mercenaries to ensure they are left in peace.{{Ref|name=DialogueRaces|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue R|Races dialogue topic]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

====Alessian Empire and the Camorian Empire====
In {{Year|1E 1029}}, Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] pursued the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]] from [[Lore:Verkarth (Hammerfell)|Verkarth]] to Bangkorai Pass, where the Bretons held the line and fractured the undead forces, easing the Empire's hunt. Hestra was impressed, and offered High Rock admission into the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]], which they accepted.{{ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} Thus, the Bretons joined the Empire of Men and accepted Alessia's [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]].{{ref|name=TBMOP}} In {{Year|1E 1100}}, ambassadors of the [[Lore:Camorian Empire|Camorian Empire]] were accepted into [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] and granted them the right to call themselves a kingdom under the newly crowned [[Lore:Farangel|King Farangel Gardner]].{{Ref|name=WJotB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}

At an unknown time period during the First Era, an alliance known as the [[Lore:League of Unrest|League of Unrest]] was created between Daggerfall, Wayrest, and Balfiera against the [[Lore:Imperial|Cyrodiils]].{{ref|name=RGMaiko|[[Redguard:Maiko K'Elmar|Maiko K'Elmar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

[[File:ON-trailer-AllFlagsNavy.jpg|thumb|left|The All Flags Navy]]

The [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] circa {{Year|2200}}, which spread outward from cities like Daggerfall and Hegathe, cut the population of the Iliac Bay, and Tamriel as a whole, in half.{{ref|name=AHOD}}{{ref|name=TTP|{{Cite book|1. The Thrassian Plague}}}} After several years, widespread belief emerged that the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] were responsible for maliciously inflicting the disease on Tamriel.{{ref|name=TTP}} To hold them accountable, the Alessian Emperor granted command of an Imperial fleet to the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian]] King of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]], [[Lore:Bendu Olo|Bendu Olo]].{{ref|name=ONSHV3|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 3}}}} Bendu sought out allies to bolster his forces, and many Breton sailors and warships helped form what would become known as the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]].{{ref|name=JOTE|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}} Some Bretons among the initial fourteen ships which formed the basis of the All Flags Navy were Captain Isara Davaux of the King's Jest, Renwic Moorcroft of the Pristine Halberd, and [[Lore:Coin-barons|Coin-Baron]] Nemic Santerre of the Silver Lantern.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The All Flags Navy Started Here}}}} Bendo's call also attracted knightly figures such as [[Lore:Joslin Garick|Lady Joslin Garick]] and Baroness [[Lore:Belene Chatillon|Belene Chatillon]].{{ref|name=ONCG|[[ON:Cynric Ginise|Cynric Ginise]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|name=ONSKO|{{Cite Book|Systres Knightly Orders}}}} The local Stonelore Circle also lent a ship and druids for the coming conflict.{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sea Stone}}}} Sometime around {{Year|1E 2243}}, the Navy arrived in the Systres Archipelago, where they established the Navy's largest base and shipyard within High Isle's Gonfalon Bay.{{ref|name=ONSHV3}} After various skirmishes, the Navy launched a final assault on [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] in {{Year|1E 2260}}. The entire landmass was sunk into the ocean, catching half the fleet in a giant whirlpool. The remaining influential figures returned to the Systres, where these representatives of each race signed the Concordat of Fraternity on All Flags Islet, in hopes to bring about peace and cooperation.{{ref|name=ONSHV3}}

[[File:ON-trailer-Skingrad.png|thumb|left|Skingrad in Cyrodiil]]
Angry merchants from Wayrest called their fellow Bretons to secede from the Alessian Empire circa {{Year|1E 2300}}, citing prejudice against elven heritage and increasing Imperial tithes to support their position.{{ref|[[Online:Stormhaven Antiquities#Secession Stamp Block|Secession Stamp Block]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} High Rock formally reasserted its independence in {{Year|1E 2305}}.{{ref|name=BSOHR}} Despite resenting this secession, the Alessian Order did not intervene until King Desynan of [[Lore:Skingrad|Skingrad]] and Queen Margert Gardner of Wayrest arranged a marriage between their children. Fearing a dynastic alliance between High Rock and Colovia, Arch-Prelate Zirnius forbid the union, and when Desynan appointed an official to approve the marriage anyway, Zirnius dispatched his legions to punish the Colovians. This conflict was initially "anarchy, local uprisings, and shifting alliances" that were contained within the Colovian Estates, but later shifted to Breton lands within the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=ONOWR|{{Cite Book|On the War of Righteousness}}}} The Legions of Piety and Grace were sent under Abbot-General [[Lore:Priscus Mactator|Priscus Mactator]] to reconquer the Bretons, but could not break through the Bangkorai Garrison.{{ref|name=BSOHR}} This loss would embolden the rebelling Colovians, officially kickstarting the [[Lore:War of Righteousness|War of Righteousness]] in {{Year|1E 2321}}.{{ref|name=ONSHV4}}{{intnote|Desynan|[nb 5]}} The Alessian Order's heavy losses in the west forced them to tax and conscript people from eastern Cyrodiil, causing angry Nibeneans to rebel and slay the Arch-Prelate and destroy the Order's great cathedral at [[Lore:Lake Canulus|Lake Canulus]]. With no leadership or income, the Alessians in High Rock abandoned their invasion.{{ref|name=ONOWR}}
[[File:ON-concept-Martinne Guimard.png|thumb|right|Statue of Martinne Guimard]]
Although the Systres had been claimed by Colovia after the All Flags Navy's victory over the Sload, the financial costs of the war led them to sell the archipelago to High Rock Coin-barons circa {{Year|1E 2327}}. [[Lore:Martinne Guimard|Duchess Martinne Guimard]] became the archipelago's new ruler, and in {{Year|1E 2328}}, named the largest island High Isle in honor of High Rock. To the delight of the Systreans, their archipelago was not only untouched by the War of Righteousness, but remained "an untouched cradle of Breton history". Druidism was alive and well, and Breton culture was at the forefront thanks to the Bretons who stayed behind after the assault on Thras.{{ref|name=ONSHV4}} 

High Rock was independent once more, but the secession came at great cost. The War of Righteousness halved the population of the Iliac Bay a second time.{{ref|name=AHOD}} The Alessian collapse struck the stubborn Bretons harder than the other races, as they had no unified governing structure. Warring fiefs would rage across the peninsula in the coming centuries as various rulers attempted to assume control.{{ref|[[Online:Stormhaven Antiquities#Noble Knight's Rest|Noble Knight's Rest]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The Systres remained in Breton hands, despite later transferring from [[Lore:Guimard|House Guimard]] to [[Lore:House Mantel|House Mantel]], but in {{Year|1E 2484}}, Mount Firesong erupted once more, devastating the archipelago and killing many of its inhabitants. Starvation and illnesses among the lower classes led to political unrest. The Stonelore Circle managed to pull the city folk back from the brink of death by developing sprawling pastoral and agricultural operations, leading to a renaissance of Druidism. This time period was referred to as the Green Years.{{ref|name=ONSHV5|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 5}}}}

====The Second Empire====
The first appearances of the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] in Tamriel were pirates (later assumed to have been scouts for an invasion force).{{ref|name=PGE3OL}} These raiders established themselves on the islands to the west of Wayrest around {{Year|1E 800}}, antagonizing the Bretons of the region.{{ref|name=WJOTB}} Finally, around {{Year|2700}}, Akaviri legions invaded northern Tamriel,{{Ref|name=LOTD|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Dragonguard}}}} which  prompt the people of Wayrest to move within the walled estate of the Gardners for protection in {{Year|1E 2702}}.{{ref|name=WJOTB}} With the Akaviri invasion, the population of the Iliac Bay was halved a third time. It is said that if Daggerfall had not been hardened and fortified through wars in its first thousand years of existence, the Akaviri may have dominated the Iliac Bay into modernity.{{ref|name=AHOD}}

The [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] was established in {{Year|1E 2703}} after [[Lore:Reman I|Reman Cyrodiil]] suppressed the Akaviri invasion of Tamriel at the battle of [[Lore:Pale Pass|Pale Pass]].{{Ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} With the Akaviri's numbers and tactics boosting his power, Reman went on to pacify most of Tamriel.{{Ref|name=LOTD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1C}} In an effort to make Cyrodiil more cosmopolitan, Reman I incorporated many elements of High Rock society into his own.{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} Though conquered, High Rock's virulent sectarianism would remain intact.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}

The distant shores of the Systres were also affected by Reman Cyrodiil's ambitions. In {{Year|1E 2704}}, the Reman Empire reclaimed the archipelago and reinstalled Breton Coin-barons, which marked the end of the Green Years.{{ref|name=ONSHV6|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 6}}}} The High Rock Coin-barons committed various injustices when they utilized their merchant fleet to move illicit goods. The Coin-barons were also involved in [[Lore:Slavery|slavery]], which was a part of Breton culture at that time, and established a slaver fort on Amenos.{{ref|group=UOL|name=HIDD|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1470462346 Twitch Stream of ''High Isle Dev Discussion''], time stamp 1:19:32-1:20:20}}{{ref|name=ESOEHI|[[ON:High King Emeric|High King Emeric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} Over time, the Spire of the Crimson Coin on High Isle, where many grim and debaucherous celebrations took place, became a sort of monument to the excesses of these rulers.{{ref|[[ON:Spire of the Crimson Coin|Spire of the Crimson Coin]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Squire Rayan|Squire Rayan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

As part of the Second Empire, High Rock's soldiers participated in the 80-year [[Lore:Four Score War|Four Score War]] against [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. According to the historical fiction ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]'', a Breton man named [[Lore:Cassyr Whitley|Cassyr Whitley]] of [[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]] defected from the Imperial army and spied on the Dunmer's behalf. After Whitley's mistake cost Morrowind the fortress of [[Lore:Ald Marak|Ald Marak]], he returned to High Rock, where he met the Dunmeri exile [[Lore:Turala|Turala]]. When Turala's coven and child were lost to an Orcish raid, Whitley deceived her into believing the attack was perpetrated by assassins employed by Duke [[Lore:Brindisi Dorom|Brindisi Dorom]] of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], prompting her to summon [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] for revenge.

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Mages Guild (Daggerfall).jpg|thumb|right|A Mages Guild building in High Rock]]
High Rock had been possibly the most prosperous and stable province under the Reman Dynasty,{{ref|name=TBMOP}} and continued to benefit under by the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentates]]. Concurrent with the rule of [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]], urban Bretons started systematically organizing themselves alongside the hierarchy the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] sometime after its founding in {{Year|2E 230}}. The Mages Guild became a formidable force within High Rock,{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} but it was not officially sanctioned within the Second Empire until the [[Lore:Guild Act|Guild Act]] of {{Year|2E 321}}. The Guild Act also standardized regulation of trade and transactions across the Empire, and was adopted as de facto law across Tamriel, including in High Rock.{{ref|name=HKEAYQ|{{Cite Book|High King Emeric Answers Your Questions}}}}

Circa {{Year|2E 312}}, the Bretons on [[Lore:Betony|Betony]] were attacked by War Chief [[Lore:Yzzgol|Yzzgol]] and his [[Lore:Seamount Clan|Seamount Clan]]. King [[Lore:Renwic|Renwic]] attempted to defend his island with necromancy and an ancient Ayleid relic, but failed. Yzzgol exterminated the Bretons and renamed the island Betnikh.{{ref|name=ONKhazun|[[Online:Rozag gro-Khazun|Rozag gro-Khazun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONKingRenwic|[[Online:King Renwic|King Renwic]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} King [[Lore:Casimir II|Casimir II]] of Wayrest formed an alliance with a [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] named [[Lore:Nahfahlaar|Nahfahlaar]] some time during the second century, which was ended by the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]] in {{Year|2E 369}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}} Circa {{Year|2E 428}}, High Rock was plagued by the [[Lore:Curse of the Crimson Moon|Curse of the Crimson Moon]], a [[Lore:Werewolf|werewolf]] crisis which prompted the creation of the [[Lore:Silver Dawn|Order of the Silver Dawn]] to hunt werebeasts.{{ref|name=ESOPJ|{{Cite Book|Purifier's Journal}}}}

====The Interregnum and the Three Banners War====
The Second Empire collapsed with the assassination of Akaviri Potentate [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]] in {{Year|2E 430}}, marking the start of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. Left without a [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] Emperor, the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] was passed between petty warlords for centuries. High Rock fared much better than the other provinces at this time, despite being fractious in nature.{{ref|name=PGE3High}} Another Orsinium was accepted as a territory of the Second Empire during Savirien-Chorak's reign,{{ref|name=PGE1WR}}{{ref|name=RM8TO|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 8: Orc Style}}}}{{ref|name=HKEAYQ}} and [[Lore:Frostbreak Fortress|Frostbreak Fortress]] was built to help defend it.{{ref|[[Online:Frostbreak Fortress|Frostbreak Fortress]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} With the Second Empire's fall, Orsinium lost its protection, and an assault spearheaded by the Bretons of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] destroyed it once more in {{Year|2E 431}}.{{ref|name=PGE1WR}}{{ref|name=TFoKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}} The Bretons seized the land, and the Orcs scattered across northern Tamriel.{{ref|name=GttDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=OOS|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Skyrim}}}}

[[File:ON-concept-Daggerfall Covenant-4500x2040.jpg|thumb|left|Races of the Daggerfall Covenant]]
In {{Year|2E 542}}, Reachmen under [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach the Black Drake]] invaded High Rock, sacking Evermore and Camlorn. They were defeated at the gates of Daggerfall by [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric of Cumberland]], which led to the formation of the first [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], signed by the kings of Daggerfall, Camlorn, Shornhelm, [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]] and Evermore. In {{Year|2E 563}}, Emeric became the king of Wayrest. Three years later, King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]] of Shornhelm [[Lore:Ranser's War|invaded]] Wayrest, angry at Emeric for rejecting his daughter's hand to instead wed [[Lore:Maraya|Maraya]], daughter of King [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]]. The Covenant defeated him at Markwasten Moor with the help of the Orcs, who were promised the return of Orsinium for their aid.{{ref|name=TFoKR}} Enraged by this, Ranser ordered his Spymaster to round up many Orcs in Rivenspire to be interrogated and executed. The Spymaster would end up murdering hundreds of innocent Orcs during the course of Ranser's War.{{ref|name=Marisette|[[On:Marisette|Marisette]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Gerard|[[On:Gerard Althen|Gerard Althen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-render-House Dufort Banneret Style.jpg|thumb|right|House Dufort knights in Cyrodiil during the Three Banners War]]
These events led to the formation of the Greater Daggerfall Covenant, an alliance between Emeric, the Redguard King Fahara'jad, and King [[Lore:Kurog gro-Orsinium|Kurog gro-Orsinium]] of the Orcs.{{ref|name=ToaM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} Kurog demanded restitution for the ivory Bretons looted during the Siege of Orsinium as part of his terms for joining the Covenant.{{ref|name=ESOIvory}} The Covenant became an economic powerhouse as trade flourished.{{ref|name=THOTE|{{Cite Book|True Heirs of the Empire}}}} The Covenant later fought in the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] during King Emeric's bid for the throne of Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=ToaM}} The kings of the Daggerfall Covenant looked to Reman I as a model Emperor, and saw themselves as the spiritual heirs to the Second Empire.{{ref|name=GttDC}} To promote their claim, the Covenant claimed it would restore "the glory of Reman's legacy" by promoting freedom, worship of the Divines, and trade.{{ref|name=THOTE}} Race relations between the Bretons and Orcs generally improved,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Orcs? Could Be Worse}}}} despite persistent anti-Orc sentiment.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Orcs: The Vermin Among Us}}}} The Direnni elves were also accepted within the Covenant's borders.{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Warning to the Aldmeri Dominion}}}}

====Fate of the Systres Archipelago====
[[File:ON-misc-High Isle 02.png|thumb|left|A clash with the Ascendant Order on High Isle]]
With the destruction brought by the Three Banners War, the [[Lore:Ascendant Order|Ascendant Order]] gained traction as a pan-Tamrielic movement.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOGR|[https://gamerant.com/elder-scrolls-online-high-isle-loremaster-lead-writer-interview/ Elder Scrolls Online's Loremaster, Lead Writer Talk What's To Come In 2022]}} Many common folk were drawn in by leaders of the faction, the Ascendant Lord and his Ascendant Magus, who promised the downfall of the tyrants growing richer and powerful from misery and bloodshed.{{ref|name=MTCALM|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character(s) - The Ascendant Lord and Magus}}}} Breton-influenced, the Ascendant Order likened themselves to a knightly order, though most other orders thought of them as nothing more than well-armed fanatics.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOGR}} Nevertheless, they infiltrated nearly every level of Breton society,{{ref|name=ONU34|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/62239 The Elder Scrolls Online: High Isle & Update 34 Now Live on All Platforms] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} and established the Systres Archipelago as their main base of operations.

In truth, the leaders of the Ascendant Order were nobles. The Ascendant Magus was Count Damard of House Dufort, while the Ascendant Lord was [[Lore:Bacaro Volorus|Lord Bacaro]] of House Volorus from Cyrodiil. Bacaro's partner and children were killed during the [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]], and when the Three Banners War came around, he created the [[Lore:Society of the Steadfast|Society of the Steadfast]] as a humanitarian organization.{{Ref|name=SoST|{{Cite Book|The Society of the Steadfast}}}} Lord Bacaro settled in his holdings on the Systres and over time built goodwill through his Steadfast Society. He then planned to leverage his reputation to lure the major players responsible for the Three Banners War to their deaths. The leaders accepted Bacaro's invitation to High Isle for peace talks, but the plot to assassinate them ultimately failed.{{ref|[[Online:The Ascendant Storm|The Ascendant Storm]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|name=ESODR}}
[[File:ON-wallpaper-Firesong-2880x1620.jpg|thumb|right|Conflict on Galen]]
Concurrently, the druids on Galen were involved in a civil war orchestrated by Bacaro's unwitting pawn, Archdruid Orlaith of the Firesong Circle. When Orlaith failed to become the Druid King and forcibly unify the druids under one banner, Bacaro took matters into his own hands. Bacaro was also a member of House Merchad through his mother's bloodline, a noble house with ties to the last Druid King, Kasorayn. As its last living descendant, he sought to claim the Ivy Throne of the Druid King as a step towards taking the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]].{{Ref|name=ONLBJ|{{Cite Book|Lord Bacaro's Journal}}}} The Vestige confronted Bacaro at Mount Firesong, where he had ascended into a false Druid King. Utilizing his newfound power, Bacaro enslaved the dormant spirit of the volcano, which was capable of decimating Tamriel's armies.{{ref|name=ESODL}} However, Volorus was defeated, saving the archipelago from destruction.{{ref|name=THLord|Events of [[ON:The Hidden Lord|The Hidden Lord]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|name=TIThrone|Events of [[ON:The Ivy Throne|The Ivy Throne]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} 

What followed afterward was a more unified Systres Archipelago. The feudal Bretons and the druids that inhabited Galen began talks to build better relations, and the mending of wounds between the feuding noble houses of High Isle and Galen began.{{ref|name=ESOSSM}}

====The Third Empire====
During the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]], a Nord-Breton alliance captured [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]] in {{Year|2E 852}}. General Talos recaptured the city and integrated the Nordic forces, impressed by his [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]], into his own army.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} He executed the Breton leadership, and variously executed, enslaved, and sent the Breton forces back to High Rock.{{ref|name=BoST|{{Cite Book|Battle of Sancre Tor}}}} Those Bretons who remained sought to counter Talos with Reach-magic, and in {{Year|2E 854}}, entrusted a Reachman to assassinate [[Lore:Cuhlecain|Cuhlecain]] and his general. Talos survived the attack, and went on to found the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]]{{ref|name=PGE1C}}—though ''[[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|The Arcturian Heresy]]'' claims Talos himself slew Cuhlecain, and placed the blame on his enemies.{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}

At some point in their history, the Bretons were ruled by witch-kings.{{ref|name=CP}}{{Ref|name=DGP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Douglas Goodall's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Talos, now known as [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], would easily conquer the squabbling Breton kingdoms, but would find it impossible to truly squash the sectarian conflicts between them that hampered trade and prosperity.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=PGE3High}} Septim opted to entrust a governor of his choosing to end the feud between High Rock's witch-kings, which was a successful endeavor.{{ref|[[Redguard:Krisandra|Krisandra]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

===Third Era===
[[File:DF-misc-Death.jpg|right|thumb|Druids carrying a coffin in the Iliac Bay circa 3E 417{{ref|name=DFDeath}}]]
The Dream of Kasorayn foretold of druids retaking their place on Tamriel, and a possible future where the druidic circles would unite under one banner and reestablish themselves as the Druids of Galen.{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1604662466 Twitch Stream of ''ESO Live Legacy of the Bretons Autumn Event''], time stamp 59:10-59:26}}{{ref|name=ESOYoutubeFiresongTour|group=UOL|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JhQeU-Izx1w YouTube Stream of ''Firesong DLC Guided Tour with the Developers''], time stamp 25:33-26:08}}{{ref|name=ESOOrlaith|[[ON:Archdruid Orlaith|Archdruid Orlaith]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}}{{ref|name=ESODL|[[ON:Druid Laurel|Druid Laurel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} As previous attempts to reinvigorate their ways had led to great bloodshed,{{ref|name=ESODruidKing}}{{ref|name=ESOOrlaith}} druids instead began to seek a future which would not depend on a king or a prophecy to improve the world.{{ref|name=ESOSSM}} In this respect, Kasorayn's dream may simply be a metaphor, encouraging druids to live life as if the dream was close to fruition.{{ref|name=ESODL}} It is unknown what parts of the prophecy came true, but by the fifth century of the Third Era, druids were once again present in the regions surrounding the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=DFDeath|[[Daggerfall:Death|Death]] cutscene in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Dragon's Eyrie/Daggerfall|ns_base=General}}}} "By the ArchDruid" is a common exclamation by Bretons of this era.{{Ref|name=ARExclamations|[[Arena:Breton|Breton]] exclamations in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFExclamations|[[Daggerfall:Breton|Breton]] exclamations in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

By the Third Era, there were eight major Breton powers in the province of High Rock: the kingdoms of Daggerfall, Camlorn, Wayrest, Shornhelm, [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]], Evermore, [[Lore:Farrun|Farrun]], and [[Lore:Jehanna|Jehanna]].{{ref|name=EWR}} Since most Emperors in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] were Bretons or raised in High Rock, Bretons were generally supportive of [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] rule, but were often disgruntled by its ineffectual protection.{{ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}} However, the decentralized nature of Breton society also fostered surprising resilience during times of upheaval.{{ref|name=PGE3High}} One of the best examples of this was during the defeat of the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]], when four of the smaller Breton city-states&mdash;Dwynnen, [[Lore:Kambria|Kambria]], [[Lore:Phrygias|Phrygias]], and [[Lore:Ykalon|Ykalon]]&mdash;led a united force that halted the Usurper's long northward advance and finally defeated him in the [[Lore:Battle of Firewaves|Battle of Firewaves]] in {{Year|3E 267}}.{{ref|name=TFOTU}} Baron Othro, who had already gained fame for his defeat of the Lich of [[Lore:Castle Wightmoor|Castle Wightmoor]] during the [[Lore:Battle of Wightmoor|Battle of Wightmoor]] fourteen years prior, formed the modern barony of Dwynnen, which would eventually grow into of the more powerful city-states in western High Rock.{{ref|name=TFOTU}}
[[File:LO-misc-Daggerfall History.gif|thumb|left|The Battle of Cryngaine Field]]
Other wars which occurred in High Rock during this Era included the [[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]] in 3E 397, where Skyrim conquered land in the east which included the Kingdom of Jehanna. It later came to light that [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]] was behind Skyrim's aggression. When [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Emperor Uriel Septim VII]] regained his throne following the Imperial Simulacrum, great efforts were taken to revert the damage done by Tharn. At the other end of High Rock, the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] was fought between Daggerfall and Sentinel over the eponymous island in 3E 405. The [[Lore:Wars#Battle of Cryngaine Field|Battle of Cryngaine Field]] resulted in Daggerfall's victory, though at the cost of the life of their [[Lore:Lysandus|King Lysandus]]. His son and heir, [[Lore:Gothryd|Gothryd]], was crowned the new king of Daggerfall after the war.{{ref|name=PGE3High}}
[[File:LO-misc-Numidium Wall.png|thumb|right|The [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]], responsible for the Warp in the West]]
On the 7th of Frostfall in {{Year|3E 417}}, the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] occurred. The roughly two dozen city-states comprising southwestern High Rock were suddenly, forcefully, and mysteriously united into two kingdoms: Daggerfall and Wayrest, with Orsinium taking control of part of the Wrothgarian Mountains in central High Rock.{{ref|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of Iliac Bay – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}}{{ref|name=TWINT|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} The amount of squabbling kingdoms in High Rock was reduced from about a hundred to five. According to Imperial sources, it was the most unified High Rock had ever been.{{ref|name=PGE3High}}{{NewLeft}}

The populace and culture of the Systres remained largely Breton by {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|name=OBBR}} Despite this, some maps presented the islands as part of Hammerfell's territory just years prior during {{Year|3E 427}}.{{intnote|Systres|[nb 1]}}

===Fourth Era===
In the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] and the destabilization of the Third Empire, the combined forces of Hammerfell and High Rock sacked Orsinium, leading many Orc refugees to flee east into Skyrim.{{ref|name=LoS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls}}}}

High Rock was still part of the Empire when [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]] ascended the throne of Cyrodiil in {{Year|4E 168}}, and assisted Hammerfell during the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]]. The [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Third Aldmeri Dominion]] took Hammerfell's southern coastline, leaving Hegathe the lone surviving state. Those who survived the [[Lore:March of Thirst|March of Thirst]] joined Legion reinforcements from High Rock in northern Hammerfell in {{Year|4E 173}}, and the Dominion suffered losses in the province soon after. The Dominion withdrew in {{Year|4E 174}}. Bretons also participated in the climactic [[Lore:Battle of the Red Ring|Battle of the Red Ring]] in {{Year|4E 175}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

[[File:LG-quest-Regicide 02.jpg|thumb|left|Corsairs sack Wayrest (Legends)]]
In {{Year|4E 188}}, King Barynia of Wayrest was overcome by paranoia when rumors of conspiracy reached his ears. He purposely left his city vulnerable to outside attacks, hoping his enemies would be killed in the chaos. His actions led to the sack of Wayrest by Breton corsairs. The city's nobles hired an [[Lore:Forgotten Hero|assassin]] from the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] amid the conflict, though Barynia's fate is ultimately unknown.{{ref|name=LTFDB|[[Legends:The Fall of the Dark Brotherhood#Episode 2: The Corsair Conspiracy|Episode 2: The Corsair Conspiracy]] quest in [[Legends:The Fall of the Dark Brotherhood|The Fall of the Dark Brotherhood]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} The corsairs raided and destroyed the Dark Brotherhood sanctuary hidden in Wayrest before they departed.{{ref|name=LTFDB}}{{ref|name=CJ2|{{Cite Book|Cicero's Journal - Volume 2}}}} Piracy would remain an issue by {{Year|4E 197}} around the Wayrest area.{{ref|name=Cookbook2}}

Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]] looked to their neighbors in High Rock for aid in the midst of the [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|Skyrim Civil War]]. The Bretons, who had good relations with the Empire, refused to respond to the Stormcloaks.{{Ref|name=SRUlfricGalmar|Dialogue between [[Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]] and [[Skyrim:Galmar Stone-Fist|Galmar Stone-Fist]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Culture==
[[File:LO-misc-highrock1.jpg|thumb|right|A sketch of a northern guardsman by '[[Lore:¥R|¥R]]'|150px]]
Bretons are an intelligent, individualistic, energetic, pragmatic, and humorous people.{{Ref|name=MaraWasserman|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Marilyn Wasserman's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{Ref|name=TRB5|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 5}}}}{{ref|name=WJOTB}} They are said to enjoy intellectual pursuits; they often have an affinity for anything related to logic and ordered complexity.{{ref|name=KE9}} Their love of knowledge and affinity for commerce drives them into a host of careers, including trading, the military, sailing, medicine, textiles, manufacturing, writing, theology, philosophy, banking, all kinds of artistry, and other scholarly pursuits.{{ref|name=WJOTB}}{{ref|name=FOA}}{{ref|name=BB|{{Cite Book|Banker's Bet}}}}{{ref|name=16AOM|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness}}}}{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}} Their scholarly institutions include the [[Lore:Invisible College of Daggerfall|Invisible College of Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:School of Julianos|Schools of Julianos]].{{Ref|name=CS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 71: Coldsnap Style}}}}{{ref|name=DFJ|[[Daggerfall:School of Julianos|School of Julianos]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Espionage has also proven to be one of their strong suits; Breton double agents, assassins, and spies have turned the tide of wars throughout recorded history.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=BOST|{{Cite Book|Battle of Sancre Tor}}}}{{ref|name=2920FS|{{Cite Book|2920, First Seed (v3)}}}}{{ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}}{{ref|name=CF|{{Cite Book|Chance's Folly}}}} Bretons value the principles of freedom, praising it in their tavern songs and propaganda,{{ref|name=TBoGM}}{{ref|name=THotE|{{Cite Book|True Heirs of the Empire}}}} but this only extends so far in practice.{{ref|name=THotE}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, the people of Evermore were allowed to freely criticize Queen Arzhela and the royal family,{{ref|[[Online:Garel Green-Garters|Garel Green-Garters]], [[Online:Usquebald Emax|Usquebald Emax]], [[Online:Moumou Edier|Moumou Edier]], [[Online:Sextus Denter|Sextus Denter]], [[Online:Marcius Cipius|Marcius Cipius]], and [[Online:Nari Buteo|Nari Buteo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ARE|{{Cite Book|A Royal Embarrassment}}}} while using excessive profanity in the presence of nobility in High Isle was criminal.{{ref|[[ON:Timeo Dubroc|Timeo Dubroc]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} In the Third Era, a prolific bard and poet Darius Shano is also said to have criticized nobility, commoners and the Divines alike, receiving much scorn, but was only executed after ridiculing Tiber Septim.{{ref|name=16AOM9}}

It is a tradition for Breton parents to keep their childrens' bronzed baby teeth in a jar as remembrances.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Oddities#Bronzed Baby Teeth|Bronzed Baby Teeth]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some Breton families have retained heirlooms from their Nedic ancestors,{{ref|[[ON:Contraband N#Nedic Sword Pommel|Nedic Sword Pommel]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and tea sets are common family heirlooms.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Utensils#First Era Rivenspire Tea Service|First Era Rivenspire Tea Service]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Breton families host annual summer reunions, where they gather [[Lore:Candlefly|candleflies]] to illuminate their dinner, then release them into the evening sky after dessert.{{ref|name=ONTCL|[[Online:Temporary Candlefly Lantern|Temporary Candlefly Lantern]] memento description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Quilting and sewing circles are examples of hobby groups one might find in High Rock.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband L#Lovelorn Comforter|Lovelorn Comforter]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Contraband L#Lovelorn Quilt|Lovelorn Quilt]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A quilt of "lost hope" may be crafted by a Breton mother and daughter to work through past regrets and sorrows.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Dry Goods#Lost Hope Quilt|Lost Hope Quilt]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Using [[Lore:Malachite|malachite]] to make needles is popular among the Bretons of eastern High Rock.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Utensils#Malachite Needle|Malachite Needle]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

A Third Era joke book poked fun at Breton promiscuity.{{ref|name=LoreJokes|{{Cite Book|Jokes}}}} The [[Lore:Prostitutes Guild|Prostitutes Guild]] has a large, almost palatial guildhouse located in an affluent part of Camlorn.{{ref|name=MOT4|{{Cite Book|Mystery of Talara, v 4}}}} Some Bretons utilize [[Lore:Drugs|drugs]] to enhance their bedchamber activities.{{ref|name=AIOM|[[Online:Contraband A#Amorous Incense of Mara|Amorous Incense of Mara]] item description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Waking Day|Waking Day]] is a Breton holiday originally invented to wake the spirits of nature which evolved into an orgiastic celebration of winter's end.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Waking Day|Waking Day]] holiday in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The druids of the settlement of [[Lore:Llanshara|Llanshara]] on the Systres isle of Galen possessed an artifact which caused extreme arousal to those in its vicinity.{{ref|name=TSRR|[[Online:The Sea Runs Restless|The Sea Runs Restless]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}}{{ref|name=MinaN|{{Cite Book|Minahel's Note|ns_base=on}}}} This same settlement had a 'Ritual of Life's Commencement', an ancient tradition celebrating the creation of life through the sexual union of druids.{{ref|name=RoLC|{{Cite Book|Ritual of Life's Commencement|ns_base=on}}}} The Bretons of High Isle idolized [[Lore:Mermaid|mermaids]] as fertility icons.{{ref|name=HighIdol|[[Online:Contraband E#Ebony Mermaid Figurine|Ebony Mermaid Figurine]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Bretons have the Law of Consanguinity, which bans marriage between close relatives,{{Ref|name=TSoPE|{{Cite Book|The Story of Princess Eselde}}}} though Breton nobility are often rumored to practice incest. Prominent Bretons like King Ranser allegedly slept with every sibling in his royal line; rumors even implied that his daughter [[Lore:Rayelle|Rayelle]] was a product of one such coupling. ===Death===
:→''For more detailed information, see: [[Lore:Breton Death Beliefs|Breton Death Beliefs]]
[[File:ON-place-Pelin Graveyard.jpg|thumb|left|A Breton graveyard]][[File:ON-misc-Noble Knight's Rest.png|thumb|right|A Knight's Carved Sarcophagus]]
People of the northern regions of High Rock cremate their dead without exception, while burial remains common in the south. The Breton tradition of cremating the dead dates back to the days of High Rock's witch kings,{{ref|name=CP|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation}}}} with cremains commonly being stored in urns.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband B#Breton Funeral Urn|Breton Funeral Urn]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Bretons of the south build stone cemeteries to house their dead, and the nobility has always been interred in ornate mausoleums. Many Breton cemeteries are designed to provide direct sunlight on certain graves on highly specific high holy days on the Breton calendar. The ancient cemetery of Cath Bedraud, home to the corpses of many generations of Breton royalty, is constructed in a series of concentric bands atop a hill, with the oldest, most prestigious graves positioned in the high center of the graveyard.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Southern Bretons also sometimes practice [[Lore:Mummy|mummification]].{{ref|name=DF|[[Daggerfall:Mummy|Mummies]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Defiler Spirit|Defiler Spirit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Bretons have been in Tamriel since before recorded history. Breton cuisine is considered to be some of the very best in Tamriel, with many of Tamriel's greatest cooks having been Bretons.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Gianna|Gianna]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:To Kill an Empire|To Kill an Empire]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Breton cuisine takes advantage of the effects of alchemy through cooking, with specific ingredients not mattering but the principles of composition, the balance of essences, flavors, oils, and herbs; the preparation of these meals can cause them to act as a potion might.{{ref|name=tIC28|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}{{rp|28}} The complexity of certain recipes means that some Breton cooks use sundials to track cooking times.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband B#Bronze Windowsill Sundial|Bronze Windowsill Sundial]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Examples of a light Breton meal include grilled and deboned rock pigeons, combwort chutney, ballom pudding, and syllabub (milk and cider).{{ref|name=PaP|{{Cite Book|Picnic at Pelin (A Horror Story)}}}}

===Architecture===
Construction of Breton villages and towns vary depending on their location. For example, buildings in mountainous regions such as Kambria are fully stone. In southwestern High Rock, buildings are comprised of wood or stone with thatched roofs. Breton cities are described as having an austere grandeur.{{ref|name=PGE3High}}{{ref|name=CKL1|{{Cite Book|Charwich-Koniinge, Volume 1}}}} According to Imperial sources, function is valued over form, resulting in earthen, hand-crafted dwellings free from ornament.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} In reality, Breton architecture often features patternwork, sometimes exceptionally ornate; basic patterns include common Breton motifs such as the trefoil and basic knotwork. More ornate examples on buildings such as castles, cathedrals, and guild buildings feature detailed friezes, bas reliefs, and intricate stained glass windows. The triquetra motif was particularly common during the mid-Second Era, and was featured on numerous Breton buildings, furniture and other crafts. Breton architecture features heavy use of the pointed arch, evident in their walls, doors, windows, and towers.{{ref|Appearance of Breton settlements and furniture in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Pointed arches in Breton architecture are speculated to be a remnant of Direnni influence.{{ref|[[Online:Breton Well, Storm Grey|Breton Well, Storm Grey]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

Breton class stratification is reflected in their architecture, however, even the lower classes might have spaces that are fancy when compared to elsewhere.{{Ref|name=ONHCP|group=UOL}} Most people in High Rock live in small cottages in the countryside or townhouses in the cities; only the nobility and their servants live in large castles. Despite efforts under Tiber Septim to demilitarize the province by demolishing defensive structures, many villages are still surrounded by high walls, and fortresses dot the landscape.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=CKL1}} In the Systres Archipelago, the norm is that medium end homes have red clay tile flooring, while high end homes have elegant parquet flooring.{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.artstation.com/artwork/X1L9xY ESO: High Isle Tile Floor] and [https://www.artstation.com/artwork/nEVbN4 ESO: High Isle Parquet Floor] in ArtStation}}

Breton architecture shares similarities to the "Western Imperial Style", a style synonymous with villages throughout the western Empire, but which also features prominently in Imperial centers in the east, such as [[Lore:Pelagiad|Pelagiad]] and [[Lore:Caldera|Caldera]].{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue C|Caldera]] dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue P|Pelagaid]] dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Both Imperials and Bretons have influenced Redguard cosmopolitan architectural styles.{{ref|name=PGE1Hammerfell|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}

The druids create settlements where manmade constructions intertwine with and honor nature,{{ref|name=ONGTSC|{{Cite Book|Glimmertarn: A Stonelore Community}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Initiate Hinadin|Initiate Hinadin]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|firesong}}}} echoing the old druidic belief that civilization should develop alongside nature and not tame it.{{ref|name=ESODR}}{{intnote|Architecture|[nb 6]}} 

[[Lore:Mad Architect|Dutheil, the Mad Architect]], was a Breton architect from the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] whose talents led him to work for multiple Daedric Princes across the various planes of Oblivion, in exchange for the opportunity to commit vengeance against those that wronged him. He was known for his black-spired castles, with some being built on Nirn, such as the Crypt of Hearts.{{ref|name=TAWPT|{{Cite Book|To All Who Pass Through}}}}{{ref|name=EndSta|[[Online:The Endless Stair|The Endless Stair]] loading screen text}}{{ref|name=ESOTubidan the Alarmist|[[Online:Tubidan the Alarmist|Tubidan the Alarmist]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Req|{{Cite Book|Requisition Order}}}}

===Fashion: Armaments, Cosmetics, etc.===
[[File:ON-concept-Nobles' Fashionable Attire.jpg|thumb|left|Female Breton Fashion in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Influenced by constant warfare, Breton fashion is very utilitarian with various flairs,{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} while Breton arts and crafts are influenced by their time under the Direnni.{{ref|name=ONBS}} Examples of both together are Breton armor and weapons, which are ornate but practical,{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} yet they also display a sophistication reminiscent of elven elegance.{{ref|name=ONBS}} For clothing, Breton men like to look good in their everyday wear while they're at their trades,{{ref|[[Online:Wayrest Suede Doublet Ensemble|Wayrest Suede Doublet Ensemble]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while women may add a feminine flair to their clothing.{{ref|[[Online:Corseted Riding Outfit|Corseted Riding Outfit]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Those seeking to display their wealth do so through rich fabrics and glistening ornamentation.{{ref|[[Online:High-Collared Coin Ball Gown|High-Collared Coin Ball Gown]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The nobility prefers clothing that is elegant yet austere, which harkens back to High Rock's tradition of knightly orders.{{ref|[[Online:Soiree in Camlorn Evening Suit|Soiree in Camlorn Evening Suit]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Nobles typically wear simple yet elegant robes over their clothing when traveling to avoid sullying them, and display their wealth through the metal fastenings holding the robes together.{{ref|[[Online:Courtly Traveling Attire|Courtly Traveling Attire]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Nobles from Wayrest are known to throw dress-up parties utilizing suits of fur from various animals, including werewolves.{{ref|name=ONMenninia|[[Online:Menninia|Menninia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-concept-Breton Heavy.jpg|thumb|right|Breton plate armor]]
Breton arms are described as standard fare, in that when one thinks of a sword, a Breton blade will typically appear. Despite this, Breton arms and armaments are not unvaried and have can have their own flair, such as that shields may be emblazoned with lion heads, or [[Lore:Staves|staves]] can be carved and adorned with enchanted gems.{{Ref|name=ILAABC|{{TIL|Arms and Armor of the Breton Champion, Vignac Antieve of Evermore|arms-and-armor-breton-champion-vignac-antieve-evermore}}}} The mass-produced, one-handed, double-edged heavy broadsword commonly used by the Imperial Legions of the Third Empire were inspired by elaborate, decorated Breton broadsword designs. Broadswords are commonly used with shields, and Bretons favor the heavy tower shield. [[Lore:Highland Bretons|Highland Bretons]] were the first to use the heavy two-handed sword called the claymore, but their Redguard neighbors quickly adapted them to their own styles of fighting.{{ref|name=MWGaro|[[Morrowind:Garothmuk gro-Muzgub|Garothmuk gro-Muzgub]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Breton bows tend to be longbows&mdash;weapons made of one piece of springy wood. Yew, elm, and ash are common in Breton longbows, as the wood is dense and strong, but flexible if tillered properly.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bowyer and Fletcher}}}} The Bretons of old were said to have put [[Lore:Skyshard|skyshard]] silvers into the hilts of their swords, while early mages are believed to have built staves from roots of the [[Lore:Wyrd Tree|Wyrd Tree]].{{ref|name=ONABS|[[Online:Ancestral Breton Style|Ancestral Breton Style]] antiquity entries and [[Online:Crafting Motif 93: Ancestral Breton Style|motif description]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-misc-Breton Outfits.jpg|thumb|left|Male Breton Fashion in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Breton armor consists primarily of leathers, chainmail, scale mail, and plate armor, with tabards and heraldry added to the mix.{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL}}{{ref|[[Online:Armored Knights Pack|Armored Knights Pack]] various costume descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ILAABC}} The wealthy are known to add additional materials in the forging process of their plate armor to vary the color of their armor.{{Ref|name=ILAABC}} Bretons see tanning as a fine art. Quality pieces can use soft hides from animals such as [[Lore:Guar|guars]] and cattle strategically placed at areas such as the joints of a gorget, or a [[Lore:Wolf|wolf]]'s hide for vambraces.{{Ref|name=ILAABC}} Werewolf leather is prized by both armorsmiths and alchemists, and it is known to be utilized especially by Glenumbrans.{{ref|name=ONMenninia}}{{ref|[[Online:Hide of the Werewolf|Hide of the Werewolf]] generic and unique items dropping in [[Online:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} For metals, Bretons are known to use a variety: [[Lore:Iron|iron]] and [[Lore:Steel|steel]] are common, with [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] among the more exotic options.{{ref|name=SRSKA|[[Skyrim:Spell Knight Armor (item)|Spell Knight Armor]] equipment in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Paladins|Paladins]] from Daggerfall found a way to make armor stronger than ebony using [[Lore:Rubedite|rubedite]], a red ore.{{ref|[[Online:Rubedite Ore|Rubedite Ore]] surpassing [[Online:Ebony Ore|Ebony Ore]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Enchanted armor created with metalworking tools blessed by Akatosh and Zenithar was coated with a rubedite metal enamel, then tempered with captured dragonflame.{{ref|[[Online:Daggerfall Paladin|Daggerfall Paladin]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Tradition dictates that mages Tamriel-wide wear sturdy and practical robes, as magic itself is their armor.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Undeniable Truths of Attire}}}} Magic as armor is especially true for Breton mages, who are known for utilizing their natural magical talents to stand their ground against the fiercest opponents in battle by summoning fierce [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]] to aid them, or augmenting themselves with wards and bound armor.{{ref|[[Legends:Breton|Breton Race Description]] and related content in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} Bretons utilize cloth to craft their light-weight armor, which may be structurally reinforced using bone, horn, or cartilage. The outer layer of jerkins and breeches may contain damask or ornamental silk. Underneath that is a layer or layers of durable fabrics such as cambric or burlap, which are often quilted or padded for protection from blows.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Clothier: Light Armor Basics}}}} Distinct from mages are spell-knights, comparable to the Tamrielic tradition of battlemages, who may don heavy armor and wield melee weapons along with their spells in combat.{{ref|name=ONLOTB}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Folly in Fixation}}}} Breton spell-knights are known for full plate armor of varying materials, and due to their combat style, their enemies who survive are left scarred from flame and sword.{{ref|name=SRSKA}}{{ref|name=COTH}} The armor used by the Daggerfall Covenant, notably their helmets and shoulder cops, were influenced primarily by Breton knightly orders.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 26: Daggerfall Covenant Style}}}}
[[File:ON-concept-IC Concept 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Daggerfall Covenant's primarily Breton-Knight and Second Empire influenced armor]]

Druidic armorers, best described as plant-shapers, use their magic to improve their equipment's defensive and offensive traits. Their work is essentially vegetation carved and coaxed into intricate structures with a density nearly that of iron.{{ref|[[Online:Y'ffre's Will Style|Y'ffre's Will Style]] motif description in [https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/63457 January 2023 Crown Store Showcase]}}{{ref|name=ONYWS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 115: Y'ffre's Will Style}}}} The wood used remains alive, aware, and unharmed. Druids also use leather for their armor, but with a sense of loss and respect for the animal it was harvested from.{{ref|name=ONYWS}}

===Domesticated Animals===
Bretons raise a variety of domesticated animals for various purposes. Livestock such as [[Lore:Cow|Cows]], [[Lore:Horse|Horses]], [[Lore:Sheep|Sheep]], [[Lore:Pig|Pigs]], and pets such as [[Lore:Dog|Dogs]] and [[Lore:Cat|Cats]], can be found throughout High Rock.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Passive Creatures|Passive Creatures]] locations in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The Shornhelm Shepherd is a dog bred for sheep herding in Rivenspire's rugged terrain. They are known for their strength, speed, and their fierce loyalty, and they can be used as guard dogs.{{ref|[[Online:Shornhelm Shepherd|Shornhelm Shepherd]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Great Daenian Hound are gentle giants bred by the Beldama Wyrd as protectors and companions. They were adopted as trackers by the werewolf-hunting Silver Dawn.{{Ref|[[Online:Great Daenian Hound|Great Daenian Hound]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|[[Online:Silver Daenian Werewolf Tracker|Silver Daenian Werewolf Tracker]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Menevian Coachdog is known for its white coat with dark spots. They are perceptive and serve as deterrents to roadside hazards for the carts and coaches they often accompany.{{ref|[[Online:Menevian Coachdog|Menevian Coachdog]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Breton Terrier, originally bred to kill rats around the Iliac Bay's docks, is known for its joyful disposition.{{ref|[[Online:Breton Terrier|Breton Terrier]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Glenumbra Border Wolf resembles Breton dog breeds in coloration, and has been bred as a mount to carry couriers though harsh weather and the dark of night.{{ref|[[Online:Glenumbra Border Wolf|Glenumbra Border Wolf]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Wealthy Bretons often let Piebald Cats wander the hidden passages of their manor homes. This tradition arose thanks to infestations of Sheogorath's never-there beetles, which the cats eventually purged.{{ref|[[Online:Piebald Cat|Piebald Cat]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Indigo Senche-Panther, a [[Lore:Senche-Cat|Senche]] originally bred to be mounts by a reclusive family in Rivenspire, is said to hunt werewolves for amusement.{{ref|[[Online:Indigo Senche-Panther|Indigo Senche-Panther]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Senche-Serval mounts originating from the Summerset Isles{{ref|[[Online:Sapiarchic Senche-Serval|Sapiarchic Senche-Serval]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} have also seen some use in High Rock and the Systres. The Ashgray Senche-Serval breed, once only possessed by nobles of House Mornard, became a more common mount throughout Tamriel after the house fell on hard times.{{ref|[[Online:Ashgray Senche-Serval|Ashgray Senche-Serval]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Knights of the Silver Rose|Knights of the Silver Rose]] utilized Senche-Servals to rid their fortress of pests and other troubles.{{ref|[[Online:Silver Rose Senche-Serval|Silver Rose Senche-Serval]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Evermore Painter's Moth, local to the eponymous city, is a moth whose natural pigments are utilized by artists for their paintings, a notable example being the portrait of Duke Blaise Guimard. Artists have domesticated and have even raised sanctuaries for these insects.{{ref|[[Online:Evermore Painter's Moth|Evermore Painter's Moth]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The ravens of Rivenspire, some of the smartest of Tamriel, have often served as companions.{{ref|[[Online:Eyebright Raven|Eyebright Raven]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The blue cockerals of Alcaire are known for their "noble duty" of screeching at daybreak which continues throughout the morning.{{ref|[[ON:Alcaire Blue Cockeral|Alcaire Blue Cockeral]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Two [[Lore:Chub Loon|Chub Loon]] breeds are kept for companionship in the Systres. The Hay-Crown breed are seen as omens of good harvests, and they were originally from Rivenspire where they were rendered extinct, but those that were taken to [[Lore:High Isle|High Isle]] survived.{{ref|[[Online:Hay-Crown Chub Loon|Hay-Crown Chub Loon]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Fellbraun Chub Loon is native to the island of Galen, and are seen as a blessing from Y'ffre.{{ref|[[ON:Fellbraun Chub Loon|Fellbraun Chub Loon]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

The Bretons are known to keep a variety of mounts. ===Maritime and Mercantile Industry===
Bretons have a thriving and often intrepid merchant class that intersects with maritime activities.{{ref|name=TBMOP}} "Merchant princes",{{Ref|name=NAA|{{Cite Book|Northpoint: An Assessment}}}} "warrior-merchants",{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} and "merchant knights"{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}} are some societal archetypes, and Bretons that are encountered outside High Rock are often merchants or mercenaries.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}} Indeed, Bretons are often described as shrewd merchants, and as a race they are said to value prosperity over glory.{{ref|name=TCCAG}} ''The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel'' stereotypes Bretons as worshipping money over the Divines.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Sometimes Breton's merchant-oriented side of society manifests in a darker way via illicit activities. Breton lackeys and merchant lords have been involved in cartels, [[Lore:Drugs|drugs]], piracy, or at times even slavery.{{ref|name=ALML|{{Cite Book|Abah's Landing Merchant Lords}}}} High Rock's Coin-barons participated in slavery and other illicit practices during the First Era.{{ref|group=UOL|name=HIDD}}{{ref|name=ESOEHI}}

[[File:ON-concept-High Isle Shipbuilders.jpg|right|thumb|500px|Breton shipbuilders]]
Some of the earliest known Breton-made vessels are contained in the oral histories of the druids. It is said that Y'ffre taught the last Druid King how to sing seaworthy vessels into existence, and he in turn taught the druids to do the same. The plants and trees of High Rock are said to had been altered to the exact specifications needed to be used as materials for this endeavor.{{Ref|name=EOTD}} Similarly, scholars theorize that the trees of the Systres island of High Isle were also influenced by the druids to be well suited for shipbuilding.{{ref|name=ONAHSB|{{Cite Book|A History of Shipbuilding, Vol. 1 of 27}}}}

By the mid-Second Era, Bretons dominated in the naval shipbuilding industry, centering most operations along their prime trade routes. In the Systres Archipelago, the Bretons of House Dufort became perhaps the premier shipbuilders of Tamriel. Vessels from Dufort Shipyards, which entered every navy, were ranked among the finest. Due to "integrated business concerns", they shared a mutual reliance with their rivals House Mornard, as Dufort vessels transported and protected a great deal of Mornard trade goods.{{ref|name=ONAHSB}}{{Ref|name=Dufort|{{Cite Book|History of House Dufort}}}}

The Bretons of High Rock primarily utilize the waters of the Iliac Bay, the Eltheric Ocean,{{ref|name=GP}} and the Bjoulsae River for trade. Strategic and economic concerns have led to a great deal of naval warfare over the years.{{ref|name=PGE1WR}} The Masconian Trade Way was created as an anti-piracy measure.{{ref|name=WJOTB}}{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Not even the Bjoulsae River is safe, as its small islands often serve as bases for sea raiders.{{ref|name=Load|[[ON:Blackheart Haven|Blackheart Haven]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} One notorious island was known as [[Lore:Blackheart Haven|Blackheart Haven]], after a pirate captain that secured a fearsome reputation among the locals.{{ref|name=Redbeard|[[ON:Glirion the Redbeard|Glirion the Redbeard]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Load}}

A typical Breton merchant ship is a large wooden vessel rigged with square sails. These ships have also been utilized to carry troops.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Systrean caravals have been seen in ports as far as [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]].{{ref|[[Online:Morlia|Morlia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Warrior-sailors from High Isle often take on posts on escort ships to hone their martial prowess on the seas.{{ref|[[Online:Sailor-at-Arms|Sailor-at-Arms]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During the Second Era, Breton ship architect Jeromec Lemal sought to revolutionize naval warfare by including thicker masts, extra backstays, a narrower keel, lighter ballastas, reinforced hulls, and other improvements.{{ref|[[Online:Jeromec Lemal|Jeromec Lemal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Bretons of the Systres Archipelago export many raw materials and trade goods.{{ref|name=MotEA|{{Cite Book|Mysteries of the Eltheric Ammonite}}}} Primary industries include logging, mining, and shipbuilding.{{ref|name=ONSHV6}} An especially important commodity is [[Lore:Eltheric Ammonite|Eltheric Ammonite]], which washes up in the archipelago's shore. Collectors covet their beauty, while mages find them attractive as a perhaps more ethical raw source of magicka than [[Lore:Soul Gems|soul gems]].{{ref|name=MotEA}} [[Lore:Larimar|Larimar]] is another important trade good, a milky-blue gemstone exclusive to the Systres, making it extremely rare and expensive.{{Ref|name=OSRCodex}}{{ref|name=ONSHV6}} Though it has become a highly coveted rarity over time,{{ref|name=ONSHV6}} shipments of [[Lore:Frog-Metal|Frog-Metal]] from the Systres isle of Amenos are still exported.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Captured by the Dreadsails}}}} Druid-crafted volcanic jewelry has also been exported.{{Ref|name=OSRCodex}}

The biggest source of [[Lore:Orichalcum|Orichalcum]] in history was found near Wayrest circa {{Year|2E 561}}, which improved trade throughout High Rock.{{ref|name=GttDC}}{{ref|name=ToaM10|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch, Ch. 10}}}} Feathers belonging to [[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]], such as those from the Shrieking Scar in the Daggerfall highlands, are valued for use in quality calligraphy sets and kites.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}}{{ref|[[Online:Treasures H#Harpy Feather Calligraphy Set|Harpy Feather Calligraphy Set]] and [[Online:Treasures H#Harpy-Wing Kite|Harpy-Wing Kite]] items in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Earthenware is a common form of pottery in High Rock, and is used for goods such as chamber pots.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband C#Ceramic Shornhelm Chamber Pot|Ceramic Shornhelm Chamber Pot]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Bretons engage in a great deal of interprovincial trade. Under the Daggerfall Covenant, goods of Hammerfell reached a broader market in High Rock, including luxuries such as tapestries, embroidered throw pillows, and delicious pomegranate wine. As a downside, trade became restricted and less frequent with eastern Skyrim, which joined the enemy Ebonheart Pact, and some dependent on Nordic trade went out of business.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Comments of Maxence, banker}}}} The Bretons of High Isle, though, greatly profited due to their neutrality in the conflict.{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ}}

===Recreation: Art, Sports, etc.===
[[File:ON-crown store-Key Harp.jpg|thumb|left|The Key Harp, favored in High Isle]]
Bretons have a variety of recreational activities. Bardic traditions serve as a unifying legacy for the Bretons,{{ref|name=POT}} with examples including works such as "The Gonfalon Maid" celebrating love, "Blessings of Y'ffre" for the eponymous god,{{ref|[[Online:Bardic Performances#Elder Scrolls Online: High Isle|High Isle bardic performances]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or "The Battle of Glenumbria Moors" for the historical and pivotal victory.{{ref|name=TBoGM}} [[Lore:Kieran|Kieran the Bard]], a noted literary figure, inspired shrines called Kieran Theatres throughout the Iliac Bay,{{Ref|name=DFShrines}} while the bard and poet [[Lore:Darius Shano|Darius Shano]] serves as a reminder to not deal with daedra.{{ref|name=16AOM9|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness, v. IX}}}} Named the Poet-Champion by Potentate Savirien-Chorak, [[Lore:Nynelle Dumaris|Nynelle Dumaris]] rose to prominence with her famous sonnets ''[[Lore:A Lissome Sprite|A Lissome Sprite]]'' and ''[[Lore:Fearless as the Sun|Fearless as the Sun]]'' following her victory in a poetry tournament in the Imperial City in {{Year|2E 400}}.{{ref|name=TPCOV|{{Cite Book|The Poet-Champion of Vastyr}}}}{{ref|name=ALS|{{Cite Book|A Lissome Sprite}}}}{{ref|name=FATS|{{Cite Book|Fearless as the Sun}}}} A painting called "Frolicking Trolls of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]]", which depicts smiling frost trolls dancing in the snow, is said to be how a Breton imagines the [[Lore:Frost Troll|frost trolls]] of Skyrim.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures F#"Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch"|"Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch"]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Bretons play a variety of musical instruments. Lutes and drums are standard,{{ref|[[Online:Bisquelas the Bard|Bisquelas the Bard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but they also play a variant of a lyre known as a talharpa,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Dozzen Talharpa}}}} and bugles often sound during war campaigns.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures L#Lion Guard Bugle|Lion Guard Bugle]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They have bellowing instruments known as concertinas,{{ref|[[Online:Treasures H#High Isle Concertina|High Isle Concertina]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while key harps are the favored instrument in High Isle.{{ref|[[Online:Collectible Emotes#Key Harp|Key Harp]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Bagpipes in Galen were made from a [[Lore:Hoarvor|hoarvor]]'s bloat-sac and proboscis, often adorned with [[Lore:Parrot|parrot]] feathers.{{Ref|name=TIGalen|[[Online:Treasures/Galen#Hoarvor-Sac Bagpipes|Hoarvor-Sac Bagpipes]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} Druids purportedly began using pan flutes after a group of [[Lore:Faun|Fauns]] proffered how to make them.{{Ref|[[Online:Treasures D#Druid's Whispering Willow Flute|Druid's Whispering Willow Flute]], [[Online:Collectible Emotes#Play Pan Flute|Play Pan Flute emote]] descriptions}} The Stonelore druids are known to wear a bracelet of bells during their ritual dances around fires.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures D#Druid's Whispering Willow Flute|Druid's Whispering Willow Flute|High Isle Concertina]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Bretons have a multitude of recreational activities, including a sport called "Bretonball", which is played with rackets.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Oddities#Bretonball Victory Plaque|Bretonball Victory Plaque]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Racing rowboats is a centuries old pasttime centered around the Bjoulsae River.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures/Games#Bjoulsae Racing Oar|Bjoulsae Racing Oar]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Shuttlecocks, traditionally used as a lure in falconry, were repurposed into a sport involving making it fly with wooden paddles.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures/Games#Gull Feather Shuttlecock|Gull Feather Shuttlecock]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Triangle Chess involves a three-sided board.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures/Games#Noble House Triangle Chess Set|Noble House Triangle Chess Set]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Khimera, themed around the Chimeras of legend, is a traditional Breton yard game played by children.{{Ref|name=TBOG}} The denizens of High Isle enjoy "Proof", a murder mystery role-playing card game based on the ''[[Lore:Investigator Vale|Investigator Vale]]'' book series.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures/Galen#Proof!|Proof!]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Society==
===Politics===
:''"You think politics here are something? Well, we Bretons invented politics."'' — ''Anton Virane''{{ref|name=SRAV}}
[[File:ON-render-ESO Tavern Event Announcement.jpg|thumb|left|Breton nobles at a social gathering]]
High Rock is segmented into city-states, encouraging petty nationalism and infighting between the kingdoms. Most Breton cities are sprawling trade hubs with highly developed social strata. The poor peasantry lies at the bottom of the hierarchy, above them is a middle class of mostly merchants and artisans, and noble ruling families sit at the top. Additionally, there are the many autonomous knightly orders, as well as a small haughty magical elite.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} The jockeying for power among the various powers of the Iliac Bay region is a deeply ingrained and even cherished part of Breton culture.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} Breton society is fractious and quarrelsome, but internal conflict seems to disappear when their way of life is threatened by outside forces.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}}

Bretons' largely agrarian and feudal society, as well as their obsession with nobility, status, and lineage, was shaped by their days as vassals of Clan Direnni.{{ref|name=ONBS}}{{ref|name=TBMOP}}{{Ref|name=MDBMM}} Bretons borrowed and modified their elven ancestor's feudal system to suit their needs, removing the aspect that forced people to stay in the same social class for their rest of their lives.{{Ref|name=MDBMM}}{{Ref|name=ONHCP|group=UOL}} Indeed, their outlook is shaped from breaking free from their elven masters and taking High Rock for themselves. Thus Bretons live lives of excess and believe in upwards mobility to the extent that even those at the very bottom of the social ladder feel like they can always aspire to become better and advance to the top of the social ladder.{{Ref|name=ONHCP|group=UOL}} Despite this, there is still however a sense of elitism and otherism in Breton society. Some nobles may look down at common folk as inferior to them.{{ref|name=ONAV|[[ON:Albense Varin|Albense Varin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Charbel Pamarc|Charbel Pamarc]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

Though Bretons of lower status have several paths to prosperity, becoming a nobleman by performing quests and services to curry favor with various rulers is considered the best way, which has created a cultural "quest obsession" among young Bretons.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=FAOT|{{Cite Book|Famed Artifacts of Tamriel}}}} The desire to find some great opportunity for fortune and glory has made Bretons venture all over Tamriel, even to such remote locations as [[Lore:Thirsk|Thirsk]] on the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], where one adventurous Breton once earned the position of chieftain.{{ref|name=PGE1S}}{{ref|name=TCATD|{{Cite Book|The Cake and the Diamond}}}}{{ref|name=TAHR|{{Cite Book|Thirsk, a History -- Revised}}}}
[[File:LO-misc-Daggerfall Noble.jpg|thumb|130px|right|A noble from Daggerfall]]

===Peerage===
:''"Find a new hill, become a king"'' — ''Breton Proverb''{{ref|name=PGE1High}}
[[File:LG-cardart-High King Emeric.jpg|thumb|left|High King Emeric Cumberland, leader of the Daggerfall Covenant]]
Across High Rock, traditions of the peerage differ slightly from region to region. The base rule of the eight kingdoms is that if a woman is ruling one of these areas, she is called the Queen. The husband of a Queen and the wife of a King is not necessarily of equal rank – they may not be Kings and Queens themselves. Their children are Princes and Princesses. Their grandchildren are also Princes and Princesses. If a male ruler dies, his wife takes the title Dowager Queen, providing there is not a Dowager Queen already. In the Kingdom of Daggerfall, it is permissible for there to be two persons with the same title. If a female ruler, who does not share rank with her husband, dies, there is no male equivalent to the word Dowager. Widowers of Queens usually take another title, either a lesser family title or one given by their children. There have been few men in the history of High Rock who have fallen from being addressed as King to being addressed as Mister at the death of their wife.{{ref|name=EWR|{{Cite Book|Etiquette With Rulers}}}}

Other regions in High Rock are ruled by Dukes and Duchesses, Marquises (or Marquesses) and Marquises (or Marchionesses), Counts and Countesses, Viscounts or Viscountesses, Barons or Baronesses, and Lords or Ladies. This is theoretically listed from highest to lowest rank, but the ruler of a territory outranks all other nobles, regardless of title.

Counts usually enjoy less autonomy than dukes and duchesses, as their territories are subservient to the duchies they are part of. However, they serve as vital links to guilds, churches, and scholastic organizations that might otherwise receive less attention from the duchy's rulers. Beneath counts and countesses, peerage hierarchy flattens considerably. Barons and baronesses stand above most, but these titles rarely carry robust land rights. Instead, they serve as titles of honor, granting the noble certain privileges at court. Dignitaries should address barons and baronesses as "my lord" and "my lady" respectively.{{ref|name=NRAT|{{Cite Book|Noble Ranks and Titles}}}}

Dwynnen, for example, is a Barony, and the Baron or Baroness of Dwynnen outrank any other noble in that territory, even Dukes and Counts. In theory, (again, this may not be the case according to local custom) the eldest son or daughter of a noble takes their parent's highest family title below their parents. Thus, the Duke of Northmoor, who is also the Marquis of Calder, had a daughter who became the Marchioness of Calder.{{ref|name=EWR}}

Kings and Queens are always addressed as "Your Majesty" in conversation; Dukes and Duchesses, "Your Grace". All other rulers may be addressed with their title and name, or Lord or Lady and their name.{{ref|name=EWR}}

One way of telling who is in charge of a region is to pay some attention to the names of taverns and shops in a region. By tradition, many of these are called names such as "The Duke's Fox" or "The Lady's Provisions." This, more often than not, is the title of the ruler. If the shop's name is, for example, "Lady Annisa's Provisions" or "Lord Boxworth's Fox", that is probably the name of a local titled merchant, not the ruler. A store with an unnamed ruler's title has probably been around for some time, and does not bother to change its name with the new name of the ruler.{{ref|name=EWR}}

Emeric of Cumberland, as a result of being the architect of the Daggerfall Covenant, became High King and the first to ever claim supreme leadership over all High Rock. All the city-states of northwest Tamriel swore loyalty to the Covenant's Royal Council.{{ref|name=GttDC}} The various monarchs of High Rock were bound to him via trade agreements,{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} which included "dozens of compromises and carefully negotiated provisions".{{ref|name=GttDC}}

===The Role of Magic among Bretons===
[[File:LG-cardart-Lion Guard Strategist.png|thumb|left|Breton Spell-Knights]][[File:LO-misc-Breton.jpg|thumb|right|Breton mage that dabbled in the dark arts]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''You Bretons have a reputation for mystical aptitude, but I would argue that the cost of that success has been a lack of lasting impact on the greater magical community. I could find numerous instances of Mage So-And-So making a splash with war magics for a few decades, and then dying. Or a great divinator prognosticating portentous events, and then dying. Breton mages burn brightly but briefly, I suppose, a sad commentary on the lifespan your uncaring gods have gifted your people.''|Divayth Fyr{{Ref|name=LAHT}}}}
Bretons are known to be inventive wizards.{{ref|name=TCCAG}} Magic permeates throughout all of Breton society, from the serf all the way up to the king.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLI}} Though no two mages weave their spells in exactly the same way,{{ref|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:DLC#Summerset|ESO: Summerset]]}} the average Breton mage is said to perform "flamboyant gesticulations" when spellcasting.{{ref|name=WAS|{{Cite Book|Wind and Sand}}}} Some Bretons are born with innate gifts for unique kinds of magic, such as [[Lore:Thaumavocalism|thaumavocalism]].{{Ref|name=MBFP|[[Online:Meryline Barthel|Meryline Barthel]]'s dialogue and actions during the quest [[Online:A Father's Pride|A Father's Pride]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Due to the innate magical ability of Bretons, children are tested for their magical potential at an early age, and those who pass enter apprenticeship programs funded by the Mages Guild or independent sponsorships.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} Children are known to practice illusion spells on each other in the streets of cities such as Daggerfall.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Fahamal, Trader}}}} In more remote regions, such as Glenpoint and the Wrothgarian Mountains, witches and medicine men, barely distinguishable from Orcish shamans, hold sway over the superstitious peasants with feats of untutored, but often impressive, magical ability.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} Many use their magical talents to earn success,{{ref|name=PGE1High}} such as undertaking an ancient traditional role of a court mage.{{ref|name=ONCDD}} Not much is known about the magical elite that sit right below the ruling families in the social hierarchy, but these gifted see themselves as above the rest.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}{{ref|name=POT}}

===Glenumbrans===
[[File:ON-crown store-Camlorn Top Hat Pack.jpg|left|thumb|Camlorn Top Hats]]
In the mid-Second Era, the region of Glenumbra included Daenia, [[Lore:King's Guard|King's Guard]] and the [[Lore:Cambray Hills|Cambray Hills]], each distinguishable by many unique elements. In southern Glenumbra, Daenia is the home of the Bretons' quest obsession: the practice by which young serfs or peasants perform great deeds to elevate their societal standing. Daenian tales commonly include lowly individuals going on great quests to attain respect, glory and status. Consequently, the people of Daenia have reputations as independent spirits. Daenia is also home to the Wyrd subculture, and nature-worshipping witches reside in the region's dense forests.{{ref|name=GP|{{Cite Book|Glenumbra's People}}}}

Glenumbra's Cambray Hills, most prominent in Camlorn, has been a melting pot with an aristocracy described as poets and artists.{{ref|name=GP}} The region lends its name to the famed Cambray Hills Academy of Art.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband C#Cambray Hills Academy of Art Sketchbook|Cambray Hills Academy of Art Sketchbook]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Cambray Hills are haunted, and ghost stories abound among the populace. Camlorn has served as an open market for goods coming in from the Iliac Bay and the waters west of High Rock, dealing peacefully with Daggerfall and Wayrest to its benefit. The swamp-dwellers of the eastern bogs are the Cambray Hills' largest subculture.{{ref|name=GP}} Though apparently originally founded during the First Era,{{ref|name=2920FS}} the modern iteration of the Barony of Dwynnen would be founded by the legendary [[Lore:Othrok|Baron Othrok]] in {{Year|3E 253}} after the Battle of Wightmoor. It would go on to become cultural center of the region in the Third Era.{{ref|name=TFOTU}}

The northermost reaches of Glenumbra became known as King's Guard, after the mountain range separating Glenumbra from Rivenspire and Stormhaven. [[Lore:Crosswych|Crosswych]] is the center of trade between Glenumbra and its neighboring regions, and another cultural melting pot of Bretonic city-states. The Red Rook bandit clan originated in King's Guard as a loose group of hunters and highwaymen that targeted caravans traveling across regional borders.{{ref|name=GP}} Kambria is another notable city in this region, renowned for its picturesque nature and many libraries. Kambria rules over the windswept plateau in the north, which is still dotted with the fortresses that were ruined during the Tiber War.{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=TFOTU}}{{ref|name=CKL1}}

===Hillmen of Rivenspire===
[[File:ON-place-Rivenspire.jpg|right|Rivenspire shapes its people|thumb]]
The [[Lore:Hillmen|hillmen]] are the people of Rivenspire,{{ref|name=TFoKR}}{{intnote|Hill Giants|[nb 3]}} known as the backwater of High Rock.{{ref|name=TFoKR}} The region's people are shaped by its tortured landscape.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} They've been described as "surly",{{ref|name=TFoKR}} "gloomy", "laconic", and having "little to say to strangers".{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} They are also known for their "overactive imaginations".{{ref|name=ONBF}} Hillmen hunters can make a decent living off of the pelts of wolves and giant bats, as well as from spider venom, but must be wary of Rivenspire's many hostile creatures.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Ilyes, huntsman}}}} Restless spirits are known to haunt the region's forts,{{ref|name=ONOT|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/25855 One Tamriel: See the Sights of Rivenspire] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} and rumors exist of "evil fog" where the souls of fallen sailors stir off the coast.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Overheard at The Crow's Foot Inn}}}}

Rivenspire's dark history has haunted the region's people.{{ref|name=ONOT}} A number of atrocities and despots have made their mark, such as the violence against the Direnni under the orders of King Festrien Spenard I,{{ref|name=ROTPO}} and the Purge of the Wyrd-Hags during [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]] under King Berthulic.{{ref|[[Online:Lorkrata Hills|Lorkrata Hills]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SCCOTN|{{Cite Book|Shornhelm, Crown City of the North}}}} Fighting broke out between the natives and Ayleids who migrated to the region, resulting in incalculable damage. These elves had attempted to transform what they called the "Land of Cold Rocks"{{Ref|name=ROL|{{Cite Book|The Remnant of Light}}}} into a region full of life, but their method for doing so, the Light of Life was damaged in the conflict. It became the Lightless Remnant, a corrupting influence on Rivenspire. This influence was further amplified when it was touched by [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] during the Second Era,{{ref|name=Wynaldia|[[Online:Wynaldia|Wynaldia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} leading to a vampire crisis perpetuated by one of Rivenspire's noble houses, the [[Lore:House Montclair|Montclairs]].{{ref|name=BOR|{{Cite Book|Bloodfiends of Rivenspire}}}}

For all their differences, the noble houses of Rivenspire hold many of the same traditions. Each house keeps its own fighting force, though in times of war or other emergencies, it is not uncommon for house troops to join with the city guard to form a single fighting force in defense of the region.{{ref|name=HTARH|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} The nobility of Rivenspire are known for burying their dead beneath the gnarled trees of Westmark Moor (except for monarchs, who are all buried in the great cemetery of [[Lore:Cath Bedraud|Cath Bedraud]] in Glenumbra).{{ref|name=TBOWM|{{Cite Book|The Barrows of Westmark Moor}}}}

===Knightly Orders===
:→''For more detailed information, see: [[Lore:Breton Knightly Orders|Breton Knightly Orders]]''
[[File:ON-render-Castle Navire.jpg|left|thumb|A Knightly Tournament]]
Breton society is famous for its knightly orders,{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}} with notable examples including Daggerfall's Knights of the Dragon, the [[Lore:Knights of the Rose|Knights of the Rose]] of Wayrest, the [[Lore:Knights of the Flame|Knights of the Flame]] of [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]], and Dwynnen's [[Lore:Order of the Raven|Order of the Raven]]. Some Bretons served as knights under Clan Direnni sometime before the fall of the Direnni Hegemony.{{ref|name=ONARCP}}{{ref|name=ONTNS}} However, it is commonly accepted that Bretons established their militant orders of knights when they became independent from Direnni elves. High Rock's cultural history is founded on tales of such noble and chivalrous knights, which supposedly fought for Breton liberation and drove the Direnni back to Balfiera Isle. The knightly orders were founded to carry on the tradition of nobles-in-arms and to ensure the province would have defenders at the ready should invading armies come to their gates.{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}} The [[Lore:Lion Guard|Lion Guard]] was one such order before the formation of the Daggerfall Covenant in the Second Era.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Breton knights espouse traditional codes of chivalric principles and are among the most prominent questers of High Rock.{{ref|name=TL}}{{ref|name=ONDHQ}}
[[File:BK-misc-Breton Warrior.jpeg|right|thumb|A Breton warrior]]
Historically, knightly orders were composed of the high-born, providing a place for excess nobility. As High Rock became more prosperous, the merchant classes increasingly joined.{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}} It is more difficult for commoners,{{ref|name=ONAV}} but not impossible. Lady Joslin Garick, a captain in the All Flags Navy, is but one commoner who became a renowned knight.{{ref|name=ONCG}} Knighthood confers a measure of nobility, and can be rewarded to the lower classes for significant contributions to society. If the position was not achieved through conflict, the membership is nominal only, such as with the "merchant knights", great contributors to the economy who do not take up arms, but instead heavily contribute to a knightly order's finances.{{ref|name=ONTKOHR}}

Most competent knightly orders require applicants to have some degree of martial knowledge or skill before they are considered for membership. Less reliable orders may grant promotions in rank over a knight's nobility, wealth, or charisma, but generally, a knightly order will require their members to be skilled warriors so as not to tarnish the group's reputation.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Traditions of the Lion Guard}}}} Etiquette, swordsmanship, archery, and medical skill are factors considered when assessing potential recruits.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Knightly Orders#Guild Skills|List of required skills]] for all [[Daggerfall:Knightly Orders|Knightly Orders]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The spell-knight is an archetype that encapsulates the Breton staples of knighthood and the arcane,{{ref|name=ONLOTB}}{{Ref|name=MTCSSM}}{{ref|name=COTH|{{Cite Book|Crypt of the Heart - Draft}}}} while the druid-knight brings the druidic arts into the lifestyle of a knightly order.{{ref|name=ESOSSM}}{{ref|name=ONSKO}} The Reachmen were inspired to breed their own hardy steeds when they saw how mounted Bretonic knights fought during their invasion of High Rock.{{ref|name=ONWKC}}

Knights are addressed with the honor of "sir" or "dame". Knights subscribe to their own hierarchies and customs as dictated by their order. In most cases, these orders are governed by a marshal or a knight-commander. Regardless of the marshal's high station, their honorific remains the same.{{ref|name=NRAT}} In older chivalric orders, heavy rings served as a knight's symbol of membership.{{ref|name=ROTPO}} Each of the Eight Divines have their own templar knightly order as the militant arm of their respective temples.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Temples#Temples and Templar Knightly Orders|Temples and Templar Knightly Orders]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} One of High Rock's most notable heroes was the Breton knight Eleidon, owner of the eponymous [[Lore:Eleidon's Ward|shield]].{{ref|name=FAOT}}

===Systreans===
[[File:ON-prerelease-Firesong 03.jpg|right|thumb|Druids and knights of Vastyr uniting against Maormer pirates]]
The Systreans are the inhabitants of the Systres Archipelago. Primarily the descendants of the Bretons who settled on High Isle following the All Flags Navy's raid on Thras,{{ref|name=ONSHV4}} the Systreans are major maritime traders, have their own knightly orders, and mostly follow a feudal society.{{ref|name=LABHI}}{{ref|name=SHV|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Vastyr}}}} Of course, druids settled in the archipelago long before,{{ref|name=LABHI}} and though they prefer life in the forest, they have at times influenced and intermingled with the feudal Bretons.{{ref|name=ONSHV5}}{{ref|name=ONSHADG}}

The Systres is an established neutral ground from conflicts, which the Bretons of High Isle greatly profit from.{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ}} [[Lore:House Dufort|House Dufort]] sells respite to people looking to get away from mainland troubles.{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ}} It is considered a home away from home for much of the Breton elite,{{Ref|name=ONHIPTZ}} who treat it like a playground and resort.{{Ref|name=ONHCP|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL}} High Isle elite always dress in their finest to project their status and their barely-hidden ambitions,{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL}} and the settlement of [[Lore:Gonfalon Bay|Gonfalon Bay]] has been described as "pompous".{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL}}

The isle of Galen developed a more working class population, and they are the most accepting of Druidism.{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL}} Unlike High Isle, where mainland influence established a feudal society long ago, most of Galen remained an untamed wilderness, with city Bretons are limited to the southern part of the land, in the city of [[Lore:Vastyr|Vastyr]].{{ref|name=SHV}}{{ref|name=ESOLiveFiresong|group=UOL}} The city features a blend of druidic and city Breton architecture, thanks to the [[Lore:House Mornard|House Mornard]]'s connections to Druidism.{{ref|name=SHV}}

The undesirables of Systrean society are taken to the lawless prison island of [[Lore:Amenos|Amenos]], where history shows its prisoners are forced into mining its resources.{{ref|name=ONSHV6}} At one point, some were taken to nearby [[Lore:Whalefall|Whalefall]] to be used for sport.{{Ref|name=ONLouna|[[Online:Louna Jolvanne|Louna Jolvanne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONLendanor|[[Online:Lendanor|Lendanor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

==Religion==
[[File:ON-concept-High Isle Cathedral.jpg|left|Breton cathedral on High Isle|thumb]][[File:ON-Statue-Breton Statue, Arkay.jpg|thumb|right|A statue of Arkay (ESO)]]
Bretons believe two opposing forces, called the [[Lore:Anu|Light]] and the [[Lore:Padomay|Dark]] by some, have existed since before the gods were born. There are different interpretations, but they are said to be antithetical to each other, and evenly matched, but defy mortal conceptions of good and evil. They are technically immortal, as they do not live, only exist. The Aedra and Daedra are seen as pale reflections of the eternal conflict between the Light and Dark. Their endless struggle creates energies that distort their surroundings, and those energies are so powerful that life can appear.{{ref|name=TLD|{{Cite Book|The Light and the Dark}}}}

The undead and the Daedra tend to align with the Dark, as their natures are more akin to it. Mortals and the creatures of Mundus are more aligned with the Light. The evils of mortalkind more attuned to the Dark are incredibly dangerous. Magic is believed to come directly from the impersonal, intermingling energies swirling about both entities. Black magic is more a matter of intent than effect: destruction spells on their own are not black magic, for example, but are black magic when cast upon innocent people. The defeat of Daedra strengthens the light just a little. Conversly, the destruction of a Tamrielic creature strengthens the Dark. The gods are believed to mostly align with the Light, save for an ambiguous few. Mortals and those that reside in [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] can perceive traces of the Light and Dark; the Light inspires most mortals to behavior deemed good by human standards. What the denizens of Mundus can perceive of the Dark is utterly evil by human standards; those who have visions of the Dark are often driven mad. The Dark is described as an emptiness that draws the soul in to be twisted and destroyed.{{ref|name=TLD}}

According to the forbidden texts of Stormhaven's [[Lore:Priory of the Golden Staff|Priory of the Golden Staff]], Mehrunes Dagon was created by the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] to unmake the flawed creation of Mundus in accordance to [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]'s plans.{{Ref|name=DCT|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}}{{Ref|name=MTCSC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sister Celdina}}}} Some think Bretons are not disposed to "excessive religion",{{ref|name=AHOD}} while others consider them nearly as devout as Colovian Imperials.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} Some rural lords of High Rock were known to practice patron spirit worship, and offending them was considered an ill omen by local shamans.{{Ref|name=ArenaRumors|[[Arena:Rumors#Province General Rumors|Province General Rumors]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

===Religious History===
[[File:ON-load-Heart of the Wyrd Tree.jpg|thumb|left|The Wyrd Tree]]
The earliest Bretons, the Manmer, worshipped gods derived from the faiths of their Nedic and Aldmer ancestors, including [[Lore:Sheor|Sheor]]{{ref|name=CN|[[ON:Curator Nicholas|Curator Nicholas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]].{{ref|name=LADCG}} These Manmer inherited a form of nature worship from the Nedes, but this faith split into two separate subcultures. The witches of the Wyrd became their own entity due to the elves, and these witches continue to worship "old gods of the ancient past".{{ref|name=LADCG}} Druidism, on the other hand, arose shortly after the Bretons first came about,{{ref|name=ONWD}}{{ref|name=ONLOTB}} and their Y'ffre-centric religion survived in the Systres Archipelago after their exodus.{{ref|name=ONSHV}} Druidism would later be reintroduced to mainland Tamriel, specifically around the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=DFDeath}}{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL}}

Details of Aldmeri religious influence among the earliest Bretons are sparse. Evidence of peaceful cultural diffusion of religion between men and mer exists in the form of unearthed ancient idols of Mara as depicted in the elven pantheon.{{ref|name=Manmer}} Interestingly, Sheor is depicted positively in a lovers embrace with this elven Mara figure,{{ref|name=Manmer}} when Sheor is demonized in modern Breton religion,{{Ref|name=VOFB}} just as the pantheon equivalent of Lorkhan was demonized by Aldmer.{{ref|name=VOFHE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The High Elves}}}} Worship of elven gods still exists in modern Breton culture; Bretons that emphasize their elvish blood are often drawn towards Phynaster.{{Ref|name=VOFB}} The gods Magnus and Y'ffre are traditionally associated with elven pantheons.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Druids claim that though the Nedes did not know the name "Y'ffre" until the "daughters and sons of … Anuiel" introduced it, they still felt Y'ffre's presence in their hearts and channeled his power.{{ref|name=ONWD}}

Most Bretons transitioned to the Eight [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] under the Alessian Empire. The Eight Divines are worshipped widely in mainstream Breton culture,{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} but other traditional deities still persist nonetheless. Sheor, Phynaster, Y'ffre and Magnus have significant cults in High Rock.{{ref|name=VOF}}

Daedra worship is often seen in a negative light among Bretons. During the time of the Three Banners War, it was described as something needing to be cleansed.{{ref|name=THOTE}} Negative sentiments against it required intervention from King Kurog, who legitimized his people's religious worship among their Daggerfall Covenant allies by claiming that Mauloch wasn't a Daedric Prince like [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]].{{Ref|name=AUADC1|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)|Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)]]}} High King Emeric and his counselors tolerated the [[Lore:Spirit Wardens|Spirit Wardens]] of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] due to their charitable contributions, their ability to be inconspicuous, and their ability to produce potions capable of combating the nightmares inflicted by the [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]] cult known as the [[Lore:Supernal Dreamers|Supernal Dreamers]].{{ref|name=Altien|[[ESO:Master Altien|Master Altien]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Knights of the Silver Rose ousted a count of Daggerfall for his worship of Meridia during this time period.{{ref|name=OCESO|{{Cite Book|Artifact Record: Opal Charm}}}}

===Major Gods===

====[[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]====
The Dragon God of Time is acknowledged as the first of the gods to emerge from the Beginning Place. He embodies endurance, invincibility, and everlasting legitimacy.{{ref|name=VOF}} Akatosh is hailed by the [[Lore:Akatosh Chantry|Akatosh Chantry]] in the Iliac Bay, and the [[Lore:Order of the Hour|Order of the Hour]] is his sword.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Akatosh Chantry|Akatosh Chantry]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=OOTH|[[Daggerfall:Order of the Hour|Order of the Hour]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} He is the patron deity of Wayrest and the [[Lore:Isle of Balfiera|Isle of Balfiera]].{{ref|Patron deities for [[Daggerfall:Wayrest|Wayrest]] and the [[Daggerfall:Isle of Balfiera|Isle of Balfiera]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

====[[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]]====
Arkay (also spelled Ark'ay){{ref|name=ATG|{{Cite Book|Ark'ay The God}}}} is the god of burials and funeral rites, and is associated with the seasons.{{ref|name=VOF}} According to a [[Lore:Ark'ay The God|Breton story]], Arkay was originally a mortal who ascended to godhood. As the God of the Cycle of Birth and Death,{{ref|name=TDCP11}} he is charged with keeping the balance of death and life in the universe.{{ref|name=ATG}} In the Iliac Bay, the [[Lore:Order of Arkay|Order of Arkay]] celebrates the Blessed Neutrality that Arkay stands for. They do not offer blessings, for to give favor or disfavor upon one would upset the eternal balance. Their libraries and sages are some of the finest in Tamriel.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Order of Arkay|Order of Arkay]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Knights of the Circle|Knights of the Circle]] are the military arm of the Order.{{ref|name=KOTC|[[Daggerfall:Knights of the Circle|Knights of the Circle]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Arkay is the patron deity of the [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]] and [[Lore:Shalgora|Shalgora]].{{ref|Patron deities for [[Daggerfall:Shalgora|Shalgora]] and the [[Daggerfall:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Arkay is also the patron of [[Lore:House Tamrith|House Tamrith]], who are devout followers of the Light.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband H#House Tamrith Prayer Shawl|House Tamrith Prayer Shawl]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Contraband I#Inspirational Illuminations of the Light|Inspirational Illuminations of the Light]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The scions of House Tamrith study the Way of the Light and obey Arkay's teachings.{{ref|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} Their holy symbols bear the crest of House Tamrith and the symbol of the Light of Arkay.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband H#House Tamrith Holy Symbol|House Tamrith Holy Symbol]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Arkay's holy relics can be used to combat the undead.{{ref|name=ESOAlvaren|[[Online:Alvaren Garoutte|Alvaren Garoutte]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Archaic Relics|Archaic Relics]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====[[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]]====
The Goddess of Beauty is very popular. She has many different cults dedicated to different aspects of her worship: some are dedicated to women, some to aesthetics and art, and others to erotic instruction.{{ref|name=VOF}} In the late Third Era, the [[Lore:House of Dibella|House of Dibella]] was responsible for the administration of temples dedicated to Dibella in the province of High Rock, and its militant branch, the [[Lore:Order of the Lily|Order of the Lily]], was also active in the province at this time.{{ref|name=HOD|[[Daggerfall:House of Dibella|House of Dibella]] and the [[Daggerfall:Order of the Lily|Order of the Lily]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Dibella is the patron deity of the regions of [[Lore:Koegria|Koegria]] and [[Lore:Menevia (county)|Menevia]],{{ref|Patron deities for [[Daggerfall:Koegria|Koegria]] and [[Daggerfall:Menevia|Menevia]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and is venerated as a member of the Breton pantheon.{{Ref|name=VOF}}

====[[Lore:Jephre|Jephre]]====
Jephre (also known as Jeh Free){{Ref|name=KEII}} is the God of the Forest and the spirit of the "now". Jephre is typically considered an elven god, and is understood to have turned himself into the Earth Bones and established the laws of nature.{{ref|name=VOF}} He is still revered by some Breton hunters and farmers.{{ref|name=TIEGTT}} The Beldama Wyrd also worship him.{{ref|name=WCONHR|{{Cite Book|Witch Cults of Northern High Rock}}}} Bretons respect the Vicars of Jephre, a group of his adherents who are known defenders of the natural world.{{ref|[[Online:Priest of the Green|Priest of the Green]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The druids recognize Jephre as Y'ffre, the Green-King, the Slumbering Father of Nirn, who is their primary deity.{{ref|name=ONWD}}

====[[Lore:Julianos|Julianos]]====
The God of Wisdom and Logic is a favorite of Breton mages. He is the god of history, literature, law and contradiction.{{ref|name=VOF}} In the Iliac Bay, temples dedicated to him are known as [[Lore:School of Julianos|Schools of Julianos]].{{ref|[[Daggerfall:School of Julianos|School of Julianos]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} As their name implies, these temples are considered educational institutions.{{ref|name=TROZ|{{Cite Book|The Ransom of Zarek}}}} They are believed to have spread the term "magicka" to describe the "power associated with performing magic".{{ref|name=KE12|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part XII}}}} The templar knightly order associated with the School of Julianos is the [[Lore:Knights Mentor|Knights Mentor]].{{ref|name=KM|[[Daggerfall:Knights Mentor|Knights Mentor]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Julianos is the patron deity of the regions of [[Lore:Daenia (duchy)|Daenia]], Dwynnen and [[Lore:Tulune|Tulune]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Daenia|Daenia]], [[Daggerfall:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]] and [[Daggerfall:Tulune|Tulune]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

[[File:ON-misc-Kynareth.png|Systrean statue of Kynareth (ESO)|thumb|right]]
====[[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]]====
The Goddess of Air is a patron to sailors and travelers. Legend holds she was the first to agree to Lorkhan's idea to invent the mortal plane, and she provided a space for its creation. Kynareth is associated with rain.{{ref|name=VOF}} The island of Betony is considered holy land of Kynareth. This was a factor for Daggerfall's involvement in the War of Betony. It was also the reason why the conflict escalated to the [[Lore:Battle of Cryngaine Field|Battle of Cryngaine Field]], which ended the war.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|ns_base=BK}}}} The [[Lore:Temple of Kynareth|Temple of Kynareth]] does her work in the Iliac Bay. In the Third Era, her Temple contained the greatest physicians in Tamriel; their ability to cure magical and mundane diseases was said to be unrivaled.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Temple of Kynareth|Temple of Kynareth]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Kynaran Order|Kynaran Order]] is the militant arm of the Temple.{{ref|name=KynOr|[[Daggerfall:Kynaran Order|Kynaran Order]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Kynareth is the patron deity of [[Lore:Alcaire (fiefdom)|Alcaire]], Daggerfall and the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Alcaire|Alcaire]], [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall (Region)|Daggerfall]] and the [[Daggerfall:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The air spirits Amaro, Pina, and Tallatha, are acknowledged as [[Lore:The Fingers of Kynareth|the fingers of Kynareth]] by the Skeffington Coven.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Hearth Fire (v9)}}}}

====[[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]====
Magnus is the god of sorcery, who withdrew from creation at the last second. He is represented by a golden eye, an astrolabe or a telescope, but a staff is his most common symbol. Legend holds that he can possess powerful wizards and lend them power.{{ref|name=VOF}} Breton followers of Magnus sometimes use wicker effigies as stand-ins for themselves when performing certain rituals.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Ritual Objects#Magnus Cult Wicker Effigy|Magnus Cult Wicker Effigy]] item description and appearance as a common contraband item from Glenumbra in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

One known Breton sect that worshipped Magnus is the Priory of the Golden Staff. The Priory believed in Magnus's grand design for the Mundus, and his disappointment at the flaws introduced into his creation. They also believed that some of the Magna Ge once sought a tool to unmake what had been made wrong, in order that it could be remade in accordance with the Architect's plan.{{Ref|name=DCT|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}}

====[[Lore:Mara|Mara]]====
Mara is the Mother-Goddess, and is married to Akatosh in Breton lore.{{ref|name=VOF}} She is the patron deity of several regions of High Rock, such as [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]], Betony, [[Lore:Glenumbra Moors (barony)|Glenumbra Moors]], and [[Lore:Northmoor|Northmoor]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Anticlere|Anticlere]], [[Daggerfall:Betony|Betony]], [[Daggerfall:Glenumbra Moors|Glenumbra Moors]] and [[Daggerfall:Northmoor|Northmoor]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} In Anticlere, the 21st of Last Seed is known as [[Daggerfall:Holidays#Last Seed|Appreciation Day]], an ancient holiday of thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. It is considered a holy and contemplative day devoted to Mara, the goddess-protector of the region.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Appreciation Day|Appreciation Day]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Temples dedicated to the Divine are administered by the [[Lore:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]], a religious organization dedicated to Mara and her teachings, and can be found in major urban centers throughout the province.{{ref|name=DFBenevolence|[[Daggerfall:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Maran Knights|Maran Knights]] are a controversial knightly order dedicated to Mara and the protection of her temples.{{ref|name=MK|[[Daggerfall:Maran Knights|Maran Knights]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

====[[Lore:Phynaster|Phynaster]]====
Phynaster is an Altmer hero-god who is the patron deity and teacher of the Direnni. He is often worshipped by Breton mages who care about their elven blood.{{ref|name=VOF}}

====[[Lore:Sheor|Sheor]]====
Also known as the Bad Man, Sheor is the source of all strife. He originated as a god of crop failure, and is believed to be a demonized version of the Nordic Shor.{{ref|name=VOF}} Some sculptings created by the Bretons' Nedic ancestors have been found in [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]], which portray what appears to be their version of Sheor before he was villianized, paired with Mara as an Aldmer.{{ref|name=Manmer}}

[[File:ON-misc-Stendarr.jpg|thumb|right|Systrean statue of Stendarr (ESO)]]
====[[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]]====
Stendarr (also known as Shandar) is known as the God of Mercy, and is the patron of magistrates, rulers, and knights errant. He is a god of compassion and righteous rule.{{ref|name=VOF}} Temples dedicated to the Divine can be found throughout the Iliac Bay. Administered by the [[Lore:Temple of Stendarr|Temple of Stendarr]], a religious organization dedicated to Stendarr and his teachings, the temples offer healing and training to the population of the Iliac Bay. Some mock their compassion, calling them sentimental fools and tenderhearts; but everyone depends on Stendarr when Sai, the God of Luck, abandons them.{{ref|name=DFStendarr|[[Daggerfall:Temple of Stendarr|Temple of Stendarr]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Crusaders|Crusaders]], also known as the Knights of Stendarr, are a knightly order dedicated to him which protect his temples against its adversaries, such as the [[Lore:Citadel of Ebonarm|Citadel of Ebonarm]].{{ref|name=DFCrusaders|[[Daggerfall:Crusaders|Crusaders]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Stendarr is the patron deity of [[Lore:Bhoriane|Bhoriane]], Phrygias and [[Lore:Urvaius|Urvaius]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Urvaius|Urvaius]], [[Daggerfall:Bhoriane|Bhoriane]] and [[Daggerfall:Phrygias|Phrygias]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

====[[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]]====
Zenithar (often shortened to Z'en) is the God of Work and Commerce, a cultivated god of merchants, artisans, and middle nobility. His faithful tout him as a god "that will always win".{{ref|name=VOF}} He teaches Bretonkind that they will be rewarded if they work hard. He also teaches them to be wise in their spending, and to never steal from others. Many of his rewards are those of the spirit: being productive, whether farming, smithing, or creating, honors Zenithar. Zenithar wishes for his faithful to find joy in their work.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mysteries of the Divines}}}} In the Iliac Bay, the [[Lore:Resolution of Zenithar|Resolution of Zenithar]] pays heed to the god.{{ref|name=ResoZen|[[Daggerfall:Resolution of Zenithar|Resolution of Zenithar]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Zenithar is not merely the God of Merchants and Commerce; he is also a warrior god. The militant arm of the Resolution, the [[Lore:Knights of Iron|Knights of Iron]], represent this part of him.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Knights of Iron|Knights of Iron]] faction information in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Zenithar is the patron deity of [[Lore:Gavaudon (fiefdom)|Gavaudon]], Kambria and [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Gavaudon|Gavaudon]], [[Daggerfall:Kambria|Kambria]] and [[Daggerfall:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

====[[Lore:Q'Olwen|Q'Olwen]]====
Little is known about this deity, except that it is associated with those in pursuit of lore.{{Ref|name=DFpreview|group=UOL}} Q'Olwen is worshipped in the Iliac Bay area, where shrines to him can be found.{{Ref|name=DFShrines}}

====[[Lore:Raen|Raen]]====
Raen is the Breton God of Agriculture.{{Ref|name=DFpreview|group=UOL}} Reliquaries of Raen can be found across the Iliac Bay region.{{Ref|name=DFShrines}}

====[[Lore:Reymon Ebonarm|Reymon Ebonarm]]====
Reymon Ebonarm is the God of War, a deity of some significance to the Bretons.{{Ref|name=ILTEA|{{TIL|The Ebon Arm — The Imperial Library|ebon-arm}}}} He is featured in the Bretonic historical novel ''King Edward''. He has shrines in the Alcaire, Daenia, Daggerfall, Dwynnen, Ilessan Hills, Kambria, Wayrest, and the Wrothgarian Mountain regions of High Rock.{{Ref|name=DFShrines}}

====[[Lore:Sai|Sai]]====
Sai is the God of Luck. He was born a mortal and had the talent to spread luck to others, but not to himself. Legend says he was a soldier, struck down in his first battle just as it was won. [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]] appeared to him, and offered him immortality if he agreed to spread his luck around. He said the gods were overworked, and thought Sai's inborn talent would balance things out. He readily agreed, and he was told that he could keep his body for a time before it began to fade.{{ref|name=PIX|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part IX}}}} Excessive worship of Sai causes the disease known as [[Lore:Disease#Sai's Affliction|Sai's Affliction]]. Sufferers of the disease have been abandoned by Sai, and long for the god's presence. They are driven to incessant gambling, seeking proof of the god's favor. This usually leaves the victim in poverty or debt.{{ref|name=PIX}} Followers of Sai sometimes convert to worshipping [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]] when Sai abandons them.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Temple of Stendarr|Follower of Stendarr]] dialogue in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Reliquaries dedicated to Sai can be found throughout the Iliac Bay region.{{Ref|name=DFShrines}}

====[[Lore:Sethiete|Sethiete]]====
Sethiete (also called Seth){{Ref|name=ARGideon|[[Arena:Gideon|Gideon]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} is a deity that is worshipped across Tamriel. The nature of Sethiete is unknown, but what is known is his influence. His places of worship are known as the [[Lore:Temple of Sethiete|Temples of Sethiete]], and one of his temples is a landmark in the city of Camlorn that anchors the main street with the Royal Palace.{{Ref|name=MOT1|{{Cite Book|Mystery of Talara, v 1}}}}

====[[Lore:Vigryl|Vigryl]]====

==Notes==
*Bretons traditionally fulfill the role of being the European [[wikipedia:Middle Ages|medieval]] race of The Elder Scrolls universe.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOEGT|[https://gamingtrend.com/feature/interviews/exclusive-gaming-trend-interview-with-the-elder-scrolls-online-art-director-cj-grebb/ Exclusive Gaming Trend interview with The Elder Scrolls Online Art Director CJ Grebb]}}
*Bretons pull from many influences, such as from the real-world [[wikipedia:Bretons|Bretons]], the [[wikipedia:Celts|Celtic]],{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOCGM|[https://www.cgmagonline.com/articles/editorals/8-year-elder-scrolls-online/ After Eight Years, Bretons Finally Take Centre Stage in The Elder Scrolls Online]}} and the [[wikipedia:French|French]].{{Ref|name=DGI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Douglas Goodall Interview}}}}
**Breton society is heavily influenced by [[wikipedia:Arthurian literature|Arthurian legend]].{{Ref|name=ON2022|group=UOL}} The influence of [[wikipedia:Game of Thrones|Thronesian]] politics has also played a minor role.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLI}}
**The largest real-world influence on the feudal Bretons of High Isle in the Systres Archipelago was the [[wikipedia:Ancient Rome|ancient Roman]] city of [[wikipedia:Baiae|Baiae]], known for its hedonistic practices and having local volcanic activity.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOCGM}} To differentiate them from other Bretons, Systreans draw from the [[wikipedia:Germanic peoples|Germanic]] medieval period for their castles, armor, and motifs.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ESOEGT}}
**The architecture of the Druid subculture takes inspiration from the [[wikipedia:Moai|Moai]], [[wikipedia:Olmecs|Olmecs]], and the [[wikipedia:Stonehenge|Stonehenge]]. Influences of their [[wikipedia:Rock-cut architecture|Rock-cut architecture]] came from ancient [[wikipedia:Turkey|Turkey]], such as the [[wikipedia:Lycia|Lycian tombs in Dalyan]] and [[wikipedia:Göreme Historical National Park|Göreme Historical National Park]].{{ref|name=FDTZ|[[General:Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone|Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone]] on the official {{g|on}} website}}
*{{LNote|Systres|^|The Morrowind Codex map denotes provinces by color, and has the Systres Archipelago with the same color as Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=MCMap|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Tamriel]] – [[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex|Morrowind Codex]]}}}}
* {{LNote|Reachmen|^|Although they are thought to share descent, Bretons do not consider themselves kin to the Reachmen, and the Reachmen do not see themselves as Bretons.{{Ref|name=LoreAnswers|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Lore|Ask Us Anything: Lore]]}}{{ref|name=ABAYQ|{{Cite Book|Arthenice Belloq Answers Your Questions}}}} This sentiment is shared by other races.{{Ref|name=ESOContrabandOEEC|[[Online:Contraband O#Orsimer Enemies Ear Collection|Orsimer Enemies Ear Collection]] contraband in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}}}
*{{LNote|Hill Giants|^|Rivenspire in ESO was [[Online:Cut_Content/Rivenspire#The Hillmen of Rivenspire|originally planned]] to include the spirits of these race of giants that were called "hill giants". The region would have also had Bretons with hill giant heritage called hillmen that were larger than the average person, similar to the [[Lore:Half-Giant|half-giant]] race. These hillmen would have also had some Nordic heritage. Only a loading screen hinting at these hill giant's existence remains in the final game, but the term hillmen still appears as a demonym for Rivenspire's people in the book ''[[Lore:The Fury of King Ranser|The Fury of King Ranser]]'' in reference to [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]].}}
*{{LNote|Chimer|^|The time period of the book ''[[Lore:King Edward|King Edward]]'' takes place after the founding of Wayrest, which is after the Chimer became the Dunmer. ''[[Lore:The Real Barenziah|The Real Barenziah]]'' places Edward and Moraelyn banishing the Nords long before this, when the Dunmer were still the Chimer.}}
*{{LNote|Desynan|^|The book ''[[Lore:On the War of Righteousness|On the War of Righteousness]]'' states that the Alessian Order refrained from sending a military response to High Rock until after the marriage between the children of King Desynan of Skingrad and Queen Margert Gardner of Wayrest in {{Year|1E 2319}}.}}
*{{LNote|Architecture|^|In the Systres Archipelago, druidic architecture is intended to invoke the feeling of a culture that has embraced nature yet is not primitive.{{ref|name=FDTZ|[[General:Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone|Firesong Developer Preview—The Zone]] on the official {{g|on}} website}}}}
*{{LNote|Pale|^|House Spenard ruled for 180 years before being dissolved by House Dorell, a house that did not emerge until the ninth century of the First Era.{{Ref|name=ONRKT|[[Online:Rivenspire Antiquities#Riven King's Throne|Riven King's Throne]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}{{Ref|name=NAA}}}}
*In ''[[Lore:A_History_of_Daggerfall|A History of Daggerfall]]'', the Direnni were originally described as native Bretons. Although they are described to be predominantly Altmer in later content, they still have some [[Lore:Chimere Graegyn|humans]] in their ranks.
* Bretons have a higher tolerance to [[Lore:Alcoholic Beverages|alcohol]] but take longer to recover.{{Ref|name=ARAlcoholic|[[Arena:Services#Alcohol_Consumption|Alcohol stats]] for [[Arena:Breton|Breton]]s in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

So I even had screen capture somewhere of myself [as] my character the game, it was the Bretons." Jonah Lobe asks, "So basically the Bretons are Cubans?" to which Dennis responded, "Basically yeah, in my mind [laughs]!"{{ref|name=JonahLobe|group=UOL|1=''[https://youtu.be/WhS31EVS3_4?t=2207 You're Finally Awake: Nine Developers Recount the Making of Skyrim]''}}

He also voices the Ghost of King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]], which would mean the Bretons, at least those of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] possessed the real world Danish accent of LeFay.
