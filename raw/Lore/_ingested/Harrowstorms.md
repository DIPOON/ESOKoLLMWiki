# Lore:Harrowstorms

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Trail|Magic}}[[File:ON-concept-Harrowstorms.jpg|thumb|right|A Harrowstorm with Ritual Pikes]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Harrowstorms|Harrowstorms]] are arcane weather events created with a combination of the magic of the [[Lore:Icereach Coven|Icereach Coven]] and ancient [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]] that appeared without too much warning, leaving death and destruction behind. The energies created by those storms were capable of instantly killing many people or transforming them into near-mindless shells known as [[Lore:Harrowed|harrowed]], although others become [[Lore:Bloodfiend|feral vampires]] known as harrowfiends, but some who were in the vicinity of the storms were not always affected.{{ref|name=ON|{{Cite Book|On Harrowstorms}}}} The use of the ritual fetishes and alchemy needed to summon harrowstorms is known as '''harrowcraft'''.{{ref|[[ON:Tayfaern|Tayfaern]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Historical Use==
[[File:ON-quest-Danger in the Holds 07.jpg|thumb|right|Karthwatch in the aftermath of a Harrowstorm]]
Around the year {{Year|2E 582}}, harrowstorms were used several times by the Icereach Coven and the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]] in order to further their plans in the regions of Western [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], parts of [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]] and [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]].{{ref|name=ESOGreymoor|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOMarkarth|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

In Western Skyrim, random harrowstorms were often summoned in order to allow the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s to fret over them while the Host prepared more powerful versions.{{ref|name=GHC|{{Cite Book|Gray Host Communique|ns_base=ON}}}} The Swords of Solitude, one militia of [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], often recruited groups of volunteers to deal with those storms that occurred all over the region.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Halt the Harrowstorms (Greymoor)|Halt the Harrowstorms (Greymoor)]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

Near the city of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], a storm was summoned against the temple of [[Lore:Kilkreath Temple|Kilkreath]], a temple dedicated to the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], with the storm being met with unexpected resistance, as the enemies of the Host noticed the movements and sought to reveal their plans.{{ref|name=PO|{{Cite Book|Pentarch's Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} Despite the resistance, the storm was still devastating, killing many of the priests and affecting the interior of the temple as well.{{ref|name=ESOBavian|[[Online: Priest Bavian| Priest Bavian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Efforts were later made to stop the remaining corruption, but a legacy of the harrowstorm was the abandonment of the temple, which was still found in ruins about a thousand years later in {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Meridia's Brilliance|Meridia's Brilliance]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOBavian}}{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

A failed attempt at summoning a harrowstorm was made near the underground city of [[Lore:Dusktown|Dusktown]], as [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] and their allies were able to destroy the ritual pikes involved in the ritual. Despite the success, energies collected by the harrowstorms were still capable of resurrecting Exarch Ulfra of the Gray Host, a powerful [[Lore:Werewolf|werewolf]] behemoth.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Dark Clouds Over Solitude|Dark Clouds Over Solitude]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

Another attempt of using a harrowstorm happened near the town of [[Lore:Morthal|Morthal]], which caught several soldiers of the city, but the witch pike that supported the storm was destroyed soon after and the location was spared. Meanwhile, the hold capital of [[Lore:Karthwatch|Karthwatch]] was not so lucky, with the city being devastated by another storm, transforming many of the locals and even the jarl into harrowfiends. The storm, combined with other factors, allowed for Karthwatch and the rest of the [[Lore:Karthald|Karthald]] hold to eventually cease existing as its people were either dead or scattered.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Danger in the Holds|Danger in the Holds]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

After the death of Exarch Tzinghalis, the Gray Host was preparing a test of a "final harrowstorm" in the Undergrove, a place located within an ancient barrow.{{ref|name=GHO|{{Cite Book|Gray Host Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} The plans for this massive storm were discovered, with several defenders of Skyrim beginning preparations for an elixir that would be able to hold the effects of the arcane phenomena, with this elixir being tested on the Undergrove and proving successful, with the Undergrove's storm being destroyed right after.{{ref|Events of [[Online:The Gray Host|The Gray Host]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-quest-Greymoor Rising 08.jpg|thumb|right|A massive Harrowstorm in Solitude]]

Eventually, this final harrowstorm was completed and unleashed from within [[Lore:Greymoor Keep|Greymoor Keep]], rising to affect the capital city of Solitude, with the Host using it to resurrect the last of their numbers. Many exposed to the storm had their life energies taken by it, but efforts of the defenders of the city among others were able to stop the storm and kill High King [[Lore:Svargrim|Svargrim]], who had been merged with Greymoor Keep and thus his end meant the end of the harrowstorm as well.{{ref|Events of [[Online: Greymoor Rising|Greymoor Rising]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

In the aftermath, the elixir against the harrowstorms were distributed to the holds and there were hopes of producing a cure for the harrowed, but not for the harrowfiends who had more of their life energies removed from them.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Daughter of the Wolf|Daughter of the Wolf]] in [[Online:Greymoor|ESO]]}}

Later during their campaign in the Reach, similar random harrowstorms appeared in several areas of the region, with the city of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] having an agent that recruited outsiders to deal with them.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Halt the Harrowstorms (Markarth)|Halt the Harrowstorms (Markarth)]] in [[Online:Markarth|ESO]]}} Storms were frequent enough in the region to the point that the agent of Markarth pointed out that new ones would likely appear in the next day.{{ref|name=ESONelldena|[[Online:Nelldena|Nelldena]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

==Ingredients==
One of the ingredients for the harrowstorms is a plant known as [[Lore:Netherroot|netherroot]] that only grows in isolated patches in the depths of Blackreach, the Gray Host studied the plants in order to improve the concoction for the harrowstorms even further to create more effective and powerful results. Eventually, the effects of those studies allowed for a more complete exchange of energy and better versions by the day.{{ref|name=NN|{{Cite Book|Netherroot Notes|ns_base=ON}}}}

The Icereach Coven did not use a single variation of netherroot, as a variety that grows in large quantities beneath the village of [[Lore:Karthwasten|Karthwasten]] was also sought, this different form required another component but still served the Coven's purposes quite well. The root itself grew not only in larger numbers, but also faster and would be able to create more powerful harrowstorms if used.{{ref|name=OFR|{{Cite Book|Orders from R|ns_base=ON}}}}

Ritual pikes were used to keep the storms active, the Icereach Coven was eventually able to prepare the storms with a single pike after enough development, this smaller version did not provide large storms but still harvested the life force the Coven wanted.{{ref|name=SEP|{{Cite Book|Sister Elra's Proclamation|ns_base=ON}}}}

Another powerful reagent necessary was a gray reliquary.{{ref|name=LFSG|{{Cite Book| Letter from Sister Glynolde|ns_base=ON}}}} Those ancient ominous stone vessels contained the ancient charred remains of the members of the Gray Host and were considered a haunting addition to even the darkest crypts when not used for the storms.{{ref|[[Online:Undercroft Furnishings/Grave Goods|Gray Reliquary]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Effects==
The effects of the harrowstorms were the extraction of the life energies of the living within their area of influence, killing or transforming the ones they touched. The deaths and the transformation into bloodfiends known as harrowfiends were considered to be a side-effect, being observed in many moments, even when the Icereach Coven was still improving the harrowstorms to be larger and affect populated areas.{{ref|name=ICN|{{Cite Book|Icereach Coven Note |ns_base=ON}}}}

By design, [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropes]] were immune to the effects of the harrowstorms, but studies were being made by using netherroot and blood distillate of werewolves in order to weaken them. There were still questions about whether or not the [[Lore:Souls|soul]] would be able to be exchanged when it succumbed to the ritual though.{{ref|name=LI|{{Cite Book|Lycanthropic Immunity}}}}
