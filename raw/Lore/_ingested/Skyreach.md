[[Lore:Skyreach|Skyreach]] was a [[Lore:Nede|Duraki Nede]] City of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] hidden high into the mountains of [[Lore:Craglorn|Upper Craglorn]], in the [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] portion of the [[Lore:Western Reach|Western Reach]]{{ref|name=VirWeb|[[General:Vir2l.com Archive#The Elder Scrolls Travels Shadowkey|The Elder Scrolls Travels Shadowkey]] on the ''vir2l.com'' website}}. It was one of last remaining bastion of the Nedes of the region during the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]] invasion.{{Ref|name=Explorer|{{Cite Book|Skyreach Explorer}}}}
{{NewLeft}}

==Layout and Geography==
Skyreach is located high in the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]], east of [[Lore:Dragonstar|Dragonstar]] close to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]'s border. It is composed of high towers built on the top of the mountain, halls carved deep in the rock and open courtyards.

The highest tower is called the [[Online: Skyreach Pinnacle|Pinnacle]] and houses a great laboratory, where [[Lore:Nirncrux|nirncrux]] was used to harness the power of the stars and create weak [[Lore:Celestial|Celestials]] that mimicked mundane animals and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. {{ref|name=Crux|{{Cite Book|Nirncrux: A Study}}}}

[[File:ON-interior-Skyreach Catacombs.jpg|thumb|right|The Catacombs of Skyreach]]
The ancient Nedes buried the dead of Skyreach, both commoners and nobles alike, within massive chambers and passages carved in the rock. They created a large network of [[Online:Skyreach Catacombs|catacombs]] beneath the city.

Several temples to the [[Lore:Celestial|Celestials]] where built by the ancient Nedes outside the city, including the temple of the [[Lore:Loth'Na Caverns|Loth'Na Caverns]] and the [[Lore:Sanctum Ophidia|Sanctum Ophidia]] dedicated to the [[Lore:Serpent|Serpent]].

==History==
===Foundation and early History===
[[File:ON-load-Skyreach Pinnacle.jpg|thumb|left|Aetherion in the laboratory of Skyreach Pinnacle]]
Skyreach was founded by the [[Lore:Duraki|Duraki]], a [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] tribe. The Duraki were among the Nedic peoples conquered by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] in the Merethic and early First Era. Several Dwemer cities, such as [[Lore:Mtharnaz|Mtharnaz]], utilized Nedic slavery.{{Ref|name=Erold|[[ON:Erold|Erold]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} It is during their time of servitude that they acquired from the Dwemer the building techniques they later used for themselves to build the [[Lore:Apex Towers|Apex Towers]] of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] and ultimately the city of Skyreach.

It is theorized that Skyreach was originally built as a private estate for the Duraki's High Kings. By the time of Durac's reign, it had become a city that housed some of the Duraki populace.{{ref|name=Explorer|}}{{ref|name=Sara|}}

The [[Lore:Cult of the Stars|Cult of the Stars]] experimented to create Celestial beings in Skyreach. The kings of Skyreach constructed a great laboratory at Skyreach Pinnacle, where they used [[Lore:Nirncrux|nirncrux]] to harness the power of the stars. At first, they created only weak Celestials that mimicked mundane animals and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. These ranged in power from the lowly Celestial Spiders to the fearsome entity known as [[Lore:Aetherion|Aetherion]]. All of these volatile creatures were bound within the Pinnacle by Nedic wards.

===Fall of the City during the Yokudan Invasion===
[[File:ON-npc-High King Durac 02.jpg|thumb|left|High King Durac in his undead form in the 2nd Era]][[File:ON-creature-Virmaril the Betrayer.jpg|thumb|right|Virmaril in his lich form]]

Some time later, [[Lore:Durac|Durac]], possibly the namesake of the Duraki,{{intnote|DuracName|[nb 2]}} was known as the High King of Craglorn and the Duraki people.{{ref|name=Explorer}}{{Ref|name=Sara|{{Cite Book|Saradin's Diary}}}} He and the royal family, including his daughter, [[Lore:Saradin|Saradin]], resided in the massive complex of Skyreach.  Durac maintained a council with eight other Nedic kings that swore to him, including those of Blood, Flame, Forest, Frost, Shadow, Spirit, Storm, and Sun.{{ref|name=Explorer|}} Additionally, he had the counsel of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] [[Lore:Virmaril|Virmaril]], a close friend of his who advised him for years.{{Ref|name=Fight|Events of [[ON:The Reason We Fight|The Reason We Fight]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Unbeknownst to High King Durac, Virmaril and Princess Saradin had garnered romantic interest for each other. Upon Virmaril asking Durac for Saradin's hand in marriage, the High King was furious. He said that Saradin could not possibly marry an elf, and instead arranged for her to marry King [[Lore:Kestic|Kestic]] of the northern forest tribes.{{ref|name=Sara}} Though Saradin was hurt, she took it in stride and accepted her duty as princess.{{ref|name=Sara}} In contrast, Virmaril went into a rage, feeling betrayed by Durac, whom he had faithfully advised for years.
{{Ref|name=Virm|{{Cite Book|Virmaril's Journal}}}} Though he tried to escape Durac's ruling by running away with Saradin, he was rejected, and became cold and distant.{{ref|name=Sara}}

Following the third Yokudan warrior wave of {{Year|1E 808}}, a following known as the [[Lore:Anka-Ra|Anka-Ra]] arose within the ranks of the invaders. Led by self-proclaimed emperor [[Lore:Tarish-Zi|Tarish-Zi]], the wave cut through southern Hammerfell and approached Craglorn.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Warlords of the Ra Gada}}}} The Nedic kings convened at Skyreach at the behest of High King Durac to hear out Advisor Virmaril's ritualistc plan to repel the invasion. Though they believed that Virmaril's incantation would beat back the Yokudan army, he instead turned it on the kings in bloody vengeance. He subjugated the council of kings, Durac among them, becoming a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] and raising the dead of Skyreach.{{ref|name=Virm}}{{ref|name=Shav|{{Cite Book|Watcher Shavmar's Journal}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Virmaril the Betrayer|Virmaril the Betrayer]] character in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Virmaril's betrayal did not go unnoticed by the Duraki, as soon after his regicide an extermination force was sent into the catacombs to destroy him. Virmaril soundly defeated the team, benefited by his undead state,{{ref|name=Shav}} and was furious that the Nedes dared to attack him.{{ref|name=Virm}} One of the members of the force, Shavmar of the Perena tribe,{{ref|name=WatcherRitualStole|[[Online:Craglorn Antiquities|Watcher's Ritual Stole antiquity]] codex entry by [[ON:Verita Numida|Verita Numida]] in [[ON:Greymoor|ESO]]}} managed to escape the catacombs. He gathered two other Perena [[Lore:Soul Magic|soul mages]], Garalan and Armok, and entered the catacombs once more in an attempt to bind the Betrayer where killing him failed.{{ref|name=WatcherRitualStole}}{{ref|name=Shav}} The trio were successful in weakening Virmaril, pushing back to the deep chambers of the catacombs and sealing him behind a soul ward and placing him in a sleep-like state.{{ref|name=Shav}}{{ref|name=Virm}} 

Despite the defeat of Virmaril, the Duraki, and their seat of power, Skyreach, met their end at the hands of the Yokudan armada.{{ref|name=Deathlands|{{Cite Book|Nedes of the Deathlands}}}}{{ref|name=Crusader|[[ESO:Crusader Dalamar|Crusader Dalamar]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Among the defensive tactics used to combat the Yokudans was the release of the previously sealed Celestials, beings imbued with magical energy.{{ref|name=Explorer|}}{{ref|name=Vyger|[[ESO:Mederic Vyger|Mederic Vyger]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The most fearsome of the beasts, [[Lore:Aetherion|Aetherion]], was a [[Lore:Daedric Titan|great winged terror]] with near immortality.{{ref|name=Vyger}}{{ref|name=ESOAe|[[ON:Aetherion|Aetherion]]'s appearance in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Yokudans contained the released creatures by restoring the old Nedic wards in the uppermost tower of Skyreach.{{ref|name=Vyger}}{{ref|[[ESO:Skyreach Pinnacle|Skyreach Pinnacle]]'s loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Activity in the ruins during the Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 582}}, the [[ON:Scaled Court|Scaled Court]] have taken control of the ruins in search of [[ON:Nirncrux|nirncrux]].
They excavated the catacombs in an attempt to control the powerful undead within, but were slain after awakening a lich named [[ON:Virmaril the Betrayer|Virmaril the Betrayer]]. According to ''[[ON:Skyreach Explorer, Volume Four|Skyreach Explorer, Volume Four]]'', [[ON:Reginus Buca|Reginus Buca]] and [[ON:Verita Numida|Verita Numida]] of the [[ON:Antiquarian Circle|Antiquarian Circle]] are somewhat responsible for allowing Virmaril the Betrayer to awaken.
The court also accidentally released Celestial creatures from the magical wards the Nedes set up long ago in Skyreach's Pinnacle.
