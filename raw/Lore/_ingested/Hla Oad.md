[[Lore:Hla Oad|Hla Oad]] was a tiny fishing village at the southern end of the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] region,{{Ref|name=Hla Oad|[[MW:Hla Oad|Hla Oad]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=GDH}} built along [[Lore:Odai River|Odai River]] southwest of [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]] on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]].{{Ref|name=GDH|[[MW:Generic Dialogue H#Hla Oad|Hla Oad]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=GTB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Balmora}}}}{{Ref|name=GDO|[[MW:Generic Dialogue O#Odai|Odai]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}
==History==
[[File:ON-place-Sarandas' House.jpg|thumb|right|Sarandas family's house]]
===Second Era===
Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, Hla Oad was not yet a town, though the humble fishing shack of Sarandas' family stood at the site of the future settlement.{{Ref|name=TSS|[[ON:Turseth Sarandas|Turseth Sarandas]], and [[ON:Broder Sarandas|Broder Sarandas]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Third Era===
[[File:MW-book-Balmora Region.jpg|thumb|left|A map depicting Hla Oad]]
Circa {{Year|3E 427}}, it was an active settlement and it offered few services.{{ref|name=Hla Oad}}{{Ref|name=GDS|[[MW:Generic Dialogue S#Services|Services in Hla Oad]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] criminal organization wielded considerable influence within the settlement.{{ref|name=GDS}}{{ref|name=GDSO}} Among the few services available were independent operators such as Perien Aurelie the pawnbroker and Pallia Ceno, as well as [[Lore:Cammona Tong|Camonna Tong]] affiliated businesses that offered their services only to their own people.{{ref|name=GDS}} At the time, Fatleg's Drop-Off was the only notable tradehouse in the settlement, and it was owned by the Redguard Trasteve.{{Ref|name=GDSO|[[MW:Generic Dialogue S#someone in particular|someone in particular in Hla Oad]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Along with other settlements of the Bitter Coast, also known as the Smugglers Coast, the region was defined by its secluded coves and small islands that offered ideal refuge for illicit trade, and it was widely rumored to be a major stopover for smugglers,{{Ref|name=SI|{{Cite Book|Smuggler's Island}}}}{{Ref|name=GDB|[[MW:Generic Dialogue B#Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=SI|{{Cite Book|Smuggler's Island}}}} who were known to occasionally sell goods in the settlement.{{Ref|name=GDG|[[MW:Generic Dialogue G#Grytewake|Grytewake]] topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Some of these smuggling operations were particularly alarming, including those involving [[Lore:Ash Statues|Ash Statues]] and activities that furthered the goals of the [[Lore:House Dagoth|Sixth House]].{{Ref|name=CC|[[MW:Crassius Curio|Crassius Curio]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=VTO|[[MW:Velfred the Outlaw|Velfred the Outlaw]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=VTOQ|[[MW:Velfred the Outlaw (quest)|Velfred the Outlaw]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

It was a fairly isolated settlement, though a rough track along the Odai River connected it to Balmora. Reaching [[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]] required a long and unpleasant walk south along the coast.{{Ref|name=GDH}} Ships from both the Foreign Quarter docks in [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] and from [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] regularly traveled to Hla Oad.{{Ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vivec}}}} The local shipmaster, Baleni Salavel, arranged travel from the settlement to other destinations.{{Ref|name=GDSO}} Ships departing from Hla Oad regularly sailed to [[Lore:Gnaar Mok|Gnaar Mok]] and Ebonheart.{{Ref|name=GDH}}{{Ref|name=GDSO}}

That same year, members of the Camonna Tong sought to smuggle [[Lore:Moon Sugar|moon sugar]] to Balmora using a [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] slave who was forced to swallow the ilicit goods for the time of transportation. They asked a traveler to deliver her there. It is unknown whether their scheme succeeded and the moon sugar was delivered to its destination, or whether the traveler instead helped the woman escape from captivity, taking her to the Argonian Mission and placing her under the care of [[Lore:Im-Kilaya|Im-Kilaya]] of the [[Lore:Twin Lamps|Twin Lamps]].{{Ref|name=RIB|[[MW:Rabinna's Inner Beauty|Rabinna's Inner Beauty]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Like much of Vvardenfell, its fate is unclear, as it may have been destroyed during the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]].{{ref|name=OTGC|{{Cite Book|On the Great Collapse}}}}<noinclude>
