The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]], also referred to simply as the '''Champion'''{{ref|[[OBMobile:Narrative|Player character's name]] in dialogue in ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}} among various [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch#Fame and Infamy|other titles]], was a [[Lore:Hero|doom-driven prisoner]] who played an essential part in ending the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] that ravaged [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of {{g|oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=TOC|{{Cite Book|The Oblivion Crisis}}}} That same year they would defend the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] from the invading [[Lore:Forces of Order|Forces of Order]] and find themselves [[Lore:Mantling|ascending]] to [[Lore:Gods|godhood]] as the new [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric '''Prince''']] '''of [[Lore:Sheogorath|Madness]]'''.{{ref|name=Shivering Isles|Events of {{g|shivering isles|oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on [https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2010/12/26/decrypting-the-elder-scrolls.aspx Decrypting The Elder Scrolls] [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}}<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Background===
Very little is known about the hero's personal history; they are a long-lost relative to both the infamous [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] [[Lore:Greywyn Blenwyth|Greywyn Blenwyth]],{{ref|name=Vile|[[Oblivion:Vile Lair#Background|Vile Lair ''obliviondownloads.com'' description]]}}{{ref|name=Greywyn|[[ESO:Greywyn Blenwyth|Greywyn Blenwyth]] in [[ESO:Blackwood (Chapter)|ESO: ''Blackwood'']]}} and former Arch-Mage of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] [[Lore:Taris Rendil|Taris Rendil]].{{ref|name=Frostcrag|{{Cite Book|Frostcrag Spire Memoirs}}}} Before his assassination, [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|Emperor]] [[Lore:Uriel VII|Uriel Septim VII]], who possessed [[Lore:Sight|the Sight]], foresaw the hero's presence as an omen of his death, leading into the Oblivion Crisis.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}

===The Oblivion Crisis===
[[File:OB-misc-Anniversary Art.jpg|thumb|right|Escaping the Imperial City Prison]]
On the 27th of Last Seed, 3E 433, the hero awoke to find themselves locked up in [[Lore:Imperial City Prison|Imperial City Prison]] for unknown reasons. As [[Lore:Fate|fate]] would have it, Emperor Uriel Septim VII and a contingent of [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] were attempting to flee from the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] after his heirs were murdered, and their secret escape route lead through the hero's cell. The emperor recognized the hero from his dreams of the [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|Gates of Oblivion]] and allowed them to follow the group to freedom. After repelling many attacks from would-be assassins, sensing his imminent demise, the emperor tasked the hero with seeking [[Lore:Jauffre|Brother Jauffre]] at [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]], and delivering to him the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], because Jauffre alone knew of Uriel VII's [[Lore:Martin Septim|secret son]], forewarning them of the [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Daedric Prince of Destruction]]. Cornered and with his Blades occupied defending their ruler from another attack, the emperor bid a final farewell to the hero, before finally being killed from behind.{{ref|name=TQ|Events of [[Oblivion:Tutorial|Tutorial]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
[[File:OB-misc-Anniversary Art22.jpg|thumb|left|Breaking the Siege of Kvatch]]
The hero journeyed to [[Lore:Weynon Priory|Weynon Priory]] and delivered the Amulet to Jauffre, grandmaster of the Blades.{{ref|name=DTA|Events of [[Oblivion:Deliver the Amulet|Deliver the Amulet]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} After the hero relayed to Jauffre what happened, the grandmaster realized the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] threat and tasked the hero to travel to [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]] and find Uriel VII's illegitimate son, [[Lore:Martin Septim|Brother Martin]], who was living as a priest in the Chapel of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]].{{ref|name=FTH|Events of [[Oblivion:Find the Heir|Find the Heir]] in Oblivion}} When the hero arrived in Kvatch, they found the city under siege by Daedra, pouring out of a large Oblivion Gate. The hero helped the town's remaining guard close it and rescue the surviving citizens taking refuge in the ruins of the city's chapel,{{ref|name=BTSK|Events of [[Oblivion:Breaking the Siege of Kvatch|Breaking the Siege of Kvatch]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} as well as fight their way through Castle Kvatch to attempt to save the [[Lore:Ormellius Goldwine|count]].{{ref|name=BCK|Events of [[Oblivion:The Battle for Castle Kvatch|The Battle for Castle Kvatch]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} For their efforts in aiding the city, the hero became known as the '''Hero of Kvatch'''. Once the Daedric forces were defeated in the city, the hero found Brother Martin in the chapel, and escorted him back to Jauffre.{{ref|name=BTSK|Events of [[Oblivion:Breaking the Siege of Kvatch|Breaking the Siege of Kvatch]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Arriving at Weynon Priory, they helped fend off an attack by the same people that assassinated Martin's father and half-siblings, the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] cult. The hero protected Jauffre from the assassins but discovered that the Amulet of Kings was stolen in the chaos.{{ref|name=WP|Events of [[Oblivion:Weynon Priory (quest)|Weynon Priory]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Jauffre and the hero escorted Martin to [[Lore:Cloud Ruler Temple|Cloud Ruler Temple]] to be guarded by the Blades, where they recognized him as the [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] Emperor Martin Septim. At the Blades sanctuary, Jauffre tasked the Hero of Kvatch with finding more information on the Mythic Dawn cult, locating their headquarters, and retrieving the Amulet of Kings so Martin Septim can relight the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] and end the crisis. In the Imperial City, the hero worked alongside Blades member [[Lore:Baurus|Baurus]] and [[Lore:Tar-Meena|Tar-Meena]] of the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]] to decipher the Mythic Dawn's [[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes'']], a series of books written by the cult's leader, [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], discovering a clue that lead to the Tomb of Prince [[Lore:Camarril|Camarril]] in [[Lore:Green Emperor Way|Green Emperor Way]]. The tomb itself, when cast at by the midday sun, revealed an engraved map pinpointing the headquarters of the Mythic Dawn at [[Lore:Lake Arrius|Lake Arrius]].{{ref|name=TPOD|Events of [[Oblivion:The Path of Dawn|The Path of Dawn]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The Hero of Kvatch followed the location from the runic map to Lake Arrius Caverns and entered the Mythic Dawn headquarters. The hero came across the Warden of the Shrine of Dagon, [[Lore:Harrow|Harrow]]. The warden asked the hero to hand over their belongings and don the robes of the Mythic Dawn. Reports differ on if the hero complied or chose to fight their way through the cult. Some reports say the hero cooperated with the cult, and even performed a ritual sacrifice of Jeelius, an Argonian priest. Other reports claim that the hero fought their way to the Shrine of Dagon, defeating every cultist they came across. Either way, the hero arrived to find Mankar Camoran performing a ritual. While Mankar disappeared with the Amulet of Kings through a portal to "[[Lore:Gaiar Alata|Paradise]]", the hero to managed to successfully steal the cult's bible, the [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|Mysterium Xarxes]]. With the book in their possession, the hero returned to Cloud Ruler Temple and delivered it to Martin. With the Mysterium Xarxes, Martin believed he could find out where to find Mankar Camoran.{{ref|name=DS|Events of [[Oblivion:Dagon Shrine|Dagon Shrine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

At some point soon after returning from the Mythic Dawn headquarters, [[Lore:Fort Sutch|Fort Sutch]] was attacked by a Daedric invasion. The Hero of Kvatch managed to fight their way through the Daedric forces and close the Oblivion Gate.{{ref|name=AFS|Events of [[Oblivion:Attack on Fort Sutch|Attack on Fort Sutch]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Back in Cloud Ruler Temple, Jauffre discovered that a couple of spies were found along the road near the temple. He tasked the hero with finding these spies and discovering their plans. The commander of the Blades at Cloud Ruler Temple, [[Lore:Captain Steffan|Captain Steffan]], mentioned that these spies have been seen around the nearby [[Lore:Standing_Stones#Runestones|runestone]] at dusk. The hero tracked down the spies, and slew them. Then, with the help of [[Lore:Captain Burd|Captain Burd]] of [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]], the hero located the enemy base and discovered their plans to attack both Cloud Ruler Temple and Bruma through the use of Oblivion Gates.{{ref|name=Spies|Events of [[Oblivion:Spies|Spies]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} At this point, Martin Septim had discovered that in order to follow Mankar Camoran to Oblivion, four items were needed. The only one that Martin had deciphered thus far was the "blood of a Daedra Lord". Because a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] is made of the essence of its [[Lore:Daedric Prince|lord]], Martin surmised that one such artifact would suffice. It is unknown which artifact was retrieved by the hero, though it is certain that the hero had to complete a trial to receive it from its Daedric Prince.{{ref|name=BOTD1|Events of [[Oblivion:Blood of the Daedra|Blood of the Daedra]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} With the Daedric artifact in his possession, Martin continued to decipher the Mysterium Xarxes, but told the hero that Jauffre had a task for them. Jauffre informed the hero that an Oblivion Gate had opened near Bruma, and Captain Burd wanted the hero to teach them how to close it. The Hero of Kvatch arrived just in time to help repel a wave of Daedric invaders, and they lead the charge into the gate. Making their way to the top of the Sigil Keep, the hero and Captain Burd will defeat the Sigil Keeper and enter the Sigillum Sanguis. The hero then fought their way to the [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]] and removed it, closing the gate. With this success, Captain Burd thanked the hero, who then returned to Jauffre.{{ref|name=OBG|Events of [[Oblivion:Bruma Gate|Bruma Gate]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} While the hero was away, Martin managed to translate the next item from the Mysterium Xarxes, "the blood of a [[Lore:Divines|Divine]]". Despite the fact that the Divines allegedly had no artifacts, Jauffre had the idea to use the blood of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], who became the Ninth Divine [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] upon his death. The Blades knew of the [[Lore:Armor of Tiber Septim|Armor of Tiber Septim]], which lay in the ruins of [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]]. However, Jauffre warned the hero of an evil that lurked within the ruins, and feared he was sending the hero to their death. Nonetheless, the hero ventured into the catacombs, fighting through many [[Lore:Ghost|ethereal]] [[Lore:Wraith|dead]]. The hero came across Rielus, one of the top Blades in the service of Tiber Septim. The ghost of Rielus told the hero about the curse of the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], which bound the souls of four Blades to guard the ruins and barred the Armor of Tiber Septim from entry. The Hero of Kvatch defeated each ghost of the Blade members and freed them from the curse. The spirits of the Blades members then dispelled the evil enchantment on the armor, allowing the hero to retrieve it from the Tombs of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman]] Emperors. The hero then brought the artifact to Martin, who had already deciphered the third item, a Great [[Lore:Welkynd Stone#Great Welkynd Stones|Great Welkynd Stone]]. Martin knew of only one that hadn't been lost or plundered, the Great Welkynd Stone in the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruin of [[Lore:Miscarcand|Miscarcand]].{{ref|name=BOTD2|Events of [[Oblivion:Blood of the Divines|Blood of the Divines]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

At some point before retrieving the Great Welkynd Stone, the hero was tasked by Jauffre with requesting aid from the other cities to help in Bruma's continued defense. Each city's count or countess agreed under the condition that the Oblivion Gates threatening their cities were closed. After the Hero of Kvatch entered each gate and removed their Sigil Stones, thus closing the gates, the cities agreed to lend aid to Bruma.{{ref|name=AFB|Events of [[Oblivion:Allies for Bruma|Allies for Bruma]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The Hero of Kvatch made their way to Miscarcand and entered the ruin. The hero fought through waves of [[Lore:Zombie|zombies]], [[Lore:Goblin|goblins]], and [[Lore:Skeleton|skeletons]] until finally reaching the Great Welkynd Stone. After they retrieved the stone, the undead [[Lore:King of Miscarcand|King of Miscarcand]] was released and attacked the hero. Once the [[Lore:Lich|lich]] was defeated, the hero was able to bring the stone back to Martin at Cloud Ruler Temple.{{ref|name=OMQ|Events of [[Oblivion:Miscarcand (quest)|Miscarcand]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The hero arrived at the temple to find Martin speaking with Jauffre about a daring plan. Martin revealed to the hero that he deciphered the final item from the Mysterium Xarxes, a [[Lore:Great Sigil Stone|Great Sigil Stone]]. Martin had the idea of letting a Great Gate open near the city of Bruma, so the Great Sigil Stone powering the gate could be retrieved. Jauffre was worried about the risk, and Martin knew the [[Lore:Countess Narina Carvain|Countess]] of Bruma would not like the idea, but this plan was the only way to retrieve the fourth item. For his part, Martin would lead the Blades in defense of Bruma, acting as an Emperor should. The hero was tasked with convincing the Countess of the plan, which they succeeded in doing after the Countess realized the situation was hopeless while this plan offered a possibility victory against the Daedra. The Countess met with Martin Septim at the Chapel of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], and they prepared for the battle ahead. Martin Septim then led the amassed forces of Bruma against a crescendo of opening Oblivion Gates. Wave after wave of Daedra poured through the gates, but they were each defeated. The hero defended Martin Septim against each threat, making sure the Emperor stayed alive. At last, the Great Gate opened, and Martin's plan could be enacted. The Hero of Kvatch charged through the gate, and entered into [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] once more. {{ref|name=ODOM|Events of [[Oblivion:Defense of Bruma|Defense of Bruma]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Inside of the Great Gate, the hero rushed to the Great Sigil Stone. A [[Lore:Siege Crawler|Daedric Siege Crawler]] slowly advanced to the gate, pressuring the hero to move quickly. Fighting their way through the Daedra, the hero ascended the World Breaker tower, and retrieved the Great Sigil Stone. The Great Gate closed, and the Siege Crawler collapsed halfway through the portal. The hero emerged from the gate and helped defeat the remnants of the Daedric forces. The city was saved, and the hero was dubbed the '''Savior of Bruma'''. Martin told the hero to meet him at Cloud Ruler Temple when they were ready to follow Mankar Camoran into his "Paradise".{{ref|name=GG|Events of [[Oblivion:Great Gate|Great Gate]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

[[File:OB-creature-Mehrunes Dagon.jpg|thumb|right|Mehrunes Dagon, the Daedric Prince of Darkness and Destruction]]
Upon arriving at Cloud Ruler Temple, the hero will find that Martin has prepared the ritual to open the portal. When the hero was ready, Martin opened the portal and the Hero of Kvatch entered into Mankar Camoran's "Paradise". Fighting through Daedra, the hero will come across the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] [[Lore:Kathutet|Kathutet]], who holds the [[Lore:Bands of the Chosen|Bands of the Chosen]]. The dremora will offer the Bands in exchange for freeing a trapped [[Lore:Xivilai|Xivilai]] named [[Lore:Anaxes|Anaxes]]. Whether the hero accepted the task or not is unknown, but either way the hero retrieved the Bands of the Chosen. With them, the hero could enter the [[Lore:Forbidden Grotto|Forbidden Grotto]]. Once inside the Grotto, the hero could not leave it with the Bands still on, and they could not remove the Bands either. In the Grotto, the hero came across [[Lore:Eldamil|Eldamil]], a Mythic Dawn cultist that had become disillusioned with Mankar Camoran. Eldamil will offer to remove the Bands if the hero promised to kill Mankar Camoran, and asked the hero to pretend to be his prisoner to get past the Daedra. The hero trusted Eldamil, and was rewarded when the disillusioned cultist upheld his end of the bargain. Eldamil removed the Bands, allowing the hero access to the next part of the realm. The hero made their way to Carac Agaialor, Mankar Camoran's palace. There, the hero came across Mankar's children, [[Lore:Raven Camoran|Raven]] and [[Lore:Ruma Camoran|Ruma Camoran]]. They were expecting the hero, and led them to their father's throne room, where Mankar himself awaited with the Amulet of Kings. A fight ensued, the hero against the Camoran family. Despite the numerical advantage and the usage of powerful sorcery, the Hero of Kvatch struck down Mankar Camoran and retrieved the Amulet of Kings. With Mankar dead, his "Paradise" was destabilized and collapsed. The hero emerged in Cloud Ruler Temple and handed the Amulet to Martin. Martin hesitantly put on the Amulet of Kings, confirming his [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim blood]]. Martin will reveal that while the hero was gone, he sent a message to [[Lore:Ocato of Firsthold|High Chancellor Ocato]] about his claim to the throne and imminent arrival to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] to relight the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]].{{ref|name=OP|Events of [[Oblivion:Paradise (quest)|Paradise]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion]]''}}

The Hero of Kvatch and the Blades then escorted Martin to be crowned at the Imperial City, where the Emperor met with Chancellor Ocato. While the Chancellor was about to officially recognize Martin Septim as Emperor of Tamriel, a messenger arrived with news that Oblivion Gates had opened ''inside'' of the Imperial City. Suddenly, Daedric invaders entered the palace. With the help of the Palace Guards, the Blades, and Ocato, the Daedra were quickly defeated, though this was only the start of the battle. After exiting the palace into [[Lore:Green Emperor Way|Green Emperor Way]], a battalion of Daedra swarmed the Emperor. Alongside the Blades, the Hero of Kvatch defended Martin Septim as he made his way to the [[Lore:Temple District|Temple District]] and towards the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]]. Before they could make it to the temple, [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] himself arrived. With the arrival of the Daedric Prince, all was lost. The [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|Liminal Barriers]] that protected [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] from Daedric threats had been completely extinguished, and lighting the Dragonfires would not end the crisis as planned. Martin Septim frantically tried to think of a plan, and in a moment of desperation, asked the Hero of Kvatch to get him past Mehrunes Dagon to the Temple of the One. As Blades members and city guards tried in vain to fight the unstoppable Daedric Prince, the Hero of Kvatch managed to get the Emperor past him and into the temple. Here, Martin smashed the Amulet of Kings and became a fiery golden [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]], the [[Lore:Akatosh|Avatar of Akatosh]]. The Avatar of Akatosh fought with Mehrunes Dagon, immortal against immortal. The golden dragon finally managed to wound Dagon, and banished the Daedric Prince back to Oblivion. At last the crisis had ended, and Nirn was saved from the Daedric threat.{{ref|name=LTDF|Events of [[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} For their efforts in ending the Oblivion Crisis, Chancellor [[Lore:Ocato|Ocato]] of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] awarded the Hero of Kvatch with the title of Champion of Cyrodiil, and gave them their own set of [[Lore:Imperial Dragon Armor|Imperial Dragon Armor]].{{ref|name=IDA|[[Oblivion:Imperial Dragon Armor|Imperial Dragon Armor]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Becoming the Divine Crusader===
[[File:OB-prerelease-Facing Umaril the Unfeathered.jpg|The Divine Crusader facing Umaril the Unfeathered|thumb|right]]
At the same time as the Oblivion Crisis, [[Lore:Umaril|Umaril the Unfeathered]] returned to Tamriel to seek vengeance upon the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Divines]] who had brought about his fall.{{ref|name=OBUmaril|[[Oblivion:Umaril the Unfeathered (person)|Umaril the Unfeathered]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} [[Lore:Auroran|Aurorans]] under his command desecrated the chapel of [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]] in [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]], and later the chapel of [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] in [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]].{{ref|name=Pilgrimage|Events of [[Oblivion:Pilgrimage|Pilgrimage]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}

The Hero of Kvatch learned of Umaril's return from an elderly [[Lore:The Prophet|prophet]] in Anvil, and that he must be conquered in both the physical and spiritual plane in order to be vanquished, a feat that would only be possible with the aid of the Divines and the [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Crusader's Relics]].{{ref|name=Pilgrimage}} The hero gathered the eight Relics originally used by [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] to destroy Umaril's physical form. With the prophet's guidance, the Crusader went on to find something which Pelinal did not, could not, have had when he battled Umaril: the Blessing of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]].{{ref|name=TBOT|Events of [[Oblivion:The Blessing of Talos|The Blessing of Talos]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} Armed with Talos' blessing, the Hero of Kvatch, now known as the '''Divine Crusader''' reborn confronted Umaril and his hosts in the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] fortress [[Lore:Garlas Malatar|Garlas Malatar]]. Umaril noted with begrudging respect that the reach of his old nemesis Pelinal was long and engaged the Divine Crusader in battle. The hero not only defeated Umaril on the mortal plane, but was able to follow him into the spirit realm, where the Unfeathered finally met his end.{{ref|name=Shrine|Events of [[Oblivion:The Shrine of the Crusader|The Shrine of the Crusader]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=Priory|Events of [[Oblivion:Priory of the Nine|Priory of the Nine]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=NF|Events of [[Oblivion:Nature's Fury|Nature's Fury]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=TPOTR|Events of [[Oblivion:The Path of the Righteous|The Path of the Righteous]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=WOTA|Events of [[Oblivion:Wisdom of the Ages|Wisdom of the Ages]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=SM|Events of [[Oblivion:Stendarr's Mercy|Stendarr's Mercy]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=TFS|Events of [[Oblivion:The Faithful Squire|The Faithful Squire]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=TSOTC|Events of [[Oblivion:The Sword of the Crusader|The Sword of the Crusader]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}{{ref|name=FMMWTDOM|[[General:Face Meridia's Minions Within the Depths of Malatar|Face Meridia's Minions Within the Depths of Malatar]]}}{{ref|name=Umaril|}}{{ref|name=Umaril|Events of [[Oblivion:Umaril the Unfeathered (quest)|Umaril the Unfeathered]] in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}

===Stopping the Greymarch===
[[File:SI-creature-Jyggalag.jpg|thumb|right|Jyggalag, the Prince of Order]]
After hearing rumors that a [[Lore:Strange Door|strange door]] had opened in the [[Lore:Niben Bay|Niben Bay]], the hero traveled offshore [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]]. Investigating these rumors leads the hero to being summoned to the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] to assist Lord [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] in countering an imminent threat to the realm. After defeating the [[Lore:Gatekeeper|Gatekeeper]] guarding the entrance to the realm proper, the hero traveled to the realm's capital, [[Lore:New Sheoth|New Sheoth]], and was subsequently tasked by the Prince of Madness himself to assist his servants with reactivating several institutions for the security and stability of the Isles, while countering any threats from the newly emerging [[Lore:Forces of Order|Forces of Order]]. When it is revealed Sheogorath was, in a past life, the Daedric Prince of Order [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]], and soon transformed into his former self, the hero took it upon themselves to mantle the '''Madgod''' and defeat the [[Lore:Forces of Order|Forces of Order]] from conquering the Isles.{{ref|name=Shivering Isles}} Despite their many personality changes in the following era, they would go on to consider Martin "the best Septim that's ever ruled".{{ref|name=Skyrim}}

==Fame and Infamy==
[[File:OBR-statue-Savior of Bruma.jpg|thumb|right|[[Lore:Statues|Statue]] of the Savior of Bruma]]
During their travels of Cyrodiil, the Hero was involved in many quests, earning many titles and adding to their renown:
*Referring to their service to Martin Septim, the hero was called '''Champion of Septim''' by the [[Lore:Ghost|spirits]] of Miscarcand.{{ref|[[OBMobile:Narrative#Miscarcand|Spirit]]'s dialogue during [[OBMobile:Miscarcand (quest)|Miscarcand]] in ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}}
*Following Martin's sacrifice, the hero was awarded the title of '''Champion of Cyrodiil''' by Chancellor Ocato, becoming the seventh person to ever bear that title.{{ref|name=IDA}}
*In the Imperial City [[Lore:Imperial City#Arena District|Arena]], they rose through the ranks of the Blue Team, eventually becoming the new '''Grand Champion''' after defeating its previous title holder, [[Lore:Agronak gro-Malog|Agronak gro-Malog]].{{ref|name=Oblivion}}
*After murdering in cold blood, the hero was invited to join the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]], rising through the ranks as they unearthed a plot against the guild, and ending it as the [[Lore:Black Hand|Black Hand]]'s '''Listener'''.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}
*As a member of the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]], they were promoted to its new Guild '''Master''' after bringing down the rival [[Lore:Blackwood Company|Blackwood Company]].{{ref|name=Oblivion}}
*With the return of [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]], the hero assisted the Mages Guild in combating the [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancer]] threat, becoming powerful enough to replace the institution's late '''Arch-Mage'''.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}
*As a member of the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]], the hero broke into several well-protected locations to steal powerful artifacts, including an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] from the [[Lore:Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace]] itself, restoring [[Lore:Corvus Umbranox|Corvus Umbranox]] to the throne of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] and becoming the new '''[[Lore:Gray Fox|Gray Fox]]''' in his stead.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}
*Following their investigation of disappearances along the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]] at the behest of a priest of the [[Lore:Order of the Hour|Order of the Hour]], the hero discovered a [[Lore:Time Wound|Time Wound]] that had been opened by a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] that was hoping to [[Lore:Time Magic|travel back in time]] through the use of an Elder Scroll, deep within the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruins of [[Lore:Beldaburo|Beldaburo]]. Proceeding past the barrier created by the forces emitted by the wound with the Order priest's blessing, the hero slew the lich and closed the Wound before it could be fully opened, thereby saving reality itself from collapsing. For this deed of saving time itself, the hero was named a '''Paladin of the Order of the Hour'''.{{ref|[[Oblivion:A Wound In Time|A Wound In Time]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Saving Time Itself|Saving Time Itself]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|name=ONCD|{{Cite Book|Corelian's diary}}}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Ilav Dralgoner|Ilav Dralgoner]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}
*For their slaying of the leader of the [[Lore:Black Bow Bandits|Black Bow Bandits]], they were named '''Knight-Errant of the [[Lore:Knights of the White Stallion|Knights of the White Stallion]]'''.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Count Marius Caro|Count Marius Caro]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Knights of the White Stallion|Knights of the White Stallion]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}  
*For their actions of exposing the leader of the [[Lore:Order of the Virtuous Blood|Order of the Virtuous Blood]]  as a [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] himself and slaying him, the hero was named an '''Honorary Brother/Honorary Sister''' of the organization.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Roland Jenseric|Roland Jenseric]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Order of the Virtuous Blood (quest)|Order of the Virtuous Blood (quest)]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}  
*For their actions of safely returning the heir, Martin Septim, during the course of the Oblivion Crisis, the hero might've been inducted into the Blades, becoming a '''Knight Brother/Knight Sister''' of the organization.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Jauffre|Jauffre]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
*For their actions of saving [[Lore:Farwil Indarys|Farwil Indarys]] from within an Oblivion Gate during the course of the Oblivion Crisis, the hero might've been inducted into the [[Lore:Knights of the Thorn|Knights of the Thorn]], becoming an '''Honorary Knight''' of the organization.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Jauffre|Jauffre]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Farwil Indarys|Farwil Indarys]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:The Wayward Knight|The Wayward Knight]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
