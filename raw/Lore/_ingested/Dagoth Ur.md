[[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] ({{IPA|ˈdeɪˌgɒθ ˈʌɹ}} {{pronunciation|DAY|GOTH|ERR}}{{Ab|LO-audio-word-Dagoth Ur-Morrowind.ogg}} or {{IPA|ˈdæ.gə.θəɹ}} {{pronunciation|DAG|guh|therr}}{{Ab|LO-audio-word-Dagoth Ur-Arena.ogg}}), originally '''Voryn Dagoth''',{{ref|name=MFDU|{{Cite Book|Message from Dagoth Ur}}}} also known as the '''Sharmat''',{{ref|name=Sithis|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}}{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}} as well as '''Awakened Lord of the Sixth House''',{{ref|name=Gares|[[MW:Dagoth Gares|Dagoth Gares]]' dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} '''Lord Dagoth''' and '''Father of the Mountain''' to his followers,{{Ref|name=GenericDialogueD|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue D|Dagoth Ur + Dissident Priests]] dialogue topic in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} was the immortal Lord High Councilor of [[Lore:House Dagoth|House Dagoth]]. In Tribunal mythology, he is demonized as '''the Devil''' and '''the Enemy''', '''False Dreamer''',{{ref|name=36L}} '''Devil Dagoth Ur''',{{Ref|name=GenericDialogueD}} and '''Devil of Dagoth'''.{{ref|name=FSOKW}}<noinclude>

In life, Voryn Dagoth was a close friend to the Hortator [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]], whom he considered his brother.{{ref|name=MFDU}}{{ref|name=TBORM}} In the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], he inadvertently caused the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] by informing Nerevar that House Dagoth had discovered the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]'s worship of a secret source of power, the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], which the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] deemed profane and heretical.{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} Accounts vary significantly on what actions he took before, during, and after the battle, but regardless, it resulted in Dagoth and the Sixth House being defeated and condemned as traitors, their names being largely erased from [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] history by the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]].{{ref|name=PS|{{Cite Book|Poison Song}}}} According to various accounts, including his own,{{ref|name=Gares}} he was struck down by Nerevar himself.{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}}{{ref|name=FSOKW}}

Unbeknownst to the Tribunal, Dagoth and the Sixth House were not completely wiped out, and he and his kin slept beneath Red Mountain for millennia.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} He would awaken in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], creating plans to build a second [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] named [[Lore:Akulakhan|Akulakhan]] to conquer [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} He wreaked havoc on [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] for centuries by robbing the Tribunal of their source of divine power{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} and creating legions of [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]] monsters and [[Lore:Ash Creatures|ash creatures]] by unleashing [[Lore:The Blight|the Blight]].{{ref|name=DUDialogue}} His reign of terror would end in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] when the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] ventured into Red Mountain and defeated him.{{ref|name=MW|Events of [[MW:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}}

==History==
===Early Life===
[[File:MW-banner-House Dagoth.png|left|thumb|Banner of House Dagoth]]
[[File:MW-place-Kogoruhn.jpg| Kogoruhn, home fortress of House Dagoth|thumb|right]]
Voryn Dagoth was the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] Lord High Councilor of [[Lore:House Dagoth|House Dagoth]]{{ref|name=MWVivec|[[MW:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} one of the seven [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] of Resdayn which ruled the land alongside their allies the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]. He resided in [[Lore:Kogoruhn|Kogoruhn]] (Unbroken Home in Dark Elvish) the clanstead of the House.<ref name=MWHasphat>[[Morrowind:Hasphat Antabolis|Hasphat Antabolis]]' dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]</ref> The rulers of the First Council were [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Nerevar]] the Hortator of the Chimer, and [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac Dwarf-Orc]] the King of the Dwemer.{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} Voryn was a trusted friend of both rulers,{{ref|name=TBORM|}} particularly Nerevar who he was of the same generation as,{{ref|name=MWVivec|}} and shared a brotherly bond with.{{ref|name=MFDU|{{Cite Book|Message from Dagoth Ur}}}} He also served Nerevar loyally as his general and retainer.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}{{Ref|name=DivaythFyr|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He was a mighty sorcerer and enchanter.{{ref|name=MWVivec|}}

===The War of the First Council===
====Loyal Dagoth Ur====
''Voryn Dagoth played a heavy role in the divide of and eventual [[Lore:War of the First Council|war]] among the First Council, although accounts greatly differ on what side he was on. Certain stories, namely that of both the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]] and Vivec himself, tell a similar tale that differ in key aspects.''

These accounts involve Lord Dagoth approaching Nerevar and his advisors, [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], and [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] with knowledge that House Dagoth had discovered the source of the long unknown secret power source the Dwemer worshipped, that of the legendary [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]]. Dagoth explained that the Dwemer had used this profane power to make themselves immortal and thus beyond the measure of the gods, and that the High Priest, and Chief Tonal Architect of the Dwemer [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac the Blighter]] even had gone as far as to devise [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|tools]] to build a [[Lore:Numidium|New God]] that served as a mockery of Chimer faith and a fearsome weapon that the Dwemer would use to claim Resdayn for their own.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}}

Nerevar was troubled by this and approached Dumac with these accusations and asked if they were true. Kagrenac and his priests had kept the construction of the New God, Numidium, a secret from the king and thus Dumac claimed innocence for his people of any wrongdoing. Kagrenac approached Nerevar and asked him who he thought he was to presume to judge the actions of the Dwemer. Nerevar was no less troubled by this meeting with the Dwemer, so now made pilgrimage to [[Lore:Holamayan|Holamayan]], the sacred temple of the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], who confirmed that everything Dagoth had said was true, and that the construction of this New God should be prevented at all cost for not only the sake of Resdayn, but for the [[Lore:Nirn|whole world]].{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}}

[[File:MW-misc-Anniversary Art 2025 Crop.png|Dagoth Ur among the First Council|thumb|left]]
Nerevar, angered that Dumac seemingly lied to him, returned to Red Mountain one final time arrayed in arms and armor and surrounded by his hosts, he hoped to preserve the peace, but demanded to Dumac and his people that they give up their worship of the Heart of Lorkhan. Dumac was still unaware of the plans of the New God, but was proud and protective of his people and the two quarreled bitterly and thus went to war.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}}

The War of the First Council concluded at the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]]. According to the aforementioned accounts, by the combined cleverness of the Tribunal and Nerevar, the Dwemer armies were largely drawn out of their fortress of Red Mountain into the open fields, which allowed Nerevar, Dagoth, and a small group of companions the opportunity to sneak into the Heart Chamber by secret means. There, the two kings Nerevar and Dumac met and both fell from grievous wounds and draining magics.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}} The similar accounts disagree on exactly what happened next at this point. 

Vivec's retelling says that with his king down, and threatened by Voryn Dagoth and others, Kagrenac turned his tools upon the Heart, and at that moment all Dwemer in the world disappeared without a trace.{{ref|name=TBORM|}} The Ashlander account instead claims that before Kagrenac could use his tools, he was slain by Lord Dagoth who presented them to his dying friend Nerevar and asked him what to do with the tools. Nerevar summoned Azura and the Daedric Prince showed them how to use the tools to separate the power of the Heart from the Dwemer people, and the Tribunal and their armies then bore witness to all of the Dwarves turn into dust with their stolen immortality taken away.{{ref|name=NARM|}} Regardless, the accounts continue on that Dagoth bade to Nerevar that the tools of Kagrenac be destroyed, so as to avoid future evils should the tools fall in the wrong hands. But Nerevar was confused by his wounds and sorrow for the loss of his old friend Dumac and the Dwemer whom he still loved, and also did not wish to ignore the Tribunal's council yet again and thus went to confer with them, leaving his general and friend Voryn Dagoth with the words "Stay here, loyal Dagoth Ur, until I return."{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}}

Accounts once again disagree on what happened from this point forward, a gravely injured Nerevar went to receive council from the Tribunal. According to the Ashlanders, the Tribunal decided that the Chimer should attempt to harness this power for themselves so Nerevar might claim Resdayn and the very world for their people. Nerevar did not expect or want that and so asked his queen and generals to summon Azura yet again for her guidance. The Tribunal who had become greedy and coveted the Heart betrayed Nerevar. They feigned the ritual to summon Azura but used poisoned candles, poisoned robes, and poisoned invocations, killing the Hortator.{{ref|name=NARM|}}
[[File:MW-misc-Anniversary Art 2024.jpg|Indoril Nerevar|thumb|right]]

Azura appeared before them and vowed to use her powers over dusk and dawn to make sure Nerevar would [[Lore:Nerevarine|come back]] and make things right again. Azura before departing then placed a curse upon the Tribunal and the Chimer people as a whole turning their skin ashen and their eyes into fire, the Chimer had become the Dark Elves, the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]. The Tribunal then returned to the Heart Chamber and met with Dagoth-Ur, who saw what had been done, for his skin had changed as well. He tried to avenge the death of Nerevar but was defeated by the Tribunal who thought him dead. The Tribunal found the tools Dagoth had been guarding and, through study of Kagrenac's methods, turned themselves into gods.{{ref|name=NARM|}}

Vivec's account of the council, and subsequent actions of himself and his fellow Tribunes greatly differs. His claim is that the Tribunal advised that the tools be preserved and studied for the welfare of the Chimer and as a precautionary in the event the Dwemer should ever return. Nerevar voiced grave misgivings but agreed to the council under one condition. That all of them swear oaths under Azura that the tools would never be put to use in the profane manner that the Dwemer had intended. Everyone readily agreed and swore the solemn oaths at Nerevar's dictation.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}

Upon returning to Red Mountain, Nerevar and the Tribunal found Dagoth Ur unwilling to yield the tools to them, saying that only he could be trusted with them and that they were dangerous: Dagoth had seemingly learned the power of the tools in the period of time he was left alone with them. Nerevar and his guard resorted to taking the tools by force. Somehow Dagoth Ur and his retainers escaped, but the tools were retrieved and put within the care of Sotha Sil for safekeeping. For some years the Tribunal kept the oaths they swore with Nerevar before Azura, but in secret, Sotha Sil had been studying the tools and managed to divine their mysteries. He approached his fellow Tribunes with a vision of peace, prosperity, and a better world. They then made the pilgrimage to Red Mountain and used the tools upon the Heart to transform themselves, and instantly Azura appeared before them, vowing that [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|Nerevar would return]] to punish them for their perfidy, subsequently cursing them by turning their skin ashen and their eyes of fire, the Chimer into Dunmer.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}

====Devil of Dagoth====
''Other accounts of the War of the First Council and ultimate conclusion at the Battle of Red Mountain go in a much different direction in regards to Dagoth Ur. Whereas the aforementioned accounts have him staying loyal to his fellow Chimer and his liege Nerevar and fighting against the Dwemer at their side, some say otherwise.'' 

According to the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] legends of the ''[[Lore:Five Songs of King Wulfharth|Five Songs of King Wulfharth]]'' the Nords upon learning that the Dwarves and Devils (Chimer) of the eastern kingdoms had started to fight again, the Nords hoped they might be able to reclaim their [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|ancient holdings]] because of it. They made plans for attack but quickly gave up after realizing they had no strong King to lead them. But suddenly Voryn Dagoth, who the Nords referred to as the Devil of Dagoth arrived swearing peace, and claiming he knew where the Heart of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] was. Dagoth informed the Nords that the Devils and Dwarves had the heart of Shor but the Nords grew suspicious asking why he would betray his countrymer in such a manner to which the Devil of Dagoth responded that his people have betrayed one another since the beginning of time, and this was so and so the Nords believed him.{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}

The [[Lore:Tongues|Tongues]] sung Shor's ghost again and Shor gathered an army as he did of old, and then he sucked in the long-strewn ashes of [[Lore:Wulfharth|King Wulfharth]] and remade him, for he needed a good general. But the Devil of Dagoth petitioned to be that general too, and pointed out his role as the blessed harbinger of the holy war, thus Shor had two generals, the Ash King Wulfharth and the Devil of Dagoth. And they marched on to the eastern kingdoms with all the sons of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|name=FSOKW|}}

The next song of King Wulfharth claims that the Devil of Dagoth had tricked the Nords, the Heart of Shor was not in the eastern kingdoms and never had been, as soon as Shor and his army arrived to Red Mountain, the Devils and Dwarves fell upon them. Their sorcerers lifted the mountain and threw it onto Shor, trapping him under Red Mountain for all of time. The Nord army was slaughtered but not before Wulfharth managed to kill Dumalacath the Dwarf king and doom his people. Vehk the Devil then blasted the Ash King into Hell but [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] lifted him from Hell and showed the color of blood when it is brought by betrayal, and the Nords vowed to never trust another Devil again.{{ref|name=FSOKW|}}

A subsequent secret song titled "The Truth at Red Mountain" claims that the Heart of Shor was indeed in Resdayn as Dagoth Ur had promised. Dagoth Ur admitted that the Tribunal had betrayed Nerevar's trust and had sent him to Lorkhan (for that is what they called Shor in Resdayn) so that the god might wreak vengeance on the Dwemer for their hubris, as they believed Nerevar's peace with the Dwemer would bring ruin to the [[Lore:Veloth|Velothi]] way. Lorkhan vowed that for his own reasons the Dwemer would indeed die by his hand.{{ref|name=FSOKW|}} 

Lorkhan claimed Nerevar the son of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], one of the strongest [[Lore:Padomay|Padomaics]] and recognized him as a hero to his people despite his treacherous Tribunal and said he would muster enough that the battle ahead would be hard going. And so Dagoth-Ur, who wanted the Dwemer as dead as the Tribunal did, went to [[Lore:Kogoruhn|Kogoran]] and summoned his House chap'thil, his [[Lore:Nix-Hound|nix-hounds]] his wizards, archers, his stolen men of brass. And the Ash King Wulfharth went and made peace with the [[Lore:Orcs|Orcs]] in spite of his Nordic blood, and they brought many warriors but no wizards at all.{{ref|name=FSOKW|}}

At the last Battle of Red Mountain, Lorkhan, Dagoth Ur, and Wulfharth confronted Nerevar who wielded [[Lore:Keening|Keening]], Dumac who wielded [[Lore:Sunder|Sunder]] and [[Lore:Alandro Sul|Alandro Sul]] the immortal son of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] who wielded the [[Lore:Wraith Mail|Wraith Mail]]. Lorkhan had reunited with his Heart, but he had long been from it and needed time to gain his full strength. Wulfharth and Sul dueled but Wulfharth was unable to strike him, he fell from grievous wounds but not before shouting Sul blind. Dumac was able to strike Lorkhan's Heart with Sunder but was subsequently slain by Dagoth Ur. Nerevar turned away from Lorkhan and struck down Dagoth Ur in a rage but was then left open to receive a mortal wound from Lorkhan. But Nerevar feigned the death that was coming early and struck Lorkhan with surprise on his side. The Heart had been made solid by Dumac's earlier tuning blow with Sunder and because of this Nerevar was able to cut it out with Keening, dealing Lorkhan a second death and the whole thing was thought over.{{ref|name=FSOKW|}}

====The Traitor House====
''While the Ashlander, Nordic, and Vivec's account feature Dagoth Ur and his allies as enemies of the Dwemer, several others sources claim the opposite.''

As the story goes, religious disputes were formed between the secular Great Houses of the First Council, House Dwemer and House Dagoth and the orthodox Houses [[Lore:Indoril|Indoril]], [[Lore:Dres|Dres]], [[Lore:Hlaalu|Hlaalu]], [[Lore:Telvanni|Telvanni]], and [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]].{{ref|name=MWNev|[[MW:Generic Dialogue N|"Nerevar" generic dialogue]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}}{{ref|name=TRN|{{Cite Book|The Real Nerevar}}}} The basis of the disputes was about the sorceries and enchantments practiced by the Dwemer and declared profane by the other houses.{{ref|name=TWOTFC|}} The alliance with House Dagoth came naturally as by some accounts House Dagoth had taken to practicing some of the rituals and principles pioneered by High Craftlord Kagrenac, with Dagoth Ur himself adopting the views and motivations of Kagrenac.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} They also found themselves aligned with western allies in the Nords and Orcs who were drawn in from the promises of land and booty.{{ref|name=TWOTFC|}}{{ref|name=MWNev|}}

House Dagoth, House Dwemer and the others held northwest Resdayn and delivered the widely dispersed and poorly organized Orthodox Houses defeat after defeat until Nerevar was made undisputed general of all House troops and levies.{{ref|name=TRN|}} Nerevar also secured aid of the nomadic  Ashlanders who proved to be vital as scouts in a major battle on Vvardenfell in which the Secular forces were outmaneuvered and defeated and the survivors were forced to flee to Dwemer stronghold of Red Mountain.{{ref|name=TRN|}}{{ref|name=TWOTFC|}}

After a brief siege, an act of treachery allowed Nerevar and his troops to enter the fortress.{{ref|name=TWOTFC|}} The Secular were defeated with great slaughter, Dagoth Ur managed to deal a mortal wound to Nerevar,{{Ref|name=DivaythFyr}}{{ref|name=SN|{{Cite Book|Saint Nerevar}}}} but thereafter terrible sorceries were used that resulted in the utter extermination of the Dwemer, House Dagoth, and their allies.{{ref|name=TRN|}} Nerevar died shortly after the battle to his wounds but lived long enough to see the birth of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Temple]] and bless the unity of the Dunmer into the safekeeping of [[Lore:Almsivi|Almsivi]], the Temple, and all its [[Lore:Temple Saints|communion of saints]].{{ref|name=SN|}}{{ref|name=MWNev|}} The Ashlanders on the other hand say one of his final acts was promising to honor the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Ancient Spirits]] and the Tribal Law and that he will come again to honor that promise.{{ref|name=NevCultNotes|{{Cite Book|Nerevarine cult notes}}}}

====Sharmat====
[[File:GEN-misc-Foul Murder.png|right|thumb|The murder of Nerevar by the Tribunal, Voryn Dagoth forced into the dirt by mass altering powers of Kagrenac's Tools]]

''Vivec has alternative accounting of the battle in his [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|Sermons]].''

He claims that the Dwemer and Chimer lived under the wise and benevolent rule of Almsivi and their champion the Hortator but the Dwemer became foolish and challenged their masters. The Nords and Ysmir Wulfharth came to the aid of the Dwemer, who were led by King Dumac and High Priest Kagrenac. Nerevar led the Chimer armies and, wielding the Ethos Knife, slew Dumac at Red Mountain and saw the heart bone for the first time. Meanwhile, the [[Lore:Mournhold|Mourning Hold]] was attacked by [[Lore:Dwarven Centurion|Dwarven Centurion]]s and Dwemeri [[Lore:Tonal Architecture|tonal architects]], before being repelled by Almalexia and Sotha Sil.{{ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Nerevar ventured deeper into Red Mountain in search of the Sharmat, Dagoth Ur but "went too far inside" and the mountain erupted. It was then that Dwemeri high priest Kagrenac "the Blighter" unveiled the ultimate Dwemer weapon: a "[[Lore:Numidium|walking star]]", which immolated the Chimer armies and blasted the land with such force that the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] was created. It took the combined might of the Almsivi but they eventually destroyed the construct; as it died the Dwemer vanished.{{ref|name=36L36|}}

Regardless of the validity of the various tellings, what is known following the Battle of Red Mountain is that the Dwemer were gone, the westerners were banished from Morrowind, Nerevar was soon perished, the Chimer had become Dunmer, Voryn Dagoth was believed dead, and Resdayn (now Morrowind) was under unchallenged rule by the newly ascended trio of living gods, Almsivi.

===Sixth House===
[[File:ON-banner-House Dagoth.png|Banner of House Dagoth flying once again|thumb|left]]
With the newly ascended Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Vivec now establishing rule over Morrowind, they were quick to declare House Dagoth, now disgraced and referred to only as the "Sixth House" or even "the House we do not name" as oath breakers who were reviled for their treachery.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Seven Curses}}}} The Sixth House was destroyed by Almsivi,{{ref|name=PS|{{Cite Book|Poison Song}}}} the remnants and relics of the House were set to flame and discarded all aspects of the House and its history was scrubbed from the history books.{{ref|name=PS|}}{{ref|name=SHT|[[Online:Sixth House Ritual Table|Sixth House Ritual Table]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After debates over true annihilation to fully erase the legacy of the accursed House or mercy which eventually prevailed, the surviving orphan children of the Sixth House were adopted by the five Great Houses and raised by way of their own traditions.{{ref|name=PS|}}

House Dagoth had survived, or perhaps been revived in the hearts and minds of some Dunmer. One novel claims in the years shortly after the Battle of Red Mountain a Dunmer boy named Indoril-Tay of House Indoril began having strange dreams in which a Song resonated deeply in his consciousness. Later he would learn these dreams and this Song deeply embedded in his psyche was that of the Sixth House of which he was truly a member of rather than Indoril, in fact he was supposedly the heir of Dagoth Ur himself named Dagoth-Tython, his lover was Dagoth-Acra, described as at least metaphorically, or perhaps even biologically his sister who together led by the Song killed many Dunmer of the Traditional Houses. After being routed and confronted Dagoth-Tython scaled to the highest peek of Red Mountain and leapt into the mouth of the mighty volcano, while Dagoth-Acra appeared to be pregnant with their child.{{ref|name=PS|}}

[[File:LG-cardart-Power of the Almsivi 02.png|Almsivi, the undisputed rulers of Morrowind after the banishment of the Sixth House and Dwemer|thumb|right]]
Another tale describes a confrontation held in [[Lore:Abernanit|Abernanit]] between [[Lore:Rangidil Ketil|Rangidil Ketil]], commander of the [[Lore:Ordinators|Temple Ordinators]] and blessed by Almsivi, and his adversary Dagoth Thras, the self proclaimed heir of the Sixth House. The two fought bitterly but Rangidil could not get an effective strike on Thras who was a master of the shield, and was struck down. Before being executed, Rangidil pleaded with Dagoth Thras to teach him the secrets behind his shield mastery to which Thras obliged. This was a ruse by Rangidil who delivered the confused Dagoth Thras with what became known to history as "The death blow of Abernanit."{{ref|name=TDBA|{{Cite Book|Death Blow of Abernanit}}}}

[[File:ON-npc-Nevena Nirith.jpg|Supposed blood heir of House Dagoth, Nevena Nirith|thumb|left]]
Yet another occurrence of a supposed heir of the House Dagoth line was in {{Year|2E 582}}. A member of the Tribunal Temple named Nevena Nirith journeyed to the ruins of the clanstead of House Dagoth, Kogoruhn to recover an item for the temple.{{Ref|name=ND|{{Cite Book|Nevena's Diary|ns_base=Online}}}} During this time period, the Sixth House was under attempts at a revival as cultists excavated the site claiming to be drawn by the relics of the House to do so, and also anticipating the leadership of blood heirs of the accursed Dagoth line.{{ref|name=WS|{{Cite Book|Wakener's Sermon}}}}{{Ref|name=EO|{{Cite Book|Excavation Orders|ns_base=Online}}}} Nevena and the other cultists came to the conclusion that she was among these blood heirs, she had forsaken the Temple and claimed Kogoruhn as her birthright.{{Ref|name=ND|}} Her brother, Tythis Nirith an Ordinator who was worried for her employed the aid of [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] to help her.{{ref|name=Tythis|[[Online:Tythis Nirith|Tythis Nirith]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Upon finding her, Nevena was inconsolable and had to be struck down,{{ref|name=Nevena|[[Online:Nevena Nirith|Nevena Nirith]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the Vestige returned with the news to Tythis who lamented and rejected the idea that his sister and by extension himself, was an heir of the Lost House.{{ref|name=Tythis|}}

Vivec also at some point after the Battle of Red Mountain published his 36 Lessons, Sermons that served as holy text to guide the Dunmer people among other subliminal and secretive purposes. They were set in the time period of the rule of Nerevar with Almsivi as his God council and advisors. In mentoring and teaching Nerevar and preparing him for the trials to come, Vivec made several references to Dagoth Ur, who he referred to as the Sharmat. Vivec claimed the Sharmat was the false dreamer who resided within Red Mountain and was the double of the ruling king.{{ref|name=36L11|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 11}}}}{{ref|name=36L13|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 13}}}}{{ref|name=36L15|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 15}}}}{{ref|name=36L17|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 17}}}}{{ref|name=36L36|}} Vivec also worried on one particular occasion that the enemy of old, Dagoth Ur had returned during a plot against his power although this was not the case.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Divine Conundrum|Divine Conundrum]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Another Dunmeri mythical text claims Lorkhan was the spawn of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]] who was sent to destroy the Mundus, Lorkhan approached [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] and the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Givers]] as a friend to deceive them, the text urged the figure of prophecy to approach the Sharmat Dagoth Ur and do the same.{{ref|name=Sithis|}} Some Dunmer experienced dreams of the Sharmat lurking and waiting beneath the Mountain.{{ref|name=ONRV|Events of [[Online:Reclaiming Vos|Reclaiming Vos]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Return===
:"''But beneath Red Mountain, Dagoth Ur had survived. And even as the light of our bold new world shined ever more brightly, beneath Red Mountain, the darkness gathered, a darkness that was close kin to the bright light that Sotha Sil coaxed from the Heart of Lorkhan with the Tools of Kagrenac."'' — Vivec on the return of Dagoth Ur
[[File:LG-cardart-Hand of Dagoth.jpg|right|thumb|Dagoth Uthol, one of the Ash Vampires]][[File:LG-quest-The Heart, Revealed 02.jpg|thumb|left|Dagoth Ur, renewed by the Heart of Lorkhan's power]]
Dagoth Ur's essence had somehow not been fully destroyed at the Battle of Red Mountain as initially believed, he and his kin lay dreaming beneath the sills of Red Mountain (a time period known as the Long Sleep.){{ref|name=DUP|}} Voryn Dagoth's body was dead but Dagoth Ur had formed a unique connection with the Heart of Lorkhan and become the False Dreamer,{{ref|name=36L|}} and despite his death the Sharmat dreamt that he lived{{Ref|name=Nibani|[[Morrowind:Nibani Maesa|Nibani Maesa]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and thus he and his seven kin were revived in the year {{Year|2E 882}}.{{ref|name=DUP|}} Upon awaking refreshed underneath Red Mountain, Dagoth Ur and his kin moved into the Heart Chamber and Dagoth Ur bound himself and his brothers to the Heart in a ritual of his own devising{{ref|name=DUP|}} and causing Red Mountain to [[Lore: Days of Fire|erupt a second time]].{{ref|name=days_of_fire|{{Cite Book|The Pilgrim's Path}}}} Immediately thereafter initial stages of construction began on a project conceived during the Long Sleep for a Second Numidium known as [[Lore:Akulakhan|Akulakhan]] which was tended to by his seven [[Lore:Ash Vampire|heartwight]] brothers and atronach constructs.{{ref|name=DUP|}}

Later that same year, Vivec, Almalexia, and Sotha Sil made their annual journey to Red Mountain, a process disguised as a pilgrimage to the Dunmer faithful but actually a crucial time for them to renew their divine energy with Kagrenac's Tools. Dagoth Ur had made a careful point to keep his return, and the construction of Akulakhan a secret from the Temple and upon Almsivi's arrival they were ambushed by Dagoth Ur and the heartwight Ash Vampires who denied them access to the Heart Chamber and drove them from Red Mountain.{{ref|name=DUP|}} 

[[File:ON-place-Red Mountain.jpg|Mount Dagoth-Ur|thumb|left]]
The return of Dagoth Ur and the weakening of Almsivi via being denied access to the Heart of Lorkhan came at a crucial time in Morrowind's history. The rise of an individual known as [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] and his [[Lore:Tiber Wars|rapidly expanding]] [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] was already decades in the making and despite the daunting task of facing the Dunmer, Tiber Septim's need of the valuable [[Lore:Ebony|Ebony]] resource, and the dream of a fully united Tamriel had him finally set his sights on Morrowind. Initial skirmishes were had between the Empire and Dunmer House armies, but mutual reservations grew between both Almsivi and Tiber Septim who acknowledged the daunting task of facing each other, along with the threat proposed by the returned Dagoth Ur who held passionate hatred for both Almsivi and the Empire. Because of this Vivec and Tiber Septim signed the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]], a treaty that allowed near full autonomy for religious and cultural practices of the Dunmer, while providing the Empire important strategical concessions such as the acquisition of Morrowind as an Imperial province, and Tiber Septim being gifted the all powerful golem Numidium.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}

Many Dunmer felt furious and betrayed by their living gods for reaching a truce with the westerners and allowing outlanders to enter their land as a result of it. Dagoth Ur who also resented this used this to his advantage and was able to recruit many to his side. Beyond mere diplomatic methods, Dagoth Ur also possessed the power of dream compulsion which he used to recruit many to his side.{{ref|name=DUP|}} In fact Dagoth Ur, in his sustained shadow immortality had unparalleled supernatural abilities. With his dream compulsion he was able to enter the hearts and minds of citizens all throughout Vvardenfell.{{ref|name=DUP|}} Furthermore, from his fortress of Red Mountain (now also holding the name Dagoth-Ur for the god who resided within) he was able to spread the [[Lore:The Blight|Blight]] out of his citadel, and unto Vvardenfell. The Blight was conferred through intense ash storms which cloaked the sky and blotted out the sun. It killed flora, fauna, and citizen alike, and what wasn't killed was often stricken by the various diseases caused by the Blight. The most dangerous among these being the Divine Disease [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]]. This Divine strain was also concocted by Dagoth Ur and mutated the individual to bloated, grotesque forms and usually caused them to lose their mind in totality. The weaker beings who received this "gift" became deranged [[Lore:Lame Corprus|Corprus]] [[Lore:Corprus Stalker|monsters]], stronger cultists among the rank of the Sixth House could become the various distorted [[Lore:Ash Creature|ash creatures]]. Dagoth Ur was seen as a mad god who believed the ends justified the means and that he did right for the Dunmer people. An Ashlander wise woman described him as hearing laughter and love yet creating monsters and ghouls, and that he wooed as lover yet wrought fear and plague.{{Ref|name=Nibani|}} His blight and corprus was seen as a gift by him and his followers, the mutilation of the bodies of the Dunmer he viewed as them shedding the cursed skin brought to them by the Tribunal. In the dreams he invaded he would often speak to the dead as if they were alive and speak to the dreamer as a kind friend despite his motive to entrap them with his sorcery.{{ref|Dream dialogue during [[MW:Sleepers Awake|Sleepers Awake]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

[[File:LG-cardart-Sixth House Amulet.png|Sixth House Amulet|thumb|right]]
Dagoth Ur in the flesh never left his fortress of Red Mountain as he saw to personally guarding the Heart of Lorkhan. However, yet another of his powers was the ability to perceive and communicate through his cultists which he used as conduits for his motivations and voice, furthering his reach throughout Morrowind.{{ref|name=DUP|}} The Sixth House saw great expansion in the early years of the fourth century of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]. The old home base of House Dagoth, Kogoruhn was recaptured and occupied by trusted servants of Dagoth Ur. Smugglers organizations were exploited by Sixth House cultists to spread their influence, small cells of Sixth House cultists were implanted into every single town in Vvardenfell. Larger bases for the House were spread throughout remote dungeons where creatures were bred, and cultists were trained for what lay ahead. All the while further construction of Akulakhan was in motion.{{ref|name=DUP|}}

===Hour of Wakening===
Now possessing only one of Kagrenac's tools, the gauntlet [[Lore:Wraithguard|Wraithguard]], the Tribunal could not - dared not - even pass the bounds of their own Ghostfence to recapture the others. The Tribunal became strained with the war with Dagoth Ur, focused on protecting their people, but many thought that as gods they had become almost like Western gods: absent and silent, no longer among the public with a personal presence. Almalexia had been more concerned with Dagoth Ur and his threats for years, becoming a hard god in hard times, and Mournhold was no longer the "City of Love" it once was.{{Ref|name=GenericDialogueL|[[MW:Generic Dialogue L#Latest Rumors|Latest Rumors: Mournhold]] dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} As Sotha Sil and Almalexia withdrew from the world, Vivec stood alone in maintaining the Ghostfence, an effort that took so much of his energies he could not stir out of his palace, and his Temple's Ordinators ran out of his control and became ever more fanatical and rigid, more oppressive than protective, as the faith of the Dunmer wavered. Meanwhile, Dagoth Ur set about constructing [[Lore:Akulakhan|Akulakhan]], the Second [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]], a brass machine god he would use to conquer all of Tamriel.{{ref|name=DUP}} [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]] uncovered that the ultimate source of the Tribunal's divinity was likely the same as the source of Dagoth Ur's evil power, and Dagoth Ur's power seemed to wax as the Tribunal's power waned.{{Ref|name=GenericDialogueD}}{{FMI}}

===The Coming of the Nerevarine===
[[File:MW-place-Dagoth Ur.jpg|left|thumb|Dagoth Ur's fortress within Red Mountain]][[File:MW-creature-Dagoth Ur.jpg|Dagoth Ur (Morrowind)|right|thumb]]
All this was to change with the advent of the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] circa {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=MW|Events of [[MW:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} This outlander to Morrowind was the reincarnation of Nerevar prophesied by Azura who would return to rectify the dishonorable acts of his councilors.{{ref|name=NARM}} Knowing better than to dismiss the Goddess of Dusk and Dawn, both Vivec and Dagoth Ur prepared for the Nerevarine's arrival as best they could, though they did not know when, where, or by whom the prophecy would be fulfilled. The Nerevarine met with Vivec, who offered him advice concerning how to defeat Dagoth Ur, even though he knew that the Nerevarine's success, and the destruction of the Heart of Lorkhan, would mean the loss of his power as a demigod.{{ref|name=36L}}{{ref|name=PTDDU}} Dagoth Ur offered him power, and the chance to use Akulakhan to drive the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] out of Morrowind.{{ref|name=MFDU}} Nerevarine wasn't the only one Dagoth Ur contacted. [[Lore:Orvas Dren|Orvas Dren]], the kingpin of the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]], was also given an offer of cooperation. Dagoth offered him to crush the Empire and drive the foreigners from the Morrowind.{{Ref|name=MWOD|[[Morrowind:Orvas Dren|Orvas Dren]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The deception, treachery, and confrontation that Dagoth Ur encouraged among his followers left him unable to understand the Nerevarine; he could not deduce beforehand whether the Nerevarine sought to fight him or join him, even when the Nerevarine finally confronted him in the bowels of Red Mountain.{{ref|name=MFDU}}{{ref|name=DUDialogue|Dagoth Ur's dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Nerevarine ultimately severed Dagoth Ur's connection to the Heart of Lorkhan, thereby destroying Akulakhan. Dagoth Ur became mortal once more, losing his divinity. Although he was nonetheless a mighty sorcerer, the Nerevarine bested him in combat and brought a final end to the ancient evil.{{ref|name=TCotSH|Events of [[Morrowind:The Citadels of the Sixth House|The Citadels of the Sixth House]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

With Dagoth Ur and all his kin slain, the Nerevarine was hailed as a hero. Despite this, some heeded the Nerevarine to remember that, once before, Lord Nerevar thought Dagoth Ur and all his kin were dead, warning the hero to stay vigilant in case Dagoth Ur somehow ever returned.{{Ref|name=GenericDialogueD}}

===Influence in the Fourth Era===
{{Quote Box|''Dagoth Ur is dead. I hope we will no longer be troubled by his dreams. But I wonder, too, what the ghost of a god would be. And can a dead god dream?''|Hassour Zainsubani}}
[[File:SR-npc-Erden Relvel.jpg|thumb|left|Erden Relvel, priest of Dagoth Ur]]
Following the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] of {{Year|4E 5}}, widespread worship of the Tribunal ended as the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] guided the Dunmer people back to their worship of the three [[Lore:Reclamations|Reclamations]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} Cults of Tribunal worshippers continued to exist well into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], although these individuals were persecuted by the New Temple as heretics.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} One such cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]]. This group dedicated themselves to preserving the relics of the Tribunal Temple, including the {{Lore Link|Mask of Dagoth Ur}}. Prior to the temple's construction, the mask had been passed to {{Lore Link|Matriarch Drevlan}} by her predecessor, who warned her never to open the chest it was stored in.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Matriarch Drevlan's Note}}}} The Matriarch duly locked the chest in the temple armory, behind a portcullis which could only be raised in the presence of the masks of the three Tribunes.{{ref|name=PAN|{{Cite Book|Priest Arthamis' Note}}}}

However, some of Dagoth Ur's influence remained within the mask. A few of the temple priests were corrupted by the Sharmat's voice, which pounded in their heads like a stone heart.{{ref|name=PAN}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the Diviner {{Lore Link|Erden Relvel}} eventually managed to reach the mask when Dagoth Ur used his power to raise the portcullis, allowing Erden to escape with the artifact without alerting the Matriarch. From listening to his master's words, Erden became a priest of Dagoth Ur and learned how to spawn Corprus. He used this knowledge to create [[Lore:Ash Zombie|Ash Zombie]]s, transforming his fellow Temple priest Arthamis and others into unthinking minions of Dagoth Ur. He then sent these creatures to attack the temple at Ashfall's Tear. Ultimately, he hoped to spread his master's "cure" across the entire world.{{ref|name=ERF|{{Cite Book|Erden Relvel's Note}}}}

Knowing that the cult would eventually discover that the mask had been taken, Erden left a note in its place, summoning whoever possessed the three masks of the Tribunal to face him in combat. He believed that he needed to face the Tribunal's champion similar to how Dagoth Ur faced the Nerevarine.{{ref|name=ERF}} Wearing the Mask of Dagoth Ur and the [[Lore:Robe of the Lich|Robe of the Lich]], Erden awaited the arrival of this fated champion at the graveyard near [[Lore:Tel Mithryn|Tel Mithryn]], surrounded by his Ash Zombie minions. He also learned how to control the [[Lore:Ash Spawn|Ash Spawn]], strange beings animated by [[Lore:Heart Stone|Heart Stones]]. The [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] subsequently answered Erden's summons and defeated him, claiming the mask and robes.{{ref|[[SR:Ashen Heart|Ashen Heart]] quest in the [[SR:Ghosts of the Tribunal|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation]] for ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
