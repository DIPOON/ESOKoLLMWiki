[[Lore:Morwha|Morwha]], (also called the '''Teat God''',{{ref|name=VOF}} '''Lady Morwha''',{{ref|name=SLMTS}} and '''Desire's Root'''){{Ref|name=MDR|[[ON:Statuette: Morwha, Desire's Root|Statuette: Morwha, Desire's Root]] in {{g|ESO}}}} is the [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudan]] goddess of fertility and love. She is a fundamental deity in the Yokudan pantheon and is the favorite wife of [[Lore:Tall Papa|Tall Papa]]. Morwha is always portrayed as four-armed, so that she can 'grab more husbands'.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Her shrines are the shape of a beehive.{{ref|name=CM|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 6: Redguard Style}}}} She was described by potentate [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]] as the lusty fertility goddess of the Yokudans, but not as lusty as [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]].{{ref|name=SH|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Height (v7)}}}} She is sometimes depicted alongside her winged guardians,{{Ref|name=MB|[[ON:Morwha's Blessing|Morwha's Blessing]] in {{g|ESO}}}} or surrounded by flames and spears.{{ref|name=RGCompanion|{{Cite Book|The Redguard Companion|ns_base=Books}}}}<noinclude>

==Worship==
[[File:RG-place-Arkay Fertility Statue.jpg|Arkay's Fertility Statue, known as Morwha to the Redguards|thumb|right]]
Her priestesses are known to officiate and marry couples.{{ref|name=PoW|[[Online:Priestess of Morwha|Priestess of Morwha]] dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} She can be conflated with [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] of the divines, another goddess of fertility.{{ref|name=VOF}} There is a Morwha conservatorium in the city of [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]].{{ref|name=SH}} Visiting worshippers may place candles on her shrine.{{ref|name=CM}}

Ornate silver teaspoon sets of four are a way of honoring Morwha, one for each of her breasts.{{ref|name=SLMTS|{{Item Link|Silver "Lady Morwha" Teaspoon Set|id=74472|level=1|quality=3}} item description  in {{g|ESO}}}} Silver plates and ceramic milk pitchers adorned with Morwha imagery are common in Hammerfell.{{ref|name=BSP|{{Item Link|Bountiful Silver Plate|id=62307|level=1|quality=2}} item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MMP|{{Item Link|Morhwa's Milk Pitcher|id=61511|level=1|quality=3}} item description in {{g|ESO}}}} There is one known play involving Morwha, 'Lady Morwha's Latest Marriage'.{{ref|{{Item Link|Prince Hulajabad's Double Bosoms|id=74486|level=1|quality=3}} item description in {{g|ESO}}}}

Children in Hammerfell are known to play a game called Morwha's Grasp, which requires the use of one of the many statues of Morwha. Kids place the statue on a moving surface, such as a potter's wheel or on a greased plate or rock so that the statue can spin easily. One kid spins the statue, then runs away. The rest of the kids try to toss various rocks, rings, and so on and try to get Morwha to "catch" them. The first player to get an object in one of the Morwha's hands or hanging from her arms is the winner. Getting something wrapped around her head does not count. The priests of Morwha do not mind this game and even encourage it.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Games and Pastimes of Tamriel v. 3}}}}

A monument for healing called "[[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]]'s Fertility Statue" stands on the island of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]],{{ref|name=RGCompanion|{{Cite Book|The Redguard Companion|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|54}}{{ref|[[Redguard:Arkay Fertility Statue|Arkay's Fertility Statue]] in {{g|redguard}}}} where a [[Lore:Stros M'Kai#Locations|Temple of Arkay]] also stood,{{ref|[[Redguard:Temple of Arkay|Temple of Arkay]] in {{g|redguard}}}} but it is most likely a Redguard depiction of Morwha. This confusion is likely due to the fact that according to some sources the Bretic deity Arkay is mirrored in the Redguard pantheon as Morwha.{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=Pantheon|group=UOL|[[General:Pantheon|''Pantheon'']]}}
