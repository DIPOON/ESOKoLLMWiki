[[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]] is the goddess of the heavens, the winds, the sea, the elements, and the unseen spirits of the air.{{Ref|name=DC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Charmel Favraud|Charmel Favraud]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Light-Keeper Menoit|Light-Keeper Menoit]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She is a member of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] and Patron of sailors and travelers.{{Ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue K|Dialogue]] in {{g|Morrowind}}}} Kynareth is often invoked for auspicious stars at birth and for good fortune in daily life. In some legends, she is the first to agree to [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]'s divine plan to create the mortal plane, and provides the space in the void for its creation. Furthermore, she is associated with rain, a phenomenon that is said not to have occurred before the removal of Lorkhan's divine spark. Some sources describe [[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggans]] as creations of Kynareth.{{ref|name=Avita|[[Oblivion:Avita Vesnia|Avita Vesnia]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=Fury}}<noinclude>{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}<noinclude>

==Mythology==
The [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]] is described as being Kynareth's sacred mountain by the [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]], where she breathed life into mankind, and then taught them the thu'um.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Arngeir|Arngeir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Nords and Nedes say that Kyne was the wife of Shor and that upon his death her tears brought the first rain to the world.{{ref|name=TSOP2}}

Kynareth is the mother of the [[Lore:Demigod|demigod]] [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], she is also suspected to have communicated with [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]] through visions of [[Lore:Pelinal|Pelinal]]'s imminent arrival.{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}

==Worship==
===Nordic===
[[File:SR-concept-Hawk Mural.jpg|A depiction of Kyne|thumb|right]]
According to followers of the Divines, the Sky Goddess [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], worshipped by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] as the strongest of the Sky spirits and the widow of Shor, is the Nordic aspect of Kynareth. In Shor's absence following his death, Kyne acts as the head of the Nordic Pantheon, and her role as a warrior Goddess remains the strongest out of the rest of the Nordic Pantheon following Imperial syncretization.{{Ref|name=DATN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}} 

Kyne is credited with 'breathing life into the world' at the top of the Throat of the World, exhaling atop the mountain to create mankind.{{ref|name=COTS|{{Cite Book|Children of the Sky}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=Froki|[[Skyrim:Froki Whetted-Blade|Froki Whetted-Blade]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Rain is said to be Kyne's tears as she mourns the loss of her beloved [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], since rain is believed not to have appeared until after his divine spark was forcibly removed.{{Ref|name=VOF}} It is said Kyne gifted men with the [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]], either through her daughters or in tandem with [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]], so they could harness the [[Lore:Dragon Language|language of the dragons]] and use its power to save themselves from the wrath of [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] and the dragons.{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=Hrothgar|[[Skyrim:High Hrothgar|The Etched Tablets of High Hrothgar]]}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|330}} Kynareth has a temple in the city of [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]]; one of the duties of this temple is to tend the [[Lore:Gildergreen|Gildergreen]], which is considered sacred to Kynareth's followers.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Danica Pure-Spring|Danica Pure–Spring]]'s dialogue in {{g|skyrim}}}}

===Reachman===

After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Kynareth, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In the Fourth Era, the [[Lore:Forsworn|Forsworn]] Reachmen, who strongly opposed their invader's traditions, were observed gathering at the shrine of Kynareth. It remains uncertain whether they were actively worshiping her, however.{{ref|Events of [[Skyrim:Fort Sungard|Fort Sungard]] in {{g|skyrim}}}} In the Second Era, a Reachfolk group - [[Lore:Dreadhorn Clan|Dreadhorn Clan]] was known to use shamanistic Kyne-magic.{{ref|name=ESOLiveFALK|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live - Tour of Falkreath Hold‎|ESO Live - Tour of Falkreath Hold‎]]}}

===Imperial===
[[File:LG-cardart-Plea to Kynareth.png|A soldier pleading to Kynareth|right|thumb|150px]]
St. Alessia syncretized elements of the Elven and Nordic Pantheons, forming the Imperial interpretation of Kynareth.{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} 

Kynareth is often associated with [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]]. Craftsmen use natural materials to create, and so one cannot revere Zenithar without acknowledging the power of Kynareth.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Carodus Oholin|Carodus Oholin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} [[Lore:Canah|Canah]] feathers can be offered to Kynareth at her shrines; in return, the one making the offering will temporarily transform into a Canah.{{ref|[[Redguard:Shrines of Kynareth|Shrines of Kynareth]] in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

In contrast to the other Nine Divines, Kynareth has no known Chapel in the great cities of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=OBEvents|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Some of Kynareth's faithful have claimed that it is not right to build over nature, Kynareth's work, simply so one can worship her, and so have created altars to the goddess in the Cyrodiilic wilderness, instead.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Jesper|Jesper]]'s dialogue in {{g|kotn|Oblivion}}}}

===Breton===
[[File:ON-misc-Kynareth.png|A Bretonic statue of Kynareth (ESO)|thumb|right]]
The island of [[Lore:Betony|Betony]] is considered holy land of Kynareth. This was a factor for [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]]'s involvement in the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]]. It was also the reason why the conflict escalated to the [[Lore:Battle of Cryngaine Field|Battle of Cryngaine Field]], which ended the war.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|ns_base=BK}}}} Kynareth is the patron deity of [[Lore:Alcaire|Alcaire]], [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Alcaire|Alcaire]], [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall (Region)|Daggerfall]] and the [[Daggerfall:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Saint [[Lore:Stental|Stental]] was a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] [[Lore:Breton#Saints|saint]] who is best remembered for slaying the demon of Overlook Hill, rescuing the three Sisters of Kynareth from a terrible fate. A memorial, named "The Saint Slays the Leaper", was erected in [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]] to honor his courage in {{Year|2E 388}}, which depicts Stental armed with a sword facing the [[Lore:Leaper|Leaper]] demon, depicted as a crouched gargoyle.{{ref|name="Stental"|{{Cite Book|Saint Stental}}}}{{ref|name=Statue|Appearance of [[:File:ON-statue-Saint Stental 02.png|statue]] near [[Online:Dreughside|Dreughside]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Kynareth is an important Divine in [[Lore:Gonfalon Bay|Gonfalon Bay]]. To the locals, she is the patron of sailors and fisherfolk. Every couple of weeks, people celebrate an informal holiday to Kynareth called Angler's Day. Taverns will sell fish-based food at a discount, like Hundred-Year Bisque or Grilled Sweet and Sour [[Lore:Sea Adder|Sea Adder]]. Meanwhile, anglers can drink for free in whatever establishment they can find.{{Ref|name=LABHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}} The Gonfalon Head Lighthouse is even sacred to the Goddess of Winds and is tended to by an [[Lore:Temple of Kynareth|order of priests]].{{Ref|name=VGTHI|{{Cite Book|Visitor's Guide to High Isle}}}}

==Artifacts==
===Boots of the Crusader===
[[File:OBR-item-Boots of the Crusader.jpg|right|thumb|150px|Boots of the Crusader]]
The Boots of the Crusader (also known as the {{Anchor|Boots of Kynareth}}) were created by Kynareth. The wearer will not be attacked by creatures of the forest. Sir Juncan of the original Knights died while searching for the boots. The pilgrim visited the Shrine of Kynareth, and was told the location of the "Grove of Trials" by the high priestess (later to become Sir Avita Vesnia). Kynareth tested the pilgrim by sending the Forest Guardian, a giant [[Lore:Bear|bear]], to attack them. When the pilgrim didn't fight back, having reverence for nature, Kynareth allowed them entrance to the grotto where the boots were hidden. The boots were needed to retrieve the Mace of the Crusader; with them, the wearer's faith is strengthened and they can cross the void of doubt.{{ref|name=Fury|[[OB:Nature's Fury|Nature's Fury]] quest in {{g|kotn|Oblivion}}}}

==Holy Sites==
* [[Lore:Eldergleam|Eldergleam Sanctuary]] - A cave containing the Eldergleam, an ancient tree believed to be older than metal or Men and Elves, which can only be harmed through Old Magic. A pilgrimage site, and guarded by [[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggans]].{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Danica Pure-Spring|Danica Pure-Spring]]'s dialogue}} 
* Temple of Kynareth in [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] - In the adjacent plaza is the Gildergreen, a cutting of the Eldergrleam, believed to be sacred to Kynareth and a destination for pilgrims. 
* [[Lore:Kynesgrove|Kynesgrove]] - The woods above Kynesgrove are considered sacred, and thus are protected against woodcutting and forestry. 
* [[Lore:Shrine of Kynareth|Kynareth's Shrine]] in the Great Forest - Located in the Great Forest, as it is believed by those in Cyrodiil that a temple of stone would displease Kynareth.
* [[Lore:Betony|Isle of Betony]] - The island of Betony in the middle of the Iliac Bay is considered sacred to Kynareth, and her temple on the island also functioned as an insane asylum, where the former Emperor Pelagius the Third was interred. The sacred nature of the island was one of the contributing factors of the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]].
