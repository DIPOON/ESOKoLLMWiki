[[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]], the '''Multiplier of Motions Known''', is the scion of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and rules over the pocket realm of [[Lore:Maelstrom|Maelstrom]], hence he is known as the  '''[[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] of Maelstrom'''.{{ref|name=ONFNH|[[Online:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} The demiprince is known for his sword-teachings, and he is said to haunt the warriors who fell and still wonder why.{{ref|name=Sermon1|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 1}}}} He takes the form of a demonic [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] for strategic purposes, specifically to be both a nimble and a smaller target.{{ref|name=AMB1}}{{ref|name=AMB2}} His known wardrobe includes armor resembling that worn by the [[Lore:Aureal|Aureal]], and a [[Lore:Daedric Equipment|Daedric]] helmet known as the [[Lore:Flame Monarch's Crown|Flame Monarch's Crown]], which he supposedly wore when he visited [[Lore:Infernace|Infernace]].{{ref|name=FMC|[[Blades:Flame Monarch's Crown|Flame Monarch's Crown]] description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
According to his tutor and minion Riparius, Fa-Nuit-Hen existed "since before time began".{{ref|name=ONTF}} In regards to who sired him, Fa-Nuit-Hen stated that he is a scion of Boethiah, hence his natural disposition to teaching fighting motions.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2}} Despite this, the information broker Madam Whim believes that the demiprince did not actually originate from Boethiah, while also stating that "Boethiah supports their claim of scionship". In the same interview, Whim would describe how Daedric Princes have been known to adopt scions in the past.{{Ref|name=LAMM|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} While discussing the meaning of Fa-Nuit-Hen's name, Whim presented her preferred explanation that the demiprince may have taken the moniker in an effort to ensure a well welcome by the chosen people of Boethiah, the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]. Whim also dismissed the theory of his name being based on the [[Lore:Thu'um|art of Tongues]] from the [[Lore:Dragon Language|dragon language]] and discounted some of the adventures the demiprince claims he had as possibly jokes or half-truths.{{Ref|name=LAMM}}

Some of Fa-Nuit-Hen's adventures happened at an unspecified time. His victory in defeating the Five Recalcitrants alongside the Baron Who Moves Like Light Glittering Through Crystal left the ground "littered with fangs and antlers". The Baron Who Moves Like Heat Lightning fought alongside him and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] against the Borers from Within and The Hinged Ones.{{ref|name=AMB2}} Fa-Nuit-Hen has also collected "the known motions of every martial discipline in Nirn", the earliest being the "Prismatic Vector Dance of the Ehlnofey".{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}} He even obtained some of his motions from observing the god [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]] making way.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR}} The demiprince is said to capable of splitting lightning, and severing rain from a thundercloud.{{ref|name=MTCFNH|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Fa-Nuit-Hen}}}}

===First Era===
The first sermon of [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|''The Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec'']] tells of sometime before the [[Lore: First Empire of the Nords|Skyrim Conquests]], when Fa-Nuit-Hen appeared to a [[Lore:Velothi|Velothi]] tribeswoman, the wife of a Chimer netchiman, who had been impregnated with an egg by the [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh]] before being brought to [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] by Sotha Sil. The egg contained the unborn [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]], and seven [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] known as the Barons Who Move Like This appeared before the woman to teach the egg new swordsmanship motions. When Fa-Nuit-Hen appeared, he told the netchiman's wife to seek the [[Lore:Nerevar|Hortator]] in the land of [[Lore:Indoril|Indoril]] before combining with the Barons to form a terrible pillar of fighting styles and teaching Vivec.{{ref|name=Sermon1}} 

The first sermon holds a contradiction, however, as Vivec reportedly predates Sotha Sil, who he saved when [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] wiped out [[Lore:House Sotha|House Sotha]] at in the town [[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]]. As the sole survivor of his House, Sotha Sil would then be raised by Lord Vivec, and would eventually ascend to becoming known as the Clockwork God.{{ref|name=Saryoni|[[Morrowind:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=ABHoAS|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of Ald Sotha}}}} Fa-Nuit-Hen himself verifies the sermon's claims to a degree, stating that he did in fact meet Vivec in his youth, and made an impression on him.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} Fa-Nuit-Hen has also elaborated on the lesson he gave to Vivec within the first sermon, supporting that the teaching had occurred.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2}}

Fa-Nuit-Hen would later befriend the [[Lore:Demigod|demigod]] [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], with whom he would "drink and dice together",{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR}} and then go on to "lay waste to the beasts of the land".{{ref|name=MTCFNH}}

===Second Era===
Fa-Nuit-Hen's barons would later adventure outside the realm, with some opting to serve other masters, but would subsequently return to him. As he is a "collector of martial styles, a combat connoisseur, [and an] arms trainer to the great and lofty", he asked his Barons to use their new experiences to help build the Maelstrom Arena. Unfortunately for Fa-Nuit-Hen, this endeavor would cost him, as his barons tried to outdo each other, pouring too much of their essence and animus into creating their respective sections of the arena that they ceased to exist.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} This was especially costly to him due to the fact that demiprinces are specialized in a certain area, leaving weaknesses in other parts. In the case of Fa-Nuit-Hen, while he was gifted in martial styles, his memory was weak, leading to his creation of Tutor Riparius, who served as an intermediary for his memory.{{ref|name=ONTF|[[Online:Tutor Riparius|Tutor Riparius]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} Slowly, the Demiprince would lose his memories of his Barons, potentially leaving nothing but their arenas to remember them by.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} 
[[File:LO-book-Fa-Nuit-Hen.jpg|thumb|left|The Demiprince inside the Bedlam Veil|class=backdrop]]
The Maelstrom Arena would still hold competitions regardless of the Demiprince's fading memory, claiming thousands of lives.{{ref|[[Online:Maelstrom Arena (place)|Maelstrom Arena]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} Circa, {{Year|2E 582}}, a [[Lore:Soulless One|champion]] discovered the whereabouts of the Maelstrom Arena's entrance, and entered Fa-Nuit-Hen's realm. Inside, the champion was well known by the Demiprince, whose reputation preceded them. The hero defeated all the Arena's combatants, leading to the restoration of the demiprince's memories. While his memories remained intact, he would restore his barons to their intended state, and would continue to build his Maelstrom Army to one day conquer the Aurbis.{{ref|name=ONMAQ|[[Online: Maelstrom Arena (quest)|Maelstrom Arena]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

Vivec once uttered his old master's name in battle with [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]], when he performed eight sword strokes so quickly that they appeared without mortal notice.{{ref|name=Vivecmeeting|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Lord Vivec's Sword-Meeting With Cyrus the Restless}}}}

==Barons of Move Like This==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none;|''The Barons are Fa-Nuit-Hen, and the Demiprince is them. The Motions Known they mention in their title are the Baron themselves, personified within the Demiprince and given internal and (eventually) external life.''|Madam Whim{{Ref|name=LAMM}}}}
The '''Barons of Move Like This''' (also called the '''Barons-Who-Move-Like-This''' or '''Barons Who Move Like This''') are paragons of the martial arts styles of Oblivion. They are also psycho-magical projections from the mind of Daedric Demiprince Fa-Nuit-Hen, his concept of perfect masters of combat.{{ref|[[Online:Maelstrom Baron|Maelstrom Baron]] polymorph description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Known barons include:
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like a Dancer's Hips''' - Known to have spent time with [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]. They created Seht's Balcony.{{ref|name=ONFNH}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like Flickering Flame''' - Known to have been one of Fa-Nuit-Hen's favorite barons. He created the Spiral Shadows, inspired by the [[Lore:Spiral Skein|Spiral Skein]].{{ref|name=ONFNH}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like Heat Lightning''', also known as the '''Scorching Storm'''{{Ref|name=MOTSS|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|In Memoriam of the Scorching Storm}}}} - Known to have visited the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] and fought beside Seht and Fa-Nuit-Hen when they battled the Borers from Within. Seht and Fa-Nuit-Hen also dueled The Hinged Ones while Heat Lightning overcharged the Null Pistons until they cracked and burst.{{ref|name=AMB2|{{Cite Book|A Memory Book, Part 2}}}} He created Seht's Flywheel.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} A memorial plaque in his honor was housed within Bedlam Veil, specifically in the Hall of Barons.{{Ref|name=MOTSS}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like Light Glittering Through Crystal''' or '''Light-Through-Crystal''' - Known to be obsessed with focus, with it being described as his reason for being. In battle, he moves from point to point almost instantaneously, scintillating from front to flank to other flank, his attacks flashing out like malondo-rays.{{ref|name=AMB1|{{Cite Book|A Memory Book, Part 1}}}} He created the Skein Grotto, and had an "unwholesome fascination" with [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]].{{Ref|name=CC|group=UOL|''[[ON:Cut Content#Orsinium|ESO Cut Content]]'' - [[ON:Maelstrom Arena (place)|Maelstrom Arena]] dialogue}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like a Shivering Droplet''' - Known to have served the [[Lore:Sheogorath|Mad God]] for a time. He created the Vale of the Surreal.{{ref|name=ONFNH}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like a Smoking Plume''', also known as the '''Ashy Wind'''{{Ref|name=MOTAW|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|In Memoriam of the Ashy Wind}}}} - Known to be a great admirer of Mehrunes Dagon, having mastered the martial knowledge of the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]]. He created the Theater of Despair.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} A memorial plaque in his honor was housed within Bedlam Veil, specifically in the Hall of Barons.{{Ref|name=MOTAW}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like Sunlight on Waves''', also known as the '''Salt Spray'''{{Ref|name=MOTSSP|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|In Memoriam of the Salt Spray}}}} - Known to have created the Rink of Frozen Blood.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} A memorial plaque in his honor was housed within Bedlam Veil, specifically in the Hall of Barons.{{Ref|name=MOTSSP}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like a Torrent in Spate''' - Known to have created the Igneous Cistern.{{ref|name=ONFNH}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like a Wary Moth''', also known as the '''Carver of Shadows'''{{Ref|name=MOTCOS|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|In Memoriam of the Carver of Shadows}}}} - Known to have created the Vault of Umbrage.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} A memorial plaque in his honor was housed within Bedlam Veil, specifically in the Hall of Barons.{{Ref|name=MOTCOS}}
*'''The Baron Who Moves Like Wind in the Reeds''', also known as the '''Gentle Warrior'''{{Ref|name=MOTGW|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|In Memoriam of the Gentle Warrior}}}} - Said to have a gentle soul. He created the Drome of Toxic Shock.{{ref|name=ONFNH}} A memorial plaque in his honor was housed within Bedlam Veil, specifically in the Hall of Barons.{{Ref|name=MOTGW}}
