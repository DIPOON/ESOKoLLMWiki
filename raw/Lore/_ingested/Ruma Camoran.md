[[Lore:Ruma Camoran|Ruma Camoran]] was a powerful [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] mage and the daughter of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]. Along with her brother, [[Lore:Raven Camoran|Raven]], she served as a lieutenant in their father's cult,{{Ref|name=RC|[[OB:Raven Camoran|Raven Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=RC2|[[Oblivion:Ruma Camoran|Ruma Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]]—a group of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] worshippers responsible for the assassination of [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] in {{Year|3E 433}} as part of their attempt to bring about the destruction of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{Ref|name=MC|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} As a daughter of Mankar Camoran and therefore likely a granddaughter of [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]] she had ties to the royal [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}}{{Ref|name=TOC|{{Cite Book|The Oblivion Crisis}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:OB-npc-Raven Camoran.jpg|thumb|right|Raven Camoran, Ruma's brother]]
===''My first Daughter ran from the Dagonite road''===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Endeth we seek through our Dawn, all endeth. Falter now and become one with the wayside orphans that feed me. Follow and I shall adore you from inside. My first daughter ran from the Dagonite road. Her name was Ruma and I ate her with no bread, and made another, which learned, and I loved that one and blackbirds formed her twin behind all time.''|''[[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3|Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes Book Three]]'' by Mankar Camoran{{Ref|name=MDC3}}}}

Little is known about Ruma Camoran's past and origins. She was identified as the daughter of Mankar Camoran, leader of the Mythic Dawn cult.{{Ref|name=RC2}} Her name is derived from part of the phrase "AE ALMA RUMA" found in her father's ''Commentaries''.{{ref|name=MDC3}} It also bears similarity to the name of the [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] of Alma Ruma.{{ref|name=STOAR|[[ON:Scroll Temple of Alma Ruma|Scroll Temple of Alma Ruma]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The ''[[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Commentaries]]'' suggest that the nature of her and her brother Raven's creation was a more metaphysical event. According to them Mankar Camoran's first daughter chose not to follow in his footsteps in worshipping Merhrunes Dagon, and so Mankar cannibalized her and "made another", who embraced the worship of Dagon and thus earned his love. She was thus remade after initially rejecting the Dagonite path, and then "[[Lore:Blackbird|blackbirds]] formed her twin behind all time".{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} According to some sources Ruma gave birth to herself, and her father was the father. She also gave birth to her brother, but he was not her son.{{Ref|name=MK|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride's Posts|Michael Kirkbride's Posts]]}}

===Member of the Cult of Mythic Dawn===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''The Dawn is breaking''|''[[Lore:Jearl's Orders|Jearl's Orders]]'' by Ruma Camoran{{Ref|name=JO|{{Cite Book|Jearl's Orders}}}}}}

Ruma was an active member of the cult by 3E 433, the final year of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]. She was present at the cult's lair near [[Lore:Lake Arrius|Lake Arrius]], preparing to oversee the initiation, which involved the life sacrifice of Jeelius, an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] priest from the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]].{{Ref|name=RC2}}{{Ref|name=Jeelius|[[OB:Jeelius|Jeelius]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} During this time, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] infiltrated the cult's hideout and stole the Mysterium Xarxes from their possession. The details surrounding the events are unclear, including how the Hero managed to acquire the book.{{Ref|name=RC2}}{{Ref|name=DS|Events of [[OB:Dagon Shrine|Dagon Shrine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} There were bardic tales that suggested the Hero used trickery and disguise, but this was believed to be pure speculation.{{Ref|name=TOC}} What is certain, however, is that Ruma was killed by the Hero of Kvatch.{{ref|name=Eldamil}}

She also oversaw the cult's spy activities in [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] and operations related to the opening of the [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|Oblivion Gates]]. Ruma gave the order to open the Oblivion Gate near Bruma. One of her agents, Jearl, was instructed by Ruma to spy on the [[Lore:Cloud Ruler Temple|Cloud Ruler Temple]] alongside Saveri Faram, in preparation for an attack on Bruma.{{Ref|name=JO}}{{ref|name=Jearl|[[OB:Jearl|Jearl]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=Spies|Events of [[OB:Spies|Spies]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

[[File:OBR-npc-Ruma Camoran.jpg|Ruma Camoran (Oblivion Remastered)|thumb|left]]
Regardless, Ruma eventually returned to [[Lore:Paradise|Paradise]], granted a form of [[Lore:Immortality|immortality]] as long as her father remained alive. Mankar's followers would reform in the realm if they ever died, wherever their death took place.{{ref|name=RC2}}{{ref|name=Eldamil|[[OB:Eldamil|Eldamil]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} There, she ultimately faced off against the Hero of Kvatch, fighting alongside her brother and father. She met her end when Mankar Camoran was slain and Paradise began to crumble.{{Ref|name=RC}}{{Ref|name=Paradise|Events of [[Oblivion:Paradise (quest)|Paradise]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
