[[Lore:Torval|Torval]] is one of the eight major cities in the province of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Elsweyr towns.png|Map of Elsweyr – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} and is situated in the dense jungles of the [[Lore:Tenmar Forest|Tenmar Forest]]. Torval is the seat of power for the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]], the spiritual and temporal leader of the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. Their tribe lives with them in exotic palaces and symmetrical [[Lore:Moon Sugar|moon sugarcane]] gardens.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}} The city has a long history associated with the Mane, going back to its time as one of the sixteen kingdoms.{{Ref|name=TSK|{{Cite Book|The Sixteen Kingdoms}}}} In modern-day, Torval is known for its southern wines.{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Torval|Torval]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-flora-Moon Sugar Cane.jpg|thumb|rihgt|Moon Sugarcanes]]
Torval is located on the southwestern corner of Elsweyr,{{Ref|name=LOMapEL|[[:File:LO-map-Elsweyr (PGE1).jpg|Map of Elsweyr – Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}} near the Valenwood border, which is only a few hundred miles away.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} It is part of the Kingdom of [[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]],{{Ref|name=LOMapEL}}{{Ref|name=CM74|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 74: Pellitine Style}}}} the southern region of Elsweyr that is defined by its jungles and rainforests.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} The Torval region has a damp climate{{Ref|name=CM74}} and the earth around the city contains [[Lore:Ochre|ochre]], a natural clay earth pigment. Torval Royal Ochre, in particular, is a rare and valuable dye.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresOchre|[[Online:Treasures B#Ball of Royal Ochre Mohair Yarn|Ball of Royal Ochre Mohair Yarn]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Mane and their tribe live in large stately palaces made from massive timber from Valenwood [[Lore:Oak|oak]]. Around these palaces are symmetrical sugarcane gardens, where the Mane often meditates in his palanquin.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Torval is neighbored by several settlements, including Darvulk Haven to the far southeast, Portneu View to the northeast, and Valenwood to the west,{{Ref|name=ARMap}} toward Cormeir Spring and [[Lore:Haven|Haven]].{{Ref|name=ARMapVW|[[:File:AR-map-Valenwood towns.png|Map of Valenwood – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

==History==
===Early History===
Like the other sixteen kingdoms of ancient Elsweyr, Torval started as one of the original clans of nomadic Khajiit, all of whom excelled in a trade or discipline and swore fealty to the [[Lore:Moon-Emperor|Moon-Emperor]].{{Ref|name=TSK}}{{Ref|name=AAPAI|{{Cite Book|Anequina and Pellitine: An Introduction}}}} Torval was defined by its worship of the Mane.{{Ref|name=TSK}}{{Ref|name=SOK|{{Cite Book|Song of Kingdoms (lyrics)}}}} As these clans mastered their roles, they pushed themselves into defined geographical areas and the concept of tribal regions was established from there. This is how the idea of sixteen kingdoms came to fruition.{{Ref|name=AAPAI}} Before they were full-fledged kingdoms, however, they were petty fiefdoms ruled by a cruel hunter-aristocracy helmed by the Hunt-Lords.{{Ref|name=OOTKMT|{{Cite Book|Origins of the Khajiiti Martial Tradition}}}}

The [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] was a tumultuous time for the Khajiit as their society was at the heel of these Hunt-Lords. The [[Lore:Temple of Two-Moons Dance|Temples of Two-Moons Dance]] were the only ones to check them as Khajiiti adepts and Torval clergies held significant cultural influence. Despite the Hunt-Lords' attempt to oppress the people, their efforts were proven futile as the Khajiit developed the modern-day [[Lore:Claw-Dances|Claw-Dances]] and rebelled. Over the course of a thousand years, the hunter's caste system was abolished all over ancient Elsweyr and these fiefdoms became kingdoms.{{Ref|name=OOTKMT}} Throughout history, these people lived in harmony among each other and their dominance was determined by the phases of the moons. When the moons were both halves, Torval was at its peak in power.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Sugar and Blood}}}}

In {{Year|1E 2200}},{{Ref|name=SH3|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 3}}}} the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] led to the dissolution of the sixteen kingdoms, including Torval.{{Ref|name=AAPAI}} The clans of the south gathered in [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]] to the east, where the Khajiit were already able to adapt and the moon-priests of the [[Lore:Pa'alatiin Clan|Pa'alatiin Clan]] guiding them through the hardship. With Senchal as their capital, southern Elsweyr became known as the Kingdom of [[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]] and distinguished themselves from the nomads of the [[Lore:Anequina|north]].{{Ref|name=HOPAO|{{Cite Book|History of Pellitine: An Overview}}}}

===Torval in the Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-Lord Gharesh-ri 02.jpg|thumb|right|Lord Gharesh-ri]]
[[File:ON-npc-Mane Akkhuz-ri 03.jpg|thumb|left|Akkhuz-ri, the Mane]]
In the mid-Second Era, the Mane was [[Lore:Akkhuz-ri|Akkhuz-ri]] and his advisor, the Speaker of the Mane was Lord [[Lore:Gharesh-ri|Gharesh-ri]], both of whom had known each other since childhood.{{Ref|name=ONGharesh|[[Online:Lord Gharesh-ri|Lord Gharesh-ri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|2E 560}}, the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] had broken out from Black Marsh{{Ref|name=TEATC|{{Cite Book|The Eagle and the Cat}}}} and five years later, had reached Elsweyr, specifically Senchal.{{Ref|name=HOSAO|{{Cite Book|History of Senchal: An Overview}}}} 

It did not take long for the flu to spread all over Elsweyr, including Torval. As people across the province slowly found ways to mitigate the disease, Lord Gharesh-ri assembled what was left of the [[Lore:Mane's Legion|Mane's Legion]] to maintain order and use their knowledge to the people's benefit. Queen Ayrenn offered assistance to the Khajiit and in order to fight a common enemy, both factions decided to come together.{{Ref|name=TEATC}}

Akkhuz-ri and Gharesh-ri were present in {{Year|2E 580}} when the signing of the [[Lore:Elden Accord|Elden Accord]] took place between leaders of the First [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]].{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn|[[Online:Queen Ayrenn#The Moonlit Path|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue during the quest, "[[Online:The Moonlit Path|The Moonlit Path]]" in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Gharesh-ri signed the Elden Accord and thus established Elsweyr as a member of the trans-provincial alliance.{{Ref|name=ONGharesh}} He became a member of the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] Inner Council; advisors that Queen Ayrenn had the utmost trust in.{{Ref|name=AOSAYQ|{{Cite Book|Aicantar of Shimmerene Answers Your Questions}}}} Meanwhile, other members of the Thalmor were figureheads,{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ZeniMaxInterview|[[General:ESO Writing Team AUA|ESO Writing Team r/elderscrollsonline Ask Us Anything]]}} such as Lady Radurra-dra of Torval, who represented southwestern Elsweyr.{{Ref|name=KH|{{Cite Book|Khajiiti Honorifics}}}}

Two years later in {{Year|2E 582}}, Akkhuz-ri became possessed by the [[Lore:Dark Mane|Dark Mane]] and rampaged across [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]] to the north. When the two were separated in [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]], Akkhuz-ri relinquished his title as Mane and left it between the twins, Khali and Shazah. One of the twins became the new Mane in the Temple of the Dance in [[Lore:Dune|Dune]], while the other took on the immortal task of bearing the Dark Mane's mantle and sealed away in [[Lore:Moonmont|Moonmont]].{{Ref|name=ONReaper|[[Online:Reaper's March#Quest Map|Reaper's March]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|2E 864}}, the Mane was [[Lore:Nhad-hatta|Nhad-hatta]], who ruled during [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s conquests of the late Second Era. An Imperial diplomat arranged a meeting with the Mane in his palace in Torval but was denied entry by the guards.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

===Torval in the Third Era===
By around {{Year|3E 80}}, Torval underwent a rebellion,{{Ref|name=TWQ1|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v1}}}} one of many that plagued Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim II|Uriel Septim II]]'s reign.{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}} He brokered a deal with the King of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] at the time, [[Lore:Mantiarco|Mantiarco]] who donated millions of gold in exchange for a bride, thus establishing an alliance with the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]]. The Emperor agreed and the money was used to quell the rebellion in Torval.{{Ref|name=TWQ1}} Later in time, Uriel II's great-grandson, [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius III]] was moved across Tamriel to different healing institutions and asylums to relieve him of his [[Lore:Madness (disease)|madness]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE2|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 2}}}} The Emperor's visit to the asylum in Torval was adapted into a story written by the famed author [[Lore:Waughin Jarth|Waughin Jarth]] called ''"[[Lore:The Asylum Ball|The Asylum Ball]]"''. Pelagius III decides to hold a lavish ball in the asylum, and the old compound became a beautifully decorated ballroom with an orchestra in the pit. After the event, Pelagius III was moved to another location in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] and reportedly died three months later{{Ref|name=TAB}} in {{Year|3E 153}}.{{Ref|name=BHOTE2}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Torval was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Mojtabe and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Corinth|Corinth]].{{Ref|name=AR}} These ten years were marked with conflicts between the provinces, and Elsweyr was caught amidst the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]] against Valenwood to the west. How this event started is up to debate but the Khajiit felt it began with the so-called [[Lore:Slaughter of Torval|Slaughter of Torval]], where the Bosmer invaded the city and killed thousands, only to fall back after Khajiiti reinforcements intervened.{{Ref|name=MUT|{{Cite Book|Mixed Unit Tactics}}}} In a map of capital cities across the provinces circa {{Year|3E 427}}, Torval is listed among them in Elsweyr.{{Ref|name=MCMap|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Tamriel]] – [[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex|Morrowind Codex]]}}

===Known Rulers===
{{Quote Box|''The King and His Court come down the road<br>The Sacred Mane of Torval<br>Servants of hirsute majesty<br>The loyal guards of Torval''|Excerpt from ''[[Lore:The Sixteen Kingdoms|The Sixteen Kingdoms]]''}}
:''For a list of Manes, see the "previous manes" section in the following article: '''[[Lore:Mane#Previous Manes|Mane]]'''''.
For the longest time, Torval was ruled by a religious figure known as the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} While [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]] is acknowledged as the first Mane,{{Ref|name=ONShazah|[[Online:Shazah|Shazah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} that title has long been associated with Torval since its time as one of the sixteen kingdoms.{{Ref|name=TSK}} In the traditional Khajiiti child's chant, ''[[Lore:The Sixteen Kingdoms|The Sixteen Kingdoms]]'', Torval's ruler is referred to as the Sacred Mane of Torval.{{Ref|name=TSK|{{Cite Book|The Sixteen Kingdoms}}}} By the Third Era, Torval was ruled by a King/Queen.{{Ref|name=AR}}

The Mane employs an advisor and political representative called the Speaker of the Mane, who governs in their stead.{{Ref|name=ONShazah}} The Mane is also guarded by an elite force of Khajiiti warriors called the [[Lore:Bi-Lunar Guard|Bi-Lunar Guard]], who mount themselves on Black Mane [[Lore:Lion|lions]]{{Ref|name=ONMane}} and heavily guard the main premises.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-place-Temple of Two-Moons Dance.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Temples of Two-Moons Dance]]
[[File:ON-crown store-Black Mane Lion.jpg|thumb|left|The Black Mane Lion]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Khajiit#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Khajiit#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Khajiit page.''
The Temple of Two-Moons Dance in Torval is considered the best place in Tamriel to train in [[Lore:Hand-to-hand|hand-to-hand]] combat, and its masters have taught people from all over the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]]. They teach everything from ancient to modern techniques, and their students have become famous. One such example was [[Lore:Taren Omothan|Taren Omothan]],{{Ref|name=MZT|{{Cite Book|Master Zoaraym's Tale}}}} a monk and master trainer who took residence in [[Lore:Holamayan|Holamayan]] in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{Ref|name=MWTaren|[[Morrowind:Taren Omothan|Taren Omothan]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} When the time came for students to graduate and move on from the temple, a grand festival of celebration commences, in which debates and competitions are held.{{Ref|name=MZT}} 

While the Temple of Two-Moons Dance are led by the [[Lore:Lunar Clergy|lunar clergy]], the [[Lore:Torval Curiata|Torval Curiata]] are a sub-faction that enforces the epiphanies of [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar]] and fight heretics that oppose it.{{Ref|name=ONSabannar|[[Online:Inquisitor Sabannar|Inquisitor Sabannar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Torval, the people use ritual incense burners made out of [[Lore:Bronze|bronze]] and with moon-sugar canes.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresCenser|[[Online:Treasures T#Torval Ritual Censer|Torval Ritual Censer]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Although Torval is known for wines,{{Ref|name=AR}} other brands of [[Lore:Alcoholic Beverages|alcohol]] are exported from the city, such as brandy,{{Ref|name=ONBrandy|[[Online:Dragonhold Quest Items#Torval Brandy|Torval Brandy]] quest item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} and [[Lore:Mead|mead]].{{Ref|name=F2}} There is an active market in Torval and nearby Senchal for relics from the [[Lore:Anequine Conquests|Anequine Conquests]], especially artifacts from [[Lore:Darloc Brae|Darloc Brae]]'s time.{{Ref|name=ONLoadKuna|[[Online:Kuna's Delve|Kuna's Delve]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} By the waning years of the Third Era, the Gold Cat of Senchal, Ya'Tirrje had a drug-smuggling business in Torval, among other noteworthy cities.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

==Notes==
*The name "Torval" is an alternate name for [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]],{{ref|name=Opsius|[[OB:Decentius Opsius|Decentius Opsius]]' dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] explorer who fully charted the sea lanes of Tamriel. He was active in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], when he first documented the "cat demons" which are speculated to be the ancient Khajiit.{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father of the Niben}}}} It is possible that the city of Torval was named after Topal the Pilot.
*''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Torval's gladiatorial team would have been called "[[Lore:Fire Drakes|the Firedrakes]]".{{Ref|group=UOL|[http://www.imperial-library.info/content/go-blades Original TES: Arena texts at the Imperial Library]}}
