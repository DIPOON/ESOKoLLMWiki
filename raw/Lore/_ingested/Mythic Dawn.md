==History==
===Origins===
When exactly the Mythic Dawn cult came into existence is unknown, but their prominence can supposedly be traced back to the early days of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=OBTarMeena|[[Oblivion:Tar-Meena|Tar-Meena]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Oblivion Crisis===
The cult carried out an assassination plot against Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] and his heirs in {{Year|3E 433}}. Their deaths brought about the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], which resulted in a [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] invasion of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] and the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{ref|name=Oblivion}}{{ref|name=TOC|{{Cite Book|The Oblivion Crisis}}}} The assassination of the Emperor was intended to break the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|covenant]] which protected Nirn from [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] and launch a Daedric invasion by Mehrunes Dagon into Tamriel. With each [[Lore:Magical Transportation|Oblivion Gate]] that opened, the [[Lore:Dragonfires|barriers]] between [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and Oblivion weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [[Lore:Astronomy|Firmament]], would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The Mythic Dawn was led by the "madman" [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], whose son Raven and daughter Ruma were his key lieutenants; all three were slain by the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]].{{ref|name=TOC}}{{ref|name=ParadiseQuest|Events of [[Oblivion:Paradise (quest)|Paradise]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} The Mythic Dawn had many sleeper agents posing as regular townsfolk scattered throughout Tamriel, as well as a shrine to Mehrunes Dagon hidden in a cavern near [[Lore:Lake Arrius|Lake Arrius]] that served as their base.{{ref|name=TOC}}

===Latter Days===
In the time following the Oblivion Crisis' conclusion, numerable groups would form, pledging to eradicate the Mythic Dawn remnants from Tamriel.{{Ref|name=TKOTR}}

One such militia group, seemingly operating in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], led by Lenne of [[Lore:Clan Axe-Bearer|Clan Axe-Bearer]] had somehow come to discover and acquire a Daedric Artifact of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], the [[Lore:Mehrunes' Razor|Mehrunes' Razor]]. It was then that the militia was renamed to the [[Lore:Keepers of the Razor|Keepers of the Razor]]. It was decided that the dagger would be disassembled into three pieces, and be kept under guard by the Keepers' three highest members of the order's inner circle. This burden would be passed down through the generations, from descendant to descendant.{{Ref|name=TKOTR|{{Cite Book|The Keepers of the Razor}}}} 

Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, in times contemporary to the [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Stormcloak Rebellion]], a museum dedicated to Mythic Dawn cult had been opened in the Skyrim city of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]] by [[Lore:Silus Vesuius|Silus Vesuius]], whose own ancestors had been Mythic Dawn cultists.{{ref|name=SRPieces|Events of [[Skyrim:Pieces of the Past|Pieces of the Past]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Silus commissioned the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] to recover the pieces from the Keepers' descendants. And the Mehrunes' Razor was rejoined.{{ref|name=SRPieces}}

===Revival===
From {{Year|4E 200}} to [[Lore:Fourth Era#Third Century|201]], a new order of Mythic Dawn cultists surged from within the deeps of the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]], at a place called Red Scar Cavern, where members of the fallen order used to gather. The High Priest, [[Lore:Vonos|Vonos]], was the first to find this place, having been drawn to a buried [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|Oblivion Gate]] via premonition and dreams supposedly sent him by Daedra Prince Mehrunes Dagon. For months Vonos dug deeper and deeper, stone by stone. Others began to heed Dagon's call, and found their way to Red Scar. Eventually, they uncovered an ancient Mythic Dawn temple, with the envisioned Oblivion Gate disappointingly lying destroyed like all others, though Vonos understood the Liminal Barriers were weakened in this place. The renewed Mythic Dawn here made it their mission to locate the scattered pieces of the Gates across the land and reopen the Jaws of Oblivion, and they succeeded...{{ref|name=TheCause|Events of ''[[Skyrim:The Cause|The Cause]]''}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}}

High Priest Vonos continued to be haunted with dreams, these however, were about [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and his [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]], about the return of the World-Eater, [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], and the rise of the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] -- both identifiable to Vonos as fragments of Akatosh's very soul. Vonos designed a scheme that would make the Last Dragonborn an unwitting instrument of Dagon's will. Vonos set up the Last Dragonborn to obtain an artifact of untold power, a Great Welkynd Stone of the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], with this artifact in one hand, and a weapon in the other, the Last Dragonborn would strike down Vonos, unknowingly enabling a designed ritual which would serve as the ultimate betrayal of Akatosh, thereby corrupting the Stone and causing the Oblivion Gate to open. And from within the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]], on the other side of the portal, the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] Valkynaz: Gatanas and [[Lore:Methats|Methats]] awaited to lead the incoming assault. Before they could do this however, the Last Dragonborn entered the gate and struck them down. Simultaneously the [[Lore:Vigil of Stendarr|Vigil of Stendarr]] scoured the temple of cultists. However, the Gate's Sigil Stone had been destroyed by the Daedra to prevent the Gate's closure, and the portal remained permanently open, potentially leaving Tamriel vulnerable to Daedric invasion.{{ref|name=TheCause|Events of ''[[Skyrim:The Cause|The Cause]]''}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal}}

==Beliefs==
According to the beliefs of the Mythic Dawn, the mortal world was actually the [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of Lorkhan, who was in fact a [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]]. Per this belief, the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], the gods mortals generally worship, were actually Lorkhan's betrayers, who stole the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world.{{ref|name=Camoran|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries|}}}} Thus, Mehrunes Dagon undertook the Oblivion Crisis not to destroy Tamriel, but to "liberate the Occupied Lands" from the Aedra.{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In Tamriel, the cult called their enemies the "Dragon's Blood"—a reference to the covenant between [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} More prosaically, it refers to the line of [[Lore:Tamrielic Emperors|rulers]] keeping the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] alight.{{ref|name=MDC}} In particular, Mankar Camoran had a "long duel" with the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]],{{ref|name=OBMankar}} dating back to the reign of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], when it is speculated the ''Commentaries'' were written.{{ref|name=OBTarMeena}}

==Commentary on Beliefs==

The beliefs of the Mythic Dawn were met with derision by [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]]. She described the assertion that [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] is not distinct from Oblivion but rather one of its constitutive realms as false, and mocked the claim that the realm belongs to Dagon by birthright as "convenient". Lyranth questioned whether the information had come from Dagon himself or simply been spread by his cultists, and advised that "one should pay less attention to idiotic cultists".{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}
