[[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]], also known as '''Queen Alessia''', '''Saint Alessia''',{{Ref|name=ADiF1|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 1}}}} the '''Lady of Heaven''',{{Ref|name=TA}} '''Queen-ut-Cyrod''',{{Ref|name=TA}} '''Queen of Mortals''',{{Ref|name=TOSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} '''Slave Queen of Cyrodiil''',{{Ref|name=2920:SH|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Height (v7)}}}}{{Ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} '''Mother of the Empire''',{{Ref|name=TPKOTN|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Oblivion: Knights of the Nine]]''}} '''Queen of Stars''',{{Ref|name=TPKOTN}} and '''El-Estia''',{{Ref|name=Remanada|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}} was the first Empress of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and the founder of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], the first of three Cyrodilic empires. She led the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] against the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] in the third century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], freeing the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrod]] from slavery and founding the Alessian Empire. As the first Empress of Cyrodiil, she established a new religion, a fusion of the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] and [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] pantheons known as the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]]. On her deathbed in {{Year|1E 266}},{{Ref|name=COTF|{{Cite Book|Cleansing of the Fane}}}} she was canonized by [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]{{Ref|name=TOSA}} or [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]],{{ref|name=TSOP8}} and her soul placed in the central stone of the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], thus forging Akatosh's covenant with men to protect [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] from the forces of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]{{Ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} and starting the [[Lore:Oversoul|Oversoul]] of emperors.{{ref|name=WWY|{{Cite Book|Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?}}}} She has also been stated to have achieved [[Lore:Apotheosis|apotheosis]] in death,{{Ref|name=COTF}}{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}} and was described as becoming wife to [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] and [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-el]], in addition to her marriage with Morihaus.{{Ref|name=Remanada}} Among worshippers of [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]], Alessia is the [[Lore:Imperial Saints|patron saint]] of humanity.{{Ref|[[Online:Philius Dormier|Philius Dormeir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Empress is associated with [[Lore:The Thief|The Thief Constellation]].{{Ref|name=HACBAYQ|{{Cite Book|High Astrologer Caecilus Bursio Answers Your Questions}}}}

Alessia is not her birth name, but rather a corruption of a title, '''Al-Esh''' ("High Highness"), that was given to her by her followers. Another title bestowed on her was '''Paravant''' ("The First").  From corruptions of these two titles come countless other names by which she is known to various peoples, including '''Perrif''', '''Paraval''', '''Pevesh''', '''Perrethu''', '''Paravania''', '''Esha''', and '''Aleshut'''.{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}} The [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]] was so-called because she purportedly appeared to the prophet [[Lore:Marukh|Marukh]] in a vision and bestowed her teachings upon him, for which she was known by the Order as the "'''Enlightened One'''".{{Ref|name=TIOD|{{Cite Book|The Illusion of Death}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} <includeonly>For more information, see the [[Lore:Alessia|full article]] on Alessia.</includeonly><noinclude> The Ayleids derogatorily called her the "'''[[Lore:Cattle|cattle]]-queen'''" during the Rebellion.{{Ref|name=UiU|{{Cite Book|Umaril is Undone}}}}

==History==
===Biography===
{{Quote Box|''And this thing I have thought of, I have named it, and I call it freedom. Which I think is just another word for Shezarr Who Goes Missing...''|Alessia while speaking to [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]]{{Ref|name=TSOP2}}}}
[[File:ON-statue-St. Alessia, Paravant.jpg|left|thumb|[[Lore:Breton|Bretonic]] First Era [[Lore:Statues|statue]] of St. Alessia, Paravant holding the Amulet of Kings (ESO)]]
Alessia was born to an unknown tribe and grew up in [[Lore:Sardavar Leed|Sardavar Leed]], also known as Sard.{{Ref|name=TA}} In [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrod]], the heart of the [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire]], all races of men were slaves of the Ayleids. She prayed to Akatosh and the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] for liberation from the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] armies of the Ayleids.{{Ref|name=TAOK}}

Alessia's prayers were answered when she was granted three visions by Akatosh at [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]].{{Ref|name=CATAYQ|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions}}}} Another legend states she received a vision from Akatosh in one of the old Ayleid shrines beneath the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], after which her followers tore it down and built the [[Lore:Dragonfire Cathedral|Dragonfire Cathedral]].{{Ref|name=ONDrake|[[Online:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}} Regardless, once she was free she began an open revolt against her slavemasters.{{Ref|name=TLST|{{Cite Book|The Legendary Sancre Tor}}}} Working alongside her demigod lover [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], Alessia raised an army. In {{Year|1E 242}}, Pelinal Whitestrake wandered into her encampment covered in Ayleid blood; her second champion had arrived.{{Ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}} With momentum growing stronger, human forces took control of Cyrodiil east of the [[Lore:Niben River|Niben River]].

[[File:ON-furnishing-Saint's Wrath Tapestry, Large 02.jpg|Tapestry depicting the saint's wrath|thumb|right]]
Forging alliances with [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and rebel Ayleid lords,{{Ref|name=TLKOTA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} Alessian forces encircled the capital of the White-Gold Tower. With coalition forces hesitant to attack, Pelinal Whitestrake stormed the tower alone and slew the Sorcerer-King [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]]. Eventually Morihaus followed with the rest of the army and captured the citadel. The Ayleids were gradually pushed out of Cyrodiil; many became refugees, fleeing to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{Ref|name=ASiV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Survivals in Valenwood}}}}

With the capture of the White-Gold Tower in {{Year|1E 243}},{{ref|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}} Alessia declared herself the first Empress of the new Cyrodilic empire, and established Sancre Tor as her holy city. According to legend, Akatosh made a covenant with Alessia and gifted her the Amulet of Kings as a token of this pledge and proof of her claim.{{Ref|name=TOSA}}{{Ref|name=TLST}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad}}}} His second gift was the Sublime Brazier, a powerful relic built in the Dragonfire Cathedral that was instrumental in upholding the covenant through the lighting of the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]].{{Ref|name=ONDrake}} Empress Alessia inherited the Laureled Diadem from the Ayleid Empire, though instead of destroying it, she fashioned it with Twin Red Diamonds and wore it as the Star-Made Diamond Diadem.{{Ref|name=SMDD|[[ON:Star-Made Diamond Diadem|Star-Made Diamond Diadem]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

One of the first challenges facing the new empire was the establishment of a religion acceptable both to the people of Cyrodiil, accustomed to an Aldmeri-derived pantheon, and to her Nordic allies, who were opposed to any elven deities.{{Ref|name=SATD}} As a compromise, she established the Eight Divines, which incorporated elements of both Aldmeri and Nordic religions.{{Ref|name=SATD}} As of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], this religion remained the dominant faith of Cyrodiil, although, with the addition of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], it became more commonly known as the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]]. At the end of the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], the Empire signed the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], which outlawed worship of Talos throughout the Empire, reverting the official Imperial pantheon to Alessia's original pantheon.{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

At some point, Alessia bore a son named [[Lore:Belharza|Belharza]].{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Minotaurs}}}}{{ref|name=TToM|{{Cite Book|The Truth of Minotaurs}}}}{{Ref|name=SMDD}} Belharza was thought to be fathered by Morihaus and is considered by some to be the first [[Lore:Minotaur|Minotaur]].{{ref|name=OM}}{{ref|name=TToM}}{{Ref|name=ONBB|[[ON:Belharza's Band|Belharza's Band]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Their union created the entire Minotaur race.{{ref|name=OM}}{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}}

Pelinal was supposedly present at Alessia's death bed despite his own death years earlier. He described another half that along with himself would take her up and reveal their true faces, "which eat one another in amnesia each Age", to her.{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}}{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}} Upon Alessia's death in 1E 266, she was said to have been buried in the catacombs beneath Sancre Tor. This later developed into a tradition for [[Lore:Reman|Reman Cyrodiil]] and the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]], who were also buried under the city; however, a competing tradition asserts Alessia was instead buried on the site of the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]] in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{Ref|name=TLST}} On her death, Belharza succeeded her as the second Emperor of Cyrodiil.{{Ref|name=OM}}{{Ref|name=LCLAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}}

===Legacy===
[[File:GEN-misc-Belharza Child.jpg|Sketch of Alessia and Morihaus' child, Belharza|thumb|right]]
Following the rise of the Alessian Order, depictions of Saint Alessia were posthumously outlawed, leaving very few contemporary depictions of Alessia herself.{{Ref|name=StAAP|[[Online:St. Alessia, Paravant|St. Alessia, Paravant]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The earliest known depictions were believed to have been created nearly a millennium after her reign had ended, and are presumed to have been commissioned as gifts for Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] by the newly admitted province of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{Ref|name=StAAP}} [[Lore:Fort Alessia|Fort Alessia]], situated just east of Sardavar Leed, was presumably named after her.{{ref|name=FA|[[OB:Fort Alessia|Fort Alessia]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Some sources claim that the [[Lore:Sight|prophet]] [[Lore:Marukh|Marukh's]] teachings, which questioned the validity of Elven rule, were inspired by him speaking to the "Enlightened One", Saint Alessia, in a vision. These sentiments led to an increasingly abstract and unknowable depiction of a [[Lore:The One|Single God]].{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} They heavily influenced the Alessian Order and their secret sect, the [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selective]]. The priesthood of Marukh saw no difference between spiritual and political matters. This was the religion of the Alessian Empire, and it taught that to resist the Emperor was to resist the Gods.{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}}

The [[Lore:Remanada|Remanada]], a mythical text describing the life of [[Lore:Reman Cyrodiil|Reman Cyrodiil]], claims that King [[Lore:Hrol|Hrol]] was visited by the spirit of Saint Alessia, who "bore in her left hand the dragonfire of the aka-tosh and in her right hand the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|jewels of the covenant]] and on her breast a wound that spilt [[Lore:Void|void]] onto her mangled feet", and was considered to be an incarnation of the land itself. From the union of Hrol and Alessia at Sancre Tor, an act which caused Hrol's death, it is said that [[Lore:Reman|Reman Cyrodiil]] was conceived, though skeptics suspect that this heritage was a fabrication intended to legitimize his rule.{{Ref|name=Remanada|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}}{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}{{ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}}

The mysterious [[Lore:The Prophet|Prophet]] of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] had knowledge of the Amulet's oversoul and claimed to be able to sense its presence and hear it speak.{{ref|name=OBProphet}} The Prophet claimed he could hear Alessia weep for fallen Cyrodiil, and ask for her issue to walk in her steps and for blood akin to hers to rekindle the dragonpact.
