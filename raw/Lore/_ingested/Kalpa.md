# Lore:Kalpa

<noinclude>{{Trail|Appendices}}</noinclude>
A [[Lore:Kalpa|Kalpa]] (or '''Worldskin'''){{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} is an epoch of time consisting of the birth, life, and death of a world.{{ref|name=PTHNX|[[SR:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The chaos of the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] is believed to take place between each kalpa in a period of untime that marks the end of the previous world and allows the creation of the next. The moment when time became linear is considered to have been the first day of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] and of the current kalpa as a whole.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Shor Son of Shor|ns_base=GEN}}}} These segments of time are sometimes subject to temporary interruptions called [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Breaks]], which are momentary returns to the non-linearity and chaos of the Dawn Era.{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}<noinclude>

==Cultural Beliefs==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''And the awful fighting ended again.''|''[[General:Shor Son of Shor|Shor Son of Shor]]''}}
It is said that both Men and Mer know Tamriel is where the "nexus of creation" took place, as well as where the Last War will happen.{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}}

The dragon Paarthurnax referred to kalpas as being "the egg of the next world", and considered [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]]'s defeat at the hands of the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] enough to stop (either permanently or temporarily) the end of the world.{{ref|name=PTHNX}}

According to [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] (Or potentially, [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]]),{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} the end of the Kalpa is an echo of [[Lore:Convention|Convention]], in which he and Akatosh eat each other in madness.{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}}

According to some sources, the destruction of [[Lore:Lyg|Lyg]] took place in the prior kalpa.{{ref|name=9C}}{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Pocket Guide to the Empire, Second Edition|Pocket Guide to the Empire, Second Edition]]}} It is also said that the "half-Elf" [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril]]'s divine father hailed from the "world-river" of the previous kalpa.{{ref|name=TSOP3|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 7}}}}

===Ayleids===
According to the Ayleid Creation Myth, the [[Lore:Anuad|Anuad]], the current world was proceeded by twelve, which were destroyed by the great battle between Anu and Padomay.{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}

===Dunmer===
According to the teachings of the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] God [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], the law of time is "static change", which he compares to a [[Lore:Ouroboros|reptile coiled in wheel shape]]. Vivec explains that "cosmic time is repeated" through echoes of the primordial dichotomy of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], called the [[Lore:Enantiomorph|Enantiomorph]]. Those who reenact that dichotomy are said to cause catastrophes that end the universe. In the process, the actors themselves are said to enter the [[Lore:Aetherius|Underworld]], subsequently revising their own histories in retrospect as an  [[Lore:Dawn Era|age of gold]]. Following the demise of the old universe, these revisions transform into "[[Lore:Earthbones|the structure]] of [[Lore:Mantling|future nostalgia]]", allowing for further modification and reenactment. {{ref|name=36L2|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 2}}}}{{ref|name=36L21|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 21}}}}{{ref|name=36L11|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 11}}}}{{ref|name=36L35|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 35}}}}

===Nords===
The [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s believe that the "Dead Gods", such as [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], died to bring the current kalpa, which itself is a byproduct of its predecessor's destruction at the hands of [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] the World-Eater, and that this world shall also be destroyed to create a new one.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel}}}} What precedes it is the Final War,{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNTR|[[General:The Nords' Totemic Religion|The Nords' Totemic Religion]]}} the final doom,{{ref|name=SRHS|[[Skyrim:Hero of Sovngarde|Hero of Sovngarde]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} the final battle,{{ref|name=SSCSS|[[Skyrim:Stormcloak Soldier (Sovngarde)|Stormcloak Soldier]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Sovngarde (quest)|Sovngarde]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}} where the Nords, led by the new gods of the current kalpa known as "Twilight Gods", "will show their final, best worth". Thus far the only Twilight God besides Alduin, is [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], the Dragonborn totem.{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNTR}}

===Redguards===
The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s also believe in a cyclical world that is, or at least was, periodically consumed by the serpent god [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]],{{ref|name=VOF}} described as eating himself and being reborn similar to how a river empties itself into the sea yet is reborn at its source.{{ref|name=KS|{{Cite Book|Knowing Satakal}}}} Satakal was depicted by the [[Lore:Na-Totambu|Na-Totambu]] as an [[Lore:Ouroboros|ouroboros]], a snake eating its own tail.{{ref|name=ESOCoS|[[Online:Coil of Satakal|Coil of Satakal]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Crown|Crown]] scholar and historian The Unveiled [[Lore:Azadiyeh|Azadiyeh]] wrote of Satakal pulling [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] beneath the waves due to the "Hunger of [[Lore:Sep|Sep]]" filling the hearts of mortals, bringing evil to Yokuda. She described it as a "Time of Ending", with their voyage to Hammerfell being described as a "Time of Beginning".{{ref|name=THoS|{{Cite Book|The Hunger of Sep}}}} Azadiyeh also wrote a plea to the people of Hammerfell to return to reverence of their traditional gods and traditions, believing that if they did not honor their ancestors, it would bring about a new "Ending Time", worse again than those before.{{ref|name=PfOE|{{Cite Book|Plea for Open Eyes}}}} 

One Redguard proverb spoke of the world as being Satakal's egg, which he will later devour,{{ref|name=KS}} similar to the way the dragon Paarthurnax described the cycle of kalpas.{{ref|name=PTHNX}} 

Some Yokudan myths describe the Second Serpent, [[Lore:Sep|Sep]], creating Nirn out of previous dead worldskins, rather than Satakal the First Serpent.{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} Some Redguard pilgrims describe the cycle of worldskins as Satakal eating himself and Sep repeatedly tricking the spirits to make Nirn each cycle, as well as stealing a Pearl from Ruptga. When the pearl is uncovered, water runs from it over the deadskins of Satakal, and the Hungry Stomach of both Sep and Satakal is satisfied, only for the cycle to then repeat. {{ref|[[Online:Hayazzin|Hayyazin]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Sep is regarded to be a [[Lore:The Serpent|hungry void]] in the sky,{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} while Satakal is regarded to rise from the starry depths of the ocean.{{ref|name=THoS|{{Cite Book|The Hunger of Sep}}}} To some Redguards, this symbolizes their dichotomy; "Does not the serpent made of sky above reflect the serpent made of the sea below? Yea, it is so."

===Argonians===
The Argonians of the [[Lore:Adzi-Kostleel|Azdi-Kostleel]] tribe believe in a cyclical world comparable to the one described in the Yokudan creation myth, with [[Lore:Atakota|Atakota]] having similarities to Satakal, periodically consuming and recreating the world.{{ref|name=COR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}} The Argonians of the [[Lore:Nisswo|Nisswo]] tribe also acknowledge Atakota and [[Lore:Lorkhan|his shadow]]. The Shadow eventually devours the broken halves of Atakota before the World can end, to save his children and keep them within his secrets.{{ref|name=COR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}}{{ref|name=ESOWUS|[[Online:Walks-Under-Shadow|Walks-Under-Shadow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Tales about an explorer deep in Black Marsh describe a golden Argonian whose face changed twelve times. Soon after the explorer witnessed a vision of many wheels and their towers, that began to break, before quickly the Shadow of Atakota came to devour them.{{ref|name=LTOTFE4|{{Cite Book|Lost Tales of the Famed Explorer: Fragment VI}}}}

===Khajiit===
It is said that the spirits of [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] whose souls have not [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|been corrupted]] are taken by [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]] to the [[Lore:Sands Behind the Stars|Sands Behind the Stars]], where they await for the "Next Pounce".{{ref|name=ESOLMD|{{Cite Book|Litter-Mates of Darkness}}}} 

Pre-[[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|ri'Datta]] myths state that at the end of time, Khenarthi will summon the united spirits of the Khajiiti people to defend creation.{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|All Our Perfect Forms}}}} It is also said that [[Lore:Tojay-raht|Tojay-raht]] fighters train for the "War at the End".{{ref|name=AOPF|{{Cite Book|All Our Perfect Forms}}}}

Certain tales about [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]], describe a cyclical battle that has happened repeatedly at the Dawn of the world. These tales also describe Boethra seeking the truth the [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selectives]] sought, and coming upon twelve worlds, finding among them two serpents in battle.{{Ref|name=TBOB|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}}

===Reachfolk===
The [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] believe that at the "end of all days", [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] will fight alongside them,{{ref|name=ONHSG|[[Online:High Shaman Glynroch|High Shaman Glynroch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]] will beat again, reawakened by feeding on the deaths of mortals, and its darkness will spread from its depths to consume everything, sparing only [[Lore:Namira|Namira]]'s faithful.{{ref|name=ONA|[[Online:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Tsaesci===
The Tsaesci refer to the birth of time as "the Striking",{{ref|name=36L21|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 21}}}} which split the [[Lore:Aurbis|Egg]] into twelve worlds. Each world was said to be a serpent with a name, that coiled unto themselves and became more eggs.{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:The Tsaesci Creation Myth|The Tsaesci Creation Myth]]}}
