[[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] (archaically known as '''Bruhmaht'''){{ref|name=TSE|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Shonni-etta|ns_base=General}}}} is a Nibenese city located in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], north of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. Bruma's population is primarily [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s, which is reflected in the city's architecture. It is always relatively cold and snow-covered.{{ref|name=GTB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Bruma}}}} The city's population includes followers of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] as well as those who follow the traditional [[Lore:Gods|Nordic Pantheon]].

The [[Lore:Silver Road|Silver Road]] connects Bruma to the Imperial City, while the [[Lore:Orange Road|Orange Road]] connects it to [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]]. [[Lore:Cloud Ruler Temple|Cloud Ruler Temple]] is located nearby. Bruma has a small sewer system which connects up with the natural caves that run beneath the town.{{ref|name=OBMRecover|[[OBMobile:Recover the Amulet|Events]] of ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}} This underground area was used by the Mythic Dawn during the Oblivion Crisis. The deeper levels of the sewers are home to [[Lore:Troll|troll]]s. The city also houses the Great Chapel of the youngest of the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]], [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], which dominates the center of town.<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:LO-map-Cyrodiil (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumb|left|Map of Cyrodiil, showing Bruma in the north]]
===First Era===
The settlement has likely been in existence since at least {{Year|1E 1167}}.{{ref|name=IG|{{Cite Book|Imbel Genealogy}}}} A so called “beggar-king of Bruhmaht” is recorded as being present at the court of the thirteen year old [[Lore:Reman|Reman I]], said to have swiftly decapitated a lord of [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]] for speaking in impatience to the Emperor.{{ref|name=TSE|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Shonni-etta|ns_base=General}}}}
===Second Era===
During [[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|Varen's Rebellion]] ({{Year|2E 576}} - {{Year|2E 577}}), Bruma was a base for the Colovian troops.{{ref|name=CotFC5|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 5}}}} The [[Lore:Second Legion|Second Legion]] was stationed in the vicinity of Bruma in peacetime{{ref|name=ImperialWarMastiff|Description of the [[Online:Imperial War Mastiff|Imperial War Mastiff]]}}, although it originally came from the Gold Coast.{{ref|name=WolfandDragon|{{Cite Book|The Wolf and the Dragon}}}}

At some point during the Three Banners War, [[Lore:Orpheon the Tactician|Orpheon the Tactician]] and his army put Bruma to siege. For a fortnight, the army's siege engines were used against its walls before Orpheon sent Flamedancer Ajim-Rei into the city to set afire the buildings. The city was then sacked by Orpheon's army, and this event was part of the campaign that lead to Orpheon momentarily being [[Lore:Battlefield Emperors|crowned]] as [[Lore:Emperor|Emperor]].{{Ref|name=SoB|{{Cite Book|The Sacking of Bruma}}}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, Bruma became neutral ground during the ongoing [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]], although it was initially within the territory controlled by the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]]. The city was attacked by [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] and [[Lore:Shadowed Path|Shadowed Path]] cultists in the name of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]. A [[Lore:Dark Anchor|Dark Anchor]] was then opened above the ruins of the city in an attempt to draw it into [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] as part of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], as well as to provide Daedric reinforcements. Several citizens barricaded themselves within key buildings, and the survivors sent pleas to both the Daggerfall Covenant and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] for aid.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bruma Pleads for Aid|ns_base=ON}}}} Passing soldiers helped the citizenry recover and fight back against the invaders, and the Dark Anchor was eventually lifted.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
In {{Year|3E 433}}, Bruma came under attack from the forces of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]].{{ref|name=OBBG|[[Oblivion:Bruma Gate|Bruma Gate]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] planned to sack the city as they did [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]], by opening a {{Lore Link|Great Gate}} to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] and allowing a Daedric siege force to enter Nirn. These plans were discovered by the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], but were allowed to continue at the request of [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] in order to obtain a [[Lore:Great Sigil Stone|Great Sigil Stone]]. Soldiers from the other cities of Cyrodiil flocked to Bruma to aid in the defense.{{ref|name=OBAfB|[[Oblivion:Allies for Bruma|Allies for Bruma]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Rallied by Martin himself, the soldiers held back the tide of Daedra long enough for the [[Lore:Hero#Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] to enter the Deadlands and retrieve the Sigil Stone, ending the invasion. The [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis#Defense of Bruma|Defense of Bruma]] became one of the most important conflicts of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{ref|name=OBBruma|[[Oblivion:Defense  of Bruma|Defense of Bruma]] and [[Oblivion:Great Gate|Great Gate]] quests in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} During the same year, the Bruma [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] branch was burned to the ground in a surprise attack from the [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]].{{ref|name=OBAPR|[[Oblivion:A Plot Revealed|A Plot Revealed]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
===Fourth Era===
During the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the [[Lore:Crimson Dirks|Crimson Dirks]] had members in the city.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Crimson Dirks}}}} The [[Lore:Goblin#Three Feather Tribe|Three Feather tribe]] survived into the era and may have used [[Lore:Gromm's Pass|Gromm's Pass]] to cross into [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Letter to Clexius|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Bruma Sanctuary of the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] was destroyed circa {{Year|4E 186}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Cicero's Journal}}}} Klija Wolf-Claw, a Nordic warrior from Bruma, participated in a contest between the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] and the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]] during {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Battle of Champions|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} In the same year, the city was visited by a party of Mythic Dawn, who kidnapped an [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] researcher for use in their excavation of [[Lore:Rielle|Rielle]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Excavation Leader's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}
