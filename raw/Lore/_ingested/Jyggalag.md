[[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]], the '''Prince of Order''',{{ref|name=SIDyus|[[Shivering:Dyus|Dyus]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}{{ref|name=LGDyus|[[Legends:The Warrior|Dyus]]' dialogue in [[Legends:Legends|''Legends'']]}} also known as '''Gray Prince of Order''',{{ref|name=TTIS3}} is a [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] whose sphere represents logical order and deduction; his great library once contained a logical prediction of every detail of the world and of every action that would ever take place on [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] or [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], long before they actually happened.{{ref|name=SIDyus}} He was cursed in antiquity by the rest of the Daedric Princes to become the Prince of Madness, [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]].{{ref|name=SIJyggalag|[[Shivering:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} After the events of the last [[Lore:Greymarch|Greymarch]], Sheogorath and Jyggalag are no longer the same Daedric Prince; however, they are still connected in other ways.{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Cartogriffi's Posts|Cartogriffi's Posts]]}}

Jyggalag commands his own Daedra, the [[Lore:Knights of Order|Knights of Order]], which are spawned from {{Lore Link|Obelisks of Order|obelisks}} summoned by his followers, the [[Lore:Priests of Order|Priests of Order]]. Together, they constitute the [[Lore:Forces of Order|Forces of Order]].{{ref|name=Shivering|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} He is viewed as stern,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Darkest Divinities}}}} bleak, and colorless,{{ref|name=SISheogorath|[[Shivering:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} with him and his soldiers lacking originality.{{ref|name=SIHaskill|[[Shivering:Haskill|Haskill]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Mythic Accounts===
{{Quote Box|''Once, I ruled this Realm, a world of perfect Order. My dominion expanded across the seas of Oblivion with each passing era.''|Jyggalag}}
====The Curse of Madness====
In the time before recorded history,{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Jyggalag's [[Lore:Jyggalag's Realm|realm of perfect order]] expanded across the seas of Oblivion, and the other Daedric Princes had grown fearful and jealous of him and his power. They cursed him to live as [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], the incarnation of the thing he hated most: madness—the essence of chaos itself.{{ref|name=SIJyggalag}}{{ref|name=LGDyus}} He was, however, allowed to return to his true form at the end of every era in order to retake the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] from his mad alter-ego in an event known as the Greymarch. But once a Greymarch was complete, Jyggalag transformed back into Sheogorath, starting the cycle again.{{ref|name=SIJyggalag}}

Another view of the event of Jyggalag being cursed to madness comes from the sermons of [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Apostles]], which claim that the Daedra fear wisdom and order, and thus "fear [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] above all others". They hold that Jyggalag is closest to Sotha Sil in principle, and that he alone knew his nature, going mad in the knowing.{{ref|name=TTIS3|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 3}}}}{{ref|name=ONClockwork|[[Online:Contraband/Ritual Objects#Clockwork Jyggalag|Clockwork Jyggalag]] contraband item in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Apostles believed that the Daedra and Oblivion are nothing more than illusion themselves, a consequence of the flaws of the [[Lore:Mundus|creation]] of the [[Lore:Aedra|et'Ada' Gears]], the result of the [[Lore:Void|Void]] taking root within the cracks caused by said flaws. Though many [[Lore:Ada|Ada]] have ambitions, they learned their lesson from the banishment and tend to keep them within their own spheres, lest they suffer the same fate as Jyggalag.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}}

[[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]], Sheogorath's chamberlain, claims to be a [[Lore:Vestige (creature)|vestige]] of a mortal who mantled the Madgod in a past event.{{ref|name=CHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Chamberlain Haskill Answers Your Questions}}}} The exact details of this interaction remain unclear (as Sheogorath appears keen to avoid the subject), but Haskill claims the pair "go back to the beginning", and the incident somehow involved Jyggalag.{{ref|name=CHAYQ}}{{Ref|name=IWTDSI|{{Cite Book|Interview With Two Denizens of the Shivering Isles|ns_base=GEN}}}}

====Chamberlain Dyus====
[[File:LG-quest-The Warrior 03.jpg|thumb|right|Jyggalag and Dyus]]
There came a time when the Prince of Order decided someone was needed to take care of his realm's mundane affairs, and the task fell upon [[Lore:Dyus|Dyus of Mytheria]], who became Jyggalag's chamberlain and the keeper of his Great Library.{{ref|name=SIDyus}}{{Ref|name=IWTDSI}} Dyus performed all the tasks Jyggalag found distasteful.{{Ref|name=IWTDSI}}

Upon taking over the realm, Sheogorath banished or killed every aspect of Order that remained in the Shivering Isles, save for Dyus. The Great Library was set aflame, as the Madgod believed that "personal choice defied logical prediction". Yet, he allowed Dyus to remain, for he was unwilling to destroy the knowledge the chamberlain possessed. Forbidden to die, Dyus was locked away for all time in the remnants of the once proud library.{{ref|name=SIDyus}}

===Third Era===
In {{Year|3E 433}}, a doorway to the Shivering Isles appeared in the middle of [[Lore:Niben Bay|Niben Bay]], The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] entered the realm to find that Sheogorath was searching for a mortal Champion.{{ref|name=SIDoor|Events of [[Shivering:A Door in Niben Bay|A Door in Niben Bay]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} The next [[Lore:Greymarch|Greymarch]] was about to begin.{{ref|name=SISheogorath}}

Before long, [[Lore:Knights of Order|Knights of Order]], soulless servitors of Jyggalag, began appearing in the Isles, drawn to strange obelisks dotting the realm. {{ref|name=SRBaiting|Events of [[Shivering:Baiting the Trap|Baiting the Trap]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Meanwhile, Sheogorath revealed his plan: the Hero would break the cycle of the Greymarch by becoming Sheogorath (or at least, mantling his powers). The Madgod believed this would make things "different this time around", as the "Rules" of the Greymarch necessitated his absence (though he eventually confessed the truth of his dual nature to the Hero).{{ref|name=SISheogorath}}

[[File:SI-creature-Jyggalag 03.jpg|thumb|left|Jyggalag's visage during his farewell to the new Prince of Madness]]
The Greymarch began with an assault on the Fringe, the gateway to the Shivering Isles.{{ref|name=SIFringe|Events of [[Shivering:Retaking The Fringe|Retaking the Fringe]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Due to the actions of the Hero in retaking the Fringe, the Forces of Order next targeted the Wellsprings of the Isles' Daedric guardians.{{ref|name=SIHelpless|Events of [[Shivering:The Helpless Army|The Helpless Army]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Sheogorath celebrated this development, as it was not a tactic Order had tried before.{{ref|name=SISheogorath}} The Hero dealt with this new threat, but it was a hollow victory, as Sheogorath soon transformed into Jyggalag.{{ref|name=SIHelpless}}{{ref|name=SISymbols|Events of [[Shivering:Symbols of Office|Symbols of Office]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}

Chamberlain [[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]] still held out hope, however, for if the Throne of Madness was not empty when Jyggalag arrived, there was still a chance to prevail. He took the Hero to see the captive [[Lore:Dyus|Dyus of Mytheria]], who suggested a new [[Lore:Staff of Sheogorath|Staff of Sheogorath]], imbued with the power of the realm, would allow the Hero to occupy the Throne (though true to form, he also predicted failure was "a certainty"). With the chamberlain's assistance, the Hero created the staff and assumed the powers of the Madgod.{{ref|name=SIDyus}}{{ref|name=SISymbols}}{{ref|name=SIRoots|Events of [[Shivering:The Roots of Madness|The Roots of Madness]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}

This development came none too soon, for the Forces of Order were already breaching the walls of Sheogorath's Palace. Before long, Jyggalag himself appeared and slew the remaining guards, leaving only the new Madgod standing between him and the end of another Greymarch. The Prince of Order fought hard, but was unable to best the Hero. Ultimately, Jyggalag yielded, conceding the Shivering Isles, and congratulating the Hero on breaking the cycle of the Greymarch and on becoming the new Prince of Madness. The Prince of Order then took his leave, returning to roam [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] once more.{{ref|name=SIEnd|Events of [[Shivering:The End of Order|The End of Order]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Dyus presumed he may have sought revenge on those who cursed him, or pursued loftier goals, perhaps never to return to the Shivering Isles again.{{Ref|name=IWTDSI}} Regardless, Jyggalag will one day reassume his role as the Prince of Order, with unknown ramifications for the complicated and opaque balance of power within Oblivion.{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on [https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2010/12/26/decrypting-the-elder-scrolls.aspx Decrypting The Elder Scrolls] [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:LG-quest-A Reckoning 01.jpg|The memories of Jyggalag drove the Prince to madness|thumb|left]]
Though Sheogorath was very knowledgeable about Jyggalag's motivations and involvement in previous Greymarches,{{ref|name=SISheogorath}} sometime in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]],{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} he suppressed the memories of his previous life as Jyggalag as they caused even him to go mad. The Dunmer [[Lore:Talym Rend|Talym Rend]] was able to defeat Sheogorath at his own game when he forced the Madgod to relive these memories.{{Ref|name=LegendsIsles|Events of the [[Legends:Isle of Madness (story)|Isles of Madness]] story in [[Legends:Legends|''Legends'']]}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, a mage named [[Lore:Thoron|Thoron]] came into possession of the Sword of Jyggalag. The sword instructed Thoron to invoke a ritual that tore the veil between Tamriel and the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]], and ultimately wished to destroy the isles much like its master had done in past Greymarches. The Last Dragonborn put an end to the ritual by killing Thoron, and came into possession of the sword.{{ref|name=SRCCOrder|Events of [[Skyrim:Restoring Order|Restoring Order]] from the [[Skyrim:Saints & Seducers|Saints & Seducers]] [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
