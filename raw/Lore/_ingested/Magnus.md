[[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], also known as the '''God of Magic''' (or simply '''Magic''' itself),{{Ref|name=BtAoM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} the '''Sun God''', the '''Great Architect''',{{Ref|name=DCT|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}} '''He Who Abstained''',{{Ref|name=TGoM|{{Cite Book|The Gifts of Magnus}}}} and the '''Sorceror-King''',{{ref|[[Arena:Artifact Quests#Staff of Magnus|Artifact Quest]] dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}  is a prominent [[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]], one of the Original Spirits. During the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] persuaded Magnus and several other et'Ada to help create the mortal plane, [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]]. Magnus was said to have been the architect of Mundus, as he created the schematics and diagrams needed to construct it.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{Ref|name=VoFitE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in the Empire}}}}{{Ref|name=BtAoM}}<noinclude>

As Mundus began to form, it became evident that many of the et'Ada would be required to sacrifice much of their power in order to form the mortal plane.{{Ref|name=BtAoM}} As their aspects began to die off, many of the et'Ada vanished completely.{{ref|name=TM}} Magnus decided to terminate the project.{{Ref|name=BtAoM}} He fled to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] in the last of Mundus's birth-pains, and his departure tore a hole which became [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]'s '''[[Lore:Sun|sun]]''', itself known as Magnus.{{Ref|name=TM}}{{Ref|name=VoFitE}}{{Ref|name=BtAoM}}{{Ref|name=36LoV33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}}

The et'Ada who chose to stay became the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]].{{Ref|name=TM}} Many other et'Ada, who became known as the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]], followed Magnus, tearing smaller holes in the sky, which became the stars,{{ref|name=EoMN|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}}{{Ref|name=Nhalan|[[Online:Nhalan|Nhalan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|group=UOL|''[[General:Cosmology|Cosmology]]''}} although it is sometimes believed that the stars are actually fragments of Magnus himself.{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}} Magnus didn't take a mate; instead, he fashioned his offspring from aether,{{Ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}} with his daughters emerging from forges. Those offspring comprised the Magna Ge, which included the Nine Coruscations. The eldest was the [[Lore:Ithelia|Prime Archon]], followed by [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]], and the others: [[Lore:Iana-Lor|Iana-Lor]], [[Lore:Londa-Vera|Londa-Vera]], [[Lore:Sheza-Rana|Sheza-Rana]], [[Lore:Unala-Se|Unala-Se]], [[Lore:Valia-Sha|Valia-Sha]], [[Lore:Xero-Lyg|Xero-Lyg]], and [[Lore:Mnemo-Li|Mnemo-Li]].{{ref|name=EoMN}}{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}

After Magnus's departure, the et'Ada convened at the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] at an event known as Convention and decided to punish Lorkhan for his trickery.{{Ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}}{{Ref|name=BtAoM}}{{ref|group=UOL|''[[General:Nu-Mantia Intercept, Letter Four|Nu-Mantia Intercept, Letter Four]]''}}

==Legacy and Worship==
In the eras that followed, many [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] and [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] venerated Magnus as a god and he became a part of their respective pantheons.{{Ref|name=VoFitE}} Some Altmeri sources claim [[Lore:Syrabane|Syrabane]] was a student of Magnus who succeeded him after his retirement; others portray him as a "facet" of Magnus.{{Ref|name=ILSA|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/lawrence-schick-and-phrastus-altmer-culture-0 Interview with Lawrence Schick and Phrastus on Altmer Culture (2021/2023)]}} The [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], masters of the arcane arts, worshiped him as the god of Sight, Light, and Insight and were known to dedicate temples in his name.{{Ref|name=GaL|{{Cite Book|Glories and Laments}}}} The Priory of the Golden Staff were peaceful followers of Magnus headquartered near [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]],{{Ref|name=MtC:SC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sister Celdina}}}} teaching of Magnus's grand design for the Mundus, and his disappointment at the flaws introduced into his creation. They believed that Magnus watches from the sun, waiting to repair what the others set amiss and were in possession of forbidden texts that revealed a darker design; some of the Magna Ge once sought a tool to unmake what had been made wrong, in order that it could be remade in accordance with the Architect's plan, which became [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]].{{Ref|name=DCT}}{{Ref|name=SC|[[ON:Sister Celdina|Sister Celdina]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Breton|Breton]] followers of Magnus sometimes use wicker effigies as stand-ins for themselves when performing certain rituals.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband/Ritual Objects#Magnus Cult Wicker Effigy|Magnus Cult Wicker Effigy]] item description and appearance as a common contraband item from Glenumbra in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] worship '''Magrus''', where he is commonly known as the '''Cat's Eye''' or the '''Third Eye of [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]]'''. An ancient belief that predate the religious reforms of the Riddle'thar Epiphany tells a story that when Magrus fled from [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]] and Lorkhaj, he could only see out of one eye and fell into the [[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]]. There Azurah judged him as too full of fear to rule a sphere, and she tore out his other eye. Magrus left to the heavens blinded, but Azurah made of his eye a stone to reflect the Varliance Gate, which is known as the Aether Prism. However, some Khajiiti sorcerers hold that Magrus left the eye willingly as an offering to Azurah and her children, and these magi still utter prayers to his name.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}}

[[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiilic]] legends say Magnus can inhabit the bodies of powerful mages and lend them his power. He was also associated with [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]], often known as the Underking, and is sometimes represented by an astrolabe, a telescope, or, more commonly, a [[Lore:Staff|staff]].{{Ref|name=VoFitE}} He was rumored to have had dealings with the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], going as far to entrust them with a map to his staff.{{ref|name=LMIL|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/staff-magnus Staff of Magnus] on The Imperial Library}} The [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]], which takes place across Tamriel, was originally in celebration of Magnus.{{ref|name=ONBreda|[[Online:Breda|Breda]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], the worship of Magnus was strictly prohibited for Imperials in [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] society.{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}} 

[[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] legends, specifically from [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]'s famous sermons claim that [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] was banished from the library of the sun by the power of Magnus.{{ref|name=LOV|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}}

[[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] celebrate their version of [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]],{{Ref|name=NLD|Appearance of [[Online:The Hunter's Repose|The Hunter's Repose]] and [[ON:Markarth|Markarth]] during [[ON:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]]}} a holiday originally dedicated to Magnus and pray for Hrokkibeg, the aspect of [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] to protect the sun. They believe that to any attempts to hinder Magnus' slow return are doomed to fail.{{Ref|name=NLFI|{{Cite Book|New Life Festival Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

[[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]] do not consider Magnus as the Divine, for though he gave much, he did not give all. His Aedric nature is not questioned, but he is not worshipped. His gift of magic is considered a dubious contribution that does the world at least as much ill as it does good.{{ref|name=AYQ|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}}

[[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] [[Lore:Spinners|Spinners]] also claim that the [[Lore:Sun|sun]] and [[Lore:Stars|stars]] are holes pierced in the wall of night by Magnus—and [[Lore:Magna Ge|other spirits]]—seeking to escape Nirn.{{Ref|name=Girnalin|[[ON:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Artifacts==
He supposedly created and used the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]], one of the elder artifacts of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Legend tells that it's the only artifact capable of containing his immense power.{{Ref|name=Mirabelle|[[SR:Mirabelle Ervine|Mirabelle Ervine]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In time, the staff will abandon the mage who wields it before they become too powerful and upsets the mystical balance it is sworn to protect.{{Ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} Another artifact attributed to him is the [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]], an ancient relic of immense power that was found buried under the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic city of [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] and later rediscovered in {{Year|4E 201}}.{{Ref|name=NoT|{{Cite Book|Night of Tears (book)}}}}{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

In [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], a [[Lore:Doomstones|magical stone]], supposedly associated with a long-forgotten cult, can be found southeast of [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]]. Known as the Magnus Stone by the populace, it is said to grant a powerful blessing that bolsters the magical abilities of certain individuals.{{Ref|name=NDS|{{Cite Book|New 'Doomstones' Series!|ns_base=ob}}}}{{Ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
