[[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], known as the '''Prince of Paths''', the '''Mistress of the Untraveled Road''', the '''Unseen''', the '''Fate-Changer''', the '''Dark Reflection''', the '''Last Tomorrow''', the '''Unweaver''', and '''She Who Saw and Wept''', is the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Paths.{{ref|name=MoraCoF|[[Online:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Chronicle of Fate|Chronicle of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{ref|name=TorvPrologue|[[Online:Torvesard|Torvesard]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some believe her will has some kind of relation to [[Lore:Hero|unbound Prisoners]].{{ref|name=TNC}} She supposedly had the ability to shape destiny, alter [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and rearrange its strands according to her whim. Considering this power a threat to the fabric of reality, [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], in alliance with [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], erased all traces of her memory from existence in order to preserve reality.{{ref|name=Mora|[[ON:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ithelia left behind her loyal servant, [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]], as a contingency plan in her absence.{{ref|name=ONTorvesard|[[Online:Torvesard|Torvesard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

As of {{Year|2E 582}}, she was unknown to the academic community,{{ref|name=ONAzandarAC|[[Online:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{ref|name=Mora}} though some scholars had uncovered evidence of forgotten [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] deities.{{ref|name=TSOJ}}{{Ref|name=Gandrinar}} Torvesard, in an alliance with [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] and [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]], was able to recover his memory of his Prince and began his efforts in finding and freeing her in earnest. Eventually, Ithelia's memory was restored, with her then setting out to reclaim all her power. However, with the help of [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] and the support of Hermaeus Mora, she saw the destruction her path would result in, even for herself, and accepted being exiled, seemingly with no possibility of return, to a world without magic, thus ending the danger to reality. The connection to the other reality was then severed using [[Lore:Abolisher|Abolisher]]. Hermaeus Mora then once again erased all memory of Ithelia’s existence from every being, save for the Vestige and himself.{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=FateForgottenPrince|Events of [[ON:Fate of the Forgotten Prince|Fate of the Forgotten Prince]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Mora}}

==History==
===Star Orphan===
[[File:ON-place-The Whispered Path.jpg|thumb|left|A statue of Ithelia]]
Originally the eldest member of the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Star-Orphans]], Magna Ge that followed the parabolas that led away from [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], Ithelia was known as the '''White Star''', the '''Prime Archon''' and the '''Queen of Fates Unwritten'''.{{ref|name=TNC}} 

When Ithelia was shown the [[Lore:Apocrypha|Infinite Abyss]] and the Indeterminate Sea (Mora Obscura), she discovered the Indefinite Limits of [[Lore:Void|Lorkh-Apeiron]]. It is said she bears connection to the harmony within duality that exists within the dichotomy of [[Lore:Anuiel|Anui-El]] and [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]], and the  unity of opposites that contained [[Lore:Varliance|starlight]] and [[Lore:Many Paths|endless possibility]] beyond [[Lore:Aurbis|cosmic interplay]]. Within texts discussing her, it is said she bears some connection to the "madness of the Time God" and the "first challenge of his shadow", who in nothingness saw those endless possibilities first. She also has unknown connection to the establishment of linear time, as layered upon endless possibility by the afixing of [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Ada-Mantia]] and its Zero Stone, a stable spire afixed by a stone of nothing possible,{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=AU4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Texts discussing her also speak on how [[Lore:Akatosh|Aka]] disappeared in the South, and [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] learned why Aka's insanity is all that is and could be. From this lesson, [[Lore:Convention|Convention]] and the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] was fixed to cleave a path through the everything to reach Numancia.{{ref|[[Online:The Dark Descent|The Dark Descent]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} As a result, echoing the endeavor of the Prime Archon, Ithelia, her Ayleid followers began to use violence against Man to bring forth a [[Lore:Hero|Numinous]] Paravant, whose metaphysical nature would give them unbound hands and the ability to achieve Ithelia's goal, to break the shackles of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and give every being the world and history they desired.{{ref|name=TNC|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}

Ithelia found worshippers in the Mythic Age. Her Ayleid followers prayed and offered sacrifices to her before embarking on a journey.{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOTG|[https://www.thegamer.com/the-elder-scrolls-online-narrative-director-ithelia-wiped-from-history-again/ TheGamer — ESO Narrative Director Hints At Ithelia Being Wiped From History Again], time stamp 25:33-26:08}} She had a significant amount of ties to the region known today as the [[Lore:West Weald|West Weald]].{{ref|name=ESO2024GR}}

===Imprisonment===
[[File:ON-place-The Mythos Vaults.jpg|The spire within the Mythos that served as Ithelia's Prison|thumb|left]][[File:ON-Ithelia's-Prison.png|Ithelia's Prison|right|thumb]]
At some point in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]],{{intnote|nb2|[nb 2]}} after Ithelia altered [[Lore:Fate|fate]] to avert a coming catastrophe, conflict arose when Mora examined her fate threads and realized Ithelia's power had already caused irreparable damage to the fabric of fate and that her continued existence endangered all reality. Mora's fears intensified when Ithelia created her [[Lore:Loom of the Untraveled Road|Loom of the Untraveled Road]], a great fate altering engine via which she intended to tap into the power of the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]] and give every being their own desired history, breaking the shackles of fate. Perceiving that the Many Paths were splintering under Ithelia's "unnatural manipulations" and foreseeing that reality would shatter in the attempt to achieve what Ithelia desired if the Loom was used, Mora took action.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Vargas|[[ON:Shardmarshal Vargas|Shardmarshal Vargas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ItheliasFury|Events of [[ON:Ithelia's Fury|Ithelia's Fury]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-place-The Inner Sea 03.jpg|The submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora that serves as a portal to Apocrypha, within the Inner Sea|thumb|left]][[File:ON-npc-Glyphic of Lost Memories.jpg|thumb|right|Hermaeus Mora discussing the threat of Ithelia]]

Eventually, an alliance formed between Hermaeus Mora, [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]]. Before they acted, Mora warned Ithelia to abandon her plan to use the Loom and alter fate at the city of [[Lore:Niryastare|Niryastare]], but she refused.{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Relics|Events of [[ON:Relics of the Three Princes|Relics of the Three Princes]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Each of the three Princes allied with Mora took possession of their chosen mortal champions and met at a submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora in the ruined city of [[Lore:Anudnabia|Anudnabia]], which had been dragged beneath the waves by Ithelia's fateweaving. There, they entered a hidden [[Lore:Planes of Existence|Demiplane]] imperceptible to her powers, where they could plan their attack in secrecy. Each Prince crafted a weapon to counteract Ithelia’s fate-manipulating abilities. To track her, Mephala forged the [[Lore:Skein of Secrets|Skein of Secrets]], enabling her to locate Ithelia and her Unseen by tracing the remnants of their power, normally invisible to magical observation.{{ref|name=Beragon|[[ON:Beragon|Beragon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}} To perceive her, Azura created the [[Lore:Lamp of Clarity|Lamp of Clarity]], illuminating Ithelia, her Unseen, and any open portals to the Path that could reach her.{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Beragon}} To reach her, Boethiah forged [[Lore:Abolisher|Abolisher]], a blade capable of opening, closing, and severing connections between Paths.{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Beragon|}}{{ref|name=Alea|[[ON:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Finally, Mora created the [[Lore:Echonir|Echonir]], a weapon capable of tracking and erasing any lingering memory of Ithelia.{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Examination|[[Online:Echonir Examination|Echonir Examination]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Thus prepared, the allied Princes and their armies attacked. Ithelia was ultimately cornered within the Ayleid city of [[Lore:Miscarcand|Miscarcand]], which was a great shrine to her.{{ref|name=Gambit|Events of [[Online:King Nantharion's Gambit|King Nantharion's Gambit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She was defeated through the combined efforts of the other Princes, drained of her powers to the extent of having no more strength than a mortal,{{ref|name=Leramil}} and imprisoned within the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha, a place her own powers couldn't perceive. However, before she was sealed away, two things occurred. The first was that Ithelia enacted a failsafe within the mind of her scion, [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]], that would allow him to one day recall events associated with her imprisonment in the form of dreams and lead him to the path of freeing her.{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=TJ|{{Cite Book|Torvesard's Journal}}}} The second was that, through her own powers to perceive fate and the Many Paths, Ithelia saw that Hermaeus Mora was correct about the threat she represented, a discovery she would forget due to her imprisonment.{{ref|name=Ithelia}} With Ithelia imprisoned, the four Princes designed a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Dandera|[[ON:Priest Dandera Helas|Priest Dandera Helas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dugikh|[[ON:Acolyte Dugikh|Acolyte Dugikh]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Adept|[[ON:Adept Rakzzin-jo|Adept Rakzzin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PoFate|Events of [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Two of the other Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought in this matter were [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]] and [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]]. However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sacrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.{{ref|name=Hidden|Events of [[ON:A Hidden Fate|A Hidden Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Vaermina|[[ON:Vaermina|Vaermina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ithelia's imprisonment was complete, and would become Mora's most guarded secret, the only recollection of the event in existence split between three [[Lore:Glyphic|Glyphics]] within [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]].{{ref|name=MoraCoF}}{{ref|name=GoLM|The vision from the Glyphic of Lost Memories, witnessed during [[Online:Chronicle of Fate|Chronicle of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{ref|name=Mora}}

===Prelude to Return===
[[File:ON-render-The Blind.jpg|The Blind within the Bedlam Veil|thumb|left]][[File:ON-render-Arandros the Awakened.jpg|The Recollection within the Oathsworn Pit|thumb|right]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, the Daedric Prince Ithelia returned to [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbic]] affairs. Vaermina and Peryite invaded and nearly destroyed Apocrypha via a realm corroding plague unleashed by the Taskmaster, in their efforts to retrieve the Glyphics that held the last recollection of Ithelia. Though the two Princes were foiled with the aid of [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]], in the chaos, the Dremora Torvesard managed to recover the final Glyphic and regain his lost memories of Ithelia.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=NecromEvents|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard|}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Calamity|[[ON:A Calamity of Fate| A Calamity of Fate]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Unhealthy|[[ON:An Unhealthy Fate| An Unhealthy Fate]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With Torvesard's memories restored, Hermaeus Mora's spell started to weaken, causing other servants of Ithelia to start recovering some of their lost knowledge. These servants of the Prince of Paths started looking for artifacts which would help with the release and restoration of their Prince.{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=PoFate}} Concurrently, the [[Lore:Recollection|Recollection]], a [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] cult of [[Lore:Ayleid Revivalists|Ayleid revivalists]] dedicated to Ithelia, formed from the descendants of her ancient worshippers that had fled to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], under the leadership of Valenwood nobleman [[Lore:Nantharion|Nantharion Rayn]]. Plagued by the feeling of an "aching hole" in the world, an "emptiness where something should be but nothing is", the Recollection formed with the goal of restoring their Prince and lost realm, an event dubbed the "Day of Remembering".{{ref|name=FN|{{Cite Book|Faenalir's Letter|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=NJ|{{Cite Book|Niborwen's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|{{Cite book|ns_base=ON|The Day of Remembering}}}}{{ref|{{Cite book|A Call for Recollection}}}}{{ref|{{Cite book|ns_base=ON|Warning to the Weald}}}}

The [[Lore:The Blind|Blind]], a powerful Daedric servant of Ithelia aided by her cultists, the [[Lore:The Blind Path|Blind Path]], attacked the [[Lore:Maelstrom Arena|Maelstrom Arena]]'s Bedlam Veil in an effort to retrieve the Echonir.{{ref|name=vault|Events of [[Online:The Forgotten Vault|The Forgotten Vault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ILS|{{Cite Book|Initiate's Last Sight|ns_base=Online}}}} The Recollection attacked the [[Lore:Oathsworn Pit|Oathsworn Pit]], training grounds of the [[Lore:Oathsworn|Oathsworn]], an [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] warrior sect dedicated to [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], in an effort to retrieve the [[Lore:Staff of Many Paths|Staff of Many Paths]],{{ref|name=Dulzush|[[ON:Dulzush Forgefire|Dulzush Forgefire]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Bazrag|[[ON:Chief Bazrag|Chief Bazrag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Strife|Events of [[ON:Victory Through Strife|Victory Through Strife]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and Torvesard himself sought out the relics of the three Princes that had delivered Ithelia's defeat, the Lamp of Clarity, the sword Abolisher, and the Skein of Secrets.{{ref|name=PoFate}} Though the Recollection and Blind Path were repelled with the aid of the Vestige and the Echonir wasn't seized,{{ref|name=vault|Events of [[Online:The Forgotten Vault|The Forgotten Vault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the Staff of Many Paths was taken by the Recollection.{{ref|name=Strife|Events of [[ON:Victory Through Strife|Victory Through Strife]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-quest-Prisoner of Fate.jpg|Torvesard using Abolisher to cut open the path to Ithelia|thumb|right]]

Investigating the threads of fate and witnessing the memories of Ithelia’s imprisonment, [[Lore:Leramil the Wise|Leramil the Wise]] and the Vestige pursued Torvesard after he stole Abolisher from the hidden shrine of Boethiah where it was kept. Passing through the submerged shrine of Anudnabia, Torvesard used the blade to enter the Mythos and attempt to free his Prince. He summoned Daedra allied with Ithelia to delay the Vestige and carved a path into the spire believed to be her prison, only to find it empty. Ithelia was already gone. In remembering her, Torvesard restored fragments of her lost power, weakening her bonds until she broke free. He fled through a portal to seek her out, convinced she would be drawn to places tied to her fragmented memories. Hermaeus Mora then revealed the truth. The spire was never the prison. Forgetting was. Once Ithelia was remembered, her shackles failed. Unable to foresee what followed, Mora sent Leramil and the Vestige away to await a new path to open.{{ref|name=PoFate}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Mora}}
[[File:ON-render-Infinite Archive 08.png|Tho'at Replicanum's invasion of the Infinite Archive|thumb|left]]
Around the same time, [[Lore:Tho'at Replicanum|Tho'at Replicanum]], a [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lord]] associated with Ithelia{{ref|group=UOL|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JhQeU-Izx1w Youtube Stream of ESO's ''10-Year Anniversary Celebration Livestream''], time stamp 38:53-39:36}}{{ref|name=LAIA}}{{ref|name=ESO2024GR|[[General:ESO 2024 Global Reveal|ESO 2024 Global Reveal]]}} who had taken on the form of "vivified ink", invaded the [[Lore:Infinite Archive|Infinite Archive]], an Adjunct Realm of Apocrypha, in search of something. Prior to this event many fateline connections between the Index, the archival mechanism that restored things within the realm that were destroyed, and its contents broke. Tho'at splintered her [[Lore:Nymic|nymic]] and imbued it into the tomes of the Archive, allowing her to create a potentially infinite number of [[Lore:Maligraphy|Maligraphies]], Daedric facsimiles of vivified ink that replicated the creatures and objects described in the texts that spawned them, extensions of Tho'at herself.{{ref|name=ESOMM|[[ON:Master Malkhest|Master Malkhest]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:The Margins of Ire|The Margins of Ire]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=MalkhestArchive|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}}

Though replicas, each Tho'at Replicanum copy had a slightly different nymic, so they were all separate beings to one another, and different nymics had to be gathered to banish each. 
The Vestige and Malkhest would continue working to banish the spreading presence of Tho'at and the Maligraphies from the Infinite Archive.{{ref|name=ESOMM}}{{ref|name=ESOMMR|[[ON:Master Malkhest|Master Malkhest]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:Replication Elimination|Replication Elimination]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Return and Restoration===
[[File:ON-npc-Ithelia.jpg|Ithelia in Elenglynn|thumb|left]]
In the [[Lore:West Weald|West Weald]], the Recollection used the Staff of Many Paths to imbue [[Lore:Greenspring Seeds|Greenspring Seeds]] with [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] energies. These seeds are normally used by [[Lore:Greenspeakers|Greenspeakers]] to grow new sections of forest, but the corruption strengthened and twisted their [[Lore:Magic|magic]], creating Wildburn Seeds. The Recollection used these to cause a thick jungle to cover much of the West Weald overnight, and in doing so created the [[Lore:Wildburn|Wildburn]] on its outskirts, an area of magical land corruption that mutated living beings.{{ref|name=Darolith|[[Online:Greenspeaker Darolith|Greenspeaker Darolith]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GreenspeakerSorilen|[[ON:Greenspeaker Sorilen|Greenspeaker Sorilen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SeedsSuspicion|Events of [[ON:Seeds of Suspicion|Seeds of Suspicion]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Nantharion|[[ON:King Nantharion|King Nantharion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}{{ref|name=Tribune|[[ON:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Nantharion Rayn then led Bosmer settlers from [[Lore:Malabal Tor|Malabal Tor]] into this forest, named it the Dawnwood, and founded [[Lore:Vashabar|Vashabar]] atop the Ayleid ruins of Miscarcand, crowning himself its king. There, he and many of his followers abandoned the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]] in favor of a new philosophy known as the [[Lore:Dawnway|Dawnway]].{{ref|name=Nantharion|[[ON:King Nantharion|King Nantharion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MtCN|{{Cite book|Meet the Character - Nantharion}}}}{{ref|name=Gambit|Events of [[Online:King Nantharion's Gambit|King Nantharion's Gambit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At the same time, mystical events called Mirrormoor Incursions spread throughout the Weald as Ithelia’s realm bled into Nirn and her Daedra searched for their lost Prince.{{ref|name=Incursions|[[Online:Mirrormoor Incursions|Mirrormoor Incursions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-npc-Shardmarshal Vargas.jpg|Shardmarshal Vargas with Abolisher in the Feldagard Keep|thumb|right]]
Stripped of her memories and power, Ithelia wandered the West Weald until she was drawn to the ruins of Elenglynn, once an Ayleid citadel devoted to her worship,{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=PathsChaos|Events of [[Online:Paths of Chaos|Paths of Chaos]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} where the Vestige and Leramil encountered her while following disturbances in fate. There, Ithelia briefly relived a past confrontation with Hermaeus Mora over her manipulation of destiny before vanishing once more.{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=PathsChaos}} The crisis deepened at [[Lore:Feldagard Keep|Feldagard Keep]], where Ithelia’s lieutenant [[Lore:Shardmarshal Vargas|Shardmarshal Vargas]] led the Recollection and [[Lore:Shardborn|Shardborn]] using the artifact Abolisher to open portals that bypassed the keep’s defenses. With the aid of [[Lore:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]], the Vestige repelled the attack, pursued Vargas into Mirrormoor, and seized the artifact.{{ref|name=PathsChaos|Events of [[Online:Paths of Chaos|Paths of Chaos]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Cohort|Events of [[Online:The Missing Cohort|The Missing Cohort]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Hold|Events of [[Online:Hold the Line|Hold the Line]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}{{ref|name=Tribune|[[ON:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Fate’s ripples then led the Vestige and [[Lore:Beragon Sharp-Dagger|Beragon Sharp-Dagger]] near Vashabar, where they uncovered King Nantharion’s alliance with the Recollection and their role in the Wildburn, and where his attempt to create a new Wildburn Seed using the Staff of Many Paths was interrupted, unleashing its power prematurely and destroying the Recollection’s living fortress of [[Lore:Hoperoot|Hoperoot]].{{ref|name=Darolith|[[Online:Greenspeaker Darolith|Greenspeaker Darolith]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GreenspeakerSorilen|[[ON:Greenspeaker Sorilen|Greenspeaker Sorilen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SeedsSuspicion|Events of [[ON:Seeds of Suspicion|Seeds of Suspicion]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Nantharion|[[ON:King Nantharion|King Nantharion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}{{ref|name=Tribune|[[ON:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-npc-King Nantharion 02.jpg|King Nantharion before the Great Well, wielding the Staff of Many Paths|thumb|right]]
After a memory of Ithelia was revealed by the Echonir at Hoperoot, the Vestige traveled to the Outcast Inn. There they met Leramil, who had been investigating a dangerous knot forming in the threads of fate leading to the location. Leramil explained that an unknown force was tangling the threads, disrupting the concepts of cause and effect and allowing impossible events, such as a town erupting in flames without a spark. If left unchecked, the threads could snap, collapsing localized reality and erasing everything present from existence. Inside the inn, the Vestige found Ithelia, whose presence was warping the surroundings and pulling echoes from other Paths into reality. Clouded by converging realities, Ithelia asked for help gathering the reagants needed to create a beverage called Sage's Dream, which she may drink to regain control. In the inn's cellar, she uncovered the Lamp of Clarity, imbued with Azura's will, which revealed a series of portals to other Paths. There, the Vestige and the Prince witnessed alternate Ithelias: one defiant, one surrendered, and one so enraged she unraveled her Path entirely, despite Hermaeus Mora’s efforts to stop her. The Vestige successfully brewed Sage's Dream from the essence of Ithelia’s reflections across the Paths, strengthening her connection to her alternate selves. With control restored and more memory and power regained, Ithelia lamented her nature as a Daedric Prince and the consequences it had wrought before departing.{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=ManyPathsInn|Events of [[Online:The Many Paths|Many Paths]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}

Investigating the ruins of Niryastare at the behest of Beragon, who had tracked the Recollection there, the Vestige recovered the last relic, the Skein of Secrets, and uncovered Nantharion's plan. The Wildburn Seeds were intended to reawaken the magic of the Ayleids sleeping beneath the West Weald, so that the Recollection could use the power of the [[Lore:Ayleid Wells|Great Well]] of Miscarcand, a repository of immense power. At Miscarcand the group made their way to the Well, escaping a memory within which Vargas had locked them through the power of Abolisher in the process. Nantharion attempted to use the Staff to drain the energies of the Great Well in order to restore Ithelia to her ancient power and create a Wildburn that'd cover all of the West Weald. Though Nantharion was slain, the servants of Ithelia retrieved the Staff and channeled the Well's power into her, restoring her ancient might.{{ref|name=Nantharion|[[ON:King Nantharion|King Nantharion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}{{ref|name=Tribune|[[ON:Tribune Alea Idolus|Tribune Alea Idolus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Gambit|Events of [[Online:King Nantharion's Gambit|King Nantharion's Gambit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Ithelia's True Plan===
[[File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg|The Loom of the Untraveled Road|thumb|left]]
Though the Shardborn continued their incursions against the West Weald, Beragon surmised that their attacks were only meant to serve as a distraction to prevent the discovery of Ithelia's true plan. At Beragon's behest, the Vestige used the Skein of Secrets to track Ithelia's presence to the Ayleid ruins of [[Lore:Wendir|Wendir]]. In the ruins, the Vestige used Abolisher to tear open a planar disturbance that Leramil sensed, a portal that had been closed, which the group, consisting of the Vestige, Beragon, Leramil, and Alea, crossed to find themselves in an unknown section of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]]. The group found themselves in a realm that was gradually transforming, with crystals growing from every surface. Leramil realized that Fargrave had once been part of Mirrormoor, and was simply responding to the return of its Prince.{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}

The group was confronted by Shardmarshal Vargas, who banished Beragon and Alea to a dark and timeless mirror-reflection of reality, while Leramil barely shielded herself and the Vestige from this fate. Using the Echonir, they reached an inactive magical gate and restored it through the Lamp of Clarity by accessing a reality where it still functioned. The gate led to a sub-plane housing the Loom of the Untraveled Road, but a dead end forced the Vestige to use the Skein of Secrets, only for a trap that Ithelia had set in place to drain it, preventing the three Princes’ relics from being used against her within her own realm. With no options left, Leramil attempted to portal directly to the Loom, but Vargas diverted the spell, sending them to Ithelia’s former prison. There, Leramil realized the surrounding mirrors were actually windows into other Paths. The Vestige used the Lamp of Clarity to reopen them, restoring Beragon and Alea at the cost of the Lamp’s power. The group then used Abolisher to open a portal back to the Loom.{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}

In the Loom, Ithelia's plan was revealed. It was a great fate-altering engine, a series of portals within portals leading to the Many Paths that would allow Ithelia to access alternate histories from within the Paths and weave them into reality. Ithelia intended to use the Loom to rewrite history so that her conflict with the Princes had never occurred, and ultimately to break the shackles of fate by giving each and every being their own desired history. Leramil explained that the Loom had been the threat Mora foresaw, as its use would not produce Ithelia's intended outcome, but rather cause reality itself to shatter in its attempt to reshape itself as Ithelia desired. The group confronted Shardmarshal Vargas while Ithelia drew in power from the Many Paths through the Loom and Torvesard helped his Prince. The Shardmarshal was defeated, and the Vestige used Abolisher to sunder the portals making up the Loom, destroying it, but also depleting Abolisher's energies in the process. An enraged Ithelia, now filled with so much power from the Many Paths it overwhelmed even her control, vanished.{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Beragon}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}

Soon after, the [[Lore:Watcher|Watchling]] fate-auditor of Hermaeus Mora, [[Lore:Scruut|Scruut]], appeared, warning of terrifying disturbances in fate that would soon unravel all reality. Tracking the disturbance, the Vestige, Scruut, and Leramil, discovered a disturbing truth. Ithelia's power was causing distortions of fate so severe they were developing into reality tears, holes in the fabric of reality itself, the result of a piece of existence itself having been destroyed due to instabilities in fate. Visible as rifts in space filled with nothingness, they would shred and destroy fate threads surrounding them, and erase from existence anything unfortunate enough to touch them. The rifts would keep manifesting more and more across various locations and planes of existence, causing reality to crumble a bit further with each passing moment, multiplying and growing until all existence itself was undone, akin to pulling on a loose thread until one's whole tunic unravels.{{ref|name=Scruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=ItheliasFury|Events of [[ON:Ithelia's Fury|Ithelia's Fury]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
===The Star Falls Again===
With the danger greater than ever, the Vestige took the depleted relics to hidden shrines of the three Princes that had created them, restoring their power. The group then returned to the Loom of the Untraveled road, still an unstable area that could lead to one becoming lost in the Paths despite the Loom's destruction, and used the Echonir to reveal Ithelia's location. The memory revealed Ithelia's final plan, if Hermaeus Mora wouldn't allow her to reclaim her realm of Mirrormoor, then she would simply take Apocrypha from him and rebuild Mirrormoor upon its ruins. The Vestige then left for [[Lore:Ceyond|Ceyond]], to retrieve a shard from one of the first Daedra Ithelia had made, the Primordial Shattered Titan. With the shard, an artifact that could stop Ithelia could be created. The Vestige retrieved the shard but, in the process, they were confronted by Torvesard, who used a reality tear in an attempt to erase them from existence. The Vestige was pushed out of the way by [[Lore:Gadayn|Curate Gadayn]], who was erased instead.{{ref|name=Scruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=ItheliasFury|Events of [[ON:Ithelia's Fury|Ithelia's Fury]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}
[[File:ON-npc-Ithelia 05.jpg|Ithelia in Apocrypha as the Last Tomorrow|thumb|left]]
Using the shard and an arcane ritual, the group forged the [[Lore:Mirror of Truth|Mirror of Truth]], an artifact that forces anyone who gazes into it to confront their innermost self and desires, then entered Apocrypha to stop Ithelia, only to find the realm being rewritten in her image and crawling with her Shardborn. Separated from Scruut, who stayed behind to seal spreading reality tears, the group pushed toward Hermaeus Mora’s sanctum, where Mora had been imprisoned and his power siphoned through the Staff of Many Paths wielded by Torvesard. While Torvesard continued the drain, the Vestige confronted Ithelia and turned the Mirror on her, revealing that before her imprisonment she had known Mora’s prophecy was inevitable and had created her Scions to defy a fate that could not be escaped, knowledge she lost when sealed away. Now swollen with power stolen from the Loom, she knew her mental clarity would soon collapse into madness and agreed to be imprisoned again, but Torvesard refused, turning the Staff on Ithelia in desperation, absorbing her power and Mora’s and imprisoning both Princes. Leramil summoned the Vestige’s allies through portals, and together they struck Torvesard down before he could master the stolen and unstable divine power.{{ref|name=Scruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=ItheliasFury|Events of [[ON:Ithelia's Fury|Ithelia's Fury]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=FateForgottenPrince}}
[[File:ON-npc-Ithelia and Torvesard.jpg|thumb|right|''"And Torvesard, my most faithful. I consign your essence to nothingness, never to return. Be at peace at last."'']]
Freed, Ithelia returned Mora’s power and dispersed her excess energy into the [[Lore:Void|Void]]. She then unmade Torvesard’s [[Lore:Vestige (soul)|vestige]], ending him permanently as a final mercy, since his nature as her servant would have compelled him to reform endlessly in service to her restoration. Agreeing to containment despite knowing it would fail, Ithelia explained that as a Daedric Prince she was unchanging, and that her power would inevitably return, dooming her to madness and fury once more. This slow, chaotic restoration had caused a fate tangle at the Outcast Inn. The Vestige proposed a new solution. If the existence of Daedra and magic made her return inevitable, then perhaps a reality within the Many Paths where such concepts did not exist could contain her. Though initially doubtful that anything but imprisonment could restrain a Daedric Prince, Mora conceded. He found the Path, and together they opened a portal. Before departing, Ithelia thanked the Vestige, granted Leramil a glimpse of the Many Paths to ease her grief over Gadayn’s erasure, and stepped through. Mora then had the Vestige use Abolisher to sever the connection between realities, ensuring Ithelia could never return to endanger the world again.{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=FateForgottenPrince}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Mora}}

===Forgotten Legacy===
With Ithelia gone, the tears in reality were repaired through use of a spell originating from Hermaeus Mora himself. The spell was cast at nexus points in reality, locations which naturally resonate throughout the realms, allowing the energies opposite to the force that created the tears to be inserted into the fabric of reality and resonate across Aurbis, beginning the restoration of all damage to fate, though completion of the process would take some time. The energy that counteracted the damage came from the relics of the three Princes, Abolisher, the Lamp of Clarity, and the Skein of Secrets, which permanently lost their Daedric power and were rendered mundane objects.{{ref|name=Scruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=InMemoryOfQuest|Events of  [[Online:In Memory Of|In Memory Of]] in  [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

Following Ithelia's departure, Hermaeus Mora cast his spell, and Ithelia's memory was removed from every mind across all reality once more, replaced only by recollections of Torvesard's scheme with Vaermina and Peryite, and the Recollection cult. Mirrormoor itself would be remembered, but only as a shattered ruin, whose inhabitants were masterless. Hermaeus Mora and the Vestige, who had earned the Prince's trust, would be the only ones to remember the truth, the last reminder of the Prince of Paths and of a world that might've been. However, though Ithelia was gone, seemingly forever, when questioned on the matter, Mora conceded that even he could not always see the final path the future would take.{{ref|name=InMemoryOf|Discussion with [[Online:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] during [[Online:In Memory Of|In Memory Of]] in  [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}{{ref|name=InMemoryOfQuest|Events of  [[Online:In Memory Of|In Memory Of]] in  [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

==Ithelia and Fate==
[[File:ON-crown store-Mirrormoor Memory Face Marking.jpg|Markings resembling those of Ithelia, found on those touched by Mirrormoor's power over fate's threads|thumb|right]]
As Mistress of the Untraveled Road, Ithelia wields the power to unravel fate and rearrange it to her will, shaping destiny itself. Even her mere presence can tangle fate’s threads or draw echoes of other realities into her own, trapping them between worlds. When fully in command of her power, existence and fate can be bent to accommodate her choices. From her seat in Mirrormoor, when utilizing the Loom of the Untraveled Road, Ithelia can rewrite history, undo mistakes, unlock unrealized possibilities, replace reality with alternate timelines drawn from the Many Paths, or potentially even grant every being a history shaped entirely by their desires. This power is dangerous, however. Its use can create anomalies such as fate knots, or become even worse if they transform into reality tears, which can threaten existence itself. If sufficiently enraged, Ithelia can unravel reality around her, shredding existence until nothing remains. Though reality strives to conform to her will, it may shatter in the attempt to do so. For this potential to endanger reality, either willingly or as a side effect, Ithelia was given the title the Last Tomorrow, a prophesied destroyer of reality and a threat to the pillars of causality as foretold by Hermaeus Mora. Due to the nature of her power, Ithelia's presence is said to untether all consequences, causing fate's threads in her immediate surroundings to seemingly vanish and so effectively disabling the fate sight of those who can perceive them.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}

As a Star-Orphan of the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] before becoming a Daedric Prince, Ithelia was the White Star and Queen of Fates Unwritten. Additionally, she is said to will that a [[Lore:Hero|Prisoner Unbound]], beings that can forge their own fate, always exist.{{Ref|name=TNC}}
[[File:ON-crown store-Mirrormoor Memory Body Marking.jpg|Markings resembling those of Ithelia, found on those touched by Mirrormoor's power over fate's threads|thumb|left]]
As a Daedric Prince, after Ithelia used her power to alter fate to avert a coming catastrophe, Mora examined her fate threads and perceived that Ithelia's power had already caused irreparable damage to the fabric of fate and that, if allowed to continue, her very existence endangered all of reality. Mora's fears intensified when Ithelia created her [[Lore:Loom of the Untraveled Road|Loom of the Untraveled Road]], a great fate altering engine via which she intended to tap into the power of the Many Paths and give every being their own desired history, breaking the shackles of fate. Believing that Ithelia's plans would result in the end of reality, Mora repeatedly confronted her to demand she stop, but she refused to listen. Because of this, Mora, Azura, Boethiah, and Mephala, warred against Ithelia and imprisoned her within the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha, after which Mora erased all memory of her existence from reality.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Rakzzin-jo|[[ON:Adept Rakzzin-jo|Adept Rakzzin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Staff of Many Paths|Staff of Many Paths]] was a powerful artifact associated with Ithelia, capable of causing strange phenomena such as a novice soundly defeating an expert warrior or objects to disappear to take place around it. The Staff also possessed the ability to drain, hold, and imbue beings and objects with immense amounts of energy. After Torvesard, its last known wielder, was defeated, the artifact vanished in a flash of light and its fate is unknown.{{ref|name=Vargas}}{{ref|name=SeedsSuspicion|Events of [[ON:Seeds of Suspicion|Seeds of Suspicion]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Gambit|Events of [[Online:King Nantharion's Gambit|King Nantharion's Gambit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=FateForgottenPrince}}

==Ithelia and the Many Paths==
[[File:ON-npc-Ithelia 03.jpg|An alternate Ithelia|thumb|left]][[File:ON-npc-Ithelia 04.jpg|A different alternate Ithelia|thumb|right]]
As the Prince of Paths, Ithelia can perceive all the Many Paths at once. Though she can't outright control the Many Paths, she can travel between them, or follow their threads and reach out to alter their fates. All alternate Ithelias across the Many Paths are connected to one another, a connection strengthened when Ithelia imbibes the Sage's Dream, a brew formed from the essence of many of her "reflections" from different Paths, which allows her to incorporate knowledge and power from them. Ithelia can also share her perception of the Many Paths with others, allowing them to briefly view the Many Paths as she herself does.{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}   

Through her power to influence the Many Paths, Ithelia is said to be able to reshape the fates. Using her Loom of the Untraveled Road, a series of portals within portals leading to the Many Paths, it is said Ithelia can access alternate fates and histories and weave them into reality to replace the history that exists. Ithelia's ultimate goal prior to her imprisonment was to use the Loom to give each being their own desired history and so break fate's shackles entirely.{{ref|name=Ithelia}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}} Fearing that Ithelia's unnatural manipulations, which had already caused the Many Paths to splinter, would lead to the end of reality, Hermaeus Mora and three allied Daedric Princes took action to imprison her.{{ref|name=Mora}}

==Servants==
[[File:ON-concept-Shardborn Style.jpg|thumb|left|Clan Shardborn Armor and Weapons]][[File:ON-concept-Lucent Sentinel Style.jpg|thumb|right|Lucent Sentinel Armor and Weapons]]
The [[Lore:Shardborn|Shardborn]] are the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] servants of Ithelia.{{ref|name=ESO2024GR}} For milennia, the Dremora [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]] was Ithelia's last remaining servant, having had just enough of his memory preserved to come and save her.{{ref|name=ONTorvesard}} Ithelia has many glass-like crystalline servants in her service, they often took more familiar forms such as dragons, mantikoras, mind terrors, and hollow sentinels.{{ref|name=ESO2024GR}}{{Ref|name=TFV|[[ON:The Forgotten Vault|The Forgotten Vault]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Scions of Ithelia|Scions of Ithelia]]''}} Among her more unique servants are [[Lore:Crystal Atronach|Crystal Atronachs]], [[Lore:Crystaljack|Crystaljacks]], [[Lore:Fractured Remnant|Fractured Remnants]], [[Lore:Mirrorplasm|Mirrorplasms]], [[Lore:Shattered Shard|Shattered Shards]], and [[Lore:Skeletal Shard|Skeletal Shard]]s.

[[Lore:Tho'at Replicanum|Tho'at Replicanum]] is a [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lord]] composed of living glass like these servants, who was responsible for invading the [[Lore:Infinite Archive|Infinite Archive]].{{ref|name=LAIA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}} The Blind was a glass-like large Dremora servant of Ithelia who invaded the Bedlam Veil.{{ref|name=ILS|{{Cite Book|Initiate's Last Sight|ns_base=Online}}}}

Ithelia's Daedric servants, the Unseen, such as the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] Torvesard, also possess a degree of ability to manipulate the strands of fate. This allows them to accomplish tasks such as entering the [[Lore:Knower's Eye|Eyes of Mora]] without the corresponding key and even to alter the memories of the Prince contained there, thereby rewriting the event depicted itself. In addition this ability to influence fate has been used against enemies, be it to banish them to the Many Paths, casting them through both physical distance and realities, or to use reality tears to erase them from existence. They  are not visible to the gaze of other Daedric Princes, even that of Hermaeus Mora, which is why they're called the Unseen, but their presence can be felt indirectly through the distortion they cause in fate's threads.{{ref|name=Mora}}{{ref|name=Leramil}}{{ref|name=Vargas}}{{ref|name=ONTorvesard|}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}}

Because of the ability they and their Prince possess to influence fate, the Daedra of Mirrormoor believe that there is no fate that can't be altered. To reflect this belief, they often wear designs of glimmering threads upon their skin, to remind themselves that one's fate and future are one's own making.{{ref|name=Face|[[ON:Threads of Mirrormoor Face Art|Threads of Mirrormoor Face Art]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Mirrormoor Daedra believed themselves to be the owners of the [[Lore:Arcane Knot|Arcane Knot]], a dangerous mystical object said to possess the power to connect the different realities, the Many Paths, to one another, treating them as if they'd never been separate to begin with, and even allowing one to cross over from one to the next. The Knot was long held within the Lucent Citadel in [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]], until a group of adventurers retrieved it for the Scribes of Mora, having defeated the Mirrormoor Daedra attempting to reclaim it.{{ref|name=Keshargo|[[ON:Keshargo|Keshargo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Neletai|[[ON:Neletai|Neletai]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ScribeNairoline|[[ON:Lucent Citadel|Scribe Nairoline's Letter]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=KnotAwaits|Events of [[ON:The Knot Awaits|The Knot Awaits]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|nb1|^1|''[[Lore:The Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]]'', a religious text of Ayleid origins, mentions the "Numinous Paravant", a being with "unbound hands", and thus the potential to achieve the goal of Ithelia to reach Numancia, breaking the shackles of fate and giving everyone their own desired history. Ithelia's Ayleid worshippers seemed to believe they could somehow cause the Numinous Paravant to appear by committing violence against Man.{{ref|name=TNC}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}} The Numinous Paravant might've been [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]], who rebelled against the Ayleids, and one of whose titles is "Paravant".{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}}}} 
*{{LNote|nb2|^2|Ithelia was worshipped during the Mythic and earlier age{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOTG}} and the imprisonment of Ithelia occurred after [[Lore:Morrowind|Resdayn]] was settled.{{ref|name=CG|[[ON:Curate Gadayn|Curate Gadayn]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] author of ''[[Lore:Memories Lost|Memories Lost]]'' recounts waking with the inexplicable sense that something important was missing, even though no memory of it remained, a clue Torvesard later used in the search that eventually revealed Ithelia had been erased.{{Ref|name=MTCT|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Torvesard}}}}}}
*[[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] referred to Fargrave as "a shattered ruin of reflection and sorrow", making it possible that he might have known some elements of the realm's past.{{ref|name=LA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City}}}}
*In ''[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]]'', the Bosmer [[Lore:Deslandra|Deslandra]] attempted to enact an event known as the Great Nullification, which would forever sever the flow of [[Lore:Magic|magicka]] and its influences on [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Cyrodiil Gazetteer''}}{{rp|52}}{{ref|name=HRGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''High Rock Gazetteer''}}{{rp|50}}{{ref|name=VWGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Valenwood Gazetteer''}}{{rp|38}}{{ref|name=BOTSEAB|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Limited Edition Art and Lore Book''}}{{rp|19}} In some playthroughs where the heroes are defeated, she can succeed, leaving one of those failed playthroughs as a potential reality Ithelia could have exiled herself to.
*The ultimate goal of Ithelia, a former Magna Ge, is described in the [[Lore:Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]], an Ayleid religious text, as cleaving through everything to reach "Numancia", the breaking of the shackles of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and the limitations of linear time imposed by [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Ada-Mantia]], to give every being their own desired history.{{ref|name=TNC|}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}} In the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Mythic Dawn Commentaries]], the term "Nu-Mantia" is used by [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] as an exclamation associated with "liberty" and the "promise of paradise", the goal of the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]]. As Mehrunes Dagon, the Mythic Dawn's patron deity, is said in the same text to have been created by the Magna Ge, the two concepts might be related.{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}
*[[Lore:The Wandering Spirits|Wandering Spirits]] recounts the tale of [[Lore:Akha|Akha]], who vanished in the South, with [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]], who has himself been described as both weaver and embodiment of time's all-encompassing tapestry,{{Ref|name=Ja'darri|[[Online:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} appearing in his place, and bearing his crown and rule of his kingdoms across the Many Paths. This event might be the same as the disappearance of Aka linked to the afixing of Ada-Mantia that Ithelia is said to have witnessed, from a different cultural perspective.{{Ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}
*Ithelia's claim that Daedra are "unchanging" flies in the face of [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s claim that ''"[e]verything changes. Even Daedric Princes. Especially Daedric Princes."''{{ref|name=SISheogorath|[[Shivering:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Given Sheogorath's madness, it is impossible to tell whether this was a statement of fact or just a reference to his transformation into [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]].
