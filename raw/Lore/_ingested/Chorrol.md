[[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]]  is a [[Lore:Colovia|Colovian]] town in northwestern [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], between the [[Lore:Great Forest|Great Forest]] of west Cyrodiil and the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian Highlands]] of the [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] border.{{ref|name=OB|[[OB:Chorrol|Chorrol]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} It is a boulder-strewn area where large oak canopies shade its streets.{{Ref|name=TTaN|{{Cite Book|Tamriel, Now and Then – Places|ns_base=General}}}} Citizens of Chorrol are known as "Chorrolians".{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}}<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:OB-place-Great Chapel of Stendarr.jpg|The Great Chapel of Stendarr|left|thumb]][[File:OB-ChorrolShield.png|Symbol of Chorrol|right|thumb|Symbol of Chorrol]]
The city is divided into five main districts: Fountain Gate, Castle Chorrol, Chapel Street, West District, and the Great Oak Place.{{ref|name=GtC|{{Cite Book|Guide to Chorrol}}}} It is protected by thick walls and guard towers.{{ref|name=OB}} Chorrol also possesses its own sewer system.{{Ref|name=OBCrumbling|[[OB:Crumbling Mine|Crumbling Mine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===County Chorrol===
[[File:OB-place-Sancre Tor.jpg|thumb|right|Sancre Tor]]
'''County Chorrol''' is a region centered around the eponymous city in western Cyrodiil and is ruled by the Count of Chorrol. Like other counties in the province, its size is comparable to the [[Lore:Hold|holds]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]],{{Ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} but their borders have yet to be explicitly defined on any map. The north-northwest corner of Cyrodiil, northwest of the [[Lore:Orange Road|Orange Road]] in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] is confirmed part of the county. Some notable locations in this area include [[Lore:Cloud Top|Cloud Top]], [[Lore:Lipsand Tarn|Lipsand Tarn]], [[Lore:Ninendava|Ninendava]], and [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]]. Chorrol is also located where the Great Forest meets the Colovian Highlands in the north.{{Ref|name=OBMap|[[:File:OB-map-Cyrodiil.jpg|Map of Cyrodiil – The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion]]}}

The County is known for its plentiful mines and timber. They also export wool and mutton. They are self-described as hard-working, tough and independent mountain people,{{Ref|name=OBArriana|[[Oblivion:Countess Arriana Valga|Countess Arriana Valga]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} as well as god-fearing people that adhere to the Ten Commands.{{Ref|name=OBChanel|[[Oblivion:Chanel|Chanel]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=OBSelvilo|[[Oblivion:Gureryne Selvilo|Gureryne Selvilo]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==History==
===Merethic and First Era===
[[File:ON-place-Chorrol 02.jpeg|The Great Oak circa {{Year|2E 582}}|right|thumb]]
While not much is known about the town's early history, the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] knight [[Lore:Cadwell|Cadwell]] hailed from Chorrol,{{Ref|name=LSD|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Lawrence Schick's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} and Chorrol has housed a chapel to [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]] since at least the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. The position of Archbishop of Chorrol was filled by one Vincius Imbrex by no earlier than {{Year|1E 1051}}.{{ref|name=TFA|{{Cite Book|The Four Abominations}}}} The [[Lore:Academy of Chorrol|Academy of Chorrol]] was a scholarly institution which was based in the city at some point, but very little is known about it.{{ref|name=AoH|{{Cite Book|Aspects of Lord Hircine}}}} The first Count of Chorrol was [[Lore:Kantus Jeril|Kantus Jeril]], who was appointed for his heroism at the Battle of Pale Pass.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Loading Screens|Oblivion loading screens]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Chorrol.jpg|Chorrol circa {{Year|2E 582}}|thumb|left]]
The city had become a focal point for merchants in the shade of an oak tree since the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] when it was known as "Little Oak Place".{{ref|name=TCC}}

[[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] was the Duke of Chorrol before [[Lore:Leovic|Emperor Leovic]]'s legalization of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship caused him to take up arms against the Emperor in the [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]] in {{Year|2E 576}}.{{ref|name=CotFC1|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}} After being defeated at [[Lore:Fort Ash|Fort Ash]], Varen took control of the Legion of Chorrol, enabling him to defeat Leovic's troops and force them to retreat into the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]], paving the way for the [[Lore:Colovia|Colovian Estates]] to unite and fight under his banner.{{ref|name=EfEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}} During the Colovian Revolt, Varen drew his strongest support from the Colovian Estates, including the old nobility of County Chorrol.{{ref|name=SZLMIwLS|{{Cite Book|Special ZOS Lore Master Interview with Lawrence Schick|ns_base=GEN}}}}

The powerful [[Lore:Motierre|Motierre]] family have also been firmly established in Chorrol since at least {{Year|2E 579}}.{{ref|name=TCC}} Chorrol was significantly affected by the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] in {{Year|2E 579}} when earthquakes ruptured the ground, splitting the town and leaving a gaping fissure infested with [[Lore:Flame Atronach|flame atronachs]] that killed Doran, the town's prefect. Wooden walkways were erected over the chasm but the noxious fumes caused many to fall to their deaths while crossing.{{ref|name=TCC}} Many buildings, including the Great Chapel, were damaged or destroyed by the constant earthquakes, with the Oak and Crosier inn resorting to nailing furniture to the floor.{{ref|name=Gil|[[ON:Gil Brousseau|Gil Brousseau]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}. During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the town was initially located within the territory held by the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], although it changed hands many times over the course of the war. However, the real power over the roads around Chorrol was determined by control over nearby {{Lore Link|Fort Rayles}}.{{ref|[[ON:Chorrol|Chorrol]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point following the [[Lore:Sack of Sancre Tor|Sack of Sancre Tor]] in {{Year|2E 852}},{{ref|name=BoST|{{Cite Book|Battle of Sancre Tor}}}} a statue dedicated to [[Lore:Osla|Saint Osla]] entitled "The Touch of the Healer's Hand" was erected just inside the city gates.{{ref|name=OBPrima|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|361}} Known locally as "the fountain",{{ref|[[OB:Chorrol Guard|Chorrol Guard]] dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} this statue depicts Saint Osla ministering to the fallen at the Sack of Sancre Tor.{{ref|name=OBPrima}}{{rp|361}} It was erected in memory of all who died in that great battle. The district near the town's main gate came to be known as Fountain Gate following its construction. At some point, the statue seemingly was indeed a fountain and was surrounded by a pool, but by {{Year|3E 433}} it had dried up.{{ref|name=GtC}}{{ref|The [[OB:Chorrol|statue]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Third Era===
[[File:OB-place-Chorrol.jpg|The Great Oak of Chorrol|thumb|right]][[File:OB-place-Chorrol 03.jpg|The statue of [[Lore:Osla|St. Osla]] at Fountain Gate|left|thumb]]
At the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Chorrol was ruled by Countess [[Lore:Arriana Valga|Arriana Valga]], widow of the hereditary Count [[Lore:Charus Valga|Charus Valga]] of Chorrol.{{ref|name=GtC}} Circa {{Year|3E 432}}, a political marriage was arranged between Lady [[Lore:Alessia Caro|Alessia]] of Chorrol and Count [[Lore:Marius Caro|Marius Caro]] of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]].{{ref|{{PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, Chorrol came under siege by the forces of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] when an [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|Oblivion Gate]] opened outside the city walls during the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{ref|[[OB:Allies for Bruma|Allies for Bruma]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
{{NewLine}}

===Fourth Era===
In the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the [[Lore:Crimson Dirks|Crimson Dirks]] raided Colovian furs from the city.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Crimson Dirks}}}} Members of the Dirks would sometimes gather at The Grey Mare.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Casival's Note|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}
