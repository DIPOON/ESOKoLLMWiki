[[Lore:Gilane|Gilane]] is one of the eight major cities in the province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} and is located on the south-central coast near the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]], across the isthmus toward [[Lore:Hew's Bane|Hew's Bane]].{{Ref|name=RGMap|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}} In the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city was the seat of power for the eponymous '''Kingdom of Gilane'''.{{Ref|name=TWQ4|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v4}}}}<noinclude>
{{NewLine}}

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-interior-Volenfell 02.jpg|thumb|right|Volenfell]]
[[File:ON-place-Prince's Gate.jpg|thumb|left|The Prince's Gate]]
Gilane is situated in southern Hammerfell, just south of the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]].{{Ref|name=LOMap|[[:File:LO-map-Hammerfell (Anthology).jpg|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls Anthology'']]}}{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Hammerfell}}}} Sources from the late Third Era suggest that the [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]] clan's former capital city, [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]] is located somewhere outside the town.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Just further south is an isthmus that connects the mainland with the Hew's Bane peninsula,{{Ref|name=RGMap}} formerly known as [[Lore:Khefrem|Khefrem]]'s Boot.{{Ref|name=IDOPH|{{Cite Book|In Defense of Prince Hubalajad}}}} The main road runs right on it and is surrounded by a rocky mountain pass that begins in Gilane and ends at the Prince's Gate, an old, fortified structure on Hew's Bane.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}{{Ref|name=ONHews|[[Online:Hew's Bane|Hew's Bane]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}}

Gilane is specifically on the west side of the isthmus, on the large bay it shares with Hegathe, where it has a port.{{Ref|name=LOMap}}{{Ref|name=AR}} Though the city's allegiance is unknown, the southern coast of Hammerfell is predominantly held by the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]].{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Gilane was neighbored by several settlements, such as [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] to the far west and Lainebon Place to the northeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

==History==
When Gilane was founded is unknown, however, the Tavan wave of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] was known to have settled this portion of the province.{{Ref|name=CM28|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} By the late first century of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] in {{Year|3E 98}}, the twenty-one-year-old Prince [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus]] was engaged to the Queen of Gilane, Bianki,{{Ref|name=TWQ3|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v3}}}} and eleven years later, the two were married and had a healthy brood of children.{{Ref|name=TWQ4|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v4}}}}{{intnote|Wife|[Note 1]}} His reign over the kingdom was prosperous and his relationship with other kingdoms were peaceful, much like with his siblings and their domains.{{Ref|name=TWQ5|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v5}}}}

When the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]] started in {{Year|3E 121}}, King Cephorus and his Redguard army were at the forefront of the conflict, especially as he re-conquered western [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] in the summer of {{Year|3E 125}}. Cephorus' armies were focused throughout High Rock and eventually moved back into Hammerfell, where he confronted [[Lore:Uriel III|Uriel III]]'s armies in [[Lore:Ichidag|Ichidag]] in {{Year|3E 127}}. Cephorus' forces overtook Uriel III and the King arranged his transport to his castle in Gilane, where he would have been put on trial. But before the Emperor arrived, an angry crowd tore through his carriage and killed him. Not long after the incident, King Cephorus was proclaimed the Emperor of Tamriel as Cephorus Septim I.{{Ref|name=TWQ7|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v7}}}}{{Ref|name=BOTWQ|{{Cite Book|Biography of the Wolf Queen}}}} By {{Year|3E 136}}, Queen Bianki had passed away and all of her children died during the War of the Red Diamond, and four years later in {{Year|3E 140}}, Emperor Cephorus I passed away himself after falling off a [[Lore:Horse|horse]].{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Gilane was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Coc and had a rivalry with Hegathe and [[Lore:Taneth|Taneth]].{{Ref|name=AR}} Not much is known about Gilane during the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], but by {{Year|4E 172}}, the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] claimed the southern coast of Hammerfell and all of its cities, except for the former capital, Hegathe. When the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]] was signed in {{Year|4E 175}}, it marked the official cession of southern Hammerfell to the Thalmor, but five years later in {{Year|4E 180}}, the Redguards reclaimed it after the signing of the [[Lore:Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai|Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai]]. Much of the region was left devastated after the war, however.{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-icon-armor-Helm-Abah's Watch.png|thumb|right|Abah's Watch helm imported from Gilane]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Redguard#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Redguard#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Redguard page.''
The musk of the local [[Lore:Fennec Fox|Fennec Fox]] is made into a perfume that is considered very fashionable for the nobility of southern Hammerfell, including Gilane.{{Ref|name=ONContra|[[Online:Contraband B#Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume|Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Gilane is known to export some of its goods, such as turbaned conical helmets and fine glass goblets with gilt brims.{{ref|name=CM32:AW|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 32: Abah's Watch Style}}}}{{Ref|name=SoFGG|[[Online:Contraband/Drinkware#Set of Four Gilane Goblets|Set of Four Gilane Goblets]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Greens seasoned with [[Lore:Garlic|garlic]] is local dish in Gilane.{{Ref|name=ONGreens|[[Online:Gilane Garlicky Greens|Gilane Garlicky Greens]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Like other cities on the Hammerfell coast, the Redguards of Gilane observe the 20th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] as the [[Lore:Day of Shame|Day of Shame]]. According to legend, the [[Lore:Crimson Ship|Crimson Ship]], a vessel full of [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]] victims of the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] first left [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] on the 9th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]] and was denied refuge from every port on Tamriel. It was later westbound for the Abecean Sea and was never seen again. It is said to one day return and the Day of Shame was created in remembrance of that.{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|7}}{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Shame|Day of Shame]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}
