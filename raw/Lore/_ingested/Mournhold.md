[[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] (also known as '''Mourning Hold'''){{Ref|name=36LOVS8|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 8}}}} is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – The Elder Scrolls: Arena]]}} located in the heart of the [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] region. The city is perhaps the largest and potentially oldest in Morrowind, and the district seat of the [[Lore:House Indoril|Great House Indoril]], staunch followers of Dunmer faith and firm believers in preserving their native culture.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=GHOM|{{Cite Book|Great Houses of Morrowind}}}}{{Ref|name=UHI|{{Cite Book|Understanding House Indoril}}}} It is also the epicenter of Dunmer religion, where the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] reigned over the province{{Ref|name=PGE1}} until they were replaced by the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]].{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}}

The author [[Lore:Enric Milres|Enric Milres]] described the city as "dark and brooding",{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Alik'r}}}} but to others, it is noted for its grand vastness and majestic architecture.{{Ref|name=POM}}{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|EGT|Morrowind}}}} For many years, it was the home of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], one of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god-kings of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} As her holy city, it attracted pilgrims and temple priests to her palace, [[Lore:Mournhold Temple|Mournhold Temple]].{{Ref|name=APGTM}} Both the city and the greater one that surrounds it are known as '''[[#The City of Almalexia and Mournhold's environs|Almalexia]]'''.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=BOB1}} For much of the Dunmer's history, Mournhold had been the capital of Morrowind{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=APGTM}} until the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], when it was moved to [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]].{{Ref|name=DBAdril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} The city is known by many titles, such as the '''City of Light and Magic''',{{Ref|name=APGTM|{{Cite Book|A Pocket Guide to Mournhold}}}}{{Ref|name=TRGenVoiced}} the '''City of Gems''',{{Ref|name=TRB1|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 1}}}} and the '''City of Love and Charity'''.{{Ref|name=MWGenRumors|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue L#Latest Rumors|Mournhold]] latest rumors in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:TR-map-Mournhold.jpg|thumb|left|Map of Mournhold in the late Third Era]]
[[File:TR-place-Mournhold, Great Bazaar.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Bazaar ca. 3E 427]]
Mournhold is located in south-central Morrowind, in the [[Lore:Lagomere|Lagomere]] region of Deshaan.{{Ref|name=ONMournhold|[[Online:Mournhold|Mournhold]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The region is described as a broad and fertile plain that slowly shifts into the swamps of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Mournhold lies in its very center, surrounded by large mountains, low-lying wetlands, a river that flows west beside the city. Many of the region's roads converge here, whether it is from the northwest by the Obsidian Gorge or the main southern road.{{Ref|name=ONMournhold}} Outside the city lies a crawling area of homes, citizens, vendors,{{Ref|name=SSLW|{{Cite Book|Sotha Sil's Last Words|ns_base=GEN}}}} as well as noble estates, such as Quondam Indorilia, a luxury manor for a noble Indoril family,{{Ref|name=ONQuondam|[[Online:Quondam Indorilia|Quondam Indorilia]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and even King Hlaalu Helseth's palace.{{Ref|name=AGAD}}

There is a lot of buried ruins and subterranean complexes under Mournhold, such as the ruins of [[Lore:Bamz-Amschend|Bamz-Amschend]] and Norenen-dur, and the caves of [[Lore:Old Mournhold|Old Mournhold]].{{Ref|name=TRMournhold|[[Tribunal:Mournhold|Mournhold]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Mournhold is neighbored by several settlements, such as [[Lore:Amber Forest|Amber Forest]] to the east, [[Lore:Darnim Watch|Darnim Watch]] to the north, [[Lore:Eagle Moor|Eagle Moor]] to the west, [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] to the west-northwest, Old Keep to the southwest, and [[Lore:Silnim Dale|Silnim Dale]] to the south.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

Mournhold is noted by the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] chronicler, [[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]] for its ''"graceful green spires, soaring basalt archways, and golden finery along even the most modest buildings."''{{Ref|name=EGT}} Mournhold proper is centered around the large, central building. In the mid-Second Era, this building was the City Center, which housed its bank and administration,{{Ref|name=ONMournhold}} but in the late-Third Era, this was the [[Lore:Royal Palace|Royal Palace]], the city's seat of power.{{Ref|name=TRMournhold|[[Tribunal:Mournhold|Mournhold]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Generally speaking, south of its center is [[Lore:Plaza Brindisi Dorom|Plaza Brindisi Dorom]],{{Ref|name=ONMournhold}}{{Ref|name=TRMap|[[:File:TR-map-Mournhold.jpg|Map of Mournhold – The Elder Scrolls III: Tribunal]]}} an open parkland where Mournhold's people come to seek solace and pray to the Tribunal.{{Ref|name=APGTM}} Its main landmark was a statue depicting Almalexia and Mehrunes Dagon's legendary battle in the late First Era, but in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], House Indoril erected a new building there to house pilgrims and priests.{{Ref|name=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}}

Mournhold has several markets and bazaars,{{Ref|name=APGTM}} like the Royal Bazaar and the Mourner's Market.{{Ref|name=ONMournhold}} But the Great Bazaar is the city's largest.{{Ref|name=APGTM}} In the late Third Era, it was located east of the Royal Palace and was divided by a large bridge. The eastern half housed several establishments, while the western half featured an outdoor theater and several merchant stalls. Its residential district is known as Godsreach, and it was west of the Royal Palace in the late Third Era. It contained several noble manors and a building for artisans called the Craftsmen's Hall.{{Ref|name=TRMournhold}} North of the city's center is Mournhold Temple, the former seat of power of Almalexia. Ever since her downfall, renovations were made to remove her mark and a new sanctuary was added.{{Ref|name=TMFN}} It is surrounded by the Temple Courtyard, a wide, green parkland tended to by gardeners. The foliage and trimmed paths were considered hymns of praise to Almalexia.{{Ref|name=TRPop|[[Tribunal:Gee-Pop Varis|Gee-Pop Varis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The City of Almalexia and Mournhold's environs===
[[File:LG-cardart-Mournhold Pilgrim.png|thumb|right|A Tribunal pilgrim in Mournhold]]
'''Almalexia''' is the larger city that surrounds Mournhold and is named after the Dunmer goddess, Almalexia, who made the city her home.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} It was built in response to [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s growing power in the late years of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ZeniMaxInterview|[[General:ESO Writing Team AUA|ESO Writing Team r/elderscrollsonline Ask Us Anything]]}} Almalexia is described as grand, in a vast, sprawling way{{Ref|name=POM}} while Mournhold is described by the 1st Edition of the ''[[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire|Pocket Guide to the Empire]]'' as a sprawling temple/palace, a city within a city.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

The '''Kingdom of Mournhold''' refers to the political region that surrounds the city.{{Ref|name=BOB1|{{Cite Book|Biography of Barenziah, v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} The Lorddom of Mournhold owns several mines across the kingdom, and its people often work there as laborers.{{Ref|name=TRB1}} According to ''The Real Barenziah'' series, the Staff of Chaos was supposedly kept in [[Lore:Ephen|Ephen]]'s tomb hidden in the kingdom's mines.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} 

Similarly, Almalexia is the central location for the Indoril District, the political territory ruled by House Indoril. In the late Third Era, this land encompassed the Morrowind heartland, from the southern [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] coast to the eastern mainland coast.{{Ref|name=GHOM}} Some locations historically under their governance include [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]],{{Ref|name=ONDavon|[[Online:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Draloris|Draloris]],{{Ref|name=MWMapConcept|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-map-Morrowind Concept.jpg|Concept Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind'']]}} and [[Lore:Gorne|Gorne]].{{Ref|name=ONGivyn|[[Online:Garalo Givyn|Garalo Givyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:TR-interior-Manor District (East Building).jpg|thumb|left|The ruins of the original Mournhold]]
{{Quote Box|''The rivalry between Ebonheart and Mournhold reached back almost to the dawn of Morrowind's history. Once the two nations had been one, all the lucrative mines held in fief by the Ra'athims, whose nobility retained the High Kingship of Morrowind. Ebonheart had split into two separate city-states, Ebonheart and Mournhold, when Queen Lian's twin sons -- grandsons of the legendary King Moraelyn -- were left as joint heirs.''|An excerpt from ''[[Lore:The Real Barenziah, v 5|The Real Barenziah, v 5]]''}}
Mournhold's earliest history is unclear, surrounded by hearsay and contradictory sources. The 1st Edition of the ''Pocket Guide to the Empire'' claims that Mournhold is the oldest city in Morrowind;{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} however, ''[[Lore:The Real Barenziah|The Real Barenziah]]'' book series claims that [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] is the eldest city-state, with its rulers often quoted saying, ''"first among equals"''.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} 

Modern Mournhold is also built over the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] city of [[Lore:Bamz-Amschend|Bamz-Amschend]]. The rumor of its existence has been repeatedly denied by the people of Mournhold,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} but the ruins are undoubtedly beneath it. Bamz-Amschend's history before the Dwemer's disappearance is unknown, though it hosted a Dwemer Council Meet.{{Ref|name=CON|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of Nchuleft}}}} Mournhold is also known as the lost city-state of the [[Lore:First Empire (Mournhold)|First Empire]],{{Ref|name=AR}} but what this is referring to is unknown.

Mournhold and Ebonheart's rivalry dates back to the dawn of Morrowind's history. They were originally part of one nation ruled by the [[Lore:Ra'athim|Ra'athim Clan]],{{Ref|name=TRB4}} a noble family from Ebonheart with an affinity for mining. One of Mournhold's earliest known natives is [[Lore:Ephen|Ephen]], remembered now as a deity of a temple cult and but in life was the step-brother to the Ra'athim Kings, [[Lore:Cruethys|Cruethys]] and [[Lore:Moraelyn|Moraelyn]].{{Ref|name=TRB4}}{{Ref|name=KEVII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VII}}}} The Ra'athim nation was eventually split between two city-states, modern Ebonheart and Mournhold, when Queen Lian's sons, grandsons of King Moraelyn, became joint heirs.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} The Ra'athim Clan was affiliated with [[Lore:House Mora|House Mora]], but by the end of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Mournhold was ruled by House Indoril. The Great House was founded sometime in the early First Era{{Ref|name=UHI}} but its early history with Mournhold is unknown. 

The earliest dated information comes from [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]'s [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|thirty-six lessons]]. Mourning Hold was the capital of Veloth, referring to ancient Morrowind, and was located in the lands of House Indoril.{{Ref|name=36LOVS5|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 5}}}} When [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] arrived with the [[Lore:Dwemer Animunculi|simulacrum]] that bore Vivec, they were greeted by [[Lore:Ayem|Ayem]], the Queen of Mournhold and her "husband-state", a flickering image channeled to her needs. She ruled with her council called [[Lore:The Shouts|the Shouts]], a now forgotten guild who carried the whims of the [[Lore:Chimer|Velothi]] people.{{Ref|name=36LOVS8}}{{intnote|Ayem|[Note 1]}} She also commanded the Orphanage, a legion that assisted Nerevar in a battle with rebellious Dwemer.{{Ref|name=36LOVS30|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 30}}}}{{Ref|name=WMBTM|{{Cite Book|What My Beloved Taught Me|ns_base=GEN}}}} Shortly after this was the legendary [[Lore:Conquest of Morrowind|Conquest of Morrowind]],{{Ref|name=36LOVS8}}{{Ref|name=36LOVS9|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} one of many battles in the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]] between {{Year|1E 240}} and {{Year|1E 416}}.{{Ref|name=TLST|{{Cite Book|The Legendary Sancre Tor}}}}{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|5}}

The [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]] was a significant moment in ancient Morrowind's history. The conflict began when [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] erupted for the first time in {{Year|1E 668}}, known in history as [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]]. The eruption laid waste to at least half of Morrowind, causing a permanent shift of population south into Deshaan,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} where Mournhold presides.{{Ref|name=ONMournhold}} The Dwemer and their automatons attacked Mournhold, destroying its eleven gates. According to Vivec's [[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36|thirty-sixth sermon]], Almalexia retaliated by turning into the ''"Face-Snaked Queen of Three in One,"'' and those who looked upon her were ''"overcome by the meaning of [[Lore:Stars|stars]]"''.{{Ref|name=36LOVS36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}}

===The Fall of Mournhold and Almalexia's Governance===
[[File:MW-place-Plaza Brindisi Dorom 03.jpg|thumb|right|The Statue of Almalexia and Mehrunes Dagon's battle]]
[[File:ON-concept-Tribunal Temple.png|thumb|left|The Tribunal Temple of Mournhold]]
By the late years of the First Era, Mournhold was ruled by Duke-Prince [[Lore:Brindisi Dorom|Indoril Brindisi Dorom]].{{Ref|name=2920V8|{{Cite Book|2920, Last Seed (v8)}}}} As the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]] raged at this time, the Duke often conducted slave-raids in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]], creating a particular grudge with Warchief [[Lore:Ulaqth|Ulaqth]] of [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]].{{Ref|name=2920V3|{{Cite Book|2920, First Seed (v3)}}}} This manifested in the Battle of [[Lore:Bodrum|Bodrum]] when Ulaqth's flank attacked a company waving Mournhold's flag. The Imperial Army was defeated when they fell into a wall of spears in the [[Lore:Pryai River|Pryai River]]. Against the Tribunal's wishes, Brindisi Dorom met with the [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]], leader of the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], to plan out Emperor [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]]'s assassination, but officially, it was never fulfilled.{{Ref|name=2920V8}}

The Duke was present with the Tribunal and Prince [[Lore:Juilek|Juilek]] in Fervinthil to sign the [[Lore:Cervant Truce|Cervant Truce]], and later in Ald Lambasi to celebrate the end of the Four-Score War.{{Ref|name=2920V8}} In early [[Lore:Sun's Dusk|Sun's Dusk]], a red mist hung over Mournhold as lightning flashed in the sky and hard winds bellowed across the city. Then a gout of ever-erupting fire burst out of Castle Mournhold, and heavy smoke filled the streets, igniting anything into fire. [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] had been summoned to Tamriel and unleashed his army of [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]] onto Mournhold. Citizens were plucked from their hiding places and thrown to their deaths.{{Ref|name=2920V11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}

Mournhold tried in vain to fight back, like those who defended Mournhold Temple, but many of its people, including Duke Brindisi Dorom, were lost. The only thing that kept the city from burning was the sputtered blood of its people.{{Ref|name=2920V11}}{{Ref|name=ONSelveni|[[Online:Selveni Andalor|Selveni Andalor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} In a thin needle of light, Almalexia and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] appeared before Mehrunes Dagon and engaged in combat. Almalexia unsheathed her blade [[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]] and stabbed him in his breast, while Sotha Sil lashed at him with whips of [[Lore:Bronze|god-bronze]].{{Ref|name=2920V11}}{{Ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} Chunks of his flesh fell and turned into a multitude of creatures now called [[Lore:Ruinach|Ruinachs]].{{Ref|name=TTIS8}}{{Ref|name=BPTMR|{{Cite Book|Blackwood Preview—The Mighty Ruinachs|ns_base=GEN}}}} Now with a large wave of daedra, Almalexia singlehandedly killed them as Sotha Sil unleashed a devastating blow. But Dagon landed a final attack on Sotha Sil's jaw.{{Ref|name=TTIS8}}

The [[Lore:Battle of Mournhold|Battle of Mournhold]] ended when Sotha Sil whispered a final [[Lore:Nymic|chrononymic]] death-word and exploded Dagon back into Oblivion. His banishment caused a rift between Tamriel and Oblivion until Sotha Sil sealed it tightly,{{Ref|name=TTIS8}} but by then, Dagon had taken so much of Mournhold back with him. The [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of heroes in the battle, namely Ordinator Nilera and Priest Sovisa were sent to False Martyrs' Folly in the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]],{{Ref|name=ONSelveni}} and [[Lore:Wretched Spire|chunks of the city]] itself were flung into Oblivion, along with any citizens trapped in them.{{Ref|name=ONFaven|[[Online:Faven Indoril|Faven Indoril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}{{Ref|name=WTSF|{{Cite Book|When the Spires Fell}}}}

After the battle, Almalexia and Sotha Sil healed in [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]]{{Ref|name=2920V11}} as Mournhold's people were left to rebuild. Almalexia returned to the city and found inspiration in both the people's spirits and the resilience of its plantlife. Brindisi Dorom's heir was coming from the north to take his father's place. It was there she decided to make the city her home and believed it had a bright future ahead.{{Ref|name=2920V12|{{Cite Book|2920, Evening Star (v12)}}}} How long it took for Mournhold to rebuild is unknown. The modern Mournhold and its sewers were built over the ruins of the previous one, now known as [[Lore:Old Mournhold|Old Mournhold]].{{Ref|name=TREffe}} One of the city's current districts became known as Plaza Brindisi Dorom, likely in honor of the former Duke and a statue depicting Almalexia and Dagon's battle was erected in its center.{{Ref|name=TRMournhold}}

===Mournhold in the Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-City Center 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Mournhold City Center ca. 2E 582]]
[[File:ON-concept-Ebonheart Pact banner.png|thumb|right|145px|Banner of the Ebonheart Pact]]
Mournhold was an active settlement in the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. After the Sack of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], the invading [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]], led by [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]], traveled south and bypassed [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] to target Mournhold.{{Ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King}}}} Having sensed the impending attack, Almalexia made a widespread call-to-arms that brought Dunmer from across Morrowind and their occupied territories.{{Ref|name=FATS|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}} The Tribunal Army, led by the Indoril Grandmaster, [[Lore:Tanval Indoril|Tanval Indoril]] and Almalexia staged a fighting retreat into the ashlands and eventually corner them in central [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]].{{Ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}{{Ref|name=EGTS|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}}

At the [[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|Battle of Vivec's Antlers]] in {{Year|2E 572}}, the Dunmer, together with the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] of [[Lore:Eastern Skyrim|Eastern Skyrim]] and the free [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] of northern [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] defeated the Akaviri invaders.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=JTSK}} An agreement was signed in Ebonheart for the three races to unite and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was created thereafter,{{Ref|name=UA|{{Cite Book|Unexpected Allies}}}} with Mournhold chosen as its capital. From there, the Pact's chosen leader, [[Lore:Jorunn the Skald-King|Jorunn the Skald-King]] presided over the [[Lore:Great Moot|Great Moot]], a council that represented the three equal races.{{Ref|name=AUAEP2|{{Cite Book|Ask Us Anything: Ebonheart Pact (Part 2)|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{Ref|name=ITTLOTESO|{{Cite Book|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|ns_base=ON}}}}

===The Llodos Plague and Late Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-Magistrix Vox.jpg|thumb|right|Magistrix Urili Vox]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, a devastating disease known as the [[Lore:Llodos Plague|Llodos Plague]] began to spread across Deshaan, causing uproar in the region. Many people from Narsis fled east to Mournhold to escape the plague{{Ref|name=AJ|{{Cite Book|Anonymous Journal}}}} and to keep order, Mournhold enforced a law for citizens to register at the city center.{{Ref|name=ONRegister|[[Online:Ordinator (Mournhold)|Ordinator]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Maulborn|Maulborn Cult]], who created the plague gathered in the Mournhold Sewers, preparing to make their move on the city. After registering, [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] received a formal invitation to meet Almalexia in Mournhold Temple. There they informed her about the Maulborn, and with her blessing, began an investigation with the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] agent, [[Lore:Naryu Virian|Naryu Virian]].{{Ref|name=ONFavor|[[Online:A Favor Returned|A Favor Returned]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Their sleuthing brought them to the [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] farmstead, where they found a hatch to the Mournhold Sewers. Delving deeper, they confronted the Maulborn Commander, Kalara and her captive Farona Telvanni. Just then, Almalexia intervened and used powerful magic to destroy the Maulborn.{{Ref|name=ONUnderground|[[Online:The Mournhold Underground|The Mournhold Underground]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} But as that happened, the Maulborn's leader, Magistrix [[Lore:Urili Vox|Urili Vox]] used daedric magic to open Mournhold Temple's wards, allowing her cultists and daedra to storm its halls. Her plan was to defile the temple, steal countless holy artifacts, and let the daedra loose into Mournhold.{{Ref|name=ONThree|[[Online:The Seal of Three|The Seal of Three]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In the High Chapel, the Vestige defeated the Maulborn's champion, Vanus Sathram and restored Mournhold Temple, but Magistrix Vox made off with the Judgment of Veloth and fled east into the [[Lore:Siltreen|Siltreen]] region. For their heroism, Almalexia named the Vestige one of her [[Lore:Hands of Almalexia|Hands]] and let them pursue Vox, with their first stop being [[Lore:Selfora|Selfora]].{{Ref|name=ONThree}} Ultimately, Magistrix Vox was defeated in [[Lore:Eidolon's Hollow|Eidolon's Hollow]] and the Judgment of Veloth was safely returned.{{Ref|name=ONVeloth|[[Online:The Judgment of Veloth|The Judgment of Veloth]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Sometime later, [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] traveled with the Vestige to the alliance capitals, hoping to gain their cooperation for a final attack against [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] and his ongoing [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]].{{Ref|name=ONMessage|[[Online:Messages Across Tamriel (Pact)|Messages Across Tamriel]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] came to rule a [[Lore:Third Empire|new Empire]] in Cyrodiil and began a grand conquest of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] called the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]]. As the war was focused elsewhere, Almalexia commissioned the project of a larger city surrounding Mournhold in response to his rising power.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=ZeniMaxInterview}} The greater city of Almalexia was created, while the original city and Temple compound in the middle retained its name of Mournhold.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}

After [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]'s annexation into the Empire, Tiber Septim and the [[Lore:Imperial Army|Imperial Army]] turned their sights to Morrowind. House Indoril and the Temple proposed to fight to the death, with Houses Redoran and Dres at their sides{{Ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}} and trusting in their powerful magicks.{{Ref|name=BOB1}} But over time, the Great Houses could not organize a coordinated attack. After several destructive battles, the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] was signed, formally integrating Morrowind as an Imperial Province.{{Ref|name=OM}} Mournhold was the site of a significant battle that laid it to ruin. What became of the city's rulers is unknown, but their child, the five-year-old [[Lore:Barenziah|Barenziah]] and her nurse were found in the wreckage. An Imperial General, a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] named [[Lore:Symmachus|Symmachus]] suggested to Tiber Septim that she may be useful and had her sent away until she could return to Morrowind.{{Ref|name=BOB1}}

===Post-Tiber War and Barenziah's reign===
[[File:LG-cardart-Queen Barenziah 02.jpg|thumb|left|Queen Barenziah of Morrowind]]
{{Quote Box|''"Behold the Lady Barenziah, born to the land of Mournhold! Grant her thy blessings and thy counsel, ye kind gods, that she may ever rule well over Mournhold, its ken and its weal, its kith and its ilk."<br /><br />"Bless her, bless her," all the people intoned along with their Lord and Lady, hands upraised.''|Excerpt from ''[[Lore:The Real Barenziah|The Real Barenziah]]''}}
Ever since Morrowind's integration into the Cyrodilic Empire, Mournhold was under military rule, but it slowly returned to a civilian government by the time Barenziah was sixteen years old. While she was sent away to the Imperial City, Symmachus returned to Mournhold to rule as governor until Barenziah was old enough to inherit the throne.{{Ref|name=TRB2|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 2}}}}{{Ref|name=BOB2|{{Cite Book|Biography of Barenziah, v 2}}}}

Symmachus fought hard to keep Mournhold alive and healthy, when its counselors would have picked it bone dry. Tiber Septim in his conquest of Morrowind, took the [[Lore:Horn of Summoning|Horn of Summoning]] from Ephen's tomb and left the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]] with Mournhold.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} Ebonheart was jealous that they were trusted with its safekeeping and claimed it was their right as the eldest city-state to have it. Mournhold contended that it was King Moraelyn himself who trusted Ephen to keep it, and they were undoubtedly his birthplace.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} With help from a rag-tail end of the Ra'athim Clan, Symmachus successfully hid the Staff of Chaos in Ephen's tomb, where it sat for years.{{Ref|name=TRB4}}

Barenziah's time to inherit the throne eventually came when she turned eighteen, so she embarked from the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] to Morrowind.{{Ref|name=BOB2}}{{Ref|name=TRB3|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 3}}}} At the Mournhold border, she was greeted by Symmachus and continued to the city, where she was met with a grand parade of entry and several speeches of welcome. The ceremony was a blend of ancient Mournhold traditions and those mandated by Imperial decree. With her ascension, she took an oath and was sworn to serve the Empire, Tiber Septim, and the people of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} Her title as Queen of Morrowind was more ceremonial{{Ref|name=PGE3}} as power was divided across the Great Houses.{{Ref|name=GHOM}} It derives from early [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] traditions of a "high chief of the clans", a role similar to the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] High King.{{Ref|name=KRP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ken Rolston's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}} The Armistice was signed to give Morrowind autonomy under the Empire's banner, but Tiber Septim desired a central figure in the east that could represent him, so Barenziah was appointed, having descended from Mournhold's rulers.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} 

Upon her ascension, Symmachus became her Prime Minister, but in time, he proposed to Barenziah and became her royal consort.{{Ref|name=TRB4}}{{Ref|name=BOB3}} Queen Barenziah was unshakeably popular across Morrowind. Despite her upbringing in the Empire, the Dunmer viewed her as one of their own, "held captive" as they were by the West. Symmachus was viewed with suspicion by Morrowind's stiff-necked folk for his peasant ancestry and ties to the Empire, but Emperor [[Lore:Pelagius I|Pelagius I]] trusted him more than Barenziah. As the years went on, both Barenziah and Symmachus were widely beloved.{{Ref|name=TRB4}}{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}

As the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]] flourished under many different monarchs, Barenziah held the same role as Queen of Morrowind, and Mournhold remained loyal allies to the Empire.{{Ref|name=TRB4}}{{Ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} For a time, the future Emperor, [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus Septim]] served in their court as a counselor when he was 19 years old.{{Ref|name=TWQ3|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v3}}}} Thirty or so years later, Symmachus fought alongside Kings [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus]] and Magnus Septim in the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]], winning many battles against the [[Lore:Potema|Wolf-Queen]] of [[Lore:Solitude (kingdom)|Solitude]] and Emperor [[Lore:Uriel III|Uriel III]].{{Ref|name=TMOP}} Barenziah's reign proceeded for years with its own triumphs and tribulations. It had its fair share of crises, storms, famines, plots, and conspirators, but it also prospered. Mournhold's people were fed and productive. The only issue was the lack of heirs, and after hundreds of years, they grew concerned about their lack of progress.{{Ref|name=TRB4}}

===Civil Unrest and Barenziah's flight from Mournhold===
[[File:AR-item-Staff of Chaos.png|thumb|left|The Staff of Chaos]]
{{Quote Box|''May his spirit rest among his ancestors. But he was king in name only. The Dunmer do not, and never have, submitted to kings like the Westerners, regardless of what the Empire says.''|Mournhold citizens on King Llethan's death{{Ref|name=TRGenKing|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue K#King Llethan's death|King Llethan's death]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}}}
In {{Year|3E 389}}, [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]], the [[Lore:Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel|Imperial Battlemage]] impersonated Emperor [[Lore:Uriel VII|Uriel VII]], bringing about a decade of chaos across Tamriel called the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]]. He achieved this by having the Staff of Chaos, an artifact of immense power from Mournhold's mines. The story of how Jagar Tharn acquired the staff involved Barenziah and her encounter with the figure known as the Nightingale.{{Ref|name=BOB3|{{Cite Book|Biography of Barenziah, v 3}}}} 

Widely published works such as ''The Real Barenziah'' and ''[[Lore:Biography of Queen Barenziah|Biography of Queen Barenziah]]'' series claim that Jagar Tharn posed as the Nightingale, coerced her to reveal Ephen's tomb, and took the staff, only to reveal his hand much later.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} An obscure tome on the [[Lore:Nightingale Trinity|Nightingales]] asserts that the thief was [[Lore:Drayven Indoril|Drayven Indoril]], at the time, one of Skyrim's greatest thieves, and was hired by Jagar Tharn to do it.{{Ref|name=TN2|{{Cite Book|The Nightingales Vol. 2}}}} In any case, the Staff was gone, and for a time, nothing came of it.{{Ref|name=BOB3}}

Shortly after, Barenziah and Symmachus' first child, Prince [[Lore:Helseth|Helseth]] was born in {{Year|3E 376}}. Eight years later, Princess [[Lore:Morgiah|Morgiah]] was born and named after Symmachus' mother.{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|15}}{{Ref|name=BOB3}} Mournhold's relationship with the Empire was worsening for seemingly no reason as taxes and quotas increased over years. Suspecting this was a test of loyalty, Symmachus lengthened work hours and raised tariffs, unintentionally bringing civil unrest between commoners and nobles alike. In fear of the growing troubles, Barenziah took her children to the Imperial City, hoping to gain an audience with the Emperor and persuading him against the rising taxes. In the meantime, Symmachus stayed behind in Mournhold to deal with any complaints and stave off a potential insurrection.{{Ref|name=TRB5}}{{Ref|name=BOB3}}

The troubles in Mournhold escalated when the peasants organized a few disgruntled nobles and together called for Symmachus' resignation.{{intnote|Revolt|[Note 2]}} Only the [[Lore:Imperial Guard|Imperial Guard]] and retainers of Barenziah's house were at his side, but despite hostilities, Symmachus was still in control.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} That was until {{Year|3E 391}},{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|15}} when Symmachus was killed in battle. The Imperial Guard deserted him and joined the rebels, while any loyal troops left suffered greatly from the loss. Imperial envoys recognized the rebel leader as the new King of Mournhold (and Morrowind in general),{{intnote|King|[Note 3]}} while Barenziah and her family were stranded in the Imperial City. By then, she realized Jagar Tharn replaced the Emperor and was responsible for the Staff of Chaos' disappearance.{{Ref|name=TRB5}}{{Ref|name=BOB3}}

What Mournhold was like under the rebel leader's reign is unknown, but throughout the Imperial Simulacrum, it was still an active settlement. At the time, it had a rivalry with Ebonheart, [[Lore:Narsis|Narsis]], [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]], and [[Lore:Tear|Tear]] and was ruled by a certain King Zirik.{{Ref|name=AR}} In time, the chaos in Mournhold died down, but by then, Barenziah abdicated the throne and married King [[Lore:Eadwyre|Eadwyre]] of [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]]. In her stead, her uncle from [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]], [[Lore:Athyn Llethan|Athyn Llethan]] became the new King of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=BOB3}} Unlike the previous rulers, Athyn Llethan was disliked by his people and few took him seriously, but he ruled with dignity.{{Ref|name=TRGenKing|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue K#King Llethan's death|King Llethan's death]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}{{Ref|name=TREffe|[[Tribunal:Effe-Tei|Effe-Tei]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} His reign would last for about 36 years and he groomed his nephew, [[Lore:Talen Vandas|Talen Vandas]] to succeed him in due time.{{Ref|name=TRGenTalen|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue T#Talen Vandas|Talen Vandas]] generic description in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

===Enter the New King of Morrowind===
[[File:TR-npc-King Hlaalu Helseth.jpg|thumb|left|King Hlaalu Helseth]]
{{Quote Box|''The new king is manipulative, ruthless, and calculating. He is exactly what Morrowind needs.''|Famous quote of a Nord diplomat{{Ref|name=PGE3}}}}
While Athyn Llethan ruled in Mournhold, Barenziah and her children were in High Rock as members of the royal House of Wayrest. With Helseth as the oldest child, he was in line to inherit the kingdom's throne after Eadwyre's death. But this put him in direct competition with Princess [[Lore:Elysana|Elysana]], Eadwyre's first child from his previous marriage. Their power struggle ended after Eadwyre's death in {{Year|3E 418}}, and Elysana became the Queen of Wayrest. One of her first decrees was to banish Helseth and Barenziah from her kingdom.{{Ref|name=AGAD|{{Cite Book|A Game at Dinner}}}}{{Ref|name=LRPTESP|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/The Evening Star Parade|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 7/The Evening Star Parade – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} Morgiah, by then, was already married to King [[Lore:Karoodil|Karoodil]] of [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]] and lived in the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]]. As Morrowind returned to peace, the two returned to Mournhold and were well received by Athyn Llethan and the court.{{Ref|name=AGAD}}

Barenziah, however, had no interest in resuming her role as Queen and wanted to retire to her family estates. Helseth, meanwhile, lingered around Llethan's court, and rumors circulated over his ambitions for the throne.{{Ref|name=AGAD}} At some point, Talen Vandas passed away, reportedly from a hunting accident despite his talents as a warrior.{{Ref|name=TRHelseth|[[Tribunal:King Hlaalu Helseth|King Hlaalu Helseth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=TRMero|[[Tribunal:Plitinius Mero|Plitinius Mero]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Then suddenly, Athyn Llethan passes away, with official sources claiming it was from old age. Without anyone else to take the throne, Helseth ascended as the new King of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TRGenKing}}{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

Helseth's rise to power was controversial and sparked much discussion. To outside observers and his own people, he was an enigma.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Many people questioned the circumstances around Talen Vandas and Athyn Llethan's death.{{Ref|name=TRGenKing}}{{Ref|name=TRGenTalen}} Ravani Llethan, the late King's widow, [[Lore:Fedris Hler|Fedris Hler]], steward to Almalexia, and many other Dunmer believed Helseth had some hand in his death{{Ref|name=TRRavani|[[Tribunal:Ravani Llethan|Ravani Llethan]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}{{Ref|name=TRHler|[[Tribunal:Fedris Hler|Fedris Hler]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} but whether that was the case is unknown. A newspaper that circulated Mournhold at the time, known as the Common Tongue, brought to light many examples of Helseth's manipulative tactics, from his time as the Prince of Wayrest.{{Ref|name=TCT|{{Cite Book|The Common Tongue}}}} The newspaper helped spread rumors of his potential assassination attempt on King Llethan.{{Ref|name=TRTienius|[[Tribunal:Tienius Delitian|Tienius Delitian]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

Though there was always a problem between the Tribunal Temple and Imperial administration, Helseth's ascencion made things worse. Then in {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Sixth House|Sixth House]] and [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] returned to wreak havoc on Vvardenfell. Since then, Almalexia changed her demeanor, becoming a harsher and unsympathetic person.{{Ref|name=TREffe}}{{Ref|name=TRBarenziah|[[Tribunal:Barenziah|Barenziah]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} But rumors of the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] began to spread wide across Morrowind,{{Ref|name=PGE3}} and as they handled the situation on Vvardenfell, Helseth's informants grew wary of their presence and felt they could pose a threat to the King. Because of this, Helseth hired the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] to attack them. When they failed, it instead brought the hero to Mournhold.{{Ref|name=TRMain|[[Tribunal:Main Quest|Main Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

===The Nerevarine in Mournhold and the Death of a Mad God===
[[File:TR-quest-The Blade of Nerevar.jpg|thumb|left|Pieces of Trueflame, the Blade of Nerevar]]
{{Quote Box|''Your work in Morrowind is not finished, Nerevarine. The Tribunal are dead, and the faith of my people is shaken. Protect them. Defend these lands. The skies of Mournhold are clear once again. Let these people suffer no longer. Now go, mortal. Embrace your destiny, and go with my blessing.''|[[Lore:Azura|Azura]]'s words to the Nerevarine}}
The Nerevarine eventually tracked the Brotherhood to the Manor District in Old Mournhold, where they destroyed the guild. Since then, they roamed the city, doing quests for Mournhold Temple and even the Royal Palace. For the temple, they cleansed the Shrine of the Dead and acquired [[Lore:Barilzar's Mazed Band|Barilzar's Mazed Band]] for Almalexia.{{Ref|name=TRMain}} As per the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|Nerevarine Prophecy]], the Tribunal's source of divine power, the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] was destroyed and their influence slowly faded.{{Ref|name=MWCitadels|[[Morrowind:The Citadels of the Sixth House|The Citadels of the Sixth House]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

Almalexia was unable to reconcile with this fact and in the background, grew mad and unstable. With the Mazed Band, she teleported to the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] to kill Sotha Sil and unleash his [[Lore:Fabricants|fabricants]] onto Plaza Brindisi Dorom.{{Ref|name=TRAlmalexia|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} The Nerevarine destroyed these invaders and investigated the newly opened Bamz-Amschend to find the [[Lore:Dwemer Animunculi|Dwemer animunculi]] also attacking them, much to their confusion.{{Ref|name=TRMain}}

After their investigation, the Nerevarine began to work directly under Almalexia but since the attack, the Tribunal's faithful began to lose hope in them. Eno Romari was a local in Mournhold who founded the [[Lore:End of Times|End of Times]] cult. He believed that with the Tribunal's loss in power, the next era will come and with a violent transition where the gates of Oblivion will open and flood Tamriel with daedra. Salas Valor was one of Almalexia's Hands but left her service as she became distant. Now he roamed the city's streets, frigthening the citizens and his fellow Ordinators. Almalexia sent the Nerevarine to deal with each of them. As a show of power to her people, Almalexia sent the hero to Bamz-Amschend to activate the long-abandoned [[Lore:Weather Witch|Weather Witch]] hidden inside and manifest ashstorms in Mournhold.{{Ref|name=TRMain}}

After much time had passed, Almalexia fully recognized the hero as the Nerevarine and gave them the task of reforging [[Lore:Trueflame|Trueflame]], Nerevar's legendary sword. Their quest brought them to [[Lore:Karrod|Karrod]], Helseth's champion who possessed a piece of the broken blade, [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]], a wealthy collecter of artifacts, who gave them a Dwemer Battle Shield in exchange for two artifacts, Craftsmen's Hall, where the master smith, [[Lore:Yagak gro-Gluk|Yagak gro-Gluk]] mended the pieces together, and finally the  ruins of Bamz-Amschend, where the [[Lore:Dwarven Spectre|Dwarven Spectre]], [[Lore:Radac Stungnthumz|Radac Stungnthumz]] reignited the sword's magical fire.{{Ref|name=TRBlade|[[Tribunal:The Blade of Nerevar|The Blade of Nerevar]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

With Trueflame restored, Almalexia gave the hero her final mission, to enter the Clockwork City and defeat Sotha Sil, who she claimed went mad and became unstable. In reality, she planned to lure the Nerevarine away and kill them, turning them into a martyr that died fighting the mad god Sotha Sil. In turn, she would unite her people under one god, one faith, and one divine law, without Helseth or the Empire. In the end, Almalexia was slain and with the Mazed Band, the Nerevarine left the Clockwork City.{{Ref|name=TRMad|[[Tribunal:The Mad God|The Mad God]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

===Fall of the Tribunal and Helseth's Declaration===
[[File:MW-banner-Helseth.png|thumb|right|130px|Hlaalu Helseth's banner]]
[[File:LG-cardart-The Red Year 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Red Year in Mournhold]]
With Almalexia's death and the Tribunal's fall from power, a great deal of change swept through Mournhold and Morrowind. Helseth immediately, and unapolegetically, assumed power from the vacuum left behind by the Tribunal.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/Playing Houses|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 12/Playing Houses – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} His first edicts tackled the traditional power structures of the Dunmer, such as the outlawing of [[Lore:Slavery|slavery]] in Morrowind.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Helseth believed that while it served its purpose in the past, did more harm than good and felt no discussion was needed to proceed with its abolishment. In doing so, he anticipated slave revolts but warned House Dres ahead of time.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} Helseth is quoted for saying, ''"if there is to be a revolution, it is best done by a King."{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

On the other hand, Helseth's reformation of the [[Lore:Grand Council|Grand Council]], including the heads of the Great Houses, was seen by many as a return to Dunmer tradition.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Helseth married Dres Vendama of [[Lore:Bodrum|Bodrom Mora]], and their ceremony was a mix of ancient Velothi and modern Imperial traditions. After their marriage, she was known as Queen Dinara and together with Queen-Mother Barenziah, assisted Helseth by parlaying with allied Houses and the [[Lore:Imperial Court|Imperial Court]].{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}}

By the tail-end of the Third Era, the Great Houses had adjusted to the shift of power dynamics after many years of stagnation. Houses Dres and Hlaalu embraced the new changes, and renounced the slave trade. In turn, they were propelled into success, but on the other hand, the Indoril and Redoran struggled to adapt,{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=OBRadiant|[[Oblivion:Radiant Conversations#Dialogue By Topic|Radiant Conversations]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} having given no time to prepare for Morrowind's emacipation. Their reaction was violent. On the night of Helseth and Dinara's wedding, House Redoran seized Bodrom Mora and held Dinara's brother, Dres Obael captive. Shortly after, Indoril assassins made an attempt on Barenziah's life and in retaliation, Helseth attacked the Indoril stronghold, Bal Therec.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}}

These events led to alienation between Helseth's kingdom and the Great Houses Indoril and Redoran. In time, civil war broke out in Morrowind. While Helseth reigned over most of the mainland, the Indoril Redoran Alliance held the north and south regions, claiming most of Vvardenfell, land around Bodrom Mora and former Dres lands.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} The outcome of this civil war is unknown but in {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] occurred and it laid waste across Tamriel. The Empire was stretched thin and pulled most of their forces from Morrowind to combat the daedra in Cyrodiil. With no standing army, they only relied on scattered and uncoordinated resistances. In the end, House Redoran took charge and rallied an army that could fight back.{{Ref|name=DBAdril}}{{intnote|Fight|[Note 4]}}

By the Fourth Era, the Empire releashed Morrowind from its control. For their loyalty to the westerners, House Hlaalu was stripped of their position as a Great House and lost their seat on the Grand Council. They became a scapegoat for all of the Dunmer's suffering.{{Ref|name=DBAdril}} How this affected King Hlaalu Helseth, the Imperial King of Morrowind and Mournhold is unknown. By the Fourth Era, the Royal Palace was unoccupied{{Ref|name=TMFN}} and Helseth and his family's fate were left unknown.

===The Fourth Era and Onward===
Five years later in {{Year|4E 5}}, otherwise known as the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] erupted for the first time in years, causing the widespread destruction of Vvardenfell and other natural disasters in Morrowind. Mournhold was relatively unscathed,{{Ref|name=TRYII|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}} save for heavy ashstorms.{{Ref|name=LGYear|[[Legends:The Red Year|The Red Year]] card in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} A few Dunmer believed it was the Tribunal protecting them, while others blamed the Tribunal for it happening. Some disagreements even turned into fights. But with news of countless deaths, there was not a single night for a month where people openly weeped and grieved for deceased relatives.{{Ref|name=TRYII}}

A Redoran councilor living in Mournhold organized a relief effort a month later to rebuild Vvardenfell, especially settlements that were hit the hardest like Balmora.{{Ref|name=TRYII}} Only a year later, however, Mournhold was sacked by the Argonians in the [[Lore:Accession War|Accession War]]. In their conquest of Morrowind, the Argonians cut a bloody swathe across the southern region but the [[Lore:Redoran Guard|Redoran Guard]] pushed them back before they could destroy the rest of the province. With Mournhold in disarray, the capital was changed to Blacklight in northwest Morrowind{{Ref|name=DBAdril}} and the Redoran Guard stayed in Mournhold.{{Ref|nname=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}}

House Indoril, having been intertwined with the Tribunal Temple suffered greatly from the Tribunal's downfall. Inner conflict plagued the Temple priesthood until the [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]] assumed control, with Tribunal loyalist cast out. House Indoril and the New Temple, who served the original Good Daedra, now known as the Reclamations, had become one of the same, but power was entirely passed to the New Temple.{{Ref|name=TR}} By {{Year|4E 201}}, Mournhold felt emptier than it normally was. The Royal Palace was still unoccupied and the Redoran Guard were reassigned to other parts of Morrowind. Renovations were still made to Mournhold Temple as vestiges of Almalexia's influences were removed and a new sanctuary was added. House Indoril built a new building in Plaza Brindisi Dorom for priests and pilgrims to pay respects at the temple.{{Ref|name=TMFN}}

===Known Rulers===
[[File:SR-icon-misc-Barenziah'sCrown.png|thumb|right|Barenziah's coronation crown]]
The royal family that rules the city is known as the '''House of Mournhold'''.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} It has been mostly ruled by the same family since as late as the late Second Era, from Barenziah's parents, who perished in Tiber Septim's sack of Almalexia,{{Ref|name=BOB1}}{{Ref|name=TRB1}} to King Hlaalu Helseth at the tail-end of the Third Era.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} This family comes from a very presitigious lineage. Barenziah's family and her uncle, Athyn Llethan, hail from the Ra'athim Clan of Ebonheart,{{Ref|name=IWTB|{{Cite Book|Interview With Three Booksellers|ns_base=GEN}}}} former retainers to the High Kingship of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} Athyn Llethan is also a descendant of [[Lore:Hlaalu Brevur|Hlaalu Brevur]]{{Ref|name=KRP|group=UOL}} and [[Lore:Hlaalu Pasoroth|Hlaalu Pasoroth]].{{Ref|name=HT|{{Cite Book|Hallgerd's Tale}}}}

Barenziah's exact house affiliation is unknown, but Athyn Llethan is explicitly stated to be from House Hlaalu.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Previous rulers of Mournhold, such as Brindisi Dorom, were from House Indoril. The Kingship of Morrowind, under the Empire, was also known as the Dukes of Mournhold.{{Ref|name=HP|{{Cite Book|Hospitality Papers}}}} Imperial Dukes are the rulers of Morrowind's districts, as seen with [[Lore:Vedam Dren|Vedam Dren]], the Duke of [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] and Vvardenfell.{{Ref|name=MWGenEbonheart|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue E#Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} In the Imperial City, an ambassador to the Court of Mournhold was present in the Emperor's court.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} What became of these titles after the Empire cut ties in Morrowind is unknown.{{Ref|name=DBAdril}}

The birth of an heiress in Mournhold is a sign of peaceful and prosperous times in the future. When the Rite of Naming commences, businesses are closed, and everyone gathers at the Royal Palace to witness it.{{Ref|name=TRB1}} Native representatives of Mournhold's people were mostly elders, as per traditional elven customs.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} The city has an administration that oversees various aspects of Mournhold, including the sewers.{{Ref|name=ONLoadSewers|[[Online:Mournhold Sewers|Mournhold Sewers]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some of Mournhold's greatest treasures are related to its royal family. The [[Lore:Dagger of Symmachus|Dagger of Symmachus]] is a [[Lore:Glass|glass]] ceremonial dagger wielded by Symmachus.{{Ref|name=TRTorasa|[[Tribunal:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} The [[Lore:Crown of Barenziah|Crown of Barenziah]] was the Queen's ceremonial crown and embedded with twenty-four purple gems before it was stolen in a heist.{{Ref|name=SRVex|[[Skyrim:Vex|Vex]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=GTBT|{{Cite Book|Guide to Better Thieving}}}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:TR-prerelease-High Ordinator.jpg|thumb|left|A High Ordinator and Mournhold Temple]]
[[File:ON-banner-House Indoril.png|thumb|right|125px|Banner of House Indoril]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Dunmer page.''
Mournhold is a central location in Morrowind; not only has it been the province's capital for much of its history, but it is also the seat of power for both House Indoril and its Temple faiths. Ever since Red Mountain's first eruption, Morrowind's population shifted south toward Deshaan and southern Morrowind. Since then, Mournhold has become the province's largest city.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Citizens are not known for being cordial or encouraging to visitors.{{Ref|name=POM}} Rumors during the Imperial Simulacrum described a "great evil" that hid under the city and drove its citizens to [[Lore:Madness (disease)|madness]]. Another claimed that no one has ever seen the bodies of the slain after a battle on its soil.{{Ref|name=AR}}

Mournhold is seen as the mercantile heart of Morrowind and hosts many merchant districts. Most famously, the Great Bazaar is generally located in the eastern portion of the city,{{Ref|name=APGTM}} southeast of the Tribunal Temple.{{Ref|name=TRMap}} It is the largest and heavily secured market in Mournhold.{{Ref|name=APGTM}} Some of the highest-priced items available in Almalexia and nearby Narsis include the flying insect mounts of [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} House Hlaalu held great sway in Mournhold and Deshaan in general.{{Ref|name=TGHATU|{{Cite Book|The Great Houses and Their Uses}}}} To the south of Mournhold is Deepcrag Den, the city's primary source for fresh [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]] eggs. Though it is not as successful as [[Lore:Darkshade Caverns|Darkshade Caverns]], it has the clear-cut advantage of being so close to the city.{{Ref|name=ONLoadDeepcrag|[[Online:Deepcrag Den|Deepcrag Den]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} An outdoor theater is present in the Great Bazaar and hosts seasonal performances, from plays to choirs.{{Ref|name=APGTM}} The Mournhold Players was the local theater troupe that frequently performed in the Great Bazaar.{{Ref|name=TRMeryn|[[Tribunal:Meryn Othralas|Meryn Othralas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} 

Mining is a significant source of income and work in Mournhold,{{Ref|name=TRB1}} going as far back as when it was a Ra'athim fief.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} Commoners were put to work in the mines, even the future royal consort, Symmachus, and his father before him were miners.{{Ref|name=TRB1}} The native Indalen clan are known for excavating [[Lore:Adamantium|adamantium]] from Old Mournhold's deepest caves and crevices.{{Ref|name=TRBols|[[Tribunal:Bols Indalen|Bols Indalen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} The obscure deity known as Ephen, who hailed from Mournhold, is depicted with a hammer in hand and poised on an adamantium anvil.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} Mournhold has a long-established rivalry with Ebonheart, the other half of the original Ra'athim nation. This rivalry has surfaced a few times throughout history, once regarding the Staff of Chaos' ownership{{Ref|name=TRB5}} and another time during the Imperial Simulacrum.{{Ref|name=AR}}

The [[Lore:Mournhold Sewers|Mournhold Sewers]] are expansive, and although they are under the city administration's watch, there are large sections of it that are completely forgotten{{Ref|name=ONLoadSewers}} and as a result, occupied by the city's poor and lawless.{{Ref|name=TREffe}} The city's outlaws refuge is hidden here, in the ruins of a Tribunal chapel.{{Ref|name=ONLoadOutlaws|[[Online:Mournhold Outlaws Refuge|Mournhold Outlaws Refuge]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Throughout the years, daedric wards have been placed throughout the sewers to allow travel across the city under no suspicion from the Ordinators.{{Ref|name=ONNaryu|[[Online:Naryu Virian#The Mournhold Underground|Naryu Virian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Deeper into the sewers are the ruins of Old Mournhold, where many First Era treasures lie in secret and attract adventurers to explore it.{{Ref|name=TREffe}} 

===House Indoril and the Temples===
Mournhold is the capital of House Indoril, one of Morrowind's Great Houses. The Indoril are known for their strong ties to the Dunmer religions. For much of their history, they served as stewards to the Tribunal and held positions within the Tribunal Temple faith.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=GHOM}} Even when [[Lore:Ancestor Worship|ancestor worship]] fell out of favor,{{Ref|name=TRGavas|[[Tribunal:Gavas Drin|Gavas Drin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} House Indoril still revered their ancestors.{{Ref|name=UHI}} When the Reclamations replaced the Tribunal, House Indoril still wielded power but became one with the temple faith.{{Ref|name=TR}} They also believe in the absolute preservation of traditional Dunmer culture and see change as an enemy that would shake their foundation.{{Ref|name=UHI}} This was especially seen when Morrowind was incorporated into the Empire. Many nobles across House Indoril committed suicide, rather than accepting the Empire, causing the House to lose significant power.{{Ref|name=OM}} Since then, House Indoril has been openly hostile to Imperial culture and religion.{{Ref|name=GHOM}}

The Mournhold aesthetic of religious design centers on Almalexia, as she was the city's patron deity. Reginus Buca of the [[Lore:Antiquarian Circle|Antiquarian Circle]] claims that Almalexia worship stylistically resembles general Tamrielic norms.{{Ref|name=ONDaisAnti|[[Online:Deshaan Antiquities#Blessed Dais of Almalexia|Blessed Dais of Almalexia]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Almalexian priests are given Mother Stones, dark crystals that teleport their user back to Mournhold once it is attuned.{{Ref|name=ONSelveni}}{{Ref|name=ONMother|[[Online:The Deadlands Quest Items#Mother Stones|Mother Stones]] quest item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} Under her reign, [[Lore:Levitate|levitation]] was banned in the city. It is said that no one could raise their head higher than Almalexia.{{Ref|name=TREffe}}

Mournhold's main peacekeepers are the [[Lore:Ordinators|Ordinators]], the holy-guard and priest soldiers of the Temple.{{Ref|name=MWGenOrdinator|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue O#Ordinators|Ordinators]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} They are noted for their armor rich in [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] and [[Lore:Malachite|glass]], and their ever-watchful gaze.{{Ref|name=EGT}} In the mid-Second Era, the city was guarded by the regular sect of Ordinators, the same seen in [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]],{{Ref|name=ONOrdinators|[[Online:Ordinators|Ordinators]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but in the late Third Era, it was guarded by the [[Lore:High Ordinators|High Ordinators]], those who were deemed fit to guard Almalexia's holy city and the strongest warriors in Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TRFedris|[[Tribunal:Fedris Hler|Fedris Hler]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} The Ordinators at large are divided into four orders and all of them answer to the [[Lore:Alma Rula|Alma Rula]], who resides in Almalexia city.{{Ref|name=MWGenOrdinator}}

Unlike other parts of Morrowind, like Necrom, Mournhold is more cosmopolitan and open-minded when it comes to burying foreigners. At most, one will find names on a plaque beside a tomb.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} The Shrine of the Dead in Old Mournhold was previously a source of great power, one that allowed the living to channel the ancestors and harness their power. It was abandoned after the Tribunal became Morrowind's main faith, but was restored in the late Third Era.{{Ref|name=TRGavas|[[Tribunal:Gavas Drin|Gavas Drin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}
