The [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]], also known as the '''Great Hunt''', is a realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] created and ruled over by [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of the Hunt.{{ref|name=SRKodlak|[[Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=KWEBM|[[Bloodmoon:Korst Wind-Eye|Korst Wind-Eye]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOT|[[Online:Thoreki|Thoreki]]'s dialogue during the quest [[Online:Hircine's Gift|Hircine's Gift]]}} It has been described as a realm of dense woodland and vast grasslands populated by [[Lore:Werebear|werebear]]s, wild [[Lore:Cow|cattle]] and [[Lore:Unicorn|unicorn]]s. It is a savage land inhabited by vicious creatures such as [[Lore:Bear|bear]]s, [[Lore:Wolf|wolves]], werecreatures, and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. These creatures are generally much larger than their counterparts in the mortal realm.{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}} The spirits of animals from the mortal plane are a common sight within the Hunting Grounds, as Hircine brings them in to please his followers.{{ref|name=ESOLive|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/281631351 Twitch Stream of ''The Elder Scrolls Online: Wolfhunter First Look''], time stamp 30:16-30:36}} Some Daedra from the realm take form of sapient animals.{{Ref|name=TF|[[ON:The Fox|The Fox]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}} All who die within the Hunting Grounds are doomed to remain within the realm after death.{{ref|name=ESOHDW|[[Online:Hanu|Hanu]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Wolfhunter|Wolfhunter]]''}} Inhabitants of the realm may take on the shapes of various animals to participate in the eternal hunt.{{ref|name=Fox|[[Online:The Fox|The Fox]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

Followers of Hircine are promised an afterlife within the Hunting Grounds, where they can indulge in spectacular hunts, as well as experience an endless cycle of violence as well as death and rebirth, reborn endlessly in bodies that never fall ill.{{ref|name=tGotH|{{Cite Book|The Glory of the Hunt}}}} Its inhabitants reside in the many hunting lodges dotting the landscape. The forests of the Hunting Grounds are teeming with powerful beasts, which allow Hircine's followers to engage in the most spectacular hunts. For the devoted, it is a paradise.{{ref|name=THP|{{Cite Book|The Huntsman Prince}}}} The souls of [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropes]] are claimed by Hircine and spend eternity experiencing the thrill of the hunt in his Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=SRKodlak}} However, it is possible for a soul to transfer to the proper afterlife (such as [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]]) if their lycanthropy is cured posthumously, provided their soul is yet to have been taken to the Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=GOTD|Events of [[SR:Glory of the Dead|Glory of the Dead]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} During the daylight hours, [[Lore:Werebear|werebears]] and fierce [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] stalk the land in their continual Hunt. When the sun sinks below the horizon, Hircine announces himself with a pack of werewolves to take his turn in the cycle.{{ref|name=TIC}}<noinclude>

Though the term Great Hunt is an alternate name for the realm, it is primarily used as the name for the spectacular contests conducted by Hircine.{{ref|name=ESOT}} In the Hunting Grounds, Great Hunts are a constant occurrence,{{ref|name=ESOHDW|[[Online:Hanu|Hanu]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Wolfhunter|Wolfhunter]]''}} but outside of his realm, they are rare and are rumored to happen once per era.{{ref|name=KWEBM}} Great Hunts are called upon in Nirn for Hircine's own amusement,{{ref|name=Bloodmoon|Events of [[Bloodmoon:Hircine's Hunt|Hircine's Hunt]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} or to punish those that defy him.{{ref|name=SRLS|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Daedric Princes|Hircine Loading Screen]] description in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{Ref|name=SRIMBM|Events of [[Skyrim:Ill Met By Moonlight|Ill Met By Moonlight]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Due to the constant occurrence of the Great Hunts the [[Lore:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]] occurs naturally in the realm.{{ref|name=ESOHDW}}{{Ref|name=TGHESO|Events of [[ON:The Great Hunt|The Great Hunt]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=HG}}

===Geography===
[[File:ON-load-Hunter's Glade.jpg|Hunter's Glade|thumb|right]][[File:ON-load-March of Sacrifices.jpg|Wispwood|thumb|left]]
Much of the Hunting Grounds consists of a vast wilderness, meadows, and mountains, making it familiar to mortals, but not fully.{{ref|name=ESOMOS|[[Online:March of Sacrifices|March of Sacrifices]] loading screen description in [[Online:Online|''ESO'']]}} It is primarily an endless forest,{{ref|name=ESOHGLS|Various [[Online:The Hunting Grounds|The Hunting Grounds]] loading screen descriptions in [[Online:Online|''ESO'']]}} though it contains other biomes. An example is a grassy savanna with a red sun overhead, whose rays painted the river bloody. Herds of antelopes with twisting horns inhabit this area,{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|243}} as well large, six feet high cattle with an almost equal shoulder and horn span.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|244}} The savanna's river falls through a large precipice, and flows into a large canyon that narrows down the path.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|246}}

The March of Sacrifices is a part of the Hunting Grounds made to resemble a Tamrielic marshland. It consists of three sections—the Bloodscent Pass, the Wispwood, and the Nightlands, and each tailored to resemble a different time of day. As a hunter passes through through the marsh, they will see a sudden transition from dusk to nighttime, and the flora will change in luminosity from a bright orange to blue to reflect. It is inhabited by [[Lore:Lurcher|Lurchers]], [[Lore:Wispmother|Wispmothers]], [[Lore:Indrik|Indriks]], and both hostile and docile spectral fauna.{{ref|name=OnlineWH|[[Online:March of Sacrifices|March of Sacrifices]] appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Wolfhunter|Wolfhunter]]''}}

The [[Lore:Hunter's Grotto|Hunter's Grotto]] is another section of the Huntsman's realm, which leaks into [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]]. It is a lava tube featuring a clash of lush vegetation and magma within its cavernous depths. The [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] of the [[Lore:Spiritblood Clan|Spiritblood Clan]] enter it to undergo its challenges as part of their initiation into adulthood.{{ref|name=OnlineVH|Events of [[Online:Vateshran's Rites|Vateshran's Rites]] of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

The Hunter's Glade is yet another part of the realm, accessed through the portal in [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]]. It is an expansive forested glade, where werewolves may remain transformed indefinitely. Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the ruins where the portal was located were available for purchase as a private residence.{{Ref|name=HG|[[ON:Hunter's Glade|Hunter's Glade]] loading screen and appearance in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
[[File:ON-place-Hunter's Glade 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Bloodmoon]]
The most widely known legend around the origin of the [[Lore:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]] has Hircine creating the artifact out of his own hide as a reward for the first mortal to escape his realm.{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}}

===Second Era===
A rift to the Hunting Grounds opened within [[Lore:Scrivener's Hall|Scrivener's Hall]] on the 1<sup>st</sup> Last Seed, {{Year|2E 570}}, and closed on the same day.{{Ref|name=MRT|{{Cite Book|Mazandi's Rift Tracking|ns_base=ON}}}} 

The [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] saw much traffic through the Hunting Grounds from Nirn. Hircine sought the [[Lore:Green Lady|Green Lady]] as a trophy for generations, for reasons that remain ambiguous. One theory as to why was that she represents the strongest of the Bosmer, who are said to be among the greatest hunters of Nirn.{{ref|name=ONSOU|[[Online:Shade of Ulthorn|Shade of Ulthorn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After the Bosmer Gwaering was chosen as the Green Lady, she was obligated to marry the new [[Lore:The Silvenar|Silvenar]], Indaenir, for the good of the Bosmeri people. Her former lover [[Lore:Ulthorn|Ulthorn]] became bitter, and Hircine took advantage of his passion, forging him into a faithful servant within a "prison of the mind" in the Hunting Grounds. Ulthorn became a husk of his former self known as the Hound, and sought to usurp the Silvenar's role so he could be bonded to the Green Lady through the handfast. If Ulthorn were to sever the bond, the Bosmer would revert into their primordial nature, as the Green Lady by nature would influence the Bosmer people to grow feral,{{ref|name=ONMalabal|[[Online:Gwaering|Gwaering]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Restore the Silvenar|Restore the Silvenar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} turning them into a bestial horde with an unquenchable thirst and hunger.{{ref|name=SIDiag|[[Online:Spinner Indraseth|Spinner Indraseth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

After becoming the Hound, Ulthorn returned to his home town of Wilding Run, and wiped the local populace as he hunted them for sport.{{ref|name=ESOOWN|{{Cite Book|One Wilding Night}}}} The town was subsequently overseen by Gamekeeper Ozzai, a [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]] entrusted with utilizing the mortal trophies of Hircine's conquests, by hollowing them out into shells that will roam Nirn hunting indiscriminately. After reports that researchers of the Mages Guild started vanishing reached the Fists of Thalmor, they investigated the site and were almost wiped out by Ozzai's creations during an ambush, prompting them to resort to entrusting an adventurer with the investigation. After discovering a shade of what was left Ulthorn's humanity, it guided them into the Hunting Grounds, where the Harvester was located and slain in her lair, eliminating a potential army from aiding Ulthorn's forces.{{ref|name=ESOTTB|Events of [[Online:The Ties that Bind|The Ties that Bind]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Around this time, some individuals who were infected with lycanthropy and sought to become one of Hircine's creatures were found by those kin to Hircine's own Packmaster [[Lore:Songamdir|Songamdir]]. These lucky few were invited into the Hunting Grounds to participate in an ancient ritual the Packmaster. They presented a tribute to Hircine and his pack, and after a successful hunt, they were inducted into Hircine's own pack of werewolves, and were given greater mastery over their form than the average lycanthrope.{{ref|name=ESOHG|[[Online:Thoreki|Thoreki]], [[Online:Songamdir|Songamdir]], and [[Online:Hircine|Hircine]]'s dialogue during the quest [[Online:Hircine's Gift|Hircine's Gift]]}}

Later that year, Hircine hosted the Great Hunt in his plane, inviting mortals from Tamriel to participate. During this time, one of the first turned, the werewolf lord [[Lore:Vykosa|Vykosa]], amassed the [[Lore:Moon Hunter Pack|largest werewolf pack]] that Tamriel had ever seen.{{ref|name=RJ|{{Cite Book|Rolea's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} She plotted to use her pack to take over [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and exact revenge on Hircine, for she and other werewolves felt they were tricked into playing the part of prey instead of the role of a predator.{{ref|name=ESOTHCC|{{Cite Book|The Hungry Cat's Curse|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=LtM|{{Cite Book|Letter to Mylenne|ns_base=Online}}}} Vykosa sent her lieutenant Balorgh along with a reconnaissance squad to infiltrate the Hunting Grounds, though he and his pack would instead opt to participate in the Hunt and seek Hircine's Boon.{{ref|name=LTB|{{Cite Book|Letter to Balorgh|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=BPESO|{{Cite Book|Balorgh's Plan|ns_base=Online}}}} Unfortunately for Balorgh, Hircine would ultimately grant his boon to the true winners of the Great Hunt, the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]].{{ref|name=TGHESO}}

===Fourth Era===
During the [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|Umbriel Crisis]], Hircine impeded the journey of [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus Mede]] and his companions as they were passing through his Hunting Grounds to reach their destination. As eternal hunts are the norm within the realm, Mede and his companions became unwilling prey. They were slowly being bottled in by drivers until nighttime, where Hircine would arrive with his pack of werewolves. Nighttime came, two horns blared, and a deep and primal voice was heard upstream that was capable of leading one to a mindless terror. At a glance, they saw an enormous silhouette of a man with the horns of a stag and they knew Hircine had arrived. To ward off Hircine and his pack, the members of the Khajiiti [[Lore:Khajiit#Khajiiti Clans|F'aashe]] clan that accompanied Mede stayed behind to stall them, allowing Mede and his companions to escape the Hunting Grounds.{{ref|name=TIC}}

Following Harbinger [[Lore:Kyrnil Long-Nose|Kyrnil Long-Nose]]'s founding of [[Lore:The Circle|the Circle]] in the late Second Era,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} the upper ranks of the [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] became infected with lycanthropy after Harbinger [[Lore:Terrfyg|Terrfyg]] made a bargain with the witches of the Glenmoril Coven.{{ref|name=KJ|{{Cite Book|Kodlak's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The terms were that if the Companions were to hunt in the name of Hircine, they would be granted great power, but the Circle was deceived into believing it would not be a permanent affliction. Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, some members of the Circle, led by Harbinger [[Lore:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]], would eventually conclude it was not a gift, but a curse with serious spiritual implications that would threaten their souls' opportunity to reach Sovngarde, and worked to eliminate it from the Companions.{{ref|name=SRKodlakBH|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue during [[SR:Blood's Honor|Blood's Honor]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} After his death, the order managed to cure Kodlak of his affliction, allowing him to join his ancestors in Sovngarde.{{ref|name=GOTDKodlak|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue after killing his [[SR:Kodlak's Wolf Spirit|Wolf Spirit]] during [[SR:Glory of the Dead|Glory of the Dead]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, he hoped that someday, he and other honored warriors could invade the Hunting Grounds and rescue the souls of the Companions who did not have the chance to free themselves from Hircine's grasp before death. He called this imagined battle "The Harrowing of the Hunting Grounds",{{ref|name=GOTDKodlak}} but whether or not it is possible to posthumously wrench a werewolf's soul free from the realm through battle is unclear.
