# Lore:Warp in the West

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore History Trail}} {{About|the event|the book|The Warp in the West}} [[File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|thumb|right|The Iliac Bay before the Warp in the West]][[File:DF-map-Warp in the West.jpg|thumb|right|The four kingdoms of the Iliac Bay after the Warp in the West (fate of Balfiera is unknown)]][[File:MW-book-Divine Metaphysics p3.jpg|thumb|right|Dwemer sketch of Numidium from ''[[Lore:Divine Metaphysics|Divine Metaphysics]]'']]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] (also known as the '''Miracle of Peace''',{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} or the '''Second Numidian Effect'''){{ref|name=LOTE|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Emperors}}}} was a phenomenon which took place between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall in {{Year|3E 417}}, when events stemming from the death of King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] set in motion an upheaval in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} This event is sometimes called a [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] and compared to the [[Lore:Dragon Break#The Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]] of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]],{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}} though its effects were localized to the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

An unknown [[Lore:The Agent|Hero]], believed to have been an agent of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], gained control of a mighty artifact known as the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]]. The Totem allowed its bearer to control [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]], the ancient [[Lore:Weapon|weapon]] once used by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] to conquer [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The agent was unable to personally use the Totem, but several major factions around the Iliac Bay engaged in a bidding war, each attempting to acquire the artifact.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} In the end, [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], and the [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Emperor]] all gained control of Numidium and used it to consolidate their political powers, somehow at the same exact time. On the 9th of Frostfall, there were forty-four city-states in the Iliac Bay; on the 11th, after a number of mysterious cataclysms and anomalies, there were only four—Sentinel, Wayrest, Daggerfall, and Orsinium—all swearing loyalty to the Emperor.{{ref|name=TWITW}}{{ref|name=LOTE}}<noinclude>

Other instances of the Totem's usage had less overt ramifications. [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco, the King of Worms]], used the Totem to fuel his apotheosis and become a god.{{ref|name=LOTE}} The [[Lore:Underking|Underking]] is believed to have reclaimed his missing "heart"{{ref|name=DFWho|"[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?]]" — Letter from the Underking}} from Numidium, giving him the death he had sought for so long and also creating an [[Lore:Anti-Magicka|anti-magic]] zone several miles wide around the area.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN|[[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|''The Daggerfall Chronicles'' Narrative]]}} While unconfirmed, there are rumors that the agent of the Blades was killed while attempting to activate Numidium.{{ref|name=DFCN}}

==History==
===Background===
{{Quote Box|''Numidium was supposed to be a giant so big his hands could knock the moons from the sky.''|[[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed|Anonymous]]}}
[[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] was a colossal automaton created by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] and later used by Emperor [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] in his conquest of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. In order for Numidium to function, the Emperor's Battlemage, [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]] created two legendary artifacts. One was the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]], which allowed its bearer to control Numidium; the second was the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]], a huge gem containing the "heart", or life force, of a powerful individual, and used to power the golem.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

According to legend (though [[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|some sources]] dispute this version), Arctus used his own heart to power the Mantella, but objected to Numidium's further use after Tamriel was united. The Battlemage attempted to reclaim his heart; both he and Numidium were destroyed in the process, and the Totem and Mantella were lost. Unable to truly die without his heart, Arctus became the creature known as the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

Over the next four centuries, the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] were tasked with finding and reassembling the remains of Numidium. By {{Year|3E 401}}, the Totem of Tiber Septim was known to have been found, and in the possession of {{Lore Link|Lord Woodborne}} of [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN}} Sometime that year, the location of the final missing piece, the Mantella, was divined.{{ref|name=DFCN}}

In {{Year|3E 403}}, King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] was assassinated, though this fact was not immediately known. In {{Year|3E 405}}, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] sent an [[Lore:Hero|agent]] of the Blades to investigate, who revealed that Lord Woodborne killed the king in a scheme to seize power. The agent then recovered the Totem from Woodborne's co-conspirator, Lysandus' son [[Lore:Gothryd|Gothryd]], the new King of Daggerfall.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

Ownership of the Totem was now up for grabs, and the powers of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]  made their offers.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

===Key Figures===
[[File:DF-outro-Underking.png|right|thumb|The [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], clutching the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]]]]
{{Quote Box|''Only Crowned Heads will hold the Totem True<br>First is One Who Killed, But Did Not Kill Another<br>Second is One Who Is Two Devoured By Its Young<br>Third is One Who Made One Slave and Many Free<br>Fourth and Fifth are Two Who Compete for Mud<br>Sixth is One Who Brings a Home to the Homeless<br>Seventh is One Who Lords All, But Does Not Lord At All''|[[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] the sorceress}}
Zurin Arctus placed a seal upon the Totem of Tiber Septim during its creation, only those of royal lineage or with a special supernatural affinity would be deemed worthy of wielding its power; all others would be killed if they attempted to activate Numidium.{{ref|name=DFMantella|"[[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed|The Mantella Revealed]]" — Letter 3}} Ultimately, seven "worthy" factions emerged as potential claimants for the artifact. They were:
*[[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], and [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], represented by Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]], King [[Lore:Eadwyre|Eadwyre]] and Queen [[Lore:Barenziah|Barenziah]], and King [[Lore:Gothryd|Gothryd]], respectively. These three kingdoms wanted to destroy their enemies (namely, the other two kingdoms), and become the dominant power in the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN}}
*[[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco, the King of Worms]] desired the Totem not for Numidium's might but for the Mantella itself, intending to use its power to transform himself into a god.
*The [[Lore:Underking|Underking]] wanted to reclaim his heart, as he had failed to do centuries before, and finally be able to rest in death.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN}}
*The Warlord [[Lore:Gortwog|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]] wished to recreate the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]ish homeland of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], and to see his people treated as equals with the other races of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN}}
*Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel VII]] and the Blades, through his agent Lady [[Lore:Brisienna|Brisienna]], planned to use Numidium to pacify the rebellious kingdoms of the Iliac Bay and restore Imperial order over [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and Hammerfell.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFCN}}

A worthy individual was chosen by the mysterious agent of the Blades, who shortly thereafter was able to recover the Mantella from its resting place in [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]]. With everything in place, the bearer of the Totem of Tiber Septim activated Numidium.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

===The 9th of Frostfall===
{{Quote Box|''I was traveling north-east along the foothills of the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]], on the 9th of Frostfall, when I felt a great heat behind me, like a fire. I turned, but I regret to say I cannot tell you what I saw. The healers tell me my eyes were burned out of my sockets''|''[[Lore:The Warp in the West|The Warp in the West]]'' — The Report of High Rock Agent 'Graylady'}}
[[File:DF-misc-Numidium.jpg|thumb|right|Numidium, rising from the waters of the Iliac Bay]]
What occurred that day is unclear. Reports by the Blades are contradictory and paradoxical, as most agents were lost during the chaos. Their investigations concluded there was no plausible historical account of what exactly happened, only that a "miracle" occurred. Incredibly, in the first of many reported [[Lore:Dragon Break|distortions of time]], it appears that each of the seven powers took ownership of the Totem—simultaneously—and each of them achieved their goals to some degree.{{ref|name=WWYDB}} In fact, it seems these seven and their armies were aware of where (and when) they really were and what had actually transpired during the Warp, though few explanations proved forthcoming.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

The incident began on the 9th of Frostfall {{Year|3E 417}} and lasted through the 11th. To some observers the days merged into one, with no explanation for the missing time; other events progressed much further than those three days could account for.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

Natural disasters ravaged the region, from earthquakes and freak sandstorms in the [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]] to fires and tsunamis along the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]]. [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] and [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]] reportedly clashed in what had been the Principality of [[Lore:Gavaudon|Gavaudon]], in one of a reported seven titanic battles waged simultaneously around the Iliac Bay that day. Another such battle was a three-way clash between [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], Wayrest, and Orsinium in [[Lore:Daenia|Daenia]] and the [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]]. The resulting firestorm set the forests ablaze in [[Lore:Ykalon|Ykalon]] and [[Lore:Phrygias|Phrygias]], and the area surrounding the battlefield was said to have been "decimated".{{ref|name=TWITW}}

Some days after the Warp, an Imperial ambassador (and Blades agent) to the Court of Wayrest pressed the royal couple for information; Queen [[Lore:Barenziah|Barenziah]] simply replied, "We do not know." The ambassador was later able to learn from King [[Lore:Eadwyre|Eadwyre]] that a "powerful, ancient weapon" was going to be activated, and had attempted to buy it from a young adventurer before their enemies could use it against Wayrest. On the morning of the 9th, Wayrest was suddenly under attack; and simply reacted by sending its armies as well. How those armies were able to traverse the distance to the battlefields so quickly is still unexplained.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

Before the events of the 9th of Frostfall, the Iliac Bay region was comprised of forty-four independent regions. On the 11th, there were four—Daggerfall, Sentinel, Wayrest, and Orsinium—their new borders demarcated by a blackened, desolate no-man's land. The Blades reported "other strange events" also occurred as well, which somehow prevented the warring kingdoms from breaking away from the Empire (and possibly more). The fighting continued for months afterward, but those clashes were nothing compared to the raw power unleashed during the Warp in the West.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

===The "Miracle of Peace"===
{{Quote Box|''The catastrophic destruction of landscape and property and the large loss of life attending upon this miracle is understood to have been <nowiki>'</nowiki>tragic, and beyond mortal comprehension.<nowiki>'</nowiki>''|''[[Lore:The Warp in the West|The Warp in the West]]'' — Ulvius Tero}}
In the years that followed, the Warp in the West gained another sobriquet, the Miracle of Peace. Despite the indescribable death and destruction that took place, the end result was a stable, relatively peaceful [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] loyal to the Empire{{ref|name=TWITW}} and more unified than at any point in modern history.{{ref|name=PGE3High|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}} By {{Year|3E 433}}, the rulers of the four kingdoms were deemed to be "ordained by [[Lore:Nine Divines|the Nine]]", and most {{Lore Link|Bretons}} had come to believe the ancient [[Lore:Gods|Gods]] of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] ([[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], [[Lore:Mara|Mara]], and [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]){{ref|name=TWITW}} had scoured the land and made it whole once again.{{ref|name=PGE3High}} The Blades strongly doubted this "miracle" was of divine origin.{{ref|name=TWITW}}

Sentinel grew to encompass the entire northern coast of Hammerfell, from [[Lore:Abibon-Gora|Abibon-Gora]] in the west to [[Lore:Satakalaam|Satakalaam]] in the east. After the death of Queen Akorithi, her son [[Lore:Lhotun|Lhotun]] took the throne; his rule was marked by reverence for the kingdom's [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n past but respect for modern Imperial ways.{{ref|name=PGE3Hf|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}}

King Gortwog proved to be a political and diplomatic genius, and Nova Orsinium{{ref|name=LOTE}} gained recognition by the Empire, though was still considered a part of High Rock at the time.{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}} Within ten years of the Miracle of Peace, Orcish citizens were found in all walks of life, even in far-flung corners of the Empire.{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The appearance of the [[Lore:Necromancer's Moon|Necromancer's Moon]] in the years following the Miracle of Peace was believed by [[Lore:Worm Cult|Mannimarco's followers]] to be the physical embodiment of his apotheosis.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Necromancer's Moon (book)}}}}

Numidium appears to have vanished from Tamriel during the Miracle of Peace,{{ref|name=TWITW}} and the Underking has also not been seen since.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} It is rumored that they were both annihilated when the Underking reclaimed his heart, and that an anti-magic area was created in the area as a result, though its exact location is not known.{{ref|name=DFCN}} The mysterious agent who found the Totem of Tiber Septim also disappeared in the chaos.{{ref|name=TWITW}}
