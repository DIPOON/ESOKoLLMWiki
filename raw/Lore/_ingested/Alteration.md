'''Alteration''' is one of the six schools of [[Lore:Magic|magic]]. Spellcraft of the School of Alteration involves, simply put, the distortion of reality (usually in a localized scale) through the direct imposition of the caster’s will. Alteration demands uncommon sense from the caster and the understanding that the school is all about possibilities and turning things into what they ''could'' be. In conclusion: the realization that physical law can be broken. The world perceived by mortality is based on the perception of greater forces impressed upon it, be it divine element or something beyond said element. While important, it is irrelevant to the caster. What matters is the persuading of said forces, convincing them that the spellwork practiced (or better, suggested) is easier to follow than what is already there. Based on this principle, subtle changes to reality are more likely to be a success. Nevertheless, the change is temporary, the world will resume to its original state, no matter how much the mage desires otherwise. Reality inevitably advances on the spellwork, fading it away.

It is worth mentioning that while the Schools of Illusion and Alteration are easy to confuse by attempting the creation of the nonexistent, they are respectively not bound and bound by the laws of nature. Meaning that Illusion alters perception in oneself or others, in the mind of the caster and the target. The effects of Alteration conclude in a reality recognized by all.{{ref|name=ROF|{{Cite Book|Reality & Other Falsehoods}}}}{{ref|name=BW|{{Cite Book|Breathing Water}}}}{{ref|name=PSOM|{{Cite Book|Proposal: Schools of Magic}}}}




==History==
The spells of the school seem to have originated with the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] of southern [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] no later than the second century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], when they used them to resist the armies of [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] as they swept across the continent.{{ref|name=DOTN|{{Cite Book|Daughter of the Niben}}}} Their mastery of such spells extended the siege of the area around what would later be [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] by some time. The usefulness of the school in the art of war was illustrated again at the end of the first era, when the Imperial forces used it to outwit [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] by sending an entire army underneath Lake Coronati to take the city of [[Lore:Ald Marak|Ald Marak]].{{ref|name=2920v6|{{Cite Book|2920, MidYear (v6)}}}} These military applications were evidently in the mind of Berevar Bero, an [[Lore:Illusion|illusionist]] who, between late 3E 399 and 3E 426, made a speech that argued the School of Destruction should be merged into the School of Alteration.{{ref|name=RTBS|{{Cite Book|Response to Bero's Speech}}}}

In {{Year|3E 431}}, the Council of Mages decided that Alteration magic in Cyrodiil was to become the responsibility of the guildhall in the town of [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]].{{ref|name=MGC|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}}

==Types of Alteration==
Typically, the spells boil down into the following types: shielding, creating objects, changing weights, water effects, actions on locks and movement through the air.

* There are two types of shield spell: one simply protects the caster from a proportion of physical damage, and the other does this but also adds extra protection against a particular elemental attack.

* Armor spells can turn a caster's flesh into a different material, such as oak, [[Lore:Iron|iron]], or [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]], to protect against damage.

* Transmutation spells can turn iron into [[Lore:Silver|silver]], and silver into [[Lore:Gold|gold]].

* Weight-changing magic can produce a "Feather" effect that reduces the weight of any items carried, or a "Burden" effect that increases their weight.

* The effects of alteration magic are among the most useful, with spells that allow the caster to breathe underwater or to walk on its surface. Also in this class is a spell that enabled the caster to swim more quickly, but this spell has fallen into disfavor, as the Fortify Speed spell from the college of [[Lore:Restoration|Restoration]] is useful on land as well as water.

* Alteration magic can be used on locks, whether to open them or secure them.

* There are three alteration effects that change the caster's motion through the air. One greatly increases the power and range of leaps, making genuinely superhuman jumps possible. The second effect reduces the speed at which the target falls, eliminating all falling damage. The third effect is genuine levitation, allowing the spell's target to move freely through the air. These effects were restricted in the [[Lore:Levitation Act|Levitation Act]] of {{Year|3E 421}}.

* Glowlights can be summoned to provide a light source for the caster, or to illuminate a target.
