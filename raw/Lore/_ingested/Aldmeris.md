[[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]], also known as the '''Isle of Aldmeris''',{{ref|name=TUSI|{{Cite Book|The Ubiquitous Sinking Isle}}}} the '''Island of Start''',{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Mantia Intercept|ns_base=General}}}} '''Lost Aldmeris''',{{ref|name=PGE3OL}} '''Old Aldmeris''',{{ref|name=LS|[[ON:Toothmaul Gully|Toothmaul Gully]] and [[ON:Ilayas Ruins|Ilayas Ruins]] [[ON:Loading Screens|loading screens]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} and '''Old [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]]''',{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} is the mythical lost continent from which the [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] are said to have originated. The term Aldmeris also refers to the [[Lore:Ehlnofex Languages|language]] spoken by the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]].{{Ref|name=ONHTOS|[[Online:Rivenspire Antiquities#House Tamrith Official Seal|House Tamrith Official Seal]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Virtually nothing is known of the elven homeland. Its location, its environment, its politics, its religion, and even its existence are the stuff of conjecture.{{ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} By some accounts, Aldmeris may have been located somewhere in the South [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]].{{ref|name=SotSP|[[Online:Seat of the Snow Prince|Seat of the Snow Prince Antiquities Codexes]] from [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} According to Altmeri epic poetry, when the [[Lore:Ancients|Ancients]] sailed to the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] and landed at the northern tip of [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]], they lost the means to navigate back to Aldmeris, but had reason to believe their homeland lay in one of three different directions: south, northwest, or [[Lore:Tamriel|northeast]].{{ref|name=TLOF}}{{Ref|name=FOTN}}<noinclude>

==Mythology==
Little is known of Aldmeris' history apart from the traditional legend from the Early [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]. In the Anuad, a large fragment of the Ehlnofey world landed on Nirn relatively intact, and the Ehlnofey living there were the ancestors of the Mer. These Ehlnofey fortified their borders from the chaos outside, hid their pocket of calm, and attempted to live on as before.{{Ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} Some sources say that to make up for Convention, [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] led the original Aldmer against the armies of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] in mythic times, vanquishing that tyrant and establishing the first kingdoms of the Altmer: [[Lore:Altmora|Altmora]] and Old Ehlnofey.{{Ref|name=VoF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} The Aldmer were "chased south and east" to Old Ehlnofey after Auriel's forces lost Altmora to the [[Lore:Men|Men]], and Lorkhan was close behind.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} Other Ehlnofey arrived on Nirn scattered amid the confused jumble of the shattered worlds, wandering and finding each other over the years. Eventually, the wandering Ehlnofey found the hidden land of Old Ehlnofey, and were amazed and joyful to find their kin living amid the splendor of ages past. The wandering Ehlnofey expected to be welcomed into the peaceful realm, but the Old Ehlnofey looked on them as degenerates, fallen from their former glory. For whatever reason, war broke out, and raged across the whole of Nirn. The Old Ehlnofey retained their ancient power and knowledge, but the Wanderers were more numerous, and toughened by their long struggle to survive on Nirn. This war reshaped the face of Nirn, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans; the Old Ehlnofey realm, although ruined, became [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{Ref|name=TAA}}

==History==
In more pragmatic records, the continent faced an unspecified threat or crisis, causing the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] to leave in search of a new home across the seas.{{ref|name=BTAOM}} The Aldmeri refugees then became lost in a storm,{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} and eventually landed in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], at Nine-Prow Landing on the island of [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]] in the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]].{{ref|name=TLOF|{{Cite Book|The Lay of Firsthold}}}}{{ref|name=OTA|{{Cite Book|Ode to Auridon}}}} These Aldmer were the [[Lore:Ancients|Ancients]], the ancestors of the modern-day [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]].{{ref|name=AE|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored}}}} The [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]]s also trace their roots to Aldmeris,{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}}{{ref|name=TSoP|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal}}}} and one of the origin stories for the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] involved them arriving in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] from Aldmeris.{{Ref|name=TGS|{{Cite Book|The Green Singing}}}} What befell Aldmeris after the Aldmer left it is an enduring mystery, theorized to be the result of the elves' fall from Aedric grace.{{ref|name=TUSI}} Some claim that the angry gods of the Aldmer sank the land into the sea, others that Aldmeris has left [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] altogether and will not return until the races of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] are reunited.{{ref|name=PGE3OL}}

A second legend associated with Aldmeris concerns the rise of the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]], the ancient rivals of the Altmer. Translations of tapestries in the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] assert that an exceedingly wealthy Aldmer nobleman named [[Lore:Orgnum|Orgnum]] funded and led a rebellion against the rulers of Aldmeris. The rebellion was quashed, and Orgnum and his followers were banished to [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]] behind a wall of impenetrable mists that would forever prevent them from returning to Aldmeris.{{ref|name=PGE3OL}} This exile proved so effective that the followers of Orgnum never again disturbed their former countrymen. The new Aldmeri homeland of Summerset, however, was not so lucky.{{ref|name=TMoP|{{Cite Book|The Maormer of Pyandonea}}}} For their part, the Maormer believe that they are directly descended from the purest strain of Aldmer, and were wrongfully driven out of Aldmeris by the Altmer, whom they deride as a "mongrel race" that stole the Maormer's birthright, the Summerset Isles.{{ref|name=LS}}{{ref|name=TCPOA|{{Cite Book|The Chosen People of Aldmeris}}}}

In the Middle Merethic Era, the Aldmer of Summerset organized the earliest known expedition to relocate Aldmeris and learn its fate. Three ships, the ''Niben'', the ''Pasquiniel'', and a third whose name is lost to history, were each given a crystalline ball called a [[Lore:Waystone|waystone]] and directed to search in the direction to which their waystone was attuned. The ''Pasquiniel'' went south, the unnamed ship went northwest, and the Niben, captained by the famed [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]], went northeast and discovered mainland Tamriel. Only the ''Niben'' returned to Summerset after an inadvertently lengthy tour of Tamriel's coasts and rivers; the other two ships were never heard from again. Although the expedition failed in its goal to find Aldmeris, it is assumed that the Aldmer had a compelling reason to believe their former homeland lay in one of those three directions.{{Ref|name=FOTN}}

A triptych titled "Echoes of Aldmeris", preserved by a fixative spell and recovered by archeologists in the middle Second Era, depicts Aldmeris in distinctive style with proud spires, floating obelisks, and wispy, concentric clouds. The accuracy of the portrayal is doubtful, though one Altmer scholar stated that it "stirs up a feeling in me ... like entering my father's house after a long journey abroad".{{ref|name=TLSB|[[Online:Auridon Antiquities|Echoes of Aldmeris]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Society==
The politics, religion, and culture of Aldmeris are mysteries. Translations of ancient tapestries and texts preserved in the Crystal Tower vaguely describe a beautiful yet alien land. Notably, representations of Aldmeris always depict it as devoid of all forms of plant and animal life with the exception of the Aldmer themselves. The land is shown as an endless city, built upon itself over and over again until no trace of nature remained.{{ref|name=PGE3OL}} It is said that should the burgeoning sister cities of [[Lore:Sunhold|Sunhold]] and [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] ever merge, an eventuality prevented by the mountain range between them, they would form a metropolis on a scale not seen since Lost Aldmeris.{{Ref|name=OBGYN|{{Cite Book|Our Blessed Isles: A Guide}}}} Despite the belief that it was devoid of vegetation some Altmer of the times of Interregnum were known to exclaim "by the shining trees of Aldmeris".{{Ref|name=ONGladiator Andulalion|[[Online:Gladiator Andulalion|Gladiator Andulalion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Altmer believe that they are all that remains of Old Ehlnofey, keeping their heritage pure and civilization unspoiled to be in line with was once practiced in Aldmeris.{{Ref|name=CotB|{{Cite Book|Corruption of the Blood}}}} Some scholars assert that Altmeri traditions are closer to pre-[[Lore:First Era|First Era]] society than any other race's, and although Altmeri culture has still deviated and ramified in many ways, studying it provides insight into its ancient origins.{{Ref|name=CM:1|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 1: High Elf Style}}}} According to legend, the highest towers of Aldmeris were reserved for interring the dead, and this tradition is still practiced among the Altmer of Summerset.{{ref|name=PGE3OL}}

* In an out-of-game text by [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]], when discussing portentions of the coming [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] with the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]], the [[Lore:Moth Priests|Moth Priest]] Nu-Hatta contends that Aldmeris never was a physical homeland, but rather a collection of mental images left over from the chaos of the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]].{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}} The text claims all elven peoples shared this tapestry of racial memory and as they diverged along cultural lines, their images of Aldmeris changed as well, and the figurative heritage was sundered, represented by the Towers that now dot Tamriel.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}
