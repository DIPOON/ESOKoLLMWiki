[[Lore:Gryphon|Gryphon]]s (also spelled '''Griffin'''{{Ref|The names of various [[Arena:Inns|Inns]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} or '''Griffon'''){{Ref|name=Tuminderion|[[ON:Tuminderion|Tuminderion]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} are large winged creatures that live primarily on the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]], within the province of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]],{{ref|name=SumGryph|Appearance of [[ON:Gryphon|gryphons]] on [[ON:Summerset|Summerset]] in [[ON:Online|ESO:]] [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]}} though they can also be found in the province of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]],{{ref|name=ElsGryph|Appearance of [[ON:Gryphon|gryphons]] in [[ON:Northern Elsweyr|Northern Elsweyr]] in [[ON:Online|ESO:]] [[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]}} and possibly the provinces of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], due to [[Lore:Harpy|Harpy]] clan leaders native to the provinces being known to wear dyed gryphon feathers.{{ref|Dialogue during the [[Daggerfall:The Harpies' Nest|Harpies' Nest]] quest in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Gryphon's feather|Gryphon's feather]]s in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} They are generally found across the mountains of Summerset, primarily around [[Lore:Eton Nir|Eton Nir]], and specifically at the northeast point at [[Lore:Gryphon Aerie|Gryphon Aerie]].{{ref|Large gatherings of [[ON:Gryphon|gryphons]] at [[ON:Gryphon Aerie|Gryphon Aerie]] in [[ON:Online|ESO:]] [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]}} They can be described as a combination of a [[Lore:lion|lion]] and an [[Lore:eagle|eagle]], though they bear more of a resemblance to an eagle on four legs. Gryphons are fiercely territorial.{{Ref|name=Kinlady|[[Online:Kinlady Helenaere|Kinlady Helenaere]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} They will attack anyone who intrudes upon their nests or hunting grounds.{{ref|name=JF}} Gryphons are predators and eat live prey. Gryphons aren't picky eaters, and will capture anyone foolish enough to traverse their hunting grounds.{{ref|name=JF}} Young gryphons are known as fledglings.{{Ref|[[Online:Fledgling Gryphon|Fledgling Gryphon]] creature in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} [[Lore:Salamander|Salamander]]s, [[Lore:Deer|deer]] and [[Lore:Cow|cow]]s are among the animals that may find themselves hunted by a hungry gryphon.{{ref|Appearance of gryphons in the wild near the corpses of [[ON:Salamander|Salamanders]], [[ON:Cow|Cows]], and other animals in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Gryphons often make their nests on the sides of mountains or upon cliffs. They occasionally drag their prey back to their nests.{{ref|Gryphon nest appearance and locations in [[ON:Online|ESO:]] [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]}}

Gryphons are revered by the [[Lore:Altmer|High Elves]].{{ref|name=JF|[[ON:Justiciar Farowel|Justiciar Farowel]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Birds of a Feather (quest)|Birds of a Feather]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The Altmer fear the gryphon's ferocity, but respect their strength and freedom.{{ref|name=JF}} In some parts of Summerset, such as [[Lore:Sunhold|Sunhold]] and [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]], the gryphon is sacred.{{ref|name=JF}} In ancient times, many High Elves considered gryphons unsuitable to be mounted, but now the city-states of [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]] and [[Lore:Sunhold|Sunhold]] are known for their tamed gryphons, which are a part of their iconography.{{Ref|name=TFoG|{{Cite Book|The Flight of Gryphons}}}}{{Ref|name=TWoEN|{{Cite Book|The Welkynars of Eton Nir}}}}

[[Lore:Gryphon's feather|Gryphon's feather]]s are circulated in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region and have [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemical]] properties.{{Ref|[[Daggerfall:Gryphon's feather|Gryphon's feather]] ingredient in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The feathers can also be used to make tools such as brooms,{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gryphon-Feather Broom|Gryphon-Feather Broom]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and down feathers are used to make pillows.{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gryphon Down Pillow|Gryphon Down Pillow]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Gryphon feathers have an iridescent vane, and are prized by the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]].{{ref|{{Item Link|Large Gryphon Feather|questid=6521}} item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Unfertilized yolks from gryphon eggs,{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gryphon Egg Lotion|Gryphon Egg Lotion]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} as well as secretions including saliva, can be used to make skincare products.{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gryphon Nest Extract Moisturizer|Gryphon Nest Extract Moisturizer]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Gryphon vellum is a popular material among Altmer monks for making illuminated texts, and it has a unique texture due to the feather-dimpling.{{Ref|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gryphon Vellum|Gryphon Vellum]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Summerset, it is illegal to collect gryphon eggs for the purpose of selling them somewhere off the island.{{ref|name=ESOMiranrel|[[ON:Miranrel|Miranrel]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Gryphon Grievance|Gryphon Grievance]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

As many creatures are, the Gryphon is susceptible to a slew of diseases. One well-known example is a condition known as Wing Rot, which affects the wings of the afflicted.{{ref|name=ROWRCT|{{Cite Book|Report on Wing Rot Curative Trials|ns_base=Online}}}} Wing Rot is known to be a chronic infection that likely affects a gryphons' ability to fly, and as such, young gryphons with the affliction are often kicked out of the nest and eaten by [[Lore:Welwa|welwas]].{{ref|name=ROWRCT}} The conservatory at [[Lore:Sil-Var-Woad|Sil-Var-Woad]] began working on a cure for the disease during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], allowing young gryphons with the disease to live comfortable lives in captivity.{{ref|name=ROWRCT}}

==History==
[[File:ON-creature-Darremalatu 02.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Welkynars next to her gryphon partner]]
While not spoken of Altmeri records, the [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]] tribes were the first on the Summerset Isles to tame gryphons. They were said to be quite adept at capturing and training the creatures.{{ref|{{Cite Book|King's Haven Trade Record}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Mehdze|Mehdze]]'s dialogue in ESO:Summerset}} 

In early Altmeri history, the Altmer sought to tame gryphons but were unable to because of their ferocity. Early Altmer tribes lived in the mountains and crags of Summerset Isle where they would harvest gryphon eggs, hoping for them to hatch and thus later be tamed.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} However, these eggs would never hatch. All was not entirely lost, as these eggs would be used in large feasts, since they were considered delicacies for kings.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} This lasted for an uncertain time, until one day when the Altmer tribesman [[Lore:Ulorome|Ulorome]] found an abandoned egg in the aftermath of a gryphon nest hunt.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} He took the egg home, and knowing that it would most likely not hatch, prepared a feast for his people and placed the egg in a healthy fire. The tribe would sing and dance to bless their meal, but these factors would inadvertently lead to the hatching of the egg.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} The light from the fire made the young fledgling look like a [[Lore:Phoenix|phoenix]] with red feathers. This gryphon would be named [[Lore:Cel-hinwe|Cel-hinwe]].{{Ref|name=TFoG}}

This ancient tribe is the predecessor of [[Lore:Sunhold|Sunhold]], and Ulorome's lineage would become the modern-day Kinlords of Sunhold. The gryphons descended from Cel-hinwe would become the city's protectors.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} In [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]i legend, Cel-hinwe and his lineage are known as "the Red Wind." Many believe that the Kinlord and Gryphon cannot live without the other.{{Ref|name=TFoG}} Since that time, taming gryphons is known as a possible feat.

The [[Lore:Welkynars|Welkynars]] of [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]] are known for riding their gryphons as part of their duties of protecting the Eternal Isle.{{Ref|name=TWoEN}} Because they respond to urgent calls across the sub-continent, they utilize gryphons for fast transport from their aerie in Eton Nir to as far as the southern coast. The Welkynar Gryphon Riders have long been a part of Summerset's history.{{Ref|name=TWoEN}} Gryphon plumage is used in crafting the Welkynars' weapons and armor.{{Ref|name=CM67|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 67: Welkynar Style}}}}

[[Lore:Quasigriff|Quasigriffs]] are large, feathered, wingless quadrupeds that are descended from Summerset gryphons. Created by [[Lore:Irastimil Direnni|Irastimil Direnni]], they are the product of wizardry and selective breeding.{{ref|[[Online:Quasigriff (mount)|Quasigriff]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Training==
The Welkynars are the only organization known to regularly tame gryphons. Training a gryphon is considered a dangerous task, because of how violent and deadly they can be. A fledgling could nip off a couple of fingers if one is not careful.{{Ref|name=TGofW|{{Cite Book|The Gryphons of the Welkynars}}}} Due to their snapping beaks, gloves are an essential part of training a young gryphon.{{Ref|name=CM67}} Every Welkynar must undergo a bonding process with their gryphon. Eggs are collected from wild gryphon nests, which itself is a dangerous task.{{Ref|name=TGofW}} An egg must be collected - an orphaned fledgling cannot be trained. Once the egg is hatched, the bonding process begins, which initially starts with a magical ritual to first create the bond.{{Ref|name=TGofW}} From then on, the process continues through familiarity, where the aspiring Welkynar must care for the fledgling, and expect many tiring days and nights. Once the process is complete, the gryphon becomes a loyal soldier to the Welkynar, and the experience is considered unimaginable. It essentially becomes an extension of the rider. Once the process is successfully completed, the aspirant becomes a true Welkynar.{{Ref|name=TGofW}}

A select group of members of {{Lore Link|Ascendant Order}} active in the [[ON:Coral Aerie|Coral Aerie]], an islet off the northern shore of Summerset Isle {{ref|name=GWL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Gryphon Watching Log}}}}{{ref|name=Coral Aerie|[[ON:Coral Aerie| Coral Aerie]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Sea Giant|Sea Giant]] Yandir who participated in raid of [[Lore:Kyne's Aegis|Kyne's Aegis]] in {{Year|2E 582}} were also able to tame gryphons.{{ref|name=ESOYandirSkills|[[ON:Yandir the Butcher|Yandir the Butcher]]'s abilities and pet in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

===Snowcap Gryphon===
Snowcap Gryphons are found at the utmost highest peaks of Eton Nir, where the snow never melts. They are largely pale, and because of this, they are perfectly camouflaged among the clouds. For most of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Snowcap Gryphons were considered a myth.{{Ref|[[Online:Snowcap Fledgling Gryphon|Snowcap Fledgling Gryphon]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
