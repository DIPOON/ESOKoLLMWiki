Vaermina's [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|plane of Oblivion]] is [[Lore:Quagmire|Quagmire]], described by observers as a nightmarish land.{{Ref|name=TDOO|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=SRErandur|[[Skyrim:Erandur|Erandur]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} It is said Vaermina hungers for the memories of mortals, collecting them from her citadel at the center of the realm, and leaving them with visions of horror and despair.{{ref|name=SRErandur}} Every few minutes, reality shifts and becomes ever more horrifying.{{ref|name=TDOO}} It is not known what Vaermina does with these memories, but it is assumed to be malevolent.{{ref|name=SRErandur}}

Vaermina is said to be an enemy of [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]], and [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], but an ally of [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]].{{ref|name=DFFactions|[[Daggerfall:Oblivion|Factions]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} She is also at odds with [[Lore:Azura|Azura]].{{ref|name=TIEGTT}}{{ref|name=AWT|[[Online:Azura's Winged Twilight|Azura's Winged Twilight]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Azura's Aid|Azura's Aid]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Her summoning day coincides with the [[Lore:Merchants Festival|Merchants' Festival]], the 10<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Height.{{ref|name=DFHolidays|[[Daggerfall:Holidays|Holidays]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

==In Religion and Culture==

===Khajiiti Religion===
'''Varmiina''', also called the '''Lost Daughter''', is a spirit found in [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] traditions predating the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]]. She is said to be a child of [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]], born out of her mother's fear of losing her children. [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] killed Varmiina in the Underworld, and now she can only haunt the dreams of the Khajiit. It is believed that she tests them by playing to their fear, for she cannot directly harm the [[Lore:Lunar Lattice|Lunar Lattice]].{{ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}

According to one pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany Khajiit account, during a battle to defend the [[Lore:Moons|Lunar Lattice]] from [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molagh]] and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Merrunz]], Noctra, is said to have learned from Azurah's vanquishing of Vaarmina, and used the [[Lore:Skeleton Key|Skeleton Key]] on herself in order to stop Merid-Nunda from accessing the [[Lore:Sun|Aether Prism]].{{Ref|name=Blade|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume III}}}}
===Sixteen Accords of Madness===
One tale in the ''[[Lore:16 Accords of Madness, v. IX|Sixteen Accords of Madness]]'' involves the Lady of Nightmares learning about the importance of madness for dreams from the Mad God [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]. In the story, Sheogorath and Vaermina make a wager over the mind of Darius Shano. Shano was a disciple of Vaermina who had been given dreams that nurtured his literary inspiration by his patron, and thus found acclaim as an emerging bard and poet. The Prince of Madness, Sheogorath, argued that it was the hatred of mortals that confirmed one's greatness, rather than their love, and so the two Princes made a bet to see who could inspire greater hatred of Shano. Each Prince agreed to exert their influence on the mortal for ten years. For her ten years, Vaermina showed Shano terrible nightmares, but the shocking scenes he saw were put into his new work, which both revolted and fascinated the public. Than, after ten years, the dreams completely ceased. Bereft of that which had given him meaning, his contact with a deity, Shano soon began to believe that he'd imagined the whole experience, and the gods did not exist at all. The disillusioned Shano performed a number of displays which the public deemed blasphemous and heretical and, eventually, wrote a text ridiculing [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] for integrating into the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]] cult. For his actions Shano was executed for blasphemy. When the Princes met again, Vaermina accused Sheogorath that he'd done nothing during his time with the mortal. But Sheogorath simply responded that doing nothing was his influence, bereft of that which had given him meaning, Shano had fallen into bitterness and resentment that turned into a rage filled madness, and the poet's soul now resided in Sheogorath's own realm. Thus did Vaernima learn that, without madness, there are no dreams, and no creation, a lesson she would never forget.{{ref|name=Accords|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness}}}}

===Worship===
Vaermina often communicates to her worshipers while they sleep.{{ref|name=TOD|{{Cite Book|The Omen of Deception}}}} Exposure to the horrors Vaermina shows them may cause her faithful to be less inclined to feel fear.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Aviera Nirol|Aviera Nirol]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Tenville|Tenville]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Her cultists perform blood sacrifices to appease and petition Vaermina,{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Promise Made|ns_base=Online}}}} as well as to summon Daedra from Quagmire. They are not averse to using dark magic.{{ref|name=ErandurFol|[[SR:Erandur|Erandur]]'s [[SR:Erandur#Follower Dialogue|Follower Dialogue]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Some cults that venerate Vaermina do not allow the children within their ranks to socialize.{{ref|name=ErandurFol}}

Vaermina's followers sometimes partake in rituals that involve falling asleep or forcing captives to have vivid nightmares. Some of her cultists steal memories from sleeping innocents. The priests of Vaermina who dwelled in Nightcaller Temple created a gas called the Miasma for their sleeping rituals. It lulls those it affects into a deep slumber, and was designed to slow the aging process, as the rituals in Nightcaller Temple could last for months or years.{{ref|name=SRErandur}} Vaermina's militant cults kidnap citizens to use as vessels for Vaermina's Daedric minions.{{ref|name=LTS}} The victim is put into an enchanted sleep and bound in front of a portal from Quagmire. A Dremora's heart is used to summon a Daedric spirit to inhabit the body.{{ref|name=BPTSF|[[Online:Brother Perry|Brother Perry]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Slumbering Farmer|The Slumbering Farmer]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:SR-npc-Veren Duleri.jpg|thumb|right|A Priest of Vaermina wearing purple robes]]
Priests of Vaermina are reputed to be master [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemists]], their highly-prized potions fetching handsome prices on the black market. Notable among these is a concoction known as "Vaermina's Torpor", which places the imbiber in a state known as the "Dreamstride".{{ref|name=TD|{{Cite Book|The Dreamstride}}}} Subjects are granted the ability to enter the dreams of others and travel distances in the real world.{{ref|name=SRErandur}}{{ref|name=TD}}{{ref|name=Dreamwalkers|{{Cite Book|Dreamwalkers}}}} One seems to vanish when entering the Dreamstride, only to reappear in the location their dream-self was in when the effect wears off; subjects have been known to reappear thousands of miles away in minutes. However, the Dreamstride is not without risks, and death or disappearance remain very real possibilities.{{ref|name=TD}}

The [[Lore:Supernal Dreamers|Supernal Dreamers]] were a cult dedicated to Vaermina that was active during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. They sought to plunge the world in Vaermina's darkness. The waking world was full of suffering, lies and starvation, and the Dreamers believed that they would create a better world.{{ref|name=IDWA|{{Cite Book|In Dreams We Awaken}}}} The Supernal Dreamers believed that those who refused to accept this, those who were driven mad by Vaermina's dreams, were weak, unworthy, and blind to Vaermina's truths.{{ref|name=DOTHC|{{Cite Book|Dreamers Our Time Has Come}}}}

There exists people known as Dreamwalkers, who can step into the dreams of another through the use of a simple spell. Some believe these Dreamwalkers must have sold their souls to Vaermina to enter her plane, but this claim cannot be verified. Their abilities are compared to that of Vaermina's priests, who can enter the Dreamstride using an alchemical concoction. The Dreamwalkers' abilities seem innate; not taught nor hereditary. Many of the Dreamwalkers acknowledged in academic works were noted to be kind and gentle, using their abilities to help others. They are capable of eliminating painful memories and curing ills that touch the mind.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Dreamwalkers}}}}

A shrine to Vaermina located southwest of [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]] dates back to at least the Interregnum. The devoted who worship at this shrine seek for her to grant them true visions";{{ref|[[Oblivion:Aymar Douar|Aymar Douar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} they believed the nightmares she sent them allowed them to see the world as it truly is.{{ref|[[Online:Molacar|Molacar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They receive visions and portents from her in their dreams,{{ref|[[Online:Hears-the-Wind (Vaermina's Shrine)|Hears-the-Wind]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and their Nightcaller interprets the Prince's message.{{ref|name=ESOCaledonia}} 

The ruin of [[Lore:Ten-Maur-Wolk|Ten-Maur-Wolk]] in [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] is dedicated to Vaermina.{{ref|name=AFT}}

==History==
[[File:ON-npc-Glyphic of Lost Memories.jpg|A memory of the meeting between Peryite, Vaermina, and Hermaeus Mora|thumb|right]]
At some point in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] sought out the aid of the other Princes in imprisoning the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], whose manipulations of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]] and planned use of the [[Lore:Loom of the Untraveled Road|Loom of the Untraveled Road]] threatened to unravel all of [[Lore:Aurbis|reality]].{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LeramilWise|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Untraveled|Events of [[ON:The Untraveled Road|The Untraveled Road]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Eventually an alliance was formed between Hermaeus Mora and [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], who proceeded to defeat Ithelia through their combined efforts and design a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.{{ref|name=Mora|[[ON:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dandera|[[ON:Priest Dandera Helas|Priest Dandera Helas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Prisoner|Events of [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Two of the Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought were Vaermina and [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]]. However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sancrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.{{ref|name=Hidden|Events of [[ON:A Hidden Fate|A Hidden Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Vaermina|[[ON:Vaermina|Vaermina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point, a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] noble from [[Lore:Menevia|Menevia]] named [[Lore:Galthis|Galthis]], started to dwell in dreams more than in the waking world, believing the colors of his dream homeland were more true and pure than those of its waking version. Galthis sought to reside in his "Reverie-Menevia" forever. Speaking the name over and over, it soon morphed into "Vaermina". Then, the Prince came to him and took him to her side as her consort, naming him Supernal Dreamer and First Nightcaller. In this event Galthis was transformed into the Night Terror, a shapeshifting creature of dreams.{{ref|name=TDBD|{{Cite Book|To Dream Beyond Dreams}}}}{{ref|name=ONGalthis|[[Online:Galthis|Galthis]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONGambit}}
===Second Era===
====During the Interregnum====
[[File:ON-creature-Galthis.jpg|thumb|left|The Night Terror]]
Some time during the Second Era, Azura foresaw that Vaermina would attempt to plunge [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]] into a nightmarish state. She called upon an Orc named {{Lore Link|Durak}} to travel to the region, where he founded the {{Lore Link|Spirit Wardens}} to protect the land from Vaermina's plague of dreams.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Founding of the Spirit Wardens}}}} The Spirit Wardens used an artifact called the Dream Shard to produce dreamless potions to protect themselves and others from Vaermina's influence.{{ref|name=OCOP}}{{ref|[[Online:Abbot Durak|Abbot Durak]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Azura's Guardian|Azura's Guardian]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, the plot foreseen by Azura would come to fruition when a cult known as the Supernal Dreamers sought to spread her influence across Tamriel in the wake of the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]]. The cult undermined the efforts of the Spirit Wardens wherever they could,{{ref|Events of [[Online:Azura's Guardian|Azura's Guardian]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and desecrated Azura's shrine in [[Lore:Gavaudon|Gavaudon]].{{ref|[[Online:Abbot Durak|Abbot Durak]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Another Omen|Another Omen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With the cult's assistance, Vaermina's Omens invaded the dreams and minds of influential figures throughout [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]].{{ref|Events of [[Online:Godrun's Dream|Godrun's Dream]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This conspiracy culminated with her consort {{Lore Link|Galthis}},{{ref|name=AWT}} the first Nightcaller,{{ref|name=TDBD|{{Cite Book|To Dream Beyond Dreams}}}} taking over the mind and body of High King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] in [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]]. Luckily, the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] intervened, and the plot was ultimately foiled when the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] entered Emeric's dream and defeated Galthis the Night Terror, ending Vaermina's control of the Covenant.{{ref|name=ONGambit|Events of [[Online:Vaermina's Gambit|Vaermina's Gambit]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Later that same year, Vaermina would exploit the weakened barrier between Oblivion and the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] in [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]] by merging [[Lore:Isle of Gold|Skald's Retreat]] with her own realm. She enthralled a bard and instructed her to sing an ancient song that allowed Skald's Retreat to be swallowed by Quagmire.{{ref|[[Online:Engling|Engling]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Song of Awakening|Song of Awakening]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She also ordered her minions to destroy any tomes or artifacts mentioning her name, as Vaermina doesn't like anyone knowing about her.{{ref|name=ESOBragur|[[Online:Loremaster Bragur|Loremaster Bragur]]'s dialogue during and after [[Online:Guard the Knowledge|Guard the Knowledge]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Vaermina's hold on the college would be broken by the Vestige once more when they woke the sleeping bards and had them sing an ancient Nord epic, breaking the spell placed on the isle.{{ref|name=ONAwakening|Events of [[Online:Song of Awakening|Song of Awakening]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
====Invasion of Apocrypha====
[[File:ON-crown store-Kindred's Concord Style.jpg|Members of the Hidden Kindred in Apocrypha|thumb|left]]
During that same time, the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] [[Lore:Torvesard|Torvesard]], former servant of Ithelia, approached Peryite and Vaermina, seeking their aid in uncovering the truth behind a recurring dream he had. The dream was a suppressed memory of the meeting before Hermaeus Mora, Vaermina, and Peryite, that was preserved due to the last act of Ithelia, who'd saved Torvesard at the last moment. Torvesard was told that Vaermina and Peryite had experienced the same dream, though in their case it was not quite as vivid. The two Princes, furious with Hermaeus Mora for altering their memories, formed an alliance with Torvesard and with each other, determined to enter [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] and uncover the forgotten truth.{{ref|name=Torvesard|[[ON:Torvesard|Torvesard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TorvesardJournal|{{Cite Book|Torvesard's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}

In order to uncover the truth Mora had hidden, Vaermina and Peryite had their followers form an alliance and form a new cult devoted to both Princes called the Hidden Kindred, which the high priest of Peryite, Blightcrown, assumed leadership of.{{ref|name=NewCult|{{Cite Book|A New Cult Arises|ns_base=Online}}}} The Hidden Kindred also formed an alliance with Telvanni Master [[Lore:Shelreni Baro|Shelreni Baro]].{{ref|name=Shelreni|[[ON:Master Shelreni|Master Shelreni]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Shelreni|[[ON:Master Shelreni|Master Shelreni]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Spirit|Events of [[ON:Spirit of Fate|Spirit of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-quest-A Calamity of Fate 02.jpg|The memory contained in the Glyphic|thumb|right]]
In order to proceed with their plans, the Hidden Kindred sought to retrieve a powerful [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] called the [[Lore:Fulcrum Obscura|Fulcrum Obscura]]. The relic, crafted long ago by Hermaeus Mora for [[Lore:Durdryn|Prior Durdryn]], had the power to uncover any hidden secret and was kept within the necropolis of [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]].{{ref|name=TPF|{{Cite Book|The Prior's Fulcrum}}}}{{ref|name=OTFO|{{Cite Book|On the Fulcrum Obscura}}}} Disguised as a prelate, Blightcrown infiltrated the city and had his Hidden Kindred place [[Lore:Censer of Corruption|Censers of Corruption]], plague spreading relics which contained "Peryite's blessed breath" throughout the chapel, hoping the afflicted [[Lore:Keepers of the Dead|Keepers of the Dead]] would reveal the relic's location. Investigating the spread of the illness that had gripped the Keepers, the Vestige found evidence of the Hidden Kindred's activities and plans on the ship they'd used to arrive, and tracked them to the catacombs with the aid of Curate Gadayn. In the catacombs, the Vestige encountered Blightcrown and Torvesard, who fled with one half of the Fulcrum Obscura after the artifact cracked, as it contained the power they needed even when broken.{{ref|name=KOTF|Events of [[Online:Keeper of the Fate|Keeper of the Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BO|{{Cite Book|Blightcrown's Orders|ns_base=ON}}}}

The Hidden Kindred carried out operations in multiple locations in service to their goals, including in the Alavelis [[Lore:Malachite|Glass]] Mine and in the Anchre [[Lore:Kwama|Kwama]] Egg Mine, where Zaedare the Afflicted infected the eggs with diseased ichor, in an attempt to destroy Necrom by spreading the infection to it when the eggs reached its markets.{{ref|name=Zaedare|[[ON:Zaedare the Afflicted|Zaedare the Afflicted]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=EggMine|[[ON:Tainted Egg Mine Report|Tainted Egg Mine Report]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Under|Events of [[Online:Underwritten|Underwritten]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Blightcrown betrayed Shelreni Baro and sacrificed her, so Vaermina could possess her body and enter Apocrypha through a special portal formed using a spell from the [[Lore:Black Books|Black Book]] The Tormenting Eye. Around {{Year|3E 405}}, Vaermina was summoned by an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]] in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region. The Prince gave the agent her [[Lore:Skull of Corruption|Skull of Corruption]] in exchange for killing a treacherous [[Lore:Lich|lich]].{{ref|name=DFVaernima|Events of [[Daggerfall:Vaernima's Quest|Vaernima's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

The [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] was afflicted with [[Lore:Vampirism|vampirism]] while exploring [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] in {{Year|3E 426}}, and sought out [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] for a cure. Curiously, though he is known for creating vampires{{ref|name=OLBTM|{{Cite Book|Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie}}}} and to whom [[Lore:Galur Rithari's Papers|contrite ones turn to]] for relief from their curse, the Prince of Pain claimed he had to retrieve the cure from Vaermina after some difficult "discussion".{{ref|name=MWCure|Events of [[Morrowind:A Cure for Vampirism|A Cure for Vampirism]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}}{{ref|name=MWMolag|[[Morrowind:Molag Bal|Molag Bal's dialogue]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Orb of Vaermina|Orb of Vaermina]] was stolen from the dreamworld and brought into reality by the wizard Arkved, who brought the Orb to his tower in the [[Lore:Nibenay Basin|Nibenay Basin]], and started using it to explore the realm of dreams at will. Vaermina wanted it back, and tasked the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] with retrieving it. Reality had become warped inside the tower due to the Orb's influence, transfiguring it into a place of waking nightmares, but the Hero braved the horrors and recovered the Orb. Vaermina rewarded the Hero with the Skull of Corruption, and promised Arkved would never again escape his nightmares.{{ref|name=OBVaermina|Events of [[Oblivion:Vaermina|Vaermina's quest]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-npc-Vaermina Statue.jpg|thumb|right|Vaermina's statue in Nightcaller Temple]]
In {{Year|4E 201}}, the city of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] was being plagued by continuous nightmares. While in town, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] met Erandur, a priest of [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] (and former priest of Vaermina) who explained the disturbances were originating from the nearby Nightcaller Temple. Years before, the temple had been invaded by a band of Orcs, and as a last resort Vaermina's priests released the Miasma, a gas which placed everyone within into a never-ending, ageless sleep. The pair entered the temple ruin and soon found the source of Dawnstar's nightmares—the [[Lore:Skull of Corruption|Skull of Corruption]], which had begun feeding off the memories of those nearby.{{ref|name=SRWaking|Events of [[Skyrim:Waking Nightmare|Waking Nightmare]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

The path to Vaermina's artifact was blocked by a magical barrier, but Erandur had an idea—the Dragonborn would enter the Dreamstride. The priest and the Dragonborn fought their way past awakening Orcs and cultists, and located a vial of Vaermina's Torpor. The Dragonborn imbibed the concoction, and entered a dreamlike memory of the temple under attack, from the point of view of the one who released the Miasma—Erandur. When the vision ended, the Dragonborn reappeared inside the barrier, disabling it.{{ref|name=SRWaking}}

The duo pressed on toward the Skull of Corruption, battling their way past Erandur's former friends. The priest then prepared to banish the artifact back to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], but Vaermina tried to intervene. The prince pleaded with the Dragonborn to kill Erandur before he could complete his ritual, and offered the Skull as a reward for this service. What choice the Last Dragonborn made remains unknown.{{ref|name=SRWaking}}
