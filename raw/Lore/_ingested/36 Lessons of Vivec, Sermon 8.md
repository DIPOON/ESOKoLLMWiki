And presently {{Lore Link|Nerevar}} and {{Lore Link|Vivec|MWlink=Morrowind:Vivec (god)}} were within sight of the {{Lore Link|Mournhold|capital}} and the [[Lore:House of Troubles|Four Corners of the House of Troubles]] knew that it was not time to contest them. The caravan musicians made a great song of entrance and the eleven gates of the Mourning Hold were thrown wide.

{{Lore Link|Almalexia|Ayem}} was accompanied by her husband-state, a flickering image that was channeled to her ever-changing female need. Around her were {{Lore Link|the Shouts}}, a guild now forgotten, who carried with them the whims of the people, for the {{Lore Link|Chimer|Velothi}} then were still mostly good at heart. The Shouts were the counselors of Ayem and the country, though they sometimes quarreled and needed {{Lore Link|Sotha Sil|Seht}} to wring them into usefulness. Ayem approached Nerevar, who was by now adorned in the flags of [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]]. He gifted her with the simulacrum of the netchiman's wife and the egg of Vivec inside.

Ayem said to Nereva{{nst|ON=r:<br>"|r, '}}Seht who is {{Lore Link|Azura}} has revealed that war is come and that the Hortator that shall deliver us will approach with a solution walking at his side.{{nst|ON="|'}}

This netchiman's wife you see at my side is a sword and a symbol and there is prophecy inside. It tells me that, like it, we must for {{nst|ON=a while|awhile}} be like he is and, as a people, cloaked in our former enemies, and to use their machines without shame.{{nst|ON="|'}}
