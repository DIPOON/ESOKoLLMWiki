The '''Bloodfall Kingdom'''{{ref|[[Castles:Rulings/GOB11|Goblin ruling]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} is a petty kingdom and a vassal of the Empire in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] ruled by the gro-Batul family. By [[Lore:Fourth Era|4E 180]], it had existed for at least three generations.{{ref|name=BFB|[[Blades:Bloodfall Banner|Bloodfall Banner]] item in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} The Kingdom was founded by [[Lore:Durak gro-Batul|Durak gro-Batul]], the first Bloodfall King.{{Ref|name=DA|[[Blades:Durak's Armor|Durak's Armor]] Item description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} King Durak was a very respected figure in the region.{{Ref|name=KS|[[Blades:King's Sepulcher|King's Sepulcher]] decoration description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} His son, Bazul gro-Batul, took over as regent in his stead when Durak went off to serve in the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]]. Bazul was not a popular ruler: executions for petty crimes became commonplace, and he was feared by many. When his father learned of this, he disinherited Bazul, and his granddaughter, [[Lore:Urzoga gra-Batul|Urzoga gra-Batul]], was named as his successor. Urzoga, who was considered a far better alternative to Bazul, became the Queen when her grandfather died, six months after the Great War ended.{{ref|name=JtE|[[Blades:Junius the Elder|Junius the Elder]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}<noinclude>

The Bloodfall Kingdom neighbors the kingdom of [[Lore:Odar|Odar]].{{Ref|name=CastlesRulings|[[Castles:Rulings|Rulings]] text in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}

==History==
[[File:BL-prerelease-Forest Ruins.png|A forest in Bloodfall|right|thumb]][[File:BL-decoration-King's Sepulcher.jpg|A statue dedicated to the first Bloodfall King|left|thumb]]
In the Fourth Era, a portion of Cyrodiil was under the direct control of the Bloodfall Kingdom.{{ref|name=BFB}} The town of [[Lore:Rivercrest|Rivercrest]]{{Ref|name=CastlesRulings}} fell under its jurisdiction. Queen Urzoga had to rely on mercenaries to defend the kingdom, as many of its actual soldiers died alongside her grandfather during the Great War.{{ref|name=TBQ|[[Blades:Urzoga gra-Batul|Urzoga gra-Batul]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} These mercenaries were hired to defend the Kingdom against bandits and other threats, and because of the expense, the Queen couldn't afford to have towns skipping out on paying their taxes.{{ref|name=TBQ}} These mercenaries would be sent to collect the taxes, and against the Blood Queen's wishes, razed the town and destroyed a statue of the town's founder. This unleashed a powerful lich whose goal was to destroy the entire world.{{ref|name=TBQ}} [[Lore:The Warrior (person)|The Warrior]] aided the Queen by protecting her kingdom from bandits, Goblins, and other threats.{{ref|name=EoBs|Events of ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}
[[File:BL-quest-The Impenetrable Grove.jpg|Graveyard in Bloodfall|right|thumb]][[File:BL-quest-The Big Hit.jpg|A town in Bloodfall|right|thumb]]

Uzorga was still ruling Bloodfall by {{Year|4E 201}}, when remnants of the Greencap Bandits, led by Uzorga's son Urzog, stole the Headman's Cleaver, a family heirloom of  great value to the kingdom.{{ref|name=Shogarz}}{{ref|[[Blades:Headman's Cleaver|Headman's Cleaver]] in {{g|Blades}}}} Urzog had plans to murder his younger brother Shogarz gro-Batul, who had been named the heir to the Bloodfall throne.{{ref|name=Urzog|{{Cite Book|Urzog's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Bloodfall Queen, angry at the theft, would send Shogarz, whom was also her strongest knight, to Skyrim to get revenge on the bandits.{{ref|name=Shogarz|{{Cite Book|Letter from Shogarz gro-Batul|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Shogarz had a desire to confront his older brother, who he had long suspected was the Darkwater Snake.{{ref|name=Shurgoz|{{Cite Book|Shogarz's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Shogarz would send out a letter to capable warriors in case he didn't make it. The [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] received the letter and went to Lost Knife Hideout, discovering that Shogarz was captured and killed. The Dragonborn successfully confronted the Greencap Bandits and Urzog, retrieving the Headman's Cleaver as a reward. With both of Urzoga's sons, Shogarz and Urzog, now dead, it is unknown what will become of Bloodfall, as the kingdom is left with no heirs.{{ref|name=BitW|Events of [[Skyrim:Blood in the Water|Blood in the Water]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notable Places==
[[File:BL-prerelease-Town.png|The town of Rivercrest|right|thumb]][[File:BL-place-Old Arena.jpg|The Old Arena|right|thumb]]

*'''Brold's Tower''' - An Ayleid tower used by the wizard Brold.
*'''[[Lore:Blackridge|Blackridge]]''' - A village that was abandoned in {{Year|4E 170}}. 
*'''Bloodfall Woods''' - A forest.
*'''Deadbrook Hollow''' - An Imperial fort. It was occupied by the Greencap Bandits in {{Year|4E 180}}, however, the Warrior cleared them out.
*'''Drachurst Keep''' - A haunted Imperial fort, located a day south of Rivercrest.
*'''Fort Cassynder''' - An Imperial fort named after [[Lore:Cassynder|Emperor Cassynder]].
*'''Gorrham''' - A small village on the kingdom's outskirts that was accidentally burned down during a troll attack.
*'''Highwinter Castle''' - An abandoned Imperial fort. 
*'''Hangman's Wood''' - A forest rumored to be haunted. 
*'''[[Lore:Rivercrest|Rivercrest]]''' - An Imperial town founded in the First Era over Ayleid ruins.
*'''Sharolg's Village''' - An Orc village led by the Chieftain Sharolg gro-Ugdub 
*'''The Old Arena''' - An arena outside the town of Rivercrest.
*'''Woodhaven Forest''' - A forest west of Rivercrest, known to be used by woodcutters of Rivercrest.{{ref|[[Blades:Varro Matius|Varro Matius]]' dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}
*'''Woodhope Keep''' - Imperial keep.
