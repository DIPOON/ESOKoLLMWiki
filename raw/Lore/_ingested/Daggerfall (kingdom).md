[[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] is one of the nine historical {{nbsl|Lore:Kingdoms of High Rock|Breton kingdoms}} in the province of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]],{{Ref|name=EWR|{{Cite Book|Etiquette With Rulers}}}}{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-High Rock towns.png|Map of High Rock – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} located in the southwest portion of the province, on the north half of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. Throughout much of history, Daggerfall was one of the largest kingdoms in High Rock, from the early First Era before the rise of its rival, [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]] and its incorporation into the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]],{{Ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/High Rock}}}} to the turn of the Third Era, where it was one of the province's five kingdoms.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/High Rock}}}} It is named after its capital city, [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]].{{Ref|name=AHOD}}<noinclude>

===Early History===
{{Quote Box|''North of the Highest bluffs, south of the moors, west of the hills, and east of the sea is called DAGGERFALL. 110 men, 93 women, 13 children under 8 years of age, 58 cows, 7 bulls, 63 chickens, 11 cocks, 38 hogs live here.''|Daggerfall's entry in the ''[[Lore:Book of Life|Book of Life]]''{{rp|933}}}}
While there is substantial archaeological evidence to suggest that [[Lore:Proto-Breton|humans]] have lived where Daggerfall city is now located, at least a thousand years before recorded history, the first time the name is used for a specific area was discerned around {{Year|1E 246}}, during the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]]{{Ref|name=AHOD}} helmed by High King [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage]] and the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|Nordic Empire]].{{Ref|name=PGE1}} One of the earliest mentions of the name in recorded history comes from a record in their census, the [[Lore:Book of Life|Book of Life]]. By tradition, historians perpetuate the myth that Daggerfall's name comes from the knife the first chieftain used to create the borders. The Third Era author of ''"[[Lore:A History of Daggerfall|A History of Daggerfall]]"'', Odiva Gallwood believes other myths have equal validity.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}

Daggerfall was one of many fortified coastal towns built by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] during their occupation and its people were able to thrive on the land and the sea as fisherfolk.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1}} However, when High King [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]] was killed in {{Year|1E 369}}, Skyrim was in disarray as the [[Lore:War of Succession|War of Succession]] pitted the holds against each other and severely weakened the Nordic Empire's hold beyond its borders.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=OATD|{{Cite Book|Olaf and the Dragon}}}} The story of how High Rock was able to liberate itself from Skyrim is one established in legend and it involved the mythical heroes and companions, King [[Lore:Edward|Edward]] of Daggerfall and King [[Lore:Moraelyn|Moraelyn]] of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]].{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}}

According to legend, Edward and Moraelyn utilized an ancient artifact known as the [[Lore:Horn of Summoning|Horn of Summoning]] to free High Rock and their spirits from the Nords. Most people believe that the Horn was buried with Edward in his tomb underneath Daggerfall City, but in actuality, it was laid elsewhere in Tamriel.{{Ref|name=TRB4}} The elves of [[Lore:Direnni|Clan Direnni]] played a significant part in High Rock's liberation and reigned over the region as the Direnni Hegemony.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} In the years since the Nords' expulsion, the Kingdom of Daggerfall had only grown stronger as it greatly expanded its military.{{Ref|name=AHOD}} At the time, it was ruled by chieftain-kings.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1WR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Wild Region}}}} When [[Lore:Aiden Direnni|Aiden Direnni]] invoked the [[Lore:Rights Charter|Rights Charter]], many Breton citizens were enlisted to fight the war against the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessians]]{{Ref|name=ALOSAS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}} and Daggerfall served as a minor base of operations for the elves.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}

The [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]], while it brought victory to the Direnni Hegemony, destitute them as they failed to stop the emergence of [[Lore:Greater Bretony|Greater Bretony]]'s nobility.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} As well as their modern-day [[Lore:Knights|knightly orders]] (chief among them included Daggerfall's Knights of the Dragon) who were able to push them back to their home on the [[Lore:Isle of Balfiera|Isle of Balfiera]].{{Ref|name=TKOOHR|{{Cite Book|The Knightly Orders of High Rock}}}} All of High Rock was free from elven control by {{Year|1E 500}}{{Ref|name=PGE1}} and as Daggerfall continued to grow, in {{Year|1E 609}}, it was able to establish itself as a predominant kingdom and power in southern High Rock after King [[Lore:Thagore|Thagore]] defeated [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]]'s army. It has held onto this title ever since.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}

===King Joile and the Siege of Orsinium===
[[File:ON-interior-Old Orsinium.jpg|thumb|right|The ruins of Orsinium]]
[[File:ON-npc-King Joile.jpg|thumb|left|King Joile of Daggerfall]]
For much of the tenth century in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Daggerfall was ruled by King [[Lore:Joile|Joile]], who witnessed the original [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]'s growth, the [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] raiders' attack on denizens of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]] and the blockade on Wayrest's trade lanes.{{Ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}{{Ref|name=ONAntiSHMap|[[Online:Stormhaven Antiquities|Antique Map of Stormhaven]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} In {{Year|1E 948}}, the king wrote a letter to [[Lore:Gaiden Shinji|Gaiden Shinji]], leader of the [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]] about his grievances to the situation.{{Ref|name=WJOTB}} Only when King [[Lore:Golkarr|Golkarr]] applauded these raiders did Joile decided to act and declare war on the Orcs.{{Ref|name=ONTrystan|[[Online:Lord Trystan|Lord Trystan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

The [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]], as it is remembered in history, was a thirty-year-long conflict in which the kingdoms of Daggerfall and [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]] united to destroy the original kingdom of Orcs.{{Ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Orsinium}}}} The Knights of the Dragon, at the time led by Lord Trystan, worked in tandem with the Order of Diagna and the orders grew to respect each other in the process. Much to their chagrin, however, King Joile enlisted [[Lore:Mercedene|Mercedene]], leader of the [[Lore:Winterborn|Winterborn]] mercenaries from [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]], and gave her power over Lord Trystan.{{Ref|name=ONTrystan}}

King Joile and Gaiden Shinji executed King Golkarr in Grudgement Hall, Orsinium.{{Ref|name=TGSOO|{{Cite Book|The Great Siege of Orsinium}}}} But despite the death of the Orcish king, the siege relented and [[Lore:Baloth Bloodtusk|Baloth Bloodtusk]] reinforced the city. The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] [[Lore:Gallant|gallant]] believed that the war had lasted longer than it otherwise should have and wanted King Joile to call it off. Lord Trystan proposed a duel between the Redguard and Baloth Bloodtusk to put an honorable end to the war. The King had other ideas in mind, however, and had General Mercedene kill both warriors in the midst of their duel. It became apparent that King Joile's reason for starting the war was not well-intention, but rather, it was a means to conquer and expand his kingdom. This act of betrayal resonated with Lord Trystan and the Knights of the Dragon, who withdrew from Joile's conquest.{{Ref|name=ONTrystan}}{{Ref|name=ONFathers|[[Online:The Ashes of Our Fathers|The Ashes of Our Fathers]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

Joile and the Daggerfall Army traveled south into [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] via the [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]] in {{Year|1E 973}}, but their invasion was short-lived as they were confronted by six [[Lore:Ansei|Maidens]] of the [[Lore:Spirit Sword|Spirit Sword]] named Cegila, Julia, Kati, [[Lore:Makela Leki|Makela Leki]], Patia, and Zell. In the end, only one of the six Maidens survived and Joile's army was defeated single-handedly by Makela Leki, who activated her [[Lore:Shehai|Shehai]]. Joile was decapitated by the maiden and his armies scattered.{{Ref|name=FTMSOML|{{Cite Book|From The Memory Stone of Makela Leki}}}} The Siege of Orsinium ended in {{Year|1E 980}} and although Daggerfall was successful in their assault, the Orcs' downfall gave rise to their most direct competitor, [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], which formally became the Kingdom of Wayrest in {{Year|1E 1100}}.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=WJOTB}}

===Daggerfall in the First and Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-King Donel Deleyn.jpg|thumb|right|King Donel Deleyn of Daggerfall]]
[[File:ON-load-Glenumbra.jpg|thumb|left|[[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]]' painting of Daggerfall's street (ca. Folbert's reign){{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}]]
After Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] incorporated High Rock in {{Year|1E 1029}},{{Ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} Daggerfall's capital was able to flourish as an important settlement but much like the rest of western Tamriel, it was devastated by the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]],{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1}} which originally broke out in {{Year|1E 2200}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} While the nobility of Daggerfall and other kingdoms traveled to Balfiera for refuge,{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|6}} generally speaking, the Iliac Bay's population decreased by half.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}

It saw a further decline with the [[Lore:War of Righteousness|War of Righteousness]] between {{Year|1E 2321}} and {{Year|1E 2331|2331}},{{Ref|name=AHOD}} the conflict that spelled the end of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] and came after High Rock left the Empire.{{Ref|name=SH4|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 4}}}} From a combination of the Alessians' inability to control the distant province, their disdain for the Bretons' "tainted" heritage, and their heavy taxation, Daggerfall and the other kingdoms withdrew.{{Ref|name=OTWOR|{{Cite Book|On the War of Righteousness}}}} In an unspecified event in the First Era, Daggerfall, Wayrest, and the Isle of Balfiera joined together as the [[Lore:League of Unrest|League of Unrest]] to fight against the Cyrodiils.{{Ref|name=RGMaiko|[[Redguard:Maiko K'Elmar|Maiko K'Elmar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

Starting from the early fourth century of the Second Era, the Kingdom of Daggerfall was ruled by [[Lore:House Deleyn|House Deleyn]],{{Ref|name=EGT}} who are descendants of the First Era hero, the Ivory Lord.{{Ref|name=TILAHB|{{Cite Book|The Ivory Lord: A Hero Born}}}} [[Lore:Donel Deleyn|Donel Deleyn]] started this dynasty in {{Year|2E 342}} and political marriages with nobles from [[Lore:Aldcroft|Aldcroft]] and [[Lore:Northmoor|Northmoor]] strengthened the house's power.{{Ref|name=EGT}} Daggerfall under King [[Lore:Maxevian|Maxevian]] participated in the siege against the Province of Orsinium mainly orchestrated by [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]].{{Ref|name=KMO|{{Cite Book|King Maxevian's Orders}}}}{{Ref|name=TFOKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}} By his order, a regiment of knights stayed behind in Watcher's Hold to prevent any Orcish incursion from coming west.{{Ref|name=KMO}}{{Ref|name=LTKM|{{Cite Book|Letter to King Maxevian}}}}

Sometime in the sixth century of the Second Era, the kingdoms of High Rock engaged in all-out warfare with each other in the [[Lore:Battle of Granden Tor|Battle of Granden Tor]] but in the years since experienced peace.{{Ref|name=TOAM3|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch, Ch. 3}}}} At the time, Daggerfall would have been ruled by King [[Lore:Bergamot (person)|Bergamot]],{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=SCCOTN|{{Cite Book|Shornhelm, Crown City of the North}}}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} who also led the Knights of the Dragon in the capital city's defense against Emperor [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] and his [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] horde.{{Ref|name=TOAM3}} Upon realizing how weak their own kingdoms were and as a means to collectively unite against any foreign threat, the kings of High Rock formed an alliance known as the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]]. Daggerfall was quick to propose their city as the capital but Wayrest's recent growth brought it to their favor. When the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] of {{Year|2E 560}} passed, it claimed King Bergamot and left the throne to [[Lore:Folbert the Wide|Folbert the Wide]].{{Ref|name=EGT}}

With this alliance, Folbert was present with the other kings in [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]]'s wedding in {{Year|2E 566}}{{Ref|name=TOAM6|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch, Ch. 6}}}} and later Daggerfall became instrumental in [[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]] not long after. Despite the alliance with Wayrest, however, King Folbert believed the city's rise in prominence was a stroke of luck and that if the kingdom overextended itself, Daggerfall would come in and rise back up top. This did not come up in his lifetime, however, as he passes away.{{Ref|name=EGT}} An [[Lore:Oracle|oracle]] of the [[Lore:Wyrd|Wyrd]] named Marieve was able to predict King Folbert's death{{Ref|name=ONVanus|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and by {{Year|2E 582}}, the throne was held by Casimir.{{Ref|name=ONCasimir|[[Online:King Casimir|King Casimir]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Although the kingdom remained affiliated with the Covenant, the King was adamant to make the Knights of the Dragon a separate entity from the Covenant's greater armies.{{Ref|name=ONCroix|[[Online:Chamberlain Croix|Chamberlain Croix]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} But when the [[Lore:Bloodthorn Cult|Bloodthorn Cult]] invaded Glenumbra, the knightly order assisted the Covenant and the [[Lore:Lion Guard|Lion Guard]] on their final attack in [[Lore:Cath Bedraud|Cath Bedraud]].{{Ref|name=ONMists|[[Online:Mists of Corruption|Mists of Corruption]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Daggerfall and the Third Empire===
[[File:LO-map-High Rock (PGE1).jpg|thumb|right|Map of High Rock in the 1st Edition Pocket Guide of the Empire]] 
The province of High Rock was fully incorporated into the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] after [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] easily corralled the squabbling kingdoms{{Ref|name=PGE3}} and employed the provisional governors to end the witch-kings' reign and feuds.{{Ref|name=RGKrisandra|[[Redguard:Krisandra|Krisandra]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} By the time the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]] was in effect across the continent, Daggerfall's capital city was considered the de-facto capital of the province, by virtue of its ancient history, prominence, and prosperity.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} In a map of other capitals in the Empire, Daggerfall is listed among them.{{Ref|name=MWMapPOT|[[:File:MW-book-Provinces of Tamriel.jpeg|Map of Tamriel – Provinces of Tamriel]]}} By Cyrodilic tradition, the Kingdom of Daggerfall was able to retain its right to have a royal court.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} When Sentinel was governed by [[Lore:Senecus Goddkey|Senecus Goddkey]], nobles from Daggerfall traveled to the capital city for an exotic retreat.{{Ref|name=PGE1HF|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}}

While it is unknown where Daggerfall sided in the war, western High Rock was entrenched in the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]]. The [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] split the Imperial armies between the western army in the Iliac Bay and the eastern army in coastal [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire, v 1}}}} In the summer of {{Year|3E 125}}, King [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus]] of [[Lore:Gilane (kingdom)|Gilane]] was able to reconquer western High Rock. Meanwhile, the death of his niece, [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]] around the same time sparked outrage toward [[Lore:Potema|Potema]] and [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel III]], which inspired more people to the Empire's cause.{{Ref|name=TWQ7|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v7}}}} 

The war ended when Uriel III was defeated by Cephorus I at the Battle of Ichidag in {{Year|3E 127}}. By then, the kingdoms of High Rock turned toward the Empire{{Ref|name=BHOTE2|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire, v 2}}}} and received new levels of autonomy and independence.{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} In early {{Year|3E 266}}, the throne of Daggerfall was contended between Helena and her cousin, Jilathe. It was because of this the Kingdom of Daggerfall could not rally other leaders in the Iliac Bay to fight the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]].{{Ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}}

===The History of the War of Betony===
[[File:DF-prerelease-Army.gif|thumb|right|The Battle of Cryngaine Field]]
[[File:DF-map-Betony.png|thumb|left|The Island of Betony]]
Even though the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] passed at the turn of the century, [[Lore:Third Era Wars#Fractious Wars|fractious wars]] continued to plague the continent. The Iliac Bay was the center of one such conflict, as it involved the Kingdoms of Daggerfall and Sentinel, and the small island of [[Lore:Betony|Betony]]. The island's local lord, [[Lore:Mogref|Mogref]] agreed to become a vassal to King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] and Queen [[Lore:Mynisera|Mynisera]] of Daggerfall for protection and monetary reasons.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|ns_base=BK}}}}{{Ref|name=TWOBN|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Newgate)}}}}

One person who pushed for the vassalage was the Archpriest of Kynareth, Vanech,{{Ref|name=TWOBN}} who believed Betony was the holy land of his patron. Complications arose when King [[Lore:Camaron|Camaron]] of Sentinel intervened by citing a two-hundred-year-old agreement. Despite the counsel of their own respective wise women, who foresaw their deaths in the conflict, both kings pressed forward with the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] in {{Year|3E 402}}.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}

The war began with a sea battle on the Daggerfall Bluffs,{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} aptly named the Battle of the Bluffs.{{Ref|name=TWOBN}} Daggerfall came out victorious thanks to Lord [[Lore:Bridwell|Bridwell]], general of the Daggerfall military.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}{{Ref|name=TWOBN}} In the next subsequent battles, the battle in Glenpoint's foothills and the Siege of [[Lore:Craghold|Craghold]], Daggerfall claimed victory. Over the course of the war, Lysandus' court sorceress, [[Lore:Medora Direnni|Medora Direnni]] and his mother, [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] became more adamant to end the war but as enthusiastic as Lysandus was to his victory at hand, Nulfaga left the Court of Daggerfall and retired to her [[Lore:Shedungent|castle]] in the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}{{Ref|name=TWOBN}}

Her departure made King Lysandus reconsider the war and attempted to negotiate with King Camaron in [[Lore:Reich Gradkeep|Reich Gradkeep]], with its lord, [[Lore:Graddock|Graddock]] serving as a neutral mediator. The summit took place in the city's palace and was initially civil. It was decided on the [[Lore:Treaty of Reich Gradkeep|Treaty of Reich Gradkeep]] that both kingdoms would have joint lieges of the island. This solution was never presented to the kings as Archpriest Vanech switched the treaty for another, one that would have caused offense to King Lysandus. According to legend, Lord Bridwell smashed the fake treaty with his battle axe, which created chaos in the palace. The region's capital city was in a bloodbath and both kingdoms continued their fight out in the wilderness.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}

====The Battle of Cryngaine Field and the aftermath====
[[File:DF-npc-Lord Bridwell (face).png|thumb|right|Lord Bridwell]]
The generals were able to quell their armies and Daggerfall returned to their camp for the final battle. The kingdom's armies made their base in [[Lore:Ravennian Forest|Ravennian Forest]] while Sentinel camped in [[Lore:Yeorth Burrowland|Yeorth Burrowland]], with [[Lore:Cryngaine Field|Cryngaine Field]] between them. While the armies were away, Queen Mynisera discovered her husband's affair with Medora Direnni and banished her from the court. When this news reached Lysandus, he decided to leave his life as King behind so that he could spend the rest of his life in anonymity with Medora on Balfiera. He made a deal with his son, Prince [[Lore:Gothryd|Gothryd]], and orchestrated a plan to fake his death. Lord [[Lore:Woodborne|Woodborne]] of Wayrest met with Lysandus in camp and escorted him to [[Lore:Tamarilyn Point|Tamarilyn Point]] while an imposter would take his place.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} 

A week later, the [[Lore:Battle of Cryngaine Field|Battle of Cryngaine Field]] commenced and in the heat of battle, an unnatural fog conjured by the dragon-familiar, [[Lore:Skakmat|Skakmat]] enveloped the battlefield. Gothryd killed the imposter, with the narrative being that Sentinel's archers fired blindly into the fog. Gothryd, who was popular among soldiers was crowned behind the battlefront and led the army for the rest of the battle.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} While texts on the war written by [[Lore:Fav'te|Fav'te]] and [[Lore:Vulper Newgate|Vulper Newgate]] claim that Gothryd killed King Camaron in a confrontation,{{Ref|name=TWOBN}}{{Ref|name=TWOBF|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Fav'te)}}}} it was Lord Bridwell who defeated him. The Sentinel army, led by Lord [[Lore:Oresme|Oresme]] surrendered and Betony was fully relinquished to the Kingdom of Daggerfall in {{Year|3E 403}}.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}

As part of the formal peace treaty, the newly-named King Gothryd proposed to the Princess of Sentinel, [[Lore:Aubk-i|Aubk-i]], and while some viewed it as a gesture toward peace and friendship, others like members of the Royal Court of Sentinel saw her as a political prisoner. Despite the measures taken for Lysandus' retirement, he was assassinated and in her grief, Medora Direnni tried to conjure his spirit. After many failed attempts and more powerful rituals, a host of [[Lore:Undead|undead]] was summoned and plagued the Isle of Balfiera.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} Coincidentally on the 13th of Rain's Hand, known in Daggerfall as the Day of the Dead, the [[Lore:Ghost|ghost]] of King Lysandus appeared in Daggerfall's capital and haunted its streets at night with his own host of ghosts in {{Year|3E 404}}.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}{{Ref|name=DFDead|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of the Dead|Day of the Dead]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} King Gothryd became distrusted among his citizens and rumors circulated about how he killed his father in the Battle of Cryngaine Field.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}

===The Miracle of Peace and onward===
[[File:DF-npc-Gothryd (face).png|thumb|left|King Gothryd]]
[[File:DF-npc-Lysandus Ghost.png|thumb|right|The Ghost of King Lysandus and his departure from the mortal plane]]
In {{Year|3E 405}}, an [[Lore:Agent|Agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] was sent to the Kingdom of Daggerfall by the Emperor, [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel VII]] to investigate King Lysandus' hauntings and to assist in a small personal manner related to the Dowager Queen, Mynisera.{{Ref|name=DFIntro|[[Daggerfall:Introduction|Introduction]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} However, a supernatural storm{{Ref|name=DFPrivateer|[[Daggerfall:Privateer's Hold|Privateer's Hold]] story quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} created by Nulfaga{{Ref|name=DFStorm|[[Daggerfall:Background Information#Reward|Employer]]'s dialogue during the "[[Daggerfall:Background Information|Background Information]]" quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} caused their ship to wreck off Daggerfall's coast and washed them ashore in [[Lore:Privateer's Hold|Privateer's Hold]], a dungeon near the Daggerfall-Tulune border. The Agent was able to escape the dungeon and embark across the Iliac Bay, where they became involved in the politics and affairs of the region.{{Ref|name=DFPrivateer}}

After the Blades had besieged [[Lore:Woodborne Hall|Woodborne Hall]] in Wayrest, Lady [[Lore:Brisienna Magnessen|Brisienna Magnessen]] made contact with the Agent and gave them the task of retrieving the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]] from [[Lore:Castle Daggerfall|Castle Daggerfall]], believing that King Gothryd was given the totem by Lord [[Lore:Woodborne|Woodborne]] before the siege of his home. After the Agent took the totem, various people involved in the Iliac Bay, from the kingdoms' monarchy to the Empire made contact with the Agent in the hopes of obtaining the totem and its godly power. Gothryd was one of these individuals, however, instead of a cordial letter, the king sent his knights to take it by force but it is unknown if the Agent complied with them.{{Ref|name=DFTotem|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Fets the Totem?]] story quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

In fact, what became of the Agent and the Totem is unknown to history as the Blades lost contact with them since their last encounter with Lady Brisienna. In {{Year|3E 417}}, a magical phenomenon known as the [[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]] occurred over the Iliac Bay and changed it ever since. At the time, the kingdoms warred against each other and laid waste to the region. A three-way clash between Daggerfall, Wayrest, and [[Lore:Nova Orsinium|Nova Orsinium]] took place on the 9th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]], and the land between them was destroyed as a fire started in [[Lore:Daenia (duchy)|Daenia]] and [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]], and spread northeast toward [[Lore:Ykalon|Ykalon]] and [[Lore:Phrygias|Phrygias]].{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

The Miracle of Peace occurred on the 10th of Frostfall and once it passed, it was suddenly the 11th of Frostfall. The forty-four nations in the Iliac Bay became four Imperial counties; the previous kingdoms, which had expanded and encroached on these smaller countries. Daggerfall took over much of the western Bretonic-Iliac Bay region, from as far as Ykalon and western Anticlere and now shared its border with Wayrest. Peace had been achieved, and not just across the Iliac Bay{{Ref|name=TWITW}} but the entire province. High Rock encompassed five major kingdoms{{Ref|name=PGE3}} and the newly formal province of Orsinium.{{Ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Orsinium}}}} Daggerfall in the late years of the Third Era only prospered as King Gothryd and Queen Aubk-i had their first child, [[Lore:Camaron (prince)|Camaron]]. At the turn of the era, they celebrated his marriage with Lady Kelmena of [[Lore:Camlorn (kingdom)|Camlorn]], which suggested a possible alliance with the kingdom.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:SR-icon-construction-Shrine of Kynareth.png|thumb|right|A Shrine of Kynareth]]
[[File:DF-place-Knightly Order.png|thumb|left|A Knights of the Dragon garrison]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Breton#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Breton#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Breton page.''
Although the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] are unsentimental to their past, Daggerfall is of considerable antiquity{{Ref|name=PGE1}} and its people are obsessed with its past. The kingdom keeps an ancient record known as the Annals of Daggerfall, and with it has documented and preserved many things, including King Joile's original letter to Gaiden Shinji from 1E 948.{{Ref|name=WJOTB}}

The Bretons of Daggerfall celebrate holidays annually, some of which are recognized across the continent or province. But some are only observed in the kingdom, such as [[Lore:Dancing Day|Dancing Day]] on the 23rd of [[Lore:Midyear|Midyear]]. Everyone from the nobility to the lower class celebrates this day with merriment and festivities. It is unknown who started this tradition but it was popularized by the Red Prince, Atryck in the Second Era.{{Ref|name=DFDancing|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Dancing Day|Dancing Day]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} 

The [[Lore:Day of the Dead|Day of the Dead]], held on the 13th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]], however, has a darker tone as the people believe in a superstition that the [[Lore:Death|dead]] would rise on this day and wreak havoc across the kingdom.{{Ref|name=DFDead}} The holiday harkens back to Bretonic religion in antiquity and despite its modern meaning, suggests [[Lore:Ancestor Worship|ancestral worship]], which defined the religion at the time.{{Ref|name=HOTIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}} Interestingly, this was the day that King Lysandus returned from the grave after his death in the War of Betony.{{Ref|name=DFDead}} As a country within the Glenumbra region, the people of Daggerfall once observed the [[Lore:Day of Release|Day of Release]] on the 8th of [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]]. To remember the Battle of Glenumbra Moors, a battle where Aiden Direnni was able to banish the Alessian Order from High Rock. By the Interregnum, this holiday was recognized across Glenumbra{{Ref|name=ONRoisterer|[[Online:Day of Release Roisterer|Day of Release Roisterer]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but by the late Third Era, only the Barony of [[Lore:Glenumbra Moors (barony)|Glenumbra Moors]] remembers this battle and continues the holiday tradition.{{Ref|name=DFRelease|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Release|Day of Release]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Of all the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]], the Goddess of the Winds and the Seas,{{Ref|name=ONMenoit|[[Online:Light-Keeper Menoit|Light-Keeper Menoit]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]] is held in the highest regard across the Kingdom of Daggerfall. Not only is her temple, the [[Lore:Temple of Kynareth|Temple of Kynareth]] prevalent across the region, from Gothway Garden to Penbrugh and Singmoth,{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall (Region)|Daggerfall]] region in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} she is recognized as the goddess-protector of the region. Even the island under their protection in the late Third Era, Betony was holy land associated with Kynareth.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}} The kingdom's heraldry features a four-legged [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]],{{Ref|name=DFBanner|[[:File:DF-banner-Daggerfall.png|Banner of Daggerfall – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}} a mythical creature that went nigh extinct after the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] of the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{Ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} Daggerfall's association with the creature can be seen with its local [[Lore:Knights|knightly order]], the [[Lore:Knights of the Dragon|Knights of the Dragon]]. Even the sovereign of Daggerfall refers to themselves as the Dragon.{{Ref|name=KOTD|{{Cite Book|Knights of the Dragon (book)}}}}{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfallEnding|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius#Daggerfall: Gothryd|Daggerfall]] ending description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} A piece of treasure in Daggerfall is the Daggerfall Champion's Chestplate, a heavy armor cuirass.{{Ref|name=ONChestplate|{{Item Link|Daggerfall Champion's Breastplate of the Wyrd Tree|summary=1|quality=2|id=29947}} in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONLanis|[[Online:Sir Lanis Shaldon#Angof the Gravesinger|Sir Lanis Shaldon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Beyond the cities' walls are a variety of other races and subcultures that live in the kingdom. [[Lore:Barbarians|Barbarians]] live in keeps like the Hold of Copperham and Ashfield Tower and many [[Lore:Giant|Giant]] strongholds are scattered throughout the region, such as Druigod, Tricergon, and Yagrugoth.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall (Region)/Dungeons|Daggerfall]] dungeons in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]] also live in the region and beyond just the cliffs outside of the city, the Shrieking Scar,{{Ref|name=ONScar|[[Online:Shrieking Scar|Shrieking Scar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} there are harpy nests like the Aviary of Morarock and the Nest of Vojgon.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}} The Sisters of the Bluff is a [[Lore:Witches|witches coven]] that operates in the region, specifically in the Daggerfall Bluffs and south of the Burning Martyr of Kynareth.{{Ref|name=DFCoven|[[Daggerfall:Witch Covens#The Sisters of The Bluff|The Sisters of The Bluff]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The [[Lore:Vraseth|Vraseth]] clan of [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] is the dominant bloodline in the region,{{Ref|name=DFVraseth|[[Daggerfall:Vraseth|Vraseth]] faction in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} with [[Lore:Vraseth (person)|ancestry]] that leads directly to the original vampire, [[Lore:Lamae Beolfag|Lamae Beolfag]].{{Ref|name=ONVraseth|[[Online:Vraseth|Vraseth]] NPC in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} There are many different vampire haunts across the region, such as the Haunt of Magnoghan and Castle Vladimyth.{{Ref|name=DFDungeons}}

==Geography==
[[File:DF-place-Castle Necromoghan.png|thumb|right|Castle Necromoghan]]
Daggerfall is a coastal region on the western edge of High Rock, right on the Iliac Bay and the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]].{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall}} Geographically speaking, the kingdom's capital city is located on the tip of [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]], more specifically the [[Lore:Daenia|Daenia]] region,{{Ref|name=ONDaggerfall|[[Online:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} not to be confused with the Third Era duchy of the same name. Much of the coast is known as the Daggerfall Bluffs{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}{{Ref|name=DFCoven}} but on the south-central part of the coastline are two unnamed peninsulas. Between these peninsulas is a bay and the biggest city within this area is the capital of Daggerfall itself.{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall}}

Many cities dot the region, from the western and southern coast, to the northern and eastern borders. These included Aldingwall, Baelmoth, Burgwall, Charing, Charening, Charton, Chesterwark, Cromwych Hill, Deerbridge Hollow, Gallotale, Grimfort, Holham, Ipshead, Kirkcart Wood, Knightstown, Longtown, Longtry Field, Mercester, Midbury, Newcester, Oxway, Penbrugh, Reyford, Ripmarket, Singcroft, Singmoth, Stokworth, Tunbeth Hamlet, Uptower Moor, Westhead Moor, Whitewood Borough, and Wildertown.{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall}}

In {{Year|3E 405}}, the Kingdom of Daggerfall was neighbored by several other regions, including [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]] to the north, [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]] to the north-northeast, [[Lore:Shalgora|Shalgora]] to the east, and [[Lore:Tulune|Tulune]] to the northwest.{{Ref|name=DFMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Labeled Map of the Iliac Bay – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}} The border with Tulune is defined by an inlet.{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall}} After the Miracle of Peace settled and redefined the Iliac Bay, Daggerfall's borders expanded and absorbed its neighbors, to the point it now bordered the newly-expanded Kingdom of Wayrest. The kingdom's new border went as far north as Ykalon and as far east as western Anticlere.{{Ref|name=TWITW}}

==Known Rulers==
[[File:DF-banner-Daggerfall.png|thumb|right|Daggerfall's banner, displaying its crest]]
The Kingship of Daggerfall is sometimes known as '''the Dragon of Daggerfall''',{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfallEnding}}{{Ref|name=DFCinematic|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall CES Cinematic|ns_base=GEN}}}} or simply '''the Dragon'''.{{Ref|name=KOTD}} Although the various dynasties go by their own names, such as the Deleyn Dynasty,{{Ref|name=EGT}} the current family that rules the kingdom are collectively known as the '''House of Daggerfall'''.{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|15}}

In the kingdom's early years, it was ruled by chieftain-kings.{{Ref|name=AHOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} In service to the monarch is the aptly named '''Court of Daggerfall''',{{Ref|name=DFDaggerfall}} also called the '''High Court of Daggerfall'''.{{Ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}} A tradition of the Iliac Bay is that the Archpriest of the region's main temple serves the court and in this case, the Archpriest of Kynareth works under the King.{{Ref|name=DFNarrative}}{{Ref|name=DFChil'a|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Chil'a|Chil'a]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Unlike most other nations, where there can only be one Dowager Queen, the Kingdom of Daggerfall is an exception to this rule as both Nulfaga, wife of King Arslan II, and Mynisera, wife of King Lysandus held this title at the same time.{{Ref|name=EWR}}

Early in the city's history, the kings of Daggerfall lived in a fortress now known as Daggerfall Overlook, which is located southeast of the city.{{Ref|name=ONLoadOverlook|[[Online:Daggerfall Overlook|Daggerfall Overlook]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Now they live in [[Lore:Castle Daggerfall|Castle Daggerfall]], which is found in the highest point of the city and overlooks the surrounding area and sea.{{Ref|name=ONLoadCastle|[[Online:Daggerfall Castle|Daggerfall Castle]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
