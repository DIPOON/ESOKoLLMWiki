</noinclude>[[Lore:Ghostgate|Ghostgate]] was the main gateway through the ancient [[Lore:Ghostfence|Ghostfence]] artifact to the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] region, which prevented monsters affected by [[Lore:The Blight|The Blight]] from leaving. Ghostgate housed a large [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] and contingents of both Buoyant Armigers and Ordinators. The two towers of Ghostgate, called Dusk (west) and Dawn (east), were constructed alongside the Ghostfence after many failed campaigns to defeat [[Lore:Dagoth Ur (god)|Dagoth Ur]] following his {{Year|2E 882}} reawakening.{{ref|name=Vivec|[[Morrowind:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>

There was a route off the [[Lore:Foyada Mamaea|foyada]] leading up to the Ghostfence that would have taken one to [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]], which rose up to the Ghostfence briefly, so you could have peered through at the quaint, curious and homicidal inhabitants of the Red Mountain region, before dropping south and skirting the [[Lore:Daedric Shrines|Daedric shrine]] of [[Lore:Ramimilk|Ramimilk]] as it turned west again. Alternatively, one could have simply backtracked down the Foyada Mamaea until they reached the turnoff to [[Lore:Buckmoth Legion Fort|Buckmoth Legion Fort]] and [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]].

According to an annal of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] led a siege on Ghostgate after the destruction of Ald'ruhn.{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Fall of Ald'ruhn|ns_base=GEN}}}} The Dunmer's prayers to [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] and the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] went unanswered, but the outcome of the siege was never revealed.
