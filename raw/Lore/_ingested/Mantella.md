# Lore:Mantella

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}[[File:DF-quest-Journey to Aetherius 08.jpg|thumb|right|The Mantella]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]] was a massive green gem, a "Crux of Transcendence"{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} that contained the "heart", or life force, of the entity known to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] as the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Twice, the Mantella was used to power the gigantic brass golem, [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]]—first by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] when he conquered Tamriel, and again during the events of the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

The Mantella was lost after Tiber Septim's conquests. For most of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the Mantella was hidden on a plane of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] known as the [[Lore:Mantellan Crux|Mantellan Crux]]. It was later located by [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] the sorceress and recovered by an [[Lore:Hero#The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], which led directly to the Miracle of Peace.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=TWITW}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Second Era===
{{Quote Box|''The Mantella was infused with the life [f]orce of Tiber Septim's Imperial Battlemage, and with it, Septim crushed all who stood in his way.''|[[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed|A letter from a friend]]}}
Under the terms of the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] that brought [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] into the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Emperor [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] received the ancient [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] construct, [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]]. The Mantella was created by Imperial Battlemage [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]] as a replacement for Numidium's original power source, the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=TAH}}{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}}{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[Morrowind:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The gem needed to be infused with the life force of a powerful being, so Arctus used his own{{ref|name=DFMantella|[[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed|The Mantella Revealed]], Letter 2}} (though [[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|one source]] claims he trapped the heart of [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth the Ash-King]], another saying it cost Tiber Septim his own heart to create it;{{Ref|name=D4C|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|205}} it has been suggested that both men may be [[Lore:Shezarrine|mortal avatars]] of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]).{{ref|name=TAH}} While the Underking's heart within the Mantella was "not exactly Lorkhan['s]",{{ref|name=TAH}} it was sufficient to awaken Numidium, and with it Tiber Septim was able to conquer all of Tamriel.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

Numidium was destroyed not long after; some say Zurin Arctus became infuriated that the Emperor had begun using Numidium to crush the neutral royal families of Tamriel and enthrone those loyal to him. The Battlemage attempted to reclaim his heart but was killed in the attempt, and as a result he was transformed into the undead [[Lore:Underking|Underking]].{{ref|name=DFMantella}} In other accounts, it was an already-undead Underking who attacked Numidium.{{ref|name=TAH}} Each account ends the same; Numidium was obliterated and its pieces scattered across Tamriel, and the Mantella was blown out of reality and into [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], where it came to rest in a plane that would come to be known as the [[Lore:Mantellan Crux|Mantellan Crux]].{{ref|name=TAH}}{{ref|name=DFMantella}}{{ref|name=DFNulfaga|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Nulfaga's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

In the years that followed, Tiber Septim sent out agents to recover the scattered pieces of Numidium, and they would become known as the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]]. Likewise, the Underking sent his forces to recover the Mantella.{{ref|name=DFMantella}}

===Third Era===
[[File:DF-quest-Journey to Aetherius.png|thumb|right|The Mantellan Crux]]
In {{Year|3E 401}}, the Mantella's location was divined by the sorceress [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]], the Dowager Queen of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], in her astral travels. However, she went mad in {{Year|3E 403}} following the death of her son, King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]]. Nulfaga retreated with the secret of the Mantellan Crux to [[Lore:Shedungent|Shedungent]], her castle in the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{Ref|name=D4C}}

In {{Year|3E 405}}, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] sent an [[Lore:Hero#The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] to investigate Lysandus' death, and why the King's spirit was haunting the city of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]. The agent discovered the king had been murdered and brought down the culprit, and also recovered the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]]—another artifact from Numidium, which allowed the bearer to control the automaton. In choosing a bearer for the Totem, The agent [[Lore:Dragon Break|somehow]] gave it to seven different powers around the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}  

Nulfaga, clear-headed with her son's shade at rest, then sent the agent to the Mantellan Crux to recover the gem.{{ref|name=DFJourney|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Journey to Aetherius]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Upon the Mantella's return to Tamriel, Numidium sprang to life once more; seven separate realities unfolded simultaneously across portions of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] in {{Year|3E 417}}, an event known as the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=TWITW}}{{ref|name=DFJourney}} In the end, the Underking was able to finally reclaim his heart from the Mantella, resulting again in the destruction of Numidium and giving him the death he had sought for so long.{{ref|name=DFEnd|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius#The End|''Daggerfall'' ending cinematics]]}}
