[[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] ([[Lore:Ta'agra|Ta'agra]]: ''Ne Quin-al''),{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Sugar and Blood}}}} also known as '''Anaquina''',{{Ref|name=MoE|[[:File:LO-map-Elsweyr (PGE1).jpg|Map of Elsweyr from the Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st ed]]}} '''Ana'quinal''',{{ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} or '''Northern Elsweyr''',{{Ref|name=ESOLoadingNE|[[Online:Northern Elsweyr|Northern Elsweyr]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} is a historical region and former [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] kingdom in southern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. It is one of the two traditional regions of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], along with its southern neighbor, [[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]]. Anequina traces its history back to [[Lore:Anequina Sharp-Tongue|Anequina Sharp-Tongue]], who served as the queen of the Ne Quin-al Prides in the time of the sixteen kingdoms.{{Ref|name=KaTD|{{Cite Book|Khunzar-ri and the Demons}}}} It emerged as one of the largest kingdoms in the province following the [[Lore:Anequine Conquests|Anequine Conquests]]. It merged with the rival kingdom of [[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]] in {{Year|2E 309}} to form the [[Lore:Elsweyr Confederacy|Elsweyr Confederacy]], uniting the region for the first time.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Anequina occupies the northern half of Elsweyr,{{Ref|name=MoE}}{{ref|name=ONMap|[[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online]] [[Online:Maps|maps]]}} a region of harsh badlands and arid plains.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}<noinclude>

==History==
Sometime within the [[Lore:Merethic_Era|Merethic Era]], Elsweyr was known to consist of sixteen independent realms that cooperated with each other for their mutual protection and economic benefit. Ne Quin-al was famed for its great warriors and [[Lore:Temple of Two-Moons Dance|Temple of Two-Moons Dance]]. Traditionally, the city-states of Elsweyr shared power based on the lunar phases of [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]], and Ne Quin-al's influence was said to be at its height when both moons were full.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} By {{Year|1E 461}}, Ne Quin-al was ruled by the legendary [[Lore:Darloc Brae|Darloc Brae]], the Golden Beast of Anequina.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}} Darloc Brae's reign was considered glorious by the Khajiit, as he led the [[Lore:Wars#Anequine Conquests|Anequine Conquests]] and expanded his kingdom's territory to its greatest extent, from as far west as [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] to as far east as [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]].{{ref|name=ESO-Load|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading screens]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The devastating [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] of {{Year|1E 2260}}{{ref|name=EPTE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}} proved particularly disruptive to Elsweyr's delicate political balance, and when it subsided the original sixteen realms had consolidated into two survivors: [[Lore:Pellitine|Pa'alatiin]] and Ne Quin-al, or Pellitine and Anequina as they were known in Cyrodilic.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} The two kingdoms fell into a bitter, often violent feud that stalemated for centuries,{{Ref|name=PGE1}} even as both kingdoms were incorporated into the Empire of [[Lore:Reman Cyrodiil|Reman Cyrodiil]];{{Ref|name=ALHOFR}} Anequina had developed a disciplined warrior culture, but Pellitine had wealth enough to hire mercenaries.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} The feud finally ended in {{Year|2E 309}}, as the kingdoms' rulers, [[Lore:Keirgo|Keirgo]] of Anequina and [[Lore:Eshita|Eshita]] of Pellitine, married and united their realms into the Elsweyr Confederacy. This move was initially deeply unpopular among the tribes of Anequina, who felt that their nobility had betrayed them, and they rebelled and besieged Anequina's cities. After the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] ignored Keirgo's pleas for aid, [[Lore:Mane|Mane]] [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]] quelled the unrest by instituting another moon-based power-sharing system between the tribes and the nobility.{{Ref|name=PGE1}}{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

Many years later amidst the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] in {{Year|2E 576}}, the city of Rimmen, the capital of Anequina, was ruled by King [[Lore:Hemakar|Hemakar]] and Queen [[Lore:Numara|Numara]]. As Emperor [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] was occupied in Cyrodiil, [[Lore:Euraxia Tharn|Euraxia Tharn]] was sent on Imperial business to Rimmen, where the King welcomed her kindly. This proved to be the King's greatest mistake. Using her militia of [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] mercenaries, Euraxia Tharn conquered significant parts of the Northern Elsweyr and executed the royal family. She was known as a tyrant across Anequina, from Rimmen to Riverhold. As time went on, Queen Euraxia imposed heavy and costly taxes such as the fur tax,{{Ref|name=ESOAbnur|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} built siege weapons meant to destroy Rimmen should the people rebel, and even work with a cult of necromancers led by {{Lore Link|Zumog Phoom}}.{{Ref|name=TFC|{{Cite Book|The Frostfall Coup}}}} Euraxia's forces spanned across Northern Elsweyr, eventually taking Riverhold in the western hills. In {{Year|2E 582}}, the Speaker of the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]], {{Lore Link|Gharesh-ri}} joined the {{Lore Link|Northern Elsweyr Defense Force}} in their battle against the Usurper Queen of Rimmen. Because of the ongoing [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] were pre-occupied in Cyrodiil, and so the Khajiit were on their own in the civil war. The Defense Force, under the command of Gharesh-ri and his agent, [[Lore:Khamira|Khamira]] retook Riverhold and established their base there, but that was before their situation worsened.{{Ref|name=ESOGharesh|[[Online:Lord Gharesh-ri|Lord Gharesh-ri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Ancient [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]] returned to wreak havoc across Elsweyr when [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]], the Overlord of Nibenay, and the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] released these beasts from their imprisonment in the [[Lore:Halls of Colossus|Halls of Colossus]].{{Ref|name=ESOColossus|[[Online:The Halls of Colossus|The Halls of Colossus]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Euraxia Tharn joined forces with the Dragons, not knowing that she was merely their puppet and giving her the false idea that she was above them.{{Ref|name=ESOMulaamnir|[[Online:Mulaamnir|Mulaamnir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Abnur Tharn and the Vestige attended a parlay with Euraxia Tharn at the Rimmen Palace, only to be rejected and inciting another assault onto Riverhold.{{Ref|name=ESOUsurper|[[Online:The Usurper Queen|The Usurper Queen]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} But after their failed attack, the Vestige, Abnur Tharn, and Khamira infiltrated the Rimmen Palace where they burned down their siege weapons and assassinated Queen Euraxia Tharn. The throne was empty and Hemakar's last remaining heir, Khamira proclaimed herself the Queen of Anequina.{{Ref|name=ESOQueens|[[Online:Two Queens|Two Queens]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} From the capital of Anequina, Queen Khamira commanded the Defense Force to end Zumog Phoom's attempt to revive {{Lore Link|Cadwell the Betrayer}} and the Dragon's plot to harvest the lunar energy of [[Lore:Jode's Core|Jode's Core]].{{Ref|name=TFO|Events of [[ON:The Final Order|The Final Order]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{Ref|name=CTB|Events of [[ON:Cadwell the Betrayer (quest)|Cadwell the Betrayer]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{Ref|name=JC|Events of [[ON:Jode's Core (quest)|Jode's Core]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} As of then Rimmen and the Kingdom of Anequina were parts of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]] and referred to as Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]]'s domain.{{ref|name=Khamira|[[ON:Khamira|Khamira]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{ref|name=Ayrenn|[[ON:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}

In the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Anequina lent its name to the "Heart of Anequina", a pivotal battle of the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]] between Elsweyr and [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] in which the Khajiit triumphed. The Khajiit artist Cherim, a veteran of the conflict, created an elaborate tapestry that depicted the battle.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Cherim's Heart of Anequina}}}}

In {{Year|4E 115}}, the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] backed a coup that dissolved the Elsweyr Confederacy. In its place, the kingdoms of Anequina and Pelletine were reestablished and made client states of the third [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

==Geography==
A huge portion of Anequina's landscape is dominated by various canyons, with the biggest being known as the Scar. The Scar stretches in the kingdom's northeast area starting from the southwest of Rimmen and ends near the border of Valenwood's [[Lore:Grahtwood|Grahtwood]]. The Scar consists of various subsections, and starting from the east is Scar's Edge, a name clearly used to indicate the edge of the canyon. To the northeast is the Ashen Scar, home to a towering shrine to [[Lore:Azura|Azura]]. Southeast, there is a notable fork with the canyon winding around the city of [[Lore:Orcrest|Orcrest]], leading to the subsection known as the weeping scar, a death trap that is home to the [[Lore:Vampire|Vampire]] clan, the [[Lore:Tenarr Zalviit|Tenarr Zalviit]]. Back where the canyon forks around Orcrest, the path southeast passes through the canyon city known as the Stitches, a haven for the unlawful. Scab Ridge is the canyon subsection in the southeast area of Anequina, near the city of {{Lore Link|Cicatrice}}. Finally, the winding canyon ends at the area called Scar's end, near the border of Valenwood.
