==Mythic Origin==
Many variations of the origin of Malacath exist, with the truth ultimately being unclear. The Prince is commonly believed to have been created when [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] ate the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] ancestor spirit, [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]],{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=THOT}}{{ref|name=TCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}} while Trinimac's most devout Elven followers are said to have been transformed into the [[Lore:Orc|Orsimer]] ("Pariah Folk" in [[Lore:Ehlnofex Languages|Aldmeris]]), or Orcs, in that same event.{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=TFOT|{{Cite Book|The Fall of Trinimac}}}} However some Orcs believe that Trinimac still exists and Malacath is a separate entity.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} While Malacath himself has said that this tale is far too "literal minded".{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls}}}} In addition, the [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]] information broker of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]], Madam Whim, has claimed that no living being can truly claim to understand how the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] and the Daedric Princes relate to one another, and that no information she's ever come across in her long life indicates stories of the Aedra to be more than stories. According to Whim, much like a "backwater village bully who falls in with a bandit gang", a Daedric Prince might go out of their way to avoid questions or quash rumors that claim they were once "an all-powerful Aedra". Whim finishes her comment by noting the "peculiar and particular cruelty" with which the written histories of Nirn treat the Orsimer, and asking, which is more likely, that an all-powerful Aedra found itself consumed by a Daedric Prince and recast in the mold of a pale shadow of itself,  or that mortal scholars with a bias eagerly leapt on the metaphor that the Daedric Prince most associated with the Orc people was quite literally the excreted remains of a once-great god?{{Ref|name=Maelstrom|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}}

According to some sources the popular origin of Malacath is more of a traditional belief than anything else and, whatever his origin, there are none alive who could say what the truth is for certain. It is also said that, whatever his origin, Malacath is a Prince of Oblivion and the equal of Azura, Boethiah, Sheogorath, [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] and all the others. And that, while it is possible Malacath came into existence in a different way than the rest, nowdays he is powerful and evoked whenever someone has felt betrayed, which in Tamriel is quite often.{{ref|name=TPI|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson Interview|Ted Peterson Interview]]}}

===The Devouring of Trinimac===
According to popular legend, Malacath was once [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], the most powerful of the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] ancestor spirits,{{ref|name=TTNOO}} the "greatest knight of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]]".{{ref|name=LOS}} During the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] began appearing in visions to the prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]], instructing him to create a new sect, who would eventually become the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]. This was blasphemous to the priests of Trinimac, who threatened to exile the dissidents.{{ref|name=TFOT}}

The more popular versions of the tale says that Boethiah then ate Trinimac.{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=TCO}}{{ref|name=TFOT}} One version in particular says Boethiah appeared as a beautiful lass, and asked Trinimac for a kiss. When he obliged, Boethiah swallowed him whole.{{ref|name=TILLOS|{{TIL|''Lord of Souls'' Lore Notes|lord-souls-lore-notes}}, The Imperial Library}} As the priests prepared to pass judgment on the Velothi dissidents, Boethiah appeared before them in Trinimac's form.{{ref|name=TFOT}} "Trinimac" burped, farted, and spoke foolishly.{{ref|name=TILLOS}} And when the crowd grew large enough, Boethiah told them what had been whispered to Veloth and demonstrated the "lies" of Trinimac's teachings, all in Trinimac's voice.{{ref|name=TCO}}{{ref|name=TFOT}} When the priests were sufficiently shamed and broken, Boethiah "squeezed out a great pile of dung" before the crowd, all that remained of the once proud knight, as proof that this was the truth.{{ref|name=TCO}}{{ref|name=TFOT}}{{ref|name=TILLOS}} Some accounts state the followers of both Boethiah and Trinimac are said to have rubbed the dung upon themselves and "changed their skins";{{ref|name=TA}} others claim that the dung pile "slunk away in shame".{{ref|name=TILLOS}}

These tales say that after being swallowed by Boethiah, Trinimac was tortured within her bowels, twisting and transforming him.{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=TFOT}} After being released, Boethiah banished Trinimac to what would become his realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], the Ashpit. There, Trinimac faded away and Malacath, an enraged being bent on revenge, was born.{{ref|name=TFOT}} And with Trinimac's transformation, so changed his devoted followers; thus were born the Orsimer, the "Pariah Folk", who follow Malacath to this day.{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=TFOT}}{{ref|name=TILLOS}}

===Orcish Origin Myths===
Views on Malacath and his possible relationship to Trinimac vary among the Orcs.

In one Orcish telling, Trinimac confronted Boethiah for creating the dissident movement and challenged her to a battle. During the fight, Trinimac was about to strike down Boethiah when [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] appeared; the Webspinner stabbed Trinimac in the back. As Trinimac kneeled helpless, Boethiah invoked a ritual to scar and twist Trinimac's appearance, then banished the Aedra to a [[Lore:Ashpit|place of ash]]. In a rage, Trinimac cut open his chest and "[tore] the shame from his spirit", being reborn as Mauloch.{{ref|name=MOF}}

Some Trinimac cultists believe the Aedra fooled [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], and rather than being transformed by his ordeal, he instead absorbed a portion of Boethiah's power and gave it to his followers, thus making the Orsimer "improved Elves."{{ref|name=VOFTO}} Others believe that Malacath is actually an agent of Boethiah who has somehow imprisoned Trinimac,{{ref|name=BVTH|[[Blades:Villager#The Heretic|Villager Dialogue]] from [[Blades:Blades|Blades]] during [[Blades:The Heretic|The Heretic]] questline}} a wholly separate entity, and lies to keep them as pariahs under his thumb.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} 

Reflecting these disparate viewpoints, "Mauloch" is seen by some as an Aedra like Trinimac, whereas "Malacath" is a Daedra.{{ref|name=DWAAPOT}}{{ref|name=ONObsidian}}

===Khajiit===
In Pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] texts, the demon Orkha was a spirit that followed [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]] back through the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]] and spoke only in curses of affliction, knowing no other words. [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]] and Boethra battled Orkha, but the demon could only be banished and would not die.{{Ref|name=TAS}}

===From Exile to Exodus===
According to the myth recounted in ''[[Lore:From Exile to Exodus|From Exile to Exodus]]'' by Tarvyn Aram, during the Velothi exodus, the Velothi encountered the followers of Trinimac, who outnumbered them three to one. While Trinimac remained silent, his followers called the dissidents rebels, traitors, and filth. Boethiah then intervened, revealing the truth about the followers' identity as Ornim that had forgotten their days of [[Lore:Iron Orc|iron]] and [[Lore:Wood Orc|wood]], sea and fire, and become convinced they were [[Lore:Elf|elves]]. Boethiah revealed that they had been deceived by the curses of a demon, who'd made it so that when they looked upon him they saw Boethiah herself. But it was Boethiah who'd brought them the [[Lore:Orichalc|Orichalc]], who'd taught them the benefits of war, how to hold their blades, and how to reach beyond. Boethiah then gestured with her hands, forming a triangular sign that could only be true, and walked in a way that revealed the path to achieve an Exodus. Thus the curse was lifted from their eyes. Where they once saw Trinimac, Greatest of All Warriors, they now saw '''Malak, the King of Curses'''. And where they once saw Boethiah, Daughter of Blades, they now saw Trinimac, as she had always been. The Warrior of East and West and of the [[Lore:Tamriel|Starry Heart]], who once bore the burden of separating [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|divinity]] from her [[Lore:Lorkhan|beloved]]. Enraged, Malak uttered an unknown curse and jumped into Boethiah's, into Trinimac's, throat, entering her very being and causing her to collapse in agony, retching blood and ichor. Thus Boethiah-Trinimac collapsed, seemingly lifeless and her followers despaired.{{Ref|name=FETE}} 

However, Mephala and Azura intervened, for though Malak had forgotten, to contend with Boethiah was to contend with the Triangular Gate. The two cast their own spell that echoed the music of [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn]], aiding Boethiah in casting out Malak and his curse. Malak rose covered in Boethiah's blood and bile. Yet he now appeared different, not as the wretched Malak King of Curses, but as something more akin to the Trinimac his followers had loved. He now wore new armor and held a gleaming red axe, and his helm bore a tusked visage of the spurned and the opressed.{{Ref|name=FETE}} From beneath his new helm Malacath growled that Boethiah had forgotten what it meant to be an exile, and uttered a new terrible curse that had never been spoken. The sky split open with thunderous rain, the wind howled with a killing gale, and the very earth beneath them was sundered as fire and death rose from the pits. In the triangular field of divine chaos, Azura took a third of the followers and brought them to the [[Lore:Morrowind|star-wounded east]], while Mephala took another third and took them to the southeast, for she alone did not fear the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]].{{Ref|name=FETE}} Boethiah than tore out the last of her guilt under the [[Lore:Sun|sun]] and transported the third of the followers that remained to Oblivion. The land they had previously occupied drifted into the sea, but was not wholly destroyed, as it still had stories to tell.{{Ref|name=FETE}} 

The followers found themselves in a [[Lore:Ashpit|deadened realm of smoke and ash]]. It was here that the worst of Malacath's fatal curse had been diverted by Boethiah. Malacath laughed that Boethiah had let him destroy her gift from the [[Lore:Auri-El|king]] that had been an idyllic paradise, but Boethiah responded her spheres were many and houses innumerable, and so she had no need of it.{{Ref|name=FETE}}

Malacath attacked once more, claiming he'd never stop hunting Boethiah. But, with a single precise strike, he was cleaved into the ashen wastes, his bones forming a new foundation. His blood mingled with the ash and soon the realm was his. Boethiah then spent time with the surviving followers, their skins changing under her teachings and the influence of the realm. When Malacath's spirit reappeared, Boethiah addressed him, urging him to stop trying to enforce his will through curses both he and his followers knew to be untrue, but instead to seek glory through struggle and to understand the Will Against Rule. In this way Malacath could come to learn the proper tendencies of the [[Lore:Hero|hero]] and know something of Love. She then formed a powerful sign with her hands, encapsulating Malacath and his followers into a singular sphere, casting it back onto the mountains of Nirn. Thus emerged the god Mauloch and the great Orcs, who would from than on build strength through adversity. Together, Boethiah and Mauloch tested and hardened their chosen peoples against one another, therefore guiding them further toward an Exodus.{{Ref|name=FETE|{{Cite Book|From Exile to Exodus}}}}

==The Accords of Madness==
Many accounts paint Malacath as a Prince who is very protective of his followers, and who becomes vindictive when they are wronged. Among the tales of the Madgod Sheogorath found in the ''[[Lore:16 Accords of Madness|Sixteen Accords of Madness]]'' is a story about Malacath and his demiprince son, [[Lore:Emmeg Gro-Kayra|Emmeg Gro-Kayra]]. Sometime before the founding of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], Sheogorath (in disguise) had given Emmeg, a renowned Orcish warrior, the [[Lore:Neb-Crescen|Neb-Crescen]]. When drawn, the sword set off a bloodlust within Emmeg, and he murdered a young Orc girl in his frenzied haze and ran away. Sheogorath then summoned Malacath to where the slaughtered Orc girl was and instigated his vengeance. Sheogorath persuaded Malacath to avenge the Orc girl's death using the weapon of Sheogorath's choice and banish the murderer to his plane of the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]. Malacath manifested in the direction the killer was fleeing, and drew the blade, which further fueled his rage. The prince quickly crossed the distance, and cleanly lopped Emmeg's head off his body, and what followed was a sudden silence. Seeing the severed head, Malacath realized what he had done; he had damned his biological son to the realm of the Mad God. To further exacerbate the pain, Sheogorath came to the scene of the infanticide to claim the still sentient head of Emmeg and the Neb-Cresen. Malacath stayed behind and mourned as he heard the pleas of his son being carried off into the distance.{{Ref|name=16Accords12|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness, v. XII}}}}

==Worship==
===Orcs===
[[File:ON-concept-Malacath Armors.jpg|thumb|right|Orcish Malacath Cult Style]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''God of curses, hear my prayer!<br>Lord of the betrayed, give me strength!<br>Keeper of the grudge, harden my heart!<br>Holder of the broken promises, ignite my anguish!<br>Master of the sworn oath, grant me the ferocity to overcome my enemies!<br>Malacath, hear my prayer!''|''[[Lore:Prayer to the Furious One|Prayer to the Furious One]]''}}
While the Orsimer believe many gods exist, they only venerate one.{{ref|name=VOFTO}} Malacath, whom they also call '''Mauloch''', the '''Orc-Father'''{{ref|name=MOF|{{Cite Book|Mauloch, Orc-Father}}}} the '''Great Chief''', the '''First Orc''',{{ref|name=VOFTO}} the '''Daedric Lord of the Orcs''', the keeper and '''Lord of the Sworn Oath and the Bloody Curse''',{{ref|name=Sharamph|[[SR:Sharamph|Sharamph]] dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=OSGD|[[Skyrim:Generic Dialogue#Orc Strongholds Dialogue|Skyrim, Generic Dialogue]]}}  and '''Ashpit's Lord'''.{{ref|name=RSH|[[ON:Rivenspire Skyshard Hunter|Rivenspire Skyshard Hunter]]}} Curiously, "Mauloch" is seen by some as an [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] like Trinimac, whereas "Malacath" is a Daedra.{{ref|name=DWAAPOT}}{{ref|name=ONObsidian|[[Online:Obsidian Scar|Obsidian Scar]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} While some consider the Aedric Mauloch to be a different being altogether, others believe that Mauloch and Malacath are one and the same, a Pariah God who has been rejected by both the Aedra and Daedra.{{ref|name=ONAbamath|[[Online:Abamath|Shrine of Mauloch]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Orcs that live in the strongholds choose to live by the [[Lore:Code of Malacath|Code of Mauloch]] (or Malacath), a set of unwritten rules which instructs them on how to deal with matters like honor and vengeance, but also covers daily life.{{ref|name=VOFTO}}{{ref|name=TCOM}}

However, there are some Orcs who continue to venerate [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]]. Some Trinimac cultists believe the Aedra fooled [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], and was not actually transformed by his ordeal, and instead absorbed a portion of Boethiah's power and gave it to his followers, thus making the Orsimer "improved Elves."{{ref|name=VOFTO}} Others instead believe that Malacath is an agent of Boethiah who has somehow imprisoned Trinimac.{{ref|name=BVTH|[[Blades:Villager#The Heretic|Villager Dialogue]] from [[Blades:Blades|Blades]] during [[Blades:The Heretic|The Heretic]] questline}} The latter cultists believe that Malacath and Trinimac are actually wholly separate entities, and that Malacath lies to keep them as pariahs under his thumb.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} Some of the Orcs who revered Trinimac and rejected Malacath, believed that Malacath's traditions were stifling and had caused the Orcs to lose their way.{{ref|name=Alga|[[Online:Forge-Mother Alga|Forge-Mother Alga]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Others thought that the view many Orcs had of Malacath as their protector was false, as Malacath's real passion was vengeance, and so belief in him held the Orcs back.{{ref|name=Solgra|[[Online:High Priestess Solgra|High Priestess Solgra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Trinimac worship was known to be prevalent in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] when it was adopted by King [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog]] of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]],{{ref|name=MAT}}{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and was also advocated by King [[Lore:Gortwog gro-Nagorm|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]] of Nova Orsinium following the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]. Gortwog's policies on Malacath and Trinimac worship angered Orcs both within and outside of his territory, and some worried about the consequences of abandoning Malacath.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} While Gortwog's city would soon fall in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the belief in Trinimac's imprisonment would endure, though now considered extremely heretical by most Orcs. {{ref|name=BVTH}}

Orcs that revere Malacath believe that the afterlife promises rewards of immortality, abundant food and drink, and constant battle deep within the Ashen Forge. They believe that, as the ultimate expression of the Orc stronghold, the Ashpit bastion stretches endlessly across the planes, extending even behind the [[Lore:Stars|stars]] to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] to grant access to the soul of every worthy Orc who crosses into the afterlife, and that within Malacath's stronghold, every Orc is a chief, every chief has a thousand wives, and every wife has a thousand slaves to cater to their every need.{{ref|name=OtA}} 

Orcs that pass into the afterlife are believed to be thrust into the perpetually burning white-hot coals that are the very manifestation of the Code of Malacath, they are honed, tempered, and made whole in the cleansing flames of Malacath's hearth, said to burn hotter than the [[Lore:Sun|sun]], through a process called the ritual of tempering, which all Orcish [[Lore:Souls|souls]] bound for the Ashpit must undergo when entering the realm. The grudges and fury of these Orcs are believed to be heated and melted within the flames, in order to eventually be forged into the next generation of mortal Orcs. With their grudges returned to the living world, the immortal Orcs of the Ashpit are thus freed to create new grudges. Some of the most memorable of these new grievances are said to be folded and hardened on the {{Lore Link|Anvil of Ash}} to create weapons and armor of legendary stature.{{ref|name=OtA|{{Cite Book|On Orcs and the Afterlife}}}}{{ref|name=ONThazeg|[[ON:Thazeg|Thazeg's dialogue]] in [[Online:Free Spirits|ESO]]}} There is said to be no place for a gentle soul in Malacath's Ashen Forge, such souls are instead believed to be given to Trinimac, who watches over all those who have fallen and guides them to their final reward.{{ref|name=Bazrag|[[Online:Chief Bazrag|Chief Bazrag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Orcish cults to Malacath include the [[Lore:Wrathful Flame|Wrathful Flame]], a religious order tasked with relighting Malacath's pyre in Old Orsinium every ten years,{{ref|name=ONN}} and Malacath's Brutal Breed, a cult that arose after the fall of Orsinium and was dedicated to wiping out all [[Lore:Bretons|Bretons]] and [[Lore:Redguards|Redguards]] for all their slights, real or imagined, against the Orcs.{{ref|[[Online:Malacath's Band of Brutality|Malacath's Band of Brutality]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Oathsworn|Oathsworn]] are an Orcish warrior sect dedicated to Malacath. The Oathsworn have been said to attribute all comfort to their god, and to use the fiery heart of their sacred home to light their forges. Oathsworn do not reside solely in the Oathsworn Pit itself. Being among the greatest warriors in Tamriel, they seek out real enemies to test themselves against. Orcish clans are forbidden from attacking the Oathsworn, as doing so goes against the code. For a long time, the Oathsworn had been tasked with guarding the [[Lore:Staff of Many Paths|Staff of Many Paths]], an artifact of [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], until its theft during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]{{ref|name=Pit|[[Online:Oathsworn Pit|Oathsworn Pit]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Bazrag|[[Online:Chief Bazrag|Chief Bazrag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Faenalir|[[Online:Faenalir's Letter|Faenalir's Letter]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dulzush|[[Online:Dulzush Forgefire|Dulzush Forgefire]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Strife|Events of [[Online:Victory Through Strife|Victory Through Strife]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

They also believe that, to Malacath, a bow is the weapon of a hunter, not a warrior, and to attack from a distance does not honor him. Malacath is claimed to warn against enemies speaking fair before they act foul, and to expect his followers to keep going even in harsh conditions. The Cult of Malacath believes that betrayal is inevitable and so they must always be looking askance. Lastly, their god is said to call for a layered defense and for arms and armor crafted to the highest standards. They decorate their armor and weapons with various symbols thought to be sacred and to represent protective properties.{{ref|name=CMMS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 23: Malacath Style}}}}

====[[Lore:Code of Malacath|The Code of Mauloch]]====
[[File:ON-quest-Flames of Forge and Fallen 04.jpg|thumb|right|A carving of Malacath at his shrine in Old Orsinium]]
The Code of Mauloch contains many simple precepts, which are mostly tacit{{ref|name=VOFTO}} and include prohibitions on theft, murder, and assault, though ample exceptions are made to these rules.{{ref|name=TCOM}} More explicitly, the Code encourages respect for forging and blacksmithing, requiring vengeance for insulted honor, and recognizing that death in combat is pleasing to Mauloch.{{ref|name=VOFTO}} Orcs believe if something is not worth fighting for, it is beneath the Code.{{ref|name=TCOM}}

The Code sets rules for how to select a stronghold's chief through challenge and combat, and the "traditional roles" of the chief and his wives.{{ref|name=VOFTO}} The Chief is normally the strongest male, and it is he who makes decisions for the clan and decides when the Code of Mauloch has been satisfied. Nearly all the women of the stronghold are the Chief's wives or daughters; the exception being the stronghold's wise woman, who handles healing and spiritual matters. Disputes are settled through short, violent fights. Orcs who displease the Chief are normally banished from the stronghold, being forced to live among the other races.{{ref|name=TCOM}} 

Orc strongholds have no jails.{{ref|name=TCOM}} If someone commits a crime, the penalty is the "Blood Price", to be paid to the victim or their surviving relatives.{{ref|name=VOFTO}} This involves payment in goods as restitution, or bleeding "enough" to satisfy the victim that punishment has been met.{{ref|name=TCOM}}

===Nords===
[[File:SR-concept-Snake Mural.jpg|A depiction of Orkey|thumb|right]]
The traditional [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic belief system contains two deities that are seen as being connected to Malacath. The first is '''Mauloch''', who is known by the Nords as the '''God of Orcs''' and the "'''Mountain Fart'''". He is clearly identified with Malacath, and tests the Nords through warfare. He is considered a testing god.{{ref|name=VOFTN|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Nords}}}}

The other is '''Orkey''', also called '''Old Knocker''', who is the god of mortality.{{ref|name=TTNOO}}{{ref|name=VOFTN}} [[Lore:Orkey|Orkey]] is a primal [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]]n death god,{{ref|name=TAX|{{Cite Book|Tu'whacca, Arkay, Xarxes}}}} a "loan-god" whose worship stems from the days when the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]i ruled Atmora.{{ref|name=VOFTN}} He is said to be a fusion of aspects of Mauloch and [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]],{{ref|name=VOFTN}} though others suggest that Arkay is a fusion of Orkey and the Aldmeri god [[Lore:Xarxes|Xarxes]].{{ref|name=TAX}} Whatever the case, Orkey is also the Nordic god of the dead.{{ref|name=TAX}} His priests oversee the Halls of the Dead of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and ensure that the remains of the departed are properly consecrated and cared for.{{ref|name=ONHall|[[Online:Hall of the Dead|Hall of the Dead]] loading screens in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The earliest Atmoran beliefs involved the worship of totemic animals, which evolved into the Nordic pantheon.{{ref|name=DATN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords|ns_base=Online}}}} Orkey's animal totem is the snake.{{ref|name=TSOG|{{Cite Book|The Song of Gods|ns_base=Online}}}}

Numerous Nordic myths involve the Old Knocker; it is said he has attempted to "ruin" the Nords since the days of Atmora,{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} and his battles with [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]] are "legendary".{{ref|name=TTNOO}} The Nords believe that Orkey "stole their years away".{{ref|name=FSOKW}} It is said that the ancient Atmorans were once as long-lived as Elves, until Orkey, through "heathen trickery", fooled the Atmorans into a bargain that "bound them to the count of winters."{{ref|name=VOFTN}}

Another tale says that during the reign of King Wulfharth, Orkey summoned the ghost of [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], and the Nords were "eaten down to six years old" (some interpretations say their lifespans were simply reduced to six years).{{ref|name=VOFTN}}{{ref|name=FSOKW}} Wulfharth asked for [[Lore:Shor|Shor]]'s help, and his ghost fought Alduin's ghost on the spirit plane.{{ref|name=FSOKW}} Shor defeated the Time-Eater, then removed the curse from the Nords and "[threw] most of it onto the nearby Orcs", Orkey's folk, and they were "ruined".{{ref|name=VOFTN}}{{ref|name=FSOKW}}

Nords keep small statues of testing gods in their households and try to keep them appeased.{{ref|name=ContrabandN|[[ON:Contraband N#Nord House God|Nord House God]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Around the same time, as part of the schemes of the [[Lore:Ulthorn|Hound]], the Drublog Clan imprisoned the [[Lore:The Silvenar|Silvenar]], and attempted to use an artifact called the [[Lore:Runestone of Malacath|Runestone of Malacath]] in order to carry out the Ritual of Unbinding, a Daedric rite which would cut away all of the Silvenar's ties, mystical and magical, and thus sever him from all links to mortal and immortal realms. The Drublog even invoked Malacath's Daedra servants to have them oversee the rite. However, the timely intervention of the Vestige and the sacrifice of a willing [[Lore:Souls|soul]] through the Ritual of the Proxy, which created a key that could break the ritual's bonds, allowed for the Silvenar to be released, and his links to the Bosmer to be restored.{{ref|name=Ritual|[[Online:Ritual of Unbinding|Ritual of Unbinding]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Prisoner|Events of [[Online:The Prisoner of Jathsogur|The Prisoner of Jathsogur]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Sariel|[[Online:Ambassador Sariel|Ambassador Sariel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Raen|[[Online:Ambassador Raen|Ambassador Raen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Indaenir|[[Online:Indaenir|Indaenir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

===Third Era===
[[File:OBR-quest-Malacath.jpg|Shrine of Malacath in Cyrodiil (Oblivion Remastered|thumb|right]]
Around {{Year|3E 405}}, Malacath was summoned by an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]]. The Prince told of a [[Lore:Daedra Seducer|Daedra Seducer]], a "little panikosa" to whom Malacath had given the beauty she always wanted. The Seducer had then betrayed him and fled to a nearby dungeon. Malacath wanted her dead. The agent obliged, and once the deed was done discovered the Seducer's part in a local murder. This evidence was brought before the local ruler, thus solving the case and boosting the agent's fame. Malacath rewarded the hero with [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]].{{ref|name=DFMalacath|[[Daggerfall:Malacath's Quest|Malacath's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

The [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] also summoned Malacath in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] in {{Year|3E 427}}. Malacath asked the hero to seek revenge on behalf of an Orc adventurer, Kharag gro-Khar, who was denied his rightful fame and glory. After vanquishing a great threat, Kharag's [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] partner, Oreyn Bearclaw, took all the credit.{{ref|name=MWMalacathDialog|[[Morrowind:Malacath's Quest|Malacath]]'s dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} Though both Kharag and Oreyn were long dead, Malacath's ire was so great that he requested the Nerevarine slay the Dunmer's last remaining descendant, Farvyn Oreyn. When confronted, Farvyn admitted the whole story was a lie, and attacked. The Nerevarine prevailed, and received the [[Lore:Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw|Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw]] as a reward.{{ref|name=MWMalacath|[[Morrowind:Malacath's Quest|Events]] of Malacath's quest in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} The Nerevarine later sold the Helm to the Museum of Artifacts in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=TRMuseum|[[Tribunal:The Museum|Events]] of The Museum quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}. Malacath, however, was mistaken; Farvyn Oreyn was ''not'' the last of his bloodline {{ref|[[OB:Modryn Oreyn|Modryn Oreyn]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}.

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Malacath commanded the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] to free a group of [[Lore:Ogre|ogres]] from enslavement in the mines of a noble on the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Once the Prince's "little brothers" were released from their bonds, the Hero was given Volendrung as a reward.{{ref|name=OBMalacath|[[Oblivion:Malacath|Events]] of Malacath's quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''How I laughed when you  betrayed them, turned your homeland into no less an ash pit than my realm. The proud issue of the Velothi, humbled at last. By one of their own. And still there is the curse you made, unfulfilled.''|Malacath to Sul regarding the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]]{{ref|name=LOS}}}}
[[File:SR-quest-The Cursed Tribe 07.jpg|thumb|right|A slain orc at the base of a forgotten shrine]]
Around {{Year|4E 48}}, a gravely wounded [[Books:The Infernal City/Prince Attrebus Mede|Prince Attrebus Mede]] and his companion [[Books:The Infernal City/Sul|Sul]] found themselves in the [[Lore:Ashpit|Ashpit]] while on a quest to stop the floating city of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]]. Fled there from the [[Lore:Scuttling Void|Scuttling Void]] while on the brink of death. Attrebus's wounds were tended to by an Altmer woman with pale skin, rosy gold hair, and green eyes, named Silhansa. Silhansa questioned Attrebus on their circumstances, and the Imperial prince explained their quest to stop Umbriel. It was than that Silhansa revealed herself to be Malacath in disguise and grew to take on a different form, gray haired and gray eyed and a hundred feet tall, while the group's surroundings changed to a beautiful garden. Malacath claimed he had no interest in Attrebus, but he'd healed their wounds, and ultimately agreed to help the pair, due to his interest in Sul, whose motivation was revenge. Sul had made an oath of vengeance to Malacath himself, and the Prince had been delighted by the wizard's destruction of Morrowind, now no less an ashpit than Malacath's own realm, something  which delighted the Prince. After extracting a promise from Attrebus that he'd call on him one day if he had use for him, and warning the pair to not disappoint him, Malacath teleported them to [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], where they'd seek the [[Lore:Umbra|Umbra Sword]].{{ref|name=LOS}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] arrived at the Orc stronghold of [[Lore:Largashbur|Largashbur]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] while it was under attack by [[Lore:Giant|giants]]. According to Atub, the wise woman, the tribe was cursed by Malacath. The Dragonborn helped Atub summon the Prince, who proceeded to berate the Chief, Yamarz, for being so weak that giants would openly attack Largashbur. Malacath demanded Yamarz kill the leader of the giants and return with his warhammer as an offering.{{ref|name=SRCursed|[[Skyrim:The Cursed Tribe|Events]] of The Cursed Tribe quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

Yamarz was perturbed by this development, and demanded the Dragonborn "assist" him in getting to the giants' lair. Once there, Yamarz asked the Dragonborn to kill the giant and let him take the credit. It is not clear what happened next, but the Dragonborn killed the giant and Yamarz was slain—either by the giant or at the hands of the Dragonborn. Regardless, the Dragonborn returned to the stronghold with the warhammer, and Malacath appeared once more. The Prince named a new chief and transformed the warhammer into Volendrung, which he gave to the Last Dragonborn as a reward.{{ref|name=SRCursed}}

Around the same time, [[Lore:Goblin|goblins]] from Cyrodiil began appearing near [[Lore:Riften|Riften]], not far from Largashbur.{{ref|name=SRBlue|[[Skyrim:Blue in the Face|Events]] of Blue in the Face in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Troublingly, it was rumored they were being led by the Blue God himself.{{ref|name=LTC}} The Last Dragonborn investigated the matter and found that the "Blue God" was merely an exiled Orc from Largashbur, covered in [[Lore:Steel Blue Entoloma|blue mushroom]] paste, whom the goblins mistook for their deity.{{ref|name=BGJ}}{{ref|name=SRBlue}}

==Artifacts==
===Brutal Bands===
[[File:ON-icon-armor-Malacath's Band of Brutality.png|right|thumb|A Brutal Band]]
[[Lore:Malacath's Brutal Breed|Brutal Bands]], are heavy iron rings. When a Brutal Band is worn, the wearer's hands instinctively curl into fists and the sound of distant drums and crashing steel fills the ears of the wearer, imbuing them with all the bitterness of Orckind and all the strength of Malacath. The Orsinium codex states that the Brutal Bands are from Malacath himself and that they were forged in the Ashpit.

Ritual dust from the Ashpit was given to the most devout followers of Malacath in the Brutal Breed by Malacath himself, these followers would place their Brutal Bands in the dust while they slept, believing that it would charge the Band with energy from the Ashpit. Ritual oil was also carried in urns that bore the symbol of the Brutal Breed, with this oil being carried and used on the Brutal Bands by every cultist as a form of prayer and as a request for perseverance in the face of endless adversity. The Brutal Bands of the supplicants have portions of Malacath's true name on them. The upper hoops on the Bands represent the blessing of Scourge.

* The following information extends the «[[Lore:Malacath#Malacath_Cult_Shapeshifters|Malacath Cult Shapeshifters]]» section and is taken from the official ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]'' source, however, it contains apparent anachronistic lore contradictions, so it is unknown if the event or its seemingly anachronistic elements are considered canon; the complete story plot features [[Blades:Henrik Seven-Swords|Henrik Seven-Swords]]{{ref|name=Seven|[[Castles:The Lessons of Seven-Swords|The Lessons of Seven-Swords]] event in [[Castles:Castles|Castles]]}} and [[Oblivion:Owyn|Owyn]]{{ref|name=Inquiry}}, characters who are considered unlikely to have lived at the same time period:

:: At some point during the reign of the [[Lore:Dynasty of Odar the Brave|Dynasty of Odar the Brave]], a group of shapeshifting cultists of Malacath infiltrated the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] Arena by posing as gladiators, competing in the ongoing Dragon Games. After an investigation at the behest of an Arena Blademaster, Owyn, the identity of the shapeshifters as cultists of Malacath was uncovered, and the information was relayed to the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]]. The cultists ultimately used the Dragon Games as cover to launch an assault backed by a force of shapeshifters against the Imperial City itself, setting the capital ablaze. However, alerted by the Capital Blademaster, the ruling monarch of Odar kingdom ordered their forces to launch coordinated counterattack, defeating the cult's leaders and routing their forces, ending the assault.{{ref|name=Glory}}{{ref|name=Inquiry}}{{ref|name=Mayhem}}{{ref|name=RulingsGlory}}

Malacath's voice too is described as akin to beams of wood rending.{{ref|name=LOS}}
