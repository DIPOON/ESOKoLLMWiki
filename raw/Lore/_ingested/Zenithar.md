[[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]], the '''God of Work and Commerce''', the '''Provider of our Ease''',{{ref|[[Online:Head Markings/Crown Crate Rewards#Fortune's Anvil Face Markings|Fortune's Anvil Face Markings]] collectible description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} is one of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]].{{Ref|name=FMGAE|{{Cite Book|For my Gods and Emperor}}}}{{Ref|name=CK1|{{Cite Book|Charwich-Koniinge, Volume 1}}}}{{Ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Invocation}}}} The Trader God is frequently seen as the same deity as the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]]i god [[Lore:Z'en|Z'en]].{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} In the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], however, Zenithar is considered a more cultivated god of merchants and middle nobility, being the deity of wealth, labor, commerce, communication,{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=GTC|{{Cite Book|Guide to Cheydinhal}}}} and community.{{Ref|name=MZP|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Zenke's Posts|Michael Zenke's Posts]]}}

He has strong ties to [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{Ref|name=VOF}} The introduction of the worship of Zenithar was largely tolerated in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] due to his similarities to a [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n agricultural deity.{{Ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} His worshippers say that, despite his mysterious origins, Zenithar is the god 'that will always win'.{{Ref|name=VOF}} His priests teach that the path to peace and prosperity is through earnest work and honest profit, not through war, bloodshed, or theft.{{Ref|name=TCND|{{Cite Book|Ten Commands: Nine Divines}}}} Zenithar is seen as a warrior god, but one who is restrained and reserved in times of peace.{{Ref|name=Daggerfall|[[Lore:Knights of Iron|Events]] of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=KE10|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part X}}}}

He is thought to be associated with [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]],{{Ref|[[Oblivion:Sir Ralvas|Sir Ralvas]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}} as well as a large blue star sometimes seen in the skies of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{Ref|name=KE8|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VIII}}}} It's also said that he is most in touch with the mortal world, and that he created the Mace of the Crusader for [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] to help him defeat [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]].{{Ref|name=KOTN|Events of [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Oblivion]].}}

Zenithar appeared to the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] during their service to the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]], as a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] named Jon Hawker, and after he had been freed, the Nerevarine received his gauntlets, Zenithar's Warning and Zenithar's Wiles.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Ring in Darkness|Ring in Darkness]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>

==Worship==
[[File:ON-crown store-Furnishing Pack Chapel of Zenithar.jpg|left|thumb|Chapel of Zenithar]][[File:ON-place-Cropsford 10.jpg|A shrine to Zenithar in [[Lore:Cropsford|Cropsford]]|right|thumb]]
Temples devoted to him are sometimes called [[Lore:Resolution of Z'en|Resolutions]]. Worship of Zenithar is typically more prevalent in some geographic areas, such as [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], [[Lore:Totambu|Totambu]], and [[Lore:Kambria|Kambria]].{{Ref|name=CK1}} 

A [[Lore:Smithing|blacksmith's]] anvil is his symbol, representing labor and production. The [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] [[Lore:Merchants Guild|Merchant's Guild]] is known to award small golden anvils in Zenithar's name to upstanding members.{{ref|{{Item Link|Golden Anvil of Zenithar|id=62614|level=1|quality=3}}}} Worshippers of Zenithar often keep small devotional anvils in their homes and pewter scale weights engraved with a blue star in the coin purses for financial luck in trying times.{{ref|name=ContrabandZ1|[[ON:Contraband Z#Zenithar Wood Sculpture|Zenithar Wood Sculpture]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ContrabandZ2|[[ON:Contraband Z#Zenithar's Measure|Zenithar's Measure]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

One form of an offering to Zenithar is to burn crops at a shrine of him to offer thanks for prosperity.{{ref|name=Antias|[[Online:Offerings to Zenithar|Offerings to Zenithar]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Worshippers also use special prayer beads to call forth good luck and success in all types of negotiations.{{ref|name=ContrabandZ3|[[ON:Contraband Z#Zenithar Negotiation Beads|Zenithar Negotiation Beads]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} During special celebrations dedicated to Zenithar, many cities all around Tamriel were decorated with imagery of the blue star, coins and crops.{{Ref|name=ZOZ|Appearance of major [[Online:Cities|Cities]] during [[ON:Zeal of Zenithar|Zeal of Zenithar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Zentihar is also venerated on the isle of [[Lore:Solstice|Solstice]], the seat of [[Lore:Clan Corelanya|Clan Corelanya]]. His worship was introduced to [[Lore:Sunport|Sunport]] by Imperial traders from Leyawiin. As the patron of commerce and craftsmanship, he has gained a devoted following among Sunport's merchants and traders.{{Ref|name=VOFS|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: Solstice}}}}

During the Interregnum, the free city of Markarth was one of many cities decorated with Zenithar images and devotional objects during special celebrations dedicated to the god.{{Ref|name=Markarth|Appearance of [[Online:Markarth|Markarth]] during [[ON:Zeal of Zenithar|Zeal of Zenithar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After Imperialization and the loss of independence, some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Zenithar, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} As of then a shrine of Zenithar was found close to the [[Lore:Four Skull Lookout|Four Skull Lookout]].{{Ref|name=FSL|[[Skyrim:Four Skull Lookout|Four Skull Lookout]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SOZ|[[Skyrim:Shrines#Shrine of Zenithar|Shrine of Zenithar]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Some [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] followers of the Eight Divines considered the sacred toil, fortitude, and triumph in the face of hardship spoken of by the prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] as virtues best embodied by Zenithar.{{Ref|name=UV|[[ON:Uvoo Vadryon|Uvoo Vadryon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

==Artifacts==
===Golden Anvil===
[[File:ON-place-Zenithar's Abbey 06.jpg|thumb|right|Golden Anvil]]
A holy relic of Zenithar, that was made of unremarkable iron, by a pious smith, Zenithar honored the smiths work by turning the anvil into gold. The anvil would then fall under the care of the priests of Zenithar's Abbey, which later fell into ruins by avarice.{{Ref|name=ONDaluion|[[Online:Daluion|Daluion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] resolute, {{Lore Link|Daluion}} traveled to the ruined {{Lore Link|Zenithar's Abbey}} to find the long-lost Golden Anvil and learn the story of the abbey's arbitrator, {{Lore Link|Gavos Douar}}. With the help of an [[Lore:The Vestige|adventurer]], they were able to bypass the monsters that inhabited the ruin and recover the sigil keys to the adytum on the east side of the abbey. 

They found the decrepit and corroded Golden Anvil on the temple's main altar as well as an elderly Gavos Douar, who tried to preserve the anvil for many years. Before Zenithar's faith was forever lost in the abbey and the anvil, Daluion blessed it with the adventurer's toil and it brought holy light back to the Golden Anvil.{{Ref|name=ONAnvil|[[Online:The Golden Anvil|The Golden Anvil]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} Shortly after its discovery, Daluion returned to the temple in [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] and informed the Primate of the anvil's location. The Knights of Iron were deployed to recover it and cleanse the abbey.{{Ref|name=ONDaluion}}

===Mace of the Crusader===
[[File:OB-item-Mace of the Crusader.jpg|right|thumb|150px|The Mace of the Crusader]]
The [[Lore:Mace of the Crusader|Mace of the Crusader]] (also known as the {{Anchor|Mace of Zenithar}}) was created by Zenithar. Those it strikes are burnt by holy flames, and it has the ability to turn the undead. According to legend, when Pelinal Whitestrake was slain, a messenger carried the mace to the settlement of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]]. Centuries later, the master craftsman Saint Kaladas built the Great Chapel of Zenithar to honor the legend. When Kaladas died and was entombed in the Chapel, those who prayed to him began to receive visions of the mace. In the vision, the mace can be seen in the distance, separated by a void. The void symbolizes doubt, and an inscription on the tomb says to "walk in the faith". Sir Ralvas of the original Knights attempted hundreds of times to reach the mace, but failed each time. The pilgrim later discovered that the Boots of the Crusader are needed to cross the void; Zenithar has close ties to Kynareth, as craftsmen need to take resources from the natural world. With the boots, the path of faith was illuminated, and the pilgrim recovered the mace.{{ref|[[OB:The Path of the Righteous|The Path of the Righteous]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}
