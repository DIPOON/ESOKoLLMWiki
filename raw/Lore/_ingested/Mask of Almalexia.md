# Lore:Mask of Almalexia

The [[Lore:Mask of Almalexia|Mask of Almalexia]] is the [[Lore:Daedric Equipment|Daedric]]{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|322}} ceremonial War Mask{{ref|name=TIEGTTM|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Morrowind}}}} (also known as a killing mask){{ref|name=TTISV8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} of the Tribunal god [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]]. It features prominent tusks and serpentine aesthetics, fitting of the goddess's title of the Face-Snaked Queen.{{ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}}

It is one of the [[Lore:Masks of the Tribunal|Masks of the Tribunal]], the other two being the [[Lore:Mask of Sotha Sil|Mask of Sotha Sil]], and [[Lore:Vivec's Ash Mask|Vivec's Ash Mask]].{{ref|name=SRMT|{{Cite Book|Masks of the Tribunal (book)}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
===First Era===
[[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36|''The Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec, Sermon Thirty-Six'']] describes the events leading up to the Red Moment. The [[Lore:Mournhold|Mourning Hold]] was attacked by [[Lore:Dwarven Centurion|Dwarven Centurion]]s and Dwemeri [[Lore:Tonal Architecture|tonal architects]], before being repelled by Almalexia. At this moment, she was described as becoming the "Face-Snaked Queen of the Three in One",{{ref|name=36L36}} but is unknown if she had her war mask at this time.

In {{Year|1E 2920}}, after being falsely led to believe that the massacre of her [[Lore:Skeffington Coven|witch coven]] was at the hands of her former lover, [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] was summoned by the witch [[Lore:Turala|Turala]] to destroy Mournhold for revenge, and he complied.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Frostfall (v10)}}}}

During the carnage, [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and Sotha Sil arrived to stop the Prince. Enraged, the two donned their killing masks, and the battlefield was soon lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. Almalexia plunged [[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]] into Dagon's flesh and carved him from within. Meanwhile, Sotha Sil, enacting vengeance for Ald Sotha, used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Prince into submission. Wounded, Sil allegedly whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}}{{ref|name=2920v11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}

===Second Era===
The practice of designing and wearing [[Lore:Daedric Faces|masks modeled after important figures]] in Dunmeri culture was not uncommon.{{ref|name=DFO|[[MW:Daedric Face of Terror|Daedric Face of Terror]],[[MW:Daedric Face of Inspiration|Daedric Face of Inspiration]], and [[MW:Daedric Face of God|Daedric Face of God]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL|[[:File:LO-misc-Daedric Helms.jpg|Morrowind Daedric Helms Concept Art]]}} Mercymother's Crowns were replica masks sometimes worn by priests of the Tribunal Temple during the [[Lore: Festival of Forbearance|Festival of Forbearance]]. Those who sought Mother Morrowind's mercy found her visage comforting, while those who would do harm to the Dark Elf people felt an unnameable terror lurking behind the eyes.{{Ref|name=MM|[[ON:Mercymother's Crown|Mercymother's Crown]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
When the Nerevarine came to Mournhold after Dagoth Ur's fall in {{Year|3E 427}}, Almalexia sought to trick, entrap, and destroy him as part of this plot. She first turned on [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and slew him in his [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]], then unleashed its [[Lore:Fabricants|mechanical inhabitants]] into her own city in order to frame her old friend. Her ruse lured the Nerevarine to Sotha Sil's legendary home, where she hoped that the reincarnation of her husband would be killed by the inhabitants. When the Nerevarine persevered, Almalexia donned her killing mask, and tried to finish the job herself. But her powers failed her, and she died at the Nerevarine's hand in the Clockwork City.{{ref|name=TRMG|[[TR:The Mad God|The Mad God]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

Almalexia and Sotha Sil's bodies were retrieved by Vivec, who interred them in the proper Velothi fashion.{{ref|name=MKPosts|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

===Fourth Era===
Despite the rise of the New Temple, worship of the Tribunal continued into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] and was driven underground. These cults were considered heretical by wider [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] society and were persecuted by the New Temple.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} One cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]]. The Ashfall's Tear cult gathered and preserved many relics of the old Tribunal Temple, such as Mother Morrowind's mask.

Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was alerted to the cult's existence when they attempted to recover several additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.{{ref|name=GotT|Events of the quest [[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal (quest)|Ghosts of the Tribunal]].}} One member of the cult, a Dunmer named Erden Relvel, became corrupted by the dangerous relics in the cult's possession and became a priest of [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]]. He created [[Lore:Ash Zombie|ash zombie]] minions and sent them to attack the temple at Ashfall's Tear, but he was ultimately killed by the Last Dragonborn.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Ashen Heart|Ashen Heart]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Masks of the Tribunal, along with the Sharmat's were recovered from this ordeal.
