[[Lore:Taneth|Taneth]] is one of the eight major cities in the province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} situated on the coastal highlands of southern Hammerfell. Taneth is a bustling and important port city{{Ref|name=SRMap}} occupied by the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]], the modern-day remnant of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]].{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Hammerfell}}}} Taneth is the seat of power for the eponymous polity, the '''Kingdom of Taneth'''.{{Ref|name=ONBanner|[[:File:ON-furnishing-Banner of Taneth.jpg|Banner of Taneth]] furnishing in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
Taneth is located in the south-central region of Hammerfell, deep in the Forebears' territory.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} The ancient tomb of the [[Lore:Al-Danobia|Al-Danobia]] bloodline is located northwest of the city and it is considered part of the Kingdom of Taneth.{{Ref|name=ONDanobia|[[Online:al-Danobia Tomb|Al-Danobia Tomb]] quest location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} However the peninsula from across the bay, [[Lore:Hew's Bane|Hew's Bane]] is considered far from their reach.{{Ref|name=ONZeira|[[Online:Zeira|Zeira]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} Taneth is established on the eastern side of [[Lore:Hew's Bay|Hew's Bay]]{{Ref|name=MOTHBTGAYQ|{{Cite Book|Members of the Hew's Bane Thieves Guild Answer Your Questions}}}} and is equipped with a harbor.{{Ref|name=IWPTZ|{{Cite Book|Iron Wheel Prisoner Transfer: Zeira|ns_base=ON}}}} A short river flows through the city, and the [[Lore:Tark River|Tark River]] is found far to the north.{{Ref|name=RGMap|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel]]}} Taneth is neighbored by several settlements, including Lainebon Place to the west, [[Lore:Riverview|Riverview]] to the northwest, and [[Lore:Roseguard|Roseguard]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

==History==
Taneth was founded by the Ra Gada, but the al-Danobia bloodline had always held significant power over the Kingdom for many untold years.{{Ref|name=ONPercius|[[Online:Percius Loche|Percius Loche]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} It is named after the ancient noble, Danobia.{{Ref|name=ONFalorah|[[Online:Magnifica Falorah|Magnifica Falorah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}}  It is unspecified which wave came through the region, but the Tavan wave (who are known for their avian-inspired imagery) was known to have traveled along the southern coast of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=CM28|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} There has always been some form of contempt between Taneth and another trading city, [[Lore:Abah's Landing|Abah's Landing]] and their Merchant Lords, who supposedly benefit from the pirates and bandits that steal from Taneth.{{Ref|name=ONFalorah}} 

===The Thieves Guild and the Iron Wheel===
[[File:ON-npc-Magnifica Falorah.jpg|thumb|right|Falorah al-Danobia; Magnifica of Taneth]]
[[File:ON-quest-Forever Hold Your Peace.jpg|thumb|left|The Wedding in Abah's Landing]]

In the wake of the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]],{{Ref|name=TEG|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Hammerfell}}}} in 2E 560,{{Ref|name=PGETWR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Wild Region}}}} [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]] of [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]] became the High King of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=TEG}} At some point before that back when he was still a prince of [[Lore:Antiphyllos|Antiphyllos]],{{Ref|name=TABKF|{{Cite Book|The All-Beneficent King Fahara'jad}}}}{{Ref|name=TRLOS}} he married the "Flower of Taneth," Za-Rifah al-Danobia, who bore three children, [[Lore:Maraya|Maraya]], [[Lore:Lakana|Lakana]], and the Crown Prince, [[Lore:Azah|Azah]]. She later died from childbirth and was greatly mourned throughout the province.{{Ref|name=TRLOS|{{Cite Book|The Royal Lineage of Sentinel}}}} After the incident, King Fahara'jad and Za-Rifah's younger sister, [[Lore:Falorah al-Danobia|Falorah al-Danobia]] became rather close.{{Ref|name=ONZeira}} When the Bailiffs Guild collapsed in the mid-Second Era, they were soon employed by Magnifica Falorah and rebranded into a private militia, the [[Lore:Iron Wheel|Iron Wheel]].{{Ref|name=MTCCIR|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Chief Inspector Rhanbiq}}}}

Sometime later, the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] of Abah's Landing had attempted an elaborate heist to steal the dowry of Danobia herself in the Al-Danobia Tomb. However, after the heist had ended in failure and the subsequent "death" of the guildmaster, [[Lore:Nicolas|Nicolas]]. Sometime after the incident, Magnifica Falorah was approached by the merchant-lord and proprietor of Hubalajad's Palace, Cosh, who promised to return the dowry that was stolen from her. He pointed her to the city across the bay, Abah's Landing, where the perpetrators were located. Thus she dispatched the Iron Wheel to Hew's Bane, where they began their search for the missing dowry and the Thieves Guild. The other merchant-lords had feared that Taneth wanted to conquer Abah's Landing.{{Ref|name=ONZeira}} 

Not long after, the merchant-lord, Cosh was set to marry the Magnifica at Hubalajad's Palace, however, the current guildmaster, [[Lore:Zeira|Zeira]] exposed Cosh as Nicolas, the former guildmaster and the one that not only stole the dowry but desecrated the tomb. Both guild masters were promptly arrested.{{Ref|name=ONPeace|[[Online:Forever Hold Your Peace|Forever Hold Your Peace]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} Before they would be taken to Taneth for trial, the Thieves Guild had freed Zeira from her prison in [[Lore:No Shira Citadel|No Shira Citadel]] and made peace with the Magnifica.{{Ref|name=ONPrison|[[Online:Prison Break|Prison Break]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} They made a secret deal, one that allowed the Thieves Guild to operate in Taneth while they keep the merchant-lords in check. Both parties had left the ordeal behind in good faith and the Iron Wheel left Hew's Bane with Nicolas in chains. His fate is unknown.{{Ref|name=ONZeira}}

===Taneth in the Third Empire===
[[File:LO-map-Hammerfell (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumb|right|Map of Hammerfell ca. 3E 433]]
The cities of Taneth and Rihad were in the path of the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]], who began an invasion of Western Tamriel in {{Year|3E 249}}. Most of the Redguard locals claimed that the cities would have defeated his armies easily, but when the [[Lore:Battle of Dragontooth|Battle of Dragontooth]] took place in {{Year|3E 253}}, both the cities were sacked relentlessly.{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|8}} Those refugees traveled northward until they eventually came into the Barony of [[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]].{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}} Considering that these were Forebears cities, the Crown warlords of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] did not assist them, and in return, the southern cities did not help defend Elinhir during the [[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]] later in the Third Era, preferring to watch from afar.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Taneth was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Lhezem and it had a rivalry with [[Lore:Gilane|Gilane]] and [[Lore:Rihad|Rihad]].{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Taneth|Taneth]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Years later at the tail-end of the Third Era, King [[Lore:Lhotun|Lhotun]] founded a third political party called the [[Lore:Lhotunics|Lhotunics]] that brought contempt between the Crowns and the Forebears. Queen Clavilla of Taneth tried to have charters of independent guilds revoked from Sentinel, albeit unsuccessfully. Her argument was that accepted worship of [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]] was heresy in the eyes of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]].{{Ref|name=PGE3E}}

When the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] began in {{Year|4E 171}}, the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] landed onto southern Hammerfell and swiftly took control of the region. Roughly a year or two later, every city across southern Hammerfell, including Taneth were sacked and captured by the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], save for [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] to the west. While it is known that Taneth had fallen,{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}} the story of how is currently uncertain. The [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'ri]] Warrior, Kematu claimed that the city would have stood its ground at least had it not been sold out by the noblewoman, Iman of [[Lore:House Suda|House Suda]].  When the noble houses realized her deception, she reportedly fled east into the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where he followed suit.{{Ref|name=SRKematu|[[Skyrim:Kematu|Kematu]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} It is unknown what happened to Taneth after the Great War had ended in {{Year|4E 175}}. Much of southern Hammerfell was ceded from the Third Empire per the agreement in the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], even though there were already Aldmeri soldiers there. Hammerfell's fight against the Dominion ended in {{Year|4E 180}} and while the province was liberated from the elves, the south was left in complete ruin.{{Ref|name=TGW}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:LO-people-Hubalajad.jpg|thumb|left|Prince Hubalajad]]
[[File:ON-banner-Taneth.png|right|150px]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Redguard#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Redguard#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Redguard page.''
Taneth is the seat of power of a kingdom that spans Hammerfell's southern coast and the only known place in the region is the Al-Danobia Tomb.{{Ref|name=ONDanobia}} Its coat of arms depicts a [[Lore:Heron|heron]].{{Ref|name=ONBanner}} In a Taneth-styled [[Lore:Marriage|marriage]], it is a tradition that the groom should be presented to the guests before the wedding.{{Ref|name=ONZeira}}

Beyond the Danobia bloodline, Taneth is the home of several noble families, such as House Suda{{Ref|name=SRSaadia|[[Skyrim:Saadia|Saadia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and the Moguls. The latter is held in high prestige and is famous for its calvary. Their family's totem is the [[Lore:Fennec Fox|Fennec Fox]], a creature that is commonly native in the wastes of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=ONFennec|[[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Pets#Fennec Fox|Fennec Fox]] crown store showcase text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The musk of the Fennec Fox is also very fashionable for nobles across southern Hammerfell cities, such as Taneth.{{Ref|name=ONContraband|[[Online:Contraband B#Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume|Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Like other cities on the Hammerfell coast, the Redguards of Taneth observe the 20th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] as the [[Lore:Day of Shame|Day of Shame]]. According to legend, the [[Lore:Crimson Ship|Crimson Ship]], a vessel full of [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]] victims of the Knahaten Flu first left [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] on the 9th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]] and was denied refuge from every port on Tamriel. It was later westbound for the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] and it was never seen again. It is said to one day return and the Day of Shame was created in remembrance of that.{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|7}}{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Shame|Day of Shame]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Taneth and nearby Abah's Landing, or more specifically Hew's Bane has a connection with each other and often influence each other. Trade going to Taneth, either through the mainland or through the sea has always been affected by the merchant lords of Abah's Landing. They tend to hire pirates or bandits to interfere in the traffic in Taneth.{{Ref|name=ONFalorah}} There is an oft-repeated expression from Taneth for wasting money, "laying foundation with [[Lore:Hubalajad|Hubalajad]]'s coin", which is in reference to the eponymous prince's unfortunate history. One of Taneth's most esteemed historians was Lady [[Lore:Cinnabar|Cinnabar]], who wrote about many topics but also wrote the book, "[[Lore:In Defense of Prince Hubalajad|In Defense of Prince Hubalajad]]".{{Ref|name=IDOPH|{{Cite Book|In Defense of Prince Hubalajad}}}}

Taneth is located on a major western trading route utilized by the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]]. Through this route, it is connected to the cities Rihad and Hegathe.{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|East Empire Company (4th Era Map) – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} The city is the home of a guild of bodyguards called the Iron Wheel, which was formerly a prominent branch of the Bailiffs Guild. In its prime, it overshadowed the branch in Sentinel and it rebranded into what it is now after the Imperial troubles.{{Ref|name=MTCCIR}}
