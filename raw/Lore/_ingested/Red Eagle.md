[[Lore:Red Eagle|Red Eagle]], or '''Faolan''' in the [[Lore:Reach-tongue|language of the Reach]], also known as '''King of the Reach''',{{ref|name=TRC}} '''Lord of the Reach''',{{ref|name=TLORE}} '''High Lord of the Reach''',{{ref|name=RER}} and '''Diamond's Doom'''{{Ref|name=RC|[[ON:Ragebound Crate|Ragebound Crate]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CSS|[[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Crown Crates#Ragebound Crown Crate Season|Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase]] of [[ON:Ragebound Crate|Ragebound Crown Crate Season]]}} was an ancient king who, armed with a flaming sword, rallied his people and drove back the armies of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=LL|{{Cite Book|Lost Legends}}}} Over time, accounts of Red Eagle have become distorted and embellished, but they seem to be based on truth: the [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] chronicles of Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] mention a rebel leader who was eventually cornered and slain in battle around the year {{Year|1E 1030}}, at the cost of a full legion of men.{{ref|name=LL}}{{ref|name=TLORE|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Red Eagle}}}} Curiously, stories of a similar king and his legendary blade appear in other ancient myths of the [[Lore:The Reach|Reach]].{{ref|name=TLORE}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Rebel King of the Reachfolk===
[[File:ON-misc-Briarheart Ritual 02.png|Briarheart ritual|thumb|left]]
Red Eagle was born in the [[Lore:Sundered Hills|Sundered Hills]] and named for the screeching bird-call that greeted his birth, as well as the crimson blooms on the autumn hills. The augurs foresaw the boy's destiny as a warrior without peer who would unite the ten warring kingdoms of the Reach under his name. His people expanded and prospered until Hestra and her armies arrived; one by one, the kings of the Reach swore fealty or fell to her. When Red Eagle refused to yield, the scared elders cast him out.{{ref|name=TLORE}}

During this exile, Faolan came across a mysterious and powerful [[Lore:Hagraven|hagraven]], also known as the Hag in early works,{{Ref|name=TTWOT4|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, IV}}}} who offered him the power to free his people in exchange for his heart. Red Eagle had to clear his mind before he could embrace his destiny and filled his heart with the thing he loved the most - his people.{{ref|name=ESORedEagle}} He accepted the offer and became the first [[Lore:Briarheart|Briarheart]],{{ref|name=Antiquity|[[Online:Red Eagle Cave Painting|Red Eagle Cave Painting]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} becoming a spirit of vengeance, pitiless, and beyond remorse and bringing an onslaught of violence and death to the occupying Imperials.{{ref|name=TLORE}}

In the years that followed, the exiled Red Eagle became known as the untamed spirit of the Reach, unbowed, unbroken, stained by the blood of his foes. He wielded the powerful flaming blade called [[Lore:Red Eagle's Fury|Red Eagle's Fury]].{{ref|name=ESORedEagle|[[ON:Red Eagle's Song (quest)|Red Eagle's Song]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[SR:Red Eagle's Fury|Red Eagle's Fury]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} He gathered [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] who clung to the old ways and yearned for freedom to forge a new nation. The rebels grew in strength and numbers, and none could stand against them; after two years, they had all but succeeded in driving the foreigners out of the Reach.{{ref|name=TLORE}}

===Restoration of Shehai===
At some point in his life after he accepted his fate as a Briarheart he was visited by the Yokudan sword-saint [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]] who lost his ability to manifest his [[Lore:Shehai|Shehai]]. He traveled to the Reach due to the tales of a legendary warrior wielding a flaming blade. Ultimately Faolan agreed to help the Ansei and taught him how to reclaim his Shehai in the Sacred Cave in [[Lore:Red Eagle Redoubt|Red Eagle Redoubt]] by focusing on what he loved most. For Faolan it was his people, for Rada - the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]].{{ref|name=ESORedEagle}} The knowledge bestowed by Faolan allowed Rada to "reforge his Shehai in bitter blood". This process permanently turned Rada's spirit sword bloody red.{{ref|[[ON:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]]'s dialogue during the [[ON:Kingdom of Ash|Kingdom of Ash]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Last Stand===
{{Quote Box|style=float:right;|''Faolan, the Red Eagle, last drew his blade at sunset. Naked was his breast and arm and head. The light of his eyes dimmed, the hagraven's due.<br>But the blade burned bright, as did the fury left to him. And he faced the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|legions]] that bid him kneel.<br>No ring or steel could turn his blade. Men were hewn through as reeds in wind. Red Eagle's flesh wept blood, blackened and thick with sorcery.<br>Blade and arrow found him, time and again. Faolan did not fight to win. His aim was punishment.<br>The sun rose over the field of dead. Red Eagle stood, though no life was left to him.<br>The tomb was opened and the eagle flew no longer.''|Reach [[Lore:Vateshrans|vateshran]] story about Faolan.{{ref|[[Online:Vateshran Calbokh|Vateshran Calbokh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}}}
Faolan and his followers engaged in an extended guerrilla campaign which, while tactically successful, was not enough to stem the seemingly endless waves of Imperial reinforcements. Hestra's generals returned with an even bigger invasion force and laid siege to Red Eagle's stronghold until he himself came forth for battle, alone and naked, and purportedly killed a thousand soldiers before they brought him down.{{ref|name=TLORE}} Red Eagle was taken to a prepared tomb, and with his remaining strength, he presented his sword to his people, and swore an oath: Fight on, and when at last the Reach is free, his blade should be returned, that he might rise and lead them again.{{ref|name=TLORE}} 

Mortally wounded{{ref|name=TLORE|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Red Eagle}}}} or dead{{ref|name=TTWOT4|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, IV}}}} Red Eagle was carried by the weeping cortege up the crag at dawn. Reachfolk followed with pots of resin and ash in order to prepare his body for burial. Ashes were scattered to lay upon his body which was exposed to the chiefs of numerous clans. The ceremony was interrupted by Hag who took the briarheart from Faolan's chest despite the protests of the attendants. Once she left, the ceremony continued, and he was carried deep into the cave in the heart of the mountain, which was prepared as a tomb, and was then "laid bare for the last time in sleepless rest". The stone was sealed, wax poured and flints shattered.{{ref|name=TTWOT4}} After the burial numerous traps were set to dissuade intruders and grave robbers. The resting place seemed unadorned at first, but in the deeper parts of the tomb, the rippled rock walls were replaced by ornate man-made walls of hewn stone and covered with chiseled murals depicting scenes of Red Eagle's final battle. Despite the rich ornamentation of the room and later parts of the corridor that led to it, the sarcophagus placed on the dais in the central part of the chamber was simple and unadorned.{{ref|name=TRC}}{{Ref|name=RCS|[[Skyrim:Rebel's Cairn|Rebel's Cairn]]'s location in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Faolan was buried with his legendary weapon — Red Eagle's Bane, which was put on a pedestal near to his body.{{ref|name=TRC|{{Cite Book|The Red Curse}}}}

===Legacy===
[[File:ON-npc-Bjora.jpg|thumb|left|Bjora, the only known direct descendant of Faolan]]
[[File:ON-tribute-patron-Red Eagle.png|thumb|right|Tales of Tribute token bearing the image of the Faolan]]
At some point before {{Year|2E 582}} Red Eagle's tomb was visited by a Breton scholar of the Red Eagle's legacy Dettethor Pantenne. As soon as he reached the entrance to the tomb he felt drawn by the flittering whispers and distant horns, calling him forth. He eventually reached the burial chamber and gazed in awe at the legendary sword of the Faolan. Attracted by it Pantenne took the blade's handle to his hands and then he heard the voice of Faolan himself. Red Eagle's convinced him with visions and promises of power to place the sword in the sarcophagus alongside his body. After a while, the Reachfolk king awakened from his slumber in a skeletal form. Scholar's mind was filled with terrific visions of destruction. In a rush of adrenaline, Dettethor managed to steal the blade from the freshly awakened Faolan and ran away towards the exit of the tomb. At some point of his escape, he lost the blade and triggered a mechanism that sealed the tomb.{{ref|name=TRC}}

By the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Red Eagle's last known descendant was [[Lore:Bjora|Bjora]], of Karth. Taken as a baby by the Nords of Karth during a raid on a Reachman camp, Bjora was raised as a Nord, in ignorance of her true heritage, and would go on to help them establish the settlement of [[Lore:Karthwatch|Karthwatch]].{{ref|name=Helsi|[[Online:Helsi|Helsi]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In 2E 582, Bjora was kidnapped by the [[Lore:Icereach Coven|Icereach Coven]], who believed she was prophesied to lead as their Queen. After destroying Karthwatch in a Harrowstorm, they brought her outside Sundered Towers and began using magic to implant memories in her, including a vision of Red Eagle handing her a crown of briars while [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|a frightening, fanged man]] declared that she would lead his armies.{{ref|[[Online:Bjora|Bjora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The ritual was ultimately interrupted by her childhood friend, Hafskjar, assisted by [[Lore:The_Vestige|the Vestige]].{{ref|[[Online:The Blood of Old Karth|The Blood of Old Karth quest in ESO]]}}

He was remembered not only as a great warrior and a hero but also as a great teacher by the Vateshrans of the Reach.{{ref|name=Vateshran Ovra|[[Online:Vateshran Ovra|Vateshran Ovra]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Although it is unknown if Red Eagle's Fury was a manifestation of Shehai or not, his teachings allowed another Redguard swordmaster, [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]] to achieve his lifelong ambition of summoning his Spirit Sword by putting aside his devotion to duty and focusing on what he loved—in his case, his companion [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]].{{ref|name=ESORedEagle}}

As of {{Year|2E 582}} allegedly some of the charred pieces of armor from Imperial soldiers defeated by Red Eagle and his legendary flaming sword were found across Tamriel.{{ref|name=ContrabandC|[[ON:Contraband C#Charred Battle Trophy|Charred Battle Trophy]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

One of the pieces of the [[Lore:Tales of Tribute|Tales of Tribute]], a popular card game originating from the [[Lore:Systres Archipelago|Systres Archipelago]] played continent-wide during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] in [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]{{ref|name=TSOT|{{Cite Book|The Secret Origins of Tribute|ns_base=Online}}}} bore the image of Red Eagle. A deck themed after the Reachfolk was part of the game.{{ref|name=TOTSO|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Tales of Tribute: Series One}}}}

By the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], a descendant offshoot of the Reachmen, the [[Lore:Forsworn|Forsworn]], drew several parallels to the struggles of Red Eagle and his oppression under an occupying force. As such, many of them extolled his teachings and practices, and looked up to him as their savior. They planned to resurrect him through the ancient ritual.{{ref|name=RER|{{Cite Book|Red Eagle's Rite|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] plundered Red Eagle's tomb and killed the undead being he had become, claiming his mighty sword.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of [[Skyrim:Rebel's Cairn (quest)|Rebel's Cairn]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
