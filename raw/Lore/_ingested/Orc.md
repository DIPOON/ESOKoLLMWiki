==Origins==

According to various accounts, the Orcish race was created when their patron god [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] was transformed by [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]]. He became [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] (or Mauloch) and his faithful elven followers became the cursed folk, or 'Orsimer', and were henceforth known as Orcs.{{ref|name=VoF:TO}}{{ref|name=TTNOC}}{{ref|name=FOTN}}{{ref|[[Castles:Orc|Orc]] [[Castles:Races|racial]] description in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}

One Orcish account from the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] is that Trinimac was enraged by [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] and his cult trespassing on Tamriel during their exodus, and challenged his patron Boethiah to a duel. Just as Trinimac was about to succeed, a second Prince, [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], interfered and stabbed Trinimac in the back. Boethiah used the opportunity to curse Trinimac, transforming both him and his followers as well as casting him into a [[Lore:Ashpit|place of choking air and ash]]. There, Trinimac was reborn in his blood and rage and became Malacath, the God of Curses.{{ref|name=MOF|{{Cite Book|Mauloch, Orc-Father}}}} 

The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] account instead starts  with Trinimac spreading "falsehoods" about [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], drawing Boethiah's ire. With another difference being Boethiah triumphs over Trinimac alone by tricking him into his mouth and devouring him whole. He proceeds to take Trinimac's form and speak with his voice, and together with Mephala showed the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] the rules of the [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]]. Once he finished, he relieved himself of Trinimac there on the spot, having tortured his spirit in her belly. That excrement became Malacath, and his followers became the Orcs.{{ref|name=tCO|{{Cite Book|The Changed Ones}}}}{{ref|name=THOT}}{{ref|name=VoF:TO}}{{ref|name=TFoT|{{Cite Book|The Fall of Trinimac}}}} One version of this story claims the followers of Boethiah and Trinimac rubbed the "soil of Trinimac" upon themselves and changed their skins.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Civility and Etiquette: Wood Orcs I}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Some particularly bigoted [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] described Orcs as being the twisted remains of the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]. Calling them "the feculence of Boethiah" and "leavings so loathsome that they had to sculpt their own dung-god from filth to give their existence any meaning."{{ref|name=AltmerRacist|{{Cite Book|Corruption of the Blood}}}}

In a version of the tale told to children in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], Trinimac, described as the greatest knight of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] and the champion of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], sought out Boethiah to chastise him for his misdeeds. The Daedric Prince knew he was coming and would first disguise himself as an old woman, who told Trinimac their younger brother knew where Boethiah was, and that she would tell him where he was if he scratched her back, covered in boils. Trinimac did so and then Boethiah would disguise as an old man, the old man told Trinimac his younger sister knew where Boethiah was, and would tell him if he washed his feet. Trinimac would wash the old man's disgusting feet, Boethiah would then take the form of a beautiful young woman. Trinimac dreaded meeting with the younger sister, but was relieved when he saw the beautiful woman, who said she would tell Trinimac where Boethiah was in exchange for a kiss. Trinimac gladly agreed only for Boethiah to open her mouth extremely wide as he leaned into kiss her. Boethiah would swallow Trinimac in a single gulp. Boethiah then took Trinimac's form, and made him make a fool of himself. Boethiah would then squeeze him out as a massive pile of dung, which would get up and slunk away in shame. The once great knight was transformed into the Prince Malacath, and all those who loved him were changed as well, becoming the Orcs. A disguised Malacath, when told of this account, derided the story as being too "literal-minded".{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|Part 1, Prologue}}

An account told by the Orcs of the [[Lore:Bloodfall Kingdom|Bloodfall Kingdom]] in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] differs slightly from previous tellings. In it, Boethiah had become jealous of the adoration Trinimac received. Boethiah, knowing she wasn't a match for Trinimac, tricked her Velothi followers into spreading blasphemous ideas. This angered Trinimac and his priests, so he confronted Boethiah, but Mephala stabbed Trinimac in the back. Boethiah would open her jaw until she was all mouth, and swallow Trinimac. She trapped him inside her and assumed his form. Then, she appeared before Trinimac's priests and humiliated them. She excreted Trinimac before them and cursed him and all his followers.{{ref|name=BVTH}}

||{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''And Boethiah proclaimed, "Hearken unto me, Ornim! For yes, I know you as Ornim even if your chief does not." And this caused the followers of Trinimac to quieten, for the Orsimer in that time had been so convinced they were Elves that they had forgotten their days of stone and wood, sea and fire.''|''[[Lore:From Exile to Exodus|From Exile to Exodus]]''}} In contrast to the accounts of elven origins for the Orcs, several sources cast doubt on these claims and paint them as beastfolk or betmer.{{ref|name=MWGenBetmeri|[[MW:Generic Dialogue B#Betmeri|Generic dialogue]] on the topic of '''Betmeri''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=MWOrcD|[[MW:Orc|Orc]] [[MW:Races|racial]] description in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=TDB}} These sources claim that in the early Merethic Era the ancestors of Orcs along with other aboriginal beastfolk lived in Tamriel, predating the Velothi exodus.{{ref|name=BTAOM|}}
Some even claim the Orcs of the Wrothgarian Mountains lived there as early as the late Dawn Era.{{ref|name=MOC|{{Cite Book|Map of Clans|Map of Clans}}}}  Whether or not they were always there, the Orcs are said to have eternally felt compelled to return "home" to the Wrothgarian Mountains.{{ref|group=UOL|[https://www.imperial-library.info/content/ted-peterson-high-rock-and-summerset-legendary-edition Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Legendary Edition — The Imperial Library]}} Specific clans such as the Wood Orcs, were also said to already reside in Valenwood when elves arrived on Tamriel from Aldmeris.{{Ref|name=ESODra'bulLoad|[[Online:Dra'bul|Dra'bul]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In fact, the earliest known Aldmeri explorer of Tamriel, [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]] recounted encountering supposed cannibal Orcs in southern High Rock during his travels. Scholars analyzing this text have pondered if perhaps the elven laced narrative of the Velothi exodus was wrong, and that the Orcs were an aboriginal tribe predating the Aldmeri colonization.{{ref|name=FOTN|}} 

One Dunmeri (or Chimeri) account of the Trinimac legend makes a claim that Orcs had possibly been deceived or confused, claiming they had been thoroughly convinced they were elves, to the point of forgetting their days of stone, wood, and fire. This version of Orcish origins is presented in the myth detailed in ''[[Lore:From Exile to Exodus|From Exile to Exodus]]'' by Tarvyn Aram. In this account, during the Velothi exodus, the Velothi came across the followers of Trinimac, who outnumbered them three to one. While Trinimac remained silent, his followers called the dissidents rebels, traitors, and filth. Boethiah then intervened, revealing the truth about the followers' identity as Ornim, who had been deceived by a demon's curses into thinking they were elves, not Ornim. Boethiah then gestured with her hands, forming a triangular sign, revealing the path to achieve an Exodus. As the attendees witnessed this, the veil was lifted from their eyes. They no longer saw Trinimac but Malak, the King of Curses. Similarly, Boethiah appeared as Trinimac, as she had always been, the Warrior of East and West, the one who bore the burden of separating [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|divinity]] from her [[Lore:Lorkhan|beloved]]. Enraged, Malak cursed and attacked Boethiah by entering her mouth, causing her to collapse in agony, retching blood and ichor. Thus Boethiah-Trinimac collapsed, seemingly lifeless and her followers despaired. However, Mephala and Azura intervened, aiding Boethiah in casting out Malacath and his curse.{{Ref|name=FETE|}} 

Malak rose covered in Boethiah's blood and bile. Yet he now appeared different, not as the wretched Malak King of Curses, but as something more akin to the Trinimac his followers had loved. He now wore new armor and held a gleaming red axe, and his helm bore a tusked visage of the spurned and the opressed. Malacath growled that Boethiah had forgotten what it means to be an exile, and uttered a terrible curse that split the sky and earth and engulfed the area in storm and fire, the land they were on itself drifting into the sea.{{Ref|name=FETE|}} 

The followers were divided among the Daedric Princes, with Boethiah ultimately transporting the remaining followers to a [[Lore:Ashpit|deadened realm of smoke and ash]] in the Oblivion. It was here that the worst of Malacath's fatal curse had been diverted by Boethiah. Malacath laughed that Boethiah had let him destroy her gift from the [[Lore:Auri-El|king]], that had been an idyllic paradise. There, Boethiah confronted Malacath once more, defeating him, and guiding the Orcs to find strength through adversity. With a single precise strike, Malacath was cleaved into the ashen wastes, his bones forming a new foundation. His blood mingled with the ash and soon the realm was his. Boethiah then spent time with the surviving followers, their skins changing under her teachings and the influence of the realm. When Malacath's spirit reappeared, Boethiah addressed him, urging him to seek glory through struggle and to understand the Will Against Rule, rather than attempting to enforce his will through curses he and his followers knew to be untrue. She then formed a powerful sign with her hands, encapsulating Malacath and his followers into a singular sphere, casting it back onto the mountains of Nirn. Together, Boethiah and Mauloch tested their chosen peoples, leading them toward an Exodus.{{Ref|name=FETE}}

Orcs are often referred to as Goblin-ken, an umbrella term that consists of other races such as [[Lore:Ogre|Ogres]], [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]], and [[Lore:Gremlin|Gremlins]].{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}}{{ref|name=BrC}}{{Ref|name=MoM}}{{ref|name=TTNOC}} Beyond this mere classification, some sources speak to a specifically close relation between Orcs and Goblins.{{ref|name=APG|[[Books:Arena Player's Guide|Arena Player's Guide]] description of [[Arena:Orc|Orcs]]}}{{ref|name=GF|[[Books:Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel|Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel, The Goblin Filth, Grutbug the Unhealthy]]}}{{ref|name=EGE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}}  Some of the beliefs of people who were bigoted against Orcs connect them to Goblin-ken and other creatures in especially negative ways. The [[Lore:Winterborn|Winterborn]] tribe of [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] believe that Malacath created Orcs, along with Ogres and [[Lore:Troll|trolls]], not as his children, but to provide hardships and to test his chosen followers.{{ref|name=MATR|{{Cite Book|Malacath and the Reach}}}} [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] believed that Orcs, along with Goblins and trolls, were among "the miscreated", malicious and brutish creatures who, as [[Lore:The One|the One]] teaches, must be united against by all sentient folk of good will instead of striving against each other.{{ref|name=RB|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 3}}}}

It is unknown which origins are true, if either is fully true, or if the truth lies somewhere in spurts between both accounts. The blood of the Orcs, along with the blood of the Altmer, Bosmer, Dunmer and Falmer serves as a facsimile of Dwemer blood, possibly validating their elven nature.{{ref|name=DTT|[[SR:Discerning the Transmundane|Discerning the Transmundane]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} One aspect that muddles things is that while some stories claim that Trinimac's duel with Boethiah occurred in the late [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]],{{ref|name=TTNOC}}{{ref|name=TA}} Saint Veloth's exodus to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], which preempted it, was safely within the Merethic Era.{{ref|name=VtP|{{Cite Book|Veloth the Pilgrim}}}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}} 

In some cases even a source that appears to lend credence to one side, also potentially supports the other. In the aforementioned Exile to Exodus text, despite referencing the generally Elven leaning Velothi Exodus origins for Orcish people, in the same text it calls into question the truth of them being Elves or not.{{Ref|name=FETE}} On the other hand, the translator of the aforementioned writings of Topal the Pilot, despite referencing Orcs in High Rock before Elven arrival, also provides an explanation that leaves room for the "Orcs" in question to possibly refer to a separate cursed folk.{{ref|name=FOTN|}} 

The various races of Tamriel seem to have no clear consensus, with various outsider sources inconsistently painting the Orcish people as both Beastfolk, and Elven in nature. The Orcs themselves, despite religious schisms and other disagreements, provide very few definitive claims on their nature one way or another. Those Orcs that do believe in Elven origins for themselves suggest that Trinimac absorbed some of Boethiah's strength and passed it on to his followers. In this way the Orsimer can be seen as "improved Elves."{{ref|name=VoF:TO|}}{{Ref|name=MoM}} Conversely, a book that spoke to a very pro Elvish origins story for the Orcs was banned by the Daggerfall Covenant of the Interregnum and labeled as vile, anti-Orc propaganda, showing that some Orcs are staunchly pro Beastfolk origins as well.{{ref|name=TTNOC|}}

Some prominent Orcs who worshipped Trinimac agreed with the premise of their race being deceived. Namely two of the most prominent Orcish kings in history, [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog gro-Orsinium]] and [[Lore:Gortwog|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]], believed Malacath was a liar who had misled the Orcs and instead emphasized Trinimac worship during their reigns claiming that Trinimac was a separate entity entirely and still alive.{{ref|name=Kurog|[[ON:King Kurog|King Kurog]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PGE3O|}} These beliefs spread among the Orcish people and persisted well into the Fourth Era, albeit as heresy.{{ref|name=BVTH|[[Blades:Villager#The Heretic|Villager Dialogue]] during [[Blades:The Heretic|The Heretic]] in [[Blades:Blades|Blades]]}}

==History==
===Merethic Era===
Orcs claim they have lived in Skyrim since before the Nords came, and that Malacath blessed the lands solely for them.{{Ref|name=OSGD|[[Skyrim:Generic Dialogue#Orc Strongholds Dialogue|Orc Strongholds Generic Dialogue]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=OOS}}
===First Era===
The Orcish people were ostracized and persecuted for much of their early history. Many ancient [[Lore:Cursed Legion|Orcish champions]] fiercely defended whatever borders they could carve out, such as [[Lore:Gromma the Hairless|Gromma the Hairless]] and [[Lore:Emmeg Gro-Kayra|Emmeg Gro-Kayra]], the son of Malacath who defended the clan of [[Lore:Normar Heights|Normar Heights]], near present-day [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]].{{Ref|name=AOMXII|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness, v. XII}}}}{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}} Another tribe of Orcs were known to have lived around {{Lore Link|Lake Halcyon}} before being displaced by [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] refugees from [[Lore:Nenalata|Nenalata]] between {{Year|1E 375}} and {{Year|1E 452}}.{{ref|name=ALOSAS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}} Many other tribes chose to remain in, or even underneath, the Wrothgarian mountains of High Rock, such as Clan Morkul.{{ref|name=ESOMorkulLoad|[[Online:Morkul Descent|Morkul Descent]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} Many other orcs chose to settle elsewhere. For example, the Wood Orcs chose to remain within Valenwood, while Orcish raiding parties had settled as far east as Morrowind and as far as the northern wastes near [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]] of Skyrim, where they clashed with Chimer and Atmorans.{{ref|name=TRN|{{Cite Book|The Real Nerevar}}}}{{ref|name=TTNOC}}

Sometime between {{Year|1E 668}} and {{Year|1E 700}} High King [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]] made peace with the Orcs, who centuries before counted among his enemies, and forged an alliance between the two races in order to bolster his forces. The combined Nordic and Orcish army clashed against the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemeri]] armies at the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]].{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}

During the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]] [[Lore:War of the Singers|War of the Singers]], Orcs were part of the outcast and brigand army of [[Lore:Hira|Emperor Hira]] that sought to eliminate the [[Lore:Sword-singers|Sword-singers]].{{ref|name=RTHATH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}}
[[File:LO-map-Orsinium (Oblivion Codex).jpg|left|thumb|[[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], the home of the Orcs]]
After the rulers of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] set free the enslaved betmer during the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] of the First Era, many Orcs and other goblin-ken fled north to High Rock after being allowed to settle north of Valenwood. This sudden influx of clans and people led directly to the founding of the first Kingdom of Orsinium by [[Lore:Torug gro-Igron|Torug gro-Igron]] in the Wrothgarian Mountains.{{ref|name=PGE1W}} The exact dating of this event is unclear, but various pieces of evidence place the founding of Orsinium in the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.{{ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}{{ref|name=BSHK}}{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}} No matter what, Orcs had no province of their own and were thus viewed as a constant threat to the other races, especially once the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] would arrive and further displaced many Orcish clans of Hammerfell.{{ref|name=PGE1W}}

In {{Year|1E 874}}, when Warlord {{Lore Link|Thulgeg}}'s army of Orcs and [[Lore:Goblin|Goblin]]s was driven from [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] by the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s, the Bretons denied them access to [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]] and forced them to flee northeast, trudging all the way through the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]] before they finally reached [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]].{{ref|name=BSHK|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}}{{Ref|name=TMARCH|{{Cite Book|Thulgeg's March}}}} Tensions only continued to fray as Orcish raids on human settlements increased in frequency, despite Orsinium's subversive actions angering Malacath.{{ref|name=MalDia|[[Online:Malacath|Malacath]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} In the tenth century of the First Era, King [[Lore:Golkarr|Golkarr]] of the Orcs amassed an army and was determined to seize control of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]],{{ref|name=TGSO|{{Cite Book|The Great Siege of Orsinium}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1W}} upon which the nomadic Breton [[Lore:River Horse Bretons|Bjoulsae River Tribes]] lived.{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Mirror}}}} They intended to force Wayrest to pay for regular use the river for travel and trade.{{ref|name=PGE1W}}
[[File:ON-interior-Honor's Rest 03.jpg|thumb|right|Baloth Bloodtusk and Gaiden Shinji, assassinated by King Joile's general during the Siege of Orsinium]]
In {{Year|1E 948}}, [[Lore:Joile|King Joile]] of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] used the extremely antagonistic actions of the Orcs to his advantage, forming an alliance with the Order of Diagna and [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]]. Their combined might laid waste to the first Kingdom of Orsinium, which had long since lost the favor of Malacath.{{ref|name=MalDia}} This event became known as the very first [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]]{{ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}, though the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] claim to have actually been the first to sack the city.{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}} After driving the Orcs out of High Rock, Joile would go on to betray his allies and attempt to invade [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=FTMSoML|{{Cite Book|From The Memory Stone of Makela Leki}}}}

===Second Era===
The concept of Orsinium still endured in Orcish society, however, and a second Orsinium was later accepted as a territory of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] during the reign of the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]] [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]].{{ref|name=PGE1W}}{{ref|name=ON}}{{ref|name=RM8TO|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 8: Orc Style}}}}{{ref|name=HKEAYQ|{{Cite Book|High King Emeric Answers Your Questions}}}} This only lasted until the assassination of Savirien-Chorak in {{Year|2E 430}}, after which Orsinium lost its status and protection as an Imperial territory. Shortly thereafter, the city was sacked in {{Year|2E 431}} by an assault spearheaded by the Bretons of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]].{{ref|name=TRHOKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1W}}{{ref|name=TFoKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}}

The Bretons once again seized control of the land and scattered the Orcs across northern Tamriel.{{ref|name=GttDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=OOS|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Skyrim}}}} Some Orcs chose to retreat to their strongholds in northern Wrothgar, while others set out to reassert ancient territorial claims in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. Chief [[Lore:Yashnag gro-Yazgu|Yashnag gro-Yazgu]] in particular succeeded in carving out a chiefdom in western [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath]] that persisted until {{Year|2E 467}}, when Yashnag and his champions were slain by [[Lore:Hakkvild Yashnag-Slayer|Hakkvild Yashnag-Slayer]], the Jarl of Falkreath, in a ritual of single-combat.{{ref|name=OOS}} 

Orc warriors at one point raided [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] and plundered a legendary [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] staff named [[Lore:Fenmender|Fenmender]]. Centuries later, it would become a source of conflict between the [[Lore:Cyrodilic Collections|Cyrodilic Collections]] who sought the return of Argonian antiquities to their rightful owners in Black Marsh, and the House of Orsimer Glories who saw to store it in their museum as they considered it a piece of Orcish history.{{ref|name=HRGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''High Rock Gazetteer''}}{{rp|21}}

In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Orsinium was controlled by the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], and was given to [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog gro-Bagrakh]]'s clan as a reward for helping King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] defeat [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]], the king of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]]. Kurog would end up becoming the king of Orsinium, which subsequently joined the Second Daggerfall Covenant. {{ref|name=GttDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=TFoKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}} The Orcs aiding King Emeric pushed Ranser over the edge, and he ordered his Spymaster to round up many Orcs in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] to be interrogated and executed. The Spymaster murdered hundreds of innocent Orcs during the course of [[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]] as a result.{{ref|name=Marisette| [[On:Marisette|Marisette]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} {{ref|name=Gerard|[[On:Gerard Althen|Gerard Althen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Race relations between the Bretons and Orcs improved during the Covenant's existence,{{ref|name=OCBW|{{Cite Book|Orcs? Could Be Worse}}}} despite the persistence of anti-Orc sentiment.{{ref|name=OTVMU|{{Cite Book|Orcs: The Vermin Among Us}}}} King Kurog himself was brought down by internal Orcish politics - largely of the making of his own mother, playing off the religious extremist factions of Trinimac and Malacath and culminating in an attempt to massacre other Orcish chieftains and frame Kurog's former friend turned critic, Bazrag gro-Fharun: the latter in fact was entirely innocent of any plotting, and was chosen by the surviving Orcish chieftains as King of Orsinium to replace the fallen Kurog. Despite his own distaste for city life and preference for the traditions of independent strongholds with no central ruler, Bazrag accepted the title, kept to Kurog's international agreements and remained part of the Daggerfall Covenant.{{ref|name=ON}}

With the rise of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] and the establishment of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], there was a major negative shift in the view on Orcs. Tiber Septim had a famous hatred of Orcs, he viewed them as lesser beings and denied them citizenship within the Third Empire.{{ref|name=RB}}{{ref|name=PGE1W}}

==Society==
[[File:SR-place-Dushnikh Yal.jpg|thumb|left|An Orcish Stronghold]][[File:ON-crown store-Orc Wise Woman's Vestment.jpg|right|thumb|Orc Wise Woman]]
Even though Orsinium is considered the home of the Orcs, they are known to have settlements all around Tamriel. At the time of Kurog's rule, the whole of [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]] was dotted with Orc strongholds. The isle of [[Lore:Betony|Betony]] was inhabited by the [[Lore:Seamount Clan|Seamount]] Orcs in the mid-Second Era. The Orcs often settled in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], founding strongholds like [[Lore:Dushnikh Yal|Dushnikh Yal]], [[Lore:Largashbur|Largashbur]], [[Lore:Narzulbur|Narzulbur]], [[Lore:Mor Khazgur|Mor Khazgur]], [[Lore:Cradlecrush|Cradlecrush]] and [[Lore:Grezogbur‎|Grezogbur‎]].{{ref|name=ONHMB|[[Online:Hunt-Master Bulozog|Hunt-Master Bulozog]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}} The height of Orcish power in Skyrim came in the mid-Second Era when [[Lore:Yashnag gro-Yazgu|Yashnag gro-Yazgu]] established an entire chiefdom in [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]] before he was killed.{{ref|name=OOS}} Many tribes inhabit [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], known collectively as the [[Lore:Wood Orcs|Wood Orcs]], whereas [[Lore:Iron Orcs|Iron Orcs]] inhabit the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]], particularly the region known as [[Lore:Upper Craglorn|Upper Craglorn]].{{ref|name=SR|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Orcs of the Wrothgarian Mountains are known as the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Orcs|Wrothgarian Orcs]].{{ref|name=Orzorga}} In the northern hills of Morrowind, there are the [[Lore:Mountain Orcs|Mountain Orcs]],{{ref|name=THOT}} and Malahk-Orcs.{{Ref|name=IWADE|{{Cite Book|Interview with a Dark Elf|ns_base=GEN}}}}

The Orcs have considered themselves outcasts like their deity Malacath, and this is reflected in much of their culture. For instance, unlike other races, the Orc tradition is to have weddings take place at midnight.{{ref|name=TWWD|{{Cite Book|The Wraith's Wedding Dowry}}}} Traditional Orcish society is centered around stronghold settlements, each one striving for complete independence in all matters. Orc society is based on polygyny—a stronghold's tribe is controlled by a single chieftain: no other males are permitted to take wives or father children. The chieftain is replaced by whichever one of his sons grows strong enough to challenge and kill him. The strength of a tribe (and the favor granted to it by Malacath) depends heavily on the personal strength of the chieftain.{{ref|name=TCOM|{{Cite Book|The Code of Malacath}}}}

The law of the Orcs is based on the [[Lore:Code of Malacath|Code of Malacath]], an unwritten law. The Code prohibits things such as theft and unwarranted violence, but also places heavy emphasis on honor and personal strength in resolving conflicts. Orcs do not use imprisonment as punishment: those who violate the code must either pay material compensation or pay a "blood price", which entails allowing the one they offended to spill their blood until the price has been satisfied.{{ref|name=TCOM}} Some Orcs use a scale for weighing out blood price amounts, with different gold weights labeled "Chief", "Wife", and "Warrior".{{ref|name=ContScale|[[Online:Contraband J|Judicial Blood Price Gold Scale]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In other cases, some Orcs keep a detailed record of all of the grievances and insults their family has suffered, with dates, specifics, and planned responses.{{ref|name=ContLedger|[[Online:Contraband O|Orcish Ledger of Significant Grudges]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Orc women are expected to contribute to the strength of the stronghold just as men, as warriors, hunters, or herbalists. Traditionally, the Orcish art of smithing is done by the women, and a chieftain's second wife is called the forgewife for this reason. The chieftain's first wife is known as the huntswife and is responsible for patrolling the stronghold and providing the stronghold with game. Other roles for wives include the hearthwife, responsible for more domestic duties in the stronghold and shieldwives, who can serve more as bodyguards to the chieftain. A wise woman handles a tribe's spiritual matters and healing needs. Some clans, such as the Kalmur, physically represent the bonds and memories of their stronghold as a "[[Lore:Totem Spirit|totem spirit]]". This spirit is protected by the clan wise woman, who will in turn commune with it for advice when necessary.{{ref|name=Khazasha| [[On:Khazasha|Khazasha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} However, Orcish society is still very patriarchal. All stronghold women are either the wives or daughters of chieftains, with the exception of the wise women, who are typically the mothers of chieftains.{{ref|name=TCOM}}{{Ref|name=OSGD|[[Skyrim:Generic Dialogue#Orc Strongholds Dialogue|Orc Strongholds Generic Dialogue]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Daughters of the chieftain are usually traded to other strongholds to marry other chieftains.

[[File:MW-npc-Dul gro-Dush.jpg|thumb|right|Orcish Legionnaire (Morrowind)]]
However, many Orcs seek to escape traditional life in the strongholds. Orc women may want to escape being "just another wife" to the chieftain. They leave to join the Imperial Legion, see the world, or otherwise seek their fortune; some eventually return to the strongholds, but many do not. Orcs who do not live in strongholds are derisively called "city Orcs" by those that do, and are considered soft outsiders just like non-Orcs.{{ref|name=TCOM}} Given their history of provincial homelessness, frequent military service, and the demand for their craftsmanship, it's not surprising that Orcs have traveled all around Tamriel and are now a commonplace sight in cities throughout.{{ref|name=GTC|{{Cite Book|Guide to Cheydinhal}}}}{{ref|name=GTB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Bravil}}}}{{ref|name=GTS|{{Cite Book|Guide to Skingrad}}}}{{ref|name=DIF2|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 2}}}} Still, Orcs who live in the cities with other races are likely to face prejudice. Their appearance makes interbreeding with other races of Tamriel so rare that it is commonly thought impossible.{{ref|name=NORP|{{Cite Book|Notes on Racial Phylogeny}}}}{{ref|name=JOTLL|{{Cite Book|Journal of the Lord Lovidicus}}}}

[[Lore:Vosh Ball|Vosh Ball]], also known as "Courage" Ball, is a traditional sport played by the Orcs where two teams of six are armed with three balls each and throw them at each other until only one side is still standing. Early iterations of the sport used a metal or wooden ball, but they have since been replaced by leather ones.{{ref|name=VB|{{Cite Book|The Sport of Clans}}}} In addition to games, Orcs have their own holidays and traditions that they celebrate as well, such as Malacath's Vengeance Day{{ref|name=VDD|[[ON:Vengeance Day Dress|Vengeance Day Dress]] costume description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the annual [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life]] celebration thrown by the Seamount Clan, the Stonetooth Bash.{{ref|name=ONBreda|[[Online:Breda|Breda]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Vaia|Vaia]]'s Golden Ash is considered the symbol of the unity, heritage and strength of the Orc people.{{Ref|name=CMTS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 22: Trinimac Style}}}}

==Culture==
===Martial Tradition===
[[File:ON-concept-Orc Shield.png|right|200px]][[File:MW-race-Orc 03.jpg|thumb|left|An Orc warrior - Stories about the exploits of warriors are revered in Orcish culture]]
Warfare and conflict is a common theme among the Orcs and is deeply ingrained into their culture. According to some, an Orc is more likely to strike first than start up a conversation, and that strike is always meant to maim or kill. Outsiders have observed that Orcs demonstrate unshakeable courage in battle and uncompromising endurance in hardships that would overwhelm anyone of another race. Fierce and strong, the Orcs ability to wage war cannot be underestimated and their physical size and prowess make them gravitate toward two-handed weapons.{{Ref|name=MoM|{{Cite Book|Orcs: Monsters or Misunderstood?}}}} Orcish battle preparations are said to be a fearsome sight, especially for those used to the strictly-regimented organization of traditional armies. Drinking, clashing weapons on shields, burning of offerings to Mauloch and guttural, violent war-chants whip them into a blood-crazed frenzy.{{Ref|name=FaBTJ|{{Cite Book|From a Breton travel journal}}}}

In war, tribal Wood Orcs wear red face paint that is made from sacrificial blood, wet clay and ground herbs. The very act of creating this paint is ritualistic and prayer-like, and is an honor granted to only a handful of the chieftain's daughters.{{ref|name=Paint|{{Cite Book|The Red Paint}}}} Other Wood Orcs of [[Lore:Malabal Tor|Malabal Tor]] revered an ancient red-water spring they discovered deep underground. called the "Blood of Mauloch". These Orcs built the settlement of [[Lore:Abamath|Abamath]] around the spring, believing Mauloch gave his blood to empower them. While the original settlement was destroyed, many Wood Orcs who trace their ancestry back to Abamath paint their faces red, hoping to cleanse their shame with displays of renewed ferocity.{{ref|name=Arithiel|[[Online:Arithiel (person)|Arithiel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Abamath|{{Cite Book|The Taking of Abamath}}}}

Though widely despised and feared, Orcs have nevertheless slowly won acceptance across the Empire and a fierce loyalty runs deeply through their culture.{{Ref|name=MoM}} By the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], many Orcs leave their strongholds and use their talents to serve in the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]], who welcome their skill in battle and smithing.{{ref|name=Ghorbash|[[Skyrim:Ghorbash the Iron Hand|Ghorbash the Iron Hand]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Magic===
Some parts of Orcish society believe they aren't suited to practice magic, and Orcish mages are generally less common compared to the warriors class.{{Ref|name=ODJ|{{Cite Book|Oshgura's Destruction Journal}}}} Despite this, their study of magic is quite distinct from the magic that is learned in traditional elven schools. It is said to come from the "meat and bones" of the physical world, bordering on [[Lore:Blood Magic|Blood Magic]]. All wise women in Orcish strongholds learn to use blood in their craft, believing it the best source to fuel their healing spells after engaging in combat.{{ref|name=Khazasha}}

===Language===
[[Lore:Orcish|Orcish]] is the language of the Orcs, and evolved from [[Lore:Old Orcish|Old Orcish]]. While it is unknown precisely when the transition from Old Orcish to modern Orcish took place, it could not have occurred any later than {{Year|3E 417}}, by which time modern Orcish was well established in the Orcs of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall|[[DF:Orcish|Orcish]] skill in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} There is evidence suggesting the Old Orcish language was largely forgotten by {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=Kharsthun}} Orcs have no concept of goodbye, and instead they come and go as they please.{{ref|name=ESOBazrag}}

===Food===

Because of their tight-knit tribal communities, the Orcs cook many communal meals where a large central dish is shared among many individuals. {{Ref|name=Cookbook|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook|ns_base=BK}}}}
The [[Lore:Echatere|Echatere]] serve as the primary lifestock for the Orcs living in the Wrothgarian Mountains; not only do the Echatere provide meat and fur to feed and keep the Orcish people warm, they also provide milk and serve as their primary beasts of burden.{{Ref|name=AAE|{{Cite Book|All About Echatere}}}}

Echatere cheese, along with spiced ale, play a role in Orcish marriage proposals. An Orc chief who wants to woo a wife must first gather these and present them to the maiden as a present as part of the courting process.{{ref|name=Rlfl|[[Online:Rulfala|Rulfala]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

===Architecture and Art===
[[File:ON-misc-Scarp Keep Throne Room Tapestry.jpg|thumb|right|An Orcish tapestry]][[File:LO-misc-Orc Sculpture.png|thumb|Orcish wall-mounted masks]]
Before Orcs built their structures on the surface, it is believed that the earliest Orcs made their homes underground.{{ref|name=ESOMorkulLoad}} Orcish architecture is pragmatic given their history of frequently being attacked by their neighbors. While Redguard and Breton architecture features engravings, gold highlights and domes to create beautiful buildings, Orcs cannot afford such luxury. Instead, Orcs place much greater value on building quickly and building strong.{{Ref|name=Stonemason|{{Cite Book|Grudash gro-Shugharz, Orcish stonemason}}}}

Orcish towns and strongholds tend to be built next to natural resources and may vary depending on the region or location. Several Orcish cities and strongholds in High Rock are made out of stone, while other strongholds, especially those in Skyrim, are made out of wood. Orcish buildings and walls are built to withstand siege, making it extremely difficult to root out strongholds as they can only be defeated with the strongest martial forces. Nordic Jarls have been known to die in vain trying to destroy them, and when they succeed, they are often rebuilt a few generations later unless a fort is maintained nearby. The upkeep of forts makes it hard for the Nords to prevent the strongholds from returning, and thus Orcish strongholds have been known to persist for thousands of years.{{Ref|name=OOS}}

The stronghold at [[Lore:Cradlecrush|Cradlecrush]] was unable to be conquered by Nordic armies, even after the natural resources of the stronghold's mine dried up.{{Ref|name=OOS}} King Kurog's Orsinium during the second Era was a impressive feat of architecture: the city itself contained plumbing, bath houses, a water wheel, many businesses, and guild halls for the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] and [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]]. There were additional plans of adding buttressing to the aqueducts, improving roads, and expanding public housing.{{Ref|name=CS|[[Online:City Solicitor|City Solicitor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

While they are renowned for their smithing skills, Orcs are also skilled artisans and craftsmen in other areas. Many of their crafts are themed around their rugged outlook, and reflect their unique lifestyle and hardships. Orcish craftsmen are known for their ability to create delicate and beautiful artwork and tapestries,{{Ref|name=OPZD|[[Online:Contraband O#Orsinium Pink Zinfandel Decanter|Orsinium Pink Zinfandel Decanter]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} which tend to depict ancient Orcish heroes, battles, and hunts. Statues of Orcish figures are made out of bronze or stone, and take artisans hundreds of hours to build.{{Ref|name=OF|[[Online:Orcish Furnishings|Orcish Furnishings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONKurog|[[Online:King Kurog|King Kurog]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Anger of a King|The Anger of a King]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} They are known to carve heavy, roughly hewn effigies of [[Lore:Bear|bears]] and [[Lore:Mammoth|mammoths]] but their significance is unknown.{{Ref|name=OF}} Orcs announce great events by erecting symbolic totems. These totems have various purposes, such as marking territory, heralding war or foretelling battle.{{Ref|name=OWT|[[Online:Orcish Furnishings#Orcish War Totem|Orcish War Totem]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

During the mid-Second Era, much of their architecture and craft was inspired by the Tilted Square philosophy. This abstract doctrine, based around sacred geometric shapes, synthesized the teachings of Malacath into their work. Proverbs such as "Betrayal is inevitable", "Orcs must be constantly looking askance", or "Our enemies speak fair before they act foul" heavily inspire their approach. Chevrons were considered the symbol of deflection, while the Tilted Square itself was seen as the symbol of instability. These maxims resulted in curious outcomes.{{Ref|name=MS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 23: Malacath Style}}}} For example, axes are used to fell objects, so Orcs naturally inscribe them with symbols of instability. Conversely, boots are required to keep them stable, so they are adorned with the chevron: upright for stability, or inverted for grip.{{Ref|name=MS}} Using three-stanza epitaphs, was a common practice when it came to Orcish monuments and memorials; many of the monuments found on Sorrow mountain contained these stanza epitaphs.{{ref|name=Kharsthun}}

===Fashion: Armor, Weapons, etc.===
[[File:ON-concept-Nobles' Fashionable Attire 03.jpg|thumb|left|Male Orc Fashion in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Orc clothing come in a variety of styles. Modern orcs tend to use a bias-cut-placket clothing,{{ref|[[Online:Eternity Tunic with Pants|Eternity Tunic with Pants]] costume description in {{g|on}}}} or wear clothing befitting their role in society. Forge-wives may look to wear clothing made out of non-flammable gray scathecraw fibers that protects from forge embers or molten metal while performing their tasks.{{ref|[[Online:Forge-Wife's Impervious Frock|Forge-Wife's Impervious Frock]] costume description in {{g|on}}}} To cover a suit of chainmail, orcs may opt to use robes.{{ref|name=LEAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Eloisse Answers Your Questions}}}} For the wealthy, these robes would be made out of silk or other fabrics, which are then accented with steel or gold to convey the wearer's wealth and power.{{ref|[[Online:Brilliance Brocade Robes|Brilliance Brocade Robes]] costume description in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Summit Lord's Silken Robes|Summit Lord's Silken Robes]] costume description in {{g|on}}}} Some orcish clans are known to tattoo themselves.{{ref|name=Umutha}}
[[File:ON-misc-Orc Outfits.jpg|thumb|right|Female Orc Fashion in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Orcs are famed for their skill in smithing. They consider themselves (as well as by many others) to be the best armorers in the world.{{ref|name=MWGDO|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue O|Orc]] dialogue topic in {{g|mw}}}} Orcish armor is built to be functional and utilitarian in nature. It is also typically thick and very heavy in that the sturdiest of other races may have difficulty moving in it. Orcish frames are well suited for their style of armor, whose tremendous weight they are said to work with since basically childbirth. Indeed, any difficulties wearing it such as immobility they see as yet another hardship they must endure. Their armor, especially their gauntlets and sabatons, are typically jagged and sharp with the intention of being utilized to cause misery upon enemies it may clash with. Fur is sewn into the folds to prevent self-inflected damage from their armor.{{Ref|name=ILAAOC|{{TIL|Arms and Armor of the Orcish Champion, Gushagub Gro-Shugdurbam|arms-and-armor-orcish-champion-gushagub-gro-shugdurbam}}}} Words that have been used to describe the various types of orcish weapons are terrifying, jagged, crude, irregular, or unsubtle.{{Ref|name=ILAAOC}}{{ref|[[Blades:Orcish|Orcish]] quality in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} A certain variety of Orcish medium armor based on High Elven designs can be described as ornate steel plate worn over cloth padding, and the metal is light and comfortable in comparison to other steel plate armors. It is typically the medium armor of choice for both nobles and mercenaries in both east and west Tamriel, and requires paying a premium price to obtain.{{ref|name=Codex|[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex#Items|Items Codex on ''elderscrolls.com'']]}}{{ref|name=MWGaro|[[Morrowind:Garothmuk gro-Muzgub|Garothmuk gro-Muzgub]]'s dialogue in {{g|mw}}}}

Orcs are well known for working with [[Lore:Orichalcum|Orichalcum]], which they use to forge armor and weapons. Like the rest of their creations, the resulting armaments are heavy and require great endurance to wield, but offer peerless strength and durability.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}} Orcs of the Trinimac Cult use [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] as a material to accent their armors and weapons in token of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]'s blood when Trinimac tore out his heart. They believe that [[Lore:Zylvara|Zylvara]], the great eagle bore the Golden Champion to his reckoning with the [[Lore:Man|man]]-demon [[Lore:Quarvish|Quarvish]].{{Ref|name=CMTS}} Orcs also have skilled tanners who have a unique skill in tooling leather for exceptional flexibility and toughness.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Orcs are fond of shields, so much so that they have many traditional sayings dedicated to wielding them.{{ref|name=OOSS|{{Cite Book|Old Orc Sayings: Shields}}}} Though some orcs may shun magic, they recognize the utility of enchanted equipment and find enchantments to be visually appealing.{{Ref|name=ILAAOC}}

===Domesticated Animals===
[[File:ON-creature-Echatere.jpg|thumb|left|Echatere]]
[[Lore:Echatere|Echatere]]s are large herbivorous mammals{{ref|name=Orzorga}} with tusks, pointed hooves, and large antlers. Found mainly in [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]], they serve the Orcs as livestock and beasts of burden. They provide meat, fur,{{ref|name=AAE|{{Cite Book|All About Echatere}}}} vellum,{{ref|[[Online:Contraband F#Fine Echatere Vellum|Fine Echatere Vellum]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and milk for the Orcs.{{ref|name=Orzorga|[[Online:Orzorga|Orzorga]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Kindred Spirits|Kindred Spirits]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOBul|[[Online:Bulfor the Cheesemonger|Bulfor the Cheesemonger]]'s existence and dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=DDDDH|{{Cite Book|Dark Elves, Dark Hearts}}}} Orcs also have rare "shaggy giant centipedes" that serve as herdbeasts. These centipede-like creatures can live only at high altitudes on thrive on the alpine and sub-alpine forage.{{ref|name=PGE1W}} A stereotype that orcs eat horses exists,{{ref|[[Oblivion:Snak gra-Bura|Snak gra-Bura]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} but orcish-made tapestries shows them being utilized for both hunting and in wars.{{ref|[[Online:Orcish Tapestry, Hunt|Orcish Tapestry, Hunt]] depiction in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Orcish Tapestry, War|Orcish Tapestry, War]] depiction in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Orcs train [[Lore:Bear|bears]] as guard mounts for their strongholds.{{ref|name=KyneC|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion}}}}{{rp|28}} It is said that [[Lore:Torug|King Torug]] allied with the mightiest snow bear of Wrothgar after earning her trust. According to legend, Torug spilled his own blood to draw her out, brought echatere meat and clean water to feed her and brought fire to warm her. Coldwind accepted King Torug's offerings, for Torug had treated her as an equal, and not as a master.{{ref|name=ONColdwind|[[Online:A Cold Wind From the Mountain|A Cold Wind From the Mountain]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} The beast earned the name "Coldwind" by being fast and icily brave while serving as the king's mount in hundreds of battles, and also led to King Torug founding Orsinium's Bear-Rider cavalry. By taming Coldwind, Torug supposedly bent all the bears of the land to his will.{{ref|name=Umutha|[[ON:Curator Umutha|Curator Umutha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} After many years of service, Coldwind grew weary so Torug found her a den in the far north so the bear could retire and pass on her bloodline to many powerful cubs.{{ref|name=Coldwind|{{Cite Book|Coldwind's Skull}}}}{{ref|name=CDen|[[Online:Coldwind's Den|Coldwind's Den]] loading screen in [[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|ESO]]}}

Orcs bred mammoths to be smaller to make them more manageable as livestock. They escaped and started breeding in the wild after orcish ranches containing them got raided by other orcs. These pocket mammoths as they have been dubbed are easily domesticated, especially if one is obtained during their youth.{{ref|[[Online:Pocket Mammoth (pet)|Pocket Mammoth]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Welwa|Welwa]] inhabiting the wilds of [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] are often domesticated and armored by the [[Lore:Iron Orcs|Iron Orcs]] of the region, who use them as war beasts. The Iron Orc belief of labor long and hard has been instilled into their welwas, and will push their riders to the limit.{{ref|[[ON:Longhair Welwa|Longhair Welwa]]'s pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Iron Orc Welwa|Iron Orc Welwa]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Seamount Clan claim that their ancestors tamed killer [[Lore:Whale|whales]] in the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] and trained them for war. Riders of those whales were said to be capable of harpooning ships and steer them by using the strength of the animal, with evidence of this long-lost tradition surviving in siege gear that was later recovered in the Second Era.{{Ref|name=ONCornerAnt|[[Online:Betnikh Antiquities#Cornerstone of Reman II|Cornerstone of Reman II]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} They were also described as the fiercest raiders of the sea before they settled on Betony.{{ref|name=ESORozag|[[Online:Rozag gro-Khazun|Rozag gro-Khazun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Religion==
===Malacath===
[[File:ON-place-Malacath Statue.jpg|thumb|left|Malacath, the Orc-Father]]{{Quote Box|style=float:right; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Do not steal.''<br>''Do not kill your kin.''<br>''Do not attack without cause.''<br>''Those who break these rules must pay the Blood Price.''|''[[Lore:Mauloch, Orc-Father|Mauloch, Orc-Father]]''}}
Mainstream Orcish religion is fairly unique to Tamriel in that it is monolatrous. Most Orcs revere [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], the Daedric Prince of Curses.{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} The orthodox belief among Wrothgarian and Wood Orcs is that Malacath is a broken Trinimac, transformed by Boethiah after he in some way stole the former Champion of Auriel's form.{{ref|name=VoF:TO}} There is debate among Orcs on whether he is Malacath, the Daedric outcast of Oblivion, or Mauloch, the Aedric outcast of Aetherius. Some even believe that he is both and has fittingly been cast out by both groups of divines.{{ref|name=ONAbamath|[[Online:Abamath|Shrine of Mauloch]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Regardless, after being reborn in his ashy prison, the newly-transformed Malacath cursed Boethiah and created the Code of Malacath, a central tenet to Orcish religion.{{ref|name=MOF}} While exact interpretations vary between clans{{ref|name=TCOM}}, his main commandments are as follows.

According to some priests, praying to Malacath for strength is futile because he cannot grant it. Instead, Malacath issues the challenge to claim strength from themselves.{{Ref|name=Thazeg|[[Online:Thazeg|Thazeg]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Upon death, followers of Malacath (or Mauloch) believe they will end up in Malacath's realm of the [[Lore:Ashpit|Ashpit]]. Here, Orcs are promised rewards of immortality, abundant food and drink, and constant battle deep within the Ashen Forge. Lending to the ambiguity of Mauloch's Aedric status, it is said that the Ashpit extends into [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], allowing worthy Orsimer to freely transition into a more traditional afterlife.{{ref|name=OtA|{{Cite Book|On Orcs and the Afterlife}}}}

===Trinimac===
Some fringe members of Orsimer society choose to instead worship [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]]. Some believe that Trinimac was the one that absorbed power from Boethiah, improving the Orsimer in the process,{{ref|name=BVTH|}} while other worshippers think that Malacath and Trinimac are actually wholly separate entities, and that Malacath lies to keep them as pariahs under his thumb.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} The latter belief has briefly risen to prevalence on numerous occasions, typically under newly-crowned kings of Orsinium who sought to form friendly relations with their neighbors, as was the case with King [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog]] of Second [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] and King [[Lore:Gortwog gro-Nagorm|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]] of Nova Orsinium.{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PGE3O}} In both of these cases, the transition to Trinimac worship was brief and controversial, with Orcs reverting back to Malacath worship after their deaths.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{ref|name=PGE3O}}

The followers of Trinimac believe that when they die, they ascend directly to Aetherius to join with their ancestors. The afterlife of Trinimac's faithful also consists of endless war and celebrations, but with a greater emphasis on once again spending time with family members who came before them.{{ref|name=VoF:TO|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Orcs}}}}

===Boethiah===
Boethiah holds a role in Orcish culture, despite the scarcity of preserved accounts of her veneration. Evidence and texts related to the worship of Boethiah by the Orcs exist. The esoteric text ''From Exile to Exodus'' discusses Boethiah's teachings to the Orcs.{{Ref|name=FETE}}

In the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]],{{ref|name=Curate|[[ON:Curate Gadayn|Curate Gadayn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Boethiah was known to have Orcish followers. Among them, Dugikh emerged as a vessel for the Prince when Boethiah, alongside Azura, Mephala, and [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], sought to banish [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]].{{ref|name=Acolyte|[[ON:Acolyte Dugikh|Acolyte Dugikh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=POF|Events of [[ON:Prisoner of Fate|Prisoner of Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Other===
[[File:DF-creature-Orc Shaman.gif|thumb|right|Orc Shaman]]
Orcish religion includes: Shamans,{{ref|name=OrcShaman|[[Daggerfall:Orc Shaman|Orc Shaman]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Witch Doctors,{{ref|name=DaggerWitchDoctor|Dialogue during [[Daggerfall:An Unexpected Journey Part II|An Unexpected Journey Part II]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and Wise Women{{Ref|name=OSGD}} as spiritual guides.

[[Lore:The Allfire|The Allfire]] is some form of obscure afterlife, seemingly associated with Orcs and goblin-ken, and referred to in curses such as: "blast them to the Allfire!"{{Ref|name=Shadowkey|[[Shadowkey:Olpac Trailslag|Olpac Trailslag]]'s dialogue during [[Shadowkey:Trailslag's Goods|Trailslag's Goods]] quest in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}}

In Orcish shamanism, a spirit called Gar-shutan (Sacred Beast) comes to the world in search of nobility. When it does, it appears in a vision to the Abbas (Seeker), the bravest warriors, through whispers, and has been seen in dreams hovering in the clouds. Complimentary to the Abbas is the Abasseen (Seeker's Companion), who may appear in these visions alongside the Abbas. When Gar-shutan appears, it does so always in a different form to test the clarity of the Abbas's vision. When called upon, it is customary for the Abbas to seek out the Abasseen and go to together on a holy quest to slay Gar-shutan. In combat, Gar-shutan tests the Abbas and thereby tests his people. The sacrifice of blood, theirs or beast's, is said to bring prosperity and joy to the Abbas's people. If neglected for too long, Gar-shutan may get tired of waiting, and may, inferably, remove the opportunity of letting the duo face the it in battle.{{ref|name=CW11|group=UOL|[[Skyrim:Unfinished Quests#CWMission11|''Skyrim'' Unfinished Quests]]}}

====Religious Orders and Cults====
[[File:ON-misc-Vaia's Golden Ash.png|thumb|right|Vaia's Golden Ash]]
The Orcs have several religious orders and cults dedicated to the worship of Malacath and Trinimac.

'''[[Lore:Agra Crun|Agra Crun]]''': A religious order dedicated to protecting the sacred word of Malacath. Their name translates to Blood Shield in the common tongue. The group operates in secrecy, but for the few that know of them, they are seen as a beacon of Light. {{Ref|name=Azrath| [[Online:Azrath|Azrath]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

'''[[Lore:Malacath's Brutal Breed|Malacath's Brutal Breed]]:''' This was a group of fanatical Malacath followers that existed during the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. Their main purpose was to hunt down and murder Bretons and Redguards for every real and imagined slight perpetrated by them against the Orcs. Legends about the Cult say the group sacrificed an entire Breton village to the Ashpit.{{ref|[[Online:Malacath's Band of Brutality|Malacath's Band of Brutality]] Antiquity Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

'''[[Lore:Oathsworn|Oathsworn]]:''' They are an elite group of Orcish combatants devoted to [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]]. They are based out of their training ground in the Oathsworn Pit  located in the [[Lore:Reach|Reach]]. They are considered to be among the most fearsome warriors in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and are masters of multiple forms of combat.{{Ref|name=LotP|{{Cite Book|Lessons of the Pit}}}}

'''[[Lore:Vosh Rakh|Vosh Rakh]]''': A group of fanatics dedicated to the worship of Trinimac. Their goal is to bring the Orcs back to their Aldmeri roots. Vosh Rakh in Orcish translates to Blade of Courage, which directly refers to Trinimac's legendary weapon, Penitent. They see themselves as the Sword made manifest, and their goal is to rid orcish society of the traditions that held the Orcs back for many years. {{Ref|name=VRB|{{Cite Book|Vosh Rakh (book)}}}}

'''[[Lore:Wrathful Flame|Wrathful Flame]]''': A religious order dedicated to lighting Malacath's Pyre in the ruins of the first Orsinium.{{ref|name=ONBP|[[Online:Flames of Forge and Fallen|Flames of Forge and Fallen]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

====Death====
When an Orc dies, his or her remains will be burned down to ashes, which can be handled by their kin. Ashes from chiefs, kings and elders are usually forged into swords and shields, or any other item that their relatives or successors may want to remember the departed ones.{{ref|name=ESOUugus|[[Online:Priest Uugus|Priest Uugus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOBazrag|[[Online:Chief Bazrag|Chief Bazrag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}} This practice is called the "beshkar-nor," the death-forge; it is traditionally reserved for Orcs of great stature, such as a leader, elder or hero. The blood is either drained from the body, or the body is reduced to ash and then saved for the processing of forging tools, weapons and armor.{{ref|name=SRotO|{{Cite Book|Strange Rituals of the Orsimer}}}}
[[File:ON-place-Honor's Rest.jpg|thumb|right|Honor's Rest]]

The ashes of the Orcish dead are customarily placed under a cairn, out in the field, under the open sky. Their arms and armor are placed over this cairn, and their relatives would then come to honor their ancestors. Burial is not a practice among the Orcs, although they respect other races' traditions.{{ref|name=ESOBrulak|[[Online:Brulak|Brulak]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=ESORozag|[[Online:Rozag gro-Khazun|Rozag gro-Khazun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A few hours after a family repays their respects, the bodies of the dead who are not cremated and are left where they fell, despite popular lore to the contrary, are removed from the area, this is done to prevent the dangers of decay and disease; what happens to the bodies afterwards is a secret the Orcs are not willing to tell outsiders.{{ref|name=SRotO}}

The mountain of [[Lore:Sorrow|Sorrow]], in Wrothgar, is an ancient burial site for Orcs. Grand tombs, cairns, and wedge tombs, were often placed on the mountain by the Orcs, at the expense of many lives due to the many dangers of the mountain. Only the strongest Orcs were able to survive the challenges of the mountain. The corpses of those who died on the mountain were left behind. Legends state that seven Orcish clans were wiped out, in the process of building the tomb of King Torug. Eventually the Orcs stopped using Sorrow as a place of burial.{{ref|name=SRotO}}{{ref|name=Kharsthun|[[Online:Kharsthun|Kharsthun]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

Another famous burial site is the catacombs of [[Lore:Honor's Rest|Honor's Rest]], in southern [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]]. This extensive mausoleum was built by Orc, Breton, and Redguard masons after the destruction of the first Orsinium. The corpses of Bretons and Redguards and the ashes of the Orcs who died in the long siege were placed here, including those of the heroes and rivals [[Lore:Baloth Bloodtusk|Baloth Bloodtusk]] and [[Lore:Gaiden Shinji|Gaiden Shinji]]. The ruins were hidden and remained undiscovered until the reign of King Kurog in the Second Era.{{ref|name=ESOAshes|[[Online:The Ashes of Our Fathers|The Ashes of Our Fathers]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOLozruth|[[Online:Lozruth|Lozruth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOSharela|[[Online:Sharela|Sharela]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|nb1|1.|The records of Orcs being granted the status of "people" was largely given as a way to explain why Orcs were now a playable race in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''. In their first appearance in ''[[Arena:Arena|TES: Arena]]'', Orcs were considered monsters and described as larger version of goblins that were not overly intelligent, but still cunning enough to stalk their prey and set up ambushes.{{ref|name=APG}} In ''Daggerfall'', Orcs were given more depth, with many Orcs such as Gortwog being capable of perfect speech and are just as intelligent as any other "civilized" race in Tamriel.}}
* [[wikipedia:Orc|Orcs]] originate in the works of [[wikipedia:J. R. R. Tolkien|J. R. R. Tolkien]]'s [[wikipedia:Tolkien's legendarium|legendarium]], with developers commenting that any appearance of Orcs in fantasy are going to be influenced on some level by Tolkien. There were early attempts to differentiate Orsimer from the Orcs of [[wikipedia:Middle-earth|Middle-earth]] and move away from the [[wikipedia:Noble savage|noble savage trope]], with the [[wikipedia:Samurai|Samurai]]-style [[Daggerfall:Armor|Orcish armor]] from ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' inspiring an idea to explore Orsinium being a conduit to an extraplanar dimension containing an advanced [[wikipedia:Atlantis|Atlantis]]-type "Orc Prime" (similar to the extraplanar [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview/REDGUARD.TXT|backstory]] of the [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]]), where honored Shoguns gave mystical orders and wore strangely-sculpted ''Trinimasks''.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} While this idea did not have much movement behind it, it did have some influence in other parts of the series, such as masks being a commonplace Orcish decoration (some [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanted]] to have magical powers){{Ref|name=GM|{{Cite Book|Guthrag's Mask}}}} and certain mythological stories describing Orcs originating as a race while enduring the metamorphosis in the Ashpit.{{Ref|name=FETE}}
