The [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]], also known as '''Hoonding'''{{ref|name=ContHoon|[[Online:Contraband H|Hoonding's Golden Quill]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or '''The Make Way God''', is the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]] spirit of 'perseverance over infidels'. The HoonDing has historically materialized whenever the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s need to 'make way' for their people.{{Ref|name=VoFCR|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}}{{Ref|name=VoFTF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}}
<noinclude>
==History==
In the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], HoonDing is first known to have manifested as an avatar by [[Lore:Diagna|Diagna]]. Diagna was instrumental to the defeat of the [[Lore:Lefthanded Elves|Sinistral Mer]], as he brought [[Lore:Orichalcum|orichalc]] weapons to the Yokudan people to win the fight. A story surrounding The HoonDing Gong details Diagna crashing his fist against it, and swords fell from the walls of the [[Lore:Orichalc Tower|Orichalc Tower]] like "needles from a dry pine".{{ref|name=LAB|[[Online:Lefthander's Aegis Belt|Lefthander's Aegis Belt]] codex entry}} In [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], he led a very tight knit group of followers against the [[Lore:Orc|Orsimer]] of the first [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], presumably the [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]] during the [[Lore:Siege of Orsinium|Siege of Orsinium]], during the height of their ancient power.{{Ref|name=VoFCR}} He had then faded into obscurity, and is now little more than a local power spirit of the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]].{{Ref|name=VoFitE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in the Empire}}}}

[[Lore:Frandar Hunding|Frandar Hunding]] was also an aspect of HoonDing.{{ref|name=ContHoon}}{{ref|name=ContraHoon|[[Online:Contraband H|Hoonding's Prayer Stone]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During the arrival of the Yokudan people to Tamriel, he led them against [[Lore:Malooc|Malooc]] the Horde King, the enemy god of [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] who led an army of [[Lore:Goblin|giant goblin]]s against the Redguards in the First Era. Then HoonDing appeared to drive out the goblin force all the way to the east.{{Ref|name=VoFitE}}

During the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]], HoonDing was said to have been "a sword or a crown, or both". This refers to either or both Crown [[Lore:A'tor|Prince A'tor]], the heir to the throne who was transformed into the [[Lore:Soul Sword|Soul Sword]], and/or [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]], a [[Lore:Crowns|Crown]] who wielded the Soul Sword. Together, they could have also been an aspect of HoonDing.{{Ref|name=VoFitE}} Cyrus was believed to be more than just an ordinary Redguard, which is one of the reasons why he was able to slay the legendary dragon [[Lore:Nahfahlaar|Nafaalilargus]].{{Ref|name=GT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Gary Noonan's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

==Notes==
* [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]] stated he learned a new motion or two when he observed the HoonDing "making way".{{Ref|name=Fanny|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}}
* [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]] is a Yokudan deity or group of deities that share the same designation, with legends saying that Ebonarm is just another manifestation of the HoonDing. Many post-apocalypse manifestations of the HoonDing have individualized, such as Diagna, and Ebonarm may be among them.{{ref|group=UOL|name=MK|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}
*HoonDing's Watch was a Ra'Gada settlement in the southeastern [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r Desert]].
