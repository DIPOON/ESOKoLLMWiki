==Religion and Culture==
[[File:SR-interior-Temple 04.jpg|Shrine to Mephala (Dragonborn)|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|''As known in the West, Mephala is the demon prince of murder, sex, and secrets. All of these themes contain subtle aspects and violent ones (assassination/genocide, courtship/orgy, tact/poetic truths); Mephala is understood paradoxically to contain and integrate these contradictory themes.''|''[[Lore:Vivec and Mephala|Vivec and Mephala]]''}}
In [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], Mephala is the ancestor spirit that taught the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] the skills they would need to evade their enemies or to kill them with secret murder. Enemies were numerous in those days since the Chimer were a small faction.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} Mephala, along with [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], organized the clan systems that eventually became the basis for the Great Houses.{{ref|name=VoF}} Chimer shrines to Mephala were referred to as "Mephala's Nests".{{ref|The [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] for [[Online:Mephala's Nest|Mephala's Nest]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Some versions of the tale of [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]]'s fall claim that Mephala was an integral part of his transformation. In one such iteration of the story, Trinimac battles Boethiah for turning the Velothi against their Aedric ancestors, and is stabbed in the back by Mephala just as he's about to deal a major blow. Trinimac was too wounded to fight further, allowing Boethiah to disfigure him and cast the disgraced god into Oblivion. Trinimac tore the shame of failure from his chest, which created [[Lore:Mauloch|Mauloch]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mauloch, Orc-Father}}}}

Mephala is one of the "Good Daedra" of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal faith]].{{ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue G#Good Daedra|generic dialogue]] on the topic of the ''Good Daedra'' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} She is known as '''Black Hands Mephala'''{{ref|[[MW:Eno Hlaalu|Eno Hlaalu]]'s dialogue during [[Morrowind:Belt of Sanguine Fleetness (quest)|Belt of Sanguine Fleetness]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and the '''Anticipation of Vivec'''.{{ref|name=VaM|{{Cite Book|Vivec and Mephala}}}} She is various and many, and is androgynous.{{ref|name=TA}} [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] was touted by the Tribunal Temple as a transcendent evolution of Mephala. The darker themes connecting Vivec to Mephala are not recorded in popular liturgy, but they were understood and accepted by adherents of the faith as important to Vivec's divine identity.{{ref|name=VaM}} It was understood that Vivec had an unnatural attraction to lustful thoughts and murderous intentions.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Understanding the Living Gods}}}} Vivec was compassionate and protective of his people, who hailed him as their benevolent guardian. Undercurrents of violence, lust and conspiracy were present in the Dunmer's perception of him nonetheless.{{ref|name=VaM}}

Vivec's religious profile is awash in duality, as is befitting his relation to Mephala. One cultural relic that reflects this is the persistent myth that Vivec conspired with [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] to murder Lord [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]]. The notion was born from [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]] oral tradition, and was contradicted by the Tribunal Temple's teachings. True or not, the tale was firmly established in Dunmer society. This myth presents a darker facet of Vivec's persona for his followers to ponder, one that is tied to his association with Mephala, who embodies conspiracy, secrets and murder.{{ref|name=VaM}}

To the Khajiit, she is known as Mafala.{{ref|name=ESOShando-ri|[[Online:Shando-ri|Shando-ri's dialogue]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They also called her the '''Teaching Mother''', the '''Elder Spirit''', the '''Keeper of the Ancient Secrets of Fadomai''',{{Ref|name=TWaS}} the '''Clan Mother of Dark Secrets''',{{ref|name=AbodeLoading|[[Online:Abode of Ignominy|Abode of Ignominy's]] Loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESONarama-ko|[[Online:Narama-ko|Narama-ko's dialogue]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the '''Eight-Clawed'''. Ancient sermons claim that she served as the recorder of hidden guilt and eternal shame. She was considered an ancestor spirit and a teacher of the old ways;{{ref|name=ESOShando-ri}} however, her worship fell out of favor after the event known as the "Sinner Suicides".{{ref|name=AbodeLoading}} Mafala, along with Azurah and Boethra, are said to be the only ones capable of summoning an obscure spirit of vengeance, as only they know its true name. The spirit appears as a black panther, a warrior in [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] armor, or a hidden sword.{{ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}}

The [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]] of the [[Lore:Morkul Clan|Morkul Clan]] once revered Mephala. As with most of Mephala's cults, they called spiders her "children". The clan ritualistically sacrificed people to power their clan's [[Lore:Blood Magic|Blood Forge]] in her name.{{Ref|name=GJ}}

===Association with Assassins===
[[File:ON-misc-Cradle of Shadows Mephala Statue.jpg|A statue of Mephala hanging from a ceiling (ESO)|thumb|right]]
Mephala directly helped to found the infamous cult/society of the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], and they claim to murder for the Daedra's glory. Some scholars also argue that when the Morag Tong was banished from the rest of Tamriel, they were allowed to continue to operate in Morrowind when they replaced their worship of Mephala with that of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]. As a reaction to this, the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] was formed, being led by the mysterious [[Lore:Night Mother|Night Mother]], who some insist is just another form of Mephala.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Fire and Darkness}}}} Prior to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], the branch of the Dark Brotherhood that operated in the Iliac Bay openly worshipped Mephala.{{ref|name=AAO}} Some within the organization recognize her role, although it has become obscure over time.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Lie We Tell Ourselves|ns_base=Online}}}}{{FMI}}

Mephala was the patron of the [[Lore:Silken Ring|Silken Ring]], a group of assassins comprised of deserters from the Dark Brotherhood, the Morag Tong, and the [[Lore:Shadowscales|Shadowscales]]. The group was led by Dranos Velador, a former member of the Morag Tong, and the Lady of Lace, a hoarvor Daedra devoted to Mephala.{{ref|name=HTA|[[Online:Hides-the-Ashes|Hides-the-Ashes]]' dialogue during [[Online:Silk and Shadow|Silk and Shadow]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Shadows of the Hist|Shadows of the Hist]]''}} The Lady of Lace granted the Silken Ring Mephala's blessings, which made them deadlier assassins.{{ref|name=GMD|{{Cite Book|Geel-Ma's Diary|ns_base=Online}}}} The Silken Ring believed the Brotherhood and the Shadowscales did not understand Sithis's true nature, but Mephala does.{{ref|[[Online:Tsatva-Lan|Tsatva-Lan]]'s dialogue at the end of [[Online:Silk and Shadow|Silk and Shadow]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Shadows of the Hist|Shadows of the Hist]]''}} The organization's founder felt the Morag Tong merely paid lip service to Mephala. With the Silken Ring's founding, he felt he would be doing her bidding directly.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Dranos's Diary|ns_base=Online}}}}

===Worship===
[[File:ON-concept-Prince-Mephala-emblem.png|An emblem representing Mephala|thumb|left]]
Gardeners may occasionally ask Mephala to spare their gardens from slugs and worms due to her association with spiders.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Persistence of Daedric Veneration}}}} Spiders and related imagery (such as webs) are often used in devotional pieces to Mephala.{{ref|name=DR|{{Cite Book|Dark Ruins}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Contraband E#Ebony Mephala Idol|Ebony Mephala Idol]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Contraband U#Ulathi Silver Spiderweb Armlet|Ulathi Silver Spiderweb Armlet]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Pieces are often made from silvery or solid-black materials, such as ebony or [[Lore:Hematite|hematite]].{{ref|[[Online:Contraband H#Hematite Worry Stone|Hematite Worry Stone]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Textiles created in Mephala's honor are usually woven from spider silk.{{ref|name=ONShawl|[[Online:Contraband M#Mephala Spidersilk Prayer Shawl|Mephala Spidersilk Prayer Shawl]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Cult of the Spider|Cult of the Spider]] (also called the "Spider Cult") is an illegal form of worship that honors Mephala. individual spider cults are sometimes referred to as "Webs". People generally don't purposefully seek to join the cult. As one of its members described it during an interview in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the weaving of Mephala's web draws people into their service. That is not to say Mephala recruited them; rather, the cultists believe that their involvement in the cult is as inevitable as death itself. The Spider Cult has a leader known as the Widow, who communes with Mephala and grants duties to subordinate members. Sometimes, Mephala wants someone dead; these jobs fall to the [[Lore:Deathweavers|Deathweavers]]. The rest of the cult consists of Scuttlers, who perform more discreet tasks to spin their master's webs.

The Spider Cults use sex and seduction as tools, and induction into the cult often requires prospective members to engage in such activities with the Widow or another designated party. Some Webs are more zealous about this than others. The cult's use of sex has earned them a reputation for being "debauched orgy clubs", a claim that is mostly inaccurate.{{ref|name=IWASC|{{Cite Book|Interview with a Spider Cultist}}}} Some spider cults execute members who express doubt in their faith to Mephala.{{ref|name=JD26}} Spider cults often keep giant spiders, which they feed live mortals; one who intrudes upon a spider cult's domain may find themselves a meal to the sect's eight-legged pets. Those who are faithful to Mephala are able to command spiders,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Journal, Day 32|ns_base=Online}}}} which are surprisingly easy to train.{{ref|[[Online:Goldenback Spider Lackey|Goldenback Spider Lackey]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Followers who worship at Mephala's shrine in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s [[Lore:Great Forest|Great Forest]] leave offerings of nightshade at the shrine between midnight and dawn if they seek an audience with the Prince.{{ref|name=OBDredena}}

A seemingly universal concept among Mephala's worshippers is a simple mantra, referred to by one Morkul cultist as "Her whispers": "Lust is love. Lies are truth. Death is life."{{ref|name=Skalg}}{{ref|name=DR}}

As of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], some [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] cults of Mephala were active.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}}

==History==
===Earliest Interactions===
[[File:ON-place-The Spiral Skein.jpg|thumb|right|Mephala's realm, the Spiral Skein]]
[[Lore:Rajhin|Rajhin]] stole the [[Lore:Ring of Khajiiti|Ring of Khajiiti]] from Mafala prior to his ascension to godhood. During this period in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]'s history, there was no such thing as a [[Lore:Mane|Mane]].{{ref|name=RajhinShadow|[[Online:Rajhin's Shadow (Northern Elsweyr)|Rajhin's Shadow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The moons were new on the night of the crime, thus shined no light upon Nirn. The darkness gave Rajhin access to Oblivion, where he found Mafala, Azurah, and a third Daedric Prince arguing over who claimed dominion over the night. Rajhin listened to their argument for a while before revealing his presence. He offered to settle their debate, as there is no one more familiar with the night than a thief. At first, Mephala regarded Rajhin with malice, and sought to eat him. However, Azurah stopped her. Each Prince told Rajhin why they deserved to be chosen, and the thief caught sight of the Ring of Khajiiti on Mephala's eighth arm. He seduced Mafala by appealing to her vanity, and stole the ring off her arm while they made love.{{ref|[[Online:Humble Zaham|Humble Zaham]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} He spotted the [[Lore:Ebony Blade|Ebony Blade]] as he was leaving, and made off with both artifacts.{{ref|name=ESOTGT|{{Cite Book|The Thief God's Treasures}}}} Rajhin used the Ring of Khajiiti's powers to make himself as invisible, silent, and quick as a breath of wind. Using the Ring, he became the most successful burglar in the history of Elsweyr, eventually being elevated to the Thief God of the Khajiit.{{ref|name=ESOTGT}}

Long ago, the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] city of [[Lore:Erokii|Erokii]] thrived in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]]. It was ruled by princess Anurraame, whose wisdom, grace, and beauty were unsurpassed. She married a prince from another state, and their relationship was happy for a time. However, Anurraame's husband was away for extended periods of time. The princess's passion died, and she took a younger warrior as her lover. Her husband eventually caught wind of the affair, and gathered his army to storm the city. Anurraame pledged to face him in battle, and her lover promised to muster his own forces in defense of the city. When the day of the battle arrived, Anurraame saw that her lover had betrayed her for a bribe, as his legions had joined her husband's. The spurned princess called upon the Daedra to help defend her city, and Mephala answered her plea. She commanded Anurraame to pour all her tears in a basin, then imbued them with the power of Anurraame's hatred for her traitorous lover, which was only strengthened by the genuine love she had for him before he turned on her. Her tears hardened into a powerful artifact known as the [[Lore:Tears of Anurraame|Tears of Anurraame]], which the princess used to destroy both armies and her city in the blink of an eye.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Tears of Anurraame}}}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-quest-Edge of Darkness.jpg|thumb|right|Mephala's shrine in Nerien'eth's study]]
Some time in the early [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] named {{Lore Link|Nerien'eth}} uncovered the Ebony Blade while scouring [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] for artifacts to study. He brought it to his school of magic, [[Lore:Agea Relle|Agea Relle]] for study, believing it to be an ancient [[Lore:Akavir|Akaviri]] blade.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Star Teeth, Volume I}}}} Mephala whispered to Nerien'eth through the blade, and gradually corrupted his mind.{{ref|[[Online:Nerien'eth|Nerien'eth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Mezeluth|Mezeluth]]'s chamber during [[Online:Edge of Darkness|Edge of Darkness]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Mephala convinced him that his wife, {{Lore Link|Alanwe}}, was having an affair with one of her fellow instructors at Agea Relle.{{ref|[[Online:Alanwe#First Phylactery|Alanwe]]'s dialogue at the first phylactery during [[Online:Lover's Torment|Lover's Torment]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Love's Eternal Flame}}}} Eventually Nerien'eth snapped and ensnared the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of his students and fellow researchers. He summoned [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] to torture their souls for eternity, and shattered Alanwe's soul so that she would suffer the most.{{ref|[[Online:Alanwe|Alanwe]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Lover's Torment|Lover's Torment]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Mephala was greatly pleased by Nerien'eth's murder of so many who loved and trusted him, and so petitioned the [[Lore:Mad Architect|Mad Architect]] to construct elaborate instruments of torture from the remnants of the academy.{{ref|name=RO}} Agea Relle became known as the [[Lore:Crypt of Hearts|Crypt of Hearts]], and was avoided by the people of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] for many years.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] text for [[Online:Crypt of Hearts I|Crypt of Hearts I]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, a group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] entered the Crypt and cleared it of undead and Daedra, freeing the captured souls and restoring Alanwe's spirit.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Lover's Torment|Lover's Torment]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-creature-Sithera.jpg|A Spider Daedra, one of Mephala's servants|thumb|left]]
Instead of departing for the afterlife, Alanwe stayed behind to protect the vault from Nerien'eth, who returned in force later that year. Using the Ebony Blade, Nerien'eth summoned minions of Mephala, powerful Daedra who aided him in recapturing the lost souls.{{ref|[[Online:Alanwe#Edge of Darkness|Alanwe]]'s dialogue at the beginning of [[Online:Edge of Darkness|Edge of Darkness]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Undaunted returned, and once again aided Alanwe by investigating Nerien'eth's return. The group managed to locate the Ebony Blade, and were forced to slay Nerien'eth when he wielded it in battle. Alanwe's spirit then took the blade and communed with Mephala, the unknown whisperer. Mephala attempted to corrupt her by promising to help destroy the [[Lore:Weapons|weapon]], but Alanwe refused to listen to the Prince. Instead, she used her own soul to place a charm on it so that it would never remain with an individual for long. She then sent it far away, although the blade was fully empowered at the time and fought against her will, yearning to eventually return home. However, Alanwe's sacrifice did deny Mephala the blade's use during a great war between the forces of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|[[Online:Mephala#Edge of Darkness|Mephala]]'s dialogue at the end of [[Online:Edge of Darkness|Edge of Darkness]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

A lost temple to Mephala was uncovered by the [[Lore:Falinesti|Falinesti]] Faithful at the lost city's autumn resting site. The Prince took advantage of the faithfuls' yearning for answers and purpose, and spoke to them. They formed the Arbordawn Cult and became her Deathweavers. Assisted by a [[Lore:The Vestige|hero]], acclaimed mage [[Lore:Telenger the Artificer|Telenger the Artificer]] unsealed the temple and encountered Mephala within during their investigation.{{ref|[[Online:Mephala|Mephala]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Arbordawn Cult|The Arbordawn Cult]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Telenger promptly resealed the temple to prevent Mephala's influence from escaping.{{ref|[[Online:Telenger the Artificer|Telenger the Artificer]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Arbordawn Cult|The Arbordawn Cult]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] did not apply to Daedric Princes that were not present during its creation, so the [[Lore:Daedric Triad|Daedric Triad]] composed of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], Mephala, and [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] attempted to remake Nirn in their image during the Daedric War of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. Mephala and Clavicus Vile aided the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] in stopping Nocturnal from achieving infinite power circa {{Year|2E 582}} after she betrayed them.{{ref|[[Online:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s betrayal during [[Online:Lost in Translation|Lost in Translation]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Mephala|Mephala]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Necessary Alliance|A Necessary Alliance]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Mephala#The Crystal Tower|Mephala]]'s dialogue and actions during [[Online:The Crystal Tower|The Crystal Tower]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Vile and Mephala negotiated with Sotha Sil, and agreed to cease direct interference in the affairs of Nirn.{{ref|[[Online:Sotha Sil#A New Alliance|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A New Alliance|A New Alliance]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, a strange glass phial was in circulation among Tamriel's black markets. The bottle appeared empty, but when uncorked, dark, seductive whispers could be heard. The voice was believed to be that of Mephala.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband P#Phial of Daedric Whispers|Phial of Daedric Whispers]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] summoned Mephala and was tasked with assassinating a noble in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] area.{{ref|name=DFMQ|Events of [[Daggerfall:Mephala's Quest#Additional Dialogue|Mephala's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The troublesome noble sought the location of the {{Lore Link|Nightside Asylum}}, which was the base of operations for the Iliac branch of the Dark Brotherhood.{{ref|Dialogue that can occur when asking for ''any news'' after successful completion of [[Daggerfall:Mephala's Quest|Mephala's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} In return for completing this task, a worshipper of Mephala rewarded the agent with the Ebony Blade.{{ref|name=DFMQ}}

In {{Year|3E 427}}, Mephala tasked the [[Lore:Hero#Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] with assassinating a member of the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] who was killing without obtaining writs. In return for dealing with the rogue member, the Nerevarine was awarded the Ring of Khajiiti.{{ref|name=MWMephala|Events of [[Morrowind:Mephala's Quest|Mephala's Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] summoned Mephala and was tasked with starting a feud between two prominent families, the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] Ulfgar and [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] Dalvilu, in the settlement of [[Lore:Bleaker's Way|Bleaker's Way]]. The Hero murdered the leaders of the two families and planted evidence which implicated the other. The settlement descended into chaos, and the two families fought until only one remained. Mephala then rewarded the Hero with the Blade.{{ref|name=OBM|Events of the quest [[Oblivion:Mephala|Mephala]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
In {{Year|4E 201}}, Nelkir, the young son of Jarl [[Lore:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]], stumbled upon the locked door to the chamber where the Ebony Blade was kept. Mephala spoke to the child through her artifact, whispering secrets through the door. Word spread through the city of the jarl's "dark child", and the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] was tasked with helping the child. When questioned, Nelkir spoke of the "Whispering Lady". The Dragonborn investigated the "Whispering Door", and was contacted by Mephala. Under the guidance of Nelkir, the Dragonborn obtained a key to the door, and retrieved the Ebony Blade. However, having been locked away for so long, the weapon had lost much of its power. Mephala instructed the Dragonborn to empower the Blade by murdering people who trusted them.{{ref|name=SRTWD|Events of [[Skyrim:The Whispering Door|The Whispering Door]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notes==
*[[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] noted that Mephala shares a surface level similarity with [[Lore:Shagrath|Shagrath]], the Goddess of Spiders, in that they are both older than Creation and associated with spiders.{{ref|name=MtCS|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Selene}}}}
*The Khajiit refer to [[Lore:Sangiin|Sangiin]] as the ''God of Death and Secret Murder''.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Khajiit}}}} The Dunmer associated Mephala with assassination, but Sangiin appears to carry similar themes in Khajiiti faith.
*The Morag Tong's description of Mephala's black hands draws a parallel with the [[Lore:Black Hand|Black Hand]], the Dark Brotherhood's ruling body which is controlled by the whispers of the Night Mother.
