== Yashnag's Chiefdom vs. Western Skyrim ==
c. 2E 435 &mdash; {{Year|2E 467}}

There was a conflict between [[Lore:Yashnag gro-Yazgu|Yashnag]]'s Chiefdom (an Orcish state in western Falkreath Hold) and the Nordic kingdom of Western Skyrim from c. 2E 435 to {{Year|2E 467}}. The conflict has no official name, and it is unclear when it started, but it began 'more than 30 years' before 2E 467 and must have started after the Sack of Orsinium and the Skyrim Schism in 2E 431.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Skyrim}}}}

==[[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]]==
[[File:LG-cardart-Emeric, Covenant King.png|thumb|King Emeric]]
{{Year|2E 566}} - {{Year|2E 567}}{{ref|name=TFOKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}}

King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]] had been courting Rayelle, daughter of King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]] of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]], when he met and abruptly married Princess [[Lore:Maraya|Maraya]], daughter of the Redguard King [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]] of [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]]. Ranser was deeply offended, and withdrew his diplomats from Wayrest, surreptitiously raising levies and hiring mercenaries in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] over the course of a year.{{ref|name=TATOAM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} Finally, in [[Lore:Calendar|Last Seed]] of 2E 566, he swept down from the northern mountains, taking Emeric by surprise and nearly razing Wayrest immediately. Only the efforts of the king's personal guard saved the city, and forced a protracted siege.{{ref|name=TFOKR}} Ranser's forces were insufficient to take the fortified city by storm, and so the siege dragged on as the tide of the war turned against him. Ranser's aggression was unpopular even among his own nobles, and several prominent houses of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] withdrew their military support and sued for peace.{{ref|name=HTARH|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} While internal stresses took their toll, the original [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] paid dividends, as [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], and [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] were compelled to come to Wayrest's aid.

Meanwhile, Emeric was cannily leveraging his diplomatic connections. His recent marriage ties with the Redguards summoned a fleet of fierce warriors across the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]], while an offer to restore [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] brought the Orcs into the fray.{{ref|name=TFOKR}} Under [[Lore:Kurog gro-Bagrakh|Kurog gro-Bagrakh]], the old enemies of Shornhelm burned Ranser's holdings and were waiting to prevent the troops' retreat from the advancing Breton and Redguard forces. Caught between the hammer and anvil at Markwasten Moor, Ranser's army was utterly destroyed. Only a select few were able to escape with the king, who retreated to a tor overlooking the smoking ruins of Shornhelm, hoping to make a last stand.{{ref|name=KRT|{{Cite Book|King Ranser's Tirade}}}} Driven to desperation, Ranser turned to dark sorcery, but was murdered during the ritual by one of his own generals.{{ref|name=TTFOKR|{{Cite Book|The True Fate of King Ranser}}}} The Crown of Shornhelm went missing during the chaos, and that evil place has henceforth bore the name {{Lore Link|Traitor's Tor}}.{{ref|name=SCCOTN|{{Cite Book|Shornhelm, Crown City of the North}}}}

==Second Akaviri Invasion==
{{Year|2E 572}}{{ref|name=GTTEP|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Ebonheart Pact}} — anonymous ethnology (~2E 580)}}

Also called the '''Liberation War''' by the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heita-Meen Answers Your Questions}} – Q & A (2E 582)}} A fleet originating from the [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]i nation of [[Lore:Kamal (place)|Kamal]], under the command of [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]], invaded northern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] of the Second Era. United by a common enemy, the Nords of Eastern Skyrim, the Dunmer of Morrowind and the Argonians of Black Marsh jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders and formed an unusual alliance, the Ebonheart Pact.

=== Major Battles ===

* '''Battle of Windhelm''' or '''Sack of Windhelm''', 7 Sun's Height – 7 Last Seed 2E 572:{{ref|name=SIR}} For reasons unknown, the Akaviri fleet, although originating from the east, bypassed the eastern shores of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] and [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], instead landing near [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], in the north of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}} (c. 2E 582)}} Windhelm was taken by surprise and utterly sacked, resulting in the death of Queen [[Lore:Mabjaarn Flame-Hair|Mabjaarn Flame-Hair]] and her heir, Princess [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]], who both fell leading their troops,{{ref|name=TSAI}} known as the Blood Claws.{{ref|name=SIR|{{Cite Book|Second Invasion: Reports}} — anonymous diary of a Blood Claws commander (2E 572)}} Nurnhilde's younger brothers reached Windhelm during the battle, but too late to prevent its sack:{{ref|name=Brothers' War}} [[Lore:Fildgor|Fildgor Strong-Prince]] at the head of his Stormfist Brigade,{{ref|name=TSC|{{Cite Book|The Stormfist Clan}} (c. 2E 580)}} [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-Prince]] leading the "Pack of Bards".{{ref|name=JTSK}} The twin-brothers joined forces to repel the Akaviri invaders.{{ref|name=Brothers' War}}{{ref|name=TSC}}
[[File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg|thumb|500px|[[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]], [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]], and [[Lore:Indoril|Tanval Indoril]] at the [[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|Battle of Vivec's Antlers]] near [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]], which led to the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]].]]
* '''[[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|Battle of Vivec's Antlers]]''', 2E 572: Jorunn then sought the aid of the reclusive [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]], who summoned the mythical Ash-King, [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]].{{ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}} (c. 2E 575)}} With the aid of Wulfharth, Jorunn rallied his troops and fortified [[Lore:Riften|Riften]], expecting an imminent assault.{{ref|name=TSAI}} However, the invaders simply bypassed the city, passing into [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] in western [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|name=TSAI}} They were opposed by Dark Elf forces commanded by general Tanval of [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]], who staged a fighting retreat as the Akaviri slowly advanced towards eastern Stonefalls.{{ref|name=TSAI}}<br />
:There, on a beach of the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] just to the west of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] that would later be named [[Lore:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]],{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[ON:Online|ESO]] (2E 582)}} [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]i troops under the command of the living god [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] met and held them, while Jorunn and Wulfharth (and allegedly the Stormfirst Brigade{{ref|name=TSC}}) pursued the Akaviri force into a surprise pincer attack.{{ref|name=TSAI}} The fighting was fierce, but the Nord army was overextended, and victory seemed uncertain until an elite Argonian phalanx of {{Lore Link|Shellbacks}} led by [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]]{{ref|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}} (2E 582)}} reinforced the Dunmeri army, turning a stalemate into a slaughter.{{ref|name=UA|{{Cite Book|Unexpected Allies}} — Commentary on the Ebonheart Pact by a Nord soldier
(2E 582)}} With the aid of a tidal wave summoned by [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]], the Kamal were obliterated by the three unlikely allies.{{ref|name=ESO}}<br />
:This conflict united three races who had held great animosity towards each other for centuries, leading to the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]].{{ref|name=UA}} The Dunmer freed the Argonian slaves in recognition of their crucial aid.{{ref|name=GTTEP}} However, the Nords of Western Skyrim did not join the Ebonheart Pact, as they had not been affected by the Second Akaviri Invasion and thus saw no need for an alliance, were skeptical that a Nordic–Dunmeri–Argonian alliance could work given their historic emnity, and had also been at odds with their fellow Nords of Eastern Skyrim for a long time.{{ref|name=TSAI}}

* '''Brothers' War''', 2E 572: As Jorunn the Skald-Prince returned home to Windhelm, a succession dispute arose between him and his twin-brother [[Lore:Fildgor|Fildgor Strong-Prince]], who had jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders after the Sack of Windhelm in which their elder sister Queen Nurnhilde fell.{{ref|name=Brothers' War|[[Lore:The Brothers' War|The Brothers' War]] — anonymous history (c. 2E 575)}}{{ref|name=TSC}} The brothers disagreed on who should succeed their sister and how to rule the land going forward, leading Jorunn to challenge Fildgor to single combat.{{ref|name=Brothers' War}}{{ref|name=TSC}} This long and fierce duel, known as the [[Lore:The Brothers' War|Brothers' War]], was won by Jorunn, who exiled Fildgor and was crowned the Skald-King of Eastern Skyrim,{{ref|name=Brothers' War}}{{ref|name=TSC}} three weeks after the Battle of Vivec's Antlers.{{ref|name=JTSK}}

==Frostfall Coup==
[[File:LG-cardart-Frostfall Coup.jpg|thumb|Euraxia Tharn during the Frostfall Coup]]
{{Year|2E 576}}{{ref|name=TFC|{{Cite Book|The Frostfall Coup}}}}

[[Lore:Euraxia Tharn|Euraxia Tharn]] entered [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] as the Empire's envoy following the start of [[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|Varen's Rebellion]]. Received at [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]] as an emissary of the new emperor at the head of a large contingent of [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] mercenaries, Euraxia turned on her hosts and deposed [[Lore:Hemakar|King Hemakar]] of Rimmen in the '''Frostfall Coup''', slaughtering the royal family and establishing herself as queen.{{ref|name=TFC}}{{ref|name=ESOKhamira|[[ON:Khamira|Khamira]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} From Rimmen Euraxia consolidated an iron grip over much of northern Elsweyr,{{ref|name=TFC}} training siege weapons on her own capital to quell any signs of revolt from the local [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]].{{ref|name=AbnurElsweyr|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Unbeknownst to Euraxia, however, Hemakar's daughter [[Lore:Khamira|Khamira]] survived the Frostfall Coup, and would ensure her eventual overthrow after the Usurper Queen's attempt to ally with [[Lore:Kaalgrontiid|Kaalgrontiid]] and his rage of [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]] in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=TwoQueens|Events of [[ON:Two Queens|Two Queens]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}
