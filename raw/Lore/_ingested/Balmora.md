[[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]] ([[Lore:Dark Elvish|Dark Elvish]] for ''"Stonewood"''{{Ref|name=MWMatuul|[[Morrowind:Sul-Matuul|Sul-Matuul]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} or ''"Stoneforest"''){{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Stoneforest|Stoneforest]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}{{Ref|name=IWTB|{{Cite Book|Interview With Three Booksellers|ns_base=GEN}}}} was one of the four cities of the district of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], known primarily as the district seat of power for the Great [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]]. The city is built in the river valley of the [[Lore:West Gash|West Gash]] and was the largest settlement on Vvardenfell, only behind the temple-metropolis, [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]].{{Ref|name=GtV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}} It was initially a stronghold of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]], but through some business and architectural maneuvers, it was assimilated into a town of House Hlaalu.{{Ref|name=HCS|{{Cite Book|Hlaalu Construction Syndic}}}} The city was devastated during the eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] in {{Year|4E 5}}, but was subsequently rebuilt and re-inhabited.{{Ref|name=TRY2|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}} <noinclude>

==Layout & Geography==
[[File:MW-book-Balmora Region.jpg|thumb|left|Map of the Balmora Region]]
[[File:MW-place-Odai River.jpg|thumb|right|The Odai River & Balmora]]
Balmora is a vast city built on the banks of the [[Lore:Odai River|Odai River]], in the midst of a great valley that marks the southernmost edge of the West Gash. It was essentially the gateway between the West Gash and the pristine grazelands that laden the south-central coast. Much of the land that surrounds the city is rocky scrubland, natural stone menhirs, and green hills. It provides little flatland for other people to settle on but despite this, the roads are generally toward ground-level, making them easily identifiable. In the Third Era, the Imperial Fort Moonmoth was built southeast from Balmora, right on the [[Lore:Foyada Mamaea|Foyada Mamaea]]. The main road ran through Balmora, going further north toward the Imperial-charter town, [[Lore:Caldera|Caldera]] and the rest of the region, or going down south near [[Lore:Lake Amaya (Vvardenfell)|Lake Amaya]] and [[Lore:Pelagiad|Pelagiad]]. The city itself is split into three districts. The Commercial District is found on the western side of the river, where it is the home of several businesses and the town plaza. Each city gate is found in this area and the [[Lore:Silt Strider|Silt Strider]] port is located by the south gate. The [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] had their own council club in the commercial district. The main square and the road that contains the local guilds is called the Trade Street.{{Ref|name=MWGenericF|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue F|Fighters Guild]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Labor Town, on the east side of the river is the general residential district, housing the common-folk and several corner clubs. Finally, the High Town is on the northwestern hill that looms over the city. It is accessible from the commercial district, which lies at the base of the hill. It houses Balmora's nobility and even the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] of Balmora.{{Ref|name=GtB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Balmora}}}} Stoneforest is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Markgran Forest|Markgran Forest]] to the north and [[Lore:Old Run|Old Run]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

==History==
Balmora was already around during the time of [[Lore:Ulfsild the Evergreen|Ulfsild the Evergreen]], who used the ''[[Lore:Fable of the Netch (Annotated)|Fable of the Netch]]'' to locate the [[Lore:Netch|Netch]] [[Lore:Luminary|Luminary]] whose presence around Vvardenfell caused an increase of happiness around the island. The fable mentions a [[Lore:Silt Strider|Silt Strider]] caravanner from a "stonewood riparian settlement" Ulfsild deduced was Balmora making a fortune from encountering the Netch, whose presence caused the panicked Silt Strider to launch the caravanner into the water, allowing the caravanner to find a cache of treasured jewels that had been lost in the area.{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Fable of the Netch (Annotated)}}}}{{ref|name=Crow|[[Online:The Crow|The Crow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Balmora is mentioned in the historical-fiction, ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]''. The book mentions a luxurious palace in Balmora owned by the Warrior-Poet of the Tribunal, [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]{{Ref|name=2920Last}} with a grand plaza in the front. It also says that [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] came here to heal after their confrontation with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], which left [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] burned asunder.{{Ref|name=2920Dusk|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}} However, the modern iteration of Balmora was built sometime after the fall of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{ref|name=MWGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Morrowind Gazetteer''}}{{rp|6}}

===Balmora of House Redoran and Hlaalu Dealings===
[[File:MW-banner-House Hlaalu.png|thumb|left|Banner of House Hlaalu]]
[[File:SR-banner-House Redoran.png|thumb|right|Banner of House Redoran]]
In the midst of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], the Great House Redoran expanded their reach into the island of Vvardenfell and sought to build various settlements across the region. In light of this, House Redoran struck a deal with the {{Lore Link|Hlaalu Construction Syndic (faction)}}, in which they used their labor and resources to build these new towns for what was deemed a suspiciously low cost. Bustling trade towns like Balmora and [[Lore:Suran|Suran]] were built (or expanded upon, according to some sources),{{Ref|name=2920Last|{{Cite Book|2920, Last Seed (v8)}}}}{{Ref|name=IG|{{Cite Book|Imbel Genealogy}}}} Though the contract stated that the towns were to be built in the Redoran style of architecture, the Syndic built them in the Hlaalu-style of architecture. At that point, House Hlaalu's only foothold was the fishing-village of [[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]] but their involvement in these places gave them the leeway to create business opportunities and have a near-monopoly on the region's trade.{{Ref|name=HCS}} Even then, Balmora and Suran were still governed by House Redoran, with the former administrated by Councilor {{Lore Link|Eris Releth}}.

In {{Year|2E 582}}, the son of the councilor, {{Lore Link|Ulran Releth}} was a Redoran officer and unlike most house-Dunmer, did not have hatred toward the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]]. When one of his soldiers killed an ashlander trader despite Ulran's demand to stand down and targeted another, the captain was forced to put him down, which promptly had him exiled by the Redoran council. He left to the wilderness and took refuge with the [[Lore:Zainab Tribe|Zainab Tribe]] near {{Lore Link|Kudanat}}. However, the then newly-promoted Captain {{Lore Link|Brivan Malrom}} gathered his Khajiiti mercenaries and set siege to the camp, slaughtering tribes folk in droves despite orders to avoid violence. Ulran was killed deep in the mines.{{Ref|name=ONUlran|[[Online:Ulran Releth|Ulran Releth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=HRO|{{Cite Book|House Redoran Orders|ns_base=ON}}}} Releth's banishment brought ire to the Councilor's daughter, {{Lore Link|Veya Releth}} and so she ran away in home to find her brother, completely unaware of his fate. A town-wide search was conducted by the city guard, to the point where outlanders were asked to help.{{Ref|name=ONFaldrus|[[Online:Sergeant Faldrus|Sergeant Faldrus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===The Releth Conspiracy & the Redoran's Folly===
The councilor enlisted the help of [[Lore:The Vestige|outlander]] and after chasing Veya's close friends, came into contact with the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] and started to look for Veya's mentor, the assassin, [[Lore:Naryu Virian|Naryu Virian]].{{Ref|name=ONPast|[[Online:Fleeing the Past|Fleeing the Past]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The outlander had become involved in what had turned into a conspiracy, stealing a register in a manor in Labor Town to uncover details about Ulran's exile. Veya was hiding out in an old fungal cave in the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] until the Redoran mercenaries had tracked her down. Veya, Naryu, and the outlander attempted to escape through the {{Lore Link|Mallapi}} tunnels until they were cornered by Captain Brivan and Councilor Eris. They were able to pass through on the condition that they meet again on Veya's own terms and so the trio went to the ruined Kudanat Camp, where they learned about Ulran's fate.{{Ref|name=ONFaith|[[Online:Of Faith and Family|Of Faith and Family]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

The truth came out when Veya interrogated a Redoran soldier imprisoned in {{Lore Link|Hlormaren}}. The incident with Ulran was meant to be a test of loyalty to House Redoran conducted by then lieutenant Brivan. Even the initial instigation was a ploy to test the officer. In her unbridled rage, Veya left to kill the captain, her father, and the Redoran Council at the garrison outside of town.{{Ref|name=ONVatola|[[Online:Vatola Telem|Vatola Telem]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Her revenge was delivered ruthlessly on Brivan and Eris, but the outlander subdued her before she could cause any harm. High Councilor {{Lore Link|Meriath}} intervened and promised to sweep the incident under the rug as long as her story was corroborated. Since the Redoran acted in poor faith and Morag Tong rules were violated by Veya's actions it was appropriate and beneficial to all involved to cover up the whole situation. In the end, Naryu and the outlander were left to decide Veya's fate.{{Ref|name=ONReunion|[[Online:Family Reunion|Family Reunion]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

In the mid-Second Era, when half of Vvardenfell was occupied by House Redoran, Balmora became the new capital of sport hunting in Morrowind, with game such as [[Lore:Nix-Ox|Nix-Ox]]en and [[Lore:Fetcherfly|Fetcherfly]] Hive Golems being pursued.{{Ref|name=ONHoliday|[[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Costumes#"Holiday in Balmora" Outfit|"Holiday in Balmora" Outfit]] description text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Balmora in the Third Empire===
[[File:MW-npc-Nileno Dorvayn.jpg|thumb|right|Kinsmen Nileno Dorvayn]]
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the village of Stoneforest was an active settlement. It was ruled by Count Caskan and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Karththor Dale|Karththor Dale]].{{Ref|name=AR}} In {{Year|3E 414}}, the formerly protected Temple-territory, Vvardenfell had been reopened to the local populous of the Empire and waves of people built communities in the previously uninhabited region. The island was split into four territories and House Hlaalu claimed the southwest portion of Vvardenfell that had once belonged to House Redoran, extending between the Bitter Coast and Balmora, to the Ascadian Isles and Suran. The city of Balmora had grown exponentially in the years that followed Vvardenfell's organization as a Provincial District under Duke Vedam Dren, becoming the second-most populated settlement behind Vivec City.{{Ref|name=GtB}}{{Ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=KRP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ken Rolston's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} In {{Year|3E 426}}, there was a minor tax revolt in Balmora which was suppressed. The Hlaalu claimed there was no harm done in any manner, while the Redoran claimed that there was significant damage in property and life.{{Ref|name=YB|{{Cite Book|Yellow Book of 3E 426}}}}{{Ref|name=RB|{{Cite Book|Red Book of 3E 426}}}} It did concern the Hlaalu Council, who sent deputations to Duke [[Lore:Vedam Dren|Vedam Dren]] about the high tax rates and the effects of the high tariff of trade.{{Ref|name=YB}}

During [[Lore:The Blight|the Blight Outbreak]] in {{Year|3E 427}}, the Hlaalu Council were not stationed in their regional-capital, instead, they were in their various estates and manors in the Ascadian Isles. The highest-ranking member of House Hlaalu that stayed in the council hall was Kinsmen {{Lore Link|Nileno Dorvayn}}, who governed the city while the council was away. It was at this time that a certain outlander was present on Vvardenfell. They frequented Balmora's labor town where their [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] contact, [[Lore:Caius Cosades|Caius Cosades]] would relay them the information and missions in regards to the Nerevarine Prophecy.{{Ref|name=MWMain|[[Morrowind:Main Quest|Caius Cosades]]' main quests in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Eventually, Cosades was recalled back to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and that individual was soon recognized as the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]].{{Ref|name=MWMehra|[[Morrowind:Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies|Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===The Red Year & Onward===
In {{Year|4E 5}}, the [[Lore:Ministry of Truth|Ministry of Truth]] crashed into Vivec City and caused a great chain reaction that led to the eruption of Red Mountain. The event became known as the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], in which severe ashstorms and lava floods pillaged the entire island of Vvardenfell. While mainland cities like [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] were largely left unscathed, the entirety of Vvardenfell was flooded by seas of lava and great cities from Balmora to [[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] were completely destroyed. A Redoran-led relief effort was sent into the island roughly a month later. The commoner, {{Lore Link|Deros Dran}} was sent to Balmora and found the city in shambles, with nothing left standing. There, Deros Dran spent two months rebuilding, which could imply that Balmora took roughly two months to rebuild.{{Ref|name=TRY2}} By the late Fourth Era, Balmora was still an inhabited settlement. The Nord Tyra Blood-Fire, founder of the infamous {{Lore Link|Crimson Dirks}} bandit gang, grew up an orphan in the streets of the city and was still alive in {{Year|4E 201}}. Given her human lifespan, this would indicate that Balmora was an active settlement only a few decades prior, well over a century and a half after the city's devastation during the Red Year.{{Ref|name=TCD1|{{Cite Book|The Crimson Dirks, v1}}}} Despite this, a Thane in Skyrim spoke of Balmora as if it were no longer standing in 4E 201.{{Ref|name=SRErikur}}
