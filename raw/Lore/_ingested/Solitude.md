[[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], also known as '''Haafingar''',{{Ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} is one of the nine major cities in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Skyrim towns.png|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:MER-art-Skyrim Anthology Map.jpg|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} situated on the highland bluffs of [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar Hold]]. The city is a major trading capital in the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]], with an expansive and illustrious history that gives it its prominent political power. It is the home of the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]], an institute that preserves the ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] traditions of [[Lore:Music|music]] and story-telling,{{Ref|name=PGE1E}} as well as [[Lore:Coterie of Organized Scholars|Coterie of Organized Scholars]], which was one of the oldest scholarly institutions in the region.{{ref|name=Contraband|[[ON:Contraband C#Coterie of Organized Scholar's Stamp|Coterie of Organized Scholar's Stamp]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Tinzen|[[ON:Tinzen|Tinzen]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Solitude is the [[Lore:Empire|Imperial]] seat of power in Skyrim and has been the most influenced by Imperial culture since the union of King [[Lore:Mantiarco|Mantiarco]] and Queen [[Lore:Potema|Potema Septim]].{{Ref|name=SRLoad|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#City-Specific|Solitude & Winterhold]] loading screen in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=WTWXI|{{Cite Book|Walking the World, Vol XI}}}}{{Ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Skyrim's Rule}}}} Solitude is currently the capital of Skyrim,{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|418}} and in the provincial schism, it was the capital of Western Skyrim.{{Ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}}<noinclude>

==Layout & Geography==
[[File:SR-place-Solitude 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Great Arch]]
[[File:SR-place-Solitude 03.jpg|thumb|right|The Well District]]
Solitude is one of Skyrim's largest cities, as well as one of its key ports, built on a natural landmark known as the Great Arch. This arch proved vital in Solitude's growth as a kingdom.{{Ref|name=WTWXI}} In its later years, the main city was built atop of this arch while the busier-sections of Solitude, such as the crowded harbors on the coast are built underneath, next to the [[Lore:Karth River|Karth River]].{{Ref|name=PGE1E}} The main road runs underneath the arch and goes around the region's east coast until it reaches the hinterlands and the provincial border. The city lies on the southern highlands of [[Lore:Mount Kilkreath|Mount Kilkreath]], specifically, the highest point on top of the arch. Because of the uneven terrain outside of the city walls, there is very little room for private land for farms. There was a lumber mill built on the Karth River and even small farms on the highlands, between the main road and the docks. Solitude is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Dragon Bridge|Dragon Bridge]] to the south and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] to the far west, towards [[Lore:Jehanna|Jehanna]].{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

The road towards Solitude's entrance is heavily-defended by the gates, the Squall Gate and the Storm Gate, as well as several towers. Solitude contains a unique style of architecture only seen in Haafingar, consisting of rounded architecture and pointed roofs. This is attributed to High King Erling. The city's western district is known as the Well District (also called the ''Market District''),{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|418}} To the north, on the mountainside is {{Lore Link|Castle Dour}}, the city fortress that contains the Temple of the Divines and the Emperor's Tower. The city's eastern district, known as the Avenues District (also called the ''"Stately Avenues of Old Solitude"''{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|420}}{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Alik'r}}}} or commonly the ''"Avenues"''){{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|420}} is the residential area, known for its regal and grandiose streets.{{Ref|name=TA}} The Bards College, the Hall of the Dead, and the seat of power, the [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]] are located here. Both the east and west sides are separated by an arching bridge that connects the Castle Dour to the Windmill, one of Solitude's most iconic landmarks. Its wind power is used to open the massive warehouse that is stored underneath the mountain.{{Ref|name=WTWXI}} As stated before, the docks are located underneath the Great Arch, but it is said that it can contain a hundred shops.{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} There is a vast cavern underneath the mountain that is currently used by the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]] as one of their largest warehouses.

==History==
===Solitude's Early History===
[[File:SR-interior-Temple of the Divines.jpg|thumb|left|The Temple of the Divines]]
Solitude's founding dates back centuries ago before it extended onto the great arch. It was an encampment on the Karth River situated at the base of the landmark, where it was used as a windbreaker. It was an ideal base for the ancient Nords, who built fur and wooden shelters on the river's shores.{{Ref|name=SACJ|{{Cite Book|Solitude: A Charred Journal}}}} The founders of Solitude were said to be deeply devoted to the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]], and this sentiment continues to live in modern-times as the local temple became the biggest place of worship in Skyrim. The town started as a simple castle keep, which is now known as Castle Dour, but it later gained more of its footing as a well-defended settlement during the coronation of High King [[Lore:Erling|Erling]], who ordered the construction of Solitude's outer walls around the keep, and requested for each stone of the wall to be blessed by the Divines.{{ref|[[Online:Vundling|Vundling]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} He later ordered the construction of a towering bridge that separates the Well District from the east-side. The city's unique style of architecture was chosen by the High King himself, but the true mastermind that created it is unknown, though the person, {{Lore Link|Thryrahilde}} is said to have championed the motif before the walls were built.{{Ref|name=SACJ}} Sometime before the province of Skyrim was formally founded by High King [[Lore:Harald|Harald]] in {{Year|1E 143}}, the Emperor's Tower, the original home of Haafingar's kings and the centerpiece of the keep was replaced by the [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]].{{Ref|name=WTWXI}}

[[File:SR-quest-Tending the Flames 03.jpg|thumb|right|The Burning of King Olaf]]
The earliest known texts that describe Solitude as a well-established kingdom date to the early First Era, when the province held ties with the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]], concurrent to the formation of the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]]. The Kingdom of Solitude was one of the greatest oppositions against [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|Olaf One-Eye]], who rose to supremacy in the wake of the [[Lore:War of Succession|War of Succession]]. Olaf's wrath over the city was swiftly-executed{{Ref|name=KOV|{{Cite Book|King Olaf's Verse}}}} and animosity between the king and the city created one of Solitude's longest-living traditions, the ''Burning of King Olaf''. It is held annually in the courtyard at the Bards College. Solitude has since become one of the most significant trading ports, collaborating with other cities of worth such as [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]], [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], and [[Lore:Anvil|West Anvil]].{{Ref|name=PGE1E}} Following the [[Lore:First Akaviri Invasion|First Akaviri Invasion]] in {{Year|1E 2704}}, Emperor [[Lore:Reman|Reman I]] shifted focus to the Reach, splitting into its [[Lore:The Reach|east]] and [[Lore:Western Reach|western-half]]. He utilized several forays from both Solitude and [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] to quell the spirits of the local [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], but these attempts were unsuccessful since taming the landscape was much harder for these soldiers.{{Ref|name=EGTB|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}}

===The Latter First Era & The Birth of Western Skyrim===
[[File:ON-npc-High King Svargrim.jpg|thumb|left|High King Svargrim; the Wolf of Solitude]]
In the late-First Era, the Kingdom of Solitude was ruled by the tyrannical High Queen, "Head Rolling" {{Lore Link|Roleke}}, who killed twenty rebels within less than five minutes. Her reign brought contempt among her people, who took arms and began their siege over the Blue Palace. From another region, the young jarl of a forgotten hold, {{Lore Link|Irthvyd}} joined the uprising and approached the city gates. However, he was stopped by a visage of [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], who told him that the High Queen was killed by her progeny, who in turn died in battle. Solitude was in need of order and the Goddess of Storms chose Irthvyd to take assume the mantle of High King. He was thus named the new High King of Solitude and established peace throughout Haafingar Hold, however, he died without an heir, and so his senior advisor, {{Lore Link|Temylda}} ascended the throne after a fight with several other jarls.{{Ref|name=SOSR|{{Cite Book|Scandals of Solitude's Royalty}}}}

[[File:ON-npc-Queen Gerhyld.jpg|thumb|right|Queen Gerhyld]]
Forty years later, Temylda's reign ended and a new leader was chosen, out of bloodshot and violence. The warrior, {{Lore Link|Alldimar}}, known for his many skirmishes with the Reachmen, won the title of High Kingship from the child successor, using his years of knowledge in arcane magic and his great sense of warfare. Peace swept through the kingdom but random people throughout his tenure died of bizarre accidents, and his cold-eyed advisor, {{Lore Link|Freiretta}} was the subject of countless rumors and conspiracies. By the time Alldimar had passed, the secrets were revealed. The skulls of those "accident" victims were stored in a hidden compartment of the High King's quarters. Whatever purpose it may have been is unknown, but he has since been remembered as the "Ghostmaker". Servants at the Blue Palace say they hear voices and see shapes out of the corner of their eye to this day.{{Ref|name=SOSR}}

In {{Year|2E 431}}, the assassination of High King [[Lore:Logrolf|Logrolf]] brought about a time of change in the province of Skyrim. Naturally, the throne would have been passed to the High King's next of kin, ideally, Jarl [[Lore:Freydis|Freydis]] of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]]. However, tension had arisen between the east and west when Jarl [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]] of Solitude had challenged her claim to leadership and thus called for a moot. The moot had chosen Svartr as their High King, and what followed was a decisive schism that created the West and East Kingdoms of Skyrim, with the west under the rule of High King Svartr of Solitude.{{Ref|name=TCoF|{{Cite Book|The Crown of Freydis}}}} His domain became the capital of the western kingdom, which remained active for several years. One of his descendants, [[Lore:Svargrim|Svargrim]] the Wolf had defended the hold throughout his reign, such as when he beheaded the [[Lore:Sea Giant|Sea Giant]] captain, {{Lore Link|Kruntharth}} and later his escapades in the {{Lore Link|Great Troll Rut}} of {{Year|2E 565}}, a time of unspeakable horror. Since that day, [[Lore:Troll|Troll]]s had avoided Solitude for years.{{Ref|name=MTCHKS|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - High King Svargrim}}}}

===Harrowstorms and the Calamity of Solitude===
[[File:ON-quest-Dark Clouds Over Solitude.jpg|thumb|left|Gerhyld's Assassination]]
[[File:ON-quest-Greymoor Rising 08.jpg|thumb|right|The Harrowstorm over Solitude]]
After a failed assassination attempt on the Eastern High King, [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]], the [[Lore:Icereach Coven|Icereach Coven]] had traveled to the heart of Western Skyrim and began to sow the seeds for their next endeavor.{{Ref|name=ONCoven|[[Online:The Coven Conundrum|The Coven Conundrum]] prologue quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Agents of the Skald-King, namely [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]] traveled to the capital, Solitude and began their investigation. Their sources uncovered strange houseware imported from [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] and pamphlets for a {{Lore Link|Dusktown|mining operation}} in the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]] handed out by [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]].{{Ref|name=BJ|{{Cite Book|Brondold's Journal}}}}

After one of these agents was killed in action, Lyris enlisted the help of a [[Lore:The Vestige|traveler]] and met with the Queen of Western Skyrim, {{Lore Link|Gerhyld}}. Unlike the High King, who has a deep hatred for the Skald-King and thus suspects the agents, she was more willing to hear them out. She offered them more time to provide irrefutable proof. They scoured the city for more information and later traveled to Mount Kilkreath, where they witnessed a massacre at the [[Lore:Kilkreath Temple|Kilkreath Temple]] and disrupted the first of several [[Lore:Harrowstorms|harrowstorms]].{{Ref|name=ONGathering|[[Online:The Gathering Storm|The Gathering Storm]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} With all of the evidence in hand, the two return to Solitude, only to find the Queen had been assassinated.{{Ref|name=ONClouds|[[Online:Dark Clouds Over Solitude|Dark Clouds Over Solitude]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Despite the incident, High King Svargrim chose not to make the proper initiative to deal with the threat, so his daughter, Princess {{Lore Link|Svana}} and the eastern agents took it upon themselves to warn the neighboring holds, [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]] and [[Lore:Karthald|Karthald]] about the impending danger. While they managed to recruit Jarl {{Lore Link|Reddharn}} of [[Lore:Morthal|Morthal]], they found the mountain town of [[Lore:Karthwatch|Karthwatch]] devastated at the hands of another harrowstorm created by the coven. Refugees were escorted back to Solitude, where they remained safe within the confines of the city. Through Jarl Reddharn and Magister {{Lore Link|Fridelja}} of Karthwatch's testimonies, Svargrim begrudgingly moved forward by rounding up the harrowed to lock them away in the Tower of the Wolf in Castle Dour.{{Ref|name=ONDanger|[[Online:Danger in the Holds|Danger in the Holds]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} He later sent soldiers to guard Solitude's outer defense and began to stockpile Castle Dour for the impending siege. One of their allies, the vampire, {{Lore Link|Fennorian}} of [[Lore:House Ravenwatch|House Ravenwatch]] began to develop an elixir that would bypass the harrowstorm and disrupt its effects. It was after everyone in his hold turned against him, Svargrim revealed his allegiance with the Gray Host and their lord, [[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]].{{Ref|name=ONHost|[[Online:The Gray Host|The Gray Host]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} 

[[File:ON-quest-Daughter of the Wolf.jpg|thumb|right|The Skald-King and the Daughter of the Wolf]]
[[File:ON-quest-Daughter of the Wolf 05.jpg|thumb|left|The Mourning Aftermath]]
After his reveal, the Gray Host took control over Castle Dour, which was sealed from the rest of the city. Svargrim disappeared from the palace and delved into the underground realm of [[Lore:Greymoor Caverns|Blackreach]]. Svana and her companions followed suit and fought their way through {{Lore Link|Greymoor Keep}}, an enormous vampire castle found directly underneath Solitude. In the keep, the High King underwent a gruesome ritual that turned him into a [[Lore:Vampire Lord|vampire lord]] and provided him control over the harrowstorm that would soon engulfed Solitude. From the Temple of the Divines, the harrowed poured out of Castle Dour and attempted to break through to the city proper. Fennorian and Lyris Titanborn fought waves upon waves of enemies at the base floor of the keep while Svana and the traveler ascended the Tower of the Wolf to confront High King Svargrim. After a great battle, the High King was slain and the Gray Host had been defeated.{{Ref|name=ONRising|[[Online:Greymoor Rising|Greymoor Rising]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Mere moments after, the High King of Eastern Skyrim, Jorunn, and his son, Prince {{Lore Link|Irnskar}} had marched into Solitude, offering their assistance to the cause, despite their history with the west. Everyone gathered for a traditional wake, dedicated to the people that lost their lives throughout the event. Even with the city saved from crisis, the war with the Gray Host continued. Fennorian developed his elixir, and the Jarls of Skyrim devised a plan to spread the cure across their holds.{{Ref|name=ONDaughter|[[Online:Daughter of the Wolf|Daughter of the Wolf]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

===Wulfharth Heirs===
Around the time of the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]], in the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], it is insinuated that the [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]] Heirs reigned over Haafingar, and employed a militia of marines referred to as the {{Lore Link|Longboat Legions}}. [[Lore:Tobias Hold-None|Tobias Hold-None]] served the Heirs for a time as a Longboat marine, before he later deserted and turned smuggler.{{Ref|name=RGTobias|[[General:Redguard.com Archive/Characters|Tobias' Biography – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}}

===The Wolf-Queen & the Mad King===
[[File:SR-npc-Pelagius the Mad.jpg|thumb|right|King Pelagius Septim III]]
[[File:SR-place-Blue Palace 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Blue Palace; Castle Solitude]]
In the early [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the elderly King [[Lore:Mantiarco|Mantiarco]] reigned over Solitude along with his late wife Queen Amodetha who was loved by the old Nordic Houses. His trusted military warchief was Lord Thone and the heir apparent was his son Balthorgh. In the years of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], under the rule of Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim II|Uriel Septim II]], the relations between the Nordic kingdom and the Empire had slowly waned, prompting the union between the sixty-two-year-old King Mantiarco and the then fourteen-year-old, [[Lore:Potema|Potema Septim]]. At first, she was only a pawn of a political marriage, but the King's love for her had led to her rise in power to totality. After several miscarriages, in {{Year|3E 97}}, Potema gave birth to [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel Mantiarco]]. Potema and a lord named Vhokken conspired to plant evidence of the late Queen Amodetha having an affair with the King's trusted warchief Lord Thone, siring Barthorgh. This led to the heir apparent being banished from Solitude, Lord Vhokken replacing him as warchief, and the newborn Uriel Mantiarco quickly named the heir of Solitude.{{ref|name=TWQ2|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v2}}}} In {{Year|3E 99}}, the Emperor and later in the spring, King Mantiarco had passed away, leaving the kingdom in a joint-rule between King Uriel and Queen Potema. In this time, the Queen had established relationships across the northern tier of the empire, (i.e., [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] and [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]]), sowing the seeds of contempt against the Empire.{{Ref|name=BotWQ|{{Cite Book|Biography of the Wolf Queen}}}}

Her relationship with the Empire came to a climax in the coronation of Empress [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]] when she degraded the newly-named ruler and her mother, {{Lore Link|Magna}} by questioning their legitimacy to the Ruby Throne. King Uriel III and Queen Potema were banished from the inner court, returning to Solitude to form a large-scale rebellion against the Third Empire. These events gave way to the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]], which escalated to the death of the Empress in {{Year|3E 114}} and the ascension of Emperor Uriel Septim III. But the conflict slowly ended in the favor of Kings [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus I]] and [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus]] when the former defeated the Emperor and brought him to his death in [[Lore:Gilane|Gilane]].{{Ref|name=BotWQ}} 

By {{Year|3E 137}}, the kingdom became a land of death and the elderly Queen Potema was kept in the Blue Palace, as Emperor Cephorus I relentlessly sacked the city of Solitude. With no real army at her disposal, Potema used [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]], her necromancers and the undead to face against her brother. She died sometime during the siege but her legacy continued through underground factions like the {{Lore Link|Hörme}}.{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} But some theologians hypothesized that Potema became a spiritual-daedra that continually haunts the Blue Palace and turns its monarchs insane. This is one of the theories that is attributed to [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius III]]'s insanity.{{Ref|name=BotWQ}}

Following Potema's defeat, Pelagius III was named the King of Solitude, placed there by Emperor Cephorus.{{Ref|name=BotWQ}} In his time spent in Solitude, Pelagius began to exhibit signs of insanity which earned the moniker, ''"Pelagius the Mad."'' There were many stories of his peculiar eccentricities, such as his constant shift in weight but expressive personality. These were far and few between, but only became worse when his father, Magnus I became the Emperor of Tamriel in {{Year|3E 140}} and more attention went towards the Kingdom of Solitude, knowing that Pelagius III was the heir-apparent. Legends had spread throughout the Empire as Pelagius III's exploits became more well-known, such as countless declarations of war to the Kingdom of [[Lore:Silvenar|Silvenar]] or the many cases of stripping during public speeches. By the decree of his father, Pelagius III married Duchess [[Lore:Katariah|Katariah]] of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], whose family, the [[Lore:Ra'athim|Ra'athim Clan]] was acquainted with Potema during the war. Despite the political motives, it was also a personal choice by the Emperor, who recognized Katariah as a shrewd diplomat. When the Emperor passed away in {{Year|3E 145}}, Pelagius III and Katariah moved to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and his sister, [[Lore:Jolethe|Jolethe]] became the Queen of Solitude.{{Ref|name=TMoP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}}

===The Late-Third & Fourth Era===
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Solitude was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Hania and it had a rivalry with [[Lore:Snowhawk|Snowhawk]].{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Solitude|Solitude]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} At some point in time, the Kingdom of Solitude waged war with the distant Kingdom of [[Lore:Farrun|Farrun]]. They left one of the soldiers, the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]]i mercenary, {{Lore Link|Menegur}} to man the walls of [[Lore:Cascabel Hall|Cascabel Hall]] against the enemy. After three months of maintaining Cascabel, Solitude and Farrun made a truce and gave the castle to Menegur for his valiant effort.{{Ref|name=TRG|{{Cite Book|The Rear Guard}}}} By the waning years of the Third Era in {{Year|3E 432}}, it was ruled by King [[Lore:Thian|Thian]], in the time where Solitude was one of the most influential counties in the province. Under Thian's leadership, it expanded much further with the union with Queen [[Lore:Macalla|Macalla]], engulfing another major port city, the town of [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]. But it also went further north with the annexation of the Fiefdom of [[Lore:Roscrea|Roscrea]], former territory conquered by Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim V|Uriel Septim V]] in {{Year|3E 271}}. Solitude soon sponsored several expeditions that attempted to explore the northern waters beyond Skyrim's coast and Roscrea.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}}

[[File:SR-npc-High King Torygg.jpg|thumb|left|High King Torygg]]
[[File:SR-npc-Elisif the Fair.jpg|thumb|right|Jarl Elisif the Fair]]
Another city that prospered in that time was the former capital, [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]],{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} but its legacy was later destroyed by the [[Lore:Great Collapse|Great Collapse]] in {{Year|4E 122}}.{{Ref|name=OtGC|{{Cite Book|On the Great Collapse}}}} By the next couple centuries, Solitude was ruled by High King [[Lore:Istlod|Istlod]], who led for nearly twenty-five years until he passed away. The moot later chose his son, [[Lore:Torygg|Torygg]] to be his successor.{{Ref|name=SRStentor}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, the High King was approached by the Jarl of Windhelm, [[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]], who challenged him in a duel for leadership. What many did not realize was that he used the power of his [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]] to obliterate the High King and kill him. With the help of his supporters, he escaped Solitude and prepared for war.{{Ref|name=SRStentor|[[Skyrim:Sybille Stentor|Sybille Stentor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=NA|{{cite Book|Nords Arise!}}}} 

The [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|Skyrim Civil War]] followed suit, between the Imperial power in west Skyrim that was spearheaded by the military-governor, [[Lore:Tullius|Tullius]] and the ruling-regent, [[Lore:Elisif the Fair|Elisif the Fair]], and the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]], the nationalist rebellion in the [[Lore:Old Holds|Old Holds]] maintained by Ulfric Stormcloak's court in Windhelm. General Tullius and Legate [[Lore:Rikke|Rikke]] oversaw the operations of the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] from Castle Dour while Jarl Elisf continued to govern Haafingar from Solitude.{{Ref|name=SRTullius|[[Skyrim:General Tullius#Joining the Legion|General Tullius]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

In that same year, a necromantic cult tried to resurrect Potema from the mountains{{Ref|name=SRWolf|[[Skyrim:The Man Who Cried Wolf|The Man Who Cried Wolf]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and her spirit later fled to the Solitude Catacombs, to take her corporeal form and pose a significant threat. She was later exorcised by a [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|warrior]] serving the court.{{Ref|name=SRAwakened|[[Skyrim:The Wolf Queen Awakened|The Wolf Queen Awakened]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} For the first time, the Burning of King Olaf was postponed for the year. The Jarl had seen it as poor taste following the malice at the palace, but it was something that the Headmaster at the Bards College, [[Lore:Viarmo|Viarmo]] could not take lightly. To convince Elisif to start the festival, he sent a student to retrieve a lost part of the [[Lore:Poetic Edda|Poetic Edda]] known as [[Lore:King Olaf's Verse|King Olaf's Verse]], in the hopes to convince the Jarl to commence the festival. Fortunately, their endeavors worked in their favor, and the Burning of King Olaf had went successfully.{{Ref|name=SRTending|[[Skyrim:Tending the Flames|Tending the Flames]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Whispers and Shadows from the Shivering Isles===
Later that year, Influences from the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] slipped through into Solitude. First, the former [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] High Priest of [[Lore:Mania|Mania]], [[Lore:Dervenin|Dervenin]], who at the time seemed like a simple beggar, convinced the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] to assist him in finding his master. This resulted in the Dragonborn unknowingly entering the mind of the dead [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]], and was tasked with convincing Sheogorath to return to the Shivering Isles. He agreed to return them, but only if the Dragonborn could escape Pelagius' mind. Sheogorath decided to assist the Dragonborn by providing the Wabbajack, along with instructions on how to use it to remedy Pelagius of his insanity. Having completed several challenges and conquering the dead emperor's inner fears, the Dragonborn was able to return to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], but not before being given the [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] by Sheogorath as a parting gift.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:The Mind of Madness|The Mind of Madness]] daedric quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

[[File:SR-quest-Restoring Order.jpg|thumb|right|The Shivering Isles, spreading into Solitude's sewers]]
Later, the Shivering Isle's influence would spread on a larger scale. A manic mage named [[Lore:Thoron|Thoron]] began conducting experiments within the city in an attempt to transport himself to Mania. He managed to summon [[Lore:Staada|Staada]], a powerful [[Lore:Aureal|Aureal]], who attacked him and forced him to bring his operations underground.{{Ref|name=TJ|{{Cite Book|Thoron's Journal}}}}

Thoron took refuge in the sewers beneath Solitude, which he found melded with a [[Lore:Root|Root Tunnel]], a sign of the Isles' influence. He continued his experiments here, and hired bandits to search for relics that might be associated with the Shivering Isles in his mission to find his way to Mania. The bandits he hired found the [[Lore:Sword of Jyggalag|Sword of Jyggalag]] in the possession of a [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] caravan, and brought it to Thoron. The sword revealed to him a method by which the barriers between Tamriel and the Shivering Isles could be broken. From deep within the root tunnels, he widened the gap between the two realms, allowing the Isles influence to spread throughout Skyrim in various ways. One example of this was [[Lore:Madness Ore|madness ore]] appearing all over the province in seemingly impossible locations.{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=SR|Note on Amber and Madness Ore}}}} The Sword of Jyggalag ultimately tried to manipulate Thoron into helping it break through into the Shivering Isles, seeking its destruction, {{Ref|name=TJ}} but this ritual was stopped circa 4E 201 by the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]], who killed Thoron and removed the Sword from the heart of the burgeoning Root system.{{ref|[[SR:Restoring Order|Restoring Order]] quest from the [[SR:Saints & Seducers|Saints & Seducers]] [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation]]}}

===Historical Notes===
*An unnamed King of Solitude once summoned the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] and received the [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] in his attempt to acquire the [[Lore:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]] from [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]. He was later called a madman.{{Ref|name=Wabbajack|{{Cite Book|Wabbajack (book)}}}} In the years that followed Potema Septim's reign, it was rumored that each monarch that lived in Castle Solitude went insane. Another example was Pelagius III, aptly titled 'the Mad'.{{Ref|name=BotWQ}}
*During the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]], advisors from Solitude traveled to [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] and offered their assistance to the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. They insisted that heavy armor be used in the Battle of Zelinin, however, this had inadvertently cost them the fight. They were promptly sent back to Solitude and the armor was thrown out.{{Ref|name=CHOA|{{Cite Book|Cherim's Heart of Anequina}}}}

==Notes==
*The [[Lore:Wolf|wolf]] in the city's coat of arms is a representation of the people's spirit.{{Ref|name=OTTF|{{Cite Book|Ode to the Founding}}}} The monarchs of Solitude have been known to adopt this moniker; for example, Potema Septim was called the Wolf-Queen, and Svargrim was known as the Wolf of Solitude.
*There is a lesser-known figure called the Ancient Sage of Solitude that may have been one of the oldest living figures in recorded times. He is compared to the old [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] that live on [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]].{{Ref|name=TPC|{{Cite Book|The Pig Children}}}}
*''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Solitude's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Ravagers".{{Ref|group=UOL|[http://www.imperial-library.info/content/go-blades Original TES: Arena texts at the Imperial Library]}} ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]'' brought back many of these arena teams to compete in an event known as the Dragon Games, but renamed Solitude's arena team into the "Solitude Wolves".{{Ref|[[Castles:Gladiatorial Glory|Gladiatorial Glory]] event in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}
