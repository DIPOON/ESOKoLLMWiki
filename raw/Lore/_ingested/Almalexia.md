[[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], also known as '''Almalexia the Lover''',{{ref|name=TCOV|{{Cite Book|The Cantatas of Vivec}}}} '''Almalexia the Warden''',{{ref|name=MGA|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue A|Almalexia]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and '''Ayem''',{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}} was one of the three God-Kings who constituted the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]], or Almsivi, where she is representing '''Alm''', along with [[Lore:Vivec (god)|Vivec]] and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]. She was very popular among the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], who called her "'''Healing Mother'''", "'''Lady of Mercy'''", "'''Mother Morrowind'''",{{ref|name=LG|{{Cite Book|The Living Gods}}}} "'''Sacred Lady'''",{{ref|name=ESOOrdinator|[[Online:Ordinator (Mournhold)|Ordinator]]'s dialogues in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and '''Mercymother'''.{{ref|name=APO|[[ON:Almalexia (Patron)|Almalexia]]'s patron description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They knew her as the source of compassion, sympathy, and forgiveness, the protector of the poor and weak, and the patron of teachers and healers.{{ref|name=LG|{{Cite Book|The Living Gods}}}} She resided in the temple city of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] within the city of [[Lore:Almalexia (city)|Almalexia]], the capital of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], and was closely associated with [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]]. 

She was also the most personable of the Tribunal and was well known for walking among her people. As a mortal, she was the wife of Lord [[Lore:Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]], [[Lore:First Council|First Council]]or of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]]. As a living god, she became the Consort of Lord Vivec,{{ref|name=MGA}} who associated her with the stars.{{ref|name=36L}} Her other titles include '''Face-Snaked Queen of the Three in One''',{{ref|name=36L}} '''Face-Snaked Fury''',{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}} '''First Face''',{{ref|name=RPTrialOfVehk|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]'s posts in [[General:Trial of Vivec|''The Trial of Vivec'']]}} and '''Most Blessed Lady Almalexia'''.{{Ref|name=MGA}}

Originally the counselors to Nerevar, the Tribunal came to power among the Dunmer following her husband's death at the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] circa {{Year|1E 700}}. Before his death, they swore oaths to Nerevar upon [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] that they would never use the [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Tools of Kagrenac]] on the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] in order to steal its divine essence for themselves. A few years later, Almalexia and her fellow Tribunes broke that oath to become living gods. This led to the transformation of the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] into the Dunmer, and the Tribunal became their immortal protectors, leaders, and gods.{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}} The truth of how they gained their divinity was covered up; according to the Heirographa, the orthodox public teachings of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]], their divinity was a more spontaneous result of their supernatural virtue, discipline, wisdom, and insight.{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}}{{ref|name=FOTT|{{Cite Book|Fellowship of the Temple}}}} <includeonly>For more information, see the [[Lore:Almalexia|lore article]].</includeonly><noinclude>

The power of Almalexia and the other Tribunes declined during their struggle with [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] throughout the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], and Almalexia eventually lost her mind along with her power. During the time of the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecies|Nerevarine Prophecies]] in {{Year|3E 427}}, she killed Sotha Sil and then died in a failed attempt to kill the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]].{{ref|name=Tribunal|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

==History==
===Origins===
[[File:MW-concept-Almalexia.jpg|Mother of Morrowind, Almalexia|thumb|left]]
Little is known of Almalexia before she became Nerevar's wife. Temple myth holds that on [[Lore:Mount Assarnibibi|Mount Assarnibibi]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] oversaw the ninety-nine lovers of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] who gave birth to her. {{ref|name=Saryoni|[[Morrowind:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} She hailed from one of the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses of Morrowind]].{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}} Early in her life Almalexia found herself ruling the capital city of Morrowind, [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] as its queen.{{Ref|name=LOV8|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 8}}}} Along with ruling Mournhold as the capital city's queen, Almalexia also ran "The Orphanage" and commanded an orphan legion.{{Ref|name=LOV30|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 30}}}}{{ref|name=WMBTM|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|What My Beloved Taught Me|ns_base=GEN}}}} Almalexia also commanded a faction known as [[Lore:The Shouts|The Shouts]] in these days who carried out her will.{{Ref|name=LOV8|}} 

===Formation of the First Council===
[[File:MW-misc-Anniversary Art 2025 Crop.png|Almalexia among the First Council|thumb|right]]
It is written that Ayem approached a netchiman's wife and informed her an image of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] resided within her before tossing her into the sea to the [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] who changed her sex so she could give birth to the image as an egg.{{ref|name=LOV1|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 1}}}} She was then approached by [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Seht]] who proclaimed that the netchiman's wife held an egg of his brother-sister which she was to nurture until the arrival of the Hortator before lifting her back to the surface to land.{{ref|name=LOV1}} 

Eventually the egg-image would be led to the capital city of Mournhold by a caravan captain, [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] of [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]]. Nerevar gifted the egg-image to Almalexia, with Ayem informing Nerevar that Sotha Sil prophesied his coming. The egg-image realized its true form as the Lord of the Middle Air, Vivec. Vivec paid respect to Almalexia as his queen and gave council that the alliance of himself, Ayem, Sotha Sil, and Nerevar make a strategic alliance with their enemies the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{Ref|name=LOV8|}}

The Chimer and Dwemer aligned and went to war with the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] occupiers. Nerevar and Vivec sought to personally defeating and repelling various Nordic chieftains such as [[Lore:Hoag Merkiller|Hoag Merkiller]], and [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]] in direct combat to great effect.{{ref|name=LOV9|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9}}}} With the Nordic chieftains defeated and their armies routed, the Nordic Empire was repelled from Morrowind at the hands of the Chimer-Dwemer alliance. This triumph led to the formation of the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]].{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} 

Almalexia and Nerevar thereafter were wed  [[Lore:Dumac|Dwarf-King Dumac]] presented them with the marvelously crafted twin blades [[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]] and [[Lore:Trueflame|Trueflame]] as a gift.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Nerevar and Dumac had become blood friends and Resdayn knew unprecedented peace.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia}} Nerevar led the Chimer of the First Council as the Hortator, with his Tribunal of councilors, Vivec and Sotha Sil, his generals, and Almalexia, his queen, abiding among him.{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=NARM}} Before Almalexia's marriage to Nerevar, she was said to possess a flickering image that accommodated her female needs known as a husband-state.{{Ref|name=LOV8|}}

[[File:TR-place-Mournhold Temple.jpg|Almalexia's holy city of Mournhold|thumb|left]]
[[File:MW-misc-Anniversary Art 2024.jpg|Almalexia's husband, Indoril Nerevar|thumb|right]]
Almalexia's actions during the days of the First Council are scarcely documented. Vivec's Sermons describe a few things such as her giving council to Vivec before he set out to destroy his eight mightiest [[Lore:Monster Children|Monster Progeny]],{{ref|name=LOV18|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 18}}}} and lending her orphan legion to her husband Nerevar to fight off an army of the mysterious [[Lore:Grabbers|Grabbers]].{{Ref|name=LOV30|}} 

===War of the First Council===
''The [[Lore:War of the First Council|fall of the First Council]] that concluded with the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] is mired by different perspectives with their own accounts of the events as they took place. These accounts have various tellings on Vivec's role in the battle.''

====Alandro Sul's Account====
[[File:GEN-misc-Foul Murder.png|A depiction of the Tribunal murdering Nerevar|thumb|right]]
[[Lore:Alandro Sul|Alandro Sul]], a shield-companion of Nerevar came to live among the [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlanders]] following the battle, he relayed his version of events. [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Voryn Dagoth]] approached Nerevar and the Tribunal with proof that the High Engineer of the Dwemer, Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]] had discovered the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] and was using [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|special tools]] to harness power from the Heart and create a New God, the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]]. Ayem and the rest of the Tribunal urged war against the Dwemer for this sacrilege which eventually came to pass.{{ref|name=NARM|}}

As the Tribunal battled the Dwemer out in the fields, Nerevar and Voryn Dagoth infiltrated the Heart Chamber where they were able to slay King Dumac and Kagrenac, but Dumac had dealt Nerevar grievous wounds. Nerevar summoned Azura who showed Nerevar and Voryn Dagoth how to use the Tools of Kagrenac to separate the power of the Heart from the Dwemer and they turned into dust and the race was no more. Voryn recommended the tools be destroyed for the danger they represented but Nerevar wished to consult with the Tribunal once more. The Tribunal urged Nerevar to use the tools to claim Resdayn, and perhaps the whole world for the Chimer, but Nerevar did not expect this counsel and thus wished to summon Azura again.{{ref|name=NARM}}

The Tribunal had become greedy and coveted the power of the Heart of Lorkhan. They feigned the ritual to summon Azura, Almalexia used poison candles, Sotha Sil used poisoned robes, and Vivec used poisoned invocations to murder Nerevar.{{ref|name=NARM}} Other imaginings of this murder of Nerevar involve the Tribunal killing Nerevar by Vivec plunging Muatra through him, Sotha Sil severing his face, and Almalexia severing his feet.{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Foul Murder|ns_base=GEN}}}}

====Songs of King Wulfharth====
The [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] have their own account of the Battle of Red Mountain. The Nords were approached by Voryn Dagoth (who they referred to as the Devil of Dagoth), who promised of peace and claimed that he knew where the Heart of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] was. The Nords were ecstatic at the prospect to reunite their god, with his heart and so their [[Lore:Thu'um|Tongues]] sung the ghost of Shor back into the world. Shor gathered an army and sucked in the long-strewn about ashes of [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]] to serve as his general. The Devil of Dagoth petitioned that he too should be named general for his role in bringing about these events and so Shor had two generals and the collective unit marched onto Resdayn.{{Ref|name=FSKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}

The Devil of Dagoth, had evidently tricked the Nords and Shor for when they arrived to Red Mountain, the Heart was not there and all the Devils and Dwarves fell upon them, with their sorcerers lifting up the mountain and throwing it on top of Shor, trapping him under Red Mountain until the end of time. The Dwemer and Chimer then began to slaughter the Nords but not before Wulfharth killed Dumac and doomed his people, then Wulfharth's old enemy Vehk the Devil blasted the Ash King Wulfharth into Hell and it was over.{{Ref|name=FSKW|}}

A secret retelling of the Songs of King Wulfharth instead claim that Voryn Dagoth had not tricked the Nords and that the Heart of Lorkhan (for that is what Shor was called in Resdayn) was indeed in Resdayn. The Devil of Dagoth admitted that the Tribunal had betrayed Nerevar's trust and sent Voryn Dagoth to the Nords in hope that their god Shor would seek vengeance upon the Dwemer for their hubris, and that the alliance between the Chimer and Dwemer forged by Nerevar would bring ruin to the Velothi way. Lorkhan claimed he did not seek vengeance on the Dwemer for the reasons the Tribunal believed he did, but nevertheless they would die by his hand.{{Ref|name=FSKW|}}

This betrayal by the Tribunal of Nerevar led to the destruction of the Dwemer race, the defeat of Lorkhan at the hands of Nerevar, but ultimately the death of Nerevar as well by the mortal wounds dealt to him by Lorkhan.{{Ref|name=FSKW|}}

====Vivec's Account====
[[File:MW-creature-Heart of Lorkhan.jpg|The Heart of Lorkhan (Morrowind)|thumb|left]]
Vivec himself has his own perspective of the battle that he refers to as the true account.{{ref|name=MWVivec|[[MW:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} In large part, Vivec's telling is very similar to that of Alandro Sul and the Ashlanders with the big divergence coming from the point where Nerevar approaches them for counsel on what to do with Kagrenac's Tools. The Tribunal advised that the Dwemer may not be gone forever, but merely displaced to some outer realm, and that they should preserve the tools and study them so the Chimer can be safe for future generations. Nerevar voiced grave misgivings about this but agreed under the condition that the Tribunal would all swear oaths upon Azura that they would never use the tools in the same profane way the Dwemer intended, so as to make themselves beyond the measures of the gods. The Tribunal all agreed and swore oaths at Nerevar's dictation.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}

When the Tribunal and Nerevar went to retrieve the tools, Voryn Dagoth who was safeguarding them refused to relinquish them, insisting that only he could be trusted with them. The Tribunal and Nerevar took them by force and they were given to Sotha Sil for study and safe-keeping.{{ref|name=TBORM|}} Vivec bluntly states that the Tribunal did not murder Nerevar as the Ashlanders claim, and dismisses the tellings of Alandro Sul.{{ref|name=MWVivec|}}

====Vivec's Sermons====
[[File:ON-concept-Almalexia Banner.jpg|Banner depicting the Lady of Mercy|thumb|right]]
Vivec has alternative accounting of the battle in his [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|Sermons]]. In this variation Vivec claims that the Dwemer and Chimer lived under the wise and benevolent rule of Almsivi and their champion the Hortator but the Dwemer became foolish and challenged their masters. The Nords and Ysmir Wulfharth came to the aid of the Dwemer, who were led by King Dumac and High Priest Kagrenac. Nerevar led the Chimer armies and, wielding the Ethos Knife, slew Dumac at Red Mountain and saw the heart bone for the first time. Meanwhile, the [[Lore:Mournhold|Mourning Hold]] was attacked by [[Lore:Dwarven Centurion|Dwarven Centurion]]s and Dwemeri [[Lore:Tonal Architecture|tonal architects]]. Almalexia threw down her cloak and donned her serpentine [[Lore:Mask of Almalexia|War Face]], becoming the Snake-Faced Queen. She wreaked havoc on the armies of the Dwemer and those who looked upon her in this terrible state were overcome with the meaning of the stars. Sotha Sil further assisted by summoning Clockwork [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] who were mockeries of the Dwemer machines, and dragged their counterparts underneath the sea.{{ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Nerevar ventured deeper into Red Mountain in search of the Sharmat, Dagoth Ur, but "went too far inside" and the mountain erupted. It was then that Dwemeri high priest Kagrenac "the Blighter" unveiled the ultimate Dwemer weapon: a "[[Lore:Numidium|walking star]]", that blasted the land with such force that the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] was created. It took the combined might of the Almsivi but they eventually destroyed the construct; as it died the Dwemer vanished.{{ref|name=36L36|}}

===Rise to Her Rightful Station===
After the Battle of Red Mountain, despite the narratives of the various accounts, it was universally agreed upon that the Dwemer had disappeared, the westerners that took place in the battle had been banished, Voryn Dagoth appeared to be dead, and the Hortator Nerevar was dead. The other, and most important conclusions from the battle was the curse of Azura, and the ascension of the Tribunal to living gods. 

According to Alandro Sul, the curse of Azura occurred after the Tribunal betrayed Nerevar by murdering him. Vivec instead claims the curse occurred a few years later when they used Kagrenac's Tools to tap into the Heart of Lorkhan and become gods, which betrayed the oath Nerevar made them swear. Either way, the curse was attributed to the sins of the Tribunal by Azura and she appeared before them and changed their golden skin into ashen gray, their eyes into a volcanic red; the Chimer had become the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]. Azura also vowed that she would use her powers over dawn and dusk to make sure Nerevar would [[Lore:Nerevarine|come back]] and punish them for their perfidy.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=NARM|}}

As for the ascension of the Tribunal, after Kagrenac's Tools were given to Sotha Sil for study and safekeeping, Sotha Sil was able to divine their mysteries and learned how to use them to tap into the Heart of Lorkhan. Sotha Sil approached Vivec and Almalexia with a vision of a better world of justice and prosperity, heralded by the Tribunal as the immortal guides and patrons. The Tribunal then made pilgrimage to the Heart Chamber at Red Mountain and used the tools to transform themselves into gods; they were now the [[Lore:Almsivi|Almsivi]].{{ref|name=TBORM|}}

Other accounts exist on both the ascension of the Tribunal and the changing of the Chimer into Dunmer. Many Dunmer would go on to believe that the Tribunal were indeed mortal guardians, but instead of any knowledge of the Heart of Lorkhan they believe they achieved divine substance through superhuman discipline and virtue and supernatural wisdom and insight.{{ref|name=LG|{{Cite Book|The Living Gods}}}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}}{{ref|name=FOTT|{{Cite Book|Fellowship of the Temple}}}} Furthermore Vivec in his Sermons speaks of himself and his fellow Tribunes as divine, and godly in nature many years before the events of Red Mountain had occurred. Some degree of truth very likely lies in these accounts that speak of divinity of the Tribunal before Red Mountain as they are written to have achieved miraculous deeds even before this event,{{Ref|name=OrdinN|[[ON:Ordinator Nelyn|Ordinator Nelyn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and certain sources describe them in near-divine ways before the Battle of Red Mountain such as claiming they had not yet attained "true godhood"{{ref|name=ABHoAS|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of Ald Sotha}}}} and attesting to their "near-godlike power".{{Ref|name=TaT|Description of the Sword of the Saint during [[ON:Trials and Tribulations#Makela Leki's Blessing|Trials and Tribulations]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} That said, the members of the Tribunal are open to the fact that ultimately what made them true gods was the power harvested from the Heart of Lorkhan.{{ref|name=TBORM|}}{{ref|name=MWVivec|}}{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|}} 

[[File:LG-cardart-Almalexia's Blessing.png|Almalexia displaying her divine power|thumb|left]]
Vivec also had an alternative account for what caused the Chimer to become Dunmer in his sermons, that [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] asked the Chimer to become the color of bruise, and Vivec ultimately caused this to come about with his lamentations and brooding making the Chimer's skin pregnant darkness.{{ref|name=LOV10|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 10}}}}{{ref|name=LOV31|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 31}}}}{{ref|name=LOV32|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 32}}}}

Vivec, Almalexia, and Sotha Sil now gods of a newly changed race, approached their people who were afraid of their new appearances, but Sotha Sil gave an impassioned speech to his people stating that it was not a curse, but a blessing, a sign of their changed natures as a people that no longer had to toil under arbitrary and aloof gods, but deal directly and build a new life with their immortal friends and patrons, this speech inspired the Dunmer people and harkened them to their new gods.{{ref|name=TBORM|}} Sotha Sil in staying true to his speech and sympathy for his people retained his Dunmeri appearance.{{ref|name=DivaythFyrCWC|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} Almalexia fully reverted back to her golden Chimeri appearance, making her the last known individual to have this look.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexiaAp|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Vivec chose to take a half Chimer, half Dunmer appearance to embrace his dualistic nature and so he could separate the bronze of the Old Temple from the blue of the New Temple.{{ref|name=LOV31|}} Despite his bold words in speech, Sotha Sil was initially worried that Almsivi had doomed their people by cursing them, Almalexia on the other hand viewed them not as cursed, but blessed with divine protection.{{ref|name=MPStars|Dialogue of various [[Online:Star|Memories]] in the [[ON:Mnemonic Planisphere|Mnemonic Planisphere]]}}

===The Three in One===
[[File:LG-cardart-Power of the Almsivi 02.png|Almsivi, the God-Kings of Morrowind (Legends)|thumb|right]]
Almsivi as the new masters of the province began to restructure the foundation of the society as they saw fit. The name Resdayn was no more, the Dunmer province was now known as [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. Where once there was seven [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] of the First Council, [[Lore:House Indoril|Indoril]], [[Lore:House Dres|Dres]], [[Lore:House Redoran|Redoran]], [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]], [[Lore:House Hlaalu|Hlaalu]], [[Lore:House Dagoth|Dagoth]], and [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], the legacies of House Dwemer and House Dagoth were destroyed by Almsivi.{{ref|name=PS|{{Cite Book|Poison Song}}}} The Grand Council now made up the five remaining Great Houses, but the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] which was the religious faction dedicated to Almsivi, had the most power and influence.{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3M|}}

Almalexia, Vivec, and Sotha Sil each had an eponymous city dedicated to their worship that shared their host Tribunes name.{{ref|name=PGE1M|}} Each city also had an alternate name to distinguish the city from the Living God who resided within, [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]], and [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]]. Each Living God also had an honor guard that was associated most strongly with that particular Tribune, For Almalexia, this was her [[Lore:Hands of Almalexia|Hands of Almalexia]],{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|}} for Sotha Sil, the [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]]{{ref|name=ItCA|{{Cite Book|Inveigling the Clockwork Apostles}}}} and for Vivec, his [[Lore: Buoyant Armigers|Buoyant Armigers]].{{ref|name=BASV|{{Cite Book|Buoyant Armigers: Swords of Vivec}}}} Each Tribunal also sympathized with one particular Great House above the rest. Almalexia sympathized with the comfortable and secure serenity of Indoril, Sotha Sil matched the iconoclastic, profane, and unconventional disposition of House Telvanni, and Vivec, much like the Dunmer of House Redoran, was driven by creeds and deeds.{{ref|name=MWVivec|}} The three were even associated with the times of day, Sotha Sil with the morning sun, Almalexia with her mid-day mercy, and Vivec's star-spun night.{{ref|name=CotT|[[Online:Chronometer of the Tribunal|Chronometer of the Tribunal]] from [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Vivec outlines several metaphysical roles for each individual member of Almsivi in his Sermons. Ayem is said to be the Mother who is active and clawed like a nix-hound, yet the holiest of those that reclaim their days.{{ref|name=LOV6|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 6}}}} She is also said to be the "plot" of the fate of Morrowind,{{ref|name=LOV13|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 13}}}} and the splendor of the stars is her domain,{{ref|name=LOV11|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 11}}}} with she herself being the star of Almsivi.{{ref|name=LOV13|}} Vivec was most commonly viewed as the most popular and public of the Tribunal,{{ref|name=VAM|{{Cite Book|Vivec and Mephala}}}}{{ref|name=LG|}} especially on Vvardenfell.{{ref|name=ULG|{{Cite Book|Understanding the Living Gods}}}} However, some accounts claim Almalexia the most popular of the three{{Ref|name=VFDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and at one point in time Sotha Sil was as public facing as any of them.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|}}

[[File:ON-furnishing-Chronometer of the Tribunal 13.jpg|Almalexia in the midday sun|thumb|left]]
One of the first tests the Living Gods of Morrowind would face would be the attempt at vengeance by Ash-King Wulfharth. After the Battle of Red Mountain, Wulfharth's ashes blew back to Skyrim where he embraced and was embraced by the Nords. But Wulfharth after his multiple incarnations no longer had a national identity, his only desire for the Nords was for them to help him destroy the Tribunal. Wulfharth gathered an army and summoned a mighty storm and marched on Morrowind. The Dunmer however, with their newly formed unity and might under Almsivi, was too powerful and drove Wulfharth and his armies away.{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}

Almsivi also helped the Dunmer navigate the crisis of the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]]. The Dunmer believe eight stars fell to the world, one for each [[Lore:Divines|iniquity]] that [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] made clear to the world. [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] read these signs and told the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Anticipations]] who confirmed them and  made wards against them, and then sent Almsivi to steer the Dunmer clear of harm. Even the [[Lore:House of Troubles|Four Corners]] rose up to assist the Dunmer people.{{ref|name=WWY|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}} [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] identified Almsivi along with a few other key figures as individuals who could identify where they were when the Dragon Break occurred.{{ref|name=WWY|}}

Years later, in {{Year|1E 2703}}{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Morrowind}}}} The [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] of [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]] descended on Tamriel passing through Morrowind on their way to Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=OBNarina|[[Oblivion:Countess Narina Carvain|Countess Narina Carvain]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Realizing that they had a common enemy the Tribunal and Reman reached an agreement to join forces to defeat the invaders. Morrowind and the Army of Reman were successful in defeating the Tsaesci, and thereafter Reman integrated many of the Tsaesci veterans into his own armies, and his [[Lore:Dragonguard|personal service]].{{ref|name=PGE3M|}}{{ref|name=OBNarina|}}

===The Lady of Mercy===
[[File:ON-misc-Chronometer of the Tribunal.png|thumb|left|The Tribunal represented as times of day]]
Almalexia wielded her divine power for the protection and benefit of the Dunmer for thousands of years. Sotha Sil largely remained aloof and mysterious, but Almalexia and Vivec both took very active roles in leading their people throughout the [[Lore:First Era|First]] and [[Lore:Second Era|Second]] eras. Her acts of amazing power and kindness are countless.{{ref|name=TRMehra}} Her blessing was said to do extraordinary things, like protect a person infected with [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]] from suffering the disease's effects.{{ref|name=DCMorrowind|[[MW:Disease Carrier|Disease Carrier]]'s quest journal entries in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} [[Lore:Felms|Saint Felms the Bold]] is said to have been inspired by the voice of Almalexia in his head.{{ref|name=MWdialogue|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue S|Saint Felms]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=LOTS|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Saints}}}} The [[Lore:Hands of Almalexia|Hands of Almalexia]] were her personal guard, and were often called the greatest warriors in all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=Tribunal}} Like Vivec, she wrote extensively, though she focused on children's fables and similar works meant to nurture the morality of her people rather than Vivec's more cerebral, spiritual guidance.{{ref|name=TCOV}}{{ref|name=36L}}{{ref|name=HOBA|{{Cite Book|Homilies of Blessed Almalexia}}}}{{ref|name=TBODAD|{{Cite Book|The Book of Dawn and Dusk}}}}
[[File:MW-place-Plaza Brindisi Dorom 02.jpg|right|thumb|Statue of Almalexia battling Mehrunes Dagon]]
In {{Year|1E 2920}}, [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] destroyed the city of Mournhold. After an epic battle, he was defeated by Almalexia and Sotha Sil. This was portrayed in the historical fiction ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]'', which also suggested Almalexia foresaw in her dreams the end of the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]], as well as the end of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]].{{ref|name=2920:TLY|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}

In {{Year|2E 572}}, Almalexia fought alongside [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]], also known as the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], against [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] during an [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]i invasion of northern Tamriel.{{ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}}{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}} Sources seem to disagree on certain aspects of this conflict. ''[[Lore:Mysterious Akavir|Mysterious Akavir]]'' treats Morrowind as the target of the Akaviri invasion, though [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] was apparently the Akaviri's initial target. ''[[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|The Arcturian Heresy]]'' claims Almalexia was the one who summoned the Underking to fight alongside the Tribunal (even though in life, he had been one of their greatest enemies). ''[[Lore:Jorunn the Skald-King (book)|Jorunn the Skald-King]]'' contradicts this, crediting the [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]] with summoning the Ash-King. The book also claims that Dir-Kamal's army was crushed by several Tamrielic military forces, not just the Dunmer army under Almalexia's command, and that this occurred at a place called [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]]. The other sources do not mention the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic or [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] forces which played roles, and ''Mysterious Akavir'' says Dir-Kamal was defeated at Red Mountain.
[[File:ON-npc-Almalexia.jpg|thumb|right|Almalexia (ESO)]]
Following the Akaviri invasion, Almalexia was a strong supporter of the formation of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]]. It was she who convinced the Great Houses (sans [[Lore:Telvanni|Telvanni]]) to join the Pact. During the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]] of {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] was threatened by a cult known as the {{Lore Link|Maulborn}}, who sought to topple the Tribunal and return the Dunmer to Daedra worship. They were led by [[Lore:Urili Vox|Magistrix Urili Vox]], once a handmaiden of Almalexia who turned against her following her son's execution. The insurgents were ultimately put down.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Return of Dagoth Ur===
[[File:TR-creature-Almalexia 04.jpg|left|thumb|Lady Almalexia (Morrowind)]]
In {{Year|2E 882}}, Dagoth Ur awoke beneath Red Mountain. The former Lord High Councilor of [[Lore:House Dagoth|House Dagoth]] had been thought dead, but through unknown means, he had actually forged his own connection to the Heart of Lorkhan, drawing divine power from it just like the Tribunal did, and was merely sleeping. When the Tribunal arrived at the mountain for their annual ritual bath to replenish their power, Dagoth Ur and his minions attacked them and drove them away, decisively cutting them off from access to the Heart.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} The Tribunal would spend almost four hundred and fifty years, virtually all of the Third Era, slowly losing ground to him. Their preoccupation with what had become the Devil Incarnate for the Dunmer people is perhaps one reason why Vivec avoided war with [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] and signed the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]], making Morrowind an autonomous province of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]].{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}}

With the advent of Imperial rule in Morrowind, Mournhold became a town of two minds: there was the monarchy led by the Imperial-appointed king, and the Temple led by Almalexia. While there has been no open hostility between the two, there were always undercurrents of tension.{{ref|name=TRBarenziah|[[TR:Barenziah|Barenziah]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} The power mainly stayed with the Temple, and the monarchy seemed to acquiesce to this reality (the king was, after all, the vassal ruler of the Empire, and Morrowind had retained its autonomy under the terms of the Armistice).{{ref|name=Tribunal}}{{ref|name=OM}} However, many feared the violence that would ensue should the king ever attempt to challenge Almalexia's authority.{{ref|name=Tribunal}}

The Tribunal made seasonal campaigns to Red Mountain, and slew Dagoth Ur and his kin, the [[Lore:Ash Vampire|ash vampires]], though the Heart always revived them in time. Almalexia had always had a tendency to brood, and without access to the Heart of Lorkhan, her divinity ebbed.{{ref|name=MWVivec|[[MW:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} This weighed heavily on her,{{ref|name=MWVivec}} and she became a harsher, more unsympathetic patron.{{ref|name=Tribunal}} She stopped walking among her people around {{Year|3E 377}}.{{ref|name=TRGalsa|[[TR:Galsa Andrano|Galsa Andrano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

When the Tribunal realized they could not defeat Dagoth Ur, they built the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Great Ghostfence]] to contain him around {{Year|3E 417}}.{{ref|name=MWVivec}}{{ref|name=TRMehra|[[TR:Mehra Helas|Mehra Helas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} This seemed to work, until Almalexia and Sotha Sil were attacked during a campaign and lost two of the Tools of Kagrenac, [[Lore:Sunder|Sunder]] and [[Lore:Keening|Keening]].{{ref|name=MWVivec}} Though Vivec rescued his fellow Tribunes, the Tools were lost, and both the real impact of their loss and what it signified proved crippling.{{ref|name=Tribunal}} Almalexia and Vivec stopped communicating. Vivec grew concerned that she would pose a danger to herself and others, and might even try to harm him.{{ref|name=MWVivec}} Almalexia ceased appearing in public altogether,{{ref|name=TRMehra}} and the incident was the last known time Sotha Sil was seen alive. Vivec was left to spearhead the fight against Dagoth Ur alone.{{ref|name=DUP}}{{ref|name=PTDDU|{{Cite Book|Plan to Defeat Dagoth Ur}}}} The Tribunal was undone.

===The Death of Almalexia===
[[File:TR-creature-Almalexia 02.jpg|left|thumb|Almalexia in her War Headdress (Morrowind)]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''She believes her tales implicitly. As does everyone else. Her capacity for deception appears limitless. She sows lies like a master gardener sows seeds, and the harvest of trust and adulation is breathtaking in scope. ... Almalexia does what she does because she cannot do otherwise. It will not end well. But then, even the best endings rarely bring joy.''|[[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]}}
Almalexia had changed. Those few who still saw her noticed that she had begun to act more like a warrior queen than the Lady of Mercy.{{ref|name=TREffe|[[TR:Effe-Tei|Effe-Tei]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Those closest to her knew that she had turned into a wholly different person, one who was obsessed with maintaining her image at the expense of everything else.{{ref|name=TRSalas|[[TR:Salas Valor|Salas Valor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} In lieu of divine power, Almalexia exploited her knowledge of ancient and powerful relics to inflict terrible punishments on her people for what she perceived as their lack of faith, such as by using Dwemeri machinery to cover her capital city in ash storms. But in her madness, she concocted a new goal: to become the one true god of the Dunmer, uniting all of her people under one faith and authority - and destroying any who interfered.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia}} When the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] came to Mournhold after Dagoth Ur's fall in {{Year|3E 427}}, Almalexia sought to trick, entrap, and destroy him as part of this plot. She first turned on [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and slew him in his [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]], then unleashed its [[Lore:Fabricants|mechanical inhabitants]] into her own city in order to frame her old friend. Her ruse lured the Nerevarine to Sotha Sil's legendary home, where she hoped that the reincarnation of her husband would be killed by the inhabitants. When the Nerevarine persevered, Almalexia tried to finish the job herself. But her powers failed her, and she died at the Nerevarine's hand in the Clockwork City.{{ref|name=Tribunal}} Though Almalexia would call Vivec a fool in her final hours,{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia}} Vivec, the last remaining Tribune,{{ref|name=TRAzura|[[Morrowind:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} only expressed pity for his fallen, deranged lover.{{ref|name=MWVivec}} He later retrieved her body, and interred her and Sotha Sil in the proper Velothi fashion.{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}}

According to Azura, the death of Almalexia was a boon for all of Morrowind, even if the people did not understand it at the time. The Daedric Prince professed that the Lady of Mercy would have betrayed the Dunmer as surely as she had betrayed all those she loved, for this was her true curse.{{ref|name=TRAzura}} By {{Year|3E 432}}, the Empire reported that Almalexia, Dagoth Ur, Sotha Sil (and possibly Vivec) were destroyed in the "Nerevarine's fury" and that the Nerevarine likewise had vanished.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}

===Continued Worship===
Despite her death, many Dunmer—including most of the Redoran and the Temple faithful—continued to believe for many years that Almalexia still lived, and considered it heretical to suggest otherwise.{{Ref|name=MGA}}{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 12: Playing Houses|''Playing Houses'']]}} Support for the Temple's worship of Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Vivec gradually weakened over the years, and it was not until after the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] in {{Year|4E 5}} that all three of the Tribunes were now widely believed dead or disappeared, causing a long-brewing schism within the ranks of the Temple priesthood to erupt. The former [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]] were in full control of the Temple hierarchy in the aftermath, with Tribunal loyalists either purged or recanted, and the newly dubbed "[[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]]" now declared the worship of the Tribunes a result of misguided teaching. The Dunmer returned to the veneration of Boethiah, now called one of the "[[Lore:Reclamations|Reclamations]]", and managed to make their way without their Healing Mother watching over them. Almalexia is still remembered and honored as one of the [[Lore:Temple Saints|greater saints]] of the Dunmeri faith,{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} but not one who was ever supposed to be one of the cornerstones of the religion.{{ref|name=DBGaldrus|[[Skyrim:Galdrus Hlervu|Galdrus Hlervu]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} By {{Year|4E 201}}, the last vestiges of Almalexia's marks were gone from Mournhold.{{ref|name=TMFN|{{Cite Book|To Milore from Nilara}}}}

Despite the majority of the Dunmer people turning away from Almalexia worship, the Goddess was still revered by certain sects. These devotees were regarded as heretics by the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] and were persecuted for their beliefs.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} One cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]]. The cultists raised a giant [[Lore:Statue|statue]] to the Goddess and planned to use old propaganda to restore widespread Tribunal worship.{{ref|name=GotT|[[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal (quest)|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation Club]]''}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Her Word Against Theirs|Her Word Against Theirs]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation Club]]''}} These devotees counted several [[Lore:Hands of Almalexia|Hands of Almalexia]] among their ranks, a tradition which survived the Goddess's death.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Hand Indobar's Journal}}}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was alerted to the cult's existence, although it is unknown if the Dragonborn aided the cult or destroyed it.{{ref|name=GotT}}

==Personal Life==
===Personality===
Almalexia showed a great amount of compassion and sympathy for her people, and was known as a protector of the poor and weak.{{ref|name=MGA}} She also had exceptional political skills.{{ref|name=2920:TLY}} Upon seeing Mournhold burned and squandered, she wept, and turned her heart to "molten brass", becoming furious towards Dagon. Sotha Sil sealed the memory away in his great mnemonic planisphere as a reminder of her love's high price.{{Ref|name=TSiS}}

===Combat Prowess===
Almalexia was a skilled and powerful combatant. It is said she was even capable of defeating Vivec in single combat if such a duel were to have occurred.{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}} When entering battle, she yells "ERAM VAR AE ALTADOON!", rending her garments, and donning her killing masks, and draws her sword Hopesfire. During the battle with Dagon amid a firestorm, she skipped over the flames like a river-stone and with a mighty scream, she plunged the blade deep into Dagon's breast and turned it like a jailer's key. Scorching blood spewed out of the wound, scalding her hands and face.{{Ref|name=TSiS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} The Daedra's claws dug into her belly, spreading poison through her veins while Almalexia throttled him, sending him back to Oblivion for a long time. Afterwards, Almalexia lay on a bed of silk, tended to by Vivec's own healers. Her face, even her lips, was gray as stone, and blood stained through the gauze of her bandages.{{ref|name=2920:TLY}}
===Sight===
Almalexia was known for her various [[Lore:Sight|prophecies]] such as those related to the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]].{{ref|name=2920:TLY}} Despite prophetic tendencies from both Vivec and Sotha Sil, Ayem claimed that they were not seers and that only she was privy of what would come to pass.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia}} She also had a vision of the arrival of [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]], a soulless, deathless one who could save [[Lore:Gil-Var-Delle|Gil-Var-Delle]].{{Ref|name=HOD|[[ON:High Ordinator Danys|High Ordinator Danys]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
