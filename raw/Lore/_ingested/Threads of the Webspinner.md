# Lore:Threads of the Webspinner

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}[[File:MW-quest-Threads of the Webspinner.jpg|thumb|right|The Threads of the Webspinner]]__NOTOC__</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Threads of the Webspinner|Threads of the Webspinner]] are 27 pieces of extravagant enchanted clothing and jewelry associated with both [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] and [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]]. The story goes that Mephala sought to reward her devoted followers within the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], and turned to Sanguine to create them.{{ref|name=EnoMW|[[MW:Eno Hlaalu|Eno Hlaalu]]'s dialogue regarding the [[MW:Belt of Sanguine Fleetness|Belt of Sanguine Fleetness]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Each Thread enhances a single skill, such as weapon skills, speechcraft, and magic.<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}
==History==
At an unknown time, the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] stole the Threads of the Webspinner from the Morag Tong, but Mephala's subtle manipulations would draw them back into [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=EnoMW}} The Brotherhood gained a foothold in both Morrowind's mainland, and in the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], with those stationed on the island having the artifacts. On Vvardenfell, they negotiated with the cult of Mehrunes Dagon so they could be sheltered by them in their associated [[Lore:Daedric Ruins|Daedric ruins]] throughout Vvardenfell. {{Lore Link|Eno Hlaalu|Eno Hlaalu}}, Grandmaster of the Morag Tong, took notice and began ordering an assassin to wipe out the strongholds of the Brotherhood, and recovering the threads. Eno heard about a powerful Dagonite, an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] by the name of {{Lore Link|Carecalmo}}, whose influence with the cult of Mehrunes Dagon was great, and ordered the agent to offer an ultimatum to the him to stop providing refuge for the Brotherhood, but the cultist attacked the agent of Mephala for daring to command him. One of the Threads was recovered from Carecalmo's corpse and given to Eno Hlaalu for safekeeping.{{ref|name=MWUC|[[MW:Eno Hlaalu|Eno Hlaalu]]'s dialogue during [[Morrowind:Ultimatum for Carecalmo|Ultimatum for Carecalmo]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The assassin would continue recovering the Threads for the Tong and wiping out the Brotherhood and the Dagonites from Vvardenfell. The culmination of this task was the writ of execution being issued for [[Lore:Severa Magia|Severa Magia]], the Night Mother of the Dark Brotherhood. The assassin was sent to the ruins of [[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]], which served as the headquarters of the Brotherhood in Vvardenfell. There, the Tong infiltrated the ruins and executed the Severa Magia, which helped to put an end to the Dark Brotherhood's activities on Vvardenfell. From the aftermath of the battle, the Tong liberated six Threads of the Webspinner.{{ref|name=MWESM|Events of [[Morrowind:Execute Severa Magia|Execute Severa Magia]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Eventually all the Threads were discovered. After eradicating the Dark Brotherhood and the Dagonites from Vvardenfell, the Threads were given to Eno Hlaalu for safekeeping, and the assassin was blessed with Mephala's Skill as a reward.{{ref|name=MWTW|Events of [[Morrowind:Threads of the Webspinner|Threads of the Webspinner]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} In their desire to advance to the rank of Grandmaster, it's unknown if the assassin allowed Eno Hlaalu to live and retire to [[Lore:Vounoura|Vounoura]], and take the Threads with him, or slew him to advance per tradition, and retrieved the Threads for themselves.{{ref|name=MWG|Events of [[Morrowind: Grandmaster|Grandmaster]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
