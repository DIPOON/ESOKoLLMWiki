[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]] is the last known living [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]. Bloated and sick with [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]] disease, he was under the care of the ancient [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] wizard [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]], in [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWDF|[[Morrowind:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Early History===
In Yagrum's prime, he was considered a wise scholar.{{ref|name=SSLW|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Sotha Sil's Last Words|ns_base=General}}}} Before the disappearance of the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwarves]], Bagarn was a Master Crafter in the service of the chief Tonal Architect, Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Yagrum survived the disappearance because he was in another dimension at the time, an undescribed "Outer Realm". Yagrum Bagarn would later return to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and has been looking for his people ever since with no success.{{Ref|name=MWYB}}

Yagrum is mentioned by [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] in the book ''[[Lore:Where Were You ... Dragon Broke|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke]]'', as one of the people that experienced the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]], a phenomenon where linear time broke and became non-linear. As to the question where Yagrum himself was when this event happened, Mannimarco stated "The Last Dwarf would talk, if they would let him".{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}

At one point, Yagrum befriended Divayth Fyr.{{Ref|name=MWYB}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book,{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing artifacts such as [[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]], the [[Lore:Bow of Shadows|Bow of Shadows]], and the [[Lore:Staff of Hasedoki|Staff of Hasedoki]] among various items of "unimaginable significance".{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}} At this point of time, it went under the name ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Artifacts|Tamrielic Artifacts]]''.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}}

===Third Era===
As Yagrum learned of more artifacts, he catalogued them in his research and made revisions in his book. Three more artifacts, the [[Lore:Bloodworm Helm|Bloodworm Helm]], the [[Lore:Daedric Crescent|Daedric Crescent]], and the [[Lore:Dragonbone Cuirass|Dragonbone Cuirass]], were added to his catalogue of artifacts. The book itself went through various name changes, such as ''[[Lore:Famed Artifacts of Tamriel|Famed Artifacts in Tamriel]]'',{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Famed Artifacts of Tamriel}}}} and what finally stuck was the name ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]''.{{Ref|name=TL}}{{Ref|name=TLR|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore: Revised}}}}

Yagrum Bargarn would at one point in time contract Corprus, causing him to fall into a state of insanity. Divayth Fyr took him in and cured much of his dementia, as well as trying out various harmless spells and potions to cure him, though none of them worked.{{Ref|name=MWYB}} He also assisted Fyr in effectively curing the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] of their Corprus during [[Lore:The Blight|The Blight]] crisis in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|[[Morrowind: Corprus Cure|Corprus Cure]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Divayth noted that while Yagrum still showed signs of corprus, his memory seems to slowly be coming back from time to time.{{ref|name=SSLW|group=UOL}} By {{Year|4E 201}}, reports of Bagarn's survival were known by mages across the continent.{{Ref|name=Marcurio|[[Skyrim:Marcurio|Marcurio]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
