</noinclude>[[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] is a region that lies on the southern coast of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and is home to the feline [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. It is divided into two major climates: savannahs, badlands and dry plains in the north, and fertile lands of jungle and rainforests in the south.

Originally, sixteen Khajiiti clans roamed the province, and were often simplified as belonging to a region or realm. With a combination of outside pressures and mastery of areas of expertise, it became more accurate to associate a clan with an area. Eventually, sixteen tribes became [[Lore:Empires#Sixteen_Kingdoms_of_Elsweyr|sixteen kingdoms]]: [[Lore:Ne-Quin-Al|Ne-Quin-Al]], [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]], [[Lore:Riverhold|Riverhold]], [[Lore:Dune|Dune]], [[Lore:Orcrest|Orcrest]], [[Lore:Verkarth (Elsweyr)|Verkarth]], [[Lore:Meirvale|Meirvale]], and [[Lore:Helkarn|Helkarn]] in the north; [[Lore:Alabaster|Alabaster]], [[Lore:Bruk'ra|Bruk'ra]], [[Lore:Pa'alatiin|Pa'alatiin]], [[Lore:Corinthe|Corinthe]], [[Lore:Tenmar|Tenmar]], [[Lore:Torval|Torval]], [[Lore:Khenarthia|Khenarthia]], and [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]] in the south.{{ref|name=ESOSongofKingdoms|[[Online:Bardic Performances#Song of Kingdoms|Song of Kingdoms]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The lullaby known as the Hasa Zha'ja explains how these clans got their name, while the poem known as Zhan Zhab tells of the transition from clan names being used to refer to the names of regions. Due to the Thrassian Plague, these sixteen clans changed their tribal functions or they may have died due to starvation and other hardships. These clans essentially became two and were separated ideologically and geographically as what we would come to know as the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine.{{ref|name=ESOAPAI|{{Cite Book|Anequina and Pellitine: An Introduction}}}} These two kingdoms would eventually be united into the [[Lore:Elsweyr Confederacy|Elsweyr Confederacy]], and once more separated in two kingdoms in the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Third Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Sugar and Blood}}}}

==Geography==
Elsweyr is bordered by [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] to the west and by [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] to the north and east, with access to [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]] and the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]] to the south. [[Lore:Lake Vread|Lake Vread]] is a body of water located somewhere in the eastern portion of Elsweyr.{{Ref|name=FSOBDE|{{Cite Book|First Scroll of Baan Dar (Excerpt)|ns_base=ON}}}}

Elsweyr is divided into two main regions: [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] in the north and [[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]] in the south. The island of [[Lore:Khenarthi's Roost|Khenarthi's Roost]] in the Southern Sea is also included in the province of Elsweyr. The [[Lore:Saimisil Steppes|Saimisil Steppes]] is another region found within the province.{{Ref|name=AT|{{Cite Book|Ahzirr Traajijazeri}}}}

===Anequina===
[[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] encompasses the northern half of Elsweyr. The region is dominated by harsh badlands and dry plains,{{ref|name=PGE1E}} which gave rise to a hardened warrior culture among the local Khajiit that viewed their neighbors to the south as decadent and depraved.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

====The Scar====
[[Lore:The Scar|The Scar]] (also known as the '''Scar of Anequina''' or simply the '''Anequina Desert'''){{Ref|name=ESOLoadMeirvale|[[Online:Meirvale Keep|Meirvale Keep]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} is a large desert that runs at the center of [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]].

The Scar is also the name of the main feature of the region: a large canyon that crosses the entire desert from the southwest to the northeast. It is a landscape of harsh wastelands, desolate  mesas and treacherous cliffs. Smaller canyons like the Ashen Scar connect to the main chasm.

The largest settlement, is the city of [[Lore:Orcrest|Orcrest]] in the north. Built on top of a mesa, in the middle of the chasm, it is only accessible via narrow stone bridges. At the other end of the canyon, {{Lore Link|The Stitches}} is a shanty town crippled by criminal activities.

====The Rim Territories====
The [[Lore:Rim Territories|Rim Territories]] (or simply known as '''the Rim'''){{Ref|name=TSK|{{Cite Book|The Sixteen Kingdoms}}}} is composed of the eastern borderlands of Anequina, around the regional capital of [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]]. It Includes the hills and badlands surrounding the City and the southern valley that leads to [[Lore:Hakoshae|Hakoshae]]. 

The region is the namesake of the [[Lore:Rim-Men|Rim-Men]], a group of [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]] that descend from the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{Ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}} The Rim is irrigated by the [[Lore:Anequina Aqueduct|Anequina Aqueduct]], providing water to its inhabitants and allowing the culture of Moonsugar cane.

The [[Lore:Tenmar Borderlands|Tenmar Borderlands]] in the south are attached to the Rim Territories. It consists of a fertile valley between the desert of the Scar and the jungle of Pelitine, stretching all the way to [[Lore: Grahtwood|Grahtwood]]. It includes the settlement of [[Lore:Merryvale|Merryvale]] and the [[Lore:Moon Gate of Anequina|Moon Gate of Anequina]].

====The High Savannah====
The [[Lore:High Savannah|High Savannah]] is an area of arid savannah and dry grassland that stretches along the northern border of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]].{{Ref|name=GTNE|{{Cite Book|Guide to Northern Elsweyr}}}} Its largest settlement is the city of [[Lore:Riverhold|Riverhold]].{{Ref|name=ESO|[[Online:High Savannah|Appearance]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} There are twisted trees found around the region,{{Ref|name=RRR|{{Cite Book|Riddles of the Rithana-di-Renada|ns_base=Online}}}} and stiff savannah breezes blow through [[Lore:The Rim|The Rim]] from the savannah.{{Ref|name=ESOLain|[[Online:Lalaine Senyan|Lalaine Senyan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}

====Reaper's March====
[[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]] is a region connecting the jungles of northeastern [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] with the rolling plains of northwestern [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]. 

Only the eastern half of Reaper's March is considered a part of Elsweyr: the area called [[Lore:Dawnmead|Dawnmead]] surrounding the city of [[Lore:Dune|Dune]] and a fraction of [[Lore:Jodewood|Jodewood]] in the south, including the city of [[Lore:Rawl'kha|Rawl'kha]].

Despite the dry conditions of Reaper's March, the Khajiit have managed to farm and harvest [[Lore:Moon Sugar|moon sugar]] in the area for generations through extensive irrigation projects.{{ref|name=MSITM|{{Cite Book|Moon-Sugar in the March}}}}

Khajiiti temples in the region are noted for their awe-inspiring beauty, though there are many other monuments and grand structures which are decayed and ruined.{{ref|name=TME|{{Cite Book|The March Explored}}}}<noinclude>

===Pellitine===
[[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]] encompasses the southern half of Elsweyr. The region is considerably more fertile than the arid north, and is replete with jungles, rainforests, and river basins.{{ref|name=PGE1E}} The Khajiit of Pellitine were consequently wealthier than their neighbors to the north, whom they derided as uncouth barbarians.{{ref|name=PGE3E}} The seat of the Mane, [[Lore:Torval|Torval]], is located here.

==History==
===Since the Beginning of Time===
The origins of the region of today's Elsweyr and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] lies far back in the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], when [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] was only one large continent. As the war among the wandering [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] and Old Ehlnofey broke out, the continent broke apart into four.{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} The later lands of Elsweyr remained on what is now known as [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The Khajiiti creation myth explains the hot deserts of northern Elsweyr and the wet and poisonous [[Lore:Tenmar Forest|Tenmar Forest]] in the south to be made by [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]] as revenge for being tricked by [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]].{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}

Early records and encounters reveal the Khajiit to have lived far beyond today's borders of Elsweyr—reaching north into the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]], close to today's [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}}, and west into [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]]—making them the dominant culture of southern Tamriel. It was already highly developed as [[Lore:Man|Men]] and [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] just arrived on the continent and fought for dominance in the north.{{ref|name=PGE3E}} The ancient Khajiit in the Heartlands also may be responsible for the extinction of the [[Lore:Bird Men|Bird Men]] home to [[Lore:City Isle|City Isle]].{{ref|name=FOTN}} In some of Tamriel's oldest history, the province of Elsweyr was ruled by the [[Lore:Moon-Emperor|Moon-Emperor]]. Gracious architecture that shows veneration towards the moons derives from this, as seen across modern-day [[Lore:Dune|Dune]]'s high walls.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=BK|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion}}}}

===The Sixteen Kingdoms===
Sometime in the Merethic Era, when Elsweyr was composed of sixteen kingdoms, Kaalgrontiid and his horde of [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]] came to dominate the land and above all, consume the Moon's lunar power. Kaalgrontiid is believed by some to originate from Alduin's horde in [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]] and [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], having been driven to split off from the bulk of his kin in the north in order to sate his own pride and ambition.  This behavior is considered normal for dragons, who are naturally driven to seek mastery.{{Ref|name=LADragons|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Dragons in the Second Era}}}} Kaalgrontiid in particular hoped that with the Moon's power, he would become [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]'s equal.{{ref|name=ESOKaalgrontiid|[[Online:Kaalgrontiid|Kaalgrontiid]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} To stop them, The Khajiit hero [[Lore:Khunzar-ri|Khunzar-ri]] recruited four companions, {{Lore Link|Nurarion the Perfect}}, [[Lore:Flinthild Demon-Hunter|Flinthild Demon-Hunter]], [[Lore:Anequina Sharp-Tongue|Anequina Sharp-Tongue]], and [[Lore:Cadwell|Cadwell the Betrayer]].{{Ref|name=KatD|{{Cite Book|Khunzar-ri and the Demons}}}} When fighting them proved to be futile, Cadwell suggested that it may be best to convince the dragons that they were now a willing part of their plans. In preparation, Anequina attuned to {{Lore Link|Shadow Dance Temple}}, incited the moons to align to form an eclipse, allowing the {{Lore Link|Moon Gate of Anequina}} to open a portal the Plane of [[Lore:Jode|Jode]], where the object the dragons desired, [[Lore:Jode's Core|Jode's Core]], rested. The dragon's ambitions would be their undoing, as the dragons accessed the [[Lore:Plane of Jode|Plane of Jode]], but Khunzar-ri convinced them to not simply absorb the power of the Core like they originally intended, but instead store their energy within it in the guise that they would combine the lunar power with their own.{{ref|name=ESOCadwell|[[Online:Cadwell|Cadwell]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOKhamira|[[Online:Khamira|Khamira]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The gamble worked, and the dragons were weakened enough for them to retreat back into the wilderness.{{Ref|name=KatD}} To finish the job, Khunzar-ri once again convinced the dragons to play a part of his scheme. He managed to lure them into the [[Lore:Halls of Colossus|Halls of Colossus]], where he managed to seal them away. The Dragons would be obscured as "demons" in legends, and become the fabled "Demon Weapon". Their power would be coveted by many people.{{Ref|name=KatD}}

By the beginning of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], the sixteen realms in the region of today's Elsweyr still existed.{{ref|name=Pacrooti|[[ON:Pacrooti|Pacrooti]]'s hireling letters in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} These were not comparable to the kingdoms of Men and Mer, as those Khajiiti realms were not fighting each other for power or land. Instead they were existing in harmony and complementary. Each such kingdom was specialized on providing goods or services and shared it with their neighbors.{{ref|name=EGTE|{{Cite Book|EGT|Elsweyr}}}} [[Lore:Ne Quin-al|Ne Quin-al]], also known as ''[[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]]'' in Cyrodilic, for example provided their excellent trained fighters of the [[Lore:Two-Moons Dance|Two-Moons Dance]] to [[Lore:Torval|Torval]] in exchange for fish and other goods from the sea. Influence and power shifted constantly, and the dominance of the regions cycled through the kingdoms depending on the phases of [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]]. For example, Ne Quin-al was in power at full moons, while Torval was when both moons were half, or [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]] when they were both new.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

In all this time, the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] seemed to have shown little activity in the regions controlled by the Khajiit.{{ref|name=Burri|[[ON:Centurion Burri|Centurion Burri]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Only after {{Year|1E 243}}, when [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] begun to persecute the Ayleids, the clan of [[Lore:Atatar|Atatar]] tried to find refugee in the Khajiiti lands. However, most of them died on the ill-fated expedition.{{ref|name=ASIV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Survivals in Valenwood}}}}

At similar time [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] has been slaying many Khajiit, having them mistaken for another strain of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]. [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]] is being attributed to have repelled this attack{{ref|name=VOFTK|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Khajiit}}}}, although it also is said Pelinal had realized his error himself.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}
[[File:ON-place-Do'Krin Monastery.jpg|right|thumb|Do'Krin Monastery was built with the loot of the Anequine Conquests]]
In the [[Lore:First Era#Fifth Century|fifth century of the First Era]]{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}} the Golden Beast of Anequina, [[Lore:Darloc Brae|Darloc Brae]], expanded the territory of Ne Quin-al in the process of the [[Lore:Anequine Conquests|Anequine Conquests]] until it ranged from [[Lore:Khaj Rawlith|Khaj Rawlith]] in the west, over [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]], up to the north at [[Lore:Larsius River|Larsius River]], and [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]] in the east. The loot of the Conquests he used to build [[Lore:Do'Krin Monastery|Do'Krin Monastery]]{{ref|name=LSDKM|[[ON:Loading Screens|Loading Screen of Do'Krin Monastery]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LSKR|[[ON:Loading Screens|Loading Screen of Khaj Rawlith]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He was also invited to the coronation of the [[Lore:Gorieus|Emperor Gorieus]] in {{Year|1E 461}}.{{ref|name=RTR}}

With the year of {{Year|1E 668}} came [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]], an event caused by the eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]].{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}}

Over many centuries there are no further records of the happenings in the kingdoms of the Khajiit. Only the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] from {{Year|1E 1200}} until {{Year|1E 2208}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{TIL|Where were you when the Dragon Broke?|dragonbroke}} — '' [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]''}} was mentioned to be observed by the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]], using the constancy of the [[Lore:Ja-Kha'jay|Ja-Kha'jay]] to record it.{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}

The political balance in the sixteen Khajiiti kingdoms proved stable enough for thousands of years. Neither the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] nor the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] of Valenwood dared to extend their borders too far into the lands of the Khajiit, but caused increasing animosity between the Khajiiti realms.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}{{ref|name=EGTE}}

===The Two Kingdoms===
After two millennia, the political landscape of the Khajiiti territory changed drastically with the emerging [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] of {{Year|1E 2260}}.{{ref|name=EGTE}} It expanded along the trading routes deep into the realm of the Khajiit and caused mass mortality. The balance between the sixteen kingdoms was finally destroyed as the survivors were forced into undesired roles; initial consternation turned to distrust and violence.

The remains of the original sixteen realms were two realms with opposing cultures: The northern [[Lore:Ne Quin-al|Ne Quin-al]] with its many warrior clans, and the wealthy southern [[Lore:Pa'alatiin|Pa'alatiin]], also known as ''[[Lore:Pellitine|Pellitine]]'' in Cyrodilic. Both kingdoms fought for centuries, but neither could ever gain the upper hand in Elsweyr.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}{{ref|name=EGTE}}

====In the Second Empire====
With the rise of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]] in {{Year|1E 2703}}, the newfound [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] began to expand. Also, the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine could not resist the Empire's power and were conquered, alongside a propaganda campaign to justify the invasion.{{ref|name=MBHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Moon Bishop Hunal Answers Your Questions}}}} Later, in {{Year|1E 2920}}, [[Lore:Naghea|Queen Naghea]] of [[Lore:Riverhold|Riverhold]] lead the left flank for the Empire on in the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Battle of Bodrum]].{{ref|name=2920FS|{{Cite Book|2920, First Seed (v3)}}}} In the same year, the King of Senchal, [[Lore:Dro'Zel|Dro'Zel]], became appointed advisor to the court of [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]].{{ref|name=2920SD|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}

As long as Anequina and Pellitine were part of the Empire, they found security in it, but also never stopped rebelling against it at the same time.{{ref|name=PGE3E}} The Empire never could bring an end to the fights between the two Khajiit kingdoms.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}{{ref|name=EGTE}}

===The Formation of the Elsweyr Confederacy ===
[[File:ON-concept-Elsweyr Map.png|right|thumb|Elsweyr around the Second Era]]
The ongoing strife between Anequin and Pellitine was finally resolved by the Khajiit themselves. [[Lore:Keirgo|Keirgo]] of Anequina and [[Lore:Eshita|Eshita]] of Pellitine married in {{Year|2E 309}} and combined the two conflicting kingdoms, aware of the political impact, into one: The [[Lore:Elsweyr Confederacy|Elsweyr Confederacy]]. The reason for the name is a matter of debate; either a reference to the old Khajiiti proverb "A perfect society is always found elsewhere", or to the [[Lore:Sands Behind the Stars|Sands Behind the Stars]], in [[Lore:Ta'agra|Ta'agra]] called ''Llesw'er''.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}{{ref|name=EGTE}} The latter however is a paradise promised by the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar]]{{ref|name=PGE3E}} two years after the Elsweyr Confederacy had already formed.{{ref|name=ONLSRT|[[ON:Loading Screens#Location Messages|ESO Loading Screens: ''Rawl'kha Temple'']]}}

====The Rebellion====
The formation of the new kingdom did not bring the expected peace. The cleft between tribal culture of the north and nobility of the south was still present. The power between the two former kingdoms, each had their own central government and allied tribes, shifted to the nobility. The chieftains of the north felt betrayed by both their ruling classes and forgot about their vendettas only to sign treaties of their own. Soon the cities of former Anequina were under constant attack, leaving King Keirgo no choice than to petition to the Empire for help. But the Empire itself was in similar unrest, as they lost their own ruler, [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Potentate]] [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]], assassinated in his palace in Senchal, in {{Year|2E 324}}.{{ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Darkness}}}} As [[Lore:Ne Quin-al|Ne Quin-al]], the old capital, fell to the rebels, the young Confederation seemed to soon break apart.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}

[[File:ON-place-Rawl'kha Temple.jpg|right|thumb|Rawl'kha temple in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
As the rebellion kept going on, [[Lore:Mane|Mane]] [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]]—the spiritual leader of the Khajiit—revealed the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]] in the [[Lore:Temple of Two-Moons Dance|Temple of Two-Moons Dance]] of [[Lore:Rawl'kha|Rawl'kha]] in {{Year|2E 311}}.{{ref|name=ONLSRT}} The edicts from this key event in Khajiiti theology however were not accepted by everyone at this time{{ref|name=Shazah|[[ON:Shazah|Shazah]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}, leading to those exiled and their temples deconsecrated.{{ref|name=ONLSCS|[[ON:Loading Screens#Location Messages|ESO Loading Screens: ''Claw's Strike'']]}} Rid-Thar-ri'Datta was since then known as the First Mane, although there have been at least two Manes before him.{{ref|name=Shazah}} The Mane, usually nonpartisan, used Riddle'Thar to proclaim a new system of dividing the powers in Elsweyr. Bound to the dance of the two moons—their different phases—the power in a region shifted between the nobility of the cities and the chieftains of the deserts. The Mane itself was overseeing this shift of powers, and as this system was implemented it finally brought peace to Elsweyr some time around or after {{Year|2E 324}}—lasting under many more Manes.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

Some time after the last Potentate, [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]], of the Second Empire was killed in {{Year|2E 430}}{{ref|name=TBOD}}, warlord [[Lore:Attrebus (warlord)|Attrebus]] persecuted the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The Khajiit granted the [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] and others asylum in and around Rimmen in northern Elsweyr.{{ref|name=PGE1E}} They later built the [[Lore:Tonenaka|Tonenaka]] in the city.{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|164}}{{ref|name=CMT|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 53: Tsaesci Style}}}}

====Outbreak of the Knahaten Flu====
Two hundred years after the rebellion—that followed the formation of the Elsweyr Confederacy—the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] ravaged the land of the Khajiit just as unprepared as the Thrassian Plague before.{{ref|name=TEATC|{{Cite Book|The Eagle and the Cat}}}} The flu, reported the first time in [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] in {{Year|2E 560}}{{ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Argonia}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1W|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}}, appeared in Elsweyr the first time on [[Lore:Sweet Street|Sweet Street]] in the [[Lore:Black Keirgo|Black Keirgo]] slums of Senchal. First dismissed as a toxin in the goods by the city's elders, it spread over to [[Lore:Dagi's Pride|Dagi's Pride]], [[Lore:Squint-Eye|Squint-Eye]], and the docks of [[Lore:Alabaster|Alabaster]]. The expansion of the flu accelerated, soon to be in [[Lore:Torval|Torval]], [[Lore:Orcrest|Orcrest]] in {{Year|2E 565}}{{ref|name=Shazah}}, [[Lore:Dune|Dune]], [[Lore:Corinthe|Corinthe]], and everywhere between.{{ref|name=TEATC}}

As the Khajiit were focused on fighting their own extinction, [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]n armies, in the time of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]], invaded the northern part of Elsweyr and annexed it. Their invasion went as far as [[Lore:Riverhold|Riverhold]] and Orcrest, until they were fought back there in {{Year|2E 562}} or a few years later.{{ref|name=Khali|[[ON:Khali|Khali]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

In the meantime, [[Lore:Clan Mother|Clan Mother]] [[Lore:Mizaba-ko|Mizaba-ko]] of Corinthe identified the transfer of the flu, and [[Lore:Rathuni-la Dawnwhisker|Rathuni-la Dawnwhisker]] distilled a sorghum-tea that mitigated the worst of the symptoms. [[Lore:Khajiiti Nobility|Lord]] [[Lore:Gharesh-ri|Gharesh-ri]], [[Lore:Speaker for the Mane|Speaker for the Mane]], organized the remnants of the [[Lore:Mane's Legion|Mane's Legion]] to maintain order and use the newfound knowledge. However, all this did not prevent further spreading of the flu and many more dead. When hope was almost lost, unexpected help came from the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] of [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], bringing direly needed physicians, healers, and supplies. This aid gradually defeated the Knahaten Flu and saving the Khajiit. Most of them were however suspicious about the Altmer's help, as it was given to preserve the Khajiit as possible alliance partners in times of need.{{ref|name=TEATC}}

In {{Year|2E 576}} [[Lore:Euraxia Tharn|Euraxia Tharn]] became Queen of Rimmen after the [[Lore:Frostfall Coup|Frostfall Coup]].{{ref|name=HTON|{{Cite Book|House Tharn of Nibenay}}}}

====In the First Aldmeri Dominion====
With the death of King [[Lore:Hidellith|Hidellith]] of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] in {{Year|2E 580}}, Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] took up the throne.{{ref|name=ATUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn: The Unforeseen Queen}}}} As around this time invading armies moved towards southwest Tamriel, she summoned the Khajiit to stand by her side, as compensation for helping them defeating the Knahaten Flu. The signing of the [[Lore:Elden Accord|Elden Accord]] by Queen Ayrenn, [[Online:Lord Gharesh-ri|Lord Gharesh-ri]] and King [[Lore:Aeradan Camoran|Aeradan Camoran]] formed the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|First Aldmeri Dominion]] in which the Khajiit thus entered.{{ref|name=Gharesh-ri|[[ON:Lord Gharesh-ri|Lord Gharesh-ri]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TEATC}} Under the united banner of the Dominion, they now also moved into the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]].{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}}{{ref|name=WTNAI|{{Cite Book|Welcome to New Aldmeri Irregulars}}}}

=====Attack on Reapers March=====
[[File:ON-concept-Reapers March.jpg|right|thumb|Reapers March in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
The [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] also hit [[Lore:Reapers March|Reapers March]] in {{Year|2E 582}}. [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] from the [[Lore:Coldharbour|realm]] of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], with the help of the [[Lore:Worm Cult|Worm Cult]], appeared all over the region in an attempt to suck it into Coldharbour.

Also in this year, the Colovians once again invaded northern Elsweyr. [[Lore:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]] worked together with the [[Lore:Stonefire Cult|Stonefire Cult]], and they were able to corrupt the current Mane [[Online:Akkhuz-ri|Akkhuz-ri]] as he was on a travel to Valenwood—to show condolence to the death of [[Lore:Silvenar|Silvenar]] [[Lore:Edhelorn|Edhelorn]] in the same year.{{ref|name=Bodring|[[ON:Bodring|Bodring]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Mane was henceforth possessed by the [[Lore:Dark Mane|Dark Mane]]—a [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|Dro-m'Athra]]—and worked together with Javad Tharn.{{ref|name=Khali}}{{ref|name=Kazirra|[[ON:Kazirra|Kazirra]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TheColovianOccupation|Events of quest [[ON:The Colovian Occupation|The Colovian Occupation]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} On the way to the [[Lore:Handfasting|Handfasting]] of the new Silvenar [[Lore:Indaenir|Indaenir]] and [[Lore:Green Lady|Green Lady]] [[Lore:Finoriell|Finoriell]], Akkhuz-ri attacked his followers and fled to Reaper's March.{{ref|name=Gharesh-ri}}

On his way, Akkhuz-ri freed the Dro-m'Athra in the long forgotten temple of Khaj Rawlith, below [[Lore:Fort Grimwatch|Fort Grimwatch]], to aid Colovian siege there. The [[Lore:Soulless One|Moon Hallowed]] and both [[Lore:Lunar Champion|Lunar Champions]], [[Lore:Shazah|Shazah]] and [[Lore:Khali|Khali]], fought the Colovians and Dro-m'Athra back with the aid of the spirit of Rid-Thar-ri'Datta.{{ref|name=GrimSituation|Events of quest [[ON:Grim Situation|Grim Situation]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GrimmerStill|Events of quest [[ON:Grimmer Still|Grimmer Still]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In Arenthia, the Moon Hallowed helped the local militia to end the Colovian occupation, destroy a Dark Anchor, and drive the Dark Mane out of Akkhuz-ri.{{ref|name=TheColovianOccupation}}{{ref|name=StonefireMachinations|Events of quest [[ON:Stonefire Machinations|Stonefire Machinations]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Mane resigned due the dark influence that had befallen him{{ref|name=AkkhuzRi|[[ON:Akkhuz-ri|Akkhuz-ri]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}, leading the two Lunar Champions on the way to become his successors. In the temple of Rawl'kha, Shazah and Khali faced the [[Lore:Trial Path|Trial Path]] successfully. Before this point there never have been two Lunar Champions at the same time to contest for the position of the Mane before in recorded history.{{ref|name=Kauzanabi-jo|[[ON:Kauzanabi-jo|Kauzanabi-jo]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TheFirstStep|Events of quest [[ON:The First Step|The First Step]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Martialis Tharn|Martialis Tharn]], Javad Tharn and the Stonefire Cult also sought to acquire the [[Lore:Golden Claw|Golden Claw]]{{ref|name=RidTharRiDatta|[[ON:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}—a powerful relic from the era of Mane [[Lore:Tullar-dra|Tullar-dra]]{{ref|name=Azahrr|[[ON:Azahrr|Azahrr]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}—in Do'Krin Monastery, but were fought back by the Moon Hallowed and Rid-Thar-ri'Datta's spirit.{{ref|name=MotesInTheMoonlight|Events of quest [[ON:Motes in the Moonlight|Motes in the Moonlight]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

After this, Shazah, Khali and the Moon Hallowed fought the Dark Mane and by him and Javad Tharn released Dro-m'Athra in [[Lore:Moonmont|Moonmont]].{{ref|name=RidTharRiDatta}} One of the two Lunar Champions sacrificed herself to contain the Dark Mane, leaving only one Lunar Champion left.{{ref|name=TheGoldenClaw|Events of quest [[ON:The Golden Claw|The Golden Claw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-place-Dune.jpg|right|thumb|Dune in {{Year|2E 582}}]]
Later the Moon Hallowed and remaining Lunar Champion arrived in [[Lore:Dune|Dune]]. At this time, the city was under an attack of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], summoned up by [[Lore:Bayya|Bayya]] and Javad Tharn.{{ref|name=Bayya|[[ON:Bayya|Bayya]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Esan|[[ON:Esan|Esan]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dagila|[[ON:Dagila|Dagila]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The city was liberated, the by the Stonefire Cult befouled temples{{ref|name=Shazah}} cleansed, and a Dark Anchor at the Shrine to Lorkhaj in the temple district destroyed.{{ref|name=TheFiresOfDune|Events of quest [[ON:The Fires of Dune|The Fires of Dune]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In presence of the Dominion's leaders and other important figures at the [[Lore:Two Moons Path (temple)|Two Moons Path]] temple, the Moon Hallowed and Lunar Champion walked the last path—the [[Lore:Two Moons Path|Two Moons Path]]. The other Lunar Champion that sacrificed herself before was set free by this, able to reach [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]].{{ref|name=RidTharRiDatta}} Returned from the Path, the remaining Lunar Champion rose as new Mane of the Khajiit.{{ref|name=TheDenOfLorkhaj|Events of quest [[ON:The Den of Lorkhaj|The Den of Lorkhaj]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

With the destruction of the [[Lore:Great Shackle|Great Shackle]] and the end of the Planemeld within the same year—{{Year|2E 582}}—also the Dark Anchors no longer threatened Reapers March.{{ref|name=BreakingTheShackle|Events of quest [[ON:Breaking the Shackle|Breaking the Shackle]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TheFinalAssault|Events of quest [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

=====Dragons over Elsweyr=====
After the retrieval of the Wrathstone around {{Year|2E 582}}, the Imperial battlemage [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]] inadvertently unleashed the Dragons from their containment from the Halls of Colossus, thus causing them to continue in their quest for the Moon's Lunar Power. The Dragons allied themselves with the forces of the Queen of [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]], [[Lore:Euraxia Tharn|Euraxia Tharn]], who were lead to believe that the dragons were willing to be used as tools. The Dragons were quick to cross Euraxia when she was no longer a use to them, and was left without their aid against the forces of Elsweyr Defense Force as well as the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]]. The turn of events resulted in her death and the return of Rimmen to Khajiit control. Euraxia's forces now without a leader, allied with the Dragons to avoid their wrath.{{ref|name=ESOTFO|Events of [[ON:The Final Order|The Final Order]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The Dragons were put to a halt when the Vestige stepped into the Plane of Jode and defeated Kaalgrontiid and his kin, preventing the lunar power from being completely absorbed.{{ref|name=ESOJC|Events of [[ON:Jode's Core (quest)|Jode's Core]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}

The dragon Laatvulon sought to continue his master Kaalgrontiid's plan of bringing on the Dark Aeon, intending for Kaalgrontiid to become Akatosh's equal.  To this end, Laatvulon created a Khajiiti dragon cult, the New Moon Cult, whose ranks were bolstered by many of the vulnerable Khajiit from the region, such as those who were affected by the Knahaten Flu. The red dragon [[Lore:Nahfahlaar|Nahfahlaar]], who was Laatvulon's ancient rival, dueled Laatvulon near the city of [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]] in southern Elsweyr, but was overpowered due to Laatvulon's newfound power. Afterwards, Taking refuge in [[Lore:Tideholm|Tideholm]], Nahfahlaar was encountered by the Dragonguard, which been recently reformed by the Redguard Blademaster [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]]. Seeing that Nahfahlaar meant no harm, an alliance was forged once again with former foes/allies, one in which Nahfahlaar served as a valuable asset. This alliance led to victory, and Laatvulon, the Demon from the East, was slain.{{ref|name=ESONahfahlaar|[[Online:Nahfahlaar|Nahfahlaar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}

Nahfahlaar subsequently aided the Dragonguard in defeating Kaalgrontiid and the remnants of the New Moon Cult. Kaalgrontiid returned and planned to siphon the energy he collected from Jode's Core and become empowered by a giant cluster of [[Lore:Aeonstone|Aeonstone]]. To prevent anyone from disrupting his plans, he utilized the energy of the Aeonstone to create an aegis, a massive and impenetrable ward, around the ancient fortress of Dragonhold. Through the combined efforts of Nahfahlaar, the Dragonguard, and Queen [[Lore:Khamira|Khamira]]'s forces, Kaalgrontiid was slain. However, the Dark Aeon became unstable as a result of Kaalgrontiid's machinations and threatened to destroy the entire continent of Tamriel. It required the combined power of Abnur Tharn and Nahfahlaar to prevent the Aeonstone from affecting the area outside of Dragonhold. Nahfahlaar and Abnur were successful, but only at the cost of Abnur's life. With Tharn's sacrifice, the rage of Dragons was stopped.{{ref|name=ESONahfahlaar}}

=====After the Dragons=====
The Akaviri of Rimmen later joined the cause to restore the Second Empire, when remnants of the [[Lore:Kamal (race)|Kamal]] army of the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] of {{Year|2E 572}} tried seizing the Throne from Attrebus' successors. This attempt did not succeed, and the Khajiit used the chaos of these events to reclaim lost land in a series of border wars.

Rimmen seceded from Elsweyr in {{Year|2E 812}}, forming the Kingdom of Rimmen. They however still were paying taxes to the Mane in return for protection of their independence.{{ref|name=PGE1E}}

====In the Third Empire====
Towards the end of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the ascending [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] conquered parts of Anequina and cast out all Khajiit. There, in the Halls of Colossus, he reconstructed and reactivated [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]].{{ref|group=UOL|name=SMIWDOT|{{TIL|Skeleton Man's Interview with Denizens of Tamriel|skeleton}}}} Not long after, Senchal was besieged by [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial]] troops under General [[Lore:Pottreid|Pottreid]]. The population did not surrender and was slain entirely.{{ref|name=Attrebus|[[Redguard:Attrebus|Attrebus]]' [[General:Redguard.com Archive/Characters#Attrebus|biography]] on Redguard's official website}} Ultimately, Elsweyr was annexed to become a province of Tiber Septim's [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], but as under the Second Empire, there have been rebellions against the Empire.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

Under the reign of Emperor [[Lore:Uriel II|Uriel Septim II]] a rebellion broke out in Torval. It proved too strong and forced the Emperor to lend money from King [[Lore:Mantiarco|Mantiarco]] to pay additional mercenaries next to the Imperial Legions to strike it down successfully. In return he had to promise him the hand of his daughter, [[Lore:Galana|Galana]].{{ref|name=TWQ1|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v1}}}}

=====The Five Year War=====
[[File:LO-map-Valenwood (Oblivion Codex).jpg|right|thumb|Valenwood at the end of the Third Era]]
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]]—from {{Year|3E 389}} to {{Year|3E 399}}—a conflict between Valenwood and Elsweyr escalated:{{ref|name=PGE3E}} In {{Year|3E 395}}, Bosmer invaded Torval, slaying thousands of citizens as retaliation for, as they claimed, Khajiiti bandit attacks. This [[Lore:Slaughter of Torval|Slaughter of Torval]] marked the beginning of the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]] for the Khajiit.{{ref|name=MUT|{{Cite Book|Mixed Unit Tactics}}}} The Bosmer date its beginning back to {{Year|3E 394}}.{{ref|name=CHOA|{{Cite Book|Cherim's Heart of Anequina}}}} After a lost battles at [[Lore:Zelinin|Zelinin]], the Khajiit won the upper hand in all following: the [[Lore:Heart of Anequina|Heart of Anequina]], {{Year|3E 396}} at [[Lore:Fort Sphinxmoth|Fort Sphinxmoth]], [[Lore:Athay|Athay]]{{ref|name=ADIF3|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 3}}}} and [[Lore:Vindis|Vindis]].{{ref|name=ADIF4|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 4}}}} The Khajiit occupied both banks of [[Lore:Xylo River|Xylo River]] in [[Lore:Malabal Tor|Malabal Tor]].{{ref|name=CHOA}}{{ref|name=PGE3E}} At the end of the Imperial Simulacrum, with Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]]{{ref|name=ALOUS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Uriel Septim VII}}}} back on the throne, the Empire tried to give back the land to the Bosmer. But Khajiit had already settled down in the area and refused to leave, leaving the Empire to leave the situation, possibly convinced that the land rightly belonged to the Khajiit by ancient treaty, and not to cause another conflict.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

=====Loss of Territory to Leyawiin=====
At or before {{Year|3E 432}} there has been another border conflict; Between Elsweyr and the County of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]]. A long lasting dispute was resolved by an agreement between the Mane and [[Lore:Marius Caro|Marius Caro]], Count of Leyawiin, leading to a loss of territory for Elsweyr comparable to the win of the previous Five Year War. This agreement however is not accepted by all Khajiit, and so the [[Lore:Renrijra Krin|Renrijra Krin]] tried to take back the land.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

The last two decades of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] in Elsweyr were marked by the flourishing of [[Lore:Moon Sugar|Moon Sugar]] trade. Under this influence, the formerly dangerous slum Senchal rose up to a resort for wealthy and powerful Khajiit. The sugar trader [[Lore:Ya'Tirrje|Ya'Tirrje]], called Gold Cat by his wealth, had a rumored villa in the port city, and paid the guards to keep it crime-free, while continuing his business in Torval, Corinthe and Rimmen.{{ref|name=PGE3E}}

===Dissolution===
====After the Oblivion Crisis====
[[File:Mfull Sfull.jpg|right|thumb|Masser and Secunda vanished for two years in the Void Nights]]
After the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] and the following assassination of Potentate [[Lore:Ocato of Firsthold|Ocato of Firsthold]], the [[Lore:Elder Council|Stormcrown Interregnum]] begun.{{ref|name=RT4|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} In this time, Elsweyr once again found itself independent of the Empire, but was granted no peace. In or around {{Year|4E 48}} the Mane was assassinated, sinking the south of Elsweyr into chaos. During this time, an unknown proclaimed himself Potentate of Rimmen and surrounding areas.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|179}} While the city was rich of Moon Sugar, distribution was tightly controlled, and entirely forbidden to Khajiit clans refusing to work in the city.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|179, 186}}

====Void Nights and Great War====
In {{Year|4E 98}}, Masser and Secunda vanished from the sky for two entire years. The so-called [[Lore:Void Nights|Void Nights]] ended in 4E 100 with the return of the moons, while the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] took credit for bringing them back. Whether or not they were actually responsible for this turnaround remains unresolved. Nevertheless, as the Khajiit people and culture are tied inexorably close to the moons, the Thalmor were able to win great influence over them as their apparent saviors. Within the following fifteen years, until {{Year|4E 115}}, the influence of the Empire dwindled so much it was unable to stop the coup which dissolved the Elsweyr Confederacy and recreated the original kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine as client states of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Third Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=TGW}}

In {{Year|4E 171}}, Aldmeri armies invaded Hammerfell and Cyrodiil simultaneously. A strong force commanded by the Thalmor general Lord [[Lore:Naarifin|Naarifin]] attacked Cyrodiil from the south, marching out of hidden camps in northern Elsweyr and flanking the Imperial defenses along the Valenwood border.{{ref|name=TGW}}

==Other Information==
===Cuisine===
:''→ See the main article: '''[[Lore:Khajiit Cuisine|Khajiit Cuisine]]'''''
The cuisine of Elsweyr is invariably sweet. Meals such as candies, cakes, puddings, sugarmeats and honey stews makes the staples of the Khajiit diet. Non-Khajiiti are often advised against eating these dishes as most of them contain moon sugar, a dangerous substance to which the Khajiit have both a natural predisposition and a resistance developed from a lifelong consumption of the substance. On such occasions, merely touching the food with lips is considered a polite form of declining.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{ref|name=PGE1E}} Elsweyr Fondue is a dish containing ale, moon sugar and Eidar cheese, which is a prime example of the cuisine.
