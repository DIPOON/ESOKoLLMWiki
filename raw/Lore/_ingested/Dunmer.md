The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], also known as '''Dark Elves''',{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} '''Ash-Elves''' by the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]],{{Ref|name=TWMD|{{Cite Book|Transcribed Words of Mehrunes Dagon}}}}{{Ref|name=Skrix|[[ON:Skrix|Skrix]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} "'''Pureblooded Folk'''" by themselves,{{ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} or the archaic '''Moriche''',{{ref|name=TWE|''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' variant of {{Cite Book|The Wild Elves}}}}<noinclude>{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}</noinclude> are the grey-skinned, typically red-eyed [[Lore:Mer|elven]] peoples of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. In the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], "Dark Elf" is the common usage, but they and their [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] brethren prefer the term "Dunmer".{{ref|name=PGE1M}} "Dark" is variously interpreted as meaning "dark-skinned", "gloomy", and "ill-favored by fate", with the Dunmer and their national identity embracing these various connotations with enthusiasm.{{Ref|name=MWGND|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue D|Generic Dialogue]] on the topic of "Dunmer" in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Their combination of powerful intellects with strong and agile physiques often produce great warriors and sorcerers.{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}} On the battlefield, Dunmer are noted for their skill with a balanced integration of the sword, the bow and destruction magic. Dunmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.{{ref|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4|Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4]]}}{{ref|[[Lore:The Real Barenziah, v 2|The Real Barenziah, v 2]]}} Some of the most ancient and powerful Dunmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for several thousands of years through the use of potent magical practices.{{ref|name=Telvanni|[[Lore:Neloth|Neloth's]] presence in [[Morrowind:Neloth|Morrowind]] and [[Skyrim:Neloth|Skyrim]], and [[Skyrim:Neloth%23Related_Quests|Related Quests]] in his study of [[Lore:Magic|Magic]]}} In character, they are generally grim, aloof, and reserved.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}
{{TOCright|limit=2}}
Dunmer distrust and are treated distrustfully by other races.{{ref|name=AATD|{{Cite Book|Ancestors and the Dunmer}}}} They are often called proud, clannish, ruthless, and cruel, from an outsider's point of view, but greatly value loyalty and family.{{ref|name=AATD}} They also apparently have a reputation for quick tempers.{{ref|name=ONTilenra|[[ON:Tilenra Sildreth|Tilenra Sildreth]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Despite their powerful skills and strengths, the Dunmer's vengeful nature, age-old conflicts, betrayals, and ill-reputation prevent them from gaining more influence. Those born in their homeland of Morrowind are reputed to be considerably less friendly than those who grew up in the Imperial tradition.{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

Dark Elves are especially susceptible to skin diseases, which are collectively referred to as "corprus" (not to be confused with the [[Lore:Corprus#The Divine Disease|Divine Disease]]), being named after the ancient Velothi word for "skin blight".{{ref|{{Cite Book|Skin Blights By Any Other Name}}}}

==History==
[[File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg|Fresco of Veloth leading his people to Resdayn|thumb|right]]
The history of the Dunmer begins with the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]&mdash;a race of elves with pale gold skin who followed the prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] from [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isle]] to Resdayn (present-day Morrowind) seeking religious freedom.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=LotS|{{Cite Book|Lives of the Saints}}}} Their Aldmer predecessors came to Tamriel with deep-rooted traditions of ancestor worship. As Aldmer society grew more stratified, the class divide became a prominent aspect of society and bled into religious beliefs. The ancestor spirits of the upper castes grew in popularity, and in time, they were converted into gods. Where once [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] had been an ancestor spirit of the nobility, all the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] would eventually claim Auri-El as a common ancestor.{{ref|name=PG3E|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}}{{ref|name=VOF}}

Those who would become the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] were among the mer who vocally disapproved of these societal changes. The Keepers of the Old Ways of Aldmeris believed that all divine forces are driven by ancestor spirits; even the Aedra and Daedra are just the spirits of exceptional mortals who attained power in the afterlife.{{ref|name=TOW|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}} The Psijics' defiant settling of Artaeum was the first of many departures from what is now the home of the High Elves, and a mass exodus of the lower classes soon occurred.{{ref|name=PG3E}} The Chimer (who are also referred to as "the Velothi people", a moniker later upheld by the [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]]) were among those who fled.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Exodus from Summerset}}}}

===First Era===
The Dunmer were born from the ashes of the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]].{{ref|name=PGE1M}} The infamous confrontation around {{Year|1E 700}} led to the death of an ancient and respected war leader known as [[Lore:Nerevar|Lord Indoril Nerevar]], the destruction of the [[Lore:Sixth House|Sixth House]], the disappearance of the entire [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] race then present on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], and the ascension into godhood of four of Nerevar's councilors and closest friends, culminating in a curse from [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] (the Goddess of Dusk and Dawn and the patron of Nerevar) that transformed the Dunmer into their present appearance.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=TRN|{{Cite Book|The Real Nerevar}}}}

Azura's curse was motivated in response to Nerevar's councilors, the general [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], the sorcerer [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], and his wife [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], breaking an oath they had sworn to Nerevar and Azura by stealing the divine power of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] for themselves.{{ref|name=TBORM}} When this [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] of new living gods showed no remorse to Azura, she cursed them and all Chimer, tying the fate of the Dunmer and Tribunal together until the end of time.{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=NARM}} Some Dunmer don't consider the darkening of their skin to be a curse, but rather see their sober complexion as a gift to mark the ascent of the Tribunal.{{ref|name=Vunal|[[Online:Sorcerer Vunal|Sorcerer Vunal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Writings briefly circulated across [[Lore:Cyrod|Cyrod]] in the aftermath with exaggerated claims such as the curse signaled the "end times" and staring into their eyes would cause one to "fall into Oblivion itself",{{ref|name=ACitC|{{Cite Book|A Change in the Chimer}}}}. Scholars of the time quickly discerned that their nature remained unchanged, the Dunmer continued to live their lives the same way the Chimer had done before the curse, and as the change in appearance was merely physical it would not warrant any valid hysteria.{{ref|name=ACitC}}
[[File:ON-furnishing-St. Nerevar, Moon-and-Star 02.jpg|thumb|right|A bust depicting Hortator Indoril Nerevar]]
The House Dunmer came to worship the Tribunal as their new gods, following their teachings and supplanting the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Daedric patrons]] of the Chimer, which were relegated to Anticipations of the new Triunes.{{ref|name=VOFD|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} The nomadic Ashlander Tribes, on the other hand, decried the Tribunal as false gods and continued the Chimer traditions, especially those of Daedra worship.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=VAM|{{Cite Book|Vivec and Mephala}}}} As a result, the Ashlanders would subsequently spend over three millennia ostracized from House Dunmer society and driven to the margins of Morrowind.{{ref|name=NFH|{{Cite Book|Notes from Huleeya|ns_base=Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=AshCult|[[MW:Generic Dialogue A#Ashlander Culture|Generic dialogue]] regarding the topic of ''Ashlander Culture'' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=ShaaliK|[[Online:Shaali Kulun|Shaali Kulun]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=FTirinaat|[[Online:Farseer Tirinaat|Farseer Tirinaat]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

For thousands of years, the divine Tribunal used their power and leadership to protect the Dunmer from foreign invasion and, later, from [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]], their ancient archenemy and the devil incarnate for the Dunmer (for he, too, had stolen divine power).{{ref|name=TBORM}}

The Dunmer of Morrowind came into conflict with the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] over the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] complex that would one day be the city of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] during [[Lore:Hestra|Empress Hestra]]'s reign. The conflict occurred during or prior to the eleventh century of the First Era. This 'War for Silyanorn' included the naval Battle of [[Lore:Tear|Tear]] Harbor, wherein the [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] fleet was defeated by the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial fleet]] offshore of the House Dres capital city.{{ref|name=Callidus|[[Online:Strillian Callidus|Strillian Callidus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} Though the Imperials claimed the Indoril flagship after the battle, the war's outcome appears to have been in the Dunmer's favor, as Stormhold was under Dunmer rule as late as {{Year|2E 572}}.{{ref|name=KTBTVP1|{{Cite Book|Keshu: Travels Beyond the Village, Part 1}}}}{{ref|name=KTBTVP2|{{Cite Book|Keshu: Travels Beyond the Village, Part 2}}}}{{ref|name=SCOS|{{Cite Book|Stormhold, City of Shadowfen}}}}{{ref|name=SFLS|[[Online:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Centuries later, the Dunmer butted heads with the [[Lore:Second Empire|Reman Empire]]. Seeking to conquer the land of the Dark Elves, [[Lore:Reman II|Reman II]] initiated an invasion of Morrowind in {{Year|1E 2840}}.{{ref|name=R2TLOA|{{Cite Book|Reman II: The Limits of Ambition}}}} For the next eighty years, war raged between the Empire and Morrowind as the former attempted to subdue the latter. Even after Reman II's death at Dunmeri hands, his successors [[Lore:Brazollus Dor|Brazollus Dor]] and [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]] continued the war.{{ref|name=R2TLOA}} By the final year of the First Era in {{Year|1E 2920}}, both sides were nearing exhaustion. After a disastrous Imperial defeat at [[Lore:Bodrum|Bodrum]], and the Dunmer's catastrophic defeat at [[Lore:Ald Marak|Ald Marak]], both sides entered peace talks.{{ref|name=2920LYOTFE|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}} The assassination of Reman III and his heir by the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] brought the war to an abrupt end. Reman III's successor, Potentate [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]], signed the Cervant Truce with the Dunmer,{{ref|{{Cite Book|High King Emeric Answers Your Questions}}}} ending the Imperial invasion with a de facto victory for Morrowind and making it the only nation of Tamriel to never be controlled by the Second Empire.{{ref|name=2920LYOTFE}}{{ref|name=PGE1-P|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Prologue}}}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg|thumb|right|King Jorunn alongside Vicecanon Heita-Meen and Grandmaster Tanval Indoril]]
The Dunmer entered the Second Era as the only nation of Tamriel outside of the influence of the Second Empire under the Akaviri Potentate.{{ref|name=PGE1-P}} In contrast with the lengthy Four Score War, the opening centuries of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] were relatively uneventful. In {{Year|2E 324}}, Potentate Versidue-Shaie was assassinated in [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]] by the Morag Tong, who scrawled "MORAG TONG" upon the walls of the Potentate's residence in his own blood.{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}}{{ref|name=FAD|{{Cite Book|Fire and Darkness}}}}{{ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Darkness}}}} The assassination led to the instantaneous and unanimous outlawing of the Morag Tong in all corners of Tamriel, with the exception of its home province of Morrowind. This notoriety is believed by most scholars to have resulted in the splintering of the Tong and the creation of the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]].{{ref|name=FAD}} Despite the infamy they gained after Versidue-Shaie's assassination, the Morag Tong struck again in {{Year|2E 430}} and killed his successor [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]].{{Ref|name=ESOAshur|[[Online:Ashur|Ashur]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} and what was believed to be all of his heirs on the same night (though one, Proconsul [[Lore:Beloren-Kaie|Beloren-Kaie]], survived and was later slain by Morag Tong assassin Methas Daram).{{Ref|name=ESOProconsul|[[Online:Proconsul Beloren-Kaie|Proconsul Beloren-Kaie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{Ref|name=ESOAshur}} The Potentate's death at the hands of the Tong promptly resulted in the final disintegration and collapse of the Second Empire, and the beginning of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{ref|name=TBOD}}{{Ref|name=PGE3Eras|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}}

After the coronation of [[Lore:Svartr|High King Svartr]] of Western Skyrim in {{Year|2E 431}}, a delegation from Lord Vivec presented the instrument 'Jarlsbane' to the High King. After being declared "grossly untunable and overly complicated" by the court musicians, it sat in [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]'s [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]] as a curiosity for many years. It was rediscovered over a century later by Master Bard Endroni Selvilo of House Redoran shortly before {{Year|2E 582}}. Nevertheless, the instrument remained in the city, relocated to the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]].{{ref|name=J|{{Cite Book|Jarlsbane}}}}

In {{Year|2E 559}}, a dispute over a House Hlaalu trading post in contested territory erupted into an armed conflict known as the [[Lore:War of Two Houses|War of Two houses]]. The so-called "war" consisted of only one battle, fought by House Dres mercenaries and Hlaalu guards at the trading post. Despite the Dres outnumbering the Hlaalu five-to-one, the conflict ended with a decisive House Hlaalu victory when the leader of the Hlaalu guards, Purilla Falen, defended a portal that allowed a full cohort of Hlaalu battle-merchants to swarm the Dres mercenaries.{{ref|name=WTH|{{Cite Book|War of Two Houses}}}} Prior to the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]], the city of Davon's Watch was sacked by marauding Nords under the leadership of Thane [[Lore:Unnvald Ironhand|Unnvald Ironhand]], who was renowned among the Nords of Eastern Skyrim for his raids into Morrowind.{{ref|name=Hallfrida|[[Online:Hallfrida|Hallfrida]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At the same time, the Argonian hero-to-be [[Lore:Keshu the Black Fin|Keshu]] and her [[Lore:Black Fin Legion|Legion]] waged "war" against House Dres by raiding some of the slave caravans headed for Morrowind for a number of seasons.{{ref|name=Keshu|{{Cite Book|Keshu}}}}
[[File:ON-place-Vivec's Antlers Land-Coral.jpg|The Kamal invaders met their watery end among the coral of Vivec's Antlers.|thumb|right]]
The Dunmer were key players in the [[Lore:Liberation War|Liberation War]], better remembered as the Second Akaviri Invasion. In {{Year|2E 572}}, a strong [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] force led by [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] besieged [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] in Eastern [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], killing Queen [[Lore:Mabjaarn Flame-Hair|Mabjaarn Flame-Hair]] and Princess [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]]. Leaving the ruined city behind, the eastern army continued on their way to [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] but found it heavily fortified by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]], who were led by [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] and [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth Ash-King]], the latter of whom was called back to Nirn from Sovngarde by the Greybeards.{{ref|name=JtSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}}{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}

The invaders decided to bypass the Nordic city and entered Morrowind instead. Led by Almalexia, the Dunmer army fought hard, but was forced to retreat into Stonefalls (although it is rumored that Mother Morrowind and the Skald-Prince collaborated with one another to set this up despite their races' ancient enmity).{{ref|name=AtS|{{Cite Book|Against the Snakes}}}} With their backs towards the Inner Sea, the Akaviri fought viciously against the combined Nord-Dunmer force, but with the rest of the Akaviri fleet on the horizon it seemed that the Tamrielics would be overrun and slaughtered.{{ref|name=AtS}}{{ref|name=UA|{{Cite Book|Unexpected Allies}}}}

While the Nords and Dunmer fought the Akaviri to the north, a group of slaves led by [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]] took the city of Stormhold from their captors. Stormhold was a prominent hub for the slave trade at the time,{{ref|name=SFLS}}{{ref|name=SCOS}} and the Dunmer's primary foothold in Black Marsh.{{ref|name=ESOKjarc}} Heita-Meen had received a vision from the Hist showing the fate of the Nord and Dark Elven armies if their losing battle continued unabated. Thus, she rallied the Shellbacks, and marched into Stonefalls to join the Tamrielic offensive. The Keshu's Black Fin legion likewise answered Jorunn Skald-King's request for aid and joined the battle.{{ref|name=Keshu}} Some of the Dark Elves saw what they perceived as an army of angry slaves approaching, and turned their blades against them at first.{{ref|name=AtS}} However, the Argonians' intent was soon realized, and together, with the assistance of a great tidal wave summoned by Vivec,{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=Brela|[[ON:Priestess Brela|Priestess Brela]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the three races claimed victory against the invaders and drove them into the sea, where they drowned by the thousands.{{ref|name=TSAI}} Thus, the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was formed out of the Kingdom of Eastern Skyrim, the Argonian tribes of Shadowfen and Thornmarsh, and the Dunmeri Great Houses Redoran, Indoril, Hlaalu, and Dres.

In gratitude for their timely assistance, the Pact-loyal Houses granted the Argonians freedom from slavery under the terms of the Pact, and nominally outlawed slavery altogether.{{ref|name=PP:C|{{Cite Book|Pact Pamphlet: Congratulations!}}}}{{ref|name=UA}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}} House Telvanni refused to join the Pact, and as a result freedom was not guaranteed for outlanders in Telvanni territory, as the Ebonheart Pact's jurisdiction did not extend there. House Dres continued to keep non-Argonian slaves after the signing of the Pact, though they dismissed accusations of doing so as 'slander and calumny', and did not 'officially' condone the perpetuation of slavery within the areas they administrated. Outwardly, the Dres nominally remained loyal to the alliance and all it stood for.{{ref|name=PP:C}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=ZOSCorrespondence|[[General:ZOS Correspondence|ZOS Correspondence]]}}

[[File:ON-icon-Ebonheart Pact.png|The sigil of the Ebonheart Pact, which Redoran, Indoril, Dres, and Hlaalu joined|thumb|left]]
Though they shared a common bond, the Ebonheart Pact was a fragile alliance. The Argonians did not forget the centuries of slavery they endured beneath the heels of the Dark Elves. The abolition of Argonian slavery did not result in the immediate cessation of all slaver activity, and it would take a year for most Dark Elven holdings to comply with the Tribunal's order. When Keshu the Black Fin and her Black Fin Legion toured Morrowind as ambassadors for the Argonian people in the early days of the Alliance, they were met with a good deal of fear and hatred. The local Dunmer were uncomfortable with the prospect of a heavily armed contingent of Argonian soldiers approaching their village. They were turned away in some towns, and attacked by those with sizable militias. Not every Dark Elf would give the Saxhleel ambassadors the cold shoulder. Having heard of their efforts at the Battle of Vivec's Antlers, some were very welcoming and appreciative to their southern neighbors. Eventually, the people of Morrowind would grow more accustomed to the presence of free Argonians.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Black Fin: Foreign Adventures, Part 2}}}}{{ref|name=AAU}}

[[File:ON-npc-Vicecanon Servyna.jpg|Vicecanon Servyna of Stormhold|thumb|right]]
Dunmeri heroes of the Liberation War include Grandmaster {{Lore Link|Tanval Indoril}}, who fought back-to-back alongside {{Lore Link|Holgunn One-Eye}} when their squads were slaughtered.{{ref|[[Online:Frikkhild|Frikkhild]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Dunmer used more wood than bone in their weapons and armor when the Pact was formed. Some bone molders learned the carpenter's trade to stay in business during this time.{{ref|[[ON:Byla Drethan|Byla Drethan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In Stormhold, conditions changed after the Pact was formed and slavery was outlawed. Slavers who operated out of Stormhold were prosecuted, sentenced to hang in cages from the city's Ayleid arches for a year, then exiled from Black Marsh.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Black Marsh}}}} Shadowfen was returned to Argonian hands,{{ref|name=ESOPideelus|[[ON:Aide Pideelus|Aide Pideelus]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and Stormhold was outfitted with three vicecanons, one from each of the Pact's races. All three were heroes of the Akaviri War. Among them was Vicecanon Heita-Meen, who received that fateful vision from the Hist during the war, as well as Vicecanon Servyna of the Dunmer and Hrondar of Skyrim.{{ref|[[ON:Aide Diina|Aide Diina]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

During the Alliance War, a string of murders perpetrated by the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#First Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] occurred within Stormhold. Tensions were high between the Dark Elf and Argonian populations of the city. Some Dunmer felt the Argonians resented them for their history of slavery, while others outright loathed the Argonians, holding onto the opinion that they were "lesser".{{ref|name=ESOTiiril}} Some of the Dark Elves felt the Pact was a mistake.{{ref|name=ESOGethis|[[ON:Gethis Omobar|Gethis Omobar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Three Tender Souls|Three Tender Souls]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESODendras|[[ON:Dendras Indalor|Dendras Indalor]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Three Tender Souls|Three Tender Souls]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} All of the victims were Dark Elves, so some mer assumed the Argonians may have been responsible.{{ref|name=ESOTiiril|[[ON:Tiiril Alor|Tiiril Alor]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Three Tender Souls|Three Tender Souls]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some even entertained the idea that the [[Lore:Shadowscales|Shadowscales]] were involved,{{ref|name=ESODendras}}{{ref|name=ESOGethis}} but all was put to rest when the vicecanons discovered the Dominion's presence in the ruins of nearby [[Lore:Silyanorn|Silyanorn]].{{ref|Events of [[Online:Getting to the Truth|Getting to the Truth]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

After Dagoth Ur awakened in {{Year|2E 882}}, the Tribunal had to devote their full attention to him, allowing for the [[Lore:Armistice|Treaty of the Armistice]] with [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s Third Empire that made Morrowind an autonomous province.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=OM}}{{ref|name=ASHOM}}{{ref|name=PTDDU|{{Cite Book|Plan to Defeat Dagoth Ur}}}} Many Dunmer were shocked and angered by the signing of the Armistice, which they viewed as a betrayal on behalf of the Tribunal.{{ref|name=Orvas|[[Morrowind:Orvas Dren|Orvas Dren]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

====The Armistice====
Morrowind's military power was severely limited by the Armistice. Tiber Septim demanded that the Tribunal turn over the Numidium to the Empire, and to allow the Imperial Legion a sizable presence in the province.{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} In exchange, Morrowind was permitted to keep its autonomy, maintaining the power of the Tribunal Temple and the Great Houses,{{ref|name=OM}} while being supervised by Imperial representatives in the capital, [[Lore:Almalexia (city)|Almalexia City]], and in each of the six newly-created administrative districts.{{ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} Morrowind was to be governed under Imperial Law, with exceptions being made for Great House Law. Local House guards were permissible, and the Empire generally tried to stay out of Tribunal affairs. The province was to be ruled by a figurehead King, who was chosen directly by the Emperor.{{ref|name=ASHOM}} Additionally, and perhaps most importantly in the minds of Dunmer elites, the practice of slavery (and persecution of abolitionists) in Morrowind was protected by the Empire.{{ref|name=BB|{{Cite Book|Brown Book of 3E 426}}}} Dunmer religious practices were similarly protected.{{ref|name=OM}}

The Armistice was supported most enthusiastically by [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]], with [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]] grudgingly following Vivec's lead, while [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] did so only with the strong provisions protecting slavery.{{ref|name=OM}} [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] remained neutral and isolationist throughout the proceedings, until the support of Hlaalu and Redoran was officially established, when they joined in on the support of the treaty.{{ref|name=OM}} [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] bowed to the will of the Tribunal, but remained hostile to the Empire; in fact, many Indoril nobles chose ritual suicide rather than to rule an occupied nation.{{ref|name=OM}} At this time, House Hlaalu seized the opportunity to settle old scores with Houses Indoril and Redoran, using its new alliance with the Empire to reclaim lost lands and to place Hlaalu nobles in positions of power within the new Imperial administration. House Indoril remained powerful in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], Morrowind's capital.

Following the Armistice, the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] became a Temple preserve with some small settlements maintained by the Great Houses as well as scattered [[Morrowind:Ashlanders|Ashlander]] tribes. However, the island was not opened to large-scale settlement by civilians or exploitation by the East Empire Company until {{Year|3E 414}}.{{ref|name=ASHOM}}

====Third Era Diasporas from Morrowind====
Throughout the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], several waves of Dunmer refugees have left Morrowind. The earliest example was roughly half a century from {{Year|3E 433}}. These immigrants left for Cyrodiil to escape the Tribunal Temple's doctrines and the province's rigid society. They mainly settled in [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]], where they greatly shaped its culture. One of these immigrants was [[Lore:Andel Indarys|Andel Indarys]] of House Hlaalu who became Count Cheydinhal in a noticeably short time. A schism occurred between the local [[Lore:Nibenese|Nibenese]] and the Dunmer, especially with the suspicion surrounding Count Indarys' ascension.{{Ref|name=GTC|{{Cite Book|Guide to Cheydinhal}}}}{{Ref|name=OBMach|[[Oblivion:Mach-Na|Mach-Na]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Another exodus took place later in the era. These Dunmer went to [[Lore:Winterhold (city)|Winterhold]] and brought about a renaissance of power and influence. Winterhold's culture became enriched with new ideas from the east and its old mercantile spirit had been revived. The [[Lore:Ysmir Collective|Ysmir Collective]] became a cornerstone of academic life at the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] when books were recovered from destruction in Morrowind.{{Ref|name=PGE3SR|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} Year after year, more Dunmer traveled to the College of Winterhold and so many elves called the city home. After the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], however, the Nords held a growing distrust in magic that affected their relationship with the Dunmer and the College mages. Many of them left so they would not have to endure their resentment.{{Ref|name=SRBrelyna|[[Skyrim:Brelyna Maryon|Brelyna Maryon]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
In the first few years of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], as Vivec no longer held [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]] in place, it began its descent into the city of [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec]] once more. The [[Lore:Ingenium|Ingenium]], a magical machine powered by souls, was created to hold the moon in place, used to create a [[Lore:Portals|rift]] to the [[Lore:Fields of Regret|Fields of Regret]] from where it leeched the energy used to hold the moon aloft, an act theorized to have occurred with the approval of the soul hungry [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]].{{ref|name=IC|Pages 257-258 of [[Books:The Infernal City|''The Infernal City'']]}} A Dunmer named [[Books:The Infernal City/Sul|Sul]] destroyed the Ingenium in an attempt to save his lover from being used to power it.{{ref|name=TIC}} Consequentially, Baar Dau hit Vivec City with the full strength of its original descent, destroying Vivec City and triggering a series of natural disasters that devastated Vvardenfell and the province as a whole in what is now called the Red Year.{{ref|name=TIC}}

In the aftermath, the Argonian armies of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] invaded the south and east of the land, but were repelled by the armies of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]].{{ref|name=TIC}}{{ref|name=LTJ|{{Cite Book|Lymdrenn Tenvanni's Journal}}}}{{ref|name=Adril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} As a result of the calamities, many Dunmer have fled to other places around Tamriel, notably [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and the small island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], which had become a territory of the sovereign nation of Morrowind in {{Year|4E 16}}.{{ref|name=TIC}} House Redoran became the eminent power in Morrowind for their efforts to protect the province, and the capital was moved accordingly to the ancestral Redoran seat of [[Lore:Blacklight|Blacklight]].{{ref|name=Adril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} While the precise date or circumstances are unknown, Morrowind had ceased to be considered a territory of the Third Empire by {{Year|4E 48}}.{{ref|name=Adril}}{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|109}}{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|16}}

==Culture==
Dark Elven culture is divided between the nomadic Ashlanders and the settled Great Houses.

Some Dark Elves keep [[Lore:Nix-Hound|nix-hounds]] as pets. While usually vicious predators, nixes are well-known to be affectionate, attentive, and faithful pets that will follow their owners around. They are also commonly used as trackers, watch-hounds, and netch-deflators.{{Ref|name=Creation|[[Skyrim:Nix-Hound|Nix-Hound]] Creation Club add-on in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=Manual|{{Cite Book|Nix-Hounds: A Manual for New Owners}}}}{{Ref|[[ON:Bal Foyen Nix-Hound|Bal Foyen Nix-Hound]] [[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Pets#Bal Foyen Nix-Hound|Crown Store Showcase]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Nix-Hounds were used as war dogs in the House Dagoth chap'thil.{{Ref|name=FSoKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} Ashlanders use nix-hound parts in a variety of their tribal customs.{{Ref|name=ONSorimNakar|[[Online:Huntmaster Sorim-Nakar#Tarra-Suj Hunt|Huntmaster Sorim-Nakar]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Tarra-Suj Hunt|Tarra-Suj Hunt]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} [[Lore:Pony Guar|Pony guar]] are also popular pets.{{ref|[[Online:Pony Guar (pet)|Pony Guar]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

For these reasons, beggars were considered rare enough that some citizens claimed no beggars could be found within Morrowind as late as the closing years of the Third Era.{{ref|[[MW:Generic Dialogue M#Morrowind|Generic dialogue]] on the topic of '''Morrowind''' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Agriculture===
Living in a province of ashlands and fungal forests, the Dunmer have vastly different crops and livestock than the rest of Tamriel. Farmers in southern Morrowind raise the reptilian [[Lore:Bantam Guar|bantam guar]] for their eggs and meat, similarly to [[Lore:Chicken|chickens]] in other parts of the world. Some Dunmer raise pony guar for their skins and meat.{{ref|name=MFPO|{{Cite Book|Morrowind Fauna, Part One}}}} The Dunmer of Deshaan have taken to herding [[Lore:Shalk|shalks]] for their carapaces, meat, and resin.{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Morrowind}}}} Bloated shalks can be milked to obtain "shalk blue", which is used as a pigment in tattoo ink.{{ref|[[ON:Contraband S#"Shalk Blue" Tattoo Ink|"Shalk Blue" Tattoo Ink]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Netchimen take on the profession of domesticating and herding [[Lore:Netch|netches]]. While feral betties usually keep a harem of several bulls, netchimen often keep the female-male ratio low for faster breeding.{{ref|name=MWFrinnius|[[Morrowind:Frinnius Posuceius|Frinnius Posuceius]]' dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} As netches are dangerous to harvest for even the most experienced netchimen, netch products are relatively scarce in comparison to others.{{ref|name=SD|{{Cite Book|Settling the Debate}}}} [[Lore:Netch Leather|Netch leather]] and [[Lore:Netch Jelly|jelly]] are both products the Dark Elves derive from these gaseous floating creatures.{{ref|[[MW:Netch Leather|Netch Leather]] dropping from [[MW:Netch|netches]] and its uses in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|[[SR:Netch Jelly|Netch Jelly]] dropping from [[SR:Netch|netches]] and its uses in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}{{ref|name=KCAHC|[[Books:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion Characters and Summary #Morrowind|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]]}}

====Beasts of Burden====
The Dark Elves make use of [[Lore:Nix-Ox|nix-oxen]] (fungus eating arthropods) and [[Lore:Guar|guar]] (bipedal reptiles with short arms) when they seek animal labor. Guar are standard mounts and pack animals in Morrowind.{{ref|name=ESOGNG|[[Online:Green Narsis Guar|Green Narsis Guar]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Dwarven Guar|Dwarven Guar]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Guar hide is cured to make clothing and household goods.{{ref|[[MW:Generic Dialogue G|Generic Dialogue G]] about '''Guar Hide''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Nix-oxen were particularly important on Vvardenfell before they were all wiped out by the Ash Blight.{{ref|name=VTAN|[[General:ESO: Morrowind - Vvardenfell Then and Now|Vvardenfell Then and Now]] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} Nix wranglers hunted for wild nix-oxen to sell on the market. Younger nix-oxen were more valuable because their chitin was more durable, making them easier to tame. Nix-oxen were tamed by opening the creature's armored back and tampering with a bundle of nerves beneath the flesh (known as the "drive chamber") to render the nix docile. Wealthier nix farmers could hire tradesmen known as "drenlyns" to tame their nix-oxen for them.{{ref|name=DEDH|{{Cite Book|Dark Elves, Dark Hearts}}}}

After the Ebonheart Pact outlawed slavery of their Nord and Argonian allies, farmers who relied heavily on members of those races for slave labor began to employ nix-oxen in place of slaves. In particular, nix-oxen were used to plow fields. Nix-oxen have a second pair of appendages located on the sides of their head known as pedipalps, which are used by the beasts as a form of protection. Farmers forced their nix-oxen to drag these appendages through the soil to create furrows for crops such as [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]] and [[Lore:Marshmerrow|marshmerrow]]. {{ref|name=APMNO|{{Cite Book|A Petition for the Mighty Nix-Ox}}}}

As the years went by, a nix's pedipalps would erode from being dragged through the dirt. Not only would a creature with eroded pedipalps be useless to the farmer, but it would lose a valuable defense mechanism. Nix-oxen whose pedipalps were too eroded for the beast to work were either butchered for their carapaces or left to starve in the ashen wastes. The lack of proper pedipalps made survival for abandoned nix-oxen virtually impossible. In {{Year|2E 582}}, a concerned House Dunmer farmer drafted a petition in an attempt to pass a law requiring all farmers to retire their nix-oxen after a certain amount of years. The farmer believed that the practice of abandoning nix-oxen who could no longer work was inhumane, and saw such treatment as unfair considering how much of a boon nix-oxen were to Dunmeri farming communities which lacked slaves.{{ref|name=APMNO}}

The Marshmerrow, Salt Rice, and Golden Reed societies of the Dissident Priests all have their own unarmed, unarmored martial arts traditions.{{ref|[[MW:Garothmuk gro-Muzgub|Garothmuk gro-Muzgub]]'s dialogue regarding the topic of '''martial arts''' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Architecture===
The Dunmer are known for some of the most alien architecture in Tamriel. All Dunmer buildings have to be well-suited to deal with the climate of their particular region, which varies greatly across the province. The [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] build bizarre, twisted mushroom towers and dwellings with magic. {{ref|name=GHOM|{{Cite Book|Great Houses of Morrowind}}}} This style was present in Vvardenfell in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second]]{{ref|name=ONFiri|[[Online:Madam Firilanya|Madam Firilanya]]'s correspondence mentions the Telvanni towers}}{{ref|name=ESOMW|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and Third Eras.{{ref|name=Sadrith Mora|[[Morrowind:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} [[Lore:House Redoran|Redoran]], [[Lore:House Indoril|Indoril]], [[Lore:House Dres|Dres]] and [[Lore:House Hlaalu|Hlaalu]] use more conventional methods to build their structures.

The Redoran build organic, crab-like houses. The Redoran town of [[Lore:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]] on Vvardenfell was famous for being built around the shell of a long-dead Emperor Crab.{{ref|name=GHOM}} This style was present in Vvardenfell in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second]]{{ref|name=ESOMW}} and [[Lore:Third Era|Third Eras]].{{ref|name=Ald'ruhnTES3|[[Morrowind:Ald'ruhn|Ald'ruhn]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Hlaalu build simple, cubic stone houses. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second{{ref|name=ESOMW}} and Third Eras.{{ref|name=Balmora|[[Morrowind:Balmora|Balmora]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The style of architecture known as Velothi style is seen in ancestral tombs, ancient towers, and Temple buildings.{{ref|name=MorrowindVelothiBuildings|[[Morrowind:Ancestral Tombs|Ancestral Tombs]], [[Morrowind:Velothi Towers|Velothi Towers]], and [[Morrowind:Temples|Tribunal Temple Buildings]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Basalt|Basalt]] is often considered to be unsuitable for delicate stonework, but early Dunmer are known to have crafted with it regardless.{{ref|[[Online:Bal Foyen Antiquities#Basalt Table-Game Set|Basalt Table-Game Set]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

Responsible Architecture is a style that originated in the First Era and is attributed to the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]].{{ref|name=FALT|[[Online:Fort Arand|Fort Arand]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=VOF}} This style was similar to that seen in various [[Lore:Daedric Ruins|Daedric ruins]] in Morrowind, and it was used by the famed architect Guylaine Marilie to build a prison to contain Daedra at the site where Fort Arand was later constructed.{{ref|name=FALT}} A common architectural style in mainland Morrowind features ornate stone buildings with peaked, shingled roofs. It was common in mainland Morrowind in the Second Era and even extended into Cyrodiil. The city of Mournhold retained the style in the Third Era.{{ref|name=MournholdinTES3|[[Tribunal:Mournhold|Mournhold]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}{{ref|name=ON}}

Unlike their settled brethren, the Ashlanders are nomadic, and live in migrating encampments of large tents made of netch hide.

==Society==
[[File:MW-concept-11.jpg|thumb|right|Sketch of an Indoril guard]]
The Dunmer have been defined by their environment. [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] looms as large in their collective thought as it does on the horizon.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} Its ash and lava sculpted the attitudes of Vvardenfell's residents.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} One does not have to travel far outside of many cities to find a giant [[Lore:Foyada|Foyada]] cutting through the land like a scar. Native-born Dunmer tended to look down on "outlanders", which include both other races and Dunmer born outside of Morrowind, though the intensity of this ethnocentrism varies.{{ref|name=ASHOM}} For thousands of years under the Tribunal, Dunmer society was structured much like the Chimer society had been: domestic Great Houses governed by Ruling Councils competed against each other for power and territory, while nomadic groups eschewed relative modernity for ancient tribal practices.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=ASHOM}}{{ref|name=GHOM}}

Houses tend to rise and fall; many have faded to extinction over the years or sprouted up to challenge others.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=GHOM}}{{ref|name=TBOMB|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Molag Beran}}}} At the close of the Third Era, the five most important houses were Hlaalu, Redoran, Telvanni, Dres and Indoril, each which had existed for thousands of years, since at least the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]].{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=GHOM}} While the details are limited, it is known that each of the Houses survived the cataclysms following the fall of the Tribunal Temple - with the exception of House Hlaalu, which lost its position to the previously-unknown House Sadras. In the most recent two centuries, House Redoran has become the most powerful of the Great Houses, taking the mantle the Hlaalu had held prior, and the Indoril before them.{{ref|name=DBMorvayn|[[Skyrim:Lleril Morvayn|Lleril Morvayn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} Historically, Indoril has been so heavily involved with the Temple itself that it is hard to distinguish the two.{{ref|name=OM}} Following the collapse of the Tribunal Temple, House Indoril and the New Temple became regarded as one and the same.{{ref|name=TheRec|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}}

In the Ashlands, native tribes ruled without laws or government and lived strictly by codes, rituals, and ancient traditions usually dictated by a wise woman or seer and implemented by a clan leader. They lived in the deserts and plains of the [[Lore:Ashlands|Ashlands]] and [[Lore:Grazelands|Grazelands]].
:''→ See also: '''[[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlanders]]'''''

Prior to the Imperial occupation, Morrowind was an agrarian aristocracy: mostly free farmers, herders, and fishermen ruled by great houses and their noble councils. Since the Third Empire's occupation, especially on Vvardenfell, the Dunmer developed a mercantile economy on the model of the Empire, ruled by the Emperor, law, and legions, but driven by trade in crafts and goods.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue M#Morrowind Lore|generic dialogue]] regarding the topic of '''Morrowind Lore''' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

====Practices and Norms====
The peaceful status quo for Dunmer was considered quite brutal by westerners, even when led by living gods.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=PGE3M}} Political machinations are constant.{{ref|name=ASHOM}} Assassinations are common, with the legally established [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] providing services.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=OM}} A huge amount of black market activity thrives along Morrowind's extensive coastlines. [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissidents]] to the Temple and other subversives over the years have been hunted and persecuted.{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}}

Slavery used to be commonplace.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} Mostly [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] or Argonian slaves were trafficked, although many men and mer were also enslaved.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} Goblins were also seen as a significant slave "resource".{{ref|name=ONAldryn|[[Online:Aldryn Dres#Dialogue|Aldryn Dres]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Third Empire of Tiber Septim had banned slavery, but Dunmer were allowed to continue the practice in their entrance to the Empire as part of the Armistice, defining it as one of their own "sacred and traditional laws". Despite the claim that owning slaves was their right, many Dunmer still found the practice abhorrent and immoral,{{ref|name=OTT|{{Cite Book|Omaren Trial Transcript}}}} and abolitionist groups continued to crop up within Dunmeri society.{{Ref|name=MWGNA|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue A|Generic Dialogue]] on the topic of "Abolitionists" from ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} By the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|King Helseth]] officially abolished slavery.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}

[[Lore:Necromancy|Necromancy]] is practiced openly by some Dunmer, though never with Dunmer corpses. The corpses of animals, insects and other races are considered fair game for necromancers.{{ref|name=AATD}}

==Religion==
Historically, the Dunmer have resisted worshipping the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] of the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]], whom they viewed as heretical apostasies. Occasionally, adherents of the Divines were punished severely.{{ref|name=OE:MS|{{Cite Book|Ordinator Edict: Mandate Sixteen}}}}

Following ALMSIVI's fall, the Dunmer were swift in re-establishing the traditions of ancestor and Daedra worship that Ashlanders had kept alive as the New Temple.{{ref|name=TheRec}}{{ref|name=TA}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s conversation with the [[Morrowind:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] after [[Morrowind:The Citadels of the Sixth House|of Dagoth Ur's defeat]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Dunmeri religious iconography has made use of the triangle and the pyramid throughout history, from the Chimer's worship of the Good Daedra,{{ref|Appearance of the [[Online:Bal Foyen Antiquities#Tri-Angled Truth Altar|Tri-Angled Truth Altar]] and its related Antiquity Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} to the Living Gods of the Dunmeri Tribunal.{{ref|Appearance of the pyramid-shaped monoliths in the cloisters of the [[Online:Tribunal Temple|Mournhold Temple]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Three is a lucky number associated with the Tribunal. Some objects are designed to do things in threes, such as muffin pans that bake only three muffins.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband G#Gorapple Muffin Pan|Gorapple Muffin Pan]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Ancestor Worship===
[[File:SR-interior-Temple Ancestral Tomb.jpg|An ancestral tomb|thumb|right]]
Ancestor worship is a hallmark of Dunmer culture. They believe the spirits of the dead live on in the mortal realm. The spirits know and can affect the future, speak with other spirits, and perform great feats of magic. As such, the Dunmer honor their deceased ancestors, giving them gifts and petitioning them for aid and protection.{{ref|name=MWGenAncestors|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue A#Ancestors|Generic dialogue]] regarding the topic of '''Ancestors''' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Recently deceased spirits have the freshest knowledge of their living relatives. Though they are the weakest of the ancestor spirits, they are the quickest to guide and protect their kin when called upon. The spirits of heroes and wise women are strong. The Dunmer pass down these ancestors' stories so they don't forget their wisdom. The worst of the ancestor spirits are evil and seek to do harm. These spirits are called ghosts, devils, and daemons.{{ref|name=MWGenAncestors}} Ghosts in particular are mad spirits: those who are forced to remain on the mortal plane for one reason or another. Spirits who are bound to the world willingly are less likely to go mad, but for those who do not consent to being bound, it is inevitable.{{ref|name=AATD}} The Dark Elves ask their kinder ancestors to protect them from malevolent spirits. Unlike most other races, the Dunmer count the Daedra among their ancestors. The Daedra are the oldest and greatest of the ancestor spirits. Though powerful, they are dangerous and difficult to understand.{{ref|name=MWGenAncestors}}

House Dunmer have family shrines in their homes to venerate their ancestors. In poorer homes, the shrine may consist of a hearth or alcove where family relics are displayed, but wealthy House Dunmer set aside an entire room for their ancestors to use. The shrine is referred to as the "Waiting Door", and represents the door to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]. When a family member dies, their remains are interred in ash pits within ancestral tombs. Small bones (such as finger bones) may be placed in the family shrine.{{ref|name=AATD}} Traditionally, smaller communities maintain a hard-and-fast rule against non-Dark Elves being interred within family crypts. Larger cities like Mournhold or Ebonheart are more open-minded, but even in those cosmopolitan communities it's unlikely to find more than a handful of foreign names on the plaques beside a tomb.{{ref|name=LA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}}

The Dark Elves' murky reputation among the human races is due in part to the practices and spirit magic associated with ancestor worship; they abhor the notion of summoning one's ancestors from beyond the veil. The Dunmer summon dead ancestors to consult their wisdom and ask them to protect their tombs, but they don't consider this necromancy. Necromancy outside of acceptable clan rituals is taboo. Other elves consider the Dunmeri practice of displaying the bones of deceased relatives in ash pits repugnant.{{ref|name=AATD}}

===Tribunal Temple===
[[File:MW-shrine-Tribunal.jpg|thumb|left|Shrines dedicated to ALMSIVI]]
{{Lore:Tribunal Temple}}

The Great Houses generally followed the established Tribunal Temple, which preached faith in the Tribunal, respect for the "Good Daedra" who recognized the Tribunal, and veneration of ancestors.{{ref|name=AATD}} There was some practicality to this: ancestral remains are used to power [[Lore:Ghostfence|ghost fences]] to ward off spirits, notably the one created by the Tribunal at the closing of the Second Era that surrounded Red Mountain for hundreds of years to keep Dagoth Ur and his minions at bay.{{ref|name=PGE1M}}{{ref|name=AATD}}{{ref|name=ASHOM}}

The Temple's priestly writings are called the "Hierographa". In the late Third Era, Archcanon Tholer Saryoni collected Vivec's most famous sermons and popular explanations of his Gospels. He used this information to write ''[[Lore:Saryoni's Sermons|Saryoni's Sermons]]'', which went on to become the best selling of the Temple's annotated texts; by {{Year|3E 427}}, a copy of ''Saryoni's Sermons'' could be found in most every Dunmeri household. Many editions were printed, the most elaborate of which were handsomely illuminated with Vivec's quotations from the Gospels for days, seasons, and festivals.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Saryoni's Sermons|ns_base=Morrowind}}}}

* According to [[General:Mark Bullock|Mark Bullock]], [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]], [[General:Ken Rolston|Ken Rolston]] and [[General:Todd Howard|Todd Howard]], the Dunmer and Morrowind were greatly inspired by the cultures and settings of [[wikipedia:Egypt|Egypt]], [[wikipedia:Japan|Japan]], ''[[wikipedia:Conan the Barbarian (disambiguation)|Conan the Barbarian]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Dune (franchise)|Dune]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Gladiator (2000 film)|Gladiator]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Labyrinth (1986 film)|Labyrinth]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Legend (1985 film)|Legend]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Mad max|Mad Max]]'', ''[[wikipedia:RuneQuest|RuneQuest]]'', ''[[wikipedia:Star Wars|Star Wars]]'', ''[[wikipedia:The Dark Crystal|The Dark Crystal]]'' and the works of [[wikipedia:Hayao Miyazaki|Hayao Miyazaki]].<ref>{{Cite Web|url=https://www.imperial-library.info/content/interviews-MW-team Interview With Morrowind's Developers}}</ref><ref>{{Cite Web|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071019071012/http://planetelderscrolls.gamespy.com/View.php?view=Articles.Detail&id=27 Development Team chat}}</ref><ref>{{Cite Web|url=https://www.polygon.com/2019/3/27/18281082/elder-scrolls-morrowind-oral-history-bethesda|title=Morrowind: An oral history|last=Kane|first=Alex|work=[[Polygon (website)|Polygon]]|date=March 27, 2019|access-date=January 1, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite Web|url=https://www.eurogamer.net/the-hidden-secrets-of-the-elder-scrolls-morrowind|title=The hidden secrets of The Elder Scrolls 3: Morrowind|first=Cian|work=[[Eurogamer|Eurogamer]]|date=December 3, 2019}}</ref> Additionally, a large proportion of names of Ashlanders, Egg Mines, and Daedric shrines are modeled on ancient [[wikipedia:Assyria|Assyrian]] and [[wikipedia:Babylonia|Babylonian]] names from the third millenium to the first millenium BCE.{{Ref|name=MWEE|[[Morrowind:Easter Eggs#Ashlander names|Easter Eggs]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
*{{LNote|nb1|^|A single instance of the Dark Elves being referred to as '''Drow''' appears in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'', but this was dropped completely thereafter.{{ref|name=Drow|[[Arena:Artifact_Quests#Ebony_Mail|''Ebony Mail'']] quest in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} It is presumably an artifact from an earlier iteration of the game, when it featured more [[wikipedia:Drow|material derived]] from ''[[wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons|Dungeons & Dragons]]''.}}
