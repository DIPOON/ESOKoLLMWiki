[[Lore:Telenger the Artificer|Telenger the Artificer]] was a celebrated genius, writer, explorer, and scholar on topics of anthropology and [[Lore:Magic|magic]],{{ref|name=TA}} as well as an archmage,{{Ref|name=Neetra}} a canonreeve, a powerful [[Lore:Conjuration|conjurer]] and magical engineer.{{ref|name=TTIEGTTSI}} He was in service to both the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]],{{ref|name=TA}} and the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]].{{ref|name=TTIEGTTSI}} He was a favored figure of the Mages Guild, a well-known personality in [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]], and a researcher who traveled widely across the world.{{ref|name=MTCACA}} He was a man of considerable repute, regarded by some of his compatriots as a national treasure, an Archmage and a living connection to the past.{{ref|name=Oinara|[[ON:Oinara|Oinara]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Mages Guild Career===
{{TOCleft}}[[File:ON-place-Mages Guild (Skywatch).jpg|thumb|right|Mages Guild chapter in Skywatch]]
Little is known of Telenger's early life, though by {{Year|2E 582}} he was regarded as an old and accomplished mage.{{Ref|name=Andewen}} Over the years he had taken on numerous apprentices, both former and current, and held a high position within the Mages Guild.{{Ref|name=TA}} He was also known to have a long-standing acquaintance with the Guild's founder, [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]. While their interactions were not always entirely respectful, they nevertheless regarded one another as friends.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=VG|[[ON:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They worked together to ensure the Guild's future success.{{ref|name=RNC|[[ON:Rudrasa|Rudrasa]], [[ON:Nemarc|Nemarc]], and [[ON:Curinure|Curinure]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He held a leadership position in the chapter of the Guild at [[Lore:Skywatch|Skywatch]],{{Ref|name=Neetra}} the city he called home.{{Ref|name=GC}}

Telenger once led students on an expedition to [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]] to study [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruins. The venture ended poorly for most, though a few escaped unscathed. His students were forbidden from discussing the incident.{{ref|name=Raelys|[[ON:Raelys|Raelys]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|2E 581}}, he attended a debutante ball in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], where he drew the attention of a cousin of Elydrina Nathriin of [[Lore:Shad Astula|Shad Astula]], who appeared to take a romantic interest in him, though little is known of any outcome.{{ref|name=MTCACA}}

Telenger taught many students over the years. At some point, Merormo, an Altmeri mage, was banished from the College by Telenger due to his continued ill-advised experiments.{{ref|name=DL|{{Cite Book|Dismissal Letter|ns_base=Online}}}} These magical experiments later caused various troubles in Auridon, for which he may have faced severe consequences.{{Ref|name=Meromo|[[ON:Merormo|Merormo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Another of Telenger's former pupils, after years of studying lenses and hues, discovered a method to create red lenses that did not fade over time.{{ref|name=CM|[[Online:Carmine Monocle|Carmine Monocle]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Another member of the Guild, Neetra, once attempted to repair her damaged [[Lore:Dwarven Spider|Dwarven spider]] companion. Telenger forbade the effort, arguing that the Dwemer components required were too valuable to be spent on what he considered a mere pet. Determined to restore the construct regardless, Neetra secretly enlisted the help of an adventurer to gather the necessary parts and repair it despite Telenger's ban.{{ref|name=Neetra|[[ON:Neetra|Neetra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of Telenger's former students, Pircalmo, later left the Guild and stole one of his wands. After falling into danger, he managed to resolve the situation with the help of an adventurer and notes left by his former mentor, eventually becoming the town mage of [[Lore:Willowgrove|Willowgrove]].{{Ref|name=OPER}}{{ref|name=Pircalmo|[[ON:Pircalmo|Pircalmo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=QC|Events of [[ON:Questionable Contract|Questionable Contract]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Telenger was admired by many members of the Guild,{{ref|name=Ghadar|[[ON:Ghadar|Ghadar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Raelys}} who sought his favor,{{Ref|name=Ruucaramil}}{{Ref|name=Candangarilde|[[ON:Candangarilde|Candangarilde]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and opportunity to work with him was met with great excitement,{{ref|name=Aillard|[[ON:Aillard|Aillard]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and considered a rare honor.{{ref|name=SS|[[ON:Spark-Scale|Spark-Scale]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Others found him stubborn, insufferable,{{ref|name=Umbarile}}{{ref|name=Neetra}} and unstable,{{ref|name=Meryon|[[ON:Meryon|Meryon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} yet many were still willing to do almost anything to secure his recommendation.{{Ref|name=Ruucaramil|[[ON:Ruucaramil|Ruucaramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Camaldetuile}} Some accounts suggested that some of those who served the Artificer were prone to dangerous accidents, as many, though not all, sought to impress him and advance in rank, sometimes despite lacking the skill their recommendations claimed. It was said that while Telenger's mastery of archaeology was vast, he could at times be naïve to the ambitions and motives of those in his service.{{ref|name=Fistanawe|[[ON:Fistanawe|Fistanawe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Ejukki|[[ON:Ejukki|Ejukki]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} His word carried great sway within the guild, and his recommendation was considered highly valuable, as many of those he endorsed went on to successful careers, some attaining positions such as Wizard, or even Archmagister, like Astrinque, who had once served as his assistant.{{ref|name=Camaldetuile|[[ON:Camaldetuile|Camaldetuile]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Telenger received letters of recommendation from other guild members, such as Namolelcare of Firsthold, who praised promising students and urged Telenger to accept them for further study under him.{{ref|name=LTT|{{Cite Book|Letter to Telenger|ns_base=Online}}}} Some admirers regarded Telenger as one of the greatest mages of his time, praising his many honors, his travels across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in search of lost lore,{{ref|name=SE}} and even his appearance,{{ref|name=SE}}{{ref|name=Naarcaano|[[ON:Naarcaano|Naarcaano]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=MTCACA}} considering him the most decorated living member of the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=SE|[[ON:Student Endannie|Student Endannie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Many hopeful scholars sought apprenticeship under Telenger. One such candidate was a [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] named Rollin, who was entrusted with several relics to study and write analyses on. However, he lost them after a night of heavy drinking in [[Lore:Rawl'kha|Rawl'kha]]. Although the relics were recovered, it is unclear whether they were returned to Rollin so he could complete his report and present it to Telenger's apprentice, Meldil, or given directly to Meldil, who, upon learning of Rollin's negligence, intended to report the matter to Telenger and end the Bosmer's hopes of apprenticeship.{{ref|name=Meldil}}{{ref|name=Rollin|[[ON:Rollin|Rollin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ANTF|Events of [[ON:A Night to Forget|A Night to Forget]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Dune and the Queen's Return===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''My High Elf companion couldn't quite hide the haughty nature of his upbringing, but his presence was certainly a vast improvement on past escorts. We discussed many highbrow topics, disagreeing on much but never coming to blows: Little wonder Telenger is regarded as a fine diplomat, or "canonreeve."''|Flaccus Terentius{{ref|name=TTIEGTTSI}}}}
[[File:ON-concept-Queen Ayrenn.jpg|thumb|left|An Altmeri print celebrating Ayrenn's return]][[File:ON-place-Dune.jpg|thumb|right|Dune]]
At some point between {{Year|2E 578}} and 2E 581, Telenger served as an envoy of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] in [[Lore:Dune|Dune]]. On one occasion, he dined with Moon-Bishop [[Lore:Hunal|Hunal]] and the [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] scholar [[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]]. Though somewhat haughty and prone to lofty scholarly discussions, he was regarded as a capable diplomat and canonreeve, and he later arranged passage for Flaccus aboard his Swan Ship from [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]] to [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]] in northern Auridon.{{ref|name=TTIEGTT}}{{ref|name=TTIEGTTSI}}

During the voyage he demonstrated his magical ability by summoning [[Lore:Storm Atronach|storm]] and [[Lore:Flesh Atronach|flesh]] [[Lore:Atronach|atronachs]], and in Firsthold he shared gossip about Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]]'s adventures before her return to the Isles. While there, during the celebrations of Queen's return to the Summerset Isles Telenger introduced Flaccus to Falarel, a former jester of [[Lore:Rilis XII|Rilis XII]] and a bard of some renown, who later led them into the old castle vaults claiming to have encountered the [[Lore:Ghost|ghost]] of his former master. The encounter proved disastrous when the spirit of Rilis XII appeared, leading to chaos within the tombs. In the aftermath Flaccus was arrested for trespass, and later deported to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]]. Little is known about how the incident affected Telenger, as he appeared to have retained an important position in Summerset afterward, though his whereabouts were unknown to Flaccus.{{ref|name=TTIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}}{{ref|name=TTIEGTTSI|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Summerset Isles}}}}

===Skywatch===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Telenger the Artificer calls our fair city home! He runs a small shop and exhibition space for the Mages Guild on the west side of town. You can see relics from his travels, magical displays—all kinds of things!''|Guide Culast{{Ref|name=GC}}}}
[[File:ON-place-Telenger's Emporium 03.jpg|thumb|left|Telenger's Emporium]][[File:ON-place-Skywatch Sanctuary.jpg|thumb|right|Skywatch Sanctuary,a ruin in Skywatch, once studied by Telenger before later being used as a hideout by Azandar al-Cybiades]]

Little is known of Telenger's exact origins, though he was known to consider Skywatch his home.{{Ref|name=GC}}{{ref|name=Andewen}} He was known to have a leadership position within the Mages Guild of Skywatch.{{ref|name=Neetra}}

Telenger spent years excavating a magically potent [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] ruin at the heart of Skywatch,{{Ref|name=MTCACA}} in the middle of the city, in the royal courtyard and gardens,{{ref|name=GC}} taking a careful approach to avoid unleashing dangerous forces. He invited the powerful [[Lore:Arcanists|arcanist]] [[Lore:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]] to assist as a consultant on the deep aetheric constructs. Two weeks later, Telenger returned to find the planar gates sealed, most magicka wells capped, and Azandar moving some of his belongings onto the site, claiming the portals were "pedestrian pablum only interesting to children" and that he was doing Telenger a favor by closing the enigmatum that had existed there since the times of the Aldmer.{{ref|name=MTCACA|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Azandar al-Cybiades}}}} The incident became a major scandal and greatly strained relations between the two scholars.{{ref|name=AAC}} As a result, the site was officially closed to everyone. The entrance was later guarded by a member of the Mages Guild, who was forbidden to speak of it at Telenger's request.{{Ref|name=WizardNisira|[[ON:Wizard Nisira|Wizard Nisira]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GC|[[ON:Guide Culast|Guide Culast]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In reality, those guards were the result of a compromise Azandar made with Telenger.{{ref|name=AAC|[[ON:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Telenger's Emporium was a shop in the west side of Skywatch,{{Ref|name=GC}} located close to the local chapter of the Mages Guild. The establishment displayed various curios and relics gathered by Telenger,{{Ref|name=TE}}{{Ref|name=SC}} including a [[Lore:Dwarven Centurion|Dwemer Centurion]] recovered from a ruin in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]],{{ref|name=Eldwen}} that visitors could briefly activate for demonstration.{{ref|name=TE}}{{ref|name=SC}}{{ref|name=RMSIHSY}} The construct also attracted Azandar's attention.{{Ref|name=AAC}} The shop often drew scholars, guild members, and curious onlookers, many of whom admired Telenger's collection of unusual artifacts.{{ref|name=TE|[[Online:Telenger's Emporium|Telenger's Emporium]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SC|[[ON:Skywatch Celebrant|Skywatch Celebrant]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RMSIHSY|[[ON:Raerabhi|Raerabhi]], [[ON:Maedarri|Maedarri]], [[ON:Shonsora|Shonsora]], [[ON:Irnadza|Irnadza]], [[ON:Hezura|Hezura]], [[ON:Shamiri|Shamiri]], and [[ON:Yasana|Yasana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of his students, Eldwen, studied [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] relics there under Telenger's guidance,{{ref|name=Eldwen|[[ON:Eldwen|Eldwen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while the shop itself was managed by Nerassil the Bound, a spirit magically bound by Telenger. Nerassil begrudgingly served as the Emporium's enchanter and merchant, selling miscellaneous objects recovered from Telenger's expeditions, a task he frequently complained about while expressing deep resentment toward his master.{{ref|name=NTB|[[ON:Nerassil the Bound|Nerassil the Bound]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Ezduiin===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''I am Telenger the Artificer—renowned genius, author, and explorer. Ezduiin is my latest endeavor. I will bring further glory and honor to the Mages Guild!''|Telenger the Artificer{{ref|name=TA}}}}
[[File:ON-place-Ezduiin.jpg|thumb|left|Ezduiin]][[File:ON-interior-Ezduiin Undercroft.jpg|thumb|right|Ezduiin Undercroft]]
In 2E 582, Telenger led an expedition to [[Lore:Ezduiin|Ezduiin]], north of Skywatch.{{Ref|name=TE}} It was considered to be an important project of the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=Erronwe|[[ON:Erronwe|Erronwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The venture encountered numerous difficulties, beginning with the appearance of spirits and later worsening due to problems with supplies.{{Ref|name=TE}}{{ref|name=RHE|[[ON:Rellus (High Elf)|Rellus]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Parmtilir|[[ON:Parmtilir|Parmtilir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Matters grew more serious when his chief scholar and assistant, [[Lore:Andewen|Andewen]], a former student of his and now a Master of Incunabula in Skywatch, and her team went missing.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=Umbarile|[[ON:Umbarile|Umbarile]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The expedition had been searching for the ancient relic Mallari-Mora, which Telenger intended to present to the Council in hopes of earning recognition and reward, though Valaste expressed doubts about the undertaking. Telenger requested a help of an adventurer to salvage the situation.{{Ref|name=TA}} They found Andewen and learned that some of the spirits that had appeared seized the researchers before vanishing. The adventurer ventured into the ruin's undercroft. The one responsible was [[Lore:Uricantar|Uricantar]],{{Ref|name=TMM}} a former student of Telenger's whom he had once cherished almost like a son.{{ref|name=Aerchelas|[[ON:Aerchelas|Aerchelas]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Despite this Telenger had been forced to cast him out of the guild and forbid him from using magic, as Uricantar could never accept that power must have limits and that some prices are too great to pay.{{ref|name=Andewen|[[ON:Andewen|Andewen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Meldil|[[ON:Meldil|Meldil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Obsessed with power and legacy, Uricantar sought the [[Lore:Mallari-Mora|Mallari-Mora]] for his own purposes, viewing Telenger's students and others as pawns in a game of magical dominance.{{ref|name=Uricantar|[[ON:Uricantar|Uricantar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He seized the Mallari-Mora, rekindled the ruin's ancient spirits, and began using them as guardians and servants. When Telenger arrived with Andewen, Uricantar attempted to assert control over the relic and claim his own ascendancy.{{ref|name=UJ|{{Cite Book|Uricantar's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} Thanks to the intervention of an adventurer, the group, largely composed of Telenger's students, was rescued, and Uricantar was killed.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TMM|Events of [[ON:The Mallari-Mora|The Mallari-Mora]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He rewarded the adventurer with a staff bearing his personal mark.{{Ref|name=Artificer'sBolt|{{Item Link|Artificer's Bolt|summary=1|quality=2|id=29953}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The spirits awakened at the site were also laid to proper rest.{{ref|name=Sirinque|[[ON:Sirinque|Sirinque]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=AAOK|Events of [[ON:An Act of Kindness|An Act of Kindness]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}Once the situation at Ezduiin was resolved, he expressed his eagerness to present his findings to the council alongside Andewen, noting that he and Valaste would have much to discuss.{{Ref|name=TA}}

===Falinesti===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''No! How dare you, mortal?''—Mephala{{Ref|name=Mephala}}<br>''I am the Artificer, madam! That's why I dare!''—Telenger the Artificer{{Ref|name=TA}}}}
[[File:ON-place-Falinesti Autumn Site.jpg|thumb|left|Falinesti Autumn Site]][[File:ON-quest-The Arbordawn Cult.jpg|thumb|right|Telenger caught in Mephala's web]]
Telenger's next excavation was planned for Valenwood. He had already intended to venture there during his time at Ezduiin. In the frontier of Valenwood, he chose to study the [[Lore:Falinesti Autumn Site|Falinesti Autumn Site]].{{ref|name=TA|[[ON:Telenger the Artificer|Telenger the Artificer]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It was there that Telenger led another project, regarded by the members of the Guild as highly interesting.{{ref|name=Calircarya|[[ON:Calircarya|Calircarya]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Berdonion|[[ON:Berdonion|Berdonion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Telenger, the Artificer, led a Mages Guild expedition near the [[Lore:Arbordawn Cult|Arbordawn Cult]]'s site,{{ref|name=TJOITP}} uncovering the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] temple of [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]],{{ref|name=TJOITP}}{{Ref|name=TA}} which the cult believed held the key to Falinesti's return. Though he forbade them from opening it, the cultists claimed to feel its power calling, certain that once unleashed, the city would be restored.{{ref|name=TJOITP|{{Cite Book|The Journal of Indring the Patient|ns_base=Online}}}} Telenger disregarded the cult's warnings and explored the deeper tunnels leading to the Halls of Ichor, where the Arbordawn Cult had sealed passages with corruption stones. Fascinated by the relics he found, he pressed onward.{{ref|name=FR}} Though the cultists attacked the researchers, they were swiftly dealt with by Telenger,{{ref|name=K|[[ON:Kelurmend|Kelurmen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} who judged them little threat and transformed their leader, [[Lore:Indring|Indring]] into harmless poultry.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=FR|{{Cite Book|Fascinating Relics|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=Indring|[[ON:Indring|Indring]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Believing the Daedra to be the true threat, Telenger led the assistant into the temple, where they encountered Mephala's forces, and learned that the Arbordawn cultists had become her servants, the [[Lore:Deathweavers|Deathweavers]]. Recognizing the danger, Telenger used powerful magic to reseal the temple and cut off Mephala's influence, after which he concluded the threat was contained, though the mystery of the vanished city of Falinesti remained unsolved.{{Ref|name=TAC|Events of [[ON:The Arbordawn Cult|The Arbordawn Cult]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=Mephala|[[ON:Mephala|Mephala]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He rewarded the adventurer with a garment he had personally enchanted, resistant to all but the most powerful spells.{{ref|name=AV|{{Item Link|id=10927|summary=1|quality=2|Artificer's Vestments}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

While still at the Falinesti site, Telenger had already begun planning his next project, which would likely involve one of the planes of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], remarking that it had been some time since he last explored [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]]. Little is known about this endeavor or whether it ever took place.{{Ref|name=TA}}

===Coldharbour===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''I'm sure Telenger's around here somewhere. He's really quite clever. And dashing, to boot! He reminds me of my … my good friend, Stibbons.''|Lady Clarisse Laurent{{ref|name=LCL}}}}
[[File:ON-quest-The Library of Dusk 06.jpg|thumb|left|Telenger in the Library Vault]][[File:ON-place-Library of Dusk.jpg|thumb|right|The Library of Dusk]]
Telenger participated in the meeting of the three alliances at Stirk, recognizing the challenge of uniting the leaders and organizing the expeditionary force to Coldharbour, and chose to join the force himself.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TWOTC|Events of [[ON:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point during the campaign against the forces of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] Telenger, together with two other mages and scholars [[Lore:Raynor Vanos|Raynor Vanos]], and [[Lore:Clarisse Laurent|Clarisse Laurent]] decided to take advantage of their good fortune and explore the Library of Dusk. The potential knowledge to be gained far outweighed any perceived risks.{{ref|name=RV|[[ON:Raynor Vanos|Raynor Vanos]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Both of his companions eventually became enthralled by a powerful Daedric spell.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=LCL|[[ON:Lady Clarisse Laurent|Lady Clarisse Laurent]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TTDL|Events of [[ON:Through the Daedric Lens|Through the Daedric Lens]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He was kept prisoner in a cell with a banner of the [[Lore:Stars|Star]] of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] and blue [[Lore:Crystals|crystals]], and was regarded as a powerful, capable mage by his captors.{{ref|name=PT|{{Cite Book|PRISONER: TELENGER|ns_base=Online}}}} By the time the rescue arrived, he already managed to leave his cell but remained within the complex.{{ref|name=IYCRTOI|{{Cite Book|If You Can Read This, Open It|ns_base=Online}}}}{{Ref|name=TLOD|Events of [[ON:The Library of Dusk|The Library of Dusk]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Telenger concealed a note with a spell that made it invisible to Daedra so that any mortal who discovered it could be trusted. In the message he warned his fellow captives, Clarisse and Raynor, about the overwhelming magical illusions within the place and admitted that even he had underestimated their power. Realizing that drastic measures were necessary, Telenger resolved to seal himself inside a vault in the lower levels, hoping to remain clear-headed long enough to devise a plan of escape and eventually flee alongside the other captives.{{ref|name=IYCRTOI}} In the vault, Telenger discovered a portal radiating overwhelming power, sensing that whatever lay beyond sought to pierce the veil of illusion over the Library of Dusk. Recognizing its potential use in the battle against Molag Bal, he identified it as one of the [[Lore:Lights of Meridia|Lights of Meridia]], legendary sources of power once used to hurl the Hollow City into Coldharbour. He entrusted the adventurer who had come to rescue the scholars with a Teleportation Trajectory Token, a runic device he attuned to the portal that would ensure a safe return. He instructed them to enter the portal, recover the Light, and bring it back to the camp outside the ruins. Once freed, he continued contributing to the the war effort in the [[Lore:Hollow City|Hollow City]], intending to use the Light to aid the war effort, study its powers, and investigate ways to dismantle Molag Bal's [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] mechanism.{{Ref|name=TA}}{{Ref|name=TTTT|[[Online:Coldharbour Quest Items#Telenger's Teleportation Trajectory Token|Telenger's Teleportation Trajectory Token]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TLOD}}

===After Planemeld===
[[File:ON-wallpaper-Celebrate 10 Years of Amazing Stories and Characters with this New Wallpaper-7680x4320.jpg|Telenger beside Malkur Valos and Azandar al-Cybiades (center-right)|thumb|right]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Telenger is the one who makes brash decisions and puts us all in danger, and I'm the one who helps fix it. Yet as soon as I lead, I find myself in the same position as him!
For now I'd rather clean up his messes, rather than make my own.''|Andewen{{Ref|name=Andewen}}}}

Eventually, the Planemeld came to an end, and the united forces that Telenger had been part of were victorious. He survived those events.{{Ref|name=LLTDL}}{{Ref|name=Andewen}} During her later exploits in Wrothgar, Lady Laurent kept up a correspondence with Telenger.{{ref|name=LLTDL|{{Cite Book|Lady Laurent's To-Do List|ns_base=Online}}}} Later, during the time of the Daedric Triad's activities in [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]], his former apprentice Andewen conducted an expedition and research in [[Lore:Ebon Stadmont|Ebon Stadmont]]. While she took a short sabbatical to pursue some of her own studies, she still expected to join him on many other adventures in the future.{{Ref|name=Andewen}} 

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was Telenger the Artificer, portrayed in a conversation with [[Lore:Malkur Valos|Malkur Valos]] and Azandar al-Cybiades.{{ref|name=P10YA|[[Online:Painting: 10-Year Anniversary|Painting: 10-Year Anniversary]] furnishing depiction in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Alinor===

And how better to do this than to find a way to travel to and from the vanished Isle of Artaeum, mystically removed from Nirn by wizardry of mysterious Psijic Order?''|[[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]{{Ref|name=RDJ}}}}

Aside from his headquarters in Skywatch, Telenger also maintained his own workshop in the capital of the Summerset Isles, [[Lore:Alinor|Alinor]].{{Ref|name=RDJ|{{Cite Book|Razum-dar's Journal}}}}

In 2E 582, Telenger became involved in uncovering a conspiracy against Proxy Queen [[Lore:Alwinarwe|Alwinarwe]] orchestrated by [[Lore:Rinwaray|Rinwaray]]. While speaking with [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], the leader of the [[Lore:Eyes of the Queen|Eyes of the Queen]], in Alinor, Telenger lamented that despite considering himself the greatest living expert in [[Lore:Teleportation|teleportation magic]], he could not obtain the prestigious post of [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|Sapiarch]] of Transliminal Travel as [[Lore:Camilonwe of Alinor|Camilonwe of Alinor]] still held the position. Seeking to prove his superiority, Telenger devised an ambitious magical experiment. After a month of work, he constructed a device called the [[Lore:Hyperagonal Locational Determinator|Hyperagonal Locational Determinator]], capable of locating any place in the [[Lore:Gray Maybe|Gray Maybe]], even those hidden by powerful wards. Using it alongside a projection device, he successfully transported himself to the vanished island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], infiltrated [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s quarters in [[Lore:Ceporah Tower|Ceporah Tower]], and returned with research notes belonging to Sotha Sil as proof of the feat before the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijics]] could discover his intrusion. Unable to reveal this exploit openly due to the numerous laws he had broken to perform this feat, Telenger confided in an advisor named Penewen. At her suggestion, he used the same devices to send her to the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] so she could later testify to his magical prowess.{{Ref|name=RDJ}} She was, in fact, on a mission and hoped to find a connection between the Clockwork City and Artaeum.{{Ref|name=Penewen|[[ON:Penewen|Penewen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After a few days there, she returned with the help of Telenger's devices, showered him with effusive praise, and then essentially disappeared from the artificer's life. This mystery led Razum-dar to investigate further, ultimately uncovering connections that helped expose the treacherous schemes unfolding within the Proxy Queen's court.{{Ref|name=RDJ}}

Penewen, who was in reality High Magistrate Rinwaray in disguise, used the opportunity to obtain and test Sotha Sil's [[Lore:Seht's Affect Inducer|Affect Inducer]], a device capable of instilling emotions in living people. Her actions, enabled by Telenger's devices, became part of a wider conspiracy within the Proxy Queen's court, in which Rinwaray intended to use the Inducer to emotionally enslave Proxy Queen Alwinarwe. Razum-dar's investigation, aided by various artifacts created by Teleger and his travels across multiple planes, ultimately uncovered a dangerous scheme. During this time, Rinwaray returned to the Summerset Isles and made her way to Teleger's workshop. There, she used Seht's Affect Inducer on Teleger and ensured he was bound near a stone jetty on the shore. Razum-dar later found him tied to the backside of a pier, barely keeping his head above the incoming tide. Once freed, Teleger provided Razum-dar with a personal silk scarf belonging to Rinwaray, which allowed the [[Lore:Clairvoyeur|Clairvoyeur]], another of his creations, to track her. Using the device, Razum-dar located Rinwaray and confronted her, ultimately defeating her and putting an end to her nefarious plot.{{Ref|name=RDJ}}

==Research==
===Publications===
Telenger was a prolific writer, authoring many books on a variety of subjects. His interests tended more toward practical studies than metaphysical ones.{{Ref|name=FNOTS}} One of his primary research interests was history,{{Ref|name=THOWE}} and the anthropological study of various cultures, including the Bosmer,{{Ref|name=THOWE}}{{Ref|name=WENAB}} [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]],{{Ref|name=FOTSV}}{{Ref|name=CMP}}{{ref|name=SKO|[[Online:Sorcerer-King Orgnum|Sorcerer-King Orgnum]] deck description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Sload|Sload]].{{Ref|name=FNOTS}}{{Ref|name=ALC}} He also worked on texts focused on the area of [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanting]].{{Ref|name=EOTR}}{{ref|name=WAP}}

At the request of Queen Ayrenn's advisor Nuulehtel, he compiled a brief account of the Maormer of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]] titled ''[[Lore:Fang of the Sea Vipers|Fang of the Sea Vipers]]'', urging his Altmer readers to exercise caution but also to have courage.{{ref|name=FOTSV|{{Cite Book|Fang of the Sea Vipers}}}} In his other works he also described the armaments of the Maormer.{{ref|name=CMP|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 64: Pyandonean Style}}}} His works also focused on Bosmer culture. ''[[Lore:The Humor of Wood Elves|The Humor of Wood Elves]]'' was a compilation of Bosmeri jokes,{{ref|name=THOWE|{{Cite Book|The Humor of Wood Elves}}}} while ''[[Lore:Wood Elf Nicknames and Bynames|Wood Elf Nicknames and Bynames]]'' detailed the differences between Bosmeri bynames and nicknames and the Bosmeri cultural traditions surrounding those appellations.{{ref|name=WENAB|{{Cite Book|Wood Elf Nicknames and Bynames}}}} ''[[Lore:A Loathsome Civilization|A Loathsome Civilization]]'' was Telenger's work on the Sload based on a text appropriated from a defeated Maormeri fleet, which he speculated to be the journal of a diplomat to [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] from before {{Year|1E 2260}}. He also sent the journal to his colleagues in Alinor to verify its authenticity.{{ref|name=ALC|{{Cite Book|A Loathsome Civilization}}}} Another of his works, ''[[Lore:Further Notes on the Sload|Further Notes on the Sload]]'', focused on the Sload's conflicts with the Maormer, the nature of the [[Lore:Coral Tower|Coral Tower]] and [[Lore:Sload Soap|Sload soap]], and the maturation process of the Sload.{{ref|name=FNOTS|{{Cite Book|Further Notes on the Sload}}}} Sapiarch Hannayel of the College of Sapiarchs was among the scholars who reviewed Telenger's works on the Sload. The two had met before at a dinner, though Telenger was noticeably displeased that the Sapiarch could not recall every line of his writings.{{ref|name=LTI|{{Cite Book|Letter to Imedril}}}} He also took an interest in studying Khajiiti riddles.{{ref|name=Zuzik|[[ON:Zuzik|Zuzik]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Telenger was also inspired after attending a stage production of ''[[Lore:The Lusty Argonian Maid|The Lusty Argonian Maid]]''. This led him to write ''[[Lore:The Argonian Maid—An Oral Tradition|The Argonian Maid—An Oral Tradition]]'', in which he researched the complicated origins of the bawdy tale.{{ref|name=TAMOT|{{Cite Book|The Argonian Maid—An Oral Tradition}}}} His ''[[Lore:Enigma of the Runestones|Enigma of the Runestones]]'' focused on the origins of the [[Lore:Runestones|runestones]] and their classification, as well as mentioning various scholars' debates on the subject.{{ref|name=EOTR|{{Cite Book|Enigma of the Runestones}}}} According to Gabrielle Benele, Telenger's treatises on runic praxis discussed several possible origins of runestones, though none of his theories suggested a connection to Oblivion. Later, however, members of the Antiquarian Circle uncovered evidence indicating that runes could in fact transcend the veil between realms.{{Ref|name=DES|[[Online:Daedric Enchanting Station|Daedric Enchanting Station]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In his ''[[Lore:Words and Power|Words and Power]]'', Telenger proposed the theory that languages in Tamriel may possess inherent magical properties. Using examples such as runestones, enchanting glyphs, and historical texts, he argued that words and symbols might channel or invoke magical power even when not fully understood.{{ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Power}}}} In ''[[Lore:On Pircalmo's Emergency Reversal|On Pircalmo's Emergency Reversal]]'', Telenger wrote a farewell letter to one of his former students. In it, he offered encouragement and outlined a ritual meant to help Pircalmo on his new path as a mage.{{ref|name=OPER|{{Cite Book|On Pircalmo's Emergency Reversal}}}} He also provided answers to many scholarly questions through correspondence, particularly on topics such as [[Lore:Soul Magic|soul magic]], enchantment, as well as the Sload and the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] associated with them.{{ref|name=TTAAYQ|{{Cite Book|Telenger the Artificer Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=TTAAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Telenger the Artificer Answers Your Questions 2}}}}

===Artifice and Spellcraft===
Telenger the Artificer was also known for crafting a variety of curios and mechanical novelties. Among these were alchemical igniters, gold-plated devices designed to light smoking pipes.{{ref|name=TAAI|[[ON:Treasures A#Alchemical Igniter|Alchemical Igniter]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Another device attributed to him was the Perpetual Motion Gyroscope, said to spin endlessly despite having no visible source of power.{{ref|name=TPMG|[[ON:Treasures P#Perpetual Motion Gyroscope|Perpetual Motion Gyroscope]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Both of these items bore Telenger's personal mark.{{Ref|name=TAAI}}{{ref|name=TPMG}} He also designed intricate toys such as clockwork soldiers, likely intended as gifts for the children of nobles.{{ref|name=TCCTS|[[ON:Treasures C#Clockwork Toy Soldier|Clockwork Toy Soldier]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Other creations included the Artificer's Bolt, a lightning staff known for its scent of rain and marked with his personal sigil,{{Ref|name=Artificer'sBolt}} as well as the Artificer's Vestments, a garment he personally enchanted to resist all but the most powerful spells,{{ref|name=AV}} both of which he gifted to the Vestige in gratitude for their assistance.{{Ref|name=TA}}

Despite his mastery of magical artifice, Telenger was openly critical of using arcane magic to replace ordinary labor. On one occasion he mocked such excess, remarking that summoning a meteor to mine ore rather than performing the work properly was ostentatious and ridiculous.{{ref|name=MM|[[Online:Meteor Mining|Meteor Mining]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He was also a creator of some spells, such as Wonderful Linking Cantrip.{{ref|name=OPER}}
