{{LetterPic|T}}he designations of Gods, Demons, [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], and {{Lore Link|Daedra}}, are universally confusing to the layman. They are often used interchangeably.

"Aedra" and "Daedra" are not relative terms. They are [[Lore:Elvish|Elvish]] and exact. [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] is a Daedra both in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. "Aedra" is usually translated as "ancestor," which is as close as [[Lore:Cyrodilic|Cyrodilic]] can come to this Elven concept. "Daedra" means, roughly, "not our ancestors." This distinction was crucial to the {{Lore Link|Dunmer}}, whose fundamental split in ideology is represented in their mythical genealogy.

Aedra are associated with stasis. Daedra represent change.

Aedra created the [[Lore:Mundus|mortal world]] and are bound to the {{Lore Link|Ehlnofey|Earth Bones}}. Daedra, who cannot create, have the power to change.

As part of the divine contract of creation, the Aedra can be killed. Witness [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] and the moons.

The protean Daedra, for whom the rules do not apply, can only be banished.
