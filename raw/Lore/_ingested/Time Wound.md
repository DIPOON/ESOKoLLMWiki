# Lore:Time Wound

<noinclude>{{Trail|Appendices}}__NOTOC__</noinclude>
'''[[Lore:Time Wound|Time Wounds]]''', also known as '''Tiid-Ahraan''' in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragon Language]], are tears created in the fabric of space when time is shattered within a localized area. These [[Lore:Time Magic|distortions of time]] and space have some similarities to [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]]s.{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|[[Skyrim:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In appearance they can resemble a slight shimmering in the air,{{ref|[[SR:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} or a vortex of golden and purple light.{{ref|[[ON:Sunspire|Sunspire]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Time Wounds==
===Throat of the World===
The first Time Wound in known history was created in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] during the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], and served to bring about the end of the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]].{{ref|name=SkyrimAB|[[Skyrim:Alduin's Bane|Alduin's Bane]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Near the end of the war, the ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s confronted [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] at the summit of the [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]].{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}{{ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}} With no conventional means to defeat the [[Lore:Dragon|wyrm]], [[Lore:Felldir the Old|Felldir the Old]] used an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] to perform an incantation that ejected Alduin from their time, propelling him into the future and leaving behind a Time Wound.{{ref|name=SkyrimAB}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, Alduin emerged from the Time Wound and began the process of resurrecting his [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]] allies all across Skyrim.{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}{{ref|[[SR:A Blade in the Dark|A Blade in the Dark]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] later used the same Elder Scroll to exploit this Time Wound and gaze into the past, thereby gaining lost knowledge of the [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]].{{ref|name=SkyrimAB}}

===Sunspire===
A Time Wound also appeared at the peak of the [[Lore:Sunspire|Sunspire]] temple in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], when [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]]'s battle cry put an end to [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]]'s slaughter of the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] and cast him out of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]].{{Ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the Dragons {{Lore Link|Nahviintaas}}, {{Lore Link|Lokkestiiz}}, and {{Lore Link|Yolnahkriin}} returned to the affairs of mortals, posing as gods. The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] devotees of Sunspire Temple believed Nahviintaas to be Alkosh, and gathered pilgrims from across [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] to serve him. However, Nahviintaas' true goal was to tear the Time Wound wider in order to "correct the mortal mistake" and restore the "natural order". This threat was ended when all three Dragons were slain by the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]].{{ref|[[ON:The Return of Alkosh|The Return of Alkosh]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Beldaburo===
Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] named Corelian attempted to use an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] to travel back in time to prevent their past self from ever obtaining a state of lichdom. Corelian feared the gradual and incurable descent into madness brought about by their unnatural existence, and stole the Elder Scroll from the [[Lore:Order of the Hour|Order of the Hour]] in a misguided attempt to avoid the ultimate fate of most liches.{{ref|{{Cite book|Corelian's diary}}}} However, Corelian's meddling disrupted the flow of Time, resulting in the creation of a Time Wound deep within the ruins of [[Lore:Beldaburo|Beldaburo]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Primate [[Lore:Ilav Dralgoner|Ilav Dralgoner]] of the Order of the Hour discovered the existence of this Time Wound, and tasked the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] with recovering the Elder Scroll before the Wound could be fully opened. With the Order's blessing, the Hero was able to penetrate the magical barrier created by the Wound within Beldaburo, and slay Corelian. This destroyed the Time Wound, allowing Time to flow in accordance with [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]'s rules once more and thereby saving reality from collapsing. With the threat ended, the Hero was made a Paladin of the Hour by the grateful priest, who could sense time had indeed been healed.{{ref|[[Oblivion:A Wound In Time|A Wound In Time]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Saving Time Itself|Saving Time Itself]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Ilav Dralgoner|Ilav Dralgoner]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}
