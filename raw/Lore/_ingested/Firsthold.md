[[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]] (also spelled as '''First Hold'''){{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Firsthold|Firsthold]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}  is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] and is located on the northernmost tip of [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]], the second-largest island of the archipelago.{{Ref|name=ONFirsthold|[[Online:Firsthold|Firsthold]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Just as the name references, Firsthold is the oldest city in the province and was founded by the first [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] inhabitants of Tamriel, who had previously sailed across the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] to escape the destruction of their homeland, [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]].{{Ref|name=TLOF|{{Cite Book|The Lay of Firsthold}}}}{{Ref|name=AEXII|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored, Chapter XII}}}} The city is also where the mage [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] founded the original [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] charter and thus provided the common people with access to [[Lore:Magic|magic]] and other arcane pursuits.{{Ref|name=OOTMG|{{Cite Book|Origin of the Mages Guild}}}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-place-Firsthold Wayshrine.jpg|thumb|right|The Firsthold wayshrine]]
[[File:ON-place-Mages Guild (Firsthold).jpg|thumb|left|The Mages Guild of Firsthold]]
Firsthold is located on the highest point of Auridon, in the [[Lore:Vafe|Vafe]] region.{{Ref|name=ONFirsthold}} From here, the city looks across the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] to the north. Just off the coast is the small island of [[Lore:Calluis Lar|Calluis Lar]], where local pirates congregate and organize raids on royal ships.{{Ref|name=TFR|{{Cite Book|The Firsthold Revolt}}}} Though the city is not built on its shore, Firsthold's position on the peak of Auridon accents the northern end of the [[Lore:Auridon Strait|Auridon Strait]], the body of water between Auridon and [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]].{{Ref|name=ONFirsthold}}

Firsthold, in particular, sits at the top of a hill, at the base of a mountain right on the tip of Auridon. It sits in a well-defended spot, surrounded by mountains and separated from the northern Vafe coast by a large river. It also sits above sea level, behind a vast and imposing wall of craggy cliffs.{{Ref|name=EGT}} The land around it is heavily forested with [[Lore:Maple|maple]], [[Lore:Laurel|laurel]] and [[Lore:Larch|larch]] trees, and the city itself is at the end of Auridon's roadways.{{Ref|name=ONFirsthold}} 

The southern gate leads to the lower coast on the Auridon Strait. The eastern entrance is a river bridge leading into the greater Vafe region. Firsthold is neighbored by several settlements, one of which includes West Guard to the far southeast. On an island just southwest of the city is [[Lore:Belport Run|Belport Run]], which has no road connections to any other part of the province.{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Summurset Isle towns.png|Map of Summurset Isle – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

Firsthold is noted for its beautiful and clean cityscape. Its docks are described as pristine{{Ref|name=EGT}} and their streets are supposedly made from crystals and silk, and shine like mirrors.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/Playing Houses|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 12/Playing Houses – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} The central city section is centered around a river canal that forms a Y-shape and divides the area into threes. The western section of Firsthold is an open parkland with a cave entrance and a winding road to the southwest gate. The eastern section between rivers is filled with homes and the local Mages Guild, while the north section is the Firsthold castle grounds.{{Ref|name=ONFirsthold}}

The [[Lore:Diceto|Diceto]] is a great river that runs through the city's royal parks and [[Lore:Kanthleaf|Kanthleaves]] grow around the area.{{Ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}} Overlooking the Abecean Sea are the city's high cliff palaces, of which includes Firsthold's palace, where the royal family lives.{{Ref|name=TFR}} The palace is described as enormous and shines bright like a white [[Lore:Diamond|diamond]].{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} Underneath the cliffside wall is the port town, built into a tightly protected hollow that is big enough to fit large ships.{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Summerset Isles}}}}

==History==
===The Lay of Firsthold and the Odyssey of Topal===
[[File:ON-concept-The Lay of Firsthold.jpg|thumb|left|The Lay of Firsthold]]
[[File:ON-place-Nine-Prow Landing.jpg|thumb|right|Nine-Prow Landing]]
Firsthold was founded sometime in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] by the original Altmer ancestors of Auridon, the [[Lore:Ancients|Ancients]]. The northernmost shore of the island, known now as Nine Prows Landing, is considered by tradition the first place where these ancestors first landed.{{Ref|name=AEXII}} As the story goes, from their lost home of Aldmeris, High Lord [[Lore:Torinaan|Torinaan]] led the Ancients across the Eltheric Ocean to this very spot.{{Ref|name=TLOF}}

Scholars give different dates when the Aldmer specifically arrived on Tamriel. Fenlil the Wayfarer's research leaves him to believe it was in the middle of the early Merethic Era,{{Ref|name=AEXII}} but [[Lore:Aicantar of Shimmerene|Aicantar of Shimmerene]] states it to be the middle Merethic Era, which by then, the aboriginal [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] of the mainland had already built preliterate communities.{{Ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} In any case, Torinaan proclaimed himself Kinlord of the land and named it Auridon, enchanted by the golden dawn. He swore an oath to never depart from his new kinhold and built his home.{{Ref|name=TLOF}}

Since then, the Ancients had spread themselves out across the Summerset Isles, and although more kinholds were founded since, Torinaan's home will always be the "first among holds", hence the name.{{Ref|name=TLOF}}{{Ref|name=OTA|{{Cite Book|Ode to Auridon}}}} Some years later, by the time the Aldmer had settled Summerset Isle, Firsthold was a port. The [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] author, Florin Jaliil was able to translate the [[Lore:Aldmeris (language)|Aldmeris]] epic of [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]], known as the ''[[Lore:Udhendra Nibenu|Udhendra Nibenu]]'' and thus provide context for his Merethic Era voyages. The book describes three ships who were tasked with finding a way back to Aldmeris, using Firsthold as their launch site. While the first ship and its pilot are unknown, the second was the Pasquiniel helmed by Illio, and the third was the Niben, helmed by Topal himself.{{Ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}}

After traveling northeast to present-day Tamriel, only the Niben returned to Firsthold and it was laden with all sorts of items, from [[Lore:Gold|gold]], spice, furs, and strange creatures. Sometime later, Topal leaves again for Tamriel and roughly eighty months later, according to Florin Jaliil, he is on the eastern Tamrielic coast, between the [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]] region and [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]], looking for a passage back to Firsthold. In a miscalcuation, Topal had inadvertently discovered the eponymous [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]], the [[Lore:Niben River|Niben River]] named after his ship, and [[Lore:Lake Rumare|Lake Rumare]]. Florin Jaliil further theorizes that after coming back down the river and traveling southward, Topal and the Niben were able to return back home to Firsthold.{{Ref|name=FOTN}}

===Firsthold throughout Summerset's History===
[[File:ON-npc-High Kinlord Rilis I.jpg|thumb|left|High Kinlord Rilis I]]
[[File:ON-npc-Vanus Galerion 03.jpg|thumb|right|Vanus Galerion, First Mage of Firsthold]]
Sometime after Torinaan's reign over Firsthold, the city came under the control of [[Lore:House Rilis|House Rilis]], starting from High Kinlord Rilis I. They were described as a line of proud warriors{{Ref|name=ONElanwe|[[Online:Lady Elanwe|Lady Elanwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and have fought in conflicts of the past, like when Rilis III fought in the Battle of Ten Maws.{{Ref|name=ONRilisIII|[[Online:High Kinlord Rilis III|High Kinlord Rilis III]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though much of Summerset's history throughout the First and Second Eras is largely unknown to the mainland, what is known is that internal conflicts occurred between the cities, like [[Lore:Skywatch|Skywatch]] and Firsthold, and they often sparked to full-out war.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Summerset}}}}

Other events include attempts at usurping the Throne of Alinor from the royal family. Kinlady [[Lore:Arlinwe|Arlinwe]] was a noble from Firsthold and rival to the leading High King at the time, [[Lore:Naurnindon|Naurnindon]]. One of her allies was Lady [[Lore:Earlenque Corelanya|Earlenque]] of the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya Clan]], whose majority were forced to flee their recently lost territory in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and live as refugees in the remaining Corelanya estates on Summerset.{{Ref|name=HOCC3|{{Cite Book|History of Clan Corelanya, Pt. 3}}}}

Earlenque hoped that Arlinwe as High Queen would give her the means to retake their kingdom from the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]]. But after Arlinwe moved too boldly against the High King in {{Year|1E 909}}, she lost her chance to supplant him and any allies associated with her lost favor with Alinor. Lady Earlenque was given an ultimatum to lose her properties and titles, or leave in exile, and so she chose the latter. Many of Arlinwe's allies chose to leave with the Corelanya Clan and together they built a new home on [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]], an island on the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]], south of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]].{{Ref|name=HOCC3}}

Despite conflicts between cities and other events, the legacy of House Rilis went untouched and respected until the reign of [[Lore:Rilis XII|Rilis XII]], who in his youth was a hero of the people but hid a darker side that proved to be his ultimate downfall.{{Ref|name=ONCirion|[[Online:Keeper Cirion|Keeper Cirion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During his rule, the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic]] student, [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] began to assemble like-minded magic users to his new enterprise based in Firsthold's urban center,{{Ref|name=OOTMG}} at the time known as "Galerion's Folly".{{Ref|name=KRATMG|{{Cite Book|Kinlord Rilis and the Mages Guild}}}} At that point, magic was something that only the upper echelon of society and academia could use, and it was only something practiced out in secluded areas. But Vanus Galerion hoped to introduce the arcane arts to the common folk and even provide services for anyone in need.{{Ref|name=OOTMG}} 

The Master of Incunabula, [[Lore:Valaste|Valaste]] writes in her essay discussing the Kinlord's role in the guild's founding, that Rilis XII saw this enterprise as an opportunity to play mages and commoners against each other{{Ref|name=KRATMG}} and in {{Year|2E 230}}, initiated the [[Lore:Charter Conclave|Charter Conclave]], a meeting between him, Vanus Galerion and the Ritemaster of the Psijic Order, [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]] to formally lay out Galerion's intentions. Though no records exist of this meeting, the Mages Guild was formally approved and was spread out across Tamriel,{{Ref|name=OOTMG}}{{Ref|name=KRATMG}} at the time Galerion even gained the title of "First Mage of Firsthold".{{Ref|name=ONElanwe}} By then, Rilis XII was already interested in daedric affairs, making pacts and dealing in evil magic.{{Ref|name=ONElanwe}}{{Ref|name=KRATMG}} In his pursuit of power, he ordered the Great Orrery's disassembly and fired the Varmaster when it could not [[Lore:Sight|predict]] his future in {{Year|2E 235}}.{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

Rilis XII's downfall came when he presented his powers and its possibilities to other nobles. But in fear, he was sentenced to the [[Lore:The Banished Cells|Banished Cells]]{{Ref|name=MOKR1|{{Cite Book|Manifesto of Kinlord Rilis XII}}}} however his immense power and daedric pact proved to be too much to sustain him.{{Ref|name=ONCirion}} Upon his death, his son, [[Lore:Rilis XIII|Rilis XIII]] sealed his spirit within a binding stone and enlisted the [[Lore:Keepers of the Banished Cells|Keepers]] with the life-long task of guarding him.{{Ref|name=ONCirion}}{{Ref|name=MOKR4|{{Cite Book|4th Manifesto of Kinlord Rilis XII}}}} With his father left in prison, Prince Rilis XIII takes up his throne as the High Kinlord of Firsthold.{{Ref|name=ONCirion}}{{Ref|name=ONRilisXIII|[[Online:High Kinlord Rilis XIII|High Kinlord Rilis XIII]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Rilis XIII's reign and the Daedric Invasion===
[[File:ON-npc-High Kinlord Rilis XIII.jpg|thumb|left|High Kinlord Rilis XIII]]
[[File:ON-quest-Sever All Ties.jpg|thumb|right|An Oblivion Gate in the Castle Courtyard]]
Rilis XIII continued his governance of Auridon many years later. With his father's death, he decided to leave behind [[Lore:Castle Rilis|Castle Rilis]] and instead lived in Firsthold Castle within the city limits. Even though Skywatch was still the region's capital, he saw fit to remain close to his ancestral home.{{Ref|name=ONRoth}} Rilis XIII was High Kinlord when High King [[Lore:Hidellith|Hidellith]] passed away in {{Year|2E 580}}. Upon the news, Princess [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] returned to the Summerset Isles via the city docks in Firsthold, where she was greeted by the Court of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]], her friends and family.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=ATUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn: The Unforeseen Queen}}}}

In the short time since her ascension to the throne, Queen Ayrenn formed the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] with the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]].{{Ref|name=ATUQ}} To familiarize herself with the newly united realm, Ayrenn and her court took a tour across the Aldmeri Dominion, and in {{Year|2E 582}}, she traveled through Auridon.{{Ref|name=ONFirtorel|[[Online:Firtorel|Firtorel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONTreasureQ|[[Online:Treasures Q#Queen's Tour Schedule|Queen's Tour Schedule]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} However, a radical group of Altmer called the [[Lore:Veiled Heritance|Veiled Heritance]] sprung their attack against the Dominion. After High Kinlady [[Lore:Estre|Estre]] of Skywatch revealed herself to be the group's leader, the Veiled Heritance began their large-scale attack on Auridon, beginning with the town of [[Lore:Dawnbreak|Dawnbreak]].{{Ref|name=ONSinyon|[[Online:Canonreeve Sinyon|Canonreeve Sinyon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TGOA|{{Cite Book|The Gift of Arson|ns_base=ON}}}}

Rilis XIII expressed to Estre his decision to remain neutral and would rather have Auridon's best interests in mind.{{Ref|name=LTE|{{Cite Book|Letter to Estre|ns_base=ON}}}} Ultimately, however, Firsthold was the most important stronghold in turning the tides of this war.{{Ref|name=TGOA}} After she escaped from Skywatch, Estre arrived and was accepted into Firsthold with open arms by Rilis XIII, who was completely unaware of her treachery. Without a moment's notice, Estre summoned [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|oblivion gates]] to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] and daedra poured into the city.{{Ref|name=ONCurime|[[Online:Curime|Curime]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Estre sealed Firsthold Castle with a powerful barrier, powered by wards and guarded by [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]]. Ayrenn's battlereeve, [[Lore:Urcelmo|Urcelmo]] led the counterattack from the Mages Guild and commanded a militia made from the [[Lore:Eyes of the Queen|Eyes of the Queen]] and the [[Lore:Eagle's Talons|Eagle's Talons]].{{Ref|name=ONUrcelmo|[[Online:Battlereeve Urcelmo|Battlereeve Urcelmo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONBreaking|[[Online:Breaking the Barrier|Breaking the Barrier]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

An [[Lore:The Vestige|Agent]] of the Dominion rendezvoused with Urcelmo and began their plan to retake the city. While mages of the guild were performing a ritual to break the barrier, the Agent was out in Firsthold, dispelling the wards with a talisman.{{Ref|name=ONBreaking}} With the barrier broken, a final push toward Firsthold Castle was made. Before confronting High Kinlady Estre, the Agent destroyed oblivion gates around the castle and entered the final one in Firsthold Castle with their companions, [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], Battlereeve Urcelmo, and the mage Sinien. In the caldera of the Refuge of Dread, they had their final battle with the High Kinlady and saved Firsthold. High Kinlord Rilis XIII was saved and Queen Ayrenn finished her tour through Auridon in the aftermath of the battle. The Queen sent the Agent across the sea to [[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]] to assist the [[Lore:Aeradan Camoran|King]] of the Wood Elves.{{Ref|name=ONSever|[[Online:Sever All Ties|Sever All Ties]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Firsthold and Auridon had been saved from a daedric invasion but it took some time for the city to recover fully. Text and reference works were lost from the Firsthold Royal Library and so the High Kinlord asked leading experts across the region to replace old text with new accounts. The skilled [[Lore:Smithing|blacksmith]] from [[Lore:Vulkhel Guard|Vulkhel Guard]], Vandalion Brightsteel provided his definitive description to making Summerset [[Lore:Malachite|glass]] armor.{{Ref|name=CM16}}

===Firsthold in the Third Era===
[[File:DF-npc-Morgiah (face).png|thumb|left|Queen Morgiah]]

According to the historical-fiction ''[[Lore:The Wolf Queen|The Wolf Queen]]'', in {{Year|3E 80}}, Prince [[Lore:Amiel Septim|Amiel]] of the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim]] family visited Firsthold to meet the Archmagister of the Mages Guild.{{Ref|name=TWQ1|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v1}}}} In {{Year|3E 110}}, the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]{{Ref|name=BHOTE1}} in their last-known invasion of the province, the [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]].{{Ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}} Despite having assistance from Emperor [[Lore:Antiochus|Antiochus]] and the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial Navy]], the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm accredited to the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=TWQ5|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v5}}}}

When [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius]] became the King of [[Lore:Solitude (kingdom)|Solitude]], he was visited by the Princess of Firsthold who famously wrote to her brother that "the king's gripped my hand and it felt like I was being clutched by a skeleton. Pelagius is greatly emaciated, indeed." Even though just five months earlier, he was described as a "hale and hearty soul with a heart so big, it widens his waist".{{Ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Firsthold was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Kalalian and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]].{{Ref|name=AR}} 

Roughly around this time, the King of Worms, [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] had been terrorizing the Kingdom of Firsthold, resulting in the death of its king. His brother, [[Lore:Reman Karoodil|Reman Karoodil]] was named the new King of Firsthold, with his brother's son, Aedrin, remaining as the crown prince and heir apparent.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} Similarly, Reman's son passed away sometime around {{Year|3E 405}}.{{Ref|name=DFWedding|[[Daggerfall:Morgiah's Wedding|Morgiah's Wedding]] story quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} With no seeable prospect in the [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] kingdom of [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], Princess [[Lore:Morgiah|Morgiah]] conspired with the King of Worms so that she may insert herself into the Summerset Isles nobility.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}}

Princess Morgiah offered her "first" in exchange for Mannimarco to leave Firsthold and give him the means to speak with his son once more.{{Ref|name=DFWedding}} In the end, Reman was given his chance to talk with his son, and the King of Worms' necromantic forces were gone. Morgiah's explanation for Mannimarco's disappearance was that the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] had vanquished him. Grateful for her help, their three-year-long engagement quickly commenced despite all opposition.{{Ref|name=DFWedding}}{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}}

===Reign of the Black Queen and Onward===
Morgiah's arrival in the otherwise insular Summerset Isles brought her contempt from many locals and peasants. Many of them took to calling her "the Black Queen" but King Karoodil paid no mind to it. This resentment also resided in nobility, most notably with Lady Gialene, King Karoodil's chief concubine and daughter of the King of Skywatch, who partnered with the [[Lore:Trebbite Monks|Trebbite Monks]] to coordinate Morgiah's downfall.{{Ref|name=TFR}} While the King was away dealing with [[Lore:Pirates|pirates]] on Calluis Lar, the Trebbite Monks revolted in a well-coordinated attack on the city. But Morgiah was able to outsmart her detractors by feeding Gialene false intel and thus convincing the Trebbite Monks to use [[Lore:Mysticism|mysticism]] to cast reflective spells on themselves. On the dawn of the siege, however, Morgiah, instead of using battlemages as believed, used archers to shoot them down. And with the reflective spell, denied them magical [[Lore:Restoration|healing]]. The monks were defeated and forced to retreat, while Lady Gialene was sent back to her father's court in Skywatch.{{Ref|name=TFR}}

As the years went by, the royal couple bore children, Prince [[Lore:Goranthir|Goranthir]] and Princess [[Lore:Rinnala|Rinnala]], who, despite their Altmer ancestry, were fully [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] in appearance.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} Though many people in Firsthold could not bear the idea of non-Altmer inheriting the throne,{{Ref|name=TFR}} the two were eligible by the late years of the Third Era. At that time, the younger generation of Altmer in the province had grown accustomed to and gained respect for mainland Tamriel's foreign cultures and races, while also heavily criticizing the rigid hierarchy and xenophobia of their people.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} 

When the two children inherited their parents' throne is unknown, but after the death of Potentate [[Lore:Ocato of Firsthold|Ocato of Firsthold]] and the subsequent [[Lore:Stormcrown Interregnum|Stormcrown Interregnum]], the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] were quick to claim power over the province. Not only did they overthrow the monarchy across the Isles, but they also conducted one of their many pogroms against those who were not "of the blood of the Aldmer" as well as dissidents.{{Ref|name=RTIV|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}}

===Known Rulers===
[[File:ON-place-Firsthold Castle.jpg|thumb|right|Firsthold Castle]]
Firsthold is an important city on the island of Auridon. For much of history, it was the seat of power for House Rilis, the royal family that ruled all over the island for many generations.{{Ref|name=ONRoth|[[Online:Rothondothrin|Rothondothrin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though other places like [[Lore:Dawnbreak|Dawnbreak]] and [[Lore:Silsailen|Silsailen]] are administrated by their local canonreeve,{{Ref|name=ONSinyon}}{{Ref|name=ONValano|[[Online:Canonreeve Valano|Canonreeve Valano]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the general Firsthold region was under their control.{{Ref|name=ONElanwe}} Despite its position in the region's politics, it was neither the capital nor where the family lived until much later. The southern city of Skywatch is considered Auridon's capital while the Rilis family lived in the eponymous Castle Rilis until after Rilis XII's reign. Rilis XIII was the first to make Firsthold his home and the seat of his power.{{Ref|name=ONRoth}}

Historically, Firsthold's rulers went by the title of High Kinlord{{Ref|name=ONRilisXIII}} or High Lord,{{Ref|name=AR}}{{Ref|name=ONDespot|[[Online:Unique Items#Aldmeri Dominion|Despot's Shoulderplates]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} thus the land is recognized as a kinhold.{{Ref|name=TLOF}} The family is generally known as the '''Royal House of Firsthold'''{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} or more simply the '''high family'''.{{Ref|name=ONElanwe}} By the Third Era, Firsthold was the capital of the eponymous '''Kingdom of Firsthold''' and its ruler held the title of King/Queen.{{Ref|name=AR}}{{Ref|name=DFWedding}}{{Ref|name=AGAD|{{Cite Book|A Game at Dinner}}}} Though the size of the kingdom is currently unknown, the Trebbite Monks are recognized as citizens and their monastery fell within its borders. The city of Skywatch was also the seat of a King{{Ref|name=TFR}} but it is unknown if they were a sovereign state or still part of Firsthold.

For the longest time, the Psijic Order served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles. If anyone wanted their counsel, their best method was either through a select few places or the kings themselves. They continued this service up until their island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] first vanished in the Second Era, around when the Mages Guild was formed. When they returned several years later for the Third Era, they continued where they left off{{Ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} until Artaeum disappeared a second time sometime in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] for whatever reason.{{Ref|name=SRTolfdir|[[Skyrim:Tolfdir|Tolfdir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Culture and Society==

The ''[[Lore:Songs#Altmer Songs|Lay of Firsthold]]'' is a ballad about the Aldmer's journey across the sea, and Torinaan's discovery of Firsthold and the land around it.{{Ref|name=TLOF}}{{Ref|name=ONTreasureBox|[[Online:Treasures L#"Lay of Firsthold" Music Box|"Lay of Firsthold" Music Box]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Altmer that live in Firsthold help keep ancient traditions and customs alive in the changing modern world.{{Ref|name=AR}} Firsthold has some notable places of interest, such as displayed mosaics{{Ref|name=ONAntiMusic|[[Online:Music Box, Dreams and Memories|Music Box, Dreams and Memories]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} and royal parks.{{Ref|name=WAP}} The Firsthold Royal Library had some of the oldest books in the Third Empire. It is run by the chief archivist and at one point had a fifty-foot tall metal statue of [[Lore:Julianos|Julianos]],{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} the [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodilic]] god of literature, law, and history.{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}

But one of its most iconic landmarks is the [[Lore:Great Orrery|Great Orrery]].{{Ref|name=PGE3AS}} It is overseen by the Varmaster.{{Ref|name=LEoERZI}}. It remains as the legacy of the [[Lore:Sun Birds of Alinor|Sun Birds of Alinor]], attempted explorers of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] from the Merethic Era. The orrery's spheres are made of celestial minerals that they gathered.{{Ref|name=PGE3AS}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} A [[Lore:Altmer Cuisine#Fruit and Cheese Plate|Fruit and Cheese Plate]] dish originates from Firsthold.{{Ref|name=ONFruit|[[Online:Firsthold Fruit and Cheese Plate|Firsthold Fruit and Cheese Plate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

As per tradition across the province, Firsthold holds a Cotillion throughout the week, and in the city, it marks the start of the social season. Nobles are emphasized to wear all-white clothing but anyone can sport any style.{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL}} Firsthold also has a masquerade season, in which people wear costumes and masks for the celebration.{{Ref|name=LRPHH|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/Hearthfire Hearthfires|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 11/Hearthfire Hearthfires – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}}{{Ref|name=LRPWOTW|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/War of the Wormgod|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 13/War of the Wormgod – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} Firsthold became a primary stop in the [[Lore:East Empire Trading Company|East Empire Trading Company]]'s shipping lines and connected to other cities like [[Lore:Stros M'Kai (city)|Stros M'Kai]] and [[Lore:Woodhearth|Woodhearth]].{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}}

Many notable figures have either lived in Firsthold or were born there. The notable scholar, Ahrtabazus lived in Firsthold in {{Year|2E 456}} when he did an in-depth study on the nature of [[Lore:Faerie|Faeries]] and proposed a controversial theory in his book called ''the Faerie Chain''.{{Ref|name=TF|{{Cite Book|The Faerie}}}} Olpion of Firsthold was a mage of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] that attempted to break the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|liminal bridges]] to the Deadlands of Oblivion but failed in his ritual.{{Ref|name=TODP|{{Cite Book|Tome of Daedric Portals}}}} Perhaps Firsthold's most accomplished citizen was Ocato of Firsthold, the former High Chancellor of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] in the latter years of the Third Era, and later the Potentate of the Third Empire after the death of the Septim Dynasty. He served alongside [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] for many years and was both his closest confidant and chief advisor.{{Ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|4}}{{Ref|name=RTIII|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. III}}}}
