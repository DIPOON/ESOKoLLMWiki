[[Lore:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilight]]s are intelligent winged [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] with claw-like limbs, black horns, and spiked tails. They can have blue or purple skin, and their eyes can be red or blue.{{ref|Appearance of [[ON:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilights]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|Appearance of [[MW:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilights]] in [[ON:Morrowind|Morrowind]]}} Their bodies are dappled with scales. They are known to operate as servants and messengers of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]].{{Ref|name=savant|[[Morrowind:Savant|Savant]]'s dialogue from ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}} In appearance they have a beautiful feminine form similar to that of [[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]] with [[Lore:Bat|bat]]-like wings, though the feminine aspects of the winged twilights are more "ravishing", and their long, sharp, hooked tails are immeasurably more deadly.{{Ref|name=DD}} When working together, they follow a matriarch.{{Ref|name=SS|Events of [[ON:Saving Stibbons|Saving Stibbons]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude> 
The brutish and masculine [[Lore:Grievous Twilight|grievous twilight]] is a corruption of the winged [[Lore:Twilights|twilight]].{{ref|name=QC2015|group=UOL|[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v{{=}}rqSGcTdQOqk Imperial City & Beyond - The Elder Scrolls Online: Tamriel Unlimited QuakeCon 2015 Panel 7:22-8:26]}}

Winged twilights are resistant to fire, frost, poison, and shock and can reflect spells.{{Ref|name=MW|Item drops/resistances in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Winged twilights are not short on cunning; they stalk their prey, waiting for an opportunity to take it down with their swift strikes. They swing their barbed tails to injure their foes while airborn.{{Ref|name=KCAHC|[[Books:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]] p. 116-117}} Some winged twilights wield shock spells in battle.{{ref|[[ON:Winged Twilight|Winged Twilight]] abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They can also shriek at their prey to incapacitate it. Their screams are deafening, and those caught in front of a winged twilight as it shrieks will become stunned and confused. Their talons are used to tear flesh, as well as grip rocks, branches and other vantage points.{{Ref|name=KCAHC}}

Though typically found in the employ of Azura,{{Ref|name=savant}} they also serve of other [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]], such as [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]],{{Ref|name=Ashalmimilkala|Presence of Winged Twilights at [[ON:Ashalmimilkala|Ashalmimilkala]]}} [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]],{{Ref|name=EoA|[[ON:Irrai|Irrai]]'s dialogue and actions during [[ON:Eyes of Azura|Eyes of Azura]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]].{{Ref|name=TES|Twilights summoned during [[ON:The Ebon Sanctum|The Ebon Sanctum]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They occasionally ensorcell and deceive mortals, sometimes under the guise of serving a more trustworthy Prince.{{Ref|name=LTN|{{Cite Book|Loriasel Tablet Notes}}}} Some winged twilights become infatuated with mortals and abduct them, holding them hostage until they return their affections.{{Ref|name=SS}}{{Ref|name=IIMBY|{{Cite Book|If I May Beseech You|ns_base=ON}}}} They may be lascivious{{Ref|name=AMoYT|{{Cite Book|A Moment of Your Time?|ns_base=ON}}}} and insistent{{Ref|name=SBU|{{Cite Book|Situation Becoming Urgent|ns_base=ON}}}} towards their captives. One particular instance of winged twilights abducting mortals involved a nest at {{Lore Link|Esutanamus}} in {{Year|2E 582}}. A winged twilight matriarch kidnapped a young adult, and whenever the young mer tried to leave, the Daedra swooped down onto his head, tussled his hair with their claws and pushed him back into the ruins, cooing as they herded him back into their lair.{{Ref|name=RtR|Events of [[ON:Rising to Retainer|Rising to Retainer]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Seythen Rendo|Seythen Rendo]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Rising to Retainer|Rising to Retainer]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Molag Grunda|Molag Grunda]] is a winged twilight who is acknowledged as the daughter of Molag Bal.{{ref|Molag Bal's dialogue during [[MW:A Cure for Vampirism|A Cure for Vampirism]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=Cadwell|[[Online:Cadwell#Crossing the Chasm|Sir Cadwell's dialogue]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

==Uses==

[[Lore:Void Salts|Void Salts]], ichor, and membranes are reagents that can be collected from their corporeal remains.{{Ref|name=MW}}{{Ref|name=HW|{{Cite Book|Hanin's Wake}}}} Ichor of winged twilight smells similar to eucalyptus or mint and can be used to brew potions of [[Lore:Alteration|levitation]].{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}{{rp|41, 220}} The long, extremely sharp hooks on the tips of their wings can be used to concoct potent poisons.{{Ref|name=KCAHC}} 
Winged Twilight wing membranes were ocasionally used for straining in alchemy.{{Ref|name=HW}}

When conjured by mortals, winged twilights can be ordered to heal their summoner.{{Ref|[[Online:Twilight Matriarch|Twilight Matriarch]] abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
*Early concepts for ''Morrowind'' suggested that they were initially going to be known as "Winged Seducers", playing off the winged [[Daggerfall:Daedra Seducer|Daedra Seducers]] encountered in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' and [[BS:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducers]] found in [[BS:Battlespire|''Battlespire'']], but were later altered into a more bestial type of Daedra, with the term "seducer" later becoming equated with the [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]].
