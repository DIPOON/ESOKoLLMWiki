[[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]] (also spelled '''Eldenroot'''){{Ref|name=TBA1|{{Cite Book|The Black Arrow, v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Eldenroot|Eldenroot]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} is one of the eight major cities in the province of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Valenwood towns.png|Map of Valenwood – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} situated in the southern heart of the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]]'s great forests, [[Lore:Grahtwood|Grahtwood]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadGraht|[[Online:Grahtwood|Grahtwood]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Not only is Elden Root the self-proclaimed "center of the Wood Elven nation",{{Ref|name=AR}} but it is also one of Valenwood's six major countries.{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Valenwood}}}} It is a vast hub of trade and commerce, where many wealthy merchants and officials retain large-scale operations.{{Ref|name=TBA1}} At the center of it all is one of the legendary [[Lore:Elden Tree|Elden Trees]], the titanic subspecies of the already enormous [[Lore:Graht-oak|graht-oak]] trees that laden the southeast.{{Ref|name=ONLoadGraht}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadRoot|[[Online:Elden Root|Elden Root]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Layout & Geography==
[[File:ON-place-Great Trunk Artisans Court.jpg|thumb|right|The Great Trunk Artisans Court]]
[[File:ON-place-Outside Inn.jpg|thumb|left|Outside Inn]]
The Elden Tree is one of the migratory graht-oak trees of Valenwood,{{Ref|name=ONLoadRoot}} but it has rooted itself in central Grahtwood, next to a large lake east of it. Most of the city itself is set throughout the branches and canopies of the Elden Tree, but there is also an outer district that runs along the eastern base of the tree, through the hills and the lake. The roots of the graht-oak run over the outer streets and even borough underneath the earth, but with the help of the local [[Lore:Greenspeakers|greenspeakers]], they also form long, wiry bridges over the lake and streets. They also create graht-oak tree pods, the typical one-room home of the Bosmer.{{Ref|name=ONSnug|[[Online:Snugpod|Snugpod]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Ayleid ruins that dot the landscape are spread out and completely inaccessible. A blocked entrance can be found on the island southeast of the tree, on the main road that runs through the city.{{Ref|name=ONElden|[[Online:Elden Root|Elden Root]] location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The terrain underneath the Elden Tree has been used for a several purposes, but more often than not as extra living space. The old Ayleid Ruins of Elden Hollow were once used by noble Ayleid clans as a living quarter before it was converted into a cemetery for [[Lore:Centaur|centaur]]s and [[Lore:Minotaur|minotaur]]s.{{Ref|name=ONHollowI|[[Online:Elden Hollow I|Elden Hollow]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONDracien|[[Online:Dracien Montue|Dracien Montue]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} There is a cave system directly underneath the Elden Tree called the Middens, where the lowlife and malcontent conduct their business or hide away from the law.{{Ref|name=ONElden}}{{Ref|name=ONAzareth|[[Online:Azareth|Azareth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Elden Root is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Archen Cormount|Archen Cormount]] to the south-southwest, [[Lore:Lynpar March|Lynpar March]] to the east, {{Lore Link|Meadow Run}} to the west, [[Lore:Moonmont|Moonmont]] to the north, and [[Lore:Wasten Brukbrook|Wasten Brukbrook]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

The first floor of the tree is a vast and hollowed-out hall, centered around the local [[Lore:Wayshrines|wayshrine]]. Many merchants and artisans set their shops in the southern end of the hall and the hill toward the tree, while the people tend to gather and converse in the middle. The north end has a small pond and meditation spot where the local [[Lore:Spinners|spinners]] are located and tell stories to anyone interested.{{Ref|name=ONGirnarlin|[[Online:Girnalin|Girnalin]] NPC in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The west end is the hallway that ascends higher up the tree, to the second floor, the city's great marketplace known as the Great Trunk Artisans Court. This area is adorned with vines and garlands with either glowing blue or yellow flowers, and at the center of it all is a quaint blue tree. The area is also paved and assorted with stones, from the floor, to the sturdy stone wall that holds the incline to the Throne Room, and the stone circles and menhirs that decorate the area. {{Ref|name=ONElden}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:ON-interior-The Orrery.jpg|thumb|right|The Orrery of Elden Root]]
[[File:ON-place-Elden Hollow.jpg|thumb|left|The Elden Hollow]]
The Elden Tree that served as the basis of the settlement is one of the many graht-oaks trees known collectively as the [[Lore:Green-Sap|Green-Sap]], which grew from the {{Lore Link|Perchance Acorn}}.{{Ref|name=AE4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} The settlement was initially occupied by an ancient tribe of Wood Elves ruled by the Treethane, that lived independent from the other tribes until King [[Lore:Eplear|Eplear]] founded the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] in {{Year|1E 0}} and annexed every tribe under his newfound Empire.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} Much like other settlements based in the graht-oak trees, Elden Root migrated throughout the forest once every season and with the [[Lore:Ayleid Diaspora|Ayleid Diaspora]] in {{Year|1E 243}}, saw the arrival of [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] refugees from [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{Ref|name=AE4TET}}

Among the travelers that came to Elden Root was King [[Lore:Anumaril|Anumaril]] of [[Lore:Abagarlas|Abagarlas]], who carried a segment of his [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]] as a femur. Anumaril sought to re-create the [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire]] and the majesty of the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] with the Green-Sap of Elden Root. But despite his agreement to not disturb the Green, he met with the Great Camoran, who showed him the Perchance Acorn. He placed the segment in the roots and created the Definite Acorn, which in turn rooted Elden Root in central Grahtwood. His last known creation was the Orrery of Elden Root, where he placed the segment-scepter between the moons. However, because of Anumaril's lack of understanding Bosmeri magic, nothing happened and his plans failed. But as the historian, {{Lore Link|Beredalmo the Signifier}} puts it in the mid-Second Era, "The Ayleid fangler's plan could not succeed—and yet neither could it fail. For this is a story that has not yet found its ending."{{Ref|name=AE4TET}}

The Ayleids that lived in Elden Root expanded on the Elden Tree and built universities and libraries.{{Ref|name=ACOV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} Some of the more important Ayleid clans were given the right to excavate underneath the Elden Tree and build dwelling halls. That section later became known as Elden Hollow,{{Ref|name=ONHollowI}} and it was later used as a large tomb for generations of centaurs and minotaurs before it was abandoned.{{Ref|name=ONDracien}} All of Valenwood continued underneath the Camoran reign for many years until the province's defeat against the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] in {{Year|1E 2714}}. As a contingency plan for a possible uprising, the Empire granted independence to each of the province's treethanes, so that they may never unite against a common foe. Elden Root was among five other countries that turned from a local trading post into a fully-fledged political power in its own right.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} Elden Root was named the capital of what was then called, the Kingdom of Grahtwood.{{Ref|name=ONLoadGraht}} The known scope of the kingdom ranged from the town of [[Lore:Cormount|Cormount]],{{Ref|name=TROC|{{Cite Book|The Rise of Cormount}}}} all the way to the [[Lore:Long Coast|Long Coast]]. The outer reaches of the geographical region like [[Lore:Reman's Bluff|Reman's Bluff]] were beyond its borders.{{Ref|name=TFOS|{{Cite Book|The Founding of Southpoint}}}}

===The Camoran Dynasty and Elden Root===
[[File:ON-npc-King Camoran Aeradan.jpg|thumb|left|King Camoran Aeradan]]
[[File:ON-npc-Gelthior.jpg|thumb|right|Camoran Gelthior]]
With the dissolution of the Second Empire in {{Year|2E 431}}, the Camoran Dynasty tried once again to reinstate their power, but because of how culturally divided the other nations were, the possibility of unification was not foreseeable. Because of this, each of the different treethane kingdoms waged war with each other and saw invasions coming from their neighbors such as the [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]ns and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]].{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} After they sacked both [[Lore:Haven|Haven]] and [[Lore:Woodhearth|Woodhearth]] on the coast, the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] marched inland and besieged not only Elden Root, but the Elden Tree itself.{{Ref|name=ACOV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} When the Elden Tree of [[Lore:Falinesti|Falinesti]] vanished without a trace, it left Elden Root as the seat of the Camoran Dynasty. The [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] scholar, {{Lore Link|Andewen}} dates the city's disappearance roughly around {{Year|2E 382}} and {{Year|2E 432|432}}.{{Ref|name=ONAndewen|[[Online:Andewen|Andewen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

By the fifth century, the city was ruled by King [[Lore:Aeradan Camoran|Aeradan Camoran]]. His ascension to the throne was contested by his cousin, {{Lore Link|Camoran Gelthior}} of Cormount. The King sought to "renew" the land and the philosophy of the Bosmer people, however, Gelthior believed that the people deserved a ruler that understands the ancient compacts of the [[Lore:Green Pact|Green Pact]]. A civil war broke out between Aeradan's loyalist and the {{Lore Link|Blacksap Movement}} in {{Year|2E 489}}.{{Ref|name=TROC}}{{Ref|name=ONGelthior|[[Online:Gelthior|Gelthior]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The war lasted for roughly a century{{Ref|name=AUA:AD1|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Aldmeri Dominion (Part 1)|Ask Us Anything: Aldmeri Dominion (Part 1) –'' The Elder Scrolls Online'' Website]]}} and in its latter-half saw the involvement of the Colovians, who were in favor of the Blacksap.{{Ref|name=ONErvalir|[[Online:Ervalir|Ervalir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  Aeradan was facing a losing battle and decided to call for support from Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] in {{Year|2E 580}}. Their combined forces were able to crush the Blacksap and send them back into Cormount.{{Ref|name=AUA:AD1}}

With their newfound friendship, both parties had created the First [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]],{{Ref|name=AUA:AD1}} along with the Khajiit of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]. King Aeradan and Queen Ayrenn, along with the Speaker of the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]], {{Lore Link|Gharesh-ri}} signed the {{Lore Link|Elden Accord}}, which solidified their alliance. The Dominion proclaimed Elden Root as their capital,{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn|[[Online:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=AUA:AD2|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Aldmeri Dominion (Part 2)|Ask Us Anything: Aldmeri Dominion (Part 2) – ''The Elder Scrolls Online'' Website]]}} and built the Altmer Embassy for citizens of the alliance, with the promise of a Khajiit Embassy in {{Year|2E 581}}.{{Ref|name=ONCamoran|[[Online:King Camoran Aeradan|King Camoran Aeradan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Prince [[Lore:Naemon|Naemon]] was placed in Elden Root as Queen Ayrenn's representative in the court, if she was ever away.{{Ref|name=ONNaemon|[[Online:Prince Naemon#The Honor of the Queen|Prince Naemon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Elden Root in the Aldmeri Dominion===
[[File:ON-place-Altmer Embassy.jpg|thumb|left|The Altmer Embassy in Elden Root]]
[[File:ON-creature-Prince Naemon.jpg|thumb|right|Prince Naemon the Ogrim]]
After a year, the Khajiit Embassy was never built and it caused a stir among the people in Elden Root.{{Ref|name=ONCamoran}} The project was headed by Ambassador {{Lore Link|Tarinwe}} of the Altmer Embassy, and Prince Naemon sent an [[Lore:The Vestige|Agent]] of the Dominion to investigate the situation. The ambassador claimed that supplies were ready for the Khajiit, but upon a closer look, the supplies were practically useless, much to the confusion of the Prince. The Agent traveled to the nearby ruins, where stonemasons were supposed to acquire materials, but discovered dead bodies laden across the mire. The King was also suspicious of the Ambassador, and sent one of the [[Lore:Vinedusk Rangers|Vinedusk Rangers]], {{Lore Link|Azareth}} to investigate. The two uncovered a conspiracy against the Dominion and that Tarinwe made an attempt to escape via the Middens underneath the Elden Tree. She was captured and put on trial for her actions.{{Ref|name=ONHonor|[[Online:The Honor of the Queen|The Honor of the Queen]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Not long after the situation, Queen [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]] traveled to Elden Root to begin the ratification ceremony, which would divine her capacity to rule as Empress. But in order to go forward with the process, the ceremony needed three items of importance, a "divine spark," an Ayleid source of power, and a skilled operator. The Agent traveled to separate corners of the region, to the west, they traveled to the {{Lore Link|Falinesti Winter Site}}, where they acquired the "divine spark," {{Lore Link|Rajhin's Mantle}}. To the northeast, they traveled to the {{Lore Link|Reliquary of Stars}}, where they would locate the {{Lore Link|Heart of Anumaril}}, which originally brought life to the Orrery. Finally, they traveled to the southwest, to the town of [[Lore:Southpoint|Southpoint]], where the Conservator, {{Lore Link|Daraneth}} was located.{{Ref|name=ONRule|[[Online:Fit to Rule|Fit to Rule]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With every component in hand, the ratification ceremony started as scheduled, but before the Queen would enter the Orrery, Naemon entered first and was transformed into an [[Lore:Ogrim|Ogrim]]. He was slain by the Agent and despite the death of her brother, Ayrenn continued with the ceremony and received a vision of the Imperial City and Tamriel, at peace under the Aldmeri Dominion.{{Ref|name=ONOrrery|[[Online:The Orrery of Elden Root|The Orrery of Elden Root]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ayrenn's rule had solidified further, but when the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]] was stolen from [[Lore:Marbruk|Marbruk]], she sent the Agent to locate it.{{Ref|name=ONMagnus|[[Online:The Staff of Magnus|The Staff of Magnus]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In the latter years of the Interregnum, the tribes of Valenwood were once again divided and left to their own devices. But the arrival of the Second Aldmeri Dominion saw the unification of the province again.{{Ref|name=PGE3E}} Because of the moving nature of Falinesti, the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] decided to move the capital of the province to Elden Root.{{Ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Aldmeri Dominion}}}}

===Elden Root in the Third Empire===
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Eldenroot was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Ulandra and it had a rivalry with several cities (namely [[Lore:Greenheart|Greenheart]], [[Lore:Silvenar|Silvenar]], and [[Lore:Southpoint|Southpoint]]).{{Ref|name=AR}} At around that time, the province saw radical changes that made it unsafe for travelers and after two months of investigation, Elden Root rangers were decapitated and their heads were presented to the Queen by an emissary of [[Lore:Selene|Selene]], High Priestess of [[Lore:Shagrath|Shagrath]]. The Priestess gave the Queen an ultimatum, where if they did not surrender Valenwood, they would all die.{{Ref|name=ARUlandra|[[Arena:Queen Ulandra|Queen Ulandra]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} At that same time, the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] had made their way toward Eldenroot after they acquired their second piece of the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]], in the hopes of obtain the third piece in the [[Lore:Elden Grove|Elden Grove]]. The Champion made contact with the Queen, who offered the location's whereabouts, only if the warrior defeated Selene in her domain, [[Lore:Selene's Web|Selene's Web]] and obtain her heart, a cloudy white, opaque diamond. After the threat had been vanquished, Queen Ulandra marked Elden Grove on their map, and the traveler continued with their quest.{{Ref|name=ARElden|[[Arena:Elden Grove|Elden Grove]] story quest in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Ever since the Champion's victory over [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]], Imperial-loyalists would re-trace their steps across the provinces, and occasionally made their way through Eldenroot.{{Ref|name=AMM|{{Cite Book|A Minor Maze}}}}
