The [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]], also known as the '''Guild of Mages''', was founded by [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] circa {{Year|2E 230}}.{{ref|name=MGC}}{{Ref|name=TDC|''[[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles|The Daggerfall Chronicles]]''}}{{rp|6}}{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}} It was a professional organization once found throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] which was dedicated to the study and application of [[Lore:Magic|magicka]] and [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]]. They were described as a collection of mages, sorcerers, and mystics, who pooled their resources and talents for the purposes of research and public charity.{{ref|name=OMG}} The [[Lore:Aurbic Eye|Aurbic Eye]] was the official symbol of the Guild.{{Ref|name=OblivionMG|[[Oblivion:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] rank symbols in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

The Mages Guild was a powerful force in Tamriel prior to its dissolution, given free rein to operate across the land by Tamrielic rulers. A dangerous foe and aloof ally, it rarely became involved in political conflicts, but was profoundly influential when it did.{{ref|name=OMG}}<noinclude>

==Organization==
[[File:OBR-place-Chorrol Mages Guild.jpg|A Guild hall|thumb|left]][[File:OBR-npc-Mage Apprentice Breton.jpg|Apprentices attending a lecture at the Arcane University|thumb|right]]
The Mages Guild was led by an '''Arch-Mage''', and guided by the '''Council of Mages''', made up of six Archmagisters (one of them the Arch-Mage). By the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the Arch-Mage and the Council of Mages were headquartered at the Guild's [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]] in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], which the Guild held out as the foremost magical research center in Tamriel ever since its founding in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=Traven}} The Guild had a mandate to establish laws on the proper use of magic,{{Ref|name=MGC|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}} and the Council decided important Guild policies, such as regulations on [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]], the sale of [[Lore:Spells|spells]] in a local Guild Hall, and how Guild law would be enforced. They also administered recruitment. However, local Guilds demonstrated some discretion in their rulemaking, leading to sometimes dramatic differences. Guild halls existed in most of Tamriel's cities, and each was run by a local Guild Magister (alternatively known as a Hall Magister). Below them were the Master of Incunabula (alternatively called the High Incunabulist),{{ref|name=KRatMG|{{Cite Book|Kinlord Rilis and the Mages Guild}}}} keeper of the guildhall's tomes,{{ref|name=Valaste|[[Online:Valaste|Valaste]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the Master at Arms. The Master of Incunabula had a counsel of two, the Master of Academia and the Master of the Scrye. The Master at Arms also had a counsel, the Master of Initiates and the Palatinus (the leader of the local chapter of the [[Lore:Order of the Lamp|Order of the Lamp]]).{{ref|name=OMG|{{Cite Book|Origin of the Mages Guild}}}} The Mages Guild had a system for lodging complaints, such as for the misattribution of credit among Guild members.{{ref|name=Largesse|[[Online:The Largesse of the Archmagister|The Largesse of the Archmagister]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Candidates for membership had to present themselves to the Steward of a Guild Hall for examination and approval. The [[Lore:Mages Guild Charter|Guild's charter]] outlined rules of conduct between members; crimes against fellow members were treated with the harshest discipline, and whether the perpetrator could regain their former status in the Guild was determined by the Arch-Mage. Starting from {{Year| 3E 431}}, any crime committed against the Guild resulted in an immediate suspension of Guild membership that could be lifted at the discretion of the Steward of the Council of Mages. Multiple suspensions could result in permanent dismissal from the Guild.{{Ref|name=MGC}} The Guild also had rules about fraternization and lewd advances.{{ref|name=LC|[[Online:Khara-ko|Khara-ko]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In Third Era [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], members would be given magical [[Lore:Staff|staves]], which were considered important status symbols.{{ref|name=Raminus|[[Oblivion:Raminus Polus|Raminus Polus]]' dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=Staff|Events of [[Oblivion:A Mage's Staff|A Mage's Staff]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} Advancement through the ranks would be met with various rewards, such as powerful spells.{{ref|name=Raminus}}{{ref|name=Plot|Events of [[Oblivion:A Plot Revealed|A Plot Revealed]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} When needed for Guild business, members were sometimes also freely provided Guild resources, such as [[Lore:Scrolls|magic scrolls]].{{ref|name=KudEi|[[Oblivion:Kud-Ei|Kud-Ei]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=Carahil|[[Oblivion:Carahil|Carahil]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=Anvil|Events of [[Oblivion:Anvil Recommendation|Anvil Recommendation]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}

The position of "Chronicler" was a somewhat informal title within the Mages Guild hierarchy, passed between Adepts with a penchant for the "identification and illumination of ancient secrets". Their duties involved selling arcane goods, which made some question if it was actually a "fancy title for a clerk".{{ref|name=Chronicler|[[Online:Chronicler Firandil|Chronicler Firandil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Chroniclers were known to sometimes compile and copy ancient texts for analysis and dissemination through the Guild.{{ref|name=Largesse}} The Guild would provide preservation spells and cleaning supplies so ancient tomes in their custody could be maintained.{{ref|name=FLttMG|{{Cite Book|First Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|ns_base=ON}}}}{{ref|name=SLttMG|{{Cite Book|Second Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|ns_base=ON}}}}{{ref|name=TLttMG|{{Cite Book|Third Letter to the Mages Guild, Draft Copy|ns_base=ON}}}} Some Guild members were tasked with keeping an adequate supply of magical foci, such as staves, on hand for any mages that required them.{{ref|name=Tharja|[[Online:Tharja|Tharja]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-crown store-Mages Guild Sentry Cat.jpg|Mages Guild sentry cat (ESO)|A Mages Guild Sentry Cat|thumb|right]]
The Mages Guild had a variety of means of communication. Conjured [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] such as [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] might be dispatched to deliver messages to other organizations, such as the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Watch]].{{ref|name=Misdirection|Events of [[Oblivion:Misdirection|Misdirection]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=Hieronymus|[[Oblivion:Hieronymus Lex|Hieronymus Lex]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} Mages Guild Message Stones could conjure projections of the sender to communicate with the recipient, while Summoning Medallions allowed the recipient to directly summon the sender to their location.{{ref|name=MGMS|Use of Mages Guild Message Stone and Summoning Medallion during [[Online:Through a Veil Darkly|Through a Veil Darkly]] in [[Online:Online|Online]]}}

Some guildhalls were known to be protected with magical wards.{{ref|name=Ohhendel|[[Online:Ohhendel|Ohhendel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Guild was also known to often utilize Mages Guild Sentry Cats as a security measure, leading to them being a common sight in guild halls. The keen senses of these cats enabled them to detect all manner of intruders, even those that were magically concealed or from realms of Oblivion.{{Ref|name=MGSC|[[Online:Mages Guild Sentry Cat|Mages Guild Sentry Cat]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Services==
[[File:OBR-misc-Spellmaking Altar.jpg|A Spellmaking Altar|thumb|left]]
The Mages Guild Charter from the Emperor specified that the Guild must provide magic services to the public. Anyone could purchase potions, alchemical ingredients, magical items, and a selection of standard spells from the Guild. However, training, goods, and services were cheaper for members, and the Guild stewards were sometimes able to provide members with work. Services deemed potentially dangerous to the public at large, such as spellmaking and [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanting]], were only made available to higher-ranked members in good standing.{{Ref|name=MGC}}{{ref|name=MoS}} The Guild was known to sell standardized, ready-made spells that could be cast even without understanding of the underlying magical theory, which came to be the usual manner in which spells were acquired. However, such spells were generally not as powerful as those cast by a mage with real knowledge.{{ref|name=BW|{{Cite Book|Breathing Water}}}} Spells available for purchase were distributed across the various halls according to the Mages Guild Charter, with each specializing in a specific school of magic.{{Ref|name=MGC}} One could also pay the Guild to replace the enchantment on a magical item.{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} The Guild also evaluated weapons and items brought to them for their magical potential.{{ref|name=WFDF}} The Guild got most of the business during the [[Lore:Witches Festival|Witches Festival]], since weapons and items were evaluated for their mystic potential free of charge and magic spells were only one half their usual price during the holiday. Magic scrolls were also discounted.{{ref|name=WFDF|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Frost_Fall|Witches Festival]] description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}<noinclude> 

The Mages Guild is credited with making magic, once restricted to the aristocracy, intelligentsia, and solitary covens and mages, much more available to the public.{{ref|name=OMG}} Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, itemmakers, and spellmakers were originally codified by the Guild's founder [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]], who is credited with devising the schools of magic so they would be comprehensible by the masses, as well as inventing common tools of alchemy and enchanting so everyone could safely perform magic, making him the progenitor of modern magic in Tamriel. Over time, Guild restrictions on magical services would change along with risk assessments.{{ref|name=Feyfolken|{{Cite Book|Feyfolken}}}} The Guild was sometimes asked to provide more obscure magical services, such as using [[Lore:Stone Magic|Stone Magic]] to reshape structures.{{ref|name=Laurina|[[ON:Laurina Alextus|Laurina Alextus]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-misc-Group Teleportation.jpg|Mages Guild performing a group teleportation ritual|thumb|left]]
The Mages Guild were Tamriel's leading archaeologists, and were somewhat notorious for their zealous pursuits of a variety of [[Lore:Artifacts|artifacts]], such the [[Lore:Guardian's Eye|Guardian's Eye]].{{ref|name=Tharayya|[[Online:Tharayya|Tharayya]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Guild was willing to steal to get such artifacts on occasion,{{ref|name=DFTLA|[[Daggerfall:The Lost Artifact|The Lost Artifact]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and some suspected they were willing to go further than that.{{ref|name=Tharayya}} The Guild was known to maintain vaults of magical artifacts. Dangerous relics were sequestered in such vaults under heavy arcane wards, but access could be granted to qualified guild members for research purposes, following a formal request.{{ref|name=History}}{{ref|name=Proposal}}
[[File:OBR-npc-Thalfin.jpg|A Mages Guild Battlemage|thumb|right]]
Before its disbandment, one could purchase instant teleportation between the regional halls. This service was available to members and non-members alike, though members were generally given a significant discount.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Erranil|Erranil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} It also had a subsidiary known as the Travelers League, only available to high-ranking members, which could teleport someone from anywhere in the world. This League was present in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] during the fifth century of the Third Era.{{Ref|name=DFTravelers|[[Daggerfall:Travelers League|Travelers League]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Another Mages Guild subsidiary of this time and place was the Cabal, composed of [[Lore:Conjuration|conjurers]] who'd provide Guild members of sufficient rank the service of summoning the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] on their Summoning Days.{{Ref|name=DFSummoning|[[Daggerfall:Daedra Summoning|Cabal Daedra Summoning]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Mages Guild often employed [[Lore:Portals|portals]] designed to move people and material quickly, which were less draining than personal teleportation spells and made it possible for many people to travel en masse.{{ref|name=LaloriaranCitadel|[[ON:King Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Guild would also use Portal Anchors, arcane devices that stabilize portals for a longer duration when needing to transport groups.{{ref|name=PortalAnchor|[[Online:Seasons of the Worm Cult Quest Items|Portal Anchor]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Stirk|[[Online:The Stirk Fellowship|The Stirk Fellowship]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Guild often intervened upon reports of dangerous magical practices, either at the behest of the Council of Mages, or by order of the local guild hall heads. 
A rogue mage killing traveling merchants or a necromancer coven might draw a single Guild investigator or warrant an entire contingent of battlemages.{{ref|name=Ambush|Events of [[Oblivion:Ambush|Ambush]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} Members might be asked to infiltrate rogue magical organizations or slay a necromancer attempting to ascend to [[Lore:Lich|lichdom]].{{ref|name=Carahil}}{{ref|name=Anvil}}{{ref|name=Traven|[[Oblivion:Hannibal Traven|Hannibal Traven]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=Liberation|Events of [[Oblivion:Liberation or Apprehension?|Liberation or Apprehension?]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} Accounts by members of the Guild's Order of the Lamp describe being sent on missions involving fighting [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], raging [[Lore:Elemental Daedra|Atronachs]], and massive armies of the [[Lore:Undeath|undead]] summoned by necromancers.{{ref|name=VLAttS|{{Cite Book|Votary Llaren's Addition to the Scholarium}}}}

Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, the bodyguard of the [[Lore:Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel|Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel]] was a member of the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=Evangeline|[[Oblivion:Evangeline Beanique|Evangeline Beanique]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}

==History==
[[File:LG-cardart-Mages Guild Retreat.png|thumb|right|Mages Guild Retreat (Legends)]]
===Second Era===
[[File:OBR-statue-Vanus Galerion.jpg|thumb|left|Vanus Galerion, founder of the Guild]]
The Mages Guild was founded by [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] circa {{Year|2E 230}}.{{ref|name=MGC}}{{Ref|name=TDC|''[[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles|The Daggerfall Chronicles]]''}}{{rp|6}}{{Ref|name=XanTimeline|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Xanathar's Library Archive/History|ns_base=General}}}} Study of magic had been dominated by many separate, isolated groups, such as the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] (of which Galerion was a member), which had begun falling out of grace with society. Its policy of isolationism led to mistrust, and its religion (best described as a type of [[Lore:Ancestor Worship|ancestor worship]]) was an increasingly unfashionable philosophy. With the formation of the Mages Guild, Galerion sought to both unify the community of mages in the study of magic as well as spread to the public the benefits of the magical arts.{{ref|name=OMG}} Galerion named the top leadership position the Arch-Mage in memory of [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]], the first recorded mage to claim the title.{{ref|[[Online:Glade of the Divines|Glade of the Divines]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The idea of mages, sorcerers, and assorted mystics pooling their resources for the purpose of research and public charity was a revolutionary concept in the early Second Era, and elicited a great deal of animosity against Galerion. In the "Charter Conclave" of 2E 230, he was brought to explain himself to his former mentor [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]], the King of [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]] [[Lore:Rilis XII|Rilis XII]], and other notable figures.{{ref|name=KRatMG}}{{ref|name=MKOW|{{Cite Book|Mannimarco, King of Worms}}}} While Galerion's speech was not recorded for posterity (creating much scholarly interest into what rhetoric Galerion must have employed), the charter was approved.{{ref|name=OMG}} The fearful commonfolk had been urging their king to settle the issue of "Galerion's Folly" (as it was known at the time), but instead Kinlord Rilis became a sort of co-founder of the Guild. Some posit he saw Galerion's plan as a way to further his own ends, as he later made pacts with Daedra and demonstrated sadistic tendencies. Before his cruelty became well-known, the common folk would believe that the Kinlord had made a deal with "dangerous mages", a misconception the Guild hastily sought to rectify.{{ref|name=KRatMG}}

The passing of the [[Lore:Guild Act|Guild Act]] of {{Year|2E 321}} would further legitimize the Mages Guild by sanctioning its presence within the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{ref|name=MGC}}{{Ref|name=HotFG|{{Cite Book|History of the Fighters Guild}}}}

====Postformation====
[[File:ON-item-armor-Order of the Lamp Style.jpg|thumb|left|Order of the Lamp Armor]][[File:ON-npc-Vanus Galerion 03.jpg|Arch-Mage Vanus Galerion|thumb|right]]
Immediately following the formation of the Mages Guild, security concerns emerged. Initially hiring guards, Vanus Galerion realized what the Tamrielic nobility had known for centuries: money alone did not buy loyalty. This led Galerion to form the knightly Order of the Lamp.{{ref|name=OMG}} The signing of the Guild Act also soon allowed for a great proliferation of knowledge on exotic materials. The Guild is credited with discovering how to refine magical ores to create weapons and armor surpassing even those made of [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] in their quality.{{Ref|name=MAMA|{{Cite Book|Master Assistant Materials Acquirer Pacrooti Answers Your Questions}}}}

Before Mages Guild chapter houses had spread to the mainland of Tamriel, Galerion crafted extraordinarily powerful tools of enchanting and alchemy. These original tools were more automated, essentially providing the layperson without any understanding of magic the capabilities of a master enchanter and alchemist for any purpose, so long as they could pay for it. Even an enchanted sword that could "cleave the world in twain" is said to have been theoretically possible to make through these original tools, obstructed only by enormous cost in magicka and gold. This level of accessibility is said to have resulted in a number of incidents involving laypeople wielding dangerous arcane objects well beyond their ken, which led Galerion to have the originals destroyed and replaced by less powerful versions requiring more expertise from the user.{{ref|name=Feyfolken}}

The Guild gradually gained footholds in mainland Tamriel. Many superstitious and fearful rulers banned them from their lands, but their descendants would realize the benefits of allowing Guild operations in their territory. While the Mages Guild strove for neutrality in political conflicts, on rare occasions its participation has been the ultimate decider, and it proved to be a dangerous foe to its enemies.{{ref|name=OMG}} The Guild was a boon for the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]], who are known for their innate magical ability and divisive political culture. Urban Bretons had their children were tested for magical potential at an early age and systematically organized alongside the Mages Guild hierarchy. Those with talent entered apprenticeship programs funded by the Mages Guild or independent sponsorships.{{ref|name=PGE1High|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} A notable Breton alumni is [[Lore:Gyron Vardengroet|Gyron Vardengroet]], who discovered the secret to immortality through alchemy.{{ref|name=TS|{{Cite Book|The Sage}}}} However, rural areas of High Rock often remained under the sway of shamans and covens.{{ref|name=PGE1High}} 

As only [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] were permitted to be on the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]], Mages Guild halls there consisted of only High Elves until [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] opened the borders to the other races.{{ref|{{Cite Book|An End to Isolation}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Dessathiel|Dessathiel]] and [[Online:Ohlinonde|Ohlinonde]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]] in Summerset viewed the Mages Guild's as beneath them, possessing a more limited understanding of magic than their own, and hindered the Guild's activities in [[Lore:Lillandril|Lillandril]]. Members of the local Mages Guild believed this was attitude was due to fear and racial animosity.{{ref|[[Online:Mages Guild (Lillandril)|Lillandril Mages Guild]] resident's dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Early on in the Second Era, [[Lore:Felix Augustus|Felix "Blackcaster" Augustus]] and his followers split from the Mages Guild to practice their arts in [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]], where they opened a mage's academy. While some considered them hedge mages rebelling against the authority of the Mages Guild, the [[Lore:Blackcasters|Blackcasters]] objected to the claim that they were renegades. They asserted their endeavor had nothing to do with any disagreements with the Guild, but was instead motivated by a desire to explore territories beyond the Guild's reach, and that Felix Blackcaster remained a Mages Guild member in high standing who kept in contact with his superiors in the Mages Guild.{{Ref|name=TFE|{{Cite Book|The Flourishing of Elinhir}}}} Despite this, members of the Mages Guild did seem to view the Blackcasters as renegades.{{ref|name=ML|[[Online:Minerva Lauzon|Minerva Lauzon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Supreme Power|''Supreme Power'']]}}

====Mannimarco Gains Influence====
Seeking to normalize necromancy and gain a following to his discipline, [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] created his [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]] to rival the ascendant Mages Guild,{{ref|name=TOTTBW|{{Cite Book|The Order of the Black Worm}}}}{{ref|name=WS|{{Cite Book|Worm Saga}}}} and the two organizations have warred since the Guild's founding.{{ref|name=EU|[[Online:Ellina Urbyn|Ellina Urbyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  Vanus Galerion believed Mannimarco was responsible for the spell to trap a [[Lore:Souls|soul]] becoming common knowledge among mages, leading to strictures in the Guild against soul-trapping sentient mortals. Due to the readily available presence of [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gems]] in nearly every bazaar of Tamriel, Galerion grudgingly co-opted the practice in an effort to control its use. Since the art was not a part of the core curriculum of the Guild, being taught only to the most experienced and dependable wizards for specific uses, Galerion codified and systematized the various practices of soul-trapping magics into a grimoire of a few, certain spells to be taught to students. Souls were classified into two classes; the legal, "White" souls consisted of smaller essences from beasts or animals; the illegal, "Black" souls were derived from sentient mortals. Only spells that would capture the legal "White" souls were taught.{{ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} In time, it became impossible for standard soul trap spells taught to capture a Black Soul within a regular [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gem]], instead requiring the use of the more dangerous and elusive Black Soul Gems.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Souls, Black and White}}}}

At some point following the Guild's formation, [[Lore:Vastarie|Vastarie]], a necromancer who had been a disciple in the Psijic Order alongside Galerion and had worked with Mannimarco to retrieve the first Black Soul Gems from [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], attempted to present the [[Lore:Sigil Geode|Sigil Geodes]] she'd invented to the fledgling Guild. The geodes allowed for a soul to be captured upon the moment of death and then be interrogated for their knowledge at the mage's leisure. The soul could then be released or moved between crystals at will without the need of enchantment. The demonstration was attended by a number of students and Galerion himself, but when it ended, it is said that Galerion's face was white with horror. He delivered a furious and vindictive speech condemning the practice and stormed out, with some students following him.{{ref|name=TLoV|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Vastarie}}}}

====The Interregnum====
While the Guild maintained a strict hierarchy, many knew that this masked severe disagreements. Galerion eventually left the Guild in disgust, claiming it had become "nothing more than an intricate morass of political infighting."{{ref|name=OMG}} Despite this, Galerion still held the venerable title of Archmagister Emeritus, and influenced many Guild affairs.{{Ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} His hiatus would last for over a century, but he would eventually return to his duties as the Archmagister due to the crisis that would follow the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] in {{Year|2E 578}}.{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} Mannimarco and his cult made a flurry of political actions to gain power,{{ref|name=MtcFE|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Father Egnatius}}}} converting Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]] to worshipping [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] and manipulating her into expelling the Mages Guild from the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] so that his Worm Cult could operate freely. Mannimarco effectively became the true power behind the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]].{{Ref|name=ONAbnurTharn|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CotFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}{{ref|name=AL|{{Cite Book|Anchorite's Log}}}} By framing the Mages Guild for the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]], Mannimarco was able to install his Worm Cult under the name Fellowship of Anchorites in the Arcane University following the Guild’s exile. He then removed several of the strictures formerly imposed by the Mages Guild, with the notable result that necromancy was legalized.{{ref|name=CotFC4}}{{ref|name=CATAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions 2}}}}

[[File:ON-load-Eyevea.jpg|thumb|left|Eyevea]]
While the Mages Guild got a foothold in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]],{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}} the province's [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] remained independent from it.{{ref|name=SrLS|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading Screens]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} From [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], the departed founder of the College of Winterhold, Shalidor, perceived a threat to his legacy, leading him to manifest on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}. The legacy which concerned him was not the College, but the island of [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]]. He had established as a retreat and sanctuary for all mages, but it was under threat by the trickster [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], so Shalidor enlisted the help of the Guild to save it. The Guild was ultimately able to re-establish a central headquarters on Eyevea.{{ref|name=Online|[[Online:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Guild member [[Lore:Sara Benele|Sara Benele]] played a pivotal role in preventing an explosion of magic from the obelisk that would have destroyed all of Craglorn.{{Ref|name=ONSaraBenele|[[Online:Sara Benele|Sara Benele]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Invasion of Coldharbour====

This negotiation, on the island of [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]], faced significant hurdles. After weathering an attack by Molag Bal's forces, the alliances did endorse action, but a smaller one than what Galerion had hoped for: the combined Mages Guild and [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] forces would invade Coldharbour.{{ref|name=Online}}{{ref|name=OnlineStirk|Events of [[Online:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Galerion|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion's]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====The Stirk Fellowship====
Sometime after the Planemeld, the Mages and Fighters Guilds were targeted by the resurrected Worm Cult. With the guild hall underneath [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] devastated, and the Fighters Guild leader [[Lore:Merric at-Aswala|Merric at-Aswala]] killed by a strange new device known as a [[Lore:Soul Reaper|Soul Reaper]], Mages Guild leader Vanus Galerion arrived at the city to accompany his compatriots to the Cult's hideout near the lighthouse west of [[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]]. There, they learned the Worm Cult planned to hide the Soul Reapers across Tamriel and then activate them to siphon souls at a massive scale, making them a top priority target to disable.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Resurrection and Revelation}}}} However, Vanus Galerion was abducted by the [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]] Mezamma disguised as trusted ally Skordo the Knife, who teleported him to the remote island of [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]]. Vanus's staff was left behind, which Gabrielle Benele retrieved to put it under safekeeping. With the two organizations now without their guildmasters, the guilds held a summit on Stirk to negotiate with the ambassadors of the Three Alliances for allies. They proposed the creation of Stirk Fellowship for the purpose of combating this new threat when Vanus projected himself out of his staff on Stirk and informed them of the danger the Soul Reapers posed.{{ref|[[ON:A Guild in Crisis|A Guild in Crisis]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Justice for the Fallen|Justice for the Fallen]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

With Gabrielle Benele and [[Lore:Azah|Prince Azah]] assuming the roles of acting guildmasters for the Mages Guild and Fighters Guild respectively, they sought to fully convince the Three Alliances to join their fight against the Worm Cult. Azah already had significant influence with the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], being the son of one of the Covenant's leaders, [[Lore:Fahara'jad|King Fahara'jad]].{{Ref|name=MTCA|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Prince Azah of Sentinel}}}} Gabrielle and Azah invited the [[Lore:Eyes of the Queen|Eyes of the Queen]] spymaster, [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], to inspect the devastated guild halls as well as their captured enemy correspondences. Razum-dar's investigations were fruitful, allowing him to deduce that the new leader of the Necromancers was a figure known as [[Lore:Wormblood|Wormblood]], who had close ties or possibly a blood relation to [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]. The evidence convinced the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] to combat the Worm Cult resurgence.{{Ref|name=MTCWB|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Wormblood}}}} Gabrielle concurrently entrusted the Argonian [[Lore:Walks-in-Ash|Walks-in-Ash]] with being the link to convince the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] to join the Stirk Fellowship, as they were a founding member of the Pact and also a combatant that fought alongside the Mages Guild during the Tamrielic Invasion of Coldharbour.{{Ref|name=MTCWA|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Walks-In-Ash}}}}

====The Final Clash of Mannimarco and Galerion====
Sometime after 2E 582,{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Lawrence Schick's Posts|Lawrence Schick's Posts]] discussing why Vanus and Mannimarco never interact during [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Vanus Galerion would confront the lich Mannimarco, his once childhood friend and orchestrator of the Planemeld, in a mountain pass with Mages Guild fighters and the Lamp Knights. As the two armies clashed, lightning and frost cascaded around them. The necromancers summoned various undead creatures to their sides which were battered by streams of light forged by the battlemages. It is said that "with but a touch" Galerion "shook the mountain to its base" and the undead horde fell. However, heeding their dying cries the King of Worms, Mannimarco, finally emerged. Galerion noticed, then, that Mannimarco was no longer alive: he had turned himself into a lich, thus gaining immortality. It is said Mannimarco opened his maw and exhaled a miasma of darkness over the mountain, causing all present to feel the touch of death. Eventually, the Worm King's power failed and his artifacts were ripped from him, ending the battle. For a long time Mannimarco was thought to have died in the battle. Although Galerion himself was killed, Mannimarco clung to his state of undeath, and the practice of necromancy would live on. Mannimarco's followers were either killed or scattered, and the triumphant army Galerion left behind secured his legacy and the legacy of the Mages Guild.{{ref|name=MGC}}{{ref|name=MKOW}}

===Third Era===
[[File:OBR-place-Arcane University.jpg|The Arcane University, headquarters of the Mages Guild, circa 3E 433 (Oblivion Remastered)|thumb|left]][[File:OBR-npc-Imperial Battlemage.jpg|An Imperial Battlemage on guard duty at the Arcane University|thumb|right]]

With the signing of the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] at the end of the Second Era, hostilities arose between [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] and the Mages Guild that would linger to the end of the Third Era. ==Teachings==
[[File:ON-crown store-Mages Guild Recall.jpg|Mages Guild Recall, transportation spell designed by the Guild that combines teleportation and illusion magic{{ref|name=Recall|[[ON:Mages Guild Recall|Mages Guild Recall]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}|thumb|left]][[File:LG-cardart-Mages_Guild_Recruit.png|thumb|right|Mages Guild Recruit (Legends)]]
Many of the greatest mages graduated from the Mages Guild. This was partly due to the Guild's prominence in Tamriel and open-ended recruitment policy. The Mages Guild encouraged all mages of varying skill and interest to join, and promoted an active regimen of practicing one's chosen spells to gain mastery over their specific school of magic.{{ref|name=MoS|{{Cite Book|Manual of Spellcraft}}}}

The Mages Guild taught its students to use magic in various ways, including the summoning of [[Lore:Elemental Daedra|Elemental Daedra]], [[Lore:Alchemy|Alchemy]], and Dark Magic. After several centuries, this aggregation of magical research and knowledge became haphazard and disorganized, and a new model based off the [[Lore:Shad Astula|Shad Astula Academy]]'s curricula was proposed by Gabrielle Benele around the time of the Three Banners War.{{ref|name=PSOM|{{Cite Book|Proposal: Schools of Magic}}}} Vanus Galerion sent Gabrielle a stack of rejection letters for this proposal,{{ref|name=WAA|{{Cite Book|What's an Arcanist?}}}} yet would later be credited for creating the schools of magic.{{ref|name=TBAOT}}

While the specific schools changed over the centuries, seven major schools of spell-casting and two other schools of arcane arts were taught extensively by the Mages Guild, providing students with a better structure to learn the concepts of wizardry in half the time of the old curricula.{{ref|name=PSOM}}

<li>[[Lore:Alteration|Alteration]] spells alter the physical and magical properties of the target, making objects heavier or lighter, granting elemental and physical shields, bestowing the ability to breath under and walk on water, and opening locks.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Conjuration|Conjuration]] spells call upon otherworldly entities through telepathy, and certain skilled Conjuration mages can develop telepathic links with each other. Conjuration spells augment the caster by granting them [[Lore:Lesser Daedra|Daedric]] and Undead guardians, Daedric weapons and armor, and the ability to repel the undead.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Destruction|Destruction]] spells harm the target by damaging its health with either elemental or magical attacks, draining and damaging its attributes, skills, health, magic, and fatigue, making it weak to the elements, poisons and magic, and corroding its armor and weapons.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Illusion|Illusion]] spells affect light and a sentient target's mind. They can harm the target by commanding, demoralizing, paralyzing, silencing, and causing it to frenzy, or augmenting it by rallying, charming, calming it, granting it invisibility, night-vision, translucency and illuminating it.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Mysticism|Mysticism]] is an obscure school, though its spells seem to manipulate magicka itself. With spells that bind the target's soul, this school is closely related to necromancy. Spells can augment the target by granting it the ability to detect life, reflect damage, absorb and reflect spells or harm it by dispelling its magical effects and trapping its soul. Spells can also move objects through space with telekinesis. The nature of the School of Mysticism is the subject of much scholarly debate.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Mysticism (book)}}}}</li>
<li>[[Lore:Restoration|Restoration]] spells augment the target by restoring its health, attributes, stamina, and magicka, fortifying its health, attributes, skills, stamina, and magicka, granting it resistances to the elements, magic, disease, paralysis, poison, and un-enchanted weapons, curing it of disease, poison and paralysis or harm the target by absorbing its health, magicka, stamina, attributes and skills.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Thaumaturgy|Thaumaturgy]] does not change the appearance or structure of a force or object, but can manipulate laws temporarily. This allows thaumaturges to perform acts which can only be achieved through brute force in other schools of magic, such as levitation, etherealness, detection, pacification, water walking, teleportation, summoning and the manipulation of other magical forces.{{ref|name=BS|[[Battlespire:Magic and Spells|''Battlespire'' Thaumaturgical Spells]]}}{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Thaumaturgy Spells|''Daggerfall'' Thaumaturgical Spells]]}}</li>
<li>[[Lore:Alchemy|Alchemy]] is the act of mixing, boiling and distilling various substances to obtain their chemo-magical properties and create potions and poisons.</li>
<li>[[Lore:Enchanting|Enchanting]] is the act of endowing objects with magical properties through the use of a soul, almost always with the use of a soul gem. The effects of enchanted apparel may augment the wearer, and the effects of enchanted weapons may harm the target.</li>
</ul>

Besides banning or discouraging necromancy,{{ref|name=TBAOT}} the Guild also outlawed "beastfolk magic", which they fear as dangerous wild hedge-wizardry commonly practiced by the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], who are said to have learned it from the [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]].{{ref|name=PGE1High}}{{ref|name=EGTB|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}}
