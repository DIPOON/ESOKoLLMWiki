# Lore:Mace of Molag Bal

When your enemies lie broken and bloody before you, know that I will be watching.''|[[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]}}[[File:ON-skill-Swing.jpg|Molag Bal wielding his mace|thumb|left]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Mace of Molag Bal|Mace of Molag Bal]], also known as the '''Vampire's Mace''', is a [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] attributed to [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], the Lord of Domination and Enslavement. Its enchantment drains the stamina and magicka of its victims and transfers them to the bearer. It also has been known to have the ability to transfer an enemy's strength to its wielder or trap their soul. It has been said to be a good weapon of choice for vanquishing wizards.{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}}{{ref|name=SR|Events of [[Skyrim:The House of Horrors|The House of Horrors]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Many legends surround the mace, but its origins can be traced back to when Molag Bal deceived an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]ish blacksmith and enslaved him in pits of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]]. The blacksmith was turned into a [[Lore:Soul Shriven|Soul Shriven]] and forced to forge the mace.{{ref|group=UOL|name=Schick|[http://youtu.be/sz6CbICIIgM?t{{=}}1m10s Video interview with Lawrence Schick (1:10)]}}{{ref|name=RM14D|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 14: Daedric Style}}}}

Due to Molag Bal's penchant for meddling in mortal affairs, he grants the mace to those he deems worthy but has also been quite free with his artifact. Molag Bal assumes that when such a powerful artifact falls into mortal hands, a trail of death and destruction is sure to follow.{{ref|name=TL}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=Schick}} Some believe the mace to be an object of Daedra worship.{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} Molag Bal's followers refer to it as '''the Master's Mace''',{{ref|name=BtMM|{{Cite Book|By the Master's Mace}}}}{{ref|name=ESOJavad|[[Online:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and often swear by it.{{ref|[[Online:Aelif|Aelif]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Will of the Council|Will of the Council]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}
 
==History==
[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended the sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing the Mace of Molag Bal among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Mace of Molag Bal and the other artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}

During Molag Bal's attempted [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}, he was witnessed wielding the mace himself, and utilized it while dueling the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]].{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] received the Mace of Molag Bal in exchange for eliminating a heretic mage.{{ref|name=DF|Events of [[Daggerfall:Molag Bal's Quest|Molag Bal's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} In {{Year|3E 427}}, the mace was also awarded to a supplicant who dispatched a lazy [[Lore:Daedroth|Daedroth]] minion named [[Lore:Menta Na|Menta Na]] for not wreaking the havoc and terror he was created for.{{ref|name=MW|Events of [[Morrowind:Molag Bal's Quest|Molag Bal's Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} During the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], Molag Bal spread corruption by having the [[Lore:Hero#Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] incite a local pacifist to murder; again, the mace was given as a reward.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Molag Bal|Molag Bal's quest]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

The artifact would later come into the possession of two distinct individuals from the Blades, both which went into hiding from the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] after the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Rise and Fall of the Blades}}}} One belonged to a party known as the [[Lore:Sheathed Blades|Sheathed Blades]], and they looted the Mace of Molag Bal sometime between {{Year|4E 176}} and {{Year|4E 201}} off the corpse of an Arch Necromancer they defeated in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|Arch Necromancer [[SkyrimTAG:Legendary Encounters|legendary encounter]] reward in the ''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}} The second was the [[Lore:Hero|Warrior]], who is thought to have looted the mace around {{Year|4E 180}} from an extravagant chest.{{ref|[[Blades:Artifacts|Artifact]] acquisition methods in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Molag Bal presented the mace to the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] for helping to capture the soul of a priest of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]].{{ref|name=SR}}
