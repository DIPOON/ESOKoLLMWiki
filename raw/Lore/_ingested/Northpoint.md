[[Lore:Northpoint|Northpoint]] (also spelled as '''North Point'''){{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:North Point|North Point]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} is one of the nine historical {{nbsl|Lore:Kingdoms of High Rock|Breton kingdoms}} in the province of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]],{{Ref|name=EWR|{{Cite Book|Etiquette With Rulers}}}}{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-High Rock towns.png|Map of High Rock – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} located on the northeast point of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]]. Northpoint is an important trading center along the northwest trading route on the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]], as well as the seat of power for [[Lore:House Dorell|House Dorell]], one of the three major houses of Rivenspire. Their power extends across northern Rivenspire, including farmland around the city and beyond the sea.{{Ref|name=NAA|{{Cite Book|Northpoint: An Assessment}}}}{{Ref|name=ONAlard|[[Online:Baron Alard Dorell|Baron Alard Dorell]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} As of {{Year|3E 432}}, Northpoint was one of five major kingdoms in High Rock.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/High Rock}}}}<noinclude>
{{NewLeft}}

==Layout and Geography==
Northpoint is located at a very strategic point in Rivenspire. It hosts the only port in the entire region, where the Eltheric Ocean meets the [[Lore:Great Bay|Great Bay]], and it is the last major settlement on Rivenspire's main road which circles around the southern face of the mountains. Northpoint sits at the top of [[Lore:Dore Elard|Dore Elard]], a mountain that overlooks the coast and provides a grand vista of the ocean and the beaches that surround it. There is a smuggler's tunnel underneath the mountain that leads straight to the lighthouse. The landscape around the city consists of grassy plains with privately-owned farmsteads and large rocky outcrops between the coast and the river in southern Boralis.{{Ref|name=ONNorth|[[Online:Northpoint|Northpoint]] location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} These farms were leased by House Dorell, becoming their second business endeavor after to sea trading.{{Ref|name=NAA}} The main road runs through these farmlands and around Northpoint's southwest wall to the coastline, where the city's port, Northsalt Village, is located. Beyond the village is a straight path to [[Lore:Westwind Lighthouse|Westwind Lighthouse]]. Northpoint is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Normar Heights|Normar Heights]] to the southwest.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

At the walled city's center is a large fountain and the seat of power, Dorell Manor. From there, Northpoint's three main roads disperse toward Lanthorn, the western gate, and the southern gate. South of Dorell Manor is the city's graveyard and along the southern walls are large towers. Some of the city's biggest buildings are located in the center, including the inn and the smithy, and further out are manorhouses and average homes. The marketplace is located along the northeastern wall of the city.{{Ref|name=ONNorth}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:ON-place-Northsalt Village.jpg|thumb|right|The original port at northpoint]]
[[File:ON-place-Dorell Manor.jpg|thumb|left|Dorell Manor]]
Northpoint was founded in the 9th century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] by [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] entrepreneur [[Lore:Yric Flowdys|Yric Flowdys]] during his routine summer trip between [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]. Yric made note of harborage on Rivenspire's coast, and the potential for its deep waters to accommodate large vessels. Believing it could serve as a waystation between the two coastal cities, for ships to restock, repair, or wait out storms, Yric constructed his harbor at Northpoint. Not long after, he oversaw the construction of a small walled keep and warehouse southeast of the port, on the peak of Dore Elard. Eventually, the town grew and Yric took on the mountain's name as his own. The newly christened House Dorell further developed their maritime endeavors and leased land beyond the town to farmers, establishing a new source of income.{{Ref|name=NAA}}

Over time, the town on the hill became known as Northpoint, and the port at Northpoint was renamed Northsalt Village.{{Ref|name=NAA}} When the province of High Rock was brought into the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] in {{Year|1E 1029}},{{Ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} House Dorell were granted a barony by Empress [[Lore:Hestra|Hestra]] brought great prosperity to the entire province throughout the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. The fortunes of both the House and Northpoint have constantly shifted with the flow of the northwest trade ever since. The fruits of their labor had eventually earned them the kingship of Rivenspire in the [[Lore:First Era#Twenty-Fourth Century|twenty-fourth century]] of the First Era, and ever since, House Dorell has been regarded as elite among the kingdom's nobility.{{Ref|name=NAA}}

===Northpoint in the Interregnum===
[[File:ON-npc-Lord Ellic Dorell.jpg|thumb|left|Lord Ellic Dorell]]
[[File:ON-npc-Lady Lleraya Montclair.jpg|thumb|right|Lady Lleraya Montclair]]
By the mid-sixth century of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], House Dorell was led by Baron [[Lore:Alard Dorell|Alard Dorell]],{{Ref|name=NAA}} and when King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]] of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] declared civil war against High King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]], House Dorell answered the call to arms and joined Rivenspire's armies against the south. After the death of the King and his lineage, however, the leaders of Rivenspire's three great houses assembled together to form a triumvirate called the [[Lore:Council of the North|Council of the North]], which ruled the kingdom in the interim.{{Ref|name=SCCOTN|{{Cite Book|Shornhelm, Crown City of the North}}}} Baron Dorell assumed the position along with Barons [[Lore:Esmark Tamrith|Esmark Tamrith]] and [[Lore:Wylon Montclair|Wylon Montclair]].{{Ref|name=NAA}}{{Ref|name=SCCOTN}}{{Ref|name=HTARH|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} While Alard lived in the family estate in Shornhelm, his son, [[Lore:Ellic Dorell|Ellic Dorell]] governed Northpoint and its environs from the family manor in town.{{Ref|name=NAA}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, there was a plot conducted by [[Lore:House Montclair|House Montclair]] to take over Rivenspire, and the Baron's daughter, Lady [[Lore:Lleraya Montclair|Lleraya Montclair]] had set her sights on Northpoint. She arrived in the city and met with Ellic Dorell, promising him help.{{Ref|name=ONEllic|[[Online:Lord Ellic Dorell|Lord Ellic Dorell]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She advised him to gather the city's people and as they gathered en masse, she used the [[Lore:Lightless Remnant|Lightless Remnant]] to turn them into [[Lore:Bloodfiend|bloodfiends]]. [[Lore:Qariar|Qariar]] helped some people escape to the Sloshing Tankard inn, but most of the city's citizens were turned into feral [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]], and Lady Montclair sealed Northpoint from the outside.{{Ref|name=ONQariar|[[Online:Blademaster Qariar|Blademaster Qariar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Dorell Manor, Lady Montclair used the remnant for an [[Lore:Illusion|illusion]] spell, a veil that charmed the nobles to her favor.{{Ref|name=ONLleraya|[[Online:Lady Lleraya Montclair|Lady Lleraya Montclair]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONVerandis|[[Online:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Outside of the city, Baron Alard Dorell and the armies of Shornhelm amassed to retake the city. He sent several agents across the field to find a way into the city and discovered the smuggler's tunnel underneath the lighthouse on the north shore. A Shornhelm guardsman, [[Lore:Skordo the Knife|Skordo the Knife]], devised a plan for the members of the invasion force to enter the city and find a safe house from which to stage their attack. They eventually took over the manor of Baron [[Lore:Chirane|Chirane]] and rendezvoused with Blademaster Qariar. Baron Alard sent an [[Lore:The Vestige|Agent]] of the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] to confront Lady Lleraya Montclair and find Ellic Dorell. With help from [[Lore:Verandis Ravenwatch|Verandis Ravenwatch]], the Agent broke the veil and defeated the vampire mistress. For allowing Lady Montclair to take over the city and causing the death of many people, Baron Alard punished his son and relieved him of his status.{{Ref|name=ONLiberation|[[Online:The Liberation of Northpoint|The Liberation of Northpoint]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After securing Northpoint, the forces of Shornhelm gathered at the base of the [[Lore:Shrouded Pass|Shrouded Pass]] for a final attack against Baron Wylon Montclair.{{Ref|name=ONPuzzle|[[Online:Puzzle of the Pass|Puzzle of the Pass]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Northpoint in the Third Empire===
By the late-Interregnum, the kingdoms of High Rock were in a weak and fractured state, which made them easily conquered by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]].{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/High Rock}}}} Historically, Northpoint was a city ruled by the Barons of House Dorell, a major faction that swore fealty to the Kingdom of Rivenspire seated in Shornhelm.{{Ref|name=NAA}} By the late Third Era, however, Northpoint was recognized as a fully-fledged kingdom in its own right, separate from its equal, Shornhelm.{{Ref|name=EWR}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Northpoint was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Elona and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] and Shornhelm. When the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] took place in {{Year|3E 417}}, the kingdoms of Northpoint and Evermore took advantage of the chaos claim territory around them. Their expansion was careful to avoid confrontation with the kingdoms of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] ([[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]]) or even [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]'s forces in the post-[[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]]. Despite that, however, border skirmishes had become a common occurrence between neighbors, specifically Northpoint, Evermore, and even [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]]. By the waning years of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Northpoint was one of the five kingdoms left in High Rock, quietly watching its neighbors with distrust.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

==Culture==
[[File:ON-map-Boralis.jpg|thumb|right|Map of Boralis]]
[[File:ON-place-Orc's Finger Ruins.jpg|thumb|left|Orc's Finger Ruins]]
:''For Breton culture as a whole, see the [[Lore:Breton#Culture|Culture]] section on the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] page.''
Traditionally, Northpoint has been the seat of power for Rivenspire's general northern region, [[Lore:Boralis|Boralis]] as well as its patron house, Dorell.{{Ref|name=ONAlard}} Before it was a modern-day kingdom, however, it was a barony first established in 1E 1029,{{Ref|name=NAA}} one that answered to the historical Kingdom of Rivenspire.{{Ref|name=SCCOTN}} While the Kings and Queens of High Rock are typically buried in [[Lore:Cath Bedraud|Cath Bedraud]], a grand cemetery in northernmost [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]],{{Ref|name=COCB|{{Cite Book|Catacombs of Cath Bedraud}}}}{{Ref|name=TBOWM|{{Cite Book|The Barrows of Westmark Moor}}}} HBarons of House Dorell were buried in the [[Lore:Sanguine Barrows|Sanguine Barrows]], a sprawling graveyard in [[Lore:Westmark Moor|Westmark Moor]].{{Ref|name=TBOWM}} Over time, Northpoint has elevated to the status of kingdom{{Ref|name=EWR}} and even expanded its borders in the wake of the [[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]].{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

According to local legends, there is an immortal and legendary [[Lore:Leviathan|leviathan]] called [[Lore:Ithguleoir|Ithguleoir]] that is said to haunt the Eltheric Ocean, from Northpoint to the [[Lore:Glenumbra Banks|Glenumbra Banks]].{{Ref|name=ONCeramic|[[Online:Contraband F#Fine Ceramic Leviathan Pitcher|Fine Ceramic Leviathan Pitcher]] contraband text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Ith|{{Cite Book|Ithguleoir (book)}}}} There is a children's chant from Northpoint that is in reference to the [[Lore:Orc's Finger Ruins|ruins]] nearby, which goes as follows: "Do not dally, do not linger, in the tomb they call Orc's Fingers".{{Ref|name=ONLoadFinger|[[Online:Orc's Finger Ruins|Orc's Finger Ruins]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The story goes that a long time ago, there was an [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] warrior that came to the region for spoils and battle but died to a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] in the ruins.{{Ref|name=ONLothson|[[Online:Lothson Cold-Eye|Lothson Cold-Eye]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

When Yric Flowdys founded his new port, he minted several gold coins to commemorate the city's complete construction. Officially, there were only one hundred of these coins, and most of them are lost in private collections. However, according to {{Lore Link|Reginus Buca}}'s research, those numbers were fabricated by archaeologists to inflate their value. In addition, some coins were counterfeited, using lightweight alternatives to gold.{{Ref|name=ONCoin|[[Online:Rivenspire Antiquities#Northpoint Founding Coins|Northpoint Founding Coins]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} By the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], Northpoint was a major port in the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]]'s northwest lines. The ships that docked or passed through Northpoint came from other neighboring cities, such as [[Lore:Farrun|Farrun]], [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]], [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]], Solitude, and [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]].{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}}
