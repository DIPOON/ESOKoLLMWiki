[[Lore:Bergama|Bergama]] is a large city found in the dry canyons of the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]], in the province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. Despite its intentional location in the deep desert, Bergama is a bustling trading hub that provides travelers with a safe sanctuary in the harsh desert environment.{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Hammerfell}}}}{{Ref|name=ONIstah|[[Online:Justice Istah|Justice Istah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It was established by the [[Lore:Na-Totambu|Na-Totambu]], the former nobility of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] as a place to preserve their ancient tradition and customs from [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielic]] influence.{{Ref|name=ABOOH|{{Cite Book|A Betrayal of Our Heritage}}}} In the present day, Bergama is associated with their descendants, the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]].{{Ref|name=EGT}} It is the capital city of the eponymous {{nbsl|Lore:Bergama (barony)|barony}}, which is completely surrounded by the unconquered Alik'r Desert territory.{{Ref|name=DFMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of the Iliac Bay – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-concept-Market.jpg|thumb|left|The Open Air-Markets of Bergama]]
[[File:DF-Location-28700-Automap.png|thumb|right|Map of Bergama (Daggerfall)]]
Bergama is a large and fortified city that, geographically, is located in the [[Lore:Hollow Wastes|Hollow Wastes]] of the Alik'r Desert, in a canyon that bridges [[Lore:Myrkwasa|Myrkwasa]] with northeastern Alik'r{{Ref|name=ONBergama|[[Online:Bergama|Bergama]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but politically, it is found in the southern half of the Bergama barony, near the border.{{Ref|name=DFBergama|[[Daggerfall:Bergama|Bergama]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The land outside of it is largely uninhabited, save for Alik'ri nomads like the [[Lore:Ash'abah|Ash'abah]],{{Ref|name=EGT}}, the desert creatures, and the oasis of Ogre's Bluff.{{Ref|name=ONAlik'r|[[Online:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ancient and decrepit mausoleums, such as the Gharm Graveyard and the Cairn of Marquis Barovar exist outside of Bergama, and these places are defiled by grave robbers and haunted by wraiths.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=DFDungeons|[[Daggerfall:Bergama/Dungeons|Bergama dungeons]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The city's architecture uses large, sturdy domes to insulate the desert heat, and ornate walls made of heavy stone to defend it.{{Ref|name=EGT}} The city can be divided three ways by the dense, western district, the northern open parkland, and the Hall of Judgment area. The latter contains the aforementioned Hall of Judgment, a Ra Gada-era palace that currently serves as the city's seat of power. It is surrounded by desert gardens and [[Lore:Palm Tree|palm trees]]. The northern section is a large open square and the Stone Oasis Inn, the city's main tavern and inn. The western section contains one of the city's most well-known landmarks, the bazaar. It is an open-aired market with businesses active all day and night.{{Ref|name=EGT}} Above the bazaar is a series of tall, hovering bridges that intertwine all over the city.{{Ref|name=ONBergama}} At different points, they contain the city's signal fires, which are used whenever Bergama is about to come into contact with an enemy.{{Ref|name=ONAubatha}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:ON-place-Hall of Judgement (Bergama).jpg|thumb|right|The Hall of Judgment]]
Bergama's origins date back to the ninth century of the First Era. The Hall of Judgment was built when the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] first embarked on Hammerfell,{{Ref|name=ONSulma|[[Online:Magistrate Sulma|Magistrate Sulma]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} which was at least {{Year|1E 808}}.{{Ref|name=HOTIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}} The Na-Totambu eventually built the town as one of their settlements in the Alik'r Desert and thousands of years later, they have continued to uphold Yokudan culture.{{Ref|name=ABOOH}} Today, many Crown families are descended from the original nobility.{{Ref|name=ONIstah}} 

In an unspecified time, Bergama was devastated by a deathly plague that rendered any survivors terribly ill, including the city's sworn protectors. An Alik'ri warlord named [[Lore:Emeesh Poison-Tongue|Emeesh Poison-Tongue]] caught wind and attempted to take advantage of this by uprooting the city at night. The only person who stood watch at the time was the sworn blade, [[Lore:Zoreh the Tenacious|Zoreh the Tenacious]]. Despite her affliction, she acted quickly by lighting Bergama's signal fires, in the hopes of tricking Emeesh's raiders that the plague had passed and Bergama's defenders were ready. Zoreh's gambit paid off but even as Emeesh's raiders fled, she killed as many enemies as she could before her immediate death. To this day, Bergama honors her sacrifice with the Signal Fire Sprint, a tradition of the city's New Life celebration.{{Ref|name=ONAubatha|[[Online:Aubatha|Aubatha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO: Holiday Special]]}}

===Bergama in the Second Era and onwards===
When King [[Lore:Ramzi|Ramzi]] died from the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] that initially broke out in {{Year|2E 560}}, a young Prince [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]] made a pilgrimage from Bergama to [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] and declared himself King, much to the chagrin of the Crowns.{{Ref|name=EGT}} In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the town was governed by Magistrate Sulma, a Forebear, and during her tenure, she received criticism from Crown officials, thus leading to mistrust between both sides.{{Ref|name=ONQadim|[[Online:Qadim|Qadim]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

In {{Year|2E 582}}, people began to disappear and rumors started to spread around town. Justice Istah and his bailiffs began to undertake an investigation and had garnered distrust from fellow Crowns for working with the Forebear magistrate. Suffice it to say, the Justice employed an [[Lore:The Vestige|adventurer]] to help them uncover the truth. But while the adventurer was out sleuthing, the Justice was falsely incarcerated. The adventurer was able to solve the issue and revealed the Magistrate's dealings with the [[Lore:Withered Hand|Withered Hand]]. In the hopes of supplying the [[Lore:Necromancers|necromancers]] with bodies, the Magistrate believed that her city could have been spared from their conquest of the Alik'r Desert. Whether the truth was revealed or not is unknown but Bergama moved on from the situation.{{Ref|name=ONMissing|[[Online:Gone Missing|Gone Missing]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At that same time, a marriage between a Crown and Forebear causes controversy in the community.{{Ref|name=ABOOH}} The Crown bride, Tiela was left at the altar while the Forebear groom, Casnar at-Tarin was nowhere to be found. An adventurer was sent out to find the groom and found him hiding from hired thugs in his home. The adventurer was able to negotiate with the gang leader, Uncle Thakh gro-Ram, who offered to help Casnar and have his men escort him to the altar. Despite Tiela's father, Jagnas' best efforts to call it off, the wedding was a success.{{Ref|name=ONAltar|[[Online:Left at the Altar|Left at the Altar]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|3E 405}}, Bergama was ruled by an unnamed Baron and hosted the [[Lore:Akatosh Chantry|Akatosh Chantry]].{{Ref|name=DFBergama}} In the dead of night, on the 9th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]] in {{Year|3E 417}}, the [[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]] swept across the Iliac Bay and magically altered its political landscape. A [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] agent named Briarbird wrote her account of the events. After a gigantic sandstorm blew over and buried her camp, she reached Bergama by nightfall and discovered the town in chaos. The barony had been captured by the [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Kingdom of Sentinel]], at the time ruled by Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]] and the Lord of Bergama's fortress was in ruins. It was the 11th of Frostfall and everyone, except Akorithi's soldiers had completely missed the previous day.{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

===Known Rulers===
The town of Bergama is overseen by a Magistrate.{{Ref|name=ONSulma}} Despite the Crowns' power over the settlement, the Magistrate can be a Forebear. Under their authority is the Justice, the head of security in Bergama. The people of the town can elect the Justice and although they answer to the Magistrate, they keep their own counsel.{{Ref|name=ONIstah}} The Justices themselves are famous beyond Bergama.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresBadge|[[Online:Treasures B#Burnished Judgment Badge|Burnished Judgment Badge]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-quest-Signal Fire Sprint.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Signal Fires]]
[[File:DF-map-Bergama.png|thumb|left|The Barony of Bergama]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Redguard#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Redguard#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Redguard page.''
Bergama and its locals perpetuate myths and legends. The people will siphon [[Lore:Duneripper|duneripper]] blood and make tonics from it because of a belief that it will prolong their life.{{Ref|name=NOAH|{{Cite Book|Notes of Adalabar, hunter}}}} Another legend is the story of how an evil [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] sorcerer turned a hundred baby [[Lore:Monkey|monkeys]] in Bergama into [[Lore:Gold|gold]] statues.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresGold|[[Online:Treasures G#Golden Monkey Statuette|Golden Monkey Statuette]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Despite Bergama's affiliation with the Crowns,{{Ref|name=EGT}} under the Third Empire, the region at large had a patron deity in the form of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], whose temple, the [[Lore:Akatosh Chantry|Akatosh Chantry]] had many chapters across the barony.{{Ref|name=DFBergama}} The Fighters Guild in Bergama is one of several in the Iliac Bay's desert region that also functions as a temple to [[Lore:Ebonarm|Ebonarm]], the God of War.{{Ref|name=DFTrainers|[[Daggerfall:Fighters Guild#Fighter Trainers Guildhalls|Fighter Trainers]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFEbonarm|[[Daggerfall:Citadel of Ebonarm|Citadel of Ebonarm]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The Redguards of Bergama observe the [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]] (1st of [[Lore:Morning Star|Morning Star]]) with the Signal Fire Sprint. A long time ago, Zoreh the Tenacious was able to single-handedly defend the town by lighting Bergama's signal fires. In honor of her sacrifice, everyone from the youth, the elderly, and even the injured can participate in the New Life celebration with a recreation of Zoreh's sprint. It starts when someone sounds the horn, symbolizing the warlord's approach and the participants begin their run. The sprint can be done either as a team or solo.{{Ref|name=ONAubatha}} 

Coinciding with the [[Lore:Old Life Festival|Old Festival]], which is observed across the Third Empire, the Alik'r Desert at large observes the 18th of [[Lore:Evening Star|Evening Star]] as [[Lore:Baranth Do|Baranth Do]], which means ''"Goodbye to the Beast of Last Year"'' in [[Lore:Yoku|Yoku]]. It is a local new Year celebration people dress as demonic representations of the last year and revel for the new one.{{Ref|name=DFBaranth|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Baranth Do|Baranth Do]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Alik'r also acknowledge other holidays, including [[Lore:Ovank'a|Ovank'a]] (12th of [[Lore:Morning Star|Morning Star]]),{{Ref|name=DFOvank'a|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Ovank'a|Ovank'a]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}, the [[Lore:Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] (26th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]]),{{Ref|name=DFFestival|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Fiery Night|Fiery Night]] (29th of [[Lore:Sun's Height|Sun's Height]]),{{Ref|name=DFFiery|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Fiery Night|Fiery Night]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and [[Lore:Dirij Tereur|Dirij Tereur]] (5th of [[Lore:Frostfall|Frostfall]]).{{Ref|name=DFDirij|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Dirij Tereur|Dirij Tereur]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}
