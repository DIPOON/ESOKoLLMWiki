The [[Lore:Volkihar|Volkihar]] are the most powerful tribe of [[Lore:Vampirism|vampires]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. The Volkihar are paranoid and cruel, and their breath is said to freeze their victims' blood in the veins. They are said to usually live in eastern Skyrim, beneath the ice of remote and haunted lakes, never venturing into the world of men except to feed.{{Ref|name=IB|{{Cite Book|Immortal Blood}}}} Despite these claims, they are sometimes known to dwell within vampire lairs with others of their kind, and can be found all over Skyrim.{{Ref|name=Skyrim|Appearance in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They can reach through the ice of their lakes without breaking it to grab unsuspecting victims from the surface.{{Ref|name=IB}} Members of the Volkihar bloodline are infected with the vampire's disease called [[Lore:Sanguinare Vampiris|Sanguinare Vampiris]], instead of the more common [[Lore:Porphyric Hemophilia|Porphyric Hemophilia]].{{Ref|name=Skyrim}}

[[Lore:Harkon|Lord Harkon]] and his family are the progenitors of the Volkihar clan. They are pure-blooded [[Lore:Vampire Lord|Vampire Lords]], having been given the gift of vampirism directly by [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]. Harkon and his court dwell within the ancient [[Lore:Castle Volkihar|Castle Volkihar]], on a remote island off the coast of [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], near the border of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. The Volkihar bloodline thins with each new generation, becoming weaker and eventually losing the ability to transform, though the progeny and "grandchild" of a pure-blood are still pure enough to transform into a Vampire Lord but are still considered to be half-breeds, at least compared to the Daughters of Coldharbour and Harkon. According to the ancient vampire Vingalmo, certain star signs as well as a mortal descendant of Harkon will allow vampirism to be "faithfully" accepted by the descendant, thus creating a vampire lord seemingly regardless of generation.{{Ref|name=Dawnguard|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}<noinclude>
==Hierachy==
[[File:SR-npc-Volkihar Vampire Clan.jpg|thumb|right|Members of Harkon's court]]
Volkihar vampires are not a part of one main cohesive organization. Lord Harkon's court, also known as the {{Lore Link|Volkihar}}, are ancient and purer vampires, who shun the scattered thin-blooded nomadic vampires on mainland Skyrim. The court itself is prone to infighting between Harkon's power-hungry courtiers, who have henchmen and support bases of their own. The court occasionally kills feral vampires around Skyrim to prevent them either riling the prey, alerting humans to their presence, or getting rid of the competition. Most members of the court feel nothing but contempt for their thin-blooded descendants, viewing them as nothing more than diseased mortals that potentially threaten their cover and existence.

One of the known punishments among the Volkihar clan is the exile. A vampire exile typically had the skull brand symbol carved into their forehead.{{Ref|name=Exile's Skull Brand|[[ON:Contraband E#Exile's Skull Brand|Exile's Skull Brand]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

To attend masked revels in Skyrim and beyond, some vampires wear Masks of Entangled Paths to hide their natures from their prey. The knotwork design also helps other vampires identify their fellow kin.{{Ref|name=MoEP|[[ON:Mask of Entangled Paths|Mask of Entangled Paths]] hat description}}

The clan employs inky black [[Lore:Death Hound|Death hounds]] which rarely venture beyond the Castle, better serving their masters at home. The ones wandering after folk are probably some form of spy for the Volkihar vampires.{{Ref|name=VDH|[[ON:Volkihar Death Hound|Volkihar Death Hound]] pet description}}

==History==
[[Lore:Movarth Piquine|Movarth Piquine]], once a famous vampire hunter, went searching for the Volkihar vampires and managed to kill one.{{Ref|name=IB}} {{Lore Link|Majorn the Ancient}} was the vampiric patriarch of the village of {{Lore Link|Wittestadr}} in [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]] during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], and belonged to the Volkihar Clan.{{Ref|name=Rala|[[ON:Rala|Rala]]'s dialogue in [[Online:The Better of Two Evils|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Essenia|Blood Queen Lady Essenia]] was the master of Greymoor Keep during the Planemeld and dominated Western Skyrim at the time, causing members of [[Lore:Ravenwatch|Ravenwatch]] to question why local clans such as the Volkihar had not made a move against her. It was later discovered she was part of the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]].{{ref|name=ESOGwendis|[[ON:Gwendis|Gwendis]]' dialogue during [[ON:The Lady of Blood|The Lady of Blood]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the Volkihar vampire clan grew bold as Lord Harkon sought to find his lost daughter [[Lore:Serana|Serana]], who was involved in [[Lore:Tyranny of the Sun|a prophecy]] which would darken the sun and allow vampires to rule Tamriel. The Skyrim branch of the [[Lore:Vigilants of Stendarr|Vigilants of Stendarr]] were almost wiped out in an open attack by the vampires, and raids on major cities became increasingly frequent. To counter this aggression, an ancient order of vampire hunters named the [[Lore:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]] was reformed. The prophecy was realized by Serana and the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]], who retrieved [[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]] and learned how it could be corrupted to temporarily block the sun. Lord Harkon was then defeated with the bow, although it is unknown if the Dragonborn replaced him as leader of the Volkihar, or destroyed the court. Either way, vampire attacks in Skyrim's cities became less frequent.{{Ref|name=Dawnguard}}
