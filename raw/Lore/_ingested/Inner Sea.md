The [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] (also called the '''Inland Sea'''{{Ref|name=MWElone|[[Morrowind:Elone|Elone]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}, the '''Narrow Sea''',{{Ref|name=AFATD1|{{Cite Book|A Feast Among the Dead, Chapter I}}}} and the '''Vvardenfell Rift'''{{Ref|name=RoKZ|{{Cite Book|Ruins of Kemel-Ze}}}}) is a body of water that separates the mainland of the Imperial province of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] from the large island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. It stretches from the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]] and [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] regions in western Vvardenfell all the way to [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] in the east. It meets the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] twice: the two meld together near Azura's Coast, and again near the large and icy island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] northwest of Vvardenfell. There are coral reefs submerged in the waters of the Inner Sea.{{ref|name=BW|{{Cite Book|Breathing Water}}}}

There are conflicting reports on the origins of the Inner Sea. Some sources accredit an eruption of Red Mountain to its creation,{{Ref|name=RoKZ}} whereas Vivec's Sermons point to the usage of the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] circa {{Year|1E 700}}.{{Ref|name=TSLT|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}}{{ref|name=DI1|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol I}}}} However, some sources attest that the Inner Sea was present before both the first major eruption of Red Mountain and the activation of the Numidium.{{ref|name=TBOS|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Strife}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} Furthermore, [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]] left behind maps of the Inner Sea dating to the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Middle Merethic Era|Middle Merethic Era]], far before the first Dwemer colonies.{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father of the Niben}}}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}

The main port of the Inner Sea on Vvardenfell is [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Ebonheart]], with minor ports being [[Lore:Hla Oad|Hla Oad]], [[Lore:Gnaar Mok|Gnaar Mok]], {{Lore Link|Molag Mar}}, [[Lore:Tel Branora|Tel Branora]] and the Imperial outpost of [[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]] with its lighthouse Grand Pharos guiding mariners. The [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] once held a strategic outpost called [[Lore:Fort Firemoth|Fort Firemoth]] on an offshore island of the same name, but it was overrun by an undead army and never reclaimed. The Inner Sea is home to a species of bioluminescent and sentient {{Lore Link|Land Coral}} that grows exclusively on the land around it. The fields of [[Lore:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]] is the most notable example of a site containing this species of Land Coral. Vivec's Antlers was also a battlefield in the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] of {{Year|2E 572}}, for the final battle involving the [[Lore:Kamal (race)|Snow Demons]] of [[Lore:Kamal (place)|Kamal]] and their invasion of the province of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{Ref|name=Akaviri|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}}

The coast of Vvardenfell is rugged and treacherous, with shipwrecks scattered among the menhirs that protrude from the water. It has been a major center of {{Lore Link|smuggling}} operations by the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] and other groups.<noinclude>
