[[Lore:Gortwog|King Gortwog gro-Nagorm]], '''Warlord of the Orcs'''{{ref|name=DFMedora|[[Daggerfall:Dust of Restful Death|Medora Direnni's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} (also titled '''Lord of Orsinium'''{{ref|name=DFGortwog|[[Daggerfall:Barenziah's Book|Gortwog's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and '''King of Orcs and Warlord of the Subterranean Realms'''){{ref|name=DFMyniseraLetter|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Mynisera's letter to Gortwog]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} was the ruler of the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]ish people in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] and chieftain of the [[Lore:Minat|Minat Orc tribe]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=DFC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}} He is remembered as a skilled diplomat and a political genius.{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}

The Orcish race had been homeless for centuries when Gortwog founded the independent kingdom of [[Lore:Orsinium|Nova Orsinium]] in {{Year|3E 399}}.{{ref|name=PGE3O}}{{ref|name=HOPTTO|{{Cite Book|How Orsinium Passed to the Orcs}}}} Gortwog set about gathering dirt on his rivals to bolster his political situation, and in the process bought a stolen letter written by Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]]. It had been sent to the then-Queen [[Lore:Mynisera|Mynisera]] of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], but had never reached her.{{ref|name=DFC}}{{ref|name=DFOrcish|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Orcish Emancipation]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The release of this letter contributed directly to events which culminated in the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} 

Following the "[[Lore:Miracle of Peace|Miracle of Peace]]", his kingdom had expanded to control much of the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] as well as valuable coastline along the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]]. By {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] recognized Gortwog as king, and Orsinium as a county of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], though portions of the kingdom extended into [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=TWITW}}<noinclude>

==History==
===A New Kingdom===
[[File:DF-concept-CutsceneGortwog2.png|Gortwog on his throne|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|''I aspire to raise my race from the mire. I will bring them to equal standing with the other races of Tamriel…My goal is not to conquer Tamriel, but to create the {{sic|orcish|Orcish|description=lowercase letter}} homeland.''|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Gortwog]], Warlord and King{{ref|name=DFWGTTLetter|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Letter from Gortwog]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}}}
The story of Gortwog gro-Nagorm is the story of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]'s rebirth. Tales say he acquired land near the former site of Orsinium after winning a duel in {{Year|3E 399}}.{{ref|name=HOPTTO}} He then constructed a new city, also named Orsinium, for his people within the nascent [[Lore:Orsinium Area|Orsinium Area]], ruling from his citadel of steel.{{ref|name=PGE3O}}{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} But despite this, most in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region saw the Orcs as villains,{{ref|name=DFMedora}} beneath contempt.{{ref|name=DFBusiness|[[Daggerfall:Business with Orcs|NPC's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

[[File:DF-map-Orsinium Area.png|thumb|right|The original extent of the Orsinium Area]]
In {{Year|3E 402}}, the [[Lore:War of Betony|War of Betony]] broke out between the kingdoms of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]] and [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]]. While historians recorded that Daggerfall's King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] was felled by an arrow during the climactic battle at [[Lore:Cryngaine Field|Cryngaine Field]],{{ref|name=NWOB|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Newgate)}}}}{{ref|name=FWOB|{{Cite Book|The War of Betony (Fav'te)}}}} he was actually slain by treachery before it.{{ref|name=DFStronghold|[[Daggerfall:The Ancient Watcher|Agent of the Underking's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}  [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], which had remained neutral in the conflict, sent delegations to meet with the warring kings on the eve of the battle.{{ref|name=DFHelseth|[[Daggerfall:Blackmail|Prince Helseth's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Among those sent to meet King Lysandus was Lord [[Lore:Woodborne|Woodborne]], a minor noble who secretly schemed to usurp the throne of Wayrest.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} While [[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles/Narrative|some sources]] claim it was Gortwog and his Orcs who slew the king on Woodborne's orders,{{ref|name=DFC}} in truth, Lysandus was assassinated by Lord Woodborne himself.{{ref|name=DFWayrest|[[Daggerfall:Painting the Truth|Contents of the magical painting]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=DFWoodborne|[[Daggerfall:Lysandus' Revenge|Lord Woodborne's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

King Lysandus had supported Gortwog's claim to sovereign rule.{{ref|name=DFMyniseraLetter}} When Gortwog got word of the planned assassination, he sent his archers to waylay and attack the Wayrest delegation en route,{{ref|name=DFHelseth}} despite knowing that this act would damage his efforts at [[Lore:Empire|Imperial]] recognition.{{ref|name=DFDustWrapping|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Gortwog's letter to Medora]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Orcish warriors were slain in the attempt.{{ref|name=DFMedora}}

===The Emperor's Letter===
In {{Year|3E 401}}, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] learned that Lord Woodborne had acquired the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]],{{ref|name=DFUriel|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Emperor Uriel Septim VII's stolen letter]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} an artifact which allowed its bearer to control [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s ultimate weapon, [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} He wrote to Queen [[Lore:Mynisera|Mynisera]] of Daggerfall, asking her to use her influence with the court of Wayrest to try and recover the Totem.{{ref|name=DFUriel}} Unfortunately, the letter never reached her, its delivery delayed by the War of Betony. When it arrived, Lysandus' son [[Lore:Gothryd|Gothryd]] was the new King, and as the letter was addressed to "the Queen of Daggerfall", it was delivered to the new Queen, [[Lore:Aubk-i|Aubk-i]].{{ref|name=DFMynisera|[[Daggerfall:The Emperor's Courier|Mynisera's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The letter didn't remain secret for long; some say it was stolen by the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] and sold to the highest bidder, Gortwog.{{ref|name=DFMorgiah|[[Daggerfall:Morgiah's Wedding|Princess Morgiah's dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Others say it was Queen Aubk-i herself who sold it to the Orc chieftain.{{ref|name=DFC}} Another source posits that Aubk-i's lady in waiting, [[Lore:Cyndassa|Cyndassa]], understood that the information in the letter was valuable even if she did not understand its content, and sold the information to the highest bidder.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=10th|''[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary]]'', originally found at Elderscrolls.com}} Regardless, it is believed Gortwog then sold copies of the letter to the major powers of the Iliac Bay region, as the Orcs stood to gain much from the resulting conflict.{{ref|name=DFOrcish}} When Gortwog first came to learn of the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]]'s existence, he consulted with [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] on what exactly it was, becoming one of the few powers along with [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] and Mannimarco to know that [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] held the answer to its exact location.{{ref|name=10th|''[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary]]'', originally found at Elderscrolls.com}}

When the ghost of King Lysandus began haunting the city of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], it attracted the Emperor's attention. Uriel VII sent an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] to investigate, and to search for his missing letter (though he downplayed its significance).{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFIntro|[[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]]'s monologue in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''{{'}}s introduction}} This agent crossed paths with Gortwog several times, and even braved the dungeons beneath the steel citadel of Orsinium to retrieve the Emperor's missing letter.{{ref|name=DFOrcish}} On another occasion, Gortwog had obtained and intended to publish copies of a suppressed [[Lore:The Real Barenziah, Part VI|book]], the contents of which would cause considerable embarrassment to Queen [[Lore:Barenziah|Barenziah]] of Wayrest. The queen hired the agent to retrieve the tome, which turned into a race against the [[Lore:Cult of the Black Worm|Necromancers]] for the manuscript.{{ref|name=DFBarenziah|[[Daggerfall:Barenziah's Book|Barenziah's Book]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Orc chieftain also provided the agent with the whereabouts of a magical ingredient to soothe the spirit of King Lysandus, for which Gortwog gained the sorceress [[Lore:Medora Direnni|Medora Direnni]]'s support of his claim to the [[Lore:Mantella|heart of Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=DFDust|[[Daggerfall:Dust of Restful Death|Dust of Restful Death]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

===The Warp in the West===
[[File:DF-place-Orsinium.png|The iron Stronghold of Orsinium, Gortwog's citadel|left|thumb]]
{{Quote Box|''Among the most significant results of the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Second Numidian Effect]] are the foundation of the orc city-state Nova Orsinium; the transfiguration and apotheosis of [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]], King of Worms; and the return of [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]], the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], to the affairs of men.''|Niso, ''[[Lore:Lives of the Emperors|Lives of the Emperors]]''}}
Ultimately, the agent recovered the Totem of Tiber Septim, which set off a bidding war between seven major powers in the Iliac Bay region. Gortwog made his case plainly, possibly offering either the [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|Warlock's Ring]] or [[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]] in exchange.{{ref|name=DFWho|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{intnote|DF|[nb 1]}} The Orc king was given the Totem, though curiously, the other six powers were also simultaneously given the artifact.{{ref|name=TWITW}} After the agent retrieved the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]] from the plane of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], Numidium was ready to be activated.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} And on the 9th of Frostfall, {{Year|3E 417}}, it was.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=TWITW}}

Time and reality became "[[Lore:Dragon Break|warped]]" as the seven powers used a Numidium to their own ends, giving the phenomenon its name, the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]]. Three days merged into one, though events progressed further than even three days could account for; among the known occurrences was a titanic three-way battle between Daggerfall, Wayrest, and Orsinium midway between their kingdoms that left the surrounding countryside "decimated". The Warp ended as suddenly as it began, and as a result Orsinium was one of but four kingdoms that remained in the Iliac Bay region. Orsinium's borders were greatly expanded to include much of the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]], as well as parts of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]]coast and [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=TWITW}}

===Recognition and Peace===
[[File:LO-map-Orsinium (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumb|left|Orsinium's location in High Rock, circa {{Year|3E 433}}]]
King Gortwog's struggles had not been in vain; the Orcish people were given equal status among the races of the Empire, and within ten years Orcs could be found across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], in all walks of life.{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} His political insight was respected in certain circles, including [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]], where it was particularly admired by Duke [[Lore:Vedam Dren|Vedam Dren]].{{Ref|name=DVD|[[MW:Duke Vedam Dren|Duke Vedam Dren]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} By {{Year|3E 432}}, the Iliac Bay was at peace, and Nova Orsinium was recognized as a kingdom of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] by the Empire, though King Gortwog continued to press for elevation to Provincial status.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} Gortwog was still the ruler of Orsinium circa {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Adventurer|Adventurer]] dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Orsinium was not wholly without conflict, however. King Gortwog established a new priesthood devoted to the worship of [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]], the ancient Orcish Hero, who according to legend was devoured by [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and became the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]]. Traditionally, the Orcs had worshipped [[Lore:Mauloch|Mauloch]] (another name for Malacath). Gortwog, however, believed Trinimac was still alive, and that he and Malacath were separate entities. This became the official stance of the shaman priests of Orsinium, and further, that Malacath was a demon who wanted the Orsimer to remain pariah folk forever. To some traditionalist Orcs within Orsinium—and most Orcs without—these beliefs were dangerously heretical.{{ref|name=PGE3O}} 

The outcome of this religious schism is not known, but worship of Malacath outside of Orsinium was still strong well into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, there are also Orcs in the Fourth Era that abide by Gortwog and think Malacath is a deceiver like Boethiah, that Trinimac still lives, but that he's in some kind of prison.{{ref|[[Blades:The Heretic|The Heretic]] quest in {{g|BL}}}}

The result would have been the rise of Orsinium and the further crumbling of the Empire.
