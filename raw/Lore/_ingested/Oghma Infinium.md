# Lore:Oghma Infinium

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}__NOTOC__[[File:OBR-item-Oghma Infinium.jpg|thumb|right|The front cover of the ''Oghma Infinium'' (Oblivion Remastered)]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]], also known as the '''Tome of Power''', the '''Librum of Knowledge''',{{ref|name=TLOI|{{TIL|Oghma Infinium Artifact Dialogue|artifact-dialog}} from ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} and the '''Book that Knows''',{{ref|name=ESOTGT|{{Cite Book|The Thief God's Treasures}}}} is an ancient tome of knowledge, and an [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] of great power. It was written by [[Lore:Xarxes (god)|Xarxes]], the scribe of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], who had been granted the knowledge within by [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]].{{ref|name=OBHermaeus|[[Oblivion:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The name of the tome comes from [[Lore:Oghma|Oghma]], the wife of Xarxes, whom he created from his favorite moments in history.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} The knowledge within the tome grants the reader access to the artifact's energy, which can be manipulated to achieve near [[Lore:Demigod|demi-god]] abilities.{{ref|name=ARDescription|[[Arena:Oghma Infinium|Description]] in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}}{{Ref|name=DFOghma|[[Daggerfall:Oghma Infinium|Description]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} The reader is instantly bombarded with a knowledge intake that could hemorrhage the brains of lesser mortals.{{ref|name=PRIMA|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide#Legendary Edition|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Legendary Prima Official Game Guide]]}}{{rp|386}} The Paths of Steel, Shadow and Spirit are the three main sections of the tome.{{ref|name=OBOghma|Sections within the ''[[Oblivion:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]]'' in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [[Lore:Tracts Perilous|Tract Perilous]].{{Ref|name=OTP}} Once the Oghma Infinium is used, it disappears from its owner's possession.<noinclude>

==History==
===Unspecified===
The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] believe that sometime during [[Lore:Rajhin|Rajhin]]'s life, he stole the [[Lore:Ring of Khajiiti|Ring of Khajiiti]] from [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], which allowed him to perform many grand feats of thievery that would ascend him to the Cat-King of Thieves. Among these feats occurred when he visited [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] seeking treasure, which led to him stealing the Oghma Infinium. Rajhin kept the book hidden in his possession, and occasionally Hermaeus Mora manages to find it, but only because Rajhin chose to give it back to him on his own whim.{{ref|name=ESOTGT}}{{Ref|name=HRSTBTK|{{Cite Book|How Rajhin Stole the Book that Knows}}}}

===First and Second Eras===
After the fall of their empire in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], many [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] tribes fled [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] to resettle in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]]. One such Ayleid settlement was located just across the border, on the southern banks of the [[Lore:Strid River|Strid River]] in the [[Lore:Northern Woods|Northern Woods]] of Valenwood, west of modern [[Lore:Arenthia|Arenthia]]. These worshippers of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] had come into possession of two dangerous tomes of knowledge: the ''Oghma Infinium'' and the {{Lore Link|Book of the Frozen Legion}}. They attempted to learn the secrets contained within, but instead their city collapsed into an underground cavern, now only known as the "swallowed city". Following the gradual absorption of Ayleids into [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] society, the Bosmer descendants of the lost city attempted to rebuild. This new settlement was in existence until {{Year|2E 582}}, when [[Lore:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]] and his [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]n forces invaded [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]]. One of his generals, an [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] named Graccus, commandeered a large invasion force and burned the Bosmer city to the ground, building a mansion over the ruins. This mansion, which came to be known as the {{Lore Link|Vile Manse}}, was used by Graccus as a staging ground for exploring the subterranean tunnels in search of the ''Oghma Infinium''.{{ref|name=ESO|[[Online:The Waking Dreamer|The Waking Dreamer]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OTP|{{Cite Book|On Tracts Perilous}}}}

Upon finding the tome, Graccus managed to survive the "cosmic ululation", while also summoning powerful [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] to guard the ruins. The Book of the Frozen Legion also summoned several [[Lore:Frost Atronach|Frost Atronach]]s, freezing many Bosmer and transforming some into icy undead. Graccus was determined to use the ''Oghma Infinium'' to aid the invasion and allow the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire]] to crush the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]]. However, Javad withdrew military support once he learned of Graccus' apparent inactivity,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Missive from Cyrodiil|ns_base=ON}}}} and Hermaeus Mora decided that he was not worthy to continue possessing the tome. [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]] then entered the Vile Manse, and Mora promised the ''Oghma Infinium'' as a reward for defeating Graccus. However, the Prince was not willing to risk the Vestige against the tome's secrets, and snatched it away upon Graccus' death. It was duly returned it to its home in Apocrypha, and the {{Lore Link|Discourse Amaranthine}} was offered in its stead.{{ref|name=MoraON|[[Online:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
The tale of the book titled ''[[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]]'' tells of a [[Lore:Pelagius III|newly ascended]] king of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] who seeking the knowledge from the Oghma Infinum to help rule his city, attempted to summon Hermaeus Mora. However, since the king had attempted to summon the Daedric Prince during a storm, instead came forth [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] who gave him the [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]] and turned the king mad.{{ref|name=Wabbajack|{{Cite Book|Wabbajack (book)}} (book)}}{{ref|name=DFSummoning|[[Daggerfall:Daedra Summoning|Daedra summoning]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

[[File:OB-book-Mysterium Xarxes 01.jpg|thumb|right|Page 1 of the ''Oghma Infinium'' (''Oblivion''){{intnote|nb1}}]]
Sometime during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]], the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] obtained the tome in a dungeon after finding a map to its location.

Just before the events of the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] area was sent by Hermaeus Mora to assassinate a noble who had displeased the Prince. In return, Mora promised the ''Oghma Infinium'', which was in the possession of one of his worshippers. The agent succeeded, and the tome passed hands.{{ref|name=Daggerfall|[[Daggerfall:Hermaeus Mora's Quest|Hermaeus Mora's Quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} 

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Mora again offered the tome as a reward, this time to the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]]. Mora summoned the Hero to his shrine in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]], on the border with Skyrim, after seeing how capable the Hero was with dealing with the other [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]]. Mora had the Hero collect souls from the ten main [[Lore:Races|races]] of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], which were needed by his followers to perform a divination. The Hero succeeded and was given the tome.{{ref|name=HMOB|[[Oblivion:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora's]] quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
Sometime after the events of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the tome was sealed inside an ancient [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] lockbox so that no more of its dangerous knowledge could be shared with mortals. This displeased Hermaeus Mora. The lockbox, located in a small cave out on the ice sheets off the coast of northeast [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], was massive in size and seemingly bigger on the inside than physically possible. It could only be opened by those of Dwarven blood. A follower of Hermaeus Mora, [[Lore:Septimus Signus|Septimus Signus]], was manipulated by the god and tasked with opening the lockbox.{{ref|name=SRSeptimus|[[Skyrim:Septimus Signus|Septimus Signus]]' dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Signus believed the lockbox contained the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]]. Signus was told by Mora that he would need the knowledge of an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] to divine the way to open the lockbox. Signus was an expert on Elder Scrolls, and when the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] came looking for one in {{Year|4E 201}}, Signus pointed them towards Blackreach. The Last Dragonborn found the Elder Scroll within the Tower of Mzark, taking it and inscribing its knowledge onto a blank Dwemer lexicon. Signus used the knowledge to discover how to open the lockbox. As the Dwemer were all but extinct, Signus theorized that he could inject himself with the blood of all surviving [[Lore:Mer|elves]] to trick the lock. Mora then appeared to the Dragonborn in the form of a Wretched Abyss and revealed that Signus was no longer of use to him due to his broken mind. The Dragonborn retrieved samples of blood from [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]], [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]] and [[Lore:Orc|Orcs]]. Signus succeeded in opening the lock, only to discover that it contained the ''Oghma Infinium''. He was turned to dust attempting to read it. The Last Dragonborn took the tome and was contacted by Mora, who was pleased that his knowledge would again be put to use.{{ref|name=Skyrim|[[Skyrim:Discerning the Transmundane|Discerning the Transmundane]] quest in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
