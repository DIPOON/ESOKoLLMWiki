'''Note:''' The [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], also known as the '''Dawn Age''',{{ref|name=TBOH|{{Cite Book|The Blessings of Hircine}}}} the '''Beginning Times''',{{ref|name=ESOWH|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/25953 Next Crown Crate Season: The Elder Scrolls Online's Wild Hunt] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} or the '''Chaos Times''',{{ref|name=ESOFara|[[Online:Fara Garnona|Fara Garnona]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} was a period during which time followed an incomprehensible nonlinear path and the very laws of nature remained unset, making a timeline an artificial fabrication. A conflict was simultaneously a mere ideological difference of opinion and a manifest war. As noted in ''[[Lore:The Lunar Lorkhan|The Lunar Lorkhan]]'', this "War of Manifest Metaphors" renders the era mostly incapable of supporting a narrative. Since fixed dates during this time are unknowable, there is no specific nomenclature for years within the Dawn Era. Further, some events, such as the [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]]i dissident movement, are attributed to the Dawn Era by some sources and to the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] by others. In later eras, in various areas and over varying periods of time, people have experienced a refrain of the chaos of the Dawn, which is known as a [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]].
{{History Entry|The Beginning||Though the details vary with each culture and religion, all legends of the beginning of existence which have been passed down speak of two entities who appeared in the [[Lore:Void|Void]] and soon found themselves in conflict.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n legend says Satak, the Snake who came Before, eventually merged with Akel, its "Hungry Stomach", to create [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]], the Yokudan God of Everything. Satakal is considered a fusion of the concepts of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], [[Lore:Mer|elven]] personifications of the primordial forces of stasis and change respectively. Other dichotomies include Ak-El, [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]]-[[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]],{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} and [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]]-[[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]].{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} Even the [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]] worshipped by ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s faced a twin force, [[Lore:The Adversary|the Adversary]].{{ref|name=COTAM|{{Cite Book|Children of the All-Maker}}}} Regardless of the nomenclature, all seem to represent the dichotomy between existence and nothingness, order and chaos,{{ref|name=TM}} light and dark.{{ref|name=TAA}}{{ref|name=TLATD|{{Cite Book|The Light and the Dark}}}}}}{{History Entry
|Creation||The [[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]], the Original Spirits, crystallize in the Beginning Place,{{ref|name=TAA}} springing forth from the two primordial forces.{{ref|name=TM}} Legend speaks of twelve worlds birthed into existence where life flourished.{{ref|name=TAA}} The interplay of the light and the dark becomes creation - known variously as [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], Gray Maybe, Arena Supermundus, and Satakal.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=PGE3Arena|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Arena Supermundus}}}} When the "First God" [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] forms, time begins, and it becomes easier for some spirits to achieve structure and become aware of themselves as beings with a past and a future. Their constant flux and interplay create more spirits. The strongest of the recognizable spirits crystallize: [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]], [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]], [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], [[Lore:Magnus (god)|Magnus]], [[Lore:Ruptga|Ruptga]], etc. Others remain as concepts, ideas, or emotions, their personalities slowly congealing.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=TM}}

The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]i creation myth similarly associates the beginning of creation with maternal death.{{ref|name=WOCMA}}}}{{History Entry

* According to [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] beliefs, [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]], the god of everything, periodically consumes all of creation in order to begin anew, over and over. Lesser spirits unable to make the Walkabout are eventually consumed. [[Lore:Ruptga|Ruptga]] places stars to guide lesser spirits to the Far Shores, but after numerous cycles, there are too many spirits for him to help.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}}

* [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] is either condemned to exile in the mortal realms, or killed in battle leading the armies of Men against Auriel and the Aldmer. His [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart]], impervious to destruction, is cast from the Tower or fastened to an arrow and shot into [[Lore:Inner Sea|the sea]], forming a [[Lore:Red Mountain|volcano]] where it lands, and Lorkhan is left to wander the creation of the et'Ada.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}} As the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s say, he "slinks around in a dead skin".{{ref|name=TM}}
* The development of mortals now follows slow, comprehensible paths.{{ref|name=TAA}} The progeny of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] slowly diversify and find their way back to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], which for many of the earliest years of creation is thought to have only been inhabited by the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]].{{ref|name=PGE3All}} The people of Tamriel become the mer (elves): The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] (Deep Ones), the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] (Changed Ones), the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] (Green or Forest Ones), and the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] (Elder or High Ones). The fracturing of the Aldmer along cultural and racial lines is the Sundering of [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]]. The continent no longer exists in any recognizable form, assuming it ever did. The other Ehlnofey become the beasts and the [[Lore:Man|humans]] of Nirn, who will eventually split into racial groups, including the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]], [[Lore:Nord|Nords]], and Tamrielic Aboriginals, as well as the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]].{{ref|name=TAA}}}}
