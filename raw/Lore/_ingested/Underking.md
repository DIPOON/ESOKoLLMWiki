The [[Lore:Underking|Underking]] (sometimes '''UnderKing'''), or the '''Evil One''', was a mysterious figure of myth and legend, known throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] (and by some accounts, even earlier).{{ref|name=ARManual|{{Cite Book|Arena Player's Guide|ns_base=bk}}}}{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} His legions of living and undead followers were called his Agents, and he was known to be opposed to [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco, the King of Worms]] and [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|his cult of necromancers]].{{ref|name=ARManual}}{{rp|50}}{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} The Underking was one of seven factions in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region that played a major role in events preceding and during the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] in {{Year|3E 417}}.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} 

The Underking was the "old bugbear" of fairy tales dating back to the earliest years of the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim dynasty]]. Some tales described him as a very old, very powerful [[Lore:Lich|lich]]—or at least, something resembling one. Others claimed he was the most powerful mage who ever lived. Somehow, this mage "lost his heart" and his very being was transformed. The mage's heart "disappeared", and without it, the Underking could not truly live or die.{{ref|name=ARManual}}{{ref|name=DFGD|[[Daggerfall:Generic Dialogue|Generic dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The stories were largely true, though the Underking's identity remains a matter of dispute. Most sources (notably, the Underking himself){{ref|name=DFWho|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?#The Underking|Letter from the Underking]] during [[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} claim he was once [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]], Battlemage of Emperor [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} Zurin also went by his [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] name Arnand the Fox.{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}{{Ref|name=IWTB|{{Cite Book|Interview With Three Booksellers|ns_base=GEN}}}} As the Underking, Arctus has been associated with [[Lore:Magnus (god)|Magnus]], the god of magic,{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and was claimed to be the most powerful mage to ever live.{{ref|name=DFGD}} However, according to ''[[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|The Arcturian Heresy]]'' the Underking was previously [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]], a supernatural being said to have lived many lives, and who was an ally of Tiber Septim. Among Wulfharth's numerous purported titles was "Underking".{{Ref|name=TAH}} The King of Worms was also rumored to be the Underking.{{ref|name=DFAW|[[Daggerfall:The Ancient Watcher#Additional Dialogue|Quest-related dialogue]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}<includeonly>

For more information, see the [[Lore:Underking|main Lore article]].</includeonly><noinclude>

==History==
===Second Era===
There are differing accounts of the Underking's origin, though the stories share some similarities:
====Zurin Arctus====
The [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] saw Emperor [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] wage a series of [[Lore:Tiber Wars|wars]] with the goal of conquering or otherwise pacifying all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=BHOTE|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire, v 1}}}}  At his side was the Imperial Battlemage [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} The final years of the era saw the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] turn its attention to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. Rather than wage war, the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] signed an [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]]. While the exact terms were never disclosed, it was speculated that the living god [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] agreed to give the Empire [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]]{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}}—a colossal brass golem created by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DM|{{Cite Book|Divine Metaphysics}}}}{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[Morrowind:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}—to aid in conquering the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]].{{ref|name=OM}} It was further speculated that Zurin Arctus played a major role in brokering the Armistice.{{ref|name=OM}}

[[File:DF-item-Totem.png|thumb|right|The Totem of Tiber Septim]]
With the power of Numidium, the Empire could easily conquer the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#Second Dominon|Second Aldmeri Dominion]], but there was a problem: Numidium would not function.{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters|[[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed#Letters You May Recieve|Letters you may receive]] during [[Daggerfall:The Mantella Revealed|The Mantella Revealed]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} By Tiber Septim's order, Zurin Arctus crafted a magical artifact that gave its bearer the power to control the Empire's new weapon—the [[Lore:Totem of Tiber Septim|Totem of Tiber Septim]].{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters}} The Battlemage also placed a seal on the Totem; anyone not of the Septim bloodline (or at least, a royal lineage) or possessing a particular supernatural quality would be killed if they attempted to use it.{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters}}

[[File:DF-item-Mantella.png|thumb|right|The Mantella]]
But the Totem was not enough; Numidium lacked a power source. The Dwemer had originally used the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]],{{ref|name=TAH}} but this was no longer available.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} Undeterred, an alternate source of power was devised, a massive green gem known as the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]], which was infused with Arctus' own life force.{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters}} Numidium was reactivated in {{Year|2E 896}} at [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]] in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]],{{ref|name=WWYDB}} and the Isles were no match for its terrible might; it is said that the siege of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] lasted only an hour before the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] surrendered.{{ref|group=UOL|name=FPKirkbride|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=gen}}}} Tamriel was united, and the emperor declared the beginning of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] beginning the following year.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}

Tiber Septim betrayed his Battlemage, however.{{ref|name=DFKoW|[[Daggerfall:The King of Worms|The King of Worms]]' dialogue during [[Daggerfall:Soul of a Lich|Soul of a Lich]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Once the Empire's enemies were defeated, the emperor began using Numidium to crush the neutral royal families of Tamriel and install loyalists. Zurin Arctus, furious at this misuse, fought to reclaim the Mantella. In the battle that followed, both the Battlemage and Numidium were annihilated and the Mantella—their shared "heart"—was blown into the netherworld of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]]. The Totem of Tiber Septim was also lost as the remains of Numidium were scattered across Tamriel, and Arctus, cut off from the Mantella, fell into a state of semi-slumber in a subterranean vault somewhere in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters}}{{ref|name=10th|''[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary]]'', originally found at Elderscrolls.com}}

As the Third Era progressed, the Empire's elite soldiers—who would come to be known as the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]]—were tasked with collecting the remains of Numidium. Zurin Arctus became known as the Underking, and sent his minions forth to search for the Mantella.{{ref|name=DFMantellaLetters}}

====Wulfharth====
Another account claims the Underking was [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]], a High King of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], who periodically returned to life at several points in history, and was thought to be a mortal avatar of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]]{{ref|group=UOL|name=FPKirkbride}} (the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic version of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]).{{ref|name=TAH}}{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} His later incarnation, the Ash-King, is said to have been summoned back to life to fight the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] at the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] in the First Era,{{ref|name=TAH}}{{ref|name=FSOKW}} and again to [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] in {{Year|2E 572}} to repel the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]].{{ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}}{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} Some accounts of the latter battle refer to the Ash-King as "the Underking".{{ref|name=TAH}}{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}}

Wulfharth the Underking was an advisor to and secret magical ally of {{Lore Link|Hjalti Early-Beard|Hjalti Early-Beard}}, who would in time become Emperor Tiber Septim. When the emperor turned his attention to Morrowind, the Underking urged him to conquer the land, as Wulfharth had long hated elvenkind. However, the Armistice was signed instead; Wulfharth felt betrayed by the concessions given to the Dunmer and abandoned the Empire. Without the Underking, Tiber Septim abandoned hope of creating a united Tamriel.{{ref|name=TAH}}

Meanwhile, as Numidium now belonged to the Empire, Tiber Septim had Zurin Arctus research it. The Battlemage eventually learned of the Battle of Red Mountain, of Numidium's true purpose and power, and of the Underking's involvement. However, his research was based off incomplete Dwemer plans. Arctus came to the conclusion that Numidium was powered by the "heart" (life force){{ref|name=DFWho}} of Lorkhan's physical body—that is, his mortal avatar, Wulfharth.{{ref|name=TAH}}

Upon hearing this revelation, Tiber Septim realized that with Numidium, he could complete his conquest of Tamriel, and set a trap for Wulfharth. The Underking returned upon being told the emperor had decided to attack the Tribunal after all, but was met by the Imperial guards. A battle ensued, in which Zurin Arctus cast a Soul Trap spell on the Underking. As he died, Wulfharth's "heart" tore a hole through the Battlemage's chest, killing him. The emperor then appeared, collected the soul gem containing Wulfharth's heart (the Mantella), and covered up the incident by claiming Arctus had been killed while attempting a coup. Numidium was then used to conquer Summerset Isle; shortly after the battle, a "rotting undead wizard" appeared and blew Numidium to pieces, though he was then crushed by the golem's death throes.{{ref|name=TAH}} Proponents of the Arcturian Heresy claim this wizard is Zurin Arctus, whom they view as the one true "Tiber Septim" that renders the current Septim Regime illegitimate.{{ref|name=Redguard Forum Madness|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Redguard Forum Madness|ns_base=General}}}}

===Third Era===
[[File:OBR-statue-Pelagius IV.jpg|thumb|left|Pelagius, grandson of Tiber Septim, who the Underking advised]]
====Aftermath====
Tiber Septim was then crowned First Emperor of Tamriel, and ruled for thirty-eight more years. During his reign, the holy site of Sancre Tor had been overrun with evil. Tiber sent his four mightiest Blade agents to determine what had befell this place. It turned out to be the works of Zurin Arctus, the Underking who cursed the Blades to a permanent undead state in which they guarded the halls of the ruined site as an act of vengeance against Tiber.{{Ref|name=OBGoR|[[Oblivion:Ghost of Rielus|Ghost of Rielus]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

Upon his death, Tiber Septim  was succeeded by his grandson, [[Lore:Pelagius Septim I|Pelagius I]], to whom the Underking also appeared, claiming to have been sent by Tiber himself to help the new emperor run his Empire.{{ref|name=TAH}} Under the guidance of The Underking, Pelagius continued his predecessor's legacy with the prosperous and great rule of his Golden Age. This was, however, cut short when Pelagius was murdered by Dark Brotherhood assassins.{{ref|name=BHOTE}} 

Another event that took place in this early Third Era time period was Zurin enchanting a piece of [[Lore:Dragonbone Mail|marvelously crafted Dragon-bone armor]] as a means of paying off a debt he owed to a traveling warrior.{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} Despite these various deeds, the Underking had expended nearly all of his power in his defeat of the Numidium and was forced to retire to a subterranean vault in High Rock where he could slowly rebuild his strength over many years.{{ref|name=10th}}{{ref|name=DFC}}

====Imperial Simulacrum====
During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]], the [[Lore:Hero|Eternal Champion]] was known to have encountered the Underking's minions, who were described as fanatical and seemingly insane. Of particular note, the Champion was tasked with retrieving a document from the lair of [[Lore:Golthog the Dark|Golthog the Dark]], a necromancer rumored to be servant of the Underking in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. In return, Queen {{Lore Link|Blubamka}} of [[Lore:Rihad|Rihad]] assisted in uncovering the location of a piece of the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]].

The Eternal Champion also recovered the [[Lore:Ring of Khajiiti|Ring of Khajiiti]] from a crypt somewhere in Tamriel where it had been reportedly hidden by the Underking, with the only known clue to its whereabouts at the time being a map located in a dungeon in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]].{{ref|name=Arena|Events of ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

====The Warp in the West====
In {{Year|3E 401}}, the location of the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]] was discovered by [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] the sorceress, queen mother of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]].{{ref|name=DFC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}} Around this time, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] received word that the Totem of Tiber Septim had also been found.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=DFC}} In {{Year|3E 405}}, Uriel VII sent an [[Lore:Hero#The Agent|Agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] to the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region to investigate the death of King [[Lore:Lysandus|Lysandus]] of Daggerfall, and also to find a missing letter he had written to that kingdom's queen.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} The Underking figured prominently in many of the events to follow, culminating in the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

Prince [[Lore:Arthago|Arthago]] of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]], eldest son of King [[Lore:Camaron|Camaron]] and Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]], had vanished in {{Year|3E 400}}; a popular rumor was that he had been taken by the Underking.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}{{ref|name=NFOS|{{Cite Book|Night Falls on Sentinel}}}} Prince [[Lore:Lhotun|Lhotun]] wanted to know the full story, and hired the Agent to find out more. With the help of the Underking's minions, the Agent was able to uncover the truth—that the king and queen were behind the disappearance.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:The Missing Prince#Finding the Evidence|Death Certificate]] found during [[Daggerfall:The Missing Prince|The Missing Prince]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The Agent's work in Sentinel eventually led to being contacted by the Underking to retrieve an item stolen by the [[Lore:Mannimarco|King of Worms]] and his necromancers, which they had cursed and given to the Blades. The Agent was able to infiltrate the Blades' hideout in [[Lore:Ykalon|Ykalon]] and recover the item before it could bring harm upon the group.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}}

The Agent eventually discovered that King Lysandus had been murdered by {{Lore Link|Woodborne|Lord Woodborne}}, a noble from [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], in a bid to attain power—and that he had for some time been in possession of the Totem of Tiber Septim.{{ref|name=Daggerfall}} The Agent then recovered the Totem from the heavily guarded treasury of Castle Daggerfall, which immediately sparked a bidding war between seven major powers of the Iliac Bay (including the Empire) for its possession. The Underking was one of these, and made his offer:

:''"Centuries ago, Tiber Septim ruled the land and forged an empire with great Numidium. The secret of Numidium's power lies in its heart, carried within the Mantella. It is the heart of Tiber Septim's battlemage. It is my heart. It is my Mantella. It is my Totem. It belongs to me, and to none other. I have won and lost an empire. I have no desire to tamper further with mortal affairs. I have in my possession [[Lore:Necromancer's Amulet|Necromancer's Amulet]], one of the world's greatest artifacts. You may have it in {{sic|in|description=duplicate word}} exchange for the Totem."{{ref|name=DFWho}}''

In the end, the Agent gave the Totem to the Underking, then traveled to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] and recovered the Mantella.{{ref|name=DFJourney|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Journey to Aetherius]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} At the Underking's bidding, Numidium was activated once more; he awoke from his centuries-long sleep, raced across the bay to meet the automaton, and rejoined with his heart in a massive explosion.{{ref|name=DFJourney}} For a brief moment, he was flesh and blood again, then finally able to attain the true death he had long sought.{{ref|name=DFJourney}} The destruction of Numidium created an anti-magic zone of several miles' radius around the area.{{ref|name=10th}}

Impossibly, through some sort of [[Lore:Dragon Break|time distortion]], each of the other six factions simultaneously obtained the Totem as well, and activated Numidium to their own ends. The resulting cataclysm permanently reshaped the kingdoms of the Iliac Bay, and the incident was later dubbed by the Blades as the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=TWITW}}

====Oblivion Crisis====
In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] traveled to the ruins of [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]] in the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] to recover the {{Lore Link|Armor of Tiber Septim}}. The ruins had been sealed by the Blades in {{Year|3E 36}} after four members had gone missing within. Once inside, the Hero encountered the ghosts of the four Blades, who had been cursed by the Underking along with the rest of the ruins. With their help, the Hero was able to break the curse and recover the armor.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==Servants==
The Underking was known to send his forces and undead soldiers on errands whose goals only he was privy to, though no uniform was needed to declare allegiance to him.{{ref|name=ARManual}}{{Ref|name=ARFaction}} It is said those who associated with the Underking risked their sanity, with his forces being known for madness and for their fanaticism in battle.{{Ref|name=ARFaction}} One particular group of mages and undead creatures wandered the land, claiming to be Agents of the Underking.{{ref|name=DFGD}}{{Ref|name=ARFaction}} Many among their ranks included unsavory characters such as rogues, assassins, and [[Lore:Nightblades|nightblades]]. Of the undead they employed, [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]], [[Lore:Lich|liches]], [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]], [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]], [[Lore:Skeleton|skeletons]], [[Lore:Ghoul|ghouls]], and [[Lore:Zombie|zombies]] were known to be among them.{{Ref|name=ARFaction}} 

The Underking was also known to have Goblin and Orcish minions,{{Ref|name=ARFaction}} Golthog the Dark was a necromancer rumored to serve the Underking who commanded a band of Goblins.{{Ref|name=ARQB|[[Arena:Queen Blubamka|Queen Blubamka]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Monsters such as [[Lore:Lizard Man|lizardmen]], [[Lore:Golem|artificial constructs]] like [[Lore:Iron Golem|Iron Golems]], and creatures from the [[Lore:Oblivion|Nether Planes]] such as [[Lore:Hell Hound|Hell Hounds]] and [[Lore:Fire Daemon|Fire Daemons]] all found places among his ranks.{{Ref|name=ARFaction}}
