# Lore:House Dres

[[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] (also called '''Clan Dres'''){{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} is one of the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], with presence only on the mainland. It governs the Dres District of southeastern Morrowind from its ancestral capital city of [[Lore:Tear|Tear]], also called 'Dres' and noted for its proximity to [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]].{{ref|name=Falen|[[Morrowind:Drulene Falen|Drulene Falen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The district also included parts of the fertile [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] plain, the southern areas of the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]], and bordered the [[Lore:House Indoril|Indoril District]], [[Lore:House Hlaalu|Hlaalu District]], and the swamps and marshes of the Black Marsh regions of [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] and [[Lore:Thornmarsh|Thornmarsh]].{{ref|name=GHOM|{{Cite Book|Great Houses of Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1M}} In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], House Dres also controlled the cities of [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]]{{ref|name=Othral|[[Online:Drathus Othral|Drathus Othral]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]]{{ref|name=Holgunn|[[Online:Holgunn One-Eye|Holgunn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:City at the Spire|City at the Spire]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} in central Morrowind. At least once, House Dres managed to gain a minor foothold in the Telvanni lands of the eponymous peninsula.{{ref|name=Virian|[[Online:Naryu Virian|Naryu Virian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}{{ref|name=Camp|[[Online:House Dres Encampment|House Dres Encampment]] locale in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}} House Dres has had no recorded settlements on the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. Despite this, some Dres families lived on the island in ancient times before relocating to the mainland, and some Dres ancestral tombs may still be found on the isle. Among them is the ancient {{Lore Link|Dreloth}} clan, a cadet branch of the Great House, as well as the Maren, Othrelas, Serano, and [[Lore:Velas|Velas]] clans.{{Ref|name=ONLoadDreloth|[[Online:Dreloth Ancestral Tomb|Dreloth Ancestral Tomb]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=DresTombs|[[Online:Ancestral Tombs|Ancestral Tomb]] markers in ESO:Morrowind}} It was formally established as a Great House before the middle of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] by Grandmaster [[Lore:Thalthil Dres|Thalthil Dres]] after a slave-raid over [[Lore:Thorn|Thorn]].{{ref|name=DresMedal|Description of [[Online:Contraband D#Dres Grandmaster Memorial Medal|Dres Grandmaster Memorial Medal]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=UHD|{{Cite Book|Understanding House Dres}}}}{{ref|name=DresCup|[[Online:Bal Foyen Antiquities|Great House Gold Chalice]] lore entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Dres have a mainly rural but still very wealthy agrarian agricultural society, maintaining vast [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]] plantations on the plains and marshes surrounding Tear.{{ref|name=GHOM}} The Dres were also the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] who shipped the majority of slaves to the other Great Houses (on par with the Indoril and Telvanni),{{ref|name=Brallion|[[Morrowind:Brallion|Brallion]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}  keeping thousands of captives, mostly [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s and [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] in their infamous slave-pens of Tear and the surrounding plantations.{{ref|name=PGE1M}} The Dres are also known among the Great Houses for maintaining a persistent tradition of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] [[Lore:Good Daedra|worship]] and [[Lore:Ancestor Worship|ancestor reverence]], their staunch support of unity between the Great Houses, and their emphasis on inter-House relations and the improvement of Dunmeri society as a whole.{{ref|name=UHD}}{{Ref|name=VivecTES3}}<noinclude>

Saint [[Lore:Llothis|Llothis]] the Pious is the patron saint of House Dres.{{ref|name=MDGH|{{Cite Book|Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses}}}}

==History==
===First Era===
:''"The isolationist stance of House Dres was instilled in its customs and world outlook from the very beginning. Not only do we consider the other cultures and races to be inferior to the Dunmer, we deem any extended contact with them where we were not in total control to present a danger to Dunmer society. Ideas, like a plague, can spread without warning and prove to be impossible to contain. Instead, we should concentrate on inter-House relations and improving the position of the Dunmer to make our society stronger and eternal."'' – ''[[Lore:Understanding House Dres|Understanding House Dres]]''
[[File:ON-place-House Dres Crypts.jpg|thumb|left|The House Dres burial crypts in Kragenmoor, excavated in the First Era]]
House Dres was founded in ancient times, formally established after a successful slave-raid on Thorn by Grandmaster Thalthil Dres before the middle of the First Era.{{ref|name=DresMedal}}{{ref|name=UHD}}{{ref|name=DresCup}} The House has existed since the time of the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] and the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]].{{Ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}} By the 84th year of the Golden Peace, the Dres had already built their fortune on saltrice plantations and slave labor. They prided themselves on maintaining a persistent tradition of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship and ancestor reverence. According to Grand Historian Solamar Dres, the isolationist stance of House Dres was instilled in its customs and world outlook from the very beginning.{{ref|name=UHD}} It is said in [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]'s Sermons that the Chimeri hero, {{Lore Link|Dres Khizumet-e}} fought against the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s in the {{Lore Link|Conquest of Morrowind}} during the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]] in the early-First Era. He was devoured by the demon chieftain, {{Lore Link|Chemua}} and his spirit was sent to assassinate the Hortator, but it failed. Chemua could turn the rain of Veloth into bile and destroyed six Chimeri villages before he was slain by Vivec and the Hortator.{{Ref|name=36LoVS9|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9}}}} The now extinct [[Lore:Cantemiric Velothi|Cantemiric Velothi]] settled in northeast Argonia near modern Dres territory, and it is said by [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] that ancient Chimer territories were "stolen" by Argonia, possibly referring to House Dres territory once stretching as far south as the formerly Dres city of [[Lore:Thorn|Thorn]].{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Argonia}}}}{{Ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}}
[[File:MW-concept-Great House Dres.jpg|thumb|right|A warrior of Great House Dres]]
Prior to the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]], thirty of the most influential and revered Chimer Clans worked together to build the fabled Library of Andule, a grand repository containing the genealogical records related to the earliest [[Lore:Veloth|Velothi]] settlers and other assorted knowledge about the earliest history of the Great Houses.{{ref|name=Bradyn|[[Online:Librarian Bradyn|Librarian Bradyn]]'s dialogue in ESO:Morrowind}} Four of the thirty Revered Families hailed from House Dres; the Maren, Othrelas, Serano, and Velas Clans.{{ref|name=DresTombs}} The locations of the tombs were presumed lost in the aftermath of the War of the First Council, along with the Library of Andule and the records contained within.{{ref|name=Bradyn}} The four tombs were rediscovered in {{Year|2E 582}}, alongside the other twenty-six families and the Library of Andule itself.{{ref|name=LibraryOfAnduleQuests|Events of [[Online:The Ancestral Tombs|The Ancestral Tombs]] and [[Online:The Lost Library|The Lost Library]] in ESO:Morrowind}}

Like the rest of the Great Houses, House Dres participated in the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] during the War of the First Council under the leadership of Hortator Indoril [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] around {{Year|1E 700}}.{{Ref|name=TWOTFC}}

Over time, House Dres solidified their position as Morrowind's agrarian powerhouse, claiming that their agricultural might fed Morrowind and providing the majority of the foodstuffs necessary for the existence and continuation of Dunmer society. The House's vast wealth was drawn from this agricultural dominance, supplemented "nicely" by their control of the slave trade. They believed that the Dunmer should concentrate on inter-House relations and improving the position of Morrowind, in an effort "to make our society stronger and eternal".{{ref|name=UHD}} They have been at the forefront of the Dunmeri slave trade since the middle of the First Era.{{ref|name=DresCup}}

House Dres adopted the linked chains of a slave's manacles as their heraldry, referencing the source of their power and their dominance of the slave trade. They likewise adopted the color grey as a designation.{{ref|name=DresColor|House Dres banners and attire as seen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DresCup}} 

In the middle of the First Era, the burial crypts of the noble Dres family were excavated beneath the city of Kragenmoor by Argonian and Khajiiti slaves.{{ref|name=DresCrypts|[[Online:House Dres Crypts|House Dres Crypts]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point during or prior to the eleventh century of the First Era, the Dunmer of House Indoril and House Dres came into a little-known conflict with the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]] known as the War for Silyanorn - the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] site that would one day become the city of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]]. The war included the naval Battle of Tear Harbor, wherein the Indoril fleet was defeated by the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial fleet]] offshore of the House Dres capital city. The victorious [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]] claimed the former Indoril flagship and christened it the Dominus Fatum. In {{Year|1E 1033}} during the reign of [[Lore:Hestra|Empress Hestra]], the vessel's crew would go on to capture the infamous Argonian pirate [[Lore:Red Bramman|Red Bramman]] in [[Lore:Blackrose|Blackrose]] by braving the uncharted rivers of Shadowfen, though the Dominus Fatum was lost to the bogs of [[Lore:Murkmire|Murkmire]] in the process. The ship's wreck remained mostly intact for over two thousand years and was re-discovered in {{Year|2E 582}}, suspended by a large tree in Murkmire.{{ref|name=Callidus|[[Online:Strillian Callidus|Strillian Callidus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The outcome of the war is unknown, but Stormhold - built upon the ruins of Silyanorn - was under Dunmer control as late as {{Year|2E 572}}.{{ref|name=SCOS|{{Cite Book|Stormhold, City of Shadowfen}}}}{{ref|name=SFLS|[[Online:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] loading screen text in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Keshu|{{Cite Book|Keshu}}}}

Great House Dres' role during the eighty-year [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]] against the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] is unknown.

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-Grandmaster Omin Dres.jpg|thumb|right|Grandmaster Omin Dres, circa 2E 582]]
:''"To spread culture and truth to the benighted: this is our commitment and burden."'' — ''[[Lore:Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses|Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]]''

During the Second Era, House Dres controlled the city of Kragenmoor in [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]], where Grandmaster Omin Dres, leader of the House in the mid-Second Era, resided as of {{Year|2E 582}}. Dres Councilman Drathus Othral ruled Ebonheart on the Dres' behalf, though [[Lore:House Redoran|Redoran]], Hlaalu, and Indoril also held significant influence in the city. The House also maintained plantations around the capital of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=DresFarm|[[Online:House Dres Farmstead|House Dres Farmstead]] location in [[Online:Lagomere|Lagomere]]}} In northern Black Marsh, the cities of Stormhold and Thorn were both under Dres rule. Stormhold served as the Dres' primary foothold in Black Marsh,{{ref|name=Keshu}}{{ref|name=SCOS}}{{ref|name=SFLS}} while Thorn was home to Councilman Glathis Dres, and was surrounded by slave-operated saltrice plantations.{{ref|name=FAtS|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}} Despite the opulence and wealth of Dres nobles, thieves around this time feared the Great House, encapsulated by the popular mantra of "Don't cross the Dres". Dres justice was rumored to not bother with [[Lore:Ordinator|Ordinators]] or local guardsmen.{{ref|name=TGHATU|{{Cite Book|The Great Houses and Their Uses}}}} In Black Marsh, House Dres employed the Argonians of the [[Lore:Archein|Archein]] tribe in the capture and selling of other Argonians.{{ref|name=FP|{{Cite Book|name=FP|Freedom's Price}}}}{{ref|name=SCOS}}{{ref|name=FAtS}}

In {{Year|2E 559}}, a dispute over a House Hlaalu trading post in contested territory erupted into an armed conflict known as the [[Lore:War of Two Houses|War of Two Houses]]. The so-called "war" consisted of only one battle, fought by House Dres mercenaries and House Hlaalu guards at the trading post. Despite outnumbering the Hlaalu five-to-one, the conflict ended with a decisive House Hlaalu victory when the leader of the Hlaalu guards, Purilla Falen, defended a portal that allowed a full cohort of Hlaalu battle-merchants to swarm the Dres mercenaries.{{ref|name=WTH|{{Cite Book|War of Two Houses}}}} 

Some years before the battle of [[Lore:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]], the Argonian hero-to-be [[Lore:Keshu the Black Fin|Keshu the Black Fin]] traveled to the Dres-controlled city of Stormhold in Black Marsh. After two of her companions were abducted by House Dres slavers, Keshu - with the assistance of a younger [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] - stormed the House Dres slaver's enclave on the edge of the city and rescued their companions. The experience prompted Keshu to cobble together a small band of militant Argonians informally known as the  Black Fin's Legion, who proceeded to wage "war" against House Dres by raiding Dres slave caravans headed for Morrowind for a number of seasons. The conflict abruptly ended in {{Year|2E 572}} when the Black Fin's Legion answered a call for aid from Jorunn the Skald-King, and participated in the battle of Vivec's Antlers, and subsequent alliance.{{ref|name=Keshu}}   
[[File:LO-misc-Ragman of Tear.jpg|thumb|left|100px|Ragman of Tear]][[File:LO-misc-Dres slaver.jpg|thumb|right|150px|A slaver of House Dres near the [[Lore:Black Marsh|southern]] border]]
Shortly before the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] in {{Year|2E 572}}, Councilman Glathis Dres was murdered in Thorn by his Argonian slave, [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]], who would go on to lead the force of Argonian Shellbacks at the [[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|Battle of Vivec's Antlers]].{{ref|name=FAtS}} After the defeat of [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]]'s [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] forces, the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was signed. In recognition for their assistance, the Great Houses of Morrowind (with the exception of [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]]) liberated their slaves. House Dres released the slaves of allied Pact races, most notably the Argonians,{{ref|name=Hlor|[[Online:Nadras Hlor|Nadras Hlor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but continued to unofficially maintain their slave-holding ways with non-Pact races.{{ref|name=NH|[[Online:Noisy-Hunter|Noisy-Hunter]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PP:C|{{Cite Book|Pact Pamphlet: Congratulations!}}}} The Dres were noted for the "persistent slander and calumny" that surrounded their membership in the Ebonheart Pact in the years that followed. They nevertheless remained loyal to the alliance and all it stood for, and 'officially' refrained from condoning the perpetuation of slavery within the areas they administrated.{{ref|group=UOL|name=ZOSCorrespondence|[[General:ZOS Correspondence|ZOS Correspondence]]}} After the eruption of the [[Lore:Alliance War|War of the Three Banners]] in {{Year|2E 580}}, House Dres' warriors would be among those that fought for control of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]].{{ref|name=CyroBanners|Presence of House Dres banners in the [[Online:Southern Morrowind Gate|Southern Morrowind Gate]] Pact basecamp in Cyrodiil}} As a result of the Ebonheart Pact's creation, the formerly Dres-ruled Black Marsh cities of Stormhold and Thorn were returned to Argonian control, with local tribes benefitting from cessation of the raids of the Dunmeri slavers to the north.{{ref|name=SFLS}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

In {{Year|2E 573}}, the Dres capital city of Tear suffered a series of disasters, including summer chills, a flood, a miasma that settled over the city, fouled drinking water, and plague. With the healers of the city overwhelmed, and nearby communities declining aid for fear of spreading the plague, the healer Rindral Thirano was driven to bargain with the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] for the city's survival. At the cost of taking all the ills of the city upon himself, Rindral ensured Tear's recovery and good health.{{ref|name=DwaDP|{{Cite Book|Deal with a Daedric Prince|ns_base=Online}}}}  

During the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]]'s invasion of Stonefalls in {{Year|2E 582}}, House Dres struggled to maintain order in Ebonheart.{{ref|name=EbonheartESO|Events of [[ON:Restoring Order|Restoring Order]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Kragenmoor, a grief-stricken Tanval Indoril's Vanguard marched on Kragenmoor and took Grandmaster Omin's son Sen hostage, in exchange for access to the peak of [[Lore:Tormented Spire|Tormented Spire]] and the [[Lore:Brothers of Strife (creature)|Brother of Strife, Sadal]]. The [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]]'s arrival in the city shortly afterwards saw Sen Dres released.{{ref|name=KragenmoorESO|Events of [[ON:Aggressive Negotiations|Aggressive Negotiations]] and [[ON:Saving the Son|Saving the Son]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At the behest of Grandmaster Omin, the Vestige proceeded to slaughter the remaining Indoril Vanguard in retaliation for their transgressions.{{ref|name=Kragenmoor2ESO|Events of [[ON:Vengeance for House Dres|Vengeance for House Dres]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Deshaan, Houses Dres and Hlaalu clashed around the ruins of Mzithumz,{{ref|name=MzithumzESO|Events of [[ON:Mechanical Murder|Mechanical Murder]], [[On:That Which Matters Most|That Which Matters Most]], and [[ON:Dissonant Commands|Dissonant Commands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while Dres members unwittingly furthered the agenda of the Maulborn Cult in Mournhold.{{ref|name=MournholdDresESO|Events of [[ON:A Favor Returned|A Favor Returned]] and [[ON:The Mournhold Underground|The Mournhold Underground]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the Telvanni Peninsula, House Dres agents conducted "Operation Siphon" - a long-term intelligence gathering scheme - against their "hated rivals" in the House Telvanni leadership near the settlement of [[Lore:Ald Isra|Ald Isra]] on behalf of Councilor Ralds.{{ref|name=DO|{{Cite Book|House Dres Orders|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=RoOS|{{Cite Book|Report on Operation Siphon|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=LCT|{{Cite Book|Letter Concerning Tredecim|ns_base=Online}}}} The Operation was partially thwarted when the Telvanni Masters became aware of one of the two spies in their midst, and a [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] assassin was sent to dispatch the individual.{{ref|name=AldIsra|Events of [[Online:Tracing Shadows|Tracing Shadows]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}

Bounty Hunters in the employ of House Dres during this time were presented with writs of arrest that authorized them as an agent of the Dres commissioned by the Hortator of the Great House to  re-acquire "live property". Said agents were fully authorized to distribute appropriate compensation to any guard command willing to allow the agent to go about the business of the re-acquisition of missing "property" without hindrance.{{ref|name=AW|{{Cite Book|Arrest Writ|ns_base=Online}}}} Despite their xenophobic outlook, the Dunmer of House Dres were known to trade with organizations from outside Morrowind during the mid-Second Era. In {{Year|2E 582}}, Grandmaster Omin Dres sent the [[Lore:Skywatch|Darkvale Coster]], a large Dominion-affiliated merchant group operating out of [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]], a missive indicating his interest in commerce. The Darkvale Coster, in turn, sent a representative bearing trade goods, such as potions, to Kragenmoor.{{ref|name=Darkvale|[[Online:Caalorne|Caalorne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

At some point after {{Year|2E 850}} and before {{Year|2E 864}}, the Khajiit [[Lore:S'rathra|S'rathra]] funded a raid on a slave camp near Tear with his own wealth, with the objective to liberate Khajiiti slaves from the camp. Among the participants of the raid was the famed [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] hero, [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]].{{ref|name=Origin|{{Cite Book|The Origin of Cyrus!|ns_base=Books}}}}

In the late Second Era, [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s legions turned their eyes towards Morrowind. Following House Indoril's lead, House Dres and House Redoran swore to resist the western invaders to the death. However, the Dres and Indoril advocated allowing the western invaders to enter the interior of Morrowind, where they'd battle them via a guerrilla war. With the Redoran manning the western borders alone, the Telvanni remaining neutral, and the Hlaalu advocating accommodation, Morrowind was disunited. When [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] signed the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] after only a handful of skirmishes, House Dres grudgingly bowed to the will of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]], but only with strong provisions protecting slavery.{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}}

===Third Era===

:''"House Dres represents the past of pre-Tribunal Great House culture, a persistent tradition of Daedra- and ancestor-worshipping civilized Dunmer clans."'' – ''[[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]''{{Ref|name=VivecTES3|[[Morrowind:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}
[[File:LG-cardart-Dres Tormentor.png|thumb|120px|left|A Dres Tormentor]]
House Dres lost most of their support from their traditional allies when the members of House Indoril began to commit suicide after the Armistice was signed. They remained hostile to Imperial culture throughout the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], and in particular refuted any attempts to limit the institution of slavery.{{ref|name=GHOM}} Further complicating matters was the loss of both Ebonheart and Kragenmoor to House Hlaalu rule during the Third Era.{{ref|name=conceptmap|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-map-Morrowind Concept.jpg|Concept map of Morrowind, released during the Tenth Anniversary of TES]]}}{{ref|name=CCL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Caius Cosades' Letter|ns_base=General}}}}

In {{Year|3E 396}}, during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] of [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]], a slave revolt was instigated by Argonian rebels in the Dres District after a Dunmeri merchant named [[Lore:Roris|Roris]] was brutally tortured to death for refusing to renounce his faith in the Tribunal. This escalated until the swampy plains erupted in blood and fire, with a full-scale conflict between Morrowind and Black Marsh brewing. The [[Lore:Arnesian War|Arnesian War]] formally began when news of Roris' death reached the rest of Morrowind. The Dunmer's armies ultimately defeated the Argonians and expanded Morrowind's territory to the south.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}

Prior to the coronation of Prince [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|Hlaalu Helseth]] in {{Year|3E 427}}, a House Dres agent infiltrated the Prince's inner circle of advisors on behalf of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] [[Lore:Dhaunayne Aundae|Dhaunayne Aundae]]. A few months later, a letter began circulating around the ashlands of Vvardenfell, and later the mainland of Morrowind. The letter, recounting one of Helseth's schemes to root out spies in his midst, was addressed to Dhaunayne and purportedly written by the unidentified Dres agent. Though House Dres publicly denounced the work and no record existed of an association between Dhaunayne Aundae and the House, Prince Helseth and [[Lore:Barenziah|Queen Barenziah]] had it privately printed into bound copies and sent to libraries and booksellers throughout Morrowind.{{ref|name=AGAD|{{Cite Book|A Game at Dinner}}}}

Following the opening of Vvardenfell to settlement in {{Year|3E 414}} by the Empire, both Dres and Indoril strongly objected to the Imperial opening of Vvardenfell to colonization for political and religious reasons, and both were reluctant to join the land rush even years later for fear of being seen as hypocrites.{{ref|name=KRP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ken Rolston's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Due to a lack of holdings in Vvardenfell, House Dres was entirely removed from the events that saw the rise of the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]], the destruction of [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] and the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], the end of the [[Lore:The Blight|Blight]], and the downfall of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|name=MWEvents|Events of the [[Morrowind:Main Quest|Main Quest]] of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

By the year {{Year|3E 432}}, it was said that House Dres had changed their approach to the changing times, and begun to embrace the arrival of new traditions alongside the return of the old. As a result, the Great House appeared to be on the rise.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, rumors circulating in the province of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] suggested that House Dres had finally accepted Imperial rule and renounced the slave trade. The House sought new allies in King Hlaalu Helseth and Great House Hlaalu, who were now the dominant power in the land, through Helseth's marriage to Lady Dres Vendama of Bodrum.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL}} Further rumors suggested the alliance between House Dres and House Hlaalu went on to pick apart "the carcass of ruined Indoril" together.{{ref|name=OBRumors|Rumors heard during [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} The truth of these rumors, or the outcome of the alliance, is unknown. 

Shortly afterwards that same year, the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] withdrew from Morrowind during and in the aftermath of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{ref|name=DBArano|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} It is unknown how House Dres weathered the events of the Crisis, or what became of their newly-forged alliance with House Hlaalu after the latter's loss of influence and Great House status.

===Fourth Era===
:''"They cut a bloody swath across most of the southern region of Morrowind. Nobody seems certain why it happened... an attempt at expansion, revenge for the enslavement of their people or perhaps both. Whatever the case may be, thousands of Dunmer needlessly lost their lives and many more were exiled from their homeland."'' —Adril Arano

In {{Year|4E 5}}, the eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] sent tremors throughout Morrowind. The Dres lands near the marshy border regions suddenly became submerged, having been engulfed by the swamps. The southern wall of Tear collapsed as a result.{{ref|name=TRY|{{Cite Book|The Red Year}}}} Following the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], the Argonians of Black Marsh invaded Morrowind and ravaged the southern and eastern reaches of the province before being stopped and expelled.{{ref|name=DBArano|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} While it is not clear whether the invasion was an attempt at expansion or revenge for the enslavement of their people, the Dres District was the most southerly region and the home of their former slave-masters. It would have been the first area they laid siege to, and where the most personal prejudice would have played a role. While House Dres still sits on the Grand Council of Morrowind and is thus known to have survived the calamities in some form,{{ref|name=DBMorvayn|[[Skyrim:Lleril Morvayn|Lleril Morvayn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} the modern-day condition of the House and its holdings is unknown.

==Culture==
The focal point of Dres culture and economy is agriculture,{{ref|name=GHOM}} and their most notable produces being saltrice and lichen.{{ref|name=FAtS}}{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} Despite their once notorious status as slavers, it is not an essential part of their identity, and the Dres have gone through periods of banning the use of Argonian slaves,{{ref|name=SFLS}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI}} and even fighting with other Houses to keep slavery outlawed.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=OBRumors}}

Theater is a notable aspect of Dres culture. The [[Lore:Mirthless Order|Mirthless Order]], based in the traditionally Dres city of [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]], protect the sacredness of humor of an ancient rite written by Dunmer comedians, and would even deal death to any who performed the rite improperly.{{Ref|name=GR|[[ON:Geril Randoro|Geril Randoro]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|Events of [[Online:This One's a Classic|This One's a Classic]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Certain members of House Dres are fluent in [[Lore:Dark Elvish|Dark Elvish]]. The Dres are skilled mages,{{ref|name=Origin}} utilizing a strategy of striking with all their might to win against enemies by flooding them with destructive spells.{{Ref|name=CCC|group=UOL}} Agents of Dres make skilled spies.{{ref|name=AGAD}}

The Dres believe that the law should serve to benefit them, not be their master, and will decree laws secretly and write them ambiguously. During this process, they consult the [[Lore:Constellations|heavens]], which encourage clever interpretations thanks to their everchanging nature.{{Ref|name=CCC|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Cunning, Contempt, Confusion|ns_base=GEN}}}} The Hlaalu say the Dres are skilled at making money, but have not learned how not to make enemies.{{ref|name=GF|{{Cite Book|Grasping Fortune}}}}

===Agriculture===
The Dres are trained in handling all manner of megafauna insects of Morrowind. Among these arthopods are six-legged insects used as beasts of burden, which appear similar to [[Lore:Nix-Ox|Nix-Oxen]].{{ref|name=Origin}} Trained [[Lore:Nix-Hound|Nix-Hounds]] often accompany Dres warriors as guard dogs.{{ref|[[ON:Nix-Hound|Nix-Hound]] usage in [[Online:House Dres Farmstead|House Dres Farmstead]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Parrapton|Parraptons]]{{ref|name=TPTheBlueDawn|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP/The Blue Dawn|''The Blue Dawn'']]}} are insects typically the size of [[Lore:Horse|horses]] that are used as aerial mounts by House Dres, and are kept in stables resembling tall [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]] silos.{{ref|name=Origin|{{Cite Book|The Origin of Cyrus!|ns_base=Books}}}} Members of Dres are said hold bull-riding matches with their parraptons.{{ref|name=TPTheBlueDawn|group=UOL}}

Following a strict hierarchy, approval from the queen is needed to ride her children, who is a behemoth twice the size of her kin. Such an audience must not be taken lightly, for she may deny a request for fear for her children's safety. Furthermore, the queen also handpicks which of her children is best fit for the rider. Riders powder their hands and rub them together to signal to their parrapton, which depending on context, will signal when they are to fly or land.{{ref|name=TPTheBlueDawn|group=UOL}}

* Thalthil Dres' raid on Thorn that established House Dres as a Great House is memorialized in a heartbreaking popular silhouette-painting called "The Slave-Coffles of Thorn".{{Ref|name=ONContrabandP|[[Online:Contraband P#Painting of "The Slave-Coffles of Thorn"|Painting of "The Slave-Coffles of Thorn"]] contraband in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
* Prince [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus Mede]] (born in {{Year|4E 26}}) was said to have ''"rescued an entire colony of [Argonians] from slavery"'' some time before {{Year|4E 48}}, bringing into question the survival of the slave trade after both Helseth's abolition in {{Year|3E 433}} and the Accession War in {{Year|4E 6}}.{{Ref|name=TIC|[[Books:Greg Keyes Novels/Annaïg Hoïnart|Annaïg]]'s dialogue on page 19 of [[Books:The Infernal City|The Infernal City]]}} 
* Several settlements appeared in a pre-release concept map of Morrowind under the control of House Dres during the Third Era. These Dres-ruled settlements included Tear, [[Lore:Necrom|Necrom]], [[Lore:Alavelis|Alavelis]], [[Lore:Silnion Vale|Silnion Vale]], [[Lore:Rathendis Falls|Rathendis Falls]], and [[Lore:Salen Vulgate|Salen Vulgate]].{{ref|name=conceptmap|group=UOL}} Likewise, [[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]] implies Dres-dominated towns also include {{Lore Link|Bodrum|Bodrum Mora}}, Daruhn, Tagghil Plantation, and Tel Dresori, and that they recently took control of western settlements such as Ald Ra'athim.{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 12: Playing Houses|''Playing Houses'']]}}
* According to Loranna's RP, there is a Grand Council of House Dres, of which Lord Minsero of Tel Dresori was a senior member.{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL}}
