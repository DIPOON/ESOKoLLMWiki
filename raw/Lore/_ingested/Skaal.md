</noinclude>The [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] are an offshoot tribe of [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s living in a [[Lore:Skaal Village|village]] in northeastern [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]. They are known as a trusting and hospitable people, welcoming strangers into their village without any suspicion. Their village is located to the east of the [[Lore:Isild River|Isild River]], along the [[Lore:Felsaad Coast|Felsaad Coast]] just north of [[Lore:Lake Fjalding|Lake Fjalding]]. The Skaal believe Solstheim was originally part of Skyrim, which was turned into an artificial island during a climactic battle between [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] loyalists and rebels, with the Skaal being descended from the loyalist faction. Multiple Dragon Cult ruins were uncovered during the Fourth Era, which included carvings that corroborated the oral history preserved by the Skaal.{{Ref|name=TGATT}} The scholar [[Skyrim:Tharstan|Tharstan]] came to the conclusion that the legend described in the ''Guardian and the Traitor'' was likely true with the additional archaeological evidence supporting it, and the Skaal formed a distinct culture overtime thanks to the isolation gained from the conflict. {{Ref|name=Tharstan|[[Skyrim:Tharstan|Tharstan]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} 

The Skaal are self-sufficient, with the land providing all they require. As such, they rarely accept coin in trade from travelers.{{Ref|name=Frea|[[Skyrim:Frea|Frea]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
The term "Skaal" first appeared as the name of one of the longships used by the [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] to cross the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]] from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]], with the ''Skaal'' sailing south-southeast from Atmora through the bitter winds of the Tidal Woe.{{ref|name=SotR7|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 7}}}}{{Ref|name=T500MC|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Five Hundred Mighty Companions or Thereabouts of Ysgramor the Returned|ns_base=GEN}}}} Visuals carved onto horker scrimshaws depict the early ancestors of the Skaal people running their boats aground on the land of Solstheim.{{Ref|name=SCHT|[[ON:Contraband S#Skaal-Carved Horker Tusk|Skaal-Carved Horker Tusk]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Early in the {{Year|3E 300|third century}} of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], a group of Skaal left the village, hoping to return more to their roots. These Skaal were led by [[Lore:Hrothmund the Red|Hrothmund the Red]], and founded [[Lore:Thirsk|Thirsk Mead Hall]], a settlement on the southern shore of Lake Fjalding{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|Thirsk, a History}}}} (though some local Nords believe the history of the hall dates back to the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]{{ref|name=Thirsk|[[Bloodmoon:Thirsk|Thirsk]] residents' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}). There, they lived like the Nords of old, hunting and fighting without restraint, with the mightiest warrior rising to leadership.{{ref|name=DBKuvar|[[Skyrim:Kuvar|Kuvar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}<includeonly>

{{FMI}}</includeonly><noinclude>

The [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] at [[Lore:Fort Frostmoth|Fort Frostmoth]] lost a lot of men from conflicts with the local Nords at the end of the Third Era. These Nords, described as "animal-worshipping savages", seemingly had similar beliefs to the Skaal.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Neville|Neville]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

Following the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], the ashfall from [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] gradually worsened, poisoning the land and water. This causes animals to have less to eat and become desperate, driven north in search of food, into Skaal lands.{{Ref|name=WWB|[[Skyrim:Wulf Wild-Blood|Wulf Wild-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

==Beliefs and Practices==
Though they are Nords by race, the Skaal embrace their own culture and religion, based around worship of the [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]].{{ref|name=COTAM|{{Cite Book|Children of the All-Maker}}}} The Skaal are animists, believing in a creator deity and a variety of spirits throughout the natural world.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} They worship the island's [[Lore:Wolf|wolves]] and [[Lore:Bear|bear]]s, and are known to perform ritual sacrifice by feeding those sentenced to death to the ceremonial [[Lore:Caenlorn Wolf|Caenlorn Wolves]].{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:The Skaal Test of Wisdom|The Skaal Test of Wisdom]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} They believe when something dies, be it an animal or a person, the spirit of the deceased is returned to the All-Maker, who then reforms it into a new being and returns it to the mortal plane, to live out a new life, in a new body. As such, the Skaal do not view death the same way as other Nords. All life is sacred to the Skaal, and they try to live in harmony with nature, impacting their environment as little as possible. They hunt deer, bear, [[Lore:Horker|horker]], and {{Lore Link|muskrey}}, only to the extent necessary for survival, never for enjoyment or practice, and gather their firewood from dead trees that have already fallen.{{ref|name=COTAM}}{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}} 

The antithesis of the All-Maker is a being known as [[Lore:The Adversary|the Adversary]]. The Adversary has many aspects, each one of them designed to test the Skaal, to push them so that they do not become lazy and weak. One aspect of the Adversary is [[Lore:Thartaag|Thartaag, the World-Devourer]], likely the Skaal's version of [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], the World-Eater. Legend says that Thartaag will return at the End of Seasons, and bring an end to all of creation.{{ref|name=SASS}}

===Stalhrim===
[[File:BM-item-Stalhrim Deposit.jpg|thumb|right|Stalhrim deposit]]
The Skaal consider [[Lore:Stalhrim|Stalhrim]] to be holy and infused with divine power, and at one point shunned its usage.{{ref|name=Graring|[[BM:Graring|Graring]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=Hidar|[[BM:Hidar|Hidar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=Aenar|[[BM:Aenar|Aenar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{Ref|name=BFF|[[ON:Brazier of Frozen Flame|Brazier of Frozen Flame]] Antiquity Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} They also believe the art of forging Stalhrim into weapons and armor is sacred, and certain members of the Skaal were charged as keepers of this ancient tradition.{{ref|name=Deor|[[Skyrim:Deor Woodcutter|Deor Woodcutter]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} As such, knowledge of stalhrim smithing is very rare and typically known only to the Skaal. However, local Nords and the warriors of [[Lore:Thirsk|Thirsk]] have also been known to possess such knowledge.{{ref|name=DBloading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Solstheim|Loading screens]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Skaal Amulet|Skaal Amulet]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} The [[Lore:Mace of Aevar Stone-Singer|Mace of Aevar Stone-Singer]], a prized Skaal [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]], is made from stalhrim.{{ref|name=BM|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}

===All-Maker Stones===
[[File:BM-place-Sun Stone.jpg|thumb|right|An All-Maker Stone]]
The [[Lore:Standing Stones|All-Maker stones]] are an essential part to skaal religion. They represent the most important parts of nature and the greatest gifts of the all maker.

There are six of these standing stones on Solstheim:
*The wind stone
*The beast stone
*The sun stone
*The water stone
*The earth stone
*The tree stone

===Caenlorn Wolves===
The [[Lore:Caenlorn Wolf|Caenlorn wolves]] are ceremonial spirit wolves residing in the realm of the All-Maker. The shaman of the Skaal will summon the wolves for executions of criminals. Death by the these wolves is seen as an honorable death and they are able to purify the souls of the convicts, so they can be reborn.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Tharsten_Heart-Fang#The_Skaal_Test_of_Wisdom|Dialogue with Heart-Fang about the wolves]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}

===Ristaag===
The Ristaag is a ceremony to cleanse and bless the Skaal tribe in times of great need.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Korst_Wind-Eye#The_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang|Cleansing ceremony]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} The Skaal perform a ritual hunt to please the All-maker. A {{Lore Link|Spirit Bear|Spirit Bear}}, summoned by the sacred [[Lore:Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang|totem of claw and fang]], needs to be killed, so its heart can be offered to the All-Maker.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Rolf_Long-Tooth#Quest-Related Events|Summoned by totem of claw and fang]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}

===Secrets of the Skaal===
The Skaal guard ancient knowledge, handed down from shaman to shaman. The All-Maker taught them to talk to the wind or how to listen to the earth. And always there has been old [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Herma-Mora]], the demon of knowledge, willing to steal these secrets. The Skaal have many tales of Herma-Mora trying to deceive them into giving him what he wants. These stories also tell of the day when the Skaal will have to give up their secrets and Mora finally wins.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Storn Crag-Strider|Storns]] dialogue in 'The gardener of men' in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===Bloodmoon===
The Skaal also recognize [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] as an enemy. He is a demon god whose coming is predicted by the [[Lore:Bloodmoon Prophecy|Bloodmoon Prophecy]], which foretells when he will host the [[Lore:Hunter's Game|Hunter's Game]] on Nirn. During this, he walks the lands with his Hounds and chooses which men shall become hares in his hunt.{{ref|name=BMKorst|[[Bloodmoon:Korst Wind-Eye|Korst Wind-Eye]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} 

His children, the [[Lore:Lycanthropy|Lycanthropes]], are viewed as twisted mockeries of the [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]]'s noble creatures.{{ref|name=SRWWB|[[Skyrim:Wulf Wild-Blood|Wulf Wild-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} They believe that Man was not meant to live a dual life as both rational being and animal.{{ref|name=BMKorst}} <br>
The Skaal believe a [[Lore:Mummy|mummified]] wolf paw capped at the ankle with [[Lore:Silver|silver]] can ward away werewolves.{{Ref|name=ESOGnarledPaw|[[Online:Contraband_G#Gnarled Paw|Gnarled Paw]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Skaal Legends==
===The Traitor===
According to legend, long ago the world was ruled by [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]], who enslaved the race of man and forced them to build fantastic temples in their honor. Directing the slaves in their name were the Dragon Priests, a group of men who worshipped the dragons and were given power in exchange for their devotion. These priests were powerful mages, and had the gift of the [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]]. One of these dragon priests, known only as the [[Lore:Miraak|Traitor]], was seduced by [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Herma-Mora]], the Skaal's name for the Daedric Prince Hermaeus Mora. His plot to overthrow the dragons was discovered by another dragon priest, known as the {{Lore Link|Vahlok the Jailor|Guardian}}. The Guardian confronted the Traitor, and the two fought a terrible battle. So great was their power, Solstheim was sundered from [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. The Guardian proved to be the more powerful, and bested the Traitor. Just as the Guardian was about to land the final blow, Herma-Mora snatched the Traitor away, rescuing him from certain death. As a reward for his loyalty, the Guardian was given Solstheim to rule, where his reign is remembered as peaceful and prosperous, and he is remembered as a wise and just ruler.{{ref|name=TGATT|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}}

===Aevar===
In another tale, originating from when the Skaal were a new people, the land was at peace. The sun was warm, and the groups bountiful, and all the Skaal gave thanks to the All-Maker. Over time, however, the Skaal gave less and less thanks, becoming complacent and lazy. They forgot that [[Lore:The Adversary|the Adversary]], the antithesis of the All-Maker, is always watching, waiting for a chance to torment the All-Maker and his preferred people. Unlike the All-Maker, the Adversary has many aspects, each one with a specific name and gender. At this time, he was known as [[Lore:The Greedy Man|the Greedy Man]]. The Greedy Man lived among the Skaal for some time, until one day the powers of the Skaal suddenly disappeared. The warriors became weak, the shaman could no longer call to the beasts of the forest for aid. The elders of the Skaal thought that the All-Maker had abandoned them, and this is when the Greedy Man spoke. He told them that because of their disregard for the gifts of the All-Maker, he had taken them away. He told them that until one among them could retrieve the gifts of the All-Maker, all of the Skaal would live in misery, and then he disappeared. The Skaal spoke well into the night, and then into the next day, and continued to talk until many days passed. They knew that someone must go, but none of them wanted to bear the responsibility. It was then that a young man named [[Lore:Aevar Stone-Singer|Aevar]] spoke, and volunteered to go. He traveled into the forest, to each of the All-Maker Stones, meditating at each of them, and learned from the All-Maker how he might retrieve the gifts from the Greedy Man. He traveled across Solstheim, and retrieved all of the gifts from the Greedy Man. He meditated again, and the All-Maker spoke to him, telling them that he was pleased, and that the Greedy Man would not bother the Skaal again for many years to come. With that, Aevar returned to the Skaal, and lived according to the true ways of the Skaal.{{ref|name=SASS|{{Cite Book|The Story of Aevar Stone-Singer}}}}
