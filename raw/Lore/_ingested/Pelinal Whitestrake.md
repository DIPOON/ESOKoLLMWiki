<noinclude>{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''[and Pelinal] came to [[Lore:Alessia|Perrif]]'s camp of rebels holding a sword and mace, both encrusted with the smashed viscera of elven faces, feathers and magic beads, which were the markings of the Ayleidoon, stuck to the redness that hung from his [[Lore:Weapons|weapons]], and he lifted them, saying: "These were their eastern chieftains, no longer full of their talking."''|An excerpt from ''[[Lore:The_Song_of_Pelinal,_v_2|The Song of Pelinal, volume 2]]''}}</noinclude>[[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] was a legendary (and infamous){{ref|name=S&D|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=OBMaera}} [[Lore:Demigod|Demigod]] who fought alongside his fellow Demigod [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]] as the champion of Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] during the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] of the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=S&D}}{{ref|name=TSOP1|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 1}}}} He possessed the [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Crusader's Relics]], which were given to him by the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]].{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=ONJO|[[Online:Josette Ondre|Josette Ondre]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Legends say he often had stretches of homicidal madness, during which he slew indiscriminately and took a toll on the very landscape itself.{{ref|name=TSOP6|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 6}}}}

Pelinal is also supposedly but one name for the "legendary immortal hero" who, according to legend, conquered all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] by himself in the late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{ref|name=HT|{{Cite Book|Hallgerd's Tale}}}} However, he never united the various provinces he conquered under a single banner, as he was known to wander, build up kingdoms, and inevitably abandon them to wander again.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} Though some doubt has been placed on the idea of all these feats belonging to the same individual.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

== Titles ==
The name Pelinal means "glorious knight" in [[Lore:Ayleidoon|elvish]].{{ref|name=TSOP1}} It is a corruption of '''Pelin-El''', which means '''the Star-Made Knight'''.{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}} One story gives him the name '''Pellani''',{{ref|name=WWPD|group=UOL|[[General:What Would Pelinal Do?|''What Would Pelinal Do?'']] — [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]}} which became the [[Lore:Old Cyrodilic|Old Cyrodilic]] word for "outsiders" over time for the [[Lore:Wild Elf|Wild Elven]] tribes.{{ref|name=TWE|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves}}}} He was referred to in many different ways during the Alessian Rebellion, and was known as the '''Whitestrake''', '''Pelinal Insurgent''', '''Pelinal the Bloody''', '''Pelinal In Triumph''', '''Pelinal the Blamer''' and '''Pelinal the Third'''.{{ref|name=TSOP1}} This last moniker, "Pelinal the Third", could indicate that he is a "god guiser" who had [[Lore:Reincarnation|incarnated]] twice before.{{ref|name=TSOP1}} Another theory suggests that he is the third vision received by [[Lore:Alessia|St. Alessia]] in her prayers to the gods for mankind's freedom from the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]].{{ref|name=TSOP1}} He is often referred to as the '''Divine Crusader'''.{{ref|name=OBProphet}} Khajiiti legends remember him as the '''White Snake'''.{{ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} His close connection to Alessia sometimes had the pair referred to as '''Pelin-al-Essia'''.{{ref|name=AE4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Yet another title for Pelinal is '''Convalent Eradicator'''.{{ref|name=TSOP10|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 10}}}}

It is claimed that he had other names as an immortal sorcerer king, such as '''[[Lore:Ysmir|Ysmir]]''', '''Harrald Hairy Breeks''', and '''Hans the Fox'''.{{ref|name=BTAOM}} Though some have claimed that these names were simply conflated with Pelinal and actually belonged to separate figures.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

== Divinity and Nature ==
[[File:ON-npc-Predicant Maera.jpg|Outfit worn in honor of Pelinal|thumb|left]]
Pelinal described himself and Morihaus, in a conversation between the two, as "[[Lore:Ada|ada]]", described as a type of being able to change things through love, as Morihaus did through his creation of the [[Lore:Minotaur|Minotaurs]],{{ref|name=TSOP5}}{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Minotaurs}}}} and the same classification given to his half-divine enemy, Umaril, who, being "of the blood of the ada", would "never know death".{{ref|name=TSOP3}} Furthermore some sources classify Pelinal as a Demigod.{{Ref|name=TFS|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|The Footsteps of Shezarr}}}} Pelinal had many parallels to the Missing God [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] and his various aspects. It is said that a man once called Pelinal "the [[Lore:Shezarrine|Shezarrine]]"; the man was then smothered by [[Lore:Moth|moths]] that night.{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}} Furthermore when the Nords first bore witness to Pelinal, they claimed that [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] had returned, though Pelinal spat at them for profaning the name of Shor.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} Another link is a book of dubious nature that was supposedly authored by Pelinal that entails a conversation with the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] on the nature of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]].{{Ref|name=TLODRB|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}} 

Pelinal was said to have entered the world like a [[Lore:Padomay|Padomaic]], borne of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]] and all the forces of change therein.{{ref|name=TSOP6|}} This is consistent with myths of Lorkhan that speak of his Padomaic nature and him being begat by Sithis.{{ref|name=Sb|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}} Pelinal was also described as having been a "diamond soaked red with the blood of elves" whose "facets could un-sector and form" into a "man whose every angle" could "cut" Alessia's jailers, and who was called PELIN-EL.{{ref|name=TSOP2}} Beyond this it was said under Pelinal's armor was a gaping chest with no heart, only a [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|red rage shaped diamond]] that sang like a mindless [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]].{{ref|name=TSOP6|}} Several mythical feats are attributed to Pelinal, including having a left hand "made of killing light",{{ref|name=TSOP1}} and somehow erasing an entire month from the elvish calendar.{{ref|name=TSOP10}} Also despite his hatred of god-talk, Pelinal himself spoke of a twin nature he shared with [[Lore:Akatosh|Aka]], and secret faces that he and his twin have worn in [[Lore:Aetherius|heaven]] since even before the days of [[Lore:Convention|Convention]], speaking to Pelinal's ancient nature.{{ref|name=TSOP6|}}{{ref|name=TSOP8|}} Pelinal also exclaimed  "Umaril dares call us out, for that is how we made him!", when addressing Aka.{{ref|name=TSOP6|}}

It is possible that Pelinal was capable of [[Lore:Chronomancy|time travel]] or possessed the ability to [[Lore:Sight|see]] future events.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} He allegedly shouted the name of future ruler [[Lore:Reman|Reman]] hundreds of years before his birth during the days of the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Rebellion]].{{ref|name=TSOP3}} Additionally, he was once stated to wear armor from a future time,{{ref|name=TSOP2}} though there is no scholarly consensus on the dating when the texts that claim these things were written.{{ref|name=TSOP1}} When encountering the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]], the spirit of Pelinal would claim that he wasn't sure whether others that he'd already encountered within his "dream" had come before or after the Hero.{{ref|name=OblivionPel}} The deeds of Pelinal and his various aliases throughout many different ages of Tamriel's history are by some accounts the result of various incarnations or merely the actions of a "composite hero".{{ref|name=TSOP1}}{{ref|name=ONCK|[[Online:Captain Kaleen|Captain Kaleen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} Pelinal had association with animal imagery, namely the [[Lore:Fox|fox]] and [[Lore:Wolf|wolf]],{{ref|name=TA|}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=ONSMW|[[Online:Star-Made Wolfshead|Star-Made Wolfshead]] costume in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} animal totems of Shor and [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] respectively.{{Ref|name=DatN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}}

Pelinal's elven enemies viewed him as a mere [[Lore:Human|Man]], specifically a [[Lore:Imperial|Nibenay]] warlord. This caused the prideful elves to be confused about how so many of their kind saw defeat at the hands of Pelinal.{{Ref|name=FBJ|}}{{ref|name=PGE1E|}}{{ref|name=TSOP4}}

Pelinal also carried strange weapons and armor gifted by the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]].{{ref|name=KOTN|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}} His reputation varies widely. Among [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]], he is a hero and [[Lore:Imperial Saints|saint]] who helped free [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] from oppression.{{ref|name=OBProphet|}}{{ref|name=OBMaera|[[Online:Predicant Maera|Predicant Maera]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Among mer, he is the vicious Nibenay warlord who led the Elven Pogrom, and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] revile him because he mistook them for another type of Aldmeri, slaughtering many before his mistake became apparent.{{ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}

==History==
===Wandering King===
One legend purports that Pelinal came to Tamriel from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] as a part of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] during the [[Lore:The Return|Return]]. This tale is somewhat conflicting as it seems to mention several of Pelinal's names or incarnations as different members, while also mentioning Pelinal himself separately. It also mentions many anachronistic figures.{{ref|group=UOL|name=FHMC|}}

In Merethic Era Tamriel, it is believed that Pelinal under various names built a reputation as a wandering hero, sorcerer, and warrior.{{ref|name=BTAOM|}} Pelinal in his wandering would gather armies, conquer lands, rule over them as king, only to inevitably abandon them and repeat the process.{{ref|name=BTAOM|}} Pelinal wandered this way so much that he was considered to have conquered the whole of Tamriel over time, although never under a single banner given his propensity to abandon his kingdoms.{{ref|name=HT}} Though there is some uncertainty whether all these feats were actually accomplished by Pelinal, or whether he was conflated with other Mythic figures.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

The [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] of Cyrodiil, ruled over their [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] slaves with a brutal hand.{{ref|name=TA|}} The earliest [[Lore:Imperial|Cyro-]][[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] legends remember these cruel Ayleids being kept at bay by the god [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] who fought them on mankind's behalf. Eventually Shezarr vanishes from Cyrodiil, presumably to help other humans elsewhere, allowing the Ayleids to enslave the Nedes.{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} These legends have possible links to Pelinal given his speculated nature as the [[Lore:Shezarrine|Shezarrine]].{{ref|name=TSOP5|}}

=== The Alessian Rebellion ===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Personnes of pleasante disposition make ye most merrie companions, whether for roistering at taverne or massacre of ye Elfs''|Pelinal Whitestrake{{ref|[[Online:Cheerful|Cheerful]] personality description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}}}
[[File:MER-art-The High Priestess (crop).jpg|Alessia the Slave-Queen|thumb|left]][[File:ON-tribute-alessia-Morihaus, Sacred Bull (crop).png|Morihaus, Pelinal's "nephew"|thumb|right]]
In {{Year|1E 242}}{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{ref|name=SATD|}}{{ref|name=PGTE|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} a Nedic slave known as Alessia prayed to the goddess [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] that the Nedes could garner the might to take vengeance on their Ayleid masters for their cruelty. In return, Kyne sent down her Demigod son Morihaus and by his side was Pelinal who was arrayed in futuristic-looking armor. The pair arrived at the camp of Alessia having already killed several Ayleid chieftains; the stage for Nedic rebellion was set under the trio, with Alessia being the Slave-Queen.{{ref|name=TSOP2}} Pelinal's armor and weapons were in fact gifts from the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]], who personally blessed Pelinal as their champion in the crusade against the cruel Ayleids.{{ref|name=OBProphet|}} The armor was effective, as no Ayleid weapon managed to pierce it.{{ref|name=TSOP3|}}

He served the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|revolution]] by slaying the Sorcerer-Kings of the Ayleid city-states in single combat, including [[Lore:Haromir|Haromir]] of Copper and Tea, [[Lore:Gordhaur|Gordhaur]] the Shaper, and the Fire-King [[Lore:Hadhuul|Hadhuul]].{{ref|name=TSOP3|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 3}}}} He was by some legends lover of Alessia,{{ref|name=OBMaera}} though Pelinal is expressly stated to have loved the hoplite [[Lore:Huna|Huna]], who he had raised up from a grain-slave and loved well.{{ref|name=TSOP3}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} However, when Huna was killed by an arrow of {{Lore Link|Celethelel the Singer}}, Pelinal would descend into several flights of madness during which he cut vast swathes in mindless fury to avenge him. During some of these rampages, Pelinal was said to have turned whole swathes of land to [[Lore:Void|Void]], making the gods themselves reach down and offer him succor, so he "no longer had the will to kill the earth in whole".{{ref|name=TSOP3}}{{ref|name=TSOP6|}} Alessia had to make sacrifice to the Eight Divines during these swaths of madness to keep them from leaving in their disgust.{{ref|name=TSOP3|}} 

According to tradition, Pelinal was served by [[Lore:Vilius Tharn|Vilius Tharn]], "Blade-Serrator and Master of the Abbatoir", a claimed ancestor of the [[Lore:House Tharn|Tharn family]].{{ref|name=HTON|{{Cite Book|House Tharn of Nibenay}}}} Alessia presented Morihaus and Pelinal coronets with swords surmounted on them to represent their natures as the living weapons of the [[Lore:First Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=ONSMSC|[[Online:Star-Made Sword Coronet|Star-Made Sword Coronet]] adornment in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Around this time, the Ayleids to help even the odds made a pact with the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] [[Lore:Auroran|Aurorans]] of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], and appointed the most powerful and terrible sorcerer-king as their champion, the half-elf [[Lore:Umaril|Umaril the Unfeathered]].{{ref|name=TSOP3|}} As well as being an Ayleid, Umaril had a divine father, giving him the blood of the [[Lore:Ada|ada]], and for the first time it was Pelinal who was challenged to battle by Umaril which enraged the Star-Made Knight.{{ref|name=TSOP3|}}

After Umaril called him out, Pelinal took to personally leading the armies of rebellion and helped overtake all of eastern Cyrodiil in a swath of bloody fury.{{ref|name=TSOP4|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 4}}}} Pelinal then broke down the doors of {{Lore Link|Vahtacen|Vahtache}} freeing the Nedic prisoners who lay within.{{ref|name=TSOP4}}{{ref|name=VESO|[[ON:Vahtacen|Vahtacen]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
He later freed the [[Lore:Thousand-Strong of Sedor|Thousand-Strong of Sedor]], a famous Nedic tribe of the age.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} Pelinal then committed a pogrom that consolidated northern Cyrodiil for the [[Lore:Men-of-kreath|Men-of-kreath]], his white hair having gone brown at this point with the sheer amount of elf blood that had stained it.{{ref|name=TSOP4}}

[[File:ON-place-Sunspire Temple Grounds.jpg|Sunspire Temple, the site of Pelinal's battle with Alkosh|thumb|right]]
During one particular bout of madness when Pelinal was committing a pogrom of elves, he wandered into the lands of the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] who he mistook for elves.{{ref|name=PGE1E|}} Pelinal slaughtered the Khajiit in great number; the cat men being naturally skilled climbers attempted to escape him by climbing a nearby [[Lore:Sunspire|mountain]], but Pelinal in a demonstration of his tremendous power wrestled with the mountain until he too stood atop it.{{ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The Khajiit prayed for divine intervention and received it from the King of Time, [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]]. The two beings did battle but Alkosh ultimately defeated Pelinal and [[Lore:Thu'um|shouted]] him out of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], forming a [[Lore:Time Wound|Time Wound]] in the process.{{ref|name=ESOAzin|}}{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} This allowed Pelinal to realize his error of mistaking the Khajiit for elven kind.{{ref|name=PGE1E|}}

Towards the end of the war as rebel forces began moving closer to the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], the Ayleids attempted to blockade the northern bridge to the city.{{ref|name=TSOP4}}{{ref|name=OBMaera}} Alessia's falconers traveled to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] seeking [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] aid.{{ref|name=TSOP4}}{{ref|name=OBMaera}} The Nords came to reinforce the armies and upon laying eyes on Pelinal, said that [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] had returned.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} Pelinal was angry at this god-talk but led them anyways.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} An enraged Pelinal assaulted their lines and slaughtered all the Ayleids holding Heldon Bridge – an act which became known as "[[Lore:Pelinal's Midyear Massacre|Pelinal's Midyear Massacre]]" in later Eras.{{ref|name=OBMaera}}{{ref|name=TSOP10|}} Pelinal then led the Nordic and Nedic army into the center of Cyrod.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} He destroyed a wave of [[Lore:Storm Atronach|Thundernachs]] that Umaril had sent at the rebellion armies, and later carried his beloved friend Morihaus to [[Lore:Zuathas|Zuathas the Clever-Cutting Man]] for healing when the Demigod had fallen to a volley of bird beaks.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} At the [[Lore:Council of Skiffs|Council of Skiffs]] the Nedes and Nords alike showed great fear to storm the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] to the point Alessia gave council for delay.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} This infuriated Pelinal who saw the hesitation as cowardly and in a move of brashness stormed for the Citadel alone.{{ref|name=TSOP4}}

===Death===
[[File:OB-KotN-PelinalPainting.jpg|Painting of Pelinal and Umaril the Unfeathered in battle|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|''Our enemies have undone me, and spread my body into hiding. In mockery of divine purpose, the Ayleids cut me into eighths, for they are obsessed with this number.''|[[Lore:The Adabal-a|The Adabal-a]]}}
Pelinal and Umaril's confrontation took place in the White-Gold Tower itself, also spilling into the nearby Ayleid settlement of [[Lore:Vanua|Vanua]].{{ref|name=OblivionPel|[[Oblivion:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}{{ref|name=TSOP7|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 7}}}} Pelinal arrived to the throne room of White-Gold alone, and all around him "dead [[Lore:Auroran|Aurorans]] lay like candlelight". Surrounded by the last of the Ayleid sorcerer-kings  and their [[Lore:Daedra|"demons"]], each one "heavy with [[Lore:Varliance|Varliance"]]. Pelinal called Umaril to single combat. Umaril first weakened Pelinal by sending in soldiers whom he had been saving for that moment, knowing full well they would die, as Umaril had grown fond of ruin-from-afar tactics. The warriors of Umaril bore weapons heavy with Varliance, wrought by Umaril himself, and so could pierce Pelinal's armor.{{ref|name=TSOP7}}{{ref|[[Online:Auroran Commander Helm|Auroran Commander Helm]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} It was only after Pelinal was bleeding and wounded that Umaril finally came down to fight him. Pelinal slew Umaril nonetheless, but his victory was short-lived. Umaril had managed to deal Pelinal a mortal wound{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}} and the other elf kings, angered by Pelinal's insults against the ancestry of all those who took ship from [[Lore:Aldmeris|Old Ehlnofey]], descended upon him in a mad frenzy, and cut him into eight (by some sources nine){{ref|name=WWPD|group=UOL|}} pieces in mockery of the Divines, and carried them away, leaving only his head as a gift to Alessia. The next morning, those who remained either ran when Morihaus shook the whole tower with a bash from his horns, or were slain. Pelinal's head was then found by Morihaus, kept alive in torment by Ayleid magics, and was said to have spoken things of regret.{{ref|name=TSOP7}}{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}}{{ref|name=UiU|{{Cite Book|Umaril is Undone}}}}{{ref|name=OBProphet|}}{{Ref|name=SOT|{{Cite Book|Staff of Towers (book)}}}}{{Ref|name=FBJ|{{Cite Book|Frostbitten Journal}}}} Pelinal informed Morihaus that Umaril in his cleverness had bound himself to the realm of his mistress Meridia and with this Daedric nature was able to cheat death.{{ref|name=OBProphet|}}{{ref|name=TA|}} Pelinal warned that he would no longer be able to protect men from the wrath of Umaril but Morihaus was confident that when the time was right, his uncle would come again, as he was "blood made glorious", and so would return as "fox animal, or light".{{ref|name=OBProphet|}}{{ref|name=TA|}}

===Legacy===
[[File:OBR-npc-Pelinal Whitestrake.jpg|Pelinal's ghost (Oblivion Remastered)|thumb|right]]
[[File:OB-wallpaper-The Crusader-1600x1200.jpg|The Divine Crusader|thumb|left]]
The defeat of Umaril by Pelinal and the taking of the depleted Citadel by Morihaus spelled the effective defeat of the Ayleid resistance, the First Empire was born and Alessia claimed herself the first Empress of Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=SATD|}} A circlet was crafted with three red diamonds upon it to commemorate the three most crucial figures to the formation of the Empire, one diamond for Alessia, one for Morihaus, and one for Pelinal.{{ref|name=ONSMC|[[Online:Star-Made Circlet|Star-Made Circlet]] adornment in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Despite his death, he was supposedly present at Alessia's death bed years later. He described another half that along with himself would take her up and reveal their true faces, "which eat one another in amnesia each Age", to her.{{ref|name=TSOP8|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 8}}}} [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] claimed that Pelinal could explain his whereabouts during the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]], despite this event occurring roughly a millennium after his death.{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}

While there are those who honor his legacy, Pelinal became known as a controversial or even infamous figure to some.{{ref|name=S&D|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=OBMaera}}{{ref|name=ESOAzin}} 
At some point after Pelinal's death, scholars of the topic would gather to re-enact the battle between Pelinal and Umaril, using well-crafted illusion magics to replicate the appearance of Umaril's armor.{{ref|[[Online:Armor of the Unfeathered|Armor of the Unfeathered]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some of these scholars even tattooed imagery of this historic battle on their bodies.{{ref|[[Online:Unfeathered Battle Tattoo|Unfeathered Battle Tattoo]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Unfeathered Battle Visage|Unfeathered Battle Visage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Kaladas|Saint Kaladas]] had dreams in which he believed Pelinal Whitestrake spoke to him which drove him to build the [[Lore:Great Chapel of Zenithar|Great Chapel of Zenithar]] in [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]].{{ref|name=ONUV|[[Online:Uvoo Vadryon|Uvoo Vadryon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TGTL|{{Cite Book|Traveler's Guide to Leyawiin}}}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, the powerful golden [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]] [[Lore:Nahviintaas|Nahviintaas]] emerged in [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]], following the release of the great dragon [[Lore:Kaalgrontiid|Kaalgrontiid]] and his dragon followers from the [[Lore:Halls of Colossus|Halls of Colossus]].{{ref|name=ESOTHOC|Events of [[ON:The Halls of Colossus|The Halls of Colossus]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}. He led two other dragons, {{Lore Link|Lokkestiiz}} and {{Lore Link|Yolnahkriin}}, to pose as the returned [[Lore:Gods|gods]] of the Khajiit, Alkosh and the [[Lore:Moons|moons]] [[Lore:Secunda|Jone]] and [[Lore:Masser|Jode]], and take over the temple of Sunspire, where he attempted to expand the [[Lore:Time|Time Wound]] left from Pelinal's fight with Alkosh via his Thu'um, in the hopes of causing a [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] to rewrite time and restore draconic rule. This plan was foiled by the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], who entered the temple grounds and slew the dragons before they could achieve their goal.{{Ref|name=ESOAlkosh|[[Online:The Return of Alkosh|The Return of Alkosh]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Sunspire Dragonbreak|Sunspire Dragonbreak]] achievement in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} 

In {{Year|3E 433}}, Umaril the Unfeathered had returned to Nirn to take revenge on the Gods. A prophet of the Nine appeared stating that the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]] needed a divine crusader reborn.{{ref|name=OBProphet}} A [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|champion]] would rise to the occasion and, after completing a pilgrimage to the [[Lore:Wayshrines|Wayshrines]] of the Nine, meet with Pelinal's ghost personally above the White Gold Tower. The spirit of Pelinal would acknowledge that only great need made the [[Lore:Aedra|gods]] allow the meeting, and direct the champion to retrieve the first of the Crusader's Relics. The champion would than go on to collect the Crusader's relics, receive the blessing of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], be proclaimed an embodiment of Pelinal Whitestrake, and utterly destroy Umaril, eradicating his very spirit through the blessing of Talos to deliver him the final death Pelinal could not.{{ref|name=Oblivion|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} The champion was named the new Divine Crusader of the [[Lore:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]], an order of knights who would go on to quest in Pelinal's honor.{{ref|name=KOTN}} Pelinal was remembered as one of the greatest warriors in the history of Tamriel, compared to the likes of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], [[Lore:Vilus Nommenus|Vilus Nommenus]], and [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]].{{ref|name=HT|}}{{Ref|name=VAB|{{Cite Book|Vernaccus and Bourlor}}}}

==Notes==
* In an out-of-game text, the names Harald Hairy Breeks and Hans the Fox were mentioned among Ysgramor's Companions, and a couple others in their number were said to have "ran once with the Pelinal".{{ref|group=UOL|name=FHMC|[[General:The Five Hundred Mighty Companions or Thereabouts of Ysgramor the Returned|The Five Hundred Mighty Companions or Thereabouts of Ysgramor the Returned]]}}
* Some sources suggest Pelinal spoke with an [[wikipedia:Early Modern English|Early Modern English]] dialect.{{ref|name=TSOP10|}}{{ref|name=ONCP|[[Online:Cheerful|Cheerful]] personality description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
* Along with implications that support the idea in his in game novel, [[Lore:The Song of Pelinal|''The Song of Pelinal'']], [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]] has stated in [[General:MK's Posts|several of his out of game posts]] that Pelinal was a cyborg or [[Lore:Construct|construct]] of some kind.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}}
*Pelinal is said to have been a "diamond soaked red with the blood of elves" whose "facets could un-sector and form" into a "man whose every angle" could "cut" Alessia's jailers, and who was called PELIN-EL. It is said that, under Pelinal's armor, there was a gaping chest with no heart, only a "red rage shaped diamond that sang like a mindless [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]]". This red diamond from which Pelinal is said to have formed might have been the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], also known as the Red Diamond.{{ref|name=TSOP6|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 6}}}}{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}{{ref|name=AoK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}}
*At some point after the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]], mysterious slate-skinned figures, known as Red Diamond Guardians, periodically appeared in the Imperial City to fight the Daedra. Each one had the Red Diamond encrusted upon their torso.{{ref|[[ON:Red Diamond Guardian|Red Diamond Guardian]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} No one knew where they came from or where they went after each fray. Pelinal himself has been described as a star-made knight who formed from a red diamond, and had a red diamond that sang like a mindless dragon in his chest. Given the resemblance, its possible there might be some connection between these Red Diamond Guardians and Pelinal.{{ref|name=TSOP6|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 6}}}}{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}
