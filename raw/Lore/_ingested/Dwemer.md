The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]<noinclude> ({{IPA|'|d|w|E|.|m|3:|r}} {{pronunciation|DWE|mer}}, {{IPA|'|d|w|eI|.|m|3:|r}} {{pronunciation|DWAY|mer}}, or {{IPA|'|d|w|i|.|m|3:|r}} {{pronunciation|DWEE|mer}})</noinclude>, (literally ''''the Deep''', or '''Deep-Counseled''' or '''Secretive People''', '''people of the deep'''), are a fabled "Lost Race"{{ref|name=D2|{{Cite Book|Dwarves, v2}}}} of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] from Dwemereth, which mostly consisted of modern-day [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], where they are believed to have been the most prolific, though they also had a strong presence in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]].{{ref|name=DI1|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol I}}}}{{ref|name=D1|{{Cite Book|Dwarves, v1}}}}

The races of Mer use the term "Dwemer", which translates to '''Deep-Elves'''{{ref|name=DI1}} or '''Deep Folk'''{{ref|name=36LOV5|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 5}}}}
and connoting variously with '''deep-delving,''' '''profound,''' and '''close-counseled'''; Imperial usage is '''Dwarven,''' derived in fable from the affectionate regard of the Giant races for their 'little' Dwemer friends. These Giants have been speculated to be [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] or the more common [[Lore:Giant|Giants]].{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Ales Julalanie|Ales Julalanie]]'s dialogue in [[MW:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} They were also called the '''Children of Brass''' by [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]].{{Ref|name=LATCC|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City}}}}

The early history of the Dwemer is still clouded in mystery. There is no known story of their dissociation from the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]], which must have occurred very early in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielic]] history, as their society bore few correlations with that of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] besides some similar legal principles.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}{{ref|name=AODL|{{Cite Book|Antecedents of Dwemer Law}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3Sum|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}} According to some legends, the Dwemer originated from the same group as the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], and were treated as another tribe, and later, as a secular [[Lore:Great Houses|Great House]].{{ref|name=TWOTFC|{{Cite Book|The War of the First Council}}}}<noinclude>{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=GEN|Gary Noonan's Posts}}}}{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=GEN|Redguard Forum Madness}}}}</noinclude> Other sources however claim they already occupied the northeast of Tamriel when [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] and his people arrived there.{{ref|name=PGE3Sum}}

The Dwemer built elaborate underground cities near and beneath mountain ranges, including the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] and [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], and in the mountains of the isle [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]]. Many misconceptions about them have abounded for centuries: scholars long thought that most [[Lore:Dwemer Ruins|Dwemer ruins]] which dotted Tamriel outside Morrowind were mere outposts and that there were few significant Dwemer settlements elsewhere until {{Year|1E 420}}.{{Ref|name=DI1}} The presentation of the Dwemer in fictitious but popular novels like the ''[[Lore:Ancient Tales of the Dwemer|Ancient Tales of the Dwemer]]'' has also lodged an inaccurate impression of them in the popular consciousness, painting them as familiar, comfortable characters who were very similar to humans.{{ref|name=DHAC|{{Cite Book|Dwemer History and Culture}}}} In reality, they are better described as having been fearsome, unfathomable, and even cruel, though also careful, intelligent, and industrious.{{ref|name=TROZ|{{Cite Book|The Ransom of Zarek}}}}{{ref|name=SRKarliah|[[Skyrim:Karliah|Karliah]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{ref|name=D3|{{Cite Book|Dwarves, v3}}}} Their society consisted of free-thinking yet reclusive clans devoted to the secrets of science, engineering, and the arcane until they mysteriously disappeared around {{Year|1E 700}}.{{ref|name=DI1}}{{FMI}}<noinclude> There are a number of theories on how this happened, though none of them can be confirmed.

==History==
=== Dwemereth and Resdayn ===
The history of the Dwemer is mostly a record of their conflicts with other races and each other, and the timeline is imprecise for many of these events. They purportedly settled along the coasts of Morrowind and founded Dwemereth.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} During the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] they researched the death of the [[Lore:Earth Bones|Earth Bones]], or what is now known as the laws of nature, dissecting the process of the sacred willing itself into the profane.{{ref|name=MWBaladas}} They became involved in clashes with the newly-arrived [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] in the Late Middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] over land, resources, and religion.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} Dwemeri culture was agnostic and preferred reason to faith, while the Chimer were staunch [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-worshippers.{{ref|name=PGE3M}} However, the Nordic invasion of present-day [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] around {{Year|1E 240}} led by High King [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage the Gifted]] brought a common enemy the Chimer and Dwemer could unite against.{{ref|name=PGE3M}}{{ref|name=36LOV9|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9}}}} Although the Nords conquered all of mainland Morrowind and absorbed it into their [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|First Empire]], they never succeeded in subduing the Dwemer [[Lore:Empires#Dwemer_Kingdoms| Kingdom of Vvardenfell]], which remained independent and strong.{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} Amidst the Nords' occupation, [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac Dwarfking]] and [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]] forged an alliance which ultimately succeeded in repelling the invaders in {{year|1E 416}}, leading to the establishment of the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]] and the joint Dwemer-Chimer state of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]].{{ref|name=PGE3M}}

=== Westward Migrations ===
[[File:AR-item-Volendrung.png|Volendrung, the Great Hammer of the Dwemer|thumb|left|100px]][[File:ON-npc-Flint-Tooth Warchief.jpg|thumb|right|The ghost of a giant Goblin warchief]]
The Dwemer were present in Skyrim at least as early as {{Year|1E 221}}, when they uncovered a considerable source of [[Lore:Aetherium|Aetherium]], primarily mined in the city of [[Lore:Raldbthar|Raldbthar]], which connects to [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]]. Raldbthar Dwarves were believed to experiment with devices designed to extract the Aetherium before devising heavier machinery. As of {{Year|1E 240}}, they used personal extraction tools as well aside from the tools for large scale extraction.{{ref|name=Ancient Extraction Tool|[[ON:The Reach Antiquities#Ancient Extraction Tool|Ancient Extraction Tool]] antiquarian codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|1E 420}}, the [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]] clan opposed the accord with the Chimer and chose self-exile.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}} Their chieftain is said to have thrown their mighty hammer, [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]], across Tamriel and led his clan to wherever the hammer fell, an image which has been depicted in a number of ruins in Hammerfell. The Rourken named their new land "Volenfell," which later became known as "Hammerfell".{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}{{Ref|name=WIV|{{Cite Book|What is Volendrung?}}}}

In the following decades, [[Lore:Kragen|Clan Kragen]] decided to follow in Clan Rourken's footsteps and migrated west into [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] to establish new holdings of their own.{{ref|name=AP|{{Cite Book|Arkngthamz-Phng (book)}}}} [[Lore:Arkngthamz|Arkngthamz]] became the first Dwemer city-state in Skyrim, and its reputation for flourishing despite constant attacks from the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] encouraged other Dwemer clans to expand westward. A number of these clans soon formed a loose alliance of four city-states that was regarded as unassailable. Clan Kragen later established a sister-city, [[Lore:Fang Lair|Arkngthamz-Phng]], in the [[Lore:Dragon's Teeth Mountains|Dragon's Teeth Mountains]], which replaced Arkngthamz as the clan's seat of power.{{ref|name=AP}} Numerous Dwemer holdings and city-states soon dotted Skyrim from the Velothi Mountains to [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] in what is now [[Lore:The Reach|The Reach]].{{ref|name=D1}} The city-states burrowed deep underground, masking their true size, and several of them were linked by the gargantuan cavern known as [[Lore:Blackreach|Fal'Zhardum Din]].{{ref|name=DI3|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol III}}}} The Dwemer in Skyrim prospered for a time, but this prosperity proved to be short-lived. The city-states discovered deposits of [[Lore:Aetherium|Aetherium]] in their deepest delvings, and Arkngthamz led the research program to develop technology to harness Aetherium's power. The resulting Aetherium Forge produced Aetherium artifacts of such immense power that the Dwemer alliance immediately shattered, and the four city-states and their rivals went to war over control of the Forge. After decades of conflict, the Dwemer were weakened to such an extent that Nordic armies commanded by [[Lore:Gellir|High King Gellir]] finally succeeded in conquering them in three short years; a century would pass before the Dwemer mustered the strength to reclaim their lost lands.{{ref|name=TAW|{{Cite Book|The Aetherium Wars}}}}

The Dwarves of Hammerfell eventually a faced an otherworldly invasion from a new breed of [[Lore:Giant Goblin|giant Goblins]], who invaded via a rent in space and time known as the [[Lore:Goblin Gate|Goblin gate]].{{ref|name=RGH}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=Early}} These Goblins were generally described as taller and less "cowardly" than Goblins of the later Eras.{{ref|name=TKF|[[Online:Throne Keeper Farvad|Throne Keeper Farvad]]'s dialogue in {{g|ESO}}}} They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the province, on exodus to parts unknown.{{ref|name=RGH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part I}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=Early|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview/REDGUARD.TXT|REDGUARD.TXT]] - [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview|Daggerfall Preview]]}} The victorious Goblins were led by and worshipped the god [[Lore:Malooc|Malooc]].{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{Ref|name=T26OFSIU|{{Cite Book|The 26th of First Seed is Upon Us!|ns_base=ON}}}}{{ref|name=EGE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}} In time, Volendrung, the Great Hammer of these Rourken Dwemer, ironically came to embody the power of Malacath, who had become known as one of the Dwemer's most bitter foes.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[OB:Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=WIV}}

===Later Conflicts===
[[File:SR-creature-Falmer.jpg|thumb|right|One of the Falmer, victims of the Dwemer]]
Sometime after the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]],{{Ref|name=FBJ|{{Cite Book|Frostbitten Journal}}}} various [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] kingdoms launched a desperate search for the fabled [[Lore:Wrathstone|Wrathstone]], which they believed would lead them to a weapon that would be their salvation against the encroaching forces of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]]. An expedition marched to the Dwemer city of [[Lore:Mzulft|Mzulft]], in the hold of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where it was rumored the Wrathstone was held. The Ayleids besieged Mzulft, but failed to breach it or retrieve the artifact.{{Ref|name=IPOM|{{Cite Book|In Pursuit of Mhuvnak}}}} At the time, the Ayleids were unaware that the Dwemer only possessed the left half of the Wrathstone, and that half was stored not at Mzulft, but at the nearby Kagrenzel.{{Ref|name=IPOM}} The famed Dwemer architect Mhuvnak left Mzulft for Kagrenzel immediately after the attack to retrieve the artifact, realizing that it may need to be better guarded. It is thought that he found the defenses of Kagrenzel inadequate, and built the [[Lore:Vault of Mhuvnak|Vault of Mhuvnak]] to house it instead. Long after the time of Mhuvnak, following Mzulft's temporary fall to the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] and subsequent reclamation by the Dwemer, the Vault of Mhuvnak was buried in a mountain of ice and lost. The Dwemer of Mzulft attempted to use their Oculory to locate the "Frostvault", but no record of the results of their search was found, and the left half of the Wrathstone was not reclaimed.{{Ref|name=IPOM}}

Meanwhile, the Dwemer in Skyrim had also planted the seeds of another crisis by taking in the [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]]. While [[Lore:Harald|King Harald]] was credited with driving the last of the "elves" out of Skyrim before the end of his reign in {{Year|1E 221}}, pockets of them survived in the wilderness and eventually sought sanctuary with the Dwemer. Although they agreed to provide the Snow Elves safe haven in their underground realm, the distrustful Dwemer rendered them blind by forcing them to consume toxic fungi once found growing deep underground. The Snow Elves became servants of the Dwemer, and then, after several generations, degenerated into their corrupted slaves, the [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]].{{ref|name=TFAS|{{Cite Book|The Falmer: A Study}}}} After generations of subjugation, the Falmer rebelled, leading to the [[Lore:War of the Crag|War of the Crag]], a bloody conflict that was waged far below the rest of the world. The Nords above remained oblivious as both the Dwemer and Falmer fought and fled even further down, into [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]]'s deepest, most hidden reaches. The war lasted decades and only ended when the Dwemer vanished.{{ref|name=TFAS}}

===Disappearance of the Dwemer===
[[File:MW-book-Divine Metaphysics p3.jpg|left|thumb|Anumidium, as depicted in ''[[Lore:Divine Metaphysics|Divine Metaphysics]]'']][[File:RG-misc-Dwemer Wall.png|thumb|Dwemer autoportrait, found at Bthzark and Vvardenfell|right]][[File:MW-creature-Yagrum_Bagarn.jpg|thumb|left|Yagrum Bagarn, the Last Living Dwarf]]
====War of the First Council====
The Dwemer all across Tamriel inexplicably disappeared during the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], the biggest and final battle of the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]].{{ref|name=PGE3M}} This purportedly came about due to the efforts of Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]], who was the Chief Tonal Architect of the Dwemer responsible for designing the great freeholds of their Second Empire.{{ref|name=MWYagrum|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} Kagrenac constructed [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Kagrenac's Tools]] to harness the powers of the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], which the Dwemer had discovered beneath Red Mountain. When the Chimer found out, they considered this aim blasphemous and sought to stop it. Kagrenac's exact goal remains unclear, but it is believed he sought to heighten his race to [[Lore:Anumidium|Anumidium]].{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} The disappearance of a whole race in an instant sparked many theories,{{ref|name=PGE3M}} but is generally thought to have followed this attempt to use the tools on the Heart,{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}}{{ref|name=MWYagrum}} and simply coincided with the war.{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[Morrowind:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} It appears that the Dwemer were conflicted on their use of the Heart. Some opposed its use, warning that massive side effects were likely, while the majority of the Tonal Architects, including Kagrenac, and [[Lore:Bthuand Mzahnch|Bthuand Mzahnch]] (who wrote ''[[Lore:The_Egg_of_Time|The Egg of Time]]'' which downplayed the risks of tampering with the Heart of Lorkhan) wished to proceed.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}

====Later Theories and Evidence====
It appears that all members of the Dwemer race were simply removed from the world. When the previously untouched Dwemer ruins of Bamz-Amschend in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] were rediscovered, numerous piles of ashes were present next to weapons and armor, on chairs, and in beds, suggesting the Dwemer's bodies were suddenly reduced to ash in some way. Dwarven ghosts, often encountered in the ancient abandoned Dwemer ruins, are aggressive revenant spirits of Dwemer that are present on the mortal plane.{{Ref|name=VC|[[MW:Vala Catraso|Vala Catraso]]'s dialogue in [[MW:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} The dwarf [[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], however, is the last known living being of his kind. He returned to Tamriel from his journey in the Outer Realm, and came to discover his people missing. In {{Year|4E 201}}, Arniel Gane, a researcher at the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], attempted to recreate the circumstances of the Dwemer's disappearance after obtaining [[Lore:Keening|Keening]], one of Kagrenac's Tools, but vanished suddenly in the process, strengthening the theory that Kagrenac's use of the tools was the cause of the disappearance. Some scholars still resist the notion that the Dwemer disappeared all at once.{{ref|name=D3}}

A theory also exists that their sudden extinction was caused by their reversal of the usual processes involved in the creation of the Earth Bones, the laws of nature. Rather than creating the profane by commanding the sacred, such as in [[Lore:Conjuration|Conjuration]] magic or [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship, they sought to create Anumidium, their own sacred, from the "deaths of the profane".{{ref|name=MWBaladas}} [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], the warrior-poet god of the Dunmer, similarly believed that the disappearance of the Dwemer is in some way owed to their "divine sin"—attempting to create a god for their own purposes from the remains of the god [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]].{{ref|name=MWVivec|[[Morrowind:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} Whether their use of Kagrenac's Tools in this process or simply their intentions resulted in their extinction remains unclear in this theory.

Some Ashlander traditions assert that Nerevar, with the help of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], utilized the Heart of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] to make the Dwemer disappear.{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} However, other [[Lore:Ashlanders|Ashlander]] traditions gathered by the [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]]  hold that the Dwemer destroyed themselves, rather than that Nerevar destroyed them. According to these Dissident accounts, the claim that Nerevar is responsible is shared by the Tribunal Temple.{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}}

It is implied by the Khajiit that the events that led to the Dwemer's disappearance have had something do with time being shattered, and those who have used Numidium have not woken up to the reality of what really happened to the Dwarves.{{ref|name=Wwy|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}} The [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|36th Sermon of Vivec]] states Kagrenac had managed to activate Numidium and used it against the Chimer armies, and in this account Ayem, Seht and Vehk managed to come together to defeat the Walking Star, causing the Dwemer to be removed from the world.{{ref|name=36lesson|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Each time the device has been activated a [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] has occurred and this could explain many of the conflicting events and accounts of what had happened during the Battle of Red Mountain.{{ref|name=Wwy}} The Nordic account of the events have Lorkhan wreaking vengeance upon the Dwemer for their hubris, and that they would die by his hand.{{ref|name=WulfisShor|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}    

A few theories about the Dwemer's disappearance emerged from the popular ''[[Lore:Ancient Tales of the Dwemer|Ancient Tales of the Dwemer]]'' books, such as the idea that the Aedra and Daedra, offended by the Dwemer's religious skepticism, played a part in the disappearance,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Azura and the Box}}}} or that the Dwemer, frightened by [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], whose nature they couldn't understand nor control, hid from her sight by adopting [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]]i or [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]i cultures and would still be alive in modern days.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Dowry}}}} However, these books are actually fictional, adapted from various non-Dwemer tales with little historical accuracy and written by [[Lore:Gor Felim|Gor Felim]] under his pseudonym Marobar Sul.

Sightings of Dwemer were counted as a part of local folklore in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], but no known experts gave these rumors any credence.{{ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}}

===Impact on Redguards===
Though the Redguards of Hammerfell never lived amongst the Dwemer, they possess some notable reverence for the Dwarves, swearing by Dwarf-Kings and by the [[Lore:Volendrung|Great Hammer]] and more.{{ref|name=ARWEexcl|[[Arena:Races#Redguard|Redguard Exclamations]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}{{ref|name=DFWEexcl|[[Daggerfall:Races#Redguard|Redguard Exclamations]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Early Redguard legends potray the Dwemer as a mysterious and powerful race, capable of bending the very laws of nature to their will. Their legends considered them to be vanished but perhaps not gone.{{ref|name=DHAC}} In the early [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], it was purported that all the old cities of Dwarves were now the cities of modern Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=RGH}}

==Culture==
===Technology===
[[File:MW-item-Dwemer parchments copy.jpg||thumb|350px|Various Dwemer schematics]]
The Dwemer appear to have been a highly technologically advanced and particularly dogmatic race. Other races have still not cracked the secret behind their [[Lore:Dwarven Metal|metal]].{{ref|name=D1}} Their weapons were simple but effective; weaponsmiths relied on creating quality materials first and merely allowed the form of those materials to flow from the method of the weapon's use. Their mastery of steam and geothermal power through tapping into the natural lava sources under Morrowind allowed them to create airships, sentient machines, mechanical observatories, and lighting systems that continued to work for centuries without any maintenance. Most of their settlements are still inhabited by ''[[Lore:Dwemer Animunculi|Animunculi]]'', enchanted mechanical guardians, commonly known as "Centurions" or "Spheres".{{ref|name=D2}} They appear somehow linked to their place of origin and will lose power if removed from the vicinity.{{ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Senilius' Report}}}} This may explain why many Animunculi remain active even after so long and supports the theory that they are strongly influenced by tonal "Dwemer magic" from the ruins.{{ref|[[Online:Eldwen|Eldwen]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} Additionally, it appears that some Animunculi are capable of interpreting the actions of people around them - in a sense, 'perceiving' their intent - and responding accordingly.{{ref|name=ALJ|{{Cite Book|Aicantar's Lab Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}}

The Dwemer were somewhat comforted by their ability to empower lifeless fabrics into active beings through systematic regression techniques, denying the organic power of the gods while at the same time transcending the mortal systems of life.{{ref|name=MWBaladas}} This culture, often seen as sheer arrogance by others, allowed their technological capabilities to accelerate well ahead of any other race. In addition, the Dwemer also appear to have dabbled extensively and somewhat successfully in some of the more divine (or arcane) arts and sought to harness the supernatural powers of the Divines within their mechanical technology, even keeping an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] within the massive underground complex of Blackreach in Skyrim.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The Dwemer had headgear known as Dwarven Miters,{{ref|name=ONDwarvenVisorMiter|[[Online:Dwarven Visor Miter|Dwarven Visor Miter]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Neramo|[[ON:Neramo|Neramo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} which gave them the ability that was known as "The Calling", which seems to have been similar to [[Lore:Telepathy|telepathy]].{{ref|name=CHIM|{{Cite Book|Chimarvamidium}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}} It allowed the Dwemer to communicate with each other over great distances. Three known varieties of miters exist, with the master miter being worn by the "Tuathumz" or "central pylon" of their Thought Calling system. It contains three finials thought to be broadcast-foci.{{ref|name=ONDwarvenMasterMiter|[[Online:Dwarven Master Miter|Dwarven Master Miter]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The spiked miter has spikes believed they actually functioned as "etheric antenna," helping with long-distance Thought Calls.{{ref|name=ONDwarvenSpikedMiter|[[Online:Dwarven Spiked Miter|Dwarven Spiked Miter]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The common visor miter lacks these features. It has been suggested that, in the last moments before they disappeared, Lord Kagrenac used The Calling to summon all of the Dwemer people and rally them to carry out his plans to transcend the [[Lore:Mundus|Mortal Plane]].{{ref|name=CHIM}} Whether this was related to their demise or not is uncertain.

Some sources suggest the origins of the Dwemer's mastery over natural forces might lie with the Ehlnofey. The Ehlnofey are thought to have taken on students before they disappeared from the mortal world, passing their knowledge on to emerging races. Some theories thus propose that the use of the term "Dwarves" to refer to the Dwemer originates from ancient times when they were the "brilliant students" of the Ehlnofey with the sobriquet having been given to them by their titanic masters. The events of the Dwemer's tutelage under the Ehlnofey are described in more detail in the full text of the Anuad myth.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} Some sources claim that the emergent elves might've learned from observing the Ehlnofey and their control over natural laws. While the Aldmer clans from whom the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] would eventually emerge are said to have learned how to defy the Earthbones governing form and lifespan by watching [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], the first of the Ehlnofey to become an Earthbone who set the laws controlling form in place, teaching them how to invoke the [[Lore:Wild Hunt|Wild Hunt]], the Dwemer would learn how to defy natural laws by witnessing Ehlnofey control laws of nature such as those governing decay or the [[Lore:Portals|passage]] between [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]], and it is said that the Dwemer gained much knowledge through such observation.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

===Language===
Very little is known about [[Lore:Dwemer Language|Dwemeris]],{{ref|{{Cite Book|Dwarven Writings|ns_base=ON}}}} the Dwemeri language, but there have been many attempts to translate it using [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]] for reference and comparison. Some Dwemer books, especially records dating from after the founding of Resdayn, were written in Aldmeris, making them accessible to modern scholars. However, even in Aldmeris, many Dwemer terms remain uncertain. As the books and other artifacts in Dwemer ruins rarely show signs of wear or age, leading some to believe that the Dwemer knew of a preservative effect, perhaps a device still active which denies or controls the Earth Bones governing time and decay.{{ref|name=MWBaladas}} Several different, incomplete styles of written Dwemeris appear throughout Tamriel. The glyphs found in the ruins on Stros M'Kai differ significantly from those found in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and Morrowind, which suggests there may have been dialects or variations across the Dwemer clans. Some of spoken Dwemeris remains in prefixes and suffixes in historical names, such as "Volen-", meaning "hammer", and "-Fell" meaning "city", giving [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]]; "City of the Hammer".{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}

===Architecture===
[[File:MW-place-Nchardahrk.jpg|left|thumb|Dwemer ruins (Morrowind)]][[File:SR-place-Mzulft.jpg|right|thumb|Dwemer ruins (Skyrim)]]
The Dwemer lived in strongholds,{{ref|name=TWOTFC}} often called Freehold colonies{{ref|name=CON|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of Nchuleft}}}} or city-states,{{ref|name=TAW}} which are typically laden with ancient steam-powered contraptions, many of which are still running. They are thought to have delved down to a certain threshold in the ground, known as a "Geocline", before they began building the vital structures of a colony.{{ref|name=DI3}} There are hundreds if not thousands of Dwarven buildings made of precise square shapes, but far fewer examples of rounded or curved stonework, suggesting the Dwemer favored well-calculated designs based on angled lines rather than riskier, more imprecise calculations using arcs and curves.{{ref|name=D1}} Numerous pipes can be found in and around Dwemer ruins, and many devices and constructs appear to emit steam from their inner workings. There may be "boilers" or similar components housed inside the Dwemer Centurions. It has been speculated that the constructs are powered by some kind of checkpoint in each ruin, or possibly by the combined signal of every ruin's mechanics, but this is not certain. There seem to have been regional differences in architectural and engineering styles; for instance, the prevalent style among northern clans is now known as "Deep Venue", wherein several structures are built within natural caverns. Arcanex are smaller, largely above-ground structures that are believed to have been research centers of some kind, though little is known about them thanks to looters. Animoculotories are Dwemer factories found within many strongholds.{{ref|name=DI2|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol II}}}}

Dwemer constructs taken from Vvardenfell, where many Dwemer ruins are present, appear to shut down when a certain distance between them and [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] is reached.{{ref|name=SR}} It also seems that many objects which may be found in ruins were once covered in mesh or cloth, which has decayed over the years. This would explain why the "home comforts" of the Dwemer may seem very spartan to a visitor. There are also few large halls in most ruins. This could be due to the fact that most if not all Dwemer ruins are underground, where large interior spaces are difficult to fashion at best. Many Dwemer colonies were named after their rulers. The largest concentration of strongholds is in Vvardenfell (which translates as "City of the Strong Shield" in Dwemeris). Many [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] in Hammerfell have shown a particular interest in the Dwemer ruins there for unknown reasons.

===Appearance===
[[File:TR-creature-Radac Stungnthumz.jpg|thumb|right|Radac Stungnthumz]]
Contrary to many legends, archaeological evidence of known Dwemer ruins leads one to believe that they were about the same size as the typical human or elf, evidenced by the fact that all existing Dwarven armor is average sized (although some claim that "Dwarven armor" is either the outer shells of machines or mismatched pieces from various devices).{{ref|name=ROK|{{Cite Book|Ruins of Kemel-Ze}}}} According to fable, the moniker "dwarf" may have been given long ago by the giants to their "little" friends.<ref name=MWHasphat>[[Morrowind:Hasphat Antabolis|Hasphat Antabolis]]' dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]</ref> [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] excavation of Dwemer ruins supports the Dlyxexic theory that the translation of Dwemer as Deep Elves might also be read as Smart Elves. Indeed, perhaps the brilliant students of the Ehlnofey mentioned in [[Lore:The Annotated Anuad|The Anuad]] are the Dwarves themselves.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}} Purported descriptions of [[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|the last living Dwemer]] cannot be relied upon, as he was admittedly bloated and deformed by [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]], and the only other visual examples come from sculptures and [[Lore:Dwarven Spectre|Dwarven Spectres]] found in their ruined dwellings, who had pale eyes and ivory pale colored skin.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Dwarven Spectre|Dwarven Spectre texture]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} It can be deduced, however, that long beards were popular among the lost elves, and they preferred heavy metal armors and robes. Their remaining features could be described similarly to that of the other elven races, as no specific information exists.

===Family===
Little is known of the [[Lore:Marriage|marriage]] customs of the Dwemer, or if they even practiced marriage.{{ref|name=TalesX|{{Cite Book|The Dowry}}}} There is at least one case of a Dwemer known to have been involved in a committed romantic relationship with an Ayleid princess.{{ref|[[ON:Urenenya's Lament (quest)|Urenenya's Lament]] quest in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} They are claimed to have read their children unusual bedtime rhymes.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|A Type of Zero Still to Be Discovered|ns_base=General}}}} There is considered to be no evidence that the Dwemer kept dogs, or pets of any other kind.{{ref|[[ON:Ebon Dwarven Wolf|Ebon Dwarven Wolf]] mount description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

===Philosophy===
{{Quote Box|''A Dwemer sai{{nst|ON=d:<br>"|d, '}}Nothing is of any use. We must go and misinterpret this.{{nst|ON="|'}}''|''[[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 3|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 3]]''}}
It is unknown whether the Dwemer worshipped any gods. However, it is known that they despised and scorned the Daedra, and mocked the "foolish" rituals of their followers, "prefer[ring] instead their gods of Reason and Logic".{{ref|name=TBORM}} These may be metaphorical or literal terms; [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], the author, capitalized them in his writings which could be interpreted as an emphasized admonishment of their idolatry by means of substituting spiritual worship with absolute faith in those ideas. It could also be meant to suggest some sort of deified representation of those ideas or aspects of Dwemer thought. Since the Heart of Lorkhan offered some form of shared spiritual link among the Dwemer attuned to it - this could also refer to the collective reason and logic of the Dwemer people given voice through the powers of [[Lore:Tonal Architecture|Tonal Architecture]] and the heart of a dead god. The Dwemer religion, if it can even be called that, is said to be one of the most complex and difficult puzzles of Dwemer culture.{{ref|name=SOTA|{{Cite Book|Song of the Alchemists}}}}{{ref|name=NFAF|{{Cite Book|Nchunak's Fire and Faith}}}} 

Dwarven temples are known to exist, such as the one located deep in the mines of [[Lore:Fang Lair|Fang Lair]].{{ref|[[Arena:Fang Lair|Fang Lair]] in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}} According to the ancient engravings Dwarven executioners were known as "Chun Abakch". They were known to always conceal their features under a hood of black spidersilk, a material that was also used for funeral shrouds.{{ref|name=DEEH|[[Online:Deep Elf Executioner's Hood|Deep Elf Executioner's Hood]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Constellations===
The Dwemer were known to study the [[Lore:Constellations|constellations]] intently. Their potryals of the constellations have been described as the "Dwarven pantheon".{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:CrimeGorilla/Redguard 3D Gallery|CrimeGorilla/Redguard 3D Gallery]] — [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]}} However, they had their own names for some of the constellations: The Automaton (The Apprentice), The Warmachine (The Atronach), The Mechanist (The Mage), and The Laboratory (The Ritual).{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Redguard Companion|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|135}} However,  the Dwarves of [[Lore:Bamz-Amschend|Bamz-Amschend]] strangely seem to have potrayed certain aspects differently than the Dwarves of Bthzark. They ommitted the Laboratory from their potrayls, and potrayed the Serpent differently.{{ref|[[Tribunal:Bamz-Amschend#Hearthfire Hall|Bamz-Amschend]] in ''[[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}{{ref|The [[Redguard:Observatory|Observatory]] in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} Specifics surrounding the Dwarves' relationship and beliefs about the thirteen constellations is unknown.

===Common Artifacts===
Dwemer artifacts are highly prized throughout the Empire for trade and collecting. However, during the late Third Era [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] charters stated that all Dwemer artifacts are the property of the Emperor.{{ref|name=GenRuins|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue|Generic dialogue]] regarding [[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue D#Dwemer Ruins|Dwemer ruins]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} The trade{{ref|name=GenArtifacts|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue|Generic dialogue]] regarding [[MW:Generic Dialogue D#Dwemer Artifacts|Dwener Artifacts]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and extraction of Dwemer artifacts was forbidden under Imperial Law.{{ref|name=GenRuins}} Everything from armor to housewares is greatly sought after, and smugglers will risk execution if caught for such a profitable trade.<ref name=MWHasphat/> Dwemer weapons and armor are especially valued, renowned for their excellent craftsmanship and sturdy design.<ref name=MWArtis>[[MW:Artisa Arelas|Artisa Arelas]]' dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]</ref> The Coldsnap [[Lore:Riekling|Riekling]] tribe is notable in that they have appropriated many of Dwemer artifacts.{{ref|[[ON:Landal Gevont|Landal Gevont]]'s dialogue in [[Online:DLC#Wrathstone|ESO: ''Wrathstone'']]}}

Dwarves are known to have equipped and augmented creatures with armor, as evidenced by still-extant animals such as [[Lore:Mudcrab#Dwarven Armored Mudcrab|Dwarven Armored Mudcrabs]] and [[Lore:Dog#Mastiff|Dwarven War Dogs]] fitted with Dwarven armor.{{ref|name=SrCrab|Appearance of [[Skyrim:Dwarven Armored Mudcrab (creature)|Dwarven Armored Mudcrab]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{ref|name=EsoDog|Appearance of [[Online:Dwarven War Dog|Dwarven War Dog]] pet in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
