It is with great pleasure I present to you an aperitif to travel, an invitation to explore, a casual incitement to get out of your normal routine and see the world. Here, my dear reader, is a look at ten of the most unique, terrible, bloody, glamorous, and notable castles in all of {{Lore Link|Tamriel}}. Perhaps I'll see you at one of these fascinating structures during my travels!

CASTLE WAYREST<br>
Arguably the most famous castle in all of Tamriel, {{Lore Link|Castle Wayrest|ONlink=Online:Wayrest Castle}} has stood at the heart of {{Lore Link|Stormhaven}}—and thus, the heart of {{Lore Link|Breton|Bretondom}}—since early in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. Its most notable features are the sweeping spires that provide a commanding view of not just the {{Lore Link|Wayrest|city}}, but the countryside beyond as well. Constructed by the [[Lore:Gardner Dynasty|Gardner]] family line, its classical approach to stone-keep architecture set the standard for noble homes for centuries afterward. 

CASTLE MORNARD<br>
Poised above the deep-water port of {{Lore Link|Vastyr}} on the {{Lore Link|Galen|northernmost isle}} of the {{Lore Link|Systres Archipelago}}, it can be argued that this sprawling multi-winged bastion of {{Lore Link|House Mornard}} is the beating heart and soul of the city. In some ways, {{Lore Link|Castle Mornard}} is Vastyr. Built in the heady reconstruction days after the {{sic|{{Year|2E 365|1E 365}}|2E 365|description=incorrect year}} siege by the {{Lore Link|Gathering Storm}}, it's also a fitting memorial for {{Lore Link|Bernique Mornard}}. Said to be "the mother of Vastyr." Her legacy persists in the sweeping stone-wall curtains and sturdy reinforcement of the seaward piers.

CASTLE THORN<br>
While rumors swirl aplenty about the [[Lore:Vampirism|strange tradition]]s of the Thorn family, it is hard to argue with the grandeur of their incredible {{Lore Link|Castle Thorn|ancestral home}}. A massive central spire soars above the foothills of the {{Lore Link|Haafingar Mountains}}, with minarets adorning every one of the enormous concentric walls. I have it on good authority the family allows tours of the castle's public spaces, but only in the mornings. So be sure to arrive early.

THE ALINOR ROYAL PALACE<br>
The {{Lore Link|High Elf|High Elves}} of {{Lore Link|Summerset}} have an ancient tradition of breathtaking architecture, and the {{Lore Link|Alinor Royal Palace}} represents a line of royal succession going back thousands of years. The castle is built around several large, airy chambers topped with sky-scraping spires. It was, in fact, the massive wall of windows in the Aldmeri council chambers that inspired me to write this piece in the first place.

CASTLE EBONHEART<br>
The castle at the heart of {{Lore Link|Ebonheart|Ebonheart City}} is a beautiful and terrible old pile of stone perched like a hunting {{Lore Link|Cliff Racer|cliff racer}} at the base of {{Lore Link|Ash Mountain}}. The religious ecstasy of the {{Lore Link|Dark Elf|Dark Elves}} and their reverence for the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] results in architectural choices that may baffle and bemuse visitors, but no one can doubt the resulting three-spired structure's majestic—if gothic—glory.

SCARP KEEP<br>
Also known as {{Lore Link|Scarp Keep|Orsinium Keep}}, this sprawling stone edifice serves as the seat of power for the Orcish king. Based on the original palace at the heart of {{Lore Link|Old Orsinium}}, the ancient city that now lies in ruin, this modern construction overflows with firelight, laughter, and feasting at all hours of the day and night. You will never forget the first time you step into the cavernous main hall, looking up in awe at the massive stone pillars that flank this phenomenal space.

CASTLE NAVIRE<br>
The sprawling grounds of {{Lore Link|Castle Navire}} on the distant shores of {{Lore Link|High Isle}} almost beggar belief. Verdant fields of wildflowers run up to ancient, druid-tended woods. And then the castle itself, a staggered construction in the classical Bretonic style, serves as the abode of {{Lore Link|House Dufort}}. I had the pleasure of visiting during one of the {{sic|semi-annual|description=incorrect frequency (tourney does not occur twice yearly)}} Sapphire Tournaments, and nothing stirs the blood like the roar of an audience cheering for their knightly champions.

CASTLE RAVENWATCH<br>
Perched at the base of {{Lore Link|Rivenspire}}'s northern mountain range, {{Lore Link|Castle Ravenwatch}} might seem a bit unassuming in the face of the grandeur of some of the other sites on this list. Not so! This classical representation of Breton construction is exquisitely maintained, and the members of the {{Lore Link|House Ravenwatch|Ravenwatch}} family are incredible hosts when it comes to touring their historically significant architecture. In particular, make it a point to explore the cavernous mausoleums that run beneath the castle grounds.

CASTLE LEYAWIIN<br>
If I were forced to choose a favorite among the ancient edifices of Tamriel, Leyawiin {{Lore Link|Leyawiin Castle|Castle}} would be it. This beautiful {{Lore Link|imperial}} construction on the doorstep to {{Lore Link|Blackwood}} has everything you could want in a spectacular castle. Staunch circular towers, a soaring main hall, rambling side corridors that lead up to the main keep on the lands overlooking the town. Incredible. If you can find your way to this jewel on the {{Lore Link|Lower Niben}}, you simply must do so!

THE BLUE PALACE<br>
Spanning the great arch beyond the heart of {{Lore Link|Solitude}} in {{Lore Link|Western Skyrim}}, the {{Lore Link|Blue Palace}} may be one of the most unique constructions on the continent. Passing through a tremendous bailey, visitors enter the dramatic entrance hall with its twin sweeping staircases. This opulent demesne would seem to run contrary to the hearty reputation of the local {{Lore Link|Nord}}s. In truth, this beauty is a facade. For the Blue Palace can withstand even the most desperate of sieges, a war-ready keep with the beauty of a noble at an Imperial ball. 

That about does it, dear readers! I hope this has been an amusing trifle on the long journey from where you were to where you are going. Keep your wits about you on the road, and may [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]] keep you safe on your journey!
