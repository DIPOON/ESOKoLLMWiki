[[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] is a realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] created and ruled over by [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Domination. As befits his sphere, Bal's realm is a desolate, apocalyptic image of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], which is achieved by both mocking imitation and outright theft of parts of the mortal realm. It is inhabited by [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] and the eternally-tormented husks of mortals called [[Lore:Soul Shriven|Soul Shriven]].{{Ref|name=OTNOC|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Coldharbour}}}} 

Mortals who made deals with Molag Bal can also be found living in Coldharbour.{{Ref|name=Uxith|{{Cite Book|How We Came to Coldharbour}}}}{{Ref|name=Faraniel|[[On:Faraniel|Faraniel]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The souls of [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] are sent to Coldharbour when they die.{{ref|name=ESOYushiha|[[ON:Yushiha|Yushiha]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Blood and Tears|Blood and Tears]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{ref|name=ADP|{{Cite Book|A Daedric Proposal}}}}{{ref|name=ESOGwendi|[[ON:Gwendis|Gwendis]]' dialogue during [[Online:The Gray Council|The Gray Council]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Coldharbour has been described as a manifestation of fear and exploitation.{{ref|name=DPOT|[[ON:Coldharbour Antiquities#Daedric Pillar of Torment|Daedric Pillar of Torment]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Descriptions of the plane vary widely, but all accounts agree that Coldharbour is a dismal, cold, and largely lifeless realm.{{Ref|name=OTNOC}}<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:ON-place-The Hollow City.jpg|thumb|right|The Hollow City]]
During the time of the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], the city-state of [[Lore:Abagarlas|Abagarlas]] worshiped Molag Bal and was gifted with a great weapon, the [[Lore:Mortuum Vivicus|Mortuum Vivicus]].{{ref|name=NOTMV|{{Cite Book|Notes on the Mortuum Vivicus}}}} However, the citizens of [[Lore:Delodiil|Delodiil]] preemptively attacked, and Bal was forced to withdraw the Vivicus to the {{Lore Link|Halls of Submission}} in Coldharbour.{{ref|name=ESOTPC|Events of [[ON:The Prismatic Core|The Prismatic Core]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOWOTC|Events of [[ON:Will of the Council|Will of the Council]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

One of [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s old cohorts, [[Lore:Vastarie|Vastarie]], stole the first [[Lore:Black Soul Gem|black soul gems]] used by the King of Worms from Coldharbour.{{ref|name=TLV|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Vastarie}}}} Mannimarco believed that the secret to "trapping souls as one might capture lesser Daedra" was being held by Molag Bal, so with all her youthful courage, Vastarie braved the ashen wastes and carried out the heist as her fellow necromancers held open a portal for her.{{ref|name=TLV}} The small black gems she brought back to Nirn furthered Mannimarco's quest for power and advanced the study of necromancy as it is known to mortal-kind.{{ref|name=TLV}}

Circa {{Year|1E 2260}}, a portal to Coldharbour was opened when the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]] collapsed the [[Lore:Coral Tower (Thras)|Coral Tower]] of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]].{{ref|name=JOTS|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}} Several ships were pulled into the realm, and the {{Lore Link|Maelstrom of Bal}} remained as a link between Coldharbour and [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{ref|name=JOTS}} [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] journeyed to Coldharbour in {{Year|1E 2920}} to bargain with Molag Bal.{{ref|name=1E2920}} The mage [[Lore:Morian Zenas|Morian Zenas]] famously visited the realm during his voyages through Oblivion, and wept at the sight of the desolation.{{ref|name=TDOB}}

At some point, a group of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon's]] followers toiled as slaves to Molag Bal and his overseers in Coldharbour. These true believers in Dagon would draw upon Dagon's fire to help them survive the torment. Not expecting to escape Coldharbour, they would plot and scheme to thwart Molag Bal and his minions whenever they could Mehrunes Dagon would reward those who believed in him with the means of producing a powerful explosion that would rattle Coldharbour and remove the instruments of torture from the Daedric overseers. Mehrunes Dagon then manifested a projection of himself in the mountains of Coldharbour above the enslaved mortals and their Daedric captor and did battle with Molag Bal. Though Dagon was not able to defeat Molag Bal with just a projection, he was able to free the souls of his followers from Molag Bal's torment and take their souls to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]].{{ref|name=EoMDV2|{{Cite Book|Epics of Mehrunes Dagon, Volume 2}}}}

The most significant interaction between Nirn and Coldharbour was Molag Bal's attempted [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] in the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. In {{Year|2E 579}}, as the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] dragged on and the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] remained unlit, Molag Bal's minion [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] deceived Emperor [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] into using the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] to initiate a ritual that triggered the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]].{{ref|name=ESOTH|Events of [[ON:The Harborage (quest)|The Harborage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The ancient covenant between mortals and [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] was broken, and Nirn was set adrift in the Mundus,{{Ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}} rendering it vulnerable to Daedric incursions. Molag Bal began deploying machines known as [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] to pull Nirn into Coldharbour, and sought to merge the two realms into one.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, Aquilarios was freed from his imprisonment in Coldharbour and returned to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in an attempt to fulfill a prophecy of the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]], which had revealed to him that a Soul Shriven hero named the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] would stop Molag Bal's plans.{{ref|name=ESOTHP|[[ON:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Harborage (quest)|The Harborage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} With Varen's help, the Vestige reassembled the [[Lore:Five Companions|Five Companions]] (sans Mannimarco) in secret, recovering the Amulet of Kings and exposing Mannimarco's plot to mantle Molag Bal's godhead.{{ref|name=ESOMQ|Events of [[ON:Online|ESO]]'s main quest}} [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]], concerned by Molag Bal's growing influence on Tamriel, called for a council between the three leaders of the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]] to discuss a possible invasion of Coldharbour.{{ref|name=ESOTWOTC|Events of [[ON:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Although the alliances were unwilling to provide aid, the leaders gave their blessing for the neutral [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] and [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] to mount an invasion force.{{ref|name=ESOTWOTC}}

[[File:ON-place-Library of Dusk.jpg|thumb|right|Coldharbour]]
The Tamrielic invaders were initially thwarted by Coldharbour's defenses, being scattered all across the realm after entering a portal. The Daedric Prince [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], an ancient enemy of Bal, opened the doors of her stronghold in the realm—[[Lore:The Hollow City|the Hollow City]]—as a safe haven for the scattered invaders.{{ref|name=ESOGK|[[ON:The Groundskeeper|The Groundskeeper]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Hollow City (quest)|The Hollow City]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} After destroying [[Lore:The Great Shackle|the Great Shackle]] and assembling allies from all across Coldharbour,{{ref|name=ESOBTS|Events of [[ON:Breaking the Shackle|Breaking the Shackle]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} the {{Lore Link|Army of Meridia}} made a final assault on {{Lore Link|the Endless Stair}}, where they successfully collapsed the {{Lore Link|Planar Vortex}} between Mundus and Coldharbour, foiling the Planemeld.{{ref|name=ESOTFS|Events of [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Following his defeat, Molag Bal retreated to [[Lore:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]], when Aquilarios chose that moment to strike.{{ref|name=ESOGOS|Events of [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Using the Amulet of Kings, the Vestige was blessed with the power of the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]], with which Bal was banished to the [[Lore:Void|Void]] in his own realm. Following this, the Prince lost possession of all mortal souls he had captured.{{ref|name=ESOGOSD|[[ON:Meridia|Meridia]] and [[ON:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue during [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

That same year, the Fighters Guild was manipulated by Meridia to thwart Bal's plans to return the Mortuum Vivicus to Tamriel, and the Vivicus was ultimately destroyed.{{ref|name=ESOWOTC}} Also that year, {{Lore Link|Abagandra}}—the Lightless Remnant of the [[Lore:Doomcrag|Doomcrag]]—was given to Bal and transported to Coldharbour to prevent it devastating the Tamrielic region of [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]].{{ref|[[ON:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Lightless Remnant|The Lightless Remnant]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]] of the [[Lore:Gray Host|Gray Host]] sought a way to pull the souls of vampires out of Coldharbour.{{ref|name=MBStE|{{Cite Book|My Beloved Siblings, the Exarchs}}}}{{ref|name=VJ|{{Cite Book|Verandis's Journal}}}}<!--{{ref|name=ONGRada|[[Online:Rada al-Saran|Rada al-Saran]]'s dialogue during {{huh}} in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}--> This was one stepping stone in his quest to break Molag Bal's hold over vampires, which he believed would bring salvation to his kind.{{ref|name=ONGVerandis|[[Online:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Gray Council|The Gray Council]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He eventually found a way to bring souls back from Coldharbour through [[Lore:Harrowstorm|harrowstorms]].{{ref|[[Online:Fennorian|Fennorian]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Dark Clouds Over Solitude|Dark Clouds Over Solitude]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Geography and Inhabitants==
[[File:ON-sky-Coldharbour.jpg|thumb|left|The cloudy sky of Coldharbour]]
[[File:ON-place-Coldharbour 06.jpg|thumb|right|Forests and lakes of azure plasm in Coldharbour]]
Coldharbour supposedly emulates an apocalyptic version of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], including a defiled copy of the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] called the Lightless Oubliette.{{ref|name=TDOB|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}} It is unnaturally and persistently cold,{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}} even with magical resistance, and ancient, blackened ruins fill the landscape.{{ref|name=TDOB}} The ground is sometimes described as a barely-traversable sludge or oozing mud.{{ref|name=TDOB}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}} Even the air assaults the senses, changing rapidly from the stench of rancid death to the sweet aroma of flowered perfume.{{ref|name=1E2920|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} The sky burns in a continuous belch of fire,{{ref|name=TDOB}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}} but largely appears as a series of ever present purple clouds.{{ref|name=ESOCold}} The landscape itself is made up of craggy, floating islands of blackened rocks that are covered in a blue lichen,{{Ref|name=NJIC|{{Cite Book|Naryu's Journal/Imperial City}}}} hovering in the void.{{ref|name=ESOCold|Appearance of and in-game information on [[ON:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Notably, the realm has an abundance of [[Lore:Azure Plasm|Azure Plasm]], a type of chaotic creatia that resembles lakes of brackish, icy cold water.{{ref|name=1E2920}}{{ref|name=ESOCold}} The plane is divided into three main regions: the {{Lore Link|Shrouded Plain}}, the {{Lore Link|Black Garrison}}, and the {{Lore Link|Fist of Stone}}.{{ref|name=ESOCold}} Other important features include {{Lore Link|the Chasm}}, a gap between islands that surrounds the Fist of Stone, and the Hollow City of Meridia, which remains outside Bal's influence.{{ref|name=ESOGK}}{{ref|name=ESOCold}}

Although unsuccessful, the Planemeld had a significant impact on Coldharbour. Molag Bal struck a deal with the [[Lore:Mad Architect|Mad Architect]] to construct the Endless Stair and the Planar Vortex, as well as a labyrinthine structure known as the {{Lore Link|Vaults of Madness}}.{{ref|name=ESOPVLS|[[ON:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] text for the [[ON:Planar Vortex|Planar Vortex]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TAWPT|{{Cite Book|To All Who Pass Through}}}} The Great Shackle was built as a mooring for the Dark Anchors, and {{Lore Link|the Wailing Prison}} was established to hold the vast number of Soul Shriven being sacrificed to Bal. The [[Lore:Castle of the Worm|Castle of the Worm]] served as the headquarters of the [[Lore:Worm Cult|Worm Cult]] for a time. Some parts of Tamriel were successfully ripped into Oblivion, forming pocket realms such as the [[Lore:Village of the Lost|Village of the Lost]] that were intended to be integrated into Coldharbour proper.

The realm is home to a multitude of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. Included among them are the [[Lore:Cold-Flame Atronach|Cold-Flame Atronach]]s from the [[Lore:Fourth Sinus of Takubar|Fourth Sinus of Takubar]], the gargantuan [[Lore:Daedric Titan|Daedric Titan]]s, the [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]]s, and the pesky [[Lore:Banekin|Banekin]]. The realm has two major clans of [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], the [[Lore:Deathbringer Clan|Deathbringer Clan]] and the [[Lore:Foolkillers Clan|Foolkillers Clan]], who vie for dominance.

As well as the Daedra, many of the realm's inhabitants come from Mundus. The majority of the mortals are Soul Shriven, whose souls belong to Molag Bal and whose bodies are animated with a Daedric [[Lore:Vestige (soul)|vestige]] in their stead. They live a life of eternal torment, cursed to slowly wither away without their soul only to be reborn. They are used for labor or amusement and eventually lose all sense of self. Some of the Shriven become consumed with hunger and turn feral, eating everything they come across&mdash;including lesser Daedra and each other. Other mortals live in isolated settlements for various different reasons, such as the {{Lore Link|Shadow Walkers}} of {{Lore Link|the Moonless Walk}} and the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s of [[Lore:Haj Uxith|Haj Uxith]]. Some species of flora and fauna even seem to thrive in the realm, including [[Lore:Netch|netch]], [[Lore:Bantam Guar|bantam guar]], [[Lore:Scuttler|scuttler]]s, and various species of fish.

==Security==
[[File:ON-place-The Chasm.jpg|thumb|right|The Chasm]]
The realm possesses several qualities that function as security measures. Mages who attempt to enter Coldharbour by the way of portals will find themselves in a location they did not intend to end up at.{{ref|name=ESOMalkur|[[ON:Malkur Valos|Malkur Valos]]' dialogue during [[ON:Hall of Judgment (quest)|Hall of Judgment]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It doesn't matter if the mage knows where they want to go or not, all unauthorized portals leading into Coldharbour will leave foreign troops or entities scattered every which way and hopelessly lost.{{ref|name=ESOMalkur}} This makes it difficult to gather forces to invade, as Vanus Galerion's Coldharbour invasion force learned while attempting to halt the Planemeld in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=ESOColdQuests|Events of all combined [[ON:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The invasion force was separated after entering the portals opened by the Mages Guild, and many troops were lost to the wilds of Coldharbour or abducted by the minions of Molag Bal.{{ref|name=ESOColdQuests}} Magical constructs known as Coldharbour Sentinels are used by Molag Bal to guide his vision in Coldharbour. With their aid, he can see into whatever prison they're anchored in. These Sentinels are magically connected, so destroying one will blind the other Sentinels it was bound to. Several of these constructs were present in the {{Lore Link|Wailing Prison}} during the Vestige's fateful escape.{{ref|[[ON:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Soul Shriven in Coldharbour|Soul Shriven in Coldharbour]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Facilities==
Coldharbour is full of innumerable facilities, all built to further Molag Bal's dark schemes. Among these facilities is the {{Lore Link|Library of Dusk}}, a library filled with propaganda, or "the truth according to Molag Bal".{{ref|Loading screen text for the [[Online:Library of Dusk|Library of Dusk]] in [[Online:Loading Screens|ESO]]}} The Library of Dusk is available for use by the Prince's minions, but also serves as a trap for scholars who happen upon the plane.{{ref|name=TLODRB|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}}{{ref|name=ESOKireth|[[ON:Kireth Vanos|Kireth Vanos]]' dialogue during [[ON:Through the Daedric Lens|Through the Daedric Lens]] and [[ON:The Library of Dusk|The Library of Dusk]]}} There is another library in Coldharbour that is slightly less accessible. It is Molag Bal's personal collection, located in his stronghold. The shelves in Molag Bal's library span entire cliffs and run deep into a chasm. Unsurprisingly, plenty of the books on his shelves are very disturbing.{{ref|[[Online:Kireth Vanos|Kireth Vanos]]' dialogue during [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The plane mostly consists of great slave pens and prisons,{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}} but other facilities include those where research is conducted. The aforementioned [[Lore:Vile Laboratory|Vile Laboratory]] is one such place. There, Molag Bal has had experiments conducted on souls{{ref|Events of [[ON:The Soul-Meld Mage|The Soul-Meld Mage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and vestigial hybridization.{{ref|name=LFAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}} He is fascinated by the metamagical technology of Soul Gems, and often has a mystical research project or two in the works to develop new and more efficient methods of stealing and imprisoning the souls of mortals.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens #Location Messages|Loading Screen]] text for [[Online:The Wailing Maw|the Wailing Maw]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Not every part of Coldharbour serves a practical use. There are a lot of graveyards in Molag Bal's plane of Oblivion for no other reason than because he likes them.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens #Location Messages|Loading Screen]] text for [[Online:Mal Sorra's Tomb|Mal Sorra's Tomb]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
