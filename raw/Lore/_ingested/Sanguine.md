Sanguine has a reputation among mortals for not being "as bad" as other Daedric Princes and is likelier than most Princes to take an interest in mortal affairs.{{ref|name=SRLoading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Some see him as a harmless, inveterate drunk due to his outgoing nature and his propensity for partying and drinking [[Lore:Alcoholic Beverages|alcohol]].{{Ref|name=TVToB|{{Cite Book|The Vile Truth of Barbas}}}}{{ref|name=ESOBaseekus|[[ON:Baseekus|Baseekus]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Despite his vulgar nature, many consider Sanguine one of the more "sophisticated" Princes.{{Ref|name=LA:ABayq|{{Cite Book|Arthenice Belloq Answers Your Questions}}}} He is described by [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth]] to be one of the weaker Princes.{{ref|name=LtFayq|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}} Those who worship him or appreciate his nature claim that Sanguine has never hurt anyone maliciously.{{ref|name=Argues-with-Frogs|[[ON:Argues-with-Frogs|Argues-with-Frogs]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He enjoys playing pranks on others.{{ref|name=OBSanQuest|Events of [[OB:Sanguine|Sanguine]]'s quest in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} He is reputed for throwing the best parties in Oblivion and celebrates at the drop of a hat. One instance of these spontaneous celebrations is the 400th anniversary of Sanguine's [[Lore:Dog|dog]] learning to play "[[Lore:Undead|undead]]," which occurred sometime around {{Year|2E 182}}.{{ref|name=ESOChee-Saak|[[ON:Chee-Saak|Chee-Saak]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} All manner of entertainment can be found at Sanguine's parties, such as stage plays, orgies,{{ref|name=D4C}}{{ref|name=DFSanguine}} bardic concerts, dancing, skooma,{{ref|name=SRN|{{Cite Book|Sanguine's Revelers Note|ns_base=Online}}}} arena fights, and of course, refreshments.{{ref|name=LOTP|Events of [[ON:Life of the Party|Life of the Party]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Food and drink are usually present, but [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] is not.{{Ref|name=SISheogorath|[[Shivering:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO Writing Team AUA|ns_base=General}}}}

Partying with Sanguine can be a dangerous affair, as those who stay too long may not want to leave or are enticed to stay and may end up dying there.{{ref|name=ESOSings-with-Drink|[[ON:Sings-with-Drink|Sings-with-Drink]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Life of the Party|Life of the Party]]}} It is in Sanguine's nature to indulge the natures of others, particularly their darker desires—so to Sanguine, 'absolute control' is anathema.{{ref|name=LFNHATRAYQ2}} The Daedra in attendance may also become violent towards mortal guests.{{ref|name=ESODeroh-Metaku|[[ON:Deroh-Metaku|Deroh-Metaku]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Sanguine welcomes all mortals to his shindigs. Still, those who enter must partake in the drinks there if they wish to remain. Those who drink from Sanguine's goblet will be bound to the party and unable to leave.{{ref|name=FV|[[ON:Seducer Trilvath|Seducer Trilvath]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Foreign Vintage|Foreign Vintage]]}}{{ref|name=ESOAldmeri Spy|The [[ON:Aldmeri Spy|Aldmeri Spies]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Sanguine's cultists have tricked entertainers into signing contracts that force them to perform endlessly for Sanguine's parties.{{ref|name=GN|{{Cite Book|Galmon's Note|ns_base=Online}}}} Some desperate mortals who have been forced to stay have committed suicide to escape.{{ref|name=GN}} Mortals with businesses or families on Nirn may be tempted to abandon their families and responsibilities to stay forever.{{ref|name=ESOVivrun Radus|[[ON:Vivrun Radus|Vivrun Radus]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

One of his possessions includes a red hat.{{Ref|name=FNH|[[ON:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s dialogue}} There exists wine in Sanguine's private stock called 'Sanguine's Reserve' that allows one to control the dead. When poured over a corpse, the spirit of the mortal that the corpse belongs to can be commanded.{{ref|name=ESOSings-with-Drink}} Scarlet bottles of a rare and expensive 'Sanguine's Rose' perfume are reputed to have aphrodisiacal properties.{{Ref|{{Item Link|Bottle of Rare "Sanguine's Rose" Perfume|id=79581|quality=3}} item [[Online:Contraband/Grooming Items|description]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}</noinclude><includeonly>

==Religion and Culture==
===Breton===
[[File:ON-crown store-Saturalia Ice Steed.jpg||thumb|left|Saturalia Ice Steed associated with Sanguine (ESO)]]
The [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] holiday of [[Lore:Saturalia|Saturalia]] has a story where during a winter storm, a lost and abandoned mercenary prayed for rescue, and Sanguine answered it by [[Lore:Golem|creating]] a frozen steed for her, which allowed her to take vengeance against the lord that betrayed her on Saturalia morning.{{ref|name=SIS|[[ON:Saturalia Ice Steed|Saturalia Ice Steed]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Saturalia was originally a holiday for a "long forgotten god of debauchery", though it is unknown whether this refers to Sanguine or a different deity.{{ref|name=SDF|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Evening Star|Saturalia]] description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} However, elements of Saturalia have been adopted by other races and cultures for their [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]] thanks to merchants from [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] capitalizing on the holiday through consumer goods. Widespread adoption of Saturalia-themed products and practices across Tamriel for usage in the New Life Festival make for a booming market during this time of year, touching even Tamrielans that grew up with their own New Life traditions.{{ref|name=LATH|{{Cite book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays}}}} Gift giving is something shared among these two holidays.{{ref|name=CWCFP|{{Item Link|New Life Festival Box|id=96390|quality=2}} in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Blades:Saturalia Grove|Saturalia Grove Decoration]] in ''[[BL:Blades|Blades]]''}} Relevant to Sanguine is the youth of the gentry in [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] giving each other licentious presents for the New Life Festival that are intended to dare the recipients into lascivious behavior, though the giver's name is never revealed—it's up to each recipient to guess who their 'Secret Sanguine' was.{{Ref|name=NLFI|{{Cite Book|New Life Festival Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}}

===Imperial===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Pottery, Sanguine Repaired 02.jpg|thumb|left|A pottery relic of Sanguine worship in Colovia]]
{{Quote Box|''Mind aflame, loin afire, illicit pleasure to seek<br>From under the bed, Lord Sanguine doth peek<br>To inspire perversions in the wild and meek<br>Tales of taboos of which virgins blush to speak<br>The wild rose blooms, perfumes in the burbling creek<br>The petals, the stem, and the thorns' painful preek''|''[[General:Mad God's Masque and Bellicose Ball|Mad God's Masque and Bellicose Ball]]''}}
According to some sources, Sanguine has been a favorite for several Emperors since the foundation of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|first Empire]]. During the reign of [[Lore:Reman Cyrodiil|Reman Cyrodiil]], records indicate Sanguine resided in [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] where he is said to have helped Reman with the dubious draftsmanship of the Crendali Festivals. The vulgarities found within these festivals did nothing but hinder Imperial expansion efforts within the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] during Reman Cyrodiil's tenure.{{ref|name=ICoDL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Imperial Census of Daedra Lords|ns_base=General}}}} In more modern history however, Cyrodilic Daedra worship was conducted only in secret.{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}}

In [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], those seeking to speak with Sanguine usually offer a bottle of Cyrodiilic Brandy to one of his shrines.{{ref|name=Engorm}} The early [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] master vase painter [[Lore:Martina Russus|Martina Russus]] of [[Lore:Cropsford|Cropsford]] specialized in black figure pottery and created a [[Lore:Colovia|Colovian]] relic of Sanguine worship that combines their love of weald [[Lore:Wine|wines]] and Daedric worship into a pitcher depicting kneeling figures accepting a red liquid into their goblets, with the tip adorned with a red rose.{{Ref|name=PSR|[[Online:Pottery, Sanguine Repaired|Pottery, Sanguine Repaired]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Gold Road|ESO]]}} Followers of Sanguine have created objects such irregular five-sided [[Lore:Ivory|ivory]] dice, where the [[Lore:Sigil|sigils]] act as numbers, and small pots of balm made from human blood called "Sanguine Smiles", used at formal occasions.{{Ref|name=WWT|[[Online:Treasures/West Weald|Five-Sided Die & Sanguine Smile]] treasures in [[ON:Gold Road|ESO]]}}

===Khajiit===
[[File:ON-npc-Nisaazda 02.jpg|thumb|right|A vampiric Clan Mother summons sanguine blood creatures]]
Sanguine is known to the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] as "'''Sangiin'''": the '''Blood Cat''', the '''Blood God of the Second Litter''',{{ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Worldly Spirits}}}} or the '''God of Death and Secret Murder'''.{{Ref|name=VOFTK|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Khajiit}}}} According to the writings of [[Lore:Amun-dro|Amun-dro]], Sangiin is not evil by nature, but his sphere distracts Khajiit from the Path.{{ref|name=TWS}} His sphere encourages people to seek out the urges of blood and pleasure without purpose.{{ref|name=TWS}}{{ref|name=WoCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} "Sangiin's Midnight" is the term for when both moons are dark, and bright crimson red claw polish is used during this time.{{Ref|{{Item Link|Bottle of Red Claw Polish|id=63192|quality=2}} item [[Online:Contraband/Grooming Items|description]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} His worship is hidden from the Cat's Eye, [[Lore:Magrus|Magrus]].{{Ref|name=VOFTK}} He is prayed to in secrecy in forbidden shrines.{{Ref|name=ESOEGTE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}} Sangiin grants those born under the sign of [[Lore:The Serpent|The Serpent]] the gift of swift mending.{{Ref|name=Hanubina-ko|[[ON:Hanubina-ko|Hanubina-ko]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Wrist sheaths crafted from black leather have prayers to Sangiin written with metallic ink on the inside.{{ref|name=ContShawl|[[Online:Contraband W#Wrist Prayer Sheath of Sangiin|Wrist Prayer Sheath of Sangiin]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The trickster god [[Lore:Rajhin|Rajhin]] is said to have had a drinking contest with Sanguine.{{ref|{{Item Link|Rajhin's Final Cups|id=61459|quality=4}} item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} It is alleged that Sangiin was the one who gave Khajiit the secret of wine by spilling the blood of the grapes.{{ref|name=ONSereyne|[[Online:Sereyne|Sereyne]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

He is said to tempt Khajiit by [[Lore:Vampire|making their flesh immortal]], likely implying he grants a form of vampirism to his followers.{{ref|name=TWS}} Though darker, they are still drawn by his promise of filling their cups and tasting life as Sangiin desires.{{ref|name=EKP|{{Cite Book|Enough Kitten Play!|ns_base=ON}}}} The vampires of the [[Lore:Hollowfang Clan|Hollowfang Clan]] are devoted followers of Sangiin.{{ref|name=ESOHFC|The [[ON:Hollowfang Clan|Hollowfang Clan]]'s devotion to Sangiin and tendency to swear by Sangiin in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Clan members are known to bind their spines in leather and gird their loins, as Sangiin does before a tempestuous night.{{Ref|name=CM78:MF|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 78: Moongrave Fane Style}}}} Hollowfang vampires devoted to Sangiin often perform blood sacrifices. Their thralls may be driven to kill themselves{{Ref|name=sh|[[ON:Sacrificial Helot|Sacrificial Helot]]'s actions and [[ON:Dro'zakar|Dro'zakar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} to bring forth a Hemo Helot, an orb of blood used in rituals or to empower other vampires.{{Ref|name=TSS|[[ON:Dro'zakar|Dro'zakar]]'s abilities in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Hollowfang vampires use [[Lore:Blood Magic|Blood Magic]] to summon blood specters, and may do so while invoking Sangiin's name.{{Ref|name=Nisaazda|[[ON:Nisaazda|Nisaazda]]'s dialogue and abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Other===
[[File:ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png|An emblem representing Sanguine|thumb|right]]
To the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], Sanguine was known as the '''Blood-Made-Pleasure'''.{{ref|name=ONMI}} Those from the kingdoms that worshipped the more malevolent Daedric Princes were known to indulge in the pleasures of art-torture, such as flesh-sculpting and gut-gardening.{{ref|name=DWTA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Ayleids}}}} An ancient Ayleid manuscript attributed to Eldhaal the Inducer describes himself as being overseen and guided by Sanguine in these heinous arts, particularly in the creation of the "Messenger Beast Redeemed".{{ref|name=ONMI}}

The [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] have no use for Princes such as Sanguine and [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]].{{Ref|name=LA:ABayq}}

Sanguine worship has a strong presence on the island of [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]], with the Revelers of the Rose cult drawing a large following. This is partly due to the influence of [[Lore:Sunport|Sunport]], whose people enjoy celebration and are not overly judgmental. It helps that the revelers are also open to anyone who wishes to join their gatherings, though some believe their festivities can sometimes go too far.{{ref|name=VoFS|{{Cite book|Varieties of Faith: Solstice}}}}

Some of [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]]'s worshippers were once devotees of Sanguine. One follower of Dibella saw the two deities as stark opposites: Dibella teaches that love and beauty are meant to be treated with care and respect, whether toward people or objects, while Sanguine perverts these ideals into thoughtless indulgence that leaves only emptiness.{{Ref|name=ONHPC|[[Online:High Priest Caroril|High Priest Caroril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Another perspective comes from a former follower of Dibella who turned to Sanguine, believing Dibella was too restrictive. For her, beauty is freedom and the ability to revel in whatever brings joy, such as dancing however she wishes. Under Dibella, she felt constrained by needing to conform to appearances and grace, believing this caused her sense of beauty to become faded.{{Ref|name=ONHraewin|[[Online:Hraewin|Hraewin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Carnaval|Carnaval]] is a holiday associated with Sanguine, whose exact date is unknown, save for the fact that it is a party thrown by the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]].{{ref|name=PoDV|{{Cite Book|Persistence of Daedric Veneration}}}}{{ref|name=ESOCarnaval|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/67651 Celebrate Sanguine with the New Carnaval Crown Crates] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} It is a portmanteau of [[Lore:Sex|carnal]] and carnival. Indeed, people participating in Carnaval honor Sanguine with unrestrained celebration, boundless enthusiasm, and surrender their mind, body, and spirit to the rhythm of everlasting revelry.{{ref|name=ONCC|[[Online:Carnaval Celebrant|Carnaval Celebrant]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONFD|[[Online:Collectible Emotes#Carnaval Festive Dance|Carnaval Festive Dance]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamrielian]] customs for Carnaval can vary depending on region. For example, participants from the [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]] region decorate themselves in elaborate swirling and twirling body markings believed to draw Sanguine's gaze.{{ref|name=ONMarks|[[Online:Blackwood Carnaval Face Paint|Blackwood Carnaval Face]] and [[ON:Blackwood Carnaval Body Paint|Body Paint]] descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} If a reveler is not careful, there is a danger that they may not leave with their soul intact.{{ref|name=ESOCarnaval}}

[[Lore:Heart's Day|Heart's Day]] is a holiday celebrated throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] on the 16th of [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]], honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers{{ref|name=ONRL|{{Cite Book|Reigniting Love for Contemporary Audiences}}}}{{ref|name=EventAnnouncement|[[General:Celebrate Comradery & Companionship during the Heart's Week Event!‎‎|Celebrate Comradery & Companionship during the Heart's Week Event!‎‎]]}}{{ref|name=DF|Heart's Day [[Daggerfall:Holidays|description]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to Sanguine.{{ref|name=DF}}{{ref|name=LATH}} As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions.{{Ref|name=LATH}}

===Worship===
[[File:ON-render-Sanguines Rose Red Lip Tint.jpg|A Sanguine's Rose Red Lip Tint|thumb|right]]
Sanguine's worshipers drink, dance, wear red lipstick,{{Ref|name=SRRLT|[[Online:Head Markings/Crown Crate Rewards#Sanguine's Rose Red Lip Tint|Sanguine's Rose Red Lip Tint]] description}} and make love to honor him.{{ref|name=Engorm|[[OB:Engorm|Engorm]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBSanQuest}} According to his [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]], worshipers must revel in the moment as they've never done and fix on the passionate excess in their minds, then let it go to fully enjoy the next moment, and so on. Some say that with practice, one can see the very debauchery of Sanguine gleaming from their eyes in the form of a vermilion red color.{{Ref|name=EoS|[[ON:Eyes of Sanguine|Eyes of Sanguine]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some worship Sanguine because they think "normal" life is too boring, and they would simply rather be partying than going to work or being with their families.{{ref|name=ESOVivrun Radus}}{{ref|name=OBAshni|[[OB:Ashni|Ashni]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

During Sanguinary feasts, revelers may wear masks to conceal their identity. Though not usually violent cultists, they are willing to assassinate any uninvited guests who witnessed them partake in such events.{{ref|name=ONST|[[Online:Speaker Terenus|Speaker Terenus]]' dialogue about [[ON:Alard Cerone|Alard Cerone]] in [[Online:Sacrament: Sewer Tenement|ESO]]}} Certain extremist cult branches promise the blood of human sacrifices to Sanguine, and go to great lengths to chase down escapees to fulfill their oaths.{{Ref|name=TFG|{{Cite Book|The First Gleaner}}}} He inspires perversions in the wild and meek to seek illicit pleasures, such as convincing virgins to take part in taboos.{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Mad God's Masque and Bellicose Ball|ns_base=General}}}} When the first roses of the season bloom, the followers of Sanguine gather in candlelit gardens to caress each other's faces and mark them with markings described as "a rose-shaped path of pain and pleasure". {{ref|name=TOEM}}

They use sacred, floral-based inks and their markings are floral in design as a reminder of Sanguine's great gifts: both bliss and agony.{{ref|name=TOEM|[[ON:Thorns of Ecstasy Face Marks|Thorns of Ecstasy Face Marks]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TOEBM|[[ON:Thorns of Ecstasy Body Marks|Thorns of Ecstasy Body Marks]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} At his celebrations, shadowy goats called Rams of Dark Delights are fitted with the symbols of the party: sweet drinks and heady rose perfumes, and lead revelers through the streets.{{ref|[[Online:Ram of Dark Delights|Ram of Dark Delights]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
Although the term "sanguine" means a crimson or bloody red liquid,{{ref|name=SIDervenin|[[Shivering:Dervenin|Dervenin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} the definition of the word is actually based off Sanguine's name, as the names of Daedra predate the concepts they connote.{{ref|name=RGNGasta|[[Redguard:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} 

===First Era===
King [[Lore:Hale the Pious|Hale the Pious]] introduced four of the Daedric Princes to the written record almost a thousand years after the publication of the [[Lore:Alessian Doctrines|Alessian Doctrines]] in one of his tractates when he wrote that the wickedness of his political enemies was comparable to the depravity of Sanguine.{{ref|name=OO|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} Sanguine has been a favorite for several Emperors since the foundation of the first Empire. During the reign of [[Lore:Reman Cyrodiil|Reman Cyrodiil]] records indicate Sanguine resided in White-Gold Tower where he is said to have helped Reman with the dubious draftsmanship of the Crendali Festivals. The vulgarities found within these festivals did nothing but hinder Imperial expansion efforts within the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] during Reman Cyrodiil's tenure.{{ref|name=ICoDL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Imperial Census of Daedra Lords|ns_base=General}}}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-Samara Gautier.jpg|thumb|right|Sanguine, disguised as a Breton under the alias "Samara Gautier"<br>(ESO)]]
Around {{Year|2E 53}}, a growing Sanguine cult began gaining footholds in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{Ref|name=PSR}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, several [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] spies entered {{Lore Link|Sanguine's Demesne}} and drank from Sanguine's goblet, binding them to the party and rendering them unable to escape.{{ref|name=FV}} That same year, Sanguine was recognized as a public menace by the authorities in [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]], and a bounty was put out for his cultists.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sanguine Cult|ns_base=Online}}}} Stormhold's citizens knew to stay away from the nearby temple to Sanguine.{{ref|name=ESOBeek-Nei|[[ON:Beek-Nei|Beek-Nei]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} When the dragon {{Lore Link|Sahrotnax}} crashed into Moongrave Fane, the Hollowfang Clan considered it a great boon from Sangiin and performed many rituals in his name to prepare feasting upon the dragon's blood.{{ref|name=NJ|{{Cite Book|Nisaazda's Journal}}}}

Sanguine himself manifested in Tamriel for the [[Lore:Jester's Festival|Jester' Festival]], taking on the alias of a Breton named "Samuel Gourone". Seeking to liven up the celebrations for his fellow Prince [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], he had one of the festival goers compel a group of Daedra to perform as minstrels. Naturally, after the performance, the gathering almost turned into bloodshed before an act of absurd divine intervention turned the Daedra into confetti, presumably banishing them back to where they came.{{Ref|name=ONGBT|[[Online:Getting the Band Together|Getting the Band Together]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} During [[Lore:Heart's Week|Heart's Week]], Sanguine took on the form of a Breton named "Samara Gautier" and sought out festivalgoers to teach them of the old Sanguine traditions of Heart’s Day. These rituals included inducing intoxication through a frog’s kiss to embrace risk and indulgence, sanctifying revelry with a blood toast, and nudging lovers to seize the moment rather than leaving love to chance.{{ref|name=ONTM|[[Online:A Toast to Malcontents|A Toast to Malcontents]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

===Third Era===
Just before the events of the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] was sent by Sanguine to kill a monk who had slain his worshippers. Sanguine promised the Sanguine Rose as a reward, despite the fact that it had been set aside for his favored disciples. The agent accomplished the task, and was given the Rose by a worshipper of the Prince.{{ref|name=DFSanguineQuest|[[Daggerfall:Sanguine's Quest|Sanguine's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

Sometime before the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] was involved with Sanguine's cult and obtained the Sanguine Rose. An incident in the cult, presumed to have been caused by the dangers of the Rose, left many of his friends dead, and Martin thereafter dedicated himself to Akatosh. The forbidden knowledge on Daedric magic he had gained from the cult, however, was instrumental in bringing about an end to the Crisis.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, Sanguine enlisted the Hero of Kvatch to sabotage an excruciating uptight party of [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] Countess [[Lore:Alessia Caro|Alessia Caro]], where she and her guests suddenly found themselves stripped of all their possessions&mdash;clothes included, leaving a lasting impression on her otherwise formal public image.{{ref|name=OBSanQuest}}{{ref|name=PSSG|{{Cite Book|Pranks Spoils Society Gathering!|ns_base=Oblivion}}}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-npc-Sam Guevenne.jpg|thumb|right|Sanguine, disguised as a Breton under the alias "Sam Guevenne"<br>(ESO)]]
Circa {{Year|4E 176}}, the [[Lore:Sheathed Blades|Sheathed Blades]] came across a drunken beggar in [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] named Otto who claimed to be a Prince. He begged the adventurers to escort him to [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] and he would pay them later. Upon arriving to the city, the Sheathed Blades encountered a group of thugs that knew Otto and attempted to kidnap him. In exchange for the Prince, the thugs were willing to offer a reward. The stories diverge from here, but one account states that the adventurers refused to give up Otto, and due to the display of their loyalty, the beggar revealed himself to be Sanguine. The Daedric Prince stated his wants clearly, he wanted blood and fun, and chose [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] as his target. The Sheathed Blades therefore are rumored to have reveled in and ransacked the city, and Sanguine gave his blessing to his wine and blood covered participants.{{ref|[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]] Rags or Riches Part I-IV card events}}

The apprentice of [[Lore:Balagog gro-Nolob|Balagog gro-Nolob]], [[Lore:Urzhag gro-Larak|Urzhag gro-Larak]], made camp with some mercenaries near [[Lore:Kynesgrove|Kynesgrove]] circa {{Year|4E 197}}. Sanguine came into the camp and introduced himself as a mortal named "Sam" and offered his supplies in exchange for having a place to stay. While the mercenaries helped themselves to Sanguine's seemingly unending supply of alcoholic beverages, Urzhag only took some of his sweet peach treats. Sanguine's "Shaven Fruit" is a recipe Urzhag invented in an attempt to replicate the snack, though he never managed to make it taste as sweet.{{ref|name=Cookbook2|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook Vol. 2}}}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Sanguine, in the guise of a Breton named "Sam Guevenne", challenged the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] to a drinking contest. The events that followed culminated in the Last Dragonborn entering one of the Myriad Realms of Revelry and receiving the Sanguine Rose. This is one of the most recent recorded instances of Sanguine manifesting on Nirn.{{ref|name=SRSanguine|Events of [[Skyrim:A Night To Remember|A Night To Remember]] in  [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

===Rod of Revels===
[[File:ON-item-Rod of Revels.jpg|thumb|right|150px|Rod of Revels]]
The [[Lore:Rod of Revels|Rod of Revels]] is a [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] [[Lore:Artifacts|Artifact]] created by [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]]. It has the ability to turn any location into a small part of Sanguine's [[Lore:Myriad Realms of Revelry|Realms of Revelry]].{{ref|name=ESOGoat|[[Online:Goat_(The_Everlasting_Fair)|Sanguine]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|''ESO'']]}} It was used in [[Lore:Everlasting Fair|Sanguine's Fair]] on [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]] until being broken by the [[Lore:Lady of Riot|Lady of Riot]]. It was rebuilt with the help of the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] to overthrow the usurper and return the fair to Sanguine's control.{{ref|name=ESOFair|Events of [[Online:The Everlasting Fair|The Everlasting Fair]] in [[Online:Online|''ESO'']]}}

==Servants==
[[File:ON-event-Jester's Festival 2024.jpg|thumb|left|Daedra serving the will of Sanguine]]
Several varieties of Daedra are known to serve Sanguine. Among them are [[Lore:Ogrim|Ogrim]], [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], [[Lore:Dark Seducer|Dark Seducers]],{{ref|name=ESOSeducer Trilvath|[[ON:Seducer Trilvath|Seducer Trilvath]]'s role as one of Sanguine's minions in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Banekin|Banekin]] and [[Lore:Scamp|Scamps]].{{ref|name=LTFAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=ESOSD|The types of Daedra that appear in [[ON:Sanguine's Demesne|Sanguine's Demesne]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Ogrim serve as bouncers at Sanguine's parties.{{ref|name=LTFAYQ}} In celebration of the Jester's Festival, Sanguine once forcefully compelled a group of Daedra to form a band for entertainment, consisting of a Daedroth playing the lute, and a Harvester, Scamp, and Winged Twilight playing the flute, bongos, and belly drums.{{Ref|name=ONGBT}}

Sangiin's Thirst is known to manifest as bloody spectres resembling Khajiiti [[Lore:Gargoyle|gargoyle]]s.{{ref|[[ON:Sangiin's Thirst|Sangiin's Thirst]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Blood can also be animated in the form of orbs called Hemo Helots or moving [[Lore:Coagulant|coagulant]] slimes.{{Ref|name=TSS}} [[Lore:Hemonculous|Hemonculous]] are blood wraiths that can be summoned by Sangiin cultists.
