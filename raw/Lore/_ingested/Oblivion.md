[[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], sometimes known as '''Hell''',{{ref|[[OB:Ilend Vonius|Ilend Vonius]]' dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|Dialogue during the quest [[Redguard:Rescue Iszara|Rescue Iszara]] in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}} the '''Outer Realms''',{{ref|name=Molag Bal|[[Morrowind:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=BSIdeal|Player's dialogue with the [[Battlespire:Ideal Master#Dialogue|Ideal Masters]] in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} the '''Netherworld''' or the '''Nether Planes''',{{ref|name=ARHHD&FDesc|[[Arena:Hell Hound|Hell Hound]] and [[Arena:Fire Daemon|Fire Daemon]] descriptions from [[Books:Arena Player's Guide|Arena Player's Guide]]}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Mark Lampert: "It's Quiet... Almost Too Quiet"|ns_base=GEN}}}} is a realm within the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], and is mainly inhabited by the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]—[[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]] who did not contribute to the creation of the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].

There are at least sixteen major [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|Planes of Oblivion]], each ruled over by one of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]],{{ref|name=OBloading|[[Oblivion:Loading Screens|Loading screens]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} and over 37,000 other documented planes, including chaos realms and pocket realities,{{ref|name=DDCFA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Dossier: Cold-Flame Atronach}}}} although there exists far more than that, with accounts from the Daedra describing the minor realms in Oblivion as overall being infinite in both number and complexity.{{ref|name=Lyranth2|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}{{ref|name=Jackdaw|[[ON:Jackdaw Daedrat|Jackdaw Daedrat]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Oblivion realms are extensions of their masters.{{Ref|name=EGRN|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ns_base=General}}}} Most realms are maintained by greater Daedra or the collective will of many lesser Daedra, although some are ruled by mortals who have ascended to immortality—like the [[Lore:Ideal Masters|Ideal Masters]].{{ref|name=LMA FNH|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} The realms of the Daedric Princes are extensions of their Prince{{Ref|name=EGRN|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ns_base=General}}}}{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} and can be very extensive, with some even being described as infinite in size and shape. {{ref|name=OtA|{{Cite Book|On Orcs and the Afterlife}}}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{ref|name=Panopticon|{{Cite Book|Working in the Infinite Panopticon}}}}{{Ref|name=TESONRWRL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Talking Elder Scrolls Online's Necrom Reveal With Rich Lambert|ns_base=General}}}} Indeed, according to [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], were a Prince to simultaneously invade the realms of the other Princes to seize the power within through the use of something like the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], they'd gain infinite power through the infinite connections made.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} It has been said that, just like Apocrypha, all proper Oblvion realms are infinite in size. {{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TESONRWRL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Talking Elder Scrolls Online's Necrom Reveal With Rich Lambert|ns_base=General}}}}

Oblivion is described as a different universe or a different dimension in relation to Mundus.{{ref|name=Atronite|[[Blades:Atronite|Atronite]]'s description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|name=OnOb|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} The [[Lore:Imperial Geographical Society|Imperial Geographical Society]] described it as a black and empty sea with countless isles of wonder and cosmic whimsy, which begins where Mundus ends and surrounds it on every side. Thus, simply staring into the night sky is enough to visit Oblivion.{{Ref|name=PGE3AS|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Arena Supermundus}}}} The Oblivion scholar Telbaril Oran claimed that what the mortal senses perceive while in an Oblivion realm is crafted from illusion and metaphor, the result of their mind attempting to make sense of the surrounding chaos. Because of this phenomenon, things like the "grass" one might perceive while in an Oblivion realm were described by him as actually just being the memory of that thing as interpreted by the mortal mind.{{ref|name=Telbaril|[[ON:Telbaril Oran|Telbaril Oran]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The mechanisms which allow for [[Lore:Magic|magic]] to function in Oblivion are a matter of debate. One theory, that of "Mundial Emanation", maintains that when the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] broke through the firmament of Mundus, they unleashed a wave of creative energy which also washed over Oblivion and infused it with magic. Another theory maintains that Oblivion has its own "manifest dynamism", a form of omnipresent background energy which gives Oblivion its basic shape and laws, and can be tapped into to use magic when in those realms.{{ref|name=Lucents|{{Cite Book|Lessons on Lucents}}}} Whatever the case, Daedra have referred to magicka as a force they tap into to exert their power.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Mora|[[ON:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Causality within Oblivion is not always concurrent with Mundus and [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], thus making it possible for phenomena such as entering an Oblivion realm at one point in time and emerging in the past relative to when one entered to occur. The downside with this tactic is the unpredictability and unreliability of when you might end up.{{ref|name=DarienESO|[[Online:Darien Gautier|Darien Gautier]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|[[Online:Sibellec the Old|Sibellec the Old]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Wyrd and Coven|Wyrd and Coven]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]] was one Oblivion realm interaction with which was theorized to possibly lead to consequences in different frames of time.{{ref|name=ESOValsirenn|[[Online:Valsirenn|Valsirenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

[[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|Chaotic Creatia]] is the unformed chaos native to the planes of Oblivion, out of which the realms of Oblivion are formed.{{ref|name=Fox|[[Online:The Fox|The Fox]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LSP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Lawrence Schick's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=LMA FNH}} It is a shapeless but energetic [[Lore:Padomay|Padomaic]] material that accretes around a Daedric [[Lore:Vestige (soul)|vestige]] until it conforms to the morphotype's inherent pattern. It is therefore key to the process by which a banished Daedra forms a new body. Chaotic creatia takes a different but planar-appropriate form in every realm of Oblivion,{{ref|name=CCTAP|{{Cite Book|Chaotic Creatia: The Azure Plasm}}}} and is commonly referred to as the "waters of Oblivion."{{ref|name=Dylora|[[Shivering:Dylora|Dylora]]'s dialogue in [[Shivering:Shivering|Shivering Isles]]}}{{ref|name=Relmyna Verenim|[[Shivering:Relmyna Verenim|Relmyna Verenim]]'s dialogue in [[Shivering:Shivering|Shivering Isles]]}}{{ref|name=Staada|[[Shivering:Staada|Staada]]'s dialogue in [[Shivering:Shivering|Shivering Isles]]}}{{ref|name=Oblivion|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=SI|Saints and Seducers}}}} According to the [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]] Madam Whim, a renowned information broker operating out of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]], the original formation of the Vestiges of lesser Daedra is generally the work of a progenitor [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]], who forms them out of the Creatia of their realm and sets their nature and path at the time of their making.{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} Indeed, there have been cases of lesser Daedra that were created by their progenitor Prince to accomplish a specific purpose or task, such as the scions of [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], who were created by their Prince to help avert her foreseen imprisonment after a conflict with the other Daedric Princes.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}" New realms formed within Oblivion are said to be crafted by their creators out of Chaotic Creatia.{{ref|name=LMA FNH}} The Daedric Prince [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] is in charge of keeping [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|daedrons]] - chaotic creatia imbued with sufficient purpose and function from the exertion of Daedric or mortal will - from causing damage in Oblivion. Such will-imbued creatia can form "potentia vortices" which, if unattended, can form destructive "realm-rips", phenomena which Peryite prevents from forming across Oblivion.{{ref|name=LFNHTRPAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}} Some realms such as the [[Lore:Village of the Lost|Village of the Lost]], a border realm of Coldharbour created during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], have been observed floating upon a sea of creatia while they formed.{{ref|Appearance of [[ON:Village of the Lost|Village of the Lost]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Each Oblivion plane is different in appearance, depending upon the nature of its ruler. They can range from beautiful to desolate; some are even too strange for mortal comprehension.{{ref|name=LMA FNH}}<noinclude> According to the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth]], they share little in common and are "the very definition of change and variation, manifesting all possibilities, and validating all understanding and misunderstanding".{{Ref|name=LA:LtF|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}} Indeed, it is said that there exist planes that "flex against the will to impose order" and "twist into hyperogonal paradoxes that resist even the most sophisticated ur-logic", or simply "wink in and out of existence too quickly" for even Daedra to "find purchase upon their shores". Owing to this, the Dremora Lyranth considered it possible for even things such as a dimension of talking daggers to exist within, and claimed that her own clan the [[Lore:Foolkillers Clan|Foolkillers Clan]] once made war upon a minor realm filled entirely with laughing mirrors.{{ref|name=Lyranth2}} The rulers associated with the different realms of Oblivion are implied to be able to sense what goes on in their respective planes, and the presence of visitors to their realms.{{ref|name=ESOSoul|[[Online:Soul Shriven in Coldharbour|Events]] of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DivaythFyr|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:The Light of Knowledge|The Light of Knowledge]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Some Oblivion realms are said to have some sort of relationship with other planar regions. The [[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]], realm of [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] is said to exist adjacent to all other realms of reality simultaneously, such that it can almost be glimpsed when one turns and looks quickly while in the dark.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] for [[Online:Evergloam (Summerset)|Evergloam]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} While regarding the [[Lore:Ashpit|Ashpit]], realm of [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], it is said that, as the ultimate expression of the Orc stronghold, the Ashpit bastion stretches endlessly across the planes, extending even behind the [[Lore:Stars|stars]] to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], granting access to every worthy Orc who crosses from this life into the next.{{ref|name=OtA|{{Cite Book|On Orcs and the Afterlife}}}} The realm of Fargrave is described as a form of otherworldly conveyance, that embodies the existential concepts of transit or passage, and sits on the threshold to everywhere. Fargrave is said to serve as a natural waypoint between the planes of Oblivion, where the connections between them become easier to navigate, and to contain a multitude of [[Lore:Magical Transportation|portals]] to different realms, with the entire realm being said to be littered with dimensional gateways. Thus it is said that there is no better launching point to explore the myriad corridors of Oblivion than Fargrave, with the realm even providing the means to reach the mortal world while bypassing some of its protections such as the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]].{{ref|name=Lyranth|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOMadamWhim|[[ON:Madam Whim|Madam Whim]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Some sources claim that [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and Oblivion are one, a fact which can be proven through mathematical equations.{{ref|name=Suitors|{{Cite Book|The Four Suitors of Benitah}}}}

==Relationship with Mundus==
There are a number of locations where Oblivion intersects with Nirn. Oblivion realms connected to the mortal world through stable gateways include [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]], the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]], [[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]], and the [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]].{{ref|[[Skyrim:Karliah|Karliah]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Darkness Returns|Darkness Returns]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Doors|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=Journal|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei}}}}{{ref|name=TheCause|Events of ''[[Skyrim:The Cause|The Cause]]''}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}}{{Ref|name=SIHaskill|[[Shivering:Haskill|Haskill]]'s dialogue in the [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]}}{{ref|name=JULIOMI|group=UOL|[[General:Josh Utter-Leyton Isle of Madness Interview|Josh Utter-Leyton Isle of Madness Interview]]}}{{ref|name=HG|[[ON:Vateshran Hollows#Hunter's Grotto|Hunter's Grotto]] in {{g|ESO|markarth}}}}

Oblivion is separated from Nirn and Mundus by the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|Liminal Barriers]], which are said to be the creation of Akatosh through the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Sublime Brazier]], a sacred cauldron described as Akatosh's first light, which stretches to the very heart of Nirn.{{ref|name=LtD|Events of [[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=DoB|Dialogue from the [[Online:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}}{{ref|name=Brazier|{{Cite Book|The Sublime Brazier}}}}{{ref|name=Martin|[[Oblivion:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Some sources also speak of [[Lore:Aurora|the aurora]] or "Kyne's lights", and the Lunar Lattice, also serving as boundaries that protect Nirn from harmful influence from beyond.{{Ref|name=Celestial|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 41: Celestial Style}}}}{{Ref|name=ESOAzin|[[Online:Moon-Bishop Azin-jo|Moon-Bishop Azin-jo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} As of the end of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the sacrifice of [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] has "sealed the gates of Oblivion forever". The new barrier is noted to be too powerful for a [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]] to breach for more than fleeting moments, even though "a thousand [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gems]] would likely dissolve" in the presence of such a Stone.{{ref|name=Martin}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal}}{{ref|name=Excavation|[[Skyrim:Excavation Leader's Journal|Excavation Leader's Journal]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Some believe that Nirn is a reflection of the Daedric realms, or that the Daedric realms are an echo of Nirn. According to the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], many realms of Oblivion have a relationship with the mortal world. Indeed, it is said that Mora's realm of [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] and Nirn share a powerful connection. The fates of the two worlds are said to be inextricably linked and, because of this and its role in regulating [[Lore:Fate|fate]], it is said that, were Apocrypha to be destroyed, the resulting ripples of fate would also unravel the mortal world, and the ensuing destruction would ripple throughout reality.{{ref|name=Mora|[[ON:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} There are places in Oblivion, as there are on Nirn, which serve as nexus points in reality, locations which naturally resonate throughout the realms, thus allowing for arcane phenomena that occur there to resonate across Aurbis.{{ref|name=Scruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Oblivion is said to be less malleable than Mundus in some respects. According to the Dremora Lyranth, whereas many Oblivion realms flex against the will to impose order, Mundus submits simultaneously to the predictable influence of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|et'Ada's remains]] and the stubborn caprice of the [[Lore:Lorkhan|Liar]]. Even if some part of Mundus is destroyed, another instance of the same thing will soon arise.{{ref|name=Lyranth2}}
==Realms of the Daedric Princes==
The 17 main Daedric planes, along with the Daedric Princes they are associated with, are:
*'''[[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]]''', [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Ashpit|Ashpit]]''', [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Attribution's Share|Attribution's Share]]''', [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]]''', [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Colored Rooms|Colored Rooms]]''', [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]]''', [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]]''', [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Fields of Regret|Fields of Regret]]''', [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]]''', [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Mirrormoor|Mirrormoor]]''', [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]]''', [[Lore:Azura|Azura]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Myriad Realms of Revelry|Myriad Realms of Revelry]]''', [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]]'s realms
*'''[[Lore:The Pits|The Pits]]''', [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Quagmire|Quagmire]]''', [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]]'s realm
*The '''[[Lore:Scuttling Void|Scuttling Void]]''', [[Lore:Namira|Namira]]'s realm
*'''[[Lore:Shivering Isles|The Shivering Isles]]''', [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s realm (formerly [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]]'s [[Lore:Jyggalag's Realm|realm]])
*The '''[[Lore:Spiral Skein|Spiral Skein]]''', [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]]'s realm

These realms are noted to be fundamentally shaped and curated by the will of their respective Princes, effectively being their extensions.{{ref|name=Lyranth2}}{{Ref|name=EGRN|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=Whim}}
