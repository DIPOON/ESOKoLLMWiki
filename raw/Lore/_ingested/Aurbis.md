# Lore:Aurbis

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Places Trail}}[[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[File:LG-quest-Prologue 01.jpg|thumb|right|300px|A cosmic mass within or encompassing the Aurbis]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], also known as the '''Gray Maybe''' (which means '''[[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]''' in the [[Lore:Ehlnofex|Ehlnofex]]),{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} and at times personified as the Feminine Principle of the Cosmos [[Lore:Nir|'''Nir''']],{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} is the name given to the chaos, or totality, from which the cosmos was formed by [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]]. For all intents and purposes, it is the universe, encompassing the [[Lore:Void|Void]] and all [[Lore:Planes of Existence|Planes of Existence]] including [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], and the planes of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} It is said that there also exist planes beyond Aetherius, and harder to travel to than it, where even powerful [[Lore:Magic|magic]] such as that of the [[Lore:Sea Sload|Sea Sload]] can't reach, and the location of which is unknown to even the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]].{{ref|name=ONMarieve|[[Online:Oracle Marieve|Oracle Marieve]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The plane to which the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] transport their island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] when it is not on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] is said to be one such realm.{{ref|name=ONLil|[[Online:Lilatha|Lilatha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOIachesis|[[Online:Ritemaster Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOValsirenn|[[Online:Valsirenn|Valsirenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

Many sources describe the structure of the Aurbis as a wheel: Mundus is the hub, the empty spaces within it are the planes of Oblivion, the rim encompasses the realms of Aetherius, and the emptiness beyond it is the Void. The eight spokes connecting the rim to the hub represent the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] that played a major part in [[Lore:Dawn Era|Creation]].{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|The 36 Lessons of Vivec}}}}{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}{{ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}}{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}} The Eight Divines are also depicted as planets, orbiting [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and its moons at the center of the Aurbis.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Orrery|Orrery]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The sun, [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], was created when the god Magnus fled Mundus to Aetherius, and the stars were formed by the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] who followed.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}} According to [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], [[Lore:Anu|Stasis]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Change]], Anu and Padomay, are infinite forces and realms residing in the infinite Void, the latter infinity paradoxically enclosing the others in a manner akin to an encircling sphere. At the intersection of the two forces, where they touch, lies a "perfect circle of pattern and possibility", the Wheel, and inside that Wheel lies the Aurbis which is its foundation. Vivec claims that outside the Wheel exists the Void, which cannot truly be named and is bereft of anything. The Void is said to have more aspects than just Stasis and Change, but they cannot be named as they are outside of true language.{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}} Some Altmeri sources claim that, rather than being a single wheel, the Aurbis is instead more akin to "a telescope that stretches all the way back to the eye of Anui-El, with Padomaics innumerable along its infinite walls".{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Magnus is said to be at times, represented by a telescope.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} The [[Lore:Eye of Magnus|Eye of Magnus]] was, among other ideas, theorized by the [[Lore:College of Winterhold|College of Winterhold]] to contain the entirety of the Aurbis. Though this theory was considered dubious at best, close observations on the Eye yielded no evidence to the contrary.{{ref|name=SRPhinis|[[SR:Phinis Gestor|Phinis Gestor]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

The [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian]], perhaps [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati]],{{ref|name=VftDB|{{Cite Book|Vindication for the Dragon Break}}}} Arch-Prelate [[Lore:Fervidius Tharn|Fervidius Tharn]] describes the Aurbis as comprising "sheaths" within an overarching "integument". Through the intervention of the Magna Ge [[Lore:Mnemo-Li|Mnemoli]] and a particular manipulation, called the "Hurling Disk", of the identity between the circle of the Aurbis and [[Lore:The Towers#The Metaphysical Tower|the Tower]], the Arch-Prelate apparently believed it would be possible to detach a sheath from the Aurbis' integument and "twist" it to provoke the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] of the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]].{{ref|name=OtDotS|{{Cite Book|On the Detachment of the Sheath}}}} An [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] source similarly mentions the existence of Aurbic "skeins" that can be "disjoined" by powerful mages.{{ref|name=CM9|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 9: Argonian Style}}}}

The planes of Aurbis are made up of different [[Lore:Layers of Reality|reality layers]], spatially overlapping sections which can be accessed via the use of [[Lore:Magic|magic]] which shifts beings and objects between them, things shifted to a different layer generally can't be perceived or interacted with by those who lack the necessary arcane means.{{ref|name=LeramilHidden|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Hidden Fate|A Hidden Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Meln|[[ON:Meln the Mouthless|Meln the Mouthless]]' dialogue during [[Online:A Hidden Fate|A Hidden Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Hidden|[[ON:A Hidden Fate| A Hidden Fate]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}.{{ref|name=Blightcrown|[[ON:Blightcrown|Blightcrown]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

According to the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]], [[Lore:Souls|mortals]], by their very existence and lives, help preserve and maintain Aurbis, every child born, war fought, or field plowed by mortals, helps stabilize reality itself. It is said that mortals were designed as servants to perform tasks that maintain reality and so, the limitations that constrain them, such as the inability to comprehend and traverse the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]], were placed upon them intentionally. This truth, Ithelia claims, is generally kept from mortals, to minimize the risk of them disrupting the plans of those who benefit from this secret. Despite this, there are certain [[Lore:Hero|individuals]] who have been shown to not be bound by at least some of the usual mortal limitations, being able to comprehend and traverse the Many Paths without issue.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Aurbis is said to be a perfect circle of Possibility and Pattern.{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{ref|name=FrenchTM|[[General:The Monomyth (French translation)|French translation]] of ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}} These possibilities scatter across the Aurbis, each defined by distinct choices and outcomes that lead to different alternate realities. This web is called the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]], which have been likened to both a spiderweb that formed within a diamond, interweaving lines within a grander fixed shape, and a halo of possibilities and alternate [[Lore:Fate|fates]] that surround reality. It is said that within the Many Paths all things are possible, things which might not be real in one reality might be real within the Paths, it is even possible to encounter realities where concepts such as magic or Daedra might not exist, or realities that are a dark reflection of the world, empty and timeless and filled with darkness, the world one sees when looking into a mirror. Each reality along the Many Paths contains its own version of each entity, be they mortal or Daedra, even deities such as the Daedric Princes have their own alternate versions within the realities. These alternate versions are said to be "reflections" of one another, different in some ways, like images in a cracked mirror, but originating from the same seed{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Beragon|[[ON:Beragon|Beragon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
*The adjective '''Aurbic''' is sometimes used to describe a type of magical force associated with the fundamental structure of the Aurbis.{{ref|name=ESODoomcrag|[[ON:The Doomcrag|The Doomcrag]] loading screen in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} A related term is '''Aurbisal''', implying "of the Aurbis".{{ref|[[Online:Azandar|Azandar]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
*[[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Fen]] converted his Oblivion realm, [[Lore:Maelstrom|Maelstrom]], into a gauntlet of arenas. After [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] conquered the arena, the Barons Who Move Like This were released. Fa-Nuit-Hen used the freed barons as officers in his Maelstrom Army. He planned to conquer the Aurbis with this army, enthralling the champions of Maelstrom Arena and forming a force of the greatest warriors in existence.{{ref|name=FaNuit|[[ON:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}
*The term '''multiverse''' has also been used to refer to the various realms that exist within the Aurbis. The concept of the Many Paths extends beyond that structure.{{ref|name=LunaBeReal|[[Online:Luna Beriel|Luna Beriel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DaddySil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:A Necessary Alliance|ESO: ''Summerset'']]}}{{ref|name=ILESO|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|ns_base=ON}}}}
