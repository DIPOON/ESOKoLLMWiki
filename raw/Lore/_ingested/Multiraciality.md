Many different mortal and immortal [[Lore:Races|races]] inhabit the realms of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]]. The mortals of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] can typically be categorized into one of these racial groups. However, '''multiracial''' people do exist and can exhibit the traits of two or more races. They are sometimes described as '''hybrids''',{{Ref|name=CotDC|{{Cite Book|Crimes of the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}  or derogatorily as '''half-breeds'''{{ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{ref|name=OrcsAmogus|{{Cite Book|Orcs: The Vermin Among Us}}}}{{ref|name=LotB|{{Cite Book|Legacy of the Bretons}}}} and '''mongrels'''.{{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}{{ref|name=TRBIX|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, Part IX}}}}

One view on [[Lore:Sex#Racial Compatibility and Reproduction|multiracial reproduction]] is set out in ''[[Lore:Notes on Racial Phylogeny|Notes on Racial Phylogeny]]'', a text published by the [[Lore:Imperial University|Imperial University]]'s Council of Healers. Generally, the offspring of such a union will bear the racial traits of the mother, though some traces of the father's race may also be present.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Notes on Racial Phylogeny}}}} Some take an absolutist stance on this view, stating that all children inherit their mother's race.{{ref|name=GenericMW|[[MW:Generic Dialogue H#Humanoid Races|Generic dialogue]] regarding '''Humanoid Races''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Others have stated that if a pair of different human races have children, the offspring will bear features of both races, instead of only the maternal side.{{ref|group=UOL|name=TuttlePosts2021|{{Cite Book|Leamon Tuttle's Posts|ns_base=gen}}}} Children born to the same parents may be of different races.{{ref|name=Galiel|Appearance of [[Castles:Galiel Beleth|Galiel Beleth]] in [[Castles:Castles|''Castles'']]}}{{ref|name=Fadiya|Appearance of [[Castles:Fadiya|Fadiya]] in [[Castles:Castles|''Castles'']]}} For more information on racial compatibility, see the article on '''[[Lore:Sex|sex]]'''.

==Interracial Couples in Society==
[[File:CT-Events-Baby Boom (Large).png|An interracial couple with their child (Castles)|thumb|right]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Many of these milk-drinkers consider it heresy for a Dark Elf to marry one of another race. As if the Three care who we love and live for!''|Hrogar, a Nord married to a Dunmer{{ref|name=Hrogar}}}}
Interracial relationships are somewhat common, but they may be looked upon poorly in some cultures. Some [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] consider it heresy to marry outside of their race.{{ref|name=Hrogar|[[Online:Hrogar|Hrogar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Protecting the Hall|Protecting the Hall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] consider interracial relationships to be the worst possible offense given their stance on racial purity, and have gone to the extreme to punish those involved in such relationships. For example, an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] named Gurlak was in a relationship with his owner and slave master Nuralanya. When the relationship was found out, Gurlak was bound to a magical circle which extended his [[Lore:Lifespan|lifespan]]. He outlived his lover who was also imprisoned nearby and went on to live for thousands of years in this state of imprisonment.{{ref|[[Online:Gurlak|Gurlak]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Oldest Orc|The Oldest Orc]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] were responsible for a series of murders against refugees of mixed blood in [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] and [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]] during the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|name=LoS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|41}} "Half-elf" has been used as an [[Lore:Profanity|insult]] within Altmeri culture against Bosmer, aspersing their height.{{ref|name=Firthaedal|[[ESO:Firthaedal|Firthaedal]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], Human-[[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] marriages are generally frowned upon.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Rumors#Anvil|Anvil rumors]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Bosmer have been recorded in early history as "soiling Time's line" by taking Mannish wives.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}

==Human × Elf==
===Manmer (Aldmer-Nede)===
The most famous example of multiraciality are the Bretons of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]. Historically known as [[Lore:Manmer|Manmer]], the Nedic population of High Rock was forced to interbreed with the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] ([[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] in modern times, but Aldmer back then){{Ref|name=MDBMM|{{Cite Book|Modern Day Bretons: Man or Mer?}}}} of [[Lore:Direnni|Clan Direnni]]. ==Hybrid Races==
The following races have been described as "half-breed" or "hybrid" races, either literally or metaphorically:
*[[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]], being an all-female race, are said to reproduce by kidnapping human males. They are also said to be "deranged half-breed" creatures, but it is unclear how literal this is due to their cursed origins.{{ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{ref|name=Ghraewaj|{{Cite Book|Ghraewaj}}}}
*[[Lore:Minotaur|Minotaur]]s are a bull-like beastfolk, and it is said the [[Lore:Demigod|demigod]] [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]] gave rise to the race through his dalliances with the Nedic Slave-Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] and the birth of her son, [[Lore:Belharza|Belharza the Man-Bull]]. This origin would paint them as a cross between Nedes and a demigod in [[Lore:Bull|bull]] form.{{ref|name=OnMino|{{Cite Book|On Minotaurs}}}}
*[[Lore:Proto-Breton|Proto-Breton]]s or "Half-Elves" were the first Bretons from the early First Era and were more elven in appearance than their modern counterparts.{{Ref|name=PGE1HR}}{{ref|name=TBMOP}}
*[[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] have been theorized to be the result of interbreeding between [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] and the indigenous bestial tribes of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]].{{ref|name=ACoV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} The tall, bestial and hunchbacked [[Lore:Maormer Leviathans|Maormer Leviathans]] are described as "the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous coupling of Sea Elf and [[Lore:Sea Serpent|Sea Serpent]]".{{ref|name=TSE|{{Cite Book|The Scaled Elves}}}}
*[[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] were "bred" by [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] to be his personal guard, made as a hybrid race from both [[Lore:Xivilai|Xivilai]] and [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]].{{ref|name=OtX|{{Cite Book|On the Xivkyn}}}} However, their creation was the result of a [[Lore:Vestige (soul)|vestigial]] hybridization process; Xivilai and Dremora are immortal Daedric beings, and do not reproduce.{{ref|{{Cite book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}}

==Notes==
*''[[Castles:Castles|The Elder Scrolls: Castles]]'' does not restrict the creation of babies based on race or gender. Due to game limitations however, offspring can only belong to the race of one parent and will not bear any mixed traits.
* In ''The Real Barenziah Part IX'', [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]] is described by [[Lore:Symmachus|Symmachus]] as a "mongrel elf", allegedly being part Wood Elf, part Dark Elf, and part "only the gods know what", with dubious human ancestry. Jagar himself apparently claimed to be born in Valenwood to a Wood Elf mother. Symmachus was supposedly prejudiced against other elves (with Altmer and Bosmer being singled out in the biography as folk Symmachus abhorred for being "effete intellectuals" and "lazy thieves" respectively), instead admiring humans (particularly Bretons) for their intelligence and pragmatism. Tharn's alleged lack of human ancestry according to Symmachus was noted by the author as a perceived flaw rather than indicative of the truth.{{ref|name=TRBIX|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, Part IX}}}}
