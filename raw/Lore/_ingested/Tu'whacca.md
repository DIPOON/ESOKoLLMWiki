</noinclude>[[Lore:Tu'whacca|Tu'whacca]] (or simply '''Tuwhacca'''),{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} the '''Tricky God''', is the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n '''God of Souls''' (or '''Shepherd of Souls'''), and the '''Caretaker of the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]]'''.{{ref|name=VOFTF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}}{{ref|name=TP|{{Cite Book|Tu'whacca's Prayer}}}}{{ref|name=RftD|{{Cite Book|Reverence for the Dead}}}} Before the creation of the world, he was the god of <!--Don't change this, this is correct as is.-->'''Nobody Really Cares'''.{{ref|name=VOFTF}}<!--Don't change this, this is correct as is.--> When [[Lore:Ruptga|Tall Papa]] undertook the creation of the Walkabout, Tu'whacca found a purpose: he became the caretaker of the Far Shores, and continues to help [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s find their way into the afterlife. Though a soul may have enemies who will try to keep it from reaching the Far Shores, Tu'Whacca is sly and clever, and always outwits them.  His cult is sometimes associated with [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] in the more cosmopolitan, [[Lore:Forebears|Forebear]] regions of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], where the names Tu'whacca and Arkay are basically interchangeable.{{ref|name=VOFTF}} [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] detest this [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]]ization, considering it a betrayal of their heritage.{{ref|name=ABOOH|{{Cite Book|A Betrayal of Our Heritage}}}} Nevertheless, scholars cannot help but note the clear similarities between them, as well as the similarities they both share with [[Lore:Xarxes (god)|Xarxes]] of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], in both the etymology of their names and the divine roles they fill.{{ref|name=TAX|{{Cite Book|Tu'whacca, Arkay, Xarxes}}}}<noinclude>

==Culture and Worship==
Being an integral part of the Redguard belief system, they have developed many customs related to him. A sneeze may prompt a Redguard to say something like "Tu'whacca bless you", as ancient writings suggest a sneeze is a sign of the presence of an evil spirit.{{ref|name=TOTBP|{{Cite Book|Threat of the Baandari Pedlars}}}} The dead or dying are often referred to with the adage "may Tu'whacca have mercy on [their] soul" or "may Tu'whacca escort [their] soul to the Far Shores".{{ref|name=LJJ|{{Cite Book|Lord Jaren's Journal|ns_base=Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=SAMITA|{{Cite Book|Sacrilege and Mayhem in the Alik'r}}}} When mummifying a body, the first step Redguard priests perform is to consecrate the body with the Blessings of Tu'whacca.{{ref|name=TESOM|{{Cite Book|The Eight Steps of Mummification}}}} 

Tu'whacca was once considered the patron of mages, and some adorned their [[Lore:Staves|staves]] with his sacred [[Lore:Ibis|ibis]].{{Ref|name=CM28RG|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} 

Elaborate, oversized ibis-head masks with emerald eyes are worn by priests of Tu'whacca in the {{Lore Link|March of Wisdom}}.{{ref|name=ContrabandOTM|[[Online:Contraband O#Oversized Tu'whacca Mask|Oversized Tu'whacca Mask]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The black [[Lore:Cat#Sphynx|sphynx cats]] of the Alik'r, dubbed "Tu'whacca's Sphynx Cats", are said to bring the blessing of Tu'whacca to their household. For this reason, they are honored. They have also been observed sleeping with those who near death, giving them a reputation as guides to the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]].{{ref|[[Online:Tu'whacca's Sphynx Cat|Tu'whacca's Sphynx Cat]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
