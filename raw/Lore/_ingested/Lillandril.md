[[Lore:Lillandril|Lillandril]] is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Summurset Isle towns.png|Map of Summurset Isle – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} situated on the western hills of the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]]. The city is known for scholarly pursuits, various institutes such as the [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]] and the [[Lore:Illumination Academy|Illumination Academy]] were both found in vicinity of the coastal-town.{{Ref|name=OBIAG|{{Cite Book|Our Blessed Isles: A Guide}}}} The city's symbol is the [[Lore:Flask of Lillandril|Flask of Lillandril]], an enchanted bottle that absorbs the magic used against the bearer. Despite its infamy, it has been embraced by the people as a cultural symbol.{{Ref|name=ESOBanner|[[Online:Lillandril Banner, Hanging|Lillandril Banner, Hanging]] furnishing in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-place-College of Sapiarchs.jpg|thumb|right|The College of Sapiarchs]]
Lillandril is built atop the hills on the northwest coast, where the land gives way to the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|ocean]] to form the lowland and sunken basin. The roads are flooded by water but gradually rise above it as it gets closer to the settlement. Even though it is a port town, it is intricately built on the hillside, slowly rising around it until it reaches the upper platform and the [[Lore:Kinhouse|Kinhouse]], well above sea level and with a grand view of the [[Lore:Sea of Pearls|surrounding waters]]. The open-aired main street, which coils around the mountain leads to the upper platform, and everything in between is crowded alleys and stairways. At the height of Lillandril lies the portal master, who guards an [[Lore:Magical Transportation|egress]] that transports people to the [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]].{{Ref|name=ONLillandril|[[Online:Lillandril|Lillandril]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} It is an academic compound built atop of an island off the coast of Lillandril, where many kings and queens of Summerset go to study on their way to their throne.{{Ref|name=OTCOS|{{Cite Book|On the College of Sapiarchs}}}} Lillandril is neighbored by several settlements, such as [[Lore:Karnwasten Moor|Karnwasten Moor]] to the southwest and Marbruk Brook to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

==History==
[[File:RG-item-Flask of Lillandril.png|thumb|right|150px|The Flask of Lillandril]]
For countless centuries, Lillandril was known as the center of higher learning,{{Ref|name=OBIAG}} with the Illumination Academy and the College of Sapiarchs serving these purposes. Throughout the [[Lore:First Era|First]] and [[Lore:Second Era|Second Eras]], the cities of Summerset often engaged in conflict with each other, eventually blossoming into full-scale warfare. Lillandril would often battle [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] from the south.{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}} 

Sometime around {{Year|1E 470}}, a group of [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] discovered a magical flask with the ability to absorb magic from wherever. With its potential power, the founders used it to perform various tasks for barons across the province and to keep the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] in check. They later disappeared and crash-landed in a coral reef on what is now [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]].{{Ref|name=EA|{{Cite Book|Elven Artifacts vIII}}}} When [[Lore:Mount Firesong|Mount Firesong]] erupted in [[Lore:The Systres|the Systres]] archipelago in {{Year|1E 2484}}, people from Lillandril witnessed smoke from a distance and smelled brimstone.{{Ref|name=SHV5|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 5}}}} The Alinor-Lillandril skirmishes, one of their many conflicts throughout history took place in {{Year|2E 230}}.{{Ref|name=ONEye|[[Online:Alinor Eye Crest|Alinor Eye Crest]] major adornment description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, the tension between the College of Sapiarchs and the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] had grown exponentially since they established themselves in town. The Sapiarch, Haladan was murdered by a member of the Mages Guild, Emile Oncent, who confessed to the situation. But because of the nature surrounding his confession, a [[Lore:The Vestige|newcomer]] was tasked to uncover the truth and both Emile and Haladan's apparent relationship. The newcomer uncovered their hidden plot to cure [[Lore:Vampirism|vampirism]] for their mutual friend, Norasea, but Haladan was killed by one of her feral friends. It is unknown whether their secret was exposed to the public and Emile was sparred, or if the information was kept hidden to honor the pair's secret and thereby sparing Norasea of an execution. Regardless, the situation had been resolved.{{Ref|name=ESOMurder|[[Online:Murder In Lillandril|Murder In Lillandril]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{Ref|name=HRJ|{{Cite Book|Haladan's Research Journal}}}}

In {{Year|3E 110}}, the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]]{{Ref|name=BHOTE1}} in their last-known invasion of the province, the [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]].{{Ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}} Despite having assistance from Emperor [[Lore:Antiochus|Antiochus]] and the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial Navy]], the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm accredited to the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=TWQ5|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v5}}}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Lillandril was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Corridalf and had a rivalry with Alinor.{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Lillandril|Lillandril]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} During that decade, the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] traveled through town on their quest to re-assemble the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]], after acquiring the fourth piece in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]. They ventured to the city's Mages Guild and spoke with their loremaster, Corim Ashlen on the location of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], where the fifth piece was located. The guildhall was previously attacked by the priesthood of the [[Lore:Sheogorath|Mad God]], who sought a diamond that could reveal the tower's location. The champion retrieved the artifact from the Temple of the Mad God and returned it to Ashlen. Using the gem's power, the loremaster scribed the tower's location for the champion.{{Ref|name=ARQuest|[[Arena:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] main quest in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

===Known Rulers===
For the longest time, the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles. If anyone wanted their counsel, their best method was either through a select few places or the kings themselves. They continued this service up until their island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] first vanished in the Second Era, around when the Mages Guild was formed. When they returned several years later for the Third Era, they continued where they left off{{Ref|name=FOA|{{Cite Book|Fragment: On Artaeum}}}} until Artaeum disappeared a second time sometime in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] for whatever reason.{{Ref|name=SRTolfdir|[[Skyrim:Tolfdir|Tolfdir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Culture and Society==
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Altmer#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Altmer#Society and Religion|Society]]''' sections on the Altmer page.''
The Flask of Lillandril was a powerful artifact discovered by its ancient scribes. Despite its association with the High Elves, however, it was not of elvish origin.{{Ref|name=RGJ'ffer|[[Redguard:J'ffer|J'ffer]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} The wielders of the flask in the early First Era fled the southern provinces for [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and sought refuge with the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] in fear that Wood Elf assassins would track them down. The founders were killed and the flask was lost on Stros M'Kai,{{Ref|name=EA}} where it was later discovered by [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus the Restless]] in {{Year|2E 864}}.{{Ref|name=RGFlask|[[Redguard:A Legendary Flask|A Legendary Flask]] story quest in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} Ever since their victory over [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]], many [[Lore:Third Empire|Imperial]]-loyalists re-traced the Eternal Champion's steps through their journey, which meant they would have stopped in Lillandril.{{Ref|name=AMM|{{Cite Book|A Minor Maze}}}}

The Altmer have a common disinterest with the divine, [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], who is known by many names, such as the Apologist of Man. Despite this, the [[Lore:Sect of Harmonious Masters|Sect of Harmonious Masters]], mages from Lillandril, have dedicated their lives to working with Stendarr's light and adapting it into the School of [[Lore:Restoration|Restoration]]. The order believes they are honor-bound to teach all races their spells, so long as they are willing to learn from it.{{Ref|name=ROTHM|{{Cite Book|Rituals of the Harmonious Masters}}}} While other groups such as the [[Lore:Resolutes of Stendarr|Resolutes of Stendarr]] use the god's light to smite and defeat evil, others such as the Harmonious Masters or the [[Lore:Temple of Stendarr|Temple of Stendarr]] use it to heal.{{Ref|name=AOTR|{{Cite Book|Aura of the Righteous}}}}

The name is also used for male Altmer, either directly using ''"Lillandril"'' or spelling it alternatively as ''"Lillandriil"''.{{Ref|name=ARNames|[[Lore:Altmer Names#Arena and Daggerfall|Altmer Names]] in [[Arena:Arena|Arena]] and [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]}} By the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], Lillandril was a major port on the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]]'s southwest lines, between the cities of [[Lore:Dusk|Dusk]] and [[Lore:Stros M'Kai (city)|Stros M'Kai]].{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}} The first iteration of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] used an alloy called Lillandril [[Lore:Steel|Steel]] in the manufacturing of their longbows.{{Ref|name=CM25|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 25: Aldmeri Dominion Style}}}}
