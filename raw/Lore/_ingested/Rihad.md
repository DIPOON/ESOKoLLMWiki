[[Lore:Rihad|Rihad]] is one of the eight major cities in the province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]],{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} situated in the southernmost point of the province, near the mouth of the [[Lore:Brena River|Brena River]]. Rihad is a port city occupied by the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]], the modern-day remnants of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]].{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Hammerfell}}}}<noinclude>
{{NewLine}}

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:ON-crown store-Varlaisvea Ayleid Ruins.jpg|thumb|right|Varlaisvea south of Rihad]]
Rihad is located in the southern region of Hammerfell, where the Forebears cities are much more cosmopolitan than the traditional Crowns cities to the north.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} The city itself sits at an ideal spot on the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Coast]], next to the Brena River to the south and [[Lore:Hew's Bay|Hew's Bay]] to the north. Rihad's region has a tropical wet-dry climate and is prone to the occasional forest fire like their neighbors in [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]].{{Ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} Not far from the city, just to the south, is an impressive [[Lore:Ayleid Ruins|Ayleid ruin]] called [[Lore:Varlaisvea|Varlaisvea]].{{Ref|name=ONVarlais|[[Online:Varlaisvea Ayleid Ruins|Varlaisvea Ayleid Ruins]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Rihad is neighbored by several settlements, including Chaseguard to the northwest, Stonemoor to the northeast, and the [[Lore:Imperial Province|Imperial Province]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

==History==
===Early History===
It is unknown when the city was founded, but the Tavan wave of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] have settled along the southern coast of Hammerfell,{{Ref|name=CM28|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} which is now prominent Forebears country.{{Ref|name=PGE3}} One of the city's oldest known citizens was the Red Dervish of Rihad, Yousebh the Stringent, who is attributed with the quote, "a daughter of the House of [[Lore:Akos Kasaz|Akos Kasaz]] shall not marry an infidel of another house." It is a mantra that both the Crowns and Forebears have upheld for many years.{{Ref|name=ABOUH|{{Cite Book|A Betrayal of Our Heritage}}}} Sometime around {{Year|1E 2818}}, the War of Rihad took place between the Redguards and the [[Lore:Colovian|Colovians]]. The latter were known to employ [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] archers, but realized how undisciplined and prone to desertion they were.{{Ref|name=PGE1EAD|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Aldmeri Dominion}}}} 

At the time of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]'s occupation of Hammerfell, the [[Lore:Phyllocid Dynasty|Phyllocid Dynasty]] reigned over a province split apart by the Crowns and Forebears. Prince [[Lore:Ar-Azal|Ar-Azal]] of [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] inherited a throne rife with conflict and needed a solution to mend the province. After he received a vision from [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]] the World-Eater, who appeared as the [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Snakehead Potentate]], he traveled to the Fresco of the [[Lore:Goshawk|Goshawk]] and saw that [[Lore:Tava|Tava]] was nested with two mates, instead of the one that is normally there. King Ar-Azal took it as a sign from the [[Lore:Divines|divines]] and married one noblewoman of each faction. He married [[Lore:Fereshtah|Fereshtah]], daughter of the Forebears Grandee of [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], [[Lore:Ebrahim|Ebrahim]], and also married [[Lore:Arlimahera|Arlimahera]], daughter of the Crowns Grandee of Rihad, Murahd.{{Ref|name=TWAAHD|{{Cite Book|The Worthy Ar-Azal, His Deeds}}}}

===Rihad in the Third Empire===
[[File:AR-place-Stonekeep Halls.jpg|thumb|left|The ruined halls of Stonekeep]]
[[File:BL-npc-Dinahan-al-Rihad.jpg|thumb|right|Prince Dinahan-Al-Rihad]]
Rihad and nearby Taneth were on the path of the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]]'s invasion of western Tamriel, which started in {{Year|3E 249}}. The Redguards were fully confident that the two cities would have defeated the Usurper, but the Forebears cities were defeated in the [[Lore:Battle of Dragontooth|Battle of Dragontooth]] in {{Year|3E 253}}.{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|8}} Refugees from these cities fled north into the Iliac Bay and went as far as [[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]].{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}} Since the Crowns of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] noticeably did not help the cities to the south, the Forebears of Rihad and Taneth also did not help them in the midst of the [[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]] years later. They preferred to watch from afar instead of helping them.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Rihad was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Blubamka and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Taneth|Taneth]].{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Rihad|Rihad]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} After their escape from the [[Lore:Imperial Prison|Imperial Prison]]s, the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] was set forth on a quest by the spirit of [[Lore:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]] to retrieve the eight pieces of the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]]. The spirit tells the warrior to locate [[Lore:Fang Lair|Fang Lair]], where the first piece is located.{{Ref|name=ARFang|[[Arena:Fang Lair|Fang Lair]] story quest in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} 

Their quest eventually took them to the city-state of Rihad, where they garnered an audience with Queen Blubamka. The Queen would have relayed the information, but a band of [[Lore:Goblin|goblin]]s led by [[Lore:Golthog the Dark|Golthog the Dark]] stole a special parchment from Rihad Palace. The parchment was able to decipher an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] that would in turn reveal Fang Lair's location. The Eternal Champion returned with the parchment from the goblins in [[Lore:Stonekeep|Stonekeep]] and traveled north into Fang Lair, where they found their first piece.{{Ref|name=ARFang}} Ever since the champion's victory over [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]], Imperial-loyalists would retrace their steps across the provinces and make their way through Rihad.{{Ref|name=AMM|{{Cite Book|A Minor Maze}}}}

Not much is known about Rihad during the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], but what is apparent is that the cities across southern Hammerfell had fallen to the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], with only [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] to the west left standing. The entire southern region was left in shambles by the end of the war, even when the Redguards re-claimed the entire region, it was still in a state of ruin. When the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]] was signed in {{Year|4E 175}}, Hammerfell had officially ceded from the Empire,{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}} and at that point, the noble house of Rihad faced some issues. Prince [[Lore:Dinahan-Al-Rihad|Dinahan al-Rihad]], an Imperial loyalist was exiled from the throne and later granted asylum in Cyrodiil by the Empire. During his time there, he became an arena combatant and had active intent to retake the throne.{{Ref|name=BLDinahan|[[Blades:Dinahan-Al-Rihad|Dinahan-Al-Rihad]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-pet-Rihad Coffee Scorpion.jpg|thumb|left|The Coffee Scorpion of Rihad]]
[[File:ON-mount-Tattooed Shorn Camel.jpg|thumb|right|The Tattooed Shorn Camel]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Redguard#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Redguard#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Redguard page.''
The Redguards have been known to make regular contact with the Colovians of western Cyrodiil, especially around the shared borders where towns like [[Lore:Ontus|Ontus]] have a mixed population of both. Though most trade occurs through the Abecean Sea, the overland route between Rihad and the trading town on the Brena River; [[Lore:Leftwheal|Leftwheal]] was still important in the mid-Second Era.{{Ref|name=TGTWW|{{Cite Book|Traveler's Guide to West Weald}}}} Colovian Princes sometime hire bodyguards from Rihad.{{Ref|name=LORihad|[[:File:LO-misc-Colovian Prince.jpg|Colovian Prince with Bodyguard from Rihad – Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}}

The [[Lore:Dervishes|Dervishes of Rihad]] are a faction of talented and religious sword dancers. Sometime in the mid-Second Era, they dueled with the young and adventurous Princess [[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn]].{{Ref|name=ATUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn: The Unforeseen Queen}}}} They shave their [[Lore:Camel|camels]] and tattoo them with mystic symbols so that spirits of the [[Lore:Alik'r|deep desert]] would not lead them astray.{{Ref|name=ONShorn|[[Online:Tattooed Shorn Camel|Tattooed Shorn Camel]] mount description text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Scorpion#Coffee Scorpion|Coffee Scorpion]] of Rihad is named in homage to the city's [[Lore:Redguard Cuisine#Kaveh|kaveh]], which is considered strong, dark, and sweet.{{Ref|name=ONCoffee|[[Online:Rihad Coffee Scorpion|Rihad Coffee Scorpion]] pet description text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The musk of the local [[Lore:Fennec Fox|Fennec Fox]] is turned into a perfume, which is considered very fashionable for the nobility of southern Hammerfell, including [[Lore:Abah's Landing|Abah's Landing]]{{ref|name=PH|{{Cite Book|Prince Hew and the Haj Mota Chariot}}}} and Rihad.{{Ref|name=ONContra|[[Online:Contraband B#Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume|Bottle of Fennec Musk Perfume]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Like other cities on the Hammerfell coast, the Redguards of Rihad observe the 20th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] as the [[Lore:Day of Shame|Day of Shame]]. According to legend, the [[Lore:Crimson Ship|Crimson Ship]], a vessel full of [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]] victims of the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] first left [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] on the 9th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]] and was denied refuge from every port on Tamriel. It was later westbound for the Abecean Sea and never seen again. It is said to one day return and the Day of Shame was created in remembrance of that.{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|7}}{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Shame|Day of Shame]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition]] makes it apparent that the people of Rihad like to strip down naked and roll around in the dirt. However, these traits are more attributed to devotees of [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]], aptly known as the [[Lore:Satakal (faction)|Satakals]]. They reportedly nip people on the leg like they are [[Lore:Snake|snake]]s and perform exhibitions of "shedding their skin". They also roll on the sands for hundreds of miles, from [[Lore:Balhar|Balhar]] to the [[Lore:Nohotogrha|Nohotogrha Oasis]]. When the Empire moved onto Hammerfell, they took to the streets and bit at people until they were forced out of the cities by the Provisional Governors.{{Ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}}

One of Rihad's most well-known natives from the Third Era was [[Lore:Destri Melarg|Destri Melarg]], who spent much of his life attempting to collect and detail the lost history of the Redguards. He was born during [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s reign and moved to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] when he was nineteen. His ultimate work, ''[[Lore:Redguards, History and Heroes|Redguards, History and Heroes]]'' was never completed, having been published in separate books.{{Ref|name=NFRH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}} In the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], Rihad was one of the main ports of the [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]]'s western lines. It is connected to several cities along the mainland's coast, such as [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]], Taneth, and [[Lore:Southpoint|Southpoint]].{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:SR-map-East Empire Company.jpg|Map of the East Empire Company – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}}
