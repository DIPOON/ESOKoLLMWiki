# Lore:Tsaesci (race)

The [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] <noinclude>('''Snake Palace'''; {{IPA|'|s|eI|.|S|i}} {{pronunciation|SAY|she}} (listen<ab>LO-audio-word-Tsaesci.ogg</ab>), {{IPA|'|s|eI|.|@|.|s|i}} {{pronunciation|SAY|ess|see}},{{ref|{{Cite Book|Grommok's Journal}}}} {{IPA|'|s|eI|.|tS|i}} {{pronunciation|SAY|chee}} (listen<ab>LO-audio-word-Tsaesci_2.ogg</ab>),{{Ref|name=ESO Live Episode 2|[[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]] {{Cite Web|url=https://youtu.be/UI3D8W3_Emg?feature=shared&t=6468|title=Episode 2}}}} or {{IPA|'|z|i|.|S|i}} {{pronunciation|zee|she}}),{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}</noinclude> also known as the [[Lore:Snake|serpent]]-folk or snake men, are a race originating from the continent of [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]], east of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. While the racial status of the Tsaesci is ambiguous, with some sources claiming them as a [[Lore:Man|human]] race{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} while others claim them as a race of [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]],{{Ref|name=2920:TLY|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}{{ref|name=MA}} all known encounters with Tsaesci show they are at least a race of humanoids, with some looking indistinguishable from humans. However, the possibility of Tsaesci being shapeshifters further increases the ambiguity of their status.{{Ref|name=ESO Live Episode 2}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

Black hair is a known genetic trait of the Tsaesci,{{ref|name=MeetTheCharacter|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Chevalier Renald}}}} with some claiming them to also have scaly feet.{{ref|name=COTFC|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 8}}}} Sources which identify the Tsaesci as [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] attribute to them other physical traits such as tails, and describe them as having the bodies of "inverted [[Lore:Gorgon|gorgons]]".{{Ref|name=2920:TLY|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}{{Ref|name=LOVS7|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 7}}}}{{intnote|Legs|[Note 1]}} Adding to these contradictory claims, it is also claimed that the so-called serpent-folk apparently not only "ate" the men that lived on Akavir (although this statement can be interpreted in many ways), but also resemble them. Numerous legends shroud the origins of the Tsaesci. Some claim their forebears fell prey to a vampiric serpent race, while others suggest they were captured by them.{{ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}}{{Ref|name=Kiseravi}}{{intnote|Eaten|[Note 2]}} Some have claimed that the human appearing Akaviri that were encountered on Tamriel were merely surviving humans of Akavir who served as servitors of the serpent-folk.{{ref|name=SnakeDisc|group=UOL|[[General:Are the Tsaesci really snakemen?|''Are the Tsaesci really snakemen?'']]}}

Although immortality is attributed to them in literature,{{ref|name=MA}}{{ref|name=History of the Fighters Guild}} descendants of Tsaesci refugees in Elsweyr claim that longevity is not a trait of the race and speculate that the longevity of figures such as the Potentates had magical origins.{{ref|name=AvitaPitio|[[Online:Avita Pitio|Avita Pitio]]'s dialogue from [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online: Elsweyr]]}}

Individuals with Tsaesci and Imperial ancestry are said to be barely distinguishable from their pure Cyrodilic cousins, with the eyes and hair being the most recognizable traits of Tsaesci ancestry.{{ref|name=MeetTheCharacter}}

==History==
[[File:LO-misc-Against the Snakes.jpg|An illustration accompanying ''[[Lore:Against the Snakes|Against the Snakes]]'' of a person facing an Akaviri opponent with a serpent-like shadow cast over them|thumb|left]]
===Early History===
The [[Lore:Anuad|Anuad]] creation myth asserts that the Wandering [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] were divided onto three continents, with the descendants of those on Akavir becoming the Tsaesci (and therefore related to the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]]s and [[Lore:Atmora|Atmorans]]).{{ref|name=TAA}} 

At some point before the Second Era, the Tsaesci sought to "eat" all of the Dragons. They successfully enslaved the Red Dragons,{{ref|name=MA}}{{Ref|name=Kiseravi|[[ON:Kiseravi|Kiseravi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Elsweyr (DLC)|Elsweyr]]''}} and went on to use them as war mounts,{{ref|name=PGE1C}} but the Black Dragons managed to escape them into [[Lore:Ka Po' Tun (place)|Po Tun]]. The Tsaesci would then go to war with the [[Lore:Ka Po' Tun (race)|tiger people]] of that land, as a result the Dragons of Akavir were almost entirely wiped out.{{ref|name=MA}}

===Service to Reman===
The Tsaesci invaded Tamriel in {{Year|1E 2703}}, continuing their crusade against the Dragons in Tamriel, where they would search for the [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]].{{ref|name=RaF|{{Cite Book|The Rise and Fall of the Blades}}}} They were driven back by the forces of Emperor [[Lore:Reman|Reman I]],{{ref|name=PGE3Other|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} who they swore fealty to, claiming he was the Dragonborn they had searched for.{{ref|name=RaF}}{{ref|name=TBOTD}} From their ranks the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Akaviri Dragonguard]] was formed, dedicated to hunting Dragons.{{ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}} Additionally, Akaviri Dragon motifs and the practice of creating armor from Dragonscale would become common in Imperial culture. Their war mounts, the Red Dragons, would also come to symbolize the Empire itself.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{intnote|DragonEmblem|[Note 4]}} They left many influences on [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] institutions and culture, including the doctrines and equipment of the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]], the creation of the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]], and a reverence among the Imperial aristocracy for all things Akaviri.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} Surviving Tsaesci in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] served as mercenaries and personal guards of nobles.{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}

A number of Tsaesci even served the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] as Potentates, among them [[Lore:Sidri-Ashak|Sidri-Ashak]], [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]], and [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]]. The latter two ruled the Second Empire outright during the first half of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], a period known as the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]] that was brought on by the demise of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]].{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}}

After the fall of the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]] in {{Year|2E 430}} most Tsaesci fled [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], joining other Akaviri that lived in [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]] and the surrounding area. These Akaviri would later be known as [[Lore:Rim-Men|Rim-Men]]{{ref|name=TTS|{{Cite Book|The Tonenaka Shrine}}}} and would help the resurfaced [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Dir-Kamal]] armies to conquer [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. While they managed to acquire the throne, their reign was short-lived and they were overthrown like many other Emperors of the Interregnum that came before them.{{ref|name=PGE1E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}

==Society==
[[File:ON-npc-Proconsul Beloren-Kaie.jpg|A Tsaesci spirit returned to judge the living during the Proving Festival (ESO)|thumb|left]][[File:ON-furnishing-Akaviri Table, Stone.png|thumb|right|Four Tsaesci deity sigils (clockwise): Zisa (oceans/water), Nyfa (earth), Ilni (weather/air), and Myn (sun/fire)]]
[[Lore:Ancestor Worship|Ancestor worship]] seems to be an integral part of Tsaesci culture.{{ref|name=TPF|{{Cite Book|The Proving Festival}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Magnate Feina-Darak|Magnate Feina-Darak]]'s dialogue from [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online: Elsweyr]]}} They believe that all waterways connect the worlds of the living and the [[Lore:Ghost|dead]] and from them the [[Lore:Souls|spirits]] of their ancestors gaze upon their living descendants and their deeds,{{ref|name=DomitiaNasica|[[Online:Domitia Nasica|Domitia Nasica]]'s dialogue from [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online: Elsweyr]]}} thought to grant either good fortune or misfortune depending on whether the actions of their progeny have pleased them. Thus water occupies a special place in Tsaesci faith. One's ancestors are invoked by calling their name during the Proving Festival, a date on which it is said it is possible for spirits to cross over from the afterlife into the living world. Ancestor spirits are said to grow more powerful the more they are revered, thus a well-honored ancestral spirit will be able to cross from the afterlife into the living world more easily, take on different forms such as that of a black bladed golden warrior that is "swift as the wind", grant great fortune or misfortune, commune through and alter dreams, and even drag one to the afterlife if angered. Thus ancestor spirits are viewed as something to be appeased, able to render significant aid if properly honored but also dangerous if offended, known to deliver potentially fatal trials for what they consider insulting behavior such as verbal boasts of greatness that disrespect the dead.{{ref|name=Clever|{{Cite Book|Clever Kail-Perwa and the Great Boast}}}}{{ref|name=ONProconsul|[[Online:Proconsul Beloren-Kaie|Proconsul Beloren-Kaie]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Proving|{{Cite Book|The Proving Festival}}}} Spirits might also return to the living world for other reasons, such as taking action to maintain their own legacy, whether based on truth or not.{{ref|name=Magnate|[[Online:Magnate Feina-Darak|Magnate Feina-Darak]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Descendant|[[Online:Descendant of the Potentate|Descendant of the Potentate]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Rim-Men of Hakoshae, a community of Imperials with claims of Tsaesci descent, maintained what they believed was the Tsaesci tradition of ancestor worship. In {{Year|2E 582}}, they revived the Proving Festival&mdash;an Akaviri holiday where supplicants prove their wisdom, strength, and honor to their ancestors.{{ref|name=Magnate|[[Online:Magnate Feina-Darak|Magnate Feina-Darak]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Descendant|[[Online:Descendant of the Potentate|Descendant of the Potentate]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The Proving Festival is an important, yearly holiday of the Tsaesci to appease the ancestors and prove one's worth.{{ref|name=TPF}} It consists of three proofs which a participant has to pass: The first is the Proof of Reverence by which the ancestors are invoked and invited to enter the living world as spirits.  The Rim-Men let a paper lotus be carried down a river. The lotus symbolically swims into the realm of the dead to attract the attention of the ancestors.{{ref|name=DomitiaNasica}} The second proof is the Proof of Strength. It serves to show the ancestors one's strength and that one can resist various dangers.{{ref|name=VekSecunia}} The last proof is the Proof of Wisdom. Traditionally it consisted of scholars drafting grand essays, writers composing ornate poems or philosophers performing lengthy debates. However in Hakoshae the inhabitants use riddles for that purpose.{{ref|[[Online:Valoria Marius|Valoria Marius]]' dialogue from [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online: Elsweyr]]}}

In addition to reverence of the ancestors, at least four deities, symbolizing different [[Lore:Elements|elements]], are known: [[Lore:Ilni|Ilni]] (weather/air), [[Lore:Myn|Myn]] (sun/fire), [[Lore:Nyfa|Nyfa]] (earth), and [[Lore:Zisa|Zisa]] (oceans/water).{{ref|name=TotE|{{Cite Book|Tale of the Elements}}}} [[Lore:Z'en|Z'en]] might also be worshipped by the Tsaesci, as origins of his mythology can be traced back to Akavir.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}

The Tsaesci refer to their artistry as the Serpentine Ways of Making. Though its introduction in the Reman Empire has diluted and debased it over years, prime examples of it still exist in Tamriel, such as the [[Lore:Tonenaka Shrine|Tonenaka Shrine]] in Rimmen.{{Ref|name=CM53|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 53: Tsaesci Style}}}} Ever since their exodus from Cyrodiil, the Tsaesci refugees changed Rimmen forever, by bringing their aesthetics, culture, and food. Their vast wealth that funded the Tonenaka's construction also brought prosperity to the city.{{Ref|name=TTS}}

Tsaesci are known to have had [[Lore:Marriage|marriage]],{{ref|{{Cite Book|Akaviri Diary Translation}}}} a tradition they shared with the Rim-Men.{{ref|[[ON:Ashur|Ashur]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Unlike the other races and cultures on Nirn, the Tsaesci do not follow the tradition of [[Lore:Constellations|constellations]] in accordance with their "subdermal culture", meaning none of them possess birthsigns.{{Ref|name=MKL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Magne-Ge Pantheon|Magna-Ge Pantheon|ns_base=General}}}} The Tsaesci language is reportedly complex for Tamrielans.{{Ref|name=PGE3Other}}

The Tsaesci are known for their use of magic.{{ref|name=AvitaPitio}}{{ref|name=VekSecunia|[[Online:Vek-Drassi Secunia|Vek-Drassi Secunia]]'s dialogue from [[Online:Online|Elder Scrolls Online: Elsweyr]]}} Additionally, they utilize a special magic called '[[Lore:Kiai|kiai]]',{{intnote|Kiai|[Note 3]}} which shares similarities with Shouts of the Nordic [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]].{{Ref|name=COTS|{{Cite Book|Children of the Sky}}}} Known for their resistance to poison and prowess in combat, tales even speak of their mastery over Dragons, purportedly enslaving the mighty beasts.{{Ref|name=Kiseravi|[[ON:Kiseravi|Kiseravi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Elsweyr (DLC)|Elsweyr]]''}} Specfically they are known to have enslaved red dragons, who they use as war mounts.{{ref|name=MA}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}} Additionally, they are known to enslave the [[Lore:Goblin|goblins]] of the isles surrounding their land, who they use for labor and fresh blood.{{ref|name=MA}}

==Arms and Armor==
Two Akaviri "Blood Drinker" swords, known as [[Lore:Dawnfang and Duskfang|Dawnfang/Duskfang]] and [[Lore:Bloodthirst|Bloodthirst]], emulate the rumored traits of their Tsaesci creators. Although some sources claim Tsaesci fight without shields or armor, using only swords such as the Dai-katana in combat,{{ref|name=2920v1|{{Cite Book|2920, Morning Star (v1)}}}}{{ref|name=History of the Fighters Guild|{{Cite Book|History of the Fighters Guild}}}} eye-witness accounts from the battle at Pale Pass describe the Tsaesci not only using armor and shields, but also various types of weapons.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 92: Ancestral Akaviri Style}}}} The nature of the race is a tapestry of historical contradictions, so what little is known about them is uncertain. However, all known encounters with the Tsaesci have not only confirmed that they are humanoids, but that they also use armor and shields in combat. However, the Tsaesci use shields mostly as a form of ceremonial and decorative armor, and don't find widespread use in combat.{{Ref|name=CM53}}

Akaviri katanas, while elegant and efficient, are too expensive and sophisticated a weapon to be popular with the Legions or hero-adventurers. Well-heeled nobles, collectors, and swordmasters prize the blade for its superior balance and effectiveness.{{ref|name=Garo|[[MW:Garothmuk gro-Muzgub|Garothmuk gro-Muzgub]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} However, they are part of the standard arms for members of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]].

The tanto is a stylish dagger variant of Akaviri design. For obscure reasons, the [[Lore:Telvanni|Telvanni]] prefer them to standard daggers as a matrix for their enchantments, and Telvanni mercenaries are often equipped with enchanted tantos by their mage-lord patrons.{{ref|name=Garo}}

The wakizashi is an elegant and refined single-edged version of the more common double-edged longswords of Western design. Because the weapons themselves are rare, and few smiths know how to make them, they are not common. {{ref|name=Garo}}

==Notes==
* The Tsaesci are influenced by [[wikipedia:East Asian|East Asian]] cultures, with a particular emphasis on [[wikipedia:Japan|Japan]], through their armor, buildings and weapons.
{{note|Legs|1.|''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|The Last Year of the First Era]]'' uses the verb "to slither" when Versidue-Shiae or Savirien-Chorak move.}}

{{note|Eaten|2.|The meaning of the term "eaten" as given from what is known of the land of Akavir is not clear. Although the description of the "vampiric" Tsaesci might suggest literal meaning, it could be a metaphor for being subdued. In ''[[Lore:Mysterious Akavir|Mysterious Akavir]]'' by an anonymous writer, the Tsaesci are told to have "tried to eat all the [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]]. They managed to enslave the Red Dragons, but the black ones had fled to (then) Po Tun".}}

{{note|Kiai|3.|''[[Lore:Children of the Sky|Children of the Sky]]'' only mentions "Akaviri swordsmen". However, this probably refers to the Tsaesci, as they are said to heavily rely on swords in combat.{{ref|name=2920v1}}}}

{{note|DragonEmblem|4.| The main used emblem of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] however remained the Red Diamond.}}

*[[Lore:Diagna|Diagna]]'s cult originated in [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] during the "Twenty Seven Snake Folk Slaughter".{{Ref|name=VOF}} Whether the "Snake Folk" were the same as the Tsaesci serpent-folk is unknown.
