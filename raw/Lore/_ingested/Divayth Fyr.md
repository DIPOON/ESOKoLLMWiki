[[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] is a 4,000-year-old powerful [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] sorcerer, whose presence was said to be sensed in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] from as far away as [[Lore:Dranil Kir|Dranil Kir]], near the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]].{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=Lilatha|[[Online:Lilatha|Lilatha]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DivaythFyr|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He is a member of Great [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]], though he generally stays out of House politics.{{ref|name=RFDF|{{Cite Book|Response from Divayth Fyr|ns_base=Morrowind}}}} Fyr isn't an active member of the Telvanni Council, acting more as a consultant or advisor to them. However, he respects their command and will carry out most requests laid down by them.{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNFP|[[General:Gary Noonan's Posts|Gary Noonan's Posts]]}} As a '''Master of Secrets''' and a neutral overseer of the Old Ways, he was also once a member of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] and seeks balance.{{ref|name=MTC}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=MaS|[[General:Masser and Secunda|Masser and Secunda]]}}

Divayth Fyr is sometimes compared to renowned mages such as [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] and [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]]. He reportedly views Galerion as a talented figure who squandered his potential by dedicating it to the goals of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]]. In contrast, Fyr shares similar views with Shalidor regarding the role of mages in society; however, he considers Shalidor to have been influenced by what he describes as "short-sighted sentimentality." Shalidor is also said to owe Fyr a substantial debt, which Fyr intends to collect in the future.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}}

In the Second Era he was at least sometimes present at and around the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]] in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], where he was associated with Professor [[Lore:Morian Zenas|Morian Zenas]] and Doctor [[Lore:Alfidia Lupus|Alfidia Lupus]] and may have intended and arranged the demise of both.{{Ref|name=CM|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motifs}}}} He was also known to be a generous patron to the [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]].{{Ref|name=LAHT}} Fyr has a great respect for the Imperial Throne, even though he may not believe in it. His personal connection to the throne gives him access to Imperial facilities which house documents, and as such are of great interest. In all, the Telvanni Council and the Imperial Throne are treated quite the same in Fyr's eyes, giving him the nickname of ''Flower Child of Tamriel''.{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNFP}} In the Third Era, Fyr lived in his tower of [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]] in the [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] region of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] along with his four "daughters" (female clones of himself), [[Lore:Alfe Fyr|Alfe]], Beyte, Delte and Uupse Fyr.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}}

Divayth Fyr regularly wears a set of [[Lore:Daedric Equipment|Daedric]] armor reminiscent of armor used by other members of House Telvanni. The [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth]] once speculated that it was a pale imitation of Daedric craftsmanship put together using inferior materials,{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}} but his true power, in any case, lies elsewhere.{{ref|name=TelvanniMasterWizard|Description of the [[Online:Telvanni Master Wizard|Telvanni Master Wizard]]}}<noinclude>

==History==
===First Era===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none;|''It is a fact, that my life is somewhat shrouded in a Fog of Unknowing. My Towers stand in isolation, and I am pleased to remain as such. All that can be learned of me is little to the minds of the short lived. Do however, be my guest sometime, if you are ever in the vicinity of Tel Fyr. Learn from me what you may, but be thoughtful of your subject.''|Divayth Fyr when asked about his past}}
Divayth Fyr was born as a [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] in [[Lore:Tel Aruhn|Tel Aruhn]]{{Ref|name=SINN|{{Cite Book|Sorcery is Not Necromancy!}}}} in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], who lived until at least the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], at the age of 4000, and may still be alive today.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} Fyr studied with the Psijics on the isle of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], and later left as a "sharp-elbowed upstart".{{ref|name=MTC}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=MaS}}

He also spent some time at the [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]].{{Ref|name=LAHT}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=XG|{{Cite Book|The Xal-Gosleigh Letters|ns_base=General}}}} In his youth, he dabbled in the forbidden art of [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]], but he would eventually turn away from it to focus on other subsections of conjuration.{{Ref|name=SINN}} He is mentioned in the book ''[[Lore:The Doors of Oblivion|The Doors of Oblivion]]'', as well as ''[[Lore:Varieties of Daedra|Varieties of Daedra]]'' (written by a former apprentice), which assert that, before his interest in Corprus, Fyr traveled to the [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|Daedric planes of Oblivion]]. He is also the [[Lore:Books by Author#Divayth Fyr|author]] of at least two books. One of them he wrote because he would grow tired of conjurers being generalized as necromancers and would write a book to educate the uninformed.{{Ref|name=SINN}} Although he once displayed great skill with necromancy, he did not wish to utilize it to attain immortality.{{ref|name=TESL|[[Legends:Divayth Fyr's Trials|Divayth Fyr's Trials]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

Divayth Fyr was a friend of [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and enjoyed contemplating his riddles, as well as the contemplation of pain.{{ref|name=DivaythFyr}}{{ref|name=PainandRiddles|[[Legends:Fighters Guild Hall (puzzle)|Fighters Guild Hall (puzzle)]] and [[Legends:Leafwater Blessing (puzzle)|Leafwater Blessing (puzzle)]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} After the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] cursed the Chimer, turning them all into the Dunmer (Fyr included). Sotha Sil was one of the few who could resist Azura's curse, but chose to remain with his Dunmeri appearance,{{ref|name=MPStars|Dialogue of various [[Online:Star|Memories]] in the [[ON:Mnemonic Planisphere|Mnemonic Planisphere]]}} seemingly to aid in the transition of his people into the Dunmer, and getting them to accept that it was not a curse but a blessing.{{ref|name=NRM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} Divayth Fyr criticized him for this.{{ref|name=MPStars}} Sotha Sil would later take his place among the living gods known as the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]],{{ref|name=NRM}} yet Fyr let his friend know that he would refrain from worshipping him as such.{{ref|name=MPStars}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-npc-Morian Zenas.jpg|Morian Zenas in Apocrypha (ESO)|right|thumb]]
Sometime during the reign of Potentate [[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]], Fyr attempted to win the heart of Doctor [[Lore:Alfidia Lupus|Alfidia Lupus]], who was the Imperial Ethnographer for Potentate Savirien-Chorak from 2E 418 to 2E 431,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 1: High Elf Style}}}} but the enigmatic [[Lore:Morian Zenas|Morian Zenas]], Professor of Transliminal Arts at the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]], was also flirting with her at the same time. Although Fyr was reputed to be popular among some women and had been called a philanderer,{{ref|name=YDDiHoF|[[Online:Felvyn the Swordarm|Felvyn the Swordarm]]'s correspondence in [[ON:Online|ESO]] (''Your Dauntless Deeds in Halls of Fabrication'')}} Dr. Lupus ultimately chose Zenas over Fyr. 

Later, Fyr helped Zenas enter [[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]], from which the human wizard journeyed to various other planes of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] until he became lost in [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]]. Dr. Lupus came to believe that Fyr was responsible for Zenas's fate when she discovered that Fyr had summoned [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] from a ritual contained in the [[Lore:Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus|Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus]]. Doctor Lupus went missing shortly afterwards. {{Ref|name=CM|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motifs}}}}

During this time, Fyr was reportedly derisive toward Argonians, or, at least one Argonian in particular - [[Lore:Seif-ij Hidja|Seif-ij Hidja]], a master conjure and Morian Zenas's pupil and assistant. By Hidja's testimony, Fyr would refer to him as "the scaleskin help" when Professor Morian wasn't around. Seif-ij Hidja's account further implies Fyr left sometime around the disappearance of Dr. Alfidia Lupus, and that Seif-ij Hidja would not miss him.{{Ref|name=CM}}
===The Interregnum===
[[File:LG-cardart-Yagrum Bagarn 02.jpg|Yagrum Bagarn (Legends)|left|thumb]]
[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended Divayth Fyr.{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing artifacts such as [[Lore:Chrysamere|Chrysamere]], the [[Lore:Bow of Shadows|Bow of Shadows]], and the [[Lore:Staff of Hasedoki|Staff of Hasedoki]] among various items of "unimaginable significance".{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}

Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, Divayth Fyr planned to extend his tower of [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]] into the [[Lore:Abanabi Caves|Abanabi Caves]] until a portal to his friend's [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] inexplicably opened up inside, spilling out Refabricants and [[Lore:Fabricants|Fabricants]] from the [[Lore:Halls of Fabrication|Halls of Fabrication]]. The Fabricant threat was neutralized when a large group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] accompanied him into the cave and then entered the Clockwork City portal, defeated the Assembly General, and disabled their production. Divayth then studied the portal.{{ref|[[Online:Forging the Future|Forging the Future]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Fyr's interest in investigating the status of Sotha Sil was kindled and he would make preparations to re-enter the City. He traveled to the island of [[Lore:Dranil Kir‎‎|Dranil Kir‎‎]] to perform a Daedric ritual to open a rift to the Clockwork City using a Dwarven device. He was also trying to locate the Skeleton Key but was met with the opposition of a Psijic monk named [[Lore:Lilatha|Lilatha]], who was responsible for guarding the island. She interrupted his ritual, declined to provide any information, and urged him to leave Dranil Kir.{{ref|name=MTC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Divayth Fyr}}}}
[[File:ON-quest-Where Shadows Lie.jpg|right|thumb|Divayth Fyr and Proctor Luciana Pullo defend Sotha Sil]]
Soon after the events that transpired on the island, he enlisted the aid of the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] to journey there, where they would become involved in a plot with the [[Lore:Daedric Triad|Daedric Triad]]. [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]], one of the princes of the triad, tried to take control of the Clockwork City. She summoned Sotha Sil's Shadow, who replaced him as ruler of the Clockwork City. Using the [[Lore:Skeleton Key|Skeleton Key]], they attempted to unseat the real Sotha Sil from his Throne Aligned, and have the shadow take control of the Throne. The Vestige and the [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostle]] [[Lore:Luciana Pullo|Luciana Pullo]] intervened and defeated the shadow in combat, but Nocturnal had her shadows envelop the area as a countermeasure, in an attempt to consume the heroes. Luciana used her shadow-banishing light to keep Nocturnal's shadows at bay, and Divayth Fyr arrived during the battle and kept Sotha Sil alive and constrained the shadow. They bought the Vestige enough time to be able to remove the Skeleton Key from the Throne Aligned and awaken Sotha Sil. Sotha Sil would then reunite with his shadow and banish Nocturnal from his realm. Sotha Sil gave the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping and returned to the Cogitum Centralis to make preparations for the Daedric Triad.{{ref|name=ESOCWC|Events of [[Online:DLC#Clockwork City|ESO:Clockwork City]]}}

In the same year, Divayth Fyr ventured to the Oblivion plane of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]]. He discovered that someone intended to auction the [[Lore:Ebony Blade|Ebony Blade]]. Fyr, with the assistance of the Vestige, discovered that the item that was intended to be sold was a replica of the real artifact and punished the person responsible for the forgery.{{ref|name=WOL|Events of [[Online:Web of Lies|Web of Lies]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Although Fyr played no significant role in the defeat of Valkynaz Nokvroz and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], he attended the celebrations of those events that were hosted in Fargrave and [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]].{{ref|name=AAH|Events of [[Online:Against All Hope|Against All Hope]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=HSE|Events of [[Online:Hope Springs Eternal|Hope Springs Eternal]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was Divayth Fyr, portrayed in conversation with fellow magical genius Vanus Galerion.{{ref|[[Online:Painting: 10-Year Anniversary|Painting: 10-Year Anniversary]] item depiction in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
[[File:MW-npc-Divayth Fyr.jpg|Divayth Fyr, circa {{Year|3E 427}}|left|thumb]][[File:LG-cardart-Tel Vos Magister 02.jpg|Aryon, Fyr's Former Apprentice (Legends)|right|thumb]]
Divayth Fyr had maintained a tenuous contact with the Council House of [[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] in the Second Era, albeit through a proxy - he did not send a personal Mouth to the Council, but shared the services of Master Firuth's Mouth, Vaelin Oren, at least for such mundane affairs as the delivery of alchemical supplies; however, by the end of the Third Era he had completely withdrawn from Council affairs, despite the fact that his age and power would have made a place on the Council his for the asking. His one-time protege, [[Lore:Aryon|Aryon]] of [[Lore:Tel Vos|Tel Vos]], tried to persuade him to return,{{ref|name=MFMA|{{Cite Book|Message from Master Aryon|ns_base=Morrowind}}}} but was met with a polite refusal by the reclusive Fyr.{{ref|name=RFDF}}

Among other things, Divayth Fyr spent his time researching the Divine Disease, [[Lore:Corprus|Corprus]], and his Corprusarium was a refuge for anyone infected, including his oldest friend, Yagrum Bagarn, the last living Dwemer.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=GTV}} He took a deep interest in Dwemer history and collected many Dwemer artifacts.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} By the Third Era, Divayth Fyr has also been in possession of several [[Lore:Artifacts|Daedric artifacts]], among them were [[Lore:Scourge|Scourge]], [[Lore:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]], and [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}} He was willing to employ ruthless tactics, such as kidnapping, to achieve his ends, but did not seem to mind adventurers looting his dungeons as long as they did not harm the corprus victims under his care.{{ref|name=Morrowind}} Divayth was also at one point a slaveowner. One of his last slaves was [[Lore:Vistha-Kai|Vistha-Kai]], an argonian who refused to leave after he was set free. He became Fyr's hireling and taught himself how to fight, later becoming the warden of the Corprusarium. Vistha-Kai became a trusted partner to Divayth, and he was even trusted to accompany and train the sorcerer's daughters in martial arts.{{Ref|name=MWDF|[[Morrowind:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Prior to the ascent of the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] in {{Year|3E 427}}, Fyr believed that the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|Nerevarine Prophecy]] was impossible to fulfill, but the Nerevarine contracted Corprus from an agent of the Sixth House and sought out Fyr for the cure. The "cure" did not actually cure the disease but rather removed the negative effects, such as crippling physical deformities and insanity, while retaining the positive effects, including immunity to all diseases, increased strength and endurance, and possibly also immortality.{{ref|name=Morrowind}}

Divayth Fyr allegedly kidnapped Delyna Mandas, the daughter of Arethan Mandas, a [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]] noble. Struck with grief, Arethan Mandas fell into madness, and became the self-proclaimed Lord of West Gash, demanding tribute from travelers in the region. A Lawman of House Redoran was tasked with dealing with the issue, either by slaying the noble, or soothing him by rescuing the daughter from Fyr's clutches. The veracity of the claims is unknown, though Divayth Fyr himself has no recollection of her, nor gave any resistance in her retrieval.{{ref|name=MWMLM|[[Morrowind:The Mad Lord of Milk|The Mad Lord of Milk]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

Divayth Fyr was quite acquainted with the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], with them guarding him to the Morrowind border after he had met with Uriel Septim VII.{{ref|group=UOL|name=ROAFS|{{Cite Book|Return of a Fellow Scholar|ns_base=General}}}} He also received a letter from a Master Blades member that had spoken to [[Lore:Baurus|Baurus]], explaining the situation after Uriel had been killed by the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]], and requested that Fyr come to Cyrodiil to assist however he could; however the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] overruled this requesting that Fyr stay in Morrowind to help prevent any unrest that might occur.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Dragon's Chilled Blood|ns_base=General}}}}

===Fourth Era===
It is uncertain if the old wizard survived the cataclysms of the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]],{{ref|name=TM|[[Legends: The Midden|The Midden]] quest in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} but some sources suggest that Divayth Fyr did in fact live on.{{ref|group=UOL|name=GTNFP}} In addition, Divayth's daughter Alfe Fyr survived and moved to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where the [[Lore:Forgotten Hero|Forgotten Hero]] met her to negotiate for passage into the Clockwork City.{{ref|[[Legends: Alfe Fyr (quest)|Alfe Fyr]] quest in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}
