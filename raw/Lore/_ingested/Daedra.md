# Lore:Daedra

'''Daedra''' ({{IPA|'|d|eI|.|d|r|@}} or {{IPA|'|d|i|:|.|d|r|@}}) are immortal beings that are commonly believed not to have taken part in the creation of the [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], and thus did not become bound by the limitations imposed by the contract of its creation.{{ref|name=AAD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}} They have a very real impact on the mortal realm; in one way or another, Daedric influence touches everyone on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=PGE3Arena|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Arena Supermundus}}}}

They are regarded as counterparts to and, in a manner, opposites of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], though the distinction between the two can be confusing to the layman. The terms "Aedra" and "Daedra" are often used interchangeably with "gods" and "demons". The terms "Aedra" and "Daedra" are not relative; they are exact [[Lore:Mer|elvish]] titles which translate to "ancestors" and "not our ancestors", respectively, representing the mythical genealogy of the elves as they perceive it. The divide between Aedra and Daedra is commonly thought to have originated ''after'' their birth, during the creation of the Mundus, where most cultures maintain the Aedric beings played a leading role in the creation of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], causing them to commonly be considered the creators of the mortal world, whereas the Daedra refused to participate.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{Ref|name=AAD}}

Technically, only the plural is written "Daedra", but this word is frequently used in singular as well. The proper singular form is "Daedroth",{{ref|name=OB|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} but that has come to refer to a [[Lore:Daedroth|specific species of Daedra]].{{ref|name=VOD|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Daedra}}}}{{ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}} They are known as the "dark kin" or "darkkin",{{Ref|name=TRBoD|{{Cite Book|The Requisite Book of Daedra}}}}{{Ref|name=TWoO|{{Cite Book|The Waters of Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=Athenaeum|{{Cite Book|Battlespire Athenaeum|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|111}} but different cultures have their own myths and names for Daedra. The study of Daedra is referred to as Daedrology. Those who study Daedra are called "Daedrologists".{{ref|{{Cite Book|On the Xivkyn}}}} The most powerful of the Daedra are known as [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Princes]], and those of varying power below that of the Princes are sometimes referred to collectively as "Lesser Daedra".{{ref|name=DD}}{{ref|name=DOA|{{Cite book|Denizens of Apocrypha}}}}<noinclude>

==Mythic Origins and Cultural Significance==
===Altmeri===
According to the creation myth of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], known as ''The Heart of the World'', creation begins before the start of the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] and the beginning of time: the primordial force of [[Lore:Anu|Anu the Everything]], who encompassed and encompasses all things, created [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]], the soul of all things, so it could know itself. Anuiel in turn created [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]] for the same purpose, as the sum up of all limitations which it would use to differentiate between its attributes and ponder itself. Their interrelation created the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]], where the Original Spirits, the [[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]], emerged as "aspects of Aurbis" before the creation of the Mundus. The Aurbis, at first turbulent and chaotic, was stabilized by the emergence of [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]], the soul of Anuiel, who spread through existence as the force called time, allowing the Original Spirits to take on names and identities.{{Ref|name=TM}}{{Ref|name=VoF}}

The distinction between Aedra and Daedra started to emerge when one of these spirits, called [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], a being that was more of a limit than a nature, convinced some of the other spirits to help create a soul for the Aurbis, a place where even the aspects of aspects might self reflect. This proved to be a deception. The new world, Nirn, was a place composed of more limitations than not, and the spirits that participated in its construction began to die. Many vanished completely, prompting the architect, [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], to terminate the project and depart. When Magnus departed the Mundus, the et'Ada that took part in its creation broke into groups. Most would follow the flight of the God of Magic to become the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]], but some of those present chose instead to stay in the new world and keep working on it so it wouldn't die. Of these remaining spirits, some would follow the example of [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law, and would typically be referred to from that point on as "Earthbones" or "Earth Bones". Others would instead populate Nirn, making children "just to last". From that point, such spirits would typically be referred to as the "Ehlnofey".{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}{{ref|name=Mystery|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}} The offspring of the Aedra would gradually become weaker with each generation. As they diminished, the offspring of the Ehlnofey would differentiate and give way to the mortal races like the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]]. The "weakest souls" would be formed by Lorkhan into armies he named "Men". Altmeri myth holds that Auri-El and Lorkhan and their respective followers would then war with one another, leading to the shattering of [[Lore:Atmora|Altmora]] and [[Lore:Aldmeris|Old Ehlnofey]], the defeat of Lorkhan, and the removal of his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart]]. At the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]], a [[Lore:Convention|Convention]] was held to judge Lorkhan and determine the laws and roles of spirits within the Mundus. With the exodus of the Aedra and the departure of [[Lore:Magic|magic]] in the mythic sense, the cosmos stabilized and linear history began.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{Ref|name=VoF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in the Empire}}}}{{ref|name=Mystery|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}}{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}{{ref|name=AU4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Not all Altmeri texts agree on the exact point in time when the classification of "et'Ada" emerges, with some dating it to the initial formation of the gods rather than to their creation of the world.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}

The Altmeri creation myth describes the Daedra as "tainted et'Ada", some of which were responsible for the formation of the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], who had been led astray by them.{{Ref|name=TM}} According to some sources, though the Altmer are inclined to claim every spirit as formerly an elven ancestor, there are those spirits who lack elven approval and are therefore excluded from this classification.{{ref|group=UOL|name=PetersonInterview|[[General:Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Religion|Ted Peterson on High Rock and Summerset - Religion]]}} These spirits are defined as "Daedra" or "not our ancestors", and are generally viewed as not having a link to the mythical genealogy of the elves as they perceive it.{{Ref|name=AAD}} Because of this, reverence or emulation of the Daedra is viewed by the Altmer as betrayal of one's ancestry,{{ref|name=RtEP|{{Cite Book|Regarding the Ebonheart Pact}}}} and as an instance of heresy which the [[Lore:Divine Prosecution|Divine Prosecution]] that upholds both secular and religious law is tasked with stamping out.{{ref|name=DtOyEiS|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/26371 Discover the Organizations You'll Encounter in Summerset] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}

===Aldmeri and Psijic===
According to ''The Myth of Aurbis'', as explained to [[Lore:Uriel V|Uriel V]] by the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]], the basics of Aldmeri belief are that the Aurbis existed for time without measure as the Gray Center between the forces of Anu and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]]. Within this Gray Center emerged the magical beings of mythic Aurbis, bits of the immortal polarity given life. The first of these was [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], whose existence made it easier for other spirits to structure themselves. For a long time these spirits lived, formed, reformed, and procreated. Eventually, with Lorkhan as the instigator of the decision, the spirits told the story of their own deaths. This process is described in various terms: a transfiguration into the concrete non-magical substance of the world, a war in which all were slain with their bodies becoming the substance of the world, or as a romantic marriage and parenthood where the parent spirits naturally had to die to give way to the succeeding mortal races. Mortals were shaped in the image of the spirits, either through conscious molding or by springing forth from the matter left behind by the dead spirits. Having died, these spirits became the et'Ada, and separated themselves in nature from the rest of the magical beings of Aurbis from that point on.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The Psijics believe, therefore, that the Aedra and Daedra, the powerful spirits to whom mortals pray, are the spirits of "superior men and women whose power and passion granted them great influence in the afterworld", and were themselves bewildered by their own ancestors, going back to the original progenitors, the [[Lore:Acharyai|Acharyai]], while alive.{{ref|name=TOW|{{Cite Book|The Old Ways}}}}

===Cyrodiilic===
The [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiilic]] creation myth, ''Shezarr's Song'', holds the world was formed when [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] convinced a number of the gods, the Aedra, of the beauty of the concept of becoming mothers and fathers. The Aedra, determined to proceed regardless of cost, gave birth to the world and the life within it by sacrificing part of themselves, a painful process after which they were no longer strong and young as they'd been since the beginning. Some of the Aedra, the gods of the elves led by Auri-El, came to regret their choice and sought revenge upon Shezzarr and his allies, resolving to teach their children, the elves, to endure the suffering of the new world with dignity. Others, the gods of Men led by [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], were proud, deeming the new world and the life within it glorious despite their sacrifices, and resolving to teach their children to cherish the concepts of beauty and honor and to love one another. The Daedric Princes mocked this course of action as folly, and chose instead to create their own worlds within themselves and under their complete control for all time. They did, however, envy the creation of the Aedra, for mortals were more "surprising and entertaining" than the lesser Daedra they could create, and thus they would seek to seduce and ensnare the most powerful and passionate among them.{{ref|name=TM}}

[[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]] ascribe religious value to the extent of a spirit's sacrifice for the mortal world and hold greater reverence for those spirits who gave more for it. As such, they generally view the Daedra as unreliable false gods unworthy of worship, exemplars of selfish willfulness who were incapable of making the same sacrifice as the Aedra. They are remembered with scorn for their patronage of the slaver [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]],{{ref|name=Artorius|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}} and are viewed as a threat which the Aedra defend the world from via the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|Liminal Barriers]].{{ref|name=LtD|Events of [[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=DoB|Dialogue from the [[Online:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}}{{ref|name=Brazier|{{Cite Book|The Sublime Brazier}}}}{{ref|name=Martin|[[Oblivion:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Yokudan===
The [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]] understanding of creation broadly resembles that of the Altmer, viewing the mortal world in negative terms. According to their legend, ''Satakal the Worldskin'', all things originated from Satak, the First Serpent, on whose scales all worlds to come rested. Compelled by its Hungry Stomach, Akel, Satak started a neverending cycle of devouring itself and shedding its skin to be reborn and begin anew, becoming [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]]. When things from within the devoured worlds realized the truth of the cycle they began to take names, becoming the first spirits. These spirits sought to escape Satakal's neverending hunger and found a way to slide between its Worldskins by moving at strange angles. This was called the Walkabout, and through it was born a sanctuary from the cycle called the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]]. One of these spirits, [[Lore:Ruptga|Ruptga]], sired many children and was so tall he placed the [[Lore:Stars|stars]] in the sky to help other spirits on the Walkabout. The mortal world was formed when Sep, created by Ruptga out of previous Worldskins to be his helper, convinced some of the other spirits to stop performing the Walkabout, as they could escape the cycle by inhabiting a new world made of balled up Worldskins instead. This, however, was a trick, as having been formed of its skin Sep too carried much of Satakal's hunger, and wished to devour the spirits who followed him. Too far from the Far Shores to jump back to, and too far from the real world of Satakal to survive, the deceived spirits began to die, survived by the children they'd made.{{ref|name=TM}} Despite this story partly resembling that of the Altmer, the distinction of Aedra and Daedra is not present within Yokudan religious texts.

The Yokudan pantheon includes the figure of [[Lore:Malacath|Malooc the Horde King]], an enemy god of the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] whose army was once routed by the [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]]. Malooc is speculated by some scholars to be connected to the Daedric Prince Malacath.{{ref|name=VoF}}{{ref|name=IEGE|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Elsweyr}}}}

===Khajiiti===
[[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] creation myth holds the gods, both Aedra and Daedra, were born as siblings to the three litters of Ahnurr and Fadomai. Spirits commonly considered Aedra were generally born to the first litter, and spirits commonly considered Daedra were generally born to the second. The goddess Nirni was born to the third litter, alongside the moons, [[Lore:Azura|Azurah]] and, eventually, [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]]. Nirni wished to give birth to children, but had no place where she could do so, so she went to Lorkhaj for help. Lorkhaj made a new place but, as his heart was filled with the [[Lore:Void|Great Darkness]] of [[Lore:Namira|Namiira]] due to the circumstances of his birth within the Great Darkness, he tricked a number of his siblings into entering this place with Nirni, where many of them had to die to make Nirni's path stable. Furious at the betrayal, the surviving siblings tore out the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhaj]].{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}{{ref|name=Spirits|{{Cite Book|Spirits of Amun-dro}}}} Pre-[[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|ri'Datta]] myths also mention that Nirni eventually died when [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffer]] was also corrupted by the Great Darkness and killed her. Y'ffer himself was later slain by Azurah, [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]] and [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], and his bones were used to construct a cairn for Nirni.{{ref|name=Spirits|{{Cite Book|Spirits of Amun-dro}}}}

===Reachmen===
Diverging from other Tamrielic beliefs, the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachfolk]] view a number of the Daedric Princes as Great Spirits that are linked to the processes of the mortal world. According to them Nirn was created by [[Lore:Lorkh|Lorkh]], who desired to create not a vibrant paradise but a teaching tool, a place that taught through suffering. To create this place, Lorkh came to [[Lore:Namira|Namira]], queen of the infinite realm of spirit, who granted him a place in her endless void to make his new world. As part of his covenant with Namira, Lorkh had to make a great [[Lore:Dark Heart|sacrifice]] of his own, a sacrifice which is said to be reflected in the creation of the [[Lore:Briarheart|Briarhearts]]. Thus to the Reachfolk there are but two worlds, the world of flesh, and the infinite world of Spirit from which all dualistic competing forces emerge. They believe that [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] is the heir of Lorkh as supreme spirit of the world of flesh, contrasted by Namira, who is the queen of the spirit world. They also maintain that the natural order of the world, the eternal balance and rhythm of the world's competing forces, is maintained by [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]].{{ref|name=Great|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach}}}} 

===Clockwork Apostles===
The [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] believe Nirn was created by the "et'Ada Gears", aspects of Anu as is everything else, who were tricked by Lorkhan into believing the Great Lie, that they were separate from the all-encompassing singularity of Anu, and took names of their own to reinforce this illusion. In this belief, the Daedra and [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] are nothing more than illusion themselves, a consequence of the flaws of the et'Ada Gears' creation, the result of the Void taking root within the cracks caused by said flaws.{{ref|name=TTIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}}

===Argonian===
According to the transcribed creation myth of the Adzi-Kostleel [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] tribe, all spirits were formed from the struggle of [[Lore:Anu|Atak]], the Great Root, and Kota, the Serpent, who fought each other for so long that they eventually forgot their fight and became one, forming the entity Atakota. Atakota severed its roots and shed its skin and said the word "Maybe", giving rise to its Shadow, formed of its hunger and shed skin. Atakota continued to roil in a cycle, devouring itself and shedding its skin to be reborn and begin anew, each scale a world that it devoured. From this process the world and spirits arose, and with Atak and Kota not in conflict, things had time to begin and end, and the Shadow too fell asleep. As Atakota slumbered, the spirits started making new things that shared in their aspect and loved them, and they started growing, until they too were as large as Atakota, and forgot it had come before them and had a sleeping Shadow. Soon the worlds and spirits became too big and there was no more room for new things. In desperation, the spirits fell upon the sleeping Atakota and bit into it to drink its blood. Eventually the peace of Atakota broke, Atak remembered growing and Kota remembered being nothing, and existence fell into chaos again. In that time of chaos, some spirits drank deeply of Atakota's blood and sap, and they [[Lore:Dragons|grew scales and fangs and wings]], forgetting why they'd ever made anything other than to eat it. Kota's blood made new oceans and Atak's sap made new stones. Eventually, the roots woke the shadow and asked for its help, and its intervention put an end to the chaos that was threatening to consume everything.{{Ref|name=Root|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}}

Argonians of the [[Lore:Sul-Xan|Sul-Xan]] [[Lore:Naga|Naga]] tribe acknowledge at least one Daedric Prince, [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], revering him as the True Egg-Child of Sithis.{{ref|name=ESOLive1|group=UOL|[[General:ESO 2021 Global Reveal|ESO 2021 Global Reveal]]}} This title can be understood from a pre-[[Lore:Duskfall|Duskfall]] perspective, when the old [[Lore:Nisswo|ruling class]] of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] believed that Sithis was a malevolent destructive force,{{ref|name=Xode|[[ON:Nisswo Xode|Nisswo Xode]]'s dialogue in {{g|on|murkmire}}}} much like Dagon. The Sul-Xan's worship of the Razor Prince{{Ref|[[Online:Sul-Xan Fleshripper|Sul-Xan Fleshripper]] mount description in {{g|on|blackwood}}}} is an anomaly, as Argonian society at large does not partake in the veneration of powers from Oblivion. Indeed, there are no words in their language, [[Lore:Jel|Jel]], to describe any of the Daedra. As explained by a native speaker during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], the Argonians do not "taint their language with words for abominations from Oblivion". Instead, they use the common words from other widely-spoken languages.{{ref|name=KyCAHC|[[Books:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]]: Black Marsh, Daedroth}}

===Other Beliefs===
Though most creation myths credit the Aedra with contributing to the creation of Mundus in some manner and describe said event as the point of the split from the Daedra, there are exceptions where the formation of the mortal world is described as the work of other forces, and the distinction between Aedra and Daedra originates from their emergence itself.

According to the [[Lore:The Annotated Anuad|Anuad]], all things began with the brothers Anu and Padomay. Their interplay created Nir, who alongside Anu gave birth to the Twelve Worlds of Creation. Jealous, Padomay attacked, but was cast out of time by Anu. Life emerged on the Twelve Worlds but, eventually, a hateful Padomay returned and shattered the worlds with his blade. After Anu defeated his brother, he attempted to save what he could by combining the shattered fragments of the Twelve Worlds into a new world, Nirn. But after he did so, Padomay rose again and the two brothers finally pulled each other out of time forever. On Nirn life was seeded by the survivors of the Twelve Worlds who'd arrived in the new world alongside fragments of their original worlds, the Ehlnofey and the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]], and during this time the gods and demons of the world, the Aedra, Daedra and Magna Ge also formed out of Anu and Padomay's spilled blood, as distinct groups from the life of Nirn and from each other from the start.{{ref|name=AA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}

According to the [[Lore:Breton|Bretonic]] tale ''[[Lore:The Light and the Dark|The Light and the Dark]]'', two immortal entities representing Order and Chaos chose [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] to be their eternal battleground. This everlasting battle would create energies so powerful it distorted the world and created life, including the "people of [[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]]", who would in turn give rise to the gods, and their "daedric enemies", by believing in their myths for so long and so strongly that it caused the energies unleashed by the conflict of the Light and the Dark to bring them into being. Thus, all of creation echoes the battle between the Light and the Dark.

The [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] cult espoused the belief that the mortal world was actually the [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of the Daedric Prince Lorkhan. Per this belief, the Aedra, the gods mortals generally worship, had stolen the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world.{{ref|name=Camoran|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=Commentaries|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}

According to the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Cult of the Ancestor Moth]], the Daedra can't read the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]], as the [[Lore:Ancestor Moth|Ancestor Moths]] which represent a connection to the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls", and thus to the Aedra themselves, refuse to communicate with Daedra.{{Ref|name=LAESMP|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Elder Scrolls & Moth Priests}}}}

==Behavior and Culture==
Daedra are sometimes referred to as demons, which may be misleading. All Daedra have a penchant for extremes and are therefore capable of tremendous acts of devastation, but their different spheres make them apply their power in different ways, and their infinite diversity makes speaking in generalities about them difficult.{{ref|name=OB}}{{ref|name=VOD}} Thus, it is often impossible to accurately label them as "good" or "evil"; the one thing that can be stated with certainty is the Daedra are beyond mortal comprehension (as mortals seem to be beyond Daedric comprehension).{{ref|name=VOD}}{{ref|name=SOTD|{{Cite Book|Spirit of the Daedra}}}} [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]] once noted that even "the lowest [[Lore:Banekin|Banekin]] has a more developed sense of the Aurbis's scale and nature than your most eminent scholars".{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}

Daedra are great imitators, and their creations are described as mimicking things found on Nirn in an outlandish or even corrupted way.{{ref|name=SRWulf|[[SR:Wulf Wild-Blood|Wulf Wild-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[SR:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]}} They are capable of creating seemingly indestructible and mighty [[Lore:Daedric Equipment|Daedric]] armor and weaponry through dark rituals.{{Ref|name=CMDS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 14: Daedric Style}}}}{{ref|name=DD}} The Princes have seemingly created several types of Daedra that resemble beings of the mortal realm. The [[Lore:Skein Beetle|Beetles]], [[Lore:Skein Scorpion|Scorpions]], [[Lore:Skein Wasp|Wasps]], and [[Lore:Skein Spider|Spiders]] of Mephala's [[Lore:Spiral Skein|Spiral Skein]], the [[Lore:Crow Daedra|Crows]] of Nocturnal's [[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]], and the [[Lore:Nightmare Animal|Nightmare animal]]s of Mehrunes Dagon's [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] are some of the more obvious examples of Daedric parallels with the creatures of Nirn.

Many species of Daedra speak a language known as Daedric, the language of Oblivion.{{ref|name=Forte|{{Cite Book|Grand Maestro Forte's Research}}}}{{ref|name=DFlang|[[DF:Daedric|Daedric]] skill in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} It is not to be confused with the [[Lore:Daedric Alphabet|Daedric alphabet]]. The tongue is incredibly old and bears some resemblance to the ancient [[Lore:Ebon Stadmont|language of Ebon Stadmont]], which perhaps even predates Daedric.{{ref|name=Forte}}

Daedra such as Dremora are said to know little of fear and therefore have little use for concepts such as comfort, which are meant to shield one from frights or cares. A Daedra belonging to a clan is said to be bound and shaped by its clan bond, becoming linked to other members of that same clan in purpose and attuned to them. Where others would sense nothing, clan members might perceive each other's secret signs and intentions. It is described as a connection which can never be broken, not even by death, defining a Daedra's essential nature to the extent it should be thought of not as an allegiance but as their very identity. Thus, Daedra will often refer to each other by their clan title, such as "Bladebearer" or "Foolkiller", as one would use a name.{{ref|name=Rynkyus|[[Online:Rynkyus|Rynkyus]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The bond a clan has with their sovereign is suspected to sometimes imperil them, it is thought that when a clan's sovereign punishes them severely for failure or the sovereign themself suffers a great defeat, it may impact the clan itself, rendering it "Vanquished". Such Vanquished Daedra are scorned as exiles and outcasts and treated contemptuously.{{ref|name=On Dremora Clans|{{Cite Book|On Dremora Clans}}}} According to Daedric sources, the manner in which the Daedra perceive each other's essence can differ from how mortals view them; sources from Dremora describe them as viewing others of their kind as a "belligerent collision of acute hyper-angles".{{ref|name=DremoraStyle|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 63: Dremora Style}}}}

Daedra have expressed a diversity of opinions about the existence and nature of the Aedra. The [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] Lyranth described the Mundus as the "cemetery" of the Aedra, claiming that their original hopes for their progeny were ultimately not achieved due to the foolishness of mortals. Nonetheless, though the creators of Mundus are said to have "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", they are sometimes praised for their immense conviction and admired for having wrought order out of chaos, a "brutal coercion" which even Daedra must deem impressive. By contrast, Magnus and the Magna-Ge are viewed as lowly cowards who fled when things turned dire, and thus made it impossible to ever know what could've been achieved had they chosen to stay and keep helping.{{ref|name=Lyranth2|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}} Contrasting Lyranth's view, the [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]] information broker Madam Whim claimed that no living being existed which could explain the true relationship between the Aedra and the Daedric Princes, and that no information she'd ever come across in her long life indicated the Aedra were anything more than a story mortals told themselves to feel safer in their beds at night.{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}}

Though Daedra are usually dismissive of mortals, and interest in them is generally viewed as an oddity, there have been cases of Daedra expressing respect or even love for mortals, to the extent of provoking the ire of their clan for doing so.{{ref|name=Rynkyus|[[Online:Rynkyus|Rynkyus]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Xocin|[[Online:Xocin|Xocin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Xyria|[[Online:Xyria|Xyria]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}          

==Characteristics==
===Vestiges===
[[File:ON-creature-Soul of Dusk.jpg|thumb|right|The Soul of Dusk, a Daedric animus]]
Daedra lack an Anuic Animus, also known as a mortal [[Lore:Souls|soul]],{{Ref|name=CCTAP|{{Cite Book|Chaotic Creatia: The Azure Plasm}}}} each one instead possessing a Daedric soul known as a '''Vestige''' (sometimes also called an Animus).{{ref|name=Dylora|[[Shivering:Dylora|Dylora]]'s dialogue in [[SI:Shivering Isles|''Shivering Isles'']]}} When a Daedra is destroyed, either in Mundus or in Oblivion, its Vestige is banished to the "[[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|Waters of Oblivion]]" and then attracted back to the plane of Oblivion from which it originated.{{Ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Book of Daedra}}}}{{ref|name=Dylora}}{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBStaada|[[Shivering:Staada|Staada]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} To reflect this, a slain Daedra is often said to have been "banished" or "purged" instead of "killed".{{ref|name=TBOD}} A Daedra reforms (or "reconstitutes") according to the pattern within its Morphotype (a concept which seems to be interchangeable with the Vestige, though it more closely relates to the form of a Daedra). They do so using [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|Chaotic Creatia]] (e.g., the [[Lore:Azure Plasm|Azure Plasm]] of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]]).{{Ref|name=CCTAP}} The reconstitution process has been described by at least one type of Daedra as being both irritating and unpleasant. It involves effort on the part of the discorporate Daedra, and can result in minor variations in the final reconstituted form, such as a body part that seems "shorter than it used to be".{{ref|name=ONScruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A Daedric Prince can create a new Daedra by replacing an entity's original Animus with a Vestige, a process which was used to give rise to the original [[Lore:Daedric Titan|Daedric Titans]].{{ref|name=DDTT|{{Cite Book|Daedra Dossier: The Titans}}, Paragraph 10}}

Vestiges are described as largely immutable and static, incapable of experiencing change in spiritual nature or in certain aspects of character. It is said that they never grow or change, a reflection of their nature as beings who are "immortal" and "forever".{{fact}} This unchanging nature is described as applying even more so to Daedric Princes; the Princes themselves express inability to truly destroy or permanently remove the power of another Prince, even if the targeted Prince desires it to be so. Such was the case with the Daedric Prince Ithelia, who was stripped of her power and rendered "as powerless as a mortal" when first imprisoned, but couldn't be destroyed and gradually regained her power upon being released. Later, when Ithelia willingly relinquished her power to rid herself of her destructive rage, it was determined that her nature as a Prince would always restore her to power and freedom, sooner or later, whether she desired it or not -- also causing the rage to return. This, the true nature of a greater Daedra's "princely disposition", made it necessary for Ithelia to be exiled to a [[Lore:Many Paths|different reality]] due to the existential threat she posed.{{Ref|name=TDungeons|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONScruut|[[ON:Scruut|Scruut]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Mora|[[Online:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

A Daedra's form and nature are said to be defined by their [[Lore:Nymic|Nymic]], or "incantatory true name" in its one changeless form.{{Ref|name=NatureNymics|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Nymics}}}} According to [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], immortals such as the Daedra do not share the mortal quality of always having a chance to succeed and reach a desired outcome, even when it would otherwise be impossible, and instead would inevitably fail in the same circumstances.{{ref|name=Mora|[[Online:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This limitation is affirmed by the Daedric Prince Ithelia, according to whom the natural ability of mortals to choose who they are and alter their own [[Lore:Fate|fates]]&mdash;naturally and without the need of magic or divine power&mdash;is something of which beings such as herself or her servants are incapable.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Vestiges of Daedra are unlike the souls of mortals, in that they are fettered to existence. Where a mortal might change their spirit's vessel through some basic magic (a change a number of mages have undergone, taking on forms such as that of a [[Lore:Voriplasm|voriplasm]], a tree, a [[Lore:Tomeshell|tomeshell]], a [[Lore:Giant|giant]], or even a [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] skeleton),{{ref|name=Rectavius|[[Online:Sorcerer Rectavius|Sorcerer Rectavius]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Vorm|[[Online:Vorm|Vorm]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Orryn|[[Online:Orryn the Black|Orryn the Black]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Thallik|[[Online:Thallik Wormfather|Thallik Wormfather]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Sapling|[[Online:Strange Sapling|Strange Sapling]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} a Daedra's form cannot defy their Nymic.{{Ref|name=NatureNymics|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Nymics}}}}{{ref|name=Uldazaan|[[Online:Uldazaan the Heresy-Keeper|Uldazaan the Heresy-Keeper]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Because of this difference in mutability, even a mortal who's received power from a Daedric Prince to achieve transformation into a "mock-Daedra" remains fundamentally mortal.{{Ref|name=TDungeons|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Dungeons}}}}.

Though normally unchanging, a Daedra's Vestige&mdash;and therefore its Morphotype&mdash;can be altered and given new qualities through vestigial hybridization, a process through which a Vestige is given traits originating from a different Vestige, thus allowing a new type of Daedra to be created. Examples of this process include the creation of the [[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] by [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] through the combination of elements from both Dremora and [[Lore:Xivilai|Xivilai]] within the [[Lore:Vile Laboratory|Vile Laboratory]], and the transformation of [[Lore:Valkyn Skoria|Valkyn Skoria]], who became a being of elemental fire through the addition of elements from [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame Atronachs]] to his Vestige by Mehrunes Dagon.{{ref|name=Questions|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}}

===Nymics===
A [[Lore:Nymic|Nymic]], or "incantatory true name", is described as a "pattern or formula that defines the being created when it manifests itself from the eternal chaos of Oblivion". It is said to define both the form and the overall nature and role of a Daedra, shaping it perpetually into one changeless form for all time. Thus, the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon is defined as a god of destruction by his own Nymic and can't simply choose to be something else; if he is banished, he is reconstituted exactly as he was before because he is bound to his portfolio eternally.{{Ref|name=NatureNymics|{{Cite Book|On the Nature of Nymics}}}}

A Daedra's overall true name can be comprised of multiple types of Nymics. A "protonymic" is the description of a kindred of species for creatures that lack self-awareness. A "neonymic" is an additional Nymic that more "sophisticated" Daedra, such as Dremora, [[Lore:Skaafin|Skaafin]], or [[Lore:Watcher|Watchers]], have in addition to their protonymic. A "tribunymic" indicates clan affiliation and a "hieronymic" a Daedra's rank within it. Greater Daedra, such as the Daedric Princes, possess additional layers to their Nymics, such as the potent "egonymic", which lesser Daedra lack and of which they are largely unaware. The more complex and greater a spirit, the more Nymics it has&mdash;and the more difficult it becomes to interact with its true name via magic.{{Ref|name=GuideNymics|{{Cite Book|A Summoner's Guide to Nymics}}}}{{ref|name=Lyr|[[Online:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
===Other Characteristics===

This phenomenon has been linked to Daedra Lords such as [[Lore:Hollowjack|Hollowjack]], who is said to derive metaphysical power from feeding on the fear of all mortals praying for divine intervention,{{Ref|name=LHDR|{{Cite Book|Lord Hollowjack's Dread Realm}}}} and as a result grows stronger with time.{{ref|name=LATH|{{Cite book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays}}}}

==Lesser Daedra==
The Daedric Princes are only the most powerful of the Daedra, and many of them have many servants known generally as lesser Daedra (not to be confused with Daedroths, a specific type sometimes referred to as simply "Lesser Daedra"). Much like the [[Lore:Planes of Existence|planes]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] which they inhabit, the Daedra are described as being overall infinite in their variation.{{ref|name=Jackdaw|[[Online:Jackdaw Daedrat|Jackdaw Daedrat]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Even though many lesser Daedra are associated with a Daedric Prince in particular, in reality, any individual Daedroth can serve any Daedric Prince by taking part in an "Oath Bond", while others stay unaligned, though the specifics are characteristically unknown.{{ref|name=VOD}}{{ref|name=DD}}{{ref|name=SOTD}} Some are in service to mortals.{{ref|name=DD}} For example, the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] have been known to use Daedra as servants and instruments,{{ref|name=DD}}{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} as have many other cultures,{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}}{{ref|name=Feyfolken|{{Cite Book|Feyfolken}}}} and the Ayleids employed entire armies of Daedra in their wars against men.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} Daedra are often summoned and used in the study of magic; of course, the school of [[Lore:Conjuration|Conjuration]] deals specifically with connecting one's mind with a Daedroth and compelling its appearance in the mortal plane.{{ref|name=OB}}{{ref|name=TDOB|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}} For information on mortals summoning such Daedra, see the book ''[[Lore:Darkest Darkness|Darkest Darkness]]''.

==Notes==
*According to [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth]], the [[Lore:Foolkillers_Clan|Foolkillers Clan]] once warred with a minor realm consisting of "laughing mirrors", and noted a realm of "talking daggers" could theoretically exist. This goes to show how many more Daedra may exist than mortals are aware of, as they are said to be "as numerous and distinct as the grains of sand in your [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r desert]]".{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}} The realm of [[Lore:Attribution's Share|Attribution's Share]] is said to be inhabited by "nightmarish creatures" large enough to swallow travelers whole.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}}
*According to Julian LeFay, the word "daedra" originated from a combination of the word "daemon" as used in computing and Greek mythology, and "Phaedrus" and "Phaedo", two terms associated with Plato.{{ref|group=UOL|name=JJReddit|1=[https://www.reddit.com/r/Daggerfall/comments/7b4vh4/comment/dpglvuj/ Reddit Post by Julian Jensen (2017/11/07)]}}
