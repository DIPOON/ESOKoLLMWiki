[[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] was the leader of the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]], a cult of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] worshippers that assassinated [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] in {{year|3E 433}} in an attempt to destroy [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}<noinclude>

==History==

===Origins===
In {{year|3E 267}},{{Intnote|nb 1|[nb 1]}} during the final battle against the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]], refugees from across Tamriel had gathered in the city of [[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]]. One such refugee was a frightened Bosmeri woman by the name of Kaalys. When the battle began at sea, she was struck ill, injured, and began to feverishly scream that Mankar was coming and would destroy them all. Most initially assumed she was referring to the Usurper. An injured Bosmeri scout and former ally of Haymon, Orben, soon recognized Kaalys as the former mistress of Haymon, from before both she and Orben turned traitor and fled. She continued to scream about the coming of Mankar, yet Orben claimed he had no idea who "Mankar" could be and stated it was not one of the Usurper's aliases. At the climax of the battle, when the docks of Dwynnen were set aflame, Kaalys vanished. When Orben inquired to the healers about where she went, they claimed that she had, surprisingly, given birth and must have fled with her child, the apparent son of Haymon Camoran. He pressed them for more information, but all they could tell was the child's name, Mankar Camoran.{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}}<noinclude>

===Dagonite Road===
Little is known about Mankar's life after his mother fled with him into the wilderness of High Rock, but eventually Mankar Camoran would take to worshipping the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] of Destruction [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]]. Mehrunes Dagon chose Mankar as his mortal champion and gave him the artifact book [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|Mysterium Xarxes]], which was penned by Dagon himself.{{ref|name=OBMartin|}} Mankar pored over these writings taking away many metaphysical implications that he then wrote down in the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes'']]. Mankar also bound himself to the Xarxes using unholy rituals, and in doing so he was able to create his own [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane of existence]] known as [[Lore:Paradise|Paradise, Gaiar Alata]].{{ref|name=OBMartin|}}

Mankar had two children, a daughter named [[Lore:Ruma Camoran|Ruma]] and a son named [[Lore:Raven Camoran|Raven]]. The implications of their births are shrouded in metaphor in their own right, with Ruma said to have given birth to herself and her brother Raven.{{ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Mankar Camoran also implies in his Commentaries that Ruma chose not to follow in his footsteps in worshipping Mehrunes Dagon, and so Mankar cannibalized her and "made another one", who embraced the worship of Dagon and thus earned the love of Mankar.{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}

Mankar believed the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]] to be traitors to [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], whom he believed was a Daedric Prince whose [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] was Dawn's Beauty: [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The assassination of the Emperor was intended to break the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|covenant]] which protected Nirn from Oblivion and launch a [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] invasion by Mehrunes Dagon into Tamriel. With each [[Lore:Magical Transportation|Oblivion Gate]] that opened, the [[Lore:Dragonfires|barriers]] between Nirn and [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [[Lore:Astronomy|Firmament]], would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.{{ref|name=OBMartin|}}{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} While Mankar managed to gain possession of the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], he ultimately failed to dispose of the last remaining heir, [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]. While Mankar waited in Gaiar Alata for the barriers between Nirn and Oblivion to dissolve, he was hunted down by the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] and slain.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}

==Beliefs==
According to the beliefs of the Mythic Dawn, the mortal world was actually the Oblivion plane of Lorkhan, who was actually a Daedric Prince. Per this belief, the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]], the gods mortals generally worship, were actually Lorkhan's betrayers, who stole the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world.{{ref|name=Camoran|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries|}}}} Thus, Mehrunes Dagon undertook the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] not to destroy Tamriel, but to "liberate the Occupied Lands" from the Aedra.{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In Tamriel, the cult called their enemies the "Dragon's Blood"&mdash;a reference to the covenant between [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} More prosaically, it refers to the line of [[Lore:Tamrielic Emperors|rulers]] keeping the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] alight.{{ref|name=MDC}} In particular, Mankar Camoran had a "long duel" with the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]],{{ref|name=OBMankar}} dating back to the reign of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], when it is speculated the ''Commentaries'' were written.{{ref|name=OBTarMeena|[[Oblivion:Tar-Meena|Tar-Meena]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Commentary on Beliefs===
The beliefs of the Mythic Dawn were met with derision by [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]]. She described the assertion that [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] is not distinct from Oblivion but rather one of its constitutive realms as false, and mocked the claim that the realm belongs to Dagon by birthright as "convenient". Lyranth questioned whether the information had come from Dagon himself or simply been spread by his cultists, and advised that "one should pay less attention to idiotic cultists".{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Mehrunes Dagon & Daedra in the Second Era}}}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|nb 1|^|Camoran's ''Commentaries'' were said to have been contemporary with [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], written over 400 years before 3E 433, conflicting with Mankar's alleged birth year of 3E 267.{{ref|name=OBTarMeena}}}}
* While his race in Oblivion was Altmer, the Legends card released in the ''[[Legends:Jaws of Oblivion|Jaws of Oblivion]]'' expansion categorizes him as both Altmer and Bosmer, solidifying his ties to the Camoran Usurper.{{ref|name=LegCar|{{Card|Mankar Camoran}} card in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}
* Mankar was able to wear the Amulet of Kings, which can only be done by a [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]].{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}{{ref|name=ONAT|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It is unclear if he had always been a Dragonborn, or if he gained the power as part of the transformative experience described in the [[Lore:Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes|''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes'']]. Within his commentaries, he also stated that the transformation allowed him to commune in another tongue and "speak fire", which aligns with usage of the [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]].
*The ultimate goal of the Daedric Prince and former Magna Ge [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]] is described in [[Lore:Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]], an Ayleid religious text, as cleaving through everything to reach "Numancia", the breaking of the shackles of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] to give every being their own desired history, a state the eventual realization of which bears some connection to the limitations of linear time imposed by [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Ada-Mantia]] and [[Lore:Convention|Convention]].{{ref|name=TNC|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Mythic Dawn Commentaries]], the term "Nu-Mantia" is used by Mankar Camoran as an exclamation associated with "liberty" and the "promise of paradise", the goal of the Mythic Dawn. As Mehrunes Dagon is said in the same text to have been created by the Magna Ge, the two concepts might be related.{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}
* Game Director [[General:Todd Howard|Todd Howard]] said of Mankar Camoran: "Terence Stamp plays kind of the-- you know, he's the bad guy, somebody who comes from a [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|different line of kings]]... who wants to make this world his own. So he's kind of a priest. You know, he's not a cackling maniac. We like to have our bad guys be a little grayer. We want that moment where the player goes like... ''Maybe he's right''."{{ref|group=UOL|name=OblivionMakingOf|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zvm0CN3tQFI ''The Making of Oblivion'' documentary]}}
