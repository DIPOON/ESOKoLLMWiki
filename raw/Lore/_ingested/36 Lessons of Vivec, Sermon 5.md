Finally the simulacrum of the netchiman's wife became unstable. The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] in their haste had built it shoddily and the ashes of {{Lore Link|Red Mountain}} slowed its golden tendons. Before long it fell on its knees beside the road to the lands of the {{Lore Link|Indoril|ONlink=Online:House Indoril}} and pitched over, to be discovered eighty days later by a merchant caravan on its way to the capital of [[Lore:Resdayn|Veloth]], anon {{Lore Link|Almalexia (city)}}.

{{Lore Link|Vivec|MWlink=Morrowind:Vivec (god)}} had not been among his people all the days of his pre-life so he stayed silent and let the {{Lore Link|Chimer}} in the caravan think that the simulacrum was broken and empty{{nst|.<br>A|ON=. A}} Chimer{{nst|i|ON=}} warrior, who was protecting the caravan{{nst|,|ON=}} sai{{nst|ON=d:<br>"|d, '}}Look here how the Dwemer try to fool us as ever, crafting our likenesses out of their flesh-metals. We should take this to the capital and show our mother {{Lore Link|Almalexia|Ayem}}. She will want to see this new strategy of our enemies.{{nst|ON="|'}}

But the merchant captain sai{{nst|ON=d:<br>"|d, '}}I doubt that we shall be paid well for the effort. We can make more money if we stop at {{Lore Link|Noormoc}} and sell it to the {{Lore Link|Red Wives}} of {{Lore Link|Mehrunes Dagon|Dagon}}, who pay well for the wonders made by the Deep Folk.{{nst|ON="|'}}

But another Chimer, who was wise in the ways of {{nst|ON=the p|p}}rophecy, looked on the simulacrum with disquietude{{nst|ON=.<br>"|. '}}Was I not hired on to help you seek the best of fortunes? I say you should listen to your warrior, then, and take this thing to Ayem, for though manufactured by our enemies there is something in it that will become sacred, or has been already.{{nst|ON="|'}}

The merchant captain took pause then and looked on the simulacrum of the netchiman's wife and, though he heeded always the advice of his seers, could do no more than think of the profits to be made at Noormoc. He thought mainly of the Red Wives' form of recompense, which was [[Lore:House of Troubles|four-cornered]] and good wounded, a belly-magic known nowhere else under the moons. His lust made him deny Ayem his mother. He gave order to change course for Noormoc.

Before the caravan could get underway again, the Chimeri warrior who had counseled a passage to the capital threw his money to the merchant captain and sai{{nst|ON=d:<br>"|d, '}}I will pay you thus for the simulacrum and warn you: war is coming with the {{Lore Link|Nord|shaggy men}} of the {{Lore Link|Skyrim|north}} and I will not have my mother Ayem at uneven odds with one enemy while tending to another.{{nst|ON="|'}}

:{{nst|ON="|'}}{{Lore Link|Nerevar}},{{nst|ON="|'}} the merchant captain said, {{nst|ON="|'}}this is not enough. I am Triune in my own way, but I follow the road of my body and demand more.{{nst|ON="|'}}

Then Vivec could not remain silent anymore and said into Nerevar's head these words:
:{{nst|ON="|'}}You can hear the words, so run away
: Come{{nst|,|ON=}} Hortator, unfold into a clear unknown,
: Stay quiet until you've slept in the yesterday,
: And say no elegies for the melting stone{{nst|ON=."|'}}

So Nerevar slew the merchant captain and took the caravan for his own.

The ending of the words is [[Lore:Almsivi|ALMSIVI]].
