# Lore:Daggerfall Covenant

The [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] was a military alliance between the [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] kingdoms of [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]], and [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], the northern [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] kingdom of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] and the city of [[Lore:Hallin's Stand|Hallin's Stand]], and also [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] during the chaotic [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. It vied with the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] and [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] for control over the contested Ruby Throne in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. The Covenant saw itself as the successor to the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]] and aimed to restore the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] with the legitimacy to rule all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=GTTDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}} They advocated economic prosperity and the worship of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]].{{ref|name=THOTE|{{Cite Book|True Heirs of the Empire}}}} In this mission it was led by a Royal Council, presided over by [[Lore:Emeric|High King Emeric]], a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] merchant lord of superb diplomatic skill.{{ref|name=GTTDC}} The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s were represented by King [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]], and the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]s by King [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog]] gro-Bagrakh.<noinclude>

The faction gets its name from the treaty of mutual defense that created the first Daggerfall Covenant, which was signed at Daggerfall following [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]]'s failed invasion of High Rock.{{Ref|name=AUADC2|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 2)|Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 2)]]}} The Covenant was known for having some of the finest [[Lore:Smithing|blacksmiths]] in Tamriel.{{ref|[[Legends:Loading Tips|Loading screen tip]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} The [[Lore:Lion Guard|Lion Guard]] was a knightly order that served as the elite and personal guardians of the Greater Daggerfall Covenant's rulers.{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}{{Ref|name=AUADC1|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)|Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)]]}} Inspired by the Lion Guard, Emeric adopted the [[Lore:Lion|lion]] as his personal crest and it became the martial symbol of the Daggerfall Covenant.{{Ref|name=AUADC1}} The [[Lore:Ring of Daggers|Ring of Daggers]] was the intelligence branch of the Covenant, and enforced economic and political issues on the behalf of the faction.{{Ref|name=ONValaste|[[ON:Valaste|Valaste]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
[[File:ON-banner-Daggerfall Covenant.png|right]]
There were actually two Daggerfall Covenants, although the first was made up only of small kingdoms within High Rock. Thus, most references to the Covenant are to the second, or Greater, Daggerfall Covenant, which united multiple provinces and races.

===First Daggerfall Covenant===
The first Covenant was formed in response to the invasion of High Rock in {{Year|2E 542}} by [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] under the command of Durcorach, the "Black Drake". The invasion was a complete surprise, and was only barely repulsed after months of fighting, led by {{Lore Link|King Bergamot}} of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]] and Earl Emeric of {{Lore Link|Cumberland}}.{{ref|name=TATOAM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} In the aftermath, the kings of Daggerfall, Wayrest, [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]], and [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] signed the Covenant as a defensive pact against foreign threats.{{ref|name=GTTDC}} The kingdoms rebuilt and prospered, and Emeric ascended to the throne of Wayrest due to the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]].

===Greater Daggerfall Covenant===
[[File:ON-concept-IC Concept 02.jpg|thumb|left|Covenant style armors]]
:''"The Covenant represents the continuing ideals of Reman's Imperium, the uncontested height of Tamrielic civilization, when everyone, even the Orcs, were included in history's greatest confederation. Insofar as we seek to restore those lost glories, we deliberately hark back to them in our designs and symbolism."'' — [[Lore:Crafting Motif 26: Daggerfall Covenant Style|Crafting Motif 26: Daggerfall Covenant]]

As king, it was expected that Emeric would wed the daughter of King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]] of Shornhelm, but in {{Year|2E 566}} he suddenly married Princess [[Lore:Maraya|Maraya]], the Redguard daughter of King Fahara'jad.{{ref|name=TATOAM}} This strengthened diplomatic and trade relations between High Rock and Hammerfell, but it also enraged Ranser, who hired a host of mercenaries and stormed Wayrest. The city would have fallen, had not Emeric's shrewd diplomacy paid off.{{ref|name=TFOKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}} The other kingdoms of High Rock honored their earlier Covenant and came to his defense, while Fahara'jad, now an ally by marriage, sent elite troops across the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. These combined forces were enough to rout Ranser's army, who retreated only to find that their homeland had been sacked in their absence by the Orcs under King Kurog. Emeric had sent an emissary requesting their aid in exchange for a guarantee that Orsinium would be re-established as a sovereign territory.{{ref|name=TFOKR}} Ranser was utterly crushed, and this new arrangement of alliances formed the second Daggerfall Covenant, aiming to conquer Cyrodiil and succeed the Second Empire.{{ref|name=TATOAM}}
{{NewLeft}}
:→''For more detailed information, see: Daggerfall Covenant Campaigns section in the [[Lore:Three Banners War#Daggerfall Covenant Campaigns|Three Banners War]] article
In {{Year|2E 582}}, the Covenant invaded Cyrodiil, putting it in direct conflict with the Ebonheart Pact and the newly-formed Aldmeri Dominion. This led to open hostilities and the start of the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], with all three sides vying for control of the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-occupied [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. The Covenant also launched a simultaneous naval invasion of the Pact homeland, although it was unsuccessful. The Orcs of [[Lore:Betnikh|Betnikh]] joined the Covenant soon after. With much of its forces in Cyrodiil, the Covenant faced attacks from [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], necromancers, agents of [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s forces, and even an [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]], but managed to repel them all. It also faced political instability in [[Lore:Rivenspire|Rivenspire]] and a potential [[Lore:Crowns|Crown]] rebellion against Fahara'jad, and briefly flirted with total collapse following the death of Emeric, although the High King was promptly resurrected. Although it didn't take part directly, the Covenant sanctioned the invasion of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], which contributed to the ending of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]].{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In the autumn, civil war broke out in the region of [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]] between the kingdom of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] and several Orc clan chiefs who refused to acknowledge Kurog's rule. The violence ended with the death of Kurog, and [[Lore:Bazrag gro-Fharun|Bazrag gro-Fharun]] was crowned as king of a united Wrothgar. As Emeric's agreement was directly with Kurog and not the clan chiefs, Orsinium's continued membership of the Daggerfall Covenant became a matter of debate for Bazrag.{{ref|name=BazLLTK|[[Online:Chief Bazrag|King Bazrag]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Long Live the King|Long Live the King]]}}

===Tamriel Covenant===
Although their plan was never realized, the leaders of the Covenant intended to crown High King Emeric as Emperor of a third [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] and found a new Imperial dynasty after conquering the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]] of Cyrodiil and taking the Ruby Throne. With this achieved, the Covenant would have been in an ideal position to invade and conquer the other two alliances and proclaim the Tamriel Covenant. Under this new empire, the Royal Council would have been replaced by a Grand Council, with a seat for every kingdom in Tamriel.{{ref|name=THOTE}} This scenario was not to be, as the Daggerfall Covenant ultimately crumbled; the true [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] was proclaimed by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] in {{Year|3E 0}}, ending the Interregnum period.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}
