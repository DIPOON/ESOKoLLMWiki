# Lore:Mantling

{{Quote Box|''How do you think I learned my mystery? The Maruhkati Selectives showed us all the glories of the Dawn so that we might learn, simply: as above, so below.''|[[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] in ''[[Lore:Where Were You ... Dragon Broke|Where Were You When The Dragon Broke]]''}}
[[Lore:Mantling|Mantling]], also known as mantle-taking or [[Lore:Apotheosis|apotheosis]],{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} is the process of attaining godhood through the use of one or more of the esoteric methods known as the [[Lore:Walking Ways|Six Walking Ways]].{{ref|name=LOV6|{{cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 6}}}}{{ref|name=LOV32|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 32}}}}{{ref|name=NHOTSIT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Hatta of the Sphinxmoth Inquiry Tree|ns_base=General}}}} Summarized with the phrase "walk like them until they must walk like you",{{ref|name=NHOTSIT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Hatta of the Sphinxmoth Inquiry Tree|ns_base=General}}}} the concept of mantling entails the manipulation of [[Lore:Mythopoeia|mythopoeia]],{{ref|name=MWYagrum|[[MW:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} reenacting the Mythic patterns established by the [[Lore:Et'Ada|Et'Ada]] until their power is surrendered to the mantler.{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}}{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} In the process, the mortal and the [[Lore:Gods|deity]] become metaphysically synonymous with one another, allowing the mortal to claim the office and sphere of the mantled deity for themselves, reshaping them in the process.{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} This is explicitly juxtaposed with [[Lore:Reincarnation|Incarnation]], another kind of Apotheosis where the mortal is born with an existing divine mantle (as opposed to lacking one){{ref|name=MDC1|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 1}}}} and knowingly or unknowingly acts in the manner of the god they embody.{{ref|name=NHOTSIT|group=UOL|}}{{ref|name=FPgoodall|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Douglas Goodall's Posts}}}}

As the [[Lore:The  Towers|Tower]] touches all Mantles of Heaven,{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} mantling falls under the concept of the [[Lore:Psijic Endeavor|Psijic Endeavor]], which defines Divinity as attaining the Tower{{ref|name=VOFDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}} and suggests the end goal of mantling to be mantling the [[Lore:Godhead|Godhead]] itself.{{ref|name=BBWD|{{Cite Book|Black Book: Waking Dreams}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}}

==Known Examples==
*[[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] is a [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] with many associations with the concept of mantling. He himself speaks to the concept of different individuals encompassing his godhood.{{ref|name=SISheogorath|[[Shivering:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|''Oblivion: Shivering Isles'']]}}{{ref|name=SRSheogorath|[[Skyrim:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] interpret these events as Sheogorath being a dead god who has been replaced by something Other.{{ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}}
**His chamberlain [[Lore:Haskill|Haskill]] claimed to be a [[Lore:Vestige (creature)|Vestige]] of a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] man who mantled Sheogorath in the past.{{ref|name=CHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Chamberlain Haskill Answers Your Questions}}}}
**[[Lore:Arden-Sul|Arden-Sul]] was a Prophet who resided in the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]. He was considered the personification of Sheogorath's mortality by some denizens of the Isles (particularly [[Lore:Zealots|Zealots]]), seemingly attesting to Arden-Sul as one of his past mantlers.{{ref|name=Z|{{Cite Book|Zealotry}}}}
**The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] entered the Shivering Isles and became the champion of Sheogorath at the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{ref|name=SIDoor|Events of [[Shivering:A Door in Niben Bay|A Door in Niben Bay]] in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|''Oblivion: Shivering Isles'']]}} Sheogorath prepared them to succeed him as the new Prince of Madness and despite a setback that saw Sheogorath unable to give the Hero [[Lore:Staff of Sheogorath|his staff]], the Hero was able to create a new staff with the assistance of [[Lore:Dyus|Dyus]] and mantle the Madgod.{{ref|name=SISymbols|Events of [[Shivering:Symbols of Office|Symbols of Office]] in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|''Oblivion: Shivering Isles'']]}} The Hero of Kvatch also achieved something that other participants in the prior [[Lore:Greymarch|Greymarches]] had not; they defeated [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]] in combat and ended the Greymarch cycle, fully separating Jyggalag and Sheogorath from one another.{{ref|name=SIEnd|Events of [[Shivering:The End of Order|The End of Order]] in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|''Oblivion: Shivering Isles'']]}}{{ref|name=SIJyggalag|[[Shivering:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]]'s dialogue in [[Shivering:Shivering Isles|''Oblivion: Shivering Isles'']]}}
*The [[Lore:Wilderking|Wilderking]] was a deity residing in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].  There are said to have been many Wilderkings and Wilderqueens over the course of Valenwood's history,{{Ref|name=ONHen|[[ON:Henodras|Henodras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} though only two are known.{{Ref|name=ONMar|[[ON:Maruin|Maruin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
**[[Lore:Wilderking|Ostion]] was an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] who had the power to shape the land ever since he was a young boy. Powerful individuals in his homeland of [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset]] learned of his unique abilities and sent him to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]]. Ostion and Valenwood would become one and he was known henceforth as the Wilderking.{{Ref|name=SB|{{Cite Book|Sumiril's Book}}}}{{Ref|name=ONSumiril|[[ON:Sumiril|Sumiril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:A Tangled Knot|ESO]]}}
**[[Lore:Aranias|Aranias]] was an Altmer who, much like Ostion, displayed tremendous power over the land at a young age.{{ref|name=TWOS|Events of [[ON:The Witch of Silatar|The Witch of Silatar]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Originally being part of a contingency that was to assassinate the Wilderking, Aranias eventually realized her true destiny and replaced the dying Ostion's station, becoming the Wilderqueen.{{ref|name=TOTWK|Events of [[ON:Throne of the Wilderking|Throne of the Wilderking]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
*The Necromancer God [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] has attempted mantling twice:
** Mannimarco made a failed attempt to use the Amulet of Kings to soul trap [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] so he could mantle his station and take his place among the gods.{{ref|name=MannSoST|[[Online:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This plot proved successful in an [[Lore:Many Paths|alternative fate]], where Mannimarco took the mantle God of Schemes, ruling Nirn from a throne of bone and souls.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Traitor's Vault Temporal Tome}}}}
**Mannimarco would later on achieve apotheosis by finding his Mystery, "As Above, So Below,", attaining a Mantle from Above, bringing it Below. He claims this is how Apotheosis is done.{{ref|name=NM|{{Cite Book|Necromancer's Moon (book)}}}}{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}}

==Ambiguous Examples==
*The Missing God [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] and his various aspects have a strong association with mantling, tying back to the narrative that the Mundus was created as a means of transcending the divine:{{ref|name=VOFDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}}{{ref|name=VT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}}
**[[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] was an [[Lore:Ada|Ada]] with heavy ties to Lorkhan and his various aspects. He was referred to as the [[Lore:Shezarrine|Shezarrine]],{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}} and likened to [[Lore:Shor|Shor]]{{ref|name=TSOP4|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 4}}}} while also sharing physical characteristics with these figures such as having a gaping chest with [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|no heart]].{{ref|name=TSOP6|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 6}}}} It is thus possible Pelinal mantled this figure.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}
**Upon the mortal death of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], he was said to have ascended to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], transforming the face of the Divine, making the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight]] become [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine]].{{ref|name=OBMD|{{g|oblivion mobile}} [[OBMobile:Narrative|dialogue]]}}{{ref|name=TTM|{{Cite Book|The Talos Mistake}}}} According to some sources, Tiber Septim achieved this apotheosis by mantling Lorkhan through the Fourth Walking Way.{{ref|name=NHOTSIT|group=UOL|}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}
*The Dragon God [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] has numerous ties to mantling:
**When [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] shattered the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] and rose up as the avatar of Akatosh to banish [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] he was said to have achieved apotheosis,{{ref|name=OBLight|[[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on ''[[General:Decrypting the Elder Scrolls|Decrypting The Elder Scrolls]]'' — [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}} namely by mantling the God of Time himself.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} 
**The [[Lore:Ka Po' Tun (race)|Ka Po' Tun]] Emperor [[Lore:Tosh Raka|Tosh Raka]] managed to transform himself into a [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]], supposedly the largest one in the [[Lore:Nirn|world]].{{Ref|name=MA|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}} Some sources suggest this process actually involved Tosh Raka mantling Akatosh and becoming his rival mirror-brother of [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]].{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}}
**The Dragon God-King of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], is suggested to possibly be an attempted mantler of Akatosh, taking the role of his mirror-brother of the Nords.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]] claims Alduin tried to claim the Lordship that properly belongs to Akatosh.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|Dialogue with [[Skyrim:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]}}{{Ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} Similarly Khajiit myth claims [[Lore:Alduin|Alkhan]] ever hungers for [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]]'s Crown.{{Ref|name=TWaS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}
*The Yokudan Make-Way God [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]], bears connections to both mantling and incarnation:
**When [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus the Restless]] freed [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] from occupation by the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]],{{ref|name=Redguard|Events of ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} it was said he may have been a manifestation of the HoonDing, accidentally mantling the Spirit by fulfilling its' role.{{ref|name=VoF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} 
** Within the same conflict as Cyrus,{{ref|name=Redguard|Events of ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} it has been claimed [[Lore:A'tor|Prince A'tor]] also became a manifestation of the Hoonding, in the form of a [[Lore:Spirit Sword|sword]].{{ref|name=VoF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}}
*The [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]], also known as ALMSIVI, all have individual ties to mantling:
**Within the apocryphal events of his Trial, [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] claims ALMSIVI to have taken on mantles upon Apotheosis, springing from the basis of their [[Lore:Good Daedra|Anticipations]]. However Vivec also clarified the Tribunal never fully claimed the Mantles of the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]], due to the latter's unwillingness to fully relinquish their realms.{{ref|name=RPTrialOfVehk|group=UOL|[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 1: The Trial of Vivec|''The Trial of Vivec'']]}}
*The God of Life and Death [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] appears to demonstrate mantling or incarnation:
** According to High Rock myth, the god Arkay was originally a shopkeeper by the name of Ark'ay, who is said to have discovered a tome detailing the secrets of life, death, and the purpose of existence. After devoting his life to studying it, he ascended to godhood on his deathbed as Arkay, the God of Life and Death.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Ark'ay The God}}}} This myth is corroborated by Runil, an Arkayn priest in the Fourth Era who teaches that Arkay was once mortal and gave up that existence that mortals might better understand the vagaries of life and death.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Runil|Runil's]] Dialogue in {{g|SR}}}}

==Notes==
*The phrase "As Above, So Below" is often used by various sources to describe mantling and the duality of mortals and gods as a whole.{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}{{Ref|name=V6|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 6}}}}{{ref|name=CM|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 41: Celestial Style}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|}} This quote is a paraphrase of a [[wikipedia:As above, so below|lengthier verse from the Emerald Tablet]], a real-world esoteric text which describes the principles of hermetic alchemy, with the second verse in particular explicating the connection between the microcosm and the macrocosm.
*According to developer Ted Peterson, the myth of Ark'ay describes the god Arkay intentionally incarnating as a mortal in order to experience his sphere of Life and Death firsthand, before reascending to godhood to take his rightful place.{{ref|name=MUWRU|group=UOL|[[General:Made Up Word Round Up|Made Up Word Round Up]] thread — The Imperial Library}}
