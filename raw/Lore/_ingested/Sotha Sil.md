[[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], or '''Seht''', wizard-mystic god of the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] and '''"Si"''' in the [[Lore:Almsivi|AlmSiVi]], was the most enigmatic member of the divine [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]]. He was known as the '''Mainspring Ever-Wound''',{{ref|name=TTIS1|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 1}}}}{{ref|name=ESOTrivura|[[ON:Lector Trivura|Lector Trivura]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} the '''Father of Mysteries''', '''Magus''',{{ref|name=LG|{{Cite Book|The Living Gods}}}} the '''Magician''',{{ref|name=MWAlmalexia}} the '''Teacher''',{{ref|name=CoV|{{Cite Book|The Cantatas of Vivec}}}} the '''Sorcerer''', the '''Tinkerer''', the '''Clockwork God''', the '''Light of Knowledge''',{{ref|name=LG|{{Cite Book|The Living Gods}}}} the '''Inspiration of Craft and Sorcery''',{{ref|name=LG}} the '''God of the World Mechanism''',{{ref|name=LG}} the '''Clockwork King of the Three in One''',{{Ref|name=36L1|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 1}}}} the '''Watchmaker''',{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} patron of artificers and wizards,{{ref|name=LG}} and the architect of time and a binder of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=ONSOS|[[Online:Servant of Sotha Sil|Servant of Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He did not believe himself to be a god, but did not deprive [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] and [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] of their joy of being acknowledged as one.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} He also did not acknowledge free will, believing himself bound to creating his greatest invention, the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]]. Those that followed the faith of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] knew him for his power as well as the knowledge he provided, and for his contribution to helping the former [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] transition into their [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] identity.{{ref|name=LACW|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Clockwork City‎}}}} He also had faithful within the Clockwork City, where his [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] followed the [[Lore:The_Truth_in_Sequence|sermons of Deldrise Morvayn]] and devoted their work towards one goal: achieving Tamriel Final, a new [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=TUP|{{Cite Book|The Unseen Potential of Clockwork}}}}{{ref|name=TIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}} With the rise of the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], Sotha Sil and the rest of the Tribunal were demoted to "saints and heroes", and the three [[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]] retook their place as the center of worship for the Dunmer as "the Reclamations".{{ref|name=SRTR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}}{{ref|name=DBOthreloth|[[Skyrim:Elder Othreloth|Elder Othreloth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

Aside from his Clockwork City, another major part of Sotha Sil's legacy was the creation of the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] between himself and eight [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]]. Its terms are unknown to others, but it is believed it prevented the Daedric Princes from participating in mortal affairs directly,{{ref|name=ONAzura|[[Online:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} although it has been violated at times.{{ref|name=LACW}} He was the creator of a number of [[Lore:Artifacts|artifacts]], and of various entities such as the [[Lore:Factotum|factotums]] and [[Lore:Fabricants|fabricants]] that populated his realm.{{ref|name=TMoF|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Factotums}}}}{{ref|name=WitCC|[[General:What is the Clockwork City?|What is the Clockwork City?]] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} He also developed advanced metallic prosthesis which could replace one's limbs. Sotha Sil believed that "the truth of clockwork is for all", but his followers took measures to ensure his creations did not fall into the wrong hands.{{ref|name=LACW}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

Sotha Sil was able to make accurate and detailed predictions of how events would unfold long in advance through simulations. Sil's perception allowed him to predict events such as [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s attack on the Clockwork City, a rogue [[Lore:Clockwork Apostle|Clockwork Apostle]]'s attempt to seize the [[Lore:Clockwork Probabilis|Clockwork Probabilis]], the conversations he'd have with the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], and even his eventual death at the hands of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]].{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Sil|[[Legends:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Legends:Legends|''Legends'']]}}{{ref|name=PLJV2|{{Cite Book|Proctor Luciana's Journal, Vol 2}}}} Because of his knowledge, Sotha Sil saw himself as bearing the cruel weight of absolute, relentless, certainty. He viewed himself as the product of his circumstances, and believed that it was false to view people as the result of their choices. By his own account, he coveted the word "maybe" above all others. {{ref|name=Sotha Sil}} It is said that, in many respects, Sotha Sil's Clockwork City was Sotha Sil himself, thus aspects of the realm, such as what one would taste when eating, were shaped by his will.{{ref|name=twitch2|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live Lore of the Clockwork City|Lore of the Clockwork City]] on [[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}}

==Biography==
===Early Life===
[[File:ON-place-Ald Sotha.jpg|thumb|right|The ruins of Ald Sotha, Sotha Sil's home town (ESO)]]
Sotha Sil was born a Chimer in the town [[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]], in what is now known as the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], in the [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] region.{{ref|name=Saryoni|[[Morrowind:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Ald Sotha was the seat of [[Lore:House Sotha|House Sotha]], a minor house of the settled Chimer in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. He was known to have had a mother, whose name was lost to history, and a sister, [[Lore:Sotha Nall|Sotha Nall]].{{ref|name=ABHoAS|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of Ald Sotha}}}}{{ref|name=FSV|{{Cite Book|The Factotum's Secret Voice}}}}

Excerpts from his memories provide clues to the nature of his upbringing, such as that his family was composed of well-off nobles and that he was raised by a number of maternal figures including his lenient mother, rebellious sister, and kind nanny. The town of Ald Sotha is recounted as a tranquil place, with a fish pond, flower garden with many birds, artisanal kitchens, and many sunny days with activities involving pottery wheels, catching torchbugs in jars, and eating scrib jelly on rainy days.{{ref|name=ESOAmbient|[[Online:Sotha Nall (person)|Factotum]] [[Online:Factotum Dialogue|ambient dialogue]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} In one of his preserved memories, Sotha Sil's mother or nanny comforted a young Sil after he had sustained a knee abrasion, using the childhood nursery tale of the ''[[Lore:Brave Little Scrib|Brave Little Scrib]]'' to inspire him with courage, as his sister sang him a lullaby.{{ref|name=MPStars|[[Online:Star|Star]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Astronomer's Apprentice|The Astronomer's Apprentice]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} Another memory from his childhood involved him and his sister taking care of a dead bird by burying it in a handkerchief.{{ref|name=ESOAmbient}} A memory from his young adulthood involved his sister, Nall, trying to convince a reluctant Sil to leave their home outside of their permitted curfew.{{ref|name=MPStars}}

In the fable "Sotha Sil and the Scribs" from the ''[[Lore:Homilies of Blessed Almalexia|Homilies of Blessed Almalexia]]'', a young Sotha Sil was playing in a [[Lore:Kwama|Kwama]] egg mine as a child, and seeing a number of scribs in a deep shaft, he began to cast stones upon them for amusement, snickering as they skittered and scattered. One scrib lifted its head up and begged Sotha Sil to stop, telling him "Please, please, have mercy, little boy, for what is sport to you is suffering and death to us." when they were hit by the stones. This taught the young boy that what are idle amusements of one can be solemn tortures for another.{{ref|name=THoBA|{{Cite Book|Homilies of Blessed Almalexia}}}} Another homily titled "Sotha Sil and the Stars" tells of young Sotha laying down and gazing at the [[Lore:Magna Ge|stars]] at night. In his passion for maths, Sil decided to count every star and give each of them a name for hours on end, eventually falling asleep. When he awoke, he saw that the stars had vanished, and began to weep, learning the lesson that time cages all tasks.{{Ref|name=BAFfE|{{Cite Book|Blessed Almalexia's Fables for Evening}}}} These tales were evidently well-known in Morrowind as Nilvyn Omaren compared Dunmer slavery to the child-Seht who "makes sport of suffering".{{Ref|name=OTT|{{Cite Book|Omaren Trial Transcript|ns_base=ON}}}}

With unknown motives, the Daedric Prince [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] arrived in Ald Sotha during Sotha Sil's young adulthood and destroyed it, killing everyone in it including Sil's family.{{ref|name=FSV}}{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} It occurred at some point after he and his sister traveled to the forbidden area near the town, and took place upon a rainy morning. His family woke up to find their homes and tapestries already burning, and screamed in agony in their final moments.{{ref|name=ESOAmbient}} Sotha Sil was the sole survivor of the attack, having been saved by [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]].{{ref|name=Saryoni}}{{ref|name=ABHoAS}} House Sotha was wiped out save for Sotha Sil, and he continued to use their name.

[[File:MW-misc-Anniversary Art 2025 Crop.png|Sotha Sil among the First Council|thumb|left]]
The first sermon of [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|''The Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec'']] contradicts this claim,  placing Sotha Sil as older than Vivec. It tells of sometime before the [[Lore: First Empire of the Nords|Skyrim Captivity]], the [[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]] appearing to a [[Lore:Velothi|Velothi]] tribeswoman, the wife of a Chimer netchiman, who had been impregnated with an egg by the [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh]] before being brought to [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] by Sotha Sil. The egg contained the unborn Vivec, and seven [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] known as the {{Lore Link|Barons Who Move Like This}} appeared before the woman to teach the egg new swordsmanship motions. When Fa-Nuit-Hen appeared, he told the netchiman's wife to seek the [[Lore:Nerevar|Hortator]] in the land of [[Lore:Indoril|Indoril]] before combining with the Barons to form a terrible pillar of fighting styles and teaching Vivec.{{Ref|name=36L1}}  Fa-Nuit-Hen himself verifies the sermon's claims to a degree, stating that he did in fact meet Vivec in his youth, and made an impression on him.{{ref|name=ONFNH|[[Online:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

===Friendship with Nerevar and Advisor of the First Council===
[[File:GEN-misc-Foul Murder.png|The murder of Nerevar, with Sotha Sil on the far left, holding Nerevar's face|left|thumb]]
[[File:ON-npc-Sotha Sil and Nerevar.jpg|thumb|right|Sotha Sil and Nerevar (ESO)]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Your guidance and wisdom has been invaluable to me. Where would I be without you?'' [...] ''I'll stand beside you, my friend. Till the end.''|Nerevar to Sotha Sil}}

At the time [[Lore:Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]] and [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac Dwarfking]] repulsed the Nords in 1E 416, Sotha Sil was Nerevar's teacher and one of his closest advisers.{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} Nerevar's junior counselor and other teacher was Vivec, and Sotha Sil acted as a mentor for them both{{ref|name=TBORM}} By negotiation and compromise, Nerevar and Dumac somehow managed to preserve a fragile peace, prolonging the alliance brokered to fight the Nords. Sotha Sil, always doubted that such a peace might long survive given the bitter disputes between Chimer and Dwemer.{{ref|name=TBORM}}

Dumac Dwarfking gifted Sotha Sil a wing of [[Lore:Kemel-Ze|Kemel-Ze]] as a sign of the alliance between their two peoples.{{ref|name=Armas|[[Online:Thoryn Armas|Thoryn Armas]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Sotha Sil used this facility to do preliminary work on his lifelong project, the Clockwork City. There he studied the flora and fauna of Nirn to learn how to replicate their functions with metal and energy.{{ref|name=Protus|[[Online:Protus|Protus]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} 

At one point, he built [[Lore:The Precursor|the Precursor]], also called the "Oscillating Son", the first factotum, a mechanical construct heavily influenced by Dwemer. However, unlike Dwemer automatons, the Precursor possessed a rudimentary form of artificial intelligence. Various crystals, known as Integrals, gave the Precursor the knowledge of mathematics, the ability to use logic and reason and even the ability for self reflection.{{ref|name=ONMaker|[[Online:Precursor Maker|Construct's Integral of Calculus, Introspection and Reason]] items in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In Kemel-Ze, where he'd once studied with the Dwemer,{{ref|name=Thoryn|[[Online:Thoryn Armas|Thoryn Armas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} he used Mnemonic Fragments to store some of his memories in an early factotum called Protus. He sought to use the memories to create an artificial animus. But all he was able to achieve was the appearance of a fully developed soul,a convincing lie—artificial projection of himself.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}} Sil also experimented on the creation of the first Factotums and Fabricants, learning to differentiate between necessary flaws and those that could be improved upon, and how to counteract the inevitable degradation of his machines through self-repair. In this laboratory, he also created the Clockwork Probabilis to manipulate the Intrinsic forces that govern events on Nirn. Some of those forces are constant—like the force which compels objects to fall to the ground—while others defy prediction and categorization. The Clockwork Probabilis is a prototype, a precursor to the Throne Aligned, the control center of the future Clockwork City.{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}}{{ref|name=Protus}}
[[File:LG-cardart-Power_of_the_Almsivi_02.png|thumb|left|The Tribunal (Legends)]]
Sotha Sil participated in the War of the First Council, which started when [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Voryn Dagoth]] of [[Lore:House Dagoth|House Dagoth]] learned of a Dwemer scheme: deep in the bowels of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] on the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], the Dwemer had found the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]], and High Craftlord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]] planned to harness it and use it to power a giant [[Lore:Numidium|mechanical god]].{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} Lord Dagoth reported this to the Chimer leadership, and eventually, the Hortator Nerevar confirmed it with his patron [[Lore:Azura|Azura]]. Nerevar confronted his friend Dumac, and they quarreled bitterly. As a result, Nerevar led the Chimer to war in order to stop this "profane" usurpation.{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=NARM}} During the war's famed [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], Vivec asked Sotha Sil to come up with some plan to help stop the Dwemer's Automatons from slaughtering the Chimer.{{ref|name=MPStars}} Out of necessity, Sotha Sil created an army of Clockwork Dreugh that were inspired by Dwemeri war machines, to be used against the Dwemer. They were said to have risen up from the seas and took their counterparts to the water beneath to be swallowed by the sea.{{Ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}}

After Nerevar's death, the councilors of [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] and the Tribunal of Vivec, Almalexia, and Sotha Sil came to power among the Dunmer. They then went against Nerevar's dying wish by using the profane Tools of [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]] on the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] in order to steal its divine essence for themselves, becoming gods to the Dunmer people. The event led to the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] Azura cursing the Chimer, turning them all into the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]. As Azura rebuked the Tribunal, it was Sotha Sil who replied that her time as a god was over, and that the Tribunal as the new gods would be wise and caring of the needs of their people.{{ref|name=TBORM}} Sotha Sil was one of the few who can resist Azura's curse but chose to remain with his Dunmeri appearance.{{ref|name=MPStars}} He would aid in the transition of his people into the Dunmer, getting them to accept that it was not a curse but a blessing, and that they were new Mer, civilized and able to speak directly to their patrons, the Tribunal.{{ref|name=NARM}}

===The Clockwork God's Ambitions===
[[File:ON-misc-Chronometer of the Tribunal.png|thumb|left|The Tribunal represented as times of day]]
After the rise of the Tribunal at the Battle of Red Mountain in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], to achieve a balance between the material and spiritual world, Sotha Sil focused on mastering both magic and technology.{{ref|[[Merchandise:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Tarot Deck and Guidebook|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Tarot Deck and Guidebook]] Temperance Card, pg 41}} The Clockwork City was a magical and mechanical wonder created by him for various reasons, all of which were to serve a noble goal. He saw the Aedra's creation of Nirn as flawed and in order to perfect it,{{ref|name=twitch1|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/181985651 Lawrence Schick on Sotha Sil's Reason for creating the Clockwork City-Bethesda Twitch Stream], 13:30}} he built the Clockwork City to ensure the redemption of Tamriel, unify competing forces, and destroy the Daedra.{{ref|name=CD|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} During the genesis of the city, Sotha Sil's vision of a self-contained world made of brass and machinery was very ambitious and he developed highly advanced methods to see it through. He created the Celestiodrome, which surrounds the entire realm in the form of a glass globe  which is perceived by the realm's inhabitants as its sky. The Celestiodrome is capable of  mimicking both a day and night cycle,{{ref|name=ESOPVA|[[Online:Provost Varuni Arvel|Provost Varuni Arvel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} and the rotating girders installed on it allows for proper topography to be formed within the Realm.{{ref|name=MPStars}} In an effort to make the Clockwork City habitable, Sotha Sil sought to achieve the proper humidity needed for a breathable atmosphere for his realm.{{ref|name=MPStars}} He created the Halls of Regulation, which maintained the city's water cycle, breathable air, humidity, temperature, wind currents, as well as creating drinkable water.{{ref|name=TCCAG|[[General:The Clockwork City Adventure Guide|The Clockwork City Adventure Guide]] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}
[[File:LG-cardart-Sotha Sil's Blessing.png|thumb|right|Sotha Sil tinkering a Dwemer Automaton (Legends)]]
He drew inspiration from the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], and their influence in his work is rumored to be traceable as far back as before the disappearance of the Dwemer.{{ref|name=36L36}} Among these influences was his research on Dwemer tonal architecture. He refined it further and created items based on their research, such as tonal forks that could function as a divining rod.{{ref|name=ContFork|[[Online:Contraband D#Divaricated Tone Fork|Divaricated Tone Fork]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He also worked with Dwemer constructs, tinkering in ways beyond the capabilities of even the most talented individuals.{{ref|name=DivaythFyr|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|Appearance of [[Online:Refabricated Arquebus|Refabricated Arquebus]], [[Online:Refabricated Spider|Refabricated Spider]], [[Online:Refabricated Centurion|Refabricated Centurion]], and [[Online:Ordinated Protector|Ordinated Protector]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Sotha Sil created the humanoid automatons known as factotums to maintain the Clockwork City.{{ref|name=TMoF}} Early variants were built with a design that resembled [[Lore:Dwemer Animunculi|Dwarven automatons]] and were constructed from both Dwemer and clockwork parts.{{ref|name=ONPrec|[[Online:The Precursor (NPC)|The Precursor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} Ultimately his style became his own and the city's structures, vegetation, and wildlife are all mechanical constructs of his own design.{{ref|name=WitCC}}

Sotha Sil would go on and create part organic, part inorganic creatures known as fabricants, {{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 6}}}} which are rumored to be the first step towards the convergence of Nature and engineering.{{ref|name=WitCC}}

Sotha Sil would later become dissatisfied with the potency of regular [[Lore:Soul Gem|Soul Gems]] as power sources for his ambitious designs, and would seek to improve upon them. One such iteration was the Animus geode, but their uses were still limited.{{ref|name=MPStars}} In the case of his Factotums, he eventually came up with the animo core—a brass vessel that utilized a soul gem in some manner.{{ref|name=FurnAnimo|[[Online:Clockwork Furnishings#Energetic Animo Core|Energetic Animo Core]] furnishing item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Later, Sotha Sil would seek to uncover the secrets to everlasting life, and was the first to reverse engineer soul gems into Black Soul Gems.{{ref|[[ON:Asylum Sanctorium|Asylum Sanctorium]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} He used the aging [[Lore:Temple Saints|Temple Saints]], [[Lore:Felms|Felms]], [[Lore:Olms|Olms]], and [[Lore:Llothis|Llothis]] as test subjects, transferring their souls into Black Soul Gems, with Factotum bodies as vessel. Like others whose have transferred their souls into Factotum vessels, they went insane, causing the study of Black Soul Gems to be banned by the [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]].{{ref|name=Llandras|[[ON:Alienist Llandras|Alienist Llandras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

Towards the end of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], around {{Year|1E 2920}}, Sotha Sil spent some time on the Isle of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]], where he taught some of the new mages of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]].{{ref|name=2920v12|{{Cite Book|2920, Evening Star (v12)}}}} While there, Sotha Sil also further studied the Dwemer's creations, as well as further refined his clockwork creations.{{ref|name=ONPsijicBrassilisk|[[Online:Psijic Brassilisk|Psijic Brassilisk]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He left some of his creations behind when he departed back into the Clockwork City.{{ref|name=ESOCT|[[Online:Ceporah Tower#Sotha Sil's Study|Sotha Sil's Study]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOOriandra|[[Online:Oriandra|Oriandra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

===The Coldharbour Compact===
[[File:MER-art-Temperance.jpg|Sotha Sil the Clockwork God|thumb|right|200px]]
In response to the destruction of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] at the hands of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], Sotha Sil journeyed into [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] via the Dreaming Cavern and made a pact between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in {{Year|1E 2920}}, with eight of the more prominent Daedric Princes: Azura, [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], Mehrunes Dagon, Molag Bal, and [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]].{{ref|name=2920v4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} Known as the Coldharbour Compact, the exact terms of this agreement are unknown to the inhabitants of Nirn and Oblivion.{{ref|name=ONLyranth|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} It is theorized that it prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. It is also speculated that, in order to secure the agreement of the Princes, Sotha Sil either made a credible threat to them or presented a significant offer in exchange.{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}}{{ref|name=CD}} They were only permitted to commune with certain intermediaries, such as witches and sorcerers.{{ref|name=2920v4}}

Mehrunes Dagon's general attitude towards the Coldharbour Compact was that of indifference. To him, Sotha Sil was a mere mortal mechanist that was to be crushed like he did his childhood home, and no compact was to keep him from destroying Nirn.{{Ref|name=ONTWMD|{{Cite Book|Transcribed Words of Mehrunes Dagon}}}} This sentiment would manifest a few months later in Sun's Dusk of 1E 2920. The [[Lore:Skeffington Coven|Skeffington Coven]] of High Rock took in an exiled Dunmeri woman named [[Lore:Turala|Turala]], the former lover of the Duke of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], [[Lore:Brindisi Dorom|Brindisi Dorom]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Hearth Fire (v9)}}}} However, after a trip to [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], the Skeffington Coven was massacred by an [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] raiding party. Turala was falsely led to believe that the massacre was the work of assassins sent by Brindisi Dorom himself. Enraged, the witch called forth the Prince of Destruction to destroy Mournhold and slay her former lover, and he complied.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Frostfall (v10)}}}}

During the carnage, Sotha Sil arrived with Almalexia to Mournhold, and engaged him in the battlefield that was lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. Almalexia plunged [[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]] into Dagon's flesh and carved him from within. Meanwhile, Sotha Sil, enacting vengeance for Ald Sotha, used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Prince into submission. Wounded, Sil allegedly whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}}{{ref|name=2920v11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}} After his banishment, Sotha Sil sealed the planar doors between Nirn and the Deadlands, blocking off all access for Dagon as further punishment for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.{{ref|name=ONLyranth}}

===Second Era===
====The Triad====
[[File:ON-quest-Where Shadows Lie.jpg|left|thumb|Divayth Fyr and Proctor Luciana Pullo defend Sotha Sil (ESO)]][[File:ON-quest-Where Shadows Lie 2.PNG|The freed Sotha Sil banishes Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City|thumb|right]]
Around {{Year|2E 582}}, Nocturnal tried to take control of the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]] as part of [[Lore:The Triad|The Triad]]. Nocturnal's forces invaded the City and she summoned Sotha Sil's [[Lore:Shadow|Shadow]], who replaced him as ruler. Meanwhile, one of the Clockwork Apostles, Chancellor [[Lore:Gascone Dusant|Gascone Dusant]], sought to deliver the Skeleton Key, which had been accidentally found by a Factotum beneath the Brass Fortress, to [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], only for the Key to be stolen by the [[Lore:Blackfeather Court|Blackfeather Court]]. The [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], assisted by [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] and the Clockwork Apostles, unveiled the plot, but were unable to reclaim the Skeleton Key before it was taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow. Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempted to seize control of the Clockwork City by unlocking the Throne itself, but were stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr, and Proctor [[Lore:Luciana Pullo|Luciana Pullo]], who rescued Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow and repelling Nocturnal's darkness through a special light spell Sotha Sil had once taught Luciana. Once freed Sotha Sil banished Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City. Sotha Sil then gave the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping.{{ref|name=ESOCWC|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}}{{ref|name=ShadowSil|[[Online:Shadow of Sotha Sil|Shadow of Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Sotha Sil became involved directly in the affairs of the Nirn when [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] acquired the [[Lore:Heart of Transparent Law|Heart of Transparent Law]] and, with it, tried to take control of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]]. Nocturnal planned to use the Tower's properties of existing in every [[Lore:Planes of Existence|reality]] simultaneously, enhanced by the life force of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] which she'd drain through the use of a secret life draining technique she'd stolen from Sotha Sil's Clockwork City, to achieve omnipresence in the entire [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]]. In this manner she'd absorb the power of the other Daedric Princes as she invaded their [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]], thus gaining infinite power through the infinite connections made, and ultimately unravel reality itself to wait for the next one to arise. Nocturnal's shadow enveloped the Tower and transformed the [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] inside into shadows, while she attempted to repair Transparent Law by reconnecting it with its Heart, making the Tower whole to complete her plan.{{ref|name=ESOPO|[[Online:Ritemaster Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONLey|[[Online:Leythen|Leythen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At the same time, her forces started invading the realms of the other Princes.{{ref|name=Barbas|[[Online:Barbas|Barbas]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} However, her plans were foiled by the Vestige and the Psijic Order, assisted by the two betrayed Princes and the champion of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]. After Nocturnal's Earl was defeated, the Prince attempted to consume the Vestige and the Golden Knight with her shadows. It was than that the true nature of Meridia's champion as a living [[Lore:Light of Meridia|Light of Meridia]] manifested, allowing him to repel the darkness, and to restore Dawnbreaker by sacrificing himself. The Vestige than used [[Lore:Dawnbreaker|Dawnbreaker]] to restore Transparent Law as Sotha Sil had instructed, which banished Nocturnal, allowing the Heart to be returned to the Crystal Tower.{{ref|name=Darien|[[Online:Darien Gautier|Darien Gautier]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOSummerset|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}
====The Sealing of Kemel-Ze====
In 2E 582, [[Lore:Nilphas Drethan|Nilphas Drethan]], a rogue [[Lore:Clockwork Apostle|Clockwork Apostle]], colluded with the [[Lore:Painted Eye|Painted Eye]], a group of mercenaries that steal arcane items, allowing them to enter Kemel-Ze. Disillusioned with Sotha Sil, who he viewed as a fool for allowing an imperfect world like Nirn to keep existing, Drethan hoped to take control of the Clockwork Probabilis and use its power to make lasting changes to the reality of Nirn, believing that with it he could fix the world's flaws.{{ref|name=Protus}}{{ref|name=Logs|[[Online:Nilphas Drethan's Security Logs|Nilphas Drethan's Security Logs]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Memory|Events of[[Online:A Memory of Mystery|A Memory of Mystery]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Unbeknownst to Drethan, the Probabilis' prototype nature meant his plan was doomed to result in catastrophic failure that would've caused irreparable damage to the world. Though the rogue Apostle succeeded in gaining control of the device, he was slain by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], with the aid of Kemel-Ze's [[Lore:Factotum|Factotum]] overseer, Protus, before he could gain full control of the device's power and make any lasting change.{{ref|name=Memory|Events of[[Online:A Memory of Mystery|A Memory of Mystery]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Following Drethan's death, a "memory gestalt fascimile" of Sotha Sil, an attempt at creating an artificial [[Lore:Souls|soul]] of Sotha Sil out of Mnemonic Fragments containing his memories, revealed that the true Clockwork God had predicted all the events that had transpired. From the result of his experiments, to Drethan's attempt to take control of the Probabilis, and even the very discussion with the Vestige themselves. The fascimile of Sil explained that, though he knew the result of his experiments, he still needed to experiment and work backwards to truly understand them, and that he'd allowed the Probabilis to remain operational, despite the potential danger, because he'd predicted Drethan using it, so the linearity of time demanded that it ''could'' be activated, for Drethan to be ''able'' to use it. The Vestige might've accepted the reason given, or criticized Sil as irresponsible. Either way, a locking sequence, which Sotha Sil had pre-programmed Protus to initiate once the events involving Drethan concluded, was activated, locking Kemel-Ze and the Clockwork Probabilis away from the rest of Nirn.{{ref|name=Protus}}{{ref|name=SothaSil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Memory}}

====Awakening of Dagoth Ur====
[[File:LG-quest-The Heart, Revealed 02.jpg|The awakening of Dagoth Ur|thumb|right]]
In {{Year|2E 882}}, when the Tribunal visited Red Mountain for their annual pilgrimage to replenish their divine power, the reawakened Dagoth Ur and his minions ambushed them and drove them away after nearly killing them.{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} The event resulted in the Tribunal being cut off from access to the Heart of Lorkhan, and caused Sotha Sil to respond by beginning development of a replacement heart. His solution was the [[Lore:Mechanical Heart|Mechanical Heart]], a mechanical replication of the Heart of Lorkhan, that would act as a new source of divinity and power for his Clockwork City, powering it for all eternity.{{ref|name=TRMTCC|[[Legends:The Red Mountain|The Red Mountain]] quest in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} The development would not go smoothly, as The Heart became unstable in its early development, and to prevent the Heart from destroying the City, he created his own variant of [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Kagrenac's Tools]] to control, shape, or even dismantle the Heart if necessary. {{ref|name=SSRTCCTT|[[Legends:Sotha Sil (character)|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue during [[Legends:The Tools|The Tools]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} Sotha Sil went to great lengths to keep the Heart from those who would misuse it. He stored it within the Chamber of Lorkhan, located deep in the Clockwork City's [[Lore:Cogitum Centralis|Cogitum Centralis]]. Sotha Sil also hid his tools as a precaution across Nirn to prevent people from stealing the Heart's power.{{ref|name=GDTTARTCC|[[Legends:Galyn|Galyn]]'s dialogue during [[Legends:The Throne Aligned|The Throne Aligned]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

The Tribunal were able to kill [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] and his kin after this initial setback, but they were always revived by the Heart. To contain them, the Tribunal erected the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Ghostfence]] around Red Mountain.{{ref|name=MWVivec|[[Morrowind:Vivec (god)|Vivec]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Third Era===
[[File:TR-creature-Sotha Sil.jpg|thumb|left|Sotha Sil in death (Morrowind)]]
Sotha Sil once spent time counseling, guiding, and protecting mortals, loving them perhaps more than either Vivec or Almalexia.{{ref|name=MWAlmalexia}} He is said to have felt the stress of each of his people, but assigned them no more load than he knew they could bear.{{ref|name=ONSOS}} Sotha Sil's traumatizing childhood may be one of the first factors that contributed with his deteriorating mental health. Him being the only survivor of his House being destroyed by Mehrunes Dagon{{ref|name=ABHoAS}} may be why one of his goals was to destroy the daedra.{{ref|name=CD}} His ascension into godhood was accompanied by a silence described as coming from a place of grief and deep regret,{{ref|name=DivaythFyrCWC|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} evidently from his betrayal of his oath to Indoril Nerevar.{{ref|name=MPStars}} His lifework, the Clockwork City, was created to serve as a world-shaping device, but he felt sorrow from it being built on betrayal and untold horrors.{{ref|name=CD}} 

In time, Sotha Sil became detached from the real world. He gradually lost touch with the affairs of the Dunmer, the rest of the Tribunal, and eventually all of Tamriel.{{ref|name=MWHelas|[[Tribunal:Mehra Helas|Mehra Helas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} His obsession with perfecting his Clockwork City, and the fact that its functions are very dependent on his presence,{{ref|name=LucianaPullo|[[Online:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Luciana Pullo]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} was one of the contributing factors that would ultimately cause him to become distant, reclusive, and would lead his participation with mortal affairs to dwindle. His last known participation of mortal affairs was in {{Year|3E 417}}, when after centuries of becoming weaker and more desperate, Sotha Sil and Almalexia lost two of Kagrenac's Tools, Keening and Sunder, to Dagoth Ur's forces. Vivec rescued them, but they could not recover the stolen Tools and had to flee Red Mountain.{{ref|name=DUP}}

Before his total seclusion, despite predicting they would not meet again centuries prior,{{ref|name=CD}} Sotha Sil would have one of his final conversations with his peer Divayth Fyr, in which they discussed the disappearance of the Dwemer, his divine nature, and the location of his city among other things.{{ref|name=SSLW|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Sotha Sil's Last Words|ns_base=General}}}} Sotha Sil continued developing the Mechanical Heart, but its progress would be threatened as the day arrived, just as he predicted, where his friend Almalexia would come to kill him.{{ref|name=Aios|[[Online:Aios|Aios]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS|[[Legends:Sotha Sil (character)|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue during [[Legends:The Last Stand|The Last Stand]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} In {{Year|3E 427}}, the goddess [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], having been driven mad by her loss of immortality, manipulated the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] to retrieve [[Lore:Barilzar's Mazed Band|Barilzar's Mazed Band]], which she used to travel to the Clockwork City to murder Sotha Sil in cold blood. When Almalexia came face to face with Sotha Sil, he said not one word, making her think that even in death, Sotha Sil mocked her with silence.{{ref|name=MWAlmalexia|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} In reality, he was likely in another state of mind and was unable to respond, a result of being plugged into the control center within the Dome of Sotha Sil.{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS}} In his final moments, he attempted to use his control center to seal the Chamber of Lorkhan to prevent the Heart from falling into the wrong hands and to ensure the City would live on after his death. He was successful in sealing the Chamber and his machines began finishing the construction of the Mechanical Heart within it. 

After murdering Sotha Sil, Almalexia transported his fabricants into [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] to wreak havoc. She then tricked the Nerevarine into going to the Clockwork City to kill the late Sotha Sil, who she blamed for the attacks. Once there, she attempted to murder the Nerevarine as well, but her divine powers had waned, and as a result, she wasn't powerful enough and was struck down.{{ref|name=TRTMG|[[Tribunal:The Mad God|The Mad God]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

===Legacy===
[[File:LG-quest-Enter the City 01.jpg|thumb|left|The Clockwork City, as seen in the Fourth Era (Legends)]][[File:LO-misc-ALMSIVI.jpg|Depiction of Sotha Sil carrying his cosmic child in his 'womb' (right)|right|thumb]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:4em; margin-right:4em|''"I saw the imperfections in everything I ever attempted," said Seht, glancing at the map of Nirn. "Even imperfections in my pursuit to rid myself of them. Yet I could never stop tinkering. I could never stop creating. I loved her too much. I will give no instruction to you who have already come to know love."''|Sotha Sil}}

Azura claimed that her anticipation shed his mortality long ago, and that his death was likely no small relief to him.{{Ref|name=ADM|[[Morrowind:Azura|Azura]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The majority of the populace seemingly did not know of Almalexia's betrayal to her fellow tribune and would attribute the death of Sotha Sil to the Nerevarine.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}} When he came to say farewell to a mortal known as the Scribe, Sotha Sil claimed that after he was gone it is possible another who looked much like him may come to play his role in a new tale.{{ref|name=SSATS|{{Cite Book|Sotha Sil and the Scribe}}}} Much later, when the Provisional House was in ruin and the Tribunal faced its end, Vehk described Seht as having his belly swollen with his daughter, who was retroactively constructed and born to fulfill the task he could not. While Sotha Sil died at the hands of Almalexia, this [[Lore:Mnemoli|cosmic child]] survived him.{{Ref|name=9C|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{ref|name=36L|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}}{{ref|name=MKPosts|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} His body was retrieved by Vivec, who interred him and Almalexia in the proper Velothi fashion.{{ref|name=MKPosts|group=UOL}}

Sotha Sil's Clockwork City would continue to be powered by the Mechanical Heart after his death. The Mechanical Heart finished being fully constructed by his machines 207 years after his death in {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|name=SSRTCCTLS}} However, stories tell of a Forgotten Hero who followed a former [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostle]], [[Lore:Mecinar|Mecinar]], into the city to prevent the latter from claiming the Heart's power. The fate of the Clockwork City since is unclear- some stories suggest the City was destroyed alongside the Mechanical Heart,{{ref|name=LRTCCTCF|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue during [[Legends:The City Falls|The City Falls]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} while others suggest the hero preserved both the Heart and the City while still stopping Mecinar.{{ref|name=LRTCCKJE|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue during [[Legends:Journey's End|Journey's End]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=RCWC|Events of [[Legends:Return to Clockwork City|Return to Clockwork City]]}}

The Clockwork Apostles survived long after Sotha Sil's death, with a Dunmer known as [[Lore:Galyn|Galyn the Shelterer]] keeping the faith alive within the confines of the Clockwork City, but their fate was unknown after Mecinar's machinations.{{ref|name=GDBFMSRTCC|[[Legends:Galyn|Galyn the Shelterer]]'s dialogue in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} The Dunmer back in Tamriel itself returned to the veneration of Azura, now called one of the "[[Lore:Reclamations|Reclamations]]", and managed to make their way without their Clockwork God watching over them. Sotha Sil is still remembered and honored as one of the greater saints of the Dunmeri faith,{{ref|name=SRTR}} but not one who was ever supposed to be one of the cornerstones of the religion.{{ref|name=DBGaldrus|[[Skyrim:Galdrus Hlervu|Galdrus Hlervu]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

Despite the majority of the Dunmer people turning away from Tribunal worship, Sotha Sil was still revered by certain sects. These devotees were regarded as heretics by the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]] and were persecuted for their beliefs.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} One such cult constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]]. The cultists raised a giant statue to Almalexia and planned to use old propaganda to restore widespread Tribunal worship.{{ref|name=GotT|[[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal (quest)|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation Club]]''}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Her Word Against Theirs|Her Word Against Theirs]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation Club]]''}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was alerted to the cult's existence, although it is unknown if the Dragonborn aided the cult or destroyed it.{{ref|name=GotT}}

==The Truth In Sequence==
An interpretation of the teachings of Sotha Sil by the Clockwork Apostles existed within the text [[Lore:The Truth in Sequence|The Truth in Sequence]]. According to the text, the goal of Sotha Sil was the creation of a Second [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]—the Nirn-Ensuing—and a new Tamriel Final. This future world would be free of individual [[Lore:et'Ada|et'Ada]],{{Ref|name=TIS|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence}}}} and the Clockwork City would serve as the "[[Lore:The Towers|center of the wheel]]".{{ref|name=TUP|{{Cite Book|The Unseen Potential of Clockwork}}}} The Apostles denied the existence of [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], asserting that there is only the unity of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]], rendered imperfect in Nirn Extant by its division into "multitudes". The [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], they claimed, were "of the Nothing", a mere "lie creation tells itself". The Apostles noted that, of all the Daedra, only the "Gray Prince of Order" knew his nature, and "[[Lore:Sheogorath|went mad]] in the knowing".The Clockwork City would free Anu of these multitudes by forging unity through convergence, synthesizing [[Lore:Mer|mer]] and machine, nature and engineering, and the past and the future. To the Apostles, the greatest threat to this project was the legacy of the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], whose "ghosts ... cry out, 'Multitudes!'", and above all their "walking horror", the brass god [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]], described as an "Antipodal God-Thing" that "reigns over the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere", and the "welded knot at the center of Anu that must be untied".{{ref|name=TIS}} Indeed, in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the City's supervising Factotum Aios would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.{{ref|name=Aios}}
