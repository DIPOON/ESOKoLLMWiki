[[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] is the '''God of Worms''', the founder and object of worship of the [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]].{{ref|name=NM|{{Cite Book|Necromancer's Moon (book)}}}} Before his apotheosis he was an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] mage{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}} ([[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]], by his own account){{ref|name=WS|{{Cite Book|Worm Saga}}}} who had studied with the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]]. He eventually found his own path after he was exiled for practicing [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]], a discipline the Order deemed dangerous and heretical.{{ref|name=ESOHFU|Events of [[ON:Half-Formed Understandings|Half-Formed Understandings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=TBAOT|{{Cite Book|The Black Arts On Trial}}}} He became the most powerful necromancer that [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] has ever seen,{{ref|name=ONTPGOS|[[Online:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] Quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} becoming widely known as the '''King of Worms''' and head and archnecromancer{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}} of his often renamed Worm Cult. While the exact timing of his transformation into a [[Lore:Lich|lich]] is unknown, he is remembered in later records as the "world’s first of the undying liches," a designation of uncertain meaning.{{ref|name=MKOW|{{Cite Book|Mannimarco, King of Worms}}}}{{intnote|Lich|[nb 2]}} Through his political brilliance, his revival of necromancy from obscurity,{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai|{{Cite Book|EGT|Bangkorai}}}} and his ascension into godhood, he has left a nearly unparalleled impact on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]'s history.{{ref|name=NM}}

Mannimarco's early years are unclear. Should his claim to being an Aldmer be true, he may have lived since the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{ref|name=WS}} He was expelled from Artaeum for practicing necromancy around the year {{Year|2E 230}}, after which he gathered followers who formed the Order of the Black Worm.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}}{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=GTM|{{Cite Book|Galerion the Mystic}}}} His rivalry with [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] defined much of his known early history, especially as Mannimarco spread soul trapping and necromantic teachings throughout Tamriel.{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=WS}}{{ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} The [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] as well as [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] and his faithful were those he would come to consider his greatest enemies in life and undeath.{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=ATE|{{Cite Book|Arkay the Enemy}}}}{{ref|name=TCOA|{{Cite Book|The Consecrations of Arkay}}}}{{intnote|KW|[nb 1]}} A master manipulator, Mannimarco engaged in politics, gaining and maintaining influence by maneuvering through the courts of rising and falling empires.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} His most significant political achievement was wielding the true power behind the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] from the shadows during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|name=ONAbnurTharn|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CotFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}{{ref|name=AL|{{Cite Book|Anchorite's Log}}}}

During his attempt to attain godhood, Mannimarco suffered repeated failures, including the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], where he served [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] but was punished by him for attempting to usurp the Prince’s station.{{ref|name=MannSoST|Events of [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} These setbacks did not deter him, and his persistence finally bore fruit with his ascension during the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]].{{ref|name=Jta|King of Worms [[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius#The End|ending]] in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}  Despite his ascension, he remained directly physically active in Tamriel in some capacity until at least 3E 433, the last known death of his physical body.{{ref|name=CtK|[[OB:Confront the King|Confront the King]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} He ultimately accomplished his main goals: he slew Vanus Galerion, possibly even making a trophy out of his corpse,{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=OBM|[[Oblivion:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} and Mannimarco's divine body, the '''[[Lore:Necromancer's Moon|Necromancer's Moon]]''' (or '''the Revenant'''), thwarts Arkay by eclipsing his [[Lore:Arkay (planet)|eponymous planet]] every eight days, enabling the creation of [[Lore:Soul Gem|black soul gems]] for his followers to utilize for ensnaring the souls of the sapient.{{ref|name=NM}}{{ref|name=OBSR|[[OB:Shade of the Revenant|Shade of the Revenant]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBNM|[[OB:Necromancer's Moon|Necromancer's Moon]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==History==
Much of Mannimarco's early history is shrouded in mystery. Those who attempted to uncover it only caught fragments amidst rumors and speculation.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Paranoid and secretive, he kept even [[Lore:Wormblood|his nephew]] a secret from his closest allies; this relative abandoned his name and assumed a moniker to conceal his identity for Mannimarco's cause.{{ref|name=MNESO|{{Cite Book|Mannimarco's Notes}}}}{{Ref|name=MTC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Wormblood}}}} His origins are also further complicated by various contradictions. Mannimarco himself states that he is not an Altmer but is instead one of the Aldmer,{{ref|name=WS}} who were the original Merethic Era proto-mer.{{Ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} His biography claims he is the "world’s first of the undying liches,"{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{intnote|Lich|[nb 2]}} but if this were to be interpreted as him being the literal first lich, he would have to predate [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priest]]{{ref|name=ATD|{{Cite Book|Amongst the Draugr}}}}{{Ref|name=SRValdar|[[Skyrim:Valdar|Valdar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] liches.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Ayleid Lich|Ayleid Lich]] enemies in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Another source is even more extreme and claims that he may even also have been the first necromancer.{{ref|group=UOL|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8_cKUX8H8JM Youtube Stream of ''The Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online''], time stamp 2:42-3:40}}

===Expulsion from the Psijic Order===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Mage from infancy, blood-selected for magicka, descended from isles of Artaeum forever! Destined was I from long before birth to exceed all mortals.''|Excerpted from Worm Saga by Mannimarco}}
Mannimarco joined the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] in the early Second Era, where he met Vanus Galerion. The two quickly developed a relationship defined by both friendship and rivalry, born of mutual respect for each other’s genius. Vanus was spontaneous and intuitive, wielding magic as naturally as breathing, while Mannimarco possessed a talent for dissecting theories and exploring possibilities beyond the grasp of most mages. Their brilliance attracted followers who elevated them into positions of influence within the Order. The pair frequently debated various topics, captivating any Psijics who stopped to listen, and their debates on the secrets of magic helped establish principles that are still studied today.{{ref|name=FDESO|{{Cite Book|The First Dialogue of Mannimarco and Galerion}}}}{{ref|name=SDESO|{{Cite Book|The Second Dialogue of Mannimarco and Galerion}}}}

One such topic began as a debate about whether magicka truly exists but soon shifted into a discussion about the morality of magical alteration. Mannimarco argued that magicka does not exist as an external force and is simply the manifestation of a mage’s will. To challenge this view, Vanus transmogrified his robe to a bright green. Mannimarco remained unconvinced and explained that magicka is neither moral nor immoral, requires no consent, and is limited only by the will of the caster. Another debate focused on whether the Consecrations of Arkay apply to [[Lore:Soul Magic|Soul Magic]], which naturally brought the discussion to the nature of necromancy. Mannimarco repeated his belief that power itself has no intrinsic morality and that it is the mage who chooses to use magic for good or harmful purposes. He argued that necromancy is not inherently immoral, noting that other forms of magic, such as electromancy or cryomancy, can also be used for murder. Vanus disagreed and stated that necromancy is objectionable because it does not seek the consent of the deceased before forcing them into servitude. He added that necromancers often use deceptive methods to obtain consent or simply ignore the issue when circumstances allow.{{ref|name=FDESO}}{{ref|name=SDESO}}

[[File:ON-quest-Half-Formed Understandings.jpg|thumb|right|Mannimarco's first confrontation with Galerion on Artaeum]]
Mannimarco's curiosity with necromancy led him to spend much of his youth experimenting in the Traitor's Vault on Artaeum.{{ref|name=ESOEsulo|[[ON:Esulo|Esulo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The necromantic character of Mannimarco's research began to disturb his friend Vanus Galerion. After discovering an experiment Mannimarco had conducted on their own classmates, Galerion informed the Psijic Ritemaster [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]] of his activities, resulting in Mannimarco's banishment from the Order and departure for mainland Tamriel.{{ref|name=ESOHFU}} The Psijics' failure to suppress Mannimarco effectively, merely exiling him and leaving Tamriel to fall prey to his depredations, led Galerion to split from the Order and found the Mages Guild in {{Year|2E 230}}.{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{ref|name=ONAL|{{Cite Book|Artaeum Lost}}}}

===The Worm Cult Rises===
Seeking to normalize necromancy and gain a following to his discipline, Mannimarco created his [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]] to rival the ascendant Mages Guild.{{ref|name=TOTTBW|{{Cite Book|The Order of the Black Worm}}}}{{ref|name=WS}} and in the centuries that followed, the two organizations came to dominate the practice of magic.{{ref|name=SDESO}} At the time, necromancy had largely faded into obscurity and was regarded as a lost art, but Mannimarco’s meddling would soon revive it.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Mannimarco appealed to those in mourning, promising to help them contact or even reunite with their deceased loved ones. He also advocated against restrictions on spellcasting and offered a refuge for aspiring necromancers, who were often persecuted for seeking such knowledge.{{ref|name=WS}} Vanus believed Mannimarco was responsible for making the soul-trap spell common knowledge among mages, leading to strictures being introduced in the Mages Guild against the use of soul-trapping on sentient mortals. This resulted in the categorization of souls into the legal white souls of animals and beasts, and the black souls of fellow mortals.{{ref|name=GMoST|{{Cite Book|Guild Memo on Soul-Trapping}}}} In time, it became impossible for standard Soul Trap spells to capture a black soul within a regular soul gem, instead requiring the use of the more dangerous and elusive black soul gems.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Souls, Black and White}}}}
[[File:ON-item-Black Soul Gems.jpg|thumb|left|Black Soul Gems]]
Sometime after Mannimarco and his underlings' expulsion from Artaeum, one of his followers, [[Lore:Vastarie|Vastarie]], allied with him over the common goal of seeking "to trap souls as one might capture lesser Daedra". They believed their objective was to be found within [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], which Vastarie agreed to venture into herself. She would be the first to bring black soul gems from Coldharbour to Tamriel. They were perfect for their ambitions, as unlike other soul gems, they were "small, capable of containing even the most willful of souls, and apparently indestructible."{{Ref|name=TLV|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Vastarie}}}} Vastarie attempted to replicate black soul gems, but Mannimarco's aspirations would come to conflict with her own. Mannimarco desired to use them to gain power, while Vastarie desired to build upon them for ethical reasons. Fearing Mannimarco, Vastarie fled with her husband away from his clutches. Away from the King of Worms, Vastarie developed her own creation, the Sigil Geode, which was intended to be used to communicate with the dead, but Vanus rejected it out of disgust despite her good intentions.{{Ref|name=TLV}} 

Mannimarco later authored ''[[Lore:A World of Corpses|A World of Corpses]]'', a text that conveyed his pride in necromancy and his contempt for practitioners he deemed incompetent. The work indicated that inquiries regarding the acquisition of corpses provoked him to murderous anger, leading him to harvest the souls of those who posed such questions in painful fashion. He regarded these acts as doing "Tamriel a service by winnowing the herd of deficient mages." Tired of repeating himself, he finally set the lesson in writing, turning the book into both a warning and a guide. To him, the matter was obvious: corpses existed everywhere in Tamriel, scattered across its cities and fields by centuries of war and devastation.{{ref|name=AWC|{{Cite Book|A World of Corpses}}}}

Mannimarco had already achieved lichdom by the time his rival Vanus Galerion departed the Mages Guild in bitter disillusionment (by {{Year|2E 578}}), Vanus decrying it as “nothing more than an intricate morass of political infighting.”{{ref|name=MKOW}}{{Ref|name=ESOEGTS|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}}{{intnote|Lich|[nb 2]}} Mannimarco enhanced his powers by studying necromantic techniques preserved in the writings of the [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priests]] and the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], and by delving into the [[Lore:Crimson Book of Skulls|Crimson Book of Skulls]].{{ref|name=TOTTBW}} His followers gathered for him "cursed artifacts of yore", along with common [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemical]] such as oils, roots, and toadstools, as well as far more profane materials, including blood-tainted herbs, [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] poisons, dust from saints, and human skin, from which he is said to have extracted their power into his own being.{{ref|name=MKOW}} Mannimarco also dabbled in politics during the chaos of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. He consistently positioned himself within the courts of rising and collapsing empires, maintaining political relevance despite periods of diminished favor.{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Mannimarco consorted with daedra, most notably [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]].{{ref|name=TOTTBW}} Mannimarco became Molag Bal’s lieutenant, with the promise of becoming Nirn’s viceroy if he delivered the world to his master.{{ref|name=GoSMB|[[Online:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=IWSBAM|{{Cite Book|I was Summoned by a Mortal}}}}

===The Planemeld===
====The Soulburst====
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''In time, the name Molag Bal will be forgotten! In time, all of Tamriel will bow down before me! Soon, the God of Schemes will have a new name: Mannimarco!''|Mannimarco}}
[[File:LG-cardart-Soulburst 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Soulburst]]
Mannimarco provided counsel to Duke [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] of [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]] during the [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]] of {{Year|2E 576}}, a rebellion against Emperor [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]].{{ref|name=COTFC1|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}}{{ref|name=PQOTGC|{{Cite Book|Pirate Queen of the Gold Coast}}}} After Varen’s victory by right of conquest, he became emperor, and Mannimarco became his chief advisor.{{ref|name=COTFC1}} By {{Year|2E 578}}, Mannimarco had become one of the original [[Lore:Five Companions|Five Companions]], alongside [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]], [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]], [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]], and their leader Varen Aquilarios. Their mission was to locate the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] so they may utilize it to legitimize Varen’s claim as Emperor of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Mannimarco convinced Varen that although he was not [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]], which was a prerequisite for all true emperors at the time, slight alterations to the coronation ritual would allow them to relight the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] and convince [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] to gift Varen with the blood of a dragon.{{ref|name=COTFC1}}
[[File:ON-quest-The Harborage 04.jpg|thumb|right|Mannimarco initiates the Soulburst]]
In secret, however, Mannimarco had corrupted the ritual, inscribing glyphs and incantations designed to weaken the ancient barriers protecting Nirn and tear the veil between worlds, leaving the mortal realm adrift in Oblivion. By performing the ritual, Varen unknowingly broke the divine covenant of Akatosh and [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion, triggering the catastrophic event known as the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]].{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}{{ref|name=ESOMQEvents|[[Online:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Harborage (quest)|The Harborage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} This set the stage for the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], giving Molag Bal the chance to merge Nirn with his realm of Coldharbour and claim it permanently.{{ref|name=COTFC1}} Consumed by the fires of the Soulburst, Varen Aquilarios vanished and was officially declared dead.{{ref|name=COTFC1}}{{ref|name=EFEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}}

Prior to the Soulburst, Mannimarco was given refuge in [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]] by Ard [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]], during which time he and his Worm Cult operated out of the ruins of [[Lore:Nchuand-Zel|Nchuand-Zel]].{{ref|name=EGTBangkorai}} Following Emperor Varen's disappearance, Caddach expressed his gratitude to Mannimarco for the latter's role in his downfall, as Varen had usurped the [[Lore:Tamrielic Emperors|Imperial throne]] from his cousin Leovic. In return, he agreed to provide Mannimarco with land in the Reach for experimentation, granting him a sizeable portion of land in the hills near Markarth, accessible via a Great Lift within Nchuand-Zel. Mannimarco performed a ritual to meld this land with Coldharbour, the domain of his master, Molag Bal. However, the ritual failed; the land was sundered from Tamriel, but was not sent to Coldharbour, instead becoming lost in the Void.{{ref|name=ESOArd|[[ON:Ard Caddach|Ard Caddach]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:The End of Eternity|The End of Eternity]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} It was this sundered land which would become the realm of [[Lore:Grayhaven|Grayhaven]].{{ref|name=ESOTEoE|[[ON:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:The End of Eternity|The End of Eternity]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Following the Soulburst, Mannimarco and his cult began a flurry of political actions to gain authority. These interwoven plots had required years of preparation and, in his own words, "would make even [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] blush".{{ref|name=MtcFE|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Father Egnatius}}}} The King of Worms implicated Lyris and Sai Sahan in the emperor's supposed regicide. Lyris was captured by Mannimarco, while Abnur pledged his allegiance to Mannimarco in a bid to preserve the Empire. Sai Sahan fled with the Amulet of Kings, securing the artifact within the depths of [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]], where he hid it behind a ward of [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]].{{ref|name=COTFC1}} Mannimarco became the true power behind the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]], converting Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]] to worshipping Molag Bal, and manipulating her into expelling the Mages Guild from the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] so that his Worm Cult could operate freely.{{Ref|name=ONAbnurTharn}}{{ref|name=CotFC4}}{{ref|name=AL}} Abnur, Clivia's father, developed a deep hatred for Mannimarco after the Soulburst. Mannimarco’s suspicions of Abnur were further reinforced when Abnur asked whether the Amulet of Kings could theoretically be used to repair the damage caused by the Soulburst.{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}} Soon after, Abnur was brought to the [[Lore:Castle of the Worm|Castle of the Worm]] in Coldharbour, where he was imprisoned.{{ref|name=ONCastle|Events of [[Online:Castle of the Worm (quest)|Castle of the Worm]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Using his political leverage, Mannimarco went to great lengths to ensure necromancy dominated all other forms of magic. By framing the Mages Guild for the Soulburst, Mannimarco was able to install his Worm Cult under the name "Fellowship of Anchorites" in the [[Lore:Arcane University|Arcane University]] following the Guild’s exile. He then removed several of the strictures formerly imposed by the Mages Guild, with the notable result that necromancy was legalized.{{ref|name=CotFC4}}{{ref|name=CATAYQ2|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions 2}}}} Though Mannimarco was occupied with suppressing dissent,{{ref|name=CotFC4}}  the outbreak of the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] in {{Year|2E 580}} further destabilized Tamriel. The conflict, intensified by the Soulburst and the military aggression of the Empire of Cyrodiil under his puppet Empress Regent Clivia Tharn,{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}} created three powerful and competing factions seeking to overthrow Mannimarco's regime by capturing the Imperial City and installing their own emperor on the Ruby Throne.{{ref|name=Carlier|{{Cite Book|Lady Carlier on the topic of the Covenant}}}}{{ref|name=barracks|{{Cite Book|Posted outside barracks at Windhelm}}}} Mannimarco fueled the Imperial war machine by reanimating the bodies of fallen soldiers to wage war against his enemies.{{ref|name=GIESO2012|group=UOL|[[General:The Elder Scrolls Online: From Skyrim to Morrowind, and everything in between|''The Elder Scrolls Online: From Skyrim to Morrowind, and everything in between'']]}} Concurrently, Mannimarco's desire to employ black soul gems{{Ref|name=TLV}} was showcased on a massive scale. His Worm Cult appeared to possess a virtually unlimited supply,{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Aldimion's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}} and worked to smuggle black soul gems across the territories of the Three Alliances.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer}}{{rp|26}} Black soul gems became increasingly commonplace, to the point that many Tamrielic citizens believed them to be a recent invention.{{ref|name=CATAYQ2}}

====The Dark Anchors Fall====
[[File:LG-cardart-Dark Anchor.png|thumb|left|A Dark Anchor (Legends)]]
Though Mannimarco's meddling made it appear that Varen Aquilarios had perished in flames of the Soulburst,{{ref|name=COTFC1}} he survived. Varen vanished and later awoke near death and suffering from amnesia at the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Abbey of the Moth Priests]]. Believed to be a simple wanderer, he was given shelter and soon began studying the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]]. As he read, he recovered memories of his past and visions of Tamriel’s future. Word of these prophecies spread quickly, eventually reaching the Imperial City. When Mannimarco learned that Varen was revealing truths about the Soulburst and the events to come, he had him arrested for rumor-mongering and treason. The King of Worms ordered the abbey burned and delivered Varen into the hands of Molag Bal. After Varen's imprisonment in the Wailing Prison, his predictions became true almost immediately and the first [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] fell.{{ref|name=COTFC1}}

Mannimarco ordered the Worm Cult to place Dark Anchors throughout Tamriel, intending to utilize them to initiate the Planemeld.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer}}{{rp|22}}{{rp|38}}{{rp|42}}{{rp|43}} These devices were created in the [[Lore:Black Forge|Black Forge]] of Coldharbour and were capable of pulling portions of Nirn into Coldharbour while allowing Daedra to cross into Mundus.{{ref|name=PDA|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Projection of Vanus Galerion|Projection of Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Vanus Unleashed|Vanus Unleashed]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESODarkAnchor|[[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With each anchor placed, the barrier between worlds was further weakened.{{ref|name=PDA}} The Bosmer mage, [[Lore:Deslandra|Deslandra]], wishing to free Tamriel from the tragedies caused by magic and by extension, Daedra, infiltrated the Worm Cult to uncover their plans for the Dark Anchors.{{ref|name=BOTSEAB|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Limited Edition Art and Lore Book''}}{{rp|19}}{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Cyrodiil Gazetteer''}}{{rp|22}} This led to her epiphany; the moment that Mannimarco would launch his Planemeld, Deslandra hoped to disrupt it by breaking the chains of that which linked all Dark Anchors, resulting in a massive shockwave that would forever sever the connection between Nirn and its sources of Magicka. However, Deslandra's plans to sever the flow of magicka and its influences on Mundus could have unintended consequences like cutting off communication with deities, but she felt the benefits outweighed the potential fallout.{{ref|name=BOTSEAB}}{{rp|19}}{{ref|name=VWGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Valenwood Gazetteer''}}{{rp|38}} Fearing this, she was slain by the [[Lore:Hero of the Outer Watch|Hero of the Outer Watch]], inadvertently allowing Mannimarco to begin the Planemeld.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer}}{{rp|48,50,52,54}}

[[File:ON-trailer-The Confrontation 05.jpg|thumb|right|Mannimarco on the Ruby Throne (The Confrontation cinematic trailer)]]
Mannimarco then turned his attention to the destruction of the three major factions that engulfed Tamriel in the Three Banners War as they vied to take the Ruby Throne. ====Alternative Fate====
The Psijic Order had the means to scry secrets from [[Lore:Fate|fate]] lines beyond the bounds of Tamriel. Among these secrets were tomes that spoke of [[Lore:Many Paths|alternative timelines and realities]], where certain key events transpired differently.{{ref|[[ON:Tracker of Temporal Tomes|Tracker of Temporal Tomes]] achievement description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} In one of these alternate realities, Mannimarco defeated Molag Bal and his other enemies during the Planemeld. Mannimarco ruled over Nirn as a Daedric Prince atop a throne of bones and souls that he embedded into the sky over the Imperial City. With his victory, the world became a giant sepulcher, and the survivors of the Psijic Order even dabbled in necromancy to survive the undead onslaught that sought to purge them. With hope lost and their leader dead, the remnants of the Psijic Order attempted to perform a small-scale version of the ritual that made Artaeum vanish from Nirn, not knowing if it would work.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Traitor's Vault Temporal Tome}}}}

===The Reverse Planemeld===
====The Gift of Death====
[[File:LG-cardart-Zumog Phoom.png|thumb|left|Zumog Phoom with the Betrayer]]
With Mannimarco gone and the Planemeld ended, a few vestiges of his influence remained. Many of the remaining members of the Order of the Black Worm flocked to the side of the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]], [[Lore:Zumog Phoom|Zumog Phoom]], a former student of Mannimarco and a former ranking member of the order.{{Ref|name=MtC:ZP|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Zumog Phoom}}}}{{ref|name=ONEN|{{Cite Book|Euraxian Necromancers}}}} They aided the [[Lore:Euraxians|Euraxians]] of [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]], but Zumog Phoom was ultimately slain.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Cadwell the Betrayer (quest)|Cadwell the Betrayer]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

In truth, Mannimarco had set up a successor for himself to continue his work in the scenario that something should happen to him, one that was unknown to most, even those within his social circles. This person was Mannimarco's nephew, a character that had cut ties with their family and took on the moniker of [[Lore:Wormblood|Wormblood]].{{ref|name=MNESO}}{{Ref|name=MTC}} After Mannimarco’s fall, Wormblood stepped out of the shadows. Wormblood revived the Worm Cult and had Mannimarco's remains transported to the distant island of [[Lore:Solstice|Solstice]] with plans to resurrect him.{{ref|name=WJESO|{{Cite Book|Wormblood's Journal}}}} Under Wormblood, the Worm Cult once again allied themselves with Molag Bal's forces to enact the Reverse Planemeld, which would see Coldharbour dragged into Nirn if it succeeds.{{ref|name=NGESO|{{Cite Book|Note to Gabrielle}}}} This unholy union launched coordinated attacks on the Mages Guild and the Fighters Guild. These strikes revealed their plan: the cult had been planting devices known as [[Lore:Soul Reaper|Soul Reapers]] across Tamriel, intending to activate them to siphon souls on a massive scale.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Resurrection and Revelation}}}} In response, the guilds joined forces with the factions of the Three Banners War, forming an alliance known as the [[Lore:Stirk Fellowship|Stirk Fellowship]]. United by the threat, they set out to stop the cult.{{ref|[[ON:A Guild in Crisis|A Guild in Crisis]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Justice for the Fallen|Justice for the Fallen]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Their pursuit eventually led them to Solstice, where they hoped to defeat the remaining cultists and rescue Vanus Galerion, who had been taken by the enemy.

The Worm Cult’s attempt to resurrect Mannimarco partly failed when [[Lore:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]], acting guildmaster of the Mages Guild, transported both allies and enemies to the [[Lore:Colored Rooms|Colored Rooms]] to enact an ambush on the Worm Cult. The cult had originally planned to use the Gift of Death, a corrupted version of a [[Lore:Light of Meridia|Light of Meridia]], to bring Mannimarco back to life. However, before the confrontation, the Stirk Fellowship managed to steal the artifact from the Worm Cult and used it to lure them into the trap. During the battle within [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]’s realm, Gabrielle sacrificed herself to purify the corrupted Light and used it to resurrect the Golden Knight, [[Lore:Darien Gautier|Darien Gautier]]. Honoring her final wish, Darien aided the Stirk Fellowship in slaying the cultists and destroying their Soul Reapers. When Mannimarco realized the resurrection had failed, he enacted a contingency plan: he possessed Wormblood’s body and escaped from the Colored Rooms back to Nirn. In his new vessel, Mannimarco was unable to call upon his full power and Wormblood's consciousness was no more.{{ref|name=MNESO}}{{ref|name=WJESO}}{{Ref|name=ONRaz|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though Gabrielle was mourned, Mannimarco’s return and the arrival of the Stirk Fellowship’s main forces shifted their focus to breaching the [[Lore:Writhing Wall|Writhing Wall]] and advancing into eastern Solstice, where the Worm Cult was gathering for a final confrontation.{{ref|name=ESOTGD|[[Online:The Gift of Death|The Gift of Death]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

====The Wraithforge====
[[File:ON-npc-Darien Gautier (Light being stolen).jpg|thumb|right|Mannimarco inside Wormblood's body, absorbing Darien's light]]
On eastern Solstice, soul-sustained shackles drew Coldharbour toward Nirn, pulling like the [[Lore:Great Shackle|Great Shackle]], but in reverse and fueled instead by souls.{{Ref|name=ONWALKS|[[Online:Walks-in-Ash|Walks-in-Ash]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The wraithforge, a device capable of binding and deconstructing souls into immense energy, had once rested at [[Lore:Mor Naril|Mor Naril]]. Mannimarco made Mor Naril his base and now sought to restore the forge to the mortal realm.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Guardians of Mor Naril}}}} It was speculated that Mannimarco intended to use the wraithforge to restore his original body and continue the ambition he began with the original Planemeld: ascending into godhood.{{Ref|name=ONAzah|[[ON:Prince Azah|Prince Azah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} To power the forge, he refined the Soul Reaper’s design, creating enhanced instruments called Soul Flayers. They emitted necrotic energy that killed anything nearby while continuing their original task of harvesting souls, which were sent directly to the wraithforge.{{ref|name=MNESO}}{{ref|name=ONVR|{{Cite Book|Village Report|ns_base=Online}}}} Fortified against magic, including the light of Meridia,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Report on the Improved Reapers|ns_base=Online}}}} these devices were strategically positioned to prevent direct assault and hinder the Stirk Fellowship.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Soul Flayer Perimeter Plan|ns_base=Online}}}} The pieces had fallen into place for Mannimarco when he moved to bolster the Writhing Wall at Mortha Khevur. There, he was confronted by Darien Gautier and the Vestige. This proved to be a grave miscalculation. The King of Worms seized the opportunity to strip Darien’s body of its Light of Meridia. If Mannimarco succeeded in perverting it into a new Gift of Death, he could use it to reclaim his original body{{Ref|name=CTHESO|[[Online:Crossing the Wall|Crossing the Wall]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MNESO}} and extend the wraithforge’s range to bypass the ancient barriers erected around Solstice long ago by Meridian cultists.{{ref|name=MNESO}}{{ref|name=WJESO}}
[[File:ON-trailer-The Elder Scrolls Online - Seasons of the Worm Cult Part 2.jpg|thumb|left|Mannimarco wearing Wormblood's body, right before Mor Naril was sieged (center)]]
While the Worm Cult sought to recruit [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|House Corelanya]] Altmer sympathetic to their cultural roots in necromancy,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Join the Order of the Black Worm!|ns_base=Online}}}} Mannimarco aimed to raise a vast undead army to combat his enemies that breached the Writhing Wall. He attempted to harvest the energy of the imprisoned Vanus Galerion to animate countless undead at the Argonian necropolis of Xul-Haj.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Worm Cult Mine Orders|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Burial Site Orders|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|Events of [[Online:The Deep Tombs of Xul-Haj|The Deep Tombs of Xul-Haj]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} When his ritual was interrupted and Vanus was rescued, Mannimarco fled to Mor Naril, where he made a final push to complete his ascension to godhood. The Stirk Fellowship, unable to breach the Soul Flayers’ death aura, instead employed a [[Lore:Voskrona Guardian|Voskrona Guardian]] to disable them and advanced into Mannimarco’s lair. There, they discovered that he had not yet corrupted the Light of Meridia. Darien Gautier absorbed the Light, hoping to thwart Mannimarco’s resurrection. Despite this, Mannimarco’s body was already nearly restored, and with his proximity to the wraithforge, he was empowered and would rise again no matter how many times he was slain. Realizing ordinary attacks would fail, Darien called on the dormant power of Meridia within him, which took the form of a javelin of light for the Vestige to unleash in a final, decisive strike. With Mannimarco overwhelmed, vengeful spirits rose from the wraithforge and dragged him into the unknown, leaving the Stirk Fellowship victorious.{{ref|Events of [[Online:The Gates of Mor Naril (quest)|The Gates of Mor Naril]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TFD}}

[[File:ON-quest-The Final Dark 17.jpg|thumb|right|A defeated and impaled Mannimarco is dragged away by vengeful spirits]]
When the wraithforge fell, the souls Mannimarco had harvested were finally released from his grip. Gabrielle Benele’s spirit emerged in that moment, guiding the newly freed souls to their rightful place beyond.{{ref|[[Online:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Final Dark|The Final Dark]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Vanus Galerion believed that Mannimarco had only been banished, temporarily barred from meddling in mortal affairs. He suspected his rival’s soul lingered in Coldharbour, and that his fate was not for Vanus or this generation to decide. That destiny was in motion, though it had not yet come to pass.{{Ref|name=ONVanus|[[ON:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With the Worm Cult's defeat, the participants of the Stirk Fellowship largely went  their separate ways, with a few staying behind on Solstice to clean up the remnants of Mannimarco's forces.{{Ref|name=ONAzah}} Meanwhile, House Corelanya planned to join the Argonians in keeping watch over Mor Naril to keep it from falling to the hands of those like Mannimarco.{{Ref|[[ON:Regent Karinwe|Regent Karinwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Mages' and Necromancers' War===
[[File:RG-load-N'Gasta's Tower.jpg|thumb|left|N'Gasta, Sload Necromancer]]
Sometime after the known adventures of the [[Lore:Vestige|Vestige]], Mannimarco would escape from his imprisonment and return to Tamriel.{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Lawrence Schick's Posts|Lawrence Schick's Posts]] discussing why Vanus and Mannimarco never interact during [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} As early as {{Year|2E 864}}, the King of Worms was active near [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]]. It was he who gave the "hunting grounds" of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] to the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] necromancer [[Lore:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]].{{ref|name=Gasta|[[RG:N'Gasta|N'Gasta]]'s dialogue in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

At some point during the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]],{{intnote|Battle|[nb 3]}} Vanus Galerion headed north with an army of Mages and [[Lore:Lamp Knights|Lamp Knights]] to a cursed mountain path to settle his business with Mannimarco. Galerion and his army were met by an equally powerful army of necromancers on the mountain, and both sides were ready to do battle.{{ref|name=MKOW}} As the two armies clashed, fire, frost and lightning tore through the battlefield. Necromancers summoned waves of undead, only to have them shattered by the battlemages’ searing streams of light. His plan was to obtain the Totem so that he could command the Mantella to his side as it fell from [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], before it reached Numidium's chest. Using its power, he planned to leave his mortal frame, either to ascend to Aetherius{{ref|name=TTWGTT|[[Daggerfall:Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?|Totem, Totem, Who Gets the Totem?]] quest in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} or join the ranks of the [[Lore:gods|gods]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=Jta|}} To achieve this, Mannimarco embroiled himself in the court politics of the Iliac Bay. With the Agent's help, Mannimarco struck a bargain with [[Lore:Morgiah|Princess Morgiah]], who saw little future in [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] as others fought for succession. To secure the king's hand, she endeavored to give him that which he desired most—to speak again with a son who had died. Mannimarco held up his end of the bargain, and in return Morgiah promised to give him her "first", though exactly what this meant remains a mystery. Regardless, Morgiah and Reman were wed after a three-year engagement.{{ref|name=SFMorgiah}}

Concurrent with these events, the king of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], [[Lore:Gortwog gro-Nagorm|Gortwog gro-Nagorm]], sought dirt on [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]'s royal families.{{ref|name=10th|''[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary]]'', originally found at Elderscrolls.com}}{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|187}} He obtained a suppressed chapter of a [[Lore:The Real Barenziah, Part VI|book]], the contents of which would cause considerable embarrassment to Morgiah's mother, [[Lore:Barenziah|Queen Barenziah]].{{ref|name=DFBarenziah|[[Daggerfall:Barenziah's Book|Barenziah's Book]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Gortwog planned to publish it, but Mannimarco sent his necromancers in an attempt to steal the chapter from him so that he can use it for blackmail.{{ref|name=DFBarenziah|[[Daggerfall:Barenziah's Book|Barenziah's Book]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Ultimately, a somewhat edited version of the chapter was widely circulated.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 3}}}} Gortwog at one point approached Mannimarco for a consultation on what the Mantella is.{{ref|name=10th}}{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|187}} Gortwog had purchased a personal letter from Emperor Uriel Septim VII that was addressed to Queen [[Lore:Mynisera|Mynisera]] of Daggerfall,  which revealed the secret of the Totem of Tiber Septim.{{ref|name=DFUriel|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Emperor Uriel Septim VII's stolen letter]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} It is believed Gortwog had sold copies of the letter to the major powers of the Iliac Bay region, as the Orcs of Orsinium stood to gain much from the resulting conflict.{{ref|name=DFOrcish|[[Daggerfall:Orcish Emancipation|Orcish Emancipation]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

===The God of Worms===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''… I was here and there and here again, like the rest of the mortals during the Dragon Break. How do you think I learned my mystery? Mannimarco's Worm Cult was known to have had dealings with the Sload of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] as far back to the time of the Planemeld.{{ref|name=TOTTBW}} The Sload already revered the mortal King of Worms as a hero,{{ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} but it is said that Sload priests would speak with Mannimarco after his ascension into their new God of Worms.{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}}

Arkay's blessings serve to inhibit and thwart necromantic perversions, particularly Arkay's Law, which provides a corpse with unwavering protection from reanimation when it is bestowed properly via a ritual performed by followers of Arkay. Mannimarco directed his followers to undermine Arkay at every opportunity, hoping to one day eradicate his influence altogether.{{ref|name=ATE|{{Cite Book|Arkay the Enemy}}}}{{ref|name=TCOA}}{{intnote|KW|[nb 1]}} He was quoted in the book ''[[Lore:Where Were You ... Dragon Broke|Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?]]'', which while primarily about the Middle Dawn of the First Era, Mannimarco appeared to speak of it as being one and the same as the Warp in the West.{{ref|name=WWY}}

Various accounts of unconfirmed authenticity surround the activities of the recently ascended God of Worms. According to one account he was present for the apocryphal events of [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]’s trial at Hogithum Hall in the Imperial City, which he was said to have attended for his own amusement.{{ref|name=RPTrialOfVehk|group=UOL|[[General:Gary Noonan|Gary Noonan]]'s posts in [[General:Trial of Vivec|''The Trial of Vivec'']]}} According to another he participated in a large conflict referred to as the "War of the Wormgod", but was thwarted by a group of adventurers.{{ref|name=TPWaroftheWormgod|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP/War of the Wormgod|''War of the Wormgod'']]}}

===The Attack on the Mages Guild===
Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, Mannimarco returned to mortal affairs, seeking to destroy the Mages Guild and capture the soul of its Arch-Mage for study, as he had done repeatedly throughout history to uncover the secrets of their power.{{ref|name=OBM}}{{ref|name=NM}} The Arch-Mage at the time, [[Lore:Hannibal Traven|Hannibal Traven]], had banned necromancy shortly after his promotion in {{Year|3E 431}},{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}} driving many disillusioned guild mages to join Mannimarco. His followers soon began openly attacking the Guild, with Mannimarco himself taking part in the destruction of the [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] guildhall.{{ref|name=Plot|Events of [[Oblivion:A Plot Revealed|A Plot Revealed]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} The Council of Mages became fragmented in its response, with two factions independently attempting to "fight fire with fire" by studying Mannimarco's artifacts in the hope of discovering a means to oppose him.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Hannibal Traven|Hannibal Traven]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=OBPG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|162}} During this period, two of Mannimarco’s own relics, the [[Lore:Bloodworm Helm|Bloodworm Helm]] and the [[Lore:Necromancer's Amulet|Necromancer’s Amulet]], were swiped from the Arcane University by these rival groups, even as the Worm Cult sought to reclaim them. Caranya, who took possession of the Amulet, was secretly Mannimarco’s agent and intended to return it to him so he can become nigh unstoppable.{{Ref|name=OBNA|[[Oblivion:The Necromancer's Amulet|The Necromancer's Amulet]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Helm was taken by Irlav Jarol for study, but he was killed by an alliance between Daedra and the Worm Cult.{{Ref|name=OBBH|[[Oblivion:The Bloodworm Helm|The Bloodworm Helm]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=OBPG}}{{rp|162}}{{rp|163}} To delay Mannimarco's plans, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] recovered both artifacts,{{Ref|name=OBNA}}{{Ref|name=OBBH}} along with a unique colossal black soul gem created by the necromancers.{{ref|name=OBA|[[Oblivion:Ambush|Ambush]] quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} In a final attempt to save the Guild, Traven sacrificed himself by binding his [[Lore:Souls|soul]] to the gem, enabling the Hero to confront Mannimarco. Shielded from Mannimarco’s enthrallment magic by the filled soul gem, the Hero slew him in [[Lore:Echo Cave|Echo Cave]].{{ref|name=CtK}}

The death of Mannimarco's mortal body had no effect on the existence of the Necromancer's Moon. The Shade of the Revenant continued to shine down on Nirn after his death,{{ref|name=OBSR}} and necromancers continued to offer [[Lore:Soul|soul]]s to the Revenant in order to darken soul gems well into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|[[SR:Hob's Fall Cave|Hob's Fall Cave]] dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Sometime in the early Fourth Era, a man named [[Lore:Julius Primus|Julius Primus]] began calling himself the new King of Worms but was eventually put down by the [[Lore:Penitus Oculatus|Penitus Oculatus]].{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|Part 2, Chapter 6}}

==Servants==
:→''For more detailed information, see: [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]]
[[File:ON-crown store-Worm Cult Style.jpg|thumb|left|Worm Cultists]]
The minions of Mannimarco vary greatly. In life and undeath, he recruited into his Worm Cult members from every social class, from outcasts to powerful nobles.{{ref|name=TOTTBW}} One offshoot of the Worm Cult, the [[Lore:Stonefire Cult|Stonefire Cult]], was led by Javad Tharn who dedicated its efforts to Molag Bal during the Planemeld.{{ref|name=ONKazirra|[[Online:Kazirra|Kazirra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|[[Online:Stonefire Machinations|Stonefire Machinations]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Mannimarco’s power also extended to other necromantic groups, offering support to cults such as the [[Lore:Withered Hand|Withered Hand]] of the Alik’r.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Sacrilege and Mayhem in the Alik'r}}}} Mannimarco was also the liege-lord of famed Sload necromancer N'gasta.{{ref|name=Gasta}}

The dead were recruited to fulfill his goals, bent to serve his will via necromancy.{{ref|name=AWC}} Some of the most fearsome undead servants Mannimarco raised included the skeletal dragons Baelthon and Krahnoviir.{{Ref|name=TFD|Events of [[Online:The Final Dark|The Final Dark]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TPWaroftheWormgod|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP/War of the Wormgod|''War of the Wormgod'']]}} Mannimarco created the first [[Lore:Flesh Colossus|Flesh Colossus]] in secret when he was still training as a Psijic monk on Artaeum.{{ref|name=ONFirst|[[Online:The First Flesh Colossus|The First Flesh Colossus]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

After Mannimarco became the Necromancer's Moon, his Worm Cult waits for the Day of Reckoning in which Mannimarco would return to Nirn to reward his followers and punish those that stand in his way. Until then, his followers are to continue his work.{{ref|name=NM}} While members of Tamriel's many races joined his cause, his reach spread so widely that even Sload priests from [[Lore:Thras|Thras]], who traditionally rejected all forms of worship,{{ref|name=ALC|{{Cite Book|A Loathsome Civilization}}}} came to revere him as a god after his ascension.{{Ref|name=PoM}}
