[[Lore:Marukh|The Prophet Marukh]] (sometimes spelled '''Maruhk'''; also called '''Marukh the [[Lore:Sight|Seer]]''',{{ref|[[Online:Treasures SA-SI#Sacred Inkwell of Marukh the Seer|Sacred Inkwell of Marukh the Seer]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Prophet-Most-Simian''', and '''Monkey Prophet Maruhk'''){{ref|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}} was a [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] [[Lore:Imga|Imga]] prophet who rose to prominence during the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] and became especially esteemed among the peoples of [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]]n [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Marukh's teachings sparked the massive cultural and spiritual movement known as the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]], a theocracy that transformed the Empire and the continent. He also wrote the [[Lore:Alessian Doctrines|Alessian Doctrines]], which provided the basis of law during the Order's rule of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]].{{ref|name=TFL|{{Cite Book|The Final Lesson}}}}{{ref|name=TIOD|{{Cite Book|The Illusion of Death}}}} Marukh is credited with the credo "all are guilty until they have proven themselves innocent", which went on to become the basis for the [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]ic legal system.{{ref|name=LB|{{Cite Book|Legal Basics}}}}

Marukh's teachings both brought identity to [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], codifying the pantheon most civilized Tamriellians worship to this day, and brought conflict due to the more severe strictures he espoused. His teachings began to question the validity of Elven rule after speaking to the "Enlightened One", Saint Alessia, in a vision. These sentiments led to an increasingly abstract and unknowable depiction of a [[Lore:The One|Single God]].{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} They heavily influenced the Alessian Order and their secret sect, the [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selective]]. The priesthood of Marukh saw no difference between spiritual and political matters. This was the religion of the Alessian Empire, and it taught that to resist the Emperor was to resist the Gods.{{ref|name=RTR|{{Cite Book|Rislav The Righteous}}}}

As Marukh's teachings began to spread, hostility grew between Camoran Dynasty and the Empire. One of Marukh's chief supporters, the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic High King, [[Lore:Borgas|Borgas]], traveled to Cyrodiil to urge a joint war against the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]], but fell victim of Valenwood's infamous [[Lore:Wild Hunt|Wild Hunt]]. This ultimately led to the eventual dissolution of the Alessian Reform of Marukh in Valenwood.{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}}<noinclude>
