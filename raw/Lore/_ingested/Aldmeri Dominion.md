# Lore:Aldmeri Dominion

<noinclude>{{Lore Factions Trail}}

The [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] refers to any of at least three separate [[Lore:Empires|empires]] established by the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] on the continent of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] over the course of its history. The term "Aldmeri" is literally translated as "Home of the Elves". While the justifications have differed, each incarnation of the Dominion has sought the same objective: to reassert Elven dominance over Tamriel.<noinclude>

==First Aldmeri Dominion==
[[File:LG-cardart-Ayrenn, Dominion Queen.jpg|left|thumb|Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri, queen of the First Aldmeri Dominion]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Only the Aldmeri—the High Elves and their noble allies, the Wood Elves and Cat-Men—have the wisdom and restraint to peaceably rule the disparate peoples of Tamriel. Though we are reluctant to take up this burden, events have shown that we must. Recent events prove that the Dragon Break was not a unique event. Men always follow the destructive path of their defender and apologist, the Missing God whom we shall not name. This ends here. Once again, Elves shall rule Tamriel from White-Gold Tower... this time, forever. The world has gone wrong, and we must put it right. The original Aldmeri Dominion, also known as the First Aldmeri Dominion, was an empire founded by [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] in {{year|2E 580}}, in the midst of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}}{{Ref|name=AUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn – The Unforeseen Queen}}}}{{Ref|name=ESOKB|{{Cite Book|Elder Scrolls Online - Knowledge Base|ns_base=GEN}}}} It was conceived after Ayrenn assumed the [[Lore:Kingdom of Alinor|throne of Alinor]]. Having traveled Tamriel extensively before returning to the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], she was aware of the dire threats menacing the world and saw it as the proper duty of the Elves to put things right.{{Ref|name=AUQ}} At the same time, the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] were in the midst of a civil war and the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] were facing an infectious plague, and they both petitioned the Summerset Isles for aid. Consequently, the Summerset Isles, [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]], and [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] signed the {{Lore Link|Elden Accord}} and joined together to form the Aldmeri Dominion, with the Summerset Isles' intervention halting the Blacksap Rebellion and helping defeat the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]]. 

By the end of 2E 580, the Dominion had entered the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]]{{ref|name=TCC}} in an attempt to claim [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s Ruby Throne and control over all of Tamriel, as well as to prevent their rival alliances, the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], from doing the same. The First Aldmeri Dominion's ultimate goal was to usher in a new age of Elven rule across Tamriel in order to protect it from the reckless tendencies of the younger races, particularly those of Men, who they saw as shortsighted due to their brief lives.{{ref|name=WTNAI}} This goal was never accomplished, and the Dominion eventually dissolved before the end of the Interregnum.

==Second Aldmeri Dominion==
[[File:LO-misc-Aldmeri-Dominion.jpeg|200px|right]]

The Second Aldmeri Dominion was formed in {{year|2E 830}}{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}{{Ref|name=ESOKB|{{Cite Book|Elder Scrolls Online - Knowledge Base|ns_base=GEN}}}} when the Summerset Isles conquered Valenwood over a dynastic dispute.{{intnote|PGE|[nb 1]}} When the heirs of the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]] sought to capture Valenwood's throne, a faction of Bosmer attempted to make peace with their enemies in the [[Lore:Colovian Estates|Colovian Estates]] by offering part of the Valenwood territory in exchange for the faction's own claimant. The Altmer, upon hearing of this, invaded Valenwood, citing a stewardship clause in a thousand-year-old treaty between their nations. The Altmer established the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] as the new government in Valenwood on behalf of their claimant [[Lore:Camoran Anaxemes|Camoran Anaxemes]], whose ancestors had made the treaty. Since the Interregnum still gripped Tamriel, the Colovian armies were easily forced back by the Elves and the Second Aldmeri Dominion was born. Over the next several years, the Thalmor strengthened their hold on Valenwood and Bosmer tribes continued to skirmish with the Colovian Estates.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} The Elves also made allies with some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]], and are believed to have made a treaty with the Elsweyr Confederacy.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}{{Ref|name=PGE1EL|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}

Meanwhile, Tiber Septim rose to power in Cyrodiil and began his conquest of Tamriel. The nascent [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] expanded and encroached on Dominion territory, forcing the Bosmer attacks to subside. Despite their military setbacks on land, at sea the Elven forces were considerably more powerful and steadfastly held the southern waters from the [[Lore:Cape of the Blue Divide|Cape of the Blue Divide]] to the [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]]. Although there was no formal declaration of war between the Dominion and the Empire, Tamriel had become divided between them in an unfolding clash of civilizations.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}} The catastrophic use of the [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] ended the long confrontation in {{Year|2E 896}}, resulting in the surrender of the Summerset Isles and the human conquest of Tamriel.{{Ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} The Second Aldmeri Dominion would represent the last time for centuries that the Elves of Tamriel ruled sovereign kingdoms on the continent, the legacy of the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] and [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] holdings.

==Third Aldmeri Dominion==
[[File:LG-quest-Fugitives Dominion Guards.png|Dominion Guards|left|thumb]]
The [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] that ended the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] and weakened the Empire provided the opportunity for the Third Aldmeri Dominion to form. However, some historians of elven affairs contend that the group is more accurately named the Fourth Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=ESOKB}} The Thalmor, by then a marginalized political faction that espoused Elven superiority, painted themselves as the saviors of the Summerset Isles during the Crisis. The Altmer public believed these claims, and with the Empire too disarrayed to reassert its authority, the Thalmor's popularity swelled. In {{Year|4E 22}}, the Thalmor solidified their position and seized total control of the Summerset Isles. Seven years later, a Thalmor-backed coup overthrew Valenwood's government, routing Imperial forces and their Bosmer supporters. Alinor and Valenwood then proclaimed a union, and the Aldmeri Dominion was reborn.

For the next 70 years all contact between the Dominion and the Empire was severed, leading to speculation of civil unrest in Alinor. Whatever the cause of this silence, it ended in {{Year|4E 100}} with the abatement of the [[Lore:Void Nights|Void Nights]]: the Thalmor declared they had caused the return of [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]]. This earned them great favor in the former Imperial province of Elsweyr; the Khajiit in particular had suffered during the moons' absence. Under Thalmor influence, Elsweyr split into the kingdoms of [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] and [[Lore:Pelletine|Pelletine]] in {{Year|4E 115}}, which subsequently became client states of the Dominion.

As the Dominion grew, so did tensions between it and the Empire. In {{Year|4E 171}}, the Thalmor invaded [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and Cyrodiil after Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]] rejected their ultimatum, opening the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]]. Initially seeking only to add Hammerfell to its territory, the Thalmor soon realized that the Empire was much weaker than they initially assumed and advanced deeper into Cyrodiil. The [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] fell in {{Year|4E 174}}, and Titus II escaped and joined [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial legions]] stationed in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where he plotted his counterattack. The Thalmor army in Cyrodiil was defeated in the [[Lore:Battle of the Red Ring|Battle of the Red Ring]] the following year. After the battle, however, Titus II acknowledged that the decimated Imperial legions were exhausted and unable to continue the war against the Dominion, so he decided to agree to the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]]. This peace treaty granted much the same concessions as were demanded by the Thalmor at the beginning of the war, but nonetheless the treaty was signed,{{ref|name=TGW}} as a continuation of the war would have resulted in Imperial defeat.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Hadvar|Hadvar]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Before the Storm|Before the Storm]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Ondolemar|Ondolemar]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Diplomatic Immunity|Diplomatic Immunity]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Delphine|Delphine]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:A Blade in the Dark|A Blade in the Dark]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Under the Concordat's terms, the Great War formally ended, [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] worship was banned within the Empire, the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] were disbanded, and a significant portion of southern Hammerfell was ceded to the Aldmeri Dominion. Angered by the terms, Hammerfell rejected the Concordat, forcing Titus II to renounce it as an Imperial province. The Thalmor continued fighting Hammerfell over the next five years, but signed the [[Lore:Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai|Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai]] and withdrew its forces from the region when the war reached a stalemate.{{Ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

By {{Year|4E 201}}, the Dominion and the Empire had settled into an uneasy peace. When Skyrim became embroiled in a [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|civil war]] between Imperial loyalists and secessionist [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]], the Dominion watched the conflict warily. A long civil war would keep both Skyrim and the Empire distracted and off-balance, but a victory by either faction would hinder the Dominion's plans, one way or another.{{ref|name=TDUS|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Skyrim|Thalmor Dossier: Ulfric Stormcloak}}}} It was believed that whatever the outcome, a second conflict between the Dominion and Empire was inevitable.
