# Lore:Dragonborn

A [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] (or '''Dovahkiin''' in [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragon Language]]) is a mortal blessed with the Blood and soul of a [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] by [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], the Father of Dragons and chief of the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Divines]].{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|[[Skyrim:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Some sources claim that Dragonborn are fragments of Akatosh's soul and share this status with Alduin and possibly all of Dragonkind.{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}} Those with the blessing have an extraordinary aptitude in the use of the [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]], being able to absorb knowledge of shouts directly from the souls of slain dragons.{{ref|name=SRUlfric|[[Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} This ability breeds fear and hatred in dragons, as the removal of their soul severs their immortality and renders them beyond the reach of any [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancy]].{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} The first known Dragonborn was [[Lore:Miraak|Miraak]], a [[Lore:Dragon Priest|dragon priest]] of the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] who used the thu'um in an unsuccessful revolt against his masters.{{ref|name=TGATT|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}} However, the title itself is most often associated with the "Dragonborn Emperors" of Tamriel, who were in a divine covenant with Akatosh which maintains the barriers between [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] and [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=TOSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}}<noinclude>

==Dragonborn Emperors==
[[File:MER-Jewelry-Amulet of Kings.jpg|right|thumb|100px|The [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]]]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''So long as the Blood of the Dragon runs strong in her rulers, the glory of the Empire shall extend in unbroken years.''|''from the liturgy of the Re-Kindling of the Dragonfires''{{ref|name=TOSA}}}}
Any subject of the Empire prior to the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] would say they were ruled by the "Dragonborn Emperors", but very few understood what that title meant.{{ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}} Its usage stretches back to [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]], who founded the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Cyrodilic Empire]] in a [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] covenant with Akatosh. The Dragon God imbued Alessia's line with blood from his own heart, initiating a sacred compact which kept the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] lit and Oblivion's influence barred from [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], so long as those of the Dragon Blood wore the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]].{{ref|name=TOSA}} Ever since, the legitimate rulers of the Empire have been Dragonborn: Alessia's successors, [[Lore:Reman|Reman Cyrodiil]] and [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|his heirs]], and finally the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]]. Indeed, the title is particularly associated with [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] himself, perhaps because he made notable use of the thu'um in his early conquests.{{ref|name=MWImperialCult|Generic [[Morrowind:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]] dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} Only these true rulers, approved by Akatosh, could wear the Amulet of Kings and light the Dragonfires.{{ref|name=TBOTD}} In addition, they enjoyed the protection and service of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], descended from the Akaviri Dragonguard, who follow the Dragonborn as "the ultimate dragonslayer",{{ref|name=SRDelphine|[[Skyrim:Delphine|Delphine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} as the "mortal representative of the Dragon Blood of the divine Talos."{{Ref|name=Jauffre}} The line of Dragonborn rulers was abruptly severed through assassination at the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], leading to the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] as the Dragonfires went out. Eventually, the [[Lore:Martin Septim|last]] of the Septim bloodline sacrificed himself and the Amulet to permanently restore the liminal barrier.{{ref|name=Oblivion|[[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The [[Lore:Mede Dynasty|Mede Dynasty]] that subsequently seized control of the Empire lacks the Dragonborn trait.

==Dragonborn Heroes==
[[File:ON-crown store-Dragon Warrior.jpg|thumb|right|Armor worn by ancient Dragonborn]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Dragonborn, Dragonborn, by his honor is sworn,<br>To keep evil forever at bay!<br>And the fiercest foes rout when they hear triumph's shout,<br>Dragonborn, for your blessing we pray!''|''[[Lore:Songs of Skyrim: Revised|Songs of Skyrim]]''}}
The Dragonborn plays a prominent role in the legends of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], who tell tales of heroes who would "kill dragons and steal their power".{{ref|name=SRGuard|[[Skyrim:Whiterun Guard|Whiterun Guard]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[Online:Dragon Warrior|Dragon Warrior]] costume description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} It is not known how many Dragonborn have arisen over the course of history. The [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]] of [[Lore:High Hrothgar|High Hrothgar]] warned that many of them met their downfall because the ease with which they learned the thu'um made them arrogant. The Greybeards attempt to inculcate the values of the [[Lore:Way of the Voice|Way of the Voice]], and strongly disapprove of the violent use of thu'um in general, but tolerate it among those who have been blessed by the gods.{{ref|name=SRArngeir|[[Skyrim:Arngeir|Arngeir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

With the return of the dragons in {{Year|4E 201}} came the emergence of the first [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Dragonborn hero]] since Tiber Septim to be summoned by the Greybeards for training. In the fulfillment of an ancient [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] prophecy, known as the [[Lore:Prophecy of the Dragonborn|Prophecy of the Dragonborn]], this Last Dragonborn learned to master the thu'um and defeated [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]].{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notes==
*Most Third Era scholars believed the term was first used in reference to Saint Alessia.{{ref|name=TBOTD}} However, Miraak, taking the title of "First Dragonborn", made himself known in the Fourth Era, disproving this.{{Ref|name=DBMiraak|[[Skyrim:Miraak (person)|Miraak]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}
*In response to confusion in the Bethesda Softworks Forums about the announcement of the [[Skyrim:Miraak (person)|first Dragonborn]] in the ''[[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]'' official add-on who was not St. Alessia, [[GEN:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]] said: ''"Alessia didn't have the power to absorb dragon souls. Hers was a much more nuanced power: to dream of liberty and give it a name and on her deathbed make Covenant with the Aka-Tusk. Perhaps if you had read her histories of the Dragon War, this would be more clear."''{{Ref|group=UOL|[http://forums.bethsoft.com/topic/1421186-/page__st__30__p__21748685#entry21748685 Forum post by Michael Kirkbride]}}
*In [https://archive.org/details/game-informer-issue-214-february-2011/page/56/mode/ ''Game Informer'', Issue 214]{{rp|57}}, [[GEN:Todd Howard|Todd Howard]] said: "The dragonborn can battle the dragons on another level. They're anointed by the gods. That's why they can light the dragonfires to become emperor. They kind of help make the world whole."
*Some Dragonborn are known to possess the gift of [[Lore:Sight|Sight]]. It was said that the dragon blood possessed by members of the Septim Dynasty allowed them to see more than lesser men.{{Ref|name=Baurus|[[OB:Baurus|Baurus]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] was troubled all his life by dreams that showed him visions of the future, including his assassination and the start of the Oblivion Crisis that followed.{{Ref|name=OBUrielSeptim|[[OB:Emperor Uriel Septim|Emperor Uriel Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
*According to some, the ancient Nord hero and king [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]] was a Dragonborn.{{ref|name=jbd|Dialogue with [[Skyrim:Balgruuf the Greater|Jarl Balgruuf the Greater]]}}{{ref|name=Nels|[[OB:Nels the Naughty|Nels the Naughty]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
* Following a transformative process, [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] claimed he could speak with another tongue and "[[Lore:Thu'um|speak fire]]". He was also capable of wearing the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]], which could only be worn by those said to have the 'Dragon Blood' in their veins.{{ref|name=MDC1|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 1}}}}{{Ref|name=DS|[[OB:Dagon Shrine|Dagon Shrine]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=Jauffre|[[OB:Jauffre|Jauffre]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
*The [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] was referred to as "Dragon-born" in ''[[Lore:The Lost Prophecy|The Lost Prophecy]]'' as a symbolic way of saying they were born under the banner of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]].
