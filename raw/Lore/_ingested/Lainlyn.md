[[Lore:Lainlyn|Lainlyn]] (formerly known as '''Tava's Blessing'''){{Ref|name=AUADC1|{{Cite Book|Ask Us Anything: Daggerfall Covenant (Part 1)|ns_base=GEN}}}} is the capital of the eponymous [[Lore:Lainlyn (barony)|barony]] in south-central [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]], in the province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. It is a minor port city occupied by the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]],{{Ref|name=AUADC1}} who normally hold territories in the north and east.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}} Lainlyn is most well-known for its shipyards, which have supplied vessels for the [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Kingdom of Sentinel]]'s royal navy and [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] in its heyday.{{Ref|name=SAF|{{Cite Book|Strakes and Futtocks}}}}{{Ref|name=ONMeriq|[[Online:Meriq|Meriq]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:DF-place-Lainlyn Palace.png|thumb|left|Lainlyn Palace]]
[[File:ON-place-Warehouse (Tava's Blessing).jpg|thumb|right|The central warehouse in Tava's Blessing]]
Lainlyn lies on the north coast of Hammerfell,{{Ref|name=DFLainlyn|[[Daggerfall:Lainlyn (city)|Lainlyn (city)]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONTava|[[Online:Tava's Blessing|Tava's Blessing]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} in the [[Lore:Hollow Wastes|Hollow Wastes]] region in the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]].{{intnote|Location|[Note 1]}} In the mid-Second Era, it was a small, open-aired town on the coast, built on two islands and separated by shallow rivers, surrounded by large ridged stones. The land consists of wide, sandy dunes and clustered [[Lore:Palm Tree|palm trees]]. Just to the south is Sep's Spine, a small mountain range and the abandoned [[Lore:Dwemer Ruins|Dwemer ruins]] of Rkulftzel.{{Ref|name=ONTava}} Though [[Lore:Harpy|harpies]] are a common nuisance in the Iliac Bay, they have a heavy presence around Lainlyn.{{Ref|name=Ghrae|{{Cite Book|Ghraewaj}}}}

Here, the region's northern roads meet each other at the center. Across a shallow river is the settlement's northern section, which contained a large warehouse, several ports and the Ansei Shrine, a statue dedicated to the Ansei warriors of old. The city streets are wide but mostly covered in sand and surrounded by small stone walls with fences. At its northern tip is the local lighthouse on a rocky island. It has several ports along its shoreline, where its shipwrights worked on large vessels.{{Ref|name=ONTava}} The city is not a regular port-of-call but is active with ship construction.{{Ref|name=ONJeromec|[[Online:Jeromec Lemal|Jeromec Lemal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the late Third Era, it was a walled city in the barony's eastern-half.{{Ref|name=DFLainlyn}} Lainlyn is neighbored by several settlements, such as [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] to the west and [[Lore:Vulnim Gate|Vulnim Gate]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – The Elder Scrolls: Arena]]}}

==History==
[[File:ON-npc-Ansei Maja.jpg|thumb|right|Ansei Maja's spirit]]
Before the Redguards settled here, the [[Lore:Direnni|elves]] and [[Lore:Proto-Breton|proto-Bretons]] of the Iliac Bay built outposts here to protect their fisherfolk and seafarers from [[Lore:Desert Orcs|Desert Orcs]].{{Ref|name=PGE3}} When the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] came here after {{Year|1E 808}}, these people and their settlements replaced.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}{{Ref|name=STJOA|{{Cite Book|Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r}}}} But the elven [[Lore:Necromancers|necromancers]] still lurked in the interior and retaliated by creating an army of [[Lore:Undead|undead]]. Because of the Redguards' sacred tradition regarding the dead, no one did anything to stop them. Three [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] warriors sacrificed their souls to forge the [[Lore:Ansei Wards|Ansei Wards]], weapons that could consecrate the dead in [[Lore:Tu'whacca|Tu'whacca]]'s name.{{Ref|name=EOTAW|{{Cite Book|Epode of the Ansei Wards}}}}{{Ref|name=ONFahara'jad|[[Online:King Fahara'jad|King Fahara'jad]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of these Ansei was Maja, who was born in Tava's Blessing.{{Ref|name=AS|{{Cite Book|Ansei Shrine}}}}

The traditional tale of Ghraewaj tells a story of how the people of Lainlyn were subjugated by a coven of [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]] worshippers led by [[Lore:Noctyr-a|Noctyr-a]]. Lainlyn Palace turned into a orgy-filled den of decadence and the Baron of Lainlyn was seduced by Noctyr-a. Before he was tricked into committing suicide, the Baroness appeared and gave Noctyr-a a beautiful white robe from her closet. When she puts on the robe, Noctyr-a is turned into a giant black [[Lore:Bird|bird]] and the Baron, no longer enchanted by her magic, kills her and orders the cook. After one of their orgies, the sisterhood dined on the cooked bird. When the Baroness revealed what had just happened, the coven screamed and cawed until they transformed into [[Lore:Harpy|harpies]].{{Ref|name=Ghrae}}

In {{Year|2E 102}}, Jamshald Lainlyn erected the Ansei Shrine in the town center, honoring the Ansei and commemorating Maja's birthplace.{{Ref|name=AS}} Tava's Blessing was at one point, a territory of Sentinel, under King [[Lore:Ramzi the Distrait|Ramzi the Distrait]]. At some point, the town was raided by [[Lore:Pirates|pirates]], even when the Sentinel Army was warned.{{Ref|name=ONGeinarre|[[Online:Geinarre|Geinarre]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Later, after Ramzi's successor, [[Lore:Fahara'jad|Fahara'jad]] joined the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] in {{Year|2E 567}}, Tava's Blessing followed suit and its shipyards were put to work to build Fahara'jad's royal navy.{{Ref|name=SAF}}{{Ref|name=ONMeriq}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, the [[Lore:Withered Hand|Withered Hand]] led by the [[Lore:Necromancers|necromancer]], [[Lore:Alasan|Alasan]] ransacked Tava's Blessing and killed its citizens. However, instead of resurrecting them, he planned to turn into a [[Lore:Lich|lich]], using one of the corrupted Ansei Wards as a focus to channel energy from the dead. Fearing that the Withered Hand would target the shipyards, King Fahara'jad sent his son, Prince [[Lore:Azah|Azah]] to protect them. By the time he arrived, it was already gone.{{Ref|name=ONAzah|[[Online:Prince Azah#Alasan's Plot|Prince Azah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]] arrived not long after and together, planned to disrupt Alasan's ritual by destroying his other focuses across town. At Alasan's ritual site, in the lighthouse, the Vestige shattered the Ansei Ward, subduing Alasan for a moment.{{Ref|name=ONPlot|[[Online:Alasan's Plot|Alasan's Plot]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

With its destruction, it caused a large concentration of necromantic energy to release, raising undead all over town. Ansei Maja appeared before the Vestige and tasked them with re-forging the Ansei Ward by retrieving the gods' favor. With all the elements in place, they brought the sword to the Ansei Shrine. There, the Vestige and Ansei Maja confronted Alasan's [[Lore:Souls|soul]] and ultimately confined him into the ward. With the threat to Tava's Blessing gone, Azah took Maja's ward for safe keeping.{{Ref|name=ONPlot}} The [[Lore:Ash'abah|Ash'abah]] arrived later to cleanse the town and put down any undead remaining.{{Ref|name=ONTalia|[[Online:Talia at-Marimah#Amputating the Hand|Talia at-Marimah]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} At the same time, a ship architect remained to recover important ship designs. Using the commotion, a spy from [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] tried to steal them. Whether they successfully took one or not is unknown, but most of them were recovered.{{Ref|name=ONWarship|[[Online:Warship Designs|Warship Designs]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Sometime between 2E 582 and years later in {{Year|2E 864}},{{Ref|name=RGMap|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-800x600.png|Map of West Tamriel – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}} Tava's Blessing was renamed to Lainlyn, after the eponymous family who lived in town.{{Ref|name=AUADC1}} During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the village of Lainlyn was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Glavt and had a rivalry with [[Lore:Chasetown|Chasetown]]. By the fifth century of the Third Era, the Barony of Lainlyn was ruled by Baron [[Lore:Shrike|Shrike]] and the capital hosted a [[Lore:School of Julianos|School of Julianos]]. When the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] occurred in {{Year|3E 417}}, Lainlyn and many other regions in the Hammerfell-Iliac Bay were absorbed into the Kingdom of Sentinel, at the time ruled by Queen [[Lore:Akorithi|Akorithi]].{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

==Culture and Society==
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Redguard#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Redguard#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Redguard page.''
[[File:ON-place-Ansei Shrine (Tava's Blessing).jpg|thumb|right|The Ansei Shrine]]
The city's original name comes from [[Lore:Tava|Tava]], the Redguard's goddess of air and weather, and a favorite among sailors. Shrines of her can be found in almost every port city in the province.{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Its modern name stems from the [[Lore:Lainlyn (family)|Lainlyn]] family, who lived here since at least the mid-Second Era.{{Ref|name=AUADC1}} Traditionally, [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] use the honorific "Al-" to specify their hometown,{{Ref|name=ZOSCor|group=UOL|[[General:ZOS Correspondence|ZOS Correspondence]]}} so when it was originally known as Tava's Blessing, people used "al-Tava" as their surname.{{Ref|name=ESOUnused|group=UOL|{{TIL|ESO Unused Strings|game-data}}}} "Tava's Blessings" is also a phrase for good fortune.{{Ref|name=ONAzzadal|[[Online:Azzadal|Azzadal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Despite its association with Tava, who has been assimilated to the broader mythology of [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]],{{Ref|name=VOF}} the city and its barony's main temple was the [[Lore:School of Julianos|School of Julianos]],{{Ref|name=DFLainlyn}} dedicated to the [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodilic]] god of literature and law, [[Lore:Julianos|Julianos]].{{Ref|name=VOF}} He is, however, not a god acknowledged by the Crowns and is instead, adopted by the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]].{{Ref|name=VOFCR|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}}{{Ref|name=VOFTF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}} Ansei Maja, one of the three warriors who blessed the Ansei Wards, is considered the town's guardian spirit.{{Ref|name=ESOUnused|group=UOL}} It was Jamshald Lainlyn who built the [[Lore:Statues/Tribunal and Saints#Ansei Majah, Radan and Halelah|Ansei Shrine]] in central Lainlyn to commemorate the ancient Ansei that defended the Alik'r Desert, especially Ansei Maja.{{Ref|name=AS}}

Lainlyn and its barony observe [[Lore:Riglametha|Riglametha]] on the 12th of [[Lore:Hearthfire|Hearthfire]]. It translates in the [[Lore:Bantha|Banthan]] dialect of [[Lore:Yoku|Yoku]] as "graceful offering". It is a festival to honor the gods' graces granted to Lainlyn for centuries. Traditions dictate the many plays and performances that depict Lainlyn's great moments in history. Ghraewaj is one of the barony's most popular plays and it roughly translates into either ''"The Crows Who Were Punished"'' or ''"The Crows Who Punish."'' The story has changed and continues to change throughout the years, inciting much interest among scholars. The author [[Lore:Tidasus|Tidasus]] believed that finding the truth in its many iterations was not a profitable venture, but makes a connection between its villian, Noctyr-a and the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]], [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]].{{Ref|name=Ghrae}}

Like other cities on the Hammerfell coast, the Redguards of Lainlyn observe the 20th of [[Lore:Rain's Hand|Rain's Hand]] as the [[Lore:Day of Shame|Day of Shame]]. According to legend, the [[Lore:Crimson Ship|Crimson Ship]], a vessel full of [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]] victims of the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] first left [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] on the 9th of [[Lore:First Seed|First Seed]] and was denied refuge from every port on Tamriel. It was later westbound for the Abecean Sea and never seen again. It is said to one day return and the Day of Shame was created in remembrance of that.{{Ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|7}}{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Day of Shame|Day of Shame]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The city is known for its shipyards and where Sentinel's royal navy was built in the mid-Second Era.{{Ref|name=ONMeriq}} The leader of the shipyards is known as the Master Shipwright{{Ref|name=SAF}} or the Master Architect.{{Ref|name=ONSulim|[[Online:Master Architect Sulim|Master Architect Sulim]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Baandari Pedlars|Baandari Pedlars]] often camp in nearby Rkulftzel to trade with the shipyard workers in Tava's Blessing.{{Ref|name=ONS'rabbi|[[Online:S'rabbi|S'rabbi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Shipwrights also depend on the mines of Coldrock Diggings for [[Lore:Grit-rock|grit-rock]] to sand wooden planks and [[Lore:Holystone|holystone]] for cleaning the decks.{{Ref|name=ONLoadColdrock|[[Online:Coldrock Diggings|Coldrock Diggings]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
