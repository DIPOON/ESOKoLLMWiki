# Lore:Savior's Hide

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}__NOTOC__{{Quote Box|''You've done well, hunter. And found my favor. That skin will serve you well, child. Look more closely at it. My glories shall protect you from all this world's grievances. Good hunting.''|Hircine}}</noinclude>
[[Lore:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]], also known as '''Scourge of the Oathbreaker''',{{ref|name=SHN|{{Cite Book|Savior's Hide Note|ns_base=battlespire}}}} and '''Hircine's Hide''',{{ref|name=TRTM|Events of [[Tribunal:The Museum|The Museum]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} is a Daedric [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] commonly associated with [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]].{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}}{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} Savior's Hide once referred to a full set of armor, known as the '''Armor of the Savior's Hide''' (boots, cuirass, gauntlets, greaves, helmet, and pauldrons). Over time, the term Savior's Hide would become predominately used to refer to the '''Cuirass of the Savior's Hide''', as the rest of the armor set have not been seen since {{Year|3E 399}}.{{ref|name=CNATAOFSH|{{Cite Book|Chimere's Notes about the Armor of the Savior's Hide|ns_base=Battlespire}}}} The Cuirass makes the wearer resistant to [[Lore:Magic|magic]].{{ref|name=TL}}

There are three creation stories to the Hide, with two crediting Hircine and the third crediting [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]].{{ref|name=TL}}{{ref|name=TMKR|{{Cite Book|Tal Marog Ker's Researches}}}} The more widely known tale involving Hircine holds that the Daedric Prince rewarded the first mortal to escape his [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]] with his peeled Hide. The mortal then had the Hide tailored into the Cuirass for use in their adventures. This version of the story also claims that the Cuirass gave the wearer a resistance to magic.{{ref|name=TL}} Another version claims that it was Hircine himself who sewed the Savior's hide from the hide of a [[Lore:Werewolf|werewolf]].{{ref|name=BladesSaviorsHide|[[Blades:Savior's Hide|Savior's Hide]] description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

The third tradition which credits Malacath is lesser known and contains an inaccuracy, stating that it made the wearer ''vulnerable'' to magic. It claims that the Hide is a "captive servant of Malacath". Despite the conflict between the stories, they agree on the points that the Hide would protect from the blows of an oathbreaker, and would protect the wearer from the sting of the [[Lore:Spear of Bitter Mercy|Spear of Bitter Mercy]].{{ref|name=TMKR}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Second Era===
[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended the sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing the Cuirass of the Savior's Hide among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the cuirass and the other artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}

Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, many elaborate sets of armor existed that imitated the style of the Armor of the Savior's Hide .{{ref|[[Online:Savior's Hide Style|Savior's Hide Style]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
Sometime before {{Year|3E 172}}, [[Lore:Chimere Graegyn|Chimere Graegyn]], a retainer of the [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] clan, came into possession of the full set of armor. He used it to protect himself when tricking [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] and gaining immortality. Unfortunately for him it only protected him against Dagon's direct physical retaliation; instead Dagon formed an interpretation of the pact that allowed him to destroy Chimere's hometown and cast the town, the island it was built on ([[Lore:Caecilly Island|Caecilly Island]]), and Chimere into the [[Lore:Void|Void]].{{ref|name=TMKR}} Never wanting to see it again, he split the armor up and hid the pieces all across the island.{{ref|name=CNATAOFSH}}

In {{Year|3E 389}}, when Dagon invaded the [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]], an unnamed [[LO:Apprentice|Apprentice]] of the Battlespire fought their way through many realms of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] and eventually stumbled upon the island, unwittingly becoming Prey in the [[Lore:Great Hunt|Great Hunt]] which was being held at the time. After talking to Chimere and assembling the six pieces of the Savior's Hide, they obtained the Spear of Bitter Mercy needed to defeat the Huntsman and depart from the realm.{{ref|name=BSL5|Events of [[Battlespire:Level 5|Level 5]] in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} This apprentice went on to use the Hide to defeat Dagon along with another artifact, the [[Lore:Sword of the Moon Reiver|Sword of the Moon Reiver]].{{Ref|name=LD|{{Cite Book|Letters from the Dremora|ns_base=Battlespire}}}}{{ref|name=BSL7|Events of [[Battlespire:Level 7|Level 7]] in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

By {{Year|3E 427}}, [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]], a member of [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], who was the caretaker of the Corprusarium under [[Lore:Tel Fyr|Tel Fyr]] and also a collector or artifacts, had acquired the Cuirass. The [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] obtained the item while adhering to Divayth's strange hobby of allowing his collection to be pilfered under certain circumstances.{{ref|name=MWDivayth|[[Morrowind:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}  Later that year, the Nerevarine sold it to a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] noblewomen by the name of [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]], who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=TRTM}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] obtained the Cuirass after fulfilling Hircine's request to kill the last [[Lore:Unicorn|unicorn]] in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=OBHircine|[[OB:Hircine|Hircine]] quest of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

===Fourth Era===
Organized bandit robberies surged in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] around {{Year|4E 176}} under the leadership of a self-proclaimed "Bandit Queen", a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] by the name Marita Shuste. The [[Lore:Sheathed Blades|Sheathed Blades]] came into conflict with the bandits and slew them along with the Bandit Queen, who was the wearer of the Savior's Hide at the time.{{ref|Card 744 "Marita Shuste" wearing the Savior's Hide in the [[SkyrimTAG:Bandits!|Bandits!]] mini-campaign in ''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}}

The Cuirass re-emerged around {{Year|4E 180}}, when the [[Lore:Hero|Warrior]] came across it during their journey. The artifact is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.{{ref|[[Blades:Artifacts|Artifact]] acquisition methods in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, a werewolf named [[Lore:Sinding|Sinding]] stole the [[Lore:Ring of Hircine|Ring of Hircine]] in an attempt to control his transformations. However, this plan backfired when Hircine cursed the ring, causing Sinding to transform at random, which led to him killing a child and ending up in jail. Soon after, the [[Lore:Hero#Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] met Sinding, who asked the Dragonborn to take the Ring and free it from its curse by hunting the [[Lore:White Stag|White Stag]], before escaping from jail.  After slaying the stag, the Dragonborn learned that the Prince would not forgive Sinding's betrayal, and conducted a Great Hunt, where he tasked hunters with killing and skinning him in return for the Savior's Hide. The Dragonborn entered Bloated Man's Grotto where Sinding was hiding, where he was confronting a second time. The Dragonborn was left with the choice to kill him in order to receive Hircine's favor and the Cuirass, or to kill the other hunters trying to get Hircine's favor and the uncursed ring. It is unknown which option the Last Dragonborn chose, only that Hircine was pleased with the results.{{Ref|name=SRMoonlight|Events of [[Skyrim:Ill Met By Moonlight|Ill Met By Moonlight]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
