[[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]] is the storied afterlife of the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]],{{ref|name=TRtS|{{Cite Book|The Road to Sovngarde}}}}{{ref|name=SR|[[Skyrim:Sovngarde (quest)|Sovngarde]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} a part of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]]{{ref|{{Cite Book|"Death" of Morphotypical Entities}}}} where the greatest Nord heroes go when they die to enjoy an eternity of feasting and merriment,{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|631}} and where the [[Lore:Gods|gods]] [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] and [[Lore:Tsun|Tsun]] reside after having died to bring about the current [[Lore:Mundus|world]].{{ref|name=Divines|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}} It is ruled by Shor, created by him long ago through his [[Lore:Clever Craft|Clever Magic]],{{ref|name=TRtS|{{Cite Book|The Road to Sovngarde}}}} and contains the Hall of Valor, a supposedly gigantic feasting hall where the mead "flows like a waterfall". It is said that in Sovngarde even the tedium of immortality is unknown, as spectral foes wait in the surrounding shadows, to do battle with any who would test their mettle.{{ref|name=TRtS|{{Cite Book|The Road to Sovngarde}}}} At least one [[Lore:Word Walls|Word Wall]] was found within the realm, and many more spoke of it.{{ref|name=SWW|[[Skyrim:Word Wall|Word Walls]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Those who cross the threshold of the living and enter the afterlife are greeted below the red glow of the eternal sunset, following the pathway down between the mammoth tusks and the monolithic cowled statues and into the valley of mists—the Shadowed Vale.{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|150}} Tsun currently guards the mythical Whalebone Bridge past the Vale,{{ref|name=EN|{{Cite Book|Eydvina's Note|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} which leads to the Hall of Valor. At Shor's bidding, he has taken on the role of the master of trials, asking new arrivals to the utopia to prove their strength in combat against him before they can enter Shor's Hall.{{ref|name=SRTsun|[[Skyrim:Tsun|Tsun]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Clawthanes, the spirits of wolves who have reached Sovngarde, aid Tsun in this duty, keeping watch alongside him.{{ref|name=SovngardeCrate|[[Online:Sovngarde Crate|Sovngarde Crate]]'s descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It is said that, within Shor's Hall of Valor, protected by the power of the god himself, no gloom can grip the heart for long, and there are no shadows. However it is also said that, as it is a [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realm]] of the dead, Sovngarde is a place where the living shouldn't linger.{{ref|name=SRT|[[SR:Tsun|Tsun]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Hero|[[SR:Hero of Sovngarde|Hero of Sovngarde]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Most Nords agree that [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] leads the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of the dead into Sovngarde, which is why she is known as the ''Kiss at the End''.{{ref|name=GoSMoN|{{Cite Book|Goddess of Storm, Mother of Nords}}}} 

Some people regarded Sovngarde as a myth by the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{ref|[[SR:Vals Veran|Vals Veran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

==Entry and Acceptance==
[[File:SR-npc-Tsun.jpg|thumb|left|Tsun guarding the bridge to the Hall of Valor]][[File:ON-crown store-Sovngarde Clawthane Wolf.jpg|A Sovngarde Clawthane Wolf, who guards the Whalebone Bridge alongside Tsun|thumb|right]]
Excluding the rare usage of [[Lore:Portals|portals]], the realm can supposedly only be reached by true Nords (and their loyal beasts){{ref|[[Online:Valorous Sovngarde Guar Strider|Valorous Sovngarde Guar Strider]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} who die valiantly in battle, regardless of personality.{{ref|name=SaR|{{Cite Book|Sovngarde, a Reexamination}}}} Others believe those who die dishonorably, even in battle, are denied entry.{{ref|name=Svana|[[ESO:Svana|Svana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} This excludes those afflicted with [[Lore:Lycanthropy|Lycanthropy]], as [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] takes them to his [[Lore:Hunting Grounds|Hunting Grounds]],{{ref|name=KJ|{{Cite Book|Kodlak's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} those without [[Lore:Soul|souls]],{{ref|[[ON:Nelhilda|Nelhilda]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and those whose bodies were interred deep within [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]], as their souls cannot reach the realm.{{ref|[[ON:Alwyn|Alwyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Nords who live lives without combat do not go to Sovngarde according to some,{{ref|[[SR:Bulfrek|Bulfrek]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Aelif|[[ESO:Aelif|Aelif]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} but some exceptions to this supposed rule are known to have occurred: [[Lore:Jurgen Windcaller|Jurgen Windcaller]], who swore a life of pacifism,{{ref|name=PGE1SK|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} and [[Lore:Svaknir|Svaknir]], who was imprisoned and executed,{{ref|name=Giraud|[[SR:Giraud Gemane|Giraud Gemane]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} were known to have nevertheless ascended to Sovngarde.{{ref|Appearance of [[SR:Jurgen Windcaller|Jurgen Windcaller]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|Appearance of [[SR:Svaknir|Svaknir]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Though Nords and [[Lore:Atmora|Atmorans]] may go to Sovngarde when they die,{{ref|name=SRYsgramor|[[SR:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} is unclear whether or not other races may ascend to the realm. A priest from the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Cult of the Ancestor Moth]] who lived during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] claimed that mortals decide the destinations of their own souls through the choices they make in life,{{Ref|name=TACAVayq|{{Cite Book|The Abbot Crassius Viria Answers Your Questions}}}} seemingly absent of race. [[Lore:Giant|Giants]] have occasionally been referenced in the context of going to Sovngarde.{{ref|name=Irna|[[ESO:Irna|Irna]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Additionally, in a [[Lore:Sight|prophetic dream]], Fourth Era Harbinger of the [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] [[Lore:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]] saw all Harbingers who came before him enter Sovngarde, ending at [[Lore:Terrfyg|Terrfyg]], who took up Lycanthropy.{{ref|name=KJ}} As Terrfyg ruled after them, this would mean that [[Lore:Henantier the Outsider|Henantier the Outsider]], an [[Lore:Mer|elf]], and [[Lore:Cirroc the Lofty|Cirroc the Lofty]], a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]], went to Sovngarde.{{ref|name=GB|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} Another Redguard, a renowned warrior in the Fourth Era, believed he could go to Sovngarde,{{ref|[[SR:Ebony Warrior|Ebony Warrior]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and departed there after his death.{{ref|name=PRIMA|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide#Legendary Edition|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Legendary Prima Official Game Guide]]}}<noinclude>{{rp|431}}

Certain magical beasts that walk [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] are said to be able to help [[Lore:Ghost|spirits]] that linger in the mortal world reach Aetherius. Gathering up the souls of those who died violently, or couldn't move on from heartbreak, despair or other such causes, and providing them with sanctuary until they are ready to be ushered into the afterlife. Some of these beasts are also known to provide sanctuary to those spirits turned away from Sovngarde, with their form becoming imbued with those lost souls.{{ref|name=SovngardeCrate|[[Online:Sovngarde Crate|Sovngarde Crate]]'s descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Inter-Realm Contact==
[[File:ON-crown store-Sovngarde Stoutheart Bear.jpg|A Sovngarde spirit bear|thumb|left]]
A few Nords have received divine premonitions of Sovngarde. Kodlak Whitemane of the Companions dreamt of Sovngarde, seeing generations of Harbingers ascend to the Hall of Valor, as well as Terrfyg being pulled into the Hunting Grounds. The dream also predicted the arrival of the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]].{{ref|name=KJ}} Skardan Free-Winter, a soldier in the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]], had a dream of Sovngarde where he met Ysgramor in the Hall of Valor, filling him with determination to win the upcoming [[Lore:Battle of the Red Ring|Battle of the Red Ring]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Dream of Sovngarde}}}} Those who suffer mortal wounds in combat but are not yet dead may see Sovngarde before them.{{ref|name=Ulfgar}} Certain mortals have been known to hear the whispers of Shor since a young age, granting them knowledge of the [[Lore:Clever Craft|Clever Craft]].{{ref|name=OldMjolen|[[ESO:Old Mjolen|Old Mjolen]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Spirits have been known to depart Sovngarde to contact the living. The [[Lore:Companions#Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]] once appeared to King [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] after the defeat of [[Lore:Sinmur|Sinmur]].{{ref|[[ON:Stomping Sinmur|Stomping Sinmur]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Queen [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]] appeared to a force of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], giving them vital information to entering Sovngarde.{{ref|name=Nurn}} The Direfrost Clan, within their [[Lore:Direfrost Keep|keep]]'s mead hall, house an artifact known as the Direfrost Flame, a sacred fire that gives the Clan access to commune with their ancestors in Sovngarde.{{ref|name=TDF|{{Cite Book|The Direfrost Flame}}}} [[Lore:Ulfgar the Unending|Ulfgar the Unending]] was able to manifest as a spirit after reaching Sovngarde, blessing a stone with his magical energies.{{ref|name=Ulfgar}}

During times of need in the mortal world Sovngarde might send the spirits of dead heroes back to the living world to provide aid, occasions on which those spirits are said to ride the spirits of beasts whose souls likewise reside in that plane. Spirit beasts have also been known to return individually in order to act as companion mounts to courageous living champions, or to serve as the eyes and ears of the afterlife within the living world.{{ref|name=SovngardeCrate|[[Online:Sovngarde Crate|Sovngarde Crate]]'s descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SovngardeCat|[[Online:Sovngarde Sabre Cat Cub|Sovngarde Sabre Cat Cub]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Certain [[Lore:Thu'um|Shouts]] such as the Call of Valor are said to function by issuing a call for aid to Aetherial spirits of Sovngarde, who respond to the call and "journey beyond time and space" to give their aid.{{ref|name=Call|[[Skyrim:Call of Valor|Call of Valor]]'s description in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}} There exist magical runes which can call a spirit back into the living world from Aetherius; Archmage [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]] placed such runes on his tomb to call his soul back to Nirn during times when magic in Tamriel was under threat.{{ref|name=Shalidor|[[Online:Shalidor|Shalidor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:SR-npc-Olaf One-Eye.jpg|Olaf One-Eye, a spirit with influence over the living world|thumb|left]]
There are cases where spiritual influence from Sovngarde can affect the mortal world without planar travel taking place. One such case involved the Sovngarde-bound spirit of [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|Olaf One-Eye]] reaching out to the living via altering their dreams and subsequently using said dreams as a medium to [[Lore:Time Magic|alter time]] in a form of ultimately fatal curse. The living target of the curse would initially experience strange dreams relating to the spirit's past, eventually realizing that they were being made to travel to different eras of history in their sleep when the people in the dream noticed the intruding presence and reacted to it, resulting in them being immediately moved to a different time upon being seen, and thus revealing the intent behind the magic. The experience would repeat nightly until burns started appearing on the dreamer's body, culminating in them magically burning to ash.{{ref|name=JournalOlaf|{{Cite Book|Solitude: A Charred Journal}}}}

[[Lore:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]] claimed that one day he and the spirits of Sovngarde might attack Hircine's Hunting Grounds to reclaim the souls of the [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] trapped there.{{ref|name=Kodlak|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==History==
===Merethic Era===
[[File:BM-place-Brodir Grove.jpg|thumb|right|The standing stones at Brodir Grove on Solstheim (Morrowind)]]
During the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], a group of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s Five Hundred, after successfully taking Skyrim from the [[Lore:Snow Elf|elves]], began to search for the entrance to Sovngarde. This group consisted of Ulfgar, [[Lore:Hunroor|Hunroor]], [[Lore:Erlendr|Erlendr]], [[Lore:Nikulas|Nikulas]], and [[Lore:Grimkell|Grimkell]]. The group was close to finding an answer on the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], but before they could, Grimkell betrayed them, casting a spell that turned the group to stone in attempt to create a portal to Sovngarde. Ulfgar, however, resisted the spell, becoming a living stone and killing Grimkell for what he had done. He would spend the next several millennia wandering [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], searching for the entrance to the elusive realm.{{ref|name=Ulfgar|[[Bloodmoon:Ulfgar the Unending|Ulfgar the Unending]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]}}

===First Era===
In or before the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]],{{ref|[[SR:Farengar Secret-Fire|Farengar Secret-Fire]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} the necromancer Azaran the Cruel attempted to bind the dead souls of Sovngarde to the living world, but failed. He was later buried somewhere in Skyrim.{{Ref|name=SREthereal|[[Skyrim:Become Ethereal#Word Walls|Become Ethereal]] third word wall in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:Fildgor|Fildgor Orc-Thane]] located a hidden passage to [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]], a Nordic stronghold far in the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] with a portal to Sovngarde. He used his royal blood to enter the realm and attempted to fuse his soul with that of his dead sister, Nurnhilde, in order to usurp the throne using the [[Lore:Crown of Verity|Crown of Verity]].{{ref|name=Nurn|[[ON:Queen Nurnhilde|Queen Nurnhilde]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Forces loyal to King Jorunn pursued him to the stronghold. A Pact soldier, later given the title [[Lore:The Vestige|King's Arrow]] for their deeds, then entered Sovngarde with the blessing of Nirnhilde's spirit and defeated Fildgor. It is unknown whether he was killed by the soldier, by Jorunn, or was spared.{{ref|[[Online:Songs of Sovngarde|Songs of Sovngarde]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Third Era===
By {{Year|3E 427}}, Ulfgar the Unending had made his home on Solstheim, not far from Brodir Grove, where his companions were turned to stone. [[Lore:Hero#Nerevarine|An adventurer]], who found out that Sovngarde was reached by an honorable death in battle, slew the ancient Atmoran in battle.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Betrayal at Brodir Grove|Betrayal at Brodir Grove]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]}} Ulfgar then joined his companions in Sovngarde.{{ref|name=Ulfgar}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-quest-The World-Eater's Eyrie.jpg|thumb|right|The portal to Sovngarde within Skuldafn (Skyrim)]]
In {{Year|4E 201}}, [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]], a [[Lore:Gods|godly]] tyrant from the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]], returned to Tamriel, having been sent into the future during the Merethic Era. Alduin made his base of operations in Skuldafn. Using Skuldafn's portal to Sovngarde, Alduin began to ensnare the realm in mist and feast upon the souls of the dead, building his power in the process, a privilege he withheld from his subordinates.{{ref|name=SRO|[[SR:Odahviing|Odahviing]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRP|[[SR:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Later that year, the Dragonborn breached Skuldafn and entered Sovngarde. After passing Tsun's trial, they continued into the Hall of Valor and rallied the spirits of [[Lore:Gormlaith Golden-Hilt|Gormlaith Golden-Hilt]], [[Lore:Felldir the Old|Felldir the Old]] and [[Lore:Hakon One-Eye|Hakon One-Eye]], whom had previously bested Alduin atop the [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]].{{ref|[[SR:Alduin's Bane|Alduin's Bane]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Together, they were able to defeat Alduin, cleansing Sovngarde of his soul snare.{{ref|name=Dragonslayer|[[Skyrim:Dragonslayer|Dragonslayer]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRT|[[SR:Tsun|Tsun]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

In the same year, Harbinger Kodlak Whitemane of the Companions was killed by the [[Lore:Silver Hand|Silver Hand]].{{ref|name=SRVilkas|[[SR:Vilkas|Vilkas]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Due to his Lycanthropy, he was taken to Hircine's Hunting Grounds upon his death, despite his desires for Sovngarde.{{ref|name=KJ}}{{ref|name=SRKodlak|[[SR:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The remaining members of the Circle then convened, reassembling the legendary axe [[Lore:Wuuthrad|Wuuthrad]] and breaching Ysgramor's Tomb, where a magical relic known as the Flame of the Harbinger resided.{{ref|Appearance of [[SR:Ysgramor's Tomb|Ysgramor's Tomb]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRVilkas}} After burning the head of a [[Lore:Glenmoril Coven|Glenmoril]] [[Lore:Hagraven|hagraven]] and defeating Kodlak's wolf spirit in battle, a new Harbinger was selected for the Companions and Kodlak's soul was freed.{{ref|name=SRKodlak}}{{ref|name=Dragonslayer}}{{ref|name=SRYsgramor}}

Kodlak hoped that someday, he and other honored warriors could invade the Hunting Grounds and rescue the souls of the Companions who did not have the chance to free themselves from Hircine's grasp before death. He called this imagined battle "The Harrowing of the Hunting Grounds",{{ref|name=SRKodlak}} but it is unknown if it ever occurred.

==Notes==
* In Norwegian and Danish, ''søvn'' means ''sleep'' and ''gård'' means ''land/yard/garden'', making Sovngarde mean Land of the Sleeping (the Dead) or the Sleep Garden. The concept of Sovngarde takes inspiration from the [[wikipedia:Norse mythology|Norse]] [[wikipedia:Valhalla|Valhalla]].
*The [[Lore:Forest of Dreams|Forest of Dreams]] is a place mentioned on a Word Wall. It is a described as a place of eternal rest for Kyne's servants.{{Ref|name=SRKyne|[[Skyrim:Kyne's Peace#Word Wall Translations|Kyne's Peace]] first word wall in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} It is known that worshippers of Kyne ascend to Sovngarde upon death.{{Ref|name=Froki|[[Skyrim:Froki Whetted-Blade|Froki Whetted-Blade]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, the exact location of the Forest of Dreams is unclear, as is its connection to Sovngarde, which is also described as dreamlike{{ref|name=SSS|[[Skyrim:Stormcloak Soldier (Sovngarde)|Stormcloack Soldier]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Sovngarde (quest)|Sovngarde]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and associated with dreams.{{Ref|name=ADOS|{{Cite Book|A Dream of Sovngarde}}}}{{Ref|name=KJ}}
*The spirits of [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] can be found within the Hall of Valor.{{ref|name=Hero|[[SR:Hero of Sovngarde|Hero of Sovngarde]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
*The spirits in the Hall of Valor claim they "feast and sing, til the final doom".{{ref|name=Hero|[[SR:Hero of Sovngarde|Hero of Sovngarde]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}.
