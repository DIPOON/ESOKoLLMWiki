|}</cleantable></noinclude>'''[[Lore:Ranser's War|Ranser's War]]''' (also called '''King Ranser's War''') was a civil conflict between the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|First Daggerfall Covenant]] and its allies and the [[Lore:Rivenspire|Kingdom of Rivenspire]]. The war began when King [[Lore:Ranser|Ranser]], furious with King Emeric for reneging on possible marriage arrangements with Ranser's daughter, launched a surprise attack on Wayrest. The ensuing war lasted around a year with both sides suffering considerable casualties. In the end, the war led to large political ramifications and changes not only in [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] lands but abroad as well.

==Background==
[[File:ON-npc-Queen_Maraya.jpg|thumb|left|Queen Maraya, Jewel of Satakalaam]]
[[File:ON-item-Princess Rayelle's Half-hearted Love Letters.jpg|thumb|left|Rayelle's letter for Emeric]]
The rise of Prince Ranser to the throne of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] was a conflict in its own right. Although it was fought with words and ancient decrees instead of soldiers and weapons. When [[Lore:Hurlburt|King Hurlburt]] passed away in {{Year|2E 546}}, there were two claimants to the throne, one of [[Lore:House Montclair|House Montclair]] and the other of [[Lore:House Branquette|House Branquette]]. The former was the fourteen-year-old [[Lore:Phylgeon Montclair|Prince Phylgeon]], who was a legitimate son of the late King Hurlburt and [[Lore:Iphilia Montclair|Queen Iphilia]], while the latter was the eldest Prince Ranser, an illegitimate son born out of wedlock to a [[Lore:House Tamrith|Tamrith]] cousin.{{ref|name=SCCotN|{{Cite Book|Shornhelm, Crown City of the North}}}}

Phylgeon's claim was supported by House Montclair while Ranser's was supported by Houses Tamrith and Branquette. The [[Lore:Council of the North|Council of the North]] met to discuss and consider both claimants to the throne. After much deliberation the council narrowly voted to elevate Prince Ranser to the throne, though some cast doubt on the legitimacy of his claims brought before the council. Some of Phylgeon's advisors wanted him to fight for the crown, but the teenage prince was content to just be the Baron Montclair. This action is believed by some to have directly led to the outbreak of Ranser's war and the suffering of the Kingdom.{{ref|name=SCCotN}}

Over a decade later, [[Lore:Gardner|King Gardner]] of [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] was killed by the dreaded [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahatan Flu]] along with his entire royal line.{{ref|name=GttDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}} In that same year of {{Year|2E 563}}, Earl Emeric of [[Lore:Cumberland|Cumberland]] was elevated to the throne of Wayrest and became the first monarch of the new Cumberland Dynasty. Emeric was unmarried when he took the throne, and he and his advisors immediately began searching for a suitable bride from the other kingdoms of High Rock to be his queen.{{ref|name=ToaM|{{Cite Book|Triumphs of a Monarch}}}} Early in the search, King Ranser offered Emeric the hand of his daughter, [[Lore:Rayelle|Princess Rayelle]]. Ranser was adamant about Emeric marrying his daughter and mentioned it often to his fellow king. He also encouraged his daughter to pen letters to Emeric that played up his good looks and virility.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures P#Princess Rayelle's Half-hearted Love Letters|Princess Rayelle's Half-hearted Love Letters]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Emeric was open to the idea and considered the princess to be a goodly woman. He most likely would have married her had it not been for a fateful visit to the Kingdom of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]].{{ref|name=ToaM}}

During this visit to King Fahara'jad's court, Emeric first laid eyes upon [[Lore:Maraya|Princess Maraya]], the eldest daughter of Fahara'jad. When he saw Maraya, Emeric decided that Wayrest would have no other queen.{{ref|name=ToaM}} Emeric's sudden decision to wed a [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] princess stunned many in his court and across the land. So much so that bards sang of Maraya's surely bewitching beauty.{{ref|name=TFoKR|{{Cite Book|The Fury of King Ranser}}}} However, none were more stunned or furious than Ranser.

==Preparing for War==
[[File:ON-npc-King_Ranser_03.jpg|thumb|right|King Ranser of Shornhelm]]
Strategists viewed Emeric's unexpected decision to marry Maraya as a wise political move.{{ref|name=ToaM}}{{ref|name=TFoKR}} The marriage had the chance to strengthen commerce between the kingdoms. This turned out to be the case, as Maraya's dowry included a trade agreement between Wayrest and Sentinel that resulted in prosperity for both kingdoms. All the kings of the Daggerfall Covenant were invited to the wedding in the spring of {{Year|2E 566}}, including Ranser. He did not attend and instead withdrew his ambassador from the court of Wayrest in his ever-growing fury.{{ref|name=ToaM}}

With Emeric distracted by his new bride and trade issues around the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]], Ranser, still furious, began building up his forces to attack Wayrest. Ranser called the prominent noble houses of Rivenspire to arms: Houses {{Lore Link|House Dorell|Dorell}}, Montclair, and Tamrith, who provided the king with their troops.{{ref|name=SCCotN}}{{ref|name=HTARH|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} Smaller noble families such as [[Lore:Marcott|House Marcott]] also supported him.{{ref|name=AJD|[[Online:Aurelia Jourvel|Aurelia Jourvel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} [[Lore:House Ravenwatch|House Ravenwatch]] was the only noble house of Rivenspire that did not support him, choosing instead to remain neutral.{{ref|name=HTARH}} Ranser further bolstered his growing army by emptying his treasury to hire mercenaries.{{ref|name=TFoKR}}{{ref|name=ToaM}}

Phylgeon Montclair supposedly offered to serve as a peace envoy between the two monarchs; Ranser, however, refused the offer, and Emeric's reply is unknown. Failing to peacefully resolve the approaching conflict, Phylgeon pledged the Montclair knights to Ranser.{{ref|name=SCCotN}}

Ranser also gained the support of [[Lore:House Mornard|House Mornard]], the ruling family of the Systres Archipelago. House Mornard was traditionally a vassal of Wayrest and the former Gardner Dynasty. However, due to [[Lore:Avrippe Mornard|Duke Avrippe Mornard]]'s handling of the Knahaten Flu, the new Cumberland Dynasty viewed House Mornard with disfavor. Duke Mornard was shaken by Emeric's displeasure and began to look to other noble houses to attempt to secure his house's position in the archipelago in the future. For this reason, Avrippe forsook his vassalage and pledged his house to Ranser, sending knights and arms to his army.{{Ref|name=SHV7|{{Cite Book|Systres History: Volume 7}}}}{{Ref|name=EJ|{{Cite Book|Emeric's Judgment}}}}

With allies gathered and his army bolstered, Ranser attempted to enact revenge for what he saw as a betrayal.{{ref|name=GTTDC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Daggerfall Covenant}}}}

==Wayrest Besieged==
[[File:ON-place-Wayrest.jpg|thumb|left|A gate of Wayrest]]
It was in [[Lore:Last Seed|Last Seed]] of {{Year|2E 566}} that Ranser led his army out of Shornhelm in a fast attack to the south, with the vaunted [[Lore:Oldgate Lancers|Oldgate Lancer]] calvary at its head, his troops tore through [[Lore:Alcaire|Alcaire]] and [[Lore:Menevia|Menevia]] with lightning speed. Wayrest was almost completely unaware of their approach up to this point and had just begun mustering local militias when the Shornhelm advance guard arrived. These militias were still filing through the open city gate and were the only defense at the moment. If they were scattered and the gate taken, Wayrest could fall within the hour.

As luck would have it, King Emeric arrived at the battle for the gate with both his house guard and the [[Lore:Lion Guard|Lion Guard]]{{ref|name=TFoKR}}. Seeing the situation at the gate grow increasingly desperate, Emeric ordered his bannerman to sound the charge. The king led his combined troops out onto the field against the lancers. These fresh troops wore full armor and Emeric, though unarmored, wielded his powerful [[Lore:Enchanting|enchanted]] broadsword known as the [[Lore:Orichalc Scalpel|Orichalc Scalpel]] with great effect against the enemy. The attacking lancers were now up against veteran troops rather than just hastily gathered irregulars and were beginning to hesitate. As the battle went on the sudden arrival of a thunderstorm was the deciding factor. The lancers, pelted by hail, their horses terrified by lightning, and losing ground against Wayrest's forces broke and ran in disordered haste from the gate.

Their successful defense gave the troops time to get within the city walls and close the gates. Ranser had hoped for a swift victory but would now have to settle for putting Wayrest under siege. Though his spearhead had been blunted he was undeterred and the main body of his army would soon arrive equipped with siege engines.{{ref|name=ToaM}}

The ensuing siege drug on for several months all the way into the spring of {{Year|2E 567}}. Wayrest continued to hang on, depending on its allied kingdoms to come to its aid. The other kingdoms of the Covenant had been watching the war unfold and some even considered allowing Wayrest to fall despite being part of the defense pact. Allowing the richest city in the region to fall could not be allowed however and the kingdoms of [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], [[Lore:Camlorn|Camlorn]], and [[Lore:Evermore|Evermore]] finally mobilized their forces and marched to the defense of the besieged city.

Ranser's army was now having to fight not only the city but attacks from the countryside as well. His forces held their positions despite this as his troops were well paid and prepared for battle. It was not until a fleet of ships carrying battalions of elite [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]] warriors crossed the Iliac Bay that Shornhelm's forces could no longer hold. They were routed and the siege of Wayrest was broken. The combined forces of Emeric's Bretons and Fahara'jad's Redguards pushed Ranser's retreating forces back towards the north.{{ref|name=TFoKR}}{{ref|name=TTFoKR|{{Cite Book|The True Fate of King Ranser}}}}

==Joining of Clan Bagrakh==
[[File:ON-misc-Seal of Clan Bagrakh.png|right|thumb|The seal of Clan Bagrakh]]
King Ranser's forces were now at a disadvantage and were losing the ground they had gained at the onset of the war. They were pushed back towards Shornhelm over the course of the next several months. To make the situation worse, House Tamrith withdrew its support for Ranser and called for peace. House Dorell did the same shortly after.{{ref|name=HTARH}} In contrast, King Emeric cannily leveraged his political ties to gain yet more allies to aid the war effort. He employed privateers against Ranser's allies in the Systres. These ships took part in naval battles and sank enemy ships off the coasts of the islands.{{ref|[[Online:Jocien Rane|Jocien Rane]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Gulf of Time|A Gulf of Time]]}} Furthermore, Emeric dispatched his "chronicler" [[Lore:Zephrine Frey|Zephrine Frey]] (in reality a trusted spy) to meet with the king's friend Kurog gro-Bagrakh, a young [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] chieftain in his mid-20s.{{Ref|name=OrsiniumAuA|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ESO Orsinium AUA|ns_base=General}}}}

At the time Chief Kurog and his [[Lore:Bagrakh Clan|clan]] were warring against the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] clans of [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath]] in an attempt to reclaim the former chiefdom of [[Lore:Yashnag gro-Yazgu|Yashnag gro-Yazgu]] which had been lost in {{Year|2E 467}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Skyrim}}}} Zephrine caught up with Kurog in the mountains outside of [[Lore:Dragonstar|Dragonstar]] where he had encamped his forces. She was a longtime friend of the chief and listened as Kurog explained the difficulties of his campaign before presenting him with documents drawn up by Emeric that detailed an offer of mutual benefit if Kurog allied with him against Ranser. With Zephrine assuring him it was no Breton trickery, the two talked long into the night.

By the next morning Kurog had a list of demands for Zephrine to return to the king. The demands were great but Emeric knew that the entry of these Orcs into the war could end the conflict much more quickly. He agreed to these demands and once the signed agreement was in Kurog's hands he turned his forces toward their new enemy.{{ref|name=TCoKK|{{Cite Book|The Chronicles of King Kurog}}}}

Word of the new alliance that Emeric had forged with the Orcs eventually reached King Ranser who went mad with rage. His response was to order his Spymaster [[Lore:Gerard Althen|Gerard Althen]] to arrest and interrogate all the Orcs living in Rivenspire. If they resisted or were found suspicious in any way, they were to be executed.{{ref|[[Online:Gerard Althen|Gerard Althen]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Crimes of the Past|Crimes of the Past]]}} These orders led to the deaths of hundreds of innocent Orcs.{{ref|[[Online:Marisette|Marisette]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Crimes of the Past|Crimes of the past]]}}

==Destruction of Shornhelm and The Battle of Markwasten Moor==
[[File:ON-map-Westmark_Moor.jpg|thumb|right|The region of Westmark Moor (Markwasten Moor)]]
With Kurog's Orcs in position to the north of Ranser's retreating forces, the city of Shornhelm was open to attack while its defenders were still to the south. In its underdefended state, the Orcish troops descended upon the city. The Bretons of Shornhelm had made up the leadership of the assault that had sacked Orsinium in {{Year|2E 431}},{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}} a fact that the Orcs were aware of. They saw to it that the Bretons of Shornhelm paid their debt in full.

By the time Ranser and his forces reached Shornhelm, it was already ablaze.{{ref|name=TFoKR}} At the onset of the war, Ranser had been so confident of his victory over Wayrest that he had preemptively distributed victory flags commemorating it{{ref|[[Online:Treasures K#King Ranser Victory Flag|King Ranser Victory Flag]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and had maps drawn up depicting Rivenspire's theoretically extended borders.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures F#Fanciful Map of Rivenspire|Fanciful Map of Rivenspire]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Now, after nearly a year of conflict, it was his capital that was burned and looted, with the Orcs taking war spoils from the city.{{ref|name=TotOF|[[Online:Tusks of the Orc-Father|Tusks of the Orc-Father]] codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

It was at this point that House Montclair surrendered to Wayrest's alliance.{{ref|name=HTARH}} With the city and much of the kingdom despoiled, Ranser moved his remaining forces east to the region of [[Lore:Markwasten Moor|Markwasten Moor]]. Encircled by Emeric's allies, they prepared for a final stand.{{ref|name=TTFoKR|{{Cite Book|The True Fate of King Ranser}}}} At the ensuing Battle of Markwasten Moor, Wayrest's alliance almost completely annihilated Ranser's army.{{ref|name=TFoKR}} The Battle of Markwasten Moor was a decisive victory for Emeric's forces, destroying the bulk of Ranser's army.{{ref|name=TFoKR}}{{ref|name=SCD|[[Online:Scholar Cantier|Scholar Cantier]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The battle was so devastating that years after the end of the war, some believed that Ranser died at Markwasten Moor.{{ref|name=SCD}} However, in reality, Ranser was able to escape with a small contingent of soldiers and aides and retreat to some ruins atop a nameless tor overlooking the city of Shornhelm to make their true last stand.{{ref|name=TTFoKR}}

==The Battle of Traitor's Tor==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''"I may have lost Shornhelm, but this war is far from over! The damnable Emeric is overconfident. He will make a mistake, and my brilliance will leap to take advantage of every misstep and stumble along the way.

''"Retreating—no, strike that! King Ranser doesn't retreat!"''

''"My strategic occupation of this tor shall be remembered as the most important military victory in Rivenspire's history. Let them throw all the Orcs they have at me. This tor will stand forever! I shall win this war!"''-[[Lore:King Ranser's Tirade|King Ranser's Tirade]]}}
[[File:ON-npc-Ranser Soldier 04.jpg|thumb|right|A Ranser and Wrothgar soldier do battle]][[File:ON-npc-General_Dathieu.jpg|thumb|left|General Dathieu surveys a losing battle]]

Ranser's remnants took up defensive positions atop the tor with Ranser proclaiming that there would be no more retreat{{ref|name=KRT|{{Cite Book|King Ranser's Tirade}}}} but the reality was that they were backed into a corner anyway. Kurog's Orcs surrounded and began to attack the ruins. Ranser's most loyal and trusted advisor General Dathieu attempted to defend the ruin's walls against the attackers, even using unconventional methods such as sleep scrolls against them to no avail. The Orcs breached the crumbling walls of the ruins and the last of Ranser's wounded and battered troops defended themselves with sword and arrow.{{ref|name=ATT|Events of [[Online:A Traitor's Tale|A Traitor's Tale]]}} The army that had once besieged Wayrest was now reduced to no more than a few dozen troops which included the King's personal guard.{{ref|name=GDD|[[Online:General Dathieu|General Dathieu]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Traitor's Tale|A Traitor's Tale]]}}

Dathieu saw the hopelessness in continuing to fight and saw surrender as the only viable option that remained. The King, however, dismissed the notion, preferring instead to continue fighting to the end. The King's court magician [[Lore:Reezal-Jul|Reezal-Jul]] had told him about a strategy involving [[Lore:Magic|magic]] that would still allow the battle and perhaps the war, to be won. Despite his doubts the general sent a lieutenant to carry out this plan but maintained that if it did not bring victory then he would try to convince Ranser to surrender. Adding that it seemed the King's mental state had deteriorated since the disastrous loss at Markwasten Moor.{{ref|name=GDD}}

This lieutenant, whose name is lost to history, carried out their orders and followed through with the steps needed for this ritual. They then reported to General Dathieu that this effort had been fruitless and nothing had happened.{{ref|name=ATT}} The general decided that surrender was now the only option and sent the lieutenant to deliver these demands to the King. Ranser would hear none of it and instead insisted that the magical ritual was working as intended. Commanding the opposite of surrender, he instead gave orders for a counterattack. His personal guard under Captain Taern were to charge the Orcs ranks where they had breached the north wall. General Dathieu was ordered to lend his remaining soldiers to this last ditch attack. Ranser himself planned to join them on the battlefield.{{ref|name=KRD|[[Online:King Ranser|King Ranser]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Traitor's Tale|A Traitor's tale]]}}

Upon receiving these orders, the general was convinced of the King's madness and refused to follow the order, not willing to waste more lives on a lost cause. He planned to supersede the King's authority and restrain him so that they may surrender. He wished for Captain Taern to join him but she had already received the orders and would likely follow through with them. Dathieu sent his trusted lieutenant on one more mission to convince Taern to delay the charge while he went to confront Ranser personally.{{ref|name=GDD}}

Ranser still would not listen to reason after being confronted and instead declared Dathieu a coward.{{ref|name=KRD}} Seeing no other way to save the lives of his men, the general took matters into his own hands, quite literally stabbing King Ranser in the back. King Ranser's blood was spilled on the ground by his own general. Unfortunately, the King's blood served as the necessary catalyst for the ritual. He rose after death and cursed Dathieu who in his attempt to save the lives of his men had damned them to a cursed undeath. The Orcs wiped out the rest of the defenders who later rose again, bound to Ranser.{{ref|name=TTFoKR}} The [[Lore:Crown of Shornhelm|Crown of Shornhelm]] was lost and only Reezal-Jul survived the final battle, having managed to slink away after telling the king of the ritual.{{ref|name=GDD}} Traitor's Tor as it would come to be called was left to rot as a cursed place.

With Ranser fallen and his forces either surrendered or destroyed, the war had come to a tragic and bloody end.

==Aftermath==
Wayrest and its allies were victorious over Shornhelm and Emeric threw a party for the nobility of High Rock in celebration.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures C#Commemorative Corkscrew|Commemorative Corkscrew]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Afterwards there were changes to politics, holdings, borders, and new alliances were forged.

===The Council of The North===
[[File:ON-banner-Daggerfall Covenant.png|thumb|right|Banner of the Daggerfall Covenant]]
In the immediate aftermath of King Ranser's death, Rivenspire fell into chaos with its capital burned and lands ravaged by war. The line of House Branquette had ended and the Throne of Shornhelm sat empty. Much of the house's former domains were parceled out to Houses Tamrith and Dorell.{{ref|name=SCCotN}} Ranser's former allies in the region claimed that they had been forced to join his side and field troops against Wayrest. It was this and their withdrawal of support from Ranser that allowed them to keep their positions after the war. After the war, Baron [[Lore:Esmark Tamrith|Esmark Tamrith]] emerged as a powerful force for cooperation, and he proposed the formation of a triumvirate, loyal to the victorious Emeric, to govern the region in the King's name. This governing body would consist of the heads of Houses Tamrith, Dorell, and Montclair and be named the Council of the North. Emeric approved of the Council of the North to rule in place of a monarch until he selected a new King of Shornhelm.{{ref|name=HTARH}} The Council of the North attempted to keep peace and order in Rivenspire until the selection of a new king.{{ref|name=SCCotN}} The city of Shornhelm was also rebuilt.{{ref|[[Online:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Consequences in the Systres and elsewhere===
Not all of Ranser's former allies were welcomed back into the fold. House Mornard, who had already been looked upon with disfavor before the war, faced punishment after it. Once Emeric had dealt with matters on the mainland, he turned to the Systres and the King's justice against them was swift. Emeric issued judgment against House Mornard for acts of war and sedition. Avrippe Mornard was immediately stripped of his title of Duke of the Systres and exiled to [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] where he died a year later. House Mornard also lost a full two-thirds of its holdings. These lands and the ducal title were given to their foe, [[Lore:House Dufort|House Dufort]]. House Dufort was granted rulership of the duchy and the future leaders of House Mornard had their rank reduced to Count.{{ref|name=SHV7}} Emeric was not without some mercy; House Mornard was allowed to retain what lands it had left along with its mining concerns and trade routes.{{ref|name=HOHM|{{Cite Book|History of House Mornard}}}} However, Emeric's mercy came with a warning: if House Mornard ever again betrayed its oaths and duties, its name and holdings would be utterly stricken from the face of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=EJ}}

The smaller House Marcott also suffered for its betrayal. The house fell out of favor after the war with many of them turning to criminal pursuits to get by.{{ref|[[Online:Finia Marcott|Finia Marcott]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-npc-King Fahara'jad 02.jpg|thumb|right|King Fahara'jad]]
===Forming of the Greater Daggerfall Covenant===
The victorious alliance of Breton kingdoms, the Redguards of Sentinel, and Kurog's Orcs went on to form a great multinational alliance known as the second or Greater Daggerfall Covenant. Emeric was the architect of this new alliance and claimed supreme leadership of its ruling council, being crowned High King.{{ref|name=GttDC}} Fahara'jad had enemies among the kingdoms of southern Hammerfell so it was a cunning move to join with the covenant. This new power drove him to declare himself High King of Hammerfell despite his enemies objections. His new political power made his claims too difficult to challenge.{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}} Emeric kept his agreements with Kurog gro-Bagrakh despite the objections of [[Lore:Crowns|Crown]] Redguards and some nobles in Rivenspire. Incorporating the Orcs into the covenant happened through several demands being met such as no Redguard or Breton armies being allowed to enter [[Lore:Wrothgar|Wrothgar]]{{ref|[[Online:Ambassador Lazgara|Ambassador Lazgara]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Invitation to Orsinium|Invitation to Orsinium]]}} and the return of [[Lore:Mammoth|Mammoth]] ivory taken from Orc lands in previous conflicts.{{ref|name=TotOF}} The most consequential of these negotiations was Emeric's original offer of presenting Kurog with a Kingdom of his own to build a new [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]. With these three groups allied, a grand new alliance was formed.

===Ranser's Legacy===
King Ranser was known in life as a wise and courageous King by those who trusted him.{{ref|name=GDD}} In death, though, he was remembered as a traitor to the covenant. His image was so tarnished that his former capital city hosted annual plays depicting him as a madman.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures SA-SI#Shornhelm Players Performance Tickets|Shornhelm Players Performance Tickets]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Rumors also persisted that Ranser had slept with every sibling in the royal line and that his daughter Rayelle was the product of one of these incestuous relationships, although scholars dismissed these notions.{{Ref|name=LABAHI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Bretons & High Isle}}}}

The Spymaster Gerard Althen was hunted as a war criminal for his killing of innocents during the war.{{ref|[[Online:Crimes of the Past|Crimes of the Past]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Reezal-Jul found a place in House Montclair. General Dathieu was remembered as the betrayer of his King, the true story of the events not being known until years later.

Ranser's undead occupation persisted for over a decade upon the tor. He never made a move against his enemies, instead deciding to rule over the ruins.{{ref|name=TTFoKR}} Eventually, the curse that had been unleashed was broken and he and his soldiers were laid to rest finally at peace in death.{{ref|name=ATT}}
