[[Lore:Slavery|Slavery]] is the practice of owning an individual as property. It was once widespread throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], ranging from the practice of using defeated enemies as slaves, [[Lore:The Argonian Account, Book 4|selling one's own children]] to combat hunger, or invading land specifically to capture slaves, as the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] long did to [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]. The enslavement of [[Lore:Men|men]], [[Lore:Orsimer|Orcs]], and [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] by [[Lore:Mer|elven]] [[Lore:Races|races]] was traditionally permitted.<noinclude>

==Origins==
Slavery has existed on Tamriel since the [[Lore:Merethic Era#Early Merethic Era|Early Merethic Era]], when the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] arrived on the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]] and enslaved the native [[Lore:Goblin|goblins]].{{ref|name=AE12|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored, Chapter XII}}}}{{ref|name=ONToothmaul|[[Online:Toothmaul Gully|Toothmaul Gully]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The slave trade has existed since at least the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], when the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] grandmaster [[Lore:Thalthil Dres|Thalthil Dres]]' raid on [[Lore:Thorn|Thorn]] secured the trade for his house.{{ref|name=UHD|{{Cite Book|Understanding House Dres}}}}{{ref|name=Thorn|[[ESO:Treasures D#Dres Grandmaster Memorial Medal|Dres Grandmaster Memorial Medal]] treasure description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] slavetraders plagued Tamriel by the hundreds in the First Era, but they were destroyed by the machinations of folk-hero [[Lore:Lyrisius|Lyrisius]] and have not threatened Tamriel since.{{ref|name=TSoL|{{Cite Book|The Story of Lyrisius}}}}

==Slavery by Practitioning Race==
===Altmer===
The [[Lore:Altmer|High Elves]] are known for enslaving goblins,{{ref|[[Online:Contraband G#Goblin-Sized Weighted Shackles|Goblin-Sized Weighted Shackles]] item description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} using them for menial tasks such as mucking out dung pits and cleaning floors. Often being beaten by their elven owners, this has been known to give the goblins forms of mental trauma with escaped goblins still sweeping and being frightened of others.{{ref|name=byz|[[Online:Byz|Byz]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Goblins have also been used traditionally by the Altmer to supplement their armies.{{ref|name=FH|[[Tribunal:Fedris Hler|Fedris Hler]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]:'' ''[[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} They use other [[Lore:Goblin-ken|goblin-ken]] as slaves as well, such as [[Lore:Ogre|ogres]] and [[Lore:Gremlin|gremlins]].{{Ref|name=PGE1:WR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}} The High Elves have also been noted to enslave members of other elven races, such as Orcs and the Dunmer [[Lore:Ulvul Llaren|Ulvul Llaren]].{{Ref|name=PGE1:WR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Light Armor Forging}}}}

===Argonian===
The [[Lore:Archein|Archeins]] are a tribe of [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] infamous for their kleptocracy.{{Ref|name=TAA4|{{Cite Book|The Argonian Account, Book 4}}}} They were often embezzling money and power to themselves at the expense of their race. Some of these examples include selling other Argonians into slavery for the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]{{Ref|name=FP|{{Cite Book|Freedom's Price}}}} or exploiting their land for foreign interest, such as the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]].{{Ref|name=PGE3E|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Argonia}}}}

To help with the building of their city, [[Lore:Mazzatun|Mazzatun]] the Argonian tribe of [[Lore:Xit-Xaht|Xit-Xaht]] often captured people for months. The Xit-Xaht rarely talked next to those prisoners, but the members of the tribe were noticed to never stop moving, as they pointed and whipped as if they were mad, with one of the captives believing that the tribe would turn feral without the guidance of their leader.{{ref|name=CJ|{{Cite Book|Captive's Journal|ns_base=ON}}}}

===Ayleid===
[[File:OBR-statue-Alessia.jpg|A statue of Alessia the Slave Queen in her shackles|thumb|left]][[File:LO-misc-Ayleid Pedestal.png|thumb|right|A pedestal carving depicting Nedic slavery]]{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''And this thing I have thought of, I have named it, and I call it freedom.''|Alessia while speaking to [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]]{{Ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}}}
The [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] started their long history with slavery when they conquered the [[Lore:City Isle|Eight Islands]], taking the native [[Lore:Bird Men|bird men]]{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}} as slaves and making use of their literacy.{{ref|name=Decentius|[[OB:Decentius Opsius|Decentius Opsius]]' dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} According to legend, after [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] mysteriously vanished from [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] during the Merethic Era, the Ayleids were able to conquer and enslave the local [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]].{{ref|name=SatD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} At first, their capture was occasional, but it grew systematic and widespread.{{ref|name=DWTA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Ayleids}}}}{{ref|name=Adabal|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}} In [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]], the [[Lore:Barsaebic Ayleids|Barsaebic Ayleids]] captured Argonians as slaves, prizing their resilience.{{ref|name=Scholars|Dialogue between [[ESO:Ayleid Scholar|Ayleid Scholars]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Ayleids also took [[Lore:Duraki|Duraki]] slaves in [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]], but the specifics of the period are unknown, with later Duraki architecture displaying little to no Ayleid trace.{{ref|name=NotD}}

Ayleid slavery was cruel and reviled. To honor the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Princes]], the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]-worshipping [[Lore:Daedraphiles|Daedraphile]] clans tortured the Nedes, with the practice of [[Lore:Flesh Sculpture|flesh-sculpture]] and gut-gardening becoming a particularly widespread form of "art".{{ref|name=Adabal}}{{ref|name=DWTA}} Specific implements of art-torture, used upon slaves for strange pleasures, included the wailing wheels of [[Lore:Vindasel|Vindasel]] and the gut-gardens of Sercen. In the city-state of [[Lore:Hadhuul|Fire King Hadhuul]], slave children were incinerated for sport, and others were forced to inhale [[Lore:Drugs|drugs]], giving them visions of unseen torment. Some tribes of Nedes were completely wiped out by these religious practices, such as the [[Lore:Men-of-ge|men-of-ge]], who were sacrificed by [[Lore:Nilichi|Flower King Nilichi]] to an [[Lore:Insect God|insect god]]. Slaves were often dehumanized, unable to keep family or choose a name.{{ref|name=Adabal}}

Led by [[Lore:Alessia|Slave-Queen Alessia]], the enslaved Nedes rose against the Ayleids in the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] circa {{Year|1E 242}}.{{ref|name=SatD}} Some city-states of the [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire]] opposed slavery and assisted the Alessians in their revolt, although the resulting economic transition to a slave-less economy was wrenching.{{ref|name=ALoSaS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}}{{ref|name=Dynar|[[ON:King Laloriaran Dynar|King Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The rebellion was ultimately successful, toppling the Ayleid Empire one year later in {{Year|1E 243}}. Though the now-free Alessians led the new [[Lore:Alessian Empire|empire]], it is inconclusive whether slavery ever ended under their rule. The cultural perception of the Ayleids as brutal slavemasters persisted for centuries after their extinction.{{ref|name=TLKotA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} Indeed, the revulsion for Daedra worship that pervades most human cultures in Tamriel probably originated from the treatment of Nedic slaves by the Daedraphiles in the Late Merethic.{{ref|name=DWTA}}

===Bosmer===
Tribal [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] have the custom of the "[[Lore:Mourning War|Mourning War]]". After the war-time death of a tribe member, the clan responsible for their death is raided for captives. Typically only one, but if the deceased was of notable importance multiple hostages may be taken. These captives serve as "replacements" for the slain Bosmer and are physically tortured for an extensive period of time. When finally mentally broken, the hostages are instead happily welcomed into the clan and mentally bewildered by this sudden change of behavior, submitting to their torturers. Typically, they're also given the possessions, position and family of the Wood Elf they're replacing, though in modern times this is less common.{{ref|{{Cite Book|War Customs of the Tribal Bosmer}}}}

===Breton===
[[File:ON-npc-Enslaved Miner.jpg|A miner enslaved by the Red Rooks|thumb|right]]
[[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] have typically been on the receiving end of slavery, and had their origins as a race by being progenated by generations of Elven intermingling and slavery.{{ref|name=PGE1HR}} Despite this, during the era of the High Rock [[Lore:Coin-barons|coin-barons]], slavery was a fact of life for Bretons.{{ref|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1470462346 Twitch Stream of ''High Isle Dev Discussion''], time stamp 1:19:32-1:20:20}} The island of [[Lore:Amenos|Amenos]] was a stronghold for slavers from [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], though by the time of [[Lore:Emeric|High King Emeric]] this was no longer the case.{{ref|[[Online:High King Emeric|High King Emeric]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{Ref|name=MT|{{Cite Book|Mara's Tear}}}} However, some Bretons still practiced slavery during his reign, such as House Velmont in Hew's Bane,{{ref|name=WS|[[ESO:Walks-Softly|Walks-Softly]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}}{{ref|name=AV|[[ESO:Anais Velmont|Anais Velmont]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}} and bandit groups such as the Red Rooks.{{ref|[[Online:Enslaved Miner (Crosswych)|Enslaved Miners]] in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}} At least one vizier in High Rock purchased an entire tribe of Goblins for use as slaves from House Dres.{{ref|name=AD|[[Online:Aldryn Dres|Aldryn Dres]]' dialogue in {{g|ESO}}}}

===Dragon===
After migrating to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] from [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]], the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] became increasingly strict.{{ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} Those who resisted the influence of the cult became the slaves of the iron-fisted [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priests]],{{ref|name=Yvara|[[ON:Yvara Plouff|Yvara Plouff]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} the underlings of the [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]] themselves. In [[Lore:Valthume|Valthume]], the domain of [[Lore:Hevnoraak|Hevnoraak]], citizens would be tortured and humiliated.{{ref|name=Valdar|[[SR:Valdar|Valdar]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Thousands died in the [[Lore:Dragon War|rebellion]] against the Dragon Cult, in which some Dragons betrayed [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] after seeing his tyranny.{{ref|name=Paarthurnax|[[SR:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Dreugh===
According to the teachings of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], "the [[Lore:Mundex Terrene|Mundex Terrene]] was once ruled over solely by the tyrant [[Lore:dreugh|dreugh]]-kings, each to their own dominion, and borderwars fought between their slave oceans... No one that lived did so outside of the sufferance of the dreughs." This lasted until [[Lore:Lyg|Lyg]] was destroyed by [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], and he declared "each of the nineteen and nine and nine oceans Free."{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}}

===Dunmer===
{{Quote Box|''After that, there was magic sleep, I think. When it wore off, I was in the belly of a stinking Dark Elf boat.''|Jhaka, former slave{{ref|[[Online:Jhaka|Jhaka]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}}}
[[File:RG-comic-Page 14-Slaver Camp.jpg|thumb|right|300px|A House Dres slaver camp near [[Lore:Tear|Tear]]]]
Most slaves in Morrowind were beastfolk, such as Khajiit, [[Lore:Imga|Imga]], and Orcs, with Argonians being the most commonly kept slaves. The Dunmer historically considered all beastpeoples and [[Lore:Men|humans]] to be mere "animals", and as such "no more than domesticated chattels".{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} Since the advent of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], slavery was illegal throughout the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]], but protected by law in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. This exception was granted by Tiber Septim in the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] of {{Year|2E 896}} as a concession to the Dunmer, who saw the use of slaves as their ancient right. Recent developments have seen the rise of abolitionists such as the [[Lore:Twin Lamps|Twin Lamps]], often with support from members of the leading families on Vvardenfell. Slavery remained legal in Morrowind throughout the lifespan of the Third Empire, provoking strong reactions on both sides.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Brown Book of 3E 426}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Eastern Provinces}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|For my Gods and Emperor}}}} It was only by Imperial times that the Dunmer reluctantly acknowledged humans as people, with many Dunmer at the end of the Third Era avoiding keeping human slaves of any nation so as to not offend humans, although slaves with [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] features were common the province's north.{{Ref|name=PoM}}

It is by demonstrating our superior commitment to civilization and humanity that we best disprove the Dunmer's barbaric insistence that we are no more than animals."'' —[[Lore:Im-Kilaya|Im-Kilaya]], Head of the Argonian Mission in Ebonheart

[[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] thrived on mass abduction of Argonians and [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] into slavery, often with the support of local rival warlords selling their own kind.{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Argonian Account}}}} Dres families worked Argonian slaves on large [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]] plantations on the southeast coast of Morrowind, while others profited from the slave trade itself.{{ref|name=UHD}} They held thousands in their infamous slave-pens of [[Lore:Tear|Tear]], to be shipped by smugglers to cave holding pens on the [[Lore:Bitter Coast|Bitter Coast]]. The plantations of [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] nobles made widespread use of slaves as farm labor. In comparison, slavery was uncommon among the members of [[Lore:House Redoran|House Redoran]], though some of their warriors used slaves, pitting them against beasts for entertainment.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Radiant Conversations|Radiant Conversations]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{citation needed|source for Redoran involvement?}} [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] used slaves as servants for housework and as labor in mines. Towns like [[Lore:Molag Mar|Molag Mar]], [[Lore:Suran|Suran]] and [[Lore:Tel Aruhn|Tel Aruhn]] had regular slave markets.

In the aftermath of the [[Lore:Vvardenfell Crisis|Vvardenfell Crisis]], there were a few rebellious landowners who attempted to eliminate slavery in their lands. Some were murdered by their slaves, some were sabotaged and ruined by their House, and others were driven into poverty until they had no option but to sell their land to their neighbors who still kept slaves.{{ref|name=TedPetersonPostsHHM|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 9: High Rock Highjinks|''High Rock Highjinks'']]}} In a shrewd political maneuver, King [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|Helseth]], representing his own house of Hlaalu, formed an alliance with House Dres. Subsequently, he renounced the slave trade in {{Year|3E 430}},{{ref|name=TPPlayingHouses|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 12: Playing Houses|''Playing Houses'']]}} setting the remaining Houses of Indoril and Redoran against him in a bloody civil war.{{citation needed}} Helseth emerged from the war battered, but without the loss of his essential powers, leaving the Morrowind slave trade generally destroyed.{{ref|name=PGE3M|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}}

===Dwemer===
After their defeat at the hands of the invading Atmorans during [[Lore:Merethic Era Wars#The Return|The Return]], particularly at the [[Lore:Battle of the Moesring|Battle of the Moesring]],{{ref|name=The Betrayed|{{Cite Book|The Betrayed}}}} many [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] across Skyrim were forced to accept an offer of sanctuary from the Dwemer. As per the terms of their stay, the elves consumed a toxic fungus that rendered them blind. At first, they were servants, but eventually, the Snow Elves became slaves.{{ref|name=TF:AS|{{Cite Book|The Falmer: A Study}}}} Additionally, the [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]] Clan of Dwemer conquered Craglorn after their exodus of Resdayn, enslaving the local Duraki, using the Nedes' labor in their settlements. The Duraki seemed to respect the Dwarves' mastery of stonework, requesting burial amidst their ruins{{ref|name=Erold|[[ON:Erold|Erold]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} and utilizing their techniques in a crude way for their own structures.{{ref|name=NotD}}

The Snow Elves were herded into slave pens,{{ref|name=Irk|[[SR:Irkngthand|Irkngthand Slave Pens]] appearance and name in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} where they were subjected to torture at the hands of the Dwemer.{{ref|name=Karliah|[[SR:Karliah|Karliah]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In [[Lore:Irkngthand|Irkngthand]], the enslaved mer were forced to build their Great Statue in secret, tricking their masters into believing they had confiscated the Eyes of the Falmer.{{ref|name=TLW|{{Cite Book|Thief's Last Words|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} After generations of enslavement, the Snow Elves rebelled against the Dwarves, managing to temporarily overthrow them and take refuge in [[Lore:Blackreach|Blackreach]]'s deepest caverns. The ensuing [[Lore:War of the Crag|War of the Crag]] raged bitterly for years, until {{Year|1E 700}},{{ref|name=DW1|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol I}}}} when the Dwemer race vanished from Tamriel.{{ref|name=TF:AS}} Over time, the once-proud race degraded both mentally and physically, becoming known as the [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]].{{ref|name=Gelebor|[[SR:Knight-Paladin Gelebor|Knight-Paladin Gelebor]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[SR:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}

===Falmer===
[[File:SR-npc-Falmer Servant.jpg|right|thumb|A Falmer servant]]
According to legend, the Falmer emerge from their caverns in the dead of night to capture infants.{{ref|name=TF:AS}} They have been known to take captives from the surface.{{ref|[[Online:Alone in the Dark|Alone in the Dark]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Within the Silent City of Blackreach, the Falmer own slaves who act as guards and lookouts.{{ref|Appearance and behavior of [[SR:Falmer Servant|Falmer Servants]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They also use slaves for mining work.{{ref|Appearance and behavior of [[ON:Blackreach Thrall|Blackreach Thralls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

===Imperial===
During and after [[Lore:Euraxia Tharn|Queen Euraxia Tharn]]'s invasion of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire]] purchased slaves from [[Lore:Maormer|Sea Elf]] privateers. Queen Euraxia used clever loopholes to avoid the workers being considered slaves by law, but the truth was clear.{{ref|name=Abnur|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} The slaves were forced to mine for new ores in the desert.{{ref|name=Jahhouz|[[ON:Jahhouz|Jahhouz]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} After the [[Lore:Wars#Battle of Pale Pass|Battle of]] [[Lore:Pale Pass|Pale Pass]], the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] kept [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] slaves which played a significant part in establishing the administrative structures of the Empire, as well as in the training of its military.{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s army sold captive Bretons into slavery after the [[Lore:Wars#Battle of Sancre Tor|Battle of]] [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Battle of Sancre Tor}}}} In the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], using [[Lore:Ogre|ogres]] for slave labor was legal in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|[[Oblivion:Lord Drad|Lord Drad]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

When the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] took over [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]] during the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], they turned its people into slaves and forced them to work solely to feed them, while leaving little else for themselves.{{Ref|name=ONVyctoria|[[Online:Vyctoria Girien|Vyctoria Girien]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Young men were forcefully extracted from their homes and recruited into their ranks. When one commoner named [[Lore:Donus Falto|Donus Falto]] fled the town, it sparked the rebellion between the Imperials and the people.{{Ref|name=ONVyctoria}}{{Ref|name=ONFalto|[[Online:Tertius Falto|Tertius Falto]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Khajiit===
The Thazahrr Cartel is a slavery organization founded by the Khajiit Thazahrr-ra. It is based in [[Lore:Abah's Landing|Abah's Landing]] in southern Hammerfell and handles the slave industry in the region. Only a few Khajiit make up its ranks as the group is also comprised of Imperials, Bretons, and Redguards. By {{Year|2E 582}}, Thazahrr Goldfang was the sixth person to take the name and rank of Merchant Lord in the cartel. When the Shadow Conglomerate emerged in power, the cartel lost prominence, something that Thazahrr Goldfang hoped to reclaim and in turn, acquire the same honorific as her ancestor.{{Ref|name=ALML4|{{Cite Book|Abah's Landing Merchant Lords, V. 4}}}} In the same year, Khajiiti pirates raided [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] for Argonian slaves.{{Ref|name=TRV|Events of [[Online:The Ravaged Village|The Ravaged Village]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TSSM|Events of [[Online:The Saving of Silent Mire|The Saving of Silent Mire]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Reachfolk===
As a fractured society, each of the [[Lore:Reachfolk Clans|Reachfolk Clans]] has its own set of rules and policies. Certain clans,{{Ref|name=General|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4|Ask Us Anything: Variety Pack 4]]}} such as [[Lore:Crow-Wife Clan|Crow-Wives]],{{Ref|name=AB1|{{Cite Book|A Life Barbaric and Brutal}}}} [[Lore:Dreadhorn Clan|Dreadhorns]],{{Ref|name=Filand|[[ON:Filand|Filand]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Thornroot Clan|Thornroots]] were known for keeping slaves and captives. This was not a practice accepted by many other clans, such as [[Lore:Hillhunter Clan|Hillhunters]].{{ref|Events of [[Online:Betrayal at Briar Rock|Betrayal at Briar Rock]] in [[Online:Markarth|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Bralthahawn|[[ON:Bralthahawn|Bralthahawn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Medone|[[ON:Medone|Medone]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Freedom was considered to be of great importance in the Reach.{{ref|name=Arana|[[ON:Arana|Arana]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Ard [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]] of Markarth offered food, freedom, and protection to all his subjects,{{Ref|name=AC|[[ON:Ard Caddach|Ard Caddach]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} former captives of the other clans were free in Markarth.{{ref|name=Kjaggus|[[ON:Kjaggus|Kjaggus]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Wayward Guardians|Wayward Guardians]] were known to detest captivity and slavery, "not wishing a fate like that on anyone."{{Ref|name=Ardanir|[[ON:Ardanir|Ardanir]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Redguard===
The waves of [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] who fled the sinking of Yokuda enslaved many of the native beastfolk and Nedes of Hammerfell.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} The Duraki, the Nedes of [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]], were conquered and enslaved by the [[Lore:Anka-Ra|Anka-Ra]], who deemed them a backward people deserving of nothing more.{{ref|name=NotD|{{Cite Book|Nedes of the Deathlands}}}}

===Sload===
[[Lore:Sload|Sload]] merchants are known to purchase large quantities of slaves from Tear. It is suspected that they bring them to their native [[Lore:Thras|Thras]] for usage in [[Lore:Necromancy|Necromancy]].{{ref|namt=CP1|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation v I}}}}

===Snow Elf===
According to Nordic account, during the snow elven raid on [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]], the elves took slaves from the human population. They reasoned that the Bretons, after generations of intermingling with elves, were the descendants of these slaves,{{ref|name=PGE1HR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} but this is disputed.{{ref|name=TB:MoP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}}

===Tsaesci===
The Tsaesci have attempted, at one point, to enslave the Tang Mo. Additionally, they successfully enslaved the goblins of their nearby islands, using them for labor and for blood, which they consume for their [[Lore:Vampire|vampiric]] satiation. They similarly enslaved the Red [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]] after an attempt to "consume" them,{{ref|name=MystAka}} going on to use them as [[Lore:Air Transportation#Dragons|war mounts]].{{ref|name=PGE1C}}
