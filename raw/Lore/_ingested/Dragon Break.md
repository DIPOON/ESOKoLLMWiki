A [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] is a phenomenon where linear time is broken, and becomes non-linear. The "Dragon" refers to [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], the Dragon God of Time. A Dragon Break not only precedes significant changes in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], but challenges mortal comprehension.{{ref|name=WWYDB}} Described as a "catastrophic disruption of linear time", it is a re-alignment of time and space in response to an event that makes the normal continuity of reality impossible.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The chaos that ensues is a refrain of the chaos of the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]].{{ref|name=Before|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The area that is noticeably affected, and length of the interval measured in the areas not apparently affected, varies with each Dragon Break. Historically, the cause is often attributed to mortals manipulating divine matters.{{ref|name=WWYDB}}{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

==Known Breaks==
===The Middle Dawn===
[[File:ON-item-Staff of Towers.jpg|thumb|right|The Staff of Towers, used to bring about the Middle Dawn]]
A sect of the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]], the [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selective]], is said to have caused the longest known Dragon Break—known as the '''Middle Dawn''' (or, colloquially, as "the" Dragon Break)—which is thought to have spanned one thousand and eight years,{{ref|name=WWYDB}}{{ref|name=TDBR}} from the 13th to the 23rd centuries in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=GEN|Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?}}}}  The Selectives believed that Akatosh, the Supreme Spirit—of "unitary essence" and originally a spirit of "humanadic purity"—had been subject to the insertion of an "[[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] taint" and needed to be "restored" to its pure state via the elimination of the elements of Elven [[Lore:Auriel|Auriel]] from Imperial [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]—an act they called the "Sacred Expungement".  In attempting to enact the Expungement through the use of the [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]], the Selectives damaged the fabric of time and caused the Middle-Dawn Dragon Break.{{ref|name=Vindication|{{Cite Book|Vindication for the Dragon Break}}}}{{ref|name=Gahgdar|[[ON:Gahgdar|Gahgdar]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic]] Loremaster [[Lore:Celarus|Celarus]] claimed that misuse of the Staff of Towers had played a part in bringing about the Dragon Break, although he did not specify by whom.  According to Celarus, Dragon Breaks could be created by the "impossibly dense [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbic]] gyre" formed by the coalescence of the Staff's power.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}  This is the only Dragon Break that is a universally-known event, though some civilizations claim to have been protected from it through the intervention of a number of [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Princes]].{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}  [[Lore:Mnemoli the Blue Star|Mnemoli the Blue Star]], a [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] associated with un-time events, grew bright enough during this Dragon Break to be seen in the daytime sky.{{ref|name=EOMN|{{Cite Book|Exegesis of Merid-Nunda}}}}

One pre–[[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Riddle'Thar-Epiphany]] [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] account describes how, during the Middle-Dawn, [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]] was called from her battle with [[Lore:Malacath|Orkha]] by the [[Lore:Mnemo-Li|Blue Star]] and transported atop the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]] by [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]].  There, Boethra saw the Selectives speaking lies in a way that made them true, drawing runes in an attempt to realize a world wherein neither [[Lore:Akha|Akha]], nor [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]], nor [[Lore:Alduin|Alkhan]], nor any [[Lore:Dragon|Child of Akha]], nor any of the myriad kingdoms Akha had created along the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]]—any of the lands he'd seeded and brought into his kingdom—had ever existed.  Seeing the lie the Selectives were attempting to make real, Boethra—who had once been exiled to the Many Paths by Akha—began to wonder if she had ever been the Daughter of Blades at all, or if it had all been merely the dream of someone who had never even existed; and, so-thinking, felt something akin to fear for the first time.  Knowing they must not succeed, Boethra first calculated, and then enacted, the cuts needed to destroy both the Selectives and the alternative history they had attempted to make real.  Then, sensing that their actions had created an opportunity, she found a "tunnel" to the fate they'd sought, and opened her eyes to behold twelve spinning [[Lore:Aurbis|wheels]] surrounded by fire, revolving beneath two warring serpents: one a serpent of [[Lore:Anu|flaming feathers and crystal scales]], with a head like a hunting bird; and the other a crimson-eyed serpent of [[Lore:Padomay|black scales and white mane]], followed by all the [[Lore:Void|Void]]—somehow (in the Khajiit account) representing the "truth within the lies" of the Selectives.  While the flame-feathered serpent emanated rejection of all "[[Lore:Man|Mannish]] impurity in all the known worlds", the dark serpent—though surrounded by chaos—emanated gentleness and love for the spirits of the worlds; and in it, Boethra saw a fleeting chance for peace.  As the feathered serpent's beak found purchase beneath the scales of the white-maned serpent, Boethra, dodging through the wheels to reach, summoned her blades and struck at its eye, repelling it.  Landing on the head of the dark serpent, she drew upon its black flames, forming a blade and [[Lore:Ebony Mail|armor]] as her mind was scorched with things that were and things that had yet to be.  She named the hawk-serpent "for what it was" and—reciting the "Will Against Rule"—dashed forward, cutting concepts at strange angles and thereby (in some unknown way) ending the Dragon Break.  This event would later come to be known as the Division of Heaven by mortals who remembered the Middle-Dawn.{{ref|name=Bladesongs|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume V}}}}

Yet, in later eras, the occurrence of this event became disputed; most notably, by the scholar Fal Droon.  He posited that no cosmic disruption took place at all, and that the "Dragon Break" story was concocted in the early [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] to explain inconsistencies in the Encyclopedia Tamrielica.  He cites "scholarly inertia", obsession with "eschatology", and fanatical "Numidiumism" in order to explain the perpetuation of the error.{{ref|name=TDBR|{{Cite Book|The Dragon Break Re-Examined}}}}

Fal Droon's claim that the Dragon Break was a Third Era fabrication is apparently discredited by older sources, which are known to have described the Dragon Break as early as {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In particular, ''[[Lore:Vindication for the Dragon Break|Vindication for the Dragon Break]]'' seems to describe the Marukhati Selective's justification for the Middle Dawn before the event itself. The Dunmer of the era were hostile towards the Empire,{{ref|name=ROCW|{{Cite Book|Reflections on Cult Worship}}}} yet they still acknowledge a Dragon Break occurred.{{ref|name=WWYDB}} Furthermore, a member of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] claimed that the oversoul of emperors within the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] confirmed that a Dragon Break took place. The Council purportedly gathered information from every province. However, they only offered stories that never coincide except in mentioning that all the folk of the continent tracked the fall of eight stars, which they used to count their days.{{ref|name=WWYDB}}

An abstruse text ascribed, like the ''Vindication'', to Arch-Prelate [[Lore:Fervidius Tharn|Fervidius Tharn]] appears to describe the mechanics of the Middle Dawn in more detail as involving the detachment of a particular "sheath" from the "integument", or outer layer, of the Aurbis. This process would begin by "intercourse with the star-orphan" Mnemoli, followed by "eversion of the organ of thought" and "an employment of the Hurling Disk", "twisting the enveloping sheath into the middle dawn".{{ref|name=OtDotS|{{Cite Book|On the Detachment of the Sheath}}}}

===The Brass God===
[[File:MW-book-Divine Metaphysics p3.jpg|thumb|right|A diagram of Numidium]]
The first activation of [[Lore:Numidium|Numidium]] by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] at the town of [[Lore:Rimmen|Rimmen]] in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] is said to have preceded a Dragon Break that heralded the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]].{{ref|name=WWYDB}}{{ref|name=TDBR}} The second activation of Numidium in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region during {{Year|3E 417}} brought about another Dragon Break. [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]], and the Empire all gained control of the Totem of Tiber Septim, somehow at the exact same time, which allowed them to control the power of the golem. When this "'''[[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]]'''" ended (the effect objectively seemed to last roughly two days), the many other kingdoms of the Iliac Bay region were conquered. Each remaining kingdom consolidated its newfound territory and swore fealty to the Empire, bringing historically unusual harmony to northwestern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

Because the use of Numidium has not once, but twice been associated with a Dragon Break, it is speculated that some of the events surrounding the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] and the disappearance of the Dwemer around {{Year|1E 700}} may also be explained by a Dragon Break.{{ref|name=WWYDB}} This purported Dragon Break, sometimes called the '''Red Moment''',{{ref|name=36L37|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 37}}}}{{Ref|name=mkirc|group=UOL|{{TIL|Michael Kirkbride - IRC Q&A Sessions|michael-kirkbride-irc-quotes}}}} involved the battle that ultimately resulted in the disappearance of the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}{{Ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} The accounts of this battle often conflict with each other on various points, such as the loyalties of the parties in the battle and the events, which transpired at the climax of the battle, including how the Dwemer met their fate.{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{Ref|name=NARM}}{{Ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 26}}}}{{Ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}}

==Effects==

While it is said no mortal truly understands what happened within the bounds of the Middle-Dawn, accounts gathered by the Elder Council describe certain phenomena that allegedly occurred. Some [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiilic]] accounts from the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of the emperors in the Amulet of Kings describe how Cyrodiil became an empire across the stars, while others describe it becoming an egg. [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] accounts describe eight [[Lore:Stars|stars]] falling to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] and borders constantly shifting as the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Three Good Daedra]] and the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]] came together to protect [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] from the madness. Khajiit accounts claim that the [[Lore:Moons|Moons]] were the only constant in the heavens but only the Khajiit could see because of their use of [[Lore:Moon Sugar|Moon Sugar]], allowing them to record events while others warred with phantoms and gave birth to their own fathers, they also reference the future use of the Numidium at Rimmen as well as the Warp in the West, which wouldn't occur until the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]. [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] describes how his location, like that of all mortals, shifted during the Break.{{ref|name=WWYDB}}

Accounts of the Warp in the West describe mass disappearances with whole armies suddenly being transported hundreds of miles away, titanic storms, celestial phenomena, discontinuities of time where whole days and their events are "lost" except for a few who still experienced them, and great swathes of land being scorched and left in ruins, culminating in the overnight transformation of the kingdoms of the Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

==Time Breaches==
[[File:ON-quest-The_Shattered_Staff.jpg|right|thumb|A Time Breach]]
Time Breaches are small tears in the fabric of time, which are invisible to mortal eyes. Powerful [[Lore:Time Magic|Time Magic]] like that of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] allows one learn the approximate location of a Breach, but only gazing through a being with a different perception of time and space like the [[Lore:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]] will allow a mortal to accurately track them,{{ref|name=ESOJosajeh|[[Online:Josajeh|Josajeh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} or peer through the veil and see them.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[Online:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The influence of relics such as the Staff of Towers, even fragmented, can cause clusters of these Breaches to manifest in various locations across the land, producing raging time-storms that can keep occurring even if the cause is removed from that [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]].{{ref|name=ESOCelarus}} The Breaches can be sealed through the use of Psijic Seals, arcane anchors created by the ancient Aldmer who sought to bind any wayward spirits or forces in a given place during a time when the veil was thinner,{{ref|name=ESOJosajeh}} which allow the wielder to stabilize the rifts and harden the walls of time that surround them through the use of "chrononymics". If Time Breaches are allowed to remain, then the temporal energies will accumulate, eventually coalescing into an "impossibly dense Aurbic gyre", which is said can potentially create a new Dragon Break, break time, or worse.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus}}

Proximity to either potent arcane structures such as the [[Lore:Towers|Towers]] or mages of power great enough such as the elders of the Psijic Order, objects or beings that by their very existence exert tremendous stress on Aurbic forces around them and create arcane risk, can further destabilize Time Breaches, causing the temporal energies to spin out of control and consume those present.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus}}

Attempting to use potent Time Magic to accomplish great changes like undoing events that have already occurred can cause multiple Time Breaches to manifest. Such an event occurred when the Psijic Josajeh attempted to use the Staff of Towers in the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]]'s Time-Lost Throne Room to undo the tragedies of her past. These Breaches allowed [[Lore:Many Paths|alternate timeline]] versions of Josajeh to manifest into the world.{{ref|name=ONFall|Events of [[Online:The Towers' Fall|The Towers' Fall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOJosajeh}}{{ref|name=ESOAugur|[[Online:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

The Augur of the Obscure describes the Time Breaches as "chronosymphonic discs" and claims that should they remain unsealed the world would become one of non-linear time, making it possible for one to die before they were even born, though there was also a sixty percent chance that time would "fold inside out". According to the Augur there are creatures that can eat Time Breaches but a mortal shouldn't attempt it lest they be "sucked through their own bellybutton", nor even contemplate Breaches and non-linear time too long as even that can be dangerous.{{ref|name=ESOAugur}} Psijic Monks had the ability to use Time Breaches to springboard to a different time and place.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus}}

==Time Knots==
[[File:ON-quest-Unstuck From Time.jpg|right|thumb|A Time Knot]]
Time Knots are temporal distortions described as eddies in the stream of time that disrupt its flow within a certain area and are capable of altering causality. If time is likened to a woven fabric with threads that cross and stitch together, then a Knot is what occurs when those threads bind together unnaturally, threads of chance and potential tangling around a single point. They can manifest for a variety of reasons, including powerful spells, the activation of arcane relics, [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] trickery, or even naturally without external influence. Whatever the cause, creating a Time Knot requires a great deal of power. An area enveloped by the influence of a Time Knot will be trapped within a repeating time loop and, if left unattended, a Knot can also grow to encompass larger areas.{{Ref|name=UFT|Events of [[ON:Unstuck From Time|Unstuck From Time]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Scribes of Fate|Scribes of Fate]]''}}{{Ref|name=Saresea|[[ON:Saresea|Saresea]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Scribes of Fate|Scribes of Fate]]''}}

A Time Knot can be unraveled through the use of Time Magic like that employed by the Psijic Order, whose members will seek out and dispel such distortions. A Knot created by a common cause can be relatively simple to dispel provided the mage has the required knowledge. However, there are Knots created by uncommon sources, which can be much harder to get rid of, distortions where too many threads are bound too tightly to one another, and can even have a will of their own, resisting attempts to unravel them.{{Ref|name=UFT}}{{Ref|name=Saresea}}

One such Knot enveloped the [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] township of Bal Sunnar, trapping it in a time loop from sometime after the Battle of Red Mountain to {{Year|2E 582}} when it was undone by the Psijic Monk Saresea and the Vestige. The Knot was created by a Telvanni splinter group who sought to escape the rising rule of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] through the use of a Glyphic, an artifact of [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]. It is the physical manifestation of a [[Lore:Fate|Fate]] or knowledge and can be coaxed to transform into a tangible version of the concept it contains.{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Telvanni, recognized the Vestige and Saresea from previous loops and tried to stop them, tapping into the magic of the Knot to summon creatures from the time of ancient [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] or to conjure temporal duplicates of themselves to aid them in combat. The Knot itself resisted Saresea, exploding outward and transporting her and the Vestige both to the distant past and the distant future.{{Ref|name=UFT}}{{Ref|name=Saresea}}{{Ref|name=Nerile|{{Cite Book|Journal of Nerile Belvayn}}}}{{Ref|name=Kovan|{{Cite Book|Journal of Kovan Giryon}}}}

Ultimately Saresea and the Vestige succeeded in defeating the Telvanni Matriarch and dispelling the Knot but at a cost: Saresea was fragmented and scattered across time. Though Hermaeus Mora, who had been observing, reached out and pulled her back together she remained "multichronal", a being that manifests at multiple points in space and time simultaneously, both in the past and in the future, ranging from her own birth to her own death and beyond.{{Ref|name=UFT}}{{Ref|name=Saresea}}

The energies of a Time Knot can be drawn upon to empower someone, it is also possible for pieces of it to break off. Saresea claimed that contact with the Bal Sunnar Time Knot made her feel "all of time".{{Ref|name=UFT}}{{Ref|name=Saresea}}

==Time Ripples==
[[File:ON-quest-Ripple Effect.jpg|Two mages performing a ritual that created ripples of temporal distortion, allowing for time travel|thumb|right]]
There have been cases of mages conducting rituals that caused their surrounding area to "ripple with temporal distortion", connecting different time periods and drawing beings from one into the other. While time was distorted in this manner interacting with objects with a strong link to the different time period had the potential to transport one through time to said period, expriencing that time's events by taking the place of the one the object was tied to originally.{{ref|name=ASBIT|Events of the [[Online:A Step Back in Time|A Step Back in Time]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RippleEffect|Events of the [[Online:Ripple Effect|Ripple effect]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOAlana|[[Online:Alana Relin|Alana Relin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOSarvith|[[Online:Sarvith Yunlin|Sarvith Yunlin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Time Trap==	
[[File:ON-npc-Lady Edwyge.jpg|thumb|right|Lady Edwyge, caught in a time trap]]
Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, a localized temporal distortion known as a Time Trap formed due to the machinations of  the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] noblewoman Lady Edwyge. Edwyge stole a copy of the text ''[[Lore:Vindication for the Dragon Break|Vindication for the Dragon Break]]'' from the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] in [[Lore:Hallin's Stand|Hallin's Stand]]. She managed to convince an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] novice named Gahgdar to let her borrow the book, and then fled to the ruins of {{Lore Link|Rubble Butte}}, an ancient [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] complex located near {{Lore Link|Nilata}} in southern [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]]. She and her supporters sought to turn back time several years in order to change the course of history and ensure Edwyge became Queen of Bangkorai rather than {{Lore Link|Arzhela}}. The nobles managed to emulate the ritual performed by the Marukhati Selective and turned back time a few hours, but ended up creating a time trap within the ruins by trying to extend the ritual further.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Lady Edwyge's Notes}}}}
	
Unbeknownst to the nobles, Gahgdar had tracked Edwyge down to Rubble Butte and had entered the ruins in search of the book to avoid being kicked out of the Guild. However, he also became stuck in the time trap. [[Lore:The Vestige|The Vestige]] subsequently entered Rubble Butte and agreed to help Gahgdar recover the book from the nobles. The Vestige fought through the nobles before reaching Lady Edwyge and killing her, recovering the book in the process. However, upon reading Edwyge's notes, it transpired that she had anticipated her death and was stuck reliving the same few hours over and over again. The Vestige returned the book to Gahgdar, but the cycle simply began anew; Gahgdar would repeatedly request the recovery of the book, and Edwyge and her supporters would repeatedly be slain in order to recover it.{{ref|[[ON:Back in Time|Back in Time]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The Vestige was not caught in the time trap and was free to leave Rubble Butte, but the ultimate fate of those trapped inside is unknown.

==Notes==
* Vivec's "Scripture of the Numbers" begins with "The Dragon Break, or the Tower. 1".{{ref|name=36L29|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 29}}}} 
* The Middle Dawn has been associated with the number 17 and the "Hurling Disk".{{ref|name=OtDotS}}
* "Kalpas" are thought to be the overarching epochs into which the flow of time is divided, with the transition from the chaos of the Dawn Era to the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic]] marking the end of a previous kalpa.{{ref|name=TSOP7|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 7}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{TIL|World-Eating 101|worldeating101}}, The Imperial Library}} Dragon Breaks could possibly be understood as interruptions in the new kalpa.
* In ''[[Lore:Five Songs of King Wulfharth|Five Songs of King Wulfharth]]'', before the Battle of Red Mountain, [[Lore:Wulfharth|Ysmir Wulfharth]] said to the reticent Nordic troops, "Don't you see where you really are? Don't you know who Shor really is? Don't you know what this war is?" This was purportedly referring to their presence within the Dragon Break of the Red Moment.{{Ref|name=mkirc|group=UOL}}
* ''[[Lore:Where Were You ... Dragon Broke|Where Were You When The Dragon Broke?]]'' contains an account that is generally accepted as referring to the use of the Numidium by Tiber Septim, as well as the Warp in the West. This text also refers to Mannimarco as the "God of Worms", a title used only after the Warp allegedly allowed his apotheosis. Yet copies of the book were discovered in the Second Era, before the Warp occurred, and have the same text as those found in the Third and Fourth Eras.{{Ref|name=Online}}
* In ''[[Lore:The Dragon Break Re-Examined|The Dragon Break Re-Examined]]'', the scholar Fal Droon attempted to debunk the Middle Dawn, and thus the "doctrine of the Dragon Break" in general. The book first appeared in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|TES III: Morrowind]]'', which took place in {{Year|3E 427}}. Droon wrote of the fall of the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]] in the past tense, even though it would not occur for another six years, which suggests that he did not actually write the text until after the end of the Septim Dynasty and the Third Era. Thus, the book ironically seems to corroborate that Tamriel is susceptible to distortions in time such as the Dragon Break.
* In the [https://www.imperial-library.info/content/childs-tamriel-bestiary-french French translation of] ''[[Lore:A Child's Tamriel Bestiary|A Child's Tamriel Bestiary]]'', [[Lore:Jill|jills]] are said to be mythical beings who fix the world during dragon breaks.
*The [[Lore:The Mage|Mage]] [[Lore:Celestial|Celestial]] was theorized to be capable of altering time in a manner likened to "hiding behind a broken Dragon", traveling the "line" of time and than cutting it to wrap herself "in a "context" where "none can ever follow".{{ref|name=Learned|{{Cite Book|Coming of the Learned One}}}}
