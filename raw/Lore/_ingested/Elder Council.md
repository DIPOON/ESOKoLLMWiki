described the home of an "age-old aristocracy of battlemages," which has existed since the early [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]].{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}

Though it theoretically merely helps the [[Lore:Emperor|Emperor]] govern by dealing with various details,{{ref|name=Oblivion}} the Council has proven capable of wrestling a substantial amount of authority away from the [[Lore:Empire|Imperial]] throne, and there is often a power struggle between the two. The Council has legislative authority, and may vote on and enact new laws they deem proper. However, the reigning Emperor retains the power of veto, a right famously exploited by [[Lore:Uriel Septim VI|Uriel Septim VI]] to restore the power of the Emperor when faced with an unruly Council.{{ref|name=BHOTE|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire}}}}{{ref|group=nb|''[[Lore:How Orsinium Passed to the Orcs|How Orsinium Passed to the Orcs]]'' suggests the Council might have some judicial authority, as well (or at least, may give their advice on how to interpret laws to judicial officers within the Empire).}}

When the Council is not in some sort of disagreement with an Emperor, its members are often looking for ways to curry favor with the Ruby Throne.{{ref|name=GTTIC}} Each member receives an amulet worth a small fortune which is specially crafted as a symbol of their office.{{ref|name=SRDelvin|[[SR:Delvin Mallory|Delvin Mallory]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} As a prominent family in Cyrodiil garners more and more power and repute, people eventually start thinking it is only a matter of time before they obtain a representative on the Council,{{ref|name=Oblivion}} though it is not clear exactly how new Council members are appointed. Appointment is seen as both an honor and burden, though some nobles view it only as a means to obtain more power and influence.{{ref|name=ImpP|{{Cite Book|Imperial Politics}}}}<noinclude>

==Duties==
[[File:SR-concept-Elder Council Amulet.jpeg|left|thumb]]
===Governance===
While its importance cannot be overstated, much of the Elder Council's responsibilities consist of the intricate but dull problems of running an Empire,{{ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Skyrim's Rule}}}} such as administering Imperial taxes on vassal kingdoms.{{ref|group=nb|The Council collected direct taxes from [[Lore:Orsinium|Nova Orsinium]] and treated its leader as a recognized king, even though officially, the Orc homeland remained a county of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], not a recognized kingdom.{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}}}{{ref|group=nb|The Council chartered and funded the Black Horse Courier, a newsletter distributed for free to the citizens of Cyrodiil during the late Third Era.{{ref|name=Oblivion}}}} The Council is typically concerned with taxation, infrastructure, and minor legislation (of which the Emperor holds veto power).{{ref|name=ImpP}} 

The leaders of the Elder Council may personally command Imperial Legions during times of war.{{Ref|name=TWQ6|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v6}}}}{{ref|name=BHOTE}} During extreme emergencies the wider Elder Council may return to the provinces to deal with local matters, but the Inner Council continues to govern.{{ref|name=HCO}}

===Kingmakers, Regents, and Emergency Powers===
The Council only tends to get noticed when its members are dealing with some emergency or power struggle related to the Emperor. The Council is responsible for recognizing and crowning the next ruler upon the death of the Emperor, and they are tasked with running the government in the interim under the leadership of the High Chancellor, the de facto leader of the whole Empire during such periods.{{ref|name=OBArriana|[[OB:Arriana Valga|Arriana Valga]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=ASS!|{{Cite Book|Assassination!}}}}

The Council may also recognize an Emperor or Empress Regent to function as the Empire's ruler should the rightful Emperor be incompetent (if, for example, the Emperor is a minor or mentally deranged). Choosing the rightful Emperor theoretically is only a matter of birthright, but the Council has demonstrated some discretion in judging whether an individual's genealogical claims are accurate and whether that individual holds the closest relation to the original imperial bloodline (and, inevitably, various other considerations no doubt influence these decisions).{{ref|name=BHOTE}}{{ref|name=TWQ|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen}}}} Numerous times throughout history, though, the members of the Council have recognized an Emperor not because of that person's birthright, but because that person had become Emperor-in-fact by seizing the capital, along with the Council.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}{{ref|name=TWQ}}{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}

In the extraordinary event that the Elder Council is forced to assume a longer-term regency over the Empire (if, say, something should happen to all known heirs to the Imperial throne), the Council asserts complete control over the military forces of the Empire.{{ref|name=ASS!}}{{ref|name=LTIC|{{Cite Book|Letter to Imperial City|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} This regency remains until such time as the Council recognizes an individual's claim to be Emperor. If an individual cannot be decided upon, the Elder Council Charter authorizes the Council to appoint the High Chancellor as Potentate (a sort of steward who takes on the role of Emperor in all but name).{{ref|name=RT}}{{ref|name=2920L12|{{Cite Book|2920, Evening Star (v12)}}}}{{NewLine}}

==History==
===Alessian Empire===
The Elder Council's history stretches back to the founding of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]] by [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]].{{Ref|name=OblivionLS|[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]] [[Oblivion:Loading Screens|loading screen]]}} Their earliest known act was in {{Year|1E 266}}, when the Council elected [[Lore:Belharza|Belharza the Man-Bull]] as the second Emperor of Cyrodiil following Alessia's passing into the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]].{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Kurt Kuhlmann's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

===Akaviri Potentate===
Each Emperor of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] was advised by the leader of the Akaviri faction on the Elder Council, who bore the title Akaviri Potentate.{{ref|name=HKEAYQ|{{Cite Book|High King Emeric Answers Your Questions}}}}

In {{Year|2E 282}}, Potentate [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]] called together the Council in a meeting which has since been called the Council of Bardmont, where he announced universal martial law and the dissolution of all armies in the Empire other than his own. This ushered in one of the bloodiest periods of conflict in the history of Tamriel.{{ref|name=HOTFG}}

===Advisors to the Longhouse Emperors===
[[File:ON-misc-AbnurTharn.png|Grand Chancellor Abnur Tharn|thumb|right]]
At the height of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], in {{Year|2E 534}}, Emperor [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]] dynasty reconstituted the Elder Council.{{Ref|name=SHotLE|{{Cite Book|Secret History of the Longhouse Emperors}}}}{{Ref|name=IDRtEC|{{Cite Book|Imperial Decree Regarding the Elder Council}}}} Durcorach was a [[Lore:Reachman|Reachman]] chieftain who had conquered the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] the previous year, and although he was unfamiliar with Cyrodilic culture, he nonetheless attempted to embrace it to reinforce his connection and commitment to the Empire.{{Ref|name=SHotLE}} To this end, he purged the Elder Council of dissenters while retaining or appointing experienced Councilors whom he deemed trustworthy.{{Ref|name=IDRtEC}} The Elder Council continued to serve Durcorach's heirs, the Emperors [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]] and [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]], and also remained intact under the brief reigns of Emperor [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] and Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]]. When the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] began in {{Year|2E 582}}, the chaos that ensued scattered the Councilors, and the Elder Council was rendered defunct for an indeterminate period.{{ref|name=MOTEC|{{Cite Book|Minutes of the Elder Council}}}}{{ref|name=MtCTEC}}

===Under the Septims===
According to the disputed events in ''[[Lore:The Arcturian Heresy|The Arcturian Heresy]]'', it was the Council who came upon the aftermath of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s successful ambush of the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], which he then lied to them about, claiming that his Imperial Battlemage [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]] had killed his guards and tried to assassinate him.

Emperor [[Lore:Pelagius Septim II|Pelagius Septim II]], who inherited a debt-ridden Empire when he ascended to the throne in {{Year|3E 82}}, dismissed the entire Elder Council and allowed only those willing to pay great sums to resume their seats. He encouraged similar acts among his vassals, and though he returned prosperity to Tamriel, his critics cite consequences. First and foremost, wise but poor individuals were reportedly lost in the process.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}{{ref|name=TWQ}} The inexperience this introduced may have led to some of the troubles his son [[Lore:Antiochus|Antiochus]] faced when he in turn became Emperor.{{ref|name=BHOTE}} Not only that, it may have cost Pelagius II his life, as it was rumored that a former Council member was responsible for poisoning and killing him.{{ref|name=TWQ}} But the most long-term effect of selling off seats on the Council was, obviously, that it became composed of the wealthiest people in the Empire. What Pelagius II may not have anticipated is that the collective power these individuals wielded would often prove equal or greater to that of the Emperor, and vicious power struggles emerged as a result.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}

Twenty-two years after the death of Pelagius II,{{ref|name=TTET|{{Cite Book|The Third Era Timeline}}}} the Empress [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]] was captured at the beginning of the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]] in {{Year|3E 121}}, and the Council was outwitted by the forces of the Wolf Queen [[Lore:Potema|Potema]] and her son, [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel Mantiarco]]. Their attacks made the Council send the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|army]] off to western [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and eastern [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], leaving Cyrodiil itself weak. After a siege of the Imperial City which lasted only a fortnight, the Council recognized Potema's son as Emperor Uriel Septim III. In {{Year|3E 127}}, after Uriel III was captured and killed, they then recognized the next contender who laid claim to the Imperial City, Uriel III's uncle [[Lore:Cephorus I|Cephorus]].{{ref|name=BHOTE}} However, the emperors following the War of the Red Diamond yielded to the authority of the Council in order to keep some semblance of order in the land.{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}}

The throne passed to Cephorus' brother [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus]], who then died in {{Year|3E 145}}, and the Elder Council went on to suffer through the reign of Emperor [[Lore:Pelagius Septim III|Pelagius Septim III]], also known as "Pelagius the Mad".{{ref|name=BHOTE}} It is said Pelagius III never really ruled Tamriel, and it was the Council, along with the Emperor's wife [[Lore:Katariah|Katariah]], who made all the decisions.{{ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} In {{Year|3E 147}}, Pelagius III reportedly declared himself incompetent to reign,{{ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}} and the Emperor of Tamriel entered a private asylum. The Council made Katariah the Empress Regent of Tamriel, and after Pelagius III's death in {{Year|3E 153}}, she was formally recognized as Empress.{{ref|name=TMOP}}

===Katariah and the Council===
The disputes between Empress Katariah and the Elder Council are the stuff of legend. As a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] woman with no blood claim to the throne, she was vulnerable to all sorts of prejudices and suspicions, and some even considered her ascendancy to the throne to be the beginning of the end of the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]].{{ref|name=BHOTE}} One of her disagreements with members of the Council famously resulted in a duel which her champion won. This victory, recounted in ''[[Lore:The Armorer's Challenge|The Armorer's Challenge]]'', earned the Empress some respect in the Council. 

Nevertheless, Katariah was uncomfortable in the Imperial City and traveled extensively across the Empire, leaving the Elder Council to keep the Empire running on their own. The Council assumed a great deal of power during the reigns of Katariah and her children, and evidently took a great liking to it. [[Lore:Uriel Septim IV|Uriel Septim IV]], in particular, found it impossible to command their fealty. He consistently found himself at odds with the Council, and consistently lost the battles. Their last victory over Uriel IV was posthumous: upon his death, they voted to disinherit his children, and instead recognized a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line as Emperor [[Lore:Cephorus II|Cephorus II]]. This led to nine years of fighting with Uriel IV's heir, as well as other insurrections around Tamriel which would drag on for a century.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}{{ref|group=nb|In {{Year|3E 246}}, during the reign of Uriel IV, the Council decreed that any man without a liege who occupied a castle for more than three months would be granted the rights and titles of that estate. This was meant to discourage absent and foreign land ownership, as there were some tricky property rights dilemmas in Skyrim at the time.{{ref|name=TRG|{{Cite Book|The Rear Guard}}}}}}

===Uriel VI's struggles===
Emperor Uriel Septim VI was crowned in {{Year|3E 290}}, but he was still a boy at the time. Though his mother was given a restricted Regency of the Empire, the Elder Council retained the real power, and its members enjoyed unrestricted, unlimited freedom to promulgate laws and profit from said laws. Uriel VI found it very difficult to retake the authority of the Emperor. He was given greater responsibility over the course of several years, but wasn't allowed full license to rule until he was already twenty-two years old, and by that time, the Council had stripped the office of the Emperor of almost all its real power, save the Emperor's right to veto. After six years of vetoing, bullying, and generally strong-arming the Council, Uriel VI could finally claim to truly rule Tamriel, and a sage soon coined the saying "[[Lore:Uriel Septim V|Uriel V]] conquered [[Lore:Esroniet|Esroniet]], but Uriel VI conquered the Elder Council".{{ref|name=BHOTE}}

When Uriel VI died, he left behind a strong Imperial throne for his sister [[Lore:Morihatha|Morihatha]]. It is known that the Council frequently became frustrated with the slow pace of Morihatha's military campaigns despite their remarkable success, and it is commonly believed that a summarily tried and executed Councilman was the person who hired assassins to kill her in {{Year|3E 339}}. The succeeding Emperors of the Third Era maintained the dominance Uriel VI had established over the Council.{{ref|name=BHOTE}}

===The Oblivion Crisis===
[[File:OBR-npc-High Chancellor Ocato 02.jpg|High Chancellor Ocato|thumb|right]]
However, around the onset of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] in {{Year|3E 433}}, it was rumored that power had shifted back to the Council under High Chancellor [[Lore:Ocato of Firsthold|Ocato of Firsthold]] fifteen years beforehand, during the rule of Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]].{{ref|name=LTM|{{Cite Book|Letter to Mother|ns_base=Oblivion}}}} The Crisis began with the assassination of Uriel VII and his heirs, and it would not end until the Council found a new Emperor to light the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]]. Ocato ultimately recognized [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]'s claim to the throne on the Council's behalf, but there was no opportunity to formally crown him before he perished at the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]].{{ref|name=TOC|{{Cite Book|The Oblivion Crisis}}}}{{ref|name=OBLight|[[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===The Stormcrown Interregnum===
Following the Oblivion Crisis, the Elder Council could not find a suitable heir to the throne, so High Chancellor Ocato reluctantly agreed to become Potentate.{{ref|name=RT}} The Elder Council was not as strong in leadership as they would have liked, and made efforts to consolidate and conserve the Empire and its Provinces, though with perhaps little effect. Skyrim appeared to be on the brink of secession, and the other provinces each had their own internal and external problems to deal with.{{ref|name=TPFromTheAshes|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP#Campaign 14: From the Ashes|''From the Ashes'']]}} Ocato's assassination circa {{Year|4E 15}} precipitated the {{Lore Link|Stormcrown Interregnum}}.{{ref|name=RT}} The Elder Council fractured, leading to years of ruthless in-fighting, plots and backstabbing. Many tried to claim the Ruby Throne. Violent, unnatural storms reportedly lashed the Imperial City several times during this anarchy, always with the eye of the storm looking directly down upon White Gold Tower.{{ref|name=RT}} This chaos did not end until the [[Lore:Colovia|Colovian]] warlord [[Lore:Titus Mede I|Titus Mede]] captured the Imperial City circa {{Year|4E 22}} and deposed the Empire's ruler, Thules the Gibbering. Thules the Gibbering was not a well-liked ruler, but because he was Nibenese, many on the Council favored him over a Colovian usurper.{{ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|96}} [[Lore:Hierem|Hierem]], who was from an old and well-connected Nibenese family and a minister to Thules, helped smooth over the conquest by convincing the Elder Council to accept Titus as a liberator rather than a conqueror.{{ref|name=LOS}}{{rp|96}}
