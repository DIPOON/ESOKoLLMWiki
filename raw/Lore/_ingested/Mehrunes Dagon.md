==Mythology==
[[File:OB-book-Mysterium Xarxes 01.jpg|thumb|left|Page one of the ''Mysterium Xarxes'']]
===The ''Mysterium Xarxes''===
{{Quote Box|''When I walk the earth again, the faithful among you shall receive your reward. To be set above all other mortals, forever. As for the rest, the weak shall be winnowed, the timid shall be cast down, the mighty shall tremble at my feet and pray for pardon.''|Mehrunes Dagon, ''[[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes (book)|Mysterium Xarxes]]'' {{ref|name=MX|{{Cite Book|Mysterium Xarxes (book)}}}}{{ref|name=TILTranslating|''{{TIL|Translating the Mysterium Xarxes|translating-mysterium-xarxes}}'' at {{TIL}}}}}}
Little is known of the contents of the ''Mysterium Xarxes'' outside of the alleged translations given in [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s ''[[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes]]''.{{ref|name=MX}}{{ref|name=MDC|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries}}}} While the ''Commentaries'' were known to be used in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] to surreptitiously recruit members into Dagon's cult, the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]],{{ref|name=OBPath|[[OB:The Path of Dawn|The Path of Dawn]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} they also contain a peculiar account of the Prince's origins.{{ref|name=MDC}}

According to the ''Commentaries'', the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]] secretly created Mehrunes Dagon in "the very bowels of [[Lore:Lyg|Lyg]]".{{ref|name=MDC4|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 4}}}} Lyg, it is claimed, is an "Adjacent Place", a sort of parallel version of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]],{{ref|name=MKAMA|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride AMA|ns_base=GEN}}}} and the domain of a deity known as "the Upstart who vanishes".{{ref|name=MDC4}} Lyg, the "Mundex Terrene", was once ruled by [[Lore:Dreugh|dreugh-kings]] from the "nineteen and nine and nine" seas, who waged endless wars against each other's "slave oceans".

Dagon was created to be a prince of good, and was imbued with [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]'s most precious asset, hope. The Prince began a revolution, "threw down Lyg", and "cracked his face".{{ref|name=MDC4}} He let the lowly slaves know free will, and stirred them to violence against their masters.{{ref|name=MDC2|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 2}}}} His "red legion" overthrew the tyrant dreughs, toppled the towers of CHIM-EL GHARJYG, destroyed great cities, and slaughtered the templars of the Upstart. Hope spread like a "brush-fire" as the world was set Free,{{ref|name=MDC4}} hence his title of the '''Sea's Flame Redeemer'''.{{Ref|name=ONTWMD}}

Regardless of the veracity of this account, the tome played a role in the assassination of [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] and his heirs, starting the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{ref|name=MDC4}}{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

A story from obscure [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] myth provides an alternative origin of the Daedric Prince, claiming him to have once been a kinder being who was cursed by [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]].{{ref|group=UOL|[[GEN:The Eating-Birth of Dagon|The Eating-Birth of Dagon]]}}

==Worship and Culture==
[[File:ON-crown store-Deadlands Excoriated.jpg|Cultist markings painted with sacred crimson ink into flensed skin|thumb|left]][[File:ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes Dagon-emblem.png|thumb|right|An emblem representing Mehrunes Dagon]]
Worshippers of the Prince of Destruction are known as Dagonites{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}} and Dagonists.{{ref|name=DTtA|{{Cite Book|Dagonists Through the Ages}}}}

He is revered by many groups Daedra, such as the [[Lore:Crimson Oath|Crimson Oath]], who know the '''Great Prince''' by many names and call themselves the oathsworn of the '''Red Father''', scarlet reavers of the '''Razor King''', and bloodletters of the '''Fire-Veined God'''.{{ref|name=CM105|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 105: Crimson Oath Style}}}}<!--Add more on what the Crimson Oath think, its quality stuff-->

In most cultures, Mehrunes Dagon is little more than a god of bloodshed and betrayal.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Dagon revels in destruction on a grand scale, from mass murder to deaths resulting from floods or earthquakes. He leaves a swath of devastation whenever he manifests. In fact, even attempting to summon the Prince is frequently fatal for the conjurer and any bystanders. Trafficking with Dagon or his cultists is abhorred by most of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and is outright illegal in many places, where the punishment can be as severe as death.{{ref|name=FAAYQ|{{Cite Book|Flaminius Auctor Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=TTDMD|{{Cite Book|Thwarting the Daedra: Mehrunes Dagon}}}}{{ref|name=BV1}}.

He has a vested interest in showing flagrant displays of power to attract followers to his various cults and assert his influence on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=TTDMD}} He is an especially important deity in Morrowind, where he is the Second of the Four Corners of the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]].{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}}{{Ref|name=MWTholer|[[MW:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Worshippers of Dagon tend to be arsonists, setting buildings, animals, and even people on fire. Signs of cultist activity include unusual tremors, an uncharacteristic lack of rain and sudden onsets of unseasonably heavy rain as they attempt to incite natural disasters. Those who worship the Prince tend to bear the symbol of a fiery, rising sun.{{ref|name=TTDMD}} Dagon's cults have a tendency to plan deep and long-burgeoning conspiracies.{{ref|name=FAAYQ}}

Dagon's faithful followers are sometimes physically transformed by the Prince's blessing, an act which is said to forever change them. After undergoing bloody rites of devotion, Dagon's most zealous followers earn the right to shed their mortal form through undergoing hours of ritual scourging of their flesh. When the process is complete their skin is replaced by a coat of gleaming crimson scales. Another ritual involves flensing of the skin to  prepare the cultist receive the sacred crimson ink that will give them markings reminiscent of those of Dagon himself.{{ref|name=ONScourger|[[ON:Scourger's Scales|Scourger's Scales]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONExcoriated|[[ON:Deadlands Excoriated|Deadlands Excoriated]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} More experienced cultists of Mehrunes Dagon are noted to have a high tolerance for heat, and it is said it takes a lot of it to make them uncomfortable.{{ref|name=ONTilgo|[[ON:Initiate Tilgo|Initiate Tilgo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Argonians===
The [[Lore:Sul-Xan|Sul-Xan]] Tribe consist of [[Lore:Naga|naga]] worshippers of chaos and death, and see Mehrunes Dagon as the '''True Egg-Child of [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]'''.{{ref|name=ESOLive1|group=UOL|[[GEN:ESO 2021 Global Reveal|ESO 2021 Global Reveal]]}} This title can be understood from a pre-[[Lore:Duskfall|Duskfall]] perspective, when the old [[Lore:Nisswo|ruling class]] of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] believed that Sithis was a malevolent destructive force,{{ref|name=Xode|[[ON:Nisswo Xode|Nisswo Xode]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} much like Dagon. The Sul-Xan embrace only "the darkest, cruelest beliefs, and scorn the rest as weakness."{{ref|name=ONBS|[[ON:Britta Silanus|Britta Silanus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} Thus, they sacrifice outsiders and other Argonians alike in hopes of one day summoning Mehrunes Dagon to bring blood and destruction across Black Marsh,{{ref|name=ONSXN|{{Cite Book|Sul-Xan Note|ns_base=Online}}}} and eventually the entire world.{{ref|name=Jela|[[ON:Jela|Jela]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

The Sul-Xan's worship of the '''Razor Prince'''{{Ref|[[ON:Sul-Xan Fleshripper|Sul-Xan Fleshripper]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} is an anomaly, as Argonian society at large does not partake in the veneration of powers from Oblivion. In fact, there are no words in their language [[Lore:Jel|Jel]] to describe any of the Daedra. As explained by a native speaker during the Interregnum, the Argonians do not "taint their language with words for abominations from Oblivion". Instead, they use common words from other more widely-spoken languages to refer to the beasts.{{ref|name=KCAHC|[[BK:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]]: Black Marsh, Daedroth}}

===Dunmer===
In [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], Mehrunes Dagon is the '''God of Destruction'''{{ref|name=VOFTDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}} '''the Horror''',{{ref|name=DBOA|{{Cite Book|Death Blow of Abernanit}}}} the '''Destroyer-God''',{{ref|name=CM:104|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 104: Ancient Daedric Style}}}} the '''Dark One''',{{Ref|group=UOL|[[ON:Cut Content|ESO Cut Content]] - [[ON:Apocrypha's Gate|Apocrypha's Gate]] dialogue}} '''Lord of Blood and Flame''',{{ref|name=TMF|{{Cite Book|The Master's Truth|ns_base=ON}}}} '''Oblivion's Favored Son''', '''Calamity's Tyrant King''',{{ref|name=TDoM|{{Cite Book|The Defense of Mournhold}}}} and the '''King of Blood'''{{ref|group=UOL|''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' [[:File:MW-concept-Daedric Symbols.jpg|Concept art]]}} (contrasting his title of the '''Hope of Blood''').{{Ref|name=ONTWMD}} It is said that when the prophet [[Lore:Veloth|Veloth]] led the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] to Resdayn (now [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]), they were joined by a number of Daedric ancestral spirits, among them Mehrunes Dagon.{{ref|name=THOT}} At first, these Daedra Lords were worshipped as gods.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Anticipations}}}} However, when the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal]] came to power in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Daedra were venerated as lesser spirits, subservient to the [[Lore:Almsivi|Almsivi]].{{ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}}

Not content with this power shift, [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], and Dagon rebelled against the new Temple, which caused much strife among the [[Lore:Great House|Great Houses]].{{ref|name=THOT}} These Rebel Daedra, or Bad Daedra,{{ref|name=DD}} became the Four Corners of the House of Troubles, who tempt the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] away from the orthodoxy.{{ref|name=TA}} Nevertheless, the Dunmer consider these four to be holy in that they serve as Testing Gods who must be appeased.{{ref|name=THOT}}{{ref|name=VOFTDE}} Dagon represents Morrowind's near-inhospitable terrain,{{ref|name=VOFTDE}} and also tests the Dunmeri will to live and prosper.{{ref|name=THOT}}

[[File:MW-quest-Mehrunes Dagon of the House of Troubles.jpg|thumb|left|Mehrunes Dagon's shrine in [[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]]]]
According to legend, Mehrunes Dagon once threatened to heave a large rock at the Dunmer of [[Lore:Maar Gan|Maar Gan]]. [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] outwitted the Prince, taunting Dagon and making himself the rock's target. The rock itself was enshrined there, becoming known as the "Magic Rock of Maar Gan", and it became a pilgrimage site. Pilgrims would have to re-create the situation during a pilgrimage. A [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] named [[Lore:Anhaedra|Anhaedra]] was bound by Vivec to fill Dagon's role, and would have to endure taunts from pilgrims and try to harm them while they were protected by a powerful blessing.{{Ref|name=MWPilgrimage|[[MW:Pilgrimage to Maar Gan|Pilgrimage to Maar Gan]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} 

Another legend involves a duel between Vivec and the Prince of Destruction, the latter of whom was unarmed. Rather than dishonoring himself by fighting an  unarmed opponent, Vivec offered Dagon his own silver longsword. The Prince's Dremora were impressed by this honorable act of chivalry. The Temple claims that although they are enemies, the Dremora share a bond of respect and courtesy with the followers of the Tribunal. As part of the Pilgrimages of the Seven Graces, pilgrims seeking to join the Tribunal Temple must visit the Shrine of Courtesy in [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]]'s Puzzle Canal. A Dremora named Krazzt was bound here. Filling the role of Vivec, the pilgrim must be courteous and offer a silver longsword to Krazzt, who represents Mehrunes Dagon.{{ref|[[MW:Krazzt|Krazzt]]'s appearance in the center of [[MW:Vivec Palace|Vivec Palace]]'s Puzzle Canal, and his role during the [[MW:Pilgrimages of the Seven Graces|Pilgrimages of the Seven Graces]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=TPP|{{Cite Book|The Pilgrim's Path}}}}

===Khajiit===
In the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiiti]] pantheon, Mehrunes Dagon is '''Merrunz''',{{ref|name=ONJerkin|[[ON:Unique Items#Elsweyr|Daegonite Jerkin]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} (also spelled '''Mehrunnez'''){{ref|name=ONMehrunnez|[[ON:Champion of Mehrunnez|Champion of Mehrunnez]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} who is also called '''Daegon''',{{ref|name=HFL|{{Cite Book|Hadaz's Final Letter|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=TAS}} the '''Fire-Cat''',{{ref|name=ONNisuzi|[[ON:Nisuzi|Nisuzi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} and the '''Demon Cat'''.{{ref|name=TAS}} One of the children of [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]]'s second litter, Merrunz was given the nickname '''Ja'Khajiit'''—"kitten" in [[Lore:Ta'agra|Ta'agra]]—by his mother due to his wild, destructive nature.{{ref|name=WOCMA}}{{ref|name=TAS}} Similarly, the Khajiit often depict Merrunz as a "destructive kitten".{{ref|name=ONJerkin}}

According to pre-[[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|ri'Datta]] texts, at some point in time, Merrunz was exiled by his father, [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]]. Rather than journeying through the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths of Time]], Merrunz chose to venture into the [[Lore:Great Darkness|Great Darkness]]. There, he was defeated by the Demon King [[Lore:Molagh|Molagh]] and tortured until the world was created. Ultimately, Merrunz was freed by Molagh's wife, who used his destructive nature to attack the [[Lore:Lunar Lattice|Lunar Lattice]]. Merrunz enjoyed this and became a "kinslayer", and has since been known as the demon '''Dagon'''.{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}{{ref|name=TAS|{{Cite Book|The Adversarial Spirits}}}}

Pre-ri'Datta Khajiit myths speak of an alliance of the Adversarial Spirits [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molagh]], [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]] and Merrunz attacking the [[Lore:Moons|Lunar Lattice]], only to be repelled by its defenders, Azurah, [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]] and [[Lore:Mephala|Mafala]] who also receive the aid of [[Lore:Nocturnal|Noctra]]. In such myths the Lattice is described as burning spirits that touch it, causing Molagh to fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until Merrunz, released in those myths to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe. Though the Adversarial Spirits are repelled, the myths describe this exact conflict and events having repeated many times in the past, so many even the deities involved don't recall the number. During this conflict Mafala briefly ensnared Merrunz in her web and attempted to devour the knowledge he'd gained during his time in the Great Darkness.{{ref|name=TAS}}{{ref|name=Boethra|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}}

The duality of Khajiiti worship of the Fire-Cat can be seen early in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]'s history. During [[Lore:Darloc Brae|Darloc Brae]]'s reign, cultists of Merrunz burned down the Grand Archive in [[Lore:Corinthe|Corinthe]], which contained the province's records from the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]. However, despite his worship of the Prince of Destruction, the warlord went through great lengths to preserve the remaining texts and hide them across Elsweyr, placing them in smaller vaults and archives.{{Ref|name=OotKMT|{{Cite Book|Origins of the Khajiiti Martial Tradition}}}} Darloc Brae showcased an ambition much like Merrunz, conquering [[Lore:Anequina|Anequina]] and the surrounding territories, with some areas erected in Daegon's name.{{Ref|name=ESOLoadDarkpool|[[ON:Darkpool Mine|Darkpool Mine]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}

===Reachmen===
To the [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]], Mehrunes Dagon is the spirit of ambition and destruction, and is known as the '''Lord of Ambition'''. Life in [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] is harsh, and its inhabitants always strive to improve. This drive to become greater eventually leads some to turn to Mehrunes Dagon.{{ref|name=Aydolan|[[ON:Aydolan|Aydolan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}

===Order of the Waking Flame===
[[File:ON-concept-Dagon 02.jpg|thumb|right|Dagon's connection to Magnus and the Magna Ge depicted]]
The [[Lore:Order of the Waking Flame|Order of the Waking Flame]] embraced the beliefs Sister [[Lore:Celdina|Celdina]] formed from the forbidden texts in [[Lore:Stormhaven|Stormhaven]]'s [[Lore:Priory of the Golden Staff|Priory of the Golden Staff]], that Mehrunes Dagon was created by the Magna Ge as a weapon to unmake the flawed creation of Mundus in accordance to [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]]'s plans. It is said that Magnus the Architect watches from the [[Lore:Sun|Sun]] until the day the destruction of the flawed world is achieved, so he can return and make it anew without its flaws.{{Ref|name=DCT|{{Cite Book|Doombringer Celdina's Testament|ns_base=ON}}}}{{Ref|name=MTCSC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sister Celdina}}}} To them, Mundus is a place of pain and suffering, thus they looked to Dagon to move past their mortal perils, hoping that he would purge the impurities of existence so that it may emerge anew.{{ref|name=EoMDV1|{{Cite Book|Epics of Mehrunes Dagon, Volume 1}}}}{{ref|name=ONCeldina|[[ON:Sister Celdina|Sister Celdina]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} This contrasted the ideas of Lyranth, who believes that when the Magna Ge fled, they spat on the conviction of the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] that remained to finish creating Mundus, and Dagon's emergence and sphere may be reflective of the destruction caused by their abandonment.{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}}

The cult's High Chronicler documented epics involving Mehrunes Dagon. One teaches that Dagon does not back away from bargains. Summoned by the Enchanter Arebath for his dominion over fire and flood, which would allow him to ensure her safety. Arabeth cast a [[Lore:Spells|spell]] to call the ocean's waters to rejuvenate a dead forest. However, having miscalculated the strength of the spell, she soon found herself in mortal peril, a mountainous wave crashing down on her. Remembering his bargain Dagon unleashed a great wave of flame that turned the water to steam, the clash between Dagon's flames and Arabeth's tidal spell purged the local blighted wildlife, ensuring that lush flora would rise from the ashes.{{ref|name=EoMDV1}} Another story tells of the Prince invading [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] to rescue the enslaved souls of his followers from Molag Bal. The true believers that refused to bend instead took it upon themselves to engage in subterfuge, thwarting Molag Bal and his minions whenever they could. Mehrunes Dagon rewarded those that endured with the means of their escape; a powerful explosion that rattled Coldharbour. The destruction allowed Dagon to manifest within the realm, looming over the mountains of Coldharbour, where he engaged in combat with Molag Bal. The two Princes exchanged furious strikes, their blows blurring together with speed and sending ear-shattering thunderclaps throughout the realm. Though Dagon's mere avatar was not able to defeat Molag Bal in his full power, this distraction bought enough time for him to transport his followers that refused to bend the knee to the Deadlands. Those that had submitted, however, were left behind, struck to the ground with bodies broken and ears bleeding from the devastation of Molag Bal and Mehrunes Dagon's duel.{{ref|name=EoMDV2|{{Cite Book|Epics of Mehrunes Dagon, Volume 2}}}}

One account by a Devastator of the Waking Flame documents a vision that Mehrunes Dagon gave him in a dream. In the dream, the cultist is shown Dagon's citadel [[Lore:Ardent Hope|Ardent Hope]] and is told to commit to memory the might of the citadel and the vastness of Dagon's legions, which demonstrate that his victory is inevitable and that only in serving him can the lives of his followers find meaning. After this, Dagon transformed into a cloud of burning cinders and flew upwards to his old citadel, Destruction's Solace, the cultist dragged along with him. At Destruction's Solace the cultist witnessed a ruined citadel populated only by tormented [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]]. When he asked what had reduced the citadel and those within to its current state, Dagon revealed he'd destroyed them himself. Terrified by the revelation, the Devastator asked Dagon for a reason. Dagon responded that he did it because he is Destruction and so it is what he must do. Before dismissing the cultist's spirit back to the waking world of Nirn, Dagon urged him to measure the devastation so he could remember the finality of his purpose, and that only through Dagon would the end of his follower's lives be given meaning.{{ref|name=AVotTC|{{Cite Book|A Vision of the Twin Citadels}}}}

The Waking Flame were known to sometimes use [[Lore:Agonymium|Agonymium]] Stones inscribed with Daedric runes, to absorb the mortal pain and suffering in the surroundings of the Stones and turn them into magical power.{{ref|[[ON:Agonymium Stone (object)|Agonymium Stone]]s in [[ON:The Dread Cellar|the Dread Cellar]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Mythic Dawn===
[[File:OB-banner-Mythic Dawn.jpg|thumb|right|120px|Banner of the Mythic Dawn]]
The [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] was once one of the most secretive and mysterious of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] cults{{ref|name=OBTar-Meena|[[OB:Tar-Meena|Tar-Meena]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} before gaining infamy due to its role in the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]].{{ref|name=Oblivion}} The cult's beliefs were largely shaped by the teachings of its leader, [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], and his [[Lore:Mythic Dawn Commentaries|''Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes'']].{{ref|name=MDC}}{{ref|name=Oblivion}}{{ref|name=TOC|{{Cite Book|The Oblivion Crisis}}}} The name "Mythic Dawn" is a reference to the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], the time before the creation of the world. Camoran believed he and his disciples were destined to rule over a reborn, "liberated" [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], ushering in a new Dawn.{{ref|name=MDC1}} 

Central to the cult's beliefs was the claim that the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] are not gods, merely pretenders who have tricked mortals ever since they betrayed and attempted to destroy [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]. The cult believes the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] are the true gods of the universe, as they cannot die. Camoran also asserted that Lorkhan is one of the Daedra, and offered this as to why the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] could not be destroyed. Furthermore, as a Daedric Prince, Lorkhan has his own [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]—Tamriel.{{Intnote|nb 2|[nb 2]}} Thus, Mehrunes Dagon undertook the Oblivion Crisis not to destroy Tamriel, but to "liberate the Occupied Lands" from the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]].{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[OB:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Paradise (quest)|Paradise]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

In Tamriel, the cult called their enemies the "Dragon's Blood"—a reference to the covenant between [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and Queen [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} More prosaically, it refers to the line of [[Lore:Tamrielic Emperors|rulers]] keeping the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] alight.{{ref|name=MDC3}} In particular, Mankar Camoran had a "long duel" with the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty]],{{ref|name=OBMankar}} dating back to the reign of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], when it is speculated the ''Commentaries'' were written.{{ref|name=OBTar-Meena}}

'''Mehrunes the Razor, Mehrunes the Thieftaker, Mehrunes Godsbody''', and '''Mehrunes the Red Arms That Went Up''' are among some of the names given to the Prince of Destruction in the ''Mythic Dawn Commentaries''.{{ref|name=MDC4}}

The beliefs of the Mythic Dawn were met with derision by Lyranth the Foolkiller. She described the assertion that [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] is not distinct from Oblivion but rather one of its constitutive realms as false, and mocked the claim that the realm belongs to Dagon by birthright as "convenient". Lyranth questioned whether the information had come from Dagon himself or simply been spread by his cultists, and advised that "one should pay less attention to idiotic cultists".{{Ref|name=LAMDDSE}}

==History==
===First Era===
[[File:OB-quest-Unearthing Mehrunes Razor 13.jpg|thumb|left|The ruins of Varsa Baalim]]
In ancient times, Dagon created a construct known as the Nefarivigum to challenge those who drew near to undertake a trial. One who passed the trial would be deemed worthy of wielding [[Lore:Mehrunes' Razor|Mehrunes' Razor]]. Located in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], the Nefarivigum was discovered by the ancient [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]], who arrogantly believed they could turn away any who sought the Razor. To this end, they built the vast, ringed city of [[Lore:Varsa Baalim|Varsa Baalim]] during the height of Ayleid civilization. For many years, pilgrims came to Varsa Baalim seeking the Razor, but the Ayleids turned them away. One day in the early years of the First Era, a [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] infiltrated the city. Before long, the city was infested with undead and thrust into chaos. The city suddenly and mysteriously disappeared. None know what happened, but Varsa Baalim seemingly vanished into the mountains of the Eastern [[Lore:Nibenay Basin|Nibenay Basin]].{{ref|name=TOAC|{{Cite Book|Treatise on Ayleidic Cities}}}}

Mehrunes Dagon and his forces destroyed [[Lore:Ald Sotha|Ald Sotha]], home of [[Lore:House Sotha|House Sotha]], sometime in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. A young [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] was the only known survivor of this incident, having been rescued by [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]].{{ref|name=ABHOAS|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of Ald Sotha}}}} 

In response to the destruction of [[Lore:Gilverdale|Gilverdale]] at the hands of Molag Bal, Sotha Sil, now a [[Lore:Tribunal|living god]], sought out eight of the most influential Daedric Princes. Mehrunes Dagon was among them, and between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in {{Year|1E 2920}}, a deal was made which would become known as the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]].{{ref|name=2920v4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} Little is known about the Compact, but it is theorized that it seeks to prevent the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]],{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}} and only permits them to commune with certain intermediaries, such as witches and sorcerers.{{ref|name=2920v4}}
[[File:MW-place-Plaza Brindisi Dorom 02.jpg|thumb|right|Depiction of Almalexia's battle with Mehrunes Dagon]]
Mehrunes Dagon's general attitude toward the Coldharbour Compact was that of indifference. To him, Sotha Sil was a mere mortal mechanist that was to be crushed like he did his childhood home, and no compact was to keep him from destroying Nirn.{{Ref|name=ONTWMD}} This sentiment would manifest a few months later in Sun's Dusk of 1E 2920. The [[Lore:Skeffington Coven|Skeffington Coven]] of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] took in an exiled Dunmeri woman named [[Lore:Turala|Turala]], the former lover of the Duke of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], [[Lore:Brindisi Dorom|Brindisi Dorom]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Hearth Fire (v9)}}}} However, after a trip to [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]], the Skeffington Coven was massacred by an [[Lore:Orc|Orcish]] raiding party. Turala was falsely led to believe that the massacre was the work of assassins sent by Brindisi Dorom himself. Enraged, the witch called forth the Prince of Destruction to destroy Mournhold and slay her former lover, and he complied.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Frostfall (v10)}}}}

During the carnage, [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and Sotha Sil arrived to stop the Prince. The battlefield was lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. Almalexia plunged [[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]] into Dagon's flesh and carved him from within. Meanwhile, Sotha Sil, enacting vengeance for Ald Sotha, used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Prince into submission. Wounded, Sil allegedly whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}}{{ref|name=2920v11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}} Defeated, Dagon sought to have a final laugh, and flung a piece of Mournhold into his realm with the inhabitants intact.{{ref|[[ON:Faven Indoril|Faven Indoril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} After his banishment, Sotha Sil sealed the planar doors between Nirn and the Deadlands, blocking off all access for Dagon as further punishment for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.{{ref|name=ONLyranth|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]] believe that this battle between Mehrunes Dagon and the Tribunal marked the birth of "Sarmissonays'um ghoul-thing[s]", which emerged from Dagon's own flayed skin.{{ref|name=TTISV8}} Indeed, the [[Lore:Ruinach|Ruinachs]], a four-armed race of Daedra, are thought by some scholars, mages, and even the Ruinachs themselves, to have emerged from Dagon's flesh in the wake of his battle against Almalexia.{{ref|name=BPTMR|''[[GEN:Blackwood Preview—The Mighty Ruinachs|Blackwood Preview—The Mighty Ruinachs]]'' – The Elder Scrolls Online official website}}

It is unknown what event prompted Dagon to begin working on a method to breach the Luminal Barriers. Some possibilities include (but are not limited to) when the Dragonfires were established by [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]],{{Ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} or after Dagon was punished for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.{{ref|name=ONLyranth}} Regardless, Dagon began working on methods to bridge a path between his realm and Nirn to start his invasion, and so he began refining his dimension hopping technology.{{ref|name=BPOP|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/60051 Blackwood Preview—Oblivion Portals] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} The initial stages involved simple circular Oblivion Portals, but this would later develop into the framed doorways known as Oblivion Gates.{{ref|name=ESOLive2|group=UOL|[https://www.twitch.tv/videos/1164620475 The Elder Scrolls Online: Gates of Oblivion Year-End Event (1:30:00-1:31:01)] on [[GEN:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-trailer-Deadlands and Damnation-Dagon.jpg|thumb|left|Mehrunes Dagon bargaining with the Longhouse Emperors]]	
Several smaller, less influential cults devoted to Mehrunes Dagon were also known to be in operation during the Second Era, such as the [[Lore:Brotherhood of Destruction|Brotherhood of Destruction]] between 2E 115 and 2E 140, and the [[Lore:Bloodfire Cult|Bloodfire Cult]] in 2E 243 and 2E 244.{{ref|name=DTtA}} In particular, the Brotherhood of Destruction's attack on merchants of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] would be the first known transgression of Dagonist cultists against the Imperial capital.

At some point in the Second Era, Hranvard Frostfinger, a [[Lore:Witch|witch]] of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]] sacrificed thirteen people to Dagon in a single night, prompting the Prince to open a [[Lore:Magical Transportation|portal]] to the Deadlands along the coastline of the [[Lore:Sea of Ghosts|Sea of Ghosts]]. Though Hranvard was ultimately slain by a party of witch-hunters of the [[Lore:Direfrost Keep|Direfrost Clan]], her ritual was not stopped in time. A rift to Oblivion opened and the air started to boil before a torrent of flame poured out, engulfing thirty of the hunters in flame. From the portal, Dagon's voice boomed that he'd allow the one survivor to leave because he'd suffer more among the living. The [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] expressed skepticism of the surviving hunter's story despite the mirrored sand left behind at the coastline of the Sea of Ghosts, the missing hunters, and the remains of a ritual circle that matched the one described by the witch-hunter, dismissing the event as a meteorological phenomenon. Nonetheless the event resulted in the Direfrost Clan intensifying their war against the witch covens of Eastmarch.{{ref|name=BV1}}

At some point in the Second Era, Mehrunes Dagon commissioned the [[Lore: Mad Architect|Mad Architect]] to design the razor pits of the Deadlands for him. The Architect complied, creating razor pits said to never grow dull and to be able to cut flesh, bone, and even spirit essence, in ways that even the most powerful [[Lore:Restoration|healers]] can never mend.{{ref|name=TAWPT|{{Cite Book|To All Who Pass Through}}}}

====After the Soulburst====
[[File:ON-quest-Blessings of the Eight 02.jpg|thumb|left|Dagon communes with the Veiled Queen through a flame]][[File:ON-quest-Of Stone and Steam.jpg|Souls being drawn into the Oblivion Gate at Rockgrove|thumb|right]]
Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, displeased with the rise of the tolerant [[Lore:Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]] and her [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], [[Lore:Estre|High Kinlady Estre]] donned the role of the leader of the Altmeri separatists known as the [[Lore:Veiled Heritance|Veiled Heritance]]. In her desperate attempt to impede the pass of Queen Ayrenn and her troops to [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]], she made a pact with Dagon. Estre summoned Daedra from the Deadlands in [[Lore:Dawnbreak|Dawnbreak]]'s abandoned mine. The Daedra set the town alight and murdered its citizens until the {{Lore Link|First Auridon Marines}} intervened and organized an evacuation. The marines managed to stop the hordes from spreading across the area, and an [[Lore:Hero#SoullessOne|Eye of the Queen]] came and defeated the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] that orchestrated the assault.{{Ref|name=ESOAshes|[[ON:Through the Ashes|Through the Ashes]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Per her agreement with Mehrunes Dagon, High Kinlady Estre unleashed more Daedra upon [[Lore:Torinaan|Torinaan]], a holy site of the Eight Divines in the north. She then moved on to her ultimate target, the city of [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]].{{Ref|name=ESOBOTE|[[ON:Blessings of the Eight|Blessings of the Eight]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Here, she was let into the city by its ruler, {{Lore Link|Rilis XIII|High Kinlord Rilis XIII}}, who remained oblivious to her true nature. Once inside, she opened four Oblivion Gates across the city, allowing her Daedric army to lay siege to Firsthold and kidnap the kinlord. Estre was slain by a hero and the Oblivion Gates were closed, but most of Firsthold was left in ruins.{{Ref|name=ESOSAT|[[ON:Sever All Ties|Sever All Ties]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Later that year, Dagon's Razor Master, Erthas, attacked the Bosmeri village of [[Lore:Whisper Grove|Whisper Grove]]. The forest was set ablaze, the village was overrun by Dagonites, and a portal to the Deadlands was opened to stir the chaos. The [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] were hired by the village's leader, Gilraen, to slay the Dagonites and push back the Daedra. After Erthas' death, Mehrunes Dagon himself offered to seal the portal if Gilraen agreed to meet with him in the Deadlands. Gilraen agreed, and the portal was shut.{{ref|name=ESORE|[[ON:Razor's Edge|Razor's Edge]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Mehrunes Dagon gave Gilraen the means to survive in the Deadlands as a Razor Master, namely his eponymous Razor.{{ref|name=GilraenRTA|[[ON:Gilraen|Gilraen]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Return to Ash|Return to Ash]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Several months later, Gilraen's husband, Fingaenion, reopened the portal in a desperate bid to save his wife. He hired the Undaunted to enter Oblivion and retrieve her, but they failed.{{ref|[[ON:Fingaenion|Fingaenion]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Return to Ash|Return to Ash]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Another group of Undaunted arrived hours later and successfully intervened. The adventurers went through the portal Fingaenion re-opened to enter the Knives of Discord, a region ruled by one of Mehrunes Dagon's lieutenants, [[Lore:Valkyn Skoria|Valkyn Skoria]].{{ref|name=GilraenRTA}} The Undaunted rescued Gilraen and their comrades, slew the Valkyn, and closed the portal.{{ref|name=ESORA|[[ON:Return to Ash|Return to Ash]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Gilraen was worried that Mehrunes Dagon would seek her out, given that she facilitated the slaying of one of his lieutenants and fled the realm with his Razor.{{ref|name=GilraenRTA}}

In Elsweyr, an aspect of Darloc Brae, a worshipper of Merrunz, reanimated his ancient forces in the [[Lore:Rimmen Necropolis|Rimmen Necropolis]] and dueled with the Dremora forces of a champion of Mehrunnez (Merrunz).{{Ref|name=OtTotFM|{{Cite Book|On the Trail of the Forgotten Mane}}}}{{Ref|name=ESORNGE|[[ON:Rimmen Necropolis Group Event|Rimmen Necropolis Group Event]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Both sides were eventually defeated by [[Lore:The Vestige|wandering travelers]].{{Ref|name=ESORNGE}} In the {{Lore Link|Darkpool Mine}}, a cult called the {{Lore Link|Claws of Daegon}} were using an [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] of the Prince called the [[Lore:Deadland Hammer|Deadland Hammer]] to create a force of [[Lore:Iron Atronach|Iron Atronachs]]. The Vestige infiltrated the mine, slew the leader of the cult, and retrieved the hammer.{{ref|name=IB|Events of [[ON:Iron in the Blood|Iron in the Blood]] during [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} {{ref|name=MD|[[ON:Mara'dahni|Mara'dahni]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DH|Item Description of the [[ON:Elsweyr Quest Items#Deadland Hammer|Deadland Hammer]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

During that same period, the Sul-Xan, a tribe of Argonians faithful to Dagon, raided the ancient city of Rockgrove. The Sul-Xan hoped to use the [[Lore:Souls|souls]] of the slain together with the powerful tides of flame and steam in the region in order to summon a powerful Dagonic [[Lore:Harvester|Harvester]] named Xalvakka into Nirn. This plan would be foiled when a group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] raided Rockgrove, slew the Sul-Xan performing the ritual, and entered the Deadlands themselves to slay Xalvakka before she could cross over into Tamriel.{{ref|name=Plea| Events of [[ON:A Plea from the Flames|A Plea from the Flames]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Steam| Events of [[ON:Of Stone and Steam|Of Stone and Steam]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dust|[[ON:Dust-On-Scales|Dust-On-Scales]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

====Prelude to the Four Ambitions====
[[File:ON-wallpaper-Ottoman Gates of Oblivion-3840x2160 Full.jpg|thumb|left|Artistic depiction of the events of Dagon's Second Era invasion]]
The [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachman]] chieftain Durcorach began building a following in the Reach and allied with the [[Lore:Tagh Droiloch|Tagh Droiloch]] coven in {{Year|2E 527}}, subduing all rival clans with their help through means involving Daedric bargains.{{Ref|name=HCP:TTD|{{Cite Book|High Chancellor's Papers: The Tagh Droiloch}}}} A deal was struck with Mehrunes Dagon to have Durcorach crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil, with his sorcerers performing countless tasks in wait for the day when the "final part of the arrangement" would be carried out and Dagon will "claim his due" with the Four Ambitions.{{ref|name=ADiS|{{Cite Book|A Deal is Struck|ns_base=Online}}}} With years of preparation, Durcorach summoned a great horde of Reach warriors and descended upon Cyrodiil in {{Year|2E 533}}, seizing the Ruby Throne for his own.{{Ref|name=HoM:ASoS|{{Cite Book|History of Markarth: A Story in Stone}}}} For the Four Ambitions to come to fruition, [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] noted that everything "must be ready when the time is right". Within the Longhouse Emperor's dynasty, "promises were made" and bargains were "set in blood and stone" with Dagon as the Prince's way of not accepting failure.{{Ref|name=DID|{{Cite Book|Deciphered Imperial Document}}}}

After Emperorship passed to him, [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]] "forged" the Ambitions, which were later entrusted to his son, [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]]. Moricar had an annex built beneath Imperial City to house the last of their secrets, a safe haven for others to find so they can carry out the work. Although the bargain was intended to maintain the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]]' grip over Tamriel long after Durcorach's death, Leovic's decision to legalize Daedra worship led to a [[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|rebellion]], which ended in Duke [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] slaying Leovic in the Imperial Throne Room.{{ref|name=EFEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}} With Varen Aquilarios at the gates of the Imperial City in {{Year|2E 577}}, toward the end of [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]], Leovic gathered those who knew part of the plan in the annex to appeal to the Prince of Destruction and seek his help again. Only his steward Farrul Lupus and the leader of the Order of the Waking Flame knew the entire truth, and even they were not made privy to the changes needed to be made. The Four Ambitions were not ready by this time, with their "time" still a few years away, but had they been ready Leovic would have used them then to destroy Varen and save his legacy.{{Ref|name=DID}}{{Ref|name=TJoEL|{{Cite Book|The Journal of Emperor Leovic}}}} Leovic planned that if he failed, the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame would have to deal with the others and determine where he moved the Ambitions.{{Ref|name=DID}}{{Ref|name=TJoEL}}

[[File:BT-concept-Xivilai Moath.webp|thumb|right|Xivilai Moath, adopted son of Dagon]]
Sometime before the start of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]],{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer|[[Betrayal:Betrayal of the Second Era|Betrayal of the Second Era]] ''Cyrodiil Gazetteer''}}{{rp|52}} the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] took an interest in stopping the Order of the Waking Flame's activities in Cyrodiil. This was a goal they shared with the [[Lore:Soldiers of the Void|Soldiers of the Void]], who had a mutual interest in ensuring that Mehrunes Dagon was not summoned to Tamriel. The Waking Flame planned to perform ritualistic sacrifices to summon Xivilai Moath, who was to serve as Dagon's emissary. However, the Psijics and the Soldiers of the Void interrupted and tampered with the summoning rituals, which resulted in Moath manifesting in a weakened enough state to be banished back to Oblivion.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer}}{{rp|23}} The Soldiers of the Void alone were not enough to slay Moath, but he was successfully vanquished with the arrival of the Psijic Order's forces, delaying Dagon's arrival.{{ref|name=CyrodGazetteer}}{{rp|44}}

====Three of the Four Ambitions====
In {{Year|2E 582}}, Farrul Lupus sent [[Lore:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]] to find a book that put her on the trail of Emperor Leovic's secret, informing her that the Emperor's secret concerned a deal made with Mehrunes Dagon and the Four Ambitions.{{Ref|name=ESOEveli}} Lupus was assassinated by the Order of the Waking Flame posing as the Dark Brotherhood,{{Ref|name=ESORenna|[[ON:Renna#The Emperor's Secret|Renna]]'s dialogue in [[ON:The Emperor's Secret|ESO]]}} prompting Eveli and the Vestige to travel to the {{Lore Link|Ne Salas}} Cache Annex. They recovered a ritual that indicated that the cult planned to use the shrine there to learn the location of the Ambitions, and followed Doombringer Eshil into the Deadlands. Eshil spoke with Dagon, revealing that the High Priest sent him, with Dagon expressing that he invested a high amount of effort into the Ambitions and to his dismay the Ambitions were misplaced and the bargain struck remained unfulfilled, telling Eshil to kill the intruders to get the Ambitions.{{Ref|name=ESODoombringer|Dialogue between [[ON:Doombringer Eshil#The Emperor's Secret|Doombringer Eshil]] and [[ON:Mehrunes Dagon#The Emperor's Secret|Mehrunes Dagon]] in [[ON:The Emperor's Secret|ESO]]}} Eshil was killed by Eveli and the Vestige, who planned to find the people on Leovic's list, warn them about the Dark Brotherhood, and find out what they knew about the Ambitions.{{Ref|name=ESOEveli|[[ON:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOTES|Events of [[ON:The Emperor's Secret|The Emperor's Secret]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Vestige worked with Eveli Sharp-Arrow to uncover the mystery, and warn the surviving councilors, many of whom were known to be in the Cyrodilic region of Blackwood. Most of the councilors had reconciled themselves to the fall of the Longhouse Emperors, and managed to hang on to positions of importance—for instance, Councilor [[Lore:Tarnian Lovidicus|Tarnian Lovidicus]] served as a member of the Chamber of Legates of Leyawiin.{{ref|name=RL|[[ON:Captain Rian Liore|Captain Rian Liore]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} However, Councilor [[Lore:Ertus Vandacia|Ertus Vandacia]], the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame, still harbored designs of seizing power for himself—the Daedric power of the pact with Dagon, the Imperial Throne, and the Four Ambitions. Vandacia and his Waking Flame murdered many of his fellow councilors, but framed the Dark Brotherhood.{{ref|name=AUA|[[ON:An Unexpected Adversary|An Unexpected Adversary]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} 
[[File:ON-quest-Pyre of Ambition.jpg|thumb|left|Mehrunes Dagon, invading Fort Redmane as the realms merge]]
The Vestige and their allies raced to find the Four Ambitions before Vandacia could acquire them. The Ambitions would turn out not to be conventional weapons but were instead people empowered at birth through the mixing of Daedric power with the essence of a mortal [[Lore:Souls|soul]], a combined essence that would grow throughout their lives until it could be used as the spark that would merge Nirn with the Deadlands and summon Dagon's fire from Oblivion through their sacrifice.{{ref|name=Weapons|[[ON:A Hidden Vault|A Hidden Vault]], [[ON:A Mysterious Event|A Mysterious Event]] and [[ON:Weapons of Destruction|Weapons of Destruction]] quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOCalia}}{{ref|name=ESOSombren}}{{ref|name=ESOVandacia|[[ON:Councilor Vandacia|Councilor Vandacia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONCeldina}} After the emptying of the Ambitions's Doomvaults, special locations that were actually pocket realms, described as bottled up pieces of Nirn that were created to contain the volatile power of the Ambitions, which would rapidly grow once outside, threatening to ignite cities and consume the Ambitions themselves until they learned to control it, they were quickly moved from the indefensible city of Gideon to the much more sturdy Imperial-built [[Lore:Fort Redmane|Fort Redmane]].{{ref|name=Weapons}}{{ref|name=ESOMirri|[[ON:Mirri Elendis|Mirri Elendis]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOScattered|Events of [[ON:Shattered and Scattered|Shattered and Scattered]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOCalia}}{{ref|name=ESOSombren|[[ON:Sombren|Sombren]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Vandacia deployed an army to capture the three known Ambitions. The bulk of his army was defeated outside the walls, but Vandacia and a powerful group of cultists were able to get into the fort with portal magic.{{Ref|name=PoA|[[ON:Pyre of Ambition|Pyre of Ambition]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} 

The Ambition, Destron, was sacrificed by Vandacia, who seized his power and attempted to merge Nirn into the Deadlands, a dangerous process which had the potential to destroy both realms if disrupted.{{ref|name=ESOVandacia}} This enabled Mehrunes Dagon to participate in the assault on the fort. However, though he'd attained great power by siphoning a little of the power used during the ritual to create the Ambitions for himself,{{ref|name=ESOSombren}} Vandacia was slain through the aid of Calia, who used her power as an Ambition to drain his Daedric power,{{ref|name=ESOCalia}} ending the merge and banishing Dagon. The two known surviving Ambitions, Sombren and Calia, realized that their power was too uncontrollable to remain in Tamriel until they could channel it, and they would have to hide in the Deadlands, a realm that would naturally siphon and stabilize their power for as long as they remained there,{{ref|name=ESOCalia}}{{ref|name=ESOSombren}} meanwhile, the Vestige resolved to search for the missing fourth Ambition.{{Ref|name=PoA}}
====Last of the Four Ambitions====
[[File:ON-quest-Ambition's End.jpg|thumb|right|The realms merging at Fort Sundercliff]]
Mehrunes Dagon made moves to claim the demi plane of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]], as it was a convenient way station between Nirn and the rest of Oblivion, which would ally him to bypass the restrictions [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] had imposed on his realm due to his breaking of the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]], sealing the planar connections between the Deadlands and Nirn.{{ref|name=ESOLyranthFoolkiller}} The Waking Flame, attempted to seize control of Fargrave through the inscription of "Votive Runes", glyphs containing Dagonic prayers. in various locations. The runes would siphon the emotional energy of the mortal inhabitants, placing them in a state of increased emotion before overwhelming their minds and rendering them unresponsive. The drained energy would than sweep across Fargrave, discorporating all Daedra, including the [[Lore:Grasp of the Stricture|Grasp of the Stricture]] at once, leaving the realm defenseless for Dagon's followers to seize. This plan was ultimately foiled by the Vestige{{ref|name=ESOLyranthFoolkiller|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:The Celestial Palanquin|The Celestial Palanquin]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[ON:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} 

Unfortunately for the Prince, infighting erupted between Sister Celdina's Waking Flame and Valkynaz Nokvroz of the Ruinblood Clan over who would take credit over the utilization of the Cataclysts; machines that remake Daedra into living disasters known as [[Lore:Incarnate|Incarnates]] through the same power and process which was used to create the Ambitions.{{ref|name=ESOLyranthKey|[[ON:Lyranth|Lyranth]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Key and the Cataclyst|The Key and the Cataclyst]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Valkynaz Nokvroz seized the Ambition [[Lore:Mairead|Mairead]] and used her blood to transform himself into a "Perfected Incarnate", an elemental incarnation of fire, but was defeated by the Vestige.{{ref|[[ON:Valkynaz Nokvroz|Valkynaz Nokvroz]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[ON:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} In the end the infighting only hampered Dagon's plans, and allowed for the defeat of both factions.{{ref|[[ON:Against All Hope|Against All Hope]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} 

After it was discovered that the Four Ambitions were actually imbued with Dagon's nymic, the Prince of Destruction was summoned to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] by the Ambition {{Lore Link|Sombren}}, who stole the power of three of the Ambitions and used it to summon Dagon, influenced by both his study of the [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|Mysterium Xarxes]], and the part of all Ambitions which belonged to Mehrunes Dagon, his Egonymic, which made them susceptible to Dagon's dark impulses.{{ref|name=ESOCalia|[[ON:Calia|Calia]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} A portal was opened between [[Lore:Fort Sundercliff|Fort Sundercliff]] and Dagon's capital of {{Lore Link|Destruction's Solace}} in the Deadlands, resulting in the instant destruction of the fort and the surrounding town. Upon manifesting on Tamriel, Dagon once more began the process of merging his realm with Nirn using the power of the Ambitions. However, Dagon and Sombren were followed by a force of mortals and [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] led by the Vestige, who interrupted the ritual. 

Another Ambition, Mairead, who had secretly been prepared as a weapon that could banish Mehrunes Dagon should the need arise by emperor Moricar's modification of the Ambition ritual,.{{Ref|name=ESOMd|[[ON:Emperor Moricar|Emperor Moricar]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}{{Ref|name=PHJ|{{Cite Book|Partially Hidden Journal}}}} was able to steal power back from Sombren and used Dagon's [[Lore:Egonymic|egonymic]] to bind and weaken the Prince. With the help of the Vestige, Dagon's physical form was destroyed and he was banished to the furthest corner of the Deadlands via the use of his egonymic. This reversed the attempted merging of the realms, mitigating the destruction that had been caused to Sundercliff and the surrounding landscape.{{ref|[[ON:Ambition's End|Ambition's End]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[ON:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} After Dagon's banishment, the power of the Ambitions was stolen by Lyranth the Foolkiller, who sought to use their power, which she described as the power to truly change one's nature, even that of a Daedra, for some unknown purpose.{{ref|name=ESOLyranthFoolkiller}}

===Third Era===

At some point before the year {{Year|3E 172}} (and possibly before the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]),{{Intnote|nb 3|[nb 3]}} [[Lore:Chimere|Chimere Graegyn]], a [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] retainer and noted Daedra summoner, made a pact with Mehrunes Dagon. Chimere wore the [[Lore:Savior's Hide|Armor of the Savior's Hide]], and it protected him from the blows of oathbreakers (as Dagon now was), which gave Chimere enough time to banish Dagon to Oblivion by invoking the Prince's [[Lore:Protonymic|protonymic]].{{ref|name=BSChimere}}{{ref|name=TMKR}}

====The Imperial Simulacrum====
[[File:AR-npc-Jagar Tharn (Deluxe).png|thumb|left|Jagar Tharn]]
Mehrunes Dagon was instrumental in [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]]'s imposture and assumption of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Imperial]] throne on {{Year|3E 389}}.{{ref|name=BSAngada|[[BS:Sirran Angada|Sirran Angada]]'s dialogue in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} After Jagar banished Emperor Uriel Septim VII and [[Lore:Talin Warhaft|Talin Warhaft]]{{ref|[[Arena:Main Quest|Main Quest]] introduction in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} with the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]],{{ref|name=TRBB|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah}}}}{{ref|name=ARSilmane|[[Arena:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane's dialogue]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} the sovereign was kept in bondage by Dagon in one of his dark prisons{{ref|name=BSAthenaeum}}{{rp|180}} somewhere in Oblivion, where he experienced nothing but inexplicable and haunting nightmares.{{ref|name=ALOUS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Uriel Septim VII}}}} This period where Jagar ruled would become known as the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]].

[[File:OBR-quest-Light the Dragonfires 03.jpg|thumb|left|Mehrunes Dagon fights Akatosh]]
The Mythic Dawn focused on the eternal dawn on which Dagon would come to cleanse the world of all non-followers. In order to recover the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] and foil Dagon's plans, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] had to recover the [[Lore:Mysterium Xarxes|''Mysterium Xarxes'']], a book written by Dagon himself, so that [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]] could open a portal to the [[Lore:Paradise|Paradise]] of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]], where the amulet was being kept. Dagon himself made a short but dramatic appearance as a gigantic four-armed humanoid armed with an axe and talons at the climax of the Crisis.{{ref|name=Oblivion}} At the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]]'s [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]], Martin Septim shattered the Amulet of Kings, transforming himself into an avatar of Akatosh. Dagon was handily defeated, after which the avatar turned into a [[Lore:Statues|statue]] at the center of the ruined temple, a reminder of Martin's sacrifice, which led to the common belief that the forces of Oblivion could never again openly threaten the people of Tamriel.{{ref|name=OBLight|[[OB:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 
[[File:OBR-quest-The Crimson Cave.jpg|Mehrunes' Harbinger with Mythic Dawn cultists in Mingo Cave|thumb|right]]
During the Crisis, the Hero of Kvatch investigated a rumor that lead them to follow a series of hidden clues left behind by the Mythic Dawn cult. After retrieving all the hidden notes from the shrines of Hircine and Molag Bal, and from the Lucky Old Lady statue in [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]], the hero arrived at the Lonely Suitor Lodge. From there, the Mythic Dawn sleeper agent, Ranaline, directed them to Mingo Cave, where a hidden congregation of the cult awaited. At the cave, the hero either disguised themselves as a member of the Mythic Dawn and carried out a sacrifice that the cult demanded as proof of enmity towards the Septims, or entered the cave and slew the cultists inside. Either way, Mehrunes' Harbinger, a Dremora servant of Dagon, congratulated the hero for pleasing her lord, either through doing his will, or through killing those who were too weak to serve him, something she deemed as not being a true act of opposition, since Dagon was eternal and so would always prevail in the end. The Harbinger granted the hero the ability to summon her and receive armor and weapons imbued with Dagon's power, which she claimed were beyond the ability of mortals to craft, and so could only be given by her directly.{{ref|name=OBTrail|[[OB:Burning Trail|Burning Trail]] quest in [[OB:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]}}{{ref|name=OBCrimsonCave|[[OB:The Crimson Cave|The Crimson Cave]] quest in [[OB:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]}}{{ref|name=OBHarbinger|[[OB:Mehrunes' Harbinger|Mehrunes Harbinger]] dialogue in [[OB:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-quest-The Consequences.jpg|thumb|right|The unclosable Oblivion Gate, bypassing Martin's sacrifice (Skyrim)]]
Despite his defeat in the Oblivion Crisis, Mehrunes Dagon was still determined to invade Nirn, and set up a scheme to bypass the liminal barrier. He was aware of the existence of the [[Lore:Prophecy of the Dragonborn|Prophecy of the Dragonborn]], and sought to use it to his advantage. For this purpose, he reached out through dreams to attract a new following to his side. Dagon had a Dunmer named Vonos revive the Mythic Dawn starting in {{Year|4E 200}}, 12th of Mid Year.{{ref|name=Vonos}} He led them to a [[Lore:Red Scar Cavern|buried Mythic Dawn temple]] within [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where they were tasked with reconstructing a dormant Oblivion Gate. The Gate to Dagon's Deadlands was cleansed with personal blood sacrifices.{{ref|name=SRSJ|{{Cite Book|Skorvild's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} 

By {{Year|4E 201}}, Mehrunes' Razor was in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], having been broken into three pieces. The [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] collected these for the Mythic Dawn Museum in [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]], and was offered the restored Razor by Dagon in exchange for killing the museum's proprietor, Silus Vesuius.{{ref|name=SRPieces|[[SR:Pieces of the Past|Pieces of the Past]] quest in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Later the Dragonborn would receive word of the activities of Vonos' cult and attempt to stop them. After stopping the cult's retrieval of the [[Lore:Great Welkynd Stone|Great Welkynd Stone]] from the Ayleid ruins of [[Lore:Rielle|Rielle]], the Last Dragonborn journeyed to the cult's temple. There, they slew Vonos with the Great Welkynd stone in their belongings, and it was revealed that this was what was needed to open the Gate, as Vonos had offered himself as a sacrifice. As the Last Dragonborn was blessed by Akatosh himself, the same being that sealed the jaws of Oblivion would by extension be the key to opening them again.{{ref|name=Vonos}} 

Though it was only one Oblivion gate, this unwitting sacrifice produced a foothold that could not be closed as the Sigil Stone was disposed of to prevent its closure; even if Dagon was defeated here, he could still rebuild and try again.{{ref|name=Vonos}} Regardless, the Last Dragonborn entered into the Deadlands and defeated Valkyn [[Lore:Dremora Methats|Methats]] and [[Lore:Dremora Gatanas|Gatanas]], who were to serve as heralds for the initial assault on Tamriel. Afterward, the [[Lore:Vigil of Stendarr|Vigil of Stendarr]] arrived and put it upon themselves to guard the portal.{{ref|name=TheCause|[[SR:The Cause|The Cause]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Servants===
[[File:LG-cardart-Dremora Markynaz 03.jpg|Dremora soldiers of Mehrunes Dagon|thumb|left]][[File:ON-skill-Enkindled Flames.jpg|thumb|right|An Ash Titan at a shrine to Dagon in the Deadlands]]
Numerous [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] serve the Mehrunes Dagon, most notably the kinsmen of [[Lore:Clan Dremora|Clan Dremora]]. The [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] are said to resemble their patron "in pride, fixed purpose, and lack of subtlety", but also value honor and loyalty to the Clan and to Dagon. Among the ranks of the Dremora are the Valkynaz (or "princes"), members of the Valkyn, Mehrunes Dagon's personal guard. Below them are the Markynaz (or "grand dukes"), members of the Markyn, his Council of Lords.{{ref|name=VOD|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Daedra}}}} The Clan operates out of the [[Lore:Havoc Wellhead|Havoc Wellhead]] and is led by one of Dagon's top lieutenants, Grand Vizier [[Lore:Imago Storm|Imago Storm]].{{ref|name=BSLevel6|Events of the [[BS:Level 6|Havok Wellhead]] in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

[[Lore:Daedroth|Daedroth]], [[Lore:Clannfear|clannfear]], [[Lore:Watcher|watchers]], [[Lore:Xivilai|Xivilai]], [[Lore:Spider Daedra|Spider Daedra]], and [[Lore:Scamp|scamps]] are a common sight in Dagon's legions.{{ref|Appearance of [[OB:Daedroth|Daedroth]], [[OB:Clannfear|Clannfear]], [[OB:Xivilai|Xivilai]], [[OB:Spider Daedra|Spider Daedra]], [[OB:Scamp|Scamps]] in Mehrunes Dagon's army in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|Appearance of [[ON:Daedroth|Daedroth]], [[ON:Clannfear|Clannfear]], and [[ON:Xivilai|Xivilai]] within the [[ON:City of Ash II#Knives of Discord|Knives of Discord]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|Appearance of [[ON:Scamp|Scamps]] during the Dagonite invasion of [[ON:City of Ash I|Whisper Grove]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} As they do beneath other Princes, scamps function as servants and menials. Magma Scamps are native to the Deadlands.{{ref|[[ON:Magma Scamp|Magma Scamp]] pet description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Ruinach|Ruinachs]] are Mehrunes Dagon's elite marauders. They possess immense physical strength, and are unflinchingly loyal to their Prince. These red-skinned, four-armed Daedra resemble their master.{{ref|name=BPTMR}} The Prince's forces also include [[Lore:Fire Behemoth|Fire Behemoths]], [[Lore:Dremnaken|Dremnaken]], [[Lore:Infernium|Inferniums]], [[Lore:Ogrim|Searing Ogrim]], [[Lore:Scorion|Scorions]], and Dagonic [[Lore:Harvester|Harvesters]].{{ref|[[ON:Fire Behemoth|Fire Behemoths]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Fire Behemoth|Fire Behemoths]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Dremnaken|Dremnaken]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Infernium|Inferniums]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Harvester|Harvesters]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Searing Ogrim|Searing Ogrim]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Scorion|Scorions]] in [[ON:The Dread Cellar|The Dread Cellar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Havocrel|Havocrels]] also serve among Dagon's forces, with some among them bearing titles such as 'Duke' or 'Baron'.{{ref|[[ON:Havocrel|Havocrels]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Havocrel Duke of Fire|Havocrel Duke of Fire]] and [[ON:Havocrel Duke of Storms|Havocrel Duke of Storms]] and [[ON:Baron Zaudrus|Baron Zaudrus]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Dagon's forces include a variety of [[Lore:Elemental Daedra|Atronachs]], including [[Lore:Flame Atronach|Flame Atronachs]], [[Lore:Frost Atronach|Frost Atronachs]] and [[Lore:Storm Atronach|Storm Atronachs]],{{ref|[[OB:Daedra|Daedric enemies]] in [[OB:Planes of Oblivion|The Deadlands]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} as well as [[Lore:Iron Atronach|Iron Atronachs]].{{ref|[[ON:Iron Atronach|Iron Atronachs]] in [[ON:The Deadlands|The Deadlands]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ash [[Lore:Daedric Titan|Titans]] also serve Dagon,{{ref|name=Titan}} giving the Prince an aerial force. Gigantic versions of some of the Daedra serving Dagon such as Harvesters and Ruinachs can also be found within the Deadlands.{{ref|Giant[[ON:Harvester|Harvesters]] and [[Lore:Ruinach|Ruinachs]] in [[ON:Atoll of Immolation|Atoll of Immolation]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Though commonly associated with [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], some [[Lore:Mazken|Mazken]] are also known to serve Dagon.{{ref|name=Battlespire}} Dagon's forces also contain [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lords]] and Daedra Counts, [[Lore:Morphoid Daedra|Morphoid Daedra]], [[Lore:Vermai|Vermai]], and [[Lore:Herne (creature)|Hernes]].{{ref|name=BattlespireCreatures|[[BS:Creatures|Daedric enemies]] in [[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]}} [[Lore:Nightmare Animal|Nightmare Animals]] are flame-imbued Daedric creatures that originate from the Deadlands and are rumored to have been created by Dagon himself.{{Ref|name=PNW|[[ON:Packlord Nightmare Wolf|Packlord Nightmare Wolf]] mount description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Dagon's servants sometimes include non-Daedric creatures that have been transformed by Daedric [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]] such as Dagonic [[Lore:Mantikora|Mantikora]]{{ref|[[ON:Dagonic Mantikora|Dagonic Mantikora]]s in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Brugurikh|{{Cite Book|Brugurikh's Journal}}}} Some of Mehrunes Dagon's followers such as the Sul-Xan tribe of Argonians are known to sometimes use rituals to imbue Nirnian creatures with Dagon's chaos, transforming them into new forms, as was the case with the Sul-Xan Fleshripper [[Lore:Welwa|Welwa]].{{ref|[[ON:Sul-Xan Fleshripper|Sul-Xan Fleshripper]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Giant flame [[Lore:Nixad|Nixads]] can also be found serving among Dagon's servants.{{ref|[[ON:Magma Queen|Magma Queen]] and [[ON:Faram-Xaran|Faram-Xaran]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
