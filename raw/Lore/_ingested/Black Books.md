[[Lore:Black Books|Black Books]] are [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] [[Lore:Artifacts|artifacts]] created by [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]], the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Fate and Knowledge. Each book contains some manner of forbidden knowledge. [[Lore:Neloth|Neloth]] claims that no one actually knows where they came from, though the materials for their creation are prepared within a bindery in Apocrypha known as [[Lore:Quires Wind|Quires Wind]], before being presented to the prince to pen and complete.{{ref|name=QuiresWind|[[Online:Quires Wind|Quires Wind]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Some books are from the ancient past; some are from the future, as time appears to be more malleable for the Daedric Prince of fate and destiny.{{ref|name=DBNeloth}} 

The thick tomes are bound in black covers, with a symbol representing Mora on the front, and emit a black mist.{{Ref|name=DB|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} The books are written with [[Lore:Seeker|Seeker]] ink that takes ten years to brew, on paper pressed from trees that have never seen the sun.{{ref|name=QuiresWind}} The covers are derived from [[Lore:Lurker|Lurker]] leather, and the threads used to bind the tomes together come from silk originating from the [[Lore:Spiral Skein|Spiral Skein]].{{ref|name=BIS|Events of [[Online:Bound in Secrecy|Bound in Secrecy]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} All Black Books were regarded as [[Lore:Tracts Perilous|Tracts Perilous]] due to the hazards they could impose on their readers.{{Ref|name=OTP|{{Cite Book|On Tracts Perilous}}}}

A number of Black Books were spread across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] by Mora, and are usually located in the depths of ancient dungeons.{{Ref|name=DB}} The exact number of Black Books is unknown to everyone except for the Daedric Prince of Knowledge himself. Within every Black Book lies a forbidden secret, serving as a tantalizing temptation or reward for mortals whom Hermaeus Mora, the Great Eye, favors.{{Ref|name=OTP}}<noinclude>

Black Books are not to be confused with the tomes of [[Lore:Arcanists|arcanists]],{{ref|name=ESO2023GlobalReveal|group=UOL|[[General:ESO 2023 Global Reveal|ESO 2023 Global Reveal]]}} though Hermaeus Mora himself compared their two powers, with both being knowledge he supposedly allows into Nirn.{{Ref|name=ETHMR|[[General:ESO Twitter Hermaeus Mora Roleplay|ESO Twitter Hermaeus Mora Roleplay]]}} They can be compared to the [[Lore:Oghma Infinium|Oghma Infinium]], another tome of knowledge associated with Mora.

Black books serve as the doorways to the Mora's realm.{{Ref|name=OTP}}{{Ref|name=LTW|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil the Wise]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Through reading a Black Book, the reader will enter [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]], the realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] created and ruled over by Hermaeus Mora. Tentacles will emerge from the tome and envelop the reader. An apparition of the reader's body remains in [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], tethering their life force. Most mortals who read a Black Book are driven insane, but those who successfully journey through Apocrypha are known to discover powerful knowledge. Dying in Apocrypha while reading a Black Book may simply banish the reader back to Tamriel. Rereading a book will return the reader to the same area of Apocrypha, allowing them to attempt Mora's gauntlets again or harness the power of a different piece of knowledge.{{Ref|name=DB}} Although only one proper copy of each book exists,{{Ref|name=LTW}} the contents of Black Books can be transcribed without entering Apocrypha.{{Ref|name=DBNeloth|[[Skyrim:Neloth|Neloth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, several Black Books were unearthed on the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]].{{Ref|name=DB}}

==Known Black Books==
[[File:SR-item-Black Book 02.jpg|right|thumb|Page of a Black Book]]
===[[Lore:Black Book: Waking Dreams|Waking Dreams of A Starless Sky]]===
This Black Book was located in the [[Lore:Temple of Miraak|Temple of Miraak]]. It was apparently used by [[Lore:Miraak|Miraak]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] to flee to Apocrypha when his temple was burned down by the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]]. It was recovered by the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] and a member of the [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]], and the Dragonborn later used it to defeat Miraak.

===[[Lore:Black Book: Epistolary Acumen|Epistolary Acumen]]===
As of {{Year|2E 582}}, a copy of ''Epistolary Acumen'' was housed in the Library of Dusk within [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] and cataloged among its rare books. It was stored in vault twelve, with access strictly forbidden. It was considered a collection of illicit invocations of hostile Daedric entities.{{Ref|name=TLODRB|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}}

''Epistolary Acumen'' was discovered by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]], and taken to the city of [[Lore:Nchardak|Nchardak]] for study. It was recovered by the Last Dragonborn and [[Lore:Neloth|Master Neloth]] of [[Lore:House Telvanni|Great House Telvanni]].{{Ref|name=BBQuest5|[[Skyrim:Black Book: Epistolary Acumen (quest)|Black Book: Epistolary Acumen (quest)]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}{{Ref|name=BBQuest6|[[Skyrim:The Path of Knowledge|The Path of Knowledge]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===[[Lore:Black Book: Untold Legends|Untold Legends: The Other Lives of Ysgramor]]===
''Untold Legends'', together with ''The Hidden Twilight'', and ''The Winds of Change'' were discovered by a [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] agent in 2E 582. Reports vary as to their exact locations, with some suggesting they were found in Ondil in [[Lore:Auridon|Auridon]], others in [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]] Armature in [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]], and yet others in Silumm in [[Lore:Glenumbra|Glenumbra]].{{Ref|name=ESOQuest|Events of [[ON:Long Lost Lore|Long Lost Lore]] and locations of [[ON:Untold Legends|Untold Legends]], [[ON:The Winds of Change|The Winds of Change]], and [[ON:The Hidden Twilight|The Hidden Twilight]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

As of {{Year|4E 201}} this Black Book was located in Benkongerike, an ice cave inhabited by [[Lore:Riekling|rieklings]] that opened up into an ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] ruin.{{Ref|name=BBQuest4|[[Skyrim:Black Book: Untold Legends (quest)|Black Book: Untold Legends (quest)]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===[[Lore:Black Book: The Winds of Change|The Winds of Change]]===
''The Winds of Change'', together with ''The Hidden Twilight'', and ''Untold Legends'' were discovered by a Mages Guild agent in 2E 582. Reports vary as to their exact locations, with some suggesting they were found in Ondil in Auridon, others in Inner Sea Armature in Stonefalls, and yet others in Silumm in Glenumbra.{{Ref|name=ESOQuest}}

As of 4E 201 this Black Book was located in [[Lore:Bloodskal Barrow|Bloodskal Barrow]], burial place of the Bloodskal Clan. The [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]] [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] mine broke into the ruins in {{Year|4E 10}} and sealed it off.{{Ref|name=BBQuest3|[[Skyrim:Black Book: The Winds of Change (quest)|Black Book: The Winds of Change (quest)]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===[[Lore:Black Book: The Sallow Regent|The Sallow Regent]]===
This Black Book was located in [[Lore:White Ridge Barrow|White Ridge Barrow]], an ancient Nordic ruin.{{Ref|name=BBQuest2|[[Skyrim:Black Book: The Sallow Regent (quest)|Black Book: The Sallow Regent (quest)]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===[[Lore:Black Book: Filament and Filigree|Filament and Filigree]]===
This Black Book was located in [[Lore:Kolbjorn Barrow|Kolbjorn Barrow]], an ancient Nordic ruin. The barrow was buried in ash during the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], until the ruins were excavated in 4E 201.{{Ref|name=BBQuest1|[[Skyrim:Black Book: Filament and Filigree (quest)|Black Book: Filament and Filigree (quest)]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]&#58; [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

===[[Lore:Black Book: The Hidden Twilight|The Hidden Twilight]]===
''The Hidden Twilight'', along with ''The Winds of Change'' and ''Untold Legends'', were discovered by a Mages Guild agent in 2E 582. Reports vary regarding their exact locations, with some suggesting they were found in Ondil, a ruin in Auridon, others in Inner Sea Armature in Stonefalls, and yet others in Silumm in Glenumbra.{{Ref|name=ESOQuest}} According to the Ciphers of the Eye, this Black Book was believed to be lost in a ruin near Auridon at that time.{{Ref|name=OTP}} It was recovered by Master Neloth at some point in the Fourth Era. He hesitantly gave it to the Last Dragonborn in 4E 201.
