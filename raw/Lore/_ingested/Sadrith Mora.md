[[Lore:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] ([[Lore:Dark Elvish|Dark Elvish]] for "Mushroom Forest"){{Ref|name=MWSul|[[Morrowind:Sul-Matuul|Sul-Matuul]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} was one of the four cities on the district of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], for which it served as the district seat of power for the Great [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]]. The city also serves as the home of the Telvanni Council, but only one member lived in the city, that being the councilor in Tel Naga, the central tower in Sadrith Mora. Most ways into the city for common folk are through the [[Lore:Sea Transportation|sea]] or [[Lore:Teleportation|teleportation]], and if anyone wanted to enter the city, they must acquire a certification of hospitality.{{Ref|name=GTSM|{{Cite Book|Guide to Sadrith Mora}}}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:MW-book-Sadrith Mora Region.jpg|thumb|left|Map of Sadrith Mora Region]]
[[File:MW-place-Gateway Inn.jpg|thumb|right|The Gateway Inn]]
Sadrith Mora is built on the largest island, also known as Sadrith Mora,{{Ref|name=Gener|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue S|Generic dialogue]] regarding '''specific place''' in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} in the middle of the [[Lore:Zafirbel Bay|Zafirbel Bay]], between [[Lore:Azura's Coast|Azura's Coast]] and the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]]. It shares the island with a large [[Lore:Daedric Ruins|daedric ruin]] called [[Lore:Anudnabia|Anudnabia]],{{Ref|name=MWAnud|[[Morrowind:Anudnabia|Anudnabia]] location in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and the ancestral tomb of Clan Sadryon,{{Ref|name=MWSadryon|[[Morrowind:Sadryon Ancestral Tomb|Sadryon Ancestral Tomb]] location in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} one of the thirty revered families of Vvardenfell.{{Ref|name=ATOTTRF|{{Cite Book|Ancestral Tombs of the Thirty Revered Families}}}} 

Typically, the island is inaccessible by foot, always either by boat or through some method of teleportation.{{Ref|name=GTSM}} But in the mid-Second Era, there was a waterlogged, mostly unmarked path that ran through Tel Fyr and across the Zafirbel Bay to Sadrith Mora's docks.{{Ref|name=ONMap|[[:File:ON-map-Vvardenfell.jpg|Map of Vvardenfell – The Elder Scrolls Online: Morrowind]]}} The region is marked with tall, arched rocks and riddled with the district's largest species of mushroom.{{Ref|name=VFAF|{{Cite Book|Vvardenfell Flora and Fauna}}}} Outside of the city are operations overseen by Telvanni slavers, such as mines and [[Lore:Muck Sponge|Muck Sponge]] farms.{{Ref|name=ONSadrith|[[Online:Sadrith Mora|Sadrith Mora]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Morrowind (DLC)|Morrowind]]''}}

Tel Naga was the epicenter of Sadrith Mora and the city's largest building, surrounded by the stone-bricked street, tall samples of [[Lore:Milkcap|milkcap]] mushrooms, and other Telvanni towers. The docks were located on the west side of the city. It was divided from the rest of the city by the Gateway Inn, a two-story inn, and the only service in Sadrith Mora accessible to foreigners without hospitality papers. The city's north side was Sadrith Mora's great market, home to the city's greatest artisans and traders, and further north is the Telvanni Council House, a large orb held together by mushroom stalks. The path from the south led to [[Lore:Wolverine Hall|Wolverine Hall]], the Empire's bastion in Telvanni jurisdiction. In the center of town is the seat of power, Tel Naga, the largest tower in the city and residence of the Telvanni councilor in Sadrith Mora.{{Ref|name=GTSM}}

==History==
In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Tel Naga was occupied by the Archmagister of House Telvanni, [[Lore:Nelos Otheri|Nelos Otheri]] and the Gateway Inn was placed on the other side of the city, away from the docks.{{Ref|name=ONSadrith}} Sometime in his tenure, he developed a magical creature with his expert-level [[Lore:Mycoturgy|mycoturgy]] called the Sadrith Mora Spore Steed, based on the [[Lore:Horse|horses]] of the west.{{Ref|name=ONSpore|[[Online:Sadrith Mora Spore Steed|Sadrith Mora Spore Steed]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} It was known for its impressive laboratories and libraries.{{Ref|name=TT|[[ON:Tiras Tirethi|Tiras Tirethi]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, a magically talented [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] slave named [[Lore:Sun-in-Shadow|Sun-in-Shadow]] met with the council of Telvanni mouths and was freed from her bonds, then proceeded to the rank of a hireling in service of Nelos Otheri.{{Ref|name=ONHireling|[[Online:A Hireling of House Telvanni|A Hireling of House Telvanni]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Morrowind (DLC)|Morrowind]]''}} With help from an [[Lore:The Vestige|Outlander]], she attained the role of oathman{{Ref|name=ONObjections|[[Online:Objections and Obstacles|Objections and Obstacles]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Morrowind (DLC)|Morrowind]]''}} and along the way, clashed with Magisters such as [[Lore:Gothren|Gothren]] of [[Lore:Tel Aruhn|Tel Aruhn]]. As time passed, Sun-in-Shadow continued her work to become a magister.{{Ref|name=ONHeart|[[Online:The Heart of a Telvanni|The Heart of a Telvanni]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Morrowind (DLC)|Morrowind]]''}}

By the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Tel Naga was occupied by Councilor [[Lore:Neloth|Neloth]], which in turn made him Master of Sadrith Mora and its surrounding environs.{{Ref|name=BB426|{{Cite Book|Brown Book of 3E 426}}}} In {{Year|3E 427}}, the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] appeared on Vvardenfell to fulfill the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|prophecy]] and in order to do so, needed to be acknowledged as the Hortator by House Telvanni. They traveled to Sadrith Mora to meet with Master Neloth, who nonchalantly gave them his blessing and dismissed them just as quickly.{{Ref|name=MWHortator|[[Morrowind:Telvanni Hortator|Telvanni Hortator]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} After the events that transpired in [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]], the Arch-Mage of Vvardenfell's [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]], [[Lore:Trebonius Artorius|Trebonius Artorius]] gave the order for death of the Telvanni Councilors.{{Ref|name=MWTelvanni|[[Morrowind:Kill the Telvanni Councilors|Kill the Telvanni Councilors]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} And while it is unknown how the other councilors fared, Neloth survived the ordeal and at some point moved northwest into [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]], where he constructed a new tower called [[Lore:Tel Mithryn|Tel Mithryn]].{{Ref|name=SRNeloth|[[Skyrim:Neloth|Neloth]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}}

In that same year, there were rumors that the Gateway Inn was haunted and it displeased the Prefect of Hospitality, Angaredhel. Neloth's mouth, Arara Uvelas tried to kill the [[Lore:Ghost|ghost]] multiple times, but it came back every time. Eventually, the Prefect enlisted the help of a traveler who exposed the perpetrator as Uleni Heleran, a [[Lore:Conjuration|conjuration]] expert of the local Mages Guild, who did it as revenge against Angaredhel when he brought her to the magistrate for entering the city without hospitality papers.{{Ref|name=MWGateway|[[Morrowind:Gateway Ghost|Gateway Ghost]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

When the [[Lore:Ministry of Truth|Ministry of Truth]] crash-landed on top of [[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] in {{Year|4E 5}}, it caused a great chain reaction to [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] and resulted in an eruption that laid waste to Vvardenfell. Sadrith Mora was one of the great casualties in the entire district and many reports were sent out to the province at large. Many people on the mainland mourned the loss of their loved ones on the island. The event has since been remembered as the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], but in time, a [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran]] councilor in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] started a relief effort to rebuild and support the settlements hit the hardest on the island. It is unknown how badly Sadrith Mora was hit in the event.{{Ref|name=TRYII|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-hat-Sadrith Mora Quilted Snood.jpg|thumb|right|The Sadrith Mora Quilted Snood]]
[[File:ON-mount-Sadrith Mora Spore Steed.jpg|thumb|left|The Spore Steed of Sadrith Mora]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Dunmer page.''
As it is customed to House Telvanni, anyone that wants to enter Sadrith Mora proper must first obtain a certification of hospitality from the Prefect of Hospitality in the Gateway Inn, as it was dictated in the Collective Articles of the Council of the Great House Telvanni.{{Ref|name=HP|{{Cite Book|Hospitality Papers}}}}

There is a book called ''[[Lore:The Seed|The Seed]]'' written by the playwright, [[Lore:Gor Felim|Gor Felim]], under the alias of "Marobar Sul". It is an adaptation of an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] tale created by [[Lore:Slave|slaves]] in southern Morrowind. The setting varies in different tellings but usually, it takes place in Vvardenfell, specifically in Sadrith Mora. "Sul" claims that the original story is based on his rendition.{{Ref|name=TS|{{Cite Book|The Seed}}}}

Along the shores of Sadrith Mora and the rest of Zafirbel Bay are very rare seashells called Red Star Shells, which are very valuable in the eyes of House Telvanni. They can be found on the shoals of the region.{{Ref|name=ONLlonas|[[Online:Llonas Givyn|Llonas Givyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Morrowind (DLC)|Morrowind]]''}} Some formal attire is adorned with red star shells, thereby making it incredibly valuable and extremely rare.{{Ref|name=ONFormal|[[Online:Sadrith Mora Formal Attire|Sadrith Mora Formal Attire]] description text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A Sadrith Mora quilted snood is headwear popular among people of high power, meant to reflect their position.{{Ref|name=ONSnood|[[Online:Sadrith Mora Quilted Snood|Sadrith Mora Quilted Snood]] hat description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

One of the only ways to Sadrith Mora was through a boat service. In the late Third Era, Sadrith Mora had connections to [[Lore:Dagon Fel|Dagon Fel]], [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Castle Ebonheart]], [[Lore:Tel Branora|Tel Branora]], and [[Lore:Tel Mora|Tel Mora]].{{Ref|name=MWGals|[[Morrowind:Gals Arethi|Gals Arethi]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} When the Mages Guild was established in Wolverine Hall, the local guild guide used teleportation to send people between other guildhalls in the district.{{Ref|name=MWIniel|[[Morrowind:Iniel|Iniel]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} A [[Lore:Silt Strider|Silt Strider]] service was only available during off-peak hours in [[Lore:Sun's Height|Sun's Height]] and [[Lore:Last Seed|Last Seed]], when the journey is less hazardous. It travels across the Inner Sea to [[Lore:Ald Isra|Ald Isra]] in the southern [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]].{{Ref|name=AFATD1|{{Cite Book|A Feast Among the Dead, Chapter I|ns_base=ON}}}}
