'''Daedroths''' are [[Lore:Crocodile|crocodile]]-headed bipedal [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] typically associated with [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]].{{Ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}}  They are strong, hulking, reptilian Daedra with animalistic tendencies, but still a dangerous foe with powerful clawed arms and moderate magical ability.{{Ref|name=CC|{{Cite Book|Chaotic Creatia: The Azure Plasm}}}} Though some have been known to use weapons and wear simple armor, most attack with bare claws.{{ref|name=DFDae|Appearance of [[DF:Daedroth|Daedroths]] in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}  They have a powerful bite and the ability to spew hot flames.{{ref|name=KCAHC|[[Books:Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion]]: Black Marsh, Daedroth}}{{ref|[[ON:Daedroth|Daedroth]] abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some daedroths can spit poison or shock magic.{{ref|name=MWDae|[[MW:Daedroth|Daedroth]] abilities in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} They stand roughly at the height of a mortal (if not taller),{{ref|name=KCAHC}} and can be summoned in gargantuan sizes.{{ref|name=MawInf|[[ON:Maw of the Infernal|Maw of the Infernal]]'s huge size in the [[ON:Banished Cells II|Banished Cells II]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Particularly large daedroths are difficult to summon, and can only be controlled by those with a strong will.{{ref|name=HighKin|[[ON:High Kinlord Rilis|High Kinlord Rilis]]' dialogue in the [[ON:Banished Cells II|Banished Cells II]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Even [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] have trouble in getting Daedroths to follow their orders.{{Ref|name=ICDremoraDialogue|[[ON:Dremora Dialogue|Overheard Dremora dialogue]] in [[ON:Imperial City|Imperial City]]}} Daedroth [[Lore:Daedroth Sac|sacs]] and [[Lore:Daedroth Teeth|teeth]] are used in alchemy.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Daedroth Teeth|Daedroth Teeth]] alchemical ingredient from ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Linguists will point out that "Daedroth" is also the proper singular form of "Daedra", but for these creatures the common usage is Daedroth (singular) and Daedroths (plural), with Daedra (both singular and plural) being used as the generic term.{{ref|name=OnOb|{{Cite Book|On Oblivion}}}} The term "[[Lore:Lesser Daedra|Lesser Daedra]]" is sometimes used (again, both singular and plural), though this appellation technically refers to Daedra of all types beneath the Daedric Princes.{{ref|name=DD}}

Daedroths can be seen existing in the wilds of Oblivion as wild animals do on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=VoD2|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Daedra}}}} They can also be found serving [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]],{{ref|name=ONGBT|[[Online:Getting the Band Together|Getting the Band Together]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]],{{Ref|name=TGM|[[ON:The Grip of Madness|The Grip of Madness]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]],{{Ref|name=Supernal|[[ON:Supernal Summoner|Supernal Summoner]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} and green variants serving [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]].{{Ref|name=VoD|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Daedra}}}} Daedroths have been observed speaking [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]].{{ref|name=THD|[[DF:The Heartless Daedra|The Heartless Daedra]] quest in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|name=TOC|[[DF:The Obsessed Child|The Obsessed Child]] quest in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

The ''[[Lore:16 Accords of Madness|Sixteen Accords of Madness]]'' are stories of [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s encounters and triumphs over other Daedric Princes. The sixth volume describes a wager made between the Mad God and Huntsman after Sheogorath called to [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] on his summoning day. Intrigued by Sheogorath's bravado, Hircine accepted the Mad God's terms: three years from then, they would have a duel between their champions. Hircine imbued a daedroth with lycanthropy, making it his most deadly hunter. Sheogorath's champion was a bird that was unaware of the situation, and inadvertently caused the monster to commit suicide as it tried to pluck it off its face. With his pride greatly wounded, Hircine burned the corpse of his creation, cursed the peaks in Skyrim where the duel happened, and returned to his realm humiliated.{{ref|name=16AM6|{{Cite Book|16 Accords of Madness, v. VI}}}}

Molag Bal employed two notable daedroths as generals during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]]. The first was the "Daedroth Monarch"{{ref|name=MW|[[MW:Molag Bal's Quest|Molag Bal's Quest]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} [[Lore:Menta Na|Menta Na]], who is known as Molag Bal's "most favored pet".{{ref|name=AnchorM|[[ON:Menta Na|Menta Na]]'s appearance at [[ON:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The other was [[Lore:Hrelvesuu|Hrelvesuu]], who had taken an interest with the "toys of mortals".{{ref|name=AnchorH|[[ON:Hrelvesuu|Hrelvesuu]]'s appearance at [[ON:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} They were ordered to defend their master's Dark Anchors. However, they were vanquished by the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]], and the Anchors they were guarding were destroyed.{{ref|name=AnchorG|[[Online:General Executioner|General Executioner]] achievement in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

==Notes==
*Early Daedroth concept art reveals that they were initially based on the Egyptian god [[wikipedia:Sobek|Sobek]].
*There is no [[Lore:Jel|Jel]] word for "daedroth", as the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] people don't "taint their language with words for abominations from Oblivion." Instead, they use common words from other more widely-spoken languages to refer to the beasts.{{ref|name=KCAHC}}
*[[Lore:Fire Daemon|Fire Daemons]] share several similarities with Daedroths in [[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], including similarly reptilian forms and high intelligence stats.{{ref|[[DF:Daedroth|Daedroth]] appearance and stats in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Given their lack of appearance since Arena due to the use of the name Daemon, it's possible they were the inspiration for the Daedra species.
