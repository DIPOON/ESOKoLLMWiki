[[Lore:The Prophet|The Prophet]], also known as the '''Holy Hermit of Anvil''',{{ref|name=PD|[[OB:Generic Dialogue|Prophet]] topic in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} was a [[Lore:Sight|prophet]] of unknown identity who came to the limelight in {{Year|3E 433}} as he preached a message about the terrible half-Elf [[Lore:Umaril|Umaril the Unfeathered]], returning to the affairs of man. After an attack on the Chapel of [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]] in [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] that saw the altars desecrated and the priests killed, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] came to investigate.{{ref|name=Pilgramage|[[Oblivion:Pilgrimage|Pilgrimage]] quest in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}

The Hero spoke with the Prophet who informed them that Umaril the Unfeathered had returned to take vengeance on the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] whose champion [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]], the Divine Crusader served as Umaril and his [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire's]] undoing. The Prophet said the Divines required a Divine Crusader reborn. Someone who could complete Pelinal's unfinished work of destroying Umaril utterly. To do so, the Prophet sent the Hero of Kvatch on a pilgrimage to find the eight scattered [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Crusader's Relics]], the holy arms and armor of Pelinal, gifted to him by the Divines.{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}<noinclude>
[[File:OBR-npc-The Prophet 02.jpg|The visage of the Prophet (Remastered)|thumb|left]]
After a pilgrimage that led to the Hero meeting Pelinal's ghost face to face, and acquiring all of the Relics, the Prophet arrived to assist further. The Prophet informed the Hero that unlike Pelinal, they could face Umaril with a final Divine blessing, that of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Talos]] who changed the face of Divine upon ascension. This blessing could allow the Hero of Kvatch to follow Umaril into the spirit realm and destroy him once and for all.{{ref|name=OBProphet|}} The Prophet was able to confer this blessing to the Hero, attesting to their connection to the Divine. With this, and parting words of wisdom, the Prophet sent the Hero of Kvatch and their fellow questing knights on their way.{{ref|name=BoT|[[Oblivion:Blessing of Talos|Blessing of Talos]] quest in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} 

The Hero arrived to [[Lore:Garlas Malatar|Garlas Malatar]], defeated Umaril's physical form, used the Blessing of Talos, to follow him into the spirit realm, and destroyed him utterly.{{ref|name=Umaril|[[Oblivion:Umaril the Unfeathered (quest)|Umaril the Unfeathered]] quest [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}

The Prophet was never officially endorsed by the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Church]], with many having believed him to be crazy, but his teachings were also believed by many within the Church or not to hold truth.{{ref|name=PD}}{{ref|name=Areldur|[[OB:Areldur|Areldur]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}} His mysterious entry and departure from the scene around the return and fall of Umaril, along with no known name makes his identity very murky. However, he was familiar with former Archmage of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] [[Lore:Hannibal Traven|Hannibal Traven]].{{ref|name=OBProphet|}} 

The Prophet was versed in a wide array of topics of metaphysical and historical significance. He ruminated on things such as the nature of Pelinal and Umaril, the [[Lore:Enantiomorph|Enantiomorph]] of Tiber Septim and [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]], the [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] nature of [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]], [[Lore:Reman|Reman]], and Tiber Septim, and the Divines themselves. He could even sense the oversoul of Emperors within the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] when it was within his proximity.{{ref|name=OBProphet}} Further, he would cite various heresies said to have been forbidden by the Amulet of Kings.{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}<noinclude>
