The [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] (also known as '''Zeymahzin''' in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragon Language]]){{Ref|name=TV}} are a group of warriors who take on private and public contracts for the people of Skyrim, and who purportedly carry on the tradition of the Five Hundred Companions of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]. Although the two groups offer a similar service, the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] historically had a strong presence in Skyrim{{ref|[[Online:Guildhalls|Guildhalls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} until the Companions seemingly replaced it entirely in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. They are based in the city of [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]], within the mead hall of [[Lore:Jorrvaskr|Jorrvaskr]].{{ref|name=NOS|{{Cite Book|Nords of Skyrim}}}} The most elite members are included in the Circle of Jorrvaskr, and the chief councilor and arbitrator is known as the Harbinger, after Ysgramor, the "harbinger of us all" and the only real recognized leader of the Companions.{{ref|name=SOTR2|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 2}}}}{{ref|name=SOTR56|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 56}}}}{{ref|name=GH|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} Each new Harbinger is chosen by the last.{{ref|name=GH}}<includeonly>

To learn more of the Companions and their history, see the [[Lore:Companions|lore article]].</includeonly><noinclude>

===Five Hundred Companions===
[[File:ON-crown store-Music Box, Hymn of Five-Hundred Axes.jpg|thumb|right|A music box depicting Ysgramor captaining a ship with the Companions]]
The Companions have had an enormous impact and influence throughout Skyrim in the course of recorded history. When Ysgramor and his two sons [[Lore:Yngol|Yngol]] and [[Lore:Ylgar|Ylgar]] returned to [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]] after the [[Lore:Night of Tears|Night of Tears]], the Five Hundred Companions were those who answered their call to arms for war against the [[Lore:Mer|elves]].{{ref|name=SOTR2}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} One of them was [[Lore:Jeek of the River|Jeek of the River]], captain of the ship Jorrvaskr, Ysgramor's childhood companion, and the third Harbinger.{{ref|name=GH}}{{ref|name=SOTR7|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 7}}}} Jeek was also the keeper of the now-forgotten Oath of the Companions.{{ref|name=GH}} Jeek and his crew were Ysgramor's closest friends and the first to break away from the main group and explore the dangerous wilderness following the reconquest of [[Lore:Saarthal|Saarthal]]. They found the [[Lore:Skyforge|Skyforge]], an ancient forge of unknown origin which predated elven habitation of Skyrim. The elves avoided and feared it, as "it was known to drive a magic almost as old as [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] itself, some remnant of the [[Lore:Aedra|gods']] efforts to render a paradise in [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] before the shattering of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]". The Companions thought anything the elves feared must be worth having, and since the land around the area was fertile, Jeek of the River established the Jorrvaskr mead hall and the city of Whiterun.{{ref|name=SOTR7}}{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}}

Aside from the Jorrvaskr, the Companions also arrived in Skyrim aboard other ships, including the ships of Ysgramor's sons&mdash;the [[Lore:Darumzu|Darumzu]] and the [[Lore:Harakk|Harakk]], named after the [[Lore:Stars|stars]] favored by the Atmorans{{ref|name=SOTR2}}&mdash;as well as the Kaal Kaaz, the Krilot Lok, the Fallowfire, the Sadon Reyth, the Ylgermet,{{ref|name=ESOValdur|[[Online:Valdur|Valdur]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SOTR49|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Volume 49}}}}{{ref|name=SOTR19|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 19}}}} and the Chrion.{{Ref|name=SOTR56}} Some crews never reached the final destination&mdash;such was the fate of Yngol and the crew of the Harakk.{{ref|name=SOTR2}}

The Circle of Captains decided that each ship's crew should go forth on its own accord and make its own legend. The crew of the Fallowfire traveled south, to the lands yet unexplored by other crews, and eventually arrived in a region they called [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]]. They fought and eventually defeated the elves that inhabited the region, and the new captain, [[Lore:Vikord One-Ear|Vikord One-Ear]], ordered the construction of a great hall on the site of the final battle.{{ref|name=SOTR49}} The hall was named [[Lore:Fallowstone Hall|Fallowstone Hall]] and served as the headquarters of the Rift Companions for centuries.{{ref|[[Online:Fallowstone Hall|Fallowstone Hall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Every year on the 13th of [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]], the Nords of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] celebrate the [[Lore:Feast of the Dead|Feast of the Dead]] in the city's seat of power, the [[Lore:Palace of the Kings|Palace of the Kings]]. The feast is meant to celebrate Ysgramor's historical attack on Saarthal, the gathering of the Five Hundred Companions, and the elven defeat in Skyrim.{{Ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} Each of the Five Hundred Companions' names are famously recited during the feast.{{Ref|name=PGE1S}} The ideas of equality and unity of fighting together, drinking together, and perhaps most importantly feasting together among the Companions, as well as their prowess as hunters, were reflected in their favoured dishes.{{Ref|name=OfficialCookbook|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook}}}}

===The Harbingers===
Several hundred years later, after the Companions had been established as an independent mercenary group, the Harbinger [[Lore:Mryfwiil the Withdrawn|Mryfwiil the Withdrawn]] feared that the participation in wars and political conflict would destroy the group and decreed that Companions would not be party to wars or political conflicts of any kind.{{ref|name=GH}} Ever since, the Companions have become lauded in Skyrim as impartial arbiters on matters of honor.{{ref|name=GH}}{{ref|name=TCOS|{{Cite Book|The City of Stone}}}}{{ref|name=NOS}} At a time when racial prejudice and talk of maintaining the purity of Atmoran blood threatened to break the peace in Skyrim, the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] [[Lore:Cirroc the Lofty|Cirroc the Lofty]] was made the first Harbinger not of Atmoran descent, likely helping to allay concerns of whether other humans could "be born with the heart of a [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]". Cirroc's reign was brief, although he did install a martial prowess in the blade work of those he taught.{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} Generations later, the Elf [[Lore:Henantier the Outsider|Henantier the Outsider]] was named Harbinger after outlasting all others better suited to the role,{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} thus cementing a tradition of racial tolerance in the Companions (causing a substantial number of Companions at the time to leave). The Companions can also claim at least one woman as a former Harbinger, Macke of the Piercing Eyes.{{ref|name=GH}}

===The Circle===
In the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Kyrnil Long-Nose "gathered the true hearts of the Companions in the wilds" and invaded Jorrvaskr, "killing the usurpers" and becoming Harbinger. He founded the Circle, named after Ysgramor's Circle of Captains. The elite Companions of the Circle served as examples to other members and effectively formed a governing body bent on venerating the old ways of "honor through blood".{{ref|name=GH}}{{ref|name=EGTSkyrim}} Secretly, however, the Circle and subsequent Harbingers had been practicing [[Lore:Lycanthropy|lycanthropy]] pursuant to an agreement with the witches of the [[Lore:Glenmoril Coven|Glenmoril Coven]], who had established a presence in Skyrim.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Blood's Honor|Blood's Honor]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The specifics on how this agreement came about and the parties to it are not known. Though Long-Nose created the Circle and is lauded for "returning honor through blood" to the Companions,{{ref|name=GH}} a Harbinger named [[Lore:Terrfyg|Terrfyg]] is credited with first turning the organization to "the ways of the beast".{{ref|name=KJ|{{Cite Book|Kodlak's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Regardless, the Circle accepted this "gift" from [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]] as payment owed to them by the witches for services rendered, but were deceived into believing it would not be a permanent affliction.{{ref|name=SRKodlak|[[Skyrim:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=GH}}{{ref|name=LLOS|{{Cite Book|Lycanthropic Legends of Skyrim}}}} Around {{Year|4E 201}}, some members of the Circle, led by Harbinger [[Lore:Kodlak Whitemane|Kodlak Whitemane]], would eventually conclude it was not a gift, but a curse with serious spiritual implications, and worked to eliminate it from the Companions. During this time the group was targeted by a group of werewolf hunters called the [[Lore:Silver Hand|Silver Hand]], who succeeded in killing several members of the Circle, including the Harbinger Kodlak Whitemane himself. Seeking revenge for the death of their leader, the Companions wiped out the Silver Hand at their hideout. The Glenmoril Coven's last representatives in Skyrim were also destroyed by a [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|new warrior]] on orders from Kodlak before his death. Using the severed heads of the witches, the remaining members of the Circle were able to cleanse Kodlak's soul of lycanthropy even in the afterlife, allowing him to pass on to [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]]. The majority of the Circle also cured their own lycanthropy, although at least [[Lore:Aela the Huntress|one member]] is known to have openly remained a werewolf. Whether the new Harbinger opted to cure or keep their lycanthropy is not recorded.
