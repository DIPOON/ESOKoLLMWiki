# Lore:Moon Sugar

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Trail|Alchemy}}{{Trail|Produce}}[[File:ON-prerelease-Life in Elsweyr.jpg|thumb|right|A moon sugar plantation in Elsweyr]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Moon Sugar|Moon Sugar]] is a grainy powder of small white crystals refined from cane grasses of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]'s coasts and estuaries. It is similar in appearance to salt crystals albeit with a silver sheen. However, when light hits the substance it will appear to glow from within.{{ref|name=MSAR|{{Cite Book|Moon-Sugar: A Report}}}} Used as a spice in Elsweyr, it has modest magical properties, and is also a potent narcotic—illegal in [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] and the rest of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=MWdial|''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' [[Morrowind:Apothecary|apothecary]] dialogue}} It is commonly refined into a more potent and addictive form known as [[Lore:Skooma|Skooma]]. Humans, it seems, are even more susceptible to the effects of moon-sugar than the [[Lore:Khajiit|catmen]] themselves, and travelers to Elsweyr are thus cautioned against partaking of any of the sugar-loaded native food.{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Elsweyr}}}}<noinclude>

==Uses and Effects==
[[File:MW-item-Moon Sugar and Skooma.jpg|thumb|right|Moon sugar and skooma]]
Moon sugar has a variety of uses; it is alternately a seasoning and a magical ingredient, a source of communion with the holy moons and a dangerous and addictive drug.{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} It has analgesic and soporific effects; it makes users "happy and stupid."{{ref|name=SI|{{Cite Book|Smuggler's Island}}}}{{ref|name=ONZaban|[[Online:Zaban-ma#Dialogue|Zaban-ma]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the case of spoiled moon sugar, it is technically edible, though it will cause illness when consumed.{{ref|name=ONMizzik|[[Online:Mizzik Thunderboots|Mizzik Thunderboots]]' dialogue in [[Online:Elsweyr|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONKahiri|[[Online:Guard Captain Kahiri|Guard Captain Kahiri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Elsweyr|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Man|human]]s and [[Lore:Mer|Mer]], the effects of its consumption are exacerbated, causing a relatively brief euphoric state followed by complete exhaustion for several days.{{ref|name=BMMSM|[[Bloodmoon:The Moon Sugar Mystery|The Moon Sugar Mystery]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOBhakargo|[[Online:Bhakargo|Bhakargo]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} Khajiit are relatively resistant to these effects because they consume it in some form every day, but over consumption can cause the same euphoric state and even addiction.{{ref|name=ONLiane|[[Online:Liane#Dialogue|Liane]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}}{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}

Khajiit take advantage of these properties to tame and train [[Lore:Senche-Tiger|Senche-Tiger]]s, which have a strong sweet tooth that waxes and wanes with the moons.{{ref|name=Sugarbelly|{{Cite Book|Sugarbelly}}}} An elixir of moon sugar can be used to "allow the light of the moons into your soul" and embark on spiritual journeys.{{ref|name=ONFirstStep|[[Online:The First Step|Events]] of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Much like Senche-Tigers, Khajiit commonly have a strong sweet tooth, and in Elsweyr moon sugar is used as a spice in almost every dish.{{ref|name=ONMFGF|{{Cite Book|Moon-Sugar for Glossy Fur? Yes!}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} This makes it almost dangerous for the unwarned guest, as it may cause tooth- and stomach-aches.{{ref|name=FTNOACC|{{Cite Book|From the notes of Ancemion, Ceremony Coordinator}}}}

==Production==
Moon Sugar cane is native to the [[Lore:Tenmar Forest|Tenmar Forest]] in southern Elsweyr, but production occurs in plantations outside of that region, including the isle of [[Lore:Khenarthi's Roost|Khenarthi's Roost]] and [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]], where it requires extensive irrigation with captured rainwater.{{ref|name=ONMSITM|{{Cite Book|Moon-Sugar in the March}}}} Symmetrical sugarcane gardens surround the palaces of the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]] in [[Lore:Torval|Torval]].{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} Criminals have attempted to grow moon sugar grasses in Vvardenfell, without success.{{ref|name=MWdial}} Only one Khajiit was successfully growing moon sugar in {{Lore Link|Zainsipilu}} on the island.{{ref|name=ONFalura|[[Online:Falura Uveleth#Dialogue|Falura Uveleth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} (His claim that he fertilizes his farm with fish heads and guar dung is probably just a bluff and not his real method.) To halt the production of it, the [[Lore:Ordinators|Ordinators]] of Vivec's Hall of Justice ordered adventurers to plant false information. The misinformation said that moon sugar can be grown by combining [[Lore:Jarrin Root|Jarrin Root]] (especially dark purple samples), [[Lore:Nirnroot|Nirnroot]], [[Lore:Nightshade|Nightshade]], and lava ash, mixed in equal amounts with water and crushed into pebble sized chunks, and spreading the resulting fertilizer over the moon sugar grass.{{ref|name=PMSFT|{{Cite Book|Proven Moon-Sugar Fertilization Techniques}}}}

==Trade==
Moon sugar is illegal throughout the Third Empire but often smuggled.{{ref|name=SI}}{{ref|name=AA3|{{Cite Book|The Argonian Account, Book 3}}}} In the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], smuggling of moon sugar was prominent in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'' [[Morrowind:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] dialogue}} [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] occasionally used Khajiit slaves to transport moon sugar secretly.{{ref|name=MWRIB|[[Morrowind:Rabinna's Inner Beauty|Events]] of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Some Khajiit have also used the moon sugar trade to their own advantage, growing rich from the drugs run through the main port of [[Lore:Senchal|Senchal]].{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} In the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], moon sugar was one of several esoteric treasure goods exported from the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], namely the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] city-state.{{Ref|name=PGE1Cyrodiil|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}

==Khajiit Beliefs==
[[File:ON-concept-Azurah's Gate.jpg|thumb|right|Depiction of Azurah, who is credited with introducing Moon Sugar to the Khajiit]]
The moon-sugar of Elsweyr is the holiest of substances to the Khajiit. They speak of sugar as others speak of the soul or the lifeforce. The Khajiit understand it to be "crystallized moonlight," caught in the water of the Topal Sea and brought to the sugarcane groves of the Tenmar by the force of the twin tides.{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} Khajiiti dogma reveres the moons as divine, furnishing life into the bodies of the Cat-Men by ingestion of moon-sugar, a sacred ingredient.{{ref|name=EGTE|{{Cite Book|EGT|Elsweyr}}}} Thus, by partaking of the sugar, the Khajiit believe they are consuming small portions of their gods' eternal souls. This drives them into fits of ecstasy and abandon, and the streets of Elsweyr's major cities are full of catmen shivering in the grip of sugar fits.{{ref|name=PGE1Elsweyr}} The [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], along with his [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]], the [[Lore:Aureal|Aureal]], are associated with corrupting the benevolence of the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]'s sacred Moon Sugar. The Aureal tempt mortals to over-consume the substance.{{ref|name=Pacrooti|[[Online:Pacrooti|Pacrooti's hireling correspondence]]}} Sheogorath may appear when a user consumes Skooma through the protection of the [[Lore:Lunar Lattice|Lunar Lattice]].{{ref|name=ONHaduras|[[ON:Priest Haduras|Priest Haduras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Elsweyr|ESO]]}}

In the Khajiit creation story, [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] was given three secrets by her mother [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]]. After speaking the first two secrets and creating the Khajiit and binding them to the Lunar Lattice, she spoke the third secret, making the light shine down upon the marshes where they became the crystallized moonlight known as Moon-Sugar.{{ref|name=WOCMA|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}}
