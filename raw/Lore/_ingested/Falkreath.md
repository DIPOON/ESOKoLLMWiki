[[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]] (also called '''Falcrenth'''{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Falcrenth|Falcrenth]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} or '''Falcreath'''){{Ref|name=KEVII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VII}}}} is one of the nine major cities in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]],{{Ref|name=SRMap|[[:File:MER-art-Skyrim Anthology Map.jpg|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim'']]}}{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Skyrim towns.png|Map of Skyrim – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}} serving as the capital of [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath Hold]] and the boreal forest. Its name derives from [[Lore:Aldmeris (language)|Elvish]], though its meaning is unknown.{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|573}} The city is known as the "heroes' graveyard"; many great battles took place here for centuries, and countless warriors were buried here, leaving behind graves and monuments to them.{{Ref|name=SRNenya|[[Skyrim:Nenya|Nenya]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Because of the nature of the city, the merchants of Falkreath are known to reflect the melancholic themes of [[Lore:Death#Nord Beliefs and Customs|death]] in their business.{{Ref|name=SRLoad|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#City-Specific|Falkreath]] loading screen in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

==Layout and Geography==
Falkreath lies in the southern forests of Skyrim, on a strategic crossroad in the middle of its respective hold and the provincial border towards [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]'s wilder country, the [[Lore:Dragon's Teeth Mountains|Dragon's Teeth Mountains]].{{Ref|name=KEVII}} It is built in the forest basin between the [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jerall Mountains]] and the highlands that contain the {{Lore Link|Shriekwind Bastion}}, where a small lake forms at the base, suited for a mill in this lumber-town. It is a city that is heavily reliant to the lumber industry, due to the abundance of resources around them.{{Ref|name=SRLoad}} In the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Falkreath was much larger in scope. The jarl's longhouse once overlooked the town from the hills in the south, while the original Hall of the Dead was kept underneath the mountains. Nowadays, the current hall was a single home for the priest, as they tended to the expansive graveyard in the city's northern section. The jarl's longhouse is stationed in the dead center of the city, on the main road that runs through Falkreath. The land outside of the city is largely unpopulated, but beyond the western gate, the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] sanctuary was found just off the road.{{Ref|name=SRFalkreath|[[Skyrim:Falkreath|Falkreath]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONHold|[[Online:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath Hold]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Horns of the Reach|Horns of the Reach]]''}} The [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s of Falkreath are known to master the ability to tame [[Lore:Sabre Cat|Sabre Cat]]s, who are considered much more feral and wild than the [[Lore:Senche-Cat|Senche-Cat]].{{Ref|name=ESOCM|[[Lore:Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcase/Mounts|Elder Scrolls Online - Crown Store Showcases (Mounts)]]}} Falkreath is neighbored by several settlements, including [[Lore:Neugrad Watch|Neugrad Watch]] to the northeast, [[Lore:North Keep|North Keep]] to the far north, and Hammerfell to the west towards [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]].{{Ref|name=ARMap}}{{Ref|name=ARMapHF|[[:File:AR-map-Hammerfell towns.png|Map of Hammerfell – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}

==History==
[[File:SR-npc-Knight-Paladin Gelebor.jpg|thumb|right|Falkreath's name originates from the elves]]
===Proto-History and the Men-of-'Kreath===
The city loans its name from the [[Lore:Elvish|Elvish language]], but whatever connection the [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] had to the area in ancient times is unknown.{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|573}} The earliest allusions to Falkreath come from two poetic works of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]: ''[[Lore:The Adabal-a|The Adabal-a]]'', attributed to [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], and ''[[Lore:The Song of Pelinal|The Song of Pelinal]]'', written by various unknown authors. The former work mentions a tribe called the "men-of-'kreath", who were "imported from the North" as slaves to the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]]. According to the latter work, [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] achieved the liberation and empowerment of the men-of-'kreath after orchestrating a massacre of elves in the "northern holdings" (presumably southern Skyrim and northern Cyrodiil). As they are not described as being Nords, the men-of-'kreath seem to have been a distinct group of [[Lore:Man|humans]] that were indigenous to the region.

===Foundation and Early Nordic History===
[[File:ON-npc-Deathlord Bjarfrud Skjoralmor.jpg|thumb|left|Deathlord Bjarfrud Skjoralmor]]
Falkreath was founded by ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] warrior [[Lore:Bjarfrud Skjoralmor|Bjarfrud Skjoralmor]], who drove out "beastly" woodland inhabitants and cut down the great forest to build the settlement.{{Ref|name=EoBS|{{Cite Book|Epitaph of Bjarfrud Skjoralmor}}}} Many battles occurred near Falkreath for countless centuries, and many graves and monuments were built to commemorate the fallen. The town essentially grew around its cemeteries.{{Ref|name=SRNenya}} 

According to the ''[[Lore:Poetic Edda|Poetic Edda]]'', Falkreath was one of the places that submitted to [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|Olaf One-Eye]] as {{Lore Link|High King of Skyrim}}.{{Ref|name=KOV|{{Cite Book|King Olaf's Verse}}}} A statue once stood commemorating the spot where [[Lore:Kjoric the White|Kjoric the White]] fell in battle defending Falkreath from the First Empire; local legend has it that his son, [[Lore:Hoag Merkiller|Hoag Merkiller]], fell in exactly the same spot when he retook Falkreath years later,{{ref|name=PRIMA|[[Books:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide#Legendary Edition|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Legendary Prima Official Game Guide]]}}{{rp|901}} however, he was known to have actually fallen in the [[Lore:Battle of Glenumbra Moors|Battle of Glenumbra Moors]].

Later in the First Era, the [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] king [[Lore:Kestic|Kestic]] may have held sway over the Falkreath area, as evidenced by his epithet, "the Forest King". He was the last to hold this title, as the Nedic civilization of the [[Lore:Hammerfell|Deathlands]] fell with the arrival of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] in {{Year|1E 808}}.{{Ref|name=NotD|{{Cite Book|Nedes of the Deathlands}}}}{{Ref|name=SD|{{Cite Book|Saradin's Diary}}}} 

Falkreath's history is intertwined with that of [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]], as control of Falkreath often shifted between the Colovian estates and the holds of Skyrim.
The earliest account of this was during an interregnum between the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First]] and [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]s when the Colovian nations had split apart from each other and their leaders became thief-barons and petty-kings. That was until the arrival of [[Lore:Reman I|Reman I]] and the formation of his Empire, although Falkreath's role during this time is unknown.{{Ref|name=R|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}}

=== Falkreath in the Second Era ===
[[File:ON-place-Hakkvild's High Hall.jpg|thumb|right|Hakkvild's High Hall]]
With the death of High King [[Lore:Logrolf|Logrolf]] in {{Year|2E 431}}, Skyrim split into eastern and western parts. The Western Kingdom, which included Falkreath, followed High King [[Lore:Svartr|Svartr]], who ruled from [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]].{{Ref|name=TCoF|{{Cite Book|The Crown of Freydis}}}}{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} The Skjoralmor Clan (descended from Bjarfrud Skjoralmor) ruled as jarls of Falkreath during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], and were aligned with the Western Kingdom. 

When the Imperial Province of [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] was sacked in 2E 431, it displaced many [[Lore:Orsimer|Orsimer]], who in turn fled to Skyrim and formed a large chiefdom under [[Lore:Yashnag gro-Yazgu|Yashnag gro-Yazgu]]. The chiefdom was considered the height of Orcish power in Skyrim and it was stationed in western Falkreath Hold, often harassing the hold capital. High King Svartr was unable to stop the threat it imposed, which ultimately led to the death of the Jarl of Falkreath. His son [[Lore:Hakkvild Yashnag-Slayer|Hakkvild]] took his place and challenged Yashnag in a ritual of combat. In the end, Hakkvild had slain the Orcish chief and garnered the title "Yashnag-Slayer". His victory led to the dissolution of the chiefdom and their return to the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] in {{Year|2E 467}}.{{Ref|name=OoS|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Skyrim}}}}

During [[Lore:Kurog|Kurog gro-Bagrakh]]'s consolidation of power, he planned to reestablish the Orcish kingdom in Falkreath and create an Orcish city to rival [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]]. During this period, Kurog's forces were accumulating in the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach Mountains]], ready to take Falkreath, however, the Nords held them back. Kurog gave this plan up after becoming allies with King Emeric.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Chronicles of King Kurog, Book IV}}}}

====Icereach Witches and Imperial Intervention====
Circa {{Year|2E 577}}, after [[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|Varen's Rebellion]], Reachfolk were driven out of Cyrodiil by Imperial forces. The Icereach Coven, as former advisors and teachers of the overthrown [[Lore:Leovic|Emperor Leovic]], were among them.{{ref|name=SHOLE|{{Cite Book|Secret History of the Longhouse Emperors}}}} They were led by a powerful witch known as Mother {{Lore Link|Ciannait}}, and during their escape from Cyrodiil they wound up in the area west of Falkreath. They had taken refuge among the graves west of the town and raised numerous dead Nords from the graveyard to defend themselves from Imperial forces. Ultimately the Imperials ceased to continue their pursuit after suffering great losses and left the matter of the Reachfolk incursion to the local Nords.{{ref|name=MTCMC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Mother Ciannait}}}} The Nords eventually managed to cast the Reach witches out, driving them back to their ancient base on the island of [[Lore:Icereach|Icereach]].{{ref|name=Arvnir|[[ON:Arvnir|Arvnir]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

====Dreadhorn Clan's Siege====
[[File:ON-render-Eerika Skjoralmor.jpg|thumb|left|Eerika Skjoralmor]][[File:ON-creature-Domihaus the Bloody-Horned.jpg|thumb|right|Domihaus the Bloody-Horned]]
Under the command of [[Lore:Domihaus|Domihaus the Bloody-Horned]], the Dreadhorn Clan planned to wrest control of their ancestral lands in Falkreath Hold from the Nords,{{ref|name=MtC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Domihaus the Bloody-Horned}}}} greatly emboldened by their armaments from the [[Lore:Bloodroot Forge|Bloodroot Forge]].

In the years prior to the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]] in {{Year|2E 582}}, Falkreath was ruled by Jarl [[Lore:Hjurgol Skjoralmor|Hjurgol Skjoralmor]], who did not act justly in the days leading to the sacking of Falkreath.{{Ref|name=IRtCR|{{Cite Book|In Reply to Concerning Rumors|ns_base=ON}}}} The Jarl took to hiding in his longhouse, while his daughter and thane, [[Lore:Eerika Skjoralmor|Eerika Skjoralmor]], led the remnants of the resistance.{{Ref|name=BoF|{{Cite Book|Beware of Falkreath}}}} She held the town for some time until the intervention of the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], who delved through the battlefield and aided the Thane in their efforts to save the city. The group was able to defeat Domihaus at the Jarl's Keep and witness the coronation of Jarl Eerika.{{Ref|name=ESOFalkreath|[[Online:Falkreath's Demise|Falkreath's Demise]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Horns of the Reach|Horns of the Reach]]''}}

===Falkreath in the Age of the Septims===
In the latter years of the Interregnum, Falkreath was once again part of the [[Lore:Colovian Estates|Colovian Estates]], who were also split into petty-kings and warlords, while the divided kingdoms of Skyrim had dissolved. King [[Lore:Cuhlecain|Cuhlecain]] of Falkreath sought to unify the estates but in order to move forward, he needed to secure his northern border, towards [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]]. His prized general, [[Lore:Hjalti Early-Beard|Hjalti Early-Beard]], led his forces at the Battle of [[Lore:Old Hroldan|Old Hroldan]], but as the siege proved that breaking the walls was impossible, the next morning he used his [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]] to obliterate it and win the battle. With this newfound power, King Cuhlecain unified the west and conquered the east, preparing to proclaim himself Emperor. But he was later assassinated and Hjalti, now known as [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]], was named the Emperor. What followed was the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]].{{Ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the city-state of Falkreath was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Bjeld, and it had a rivalry with [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]].{{Ref|name=AR}} When the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] broke out in {{Year|3E 433}}, Skyrim was under siege and faced severe casualties, where it was said everything between Falkreath and [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] was laid waste by the [[Lore:Daedra|daedric hordes]].{{Ref|name=OBGate|[[Oblivion:Generic Dialogue#Tamriel Gate Responses|Tamriel Gate Response]] rumors in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-npc-Sinding 02.jpg|thumb|right|Sinding escapes from Falkreath's jail]]
[[Lore:Dengeir of Stuhn|Dengeir of Stuhn]] served as the Jarl of Falkreath in the years that preceded the [[Lore:Skyrim Civil War|Skyrim Civil War]]. He was encouraged by many people to step down as the Jarl when he supported the rebellious [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]], as opposed to the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]].{{Ref|name=SRDengeir|[[Skyrim:Dengeir of Stuhn|Dengeir of Stuhn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The position of leader of Falkreath was then given to his nephew, [[Lore:Siddgeir|Siddgeir]], who was known to be arrogant yet loyal to the Empire, although it was his steward {{Lore Link|Nenya}} that fulfilled the role as leader much more than him.{{Ref|name=SRNenya}} Legate {{Lore Link|Skulnar}} commanded the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] throughout Falkreath Hold, and the stronghold of [[Lore:Fort Neugrad|Fort Neugrad]] was a vital position for Falkreath's allegiance in the civil war.

==Notes==
*The Nords of Falkreath practice an old Nordic custom with their [[Lore:Death#Nord Beliefs and Customs|dead]] called ''grave curd,'' in which a fresh piece of {{Lore Link|cheese}} is placed on top of a loved one's coffin, and every year on the anniversary, a fifth of the cheese is consumed by the family.{{Ref|name=COSRF|{{Cite Book|Cheeses of Skyrim: Riften, Falkreath}}}}
*''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Falkreath's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Dark Shadows".{{Ref|group=UOL|[http://www.imperial-library.info/content/go-blades Original TES: Arena texts at the Imperial Library]}} ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]'' brought back many of these arena teams to compete in an event known as the Dragon Games, but renamed Falkreath's arena team into the "Falkreath Fighters".{{Ref|[[Castles:Gladiatorial Glory|Gladiatorial Glory]] event in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}
