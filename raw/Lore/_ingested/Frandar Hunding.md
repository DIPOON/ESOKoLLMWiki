[[Lore:Frandar Hunding|Frandar do Hunding Hel Ansei No Shira]], commonly known as '''Frandar Hunding''', or simply '''Hunding''',{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} was a [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n warrior-prince and is considered to be the most accomplished [[Lore:Sword-singer|Sword-singer]] and master [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] in history. Born in {{Year|1E 720}}{{intnote|nb2|[nb 2]}} in the province of {{Lore Link|High Desert}} on Yokuda in the marches of [[Lore:Hunding (region)|Hunding]]; Frandar is best remembered for being one of the first Yokudans to settle in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], giving birth to the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] people in the centuries that followed. Because of this, he is sometimes known as the "first Redguard".{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} He is also revered for developing the "Way of the Sword", a martial philosophy on blade mastery.{{ref|name=DTS|{{Cite Book|Divad the Singer}}}}{{ref|name=RTHATH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}}

Frandar is the only "given" part of his name: "Hunding" is the name of the region of his birth; "No Shira" means person of noble birth and "Hel Ansei" is his title of Sword Sainthood in the [[Lore:Yoku|language of Yokuda]].{{ref|name=RTHATH}} He is venerated as Ansei Frandar Hunding{{Ref|name=ToT|[[ON:Ansei Frandar Hunding|Ansei Frandar Hunding]] name in [[ON:Tales of Tribute|Tales of Tribute]]}} and "sacred Hunding".{{ref|name=RGTalisman}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Background===
[[File:LO-map-Hammerfell (Oblivion Codex).jpg|thumb|left|Hammerfell]]
Frandar's ancestors reach back to the beginning of recorded time in the High Desert province, living as artisans and mystics. Much his family lived through constant civil wars between various provincial lords, warrior monks, and brigands all fighting each other for land and power. Frandar's grandfather led many of the battles for the unification of Yokuda as a retainer of [[Lore:Mansel Sesnit|Mansel Sesnit]], the Elden Yokeda.{{ref|name=RTHATH}}{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}}

Prior to Frandar's birth, Sesnit was successful in unifying much of the empire. Frandar's early life came under the rule of Sesnit's successor, Emperor [[Lore:Randic Torn|Randic Torn]], and was relatively peaceful. When Frandar was 14 years old his father died, leaving him to support his mother and four brothers. Given his prowess with the sword at such a young age, this was not difficult and his services came in great demand as a guardian and escort. When Frandar was 17, Randic Torn died and plunged the empire once more into a vicious civil war. At the peak of the strife, Frandar had become a sword-singer and master Ansei. He pursued the ideal of searching for enlightenment through the path of the Sword, so duels and tests of skill were commonplace. As his fame as a swordsman grew, so did the number of people wanting to challenge him to gain their own fame.{{ref|name=RTHATH}}

By the time he was 30 years old, he had fought and won more than 90 duels, killing all his opponents. Frandar has gained such skill and mastery that he stopped using real swords and began using the [[Lore:Shehai|Shehai]] - an unstoppable sword formed out of his own spirit. With his Shehai, he traveled the land killing bands of brigands and wandering monsters. He also enlisted in the many battles and insurrections of the period.{{ref|name=RTHATH}} Around this time he also sired his only son, [[Lore:Divad Hunding|Divad]], but was absent throughout much of his life.{{ref|name=DTS}}{{ref|name=RTHATH}}

===War of the Singers===
[[File:ON-quest-Trials of the Hero.jpg|thumb|left|A recreated vision of Divad scolding his father for being a coward in the cave of Mt. Hattu]]
Upon defeating Lord Janic in his ninetieth duel, Frandar finally believed himself to be invincible and retreated to a cave on [[Lore:Hattu Mountain|Hattu Mountain]] to live the life of a hermit while formulating his philosophy of the "Way of the Sword". These writings eventually became the [[Lore:Frandar Hunding#The Book of Circles|Book of Circles]], which he wrote over the period of 30 years.{{ref|name=RTHATH}}{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell}}

In {{Year|1E 780}} his life of peace was interrupted by a group of sword-singers, including his son, who came seeking his leadership in what would become a war against the Emperor Hira.{{ref|name=DTS}} Frandar was originally against any conflict with the emperor, despite the death of his wife at his hands. He wanted to negotiate with Hira in order to save the countless lives that would no doubt be lost in a war, a stance that Divad strongly disapproved of. Frandar struggled with the burden of leadership and the choice of either fighting against the emperor which would result in exile or submitting to him.{{ref|name=ONFH|[[Online:Frandar Hunding|Frandar Hunding]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Scribes of the time reported bright flashes of light and angry voices from within the cave as Frandar and Divad argued. After five hours both emerged from the cave and Frandar reluctantly agreed to lead the rebellion against Hira in what became known as the [[Lore:War of the Singers|War of the Singers]].{{ref|name=DTS}}

Hunding devised a strategy he called the "Hammer and Anvil", and eventually won a decisive victory against the emperor at the foot of Hattu Mountain. Less than twenty thousand singers survived, but over three hundred thousand of Hira's army were killed.{{ref|name=RTHATH}} Despite their victory, Frandar mourned the dead and was still conflicted if he had made the right choice.{{ref|name=ONFH}} After the battle, Hunding and the remaining singers realized they were no longer welcome in the land, and set sail from the port city of Arch. The first of the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]], or "Warrior Wave", they eventually arrived in the new land they later named [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=RTHATH}}

===Goblin War===
[[File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei's Victory 02.png|Depiction of Frandar Hunding standing victorious over many Goblins|thumb|right]]
Upon arriving in Hammerfell, Frandar and his army discovered vast numbers of giant [[Lore:Goblin|goblin]] strongholds littering the sands. Frandar refused to co-exist with the [[Lore:Goblin#Flint-Tooth Tribe|Flint-Tooth]] tribe on their new homeland, and rallied his army to slay the beasts.{{ref|name=ONTKF}}

The giant goblins were led by their god [[Lore:Malooc|Malooc]], and were sometimes known as Malooc's Horde,{{Ref|name=T26OFSIU|{{Cite Book|The 26th of First Seed is Upon Us!|ns_base=ON}}}}  but Malooc fled east when the army of the [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]] overtook his goblin hordes.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Despite his initial success,{{Ref|name=DFHoliday|[[Daggerfall:Holidays#Festival of Blades|Festival of Blades]] holiday description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Frandar was killed during one of the bloodiest battles of the campaign, and Divad rose to take command of the Ra Gada and avenge his father.{{ref|name=ONTKF|[[Online:Throne Keeper Farvad|Throne Keeper Farvad]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After his death, Frandar was laid to rest in the sacred [[Lore:Hall of Heroes|Hall of Heroes]], where he would eventually be joined by his son.{{ref|name=ONTrial|[[Online:Trials and Tribulations|Trials and Tribulations]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Legacy===
In the centuries that followed after his death, Frandar Hunding became one of the most revered figures in Redguard history. Frandar's people honored him so much that his influence is apparent generations later. The city of [[Lore:Port Hunding|Port Hunding]] on [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] was named in his honor and is home to [[Lore:Statues/Notable_Figures#Frandar Hunding|a vast statue of him]] looking out towards [[Lore:Hunding Bay|Hunding Bay]]. Many believe that the goblins of Stros M'Kai were banished beneath the earth thanks to him.{{ref|name=RGTrithik|[[Redguard:Trithik|Trithik]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|name=RGTalisman|[[Redguard:Talisman|Talisman of Hunding]] description in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} Some Redguards who lived there would warn [[Lore:Goblin-ken|goblin-ken]] such as [[Lore:Gremlin|gremlins]] to return to their caves, lest Hunding turn them into stone.{{ref|name=RGTrithik|[[Redguard:Trithik|Trithik]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|name=RGMariah|[[Redguard:Mariah|Mariah]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} Throughout the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]] are numerous shrines dedicated to Frandar.{{ref|name=STFH|{{Cite Book|Shrine to Frandar Hunding}}}} His lineage endured at least until the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], as evidenced by living descendants such as [[Lore:Bahtra at-Hunding|Bahtra at-Hunding]].{{Ref|name=MTCBAH|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Bahtra at-Hunding}}}} However his most lasting legacy was without question his tome of martial knowledge.

====The Book of Circles====
[[Lore:The Book of Circles|The Book of Circles]] was written by Hunding in order to pass on his life long insights and knowledge. For [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s, it is an enormous part of their culture and everyday life.{{ref|name=RTHATH}}{{ref|name=FTMSOML|{{Cite Book|From The Memory Stone of Makela Leki}}}}{{ref|name=DTROHSAI|{{Cite Book|Daily training regimen of Harayya, sword-adept instructor}}}} Reading from it was abandoned some time in the middle of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] until [[Lore:Derik Hallin|Derik Hallin]], the only [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] at the time, brought back the "old ways" and instructed every warrior to read the book.{{ref|name=NFRH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}}{{ref|name=TTTTOH|{{Cite Book|The True-Told Tale of Hallin}}}} The people of the Alik'r celebrate "Dirij Tereur" every year on the 5th of Frost Fall, a holiday honoring Frandar Hunding, and festivities typically include readings from the book.{{ref|name=Daggerfall|Holidays in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Each household in Hammerfell has an alcove by its hearth just big enough to hold a copy of the Book of Circles in honor of Hunding.{{ref|name=RTHATH}}{{ref|name=FTMSOML}} The book is still highly regarded as a treatise on blade mastery and even other experts at the skill refer to it. It is said to include the principles one must master in various seasons of life, and "thirty-eight grips, seven hundred and fifty offensive and eighteen hundred defensive positions, and nearly nine thousand moves essential to sword mastery".{{ref|name=MAD|{{Cite Book|Mastery of Discipline}}}}{{ref|name=WAP|{{Cite Book|Words and Philosophy}}}} During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] forces stole four of the original scrolls entombed within Frandar's crypt in the Hall of Heroes. The [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] recovered the scrolls at Frandar's request and received his blessing to enter the Chamber of Passage and deal with the invaders.{{ref|name=ONFH}}
