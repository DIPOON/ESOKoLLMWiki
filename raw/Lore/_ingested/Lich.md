# Lore:Lich

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>[[Category:Lore-Creatures]] [[Category:Lore-All Terms]]{{Lore Bestiary Trail}}[[File:LG-cardart-Miscarcand Lich.png|right|thumb|An [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] lich (Legends)]]{{TOCright}}</noinclude>

[[Lore:Lich|Liches]] are undead [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancers]] who have embraced the power of lichdom, the most sophisticated form of Undeath. It is commonly done by transferring their [[Lore:Soul|soul]] into an object called a 'Phylactery'. They are typically selfish and power-hungry, destroying all in their search for souls to repair the Phylactery. Extremely intelligent and powerful, a commonly held myth is that if the Phylactery is destroyed the lich will also be destroyed; however, the Phylactery simply serves as a vessel during the transference, and once the necromancer has transcended the Phylactery is of no consequence.{{ref|name=TPOT|{{Cite Book|The Path of Transcendence}}}}  

Though destroying the Phylactery will not automatically destroy a lich,{{ref|name=TPOT}} a number of liches have used their Phylacteries as an anchor to allow them to revive after being slain.{{ref|name=Blood}}{{ref|name=ESOBlade}}{{ref|name=Vastarie}}{{ref|name=Squire}}{{ref|name=Shando}}{{ref|name=Mazza}} In such cases the destruction of the Phylactery is required in order to permanently slay the lich itself, with the Phylactery being described as an object to which the lich is bound due to their unique nature, and a tether along which the lich can return to the world, thus making their death no longer a concern. Phylacteries have also been likened to a spiritual focus, like an arcane vessel or container that contains larger portions of one's soul, thus making them powerful but dangerous tools.{{ref|name=Vastarie}} The form a Phylactery takes can vary, and there have been cases of liches using their towers,{{ref|name=Squire|[[Online:Squire Rayan|Squire Rayan]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Blood|Events of [[Online:Blood, Books, and Steel|Blood, Books, and Steel]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or even an entire [[Lore:Arum-Khal's Realm|realm]] they created themselves as a Phylactery, to anchor themselves and revive after death.{{ref|name=Vastarie}}{{ref|name=Mazza|[[ON:Mazza-Mirri|Mazza-Mirri]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Shando|[[ON:Shando-ri|Shando-ri]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOBlade|[[ON:The Moonlight Blade|The Moonlight Blade]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}In some cases, permanently destroying the lich might require shattering its otherwise impervious Phylactery with tools imbued with the lich's own power, and physically killing it before it can recreate a Phylactery while in its "hemomorphic spiritual form".{{ref|name=Blood}}{{ref|name=Squire}} In others one might have to shatter multiple Phylacteries and vanquish the slivers of the lich's soul guarding them in order to destroy the lich itself.{{ref|name=Mazza}}{{ref|name=Shando}}{{ref|name=ESOBlade}}{{ref|name=Vastarie}} There have been liches that achieved such a form of immortality that they could not be permanently slain and had to be sealed away through the use of a sealing ritual and an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] that would bind them to the one future where they failed to escape.{{Ref|name=Shulkunaak|[[Blades:Shulkunaak|Shulkunaak]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

There have been cases of slain liches whose [[Lore:Souls|souls]] were extracted through rites of cleansing possessing their would-be killers and consuming their spirits from within, instead, merging the lich and its vanquisher into a composite being. Such was the case for lich Erandur-Vangaril, a lich formed when the spirit of the former [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] wizard Vangaril, was consumed from within by the spirit of his former friend whom he had slain, the lich Erandur.{{ref|name=Lost|Events of [[Oblivion:Lich of Lost Boy Cavern|Lich of Lost Boy Cavern]] in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=ErandurVangaril|[[Oblivion:Erandur-Vangaril|Erandur-Vangaril]]'s appearance in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}   
<noinclude>
Only Necromancers with the utmost mastery of the Dark Arts can turn inward in their studies, attempting to manipulate their very own soul. Lichdom requires divesting one's soul and mortal form, removing the boundaries, the checks, placed on the mortal will by it. By accomplishing this feat, the Necromancer gains access to a virtually limitless magical horizon. Of course, the process exacts a heavy toll, as traveling through the Phylactery can tear a lich's psyche apart, resulting in madness. Long separation from one's soul can lead to apathy and megalomania as well, even if the Necromancer succeeds. In almost all cases, lichdom becomes a curse in very short order for those who succeed.{{ref|name=Vastarie|[[ON:Vastarie|Vastarie]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

According to one lich's account, becoming a lich greatly increased his magical power, making it possible for him to use spells the magnitude of which even those who considered themselves magically powerful had no idea of. The lich described [[Lore:Destruction|Destruction]] magic as having become as natural to him as breathing had been, claimed he'd subsequently unravel the mysteries of other schools such as [[Lore:Illusion|Illusion]] and [[Lore:Alteration|Alteration]], and even described magic as now being akin to a "friendly pet" to him. However, this increased power came at a heavy price. The lich's sanity gradually deteriorated, starting with bouts of megalomania where he'd liken himself to the gods, and gradually progressing to lapses in concentration, and finally struggling to remember his own name or even what he was. Other liches that the lich initially sought to partner with were likewise described as "raving mad", and he was forced to slay them. In the end the lich slew his own coven and started seeking a way to reverse his new state of being, to no avail.{{ref|[[Oblivion:A Wound In Time|A Wound In Time]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Saving Time Itself|Saving Time Itself]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion Remastered|Oblivion Remastered]]''}}{{ref|name=ONCD|{{Cite Book|Corelian's diary}}}}

According to some accounts by liches, most basic mortal needs, and sensations such as pain, become but distant memories to them.{{ref|name=ONCD}}{{ref|name=Zelvraak|[[Online:Zelvraak the Unbreathing|Zelvraak the Unbreathing]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Liches are said to be capable of devouring souls.{{ref|name=CelemarilBlades|[[Blades:Celemaril, The Sorcerer-King|Celemaril]]'s dialogue in [[Blades:Blades|Blades]]}}{{ref|name=J'Skar|[[Oblivion:J'skar|J'skar]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} Powerful liches can attempt to exert mental control over not just undead thralls, but also other liches, in order to repel them or to claim command over them. Even if the targeted lich resists, effects can range from difficulty focusing to feeling as if one has been struck blind and deaf.{{ref|name=Vastarie}} In combat, some liches have been known to possess abilities such as being able to see the invisible, requiring [[Lore:Mithril|mithril]] or better quality weapons to harm, and regenerating from injury.{{ref|name=J'Skar}}{{ref|name=Arena|[[Arena:Lich|Lich]] abilities in [[Arena:Arena|Arena]]}}{{ref|name=Daggerfall|[[Daggerfall:Lich|Lich]] abilities in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]}}

Lichdom is very hard to accomplish, taking large amounts of time and effort.{{ref|name=TPOT}} One method involves using the souls of mortals to achieve lichdom, the higher amounts of pain from the unwilling victims produce a purer and stronger form of lich.{{ref|name=APTL|{{Cite Book|Ascendancy: Pathway to Lichdom}}}} This method also requires a powerful magical relic, as it acts as a casting focus for 'Urelu's Loathsome Coercion', the spell used to take the souls from the victims; the higher a relic's power is, the more painful the soul tearing will be.{{ref|name=APTL}} Due to the amount of effort to accomplish lichdom, it's very rare for a traveler to encounter one, as most will stay in tombs studying obsessively.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Unhallowed Legions}}}} Liches have been known to disguise their true form using [[Lore:Illusion|Illusion]] magic to lower the defenses of unwary travelers.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Civility and Etiquette V. 5: Undead}}}}{{ref|name=Vastarie}}

Necromancy can also be used to transform people into a lich even if they are not practicing necromancers in life. Notable examples of this include Prince [[Lore:Naemon|Naemon]] and the Spinner Indinael, whose corpses were posthumously transformed into liches.{{ref|name=ESOShaNae|Circumstances surrounding the [[ON:Shade of Naemon|Shade of Naemon]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOFOF|Events of [[Online:Fulfilling One's Fate|Fulfilling One's Fate]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}  

[[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] is described in a poem as the "world's first of the undying liches",{{ref|name=MKOW|{{Cite Book|Mannimarco, King of Worms}}}} though its unclear if he was indeed the first to achieve the state of lichdom. After his [[Lore:Necromancer's Moon|ascension]], he was known to reveal the secrets of lichdom to his [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|followers]] through direct communion so that they may serve him in undeath as Worm Eremites.{{ref|name=TPOT}} One lich, the powerful [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|dro-m'Athra]] [[Lore:Arum-Khal|Arum-Khal]], managed to create an entire [[Lore:Arum-Khal's Realm|realm of existence]] to use as his Phylactery.{{ref|name=ESOBlade|[[ON:The Moonlight Blade|The Moonlight Blade]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

==Variants==
[[File:CT-creature-Nether Lich (portraits).jpg|Nether Liches (Castles)|thumb|left]]
A weaker variant of lich is the '''Nether Lich''', which can appear spectral in comparison to a normal lich.{{ref|[[OB:Nether Lich|Nether Lich]]es in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion'']]}} '''Outcasts''' are even weaker again, being closer to a spellcasting [[Lore:Wight|wight]] than a true lich.{{ref|[[Blades:Outcast|Outcast]]s in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

Archliches are a more powerful form of lich.{{ref|[[Blades:Liches|Archlich]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|[[Castles:Enemies|Nether Archlich]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} There is a type of lich called the Demi-Lich; the only known example was named {{Lore Link|Zaraphus}}.{{Ref|name=ARCorim|[[Arena:Corim Ashlen|Corim Ashlen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

'''[[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priests]]''' are a specific type of [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic lich.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Valdar|Valdar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Unlike the methods necromancers use, these liches are sustained from the transfer of life energy after death. [[Lore:Draugr|Draugr]] in the Nordic tombs transfer their life force into the body of their master, perpetuating the "life" of the priest and giving them a powerful form of undeath akin to lichdom.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Amongst the Draugr}}}} '''[[Lore:Ancient Deathpriest|Ancient Deathpriests]]''' are another type of Nordic lich, of which much less is known.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Ancient Deathpriest|Ancient Deathpriests]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] clans may look to darker avenues to appease [[Lore:Namira|Namira]], such as human sacrifices. Those that undergo such practices may seek to twist and abuse Namira's gifts to gain power, which when wielded, can cause great calamities.{{ref|name=ONGwenyfe|[[Online:Gwenyfe|Gwenyfe]]'s dialogue on [[Online:Voidmother Elgroalif|Voidmother Elgroalif]] related quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This brings out dark corruptions known as "voidstuff", which can empower a clan's witch, turning them to [[Lore:Voidmother|Voidmothers]], also known as Void Liches, which can consume the souls of their victims.{{ref|name=ONGRitesMatron|[[Online:Rites Matron|Rites Matron]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They are not traditional liches, and are considered a type of [[Lore:Shade|Shade]], dark, hateful, and hungry spirits that have been twisted by being in contact with Namira's energy, such as that of the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]].{{ref|name=ONGVerandis|[[Online:Count Verandis Ravenwatch|Count Verandis Ravenwatch]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] that become liches can also become [[Lore:Dro-m'Athra|dro-m'Athra]], giving rise to dro-m'Athra liches such as Dark Adept [[Lore:Arum-Khal|Arum-Khal]], which are said to be even more dangerous than normal liches.{{ref|name=Vastarie}}{{ref|name=ESOBlade}}

==Notes==
*Based on his research of necromancy and [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] culture, Master Sadren Sarethi theorized that [[Lore:Wispmother|Wispmothers]] are a cult of powerful sorceresses who achieved a unique state of lichdom developed by a now-forgotten [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] culture. Many alternate theories as to their origin have also been presented, however.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Wispmother}}}}
*Some liches have claimed they'd eventually return despite having no known Phylacteries that they were using as a means of resurrection.{{ref|name=Zelvraak}}
*The lich [[Lore:Celemaril|Celemaril]], who could not be killed and needed to be sealed away via the use of an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]], was noted to have a symbiotic relationship with the [[Lore:Abyss|Abyss]] [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] realm, but its unclear if that bond had any effect on his immortality.{{Ref|name=Shulkunaak}}{{ref|[[Blades:Abyssal Altar|Abyssal Altar]] description in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}
*The Lich [[Lore:Corelian|Corelian]] claimed he could read the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] in his possession, though doing so was described as difficult.{{ref|name=ONCD}}
