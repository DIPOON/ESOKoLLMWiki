The [[Lore:Evergloam|Evergloam]] is a realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], created and ruled over by [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]], the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Shadow. It is a realm of perpetual twilight, and the "cradle of shadow". The Evergloam exists adjacent to all other [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms of reality]] simultaneously; it can almost be glimpsed when one turns and looks quickly while in the dark.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] for [[Online:Evergloam (Summerset)|Evergloam]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} It consists of a primary plane and several pocket realms, including the [[Lore:Shade Perilous|Shade Perilous]] and the [[Lore:Crow's Wood|Crow's Wood]]. These realms are thought to be constantly shifting, and are perceived differently by different mortals.{{ref|[[Online:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] for [[Online:Evergloam|Evergloam]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} It is filled with forests, whose trees cast long shadows over a purple landscape. The land is dotted with crumbling buildings and sunken crypts.{{ref|name=ASoD|{{Cite Book|A Sky of Dusk}}}}<noinclude>

There is a portal to the Evergloam, the ''Ebonmere'', in the [[Lore:Twilight Sepulcher|Twilight Sepulcher]], an ancient temple in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. The Ebonmere is held open by the [[Lore:Skeleton Key|Skeleton Key]], although the artifact has occasionally been stolen from the Sepulcher by mortals. The [[Lore:Nightingales|Nightingales]] are a secretive subgroup of the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] who pledge their souls to Nocturnal and guard both the Ebonmere and the Skeleton Key. When a Nightingale dies, it is believed that their soul becomes one with the shadows. As long as the Ebonmere remains open, the souls of dead Nightingales can "walk with the shadows" and bring luck to thieves.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Karliah|Karliah]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Darkness Returns|Darkness Returns]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}  As Agents of Nocturnal, Nightingales are permitted to drink from the Ebonmere and obtain powerful boons.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s dialogue during [[Skyrim:Darkness Returns|Darkness Returns]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

Souls that end up in the Evergloam become [[Lore:Shadow|Shadows]]. They are capable of sleeping, though they never awaken fully rested. They also crave the light, but can never enjoy it, as exposure to light will banish and weaken them.{{ref|name=ASoD}} Other inhabitants of the realm include [[Lore:Nocturnal Shrike|nocturnal shrikes]], [[Lore:Crow Daedra|crow daedra]], [[Lore:Hag|hags]], [[Lore:Hagraven|hagravens]], [[Lore:Wolf#Gloam Wolves|gloam wolves]], and [[Lore:Lycanthropy|shadowy werewolves]].{{Ref|name=Cfwteg|Creatures found within the [[Online:Evergloam|Evergloam]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

==Pocket Realms==
===Shade Perilous===
[[File:BS-level4-Waterway 02.jpg|The Shade Perilous|thumb|right]]
[[File:BS-level4-Point J 02.jpg|thumb|left|The Chapel of Meditations]]
The '''Shade Perilous''' is a pocket realm of the Evergloam coveted by many Daedric clans for the harvestable darkling mana present. It is the home of the {{Lore Link|Chapel of Meditations}}, also known as the Chapel of Reflections,{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=BS|Letter to Jaciel}}}} where inhabitants gather at an altar to hold meetings and seek contemplation through Nocturnal. The realm was taken by the armies of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] during his invasion of the [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]] during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]]. Dagon's protégés, [[Lore:Faydra Shardai|Faydra Shardai]] and [[Lore:Xivilai Moath|Xivilai Moath]], became overzealous and, instead of simply obtaining passage by negotiation, took the Shade Perilous by force. Many of the clanless [[Lore:Mazken|Seducers]] who inhabited the realm betrayed the Nocturnal Lieutenant [[Lore:Jaciel Morgen|Jaciel Morgen]] and Nocturnal Subaltern {{Lore Link|Deyanira Katrece}}, claiming the area for Dagon. The Shade Perilous was left to the [[Lore:Fire Daedra|Fire Daedra]] and [[Lore:Frost Daedra|Frost Daedra]], although the two enemy clans fought over ownership while hunting down and imprisoning any non-Dagon aligned Seducers. An [[Lore:Apprentice|Apprentice]] from the Battlespire, following the retreating Daedric armies, helped to liberate the Shade Perilous, with Jaciel gaining control of the realm and ending the siege after she was released from the [[Lore:Dreamsleeve|Dreamsleeve]].{{ref|[[Battlespire:Level 4|Level 4]] in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=BSFire|[[BS:Fire Daedra|Fire Daedra]] dialogue in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=BSFrost|[[BS:Frost Daedra|Frost Daedra]] dialogue in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

===Crow's Wood===
[[File:ON-interior-Crow's Wood.jpg|The Crow's Wood|thumb|right]]
The '''Crow's Wood''' is an uncanny pocket realm of the Evergloam. It was made to resemble a nightmarish forest, although it is a poor imitation compared to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. It is inhabited by the feudal [[Lore:Blackfeather Court|Blackfeather Court]], sentient [[Lore:Crow Daedra|crow]]s who view themselves as the realm's rulers. Other inhabitants include [[Lore:Giant Bat|giant bats]], [[Lore:Ghost|ghosts]], [[Lore:Wolf|wolves]], [[Lore:Spider|spiders]], [[Lore:Wisp|wisps]], [[Lore:Wispmother|wispmothers]], [[Lore:Bear|bears]], and [[Lore:Wraith|soul wraiths]]. The realm was once ruled by a powerful soul wraith called Ghyslain, although he was banished under unknown circumstances. A [[Lore:Hagraven|hagraven]] known as the Crow Mother then came to rule the realm as the head of the Blackfeather Court.

Sometime in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] member of the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] named {{Lore Link|Rulantaril Oran}} discovered the Crow's Wood in his search for powerful forgotten spells. He created an enchanted chest to open a portal to the realm, and hid it in a small cave near the city of [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Rulantaril's Notes}}}} He attempted to gain favor with the Crow Mother by confessing his love for her. However, his trickery failed, and the hagraven imprisoned him in her tower. His son, Telbaril, entered the realm in search of his father in {{Year|2E 582}}. The Vestige entered Crow's Wood not long after, and Telbaril asked them for help in searching for his father. It is unknown if Rulantaril and his son were reunited. Ghyslain was also summoned back to the realm, but he was destroyed by the Vestige.{{ref|Events of [[ON:A Son's Promise|A Son's Promise]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Using the enchanted chest, the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] [[Lore:Order of the Eye|Order of the Eye]] sent guild members into the pocket realm to recover the {{Lore Link|Nightlight}}, a strange relic of [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] which had supposedly ended up in Crow's Wood due to the unexpected effects of the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]].{{ref|[[ON:Madness in Stonefalls|Madness in Stonefalls]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}</noinclude><noinclude>

===Garden of Shadows===
[[File:ON-place-Garden of Shadows 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Garden of Shadows]]
The '''Garden of Shadows''' is a pocket realm located deep in the Evergloam.{{ref|name=WitchmotherT|[[ON:Witchmother Taerma|Witchmother Taerma]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Plucking the Crow|Plucking the Crow]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} To the mortal inhabitants of Tamriel, the realm seems both alien and familiar. It resembles a wooded clearing in eternal dusk, filled with graves, corpses, and endless carved [[ON:Pumpkin|pumpkin]]s.{{ref|[[ON:Garden of Shadows|Garden of Shadows]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, Witchmother Taerma repeatedly used the realm to summon a powerful [[Lore:Wraith-of-Crows|Wraith-of-Crows]] known as the [[Lore:Crowborne Horror|Crowborne Horror]]. With the aid of [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] the Horror was defeated, delaying a potentially devastating attack on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. The [[Lore:Witchmothers|Witchmother coven]] also used this opportunity to drain some of the Horror's power for their own uses.{{ref|name=WitchmotherT}}
