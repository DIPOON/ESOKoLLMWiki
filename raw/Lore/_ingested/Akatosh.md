[[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], known as '''[[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]]''' (or '''Auriel''') to the [[Lore:Elves|Mer]],{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} '''Bormahu''' (Our Father) to the [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]],{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|[[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} the '''great Dragon of Time''' to the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]],{{Ref|name=TOAATO|{{Cite Book|Tales of Abba Arl: The Ox's Tale}}}} '''[[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]]''' to the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]], is the chief deity of the [[Lore:Gods_E#Eight_Divines|Eight]] or [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]] (the prescribed religious cults of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and its provinces). He is present in most [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]] religions.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} His avatar is a golden dragon,{{Ref|name=KE|{{Cite Book|King Edward}}}} and he is often called the '''Dragon God of Time''',{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}}, '''Father Akatosh''',{{ref|name=KOTNRoderic|[[OB:Sir Roderic|Sir Roderic]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}} and the '''Lord of the Dragons'''.{{Ref|name=KE|}}{{ref|name=ALAS}} He is generally considered to be the first of the [[Lore:Gods|gods]] to form in the [[Lore:Beginning Place|Beginning Place]]; some even claim he established it.{{ref|name=EGTH|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}} After his formation, other spirits found the process to be easier and the various pantheons of the world emerged.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Invocation}}}}{{ref|name=BAM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The [[Lore:Aedra|Aedric]] spirit{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=FMGAE|{{Cite Book|For my Gods and Emperor}}}} is the ultimate God of the Cyrodilic [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], where he embodies the qualities of endurance, invincibility, and everlasting legitimacy while promoting the virtues of duty, service, and obedience.{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=TCND|{{Cite Book|Ten Commands: Nine Divines}}}} Akatosh is thought to be the father of all dragons,{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and their leader [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] was titled "First-Born of Akatosh".{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} Alduin later came to proclaim himself a god, prompting [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]] to turn on him for forsaking his duty to Akatosh,{{ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} and resulting in history considering Alduin to be the Nordic aspect of Akatosh.{{ref|name=TAAD|{{Cite Book|The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy}}}} Akatosh is the patron of the [[Lore:Akatosh Chantry|Akatosh Chantry]], the religious order devoted to the worship and glorification of him, who refer to him as the "Great Dragon". [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]]s, such as the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] and the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]],  are thought to result from Akatosh's temporary loss of control over the flow of time, or his temporary "breaking", hence the name of the phenomenon.{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}{{ref|name=WWYDB|{{Cite Book|Where Were You ... Dragon Broke}}}}  Other ancient examples of Dragon Breaks may be found in other cultures, being the result of the Dragon being attacked by spirits and torn apart, like with Alkosh, who Khajiit myth holds was torn apart, before being put back together by [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]].{{ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}

==Syncretism==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none;|All Tamrielic religions begin the same. Man or mer, things begin with the dualism of Anu and His Other. These twin forces go by many names: Anu-Padomay, Anuiel-Sithis, Ak-El, Satak-Akel, Is-Is Not. Anuiel is the Everlasting Ineffable Light, Sithis is the Corrupting Inexpressible Action. In the middle is the Gray Maybe ('Nirn' in the Ehlnofex)...<br>
In any case, from these two beings spring the et'Ada, or Original Spirits. To humans these et'Ada are the Gods and Demons; to the Aldmer, the Aedra/Daedra, or the 'Ancestors'. All of the Tamrielic pantheons fill their rosters from these et'Ada, though divine membership often differs from culture to culture. Like Anu and Padomay, though, every one of these pantheons contains the archetypes of the Dragon God and the Missing God.<br>
—''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}}

There are multiple examples of Akatosh having parallels in different cultures, with variations on how widely accepted or clear the connections are. There are even claims that Akatosh (as well as [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]]) can be found in every single pantheon of worship.{{Ref|name=TM}}{{Ref|name=VOF}} While listing eight pantheons in ''[[Lore:Varieties of Faith...|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel]]'', Brother Mikhael Karkuxor assigned Akatosh to the first position of the [[Lore:Imperial|Cyrodiil]] and [[Lore:Breton|Bretony]] pantheons, [[Lore:Auriel|Auri-El]] for the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] and [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] versions, [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]] for [[Lore:Khajiit|Elsweyr]], [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] for [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]] for [[Lore:Redguard|Yokuda]], and [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] for the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] peoples. The notable omission of an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] pantheon was attributed by Mikhael Karkuxor to his "complete inadequacy in reconciling the obscure and contradictory accounts available to me on that subject". The difficulty in breaching Argonian insularism was possibly achieved by Solis Aduro, who provided the first known recording of Atakota from the oral tradition of the Adzi-Kostleel tribe in ''[[Lore:Children of the Root|Children of the Root]]''. 
===Accepted Aspects===
[[File:ON-icon-heraldry-Deities Akatosh.png|right|50px]]
The following spirits are generally considered to be understood aspects of Akatosh.
====Auri-El====
[[File:ON-statue-Auriel.jpg|thumb|right|A High Elf statue of Auri-El]]
Auri-El is the chief god of most [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] pantheons, with many Altmer and Bosmer directly claiming to be his living descendants.{{Ref|name=TM}} The syncretism between Auri-El and Akatosh is perhaps the most clear and documented. While researching ''[[Lore:The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy|The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy]]'', the author High Priest Alexandre Simon presented anecdotal evidence of most High Elves directly conceding that the two beings were the same, "that Auri-El is but Akatosh with a different name, colored by their own cultural beliefs".{{Ref|name=TAAD}} The Bosmer have similar treatment of him as the King of the Gods, "Auri-El Time Dragon".{{Ref|name=VOF}} 

Auriel's connection to Akatosh was well known by the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]]. In an attempt to correct this, at the height of their power within the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], they would be responsible for the Middle Dawn [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragonbreak]]. This was done using the [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]], the ultimate goal of was to remove all traces of elven taint from Akatosh in order to restore him to what they believed was his original humanadic purity which had been corrupted by the "[[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] taint".{{ref|name=Gahgdar|[[ON:Gahgdar|Gahgdar]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=VfTDB|{{Cite Book|Vindication for the Dragon Break}}}}

====The One====
[[Lore:The One|The One]] was the name of the Supreme Spirit worshipped by the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]], a strictly monotheistic cult devoted to singularity which ruled the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{ref|name=PGE1|{{PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Glenumbria: Alessian Orders}}}}{{ref|name=TEM|{{Cite Book|The Exclusionary Mandates}}}}{{ref|name=VfTDB|{{Cite Book|Vindication for the Dragon Break}}}} The Order's concept of the One was rooted in the belief that the God of Time Akatosh is of unitary essence, as proven by the monolinearity of Time. Furthermore, they believed that his sibling [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]] is "singularly misplaced" and therefore doubly venerated.{{ref|name=TEM}}
===World-Eater Aspects===
The following spirits govern the sphere of the End of Times, which Akatosh has disputed ties to. 
====Alduin====
[[File:LG-cardart-Alduin 02.jpg|thumb|left|Alduin]]In contrast to nearly universal acceptance of Auri-El and Alkosh as local variants of Akatosh, the syncretism of Akatosh and Alduin may be among the most controversial of examples. Also unlike previous examples, Alduin is not the chief god of the Nordic pantheon. Instead, Alduin is the bringer of the end times, and is known by the sobriquet "The World Eater".{{Ref|name=VOF}}

The origins of the ties between the two on the Imperial side can be traced to the efforts of the High Priestess of Akatosh [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]], who was faced with the difficult task of finding a working compromise between the Aldmeri and Nordic pantheons. Although this particular controversy was primarily inspired over the position that would be claimed by the other universally present god, [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]], the Nords were unwilling to allow for the worship of an elven pantheon, and her own subjects would have most likely revolted at a forced conversion to the Nordic pantheon. This resulted in the creation of the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]], with Shezarr being excluded from the total count as the "Missing God", but still venerated.{{Ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{Ref|name=APQ|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}}

As for the Nords, their distaste for Alduin can be traced to their early history. The earliest recordings of the modern Nordic religion were "The Old Ways", which worshiped various animal gods, including dragons, and featured clear parallels for the later Eight Divines. However, the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] came to prominence with support from actual [[Lore:Dragons|dragons]], leading to their rule over the early Nords. This would culminate in the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]], where the Nords overthrew the Dragon Cult.

As for Alduin himself, he is the self-proclaimed firstborn of Akatosh, claimed the lordship of Akatosh as his own, and ruled over the Nords during that period of time.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}} The cruelty of this time period left a lasting impression on Nordic society, and Alduin's own claim to being Akatosh created a natural aversion for the Imperial pantheon featuring him as their chief god. When Skyrim was incorporated into the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], the Imperials brought with them Akatosh, who the Nords saw as Alduin.{{Ref|name=DATN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}} This was acknowledged as a mistake originating from the founding of the Eight Divines, that had yet to be fully corrected by the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] by the Bishop of Akatosh Artorius Ponticus, who described it as a "brutal misunderstanding".{{Ref|name=APQ}}

However, descriptions of Alduin in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] continued to be rather contradictory, in spite of a clear rejection of him as their chief god, and researchers such as Brother Mikhael Karkuxor noting Alduin only "superficially resembles his counterpart in the Nine Divines". The confusion pertaining to Alduin is exasperated by comments from the missionary arm of the Imperial faith, the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]]. One missionary stationed in [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] described Alduin as a "heathen god".{{Ref|name=JD|[[Bloodmoon:Jeleen|Jeleen]]'s dialogue}} In [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]], Arentus Falvius, the Primate of the Great Chapel of Talos, similarly concluded that Nordic beliefs were antithetical to the Nine Divines.{{Ref|name=AFD|[[Oblivion:Arentus Falvius|Arentus Falvius]]' dialogue}}

By the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], the positions on the confusion had seemingly reversed. While leaders of Akatosh's faith in the Second Era, such as Artorius Ponticus, noted that the confusion laid with the Nordic population not understanding the distinction between the two, the Akatosh Chantry of the Fourth Era was now the one expressing the singularity of the two in spite of protests from the Nords. In ''[[Lore:The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy|The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy]]'', High Priest Alexandre Simon interviewed the Nordic population, who repeatedly asserted that Alduin and Akatosh were two different beings entirely, and that Akatosh was "first among the Divines, perseverance personified and, more than anything, a force of supreme good in the world". Ultimately, he rejected this explanation, and reiterated that Alduin and Akatosh were certainly one and the same. Another example of this comes from a book written by a Nord rejecting syncretism between Alduin and Akatosh while still accepting Akatosh as a good god worthy of worship, ''[[Lore:Alduin is Real|Alduin is Real, and He Ent Akatosh]]''.{{ref|name=TAAD}}{{ref|name=AIR|{{Cite Book|Alduin is Real}}}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Alduin reappeared from his banishment through time. He began to raise long-dead dragons from death, reinstate his draconic rule, and eat the world. The [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] appeared and defeated Alduin, saving Skyrim.{{ref|name=Skyrim}} After Alduin's defeat, his former lieutenant-turned-rival [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]] spoke of Alduin's doom. He said Alduin had doomed himself by improperly claiming the lordship of Akatosh{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax}}, showing that the dragons themselves see Alduin as distinct from Akatosh.

====Alkhan====
The ancient [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] acknowledge a World-Eater Dragon. He is a Wandering Spirit known as Alkhan, The Scaled Prince, and the Firstborn of [[Lore:Akha|Akha]]. Texts that predate the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar Epiphany]] claim that Alkhan was born when Akha bred with a demon of fire and shadow. Alkhan grew in size with each soul he ate, and ever hungered for the crown of Alkosh. He was slain by [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]] and his companions, but will return one day along the Many Paths.{{Ref|name=TWaS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}

====Thartaag====
The [[Lore:Skaal|Skaal]] recognize a version of the [[Lore:The Adversary|Adversary]] who will show up at the End of Seasons under the name [[Lore:Thartaag|Thartaag the World-Devourer]], easily comparable to Alduin.{{ref|name=ASS|{{Cite Book|Aevar Stone-Singer}}}}

====Satakal====
Similar to how Alkosh is a variant of Auri-El, Satakal is seen as a variant of Alduin. Like Alduin, Satakal brings about the end of the world, allowing for the next one.{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=TM}}

====Atakota====
Atakota is a being that was recorded from the oral tradition of the Argonian Adzi-Kostleel tribe by Solis Aduro, and has some resemblance to other known variations of Akatosh. These aspects include being responsible for time, arguably the most primary trait of Akatosh, and taking part in a cycle of beginning and ending things.{{ref|name=CotR|{{Cite Book|Children of the Root}}}}
===Dubious Aspects===
The following spirits are not generally tied to the Time Dragon, but are likened to him on occasion or share similarities to him.
====Ruptga====
Ruptga, chief of the Yokudan pantheon, is often conflated with Akatosh.{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell|{{Cite Book|EGT|Hammerfell}}}} The [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]] who acknowledge Akatosh as their chief claim that the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] know Akatosh as Tall Papa, another name for Ruptga. Both worship a god name [[Lore:Zeht|Zeht]], but the Forebears claim Akatosh is the father of Zeht, while the Crowns say it is Tall Papa.{{ref|name=VoFiT|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel}}}} On the other hand, some sources claim Ruptga came into being after Akatosh' perch from Eternity.{{ref|name=TM}}

====Almalexia====
Most traces of Akatosh disappeared from ancient [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] legends with their exodus due to his association with the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], although they would return in a fashion with the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]]. Akatosh's aspects of immortality, historicity, and genealogy would resurface in [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], the most popular traits of Akatosh being seen in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'s most popular divine [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal]]. Although female instead of male, Almalexia also takes on the role of a progenitor figure like Akatosh.{{ref|name=VOF}}

====All-Maker====
In a claim that is seemingly at odds with the idea of Alduin being connected to the Skaal's malevolent Adversary, some sources instead postulate that the benevolent [[Lore:All-Maker|All-Maker]] of the Skaal is a distant echo of Alduin. The connection is made to highlight how it is incredulous to assume the Dragon Cult (which has Alduin as its head) and the Skaal Faith (which has All-Maker as its head) are the same faith, and is not a seriously proposed by the sources that suggest it. {{ref|name=TGATT|{{Cite Book|The Guardian and the Traitor}}}}

====Peryite====
[[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]] is a spirit in the [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] pantheon, sharing traits with Akatosh, such as ties to time, strict natural order, and draconic symbolism. This suggests cultural connections between the early ancestors of Men and Mer in northwestern Tamriel.{{Ref|name=GSOTR|{{Cite Book|Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 4}}}} After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Akatosh, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Tenets==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''	"Those who heed the Dragon God of Time find themselves marked by his greatness. His eye sees what is true. To be blessed by him is to know eternity." —Psalm of the Dragon'' {{ref|name=esoAGE|[[Online:Akatosh Golden Eye|Akatosh's Golden Eye]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}}}
The Tenets of Akatosh reflect the qualities of his sphere, and these are expected of his followers who truly believe in him. Endurance: gives Akatosh the ability and strength to continue and last, this is directly associated to his role as the god of time. His followers are expected to endure in a similar matter despite whatever fatigue, stress or adverse conditions they come across, and this reflects Akatosh's lasting quality. Invincibility: another key trait of Akatosh is he cannot be defeated, conquered, or subdued, and his followers who truly believe in him cannot be either. This is Akatosh's indomitable quality. Everlasting Legitimacy: is a quality that must be examined, as it not only reflects the eternal aspect of Akatosh, but also his reverence for law, reason, and the ruling principles of heredity. As such, nothing sanctioned or blessed by Akatosh can be considered unjustified, and reflects Akatosh's lawful quality.{{Ref|name=WoTDG| {{Cite Book|Worship of the Dragon God}}}}

==Worship==
[[File:ON-render-Akatosh vs. Alduin Crate.jpg|A clash between followers of Alduin and Akatosh|thumb|left]]
Akatosh is said to have been worshipped across Tamriel since before recorded history. For centuries, his followers, cultists, and warriors, are said to have clashed with those of Alduin, the "First-Born" of the Dragons and the corruption of a dark prophecy, a terrible fight "within the family".{{ref|name=AvAC|[[Online:Akatosh vs. Alduin Crate|Akatosh vs. Alduin Crate]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some followers of ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] traditions thus adorn themselves with imagery of a clash between Akatosh and Alduin. Their markings symbolize that there must be balance in all things and that every story's beginning must come to its inevitable end, that there is no light without darkness, no fullness without hunger, and no life without death. These are thought to be the teachings of the great Dragons.{{ref|name=DFM|[[Online:Dragonclash Face Marking|Dragonclash Face Marking]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DBM|[[Online:Dragonclash Body Marking|Dragonclash Body Marking]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The faithful also symbolize Akatosh and Alduin through twin orbs of sacred fire, one golden and one crimson, swirling around each other.{{ref|name=DFO|[[Online:Dragonclash Flame Orbs|Dragonclash Flame Orbs]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Ardent worshipers of Akatosh use ritual implements associated with the deity such as sacred oils and seeds, sanctified metalworking tools blessed by Akatosh and [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]], golden dragonscales or captured ever-burning dragonflame, to craft implements associated with the deity, such as golden scales resembling those of Akatosh which they morph their own skin to resemble through magic, or paladin's armor.{{ref|name=Soulfire|[[Online:Soulfire Dragon Illusion|Soulfire Dragon Illusion]]'s Morphing Collectible descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Priests of the Dragon God believe that time heals all, and so urge the faithful to wear golden ear cuffs that emulate Akatosh in the belief they will bring healing more swiftly.{{ref|name=LotDEC|[[Online:Lord of Dragons Ear Cuff|Lord of the Dragon's Ear Cuff]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Golden [[Lore:Nix-Ox|Nix-Oxen]] are prized by the faithful of Akatosh due to their beauty.{{ref|name=GNOx|[[Online:Golden Nix-Ox Steed|Golden-Nix-Ox Steed]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:CT-ruling-Eclipse 02.jpg|An eclipse, a phenomenon sometimes associated with losing the favor of Akatosh (Castles)|thumb|left]][[File:CT-Events-Rite of Akatosh (Large).png|A warrior facing an Avatar of Akatosh during the Old Life Festival (Castles)|thumb|right]]
Faithful of Akatosh are known to sometimes create [[Lore:Living Armor|Living Armor]] in honor of the deity. These [[Lore:Golem|golems]] of Nordic origin,{{Ref|name=DL|[[ON:Dragonclash Living Armor Senche|Dragonclash Living Armor Senche]] mount description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} bear golden scale armor and flames, appearing as if untouched by time, considered a hallmark of the gilded Lord of the Dragons. They are created in dedication ceremonies which involve the faithful praying to Akatosh to make his flames glow bright within the constructs and allow his power to live within them, so they may spread his light and lead all to wisdom.{{ref|name=ALAS|[[Online:Akatosh Living Armor Senche|Akatosh Living Armor Senche]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=THLA|[[Online:Time's Herald Living Armor|Time's Herald Living Armor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Astronomy|phenomenon]] of [[Lore:Sun|Solar]] [[Lore:Eclipse|eclipses]] is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of Akatosh, and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity.{{ref|name=CTEclipse|[[Castles:Rulings#Eclipse|Eclipse rulings]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} The Ten Aphorisms of Akatosh are known to be recited by followers of Akatosh.{{ref|[[Online:Yadugar|Yadugar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Rite of Akatosh is an endeavor held by Cyrodilic elders during the [[Lore:Old Life Festival|Old Life Festival]] in preparation for the [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]], in which their party goes on a pilgrimage to rid the nearby forests of "Old Life", monsters like [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]], [[Lore:Ogre|Ogres]], and [[Lore:Troll|trolls]]. The party then challenges the manifested Avatar of Akatosh. By successfully defeating this avatar, the elders hope the God of Time will grant them youth or more time, to be at peace, or that the feat will grant them respect.{{ref|name=Rite|[[Castles:The Rite of Akatosh|The Rite of Akatosh]] event and related [[Castles:Rulings|Rulings]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}
[[File:ON-skin-Blaze of Akatosh (male).jpg|Blaze of Akatosh, the form that those with the Dragon's fire within their soul may take|thumb|right]][[File:ON-polymorph-Time's Herald Living Armor.jpg|Time's Herald Living Armor|thumb|left]] 

It is said that those who "burn with the soul of the Dragon" may be "transformed by their own inner fire", taking on a form of flame and golden scales, though whether they use this power for good or for ill remains entirely up to them.{{ref|name=BoA|[[Online:Blaze of Akatosh|Blaze of Akatosh]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Those who heed the Dragon God of Time are said to sometimes be marked by his greatness, marking one of their eyes with Akatosh's wing and turning it golden and serpentine. It is said that the eye of Akatosh sees what is true, and that to be blessed by him is to know eternity.

The [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Cuirass of the Crusader]] is thought to have been created by either [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] or Akatosh. Champions of the deity have been known to sometimes receive blessings of greater strength, being urged to trust in the [[Lore:Divines|Nine]] when their owns strength fails them.{{ref|[[OB:Priory of the Nine (quest)|Priory of the Nine]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}

==Five Commandments of Akatosh==
These commandments are those Akatosh expects all of his worshipers to follow and these commands are taught even to the faithless.
*First Commandment: "Obey your Emperor" this command comes from the fact the Empire and Akatosh go hand in hand.
*Second Commandment: "Study the Covenants", this command is studying the agreements made by Akatosh and his mortal followers, everyone that follows are urged to study these.
*Third Commandment: "Worship the Eight" Akatosh isn't a Jealous God, and expects all his followers to worship and revere the Eight.
*Fourth Commandment: "Do your duty." Akatosh expects everyone to do their duty and be responsible, failing to meet obligations is considered a sin.
*Fifth Commandment: "Heed the commands of the saints and priests." Akatosh prefers hierarchy and order  and expects his followers to follow the Church's Hierarchy.{{Ref|name=WoTDG}}

==Planet and Associated Realms==
{{Lore:Akatosh (planet)}}

The [[Lore:Spilled Sand|Spilled Sand]] is an obscure realm of existence associated with [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]].It consists of an endless expanse of sand interspersed with an oasis of golden trees, and a colossal slumbering golden dragon with scales of pure gold. The twin moons, Jode and Jone, can be seen in the sky alongside a series of stars. The realm is described as not a place but rather a "story that has and will be told time and time again" and "myth made manifest", it is said to exist at once within and outside the tapestry of time, and beyond even the Sands Behind the Stars.{{Ref|name=Nahfahlaar|[[ON:Nahfahlaar|Nahfahlaar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}}{{Ref|name=Ja'darri|[[Online:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} When questioned on whether the Spilled Sand is the realm of Alkosh itself, Ja'darri would not outright confirm it, answering instead that Alkosh is both weaver and embodiment of time's tapestry and all always exist within time's realm regardless of where they are.{{Ref|name=Ja'darri|[[Online:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} The Mask of Alkosh allows for one to journey to this realm when awoken via the blood of a dragon.{{ref|name=Dragonguard|[[Online:The Dragonguard|The Dragonguard]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} 

The "myriad kingdoms of [[Lore:Akha|Akha]] along the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]]", the realities beyond and surrounding Aurbis are thought to be ruled over and safeguarded by Alkosh, who inherited the role and crown of Akha.{{ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
