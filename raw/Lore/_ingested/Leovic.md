[[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] was an [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|Emperor]] of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and the third and last emperor of the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachman]] dynasty called the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]], who ruled the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]] during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. Leovic was described as the most Imperialized and cosmopolitan of the Longhouse Emperors due to being born in Cyrodiil and having rarely visited [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]].{{ref|name=AwtLE|{{Cite Book|Audiences with the Longhouse Emperors}}}}{{ref|name=SHotLE|{{Cite Book|Secret History of the Longhouse Emperors}}}} As the years went on, Leovic's reign was said to have gotten more and more troubled.{{Ref|name=RDM|{{Cite Book|Report on the Despot of Markarth}}}}{{ref|name=AwtLE}} He was preceded by his father, [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]], and was succeeded by [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Early Life===
Leovic was born in {{Year|2E 542}} to Emperor [[Lore:Moricar|Moricar]] in the capital of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], unlike his father and grandfather, who were born in [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}} As he was born and raised in Cyrodiil, he was more Imperialized and cosmopolitan than his father and grandfather, and was primarily immersed in the culture of Cyrodiil instead of the culture of the Reach.{{ref|name=AwtLE}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} He was educated by Imperial tutors, including High Chancellor [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]], but he also had teachers from the [[Lore:Icereach Coven|Icereach Coven]].{{ref|name=AwtLE}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Leovic's future steward, Farrul Lupus, helped raise and educate him.{{Ref|name=DID|{{Cite Book|Deciphered Imperial Document}}}} Due to his upbringing as an imperial prince, some considered him to be over-educated and spoiled.{{ref|name=AwtLE}}

When Prince Leovic came back from a successful campaign against border raiders, Moricar, the [[Lore:House Tharn|Tharn]] family, the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]], and great nobles were in the Great Hall to receive him. Moricar praised the prince and decreed that he could name his reward. Leovic did not hesitate, walking right up to the Tharns and saying, "Your Majesty, I want the Chancellor's daughter." He spoke of Abnur Tharn's daughter [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia]], and though the Chancellor had many daughters, it was understood by all which one he meant.{{Ref|name=MTCCT|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Clivia Tharn}}}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Leovic married Clivia at some point after this.{{ref|name=COTFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}{{Ref|name=MTCCT}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}

In {{Year|2E 561}}, Leovic and Moricar spent much of their time in the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|Imperial Palace]] preparing sacrifices to [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] and rituals that would see the creation of the [[Lore:Four Ambitions|Four Ambitions]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}

===Emperor of Cyrodiil===
In {{Year|2E 563}}, Moricar was wounded during an invasion of [[Lore:Western Skyrim|Western Skyrim]] and died as a result of his injuries in {{Year|2E 564}}, so Leovic succeeded him as emperor.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=SMI|{{Cite Book|Secrets of Moricar the Inheritor}}}}{{ref|name=TWoS|{{Cite Book|The Wolf of Solitude|ns_base=ON}}}} Upon Moricar's death, Leovic inherited the bargains Moricar and [[Lore:Durcorach|Durcorach]] made with Mehrunes Dagon, including the Four Ambitions.{{Ref|name=DID}}{{Ref|name=SMI}}{{Ref|name=ELdD|[[ON:Emperor Leovic|Emperor Leovic]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}{{ref|name=XBoC|{{Cite Book|Xynaa's Book of Contracts}}}}

Early in Leovic's reign, the Imperial Court was said to have felt much more like the courts that existed prior to Durcorach's conquest of Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=AwtLE}} There was hardly any mention of the Reach or the practices of the Reachfolk, which had been much more common in the courts of his father and grandfather.{{ref|name=AwtLE}} The Icereach Coven served as advisors to Leovic during his reign, as did a Reachman later known as "[[Lore:The Rat|The Rat]]".{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=MTCMC|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Mother Ciannait}}}}{{Ref|name=ESOAd|[[ON:Arvnir|Arvnir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Harrowstorm|Harrowstorm]]''}}{{Ref|name=TRd|[[Online:The Rat|The Rat]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}}

As Leovic's reign continued, he began to embrace more of his Reachfolk heritage.{{ref|name=AwtLE}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}} Initially, his embracement of his Reachfolk heritage was minor, as he simply placed Reach fetishes in the Imperial Palace and demanded that the kitchen retrieve the recipes that Durcorach and Moricar preferred.{{ref|name=AwtLE}} However, eventually, he installed idols and shrines dedicated to the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]] around the Imperial throne room, leading his subjects to perceive him as increasingly eccentric.{{ref|name=AwtLE}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=EFEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}}

[[Lore:Anvil Castle|Anvil Castle]] hosted Leovic and his court four times during the late 2E 560s.{{ref|name=ONVC|[[Online:Vateshran Calbokh|Vateshran Calbokh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}}{{ref|name=SZLMIwLS|{{Cite Book|Special ZOS Lore Master Interview with Lawrence Schick|ns_base=GEN}}}} He often visited the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]] during the summer months.{{Ref|name=GCG2|{{Cite Book|Gold Coast Guide, Part Two}}}} Locals used to consider the policies and leadership of Leovic to have dramatically increased safety on the Gold Coast.{{Ref|name=GCG1|{{Cite Book|Gold Coast Guide, Part One}}}} The Emperor tasked the [[Lore:Imperial Navy|Imperial Navy]] with patrolling the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] to guard the coast against piracy, which ensured safety and security across the region.{{Ref|name=GCG1}} Some accounts suggest that as of {{Year|2E 566}}, he was well-liked by many of Anvil's citizens, and mention instances of him waving to his subjects from the balcony of Anvil Castle.{{Ref|name=GCG1}}{{Ref|name=GCG2}} 

However, according to later sources, he was perceived as ungenerous and was unpopular among the free-spirited citizens of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]].{{ref|name=ONVC}}{{Ref|name=SZLMIwLS}} Leovic reportedly sought to show the world the ways of the Reachfolk, viewing Anvil as too "fat and still" and in need of correction. His visits were met with him being treated lavishly, and his message was not understood, which angered him. During a lavish feast for him in the city, he discarded his wine cup, upended the feast table, and tore the silk finery he had received. When Anvil's court did not "forgo its decadence", he saw Anvil as a lost cause.{{ref|name=ONVC}} After the so-called "Anvil Commotion" of Mid Year [[Lore:Second_Era#2E_569|569]], he and his court never returned to the Gold Coast again.{{ref|name=ONVC}}{{Ref|name=SZLMIwLS}}

Circa {{Year|2E 568}}, Leovic sent Consul [[Lore:Cardea|Cardea]] from her post in the Imperial City to aid his cousin, the Imperial Governor of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]], [[Lore:Caddach|Caddach]], as an administrator and to provide a conduit between Cyrodiil and the Reach.{{Ref|name=STotDF|{{Cite Book|Scary Tales of the Deep Folk}}}}{{Ref|name=PotR|{{Cite Book|Politics of the Reach}}}} In {{Year|2E 573}}, Leovic commissioned ''The Emperor's Guide to Tamriel'', a comprehensive text describing the histories and cultures of each of Tamriel's provinces.{{ref|name=EGTAF|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Author Foreword}}}}{{Ref|name=EGTC|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Cyrodiil}}}} 

At some point, the Emperor put out a request for an [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'ri]] trader to transport a shipment of arms across the desert. The trader Afarh accepted the request, and he put his family's entire estate as collateral. When bandits waylaid the shipment caravan, the Empire seized his family's lands and businesses.{{Ref|name=ONJd|[[ON:Josajeh#A Breach Amid the Trees|Josajeh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

===The Colovian Revolt===
In {{Year|2E 576}}, Leovic issued a [[Lore:Leovic's Great Spirits Proclamation|decree]] that legalized [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] worship in the Empire.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=EFEV}}{{Ref|name=TFC|{{Cite Book|The Frostfall Coup}}}} The legalization of Daedra worship quickly triggered a [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|revolt]] against Leovic, led by an erstwhile supporter, [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]], Duke of [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]] and Count of [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]].{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{Ref|name=EFEV}}{{Ref|name=SVR1|{{Cite Book|Saga of Varen's Rebellion, Part 1}}}}{{Ref|name=CCTLD|[[ON:Councilor Lovidicus|Councilor Lovidicus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

Leovic appealed to Mehrunes Dagon for assistance in suppressing the revolt, and the Prince of Destruction supplied him with Daedra to supplement his forces.{{Ref|name=TJOEL|{{Cite Book|The Journal of Emperor Leovic}}}}{{Ref|name=SVR2|{{Cite Book|Saga of Varen's Rebellion, Part 2}}}} Leovic's [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] forces, bolstered by Reachman auxiliaries and Daedra, defeated Varen's forces at [[Lore:Fort Ash|Fort Ash]], but Varen then took command of the Legion of Chorrol and pushed the Emperor's forces into the [[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlands]].{{Ref|name=EFEV}}{{Ref|name=SVR2}} Following Varen's victory, the [[Lore:Colovia|Colovian Estates]] joined the rebellion, with the exception of Anvil, where the count, [[Lore:Ephrem Benirus|Ephrem Benirus]], remained loyal to Leovic.{{Ref|name=EFEV}}{{Ref|name=SZLMIwLS}}{{Ref|name=TWatD|{{Cite Book|The Wolf and the Dragon}}}} When the revolt broke out, Leovic was broadly unpopular, and Varen found a number of allies among Heartland and [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] families as well.{{Ref|name=SZLMIwLS}} As the revolt continued, Varen and his forces captured the Nibenese cities of [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] and [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]].{{Ref|name=COTFC5|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 5}}}}{{Ref|name=MtCSS|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Sai Sahan}}}}{{Ref|name=SVR3|{{Cite Book|Saga of Varen's Rebellion, Part 3}}}} [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]],  one of Varen's allies, managed to capture Leyawiin, even though his mercenaries turned against him after Leovic had paid them off.{{Ref|name=COTFC5}}{{Ref|name=MtCSS}}

The Four Ambitions were not ready when the revolt broke out, as they were still a few years from completion; however, if they had been ready, Leovic would have used them to crush Varen's rebellion and save his legacy.{{Ref|name=TJOEL}} He prepared for the possibility of his defeat, and hid the Four Ambitions so they would not fall into the hands of Varen.{{Ref|name=TJOEL}}{{ref|name=CJR|{{Cite Book|Councilor Jirich's Records|ns_base=ON}}}}{{ref|name=PtS|{{Cite Book|Preserve the Secret}}}}{{Ref|name=ELdD}} Leovic believed it was imperative to uphold and honor the promises made to Mehrunes Dagon; therefore, he informed his steward, Farrul Lupus, of every detail of the Four Ambitions and placed documents in hidden caches so that [[Lore:Ertus Vandacia|Ertus Vandacia]] and the [[Lore:Order of the Waking Flame|Order of the Waking Flame]] could continue the plan in the event of his death.{{Ref|name=TJOEL}}{{Ref|name=ELdD}}{{Ref|name=DID}}

===Death and Aftermath===
[[File:ON-trailer-Deadlands and Damnation-Leovic.jpg|Emperor Leovic, slain by Varen Aquilarios|left|thumb]]
Leovic's reign ended in {{Year|2E 577}}, when Varen Aquilarios and his forces stormed the Imperial City and killed him in the Imperial Palace at the foot of the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]], and Varen immediately declared himself emperor.{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=EFEV}}{{ref|name=COTFC4}}{{Ref|name=CCTLD}} Varen subsequently discarded Leovic's corpse in the tombs located in the Imperial City Sewers.{{Ref|name=TRd}}{{Ref|name=MJAYQ|{{Cite Book|Mycaelis Julus Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=GL|{{Cite Book|Groundskeeper's Letter}}}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Worm Cultists]] discovered Leovic's corpse and resurrected him, first in the form of a [[Lore:Zombie|zombie]] and then as a [[Lore:Flesh Atronach|flesh atronach]]. Soldiers fighting in the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] fought and destroyed Leovic's corpse shortly after its reanimation.{{Ref|name=ICEL|[[ON:Emperor Leovic|Emperor Leovic]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Imperial City (DLC)|Imperial City]]''}}

After Leovic's overthrow, the remaining Reachmen retreated from the [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Imperial Province]].{{Ref|name=RDM}}{{ref|name=SHotLE}}{{ref|name=TWoS}} When he learned of Leovic's overthrow, Caddach set aside his Imperial title and instead took the old Reach title of Ard. Caddach also purged all surviving members of the [[Lore:Blackdrake Clan|Blackdrake Clan]], securing his hold on Markarth against his rivals.{{Ref|name=HMASS|{{Cite Book|History of Markarth: A Story in Stone}}}}{{Ref|name=RDM}}

===Afterlife in the Deadlands===
Due to the Longhouse Emperors' bargains with Mehrunes Dagon, whom Moricar suspected would renege on his promises,{{Ref|name=EMd|[[Online:Emperor Moricar|Emperor Moricar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}{{Ref|name=PHJ|{{Cite Book|Partially Hidden Journal}}}} Leovic's [[Lore:Soul|soul]] was sent to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]] after his death.{{ref|name=TLA|Events of [[ON:The Last Ambition|The Last Ambition]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}} Before his death, Leovic failed to pass responsibility for the Four Ambitions to an heir, leading to the declaration that the Longhouse Emperors had defaulted on their bargains with Mehrunes Dagon.{{Ref|name=XBoC}} Eventually, the souls of Leovic, Moricar, and Durcorach were imprisoned in the Brandfire Reformatory in the Deadlands, where Daedra tortured them by fusing the Longhouse Emperors' souls with a chunk of [[Lore:Iron|iron]] slag.{{ref|name=TLA}}{{Ref|name=ESOTAd|[[ON:The Anchorite|Mairead]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}

In 2E 582, [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] and [[Lore:Mairead|Mairead]], one of the Four Ambitions, released the Longhouse Emperors' souls from a chunk of iron slag in the Brandfire Reformatory Forge. The Vestige then questioned the spirits of the Longhouse Emperors about the Four Ambitions. Sombren and Calia, two of the Four Ambitions, along with [[Lore:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]] and [[Lore:Lyranth|Lyranth the Foolkiller]], interrupted when they entered the Reformatory Forge through a portal.{{ref|name=TLA}} Sombren then destroyed the Longhouse Emperors' spirits with frost magic, although he claimed their spirits would reform in the Deadlands eventually.{{ref|name=TLA}}{{Ref|name=ESOSom|[[ON:Sombren|Sombren]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:The Deadlands (DLC)|The Deadlands]]''}}
