</noinclude>[[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]], also spelled '''Ada'Soon Dir-Kamal''',{{ref|name=PGE1Sky|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Skyrim}}}} was the King of [[Lore:Kamal (place)|Kamal]], a nation of [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]. 

In {{Year|2E 572}}, he led an army of his people in an invasion of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], reportedly in search of a person or object roughly translated as the "[[Lore:Ordained Receptacle|Ordained Receptacle]]".{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} Dir-Kamal's army landed in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] near the city of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], which they subsequently besieged and sacked.{{ref|name=JTS|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]], the [[Lore:Underking|Underking]], was summoned to help repel the invasion of the "Akaviri demon" (sources differ on who summoned him).{{ref|name=JTS}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} Dir-Kamal decided to bypass [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] and retreat from Skyrim, instead setting his sights on [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]]. The [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s refused to let him get away and pursued him into [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], where his army was crushed between them, a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] army led by [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], and a third force of [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] Shellbacks.{{ref|name=JTS}} Remnants of Dir-Kamal's army survived this battle and resurfaced years later in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], where they seized the Imperial Throne from the successors of the Emperor [[Lore:Attrebus (warlord)|Attrebus]].{{Ref|name=PGE1-E|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Elsweyr Confederacy}}}}<noinclude>
