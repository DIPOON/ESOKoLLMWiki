[[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], once known as '''Hegathe''', the '''Deathland''' (or '''Deathlands'''), and '''Volenfell''', is a region in the west of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. It has been described as a province, kingdom, or republic throughout its history. Situated between [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]],{{ref|name=PGE3H|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Hammerfell}}}}{{Ref|name=MCMap|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Tamriel]] – [[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex|Morrowind Codex]]}} Hammerfell features diverse landscapes including beaches, jungles, grasslands, mountains, swamps, and the vast [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]] desert. It is inhabited by the human race of [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s, who migrated to Tamriel following the partial sinking of their homeland, [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]].<noinclude>  While predominantly urban and maritime, with bustling port cities, the interior of Hammerfell is sparsely populated with small farms and nomadic tribes.

All the old cities of lost Dwarves are now the cities of modern Hammerfell.{{ref|name=RGH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}}

==Geography==
[[File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|thumb|left|350px|A map of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region in the Third Era showing the forested mountains and coastal jungle regions of Hammerfell east of the Alik'r Desert]]
Hammerfell is located in the western part of Tamriel. It borders [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] to the north, [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] to the northeast, and [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] to the southeast. The border with High Rock is defined by the [[Lore: Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]],{{Ref|Map of [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell]]}}{{ref|name=mWT}}{{ref|[[:File:LO-map-Hammerfell (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Hammerfell (Morrowind Codex)]]}} though the border was formerly further south at the [[Lore: Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]] in the second Era,{{ref|name=ON}}{{ref|name=BSoHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} and the fiefdom of [[Lore:Mournoth (fiefdom)|Mournoth]] located south of the river was controlled by [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] for some time after the [[Lore: Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] in {{Year|3E 417}}.{{ref|name=TWITW}} The Cyrodilic border is defined by the [[Lore:Brena River|Brena River]], which rises in the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian Highlands]] and separates Hammerfell from the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]]. It has extensive access to the sea, with the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] to the northwest, the [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] to the west and the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] to the south. Craglorn and the Western Reach make up the borders along Skyrim's Druadach Mountains.

Hammerfell has a variety of environments, ranging from temperate to tropical climates. The coasts in particular are vibrant with plantlife, and biomes vary from open grassland, subtropical beaches, humid swamps around [[Lore:Tigonus|Tigonus]], temperate woodland mountains around the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontails]], forests around [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]],{{Ref|name=ARTerrain|[[:File:AR-map-Terrain.png|Arena terrain]] map}} and thick jungles such as the [[Lore:Bantha|Bantha]].{{ref|name=DUG|{{Cite Book|Daggerfall User's Guide|ns_base=BK}}}} The [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r desert]] is found in the northwest, with the barren wastelands of [[Lore:Dak'fron|Dak'fron]] found towards the southeast of the desert.

There is a notable level of thermal activity in the province, particularly around the Dragontail Mountains, as evidenced by lava harvested by facilities at the [[Lore:Bloodroot Forge|Bloodroot Forge]], Aldunz, and Mzeneldt.{{ref|name=SAC|[[Online:Sees-All-Colors|Sees-All-Colors]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A volcano connected to [[Lore:Molavar|Molavar]] is found near [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]],{{Ref|name=EPA|[[ON:Elinhir Private Arena|Elinhir Private Arena]] location in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=DFVolcanoMap|[[:File:DF-prerelease-IliacBayMap.jpg|Daggerfall Iliac Bay]] terrain map}} and there is at least one volcanic crater in the province.{{Ref|name=TIC}}

Hammerfell is divided into several regions, among them the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]], southern [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]], [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]], [[Lore:Khefrem|Khefrem]], and [[Lore:Sunforge|Sunforge]]. Many isles in the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]] and [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] are under Hammerfell's control, including [[Lore:The Chain|the Chain]], [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]],{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Cespar|Cespar]] and [[Lore:Herne (island)|Herne]]. The [[Lore:Systres|Systres]] and [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]] have at certain times also considered a part of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=MCMap}}{{ref|[[Online:Stirk|Stirk]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===[[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]]===
[[File:ON-concept-08.jpg|left|thumb|]]
The [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]], best known as the '''Alik'r Desert''', is a large region which forms the heart of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} In truth, the distinction between the Alik'r's shifting sands and the kingdoms of Hammerfell is hazy; travelers in the vast desert report never being entirely sure which kingdom they may be in. An enormous red mist seems to rise up from the sand in the mornings, though it disappears again well before noon.{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Alik'r}}}} Major cities of the region include [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]] in the northwest, the port city of [[Lore:Tava's Blessing|Tava's Blessing]] in the north, [[Lore:Satakalaam|Satakalaam]] and [[Lore:Kozanset|Kozanset]] in the east, as well as [[Lore:Bergama|Bergama]] in the south.{{ref|name=ON}}

Many [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] ruins lie under its sand,{{ref|name=PGE3H}} as does a stronghold of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], Wind Scour Temple.{{ref|name=TRAFOTB|{{Cite Book|The Rise and Fall of the Blades}}}} The {{Lore Link|Lost City of the Na-Totambu}} is also hidden somewhere in the wastes.{{ref|name=ON}} During the Warp in the West, there were reports of a large, spontaneous sandstorm, and the region ultimately came under the rule of Sentinel.{{ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} At the beginning of the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] in {{Year|4E 171}}, the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial legions]] in southern Hammerfell, overwhelmed by the sudden onslaught of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], were forced to retreat across the Alik'r in the now-famous March of Thirst.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

===Satakalaam===
[[File:DF-map-Satakalaam.png|left|thumb]]
[[Lore:Satakalaam (county)|Satakalaam]] is a region on the western coast of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], on the eastern coast of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. To its east lay [[Lore:Mournoth (fiefdom)|Mournoth]]; to the south, [[Lore:Totambu|Totambu]]; while [[Lore:Lainlyn (barony)|Lainlyn]] was situated to the west.{{ref|name=Map|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of Iliac Bay]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Its [[Lore:Satakalaam|provincial seat]] shares the same name. 

The people of Satakalaam have upheld the worship of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]'s ancient pantheon since their departure from the west.{{Ref|name=BBS|{{Cite Book|Blessed, Blessed Satakalaam}}}} The name draws an immediate connection to the deity, [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]] the Serpent God, a spirit observed by the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] and popular among [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'ri]] nomads affiliated with them.{{Ref|name=VOFCR|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}}

On 3rd of Sun's Dusk, the region celebrates a holiday named "Serpent's Dance", which seems to celebrate a now-forgotten serpent god dating back to the time when the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s lived in their former home of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]].{{ref|name=HotIB|{{Cite Book|Holidays of the Iliac Bay}}}} In {{Year|3E 417}}, the [[Lore:Montalion|Montalion]] bloodline predominated among [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] in the region.

==History==
===Merethic Era===
====Early inhabitants====
[[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] peoples in Craglorn are known to have existed since at least the [[Lore:Merethic_Era#Late_Merethic_Era|late Merethic Era]], where they regularly engaged in conflict with the local [[Lore:Iron Orcs|Iron Orcs]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Orcs of Tamriel, Volume 3}}}} Due to the harsh environments of Hammerfell, tribes of Desert Orcs and Giant Goblins controlled most of the province, far away from the 'civilized' races. 

====Ayleids====
During the late merethic and early first era, the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleids]] from Cyrodiil created several settlements in what was to become Hammerfell. Some were close to their territory of origin such as [[Lore:Molavar|Molavar]] and [[Lore:Balamath|Balamath]] in eastern Craglorn or [[Lore:Varlaisvea|Varlaisvea]] just across the Brena river,  but a few were far away such as [[Lore:Nilata|Nilata]] in the Fallen Wastes or even [[Lore:Salas En|Salas En]] on the western reaches of the [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r desert]] close to the rocky coast of [[Lore:Cape Shira|Cape Shira]].

===First Era===
====Dwemer====
[[File:ON-interior-Volenfell 03.jpg|The Hall of Volendrung in Volenfell, the site where the hammer landed|right|thumb]]
Hammerfell was originally called Hegathe by the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] and the Deathland by the early [[Lore:Nede|Nedic people]], both terms roughly meaning the same thing. In {{Year|1E 420}}, the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken Clan]] settled the region after opposing the creation of the joint Dwemer-[[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] state of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdayn]]. It is said that the [[Lore:Rourken (person)|leader]] of the clan threw his mighty hammer, [[Lore:Volendrung|Volendrung]], across the continent, and followed it west to their new home. The region eventually became known as [[Lore:Volenfell|Volenfell]], or "City of the Hammer", after the Dwemer capital built near the location of modern-day [[Lore:Gilane|Gilane]].{{ref|name=PGE3H}} The Dwemer were eventually driven from the province by an otherworldly invasion of giant goblins.{{ref|name=RGH|{{Cite Book|Notes For Redguard History}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=Early|[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview/REDGUARD.TXT|REDGUARD.TXT]] - [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall Preview|Daggerfall Preview]]}} Any remaining  Dwemer of Volenfell vanished in {{Year|1E 700}} along with the rest of the Dwemer race, and their empty cities were looted by [[Lore:Akavir|Akaviri]] and [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] pirates before being reclaimed by nature.{{ref|name=PGE3H}}

====Warrior Waves====
[[File:LO-misc-Warrior Wave Crop.png|right|thumb|Warrior Waves]]
Over a century passed before civilization would return to Volenfell, and when it did it took the form of "warrior waves" of migration from Yokuda, known as the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]]. This mass colonization occurred in four successive waves during the eighth and ninth centuries of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], and spanned approximately twenty eight years.{{ref|name=CM28RG|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 28: Ra Gada Style}}}} 

The first recorded wave of migration took place around {{Year|1E 780}}. After winning a war against Emperor [[Lore:Hira|Hira]], [[Lore:Frandar Hunding|Frandar Hunding]] and his army of renown [[Lore:Sword-singer|sword-singer]]s left their homeland and sailed to Hammerfell.{{ref|name=RTHATH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}} Hunding's flotilla of ships landed in Hammerfell and discovered vast numbers of giant [[Lore:Goblin|goblin]] strongholds littering the sands. Frandar refused to co-exist with the [[Lore:Flint-Tooth Tribe|Flint-Tooth]] tribe on their new homeland, and rallied his army to slay the beasts. Frandar was killed in one of the early conflicts, but his son [[Lore:Divad Hunding|Divad Hunding]] pushed onward and forged five mighty [[Lore:Shehai|Shehai]], which allowed him to drive out the giant goblins and conquer the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]].{{ref|name=ONTKF|[[Online:Throne Keeper Farvad|Throne Keeper Farvad]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=STDH|{{Cite Book|Shrine to Divad Hunding}}}}

The next wave is believed to have occurred around the time of Yokuda's sinking in {{Year|1E 792}}.{{ref|name=PGE3Other|{{Cite Book|Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition/Other_Lands}}}} This wave consisted of ships that departed from both the continent and its islands, including [[Lore:Yath|Yath]] and [[Lore:Akos Kasaz|Akos Kasaz]], the largest island of Yokuda and home to its capital. The bulk of the refugees, including the [[Lore:Na-Totambu|Na-Totambu]] ruling class, were put ashore on the island of [[Lore:Herne (island)|Herne]] while the Ra Gada sailed on to mainland [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in {{Year|1E 808}}.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}}{{Ref|name=PGE3Other}}{{ref|name=HotIB}} The fleet first landed at [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] on the southwest coast of Hammerfell. From Hegathe they spread along both southern and western coasts, while others continued north and east into Hammerfell itself.{{Ref|name=STJOA}} The fifty-six ships of the second wave, known as the "Tavan wave", sailed southeast towards [[Lore:Hunding Bay|Hunding Bay]] and the southern coast of Hammerfell.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband R#Ra Gada Lodestone Compass|Ra Gada Lodestone Compass]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CM28RG}}{{ref|name=ContPlat|[[Online:Contraband R|Ra Gada Style Platinum]] contraband item in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The island of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] was one of the first landfalls settled by the Yokudans.{{ref|name=ONloadStros|[[Online:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They discovered the peninsula of [[Lore:Hew's Bane|Khefrem's Boot]]. Led by Prince [[Lore:Hubalajad|Hubalajad]], he established the city of [[Lore:Abah's Landing|Abah's Landing]] and built a citadel named No Shira.{{ref|name=IDOPH|{{Cite Book|In Defense of Prince Hubalajad}}}}

To the north went a group of warrior-sailors under the leadership of Grandee [[Lore:Yaghoub|Yaghoub]], in ships they had brought from Akos Kasaz. Yaghoub was the first of the Ra Gada to discover the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] as he rounded [[Lore:Cape Shira|Cape Shira]]. Yaghoub, following the constellation of [[Lore:The Steed|the Steed]], decided to make his home on the northern coast of Hammerfell and established the city of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]].{{Ref|name=STJOA}}{{ref|name=LOTYC|{{Cite Book|Legend of the Yokudan Chargers}}}} Many of the Ra Gada kings were wealthy corsairs, and raided Breton coastal cities and merchant vessels along the southern Iliac Bay.{{ref|name=ONYokudan|[[Online:Yokudan King|Yokudan King]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ra Gada colonists from the island of Yath clashed with an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]]i clan known as the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya]], and attacked the Conrenlaya inhabitants of [[Lore:Salas En|Salas En]], and established their own settlement within the ruins.{{Ref|name=TSEE|{{Cite Book|The Salas En Expedition}}}} 

They quickly established settlements along the coasts of Cape Shira, and built a grand palace in a valley known as the {{Lore Link|Ash'abah Pass}}. 
The Yokudans pushed farther inland into [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]] and discovered the ruins of {{Lore Link|Nilata}}, which were also inhabited by [[Lore:Mer|Elves]].{{ref|name=ONloadNilata|[[Online:Nilata Ruins|Nilata Ruins]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The most ruthless group of Ra Gada was arguably the [[Lore:Anka-Ra|Anka-Ra]]. Led by the self proclaimed emperor [[Lore:Tarish-Zi|Tarish-Zi]], they cut a bloody swath across Hammerfell and all the way into [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] in their search for a new home.{{ref|name=WOTRG|{{Cite Book|Warlords of the Ra Gada}}}}{{ref|name=ONloadHelra|[[Online:Hel Ra Citadel|Hel Ra Citadel]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In their wake, the Nedes as a distinctive people disappeared from the historical record shortly after their arrival, although some accounts claim Nedic culture was already fading prior to the Ra-Gada.{{ref|name=NOTDL|{{Cite Book|Nedes of the Deathlands}}}}{{ref|name=TTPOLB|{{Cite Book|This Text Property of Leki's Blade}}}} The Anka-Ra established [[Lore:Hel Ra Citadel|Hel Ra Citadel]], a vast complex believed to be used as a training ground for their [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]].{{ref|name=CAC|{{Cite Book|Castles and Coffers Volume III: Hel Ra Citadel}}}}{{ref|name=EJ|{{Cite Book|Elenaire's Journal|ns=ON}}}}

====The Goblin Warlord====

During the First Era, {{Year|1E 874}}, when Warlord Thulgeg's army of Orcs and Goblins was driven from Hammerfell by the Redguards, the Bretons of [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Garrison]] denied them passage through the Pass and forced them to flee northeast, trudging all the way through the Dragontail Mountains before they finally reached Orsinium. Not a single goblin made its way through the pickets into [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}}{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|Thulgeg's March}}}}

Sometime after the foundation of the Redguard city of [[Lore:Ojwambu|Ojwambu]], a giant goblin warlord by the name of [[Lore:Mahgzoor Rockhand|Mahgzoor Rockhand]], became warlord of all the goblin tribes in the [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]]. Mahgzoor rose his great blade 'Bone-Hewer' and roared in a mighty voice like an earthquake, and declared that the day of vengeance, at last, was at hand. He led his "Endwise army" down the Dragontail Mountains and into Hammerfell, and the people of the [[Lore:Fallen Wastes|Fallen Wastes]] fled from this goblin army to Ojwambu, where the city became full of refugees. As the people started to leave the city to travel to the [[Lore:Alik'r|Alik'r]], goblin scouts saw that the Redguards had a considerable number of [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] warriors, and the smallest goblin scout was forced to return to Mahgzoor by the other goblin scouts. Once this scout returned, Mahgzoor struck his head off and then told the goblins to destroy the crop fields and block up the rivers so that nobody in the city could eat or drink, unaware that only Hallin and his fellow Ansei were in the city. After a long time of waiting, Mahgzoor became suspicious as the city should have starved at that point, so he called forth his goblin shamans, asking them if the Redguard warriors were just "shadows". To see if this was the case, the shamans sacrificed twin infants and sent a scullion to the East Gate, and this scullion was killed by a spear from Hallin. The shamans returned to Mahgzoor and told him that they were real warriors, and in response to this, Mahgzoor was enraged and killed the shamans. He then ordered his army to get ready to assault the city. This assault lasted for 17 days, where no Ansei survived except for Hallin and at the end of this assault, Hallin and Mahgzoor clashed swords for a whole day. Eventually, both were mortally wounded, with Hallin striking off the head of the Warlord Mahzgoor. They both died, but Hallin was smiling knowing he had won. The Redguards who had been using this time to train with the Book of Circles then marched to retake the lands captured by the goblins, renaming Ojwambu to Hallin's Stand.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The True-Told Tale of Hallin}}}}

====The Siege of Orsinium====
[[File:ON-interior-Honor's Rest 03.jpg|Statue of Gaiden Shinji and Baloth Bloodtusk|right|thumb]]
In {{Year|1E 948}}, [[Lore:Joile|King Joile]] of Daggerfall sent a letter to [[Lore:Gaiden Shinji|Gaiden Shinji]] of the [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]], which proposed a joint attack on Orsinium. Agreeing to provide aid, an alliance was formed between the Order of Diagna, the armies of Daggerfall (High Rock), and [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]] (Hammerfell). The Bretons of the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River Tribes|Bjoulsae River Tribes]] also agreed to lend their aid.{{ref|name=ONABS|[[Online:Ancestral Breton Style|Ancestral Breton Style]] Antiquity Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{Ref|name=TRHOKE|{{Cite Book|The Royal House of King Eamond}}}} The armies amassed and began a campaign for the destruction of Orsinium.{{ref|name=WJotB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3O|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}

During the 30-year campaign, which became widely known as the "Siege of Orsinium", the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] armies of Daggerfall, the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] soldiers of Sentinel, and the [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n [[Lore:Order of Diagna|Order of Diagna]] invaded the Orc homeland. The Orcs gave much resistance and proved themselves to be fearsome and brutal warriors, just as they did in the past. However, King [[Lore:Joile|Joile]] of Daggerfall already planned to betray the Redguards and invade Hammerfell after having destroyed Orsinium. He and his [[Lore:Reachman|Reachman]] general, [[Lore:Mercedene|Mercedene]] of the [[Lore:Winterborn|Winterborn]], tricked the Orcish hero [[Lore:Baloth Bloodtusk|Baloth Bloodtusk]] and his {{Lore Link|Savage Sons}}, who initially did not take part in the war, to join King Golkarr of Orsinium. Afterwards, he manipulated the leader of the Order of Diagna, [[Lore:Gaiden Shinji|Gaiden Shinji]], into having a duel with Bloodtusk over Orsinium's fate. During the duel, Mercedene ordered Joile's archers to kill both combatants, effectively removing the most powerful warriors of both Orsinium and the Order of Diagna for Joile.{{ref|name=ON}} In {{Year|1E 973}}, with Orsinium's defeat still years away, King Joile launched his invasion of Hammerfell, but he was defeated and killed at the [[Lore:Bangkorai Pass|Bangkorai Pass]] by the [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] warrior [[Lore:Makela Leki|Makela Leki]].{{ref|name=FtMSoML|{{Cite Book|From The Memory Stone of Makela Leki}}}}

The Siege of Orsinium finally ended in {{Year|1E 980}} when the allied forces breached the city's defenses, overran it, and razed it to the ground. Despite this loss, it did not mark the end of either the Orcs or the dream of Orsinium.{{ref|name=WJotB}}{{ref|name=PGE3O}} The city was rebuilt in the same location and razed again at least two more times over the following centuries.{{ref|name=ONOldOrs|[[Online:Old Orsinium|Old Orsinium]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}

====Tiber War====
[[File:LO-misc-Redguard History.gif|right|thumb]]

The best-understood episode of the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]] was the conquest of the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] [[Lore:Kingdom of Hammerfell|Kingdom of Hammerfell]]. Despite observing the might of the quickly expanding Empire, the already aged and ailing High King of Hammerfell, [[Lore:Thassad II|Thassad II]], resisted all forms of Imperial invasion.{{ref|name=RGIntroduction|[[Redguard:Introduction|Introduction]] of ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} It was only after his death in {{Year|2E 862}}{{ref|name=PGE1Ham}} that Hammerfell began to weaken; not from an external invasion, but rather from an internal revolution. It was a civil war between the two most prominent factions: the rebellious [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]] and the loyalist [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]], the latter of which supported Thassad's heir, [[Lore:A'tor|A'Tor]].

After initial defeats, the Forebears signed a [[#Forebear Truce|pact]] with the Emperor, granting him minor territory concessions in exchange for aiding them in the civil war. The already weakened Crowns still provided heavy resistance to invading Imperial forces, until at last they were cornered at [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] in {{Year|2E 864}}. The [[#Battle of Stros M'Kai|Battle of Stros M'Kai]] was won thanks to Admiral Richton's shrewd tactics and the aid of Septim's dragon vassal, [[Lore:Nahfahlaar|Nafaalilargus]]. The Empire swiftly took control of Hammerfell, imposing several Provisional Governors to rule different regions. 

Later that same year, an uprising led by siblings [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]] and Iszara saw the Restless League come out of hiding. They stormed the Palace, and Cyrus himself cornered Provisional Governor Amiel Richton and Imperial Emissary Dram aboard a [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemeri]] dirigible. Cyrus killed them both with the Soul Sword, which was enchanted with the soul of Prince A'tor.{{ref|name=RGRazePalace|[[Redguard:Raze the Palace|Raze the Palace]] quest in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} This act was allegedly the third incarnation of the [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]].{{ref|name=VoFitE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Thereafter, Tiber Septim came to Stros M'Kai personally to sign a treaty that held more favorable terms for the Redguards. Iszara represented Hammerfell, and was guided by the wisdom of Prince A'Tor via the Soul Sword.

It is later reflected that the Redguards become a "people without a past" because of the Tiber War and the Redguards' being "stalwart guardians of Hammerfell" during the conflict.{{Ref|name=RDBA}}{{ref|name=RGCompanion|{{Cite Book|The Redguard Companion|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|9}} Having held on to the ground for which its people "shed tears of blood", land and rock itself became under the Redguards' command.{{Ref|name=RDBA|[[Arena:Redguard|Redguard]] description in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

====War of the Bend'r-mahk (3E 397)====
[[File:SK-narrative-Start1.png|right|thumb]]
The [[Lore:War of the Bend'r-mahk|War of the Bend'r-mahk]] was a conflict that started in {{Year|3E 397}} during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] period between the provinces of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]].{{ref|name=SKmap|[[:File:Shadowkey Map.jpg|Map of the High Rock, Hammerfell and Skyrim border]] in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}} High Rock and Hammerfell fought as allies in the war against invading Skyrim forces.{{Ref|name=OBLathon|[[Oblivion:Lathon|Lathon]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}

During the war [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] defeated the forces of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] and claimed many miles of the [[Lore:Western Reach|eastern parts of those provinces]], including the cities of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] and [[Lore:Jehanna|Jehanna]].{{ref|name=PGE3Sky|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} Some of these territories were claimed to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim.{{ref|name=PGE3High|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|High Rock}}}}

The most peculiar situation in Hammerfell's territory in the war arose in the city of [[Lore:Dragonstar|Dragonstar]], where the city was divided into western (Hammerfell) and eastern (Skyrim) sections of the city, walled off from each other, with each section having its own government and a desire to take control of the entire city. even resorting to acts of terrorism.{{ref|name=PGE3Sky}} One such act was members of the Mages Guild of Dragonstar using noxious enchantments on Skyrim soldiers.{{ref|name=TMG}} The Thieves' Guild of Dragonstar were also known to be aligned with Hammerfell despite being in Skyrim's section of the city.{{ref|[[SK:Porliss Thieves' Guild|Porliss Thieves' Guild]] quest in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}}{{ref|[[Shadowkey:Clever Sed|Clever Sed]]'s dialogue in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}} The national divide in Dragonstar persisted for thirty odd years after the conflicts conclusion.{{ref|name=PGE3Sky}}

The war resulted in a deeper division between the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] factions of [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] and [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]], as the Forebear cities showed no inclination to help the Crown cities assaulted and occupied by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]], which in turn was a response to the lack of help by Crowns to the Forebears during [[Lore:Camoran Usurper's Invasion|the Camoran Usurper's invasion]].{{ref|name=PGE3H}}

The Redguards harbor a fierce grudge against [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and yearn for a chance to take back land that was seized from them.{{ref|name=PGE3H}} After peace was established, a commission was created to repair the damage done by the war.{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 7}}}}

In {{Year|4E 11–15}}, following the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] and the destabilization of the Third Empire, Orsinium was once again sacked by the combined forces of Hammerfell and High Rock, and many Orc refugees fled east into [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|name=LoS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls}}}} The kingdom eventually reformed at a new location between Skyrim and Hammerfell,{{ref|name=SRLoading|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} where it would be guarded by the [[Lore:Seventh Legion|Seventh]] and [[Lore:Fifteenth Legion|Fifteenth]] [[Lore:Imperial Legion#Known Legions|Legions]].{{ref|name=LoS}}

At some point in time, Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede I|Titus Mede I]] went to war with Hammerfell, where he met one of his most trusted generals, [[Lore:Takar|Takar]].{{Ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|Part 3, Chapter 4}}

====Night of Green Fire====
In {{Year|4E 42}}, Altmer dissidents who had fled to Sentinel from the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] in [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Alinor]] were attacked by Thalmor operatives in what became known as the [[Lore:Night of Green Fire|Night of Green Fire]]. Their efforts to fight back against the Thalmor were in vain and, by the time [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] soldiers arrived, the entire refugee district of the city had been destroyed. According to Legate Fasendil, an Altmer stationed in Sentinel at the time as a member of the Imperial Legion, the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] was not content with killing dissidents at home, so they went to Hammerfell to finish the job.{{ref|name=LF|[[Skyrim:Legate Fasendil|Legate Fasendil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Thalmor agents were also harassing and possibly murdering refugees in [[Lore:Balfiera|Balfiera]] and Sentinel around {{Year|4E 48}}; the victims were all of mixed blood or had associations considered by the Dominion  to be "unclean."{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}

====Great War====
Over a century later, the Thalmor of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] would attempt to seize control of Hammerfell from the Third Empire. Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]]'s refusal to concede to Thalmor demands triggered the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] in {{Year|4E 171}}. Aldmeri forces crossed western Cyrodiil and invaded Hammerfell's southern coast, easily sweeping through the scattered resistance put up by the disunited Redguards. Initially only seeking to conquer Hammerfell, the Dominion launched a full-scale invasion of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] when it realized the Empire was weaker than previously believed. Imperial fortunes took a turn for the better in Hammerfell two years later, when a Forebear army from Sentinel broke the siege of the Crown city of Hegathe, leading to the reconciliation of the two factions. After the Empire's hard-won victory in the Battle of the Red Ring in {{Year|4E 175}}, the Empire and Dominion signed the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]] and ended the war. Outraged that the terms of the treaty called for a large portion of southern Hammerfell to be turned over to the Dominion, however, the Redguards soundly rejected it. Titus II was forced to officially renounce Hammerfell as an Imperial province to preserve the peace, and fighting between the Aldmeri and Redguards continued. In {{Year|4E 180}}, the Dominion agreed to the [[Lore:Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai|Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai]] and finally withdrew from the region, leaving Hammerfell an independent albeit devastated nation.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

==Politics==
Redguards are divided between the cosmopolitan, open minded Forebears and the traditionalist, isolationist Crowns. The Forebears are the descendants of the Yokudan military class, the Ra Gada, and are generally open to outside cultures such as the Imperials and Bretons; the Crowns are the descendants of the Yokudan noble class and still prefer to shun outsiders, vying to preserve the customs and culture of their ancestors.

==Architecture==
[[File:ON-place-Hallin's Stand.jpg|thumb|right|[[Lore:Hallin's Stand|Hallin's Stand]], an example of the Redguard architecture]]
Redguard buildings vary from simple tents to the sturdy domed structures of the cities which incorporate many Yokudan advancements. The latter are renowned for their rigidity, flexibility, heat transference, and general ornate beauty. They are much easier to repair than most Imperial stonemasonry. While much Imperial construction requires demolition and rebuilding after conflict, many of the Yokudan accent pieces, such as the flying staircases or ornamental domes, are designed to crumble after an attack or an earthquake, which allows them to be easily replaced in sections afterward. However, these structures are still solidly built; they stand tall and firm against the harsh environment of Hammerfell.{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell}} They are characterized by thickly-built walls to help insulate against the heat of the desert, as well as a carefully-planned system of ventilation ducts. These ducts are built alongside windows high on the walls, and are covered by horizontal slats which keep out the sun while permitting the wind to enter. Each building is designed to channel even the slightest breeze from these ducts down to the floor.{{ref|name=CraftMotif6Redguard|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 6: Redguard Style}}}}

Redguard stables have been recognized for their lavishness and the ingenious design which makes them perfect for the environmental hazards the Redguards face. Their enormous mausoleums are thought to be the purest, undiluted representations of Yokudan architecture in their society. The most famous example, [[Lore:Tu'whacca's Throne|Tu'whacca's Throne]] in the Alik'r, is also a monument to the many Yokudans who died when the continent was destroyed, and it receives many pilgrims seeking to pay their respects.{{ref|name=EGTHammerfell}}

The buildings of the Redguards are notable for being heavily ornamented, with engravings and golden domes. However, some of the cities in Hammerfell are built in different styles: for example, Dragonstar consists of Nordic buildings, whereas the Colovian style can be seen in Elinhir.{{ref|name=ON}}

Since Hammerfell was home to many civilizations in the past, many different types of ruins can be found throughout the province, among them [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]], [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] and [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokudan]] ruins. In Craglorn, there are also [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] and [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] ruins.{{ref|name=ON}}

==Flora==
In the Alik'r, southern Bangkorai and Stros M'Kai, typical desert vegetation like palm trees and cacti can be found. In Craglorn, crooked trees grow on the central dry plains, whereas the eastern lands near Elinhir and northern mountains are more temperate.{{ref|name=ON}}

*[[Lore:Aloe Vera|Aloe Vera]] are tall and leafy plants that are found mainly in southern [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. The [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudans]] were well aware of the herb's potential in medicine, often using it to staunch the flow of blood and to heal wounds. It can be mixed with other ingredients to create elixirs, like combining the aloe's lacquer with a crushed leaf can lessen the effects of pox.{{Ref|name=FoH|{{Cite Book|Flora of Hammerfell}}}} 

*[[Lore:Angelica|Angelica]] - Known among those who dwell in Hammerfell as a delicacy when crystalised. It has been used to cure flatulence.{{Ref|name=FoH}}

*Arabesque Nuts can be found growing wild in the Hammerfell mountains. Their succulent nuts are often the only nourishment of adventurers who find themselves in the wilderness without rations. It is believed they have magical properties. When they are crushed and mixed with the milk of the agile footed mountain goat, a potion that allows the user to glide safely above the ground can be made.{{Ref|name=TDC}}{{rp|127}}

*[[Lore:Basil|Basil]] - This herb is beneficial for reducing swelling in the nose. It is applied as a poultice over the affected area.{{Ref|name=FoH}}

==Fauna==
The hot and dry lands of Hammerfell are home to many fierce creatures. The Alik'r Desert is inhabited by [[Lore:Duneripper|dunerippers]], [[Lore:Assassin Beetle|assassin beetles]], [[Lore:Giant Scorpion|giant scorpions]], [[Lore:Crocodile|crocodiles]], [[Lore:Jackal|jackals]] and [[Lore:Harpy|harpies]], and the local people insist that their vast plain of sand is home to a multitude of [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]]s.{{ref|name=DUG}} [[Lore:Wasp|Giant wasps]], [[Lore:Lamia|lamias]] and [[Lore:Lion|lions]] can be found in Bangkorai. [[Lore:Sep Adder|Sep adders]],{{ref|name=TotSA|{{Cite Book|Triumph of the Sep Adder}}}} [[Lore:Dragon Frog|dragon frog]]s, [[Lore:Haj Mota|haj mota]], [[Lore:Heron|herons]], jackals, and [[Lore:Kotu Gava|kotu gava]] can be found in [[Lore:Hew's Bane|Hew's Bane]]. In somewhat cooler Craglorn, one can find [[Lore:Welwa|Welwa]], [[Lore:Duneracer|duneracers]], or more peaceful [[Lore:Camel|camels]], [[Lore:Daggerback|daggerbacks]], [[Lore:Fellrunner|fellrunners]], [[Lore:Sandroach|sandroaches]], and [[Lore:Fennec Fox|fennec foxes]].{{ref|name=ON}} The [[Lore:Western Reach|Western Reach]] is home to several reptilian creatures, such as the [[Lore:Wickeder|wickeder]], the [[Lore:Bounder|bounder]], the [[Lore:Clawrunner|clawrunner]], and the [[Lore:Wormmouth|wormmouth]]. [[Lore:Wolves|Wolves]] and [[Lore:Floater|floaters]] also reside in the region.{{ref|name=Shadowkeycreatures|[[Shadowkey:Creatures|Creatures]] in ''[[Shadowkey:Shadowkey|Shadowkey]]''}}
