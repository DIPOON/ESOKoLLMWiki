# Lore:Demiprince

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Trail|Gods}}[[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[File:ON-npc-Fa-Nuit-Hen.jpg|thumb|right|Fa-Nuit-Hen, a demiprince]]</noinclude>
A [[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] (sometimes spelled '''Demi-Prince'''){{ref|name=Sheo|[[Online:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Chaos Magic|Chaos Magic]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} is the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] offspring of a [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] or [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lord]] and a lesser entity such as a mortal. They are said to emerge from a unique conjoining of purest [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|creatia]] from the realm of a Daedric Prince or Daedra Lord and "something more", including either the essence of a [[Lore:Souls|soul]] in case the other party is a mortal, or an influence more esoteric and hard to put in mortal terms. The formation of a Demiprince is always an intentional act on the part of their Princely parent, and cannot occur by accident.{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} Though their origins lie with one of the Daedric Princes or Daedra Lords, some Demiprinces like Fa-Nuit-Hen are also described as having existed since before time began, as is by and large the case with greater Daedra.{{ref|name=ONTF}}

Although they often inherit aspects of their princely parent, they are notably eccentric and can be lacking in characteristics such as foresight, memory, cruelty, or willfulness. Some acquire, or are given, minor [[Lore:Planes of Oblivion|realms]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] to rule, although many of these pocket realities are bizarre even by the standards of Oblivion. Much like the Daedric Princes, Demiprinces shape their domains via their will, able to manifest anything they can visualize clearly or determine how the realm interacts with time.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}}{{ref|name=ONTF}}{{ref|name=Nuit-Hen|[[ON:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:Maelstrom Arena (quest)|Maelstrom Arena]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Though they enjoy great power and prestige in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], the type of attention this can attract is said to be a double-edged blade, for this reason their identities tend to remain unknown, even among the Daedra.{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}} According to Demiprince [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]], Demiprinces far outstrip any mortal in power and consequence, however, they are still "but trivial compared to the Greater Princes".{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR2}}

A Demiprince that is considered scion of a Daedric Prince doesn't always arise from the creatia of that Prince's realm originally, as Daedric Princes have been known to adopt other spirits as scions even if their realm's creatia wasn't involved in their creation. Some sources claim Fa-Nuit-Hen is one such Demiprince, adopted by [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]], as no spirit could continue claiming to be a Prince's scion for so long without that Prince's approval.{{Ref|name=Whim|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}}

Some demiprinces are known to visit [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] for the express purpose of interacting with mortals, an act usually abhorred by regular Daedra.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=ONTF}}<noinclude>

Known Demiprinces include:

*[[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]], the Multiplier of Motions Known, is a known demiprince.{{ref|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 1}}}} He is the scion of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] and rules over his pocket realm, the [[Lore:Maelstrom|Maelstrom]].{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}}
*The [[Lore:Demi-Prince of Pastries|Demi-Prince of Pastries]], of whom little is known, save that she hadn't been seen for some time circa {{Year|2E 582}}. [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] considers her to be one of the few worthy of wielding his [[Lore:Wabbajack|Wabbajack]].{{ref|name=Sheo}}
*[[Lore:Dearola|Dearola]], the Daughter of [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], is referred to as a demigoddess. The Song of [[Lore:Gwyna|Gwyna]] tells that she intermingled with [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]], and one of her descendants was Chief [[Lore:Rowolan|Rowolan]], the enemy of the [[Lore:Horn-Stride Clan|Horn-stride clan]], whose mythical prowess was attributed to his lineage. The song, however, has different versions, all of which lead to Rowolan's bloodline ending.{{ref|name=TTWT|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, III}}}}

==Notes==
* [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]] is said to be the Demiprince of All Winds.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga|ns_base=GEN}}}} The word demiprince is a misnomer in this context, as the term "[[Lore:Demigod|demigod]]" more accurately describes him.{{Ref|name=LAFNJTR}}
*There are several figures who are believed to be the progeny of Daedric Princes, but it is unknown if they are demiprinces. Such individuals include: [[Lore:Alandro Sul|Alandro Sul]], [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], [[Lore:Darien Gautier|Darien Gautier]], [[Lore:Emmeg Gro-Kayra|Emmeg Gro-Kayra]], [[Lore:Haymon Camoran|Haymon Camoran]], [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]], [[Lore:Molag Grunda|Molag Grunda]], and {{Lore Link|Ozozzachar}}.
**The main story of a ''[[Wikipedia:Dungeons & Dragons|Dungeons & Dragons]]'' [[Daggerfall:Pen & Paper Worldbuilding|homebrew campaign]] (ran by [[General:Julian LeFay|Julian LeFay]] during ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''{{'}}s development) was centered around an adversary called the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]] who had risen to power and was on a trek with this supernatural army conquering [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and was the scion of a Breton woman and [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], making this the first instance in the series of a Daedra and mortal having a child together.{{ref|name=TeddersInterview|group=UOL|1=[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzjvWQCND54 ''Interview with Ted Peterson by Indigo Gaming'']}} When the Usurper was later added to the game itself, his backstory was left unclear, with his parentage being established as a legend instead.{{ref|name=DCSG|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|8}}
*Demiprinces are also called Daedra Lords,{{ref|name=ONTF|[[Online:Tutor Riparius|Tutor Riparius]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but like Daedric Princes, all Demiprinces are Daedric Lords, not all Daedra Lords are Demiprinces.
*An anonymous seemingly immortal merchant known as {{Lore Link|the Impresario}} was rumored to be a demiprince who disguised herself as a mortal in order to travel Tamriel. This was one of many fanciful theories put forward to explain her penchant for trading vast amounts of treasure in return for tales of valor and victory. She herself did not place much importance on whether or not she was mortal.{{ref|name=LATH|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - The Impresario}}}}
