[[Lore:A'tor|Prince A'tor]] was the son of High King [[Lore:Thassad II|Thassad II]] of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]]. After his father's death in {{Year|2E 862}}, the [[Lore:Forebears|Forebear]] faction, led by [[Lore:Volag|Baron Volag]], took control of [[Lore:Sentinel|Sentinel]], the capital of Hammerfell. Prince A'tor rallied his [[Lore:Crowns|Crown]] supporters at his principality of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]] and launched a bloody attempt to recapture the city by sea. However, he was taken by surprise by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s assistance of the Forebears, and was eventually defeated by their combined forces at the [[Lore:Battle of Hunding Bay|Battle of Hunding Bay]], near Stros M'Kai.{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}{{ref|name=RGIntroduction|[[Redguard:Introduction|Introduction]] of ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}

Mortally wounded, the [[Lore:Voa|Archmage Voa]] placed his soul within a soul gem, disguised as an amulet in the shape of a dragon.{{ref|name=RGIntroduction}} Following an attempt to resurrect him, his soul was later fused with his longsword to create the [[Lore:Soul Sword|Soul Sword]], which was instrumental in liberating the island of Stros M'Kai from the clutches of [[Lore:Amiel Richton|Lord-Admiral Richton]]. From the Soul Sword, he advised his lover, now Queen-Regent [[Lore:Iszara|Iszara]], in rulership.{{ref|name=Raze|[[Redguard:Raze the Palace|Raze the Palace]] quest in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on [https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2010/12/26/decrypting-the-elder-scrolls.aspx Decrypting The Elder Scrolls] [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}} He was believed to be an aspect of the [[Lore:HoonDing|HoonDing]], along with [[Lore:Cyrus|Cyrus]].{{Ref|name=VoFitE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in the Empire}}}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}
