# Lore:Mask of Sotha Sil

The [[Lore:Mask of Sotha Sil|Mask of Sotha Sil]] is the '''blessed mask'''{{Ref|name=CMDS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 56: Apostle|ns_base=Online}}}} (also known as the '''killing mask''',{{ref|name=TTISV8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} or '''warmaker helm'''){{Ref|group=UOL|name=Sehthelm|[[:File:GEN-misc-Sotha Sil, Mystery of Morrowind.webp|Seht Sketch]] — ''[[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]]''}} of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]. It embodies his idea for a ''Anuvanna'si'' (Tamriel Final), whose one of many goals was where life would undergo a synthesis of organic and non-organic nature{{Ref|name=SSNM|[[ON:Sotha Sil Novelty Mask|Sotha Sil Novelty Mask]]'s item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=twitch1|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Live Lore of the Clockwork City|Lore of the Clockwork City]] on [[General:ESO Live|ESO Live]]}} (akin to the [[Lore:Fabricants|Fabricants]]).{{ref|name=WitCC|[https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/26199 What is the Clockwork City?] on the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}} Indeed, overtime, Sotha Sil's body would become more and more synthetic, and his mask also changed to undergo this transformation. His followers, the [[Lore:Clockwork Apostles|Clockwork Apostles]], mimic this synthesis with modifying their bodies, as well as craft their headwear in the Clockwork God's image.{{Ref|name=CMDS}}

It is one of the [[Lore:Masks of the Tribunal|Masks of the Tribunal]], the other two being [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s [[Lore:Mask of Almalexia|War Mask]], and [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]'s [[Lore:Vivec's Ash Mask|Ash Mask]].{{ref|name=SRMT|{{Cite Book|Masks of the Tribunal (book)}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
It is unknown when Sotha Sil created his mask, but in the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]], his Elegiac Replication has a hologram that depicts him with it around the time he and [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] became great friends.{{Ref|name=SSANH|[[:File:ON-npc-Sotha Sil and Nerevar.jpg|Sotha Sil and Nerevar]] hologram at [[Online:Elegiac Replication|Elegiac Replication]]}} Though sources vary on its authenticity, it is claimed that Sotha Sil along with his fellow Tribunes murdered Nerevar.{{ref|name=NaRM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} He chose not to wear his mask during this ordeal as he held Nerevar's severed face.{{Ref|name=FM|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Foul Murder|ns_base=GEN}}}}

Sotha Sil had an intensive love for Dwarven industry,{{ref|name=ESODF|[[Online:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and drew inspiration for them in his creations. Their influence in his work is rumored to be traceable to the time before their disappearance, with his usage of clockwork dreughs build in the Dwemer's automaton's image that were used to assault them.{{Ref|name=TSLT|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} One of his earlier mask designs depicts a more Dwemeri-like design,{{ref|name=ONSSCG|{{Item Link|quality=legendary|Sotha Sil, the Clockwork God|id=134249}} statue from [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} which would later get sleeker and thinner by the events of the [[Lore:Daedric Triad|Daedric War]] in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].

The practice of designing and wearing [[Lore:Daedric Faces|masks modeled after important figures]] in Dunmeri culture was not uncommon.{{ref|name=DFO|[[MW:Daedric Face of Terror|Daedric Face of Terror]],[[MW:Daedric Face of Inspiration|Daedric Face of Inspiration]], and [[MW:Daedric Face of God|Daedric Face of God]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=DFTDE|group=UOL|[[:File:LO-misc-Daedric Helms.jpg|Morrowind Daedric Helms Concept Art]]}} Replicas of Sotha Sil's mask were worn by his followers as they unraveled his esoteric teachings, while travelers to Vvardenfell donned them for complex jokes after visiting a tavern.{{Ref|name=SSNM}}

By the end of the Third Era, though he was generally reclusive, Sotha Sil became detached from Tamriel.{{ref|name=MWHelas|[[Tribunal:Mehra Helas#Dialogue|Mehra Helas]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} By the time Almalexia came to kill him for her goal: to become the one true god of the Dunmer, uniting all of her people under one faith and authority and destroying any who interfered,{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Sotha Sil had nearly fully mechanized his body, with a full covering metallic mask to go with it. After the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] defeated Almalexia, her body as well as Sotha Sil's were retrieved from the Clockwork City by Vivec, who interred them in the proper Velothi fashion.{{ref|name=MKPosts|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

Despite the rise of the New Temple, worship of the Tribunal continued into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] and was driven underground. These cults were considered heretical by wider Dunmeri society and were persecuted by the New Temple.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} One cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [[Lore:Raven Rock|Raven Rock]]. The cultists planned to use old propaganda to restore widespread Tribunal worship,{{ref|name=GotT|[[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal (quest)|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Her Word Against Theirs|Her Word Against Theirs]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and sought to gather and preserve many relics of the old Tribunal Temple.

Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was alerted to the cult's existence when they attempted to recover several additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.{{ref|name=GotT}} The Mask of Sotha Sil was tracked down to the Dwemer ruins of [[Lore:Kagrenzel|Kagrenzel]]. It was initially retrieved by Curate Melita of the heretics who got caught in the ruin's trap. Successfully able to navigate Kagrenzel's [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]] inhabitants, Melita made it down stream into Stony Creek Cave, where she was apprehended by bandits, the leader who was the wielder of [[Lore:Ruin's Edge|Ruin's Edge]]. The Dragonborn navigated Kagrenzel, defeated the bandits, and retrieved both Artifacts.{{Ref|[[SR:The Edge of Ruin|The Edge of Ruin]] quest coinciding with [[SR:Ghosts of the Tribunal|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] quest from [[SR:Creation Club|Creation Club]]}}
