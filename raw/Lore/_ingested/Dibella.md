==Mythology==
According to the creation myth presented in the ''Anuad'', Dibella and the Aedra (gods) were born from the mingled blood of the primal forces [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], and therefore have a capacity for both good and evil, in contrast to the Daedra, who were born solely from the blood of Padomay and thus have less affinity for mortal affairs.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} The formation of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], the God of Time, from the mingled blood of the brothers Anu and Padomay facilitated the formation of Dibella and the other gods as they learnt to structure themselves. ''Shezarr's Song'', a [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodilic]] creation myth, acclaims the creation of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] to the sacrifices of the Aedra.{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} Dibella and the gods were subsequently bound to the Earth Bones as a result.{{Ref|name=AeD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}} Followers of the cult known as the Mythic Dawn deem Aedra like Dibella to be false gods, claiming she and others betrayed [[Lore:Shor|Lorkhan]].{{ref|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Each heavenly body in the night sky is attributed to an Aedra. [[#Planet|Dibella's]] is close to that of [[Lore:Mara|Mara]], a love goddess more closely associated with motherhood in mythology.{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=Orrery|[[:File:OB-interior-Orrery (labeled).jpg|The Orrery]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==Teachings==
Dibella, the embodiment of beauty, teaches mortals to seek truth through beauty and worship.{{ref|name=DF|Events of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Those who follow the teachings of Dibella, and propagate beauty and promote harmony, are granted charm and grace.{{ref|name=DF}} The Goddess teaches that mortals should give themselves to love,{{ref|[[Oblivion:Trevaia|Trevaia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} and commands them to open their hearts to "the noble secrets of art and love. Treasure the gifts of friendship. Seek joy and inspiration in the mysteries of love".{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ten Commands: Nine Divines}}}} Dibella holds no limit on the number of lovers one may have, but demands focus on the quality of the essence of love, not the quantity.{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}} She teaches that, "No matter the seed, if the shoot is nurtured with love, will not the flower be beautiful?", and blesses the love of those which is pure and untainted.{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}}

Followers of Dibella discourage relations with [[Lore:Undead|undead]], such as [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]], following the teachings of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]]'s priests that vampires have impure spirits.{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}} 

The Goddess is believed to make her will known through her Sybil, who is revealed to her priesthood via the ceremony known as the Exalted Protocol of the Dibellan Sybil.{{ref|name=SRHamal|[[Skyrim:Hamal|Hamal]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The ceremony is known only to the priesthood of Dibella, and is kept secret from the general population, including acolytes of other Divines.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}} The newly-chosen Sybil may be a child, who would then leave her parents to live with the priesthood.{{ref|name=TTOD|Events of [[Skyrim:The Heart of Dibella|The Heart of Dibella]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Worship==
[[File:ON-prerelease-Passion Dancer Garment.png|left|thumb|Dibella-inspired outfits]] [[File:ON-crown store-Dibella's Doll Mask Pack.jpg|Dibella's doll masks (ESO)|right|thumb]]
Dibella's relationship with her worshippers is arguably more personal, in contrast to more formal relationships between other worshippers and their Divines.{{Ref|name=RCW|{{Cite Book|Reflections on Cult Worship}}}} Dibella is known as the Divine who "pays Men in Moans".{{ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Invocation}}}} Critics of Dibella consider her overly lustful.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Song of Hrormir}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Height (v7)}}}}

A number of cults dedicated to Dibella can be found in [[#Worship in Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], [[#Worship in High Rock|High Rock]], [[#Worship in Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] and [[#Worship in Skyrim|Skyrim]], and are varied in their focus.{{Ref|name=VOFT|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith in Tamriel}}}} Some cults of Dibella focus on women, artists or aesthetics, while others concentrate on [[Lore:Sex|erotic instruction]].{{Ref|name=VOFT}} The latter practicing their "Dibellan Arts" have been known to bestow a Mark of Dibella upon the supplicant.{{ref|name=SR|Events of [[Skyrim:Caught Red Handed|Caught Red Handed]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=MOD|[[Skyrim:Mark of Dibella|Mark of Dibella]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} When worshipers of Dibella wish to honor the Lady of Love by engaging in anonymous bestowal of affection, they don a porcelain Dibella's doll mask.{{ref|[[Online:Dibella's Doll Mask, Human / Elf|Dibella's Doll Mask, Human / Elf]] hat description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

A temple dedicated to Dibella is often called a [[Lore:House of Dibella|House of Dibella]].{{ref|name=DF}} A House will typically have three or four white cats within, as they are sacred to the goddess.{{Ref|name=MCC|[[Online:Milady's Cloud Cat|Milady's Cloud Cat]] pet description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Worship in Black Marsh===
[[File:ON-place-Temple of Dibella.jpg|The Temple of Dibella in Gideon|right|thumb]]
The majority of the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] venerates the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]], and, in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], worship of Dibella and the other gods was limited to a small minority of those who had assimilated to [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] culture.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Argonians}}}}

The [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]], the indigenous humans of Black Marsh, are known to have worshipped Dibella.{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}} They viewed her as part of a trinity known as Dibe-Mara-Kin, one of the "mothers in the Around-Us", together with Mara and [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]].{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Water-getting Girl and the Inverse Tiger|ns_base=GEN}}}} Dibella was held in high regard by the Kothringi, and a Temple of Dibella was located in the city of [[Lore:Gideon|Gideon]] in the Second Era.{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}}{{ref|[[ON:Gideon|Gideon]]'s [[ON:Temple of Dibella|Temple of Dibella]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

===Worship in Cyrodiil===
[[File:BL-misc-Dibella.png|Imperial depiction of Dibella|left|thumb|200px]][[FIle:ON-crown store-Dibella, Blessed Lady Statuette.jpg|right|thumb|Imperial statuette of Dibella]][[File:ON-npc-Fentula_Caecus.jpg|right|thumb|An Imperial priestess of Dibella]]
Dibella is venerated as a member of the Cyrodilic pantheon.{{Ref|name=VOF}} In Cyrodiil, she has nearly a dozen different cults, whose beliefs emphasize a particular devotion to women, artistry, aesthetics, or erotic instruction.{{ref|''[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue D|Dibella]]'' dialogue topic in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Priestesses of Dibella around [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]] painted their ankles in the same fashion as the extinct [[Lore:Keptu|Keptu]] circa {{Year|1E 2762}}.{{Ref|name=TSe|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Shonni-etta|ns_base=General}}}} 

A Chapel of Dibella was located in the city of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] in the Second and [[Lore:Third Era|Third Eras]].{{ref|name=ESO|[[ON:Great Chapel of Dibella|Great Chapel of Dibella]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OB|[[Oblivion:Chapel of Dibella|Chapel of Dibella]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} At times, the clergy of the Anvil chapel have practiced a morning ritual of dancing nude through the city streets, and within the Chapel, it was common to see bare-breasted priestesses or nude priests groping women. However, chapel practices have had more modest periods.{{ref|[[Online:Valentina the Crone|Valentina the Crone]]'s dialogue in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, the Chapel was desecrated by [[Lore:Auroran#Auroran|Aurorans]], Daedra in the service of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], and several members of the priesthood were killed.{{ref|name=KOTN|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}} 

At this time, wayshrines dedicated to the Goddess could be found throughout the countryside of Cyrodiil, and a statue honoring Dibella stood in the Arboretum district of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{ref|name=Wayshrines|[[Oblivion:Wayshrines|Wayshrines]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=Arboretum|[[Oblivion:Arboretum|Arboretum]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Imperial Priestesses of Dibella may travel around Cyrodiil at the behest of the goddess to act as healers for the sick or to assist supplicants in other ways as needed&mdash;as a doting mother, a lover, or simply a friend.{{ref|[[ON:Fentula Caecus|Fentula Caecus]]'s dialogue in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}}

The Blushing Brush was a sect of Dibella originating in Anvil,{{Ref|name=Mornabeth|[[ON:Mornabeth|Mornabeth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and active in [[Lore:Solstice (island)|Solstice]], made up of artists who sought beauty in all things and expressed it through their art. They viewed [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]] as a corruption of Dibella's ideals, and many of their members were former followers of Sanguine who had since converted.{{Ref|name=HPC|[[ON:High Priest Caroril|High Priest Caroril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{NewLine}}

===Worship in Hammerfell===
Dibella is the patron deity of the region of [[Lore:Tigonus|Tigonus]],{{ref|name=DF}} and is popular among [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] women of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Hammerfell}}}} However, she is only venerated by the more cosmopolitan [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]] of Redguard society, who began to accept her into their pantheon under the [[Lore:Second Empire|Reman Empire]].{{Ref|name=VOFTF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}}{{ref|name=VOFCR|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}} In the late Third Era, Houses of Dibella could be found in major urban centers throughout the province. They are led by a Patriarch and allied to the [[Lore:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]], a religious organization dedicated to Mara and her teachings. The Order of the Lily was a knightly order dedicated to Dibella which protected her temples against its adversaries, such as the followers of [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]], the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] of Hedonism.{{ref|name=DF}}

===Worship in High Rock===
[[File:DF-npc-Dibella Statue.png|A Breton statue of Dibella|right|thumb|200px]][[File:ON-crown store-Dibella's Crescendo Tam.jpg|left|thumb|Dibella's Crescendo Tam, worn by Breton followers of Dibella]]
Dibella is the patron deity of the [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] regions [[Lore:Koegria|Koegria]] and [[Lore:Menevia|Menevia]],{{ref|Patron deities for [[Daggerfall:Koegria|Koegria]] and [[Daggerfall:Menevia|Menevia]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and is venerated as a member of the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] pantheon.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Bretons}}}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the worship of Dibella was criticized and discouraged by some, notably Father Pitof of the Cathedral of Daggerfall, who warned of the "charms of Dibella".{{Ref|name=AVAYQ}} However, Houses of Dibella persisted in High Rock just as they did in Hammerfell.{{ref|Faction information for the [[Daggerfall:House of Dibella|House of Dibella]] and the [[Daggerfall:Order of the Lily|Order of the Lily]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Members of the clergy in High Rock are known to dance nude in front of worshippers, and congregants may propose to become "ardor-partners" with each other.{{ref|name=DMR|{{Cite Book|Dibella's Mysteries and Revelations}}}}

===Worship in Skyrim===
[[File:SR-concept-Moth Mural.jpg|Dibella as the Moth carved in stone|right|thumb]][[File:SR-item-Statue of Dibella.jpg|A Nordic statue of Dibella|left|thumb]]
In the totemic religion of [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]], precursor to the [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] pantheon, the Silver [[Lore:Moth|Moth]] has been considered to correspond with Dibella. This worship continued in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], where the Atmoran animal gods eventually became known by their modern names.{{Ref|name=DATN|{{Cite Book|Divines and the Nords}}}}{{Ref|name=TSOG|{{Cite Book|The Song of Gods}}}} Dibella is considered one of the Hearth Gods that watch over the present world as the Goddess of Beauty.{{Ref|name=DATN}} Her worship is varied as individual cults venerate different facets of her spheres of influence.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Nords}}}} In some stories, she is acclaimed as the Bed-Wife of Shor.{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Shor son of Shor|ns_base=GEN}}}}

The Cult of Mother Moth was an ancient religion of Atmora that revered Mother Moth as a symbol of patience, excellence, softness, and love. Followers believed she governed both light and shadow, with her many eyes watching over all of Atmora. Devotees honored her through nightly rituals involving fire and burnings, symbolizing their desire for light and their acceptance of darkness. They saw light as meaningful only through contrast with the night and believed that by offering their devotion through flames, they could satisfy Mother Moth’s insatiable hunger.{{Ref|name=TACW|{{Cite Book|The Atmoran Cult Writings}}}}

In the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], shrines dedicated to the Goddess could be found both in the wilderness and in urban centers, such as at the Temple of the Divines in [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], and at the Temple of Dibella in the city of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]],{{ref|name=SRTemple|[[Skyrim:Temple of Dibella|Temple of Dibella]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Worship in the Reach===
[[File:ON-place-Markarth Wayshrine (Heart's Week) 02.jpg|thumb|left|Dibellan decorations in Markarth during the celebrations of the Heart's Day in the Second Era]][[File:SR-npc-Fjotra.jpg|thumb|right|Fjotra, a Reachfolk Sybil of Dibella]]
During the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], it was customary to decorate the steets of cities across Tamriel for the [[Lore:Heart's Week|Heart's Week]]. Reachfolk-controlled Markarth was among them. The decorations included banners, pink and red flowers associated with Dibella, as well as blue floral patterns, and hanging Amulets of [[Lore:Mara|Mara]].{{Ref|name=MarkarthMara|Appearance of [[Online:Markarth|Markarth]] during [[ON:Hearts Week|Hearts Week]]}}

In the Fourth Era the Temple of Dibella in Markarth was highly esteemed among the Reachfolk.{{ref|name=SRFjorta|[[Skyrim:Fjotra|Fjorta]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SREnmon|[[Skyrim:Enmon|Enmon]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] were known to worship Dibella and the other Divines in Karthwasten,{{ref|name=SREnmon}}{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} as well as Markarth.{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In Markarth, the position of Sybil of Dibella was typically given to a Reachwoman selected as a child to spend her whole life in devotion and communion with the goddess.{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|332}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, [[Lore:Fjotra|Fjotra]],{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|332}} the daughter of a Reachfolk couple in Karthwasten, was chosen to become Sybil.{{ref|name=SRFjorta}}

[[Lore:Broken Tower Redoubt|Broken Tower Redoubt]], the ruins of an old settlement which were occupied by [[Lore:Forsworn|Forsworn]], housed a Shrine of Dibella within a sacrificial chamber.{{ref|name=Broken Tower Redoubt|[[Skyrim:Broken Tower Redoubt|Broken Tower Redoubt]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG}}{{rp|332}} It is unclear if the Forsworn actively worshiped at the shrine or if they had in fact desecrated it.

===Helm of the Crusader===
[[File:OB-item-Helm of the Crusader.jpg|The Helm of the Crusader|right|thumb|150px]]
The Helm of the Crusader, one of the [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Crusader's Relics]], was created by Dibella in the early First Era and granted to [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]], the Divine Crusader, to allow him to defeat and banish [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]], an Ayleid Sorcerer King. Pelinal, despite his victory over Umaril, was slain and the Crusader's Relics were scattered and lost for thousands of years.{{ref|name=TKOTN|{{Cite Book|The Knights of the Nine}}}} The helm was placed at the Shrine of the Crusader at [[Lore:Vanua|Vanua]], Pelinal's place of death, where it remained until it was recovered by the [[Lore:Hero|Hero of Kvatch]] in 3E 433.{{ref|name=KOTN}}
