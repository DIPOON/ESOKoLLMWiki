[[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] ({{IPA|'|tˈamɹi-ˈɛl}} {{pronunciation|Tamri|EL}}{{Ab|LO-audio-word-Tamriel.ogg}}), also known as the '''Arena''', is one of the main continents of the planet [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{Ref|name=DUG}}{{ref|name=Cosmology|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Cosmology|ns_base=GEN}}}} This land is a great melting pot of [[Lore:Factions|cultures]] and [[Lore:Races|races]]; being home to at least a dozen races, with dozens more having died out or changed in ages gone by.{{ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} The continent is divided into nine [[#Provinces|provinces]], which have on occasion been united under the rule of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]].{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue E|Generic dialogue on the Empire]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=PGE3Man|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} The name semantically denotes both the landmass at large and a political entity, with Tamriel being identified as a kingdom and an empire.{{ref|name=ARMISF|[[Books:Arena Player's Guide/Introduction|''Arena Player's Guide'' (French)]]}}{{rp|1}}{{ref|name=DFC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|188}}{{ref|name=TESHTPCB|group=UOL|1=[https://www.escapistmagazine.com/the-elder-scrolls-at-25-how-i-created-bethesdas-legendary-rpg-series ''The Escapist: The Elder Scrolls at 25: How Ted Peterson Created Bethesda's Legendary RPG Series'']}} On [[Lore:Third Era|Septim Era]] maps, the continent is identified as the '''[[Lore:Empire of Tamriel|Empire of Tamriel]]'''{{Ref|name=ARTamriel|[[:File:AR-map-Arena (small).png|Tamriel map]] - Arena}}{{Ref|name=TESANTH|[[:File:MER-art-Arena Anthology Game Map.jpg|Tamriel map]] - ''The Elder Scrolls Anthology''}}{{Ref|name=DFTamriel|[[:File:DF-misc-Character Creation Map.jpg|Tamriel map]] - ''Daggerfall''}} or simply '''The Empire'''.{{Ref|name=PGE1Map|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (PGE1).jpg|Map of Tamriel]] – Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition}}{{Ref|name=MCMap|[[:File:LO-map-Tamriel (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Tamriel]] – [[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex|Morrowind Codex]]}} Non-independent realms of the Empire belong to an overarching political entity called Tamriel.{{ref|name=DFC}}{{rp|188}}

"Tamriel" means '''Dawn's Beauty''' in [[Lore:Elvish|Elvish]].{{ref|name=ArenaIntro}} The [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] believed that Tamriel is misnomer created by "deluded mortals" and that "Dawn's Beauty" is the most accurate name of the Land.{{ref|name=MankarDia}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} Other cultures have had their own stylizations of the name: the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] name it "Tamri-El",{{ref|[[OB:Umbacano|Umbacano's dialogue]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the [[Lore:Marukh|Marukhati]] call it "Tam! Rugh!",{{Ref|name=RFM|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Redguard Forum Madness|ns_base=GEN}}}} and the [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragons]] entitled it "Taazokaan".{{ref|name=SOSR|{{Cite Book|Songs of Skyrim: Revised}}}}<noinclude>

Bitter years of endless war had its effect on the populace, and the name Tamriel "seldom fell from anguished lips." In a place where life and death were different sides of the same coin tossed every day, the common people began calling the land of their sorrow the '''Arena'''.{{ref|name=ArenaIntro|[[Arena:Main Quest|''Arena'' Introduction]]}}{{intnote|nb2|[nb 2]}} This moniker is rooted in the metaphysics of the mundane plane itself, having been aboriginally born in "titanic battles" [[Lore:War of Manifest Metaphors|at the youth of the world]],{{Ref|name=TLL|{{Cite Book|The Lunar Lorkhan}}}}{{Ref|name=Ebonarm|{{Cite Book|The Ebon Arm}}}} and ever since being an eternally contested ground for mortal and immortal alike.{{ref|name=MankarDia}} There have only been a few glorious brief moments in which the people of Tamriel stood united and victorious against a common enemy, such as the formation of the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]] during the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian War]] and the Invasion of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]].{{Ref|name=SH|{{Cite Book|Systres History}}}}{{ref|name=WTC|Events of [[Online:The Weight of Three Crowns|The Weight of Three Crowns]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
[[File:MER-art-The World.jpg|thumb|right|Tarot of the World]]
===Early Eras===
Much of Tamriel's [[Lore:History|history]] is known and documented, but large swathes are still unknown, particularly the periods known as the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] and the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]]. Tamriel's very beginning can be traced to when the wandering [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] fought the Ehlnofey who remained settled in their former home. According to the narrative of the [[Lore:The Annotated Anuad|Anuad]], the original singular landmass of Nirn was broken apart into at least four major landmasses&mdash;Tamriel, [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]], [[Lore:Atmora|Atmora]], and [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]].{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} The wandering Ehlnofey were ancestors to all [[Lore:Men|men]], while the settled Ehlnofey were co-ancestors with the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] to all [[Lore:Mer|mer]]. It was at the end of the Dawn Era that the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] of Aldmeris began to diverge along cultural and biological lines, starting the process that would lead to the modern mer races.

In the middle of the Merethic Era an unknown threat caused the Aldmeri to leave [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]] and head towards Tamriel. They colonized many islands along its coast before settling on [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]]. Later they started moving inland, settling many areas in southwest and central Tamriel. Wherever the Aldmer settled they displaced the primitive [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] (the ancestors of today's [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s, etc.).{{ref|name=BTAOM}} During this time the Ayleids built [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] and eight lesser towers around it.{{ref|name=AE4|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Through powerful machinations and the position as the 'spoke' of the wheel within the lesser towers, White-Gold Tower became a very powerful tool&mdash;it is said that the tower is what stops [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] falling back into [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}}

Traditionally, the late Merethic Era is the time that men begin to appear on Tamriel.{{ref|name=FC}} This is not strictly true, as many races of men&mdash;such as the feathered men that [[Lore:Topal the Pilot|Topal the Pilot]] encountered, or a human race in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]&mdash;already resided on Tamriel.{{ref|name=FOTN|{{Cite Book|Father Of The Niben}}}}{{ref|name=FC}} Instead this time is taken as the period when the men of Atmora came in force to occupy and settle whole-scale on Tamriel.{{ref|name=FC}} These [[Lore:Atmora|Atmoran]]s were the ancestors of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s. While many of the original races of men died out, many of them interbred enough to become one race known as the [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]]s.{{ref|name=PGE3Cyrodiil|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}} The conquering of northern Tamriel was sparked by the decline of Atmora and its eventual loss to a catastrophic change of climate from one that supported life to one of 'permanent winter' where no life could survive for long.{{ref|name=PGE3Other|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}}

The [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] at the end of the Merethic Era ended the reign of the [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]]s.{{ref|name=TDW|{{Cite Book|The Dragon War}}}} In the third century of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] saw the humans of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] overthrow their slavers, the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], marking the point at which humans began to dominate Tamrielic history.{{ref|name=TAOK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} St. [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] made a covenant with [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] to create the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]]. The covenant stated that so long as one of Alessia's heirs wore the amulet, the realms of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] would be cut off from Nirn.{{ref|name=TOSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}}

Near the end of the seventh century, in {{Year|1E 668}}, [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] erupted for the first time in recorded history. 32 years later the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] occurred, in which the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] fought the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. The end of the war came when all Dwemer simply vanished from Tamriel, leaving behind their many artifacts and strongholds. After the war the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] took over Morrowind, and shortly after that the Chimer were changed into the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]].{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}}

The continent of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]] sank in {{Year|1E 792}}, so its peoples, the [[Lore:Yokudan|Yokudan]]s, emigrated to the area now known as [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{ref|name=PGE3Other}} The nature of the disaster is unknown but many natives think it was self-inflicted.{{ref|name=THOS|{{Cite Book|The Hunger of Sep}}}} The Yokudans, after conquering Hammerfell, took on many of the customs of the Ayleids, and after many centuries in the harsh desert conditions evolved into modern day [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s.

In {{Year|1E 2200}} the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]] claimed the lives of more than half of Tamriel's population. The losses were concentrated to the west and south. The plague was particularly hard on the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]. From sixteen harmonious kingdoms and twenty different types of Khajiit, the plague left them with catpower to form only two kingdoms. The often vastly different outlooks from the various kingdoms all shoved together led to conflict.{{ref|name=PGE3Sugar|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Sugar and Blood}}}} In response to the Plague the [[Lore:All-Flags Navy|All-Flags Navy]] was formed to exact revenge on the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]]. This was the first time all nations on Tamriel had worked together and it would have profound implications that would last for eras. In {{Year|1E 2703}} the [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] from Akavir invaded Tamriel. [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] and [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] united to defeat them. The leader of the armies, [[Lore:Reman|Reman]], went on to found the Second Empire, which brought all the nations of Tamriel, except Morrowind, under the Empire's control as provinces until 2E 430.{{ref|name=PGE3Other}}

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 560}} the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] spread from [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] through the lower provinces and then into the other provinces killing many people. [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s were immune to the disease, leading to speculation they were responsible, but this proved to be incorrect. While the disease killed almost all non-Argonians in Black Marsh it was not as devastating elsewhere, though it did wipe out entire cities, religions, and cultures.{{ref|name=PGE3Argonia|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Argonia}}}} The Knahaten Flu had a big impact on events of the next three decades. The Altmer gave aid to the suffering Khajiit, who out of gratitude joined the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]]. The ruling family of Wayrest was wiped out, leading to [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] becoming king. Emeric spent the next fifteen years expanding the newly signed [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] to include [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]]'s neighbors in Hammerfell and [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]. The Argonians then banded together, as an equal partner, with the Nords and Dunmer in the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], to protect their interests against the other two alliances.{{ref|name=LOTESO|[[Online:Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|Introduction to the Lore of ''The Elder Scrolls Online'']]}}

In {{Year|2E 582}} the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]] broke out. Tensions had been simmering between the three alliances and the [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]], but the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] and the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] threw them into open conflict to try and gain control of the Ruby Throne and save Nirn from [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]. Molag Bal's plan was foiled but the outcome of the war for the throne is unknown.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} From {{Year|2E 852}} to 854 a series of wars led by [[Lore:Cuhlecain|Cuhlecain]] and [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] led to the formation of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] under Tiber's rule. In 2E 896 Tiber conquered the Summerset Isles and for the first time all the provinces of Tamriel were united under one banner. The significance of the event led Tiber to declare a new era.{{ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}

===Third Era===
In {{Year|3E 433}} the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] occurred. Due to a lack of Alessian heirs to wear the Amulet of Kings, the Dragonfires died out. [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] attempted to invade Tamriel, destroying [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]] and the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]]. The Crisis ended when an illegitimate heir sacrificed himself to call Akatosh and defeat Dagon.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} It was claimed that Nirn could not ever be invaded again by daedra from Oblivion. When the Crisis was over a new era was announced, making the Third Era the shortest complete era to date.

===Fourth Era===
At the start of the Fourth Era Red Mountain erupted again. This time it destroyed the entire island and devastated the rest of Morrowind. The Argonians then invaded and unleashed their anger at centuries of oppression and slavery on the survivors, only being stopped on the banks of the [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]].{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} During the early Fourth Era the Empire started to crumble and then in 4E 48 [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]], a floating island from Oblivion appeared. The island made its way over Black Marsh to Morrowind, before turning and heading straight for the White-Gold Tower, causing massive devastation along the way. The island was summoned by a rogue [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] tree to kill all non-native Argonians, but quickly went its own way after appearing. The island was eventually stopped by the efforts of Prince [[Lore:Attrebus Mede|Attrebus Mede]] and {{Lore Link|Annaïg Hoïnart}}. It disappeared leaving behind hordes of corpses by the walls of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{ref|name=TIC}}

From {{Year|4E 171}} to 175 the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] between the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] and the remainder of the Empire raged across Tamriel. The Thalmor leaders of the third [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], believed that mer should rule the continent, while the men who currently occupy the throne think different. The terms of the current peace treaty drove Hammerfell to abandon the Empire, and a civil war broke out in Skyrim&mdash;the result of which will determine if Skyrim will also secede.{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

==Provinces==

A proposed tenth province, Orsinium, has never been taken very seriously except by those who want it, namely the Orcs, who wish to have a home province of their own.{{ref|name=PGE3Orsinium|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Orsinium}}}}

*When asked about who designed the Tamriel map, the original developers had this to say:
::[[General:Vijay Lakshman|Vijay Lakshman]]: ''That is crazy as a question, ultimately because there's so many people who touch the map as you end up getting from what's original scribbled down to what it becomes in the final game. But I remember I sketched out the first map, then Ted and I worked on all kinds of different parts of it, and then you hand it over to the artists, and what you get back always looks so much better than what you had envisioned. But I think ultimately questions like: "who did what?" is probably gonna be answered with: "team effort," everyone is trying to make the product the best it can be. My only contribution was recognizing how rivers run to the ocean, which is probably the only part of it that lasted.''<br>[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]: ''I remember when you had that map, and it was several sheets of paper spread across your desk, and that [you were like]: "just come in here and start filling in capitals, and names of cities, and names of places!" We did things like, [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], [which] was named that because were just like "Oh and that's... elsewhere. Another place. We'll just play around with the letters of that."''{{ref|group=UOL|name=OLAMA|1=[https://www.youtube.com/live/4ZdyP9KbmHA?feature=share&t=584 ''Oncelost Games AMA'']}}
*''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' established a continent spanning mountain range in the west called the [[Lore:Dragon's Teeth Mountains|Dragon's Teeth]], which began northern [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], spined through [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], cross the [[Lore:Imperial Province|Imperial Province]], ending in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{ref|name=RiaDialogue|[[Arena:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Subsequent titles after ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' have neglected to support this detail, instead referring to its subranges such as the [[Lore:Druadach Mountains|Druadach]] and [[Lore:Jerall Mountains|Jeralls]],{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} though the Dragon's Teeth are referred to in ''[[Online:Online|The Elder Scrolls Online]]''.{{Ref|name=AP|{{Cite Book|Arkngthamz-Phng (book)}}}}
*The lore of ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' attempted to establish a continent-spanning stream called the [[Lore:Yrinthi River|Yrinthi River]], which connected the [[Lore:Imperial Province|Imperial Province]] to the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]] of the northwest.{{ref|name=DUG|''[[Books:Daggerfall User's Guide/History|Daggerfall User's Guide]]''}}{{rp|5}} Subsequent titles have neglected to support this detail in-game.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
*In [[Books:The Daggerfall Chronicles|certain]] [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|early]] maps of Tamriel from the development of ''Arena'' and ''Daggerfall'', several sets of what appear to be islands or clouds can be seen. Two are located west of High Rock, one north of Morrowind (where Solstheim would later be placed), and one west of Morrowind, beneath the [[Lore:Northeast Archipelago|Northeast Archipelago]]. However, whether or not these were actually intended to be land masses is unconfirmed.
*According to [[Books:Arena Player's Guide|''Arena Player's Guide'']], the continent of Tamriel is sized roughly: '''3,000 to 4,000 kilometers from east to west''', and '''2,000 to 3,000 kilometers from north to south'''.{{ref|name=ARMIS|[[Books:Arena Player's Guide|''Arena Player's Guide'']]}}{{rp|55}} If you compare this to measurements presented in the later lore and or marketing materials advertising worldscales, the ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' measurements aren't consistent. However, [[General:Todd Howard|Todd Howard]] has indicated that the ''Arena'' manual can be a rather authoritative source when it establishes something specific—towering above other later references in some cases.{{ref|name=Todd25th|1=[https://youtu.be/JITgbkbIJG4?t=1401 ''The Elder Scrolls 25th Anniversary Panel'']}} The dialogue said by [[Arena:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]] in ''Arena'' in regards to being able to spy the Dragon's Teeth mountains from atop the Crystal Tower would seemingly support the measurement presented in the ''Arena Player's Guide''.{{ref|name=ARSilmane|[[Arena:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane's dialogue]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} The official readme for the ''Arena'' demo states that the world is '''twelve million square kilometers'''.{{ref|1=[https://ia601305.us.archive.org/view_archive.php?archive=/34/items/TheElderScrollsArena_1020/arenad.zip ''Arena Demo'']}} While the official website source created after ''Arena'' released states that the world is '''eight million square kilometers''' in size.{{ref|name=ArenaWebsite|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/19970605103514/http://www.bethsoft.com/html/arena.html bethsoft.com/arena]}} During the development of ''Daggerfall'' in 1996, Ted Peterson stated that Tamriel overall is about the same size as [[Wikipedia:Canada|Canada]]{{ref|[[General:Score Magazine: Ted Peterson on Daggerfall|''Score Magazine: Ted Peterson on Daggerfall'']]}} (which is approximately '''4,634 kilometers from north to south''' and '''7,560 kilometers from east to west''').
**The first ''[[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Morrowind#Vvardenfell|Pocket Guide to the Empire]]'' states the peak of [[Lore:Dagoth-Ur (place)|Dagoth-Ur]] can be seen from [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], "250 miles to the south."{{ref|name=PGE1M|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} While calculations derived from this figure can differ wildly due to various factors—including what map of Tamriel is used—they will always result in Tamriel's size being significantly smaller than what is given by the ''Arena'' materials. However, going by the measurement presented in fast travel from ''Arena'', the [[Arena:Dagoth-Ur|Mount of Dagoth-Ur]] is located 860 kilometers (534 miles) from [[Arena:Mournhold|Mournhold]]—which is more than double what the ''Pocket Guide'' states.

===Annotations===
*{{LNote|nb1|^|The Mythic Dawn believed that Dawn's Beauty is a [[Lore:Oblivion|Plane of Oblivion]].{{ref|name=MankarDia}} It is said that Daedric names primordially inspire "the concepts they connote"{{ref|name=N'Gasta|[[RG:N'Gasta#Daedra Lords|N'Gasta]]'s dialogue in ''[[RG:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} and may endure beyond [[Lore:Kalpa|kalpas]].{{ref|group=UOL|{{TIL|Michael Kirkbride IRC Quotes|michael-kirkbride-irc-quotes}}}}}}
*{{LNote|nb2|^|The first ''[[General:The Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]]'' game was initially designed as a rather simplistic gladiator game titled ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' with a new complementary fantasy world called '''Tamriel''' full of provinces and city-states as the backdrop. However the gladiator elements and the fighting arenas were cut entirely from the game later, because they found in playtesting that romping through the fantasy setting on quests was more compelling gameplay. However, the title of ''Arena'' was already set in stone for marketing and legal reasons, so the designers made up a quick in-world backstory about the setting is called '''The Arena''' to explain why a game called ''Arena'' had no arenas in it.{{ref|name=10th|''[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary|The Elder Scrolls - 10th Anniversary]]'', originally found at Elderscrolls.com}}{{ref|name=TedHeadGameSpy|group=UOL|1=''[https://web.archive.org/web/20120712142129/http://planetelderscrolls.gamespy.com:80/View.php?view=Articles.Detail&id=12 Ultimate: Ted Peterson Interview Pt. 1]''}} Subsequent titles typically prefer to use the term '''Tamriel''' over '''Arena'''. However, the idea behind '''Arena''' as a setting moniker was not abandoned whatsoever, with the concept actually being built into to the overall conflicts and metaphysics of ''The Elder Scrolls'' series.{{ref|name=MankarDia|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran's dialogue]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Arena Supermundus}}}}{{ref|name=Cosmology|group=UOL}}}}
