# Lore:Hagraven

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Bestiary Trail}} [[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[Category:Lore-Creatures]]__NOTOC__[[File:SR-creature-Drascua.jpg|thumb|right|A hagraven (Skyrim)]]</noinclude>
[[Lore:Hagraven|Hagraven]]s are creatures of corruption and decay that appear as a horrific cross between an old woman and a [[Lore:Bird|bird]].{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|110}} They were once [[Lore:Witches|witches]], but have undergone a ritual, trading in their humanity for access to powerful [[Lore:Magic|magic]], and the transformations they undergo infuse their entire beings with some element of that power.{{ref|name=HGTS|{{Cite Book|Herbalist's Guide to Skyrim}}}} Hagravens consume people alive, and especially love eating fresh eyeballs.{{ref|name=ADoE|{{Cite Book|A Diet of Eyes}}}}

Few creatures match the cunning, depravity, and repulsiveness of the hagravens. They will take through savagery what they cannot win through guile.{{ref|[[SR:Hagraven|Hagraven]]-related [[SR:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] text from ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They are usually extremely hostile to outsiders, but they retain the ability to communicate. They are equally dangerous up close or when casting spells from afar.{{ref|Hagraven abilities in ''[[ON:Online|The Elder Scrolls Online]]'' and ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} They are known for their foul stench.{{Ref|name=HBH|{{Cite Book|Herbane's Bestiary: Hagravens}}}} They possess an ability to turn into a murder of [[Lore:Crow|crows]] and attack their foes from a hundred directions at once.{{ref|name=CB|{{Cite Book|Crow Bringer}}}}<noinclude>

The transformation to Hagraven is reversible, though it is unknown if it can be undone without divine intervention. On one occasion Kyne intervened and cleansed and forgave Keeper Ormi who turned into a hagraven, after her son Sahun revealed himself to still be alive and prayed for her.{{Ref|name=ESOFKH|Events of [[ON:For Kyne's Honor|For Kyne's Honor]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Culture==
Hagravens can often be found in the company of some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] clans, who occasionally revere them as Matriarchs and leaders, or dwelling in secret groves and lairs in the wilderness with other witches and [[Lore:Hag|hags]].{{ref|name=ALBAB|{{Cite Book|A Life Barbaric and Brutal}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Breaking the Coven|Breaking the Coven]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Some clans actively revile and fear them, however.{{Ref|name=Medone|[[ON:Medone|Medone]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} ''[[Lore:Wilderness Survival Tips|Wilderness Survival Tips]]'', a collection of advice attributed to the Reachfolk lists hagravens among the dangers of the wild. It warns that if one encountered a hagraven that has truly decided someone should die, there is little hope of resisting her. At the same time, the text notes that hagravens are not especially swift and can sometimes be outrun.{{ref|name=WST|{{Cite Book|Wilderness Survival Tips}}}}

Though hagravens are predominantly associated with the Reachfolk, there are recorded instances of [[Lore:Nord|Nord]],{{Ref|name=KO|[[ON:Keeper Ormi|Keeper Ormi]]'s dialogue and appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] hagravens as well.{{ref|name=TheTrio3|[[Bloodmoon:Ettiene of Glenmoril Wyrd|Ettiene of Glenmoril Wyrd]], [[Bloodmoon:Isobel of Glenmoril Wyrd|Isobel of Glenmoril Wyrd]], and [[Bloodmoon:Fallaise of Glenmoril Wyrd|Fallaise of Glenmoril Wyrd]]'s appearance in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=TheTrio5|[[Skyrim:Ettiene|Ettiene]], [[Skyrim:Isobel|Isobel]], and [[Skyrim:Fallaise|Fallaise]]'s appearance in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} At least one Hagraven was a result of the Wild Hunt,{{Ref|name=KCHR}} and as such she was of [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmeri]] origins.{{Ref|name=PGE|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Aldmeri Dominion}}}} The [[Lore:Glenmoril Wyrd|Glenmoril Witches]] of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] were known to be hagravens.{{ref|Appearance of the [[SR:Glenmoril Witch|Glenmoril Witches]] during [[SR:Blood's Honor|Blood's Honor]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The [[Lore: Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] is considered kin to the [[Lore:Hag|hags]] and hagravens.{{Ref|name=KCHR|{{TIL|Kyne's Challenge, High Rock — The Imperial Library|high-rock-0}}}} The Crow Mother is a hagraven [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lord]] who claims to be the "Nether-Niece" of Nocturnal.{{ref|name=ONCrowMother|[[Online:Crow Mother|Crow Mother]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} This hag spawned the [[Lore:Crow Daedra|Crow Daedra]], who make up [[Lore:Blackfeather Court|the Blackfeather Court]].{{ref|name=TBFC| {{Cite Book|The Blackfeather Court}}}} 

Stone carvings with the imagery of hagravens with a distended bellies could be found in {{Year|2E 582}}. It was unclear whether it represented a fertility idol or a gruesome depiction of a hagraven devouring small children.{{ref|name=ContrabandH1|[[ON:Contraband H#Hagmother Statue|Hagmother Statue]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Uses==
[[Lore:Hagraven Claw|Hagraven claws]] can be used in alchemical concoctions.{{ref|name=HGTS}}{{Ref|name=SHC|[[Skyrim:Hagraven Claw|Hagraven Claws]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Drinking vessels made of hollowed hagraven claw were believed to increase the effects of whatever alcohol it contains.{{ref|name=ContrabandH2|[[ON:Contraband H#Hagraven Drinking Claw|Hagraven Drinking Claw]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Hagraven Feathers|Hagraven feathers]] are also used in alchemy,{{Ref|name=SHF|[[Skyrim:Hagraven Feathers|Hagraven Feathers]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and quills made from hagraven feathers are typically long and sharp. They are occasionally manicured and inset with silver.{{ref|name=ContrabandH3|[[ON:Contraband H#Hagraven Silver-Quill Pen|Hagraven Silver-Quill Pen]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} One of such quills was known as the {{Lore Link|Black Quill}}. It was commissioned by [[Lore:Razotha Tale-Teller|Razotha Tale-Teller]], a legendary Orc lorekeeper. According to legends her quill was enchanted by an Altmer wizard to ensure that any story written using this quill would never be forgotten.{{ref|name=TBQ|{{Cite Book|The Black Quill}}}} Some Reachfolk chandeliers are adorned with materials sourced from Hagravens.{{ref|name=RCH|[[ON:Reach Chandelier, Hagraven|Reach Chandelier, Hagraven]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
During the [[Lore:Wild Hunt|Wild Hunt]] created by the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] to kill [[Lore:Borgas|High King Borgas]] in {{Year|1E 369}},{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}} the Reachfolk captured a hagraven in Skyrim among the monstrosities that were spawned from the event.{{Ref|name=KCHR}}

During his exile, [[Lore:Faolan|Faolan]] the Red Eagle came across a mysterious and powerful hagraven, also known as simply the Hag in early works,{{Ref|name=TTWOT4|{{Cite Book|The Translated Works of Tosmorn, IV}}}} who offered him the power to free his people in exchange for his heart. Red Eagle had to clear his mind before he could embrace his destiny and filled his heart with the thing he loved the most - his people.{{ref|name=ESORedEagle|[[ON:Red Eagle's Song (quest)|Red Eagle's Song]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} He accepted the offer and became the first [[Lore:Briarheart|Briarheart]],{{ref|name=Antiquity|[[Online:Red Eagle Cave Painting|Red Eagle Cave Painting]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} becoming a spirit of vengeance, pitiless, and beyond remorse and bringing an onslaught of violence and death to the occupying [[Lore:First Empire|Alessians]] led by [[Lore:Hestra|Empress Hestra]].{{ref|name=TLORE|{{Cite Book|The Legend of Red Eagle}}}}

The conflict between the Hags of Glenumbra's Hag Fen and the Beldama Wyrd is as ancient as the Hag Fen itself. The arrival of the hagraven Mother Murk from [[Lore:the Reach|the Reach]] upset the balance within the swamp, twisting the world around her. Her alliance with the Bloodthorn Cult led to an increase of abductions of women around the area to convert into hags or thralls.{{ref|name=ONWT|[[Online:Wicked Trade|Wicked Trade]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} This brought the attention of an adventurer, who with the guidance of the wandering Reach-witch [[Lore:Sibellec|Sibellec the Old]], turned Mother Murk's magic against her, allowing her to be slain, and brought the Hag Fen under the control of the Beldama coven.{{ref|name=ONWAC|[[Online:Wyrd and Coven|Wyrd and Coven]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
