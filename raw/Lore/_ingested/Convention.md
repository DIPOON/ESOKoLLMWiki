'''Convention''', also known as the '''Moot at the House of We''',{{ref|name=SSoS|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Shor Son of Shor|ns_base=GEN}}}} was an event during the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] that took place on the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]], a meeting of the [[Lore:Et'Ada|et'Ada]] outside [[Lore:Aurbis|aurbic]] time during which the physical, temporal, spiritual, and magical elements of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], as well as the laws that govern them and determine the role of all spirits within them, are believed to have been set in place.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} Convention has been described as an "impossipoint".{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}} The [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofex]] war has been described by some sources as being the same event as Convention told in a different manner.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} 
==The Flight of Magnus==
According to legend, when [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], the architect of the plans for the mortal world, decided to terminate the project, the gods decided to convene at the Adamantine Tower to decide on what to do.{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} Most of the spirits departed with Magnus, becoming the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]]. But some [[Lore:Aedra|Aedric]] spirits chose to stay on Nirn after Magnus and the Magna Ge departed in order to keep working on the new world so it'd remain viable. Among those spirits, some, typically referred to from that point on as [[Lore:Ehlnofey|"Earthbones"]], are thought to have followed the example of [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law as the "bones of the earth", eternal laws of nature. While others, typically referred to from that point on as [[Lore:Ehlnofey|"Ehlnofey"]], are thought to have chosen not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, thus becoming the progenitors to mortal life, which arose from their lines and took on its current form due to a phenomenon of gradual diminishment, of each consecutive generation becoming weaker and more removed than its progenitors in stature and might.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}{{ref|name=Mystery|{{Cite Book|The Mystery of Artaeum}}}}{{ref|name=TAA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}}
==The Convention==
[[File:MW-creature-Heart of Lorkhan.jpg|The Heart of Lorkhan|thumb|left]][[File:SR-item-Auriel's Bow.jpg|Auriel's Bow (Dawnguard)|right|thumb]]
[[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]]-that-is-[[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] is said to have returned to [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] from his [[Lore:Akatosh (planet)|Dominion Planet]], signaling all [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] to convene at a static meeting that would last outside of [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbic]] time. The Time God's [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|sleek and silver vessel]] became a spike, attached to the changing earth at one of the joint-points where the Earthbones on which the physical flesh on Nirn was hung met, and radiated a "palpable reality",{{ref|name=SCaS|{{Cite Book|Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation}}}} the "glimmerwinds of its impact" warning that any spirit that entered aura with it would become recorded. That by consent of their presence, their actions here would last a period of time unassailable, and would remain so whatever might come later to these spirits, even if they rejoined the [[Lore:Aetherius|aether]] or succumbed willingly or by treachery to a [[Lore:Sithis|sithite]] [[Lore:Void|erasure]].{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}

The outcome of the Convention was twofold.

The spike of [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Ada-Mantia]] and its [[Lore:Towers|Zero Stone]] are said to have dictated the structure of reality within their Aurbic vicinity, defining for the Earth Bones their story or nature within the unfolding of the "Dragon's (timebound) Tale", and so setting in place the laws that govern the mortal world.{{Ref|name=AU4TET}} Thus also imposing certain limits on the "architect-gods" who, prior to that point, were "young", much like the world they'd crafted, and so held "every power at every time amendment at every ordering", making them mighty but also unable to ever fully manifest themselves within the world.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}

[[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] was judged, his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart]] torn out by [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]],{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=TM}} or per some sources spat out by Lorkhan himself,{{ref|name=SSoS|group=UOL}} and fastened to an arrow fired from [[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]], to land where no aspect of the new world could ever find it, after it became clear its link to the new world made it impossible to destroy.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=TM}} In the removal and landing of the Heart, the second [[Lore:Towers|Tower]], the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Tower]], is said to have been created, with the Heart itself as the First Stone. This allowed Mundus to exist without the full presence of the divine and granted it a special kind of divinity, which is called "NIRN", the "consequence of variable [[Lore:Fate|fate]]".{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}

Finally, the Aedra elected to make their exit from mortal affairs, "leaving the terrestrial sphere in their excess, for its own good", due to the danger their continued presence posed to the mortal world, which was rendered highly unstable, and even to the timeless continuity of existence. With the departure of [[Lore:Magic|magic]] in the mythic sense, linear history could finally begin, and the Dawn Era ended.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}} Even after the Aedra departed, Ada-Mantia continued to dictate reality in its vicinity, bringing unrelenting order to the chaos of the Dawn.{{Ref|name=AU4TET}} 

A different view of the events of Convention is present in the beliefs of the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]]. According to them, the mortal world was actually the [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of Lorkhan, who was actually a [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]]. Per this belief, the Aedra, the gods mortals generally worship, were actually Lorkhan's betrayers, who stole the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world. [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] names this the "First Tower Dictate", which he describes as ''"render the mutant bound where he may do no more harm. As God of the Mundus, alike shall be his progeny, split from their divine sparks. We are Eight time eight Exarchs. Let the home of Padomay see us as sole exit"''.{{ref|name=Camoran|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=MDC3|{{Cite Book|Mythic Dawn Commentaries 3}}}}
==Aftermath==
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''"The gods are good, they came from an infinite place, but for us they limited themselves and became this world. They are everything we see and touch, everything we feel.<!-- And of them all, Dibella is most kind.-->"''|A memory appearing to Colin right as he passes away, thought to be his grandmother or an avatar of Dibella{{Ref|name=LoS|Keyes, Greg. ''[[Books:Lord of Souls|Lord of Souls]]''. Del Rey Books, 2011.}}}}
[[File:OBR-place-White Gold Tower.jpg|The White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower|thumb|left]][[File:MER-Jewelry-Amulet of Kings.jpg|right|thumb|150px|The Amulet of Kings]]
[[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]] is said to have witnessed the events of Convention, inspiring them to create other [[Lore:Towers|Towers]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] once they understood that different Towers with different Stones could "tell different stories", each following its own rules inscribed by their [[Lore:Variorum Architects|Variorum Architects]] and thus establishing their own areas of influence that they'd shape.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=AU4TET}}

The [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] in particular are said to have built their Tower, the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], in "open emulation of Ada-Mantia" itself, thus granting it even greater power than the rest when it came to altering reality. In addition, the White-Gold Tower was meant to counteract the spiritual bleeding from Mundus caused by the Convention by serving as a focus point to reconnect the mortal realm with the divine.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=AU4TET}} The White-Gold Tower's Stone was the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Chim-el Adabal]], said to have been formed from a drop of blood that fell from the Heart of Lorkhan as it sailed across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], which subsequently fell into an [[Lore:Ayleid Wells|Ayleid Well]] and was crystallized by the magicka within, before being cut into a diamond shape by the elves.{{Ref|name=AU4TET}}{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad}}}}

At maturity, every [[Lore:Direnni|Direnni]] of high blood is brought into the Adamantine Tower, conducted to the Foundation Vault, and shown the Zero Stone. During the ceremony, they are allowed to touch it once so they can feel the transcendent mystical power that courses through it.{{ref|name=Once|{{Cite Book|Once}}}}

Interfering with Mundus is generally considered to be off-limits for the Aedra since the end of the ages of myth and, as a result, they tend to no longer respond to mortals directly. As such, though minor Aedric spirits do exist, they are rarely encountered since the moment Magnus withdrew from the world during creation.{{ref|name=POE|{{Cite Book|Phrastus of Elinhir Answers Your Questions}}}}

==Numancia==
[[File:ON-concept-Ithelia.png|thumb|left|The Daedric Prince Ithelia]]
The [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] religious text [[Lore:The Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]] claims that, when the [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]], and later [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]], [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]] were shown the [[Lore:Apocrypha|Infinite Abyss]] and the Indeterminate Sea (Mora Obscura), they discovered the Indefinite Limits of [[Lore:Void|Lorkh-Apeiron]]. Thus, it is said she bears connection to the harmony within duality that exists within the dichotomy of [[Lore:Anuiel|Anui-El]] and [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]], and the unity of opposites that contained [[Lore:Varliance|starlight]] and [[Lore:Many Paths|endless possibility]] beyond [[Lore:Aurbis|cosmic interplay]]. 

Ithelia was thus named an unknown title, as well as named the "the Colors of Light", by Magnus. Within texts discussing her, it is said she bears some connection to the "madness of the Time God" and the "first challenge of his shadow", who in nothingness saw those endless possibilities first. She also has unknown connection to the establishment of linear time, as layered upon endless possibility by the affixing of Ada-Mantia and its Zero Stone, a stable spire affixed by a stone of nothing possible,{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL}}{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=AU4TET|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} Texts discussing her also speak on how [[Lore:Akatosh|Aka]] disappeared in the South, and [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] learned why Aka's insanity is all that is and could be. 

From this lesson, Convention and the Adamantine Tower was fixed to cleave a path through the everything to reach Numancia.{{ref|[[Online:The Dark Descent|The Dark Descent]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} As a result, echoing the endeavor of the Prime Archon, Ithelia ,her Ayleid followers began to use violence against Man to bring forth a [[Lore:Hero|Numinous]] Paravant, whose metaphysical nature would give them unbound hands and the ability to achieve Ithelia's goal, to break the shackles of [[Lore:Fate|fate]] and give every being the world and history they desired.{{ref|name=TNC|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}

==Notes==
*If, as some sources claim, the Ehlnofey war is the same event as Convention told in a different way, than at least some of the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] might have witnessed it,{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL}} as it is said that the earliest martial discipline of Nirn was the "Prismatic Vector Dance of the Ehlnofey", which was recorded and studied by the Demiprince [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]].{{ref|name=Maelstrom|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions 2}}}}
*Some myths seemingly ascribe a degree of involvement in Dawn Era events to Daedric figures. One pre-Riddle'thar Epiphany [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] myth claims that, when Magrus fled from [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]] and Lorkhaj, one of his eyes was torn out, and so he could only see out of one eye and fell into the [[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} However, as Convention as an event is not directly described in those myths, whether a connection exists remains unclear.
*Some myths suggest the events of Convention might repeat in a cycle, possibly alluding to the concept of [[Lore:Kalpa|Kalpas]], with the chaotic [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]] taking place between the end of the previous world and the beginning of the next one. In the mythic account described in [[General:Shor Son of Shor|Shor Son of Shor]], the gods are described as warring with one another in a number of cycles, with the same events repeating in each. In the text the god [[Lore:Shor|Shor]] is described as consulting the [[Lore:Ghost|ghost]] of his "father", the Shor of a prior cycle, in the "underworld", and repeating those same actions in the current cycle.{{ref|name=BTAOM}}{{ref|name=SSoS|group=UOL}}
*''[[Lore:The Wandering Spirits|Wandering Spirits]]'' recounts the tale of [[Lore:Akha|Akha]], who vanished in the South, with [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]], who has himself been described as both weaver and embodiment of time's all-encompassing tapestry,{{Ref|name=Ja'darri|[[Online:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dragonhold|Dragonhold]]''}} appearing in his place, and bearing his crown and rule of his kingdoms across the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]]. This event might be the same as the disappearance of Aka linked to the affixing of Ada-Mantia that Ithelia is said to have witnessed, from a different cultural perspective.{{Ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}}
*{{LNote|nb1|^1|''[[Lore:The Nine Coruscations|The Nine Coruscations]]'', a religious text of Ayleid origins, mentions the "Numinous Paravant", a being with "unbound hands", and thus the potential to achieve the goal of Ithelia to reach Numancia, breaking the shackles of fate and giving everyone their own desired history. Ithelia's Ayleid worshippers seemed to believe they could somehow cause the Numinous Paravant to appear by committing violence against Man.{{ref|name=TNC}}{{ref|name=Ithelia}} The Numinous Paravant might've been [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]], who rebelled against the Ayleids, and one of whose titles is "Paravant".{{Ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}}}}
