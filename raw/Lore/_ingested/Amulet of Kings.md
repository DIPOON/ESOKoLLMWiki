# Lore:Amulet of Kings

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}__NOTOC__[[File:MER-Jewelry-Amulet of Kings.jpg|right|thumb|150px|The Amulet of Kings]][[File:OBR-item-Amulet of Kings.jpg|thumb|left|The Amulet of Kings <br>(Oblivion Remastered)]]</noinclude>
The [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]],{{ref|name=ManualArena|''[[Books:Arena Player's Guide/Introduction#English Introduction|Arena Player's Guide]]''}}{{rp|2}}{{ref|name=AoK4}}{{ref|name=AoKESO|[[Online:Artifacts#Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} also called the '''Amulet of the Kings of Glory''',{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}} was a pendant traditionally worn by the ruling emperor of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Set in its center was the '''Chim-el Adabal''', also known as the '''Red Diamond''',{{ref|name=AoK|{{Cite Book|The Amulet of Kings}}}} a huge [[Lore:Soul Gems|soul gem]] of [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] origin, and [[Lore:Aedra|Aedric]] relic,{{ref|name=Molag|[[Online:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dialogue during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} said to contain the divine power of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]],{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}} and to be a vessel of his will, tied to him in a very real way.{{ref|name=TCA}} It was held in a golden clasp surrounded by eight smaller gems that represented the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] of the original Cyrodilic pantheon, created by [[Lore:Alessia|Queen Alessia]]. It served as the symbol of the divine right of the Cyrodilic emperors.<noinclude> 

The amulet was an important component of the coronation ceremony and a powerful artifact when used for [[Lore:Sight|divination]]. The [[Lore:Souls|soul]] of each reigning emperor was enshrined within the central stone, presumably during the coronation ritual involving the Dragonfires and the divine Covenant. In this way Cyrodiil's rulers were brought together in death, forming an [[Lore:Oversoul|'oversoul']] that could provide counsel to their successors. The oversoul of emperors was capable of describing events that occurred within a [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]], where even the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]] could not.{{ref|name=WWY|{{Cite Book|Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?}}}} The amulet could only be worn by certain individuals&mdash;those who could wear it were said to have the 'Dragon Blood' in their veins. The specific requirements, however, have been a subject of debate, and they may simply have called for the attributes of a ruler or some divine mandate.{{ref|name=TBOTD|{{Cite Book|The Book of the Dragonborn}}}} When the Amulet's bearer dies, a bell tolls warning the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] of its bearer's death.{{ref|name=ManualArena}}{{rp|2}}

The Amulet of Kings was the [[Lore:Towers|Stone]] of the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]], a focal point for the Tower's reality altering power, upon which the rules according to which White-Gold shaped its domain, were inscribed.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}{{Ref|name=Enigma|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}} It was described by [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] as the most powerful Soul Gem ever created, an [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] through which all things were made possible, even imprisoning the [[Lore:Souls|animus]] of a [[Lore:Gods|god]] and using it to achieve [[Lore:Lifespan|Apotheosis]].{{ref|name=Mannimarco|[[Online:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The Amulet was used by the [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] emperors during their coronation, as part of the ritual to relight the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] in the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]] and thereby renew the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|Liminal Barriers]] separating [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] from [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=LtD|Events of [[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=DoB|Dialogue from the [[Online:Drake of Blades|Drake of Blades]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} Though the coronation ritual took place in the Temple of the One, the source of the Dragonfires was the [[Lore:Sublime Brazier|Sublime Brazier]], a sacred cauldron located in the Dragonfire Cathedral deep beneath the Imperial Sewers, said to stretch to the very heart of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and described as Akatosh's "first light".{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Sublime Brazier}}}}

The Amulet of Kings is a sacred symbol of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]], although most people think of the [[Lore:Red Dragon Crown|Red Dragon Crown]] as the Empire's main symbol of power.{{ref|[[OB:Baurus|Baurus]]' dialogue in [[OB:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} The Red Diamond has also become a symbol heavily associated with Imperials.{{ref|[[Online:Steadfast Society Ball Domino|Steadfast Society Ball Domino]] hat description in {{g|on}}}}

==History==
===First Era===
[[File:ON-statue-St. Alessia, Paravant.jpg|St. Alessia depicted with the Amulet|left|thumb]]{{Quote Box|''And Akatosh drew from his breast a burning handful of his Heart's blood, and he gave it into Alessia's hand, saying, "This shall also be a token to you of our joined blood and pledged faith. So long as you and your descendants shall wear the Amulet of Kings, then shall this Dragonfire burn—an eternal flame—as a sign to all men and gods of our faithfulness. So long as the Dragonfires shall burn, to you, and to all generations, I swear that my Heart's blood shall hold fast the Gates of Oblivion.''|An excerpt from ''[[Lore:Trials of St. Alessia|Trials of St. Alessia]]''}}

The Amulet of Kings entered the annals of human history when Akatosh (in some stories [[Lore:Shezarr|Shezarr]]){{ref|group=UOL|{{TIL|First Era Timeline|first-era}} at The Imperial Library}} forged the Covenant with Alessia and her (symbolic) descendants while she lay in her death bed. Chim-el Adabal was fastened in an amulet and became the symbol of both the Covenant and the legitimacy of Cyrodiil's rulers.{{ref|name=AoK}}{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} A popular song in the Second Era however, claimed that the Chim-el Adabal was formed when a drop of blood fell from the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] as it was flung to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], and landed down an [[Lore:Ayleid Wells|Ayleid Well]], the [[Lore:Magicka|magicka]] within fusing it to a strong red crystal, which the Ayleids fashioned into a great gem.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad}}}} Indeed, Mannimarco described the artifact as an "Ayleid relic of Divine investiture".{{ref|name=TCA}} One of the stories about [[Lore:Rajhin|Rajhin]]'s most legendary heists claim he had stolen the Amulet of Kings from Alessia for a time.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Tales of Tribute: Series One}}}}

During the [[Lore:Dragon Break|Middle Dawn]], it is said that the Elder Council questioned the oversoul of emperors within the Amulet on what had occurred during the phenomenon. Though even the Elder Scrolls could not speak of what had occurred during the untime, the oversoul is said to have spoken of the event at length. According to [[Lore:Hestra|Empress Hestra]], [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] became an Empire across the stars and, according to Shor-El, Cyrodiil became an egg. The accounts of most emperors were said to have been spoken in a language that could only be spoken sideways.{{ref|name=WWY|{{Cite Book|Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?}}}}

Following the breakup of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], the Amulet of Kings was lost amid chaotic civil war and its attendant looting. According to legend, a certain [[Lore:Hrol|King Hrol]] tracked down the spirit of Saint Alessia, who wore the Amulet of Kings around her neck. They coupled, and a hill named [[Lore:Sancre Tor|Sancre Tor]] rose on the spot. The infant Reman Cyrodiil was found upon the crest, wearing the amulet on his brow.{{ref|name=R|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}} The Amulet of Kings was restored to prominence during the Reman years, lending legitimacy to that dynasty. After that time, the Dragonfires ritual became essential to the coronation of each new Emperor. During the ritual, the new monarch was soul-linked to the Amulet of Kings and sent to the [[Lore:Temple of the One|Temple of the One]] to light the enchanted Dragonfires, which then burned until his death.{{ref|name=BoST|{{Cite Book|Battle of Sancre Tor}}}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-quest-Shadow of Sancre Tor 05.jpg|thumb|left|The Amulet of Kings, hidden in Sancre Tor]][[File:LG-cardart-Sai Sahan.png|Sai Sahan guarding the Amulet behind him|right|thumb]][[File:ON-crown store-Red Diamond Guardian.jpg|thumb|right|Red Diamond Guardians]]
In the middle of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], the [[Lore:Five Companions|Five Companions]] embarked on a quest to rediscover the Amulet of Kings. Their leader, [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]], was led to believe that the Amulet could be used in a ritual which would rekindle the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] and make him a true [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]. This would allow him to be recognized as the legitimate ruler of the Empire. After years of searching, the Companions eventually found the Amulet. In {{Year|2E 579}}, they initiated the ritual in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. It was then that Mannimarco, another one of the Companions, revealed his treachery and corrupted the ritual to break the ancient covenant between [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and mortals, a feat achieved through the inscription of arcane runes over a number of weeks.{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}} This caused the catastrophic event known as the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] which sundered the veil between worlds and set Nirn adrift in Oblivion, making it become "unmoored from the fabric of the [[Lore:Aurbis|multiverse]]", causing mages to go mad, unleashing natural disasters and mystical aftershocks across the world, and making the [[Lore:The Serpent|Serpent]] [[Lore:Stars|constellation]] appear so large it seemed to threaten all other star-signs at once. This event disrupted the connection between Nirn's inhabitants and their [[Lore:Souls|souls]], and served as the precursor to Molag Bal's [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]].{{ref|name=ILTESO|[[Online:Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online]]}}{{ref|name=Prophet|[[Online:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the subsequent chaos, [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]] took the Amulet of Kings and fled. He then hid it within the ruins of Sancre Tor.

At some point after the Soulburst, mysterious slate-skinned figures, known as Red Diamond Guardians, periodically appeared in the Imperial City to fight the Daedra. Each one had the Red Diamond encrusted upon their torso. No one knew where they came from or where they went after each fray.{{ref|[[ON:Red Diamond Guardian|Red Diamond Guardian]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Amulet was recovered from Sancre Tor in {{Year|2E 582}} by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]], Sahan and [[Lore:Lyris Titanborn|Lyris Titanborn]], retrieved in Sancre Tor after the defeat of Mannimarco, who had been planning to use it to imprison Molag Bal and use his spirit to become a [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] himself.{{ref|name=Mannimarco|[[Online:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It was later used by [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]] to perform the ritual which imbued the Vestige with the power of Akatosh. However, one of the Companions had to be sacrificed in the process. With the power granted by the ritual, the Vestige was able to defeat [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], forcing him to relinquish his grip on Nirn, and reclaim their soul.{{ref|name=ON|Events of [[Online:Shadow of Sancre Tor|Shadow of Sancre Tor]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ON2|Events of [[Online:Council of the Five Companions|Council of the Five Companions]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ON3|Events of [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} After Molag Bal's defeat, Abnur Tharn took the amulet and fled to Cyrodiil. Although it was suspected he would attempt to use it, unbeknownst to him the amulet's power had been expended for at least a generation, and he was not of the proper lineage to use it, thus making the attempt futile by his own admission.{{ref|name=ONMer|[[Online:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=AbnurRage|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:A Rage of Dragons|A Rage of Dragons]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}.

After the long decline of the Interregnum, [[Lore:Tiber Septim|General Talos]] reputedly recovered the amulet from the tomb of the Remans beneath Sancre Tor after retaking it from the Nordic and Breton invaders.{{ref|name=BoST}}

===Third Era===
[[File:LO-misc-Mankar Cameron.jpg|left|thumb|Mankar Camoran with the Amulet]][[File:LG-cardart-Avatar of Akatosh 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Avatar of Akatosh summoned by Martin's shattering of the Amulet]]

The Amulet of King's political connection to the Imperial dynasty was evident enough that battle for the Ruby Throne between [[Lore:Potema Septim|Queen Potema]] of Solitude and her son Uriel Septim III against Empress [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]] was called the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]]. A holiday called [[Lore:Broken Diamonds (holiday)|Broken Diamonds]] is celebrated in [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]] after the torn flags depicting the Red Diamond, which were trampled in the hunt for Kintyra II's executioner.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Broken Diamonds}}}}

The Hero of Kvatch recovered the amulet from the depths of the Imperial Subterrane after the assassination of Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]] at the hands of Daedric cultists. The Hero then delivered it to the Grandmaster of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]]. After escorting [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin]], Uriel's illegitimate son,  back to Grandmaster [[Lore:Jauffre|Jauffre]], the Amulet was stolen by the Mythic Dawn. The Hero was forced to enter the Paradise of [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]] in order to recover it. This achieved, preparations were made for Martin's coronation, but the ceremony was interrupted by a Daedric assault on the capital. After fighting his way to the Temple of the One, Martin was forced to smash the amulet, this being the only way to defeat [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]]. The power of the intermingled blood of the Covenant summoned a rare Aedric avatar in the form of Akatosh, a fiery dragon, thereby also causing Martin to [[Lore:Mantling|Mantle]] Akatosh and achieve [[Lore:Apotheosis|Apotheosis]], according to some sources.{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=gen}}}}{{ref|name=GIKurtKuhlmann|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann|Kurt Kuhlmann]]'s comments on ''[[General:Decrypting the Elder Scrolls|Decrypting The Elder Scrolls]]'' — [[Wikipedia:Game Informer|Game Informer]] article}}. Dagon was defeated, and the sacrifice "sealed the gates of Oblivion forever", ensuring that the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] could never again threaten the people of Tamriel. The new barrier was noted to be too powerful for even a [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]], an object in the presence of which it was said even "a thousand soul gems would likely dissolve", to breach for more than fleeting moments.{{ref|name=Excavation|[[Skyrim:Excavation Leader's Journal|Excavation Leader's Journal]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]}}{{ref|name=LtD|Events of [[Oblivion:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}} The near four thousand year history of the Amulet of Kings was ended.{{ref|name=OB|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
{{NewLine}}

==Cultural Importance==
The Red Diamond, symbolizing the Chim-el Adabal,{{ref|name=AoK4|[[Oblivion:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=AoK}} has become a major [[Lore:Symbols|symbol]] of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] and the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]].{{ref|[[Legends:Card Backs#Empire of Cyrodiil|Empire of Cyrodiil card back]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|[[Oblivion:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]] faction in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Additionally, the Red Diamond is also the badge and flag of the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim Dynasty of Emperors]].{{ref|name=BHOTE|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire}}}}{{ref|name=BD|{{Cite Book|Broken Diamonds}}}}

==Notes==
*An Amulet of Kings replica was made available as official [[Merchandise:Amulet of Kings|merchandise]].
*The name of the crystal in the center of the amulet, "Chim-el Adabal", can be translated to Royalty{{ref|name=36L12|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 12}}}} High Spirit{{Ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} Stone;{{ref|name=MWGJS|[[Morrowind:Gentleman Jim Stacey|Gentleman Jim Stacey]]'s dialogue in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} thus, the Spirit Stone of High Royalty.{{ref|group=UOL|[[General:Kurt Kuhlmann's Posts|Kurt Kuhlmann's Posts]]}}
*At some point after the Soulburst, mysterious slate-skinned figures, known as Red Diamond Guardians, periodically appeared in the Imperial City to fight the Daedra. Each one had the Red Diamond encrusted upon their torso. No one knew where they came from or where they went after each fray.{{ref|[[ON:Red Diamond Guardian|Red Diamond Guardian]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
*In an [[Lore:Many Paths|alternate reality]], Mannimarco's plan to replace Molag Bal as the Prince of Domination succeeded, and he presided over the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] from a throne formed of bone and souls placed above the White-Gold Tower. The world was plunged into darkness, flooded with undead and other horrors.{{Ref|name=TVTT|{{Cite Book|Traitor's Vault Temporal Tome}}}}
*During the ritual that imbued the Vestige with divine power, Abnur Tharn invoked the Divines and called them to imbue the Vestige with their glory. The empowered Vestige also described themselves as being filled with the "righteous, nigh-infinite, power" of the Divines, power enough to force Molag Bal to relinquish his grasp on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. After Molag Bal's defeat, Meridia also claimed she could sense the "strength of the Divines", burning brightly within the returned soul of the Vestige.{{ref|name=ONMer}} This might mean the other Divines, who are represented on the artifact by the eight smaller gems in the frame, also have an arcane link to the Amulet.{{ref|name=Abnur|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The God of Schemes|The God of Schemes]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ON3Logs|Quest logs during [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ON3|Events of [[Online:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
*According to [[Lore:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]], founder of the [[Lore:Alkosh|Pride of Alkosh]] it is said that, comparing the Amulet of Kings to the [[Lore:Mask of Alkosh|Mask of Alkosh]] is akin to comparing the sands of the hourglass to the moments that pass through time, and that whether they can truly be the same or are truly so different, is an answer only the [[Lore:Alkosh|Dragon King]] knows.{{ref|name=Ja'darri|[[Online:Ja'darri|Ja'darri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
*[[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] is said to have been a "diamond soaked red with the blood of elves" whose "facets could un-sector and form" into a "man whose every angle" could "cut" Alessia's jailers, and who was called PELIN-EL. It is said that, under Pelinal's armor, there was a gaping chest with no heart, only a "red rage shaped diamond that sang like a mindless [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]]". This red diamond from which Pelinal is said to have formed from might have been the Amulet of Kings.{{ref|name=TSOP6|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 6}}}}{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}}
* One of [[General:Lawrence Schick|Lawrence Schick]]'s plans for Abnur Tharn was to have him eventually become [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]] after centuries of prolonged contact with the Amulet of Kings, but the idea was rejected.{{Ref|name=TelMora|group=UOL|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/20191020091427/http://www.telmoraindependent.net/2019/10/03/the-tel-mora-independent-press-interview-with-lawrence-schick/ ''Tel Mora Independent Press: Post-ZOS interview with Lawrence Schick'']}}
*The mysterious [[Lore:The Prophet|Prophet]] of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] had knowledge of the Amulet's oversoul and has shown to be able to sense its presence, and claims to hear it speak.{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Knights of the Nine|''Oblivion: Knights of the Nine'']]}}
