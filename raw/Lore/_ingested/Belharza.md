[[Lore:Belharza|Belharza]] '''the Man-Bull''' was the son of the Empress [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] and her consort, the [[Lore:Demigod|demigod]] [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], and is believed to be one of the first [[Lore:Minotaur|minotaur]]s.{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Minotaurs}}}} He succeeded his mother as ruler of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] upon her death in {{Year|1E 266}}. Little is known about the emperor's life and reign as most records of the period were destroyed following the rise of the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]].{{ref|name=OM}} Private correspondence between Belharza and Alessia is held in the {{Lore Link|Library of Dusk}} in [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], though its contents are unspecified.{{ref|name=TLoDRB|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}} According to some scholars, the curvature of Belharza's horns (infamously depicted in Tertia Gallus's heretical renderings circa {{Year|1E 1300}}) are one of the only aesthetic features that sets him apart from Morihaus.{{ref|name=Horns|[[Online:Belharza's Band|Etched Silver Horns]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He was said to have wielded a weapon called the Axe of Belharza.{{Ref|name=Campaigns|[[Online:Campaigns|Campaign]] names in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It was theorized that early Alessian priests might have honored him and his father with sacramental oils.{{ref|name=Oil|[[Online:Belharza's Band|Alessian Sacramental Oil]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It was speculated by some scholars that legends about Belharza were topic of interest even among the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]].{{ref|name=Rail|[[Online:Belharza's Band|Bull's Head Gallery Rail]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Certain sources erroneously dismiss Belharza's Minotaur origins and assert he was a [[Lore:Man|Man]],{{ref|name=Oil}} who "met his fate at the biting-tips of [[Lore:Mer|Elven]] spears."{{Ref|name=TTOM|{{Cite Book|The Truth of Minotaurs}}}} A surviving document from an Alessian Order sect indirectly implies that Belharza was troubled by large [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] hordes.{{ref|name=COTF|{{Cite Book|Cleansing of the Fane}}}} One controversial interpretation of an ancient tablet fragment called the Belharza Stone asserts that the precursors of the xenophobic Alessian Order may have openly clashed with Belharza, and violently purged the minotaurs from the Empire.{{ref|name=OM}} 

[[Lore:Belharza's Band|Belharza's Band]] was a magically imbued [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] band linked to him.{{ref|name=BB|[[Online:Belharza's Band|Belharza's Band]]'s antiquity in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>
