[[Lore:Hopesfire|Hopesfire]], or '''the Blade of [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]]''', is a one-handed [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]]i sword from the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. Hopesfire and its twin, [[Lore:Trueflame|Trueflame]], represented the pinnacle of Dwemer craftsmanship. The twin blades possessed unearthly fire enchantments. They were presented as wedding gifts to Lord Indoril [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] and Almalexia by the Dwemer King [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac]].{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia|[[Tribunal:Almalexia|Almalexia]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}<noinclude><noinclude>

==History==
Though Trueflame was shattered during the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] circa {{Year|1E 700}}, Almalexia kept Hopesfire well-preserved over the years.{{ref|name=TRAlmalexia}}

Angered by the death and destruction wrought upon her city by [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]], Almalexia drew Hopesfire, and she and Sotha Sil jumped into the battlefield that was lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. Almalexia plunged her sword into the Flame Tyrant, and carved him from within.{{ref|name=TTIS8|{{Cite Book|The Truth in Sequence: Volume 8}}}} From this blade flurry originated the [[Lore:Ruinach|Ruinachs]], which fell from Dagon's flayed skin.{{ref|name=TTIS8}}{{ref|name=BPTMR|''[[General:Blackwood Preview—The Mighty Ruinachs|Blackwood Preview—The Mighty Ruinachs]]'' – The Elder Scrolls Online official website}} Almalexia took on the hordes of these ghoulish creatures which surrounded them, while Sotha Sil used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Daedric Prince into submission.{{ref|name=TTIS8}} Wounded, Sil whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".{{ref|name=TTIS8}}{{ref|name=2920v11|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Dusk (v11)}}}}

When the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]] came to Mournhold after [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]]'s fall in {{Year|3E 427}}, Almalexia sought to trick, entrap, and destroy them as part of this plot. She first turned on [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and slew him in his [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]], then unleashed its [[Lore:Fabricants|mechanical inhabitants]] into her own city in order to frame her old friend. Her ruse lured the Nerevarine to Sotha Sil's legendary home, where she hoped that the reincarnation of her husband would be killed by the inhabitants. When the Nerevarine persevered, Almalexia wielded her blue-flamed sword and tried to finish the job herself. But her powers failed her, and she died to the Nerevarine's hands in the Clockwork City.{{ref|name=TRMG|[[TR:The Mad God|The Mad God]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} 

It is unknown if the Nerevarine added Almalexia's blade to their arsenal after her defeat,{{ref|name=TRMG|[[TR:The Mad God|The Mad God]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} but rumors swirled at the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] that the Nerevarine went on an expedition to [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]] and has not been heard from since.{{ref|name=OBRC|[[Oblivion:Radiant Conversations|Radiant Conversations]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} An alternative is that she may have been buried with it when both her and Sotha Sil's corpses were retrieved from the Clockwork City by [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], who interred them in the proper Velothi fashion.{{ref|name=MKPosts|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}

Hopesfire eventually found its way into the hands of an underground cult based in [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]] that was still dedicated to the worship of the Tribunal in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. These cults were considered heretical by wider [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmeri]] society and were persecuted by the New Temple.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Heretic Dossier: Blacksmith's Confessional}}}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] was alerted to the cult's existence while the Tribunal faithful were attempting to recover additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.{{ref|name=GotT|[[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal (quest)|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] quest from [[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation]] for ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} One member of the cult, a Dunmer named [[Skyrim:Erden_Relvel|Erden Relvel]], became corrupted by the dangerous relics in the cult's possession and became a priest of Dagoth Ur. He created [[Lore:Ash Zombie|ash zombie]] minions and sent them to attack the temple at Ashfall's Tear. After recovering Hopesfire from the heretic's armory, the Last Dragonborn challenged Erden, and slew him.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Ashen Heart|Ashen Heart]] quest from [[Skyrim:Ghosts of the Tribunal|Ghosts of the Tribunal]] [[Skyrim:Creation Club|Creation]] for ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
