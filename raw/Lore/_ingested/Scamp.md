[[Lore:Scamp|Scamps]] are one of the lesser sentient [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], who often enter into the mortal world to cause mischief and carry out errands for their Princes.{{Ref|name=DD}} 
Scamps have been described disparagingly as the [[Lore:Skeever|skeevers]] of Oblivion. They are capable of more easily "wriggling thorough the gaps" between realms.{{ref|[[ON:Sorcerer Vunal|Sorcerer Vunal]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} They are relatively weak Daedra, and often cowardly.{{ref|name=BScamp}}{{ref|name=Elante|{{Cite Book|Elante's Notes}}}} They can can be easily defeated by most fighters except in large numbers. Other Daedra often look down upon them,{{ref|name=Me|[[BS:Dremora Methats|Dremora Methats]]' dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=Morphoid|[[Battlespire:Morphoid Daedra|Morphoid Daedra]] dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} though some find them endearing.{{ref|name=Spider}}

Scamps are typically thought of as dull creatures, described as semi-intelligent or as having "simple thoughts".{{Ref|name=VoD|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Daedra}}}}{{Ref|name=SotD|{{Cite Book|Spirit of the Daedra}}}} For the greater Daedra, Scamps are useful minions, as their nimble hands can carry and manipulate objects, and they are intelligent enough to be cunning opponents in combat.{{Ref|name=ONLoad|[[ON:Buraniim Isle|Buraniim Isle]] [[Online:Loading Screens|Loading Screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Of the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Princes]] they serve, they are most commonly associated with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]],{{Ref|name=DD|{{Cite Book|Darkest Darkness}}}} though they also serve [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]],{{ref|name=ON|[[ON:Scamp|Scamps]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]],{{Ref|name=MWLustidrike|[[MW:Lustidrike|Lustidrike]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=Juggle}} [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]],{{Ref|name=LA:Lyranth|{{Cite Book|Lyranth the Foolkiller Answers Your Questions}}}} and [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaermina]].{{ref|name=VaerHead|[[ON:Brother Gerard|Brother Gerard]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Dreams to Nightmares|Dreams to Nightmares]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

In appearance, Scamps are diminutive, [[Lore:Goblin-ken|goblinesque]] humanoids. They possess tapered ears, furry legs, and [[Lore:Rat|rat]]-like tails. They have sharp claws and teeth which they often use in combat or defense.{{ref|name=BScamp}} Scamps apparently possess an anus,{{ref|name=BScamp}} and are capable of defecation.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Rosentia Gallenus|Rosentia Gallenus]]' dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}</onlyinclude>

==History==
{{Quote Box|''The Duke of Scamps therefore became a lesser thing, as did all his own children. Molag Bal said to them: 'You are the sons of liars, dogs, and wolf-headed women.' They have been useless to summon ever since.''|''[[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 14|The Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec, Sermon Fourteen]]''}}[[File:LG-cardart-Invasion Scout.png|A Scamp Invasion Scout (Legends)|thumb|left]]
According to ''[[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|The 36 Lessons of Vivec]]'', Scamps were once greater beings. This religious text claims that at some point in the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], a Duke among Scamps known as [[Lore:Kh-Utta|Kh-Utta]] was summoned by Lord Mehrunes Dagon to bring the pennants of his seven legions. He bought them before the Daedric Prince's major domo, whose head was a bubble of foul water and fire, who saw the pennants and the legions they commanded. The major domo then told the Duke that while his legions were mighty, they were not enough to destroy [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] or the Triune way.{{ref|name=S7|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 7}}}}  

Later on, during the union of Molag Bal and [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]], known as the Pomegranate Banquet, the Duke of Scamps arrived, and Molag Bal looked on the seven pennants with anger. It is said then that "the King of Rape had become necessary and therefore troubled for the rest of time". Molag Bal's legions and Kh-Utta's would fall into open war, but the [[Lore:Monster Children|children of Molag Bal and Vivec]] were too elaborate in power and form for the Duke's legions to handle. As a result, the Duke of Scamps was said to have become a lesser thing, as did all his children. It said this is the reason Scamps have been useless to summon ever since.{{ref|name=S14|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 14}}}}

A burnt paper dated to {{Year|1E 503}} details a fire scamp's exploits into writing without burning the paper.{{Ref|name=Izzmothar|{{Cite Book|Izzmothar's Power}}}}

Scamps have taken part in many Daedric incursions throughout history. Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, Scamps were among Molag Bal's forces during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], as well as participating in Mehrunes Dagon's later invasion attempt in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]].{{ref|name=ON}} In {{Year|3E 398}}, the [[LO:Scamp Clan|Scamp Clan]] named "''Dagon's Loyal Troops''" took part in Dagon's campaign to invade the [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]], serving under a Daedra known as [[LO:Rishaal Tamir|Rishaal Tamir]].{{ref|name=BScamp}}{{ref|name=Spider|[[BS:Spider Daedra|Spider Daedra]] dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}{{ref|name=Athenaeum|{{Cite Book|Battlespire Athenaeum|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|102}} In {{Year|3E 433}}, they took part in the many invasions of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]],{{ref|name=OB|[[OB:Scamp|Scamp]]s in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} often serving as simple scouts.{{ref|[[Legends:Invasion Scout|Invasion Scout]] card in ''[[LG:Legends|Legends]]''}}

==Behavior and Uses==
{{Quote Box|''Keys? Keys in dark, NASTY place. Near my tail. Want look? See?''|A Scamp taunting the [[Lore:Apprentice|Apprentice]]{{ref|name=BScamp}} (listen <ab>BS-audio-Scamp Keys.wav</ab>)}}[[File:MW-concept-Scamp 01.jpg|thumb|left|A Scamp servant (Morrowind concept art)]]
Scamps are known for being mischievous, unruly, and vulgar.{{ref|name=CETS}}{{ref|name=Me}}{{ref|name=PCC}} They have been to know to enjoy playful activities like juggling balls of fire,{{ref|name=Juggle|[[LG:Bedeviling Scamp|Bedeviling Scamp]] card in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} or playing [[Lore:Music|musical]] [[Lore:Instruments|instruments]] such as flutes or drums.{{ref|[[Online:Getting the Band Together|Getting the Band Together]] quest in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

Many do not speak [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]],{{Ref|name=ONKizna|Dialogue with [[ON:Kizna|Kizna]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} instead they speak their own Daedric [[Lore:Languages|tongue]].{{ref|Dialogue with [[Online:Caal|Caal]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CETS|{{Cite Book|Creating ESO: The Scamp|ns_base=General}}}}  While Scamps are capable of being fluent in the language,{{Ref|name=MWLustidrike}} those that do know Tamrielic often speak in a strange or broken manner,{{ref|name=BScamp|[[BS:Scamp|Scamp]] dialogue in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}} with the same going for those that can write.{{Ref|name=Izzmothar}}{{Ref|name=JDN|{{Cite Book|Journal of Scamp Naal|ns_base=ON}}}}{{Ref|name=MTL|{{Cite Book|Mikget's To-Do List|ns_base=ON}}}}
[[File:MW-ing-Scamp Skin.jpg|thumb|right|Scamp Skin (Morrowind)]]
Scamps typically fill lesser positions in Daedric hierarchies.{{Ref|name=SotD}}{{ref|name=Morphoid}} Typically only ranking above simple "mindless" creatures like [[Lore:Elemental Daedra|Atronachs]] or trained beasts like [[Lore:Shalk|shalks]].{{Ref|name=Izzmothar}} Though there are exceptions, as a researcher has observed a Scamp giving orders to a [[Lore:Skaafin|Skaafin]].{{Ref|name=True|{{Cite Book|On the True Nature of Daedra}}}} [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] Kynreeves often keep Scamps as a sort of pet, giving them iron collars inscribed with runes that denote the Scamp as their property.{{ref|[[ON:Blighted Iron Collar|Blighted Iron Collar]] item description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} 

Scamps in the service of Molag Bal have often been given strange roles. Molag Bal's forces weaponized Scamps by loading them into boxes and catapulting them into enemy forces.{{ref|name=TESO|[[General:The Elder Scrolls Online - The Siege Cinematic Trailer|The Elder Scrolls Online - The Siege Cinematic Trailer]]}} If one of Molag Bal's servants is "put on trial" for their transgressions, they are tried in a facility known as the [[Lore:Court of Contempt|Court of Contempt]], where they are entitled to a counsel of Scamps outfitted in puffy wigs. However, Scamps are not allowed to speak in the Court of Contempt, due to their habitual vulgarity, and the Court has a one-hundred-percent conviction rate of defendants, otherwise known as "the Guilty". These trials are mostly for show and never end fairly.{{ref|name=PCC|{{Cite Book|Protocols of the Court of Contempt}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|On the Matter of the Prisoners|ns_base=Online}}}}<onlyinclude>

Scamps are one of the most common lesser Daedra summoned by mortals, often serving as [[Lore:Familiar|familiars]].{{ref|name=SUFS|[[Online:Summon Unstable Familiar|Summon Unstable Familiar]] and [[ON:Summon Volatile Familiar|Summon Volatile Familiar]] skills in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=CAC|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Caal: Assistant Chronicler}}}} Scamps are most commonly used by mages for simple tasks like gruntwork,{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Zagrugh's Journal}}}} but some could be entrusted with more complex tasks. One Scamp familiar was even granted full membership in the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]], serving as an assistant chronicler and merchant.{{ref|name=CAC}} The [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] also trained scamps to wear packs to carry their valuables.{{ref|[[ON:Undaunted Porter Scamp|Undaunted Porter Scamp]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
</onlyinclude>
[[Lore:Scamp Skin|Scamp skin]] is an ingredient used in [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemy]] that can be removed from Scamps while they are still bound to the mortal plane. It is a rare ingredient and alchemists have trouble keeping the substance in stock.{{ref|[[MW:Anarenen|Anarenen]] and [[MW:Tendris Vedran|Tendris Vedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} It is used for straining in alchemy and cooking.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Hanin's Wake}}}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Slaughterfish Steak|ns_base=GEN}}}} Daedric commanders have been known to skin Scamps in their service for failure to achieve desired goals.{{ref|name=Spider}} Scamp guts are used by some [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] in brewing a type of strong [[Lore:Rotmeth|rotmeth]].{{ref|[[Online:Irwerdil|Irwerdil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} Scamp liver is said to be tender and delicious.{{ref|[[Online:Gwinor|Gwinor]]'s dialoue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] warriors sometimes use Scamp-hide shields.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}} Scamp skulls may be carved with strange glyphs and arcane symbols for use in unlawful ritualistic purposes.{{Ref|name=RDS|[[ON:Treasures/Ritual Objects#Ritual Daedra Skull|Ritual Daedra Skull]] item description in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

==Variants==
Many varieties of Scamps exist, smaller and weaker Scamps are sometimes known as Stunted Scamps.{{ref|name=St|[[OB:Stunted Scamp|Stunted Scamp]]s in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Scamps that serve Molag Bal, like many of the Daedra that serve him, are known to have a blue hue. Stonefire Scamps are a horned variety that serve Molag.{{ref|[[ON:Stonefire Scamp|Stonefire Scamp]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Additionally, Molag has utilized Scamps that have been [[Lore:Mind-Shriven|mind-shriven]], they can usually be found among other mind-shriven creatures.{{ref|[[ON:Mind-Shriven Scamp|Mind-Shriven Scamp]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Magma Scamps are a variety of horned scamp that specifically serve Mehrunes Dagon. They are often engulfed in flames and their skin is comparable to cracked molten rock.{{ref|[[ON:Magma Scamp|Magma Scamp]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 
Everscamps are a distinct type of Scamp that are summoned by the [[Lore:Staff of the Everscamp|Staff of the Everscamp]], a cursed Daedric [[Lore:Staff|staff]]. These Scamps are completely non-hostile, and have a horrible smell. They follow around whoever has been cursed by the staff, making their life miserable.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Everscamp|Everscamps]] in [[Oblivion:Whom Gods Annoy|Whom Gods Annoy]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

Other beings have occasionally taken on the forms of Scamps, such as [[Lore:Celestial|Celestials]] and [[Lore:Maligraphy|maligraphies]].{{ref|[[ON:Celestial Scamp|Celestial Scamp]]s in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Infinite Archive/Enemies|Enemies]] in the [[ON:Infinite Archive|Infinite Archive]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Barbas|Barbas]], the [[Lore:Dog|hound]] of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], has been know to take on the form and persona of a Scamp named Creeper.{{ref|name=OBBarbas|[[Oblivion:Clavicus Vile|Barbas]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[MW:Creeper|Creeper]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=CreeperESO|[[ON:Creeper|Creeper]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
