The [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] (sometimes '''White Gold Tower'''),{{Ref|name=CHoA|{{Cite Book|Cherim's Heart of Anequina}}}} also called the '''Imperial Palace''', '''White-Stone Tower'''  or simply '''[[Lore:The Towers#White-Gold|White-Gold]]''',{{ref|name=Enigma|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}}{{ref|name=GTTIC|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Imperial City}}}}{{ref|name=REC|{{Cite Book|Red Eagle's Song}}}} is the enormous central spire of the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], located in the heart of the Palace District, [[Lore:Green Emperor Way|Green Emperor Way]].{{ref|name=OB|The [[Oblivion:Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}<noinclude>

White-Gold was built in emulation of the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]],{{ref|name=BOTZ|[[Online:Beacon of Tower Zero|Beacon of Tower Zero]] furnishing name, appearance and Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the oldest known structure on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} Like the other [[Lore:The Towers|Towers]] of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], some speculate that White-Gold Tower wields the power necessary to keep [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] from dissolving back into [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=TICPage|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=books}}}}{{rp|123}}{{ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Nu-Mantia Intercept|ns_base=gen}}}}

==History==
[[File:LO-book-Before the Ages of Man.png|A depiction of Aldmer with the White-Gold Tower in the Merethic Era|thumb|left]][[File:LG-cardart-Umaril the Unfeathered 02.jpg|Umaril the Unfeathered|thumb|right]]
The tower was built by the earliest [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] migrants to Cyrodiil in the middle [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] and was the center of the region occupied by their descendants, the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]].{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} The tower was originally called the '''Temple of the Ancestors'''{{ref|name=OBUmbacano}} (known to the Nedes as the '''Citadel'''),{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and was home to the [[Lore:Ten Ancestors|Ten Ancestors]], a set of Ayleid statues made from [[Lore:Meteoric Iron|meteoric iron]] and [[Lore:Meteoric Glass|glass]].{{ref|name=OBUmbacano|[[Oblivion:Umbacano|Umbacano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The distance between the Ayleids' lands and those of their theoretical overlords led to the establishment of White-Gold Tower as an independent city-state in {{Year|1E 0}};{{ref|name=BTAOM}} a state that lasted until [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] overthrew Ayleid rule in {{Year|1E 243|243}}.{{ref|name=LKotA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}}{{ref|name=SatD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} The Ten Ancestors were removed from the temple before its sacking and were dispersed among several Ayleid cities.{{ref|name=OBUmbacano}}

During the reign of the [[Lore:Ayleid Empire|Ayleid Empire]], at the time of [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]], [[Lore:Umaril|Umaril the Unfeathered]] reigned as liege-lord of the other Ayleid sorcerer-kings and holder of the Cyrodilic throne, known as the "Cyrodiil",{{ref|name=Origin|{{Cite Book|The Origin of Cyrus!|ns_base=Books}}}}{{Ref|name=UIU|{{Cite Book|Umaril is Undone}}}} from the throne room of the Temple of the Ancestors, a right he was bestowed thanks to his incredible power and status as the most favored champion of the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]].{{ref|name=MTCKN|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - King Narilmor}}}}

In {{Year|1E 243}}, the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] came to a head, and the armies of man, led by [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], attacked and took the White-Gold Tower.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} According to the official histories of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], when the tower was captured, all the Ayleids' relics of arcane power were taken and destroyed. However, some insisted that an unknown Ayleid king escaped with the chief artifacts, which eventually found themselves a home in the Reliquary of Stars in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{ref|name=ONloadReliquary|[[Online:Reliquary of Stars|Reliquary of Stars]] [[Online:Loading Screens|loading screen]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RoC|{{Cite Book|Remnants of Cyrod}}}}

Under the human-dominated [[Lore:Empire|Empires]], it served as the Imperial Palace and as a defining symbol of those Empires. Some scholars even suggest the tower itself changed Cyrodiil from a subtropical jungle into a realm of temperate forests and fields in order to meet the needs and wishes of its new human masters.{{Ref|name=SCAS|{{Cite Book|Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation}}}} The tower hosted the vaults and libraries of the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Moth Priesthood]]. It can be seen from all over the Imperial City and the surrounding countryside. Underneath the tower and in the vast [[Lore:Imperial City Sewers|Imperial City Sewers]] is the [[Lore:Barathrum Centrata|Barathrum Centrata]], the centralmost point of the sewer system and foundation of the White-Gold Tower.{{Ref|name=IAC|{{Cite Book|Imperial Architect's Correspondence}}}}

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 582}}, the Ruby Throne was located on the ground floor.{{ref|name=ESO|[[Online:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} By {{Year|3E 433}}, however, the ground floor was the location of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]] chambers.{{ref|name=OB}}

When the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]] began, the largest [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchor]] opened above the skies of the Imperial City, intent on pulling the White-Gold Tower, a metaphysical focus so important it had the potential to pull all of Tamriel in with it once taken, into [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]].{{ref|name=Tower|[[Online:White-Gold Tower |White-Gold Tower]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-place-Time-Lost Throne Room 02.jpg|The Time-Lost Throne Room|thumb|right]]
[[File:ON-place-White-Gold Tower.jpg|thumb|left|White-Gold Tower during the Planemeld]]
After the [[Lore:Dark Anchors|Great Shackle]] was destroyed and [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] was wounded by an [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]]-empowered Vestige,{{ref|name=Unleashed|Events of [[ON:Vanus Unleashed|Vanus Unleashed]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Final|Events of [[ON:The Final Assault|The Final Assault]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Schemes|Events of [[ON:God of Schemes|God of Schemes]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} a final attempt to restart the Planemeld was made.{{ref|group=UOL|[[Online:Temple District|Temple District]] background in the [[General:Future of The Elder Scrolls Online Panel from QuakeCon 2014#Temple District|Future of The Elder Scrolls Online Panel from QuakeCon 2014]]}} Molag Bal sent a [[Lore:Xivkyn|Xivkyn]] called [[Lore:Molag Kena|Molag Kena]] to pose as the seemingly-deceased Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]] and "retake" the White-Gold Tower. In truth, Kena sought an [[Lore:Elder Scroll|Elder Scroll]] within the Tower and planned to use its power to initiate the "Planemeld Obverse" and begin simultaneously pulling Coldharbour into Nirn. However, a [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|moth priestess]] called [[Lore:Terran Arminus|Terran Arminus]] had read the scroll before the Daedric occupation of the Imperial City, and had learned that she would need aid in order to save the scroll. This aid arrived in the form of a group of [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], and the prophesied Soulless One, who helped Clivia's entourage ascend the shattered reality of the Tower's upper levels before being betrayed by the imposter. These heroes defeated Molag Kena atop the Tower before she could fully complete the ritual, and in the process destroyed the Anchor above the city.{{ref|[[ON:Planemeld Obverse|Planemeld Obverse]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Terran|[[ON:Sister Terran Arminus|Sister Terran Arminus]]' dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Around the same time, the fragments of the [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]] were located by the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]]. The pieces were scattered all around Tamriel: the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine]] and White-Gold fragments in [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], the [[Lore:Orichalc Tower|Orichalc]] and [[Lore:Throat of the World|Snow]] fragments in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], the [[Lore:Numidium|Walk-Brass]] and [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red]] fragments in [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal]] fragment in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]], and the {{Lore Link|Green-Sap}} fragment in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]]. A member of the Psijic Order, the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], sealed the [[Lore:Dragon Break|time breaches]] the Staff had created around the continent with the aid of the [[Lore:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]], which allowed them to locate and retrieve the fragments. The reassembled Staff was subsequently stolen by Josajeh, a member of the Order who wanted to use the staff to [[Lore:Time Magic|alter time]] to reverse damage done to her family in the past, threatening to envelop Aurbis in a new [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]] or even unravel time entirely. The Vestige tracked Josajeh to the White-Gold Tower's Time-Lost Throne Room, a location detached from regular time and space, where she intended to use an Ayleid ritual that could, when used alongside the Tower and Staff, alter time. The Order's agent bested Josajeh and her [[Lore:Many Paths|alternate timeline]] selves that had entered the chamber through temporal rifts, and reclaimed the artifact for the Order.{{ref|name=ESOPsijic|The [[Online:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] questline in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=Augur of the Obscure|[[ON:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOCelarus}}

===Third Era===
Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, a member of the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] infiltrated the White-Gold Tower and stole an [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scroll]] from the library of the Moth Priests. The theft involved entering the basement of the White-Gold Tower, and activating a relic known as the Glass of Time, use of which revealed the path to the Old Way, the Ayleid section of the Imperial City's underground.{{ref|[[OB:The Ultimate Heist|The Ultimate Heist]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBGrayFox|[[OB:The Gray Fox|The Gray Fox]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord of Souls.jpg|Umbriel approaches the Imperial City|thumb|right]]
Around {{Year|4E 48}}, the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] city of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]] appeared in the skies of Tamriel, starting the [[Lore:Umbriel Crisis|Umbriel Crisis]].{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City}}}} 

[[Books:The Infernal City/Vuhon|Vuhon]] and [[Lore:Umbra|Umbra]], who had become one, intended to have Umbriel reach the White-Gold Tower, in order to use its power to render Umbra truly separate from Vile rather than a rogue aspect, thereby also freeing Umbriel itself. However, their plans were foiled due to a pact Vile had made with [[Lore:Attrebus|Prince Attrebus Mede]] and the mage [[Books:The Infernal City/Sul|Sul]]. In the end, Vile was able to regain the power he lost due to the crisis, seemingly reclaiming the spirit of Umbra and absorbing the thousands of [[Lore:Souls|souls]] within Umbriel's Ingenium in the process, but he and Umbriel were banished by the Umbriel-[[Lore:Hist|Hist]] before he carry out his plan to unleash more chaos in Tamriel by allowing Umbriel to fall onto the Imperial City itself.{{Ref|name=LOS}}

During the Umbriel Crisis, Imperial minister [[Lore:Hierem|Hierem]] planned to use the power of the White-Gold Tower to siphon the thousands of souls within Umbriel's [[Lore:Ingenium|Ingenium]], as he believed their power would allow him to achieve [[Lore:Lifespan|Apotheosis]] and become a [[Lore:Gods|god]]. However, he was slain before he could make the attempt.{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|}}}}

==As a Tower==
[[File:MER-Jewelry-Amulet of Kings.jpg|right|thumb|150px|The Chim-el Adabal (the Amulet of Kings), Stone of White-Gold Tower]]
White-Gold was built in open emulation of the [[Lore:Adamantine Tower|Adamantine Tower]],{{ref|name=BOTZ|[[Online:Beacon of Tower Zero|Beacon of Tower Zero]] furnishing name, appearance and Codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the oldest known structure on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Like the other Towers the elves built during the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], it was built to "tell its own story", consisting of rules inscribed by [[Lore:Variorum Architect|Variorum Architects]], thereby shaping reality within its domain. In addition, White-Gold was constructed to counteract the spiritual bleeding from [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] since the [[Lore:Convention|Convention]], serving as a point of reconnection between the mortal world and the divine.To this end, the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] entered into a pact with the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Princes]] to harvest castaway [[Lore:Chaotic Creatia|creatia]] from the [[Lore:Void|Void]]. White-Gold's resemblance to Ada-Mantia gave it even greater power to alter reality than the other elven Towers and, because it serves as a mimicry of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbic Wheel]] itself, it being meddled with could have disastrous consequences.{{ref|name=NMI|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|Nu-Mantia Intercept}}, The Imperial Library}}{{Ref|name=Enigma|{{Cite Book|Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|High Rock}}}} Indeed, some scholars believe that, were enough [[Lore:Tel Var Stones|Tel Var Stones]], used by the Ayleids as substitutes for the Zero Stone, to be removed from the Tower's walls, Creation could be undone and the Mundus could dissolve into Oblivion.{{ref|name=ONPhrastus|[[Online:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus of Elinhir]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Tower's role as a focus of mystical power meant that it being pulled into Oblivion during the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], could have resulted in the rest of Tamriel being pulled in with it.{{ref|name=WhiteGold|[[Online:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] loading scren in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Thus the [[Lore:Empire|Imperial]] Throne Room at the base of the White-Gold Tower is one of the most important metaphysical foci in all of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], so much so it could almost be said to exist adjacent to all time and space in [[Lore:Aedra|Aedric]] creation.{{ref|name=TimeLost|[[ON:Time-Lost Throne Room|Time-Lost Throne Room]] loading screen in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}. The White-Gold Tower itself is said to exist on a level beyond the physical, existing apart or detached from time.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[ON:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The nature of the Tower and its Time-Lost Throne Room allows it to serve as a catalyst for the use of powerful [[Lore:Time Magic|Time Magic]], with the Ayleids having invented a ritual that could rewrite time through the use of the [[Lore:Staff of Towers|Staff of Towers]] in conjuction with the Tower throne room. The ritual is highly dangerous, as it has the potential to create an "impossibly dense Aurbic gyre" of power that could create a new [[Lore:Dragon Break|Dragon Break]], or a catastrophe even worse than that.{{ref|name=ESOCelarus|[[ON:Loremaster Celarus|Loremaster Celarus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOJosajeh|[[Online:Josajeh|Josajeh]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''.}}{{ref|name=Augur of the Obscure|[[ON:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TowersFall|Events of [[ON:The Towers' Fall|The Towers' Fall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The White-Gold Tower's Stone was the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Chim-el Adabal]], the Amulet of Kings, said to have been formed from a drop of blood that fell from the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] as it sailed across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], which subsequently fell into an [[Lore:Ayleid Wells|Ayleid Well]] and was crystallized by the magicka within, before being cut into a diamond shape by the elves.{{Ref|name=Enigma}}{{ref|name=TCA|{{Cite Book|The Chim-el Adabal}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chim-el Adabal: A Ballad}}}}
