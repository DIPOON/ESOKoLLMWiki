'''Magical Transportation''' is performed primarily by means of '''teleportation''' [[Lore:magic|magic]]. Such forms of magic fall under many different schools, including [[Lore:Mysticism|Mysticism]], [[Lore:Conjuration|Conjuration]], and [[Lore:Thaumaturgy|Thaumaturgy]].<noinclude> Teleportation can occur via spell or via any of the other more stable methods listed below.{{NewLeft}}

==Portals==
[[File:ON-quest-Messages Across Tamriel (Covenant).jpg|A temporary, freestanding portal|thumb|left]][[File:ON-render-Infinite Archive 03.jpg|thumb|right|Portals manifested in the Infinite Archive]]
'''Portals''', also known as '''[[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]]al conjunctions''', are magical rifts that enable teleportation when entered.{{ref|[[Online:Azandar|Azandar]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Skilled mages can use portal magic to travel across Tamriel, even bypassing major obstacles, both man-made or natural.{{ref|name=WOTH|{{Cite Book|War of Two Houses}}}} Wielders of portal magic have even been known to open portals directly to different [[Lore:Planes of Existence|Planes of Existence]].{{ref|name=LunaBeReal|[[Online:Luna Beriel|Luna Beriel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Valsirenn|[[ON:Valsirenn|Valsirenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} However, the performer must know exactly where to generate the portals or he will face catastrophic consequences. This is, the mage must have been there or, at least, see clearly where the portal will spawn.{{ref|name=ONClarisse|[[ON:Lady Clarisse Laurent|Lady Clarisse Laurent]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONGabrielle|[[ON:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Portal seeds are a common component in apprentice-level portal creation.{{ref|{{Item Link|Portal Seed|id=64389|level=1|quality=1}}}} It is said that time itself sometimes refuses to work correctly around portals, making it possible for one to enter a portal first among a group but exit from it last.{{ref|name=Sai|[[ON:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Entering a portal created in a location where reality is being manipulated by powerful magic, such as the one used to shift objects and beings to different [[Lore:Layers of Reality|layers of reality]], can have unforeseen effects. Under such circumstances, it is possible for the portal to fracture, depositing those who enter in different locations or reality layers than the intended destination, or even ejecting them to become lost to the [[Lore:Void|Void]].{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Meln|[[ON:Meln the Mouthless|Meln the Mouthless]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} There have been cases of those born with natural affinity for portal magic, exhibiting exceptional skill in portal creation despite being entirely self-taught and having no formal training in the field.{{ref|name=Nahlia|[[ON:Votary Nahlia|Votary Nahlia]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Journal|{{Cite Book|Nahlia's Journal}}}} Travelling through portals has been described as "moving with the winds of the [[Lore:Souls|soul]]".{{ref|name=Netch|[[Online:The Netch|The Netch]]'dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Before its disbandment, the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] offered instant transportation between their main halls for a small fee. This service was available to members and non-members alike, though members are generally given a significant discount.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Erranil|Erranil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} It also had a subsidiary known as the Travelers League, which was only available to high ranking members and could teleport someone from anywhere in the world. This group were present in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] during the fifth century of the Third Era.{{Ref|name=DFTravelers|[[Daggerfall:Travelers League|Travelers League]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Mages Guild portals were designed to move people and material quickly, making it possible for many people to arrive to a destination en masse, and were less draining than personal teleportation spells.{{ref|name=LaloriaranCitadel|[[ON:King Laloriaran Dynar|Laloriaran Dynar]]'s dialogue during [[Online:The Citadel Must Fall|The Citadel Must Fall]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Guild would also use Portal Anchors, arcane devices that stabilize portals for a longer duration when needing to transport groups.{{ref|name=PortalAnchor|[[Online:Seasons of the Worm Cult Quest Items|Portal Anchor]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Stirk|[[Online:The Stirk Fellowship|The Stirk Fellowship]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Besides the Mages Guild, both the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] and the Church of the Nine offered teleportation spots in their shrines for those with the skills to use the relevant spells or the money to buy the related scrolls. This kind of teleportation used magic derived from the Tribunal or the Nine Divines, respectively.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Almsivi Intervention|Almsivi Intervention]]'s effect in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Divine Intervention|Divine Intervention]]'s effect in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] constructed a special portal to [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]] at the temple of [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]], which was used by [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]]. It is activated by a special staff entrusted to a Dragon Priest that guards the portal.{{ref|[[Skyrim:The World-Eater's Eyrie|The World-Eater's Eyrie]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The vampire [[Lore:Valerica|Valerica]] of the [[Lore:Volkihar|Volkihar]] Clan, created a portal to the [[Lore:Soul Cairn|Soul Cairn]] using finely ground bone meal, purified void salts, and soul gem shards, which have to be placed in a silver-lined portal vessel, blood then needs to be added to this vessel to act as a reacting agent.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Valerica's Journal}}}}

The ancient [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] moon priests practiced a rite known as the [[Lore:Shadow Dance|Shadow Dance]] or "Dance of Shadows", practiced famously by [[Lore:Anequina Sharp-Tongue|Anequina Sharp-Tongue]] and described as experiencing moonlight in its distilled form in order to "walk paths that took strange angles" to places "tangential to dreams, but never dreaming". Those attuned to the [[Lore:Moons|moons]] in this manner could "hear the moons sing" and could impact the Lunar Lattice, commanding the moons to move in the sky, open portals to other places and realms such as [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], the [[Lore:Jonelight Path|Jonelight Path]], or the moons themselves, and manipulate sources of lunar energy to perform deeds such as recharging a [[Lore:Dragonhorn|Dragonhorn]]. Descendants of the attuned could become linked to the spirits of their ancestor if they became attuned themselves, allowing them to perceive their memories and emotions and to act as vessels for them to act and communicate through should they desire it. Those who gained better understanding of their attunement learned how to "smell" magic and track and identify it by specific "scent", and gained greater mastery of portals, allowing them to open multiple portals in different locations at once and even imbue the portal magic into their blade and use it as a weapon in combat. Skilled use of the Shadow Dance was described by [[Lore:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]] as mastery of portal magic, and in some cases made its users so difficult to keep contained that the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] found it impossible to do so and eventually gave up entirely.{{ref|name=ESOKhamira|[[ON:Khamira|Khamira]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOCadwell|[[ON:Cadwell|Cadwell]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOKhun|[[ON:Khunzar-ri|Khunzar-ri]]'s dialogue during the quest [[ON:New Moon Rising|New Moon Rising]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Chronicles|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of Juha-ri}}}}{{ref|name=ESOCadwellBetrayer|[[ON:Cadwell the Betrayer (person)|Cadwell the Betrayer]]'s dialogue and abilities in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOVanus|[[ON:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=ESOLiveAUA|[[General:Lawrence Schick's Posts|Lawrence Schick's Posts from 2015 - ''Where was Cadwell originally come from?'']]}}

Certain life-force devouring [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] can transport the living to [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] in order for the dead to drain their essence.{{ref|name=ONThirster|[[Online:Soul Thirster|Soul Thirsters]] and [[Online:Baleful Ghost|Baleful Ghosts]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Shadow Magic|Shadow magic]] can be used to visit shadow realms- alternate versions of the world.{{Ref|[[Shadowkey:Multiplayer|Shadowkey multiplayer]]}}{{Ref|name=WotW|Events of [[Online:Woe of the Welkynars|Woe of the Welkynars]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

There are places in Nirn and other realms where the [[Lore:Many Paths|Many Paths]], the realities that exist beyond and surrounding the Aurbis, converge, and the way to them can be opened provided one has the required knowledge and means. Portals to the Paths could be opened by the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Ithelia|Ithelia]] and her servants, as well as through the use of the Lamp of Clarity, a relic of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]] though the artifact could only reveal gates which were already present at convergence points. [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]] also exhibited the ability to open portals to different Paths once Ithelia showed him how.{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Beragon|[[ON:Beragon|Beragon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} A number of deities have tales which describe them traversing the Paths in myths, these include [[Lore:Akha|Akha]], [[Lore:Alkosh|Alkosh]], [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]], [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Merrunz]], [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethra]], [[Lore:Malacath|Orkha]] and [[Lore:Alduin|Alkhan]].{{ref|name=Spirits|{{Cite Book|Spirits of Amun-dro}}}}{{ref|name=Bladesongs|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}} Traversing the realms of [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] through portals has been referred to as traversing the "multiverse", but this refers to travelling the realms within Aurbis itself, while the Many Paths go beyond that.{{ref|name=LunaBeReal|[[Online:Luna Beriel|Luna Beriel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=DaddySil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:A Necessary Alliance|ESO: ''Summerset'']]}}{{ref|name=Introduction|[[Online:Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Ithelia|[[ON:Ithelia|Ithelia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Leramil|[[ON:Leramil the Wise|Leramil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Teleporters==
[[File:OB-place-Frostcrag Spire Teleports.jpg|Frostcrag Spire teleportation pads|thumb|left]]
In the [[Lore:Western Reach|Western Reach]], there are transportation pads near the small village of [[Lore:Snowline|Snowline]].

The [[Lore:Battlespire|Battlespire]], had a magical transport vessel called the Star Galley, which was constructed by Imperial Battlemage and Artificer [[Lore:Clarentavious Valisious|Clarentavious Valisious]], it gave access to the other college sections. The vessel was normally kept in the Star Galley Crib. The Battlespire was also connected to a [[LO:Pillar of Light|pillar of light]] on Tamriel, which when entered would arrive at the [[Lore:Weir Gate|Weir Gate]] in the Battlespire.

[[Lore:Frostcrag Spire|Frostcrag Spire]] has teleportation pads to every Mages Guild hall in Cyrodiil, which were created by Arch-Mage [[Lore:Taris Rendil|Taris Rendil]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Frostcrag Spire Memoirs}}}} [[Lore:Xedilian|Xedilian]] in the Shivering Isles has teleporter pads.

In [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] prison, there are warp pads scattered about that can transport the user back to Helga in the Dungeon Camp.{{ref|The [[Stormhold:Places|places]] in ''[[Stormhold:Stormhold|Stormhold]]''}} Additionally, some of the crystals found in its tunnels are capable of the same effect when activated, becoming inert after one use.{{ref|[[Stormhold:Filled_Crystals#Warp_Crystal|Warp Crystal]] in ''[[Stormhold:Stormhold|Stormhold]]''}}

In the wilds surrounding [[Lore:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]], there are warp runes that can be stood upon that transport the user back to Dawnstar.{{ref|The [[Dawnstar:Places|places]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} Additionally, some runes can be carried and used to achieve the same effect, but only once.{{ref|[[Dawnstar:Magic_Items#Warp_Rune|Warp Rune]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}}

The ancient [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] fortresses around [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] are connected by magical means, such that anyone carrying the correct Propylon Indices can jump instantly from one to the next around the loop. Only one index exists for each fortress however, and most have been lost over time as the forts have been abandoned by their creators.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Folms Mirel|Folms Mirel]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} A Master Index that could be used to travel to any of the fortresses could be created by combining all the existing indices.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Master Index|Master Index]] [[Morrowind:Plugins|plugin]] in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s have their own form of teleportation magic as illustrated with Arlimahera's Altar, a device created by the eponymous [[Lore:Arlimahera|queen]] of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], who used it as an escape device from her own fortress in [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]]. It requires the use of two [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]n runestones.{{Ref|name=ONSkyward|[[Online:Skyward-Eyes|Skyward-Eyes]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Gates==
[[File:OB-place-Oblivion Gate.jpg|thumb|left|A Gate to Oblivion]]
The [[Lore:Barsaebic Ayleids|Barsaebic Ayleids]] had a realm of nexus gates which acted as portal pathways, called the [[Lore:Eld Angavar|Eld Angavar]].

The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] have portals called moon gates, a well-known one is the Moon Gate of Anequina, which when attuned through a proper ritual can teleport one to [[Lore:Masser|Jode]].{{ref|[[Online:Jode's Core (quest)|Jode's Core]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] uses Oblivion Gates to invade Tamriel through the use of Daedra, however, the Gates can also be entered to access [[Lore:Deadlands|The Deadlands]]. These Gates are held open by a [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]]. It is said that with each Oblivion Gate that opens, the barriers between [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] weaken further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [[Lore:Astronomy|Firmament]], can eventually result in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBMankar|[[Oblivion:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}  

One way of gaining access to the Soul Cairn is using the Voidgate in the Battlespire, which required a void guide to be activated.{{ref|[[BS:Level 2b|Voidgate]] in ''[[BS:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}

A temporary Shift Gate was used by the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] to travel from the Imperial City sewers to another city somewhere in Tamriel. Teleportation between cities was said to be common at the time.{{ref|[[Arena:Imperial Dungeons|Imperial Dungeons]] quest in Arena, [[Arena:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]]'s dialogue in the Arena [[Books:Arena Player's Guide|manual]]}}

The [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]] uses a large portal at the highest level of [[Lore:Lillandril|Lillandril]] to transport people from the main island to the college located across the waters. It is guarded by the Portal Master and it sits atop of a large dais.{{Ref|name=ONAstirhil|[[Online:Portal Master Astirhil|Portal Master Astirhil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

==Dark Anchors==
[[File:ON-place-Cloud Ruler Temple.jpg|Cloud Ruler Temple being pulled up by a Dark Anchor|thumb|right]]
[[Lore:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] were metaphysical devices which were used to weaken the barrier between [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]], allowing the two [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]] to be merged. They were created in the [[Lore:Black Forge|Black Forge]] using an interstitial alloy known as [[Lore:Tyranite Calx|Tyranite Calx]], and further infused with [[Lore:Soul Gems|soul gem fragments]] and trace other components.{{Ref|name=RJ|{{Cite Book|Reynila's Journal}}}}{{ref|name=PDA|{{Cite Book|Powering the Dark Anchors}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Projection of Vanus Galerion|Projection of Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Vanus Unleashed|Vanus Unleashed]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The chains were comprised of ebony-alloy cold-iron.{{ref|[[Online:The Black Forge|The Black Forge]] Loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Dark Anchors were created for use in the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s attempted conquest of Nirn which occurred after the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}} Singular Dark Anchors are capable of pulling portions of Nirn into Coldharbour,{{ref|name=VanusBTS|[[Online:Vanus Galerion|Vanus Galerion]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Breaking the Shackle|Breaking the Shackle]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and serve as [[Lore:Portals|portals]] allowing [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] to cross into Nirn,{{ref|name=ESODarkAnchor|[[Online:Dark Anchors|Dark Anchors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} each weakens the barrier between worlds further.{{ref|name=PDA}}

=={{Anchor|Obelisks of Order|Obelisk of Order}}==
[[File:LG-cardart-Jyggalag's Incursion.png|thumb|right|An Obelisk of Order]]
'''Obelisks of Order''' are grey crystalline structures found throughout the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]. The [[Lore:Forces of Order|Forces of Order]] use them as conduits into the Isles during the [[Lore:Greymarch|Greymarch]], when they begin to activate and gradually grow in power.{{ref|name=SIHaskill|[[SI:Haskill|Haskill]]'s dialogue in ''[[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Sheogorath has tried to remove them, but they always return. When active, the central crystal is levitated into the air by a continuous beam of lightning.{{ref|name=SIObelisk|[[Shivering:Obelisk of Order|Obelisks of Order]] in ''[[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}}

Each active obelisk is tended by a [[Lore:Priests of Order|Priest of Order]], a mortal who has betrayed Sheogorath to join [[Lore:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]]. With their link to Jyggalag giving them power, these priests perform rituals at the obelisks to summon [[Lore:Knights of Order|Knights of Order]] to the realm. As long as their obelisk is active, the priest cannot truly be killed. While the obelisks cannot be destroyed, they can be rendered inert by being overloaded with power. This can be done by placing [[Lore:Heart of Order|Hearts of Order]] inside the obelisk.{{ref|name=SIHaskill}} However, the obelisk can always be activated again by a priest.{{ref|name=SIObelisk}}

Although the Greymarch was ended permanently in {{Year|3E 433}},{{ref|[[Shivering:Jyggalag|Jyggalag]]'s dialogue in ''[[Shivering:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]]''}} Obelisks of Order could still be activated and used as a conduit to Jyggalag. One was used by [[Lore:Talym Rend|Talym Rend]] in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] to retrieve the [[Lore:Sword of Jyggalag|Sword of Jyggalag]].{{ref|[[Legends:Obelisk of Order|Obelisk of Order]] quest in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

==Transitus Network==
[[File:LG-cardart-Transitus Shrine.png|A Transitus Shrine|thumb|left]]
During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], the three alliances established individual '''Transitus Networks''' in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], a form of teleportation magic involving the maintenance of lit braziers. This temporary teleportation network allowed troops to instantaneously travel between keeps and outposts, although the networks seemingly had limited range and required constant maintenance by groups of mages when pushing deeper into enemy territory.{{ref|[[ON:Vother Herendas|Vother Herendas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Felycie Canis|Felycie Canis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Viriniel (Eastern Elsweyr Gate)|Viriniel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Transitus Networks are made up of small stone shrines, which glow with a cold blue flame when active.{{ref|[[ON:Transitus Network|Transitus Network]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Although similar in appearance, they are unrelated to the ancient [[Lore:Wayshrines|wayshrines]], which can only be utilized for teleportation by exceptional individuals.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Wayshrines of Tamriel}}}}

==Short Range Spells==
[[File:ON-npc-Sereyne 03.jpg|An [[Lore:Alfiq|Alfiq]] casting a teleportation spell (ESO)|thumb|left]]

It was frequently used by the [[Lore:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] as far back as the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] and still after the magic's restriction, typically in their towers to allow access to different floors.{{ref|[[ON:Tel Galen|Tel Galen]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MW|[[Morrowind:House Telvanni|Telvanni]] Towers in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} {{ref|name=DB|[[Skyrim:Tel Mithryn (settlement)|Tel Mithryn]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} In {{Year|3E 421}}, the [[Lore:Levitation Act|Levitation Act]] was passed, restricting the use of levitation magic.{{ref|name=OBDovyn|Overheard conversations regarding [[Oblivion:Dovyn Aren|Dovyn Aren]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

[[Lore:Sorcerers|Sorcerers]] commonly used lightning magic to travel short distances by "riding the lightning".{{ref|name=UOS|{{Cite Book|On the Utility of Shock Magic}}}}{{ref|name=BoltEsc|[[Online:Bolt Escape|Bolt Escape]] ability in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Dragonknights|Dragonknights]] wielded a spell to grow arcane, [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]]-like wings which they used to leap great distances.{{ref|name=DKESO|[[ON:Dragon Leap|Dragon Leap]] ability in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TSWDC|{{Cite Book|To Smite with Dragon Claws}}}}

Incantations pleading the [[Lore:Constellations|Constellations]] for bursts of speed also existed, as demonstrated by one [[Lore:Clan Direnni|Clan Direnni]] scholar of the second era.{{ref|name=DSpeed|[[Online:Norianwe|Norianwe]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Gates of Adamant|The Gates of Adamant]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The [[Lore:Montalion|Montalion]] clan of [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] located in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] have an affinity for teleporation.{{Ref|name=VOTIB1|{{Cite Book|Vampires of the Iliac Bay, Part II}}}} Vampires of [[Lore:Lamae Bal|Lamae Bal]]'s [[Lore:Disease#Noxiphilic_Sanguivoria|brood]] could dissolve into mist to quickly travel short distances.{{ref|name=ESOVamp|[[ON:Mist Form|Mist Form]] [[ON:Vampirism|vampire]] ability in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Translocator Rods==
[[File:ON-icon-quest-Translocator Rod.png|A Translocator Rod|thumb|right]]
Some members of the [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya]] High Elves were known to use Translocator Rods, enchanted devices that could easily move large objects by sending them to an empty planar space for storage, before releasing them to a desired location whenever the wielder pleased. The Rods were initially used to imprison enemies before being turned to more practical applications,{{ref|name=TranslocatorRod|[[Online:Seasons of the Worm Cult Quest Items|Translocator Rod]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Eraamine|[[Online:High Priest Eraamine|High Priest Eraamine]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} such as clearing out rubble and depositing it over a chasm to create a bridge.{{ref|name=Mystery|[[Online:Mystery of the Broken Temple|Mystery of the Broken Temple]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
