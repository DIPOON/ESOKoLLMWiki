[[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], also known as '''Morihaus the Indomitable''',{{ref|name=OBProphet|}} '''Morihaus-Breath-of-Kyne''',{{ref|name=TSOP4|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 4}}}}{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} the '''First Breath of Man''', '''Winged Bull''',{{Ref|name=LMIL|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 39: Minotaur Style}}}} '''Sacred Bull''',{{Ref|name=Remanada|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}} '''Bull of Kyne''',{{ref|name=OBProphet|}} '''Bull of the South''',{{ref|name=SFA|group=UOL|}} and '''Taker of the Citadel''',{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} was the [[Lore:Demigod|Demigod]]{{ref|name=FNH|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} of All Winds.{{ref|name=SFA|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Seven Fights of The Aldudagga|ns_base=GEN}}}} His status as an [[Lore:Ada|Ada]] deity came from his relationship to his mother, the [[Lore:Divines|Divine]] [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] (or [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]]).{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}} He was the consort of [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] and father of [[Lore:Belharza|Belharza]].{{ref|name=OM}} Morihaus was said to be the finest archer of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{Ref|name=LMIL|}} He is a figure of great importance and reverence in both [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]{{Ref|name=VOF|}} and [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{Ref|name=Skyrim|}}

Before Kyne departed from [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], she foresaw many needs coming to the land, and to assist any of those who would carry these needs on their shoulders, she created the [[Lore:Lord's Mail|Lord's Mail]], with the first recipient being her son Morihaus. At some point, Kyne took the Lord's Mail away from Morihaus due to his arrogance.{{ref|name=ARLords|[[Arena:Lord's Mail#Quest Dialogue|Lord's Mail quest dialogue]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} The artifact is also sometimes called the Armor of Morihaus due to his association with it.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} The connection with Kyne also granted Morihaus the powers of the [[Lore:thu'um|thu'um]].{{Ref|name=VOF|}} Similar to his relationship with the Lord's Mail, Morihaus was also aligned with [[Lore:The Lord|The Lord constellation]].{{Ref|name=HACBAYQ|{{Cite Book|High Astrologer Caecilus Bursio Answers Your Questions}}}}

Nearly every source describes Morihaus as a winged bull, be it a bull-man,{{ref|name=TToM|{{Cite Book|The Truth of Minotaurs}}}} man-bull,{{ref|name=TSOP5|}} or the Bull of Kyne.{{ref|name=OBProphet|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{intnote|Bullman|[nb 1]}} He had a nose-ring, horns, and was capable of flight with his golden wings.{{ref|name=TSOP2|{{Cite book|The Song of Pelinal, v 2}}}} At some point in time, Morihaus befriended the [[Lore:Demiprince|Demiprince]] [[Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen|Fa-Nuit-Hen]], and together the two would often drink, play dice, and slay beasts that plagued the land.{{ref|name=FNH|}}{{ref|name=MTCFNH|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Fa-Nuit-Hen}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Alessian Slave Rebellion===
[[File:MER-art-The High Priestess (crop).jpg|thumb|left|Morihaus' lover, Alessia]]
In {{Year|1E 242}}, Alessia, one of many [[Lore:Nede|Nedic]] slaves, who had become something of the leader among them, helped spark the beginnings of the rebellion against their cruel masters of the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] Empire. She prayed and spoke with the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] and pleaded with Kyne that next time her son Morihaus flew down, he would join their cause and rage against the Ayleids. Kyne granted not only this but alongside the bull was [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] a fellow immortal Ada. Both arrived to Alessia's camp of rebels with elven blood already spilled by their divine hands and announced to the Nedes that they had killed the eastern chieftains of the Ayleids. Thus formed the triumvirate that would seek to overthrow the Ayleid Empire and install their own.{{ref|name=TSOP2}} Alessia presented to both Pelinal and Morihaus a coronet surmounted with a sword, acknowledging them as the living weapons of the Empire to come, against whom no mortal could stand.{{ref|name=ONSMSC|[[Online:Star-Made Sword Coronet|Star-Made Sword Coronet]] Major Adornment description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
[[File:ON-tribute-alessia-Morihaus, the Archer (crop).png|thumb|right|Morihaus performing archery]]
Pelinal preferred to meet the Ayleid kings and chiefs in prearranged open combat, while the Slave-Queen Alessia and Morihaus tended to the growing armies of the rebellion.{{ref|name=TSOP3|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 3}}}} When Alessia was seen flying through the skies on the back of the Winged Bull, her followers called her Al-Esh (High Highness) for the first time.{{ref|name=TSOP4|}} On one occasion, after Morihaus had fallen to a volley of bird beaks, Pelinal carried the bull to a Nede with the [[Lore:Keptu|Keptu]] name Zuathas the Clever-Cutting Man to be healed.{{ref|name=TSOP4}} Morihaus and Pelinal had great love and respect for one another, seeing each other as family, with Pelinal referring to Morihaus as his nephew.{{ref|name=TSOP5|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 5}}}}

Never did Pelinal council Morihaus in the way of war, for the bull fought magnificently, and unlike Pelinal, never fell to madness in combat. However, Pelinal offered one rare piece of council in regards to Morihaus' relationship with Alessia, which had been growing more intimate: "We are ada, Mor, and change things through love. We must take care lest we beget more monsters on this earth. If you do not desist, she will take to you, and you will transform all Cyrod if you do this." To which the now shy Morihaus simply responded, "She is like this shine on my nose-hoop here: an accident sometimes, but whenever I move my head at night, she is there. And so you know what you ask is impossible".{{ref|name=TSOP5}} Pelinal's prophetic warning apparently panned out, as their union purportedly gave rise to a new race, the [[Lore:Minotaur|Minotaurs]] (although this assertion comes from later sources, and no corroborating documents from the time period have been found).{{ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Minotaurs}}}} [[Lore:Belharza|Belharza]] the Man-Bull was said to be the first and only child of Alessia and Morihaus.{{ref|name=OM|}}

Against the council of Alessia, Pelinal stormed [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] alone in pursuit of his enemy the half Ayleid-half Divine ada [[Lore:Umaril|Umaril the Unfeathered]]. After many successful battles against the legion of Daedra and elves that Umaril sent at Pelinal, Umaril himself confronted the bloodied Whitestrake, but even he could not beat Pelinal. After Pelinal killed Umaril, the few remaining Ayleid kings descended on the exhausted Pelinal and cut his body into eighths, leaving only his head behind for proof of the deed.{{ref|name=TSOP7|}}

Various tapestries and songs depict how Morihaus responded to the death of his beloved friend. The next morning, Morihaus rammed the White-Gold Tower so viciously it shook the whole of the tower. Morihaus and his legions then slayed their Wild Elf adversaries in over abundance in honor of Pelinal, taking the tower for control of Alessia in the process. An act that later earned him the title Taker of the Citadel.{{ref|name=TSOP7|{{Cite Book|The Song of Pelinal, v 7}}}}{{Ref|name=VOF|}}{{intnote|Cherim|[nb 2]}} Morihaus discovered the severed head of Pelinal in the throne room of White-Gold Tower. The two friends held a final conversation in which Morihaus predicted that his uncle would [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|come again]] and in a similar light. Pelinal warned that Umaril's spirit survived their encounter and that he could no longer protect mankind from Umaril's retribution.{{ref|name=TSOP7|}}{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|The Adabal-a}}}} The sacrificial defeat of Umaril by Pelinal's hand and the taking of White-Gold by Morihaus and his legions was the effective end of the rebellion. The [[Lore:Alessian Empire|First Empire]] of Man was truly born.{{Ref|name=VOF|}}

===Legacy===
[[File:ON-creature-Domihaus the Bloody-Horned 02.jpg|thumb|left|Domihaus, a winged Minotaur with an "unbroken line" (ESO)]][[File:GEN-misc-Belharza Adult.jpg|Sketch of Belharza, Morihaus' son|thumb|right]]
Morihaus outlived his beloved wife Alessia and claimed that, despite her death, she was the Lady of Heaven and bathed in stars.{{ref|name=TA|}} The Ruby Throne was succeeded by their son Belharza the Man-Bull.{{ref|name=OM|}} After her death, a renowned Ayleid smith named [[Lore:Lirulorne|Lirulorne]] struck a handful of masterwork coins to pay tribute and gave them to Morihaus.{{Ref|name=Lirulorne|[[ON:Western Skyrim Antiquities#Al-Esh Ascension Coin|Al-Esh Ascension Coin]] antiquity codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} All Minotaurs are often considered the progeny of Morihaus. While scholars have speculated that Minotaurs residing around old historical places of significance to the Empire serves as proof of their acknowledgment to their divine ancestry,{{ref|name=OM|}} at least one [[Lore:Domihaus|winged Minotaur]] directly spoke of his unbroken line and mastery over the land itself.{{ref|[[ON:Domihaus the Bloody-Horned|Domihaus the Bloody-Horned]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:The Adabal-a|The Adabal-a]], one of the oldest written accounts from the First Era, is traditionally believed to be the memoirs of Morihaus. One of its first precepts is "On the Recording of All That Haps", detailing Morihaus' argument with and execution of the Nonscriptionists, a faction against the writing of historic events. This was crucial in documenting the foul practices of the Ayleids so that such information wasn't lost to history and perhaps repeated.{{ref|name=TOOTO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}}

One particular [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] legend, dated to long after the founding of the First Empire, involves the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] manifesting in Skyrim and stealing the skin of a Nord Clever-Man. He then came upon a young female child known as Aless, named by her father after the slave-queen Alessia. Dagon intended to harm the girl, but through the power of god-guiser [[Lore:Magic|magic]], a regular bull was able to transform into the Winged Bull Morihaus himself. Morihaus protected the young girl and warned Dagon to stand down and turn back. Mehrunes claimed he had no fear of the demigod, but upon learning that all of the nearby sons and daughters of Skyrim were descending upon the scene, including the [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]], who could possibly summon the ghost of [[Lore:Shor|Shor]], Mehrunes Dagon quickly retreated back to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=SFA|group=UOL|}} A bardic song of the Nords also claims that Morihaus haunted the dreams of one Queen Vigdis Stone-Fist.{{Ref|name=SINE|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Skyrim In the New Era|ns_base=General}}}}

While some people consider Morihaus among dead divines,{{Ref|name=NJ:GC|{{Cite Book|Naryu's Journal/Gold Coast}}}} the actual fate of the demigod is unknown.
