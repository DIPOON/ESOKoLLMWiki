[[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] is a large region in the center of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], right on the southern shores of the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]].{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls|[[Online:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The ashfalls of its many volcanic mountains is both a bane and benefit to the region and its habitation. The area, over the course of history, has dealt with conflict and war, from the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] of the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]{{Ref|name=ONLoadStonefalls|[[Online:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} to the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]] of the early [[Lore:First Era|First Era]].{{Ref|name=ONMapAnti|[[Online:Stonefalls Antiquities#Antique Map of Stonefalls|Antique Map of Stonefalls]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}<noinclude>

Stonefalls is geographically bordered by [[Lore:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]] and the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]] to the east, [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] to the south, the [[Lore:Redoran|Redoran District]] to the north-northwest, and the [[Lore:Valus Mountains|Valus Mountains]] to the southwest. From across the provincial border, the region is neighbored by [[Lore:Nibenay Basin|Nibenay Basin]] in northern [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{Ref|name=ONMap|[[:File:ON-map-Tamriel 02.jpg|Map of Tamriel – The Elder Scrolls Online]]}}

==Society and Geography==
===Demographics===
[[File:ON-place-Bal Sunnar 03.jpg|thumb|left|The Telvanni township of Bal Sunnar]]
[[File:ON-npc-Kagesh Tribe.jpg|thumb|right|The Kagesh Tribe of Ashlanders]]
The region's main demographic comprises of [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]],{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} the native race of Morrowind, though its large cities host the other races of Tamriel as well.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} Here in central Morrowind, along the Inner Sea, the province's population is dense{{Ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}}{{Ref|name=MWElone|[[Morrowind:Elone|Elone]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} but particularly in Stonefalls, the population is spread out in its three major cities, [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]], [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] and [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]], as well as minor settlements like [[Lore:Senie|Senie]]. Most of these places are governed by the [[Lore:Great Houses|Great Houses]],{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} but their allegiances have swayed over the years. 

The large city of Davon's Watch had always been occupied by [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]] and it remained as their sole foothold in the region.{{Ref|name=ONDavon|[[Online:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-map-Morrowind Concept.jpg|Concept Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind'']]}} But despite this, the house has held the most power on the southern Inner Sea coast; at least in the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{Ref|name=TGHATU|{{Cite Book|The Great Houses and Their Uses}}}} [[Lore:House Dres|House Dres]] at that time as well, held more territory in the region,{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} although their main hold, Kragenmoor has changed hands between them and [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]].{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Morrowind}}}}{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL}}{{Ref|name=CCL|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Caius Cosades' Letter|ns_base=General}}}} The former have occupied it the longest since their [[Lore:Chimer|ancestors]] had worshipped the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadDres|[[Online:House Dres Crypts|House Dres Crypts]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONDenu|[[Online:Denu Faren|Denu Faren]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Ebonheart was also held between these two houses,{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL}} however, the Hlaalu have a more solid claim. The house came into control of the city when they absorbed the previous ruling house, [[Lore:House Mora|House Mora]]{{Ref|name=TRB5|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 5}}}}{{Ref|name=IWTB|{{Cite Book|Interview With Three Booksellers|ns_base=GEN}}}} and their noble family, the [[Lore:Ra'athim|Ra'athim Clan]]. The clan were previously retainers of the High Kingship of Morrowind and once controlled many modern-day cities like Ebonheart, Kragenmoor, and even [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] to the south.{{Ref|name=TRB5}}{{Ref|name=KEX|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part X}}}} Between the traditional Great Houses, [[Lore:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] held the least territory.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} The town of [[Lore:Bal Sunnar|Bal Sunnar]] is a medium-sized community in the mountains near the Telvanni Peninsula. It has historically stayed hidden and isolated from its neighbors over centuries.{{Ref|name=ONSunnar|[[Online:Bal Sunnar|Bal Sunnar]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Scribes of Fate|Scribes of Fate]]''}}{{Ref|name=SOFPBS|{{Cite Book|Scribes of Fate Preview—Bal Sunnar|ns_base=GEN}}}} However, the fort town of [[Lore:Heimlyn Keep|Heimlyn Keep]] stands over the surrounding valleys of Varanis and Zabamat, in wide open space.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} 

[[File:ON-place-Lukiul Uxith.jpg|thumb|right|Lukiul Uxith; an Argonian village in Stonefalls]]
In the early years of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], Dunmer slavers brought [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] from [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] to place them in their holdings across Morrowind.{{Ref|name=AAU|{{Cite Book|Argonians Among Us}}}} When the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was created, however, it abolished Argonian [[Lore:Slavery|slavery]] and provided them the means to settle the land. [[Lore:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]], in particular, was relinquished to the freed slaves, who built the town of [[Lore:Dhalmora|Dhalmora]] in its central basin.{{Ref|name=LFAIR}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadFoyen|[[Online:Bal Foyen|Bal Foyen]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Other Argonian citizens also built pocket communities in Stonefalls, like the village of assimilated Argonians called Lukiul Uxith and the Argonian Quarter in northern Ebonheart.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}

What remains of the Dunmer's ancient nomadic culture are the modern-day [[Lore:Ashlanders|ashlanders]].{{Ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}} In Stonefalls, there are two known tribes, the [[Lore:Kagesh Tribe|Kagesh]] and [[Lore:Ulath Tribe|Ulath]] tribes, and they both live near the Valus Mountain foothills in [[Lore:Varanis|Varanis]]. [[Lore:Goblin|Goblin]] tribes also live here.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} These tribes typically live in caves and practice subterranean agriculture. Their cousins in Cyrodiil even come across the mountains through secret passes and tunnels, and learn their techniques.{{Ref|name=ONLoadMuck|[[Online:Muck Valley Cavern|Muck Valley Cavern]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Murkwater Tribe|Murkwater Tribe]] live in the underground complex of [[Lore:Fungal Grotto|Fungal Grotto]]{{Ref|name=ONFungal|[[Online:Fungal Grotto I|Fungal Grotto I]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while the [[Lore:Shadowsilk Tribe|Shadowsilk Tribe]] live in the mountains north of [[Lore:Zabamat|Zabamat]]. They are mostly fisherfolk who tame [[Lore:Spider|spiders]] for conflict and care for the spider queen.{{Ref|name=ONShadowsilk|[[Online:Shadowsilk Tribe|Shadowsilk Tribe]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=BMIS|{{Cite Book|Burnt, Mostly Illegible Scrap|ns_base=ON}}}}{{Ref|name=ONHrogar|[[Online:Hrogar|Hrogar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Unlike most other parts of Morrowind, the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] had little foothold in the region. The ruins that scholars call Inner Sea Armature, on the northern base of Ash Mountain is the only Dwemer ruin of note in Stonefalls.{{Ref|name=ONArmature|[[Online:Inner Sea Armature|Inner Sea Armature]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]], ancient ancestors of Tamriel's human races are theorized to have once lived in Morrowind, though they have since gone extinct.{{Ref|name=FC}} For a time, numerous Nedic tribes conquered much of Stonefalls, the largest of which was the [[Lore:Rontha|Rontha]] tribe. These warriors were defeated by the Chimer, and although their descendants are long gone, relics and artifacts of their occupation can be found across the wastes.{{Ref|name=TBOS}}{{Ref|name=ONNedicAnti}}

===Culture and Industry===
[[File:ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg|thumb|left|The former Dres capital; Kragenmoor]]
[[File:ON-place-Dockyards.jpg|thumb|right|Davon's Watch's docks]]
Stonefalls has two cities with access to the Inner Sea. Davon's Watch was once the biggest port in the region and much of the trade that occurred on the sea passed through it at some point.{{Ref|name=ONRana|[[Online:Captain Rana|Captain Rana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ebonheart is another port. It sits on the converging point where the Inner Sea meets the Thir River, which travels down into Morrowind's heartland.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=ARMap|[[:File:AR-map-Morrowind towns.png|Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls: Arena'']]}}{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-map-Morrowind Concept.jpg|Concept Map of Morrowind – ''The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind'']]}} Like other parts of the sea, smugglers use caves and other hidden places like Hightide Hollow to house their operations.{{Ref|name=ONLoadHightide|[[Online:Hightide Hollow|Hightide Hollow]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Kragenmoor is a significant settlement in Stonefalls; a farming nexus that provides the region with its food and crops. The surrounding valley region is fertile and filled with plantations, like the Davenas and Sathram properties that grow [[Lore:Saltrice|saltrice]].{{Ref|name=EGT}} South of Davon's Watch is the [[Lore:Kwama|kwama]] eggmine, the Strifeswarm.{{Ref|name=ONStrifeswarm|[[Online:Strifeswarm Kwama Mine|Strifeswarm Kwama Mine]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In Zabamat, the Othrys clan of Dunmer own a large swath of volcanic pools they harvest for its [[Lore:Sulfur|sulfur]]. Large hauls of this material are carted by [[Lore:Guar|guar]] and clients like [[Lore:Alchemy|alchemists]] pay well for it.{{Ref|name=ONCloya|[[Online:Cloya|Cloya]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONKazama|[[Online:Jin-Ei|Jin-Ei]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

The Ra'athim clan of Ebonheart were originally a clan of miners that held lucrative operations across their once large fiefdom.{{Ref|name=TRB5}} As it was quoted by [[Lore:Moraelyn|Moraelyn]] in the historical fiction, ''[[Lore:King Edward|King Edward]]'', ''"Mining's in his blood, as it is in all the R'Aathim, the royal Kin of Ebonheart."''{{Ref|name=KEVII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VII}}}} Outside of the town is Emberflint Mine, an apt name for a [[Lore:Flint|flint]] mine in the vicinity of Ash Mountain.{{Ref|name=ONLoadEmberflint|[[Online:Emberflint Mine|Emberflint Mine]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Hightide Hollow was previously a mine before it was abandoned but what material it harvested is unknown.{{Ref|name=ONLoadHightide}} The caves underneath Iliath Temple were once an [[Lore:Ebony|ebony]] mine with source coveted by armorers and [[Lore:Smithing|weaponsmiths]] until it dried out.{{Ref|name=ONLoadIliath|[[Online:Iliath Temple|Iliath Temple]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After acquiring the [[Lore:Anvil of Mithas|Anvil of Mithas]] and the [[Lore:Hammer of Gharen|Hammer of Gharen]], King [[Lore:Casik|Casik]] of Ebonheart went out to revive the lost art of Dwarven smithcraft and produce the greatest arms in the land.{{Ref|name=ARCasik|[[Arena:King Casik|King Casik]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

[[File:ON-place-Othrenis.jpg|thumb|right|Othrenis, a city of the dead]]
Much like the rest of Morrowind, the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] was the main religion for native Dunmer until their decline in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]].{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} In their time, the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] themselves made annual pilgrimages to [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] through Ebonheart.{{Ref|name=ONSildras|[[Online:Disciple Sildras|Disciple Sildras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] was so enamored by the town that he desired a new Ebonheart built near his [[Lore:Vivec City|city]],{{Ref|name=OME|{{Cite Book|On Moving Ebonheart}}}} which came into fruition one way when [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Castle Ebonheart]] was built.{{Ref|name=MWEbonheart|[[Morrowind:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Iliath Temple is a large temple complex near Kragenmoor that was dedicated to the Tribunal but was previously dedicated to [[Lore:Azura|Azura]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadIliath}}

The Chimer built [[Lore:Daedric Ruins|daedric shrines]] across Morrowind, and Stonefalls hosts a variety of them dedicated to either the [[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]] or the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]]. A Mephala's Nest{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} (the name typically used for a [[Lore:Mephala|Mephalan]] temple){{Ref|name=ONLoadMephala|[[Online:Mephala's Nest|Mephala's Nest]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} was built just outside of Ebonheart. A [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] temple aptly called Sheogorath's Tongue is built on a hill overlooking Kragenmoor.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} There was once a great temple of [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]] built on Ash Mountain{{Ref|name=ONTreasureChimer|[[Online:Treasures C#Chimer Talisman of Boethiah|Chimer Talisman of Boethiah]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the ruins on [[Lore:Stonefang Isle|Stonefang Isle]] were once a temple dedicated to the Good Daedra overall.{{Ref|name=ONTreasuresBust|[[Online:Treasures T#Triple Bust of the Good Daedra|Triple Bust of the Good Daedra]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Daen Seeth|Daen Seeth]] is one of Morrowind's cities of the dead, [[Lore:Othrenis|Othrenis]], were many notable figures like Mavos Siloreth and families such as the Elendises and the Indarens are buried.{{Ref|name=ONGaryn|[[Online:Garyn Indoril|Garyn Indoril]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONMirri|[[Online:Mirri Elendis|Mirri Elendis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONUnused|group=UOL|{{TIL|ESO Unused Strings|game-data}}}}

Though daedric cults had largely fallen out of favor over the Tribunal faith,{{Ref|name=VOFTDE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Dark Elves}}}} some were still active away from civilization. The [[Lore:Cult of the Spider|Cult of the Spider]], Mephala's cult of worshippers once resided in the Fungal Grotto ruins, where they tracked a prized artifact, the [[Lore:Obsidian Husk|Obsidian Husk]]. With it, they took control of the surrounding region and used the artifact to allure travelers and turn them into loyal servants. Though their antics were stopped by the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]], it is unknown if they ever resurfaced.{{Ref|name=ONLighting|[[Online:Lighting the Shadows|Lighting the Shadows]] group quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} When it was previously active, the cult in Mephala's Nest made a particularly potent [[Lore:Wine|wine]] that was laced with spider's venom.{{Ref|name=TFW|{{Cite Book|Tamriel's Finest Wines|ns_base=ON}}}}

===Ecosystem===
[[File:ON-flora-Morrowind Cherry Blossom Trees.jpg|thumb|left|Stonefalls' Thorny Cherry Blossom Trees]]
[[File:ON-place-Vivec's Antlers Land-Coral.jpg|thumb|right|The Land Coral of Vivec's Antlers]]
The central Morrowind region, especially around the Inner Sea is described as a rich, fertile river valley, and high hills and uplands.{{Ref|name=POT}}{{Ref|name=MWElone}} However, Stonefalls is mostly dominated by the arid landscape synonymous with Morrowind as a whole. The volcanic activity from the Velothi Mountains to the northwest, as well as the twin volcanoes, [[Lore:Ash Mountain|Ash Mountain]] and the [[Lore:Tormented Spire|Tormented Spire]] has turned much of the region into ashlands.{{Ref|name=ONLoadStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=EGT}}

But despite the harsh environment, the region possesses a myriad of plant species and even farms. Some plants found in other parts of Tamriel have also grown in the ash wastes. Ash Basil is known for its strong scent and is a food source for local dreughs.{{Ref|name=ONJee|[[Online:Jee-Lar|Jee-Lar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ashreeds grow in tide pools on the coast and is used by Argonians to wrap meat.{{Ref|name=ONZasha|[[Online:Zasha-Ja|Zasha-Ja]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ashflowers are gnarled trees with small bushes of leaves found in Stonefalls' interior. [[Lore:Aloe|Red Aloe]] can also be found across the land.{{Ref|name=ONFurnishing|[[Online:Stonefalls Furnishings|Stonefalls Furnishings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Some of these plants, however, are unaffected by the volcanic soot. In the coastal cities are [[Lore:Cherry Blossom Tree|cherry blossom trees]], with large thorny branches.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=ONFurnishing}} In Morrowind's mountain country and highlands are [[Lore:Black rose|black roses]], which bloom in the shadows of stony ridges and banks.{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} The black rose also wields cultural significance in Morrowind, particularly with the Ra'athim clan and one of its members, the mythical figure Moraelyn, who sported a buckle with the "black rose of Morrowind" in the ''King Edward series''.{{Ref|name=KEVI|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part VI}}}} 

There are even several mushroom species, such as the common [[Lore:Emperor Parasol|Emperor Parasol]] of Morrowind,{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} the [[Lore:Graven Cap|Graven Caps]] around Othrenis,{{Ref|name=ONHeart|[[Online:Quieting a Heart|Quieting a Heart]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the rare [[Lore:Kwama Cap|Kwama Cap]], which grows in kwama eggmines.{{Ref|name=ONReesa|[[Online:Reesa|Reesa]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Perhaps one of the region's most noteworthy landmarks is the beaches of [[Lore:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]]. The former site of a battle against the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] of the east that is noted for its bizarre [[Lore:Land Coral|land coral]]. It is described as a maze and sensitive locals believe that the coral is self-aware and thoughtful.{{Ref|name=ONLoadAntlers|[[Online:Vivec's Antlers|Vivec's Antlers]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONWalks|[[Online:Walks-in-Ash|Walks-in-Ash]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The story goes that the coral is a gift from Vivec, who supposedly defeated the Akaviri here by flooding all of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=ONWalks}}{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} Two of Stonefalls' most prominent features are the volcanoes, Ash Mountain and the Tormented Spire. Across the region are a myriad of lava tubes and rivers (known as [[Lore:Foyada|foyadas]] in the [[Lore:Dark Elvish#Ashlander Language Dictionary|Ashlander]] tongue){{Ref|name=MWGenFoyada|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue F#Foyada|Foyada]] topic in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} found above, but especially underneath it.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadAsh|[[Online:Ash Mountain|Ash Mountain]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONLoadSunless|[[Online:The Sunless Hollow (Stonefalls)|The Sunless Hollow]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

[[File:ON-concept-Volcanic Field.png|thumb|right|"The Lurid Glow of Ash Mountain Illuminates the Ashlands" – [[Lore:Flaccus Terentius|Flaccus Terentius]]{{Ref|name=EGT}}]]
[[File:ON-interior-Fungal Grotto 03.jpg|thumb|left|The Fungal Grotto caverns]]
These caves are described as endless, subterranean spaces with half-buried ruins of unknown origin.{{Ref|name=ONLoadSunless}} The most famous example of this is the caves under Ash Mountain; a honeycomb network that some believe contain ancient and dangerous secrets.{{Ref|name=ONLoadAsh}} A theory proposed by the ''Speluncus Tamrielicum'' is that the natural cave systems of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] were once its capillaries and nodes and that they pulsated with the divine essence of [[Lore:Aedra|aedra]].{{Ref|name=ONLoadWeeping|[[Online:Weeping Wind Cave|Weeping Wind Cave]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

In Stonefalls, there are six types of lava{{Ref|name=ONTreasureLava|[[Online:Treasures F#"Field Guide to Lava" Pumice Cube|"Field Guide to Lava" Pumice Cube]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and from this activity is [[Lore:Malachite|volcanic glass]], which is used as crafting material by local artisans.{{Ref|name=ONTreasureGlass|[[Online:Treasures V#Volcanic Glass Vase|Volcanic Glass Vase]] treasure text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It can also produce [[Lore:Heart Stone|heart stones]], molten ore created from volcanic debris.{{Ref|name=ONVunal|[[Online:Sorcerer Vunal|Sorcerer Vunal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Other forms of similar material such as [[Lore:Basalt|basalt]] and [[Lore:Pumice|pumice]] laden the landscape. Sulfur, in particular can be found in clusters in the eponymous pools in Zabamat.{{Ref|name=EGT}}{{Ref|name=ONCloya}} Unlike its sister volcano, which is in a continual state of eruption,{{Ref|name=ONLoadAsh}} the Tormented Spire has remained dormant for most of history.

Many creatures live in the region, mostly those based in Morrowind, such as the [[Lore:Shalk|Shalk]], which spits fire and lives near lava. The [[Lore:Alit|alit]], [[Lore:Guar|guar]], [[Lore:Kagouti|kagouti]], [[Lore:Netch|netch]], and [[Lore:Nix-Hound|nix-hound]] can also be found across the land.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} [[Lore:Spider|Spiders]] are prevalent in the western hills and caves, and are sometimes tamed by the local populace, especially by the Shadowsilk Tribe of goblins. Their venom, though particularly deadly, has been harvested by Dunmer cultists.{{Ref|name=BMIS}}{{Ref|name=ONHrogar}}{{Ref|name=TFW}} For a time, [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] were commonplace between Ebonheart, Vvardenfell, and the surrounding Inner Sea waters, but have been targeted over centuries and now live in isolated pockets.{{Ref|name=NOTD|{{Cite Book|Notes on the Dreugh}}}} In Stonefalls, they live along the beaches, and some of which have lived as far inland as Fungal Grotto.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} The Coral Heart of Vivec's Antlers can communicate and summon them to protect it.{{Ref|name=ONSings}} Ash Mountain resonates with powerful magic ever since Balreth's capture. Both the magic and the mountain's lava flow attracted [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]] like [[Lore:Clannfear|clannfear]] and [[Lore:Scamp|scamps]].{{Ref|name=ONGaryn}}

Many unique types of fish also live in the region. On the Inner Sea, there is the [[Lore:Armorhead|Armorhead]], the [[Lore:Travally|Travally]], and the [[Lore:Akaviri Wrasse|Akaviri Wrasse]].{{Ref|name=ONFishing|[[Online:Fishing#Ebonheart Pact|Stonefalls]] fish catches in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} On the Thir River and the Brothers of Strife valley{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL}} are river fish such as the [[Lore:Ash Shad|Ash Shad]], [[Lore:Ricefish|Ricefish]], and [[Lore:Thorny Catfish|Thorny Catfish]]. In small, remote inland lakes are fish like the [[Lore:Lake Chub|Lake Chub]], the [[Lore:Rainbow Zander|Rainbow Zander]], and the [[Lore:Tench|Tench]]. In places like the Sulfur Pools, fish such as the [[Lore:Fungusfish|Fungusfish]], the [[Lore:Scum Carp|Scum Carp]], and the [[Lore:Stinkfish|Stinkfish]] live in foul water.{{Ref|name=ONFishing}}

===Village of Stonefalls===
[[File:AR-place-Stonefalls.jpg|thumb|right|Stonefalls during the Imperial Simulacrum]]
In the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], there was a settlement also called '''Stonefalls''' (alternatively known as '''Balfalls'''){{Ref|name=LH|{{Cite Book|Letter Home|ns_base=OB}}}} just south of the Inner Sea coast,{{Ref|name=ARMap}} on a long, winding river in the northernmost part of the Central Plains region. It was a territory of [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]].{{Ref|name=MWMap|group=UOL}}

During the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the village of Stonefalls was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Caspar and had a rivalry with Riverbridge.{{Ref|name=AR|[[Arena:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Stonefalls was neighbored by several settlements, such as [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] to the southeast, [[Lore:Karththor Dale|Karththor Dale]] to the west, [[Lore:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]] to the southwest, and [[Lore:Riverbridge|Riverbridge]] to the south-southwest.{{Ref|name=ARMap}}

===Early History===

Some of Morrowind's earliest inhabitants were the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]],{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Morrowind}}}} but although they mainly populated areas like Vvardenfell and the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]],{{Ref|name=BTAOM}}{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Morrowind}}}} there are scant examples of Dwemer civilization in what later became known as Stonefalls.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}}{{Ref|name=TBOS|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Strife}}}} The [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]], ancestors of the present-day Dunmer on the other hand, established themselves far across the region. Their early years scouring the province was a period of [[Lore:High Velothi Culture|High Velothi Culture]]. In this time, the Chimer were nomadic clans that often feuded with the Dwemer for resources and territory, but also because of their contrasting cultural and religious beliefs.{{Ref|name=BTAOM}}

Tension between both parties was serious. The Chimer created many powerful and dangerous weapons but none proved to be as deadly as the spellplague. Developed by two mendicant [[Lore:Namira|Namira]] mages, it was deemed uncontrollable and the Chimer lords buried it in a vault along with its creators, Rendrasa Malena and Adras Velae. That vault became the Heimlyn Keep reliquary and it has since been guarded by House Telvanni.{{Ref|name=ONVildras|[[Online:Keeper Vildras|Keeper Vildras]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The late [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]] marked the decline of High Velothi Culture. The former Chimer nomads settled down to build villages and developed early tribal cultures that evolved into the modern Great Houses.{{Ref|name=BTAOM}}

This was when permanent settlements like Stonefalls' cities were first built. The Indoril city of Davon's Watch was originally built as a fortress{{Ref|name=LRPPH|group=UOL|[[General:Loranna's RP/Playing Houses|Ted Peterson's Posts]] in Campaign 12/Playing Houses – ''[[General:Loranna's RP|Loranna's RP]]''}} and the House Dres ancestors built the crypts in Kragenmoor's mountainside{{Ref|name=ONDenu}} sometime after Heimlyn Keep was already established.{{Ref|name=ONUnused|group=UOL}} Ebonheart in the very center has long considered itself the eldest city-state of Morrowind and was once the seat of a fiefdom that controlled lucrative mines as far south as Mournhold.{{Ref|name=TRB5|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 5}}}} Stonefalls' early years, however, were rife with conflict and struggle. Throughout the Merethic and [[Lore:First Era|First Eras]], the region's borders constantly shifted{{Ref|name=ONMapAnti}} and in time suffered one of its most well-known battles.{{Ref|name=TBOS}}

===The Nedic Invasion and the Brothers' Gambit===
[[File:ON-place-Brothers of Strife.jpg|thumb|left|The Brothers of Strife monument]]
[[File:ON-quest-Rending Flames 04.jpg|thumb|right|Mavos Siloreth binding Balreth on the Charred Ridge]]
Sometime in the early First Era,{{intnote|Placement|[Note 1]}} the ancestral Nords, known at that point as the Nedes,{{Ref|name=FC|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}}{{Ref|name=TBOS}}{{Ref|name=ONVunal|[[Online:Sorcerer Vunal|Sorcerer Vunal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} had invaded Stonefalls for conquest and wealth.{{Ref|name=TBOS}} Many tribes comprised this invasion force but the Rontha tribe was chief among the largest.{{Ref|name=ONNedicAnti|[[Online:Stonefalls Antiquities#Nedic Wineskin|Nedic Wineskin]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} 

They entered the region from the pass near modern-day Fort Virak that branches in from Skyrim.{{Ref|name=ONHallfrida|[[Online:Hallfrida|Hallfrida]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Chimer's greatest generals: the brothers [[Lore:Balreth|Balreth]] and [[Lore:Sadal|Sadal]] led the retaliation but as they grew overwhelmed and acted purely in defense, blood was greatly spilled and many settlements from cities to farms were burned. In a few short years, the Nedes controlled most of the region and cut the Chimer army's access to the Inner Sea.{{Ref|name=TBOS}}{{Ref|name=ONBalreth|[[Online:General Balreth|General Balreth]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The brothers were left surrounded in a valley south of Ebonheart with a small elite force of sorcerers and troops.{{Ref|name=TBOS}} In a last-chance gambit, they conducted a ritual in which the brothers infused themselves with the enemy's blood and daedric essences to turn into fearsome weapons of destruction. With the heart of the Nedic chieftain, Ornskar, and the Essences of Wrath and Fire, the ritual was completed and the two became the [[Lore:Brothers of Strife (creature)|Brothers of Strife]].{{Ref|name=ONTuroni|[[Online:Sorcerer Turoni|Sorcerer Turoni]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONBrothers|[[Online:The Brothers Will Rise|The Brothers Will Rise]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} What happened in those ruins and how the brothers came to be had been lost to history but what followed after is considered a dark period in the Dunmer's long history.{{Ref|name=TBOS}}

Even with the Nedes defeated, the Brothers of Strife continued their onslaught and slaughtered man and mer alike. With access to the Inner Sea, a relief effort consisting of mages from Vvardenfell was made to stop them.{{Ref|name=TBOS}} Three of these mages: Genvis Faledran, Levisii Gilvayn, and [[Lore:Mavos Siloreth|Mavos Siloreth]] sealed the stronger brother Sadal in the Tormented Spire,{{Ref|name=ONTanval|[[Online:Tanval Indoril|Tanval Indoril]]'s dialogue during the quest, "[[Online:Opening the Portal|Opening the Portal]]" in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and by using a magical artifact that became the [[Lore:Coral Heart|Coral Heart]].{{Ref|name=ONSings|[[Online:Sings-With-Reed|Sings-With-Reed]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Despite this one victory, Genvis and Levisii had fallen and the other brother, Balreth was still loose in Stonefalls. Mavos Siloreth and some other mages bound him by splitting his power between two guardian [[Lore:Flame Atronach|atronachs]] on the Charred Ridge and sealed him in Ash Mountain. Many of these mage defenders, including Mavos Siloreth died and were interred in Othrenis.{{Ref|name=ONGaryn}}{{Ref|name=ONOnuja|[[Online:Onuja|Onuja]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONRending|[[Online:Rending Flames|Rending Flames]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Provisions were made to keep them imprisoned. The House Dres retainers of Kragenmoor placed a mystical veil over the Tormented Spire to prevent anyone from entering it and possessed the only key to it.{{Ref|name=ONHolgunnSpire|[[Online:Holgunn One-Eye#City at the Spire|Holgunn One-Eye]]'s dialogue during the quest, "[[Online:City at the Spire|City at the Spire]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The entrance was specifically made with two wardstones of [[Lore:The Atronach|the Atronach]] and [[Lore:The Tower|the Tower]].{{Ref|name=ONSen|[[Online:Sen Dres|Sen Dres]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Balreth's guardian atronachs were left in Ash Mountain and stood over him for centuries.{{Ref|name=ONGaryn}} When the brothers were finally put to rest, their bloody legacy went with them. Two statues were built over the ancient ruins where they were created and it stands as a testament to their sacrifice.{{Ref|name=TBOS}}

===The Conquest of Morrowind and its aftermath===
[[File:ON-place-Iliath Temple.jpg|thumb|right|The Iliath Temple dedicated to the Tribunal]]
By {{Year|1E 240}}, the [[Lore:First Empire of the Nords|Nordic Empire]] under High King [[Lore:Vrage|Vrage]] began their [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|grand conquest]] of Tamriel that lasted for nearly two hundred years.{{Ref|name=KEX}}{{Ref|name=TLST|{{Cite Book|The Legendary Sancre Tor}}}} The [[Lore:Conquest of Morrowind|Conquest of Morrowind]] was their campaign over the ancient province and is still regaled by Nordic bards in alehouses. Just like the rest of Morrowind,{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Skyrim}}}} Stonefalls endured an invasion.{{Ref|name=ONHornAnti|[[Online:Stonefalls Antiquities#Bronzed War Horn|Bronzed War Horn]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} As told by the ''King Edward'' series, the Nords held Ebonheart for a century and a half,{{Ref|name=KEXII|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part XII}}}} while the Ra'athim clan, led by [[Lore:Kronin|Kronin]] and his sons, [[Lore:Cruethys|Cruethys]], [[Lore:S'ephen|S'ephen]] and Moraelyn left their home and became guerilla raiders.{{Ref|name=KEX}} A member of House Mora went on to become a hero in the battle against the Nords.{{Ref|name=36LOVS22|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 22}}}}

By the time the Nords were defeated by the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]] in {{Year|1E 416}},{{Ref|name=PGE3AEM|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/All the Eras of Man}}}} the Ra'athim family returned home. At some point, Cruethys became the King of Ebonheart while his aunt Yoriss became the Queen of Kragenmoor sometime later.{{Ref|name=KEX}}{{Ref|name=KEXII}} In time, Moraelyn and his heirs ruled Ebonheart but were also retainers of Morrowind's High Kingship. However, when his grandsons were left as joint heirs to the throne, the family's fiefdom split between them and turned into the modern-day city-states: Ebonheart and Mournhold. Around this time as well, the High King's office was left vacant for a temporary war leader named in times of provincial emergency.{{Ref|name=TRB5}}

Like other parts across Morrowind, Stonefalls saw a significant change after the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]] and the subsequent events of [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]] in {{Year|1E 700}}.{{Ref|name=DI1|{{Cite Book|Dwemer Inquiries Vol I}}}} The eruption devastated at least half of the province and saw the population permanently shift south into Deshaan.{{Ref|name=PGE1}} Though the southern region experienced significant climate changes, a great effort was made to escape the north as maps at the time presumingly showed escaped routes.{{Ref|name=ONDeshaanAnti|[[Online:Deshaan Antiquities#Antique Map of Deshaan|Antique Map of Deshaan]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

But in the wake of Hortator [[Lore:Indoril Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]]'s death and the disappearance of the Dwemer came the Tribunal Temple and its eponymous patrons, [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]], [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]], and Vivec. The majority of Stonefalls' population accepted the three god-kings with open arms. The modern Castle Ebonheart was designed in reverence to them{{Ref|name=ATGTC|{{Cite Book|A Travel Guide to Tamriel Castles}}}} while the former temple of Azura, Iliath Temple now focused exclusively on the Tribunal.{{Ref|name=ONLoadIliath}} Other residents, like citizens of House Telvanni however, were not so welcoming. Matriarch Lladi Telvanni founded Bal Sunnar, a community of like-minded people that rejected the Tribunal in the mountains south of Davon's Watch.{{Ref|name=JONB|{{Cite Book|Journal of Nerile Belvayn}}}}{{Ref|name=JOKG|{{Cite Book|Journal of Kovan Giryon}}}}

===Stonefalls in the Second Era===
[[File:LO-misc-Against the Snakes.jpg|thumb|left|An artist's rendition of the Akaviri invaders]]
[[File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg|thumb|right|Jorunn the Skald-King and the Ebonheart Pact]]
Sometime in the Second Era, Dunmer raiders assaulted the Nord city of [[Lore:Riften|Riften]] and destroyed many farms. Among these casualties was [[Lore:Unnvald Ironhand|Unnvald Ironhand]]'s daughter and her family, save for his granddaughter, Hallfrida. Seeking vengeance, Unnvald assembled like-minded warriors from all over Skyrim and conducted a series of campaigns against Morrowind. The same year a [[Lore:Skald|skald]] of the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]] traveled with them, Unnvald's warriors sacked Davon's Watch.{{Ref|name=ONHallfrida}} His most famous battle was the Battle of the Bones, in which he defeated the [[Lore:Necromancers|necromancers]] of Fort Virak with his weapon, the Bonebane.{{Ref|name=EGTS|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|name=BOTBM|{{Cite Book|Battle of the Bones Memorial}}}} A memorial dedicated to his triumph was built just in view from the fort in the Rift.{{Ref|name=ONHallfrida}} 

In {{Year|2E 572}}, the [[Lore:Akaviri|Akaviri]] of [[Lore:Kamal (place)|Kamal]], led by [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] landed in northeast Skyrim and fought a bloody incursion down into Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} After bypassing Riften, they entered the mountain pass to Stonefalls and sacked Fort Virak before invading the region.{{Ref|name=ATS|{{Cite Book|Against the Snakes}}}} The Akaviri were met by Dunmer legions led by Almalexia and the House Indoril Grandmaster, [[Lore:Tanval Indoril|Tanval Indoril]], who staged a fighting retreat for further inland. Just as they stopped, the Nordic army led by [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]], and supposedly the ghost of [[Lore:Wulfharth|Wulfharth]], came from behind and cornered them at Vivec's Antlers.{{Ref|name=TSAI}}{{Ref|name=ATS}}

Many stories emerged from this [[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|battle]]. An Argonian army comprised of [[Lore:Archein|Archein]] warriors and the [[Lore:Shellbacks|Shellbacks]] of [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]] led by [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]] and [[Lore:Walks-in-Ash|Walks-in-Ash]] came in from the south and helped defeat the Akaviri.{{Ref|name=FATS|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}} At the same time, Jorunn contacted an old friend from Black Marsh, [[Lore:Keshu the Black Fin|Keshu the Black Fin]] to help fight the invaders with her militia, the [[Lore:Black Fin Legion|Black Fin Legion]].{{Ref|name=KTBFGTW2|{{Cite Book|Keshu: The Black Fin Goes to War, Part 2}}}} From Ebonheart, the three races came together to solidify an alliance known as the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], which guaranteed the freedom of Argonians in Morrowind.{{Ref|name=FATS}}{{Ref|name=KTBFGTW2}} Another story from the battle involved Vivec himself, who used his divine power to let his people [[Lore:Water Breathing|breathe water]] while he flooded all of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=ONWalks}}{{Ref|name=VOF}}

With the abolishment of slavery meant new opportunities for Argonians. Travel across Morrowind was more accessible{{Ref|name=PPC|{{Cite Book|Pact Pamphlet: Congratulations!}}}} and pockets of Argonian communities sprung up, such as Lukiul Uxith and Ebonheart's northern quarter.{{Ref|name=ONStonefalls}} Bal Foyen, east of Davon's Watch was relinquished to these freed Argonian slaves and turned into an enclave.{{Ref|name=ONLoadFoyen}} However, certain parts of Morrowind did not wholeheartedly acknowledge this decree. Though House Dres were members of the Ebonheart Pact, freedom was not guaranteed in their territories.{{Ref|name=PPC}} Instead of having Argonians and other Pact-affiliated races as slaves in Kragenmoor for example, [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiits]] made up the majority of these numbers.{{Ref|name=ONNadras|[[Online:Nadras Hlor|Nadras Hlor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONNoisy|[[Online:Noisy-Hunter|Noisy-Hunter]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} House Telvanni, on the other hand, were not members of the Pact largely for this subject.{{Ref|name=ABHOHT|{{Cite Book|A Brief History of House Telvanni}}}} How much this affected their nominal territories in Stonefalls is unknown.

===The Covenant Invasion and the Brothers' Return===
[[File:ON-quest-City Under Siege 02.jpg|thumb|right|Balreth's attack on the Daggerfall Covenant]]
[[File:ON-npc-Tanval Indoril.jpg|thumb|left|Tanval Indoril, Grandmaster of House Indoril]]
In {{Year|2E 582}}, the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] from the far west launched a large-scale invasion on the Ebonheart Pact's territories as part of the [[Lore:Alliance War|Alliance War]].{{Ref|name=ONDrathus|[[Online:Drathus Othral|Drathus Othral]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Led by General Serien,{{Ref|name=SOB|{{Cite Book|Scroll of Banishment|ns_base=ON}}}} much of this conflict took place in Stonefalls, but started in [[Lore:Bleakrock Isle|Bleakrock Isle]] and Bal Foyen. It began with their siege of Davon's Watch, a days-long bombardment of the city's northern district. The local commander, [[Lore:Holgunn One-Eye|Holgunn One-Eye]] and Tanval Indoril organized the defense, and as a means to destroy their fleet, Tanval summoned Balreth.{{Ref|name=ONHolgunnLegacy|[[Online:Holgunn One-Eye#Legacy of the Ancestors|Holgunn One-Eye]]'s dialogue during the quest "[[Online:Legacy of the Ancestors|Legacy of the Ancestors]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONSiege|[[Online:City Under Siege (Stonefalls)|City Under Siege]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Having completed its purpose, the Ebonheart Pact, specifically Garyn Indoril worked to re-seal Balreth into Ash Mountain. They sent an [[Lore:The Vestige|Agent]] of the Pact to Othrenis, to contact Mavos Siloreth's spirit and learn his process to seal the daedra.{{Ref|name=ONRending}} With this knowledge, the Pact was able to put Balreth to rest and continue their efforts on the remaining Covenant invaders.{{Ref|name=ONDeath|[[Online:The Death of Balreth|The Death of Balreth]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Covenant made landfall on Vivec's Antlers and spread themselves out across western Stonefalls.{{Ref|name=ONDrathus}} 

General Serien left most of his forces in Vivec's Antlers with Alexandra Conele. A mage who sought out the Coral Heart to destroy Ebonheart and potentially summon Sadal, the other Brother of Strife. All the while, the General and his soldiers went inland toward Fort Virak.{{Ref|name=ONGaryn}}{{Ref|name=ONSings}} In response to the Coral Heart's theft, the coral called dreugh to defend it against the Pact and Covenant, but when the Argonian mage, Sings-With-Reeds attuned herself to the coral, the battle shifted in the Pact's favor and Alexandra Conele was defeated.{{Ref|name=ONCoral|[[Online:The Coral Heart|The Coral Heart]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

By the time the battle at Vivec's Antlers was over, General Serien had taken over Fort Virak and reinforced its walls. Garyn Indoril led the charge to take it back but the days-on siege yielded no results. Every night, the Pact allowed the Covenant to take back the dead bodies of their comrades but unbeknownst to them, the enemy used necromancy to revive them and continue their attack. By infiltrating the fort through its ruins, the Pact defeated the Covenant, but as they confronted General Serien, he took Garyn Indoril's life. Strucken with abject grief at the death of his son, Tanval Indoril stole the Coral Heart and took his Vanguard to Zabamat, to re-awaken Sadal and destroy the Covenant.{{Ref|name=ONBreaking|[[Online:Breaking Fort Virak|Breaking Fort Virak]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONOdds|[[Online:Evening the Odds|Evening the Odds]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONGeneral|[[Online:The General's Demise|The General's Demise]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Tanval Indoril's forces marched into Kragenmoor to use their leader, Grandmaster Omin Dres' key to the Tormented Spire. When Omin Dres denied him access, he ordered his Vanguard to take Omin's son, Sen Dres hostage until he completed his mission.{{Ref|name=ONDenu}} The Agent of the Pact followed suit and rescued Sen from his prison in the House Dres Crypts.{{Ref|name=ONSon|[[Online:Saving the Son|Saving the Son]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The Grandmaster gave the Agent another key to the spire, and with it, the Pact assembled warriors to stop Tanval. He, however, was attacked by daedra conjured by Sadal, who proved too strong to control. The spirits that previously bound him returned to open the portal to his prison.{{Ref|name=ONOpening|[[Online:Opening the Portal|Opening the Portal]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After a final battle and Tanval's sacrifice, Sadal was defeated and the region was saved. Holgunn One-Eye succeeded the Indoril Grandmaster as the Pact commander of Stonefalls.{{Ref|name=ONFinal|[[Online:Sadal's Final Defeat|Sadal's Final Defeat]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Stonefalls in the Third Empire===
[[File:LO-misc-Katariah figurehead.jpg|thumb|right|A depiction of Katariah Ra'athim]]
After the conquest of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], the [[Lore:Imperial Army|Imperial Army]] massed their fleets on the western provincial border, near Kragenmoor and [[Lore:Silgrad Tower|Silgrad Tower]].{{Ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}} Though Stonefalls' history at large during the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]] is unknown, what is known is that most of the old nobility was replaced with upstarts like [[Lore:Symmachus|Symmachus]] in Mournhold. In that time, Symmachus worked with a rag-tail end of the Ra'athim Clan to deal with the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]] and for that service, they were named the King of Ebonheart. The counselors that served him, however, did much to suck his kingdom dry and pick at it to its core.{{Ref|name=TRB4|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 4}}}} Under the Third Empire, cities such as Ebonheart and Kragenmoor became the seats of kings.{{Ref|name=AREbonheart|[[Arena:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}{{Ref|name=ARKragenmoor|[[Arena:Kragenmoor|Kragenmoor]] location and rumors in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}}

Only a few years later, the Ra'athim clan of Ebonheart became acquainted with Queen [[Lore:Potema|Potema]] of [[Lore:Solitude (kingdom)|Solitude]] and were one of many disgruntled kingdoms that later supported her and her son, Emperor [[Lore:Uriel Septim III|Uriel III]] in the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}}{{Ref|name=TMOP|{{Cite Book|The Madness of Pelagius}}}} What occurred with Ebonheart and the surrounding Stonefalls region during the conflict is unknown, but one side of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]]'s battle took place through Morrowind and later Skyrim, and it was led by King [[Lore:Magnus Septim|Magnus Septim]] of [[Lore:Lilmoth (kingdom)|Lilmoth]] and his Argonian army.{{Ref|name=TWQ7|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v7}}}} After Uriel III's defeat in {{Year|3E 127}}, Potema's allies turned coat and received new levels of freedom under the new Emperor, [[Lore:Cephorus Septim I|Cephorus I]].{{Ref|name=BHOTE2|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 2}}}}{{Ref|name=TWQ8|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v8}}}}

In time, Magnus Septim succeeded his brother as Emperor and spent the rest of his life cleaning up after the war. To mend the relationship with Ebonheart, Magnus arranged the marriage of his son, King [[Lore:Pelagius III|Pelagius]] of Solitude and the Ra'athim clan's heir, [[Lore:Katariah|Katariah]].{{Ref|name=TMOP}} Her marriage into the [[Lore:Septim Dynasty|Septim family]] did a lot to establish peace across the Empire despite detractors and ushered a golden era upon her ascension as the Regent-Empress.{{Ref|name=BHOTE2}} Though times have been tumultuous since then, Morrowind eventually saw great changes between the late Third Era and the Fourth Era. With [[Lore:Hlaalu Helseth|Hlaalu Helseth]]'s rise into power, the Great Houses Dres and Hlaalu had significant growth, while House Indoril, for example, was declining. How this has affected their core territories in Stonefalls is unknown.{{Ref|name=PGE3}}

===Stonefalls at the Turn of the Era===
The subsequent [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] in {{Year|3E 433}} and the later [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] ({{Year|4E 5}}), however, did much to shift the politics of the province. [[Lore:Oblivion Gate|Oblivion Gates]] opened all over Morrowind,{{Ref|name=SRAdril|[[Skyrim:Adril Arano|Adril Arano]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]]''}} and despite Chancellor [[Lore:Ocato|Ocato]]'s decision to not pull soldiers from the provinces,{{Ref|name=OBOcato|[[Oblivion:Chancellor Ocato|Chancellor Ocato]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Morrowind was left by themselves. House Hlaalu took the brunt of the blame after the Empire relinquished Morrowind and lost both their status as a Great House and their seat in the [[Lore:Grand Council|Grand Council]].{{Ref|name=SRAdril}} Then early into the Fourth Era, the [[Lore:Ministry of Truth|Ministry of Truth]] fell onto Vivec City and caused the eruption of Red Mountain, thus province-wide devastation.{{Ref|name=TRY4|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}} The eruption had reportedly destroyed most of Morrowind's cities{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|234}} and even places as secluded as Bal Sunnar were affected. The skies above turned red and filled with smoke, while volcanic debris rained down.{{Ref|name=JOAF|{{Cite Book|Journal of Adosi Fevur}}}}

In {{Year|4E 48}}, the flying city of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]], a severed piece of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]]'s realm; the [[Lore:Fields of Regret|Fields of Regret]] appeared over the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]] and flew in a straight direction from [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]] on Black Marsh's southern coast to the [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]] where Vivec City formerly stood. People in and underneath its path were fed into the city's [[Lore:Ingenium|Ingenium]] and turned into [[Lore:Wormies|zombie thralls]] that laid waste to everything in its wake. Stonefalls would have been in its direct path, in both its initial move across the Inner Sea and its subsequent move over the Valus Mountains to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]].{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|42–46}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|Placement|^|Though there is no given date when the Nedic invasion occurred, we can use certain clues to determine its date. [[Online:Legate Sadal|Legate Sadal]]'s comment of his previous skirmishes with the Dwemer indicates this took place before their alliance, the First Council was formed.{{Ref|name=ONSadal|[[Online:Legate Sadal|Legate Sadal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The invasion's placement in the timeline can also be determined by the moment when the Nedes/Atmorans of Skyrim set themselves apart as the Nords of Skyrim, however, different sources blur this conclusion. The First Edition of the Pocket Guide explains that [[Lore:Harald|Harald]]'s formal founding of the Kingdom of Skyrim is when the Nords established themselves from Atmora,{{Ref|name=PGE1S|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Skyrim}}}} however, texts such as ''[[Lore:Great Harbingers|Great Harbingers]]'' written by Swyk the Long-Sighted suggests that the Nords began to think themselves as such by the time the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] arrived to Tamriel.{{Ref|name=GH|{{Cite Book|Great Harbingers}}}} Not to mention, other sources used Nord and Atmoran interchangeably to refer to the human rebels of the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]], way back in the Merethic Era.{{Ref|name=SRPaarthurnax|[[Skyrim:Paarthurnax (dragon)|Paarthurnax]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=FSL2|{{Cite Book|First Servant's Letter, Page 2|ns_base=ON}}}}}}
