[[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] is a [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenese]] city in the extreme southeast of the province, by the mouth of the [[Lore:Niben River|Niben River]] where it opens into [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]]. Known as the city that spans the waters,{{ref|[[OB:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} the city straddles a swampy estuary of the Niben on the small strip of land between [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] and [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] called the [[Lore:Trans-Niben|Trans-Niben]]. Leyawiin is roughly divided into four districts: Chapel District, Guild Plaza, Main Street and [[Lore:Castle Leyawiin|Castle Leyawiin]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Guide to Leyawiin}}}}

The ancient symbol of Leyawiin is a white [[Lore:Horse|horse]] on a field of green, known variously as the Ivory Horse{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Crafting Motif 101: Ivory Brigade Style}}}} or the White Stallion.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Knights of the White Stallion (faction)|Knights of the White Stallion]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The origin of the city's Ivory Horse symbol is unknown. One tradition believes it to be associated with the ancient hero [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]]. Another states that in the days of myth, Leyawiin was home to a shining white horse of divine strength and beauty who protected the river's banks.{{ref|name=TGTL|{{Cite Book|Traveler's Guide to Leyawiin}}}}

[[Lore:Topal|Topal the Pilot]] is the [[Lore:Imperial Saints|patron saint]] of the city, and a statue of him can be found by the southwest wall.{{ref|name=Opsius|[[Oblivion:Decentius Opsius|Decentius Opsius]]' dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBPrima|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|363}}<noinclude>

==History==
===First Era===
After the death of [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]] at the conclusion of the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]] in {{Year|1E 243}}, a messenger carried the [[Lore:Mace of the Crusader|Mace of the Crusader]] to the [[Lore:Man|human]] settlement of Leyawiin. Centuries later, the master craftsman Saint [[Lore:Kaladas|Kaladas]] built the Great Chapel of [[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]] in Leyawiin as a tribute to the legend.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Carodus Oholin|Carodus Oholin]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Only a couple centuries later, Leyawiin was captured by the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]i wander-kings of [[Lore:Bruk'ra|Bruk'ra]] amidst the [[Lore:Anequine Conquests|Anequine Conquests]] of [[Lore:Darloc Brae|Darloc Brae]]. Scholars theorize that at this point, the invaders converted the Great Chapel of Zenithar to a [[Lore:Temple of Two-Moons Dance|Temple of Two-Moons Dance]].{{Ref|name=ONGlassAnti|[[Online:Northern Elsweyr Antiquities#Stained Glass of Lunar Phases|Stained Glass of Lunar Phases]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} [[Lore:House Caro|House Caro]] was known to rule over Leyawiin ever since the early years of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{ref|name=TGTL}}

===Second Era===
[[File:ON-load-Blackwood.png|left|thumb|Leyawiin in the Second Era]]
The first Imperial bear cavalry came into use by the 10th Legion in {{Year|2E 23}}, walking through Leyawiin's streets each week in a bear parade.{{Ref|name=APBearCub|[[Online:Akaviri Potentate Bear Cub|Akaviri Potentate Bear Cub]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=APBear|[[Online:Akaviri Potentate Bear|Akaviri Potentate Bear]] mount description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

In {{Year|2E 299}}, Potentate Versidue-Shaie ordered the removal of Leyawiin's bridges so that large sailing vessels could pass from Topal Bay to the River Niben. However, Leyawiin's Chamber of Legates, fearing that the additional merchant traffic would simply sail through Leyawiin without stopping, managed to stall the Imperial project until it was abandoned. To this day vessels too large to pass beneath the bridges must dock south of the city and transship their cargoes to smaller riverboats.{{Ref|name=TGTL}}

As of the year 2E 569, the Chamber of Legates held a relatively minor role within Leyawiin's governance,{{Ref|name=TGTL}} but by {{Year|2E 582}}, they had risen to become a significant political force both in the city and the surrounding region. Despite Countess Caro holding the official title of ruler over Leyawiin, the practical governance of the city was primarily in the hands of the Chamber of Legates.{{Ref|name=CRL|[[ON:Captain Rian Liore|Captain Rian Liore]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}{{Ref|name=LTK|[[ON:Legate Tebeza-ko|Legate Tebeza-ko]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}{{ref|name=OTCOL|{{Cite Book|On the Chamber of Legates}}}}

In the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], during the war between the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]] and the forces of [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]], Leyawiin was allegedly single-handedly freed by [[Lore:Sai Sahan|Sai Sahan]], who fought both Emperor [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]]'s forces and his own men who had been employed by Leovic as double agents.{{ref|name=CotFC5|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 5}}}} There are many different views of this event,{{Ref|name=MM|[[ON:Marcellia Mero|Marcellia Mero]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}} and according to some other sources he was aided by allies, however.{{Ref|name=SL|[[ON:Stallion-Liege Brow Medallion|Stallion-Liege Brow Medallion]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
{{NewLine}}

===Third Era===
At the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the [[Lore:Trans-Niben|Trans-Niben]] was annexed back into Cyrodiil and County Leyawiin{{ref|[[OB:Shamada|Shamada]]'s dialogue in [[OB:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}} due to an agreement between the [[Lore:Mane|Mane]] and the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]]. Following this, a group of Khajiiti bandits and freedom fighters known as the [[Lore:Renrijra Krin|Renrijra Krin]] sought to reclaim all land along the Trans-Niben which they believed to rightfully belong to Elsweyr. They disagreed with the Mane's decision to cede the Trans-Niben and actively blamed Count [[Lore:Marius Caro|Marius Caro]] for driving Khajiit from the county's fertile land. The city itself boasted a considerable Khajiit and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] population at the time. Racial tension proved to be a problem between the Khajiit and the influx of Argonians and [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]], and Count Caro attempted to encourage {{Lore Link|Nibenese|Nibenean}} culture in the city in order to move towards mainstream Imperial society. The city was besieged by the forces of [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] during the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] in {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|name=AFB|Events of [[Oblivion:Allies for Bruma|Allies for Bruma]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
Leyawiin briefly became an independent city-state early in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. During this time, the county of [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]], which had rebelled against the Empire, warred with County Leyawiin until the rise of the [[Lore:Mede Dynasty|Mede Dynasty]] by {{Year|4E 48}}.{{Ref|name=TheInfernalCity|{{Cite Book|ns_base=BK|The Infernal City}}}} During the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]], [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Dominion]] forces marched out of hidden camps in Northern Elsweyr, marched into Cyrodiil and flanked the Imperial defenses along the [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] border. The southern coastline of Cyrodiil was then overwhelmed by the invasion forces, and Leyawiin was one of the first cities to fall to the Aldmeri Dominion's invasion in Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=GWB|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}
