==Biology==
===Morphology===
[[File:BL-event-A Darker Dungeon.png|An Abyssal Troll, a variant of troll with prominent horns (Blades)|thumb|left]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''The first step in your hunt is the proper identification of your quarry. Trolls are roughly man-shaped, with lengthy, muscular arms that end in claw-tipped fingers. The creature's large mouth is filled with jagged teeth, all the better to crunch the bones of foolish hunters who didn't purchase my book.''<br>''Without a doubt, the troll's most distinctive and unusual feature is the [[Lore:Third Eye|third eye]] nestled in the center of its forehead.''|''[[Lore:Troll Slaying|Troll Slaying]]''}}

Trolls possess a distinct ape-like physique, characterized by long arms and short legs, which facilitates the knuckle-walking they tend to do.{{ref|name=EGTS}} Their surprisingly swift movements can defy expectations for a creature of their size.{{ref|name=TS}} 

Trolls are known for their formidable strength. When they attack, they typically employ their powerful arms and sharp claws to overpower their prey,{{ref|name=TS}}{{ref|name=TSG}} and they are strong enough to hurl large objects such as boulders.{{ref|name=ESOTROLL}} They are sometimes known to attack with their legs as well,{{ref|name=OB}} and are capable of leaping an impressive distance.{{ref|name=ESOTROLL}}{{ref|name=EGTS}} Their jaws are said to be powerful enough to snap off a [[Lore:Horse|horse]]'s leg in one bite.{{ref|name=ScaredFrost|[[ON:Brelor|Brelor]]'s dialogue in [[ON:A Lasting Winter|A Lasting Winter]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

Trolls are well-known for being three-eyed,{{ref|name=TS}}{{ref|name=FGtRT}} though two-eyed,{{ref|name=ArenaT}}{{ref|name=BLUD}}{{ref|name=BMGrahl}} and even one-eyed trolls are also known to exist.{{Ref|name=CastlesRulings}} There is also some evidence for the existence of five-eyed trolls,{{Ref|name=TBConcept|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-Helms 02.jpg|Trollbone helmet concept art]]}} though tales about them are considered farcical.{{ref|name=MoNF|{{Cite Book|Monsters of Northern Folklore}}}} Most trolls have tapered ears, while some sport additional features such as horns,{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 87: Ancestral Nord Style}}}}{{ref|name=ESOTROLL|[[Online:Troll|Trolls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BLTROLL|[[Blades:Trolls|Trolls]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} or manes of thick, scraggly hair.{{ref|name=TMN}}{{ref|name=ESOTROLL}} 

Trolls are known to occasionally grow to tremendous sizes,{{ref|name=ESOTROLL}}{{Ref|group=UOL|name=TheMenagerie}} sometimes even rivaling the size of [[Lore:Giant|Giants]].{{ref|name=KyneC}} Trolls are capable of living for centuries,{{ref|name=BLATROLL|[[Blades:Ancient Troll|Ancient Trolls]] and [[Blades:Primeval Troll|Primeval Trolls]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|name=TAO|[[BL:The Ancient One|The Ancient One]] quest in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|name=SS-S|[[Bloodmoon:Svenja Snow-Song|Svenja Snow-Song]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}{{ref|name=AJUD}} possibly due to their unique regeneration ability. Trolls are said to have been much larger and more powerful during the chaos of the Dawn Era.{{ref|name=Serpent|[[Online:The Serpent (Celestial)|The Serpent]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TAO}} The [[Lore:Celestial|Celestial]] [[Lore:The Serpent|Serpent]] notably claimed that "troll blood has thinned in the modern era".{{ref|name=Serpent}}

Trolls have been known to carry parasites or [[Lore:disease|disease]]s such as [[Lore:Gutworm|gutworm]] and [[Lore:Winter Worn|winter worn]].{{ref|[[Skyrim:Gutworm|Gutworm]] from [[SR:Survival Mode|Survival Mode]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[DS:Winter Worn|Winter Worn]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} [[Lore:Troll Thirst|Troll thirst]] is a disease one can get in the wilds of [[Lore:The Pale|the Pale]], where trolls are known to frequent.{{ref|[[DS:Troll Thirst|Troll Thirst]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} Trolls were susceptible to corruption from the [[Lore:Wildburn|Wildburn]].{{ref|[[Online:Wildburn Troll|Wildburn Trolls]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Regeneration===
[[File:LG-cardart-Burning Touch.png|thumb|right|Fire is a troll's greatest weakness]]
[[File:DF-icon-ingredient-Blood2.png|Troll's Blood (Daggerfall)|thumb|left]]{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''Would a small piece of troll meat continue to grow, so the pantry would constantly restock itself? I think so, but I need a piece of troll meat to test this theory. Also, there's the fire issue. Troll meat really doesn't react well to fire, and I can't see the foresters eating raw troll flesh.''|''[[Lore:A New Recipe?|A New Recipe?]]''}}
Trolls possess the unique trait of being able to rapidly regenerate their tissue,{{ref|name=BAJ}} an ability that is only annulled if exposed to fire.{{ref|name=TS}} Their own [[Lore:Alchemy T#Troll Blood|blood]] is also known to have healing properties.{{ref|name=AlBlood}} 

There have been rare cases where a troll's regeneration can prevent their death completely, making the scorching of its corpse the only way to truly kill it.{{ref|name=RT|[[Redguard:Troll|Trolls]] in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}}{{ref|name=EGTS}}{{ref|name=ArenaT}} Though some claim that needing to burn trolls to permanently kill them is a myth.{{ref|name=BAJ|{{Cite Book|Brenus Astis' Journal}}}}{{ref|[[OB:Rumors#Wilderness|Rumors]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=OBPrima|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|55}} One cook theorized that troll meat could continuously regenerate even after their death,{{ref|name=ANR|{{Cite Book|A New Recipe?}}}} and it has been alleged that the eyes of taxidermied troll heads are still surprisingly alert, following people's movements hungrily.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures SJ-SY#Stuffed Troll Head|Stuffed Troll Head]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

The exact nature of troll regeneration is somewhat mysterious. The ability is of great interest to certain battlemages, and they have occasionally tried to acquire live specimens to study it.{{ref|name=EGTS}} One [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] who lived among trolls believed that praying to the [[Lore:Gods|god]] [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] would unlock the secrets of their regeneration.{{ref|name=TH|Dialogue during [[BL:The Heretic|The Heretic]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} There exists a strange form of life-giving energy that was capable of imbuing other creatures with a regeneration ability akin to that of trolls.{{ref|name=WOTUD|[[BL:Wrath of the Undying|Wrath of the Undying]] quest in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

==Ecology==
===Diet===
[[File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Troll.jpg|left|thumb|A frost troll dining in its lair]]
Trolls are formidable carnivores that have strong enough jaws to eat any part of a carcass, including bone. In his book ''[[Lore:Field Guide to River Trolls|Field Guide to River Trolls]]'', the scholar [[Lore:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus]] of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] described trolls as capable of "devouring anything they can catch down to fingernails and marrow".{{ref|name=FGtRT}} The author of ''[[Lore:Troll Slaying|Troll Slaying]]'' similarly commented on a troll's ability to bite through bone.{{ref|name=TS}} The remains of the hunter [[Lore:Svenja Snow-Song|Svenja Snow-Song]] were recovered from the stomach of the infamous troll known as the [[Lore:Uderfrykte Matron|Uderfrykte Matron]] with her limbs, her torso, and her head still largely intact.{{ref|name=UM}} Her husband, [[Lore:Agnar the Unwavering|Agnar the Unwavering]], described her death as instantaneous, with her body being "consumed by the beast nearly whole".{{ref|{{Cite Book|Agnar's Journal}}}}

Trolls have voracious appetites, and are characterized as being "always hungry".{{ref|name=Hungry|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Notable Transactions}}}} Members of the humanoid races are common prey of trolls.{{ref|[[ON:Deepswimmer|Deepswimmer]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Hungry|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Notable Transactions}}}}{{ref|name=KrishDulk}} An [[Lore:Iron Orc|Iron Orc]] known as Gulzog the Butcher fed the trained trolls in his care the remains of human captives, and instructed those serving under him to do the same.{{ref|name=CaF}} Children are warned of the dangers of troll contact, such as in ''[[Lore:A Gold Coast Children's Bestiary|A Gold Coast Children's Bestiary]]'', which conveys its warning of troll predation in rhyme.{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Gold Coast Children's Bestiary}}}} On occasion trolls will even capture humanoid prey alive to save for later.{{ref|name=GD}} Circa {{Year|4E 180}}, [[Lore:The Warrior (person)|the Warrior]] was known to have rescued several people that had been taken hostage by trolls.{{ref|name=VtUS}}{{ref|name=BLDN}} Frost trolls will sometimes hang their human prey from the ceilings of ice caves.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Bleakcoast Cave|Bleakcoast Cave]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[Online:Broken Helm Hollow|Broken Helm Hollow]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Despite these examples, humanoids are not an essential part of a trolls diet,{{ref|[[Online:Teethnasher the Frostbound|Teethnasher the Frostbound]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Lowland}} and they can be satisfied with other meats.{{Ref|group=UOL|name=TheMenagerie}} They have been known to kill and eat livestock.{{Ref|name=CastlesRulings}} There are also a few accounts of trolls who drink [[Lore:Alcoholic Beverages|alcohol]].{{ref|name=Trollcide|{{Cite Book|A Poorly Scrawled Note|ns_base=Oblivion}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Old Nord Drinking Song}}}} Trained or captive trolls are sometimes sated with ale,{{ref|name=CaF|{{Cite Book|The Care and Feeding of Trolls}}}}{{Ref|group=UOL|name=TheMenagerie}} and drunk trolls were known to attack travelers in the Pale.{{ref|[[Dawnstar:Drunk Troll|Drunk Trolls]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} Trolls are also occasionally preyed upon by other creatures. [[Lore:Sabre Cat|Sabre cats]] native to the [[Lore:White River|White River]] region of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] will often seek out and hunt trolls as a pack, even as cubs.{{ref|[[Online:Graywinter Sabre Cat Cub|Graywinter Sabre Cat Cub]] pet description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Behavior===
[[File:ON-creature-Troll Rockthrower.jpg|A troll throwing a large rock (ESO)|thumb|right]]
Despite their typically reclusive nature,{{ref|name=FGtRT}} trolls are known to often live in patriarchal packs.{{ref|name=TSRN|{{Cite Book|Troll Socialization Research Notes}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Irgnar's Journal}}}} These packs are led by powerful male alphas,{{ref|name=RWQ}}{{ref|name=Tograz|[[Online:Tograz|Tograz]]'s dialogue during [[ON:How Few Remain|How Few Remain]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Item Link|Limb-Render of the Senche|id=34798|summary=1|quality=3}} item description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} sometimes called Troll Kings.{{ref|[[Online:Crawg the Troll King|Crawg the Troll King]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ATA}}{{ref|name=TKMobile|[[OBMobile:The Troll King|The Troll King]] quest in ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}} Female trolls are referred to as she-trolls,{{ref|name=MTH|[[Online:Treasures M#Mounted Troll Head|Mounted Troll Head]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} troll infestations can often be traced back to a Troll Mother, a particularly powerful she-troll who may have moved into the area. Eliminating the mother is an effective means of ending the threat, although the individual trolls will still need to be hunted down.{{ref|name=TTH|[[Blades:The Troll Hunter|The Troll Hunter]] quest in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{ref|name=AJUD|{{Cite Book|Agnar's Journal}}}} 

According to the council of healers at the [[Lore:Arcane University|Imperial University]], the reproductive biology of trolls is not well understood.{{ref|name=NORP}} However there is at least one famous account of troll copulation. Circa {{Year|2E 565}}, unseasonably warm weather in Skyrim drove hordes of trolls across the western half of the province while locked in "unspeakably horrid acts". [[Lore:High King Svargrim|High King Svargrim]] personally stopped the rampage, splitting many of the mating trolls. As a result of this, trolls tend to avoid the areas closest to the walls of [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]]. This event was later known as "the Great Troll Rut".{{ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - High King Svargrim}}}}

Trolls are prone to entering a violent bloodlust if they smell the blood of an open wound.{{ref|name=Tograz}} Trolls are afraid of fire due to their weakness to it.{{ref|name=BLCG}} Most trolls do not swim, as they usually dislike water as well.{{ref|[[OB:Weebam-Na|Weebam-Na]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Trolls are known to occasionally steal [[Lore:Currency#Gold|gold]] or treasure.{{ref|name=ATA|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|A Tough Audience}}}}{{ref|[[OBMobile:Narrative#The Troll King|Dialogue]] during [[OBMobile:The Troll King|The Troll King]] in ''[[Obmobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}}{{ref|name=GD}} Some trolls seemed oddly attracted to a mysterious ore found in Lost Knife Cave in [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]].{{ref|[[Online:Mystery Metal|Mystery Metal]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

===Intelligence===
[[File:AR-creature-Troll.gif|thumb|left|A Troll (Arena)]] 
{{Quote Box|''This breach is surrounded by trolls. They're a lot smarter than you think. They're banging rocks together now, sure—but once they start wearing pants? Look out.''|[[Lore:Augur of the Obscure|Augur of the Obscure]]{{ref|name=Augur}}}}
The exact intelligence of trolls is unclear, or at least varies wildly. Trolls are generally thought to possess only rudimentary intelligence,{{ref|name=EGTS}}{{ref|name=AD}} but they are acknowledged as having some cunning. As they are smart enough to be able to avoid traps,{{ref|name=EGTS}} or even use them to their advantage.{{ref|name=GD|[[Online:Gluineth|Gluineth]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Troll's Dessert|Troll's Dessert]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=VtUS|[[Blades:Vilfred The Unseen|Vilfred the Unseen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} It is said trolls are easily tricked, causing some jesters to postulate that if one was attacked from enough different angles, it would be possible to confuse one to death.{{ref|name=KyneC}}{{rp|212}} Some sources refer to them as a [[Lore:Race|race]],{{ref|name=NORP|{{Cite Book|Notes on Racial Phylogeny}}}}{{ref|name=FGtRT|{{Cite Book|Field Guide to River Trolls}}}} while others see them only as somewhat intelligent animals.{{ref|[[SR:Isran|Isran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Exterminate|[[Skyrim:Animal Extermination (B)|Animal Extermination]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=EGTS|{{Cite Book|EGT|Skyrim}}}} They are typically classified as "monsters", alongside bestial beings such as [[Lore:Ogre|Ogres]], [[Lore:Minotaur|Minotaurs]], and [[Lore:Harpy|Harpies]].{{ref|name=LCLAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

resided in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] since the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]],{{ref|[[ON:Azhklan Troll|Azhklan Troll]]s in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Blackwood ===Exotic Trolls===
There are several more exotic types of troll. River Trolls are an elusive breed of semi-aquatic trolls, recorded as the "Trohl of Rapace" as far back as {{Year|1E 200}} in the ''[[Lore:Tamrilean Tractates|Tamrilean Tractates]]''. They typically reside in riverside caves or burrows, and can hold their breath for long periods, lurking unseen in the shallows. They are clad in shiny water-shedding scales, silver-blue in color, that are both durable and flexible. Common prey for river trolls include [[Lore:Mudcrab|mudcrabs]], large [[Lore:Fish|fish]], [[Lore:Crocodile|crocodiles]], and unwary travelers.{{ref|name=FGtRT}}{{ref|name=RTESO|[[Online:River Troll|River Trolls]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Sea Trolls are a similar type of troll. One such Sea Troll was Skullbreaker, who resided on a beach in [[Lore:Silvenar Vale|Silvenar Vale]].{{ref|[[Online:Skullbreaker|Skullbreaker]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

Lava Trolls are an extinct breed said to have lived in [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. Their existence is believed by some to only be a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] myth. If they ever did exist, they are believed to be certainly extinct circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=FGtRT}} 

The [[Lore:Udyrfrykte|Udyrfrykte]]{{ref|name=BLUD}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Thirsk, A Revised History}}}} (also spelled Uderfrykte{{ref|name=AJUD}}{{Ref|name=TV|group=UOL|Unused [[Skyrim:Throw Voice|Throw Voice]] Word Wall in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} or Udefrykte){{ref|name=SRUD|[[Skyrim:Udefrykte|The Udefrykte]] in {{g|Skyrim}}}} is the name given to members of a bloodline of legendary trolls who are the spawn of the mysterious [[Lore:Uderfrykte Matron|Uderfrykte Matron]].{{ref|name=AJUD|{{Cite Book|Agnar's Journal}}}} Her lineage has plagued the Nords since ancient times.{{ref|name=SS-S}}{{Ref|name=TV|group=UOL|Unused [[Skyrim:Throw Voice|Throw Voice]] Word Wall in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Matron was killed in {{Year|3E 433}},{{ref|name=UM|[[OB:Uderfrykte Matron|Uderfrykte Matron]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} though at least one of her offspring outlived her.{{ref|name=SRUD}}

===Modified or Artificial Trolls===
Normal trolls have sometimes been modified in unusual ways. Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the Iron Orcs of [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]] fell under the influence of the [[Lore:Celestial|Celestial]] [[Lore:The Serpent|Serpent]]. By infusing the skin and armor of trolls with [[Lore:Nirncrux|nirncrux]], the Iron Orcs hoped to raise an army of giant primordial trolls for the [[Lore:Scaled Court|Scaled Court]].{{ref|[[Online:Little Leaf|Little Leaf]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LTEB|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Letter to Exarch Braadoth}}}} This operation was sabotaged before very many trolls could undergo the procedure, although the smuggling of these nirncrux-infused trolls out of Craglorn seemingly continued.{{ref|name=CommonGood|[[Online:The Common Good|The Common Good]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dark Brotherhood (DLC)|Dark Brotherhood]]''}} 

Additionally, there have been cases of magically created artificial trolls. [[Lore:Golem|Golems]] in the form of animated troll [[Lore:Statues|statues]] are one such variant.{{ref|[[Blades:Animated Troll Statue|Animated Troll Statues]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} [[Lore:Painted Troll|Painted Troll]]s are a strange form of troll created by the [[Lore:Brush of Truepaint|Brush of Truepaint]],{{ref|name=OBPT|[[OB:Painted Troll|Painted Trolls]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|[[Legends:Painted Troll|Painted Troll]] card in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} whose [[Lore:Painted Troll Fat|fat]] has become a rare and sought-after ingredient due to its alchemical properties.{{ref|name=SRElgrim|[[Skyrim:Elgrim|Elgrim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Polymorph spells exist that allow one to take on the apperance of a frost troll.{{ref|[[Online:Frost Troll (polymorph)|Frost Troll]] polymorph in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

==Interactions with People==
===Domestication===
[[File:SR-creature-Armored Troll.jpg|thumb|right|An Armored Troll (Skyrim)]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''Alright. Now take note. He'll mind you and us Dawnguard, but there's no accounting for other folk. And heed also this. He's no pet. Mistreat the beast or leave him alone for too long, and he'll return to the wild forgetting you ever existed. Now, you be careful with the creature.''|Gunmar after selling an armored troll{{ref|name=Gunmar}}}}
Troll breeding is an unusual hobby of some people.{{ref|[[Castles:Dialogue#Mood|Generic Dialogue]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} Trolls can be kept mostly docile in captivity if sated with plenty of meat and ale,{{ref|name=CaF|{{Cite Book|The Care and Feeding of Trolls}}}}{{Ref|group=UOL|name=TheMenagerie}} and can be trained to follow commands. However, they cannot be kept as simple pets. Trained trolls that are mistreated or left alone for too long will usually return to the wild, forgetting that their owner ever existed.{{ref|name=Gunmar|[[Skyrim:Gunmar|Gunmar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Additionally, there have been cases where otherwise tame trolls have gone into violent killing frenzies when they caught the scent of blood.{{ref|name=Tograz}}

Trolls are often trained and armored for use as guards or war beasts, these armored trolls are typically armed with cestuses or similar weapons.{{ref|name=DG|[[Skyrim:Troll#Armored_Troll|Armored Trolls]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOTROLL}}{{ref|name=Chung|[[Dawnstar:Chung|Chung]]'s dialogue in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} Tamed trolls have been utilized by primitive peoples such as [[Lore:Goblin|Goblins]],{{ref|[[Online:Stomper|Stomper]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Riekling|Rieklings]],{{ref|name=IS|[[Online:Icestalker|Icestalker]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Wrathstone|Wrathstone]]''}} and the [[Lore:Ice Tribes|Ice Tribes]].{{ref|Message Boxes during [[Dawnstar:Quest Walkthrough#The Final Showdown|the quest]] in ''[[Dawnstar:Dawnstar|Dawnstar]]''}} Powerful mages, such as those in the Mages Guild, have been known to use trolls as guardians.{{ref|name=AD|[[Books:Arena Player's Guide|Arena Player's Guide]] description of [[Arena:Troll|Trolls]]}}{{Ref|name=CTArena}}{{ref|name=ArenaT}} A bank in [[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]] was known to use trolls to guard their treasury,{{ref|[[Online:Amballicil|Amballicil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} with staff even claiming that their trolls could fend off other trolls due to being [[Lore:Vampire|vampiric]].{{ref|name=VampTroll|[[Online:Satuhaz|Satuhaz]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} Trolls were known to be among [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]]'s legions during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]].{{ref|[[Arena:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Circa {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]] tamed trolls native to Skyrim and used them in their crusade against [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]]s.{{ref|name=DG}} Trolls are sometimes even used for manual labor; the Iron Orcs of the Scaled Court would force their trolls to dig for nirncrux.{{ref|[[Online:Taskmaster Zagrakh|Taskmaster Zagrakh]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

Trolls have occasionally been sought out for menageries or to be otherwise displayed.{{ref|name=CDTP}}{{ref|[[ON:Elden Root|Elden Root]] [[Online:Undaunted Enclave (Elden Root)|Undaunted Enclave]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TheMenagerie|group=UOL}} Trolls were among the many creatures kept in [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]]'s [[Lore:Feral Gallery|Feral Gallery]], a collection of creatures in [[Lore:Apocrypha|Apocrypha]] maintained by the Daedric Prince.{{ref|[[ON:The Feral Gallery|The Feral Gallery]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Necrom (Chapter)|Necrom]]''}}
At some point in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], trolls were brought to the [[Lore:Padomaic Crest|Padomaic Crest]], where they were not originally native, as part of an attempt by the [[Lore:Telvanni|Telvanni]] to create a menagerie of beasts to rival the famous [[Lore:Sil-Var-Woad|Sil-Var-Woad]] of Summerset. Said attempt failed, and the small tribes of trolls that escaped would continuously be hunted by mercenaries trying cover up the failure.{{ref|name=CDTP}} Many frost trolls were also smuggled into the [[Lore:Telvanni Peninsula|Telvanni Peninsula]] by the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]], who had captured the beasts in Labyrinthian.{{ref|name=Cammona}} Trolls are also often captured for use in gladiatorial arenas,{{ref|[[ON:Fires of Battle|Fires of Battle]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} such as in the famous [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] [[Lore:Imperial City#Arena District|Arena]],{{ref|[[Oblivion:Arena Combatants|Arena Combatants]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} or at various locations in [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]].{{ref|name=OnTrollArena|[[ON:Troll Arena|Troll Arena]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|The Arena!}}}}{{ref|[[ON:Tholbor|Tholbor]]'s dialogue during [[ON:A Foot in the Door|A Foot in the Door]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

===Socialization===
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''We were fools to think we could live so close to such creatures and live peacefully. I should've headed back to camp with you after we placed the torches down here. I thought these trolls would be different, that they would somehow understand that we didn't want to hurt them. I am now cornered and it's only a matter of time before one of the trolls decides to finish me off. I hope it is a quick death.''|[[Skyrim:Darkfall Cave Note|Darkfall Cave Note]]}} 
Attempts have been made to socialize or befriend wild trolls,{{ref|name=TSRN}}{{ref|name=ATA}} but these typically don't end well.{{ref|name=TrollF}}{{ref|name=DeadMan|[[Online:Foloril|Forloril]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=BLCG}} For instance, when a rebellious Riekling known as Krish attempted to enlist the help of several grahl to overthrow [[Lore:Castle Karstaag|Castle Karstaag]], the creatures turned on the Rieklings and ate many of them.{{ref|name=KrishDulk|[[Bloodmoon:Krish|Krish]] and [[Bloodmoon:Dulk|Dulk]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Trying to offer wild trolls food or goods has proven to do little towards decreasing their aggression.{{ref|name=BLCG}}{{ref|name=ATA}}{{ref|name=DeadMan}}

However there have been exceptions where people have managed to live peacefully among wild trolls, at least for a time. For example: In {{Year|2E 582}} the Orc researcher Grularz managed to temporarily interact safely with a group of trolls in Crestshade Mine. By adopting the troll's behavior and mannerisms, they tolerated her presence, even deep into their territory. She was ultimately killed after the conclusion of her study, when she attempted to hug the troll's patriarch.{{ref|name=TSRN}}{{ref|name=TrollF|[[Online:Friend of Trolls|Friend of Trolls]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} A more successful example is that of the Trinimac worshipper Ghorkul, who somehow managed to gain the trust of a group of wild trolls in his exile.{{ref|name=TH|Dialogue during [[BL:The Heretic|The Heretic]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

The relationship between trolls and [[Lore:Spriggan|Spriggans]] is somewhat unclear, as they have been documented having varied interactions. In some circumstances, trolls and spriggans fight against each other.{{ref|[[Skyrim:World Interactions#Spriggans fighting trolls|World Interaction]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, certain powerful Spriggans, such as Spriggan Matriarchs, are able to control and command trolls as they would other any other animal.{{ref|[[ON:Land Dispute|Land Dispute]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Viridian Sentinel|Viridian Sentinel]] of northern [[Lore:Bangkorai|Bangkorai]] was known for their ability to ward off trolls and other wild creatures of nature.{{ref|name=TVS}}

While trolls are seen as incredibly deadly to the average person,{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Last Will and Testament}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Skyrim|Darkfall Cave Note}}}} certain warriors or adventurers style themselves as troll hunters or trollslayers.{{ref|name=TS|{{Cite Book|Troll Slaying}}}}{{ref|name=TTH}}{{ref|[[ON:Trollslayer's Gully|Trollslayer's Gully]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One famous example was [[Lore:Bagrar|Bagrar]], an Orc shield-wife who earned the title of "Troll-Slayer" through her impressive combat prowess.{{ref|name=AC|{{Cite Book|Agra Crun (book)}}}} Troll hunters are known use troll musk to mask their scent while hunting,{{ref|[[Online:Treasures T#Troll Musk|Troll Musk]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and some even create enchanted weapons or special armor especially effective for fighting trolls.{{ref|[[SR:Trollsbane|Trollsbane]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[Castles:Troll Slayer|Troll Slayer]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}{{ref|name=TSmash|{{Item Link|summary=1|quality=f|Trollsmasher|id=10892}} in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TSG|{{Item Link|summary=y|quality=f|Trollslayer's Girdle|id=10943}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Yahiri|Yahiri]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Lord's Mail|Lord's Mail]], the famous cuirass of the [[Lore:Demigod|Demigod]] [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]], is sometimes referred to as the "bane of trolls everywhere".{{ref|[[Arena:Artifact Quests#The Lord's Mail|Artifact Quest]] dialogue in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Lunar [[Lore:Wolf|wolves]], a breed of wolf from Solstheim, were allegedly bred by [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elves]] and are competent at battling trolls.{{ref|[[Online:Solstheim Lunar Wolf|Solstheim Lunar Wolf]] mount description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Often hunters without a specialization in trolls may attempt to kill the creatures, either for sport or mercenary work.{{ref|name=KyneC|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Ledronor|Ledronor]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Hunting the Troll|Hunting the Troll]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=CastlesRulings}}
The [[Lore:Companions|Companions]] of [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] are often hired to exterminate trolls that attack farmers and travelers.{{ref|name=Exterminate}} The Rite of Akatosh is an endeavor held by Cyrodilic elders during the [[Lore:Old Life Festival|Old Life Festival]] in preparation for the [[Lore:New Life Festival|New Life Festival]], in which their party goes on a pilgrimage to rid the nearby forests of "Old Life": monsters like trolls and Goblin-ken.{{ref|name=OldLife}}

==Cultural Impact==
===Art and Religion===
[[File:SR-book-Trollbook.png|thumb|left|Artwork of a troll from the book ''Troll Slaying'']][[File:ON-tapestry-Orcish Tapestry, Hunt.jpg|An Orcish tapestry depicting a troll hunt (ESO)|thumb|right]]{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''The origin of the race of trolls is somewhat obscure—which is another way of saying we know absolutely nothing about where they come from, how they got here, or why they exist at all.''|[[Lore:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus of Elinhir]] in ''[[Lore:Field Guide to River Trolls|Field Guide to River Trolls]]''}}
Trolls have been present in Tamrielic folklore "from time immemorial",{{ref|name=FGtRT}} having existed since the Dawn Era.{{ref|name=Serpent|[[Online:The Serpent (Celestial)|The Serpent]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TAO}} They are known to nearly every culture and have appeared in tales of all sorts, from children's fables to the histories of the ancients.{{ref|name=FGtRT}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Brave Little Scrib and the River Troll}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Goodnight Mundus}}}}{{ref|name=KEPV}} While their origins are considered unknown by most academics,{{ref|name=FGtRT}} several cultures believe trolls to be inherently adversarial beings. The [[Lore:Winterborn|Winterborn]] tribe of [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] believe that Malacath created trolls, along with Orcs and Ogres, to provide hardships and to test his chosen followers.{{ref|name=MATR|{{Cite Book|Malacath and the Reach}}}} [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] believed that trolls, along with Orcs and Goblins, were among "the miscreated", malicious and brutish creatures who, as [[Lore:The One|the One]] teaches, must be united against by all sentient folk of good will instead of striving against each other.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Real Barenziah, v 3}}}} Skaal myth speaks of grahl as unholy beasts, and aspects of [[Lore:The Adversary|the Adversary]].{{ref|name=Aevar|{{Cite Book|The Story of Aevar Stone-Singer}}}} Some conflate trolls with [[Lore:Daedra|daedra]], the [[Lore:Undead|undead]], or other creatures of [[Lore:Padomay|the Dark]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Light and the Dark}}}}

Trolls are typically considered natural creatures,{{ref|name=TVS}} and as such have some association with the goddess [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]], who is known as the "Mother of Men and beasts".{{ref|name=FWB}} One of her most powerful [[Lore:Guardian Spirit|guardian spirit]] champions, a being faced during the goddess's sacred trials, is known to take the form of a troll.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Guardian Troll Spirit|Guardian Troll Spirit]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=FWB|[[Skyrim:Froki Whetted-Blade|Froki Whetted-Blade]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In addition, some people etch Kyne's War Prayer into troll teeth.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures E#Etched Troll Tooth|Etched Troll Tooth]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Morachellis Hag-Husband|Morachellis Hag-Husband]], a legendary [[Lore:Skald|skald]], once posited the idea that a troll's third eye might be attuned to the ethereal realm of spirits parallel to [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]], rather than the mundane world. However, it's important to note that his views on the subject were not considered particularly reliable, as he made many other dubious claims.{{ref|name=TFG|[[ON:The Frigid Grotto|The Frigid Grotto]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Various forms of art depicting trolls exist.{{ref|name=Plate|[[Online:Treasures L#The Lonely Troll Commemorative Plate|The Lonely Troll Commemorative Plate]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Treasures P#Partial Visual Guide to Trolls|Partial Visual Guide to Trolls]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=KingBust|[[Online:Bust: The Troll King|Bust:The Troll King]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} A painting known as the "Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch" is said to depict how [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] imagine the frost trolls of Skyrim; the painting depicts frost trolls smiling and dancing in the snow.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures F#"Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch"|"Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch"]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Small troll statues carved from [[Lore:Ironwood|ironwood]] by the Matrons of [[Lore:Shor's Stone|Shor's Stone]], known as garden trolls, are used to protect gardens from unwanted pests.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures G#Garden Troll Varmint Defender|Garden Troll Varmint Defender]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Bookends modeled after trolls have been made, but they frightened potential buyers so much that few sets still exist.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures M#Matching Troll Bookends|Matching Troll Bookends]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

There are several types of children's toys that are made to resemble trolls,{{ref|[[Online:Treasures R#River Troll Marionette|River Troll Marionette]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} typically dolls.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures F#Frost Troll Ragamuffin Doll|Frost Troll Ragamuffin Doll]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Treasures G#Gilly the River Troll|Gilly the River Troll]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Treasures C#Child's Ambiguous Doll|Child's Ambiguous Doll]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Troll claws with prayers carved into them are sometimes given to children and are intended to resemble a [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]]'s claw.{{ref|[[Online:Treasures C#Claw of Akatosh|Claw of Akatosh]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===Linguistics===
Trolls are commonly referred to in Tamrielic phrases and [[Lore:Exclamations|exclamations]]. "Troll's blood" is a common Nordic exclamation,{{ref|[[Skyrim:Skaggi Scar-Face|Skaggi Scar-Face]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Hectur the Fish|Hectur the Fish]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[On:Holgunn One-Eye|Holgunn One-Eye]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} while "son of a troll" is used more by Orcs.{{ref|name=Guards}}{{ref|[[Online:Lob the Cleaver|Lob the Cleaver]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Zhagush gro-Korlag|Zhagush gro-Korlag]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Various insults related to trolls include: "troll-bait",{{ref|name=Guards}}{{ref|[[Skyrim:Throw Voice|Throw Voice]] [[Skyrim:Dragon Shouts|shout]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|[[Online:Bekka Owlcloak|Bekka Owlcloack]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} "troll-breath",{{Ref|name=ONKurog|[[Online:King Kurog|King Kurog]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Orsinium (DLC)|Orsinium]]''}}{{ref|name=Guards|[[Online:Guard Dialogue|Guard Dialogue]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} "troll lover",{{Ref|name=ONKurog}} "troll-face",{{ref|name=Guards}} and "gutter-troll".{{ref|[[Online:Serileth|Serileth]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} "She-troll" is occasionally used as an insulting name for women.{{ref|[[ON:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 5}}}} Troll dung is often referred to in [[Lore:Profanity|expletives]] and insults,{{ref|name=Chung}}{{ref|[[Redguard:Trithik|Trithik]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} typically by Orcs.{{ref|name=SBHam}}{{ref|[[Online:Forge-Wife Kharza|Forge-Wife Kharza]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Stilga}} "When the troll dung hit the [[Lore:Horker|horker]]" is a phrase that refers to something going horribly wrong.{{ref|[[Online:Ghorn|Ghorn]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

Trolls are the namesake of certain spells, such as "Troll's Blood", a regeneration spell, and "Troll Strength", a strength fortifying spell.{{ref|[[Daggerfall:Troll's Blood|Troll's Blood]] spell in ''[[DF:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Troll Strength|Troll Strength]] spell in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Those born under the sign of [[Lore:The Lord|the Lord]] are sometimes referred to as having the "Trollkin curse" due to their innate weakness to fire.{{ref|[[Oblivion:The Lord|The Lord]] birthsign in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Some Nords have "Half-Troll" as their last name,{{ref|[[MW:Yngling Half-Troll|Yngling Half-Troll]] and [[MW:Greidil Half-Troll|Greidil Half-Troll]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|[[ON:Daljari Half-Troll|Daljari Half-Troll]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} but considering typical Nordic [[Lore:Nord Names|naming conventions]], it is unlikely this is literal.

==Notable Trolls==
[[File:BL-creature-The Beast of Gorrham.jpg|The Beast of Gorrham (Blades)|thumb|right]]
*'''The Ancient One''': An ancient troll, said to have roamed Nirn since the dawn of time. Circa {{Year|4E 180}}, a group of [[Lore:Aureal|Aureal]] who served [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] were rumored to have made some sort of alliance with the beast. [[Lore:The Warrior (person)|The Warrior]] ended up following these rumors through a portal and managed to slay the monstrous creature.{{ref|name=TAO}}

* '''The Beast of Gorrham''': A very large troll with distinct horns and spikes that attacked the village of Gorrham on the outskirts of the [[Lore:Bloodfall Kingdom|Bloodfall Kingdom]], causing it to be burned down.{{ref|[[Blades:Murbak|Murbak]]'s dialogue in ''[[BL:Blades|Blades]]''}} Notably, unlike most trolls,{{ref|name=BLCG|[[Blades:Constantia Galena|Constantia Galena]]'s dialogue in {{g|BL}}}} it did not fear fire. It was slain by the Warrior circa {{Year|4E 180}}.{{ref|name=BLDN|[[BL:Deadly Neighbor|Deadly Neighbor]] quest in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

*'''Boulder the Blood-drinker''': An impressive troll with several feats ascribed to it. He is said to have single-handedly halted a [[Lore:Colovian|Colovian]] attack, and to have defeated three giants by himself. He was eventually captured and made to fight in the Trollfight Arena in Reaper's March circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=Khaba|[[ON:Khaba|Khaba]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Boulder|Boulder]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OnTrollArena}}

*'''Death-In-Winter''': An alpha troll whose pack once terrorized the town of [[Lore:Darkwater Crossing|Darkwater Crossing]], until it was slain by [[Lore: The Vestige|the Vestige]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=RWQ|[[ON:Runs-With-Quickness|Runs-With-Quickness]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Our Poor Town|Our Poor Town]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

*'''Frostmaul Facecleaver''': A fearsome troll claimed to have once terrorized the back country of Reaper's March, including places like [[Lore:Vinedusk Village|Vinedusk Village]]. He was eventually captured by a trainer and made to fight in the Trollfight Arena circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=Khaba|[[ON:Khaba|Khaba]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[ON:Frosty|Frosty]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=OnTrollArena}}

*'''Icestalker''': A large, powerful frost troll who had been outfitted with [[Lore:Dwarven Metal|Dwarven]] armor by the Rieklings of the Coldsnap Tribe. It dwelt in the [[Lore:Vault of Mhuvnak|Vault of Mhuvnak]], a Dwarven ruin in [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], and was slain by members of the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]] circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=IS}}

*'''Ironfang''': A powerful nirncrux-infused troll from Craglorn that was smuggled to the [[Lore:Gold Coast|Gold Coast]] by the [[Lore:Gold Coast Trading Company|Gold Coast Trading Company]]. It was housed in the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruin [[Lore:Garlas Agea|Garlas Agea]], with it escaping being considered a threat to the entire region.{{ref|[[Online:Laronen|Laronen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Dark Brotherhood (DLC)|Dark Brotherhood]]''}} It was slain by the Vestige circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=CommonGood}}

*'''The Troll King''': A gigantic and merciless troll originally from Vvardenfell. The Troll King was eventually captured, armored, and made to fight in the Blessed Crucible, a gladiatorial arena in [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]].{{ref|[[Online:The Beast Master|The Beast Master]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}} He was slain by members of the Undaunted circa {{Year|2E 582}}, and his head was severed and taken as a trophy.{{ref|[[Online:The Troll King|The Troll King]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=KingHead}}  A decorative bust, as well as arms and armor based on his appearance would also be created.{{ref|name=KingBust|[[Online:Bust: The Troll King|Bust:The Troll King]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Troll King Style|Troll King Style]] in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

*'''The Udyrfrykte, the Uderfrykte Matron, and the Udefrykte''': See [[Lore:Udyrfrykte|this article]].
