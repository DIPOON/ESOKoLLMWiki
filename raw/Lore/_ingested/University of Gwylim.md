# Lore:University of Gwylim

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Factions Trail}}{{TOCright}}</noinclude>
The [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]] (also called the '''University of Gwilym''' or '''Gwylim University''') is an ancient scholarly organization dating back to the late [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], and was founded by the [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] scholar [[Lore:Tjurhane Fyrre|Tjurhane Fyrre]].{{ref|name=ONVerita|[[Online:Verita Numida|Verita Numida]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} The '''University of Gwylim Press''' has been responsible for many publications throughout history. It is known that the university awards the '''Gwylim Prize''' to scholars at times.{{ref|name=ONHilo Sylla|[[ON:Hilo Sylla|Hilo Sylla]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}

The University of Gwylim itself is found in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]],{{ref|name=ONVerita}} however, the term Gwylim is a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] word.{{ref|name=TeddyPP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ted Peterson's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}} [[Lore:Phrastus of Elinhir|Phrastus of Elinhir]] originally taught at the university before being forced to flee to [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]] due to the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]].{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} There is a forbidden section of Gwylim University containing books,{{ref|name=AzandarDialogue|[[ON:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and it is unknown what else it carries. They have a satellite campus called the Gwylim Annex in Solitude.{{ref|name=ONVerita}}<noinclude>

The Antiquarian Circle is the University's most prestigious historical society, which scours the continent for lost antiquities to collect and catalogue.{{ref|name=ONVerita}}

==History and Overview==
The university's faculty are known to have an interest in practical pedagogy,{{ref|name=PSoM|{{Cite Book|Proposal: Schools of Magic}}}} and is known to offer training in the magical school of [[Lore:Illusion|Illusion]].{{Ref|name=Silence|{{Cite Book|Silence}}}} Tjurhane Fyrre was also one of the finest sages of the university. His book on Ayleidic culture, ''Nature of Ayleidic Poesy'', was published by the university in {{Year|2E 12}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Wild Elves}}}} Fyrre was also responsible for writing a number of unpublished biographies on various Ayleid kings, including King [[Lore:Narilmor|Narilmor]] of [[Lore:Garlas Malatar|Garlas Malatar]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - King Narilmor}}}} Other Ayleidic texts were also published by the university, including ''[[Lore:Ayleid Cities of Valenwood|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood]]'', written by the [[Lore:Books by Author#Homfrey|Esteemed Historian Homfrey]] in {{Year|2E 455}}.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}} "The Gwylim Praxis" by the Aureate Serpent is a book that describes methods of draining magicka from intelligent creatures at the moment of death, but it is unknown what its connection to the university is.{{Ref|name=TLoDRB|{{Cite Book|The Library of Dusk: Rare Books}}}}<noinclude>

The wizard and prominent author [[Lore:Gabrielle Benele|Gabrielle Benele]] was once a member of the university before she joined the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] around the time of the Three Banners War.{{ref|name=PSoM}} She would later rejoin the university through the Antiquarian Circle and serve as their chief arcanist and leader.{{Ref|name=ONAITCC|{{Cite Book|An Introduction to Circle Correspondence|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|name=ONYvara|[[Online:Yvara Plouff|Yvara Plouff]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} She also invited a number of important figures to the University of Gwylim for interviews and discussion seminars, such as Madam Whim of [[Lore:Fargrave|Fargrave]], [[Lore:Azandar al-Cybiades|Azandar al-Cybiades]], and Master [[Lore:Malkhest|Malkhest]].{{Ref|name=LAMM|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Malacath and Maelstrom}}}}{{ref|name=LATA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - The Arcanists}}}}{{ref|name=LAIA|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Infinite Archive}}}} The famed sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] also attended one of these discussion seminars, and revealed himself to be a generous patron to the university.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}}

At some point, the university funded a scholarly expedition to the ruins of {{Lore Link|Skyreach}} in [[Lore:Craglorn|Craglorn]], led by Historian {{Lore Link|Reginus Buca}} and Ancientist {{Lore Link|Verita Numida}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|Skyreach Explorer}}}} The scholar {{Lore Link|Mederic Vyger}} was a lecturer at the university and an expert on [[Lore:Nede|Nede]]s who helped the [[Lore:Scaled Court|Scaled Court]] in their study of the Skyreach ruins in {{Year|2E 582}}, although he left the cult after awakening the ancient [[Lore:Celestial|Celestial]] creatures in {{Lore Link|Skyreach Pinnacle}} and subsequently aided the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] in restoring their wards.{{ref|[[ON:Mederic Vyger|Mederic Vyger]] during [[ON:Uncaged|Uncaged]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

''[[Lore:Ancient Tales of the Dwemer|Ancient Tales of the Dwemer]]'', [[Lore:Books by Author#Marobar Sul|Marobar Sul]]'s infamous eleven volumes of fictional stories about the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] published around {{Year|2E 670}}, was originally presented as a serious, scholarly work based on research in the archives of the University of Gwylim. The stories proved popular, and were accepted as fact. However, scholars later surmised that Marobar Sul was simply a pseudonym for [[Lore:Books by Author#Gor Felim|Gor Felim]], a prolific writer of the era, and the writings were debunked.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Dwemer History and Culture}}}} The university continued to publish the surviving fragments of Sul's writings, although they were noted as being works of fiction.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Ransom of Zarek}}}}

''[[Lore:Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie|Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie]]'', written by the scribe [[Lore:Books by Author#Mabei Aywenil|Mabei Aywenil]], was first translated and published by the university in {{Year|3E 105}}, or {{Year|2E 105}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie}}}}{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}} The university first published ''[[Lore:Frontier, Conquest|Frontier, Conquest, and Accommodation: A Social History of Cyrodiil]]'' in {{Year|3E 344}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Frontier, Conquest}}}} Doctor Intricatus of Gwylim University was noted for his ethnographic research into the "{{Lore Link|Primal}}" style of weapons and armor, worn by [[Lore:Goblin|goblins]] and other primitive beastfolk.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 13: Primal Style}}}} ''The Gwylim Praxis'' is a rare book written by the [[Lore:Books by Author#The Aureate Serpent|Aureate Serpent]], which details methods of draining magicka from intelligent creatures at the moment of death.{{ref|name=TLoDRB}}
