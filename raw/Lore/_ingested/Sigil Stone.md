# Lore:Sigil Stone

<!-- UESP / ns 130 / dump 2026-03-13 -->

<noinclude>{{Lore Artifacts Trail}}__NOTOC__[[File:SR-icon-misc-SigilStone.png|right|250px]]</noinclude>
A [[Lore:Sigil Stone|Sigil Stone]] is a pre-[[Lore:Mythic Era|Mythic]] quasi-crystalline [[Lore:Morpholith|morpholith]] that has been transformed into an extra-dimensional artifact through the arcane inscription of a Daedric [[Lore:sigil|sigil]], which can be used to create [[Lore:Magical Transportation|portals]] from [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] to [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]].{{ref|name=LB|{{Cite Book|Liminal Bridges}}}} They are spherical objects, and emit both a humming noise and a dark red glow. The morpholiths used to create the Sigil Stone can only occur in pocket voids of Oblivion, and harvesting them requires [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] assistance. The cleansed morpholith must then be presented to a [[Lore:Daedra Lord|Daedra Lord]] to perform the inscription of the sigil, which will transform the specimen into a true Sigil Stone. The Daedra Lord and the mechanic of the Sigil Stone must then jointly invoke the [[Lore:Conjuration|conjurational]] charter, which will transport the Stone through the [[Lore:Liminal Barriers|liminal barrier]] and create a temporary portal. The portal only remains open for a brief period of time, depending on the strength of the liminal barrier at the chosen location; the longest recorded time under these circumstances was only a few minutes, making this type of portal rather useless. The power of a Sigil Stone is similar to that of a filled [[Lore:Soul Gem|soul gem]], but much stronger,{{ref|name=LB}} to the extent that is has been said that even a thousand soul gems would likely dissolve in the presence of a Stone.{{ref|name=Excavation|[[Skyrim:Excavation Leader's Journal|Excavation Leader's Journal]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Many ready-made sigil stones can be found in Oblivion, and can be obtained by powerful conjurers who force an unbound Daedra into submission. [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] are often used for this purpose, although their Lord is angered by this theft.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Due to [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]'s sacrifice at the end of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the Liminal Barrier constructed by the event is too strong for even a Sigil Stone to breach for more than a fleeting moment.{{ref|name=VonosJournal|{{Cite Book|Vonos' Journal}}}}{{ref|name=OBMartin|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{ref|name=Excavation}}<noinclude>

==Uses==
[[File:LG-cardart-Sigil Keeper.png|A Sigil Keeper|right|thumb]]
Although mainly used by Mehrunes Dagon, Sigil Stones are known to also be used in other realms of Oblivion such as [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]].{{ref|[[ON:Moonhenge's Tear|Moonhenge's Tear]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Sigil Stones have other uses apart from sustaining liminal bridges. One was used as an opposing force to a Great Welkynd Stone to open a portal to [[Lore:Gaiar Alata|Gaiar Alata]]. Daedric Siege Crawlers use them as a power source. The arcane power of a Stone can be used in enchanting, consuming the Stone in the process.{{ref|name=Oblivion}} Powerful conjurers require Sigil Stones for use in rituals to summon items and Daedra from Oblivion when using the [[Skyrim:Atronach Forge|Atronach Forge]], although the Stone isn't necessary when summoning lesser objects.{{ref|name=Skyrim}} Sigil Stones can also be used to create less permanent portals to Oblivion, which can be opened at will as long as the Stone remains intact, although this method binds the stone to a particular location in Mundus.{{ref|[[ON:A Change of Heart|A Change of Heart]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
During the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the loss of the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] caused the liminal barrier to slowly weaken and allowed these liminal bridges to remain open indefinitely.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Brother Martin|Martin Septim]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} This was exploited by the [[Lore:Mythic Dawn|Mythic Dawn]] and Mehrunes Dagon, who created Oblivion Gates to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]], allowing the Daedric forces to invade [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. A method of destroying these Gates was discovered by the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] at the Sack of [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]]: removing the Sigil Stones from the well-defended keeps in the Deadlands where they were hidden would break the connection, teleporting those near the Stone back to Mundus and destroying both the Oblivion Gate and its Sigil Keep in the process. Occasionally, Gates would have multiple Sigil Stones supporting the link, and all of them would need to be removed to shut the Gate;{{ref|name=OBMobile|Events of ''[[OBMobile:Oblivion Mobile|Oblivion Mobile]]''}} similarly, one Sigil Stone may support multiple Gates. This technique for opening portals was exploited at the Battle of [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] by the united forces of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] (led by [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]]) to procure a '''Great Sigil Stone''', a more powerful specimen used to sustain larger portals such as the "Great Gates". Each Oblivion Gate which was opened weakened the liminal barrier, eventually completely diminishing it and allowing Mehrunes Dagon pass fully into Mundus. The barriers were restored when the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] was smashed, summoning the power of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] to banish Dagon.{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The legendary [[Lore:Arms of Chaos|Arms of Chaos]] were created through the use of Sigil Stones, from which they derived their power. An Orange Sigil Stone was embedded in the [[Lore:Arm of the Sun|Arm of the Sun]], and a Green Sigil Stone was affixed to the [[Lore:Arm of the Moon|Arm of the Moon]]. A mage named Ellane later discovered the means by which these [[Lore:Staves|staves]] were created, and used a Blue Sigil Stone to create the [[Lore:Warlock's Mark|Warlock's Mark]], an enchanted necklace.{{ref|name=HJ|{{Cite Book|ns_base=SR|Hyenril's Journal}}}} However, the stones were subsequently removed and returned to Oblivion, to be guarded by three Dremora known as the Guardians of the Sigil Stones. Circa {{Year|4E 201}} the Guardians were summoned and defeated by the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] in order to recover the three Sigil Stones and restore the disassembled artifacts.{{ref|name=CC|[[SR:Arms of Chaos|Arms of Chaos]] [[SR:Creation Club|Creation]] for [[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]] [[SR:Special Edition|Special Edition]]}}
