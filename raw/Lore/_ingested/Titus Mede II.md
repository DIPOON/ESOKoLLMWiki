[[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]] was an [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|emperor]] of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] and [[Lore:Mede Dynasty|Mede Dynasty]] during the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]]. Three years after his accession, Titus II was pulled into the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]] against the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#Third Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]]. After four years, Titus II and the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]] defeated the Aldmeri Dominion's main army in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], but the Empire was in no shape to continue the war, forcing Titus II to sign the controversial [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]].{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}} The Concordat outlawed the worship of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] and forced the Empire to cede a large section of southern [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] to the Dominion.{{ref|name=TGW}} The ban on Talos worship caused discontent and eventually led to the [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Stormcloak Rebellion]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].<noinclude>

==Biography==
===Background===
Little is known of Titus Mede's life prior to his accession to the throne. It is known that he had an aunt named Alexia Vici and a first cousin named [[Lore:Vittoria Vici|Vittoria Vici]].{{ref|name=SrAVd|[[Skyrim:Alexia Vici|Alexia Vici]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrVVd|[[Skyrim:Vittoria Vici|Vittoria Vici]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
 
When Titus Mede II acceded to the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]] in {{Year|4E 168}}, he inherited a weakened Empire.{{ref|name=TGW}} [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]], [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], and [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] had all ceased to be Imperial provinces.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=RTV4|{{Cite Book|Rising Threat, Vol. IV}}}} [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] was plagued by infighting between the [[Lore:Crowns|Crowns]] and [[Lore:Forebears|Forebears]].{{ref|name=TGW}} This left [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], and [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] as the only provinces under imperial rule that remained prosperous and peaceful by the time of Titus II's accession.{{ref|name=TGW}}

===The Great War===
[[File:LG-quest-A Fractured Legion 07.jpg|thumb|left|Titus Mede II during the Great War]]
Titus II had only a few years to consolidate his rule before his leadership was put to the test.{{ref|name=TGW}} On the 30th of Frostfall, {{Year|4E 171}}, the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion#Third Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] sent an ambassador to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], demanding tribute, the disbandment of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], the outlaw of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] worship, and the cession of large sections of Hammerfell to the Dominion.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=SrEsbd|[[Skyrim:Esbern|Esbern]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=BlBd|[[Blades:Boregis|Boregis]]' dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} The Emperor's generals warned of the Empire's military weakness, but despite this, he rejected the Dominion's ultimatum, thus beginning the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]].{{ref|name=TGW}}
 
Within days, armies of the Aldmeri Dominion invaded Hammerfell and Cyrodiil simultaneously. A force commanded by the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] general, Lord [[Lore:Naarifin|Naarifin]], attacked Cyrodiil from the south, and [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] soon fell to the invaders, while [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]] was besieged. Lady {{Lore Link|Arannelya}} led another army that entered western Cyrodiil from Valenwood, bypassed [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] and [[Lore:Kvatch|Kvatch]], and crossed to Hammerfell to join the Dominion forces landing on its southern coast. During {{Year|4E 172}}, the Dominion advanced deeper into Cyrodiil, capturing Anvil, Bravil, and [[Lore:Skingrad|Skingrad]]. By the end of 4E 172, Naarifin had advanced to the walls of the Imperial City. In {{Year|4E 173}}, fresh [[Lore:Imperial Legion|legions]] from Skyrim bolstered the Emperor's main army in the Imperial City, but the Dominion crossed the [[Lore:Niben River|Niben]] and began advancing up the eastern bank. By the end of 4E 173, the Imperial City was surrounded on three sides, and only the northern supply route to [[Lore:Bruma|Bruma]] remained open. On the 12th of Second Seed, {{Year|4E 174}}, the Dominion launched a massive assault on the Imperial City. One Aldmeri army went north to completely surround the city, while Naarifin's main force attacked the walls from the south, east, and west. With the city surrounded, Titus II made the decision to break out of the city rather than make a last stand. While the Eighth Legion fought a desperate and doomed rearguard action on the walls, Titus II broke out of the city to the north with his main army, breaking through the surrounding Dominion forces and linking up with reinforcements marching south from Skyrim under General Jonna.{{ref|name=TGW}} Following the Emperor's breakout, the Dominion forces captured the Imperial City, and they then sacked it.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=LgKd|[[Legends:Kellen|Kellen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=EP|{{Cite Book|Enchanter's Primer}}}}{{ref|name=SrMd|[[Skyrim:Madena|Madena]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
 
[[File:LG-quest-A Fractured Legion 06.jpg|thumb|right|Titus Mede II during the Great War]]
After the breakout from the Imperial City, Titus II and the Imperial Army retreated north to Skyrim.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=LgKd}} During the winter of 4E 174-[[Lore:Fourth_Era#4E_175|175]], the Dominion seems to have believed that the war in Cyrodiil was all but over, and they made several attempts to negotiate with Titus II. The Emperor misled the Dominion into believing that he was considering surrendering; all the while, he was gathering his forces to retake the Imperial City.{{ref|name=TGW}}
 
Titus II divided his forces into three armies. One army, with legions from Hammerfell under General Decianus, was concealed in the [[Lore:Colovian Highlands|Colovian Highlands]] near [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]]. The second army, comprised mostly of [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] legions under General Jonna, took up position near [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]]. The Emperor commanded the main army, which was positioned to the north of the Imperial City, where it would launch the main assault. On the 30th of Rain's Hand, 4E 175, the [[Lore:Great War|Battle of the Red Ring]] began. General Decianus' army swept down upon the city from the west, while General Jonna's legionaries went south along the [[Lore:Red Ring Road|Red Ring Road]]. By the fifth day of the battle, the Imperial City was surrounded.{{ref|name=TGW}} Titus II led the main assault from the north, capturing Naarifin while supposedly wielding [[Lore:Goldbrand|Goldbrand]].{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=EJ|{{Cite Book|Eranya's Journal|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Dominion forces attempted to break out of the city to the south, but they failed, and, ultimately, the main Dominion army in Cyrodiil was destroyed. The Imperial City was once again in imperial hands. After capturing Naarifin, he was hanged from the [[Lore:White-Gold Tower|White-Gold Tower]] for thirty-three days before dying.{{ref|name=TGW}} After the Battle of the Red Ring, Titus II personally decorated a legionary named Martius Silius.{{ref|name=BlATd|[[Blades:Arielle Themond|Arielle Themond]]'s dialogue in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

[[File:LG-quest-A Knife in the Dark 04.jpg|thumb|left|Titus II wounded after an assassination attempt by the Aldmeri Dominion]]
An alternate account of the events of the Battle of the Red Ring, as told by the [[Lore:Moth Priests|Moth Priest]] [[Lore:Kellen|Kellen]], asserts that it was actually a [[Lore:Hero#Forgotten_Hero|Forgotten Hero]] and not Titus II who led the Imperial Army at the battle.{{ref|name=LTFH|Events of [[Legends:The Forgotten Hero|The Forgotten Hero]] story in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} This account claims that the hero and their companions learned about Lord Naarifin's plot to sacrifice the citizens of the Imperial City to summon masses of [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] in a prophecy known as "The Culling".{{ref|name=LgKd}} They told the Emperor of the prophecy, who was horrified to hear of Naarifin's plan but knew an attack on the Imperial City was doomed to fail.{{ref|name=LAFL|Events of [[Legends:A Fractured Legion|A Fractured Legion]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} Titus II informed the hero and their companions that the Dominion could somehow predict the Imperial Legion's every move, so they volunteered to infiltrate the city and discover the source of this ability.{{ref|name=LAFL}}

The hero and their companions discovered that the [[Lore:Orb of Vaermina|Orb of Vaermina]] was what allowed Naarifin to see the Empire's movements, and after destroying it, they fled the Imperial City to inform the Emperor.{{ref|name=LIn|Events of [[Legends:Interlude|Interlude]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=LRW|Events of [[Legends:Reive's Wrath|Reive's Wrath]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} When they returned to the Emperor's camp, they found the imperial guards dead and Titus II wounded from a failed assassination attempt by the Dominion.{{ref|name=LAKitD|Events of [[Legends:A Knife in the Dark|A Knife in the Dark]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}} Titus II was too wounded to lead the assault, so he told the hero to wear his armor and masquerade as him at the Battle of the Red Ring.{{ref|name=LAKitD}} After the retaking of the Imperial City, the hero quietly disappeared, and the true Emperor took command.{{ref|name=LEpi|Events of [[Legends:Epilogue|Epilogue]] in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}

===The White-Gold Concordat===
Although victorious at the Battle of the Red Ring, the Empire was in no shape to continue the war. Titus II knew that the Empire would be in no better position to negotiate peace, and in late 4E 175, the Empire and the Aldmeri Dominion signed the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], ending the Great War.{{ref|name=TGW}} The Concordat's two most controversial terms were the ban on Talos worship and the cession of a large section of southern Hammerfell.{{ref|name=TGW}}{{ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Skyrim's Rule}}}}{{ref|name=FftT|{{Cite Book|Flight from the Thalmor}}}}{{ref|name=SrHeid|[[Skyrim:Heimskr|Heimskr]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Critics of the treaty pointed out that the terms of the Concordat were almost identical to the ultimatum the Emperor rejected in 4E 171. The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]], however, refused to accept the Concordat, being unwilling to accept the loss of so much of their territory. In order to preserve the peace treaty, Titus II was forced to officially renounce Hammerfell as an imperial province.{{ref|name=TGW}}
 
===Aftermath of the War===
After five more years of fighting, the Redguards fought the Dominion to a standstill in {{Year|4E 180}}. The two sides signed the [[Lore:Second Treaty of Stros M'kai|Second Treaty of Stros M'kai]], resulting in the withdrawal of the Aldmeri Dominion from Hammerfell. Some critics claimed that the Dominion's failure to defeat the Redguards was proof that the White-Gold Concordat was unnecessary and that if Titus II had kept his nerve, the Dominion could have been defeated by the combined forces of Hammerfell and the rest of the Empire.{{ref|name=TGW}} Some viewed Titus II's decision to sign the White-Gold Concordat as cowardice,{{ref|name=SrHeid}}{{ref|name=SrTSB|[[Skyrim:Thongvor Silver-Blood|Thongvor Silver-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} while others viewed the Emperor's decision to sign the Concordat as the only thing that allowed the Empire to survive the Great War.{{ref|name=SrHd|[[Skyrim:Hadvar|Hadvar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrDd|[[Skyrim:Delphine|Delphine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrLS|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#Lore|Loading Screens]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

In 4E 174, the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] took advantage of the Imperial Legion's preoccupation with the Great War by overthrowing [[Lore:Hrolfdir|Hrolfdir]], Jarl of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]], and declaring [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] to be an independent kingdom.{{ref|name=TBoM|{{Cite Book|The Bear of Markarth}}}}{{ref|name=SrJId|[[Skyrim:Igmund|Igmund]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|501}} The Reachmen ruled the Reach as an independent kingdom for two years until [[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]] and his Nord militia, who had been hired by Hrolfdir, drove the Reachman rebels from Markarth and retook the city in {{Year|4E 176}}.{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} The surviving Reachman rebels fled into the hills and continued to fight the Empire in the [[Lore:Forsworn Rebellion|Forsworn Rebellion]].{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=SrKd|[[Skyrim:Kibell|Kibell]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Hroldir allowed Ulfric and his militia to worship Talos in Markarth in return for retaking the city, which the Thalmor soon found out about.{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} The Thalmor sent an ambassador to the Imperial City to confront Titus II, where they demanded the arrests of Ulfric and his men for violating the terms of the White-Gold Concordat or risk the possibility of another war with the Dominion.{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}}{{ref|name=SrCed|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Faced with the threat of a second war, the Emperor was forced to send the Imperial Legion to arrest Ulfric and much of the Nord militia in an event called the Markarth Incident.{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} Following the Markarth Incident, Titus II had no choice but to suppress Talos worship by allowing the Thalmor Justiciars to enforce the terms of the treaty.{{ref|name=SRAlvd|[[Skyrim:Alvor|Alvor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===The Stormcloak Rebellion===
Soon after the Markarth Incident, Ulfric founded the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]], who  reviled Titus II for his perceived betrayal in the Markarth Incident.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|420, 552-553}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, Ulfric, now Jarl of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], killed the High King of Skyrim, [[Lore:Torygg|Torygg]], in single combat with the power of the [[Lore:Thu'um|Thu'um]].{{ref|name=SrLS}}{{ref|name=SRUSd|[[Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRSSd|[[Skyrim:Sybille Stentor|Sybille Stentor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Empire branded Ulfric a murderer and traitor,{{ref|name=SrHd}}{{ref|name=SRAlvd}}{{ref|name=SRGTd|[[Skyrim:General Tullius|General Tullius]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} while some Nords believed the duel was legal and true to the old ways of Skyrim,{{ref|name=SRUSd}}{{ref|name=SRRogd|[[Skyrim:Roggvir|Roggvir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRGerd|[[Skyrim:Gerdur|Gerdur]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} officially beginning the [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Stormcloak Rebellion]].

The western holds of [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]], the Reach, and [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath]] remained loyal to the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|261}}{{ref|name=THoS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} In the east, the [[Lore:Old Holds|Old Holds]] of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]], [[Lore:The Pale|the Pale]], and [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]] supported the Stormcloaks.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|261}}{{ref|name=THoS}}{{ref|name=SRLRd|[[Skyrim:Legate Rikke|Legate Rikke]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun]] was neutral, but Jarl [[Lore:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]] was more supportive of the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|532}}{{ref|name=SRLRd}}{{ref|name=SRJBtGd|[[Skyrim:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} After the rebellion broke out, a few months prior to Last Seed 4E 201, Titus II sent General [[Lore:Tullius|Tullius]] to Skyrim to serve as the province's military governor.{{ref|name=SrHd}}{{ref|name=SRGTd}} The Imperial Legion's efforts to quell the rebellion were hindered by the Emperor's unwillingness to send legionaries to Skyrim, as doing so would weaken Cyrodiil's defenses.{{ref|name=SRGTd}} Titus II canceled his trip to Skyrim to attend the wedding of his cousin, Vittoria Vici, when he realized his presence would necessitate a more direct role in the Stormcloak Rebellion.{{ref|name=SRAstd|[[Skyrim:Astrid|Astrid]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} On 15 Last Seed, 4E 201, the Emperor sent a letter to General Tullius, requesting that several Imperial relics be placed back into the service of the Empire and ordering him to recover the [[Lore:Lord's Mail|Lord's Mail]].{{ref|name=LtGT|{{Cite Book|Letter to General Tullius}}}}

===Assassination===
[[File:SR-npc-Emperor Titus Mede II.jpg|thumb|right|Titus Mede II aboard the Katariah]]
In 4E 201, [[Lore:Amaund Motierre|Amaund Motierre]], a member of the [[Lore:Elder Council|Elder Council]], performed the Black Sacrament to have the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] kill multiple targets in an elaborate plan to draw out and assassinate the Emperor.{{ref|name=SRTSHBB|Events of [[Skyrim:The Silence Has Been Broken|The Silence Has Been Broken]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{Ref|name=AMd|[[Skyrim:Amaund Motierre|Amaund Motierre]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=AMSL|{{Cite Book|Amaund Motierre's Sealed Letter|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} Motierre wanted to have the Emperor assassinated to usher in a change in Imperial policy.{{Ref|name=AMd}} To force Titus II to travel to Skyrim, the Brotherhood killed the first target, Vittoria Vici, at her wedding in [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]].{{ref|name=SRAstd}}{{ref|name=SRBUD|Events of [[Skyrim:Bound Until Death|Bound Until Death]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} With the Emperor soon to arrive in Skyrim, Commander Maro, the leader of the [[Lore:Penitus Oculatus|Penitus Oculatus]] in Skyrim, began preparing the security for the Emperor's visit.{{Ref|name=Gabrd|[[Skyrim:Gabriella|Gabriella]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=LfF|{{Cite Book|Letter from Father|ns_base=Skyrim}}}} The Brotherhood killed the second target, Commander Maro's son Gaius, and framed him in a plot to assassinate the Emperor, which they believed would render the Commander unable to perform his duties and weaken the Emperor's protection.{{Ref|name=Gabrd}}{{ref|name=SRBS|Events of [[Skyrim:Breaching Security|Breaching Security]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Brotherhood killed the third target, the renowned chef known as [[Lore:Balagog gro-Nolob|the Gourmet]], so an assassin could impersonate him and then poison Titus II's food.{{ref|name=SRRfD|Events of [[Skyrim:Recipe for Disaster|Recipe for Disaster]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} At the Emperor's Tower of Castle Dour, the assassin killed an individual who was believed to be Titus II, but he was actually a body double.{{ref|name=SRTKaE|Events of [[Skyrim:To Kill an Empire|To Kill an Empire]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Commander Maro used the body double to catch the assassin in the act and kill them.{{Ref|name=Marod|[[Skyrim:Commander Maro|Commander Maro]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Maro also had his agents attack the Dark Brotherhood sanctuary near [[Lore:Falkreath|Falkreath]], destroying it and killing most of the assassins.{{ref|name=SRDI|Events of [[Skyrim:Death Incarnate|Death Incarnate]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

The assassin who killed the Emperor's body double managed to survive, and they informed Motierre that they still intended to honor the contract. Motierre informed the assassin that Titus II was onboard his ship, the Katariah, in the Solitude Inlet, and once the assassin arrived, they made their way through the ship until they reached the Emperor's Quarters.{{ref|name=SRHS!|Events of [[Skyrim:Hail Sithis!|Hail Sithis!]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The assassin's presence did not surprise Titus II, who told them that he believed it was the Dark Brotherhood's destiny to assassinate him.{{ref|name=SRHS!}}{{Ref|name=ETMIId|[[Skyrim:Emperor Titus Mede II (real)|Emperor Titus Mede II]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} He only asked that the assassin honor his last request and kill the individual who ordered his assassination.{{ref|name=SRHS!}}{{Ref|name=ETMIId}} Titus II then turned around, and the assassin killed him.{{ref|name=SRHS!}} It is unknown if the assassin honored his final request.

Certain accounts assert that the destruction of the Dark Brotherhood occurred before it could assassinate Titus II.{{ref|name=SRDtDB!|Events of [[Skyrim:Destroy the Dark Brotherhood!|Destroy the Dark Brotherhood!]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
