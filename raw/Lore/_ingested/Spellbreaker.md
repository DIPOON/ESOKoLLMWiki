[[Lore:Spellbreaker|Spellbreaker]] (also spelled '''Spell Breaker''') is an ancient [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] stated to have been created on behalf of [[Lore:Rourken (person)|King Rourken]].{{Ref|name=ARTIL|[[Arena:Artifact Quests#Spell Breaker|Artifact Quest Dialogue]] in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]''}} Some believe it was forged by the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Peryite|Peryite]],{{ref|name=Mastrius|[[Morrowind:Mastrius|Mastrius]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|[[Castles:Spellbreaker|Spellbreaker]]'s description in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}} but regardless of this rumor's veracity, Spellbreaker has become associated with Peryite and is probably the best-known artifact associated with the Prince.{{ref|name=DFPeryite|[[Daggerfall:Peryite's Quest|Peryite's Quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} Superficially a [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwarven]] tower shield, it is one of the most ancient relics in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The shield not only protects its wielder from physical damage, but also from magical attacks by reflecting spells, curing the wielder of paralysis, dispelling curses, or silencing any mage about to cast a spell. It is said that the shield still searches for its original owner, and will not remain the property of anyone else for long.{{Ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
===First Era===
Spellbreaker played an important role in the historic Battle of [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]]–[[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]].{{Ref|name=TL}} [[Lore:Rourken (person)|King Rourken]] had the shield created for him in the conflict with Shalidor, but it was ultimately lost in the final battle.{{ref|name=ARTIL}} The high honor guard of Rourken also may have used gauntlets imbued with [[Lore:Tonal Architecture|tonal magics]] derived from Spellbreaker during this conflict.{{ref|name=ONRSG|[[Online:Rourken Steamguards|Rourken Steamguards]] description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
 
Sometime during the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Spellbreaker was granted to the [[Lore:Dragon Priest|Dragon Priest]] {{Lore Link|Zaan the Scalecaller}}. After being abandoned by her [[Lore:Dragon|Dragon]] lord, Zaan turned to Peryite — earning his favor and the shield — before being killed by her dissatisfied followers.

===Second Era===
Around {{Year|2E 582}}, Zaan was awoken from death by a cult of Peryite, with whom she cooperated to unleash a terrible plague on Tamriel. Zaan was defeated by members of the [[Lore:Undaunted|Undaunted]], but Spellbreaker was retaken by Peryite before they could claim it.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Plans of Pestilence|Plans of Pestilence]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living [[Lore:Dwemer|dwarf]], at one point befriended the sorcerer [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]].{{Ref|name=MWYB|[[Morrowind:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as ''[[Lore:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]'',{{ref|name=MWProphecies|{{Cite Book|The Morrowind Prophecies: Game of the Year Edition|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|241}} listing Spellbreaker among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,{{ref|name=TA|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Artifacts}}}} and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Spellbreaker and the other artifacts detailed in the book.{{Ref|name=MWFR|1=[https://cslist.uesp.net/index.php?game=mwfr&edid=Tamrielic_Lore&rectype=BOOK French version of] [[Morrowind:Tamrielic Lore|Tamrielic Lore]]}}
===Third Era===
By the time of the Imperial Simulacrum (circa {{Year|3E 389}}-{{Year|3E 399}}), the Battle of [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]]–[[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]] had become legendary, and rumors of Spellbreaker's supposed return to Tamriel were circulating throughout the Empire, though it was also rumored that the [[Lore:Necromancers|Necromancers]] were searching for the shield. The [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]] followed a rumor regarding its return to an anxious young man at a tavern, who was attempting to avoid agents of the Necromancers seeking him for his information. During the man's travels throughout Tamriel, he had come across information in an ancient text which indicated that a particular dungeon in [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]] was where a map leading to Spellbreaker's current location could be found. The man destroyed the text, but not before copying down the information, which he then relayed to the Eternal Champion. Traveling to Hammerfell, the Eternal Champion entered the dungeon and discovered the rumored map in its depths. This map led to another dungeon outside of Hammerfell, where the Eternal Champion successfully recovered Spellbreaker.{{Ref|name=ARTIL}}

In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] (circa {{Year|3E 405}}-{{Year|3E 417}}), an [[Lore:The Agent|agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] contacted Peryite in search of power. The agent reminded the Prince of a mortal from the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], and Peryite took a shine to the agent. He had the agent slay a [[Lore:Vampirism|Vampire Ancient]] who had displeased him, and in return he had one of his worshippers give the Spellbreaker to the agent.{{ref|name=DFPeryite}}

Mastrius, a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] vampire who had been imprisoned for centuries in the Salvel Ancestral Tomb near the [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] by [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], required the Spellbreaker to break the curse keeping him in his prison. In {{Year|3E 427}}, the weakened Mastrius was discovered by a fellow vampire who agreed to help free him. The Spellbreaker was found in the Dwemer ruin of [[Lore:Bthuand|Bthuand]], near the skeleton of its previous owner who had died in a cave-in. Mastrius used the shield as a conduit for his spell and broke Azura's curse, returning to full strength. He then betrayed his fellow vampire and was slain; his accomplice then claimed the Spellbreaker.{{ref|name=MWTIM|Events of [[Morrowind:The Imprisonment of Mastrius|The Imprisonment of Mastrius]] in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} Later that year, the shield came into the possession of [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]], and was put on display in her museum of artifacts in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=Tribunal|Events of [[Tribunal:The Museum|The Museum]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, worshippers of Peryite in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] attempted to become closer to the Prince by performing a risky ritual which resulted in trapping their souls in Peryite's realm of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], [[Lore:The Pits|the Pits]]. The [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] found the frozen bodies of the worshippers around Peryite's shrine by the [[Lore:Silverfish River|Silverfish River]]. The Prince spoke with the Hero, and requested that they enter Oblivion and retrieve his worshippers' souls. The Hero obliged, and was rewarded with the Spellbreaker.{{Ref|Events of the quest [[Oblivion:Peryite|Peryite]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

===Fourth Era===
The artifact would later come into the possession of two distinct individuals from the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]], both which went into hiding from the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] after the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]].{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Rise and Fall of the Blades}}}} One belonged to a party known as the [[Lore:Sheathed Blades|Sheathed Blades]], and they looted Spellbreaker sometime between {{Year|4E 176}} and {{Year|4E 201}} off the remains of an Ancient Flame Atronach they defeated in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]].{{ref|Ancient Flame Atronach [[SkyrimTAG:Legendary Encounters|legendary encounter]] reward in the ''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}} The second was the [[Lore:Hero|Warrior]], who is thought to have looted Spellbreaker around {{Year|4E 180}} from an extravagant chest.{{ref|[[Blades:Artifacts|Artifact]] acquisition methods in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] summoned Peryite and was tasked with killing the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] Orchendor, a monk of Peryite who had rebelled against the Prince. He dwelled in the Dwemer ruin of Bthardamz in Skyrim, along with many [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]] from [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] who had been cursed by Peryite with a terrible plague. The Dragonborn infiltrated the ruins, killing the afflicted Bretons before defeating Orchendor and sending his soul to the Pits. Peryite rewarded the Dragonborn with the Spellbreaker.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of [[Skyrim:The Only Cure|The Only Cure]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
