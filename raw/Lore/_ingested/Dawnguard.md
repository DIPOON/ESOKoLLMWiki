The [[Lore:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]] are a group of [[Lore:Vampire|Vampire]] hunters that originate from the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. They are based in '''Fort Dawnguard''', in the region of [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]], towards the provincial border in the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]]. It is found in the {{Lore Link|Dayspring Canyon}} of the Velothi Mountains.<noinclude>

==History==
===Second Era===
[[File:SR-concept-Dawnguard 02.jpg|200px|4th era Dawnguard member|right|thumb]]
The Dawnguard traces its origins to the early to mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{Ref|name=ESO|The description of [[Online:Fighters Guild#Active Abilities|Silver Bolts]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=Isran|[[Skyrim:Isran|Isran]]'s dialogue from [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}} Fort Dawnguard was once a stronghold owned by the Jarl of [[Lore:Riften|Riften]]. The Jarl's son was an adventurer, which proved to be his demise when he contracted [[Lore:Vampirism|vampirism]]. The Jarl of Riften, unwilling to have his son killed, used his money to build Fort Dawnguard as a prison to hold him. The hired men that guarded the prison would become the early Dawnguard. The Dawnguard was eventually forced to kill the Jarl's son, which led to the group's banishment from the Rift. However, the Dawnguard stuck to their cause of vampire hunting.{{Ref|name=Isran}} A silver and gold sword known as the {{Lore Link|Guardian's Claymore}} was bestowed on the first leader of the Dawnguard, and is renowned for its ability to block attacks.{{Ref|name=GC|[[Blades:Guardian's Claymore|Guardian's Claymore]] description in ''[[BL:Blades|Blades]]''}} The group would start to use {{Lore Link|crossbow}}s with [[Lore:Silver|silver]] bolts, a practice later adopted by the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{Ref|name=ESO}} The group would eventually dissolve and fall into obscurity. A rumor was that the group had all contracted vampirism and stalked the Rift until they were all defeated.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Sorine Jurard|Sorine Jurard]]'s dialogue from [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}}

Another story is that, far from being a noble group of vampire hunters, the early Dawnguard was a secret organization whose members would go on midnight raids to pillage houses. They stashed their loot in caches guarded by their sigils, which could only be opened by a sunfire spell. It's unknown how reliable this account is, as it comes from a priest of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] who claimed his god, who talked to him regularly, had told him some of it.{{ref|name=FN|[[Skyrim:Florentius Baenius|Florentius Baenius]]' dialogue during [[Skyrim:Bolstering the Ranks|Bolstering the Ranks]]}} Regardless, the Dawnguard did have treasure caches as described, so it is possible he was correct.

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-place-Fort Dawnguard.jpg|Fort Dawnguard in 4E 201|left|thumb]]
In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Vigilants of Stendarr|Vigilants of Stendarr]] had established a chapter in Skyrim, specifically in the region of [[Lore:The Pale|the Pale]]. One of their members, [[Lore:Isran|Isran]], decided to leave the group to reform the Dawnguard. Keeper {{Lore Link|Carcette}} had mocked Isran's desire to rebuild Fort Dawnguard, and that it was not worth the manpower to repair. Isran believed that the Vigilants were weak, and very unprepared for a real threat.{{Ref|name=Isran}} As a vampire threat had begun to emerge out of Skyrim, the word about the Dawnguard and their stronghold would spread across the province. At one point, the Vigilants of Stendarr's base of operations was wiped out by the {{Lore Link|Volkihar Clan}}, and a survivor of the group sought help from the Dawnguard.{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Dawnguard (quest)|Dawnguard]] quest in [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}} The Dawnguard's investigation of the vampiric threat, as well as, the {{Lore Link|Dimhollow Crypt}} had led them to the discovery of [[Lore:Serana|Serana]], a [[Lore:Daughter of Coldharbour|Daughter of Coldharbour]].{{Ref|[[Skyrim:Awakening|Awakening]] quest in [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}} What followed after was a conflict between the Dawnguard Vampire Hunters, and the Volkihar Clan, which would involve the prophecy known as the [[Lore:Tyranny of the Sun|Tyranny of the Sun]]. In the end, the prophecy was not fulfilled, and [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] was spared from danger. It's unknown if the Dawnguard would continue to operate in Skyrim, or if they were wiped out by the Volkihar Clan after the defeat of Lord [[Lore:Harkon|Harkon]].{{Ref|The following [[Skyrim:Dawnguard Quests#Main Quests|story quests]] in [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}}{{Ref|name=DtD| [[:Skyrim:Destroying the Dawnguard|Destroying the Dawnguard]] in [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Skyrim: Dawnguard]]}}
