[[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] (known also as the '''Immortal Plane''',{{ref|name=ONGirnalin|[[Online:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} the '''Aether''',{{ref|{{Cite Book|From Nirn to the Aether}}}} the '''[[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]]''',{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} the '''Spirit Worlds of Eternity''',{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and '''Heaven'''){{ref|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3AS}} is a [[Lore:Planes of Existence|plane]] of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] thought to be the origin of all [[Lore:Magic|magic]] and arcane arts.{{ref|name=MFTS|{{Cite Book|Magic from the Sky}}}}{{ref|name=Mysticism|{{Cite Book|Mysticism (book)}}}}{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} The [[Lore:Stars|stars]] and the [[Lore:Sun|sun]] are thought to be holes into Aetherius left by spirits (notably [[Lore:Magnus (god)|Magnus]]) who long ago became dissatisfied with [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{ref|name=MFTS}} It is a common belief that the [[Lore:Souls|soul]] of a dead person, assuming they are not [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]-[[Lore:Ghost|bound]] for some reason, goes to Aetherius, to continue on as a spirit,{{ref|name=TTM|{{Cite Book|The Talos Mistake}}}} where it is said many souls of mortals become spirits of another sort.{{ref|name=ONGirnalin|[[Online:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Materials known as [[Lore:Aetherial Fragments|Aetherial Fragments]] often become dislodged and fall to [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]; these are commonly observed by the people of Tamriel as 'shooting stars'.{{ref|name=MFTS}}

Aetherius, much like [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], consists of a multitude of realms which serve different purposes and is described as a bright sea containing many worlds "of everlasting imperfection", and as a place of "searing light" that contrasts the darkness of Oblivion.{{ref|name=PGE3AS|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Arena Supermundus}}}}{{ref|name=Sithis|{{Cite Book|Sithis (book)}}}}{{ref|name=ONConnalenwe|[[Online:Connalenwe|Connalenwe]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It is described as an infinite place, or a deep, dark, and endless sea, beyond the past and the present and the known world, where time is irrelevant and from where the [[Lore:Gods|gods]] originated.{{Ref|name=LOSouls|pg.237 of {{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|}}}}{{ref|name=ONGirnalin|[[Online:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Distributed Soul}}}} The [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]] of Aetherius are said to move, to "dance and sway". It is said that in primordial times [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]] sang to Aetherius, weaving songs so beautiful that the stars were compelled to dance and sway, continuing to wink and blink afterward in memory of that song as the realms of Aetherius which those stars are windows to likewise dance and sway to Y'ffre's music.{{ref|name=ONGirnalin|[[Online:Girnalin|Girnalin]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} For [[Lore:Nord|Nords]], their chosen place in Aetherius is [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]], the Hall of Valor.{{ref|name=SAR|{{Cite Book|Sovngarde, a Reexamination}}}} The [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] have a different place in Aetherius, called the [[Lore:Far Shores|Far Shores]],{{ref|name=TM}} as do the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]], a realm known as the [[Lore:Sands Behind the Stars|Sands Behind the Stars]].{{ref|name=TAT|{{Cite Book|Trail and Tide}}}} These locations are not necessarily exclusive to those particular races, but may depend on an individual's beliefs, choices during their lifetime and the manner of their death, including the possibility of servitude to the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] in Oblivion.{{ref|name=TACVAYQ|{{Cite Book|The Abbot Crassius Viria Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=TDOB|{{Cite Book|The Doors of Oblivion}}}}{{ref|name=TL|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Lore}}}} For instance, in {{Year|2E 582}} King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] was (temporarily) killed in the Chamber of Passage in the [[Lore:Hall of Heroes|Hall of Heroes]]; his soul was drawn through a nearby passage to the Far Shores, despite his being a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]].{{ref|name=Online|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Being worlds of the dead, certain [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]] of Aetherius like  Sovngarde have been described as places where the living shouldn't linger.{{ref|name=SRT|[[SR:Tsun|Tsun]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

The [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] are said to have originated in Aetherius,{{ref|name=ESOKB|''[[GEN:Elder Scrolls Online - Knowledge Base|Elder Scrolls Online - Knowledge Base]]''}}{{Ref|name=LOSouls|pg.237 of {{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|}}}} and are described as "Aetherius-aligned [[Lore:Gods|et'Ada"]],{{ref|name=TTGtC|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Thief Goes to Cyrodiil|ns_base=GEN}}}} having come into existence at the [[Lore:Beginning Place|Beginning Place]] alongside some of the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Princes]].{{ref|name=TM}}{{ref|name=TACVAYQ}} After [[Lore:Convention|Convention]], the Aedra, as well as varieties of uncontacted, minor Aedric spirits, are said to reside in Aetherius once again. Though minor Aedric spirits exist, they are rarely encountered since Magnus withdrew from the world at the moment of creation. This is said to be because interfering with Mundus is generally considered to be off-limits since the end of the ages of myth and, as a result, they tend to no longer respond to mortals directly{{ref|name=PGE3All|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}}{{Ref|name=Serana|[[SR:Serana|Serana]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOVairabrian|[[ON:Vairabrian|Vairabrian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=PoEAYQ|{{Cite Book|Phrastus of Elinhir Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=GU|{{Cite Book|Glorious Upheaval}}}} The [[Lore:Luminary|Luminaries]], immortal beings of pure magic are generally thought to bear some relation to Aetherius. They have been theorized to be fallen tears of Aetherius formed of its energy manifested in physical form, new lifeforms that arose naturally from the meeting of life and aetherial energy, "children" or creations of the Aedra that were sent as their proxies to herald their return and guide mortals and are to Aetherius what the Daedra are to Oblivion, aetheric constructs born from the imprinting of the subconscious eidolons of mortals upon purest magicka, a natural part of the cycle of magic that functions as the mechanism by which it is emanated to the mortal world akin to a lamp casting light or an aqueduct carrying water, Daedra in disguise, or ascended ancestor spirits, the souls of dead mortals that have taken on a new form.{{ref|name=Crow|[[Online:The Crow|The Crow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=LAS|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Scribing}}}}{{ref|name=ONUNOL|{{Cite Book|Ulfsild's Notes: The Origin of Luminaries}}}} 

The [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]],  who fled Mundus before Convention and created the stars, reside here.{{Ref|name=VBOH|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Vehk's Book of Hours|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Cosmology|ns_base=GEN}}}} The [[Lore:Mantellan Crux|Mantellan Crux]] is a self-contained plane that for a time was the resting place of the [[Lore:Mantella|Mantella]] after it was blasted into Aetherius by [[Lore:Zurin Arctus|Zurin Arctus]].

Powerful curses can sometimes cause part of Aetherius to overlay with Nirn, drawing spirits from that realm back into the world of the living.{{ref|name=ONAnde|[[Online:Andewen|Andewen]], [[Online:Meldil|Meldil]] and [[Online:Quaronaldil Duure|Quaronaldil Duure]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

According to the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Order of the Ancestor Moth]], mortal souls are the Aedric essence at the core of every mortal's existence.{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Distributed Soul}}}} Souls have also been described as the piece of every mortal which comes from Aetherius, the realm of pure light and magic, and a "tiny star" which flares into a "sun" when magic is invoked.{{Ref|name=LordSouls|pg. 52 {{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|}}}}

==Travel==
===Travel to Aetherius===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic Sunbird Feather.jpg|Prismatic Sunbird Feather|thumb|left]][[File:ON-mount-Silver Nimbus Wolf 02.jpg|A Silver Nimbus Wolf, a beast that helps usher into Aetherius|thumb|right]]
There are certain groups that managed to create vessels for Aetherial travel. During the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], a mythical order of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmeri]] explorers known as the [[Lore:Sun Birds of Alinor|Sun Birds of Alinor]] were said to have pierced the veil between [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] and Aetherius using the raw magic of the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]].{{ref|name=FurnSunbird|[[Online:Prismatic Sunbird Feather|Prismatic Sunbird Feather]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Their expeditions to Aetherius are said to be among the most famous attempts in history, however some scholars believe that actual proof of a successful journey is nonexistent.{{ref|name=FurnSunbird}} Another example is the [[Lore:Imperial Mananauts|Imperial Mananauts]] of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]], who undertook comporable expeditions to the Sun Birds,{{ref|name=PGE3AS}} during which they discovered and named the [[Lore:Slipstream Realm|Slipstream Realms]].{{ref|name=LFNHaTR|{{Cite Book|Lord Fa-Nuit-Hen and Tutor Riparius Answer Your Questions}}}} The [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] were rumored to have used vessels called "Aetherbells", that could "dive" through the realms of Aetherius and Oblivion.{{ref|name=FurnStarChart|[[Online:Stros M'Kai Antiquities|Dwemer Star Chart]] antiquity codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Some portals to Aetherius can be found on Nirn, the Chamber of Passage in the Hall of Heroes is one such gate leading to the Far Shores,{{ref|name=ONShores|[[Online:To Walk on Far Shores|To Walk on Far Shores]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} and the gate to Sovngarde in the ancient fane of [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]] is another.{{ref|name=SR|[[Skyrim:Sovngarde (quest)|Sovngarde]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

Travel to Aetherius is possible through magic, the renowned mystic [[Lore:Nulfaga|Nulfaga]] could transport others to the realm,{{ref|name=DFJourney|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Journey to Aetherius]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and certain life-force devouring [[Lore:Wraith|wraiths]] can transport the living to Aetherius in order for the dead to drain their essence.{{ref|name=ONThirster|[[Online:Soul Thirster|Soul Thirsters]] and [[Online:Baleful Ghost|Baleful Ghosts]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Certain magical beasts that walk Nirn are said to be able to help [[Lore:Ghost|spirits]] that linger in the mortal world reach Aetherius. Gathering up the souls of those who died violently, or couldn't move on from heartbreak, despair or other such causes and providing them with sanctuary until they are ready to be ushered into the afterlife.{{ref|name=SovngardeCrate|[[Online:Sovngarde Crate|Sovngarde Crate]]'s descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Spirits caught on the precipice between life and death might also have powerful wraiths sent after them to take them to Aetherius by force.{{ref|name=Netch|[[Online:The Netch|The Netch]]'dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=End|[[Online:Inevitable End|Inevitable End]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Travel from Aetherius===
[[File:ON-npc-Septima Tharn 02.jpg|An Ancestor Spirit returned from Aetherius to avenge a past grievance|thumb|left]][[File:ON-crown store-Sovngarde Stoutheart Bear.jpg|A Sovngarde spirit bear|thumb|right]]
The [[Lore:Ghost|spirits]] of the dead that reside in the afterlife are able to travel to other planes, being able to manifest both in realms of Oblivion or in the world of the living, Nirn, for a variety of reasons. Spirits have returned to the affairs of the living  to take vengeance on those who've wronged them,{{ref|name=ESOSchemes|[[Online:Ancestor Spirit|Ancestor Spirit]] and [[Online:Orcthane Berserker|Orcthane Berserker]]'s appearance in [[Online:Heart's Grief|Heart's Grief]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}, to bestow blessings to those who've aided them before departing again,{{ref|name=Ulfgar|[[Bloodmoon:Ulfgar the Unending|Ulfgar the Unending]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} to act on the behalf of a deity that they serve as emissaries,{{ref|name=Emissary|[[Online:The Emissary of Z'en|The Emissary of Z'en]]'s appearance and dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Mathragor|[[Online:Mathragor|Mathragor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} to give their approval for great deeds,{{ref|name=Ysgramor|[[Online:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Yngol|[[Online:Yngol|Yngol]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Ylgar|[[Online:Ylgar|Ylgar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or to impose trials on their living descendants. Some sources claim that all waterways are portals between the world of the living and the world of the dead, from which ancestor spirits can observe and cross into Nirn.{{ref|name=ONDomitia|[[Online:Domitia Nasica|Domitia Nasica]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Clever|{{Cite Book|Clever Kail-Perwa and the Great Boast}}}}{{ref|name=ONProconsul|[[Online:Proconsul Beloren-Kaie|Proconsul Beloren-Kaie]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Proving|{{Cite Book|The Proving Festival}}}}

During times of need in the mortal world, Aetherial planes such as Sovngarde might send the spirits of dead heroes back to the living world to provide aid, occasions on which those spirits are said to ride the spirits of beasts whose souls likewise reside in that plane. Spirit beasts have also been known to return individually in order to act as companion mounts to courageous living champions, or to serve as the eyes and ears of the afterlife within the living world.{{ref|name=SovngardeCrate|[[Online:Sovngarde Crate|Sovngarde Crate]]'s descriptions in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=SovngardeCat|[[Online:Sovngarde Sabre Cat Cub|Sovngarde Sabre Cat Cub]]'s description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Certain [[Lore:Thu'um|Shouts]] such as the Call of Valor are said to function by issuing a call for aid to Aetherial spirits, who respond to the call and "journey beyond time and space" to give their aid.{{ref|name=Call|[[Skyrim:Call of Valor|Call of Valor]]'s description in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]}} There exist magical runes which can call a spirit back into the living world from Aetherius, Archmage [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]] placed such runes on his tomb to call his soul back to Nirn during times when magic in Tamriel was under threat.{{ref|name=Shalidor|[[Online:Shalidor|Shalidor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Aetherial Influence==
[[File:SR-npc-Olaf One-Eye.jpg|Olaf One-Eye, a spirit with influence over the living world|thumb|right]]
There are cases where aetherial influence can affect the mortal world without planar travel taking place. The spirits of the dead are said to be able to exert influence over the living world, being able to grant great fortune or misfortune or commune through and alter the dreams of the living.{{ref|name=Clever|{{Cite Book|Clever Kail-Perwa and the Great Boast}}}}{{ref|name=Proving|{{Cite Book|The Proving Festival}}}} There have been cases of Aetherius-bound spirits reaching out to the living via altering their dreams and subsequently using said dreams as a medium to [[Lore:Time Magic|alter time]] in a form of ultimately fatal curse. The living target of the curse would initially experience strange dreams relating to the ghost's past, eventually realizing that they were being made to travel to different eras of history in their sleep when the people in the dream noticed the intruding presence and reacted to it, resulting in them being immediately moved to a different time upon being seen, and thus revealing the intent behind the magic. The experience would repeat nightly until burns started appearing on the dreamer's body, culminating in them magically burning to ash.{{ref|name=JournalOlaf|{{Cite Book|Solitude: A Charred Journal}}}} Some sources claim that exerting influence upon the living world in such a manner is easier for spirits which are well-honored by the living, as that reverence strengthens them.{{ref|name=Clever|{{Cite Book|Clever Kail-Perwa and the Great Boast}}}} 

Upon death, some of the accumulated experiences of mortal existence which the soul is vessel for, the [[Lore:Fjyron|Fjyrons]], the "will to peace", or memories of the dead,{{Ref|name=TA|[[Online:Treasures A#Ancestor-Silk Shawl|Ancestor-Silk Shawl]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} dissipate into the living world. This essence retains a connection to both the grand fabric of [[Lore:Aurbis|creation]] and to all aspects of the soul in all their [[Lore:Planes of Existence|destinations]]. Thus, because all aspects of a soul remain connected, tapping into the Fjyrons allows one who knows how to receive the erudition of the ancestors. Knowledge and guidance from beyond mortality, from the existence  beyond the present and the past and the known world, where time is irrelevant, allowing one to glimpse the cosmic tapestry and its threads and representing a connection to the Aedra and the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls" through the experiences of every mortal who has ever lived. Because of this some groups such as the [[Lore:Cult of the Ancestor Moth|Cult of the Ancestor Moth]] make use of this connection to receive the wisdom of the ancestral spirits in the afterlife, using it to read and interpret the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]].{{Ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Distributed Soul}}}}{{Ref|name=POTM|{{Cite Book|Pension of the Ancestor Moth}}}} This rite can be dangerous, as it is suggested to cause "aetherial residue" to build up in the body, which needs to be cleansed lest it deaden the body parts it is in contact with.{{ref|[[Online:Northern Elsweyr Antiquities|Moth Priest's Cleansing Bowl]] codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=LAESMP|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Elder Scrolls & Moth Priests}}}}

The sun is said to represent the connection between Aetherius and the Mundus, and to be the conduit through which the influence of certain Aedra such as [[Lore:Auri-El|Auri-El]] reaches the mortal world.{{ref|name=DGVyrthur|[[SR:Arch-Curate Vyrthur|Vyrthur]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=DGSerana|[[SR:Serana|Serana]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
