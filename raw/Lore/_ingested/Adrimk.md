[[Lore:Adrimk|Adrimk]] was an [[Lore:Atmora|Atmoran]] warrior and a member of the original [[Lore:Five Hundred Companions|Five Hundred Companions]]. Adrimk was one of many ancient heroes recounted in the ''[[Lore:Songs of the Return|Songs of the Return]]''. She was known for her wisdom and remembered as the '''war-teacher'''.{{Ref|name=SOTR|{{Cite Book|Songs of the Return, Vol 2}}}}<noinclude>{{NewLeft}}

==History==
[[File:ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg|thumb|right|Ylgar, the captain of Darumzu]]
Little is known about Adrimk's life, but she taught many of Atmora's greatest fighters, including the legendary sisters [[Lore:Froa|Froa]] and [[Lore:Grosta|Grosta]], to whom she first taught the art of dancing among the blades.{{ref|name=SOTR}} The sisters were renowned for creating the [[Lore:Woeful Bowstring|Woeful Bowstring]] for the [[Lore:Long-Launcher|Long-Launcher]],{{Ref|name=FAHM|{{Cite Book|Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus}}}} a bow of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]], which served him well in his many hunts and battles.{{Ref|name=TOOTO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}}{{Ref|name=EGT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}}

When [[Lore:Ylgar|Ylgar]], the son of Ysgramor set forth to the academies of honored soldiers, seeking his most trusted friends and advisors to join him on the venture of [[Lore:The Return|the Return]], she joined the crew of [[Lore:Darumzu|Darumzu]]. Initially, Ysgramor called upon the great sisters Froa and Grosta, who in turn invited her to join. Adrimk, in turn, mustered all the students under her command. At that time, many of her students were not yet known, but some would later become famous in their own right, including Hermeskr (Who Threw His Shield), Urlach (Who Breathed Fire), Ramth the Greater, Merkyllian Ramth, and the Far-Sighted Uche, who would see the first of many dawns. Together, they manned Darumzu, one of the grand Atmoran ships, and successfully landed on the mainland of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=SOTR}}{{NewLeft}}
