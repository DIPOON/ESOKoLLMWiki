[[Lore:Mara|Mara]], also known as '''Mother Mara''',{{Ref|name=APAYQ|{{Cite Book|Artorius Ponticus Answers Your Questions}}}}{{Ref|name=ATG|{{Cite Book|Ark'ay The God}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/Skyrim}}}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive: Murkmire Q&A Part 2}}}} '''Mother Mild''',{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Guide to Bravil}}}} and the '''Divine Mother''',{{Ref|name=APAYQ}} is the '''Goddess of Love, Fertility, Agriculture,{{Ref|name=VOFN|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Nords}}}} and Compassion'''.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Loading Screens#The Divines|Loading screen]] in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} She is also acclaimed as the '''Mother-Goddess''',{{Ref|name=VOFE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} and is one of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Talos Mistake}}}}

In the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], Mara was originally worshipped simply as a fertility goddess,{{Ref|name=VOFB|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Bretons}}}} but the sphere of the Divine later extended to agriculture and love. Nir, the female principle of the cosmos credited as the mother of creation, is occasionally associated with Mara as a result of her role as the goddess of fertility and agriculture.{{Ref|name=VOFN}} As a result, priests of Mara credit her with giving birth to creation.{{ref|name=Maramal|[[Skyrim:Maramal|Maramal]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Mara is considered a near universal deity as the Divine is featured in the pantheons of all the races of the continent of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] save the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]].{{Ref|name=VOFE}}<noinclude>

==Mythology==
[[File:OB-Window-Mara.jpg|left|thumb|150px|Mara]][[File:ON-quest-The Oldest Orc 02.jpg|thumb|right|Nuralanya and Gurlak reuniting in afterlife by the grace of Mara]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none|''The Goddess Mara recognized their true love and wept at their loss. Not having power over death, she could do nothing to save Shandar, but she knew that she could not let their love die. She reached down from the heavens and picked up Mara and Shandar in her arms, and placed them high in the heavens. They could be together always, and provide light in the dark night to others so that they may be safe from the evils in the world.''|''[[Lore:Mara's Tear|Mara's Tear]]''{{Ref|name=MT}}}}

According to her clergy, it was Mara who first gave birth to Creation. She then pledged to watch over mortals as her children. They also believe mortals learned to love each other from her love for all of them.{{ref|name=Maramal|[[Skyrim:Maramal|Maramal]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}
According to the creation myth presented in the ''[[Lore:The Annotated Anuad|Anuad]]'', Mara and the [[Lore:Aedra|Aedra]] (gods) were born from the mingled blood of [[Lore:Anu|Anu]] and [[Lore:Padomay|Padomay]], the good and evil primal forces, respectively, and therefore have a capacity for both good and evil, in contrast to the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]], who were born from the blood of Padomay, and thus are only evil.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} 

The creation myth of the Khajiit acclaims Mara as the Mother Cat and describes the Divine as the daughter of Ahnurr (Anu) and Fadomai (Padomay).{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi}}}} 

[[File:SR-quest-The Book of Love 07.jpg|thumb|right|Fenrig and Ruki reuniting in afterlife thanks to the actions of Agent of Mara]]

The formation of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], the Dragon God of Time, from the mingled blood of the brothers Anu and Padomay facilitated the formation of Mara and the gods as they learnt to structure themselves.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} ''Shezarr's Song'', a Cyrodilic creation myth, acclaims the creation of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]] to the sacrifice of Mara and the gods whose sacrifices were embodied as eponymous planets.{{Ref|name=AAD|{{Cite Book|Aedra and Daedra}}}}{{ref|name=Orrery|[[:File:OB-interior-Orrery (labeled).jpg|The Orrery]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} 

Mara and the gods were subsequently bound to the Earth Bones as a result.{{Ref|name=AAD}} The moons of [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]] are claimed to be the embodiment of the lovers, a mortal woman named Mara and [[Lore:Shandar|Shandar]], respectively, as created by the Divine.{{Ref|name=MT|{{Cite Book|Mara's Tear}}}} When [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]] created his daughter [[Lore:Londa-Vera|Londa-Vera]], he drew inspiration from other divinities and bestowed upon her the smile of Mara.{{Ref|name=TNC|{{Cite Book|The Nine Coruscations}}}}

Different cultures attribute different relationships between Mara and the other gods. The [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] of Skyrim consider Mara a handmaiden of [[Lore:Kynareth|Kyne]] and concubine of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Shor]] (Lorkhan).{{Ref|name=VOFN}} However, the Bretons, Altmer, and Bosmer consider Mara to be the wife of Akatosh or Auriel to the Elves.{{Ref|name=VOFB}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Wood Elves}}}}{{Ref|name=VOFA|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The High Elves}}}} Other faiths acclaim Mara as the wife of Lorkhan or concubine of Akatosh, or, in some cases, concubine of both.{{Ref|name=VOFE}} In ''Ark'ay The God'', Mymophonus the scribe claims that Mara deified Arkay, the God of Birth and Death, so to allow him to teach mortals the necessity of life and death.{{Ref|name=ATG}} In the First Era, the Divine punished [[Lore:Sai|Sai]], God of Luck, and deprived him of his body for his failure to uphold his pact with [[Lore:Reymon Ebonarm|Ebonarm]], God of War, to spread luck throughout Tamriel in the gods' absence.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|King Edward, Part X}}}} The [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], also known as the Miracle of Peace, in 3E 417 is acclaimed as the work of Mara, Akatosh, and Stendarr.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}} It is unknown if Mara successfully destroyed the [[Lore:Skull of Corruption|Skull of Corruption]], a daedric artifact, in 4E 201.{{Ref|name=WN|Events of [[Skyrim:Waking Nightmare|Waking Nightmare]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

On occasions, she would act through intermediaries to ensure that the spirits of the deceased would be united together also in death.{{ref|name=TOL}} There are also documented cases of Mara intervening more directly, enabling the departed to briefly return as ghosts to share final moments with their loved ones and guide them beyond the mortal realm. This intervention extended even to situations involving individuals from different races with distinct beliefs about the afterlife. Notably, Mara facilitated the reunion of Nuralanya, a [[Lore:Corelanya Clan|Corelanya]] [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], and an [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] named Gurlak in the afterlife.{{ref|name=Nuralanya|[[ON:Nuralanya|Nuralanya]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TOO|Events of [[ON:The Oldest Orc|The Oldest Orc]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The clergy of Mara supported this belief, teaching that even couples from different backgrounds, such as an Altmer and a [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]], would not enter death alone.{{Ref|name=LAHDMM|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Heart's Day & Mother Mara}}}}

==Teachings==
[[File:LG-cardart-Amulet of Mara.png|Amulet of Mara|left|thumb]][[File:ON-crown store-Pledge of Mara.jpg|A shrine to Mara with [[Lore:Rings of Mara|rings of Mara]]|right|thumb]]
As Goddess of Love, Mara is the bearer of love,{{ref|name=Dinya|[[Skyrim:Dinya Balu|Dinya Balu]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} and shines the light of love on all mortals.{{ref|name=Briehl|[[Skyrim:Briehl|Briehl]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}} Mara gave life to mortals so that they may love.{{Ref|name=Heart}} The gift of love was bestowed upon mortals by the Divine so to honor Mara,{{Ref|name=Heart|{{Cite Book|Heart's Day Mass Wedding}}}} and allow them to understand the true nature of the Divines.{{ref|name=Dinya}} Mara teaches that love provides nourishment and life to all mortals.{{ref|name=Dinya}} The Divine commands mortals to "live soberly and peacefully. Honor your parents, and preserve the peace and security of home and family".{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ten Commands: Nine Divines}}}} Mara's Benevolence is eternal and those who offer their love to the Divine will never be forsaken.{{Ref|name=TWM|[[Skyrim:The Warmth of Mara|The Warmth of Mara]]}} The Warmth of Mara shapes the destiny of mortals and can be felt across Tamriel.{{Ref|name=TWM}} Priests of Mara teach that the Divine is omnipresent and her love and affection can be found in the heart of all mortals, thus linking them to Mara.{{Ref|name=THOL|{{Cite Book|The Heart of Love}}}} The holy truth declares that love creates life and therefore those who love create more life.{{Ref|name=Heart}}

Mara is also the patron of [[Lore:Marriage|marriage]],{{ref|name=Dinya}} and the act of union is carried out at her shrines and temples.{{Ref|name=ROM|[[ON:Ritual of Mara|Ritual of Mara]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TBOM|Events of [[Skyrim:The Bonds of Matrimony|The Bonds of Matrimony]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The Divine teaches that the spark of union between two mortals comes from within and is not based on appearance.{{ref|name=Dinya}} The oaths and vows of marriage create life from love and are respected in the name of Mara.{{Ref|name=Heart}} The act of union is confirmed upon taking the Pledge of Mara and Mara does not place any restrictions on marriage, thus all may marry, irrespective of gender and race, and unite their souls in the holy union.{{Ref|name=APAYQ}} Mara makes her will known to her priests through visions,{{ref|name=Erandur|[[Skyrim:Erandur|Erandur]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and commands them to aid those whose love needs guidance.{{ref|name=TOL|[[Skyrim:The Book of Love|The Book of Love]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The priesthood of Mara strive to unite all mortals and are intolerant of those who are intolerant of others.{{Ref|name=DFBenevolence}} Mara's Prayer from the Doomed is a prayer recited in desperate situations.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}}

==Worship==
[[File:ON-render-Heart's Week Companions.jpg|thumb|right|Heart's Week celebrations in Daggerfall]]
[[Lore:Heart's Day|Heart's Day]] is a holiday celebrated throughout Tamriel on the 16th of [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]] and serves as the central day of the week-long Heart’s Week festival, honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers.{{ref|name=ONRL|{{Cite Book|Reigniting Love for Contemporary Audiences}}}}{{ref|name=EventAnnouncement|[[General:Celebrate Comradery & Companionship during the Heart's Week Event!‎‎|Celebrate Comradery & Companionship during the Heart's Week Event!‎‎]]}}{{ref|name=DF|Heart's Day [[Daggerfall:Holidays|description]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to [[Lore:Sanguine|Sanguine]], the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] of Debauchery.{{ref|name=DF}}{{ref|name=LATH|{{Cite book|Loremaster's Archive - Tamriel's Holidays}}}} As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions.{{Ref|name=LATH}} Since then, it has been predominantly associated with Mara.{{Ref|name=MPP|{{Item Link|Mara's Piquant Parcel|itemid=223680|quality=4}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=BHD|[[Blades:Heart's Day|Heart's Day]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}}{{Ref|name=CLAL|[[Castles:Love and Lullabies|Love and Lullabies]] in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}

===Worship in Black Marsh===
The majority of the population of the province of Black Marsh venerate the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]], and, in the Second Era, worship among the Argonians of Black Marsh of Mara and the gods was limited to a small minority of those who had assimilated to the culture of the Empire.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Argonians}}}} Imperial missionary efforts in the province in the Third Era were unsuccessful as missionaries died from local diseases, such as at Umpholo, where a Temple of Mara was constructed.{{Ref|name=Argonian|{{Cite Book|The Argonian Account, Book 4}}}} Mara is known to have been worshipped as a member of the pantheon of the [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]], the indigenous humans of Black Marsh, and was considered one of their three "mothers in the Around-Us", as well as [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]] and [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]].{{Ref|name=TWGIT|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Water-getting Girl and the Inverse Tiger|ns_base=General}}}}

===Worship in Cyrodiil===
[[File:GEN-misc-Bravil.jpg|Map of Bravil depicting Mara (top left)|thumb|left]][[File:BL-misc-Mara.png|Imperial depiction of Mara|right|thumb|200px]]
The Imperials of the province of Cyrodiil venerate Mara and the Divine is considered part of the Cyrodilic pantheon.{{Ref|name=VOFE}} A Benevolence of Mara, a cathedral dedicated to the Divine, was located in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], the capital of the province, in the late [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], under the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]].{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Morning Star (v1)}}}} In the 5th century of the Second Era, the Imperial City was also home to a Yokudan chapel in the Market District which contained a shrine to Morwha shaped like a beehive.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 6: Redguard Style}}}} A Temple of Mara, and later Great Chapel of Mara, is known to have been located at Bravil in the Second and Third Eras, respectively.{{ref|name=DOTN|{{Cite Book|Daughter of the Niben}}}}{{ref|name=POTS|Events of [[ON:Prisoners of the Sphinx|Prisoners of the Sphinx]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=GCOM|[[Oblivion:Great Chapel of Mara|Great Chapel of Mara]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} In the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], wayshrines dedicated to the Divine can be found throughout the countryside.{{ref|name=WAY|[[Oblivion:Wayshrines|Wayshrines]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} At this time, the office of Archbishop of Mara is attested.{{Ref|name=Argonian}} A wayshrine of Mara northwest of the city of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] was destroyed during the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]] in 3E 433.{{ref|name=WAY}}{{ref|name=OBDS|Events of [[Oblivion:Dagon Shrine|Dagon Shrine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} In the same year, the Great Chapel of Mara at Bravil was desecrated by [[Lore:Auroran|Aurorans]], daedra in the service of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]], and several of its priests were killed.{{ref|name=KOTNNF|Events of [[Oblivion:Nature's Fury|Nature's Fury]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}{{Ref|name=GCOM}}

Although [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]] followed the Daedra, the Aedra of Elven pantheon were still widely revered, with probably a majority of the Ayleids continuing to pay them homage, but cults devoted to the various Daedric Princes sprang up across Cyrodiil, tolerated and then celebrated.{{Ref|name=DWA|{{Cite Book|Daedra Worship: The Ayleids}}}}

===Worship in Hammerfell===
The worship of Mara in the province of Hammerfell is divided between the Crowns, who represent the conservatives, and Forebears, who represent the cosmopolitan Redguards.{{Ref|name=Crowns|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, Crown Redguards}}}} Mara is known to both Crowns and Forebears as [[Lore:Morwha|Morwha]], a fertility goddess, however, the names Morwha and Mara are used interchangeably among the Forebears.{{Ref|name=Forebears|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith, The Forebears}}}} Morwha is considered the favorite wife of Akatosh or Ruptga, to the Forebears and Crowns, respectively.{{Ref|name=Forebears}} The goddess is always depicted with four arms so that she may "grab more husbands".{{Ref|name=Crowns}} Mara is worshipped throughout Hammerfell, including the Island of [[Lore:Stros M'Kai|Stros M'Kai]],{{Ref|name=Forebears}} and is the patron deity of the regions of [[Lore:Antiphyllos|Antiphyllos]],{{ref|name=Antiphyllos|[[Daggerfall:Antiphyllos|Antiphyllos]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Ayasofya|Ayasofya]],{{ref|name=Ayasofya|[[Daggerfall:Ayasofya|Ayasofya]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[Lore:Ephesus|Ephesus]],{{ref|name=Ephesus|[[Daggerfall:Ephesus|Ephesus]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and [[Lore:Ykalon|Ykalon]].{{ref|name=Ykalon|[[Daggerfall:Ykalon|Ykalon]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} In the late Third Era, similarly to High Rock, the Benevolence of Mara was responsible for the administration of temples dedicated to Mara and the Maran Knights was also active in the province at this time.{{Ref|name=DFBenevolence}}{{Ref|name=Maran Knights}} A Conservatorium of Morwha was located in the city of [[Lore:Hegathe|Hegathe]] in the late First Era.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|2920, Sun's Height (v7)}}}}

===Worship in High Rock===
[[File:DF-sign-Benevolence of Mara.png|Symbols of the Benevolences of Mara in the Iliac Bay region|right|thumb]]
Mara, a member of the pantheon of the [[Lore:Breton|Bretons]],{{Ref|name=VOFE}} is the patron deity of several regions of High Rock, such as [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]], [[Lore:Betony|Betony]], [[Lore:Glenumbra Moors|Glenumbra Moors]], and [[Lore:Northmoor|Northmoor]].{{ref|Patron deities of [[Daggerfall:Anticlere|Anticlere]], [[Daggerfall:Betony|Betony]], [[Daggerfall:Glenumbra Moors|Glenumbra Moors]] and [[Daggerfall:Northmoor|Northmoor]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} It is said that villages which have clement weather year-round are protected by the Mother Goddess.{{ref|name=TPTheBlueDawn|group=UOL|[[General:Ted Peterson|Ted Peterson]]'s posts in [[General:Loranna's RP/The Blue Dawn|''The Blue Dawn'']]}} In Anticlere, the 21st of Last Seed was known as [[Daggerfall:Holidays#Last Seed|Appreciation Day]], an ancient holiday of thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. It was considered a holy and contemplative day devoted to Mara, the goddess-protector of the region.{{ref|name=DFAppreciation|[[Daggerfall:Holidays|Holidays]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

Nedic sculptures depicted coupled idols of an Elven Mara embracing a Mannish Lorkhan, seen as early evidence of Aldmeri culture being welcomed with open arms by the [[Lore:Manmeri|Manmer]].{{ref|name=Manmer|[[Online:Glenumbra Antiquities#Manmer Coupled Idol|Manmer Coupled Idol]] codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the late Third Era, temples dedicated to the Divine were administered by the [[Lore:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]], a religious organization dedicated to Mara and her teachings, and could be found in major urban centers throughout the province. The Benevolence of Mara was led by a patriarch and was allied to the [[Lore:Temple of Stendarr|Temple of Stendarr]], a religious organization dedicated to [[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]] and his teachings.{{ref|name=DFBenevolence|Faction information for the [[Daggerfall:Benevolence of Mara|Benevolence of Mara]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}} The [[Lore:Maran Knights|Maran Knights]] were a controversial knightly order dedicated to Mara and the protection of her temples. Some within the priesthood of Mara called for the disbandment of the order, while others argued the order was necessary to create peace.{{ref|name=Maran Knights|Faction information for the [[Daggerfall:Maran Knights|Maran Knights]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

===Worship in Morrowind===
[[File:MW-banner-Mara.png|Banner of Mara in Morrowind|right|thumb|100px]]
The Dunmer of Morrowind largely neglect worship of Mara and the Divines, however, in the late Third Era, worship of Mara and the Divines was practiced by a small minority of Dunmer on the Island of Vvardenfell. Worship of the Divine in Vvardenfell in the late Third Era was largely confined to its non-Dunmer population who prayed at shrines operated by the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]], a missionary group, which combined worship of the Divines and provided services for all the Divines at shrines found at Imperial forts.{{ref|name=ICMOR|[[Morrowind:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]]'s presence and members in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} A shrine to Mara and the Divines was also located in the Royal Palace of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] during this time.{{ref|name=TRRP|[[Tribunal:Royal Palace#Imperial Cult Services|Imperial Cult Shrine]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} Following the collapse of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] in 3E 427, worshippers of Mara and the Divines formed the majority of [[Lore:House Hlaalu|Hlaalu]] and Vvardenfell districts.{{ref|{{Cite Book|A Life of Uriel Septim VII}}}} In the Second Era, during the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]], shrines of Mara were located in the cities of [[Lore:Davon's Watch|Davon's Watch]] and Mournhold.{{ref|name=ROM}}

===Worship in the Reach===
[[File:ON-creature-Mara (wolf).jpg|A direwolf of the Shrine of the Hunt-Father, named after Mara|left|thumb]][[File:ON-statue-Reachfolk Mara Altar.jpg|An altar bearing Mara's seal in a sacred Reachman site|right|thumb|150px]]
During the Interregnum, it was customary to decorate the steets of Markarth for the celebrations of the [[Lore:Heart's Week|Heart's Week]]. The decoartions included banners, blue floral patterns and hanging Amulets of Mara, along with pink and red flowers associated with Dibella.{{Ref|name=MarkarthMara|Appearance of [[Online:Markarth|Markarth]] during [[ON:Hearts Week|Hearts Week]]}} Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, two [[Lore:Reachman|Reachfolk]] clans, [[Lore:Eagleseer Clan|Eagleseers]] and [[Lore:Six-Fords|Six-Fords]] were to hold a handfasting upon an altar of Mara at [[Lore:Hroldan Ring|Hroldan Ring]] to unite the two clans.{{ref|name=ONVAO|[[Online:Vows and Oaths|Vows and Oaths]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} One of the direwolves kept in the [[Lore:Shrine of the Hunt-Father|Shrine of the Hunt-Father]]'s grounds was named after Mara.{{ref|name=Mara|[[ON:Mara (wolf)|Mara]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Markarth (DLC)|Markarth]]''}} Some Reacholk marriage traditions share similarities to Mara worshipping Bretons and [[Lore:Snow Elves|Snow Elves]].{{Ref|name=TW|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|The Wedding}}}} 

After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Mara, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} As of {{Year|4E 201}} several Reachfolk accept marriage under Mara.{{ref|name=TBOM}} Spirits of [[Lore:Briarheart|Briarhearts]], whose original hearts were defiled are unable to pass to the afterlife,{{ref|name=BWN|{{Cite Book|Beldama Witch's Note|ns_base=SR}}}}{{ref|name=COTHD|{{Cite Book|Crypt of the Heart - Draft}}}} unless the original heart of the deceased Briarheart is purified at the shrine of Mara and properly buried.{{ref|name=SKCOTH|Events of [[Skyrim:Crypt of the Heart|Crypt of the Heart]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Worship in Skyrim===
[[File:SR-concept-Wolf Mural.jpg|Mara as the Wolf carved in stone|right|thumb]][[File:LG-cardart-Priest of Mara.png|A priestess of Mara in Skyrim|left|thumb]]
The Divine is also worshipped as a member of the Nordic pantheon.{{Ref|name=VOFE}} The Sacred Wolf was one of the most revered Atmoran animal totems, associated by later Nords with the goddess Mara,{{ref|name=Frostbane Wolf|[[ON:Frostbane Wolf|Frostbane Wolf]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} who was known as '''Mother Wolf'''.{{Ref|name=TWASC|[[ON:Contraband T#Taxidermied Wolf and Suckling Cubs|Taxidermied Wolf and Suckling Cubs]]}}{{Ref|name=OM|[[ON:Old Mjolen|Old Mjolen]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In the Second Era, Mara was associated with the [[Lore:Ice Wolf|Ice Wolves]] that inhabited the mouth of the [[Lore:White River|White River]] in the northeast of the province.{{ref|name=White River Ice Wolf Pup|[[ON:White River Ice Wolf Pup|White River Ice Wolf Pup]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Wooden figures of Mara depict a naked woman partially clothed in wolf pelts who bears fangs of her own, similar to Atmoran stone carvings.{{Ref|name=WMF|[[ON:Treasures W#Wolf Mara Figure|Wolf Mara Figure]] description}}

Worship of Mara in the province of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] was widespread in the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]] and shrines dedicated to the Divine could be found both in the wilderness and in urban centers, such as at the Temple of the Divines in [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]],{{ref|name=SRTOTD|[[Skyrim:Temple of the Divines|Temple of the Divines]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} and at the Temple of Mara in [[Lore:Riften|Riften]].{{ref|name=SRTOM|[[Skyrim:Temple of Mara|Temple of Mara]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Shrines dedicated to Mara usually have flower-spouting fertility statuettes.{{Ref|name=MFS|[[ON:Treasures M#Mara Fertility Fetish|Mara Fertility Fetish]] description}} A pond in the hold of Eastmarch was named after Mara.{{ref|name=MEP|[[Skyrim:Mara's Eye Pond|Mara's Eye Pond]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Some argued the return of the dragons in {{Year|4E 201}} was a sign from Mara signaling her displeasure with the people of Skyrim.{{ref|name=Maramal|[[Skyrim:Maramal|Maramal]]'s dialogue in [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']]}}

To Nordic traditionalists, Mara takes a lesser role in the pantheon, considered to be Kyne's handmaiden, and one of Shor's concubines.{{Ref|name=VOFN}}{{Ref|name=TTWA|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|The Tenpenny Winter...Again|ns_base=General}}}} Her worship tends to be more emphasized in areas with a large Imperial influence. Amulets of Mara are frequently worn to indicate that someone is looking to marry, as life is hard and full of hardship in Skyrim courtships tend to be very short. Marriages are often held in Riften's Temple of Mara.{{ref|name=Maramal}}

===Worship in the Summerset Isles===
[[File:LO-people-Mara.jpg|Altmeri depiction of Mara|right|thumb|150px]]
Mara is worshipped in the High Elven pantheon.{{Ref|name=VOFA}} An Altmer legend tells that Nenamil, the Archon of Song, once embarked on a pilgrimage to see Mara's beauty in person, believing that the greatest love he could feel was love for the mother of creation. His journey eventually took him to what is now known as [[Lore:Archon's Grove|Archon's Grove]], where he found a flower of surpassing beauty, the so-called Rose-of-Archon. Upon seeing the flower, he realized that Mara was more than just a being: she was beauty incarnate, found in every exquisite thing. From then on, Nenamil would preach Mara's beauty and the Rose-of-Archon would honor and represent her. Roses-of-Archon are collected by the [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] for the Divine Reverence to Mara, a ceremony that lasts for at least fifty-eight days.{{Ref|[[Online:Justiciar Tanorian#A Rose's Beauty|Justiciar Tanorian]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

==Artifacts==
[[File:ON-icon-quest-Chalice of Mara.png|right|thumb|Chalice of Mara]]
During the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], a Temple of Mara located in the town of [[Lore:Bravil|Bravil]], Cyrodiil, was home to several holy relics of Mara, including a chalice, phylactery, dagger, and circlet. The Chalice of Mara was alleged to have been a wedding gift from Mara to St. [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]], founder of the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], at her wedding to [[Lore:Morihaus|Morihaus]].{{ref|name=ONTMA|[[Online:Marcella Ammianus|Marcella Ammianus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, the Sphinxmoth Bandits, a group of bandits operating from the neighboring [[Lore:Fort Sphinxmoth|Fort Sphinxmoth]], looted the temple of its valuables, including the relics. The bandits were defeated by the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] and the relics were subsequently returned to the temple.{{ref|name=ONAffront|[[Online:An Affront to Mara|An Affront to Mara]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In {{Year|3E 427}}, on the [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Island of Vvardenfell]], the Divine appeared before the Nerevarine as an apparition in the form of a Breton woman named '''Ama Nin''' and gifted the Nerevarine her blouse and skirt.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Boots of the Apostle|Boots of the Apostle]] quest in [[Morrowind:Morrowind|''Morrowind'']]}} Some claim the [[Lore:Everflow Ewer|Everflow Ewer]] was created by Mara.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Knightfall}}}}

Of the [[Lore:Crusader's Relics|Crusader's Relics]], it is unknown whether Mara created the Cuirass or Greaves of the Crusader. The Crusader's Relics are a set of artifacts created by the Divines in the early First Era for [[Lore:Pelinal Whitestrake|Pelinal Whitestrake]], the Divine Crusader, to allow him to defeat and banish [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]], an [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] Sorcerer King. Regardless, Pelinal, despite his victory over Umaril, was slain and the Crusader's Relics were scattered and lost for thousands of years.{{Ref|name=KOTNBook|{{Cite Book|The Knights of the Nine}}}} The Cuirass of the Crusader was held by the Wyrm of Elynglenn in Cyrodiil until recovered by Sir Amiel Lannus, a member of the chivalric order the Knights of the Nine, in 3E 111.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Sir Amiel's Journal}}}} The cuirass was stored in the undercroft of the Priory of the Nine, where it lay until recovered by the Hero of Kvatch in 3E 433.{{ref|name=KOTNPOTN|Events of [[Oblivion:Priory of the Nine (quest)|Priory of the Nine]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Greaves of the Crusader were recovered by Sir Berich Vlindrel and were buried with him in the [[Lore:Vlindrel|Vlindrel]] family tomb, but was later recovered by Sir Roderic of Wayrest and his squire Lathon, who gifted the greaves to the Hero of Kvatch.{{ref|name=KOTNTFS|Events of [[OB:The Faithful Squire|The Faithful Squire]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
