[[Lore:Attribution's Share|Attribution's Share]] is the plane of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] created and ruled over by [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]],{{Ref|name=AdventureGame|''[[SkyrimTAG:Skyrim - The Adventure Game|Skyrim - The Adventure Game]]''}}{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Survival Guide to Tamriel|ns_base=Books}}}} the [[Lore:Daedric Princes|Daedric Prince]] of Deceit.{{Ref|name=Boethia dialogue|[[Oblivion:Boethia|Boethia]]'s dialogue in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Realm of Boethia is described as a country of labyrinthine policy and betrayals, with maze gardens and twisted towers. It was formerly known as '''Snake Mount'''.{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Imperial Census of Daedra Lords|ns_base=GEN}}}}{{Ref|name=AYP|group=UOL|[[General:Andrew Young's Posts|Andrew Young's Posts]]}}<noinclude> 

One realm under the Princes dominion, called '''Boethia Stadium''',{{Ref|name=TESIVOOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}} consists of stormy skies, volcanic islands and lava seas, similar to the [[Lore:Deadlands|Deadlands]].{{ref|name=RoB|Appearance of the [[Oblivion:Realm of Boethia|Realm of Boethia]] in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} Although usually avoided by mortals, Boethia uses this particular realm to host prestigious arena tournaments so that mortals can earn her favor. The most well-known of these is the {{Lore Link|Tournament of Ten Bloods}}. Champions from each of the ten major [[Lore:Races|races]] of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] are taken to the realm, where they battle one-on-one to the death. The victor earns the Prince's favor, the title of Chosen One, and a suitably martial prize, such as the [[Lore:Goldbrand|Goldbrand]].{{ref|name=OBBoethia|[[Oblivion:Boethia|Boethia]] quest in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

==History==
[[File:ON-place-The Champion's Arena.jpg|thumb|left|The Champion's Arena (ESO)]][[File:OB-place-Boethia's Realm Statue.jpg|thumb|right|A statue of Boethia within the realm (Oblivion)]]

In {{Year|2E 582}}, followers of Boethia repurposed ancient [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]ic ruins near the city of [[Lore:Dragonstar|Dragonstar]], naming it the [[Lore:Dragonstar Arena|Dragonstar Arena]]. Combatants that entered the arena would pass through Daedric portals as they completed each round, until facing Boethia's current champion in the Champion's Arena, a staging area located within her realm.{{Ref|name=DRA|Events of [[ON:Dragonstar Arena|Dragonstar Arena]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Circa {{Year|3E 433}}, Boethiah opened a portal to Boethia Stadium to allow the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] to compete in the Tournament of Ten Bloods.{{Ref|name=OBBoethia}} 

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the [[Lore:Sheathed Blades|Sheathed Blades]] entered a portal to Attribution's Share within the [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]] to chase Oviron Salith, the leader of a Dunmer cult of Boethiah that were promoting dissent and violence throughout Skyrim. For hundreds of years they hunted Salith through the realm, and he was seemingly lost forever. The Blades died dozens of times, but somehow came back to life again and again, dying in grisly ways such as being swallowed whole by nightmarish creatures.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}}

After centuries in Attribution's Share, Boethiah gave them a chance to win their freedom and the heroes were sent back to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] in 4E 201, and they are reborn, rebuilt, and recreated from their ashes with amnesia. The only task required of them is to help the Daedric Prince in her eternal fight against [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]. Frustrated by their slow pace, Boethiah returned Oviron Salith to Tamriel to act as competition. When the Blades saw their old nemesis emerge, their memories of their time in Oblivion washed upon them.{{Ref|name=AdventureGame}}
