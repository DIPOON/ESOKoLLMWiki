# Lore:Kothringi

The [[Lore:Kothringi|Kothringi]]  (or '''Lustrous Folk'''){{ref|name=AVAYQ|{{Cite Book|Augustine Viliane Answers Your Questions}}}} are a silver-skinned{{Ref|name=MS2920|{{Cite Book|2920, Morning Star (v1)}}}} tribal people indigenous to [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] who are believed to be descendants of the [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]].{{Ref|name=ONLod|[[ON:Lodyna Arethi|Lodyna Arethi]]'s dialogue during [[ON:King of Dust|King of Dust]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Once considered the only humans native to [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], they are now known to have shared the continent with many other tribal groups.{{ref|name=ONLVR|[[Online:Lost Valley Redoubt|Lost Valley Redoubt]]'s loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} The race was believed to have been driven to extinction by the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]] in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=PGE1Wild|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}}{{FMI}}<noinclude> 
{{NewRight}}
==History==
[[File:ON-place-Zuuk.jpg|thumb|left|Zuuk]][[File:ON-crown store-Stillrise Ritecaller.jpg|thumb|right|Stillrise Ritecallers]]
The presence of the Kothringi in Black Marsh predates the most ancient records. They are believed to descend from the Nedic people.{{Ref|name=ONLod}} 
According to some sources, Kothringi culture started in the early [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], or even before that time.{{ref|name=NOS}}{{ref|name=ESOAN}} In the ancient past Kothringi and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] of the [[Lore:Hissmir|Hissmir]] warred against each other. Argonians feared that Kothringi could use the power of Zaht stones against them. Little is known of the outcome of this conflict, but Hissmir remained an Argonian settlement.{{ref|name=TTB|{{Cite Book|The True Balance}}}}{{ref|name=Awas|[[ON:Awas|Awas]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
 
The Kothringi were thought to have been avid sailors, sailing as far as [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], possibly spreading the Knahaten Flu of {{Year|2E 560}} to [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]] and nearby provinces by ship. Kothringi shipbuilding is said to be very idiosyncratic with one example having unusual paddle placement, shallow draft, and a sail. Some vessels were capable of navigating both swamps and coastal waters due to this.{{ref|{{Item Link|Kothringi Tidal Canoe|id=175729}} codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

The Kothringi were thought to be exterminated by the Knahaten Flu,{{ref|name=PGE1Wild}} although some of the infected fled on the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu#Kothringi and the Crimson Ship|Crimson Ship]] westward, away from Tamriel, never to be seen again.{{ref|name=TDC|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}} However, legends tell of those who supposedly saw the ship and never lived to tell the tale.{{ref|name=PotA|{{Cite Book|Pirates of the Abecean}}}}

Despite the widespread belief of the race's extinction, several Kothringi survived the Flu through unnatural means. In {{Year|2E 561}}, the [[Lore:Chieftain Suhlak|chief]] of [[Lore:Stillrise Village|Stillrise Village]], a Kothringi settlement in [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]], entered into a desperate pact with [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]] in order to survive the disease.{{ref|name=CSTTO|[[ON:Chieftain Suhlak|Chieftain Suhlak]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Thin Ones|The Thin Ones]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] kept his end of the bargain, but did so by transforming the populace into immortal, undead [[Lore:Skeleton|skeletons]].{{ref|name=CSTTO}} The villagers maintained appearances through illusion magic, but their secret was discovered in {{Year|2E 582}} when the [[Lore:Order of the Black Worm|Order of the Black Worm]] attempted to enslave the undead inhabitants.{{ref|name=CSTTO}}{{ref|name=TTO|Events of [[ON:The Thin Ones|The Thin Ones]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The villagers successfully repelled the necromancers, but infighting broke out, and it is unknown if they chose to destroy the curse or continue living as skeletons.{{ref|name=TBE|Events of [[ON:The Bargain's End|The Bargain's End]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} In the nearby town of [[Lore:Zuuk (place)|Zuuk]], a lone Kothringi man named [[Lore:Drillk|Drillk]] became immune to the Flu by absorbing the power of a [[Lore:Keystones|keystone]].{{ref|name=ESOMorilye|[[ON:Morilye|Morilye]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Into the Temple|Into the Temple]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESODrillk|[[ON:Drillk|Drillk]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Into the Temple|Into the Temple]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} He became a person of interest to the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] in 2E 582, when both sides sought to acquire the keystone.{{ref|name=KOD|Events of [[ON:King of Dust|King of Dust]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} An agent of the Ebonheart Pact rescued Drillk from the Dominion invaders, but the Kothringi revealed that taking the keystone's power would kill him.{{ref|name=ESODrillk}} It is unknown if the Pact soldier accepted his keystone or not.{{ref|name=ITT|Events of [[ON:Into the Temple|Into the Temple]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Some Kothringi are known to have survived the events of Stillrise Village by serving the [[Lore:Ideal Masters|Ideal Masters]].{{ref|name=SR|[[Online:Stillrise Ritecaller#Costumes|Stillrise Ritecaller]] description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} 

Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, a member of the [[Lore:University of Gwylim|University of Gwylim]]'s Antiquarian Circle tracked down and discovered pieces of a magical Kothringi hunter's garment, putting it back together.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltItem|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

==Society and Culture==
[[File:ON-furnishing-Z'en Idol.jpg|thumb|right|Kothringi idol dedicated to Z'en]]
[[File:ON-item-Harpooner's Wading Kilt.jpg|thumb|left|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]
Little detail is known about the Kothringi. They generally preferred to go naked, although a few wore clothes when outside their native land.{{Ref|name=MS2920}} They appear to have worshipped a god named [[Lore:Z'en|Z'en]]&mdash;a God of Toil&mdash;{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} as well as [[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]],{{ref|name=TGTG|{{Cite Book|Traveler's Guide to Gideon}}}}{{ref|name=AVAYQ}} [[Lore:Mara|Mara]], and [[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]].{{Ref|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|The Water-getting Girl and the Inverse Tiger}}}} It was believed that at some point of the history Kothringi venerated [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], before shifting to Z'en worship.{{ref|name=ESOAN|[[ON:Shadowfen Antiquities#Silver Wish Medallion|Silver Wish Medallion]] antiquity codex text}}
 
[[Lore:Music|Music]] was very important in Kothringi society. They were known to make instruments out of a variety of different materials, such as turning hollow logs into [[Lore:Wamasu|wamasu-sized]] [[Lore:Music#Drums|drums]], plucking sinew from [[Lore:Cliff Strider|cliff striders]] to make low humming [[Lore:Music#Harps|bow harps]] and most notable of all, their [[Lore:Music#Bells And Chimes|chimes]] known to be the Kothringi's most loved instruments. A Kothringi by the name of Baelah molded hundreds of chimes from [[Lore:Tin|tin]] and [[Lore:Copper|copper]], hanging them from branches and striking them for their sounds. The Kothringi sometimes invited the [[Lore:Saxhleel|Saxhleel]] for feasts and celebrations where they played their instruments for the Argonian people.{{ref|name=COS|{{Cite Book|Chimes of Silver}}}}{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} It is believed that cultural exchange between Kothringi and Argonians took place.{{ref|name=NOS|[[ON:Nest of Shadows|Nest of Shadows]] antiquity codex description}}
Circa {{Year|2E 582}}, the [[Lore:Bards College|Bards College]] in [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]] came into possession of a Kothringi bugle created from the horn of a [[Lore:Swamp Leviathan|leviathan]].{{ref|name=KLH|{{Cite Book|Kothringi Leviathan Horn}}}} Around the same time, the [[Lore:Thieves Guild|Thieves Guild]] also stole a sacred Kothringi shovel, believed to be a blessed instrument of Z'en, from [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]].{{ref|name=KHL|[[ON:Kari's Hit List|Kari's Hit List]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Item Link|Shaman Chirah's Devotion Shovel|id=73782}} item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Kothringi Humming Dolls were toys that featured a clever arrangement of air bladders and reeds inside and hummed when squeezed.{{Ref|name=ContrabandKoth|[[ON:Contraband K#Kothringi Humming Ball|Kothringi Humming Ball]] itm description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Some considered the Kothringi peerless crafters,{{ref|name=ESOWadingKilt|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} despite their distaste for clothing. They also occasionally crafted pieces of armor.{{ref|{{Item Link|Kothringi Plate Pauldrons|quality=2|id=7440}}}} They might've been capable of difficult crafting feats such as weaving strands of metal into cord in order to create stronger nautical rope.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Kothringi spellcraft is considered unusual to the point that an experienced mage may miss its presence. Some Kothringi magic would possibly heighten the target's senses or reflexes.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Kothringi utilized [[Lore:Throwing Weapons|throwing knives]], axes and harpoons in combat.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex}}{{ref|name=TTB}}

Portable Kothringi abacuses employed varnished sphere-scarabs as slide-beads.{{ref|name=ContrbandK1|[[ON:Contraband K#Kothringi Abacus from Zuuk|Kothringi Abacus from Zuuk]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Kothringi artisans perfected a technique for weaving reeds of different colors and textures into decorative mats.{{ref|name=ContrbandK2|[[ON:Contraband K#Kothringi Six-Reed Mat|Kothringi Six-Reed Mat]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Dream catchers crafted by Kothringi could be found around Tamriel during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]].{{ref|name=ContrbandK3|[[ON:Contraband K#Kothringi Dream Catcher|Kothringi Dream Catcher]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Kothringi folklore mentions "geels-ha butcher eels", massive river predators capable of swallowing [[Lore:Haj Mota|haj mota]] whole. They would obtain bog-blue jaspers from these eels to use in fetishes.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Kothringi knights made up a large part of the imperial garrison of [[Lore:Gideon|Gideon]], and Empress [[Lore:Tavia|Tavia]], wife of [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]], the last ruler of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]], had a Kothringi knight named [[Lore:Zuuk (person)|Zuuk]] with whom she conspired to assassinate her husband.{{Ref|name=MS2920}} After Reman III was assassinated, "Lord" Zuuk returned to Shadowfen and founded a town which was eventually named after him.{{ref|name=TFOZ|{{Cite Book|The Founding of Zuuk}}}} 
The Kothringi buried their dead in cairns, often adorning these burial sites with glass beads. Some glass beads were also worn, serving as wearable instruments.{{ref|name=ESOWadingKiltCodex|[[ON:Harpooner's Wading Kilt|Harpooner's Wading Kilt]]'s codex entries in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Some residents of Gideon claim that Kothringi spirits linger in the town.{{ref|name=ESODexion|[[ON:Dexion Asellus|Dexion Asellus]]' dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Fortune hunters sometimes poked around Gideon, searching for lost Kothringi treasure.{{ref|name=ESOMadarah|[[ON:Madarah|Madarah]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Kothringi utilized [[Lore:moonstone|moonstone]] in their artwork and handicrafts, such as their statues.{{Ref|name=ContrabandMoonstone|[[ON:Contraband M#Moonstone Kothringi Maiden|Moonstone Kothringi Maiden]]'s item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}
