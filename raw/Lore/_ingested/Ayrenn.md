[[Lore:Ayrenn|Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri]]{{ref|name=TCCAG}}{{Ref|name=RazJourn}} (also known as '''Queen Ayrenn the First''' and known by titles such as the '''Eagle Primarch of the Aldmeri Dominion''' and '''Monarch of the Throne of Alinor'''){{Ref|name=RazJourn|{{Cite Book|Razum-dar's Journal, Chapter 1}}}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|An End to Isolation}}}} was the High Queen of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], the Queen of [[Lore:Kingdom of Alinor|Alinor]], and one of the major faction leaders of the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] during the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].{{ref|name=TCCAG|[[General:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion game guide]] on the official {{g|on}} website}} She founded the first [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] during the Second Era, in the midst of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], which she formed by allying with the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] and [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]. Her adventures prior to her ascension opened her eyes to the corruption brewing at the very heart of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. She competed against High King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] of the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] and [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] of the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] for the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]]. She sought to conquer [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] to usher in a new age of Elven rule across Tamriel to protect it from the reckless tendencies of the younger races, particularly those of [[Lore:Men|men]], who she saw as shortsighted due to their brief lives.{{ref|name=TCCAG}}{{ref|name=WTNAI|{{Cite Book|Welcome to New Aldmeri Irregulars}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:ON-load-Alinor Royal Palace.jpg|Alinor Royal Palace|thumb|left]]
===Early Life===
Ayrenn was born on the 5th of Second Seed in {{Year|2E 555}} to King [[Lore:Hidellith|Hidellith]] and Kinlady [[Lore:Tuinden|Tuinden]], and all of the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]] reportedly celebrated for fifty-five days. She was nimble and quick-witted in her youth, and would often disappear for days at a time. In {{Year|2E 573}}, amid many signs and portents, she disappeared, and her brother [[Lore:Naemon|Naemon]] was eventually named heir to the throne.{{ref|name=ATUQ|{{Cite Book|Ayrenn – The Unforeseen Queen}}}} She spent some time in the Royal Menagerie of [[Lore:Sil-Var-Woad|Sil-Var-Woad]], where she spent many days of her childhood with her pet [[Lore:Indrik|indrik]] Lotus.{{Ref|name=ONTalomar|[[Online:Talomar|Talomar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Adventurer Queen===
In truth, Ayrenn had decided to travel through Tamriel, seeking adventure and life lessons that cannot be taught through schools.{{ref|name=TCCAG}} The first of Ayrenn's travels took her to the Isle of Balfiera, where she trained with the Direnni in the art of war. She became a seasoned blade-dancer and she partook in studies of the arcane arts.{{ref|name=TRoQA|{{Cite Book|The Rise of Queen Ayrenn}}}}

There are many stories about how Ayrenn spent the intervening years, which Ayrenn herself states were greatly exaggerated. She did confirm stories involving her riding a bear and her time with [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] pirates who taught her [[Lore:Weather Magic|weather magic]] to be true.{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn|[[Online:Queen Ayrenn|Queen Ayrenn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Some of these tales involve her hunting [[Lore:Frost Troll|frost trolls]] in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], exploring [[Lore:Dwemer Ruins|Dwemer]] ruins, and dancing with [[Lore:Nereid|Nereids]] in the [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]]. In Cyrodiil, she crewed with a pirate captain.{{ref|name=TRoQA}} However, when she visited the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]], Ayrenn suffered personally at the hands of [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]. This encounter would be one of the reasons for she would push for the Aldmeri Dominion to enter the Three Banners War, with her belief being that [[Lore:Men|humans]] have proven time and time again that they are bloodthirsty and are not fit to rule.{{ref|name=AaWT|[[General:The Elder Scrolls Online: Alliances at War|The Elder Scrolls Online: Alliances at War]]}}{{ref|name=ESOLSP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Lawrence Schick's Posts|ns_base=General}} (01/15/16)}}{{ref|name=TCCAG}}

Ayrenn laid some groundwork before she attempted to go back to Summerset and reclaim her throne through her [[Lore:Eyes of the Queen|Eyes of the Queen]], which was originally an underground agency working to secure her rise to power for many years before her coronation.{{ref|name=Cariel|[[ON:Cariel|Cariel]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They were later legitimized with Ayrenn's rise to power and functioned as her personal guard and intelligence organization.{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn}} She met [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]] during her time adventuring the mainland, either in [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] or [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]]. The story involved a drunken schoolmarm and a purple velvet dress.{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn}} She, however, recruited him as the first among her Eyes of the Queen,{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn}} on a day where an incident involving "a certain High Elf noble seeking adventure, three bottles of whiskey, and a small fire" convinced a young Raz to leave home and go on an adventure. Never particularly fond of small-town life, Raz took the first opportunity he had to leave. Having to keep his future role as an Eye of the Queen secret, Raz chose not to tell his family that the young elf was the future Queen Ayrenn, and his mother assumed he left to chase after another pretty face.{{Ref|name=ONRaz|[[ON:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Unforeseen Queen===
[[File:ON-icon-Aldmeri Dominion.png|left|thumb|The sigil of the First Aldmeri Dominion]]
{{Quote Box|''I have no hatred for the races of Man, but they are young. Like all children, they are driven by emotion. They lack the wisdom that comes with age.  I would sooner place an Altmer infant on the Ruby Throne than surrender Tamriel to their capricious whims.<br>The Altmer, the Bosmer and the Khajiit share the common traits of intelligence, patience and reason.  We do not seek riches or plunder. Domination is not our goal, nor is the acclamation of power for its own sake.<br>Today we make our stand. Today we take back the Ruby Throne, which is ours by ancient right and the blessings of the Divines.<br>Stand with us.''|Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri}}
When her father died in {{Year|2E 580}}, she surfaced in {{Lore Link|Velyn Harbor}} to the surprise of all and returned to formally claim her birthright, becoming Queen on the 7th of Frostfall (hence her nickname, the "Unforeseen Queen"). That year, the [[Lore:Elden Accord|Elden Accord]] was signed by Queen Ayrenn, [[Lore:Aeradan Camoran|King Aeradan Camoran]] of the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]], and [[Lore:Gharesh-ri|Lord Gharesh-ri]] of the [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr Confederacy]], which resulted in the formation of the first Aldmeri Dominion in {{year|2E 580}}.{{Ref|name=ONAyrenn}}{{ref|name=Shazah|[[ON:Shazah|Shazah]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}} The Bosmer joined Ayrenn's cause thanks to the Dominion facilitating Aeradan's claim to the throne of Valenwood by stopping the Blacksap Rebellion with the massacre in [[Lore:Cormount|Cormount]], orchestrated by the Jade Butcher, General [[Lore:Endare|Endare]].{{Ref|name=ESOGelthior|[[Online:Gelthior|Gelthior]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TMaC|{{Cite Book|The Massacre at Cormount}}}} The [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] Joined the Dominion out of respect and gratitude for the many elves who had rendered aid during the worst years of the [[Lore:Knahaten Flu|Knahaten Flu]].{{ref|name=TEatC|{{Cite Book|The Eagle and the Cat}}}}{{ref|name=IttLoTESO|{{Cite Book|Introduction to the Lore of The Elder Scrolls Online|ns_base=Online}}}}

Queen Ayrenn expanded the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]]'s role to become the executive arm of the alliance, and it incorporated representatives from all three races in the Dominion's territory, in ratios appropriate to their administrative abilities. The Thalmor Inner Council served as Ayrenn's personal advisors, and included such notables as King Aeradan Camoran and Lord Gharesh-ri, as well as a number of other Bosmeri and Khajiiti nobles who were included largely as figureheads.{{ref|name=AOSAYQ|{{Cite Book|Aicantar of Shimmerene Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=Parwinel|Dialogue of [[Online:Officer Parwinel|Officer Parwinel]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|group=UOL|[[General:ESO Writing Team AUA|ESO Writing Team r/elderscrollsonline Ask Us Anything]]}}

The [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] would later come into conflict with the Daggerfall Covenant and the [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]], with their competing ideologies setting them on a course for war. This conflict began in earnest sometime in {{Year|2E 580}}. Tensions between the alliances were further exacerbated by the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]] and subsequent military aggression of the Empire of Cyrodiil under Empress Regent [[Lore:Clivia Tharn|Clivia Tharn]].{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Chorrol Crier}}}} Each alliance saw the opportunity to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime by capturing the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] and installing their own emperor on the [[Lore:Ruby Throne|Ruby Throne]], but also had to deal with the other two alliances.{{ref|name=barracks|{{Cite Book|Posted outside barracks at Windhelm}}}}

===Conflict within Dominion Borders===
[[File:LG-cardart-Ayrenn 02.jpg|Ayrenn|thumb|left]]
[[File:LO-misc-Life in the Eagle's Shadow.jpg|Ayrenn on horseback|thumb|right]]
[[File:LG-cardart-Wish.jpg|Ayrenn praying|thumb|right]]
Altmeri nationalism and xenophobia culminated in the rise of the [[Lore:Veiled Heritance|Veiled Heritance]], in response to the rise of Queen Ayrenn and the Aldmeri Dominion. Vehemently against the young queen and her Bosmer and Khajiit allies, they sought to depose her and appoint their own monarch, the Veiled Queen, [[Lore:Estre|High Kinlady Estre]] of [[Lore:Errinorne|Errinorne]]. The Heritance consisted mainly of disenfranchised veterans and youths. Many more traditional Altmer despised the Dominion due to their feelings of racial superiority and disliked Ayrenn due to her unorthodox wanderings as a youth, and as such the Heritance enjoyed widespread support.{{ref|name=LitES|{{Cite Book|Life in the Eagle's Shadow}}}}{{ref|name=WDtV|{{Cite Book|Why Don the Veil?}}}} However, Estre's plot was stopped and she was slain at [[Lore:Firsthold|Firsthold]]{{ref|name=SAT|[[ON:Sever All Ties|Sever All Ties]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Queen Ayrenn subsequently traveled to Elden Root to begin her ratification ceremony, which would divine her capacity to rule as Empress. But in order to go forward with the process, the ceremony needed three items of importance, a "divine spark," an Ayleid source of power, and a skilled operator. The Vestige traveled to separate corners of the region, to the west, they traveled to the {{Lore Link|Falinesti Winter Site}}, where they acquired the "divine spark," {{Lore Link|Rajhin's Mantle}}. To the northeast, they traveled to the {{Lore Link|Reliquary of Stars}}, where they would locate the {{Lore Link|Heart of Anumaril}}, which originally brought life to the Orrery. Finally, they traveled to the southwest, to the town of [[Lore:Southpoint|Southpoint]], where the Conservator, {{Lore Link|Daraneth}} was located.{{Ref|name=ONRule|[[Online:Fit to Rule|Fit to Rule]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} With every component in hand, the ratification ceremony started as scheduled, but before the Queen would enter the Orrery, Naemon entered first and was transformed into an [[Lore:Ogrim|Ogrim]]. He was slain by the Vestige and despite the death of her brother, Ayrenn continued with the ceremony and received a vision of the Imperial City and Tamriel, at peace under the Aldmeri Dominion.{{Ref|name=ONOrrery|[[Online:The Orrery of Elden Root|The Orrery of Elden Root]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Ayrenn's rule had solidified further, but when the [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]] was stolen from [[Lore:Marbruk|Marbruk]], she sent the Vestige to locate it.{{Ref|name=ONMagnus|[[Online:The Staff of Magnus|The Staff of Magnus]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It had been stolen by [[Lore:Pelidil|Vicereeve Pelidil]] for the [[Lore:Veiled Heritance|Veiled Heritance]], who had resurrected the recently deceased Prince Naemon as a [[Lore:lich|lich]].{{ref|name=ESOVRP|[[Online:Vicereeve Pelidil|Vicereeve Pelidil]] dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Naemon's shade took the staff to [[Lore:Hectahame|Hectahame]], an [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] ruin where the [[Lore:Heart of Valenwood|Heart of Valenwood]] was kept, and began the process of using the staff to corrupt the Heart. He also used it to subdue ancient wards protecting the ruins from [[Lore:necromancy|necromancy]], which had been put in place long ago after a failed attempt to raise an army of undead there. The Heritance hoped to kill the entire Valenwood by corrupting the Heart, turning it into an evil realm known as [[Lore:Shadow Wood|Shadow Wood]]. However, the Dominion were successful in infiltrating the ruins and killing the lich-lord, and the staff was returned to Queen Ayrenn.{{ref|name=ESOSH|[[Online:Striking at the Heart|Striking at the Heart]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

With Ayrenn and other important figures watching at the [[Lore:Two Moons Path (temple)|Two Moons Path]] temple, the Vestige and Lunar Champion walked the last path—the [[Lore:Two Moons Path|Two Moons Path]], where they would face [[Lore:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]], and the sister would become the Mane. They traveled to the Plane of Jode and witnessed a possible future which would result in the fall of the Aldmeri Dominion, should they fail in their quest.{{Ref|[[Online:The Moonlit Path|The Moonlit Path]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The pair persevered and made it to the last leg of their journey. At the Den of Lorkhaj, they encountered both Javad, and the other Lunar Champion whom was possessed by the Dark Mane. Tharn planned for his tainted Lunar Champion to walk the Path to become the Mane, as they represent the soul of the Khajiiti people, and would then ensure that the Khajiiti people would be plunged into darkness.{{ref|name=ESOJavad|[[Online:Javad Tharn|Javad Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} In the end, the Mane and the Moon Hallowed had prevailed, and the battle against Javad Tharn was over. The other Lunar Champion that was tainted sacrificed herself and was set free by this, and thus was able to reach [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]]. After their victory, at [[Lore:Dune|Dune]], [[Lore:Rid-Thar-ri'Datta|Rid-Thar-ri'Datta]] gave his blessing for the new Mane of Elsweyr, and the Dominion was truly unified.{{Ref|name=ESOLorkhaj|[[Online:The Den of Lorkhaj|The Den of Lorkhaj]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

While Ayrenn was occupied by matters elsewhere, her cousin [[Lore:Alwinarwe|Alwinarwe]] took the title of 'Proxy Queen' and sat on the Throne of Alinor, governing Summerset in Ayrenn's stead.{{ref|name=ARPLS|[[ON:Alinor Royal Palace|Alinor Royal Palace]] loading screen}} When Ayrenn decreed Summerset's borders open to trade and travel,{{ref|name=VS|{{Cite Book|Visit Summerset|ns_base=ON}}}} she faced opposition from Summerset's nobility,{{ref|name=ARoOB|{{Cite Book|A Rejection of Open Borders}}}} although this sentiment wasn't shared by all.{{ref|name=ACfOB|{{Cite Book|A Case for Open Borders}}}}

===A Chance for Peace===
Ayrenn, Emeric, and Jorunn were subsequently targeted by the [[Lore:Ascendant Order|Ascendant Order]], a pan-Tamrielic movement turned terrorist organization. The leaders of the Ascendant Order were secretly nobles, utilizing disgruntled commoners to fulfill their goals. The Ascendant Magus was [[Lore:Damard Dufort|Count Damard]] of House Dufort, while the Ascendant Lord was [[Lore:Bacaro Volorus|Lord Bacaro]] of House Volorus from Cyrodiil. Bacaro's partner and children were killed during the conflict of the [[Lore:Colovian Revolt|Colovian Revolt]], and when the Three Banners War came around, he had created the Society of the Steadfast to serve as a humanitarian organization.{{Ref|name=SoST|{{Cite Book|The Society of the Steadfast}}}} Lord Bacaro later settled in his holdings on the Systres and overtime, built goodwill through his Steadfast Society. He then planned to leverage his reputation to lure the major players responsible for the Three Banners War to their deaths with peace talks hosted on High Isle.{{ref|[[Online:The Ascendant Storm|The Ascendant Storm]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}}{{ref|name=ESODR|[[ON:Druid Ryvana|Druid Ryvana]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} 
[[File:ESO2022CinematicTeaser.jpg|thumb|left|The Ascendant Lord watches as Three Banners Alliance ships sink]]
The Ascendant Order tampered with the invitation medallions that the Society of the Steadfast had intended for the peace talks. The medallions were intercepted, and the curses they contained were dispelled.{{ref|[[Online:Ascending Doubt|Ascending Doubt]] and [[ON:A King's Retreat|A King's Retreat]] quests in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} The ships carrying the leaders of the Three Banners War sailed to [[Lore:Gonfalon Bay|Gonfalon Bay]] for the peace summit on High Isle, but as they approached the region from the north, their ships were caught in a brutal, albeit uncontrollable storm conjured by the Ascendant Magus. These ships were subsequently wrecked on [[Lore:Loch Abhain|Loch Abhain]] in High Isle and while their captains were apprehended by the Ascendant Order, the alliance leaders, Queen Ayrenn, King Emeric, and Prince [[Lore:Irnskar|Irnskar]] were lost to sea and washed ashore on [[Lore:Amenos|Amenos]]' north coast.{{Ref|name=ONDeadly|[[Online:Deadly Investigations|Deadly Investigations]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} One of Amenos' prison gangs, the Green Serpents, put up a bounty for the capture of these three nobles for the Ascendant Order. And while Ayrenn and Emeric managed to escape their clutches, Prince Irnskar was taken to their hideaway, Old Coin Fort on the north coast. Arabelle sent Jakarn and the Vestige to retrieve the nobles. They both infiltrated Amenos Station's stockade to gather intel and the latter delved into the jungle to find the allied leaders. The adventurer found Ayrenn and Emeric in the wilderness but had to fight their way through the fort to rescue the prince. With their combined efforts, they safely escaped the island.{{Ref|name=ONEscape|[[Online:Escape from Amenos|Escape from Amenos]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}
[[File:ON-quest-And Now, Perhaps, Peace 02.jpg|thumb|right|The Peace Talks]]
During the Ascendant Order's assassination attempt at All Flags Islet, the Ascendant Magus was unmasked and killed.{{ref|name=ONAS|[[Online:The Ascendant Storm|The Ascendant Storm]] quest in {{g|on|highisle}}}} Bacaro's death soon followed, his goal to ascend to become the [[Lore:Druid King|Druid King]] and use its power to decimate Tamriel's armies with a dormant volcano spirit ruined.{{ref|name=ESODL|[[ON:Druid Laurel|Druid Laurel]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}}{{ref|name=THLord|[[ON:The Hidden Lord|The Hidden Lord]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}}{{ref|name=TIThrone|[[ON:The Ivy Throne|The Ivy Throne]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} Although the peace talks were initially a trap set by Lord Bacaro, the Steadfast Society were kept in the dark about his plans and had no involvement with the Ascendant Order. Even with Lord Bacaro gone, they continued his original vision of helping those suffering from war and disaster. As Lord Bacaro had set up a trust many years ago, the society was self-sufficient, and they continued to sponsor the peace talks and take care of all the expenses.{{ref|[[Online:Captain Marguerite|Captain Marguerite]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:High Isle (Chapter)|High Isle]]''}} The outcome of these peace talks remains unknown, though the Three Banners faction leaders named the Vestige "Champion of the Alliances".{{ref|name=ONPeace|[[Online:And Now, Perhaps, Peace|And Now, Perhaps, Peace]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Legacy===
In the Fourth Era, Ayrenn is still remembered for believing that breakfast is the most important meal of the day. It is said that she ensured every Aldmeri soldier was well fed to set oneself up for a successful day. However, some accounts say that she starved her soldiers.{{Ref|name=Cookbook2|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Books|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook Vol. 2}}}}
