The [[Lore:Snow Prince|Snow Prince]] was a [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elf]] of great fighting prowess, decisiveness, and elegance who lived on the island of [[Lore:Solstheim|Solstheim]]. His kin had great respect for him, and hailed him as their savior in the [[Lore:Battle of the Moesring|Battle of the Moesring]]. The Snow Prince killed many [[Lore:Nord|Nord]]s in the battle with his ice magic and his spear, but was struck down by Finna, a twelve-year-old girl, who threw her mother Jofrior's sword at him after she had seen the Prince slay her.{{ref|name=FotSP|{{Cite Book|Fall of the Snow Prince}}}}

The Nords won the battle, but out of respect and awe they did not burn the body of the Snow Prince with the rest of his kin. Instead, they buried him in a freshly dug barrow, which later became known as {{Lore Link|Jolgeirr Barrow}}, along with his steel armor and spear. His body was not encased in [[Lore:Stalhrim|Stalhrim]] as Nordic tradition dictated, but the tomb was lined with treasures worthy of royalty and guards were assigned to protect it.{{ref|name=FotSP}}{{ref|name=OWN|{{Cite Book|Old, Wet Note|ns_base=bm}}}} News of his death spread across the land, reaching even the isolated and scattered tribes of Snow Elves across Skyrim.{{ref|name=JOMA|{{Cite Book|Journal of Mirtil Angoth}}}} Due to the Atmorans destroying many Falmeri documents, next to nothing is known about the Snow Elves' governmental structure and what duties the Prince had.{{Ref|name=SotSS|[[Online:Seat of the Snow Prince|Seat of the Snow Prince]] Antiquity Codex entry in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

The Snow Prince ordered the creation of a stone throne designed with simple lines, leading some scholars to believe this was reflective of his personality. It was excavated by the [[Lore:Antiquarian Circle|Antiquarian Circle]] around {{Year|2E 582}}.{{Ref|name=SotSS}}

Sometime before the tomb of Jolgeirr Barrow was abandoned, the guardians became aware of Angria "and her rabble" planning to steal the Snow Prince's steel armor. Heinlen the Heavy's men drove the graverobbers away, although they made off with the ancient helmet. Heinlen tried to warn Winter-Fist,{{ref|name=OWN}} but ended up at the bottom of [[Lore:Lake Fjalding|Lake Fjalding]], likely murdered by the thieves.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Lake Fjalding|Lake Fjalding]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} Angria's corpse was found in {{Year|3E 427}}, hanging from the ceiling of her hideout, the Frykte ice cave.{{ref|[[Bloodmoon:Frykte|Frykte]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} In the same year, Jolgeirr Barrow was rediscovered and looted. ''[[Lore:Fall of the Snow Prince|Fall of the Snow Prince]]'', an ancient account of the Battle of the Moesring taken by [[Lore:Books_by_Author#Lokheim|Lokheim]], was uncovered within and came into the possession of Athellor, an [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] scholar of the thought-to-be-extinct Snow Elves.{{ref|name=Bloodmoon|[[Bloodmoon:In Search of the Falmer|In Search of the Falmer]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}} The document was later published and had become popular literature in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] by {{Year|3E 433}},{{ref|name=FotSPOblivion|[[Oblivion:Fall of the Snow Prince|Fall of the Snow Prince]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} and [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] by {{Year|4E 201}}.{{ref|name=FotSPSkyrim|[[Skyrim:Fall of the Snow Prince|Fall of the Snow Prince]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}<noinclude>

==Notes==
* Prior to the release of ''[[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]'', strings pulled from ''Skyrim''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s [[Skyrim:Patch#Version_1.5.26.0|Patch 1.5.26]] indicated that a Snow Elf Prince would appear as a character. As development continued, the sideplot evolved into [[Lore:Gelebor|Gelebor]] and [[Lore:Vyrthur|Vyrthur]]'s stories as priests of Auri-El rather than the Prince appearing, though remnants of the character remained in the form of Vyrthur's throne and the [[Skyrim:Ancient Falmer Crown|Ancient Falmer Crown]].
