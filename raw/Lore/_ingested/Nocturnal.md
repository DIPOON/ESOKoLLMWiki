==Religion and Culture==
[[File:BK-misc-Survival Guide Nocturnal.jpg|right|thumb|200px|Nocturnal (tarot card)]]
===Khajiit===
A deity known as '''Noctra''' exists in ancient Khajiiti mythos.{{ref|name=J'darji|[[ON:J'darji|J'darji]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} During the cleansing of Lorkhaj's corruption, Azurah observed his Dark Heart beating like a drum, with each throb spewing out black blood that tapped the floor to form a rhythm.{{ref|name=TFDOF}} Subsequently after the death of Lorkhaj, the spirit [[Lore:Noctra|Noctra]] was born of this black blood at the steps of the Void Gate.{{Ref|name=TDS}} [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]] battled the spirit until it knew it was not [[Lore:Namiira|Namiira]], and when the fighting subsided, Noctra was brought before [[Lore:Azurah|Azurah]] to be judged.{{ref|name=TDS}} Azurah allowed Noctra to live so long as she served Azurah and her chosen people, the Khajiit.{{ref|name=TDS}} However, Noctra is rebellious by nature, so she fled to the Void with a key she stole from Azurah, leading her to send the spirit of Lorkhaj to find Noctra. What resulted from this interaction was Noctra willing to offer aid to the Khajiit when called, and so tribes may ask her for luck, and darkness to hide them and the gift of silent movement.{{ref|name=TDS}} She also protects those who walk in the shadows.{{ref|name=J'darji}} Summoning Noctra for nefarious acts is dangerous, as she brings the [[Lore:Dark Behind the World|Dark]] with her.{{ref|name=TDS}} Noctra is still acknowledged by some Khajiit following the [[Lore:Riddle'Thar|Riddle'Thar]] Epiphany.{{ref|name=J'darji}}

According to one pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany Khajiit account, Noctra addressed Boethra as mother and master, as she'd trained under her blade. During a battle to defend the [[Lore:Moons|Lunar Lattice]] from [[Lore:Meridia|Merid-Nunda]], [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molagh]] and [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Merrunz]], Noctra, after learning from Azurah's vanquishing of [[Lore:Vaermina|Vaarmina]] and steeling herself to leave all she had behind, used the [[Lore:Skeleton Key|Skeleton Key]] on herself in order to stop Merid-Nunda from accessing the [[Lore:Sun|Aether Prism]], this caused her form to become as "shadow and cloth, a cloak of darkness" and "grow mighty, taller than the rest" of the deities present, until it went on to blend with the night itself, shrouding the Aether Prism and foiling Merid-Nunda's attempt as the burning light that was covering everyone present receded. It is said that in this act, with the Key, Noctra could say that she was not Namiira at last.{{Ref|name=Blade|{{Cite Book|The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume III}}}}

===Reachfolk===
Nocturnal is seen by the [[Lore:Reachfolk|Reachfolk]] as the '''Mistress of Shadows''', an ever-watchful spirit that has guarded them since the beginning.{{ref|name=ONMOR|[[Online: Markarth Outlaws Refuge|Markarth Outlaws Refuge]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She is considered the spirit of the night, and the Reachfolk will give her offerings in the hopes that she will send her [[Lore:Crow Daedra|crows]] to watch over them.{{ref|name=ONFialyn|[[Online:Apprentice Fialyn|Apprentice Fialyn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Other Myths===
[[File:OB-item-Hrormir's Icestaff.jpg|thumb|left|Hrormir's Icestaff exists, perhaps giving credence to the ''Song of Hrormir'']]
The ''[[Lore:Song of Hrormir|Song of Hrormir]]'' attributes Nocturnal as a "Shadowy Hag" who hid her hideous form with her gray cowl. In the epic narrative, a soul of a man named Darfang was captured by Nocturnal, and Darfang was being puppeted to fight his companion Hrormir. Seeing his friend's grief, Hrormir bargained to take his place as Nocturnal's plaything to free his friend's soul. 

Hrormir exploited wordplay in his oath to make his loyalty non-binding, and then stole her cowl from her and brought back light to the lands. In the morning after Hrormir had bedded Nocturnal, everyone saw her hideous form free from obstruction. In exchange for the cowl, Nocturnal released Hrormir and accepted her defeat.{{Ref|name=SOH}} The veracity of the story is unknown, but an artifact mentioned in the story named [[Lore:Hrormir's Icestaff|Hrormir's Icestaff]] exists.{{Ref|name=HI|[[Oblivion:Hrormir's Icestaff|Hrormir's Icestaff]] appearance in ''[[OB:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}

The ''[[Lore:Fable of the Gryphon|Fable of the Gryphon]]'' tells the story of Zariah and the talking fox Gray, who sought to steal the treasure of the  [[Lore:The Gryphon|Gryphon Luminary]] and blame the theft on Nocturnal. Their scheme was overheard by [[Lore:Crow Daedra|Crow Daedra]], messengers of Nocturnal, who hunted the thieves. Zariah and Gray evaded the crows and Nocturnal's shadows and gathered the three pieces of the Gryphon's treasure from [[Lore:Riften|Riften]], from beneath [[Lore:Forelhost|Forelhost]] and from a tomb in the shadow of [[Lore:High Hrothgar|Hrothgar]]. Finally the duo were confronted by the Gryphon, who was doubtful that Nocturnal would steal his treasure as she didn't know him. As the shadows and crows closed in and Zariah attempted futilely to fight them off, a desperate Gray revealed the truth. He was originally Nocturnal's pet who had escaped, and she sought to take him back to the Evergloam. They'd sought the Gryphon's treasure only as the means to live a life where they no longer had to run, and would return it and more in exchange for protection. 

The Gryphon deemed Zariah, courageous and loyal, worthy of his power, he raked his claws through the shadows, and granted her powerful [[Lore:Magic|magic]]. The Gryphon opened the gate to his realm and moved to allow the duo through, but it was too late for both to be saved. As the shadows closed in Zariah used the power the Gryphon had granted her to conjure a tunnel of [[Lore:Varliance|starlight]] that pushed back the dark so Gray could slip through, and to than seal the gate behind him. Though safe in the Gryphon's domain, Gray keened and clawed at the door for his friend until his paws bled.{{Ref|name=FableGryphon|{{Cite Book|Fable of the Gryphon|ns_base=ON}}}}

==Worship==
[[File:SR-interior-Twilight Sepulcher 04.jpg|The Ebonmere|thumb|right]]
{{Quote Box|style=float:none; max-width:none; margin-left:0|''The singing remains, but the song is gone. No tune, no music, but whispers set to rhythm. Serve Her, be shadow, be whispers. Creeping, crawling, but I can't escape. I'm caught in the darkness, tar which clings to my clothes. I speak but am silent. I see but am blind. Blind, so blind, to nothing but Her will.''|[[Lore:Books by Author#Romien Garvette|Romien Garvette]]{{ref|name=SAW}}}}
Nocturnal's servants come in a variety of flavors. Some worship her as thieves, and see her as the patron of all thieves.{{ref|name=NGV1}} Some such groups include the {{Lore Link|Nightingale Trinity|Nightingales}}, a trio of mortals bound to protect the [[Lore:Ebonmere|Ebonmere]] within the [[Lore:Twilight Sepulcher|Twilight Sepulcher]]. It is thought that after their tenure guarding the Sepulcher the [[Lore:Ghost|spirits]] of Nocturnal's Nightingales cross into the Evergloam and become one with the shadows, becoming part of the darkness itself. In this form the Nightingale spirits enter [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] through the Ebonmere, the conduit to Nocturnal's realm of Evergloam, and guide thieves by placing their hand in theirs and granting them their uncanny luck, being responsible for events such as a novice picking an impossible lock or someone who is blind suddenly turning to face the thief reaching for their pocket. Because of this role Nocturnal is also known as Lady Luck.{{ref|name=NGV1}}{{ref|name=SRGallus}}{{ref|name=SRKarliah|[[Skyrim:Karliah|Karliah]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} 

[[File:ON-creature-Shadow Veya 02.jpg|thumb|left|One of Nocturnal's devoted followers, transformed into a [[Lore:Gloam Knight|Gloam Knight]]]]
Some worshipers find Nocturnal mystifying. Though they strive to understand her, Nocturnal's true purpose eludes them, and attempting to understand the Daedric Prince is a futile task.{{ref|name=IOA}} To understand Nocturnal is to negate her, and the Daedric Prince is not simply going to pull back her shroud of darkness to reveal her intentions to anyone.{{ref|name=IOA}} Others are drawn into her service through subtle, but not wholly unkind manipulation. Lonely individuals who are within proximity of Nocturnal's adherents may begin to dream about Evergloam and hear her voice in their head if she notices them.{{ref|name=VPTP3}} Nocturnal's Earl from the time of the Daedric Triad came to serve her this way.{{ref|name=VPTP3}} Nocturnal uses mortal emotion to her advantage, using kindness and empathy to draw in the vulnerable.{{ref|name=VPTP3}} She speaks to select mortals in their dreams, listening to them when they speak and comforting them when they're upset.{{ref|name=VPTP3}} In the case of Earl Tundilwen in {{Year|2E 582}}, Nocturnal inserted herself into her servant's life as a surrogate mother figure, and granted her power in the [[Lore:Court of Bedlam|Court of Bedlam]].{{ref|name=VPTP4|{{Cite Book|Veya's Private Thoughts, Part 4|ns_base=Online}}}}

One particularly fervent cultist's accounts of her invasion of the Clockwork City are poetic and obsessive.{{ref|name=SAW|{{Cite Book|Shadows and Whispers}}}} The account speaks of singing shrikes and grotesque imagery.{{ref|name=SAW}} The author, Romien Garvette, often repeats phrases and claims that they are unable to think of anything but "Her" will.{{ref|name=SAW}} Romien Garvette describes Nocturnal as all-encompassing.{{ref|name=SAW}}{{ref|name=CM57ESS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 57: Ebonshadow Style}}}} Whether or not this experience is universal is unclear. It is obvious that this particular case became very attached to their mistress, but the account gives outsiders some insight to how Nocturnal's influence may affect those who give themselves to her.
[[File:ON-prerelease-Witches Festival.jpg|A Witchmother transforming a supplicant into a Reaper|thumb|right]]
[[Lore:Shadow Magic|Shadow Magic]] is pervasive throughout Evergloam, and is utilized by her worshipers and her Daedric minions.{{ref|name=ESOSOSS|The [[ON:Shadow of Sotha Sil|Shadow of Sotha Sil]]'s abilities in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} Portals of pure shadow called Gloaming Gates are used to transport cultists and Daedra from Evergloam to select locations on Nirn. Gloaming Gates channel Nocturnal's power, and transport through the Evergloam allows her cultists travel great distance without fear of being detected.{{ref|name=CGD|[[ON:Chancellor Gascone Dusant|Chancellor Gascone Dusant]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Unto the Dark|Unto the Dark]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]'' (if you killed him during Deepening Shadows)}} Barriers of fluid shadow can be used to prevent access to areas, but these barriers can be negated by shining bright light on them,{{ref|name=PLPWSL|[[ON:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Proctor Luciana Pullo]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Where Shadows Lie|Where Shadows Lie]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} or by casting spells designed to banish shadows.{{ref|name=PLPUTD|[[ON:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Proctor Luciana Pullo]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Unto the Dark|Unto the Dark]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{ref|name=PLPBarrier|[[ON:Proctor Luciana Pullo|Proctor Luciana Pullo]] dispelling the shadow barrier with light during [[ON:Unto the Dark|Unto the Dark]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}} Nocturnal may transform her followers into [[Lore:Gloam Knight|Gloam Knights]] upon their death.{{ref|name=ESOGK|Veya's transformation into a [[ON:Gloam Knight|Gloam Knight]] during [[ON:The Crystal Tower|The Crystal Tower]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

The [[Lore:Witchmothers|Witchmother coven]] is the name for a loose coven of powerful [[Lore:Witches|witches]] known as Witchmothers who venerate Nocturnal and carry out her bidding. The number of coven members is unknowable and ever-changing, and they take members from all over [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. Witchmothers [[Lore:Sight|scry]] the Evergloam for disturbances that could threaten [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] and also monitor dangers that emerge following Nocturnal's Summoning Day. One such danger was the [[Lore:Crowborne Horror|Crowborne Horror]], a powerful [[Lore:Wraith-of-Crows|Wraith-of-Crows]] whose arrival was heralded by the appearance of dark feathers on various creatures, and which threatened all of Nirn in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=Vanima|[[ON:Vanima|Vanima]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} {{ref|name=Taerma|[[ON:Witchmother Taerma|Witchmother Taerma]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

==History==
===Second Era===
====The Daedric Triad====
Around {{Year|2E 230}}, a [[Lore:The Triad|Triad]] of Daedric Princes—[[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]] and Nocturnal—made a deal with the [[Lore:Sea Sload|Sea Sload]] K'Tora to acquire the Heart of Transparent Law—the Stone of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]]. K'Tora used [[Lore:Mind Magic|mind magic]] to make the Psijic leader [[Lore:Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]], steal the Heart. Trying to foil K'Tora's plan, Iachesis made a deal with Nocturnal to learn a spell with which to hide the Heart from everyone, which was cast in a language that could only be used by mortals. Unbeknownst to all, Iachesis hid the Heart within himself—but soon forgot these events, due to K'Tora's magic.{{ref|name=ESOSummerset}} Shortly thereafter, the Psijics—along with the entire island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]]—disappeared, around the time of the founding of the Mages Guild, remaining hidden for a long time.{{ref|name=ESOSummerset|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOPO|[[Online:Ritemaster Iachesis|Ritemaster Iachesis]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}
=====The Clockwork City=====
[[File:ON-quest-Where Shadows Lie.jpg|Nocturnal's shadows rushing forth to consume the Throne Aligned as Luciana struggles to hold them back|thumb|left]][[File:ON-quest-Where Shadows Lie 2.PNG|The freed Sotha Sil banishes Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City|thumb|right]]
The Triad's plan resumed around 2E 582, Nocturnal tried to take control of the [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]]. Nocturnal's forces invaded the City and she summoned Sotha Sil's [[Lore:Shadow|Shadow]], who replaced him as ruler. Meanwhile, one of the [[Lore:Clockwork Apostle|Clockwork Apostles]], Chancellor [[Lore:Gascone Dusant|Gascone Dusant]], sought to deliver the Skeleton Key, which had been accidentally found by a Factotum beneath the Brass Fortress, to Clavicus Vile, only for the Key to be stolen by the [[Lore:Blackfeather Court|Blackfeather Court]]. The [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], assisted by [[Lore:Divayth Fyr|Divayth Fyr]] and the Clockwork Apostles, unveiled the plot, but were unable to reclaim the Skeleton Key before it was taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow. 

Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempted to seize control of the Clockwork City by unlocking the Throne itself, but were stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr, and Proctor [[Lore:Luciana Pullo|Luciana Pullo]], who rescued Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow and repelling Nocturnal's darkness through a special light spell Sotha Sil had once taught Luciana. Once freed Sotha Sil banished Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City. Sotha Sil then gave the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping.{{ref|name=ESOCWC|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}{{ref|name=Sotha Sil}}{{ref|name=ShadowSil|[[Online:Shadow of Sotha Sil|Shadow of Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

=====The Crystal Tower=====
[[File:ON-creature-Shadow Veya.jpg|Nocturnal empowering her champion atop the Crystal Tower|thumb|right]][[File:ON-quest-The Crystal Tower.jpg|The Crystal Tower in the Fields of Regret while under Nocturnal's control|thumb|left]][[File:ON-item-Dawnbreaker (corrupted).jpg|thumb|left|The corrupted Dawnbreaker, Duskbringer]]
This did not stop the three Princes from attempting to execute their plan to take control of the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] on [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]] using the Heart of Transparent Law, which Ritemaster Iachesis had hidden from them, with the ultimate goal of remaking reality itself. In preparation for the completion of their plan, the Triad invaded Meridia's realm, the [[Lore:Colored Rooms|Colored Rooms]], and she was imprisoned within Nocturnal's darkness to prevent her from interfering further. Nocturnal also corrupted [[Lore:Dawnbreaker|Dawnbreaker]] into the dark blade Duskbringer{{ref|name=DeathRecaps|Name of [[ON:Earl Tundilwen|Earl Tundilwen]]'s attacks}} in order to deprive Meridia's agent, the [[Lore:Darien Gautier|Golden Knight]] of his weapon.{{ref|name=Nocturnal|[[Online:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Meridia|[[Online:Meridia|Meridia]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} After they tricked Iachesis into revealing the Heart's location within himself, Nocturnal broke through his protective wards, which had held off the other two Princes, and killed him.
 
Nocturnal then betrayed Clavicus Vile and Mephala by banishing them from her realm and taking the Heart for herself. With it she briefly took control of the Crystal Tower, hoping to use its properties of existing in every [[Lore:Planes of Existence|reality]] simultaneously, enhanced by the life force of Nirn which she'd drain through the use of a secret life draining technique she'd stolen from Sotha Sil's Clockwork City, to achieve omnipresence in the entire [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]]. In this manner she'd absorb the power of the other Daedric Princes as she invaded their [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realms]], thus gaining infinite power through the infinite connections made, and ultimately unravel reality itself to wait for the next one to arise. Nocturnal's shadow enveloped the Tower and transformed the [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] inside into shadows, while she attempted to repair Transparent Law by reconnecting it with its Heart, making the Tower whole to complete her plan.{{ref|name=ESOPO}}{{ref|name=Sotha Sil|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONLey|[[Online:Leythen|Leythen]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

At the same time, her forces started invading the realms of the other Princes.{{ref|name=Barbas|[[Online:Barbas|Barbas]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} However, her plans were foiled by the Vestige and the Psijic Order, assisted by the two betrayed Princes and the champion of [[Lore:Meridia|Meridia]]. After Nocturnal's Earl was defeated, the Prince attempted to consume the Vestige and the Golden Knight with her shadows. It was than that the true nature of Meridia's champion as a living [[Lore:Light of Meridia|Light of Meridia]] manifested, allowing him to repel the darkness, and to restore Dawnbreaker by sacrificing himself. The Vestige then used [[Lore:Dawnbreaker|Dawnbreaker]] to restore Transparent Law which banished Nocturnal, allowing the Heart to be returned to the Crystal Tower. Nocturnal's banishment from the Tower also restored Meridia.{{ref|name=Darien|[[Online:Darien Gautier|Darien Gautier]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ESOSummerset}}

Following the death of the [[Lore:Sea Sload|Sea Sload]] K'Tora, Nocturnal took control of the Abyssal Cabal's [[Lore:Abyssal Geysers|Geysers]], a phenomena which if left undisturbed would've sunk Summerset into the sea, for some time.{{ref|name=ONGeysers}}{{ref|name=Indirim|{{Cite Book|Indirim's Journal, Assembled}}}}{{ref|name=Grog|[[ON:Grog|Grog]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=Battlereeve|[[ON:Battlereeve Tanerline|Battlereeve Tanerline]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=Sinking|[[ON:Sinking Summerset|Sinking Summerset]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The island of Summerset in exchange for the Heart having been the deal the Triad originally made with the Sea Sload.{{ref|name=KTora|[[ON:K'Tora|K'Tora]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

Relating to the Sea Sload, Nocturnal may have had some minor involvement with the Sea Sload sorceress {{Lore Link|Z'Maja}}'s conquest of [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]] in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|name=ESOZ'Maja|[[ON:Z'Maja|Z'Maja]]'s dialogue and abilities in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Z'Maja possessed an amulet that granted her powerful shadow magic, which allowed her to kill countless citizens via a dark mist that enveloped the city, enrapturing the inhabitants and slowing their hearts. It also allowed her to shadowy copies of Summerset's protectors, the [[Lore:Gryphon|gryphon]]-riding [[Lore:Welkynars|Welkynars]].{{Ref|name=WPA}}{{ref|name=WOTW|Events of [[ON:Woe of the Welkynars|Woe of the Welkynars]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}

That same year in [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]], Nocturnal tasked the Vestige with retrieving the [[Lore:Shade Sickle|Shade Sickle]]. The artifact allowed the user to split a person from their shadow and turn them into a thrall. The Sickle had been stolen from the Shrine of Nocturnal by thieves hired by the [[Lore:Necromancy|necromancer]] Tumma-Maxath. The Vestige found the artifact in the necromancer's lair in [[Lore:Bloodrun Cave|Bloodrun Cave]] and returned it to the shrine.{{ref|name=ND|{{Cite Book|Necromancer's Diary}}}}{{ref|name=SON|Events of [[ON:Shrine of Nocturnal|Shrine of Nocturnal]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Blackwood (Chapter)|Blackwood]]''}}

===Third Era=== 
[[File:OBR-quest-Nocturnal.jpg|Nocturnal's shrine in Cyrodiil (Oblivion Remastered)|thumb|right]]
In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], an [[Lore:The Agent|unknown agent]] of the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] was tasked by Nocturnal to kill a mage in their stronghold in return for the Skeleton Key. However, the mage had already had a contingency plan in case the Daedric Prince had sent someone to slay them. They created a fake artifact ([[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]], [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]], or the [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|Warlock's Ring]]) that functioned identically to the true artifact to tempt the agent with, though it would degrade after several days; enough time to flee when the illusion was broken. Accounts conflict if the agent took the mage up on the offer and was bamboozled, or if they slayed the mage and received the Key in return.{{ref|name=DFNocturnal|[[Daggerfall:Nocturnal's Quest|Nocturnal's quest]] in [[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|''Daggerfall'']]}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the Eye of Nocturnal was stolen from Nocturnal's shrine in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]'s [[Lore:Blackwood|Blackwood]] region. Nocturnal tasked the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] with recovering it. The thieves, an [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] couple in [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]], hid it in a flooded cave in [[Lore:Topal Bay|Topal Bay]]. The Hero of Kvatch returned it to Nocturnal and was rewarded with the Skeleton Key.{{ref|name=OBNocturnal|[[Oblivion:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] quest in [[Oblivion:Oblivion|''Oblivion'']]}}

===Fourth Era===
[[File:SR-quest-Trinity Restored.jpg|A new Nightingale Trinity transacts the oath with Nocturnal|thumb|left]][[File:SR-npc-Mercer Frey.jpg|Mercer Frey, traitor to Nocturnal|thumb|right]]
Some time before {{Year|4E 201}}, the Skeleton Key was stolen from the Twilight Sepulcher by [[Lore:Mercer Frey|Mercer Frey]], a corrupt Nightingale. Its long-term loss resulted in a decline of the Skyrim branch of the Thieves Guild due to bad luck without Nocturnal's influence. The guild's influence declined until their only presence was in the Ratways beneath [[Lore:Riften|Riften]]. [[Lore:Karliah|Karliah]], a Nightingale, apologized to Nocturnal for failing to keep the key safe, promising to return the key to its rightful place, and brought her two new Nightingales as a gift, one of them being, the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]]. 

Nocturnal was grateful for her new Nightingales and warned Karliah to not fail her again. Mercer used the Key to bring himself luck, but he was eventually confronted by the Nightingales beneath the Great Statue of [[Lore:Irkngthand|Irkngthand]]. Mercer was killed, and the Last Dragonborn brought the Key back to the Sepulcher, reopening the Ebonmere. Nocturnal rewarded the Dragonborn with three Nightingale powers of their choosing.{{Ref|name=SRTG|[[Skyrim:Thieves Guild (faction)#Quests|Thieves Guild questline]] in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

==Notes==
*The ruins of [[Lore:Ebon Stadmont|Ebon Stadmont]] are carved with a mysterious language that resembles but isn't [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]]. This language, older than any other language on Nirn, has been theorized to predate even the language of Oblivion. Leythen, Earl of Mephala and chief linguist of the Psijic Order, also claimed the Ebon Standmont language and the Daedric power it was linked to were far older than his Prince, Mephala. The language has arcane properties which can only be used by mortals, as recitation of the script has transported people and objects through [[Lore:Time Magic|time]] and cast the spell with which Ritemaster Iachesis hid the Heart of Transparent Law. Nocturnal provided Iachesis a spell in this language, and the inscriptions in Ebon Stadmont are carved on structures featuring crows, which are symbols of Nocturnal. This might support Nocturnal's claims of being older than Oblivion and other Princes. {{Ref|name=Forte|{{Cite Book|Grand Maestro Forte's Research}}}}{{Ref|name=ONLey}}{{ref|name=TES|Events of [[Online:The Ebon Sanctum|The Ebon Sanctum]] questline in  [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}{{ref|name=ESOPO}}
*Nocturnal may be related to Noctyr-a from the story of [[Daggerfall:Ghraewaj|Ghraewaj]], the fabled origin of [[Daggerfall:Harpy|harpies]] in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]].{{ref|name=DFGH|[[Daggerfall:Ghraewaj|''Ghraewaj'']]}}
*One myth claims that [[Lore:Rajhin|Rajhin]] stole the [[Lore:Ring of Khajiiti|Ring of Khajiiti]] from Mafala on a [[Lore:Moons|moonless]] night which allowed him to enter Oblivion. There he found Mafala, Azurah, and a third Daedric Prince arguing over who claimed dominion over the night. The identity of the third Prince is not revealed. Given her sphere it might be Nocturnal.{{ref|[[Online:Humble Zaham|Humble Zaham]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}
*[[Lore:Nocturnal Shrike|Nocturnal Lieutenant]] [[Lore:Jaciel Morgen|Jaciel Morgen]] was ruler of the [[Lore:Evergloam|Shade Perilous]], a pocket realm of the Evergloam. She claimed the other Nocturnals would go to war with [[Lore:Mehrunes Dagon|Mehrunes Dagon]] over his invasion of the realm and that in the end the sky and earth would crack, all mortals would perish, and Dagon would lie defeated. As Shade Perilous is a pocket realm of Evergloam and Nocturnal Shrikes are generally associated with Nocturnal, she might've been suggesting a war taking place between Nocturnal and Dagon.{{ref|name=Jaciel|[[Battlespire:Jaciel Morgen|Jaciel Morgen]]'s dialogue in ''[[Battlespire:Battlespire|Battlespire]]''}}
