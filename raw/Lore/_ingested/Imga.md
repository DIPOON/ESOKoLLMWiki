The [[Lore:Imga|Imga]], also called '''Great Apes''' or '''Apemen''',{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}{{ref|name=Betmeri|[[MW:Generic Dialogue B#Betmeri|Generic dialogue]] on the topic of '''Betmeri''' in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} are native [[Lore:Beastfolk|beastfolk]] of [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}{{ref|name=PGE3V}} These intelligent [[Lore:Ape|apes]] are said to have flourished in Valenwood long before the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] ever arrived there.{{ref|name=PGE3V|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Valenwood}}}} They cohabit in the forests with the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] tribes, and also inhabit many of the abandoned human trading posts which had been established during the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{Ref|name=PGE1AD|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} The apemen are also reportedly found in the mountainous areas in the west and north of Tamriel, and seldom encountered in the East.{{ref|name=Betmeri}} The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] kept the apemen as slaves, though not in as great numbers as they keep [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]]s.{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}}

The most notable Imga was undoubtedly [[Lore:Marukh|Marukh]], a [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] prophet who rose to prominence during the [[Lore:Camoran Dynasty|Camoran Dynasty]]. His teachings sparked the massive cultural and spiritual movement known as the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]].{{ref|name=TIoD|{{Cite Book|The Illusion of Death}}}} Other Imga would go on to follow his teachings in the [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selective]].{{ref|name=BSoB|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}}

==History==
===Early History===
Little is known of the early history of the Imga, though they were known to flourished in Valenwood long before the Aldmer settled there.{{ref|name=PGE3V}} They may have been among the many aboriginal beastpeoples that lived in preliterate communities throughout Tamriel since the early [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{ref|name=Betmeri}}{{ref|name=BTAOM|{{Cite Book|Before the Ages of Man}}}}

===The Monkey Prophet===
At some point in the First Era, the Imga Prophet known as Marukh served a "Century of Penance" atop the Stonemeadows for toying with ape-maiden Dulsa. He would allegedly receive a vision of the deceased [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]] and would be compelled to write the Alessian Doctrines, going on to form the Alessian Order that would rise to power in the early [[Lore:First Era#Fourth Century|300s]].{{ref|name=TIoD}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}}

The [[Lore:Marukhati Selective|Marukhati Selective]], the elite sect of the Alessian Order, was said to have Imga sorcerer apes or magiapes among them. Some of these would be among them when they caused the [[Lore:Middle Dawn|Middle Dawn]].{{ref|name=BSoB|{{Cite Book|ns_base=ON|The Bladesongs of Boethra}}}}

===Brief Disappearance===
The majority of Imga disappeared from Valenwood during the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]] and [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]], believed to be part of their time-tested strategy of hiding out during periods of warfare until the strife was safely over.{{Ref|name=LEoERZI|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Episode of ESO-RP ZOS Interview|ns_base=GEN}}}} The controversial Altmer author [[Lore:Cirantille|Cirantille]] believed that they went to 'visit Falinesti', which had already vanished from [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] centuries prior, so that they could wait out the end of the Planemeld and the Three Banners War, though most of her peers disagreed with her opinion on the subject.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Lady Clarisse Laurent Answers Your Questions}}}} Sometime around {{Year|2E 582}}, there was an Imgan [[Lore:Lich|Lich]]-Queen who ruled over [[Lore:Meirvale|Meirvale]] in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], but she was later defeated by [[Lore:Razum-dar|Razum-dar]], a well-known agent.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Razum-dar's Journal, Chapter 1}}}} Also around this time, the prophecies of the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]] made references to the Imga, although to a lesser extent than in the past.{{ref|{{Cite book|Loremaster's Archive - Elder Scrolls & Moth Priests}}}} It is unknown exactly when or how the Imga returned, but they are known to have done so by {{Year|2E 864}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}

===Late Second Era and Third Era===
Imga were written of in the first edition of the [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition|''Pocket Guide to the Empire'']], where they were described sharing the forests of Valenwood with the Bosmer and [[Lore:Centaur|Centaurs]] and idolizing the Altmer. A member of the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Second Aldmeri Dominion]] circa 2E 864 known as [[Lore:¥R|¥R]], would sketch a gentleman Great Ape who was kind enough to pose for him in [[Lore:Elden Root|Elden Root]].{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}

At some point in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], when [[Lore:Simocles Quo|Simocles Quo]] was in his younger years, he was invited to join a colony of Imga in harvesting Kolllopi.{{ref|name=TRKR}} It is claimed some Imga would ally with the Bosmer during the [[Lore:Five Year War|Five Year War]] after the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] attacked one of their camps, accidentally or otherwise.{{Ref|name=MUT4|group=UOL}} ''[[Lore:A Dance in Fire|A Dance in Fire]]'', a fictionalized account set during the war, described Imga attending a revelry in Falinesti.{{Ref|name=ADiF}} Circa {{Year|3E 427}}, the Dunmer of Morrowind were known to keep many Apemen as slaves.{{Ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}}

==Culture and Society==
[[File:ON-icon-misc-Fur.png|Imga Fur|thumb|left]]
Imga are known to practice courting rituals,{{ref|name=TIoD}}{{ref|name=ONEVE|[[Online:Eveli Sharp-Arrow|Eveli Sharp-Arrow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} though not much else about their reproduction or how they show affection is well understood.{{ref|name=NORP|{{Cite Book|Notes on Racial Phylogeny}}}}{{ref|name=LoveArchive|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Heart's Day & Mother Mara}}}} In [[Lore:Falinesti|Falinesti]], colonies of Imga apes annually gather [[Lore:Kollopi|Kollopi]], an arboreal rodent, from the ends of the slenderest branches of the graht-oaks. The "ape men" are naturally dexterous, and are renowned for their agility.{{ref|name=TBA1|{{Cite Book|The Black Arrow, v 1}}}}{{ref|name=TRKR|{{Cite Book|The Red Kitchen Reader}}}} They climb beneath the Kollopi rodents then jump up to "pick" them from their perches. These Kollopi are then used to make a simple peasant dish of the same name.{{Ref|name=TRKR}} There is a beverage named for the Imga, known as "Sipping Imga Tonic".{{ref|[[ON:Sipping Imga Tonic|Sipping Imga Tonic]] in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

Some Imga see the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmeri]] as their superiors and as a portrait of an ideal, civilized society. Imga go to desperate measures to emulate the Altmeri: they wear capes, practice with the dueling sword, and attempt to speak with perfect enunciation and courtly manners despite their gravelly, baritone voices. Some Altmer seem to treat them with a degree of respect.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}} Each individual bears some kind of title, be it Baron, Duke, Earl, or the like, which they use when addressing the members of the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] (but despite what the titles suggest, there are supposedly no land-owning Imga). More extreme Imga shave their bodies and powder their skin white to seem more like the Altmeri. The Imga feel that Men are beneath them as lesser beastfolk and pretend to find their smell exceedingly offensive: when Men are around, Imga hold perfumed corners of their capes to their noses.{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}}{{ref|name=UOLTD|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|ns_base=General|The Dreadful Theft of the Sun's Dusk Ale}}}}

While the Imga share the forests with the Bosmer,{{Ref|name=PGE1AD}} it has been claimed that they have little love for them.{{Ref|name=MUT4|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Mixed Unit Tactics v4|ns_base=General}}}} Nonetheless, Imga have been known to attend revelries in Falinesti, alongside Bosmer, [[Lore:Wood Orc|Wood Orcs]], and [[Lore:Centaur|Centaurs]].{{Ref|name=ADiF|{{Cite Book|A Dance in Fire, Chapter 2}}}} In one account of a [[Lore:Leyawiin|Leyawiin]] sailor attempting to sell carved bone-pipe of "genuine Valenwood make", a Bosmer woman was able to easill recognize it as a fake as the tail was wrong on the Imga carved on the bowl.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 3: Wood Elf Style}}}}

Luxurious hand warmers made from Imga fur are known to exist. It is unknown who makes these but the sewn label inside them claim they were made from "treacherous" Imga.{{ref|{{Item Link|summary=1|quality=f|Imga Fur Hand Warmer|id=64346}} item description in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
