The [[Lore:Wing of the Dragon|Wing of the Dragon]] is a pocket realm belonging to the [[Lore:The Dragon|Luminary Dragon]]. It was shaped and hewn into being in the same way she chose her form. The reason for the realm's formation was unknown, though the [[Lore:The Indrik|Luminary Indrik]] theorized that the realms of the [[Lore:Luminary|Luminaries]] had formed due to the wisdom of [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]], which did not wish for the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.{{Ref|name=TI|[[ON:The Indrik|The Indrik]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}{{ref|name=Crow|[[Online:The Crow|The Crow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=Dragon|[[Online:The Dragon|The Dragon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}<noinclude>

The realm resembles a great cavern dotted with architecture similar to what one might find in [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]].{{Ref|name=TWOTG|[[ON:The Wing of the Gryphon|The Wing of the Gryphon]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}} As with the realms of all Luminaries, it had a warded door and a key which one had to use to enter it.{{ref|name=Crow}}

==History==
===Creation===
The realm is as old as the Dragon, created at the time of her birth. It was formed at some point after the creation of the [[Lore:Wing of the Indrik|Wing of the Indrik]].{{Ref|name=TI|[[ON:The Indrik|The Indrik]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}{{ref|name=Crow|[[Online:The Crow|The Crow]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Birth of Scribing===
[[File:ON-place-Wing of the Dragon.jpg|The door to the Wing of the Dragon|thumb|right]]
Circa {{Year|1E 2150}}, after Ulfsild had already located the [[Lore:Wing of the Indrik|Wing of the Indrik]], she'd also encounter the Dragon.{{ref|name=LAS|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - Scribing}}}}{{Ref|name=MGC|{{Cite Book|Mages Guild Charter}}}}{{Ref|name=ULTI|{{Cite Book|Ulfsild's Log: The Indrik}}}} Ulfsild heard the [[Lore:Fable of the Dragon|Fable of Mizbi and the Dragon]] from a [[Lore:Moon-Singers|Moon-Singer]] while travelling with a band of nomads through [[Lore:Malabal Tor|Malabal Tor]]. She immediately knew there was more to the tale, and spent the night sleepless thinking on it. Wandering from the campsite, Ulfsild found a young [[Lore:Cathay|Cathay]] [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] gazing up at the [[Lore:Stars|stars]]. Being asked why he was awake, the Khajiit responded with a question, wondering if "she" could see him, the one who raises her gaze twice a night from under the galloping hooves of the [[Lore:The Steed|Steed]] constellation. Ulfsild surmised that the Khajiit was speaking of the Twilight Star, of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]]. The Khajiit than offered to trade a polished [[Lore:Moonstone|moonstone]] for the feather of the [[Lore:The Indrik|Luminary Indrik]] on Ulfsild's hat. Ulfsild refused, as she'd had the feather since she was a child and it was very dear to her, and she recounted the [[Lore:Fable of the Indrik|Fable of the Indrik]] instead. Behaving as if she'd won some sort of game they'd been playing, the Khajiit once more offered up the moonstone as a fair trade for the story he'd been told. When Ulfsild tried to hand the moonstone back, he refused to take it, claiming instead she could give it back in exchange for another story or riddle. Wishing to learn more of the fable of Mizbi, Ulfsild asked the Khajiit to tell it to her again, writing down notes in the process, as she knew the story was the key to finding the Dragon.{{ref|name=ONULD|{{Cite Book|Ulfsild's Log: The Dragon}}}}

Ulfsild annotated the text and used its clues to discover the gateway to the Dragon's realm. She obtained the keys from the temple of [[Lore:Jode's Light|Jode's Light]], from the Do'Krin Monastery, from Khaj'Rawlith, and from Ja'zennji Siir to the west of [[Lore:Dune|Dune]]. Ultimately she found the door in a grove south of Willowgrove village, broke the wards, and entered the Dragon's domain.{{Ref|name=FOTDA|{{Cite Book|ns_base=Online|Fable of the Dragon (Annotated)}}}} 

Ulfsild knew what the Dragon wanted: an impossible riddle.{{ref|name=MATMD|{{Cite Book|Mizbi and the Magic Door (Annotated)}}}} Ulfsild could not give the Dragon an impossible riddle, but she could give her a paradox: if she scribed a grimoire that could also scribe out every other possible grimoire, would it eventually also create itself? The two argued over the question for hours and, eventually, the Dragon conceded. Ulfsild passed the trial, and formed a bond with the Dragon, who lent her power which changed Ulfsild's magic forever.{{ref|name=ONULIR|{{Cite Book|Ulfsild's Notes: The Impossible Riddle}}}}

Before the doors to their wings were sealed, the Luminaries, including the Dragon, fed the altar their strength. With their strength fueling it, the altar could "shape the very heart of magic itself" and translate its very essence into mortal hands, rewriting both the magic and the mortal as the practitioner scrawled their [[Lore:Souls|soul]] upon the page with the Luminary as intermediary. As beings of pure magic, the Luminaries were said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing was said to have been somehow destined to channel the power of the Luminaries from the start.{{ref|name=Crow}}{{Ref|name=TI}} The power of the Dragon herself caused new [[Lore:Ley Lines|Ley Line]] activity to take place across [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]], making Signature Scripts and Luminous Ink appear across the land, a phenomenon theorized to be tied symbolically to the Dragon's chosen shape.{{Ref|name=TI}}{{ref|name=SigilDragon|[[Online:Sigil of the Dragon Analysis|Sigil of the Dragon Analysis]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Eventually, the Scholarium was lost along with [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]], which it was located under, transported to the [[Lore:Shivering Isles|Shivering Isles]] due to a bargain [[Lore:Shalidor|Shalidor]] made with the [[Lore:Daedric Prince|Daedric Prince]] [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]] in exchange for the [[Lore:Folium Discognitum|Folium Discognitum]].{{ref|name=Crow}}{{Ref|name=Shalidor|[[Online:Arch-Mage Shalidor|Arch-Mage Shalidor]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Before departing from the Scholarium, Ulfsild visited the dragon one last time. Thinking ahead, she promised the Luminary three things, that one day another with a mind for riddles would appear, that they would offer her another impossible riddle, and that they would never claim to be Ulfsild's inheritor. {{ref|name=ONULIR}}   

With the doors closed, the altar became dormant. Without that beating heart, the mastery of Scribing was believed to stagnate. The Scholarium itself was not discovered, however, for it had been warded against mages, Princes, and powers long ago, and those defenses held strong even in the Shivering Isles.{{ref|name=Crow}}

===Rediscovery of Scribing===
In {{Year|2E 582}}, around the time Eyevea was returned to Tamriel by Shalidor and the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]], the Scholarium was rediscovered by a group of [[Lore:Knights|knights]] from the [[Lore:Order of the Lamp|Order of the Lamp]]. During their arrival, complications arose, necessitating the sealing of the doors to prevent a catastrophe, as the once dormant altar became unstable, threatening the lives of everyone present. The disaster was prevented due to the intervention of the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]], who replaced the unstable focusing crystal. This potentially also prevented the discovery of the Scholarium by Sheogorath, an event which would've been catastrophic.{{ref|name=Crow}}{{Ref|name=SCOS|[[ON:The Second Era of Scribing|The Second Era of Scribing]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}}

After the magic was calmed, the Scholarium's caretaker, the [[Lore:Luminary Crow|Luminary Crow]], directed the Vestige to seek out the other Luminaries and gain their support in order to return their strength to the altar. An act equated to inheriting Ulfsild's legacy and leaving a mark on spellcraft itself.{{ref|name=Crow}}

The Inheritor of the Scholarium retraced Ulfsild's footsteps, discovering the fable and keys, breaking the wards, and entering the Wing of the Dragon to reconnect the Scholarium with it. The Dragon gave the Inheritor a series of riddles to solve in order to give her further insight into their mind. With the aid of their allies in the Scholarium, the Inheritor figured out the solutions, slaying beasts in [[Lore:Reaper's March|Reaper's March]], seeking out treasure troves, and defeating a [[Lore:Tales of Tribute|Tales of Tribute Grandmaster]]. The Dragon than gave the Inheritor their final challenge, to give her an impossible riddle. The Vestige ultimately posed the Dragon the question of whether the were Ulfsild's Inheritor or not. Puzzled by how the Vestige knew of her conversations with Ulfsild, the Dragon was once more caught in a paradox. Only the Inheritor could know of what Ulfsild had said, yet the Vestige had never claimed to be the Inheritor and, if they weren't, they could not know of what Ulfsild had said. The Dragon acknowledged the Vestige's cunning strategy, and conceded to lend her power.{{ref|name=Dragon}}{{Ref|name=TWOTD}}{{ref|name=Riddles|[[Online:Riddles of the Dragon|Riddles of the Dragon]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=RiddlesSolutions|[[Online:Riddles of the Dragon (Solutions)|Riddles of the Dragon (Solutions)]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

[[File:ON-creature-Aut'arioth, Inheritor's Bane.jpg|Aut'arioth, the Inheritor's Bane|thumb|left]][[File:ON-quest-The Wing of the Crow.jpg|thumb|right|The Luminaries of Magic]]
With her trials completed, the Dragon granted the Sigil of the Dragon to the Inheritor. Placed at the altar, the Sigil restored the connection between the realm of the Dragon and the Scholarium, and the Dragon's strength flowed into the altar once more.{{ref|name=Dragon|[[Online:The Dragon|The Dragon]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=TWOTD|[[ON:The Wing of the Dragon|The Wing of the Dragon]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Gold Road|Gold Road]]''}} This reconnection led to new Ley Line activity across Tamriel, thus making Signature Scripts and Luminous Ink appear across the land.{{Ref|name=TI}}{{ref|name=SigilDragon|[[Online:Sigil of the Dragon Analysis|Sigil of the Dragon Analysis]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

==Fable of the Dragon==
[[File:ON-furnishing-Fable of the Dragon.jpg|The Fable of the Dragon|thumb|left]]
The ''[[Lore:Fable of the Dragon|Fable of the Dragon]]'' tells the story of Mizbi the Bard, a Khajiit that knew the answer to every riddle. Mizbi knew the answers to all riddles because the Luminary Dragon lived within her head, and made sure she knew. Mizbi and her Dragon came to the Jode's Light Monastery and asked the monks present, what it was that could be broken with but a single word. When the monks failed to answer, Mizbi replied "silence", much to the delight of the Dragon. The Dragon than showed her wings, and carried Mizbi to another monastery, circled by Claws ready to strike. The Dragon spoke through Mizbi's mouth and asked the Claws whether they were swift enough to know what dies by devouring itself. When the Claws failed to answer, the Dragon replied "a torch", and flew Mizbi away, landing within a circle of Moon-Singers. The songs of the Moon-Singers shackled Mizbi and her Dragon, and were about to split them. The Dragon once more posed a riddle, what had to be kept after it was given to someone or else it would be made worthless. As the Moon-Singers pondered the answer, which was "a promise", Mizbi and her Dragon flew away to safety once more.{{Ref|name=FableDragon|{{Cite Book|Fable of the Dragon|ns_base=ON}}}}

Chased for their mischief, Mizbi and her Dragon flew to a grove of willows where a door between worlds was located. The Dragon would take Mizbi to sanctuary, but only if she answered one final riddle. The Dragon asked, what had a wide mouth yet never spoke with its own voice. A frustrated Mizbi replied the answer was Mizbi herself, as the Dragon would always speak for her. Mizbi's mouth then opened wide and the Dragon slithered out, demanding that she speak the answer in her own voice this time. Mizbi laughed in relief, assured that the pain had all been in her head. Though she didn't answer the riddle, the Dragon deemed her answer clever enough to bring her through the gate to sanctuary.{{Ref|name=FableDragon}}
