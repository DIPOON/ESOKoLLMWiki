The [[Lore:Anuad|Anuad]] is a popular [[Lore:Mer|elven]] religious text detailing the creation of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] according to [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]] faith.{{ref|name=AA|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}}{{ref|name=Mono2|group=TRL|[[General:The Monomyth (French translation)|French translation]] of ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]''}}{{Ref|name=PGE1Ald|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}}{{ref|name=MR|[[ON:Monomyth Reforged|Monomyth Reforged]] antiquity codex entries in [[ON:ESO|ESO]]}} While many other early elven writings didn't survive the purges of the [[Lore:Alessian Order|Alessian Order]], the Anuad was able to survive into modern times by virtue of its popularity and proliferation.{{Ref|name=PGE1Ald}} A guide published by the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] during the reign of [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] described the content of the Anuad as "beautiful heresies".{{Ref|name=PGE1Ald}} Crests of Anu are sometimes printed onto published copies of it.{{ref|name=MR}}

It has been claimed that Anu and Padomay as depicted in the Anuad actually refer to their primary aspects of [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]] and [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]].{{ref|name=Mono2|group=TRL}}
The concept of [[Lore:Nir|Nir]], the feminine source of creation who the goddess [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] is sometimes associated with, is said to originate from the Anuad.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=AA}} The unabridged version of the Anuad makes reference to "brilliant students" of the titanic [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]], who some have speculated are the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]].{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} It is also known to make reference to a [[Lore:Spells|spell]] regarding "balance and chaos".{{ref|name=MR}}

==Annotated Anuad==
There exists a widely published simplified version of the Anuad, interchangeably known as "[[Lore:The Annotated Anuad|The Annotated Anuad]]", "The Anuad Paraphrased", or "A Children's Anuad".{{ref|name=AA}} Due to how easy the text is to understand, it was one of the books used in an Imperial literacy campaign on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]],  distributed with the aid of [[Lore:House Hlaalu|House Hlaalu]] and the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] in {{Year|3E 427}}.{{ref|[[MW:Literacy Campaign|Literacy Campaign]] quest in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The author Alessia Otus, a staunch believer of the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial faith]], described the book disparagingly as "a religious book appropriate for those ignorant of the mysteries of the faith, and adapted to the meanest understanding."{{Ref|name=GTL|{{Cite Book|Guide to Leyawiin}}}}
