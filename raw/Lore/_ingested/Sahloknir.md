[[Lore:Sahloknir|Sahloknir]] was a [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] whose name means "Phantom Sky Hunter" in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragon Language]].{{Ref|name=TESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}} Sahloknir was among the few dragons to survive the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]] era, instead meeting his end at the hands of the [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] hero [[Lore:Jorg Helmbolg|Jorg Helmbolg]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], with his remains being gathered by the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]] and buried in a dragon mound east of [[Lore:Kynesgrove|Kyne's Grove]].{{ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}}<noinclude>

By {{Year|2E 373}}, local legends still claimed him to be the dragon buried near the town, and the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] recorded him in their atlas of dragons as being deceased by report.{{ref|name=AOD}} In his long dormant state his mound received some attention from other groups later on such as the [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]], who built {{Lore Link|Dark Water Temple}} in the mountains right next to it.{{Ref|name=ESOTDL|[[ON:The Dragonguard's Legacy|The Dragonguard's Legacy]] quest}} By {{Year|2E 582}}, the [[Lore:Keepers of the Grove|Keepers of the Grove]] erected a shrine to [[Lore:Kyne|Kyne]] atop the mound, where they performed various prayers and rituals to the goddess.{{Ref|name=ESOSoC|[[ON:Shrine of Corruption|Shrine of Corruption]] quest}}

Upon his return in {{Year|4E 201}}, [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] began the process of resurrecting the many dead dragons buried throughout [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] by the Dragon Cult in ancient times. Sahloknir was one of the earlier dragons to be brought back to life,{{Ref|name=MoDB|[[Skyrim:Map of Dragon Burials|Map of Dragon Burials]]}} where his resurrection was witnessed by [[Lore:Delphine|Delphine]] and the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]]. Alduin saw the two and commanded Sahloknir to kill them, but the Last Dragonborn slew Sahloknir and absorbed his soul, proving to Delphine that they truly were Dragonborn.{{ref|name=ABitD|[[SR:A Blade in the Dark|A Blade in the Dark]] quest in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Sahloknir was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
