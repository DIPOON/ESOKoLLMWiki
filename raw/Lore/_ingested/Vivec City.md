[[Lore:Vivec City|Vivec City]] (often shortened to simply '''Vivec'''){{Ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vivec}}}} was the largest city on the island-district of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]], in the province of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]. In its heyday, it was one of the largest cities in eastern [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]{{Ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vivec}}}} and the holy seat of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] theology on Vvardenfell.{{Ref|name=NFTA|{{Cite Book|Note from the Archcanon}}}} It was named after [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] the Warrior-Poet, one of the [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god-kings that ruled Morrowind for most of its modern history and the one who made their home here.{{Ref|name=GTV}} The city was known as the '''City of Swords''',{{Ref|name=36LOVS24|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 24}}}} as in the laws that cut people into better shape.{{Ref|name=36LOVS23|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 23}}}}

After the [[Lore:Ministry of Truth|Ministry of Truth]] crashed onto the city, it caused [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] to erupt and destroyed most of Vvardenfell. Those chain of events are remembered as the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]], and while some settlements were later on the path toward rebuilding, Vivec City's fate is unknown.{{Ref|name=TRYII|{{Cite Book|The Red Year, Vol. II}}}} By the mid-first century of the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], what remained of it was a smoldering and charred ruin in the center of the boiling [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]].{{Ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 9}}<noinclude>{{TOCleft|limit=3}}

==Layout and Geography==
[[File:MW-place-Arena Canton.jpg|thumb|left|The Arena Canton]]
[[File:MW-book-Vivec Region.jpg|thumb|right|Map of the Vivec Region ca. 3E 427]]
Vivec City was located on the south-central coast of Vvardenfell, in the [[Lore:Ascadian Isles|Ascadian Isles]] region.{{Ref|name=MWVivec|[[Morrowind:Vivec (city)|Vivec (city)]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONVivec|[[Online:Vivec City|Vivec City]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} A land characterized by its temperate climate, lush, well-watered lowlands, and myriad lakes and rivers.{{Ref|name=GTVV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}} Much of the surrounding region was populated by a series of plantations, farmhouses, and the occassional smugglers' hideout.{{Ref|name=MWVivec}} The city was specifically built in a body of water called the [[Lore:Norvayn Bay|Norvayn Bay]],{{Ref|name=MWElone|[[Morrowind:Elone|Elone]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} between [[Lore:Elmas Island|Elmas Island]] to the east and the [[Lore:Systata Lagoon|Systata Lagoon]] to the west.{{Ref|name=MWGenS|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue S#Specific Place|Specific Place]] generic dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Here, it had direct access to the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]].{{Ref|name=MWVivec}}

The city was built in the Dunmer Metropolitan style and served as a unique example of high Dunmer architecture. It was evenly divided eight-ways by ginormous, multi-tiered blocks called cantons, each the size of a complete town. These cantons had wide, open streets on the outside of each tier, each accessible by a large incline, while the inside was where people congregated and commerced.{{Ref|name=MWVivec}} When these areas were initially built, a blessing stone, which contained small fractions of Vivec's divine power was placed in its foundation to sanctify them.{{Ref|name=ONTarvus|[[Online:Archcanon Tarvus|Archcanon Tarvus]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} The city was famous for its steam-parks, its monuments, and the canals that separate the cantons.{{Ref|name=POM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} 

The domed top layer was the plaza, a courtyard where noble manorhouses and fine shops were built into corridor walls. Below that was the waistworks, a two story section for several stores, cornerclubs, and temples. This area was the central section of a canton as it contained the most places and people, but below that on a canton's base floor was the canalworks, named so for how close they were to the canals. On the inside, it was only accessible through the downstairs waistworks or trapdoors. Here were tombs, small shops, storage rooms, and even local paupers. Finally, its last section was the underworks, essentially, the cantons' sewers. Water is drained from here and to the canals.{{Ref|name=MWVivec}}

Vivec City's northernmost and largest canton was the Foreign Quarter, a three-tiered structure, while its east and westmost cantons were the Telvanni and Hlaalu Compounds respectively. The city's main entrances were through these cantons via bridges to the Ascadian Isles. Most of the main roads in the region all ended in Vivec City. The Hlaalu footbridge lied on the [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Castle Ebonheart]]-[[Lore:Seyda Neen|Seyda Neen]] road, the Foreign Quarter footbridge traveled north into farmland, and the Telvanni footbridge was useful for travelers toward [[Lore:Molag Mar|Molag Mar]]. To the center south of these outer cantons was a two-by-two parameter of parallel cantons, the Redoran and Arena cantons to the north, connecting to the Foreign, Hlaalu and Telvanni cantons, and the St. Delyn and St. Olms cantons to the south, further connected to the Temple District in the south. The city's shape is similar to a [[Lore:Cross|cross]].{{Ref|name=GTV}}{{Ref|name=MWVivec}}

The Great House cantons held active administrative services, like the Hall of Records, the Treasury, holding cells, and several services only available to house members. Councilors and house officials took residence here, but to varying degrees. In the city's late years, at least one Hlaalu councilor named [[Lore:Crassius Curio|Crassius Curio]] lived in the Hlaalu canton and Mage-Lord Mavon Drenim was the ranking Telvanni official in the Telvanni canton. Because outlanders were originally allowed access to the Foreign Quarter, several Imperial guilds like the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters]] and [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]], the [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]] in Vivec City, and several independent services and craftspeople operated there.{{Ref|name=GTV}} Vivec City's port-of-call was known as North Landing and contained a single dock and silt strider port.{{Ref|name=MWGenS}}

South of the Redoran and Arena compounds are St. Delyn and St. Olms, twin residential and trade cantons where many apartments and shops strewn its outer streets and inner corridors.{{Ref|name=MWGenS}} Connected by two bridges to the south is Vivec City's Temple District and its main structure, the High Fane, the largest Tribunal temple on Vvardenfell. Underneath the main temple were the Halls of Wisdom and Justice, the Temple's administrative, executive, judicial, and martial offices. Hanging above it was the Ministry of Truth, a moonlet held by Vivec's power, base for the region's Ordinators, and maximum security prison. The south end of Vivec City was the Palace of Vivec, the eponymous god's home and seat of power. It was a large ziggurat with canals stacked over each other for several layers and a tall staircase to its entrance.{{Ref|name=GTV}}{{Ref|name=MWVivec}}

==History==
===Early History===
[[File:ON-prerelease-Vivec's Palace.jpg|thumb|left|The Palace of Vivec]]
[[File:MW-creature-Vivec.jpg|thumb|right|Vivec the Warrior-Poet]]
When Vivec City was built is unknown and its origins are deep rooted in the Tribunal Temple's mythology. Vivec's [[Lore:The 36 Lessons of Vivec|36 lessons]] claimed it was founded in the days of [[Lore:Resdayn|Resdaynia]],{{Ref|name=36LOVS24}} referring to the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]]'s ancient name for Morrowind. The province received its modern name sometime after the first eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] in {{Year|1E 668}}.{{Ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Morrowind}}}} The events that lead to the city's founding in the [[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 24|24th sermon]] take place after the [[Lore:Skyrim Conquests|Skyrim Conquests]] in the third century, as established in previous sermons.{{Ref|name=36LOVS9|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9}}}}{{Ref|name=36LOVS22|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 22}}}} The historical-fiction series ''[[Lore:Poison Song|Poison Song]]'' dates Vivec City's initial construction in the aftermath of the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]] and [[Lore:Sun's Death|Sun's Death]],{{Ref|name=PSVI|{{Cite Book|Poison Song VI}}}}{{Ref|name=PSVII|{{Cite Book|Poison Song VII}}}} which in the series' given timeline is roughly {{Year|1E 685}}.{{Ref|name=PSIII|{{Cite Book|Poison Song III}}}}{{intnote|Dates|[Note 1]}}

As the 24th sermon taught its disciples and pilgrims, after Vivec retrieved the [[Lore:Treasure Wood Sword|Treasure Wood Sword]] from the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], they returned to the Provisional House and looked down into the middle world to see the third of his strongest [[Lore:Monster Children|monster children]], [[Lore:Horde Mountain|Horde Mountain]]. Before he could approached it, he found it trapped by three minor [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] houses. Vivec smiled as they cheered on his arrival and he commenced a grand celebration of love and duty around the captured monster. Vivec combined the three houses to create the [[Lore:Buoyant Armigers|Buoyant Armigers]] and pierced Horde Mountain with his spear, [[Lore:Muatra|Muatra]], turning it into a big bag of bones.{{Ref|name=36LOVS24}}

He threw the bag northeast and the bones fell down, turning into foundation stones for a city while the net became bridges interconnecting them. Its segments were touched by his holy wisdom, creating the most perfect city streets in the known worlds. Vivec named this new city after his own [[Lore:Vehk (letter)|sigil]] and Chimer traveled great distances to see it, filling it with laughter and love, and the strength of the tree-shaped enemy children. Vivec's fellow Tribunal, [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]] and [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] came to give their blessings. Almalexia blessed it with the holy protection of [[Lore:House Indoril|House Indoril]], while Sotha Sil blessed it with a [[Lore:Spells|spell]], one that protected it from [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]'s dark corner and from straying far from the [[Lore:Almsivi|ALMSIVI]].{{Ref|name=36LOVS24}}

Whether there is any truth in Vivec's lessons or not, especially with the [[Lore:New Temple|New Temple]]'s rejection of Tribunal worship and their teachings,{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}} it is generally understood that Vivec's Palace has sat in Norvayn Bay for as long as Vivec decreed it. Pilgrims came far and wide to look in awe, but as more people came, the Temple Canton was built to receive them. With more visitations came the desire to trade, and with this desire came the need for construction and expansion. The Twin Saints cantons, named after Saint [[Lore:Delyn|Delyn the Wise]] and Saint [[Lore:Olms|Olms the Just]], were built, and the Great Houses, Hlaalu and Redoran came to provide their arms and wealth to enrich it.{{Ref|name=LSP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Lawrence Schick's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}}
{{NewLine}}

====The Legends of Lie Rock====
[[File:MW-quest-Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies 03.jpg|thumb|right|The Ministry of Truth]]
A key landmark in Vivec City was the Ministry of Truth, known in legend as [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]]. It origins are varied, between the Temple itself and many of Vivec City's people. The Temple had two origins, one in general myth was that [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]], the Mad Star and the Fourth Corner of the [[Lore:House of Troubles|House of Troubles]], rebelled against the Tribunal by tricking Baar Dau to change its course in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] toward the city, which he called a mockery to the heavens.{{Ref|name=TPP|{{Cite Book|The Pilgrim's Path}}}}

In Vivec's 33rd sermon, it was the seventh of his strongest monster children. After its [[Lore:Pomegranate Banquet|conception]], it hide in the [[Lore:The Serpent|Scaled Blanket]] to access the Hidden Heaven, and haggled with the [[Lore:Lorkhan|Void Ghost]] for ''"a power that no divinity will dare disobey."'' Vivec sent [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]] to destroy Baar Dau. In the heavens, he made peace with the Dominion Planets, the [[Lore:Akatosh (planet)|north]], [[Lore:Arkay (planet)|south]], and [[Lore:Julianos (planet)|third-pole-stars]], who allowed him to wander among their charges and gave him the red sight to find the moonlet. As Nerevar spoke with Lorkhan, however, Baar Dau left in the confusion to launch their attack on Vivec City.{{Ref|name=36LOVS33|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33}}}}

Vivec City's people panicked as they saw the shooting star barrel towards them but Vivec stopped it from falling by suspending it with his own magic. In the general Tribunal myth, Baar Dau was so overwhelmed by Vivec's courage and daring, it swore eternal service to the Tribunal.{{Ref|name=TPP}} But in Vivec's 33rd sermon, he stabbed it with Muatra and kept it in place with its original intentions intact. That way if his people's love and faith for him and his city ever went away, then Baar Dau would resume its fall onto the land.{{Ref|name=36LOVS33}} The meteor was hollowed out and it became the city's headquarters for its Ordinators.{{Ref|name=TPP}}

===Trials and Tribulations in the Second Era===
[[File:ON-place-Vivec City 02.jpg|thumb|left|Vivec City under construction]]
[[File:ON-quest-Divine Disaster.jpg|thumb|right|Baar Dau falling in the sky]]
By the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], there was a desire in Vivec City to expand, resulting in a new project that would build the future Arena, Hlaalu, and Redoran Cantons, with the Telvanni Canton later down the line.{{Ref|name=ONVivec}} In that time, Vivec appointed Canon [[Lore:Tarvus Indoril|Tarvus Indoril]] as his Archcanon.{{Ref|name=MTCAT|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Archcanon Tarvus}}}} But while the city had always been administered by the Temple, its governance during this time was shared with House Redoran, who at that point, controlled Vvardenfell's southern and southwestern coasts.{{Ref|name=ESOMIFOV|{{Cite Book|ESO: Morrowind – Influential Factions of Vvardenfell|ns_base=GEN}}}}

House Redoran also brokered an agreement with House Hlaalu, where they would provide the labor and resources for the construction of their settlements for a very low cost.{{Ref|name=HCS|{{Cite Book|Hlaalu Construction Syndic}}}} Whether the same could have been said about Vivec City is unclear, but while it maintained its Dunmer Metropolitan style, other Redoran towns like [[Lore:Balmora|Balmora]] and [[Lore:Suran|Suran]] were made in House Hlaalu's signature style.{{Ref|name=HCS}} A [[Lore:Granite|granite]] quarry built west of [[Lore:Lake Nabia|Lake Nabia]] near [[Lore:Foyada Mamaea|Foyada Mamaea]] became Vivec City's main supply of raw materials,{{Ref|name=ONLoadFoyada|[[Online:Foyada Quarry|Foyada Quarry]] loading screen text in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} while a woman named Shiralas became the project's overseer.{{Ref|name=ONTarvus}}

In {{Year|2E 582}}, Vivec's power waned and he began an investigation. Using one of his blessing stones from the construction, he confirmed through a divination ritual of strange happenings on Vvardenfell and sent [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] to discover the truth of his behalf.{{Ref|name=ONConundrum|[[Online:Divine Conundrum|Divine Conundrum]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} What they found was the former Clockwork mage named [[Lore:Barilzar|Barilzar]] and his research on the transfer of divine energy, that an [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]] stole a [[Lore:Sunna'rah|mystical staff]] from the [[Lore:Nycotic Cult|Nycotic Cult]] of daedra, and the self-proclaimed [[Lore:Failed Incarnates|Incarnate]] of Nerevar, [[Lore:Conoon Chodala|Conoon Chodala]] and his meeting with a renegade Ashlander group called the [[Lore:Red Exiles|Red Exiles]]. As the situation escalated, however, Vivec sent away all of his priests, with only the outlander, Archcanon Tarvus, and Canon Llevule Andrano allowed to see him in his weaker state.{{Ref|name=ONInquiries|[[Online:Divine Inquiries|Divine Inquiries]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

What they learned was that Chodala had the Red Exiles steal the staff known as [[Lore:Sunna'rah|Sunna'rah]] so that he could use it to steal Vivec's powers and further his claims as Nerevar Incarnate.{{Ref|name=ONInquiries}} But even when he was proven to be a false incarnate, Chodala persisted and continued to drain the god's power. As it continued to leave him, Red Mountain grew more active, but Vivec had enough energy left to hold Baar Dau.{{Ref|name=ONDelusions|[[Online:Divine Delusions|Divine Delusions]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} The Vestige and their Ashlander companion, [[Lore:Seryn|Seryn]] defeated Chodala in the ruins of Kaushtarari, using Barilzar's Tonal Inverter to counteract Sunna'rah. The outlander returned with Sunna'rah to Vivec's Palace and gave it to the Archcanon, who used it to further drain Vivec's power. At that point, he revealed himself to be [[Lore:Barbas|Barbas]], the daedric hound of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], who needed Vivec's divine magic to locate Sotha Sil's [[Lore:Clockwork City|Clockwork City]].{{Ref|name=ONIntervention|[[Online:Divine Intervention|Divine Intervention]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}}

With some of the last vestiges of Vivec's power taken, the magic holding Baar Dau was gone and it resumed its fall onto the city. Canon Llevule devised a plan using one of the blessing stones and restored some of Vivec's energy to keep him alive a little longer.{{Ref|name=ONDisaster|[[Online:Divine Disaster|Divine Disaster]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} With Barilzar's help, the outlander entered the Clockwork City and defeated Barbas. The three heroes, Barilzar, Seryn, and the Vestige returned Vivec his power, who then used it to restore Vvardenfell from the chaos of its near destruction. Vivec realized that Archcanon Tarvus died weeks before Barbas took his identity. After the recent events, he named Llevule Andrano his new Archcanon.{{Ref|name=ONRestoration|[[Online:Divine Restoration|Divine Restoration]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONBlessings|[[Online:Divine Blessings|Divine Blessings]] story quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]''}} In that same time, Bradyn of the Library of Vivec began a project to model all of Vvardenfell and locate the legendary [[Lore:Library of Andule|Library of Andule]], the secret repository of knowledge to the Great Houses. In time, the project was complete and thus began a great deal of research into the ancestry of the Great Houses.{{Ref|name=ONLibrary|[[Online:The Lost Library|The Lost Library]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Vivec City and the Third Empire===
[[File:MW-place-Vivec Foreign.jpg|thumb|right|The Foreign Quarter]]
By the late [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]], [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] and his new [[Lore:Third Empire|Cyrodilic Empire]] began expanding into the other provinces after a series of conflicts known as the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]]. After their successful conquest for [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]], the Empire focused their efforts on Morrowind. Despite their attempts to retaliate, their war ended after Vivec appeared before his people in Vivec City, announcing his negotiation of a treaty. The [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]] was signed with Tiber Septim and it formally incorporated Morrowind as an Imperial Province.{{Ref|name=OM|{{Cite Book|On Morrowind}}}} 

Per the terms of the Armistice, Vvardenfell was deemed a Temple preserve and was uninhabited, save for a few sanctioned Great House settlements and the pre-established Ashlander settlements.{{Ref|name=ASHOM|{{Cite Book|A Short History of Morrowind}}}} Only its major cities, such as Vivec City were made accessible to the Empire.{{Ref|name=KPR|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Ken Rolston's Posts|ns_base=GEN}}}} However, foreigners were denied access to most of the city so its final district, the Foreign Quarter was built on the north end of Norvayn Bay to accommodate them.{{Ref|name=GTV}} Before she was Empress, [[Lore:Katariah Ra'athim|Katariah Ra'athim]] knew the [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] master armorer [[Lore:Hazadir|Hazadir]] when he was just a [[Lore:Slave|slave]] in Vivec City.{{Ref|name=TAC|{{Cite Book|The Armorer's Challenge}}}} The [[Lore:Pirates|pirate]] captain Kaladas ran an active [[Lore:Skooma|skooma]] route between Vivec and [[Lore:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]], sometime before the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper's Invasion|Camoran Usurper's Invasion]] in the mid-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]].{{Ref|name=CDJ1|{{Cite Book|Cap'n Dugal's Journal, Part 1}}}}{{Ref|name=CDJ2|{{Cite Book|Cap'n Dugal's Journal, Part 2}}}}

In {{Year|3E 414}}, the King of Morrowind, [[Lore:Athyn Llethan|Athyn Llethan]] revoked Vvardenfell's status as a preserve and the ban on trade and settlement of the island. As a result, a flood of Imperial colonists and Great House Dunmer came to the island to expand on old settlements.{{Ref|name=ASHOM}} Four years earlier, however, the slowly re-surfacing [[Lore:Sixth House|Sixth House]] established cells in Vivec City's lower-class waterfronts, carefully sowing the seeds of their eventual return.{{Ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} By the city's late years, outlanders were allowed access beyond the Foreign Quarter and to the rest of the city.{{Ref|name=GTV}}

Another byproduct of the revoked ban was the arrival of the neighboring Great Houses, Hlaalu, Redoran, and Telvanni, and the new distribution of territory on Vvardenfell. Vivec City, sacred Temple sites, and practically any unclaimed territory was part of the Temple District.{{Ref|name=ASHOM}} Each of the Great Houses had a presence in Vivec City, in at least their eponymous cantons.{{Ref|name=GTV}} But House Hlaalu owned more land across the Ascadian Isles and some councilors lived in and around the city, like Crassius Curio and Dram Bero in the Hlaalu Compound, Yngling Half-Troll in the St. Olms Canton, and Velanda Omani in Elmas Island, in the East Vivec region.{{Ref|name=YB426|{{Cite Book|Yellow Book of 3E 426}}}}

===Vivec City and Moon-and-Star Reborn===
[[File:MW-npc-Mehra Milo.jpg|thumb|left|Mehra Milo]]
[[File:MW-npc-Tholer Saryoni.jpg|thumb|right|Archcanon Tholer Saryoni]]
In {{Year|3E 427}}, amid the [[Lore:Vvardenfell Crisis|Vvardenfell Crisis]], a [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] agent was sent by their spymaster, [[Lore:Caius Cosades|Caius Cosades]] to gather intel on the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]], their [[Lore:Nerevarine Cult|associated cult]], and the Sixth House cult in Vivec City. These informants included a Thieves Guild smuggler, a Morag Tong thrall, and a Temple Mehra named Milo.{{Ref|name=MWInformants|[[Morrowind:Vivec Informants|Vivec Informants]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Having been watched by the Ordinators, however, she was taken captive to the Ministry of Truth. The Agent, now on the path to becoming the Nerevarine, rescued her and escaped with [[Lore:Scrolls|scrolls]] of [[Lore:Divine Intervention|Divine Intervention]] to [[Lore:Castle Ebonheart|Castle Ebonheart]]. From there, they fled to the [[Lore:Holamayan|Holamayan Monastery]], home to the [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]].{{Ref|name=MWMilo|[[Morrowind:Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies|Mehra Milo and the Lost Prophecies]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The Nerevarine, from then on, continued their path toward fulfilling the [[Lore:Nerevarine Prophecy|Nerevarine Prophecy]] by completing its trials, and becoming recognized by the Ashlander tribes and the Great Houses as the Nerevarine and Hortator respectively. Their quest frequently brought them to Vivec City, like when they dueled the Redoran Archmaster [[Lore:Bolvyn Venim|Bolvyn Venim]] in the Vivec City Arena,{{Ref|name=MWRedoran|[[Morrowind:Redoran Hortator|Redoran Hortator]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} or found Hlaalu Councilor Dram Bero in the "haunted" house of the St. Olms Plaza.{{Ref|name=MWHlaalu|[[Morrowind:Hlaalu Hortator|Hlaalu Hortator]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

However, the Tribunal Temple in the High Fane, through the [[Lore:Order of the Inquisition|Order of the Inquisition]] began their own investigations on them, considering its heretical and contradictory nature to the temple. Archordinator [[Lore:Berel Sala|Berel Sala]] discovered their close ties to the Blades and their association to the [[Lore:Uriel VII|Emperor]]'s close interest, further questioning their validity and motives. Archcanon [[Lore:Tholer Saryoni|Tholer Saryoni]] arranged a formal, yet secret meeting with the Nerevarine to determine the truth.{{Ref|name=NFTA|{{Cite Book|Note from the Archcanon}}}} Having acknowledged their claims as both Hortator and Nerevarine, and with the threat of [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] and the Sixth House reaching its peak, Tholer Saryoni acknowledged a needed change in the Temple's policies and allowed them to met with Vivec privately in his palace, where they would discuss plans to defeat the Sharmat.{{Ref|name=MWNerevarine|[[Morrowind:Hortator and Nerevarine|Hortator and Nerevarine]] story quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

In that same time, several events took place in and around Vivec City. A string of murders occurred across the city with seven confirmed victims, five outlanders, and two Ordinators. Elam Andas of the [[Lore:Order of the Watch|Order of the Watch]] began an investigation and learned that each of the outlanders had only been in Vvardenfell for less than a week. Three were found in the Foreign Quarter, one in the Hlaalu Compound, and another in the water by the Arena. The first theory was that it was an anti-Imperial fanatic, but two Ordinators were later killed. The victims had their throats slit and neither of the Ordinators had their possessions taken. Following a lead about one outlander being threatened by a Dunmer woman, an adventurer hired by Elam Andas found the killer, a Sixth House Dreamer Prophet and ending her spree.{{Ref|name=MWKillings|[[Morrowind:Mysterious Killings in Vivec|Mysterious Killings in Vivec]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

With the Sixth House rampant at this time, Berel Sara, leader of the Ordinators in Vivec City and Temple fanatic used his influence to fight back, superceding control over the Archcanon, ignoring Temple policy and causing issues with Duke [[Lore:Vedam Dren|Vedam Dren]] and House Hlaalu. Berel Sala and Tholer Saryoni came to an understanding after a House Father of House Hlaalu recovered the lost [[Lore:Robe of St. Roris|Robe of St. Roris]] and the Archcanon presented it during reconciliation.{{Ref|name=MWControl|[[Morrowind:Control the Ordinators|Control the Ordinators]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The Guildmaster of the Vvardenfell Fighters Guild, [[Lore:Sjoring Hard-Heart|Sjoring Hard-Heart]] was killed in the Vivec City chapter but the circumstances are unclear. Secretly, he was connected to the [[Lore:Camonna Tong|Camonna Tong]] and used his influence to corrupt the Fighters Guild and target the burgeoning Thieves Guild.{{Ref|name=MWPercius|[[Morrowind:Percius Mercius]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=MWStacey|[[Morrowind:Gentleman Jim Stacey|Gentleman Jim Stacey]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Whether his death came from someone within the Fighters Guild or someone in the Thieves Guild is unknown, but the Camonna Tong lost any influence in the Fighters Guild and the Thieves Guild survived potential destruction had Sjoring attacked their guild chapters.{{Ref|name=MWHeartFG|[[Morrowind:Kill Hard-Heart (Fighters Guild)|Kill Hard-Heart (Fighters Guild)]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{Ref|name=MWHeartTG|[[Morrowind:Kill Hard-Heart (Thieves Guild)|Kill Hard-Heart (Thieves Guild)]] faction quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Post-Nerevarine and the Red Year===
{{Quote Box|''It was just deathly quiet. I felt a tingling sensation all over my body and my guar began to stomp around. Whatever it was, it was driving him crazy. As I tried to get him under control, there was a massive explosion from the center of the city. I saw the cantons fall apart before I was knocked off my feet.<br /><br />Then I remember the ground starting to rumble. It lasted for a long time and it receded into the distance as if directed towards the center of Vvardenfell. A few minutes later, the Red Mountain erupted, sending a huge cloud of fire into the sky.''|Saldus Llervu's account of the Red Year{{Ref|name=TRYII}}}}
As willed by the Nerevarine Prophecy, the Heart of Lorkhan was destroyed and the Tribunals' power faded. While Almalexia and Sotha Sil's fates are known and ceased, Vivec's fate is undetermind.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Morrowind}}}} By the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], Vivec was proclaimed missing, with a persistant rumor being that they were taken by daedra.{{Ref|name=OBRumors|[[Oblivion:Radiant Conversations#Dialogue by Topic|Radiant Conversations]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} But even without his presence, Vivec City remained an active settlement,{{Ref|name=DJ|{{Cite Book|Drothan's Journal}}}}{{Ref|name=LH|{{Cite Book|Letter Home|ns_base=OB}}}} but there was always an underlining threat literally hanging over the city.

With Vivec gone, the magic that held the Ministry of Truth was also fading. Some experts feared that it may have maintained its velocity while held by Vivec's magic,{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 4}} much like how Vivec described it in his 33rd sermon.{{Ref|name=36LOVS33}} And so some of the best minds in Morrowind, such as [[Lore:Vuhon|Vuhon]] and [[Lore:Sul|Sul]] created a machine feuled by [[Lore:Souls|souls]] to hold it called the [[Lore:Ingenium|Ingenium]]. It was designed to leave open a vent into Oblivion, specifically to the realm of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]], the [[Lore:Fields of Regret|Fields of Regret]], where the souls are sent and energy is exchanged to the Ministry.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 3}} 

How long the ingenium held the Ministry for is unknown, but the city remained active for eleven years since the arrival of the Nerevarine. At first, as many as ten slaves and prisoners were slaughtered a day. But Vuhon discovered a means to use living souls, but only certain souls that he, put simply, were "large" enough to sustain it for months at a time. By then, he only needed twelve subjects. But the incident that led to the Ministry's fall occurred in {{Year|4E 5}} when Vuhon used Sul's lover, [[Lore:Ilzheven|Ilzheven]] as a resource. Sul broke into the ingenium chamber and tried to cut Ilzheven free before a fight ensued with Vuhon. The conflict caused damage to the ingenium, resulting in Baar Dau's fall and impact onto Vivec City.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 10}}

From the personal account of a trader named Saldus Llervu, it was deathly quiet and tense when the Ministry fell. As he tried to calm his [[Lore:Guar|guar]], a massive explosion came from the center of Vivec City and the cantons fell apart. The crash caused a chain reaction where Red Mountain erupted only minutes later,{{Ref|name=TRYII}} and for the first time since the [[Lore:Days of Fire|Days of Fire]] in {{Year|2E 882}}.{{Ref|name=TPP}}{{Ref|name=DUP}} After the first day in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]], people received several reports of widespread destruction in Vivec City and the other cities, stemming from Red Mountain's eruption. Countless lives were lost and not a single night passed for months were people not openly mourning the loss of loved ones. The past year is remembered as the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]]{{Ref|name=TRYII}} and the Dunmer's faith in the Tribunal was practically diminshed.{{Ref|name=TR}}

The impact created the [[Lore:Scathing Bay|Scathing Bay]], a large, perfectly circled bay of boiling water, with a rim standing slightly above the water and opened into a larger body. All that was left of Vivec City was an island ruin of shattered stone and ash that smelled horrible, but was overgrown with colorful wildflowers and the tall [[Lore:Emperor Parasol|Emperor Parasols]] of Morrowind.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 9}} Taking advantage of the province's weakened state, the Argonians of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]] invaded only a year later in the [[Lore:Accession War|Accession War]].{{Ref|name=LTJ|{{Cite Book|Lymdrenn Tenvanni's Journal}}}} By {{Year|4E 48}}, the Scathing Bay fell under Argonian occupation and they performed some kind of ritual around it.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|Pt. 3, Ch. 9}} After the Red Year, a relief effort from Mournhold was made to restore Vvardenfell's settlements, with Balmora on the track toward recovery.{{Ref|name=TRYII}} It is unknown if there was an effort for Vivec City, especially with the Argonians' prolonged claim over the Scathing Bay.

==Culture and Society==
[[File:MW-npc-Dalse Adren.jpg|thumb|left|A Vivec City Gondolier]]
[[File:MW-interior-Arena Canton.jpg|thumb|right|The Vivec City Arena]]
:''For more information, see the: '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Culture|Culture]]''' and '''[[Lore:Dunmer#Society|Society]]''' sections on the Dunmer page.''
As previously one of the largest cities in eastern Tamriel, Vivec City boasted a dense population, as did its sister city to the west, Castle Ebonheart. Hundreds of tourists and pilgrims flocked to the city daily to see the High Fane and the Ministry of Truth, as well as offer prayers and thanks at the Palace of Vivec. Only the most devout were allowed to see Vivec in person at his palace.{{Ref|name=GTV}} Perhaps one of the last people to see him before his disappearance was [[Lore:Jiub|Jiub]], who had slain seventy-six [[Lore:Cliff Racer|cliff racers]] and attained sainthood by Vivec in front of his throne.{{Ref|name=SJO|{{Cite Book|Saint Jiub's Opus}}}}

The Saint Delyn and Olms Cantons were Vivec City's centers of population and trade. Commoners and paupers lived in apartments strewn around its outer streets while craftspeople ran their shops in spaces inside the canton. These shops, which included the Brewers and Fishmongers, Farmers and Laborers, Glassworkers, Potters, Tailors and Dyers, and Tanners and Miners Halls, are where Vvardenfell's crafts and light industries were based.{{Ref|name=GTV}} It was the craftsmer of Vivec that created rust funnelcap paint, in a process of grinding dried funnelcap mushrooms into paste that is as simple as it is potent.{{Ref|name=ONRustAnti|[[Online:Telvanni Peninsula Antiquities#Rust Funnelcap Paint|Rust Funnelcap Paint]] antiquity codex entries in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}}

The [[Lore:Sweeps|Sweeps]] were a guild of broom-handlers who made good money cleaning large city streets like in Vivec of volcanic ash from Red Mountain.{{Ref|name=IWADE|{{Cite Book|Interview with a Dark Elf|ns_base=GEN}}}} The city's main form of transportation were its gondolas, who shuttled passengers through the canals for a low-price.{{Ref|name=GTV}} While its port-of-call for larger ships and silt striders was North Landing.{{Ref|name=MWGenS}} Vivec City's Arena was famous in its heyday and a popular tourist site. Many shows from mock battles to real gladiatorial combat, and even theater were held there. The Arena could hold hundreds of spectators, while the waistworks were used by entertainers and combatants between performances.{{Ref|name=GTV}} The city even had its own gladiatorial team called the Vivec City Cliff Racers, who previously fought in a pan-Tamrielic event called the Dragon Games.{{Ref|name=CSGlory|[[Castles:Gladiatorial Glory|Gladiatorial Glory]] event in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}

The Great House cantons were significant centers of commerce for their respective factions, the Hlaalu, Redoran, and Telvanni. Each of the districts contained an active administrative center including a local treasury, hall of records, and holding cells. In the Redoran canton were two noble clans, the Dralor and Saren families, who lived in manor houses in the plaza. House Telvanni only rented their compound from the Temple, which is why they lived in Velothi towers rather than their preferred mushroom towers. Telvanni services were available in the top layers while [[Lore:Slave|slaves]] and monsters were held together in its lowest quarters.{{Ref|name=GTV}} 

In the Foreign Quarter and Hlaalu Compound canalworks were the Foreign Quarter Tomb and Hlaalu Ancestral Vaults respectively.{{Ref|name=MWVivec}} Cosmopolitan cities of Morrowind like [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]], [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]] and Vivec City were more open-minded with interring deceased outlanders, unlike smaller, tight-knit communities. But still, it was unlikely for someone to find more than a handful of foreign names in their crypts.{{Ref|name=LAHT|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive - House Telvanni}}}} Vivec City, on the other hand, had the Foreign Quarter Tomb, presumingly for the burial of foreign races and it was fairly expansive.{{Ref|name=MWVivec}} The Hlaalu Ancestral Vaults' purpose was unknown, but the Hlaalu family had another ancestral tomb on Vvardenfell, the Hlaalu Ancestral Tomb south of [[Lore:Molag Mar|Molag Mar]] and east of [[Lore:Zaintiraris|Zaintiraris]].{{Ref|name=MWTomb|[[Morrowind:Hlaalu Ancestral Tomb|Hlaalu Ancestral Tomb]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Temple Worship and Legend===
[[File:MW-place-Ministry of Truth.jpg|thumb|left|The Ministry of Truth suspended above the Temple Canton]]
{{Quote Box|''I raise lanterns to light my hollows, lend wax to the thousands the candlesticks that bear my name again and again, the name innumerable, shutting in, mantra and priest, god-city, filling every corner with the naming name, wheeled, circling, running river language giggling with footfalls mating, selling, stealing, searching, and worry not ye who walk with me.''<br /><br />''This is the flowering scheme of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]]. This is the promise of the [[Lore:PSJJJJ|PSJJJJ]]: egg, image, man, god, city, state. I serve and am served. I am made of wire and string and mortar and I accede my own precedent, world without am.''|Excerpt from the [[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 25|Scripture of the City]]}}
Being the city of a god, sometimes called the '''City of the Warrior-Poet''',{{Ref|name=CTV|{{Cite Book|Come to Vvardenfell!|ns_base=ON}}}} Vivec City was associated with the many practices and stories of the Tribunal Temple. The Scripture of the City is the 25th sermon of Vivec's 36 lessons. In the beginning he calls it, '''God's City''' and describes it as an extension of himself, or as he puts it ''"rebuilding myself"''. Vivec ends the scripture by claiming it was the flowering scheme of the [[Lore:Aurbis|Aurbis]] and the promise of the [[Lore:PSJJJJ|PSJJJJ]].{{Ref|name=36LOVS25|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 25}}}}

Pilgrims of the temple traveled all over Vvardenfell to visit the Shrines of the Seven Graces, a plaque or three-sided triolith with an inscription of its associated story. Three of these shrines were located in Vivec City, the Shrines of Generosity, Courtesy, and Daring.{{Ref|name=TPP}} The Shrine of Generosity sat at the top of the stairs to Vivec's Palace and was there to celebrate the heroes that died in the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]], as well as the common people that also suffered and died. A donation of 100 gold was left there as a sign of good fortune.{{Ref|name=TPP}}

The Shrine of Courtesy lied at the heart of the Puzzle Canal, the labyrinth of waterways under Vivec's Palace. It was dedicated to a time when Vivec battled Mehrunes Dagon and handed him his own [[Lore:Sword Not Held|weapon]] so that they could fight on equal terms. This act of chivalry moved the [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]], Mehrunes Dagon's most honorable and chivalrous servants, that they developed a bond of respect and courtesy with the Tribunal's followers.{{Ref|name=TPP}} For much of Vivec City's history, the Puzzle Canal became a place of worship to test heroes who hoped to earn Vivec's favor.{{Ref|name=GTV}} A [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] named Krazzt stood guard at the shrine, awaiting a pilgrim to present them with a plain [[Lore:Silver|silver]] [[Lore:Long Blade|longsword]].{{Ref|name=TPP}}

The Shrine of Daring was dedicated to when Vivec stopped Baar Dau's fall with his magic. A potion of [[Lore:Levitate|rising force]] was customary as a offering to the shrine.{{Ref|name=TPP}} Baar Dau's legend is known in Tribunal literature but the city's people to the Temple itself have different ideas of its origins. While the Temple and Vivec have differing accounts in ''[[Lore:The Pilgrim's Path|The Pilgrim's Path]]'' and the ''36 Lessons of Vivec'',{{Ref|name=TPP}}{{Ref|name=36LOVS33}} the common folk gave their own theories, as published by the Imperial chronicler, Minerva Calo. The names of each citizen was changed to avoid Temple reprisal. A Dunmer merchant named Tolvasa recounts when her mother told many bedtime stories about Baar Dau, her favorite being ''The Tale of the Lonely [[Lore:Magna Ge|Magna Ge]]'', in which the former Star-Orphan comforts [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] as kindred spirits in the lonely expanse of [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]].{{Ref|name=TOBD|{{Cite Book|Testimonials on Baar Dau}}}}

Instead of interpreting it as one of Vivec's monster children, the Dunmer scholar Tiram believed it was a chrysalis for the last monster child, and that once it hatched, Vivec would appear from his palace and battle it for 47 days until it broke into 36 pieces. An [[Lore:Orc|Orc]] named Narkhozug called it ''"a giant hunk of dung"'' created by [[Lore:Malacath|Malacath]] after Vivec bad mouthed [[Lore:Ogre|ogres]]. Some people had less fantastical ideas, like the Nord miner Hodstag, who believed it was a gold mine rife with precious metals or the longshoreman Donoven, who just dismissed it as a rock. The Order of the Inquisition, when presented with the question, suggested to leave such matters with the Temple and that its precise nature is a mystery.{{Ref|name=TOBD}}

In Vivec's thirtieth sermon, he fights the sixth of his strongest monster child, who was born as [[Lore:Ha-Note|Ha-Note]]. Having avoided Vivec's wrath, Ha-Note traveled sideways to the [[Lore:Adjacent Place|Adjacent Place]] and was brought in by the [[Lore:Grabbers|Grabbers]]. The Grabbers, who had never built their own city, was jealous of Vivec City and decided to build their "tower-hope" on Ha-Note's face. Vivec eventually faced the new Ha-Note known as [[Lore:City-Face|City-Face]] and killed it by stabbing its heart with the [[Lore:Ethos Knife|Ethos Knife]].{{Ref|name=36LOVS30|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 30}}}}
