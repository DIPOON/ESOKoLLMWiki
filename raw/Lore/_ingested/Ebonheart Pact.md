The [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]] was a military alliance between the [[Lore:Great House|Great House]]s [[Lore:House Dres|Dres]], [[Lore:House Hlaalu|Hlaalu]], [[Lore:House Indoril|Indoril]], and [[Lore:House Redoran|Redoran]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]], the Kingdom of Eastern [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], and the tribes of [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] and [[Lore:Thornmarsh|Thornmarsh]] in [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]{{ref|name=FATS|{{Cite Book|From Argonian to Saxhleel}}}} during the chaotic [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] of the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]. It vied with the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] and [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]] for control over the contested Ruby Throne in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] during the [[Lore:Three Banners War|Three Banners War]]. It was a creation of unlikely allies, who had long histories of strife between them, but united for mutual defense, first against [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]i invaders and later against the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedric]] cultists of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] in Cyrodiil. They aimed to wipe away the rash rule of the [[Lore:Empire|Empire]] and end once and for all mortals' entanglements with higher power from beyond [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{ref|name=TTOTEP}} The Pact was led by [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn]] the Skald-King, who headed the [[Lore:Great Moot|Great Moot]], as well as the three [[Lore:Tribunal|living gods]] of the Dunmer.<noinclude>
{{TOCright}}
The full extent of the Pact's territory in Black Marsh is disputed. Vicecanon [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]], who was called by the [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] to lead the Argonians to join the Pact, claimed that the tribes of [[Lore:Murkmire|Murkmire]] joined together with Shadowfen and Thornmarsh.{{ref|name=FATS}} However, Murkmire is a region on the southern coast of Black Marsh and centered on [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]]; although the Pact sent envoys to Murkmire to convince the tribes to join, the region was far removed from Pact lands and remained fully autonomous. Locals discounted Heita-Meen's claims as being purely aspirational.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Loremaster's Archive: Murkmire Q&A}}}} Some sources incorrectly{{ref|Location of [[ON:Murkmire|Murkmire]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} name the region surrounding the city of [[Lore:Gideon|Gideon]] as Murkmire.{{ref|name=Sybil|{{Cite Book|Augustine Viliane Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=Heita|{{Cite Book|Heita-Meen Answers Your Questions}}}} Given that this region borders Shadowfen and Thornmarsh, it is possible that Heita-Meen's claims refer to Gideon's hinterlands.{{intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}

==History==
The Pact arose out of the [[Lore:Second Akaviri Invasion|Second Akaviri Invasion]] in {{Year|2E 572}}.{{ref|name=GTTEP|{{Cite Book|Guide to the Ebonheart Pact}}}} The force under the command of [[Lore:Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal|Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal]] landed just north of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]], taking the city completely by surprise.{{ref|name=TSAI|{{Cite Book|The Second Akaviri Invasion}}}} The city was sacked and Queen [[Lore:Mabjaarn Flame-Hair|Mabjaarn]] and her heir [[Lore:Nurnhilde|Nurnhilde]] were slain, leaving the crown to Mabjaarn's son, Jorunn.{{ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} Together with his twin-brother Fildgor, he rallied a desperate defense, but the Akaviri army bypassed him, marching instead into eastern Morrowind, where they were met by Dunmeri forces under [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]]. In an unprecedented show of cooperation between races with a great deal of historical enmity, Jorunn and Almalexia worked together to encircle Dir-Kamal's forces in [[Lore:Stonefalls|Stonefalls]].{{ref|name=UA|{{Cite Book|Unexpected Allies}}}} The resulting [[Lore:Battle of Vivec's Antlers|Battle of Vivec's Antlers]] was a close-fought engagement until the surprise arrival of [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] reinforcements, who helped drive the invaders back into the sea, ending their threat to Tamriel.{{ref|name=TSAI}}  

This allegiance forged in battle was ratified with treaties signed in [[Lore:Ebonheart|Ebonheart]],{{ref|name=UA}} resulting in the manumission of Argonians enslaved by the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] as thanks for their crucial intervention.{{ref|name=GTTEP}} The Pact struggled to reconcile the desires of three such distinct factions, but was able to stay strong for over a decade due to the need for mutual defense and the camaraderie of brothers-in-arms.{{ref|name=UA}} Eventually, the Tribunal became aware of dark happenings in Cyrodiil, and called upon its allies to mount a righteous war against the corrupt and disintegrating Empire.{{ref|name=BTCOT|{{Cite Book|Breaking the Cycle of Tyranny}}}}

During its existence, it successfully overcame an invasion  by the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], through summoning the former [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] warriors, the [[Lore:Brothers of Strife (creature)|Brothers of Strife]]. It faced trouble in the form of {{Lore Link|Maulborn}} and  [[Lore:Stormfist Clan|Stormfist]] uprisings in [[Lore:Deshaan|Deshaan]] and [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], respectively. [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] was attacked by the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]], and [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]] was attacked by [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] and the [[Lore:Worm Cult|Worm Cult]]. However, the Pact managed to deal with all these threats.{{ref|name=ESO|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

== Institutions ==
=== Great Moot ===
[[File:ON-place-Great Moot.jpg|thumb|right|A Great Moot]]
The Ebonheart Pact was governed by a separate body called the Great Moot, wherein decisions were ratified by representatives from the Pact's three member nations. The Great Moot convened in the Pact's capital of [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{Ref|[[General:Ask Us Anything: Ebonheart Pact (Part 2)]]}} In {{Year|2E 572}}, [[Lore:Jorunn|Jorunn the Skald-King]] of Eastern Skyrim was named acting High King of the Great Moot after he thwarted an [[Lore:Akavir|Akaviri]] invasion with the aid of [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonian]] forces.{{ref|name=JTSK|{{Cite Book|Jorunn the Skald-King (book)}}}} In {{Year|2E 582}}, Jorunn's rule was again ratified at the summit of [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn]] following his brother [[Lore:Fildgor|Fildgor Orcthane]]'s failed attempt to usurp the throne.{{ref|Events of [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

=== Vicecanons ===
On the regional level, vicecanons represented each of the three Pact provinces of Skyrim, Morrowind and Black Marsh to oversee the safety and prosperity of that region. For example, in {{Year|2E 582}} the Black Marsh region of [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]] was overseen by vicanons Hrondar (Skyrim), Servyna (Morrowind) and [[Lore:Heita-Meen|Heita-Meen]] (Black Marsh) from its capital [[Lore:Stormhold|Stormhold]]. Vicecanons were selected based on their strategic and diplomatic skills.{{ref|Dialogue of [[Online:Aide Diina|Aide Diina]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
