[[Lore:Odahviing|Odahviing]] is a red [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] whose name means '''Winged Snow Hunter'''{{ref|name=SREsbern|[[SR:Esbern|Esbern]]'s dialogue in ''[[sr:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} (or literally '''Snow Hunter Wing''') in the [[Lore:Dragon Language|Dragon Language]]. Not much is known about his life prior to his resurrection by [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]]. It is possible he originates from [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]], as it has been said to be the homeland of red Dragons.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Mysterious Akavir}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1Cyro|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Cyrodiil}}}}

He was likely slain some time in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], perhaps during the [[Lore:Dragon War|Dragon War]], although his soul was not devoured. Like many dragons, his remains were retrieved by members of the [[Lore:Dragon Cult|Dragon Cult]], and he was buried in a dragon mound near the [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]] city of [[Lore:Riften|Riften]]. The location of his grave was discovered through interrogation of Dragon Cultists by the [[Lore:Akavir|Akaviri]] [[Lore:Dragonguard|Dragonguard]], and was later documented by the [[Lore:Blades|Blades]] in {{Year|2E 373}}.{{ref|name=AOD|{{Cite Book|Atlas of Dragons}}}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, Odahviing was returned to life by Alduin and became his ally. After the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] failed to defeat Alduin on [[Lore:Mundus|Mundus]], Odahviing was targeted for interrogation. The Dragonborn called his name through the use of the [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]], which dragons perceive as a challenge. By following the call, Odahviing was lured into {{Lore Link|Dragonsreach}}, the castle in [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] which was used by [[Lore:Olaf One-Eye|King Olaf One-Eye]] to imprison the dragon [[Lore:Numinex|Numinex]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]]. With the help of the Jarl of Whiterun, the Dragonborn used an ancient dragon trap to capture Odahviing. Ensnared and defeated, Odahviing spoke with the Dragonborn and revealed that Alduin had retreated to [[Lore:Sovngarde|Sovngarde]]. In return for his freedom, he agreed to bring the Dragonborn to [[Lore:Skuldafn|Skuldafn Temple]], an ancient ruin located high in the [[Lore:Velothi Mountains|Velothi Mountains]] reachable only by flight, where a portal to Sovngarde was located.{{ref|name=Skyrim|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

After Alduin's defeat in Sovngarde, Odahviing allied himself with the Dragonborn and provided aid in battle when called. He was uninterested in the [[Lore:Way of the Voice|Way of the Voice]], and thought that [[Lore:Paarthurnax|Paarthurnax]] was foolish for trying to convert the remaining dragons to the philosophy. Odahviing remained at the [[Lore:Throat of the World|Throat of the World]], seemingly of no great concern to the reformed Blades.{{ref|name=Skyrim}}<noinclude>
