The [[Lore:Imperial|Imperials]], also known as '''Cyrodiils''',{{ref|name=PGE1C}} '''Cyrodilics''',{{ref|name=PGE1Ham|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Cyrodiil}}}} and '''Imperial Cyrods''',{{ref|name=ImperialCyrods|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 10: Imperial Style}}}} are the well-educated and well-spoken natives of the civilized and cosmopolitan province of [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]]. Imperials are also known for the discipline and training of their citizen armies, and their respect for the rule of law.{{ref|name=LB|{{Cite Book|Legal Basics}}}} Imperials have proved to be shrewd diplomats and traders, and these traits, along with their remarkable skill and training as infantry, have enabled them to colonize various other nations and create the Empire.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Imperial|Morrowind character creation description]]}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Provinces of Tamriel}}}} Their hegemony has waxed and waned throughout the eras, and most historians refer to three distinct Empires, the ends of which each mark a new epoch in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]ic history.{{FMI}}

'''Cyrodiil''' is a pretty blanket term for almost every element of the Imperial Province: the people are called '''the Cyrodiils''', the land is called '''Cyrodiil''', the capital city of their province is called '''[[Lore:Imperial City|Cyrodiil]]''', and the ruler of the land no matter their race is called '''[[Lore:Emperor|The Cyrodiil]]'''. This is derived from a king-is-the-land tradition that reaches back to when the [[Lore:Ayleid|Elves]] ruled the land.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=CyrusComic|''[[Books:The Origin of Cyrus!/Transcription#Chain Mail: Letters to The Elder Scrolls Adventures|The Origin of Cyrus!]]''}}{{Ref|name=LOMap|[[:File:LO-map-Cyrodiil (Morrowind Codex).png|Map of Cyrodiil — Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}}<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:LO-map-Cyrodiil (Oblivion Codex).jpg|left|thumb|[[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], the home of the Imperials]]
[[File:MER-art-The High Priestess (crop).jpg|right|thumb|Alessia]]
The Imperial race emerged out of the original Nibenese tribesman, [[Lore:Nede|Nede]]s and [[Lore:Cyro-Nord|Cyro-Nords]] in the [[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]].{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}{{ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Skyrim}}}} They were subjugated by a race of [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]], the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleids]], but a rebellion against these rulers, formed under the guidance of [[Lore:Alessia|Saint Alessia]] in {{Year|1E 242}}, would prove so successful that Ayleids were driven to extinction (though they left behind many treasures{{ref|name=MFTS|{{Cite Book|Magic from the Sky}}}}).{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=TLKOTA|{{Cite Book|The Last King of the Ayleids}}}} The [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]] united Imperials and pursued campaigns in other provinces, but when the [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]n west broke away, the resulting civil war in Cyrodiil ended the Empire.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}

The eastern Nibenay Valley region and the western Colovian Estates developed distinct cultures, but still united under the Colovian [[Lore:Reman I|Reman I]] to repel an [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]i invasion in {{Year|1E 2703}}, creating the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]].{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}} The Akaviri had devastated many human lands, and Reman feared invasion from the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]], so he allowed the remaining Akaviri to live and fight for him against the Elves.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} Soon the Second Empire controlled every province of Tamriel except Morrowind.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} The events surrounding the attempted conquest of Morrowind led to the end of the Reman emperors, and the First Era, in {{Year|1E 2920}}: the Akaviri [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]], who had earned a position as a trusted advisor, took control following the deaths of the Remans and the disastrous [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]].{{ref|name=2920L|{{Cite Book|2920, The Last Year of the First Era}}}}

The [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]]s would rule until {{Year|2E 430}}, but eventually assassinations and poor leadership would make the Empire crumble, leading to the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]: for four hundred years, petty states all over Tamriel quarreled with each other, especially Cyrodiil.{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE3AS|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Arena Supermundus}}}} The Imperial Province devolved into a collection of warlords squabbling over a no-man's land of cultural stagnation.{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=DHAC|{{Cite Book|Dwemer History and Culture}}}} Eventually, it would take a [[Lore:Nord|Nord]] to reunite the Imperial Province: Tiber Septim was appointed general of the forces of [[Lore:Cuhlecain|Cuhlecain]], a petty Colovian king. He routed all of Cuhlecain's enemies who would face him and his armies on the battlefield, quickly reuniting Cyrodiil. When Cuhlecain was assassinated in {{Year|2E 854}}, Tiber Septim assumed the throne.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} In a few decades, the provinces would be reunited once more under his [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]], and this once-outsider to Cyrodiil had become the symbol of the whole realm.{{ref|name=PGE3C}}

Ironically enough, most if not all of the Emperors of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Age]] would be Nordic or [[Lore:Breton|Breton]], not Imperial. Following the cataclysms of the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the leaderless Empire would once again begin to crumble, but it was an Imperial who seized the opportunity for glory: [[Lore:Titus Mede|Titus Mede]], a Colovian king, would seize the Imperial City with less than a thousand men and declared himself the new Emperor, though not much is yet known about this new dynasty.{{ref|name=TIC|{{Cite Book|The Infernal City|ns_base=Books}}}}

==Culture==
[[File:RG-scene-Introduction 18.jpg|thumb|left||Imperial Soldiers]]
The defining feature of Imperial culture is its cosmopolitanism.{{ref|name=PGE3C}} In an effort to make his home a cosmopolitan land, Reman I took elements from the Akaviri, Colovia, Nibenay, as well as from the distant province of High Rock, and incorporated it to a "common whole".{{ref|name=PGE3C|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} Although some individual Imperials may be the most arrogant bigots in Tamriel,{{ref|name=GTA|{{Cite Book|Guide to Anvil}}}}{{ref|name=GTB|{{Cite Book|Guide to Bravil}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition}}}} culturally the Imperials are very conciliatory and congenial, making them skilled diplomats and traders.{{ref|name=PGE3C}} Even Imperial [[Lore:Vampire|Vampire]]s claim some semblance of civility.{{ref|name=MCV|{{Cite Book|Manifesto Cyrodiil Vampyrum}}}} It's easy to understand given their geography and cultural schisms: Cyrodiil borders almost every other province, and the historical separation yet underlying unity and interaction of the Cyrodilic regions made the Imperials well practiced at building bridges and maintaining order. Though there are many regions, the two most powerful effectively controlled all regions close to them: the western Colovian Estates and the eastern Nibenay Valley.

Early Imperials in eastern Cyrodiil strongly believed in the Alessian Doctrines, even after the Empire's fall, which made them disfavor many practices, notably farming.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} They defaulted to mercantilism, and became a wealthy merchant power with a vibrant society that made colorful clothing, conducted elaborate ceremonies, and loved tattoos and philosophy.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} Through their river network, they were able to export many goods, including textiles, fabrics, moon sugar, rice and armor.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} Though their beliefs were monotheistic, they often focused on the numinous nature of some facet of society, and many diverse cults operated openly over the years, dedicated to many animal spirits and hero-gods, as well as ancestor worship.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} The Colovians, in contrast, took on a more austere but adventuresome lifestyle, in keeping with their Nordic ancestry, and often became mercenaries and pirates.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=SATD}} The unification under Reman I didn't eradicate these differences, although, of course, the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] was predominant all over the province by the end of the First Era.{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}}

The respect and admiration Imperials had for Tiber Septim is nothing compared to the veneration they have for him as a god of the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]], regardless of the heresies often espoused about him.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} His accomplishments inspired them to believe they could do anything they set their mind to.{{ref|name=TKOTN|{{Cite Book|The Knights of the Nine}}}} The end of his dynasty was greeted with great mourning, although also with great pride, as his last known descendant, [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]], sacrificed himself to save all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=Oblivion|Events of [[OB:Light the Dragonfires|Light the Dragonfires]] in {{g|Oblivion}}}} Imperials know all too well that dynasties are destined to rise and fall; all it will take is time before the Glorious Empire once again reigns over Tamriel.

===Architecture===
Many Imperial buildings were constructed from limestone.{{ref|name=Limestone| Description of [[Blades:Limestone|Limestone]] in ''[[Blades:Blades|Blades]]''}} The Nibenese city of Bruma is Nordic influenced, while Cheydinhal draws from Dunmeri inspiration.{{ref|name=OBLoading|[[Oblivion:Loading Screens|Loading screen]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The Redguards have had a notable influence on [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]]'s architecture, which is of a similar style to that of nearby [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{Ref|name=OBWilbur|[[Oblivion:Wilbur|Wilbur]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} "Western Imperial Style" is an architectural style synonymous with villages throughout the western Empire. This Western Imperial Architecture is seen in places such as [[Lore:Pelagiad|Pelagiad]] and [[Lore:Caldera|Caldera]], and if one were not knowledgeable of it, they can be easily mistaken for a city in High Rock.{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue C|Caldera]] dialogue topic in {{g|mw}}}}{{ref|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue P|Pelagaid]] dialogue topic in {{g|mw}}}} Western Imperial Architecture mixes with the Dunmer urban style architecture in parts of the Hlaalu District and Vivec suburbs in Vvardenfell, it dominates in new Hlaalu and Imperial chartered towns. Houses, shops, and tradehouses are of timbered, with half-timbered, or stone construction, along with peaked roofs, right angles and flat planes, and plain, undecorated exteriors.{{ref|name=GerI|[[Morrowind:Generic Dialogue I|Generic Dialogue I]] in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Both Imperials and Bretons have influenced Redguard cosmopolitan architectural styles.{{ref|name=PGE1Hammerfell|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Hammerfell}}}} The Imperial City sewers have their own unique architecture design, with most of the Ayleid plumbing and sewage structure replaced by Imperial Civic architects to the point it isn't even Ayleid or recognized as such.{{ref|name=MJAYQ|{{Cite Book|Mycaelis Julus Answers Your Questions}}}} A Colovian architectural style used prominently during the Second Empire is simply "Second Empire" style architecture, and was often used to build strong fortified structures.{{Ref|[[General:Creating ESO: Cyrodiil's Keeps|Creating ESO: Cyrodiil's Keeps]] from the official [[Online:Online|ESO]] website}}{{ref|[[Online:Strident Springs Demesne|Strident Springs Demesne]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Clothing, Armor, and Weapons===
[[File:LG-cardart-Empire Oathman.png|thumb|right|Variations of standard Imperial Armor]]
The {{Lore Link|Ancestor Moth}} is a rare indigenous gypsy-moth of Cyrodiil,{{Ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} sacred to the Imperials. The Nibenese are known to use the ancestor silk of the gypsy-moth to craft exotic cloth, and export to others across the provinces. The singing and hymnal vocals of the ancestors are collected into a special silk-gathering ceremony, which is the ancestor silk. In the Nibenay, this silk harvest is a sacred tradition that dates back to their early history. But to the {{Lore Link|Cult of the Ancestor Moth}}, they are one of the same. The cult harvest the silk and undergo a magical ritual to enchant the fabric.{{Ref|name=PGE1C}} Ancestor silk shawls are a type of commodity made from the material, which are thought to hold memories of the dead.{{ref|name=ONAS|{{Item Link|quality=3|Ancestor-Silk Shawl|id=177360}} from [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Ancestor Silk can be fashioned into high quality light armor.{{ref|[[Online:Ancestor Silk|Ancestor Silk]] being the highest quality light armor material in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Imperial [[Lore:Newt|River-Newt]] Scale,{{ref|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-32.jpg|Morrowind Concept Art 32]]}} is a Nibenese creation, hence it is also known as Nibenay River Guard. The armor type are considered exotic heirlooms within Morrowind.{{ref|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-Helms 04.jpg|Morrowind Concept Art 4]]}} Colovians are known for producing a variety of fur products, such as socks,{{Ref|[[ON:Treasures M#Monogrammed Colovian Fur Stockings|Monogrammed Colovian Fur Stockings]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} hoods,{{Ref|[[ON:Colovian Fur Hood|Colovian Fur Hood]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} or the well-known Colovian fur helmet (also known as the Colovian Arrow-Catcher).{{ref|group=UOL|[[:File:MW-concept-Helms.jpg|Morrowind Helmet Concept Art 1]]}} The gladius is a weapon associated with Imperial culture.{{Ref|name=CM40OotH|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 40: Order Hour Style}}}}

The Tsaesci left much influence on the race, and because of them, many Imperials can claim some Akaviri descent. Despite this, Akaviri surnames are rare and prized, while trace amounts of Akaviri facial features can be found among many important Cyrodiliic citizens. Colonies of "true Akaviri" (such as [[Lore:Hakoshae|Hakoshae]]) exist in both the Empire and surrounding regions, named as such for keeping to their ancient traditions and customs and not by the purity of their blood.{{Ref|name=PGE1C}} The Tsaesci shared their weapon and armor making dubbed the "Serpentine Ways of Making" with the Imperials during the time of Reman, though this knowledge has been diluted or lost overtime.{{ref|name=TsaesciStyle|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 53: Tsaesci Style}}}} Today, Imperials are still known to use Tsaesci-style equipment to some extent, especially daikatanas and river dragonscale armor.{{Ref|name=PGE1C}}

Through the Imperial Legion, Imperials and other races that serve within their organization can see equipment of high quality metal materials. The Legion's Blacksmiths can forge heavy armor from iron, steel, while some of their most skilled can create some dwarven, orcish, ebony or even daedric quality items.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Manual of Armor}}}} The well-researched historical fiction ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]'' claims that during the war between Morrowind and the Empire, the Imperial Legion used Daedric armor along with other high quality equipment.{{ref|name=2920V6|{{Cite Book|2920, MidYear (v6)}}}} Tiber Septim coveted the vast source of Ebony in Morrowind, leading to it being one of the main reasons for his decision to invade it.{{Ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} Sometime after the signing of the [[Lore:Armistice|Armistice]], it was protected by Imperial law during the Third Empire, and could not be mined or exported without an Imperial charter.{{ref|name=Codex|[[General:Elderscrolls.com Archive/Codex#Items|Items Codex on ''elderscrolls.com'']]}} The [[Lore:East Empire Company|East Empire Company]] essentially became the sole authority to trade in raw ebony.{{ref|name=BMCM|[[Bloodmoon:Carnius Magius|Carnius Magius]]' dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon|Bloodmoon]]''}}

===Food===
:''Main Article: [[Lore:Imperial Cuisine|Imperial Cuisine]]''
Imperial cooks approach food the same way they face the rest of life: in a straightforward and proud manner. As shrewd diplomats and traders, they stock their larders with many of the best ingredients Tamriel has to offer. Cyrodiil's central location in Tamriel, paired with its wealth and power, assures that the Imperials eat well from a variety of meats, vegetables, and spices from across the continent.{{ref|name=TESOC|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls: The Official Cookbook|ns_base=BK}}}}{{rp|11}}

===Death===
:''Main Article: [[Lore:Imperial Death Beliefs|Imperial Death Beliefs]]''

Imperials call Aetherius, the realm of the afterlife, Heaven. Imperial Theosophy teaches that Heaven is also the plane of pure magicka and the seat of the Divines and the other original spirits. Oblivion surrounds Mundus every night, but from Aetherius, energy infuses the daily existence, from highest to lowest, and gives all common purpose. Its magic "brings the rain to the fields, love to the hearts, and scientific principles to the technological industries". It gives the very Sun itself. Finally, Aetherius is the home to the Aedra, those cornerstones of the Mundus whose aspects are seen "in temple, in lordship, and the high walk of heroes".

Imperials bury all their dead, commonly underground both in graveyards, built in the interior of and vicinity of cities and towns; and also in catacombs below the chapels of the Churches of the Eight or the Nine, depending on the age. Gravestones dot the terrain of Imperial graveyards, signaling the location of the buried coffins where the dead rest. This is the most common form of entombment for commoners. On the other side, the nobility is commonly buried in crypts and catacombs, usually built outside of the cities and towns or below the temples of the Imperial cities. All these places are commonly guarded by Arkay's priests, who watch over the rest of the dead.

==Imperial Subcultures==
===Colovians===
[[File:RG-load-Richton (crop).png|thumb|right|200px|Amiel Richton of Colovia]]
The Colovians (also called the Western Imperials{{Ref|name=GTV|{{Cite Book|Guide to Vvardenfell}}}} or just the Westerners){{Ref|name=PGE1C}} are the modern-day Imperials of [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]]. Their culture is influenced by their Nordic roots,{{ref|name=DTTG|group=UOL|Description in [https://www.theguardian.com/technology/gallery/2014/mar/21/elder-scrolls-online-exclusive-concept-art-gallery Elder Scrolls Online – exclusive concept art gallery – in pictures], [[Wikipedia:The Guardian|The Guardian]]}} thanks to the [[Lore:Cyro-Nord|Cyro-Nords]] that once settled in the region of Colovia. They possess the "frontier spirit" of their ancestors, leading them to inherit their simplicity, heartiness, and loyalty to their own. True to their name as Cyrodiil's iron hand, they are the strength of the Emperor, and make up the majority of the Ruby Ranks. The resolute people of the Colovian Estates managed to cause the collapse of the Alessian Order as a result of the War of Righteousness.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} The Colovians tend to take on a more austere but adventuresome lifestyle in keeping with their Nordic ancestry, and often become mercenaries and pirates.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=SATD}} They can be distinguished from the Nibenese by their distinct accent.{{Ref|name=LOS|{{Cite Book|Lord of Souls|ns_base=BK}}}}  Anvil, Chorrol, Skingrad, and Kvatch are notable Colovian cities.

The unification of the Cyrodiils under Reman I didn't eradicate the Colovians' differences with the Nibenese, although the [[Lore:Eight Divines|Eight Divines]] were predominant all over the province by the end of the First Era,{{ref|name=PGE3C}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}} and today, Colovians are known for their fierce devotion for the Divines.{{ref|[[ESO:Cathedral of Akatosh|Cathedral of Akatosh]]'s loading screen in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} They refer to "Shezarr" as "Shor" thanks to their Nordic influences.{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} The [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentates]] were additionally referred to as the "Colovian Emperor-Potentates" and the "Colovian Dynasty," but the potentates' relationship to Colovia and the Colovians has not been explained.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Darkness (Daggerfall)}}}}

When it comes to clothing, the Colovians tend to dress with a simple or martial severity, unlike the Nibenese.{{Ref|name=SZLMIWLS|{{Cite Book|Special ZOS Lore Master Interview with Lawrence Schick|ns_base=GEN}}}} Colovian wear is visually distinctive of their frontier roots, as seen with the pointed helms and fur pelts worn by Sancre Tor highlanders,{{Ref|name=LOSancre|[[:File:LO-misc-Colovian.jpg|Westerners (Sancre Tor) — Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}} or the armor worn by Colovians from County Anvil.{{Ref|name=LOAnvil|[[:File:LO-misc-Anvil Imperial.jpg|Anvil Colovian — Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}} Colovian Prince's armor resembles traditional Imperial armor, but instead wore masked morion helmets.{{Ref|name=LOPrince|[[:File:LO-misc-Colovian Prince.jpg|Colovian Prince with Bodyguard from Rihad – Pocket Guide to the Empire (1st Edition)]]}} Their sense of fashion, and even their taste have influenced other neighboring cultures, such as the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguards]] of [[Lore:Elinhir|Elinhir]].{{Ref|name=PGE1EH|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}}

===Nibenese===
[[File:ON-misc-AbnurTharn.png|thumb|right|270px|Abnur Tharn of Nibenay]]
The Nibenese are the modern-day Imperials of [[Lore:Nibenay|Nibenay]]. They are said to be the closest thing to a pure-bred Nede, due to the ancient traditions that have survived through them,{{Ref|name=CATLA1|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions}}}} and they are distant from their Nordic roots due to their ancestor's self-reliance, which separated them from Skyrim. They represent the high culture of Cyrodiil, showcased in their love for gaudy attire, philosophy, as well as their evolving ancient traditions. They are also known for their tapestries, tattoos, brandings, and elaborate ceremonies.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} When a Nibenese tattoos themselves, its typically to identify one's religion, political faction, or their lifestyle.{{ref|name=LEAYQ|{{Cite Book|Lady Eloisse Answers Your Questions}}}} Early Imperials in eastern Cyrodiil strongly believed in the Alessian Doctrines, even after the Empire's fall, which made them disfavor many practices, notably farming.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} They defaulted to mercantilism, and became a wealthy merchant power through their river network. They were able to export many goods, including textiles, fabrics, moon sugar, rice and armor.{{ref|name=PGE1C}} They are often focused on the numinous nature of some facet of society, and many diverse cults operated openly over the years, dedicated to many animal spirits and hero-gods, as well as ancestor worship.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}}{{ref|name=MH|{{Cite Book|Modern Heretics}}}} Bravil, Bruma, Cheydinhal, and Leyawiin are notable Nibenese cities.

The Imperial Battlemages are the epitome of the Nibenese's inclination towards magic. "The Elven harassment of the First Empire" gave rise to the Imperial battlemages{{ref|name=PGE1C}} as an elite unit separate from the Legions, founded during the early days of the {{Lore Link|Alessian Empire}} when the distinction between mage and warrior was more pronounced.{{ref|name=CATLA2|{{Cite Book|Chancellor Abnur Tharn Answers Your Questions 2}}}} In time, the idea of a "battlemage" became less novel, and Imperial Battlemages began to serve alongside the regular Legions as spellcasters. Regulus Tharn was paramount in reviving the tradition of the Battlemage during the Reman Dynasty.{{ref|name=HTON|{{Cite Book|House Tharn of Nibenay}}}} During this time, the Imperial Battlemages were further exalted with the creation of the "Imperial Battlemage" title, a prestigious position traditionally granted to a unit's leader.{{ref|name=CATLA2}} For disambiguation purposes, the "Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel" is more distinct, and it represents an aristocrat who serves as head of the Elder Council, vs Imperial Battlemages which represent a cadre of war-caster troops.{{ref|name=CATLA2}}{{ref|name=ESOMT|{{Cite Book|Meet the Character - Abnur Tharn}}}} Though the Imperial Battlemages were the ruling "mercantile-magocracy" of Nibeney, for a time, they were superseded by the Alessian priesthood who appealed to the common folk, but they would once again retake their place after the War of Righteousness. In the modern day, Imperial Battlemages make up a Nibenean age-old aristocracy, and alongside religious organizations and the merchant-nobility, they are above the workers plying the fields on the social hierarchy.{{ref|name=PGE1C}}

The "[[Lore:Heartlands|Heartlanders]]" are a subsect of the Nibenese. These inhabitants of the Heartlands region of Cyrodiil are secularists that are tolerant with religion, and see it as both a personal and a private matter, leading them to believe the rule of the Alessian Order as a dark age. They do not speak directly to their gods, and do not see their everyday actions as constantly survived by them.{{ref|name=ROCW|{{Cite Book|Reflections on Cult Worship}}}} The Imperial City lies in the Heartlands, and the architecture is Nibenese, except for the walls and the White Gold Tower, which are Ayleid.{{ref|name=ImperialCyrods}}

==Empires==
[[File:MW-race-Imperial 02.png|thumb|right|A member of the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]], loyal to the [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]]]]
Major portions of Imperial culture and identity involves the various Empires throughout their history.

===Alessian Empire===
The [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], also called the '''First Empire''',{{ref|name=BSOHR|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}} the '''Empire of Cyrodiil''',{{ref|name=ALSS|{{Cite Book|A Life of Strife and Struggle}}}}{{ref|name=TOO|{{Cite Book|The Onus of the Oghma}}}} and the '''Empire of Men''',{{ref|name=TBMOP|{{Cite Book|The Bretons: Mongrels or Paragons?}}}} was established by the former human slaves of the [[Lore:Ayleids|Ayleid]] Empire in {{Year|1E 243}} following the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]].{{ref|name=PGE3AEM|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|All the Eras of Man}}}} The Empire traces its founding to the moment when Saint [[Lore:Alessia|Alessia]] received the [[Lore:Dragonfires|Dragonfires]] and the [[Lore:Amulet of Kings|Amulet of Kings]] from [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]]; she used the Amulet to drive the [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]] out of the mortal realm.{{ref|name=ToSA|{{Cite Book|Trials of St. Alessia}}}} Akatosh told Alessia that as long as the Dragonfires remained lit and [[Lore:Dragonborn|her heirs]] wore the Amulet of Kings, the gates of [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]] would remain shut and [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] would be spared the depredations of the Daedra.{{ref|name=ToSA}}
:''See the main article: '''[[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]]'''''

===Second Empire===
The [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], also known as the '''Cyrodilic Empire'''{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}}, the '''Empire of Cyrodiil''',{{ref|name=HOTFG|{{Cite Book|History of the Fighters Guild}}}} and the '''Second Empire of Men''',{{ref|name=PGE1C}} was a human-led empire that, at its height, controlled every region of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] with the exception of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{Ref|name=PGE1C}} The history of the Second Empire is divided into two main periods: the reign of the [[Lore:Reman Dynasty|Reman Dynasty]] that witnessed the final centuries of the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], and the subsequent [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Akaviri Potentate]] that ushered in the [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]].
:''See the main article: '''[[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]]'''''

===Empire of Cyrodiil=== 
The [[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]]{{Ref|name=THOTE|{{Cite Book|True Heirs of the Empire}}}}{{Ref|name=KOTD|{{Cite Book|Knights of the Dragon (book)}}}} (also known as the '''Imperial Empire'''{{ref|[[ON:Empress Regent Clivia Tharn|Empress Regent Clivia Tharn]]'s dialogue in {{g|imperial city|eso}}}}) refers to an [[Lore:Imperial|Imperial]] state that existed during the early years of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. Although identified as an empire, it differed from the [[Lore:Alessian Empire|Alessian Empire]], [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]], and [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] in that its only permanent dependency was [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] itself, and it was at no time ruled by a [[Lore:Dragonborn|Dragonborn]] emperor.

:''See the main article: '''[[Lore:Empire of Cyrodiil (Interregnum)|Empire of Cyrodiil]]'''''

===Third Empire===
The [[Lore:Third Empire|Third Empire]] (also called the '''Holy Cyrodiilic Empire of Tamriel'''{{ref|name=WQ1|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v1}}}}) was declared by [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]] in {{Year|2E 854}} after the chaos of the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], and was forged through a decades-long conflict to unite [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] known as the [[Lore:Tiber War|Tiber War]].{{ref|name=PGE1C|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Cyrodiil}}}} It lasted throughout the whole of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] and two centuries into the [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], when it began to decline.

==Religion==
Imperial Religion primary focuses on the Eight Divines, or the Eight and One which includes the ascended [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Talos]].{{ref|name=TCND|{{Cite Book|Ten Commands: Nine Divines}}}}{{Ref|name=TPOblivion|[[Oblivion:The Prophet|The Prophet]]'s dialogue in {{g|kotn|Oblivion}}}} The Imperials have a lot of cults dedicated to various religions with the Imperial City.{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Drake of Blades Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=PGE1C}}{{ref|name=SATD|{{Cite Book|Shezarr and the Divines}}}} Despite this, the Imperials have a history of restricting and banning the worship of deities that are not of the traditional Eight Divines. The study of these other gods was encouraged though. During the First Empire, the Imperials were strictly forbidden from worshiping or idolizing the deities [[Lore:Magnus|Magnus]], [[Lore:Y'ffre|Y'ffre]], [[Lore:Sheor|Sheor]], and [[Lore:Phynaster|Phynaster]] and worship of these deities was considered heresy.{{ref|name=TIEGTT|{{Cite Book|The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel/High Rock}}}}

[[File:LO-people-Tiber Septim.png|thumb|right|An engraving of Tiber Septim]]
:;[[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], the Dragon God of Time
:: Chief among the gods, with mastery over the flow of time. Associated with [[Lore:Dragon|dragons]] and the qualities of endurance and virtuous service.{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}

:;[[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]], the Mortals' God
:: God of cycles, particularly that of birth and death. Presides over funerals and burial rites, as well as ushering in the changes of the seasons.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Dibella|Dibella]]
:: Goddess of beauty and art, and one of the most popular of the Divines. Widespread cults are dedicated to both healing and sexual instruction.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Julianos|Julianos]]
:: God of logic, wisdom, and the arts of [[Lore:Magic|magic]]. His temples act as educational institutions in literature, history, and law.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Kynareth|Kynareth]]
:: Goddess of the heavens, winds, and rain. Patron of sailors and travelers, and often propitiated for good fortune in life.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Mara|Mara]], the Mother-Goddess
:: Goddess of love, compassion, and the bounty of nature. Presides over marriage ceremonies, befitting her ancient origins as a fertility goddess.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Stendarr|Stendarr]], the Steadfast
:: God of mercy, justice, and righteousness. Patron of all those who wield righteous might to protect the weak, from emperors to [[Lore:Resolutes of Stendarr|holy warriors]]. {{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Zenithar|Zenithar]], the Trader God
:: God of work, commerce, and wealth, invoked for success in business ventures. Teaches prosperity through honest industry, rather than violence or deceit.{{Ref|name=VOF}}
:;[[Lore:Talos|Talos]]/[[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]
::A mortal and emperor who upon death became a divine, the respect and admiration Imperials had for Tiber Septim is nothing compared to the veneration they have for him as a god of the [[Lore:Nine Divines|Nine Divines]], regardless of the heresies often espoused about him.{{ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}}{{ref|name=TAH|{{Cite Book|The Arcturian Heresy}}}} His accomplishments inspired them to believe they could do anything they set their mind to.{{ref|name=TKOTN|{{Cite Book|The Knights of the Nine}}}} The end of his dynasty was greeted with great mourning, although also with great pride, as his last known descendant, [[Lore:Martin Septim|Martin Septim]], sacrificed himself to save all of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=Oblivion}} Imperials know all too well that dynasties are destined to rise and fall; all it will take is time before the Glorious Empire once again reigns over Tamriel.

:;[[Lore:Reman|Reman]], The Cyrodiil

He instituted the rites of becoming Emperor, which included the ritual geas to the Amulet of Kings, a soul gem of immense power. Following Alessia's victory against her Ayleid oppressors, Shezarr's worship was changed to its modern version as a watered-down version of Shor as an attempt to appease Alessia's elven pantheon worshiping subjects as well as her Nordic allies. By the end of the Third Era, worship of Shezarr had all but stopped due to increased racial tolerance.{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=SATD}}

===Daedra===
[[File:ON-furnishing-Pottery, Sanguine Repaired 02.jpg|thumb|left|A pottery relic of Sanguine worship in Colovia]]
{{Quote Box|style=max-height:350px|''To BOETHIA the Fount of Inspiration.<br><br>To HIRCINE who is Half the Conscience of Men.<br><br>To MALACATH who speaks all Language Sideways.<br><br>To MEHRUNES DAGON whose Mistress is the Blazing Sun.<br><br>To MOLAG BAL whose Breath is Most Foul.<br><br>To NAMIRA whose Works endure forever.''|Excerpt from the ''16 Acceptable Blasphemies''{{intnote|A|[nb 1]}}}}

The Imperials generally have a negative view of Daedra, with notable differences between the behavior of Colovians and the Nibenesse during the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]]. As Colovians are especially religious, they will typically refuse to associate themselves with Daedra.{{ref|name=ONKazirra|[[Online:Kazirra|Kazirra]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During this period, however, the Nibenese were more inclined to turn to Daedra worship.{{ref|name=FTLCH|{{Cite Book|From a discussion with Tatianus Lepidus, Colovian Historian}}}} In Tiber Septim's empire, the Daedric Princes were invoked as the 16 Acceptable Blasphemies.{{ref|name=PGE1I|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Invocation}}}}

The Imperial's distaste for Daedra was readily apparent during the reign of the [[Lore:Longhouse Emperors|Longhouse Emperors]], a short-lived Reachfolk Dynasty that was brought to an end after Emperor [[Lore:Leovic|Leovic]] legalized Daedra worship (allegedly at the behest of corrupt advisors). Leovic consequently drew the ire of many nobles who had once been counted among his supporters.{{ref|name=EFEV|{{Cite Book|Eulogy for Emperor Varen}}}} One of these erstwhile supporters, Duke [[Lore:Varen Aquilarios|Varen Aquilarios]] of [[Lore:Chorrol|Chorrol]], marshaled the armies of the [[Lore:Colovian Estates|Colovian Estates]] and led them against Leovic in open [[Lore:Varen's Rebellion|rebellion]] from {{Year|2E 576}} to {{Year|2E 577}}.{{ref|name=PQOTGC|{{Cite Book|Pirate Queen of the Gold Coast}}}} In the end, Varen killed Leovic in the Imperial Throne Room, and the reign of the Longhouse Emperors came to a close.{{ref|name=COTFC1|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 1}}}}{{ref|name=COTFC4|{{Cite Book|Chronicles of the Five Companions 4}}}}

[[Lore:House Tharn|House Tharn]] of Nibenay has always kept ties with the forces of Oblivion.{{ref|name=AbnurCOTFC|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Council of the Five Companions|Council of the Five Companions]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} During Emperor Varen's reign, [[Lore:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]] unwittingly helped [[Lore:Mannimarco|Mannimarco]] cause the [[Lore:Soulburst|Soulburst]], and subsequently pledged his loyalty to the necromancer (and by extension, [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]) in the interest of preserving the Empire.{{ref|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s role in causing the Soulburst in the flashback seen during [[Online:The_Harborage_(quest)|The Harborage]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=AbnurCOTW|[[Online:Abnur Tharn|Abnur Tharn]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Castle_of_the_Worm_(quest)|Castle of the Worm]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Unlike Abnur Tharn, several other members of the family had aligned themselves with Molag Bal wholesale: Abnur's nephew [[Lore:Javad Tharn|Javad]] was a prominent figure in the [[Lore:Stonefire Cult|Stonefire Cult]] (an offshoot of Mannimarco's [[Lore:Worm Cult|Worm Cult]]),{{ref|name=ONKazirra}} and [[Lore:Septima Tharn|Septima Tharn]] led the [[Lore:Seventh Legion|Seventh Legion]] in the Daedric Prince's name.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Regarding the Hall|ns_base=Online}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Septima Tharn|Septima Tharn]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Storming the Garrison|Storming the Garrison]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|[[Online:Scholar Laurette Diel|Scholar Laurette Diel]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Ravenwatch Inquiry|The Ravenwatch Inquiry]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock}}}}

The Tharn family's dealings with Molag Bal was the primary motivator for the [[Lore:Daggerfall Covenant|Daggerfall Covenant]], [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] and [[Lore:Ebonheart Pact|Ebonheart Pact]]'s invasion of Cyrodiil. At first, their goal was to depose the Tharn family, but the conflict devolved into the Alliance War.{{ref|name=AaWT|[[General:The Elder Scrolls Online: Alliances at War|The Elder Scrolls Online: Alliances at War]]}} To his credit, Abnur Tharn joined the Five Companions in their quest to end the Planemeld.{{ref|name=AbnurCOTW}}

Despite the Imperial distaste for Daedra worship, it is not banned in [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]], due in part to the Imperial Charter that permits the [[Lore:Mages Guild|Mages Guild]] to summon Daedra. However, the opinion of religious institutions and the general public is hostile, a fact that encourages Daedra worshippers to practice in secret. Some even build their shrines out in the wilderness, where they may be attended by a community of faithful in peace.{{ref|name=MH}}

==Notes==
*Imperials seem to be inspired by the peoples of [[wikipedia:Ancient Rome|Ancient Rome]] and [[wikipedia:Southern Europe|Southern Europe]]. Many aspects of Cyrodiilic culture reflect these roots, drawing from the traditions of the [[wikipedia:Roman Empire|Roman Empire]] and [[wikipedia:Mediterranean Europe|Mediterranean Europe]]. 
** Architecturally, Nibenese culture features grandiose structures, intricate ornamentation, and urban planning reminiscent of [[wikipedia:Roman engineering|Roman engineering]]. 
** The Colovian martial tradition similarly mirrors that of the [[wikipedia:Roman legions|Roman legions]], emphasizing professionalism, strict hierarchy, and standardized military equipment. 
** Imperials' fashion and common physical appearance, ranging from medium-fair to olive and tan complexions with dark hair, further highlight their Southern European inspiration. Likewise, Cyrodiilic names often echo those of Ancient Rome and [[wikipedia:Romance languages|Romance-speaking]] regions, blending classical [[wikipedia:Latin|Latin]] elements with the linguistic patterns of medieval Southern Europe.
* Imperials were not considered a distinct race within the games until ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]'', but have been a playable race in every major entry since. The only named character from the earlier games that has been confirmed to be an Imperial is Emperor Uriel Septim VII, though this knowledge only comes from the later games of the series.
