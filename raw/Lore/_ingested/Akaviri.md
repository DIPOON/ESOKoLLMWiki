# Lore:Akaviri

'''Akaviri''' is used to refer to any sentient creature that inhabits [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]], such as the Kamal and Tang Mo. No [[Lore:Mer|mer]] have ever lived there; the [[Lore:Man|men]] that did live in Akavir were all "eaten"{{intnote|Eaten|[Note 1]}} long ago by the Vampiric Serpent Folk of [[Lore:Tsaesci (place)|Tsaesci]].

== Akaviri in Tamriel ==
[[File:LO-people-Duadeen.jpg|thumb|right|[[Lore:Duadeen|Duadeen]], a half-akaviri mercenary lord]]
[[File:LO-misc-Against the Snakes.jpg|An illustration accompanying ''[[Lore:Against the Snakes|Against the Snakes]]'' of a person facing an Akaviri opponent with a serpent-like shadow cast over them|thumb|left]]
In Tamrielic history, the Akaviri first appeared as pirates. The [[Lore:Imperial Geographic Society|Imperial Geographic Society]] speculated that these could have been scouts for the eventual invasions. The strange beastfolk that manned these ships horrified early Tamriel inhabitants.{{Ref|name=PGE3|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}} After the [[Lore:Rourken|Rourken]] clan of [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] vanished without a trace and abandoned their cities, Akaviri and [[Lore:Nord|Nordic]] pirates plundered them for any value left.{{Ref|name=PGE3HF|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Hammerfell}}}} 

Akaviri pirates and raiders are prevalent in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region. Back when [[Lore:Wayrest|Wayrest]] was a small fishing village around {{Year|1E 800}}, pirates and raiders took over the islands on the [[Lore:Bjoulsae River|Bjoulsae River]], west of the present-day city. In {{Year|1E 2702}},{{Ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}} a year prior to the [[Lore:First Akaviri Invasion|First Akaviri Invasion]],{{Ref|name=PGE3}} the city's entire population was forced to move into the [[Lore:Gardner|Gardners]]' walled estate to avoid Akaviri raiders, among other issues.{{Ref|name=WJOTB|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay}}}}{{intnote|Pirates|[Note 2]}} On writing the history of [[Lore:Daggerfall (kingdom)|Daggerfall]], Odiva Gallwood believed that had the kingdom not prepared for battles in the next thousand years to come, the Iliac Bay would have been Akavirian.{{Ref|name=AHOD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}}

== Other ==
Early documented encounters between the Akaviri and the earliest inhabitants of Tamriel suggest that there may be Akaviri people with rat-like features, and others who are canine in appearance. This suggests Akaviri cultures that have yet to be discovered and perhaps may be extinct,{{Ref|name=PGE3}} or otherwise have been displaced.

==Notes==
{{note|Eaten|1.|The meaning of term "eaten" as given from what is known of the land of Akavir is not clear. Although description of the "vampiric" Tsaesci might suggest literal meaning, it could be a metaphor for being subdued. In ''[[Lore:Mysterious Akavir|Mysterious Akavir]]'' by anonymous writer, the Tsaesci are told to have "tried to eat all the [[Lore:Dragon|Dragons]]. They managed to enslave the Red Dragons, but the black ones had fled to (then) Po Tun". Also, the ''[[Lore:Akaviri Diary Translation|Akaviri Diary Translation]]'' of an Akaviri messenger states that during his journey to deliver orders to [[Oblivion:Fort Pale Pass|Fort Pale Pass]] he met another Akaviri messenger, stating that his leg had been badly hurt by wolves. As the Tsaesci are described as beings with humanoid upper body and snake lower bodies, to being entirely snake-like, this statement would suggest that men indeed continued to live in Akavir, even if they were enslaved by the Tsaesci. The Akaviri-made [[Lore:Alduin's Wall|Alduin's Wall]] shows members of the Dragonguard appearing to be human-looking.}}
{{note|Pirates|2.|An older version of the ''Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay'' from the mid-[[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] omits any mention of Akaviri pirates,{{Ref|name=WJOTBESO|{{Cite Book|Wayrest, Jewel of the Bay (Elder Scrolls Online)}}}} while a newer version from the late [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] mentions Akaviri pirates.{{Ref|name=WJOTB}}}}
