[[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]] (or '''Ark'ay'''), known as the '''Lord of the Wheel of Life''',{{ref|name=TCOA|{{Cite Book|The Consecrations of Arkay}}}} '''God of the Cycle of Birth and Death''',{{ref|name=DFGuide|{{Cite Book|The Daggerfall Chronicles|ns_base=Books}}}} '''Light of Lights''', the '''Bringer of Light''',{{ref|[[Oblivion:Priest of Arkay|Priest of Arkay]]'s dialogue in {{g|OB}}}}, and '''Lord of the Seasons'''{{Ref|name=ONPellingare|[[Online:Master Pellingare|Master Pellingare]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} is a member of the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]], and also a popular god in other cultures. Arkay is often more important in those cultures where his father, [[Lore:Akatosh|Akatosh]], is either less related to time or where his time aspects are difficult to comprehend by the layman.{{Ref|name=VOF|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith...}}}} He is the god of burials and funeral rites, and is generally associated with cyclical occasions, such as the seasons and life/death.{{ref|name=OLBTM|{{Cite Book|Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie}}}} His priests are staunch opponents of necromancy and all forms of the undead, and are empowered to bestow Arkay's blessings which prevent the forceful misuse of a mortal soul.{{ref|name=TCOA}} Thus, any body properly buried by a priest observing the proper rituals is protected by Arkay's Law from being raised as undead.{{ref|name=CP1|{{Cite Book|Corpse Preparation v I}}}} Therefore, necromancers view Arkay as their ultimate enemy, and make covert efforts to undermine his worship throughout Tamriel.{{ref|name=ATE|{{Cite Book|Arkay the Enemy}}}} Because of this association with, and protection of, mortality, he is sometimes called the '''Mortals' God'''. To the [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] of the [[Lore:Cult of Lamae Bal|Cult of Lamae Bal]] he is known as '''the Forsaker'''.{{Ref|name=ROTS|{{Cite Book|Rite of the Scion}}}}

One legend of Arkay's origins is written in the book ''[[Lore:Ark'ay The God|Ark'ay The God]]''. It states that Arkay was once a mortal shopkeeper with a passion for knowledge. He found a book written in a strange language and spent years upon years attempting to decipher it, slowly ignoring everything and everyone else around him. Eventually Arkay realized the book explained life and death itself, but by this time was at death's door with an incurable plague. Praying to [[Lore:Mara|Mara]] as a last resort, Arkay asked for more time to interpret the book. Mara gave him a choice: die now or become a god for eternity, charged with keeping the balance of death and life in the universe.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|Ark'ay The God}}}}{{Ref|name=VOF}}{{Ref|name=ATE}} Another theory of Arkay's origins is contained in ''[[Lore:The Monomyth|The Monomyth]]'', which suggests that Arkay was one of the very first spirits to "crystallize" after the start of time.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}}

==Worship==
The [[Lore:Order of Arkay|Order of Arkay]] has temples in [[Lore:Sentinel (kingdom)|Sentinel]], [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]], [[Lore:Shalgora|Shalgora]], [[Lore:Dragontail Mountains|Dragontail Mountains]], and [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]]. Followers of Arkay are known as Arkayns,{{ref|name=Nidal|[[Redguard:Nidal|Nidal]]'s dialogue in ''[[Redguard:Redguard|Redguard]]''}} and they're known for bestowing [[Lore:Consecrations of Arkay|Consecrations of Arkay]] upon the faithful.{{Ref|name=TCOA|{{Cite Book|The Consecrations of Arkay}}}}

===Bosmer===
Arkay is the god of burials, funeral rites and is associated with the seasons. The Bosmer invoke him when solving offenses against the Green Pact.{{ref|name=GTEV|{{Cite Book|EGT|Valenwood}}}}{{ref|name=VOFTWE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Wood Elves}}}} They revere him alongside another god of similar portfolio, [[Lore:Xarxes|Xarxes]].{{Ref|name=AAA}}

===Breton===
[[File:DF-sign-Order of Arkay.png|A symbol for the Order of Arkay in High Rock|left|thumb]][[File:ON-Statue-Breton Statue, Arkay.jpg|Breton statue of Arkay|right|thumb]]
Arkay has a significant presence in the city of [[Lore:Shornhelm|Shornhelm]], where he is known as the '''Light'''.{{ref|name=ONIIL|[[Online:Contraband I#Inspirational Illuminations of the Light|Inspirational Illuminations of the Light]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{Ref|name=ONEselde|[[Online:Countess Eselde Tamrith|Countess Eselde Tamrith]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} He is the patron deity of the [[Lore:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]] and [[Lore:Shalgora|Shalgora]].{{ref|Patron deities for [[Daggerfall:Shalgora|Shalgora]] and the [[Daggerfall:Ilessan Hills|Ilessan Hills]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} Arkay is also the patron of [[Lore:House Tamrith|House Tamrith]], who are devout followers of the Light.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband H#House Tamrith Prayer Shawl|House Tamrith Prayer Shawl]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=ONIIL}} The scions of House Tamrith study the Way of the Light and obey Arkay's teachings.{{ref|{{Cite Book|House Tamrith: A Recent History}}}} Their holy symbols bear the crest of House Tamrith and the symbol of the Light of Arkay.{{ref|[[Online:Contraband H#House Tamrith Holy Symbol|House Tamrith Holy Symbol]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} 

Arkay's holy relics can be used to combat the undead.{{ref|name=ESOAlvaren|[[Online:Alvaren Garoutte|Alvaren Garoutte]]'s dialogue in {{g|ON}}}} Small stone talismans resembling eyes shut in eternal rest, known as closed eyes of Arkay are often given to mourning families.{{ref|name=Contraband C|[[ON:Contraband C#The Closed Eye of Arkay|The Closed Eye of Arkay]] item description in {{g|high isle|eso}}}}

Because of his duty to maintain balance, Bretons believe Arkay may cause plague, famines and other natural disasters and so fear him as well as love him.{{ref|name=ESOPriestCR|[[Online:Priest Colby Rangouze|Priest Colby Rangouze]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Imperial===
[[File:BL-misc-Arkay.png|Imperial depiction of Arkay|right|thumb]][[File:OB-place-Great Chapel of Arkay.jpg|The Great Chapel of Arkay in Cyrodiil|left|thumb]]
The [[Lore:Imperial Cult|Imperial Cult]] is based out of Cyrodiil, and its ministries across the Empire are handled by the '''Council of the Nine''', consisting of the Primates of Cyrodiil's various Great Chapels.{{ref|name=Chana|[[OB:Chana Mona|Chana Mona]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} In Cyrodiil, the Church is divided into eight parishes, each dedicated to a divine. [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]]'s chapel is dedicated to Arkay.{{ref|[[OB:Chapels|Chapels]] in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}}
{{NewRight}}

===Nord===
[[File:SR-interior-Hall of the Dead (Whiterun) 04.jpg|right|thumb|Nordic shrine to Arkay]][[File:SR-concept-Amulet of Arkay.jpg|left|thumb|Nordic amulet of Arkay]]
The original Nordic aspect of Arkay was [[Lore:Orkey|Orkey]], a testing god associated with the Snake that governed death and enjoyed quarrels of all kind.{{ref|name=TSoG|{{Cite Book|The Song of Gods}}}} Orkey seems to have some association with Malacath and Orcs, a notable legend saying Orkey cursed the Nords (who previously were as long-lived as Elves) down to lifespan of six years before Shor lifted the curse and threw most of it onto the Orcs.{{ref|name=VoFNords|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The Nords}}}}

The shift from Orkey to Arkay happened with the Alessian Reforms that fused aspects of the Elven and Nordic Pantheons being embraced in much of Skyrim.{{fact}}

Shrines to Arkay can be found in Halls of the Dead across Skyrim, it is his shrine Nords pray to when they visit the Hall. That his divinity will watch over their ancestors on the way to the next life.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Brother Verulus|Brother Verulus]]' dialogue in {{g|SR}}}}

Nordic priests of Arkay perform rites for the dead and sometimes console the bereaved, they also keep a constant vigil against those who practice the vile arts of necromancy.{{ref|[[Skyrim:Andurs|Andurs]]' dialogue in {{g|SR}}}}

===Reachfolk===
After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]] embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Arkay, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.{{ref|name=Belchimac|[[Skyrim:Belchimac|Belchimac]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Degaine|[[Skyrim:Degaine|Degaine]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}
===Redguard===
[[File:RG-place-Temple of Arkay 02.jpg|The Temple of Arkay on Stros M'Kai|right|thumb]]
In Hammerfell, where worship of Arkay is strongest, the dead are almost always subject to Arkay's Law. There are exceptions after large battles or in remote areas where death occurs far from priests.{{ref|name=CP1}} Redguard followers of Arkay see him as the shepherd of the soul, who watches through lives and deaths, and guides one between them in the heavenly interim.{{ref|name=Nidal}}

Some sources connect Arkay with [[Lore:Tu'whacca|Tu'whacca]] through the early Nedic precursor To-Arcka. Indeed, symbolism strikingly similar to Arkay's wreath appears on many ancient Yokudan tombs.{{Ref|name=AAA|{{Cite Book|Tu'whacca, Arkay, Xarxes}}}} Tu'whacca's cult is sometimes associated with Arkay in the more cosmopolitan regions of Hammerfell, and he is often worshiped in that name by Forebears.

One source has conflated Arkay with the Yokudan goddess [[Lore:Morwha|Morwha]] in the past although it's unknown if this was speaking to a true connection or was simply a scholarly mistake.{{ref|name=RGCompanion|{{Cite Book|The Redguard Companion|ns_base=Books}}}}

==Artifacts==
===Lady Garick's Sacred Shield===
[[File:ON-item-Lady Garick's Sacred Shield.png|thumb|right|Lady Garick's Sacred Shield]]
[[Lore:Joslin Garick|Lady Joslin Garick]] was a premier [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] captain for the [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]]. Aside from her exploits, she was known for what she referred to as her [[Lore:Lady Garick's Sacred Shield|"Sacred Shield"]]. She claimed her eponymous ward was blessed by a saint of [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]], though long after her passing, legends would paint it as Arkay himself that endowed his power into it. The shield was said to preserve the wielder's life, in that no injury would befall them. The legends seemed to have some merit, for her crew would always return safely. However, the one instance she left her shield behind for safe keeping was her last voyage, as she and her crew met their demise during the All Flags Navy's assault on [[Lore:Thras|Thras]].

===[[Lore:Sword of the Crusader|Sword of the Crusader]]===
[[File:OB-item-Sword of the Crusader.jpg|The Sword of the Crusader|right|thumb]]
The Sword of the Crusader was created by [[Lore:Arkay|Arkay]]. Those it strikes are burnt by holy flames, and their magicka reserves depleted. It was discovered by Sir Berich Vlindrel of the original Knights, who brought it to the War of the Red Diamond. When he died, it was buried with him in his family tomb in [[Lore:Underpall Cave|Underpall Cave]]; in undeath, Lord Vlindrel dragged the sword down into evil, cursing it so that a righteous wielder would have stunted magicka. His wraith, infamous for haunting the [[Lore:Orange Road|Orange Road]], wielded the weapon. It was recovered by the pilgrim who, after freeing Lord Vlindrel's soul, reconsecrated it on the altar in the Great Chapel of Arkay in [[Lore:Cheydinhal|Cheydinhal]].{{ref|[[OB:The Sword of the Crusader|The Sword of the Crusader]] quest in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]: [[Oblivion:Knights of the Nine|Knights of the Nine]]''}}

==Saints of Arkay==
====Ellenica====
'''Saint Ellenica''' was a Breton priestess of Arkay.{{ref|name=ESOAlvaren}} Her prayer book is a holy relic, and contains passages that can be read to put the dead to rest.{{ref|{{Item Link|Saint Ellenica's Prayer Book|questid=4549}} quest item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She was buried in {{Lore Link|Flyleaf Catacombs}} with her prayer book, which was retrieved in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Matthiaume's Journal|ns_base=Online}}}}
====Errandil====
'''Saint Errandil''' was a High Elf saint affiliated with Arkay. He was the Great Chapel of Arkay's chief crusader against the practice of necromancy in the Mages Guild and the [[Lore:Imperial Battle College|Imperial Battle College]],{{ref|{{Cite Book|Guide to Cheydinhal}}}} he was killed by [[Lore:Umaril the Unfeathered|Umaril the Unfeathered]]'s [[Lore:Auroran|Aurorans]] in {{Year|3E 433}}.{{ref|[[Oblivion:Errandil|Errandil]] in {{g|OB}}}}

====Marben====
'''Saint Marben''' was a Breton saint affiliated with Arkay.{{ref|name=ESOAlvaren}}{{ref|{{Cite Book|Edweg's Resignation Note|ns_base=Online}}}} Saint Marben's Fingerbone is a holy relic of Arkay, and was buried at {{Lore Link|Sanguine Barrows}} until it was retrieved in {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|[[Online:Edweg|Edweg]]'s dialogue during [[Online:Archaic Relics|Archaic Relics]] in {{g|on}}}}{{ref|{{Item Link|Saint Marben's Fingerbone|questid=4559}} quest item description in {{g|on}}}}

==Notes==
* ''[[Lore:The Illusion of Death|The Illusion of Death]]'' suggests that the cycle of life and death is also called the "Arkayn Cycle".
*Arkay's lash, a cactus with a bright pink flower that has great medicinal properties, is named after Arkay.{{ref|[[Online:Iokkas|Iokkas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}
* Arkay was named after one of the original ''Daggerfall'' beta testers, R. K. Deutsch, who died in 1998.
* The word for "relating to Arkay" is "Arkayn".{{ref|{{Cite Book|The Illusion of Death}}}}{{Ref|name=AAA}}
* [[Skyrim:Florentius Baenius|Florentius Baenius]] was a 4th Era Priest of Arkay associated with the Dawnguard that claimed to directly hear the voice of Arkay. Though his colleagues were deeply skeptical of this, his supposed connection to Arkay still seemed to give him access to knowledge he should not otherwise possess.
* Various poisons in ''Skyrim'' have Arkay's symbol on their label.
