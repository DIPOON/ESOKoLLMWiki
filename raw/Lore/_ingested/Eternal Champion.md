The [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]], purportedly named '''[[Lore:Talin|Talin]]'''{{ref|name=ARManual}}<noinclude>{{Intnote|nb1|[nb 1]}}</noinclude> (b. {{Year|3E 370}}),{{ref|name=DC|{{Cite Book|ns_base=BK|The Daggerfall Chronicles}}}} was an otherwise unknown [[Lore:Hero|hero]] best known for ending the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]] in {{Year|3E 399}} by bringing together the pieces of the [[Lore:Staff of Chaos|Staff of Chaos]], killing the impostor [[Lore:Cyrodilic Emperors|emperor]] [[Lore:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]],{{ref|name=HT|{{Cite Book|Hallgerd's Tale}}}} and rescuing the true emperor, [[Lore:Uriel Septim VII|Uriel Septim VII]], and general of the [[Lore:Imperial Guard|Imperial Guard]], [[Lore:Warhaft|Talin Warhaft]],<noinclude>{{intnote|nb2|[nb 2]}}</noinclude> from a [[Lore:Oblivion|dimension of Tharn's choosing]] where they had been imprisoned for ten years.{{ref|name=ARIntro|''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' [[Arena:Main Quest#Introduction Scene|introduction scene]]}}{{ref|name=TET|{{Cite Book|The Third Era Timeline}}}}

==History==
===Early Life===
[[File:AR-menu-Character Creation House.jpg|thumb|right|The Champion's childhood home]]
The Champion's father was General Talin Warhaft, who fought in a [[Lore:Wars|battle]] at [[Lore:Borim Valley|Borim Valley]]. Along with having a mother and a sister, the Champion also had a great-aunt and a great-uncle who each lived in separate villages close to the Champion's home village. During their childhood, the Champion worked at the stables of their home village and did various other chores. The Champion also attended regular a weapons class conducted by [[Lore:Festil|Armsmaster Festil]], an elderly warrior who had been fighting since the day of the Champion's grandfather.{{ref|[[Arena:Character Creation|''Arena'' character creation]] scenario}} They became friends with [[Lore:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]] after they were summoned to the [[Lore:Imperial Court|Imperial Court]] to learn under her tutelage the ways of [[Lore:Magic|sorcery]].{{ref|name=ARManual}} At the Imperial Court they were considered only a minor member of no real consequence.{{ref|name=ARRia|[[Lore:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]]'s dialogue in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}}

===Alternate Backstories===
Outside of the purported background of the Eternal Champion as Talin, various cultures throughout [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] have conflicting claims about the hero. Supposed identities for the Champion include '''Grachta''' the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]] [[Lore:Knight|Knight]], '''Dunastyr''' the [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] Sorcerer, '''Shathra''' the female [[Lore:Ohmes|Ohmes]] [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiti]] Assassin, and '''Tyronicus''' the [[Lore:Imperial|Cyrodilic]] Battlemage.{{Ref|name=IWDG|group=UOL|[[General:Jobasha Interview|Jobasha Interview]] — Douglas Goodall}} Other unnamed identities include the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] account who claim in public the Champion was an enlightened wizard, but in private admit he was an outcast. The [[Lore:Nord|Nords]] see them as the son of a proud king. The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] have legends that they were the daughter of the living [[Lore:Gods|goddess]] [[Lore:Almalexia|Almalexia]]. The [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]] say she was an [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleidion]] Aurehliar. Even some [[Lore:Vampire|vampire]] clans have their own legends for the Eternal Champion, with the [[Lore:Lyrezi|Lysrezi]] believing him to be a vampire from a [[Lore:Planes of Existence|realm]] outside of [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]].{{Ref|name=MAS|group=UOL|[[General:Masser and Secunda|Masser and Secunda]]}}

===Imperial Simulacrum===
[[File:AR-item-Staff of Chaos.png|thumb|right|The Staff of Chaos]]
In {{Year|3E 389}}, Jagar Tharn overthrew Emperor Uriel, imprisoned him and General Warhaft in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]], and killed Ria Silmane. The Champion was imprisoned by Jagar Tharn in the [[Lore:Imperial City Prison|Imperial Dungeons]] and left to die. Using what little of her life force remained, Silmane contacted the Champion from between [[Lore:Mundus|the mortal world]] and [[Lore:Aetherius|the afterlife]] and guided them to freedom through a shift-gate, tasking them with reassembling the Staff of Chaos and rescuing the emperor.{{ref|name=Bio}}{{intnote|nb3|[nb 3]}} Silmane aided the Champion in this task by using her magic to mask the Champion's identity,{{ref|name=ARRia|[[Lore:Ria Silmane|Ria Silmane]]'s dialogue in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}} which successfully concealed the Champion's name from Tharn until seven of the Staff's eight pieces had been recovered.{{ref|name=ARTharn|[[Arena:Jagar Tharn|Jagar Tharn]]'s dialogue in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}} 

Using Silmane's guidance to find the location of the pieces, with covert assistance from [[Lore:Barenziah|Queen Barenziah]] and [[Lore:Eadwyre|King Eadwyre]], who through contact with Silmane gathered intelligence on the locations of the pieces of the Staff of Chaos to be passed on to the Champion,{{ref|name=Bio|{{Cite Book|Biography of Barenziah, v 3}}}} the Champion journeyed to [[Lore:Stonekeep|Stonekeep]], [[Lore:Fang Lair|Fang Lair]], the [[Lore:Fortress of Ice|Fortress of Ice]], [[Lore:Labyrinthian|Labyrinthian]], [[Lore:Selene's Web|Selene's Web]], the [[Lore:Elden Grove|Elden Grove]], the [[Lore:Temple of Agamanus|Temple of Agamanus]], the [[Lore:Halls of Colossus|Halls of Colossus]], the [[Lore:Sheogorath#Notes|Temple of Mad God]], the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]], the [[Lore:Mines of Khuras|Mines of Khuras]], the [[Lore:Crypt of Hearts|Crypt of Hearts]], the [[Lore:Vaults of Gemin|Vaults of Gemin]], [[Lore:Murkwood|Murkwood]], [[Lore:Black Gate|Black Gate]], and [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] on a quest that spanned the course of a decade, before ultimately descending into the depths of the [[Lore:Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace]] to face Tharn. The Champion defeated Tharn in {{Year|3E 399}}, with their battle doing considerable damage to the Imperial Palace,{{ref|[[Arena:The Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace Guard]]'s dialogue in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}} by draining the [[Lore:Jewel of Fire|Jewel of Fire]] of its power and restoring the Staff of Chaos to its full power, killing Tharn in the process. The Champion then freed Emperor Uriel and General Warhaft from Oblivion and was proclaimed by the Emperor as the [[Lore:Eternal Champion|Eternal Champion]]. They then took their place at the emperor's side, tasked with seeing to the needs of the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]].{{ref|name=Uriel|Events of [[Arena:The Imperial Palace|The Imperial Palace]] in [[Arena:Arena|''Arena'']]}}<noinclude>

===Legacy===
The Eternal Champion became somewhat of a cultural hero to Imperial Loyalists, many of whom retracing their steps as part of a pilgrimage.{{Ref|name=AMM|{{Cite Book|A Minor Maze}}}} Some remember them as one of the greatest warriors in the modern eras.{{ref|name=HT}} Their identity remained unknown to the public for years after their quest's completion,{{ref|name=HT}}{{ref|name=BHotE4|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 4}}}} which some claimed was at the will of Emperor Uriel Septim VII.{{ref|name=IWTW|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Interview With Three Writers|ns_base=GEN}}}}
