[[Lore:Dwynnen|Dwynnen]] is a barony found in the province of [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], in the region of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]]. Dwynnen played a large role in the battle against the [[Lore:Camoran Usurper|Camoran Usurper]], whose legion came across western Tamriel like an unbridled plague. It was the region's first baron, [[Lore:Othrok|Othrok]], who led the assault and overcame the warmonger.{{Ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}} 

Dwynnen is normally temperate,{{Ref|name=TWQ6|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v6}}}} and verdant all year long, except come wintertide.{{Ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Refugees}}}} It largely consists of woodland from the northernmost portion to the southern coastal region.{{Ref|name=DFMap|[[:File:DF-map-Iliac Bay (labeled).png|Map of the Iliac Bay – ''The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall'']]}} Dwynnen is neighbored by [[Lore:Anticlere|Anticlere]] to the southwest, [[Lore:Bhoriane|Bhoriane]] to the southeast, [[Lore:Kambria|Kambria]] to the east, [[Lore:Phrygias|Phrygias]] to the north, [[Lore:Urvaius|Urvaius]] to the west, and the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]] to the northeast.{{Ref|name=DFMap}}

The [[Lore:Order of the Raven|Order of the Raven]] is the barony's knightly order and they serve to protect both their country and their lordship.{{Ref|name=DFRaven|[[Daggerfall:Order of the Raven|Order of the Raven]] description in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} The patron deity of Dwynnen is [[Lore:Julianos|Julianos]]. The [[Lore:Thrafey|Thrafey]] clan of [[Lore:Vampire|vampires]] are active in Dwynnen.<noinclude>

==History==
[[File:DF-map-Iliac Bay.png|thumb|right|The Iliac Bay]]
In the historical-fiction, [[Lore:The Wolf Queen|the Wolf Queen]], Empress [[Lore:Kintyra II|Kintyra II]] and her armies marched through the Barony of Dwynnen during the [[Lore:War of the Red Diamond|War of the Red Diamond]], when they were suddenly attacked by an unnatural blizzard in {{Year|3E 121}}. Kintyra was absolutely certain that it was one of [[Lore:Potema|Potema]]'s wizard allies. She was accompanied by Duke {{Lore Link|Mentin}} of [[Lore:Glenpoint|Glenpoint]], who took her to his castle in his domain. It was there, Empress Kintyra II was executed by the Duke and Queen Potema.{{Ref|name=TWQ6}}

In the third century of the Third Era, the Camoran Usurper and his legion of warriors moved north from his domain in [[Lore:Valenwood|Valenwood]].{{Ref|name=TFOTU|{{Cite Book|The Fall of the Usurper}}}} The Usurper utilized the [[Lore:Nightmare Host|Nightmare Host]], a foul legion of undead led by an undying [[Lore:Lich|lich]] to take over Dwynnen and its people.{{Ref|name=TTET|{{Cite Book|The Third Era Timeline}}}} It was then, a man named Othrok was supposedly blessed by the [[Lore:Divines|Divines]] and led an army of men and animals to fight back against the unholy Nightmare Host. He emerged from the wilderness and defeated the undead at Castle Wightmoor on the 5th of [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]]. With his victory in the [[Lore:Battle of Wightmoor|Battle of Wightmoor]], Othrok was named the first Baron of Dwynnen and he soon expanded the province all the way to the Iliac Bay.{{Ref|name=TFOTU}} The warriors that served Othrok would later come together as the Baron's loyal vanguard and protector of his realm, the Order of the Raven.{{Ref|name=DFRaven}}

Before peace was fully restored, Baron Othrok needed to defeat the Camoran Usurper before he would conquer the northwestern world. By {{Year|3E 267}}, the Camoran Usurper approached onto the Iliac Bay and the [[Lore:Wrothgarian Mountains|Wrothgarian Mountains]], and came across a region that was ill-prepared for his arrival. With the major superpowers of the Iliac Bay unable to fight against the threat, Baron Othrok gathered the other nations, Kambria, Phrygias, and Ykalon, and formed a united naval force that rivaled the likes of [[Lore:Uriel Septim V|Uriel V]]'s invasion force of [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]. The combined force faced the Usurper at the [[Lore:Battle of Firewaves|Battle of Firewaves]] and defeated his army in that same year. Ever since then, the Baron's achievements are celebrated by Dwynnen's people in a holiday called [[Lore:Othroktide|Othroktide]], which takes place on the same day he reclaimed the barony.{{Ref|name=TFOTU}}

By {{Year|3E 405}}, the Barony of Dwynnen was ruled by an unknown Baron. When the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]] occurred in {{Year|3E 417}}, the Barony of Dwynnen became a vassal of the nearby Kingdom of [[Lore:Wayrest (kingdom)|Wayrest]], along with the rest of the eastern High Rock-Iliac region.{{Ref|name=TWITW|{{Cite Book|The Warp in the West}}}}

==City of Dwynnen==
[[File:DF-place-Dwynnen (city).png|thumb|left|Dwynnen]]
[[File:DF-Location-28615-Automap.png|thumb|right|Map of Dwynnen ca. 3E 405]]
'''Dwynnen''' is the capital of the eponymous barony, found deep in the west-central woodland, near the border toward Urvaius,{{Ref|name=LOMapAntho|[https://www.imperial-library.info/sites/default/files/imagecache/node-gallery-display/anthology%20map%202.jpg Iliac Bay Anthology Map – The Imperial Library]}} however sources such as the historical fictions, ''2920, The Last Year of the First Era'' and ''[[Lore:The Refugees|The Refugees]]'' report that it is located on the coast with its own port.{{Ref|name=2920LS|{{Cite Book|2920, Last Seed (v8)}}}} The seat of power in the capital city is Castle Wightmoor.{{Ref|name=TR}}

In the historical fiction, ''[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]'', it was reported that [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]]'s concubine, Lady [[Lore:Corda|Corda]] was escorted from across the Iliac Bay and through Dwynnen before she arrived to the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] to meet the Emperor.{{Ref|name=2920HF|{{Cite Book|2920, Hearth Fire (v9)}}}}  The book series also claims that the city was the home of the spy [[Lore:Cassyr Whitley|Cassyr Whitley]],{{Ref|name=2920HF}}{{Ref|name=2920FS|{{Cite Book|2920, First Seed (v3)}}}} whose faulty intelligence led to [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]]'s loss over the strategic fort [[Lore:Ald Marak|Ald Marak]] in the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]].{{Ref|name=TSOAM|{{Cite Book|The Siege of Ald Marak}}}}

The capital city was sacked during the Battle of Firewaves and the streets were set ablaze and filled with hazardous smoke. The people would hide in cellars to avoid the volley of fire and the Captain of Othrok's Guard would try to rally refugees to Castle Wightmoor for better protection. According to the book, ''the Refugees'', the mistress of the Camoran Usurper, Kaalys was present in Dwynnen and bore his son, [[Lore:Mankar Camoran|Mankar Camoran]].{{Ref|name=TR}}
