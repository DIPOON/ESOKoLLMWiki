[[Lore:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]] of the [[Lore:Stormcloak Clan|Stormcloak Clan]] was the Jarl of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]] and the leader of the [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Stormcloak Rebellion]]. Ulfric first rose to prominence due to his involvement in the Markarth Incident, which began when he and his militia successfully retook the city.{{Ref|name=CL|[[Legends:Card Lore#Markarth Bannermen|Markarth Bannermen]]'s card lore in ''[[Legends:Legends|Legends]]''}}{{ref|name=TBoM|{{Cite Book|The Bear of Markarth}}}}{{ref|name=SrJId|[[Skyrim:Igmund|Igmund]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} For retaking [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]], Ulfric and his militia were initially permitted to worship [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] by Jarl [[Lore:Hrolfdir|Hrolfdir]] and the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]]; however, they were soon arrested to appease the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]].{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG|{{Cite Book|The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide|ns_base=Books}}}}{{rp|501}} Following his release from prison, Ulfric and his militia known as the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]] engaged in skirmishes with the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]], although they failed to gain much support from [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]'s populace.{{ref|name=Hadvar|[[Skyrim:Hadvar|Hadvar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Vulwulf|[[Skyrim:Vulwulf Snow-Shod|Vulwulf Snow-Shod]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Solaf|[[Skyrim:Solaf|Solaf]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In {{Year|4E 201}}, Ulfric killed the High King of Skyrim, [[Lore:Torygg|Torygg]], in a duel, beginning a full-scale civil war against the Empire.{{ref|name=SRAlvd|[[Skyrim:Alvor|Alvor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Sybille|[[SR:Sybille Stentor|Sybille Stentor]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Ulfric was an incredibly divisive and controversial figure; while many of his supporters viewed him as a hero,{{ref|name=SrTSBd|[[Skyrim:Thongvor Silver-Blood|Thongvor Silver-Blood]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Jora|[[Skyrim:Jora|Jora]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrUSSd|[[Skyrim:Unmid Snow-Shod|Unmid Snow-Shod]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrHSHd|[[Skyrim:Hermir Strong-Heart|Hermir Strong-Heart]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} many of his detractors viewed him as a usurper and opportunist.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=SRGTd|[[Skyrim:General Tullius|General Tullius]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRLRd|[[Skyrim:Legate Rikke|Legate Rikke]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRJBtGd|[[Skyrim:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} In battle, he was known to wield heirlooms of his clan, such as his sword, and a battleaxe known as Bjornblad.{{ref|name=US|[[Castles:Ulfric's Sword|Ulfric's Sword]]'s description in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}{{ref|name=Bjornblad|[[Castles:Bjornblad|Bjornblad]]'s description in ''[[Castles:Castles|Castles]]''}}<noinclude>

==History==
===Background and the Great War===
[[File:LG-cardart-Ulfric Stormcloak 2.jpg|thumb|left|Ulfric Stormcloak (Legends)]]
Little is known about Ulfric prior to the [[Lore:Great War|Great War]]. He was the son of [[Lore:Hoag Stormcloak|Hoag Stormcloak]], the Jarl of [[Lore:Windhelm|Windhelm]].{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}}{{ref|name=Ulfric|[[Skyrim:Ulfric Stormcloak|Ulfric Stormcloak]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Ulfric and Jarl [[Lore:Balgruuf the Greater|Balgruuf the Greater]] of [[Lore:Whiterun|Whiterun]] were at odds with each other for many years.{{ref|name=SRGerd|[[Skyrim:Gerdur|Gerdur]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Circa {{Year|4E 161}}, when Ulfric was a lad, the [[Lore:Greybeards|Greybeards]] chose him to study the [[Lore:Way of the Voice|Way of the Voice]] and learn how to use the [[Lore:Thu'um|thu'um]] at [[Lore:High Hrothgar|High Hrothgar]], which was considered a great honor.{{ref|name=Ulfric}} He trained to become a Greybeard, spending nearly ten years at High Hrothgar and learning the Way of the Voice under [[Lore:Arngeir|Arngeir]] until the outbreak of the Great War in {{Year|4E 171}}.{{ref|name=Ulfric}}{{ref|name=TGW|{{Cite Book|The Great War (book)}}}}

Ulfric wanted to fight in the Great War, so he left High Hrothgar and joined the [[Lore:Imperial Legion|Imperial Legion]].{{ref|name=Ulfric}} While fighting in the Great War, Ulfric was captured by the [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]] during their campaign to take the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]]. Under interrogation, the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] learned of Ulfric's potential value as the son of the Jarl of Windhelm, and he was assigned as an asset to an interrogator named {{Lore Link|Elenwen}}, the future First Emissary and Ambassador to [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. Ulfric eventually broke under interrogation and was led to believe that the information he revealed was crucial in the capture of the Imperial City, although the city had in fact fallen before he broke. After the Thalmor finished interrogating him, they allowed him to escape to use him as an asset.{{ref|name=TDUS|{{Cite Book|Thalmor Dossier: Ulfric Stormcloak}}}} Following his escape, Ulfric rejoined the legion and distinguished himself fighting in the [[Lore:Battle of the Red Ring|Battle of the Red Ring]] in {{Year|4E 175}}, which recaptured the Imperial City from the Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}}

After the Battle of the Red Ring, the [[Lore:Third Empire|Empire]] and the Dominion signed the [[Lore:White-Gold Concordat|White-Gold Concordat]], ending the Great War.{{ref|name=TGW}} The White-Gold Concordat banned the worship of [[Lore:Talos|Talos]], which proved extremely controversial among the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]].{{ref|name=SR|{{Cite Book|Skyrim's Rule}}}}{{ref|name=FftT|{{Cite Book|Flight from the Thalmor}}}}{{ref|name=SrHeid|[[Skyrim:Heimskr|Heimskr]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SrNSSd|[[Skyrim:Nura Snow-Shod|Nura Snow-Shod]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===The Markarth Incident===
After the Great War, Ulfric returned to Skyrim and formed a Nord militia that would later be the basis for the [[Lore:Stormcloaks|Stormcloaks]].{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrTSBd}} According to Ulfric's Thalmor dossier, after the war, the Thalmor established contact with him, and he proved his worth as an asset.{{ref|name=TDUS}}

In {{Year|4E 174}}, the [[Lore:Reachmen|Reachmen]], led by [[Lore:Madanach|Madanach]], took advantage of the Imperial Legion's preoccupation with the Great War to overthrow [[Lore:Hrolfdir|Hrolfdir]], Jarl of [[Lore:Markarth|Markarth]], and declare [[Lore:The Reach|the Reach]] to be an independent kingdom.{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} By {{Year|4E 176}}, Hrolfdir was desperate to retake the Reach, so he turned to Ulfric and his Nord militia and promised them that he would permit the free worship of Talos in Markarth in exchange for their help retaking the hold.{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} In a short and bloody war, Ulfric and the militia laid siege to Markarth, where it is said he used the thu'um to shout rebels from the walls.{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrTSBd}}{{ref|name=SrKd|[[Skyrim:Kibell|Kibell]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} When Ulfric and his forces breached the city gates, they drove the Reachman rebels from the city.{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}} The surviving Reachman rebels fled into the hills and continued to fight the Empire in the [[Lore:Forsworn Rebellion|Forsworn Rebellion]].{{ref|name=TBoM}}{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=SrKd}}

In his book, titled ''[[Lore:The Bear of Markarth|The Bear of Markarth]]'', the imperial scholar Arrianus Arius alleges that after taking the city, Ulfric had every official who worked for the rebels executed. Ulfric's men allegedly tortured native women to learn the names of rebel fighters who fled the city or were in the hills of the Reach. Finally, he alleges that both Reachmen and Nords were executed if they did not fight with Ulfric and his men when they breached the gates.{{ref|name=TBoM}}

After Ulfric and the militia retook Markarth, Hrolfdir agreed to allow the free worship of Talos in the city.{{ref|name=SrJId}} When the Empire sent troops to reestablish order, Ulfric denied them entry into Markarth unless they also agreed to permit the free worship of Talos in the city, which they ultimately consented to.{{ref|name=TBoM}} However, the Thalmor soon found out about the promise of legal Talos worship and demanded the arrests of Ulfric and his men for violating the terms of the White-Gold Concordat or risk the possibility of another war with the Aldmeri Dominion.{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}}{{ref|name=Cedran|[[Skyrim:Cedran|Cedran]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Faced with the possibility of a second war with the Dominion, the Imperial Legion and Hrolfdir were forced to arrest and imprison Ulfric and much of the militia in an event called the Markarth Incident.{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}}{{ref|name=Cedran}} Some regard the Markarth Incident as the catalyst of the [[Lore:Stormcloak Rebellion|Stormcloak Rebellion]].{{ref|name=SrJId}}{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|501}}{{ref|name=Cedran}} Following the Markarth Incident, Emperor [[Lore:Titus Mede II|Titus Mede II]] had no choice but to suppress Talos worship by allowing the Thalmor Justiciars to enforce the terms of the treaty.{{ref|name=SRAlvd}}

===Jarl of Windhelm===
{|
|
:''"When finally set free, I returned to Windhelm, and was greeted by a city in mourning, at one with my own grief and anger. Clamoring in angry voices, calling out for justice, for war, they sat me on the throne. The Throne of Ysgramor! The throne of my father... I only hope I can prove worthy of the honor."''
|}
Ulfric perceived the arrests of himself and his militia during the Markarth Incident as a betrayal, causing him to become disillusioned with the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|263, 420}} He and his supporters believed the Empire had become too weak and subservient to the Thalmor to warrant continued support.{{ref|name=Ulfric}}{{ref|name=Galmar|[[Skyrim:Galmar Stone-Fist|Galmar Stone-Fist]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Yrsarald|[[Skyrim:Yrsarald Thrice-Pierced|Yrsarald Thrice-Pierced]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=NA|{{Cite Book|Nords Arise!}}}} Following the Markarth Incident, Ulfric became generally uncooperative to direct contact with the Thalmor and became classified as a dormant asset.{{ref|name=TDUS}} Soon after his imprisonment, Ulfric officially founded the Stormcloaks, a group dedicated to the worship of Talos and securing Skyrim's independence from the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}} During Ulfric's imprisonment, his father, Hoag, died in {{Year|4E 183}}.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}}{{ref|name=Ulfric}} Due to Ulfric's imprisonment at the time of Hoag's death, he was forced to deliver his father's eulogy via a letter he had smuggled out of prison.{{ref|name=Ulfric}}

After Hoag's death, Ulfric was released from prison to become the Jarl of Windhelm.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}} According to Ulfric, when he returned to Windhelm, he found the city's inhabitants in mourning, their angry voices clamoring for justice and calling for war. The people of the city then placed Ulfric on the Throne of [[Lore:Ysgramor|Ysgramor]].{{ref|name=Ulfric}} Ulfric and the Stormcloaks eventually began skirmishing with the Empire, although they failed to gain much support from Skyrim's populace.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=Vulwulf}}{{ref|name=Solaf}}

Some people considered Ulfric to be indifferent or even prejudiced toward Windhelm's [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] and [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]], in contrast to Hoag, who was generally more tolerant of them.{{ref|name=SOTGQ|{{Cite Book|Scourge of the Gray Quarter}}}}{{ref|name=SRAmbarys|[[SR:Ambarys Rendar|Ambarys Rendar]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRMalthyr|[[SR:Malthyr Elenil|Malthyr Elenil]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=SRBrunwulf|[[SR:Brunwulf Free-Winter|Brunwulf Free-Winter]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Ulfric was seen as indifferent to the Dunmer's poor living conditions in the Gray Quarter and did not stop Windhelm's Nord residents from harassing them.{{ref|name=SRAmbarys}}{{ref|name=SRMalthyr}}{{ref|name=SRBrunwulf}} After becoming jarl, Ulfric issued a decree that forbade the Argonians from living inside Windhelm's walls, forcing them to live and work on the city's docks.{{ref|name=Scouts|[[SR:Scouts-Many-Marshes|Scouts-Many-Marshes]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Torygg's Death===
[[File:LG-cardart-Ulfric's Zealot.png|left|thumb|Ulfric's Zealot (Legends)]]
When [[Lore:Istlod|Istlod]], High King of Skyrim, died, the [[Lore:Moot|Moot]] convened to elect his successor, which by tradition would be his son [[Lore:Torygg|Torygg]]. At the Moot, Ulfric talked about Skyrim's independence in terms just shy of treason, which Torygg reportedly respected. The Moot eventually voted to make Torygg the High King of Skyrim.{{ref|name=Sybille}}

In 4E 201, Ulfric traveled to [[Lore:Solitude|Solitude]], where he requested an audience with Torygg. Torygg and his court believed that Ulfric had traveled to Solitude to ask the High King to declare independence. However, Ulfric instead challenged Torygg to a duel in the [[Lore:Blue Palace|Blue Palace]]. Once the challenge was issued, Torygg had no choice but to accept it; if he had not, Ulfric would have had cause to call a new moot and hold a new vote for High King.{{ref|name=Sybille}} Although Torygg had some martial training, Ulfric and Torygg himself knew that he did not stand a chance against him.{{ref|name=Sybille}}{{ref|name=Ulfric}}{{ref|name=SRHKTd|[[Skyrim:High King Torygg|High King Torygg]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Accounts of the duel differ, with Ulfric claiming that he shouted Torygg to the ground and stabbed him through the heart with his sword, killing him.{{ref|name=Ulfric}} Other accounts claim that Ulfric literally shouted Torygg apart.{{ref|name=SRAlvd}}{{ref|name=Sybille}}{{ref|name=Elisif|[[SR:Elisif the Fair|Elisif the Fair]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Regardless, after killing Torygg, Ulfric fled and was able to escape Solitude when one of the city guards named Roggvir opened the gates for him.{{ref|name=Aldis|[[SR:Captain Aldis|Captain Aldis]]' dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Vivienne|[[SR:Vivienne Onis|Vivienne Onis]]' dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Greta|[[SR:Greta|Greta]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Ulfric stated that he killed Torygg to prove that he was merely a weak puppet king of the Empire and that Skyrim needed a high king who was not imperially appointed.{{ref|name=Ulfric}} Ulfric believed that Torygg would be a symbol of the perceived problems facing Skyrim, including the Empire's weakness, the White-Gold Concordat, and the banning of Talos worship.{{ref|name=Sybille}} Ulfric also believed that killing Torygg would send a message to the other jarls.{{ref|name=Ulfric}} Ulfric's detractors viewed his actions as those of a self-serving opportunist who killed Torygg to advance his ambition of becoming high king.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=SRLRd}}{{ref|name=SRJBtGd}}{{ref|name=Elisif}} Additionally, critics, along with some of his supporters, contended that Ulfric used the widely unpopular ban on Talos worship as a rallying cry merely to gain support for his bid to become high king.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=Laila|[[SR:Laila Law-Giver|Laila Law-Giver]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Dengeir|[[SR:Dengeir of Stuhn|Dengeir of Stuhn]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Saerlund|[[SR:Saerlund|Saerlund]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

===Skyrim Civil War===
[[File:SR-quest-Unbound 06.jpg|thumb|left|Ulfric being transported to Helgen (Skyrim)]][[File:SR-npc-Ulfric Stormcloak 05.jpg|thumb|right|Ulfric in Helgen (Skyrim)]]
For killing Torygg in what they viewed as an assassination, the Empire branded Ulfric a murderer and traitor,{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=SRAlvd}}{{ref|name=SRGTd}} while some Nords believed the duel was legal and true to the old ways of Skyrim,{{ref|name=Ulfric}}{{ref|name=SRGerd}}{{ref|name=SRRogd|[[Skyrim:Roggvir|Roggvir]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} officially beginning a full-scale civil war known as the Stormcloak Rebellion. In the civil war, the eastern holds of [[Lore:Eastmarch|Eastmarch]], [[Lore:Winterhold (region)|Winterhold]], [[Lore:The Pale|the Pale]], and [[Lore:The Rift|the Rift]] supported the Stormcloaks.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|261}}{{ref|name=SRLRd}}{{ref|name=THoS}} The western holds of [[Lore:Haafingar|Haafingar]], [[Lore:Hjaalmarch|Hjaalmarch]], the Reach, and [[Lore:Falkreath Hold|Falkreath]] continued to support the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|261}}{{ref|name=THoS|{{Cite Book|The Holds of Skyrim}}}} [[Lore:Whiterun Hold|Whiterun]] was neutral, but Jarl Balgruuf was more supportive of the Empire.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|532}}{{ref|name=SRJBtGd}} After Torygg's death, his widow, [[Lore:Elisif the Fair|Elisif the Fair]], succeeded him as Jarl of Solitude and became the Imperial-backed candidate for high queen.{{ref|name=SRGTd}}{{ref|name=SRGerd}}

After the outbreak of the civil war, a few months prior to Last Seed 4E 201, Titus II sent General [[Lore:Tullius|Tullius]] to Skyrim to serve as the province's military governor.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=SRGTd}} On the 15th of Last Seed, Ulfric and a small group of his men were ambushed by the Imperial Legion outside [[Lore:Darkwater Crossing|Darkwater Crossing]] in southern Eastmarch and surrendered without much of a fight.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=Ralof|[[SR:Ralof|Ralof]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} After surrendering, Ulfric was gagged to prevent him from using the thu'um and carted with other prisoners for two days until they arrived in [[Lore:Helgen|Helgen]] for execution, stopping short of their planned destination, Cyrodiil, on Tullius' orders.{{ref|name=Hadvar}}{{ref|name=Ralof}} The Thalmor attempted to intervene and prevent Ulfric's execution, as they wanted the civil war to continue,{{ref|name=TDUS}} but this attempt was unsuccessful, and Ulfric was lined up for the chopping block.{{ref|name=SRUnbound|Events of [[SR:Unbound|Unbound]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}

Before Ulfric could be executed, the [[Lore:Dragon|dragon]] [[Lore:Alduin|Alduin]] attacked Helgen, allowing him to escape.{{ref|name=SRUnbound}} After escaping Helgen, Ulfric returned to Windhelm and continued his rebellion.{{ref|name=ESVSPOGG}}{{rp|552-553}}{{ref|name=Delphine|[[SR:Delphine|Delphine]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}}{{ref|name=Orgnar|[[SR:Orgnar|Orgnar]]'s dialogue in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} Ulfric's housecarl, Galmar Stone-Fist, eventually persuaded him to recover the [[Lore:Jagged Crown|Jagged Crown]] to legitimize his claim to the High Kingship of Skyrim.{{ref|name=Ulfric}}{{ref|name=Galmar}}{{ref|name=SRTJC|Events of [[SR:The Jagged Crown (Stormcloaks)|The Jagged Crown]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} However, it is unknown if the Stormcloaks were successful in recovering the Jagged Crown from the ruins of [[Lore:Korvanjund|Korvanjund]]. Eventually, Jarl Balgruuf was forced to end his neutrality and pick a side in the war after Ulfric issued him an ultimatum in the old way: sending him his axe. Balgruuf, after consulting the members of his court, decided to side with the Empire, reluctantly allowing General Tullius to garrison troops in his city. Shortly after Balgruuf's decision, the Stormcloaks arrived, initiating the Battle for Whiterun. Getting the drop on the Imperials, the Stormcloaks set up catapults and began bombarding the city with flaming projectiles.{{ref|name=SRBFW|Events of [[SR:Battle for Whiterun|Battle for Whiterun]] in ''[[SR:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} The outcome of the battle is unknown.
