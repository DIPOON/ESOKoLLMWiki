# Lore:Maormer

<noinclude>{{Trail|Races}}[[Category:Lore-All Terms]][[File:ON-npc-Castire.jpg|thumb|left|A Maormer]]{{TOCright}}</noinclude>
[[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]], also known as '''Sea Elves''', '''Tropical Elves''',{{Ref|name=PGE1WR|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|The Wild Region}}}} '''Pyandoneans'''{{Ref|name=TWQ4|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v4}}}} (or derogatorily as '''Fish Elves'''), are a race of [[Lore:Mer|Mer]] that reside on the island kingdom of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]], far south of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]]. They possess many traits strange for elves, and they are theorized to be the result of interbreeding between [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] and the indigenous [[Lore:Beastfolk|bestial tribes]] of Pyandonea.{{ref|name=ACoV|{{Cite Book|Ayleid Cities of Valenwood}}}}  Similar to their [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] cousins in build,{{ref|name=TSE|{{Cite Book|The Scaled Elves}}}} their skin can range in tone from pearlescent white{{ref|name=ESOKonnugil|[[ON:Konnugil the Leviathan|Konnugil the Leviathan]]'s appearance in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} or grey{{ref|name=ESODespina|[[ON:Despina|Despina]]'s physical appearance in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} to blue, and [[Lore:Maormer Leviathans|Maormer Leviathans]] can have scales.{{ref|name=ESOSnakeskin|Physical appearance of [[ON:Snakeskin Brute|Snakeskin Brutes]] and [[ON:Snakeskin Berserker|Snakeskin Berserkers]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Some Maormer have strange, chameleon-like skin{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} that is entirely colorless, as if their flesh were made of some white limpid jelly.{{Ref|name=TWQ4}} Some Maormer ears are broader than those of other mer, and are flared along the outer ear like a clip point blade. Their ears are shaped in such a way that they resemble fins.{{ref|name=ESOSnakeskin}} Maormer eyes can be pale blue, with a faint distinction between the sclera and the iris. Their eyes can also be blank.{{ref|name=ESODespina}} They also may have gills, though it is unclear if this claim was literal.{{ref|name=DS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 110: Dreadsails Style}}}} Their tattoos are elaborate, and they tend to display a vicious demeanor towards the denizens of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].{{ref|name=TSE}} Maormer are acclimated to life on the waves,{{ref|name=DespinaDia|[[ON:Despina|Despina]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and are steady on their feet in even the roughest waters.{{ref|name=ASGTSE|{{Cite Book|A Sailor's Guide to Sea Elves}}}} Maormer tongues can filter saltwater and make it potable.{{ref|name=MSD|[[Online:Contraband M#Maormer Salt Drinker|Maormer Salt Drinker]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It is believed that Maormer bones are naturally springy.{{Ref|name=MBJH|[[ON:Contraband M#Maormer Bone Jaw Harp|Maormer Bone Jaw Harp]] item description in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Among the Maormer, the leviathans are the most feared. They have been described by one scholar as "the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous [[Lore:Sex#Racial Compatibility and Reproduction|coupling]] of Sea Elf and [[Lore:Sea Serpent|Sea Serpent]]". They are scaly and massive, standing a head and a half over the tallest Nords when they draw themselves to their full height, which they don't often do because they carry themselves in a beastly posture. Maormer leviathans are usually hunched over, their backs bent and arced like a snake ready to strike. When they walk, their movements are akin to slithering in an uneven predatory lope. The Leviathans appear unused to using their legs on land. They move deftly in the water, and can climb effortlessly up the slippery sides of ships. They are capable of stretching their jaws out of place to swallow their prey.{{ref|name=TSE}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

==History==
It was once believed that the Maormer of Pyandonea were originally exiled from the Summerset Isle. However, translations of tapestries in the [[Lore:Crystal Tower|Crystal Tower]] tell the tale of how the Maormer were likely separated in their original homeland of [[Lore:Aldmeris|Aldmeris]].{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}}{{ref|name=TCPOA|{{Cite Book|The Chosen People of Aldmeris}}}} According to the legend, the Maormer leader [[Lore:Orgnum|King Orgnum]] was once a phenomenally wealthy Aldmer nobleman, who used his finances to launch a rebellion against the powers of the land. He and his followers were banished for this to a place separated from Aldmeris by an impenetrable mist, Pyandonea, "The Veil of Mist". This boundary proved so effective that the followers of Orgnum never again disturbed their former countrymen in Aldmeris.{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}}

The Maormer were relentless in their drive to conquer Summerset, and there is scarcely a year throughout the [[Lore:First Era|First]] or [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]]s when they did not ravage the coastlines of the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]].{{Ref|name=PGE3S|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Summerset}}}} Every attack launched by the Maormer against Summerset has been led by Orgnum himself, and despite the number of wars and strategies employed by the Maormer, each of these attacks has proved to be an ultimate failure.{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}} They also briefly controlled [[Lore:Khenarthi's Roost|Khenarthi's Roost]], and established a treaty with the resident Khajiit.{{Ref|name=ToKR|{{Cite Book|Treaty of Khenarthi's Roost}}}}

Maormer slavers controlled the hinterlands of [[Lore:Hew's Bane|Hew's Bane]] in the latter years of the First Era, and were still active circa {{Year|2E 582}}.{{ref|[[Online:Thrall Cove (quest)|Thrall Cove]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}{{ref|name=MSM|[[Online:Contraband M#Maormer Slave Manacles|Maormer Slave Manacles]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} They were also active in Elsweyr and cooperated with [[Lore:Euraxians|Euraxians]].{{ref|name=ESOJahhouz|[[Online:Jahhouz|Jahhouz]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

The Maormer were allies of the second [[Lore:Aldmeri Dominion|Aldmeri Dominion]],{{Ref|name=PGE1AD|{{Cite Book|PGE|1|Aldmeri Dominion}}}} although the Dominion allowed the kings of [[Lore:Colovia|Colovia]] to eliminate Maormer outposts that had been established along the western coast of the mainland.{{Ref|name=PGE3S}}

The last documented appearance of the Maormer was in {{Year|3E 110}} in the [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]].{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}} The united alliance of the kings of Summerset and [[Lore:Antiochus|Antiochus Septim]],{{ref|name=BHOTE1|{{Cite Book|Brief History of the Empire v 1}}}} combining the Imperial fleet and the royal navies of Summerset Isle, together with the magical powers of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]], succeeded in destroying the Pyandonean invading fleet.{{Ref|name=TWQ5|{{Cite Book|The Wolf Queen, v5}}}} It was said that the storm brewed by the Psijic of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] so annihilated Orgnum's fleet that he was never again able to muster together enough of a force to dare another battle.{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}}

==Culture==
[[File:ON-concept-Maormer Totems.png|thumb|right|Maormer totems]][[File:ON-crown store-Maormer Boarding Party Furnishing Pack.jpg|left|thumb|Maormer furnishings]]
The Maormer leader, King Orgnum, is a deathless wizard{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} who it seems is not only immortal but grows more youthful by the century.{{Ref|name=PGE3OL|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Other Lands}}}} He is said to be the Serpent God of the [[Lore:Satakal|Satakal]].{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} Under Orgnum, Maormer identity and culture is defined by the goal of capturing the Summerset Isles and hatred for the Altmer; this is to the extent that it has been described as "practically all [the Maormer] live for".{{ref|name=ONCastire|[[ON:Castire|Castire]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} The Maormer of Pyandonea live in a clan-based society, where some breakaway clans have fallen into near-barbarism, while the core clans, loyal to King Orgnum, remain organized and disciplined martial forces.{{Ref|name=CM64:PS|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 64: Pyandonean Style}}}} Additionally, the Maormer were also known to have coastal princes. The saying "You can't cross the strait just by staring at the water." is attributed to them.{{Ref|name=MVV|[[ON:Master Metrand Vyrix|Master Metrand Vyrix]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Maormer speak the unique language of Pyandonea, a tongue said to be difficult to learn in comparison to [[Lore:Tamrielic|Tamrielic]].{{Ref|name=TWQ4}} They have their own slang as well, and are known to refer to other races as "groundwalkers"{{Ref|name=MaormerGreetings|[[Online:Greetings|Maormer Greetings]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} or "landlegs".{{ref|[[Online:Loraviel|Loraviel]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:ESO|ESO]]}}

It is unknown what other entities besides Orgnum and Satakal are followed or worshiped by Maormer, but they acknowledge numerous gods.{{ref|name=Ulondil|[[ON:Ulondil|Ulondil]]'s dialogue in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} They speak of [[Lore:Mother Sea|Mother Sea]] in reverence, but whether they refer to the entity or place is unknown.{{ref|name=ONCastire}}{{ref|group=UOL|name=CCS|[[ON:Cut Content#Summerset|Summerset Cut Content]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} At least some Pyandonean Maormer followed [[Lore:Hermaeus Mora|Hermaeus Mora]],{{Ref|name=Sea Elf Galleon Helm|[[ON:Sea Elf Galleon Helm|Sea Elf Galleon Helm]] antiquity codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} and [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]].{{Ref|name=OCST|[[ON:Captain Virindi Slave-Taker|Captain Virindi Slave-Taker]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]&#58; ''[[Online:Thieves Guild (DLC)|Thieves Guild]]''}}

The Maormer employ coin-sized coral discs intricately carved with sea serpent depictions as prayer charms, designed to float when tossed into water.{{Ref|name=TC|[[ON:Treasures C#Coral Prayer Charms|Coral Prayer Charms]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]<nowiki>:</nowiki> ''[[Online:Firesong|Firesong]]''}} Sea [[Lore:Witch|witches]] are religious figures or magic leaders among the Sea Elves.{{ref|name=Umindior|[[ON:Umindior|Umindior]]'s dialogue in {{g|ESO|firesong}}}} The Maormer have a tale that relates the origins of [[Lore:Masser|Masser]] and [[Lore:Secunda|Secunda]] to Maormer twins Chrysina and Ohmirril, who dove deep into cursed [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] waters.{{ref|name=TTM|{{Cite Book|Tamrielic Twins of Myth}}}}

The Maormer are believed to practice a powerful form of [[Lore:Snake Magic|snake magic]], which they use to tame the sea serpents of their island for use as steeds and warbeasts.{{Ref|name=PGE1WR}} These sea serpents serve as King Orgnum's guards and occasional mounts.{{Ref|name=PGE3OL}} Local legends in Hew's Bane allege that in the latter days of the First Era, Maormeri snake magic was used on ensorcelled locks when capturing slaves to ensure that they remained docile and compliant.{{ref|name=CSL|[[ON:Hew's Bane Antiquities#Coiled Serpent Lock|Coiled Serpent Lock]] antiquity codex entry in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Maormeri ships are described as looking insectoid, with membranous sails and a rugged chitin hull, flying a markedly alien flag.{{Ref|name=TWQ4}} Maormer use [[Lore:Fire Salts|fire salts]] to warm their food on long voyages.{{ref|[[Online:Tildur#Dialogue|Tildur]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

Maormer sailors are nimble acrobats who excel at boarding enemy ships.{{ref|name=ASGTSE}} The Maormer have a reputation for being adept storm mages. They have harnessed the power of water, air, and lightning to assault their enemies.{{ref|name=Neidir|[[ON:Stormreeve Neidir|Stormreeve Neidir]]'s abilities in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} Their mages also conjure [[Lore:Storm Atronach|Storm Atronach]]s, and can shape them to look like animals, such as bears and tigers.{{ref|name=ESOStormSenche|Appearance of summoned [[ON:Storm Atronach Bear|Storm Atronach Bears]] and [[ON:Storm Atronach Senche|Senche]] alongside their Maormer conjurers within [[ON:Sunhold|Sunhold]] in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} During naval battles, Maormer Sea Mages conjure winds and storms to toss their foes' ships around on the waves.{{ref|name=ASGTSE}} 

The Maormer have many methods of using storm magic to obliterate their foes. They can whip up hurricanes with the intent to destroy entire fleets. They can do so with a ritual using serpent-shaped storm totems to siphon life energy from sacrifices marked with runes. Blood sacrifices such as these are only utilized when a summoner needs to rapidly accrue and release a lot of energy. The energy from the totems can be used to form powerful hurricanes,{{ref|name=ESOMastengwe|[[ON:Mastengwe|Mastengwe]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Cast Adrift|Cast Adrift]]}} or it can be funneled into a storm atronach, which can only hold so much energy before it discorporates. Maormer mages can use wind energies to bind the creature until it performs its intended function. When it explodes, it releases a wave of energy that kills everything it touches, except for creatures with an affinity for storm energy, such as [[Lore:Thunderbug|Thunderbug]]s.{{ref|name=ESOEalcil|[[ON:Ealcil|Ealcil]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Tempest Unleashed|The Tempest Unleashed]]}}

The Maormer breed and train a wide variety of marine predators as pets and tools for combat. The winged reef vipers are commonly trained for warfare, and can leap high out of the water to land on an enemy vessel.{{ref|name=ASGTSE}} Sea Serpents large enough to capsize warships are also among the beasts the Maormer have domesticated for warfare.{{ref|name=ASGTSE}}{{ref|name=PE|Events of [[ON:Pelidil's End|Pelidil's End]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} [[Lore:Ornaug|Ornaugs]], marine reptiles resembling canines, are often domesticated and used as mounts.{{ref|Ornaugs at [[ON:Dreadsail Reef|Dreadsail Reef]] and other parts of the Systres Archipelago in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}
