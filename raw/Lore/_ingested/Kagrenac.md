Lord [[Lore:Kagrenac|Kagrenac]] (sometimes '''Kagrnak'''{{ref|name=CHIM}}), known as the '''Shop Foremer''',{{ref|name=PoM|{{Cite Book|People of Morrowind|ns_base=GEN}}}} was the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemeri]] Chief Tonal Architect during the time of the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]]. The foremost arcane philosopher and [[Lore:Smithing|mage-smith]] of the Dwemer, he was a "pioneer" in the field of  mythopoeic forces, and devised [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|tools]] with which to harness them with the intent of transcending the limits of Dwemer mortality.{{ref|name=MWYagrum|[[MW:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=KT|{{Cite Book|Kagrenac's Tools (book)}}}}{{ref|name=DUP|{{Cite Book|Dagoth Ur's Plans}}}} Kagrenac was the primary architect working on the [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|Heart of Lorkhan]] and was responsible for creating the original Brass God, [[Lore:Numidium|Anumidium]].{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}} According to some theories, it may have been his use of his tools on the Heart that caused the Dwemer to disappear from the face of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] in {{Year|1E 700}}.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{ref|name=PTDDU|{{Cite Book|Plan to Defeat Dagoth Ur}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
Kagrenac was known as a meticulous and careful planner.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}} He has been attributed with many titles over the years, among them "High Priest", "Magecrafter", and "High Engineer".{{ref|name=KT}}{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} Master Crafter [[Lore:Yagrum Bagarn|Yagrum Bagarn]], the last living Dwemer, worked as one of Kagrenac's Tonal Architects and knew him personally, and he used "High Craftlord" as Kagrenac's proper title.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{ref|name=PTDDU}} [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] was inconsistent, calling him "High Engineer" and "Kagrenac the Blighter", though he also corroborated "High Craftlord", as well.{{ref|name=PTDDU}}{{ref|name=TBORM}}{{ref|name=36L36|{{Cite Book|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36}}}} Kagrenac had numerous underlings; sources indicate [[Lore:Dahrk Mezalf|Dahrk Mezalf]] and [[Lore:Bthuand Mzahnch|Bthuand Mzahnch]] (author of ''[[Lore:The Egg of Time|The Egg of Time]]'') were just two other prominent smiths who worked under him.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{Ref|name=DMQ|Events of [[Morrowind:Dahrk Mezalf (quest)|Dahrk Mezalf]] in {{g|Morrowind}}}} Among their many amazing creations was [[Lore:Moon-and-Star|Moon-and-Star]], a specially enchanted ring that could only be worn by Nerevar, thus serving as absolute proof of their identity.{{ref|name=MWTelvanni|[[MW:House Telvanni|House Telvanni]] dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Kagrenac's Plans===
[[File:TR-place-Bamz-Amschend, Radac's Forge.jpg|An unfinished giant construct constructed from pieces in Kagrenac's Workshop|thumb|left]]
[[File:MW-book-Divine Metaphysics p3.jpg|thumb|right|Dwemeri designs explaining Kagrenac's teachings]]
His theories on how to make a new god using the Heart of Lorkhan were explained in ''[[Lore:Divine Metaphysics|Divine Metaphysics]]'', though the text remained indecipherable to anyone lacking detailed knowledge of [[Lore:Dwemeris|Dwemeris]]. Yagrum Bagarn, though he did not work on the Anumidium project, was able to understand Kagrenac's theories, but refused to explain them, as he believed Kagrenac's theories should die along with him.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}

As his alternate title "High Priest" suggests, Kagrenac was, for all intents and purposes, a religious leader in the little-understood Dwemer traditions. The precepts of Kagrenac were taught at the Dwemer settlement Kherakah, where it is said the most learned people in the world made their home. They spent their days studying Kagrenac's words and giving consideration to their place in the life to come. In Kherakah, nothing was more valued than the understanding of the self and its relationship to the Heart.{{ref|name=NFAF|{{Cite Book|Nchunak's Fire and Faith}}}} Kagrenac's library was found in a settlement known as Tureynulal.{{ref|name=Morrowind|[[Morrowind:Tureynulal|Tureynulal]] in {{g|Morrowind}}}}

However, the Dwemer were not unified in their thinking about Kagrenac's theories. While Kagrenac and some of his architects emphasized the benefits, others believed that an attempt to apply his theories posed risks which were unjustifiable, noting the unpredictable side effects of his formulas and the potential for catastrophic error.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{ref|name=MWBaladas|[[MW:Baladas Demnevanni (person)|Baladas Demnevanni]]'s dialogue in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

The [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] historian {{Lore Link|Borgusilus Malier}} first theorized that in {{Year|1E 668}}, the powerful Dwemeri philosopher-sorcerer "Kagrnak" used the silent and magical ability of the Dwemer known as "the Calling" to gather his people together for a great, sublime journey, resulting in their disappearance.{{ref|name=CHIM|{{Cite Book|Chimarvamidium}}}}

===The Anumidium Project===
At least one legend asserts that Kagrenac began acting on his theories in secret, implying that his subterfuge led directly to the [[Lore:War of the First Council|War of the First Council]]. King [[Lore:Dumac|Dumac]], knowing nothing about his High Craftlord's project, denied its existence to his friend [[Lore:Nerevar|Nerevar]], then took great offense when Nerevar returned later to demand their designs for the Heart of Lorkhan cease. The same legend says the High Craftlord was killed by [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]] during the [[Lore:Battle of Red Mountain|Battle of Red Mountain]], and it was Nerevar, with the aid of [[Lore:Azura|Azura]], who turned the Dwemer to dust.{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}} Vivec remained silent on whether Dumac knew of Kagrenac's project, but he does note that Kagrenac took great offense to Nerevar's assertions when the Hortator first questioned Dumac about it. He also notes that it was Dagoth Ur who, through unknown means, first discovered Kagrenac's plans for the Heart of Lorkhan and informed the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] leadership.{{ref|name=TBORM}} 

Most accounts of the Battle of Red Mountain do not say that Dagoth Ur killed Kagrenac, but rather, that Kagrenac used his tools on the Heart at the climax of the battle, coinciding with the moment that all Dwemer then on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] disappeared.{{ref|name=MWYagrum}}{{Ref|name=KT}}{{ref|name=PTDDU}}{{ref|name=TBORM}}

==Creations==
===Kagrenac's Tools===
[[File:MW-item-Dwemer Schematic.png|Wraitguard and Keening Dwemer Schematic|thumb|right]]
{{Lore:Kagrenac's Tools}} 

The gauntlet '''Wraithguard''' was created to safely wield the weapon tools, '''Sunder''' and '''Keening'''. Wielding the weapons without the gauntlet will often lead to the instant death of the wielder. Wraithguard also functions to empower the abilities of Keening and Sunder. Accounts of its appearance vary, but it typically looks like a gauntlet made of a material comparable to brass. 

The hammer Sunder was created to produce a specific amount of power from the Heart. When struck by Sunder, the Heart would release godlike power.It is described as a hammer of divine mass. 

The short blade Keening was created to flay and focus the power that the hammer Sunder produced. In song, it's described as a dagger made of the sound of the shadow of the moons.

==Notes==
* In ''[[Lore:Dagoth Ur's Plans|Dagoth Ur's Plans]]'', it is noted that Dagoth Ur, who used a ritual of his own devising to bind himself to the Heart of Lorkhan, had apparently adopted the views and motivations of Kagrenac.
* ''[[Lore:36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36|36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36]]'', contrary to every other source (even other works by Vivec), asserts that Kagrenac actually got his "walking star" Anumidium working at the Battle of Red Mountain. The great destruction it wrought created the [[Lore:Inner Sea|Inner Sea]], and it was only through the intervention of the Tribunal that it was defeated and the Dwemer were removed from the world.
