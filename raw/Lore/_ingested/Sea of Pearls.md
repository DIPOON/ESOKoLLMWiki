The [[Lore:Sea of Pearls|Sea of Pearls]] is a large body of water found west of the continent of [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] and its western waters, the [[Lore:Abecean Sea|Abecean Sea]].  It consists of the western-half of the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], the outer reaches of the [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]] sea-complex,{{fact}} but it mostly contains other continents beyond Tamriel's grasp, such as the sunken-islands of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]]. According to some accounts the dreaded archipelago of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]], and the underwater kingdom of [[Lore:Ul'vor Kus|Ul'vor Kus]] are within this sea. It is part of the greater [[Lore:Eltheric Ocean|Eltheric Ocean]] and it is neighbored by the Abecean Sea to the east, the [[Lore:Azurian Sea|Azurian Sea]] in the north, and both the [[Lore:Cape of the Blue Divide|Cape of the Blue Divide]] and the [[Lore:Southern Sea|Southern Sea]] to the southeast.{{Ref|name=RGMap|[[:File:RG-map-West Tamriel-1024x768.png|Map of West Tamriel – ''The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'']]}} The Sea of Pearls acquired its name from the myriad shells that wash up on the shores of its islands; they tend to have whorls like [[Lore:Nautilus|nautilus]] shells, which are light-weight and durable.{{Ref|name=ONStranglers|[[Online:Thrassian Stranglers|Nautilus Shell Guards]] codex entry in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Greymoor|Greymoor]]''}} Sailors call the waters around Thras the '''Cursed Sea'''.{{ref|name=COE|Dialogue with [[ON:Captain One-Eye|Captain One-Eye]]}}<noinclude>

==History==
====The Summerset Isles and its Ancient Rivals====
[[File:ON-place-Sea Keep.jpg|thumb|right|Sea Keep (ESO)]]
In the mid-[[Lore:Merethic Era|Merethic Era]], the [[Lore:Aldmer|Aldmer]] settled on what is now known as the [[Lore:Summerset Isles|Summerset Isles]], spreading onto the island's western coast, building great cities such as [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]], [[Lore:Corgrad|Corgrad]], and [[Lore:Lillandril|Lillandril]], even expanding into islands off the coast, on the Sea of Pearls (i.e., [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]], [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]] and [[Lore:Wasten Coridale|Wasten Coridale]]).{{Ref|name=AEXII|{{Cite Book|Auridon Explored, Chapter XII}}}} The Summerset Elves eventually became the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]], and built large, illustrious kingdoms, while staying to themselves throughout history, as the races of Tamriel evolved. Over time, the High Elves conflicted with their neighbors, the [[Lore:Maormer|Maormer]] of [[Lore:Pyandonea|Pyandonea]], and the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]]. The latter race are the inhabitants of the Sea of Pearls, residing in an archipelago of [[Lore:Coral|coral]]. The Summerset Isles had been in constant conflict with these races, over the control of the eternal isles, so much so that the province's north and west coast is heavily-defended, for the purpose of protecting the island from the Sload and Maormer. {{Lore Link|Cey-Tarn Keep|Cey-Tarn}} and [[Lore:Sea Keep|Sea Keep]] were among the many forts made on the Sea of Pearls.{{Ref|name=ESOCeyTarn|[[Online:Cey-Tarn Keep|Cey-Tarn Keep]] loading screen in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} There have been many battles between the High Elves and the Sload, such as the {{Lore Link|Sack of Skywatch}} ({{Year|1E 1301}}) and the [[Lore:War of the Uvichil|War of the Uvichil]] ({{Year|1E 2911}} — {{Year|1E 2917}}).{{Ref|name=PGE3ES|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Summerset}}}} All the while, the Altmer and the Maormer held several skirmishes in the waters between them. In {{Year|2E 486}}, High King [[Lore:Hidellith|Hidellith]] conflicted with a small fleet off the coast of Alinor, which brought them to their homeland, the island-kingdom of Pyandonea. The last known appearance of the Maormer was in {{Year|3E 110}}, during the [[Lore:War of the Isle|War of the Isle]]. It was King [[Lore:Orgnum|Orgnum]]'s fleet against the Kingdoms of Summerset, and the support of Emperor [[Lore:Antiochus|Antiochus]]' Imperial Navy. It took the arcane might of the [[Lore:Psijic Order|Psijic Order]] to defeat the grand fleet.{{Ref|name=PGE3EOL|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition/Other Lands}}}}

====The Lost Islands of Yokuda & the Ra Gada====
[[File:ON-place-Tu'whacca's Throne.jpg|thumb|left|Tu'whacca's Throne (ESO)]]
In the distant west was the island-continent of [[Lore:Yokuda|Yokuda]], the ancient homeland of the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s. It was completely surrounded by the Sea of Pearls, and it is the furthest known extent of the body of water.{{Ref|name=RGMap}} In the late years of the continent, it was ruled Emperor [[Lore:Hira|Hira]], whose attempt to take control over the Yokudan Empire from the common-folk, by persecuting the [[Lore:Sword-Singers|Sword-Singers]], warriors that followed a martial philosophy regarding the mastery of the sword known as the ''"Way of the Sword."'' Thus began the [[Lore:War of the Singers|War of the Singers]], a critical moment in the Yokudan's history, a battle between Emperor Hira's forces and [[Lore:Frandar Hunding|Frandar Hunding]]'s sword-singers. The war came to a climax at the foothills of [[Lore:Hattu Mountain|Hattu Mountain]], where Hunding's warriors were able to obliterate Hira's rag-tag armies, but at the cost of more than three hundred thousand warriors. Hunding and the sword-singers left Yokuda from the sea-city of {{Lore Link|Arch}}, for new and unknown lands across the Sea of Pearls, which was the eventual province of [[Lore:Hammerfell|Hammerfell]].{{Ref|name=RTHaTH|{{Cite Book|Redguards, History and Heroes}}}}

The Yokudans remained on their island-continent for several years after Frandar Hunding's departure to Hammerfell. In {{Year|1E 792}}, the islands of Yokuda had sunken into the Sea of Pearls, reportedly lost forever. To this day, there have been many theories regarding the cause of its extinction, whether it was natural causes, or caused by its people. One such source was that it was caused by a series of earthquakes that targeted the very foundation of the islands. Another idea was that the renegade group of [[Lore:Ansei|Ansei]] ( the highest-ranked sword-singers) known as the {{Lore Link|Hiradirge}}, masters of [[Lore:Stone Magic|stone magic]], were the cause of the island's destruction, using a forbidden sword technique. It was their way of ruining what they could not rule,{{Ref|name=PGE3EOL}} which is further corroborated by an account from the [[Lore:Celestial|Celestial]] incarnation of [[Lore:The Warrior (constellation)|the Warrior]].{{Ref|name=ESOWarrior|[[Online:The Warrior (Celestial)|The Warrior]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} Regardless, the refugees of Yokuda sailed eastward across the Sea of Pearls, in the same direction as Frandar Hunding, to the island of [[Lore:Herne (island)|Herne]]. From there, the [[Lore:Ra Gada|Ra Gada]] paved their way through Hammerfell, and re-settled as the [[Lore:Redguard|Redguard]]s of Hammerfell.{{Ref|name=PGE1EH|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Hammerfell}}}}

====The Thrassian Plague & the All-Flags Navy====
[[File:ON-place-Statue of Syrabane.jpg|thumb|right|Syrabane, the Apprentices' God]]
In {{Year|1E 2200}}, the Sload of Thras had spread the terrifying disease known in history as the [[Lore:Thrassian Plague|Thrassian Plague]]. The plague caused wide-spread pandemonium across the provinces of western Tamriel, claiming half of the lives of Tamriel, bringing the end of ancient civilizations like the Sixteen Kingdoms of [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]],{{Ref|name=AaPAI|{{Cite Book|Anequina and Pellitine: An Introduction}}}} and powerful kingdoms like [[Lore:Corgrad|Corgrad]],{{Ref|name=RJ|{{Cite Book|Rultari's Journal}}}} and the nations of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]].{{Ref|name=AHoD|{{Cite Book|A History of Daggerfall}}}} It was reported that the Thrassian Plague decimated the Tamrielic population, bringing it down by half. In retaliation, the [[Lore:Colovian|Colovian]] King of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]], [[Lore:Bendu Olo|Bendu Olo]] gathered armies across west Tamriel, including Hammerfell, the Summerset Isles, [[Lore:High Rock|High Rock]], and the continent's outer reaches like [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]],{{Ref|name=JoT1|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei, Part One}}}} for a full-scale invasion against the archipelago of Thras.{{Ref|name=PGE1EC|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/Cyrodiil}}}} 

The [[Lore:All Flags Navy|All Flags Navy]] has been described as the largest allied naval force in Tamriel's history. Among them were many historical figures, the most well-known was [[Lore:Syrabane|Syrabane]], the Altmeri God-Ancestor of Magic, who used the power of his [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|ring]] to save many people from the plague.{{Ref|name=VoFTHE|{{Cite Book|Varieties of Faith: The High Elves}}}} From the island of [[Lore:Stirk|Stirk]], the All Flags Navy had set forth across the Sea of Pearls,{{Ref|name=JoT2|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei, Part Two}}}} to besiege and slaughter the Sload to extinction. With unknown magic spewing from the [[Lore:Coral Tower (Thras)|Coral Tower]], the islands were sunken into the ocean for many years until it eventually resurfaced.{{Ref|name=PGE1ETWR|{{Cite Book|Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition/The Wild Region}}}} While people such as Bendu Olo were able to return from the Sea of Pearls,{{Ref|name=PGE1EC}} half of the fleet was sunken into the sea by the otherworldly magic that came from the Coral Tower, into the depths of [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]].{{Ref|name=JoT3|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei, Part Three}}}} The maelstrom swallowed the battlefield into Coldharbour, where it became {{Lore Link|the Lost Fleet}}.{{Ref|name=JoT4|{{Cite Book|Journal of Tsona-Ei, Part Four}}}}

==Summerset Isles==
[[File:ON-place-Lillandril.jpg|thumb|left|Lillandril (ESO)]]
The Sea of Pearls laden the [[Lore:Summerset Isle|Summerset Isle]] from the north and west coast. The cities of [[Lore:Alinor (city)|Alinor]] and [[Lore:Lillandril|Lillandril]] overlook the waters atop of the mountain foothills. The islands of [[Lore:College of Sapiarchs|College of Sapiarchs]], [[Lore:Eyevea|Eyevea]], and [[Lore:Wasten Coridale|Wasten Coridale]] are found. The Sea of Pearls empties into the sound of Summerset, where the docks of Alinor are located, the hills surrounding [[Lore:Ebon Stadmont|Ebon Stadmont]] and [[Lore:Sea Keep|Sea Keep]] descend to the beaches, and a river coming down from [[Lore:Cloudrest|Cloudrest]] empties into the water. The coast between the Alinor docks and its parent city is known as the Oleander Coast. In the area surrounding Lillandril are the lowlands known as the Augury Basin, which give way to the oceans.{{Ref|name=ESOMapS|[[:File:ON-map-Summerset Isle.jpg|Map of Summerset — The Elder Scrolls Online: Summerset]]}} This is exemplified in the sunken city now known as the [[Lore:Corgrad Wastes|Corgrad Wastes]]. The rest of the city had been swallowed by a sinkhole, resting in large gaping caves where the city's people reportedly continue to live.{{Ref|name=ESOWA|[[Online:Wasting Away|Wasting Away]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}} Because of the Sload's constant interjections into Summerset, they have left several landmarks across the main island's coast, such as [[Lore:Abyssal Geysers|Abyssal Geysers]], which are powered by their [[Lore:Water Magic|water magic]] and one of their [[Lore:Abyssal Pearl|Abyssal Pearls]], archaic items that attract the hidden monsters of the Sea of Pearls.{{Ref|name=IJA|{{Cite Book|Indirim's Journal, Assembled}}}} It is because of the Sload's invasions, the island's coast is heavily-fortified, as seen through Cey-Tarn Keep and Sea Keep.{{Ref|name=ESOCeyTarn}} Throughout the coast is riddled with silk white [[Lore:Coral#Summerset Coral|coral]], which is often festering with [[Lore:Coral Crab|Coral Crab]]. Other creatures found on Summerset's coastlines are the [[Lore:Reef Viper|Reef Viper]], the [[Lore:Salamander|Salamander]], and the [[Lore:Mud Hopper|Mud Hopper]]s.

==Yokuda==
[[File:ON-place-Pillar of the Singing Sun.jpg|thumb|right|Pillar of the Singing Sun (ESO)]]
The islands of Yokuda were once the home of the Redguards, but it eventually sank into the Sea of Pearls, supposedly lost forever. While it is widely believed that the continent is no longer inhabitable, there are reports that trade existed between Tamriel and Yokuda as far as the late-[[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]] in {{Year|3E 433}}.{{Ref|name=OBUlfgar|[[Oblivion:Ulfgar Fog-Eye|Ulfgar Fog-Eye]]'s dialogue in ''[[Oblivion:Oblivion|Oblivion]]''}} The political geography of the continent is largely unknown, but what is certain is that Yokuda consisted of four large-islands. The biggest one is [[Lore:Akos Kasaz|Akos Kasaz]], with the distinct duality, both gentle and brutal. It has been described as moody and many-sided and ruler and rebel. The capital of the empire and Akos Kasaz was the city of {{Lore Link|Totambu (Yokuda)}}. The second-largest is the island of [[Lore:Yath|Yath]], found south of Akos Kasaz. It was rugged and arid, but magnificent nonetheless. The island was once the home of what is now the [[Lore:Ash'abah|Ash'abah]], the exiled-nomads of the [[Lore:Alik'r Desert|Alik'r Desert]].{{Ref|name=TSEE|{{Cite Book|The Salas En Expedition}}}} It hosted a settlement known as {{Lore Link|Makan}}. Next was the island of [[Lore:Kanesh|Kanesh]], described as volcanic and tall, with highlands engulfed by the ravaging heat. The settlement of {{Lore Link|Lumut}} was active on the island. The next island was [[Lore:Samara|Samara]], consisting of lush, lowlands that bore the sweetest fruit and many natural harborages.{{Ref|name=TLIoOY|{{Cite Book|The Lost Islands of Old Yokuda}}}} The rest are much smaller islands that surround the much bigger ones. These include, [[Lore:Bahia|Bahia]], [[Lore:Irranhu|Irranhu]], [[Lore:Kardesh|Kardesh]], [[Lore:Klithi|Klithi]], and [[Lore:Moni|Moni]].{{Ref|name=RGMap}} There are several other landmarks, whose locations in these islands are unknown. The provinces of {{Lore Link|High Desert}} and {{Lore Link|Seawind}} are found in Yokuda, as well as [[Lore:Hattu Mountain|Hattu Mountain]] and the great port-city of {{Lore Link|Arch}}, found in these provinces respectively.{{Ref|name=RTHaTH}}

==Thras, Ul'vor Kus, & Other Islands==
[[File:RG-map-Nalonga and Ravan.jpg|thumb|left|Nalonga and Ravan (Redguard)]]
The island territories between Tamriel and Yokuda have little information to them, save for the best-known island of them all, the dreaded archipelago of [[Lore:Thras|Thras]], located southwest of [[Lore:The Chain|the Chain]],{{Ref|name=PGE1ETWR}} is found deep in the Sea of Pearls.{{Ref|name=JoT2}} The islands are constantly changing. The earliest maps of the archipelago depict sixteen islands that surround each other like a coral atoll, but throughout the eras and centuries, maps of Thras were varied and inconsistent. By the waning years of the Third Era, in {{Year|3E 432}}, the largest island of Thras was known as [[Lore:Agonio|Agonio]], and it is one of the most consistent parts of the map, albeit changing sizes. A black lagoon is found in the center of Thras, where intricate coral reefs and large shipwrecks are found.{{Ref|name=PGE3EOL}} 

The atoll of Thras is the home of the land Sload, whereas the [[Lore:Sea Sload|Sea Sload]] lived in the underwater kingdom of [[Lore:Ul'vor Kus|Ul'vor Kus]], found somewhere in the Sea of Pearls. There are more mysterious and elusive than their land brethren, believed to have disappeared centuries before the mid-[[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]] by the [[Lore:Sapiarchs|Sapiarchs]] of Summerset Isle. Instead of forming kingdoms like in Thras, the Sea Sload consist of cabals, groups of them that seek to achieve a specific goal.{{Ref|name=TMotSS|{{Cite Book|The Myth of the Sea Sloads}}}} The {{Lore Link|Abyssal Cabal}} was active in {{Year|2E 582}} and sought to take the Isle of Summerset into the ocean through sinkholes and the abyssal geysers that dot the coastlines. The Sea Sload, {{Lore Link|K'Tora}} was able to infiltrate the island of [[Lore:Artaeum|Artaeum]] and manipulate the Ritemaster of the Psijic Order, [[Lore:Iachesis|Iachesis]].{{Ref|name=ESOBM|[[Online:Buried Memories|Buried Memories]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Summerset (Chapter)|Summerset]]''}}, but their efforts were in vain. South of Yokuda is a series of islands with no information regarding them. The largest island of this archipelago was [[Lore:Nalonga|Nalonga]], which hosted the settlement of {{Lore Link|Zanzar}}, and southeast of that is [[Lore:Ravan|Ravan]]. The two smaller islands are called [[Lore:Asil Yelir|Asil Yelir]] and [[Lore:Siuol Yelir|Siuol Yelir]].{{Ref|name=RGMap}}
