The [[Lore:Hist|Hist]] are a species of giant spore trees growing in the innermost swamps of [[Lore:Black Marsh|Black Marsh]]. [[Lore:Argonian|Argonians]] are known to have deep connections with the Hist, calling themselves "people of the root", and licking the leaking sap of their trunks in religious rites. The trees are, in fact, a sentient race, claimed to be more ancient than all the races of man and mer.{{Ref|name=PGE3A|{{Cite Book|PGE|3|Argonia}}}}<noinclude>

Argonians claim that the Hist trees of Black Marsh were the original life forms on [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]]. The Hist were considered bystanders of the war between the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]], but most of [[Lore:Realm of the Hist|their realm]] was destroyed as the war passed over it.{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Annotated Anuad}}}} The Hist acknowledge [[Lore:Sithis|Sithis]]' role as the "Original Creator".{{Ref|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} All Hist are connected at the root{{ref|name=TIC|Keyes, Greg. ''[[Books:The Infernal City|The Infernal City]]''. Del Rey Books, 2009.}}{{rp|34}}—they are of one mind, and speak to each other.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|31}} However, some trees, like the city tree of [[Lore:Lilmoth|Lilmoth]] in the early [[Lore:Fourth Era|Fourth Era]], may become isolated.{{ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|34}} 

Hist roots stretch very far, deep into the heart of Nirn and out into distant lands. Hist sap that bleeds from Hist roots buried deep underground is known as deep sap.{{ref|name=XukasDAD|[[ON:Xukas|Xukas]]' dialogue during [[ON:Death and Dreaming|Death and Dreaming]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}}

In Black Marsh, Argonian eggs are usually incubated in pools of Hist sap to enable the Hist to help with the hatching ritual. If the Hist's connection to the eggs is somehow broken, it will result in the swift deaths of the unborn Argonians.{{ref|[[ON:Keepers of the Shell|Keepers of the Shell]] quest in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Shadowfen|Shadowfen]], Argonian eggs are left in the care of the {{Lore Link|Keepers of the Shell}} who tend the [[Lore:Hatching Pools|Hatching Pools]].{{ref|name=Uxith-Ei|[[ON:Keeper Uxith-Ei|Keeper Uxith-Ei]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} In [[Lore:Murkmire|Murkmire]]{{ref|[[ON:Egg-Tender Meena|Egg-Tender Meena]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}} Argonians who are hatched outside Black Marsh cannot hear the Hist.{{ref|name=EokiHOAT|[[ON:Eoki|Eoki]]'s dialogue during [[ON:The Heart of a Telvanni|The Heart of a Telvanni]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=KassandraWITW|[[ON:Kassandra|Kassandra]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Whispers in the Wood|Whispers in the Wood]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} Marsh-born Argonians are also capable of wielding [[Lore:Hist-magic|Hist-magic]], which doesn't operate on the same rules as conventional spellcraft.{{ref|name=SISRTR|[[ON:Sun-in-Shadow|Sun-in-Shadow]]'s dialogue during [[ON:Rising to Retainer|Rising to Retainer]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[ON:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} In the Argonian language, [[Lore:Jel|Jel]], Hist saplings are referred to as Hist-Deek, mature Hists are called Hist-Dooka, and elder Hists are called Hist-Tsoko. The names of the stages of a Hist's development are also used as months within the Argonian calendar.{{ref|name=TSOA|{{Cite Book|The Seasons of Argonia}}}}

==Communication==
{{Quote Box|''The Hists' wisdom is infinite, but our ability to understand them is limited. To commune with the Hist is to see, to hear, to smell, to feel, and even to taste in a way not your own. Discerning the meaning of these signs is not a simple thing.''|[[Online:Tree-Minder Pavu|Tree-Minder Pavu]]}}
The Hist is capable of communicating in a variety of methods. The Bright-Throat Tribe's Hist speaks through the wind, and the Bright-Throat Argonians create chimes in order to receive its message.{{ref|name=ESOMX|[[Online:Xukas|Xukas]]' dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} Some Argonians receive dreams from the Hist.{{ref|{{Cite Book|Remember Me}}}}{{ref|[[Online:Xukas#Root-Whisper Village|Xukas]]' dialogue in Root-Whisper Village during [[Online:The Swamp and the Serpent|The Swamp and the Serpent]] in [[ON:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}}

{{Lore:Hist Sap}}

Rarely, the Hist-Tsoko will mark an individual to carry out a sacred task. Those marked possess wooden skin and veins that glow amber, and the Sap-Speakers know to provide them with aid.{{ref|[[Online:Hist-Tsoko|Hist-Tsoko]] skin in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

In the Third Era, the Hist used their influence to call Argonians from across the Empire back to Black Marsh. Having foreseen the [[Lore:Oblivion Crisis|Oblivion Crisis]], the Hist (through the [[Lore:An-Xileel|An-Xileel]]) ordered the Argonians to assault the invading [[Lore:Daedra|Daedra]]. The Argonians poured into the Oblivion Gates with such ferocity that Dagon's lieutenants had to close them.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|19}} This resulted in an astounding victory for the Argonians and led to the collapse of the Empire in Black Marsh as the Argonians turned to the An-Xileel, who proceeded to drive out the Imperials.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|17}}

Not all Argonians enjoy complete rapport with the Hist like the marsh-born tribes (and in {{Year|4E 8}}, the An-Xileel) do, especially not lukiul.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|45}}

==Known Hist==
===The Rogue Hist of Lilmoth===
Lilmoth's Hist has a storied history. In the Third Era, it formed its own identity separate from the rest of the Hist, and isolated itself from its brethren.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|34}} The rogue Hist was killed.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|53}}

By {{Year|4E 8}}, a new Hist had grown from a single fragment of the root that survived the elder's killing more than 300 years ago.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|53}} The new tree's roots pushed and wound through most of lower Lilmoth.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|35}} The Hist cut itself off from the others once again.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|}} Usually, when speaking directly to the Hist, what one says is what they all say. Not so for the rogue Hist of Lilmoth.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|32}} The rogue Hist stopped talking to the people of Lilmoth by {{Year|4E 8}}; it only spoke to the An-Xileel and the Wild Ones.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|45}}{{rp|53}} Additionally, the An-Xileel in Lilmoth didn't take council from any other Hist, just the city tree.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|31}}

The rogue Hist was responsible for summoning [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]] to Nirn, and maintained contact with the island.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|53}}

===The Dreaming Tree===
[[File:ON-item-Remnant of Argon.jpg|thumb|right|The Remnant of Argon]]
The Dreaming Tree, also known as the whispering root,{{ref|name=ESOMJTWR|[[Online:Jaxsik-Orrn|Jaxsik-Orrn]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Murkmire (DLC)|Murkmire]]''}} was a Hist Tree belonging to the [[Lore:Root-Whisper Tribe|Root-Whisper Tribe]]. The [[Lore:Barsaebic Ayleids|Barsaebic Ayleids]] invaded the tribe's territory and harvested their souls at an alarming rate, and as a response, the Root-Whisper Hist sacrificed itself to create the [[Lore:Remnant of Argon|Remnant of Argon]], an amber artifact that could contain the souls of the entire tribe until the Ayleid incursion had passed. The Barsaebic Ayleids attempted to stop its creation, but were held back long enough for the Remnant to be completed.{{ref|name=ESODreaming|[[ON:Death and Dreaming|Death and Dreaming]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}} The creation of the Remnant resulted in the hist tree being put into a slumber, thus becoming known as the Dreaming Tree.

Although the Remnant of Argon was meant only as a temporary measure, the Ayleid occupation lasted far longer than was anticipated thanks to Myndhal's obsession. With the Root-Whisper Hist cut off from the rest of the Hist and the Root-Whisper tribe all but dead, there was no clear way for them to pass along the information regarding the Remnant's location. Eventually, all knowledge the Remnant of Argon faded into obscurity, thus keeping the Dreaming Tree in its slumber. It was remembered by the Hist and Argonians of Murkmire, a memory that invoked immense sadness.{{ref|name=ESOWhispers|[[ON:Whispers in the Wood|Whispers in the Woods]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

Eventually, a group of historians known as the Cyrodilic Collections came to Murkmire on behalf of their Argonian benefactor to collect and preserve ancient Argonian relics. Though their benefactor, Kassandra, seemed to be altruistic motivations at first glance, she secretly hoped that one of the artifacts discovered could finally allow her to become connected to the Hist, something that had been an impossibility for her ever since her birth into Dunmeri servitude.{{ref|name=ESOWhispers}} After Cyrodilic Collections discovered both the origin of the Remnant of Argon and the [[Lore:Rootmender's Staff|Rootmender's Staff]], which could lead them directly to the Remnant, Kassandra betrayed them, stealing the Staff and leaving the rest for dead.{{ref|name=ESODreaming}} Kassandra used the Rootmender's staff and a [[Lore:Vakka Stone|Vakka Stone]] to open a portal from the Nexus Realm directly to the hiding place of the Remnant of Argon, with the Vestige and Famia Mercius giving chase. 

Kassandra successfully uncovered the Remnant and, claiming to finally hear the Hist, was promptly devoured by a Miregaunt. Thanks to her control over the Remnant, however, Kassandra managed to wrest control of the Miregaunt and had to be defeated by the Vestige. Famia picked up the Remnant and carried it to the safety of the now long-abandoned Root-Whisper Village. There, the Vestige, the Bright-Throat Xukas, and the Naga Jaxsik-Orrn traveled into the Remnant of Argon itself in order to finally lead the lost Argonian souls out and reawaken the Dreaming Tree. In order to do this, one of them needed to stay behind and guide them back at the cost of their life, though it is unclear whether it was the Naga or Bright-Throat that did this. Regardless, because of their sacrifice, the Dreaming Tree was awakened, the Root-Whisper tribe was reborn, and the Remnant of Argon was finally no more.{{ref|name=ESORiver|[[ON:By River and Root|By River and Root]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Hist of Haj Uxith===
[[File:ON-quest-Wisdom of the Ages 04.jpg|thumb|right|The Hist of Haj Uxith in Coldharbour]]
At some point in the ancient past, the Hist tree of [[Lore:Haj Uxith|Haj Uxith]] made a deal with [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]] in order to ensure the survival of its tribe of Argonians. The village and its Hist tree were taken to [[Lore:Coldharbour|Coldharbour]] and preserved by Molag Bal, allowing both the Hist and its tribe to survive. The tree withered away, but was kept alive by a crystal provided by Bal, thought to be one of his [[Lore:Vampire|vampiric]] shards. However, his reason for providing this aid was simply to harvest Hist Sap from the tree. A mechanical sap collection facility was set up at Haj Uxith, manned by Bal's [[Lore:Dremora|Dremora]] servants.{{ref|name=Xohaneel|[[ON:Treeminder Xohaneel|Treeminder Xohaneel]]'s dialogue in [[ESO:Online|ESO]]}}

In time, the crystal poisoned the Hist, prolonging its suffering but not allowing it to die. With only what little sustenance the crystal provided, the tree became weak, and was no longer able to commune directly with its Argonians. Many years later, circa {{Year|2E 582}}, an argument broke out among the scholars and warriors of the tribe. The scholars wished to end the eternal suffering of their Hist and allow it to die, while the warriors refused to allow the Hist and the tribe to be destroyed.{{ref|name=Xohaneel}} Both parties turned to [[Lore:The Vestige|the Vestige]] to settle the matter, and pledged to aid the Tamrielic invasion force which had come to the realm to end the [[Lore:Planemeld|Planemeld]]. The ultimate fate of the tree is unknown.{{ref|[[ON:Wisdom of the Ages|Wisdom of the Ages]] quest in [[ON:Online|ESO]]}}

===The Trees of Umbriel===
[[File:BK-Umbriel.jpg|Art of Umbriel, showing the Hist-like trees|right|thumb]]
A distinct group of sentient trees were found on [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]], supposed to be 'cousins' of the Hist of Tamriel.{{Ref|name=LoS|Keyes, Greg. ''[[Books:Lord of Souls|Lord of Souls]]''. Del Rey Books, 2011.}}{{rp|86}} Like that floating island, they originated from a mysterious [[Lore:Realm of the Hist|realm of Oblivion]] and came to inhabit the [[Lore:Clavicus Vile's Realm|realm]] of [[Lore:Clavicus Vile|Clavicus Vile]]. In the early Fourth Era, these trees were split from the realm by [[Lore:Umbra|Umbra]], becoming an integral part of the life cycle on the floating island of [[Lore:Umbriel|Umbriel]]. Compared to the Hist of Tamriel, these trees slightly differed in appearance; the leaves were too oblate, the bark less fretted, and the smell a bit off.{{Ref|name=TIC}}{{rp|191}}

The trees of Umbriel were responsible for producing many substances used in the island's kitchens. The trees were interconnected and played an important role in the recycling of souls on Umbriel; in one sense, they ''were'' Umbriel. They lived in the Fringe Gyre, an external area of Umbriel located around the edge of the island's surface, although their root system extended down into the lower subterranean levels of the island. They were thought to be drawn to Tamriel from the [[Lore:Void|Void]] in {{Year|4E 48}} by the Hist in Lilmoth. Annaïg Hoïnart, a [[Lore:Breton|Breton]] who had infiltrated Umbriel and assimilated into the kitchen staff, planned to feed the roots of the trees poison and cripple Umbriel.{{ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|86}}{{rp|282}} Mere-Glim, Annaïg's Argonian companion, felt a connection with the trees similar to that of the Hist, albeit quieter and vague. After he was reborn as an {{Lore Link|Umbrielian}}, Glim felt an even deeper connection, and convinced Annaïg to help him save the trees.{{ref|name=LoS}}{{rp|290}} Following the destruction of the second [[Lore:Ingenium|ingenium]], these trees were responsible for teleporting the island of Umbriel to their mysterious realm.{{ref|name=LoS}}

The Pocket Guide also makes reference to their "cherished spore-trees".
