It is said she wants their love above all else, and for her worshippers to love themselves; it pains her when they do not. This attitude leads to an extremely devoted following.{{ref|name=IOA|{{Cite Book|Invocation of Azura}}}} She is also one of the few Princes who constantly maintains a female image, and is perceived accordingly.{{ref|name=TA}} In Khajiiti creation myths, Azurah is credited with introducing [[Lore:Moon Sugar|moon sugar]] to Khajiit,{{ref|name=WOCMA}} and this belief is also told within tales aimed at Khajiiti children.{{ref|name=ESOC|{{Cite Book|Cub Tales}}}}

The ''[[Lore:Five Songs of King Wulfharth|Five Songs of King Wulfharth]]'' claims that [[Lore:Alandro Sul|Alandro Sul]] is the immortal son of Azura.{{ref|name=FSOKW|{{Cite Book|Five Songs of King Wulfharth}}}} While Azura is considered one of the "good" Daedra by the [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]],{{ref|name=TA}} elsewhere she has been known to be allied with [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]],{{ref|name=DFFactions}} who is known to the Dunmer as one of the [[Lore:House of Troubles|Four Corners of the House of Troubles]].{{ref|name=THOT|{{Cite Book|The House of Troubles}}}} The people of the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region believe that Azura bewitches some of her followers to become her "lovers" and "virtual slaves".{{ref|name=DFAzura}} Her followers acknowledge that she is "cruel but wise".{{ref|name=IOA}}

In addition to being one of her many sobriquets, "[[Lore:Moonshadow|Moonshadow]]" is also the name of Azura's realm in [[Lore:Oblivion|Oblivion]]. Azura's Star, the Twilight Star, is visible at dawn and dusk low on the horizon below the [[Lore:Astronomy|constellation]] of the [[Lore:Astronomy#The Steed|Steed]].{{ref|name=TA}} Her summoning day is [[Lore:Hogithum|Hogithum]], a festival on the 21<sup>st</sup> of First Seed.{{ref|name=IOA}}{{FMI}}<noinclude>

===Anticipation and Reclamation===
[[File:ON-npc-Azura 02.jpg|thumb|left|Azura's statue in the Cavern of the Incarnate]][[File:BK-misc-Survival Guide Azura.jpg|right|thumb|Azura]]
The [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] of [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] believe that Azura is their god-ancestor, who taught the [[Lore:Chimer|Chimer]] how to be different from the [[Lore:Altmer|Altmer]] (though some of her teachings are occasionally attributed to [[Lore:Boethiah|Boethiah]]).{{ref|name=VOF}} These tales often portray her as more of a communal progenitor{{ref|name=TA}} or "cosmic force" for the Chimer rather than an ancestor or a god.{{ref|name=VOF}} They refer to her as The '''Twilight''',{{Ref|name=AI|[[Skyrim:Aranea Ienith|Aranea Ienith]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} '''Lady of Twilight''', '''Goddess of Twilight''',{{Ref|name=Faldrus|[[Skyrim:Faldrus|Faldrus]]' dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]''}} '''Goddess of Dusk and Dawn'''{{ref|name=TA}} (or Dawn and Dusk), and '''Mother of Roses'''.{{Ref|name=AI}}

Several legends hold that [[Lore:Nerevar|Lord Nerevar]] was Azura's champion, and the two had a special relationship. He consulted Azura on important matters, and it was she who blessed his ring [[Lore:Moon-and-Star|Moon-and-Star]], a unique ring made by [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemeri]] smiths before the [[Lore:First Council|First Council]] was formed, so that it would kill any who wore it except Nerevar. It was on this ring that Nerevar swore to the Ashlander tribes that he would always honor their way of life, and thus earned their allegiance.{{ref|name=NMAS|{{Cite Book|Nerevar Moon-and-Star}}}} The ring also gave him great powers of persuasion.{{ref|name=TRN|{{Cite Book|The Real Nerevar}}}} 

The Heirographa, the orthodox teachings of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]], held that Nerevar, with the help of Azura and the Tribunal, utilized the Heart of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] to make the Dwemer disappear.{{ref|name=NARM|{{Cite Book|Nerevar at Red Mountain}}}}{{ref|name=POT|{{Cite Book|Progress of Truth}}}} Some Ashlander accounts corroborate that Nerevar and Azura made the Dwemer disappear,{{ref|name=NARM}} but generally hold that the Dwemer destroyed themselves.{{ref|name=POT}}

At some time soon after the climax of the battle of Red Mountain, following the disappearance of the Dwemer, Nerevar insisted that he, Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Vivec swear upon and before Azura never to employ the [[Lore:Kagrenac's Tools|Tools of Kagrenac]] in the profane manner that the Dwemer had intended. A few years after the battle, when the Tribunal broke their oath to Nerevar and used Kagrenac's Tools to seize divine power, Azura appeared and cursed them. She assured them that her champion Nerevar would return to punish them for their perfidy, and to make sure such profane knowledge might never again be used to mock and defy the will of the gods. When Sotha Sil dismissed her claims, all Chimer were changed into Dunmer, and she professed that all the Dunmer would share the fate of the Tribunal until the end of time.{{ref|name=TBORM}}

After the apotheosis of the [[Lore:Almsivi|Almsivi]] in the [[Lore:First Era|First Era]], Azura was recognized as one of three "[[Lore:Good Daedra|Good Daedra]]" who swore allegiance to the new gods. The Temple dubbed these three the Anticipations, as they were "the early ancestral anticipations of the loving patronage of the Tribunal".{{ref|name=TA}} Azura became the '''Anticipation of Sotha Sil''', female to his male.{{ref|name=TA}}{{ref|name=VOF}} However, Azura and the other Good Daedra (Boethiah and [[Lore:Mephala|Mephala]]) continued to be venerated by the nomadic [[Lore:Ashlander|Ashlander]]s.{{ref|name=TR|{{Cite Book|The Reclamations}}}}

Azura sees into the Twilight of the future, and guides her followers through it. Those who were faithful to Azura were given a vision that led them away from the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]] before calamity struck,{{Ref|name=AI}} which was soon set in motion after {{Year|4E 5}}, the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]]. A massive eruption of [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] wiped out much of Vvardenfell and devastated the mainland as well.{{ref|name=TR}}{{ref|name=TRY|{{Cite Book|The Red Year}}}} This, combined with the apparent disappearance of the three Tribunes at the end of the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]]{{ref|name=Morrowind|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}{{ref|name=Tribunal|Events of ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}} caused a schism in the Temple, which ultimately resulted in the (former) [[Lore:Dissident Priests|Dissident Priests]] seizing control.{{ref|name=TR}} The "New Temple" that arose from this declared worship of the Tribunes a "result of misguided teaching". It also declared the triad of Azura, Boethiah, and Mephala to be the "true way". Thus, having reclaimed their status, they are now worshipped in Morrowind as the Reclamations.{{ref|name=TR}}

The [[Lore:Lepidopterans|Lepidopterans]], a sect of Azura worshippers, wear intricate butterfly masks to hide their identities. By 2E 582, these masks had become a popular trend among the disillusioned youth of mainland Morrowind.{{Ref|name=MWM|[[ON:Moonshadow Wings Mask|Moonshadow Wings Mask]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}}

===Azurah===
[[File:ON-concept-Azurah's Gate.jpg|thumb|left|Depiction of the three Phases of Azurah: Khajiit, Mer and Human]]
In [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]], Azura is a somewhat distinct entity, the goddess '''Azurah''', the '''Mother of All Khajiit''',{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} '''She Who Sits at the Precipice'''{{ref|name=AC|{{Cite Book|Azurah's Crossing}}}} and the '''Favored Daughter of Fadomai'''.{{ref|name=WOCMA}}{{ref|name=VOF}}{{ref|name=TFDOF|{{Cite Book|The Favored Daughter of Fadomai}}}} Azurah carries the burdens of the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]]'s ancestors; her spheres are Magic, Beauty, and Prophecy, and she is the "keeper of all gates and keys, all rims and thresholds".{{ref|name=TSS}} The goddess is credited with making the [[Lore:Khajiit|Khajiit]] out of the "forest people" (some of whom became the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]]) living on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]{{ref|name=WOCMA}} and binding them to the [[Lore:Ja-Kha'jay|Lunar Lattice]], (or Ja-Kha'jay) {{ref|name=TSS}} though other sources say the Khajiit were made out of "[[Lore:Altmer|Altmeri]] stock".{{ref|name=VOF}} It is said that Azurah knows the names of all the Khajiit that will ever live, and knowing her is the first step on their Path.{{ref|name=TSS}} Azurah's is the "twilight path" to love and redemption. The Khajiit believe Azurah oversees the Gates of the Crossing behind the Lunar Lattice, a twilight realm between death and the afterlife.{{ref|name=ONMazza-Mirri|[[Online:Mazza-Mirri|Mazza-Mirri]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} One religious text refers to Azura as the [[Lore:Ur-dra|Ur-dra]], alongside Namiira.{{ref|name=TFDOF}}

Azurah contains three forms, known as Phases, the Khajiit, Mer and Human form.{{ref|name=3phasesTreasure|[[ON:Treasures T#The Three Phases of Azurah|The Three Phases of Azurah]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} She is said to be more beautiful than any other spirit, save for her sister, [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]]. She killed the dark spirit [[Lore:Varmiina|Varmiina]] in the underworld, which is why Varmiina haunts the dreams of the Khajiit.{{ref|name=TDS|{{Cite Book|The Dark Spirits}}}} Some say she often walks the halls of [[Lore:Hermorah|Hermorah]]'s library, sharing in his knowledge.{{ref|name=TWS|{{Cite Book|The Worldly Spirits}}}} [[Lore:Khenarthi|Khenarthi]] is Azurah's messenger, and ferries the dead Khajiiti souls to her for judgment. Azurah tends the lanterns lit by Khenarthi in the eyes of [[Lore:Jone|Jone]] and [[Lore:Jode|Jode]], their stillborn brothers, when they burn low.{{ref|name=TSS}}

Following the rise of the Riddle'Thar she fell in prominence somewhat,{{ref|name=Vastarie|[[ON:Vastarie|Vastarie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} and is seen as a "distant mother."{{ref|{{Cite Book|Epistle on the Spirits of Amun-dro}}}}

A Khajiiti story describes her Khajiiti form as lithe, with eyes that shined like the moons, and wearing a silken dress of purple and gold when clothed.{{ref|name=AC|{{Cite Book|Azurah's Crossing}}}}

==Mythology==
===Khajiit Myth===
Azurah was born from the third litter of [[Lore:Ahnurr|Ahnurr]] and [[Lore:Fadomai|Fadomai]], after the twin Moons and their [[Lore:ja-Kha'jay|Motions]], and [[Lore:Nirni|Nirni]]. Nirni and Azurah immediately began fighting for their mother's favor, but soon an angry Ahnurr struck Fadomai, and she fled into the [[Lore:Void|Great Darkness]] with her children for protection. There, a dying Fadomai birthed her youngest, [[Lore:Lorkhaj|Lorkhaj]], and bestowed gifts upon the rest. One by one the children received their gifts and departed, until only Azurah and her mother remained. Fadomai then left Azurah her greatest gift, three secrets—instructions on how to create the Khajiit.{{ref|name=WOCMA}}

Much of what comes after are known in pre-ri'Datta Khajiiti stories. They state that Azurah wept as her dying mother told stories of love, war, and undreamed dreams; the names of all gates and thresholds, of all the spirits, and of all the Khajiit that would ever live. Azurah wept so much that the moonlight reflecting off her tears "became one with the Lattice" as Fadomai died. Azura remained within the Great Darkness for some time, consumed by her grief.{{ref|name=TFDOF}}

===Azurah and Lorkhaj===
[[File:ON-item-Dark Heart.jpg|right|thumb|The Dark Heart (ESO)]]
Sometime later, Nirni needed a place to bear children. Lorkhaj tricked his siblings into creating the [[Lore:Nirn|World]] to satisfy her desire, and many of Fadomai's children died in its creation. The survivors killed Lorkhaj{{ref|name=TSS}} and tore out his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|heart]].{{ref|name=WOCMA}} Or so they believed, for the Great Darkness in his blood filled the hole where his heart had been.{{ref|name=TFDOF}} He sought out Azurah for help.{{ref|name=TDS}} Meanwhile, Azurah remained within the Great Darkness, mourning Fadomai's death. In time, the light from her tears and the Lunar Lattice dispersed the darkness, and she found herself in a "place of [[Lore:Moonshadow|moonlight and shadow]]". Azurah then tried to return to her mother's side, but her tears had created a great sea, and beyond it stood a black gate that opened into darkness.{{ref|name=TFDOF}} And there stood Lorkhaj, his chest gaping, a shadowy imitation of his heart beating steadily, dripping black blood. But Azurah knew the names of all of the spirits, so she recognized the Great Darkness for [[Lore:Namiira|what it was]].{{ref|name=TFDOF}} Azurah was able to rip the [[Lore:Dark Heart|Dark Heart]]—and all the Darkness—out of her brother before it could consume him.{{ref|name=TFDOF}}{{ref|name=TDS}} She cast it beyond the sea, into the Void, where it became the Moon Beast, the first of the [[Lore:dro-m'Athra|dro-m'Athra]],{{ref|name=TFDOF}}{{ref|name=TDS}} and Lorkhaj perished within Azurah's embrace. Azurah made a funeral pyre for her brother before the gate, and lit it with the Twin Lanterns of Jone and Jode.{{ref|name=TSS}}{{ref|name=TFDOF}} Her tears fell upon the pyre and the ashes scattered across the Lattice.{{ref|name=TFDOF}} As a result, it is said Azurah (and Khenarthi) can call upon the "true spirit" of Lorkhaj to appear.{{ref|name=TSS}}

The spirit [[Lore:Noctra|Noctra]] was born from the [[Lore:Ebony|black blood of Lorkhaj]] at the steps of the Void Gate. Boethra was able to defeat the spirit and took her to Azurah for judgment. Azurah was merciful, and allowed Noctra to live if she agreed to serve Azurah and the Lunar Lattice. But Noctra stole one of Azurah's [[Lore:Skeleton Key|keys]] and fled into the Void. So Azurah summoned the true spirit of Lorkhaj after her, and now Noctra aids the Khajiit when she is called upon.{{ref|name=TDS}}

It is also said that an unnamed "spirit of vengeance" was born from Azurah's grief after the deaths of her mother and brother. It has no will of its own, and can only be summoned by Azurah, [[Lore:Boethra|Boethra]], and [[Lore:Mafala|Mafala]], or by those who know its true name. Tales describe its appearance as a black panther, a warrior in ebony armor, or a hidden sword.{{ref|name=TDS}}

===The Khajiit===
Because of Lorkhaj, Nirni could now make children. But she was sad because the forest people, her favorites, were "torn between man and beast" and did not know their shape. So Azurah came to Nirni and tricked her into allowing the creation of the Khajiit, offering a "gift" of new people. She spoke Fadomai's First Secret, and the Moons parted for her. She took some of the forest people and made them many shapes, for every purpose, called them the Khajiit, and put them in the best forests and deserts on Nirni. Azura told them the Second Secret and made them Nirni's secret defenders, and bound them to the Lunar Lattice. And she spoke the Third Secret, and "the Moons shone down on the marshes and their light became [[Lore:Moon Sugar|sugar]]".{{ref|name=WOCMA}}

However, [[Lore:Y'ffer|Y'ffer]] overheard Azurah speak the First Secret and told Nirni what Azurah had done. So Nirni made the lands of the Khajiit inhospitable, and allowed Y'ffer to change the forest people into [[Lore:Mer|elves]]—the [[Lore:Bosmer|Bosmer]]. And Y'ffer shouted the First Secret across [[Lore:Aetherius|the heavens]] so all the [[Lore:et'Ada|original spirits]] could cross the Lattice, but Azurah ensured that neither Ahnurr nor Lorkhaj could hear the Secret.{{ref|name=WOCMA}} Y'ffer was corrupted by the Namiira sometime after Lorkhaj's death, and murdered Nirni. [[Lore:Hircine|Hircine]], Azurah and Khenarthi slew him in retaliation for the deed, then built a cairn for Nirni using his bones.{{Ref|name=TWS}}

Sometime before the Khajiiti people's "first memory", Azurah, knowing that the false Lorkhaj, the Moon Beast, would one day covet her children, used the Twin Lanterns to summon Lorkhaj's true spirit{{ref|name=TSS|{{Cite Book|The Sky Spirits}}}} to be a sky-guardian.{{ref|name=TTTM|{{Cite Book|The Tale of Three Moons}}}} As the third moon, Lorkhaj shined his light upon the Khajiit, choosing the purest of heart to be part of the "Litter of the Hidden Moon", to learn the way of the [[Lore:Moonlight Blade|Moonlight Blade]].{{ref|name=TTTM}} And from then on, on nights of the [[Lore:Ghost Moon|Ghost Moon]], Azurah opens the Void Gate, and the Moon Beast will challenge mortals until banished.{{ref|name=TDS}} Modern Khajiit have fallen away from this belief, instead interpreting the Ghost Moon as the corpse of Lorkhaj which was flung to the moons after Convention, and forced to follow Jone and Jode forever as punishment.{{ref|name=MBHAYQ|{{Cite Book|Moon Bishop Hunal Answers Your Questions}}}}{{ref|name=TMCD|{{Cite Book|The Moon Cats and their Dance}}}}

===Magrus' Eye===
It is said that [[Lore:Magrus|Magrus]] serves as a reminder of Azurah's wrath.{{ref|name=TSS}} The Sun God once fought Boethra and Lorkhaj, and Boethra plucked out one of his eyes.{{ref|name=TSS}}{{ref|name=TWaS|{{Cite Book|The Wandering Spirits}}}} Half-blinded, Magrus fell into the Moonshadow, where Azurah judged him too fearful to rule a sphere, and tore out his other eye (though other stories say he gave Azurah the eye willingly). Azurah took the eye and fashioned it into the Aether Prism, a stone that reflects the Varliance Gate, and opens from Dawn to Dusk.{{ref|name=TSS}}

===The Mermaid of Anvil===
[[File:ON-place-Anvil 05.jpg|thumb|right|A possible depiction of an aquatic guise of Azura]]
The [[Lore:Statues/Other_Statues#Mermaid_of_Anvil|Mermaid of Anvil]] is a statue of mysterious origin. This statue of a beautiful [[Lore:Mermaid|mermaid]] or [[Lore:Selkie|selkie]] seems to be older than the city of [[Lore:Anvil|Anvil]] itself. Due to the impressive scale and grandeur of the statue, many theorize it depicts some sort of forgotten god or an unrecognized aspect of one of the Divines. Some scandalizing but notable rumors claim that the statue depicts Azura as she appeared to some ancient sea-dwelling civilization. However, what is known of extant aquatic races like the [[Lore:Sload|Sload]] and [[Lore:Dreugh|Dreugh]] casts doubt on this idea, as races such as them are not known for creating works of such beauty.{{Ref|name=TMMoA|{{Cite Book|The Mysterious Mermaid of Anvil}}}}

==History==
===First Era===
Many years after the [[Lore:Alessian Slave Rebellion|Alessian Slave Rebellion]], tensions between the descendants of enslaved [[Lore:Nede|Nedes]] and remaining [[Lore:Ayleid|Ayleid]]s grew. Culanwe, one of the most beloved servants of Azura, sought to broker peace between the Nedes and the Ayleids. An Ayleid messenger was mysteriously murdered during this, causing suspicions between the two forces to grow, until an outsider Nede and Ayleid discovered that a servant of Molag Bal was responsible, seeking to sabotage Azura's will. United against a common foe, the Ayleids and Nedes made peace, though Culanwe sought to stop Molag Bal's plots for good.{{ref|name=JoC|{{Cite Book|Journal of Culanwe}}}} She would later be captured at Laeloria and imprisoned in Coldharbour, tortured by Molag Bal for millennia, much to Azura's dismay.{{ref|name=LTN|{{Cite Book|Loriasel Tablet Notes}}}}

The disaster caused by Molag Bal at [[Lore:Gil-Var-Delle|Gil-Var-Delle]] was the catalyst that sparked the conception of the [[Lore:Coldharbour Compact|Coldharbour Compact]] between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand, {{Year|1E 2920}}.{{ref|name=LY:V4|{{Cite Book|2920, Rain's Hand (v4)}}}} It was an agreement between [[Lore:Tribunal|Tribunal]] god [[Lore:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]] and eight powerful Daedric Princes: Azura, Boethia, Hermaeus Mora, Hircine, Malacath, Mehrunes Dagon, Molag Bal, and Sheogorath. Little is known about the Compact, but it is theorized that it prevents the Princes from directly manifesting on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]]. It is also speculated that, in order to secure the agreement of the Princes, Sotha Sil either made a credible threat to them or presented a significant offer in exchange.{{ref|name=TCC|{{Cite Book|The Coldharbour Compact}}}}{{ref|name=CWCSothaDialogue|[[Online:Sotha Sil|Sotha Sil]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Clockwork City (DLC)|Clockwork City]]''}}

[[File:ON-npc-Seryn 02.jpg|thumb|right|Seryn, possessed by Azura]]

===Second Era===
In {{Year|2E 582}}, Azura asked the [[Lore:The Vestige|Vestige]] to help free the soul of one of her servants from the clutches of [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]], and to free her Oracles from pocket realms of Oblivion. 

Azura involved herself in the affairs of mortals once more when a plot concocted by [[Lore:Barbas|Barbas]] threatened to destroy the island of [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. She assisted the Vestige and her new champion [[Lore:Seryn|Seryn]] in defeating the false Nerevarine and brother of Seryn, [[Lore:Conoon Chodala|Chodala]], and begrudgingly helped return [[Lore:Vivec|Lord Vivec]]'s power so that he could stave off the [[Lore:Meteors|meteor]] [[Lore:Baar Dau|Baar Dau]]'s collision.{{ref|name=OnlineDR|[[ON:Divine Restoration|Divine Restoration]] quest in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}}

===Third Era===
Some time around {{Year|3E 405}}, Azura charged an [[Lore:Agent|agent of the Blades]] with killing a monk who had offended her. The Blade was given [[Lore:Azura's Star|Azura's Star]] for completing the task.{{ref|name=DFAzura}}

In {{Year|3E 427}}, Azura took notice of a prisoner sent from the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]] in the hopes of being a prophesied hero, the [[Lore:Nerevarine|Nerevarine]], the reincarnation of Azura's [[Lore:First Era|First Era]] champion, Lord [[Lore:Nerevar|Indoril Nerevar]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}{{ref|name=TBORM|{{Cite Book|The Battle of Red Mountain}}}} She helped guide the prisoner into fulfilling the prophecy, becoming the Nerevarine, and freeing Morrowind from the threat of [[Lore:Dagoth Ur|Dagoth Ur]]. The Nerevarine was also given Azura's Star, as a reward for helping her win a bet with [[Lore:Sheogorath|Sheogorath]].{{ref|name=Morrowind}}

In {{Year|3E 433}}, the [[Lore:Hero of Kvatch|Hero of Kvatch]] received the Star for putting down some of Azura's worshippers who had inadvertently been turned into vampires while in her service.{{ref|name=OBAzura}}

===Fourth Era===
Faithful priests of Azura were given a vision that led them away from Vvardenfell before the eruption, and the Dunmer built the shrine of Azura in Skyrim after the [[Lore:Red Year|Red Year]] as their thanks to her, so that none would forget that she watches over them all. Azura's further visions tested the faith of the priests, who gradually left the shrine afterward as they were afraid to know their own future.{{Ref|name=AI}}

By {{Year|4E 201}}, Azura's Star was in [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]], in the hands of a dying mage. He had corrupted the artifact, allowing it to trap black souls and placing his own inside it. Azura sent the [[Lore:Last Dragonborn|Last Dragonborn]] to retrieve the artifact, though when found, it was broken. The Dragonborn entered the Star and destroyed the mage's soul, though whether or not this was done with Azura's help is unclear.{{ref|name=SRStar}}
