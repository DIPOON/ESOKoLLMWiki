# Lore:Auriel's Bow

[[Lore:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's Bow]], (also spelled '''Auri-El's Bow'''){{ref|name=DGGelebor}} is an [[Lore:Artifacts|artifact]] used by the elven god [[Lore:Auriel|Auriel]]. Although it commonly takes the form of a modest elven [[Lore:Moonstone|moonstone]] bow, it has also appeared made of [[LO:Ebony|ebony]].{{ref|name=MWTherana|[[MW:Therana|Therana]]'s dialogue in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Either way, it is one of the most powerful weapons in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]].

The bow draws its power from [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]] itself, channeling it through the {{nbsl|Lore:Sun|sun}}.{{ref|name=DGGelebor|[[SR:Knight-Paladin Gelebor|Knight-Paladin Gelebor]]'s dialogue in ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}} It has the ability to turn any arrow into a "missile of death", although its enchantment effects vary: it has been known to cause magical fire or shock damage, drain an opponent's stamina and magicka reserves, or harness the power of the sun. It can also make the wielder immune to lesser attacks, and is especially devastating when used against the undead. Occasionally it can be seemingly unenchanted. Without Auriel's power behind it, however, the bow uses its own store of energy for its power. Once exhausted of this energy, the bow will vanish, abandoning its owner to reappear elsewhere.<noinclude>

Because of its link to Auriel, the bow can manipulate the sun in various ways. It was involved in a prophecy recorded in the [[Lore:Elder Scrolls|Elder Scrolls]], which foretold that the bow would be used to block out the sun.{{ref|name=DG|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}} The bow is twinned with [[Lore:Auriel's Shield|Auriel's Shield]]{{ref|name=AR/DF|[[Arena:Auriel's Bow|Auriel's]] [[Daggerfall:Auriel's Bow|Bow]] artifact descriptions in ''[[Arena:Arena|Arena]]'' and ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} and presumably [[Lore:Auriel's Quiver|Auriel's Quiver]].{{ref|name=ESOAQ|[[ON:Treasures A#Auriel's Quiver|Auriel's Quiver]] item description in [[Online:Online|ESO]]}} It purportedly smells like [[Lore:Ash Yam|ash yams]], a vegetable native to [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]].{{ref|name=MWTherana}}{{ref|name=TRTorasaAram|[[TR:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]]'s dialogue in ''[[TR:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

==History==
Legend says that in the [[Lore:Dawn Era|Dawn Era]], the bow was created by [[Lore:Anuiel|Anuiel]] for use by Auriel against the forces of [[Lore:Lorkhan|Lorkhan]] during the [[Lore:Ehlnofey|Ehlnofey]] wars. When [[Lore:Trinimac|Trinimac]] defeated Lorkhan and tore out his [[Lore:Heart of Lorkhan|heart]], Auriel fastened it to an arrow and shot it long into the sea,{{ref|name=TM|{{Cite Book|The Monomyth}}}} where [[Lore:Red Mountain|Red Mountain]] eventually formed.

The bow was traditionally held by the [[Lore:Chantry of Auri-El|Chantry of Auri-El]], the center of the [[Lore:Snow Elf|Snow Elf]] religion based in the secluded [[Lore:Forgotten Vale|Forgotten Vale]] of [[Lore:Skyrim|Skyrim]]. The Snow Elves had discovered how to use the bow's ability to harness the power of the sun to greater effect, through the use of Sunhallowed Elven Arrows. These arrows could be fired at an enemy for massive sun damage, or could be fired directly at the sun to use its rays to strike down all foes in the surrounding area. Due to its isolation, the inhabitants of the Chantry avoided being enslaved by the [[Lore:Dwemer|Dwemer]] or killed by the [[Lore:Nord|Nords]].{{ref|name=DG}}

The bow's involvement with the [[Lore:Tyranny of the Sun|Tyranny of the Sun]] prophecy began when [[Lore:Vyrthur|Vyrthur]], the Arch-Curate of Auriel, was infected with [[Lore:Vampirism|vampirism]] by one of his own initiates. Despite Vyrthur's devotion to Auriel, the god turned his back on him when he became undead. He swore revenge against Auriel; unable to kill a god, Vyrthur instead sought to destroy his influence on [[Lore:Nirn|Nirn]] by blocking the sun. To achieve this, he needed to corrupt the bow itself with the blood of a Daughter of Coldharbour, i.e. a pure-blooded female [[Lore:Vampire Lord|Vampire Lord]] who had received the disease directly from its creator, [[Lore:Molag Bal|Molag Bal]]. Vyrthur allied himself with the Betrayed, the degenerated [[Lore:Falmer|Falmer]] who had been crippled by the Dwemer and by centuries of living underground, and killed most members of the Chantry, claiming control of Auriel's Chapel to lay in wait for the prophecy to come true.

For hundreds of years, the bow appeared throughout Tamriel, with its most recent appearances being the subject of gossip. The bow was uncovered by the [[Lore:Hero|Eternal Champion]] during the [[Lore:Imperial Simulacrum|Imperial Simulacrum]], in an unknown province.{{Ref|name=ARTIL|{{TIL|Artifact Dialogue — The Imperial Library|auriels-bow-0}}}}

In the events leading up to the [[Lore:Warp in the West|Warp in the West]], a clue to the bow's location was discovered by a [[Lore:Glenmoril Wyrd|Glenmoril Witch]] living in the [[Lore:Iliac Bay|Iliac Bay]] region. In return for kidnapping the witch's great-granddaughter to become the witch's successor, she revealed the clue to a questing member of a knightly order, who retrieved it from the dungeon where it lay. However, there are conflicting accounts that the artifact centered around this story was actually a different one altogether, with Auriel's Shield or the [[Lore:Lord's Mail|Lord's Mail]] taking its place in the story.{{ref|name=DFTA|[[Daggerfall:The Artifact|The Artifact]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} A fake copy of Auriel's Bow also appeared in the region around this time; it was created by a mage in an attempt to bribe an agent of [[Lore:Nocturnal|Nocturnal]] who was sent to assassinate them. The fake bow functioned identically to the true artifact, but after several days it crumbled to dust. This account is also shaky, as some accounts claim [[Lore:Staff of Magnus|Staff of Magnus]] or the [[Lore:Warlock's Ring|Warlock's Ring]] was offered in its place.{{ref|Fake Auriel's Bow reward during [[Daggerfall:Nocturnal's Quest|Nocturnal's Quest]] in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}}

Later in the [[Lore:Third Era|Third Era]], the bow was claimed by [[Lore:Ralyn Othravel|Ralyn Othravel]], a [[Lore:Dunmer|Dunmer]] [[Lore:Ordinator|Ordinator]] and member of the [[Lore:Tribunal Temple|Tribunal Temple]] stationed at [[Lore:Ghostgate|Ghostgate]], a small military settlement along the [[Lore:Ghostfence|Great Ghost Fence]] on [[Lore:Vvardenfell|Vvardenfell]]. 

In {{Year|3E 427}}, Mistress Therana, an eccentric Councilor of [[Lore:House Telvanni|Great House Telvanni]], discovered the bow's location through unknown means, although she claimed she could smell its ash yam scent. A member of House Telvanni forcibly took the bow from Ralyn Othravel and gave it to Therana, who grudgingly accepted it.{{ref|name=MWAB|[[Morrowind:Auriel's Bow (quest)|Auriel's Bow]] quest in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} Later that year, Therana's luck would run out, as someone hated her enough to issue a writ of execution for the sum of 10,000 gold, and requested the Grandmaster of the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]] execute her. She was finally slain,{{ref|name=MWWMT|[[Morrowind:Writ for Mistress Therana|Writ for Mistress Therana]] quest in ''[[MW:Morrowind|Morrowind]]''}} and Auriel's Bow would be retrieved from her corpse and later be sold to [[Lore:Torasa Aram|Torasa Aram]], who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [[Lore:Mournhold|Mournhold]].{{ref|name=Tribunal|[[Tribunal:The Museum|The Museum]] quest in ''[[Morrowind:Morrowind|Morrowind]]: [[Tribunal:Tribunal|Tribunal]]''}}

In {{Year|4E 201}}, the Tyranny of the Sun prophecy was realized with the awakening of [[Lore:Serana|Serana]], an ancient Daughter of Coldharbour, by the reformed [[Lore:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]. Serana was the daughter of Lord [[Lore:Harkon|Harkon]], the leader of the [[Lore:Volkihar|Volkihar]] vampire clan, who had spent centuries attempting to fulfill the prophecy. The prophecy brought Serana and the [[Lore:Hero|Last Dragonborn]] to the Forgotten Vale, where they encountered Knight-Paladin [[Lore:Gelebor|Gelebor]], the lone survivor of his brother Vyrthur's attacks on the Chantry of Auriel. The two ventured into the ruins of the Snow Elf chapel and defeated Vyrthur, who had planned to use Serana's blood to fulfill the prophecy. Auriel's Bow was retrieved and subsequently used to slay Lord Harkon.{{ref|name=DG}}

==Notes==
[[File:DF-misc-MythicallySizedUpHeavenWeapons.png|thumb|right|Mantellan Crux]]
*Based on the precedent set in [[Lore:Mantellan Crux|Mantellan Crux]] dungeon as depicted in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'', which had a giant sword and crossbow floating within [[Lore:Aetherius|Aetherius]],{{ref|name=JtA|[[Daggerfall:Journey to Aetherius|Journey to Aetherius]] quest in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]''}} [[General:Michael Kirkbride|Michael Kirkbride]] pitched during the development of [[Skyrim:Skyrim|''Skyrim'']] that a mythically sized-up and extraplanar Auriel's Bow could be used by the [[Lore:Thalmor|Thalmor]] like a [[Wikipedia:Death Star|Death Star laser]] to shoot and kill the god [[Lore:Talos|Talos]] out of heaven. As a mythic consequence of this occurring, a pandemic of esoteric mental anguish incidents for [[Lore:Men|men]] occurred, as any time men would attempt to depict the Red Diamond symbol, the Red Diamond would physically crack or even explode, ultimately causing morale to fall and for the Thalmor to gain more power. However, that pitch had gone unused.{{Ref|name=MKP|group=UOL|{{Cite Book|Michael Kirkbride's Posts|ns_base=General}}}}
*On a similar note, during the plot development of ''[[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]'', [[General:Emil Pagliarulo|Emil Pagliarulo]] pitched that vampires were attempting to extinguish the [[Lore:Sun|sun]] forever, with the world being permanently sunless. However, the designers chose to make the change to the sun a timed temporary thing in-game, rather than a permanent change to the player's game world.{{ref|name=BethPod|group=UOL|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/20131221100438/http://www.bethblog.com/podcast/ ''Bethesda Podcast #16'']}} That idea was subsequently attached to Auriel's Bow being vampirically corrupted and used to shoot out the sun, which is what appears in the final version of the add-on.{{ref|name=Dawnguard|Events of ''[[Skyrim:Skyrim|Skyrim]]: [[Skyrim:Dawnguard|Dawnguard]]''}}
