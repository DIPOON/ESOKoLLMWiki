[[Lore:Savirien-Chorak|Savirien-Chorak]] was a [[Lore:Tsaesci (race)|Tsaesci]] noble from the early-mid [[Lore:Second Era|Second Era]] and the [[Lore:Akaviri Potentate|Potentate]] of the [[Lore:Second Empire|Second Empire]] after his father, [[Lore:Versidue-Shaie|Versidue-Shaie]]. Savirien-Chorak was the last leader of the Second Empire and although he proved insufficient in his role as administrator and responded poorly in crisis, some of his most well-known decrees were acknowledging [[Lore:Orsinium|Orsinium]] as a province,{{Ref|name=HKEAYQ|{{Cite Book|High King Emeric Answers Your Questions}}}} and the reformation of local militias.{{Ref|name=HOTFG|{{Cite Book|History of the Fighters Guild}}}} In the historical fiction, "[[Lore:2920, The Last Year of the First Era|2920, The Last Year of the First Era]]", he is described as a glistening ivory-yellow eel and may have been proficient in dual-wielding.{{Ref|name=2920MS|{{Cite Book|2920, Morning Star (v1)}}}}<noinclude>

==History==
Savirien-Chorak was the son of Versidue-Shaie, the former potentate of Emperor [[Lore:Reman III|Reman III]] and later his successor. According to the historical fiction, "2920, The Last Year of the First Era", he was born in [[Lore:Tamriel|Tamriel]] and has never been to [[Lore:Akavir|Akavir]]. In life, he was close to his father and friends with the Crown Prince, [[Lore:Juilek|Juilek]]. He was present with the Prince when he defeated [[Lore:Morrowind|Morrowind]]'s armies in the [[Lore:Four-Score War|Four-Score War]] and later his meeting with [[Lore:Vivec|Vivec]] soon afterward in [[Lore:Ald Marak|Ald Marak]].{{Ref|name=2920MY|{{Cite Book|2920, MidYear (v6)}}}} The books also imply he was an accomplice in his father's plot to kill the Emperor and his son.{{Ref|name=2920SS|{{Cite Book|2920, Second Seed (v5)}}}} His last appearance in the book was after Reman III's assassination in the [[Lore:Imperial City|Imperial City]] in which he left the scene to produce an alibi for both him and his father.{{Ref|name=2920ES|{{Cite Book|2920, Evening Star (v12)}}}}

Versidue-Shaie assumed the mantle of leader throughout the Second Empire and announced the end of the First Era. His reign ended years later in {{Year|2E 324}} when he was assassinated by the [[Lore:Morag Tong|Morag Tong]], the same group that killed the Emperor.{{Ref|name=TBOD|{{Cite Book|The Brothers of Darkness}}}} Savirien-Chorak took up the title so after and although he did not live up to his father's reign, the Potentate would make some noteworthy decisions. Famously, the province of Orsinium was established under his domain{{Ref|name=HKEAYQ}} (sometime between {{Year|2E 418}} and {{Year|2E 430}}){{Ref|name=CM8|{{Cite Book|Crafting Motif 8: Orc Style}}}} and was accredited later by High King [[Lore:Emeric|Emeric]] for recognizing the [[Lore:Orc|Orc]]'s strengths as allies.{{Ref|name=HKEAYQ}} One of his father's more infamous calls was to dissolve local armies across the Empire and instead enforce an early rendition of the [[Lore:Fighters Guild|Fighters Guild]] as the only mercenary organization. But as soon as Savirien-Chorak came into office, this decision was reversed but by then, the Fighters Guild firmly established itself as a group of immense worth.{{Ref|name=HOTFG}}

In {{Year|2E 400}}, Savirien-Chorak hosted months-long celebrations to honor the four centuries of potentate rule in the Empire. In that time, the Imperial City held a variety of games and competitions, including a poetry contest. Many poets and writers traveled from all over the continent to perform in front of the Potentate's court. But the one poet to leave a lasting impact on Savirien was [[Lore:Nynelle Dumaris|Nynelle Dumaris]], a bookkeeper from [[Lore:Vastyr|Vastyr]] in the distant [[Lore:Systres Archipelago|Systres Archipelago]]. Her sonnet, "[[Lore:Fearless as the Sun|Fearless as the Sun]]" was what won the Potentate over and solidified her place in his court as the Poet-Champion. She declined his offer in the end and returned home to live out her days composing more of her work.{{Ref|name=TPCOV|{{Cite Book|The Poet-Champion of Vastyr}}}}

The Potentate was assassinated in {{Year|2E 430}}{{intnote|Date|[nb 1]}} from a black dart{{Ref|name=Remanada|{{Cite Book|Remanada}}}} and it was presumed from the same bloody night that his heirs died with him in [[Lore:Sun's Dawn|Sun's Dawn]].{{Ref|name=TBOD}} One of these heirs, however, [[Lore:Beloren-Kaie|Beloren-Kaie]] escaped from [[Lore:Cyrodiil|Cyrodiil]] and fled south into [[Lore:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]] along with several Akaviri refugees. Despite their best efforts, Kaie was killed by the Morag Tong assassin, [[Lore:Methas Daram|Methas Daram]] and his protector, [[Lore:Lein-Barduik|Lein-Barduik]] took up his name and proclaimed him and his lineage nobility.{{Ref|name=ONAshur|[[Online:Ashur|Ashur]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}}{{Ref|name=ONBeloren|[[Online:Proconsul Beloren-Kaie|Proconsul Beloren-Kaie]]'s dialogue in [[Online:Online|ESO]]: ''[[Online:Elsweyr|Elsweyr]]''}} An Akavari flute called [[Lore:Highmourn Dizi|Highmourn Dizi]] was played at the funerary procession for Savirien-Chorak, and it is said that as the sad and haunting notes of the dizi were played, the Akaviri faithful in attendance broke into tears upon hearing the "distant voice of their homeland", and even the non-Akaviri attendants were moved by the mournful, yearning tones, some even more than the Akaviri themselves.{{Ref|name=HighDizzy|{{Cite Book|Highmourn Dizi}}}} The death of Savirien-Chorak and his heirs brought about the Empire's downfall over a fortnight and in turn started the [[Lore:Interregnum|Interregnum]], a period of conflict that ended with [[Lore:Tiber Septim|Tiber Septim]]'s rise to power.{{Ref|name=TBOD}}

==Notes==
*{{LNote|Date|^|Several sources provide different circumstances for Savirien-Chorak's death. Most sources such as ''"[[Lore:The Brothers of Darkness|The Brothers of Darkness]]"'', the 3rd Edition of the [[Lore:Pocket Guide to the Empire|Pocket Guide]], and the ''"[[Lore:Systres History|Systres History]]"'' series will claim that he died in 2E 430 but others such as the 1st Edition of the Pocket Guide and the ''"[[Lore:Crafting Motifs|Crafting Motifs]]"'' series originally written by Doctor [[Lore:Alfidia Lupus|Alfidia Lupus]] have the date, 2E 431. Another contradiction comes from the identity of the perpetrators, where ''"The Brothers of Darkness"'' claims that the [[Lore:Dark Brotherhood|Dark Brotherhood]] did it while ''"[[Lore:Sacred Witness|Sacred Witness]]"'' says it was the Morag Tong, whose members would then create the brotherhood.}}
*Savirien-Chorak employed Doctor Alfidia Lupus as his Imperial Ethnographer during the latter part of his reign.{{Ref|name=CM8}}
*In an older version of ''"[[Daggerfall:The Brothers of Darkness|The Brothers of Darkness]]"'' featured in ''[[Daggerfall:Daggerfall|Daggerfall]]'' and Bethesda's [[General:Xanathar's Library Archive/History|internal timeline document]] featured in Xanathar's Library, Savirien-Chorak was originally a [[Lore:Colovian|Colovian]].
